# 供養儀式
## Offering Ritual

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0859

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown

---

> There are, in brief, twenty-four procedures.
> I bow to Vairocana Buddha,
> Whose open, pure eyes are like blue lotuses.
> Relying on the King of Sūtras, the Mahāvairocana,
> I explain the various ritual manuals for offering.
> To accomplish the sequential mantra-Dharma,
> One who follows these will swiftly attain accomplishment.
> To purify the original mind and free it from defilement,
> I now briefly expound the essentials.

**1. Selecting the Site**

> According to the terrain and suitable places:
> Wondrous mountains, flanking peaks, mid-cliff ledges,
> Various cave-dwellings between two mountains,
> Where one may find security at all times.
> Pools beautifully adorned with water-chestnuts and blue lotuses,
> Beside great rivers, flowing streams, and sandbar shores,
> Far from the clamor and bustle of people,
> With leafy, spreading, delightful trees,
> Abundant milk-wood and auspicious grasses,
> Free from mosquitoes, gnats, bitter cold, and heat,
> And from evil beasts, venomous creatures, and various hindrances.
> Or places where the Tathāgatas and holy disciples
> Once in the past resided:
> Temples, stūpas, forest-retreats, and ancient hermit-caves.
> Depending on one's own preferences,
> Leave behind household life, cut off worldly affairs,
> Diligently turn away from the five desires and all the hindrances,
> Single-mindedly delight deeply in the taste of the Dharma,
> And nurture the mind to bring siddhis.

Having found the site described above, one must first bathe. The bathing has a seal and mantra. The dhāraṇī:

*Raṃ vaṃ*

Having made the hand-seal, turn to the right to protect one's own body, turn to the left to banish all māras, then point to the eight directions and above and below: this constitutes the bathing. If digging the ground, one must also use the secret seal and mantra. The dhāraṇī:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ haḥ*

These dhāraṇī seals, or those explained in other scriptures, may all be used without conflict. If one can understand the turning and rotating, all that one does will be accomplished. Next, when scooping the earth, one must also use the secret seal and mantra. The dhāraṇī:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ kaṃ*

When one wishes to empower the ground, first enter the Dharmadhātu-mind. Purify the worldly realm; at the very bottom, establish great space. From space, give rise to the wind-wheel, and so on up to the earth-wheel. Contemplate the level, fragrant-water ocean.

Next, when one wishes to measure the ground: if beginning from the northeast corner, measure southward; from the southeast measure to the southwest; from that point measure to the northwest; from the north, return to the original position. Measure the ground in this way as described above.

For all the various offering articles, arrange them following the course of the sun. Furthermore, wrathful images should combine joy and wrath. During recitation: from the first watch of the night through the third watch, and from the fourth watch to midnight. At the beginning, add *hūṃ* and emit *oṃ*; at the end, add *hūṃ* and emit. During recitation, the squatting posture is called the "virtuous seat." At the beginning of the mantra, empower the position of the root-mind. For subjugation, shift the empowerment to the white curl between the brows. For summoning, use the syllable-gate, colored like firelight. The syllable *haṃ* is colored blue-black, placed in the wind. The syllable *a* is colored like yellow gold; add the syllable *vaṃ*, colored like white moonlight, placed on the lower body. For increase, use the auspicious seat. For true pacification of disasters, use the lotus posture. At the beginning and end of the mantra, add *namaḥ*.

For recitation, at the beginning of the mantra add the syllable *oṃ*, and at the end add *svāhā*. The time is from the latter part of the night until the first appearance of the sun. For recitation from noon until sunset, maintain a pleased countenance and a tranquil mind.

Next, when the ritual procedures are complete, go to the practice-hall and sit.

**Entering the Buddha's Samaya.** The dhāraṇī:

*Namaḥ sarva-tathāgatebhyo viśva-mukhebhyaḥ oṃ asame trisame samaye svāhā*

Form the samaya seal, which purifies the three karmas. Touch all the parts of the body, reciting the words of truth. As soon as this secret seal is formed, it can purify the ground of the Tathāgata. Next form the Dharmadhātu-born seal, the banner of secret wisdom, purifying body, speech, and mind, turning it throughout the body. The dhāraṇī:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ dharmadhātu-svabhāvako hūṃ*

As the self-nature of the Dharmadhātu, so contemplate your own body; or with the words of truth, proclaim it with three turnings. One should see that one abides in the Dharma-body, stainless like empty space.

Next, with the Vajra Armor, adorn your own body. Contemplate the garment that is donned: flames of light pervade the entire body. The dhāraṇī:

*Namaḥ samanta-vajrāṇāṃ oṃ vajra-kavaca hūṃ*

**Banishing māras and removing one's own obstructions:** having recited that dhāraṇī, contemplate the stainless syllable. All the remaining evil-minded beings, upon seeing it, will scatter in all four directions. Next, confess all roots of transgression. One should contemplate the syllable *raṃ*, colored like the first rays of the sun, burning away all transgressions from beginningless time. The dhāraṇī:

*Namaḥ sarva-tathāgatebhyo viśva-mukhebhyaḥ sarvathā-udgate spharahimaṃ gaganakam svāhā*

With this dhāraṇī seal, crown the head and empower the five places, together with incense, flowers, lamps, unguent, and powdered incense, both worldly and world-transcending wondrous offering articles. All become samādhi-offerings, each with its seal and dhāraṇī. This constitutes the purification of the three karmas.

**Establishing the directional boundaries.** Using the Acala seal and dhāraṇī, remove defilements and banish hindrances, protecting one's own body. The dhāraṇī:

*Namaḥ samanta-vajrāṇāṃ caṇḍa-mahāroṣaṇa sphaṭaya hūṃ trāṭ hāṃ māṃ*

Rest the samādhi hand at the heart. With the wisdom hand, rotate universally. One should know that whatever is touched is thereby called "removing defilement." Turning to the left accomplishes banishing. When establishing the directional boundaries, all should follow the right-turning direction. All remaining matters of destroying evil and purifying obstructions are likewise.

**Inviting the holy assembly** with dhāraṇī seal. All the Buddhas and bodhisattvas have said: "They come by the power of their original vow." The dhāraṇī:

*Oṃ vajrasattva āḥ hūṃ hūṃ he he he he*

All the Buddhas, saviors of the world, summon all with this. Bodhisattvas of great power abiding on the ten grounds and the rest, and also those difficult to tame, beings of unwholesome mind: with the wisdom wind-banner, one recitation summons each. This is the summoning.

**Presenting the samaya** with dhāraṇī seal. The seal is as contemplated above, according to the samaya teaching. The dhāraṇī:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ asame trisame samaye svāhā*

Through these skillful means, correctly present the samaya. This universally increases benefit for all classes of sentient beings. They will attain siddhis and swiftly fulfill the unsurpassed vow, causing the lords of the root dhāraṇī and all the vidyās to rejoice. Make aspirations in this way.

**Banishing māras and retaining the principal deity.** Enter the gate of joy and cause the honored one to rejoice. Recite the banishing dhāraṇī:

*Hūṃ niḥsvabhāvanī hūṃ*

With the wisdom hand, banish the māras. The dhāraṇī and seal turn to the left. The samādhi hand makes a hook and retains: this is retaining the principal deity.

**Presenting the argha water.** The argha water to be offered should have been prepared in advance. Empower it with the principal deity's dhāraṇī:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ gagana-sama-sama svāhā*

Offer to all the Sugatas, used to bathe their stainless bodies. Next, purify all the Buddha's mouth-born sons.

**Offering the vajra-seat.** Form the lotus-flower seal and invite them to be seated. The dhāraṇī:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ āḥ*

Place universally in all locations: the seats where the awakened ones sit.

**Banishing obstructions born from one's own body.** With the great wisdom-sword seal and the dhāraṇī of the Holy Immovable One:

*Namaḥ samanta-vajrāṇāṃ vaṃ*

Contemplate the supreme vajra-flame burning away all obstructions, leaving none remaining. The wise one should then transform into the body of Vajrasattva. The dhāraṇī and seal correspond; distribute throughout all the parts of the body.

**Frequent recitation of dhāraṇī.** Each should recite the root dhāraṇī of their own principal deity and the vidyā one personally upholds. Having done so in this way, call its name and present the offering. First, universally establish the pure Dharmadhātu-mind, namely the syllable-gate *raṃ*, as in the previously described offering and invocation.

**Unguent offering** with the secret seal. The dhāraṇī:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ viśuddha-gandhā-udbhave svāhā*

**Incense offering.** The dhāraṇī:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ dharmadhātu-anudgate svāhā*

**Lamp offering.** The dhāraṇī:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ tathāgatārcis-spharaṇā-avabhāsana-gaganodaryā svāhā*

**Food and drink offering.** The dhāraṇī:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ arara karara maliṃ-dandame mahā-maliṃ svāhā*

Next, for all remaining offering articles to be presented, follow these same procedures: with purity and respect, make offerings to the holy assembly. Furthermore, perform the mental ritual: supremely pure and beautifully adorned, all offerings fully complete, equal as the Dharma-realm. In this realm and in other lands, universally entering all destinies, relying on the merit of all the Buddhas and bodhisattvas, there arise banners, pennants, necklace-canopies, vast and wondrous towered pavilions, celestial jeweled tree-kings, and all manner of resources everywhere. Cloud-oceans of various fragrances and flowers, boundless as empty space, each rain down offering articles. Making offerings accomplishes the Buddha's work. Contemplate and present to all the Buddhas and bodhisattvas. With the Ākāśagarbha mantra and the universal offering seal, empower with three turnings: all wishes will be accomplished. Next, use the universal seal, crown it above the head, making the vast offering. Confess all defilements. Follow the root Dharma-seat and enter samādhi. With the root seed-syllable, place it within one's own body and transform into the body of the principal deity. The dhāraṇī:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ aḥ*

**The method of recitation.** Recite the dhāraṇī three hundred thousand times: samādhi will then manifest, and one transforms into the Dharmadhātu-body. When the recitation is complete, consign the count to the principal deity. Again present the argha and other offering articles, following the initial elaborate procedure. Protect the honored one and oneself. When all is complete, release the samaya seal above the crown, protect the practice-hall and oneself. Read and recite the Mahāyāna, circumambulate stūpas, bathe images. In managing worldly affairs, the counts for the first and latter watches of the night should be equal; at noon, reduce by half.

Regarding the method of recitation, there are, in brief, two kinds: first, according to time; second, according to signs. "According to time" means that the expected count is fulfilled, or a fixed period of days, months, and so on is completed. "According to signs" means that light, flames, or sounds emanate from a Buddha's stūpa or image; one should know this is the sign that the mantra-practitioner's karmic obstructions have been purified. When the ritual is complete, one should recite this verse:

> "All dharmas arise from conditions;
> This dharma ceases from conditions.
> That dharma ceases from causes:
> Thus has the Great Śramaṇa taught."

Regarding the construction of a maṇḍala, follow the above procedures. As for its dimensions, the capacities to construct range from the largest at thirty-six cubits, to twenty-eight, sixteen, eight, six, or four cubits, down to the minimum of two cubits. All measurements use the elbow-length of the patron of suitable fortune. The shape is as described above. If it is for a king, great minister, or elder who possesses various supreme, excellent articles and seven-treasure vessels, provide accordingly.

Furthermore, if the ācārya has many clever and capable disciples, one should construct the extensive altar. If the patron has limited means and lacks the seven-treasure supreme offerings and also lacks many clever and capable disciples, one should perform the abbreviated method, according to what can be arranged. As for all offering articles, the only exclusions are the five pungent vegetables, alcohol, meat, and other impure substances. All offering vessels should be of gold, silver, copper, iron, or porcelain; all other materials are unsuitable. The food and flavors within the altar should be changed daily. Items such as fruits may be changed once every three days.

Whenever one wishes to change the offering articles or sweep and clean, one must first send off the holy ones and address the honored figures. Only then should one sweep, apply unguent, and arrange the offerings. The practitioner should say: "May the honored figures temporarily depart from the practice-hall." When sweeping is complete, apply unguent, reopen the seats, and once the reinvitation is done, perform the offering as before.

Furthermore, the central chamber of the inner court has one presiding figure: according to the patron's preference, install a Buddha or other figure as the lord of the practice-hall. Prajñā and all bodhisattvas may be arranged within at the patron's discretion, following the presiding figure.
