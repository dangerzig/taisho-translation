# 後出阿彌陀佛偈
## Later Verses on Amitābha Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0373

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown (失譯)

---

> Contemplate Dharmākara the bhikṣu,
> Who once followed King Lokeśvararāja,
> Made vows surpassing all Buddhas,
> And pledged twenty-four articles.

> Life after life he beheld all Buddhas,
> In numbers of koṭis beyond measure;
> Never abandoning the practices of past lives,
> His merit was at last fully perfected.

> The world is named "Pure";
> The Buddha's title is "Immeasurable";
> The realm is level and smooth,
> Rich in joy, with many superior beings.

> Jewel trees of various kinds
> Stand arrayed in clusters and groves;
> Trunks, branches, leaves, and flowers,
> Each with their own distinct fragrance.

> Three times daily they sway with the breeze,
> Stirring gently as if flowers were blooming;
> Their petals fall to the ground as if spread by hand,
> Mingled and scattered, level throughout.

> There are no mountains, seas, or springs at all;
> Only rivers flow,
> Whose sound is like the chanting of sūtras.

> Gods and humans enter the water in sport,
> Obtaining whatever they desire;
> The water rises level with their shoulders,
> Their wishes fulfilled according to their thoughts.

> The Buddha's lifespan spans the sands of the ten directions,
> His radiance universally boundless;
> Bodhisattvas and disciples
> Cannot be counted or measured.

> If you wish to behold that Buddha,
> Do not doubt and do not forget;
> Those who doubt remain enclosed in the lotus womb,
> Unable to emerge for five hundred years.

> Those without doubt are born seated upon a throne,
> With hands folded before the Immeasurable One;
> Wishing to travel throughout the ten directions,
> In an instant they return.

> Contemplate that bodhisattva
> Who labored diligently for koṭis of kalpas;
> Through such original practice he attained this:
> The title of World-Honored One!

> A Buddha's arising is difficult to encounter;
> Even a brief assembly is difficult to hear;
> A teacher who expounds the Dharma is difficult to meet;
> One who receives and studies is difficult to find.

> If in later times you meet the final age,
> When the Dharma is about to decline and wane,
> Together uphold and protect it;
> Practice the Buddha's Dharma of desirelessness.

> The Buddha was able to expound this essence;
> Let each one diligently contemplate and practice it.
> Receiving this immeasurable merit,
> Life after life, bow in reverence and practice.
