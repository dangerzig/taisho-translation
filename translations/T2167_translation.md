# 入唐新求聖教目錄
## Catalog of Newly Sought Sacred Teachings Obtained upon Entering Tang China

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 2167

Tang dynasty, composed by Ennin (圓仁) of Japan

---

At Chang'an, Mount Wutai, Yangzhou, and other places, the sūtras, śāstras, recitation methods, and dharma teachings, together with commentaries, biographies, and the like that were sought total 584 titles in 802 fascicles. The great maṇḍalas of the Garbhadhātu and Vajradhātu, the two realms, together with altar images of the various honored ones, śarīra relics, and authentic portraits of eminent monks and the like total 50 items.

In Chang'an: The sūtras, śāstras, commentaries, biographies, and the like sought total 423 titles in 559 fascicles. The great maṇḍalas of the Garbhadhātu and Vajradhātu, the two realms, maṇḍalas and altar images of the various honored ones, ritual implements, and the like total 21 items.

At Mount Wutai: The Tiantai teachings and traces sought, together with various commentaries, biographies, and the like total 34 titles in 37 fascicles, along with soil, stones, and the like from the mountain.

In Yangzhou: The sūtras, śāstras, commentaries, biographies, and the like sought total 128 titles in 198 fascicles. The great maṇḍalas of the Garbhadhātu and Vajradhātu, the two realms, altar models for the various honored ones, authentic portraits of eminent monks, and śarīra relics and the like total 22 items.

The above-mentioned dharma teachings, Buddha images, ritual implements, and the like were sought and obtained at Xingshan Temple, Qinglong Temple, and various other temples in Chang'an. I have respectfully recorded them in full as above.

The above-mentioned teachings and traces were copied during the summer retreat at the Great Huayan Monastery on Mount Wutai in Daizhou of the Great Tang. I have respectfully recorded them in full as above. As for the standing stones and the like, these are objects from the dwelling place of the Great Sage Bodhisattva Mañjuśrī. Ennin and his companions obtained them while on pilgrimage to the Five Peaks. As these are objects from this sacred site, they are listed here following the sūtras and teachings. It is our wish that all who see or hear of this and rejoice therein may forge a karmic connection and all become members of the retinue of the Great Sage Mañjuśrī.

The above-mentioned dharma teachings and the like: In the eighth month of the third year of the Kaicheng era of the Great Tang [838 CE], [I] first arrived at the Great Metropolitan Prefecture of Yangzhou. After visiting the various temples and completing the search and copying, [the materials] were first entrusted to the Assistant Secretary of the [Japanese] embassy, Tomo no Sukune Mikao. They have already been sent by ship to Enryakuji. However, I had not yet prepared a complete catalog to submit to the authorities. I now respectfully compile a full record with exact numbers to present herewith.

The aforementioned sūtras and śāstras, dharma teachings, commentaries, biographies, along with the various maṇḍalas, altar images, and the like were obtained through the gracious support of the nation. Traveling [with the embassy] to Tang China and thereafter seeking teachers and studying the Dharma in Yangzhou, Mount Wutai, Chang'an, and other places over the course of nine years, I sought and obtained these according to my capacity. I have respectfully recorded the categories as above. Respectfully submitted.

[The] year Jōwa 14 [847 CE], month, day. Ennin, Dharma Master Who Transmits the Lamp, who traveled to Tang China to seek further instruction in the Tiantai school, hereby submits.

On the ninth day of the fourth month of the fourth year of Enbun [1359 CE], at the monks' quarters in the western precinct of Tō-ji, [this was] copied from a certain manuscript; it is to be subsequently collated and verified against an authoritative text.

Great Dharma Master Kenpō.

On the twenty-second day of the seventh month of the fourth year of Genbun, the cyclical year *jimi* [1739 CE], repair and restoration was carried out under the direction of Okamoto Shinkei; completed by Acting Senior Priest Kenka.

In the middle of the eleventh month of the third year of Bunsei, the cyclical year *kōshin* [1820 CE], [this was] copied from the Kanchi-in manuscript of Tō-ji.

Shinchō, Acting Senior Priest of Mount Hiei, former lecturer.
