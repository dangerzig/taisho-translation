# Sūtra of the Six Perfections Spoken by Monk Saṃgha before Entering Nirvāṇa

## *Sēngqié Héshàng Yù Rù Nièpán Shuō Liùdù Jīng* (僧伽和尚欲入涅槃說六度經)

Taishō 2920

Translator unknown. An apocryphal text attributed to the monk Saṃgha (*Sēngqié*, 僧伽).

*Note: The monk Saṃgha (d. 710 CE) was a Central Asian monk who became enormously popular in Tang-dynasty China, worshipped as an emanation of Avalokiteśvara. This text, attributed to him, blends Pure Land, apocalyptic, and devotional themes.*

I declare to the good men and good women of Jambudvīpa (*Yánfútí*, 閻浮提): since my birth in Jambudvīpa, I have served as a great compassionate father, teaching and converting sentient beings. Through the cycle of transmigration in the world, across beginningless and vast kalpas, I have manifested billions of emanation-bodies to save and deliver sentient beings.

Because I see that the sentient beings of Jambudvīpa create much evil karma and do not believe in the buddha-dharma, and those who commit evil are many, I cannot bear to witness this. Therefore my body shall enter nirvāṇa, and my relics and images shall be spread throughout Jambudvīpa to guide and convert sentient beings.

Afterward, when the age of the semblance dharma fills the world and the true dharma arises again, I shall, together with the honored Maitreya (*Mílè*, 彌勒) Buddha, simultaneously descend to be reborn and shall sit together in the Transformation City (*huàchéng*, 化城) to save and deliver those with good affinities.

My original dwelling was in the Eastern Sea. This was the pure land affinity of my past lives. Because sentient beings were stubborn and foolish, difficult to convert, and did not believe in the buddha-dharma, creating much evil karma, I left my original place and went to the western lands to teach and convert sentient beings, where I was known as Śākyamuni (*Shìjiāmóuní*, 釋迦牟尼) Buddha. The eastern lands were then submerged by five hundred poisonous dragons, turning into a great sea. All sentient beings sank into the sea and were transformed into turtles, crocodiles, fish, and tortoises.

Afterward, my body came again from the western barbarian lands to be reborn in Jambudvīpa, to save and deliver those with good affinities and the seeds of Buddha-nature.

I see that the sentient beings of Jambudvīpa are everywhere vicious and evil, devouring one another. They cannot be converted. Therefore I now enter nirvāṇa. May my relics and original bones remain at Sizhou (*Sìzhōu*, 泗州).

Hereafter, if there are good men and good women who, with compassionate hearts and filial devotion, revere my image, maintain a permanent vegetarian diet, and recite my name, such people, scattered throughout Jambudvīpa: I will look upon them with compassion in this evil age when armed conflict arises on all sides and all manner of evils press upon the body, leaving no freedom.

Later, I shall descend with the honored Maitreya Buddha to be reborn in the original land. My feet shall step upon the sea waters, which will dry up. I shall then cause all the devas, nāgas, spirits, the eightfold assembly, and the holy multitudes to construct the Transformation City at the center of the Eastern Sea, with walls of gold and silver, floors of lapis lazuli, and palaces of the seven treasures.

Afterward, when I come to Jambudvīpa, I shall spread the buddha-dharma. I transmit only this sūtra to teach and convert those with good affinities. The disciples of the six perfections shall take refuge in me. In the Transformation City they will be free from the suffering and hardship of Jambudvīpa, and they will attain peace and security. Clothing and food will arise naturally. They will forever enjoy utmost bliss. Celestial Māras and heterodox practitioners will be separated by the Weak Water (*ruòshuǐ*, 弱水) and will not come to do harm.

I shall deliver six kinds of people. The first to be delivered are those who are filial to their parents and revere the Three Treasures. The second are those who do not kill sentient beings. The third are those who do not drink wine or eat meat. The fourth are those who are fair-minded and good-hearted and do not steal. The fifth are those who practice austerities (*dhūta*, 頭陀), who repair bridges, and who perform various meritorious deeds. The sixth are those who take pity on the poor and are mindful of the sick, who give clothing and food, and who aid the destitute. Such good people of the six perfections: I shall first send a hundred youths to lead them onto the treasure ship and carry them across the Weak Water, so that they are not submerged, and they shall enter the Transformation City.

If there are those who are not among my six kinds of people, who see this sūtra and do not believe in or accept it, but instead slander the true dharma, one should know that such people have karmic roots of sin from past lives and will receive evil retribution in their bodies. Perhaps they will die by bandits, soldiers, or pestilence. Perhaps they will be burned or swept away by water and fire. Perhaps they will suffer from epidemic illness, meet with lawsuits, or be cast into prison. Such unwholesome sentient beings will all suffer immeasurable afflictions. After death they will enter the hells, with no prospect of escape. For ten thousand kalpas they will not recover the human path.

Good men and good women, copy this sūtra with sincere intention, receive and uphold it. If you encounter the kalpa of flood, the kalpa of fire, black wind, or the darkening of the heavens, I will emit immeasurable radiance to illuminate you. Through this affinity you will all come to the buddha-land, together entering the Transformation City, and will all attain liberation.

[Dhāraṇī:] *Namo saṃghāya. Namo saṃghaṃ jaṭa svāhā. Tadyathā. Oṃ vajra-kṣa svāhā.*
