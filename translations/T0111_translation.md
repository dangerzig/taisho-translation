# 佛說相應相可經
## Sūtra Spoken by the Buddha on Mutual Correspondence and Accord

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 111

Translated from the Chinese. Western Jin, translated by Fǎ Jù (法炬)

---

The Buddha was in the land of Śrāvastī and summoned the bhikṣus, and the bhikṣus immediately came. The Buddha addressed the bhikṣus, and the bhikṣus responded: "Yes, we will hear from the Buddha."

The Buddha then spoke: "The unlearned associate with the unlearned; they are of the same kind, gather together, correspond, and accord with one another. The learned associate with the learned; they are of the same kind, gather together, correspond, and accord with one another.

"Those who are lustful associate with those who are lustful; they are of the same kind, gather together, correspond, and accord with one another. Those who are free from lust associate with those who are free from lust; they are of the same kind, gather together, correspond, and accord with one another.

"Those who are angry associate with those who are angry; they are of the same kind, gather together, correspond, and accord with one another. Those who are free from anger associate with those who are free from anger; they are of the same kind, gather together, correspond, and accord with one another.

"Those who are deluded associate with those who are deluded; they are of the same kind, gather together, correspond, and accord with one another. Those who are wise associate with those who are wise; they are of the same kind, gather together, correspond, and accord with one another.

"Those who give generously associate with those who give generously; they are of the same kind, gather together, correspond, and accord with one another. Those who are miserly and covetous associate with those who are miserly and covetous; they are of the same kind, gather together, correspond, and accord with one another.

"Those with few desires associate with those with few desires; they are of the same kind, gather together, correspond, and accord with one another. Those with many desires associate with those with many desires; they are of the same kind, gather together, correspond, and accord with one another.

"Those who do not uphold the precepts associate with those who do not uphold the precepts; they are of the same kind, gather together, correspond, and accord with one another. Those who uphold the precepts associate with those who uphold the precepts; they are of the same kind, gather together, correspond, and accord with one another.

"Those who are difficult to satisfy associate with those who are difficult to satisfy; they are of the same kind, gather together, correspond, and accord with one another. Those who are easy to satisfy associate with those who are easy to satisfy; they are of the same kind, gather together, correspond, and accord with one another.

"Those who are not content associate with those who are not content; they are of the same kind, gather together, correspond, and accord with one another. Those who are content associate with those who are content; they are of the same kind, gather together, correspond, and accord with one another.

"Those who do not guard themselves associate with those who do not guard themselves; they are of the same kind, gather together, correspond, and accord with one another. Those who guard themselves associate with those who guard themselves; they are of the same kind, gather together, correspond, and accord with one another."

The Buddha said: "Bhikṣus! In this way a wise person should discern these causes and conditions: what is to be practiced, let it be practiced; what is not to be practiced, let it not be practiced."

Thus spoke the Buddha. The bhikṣus received his teaching, practiced it, and bore it in mind.
