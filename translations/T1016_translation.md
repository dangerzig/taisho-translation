# 舍利弗陀羅尼經
## Śāriputra Dhāraṇī Sūtra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1016

Translated from the Chinese. Liang dynasty, translated by Sanghabhadra (僧伽婆羅).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was staying at the Great Forest Monastery in the land of Vaiśālī, together with a great assembly of twelve hundred fifty monks. At that time, the Buddha reflected that after three months he would enter nirvāṇa, and told the Elder Maudgalyāyana: "You should go throughout a thousand worlds and tell the monk assemblies to come to the Buddha's presence." Then Maudgalyāyana received the Buddha's instruction, and in one instant reached the summit of Mount Sumeru, emitting a great sound that proclaimed throughout the thousand worlds. Then at the Great Forest Monastery, forty billion monks all came to the Buddha's presence, prostrated at his feet, and withdrew to sit to one side.

Then the Elder Śāriputra reflected single-mindedly: "I shall, by the power of supernatural penetration, cause all the monk assemblies throughout the three-thousandfold great-thousandfold world system to gather at this Great Forest Monastery." Then by supernatural power he caused all the śrāvaka assemblies, pratyekabuddha assemblies, and bodhisattva assemblies throughout the three-thousandfold great-thousandfold world system to gather at once. Having arrived, they prostrated at the Buddha's feet.

Then in one instant the World-Honored One summoned the bodhisattvas, whose names were: Sudarśana Bodhisattva, Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva, Durgati-pariśodhana Bodhisattva, Tamomardana Bodhisattva, Sarva-viṣaya-samantatā Bodhisattva, Nīvaraṇa-viṣkambhin Bodhisattva, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, Gandhahastin Bodhisattva, Pratibhāna-kūṭa Bodhisattva, and Maitreya Bodhisattva, saying: "Good sons! You should go to the buddha-fields of the ten directions, numerous as the sands of the Ganges, and tell all the bodhisattvas there, those who are one-lifetime successors, those who have attained non-retrogression, and those who have realized the patience of non-arising dharmas, to come and gather here." Then those bodhisattvas, hearing the Buddha's words, replied: "Excellent! Excellent!" They received the instruction, and in one instant, by the power of supernatural penetration, gathered all the bodhisattvas from the buddha-fields of the ten directions, numerous as the sands of the Ganges. Hundreds of thousands of thirty billion nayutas of one-lifetime successor bodhisattvas, non-retrogression bodhisattvas, and bodhisattvas who had attained the patience of non-arising dharmas; hundreds of thousands of billions of nayutas of one-lifetime successor bodhisattvas; and thirty billion nayutas of non-retrogression, non-arising patience bodhisattvas, all gathered together, prostrated at the Buddha's feet, and withdrew to sit to one side.

Then Śāriputra, seeing the great assembly of bodhisattvas, reflected single-mindedly: "I shall now question the Tathāgata, the Perfectly Enlightened One, about dharmas not yet heard. By this questioning, the bodhisattvas shall be freed from doubt, shall attain unobstructed eloquent wisdom, shall hear the dharma preached by buddhas throughout worlds as numerous as the sands of the Ganges, and shall, until they attain supreme perfect enlightenment, always remember their past lives. They shall attain the four kinds of purity of bodhisattvas.

"What are the four? Purity of beings, purity of dharma, purity of eloquence, and purity of buddha-fields.

"They shall also attain four kinds of excellence. What are the four? Excellence of body, excellence of speech, excellence of mind, and excellence of birthplace.

"They shall also enter four kinds of dhāraṇī gates. What are the four? The dhāraṇī gate of inexhaustible retention, the dhāraṇī gate of skillful means for entering beings' faculties, the dhāraṇī gate of unobstructed skillful means regarding karmic fruition, and the dhāraṇī gate of profound dharma patience."

Having thus reflected, he asked his questions in order, like a *maṇi*-jewel, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I now wish to ask questions in order so that the bodhisattvas' virtue may become pure. May the World-Honored One deign to teach me."

The Buddha told Śāriputra: "If bodhisattva-mahāsattvas wish to reach all dharmas without attachment, they should recite this dhāraṇī:

*Śuni śuke make sarvānta mukhe yuktini yuktini lukti prabhī hile kalapa kalapa si sale prabhāvatī hila hili hile hilile hila hile caiti cavamī cala calane attati arāne nimadini bhadanini dhiyatini hale vimale śubhane prāniliti cipane vavavihavane vāsanghadāmi vipula prabhī saṅkara liṣaṇe dhile tiṭile mahātiṭile yaśovatī sañcale acale macale vamacale tilita viśuddhi asaṅga-vihale asaṅga-nihale nihāra-vimale nihāra-śubhani dhrīta sumi sthami bhata mahāvatī mahāpāraje samantapāraje vipulapāraje vipula-lakṣmī saratamukhe sarvatara anugati anajite dhāraṇī dhāraṇī nidāta godhile svāhā*

"Śāriputra! If a bodhisattva cultivates this dhāraṇī, they do not think of conditioned or unconditioned dharmas, do not speak of this dharma, do not observe this dharma, do not cling to this dharma, do not produce this dharma, do not seek to attain this dharma, do not cut off this dharma, do not increase or decrease it, do not see dharmas that accord or do not accord, do not see arising or ceasing, do not see the three times, do not see regression, do not see gathering or scattering, and have nothing to say. They only cultivate mindfulness of the Buddha, without being mindful of form; without being mindful of the marks of excellence or the absence of marks; without being mindful of precepts or the absence of precepts. Concentration, wisdom, liberation, and the knowledge and vision of liberation are likewise. They are not mindful of lineage, relatives, or retinue; of dwelling or non-dwelling; of reaching or not reaching. They do not discriminate the aggregates, elements, or sense-bases. They are not mindful of knowledge or non-knowledge, self-purity or other-purity, purity of beings or non-purity of beings. They do not act for their own benefit or for others' benefit. They are not mindful of purity of the three karmas, or purity of present and future conduct.

"Śāriputra! This is called mindfulness of the Buddha. It is named 'entering all dharmas.' It is named 'attaining the dhāraṇī of skillful means for practicing all dharmas.' It is also named 'cultivating the ultimate truth.' It is also named 'fulfilling wished-for samādhi.' It is named 'directed single-mindedly toward attaining bodhi.' It is also named 'the treasury of practice where all good roots depend on nothing external.' It is also named 'the dhāraṇī of concealing the marks of excellence.' It is also named 'the dhāraṇī of seeking skillful means.' It is also named 'the dhāraṇī of transcending Māra.'

"Śāriputra! This dhāraṇī has boundless practices and vows. If future bodhisattvas attain its meaning, they will achieve non-retrogression from supreme perfect enlightenment and will swiftly attain the unsurpassed path. Why? Because this is the treasury of all buddhas' merit. This is what discerns the practices of all beings. Therefore this dhāraṇī is named 'the practice of non-attainment.'"

Then the World-Honored One spoke these verses:

> Do not delight in or cling to
> The emptiness of all dharmas.
> Regarding the buddhas' enlightenment,
> Do not give rise to discrimination.
>
> Do not reckon all dharmas
> As having faults or being faultless.
> If you can cultivate this practice,
> You will swiftly attain the dhāraṇī.
>
> Listen to this sūtra:
> It is inexhaustible and indestructible.
> Cultivate the provisions of wisdom
> And you will attain the unsurpassed path.
>
> Bodhisattvas who uphold
> This dhāraṇī dharma gate
> Will hear the buddhas of the ten directions
> Expound the wondrous, subtle teaching.
>
> They will know the foremost dharma,
> Its syllables and meanings.
> Like the light of the sun and moon
> Shining everywhere without limit,
>
> The bodhisattva who comprehends all dharmas,
> Their characteristics are likewise.
> This dhāraṇī gate
> Encompasses all dharmas.
>
> When beings have doubts
> And raise all manner of difficult questions,
> The holder of the dhāraṇī
> Can explain them all.
>
> In this bodhisattva's wisdom
> There has never been regression.
> This is a true child of the Buddha
> Who will swiftly attain bodhi.
>
> The holder of the dhāraṇī
> Is remembered by all buddhas.
> Beings revere and honor them
> Because they hold the dhāraṇī.
>
> In this very life they will see
> Eighty thousand billion buddhas,
> Who will anoint their head with their hands
> And comfort them in peace.
>
> One who can, in a single day and night,
> Uphold the dhāraṇī,
> Will have all the sins of a billion thousand kalpas
> Entirely extinguished.
>
> Throughout countless kalpas
> They have cultivated various merits.
> One month of dhāraṇī practice
> Equals all those merits.
>
> All the māras of the heavens
> Cannot create obstacles
> For the merits of concentration and wisdom
> Of one who holds this dhāraṇī.
>
> Able to retain all buddhas' dharma,
> Having spoken this dhāraṇī gate,
> If there are people who have resolute faith,
> In future lives they will surely attain the unsurpassed path.
>
> In the past, Dīpaṃkara Buddha,
> Having heard the dhāraṇī,
> Was given a prophecy by all buddhas
> And attained the way, called Dīpaṃkara.
>
> If you wish in one instant
> To see buddhas as numerous as the sands of the Ganges,
> And also wish to know the dharma
> Taught by buddhas of the ten directions,
>
> Uphold the dhāraṇī
> And you will attain the fruit you desire.
> That person will surely have the power
> To purify the buddha-fields,
> With radiance and marks of excellence,
> All provisions fully complete.
>
> Without marks and without clinging,
> Not seeking any other reward,
> Only wishing with all beings
> To swiftly attain the unsurpassed path.
>
> Therefore today
> Diligently listen and receive this practice.
> In the future you will surely attain
> The unsurpassed, perfect enlightenment.

The Buddha told Śāriputra: "If a bodhisattva accomplishes four things, they will attain the dhāraṇī. What are the four? Not delighting in sensual desire, not being jealous, being able to give everything to all beings, and day and night contemplating dharmas without anger or vexation. Śāriputra! If a bodhisattva accomplishes these four things, they will attain this dhāraṇī."

Then the World-Honored One spoke these verses:

> Sensual desire and jealousy
> Are the dharma of the Māra King; they lead to the hells.
> The practitioner should abandon them.
> Material goods and dharma, both kinds,
> Should be given to all beings.
> Day and night contemplate
> Without giving rise to anger.
> If you can practice thus,
> You will attain the dhāraṇī.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra! If a bodhisattva accomplishes four things, they will attain this dhāraṇī. What are the four? Practicing forest-dwelling, maintaining profound dharma patience, not delighting in gain and fame, and being able to give everything they possess, even their body and life. If a bodhisattva accomplishes these four things, they will attain the dhāraṇī."

The Buddha spoke these verses:

> Practicing forest-dwelling
> Is praised by the virtuous.
> Body and mind tranquil and unmoving,
> Always residing in the forest.
>
> As urgent as if your head were on fire,
> Delight in developing profound dharma patience.
> Fame and material gain,
> All without attachment.
>
> With a pure, unstained mind,
> Seeking the unsurpassed path,
> Able to give all that is cherished:
> Spouse, children, body, and life,
> Without giving rise to regret or grudging,
> For all shall ultimately perish.
>
> The bodhisattva who diligently
> Cultivates these four practices
> Will soon attain
> The dhāraṇī described above.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra! If a bodhisattva accomplishes four things, they will attain this dhāraṇī. What are the four? Entering the meaning of eight syllables. What are the eight syllables?

"The syllable *bha* enters the meaning of non-self in all dharmas.

"The syllable *ra* enters the meaning of the dharmakāya, which is beyond marks of excellence and beyond the absence of marks.

"The syllable *sa* enters two meanings: the dharma of the foolish and the dharma of the wise.

"The syllable *ja* enters the meaning of birth, aging, sickness, and death, and of no-birth, no-aging, no-sickness, and no-death: no arising and no ceasing.

"The syllable *ga* enters the meaning of transcending karmic fruition.

"The syllable *tha* enters the meaning of holding the dhāraṇī dharma and transcending emptiness, signlessness, wishlessness, and the dharmadhātu.

"The syllable *śa*: *śamatha* and *vipaśyanā*. *Śamatha* and *vipaśyanā* enter the meaning of true reality in all dharmas.

"The syllable *kṣa* enters the meaning that all dharmas are arising and ceasing moment to moment, yet inexhaustible and indestructible, for they are originally quiescent.

"The meaning of these eight syllables can be known and entered; this is the first way of entering. The complete dhāraṇī dharma should be written out and upheld; this is the second way of entering. This complete dharma should be recited and remembered every half-month; this is the third way of entering. The bodhisattva should teach, praise, and share this dhāraṇī, gladdening others; this is the fourth way of entering.

"Śāriputra! By these four ways, the bodhisattva attains this dhāraṇī."

The Buddha spoke these verses:

> Write it out and uphold it,
> Contemplate the eight syllables above.
> Every half-month recite and memorize them,
> And teach all beings.
>
> You will draw near the unsurpassed path,
> The supreme abode of wisdom,
> Always see the tathāgatas,
> And hear the wondrous dharma preached.

"If bodhisattvas uphold the dhāraṇī, they will obtain four kinds of merit. What are the four? They are always remembered and protected by all the buddhas and world-honored ones of the ten directions. They are not obstructed by the Māra King. All their bodily, verbal, and mental evils are extinguished. They attain inexhaustible eloquence. Śāriputra! If bodhisattvas uphold the dhāraṇī, they attain these four kinds of merit."

The Buddha spoke these verses:

> Protected by all buddhas,
> No māra can obstruct them.
> The three evil karmas are extinguished,
> And their eloquence is boundless.

Then the Buddha told Śāriputra: "Long ago in the past, countless incalculable kalpas ago, there was a buddha named Ratnaśrīprabhā-rāja Tathāgata, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One, complete with the ten epithets, who appeared in the world to teach beings. After that buddha's nirvāṇa, there was a cakravartin king named Dhāraṇaprabhā, endowed with the seven treasures. That king had a son named Acintyaguṇaśrī, aged sixteen. After the buddha's parinirvāṇa, that prince heard this dhāraṇī being taught.

"For seventy thousand ages he never slept or was lazy. For seventy thousand ages he did not covet the throne, nor cling to body, life, or possessions. For seventy thousand ages he never once lay down but sat in meditation, constantly hearing the dharma taught by ninety billion nayutas of buddhas. Having heard the dharma, the buddha gave him a prophecy of renunciation, and after nine hundred thousand ages he attained the dhāraṇī named 'Grasping Infinite Gateways.' Having attained it, he taught it to beings, and in one lifetime established eighty billion hundred-thousand nayutas of beings in the stage of non-retrogression, destined to attain supreme perfect enlightenment.

"At that time in the assembly there was an elder's son named Candrachatra, who heard the teaching of the 'Grasping Infinite Gateways' dhāraṇī. Having heard it, he rejoiced, and by the merit of that rejoicing, ninety billion buddhas bestowed on him the prophecy: 'In upholding the dhāraṇī you are foremost. All beings who hear your teaching will be delighted, and no questioner can overthrow you. In the future, after three incalculable kalpas, all the beings you teach will attain supreme perfect enlightenment.'

"Śāriputra! What do you think? That Prince Acintyaguṇaśrī of that time, was he someone else? He was none other than Amitāyus Buddha. The elder's son Candrachatra was Dīpaṃkara Buddha. Śāriputra! In the past, I myself and the bodhisattvas heard this dhāraṇī and rejoiced. By that good root, for four million kalpas we were not born in evil destinies.

"Therefore, Śāriputra! If bodhisattva-mahāsattvas wish to swiftly attain enlightenment, they should rejoice in this 'Grasping Infinite Gateways' dhāraṇī and practice diligently. Why? Because bodhisattvas who accomplish this will abide in non-retrogression and attain perfect enlightenment. How much more so for those who write, recite, uphold, and explain it! Only the buddhas can describe the full extent of their merit."

The Buddha spoke these verses:

> Countless kalpas in the past,
> There was the Ratnaśrīprabhā Tathāgata,
> Who appeared to teach all beings
> With immeasurable benefit.
>
> After that buddha's nirvāṇa,
> There was a cakravartin king
> Whose son, named Guṇaśrī,
> Was just sixteen years old.
>
> After the tathāgata's passing,
> He heard the dhāraṇī taught.
> For seventy thousand ages
> He knew no sleep or laziness,
> Nor coveted wealth,
> The throne, body, or life.
>
> He heard ninety billion
> Nayutas of buddhas
> Expound the wondrous dharma gates,
> And single-mindedly received them.
>
> The tathāgata prophesied:
> After nine hundred thousand ages
> He would attain the dhāraṇī
> Named "Grasping Infinite Gateways."
>
> Having attained it, he taught others,
> Benefiting countless beings,
> Establishing them all in non-retrogression
> From the unsurpassed perfect enlightenment.
>
> The elder's son Candrachatra,
> Hearing the dhāraṇī taught,
> Gave rise to the merit of rejoicing,
> And all the buddhas prophesied:
>
> "In receiving the dhāraṇī
> You are the foremost.
> When you teach it to others,
> None will fail to delight.
> If anyone raises a challenge,
> None can overthrow you.
>
> In a future life,
> After three incalculable kalpas,
> All the beings you convert
> Will attain the unsurpassed path."
>
> The elder's son Candrachatra
> Was Dīpaṃkara Tathāgata.
> The dharma teacher mentioned before
> Was Amitāyus Buddha.
>
> I myself and the bodhisattvas,
> Having heard the dhāraṇī in the past,
> For four million kalpas
> Were not born in evil destinies.
>
> If bodhisattvas wish to attain
> The unsurpassed, omniscient path,
> They should diligently practice
> And rejoice in the dhāraṇī.
>
> Why is this so?
> Because of abiding in non-retrogression.
> If those who merely rejoice
> Gain such merit,
> How much more those who write, recite,
> And explain its meaning!
>
> Even if seven treasures
> Filled worlds as numerous as the sands of the Ganges,
> The merit of such giving
> Could not compare.
>
> Therefore the wise
> Should diligently practice,
> Listen to and receive the dhāraṇī,
> Seeking the unsurpassed path.

"Śāriputra! If bodhisattvas uphold this dhāraṇī, there are eight yakṣas dwelling on the Snow Mountains who will guard and protect them day and night, increasing their lifespan. What are the eight? Śūra Yakṣa, Dṛḍha Yakṣa, Īśvara Yakṣa, Nārāyaṇa-bala Yakṣa, Dharmopāya Yakṣa, Abandhya Yakṣa, Kuṭadanṣṭra Yakṣa, and Suskandha Yakṣa. Śāriputra! These eight yakṣas will always guard and protect them.

"Those who uphold the dhāraṇī should bathe and put on freshly dyed robes, constantly practice walking meditation, harbor no harmful thoughts toward any being, and continually contemplate this dhāraṇī dharma. Then those yakṣas will swiftly come to guard and protect them.

"Furthermore, there are eight great bodhisattvas dwelling in the desire heavens who will watch over and protect those who uphold the dhāraṇī. Their names are: Prabhā Bodhisattva, Prajñāprabhā Bodhisattva, Sūryaprabhā Bodhisattva, Vinaya Bodhisattva, Sarvāśāparipūrṇa Bodhisattva, Maheśvara Bodhisattva, Nakṣatra-rāja Bodhisattva, and Caryāśaya Bodhisattva. Those bodhisattvas have attained the dhāraṇī. If bodhisattvas attain this dhāraṇī, they should fulfill their vows and be grateful. If bodhisattvas hold the dhāraṇī, they should attain the true dharma and share it with all beings. If bodhisattvas uphold the dhāraṇī, they will be fully endowed with merit and accomplish profound wisdom."

When the Buddha taught this dharma, thirty hundred-thousand billion nayutas of bodhisattvas attained this "Grasping Infinite Gateways" dhāraṇī and achieved non-retrogression from supreme perfect enlightenment. Eight *vivara* numbers of devas and humans gave rise to the aspiration for supreme perfect enlightenment.

The Elder Śāriputra addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! What is this sūtra called? How should it be upheld?"

The Buddha told Śāriputra: "This sūtra is named the 'Grasping Infinite Gateways Dhāraṇī'; you should uphold it thus. It is also named 'The Path Bodhisattvas Single-Mindedly Follow'; you should uphold it thus. It is also named 'Overcoming All Māras'; you should uphold it thus. It is also named 'Attaining Omniscience'; you should uphold it thus."

Then Śāriputra and all the bodhisattvas, devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, humans and non-humans, having heard the Buddha's teaching, joyfully accepted and put it into practice.
