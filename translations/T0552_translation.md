# 佛說摩登女解形中六事經
## Sūtra of the Mātaṅgī Girl and the Six Things within the Body, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0552

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown

---

The Buddha was in Śrāvastī, at the Jetavana Monastery of Anāthapiṇḍada. At that time, Ānanda took his bowl and went to beg for food. Walking along the water's edge, he saw a young woman at the water carrying water. Ānanda asked her for water, and she gave him some. The woman then followed Ānanda to the place where he was staying.

She went home and told her mother, whose name was Mātaṅgī. At home the girl lay down and wept. Her mother asked: "Why are you weeping?"

The girl said: "If mother wishes to arrange my marriage, do not give me to anyone else. At the water's edge I saw a śramaṇa who asked me for water. I asked his name; it is Ānanda. If I can have Ānanda, then I will marry. If I cannot have Ānanda, I will not marry."

The mother went out to inquire about Ānanda. Ānanda served the Buddha. The mother learned this and returned to tell her daughter: "Ānanda follows the buddha-path and will not agree to be your husband."

The girl wept and refused to eat, saying: "Mother knows the arts of enchantment. Why not use them?"

The mother went out and invited Ānanda to come for a meal. The girl was delighted. The mother said to Ānanda: "My daughter wishes to be your wife."

Ānanda said: "I keep the Buddha's precepts. I may not take a wife."

The mother said: "If my daughter cannot have you as her husband, she will kill herself."

Ānanda said: "I serve the Buddha. I am not permitted to have relations with a woman."

The mother went inside and told the girl: "Ānanda will not agree to be your husband. He says that those who follow the path may not take a wife."

The girl wept before her mother and said: "Where is mother's power?"

The mother said: "Of all the powers in the world, none can surpass the buddha-path and arhatship."

The girl said: "Just close the doors for me and do not let him leave. By evening he will become my husband."

The mother closed the doors and bound Ānanda with her enchantment.

At the hour of the late afternoon, the mother prepared a bed. The girl was delighted and adorned herself. Ānanda would not go to the bed. The mother then lit a fire, seized Ānanda's robe, and said to Ānanda: "If you will not be my daughter's husband, I shall throw you into the fire."

Ānanda was ashamed, thinking that he was a śramaṇa, yet today he was trapped and could not escape. He immediately joined his palms and called upon the Buddha. The Buddha knew at once, and sent his supernatural power to free Ānanda.

Ānanda came to the Buddha and said: "Yesterday while begging for food, I saw a young woman at the water's edge and asked her for water. The next day, a woman named Mātaṅgī invited me to come for a meal. When I went, she seized me and tried to give me her daughter as my wife. I keep the Buddha's precepts. I may not take a wife."

The girl, seeing that Ānanda had escaped, wept at home. The mother said: "Against those who serve the Buddha, my power cannot prevail. Did I not tell you so?"

The girl wept without ceasing, constantly thinking of Ānanda.

The next day the girl went out herself to look for Ānanda. She saw him again going on his alms-round. She followed behind him, gazing at his feet and gazing at his face. Ānanda was ashamed, lowered his head, refused to look at her, and avoided her. The girl continued to follow without stopping. Ānanda returned to the Buddha's dwelling. The girl waited at the gate. Ānanda did not come out, and the girl wept and went home.

Ānanda addressed the Buddha: "The Mātaṅgī girl followed me again today and will come again tomorrow."

The Buddha then summoned the girl. Seeing her, the Buddha said: "You pursue Ānanda. What do you seek?"

The girl said: "I have heard that Ānanda has no wife, and I have no husband. I wish to be Ānanda's wife."

The Buddha said: "Ānanda is a śramaṇa and has no hair. Are you willing to shave your head? I will have Ānanda be your husband."

The girl said: "I dare to shave my head."

The Buddha said: "Go home and tell your mother. Shave your head and come back."

The girl returned to her mother and said: "Mother could not bring Ānanda to me. I went myself to inquire and ask the Buddha. The Buddha said: 'Shave your head and come back. I will have Ānanda be your husband.'"

The mother said: "I have raised and protected your hair since birth. Why would you become a śramaṇa's wife? There are great and wealthy families in this country. I myself can marry you to one of them."

The girl said: "In life and death I will be Ānanda's wife."

The mother said: "Why do you disgrace your family?"

The girl said: "If mother loves me, she should follow my heart's desire."

The mother shed tears, took up the blade, and shaved her daughter's hair.

The girl returned to the Buddha and said: "I have shaved my head."

The Buddha said: "What do you love about Ānanda?"

The girl said: "I love Ānanda's eyes, I love Ānanda's nose, I love Ānanda's mouth, I love Ānanda's voice, I love Ānanda's way of walking."

The Buddha said: "The eyes have only tears. The nose has only mucus. The mouth has only saliva. The ears have only wax. The body has only excrement and urine, all foul places. When a man and woman become husband and wife, there are foul places, and from those places children are born. When there are children, there is death. When there is death, there are tears of grief. In all of this, what benefit does the body bring?"

The girl then reflected on the foul impurities within the body. She straightened her mind and at once attained the path of arhatship. Having attained arhatship,

the Buddha said to the girl: "Rise and go to Ānanda."

The girl was ashamed. She lowered her head and knelt before the Buddha, saying: "It was truly out of foolishness that I pursued Ānanda. Now my mind has been opened. It is like a lamp in the darkness. It is like a person in a boat when the boat breaks apart and they reach the shore. It is like a blind person gaining a guide. It is like an old person walking with a staff. Now the Buddha has given me the path, and my mind has been opened in just this way."

The śramaṇas all asked the Buddha: "This girl's mother practiced the arts of enchantment. How is it that the girl attained arhatship?"

The Buddha said: "Do the śramaṇas wish to hear and know about this girl?"

The śramaṇas said: "We wish to receive the teaching."

The Buddha said: "This Mātaṅgī girl, in five hundred past lives, was Ānanda's wife. Through five hundred lifetimes they respected and cherished each other, desired and loved each other. Now, through the sūtra precepts and the path, they have both attained the path. From now on, when husband and wife meet, they are like brother and sister in appearance. This sūtra is to make the śramaṇas understand the nature of a woman's mind."

The śramaṇas then rose and made obeisance to the Buddha.
