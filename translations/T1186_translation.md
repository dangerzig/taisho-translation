# 佛說妙吉祥菩薩陀羅尼
## Dhāraṇī of Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1186

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmabhadra (法賢).

---

> I bow my head to the supreme, universal wisdom,
> The Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha,
> Whose mind and thought are pure, without peer or equal,
> Whose supernatural powers are unobstructed and unprecedented,
> Who well pervades the worlds of the ten directions,
> Transforming and guiding all beings according to their faculties.

> I bow my head to Mañjuśrī, the great guide,
> Who skillfully removes dust and defilement, whose mind is purified,
> Whose body is adorned with immeasurable merit,
> Who broadly teaches the wondrous dharma to save all classes of beings.
> Devas, nāgas, mighty asuras, and others
> All take hold of his feet and bow their heads in homage.

The dhāraṇī of Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva says:

[Dhāraṇī]
