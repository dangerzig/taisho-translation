# 佛說摩訶剎頭經
## Mahākṣatriya Sūtra, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0696

Translated from the Chinese. Western Qin dynasty, translated by Shengjian (聖堅)

---

"Great kṣatriyas, devas, people, and elders, listen clearly!

"A human body is difficult to obtain, and the unsurpassed Way is likewise. A human life is difficult to obtain, and a Buddha's appearance in the world is rare. The Buddha Śākyamuni, from the time of asaṃkhyeya kalpas past, when his body was that of a layperson, accumulated merit through cumulative effort. In every life he disciplined himself, revolving through the five destinies, not coveting wealth or treasures, giving up his body to others. He thereby became a crown prince. On the eighth day of the fourth month, at midnight when the bright star appeared, he was born. Descending to the ground, he walked seven steps, raised his right hand, and said: 'Above the heavens and below, I shall become the teacher of all people.'

"When the prince was born, heaven and earth shook greatly. The seventh Brahmā heaven, the second Trāyastriṃśa heaven's Lord Śakra, and the first Four Heavenly Kings all came down, bringing twelve kinds of fragrant water and various flowers, and bathed the prince's body. The prince established himself and became a Buddha, revealed the Way and the Dharma, and showed it to the people of the world."

The Buddha told the people of the world: "All the Buddhas of the ten directions were born at midnight on the eighth day of the fourth month. All the Buddhas of the ten directions left home and entered the mountains to study the Way at midnight on the eighth day of the fourth month. All the Buddhas of the ten directions attained the Buddha-path at midnight on the eighth day of the fourth month. All the Buddhas of the ten directions entered parinirvāṇa at midnight on the eighth day of the fourth month."

The Buddha said: "Why the eighth day of the fourth month? Because at the juncture of spring and summer, all wrongdoing and calamity are completed, all things are born, poisonous vapors have not yet stirred, it is neither cold nor hot, and the weather is mild and suitable. This is the day of the Buddha's birth. Therefore, all the people of the world should join in contemplating the Buddha's merit and bathe the Buddha image as though the Buddha were present. This is why it is shown to the people of the world."

The Buddha said: "When I was a bodhisattva in the world, I was thirty-six times Śakra, king of the devas, thirty-six times a golden wheel-turning king, and thirty-six times a flying emperor. Today, all worthy ones who have a good heart and good mind, who contemplate the grace and virtue of the Buddha Śākyamuni: if with fragrant flowers they bathe the Buddha image and seek the foremost blessing, the devas, ghosts, and spirits will bear witness and know."

The Buddha said: "A human body is difficult to obtain, and the Buddha's sūtras are difficult to hear. If one is able to reduce from one's own share, taking from the possessions that belong to the five claimants to bathe the Buddha image, one may freely have what one wishes. If one wishes to transcend the world and attain the unconditioned Way, to live always without encountering the round of birth and death, this can be obtained. If one wishes to be as diligent and courageous as the Buddha Śākyamuni, establishing oneself in the Way, this can be obtained. If one wishes to become like Mañjuśrī, an avaivartika bodhisattva who flies and teaches, fully endowed with marks among the people, this can be obtained. If one wishes to become a pratyekabuddha or an arhat, this can be obtained. If one wishes to close off and sever the three evil paths at their root and be born in heaven or among humans, this can be obtained. If one wishes to become the seventh Brahmā heaven king, or Śakra, Lord of the second Trāyastriṃśa heaven, this can be obtained. If one wishes to become a flying emperor, this can be obtained. If one wishes to be a noble and distinguished person in the world, this can be obtained. If one wishes for riches reaching hundreds of millions, this can be obtained. If one wishes for a hundred sons and a thousand grandsons, this can be obtained. If one wishes for long life without illness, this can be obtained. The people of the world would rather lose a piece of flesh from their own body than part with a single coin. When a person is born, they bring not a single coin; when they die, they take not a single coin. Wealth and possessions remain in the world while one dies and endures suffering. Use it to bathe the Buddha: this merit accompanies one through birth and death, without end."

The Buddha said: "Within and beyond Jambudvīpa, all the Brahmā devas, Śakra, ghosts, spirits, and nāgas will all protect one who bathes the Buddha image. From this cause and condition, one attains the Buddha's path of nirvāṇa."

The Buddha said: "One who bathes the Buddha image with fine incense and fragrant water will obtain one's own reward: purity and renown wherever one is. One who offers beautiful flowers to the Buddha will obtain one's own reward: an upright and beautiful complexion with none to compare. One who offers silken banners to the Buddha will obtain one's own reward: in every life born with fine garments naturally provided, without limit."

The Buddha said: "Through cumulative merit, accumulated virtue, practicing goodness with utmost sincerity, keeping the precepts, practicing patience, vigor, single-mindedness, and wisdom, I thereby attained buddhahood.

"Of the offerings collected from bathing the Buddha, they should be divided into three parts: the first is Buddha-money, the second is Dharma-money, and the third is Saṃgha-money. Buddha-money is for making and repairing Buddha images, whether of gold, copper, wood, clay, moulding, or painting; the Buddha-money is used for this maintenance. Dharma-money is for erecting towers and stūpas, monasteries, fences, walls, and buildings within and without; this is Dharma-money. Of Saṃgha-money: if there are ten thousand coins, a thousand bhikṣus should share it. If there are no assembled bhikṣus, one portion is used and one portion is given to the Dharma-money. Even among a few people, it should be divided into three parts, with one part distributed.

"Afterward, whether Dharma-money or Saṃgha-money, not a single coin above may be misused. If one privately takes a single coin and uses it for personal benefit, though one may appear generous in the present life, one will be impoverished in the extreme. In the future one will long endure suffering. It would be better to cut one's flesh with a sharp knife and give it away than to use a single coin from the Buddha-bathing offerings for private generosity. It would be better to swallow molten copper than to use a single coin from the Buddha-bathing offerings for personal favors. It would be better to throw oneself into a great fire than to give a single coin from the Buddha-bathing offerings to one's wife and children, or use it together for management, for cooked food: in the present life one will experience terror, and in future lives one's spirit will endure this pain.

"The people of the world, many of whom form the aspiration and make wishes, on the day of giving should not calculate whether it is little or much, but simply see that the service is well supplied and respectfully offered. As for the leftover dishes and food from the assembled households, what is not consumed: where should it be placed?"

The Buddha said: "Whether little or much, it should all be sent and given to the Dharma-upholding śramaṇas who tend the monastery. The assembled Saṃgha will share it among themselves. What is distributed should be expected to produce more merit. How can one eat the spring planting and expect the harvest of blessing to grow? One should not each rush to divide one's share and take it home to one's wife and children. That is like planting seeds on a rock: the roots and stems will wither completely and there will never be a time of growth. Now, with the remaining merit of the offerings, give it again to the assembled Saṃgha. This is the way to give one and receive ten thousandfold in return.

"The method of bathing the Buddha on the eighth day of the fourth month: take duliang, agastache, and aìnà, combine these three kinds of herbal fragrance, rub and steep them: this makes the blue-colored water. If there is not enough fragrance, one may substitute dark indigo dye or qinpi bark. Take turmeric, rub it by hand and steep it in water, rub it to make the red water. If there is not enough fragrance or it is unavailable, one may substitute with a flesh-colored powder. Take qiulong incense, pound and then steep it, to make the white-colored water. If there is not enough, one may supplement with white powder; if it is unavailable, one may substitute with white powder. Take white aconite, pound and then steep it, to make the yellow-colored water. If white aconite is unavailable, one may substitute with gardenia. Dark water for the black color: lastly for purification, one takes well-water drawn at dawn, called 'dark water.'

"These five-colored waters are used for bathing as described above.

"After the purification bathing of the image with clear water is complete, wipe it with white silk or white cotton. Afterward, if one bathes again on one's own, this is called the purification bathing. Its merit is no different from the first."
