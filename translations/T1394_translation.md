# 佛說安宅神呪經
## The Buddha's Teaching: Divine Mantra Sūtra for Pacifying Dwellings

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1394

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown.

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was dwelling in the country of Śrāvastī, in the Jeta Grove, the garden of Anāthapiṇḍada, together with one thousand two hundred and fifty bhikṣus. All were arhats whose outflows were exhausted, whose bodies and minds were clear and still, and who possessed the six supernatural powers without obstruction. Their names were: the greatly wise Śāriputra, Mahāmaudgalyāyana, Mahākāśyapa, Mahākātyāyana, Subhūti, and others. There were also eight thousand bodhisattva mahāsattvas, including Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva, Nāyaka Bodhisattva, Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, Vimokṣa Bodhisattva, and other such bodhisattva mahāsattvas of sovereign majestic virtue. There were also bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, upāsikās, and devas, nāgas, yakṣas, the eight classes of ghosts and spirits, all surrounding him as he taught the sublime Dharma.

At that time fifty sons of Licchavi householders came together, their bodies covered in dust, their hearts filled with grief and sorrow, looking as if they had lost their parents or beloved wives and children in life. They came before the Buddha, bowed their heads to the ground in reverence, and stood to one side.

Then the World-Honored One, knowing the answer but asking nonetheless, said: "Sons of the householders! For what reason do you have such troubled expressions, grieving and unhappy, having lost your usual composure?" The householders' sons all addressed the Buddha in unison: "World-Honored One! We do not understand: for people dwelling in the world, are there indeed auspicious and inauspicious influences concerning their homes and dwellings?" The Buddha answered: "Such matters all arise from the mental activities and dream-imaginings of sentient beings. One cannot say they are entirely nonexistent."

The Licchavis addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! Your disciples, through the blessing of even a hair's breadth of merit from past lives, have been able to behold the Tathāgata's compassionate teaching that omits nothing, opening the gate of sweet dew and moistening us with the rain of Dharma. Yet what sins have we committed that we are born in this world of the five turbidities and extreme evil, harboring grief and embracing suffering, terrified at every turn, never free even for a moment? The reason we say this is that your disciples, being shallow in virtue and meager in merit, find that our dwellings are beset by calamitous portents one after another. Evil demons compete day and night to encroach upon us. Sitting or lying down, we find no peace, as if carrying boiling water or fire within us. Recently we have lost our good intentions and have nothing to rely on. We only wish that the World-Honored One would accept our invitation, come to our dwellings, and graciously pacify our homes, commanding all the guardian spirits of the dwelling and the seasonal taboos to come constantly and protect us, so that day and night we may be at peace and all calamities eliminated." The Buddha said: "Excellent, excellent! It shall be as you say. I will know the proper time."

Then the next morning the World-Honored One commanded his disciples: "You should each arrange your robes properly. We shall enter the village. Each of you take your alms-bowl and go to the homes of the householders' sons." When the meal was finished, a seat was spread, and the World-Honored One taught the subtle Dharma for the householders, freeing them from fear and bringing joy to their bodies and minds. At that time the Licchavis each gave rise to great happiness, like a bhikṣu entering the third dhyāna.

Then the World-Honored One summoned the guardian spirits of the dwellings to come before the Buddha and told them: "From this day forward, you ghosts and spirits must not recklessly cause fright and disturbance, making these people anxious and constantly afraid. I shall send powerful ghosts and spirits to crush your bodies into fine dust."

Then the World-Honored One further addressed the great assembly: "Good men and good women! In the five hundred years after my nirvāṇa, the defilements of sentient beings will be heavy and wrong views will blaze ever more fiercely. The ways of demons will flourish in competition, and malevolent spirits will act recklessly, lurking at people's gates and doors, watching for their opportunity, seeking people's vulnerabilities, and creating inauspicious portents and all manner of obstacles. At such a time, these disciples should single-mindedly be mindful of the Buddha, mindful of the Dharma, and mindful of the bhikṣu-saṃgha. They should purify themselves through fasting and precepts, uphold the Three Refuges, the Five Precepts, the Ten Wholesome Deeds, and the Eight Precepts of the purificatory fast, and bow in repentance six times daily, diligently and energetically.

"They should invite monks of pure conduct and set out a feast for pacifying the dwelling. Burn various renowned incenses, light lamps to maintain illumination, and in the open central courtyard read this sūtra aloud: 'Since so-and-so has settled and established this dwelling, having built the southern hall, the northern hall, the eastern and western wings, the millstone and granary, the well and stove, the gates and walls, the garden, grove, and pond, and the livestock pens; or perhaps having moved buildings, disturbed the earth, or dug at improper times; or perhaps having offended and disturbed the prostrate dragon or soaring serpent, the azure dragon, white tiger, vermilion bird, or dark warrior; the six jia taboos, the spirits of the twelve hours, the spirits of the gate, courtyard, doors, and paths, the well, stove, and exposed places, the hall, the rooms, and the spirit beside the privy: I now, bearing the divine power of all the buddhas, the majestic radiance of the bodhisattvas, and the power of prajñāpāramitā, command the guardian spirits before, behind, to the left, to the right, and in the center of this dwelling, their spirit-children and spirit-mothers, the prostrate dragons and soaring serpents, the six jia taboos, the spirits of the twelve hours, the flying corpses, the evil spirits, and the wǎngliǎng ghost-spirits, whether assuming form or voice or attaching themselves by name: from this day forward you must not recklessly disturb my disciples. Spirit-children, spirit-mothers, all spirits in the dwelling, evil enchantments, gu-sorcery, wǎngliǎng and wicked demons, each remain in your place and do not recklessly encroach upon or afflict one another, causing so-and-so to be startled, disturbed, and frightened. You should act according to my teaching. If you do not comply with my words, I shall cause your heads to split into seven parts, like the branches of a tāla tree.'"

Then the World-Honored One spoke the mantra:

> "Namo buddhāya, namo dharmāya,
> Namo saṃghāya."

"I now, for my disciple so-and-so, bearing the Buddha's majestic power, speak this divine mantra:

> "One-footed beings, do not trouble me; two-footed beings, do not trouble me;
> Three-footed beings, do not trouble me; four-footed beings, do not trouble me."

"I possess the great compassion and great kindness that cares for all sentient beings. You evil demons, each return to where you belong! You must not wantonly disturb my disciples." He further spoke the mantra:

*Śveta-nāgarāja, Puṇya-putra-nāgarāja, Utpala-nāgarāja, Anavatapta-nāgarāja.*

"Mantra for establishing the boundary:

*Gabhavati gabhavati svāhā. In the east, the great spirit Nāgarāja establishes a boundary of seven li, a vajra dwelling. In the south, the great spirit Nāgarāja establishes a boundary of seven li, a vajra dwelling. In the west, the great spirit Nāgarāja establishes a boundary of seven li, a vajra dwelling. In the north, the great spirit Nāgarāja establishes a boundary of seven li, a vajra dwelling. Thus spoken three times.*

*In the east, Bhāgu, in the deep mountains, sāla trees and forked branches, shackle the necks of you hundred ghosts. In the south, Bhāgu, in the deep mountains, sāla trees and forked branches, shackle the necks of you hundred ghosts. In the west, Bhāgu, in the deep mountains, sāla trees and forked branches, shackle the necks of you hundred ghosts. In the north, Bhāgu, in the deep mountains, sāla trees and forked branches, shackle the necks of you hundred ghosts. Thus spoken three times.*

"You who preside over illness, you who preside over headaches, you who preside over the gates and doors of people's dwellings: you should restrain your poisons and must not disturb my disciples. If you do not comply with my mantra, your heads will split into seven parts."

Then the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> Building dwellings and raising halls,
> Peacefully nurturing all living beings,
> With gardens, groves, and ponds,
> Gates, walls, and latrines,
> When the mind is set on constructing homes,
> In movement and stillness, respond to the holy spirits.
> Bowing in homage, taking refuge in the Buddha,
> No demon can cause one to fall.
>
> Bright lamps shine without limit;
> The five eyes arise through them.
> The Dharma-King's great mantra-power
> Shakes and shatters demons by the hundreds of millions.
> The Tathāgata's compassion moistens all universally;
> His majestic radiance penetrates without limit.
> May all take refuge together,
> And may all evils each depart of their own accord.

The Buddha addressed the sun, moon, five planets, twenty-eight lunar mansions, the heavenly spirits, nāgas, and ghosts, all of whom had come to receive instruction: "Listen clearly. The Buddha commands: You must not advance upon or retreat from the home of so-and-so, whether they build an eastern wing, western wing, or a southern or northern hall. I command the roaming sun, the killing moon, the generals of the earth bureau, the azure dragon, white tiger, vermilion bird, and dark warrior, the yearly, monthly, and kalpa killing spirits, the six jia taboos, and the prostrate dragon of the earth bureau: do not recklessly move about. If there is any activity or movement, burn incense and announce: 'The dwelling of so-and-so is the Buddha's vajra ground. Within two hundred paces in each direction, the Buddha has given his word: all pestilence-ghosts and spirits must not recklessly act in opposition. Those who do will have their heads split into seven parts, their bodies will not remain whole, they will be denied water and drink, and they will be expelled from their original abodes. When the dwelling is complete, there will be wealth, honor, and auspicious prosperity. The fields will yield great harvests and all wishes will be fulfilled. There will be glory in coming and going and in military service. Officials will be well-suited to their posts and the household will flourish. There will be a hundred sons and a thousand grandsons, fathers will be compassionate and sons filial, men and women loyal and chaste, elder brothers good and younger brothers obedient. They will honor righteousness and benevolent virtue, and all their wishes will be as they desire. The ten directions bear witness: may they practice like bodhisattvas and attain the Way like buddhas.'"

The Buddha addressed Ānanda: "If one wishes to pacify a dwelling, one should set out forty-nine lamps in the open central courtyard, sweep and sprinkle the ground, burn incense, single-mindedly repent, and bow to the buddhas of the ten directions." Ānanda further addressed the Buddha: "What should this sūtra be called?" The Buddha said to Ānanda: "This sūtra is called the Inconceivable Divine Power of the Tathāgata's Great Compassion. It is also called the Divine Mantra for Caring for Sentient Beings, Pacifying Dwellings, and Defeating Demons."

When the Buddha had finished speaking this sūtra, the great assembly rejoiced, bowed in reverence, and put it into practice.
