# 八名普密陀羅尼經
## Dhāraṇī Sūtra of the Eight Names of Universal Secrecy

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1365

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Xuanzang (玄奘).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Bhagavat was dwelling in Śrāvastī, in the Jeta Grove at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park, together with a great assembly of twelve hundred and fifty bhikṣus, as well as innumerable bodhisattva mahāsattvas, and devas, humans, asuras, and other beings of various kinds, surrounding him on all sides.

At that time the World-Honored One addressed Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva: "Good man! The supreme vidyā-mantras that you have received and upheld are mighty and fierce in their supernatural function, and their accomplishment is difficult. Although they ultimately bring benefit, they may at first cause temporary harm. There now exists the mantra of the Eight Names of Universal Secrecy. Its majestic power is vast and great, its accomplishment is easy, its supernatural function is secret, and from beginning to end it causes no harm. Those who can receive and uphold it will certainly obtain benefit and joy. I shall teach it to you. Do you wish to hear it?" Then Vajrapāṇi, overjoyed and exultant, bowed at the Buddha's feet, knelt on his right knee, joined his palms, and requested: "World-Honored One! We humbly pray that you will teach it."

The Buddha said: "Listen carefully, reflect upon it well, and I shall now explain it to you in detail. What are the Eight Names of the Universal Secrecy Mantra? The first is Treasury of Merit. The second is Elephant-Ear Ornament. The third is Brave and Valiant. The fourth is Cloud of Supreme Truth. The fifth is Blazing Brilliance. The sixth is Subtle and Wondrous Form. The seventh is Adorned and Ornamented. The eighth is Vajra. If anyone hears these eight names, then for seven koṭi-nayuta hundreds of thousands of great kalpas in future lifetimes, they will not fall into the hells, animal realm, or hungry-ghost realm. When their life draws to its end, their body and mind will be at peace and ease. They will see buddhas and bodhisattvas appear before them and teach them the profound essentials of the Great Vehicle Dharma. Having heard this Dharma, they will certainly be reborn in Tuṣita Heaven to attend upon Maitreya. Later, following Maitreya, they will descend to Jambudvīpa, and their practices and vows will gradually increase until they reach the ultimate." The dhāraṇī states:

*Aṭṭi vaṭṭi naṭṭi khalṭi sumī sumā sukhī sarva-karma-sādhani hiri miri vīri vīri sidhantu-mī sarva-karma-sādhā-ścitra mantra-bandha svāhā.*

When the Buddha spoke this Universal Secrecy Mantra, eight thousand koṭis of evil spirits and ghosts all gave rise to joy, abandoned their poisonous minds, took refuge in the Buddha, Dharma, and Saṃgha, abandoned evil and cultivated goodness, and all went to the Buddha's presence where they spoke words of truth: "May we constantly protect and uphold this great mantra and enable those who receive and uphold it to enjoy peace and joy of body and mind." The Buddha said: "Excellent! As you have vowed. If any good man or good woman takes refuge in the Buddha, Dharma, Saṃgha, and Vajrapāṇi, is able to dwell in quiet seclusion and firmly cultivate pure conduct, draws near to stūpas, and receives, upholds, reads, and recites this mantra, then whatever wholesome wishes they seek will all be obtained, up to and including unsurpassed perfect enlightenment. They will constantly remember their past lives without forgetting."

When the Bhagavat had spoken this sūtra, Vajrapāṇi and all the bodhisattvas, as well as the devas, humans, asuras, and the rest of the entire assembly, having heard what the Buddha taught, all greatly rejoiced, believed, received it with faith, and put it into practice.
