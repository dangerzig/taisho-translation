# 佛說毘沙門天王經
## The Buddha's Teaching: Sūtra of Vaiśravaṇa Deva-King

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1245

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmadeva (法天).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was in the kingdom of Śrāvastī, in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍada's Park. At that time, Vaiśravaṇa Deva-king, together with hundreds of thousands of innumerable yakṣa retinues, came to the Buddha's dwelling during the first watch of the night. Radiating great light that illuminated all the precincts of the Jeta Grove, he prostrated with his five limbs touching the ground, bowing at the World-Honored One's feet, and stood to one side. With palms joined before the Buddha, he spoke these verses of praise:

> Homage to the Great Fearless One,
> The Perfectly Awakened One, Honored among Two-Legged Beings!
> Though the devas observe with their divine eyes,
> They cannot perceive me.
> Buddhas of past, present, and future,
> Compassionate lords of the three times,
> Each and every Perfectly Omniscient One,
> I now bow in homage to you.

When Vaiśravaṇa Deva-king had spoken these verses, he addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! There are śrāvaka bhikṣus and bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas and upāsikās, who in the wilderness, in forests, or beneath trees practice walking meditation, sitting, and reclining. Among my yakṣas and non-human beings, some have faith in the Buddha's word, some have little faith, and there are also innumerable evil yakṣas who have no faith in the Buddha's word and who trouble sentient beings, making them uneasy. Excellent, World-Honored One! The Āṭānāṭiya Sūtra can serve as a bright protection. If there are bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, upāsikās, and all devas and humans who accept, uphold, read, recite, revere, and make offerings to it, and who broadly explain it for others, it can guard and protect them all, bringing them auspiciousness." At that time, among the assembly there were yakṣas of right faith who joined their palms and said: "May the Deva-king please expound this sūtra. We wish to hear it." Then Vaiśravaṇa Deva-king silently accepted the request, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet before him, and empowered by the Buddha's majestic power, addressed the yakṣas:

"If this sūtra is obtained and broadly circulated, it can remove all afflictions of sentient beings. Therefore I now take refuge and bow in homage.

"In the eastern world there is a gandharva lord named Dhṛtarāṣṭra, endowed with great majestic power, his body radiating light like the rising sun illuminating all the world. He commands his retinue of gandharva hosts, who reverently surround him, singing, dancing, and playing music in bliss. He has ninety-one sons, all named Indra, of great power, courageous and fierce. Seeing the Buddha, the World-Honored One, they take refuge, bow, and pay reverence. Observe how these non-human beings are able to offer their reverence! That Dhṛtarāṣṭra Deva-king guards the eastern quarter. As the Buddha practices, so does he protect the world. Therefore I now bow my head in homage to the Perfectly Omniscient One, Perfect in Knowledge and Conduct, Supremely Serene.

"In the southern world there is a kumbhāṇḍa lord named Virūḍhaka, endowed with great majestic power, his body possessing light like the sun illuminating the world, and also like the great ocean, deep and vast beyond measure, which ordinary beings cannot fathom. He commands his retinue of kumbhāṇḍa hosts, who reverently surround him, singing, dancing, and playing music in bliss. He has ninety-one sons, all named Indra, of great power, courageous and fierce. Seeing the Buddha, the World-Honored One, they take refuge, bow, and pay reverence. Observe how these non-human beings are able to offer their reverence! That kumbhāṇḍa lord guards the southern quarter. As the Buddha practices, so does he protect the world. Therefore I now bow my head in homage to the Perfectly Omniscient One, Perfect in Knowledge and Conduct, Supremely Serene.

"In the western world there is a great nāga lord named Virūpākṣa, of great majestic power, his light shining far. He commands his retinue of great nāga hosts, who reverently surround him, singing, dancing, and playing music in bliss. He has ninety-one sons, all named Indra, of great power, courageous and fierce. Seeing the Buddha, the World-Honored One, they take refuge, bow, and pay reverence. Observe how these non-human beings are able to offer their reverence! That great nāga lord guards the western quarter. As the Buddha practices, so does he protect the world. Therefore I now bow my head in homage to the Perfectly Omniscient One, Perfect in Knowledge and Conduct, Supremely Serene.

"In the northern world there is a yakṣa named Kubera, of great majestic power, his body-light blazing like a great fire. He commands his retinue of yakṣa hosts, who reverently surround him, singing, dancing, and playing music in bliss. He has ninety-one sons, all named Indra, of great power, courageous and fierce. Seeing the Buddha, the World-Honored One, they take refuge, bow, and pay reverence. Observe how these non-human beings are able to offer their reverence! That yakṣa lord guards the northern quarter. As the Buddha practices, so does he protect the world. Therefore I now bow my head in homage to the Perfectly Omniscient One, Perfect in Knowledge and Conduct, Supremely Serene.

"Furthermore, in the northern world, humans live for a thousand years and do not die prematurely. The land requires no plowing, and people do not toil. Food and drink arise naturally, perfect in color and fragrance, nourishing the sense-faculties and making the body radiant and lustrous. Everywhere there are flowering and fruit-bearing trees, flowing springs, and ponds. One wanders at will, enjoying pleasures like those of the devas. Thus, Dhṛtarāṣṭra in the east, Virūḍhaka in the south, Virūpākṣa in the west, and Kubera in the north each protect the four great continents with their majestic power.

"Furthermore, the gandharva lord has yakṣa-women among his host. Some ride elephants, some ride horses, some ride camels, some ride water-buffaloes, some ride goats, some ride serpents, some ride birds, some ride boys, and some ride girls. With an elephant leading the way, they secretly travel through space to all quarters, transforming in various ways, sovereign and free. They also serve as guardians, invisible to humans. Among the yakṣas whose forms are ugly and of various kinds, they also fly through space like birds, roaming freely and secretly guarding people. Their names are Āṭānāṭa, Kunāṭa, Parikūsanāṭa, Naḍanāḍa, Pūrika, and others, together with the yakṣa-women, all residing in the great city of Āṇakāvatī in the north. Within this great city is a palace, and on its four sides are ninety-nine pools. The waters are very deep and broad, called the 'Earth Pools,' with springs flowing through and also producing rain. There are also many kinds of flowering and fruit-bearing trees, such as kiṃśuka, kakuda, and rakta-mā flowers. The fruits are sweet and delicious, beloved by all. Kalaviṅka birds, peacocks, and various other birds constantly produce wondrous sounds. There is a devaputra named Vīra ('the Courageous'), together with his retinue, who also dwells in this palace guarding the realm."

Then Vaiśravaṇa Deva-king, empowered by the Buddha's compassion, recited the mantra in sequence:

*Tadyathā iri miri irai kiṃṭa-mulaī hirai kiṃṭi-mulaī hana mi hunu rī nu mī kyori gaṃdhari gunu hi nī uki huki svāhā.*

Then Vaiśravaṇa Deva-king, having spoken the mantra, addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! Furthermore, there are great gandharva hosts who are my brothers. Their names are: Yama, Durgama, Dugama, Tamārama, Dumaduma, Dahvamatta, Sattamatta, Maṅgalamatta, Dhanamatta, Nandika, Nīlotpala, Puṇḍarīka, Candrapāṇi, Nīra, Kokila, Saṃjīva, Mukura, Pañcaśikha, Suvarṇa, Kanmana, Viṣukumiri, Hasapati, Vaṇyavati, and Jagatugati. These and other gandharva hosts confuse and trouble all sentient beings. If any of them troubles a sentient being and does not desist, upon hearing this mantra their heads shall split into seven pieces, like the branches of an arjaka tree. The mantra:

*Tadyathā kuṃṭuri kuṃṭura ehi sanā-mantikeśanā-phaṇi tiketiṣṭa-nuṣṭa-mandhu śī svāhā.*

"World-Honored One! If any śrāvaka bhikṣu, bhikṣuṇī, upāsaka, or upāsikā accepts, upholds, reads, recites, reveres, makes offerings to, and broadly explains this sūtra for others, then those gandharvas, and their parents, brothers, sons, daughters, and retinues, will be unable to cause harm. They will always come near, attend, and protect. If any should cause harm, they will immediately lose their power, be unable to attain gandharva samādhi, their heads will split into seven pieces like the branches of an arjaka tree, and they will be unable to dwell in the great city of Āṇakāvatī.

"World-Honored One! If any śrāvaka bhikṣu, bhikṣuṇī, upāsaka, or upāsikā accepts, upholds, reads, recites, reveres, makes offerings to, and broadly explains this sūtra for others, then the piśācas, and their parents, brothers, sons, daughters, and retinues, will be unable to cause harm. They will always come near, attend, and protect. If any should cause harm, they will immediately lose their power, be unable to attain piśāca samādhi, their heads will split into seven pieces like the branches of an arjaka tree, and they will be unable to dwell in the great city of Āṇakāvatī. The mantra:

*Tadyathā yumi yumi raī yumi-rāvatī ye svāhā.*

"World-Honored One! Furthermore, there are kumbhāṇḍa hosts whose names are: Nanda, Upananda, Kṛkeśumahāparaśu, Mahonarada-śahastubhūmi-cāru-marma, Kṛṣṇarohita, Aparāvaraṇa-vimalodhīri-gakaraṇa-gṛjana, Jatirodaśa-nandī-arjunoka-makamaka-elaghana, Kṛyā-gupta-trāya-kāma-rā-bhadrika, Sarvalāṅga-citracū, and others. These kumbhāṇḍa hosts confuse and trouble all sentient beings. If any of them troubles a being, upon hearing this mantra their heads shall split into seven pieces like the branches of an arjaka tree. The mantra:

*Tadyathā siri visiri siri visiri kṛṣṇa-cāgalai karaṇi piyuṃ-galaī svāhā.*

"World-Honored One! If any śrāvaka bhikṣu, bhikṣuṇī, upāsaka, or upāsikā accepts, upholds, reads, recites, reveres, makes offerings to, and broadly explains this sūtra for others, then those kumbhāṇḍa hosts, and their parents, brothers, sons, daughters, and retinues, will be unable to cause harm. They will always come near, attend, and protect. If any should cause harm, they will immediately lose their power, be unable to attain kumbhāṇḍa samādhi, their heads will split into seven pieces like the branches of an arjaka tree, and they will be unable to dwell in the great city of Āṇakāvatī.

"World-Honored One! If any śrāvaka bhikṣu, bhikṣuṇī, upāsaka, or upāsikā accepts, upholds, reads, recites, reveres, makes offerings to, and broadly explains this sūtra for others, then the pretas, and their parents, brothers, sons, daughters, and retinues, will be unable to cause harm. They will always come near, attend, and make offerings. If any should cause harm, they will immediately lose their power, be unable to attain preta samādhi, their heads will split into seven pieces like the branches of an arjaka tree, and they will be unable to dwell in the great city of Āṇakāvatī. The mantra:

*Tadyathā yumi yumi lai yumi-rāvatī ye svāhā.*

"World-Honored One! I shall now speak of the great nāga hosts. Their names are: Nanda, Upananda, Nandī-vāvasukeribhadra, Jāka-mpiyuṃ-galonadhi-karaṇa-indra-vahu-sosvasti, Kā-aśu-kumitāśu-kavipra-muktu-jayampati-prīmandara-citra, Tāra-prītuhma-citra, Prahvahūma-mahāphaṇi-śojayoti-rasumahā, Kṛṣṇa-bhūjaka-mgama-ajapāra-takṣakavāma, Kṛṣṇaśāya-maśuku, and other worldly beings. These nāgas and others trouble sentient beings. Upon hearing this mantra, their heads shall split into seven pieces like the branches of an arjaka tree. The mantra:

*Tadyathā ciri viciri ciri viciri guri gaṃdhari mataṃgī caṇḍalai svāhā.*

"World-Honored One! If any śrāvaka bhikṣu, bhikṣuṇī, upāsaka, or upāsikā accepts, upholds, reads, recites, reveres, makes offerings to, and broadly explains this sūtra for others, then those nāga hosts, and their parents, brothers, sons, daughters, and retinues, will be unable to cause harm. They will always come near, attend, and protect. If any should cause harm, they will immediately lose their power, be unable to attain nāga samādhi, their heads will split into seven pieces like the branches of an arjaka tree, and they will be unable to dwell in the great city of Āṇakāvatī.

"World-Honored One! If any śrāvaka bhikṣu, bhikṣuṇī, upāsaka, or upāsikā accepts, upholds, reads, recites, reveres, makes offerings to, and broadly explains this sūtra for others, then the kaṭapūtanas, and their parents, brothers, sons, daughters, and retinues, will be unable to cause harm. They will always come near, attend, and make offerings. If any should cause harm, they will immediately lose their power, be unable to attain kaṭapūtana samādhi, their heads will split into seven pieces like the branches of an arjaka tree, and they will be unable to dwell in the great city of Āṇakāvatī. The mantra:

*Tadyathā abhi-vahani-vatī ye svāhā.*

"World-Honored One! I shall now speak of the great yakṣa generals. Their names are: Indra, Soma, Varuṇa, Prajāpati, Bharadvāja, Īśāna, Śrīcānana, Kāma, Śreṣṭha, Kuṇḍa-gandha, Nikumbha, Maṇi-rī-maṇi-rī-maṇicāra, Praṇada, Upapañcaka, Sātaghiri, Himavat-pūraṇa, Khadira-kubiṭa-gūpāra-yakṣa, Āṭavaka-unāra-rājuhā, Naritsa-bhacitra-sena, Gaṃdharivu, Dhīrgaśakti-matālaī, Pañcara-gaṇa-sumāna-dhīrga-yakṣa, together with their retinues Triphalaī, Caṭīra-kaṇṭha, and others, and all worldly followers who are their retinue. If any of them troubles a sentient being and does not desist, upon hearing this mantra their heads shall split into seven pieces like the branches of an arjaka tree. The mantra:

*Tadyathā hiri miri hi miri kīrti mulai hiri kīrti mulai oṃ ṭuṃbi ṭuṃbi uṭuṃba uṭuṃbi uki mi ki gunuhi bhuluṃ-gari irī-śi saṃtadanī svāhā.*

"World-Honored One! If any śrāvaka bhikṣu, bhikṣuṇī, upāsaka, or upāsikā accepts, upholds, reads, recites, reveres, makes offerings to, and broadly explains this sūtra for others, then those yakṣas, and their parents, brothers, sons, daughters, and retinues, will be unable to cause harm. If any should trouble them, they will immediately lose their power, be unable to attain yakṣa samādhi, their heads will split into seven pieces like the branches of an arjaka tree, and they will be unable to dwell in the great city of Āṇakāvatī.

"World-Honored One! If any śrāvaka bhikṣu, bhikṣuṇī, upāsaka, or upāsikā accepts, upholds, reads, recites, reveres, makes offerings to, and broadly explains this sūtra for others, then the rākṣasas, and their parents, brothers, sons, daughters, and retinues, will be unable to cause harm. They will always come near, attend, and make offerings. If any should trouble them, they will immediately lose their power, be unable to attain rākṣasa samādhi, their heads will split into seven pieces like the branches of an arjaka tree, and they will be unable to dwell in the great city of Āṇakāvatī. The mantra:

*Tadyathā hiri hiri huru huru yumi yumi rāvatī ye svāhā.*

When Vaiśravaṇa Deva-king had finished expounding this Āṭānāṭiya Sūtra, he bowed at the World-Honored One's feet and withdrew to one side.

At that time, during the night watch, Śākyamuni Buddha addressed the bhikṣu assembly: "It is so! This sūtra possesses great majestic power and can serve as a bright protection. Vaiśravaṇa Deva-king, with innumerable hundreds of thousands of yakṣa retinues, radiated great light that illuminated all the precincts of the Jeta Grove, and spoke this gāthā:

> 'Homage to the Great Fearless One,
> The Perfectly Awakened One, Honored among Two-Legged Beings!
> Though the devas observe with their divine eyes,
> They cannot perceive me.
> Buddhas of past and future,
> Compassionate lords of the three times,
> Each and every Perfectly Omniscient One,
> I now bow in homage to you.'

"Having spoken this verse, he addressed the Buddha: 'There are śrāvaka bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, and upāsikās who in the wilderness, in forests, or beneath trees practice walking meditation, sitting, and reclining. Among the yakṣas and non-human beings, some have faith in the Buddha's word, some have little faith, and there are those who are extremely evil and faithless, who trouble sentient beings and make them uneasy. This sūtra is expounded for the benefit and happiness of all beings. You bhikṣus, listen well, listen well! This sūtra is true and possesses great majestic power, able to rescue and protect, so that even devas and humans are constantly protected in secret. You should accept and uphold it, and broadly circulate it.'"

When these words were spoken, devas, nāgas, yakṣas, humans, non-humans, and all others greatly rejoiced, faithfully accepted, and put them into practice.
