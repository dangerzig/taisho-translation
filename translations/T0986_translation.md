# 大金色孔雀王呪經
## Sūtra of the Mantra of the Great Golden Peacock King

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0986

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown

---

The Buddha told Ānanda: "In the past, to the south of the Himālaya King, there was a golden peacock king. The Buddha dwelt therein. By means of this Sūtra of the Great Peacock King Mantra, reciting it in the morning for self-protection brought peace throughout the day, and reciting it in the evening for self-protection brought safety throughout the night." Then he spoke the mantra:

"*Hulu hulu hulu hulu hulu hulu hulu hulu hulu hulu riri riri riri riri taṃbha-riri riri vimā-riri riri huye huye vijā vijā yūsu-kulū irā-mirā iri-mirā ciri-mirā iri-mirā iri-mi-tiri hiri-ciri-mi-ti-re taṃ-de-śu taṃ-de-tu-śu tu-gūra-jirā-cā-sāra-virā-cā ici-ri vi-ci-ri ci-ri*"

"Ānanda! I will now teach you the heart of the Great Peacock King Mantra King." Then he spoke the mantra:

"*Ici-ciri-bhiri-miri-miri-ci-mi-sū taṃ-bhiṃ-taṃ-bhiṃ jiri-ki jiri-ki ciya-virā-na mi-jī namaḥ sarva-buddhānāṃ ji-ki-ci-pata-mū-re ici-hāra-rū-he-tai mū-ra-tai mū-ra-tū-sā-aṃba-kūci-kū-nā-ci kū-kū-nā-ci-ra kū-jā-nā-ci aṭa-bhāta-yā-na bha-mā-sā-da-śā-mā ci-te iri-miri-kiri-miri khi-rā-rā mi-ri khitu-mū-re tūtū-mā-sa-tū-mi-cī-ta ri-mi-sa-tū bha-te mū-sa-te bhū-sa-ra bhū-sa-ra bhū-sa-ra bhū-sa-ra inā-bha-si-tā-bha-khi-nā kā-ra-nā-rā-mā riṃ-mi-kha-rā bhā-rā-mā kha-re iti-sa-jā-re taṃ-i-ki anā-ti bha-nā-ti anā-nā-nā-ti*"

"To cause the heavens to quickly send down rain, circumambulate, establish the boundary, and read and recite seven times." Then he spoke the mantra:

"*Nārāyaṇa-bha-nāyaṇa ari-tā-ri kūta-ri iri-mi-siti-ki-ti ri-mi-siti-mi-si iri-mi. The mantra is accomplished. Auspiciousness.*"

"Ānanda! This Great Peacock Mantra King was taught in accordance by Maitreya Bodhisattva." Then he spoke the mantra:

"*Śrī śrī śrī śrī bhadra-śuśu-ci bha-tire hare hare hare hare ni-taṃ-te śā-bha-re śi-de-śu rā-bha-ni śu-rā-bha-ni bodhi bodhi bodhi bodhi bodhi satva-bodhi bha-ri-cā-ra-ni ye svāhā*"

"Ānanda! This Great Peacock Mantra King was taught in accordance by the lord of this sahā world, the Brahma King." Then he spoke the mantra:

"*Here here here here hare miri-māri-ni ba-kuri kiri kiri kiri kiri kiri di-brahma-māra-ye kūlaṃḍha-khi vi-ṭū-ha bhū-ci bhū-śu bhū-śu bhū-śu bhū-śu bhū-śu svāhā*"

"Ānanda! This Great Peacock Mantra King was taught in accordance by Śakra, Lord of the Devas." Then he spoke the mantra:

"*Dhāra-jāntu-re māra-jāntu-re cā-ci-jāntu-tū-re mā-tha-ni kā-ta-ni ga-bha-ni here-śiri-jū-ti śiri-da-liū-nā si-ha ha ha ha ha ha si-he ci-ti vi-chi-te kū-liū-bhāra-śe tū-ṭa tū-ṭa si-ba-ṭa ba-ṭa si siri siri ka-bhire ka-bhirā-ci-re ha he hu*"

"Causing all evil and malice to be entirely subdued, seizing their hands, feet, limbs, and joints so they cannot move, up to the Trāyastriṃśa Heaven."

"*Ūci-jāri śurā-bha-ti bha-ti bha-śirā bha-śirā bha-śirā bha-śirā bha-śirā bha-śirā bha-ti svāhā*"

"Ānanda! Receive the names of the ancient ṛṣis who have emerged. Their accomplishments are final and auspicious: accomplishment of aspiration, accomplishment of mantras. They constantly practice austerities and dwell in mountain forests. Their powers are sovereign, their transformations swift. They possess the five supernatural powers, travel through empty space at will. I will now speak their names. They are: the great ṛṣi Eight Liberations; the great ṛṣi Cā; the great ṛṣi Cā-deva; the great ṛṣi Pāri; the great ṛṣi Sun-Gift; the great ṛṣi Good-Reaching; the great ṛṣi Overturned-Abiding; the great ṛṣi Kāśyapa; the great ṛṣi Elder Kāśyapa; the great ṛṣi Wrinkled-Brow; the great ṛṣi Mountain-Supporter; the great ṛṣi Joy-Divider; the great ṛṣi Named; the great ṛṣi Former; the great ṛṣi Deer-Head; the great ṛṣi Jāla; the great ṛṣi Twice-Ripened; the great ṛṣi Black Twice-Ripened; the great ṛṣi Pure-Gatherer; the great ṛṣi Mountain-Caller; the great ṛṣi Patience; the great ṛṣi Renown; the great ṛṣi Lion; the great ṛṣi Boat; the great ṛṣi Dove; the great ṛṣi Horse; the great ṛṣi Snow; the great ṛṣi Red-Eye; the great ṛṣi Difficult-Deeds; the great ṛṣi Vijñāpāya; the great ṛṣi Armor; the great ṛṣi Autumn; the great ṛṣi Venus; the great ṛṣi Jupiter; the great ṛṣi No-Dispute; the great ṛṣi Dispute-Night-Walker; the great ṛṣi Awakened; the great ṛṣi Bound-Finger-Fire; the great ṛṣi Incense-Holder; the great ṛṣi One-Horn; the great ṛṣi Ṛṣi-Horn; the great ṛṣi Gārgya; the great ṛṣi Daṇḍāyana; the great ṛṣi Head; the great ṛṣi Boundary-Divider; the great ṛṣi Kapila; the great ṛṣi Gautama; the great ṛṣi Red-Horse; the great ṛṣi Hair-Cluster; the great ṛṣi Joyless; the great ṛṣi Kinīlu. Ānanda! These are the ancient great ṛṣis who composed the four Vedas. All their works were accomplished at will. Their austerities were strict, swift, and of great awesome power. All they undertook was accomplished. They also, by means of this Sūtra of the Great Peacock King Mantra, protected so-and-so, enabling them to live a hundred years and see a hundred autumns." Then he spoke the mantra:

"*Hari hari hari chiri miri śuri śuri heri heri miri miri dha-dhū dha-cha-dhū ga-bha-ni mā-tha-ni ta-ha-ni da-ha-ni pā-ca-ni pā-ca-ni pā-ta-ni anā-ni ta-rā-cā-ra cā-ra-ni pā-ṭa-ni mo-ha-ni taṃ-bha-ni yaṃ-bha-ni svāhā*"

"The Four Heavenly Kings and all the great ghost-spirit kings also protect so-and-so by means of the Peacock King Mantra, enabling them to live a hundred years and see a hundred autumns." Then he spoke the mantra:

"*Ha-kā-ti vi-kā-ti hūra-ni hūra-ni ā-ra-ni hu-khi hu-khi bhu-khi bhu-khi ha-nai ha-nai ha-nai ha-nai ha-nai ha-nai ha-nai ha-nai ha-nai. Remove their enemies.*"

"*Daha daha daha daha daha daha daha daha daha daha. Those who harbor evil thoughts toward so-and-so, subdue them all.*"

"*Paca paca paca paca paca paca paca paca paca paca. Those who seek an opportunity to harm so-and-so, subdue them all.*"

"*Du du du du du du du. Extinguish so-and-so's enemies and evils.*"

"*Ha ha ha ha ha ha ha ha jāti jāti jāti jāti jāti jāti jāti jāti jāti. Extinguish their enemies and evils.*"

"*Culu culu culu culu culu culu culu culu culu culu heri heri heri heri heri heri heri heri heri heri miri miri miri miri miri miri miri miri miri miri hūlu hūlu hūlu hūlu hūlu hūlu hūlu hūlu hūlu hūlu citi citi citi citi citi citi citi citi citi citi he-khi he-khi ji-khi mā-khi.* Obtain worthy auspiciousness. Samantabhadra accomplishes all deeds. Vimala, Pure-and-Wondrous, Moon-Worthy, Sun-Beloved, Taṃdetu, Taṃde, Viṣaṅkare: protect so-and-so, enabling them to live a hundred years and see a hundred autumns. The Maṇi-rāja-tana Sūtra, the Mahāgandhara Bhikṣu, the Peacock King Mantra that saves from the suffering of illness. Remove their enemies."
