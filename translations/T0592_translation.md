# 天請問經
## Sūtra of a Deva's Questions

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0592

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Xuanzang (玄奘)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Bhagavān was in the land of Śrāvastī, dwelling in the Jeta Grove of the Anāthapiṇḍada Garden. At that time there was a deva of exceedingly beautiful countenance who, after the night had passed, came to the Buddha's presence, bowed at the Buddha's feet, and stood to one side. The deva's majestic radiance was great and brilliant, illuminating the entire Jeta Grove.

Then the deva addressed the Buddha in a fine gāthā:

> "What is a sharp sword?
> What is a bitter poison?
> What is a blazing fire?
> What is the deepest darkness?"

Then the World-Honored One replied to the deva in a gāthā:

> "Harsh speech is a sharp sword.
> Craving is a bitter poison.
> Anger is a blazing fire.
> Ignorance is the deepest darkness."

The deva asked again:

> "Who is said to gain profit?
> Who is said to lose profit?
> What is the strongest armor?
> What is the sharpest weapon?"

The World-Honored One replied:

> "The giver gains profit.
> The receiver loses profit.
> Patience is the strongest armor.
> Wisdom is the sharpest weapon."

The deva asked again:

> "What is a thief?
> What is the wise one's treasure?
> Who among devas and worldly beings
> Is said to be the greatest robber?"

The World-Honored One replied:

> "Wrong thinking is a thief.
> Moral conduct is the wise one's treasure.
> Among all devas and worldly beings,
> One who breaks the precepts is the greatest robber."

The deva asked again:

> "Who is the most at ease?
> Who has the greatest wealth?
> Who is always dignified?
> Who is always ugly?"

The World-Honored One replied:

> "One with few desires is most at ease.
> One who is content has the greatest wealth.
> One who keeps the precepts is always dignified.
> One who breaks the precepts is always ugly."

The deva asked again:

> "Who is a good companion?
> Who is a hostile enemy at heart?
> What is the most extreme suffering?
> What is the foremost happiness?"

The World-Honored One replied:

> "Merit is a good companion.
> Wrongdoing is a hostile enemy at heart.
> Naraka is the most extreme suffering.
> The unborn is the foremost happiness."

The deva asked again:

> "What is loved but not fitting?
> What is fitting but not loved?
> What is the most feverish illness?
> Who is the great physician?"

The World-Honored One replied:

> "Desires are loved but not fitting.
> Liberation is fitting but not loved.
> Greed is the most feverish illness.
> The Buddha is the great physician."

The deva asked again:

> "Who can cover the world?
> By whom is the world beguiled?
> Who causes one to forsake friends and kin?
> Who obstructs rebirth in heaven?"

The World-Honored One replied:

> "Ignorance covers the world.
> The world is beguiled by delusion.
> Miserliness and greed cause one to forsake friends and kin.
> Attachment obstructs rebirth in heaven."

The deva asked again:

> "What cannot be burned by fire,
> Nor shattered by the wind,
> Nor rotted by the water,
> And can buoy up the world?
> Who can contend against kings and thieves
> With valor and courage,
> And not be attacked and seized
> By humans or non-humans?"

The World-Honored One replied:

> "Merit cannot be burned by fire,
> Nor shattered by the wind.
> Merit cannot be rotted by water,
> And it can buoy up the world.
> Merit can contend against kings and thieves
> With valor and courage,
> And cannot be attacked and seized
> By humans or non-humans."

The deva asked again:

> "I still have a doubt;
> I ask the Buddha to remove it.
> From this world to the next,
> Who deceives themselves the most?"

The World-Honored One replied:

> "One who has much precious wealth
> Yet cannot cultivate merit:
> From this world to the next,
> Such a one deceives themselves the most."

At that time the deva, having heard the Buddha, the World-Honored One, speak this sūtra, was filled with joy and delight. He exclaimed that it was unprecedented, bowed at the Buddha's feet, and right before the Buddha suddenly vanished.
