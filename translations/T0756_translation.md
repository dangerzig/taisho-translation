# 佛說八無暇有暇經
## Sūtra of the Eight Inopportune and Opportune Conditions, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0756

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Yijing (義淨)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Bhagavān was in the city of Śrāvastī, in the Jeta Grove of Anāthapiṇḍada, together with a great assembly of bhikṣus and humans and devas.

At that time the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus: "You should know that in this world, the ignorant, unlearned, ordinary people constantly speak of 'inopportune conditions' and 'opportune conditions,' yet they do not understand what is inopportune and what is opportune. I shall now explain and reveal this to you. Listen carefully and reflect well upon it.

"If sentient beings wish to dwell in the holy practice and cultivate wholesome dharmas, there are eight conditions that are inopportune for cultivation. What are the eight? You should know:

"In this world, a Great Teacher appears, namely, a Tathāgata, Worthy One, Perfectly Awakened One, Complete in Knowledge and Conduct, Well-Gone One, Knower of the World, Unsurpassed One, Tamer of Men, Teacher of Devas and Humans, a Buddha, a World-Honored One, who teaches the wondrous Dharma realized by all the Buddhas, skillfully removing afflictions, leading to bodhi, ultimate nirvāṇa, and the end of all suffering. While this Dharma is being taught, a person has fallen into the hells and is undergoing great suffering. This is the first inopportune condition for one who wishes to dwell in the holy practice.

"Furthermore, bhikṣus, in this world a Great Teacher appears, complete with the ten titles, who teaches the wondrous Dharma realized by all the Buddhas, skillfully removing afflictions, leading to bodhi, ultimate nirvāṇa, and the end of all suffering. While this Dharma is being taught, a person has fallen among the hungry ghosts and is undergoing great suffering. This is the second inopportune condition for one who wishes to dwell in the holy practice.

"Furthermore, bhikṣus, in this world a Great Teacher appears, complete with the ten titles, who teaches the wondrous Dharma realized by all the Buddhas, skillfully removing afflictions, leading to bodhi, ultimate nirvāṇa, and the end of all suffering. While this Dharma is being taught, a person has fallen among the animals and is undergoing various sufferings. This is the third inopportune condition for one who wishes to dwell in the holy practice.

"Furthermore, bhikṣus, in this world a Great Teacher appears, complete with the ten titles, who teaches the wondrous Dharma realized by all the Buddhas, skillfully removing afflictions, leading to bodhi, ultimate nirvāṇa, and the end of all suffering. While this Dharma is being taught, a person has been born in the long-life heavens, without any knowledge or awareness. This is the fourth inopportune condition for one who wishes to dwell in the holy practice.

"Furthermore, bhikṣus, in this world a Great Teacher appears, complete with the ten titles, who teaches the wondrous Dharma realized by all the Buddhas, skillfully removing afflictions, leading to bodhi, ultimate nirvāṇa, and the end of all suffering. While this Dharma is being taught, a person has been born in a border region, among the low and base mlecchas, who do not know good from evil. They neither hear of nor see my four assemblies. This is the fifth inopportune condition for one who wishes to dwell in the holy practice.

"Furthermore, bhikṣus, in this world a Great Teacher appears, complete with the ten titles, who teaches the wondrous Dharma realized by all the Buddhas, skillfully removing afflictions, leading to bodhi, ultimate nirvāṇa, and the end of all suffering. While this Dharma is being taught, a person, though born in the central lands, has received an evil retribution and is deaf, blind, mute, and must use hands to communicate. Being unable to distinguish good from evil, this is the sixth inopportune condition for one who wishes to dwell in the holy practice.

"Furthermore, bhikṣus, in this world a Great Teacher appears, complete with the ten titles, who teaches the wondrous Dharma realized by all the Buddhas, skillfully removing afflictions, leading to bodhi, ultimate nirvāṇa, and the end of all suffering. While this Dharma is being taught, a person, though born in the central lands, though not deaf, blind, or mute, not needing to use hands to communicate, and able to understand good and evil, nevertheless holds wrong views and believes in inverted doctrines, saying: 'There is no giving, no receiving, no sacrifice, no good or evil karmic conditions, no ripened fruits of retribution, no present life or future life, no parents or family, no spontaneously born beings. In this world there is no arhat, rightly directed and rightly practicing, who in this life or any life, in the present Dharma, attains self-realization, perfect certification, and fully knows: "My births are ended, the holy life has been lived, I will not receive another existence." None of this exists.' Such a person holds extreme wrong views. This is the seventh inopportune condition for one who wishes to dwell in the holy practice.

"Furthermore, bhikṣus, in this world no Great Teacher appears, there is no one with the ten titles. The wondrous Dharma realized by all the Buddhas is not heard. Afflictions are not removed; there is no progress toward bodhi, no arrival at nirvāṇa, no end to suffering. Yet a person, born in the central lands, not deaf, blind, or mute, not needing to use hands to communicate, able to understand good and evil, does not hold wrong views, and says: 'There is giving, there is receiving, there is sacrifice, there are good and evil karmic conditions, there are ripened fruits of retribution, there is a present life and a future life, there are parents and family, there are spontaneously born beings. In this world there are arhats, rightly directed and rightly practicing, who in this life or any life, in the present Dharma, attain self-realization, perfect certification, and fully know: "My births are ended, the holy life has been lived, I will not receive another existence." All of this exists.' Such a person holds extreme right views. However, there is no guide to open the gate of liberation. This is the eighth inopportune condition for one who wishes to dwell in the holy practice.

"Furthermore, bhikṣus, in this world a Great Teacher appears, complete with the ten titles, who teaches the wondrous Dharma realized by all the Buddhas, skillfully removing afflictions, leading to bodhi, ultimate nirvāṇa, and the end of all suffering. While this Dharma is being taught, a person is born in the central lands, and the body received has all sense-faculties complete. The person understands words of good and evil and even holds extreme right views. Bhikṣus, you should know: this person has the opportune condition for cultivation. Bhikṣus, this opportune condition, you have already obtained it. You are born and dwell in the central lands, you have encountered my appearance in the world, you have heard the holy teaching, your sense-faculties are complete. You should give rise to vigor and always spur yourselves on with diligence, cultivating wholesome qualities. In the wholesome Dharma and Vinaya, practice as taught, teach each other in turn, repent before each other, always purify the three kinds of karma, constantly practice the ten wholesome deeds, and do not engage in what is without benefit and thereby invite later regret."

At that time the World-Honored One, wishing to restate this meaning, spoke these verses:

> "I have already taught the eight inopportune conditions;
> May all be born where there is opportunity.
> If born in a difficult place, one cannot hear the sūtras.
> All of you should listen with sincere minds.
> Pressed by the suffering of copper pillars, iron mountains,
> Smoldering ash, excrement, thorn and sword forests:
> Suffering thus in the hells,
> How could one hear the right Dharma there?
> Hunger and thirst piercing the needle-thin throat,
> Rain and river currents becoming fierce fire:
> Suffering thus among the hungry ghosts,
> How could one hear the right Dharma there?
> Ever harboring minds of terror and harm,
> Constantly wishing to devour one another:
> Suffering thus among the animals,
> How could one hear the right Dharma there?
> If in the heavens at the highest peak,
> Born there through the power of former merit,
> Living long but with awareness unclear:
> How could one hear the right Dharma there?
> Born in border regions, base and wretched places,
> Ears never hearing the sound of the Dharma being taught,
> Ignorant, dwelling always among the mlecchas:
> How could one hear the right Dharma there?
> Because of evil karma done in former lives,
> Deaf, blind, mute, with impaired faculties,
> Dull and stupid, a human ox:
> How could such a person hear the right Dharma?
> If a person does not believe in the Three Treasures,
> Saying there are no causes, no fruits, no honored kin:
> Such wrong views destroying the mind,
> How could such a person hear the right Dharma?
> When no Buddha or Great Teacher appears,
> And no wondrous Dharma flows in the world,
> If a person is born in such a dark age:
> How could one hear the right Dharma then?
> If a person is born in an opportune place,
> All eight inopportune faults removed,
> Like a sick person encountering a good physician:
> One should listen to the right Dharma with all one's heart!
> You have already obtained a human body
> And moreover have heard the right Dharma.
> Those who do not attain the holy fruit
> Are mostly born in inopportune places.
> I have taught the eight inopportune conditions;
> These are the difficult places for sentient beings.
> Those who dwell in the opportune condition:
> Such people are rare in the world.
> You have already obtained a human body
> And moreover have heard the right Dharma.
> Those who cherish and protect themselves
> Should eliminate afflictions and pride.
> If, having heard the right Dharma,
> One cannot practice as taught,
> One will cycle through the eight difficult places,
> Enduring all manner of hardship.
> Having left the inopportune places,
> Always seek to hear the right Dharma.
> Amid birth, old age, and death,
> Before long one will find liberation.
> If, having obtained a human body,
> One hears the Dharma yet acts with heedlessness,
> Later one will give rise to regret,
> Like a merchant who has lost his wealth.
> If a person hears what I have taught,
> Knowing what is opportune and what is not:
> Therefore one should exert the mind with diligence
> And rightly cultivate the holy life.
> I speak of the person with clear eyes
> Who skillfully guards against all evil,
> Whose right mindfulness can protect and watch,
> Not following the outflows.
> All latent tendencies severed,
> The great army of Māra conquered,
> One forever transcends the stream of birth and death
> And ascends to the other shore."

When the World-Honored One had spoken this sūtra, all the bhikṣus and the great assembly of humans and devas, hearing what the Buddha had taught, joyfully received it upon their heads, believed it, and practiced accordingly.
