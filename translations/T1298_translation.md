# 十二天供儀軌
## Ritual for Offerings to the Twelve Devas

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1298

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown.

---

At the eight directions of the ritual site, spread kuśa grass, lotus leaves, or other green grass, or smear circular altars forming ten positions. To the left and right of Śakra, place positions for Brahmā Deva and Pṛthivī Deva, making ten with the eight directions. If there is no room within the ritual site, use a clean space in front and establish separate square boundaries. Arrange the eight directions within, and in the center make two positions for Brahmā Deva and Pṛthivī Deva.

For the food to be offered to the ten directional devas, use a mixed porridge: non-glutinous rice, sesame, and mung beans cooked together. Make it supremely clean, pure, and fragrant. Place it in a vessel. At each seat, first place a clean leaf. Go around and place a leaf at each seat in order. First take a clean bottle of water and pour a small amount of fragrant water onto the leaf as an offering. Next, with the middle and ring fingers of the right hand, flick a small amount of fragrant paste. Next, offer one flower and place it on the seat. Next, offer burning incense. Next, offer a censer of Brahmā incense before the seat. The same censer serves all seats. Next, place one ladle of porridge on the leaf as an offering. Next, offer a small wax candle or paper candle, inserting it into the porridge. From fragrant water to candle, empower each with the respective mantra three times. When one position's offerings from water to candle are complete, proceed to the next. The candles must be carefully watched; they must not be allowed to go out before all positions have been served. Several assistants or attendants are needed, each carrying one item to serve. If one tries to carry everything alone, the candles will certainly not last through the entire rite. At each position, add one's specific prayer-words before "svāhā."

Śakra Deva of the east: he rides a white elephant-king, dwelling within five-colored clouds. His body is golden. His right hand holds a triśūla at the heart; his left hand supports his left hip. His left foot hangs down. Three celestial maidens each hold lotus flowers, or trays heaped with various flowers, or trays of blue lotus flowers. The mudrā: the right hand forms a fist placed at the hip. The left hand has five fingers extended straight and touching. Bend the earth-pinky and water-ring fingers at the middle joint. Place the wind-index behind the fire-middle. Bend the space-thumb at the middle joint. The mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ indrāya svāhā.*

Agni Deva of the southeast: he rides a blue ram. He is red flesh-colored, with flames covering his entire body. His right two hands hold a green bamboo staff and a kuṇḍikā; his left two hands hold one palm upward and a rosary. Two celestial maidens hold celestial flowers. To the left and right are ascetic sages. His left foot hangs down, his right foot is tucked. The mudrā: the left hand forms a fist placed at the left hip. The right hand has five fingers extended straight and touching. Bend the space-thumb into the palm. Bend the wind-index at the middle joint, beckoning with "svāhā." The mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ agnaye svāhā.*

Yama Deva of the south: he rides a water buffalo. His right hand holds a human-head banner; his left palm faces upward. Two celestial maidens attend him. Two ghost-messengers hold a sword and a halberd. He is reddish-black. His right foot hangs down. The mudrā: first join the palms. Both earth-pinky fingers bend at the middle joint with backs touching. Both wind-index fingers also bend with backs touching. Press each thumb upon the respective wind-index middle joint to complete. The mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ yamāya svāhā.*

The Rākṣasa Lord Deva of the southwest: he rides a white lion. His body wears armor. His right hand holds a sword standing upright; his left hand has the thumb pressing the middle and little fingers. He is red flesh-colored. Two celestial maidens attend to left and right. Two rākṣasa ghosts hold triśūla halberds. The mudrā: the right hand forms a fist placed at the right hip. The left hand has five fingers extended and touching. Bend the earth-pinky and water-ring fingers at the middle joint. Press the space-thumb tip upon the earth-pinky and water-ring fingernails. The mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ nairṛtaye svāhā.*

Varuṇa Deva of the west: he dwells in the water, riding a turtle. He is pale green. His right hand takes a sword; his left hand holds a nāga-lasso. Upon his crown are five nāgas. Four celestial maidens hold wondrous flowers. The mudrā: the right hand as before. The left hand in a closed fist with the space-thumb not entering the palm. The wind-index finger stands straight, slightly bent at the middle joint. The mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ varuṇāya svāhā.*

Vāyu Deva of the northwest: amid clouds, he rides a musk deer. He wears armor. His left hand supports his hip; his right hand holds a single-pronged-headed lance with a crimson banner attached. Two celestial maidens attend him, along with yakṣa hosts. The mudrā: the right hand as before. The left hand has five fingers extended straight and touching. Bend the earth-pinky and water-ring fingers at the middle joint. The mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ vāyave svāhā.*

Vaiśravaṇa Deva of the north: he sits upon two ghosts. His body wears armor. His left palm cradles a stūpa; his right hand holds a jewel-staff. His body is golden. Two celestial maidens hold jeweled flowers. The mudrā: first join the palms. All ten fingers internally cross. Both water-ring fingers stand erect with tips touching. Both wind-index fingers stand erect, slightly bent. The left space-thumb enters the right palm and presses the left fire-middle fingernail. The right space-thumb passes over the left space-thumb's back, enters the left palm, and presses the right fire-middle fingernail. Both wind-index fingers beckon with "svāhā." The mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ vaiśravaṇāya svāhā.*

Īśāna Deva of the northeast: formerly called Maheśvara Deva, also called Maheśvara. He rides a yellow bull. His left hand holds a kapāla cup filled with blood; his right hand holds a triśūla lance. He is pale blue flesh-colored, with three eyes and a wrathful expression, two fangs protruding upward. He wears a necklace of skulls. Within his crown are two upward-facing crescent moons. Two celestial maidens hold flowers. The mudrā: the right hand forms a fist placed at the right hip. The left hand has five fingers extended straight and touching. Bend the earth-pinky and water-ring fingers at the middle joint. The fire-middle, wind-index, and space-thumb fingers are each slightly separated. The mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ īśānāya svāhā.*

Brahmā Deva above: the mudrā is the right hand forming a fist placed at the right hip. The left hand has five fingers touching and standing slightly bent, raised just slightly above the shoulder. Visualize a lotus. The mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ brahmaṇe svāhā.*

Pṛthivī Deva below: the mudrā is first joining the palms. From the wind-index fingers downward, all four fingertips touch, widely open in front. Both space-thumbs are placed alongside the wind-index fingers. The mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ pṛthivīye svāhā.*

Sūrya Deva: the mudrā is no different from the previous Pṛthivī Deva mudrā, except that both space-thumb tips rest upon the lower joints of the water-ring fingers. The mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ ādityāya svāhā.*

Candra Deva: the mudrā is like the previous Brahmā Deva mudrā, but with the palm turned upward and a pure white moon-form visualized within. The mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ candrāya svāhā.*

The seven luminaries: the mudrā is first joining the palms with all ten fingers touching. Both wind-index and both space-thumb fingers are spread widely apart. The mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ grahā-īśvarīya-prapada-jyotiṣa-māyā svāhā.*

The twenty-eight lunar mansions: the mudrā is first forming a hollow-palmed añjali. Both fire-middle fingers cross externally. Both space-thumbs also cross. The mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ nakṣatra-neṣu-nādīye svāhā.*

Within the eight directions, add two positions: above and below, devas facing each other; luminaries to the east, mansions to the west. All offerings are the same. If one has a specific prayer, one may use them individually as well. In a homa-altar, establish each in their respective direction, visualizing them dwelling there, and also set up positions.

At the center of the altar, install the four-armed Acala: blue flesh-colored, both hands in vajra-fists, with index and little fingers each curved like hooks, placed at both corners of the mouth, with fangs. His right hand holds a sword standing upright; his left holds a lasso. He sits in half-lotus, right leg pressing left, upon a rock, with blazing radiance covering his entire body like fire.

For the present diagram of positions, the text simply establishes circular altars.

At the time of practice, one should construct them. One should face north to perform the rite.

Alternatively, the Five Great Vidyā-Kings may be depicted at the central platform.

The sequence of the twelve devas, with Acala and the Five Great Vidyā-Kings as lords of the twelve devas. After the twelve devas, one should make offerings to the consorts of the devas, then to the named spirits of the local land.
