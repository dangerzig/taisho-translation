# 增慧陀羅尼經
## Sūtra of the Dhāraṇī for Increasing Wisdom

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1372

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dānapāla (施護)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Bodhisattva Mahāmati was dwelling on the summit of Mount Sumeru. At that time, various sons of the devas came to the Bodhisattva, reverently circumambulating him and listening to the Dharma. Then a bodhisattva named Kumārarūpa joined his palms in reverence and addressed the Bodhisattva Mahāmati: "We humbly beseech your compassion: for our sake, please expound the Dhāraṇī for Increasing Wisdom, so that all sentient beings of little wisdom, dull faculties, and dim understanding may hear it and thereby have their wisdom increased."

The Bodhisattva Mahāmati thereupon proclaimed the dhāraṇī, saying:

> *tadyathā oṃ pejo pejo prajñā-vardhani jara jara medha-vardhani dhiri dhiri buddhi-vardhami svāhā*

After the Bodhisattva Mahāmati had spoken this dhāraṇī, he addressed Kumārarūpa: "If sentient beings have dull and inferior wisdom, dim and obscured faculties, and are prone to much forgetfulness, and they arouse a mind of sincere aspiration toward this Dhāraṇī for Increasing Wisdom — receiving and upholding it, reading and reciting it, writing it out and making offerings — such a person will quickly attain vast and great wisdom, with clear memory and no forgetting. If a person recites this mantra seven times or fourteen times and empowers three gě of water, then drinks it at the hour of Mǎo, doing so daily for seven or eight months, that person will naturally be able to memorize a thousand verses per day. Even if one does not drink the water continuously, one will still be able to memorize five hundred verses per day, and wisdom will gradually increase as the faculties become bright and sharp."

At that time, the Bodhisattva Kumārarūpa and all the devas and humans received this teaching with faith and put it into practice.
