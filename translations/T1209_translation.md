# 金剛頂瑜伽降三世成就極深密門
## Extremely Profound Secret Gate of Accomplishment of the Vajraśekhara Yoga Trailokyavijaya

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1209

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

> I take refuge in the holy sovereign lord,
> Samantabhadra Vajrapāṇi,
> Who, to subdue all beings,
> Manifests the Hūṃkāra body.

> Crushing the poison of the three existences,
> Causing immediate realization of bodhi,
> This is the profoundly secret
> Trailokyavijaya Yoga.

> First, relying on the True Reality King,
> Bow, purify, join, bind, and crush.
> Enter and close the Samantabhadra seal,
> Press the heart and announce the original vow.

> Having rejoiced, then also recite this:

*Mahā-krodha-sattva haṃ*

> Upon speaking, the body is at once identical
> With the Trailokyavijaya Vajra.
> The four seals empower, the jewel ablution,
> The garland and armor are prepared to bind.
> The hand-clap is equal to vajra.

> Then enter samādhi.
> Contemplate attentively the syllable *A* in the heart,
> Which becomes a moon disc or sun disc;
> Within it arises a five-pronged vajra.

> Next, invite the honored one and the buddhas
> To enter and empower the samādhi body.
> Then, according to the root teaching,
> The four seals are applied to completion:
> The five jewels, garland, armor, and hand-clap.
> Present the inner and outer offerings,
> Praise, chant, and then recite.

> If performing the complete-endowment method,
> Accomplish the body through the five aspects.
> Again, universally please the original vow.
> The wrathful four seals, five buddhas,
> Jewels, garland, armor, and hand-clap.
> With the Hūṃkāra seal
> Empower the heart while reciting the true word:

*Hūṃ sattva-vajri jvāla-māla-krodha hūṃ phaṭ adhitiṣṭha svāṃ*

> Next, the jewel [seal] with two wind-fingers as jewel,
> The dharma with fire-fingers as lotus,
> The karma with fire raised and wind pressed,
> At forehead, throat, and crown: the four seals.
> For the five buddhas, use Mahāvairocana;
> Within the True Reality Teaching King,
> One attains sovereign mastery in the Vajradhātu.

> Recite this secret mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-jvāla-prapata-kṣāya sarva-tathāgata-mahā-vajra-samaya hūṃ*

> The four buddhas share the four seals.
> The Vajra Buddha true word:

*Hūṃ sattva-vajri jvāla-māla-krodha hūṃ phaṭ abhiṣiñca svāṃ*

> The jewel uses the vajra-jewel.
> The garland and armor seals follow as before.

The garland true word:

*Oṃ vajra-māle nirṛta*

The armor mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-rakṣa nirṛta*

> The four attractions and eight offerings
> Follow the Vajradhātu ritual,
> But manifest in the wrathful mode.

The hook true word:

*Oṃ vajra-kuśa mahā-krodha-kṣāya samayaṃ hūṃ jaḥ*

The lasso true word:

*Oṃ vajra-pāśa mahā-krodha-kṣāya samayaṃ hūṃ hūṃ*

The chain true word:

*Oṃ vajra-sphoṭa mahā-krodha-kṣāya samayaṃ vaṃ*

The bell true word:

*Oṃ vajra-veśa mahā-krodha-kṣāya samayaṃ hoḥ*

The sport true word:

*Oṃ vajra-lāse mahā-krodha-kṣāya samayaṃ hoḥ*

> Again, contemplate the wisdom body before you:
> From one's own heart flows the syllable *A*,
> Which before you becomes a moon disc,
> And a vajra is born therefrom,
> Its five peaks streaming fierce flames.

> Arising three times, "I am pleased,"
> And again venerating the World-Honored One.
> Then summon the buddhas filling space.
> They enter; the vajra-chain [binds them]; rejoice.
> Again announce the mantra's manifest words.

> The vajra then transforms
> Into the Hūṃkāra Vajra:
> Fiercely wrathful upon a moon disc,
> Body streaming clusters of fire-light,
> The entire body dark blue in color.

> The great sovereign deva king
> And his consort Umā serve as his seat.
> See this clearly and distinctly.
> Then apply the four seals,
> And demonstrate the karma mudrā.

> After the full-moon [ablution], the five ablutions,
> Jewel, garland, armor, and hand-clap.
> Present the inner and outer offerings.
> Abiding in the sattva body oneself,
> Twist and hurl the vajra,
> Shaking the great trichiliocosm.

> Recite the one hundred and eight name praise,
> Summoning all the holy and worthy ones.
> Hook to summon, lasso to draw in,
> Chain to fix, bell to delight.
> The four mantras summon the wisdom body
> To enter one's own body, indivisible.

> Again apply the four seals and show the vow.
> After the full-moon [ablution], the five ablutions,
> Jewel, garland, armor, and hand-clap.
> Again present the inner and outer offerings.

> Then rise, stand with body erect.
> Raise the right foot, spin to the left,
> Subduing the arrogant one:
> The great sovereign desire-king.

> Having struck him to the ground,
> With the concentration [left foot] press upon his crown.
> With the wisdom [right foot] tread upon the queen
> Umā's breast.
> To crush his pride,
> Place the foot upon his crown.

> Having been slain and fallen,
> In the pure realm he becomes a buddha.

> While abiding in this samādhi,
> Press with extreme force, stamping the body.
> If the body shakes and releases impurity,
> Know that the honored one has taken you in.

> Concentration and wisdom vajra fists,
> The earth-fingers bind the wind-fingers' backs.
> Circling the body, contemplate the marks and excellences;
> See them clearly as before.
> While circling, recite the foot-hook:

*Oṃ kāna-kṛṣa-yu vajra hūṃ*

The stepping mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-ma hūṃ*

> Again see the moon in one's own heart,
> A vajra with five peaks,
> Within which the upheld vidyā
> Streams and shoots vajra fire.

> Abiding in this great samādhi,
> Uphold the root true word,
> Or the heart one-syllable mantra,
> Or the karma heart-within-the-heart,
> Or the Ṭaki-King true word.

> When the concentration becomes weary,
> Then sit and recite praises,
> Present offerings and make aspirations.
> Then perform the liberation dharma:
> With the original four seals of the dharma,
> At the four places pull them apart.

The heart-level true word:

*Oṃ sattva-vajra-jvāla-māla-krodha hūṃ phaṭ muḥ*

> Again, with the True Reality King,
> Pull apart the four seals.

The heart-level true word:

*Oṃ sattva-vajri hūṃ*

> When the holy assembly has returned to their palaces,
> Apply the jewel, garland, armor, and hand-clap
> To protect this ever-abiding body.
> The four bows, five dedications, and so forth
> Are all the same as in the other teachings.

> The Trailokyavijaya Yoga:
> Both hands seal at the heart,
> The wisdom hand holding a five-pronged vajra.
> The arm is extended as if thrusting downward.
> Next, an arrow; next, a sword held straight;
> The concentration [hand] holds a five-pronged hook above;
> Next, a bow; next, grasping a lasso.
> All are held with arms extended straight.

> The four faces: the front is blue,
> The right is yellow, the left is green,
> The back is red; all are wrathful.
> The great sovereign deva king and queen
> Serve as his seat, as described above.
> The Hūṃkāra Vajra
> Displays such marks and excellences.
