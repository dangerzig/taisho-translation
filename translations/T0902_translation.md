# 總釋陀羅尼義讚
## Comprehensive Explanation and Praise of the Meaning of Dhāraṇī

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0902

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空)

---

Throughout hundreds of thousands of koṭis of asaṃkhyeya kalpas, the Tathāgata accumulated the provisions for bodhi and empowered the syllables of the dhāraṇī-mantra, enabling the suddenly-awakening bodhisattva to accord with these and suddenly accumulate the provisions of merit and wisdom. In the Great Vehicle, there are two kinds of practice on the bodhisattva path leading to the realization of the unsurpassed bodhi-path: practicing according to the pāramitās and attaining Buddhahood, and practicing according to the gate of the three secrets of the mantra-dhāraṇī and attaining Buddhahood. "Dhāraṇī" is a Sanskrit word; in Chinese it is translated as "comprehensive holding." There are four kinds of holding: dharma-holding, meaning-holding, samādhi-holding, and text-holding. These four kinds of holding are mostly expounded in the exoteric Great Vehicle teachings. Dharma-holding: by attaining this holding, one destroys all defiled dharmas and realizes the pure Dharma-realm's stream-of-teaching Dharma. Meaning-holding: by attaining this holding, within the meaning of a single syllable one awakens to the practices of hundreds of thousands of immeasurable sūtras, and can expound them forward and backward with sovereign ease. Samādhi-holding: by this holding, one's samādhi of non-distraction manifests, and one awakens to the gates of immeasurable hundreds of thousands of samādhis. The bodhisattva whose compassion predominates takes birth by vow in the six destinies, yet the afflictions and secondary afflictions cannot damage their samādhi. Through this samādhi one realizes the five supernatural powers and accomplishes the benefit and joy of boundless sentient beings. Text-holding: through upholding this dhāraṇī, one accomplishes what one hears, namely all the sūtras. From all the Tathāgatas and all the bodhisattvas, one hears hundreds of thousands of immeasurable sūtras and never forgets them.

The mantra also possesses four meanings. "True" means corresponding with suchness; "word" means a true designation of meaning. The four meanings are: dharma-mantra, where the pure Dharma-realm serves as the mantra. Meaning-mantra, corresponding with the ultimate meaning, where within each syllable there is the meaning of true reality. Samādhi-mantra, where the yoga practitioner uses this mantra and, upon the mirror-wisdom moon-disc of the mind, arranges the syllables of the mantra, concentrating the mind without disturbance, thereby swiftly realizing samādhi; hence it is called the samādhi-mantra. Text-upholding mantra, from the syllable *oṃ* to *svāhā*, where all the syllables in between are each called a mantra.

It is also called "secret speech," which also possesses four meanings. Dharma-secret-speech: it is not the domain of non-Buddhist, heterodox, or Two-Vehicle practitioners. Only the bodhisattva who practices the mantra path can hear and uphold the ritual procedures, seals, and maṇḍalas. The siddhis sought through practice are called dharma-secret-speech. Meaning-secret-speech: within each syllable of the mantra, only a Buddha and a Buddha, or a great-authority bodhisattva, can fully exhaust its meaning. Samādhi-secret-speech: through the power of correspondence between the syllables of this mantra and samādhi, distributing the mantra throughout the bodily limbs, one transforms the coarse, heavy body and easily obtains a subtle, wondrous form-body, acquires the five supernatural powers, and attains sovereign authority over majestic virtue and an inexhaustible lifespan. Hearing-upholding secret-speech: one secretly receives the three-secret ritual procedures from the teacher. Only the teacher and the disciple themselves know them; they are not made known to others. Even the forms of the principal deity, the seal-mudrās, and the contemplation-gates are all secretly received and upheld. One thereby obtains the samādhi that the mind desires, the hearing-upholding that never forgets, the divine eye, divine ear, and knowledge of others' minds, and the upper, middle, and lower siddhis.

It is also called *vidyā* ("illumination"), possessing four meanings. Dharma-vidyā: when the practitioner recites it, within each syllable a radiance universally illuminates all sentient beings throughout the worlds of the ten directions who are drowning in the ocean of suffering of birth-and-death. All their ignorance and afflictions are shattered, and they all attain liberation from suffering; hence it is called dharma-vidyā. Meaning-vidyā: because the yoga practitioner accords with the meaning of the mantra, one penetrates and clearly understands prajñā-pāramitā and distances oneself from meaningless principles. Samādhi-vidyā: through visualizing the mantra seed-syllable within the moon-disc of the mind, one obtains great radiance and accomplishes the samādhi of illuminating both self and others. Hearing-upholding vidyā: one realizes the hearing-upholding dharma, able to shatter the confusion of forgetting, and realizes the accomplishment of the bodhicitta.

As described above, the meanings of dhāraṇī, mantra, secret speech, and vidyā are based on the Sanskrit texts. Furthermore, in the exoteric sūtras these four designations are proclaimed and taught, and they are also taught in the esoteric mantra teachings. There may be mantras of a single syllable, two syllables, three syllables, up to a hundred, a thousand, or ten thousand syllables, and even beyond these numbers to the immeasurable and boundless: all are called dhāraṇī, mantra, secret speech, and vidyā. If one accords with the gate of the three secrets, without needing many kalpas of difficult and painful austerities, one can transform fixed karma. It is the swift, easy, blissful path to Buddhahood.
