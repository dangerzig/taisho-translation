# 佛說菩薩修行四法經
## Sūtra Spoken by the Buddha on the Four Dharmas for Bodhisattva Practice

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 773

Translated from the Chinese. Tang Dynasty, translated by Divākara (地婆訶羅)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was dwelling in the Jeta Grove of Anāthapiṇḍada, in the land of Śrāvastī, together with a great assembly of bhikṣus, twelve hundred and fifty in all. At that time the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣus: "You have already attained immeasurable wholesome benefit; you should now seek the unsurpassed great bodhi of buddhahood. Why? Because the bodhi of buddhahood is without equal and unequaled throughout worldly and transcendent realms. If sentient beings in future ages give rise to the aspiration to seek the bodhi of buddhahood, they should cultivate four dharmas. What are these four? First, one should give rise to the great mind of bodhi; one should rather lose body and life than regress or turn back. Second, one should draw close to good friends; one should rather lose body and life than draw away from them. Third, one should cultivate patience and gentleness; one should rather lose body and life than give rise to anger and hostility. Fourth, one should rely on a place of stillness and quiet; one should rather lose body and life than long for noise and disturbance. Good sons! These four dharmas are what a bodhisattva-mahāsattva should cultivate and study."

At that time the World-Honored One then spoke these verses:

> All who wish to seek superior fruits should give rise to the mind of bodhi;
> Spurring themselves on diligently in the practice of vigor, they must rely on a kalyāṇamitra.
> Patience is praised by the Buddha; such a person is called one of great strength.
> The secluded wilderness is where the sage dwells; fearless, like a lion.

When the World-Honored One had finished speaking these verses, he further said: "Those who possess wisdom and great compassion and are able to cultivate the four dharmas stated above will be able to liberate themselves from birth and death, escape the net of Māra, attain samyak-saṃbodhi, and achieve great nirvāṇa."

When the World-Honored One had finished speaking this sūtra, the bhikṣus and the bodhisattvas, having heard what the Buddha had said, were all filled with great joy, received it with faith, and put it into practice. They paid reverence and departed.
