# 佛說觀自在菩薩母陀羅尼經
## Mother Dhāraṇī Sūtra of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1117

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmabhadra (法賢).

---

Thus have I heard. At one time the Buddha was in Vaiśālī, together with a great assembly of five hundred bhikṣus including Śāriputra and others, and also great bodhisattvas including Maitreya and others, who reverently surrounded him as he took his seat. At that time there was in the assembly a bodhisattva named Samantabhadra, who rose from his seat, went before the World-Honored One, bared his right shoulder, placed his right knee on the ground, joined his palms toward the Buddha, and said: "World-Honored One! There is a Mother Dhāraṇī of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, a great vidyā chapter and phrases, which has been joyfully proclaimed by the buddha world-honored ones of the past, present, and future. When our buddha World-Honored One was still a bodhisattva, for the sake of attaining samyak-saṃbodhi and benefiting sentient beings, he also proclaimed this dhāraṇī. Furthermore, this dhāraṇī serves as father and mother for good men and good women who seek the bodhisattva path. It is like a refuge for the orphaned, and for those without a sovereign it becomes their sovereign. If any person reads, recites, receives, and upholds this dhāraṇī, all karmic offenses are extinguished, all practices and vows are perfectly fulfilled, and even the cultivation of all the great vidyās is accomplished."

The Buddha told Samantabhadra Bodhisattva: "It is indeed as you say. I recall that long ago, when I was still a bodhisattva, seeking samyak-saṃbodhi, I traversed immeasurable, boundless worlds as numerous as the sands of the Ganges. There was a buddha-land called Sukhāvatī, and in that land there was a buddha named Amitāyus Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha. That Amitāyus Buddha is now present, always expounding the wondrous dharma to benefit sentient beings. I was in that assembly as a brāhmaṇa named Prabhāsvara, and at that time I had already attained realization of the Prabhākarī Bhūmi. In that assembly I had already proclaimed this Mother Dhāraṇī of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, which would provide great benefit for a hundred thousand nayutas of immeasurable, boundless sentient beings. At that time, all the great assemblies present, having heard this dhāraṇī, had their karmic offenses extinguished, and those who had not yet obtained the wisdom of past lives obtained the wisdom of past lives."

At that time the World-Honored One further told Samantabhadra Bodhisattva: "For the benefit of sentient beings in the future age of the declining dharma and the five turbidities, for the sake of a hundred thousand koṭis of immeasurable, boundless sentient beings who have committed the offenses of the unremitting karma, so that their offenses may be extinguished, and so that the orphaned may have a refuge, you wish to joyfully proclaim the Mother Dhāraṇī of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva. Excellent, excellent! You may proclaim it." Samantabhadra Bodhisattva, having received the Buddha's permission, then spoke the Mother Dhāraṇī of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva:

*Namo bhagavate amitābhāya tathāgatāya arhate samyak-saṃbuddhāya namaḥ ārya-avalokiteśvarāya bodhisattvāya mahāsattvāya mahā-kāruṇikāya te bhyūna namaḥ skṛtva idaṃ ārya-avalokiteśvara-mataṃ mahā-dhāraṇī-mantra-padāni saṃpravakṣyāmi imi vismṛdantu tatha imi mire ciri mire kundrā kundre śrī śī śrī gori gatūri matangi pukaśi kaṭaya mahā-vihunta-māle nīcire nīcire guhya saumya dṛśani dṛga-mānasya-mani ārya-nakṣatra-pūṇa sarva-vighna-praviśāyami sarva-nime-śūra svāhā*

At that time, after Samantabhadra Bodhisattva had spoken this dhāraṇī, he addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! If furthermore any person wholeheartedly receives and upholds this, all karmic offenses will be extinguished. In all times of fear, one will always be protected. If any practitioner wishes to see me and all the accomplished ones, they should redouble their diligence with an uninterrupted mind, and three times each day concentrate in recitation. When seven days are complete, I shall have compassion for that person and appear in my true body. Furthermore, if a practitioner thus concentrates with an uninterrupted mind, silently reciting for one month, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva will appear in his true body, grant what is wished, and even cause one to see Amitāyus Buddha. Learning and hearing will increase, one will realize the wisdom of past lives, and wherever one is born one will never be apart from the buddha-dharma. One will possess great wealth and honor, and will ultimately attain the stage of non-retrogression."

When the Buddha had finished speaking this sūtra, Samantabhadra Bodhisattva Mahāsattva and all the bodhisattvas, śrāvakas, and the entire assembly, hearing what the Buddha taught, all greatly rejoiced, accepted it with faith, and put it into practice.
