# 佛說華積陀羅尼神呪經
## The Buddha's Teaching: Puṣpakūṭa Dhāraṇī Divine Mantra Sūtra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1356

Translated from the Chinese. Wu dynasty, translated by Zhi Qian (支謙).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was in the palace of the Nāga King at Lake Anavatapta, together with five hundred great bhikṣus and a full thousand bodhisattva mahāsattvas. All had attained all dhāraṇīs, had but one more birth remaining, dwelt on the tenth bhūmi, and were next to become buddhas, like crown princes. Each of them, throughout the ten directions, had accomplished the path of buddhahood. They were endowed with great adornments and harbored no doubts about the merits of the Tathāgata. The devas, nāgas, and eight classes of beings all assembled together at the same time.

Then Siṃhavikrīḍita Bodhisattva rose from his seat, placed his right knee on the ground, joined his palms toward the Buddha, and addressed him: "World-Honored One! If good men and good women cultivate offerings to the Tathāgata, will their merit be great?" The Buddha addressed Siṃhavikrīḍita: "Do not ask such a question as 'Will the merit of good men and good women who cultivate offerings to the Tathāgata be great?' Why? Because the Tathāgata is accomplished in immeasurable discipline, concentration, wisdom, liberation, and the knowledge-and-vision of liberation. If good men and good women cultivate offerings to the Tathāgata, whether he is living or has passed away, Siṃhavikrīḍita, know that such persons will certainly, within the three vehicles, attain liberation according to their causes and conditions.

"Furthermore, Siṃhavikrīḍita! If a person sees the Buddha, the World-Honored One, gives rise to joy, honors and praises him, and presents various offerings to the Tathāgata; and if another person, after the Tathāgata's parinirvāṇa, sees his relics and gives rise to joy: the merit of these two is equal, without any difference.

"Furthermore, Siṃhavikrīḍita! If a lay bodhisattva piles up precious jewels as high as Mount Sumeru and makes offerings to śrāvakas and pratyekabuddhas, this does not equal the merit of a renunciant bodhisattva who, having aroused the mind of enlightenment, offers a single gold coin to the Tathāgata.

"Furthermore, Siṃhavikrīḍita! If a person for thousands of myriads of years personally attends the World-Honored One and fully cultivates offerings; and if another person, after the Tathāgata's parinirvāṇa, arouses the mind of enlightenment, takes a single stick of incense and a single flower, enters a saṃghārāma, and with each step raised and lowered says, 'Homage to the World-Honored One,' and with this profound understanding and earnest, devout mind makes offerings to a stūpa of the Tathāgata's relics, not seeking reward, not with doubt: until they attain liberation, for thousands of myriads of koṭis of kalpas they will not fall into the evil destinies.

"Siṃhavikrīḍita! Listen carefully. There is a dhāraṇī called Puṣpakūṭa. I shall now teach it, for the great benefit of devas and humans. If a good man is able to receive, uphold, read, recite, draw near to, and practice in accordance with the Puṣpakūṭa Dhāraṇī Mantra, his merit will surpass the above. Such a person, life after life, will obtain the power of hearing once and remembering forever. He will not fall into evil, perilous, or thorny paths, and will be free from all difficulties. He will constantly see wondrous jewels, constantly see the buddhas, and his faculties will always be complete. He will not be born into lowly or servile families. He will constantly possess the bodhisattva's vast resolve without departing from it, and will constantly obtain various immeasurable eloquence of wisdom. He will be known and seen by the innumerable buddhas and tathāgatas of the ten directions." He then spoke the mantra:

*Tadyathā: tu-rā-ṇi tha-rā-ṇi tha-rā-ni tha-rā-ni mu-ni pra-bha-sā-ṇi siddhi caṇḍī-na mu-ji nī-ha-rī ru-ga-bha-ti buddha-bha-ti ti-rī-ka-rā ji-rā-su pa-ga-ji ji-tu-bha-ti viśa-rā buddhi dharma-bha-si akṣaya ka-pī-ka-pa vi-ga-ji a-mi-ta-ka-pa kṣu-ta da-ta-ṇi jā-pa-ti tā-nī-sa-ma hi-ji śu-ka-rā-bha-ti ci-ṣa-ṇā buddha-buddhi-ya buddhi svāhā.*

"Siṃhavikrīḍita! Know that if a good man is able to read or recite this Puṣpakūṭa Divine Mantra, then during the third, fourth, and ninth months, from the eighth day to the fifteenth, he should single-mindedly contemplate the marks and excellent features of the Tathāgata. At night he should recite the Puṣpakūṭa Divine Mantra three times, and during the day likewise three times. When the full moon arrives, he should offer incense, flowers, lamps, and candles before an image and recite the Puṣpakūṭa Dhāraṇī Mantra. That very night he will dream of the Tathāgata, endowed with all marks and excellent features, seated on a lotus throne teaching the Dharma for the assembly. He will also obtain the Puṣpakūṭa Dhāraṇī Mantra. In his present life he will constantly possess a strong memory, deep faith and wisdom. From this present body until nirvāṇa, he will constantly possess the power of hearing once and remembering forever. All that he hears and sees, all sūtra-teachings, all learning and understanding, and all arts and skills, he will penetrate without obstruction. In all samādhis he will attain profound purity and will understand the Four Noble Truths and the unsurpassed Dharma-wheel."

When this sūtra had been spoken, Siṃhavikrīḍita and the devas, nāgas, the eight classes of beings, and the entire great assembly joyfully received it and put it into practice.
