# 大威儀請問
## Questions on Great Dignified Conduct

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 2884

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown (失譯)

---

If a good man or good woman handles a scripture without first washing their hands with soap powder, or sleeps before it, or prostrates on the ground before it, they will spend three hundred billion ages as insects in latrines. If one touches a scripture with unwashed hands while scratching an itch, or speaks carelessly in the presence of a scripture, one will fall into the dark hell for eight hundred million billion ages, and be reborn among foxes and hares for two hundred million ages. Spitting before a scripture, or holding objects in one's mouth while wearing amulets near it, incurs the same offenses as those described above.
