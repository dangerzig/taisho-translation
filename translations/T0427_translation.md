# 佛說八吉祥神呪經
## Sūtra of the Eight Auspicious Ones' Divine Dhāraṇī Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0427

Translated from the Chinese. Wu dynasty, translated by Zhi Qian (支謙)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the city of Rājagṛha, on Vulture Peak Mountain, together with one thousand two hundred and fifty bhikṣus and one thousand bodhisattvas, all of the rank of Maitreya and his peers.

The Buddha told the Worthy Śāriputra and all the bhikṣus: "Listen with single-minded attention!"

The Buddha told the Worthy Śāriputra: "In the east, beyond a distance of one Ganges-sand of buddha-lands, there is a Buddha named Tranquil and Entrusting Abundantly Fulfilled King Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, teaching the Dharma. His world is called Fulfilling All Wishes.

"Beyond a distance of two Ganges-sands, there is a Buddha named Dark Lapis Lazuli Abundantly Fulfilled King Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, teaching the Dharma. His world is called Compassionate Radiance.

"Beyond a distance of three Ganges-sands, there is a Buddha named Encouraging All Goodness Abundantly Fulfilled King Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, teaching the Dharma. His world is called Joyful Happiness.

"Beyond a distance of four Ganges-sands, there is a Buddha named Free from Sorrow Virtue Abundantly Fulfilled King Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, teaching the Dharma. His world is called All Joy Entering.

"Beyond a distance of five Ganges-sands, there is a Buddha named Medicine Master Abundantly Fulfilled King Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, teaching the Dharma. His world is called Fully Endowed with All Precious Dharma.

"Beyond a distance of six Ganges-sands, there is a Buddha named Lotus Abundantly Fulfilled King Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, teaching the Dharma. His world is called Fully Endowed with Fragrant Renown.

"Beyond a distance of seven Ganges-sands, there is a Buddha named Computation and Assembly Abundantly Fulfilled King Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, teaching the Dharma. His world is called All Explaining Sounds Heard Afar.

"Beyond a distance of eight Ganges-sands, there is a Buddha named Releasing All Bonds Abundantly Fulfilled King Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, teaching the Dharma. His world is called All Liberation."

The Buddha told the Worthy Śāriputra: "These Buddhas, Tathāgatas, Arhats, who have passed beyond the four paths and do not cling, Samyaksaṃbuddhas: their lands are pure, without the five turbidities, without craving and desire, without mental defilements. If there are good men and good women who hear the names of these eight Buddhas and their lands, receive them, uphold them, practice accordingly, recite them, and broadly explain their meaning for others, they will never be deluded, their spoken words will be without error, their marks and characteristics will be complete and without deficiency, and for immeasurable years they will not know scarcity.

"Such persons will never fall into the hells of Mount Tai, nor among hungry ghosts or animals. They will never aspire to the arhat or pratyekabuddha paths and enter nirvāṇa thereby, but will certainly attain the unsurpassed path of equality. They will always encounter dhāraṇī, always practice the bodhisattva path, and attain immeasurable merit.

"Foremost, the four heavenly kings will always protect them. They will not be arrested or imprisoned by the authorities, not harmed by thieves, not troubled by devas, nāgas, ghosts, or spirits. Yakṣa spirits, sorcery spirits, whether human or non-human, none will be able to harm or take advantage of them, except as a consequence of past-life karma already determined.

"If there is illness, or the terrors of water, fire, evil bird-omens, nightmares, or trouble from various māras, and the hair stands on end with fright: one should always read and recite this Sūtra of the Eight Auspicious Ones' Divine Dhāraṇī, and one will immediately be healed."

At that time, the Buddha spoke in verse:

> If one upholds this sūtra,
> With the names of the eight Buddhas' lands,
> One will not fall into the three evil paths,
> And will swiftly attain the unsurpassed path.

> Awakening to the aspiration for the path,
> Upon seeing a Buddha one gains understanding.
> Within and without, one is always joyful,
> Making offerings with a respectful mind.

> Through koṭi asaṃkhyeya kalpas,
> All evil committed is entirely eliminated.
> Upholding these Eight Auspicious Ones,
> One swiftly gains the clear teaching.

> Those who serve this sūtra
> Will be born within thousand-petalled lotus flowers.
> Precious treasures will appear for them,
> And their appearance will be unsurpassedly beautiful.

> Those who hear this honored sūtra
> And receive it with respect and joyful faith,
> Upholding, reciting, and reading it,
> Will be pure and without negligence.

> Women who have faith in this sūtra,
> Respectful and careful, without flattery,
> Will shed the female form and become male,
> Always intelligent and endowed with wisdom.

> Upholding the eight Buddhas' names,
> When going in and out, thieves cannot harm them.
> Weapons, water, fire, and poison,
> And all evil spirits cannot interfere.

> Loving and delighting in this sūtra,
> The māras cannot take advantage of them.
> Ghosts, spirits, and their retinues
> Cannot trouble or disturb them.

> Flying, they reach all the buddha-lands;
> Wherever they go, there is great abundance and joy.
> Their minds are upright and free from deviation;
> Seeing a Buddha, they are greatly joyful.

> In every life they always encounter the Buddhas,
> And serve them with an even mind.
> All evils are eliminated;
> They swiftly attain the path of nirvāṇa.

> Diligent without slackening,
> Distanced from all conditions and attachments,
> Simple, straightforward, and sincere,
> Upholding the eight Buddhas' names.

> Courageous and subduing all the māras,
> Their strength is like vajra.
> Their appearance is beautiful, with complete marks;
> None can match them.

> Generous and without miserliness,
> They are born into families of immense wealth.
> Thieves and enemies
> Naturally all disappear.

> Illness, government prosecution,
> Evil bird-omens and nightmares:
> Upholding these eight Buddhas' names,
> Reciting them, one is immediately healed.

> Those who uphold this sūtra:
> Maitreya and the other bodhisattvas,
> And foremost the four heavenly kings
> Will always together protect them.

> Whatever one wishes, one obtains.
> Leaping with great joy,
> Those who believe with single-minded faith:
> Their merit will also be like this.

At that time, the bodhisattvas: the Bodhisattva Bhadrapāla, the Bodhisattva Ratnaketu, the Bodhisattva Kiaujiḍu, the Bodhisattva Nārada, the Bodhisattva Suśīma, the Bodhisattva Mahāsusaṃvāsa, the Bodhisattva Indradatta, and the Bodhisattva Harmonious Wheel: these eight persons, having sought the path since immeasurable kalpas, have not yet attained Buddhahood, but they have made this vow: "May all the people of the world in the ten directions attain the path of the Buddha. If anyone is in urgent distress, they should call upon the names of us eight persons, and they will immediately be delivered. When their life is about to end, we eight shall fly to them and welcome them."

The bodhisattvas Maitreya and the others, together with the four heavenly kings, all addressed the Buddha: "We shall protect those who uphold the Sūtra of the Eight Auspicious Ones' Divine Dhāraṇī, and with our combined power, cause all illnesses to be healed."

When the Buddha had finished teaching this sūtra, Śāriputra, the Bodhisattva Maitreya, and all the bhikṣus, devas, nāgas, ghosts, spirits, and the asura king all greatly rejoiced at hearing it.
