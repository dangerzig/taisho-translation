# 七佛俱胝佛母心大准提陀羅尼法
## Dharma of the Great Cundī Dhāraṇī of the Heart of the Mother of Seven Koṭi Buddhas

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1078

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Śubhakarasiṃha (善無畏).

---

A separate practice from the independent division.

*Namaḥ saptānāṃ samyak-saṃbuddha-koṭīnāṃ tadyathā oṃ cale cule cundī svāhā*

The mudrā that encompasses the great maṇḍala seals of all twenty-five divisions: cross the ring fingers and little fingers of both hands inward, extend both middle fingers upright touching at the tips, bend both index fingers and press them against the first joint of the middle fingers, and press both thumbs against the middle joints of the ring fingers of the left and right hands. If one wishes to summon, move the index fingers back and forth.

The Buddha said: "This spell and mudrā can extinguish the ten evil deeds, the five heinous crimes, and all grave offenses, accomplishing all white dharmas. One with complete precepts and purity swiftly attains bodhi. Even if a lay person does not give up alcohol, meat, or married life, as long as they practice according to my method, there will be nothing that is not accomplished."

The Buddha said: "If one seeks accomplishment, first follow the altar method. Unlike the elaborate offerings of other divisions, dig the ground and smear it with fragrant clay. Set up a clean mirror that has never been used, and on the fifteenth night of the month, before a buddha image, make offerings according to one's capacity. Burn olibanum incense and set out pure water. First quiet the mind, free from all thought. Then form the mudrā and recite the spell, empowering the mirror one hundred and eight times. Store the mirror in a pouch or case and always carry it on one's person. When one later wishes to practice recitation, simply place this mirror before oneself, form the mudrā, and recite the spell. Using the mirror as the altar, one attains accomplishment."

The Buddha said: "If one wishes to hold this spell, on the fifteenth night, bathe cleanly, put on fresh clean garments, face east, sit in half-lotus posture, place the mirror in front, and set out whatever incense, flowers, clean water, and other items one has. First quiet the mind and cease all thought. Then form the mudrā, press it upon the heart, and recite this spell one hundred and eight times. When this spell is recited, it can cause beings of short lifespan to regain and extend their lives. Even kāmala disease can be cured: how much more so other illnesses! If they are not cured, that is impossible."

The Buddha said: "If a person with single-minded concentration recites to the full count of two hundred thousand, four hundred thousand, or six hundred thousand times, there is no worldly or supramundane dharma that will not be fulfilled as wished."

The Buddha said: "If a lay person, at dawn, rinses the mouth with clean water, before eating any pungent or bloody food, faces east toward the mirror, forms the mudrā, and recites the spell one hundred and eight times, continuing without interruption for forty-nine days, auspicious events will occur. Cundī Bodhisattva will cause two holy ones to constantly accompany that person. Whatever the person thinks in their mind, the holy ones will whisper it all into their ears, one thing after another."

The Buddha said: "For beings of short lifespan and many illnesses, on the fifteenth night of the month, burn olibanum incense, recite the spell and form the mudrā one hundred and eight times. Demons, ghosts, those who have lost their minds, fox-spirits, and malignant diseases will all reveal their true forms in the mirror. One may kill or release them as one wishes, and they will never return again. One's lifespan will be increased immeasurably."

The Buddha said: "If a person lacks the marks of merit and seeks promotion without success, by always reciting this spell before the mirror, merit, fortune, and official advancement will all be attained."

The Buddha said: "If a person wishes to undertake something, first contemplate the holy one Cundī, fix the mind, and recite this spell a full seven times. Sit upright, and after a brief moment the body will naturally sway. From this one knows that the undertaking will succeed and that it is auspicious. If the body remains stiff and moves forward or backward, one knows it will not succeed and that there will certainly be a calamity."

The Buddha said: "If one wishes to know whether this practice has succeeded or not, practice the spell according to the method for a full seven days. In dreams one will see buddhas and bodhisattvas, flowers and fruits, will vomit black matter and then eat white matter. From this one knows that accomplishment has been attained."

The Buddha said: "This dhāraṇī has great power. If one recites it with utmost sincerity, one will certainly attain self-realization. It can cause a withered tree to bloom: how much more so worldly rewards! If one always recites and upholds it, water, fire, weapons, enemies, and poisons cannot cause harm. If one has suddenly been killed by ghosts or spirits, form the mudrā and recite the spell seven times, pressing the mudrā-seal upon the heart. The person will be restored to life. If a dwelling is plagued by the mischief of ghosts and spirits, empower four blocks of earth with the spell and place them as anchors at the four corners. The spirits will depart."

The Buddha said: "If a person's six kinds of relatives are in discord and do not love or care for one another, recite the spell according to the method. Wherever one goes, harmony will prevail. Upon hearing one's name or seeing one's person, all will give rise to delight. Whatever one seeks will be fulfilled as wished. How much more so for one who observes the ritual fast, maintains complete precepts, and with single-minded purity recites and upholds it according to the method: without leaving this very body, one shall realize bodhi. Such is its great power."

The Buddha said: "If a person desires longevity, in an ancient stūpa, in the deep mountains, or in a pure room, using the mirror as an altar, recite the spell to the full count of two hundred thousand, four hundred thousand, or six hundred thousand times. Burn blue lotus flowers mixed with olibanum incense. In one's dreams one will dream of eating celestial medicines, or of being given a celestial formula. Or in the mirror five-colored light will appear, and within the light there will be medicine. Take and eat it as one wishes, and one shall attain longevity."

The Buddha said: "If one recites and upholds this according to the method with single-minded diligence, the powers it accomplishes are beyond description. This altar-mirror method must not be seen by others. If seen, it will not be effective and will not reach completion. It must be kept secret. This dharma cannot be spoken of; one should verify it through one's own experience. Whatever one desires will be swiftly accomplished, and one will enjoy supreme and wondrous bliss."
