# 般若波羅蜜多心經
## Heart Sūtra of Prajñāpāramitā

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0254

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Prajñācakra (智慧輪)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Bhagavān was dwelling in the city of Rājagṛha, on Vulture Peak Mountain, together with a great assembly of bhikṣus and a great assembly of bodhisattvas. At that time, the World-Honored One entered a samādhi called Vast and Profound Illumination. At that time, within the assembly there was a bodhisattva-mahāsattva named Avalokiteśvara. When practicing the profound prajñāpāramitā, he illuminated the five aggregates and saw that their self-nature is entirely empty.

Then the venerable Śāriputra, empowered by the Buddha's might, joined his palms in reverence and addressed the Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Avalokiteśvara: "Holy One! If there are those who wish to study the practice of the profound prajñāpāramitā, how should they cultivate it?" Having asked thus,

the Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Avalokiteśvara then addressed the venerable Śāriputra: "Śāriputra! If good men and good women wish to practice the profound prajñāpāramitā, they should illuminate the five aggregates and see that their self-nature is entirely empty, and they shall be freed from all suffering and adversity. Śāriputra! Form is empty; the nature of emptiness is seen as form. Form is not different from emptiness; emptiness is not different from form. This form is itself emptiness; this emptiness is itself form. Feeling, perception, volitional formations, and consciousness are likewise. Śāriputra! The nature and characteristics of all these dharmas are empty: not produced and not destroyed, not defiled and not pure, not decreased and not increased. Therefore, in emptiness there is no form, no feeling, perception, volitional formations, or consciousness; no eye, ear, nose, tongue, body, or mind; no form, sound, smell, taste, touch, or mental object; no eye-element and so on up to no mind-consciousness element. There is no ignorance and no extinction of ignorance, and so on up to no extinction of old age and death. There is no suffering, origination, cessation, or path. There is no wisdom, no realization, and no attainment. Because there is nothing to attain, the bodhisattva, relying on prajñāpāramitā, abides with a mind without obstruction. Because the mind is without obstruction, there is no fear. Far removed from inverted dream-thinking, one reaches ultimate quiescence. All Buddhas of the three periods of time, relying on prajñāpāramitā, attain the supreme, perfect enlightenment and realize true enlightenment. Therefore know that prajñāpāramitā is the great mantra, the great luminous mantra, the unsurpassed mantra, the mantra equal to the unequaled, which can remove all suffering, true and not false. Therefore the mantra of prajñāpāramitā is spoken."

The mantra is then spoken:

*Oṃ gate gate pāragate pārasaṃgate bodhi svāhā*

"Thus, Śāriputra! All bodhisattva-mahāsattvas, in the practice of the profound prajñāpāramitā, should practice in this way."

At that time, the World-Honored One calmly arose from the samādhi and praised the Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Avalokiteśvara: "Excellent, excellent! Good man! It is so, it is so, as you have spoken. The practice of the profound prajñāpāramitā should be practiced in this way. When it is practiced thus, all Tathāgatas fully rejoice."

At that time, when the World-Honored One had spoken thus, the venerable Śāriputra, the Bodhisattva Avalokiteśvara, and all in that assembly, including the devas, humans, asuras, gandharvas, and others of the world, hearing what the Buddha taught, were all greatly joyful, and they accepted it with faith and practiced it.
