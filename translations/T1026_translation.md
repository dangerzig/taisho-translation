# 佛說造塔延命功德經
## Sūtra on the Merit of Building Stūpas for Extending Life, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1026

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Prajñā (般若)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the kingdom of Śrāvastī, in the Jeta Grove, the park of Anāthapiṇḍada, together with a great assembly of bhikṣus and bodhisattva-mahāsattvas. At that time, King Prasenajit, in the midst of the great assembly, rose from his seat, adjusted his robe, bared his right shoulder, placed his right knee on the ground, bowed at the Buddha's feet, joined his palms in reverence, and addressed the Buddha: "Rare is the World-Honored One! Physiognomists have divined that in seven days my life will certainly end. I am oppressed by the suffering of impermanence, with no one to depend upon, and so I have come to the World-Honored One. I pray the World-Honored One will rescue and protect me. How can I be freed from this grief and suffering?" The Buddha told him: "Great King! Comfort yourself well and do not worry or fear. The Buddhas and Tathāgatas have skillful means that can enable the great king to obtain supreme benefit, to extend your life-span in the near term, and to ultimately attain anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi. Listen carefully, listen carefully! Reflect upon it well. I shall explain it for you in detail. Great King, you should know: if you wish to be freed from the suffering and affliction of impermanence and to enter the life-span of the Tathāgata's dharma-body, you should first generate the mind, uphold the Buddha's pure precepts, and cultivate the most supreme merit. Being able to generate this mind will extend the king's life-span." The king addressed the Buddha: "I pray the World-Honored One will rescue and protect me. As for generating the mind, upholding the Buddha's pure precepts, and cultivating the most supreme merit that you have taught, I shall carry it out, and I shall also cause the people of my kingdom to carry out this Dharma. But I do not yet know what mind to generate, what precepts to uphold, and what merit is most supreme. I pray the Tathāgata will teach me." The Buddha told the great king: "Generating the mind means generating the four immeasurable minds. Upholding precepts means upholding the precept against killing. Cultivating the supreme merit means nothing surpasses building stūpas. With compassion, rescue and protect all sentient beings; the good devas and spirits will always come to guard and protect you, never leaving you, like a shadow following a form. This will generate boundless merit for the great king. Great King! Building a stūpa of the Buddha brings merit that is difficult to conceive. The Buddhas of the three times all praise it together. I shall briefly explain the causes and conditions for the great king; may the king listen attentively. Long ago in ancient times, there was a small boy herding cattle in this land. Physiognomists came and together divined his fortune, saying that this cowherd boy would certainly die within seven days. At another time, this cowherd boy was playing with other children, heaping sand for amusement. Among them, one child pushed a sand heap into a mound, saying he was making a Buddha's stūpa, one vitasti high, or two, or three, up to four vitasti. At that time, this boy playfully heaped a sand stūpa one vitasti high, and his life-span was subsequently extended by seven years. While heaping the sand, a pratyekabuddha came walking with his alms-bowl. At that time, the children, with playful minds, offered the sand, saying: 'We offer roasted flour.' The pratyekabuddha extended his bowl to receive it, and by supernatural power the sand transformed into roasted flour. The children, seeing this, all obtained pure faith. The pratyekabuddha then gave a prophecy to all the children, saying: 'You children who built a stūpa one vitasti high, in a future life will become iron wheel-turning kings, ruling one quarter of the world. Those who built two vitasti will become copper wheel-turning kings, ruling two quarters. Those who built three vitasti will become silver wheel-turning kings, ruling three quarters. Those who built four vitasti will become gold wheel-turning kings, ruling all four quarters.' When children building such stūpas with playful minds receive such fruition, how much more so the great king who generates the most sincere mind! If good men and good women build stūpas according to the Dharma with a determined mind, even one cubit, one vitasti, one finger-joint, or one grain of barley in size, the merit obtained is without limit. The devas will always rain flowers in offering. Therefore, great King! If good men and good women wish to build a stūpa, they should first generate great compassionate mind toward all sentient beings as the forerunner, take the bodhi-mind as the foundation, then purify the ground and make an altar for offerings. Smear it with cow-dung, burn incense, and scatter flowers. Sitting facing due east, one should contemplate: 'The Buddha Bhagavān has perfected the merit of benefiting self and others, and can also fulfill the wishes of all sentient beings. I now accomplish the foremost dharma-body, generate the bodhi-mind, and with regard to the ritual procedures for building stūpas before the Bhagavān, I shall carry out each step in sequence according to the Dharma.'

"First: gather exquisite flowers, empower them with the dhāraṇī seven times, and scatter them upon the altar as an offering to the Tathāgata. The dhāraṇī:

"'*Namaḥ stathāgatāya anugata-pratiṣṭhite puṣpa-uktame tathāgatāya samyak-saṃbuddhāya tadyathā puṣpe puṣpe supuṣpe svāhā*'

"Second: when kneading the clay to form the earth-lump, contemplate the syllable *a*. This is the dharma-realm body of the Tathāgata. One should first take refuge. Say as follows:

"'*Namo daśa-digbhyaḥ sarva-tathāgatānāṃ ratna-trayāya śuddhe akṣobhya amitābha pañca bhagavān ārya-avalokiteśvarāya guru*'

"When kneading the clay, empower the clay with the koṭi-dhāraṇī twenty-one times. Building one Buddha-stūpa is like building one koṭi of Buddha-stūpas; the merit obtained is equal without difference. Therefore this dhāraṇī is called the Koṭi-dhāraṇī. The dhāraṇī:

"'*Tadyathā oṃ sukha-śmine sukha-śmine sukha-śme anugata-śānte śānte dānte dānte nīra-kulī-ye sudhā-de jura-de sarva-buddhādhiṣṭhāna-adhiṣṭhita amṛtī svāhā*'

"Third: next, contemplate the syllable *spha* upon the clay lump. One should contemplate that this clay lump is like crystal, like a sphaṭika jewel, pure within and without, bright and translucent. Empower it once with the dhāraṇī. The dhāraṇī:

"'*Oṃ vajra-udbhavāya svāhā*'

"Fourth: next, when applying red earth, contemplate the fire color, and know that all worldly and supramundane dharmas are accomplished from atoms. Having completed this contemplation, take dry red earth and smear it over the clay lump. Contemplate that all dharmas are originally unborn and ultimately empty and quiescent. Contemplate the syllable *haṃ*. Visualize the clay lump as the color of molten gold, and then build the stūpa. Empower it once with the dhāraṇī. The dhāraṇī:

"'*Oṃ alaca vilaca svāhā*'

"Fifth: next, when placing the clay lump into the mold, empower it once with the dhāraṇī, then place it in the mold. The dhāraṇī:

"'*Oṃ vajra-dhātu-garbhe svāhā*'

"Sixth: next, when inserting the dharma-body true words, empower once with the dhāraṇī, then place it within the stamp. The dhāraṇī:

"'*Oṃ dharma-dhātu-garbhe svāhā*'

"Seventh: next, when striking the stūpa-stamp, empower once with the dhāraṇī, then strike. The dhāraṇī:

"'*Oṃ vajra-pudgala kuṭāya kuṭāya svāhā*'

"Eighth: next, when finished striking, when forming the stūpa's base-steps by hand, empower once with the dhāraṇī, and set the base-steps in place. The dhāraṇī:

"'*Oṃ vajra-ayuṣe svāhā*'

"Ninth: next, when the stūpa is being removed from the mold, empower once with the dhāraṇī, then remove it. The dhāraṇī:

"'*Oṃ dharma-tiṣṭha svāhā*'

"Tenth: next, after the stūpa has been removed from the mold, when installing the stūpa, empower once with the dhāraṇī. The dhāraṇī:

"'*Oṃ supratiṣṭhita vajra svāhā*'

"Eleventh: next, after the stūpa-seat has been installed, press the top of the stūpa with the hand and empower once with the dhāraṇī. The dhāraṇī:

"'*Oṃ paramaśe svāhā*'

"Twelfth: next, when placing the parasol-canopy atop the stūpa, empower once with the dhāraṇī. The dhāraṇī:

"'*Oṃ parama-ayuṣe svāhā*'"

The Buddha told the great king: "If good men and good women, with a pure mind, build a Buddha-stūpa following this ritual procedure, whether building it themselves, commissioning others to build it, praising it, or faithfully receiving it, the merit obtained is equal to building a Buddha-stūpa, without difference. One should know that such a person, in this very life, will not be harmed by any poison, will enjoy a long life-span without untimely death, and will ultimately attain an indestructible body. All ghosts and spirits will not dare approach. The five planets and seven luminaries will follow and obey one's commands. All enemies will scatter and retreat. Wherever born, one's body will always be free from illness. All sentient beings who see one will rejoice. Those without pure precepts will have their precepts fulfilled. The untamed will be tamed. The impure will become pure. Those who have broken the fast-precepts will have their fast-precepts restored. If one has committed the four pārājikas and the five heinous crimes, even the most grievous karmic offenses will be entirely eliminated. Obstructive accumulations from beginningless kalpas will all be exhausted. If a woman wishes to obtain a son, she will give birth to a courageous, meritorious son. The Four Great Heavenly Kings will constantly surround and protect her. Such is the merit of building stūpas. If a stūpa crumbles and turns to fine dust, and the wind scatters a single dust-mote to another place, all sentient beings in the mountains, forests, rivers, and oceans through which the dust passes, who are touched by this dust, will never again receive the body of any mixed creature. Upon shedding their bodies and being reborn, they will always see the Buddha. Great King! If good men and good women clearly visualize and contemplate the building of the stūpa and the dhāraṇī with single-minded recollection, they will be forever free from water disasters, fire disasters, royal calamities, bandit calamities, and weapon calamities; none can harm them. Whether in the wilderness, in a secluded place, in a village, or in a great assembly, one who can proclaim this sūtra to the multitude thereby rescues and protects the lives of sentient beings. The merit obtained is as vast as space. If in future ages one writes and circulates this sūtra, even though no stūpa has yet been built, the merit is equal to that of building a stūpa, without difference. I have, for the great king and all sentient beings of the future, broadly opened the teaching of the merit of building stūpas for extending life. May the king bear it in mind."

At that time, King Prasenajit and the entire great assembly, having heard what the Buddha taught, all greatly rejoiced, faithfully received it, and practiced accordingly.
