# 稱讚大乘功德經
## Sūtra Praising the Merit of the Great Vehicle

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0840

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Xuanzang (玄奘)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Bhagavān was dwelling in the Dharma-Realm Treasury, in the Great Hall of Merit and Virtue adorned with jewels where all the Buddhas assemble, together with a countless great assembly of śrāvakas and great bodhisattvas, as well as devas, humans, asuras, and other innumerable great multitudes, all surrounding him.

At that time in the assembly there was a bodhisattva who, manifesting in female form, was named Virtue-Adorned Flower. Empowered by the Buddha's spiritual authority, she rose from her seat, bowed her head in homage, and addressed the Buddha: "What should be called an unwholesome companion of a bodhisattva, such that a newly-studying bodhisattva, knowing this, should keep far away?"

At that time the Buddha told Virtue-Adorned Flower: "I observe that in all the world there is no deva-māra, Brahmā, Śakra, śramaṇa, or brahmin who serves as an unwholesome companion to a newly-studying bodhisattva for the unsurpassed bodhi, as much as do those who delight in the śrāvaka and pratyekabuddha vehicles. Why? To be a bodhisattva is necessarily to seek the unsurpassed, right, and perfect bodhi for the benefit and joy of all sentient beings. Those who delight in the two vehicles are narrow and inferior in their aspirations, seeking only to realize for themselves the joy of parinirvāṇa. For this reason, newly-studying bodhisattvas should not dwell in the same monastery, stop in the same room, walk in the same meditation hall, or travel the same road with such persons.

"If a bodhisattva has already attained extensive learning in the Great Vehicle and has obtained indestructible faith, I specifically permit dwelling with them, for the purpose of arousing and leading their minds toward bodhi. If such types have good roots not yet ripened, one should not teach them the Mahāyāna Dharma-teaching, lest they give rise to slander and incur immeasurable offenses.

"Newly-studying bodhisattvas should only draw near to long-studying, widely-learned Mahāyāna bodhisattvas, so that the good roots they have planted for the unsurpassed, right, and perfect bodhi may swiftly ripen. They should not draw near to those who delight in the two vehicles. Why? Because they obstruct the bodhisattva's aspiration for bodhi, because they cause the abandonment of the aspiration for bodhi, because they diminish the aspiration for bodhi, and because they cause transgression of the bodhisattva's practice.

"A bodhisattva would rather relinquish body and life than relinquish the great aspiration for bodhi and give rise to the intention of pursuing the two vehicles. If a bodhisattva urges sentient beings to abandon the aspiration for bodhi and enter the ground of the two vehicles, or if a bodhisattva urges sentient beings to abandon the aspiration for bodhi and commit evil deeds, both will fall into hell and suffer extreme torment.

"A bodhisattva would rather guard the great aspiration for bodhi, commit the five acts of immediate retribution, and suffer hell's torment than relinquish the great aspiration for bodhi and seek to realize the fruit of the stream-enterer. A bodhisattva would rather guard the great aspiration for bodhi and endure hell's torments for a hundred thousand great kalpas than relinquish the great aspiration for bodhi and seek to realize the fruit of the once-returner. A bodhisattva would rather guard the great aspiration for bodhi and receive the body of an animal or become a hungry ghost than relinquish the great aspiration for bodhi and seek to realize the fruit of the non-returner. A bodhisattva would rather guard the great aspiration for bodhi, commit the ten evil deeds, and fall into the evil destinies than relinquish the great aspiration for bodhi and seek to realize the fruit of non-arising. A bodhisattva would rather guard the great aspiration for bodhi and enter a great fire-pit to rescue sentient beings than relinquish the great aspiration for bodhi and, like a cowardly thief, throw himself into the realm of nirvāṇa.

"When a bodhisattva, out of compassion for all sentient beings who revolve through birth-and-death without rescue, first gives rise to the aspiration for unsurpassed bodhi, all devas, humans, and asuras should make offerings to him. He has already eclipsed the ultimate fruits of all the śrāvakas and pratyekabuddhas, and has already crushed and subdued all the hosts of māras. All the evil māra-kings are greatly alarmed and terrified."

At that time Virtue-Adorned Flower, having heard the Buddha's words, again requested: "What are the hosts of māras? We pray the World-Honored One will compassionately explain."

The Buddha told Virtue-Adorned Flower: "If there are those who, upon hearing the Mahāyāna Dharma-teaching, do not rejoice, do not delight in hearing, do not seek to understand it, cannot receive it with faith, and instead mockingly laugh at it, revile and insult it, obstruct and slander it, or beat and drive away those who teach it, you should know that all such persons are the hosts of māras. They are those who delight in what is contrary to the Dharma, those of base nature, those who pursue non-Buddhist paths, those who practice deviant practices, and those who destroy right views.

"You should know that these slanderers and revilers of the Great Vehicle will fall into hell and suffer extreme torment. After emerging from there, they will be born among the hungry ghosts and for hundreds of thousands of kalpas will constantly eat filth. Later, born among humans, they will be blind, deaf, mute, with incomplete limbs, with flat and sunken noses, foolish and dull, ugly in appearance. Gradually, as their karmic obstructions are eliminated, they will wander through the ten directions. Sometimes they encounter the Buddhas, draw near and make offerings, and again hear the Great Vehicle. Having heard it, if they are able to rejoice in faith and receive it, through this they give rise to the great aspiration for bodhi. Vigorously and diligently cultivating the bodhisattva's practice, they gradually advance in their studies until they reach bodhi.

"All the Buddhas, the World-Honored Ones, teach the Dharma of the five vehicles for sentient beings without any separate intention. By the power of their original vows and in reliance on the Dharma-realm body, at all times, from all their pores, they spontaneously emit countless rays of Dharma-light. With a single wondrous voice, they equally shower the rain of Dharma. In a single assembly, among countless sentient beings, those who have previously believed in and delighted in the śrāvaka vehicle hear the Buddha teaching the Dharma of the śrāvaka vehicle. Those who have previously believed in and delighted in the pratyekabuddha vehicle hear the Buddha teaching the Dharma of the pratyekabuddha vehicle. Those who have previously believed in and delighted in the unsurpassed vehicle hear the Buddha teaching the Dharma of the unsurpassed vehicle. Those who have previously believed in and delighted in various vehicles hear the Buddha teaching the Dharma of various vehicles. Those who have previously believed in and delighted in the human and deva vehicles hear the Buddha teaching the Dharma of the human and deva vehicles. Animals, ghosts, and the rest also hear the Tathāgata teaching the Dharma in the language appropriate to each. Those who in the past have never heard the Dharma see only the Buddha sitting silently among the assembly. Those who once slandered the Great Vehicle, after experiencing suffering for countless kalpas in the great hells, among animals and hungry ghosts, and in the deva and human realms, upon hearing the Great Vehicle are immediately able to rejoice, generate profound pure faith, and give rise to the aspiration for anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi."

At that time Virtue-Adorned Flower, having heard the Buddha's teaching, again requested: "What is the Great Vehicle? What meaning does this name 'Great Vehicle' designate?"

The World-Honored One said: "Excellent, excellent! You are able to delight in hearing the merit of the Great Vehicle. Listen carefully, listen carefully! Reflect well upon it, and I shall analyze and explain for you all the meanings designated by this name 'Great Vehicle.'

"This vehicle is comprehensive, encompassing and carrying vastly without any omission: therefore it is called the Great Vehicle. This vehicle's merit is profound, subtle, and wondrous, surpassing all measure: therefore it is called the Great Vehicle. This vehicle is firm, and vain and false discriminations cannot shake it: therefore it is called the Great Vehicle. This vehicle is true and endures until the end of the future, without ever being exhausted: therefore it is called the Great Vehicle. This vehicle is vast, embracing the entire Dharma-realm, boundless and without limit: therefore it is called the Great Vehicle. This vehicle is like the ocean, absorbing and amassing accumulated treasures of merit: therefore it is called the Great Vehicle. This vehicle is like a mountain, serving as a bulwark so that adherents of heterodox paths cannot disturb it: therefore it is called the Great Vehicle. This vehicle is like space, containing all sentient and non-sentient things: therefore it is called the Great Vehicle. This vehicle is like the earth, universally able to produce and nurture worldly and world-transcending good: therefore it is called the Great Vehicle. This vehicle is like water, equally moistening all so that none are withered: therefore it is called the Great Vehicle. This vehicle is like fire, burning away all obstructions and leaving no remaining habits: therefore it is called the Great Vehicle. This vehicle is like wind, sweeping away all the clouds and mists of birth-and-death: therefore it is called the Great Vehicle. This vehicle is like the sun, illuminating all beings and bringing all to maturity: therefore it is called the Great Vehicle. This vehicle is like the moon, able to remove feverish affliction and dispel all deviant darkness: therefore it is called the Great Vehicle. This vehicle is honored and revered, reverenced by the devas, nāgas, and the eightfold assembly: therefore it is called the Great Vehicle. This vehicle is constantly praised in song by all the gandharvas: therefore it is called the Great Vehicle. This vehicle is constantly honored and revered by the four kings, Brahmā, and Śakra: therefore it is called the Great Vehicle. This vehicle is constantly served and guarded by all the nāgas and spirits: therefore it is called the Great Vehicle. This vehicle is constantly studied with diligence by all the bodhisattvas: therefore it is called the Great Vehicle. This vehicle upholds the holy lineage of all the Buddhas, causing it to flourish ever more: therefore it is called the Great Vehicle. This vehicle is perfect, complete with great majestic virtue, eclipsing all: therefore it is called the Great Vehicle. This vehicle universally provides for all sentient beings, leaving none in want: therefore it is called the Great Vehicle. This vehicle's power is like the medicinal tree, curing all illnesses: therefore it is called the Great Vehicle. This vehicle can slay the thieves of affliction in all sentient beings: therefore it is called the Great Vehicle. This vehicle can turn the unsurpassed Dharma-wheel, benefiting all: therefore it is called the Great Vehicle. This vehicle is subtle, wondrous, profound, and secret, beyond all expression: therefore it is called the Great Vehicle. This vehicle's spiritual function perpetuates the lineage of the Three Treasures and prevents it from being severed: therefore it is called the Great Vehicle. This vehicle can manifest the principles of conventional and ultimate truth in their ultimate depth: therefore it is called the Great Vehicle. This vehicle can manifest all the bodhisattva's practices, leaving none incomplete: therefore it is called the Great Vehicle. This vehicle can manifest the merit of the ground of Buddhahood, leaving nothing unprepared: therefore it is called the Great Vehicle. This vehicle benefits and brings joy to all sentient beings until the end of the future: therefore it is called the Great Vehicle. This vehicle's supreme function establishes great meaning, with wondrous application that is inexhaustible: therefore it is called the Great Vehicle. This vehicle is deep and hidden, and those with inferior inclinations cannot receive it with faith: therefore it is called the Great Vehicle. This vehicle is impartial, and only those with superior inclinations can receive it with faith: therefore it is called the Great Vehicle. This vehicle is vast, and the ignorant cannot fathom it and laughingly dismiss it: therefore it is called the Great Vehicle. This vehicle is lofty, and the wisest can penetrate it and always treasure it: therefore it is called the Great Vehicle. This vehicle surpasses the pratyekabuddha vehicle and all others, supreme and without compare: therefore it is called the Great Vehicle."

When the Buddha taught the names, meanings, essence, function, and supreme merits of this Great Vehicle, the great trichiliocosm quaked in six ways. Hundreds of thousands of myriads of kinds of celestial music sounded without being played, and wondrous celestial flowers fell in profusion. Countless devas and innumerable śrāvakas, hearing this Dharma-sound and witnessing these auspicious signs, all gave rise to the aspiration for anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi. Hundreds of thousands of billions of newly-studying bodhisattvas simultaneously realized the patient acceptance of the non-arising of dharmas.

At that time Ānanda rose from his seat, joined his palms in reverence, and addressed the Buddha: "This Dharma-gate is exceedingly rare and is able to universally benefit and bring joy to all sentient beings. By what name should we receive and uphold it and circulate it?"

The Buddha told Ānanda: "This sūtra is called 'Praising the Merit of the Great Vehicle.' It is also called 'Revealing and Teaching the Karmic Obstacles of Slandering the Dharma.' By these names you should receive and uphold it."

When the Bhagavān had finished teaching this sūtra, Ānanda and the other countless śrāvakas, Virtue-Adorned Flower and the other countless bodhisattvas, and all the devas, humans, asuras, and the entire great assembly, having heard what the Buddha taught, all rejoiced greatly, believed, received it, and practiced accordingly.
