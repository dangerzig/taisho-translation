# 佛說腹中女聽經
## Sūtra of the Girl in the Womb Listening to the Sūtra, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0563

Translated from the Chinese. Northern Liang dynasty, translated by Dharmakṣema (曇無讖)

---

The Buddha was in Rājagṛha, together with bodhisattvas, bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, upāsikās, and countless devas and humans gathered to hear the Buddha teach the sūtra.

At that time, in the assembly there was a householder's wife who was pregnant. The child in her womb was sitting with joined palms listening to the sūtra. The Buddha wished the entire assembly to see this, so he emitted a great radiance that shone upon the woman. Everyone in the assembly saw the girl in the womb sitting with joined palms listening to the sūtra, as clearly as looking in a mirror.

The Buddha, with the eight kinds of voice, asked the girl in the womb: "You are sitting with joined palms listening to the sūtra. For what reason do you do this?"

The girl, through the Buddha's supernatural power, immediately answered the Buddha: "Because all people in the world practice the ten evils, and I wish them to practice the ten good deeds, for this reason I sit with joined palms and listen to the sūtra. Because all people in the world are given to lust, anger, and ignorance, and birth and death do not cease, for this reason I sit with joined palms and listen to the sūtra. Because people in the world do not show filial devotion to their parents and do not make offerings to śramaṇas, brahmins, and practitioners, for this reason I sit with joined palms and listen to the sūtra."

When the girl had finished speaking, she was immediately born. Like a crown prince emerging from the right side, the earth shook six times. In the sky, countless devas spontaneously produced the sound of music. Heavenly flowers rained down. A spontaneous thousand-petaled lotus appeared, as large as a cartwheel, with a stem of jewels shaped like blue lapis lazuli. The girl immediately sat upon the lotus.

Śakra, lord of the Trāyastriṃśa heaven, took heavenly garments and descended from the sky. He said to the girl: "Being unclothed is unseemly. Take these garments and wear them."

The girl replied: "I will not wear these garments. Do you wish to adorn me? You are an arhat; I am a bodhisattva. You are not of my kind. I am not of the same class as you. I shall have garments come to me of their own accord."

The Buddha said to Śakra: "This is not about adorning the bodhisattva. Generating the mind for the bodhisattva path and of oneself attaining the thirty-two marks and eighty refinements: that is what adorns the bodhisattva!"

Śāriputra said to the Buddha: "From what land has this girl come, and who will bring her garments?"

The Buddha said: "This girl comes from a buddha-land to the southeast. Her land is named Pure, one hundred thousand buddha-lands from here. Having come from her homeland to see the Buddha, garments will be brought to her of their own accord." The garments from her homeland came of themselves through the sky with a rustling sound. The girl, seeing the garments arrive, put them on. She then attained the five supernatural powers. (In the girl's homeland, all had attained the five supernatural powers.)

When the girl had put on the garments, she descended from the lotus and walked to the Buddha's presence. With one step of her foot, the earth shook six times. She placed her head on the ground, paid respects to the Buddha, three times said, "Homage to the Buddha!" and circumambulated the Buddha. When she had finished, she knelt before the Buddha and said: "In this assembly there are many householders' wives. May the Buddha teach the sūtra for them, so that they may attain male bodies."

The Buddha said: "I neither make you into a man nor make you into a woman. All of this comes from one's own bodily actions."

The Buddha said: "There is one thing that can swiftly bring a male body. What is this one thing? Generating the mind for the bodhisattva path: this is the one thing. Furthermore, a woman should inwardly contemplate her own body. It is like a mechanical device, with bones and joints propping each other up, with only sinews and skin on top. A woman always fears others, like an owl, a snake, or a toad that dare not come out in daylight and always fear people. Like a servant, a woman is always in the company of foul discharges and stench. Even a king's daughter still fears others. The many evils of a woman's condition are just like this."

At that time seventy-five householders' wives in the assembly, hearing the Buddha's teaching, leaped with joy. They came forward, placed their heads on the ground, paid respects to the Buddha, and said: "We vow to generate the bodhisattva mind and become men. Until we obtain male bodies, we shall not rise from this place."

At that time seventy-five householders who had come from Śrāvastī arrived at the Buddha's dwelling. Seeing that all the wives were before the Buddha, they thought: "We have lost our wives!" They asked Śāriputra: "All these women are our wives. What are they doing here?"

Śāriputra replied: "They wish to become bhikṣuṇīs. Will you consent?"

The householders replied: "If they wish to become bhikṣuṇīs, then let us first become bhikṣus."

Śāriputra said to the Buddha: "These seventy-five householders all wish to become bhikṣus."

The Buddha called out: "Come, good men, and become bhikṣus." Their hair fell away of its own accord, kāṣāya robes appeared upon their bodies, and begging bowls appeared in their hands. They all came forward and paid respects to the Buddha.

The seventy-five wives then each removed their pearl rings and scattered them upon the Buddha. They spontaneously formed in the sky seventy-five jeweled lattice and pearl necklace canopies, within which were seven-jeweled couches with seated figures, and beside them were countless bodhisattvas listening to the sūtra.

The seventy-five women, seeing these transformations, were filled with great joy. Through the Buddha's supernatural power, they flew up and hovered in the sky. In the sky, flowers spontaneously appeared and rained down upon the Buddha. Descending from the sky, they obtained male bodies. They came forward and said to the Buddha: "We wish to become bhikṣus."

The Buddha said to the Bodhisattva Maitreya: "Take them and confer the precepts." The Bodhisattva Maitreya immediately conferred the precepts.

The girl, who had become a bhikṣu, spontaneously produced a seven-tiered flower canopy with a stem like a lotus stem. She presented it to her mother and said: "The Buddha is the teacher who liberates people in heaven and on earth. Mother, offer this flower canopy to the Buddha, the teacher of heaven and earth. After offering the canopy, mother too shall become a canopy for the world."

The girl said to her mother: "You should now generate the bodhisattva mind."

The mother answered her daughter: "From the time I first conceived you, in my dreams I constantly saw the Buddha, the Dharma, and the Saṃgha of bhikṣus. I was free from all thoughts of lust, anger, and ignorance. My body was at ease and at peace. I knew that the child in my womb was a bodhisattva mahāsattva, and that is why I was at ease."

At that time, having generated the bodhisattva mind, the mother received the flower canopy and offered it to the Buddha. The earth shook six times.

The Buddha said to Śāriputra: "The stars of the four continents can still be counted, but the parents this girl has liberated through her past and future lives cannot be numbered."

The girl said: "All people call me a woman. I shall now perform an act of truth." She then transformed into the body of a boy of eight, a novice.

When the Buddha taught this sūtra and the girl performed these transformations, countless people all generated the mind of unsurpassed true awakening. When the Buddha had finished speaking the sūtra, all were filled with great joy.
