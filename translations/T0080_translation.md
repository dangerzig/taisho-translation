# 佛為首迦長者說業報差別經
## Sūtra on the Distinctions in Karmic Retribution, Spoken by the Buddha for Householder Śuka

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 80

Translated from the Chinese. Sui Dynasty, translated by Gautama Dharmajñāna

---

Thus have I heard.

At one time, the Buddha was dwelling at the Jetavana, in the garden of Anāthapiṇḍada, in the kingdom of Śrāvastī. At that time, the Buddha addressed Householder Śuka, son of Todeyya: "Householder Śuka! I shall now expound for you the Dharma-gate of distinctions in good and evil karmic retribution. Listen carefully and reflect upon it well!"

At that time, Śuka spoke to the Buddha, saying: "Yes, World-Honored One! I gladly wish to hear it!"

The Buddha told Śuka: "All sentient beings are bound by karma, supported by karma, and turned according to their own karma. For this reason, there arise differences of superior, middling, and inferior.

"Some karma causes sentient beings to obtain the retribution of a short life; some karma causes sentient beings to obtain the retribution of a long life.

"Some karma causes sentient beings to obtain the retribution of much illness; some karma causes sentient beings to obtain the retribution of little illness.

"Some karma causes sentient beings to obtain the retribution of ugliness; some karma causes sentient beings to obtain the retribution of a fine appearance.

"Some karma causes sentient beings to obtain the retribution of little authority; some karma causes sentient beings to obtain the retribution of great authority.

"Some karma causes sentient beings to obtain the retribution of a low family; some karma causes sentient beings to obtain the retribution of a high family.

"Some karma causes sentient beings to obtain the retribution of few means of livelihood; some karma causes sentient beings to obtain the retribution of many means of livelihood.

"Some karma causes sentient beings to obtain the retribution of wrong wisdom; some karma causes sentient beings to obtain the retribution of right wisdom.

"Some karma causes sentient beings to obtain the retribution of hell; some karma causes sentient beings to obtain the retribution of the animal realm; some karma causes sentient beings to obtain the retribution of the hungry ghost realm; some karma causes sentient beings to obtain the retribution of the asura realm; some karma causes sentient beings to obtain the retribution of the human realm; some karma causes sentient beings to obtain the retribution of the desire-realm heavens; some karma causes sentient beings to obtain the retribution of the form-realm heavens; some karma causes sentient beings to obtain the retribution of the formless-realm heavens.

"Some karma causes sentient beings to obtain a fixed retribution; some karma causes sentient beings to obtain an unfixed retribution.

"Some karma causes sentient beings to obtain the retribution of border regions; some karma causes sentient beings to obtain the retribution of the central lands.

"Some karma causes sentient beings to exhaust the full lifespan in hell; some karma causes sentient beings to pass half their lifespan in hell; some karma causes sentient beings to enter and immediately exit.

"Some karma is performed but not accumulated; some karma is accumulated but not performed; some karma is both performed and accumulated; some karma is neither performed nor accumulated.

"Some karma causes sentient beings to experience joy at first and suffering later; some karma causes sentient beings to experience suffering at first and joy later; some karma causes sentient beings to experience suffering at first and suffering later; some karma causes sentient beings to experience joy at first and joy later.

"Some karma causes sentient beings to be poor yet joyful in giving; some karma causes sentient beings to be wealthy yet miserly and greedy; some karma causes sentient beings to be wealthy and able to give; some karma causes sentient beings to be poor yet miserly and greedy.

"Some karma causes sentient beings to have bodily joy but not mental joy; some karma causes sentient beings to have mental joy but not bodily joy; some karma causes sentient beings to have both bodily and mental joy; some karma causes sentient beings to have neither bodily nor mental joy.

"Some karma causes sentient beings whose lifespan is exhausted while their karma is not exhausted; some karma causes sentient beings whose karma is exhausted while their lifespan is not exhausted; some karma causes sentient beings in whom both karma and lifespan are exhausted; some karma causes sentient beings in whom neither karma nor lifespan is exhausted, who can sever all afflictions.

"Some karma causes sentient beings born in evil destinies to have a remarkably fine appearance, dignified eyes, and a radiant complexion, pleasing to behold; some karma causes sentient beings born in evil destinies to have an ugly appearance, a rough and coarse complexion, displeasing to behold; some karma causes sentient beings born in evil destinies to have bodies and mouths that are foul and malodorous, with deficient faculties.

"Some sentient beings habitually practice the ten unwholesome actions and obtain adverse external retribution; some sentient beings habitually practice the ten wholesome actions and obtain superior external retribution.

"Furthermore, householder! If sentient beings bow reverently to Buddha-stūpas and temples, they obtain ten kinds of merit; if they offer jeweled canopies, they obtain ten kinds of merit; if they offer silk banners, they obtain ten kinds of merit; if they offer bells and chimes, they obtain ten kinds of merit; if they offer garments, they obtain ten kinds of merit; if they offer vessels, they obtain ten kinds of merit; if they offer food and drink, they obtain ten kinds of merit; if they offer shoes and footwear, they obtain ten kinds of merit; if they offer incense and flowers, they obtain ten kinds of merit; if they offer lamps and lights, they obtain ten kinds of merit; if they reverently join their palms in añjali, they obtain ten kinds of merit. This is the briefly stated Dharma-gate of distinctions among the various worldly karmas."

The Buddha told Śuka: "There are ten kinds of karma that cause sentient beings to obtain the retribution of a short life: first, personally committing killing; second, encouraging others to kill; third, praising the act of killing; fourth, rejoicing upon witnessing killing; fifth, wishing for the destruction of those one hates; sixth, feeling delight upon seeing enemies perish; seventh, destroying the embryo in another's womb; eighth, instructing others to destroy it; ninth, establishing temples to the gods with the slaughter of sentient beings; tenth, inciting others to battle so they mutually harm one another. Through these ten karmas one obtains the retribution of a short life.

"There are further ten kinds of karma that cause sentient beings to obtain the retribution of a long life: first, personally refraining from killing; second, encouraging others not to kill; third, praising non-killing; fourth, feeling joy in one's heart upon seeing others not kill; fifth, upon seeing someone being killed, using skillful means to rescue and free them; sixth, upon seeing someone in fear of death, consoling their heart; seventh, upon seeing those in fear, giving them the gift of fearlessness; eighth, upon seeing people suffering from illness, arousing a mind of compassion; ninth, upon seeing people in urgent distress, arousing a mind of great compassion; tenth, bestowing food and drink as gifts upon sentient beings. Through these ten karmas one obtains the retribution of a long life.

"There are further ten kinds of karma that cause sentient beings to obtain the retribution of much illness: first, being fond of striking and hitting all sentient beings; second, encouraging others to strike; third, praising the act of striking; fourth, rejoicing upon seeing striking; fifth, distressing and troubling one's parents, causing their hearts to grieve; sixth, distressing and troubling sages and noble ones; seventh, feeling great delight upon seeing enemies suffering from illness; eighth, feeling displeasure upon seeing enemies recover from illness; ninth, administering wrong medicine to ailing enemies; tenth, eating again before previously consumed food is digested. Through these ten karmas one obtains the retribution of much illness.

"There are further ten kinds of karma that cause sentient beings to obtain the retribution of little illness: first, not being fond of striking and hitting all sentient beings; second, encouraging others not to strike; third, praising non-striking; fourth, feeling joy upon seeing those who do not strike; fifth, serving and making offerings to one's parents and to the ill; sixth, upon seeing sages and noble ones fall ill, attending to and making offerings to them; seventh, feeling joy upon seeing enemies recover from illness; eighth, giving good medicine to those suffering from illness and also encouraging others to give; ninth, arousing a mind of compassion toward sentient beings who suffer from illness; tenth, being able to regulate one's own intake of food and drink. Through these ten karmas one obtains the retribution of little illness.

"There are further ten kinds of karma that cause sentient beings to obtain the retribution of ugliness: first, being fond of anger; second, being fond of harboring resentment; third, deceiving and confusing others; fourth, distressing and troubling sentient beings; fifth, having no love or respect toward one's parents; sixth, not generating reverence toward sages and noble ones; seventh, encroaching upon and seizing the livelihood and fields of sages and noble ones; eighth, extinguishing the lamps in Buddha-stūpas and temples; ninth, mocking and despising those of ugly appearance; tenth, practicing various evil deeds. Through these ten karmas one obtains the retribution of ugliness.

"There are further ten kinds of karma that cause sentient beings to obtain the retribution of a fine appearance: first, not being angry; second, giving garments; third, loving and respecting one's parents; fourth, honoring and venerating sages and noble ones; fifth, anointing and adorning Buddha-stūpas; sixth, sweeping and cleaning the halls; seventh, sweeping and cleaning the grounds of the Sangha; eighth, sweeping and cleaning Buddha-stūpas; ninth, upon seeing those of ugly appearance, not generating contempt but arousing a mind of reverence; tenth, upon seeing those of fine appearance, understanding the past-life causes. Through these ten karmas one obtains the retribution of a fine appearance.

"There are further ten kinds of karma that cause sentient beings to obtain the retribution of little authority: first, arousing a mind of jealousy toward all sentient beings; second, feeling vexation and distress upon seeing others gain benefit; third, feeling delight upon seeing others lose benefit; fourth, arousing a mind of jealous resentment toward others' good reputation; fifth, feeling great rejoicing upon seeing loss of reputation; sixth, turning back from bodhicitta and destroying images of the Buddha; seventh, having no mind to serve one's own parents and sages and noble ones; eighth, encouraging others to practice karma of little virtue; ninth, obstructing others from practicing karma of great virtue; tenth, generating contempt upon seeing those of little virtue. Through these ten karmas one obtains the retribution of little authority.

"There are further ten kinds of karma that cause sentient beings to obtain the retribution of great authority: first, having no jealousy in one's heart toward all sentient beings; second, feeling joy upon seeing others gain benefit; third, arousing a mind of compassion upon seeing others lose benefit; fourth, feeling delight regarding others' good reputation; fifth, feeling sympathetic distress upon seeing loss of reputation; sixth, giving rise to bodhicitta, fashioning images of the Buddha, and offering jeweled canopies; seventh, respectfully welcoming one's own parents and sages and noble ones; eighth, encouraging others to abandon karma of little virtue; ninth, encouraging others to practice karma of great virtue; tenth, not generating contempt upon seeing those of no virtue. Through these ten karmas one obtains the retribution of great authority.

"There are further ten kinds of karma that cause sentient beings to obtain the retribution of low family: first, not knowing how to respect one's father; second, not knowing how to respect one's mother; third, not knowing how to respect śramaṇas; fourth, not knowing how to respect brāhmaṇas; fifth, not showing reverence to one's elders and superiors; sixth, not welcoming and making offerings to one's teachers; seventh, upon seeing one's elders and superiors, not going forward to invite them to be seated; eighth, not following the teachings and admonitions of one's parents; ninth, also not receiving instruction from sages and noble ones; tenth, despising those of low family. Through these ten karmas one obtains the retribution of low family.

"There are further ten kinds of karma that cause sentient beings to obtain the retribution of high family: first, knowing well how to respect one's father; second, knowing well how to respect one's mother; third, knowing well how to respect śramaṇas; fourth, knowing well how to respect brāhmaṇas; fifth, respectfully protecting one's elders and superiors; sixth, welcoming and attending upon one's teachers; seventh, upon seeing one's elders and superiors, going forward to invite them to be seated; eighth, respectfully receiving the teachings and admonitions of one's parents; ninth, respectfully receiving instruction from sages and noble ones; tenth, not despising those of low family. Through these ten karmas one obtains the retribution of high family.

"There are further ten kinds of karma that cause sentient beings to obtain the retribution of few means of livelihood: first, personally committing theft; second, encouraging others to steal; third, praising theft; fourth, rejoicing upon seeing theft; fifth, reducing and withdrawing from one's parents the means of their livelihood; sixth, encroaching upon and seizing wealth from sages and noble ones; seventh, not feeling joy upon seeing others gain benefit; eighth, obstructing others from gaining benefit, creating hindrances; ninth, upon seeing others practicing giving, having no mind of sympathetic rejoicing; tenth, upon seeing famine in the world, feeling no compassion but rather delight. Through these ten karmas one obtains the retribution of few means of livelihood.

"There are further ten kinds of karma that cause sentient beings to obtain the retribution of many means of livelihood: first, personally refraining from theft; second, encouraging others not to steal; third, praising non-stealing; fourth, feeling joy upon seeing others not steal; fifth, providing for and attending to one's parents' livelihood; sixth, supplying the needs of sages and noble ones; seventh, feeling joy upon seeing others gain benefit; eighth, upon seeing those seeking benefit, using skillful means to assist them; ninth, feeling delight upon seeing those who enjoy giving; tenth, feeling compassion upon seeing famine in the world. Through these ten karmas one obtains the retribution of many means of livelihood.

"There are further ten kinds of karma that cause sentient beings to obtain the retribution of wrong wisdom: first, not being able to seek instruction from wise śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas; second, openly teaching evil doctrines; third, not being able to receive, uphold, and practice the correct Dharma; fourth, praising uncertain doctrines as certain; fifth, hoarding the Dharma and not teaching it; sixth, associating with those of wrong wisdom; seventh, keeping far from those of right wisdom; eighth, praising wrong views; ninth, abandoning right views; tenth, upon seeing foolish and wicked persons, generating contempt and mockery. Through these ten karmas one obtains the retribution of wrong wisdom.

"There are further ten kinds of karma that cause sentient beings to obtain the retribution of right wisdom: first, being well able to seek instruction from wise śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas; second, openly teaching good doctrines; third, hearing and upholding the correct Dharma; fourth, upon seeing certain doctrines expounded, saying 'Excellent!'; fifth, taking pleasure in expounding the correct Dharma; sixth, associating with persons of right wisdom; seventh, protecting and guarding the correct Dharma; eighth, diligently cultivating wide learning; ninth, keeping far from wrong views; tenth, upon seeing foolish and wicked persons, not generating contempt. Through these ten karmas one obtains the retribution of right wisdom.

"There are further ten kinds of karma that cause sentient beings to obtain the retribution of hell: first, performing grave evil karma of body; second, performing grave evil karma of speech; third, performing grave evil karma of mind; fourth, giving rise to annihilationist views; fifth, giving rise to eternalist views; sixth, giving rise to views of no cause; seventh, giving rise to views of no action; eighth, giving rise to views of nonexistence; ninth, giving rise to extreme views; tenth, being ungrateful for kindness received. Through these ten karmas one obtains the retribution of hell.

"There are further ten kinds of karma that cause sentient beings to obtain the retribution of the animal realm: first, performing middling evil karma of body; second, performing middling evil karma of speech; third, performing middling evil karma of mind; fourth, giving rise to various evil karmas through the affliction of greed; fifth, giving rise to various evil karmas through the affliction of hatred; sixth, giving rise to various evil karmas through the affliction of delusion; seventh, reviling and abusing sentient beings; eighth, harassing and harming sentient beings; ninth, giving impure gifts; tenth, engaging in sexual misconduct. Through these ten karmas one obtains the retribution of the animal realm.

"There are further ten kinds of karma that cause sentient beings to obtain the retribution of the hungry ghost realm: first, performing minor evil karma of body; second, performing minor evil karma of speech; third, performing minor evil karma of mind; fourth, giving rise to excessive greed; fifth, giving rise to wicked greed; sixth, jealousy; seventh, wrong views; eighth, being deeply attached to one's means of livelihood and then dying; ninth, dying from hunger; tenth, dying from thirst. Through these ten karmas one obtains the retribution of the hungry ghost realm.

"There are further ten kinds of karma that cause sentient beings to obtain the retribution of the asura realm: first, performing slight evil karma of body; second, performing slight evil karma of speech; third, performing slight evil karma of mind; fourth, pride and arrogance; fifth, pride of self; sixth, excessive pride; seventh, great pride; eighth, wrong pride; ninth, pride upon pride; tenth, turning one's roots of merit toward the asura realm. Through these ten karmas one obtains the retribution of the asura realm.

"There are further ten kinds of karma that cause sentient beings to obtain the retribution of the human realm: first, not killing; second, not stealing; third, not engaging in sexual misconduct; fourth, not lying; fifth, not speaking frivolously; sixth, not speaking divisively; seventh, not speaking harshly; eighth, not being greedy; ninth, not being hateful; tenth, not holding wrong views. When the ten wholesome actions are incomplete and imperfect, through these ten karmas one obtains the retribution of the human realm.

"There are further ten kinds of karma that cause sentient beings to obtain the retribution of the desire-realm heavens, namely, the complete and thorough practice of the ten wholesome actions in a superior manner.

"There are further ten kinds of karma that cause sentient beings to obtain the retribution of the form-realm heavens, namely, the practice of the ten wholesome actions with outflows, conjoined with meditative concentration.

"There are further four kinds of karma that cause sentient beings to obtain the retribution of the formless-realm heavens: first, transcending all perceptions of form, extinguishing perceptions of resistance and so forth, and entering the concentration of infinite space; second, transcending all concentrations of infinite space and entering the concentration of infinite consciousness; third, transcending all concentrations of infinite consciousness and entering the concentration of nothingness; fourth, transcending the concentration of nothingness and entering the concentration of neither-perception-nor-non-perception. Through these four karmas one obtains the retribution of the formless-realm heavens.

"There is further karma that causes sentient beings to obtain a fixed retribution: if a person gives with a superior mind to the Buddha, the Dharma, the Sangha, and those who uphold the precepts, and directs this wholesome karma through aspiration and dedication of merit, they will certainly be reborn accordingly. This is called karma of fixed retribution.

"There is further karma that causes sentient beings to obtain an unfixed retribution: if karma is performed without a superior mind, not further cultivated, and without aspiration or dedication of merit toward rebirth, this is called karma of unfixed retribution.

"There is further karma that causes sentient beings to obtain the retribution of border regions: if, with regard to the Buddha, the Dharma, the Sangha, those who purely uphold the precepts, and the great assembly, one gives without a superior mind, and with these roots of merit makes the aspiration to be reborn in border regions, through this aspiration one is reborn in border regions, receiving retribution that is both pure and impure.

"There is further karma that causes sentient beings to obtain the retribution of the central lands: if, at the time of performing karma, with regard to the Buddha, the Dharma, the Sangha, those who purely uphold the precepts and practice brahmacarya, and the great assembly, one gives with superior and earnest generosity, and with these roots of merit makes the firm aspiration to be reborn in the central lands, one will again encounter the Buddha, hear the correct Dharma, and receive superior, sublime, and pure karmic fruit.

"There is further karma that causes sentient beings to exhaust the full lifespan in hell: if a sentient being, having created karma of hell, has no remorse or shame and does not turn away from it, harbors no fear in the heart but instead generates delight, does not repent, and furthermore creates additional grave and compounding evil karma, as with Devadatta and others. Through this karma one exhausts the full lifespan in hell.

"There is further karma that causes sentient beings to fall into hell and perish at the halfway point, not exhausting their full lifespan: if a sentient being has created karma of hell and accumulated it fully, but later gives rise to fear, shame and remorse, turns away from it, repents and renounces it, though without a superior mind, through this karma one falls into hell; but because of subsequent regret, one perishes halfway through the lifespan in hell, not exhausting its full duration.

"There is further karma that causes sentient beings to fall into hell, enter briefly, and immediately exit: if a sentient being creates karma of hell but, having done so, gives rise to fear, arouses superior faith, gives rise to a mind of shame and remorse, turns away from and renounces it with earnest repentance, and does not commit it again, as with King Ajātaśatru's crime of patricide and the like, one enters hell briefly and immediately attains liberation."

Thereupon, the World-Honored One spoke the following verse:

> If a person commits grave transgressions,
> Yet afterward deeply reproaches themselves,
> Repents and commits them no more,
> Such a one can uproot the fundamental karma.

"There is further karma that is performed but not accumulated: if a sentient being creates various evil karmas of body, speech, and mind, but having done so gives rise to fear, shame and remorse, keeps far from them, deeply reproaches themselves, and does not commit them again, this is called performing but not accumulating.

"There is further karma that is accumulated but not performed: if a sentient being does not personally create karma, but with a malicious mind encourages others to commit evil, this is called accumulating but not performing.

"There is further karma that is both performed and accumulated: if a sentient being, having created various karmas, has no mind of repentance and remorse, and furthermore creates them repeatedly and also encourages others, this is called both performing and accumulating.

"There is further karma that is neither performed nor accumulated: if a sentient being neither personally creates karma nor instructs others in indeterminate karma and the like, this is called neither performing nor accumulating.

"There is further karma of joy at first and suffering later: if a sentient being, encouraged by others, joyfully practices giving, but the mind of giving is not firm and one later regrets it, through this condition one is born in the human realm, and though one is at first wealthy and joyful, one later becomes poor and suffering. This is called joy first and suffering later.

"There is further karma of suffering at first and joy later: if a sentient being, encouraged and led by others, gives reluctantly and in small measure, but having given one feels joy without regret or meanness, through this condition one is born in the human realm, at first poor and suffering, but later wealthy and joyful. This is called suffering first and joy later.

"There is further karma of suffering at first and suffering later: if a sentient being is separated from good spiritual friends, with no one to encourage them, and is unable even to practice a small amount of giving, through this condition one is born in the human realm, at first poor and suffering and later also poor and suffering. This is called suffering first and suffering later.

"There is further karma of joy at first and joy later: if a sentient being draws near to good spiritual friends who encourage them to practice giving, and one gives rise to joy and steadfastly cultivates the practice of giving, through this condition one is born in the human realm, at first wealthy and joyful and later also wealthy and joyful.

"There is further karma of being poor yet joyful in giving: if a sentient being previously practiced giving but did not encounter a good field of merit, and wandered through births and deaths in the human realm, because of not encountering a good field of merit, the karmic fruit is meager and soon exhausted; but because of the habit of giving, even while in poverty one is able to practice giving.

"There is further karma of being wealthy yet miserly and greedy: if a sentient being never previously practiced giving, but then encountered good spiritual friends and briefly practiced a single act of giving, meeting an excellent field of merit, because the field is superior, one is endowed with full means of livelihood; but because one had no prior habit of giving, even while wealthy one is miserly.

"There is further karma of being wealthy yet able to give: if a sentient being encountered good spiritual friends, cultivated the practice of giving extensively, and met an excellent field of merit, through this condition one becomes immensely wealthy and abundantly prosperous, yet is able to practice giving.

"There is further karma of being poor yet miserly and greedy: if a sentient being is separated from good spiritual friends, with no one to encourage them, and is unable to practice giving, through this condition one is born in poverty and is furthermore miserly and greedy.

"There is further karma that causes sentient beings to have bodily joy but not mental joy, as with a worldly person of good fortune.

"There is further karma that causes sentient beings to have mental joy but not bodily joy, as with an arhat who lacks good fortune.

"There is further karma that causes sentient beings to have both bodily and mental joy, as with an arhat of good fortune.

"There is further karma that causes sentient beings to have neither bodily nor mental joy, as with a worldly person who lacks good fortune.

"There is further karma that causes sentient beings' lifespan to be exhausted while their karma is not exhausted: if a sentient being, dying in hell, is reborn in hell again, and similarly for the animal realm, the hungry ghost realm, and even the human realm, heaven, and the asura realm, this is called the lifespan being exhausted while the karma is not exhausted.

"There is further karma that causes sentient beings' karma to be exhausted while their lifespan is not exhausted: if a sentient being's joy is exhausted and suffering is received, or suffering is exhausted and joy is received, this is called karma being exhausted while the lifespan is not exhausted.

"There is further karma that causes sentient beings' karma and lifespan to both be exhausted: if a sentient being passes away from hell and is reborn in the animal realm or the hungry ghost realm, or even in the human realm, heaven, or the asura realm, this is called both karma and lifespan being exhausted.

"There is further karma that causes sentient beings' karma and lifespan to both be not exhausted: if sentient beings have exhausted all afflictions, namely, śrotāpannas, sakṛdāgāmins, anāgāmins, and arhats, this is called neither karma nor lifespan being exhausted.

"There is further karma that causes sentient beings born in evil destinies to have a remarkably fine appearance, dignified eyes, and a radiant complexion, pleasing to behold: if a sentient being gives rise to precept-breaking karma through the affliction of desire, through this condition, even though born in an evil destiny, they have a fine appearance, dignified eyes, and a radiant complexion, pleasing to behold.

"There is further karma that causes sentient beings born in evil destinies to have an ugly appearance, a rough and coarse complexion, displeasing to behold: if a sentient being gives rise to precept-breaking karma through the affliction of hatred, through this condition, they are born in an evil destiny with an ugly appearance, a rough and coarse complexion, displeasing to behold.

"There is further karma that causes sentient beings born in evil destinies to have bodies and mouths that are foul and malodorous, with deficient faculties: if a sentient being gives rise to precept-breaking karma through the affliction of delusion, through this condition, they are born in an evil destiny with bodies and mouths that are foul and malodorous, with deficient faculties.

"There are further ten kinds of karma that bring adverse external retribution: if sentient beings extensively practice the ten unwholesome actions, all external material things are obtained deficiently. First, because of the karma of killing, the external retribution is that the earth becomes saline and barren, and medicinal herbs lose their potency. Second, because of the karma of stealing, the external retribution is frost, hail, locusts and other insects, causing famine in the world. Third, because of the karma of sexual misconduct, evil winds and rains and clouds of dust are induced. Fourth, because of the karma of lying, the external retribution is that all things produced are foul and malodorous. Fifth, because of the karma of divisive speech, the external retribution is that the great earth becomes uneven, with steep cliffs and treacherous valleys, and stumps and jagged branches. Sixth, because of the karma of harsh speech, the external retribution is that tiles, stones, gravel, and rough and coarse harmful things that cannot be approached or touched are produced. Seventh, because of the karma of frivolous speech, the external retribution is that grasses, trees, dense forests, and branches with thorns proliferate. Eighth, because of the karma of greed, the external retribution is that seedlings and plants produce seeds that are tiny and minute. Ninth, because of the karma of hatred, the external retribution is that trees produce fruits that are bitter and astringent. Tenth, because of the karma of wrong views, the external retribution is that seedlings and plants bear no fruit and the harvest is scanty. Through these ten karmas one obtains adverse external retribution.

"There are further ten kinds of karma that bring superior external retribution: if sentient beings practice the ten wholesome actions, which are the opposite of the above, it should be understood that they correspondingly obtain ten superior external retributions.

"If sentient beings bow reverently to Buddha-stūpas and temples, they obtain ten kinds of merit: first, they obtain a fine appearance and a beautiful voice; second, whatever they say, people believe and submit to; third, in the midst of assemblies they are without fear; fourth, devas and humans love and protect them; fifth, they are endowed with great authority; sixth, sentient beings of great authority all come near and attach themselves to them; seventh, they constantly draw near to all buddhas and bodhisattvas; eighth, they are endowed with great blessed fortune; ninth, at the end of their life they are born in heaven; tenth, they swiftly realize nirvāṇa. This is called the ten kinds of merit from reverently bowing to Buddha-stūpas and temples.

"If sentient beings offer jeweled canopies, they obtain ten kinds of merit: first, their standing in the world is like a canopy, covering and protecting sentient beings; second, body and mind are peaceful and settled, free from all afflictive heat; third, all venerate them, none daring to show contempt; fourth, they have great authority; fifth, they constantly draw near to the greatly virtuous buddhas, bodhisattvas, and others as their retinue; sixth, they constantly act as cakravartins; seventh, they constantly serve as foremost in cultivating wholesome karma; eighth, they are endowed with great blessed fortune; ninth, at the end of their life they are born in heaven; tenth, they swiftly realize nirvāṇa. This is called the ten kinds of merit from offering jeweled canopies.

"If sentient beings offer silk banners, they obtain ten kinds of merit: first, their standing in the world is like a banner, and kings, ministers, friends, and acquaintances respectfully make offerings to them; second, they are wealthy and at ease, possessing great treasures; third, their good name spreads and reaches all directions; fourth, their form is dignified and their lifespan is long; fifth, wherever they are born, their practice of giving is steadfast and firm; sixth, they have great renown; seventh, they have great virtue and authority; eighth, they are born in superior families; ninth, when the body perishes and life ends, they are born in heaven; tenth, they swiftly realize nirvāṇa. This is called the ten kinds of merit from offering silk banners.

"If sentient beings offer bells and chimes, they obtain ten kinds of merit: first, they obtain a brahma-voice; second, they have great renown; third, they can recall their past lives; fourth, whatever they say, people respectfully receive; fifth, they always have a jeweled canopy to adorn themselves; sixth, they have fine jeweled garlands as their adornment; seventh, their countenance is dignified and those who see them rejoice; eighth, they are endowed with great blessed fortune; ninth, at the end of their life they are born in heaven; tenth, they swiftly realize nirvāṇa. This is called the ten kinds of merit from offering bells and chimes.

"If sentient beings offer garments, they obtain ten kinds of merit: first, their countenance is dignified; second, their skin is fine and smooth; third, dust and filth do not adhere to them; fourth, from birth they are endowed with superior and fine garments; fifth, fine and subtle bedding covers their body; sixth, they are endowed with the garment of shame and remorse; seventh, those who see them love and respect them; eighth, they are endowed with great wealth and treasure; ninth, at the end of their life they are born in heaven; tenth, they swiftly realize nirvāṇa. This is called the ten kinds of merit from offering garments.

"If sentient beings offer vessels, they obtain ten kinds of merit: first, their standing in the world is like a vessel; second, they obtain the moisture of the good Dharma; third, they are freed from all thirst and craving; fourth, if they are thirsty and think of water, flowing springs gush forth; fifth, they are never born in the realm of hungry ghosts; sixth, they obtain the sublime vessels of heaven; seventh, they keep far from evil friends; eighth, they are endowed with great blessed fortune; ninth, at the end of their life they are born in heaven; tenth, they swiftly realize nirvāṇa. This is called the ten kinds of merit from offering vessels.

"If sentient beings offer food and drink, they obtain ten kinds of merit: first, they obtain life; second, they obtain fine appearance; third, they obtain strength; fourth, they obtain peaceful and unobstructed eloquence; fifth, they obtain fearlessness; sixth, they are without any slackness or laziness and are revered by the assembly; seventh, the multitude loves and delights in them; eighth, they are endowed with great blessed fortune; ninth, at the end of their life they are born in heaven; tenth, they swiftly realize nirvāṇa. This is called the ten kinds of merit from offering food and drink.

"If sentient beings offer shoes and footwear, they obtain ten kinds of merit: first, they are endowed with fine vehicles; second, the soles of their feet are level and even; third, the tops of their feet are soft; fourth, they travel far with lightness and vigor; fifth, their bodies are free from fatigue; sixth, wherever they walk, thorns, tiles, and gravel do not harm their feet; seventh, they obtain supernatural powers; eighth, they are provided with all manner of attendants; ninth, at the end of their life they are born in heaven; tenth, they swiftly realize nirvāṇa. This is called the ten kinds of merit from offering shoes and footwear.

"If sentient beings offer incense and flowers, they obtain ten kinds of merit: first, their standing in the world is like flowers; second, their bodies are free from foul odor; third, the fragrance of their merit and the fragrance of their precepts pervade all directions; fourth, wherever they are born, their faculty of smell is not impaired; fifth, they surpass and excel the world, being the refuge and support of the multitude; sixth, their bodies are always fragrant and pure; seventh, they love and delight in the correct Dharma, receiving, upholding, and reciting it; eighth, they are endowed with great blessed fortune; ninth, at the end of their life they are born in heaven; tenth, they swiftly realize nirvāṇa. This is called the ten kinds of merit from offering incense and flowers.

"If sentient beings offer lamps and lights, they obtain ten kinds of merit: first, they illuminate the world like a lamp; second, wherever they are born, their fleshly eyes are not impaired; third, they obtain the divine eye; fourth, regarding wholesome and unwholesome dharmas, they obtain good wisdom; fifth, they dispel and extinguish great darkness; sixth, they obtain the light of wisdom; seventh, as they pass through the world, they are never in a place of darkness; eighth, they are endowed with great blessed fortune; ninth, at the end of their life they are born in heaven; tenth, they swiftly realize nirvāṇa. This is called the ten kinds of merit from offering lamps and lights.

"If sentient beings reverently join their palms in añjali, they obtain ten kinds of merit: first, they obtain superior blessed fortune; second, they are born in superior families; third, they obtain a superior and fine appearance; fourth, they obtain a superior and fine voice; fifth, they obtain a superior and fine canopy; sixth, they obtain superior and fine eloquence; seventh, they obtain superior and fine faith; eighth, they obtain superior and fine precepts; ninth, they obtain superior and fine wide learning; tenth, they obtain superior and fine wisdom. This is called the ten kinds of merit from reverently joining the palms in añjali."

At that time, after the World-Honored One had expounded this Dharma, Householder Śuka attained pure faith in the Tathāgata. At that time, Śuka bowed with his head at the Buddha's feet and spoke these words: "I now invite the Buddha to come to the city of Śrāvastī, to the home of my father, Householder Todeyya. May my father and all sentient beings long enjoy peace and happiness!" At that time, the World-Honored One, for the purpose of benefiting others, silently accepted the invitation.

At that time, Śuka, having heard what the Buddha had expounded, his heart filled with great joy, made obeisance at the Buddha's feet and withdrew.
