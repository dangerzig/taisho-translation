# 七佛讚唄伽他
## Gāthā Hymns in Praise of the Seven Buddhas

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1682

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmadeva (法天).

---

*[Translator's note: This text consists primarily of phonetically transcribed Sanskrit (梵讚, "Brahmic verse"), with Chinese characters serving as phonograms for each of the seven buddhas of the past, plus the future Buddha Maitreya. A verse of dedication follows, concluding with a Chinese quatrain. Clearly identifiable Sanskrit terms across the praises include: jagat, nara-loka, kṛpā, parahita, mokṣa, sugata, gati, tathāgata, ananta, praśama, prakāśa, dharma, namas, stuta, candra, buddha, saṃsāra, vinaya, mahāmuni, sūrya, loka, bodhi, muni, Maitreya, satya, stūpa, praṇāma, kuśala, and samāpta. Portions of uncertain reconstruction are enclosed in brackets.]*

### One Verse of Dedication and Conclusion

*stuta-vā[māya] saptā-[pūjya]-nā-[ndita] nānā-[ga]daṃ ca [aṣ]ṭa-ma bau[dhi]-sat[tvaṃ] ya[t] puṇya-[ṃ] [syān] [ni]ṣ[ṭ]ā[tmā]-pra[mi]yaṃ [ṃ]ra[māya] si[thi]-nā bha[ṃ] tu satva saptā-[pūjya]-nā sat[tva]-sarva samāpta*

> Having completed my praise of the seven buddhas of the past,
> And next having extolled the future Maitreya,
> The merit I have generated is boundless.
> May all sentient beings attain liberation.
