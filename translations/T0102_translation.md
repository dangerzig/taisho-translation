# 佛說五蘊皆空經
## Scripture Spoken by the Buddha on the Emptiness of the Five Aggregates

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 102

Translated from the Chinese. Tang Dynasty, translated by Yijing

---

Thus have I heard.

At one time the Bhagavān was dwelling at Ṛṣipatana in the Mṛgadāva near Vārāṇasī. At that time the World-Honored One addressed the five bhikṣus, saying: "You should know that form is not self. If it were self, form would not be subject to illness or suffering, and one could say of form: 'I wish form to be thus; I do not wish form to be thus.' Since it is not possible to have form according to one's wishes, know therefore that form is not self. Feeling, perception, mental formations, and consciousness are likewise. Furthermore, bhikṣus, what do you think? Is form permanent or impermanent?"

They said: "Venerable One, form is impermanent."

The Buddha said: "Since form is impermanent, it is suffering — whether the suffering of suffering, the suffering of change, or the suffering of conditioned existence. Would my well-learned śrāvaka disciples then cling to the view: 'Form is self; I possess forms; form belongs to me; I exist within form'?"

"No, World-Honored One!"

"Know that regarding feeling, perception, mental formations, and consciousness, the question of permanence and impermanence applies equally. All form — whether past, future, or present; internal or external; coarse or subtle; inferior or superior; near or far — is entirely without self. You should know to observe this well with right wisdom. All feeling, perception, mental formations, and consciousness — past, future, and present — should likewise be observed with right wisdom. If the noble śrāvaka disciples observe these five aggregates of clinging and know there is no self and nothing belonging to self, then having so observed, they know that in the world there is neither one who grasps nor an object grasped, nor any further transformation. Through their own realization they attain nirvāṇa: 'Birth is exhausted, the brahmacarya is established, what had to be done has been done, there will be no further becoming.'"

When this Dharma was taught, the five bhikṣus attained liberation of mind from all afflictions, and faithfully received and practiced the teaching.
