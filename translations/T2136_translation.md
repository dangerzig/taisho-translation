# Collection of Paired Chinese and Sanskrit Vocabulary

## *Táng Fàn Liǎngyǔ Shuāngduì Jí* (唐梵兩語雙對集)

Taishō 2136

Compiled by Tattvaguṇamiśraśāsa (*Sēng Dáduōniēduō Bōluóqúnà Míshèshā*, 僧怛多蘖多波羅瞿那彌捨沙) at the Vajrāsana Temple, Bodh Gayā. A bilingual vocabulary list.

*Note: This is a Chinese-Sanskrit bilingual vocabulary list compiled by the Indian monk Tattvaguṇamiśraśāsa at the Vajrāsana (Diamond Seat) temple beneath the Bodhi Tree in Magadha, central India. The original was presumably a parallel-column list pairing Chinese terms with their Sanskrit equivalents, but the Sanskrit column has been lost in transmission, leaving only the Chinese vocabulary organized by semantic category. The colophon records that the Japanese monk Jakuen (Jìyuán) copied and collated the text on the seventeenth day of the intercalary first month of the third year of Eishō (1058 CE). The vocabulary covers body parts, sensory experience, numbers, commerce, food, kinship, government, religion, temporal terms, geography, animals, plants, medicine, weapons, and household items.*
