# 禪行法想經
## Sūtra on Dharma Perceptions in Dhyāna Practice

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0605

Translated from the Chinese. Later Han, translated by An Shigao (安世高)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was dwelling at Śrāvastī, in Jeta's Grove, Anāthapiṇḍada's Park. The Buddha addressed the bhikṣus: "Bhikṣus!" The bhikṣus received the teaching and listened to the Buddha.

The Buddha said: "Bhikṣus! If for even the snap of a finger one contemplates the perception of death, reflecting that whatever has a body must die, this is to practice dhyāna with diligence, to act in accordance with the Buddha's teaching, and one is not foolishly eating the alms of the people in vain—how much more so for one who practices extensively! To state it in brief: if one contemplates the perception of impurity, the perception of repulsive food, the perception that all the world holds no pleasure, the perception of impermanence, the perception that impermanence is suffering, the perception that suffering is not-self, the perception that not-self is emptiness, the perception of relinquishment, the perception of dispelling desire, the perception of cessation, the perception of no-self; the perception of a dead body devoured by worms, the perception of blood flowing, the perception of bloating, the perception of blue-green putrefaction, the perception of rotting decay and putrid stench, the perception of hair fallen and flesh consumed, the perception of all bonds dissolved, the perception of joints and bones scattered, the perception of bones turning red, white, desiccated, and black, even to the color of a dove, the perception of bones crumbling to ash; the perception that the world has no refuge, the perception that the world has no solidity, the perception that the world is separation, the perception of the world's darkness, the perception that the world is difficult to endure, the perception that the world is wasteful expenditure of no use, the perception that the world is subject to calamity and worthy of aversion, the perception that all the world tends toward nirvāṇa—

"Bhikṣus, if for even the snap of a finger one contemplates these various perceptions, it is all diligent practice, it is to honor the Buddha's teaching, and one is not foolishly eating the alms of the people in vain—how much more so for one who is able to practice extensively! Therefore one should contemplate and practice these Dharma perceptions."

When the Buddha had finished speaking, all rejoiced and received it.
