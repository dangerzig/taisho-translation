# 佛說一切如來烏瑟膩沙最勝總持經
## Sūtra of the Uṣṇīṣavijaya Supreme Dhāraṇī of All the Tathāgatas, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0978

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmadeva (法天)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the land of Sukhāvatī, in the Great Good Dharma Hall, seated in serene composure. At that time, Amitāyus Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha, addressed the holy Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva-mahāsattva: "Good man! For the sake of all sentient beings who are afflicted by illness and suffering and who have short life-spans, in order to benefit them, there is the Dharma-gate of the Uṣṇīṣavijaya Supreme Dhāraṇī of All the Tathāgatas. If a person receives, upholds, reads, and recites it, they will swiftly obtain freedom from illness, long life, and peace."

At that time, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva-mahāsattva rose from his seat, joined his palms in reverence, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I now wish to hear this Dharma-gate of the Uṣṇīṣavijaya Supreme Dhāraṇī of All the Tathāgatas. May it be well proclaimed and well taught."

At that time, the World-Honored One observed the great assembly of humans and devas, then entered the samādhi of Universal Illumination and Auspiciousness. Having emerged from samādhi, he spoke this Dharma-gate of the Uṣṇīṣavijaya Supreme Dhāraṇī of All the Tathāgatas:

"*Oṃ namo bhagavate sarva-trailokya-prativiśiṣṭāya buddhāya te namaḥ tadyathā oṃ bhrūṃ bhrūṃ bhrūṃ śodhaya śodhaya viśodhaya viśodhaya asamasama-avabhāsa-spharaṇa-gati-gaganā-svabhāva-viśuddhe abhiṣiñcatu māṃ sarva-tathāgata-sugata-vara-vacana amṛta-vibhūṣite mahā-mudrā-mantra-pade āhara āhara āyuḥ-sandhāraṇi śodhaya śodhaya gaganā-svabhāva-viśuddhe uṣṇīṣa-vijaya-pariśuddhe sahasra-raśmi-saṃcodite sarva-tathāgata-avalokini ṣaṭ-pāramitā-paripūraṇi sarva-tathāgata-mati-daśa-bhūmi-pratiṣṭhite sarva-tathāgata-hṛdaya-adhiṣṭhāna-adhiṣṭhite mudre mudre mahā-mudre vajra-kāya-saṃhatana-pariśuddhe sarva-karma-āvaraṇa-viśuddhe pratinivartāya-ayuḥ-viśuddhe sarva-tathāgata-samaya-adhiṣṭhite oṃ muni muni mahā-muni vimuni vimuni mahā-vimuni mati mati mahā-mati mamata sumati tathata-bhūta-koṭi-pariśuddhe visphuṭa-buddhi-śuddhe hehe jaya jaya vijaya vijaya smara smara sphaṭa sphaṭa sarva-buddhādhiṣṭhāna-adhiṣṭhite śuddhe śuddhe buddhe vajre vajre mahā-vajre suvajre vajra-garbhe vijaya-garbhe vijaya-garbhe vajra-jvālā-garbhe vajra-udbhave vajra-saṃbhave vajrī vajrīṇi vajraṃ-bhavatu mama śarīraṃ sarva-sattvānāṃ ca kāya-pariśuddhe bhavatu me sadā sarva-gati-pariśuddhe śca sarva-tathāgata-śca māṃ samāśvāsayantu buddhe buddhaya vibudhaya vibudhaya mocaya mocaya vimocaya vimocaya śodhaya śodhaya viśodhaya viśodhaya samanta-raśmi-pariśuddhe sarva-tathāgata-hṛdaya-adhiṣṭhāna-adhiṣṭhite mudre mudre mahā-mudre mantra-pade svāhā*"

"Good man! This Dharma-gate of the Uṣṇīṣavijaya Supreme Dhāraṇī of All the Tathāgatas can extend one's life-span, eliminate transgressions, and swiftly attain purification. If one writes this dhāraṇī Dharma-gate on white silk or birch bark and places it within a stūpa, making extensive offerings, then circumambulates one thousand times with reverence and bows, and makes offerings according to one's ability, one will gain increased wisdom. If one has seven days of life remaining, one's life-span will be extended to seven years. If one has seven years of life remaining, one's life-span will be extended to seventy years. One will obtain such long life and happiness, free from all illness, and will gain knowledge of past lives and unfailing memory. If one wears it atop the head, the merits obtained and transgressions eliminated will be as described above.

"Furthermore, on clean silk or birch bark, write this dhāraṇī and one's own name in cow-yellow ink. Make a stūpa of sandalwood, place the dhāraṇī within, install it in one's room, offer extensive offerings, circumambulate one thousand times, and recite this dhāraṇī eight hundred times. All illnesses will be eliminated, and one's life-span will extend to one hundred years.

"Furthermore, if sandalwood is unavailable, one should make a stūpa of clean earth, draw a karma-vajra inside, draw a vajradhātu with four guardian gates on the outside, and write one's own name and place the dhāraṇī within. The merit obtained is no different from before. Alternatively, write this dhāraṇī in cow-yellow ink, place it in a clean vessel, cover it with another clean vessel, and install it in one's room. Make extensive offerings, and one will always be guarded and protected, free from all disasters.

"Furthermore, one should make a square maṇḍala from cow-dung that has not touched the ground, scatter white flowers upon it, and light four ghee-lamps at the four corners of the altar. Burn aloeswood and frankincense. Fill a bowl with argha water, and use white flowers to make a garland. Place this dhāraṇī either within a stūpa or within a merit-image, and set it upon the altar. The person performing the recitation should press the left hand upon the altar and hold a counting-rosary in the right hand. Three times daily, recite this dhāraṇī twenty-one times and empower three measures of water, then drink it oneself. This can eliminate all illnesses, extend life to one hundred years, resolve all enmity and grudges, bestow a wondrous voice, and grant unobstructed eloquence. Life after life, one will always obtain the supernatural power of knowledge of past lives. If one takes the previously empowered pure water and sprinkles it upon the royal palace, one's own dwelling, or the habitations of cattle and horses, it will swiftly remove the dangers of rākṣasas and nāga-serpents. One will always be guarded and protected, free from all fears. If one has an illness, sprinkle the water upon the crown of the head, and all serious diseases will be permanently eliminated. Thus are the immeasurable praises of this great dhāraṇī; whatever one undertakes will certainly be accomplished.

"Furthermore, take a willow branch, empower it with this dhāraṇī twenty-one times, and use it to clean the teeth. One will obtain freedom from illness, intelligence, and long life.

"Furthermore, fill a clean vessel with water, empower it with twenty-one recitations, and drink three measures of water three times daily. Each time one drinks, empower it with one additional recitation. All illnesses will be eliminated, and one will enjoy long life in peace and happiness."

At that time, the holy Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva-mahāsattva circumambulated Amitāyus Buddha three times, joined his palms in reverence, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! How should good men and good women make Buddha images, stūpas, and perform the homa accomplishment rites? I pray you will teach this."

The Buddha told the holy Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva: "Excellent, excellent! Great Fearless One! Listen with single-minded attention. As you have asked, I shall teach it for you." At that time, Amitāyus Tathāgata also entered the samādhi of Universal Illumination and Auspiciousness. Having emerged from samādhi, he spoke the Dharma-gate of the Amitāyus Dhāraṇī of All the Tathāgatas:

"*Oṃ amṛte amṛte amṛta-udbhave amṛta-vikrānte amṛta-gāminī amṛta-yuranī gagana-kīrti-kari sarva-kleśa-kṣayaṃ-kari svāhā*"

When Amitāyus Tathāgata had spoken this Dharma-gate, he addressed the bodhisattva: "If good men or good women use this Dharma-gate to empower clean earth, mix the earth with water and further empower it, then use the clay to make stūpas up to and including the finial, empowering each stage in sequence and making offerings, they will obtain vast and supreme benefit.

"Furthermore, the supreme method for stūpas: make stūpas of gold, silver, lapis lazuli, padmarāga jewels, or various other precious materials, adorned according to the Dharma, twelve finger-widths high, set upon a lotus-seat. On the four sides of the stūpa, place the Four Great Heavenly Kings holding banners. Before the stūpa, place Śakra-devānām-indra holding a bow. Also place the pure-abode devas, hands bearing incense, flowers, and unguent. On the left side of the stūpa, place Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva; on the right, Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva, each holding a white fly-whisk. Such is the form of the stūpa. Furthermore, paint a separate lotus, write the Uṣṇīṣavijaya Supreme Dhāraṇī of All the Tathāgatas around its circumference, and place it within the stūpa. Sprinkle and purify with scented water, and offer wondrous incense and flowers, making a thousand-fold offering. If one wishes to benefit all sentient beings, extend their life-spans, and increase their wisdom, one should always, on the eighth day of the bright fortnight, maintain purity day and night, keep the precepts in cleanness, and then before the jeweled stūpa recite this dhāraṇī one thousand times. Practice this each month, and after six full months one will obtain a life-span of one thousand years. Or, with sincere vigor, reciting day and night, one will attain a long life of a lakṣa of years. Or, with daily recitation, one's life-span will increase without limit. One will have great power, no different from devas, asuras, and others, and will travel freely through the sky, obtaining such merit. Furthermore, there is a ritual method: in a quiet place, take clean earth and mix with scented water to make clay stūpas, one, two, three, four, five, up to hundreds of thousands. Write the dhāraṇī above and place it within each stūpa. Make hundreds of thousands of kinds of great offerings. Recite this dhāraṇī seven hundred times. Daily one's wisdom will increase, and life will be extended without limit. Or, for any sentient being who is bound and imprisoned in shackles, recite this dhāraṇī and they will immediately be freed.

"Furthermore, if any sentient being, before the dhāraṇī stūpa, redoubles their offerings and daily recites eight hundred times, generating an equal mind of self-benefit and benefit for others, and practices according to this Dharma, the eight difficulties will be eliminated, one will always enjoy peace and happiness, life will be extended to one hundred years, all people will love and respect one, and before long one will obtain the supernatural power of knowledge of past lives. When that person's life ends, they will not be born in hell, the animal realm, the realm of Yama, or any evil destiny. Like a snake shedding its skin, one will immediately be reborn in the land of Sukhāvatī, obtaining great fruition and supreme and wondrous happiness, inexpressible in words. Nor will one even hear the sound of hell, let alone be born there."

At that time, Amitāyus Tathāgata further taught the method for accomplishing the painted image, so that sentient beings might obtain immeasurable life-span, be far from saṃsāra, and be liberated from all suffering. First, use thread spun by an unmarried maiden, of pure and fine quality, woven according to proper measurements into white silk. Use the finest colors to paint the form of the Uṣṇīṣavijaya Supreme Dhāraṇī merit-image with its subtle syllables. Place the image within a stūpa. The body has a thousand rays of light. It sits upon a lotus and moon-disc, fully adorned. The face is like a full moon. The image has three faces, three eyes, and eight arms. The right face is of a benevolent aspect and golden color. The left face is of a wrathful aspect, with fangs, of blue-lotus color. The central face is full and round, white in color. The first right hand is at the heart holding a karma-vajra. The second right hand holds a lotus upon which sits Amitāyus Buddha. The third right hand holds an arrow. The fourth right hand makes the wish-granting mudrā. The first left hand makes a vajra-fist holding a lasso with the index finger raised. The second left hand holds a bow. The third left hand forms the fearlessness mudrā. The fourth left hand holds a jeweled vase, with a stūpa atop the crown. On the neck, place the syllable *oṃ*. On the heart, place the syllable *aḥ*. On the forehead, place the syllable *hūṃ*. On the navel, place the syllable *trāṃ*. On the feet, place the syllable *hrīḥ*. "*A rakṣa svāhā*" as the guardian formula. Below the true words, write one's own name. On both sides of the image, paint Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva and Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva, hands holding white fly-whisks. Above the image, paint the pure-abode devas descending amṛta rain. On the four sides of the painting, depict the wrathful Vajra Acala Vidyā-rāja, Ṭakki Vidyā-rāja, Nīladaṇḍa Vidyā-rāja, and Mahābala Vidyā-rāja, each holding swords, hooks, vajras, and vajra-staffs, commanding the subjugation of evil māras. Paint the image with such single-minded devotion. When the practitioner performs this rite, one should first devote oneself with sincere mind before a relic-stūpa for one full day and night, maintaining the precepts in purity. Then, before the painted image, offer a thousand-fold offering. Beginning on the first day of the bright fortnight, recite this dhāraṇī one lakṣa of times. On the fifteenth day, again offer a thousand-fold offering. At dawn, the dhāraṇī's holy image will manifest before the practitioner. Whatever one seeks will be accomplished without exception.

"Furthermore, install this dhāraṇī painting in a quiet place, sprinkle and purify with scented water, and make offerings. Daily recite this dhāraṇī eight hundred times. Furthermore, once each month, offer a thousand-fold offering and recite the dhāraṇī one thousand times. One will obtain an immeasurable life-span, immeasurable awesome power, and freedom to travel through the sky. One will daily memorize a thousand verses with speech that is never confused, and will be able to cure all serious illnesses of sentient beings. If one cannot perform the rites as described above, simply in one's own dwelling, on the eighth day of the bright fortnight, sprinkle and purify with scented water, install the painting, and make offerings according to one's ability. With sincere mind before the image, recite this dhāraṇī seven hundred to eight hundred times. Each day at the three times, recite an additional twenty-one times. One's wisdom will increase, one will be free from illness and at peace, one's life-span will extend to one hundred years, and one will obtain knowledge of past lives. If one cannot perform the rite or recite oneself, one may commission another to do so, and will likewise obtain long life, intelligence, and wisdom.

"Furthermore, the homa method: for the benefit of sentient beings, make a round fire-pit, one cubit wide and twelve finger-widths deep. Mark the exterior with a vajra-boundary. Plaster it with a mixture of white sandalwood and white earth. Scatter white flowers within the pit, and place four ghee-lamps at the four sides. Offer incense and flowers on all four sides. Also place an argha-vase, fine and properly made. Wrap the top of the vase with white cloth, and place fruit-bearing tree branches at the mouth of the vase. Select green branches twelve finger-widths long from trees with white sap, along with dry firewood, and burn them together, then invoke the Fire Deity. After the invocation, pour three measures of ghee into the pit in three portions, empowering each with the Fire Deity's true words. Then dismiss the Fire Deity. Visualize Amitāyus Buddha clearly manifest within the pit. Then recite the Uṣṇīṣavijaya Supreme Dhāraṇī. Again, as before, pour three measures of ghee into the pit and recite this true word:

"'*Oṃ amita-ayurdhanāya svāhā*'

"Take the five grains, pinch a portion with three fingers dipped in ghee, state one's prayer, recite the true words once, and cast it into the pit. Do this up to eight hundred times. Perform the rite three times daily in the same manner. Furthermore, recite the Uṣṇīṣavijaya Supreme Dhāraṇī of All the Tathāgatas. One will be freed from all illness, gain intelligence and long life, and whatever one wishes will be accomplished without exception.

"Or on the eighth day, before the painted image, perform the homa rite three times daily, reciting this true word one thousand times. One's life-span will be extended by a thousand years. One must not harm any sentient being, and should constantly practice generosity. Such a ritual method can subdue enemies, increase wisdom, and bestow a wondrous voice. If one cannot perform the rite oneself, commission another to do so, and all evil disasters and afflictions will be pacified.

"Furthermore, the accomplishment method: as before, perform the homa rite using the five grains. Then recite the Uṣṇīṣavijaya Supreme Dhāraṇī one lakṣa of times. After the homa, recite another lakṣa. One's life-span will be extended by a lakṣa to a koṭi of years, or immeasurable years. One will travel freely through the sky and subdue māras and enemies. If one seeks wealth and auspiciousness, perform the homa as before and recite the dhāraṇī one lakṣa of times; one will obtain lasting great wealth. If one seeks official rank, perform a homa with lotus flowers one lakṣa of times; one will obtain high rank. Furthermore, the method for accomplishing the sword: install the painting before a relic-stūpa, offer a thousand-fold offering, and recite this dhāraṇī one lakṣa of times. Make a sword from the five metals and place it before the stūpa. Then recite the dhāraṇī another lakṣa of times to empower the sword. Having empowered the sword, take it in the right hand. One will obtain the power of wish-fulfilling transformation and freedom, with great awesome power and immeasurable life-span, able to bring fortune or misfortune to all sentient beings.

"Furthermore, performing the rite as before, using a vajra, a wheel, a trident, and other implements, all will be accomplished, obtaining the supreme and immeasurable merit described above. This great dhāraṇī is the heart of all the Tathāgatas, exceedingly rare. Whatever is performed as described above will certainly be accomplished. Furthermore, if a person, at the three times daily, recites the Uṣṇīṣavijaya Supreme Dhāraṇī Dharma-gate of All the Tathāgatas twenty-one times, makes offerings, and with sincere mind upholds it and explains it for others, that person and those sentient beings will obtain great happiness, long life, freedom from illness, great wisdom, and knowledge of past lives. When that person's life ends, like a snake shedding its skin, they will immediately be reborn in the land of Sukhāvatī, obtaining great fruition. Their ears will not even hear the sound of hell, let alone be born there." When the Buddha had finished teaching this sūtra, the entire world of devas, humans, asuras, gandharvas, and others, having heard what the Buddha taught, all greatly rejoiced, faithfully received it, and practiced accordingly.
