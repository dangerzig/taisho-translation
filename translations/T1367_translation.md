# 佛說大普賢陀羅尼經
## The Buddha's Teaching: Great Samantabhadra Dhāraṇī Sūtra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1367

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown.

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was in the country of Śrāvastī, in the Jeta Grove at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park. Then the Buddha addressed Ānanda: "I shall now teach the Great Samantabhadra Mantra. You should receive and uphold it.

*Tadyathā: aṭa-nāṭa ca-mī-ca-jā kuli kuli ca-yā kuli ca-yā bhadhi siddhi siddhā-bha muṃdhi.*

"Ānanda! This Great Samantabhadra Mantra wards off and extinguishes all bladed weapons, eliminates all enmity, eliminates all terrors from yakṣas, rākṣasas, bhūtas, and the like."

The names of the ghosts that prevent one from eating:

*Hu-ma-ṭu u-ṣā-hu hu hu-ma-ṭu a-ṇu-ka-pi hu-ma-ṭu bha-pa-rā hu-ma-ṭu svāhā.*

Empower water seven times, and give it to the sick person to drink.

The names of the ghosts that cause pain in the waist and legs:

*Hu-ru-ṭu pa-ṭa-rā hu-ru-ṭu vi-ma-rā hu-ru-ṭu miri-giri-pi hu-ru-ṭu svāhā.*

Empower three-colored cords of blue, yellow, and green. Tie seven knots. First bind them around the ankle, next around the thigh, and then around the waist.

The names of the ghosts that cause headaches:

*Hu-ma-ṭu mahā-kaṭa-rā vi-ma-kaṭa-rā hu-hu-rā-kaṭa-rā i-hu-kaṭa-rā i-mi-ka-ji-kaṭa-rā svāhā.*

Empower a willow twig seven times, and strike [the patient] fourteen times.

The names of the ghosts that cause dullness and stupor:

*Phu-ṭa-ṭa phu-ri-ci phu-ṭa-ṭa a-ci-ṇa hu-ṭa-ṭa phu-ri-ci-ci hu-ṭa-ṭa i-hu-bha-rā-ci hu-ṭa-ṭa si-mi-ṭu i-hu-ci-bha-rā svāhā.*

Twenty-one times; empower for seven days, three times each day.

The names of the ghosts that cause ear-pain:

*Bi-ṇi-pa a-je-ci vi-ṇi-pa hu-ṇi-ci vi-ṇi-pa i-hu-ci-ṇi-ci ṇi-pa gī-ma-ṇi vi-ṇi-pa svāhā.*

On the first day of the new moon: if the left ear aches, sit facing south. If the right ear aches, sit facing north. The door should face east. The patient sits inside the door; the mantra-master sits outside the door. [Empower] water also outside the door fourteen times, and blow on it three times.

The names of the ghosts that cause strangury:

*Bha-pa-rā phu-ri phu-ri-ci bha-pa-rā cā-mu-nā bha-pa-rā hu-hu-rā cā-pa-rā.*

For febrile diseases, ghost-and-spirit diseases, regional sorcery, poisonous gū-curses, spells, vetāḍas, and pūtanas: Good men and good women, wherever they may be, whether traveling on a road, in a waterway, or in a place of urgent peril, should recite this mantra. Then there will be no terrors from yakṣas, bhūtas, piśācas, kumbhāṇḍas, kaṭapūtanas, rākṣasas, vetāḍas, and the like. Nor will there be danger from water or fire, swords, staves, soldiers, violence, poisons, deterioration, spells, regional sorcery, or any evils from humans or non-humans. Ānanda! If there is any terror or urgent peril, one should recite this mantra, and nothing will be able to cause harm or distress. Furthermore, Ānanda! If a person receives, upholds, reads, and recites this mantra, then the devas, nāgas, ghosts, spirits, the twenty-eight classes, humans and non-humans described above will be unable to transgress against this mantra. The iron wheel and vajra wheel will bring suffering and affliction upon them, splitting their heads into seven pieces. In the four cardinal directions, the four intermediate directions, above and below, if any being gives rise to an evil mind toward such a person, all their poisonous intentions will be bound and prevented from arising. Such good men and good women should diligently read, recite, uphold, and practice it."
