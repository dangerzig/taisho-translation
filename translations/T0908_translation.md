# 金剛頂瑜伽護摩儀軌

## Vajraśekhara Yoga Homa Ritual Manual

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0908

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

> I now explain the homa,
> By which one swiftly attains accomplishment.
> Through the ritual procedures of homa practice,
> Yoga is maintained without interruption.
> All such matters as these
> Should be performed according to the vidyā.
> Performing homa according to its type
> Is the supreme practice of accomplishment.
> Homa is said to be of many kinds;
> In brief, there are five types.
> The extensive explanation belongs to the Great Yoga,
> Taught within the esoteric scriptures.
> I now explain them in brief:
> The sport of the vidyā-holder.
> Through the homa ritual manual,
> One accomplishes the clan-altar.
> The five kinds of homa activities
> Each have many varieties:
> Pacification and enrichment,
> The third is subjugation,
> Summoning is the fourth,
> The fifth is enchantment.
> Of these five homas,
> Enchantment is the most supreme.
> I now explain the *kuṇḍa* (fire-hearth),
> In accordance with yoga:
> For pacification, the hearth is perfectly round,
> And should be made thus.
> For enrichment, it should be perfectly square;
> Triangular for subjugation.
> A vajra-shaped *kuṇḍa*
> Is most supreme for summoning.
> Made long in lotus shape,
> It accords with enchantment.
> Having explained the five types,
> The *kuṇḍa* practice is supreme.
> Pacification begins in the first watch of night;
> Enrichment in the first part of day.
> The middle part of day is for performing
> The fierce methods of subjugation.
> Summoning may be done at any time;
> Enchantment in the last watch of night.
> These five yogas
> Accomplish their work through meditative absorption.
> Face north for pacification;
> Face east for enrichment.
> Face south for subjugation;
> Face west while abiding.
> Gazing upward in all directions
> Is the rite of summoning.
> For enchantment in accord,
> One should abide facing west.
> For pacification, form the Buddha-mudrā;
> For enrichment, the jewel emblem.
> Wrathful vajra for subjugation;
> Vajra hook for summoning.
> The great summoning accords
> With the Lotus Division for enchantment.
> In these five yogas,
> One should perform the homa activities.
> For pacification, burn sweet wood;
> For enrichment, use fruit wood.
> Bitter wood for subjugation;
> Thorny wood for summoning.
> Flower-wood is for enchantment:
> These five kinds of wood
> The yogin should employ.
> For the pacification hearth, inscribe a wheel;
> For enrichment, a three-pronged vajra.
> For subjugation, a single-pronged vajra;
> For summoning, make a hook.
> For enchantment, make a lotus.
> The pacification hearth dimensions:
> Full width, half-cubit height.
> Enrichment is two cubits,
> With depth at half the measure.
> The subjugation *kuṇḍa*:
> A triangle, each side one cubit,
> With depth at half.
> Summoning is one cubit long,
> Width and depth each half less.
> Enchantment is also one cubit,
> Width and depth like summoning.
> The five kinds of *kuṇḍa* altars
> Should be painted in three tiers.
> The central court has the karma-vajra,
> With lotuses painted in the four corners.
> The second court has four mudrās,
> Namely the Four Pāramitās,
> With inner offerings at the four corners.
> The third court should depict
> The eight directional devas and their retinues,
> The four corners at the four gates,
> Outer offerings and four attractors.
> Vairocana is placed at the center.
> This is the pacification *kuṇḍa*.
> The remaining four *kuṇḍa* configurations
> Follow this same three-court pattern.
> For enrichment, the central court
> Should depict the karma-jewel,
> With lotuses at the four corners.
> The second court should depict
> The retinue of Ratnasambhava Buddha.
> The third court and gates
> Are also as previously described.
> For subjugation, the central court has
> A single-pronged karma-vajra,
> With lotuses at the four corners.
> The second court should depict
> The retinue of Trailokyavijaya,
> In four wrathful forms.
> The third court and gates
> Are also as previously described,
> But all in wrathful aspect.
> For summoning, the central court
> Should depict a vajra hook,
> With lotuses at the four corners.
> The second court should depict
> The retinue of Akṣobhya Buddha.
> The third court, four corners,
> Eight directions and four gates:
> Know them as in the first *kuṇḍa*.
> For enchantment, the central court
> Depicts a lotus karma-emblem,
> With three-pronged vajras at the four corners.
> The second court should depict
> The retinue of Amitāyus,
> Depicting four honored ones.
> The third court, four corners,
> Eight directions and four gates
> Are also as previously described.
> This is the five homas,
> As taught in the Yoga Sūtra;
> Practitioners should know them.
> The four mudrās and four attractors,
> The inner and outer eight offerings,
> Arrayed in their altar positions:
> The ācārya now explains.
> The practitioner sits in the south;
> The Vajra Division should be in the south.
> The Jewel Division is in the west;
> The Dharma mudrā faces north.
> The Karma Division is in the east.
> Sport is in the southwest corner;
> Garlands in the northwest.
> Song is placed in the northeast;
> Dance in the southeast.
> Incense follows Sport's position;
> Flower-offering follows Garlands' place.
> Lamp follows Song's position;
> Perfume follows Dance's place.
> The hook is behind Vajra;
> The lasso faces the Jewel Division.
> The chain follows the Dharma mudrā;
> The bell follows Karma's position.
> Following the practitioner's rightward circuit,
> All altars should be thus,
> Arranged in cyclic order.
> In the pacification second court,
> The Four Pāramitā mudrās:
> A three-pronged vajra for Vajra,
> The Jewel mudrā in jewel-form,
> Dharma as a single-pronged vajra
> Topped with a blooming lotus,
> Karma as a karma-vajra.
> Sport: a three-pronged vajra;
> Garlands: like a jeweled crown.
> Song: depict a *kuṇḍa* harp;
> Dance: a single-pronged karma-emblem.
> The hook: a vajra hook.
> The lasso: like a coiled rope
> With a half single-prong
> At its center.
> The chain: like two rings side by side,
> Linked in the middle.
> The bell: a vajra bell.
> Lamp: a candle shape.
> Perfume: a fragrance vessel.
> Incense: a censer.
> Flowers: a flower tray.
> In the enrichment second court,
> Ratnasambhava's retinue:
> Light like a sun-disk;
> Laughter like a horizontal three-pronged vajra
> With teeth set in the middle.
> A banner like a vertical jeweled banner.
> Subjugation's four wrathful ones:
> Sattva with a three-pronged vajra;
> The King with two hooks side by side;
> Well-done with both palms
> In a finger-snapping gesture;
> Love with a vertical bow and arrow.
> The summoning second court
> Is also like the subjugation altar,
> Without increase or decrease.
> The enchantment second court,
> The retinue of Amitāyus:
> Dharma as the Dharma Pāramitā;
> Benefit as a sword shape;
> Speech depicted as a tongue;
> Cause as a sun-disk shape
> With a single-pronged karma in the center.
> The life-extension hearth is like enrichment,
> With armor shape drawn outside the hearth:
> Like a person wearing armor,
> With both sleeves hanging down.
> The sleeves like three single-prongs,
> Below like an inverted incense-cage,
> Above making a three-peaked form
> Like a three-pronged vajra.
> The inner and outer eight offerings
> And the four protectors:
> All hearths are alike.
> Each depicted mudrā
> Sits upon a lotus
> With a halo of flames.
> The eight directional deva retinues
> Are also like the various mudrās,
> All following the practitioner's seat,
> Beginning from the east.
> Indra: a single-pronged vajra
> With silk streamers flying left and right.
> The Fire Deva: a *kuṇḍikā* water-vessel
> On a lotus seat amid flames.
> Yama: a two-pronged fork
> With a human head set in the middle,
> Streamers flying like Indra's.
> The Rākṣasa Lord: a sword depicted,
> Seat with flames like the Fire Deva.
> The Water Deva: a lasso depicted
> With vajra-heads at both ends.
> The Wind Deva: a banner pennant
> Seated within a lotus.
> Vaiśravaṇa: a club depicted,
> Streamers bound as above.
> Īśāna: a half three-pronged vajra
> On a lotus seat with flaming light.
> The wise should understand well,
> Carefully, without error.

The hearth rim is two finger-breadths high and four finger-breadths wide. Inside the rim, at the base of the hearth opening, the width is two finger-breadths. The mudrā-emblem within is two finger-breadths high. Near the practitioner's side of the hearth, open a vertical neck, four finger-breadths wide and two finger-breadths long. Next, a horizontal section, ten finger-breadths long and four finger-breadths wide. Then make a lotus-petal shape of appropriate proportions. From the vertical neck to the petal tip is a total of twelve finger-breadths. The height throughout is level with the rim. All five types of hearths follow this same design. The method for preparing the ground is the same as for the great maṇḍala.

For digging the ground and empowering the spade and other implements: the mudrā is the vajra-bond with the thumbs and index fingers of both hands standing upright side by side. The mantra is recited twenty-one times:

*Oṃ nikhanavasudhā svāhā*

For empowering the clay, cow-dung, and perfumed ointment: the mudrā is both hands in añjali, bending the index and ring fingers so the second joints touch, thumbs standing upright together, the space between the index fingers forming a mouth-shape. The mantra is recited twenty-one times:

*Oṃ amṛtodbhava hūṃ phaṭ svāhā*

The mudrā and mantra for empowering the five-colored powder are as explained in the Yoga Sūtra. For empowering the ghee, honey, yogurt, milk, wood, five grains, incense, flowers, and so forth, use the Vajrakarma Bodhisattva mantra, empowering each seven times. The mudrā: with each hand, press the thumb against the ring-finger nail, the other three fingers spread open and upright like a vajra shape, then cross them with the right pressing the left. The mantra:

*Oṃ vajrakarma kaṃ*

All wood for burning in the homa is placed on the right side. The ghee goes on the lotus-leaf platform. Honey, yogurt, milk, rice-gruel, and cooked rice are placed near the right side of the hearth. On the left side, place two vessels of fragrant water. The vessels may be gold, silver, refined copper, white porcelain, or conch; the fragrance is white sandalwood, saffron, camphor, and the like. One vessel is for sprinkling the fire and offering materials; the other is for the holy assembly and Fire Deva to rinse their mouths. The sprinkling mudrā: press the thumb against the middle-finger nail, the other three fingers spread open and upright. The mantra:

*Oṃ amṛte hūṃ phaṭ*

The mouth-rinsing mudrā: the right hand in vajra-fist, extending the index finger to stir the water, empowering seven times. Then bend the four fingers into a scoop, ladle the water, lower the arm with palms together toward the body, and sprinkle the fire in a rightward circuit. The mantra:

*Oṃ varada vajradhāṃ*

For pacification: the honored one, Fire Deva, hearth, clothing, food, incense, and flowers are all white. Sit in the auspicious posture with a compassionate mind. For enrichment: all yellow, in full-lotus posture. For subjugation: all black, in squatting posture. For summoning: all red, in half-lotus posture. For enchantment: same color as summoning, in the sage's posture.

For the invitation: from the samaya through the invitation, all follow the standard method, or follow the five types of homa, becoming the five-aspect body of each division's lord. After inviting, recite praises, establish the holy assembly with the four attractors surrounding the hearth, then offer the *arghya*. Each forms their own karma-mudrā to establish their positions, displays the original samaya, and recites the homa mantra 108 times. Then take a flower, empower it with the Fire Deva mantra three or seven times, and cast it into the fire. Then form the Fire Deva mudrā: the left hand grasps the right wrist, the right hand extends the palm outward, the thumb lies horizontally across the palm, and the index finger moves like a hook, back and forth, to beckon and invite. After the offering, press the thumb against the index finger: this becomes the sending-off. The mantra:

*Oṃ ehy ehi mahābhūta devāriṣiṇi vijaya satvam agṛhītvā hutaye mahāramā siddhaye santuṣṭubhava aghnāye ha viyā kaviyā vahadanāya svāhā*

After inviting, sprinkle fragrant water three times and rinse three times. Then using the main mantra, with the large ladle take three full ladles of ghee and cast them into the fire, visualizing casting them into the Fire Deva's mouth and down to the lotus in his heart. The mantra:

*Oṃ aghnāye svāhā*

Then with this same mantra, use the small ladle to make three offerings each of honey, yogurt, milk, wood, and so forth through incense and flowers. Visualize the Fire Deva with four arms: the right hand in the fearlessness gesture, the second right hand holding a rosary; the left hand holding an ascetic's staff, the second left hand holding a *kuṇḍikā* water-vessel. Visualize from his heart, pervading his entire body, flowing forth immeasurable clouds of perfume, clouds of flowers, clouds of incense, food, lamps, and manifold offerings, making offerings to all Buddhas, bodhisattvas, pratyekabuddhas, śrāvakas, and all worldly devas. At the *svāhā* of the Fire Deva mantra, state what is sought and cast the offering. Then with the large ladle, three full ladles are offered. Empower a flower and place it at the original directional seat, requesting that he leave the hearth and return to his own seat. Then purify the fire three times. Using the four-syllable vidyā, invite the Buddhas and bodhisattvas, each to sit in their own seats. Offer three times, rinse three times, and with three full large ladles of ghee make the offering. Then with the small ladle, make three offerings each of honey, yogurt, milk, rice-gruel, cooked rice, wood, five grains, flowers, incense, and so forth, visualizing them cast into the holy honored ones' mouths, reaching their hearts.

If performing the pacification rite, add ten times the amount of sesame to the five grains. Use 108 sticks of wood, or 54, or 21. The mantra:

*Oṃ sarvapāpadahana vajrāya svāhā*

Some teachings say to use the division-mother mantra for pacification, or the honored one's own mantra, or Vairocana's mantra. In each case, above the *svāhā*, add words for the wish to remove all calamities for self and others. Concentrate the mind upon the holy assembly within the hearth. Visualize the holy assembly, from their hearts outward, through every pore of their bodies, flowing forth clouds and oceans of offerings reaching boundless worlds, making offerings to all Buddhas and removing all suffering of the three evil destinies. When the homa is complete, offer three full large ladles of ghee to the holy assembly. The remaining five grains, incense, flowers, and so forth are gathered in one vessel and offered to the worldly devas of the ten directions. The other hearths all follow this same procedure.

If performing enrichment: as before, invite the Fire Deva. Offer the holy assembly three large ladles of wood, incense, flowers, and so forth, all as before. Burn polished rice. If one wishes to extend life, burn *kuṭaja* (*kuṭaja*) grass. The life-extension hearth is like the enrichment hearth, with an armor shape drawn outside. The other incense, flowers, and so forth are all as before; only the polished rice and *kuṭaja* grass are increased tenfold over the other materials. The enrichment mantra:

*Oṃ vajrapuṣṭaye svāhā*

The life-extension mudrā: both hands in vajra-fist, extending the index fingers to hook each other, placed on the crown of the head. Visualize the body as Trailokyavijaya. On the mudrā, visualize Vairocana Buddha, from whose body flows forth celestial ambrosia, pouring down upon the practitioner's body. The life-extension mantra:

*Oṃ vajrāyuṣe svāhā*

Above the *svāhā*, add words for the wish of enrichment or life-extension for self and others, or whatever the heart desires at that time. Concentrate the mind upon the holy assembly within the hearth. Visualize from the holy assembly's hearts outward, through every pore of their bodies, flowing forth clouds and oceans of offerings reaching boundless worlds, making offerings to all Buddhas, and with radiant light illuminating and touching all sentient beings of the six destinies and four modes of birth, all attaining prosperity, wealth, nobility, and extended lifespan. Then with this same light, visualize raining the seven treasures and all needed provisions upon one's own home, and celestial ambrosia pouring down upon one's own body, pervading every pore.

If performing subjugation: as before, invite the Fire Deva. Use rapeseed oil, mustard-seed oil, water-buffalo ghee, or *rudra*-substance. First offer the holy assembly three large ladles. Then use unscented flowers or foul-smelling flowers, *guggula* resin, salt, poison, and the like, or use only iron filings, or make a figure in the shape of the target person, cut it into pieces, and cast them in.

When casting offerings to the Fire Deva, visualize from the Fire Deva's heart outward, his entire body flowing forth weapons and implements that fall upon the target person. Visualize the Fire Deva and the honored one both in wrathful form. The mantra:

*Hūṃ vajrasattvāya phaṭ*

Above the *phaṭ*, add the target person's name. Or use the method of one's own honored one, or the mantra of Acala, or Trailokyavijaya, or the Six-Footed Mañjuśrī. Visualize the wrathful honored one's body flowing forth clouds and oceans of weapons and implements, making offerings to all the wrathful honored ones throughout the sky, and then these weapons and implements falling upon the target's home and body.

If performing summoning: the invitation and all materials used are the same as enrichment. Only the flowers should be red flowers from thorny wood, or use whatever is burned in the method of the honored one. The mantra:

*Hūṃ vajrākarṣāya jaḥ*

Above the *jaḥ*, add the target person's name. Visualize from the honored one's heart outward, the entire body flowing forth immeasurable vajra hooks, making offerings to all Buddhas, bodhisattvas, and holy ones throughout the sky. Then with these hooks, hook and summon sentient beings of the three evil destinies, placing them in the wholesome realms of humans and devas. Then with these hooks, enter the target person's heart and summon them.

If performing the enchantment rite: the invitation and all materials are the same as above. Only the flowers should be red flowers, or use whatever is used in the method of the honored one. The mantra:

*Hūṃ vajraveśaya jaḥ*

Above the *jaḥ*, add the target person's name. Visualize from the honored one's body flowing forth flower-arrows pervading boundless worlds, making offerings to all Buddhas and holy ones, and striking the hearts of śrāvakas and pratyekabuddhas to dispel their aversion, and striking the mutually hostile hearts of sentient beings in the six destinies and four modes of birth. Then with these arrows, strike the target person in five places: the forehead, the two breasts, the heart, and the lower region.

For all hearths, if ghee is unavailable, milk may be used instead. If empowering a distant person, write their name or obtain the person's garment, and set the mind upon them while empowering. After making offerings to the holy assembly, use the large ladle with three full ladles to offer to the holy assembly, sprinkle three times and rinse three times. Then take the small ladle and recite the mantra for extinguishing the evil destinies, performing homa seven times, or twice seven, or three times seven, for all sentient beings. The mantra:

*Oṃ vajrapāṇi visphūṭaya sarvapāya bandhanāni pramokṣaya sarvapāyagati bhayasarvasattvān sarvatathāgata vajrasamaya hūṃ trāṭ*

Then mentally send the holy assembly back to their original seats. Using the four-syllable vidyā, guide the worldly devas of the ten directions into the hearth. Sprinkle and rinse as before. Then cast the remaining incense, flowers, five grains, ghee, honey, and so forth, each reciting the corresponding mantra once or three times. At each *svāhā*, add the words of what is sought. Then form the holy assembly's karma and samaya mudrās, recite praises, make aspirations, form the Trailokyavijaya mudrā, dissolve the boundary with a leftward rotation, and send off as in the念誦 (recitation) method. Then exit the ritual arena. Outside the arena in the eight directions, spread *kuśa* grass, lotus leaves, or other green grass, or anoint circular altars to make ten positions. At the right and left of Indra in the east, place Brahmā and the Earth Deva, making ten with the eight directions. If there is no room outside the arena for positions, then in a quiet place in front of the arena, create a square boundary. Within it arrange the eight directions, and in the center arrange two positions for Brahmā and the Earth Deva.

The food offering to the ten directional devas should be mixed gruel: polished rice, sesame, and mung beans cooked together, made extremely pure, fragrant, and delicious, placed in one vessel. At each seat, first place a clean leaf. The leaves are set in a circuit. First, with a clean vessel of fragrant water, pour a small amount onto each leaf as an offering. Next, flick a little perfumed ointment from the middle and ring fingers of the right hand as an offering. Next, offer a flower placed on the seat. Next, offer incense, burning it in a censer before each seat (one censer serves all seats). Next, place a ladleful of gruel on the leaf as an offering. Next, offer a small wax candle or paper taper, inserting it into the gruel. From the fragrant water through the candle, each is empowered with the corresponding mantra three times. After completing the offerings at each position, from water to candle, proceed to the next. Take care that the candles, once lit, do not go out before all positions have been served. Assistants or attendants, several persons each holding one item, are needed for the service; if each item must be fetched one at a time, the candles will surely not last through the procedure. At each position, above the *svāhā*, add the words of the aspiration.

The mantra for Indra, Lord of the East:

*Namo samantabuddhānāṃ indrāya svāhā*

The mantra for Agni, Deva of the Southeast:

*Namo samantabuddhānāṃ aghnāye svāhā*

The mantra for Yama, Deva of the South:

*Namo samantabuddhānāṃ yamāya svāhā*

The mantra for Nirṛti, the Rākṣasa Lord of the Southwest:

*Namo samantabuddhānāṃ nairṛtye svāhā*

The mantra for Varuṇa, Deva of the West:

*Namo samantabuddhānāṃ varuṇāya svāhā*

The mantra for Vāyu, Deva of the Northwest:

*Namo samantabuddhānāṃ vāyave svāhā*

The mantra for Vaiśravaṇa, Deva of the North:

*Namo samantabuddhānāṃ vaiśravaṇāya svāhā*

The mantra for Īśāna, Deva of the Northeast:

*Namo samantabuddhānāṃ īśānāya svāhā*

The mantra for Brahmā, Deva Above:

*Namo samantabuddhānāṃ brahmaṇe svāhā*

The mantra for Pṛthivī, the Earth Deva Below:

*Namo samantabuddhānāṃ pṛthivyai svāhā*

The mantra for the Seven Luminaries:

*Namo samantabuddhānāṃ grahāśvarya prabhāto jyotir māyā svāhā*

The mantra for the Twenty-Eight Constellations:

*Namo samantabuddhānāṃ nakṣatra-niśānakhaye svāhā*

Among the eight directions, add two positions to correspond with the devas above and below; the luminaries in the east, the constellations in the west. All offerings are the same. If there is a separate aspiration, one may use them individually. Within the homa altar, each is mentally established according to their proper direction, without setting up separate positions.

Next, the Samāpatti Homa Method is explained.

Place the object to be empowered before the ghee vessel. If the object is large, place it on the right or left side. The practitioner's own body, the ghee vessel, the object, the hearth, and the holy assembly: these five are assembled and should be arranged in cyclic sequence. Take the small ladle, fill it with ghee, and pour it upon the object to be accomplished. Recite the mantra up to the *svā* syllable, then raise the ladle and cast it into the fire. As the *hā* sound falls, draw it out long, bringing the ladle back to the object; the *hā* sound ends only then. Repeat this procedure throughout. If empowering a person, place the ladle upon their head. If the honored one's own mantra does not contain the *svāhā* syllables, they should be added when reciting. The rest is as described above.

Next, the form of the ladles is explained.

> I now next explain
> The pouring-ladle and the casting-ladle.
> By abiding in these for accomplishment,
> The mantra-holder achieves swiftly.
> The pouring-ladle is one cubit in length,
> Made of sturdy, fine-grained wood,
> Without holes or cavities.
> The bowl should be wonderfully handsome,
> Four finger-breadths across,
> One finger-breadth deep,
> In the shape of a *śrīvatsa* mark.
> Within it, a three-pronged vajra
> Should be made beautifully.
> The handle's circumference fits one hand-grip.
> Near the bowl and at the handle's end,
> Lotus patterns should be carved.
> The casting-ladle's length, roundness,
> And carved decorations
> Are all like the pouring-ladle.
> The wood is also as described before,
> Or one may use *khadira* wood.
> The bowl uses the upper joint of the thumb
> As its circumference measure,
> A little over one inch across,
> With depth at half.
> Within it, make a lotus
> Or a vajra emblem.
> I have now briefly explained
> The pouring and casting ladles.
> As the Great Seer has taught,
> Those who seek siddhi should make them:
> The mantra-holding practitioners.
