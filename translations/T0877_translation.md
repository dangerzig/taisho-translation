The Buddha told the Vajra-Secret Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva: "Having taught these vajra-deity siddhi mudrā-contracts and secret mantra-syllables, abide in great quiescence, with mind far removed from all. Within the Moon-Love Samādhi's white ūrṇā-wheel, contemplate in sequence with clear and universal manifestation. All the Tathāgatas' vajra great maṇi-cintāmaṇi jewel: all are of one dharma-nature, one true Dharma-realm, one taste of suchness, neither coming nor going, without marks and without action, the pure Dharma-body illuminating the ocean of perfect quiescence."

> These eight hundred vajra-phrases originally arise from the single Dharma of samādhi.
> Contemplating and illuminating the non-dual gate of true suchness, one enters the Tathāgata's ocean of perfect quiescence.
> Skillfully knowing the outgoing phrases and the incoming phrases, the yoga siddhi and its ritual forms:
> The myriad practices of bodhi are cultivated in a single instant, forever leaving behind the delusions of beginningless time.
> The eighty-four thousand wondrous Dharma-gates are entered through a single syllable, a single mantra, a single mudrā-contract.
> The merit of Ganges-sands cannot be calculated: they vastly liberate sentient beings without limit.
> Person-emptiness, dharma-emptiness, self-nature-emptiness: the one true Dharma-realm is originally empty.
> All the Perfectly Awakened Ones of the ten directions, the guiding masters, all rely on these vajra-phrases.
> One phrase, one mudrā, one mantra: first distribute from the heart-wheel to the crown,
> Both shoulders, left and right ear-tips, the white ūrṇā-mark, the hairline, and between the eyebrows.
> The neck, both cheeks, the philtrum, the lips, teeth, eyes, palate, and within the throat.
> Both arms and ten fingers as the pāramitās; the chest, the back, and both feet.
> The navel-wheel, and above it the vajra palace; the heart-king, the bright moon, abiding in the treasury.
> Pearl-banners and jeweled parasols adorn everywhere: without marks, without coming, and without going.
> The two eyes are lotus-flowers, the vajra lamp; play and incense at the nose.
> The siddhi seat is the vajra wheel: pure radiance like the full moon.
> Thus body and mind, one hundred and eight, contemplate in sequence and make them clear and pure.
> Suddenly awakening to the secret contract of bodhi, one freely travels the path of nirvāṇa.
> Having established the worldly siddhi, then in accord with abhiṣeka perform the recitation.
> Truly a victorious child of the Tathāgata, fulfilling the pure and white one-vehicle Dharma.
> When the armies of Māra see such a person, they join their palms and call them "Savior of the World."
> Furthermore, the vajra of non-moving wisdom, the place of ultimate attainment of the Dharma-body Buddhahood:
> Taught in the "Chapter on Supreme Meaning" of the *Yoga*. Revered and gazed upon like the World's Guide.
> Transmitting the Bhagavān's secret gate, the eight divisions and dragon-hosts all pay respect.
> When walking, sitting, or reciting: the outgoing and incoming are the marks of pure speech.
> The mouth contains a golden vajra; the two eyes' *maṭa* removes past obstructions.
> Constantly think of sitting in a bright moon's mirror, its expanse pervading the Dharma-realm like the measure of empty space.
> The mind attains freedom, with neither going nor coming; merit is constantly practiced and universally dedicated.

## Gate of the Dharma-Body Contemplation:

> Homage to Vairocana Buddha, the vajra-summit of all the Tathāgatas!
> In the great Dharma-realm practice-site, with one mind contemplate and clearly see:
> Wondrous, lofty, supremely rare Mount Sumeru, ascended by spiritual power and holy might.
> Surveying the buddha-lands of the ten directions, as though in a maṇi-jewel held in the palm.
> Awakening to this śūraṅgama samādhi, one abides in the Tathāgata's auspicious ground.
> Homage to Vairocana Buddha, the vajra-king of all the Tathāgatas!
> The ocean of self-existent radiance and quiescent extinction: the Perfectly Awakened of the ten directions all call it unsurpassed.
> The merit of Ganges-sands is fully complete; markless purity equals true emptiness.
> Awakening to this bodhi-contract samādhi, one abides in the Tathāgata's auspicious ground.
> Homage to Vairocana Buddha, the vajra-peak of all the Tathāgatas!
> The crown-mark is boundless, beyond sight, transcending the ocean of worlds in the ten directions.
> A thousand crags and ten thousand valleys of maṇi-jewels, reflected in lapis lazuli and crystal vessels.
> Awakening to this samādhi of myriad-image jewels, one abides in the Tathāgata's auspicious ground.
> Homage to Vairocana Buddha, the vajra-life of all the Tathāgatas!
> Life-span without end, beyond calculation: the land of utmost bliss with water-birds, trees, and groves.
> Within flowers, beings are suddenly born; from jewels, birds sing the Dharma's sound.
> Bodhisattvas bathe in golden-sand waters; in a single thought, throughout the ten directions, they receive their predictions.
> Awakening to this swift and non-retrogressing contemplation, one abides in the Tathāgata's auspicious ground.
> Homage to Vairocana Buddha, the vajra-seat of all the Tathāgatas!
> The ocean-seal of Ganges-sands of siddhi-jewels: boundless Sugatas ascend it.
> The circle of light pervades the Dharma-realm without obstruction, like gazing at the moon in water or a reflection in a mirror.
> Lion-thrones filling empty space, with seven-jeweled golden lotuses, pearl parasols, and canopies.
> Awakening to this siddhi-contract samādhi, one abides in the Tathāgata's auspicious ground.
> Homage to Vairocana Buddha, the vajra-body of all the Tathāgatas!
> Pervading the Dharma-realm in boundless measure, manifesting everywhere and filling empty space.
> Able to make one body into many bodies, and again manifest many bodies as one body.
> Having body and marks is the expedient coming to transform; non-body, non-marks is the unconditioned.
> Awakening to this nirvāṇa-palace samādhi, one enters the Tathāgata's auspicious ground.

The corresponding dhāraṇī flowing from Vairocana's siddhi:

*Namaḥ sarva-tathāgatebhyo viśva-mukhebhyaḥ sarvathā a ā aṃ aḥ*
