# 曼殊室利童子菩薩五字瑜伽法
## The Five-Syllable Yoga Method of Mañjuśrī Kumāra Bodhisattva

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1176

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空)

---

*Note: This text consists almost entirely of dhāraṇī passages; phonetic transcriptions of Sanskrit mantras into Chinese characters. The reconstructed Sanskrit provided below is based on the phonetic values of the Chinese transcription, standard Buddhist mantra conventions, and phonological analysis of the combinatory notations (二合, 三合, 四合). Reconstructions of uncertain passages are marked with †.*

---

The first is:

[Dhāraṇī: *a*]

The second is:

[Dhāraṇī: *drīṃ*] (four syllables combined into one)

Next, the three-syllable mantra:

[Dhāraṇī: *vāke hūṃ*]

Next, the five-syllable mantra has five varieties:

The first is:

[Dhāraṇī: *a ra pa ca na*]

The second is:

[Dhāraṇī: *oṃ vajratīkṣṇa*] (recited as a single syllable)

The third is:

[Dhāraṇī: *oṃ nūkhacina*†]

The fourth is:

[Dhāraṇī: *oṃ gagrakhaṃ*†]

The fifth is:

[Dhāraṇī: *oṃ gagasattva*†]

The six-syllable yoga mantra has six varieties:

[Dhāraṇī: *oṃ vākye nainama*†]

[Dhāraṇī: *oṃ vākye rījaya*†]

[Dhāraṇī: *oṃ vākye śīśrīsvā*†]

[Dhāraṇī: *oṃ vākye khājaya*†]

[Dhāraṇī: *oṃ vākye [?] ṣṭhāṃāya*†]

[Dhāraṇī: *oṃ vākye mānasa*†]

Next, the mantra for consecrating the initiation vase:

[Dhāraṇī: *namo stryā-dvī-kānāṃ tathāgatānāṃ hrīḥ sarva-buddhānāṃ virājyā raśmiyā viśeke raviśubha† viśuddhe mahā-māmalāvatī† vīrācale svāhā*]

The mantra for accomplishing the adornment of bodhi:

[Dhāraṇī: *oṃ rucira-maṇi-prabhavāya hūṃ*]

The praise of the Great Sage Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva:

[Dhāraṇī: *mañjuśrīye namaḥ stuvyaṃ kumāra kāradhāriṇī sphurita-jñāna trailokya-dhvaṃtahāriṇī vajratīkṣṇa mahāyāna vajraghoṣa mahāyudha mañjuśrī vajragambhīrya vajrabuddhi namaḥ stute*]
