# 佛說彌勒下生成佛經
## Sūtra of Maitreya's Descent and Attainment of Buddhahood Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0455

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Yijing (義淨)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Bhagavān was on Vulture Peak Mountain in the city of Rājagṛha, together with a great assembly of bhikṣus.

At that time, the great wise Śāriputra, foremost among the Dharma-generals, out of compassion for the world, rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, placed his right knee on the ground, joined his palms in reverence, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I now wish to ask a small question. May you graciously permit me."

The Buddha told Śāriputra: "Ask whatever you wish, and I shall explain it for you."

Then Śāriputra addressed the World-Honored One in gāthā:

> "The Great Teacher has given a prediction
> That a future Buddha will descend and be born.
> His name will be Maitreya,
> As stated in former and later sūtras.
> I beg you, Honored among Humans,
> To elaborate again in verse:
> His supernatural powers and awesome virtue
> I now delight in hearing."

> The Buddha told Śāriputra: "Listen with your whole heart.
> I shall explain at length for you
> The future Honored Maitreya.

> At that time, the great ocean's waters
> Will gradually recede by three thousand
> Two hundred yojanas,
> To reveal the path for the wheel-turning king.

> Jambudvīpa in length and breadth
> Will be ten thousand yojanas.
> Sentient beings dwelling within it
> Will fill every place completely.

> All countries will be rich and prosperous,
> Without punishment and without calamity.
> All the men and women there
> Will have been born through good karma.

> The ground will have no thorns or brambles,
> Producing only soft green grass.
> Underfoot it yields to human steps,
> As soft as tūla cotton.

> Fragrant rice will grow naturally,
> With delicious flavors in full abundance.
> Trees will produce garments
> Adorned with many colors.

> The trees will be three krośas tall,
> Always laden with flowers and fruit.
> The people of that country at that time
> Will all live to be eighty thousand years old.

> Free from all illness and pain,
> Far from affliction and always at peace,
> Complete in marks and of dignified appearance,
> Perfect in complexion and strength.

> The people will have only three ailments:
> The need for food, aging, and excretion.
> Women will not marry
> Until the age of five hundred.

> Whenever they need to relieve themselves,
> The earth will split open to receive it.
> When a person's life draws to its end,
> They will go of their own accord to the charnel ground.

> The city will be named Ketumatī,
> Capital of the wheel-turning king.
> Twelve yojanas in length
> And seven yojanas in breadth.

> All who dwell therein
> Will have planted excellent karmic seeds.
> This city will have surpassing virtue,
> And its inhabitants will all be joyful.

> Towers, terraces, and parapets
> Will all be made of the seven treasures.
> Gates, locks, and courtyards
> Will be adorned with various jewels.

> The surrounding battlements and moats
> Will all be fashioned of wondrous gems.
> Renowned flowers will fill them completely,
> And fine birds will gather and soar.

> Seven rows of tāla trees
> Will encircle and surround the city,
> Adorned with many treasures,
> All hung with nets of bells.

> Gentle breezes blowing through the jeweled trees
> Will produce all manner of wondrous sounds,
> Like the playing of eight kinds of music,
> Bringing joy to all who hear.

> Everywhere there will be ponds and pools
> Covered with flowers of various colors.
> Gardens with fragrant groves
> Will adorn the city precincts.

> In that country there will be a holy sovereign
> Whose name will be Saṃkha,
> A gold-wheel king of the four continents,
> Rich, prosperous, and of great power.

> That king's merit and virtuous deeds,
> His valor and fourfold army,
> The seven treasures all perfected,
> A thousand sons all complete.

> The four seas will all be calm and peaceful,
> Without war or weapons.
> With the correct Dharma he will govern all beings,
> His transformative influence equal to all.

> The king will have four great treasuries,
> Each in a different country.
> In each treasury there will be
> Hundreds of millions of precious jewels.

> In the country of Kaliṅga,
> A treasury named Piṅgala;
> In the country of Mithilā,
> The great treasury of Pāṇḍuka.

> The Elāpatra treasury
> Will be in the country of Gandhāra;
> In the region of Vārāṇasī,
> A treasury named Saṃkha.

> These four hidden treasuries
> Will all belong to King Saṃkha,
> Furnished by a hundred blessings,
> Their karmic fruits all perfected.

> The king's great minister,
> The brahmin Subrahmā,
> Will be learned in the four Vedas,
> A man of great learning, the national preceptor.

> Broadly versed in various treatises,
> Skilled in teaching and retaining what he hears,
> In exegesis and in grammar,
> There will be nothing he does not fully comprehend.

> He will have a daughter named Jingmiao,
> Who will be the great minister's wife.
> Renowned and of dignified appearance,
> All who see her will be delighted.

> The Great Being Maitreya
> Will depart from the Tuṣita heaven
> And come to be conceived by that lady,
> Making it the place of his final birth.

> Having carried this great sage
> For the full ten months,
> Maitreya's honored mother
> Will go to the Wondrous Flower Garden.

> Arriving in that wondrous garden,
> Neither sitting nor lying down,
> She will stand gently, grasping a flowering tree,
> And in an instant give birth to the supreme Maitreya.

> At that time, the most supreme honored one,
> Having emerged from his mother's right side,
> Like the sun emerging from clouds,
> Will radiate a great light everywhere.

> Unstained by contact with the womb,
> Like a lotus rising from water,
> His light will stream through the three realms,
> And all will look up to great Maitreya's radiance.

> At the moment of his descent and birth,
> The thousand-eyed Lord Śakra
> Will personally receive the bodhisattva in his arms,
> Joyfully greeting the Two-Footed Honored One.

> The bodhisattva at this time
> Will naturally take seven steps,
> And from wherever his feet tread
> Jeweled lotus flowers will spring forth.

> Looking all around in the ten directions,
> He will proclaim to all devas and humans:
> 'This is my final body;
> Beyond birth, I shall realize nirvāṇa.'

> Nāgas will pour down cool water
> To bathe the body of the Great Compassionate One.
> Devas will scatter wondrous flowers,
> Raining them throughout the sky.

> Devas will hold white parasols
> To shade and shelter great Maitreya.
> Each will give rise to thoughts of wonder,
> Guarding and protecting the bodhisattva.

> Nursemaids will hold the bodhisattva,
> His body bearing the thirty-two marks,
> Complete with all radiance,
> And present him to his mother.

> The charioteer will bring a carved palanquin,
> All adorned with jewels.
> Mother and child will ascend within,
> And devas together will bear the litter.

> A thousand kinds of wondrous music
> Will lead the procession back to the palace.
> As Maitreya enters the capital,
> Heavenly flowers will rain down like showers.

> On the day Maitreya is born,
> All the women with child in the palace
> Will all have safe and easy deliveries,
> Each giving birth to a wise son.

> Subrahmā, Maitreya's father,
> Beholding his son's wondrous appearance,
> Complete with the thirty-two marks,
> His heart will be filled with great joy.

> Following the method of prognostication,
> The father will know the child has two destinies:
> As a householder, he would be a wheel-turning king;
> As a renunciant, he will achieve perfect enlightenment.

> Once the bodhisattva has come of age,
> Compassionate toward all beings,
> Amid the dangers and difficulties of suffering,
> Saṃsāra turns without ceasing.

> His golden-colored radiance bright,
> His voice like the sound of great Brahmā,
> His eyes like blue lotus petals,
> His limbs all perfectly complete.

> His body will be eighty cubits tall,
> His shoulders twenty cubits broad,
> His face half the breadth of his shoulders,
> Dignified as the full moon.

> The bodhisattva, brilliant in all arts,
> Will teach well those who come to learn.
> Students young and old alike
> Will number eighty-four thousand.

> At that time, King Saṃkha
> Will erect a seven-jeweled banner,
> Seventy fathoms tall
> And sixty fathoms wide.

> When the jeweled banner is complete,
> The king will give rise to a great mind of renunciation,
> Offering it to the brahmins
> And setting up an unrestricted feast.

> The brahmins at that time,
> Numbering one thousand,
> Having received the wondrous jeweled banner,
> Will demolish it in a moment.

> The bodhisattva, seeing this,
> Will reflect: 'All worldly things are thus.
> Birth and death are the fetters of suffering.
> I must seek liberation.'

> Praying for the path of tranquil extinction,
> He will abandon the world and go forth.
> From birth, old age, sickness, and death,
> He will rescue beings and lead them out.

> Maitreya will make the vow:
> 'May eighty-four thousand persons
> Together give rise to minds of renunciation
> And all follow me in practicing the holy life.'

> On the very night of his initial resolve,
> He will abandon the world and go forth.
> On that very same night,
> He will ascend to the stage of equal awakening.

> At that time there will be a bodhi tree
> Named the Dragon-Flower Tree,
> Four yojanas tall,
> Luxuriant and flourishing.

> Its branches will spread in all four directions,
> Their shade extending six krośas.
> The great compassionate Maitreya
> Will attain perfect enlightenment beneath it.

> Supreme among humans,
> Endowed with the eight Brahmā tones,
> He will teach the Dharma to save sentient beings,
> Causing them to leave all afflictions.

> Suffering and the origin of suffering
> Will all be extinguished.
> Those who cultivate the Eightfold Noble Path
> Will ascend to the shore of nirvāṇa.

> For all the faithful,
> He will teach these Four Noble Truths.
> Those who hear this correct Dharma
> Will sincerely receive and uphold it.

> In the Wondrous Flower Garden,
> The multitudes will gather like clouds,
> Filling a hundred yojanas,
> All his retinue completely assembled.

> When the wheel-turning King Saṃkha
> Hears the profound and wondrous Dharma,
> He will relinquish all his treasures
> And with a devoted heart aspire to go forth.

> Not clinging to the upper palace chambers,
> He will earnestly seek liberation.
> Eighty-four thousand persons
> Will all follow him in going forth.

> Another eighty-four thousand
> Young brahmins,
> Hearing the king has left the worldly life,
> Will also come seeking ordination.

> The treasurer and chief elder,
> Whose name will be Sudatta,
> Together with a thousand retainers,
> Will also come seeking ordination.

> The jeweled woman Viśākhā,
> Along with her attendants,
> Eighty-four thousand persons,
> Will also come seeking ordination.

> Yet another hundred thousand
> Good men and good women,
> Hearing the Buddha proclaim the wondrous Dharma,
> Will also come seeking ordination.

> The Honored One of devas above and humans,
> The lord of great compassion,
> Having surveyed the minds of all,
> Will set forth the essential Dharma.

> He will tell the assembly: 'You should know,
> The compassionate Lord Śākyamuni
> Taught you to cultivate the correct path,
> And so you are born into my Dharma.

> 'Those who with fragrant flowers and garlands,
> Banners, streamers, and canopy adornments
> Made offerings to the Sage:
> They are born into my Dharma.

> 'Those who with uḍumbara and agarwood,
> Fragrant paste used for anointing,
> Made offerings at the Sage's stūpas:
> They are born into my Dharma.

> 'Those who took refuge in the Buddha, Dharma, and Sangha,
> With reverence constantly drawing near,
> Always cultivating good deeds:
> They are born into my Dharma.

> 'Those who within the Buddha-Dharma
> Received and upheld the precepts,
> Guarding them well without breach:
> They are born into my Dharma.

> 'Those who to the sangha of the four directions
> Gave clothing, food, and drink,
> And also offered fine medicine:
> They are born into my Dharma.

> 'Those who on the four fasting days
> And during the months of supernatural powers
> Received and upheld the eight precepts:
> They are born into my Dharma.'

> Or with the three kinds of supernatural powers,
> The bases of miraculous ability, prediction, and instruction,
> He will transform and guide the śrāvaka assembly,
> Causing all their afflictions and delusions to cease.

> At the first assembly, teaching the Dharma,
> He will broadly liberate the śrāvakas:
> Nine billion six hundred million persons
> Will be freed from the obstruction of afflictions.

> At the second assembly, teaching the Dharma,
> He will broadly liberate the śrāvakas:
> Nine billion four hundred million persons
> Will cross the ocean of ignorance.

> At the third assembly, teaching the Dharma,
> He will broadly liberate the śrāvakas:
> Nine billion two hundred million persons
> Will have their minds well tamed.

> Having turned the Dharma-wheel three times,
> Devas and humans will all be purified.
> Leading the assembly of disciples,
> He will enter the city to beg for food.

> Upon entering the city of Ketumatī,
> The streets will all be adorned.
> To make offerings to the Buddha,
> Heavenly māndārava flowers will rain down.

> The Four Kings and the Brahmā King,
> Together with the other hosts of devas,
> With fragrant flowers and garlands will make offerings,
> Flanking the Great Compassionate One.

> Devas of great awesome virtue
> Will scatter wondrous garments,
> Showering them throughout the city,
> Gazing up at the Great Medicine King.

> With wondrous jeweled flowers and incense
> Scattered along every street,
> Walking upon them
> Will be as soft as tūla cotton.

> Music, banners, and streamers
> Will line both sides of the road.
> The hosts of devas, humans, and Śakra
> Will praise the Great Compassionate One:

> 'Homage to the Honored One among devas!
> Homage to the Supreme among humans!
> Well done, Bhagavān,
> Who has compassion for the world!'

> Devas of great awesome virtue,
> Even those who serve as the māra hosts,
> With devoted hearts will join palms in reverence,
> Praising and revering the Guide.

> Brahmā King and the heavenly hosts,
> Surrounded by their retinues,
> Each with the voice of Brahmā
> Will expound the subtle and wondrous Dharma.

> In this world,
> Most will be arhats,
> Having eliminated all outflow-producing karma,
> Forever free from the suffering of afflictions.

> Devas, humans, nāgas, spirits,
> Gandharvas, asuras,
> Rākṣasas and yakṣas:
> All will joyfully make offerings.

> At that time, the great assembly,
> Having severed obstructions and eliminated doubt,
> Will transcend the current of birth and death,
> Well cultivating the practice of purity.

> At that time, the great assembly,
> Free from attachment and abandoning treasures,
> Without thoughts of self or mine,
> Will well cultivate the practice of purity.

> At that time, the great assembly,
> Having destroyed the net of craving and attachment,
> With minds perfected in meditation,
> Will well cultivate the practice of purity.

> Maitreya, honored among devas and humans,
> With compassion for sentient beings,
> For the duration of sixty thousand years
> Will teach the Dharma and liberate beings.

> Having transformed hundreds of billions to fullness,
> Causing them to cross the ocean of afflictions,
> All who have karmic connections will be rescued,
> Before he enters the city of nirvāṇa.

> After the great compassionate Maitreya
> Enters parinirvāṇa,
> The correct Dharma will endure in the world
> Also for a full sixty thousand years.

> If within my Dharma
> One can believe and receive with a profound mind,
> On the day of the future descent,
> One will surely attend the Great Compassionate One.

> If there are wise persons
> Who hear of these matters as taught,
> Who would not give rise to joyful aspiration
> And wish to meet the Honored Maitreya?

> If persons seeking liberation
> Hope to attend the Dragon-Flower Assembly,
> They should constantly make offerings to the Three Treasures
> And be diligent, never negligent."

At that time, the World-Honored One, having related these matters of the future Maitreya for Śāriputra and the great assembly, further told Śāriputra: "If there are good men and good women who, having heard this Dharma, receive, uphold, read, and recite it, expound it for others, practice it as taught, make offerings of incense and flowers, and copy this sūtra, all such persons in the world to come will certainly encounter Maitreya's descent and in the three assemblies will all receive his salvation."

At that time, when the World-Honored One had spoken these verses, Śāriputra and the great assembly rejoiced, believed, and received them, bowing their heads in respectful practice.
