# 佛說懈怠耕者經
## Sūtra of the Lazy Plowman, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0827

Translated from the Chinese. Liu Song dynasty, translated by Huijiǎn (惠簡)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was in Rājagṛha, in the Bamboo Grove, together with a great assembly of twelve hundred and fifty bhikṣus and a multitude of bodhisattvas.

At that time the Buddha was departing from Rājagṛha, intending to go to Śrāvastī. The bodhisattvas led the way ahead, with Śakra and Brahmā wearing garments and revealing bodies like those of the four heavenly kings. The assembly of bhikṣus all followed behind the Buddha. Devas, nāgas, and spirits were above, making offerings.

Not far from the city, there was a man plowing and sowing in a field. From afar he saw the Tathāgata with his attendant disciples, upright and extraordinary, of majestic spiritual authority, with a countenance as beautiful as the moon among the stars, with marvelous marks and excellent features, a golden radiance radiating gracefully, and the thirty-two marks all displaying their excellence. Seeing the World-Honored One from afar, his heart was filled with joy, and he wished to go to the Buddha, bow his head in homage, and receive the meaning of the Dharma. "A Buddha is difficult to encounter in the world; only rarely does one appear." But then he thought to himself: "My plowing is not yet finished and my sowing is not yet complete. I shall wait for a later, idle time and then go see the Buddha."

At that time the Buddha knew in his mind that this man had given rise to laziness. The World-Honored One then smiled and emitted light of five colors. The light came forth from his mouth and illuminated the realms of the ten directions, bringing peace to the five destinies. All rode upon the light and came to assemble at the Buddha's place. The hells ceased their torment, the hungry ghosts were filled, the animals conceived wholesome thoughts, people sought deliverance, and the devas, nāgas, and spirits listened to the Dharma.

The worthy Ānanda, who understood the seven meanings of timeliness, Dharma, occasion, person, and others, immediately went to where the Buddha was, knelt with palms joined, and addressed the Buddha: "What is the reason for your smile? Since you have smiled, there must surely be a meaning."

The Buddha told Ānanda: "Do you see that plowman?"

He replied: "I have seen him."

The Buddha said: "This man, since the time of Vipaśyin Buddha ninety-one kalpas ago, has been plowing and sowing in this way. Each time he has encountered a Buddha, he has always been lazy, choosing ease and putting it off, then returning in haste to plow and sow in the field of birth-and-death and the Dharma of offenses. He has not recognized the Dharma-plow that sows the field of the boundless. He has already let six Buddhas pass without receiving deliverance. Now, encountering me, he initially gave rise to a good thought but then immediately changed his mind, wishing to remain in his old laziness, content to sow the roots of transgression."

At that time the man heard this from afar. He abandoned his plow and his field, came to the place of the Buddha, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, repented of his faults, and reproached himself: "Foolish and ignorant as I am, my transgressions are exceedingly grave. I pray you will take pity on me and pardon my offenses. Blindly I have stumbled about, and my laziness has lasted long. I beg to be pardoned and forgiven, and to be delivered from birth-and-death."

The Buddha said: "Excellent! That you are able to awaken is of benefit to the Dharma and will ultimately cause you no harm." The Buddha then taught him the sūtras, showing the defilement of laziness and the benefit of diligence. The man leapt with joy and was established in non-retrogression. Devas, nāgas, spirits, and countless thousands all gave rise to the aspiration for the unsurpassed, true, and right path.

"Therefore, in studying the path one should always be diligent and never be lazy, lest one turn through birth-and-death for kalpas upon kalpas."

When the Buddha had finished speaking thus, the worthy Ānanda, the devas, nāgas, spirits, and the asura kings, having heard the sūtra, rejoiced, made obeisance, and withdrew.
