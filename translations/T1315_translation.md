# 施諸餓鬼飲食及水法
## Method for Giving Food and Water to All Hungry Ghosts

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1315

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

First, set out the food for sentient beings. Everything must be done in accordance with the Dharma. All the various items placed around should be pure and fine. Whether one portion or a small amount or one vessel, all should be placed in a clean copper vessel in accordance with the Dharma. If there is no copper vessel, white porcelain is also acceptable. If there is no porcelain vessel, a lacquered vessel may be used. The food and drink should be mixed with clean water. Stand facing east; one may also perform the rite while seated.

Those who wish to give food and drink to all hungry ghosts should first arouse the vast mind and universally invite the hungry ghosts. First recite this verse once with a sincere mind, and then perform the summoning rite. The merit and reward obtained will be beyond reckoning.

> Bhikṣus and bhikṣuṇīs who arouse the mind and uphold this,
> With one vessel of pure food,
> Make a universal offering to the ten directions,
> Extending to the limits of space, pervading the Dharma-realm,
> In all the lands of the mote-dust buddha-fields.
> All hungry ghosts,
> Those who have died long ago or recently,
> Mountain and river earth-lords,
> And even the ghosts and spirits of the wilderness:
> Please come and gather here. I now, with compassion,
> Universally give you food. May each of you
> Receive this food from me,
> And pass it on as an offering throughout the bounds of space
> To the buddhas, sages, and all sentient beings.
> May you and all sentient beings be universally satisfied.
> May you also, by means of this mantra-empowered food,
> Leave behind suffering, attain liberation,
> Be reborn in the heavens and enjoy happiness,
> Travel freely to any of the pure lands of the ten directions,
> Arouse the mind of enlightenment, practice the path of awakening,
> And in the future attain buddhahood, never to regress.
> May those who first attain the Way vow to deliver the rest.
> I also wish that you, day and night, constantly
> Guard and protect me and fulfill my aspirations.
> May the merit generated by giving this food
> Be universally transferred to all sentient beings of the Dharma-realm.
> Shared equally with all sentient beings,
> Together with all sentient beings, may this merit
> Be entirely transferred toward the true suchness of the Dharma-realm,
> Toward unsurpassed enlightenment, toward omniscient wisdom.
> May buddhahood be swiftly attained; may no lesser fruit be sought.
> May we, by means of this Dharma, quickly attain buddhahood.

With palms joined at the heart, recite this verse and perform the summoning with the mudrā. The Throat-Opening Mudrā: with the right hand, press the tip of the thumb against the face of the middle finger; the remaining three fingers are slightly bent apart. This is called the Universal Gathering Mudrā. The mantra is:

*Namaḥ prabhu-jñe-kṛta-ri tathāgatāya.*

Forming this mudrā and reciting this mantra seven times, broadly exercise the compassionate mind, wishing that all hungry ghosts in the mote-dust buddha-fields throughout the Dharma-realm all gather like clouds.

Then recite the mantra for opening the gates of hell and the throat:

*Oṃ prabhu-jñe-tri-kṛta-ri tathāgatāya.*

When reciting this mantra, hold the food vessel in the left hand and form the summoning mudrā with the right hand as before, except that after each recitation, snap the fingers once by pressing the tips of the thumb and middle finger together and snapping. The remaining three fingers are open and slightly bent. This is called the Mudrā for Breaking Open the Gates of Hell and Opening the Throat.

At that time the Tathāgata spoke the dhāraṇī of immeasurable majestic virtue, sovereign radiance, and supreme wondrous power for empowering food and drink:

*Namaḥ sarva-tathāgatāvalokite saṃbhara saṃbhara hūṃ.*

Recite this mantra seven times. All hungry ghosts will each obtain seven times seven hu of food by the measure of Magadha. After eating, all will be reborn in the heavens or in a pure land. This can cause the practitioner's karmic obstructions to be eliminated, their lifespan to increase, and in this present life they will obtain immeasurable, boundless merit, not to mention in future lives. Then form the mudrā and recite this mantra to empower the food and drink. With the right hand's thumb, rub the nail of the middle finger two or three times, then hold the three fingers straight up. Then press the thumb against the tip of the index finger and snap to make a sound. One recitation, one snap: this is the method.

Then recite the mantra for receiving the flavor of the sweet-dew Dharma, forming the Fearlessness Mudrā: raise the right arm upright with all five fingers extended straight up. The mantra is:

*Namaḥ surūpāya tathāgatāya. Tadyathā: oṃ suru suru prasuru prasuru svāhā.*

Forming the Fearlessness Mudrā as before, recite this mantra for bestowing the sweet dew seven times. This can cause food, drink, and water to transform into immeasurable milk and sweet dew, can open the throats of all hungry ghosts, and can cause food and drink to multiply abundantly so that all may eat equally.

Next, perform the Vairocana One-Syllable Heart Water-Wheel Contemplation mantra and mudrā. First, visualize the syllable vaṃ in the palm of the right hand, the color of milk, transforming into the ocean of eight meritorious qualities, from which flows all sweet-dew amṛta. Then extend the hand over the food vessel and recite. Recite the syllable vaṃ seven times, then spread open the five fingers, pointing downward over the food vessel. Visualize milk and other offerings flowing from the syllable like a sun-and-moon ocean of milk. All ghosts will be fully satisfied, lacking nothing. This is called the Mudrā for Universal Giving to All Hungry Ghosts. The mantra is:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ vaṃ.*

After visualizing and reciting this mantra seven times, pour the offering onto clean ground where no one walks, or beside a pond, or beneath a tree. Only one must not pour it beneath a peach tree, willow tree, or pomegranate tree. When the pouring is done, further call out the names of the five tathāgatas three times with a sincere mind. The merit is immeasurable.

*Namaḥ bhagavate prabhūta-ratnāya tathāgatāya.*

Homage to Ratnasaṃbhava Tathāgata, who eliminates the karma of miserliness and greed, and brings the perfection of merit.

*Namaḥ bhagavate surūpāya tathāgatāya.*

Homage to Surūpakāya Tathāgata, who shatters ugly and wretched forms, and brings the perfection of the marks and characteristics.

*Namaḥ bhagavate amṛtarājāya tathāgatāya.*

Homage to Amṛtarāja Tathāgata, who anoints the Dharma-body and mind, and causes them to receive bliss.

*Namaḥ bhagavate vipulagatrāya tathāgatāya.*

Homage to Vipulagātra Tathāgata, who makes the throat wide and capacious, enabling one to receive wondrous flavors.

*Namaḥ bhagavate abhayaṃkarāya tathāgatāya.*

Homage to Abhayaṃkara Tathāgata, who eliminates all fear and frees one from the hungry ghost realm.

If the practitioner can thus call upon the names of the five tathāgatas, through the empowerment of the buddhas' majestic radiance, all hungry ghosts will have immeasurable sins eliminated and immeasurable merit produced. They will obtain wondrous physical beauty and expansiveness, be freed from fear, and the food and drink they receive will transform into sweet-dew ambrosia of supreme flavor. They will swiftly leave behind their suffering bodies and be reborn in the heavenly pure lands.

After giving the food, the practitioner should further recite for all the ghosts and spirits the dhāraṇī of the bodhisattva samaya precepts, reciting it three times. The mantra is:

*Oṃ samaya sattvaṃ.*

After reciting three times, all ghosts and spirits will be capable of hearing the profound and secret Dharma, will all fully receive the samaya precepts, and will obtain immeasurable merit.

When all the hungry ghosts have been fully satisfied, one must then use the dhāraṇī method to dismiss them, so that they may return to their original abodes. The mantra of dismissal and liberation is:

*Oṃ vajra-mukṣa-muḥ.*

When reciting the dismissal mantra, first form the mudrā: make a fist with the right hand, press the thumb against the index finger, turn the palm upward, and snap the fingers to make a sound. This is called the Dismissal Mudrā. After each pouring of food, recite seven times and snap the fingers. This will cause all ghosts and spirits, having received the food, to depart. If they are not dismissed, they cannot leave.

If this method is not performed in full, the giving to the hungry ghosts will not be complete. Some may receive the food and others may not, and the effort will be wasted in vain. How pitiable!

If any practitioner who has aroused the mind of enlightenment can cultivate in this manner, fulfilling this method of giving to the hungry ghosts, all hungry ghosts will be fully satisfied, lacking nothing. Those who uphold this Dharma should all know this. If one uses the dhāraṇī for empowering food and drink to empower one vessel of pure food and pours it into clean flowing water, all brahmins and sages will be able to receive this food. After eating, they will praise the giver with one voice, and that person in this present life will have their lifespan extended. That person will be endowed with the majestic virtue of Brahmā and will practice the conduct of Brahmā. If one uses this mantra to empower all objects offered to the Buddha, whether water, incense, flowers, food, or drink, empowering each twenty-one times before making the offering to the Buddha, this will be equivalent to making the same variety of offerings to all the buddhas of the ten directions without any difference.
