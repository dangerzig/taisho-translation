# 金剛頂瑜伽理趣般若經
## Vajraśekhara Yoga Prajñāpāramitā-naya Sūtra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0241

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Vajrabodhi (金剛智)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Bhagavān, wondrously and well accomplished, was a Tathāgata sustained by the vajra, abiding in the wisdom of the equality of all natures, with all manner of rare and supreme merit. He had well guarded the supreme Dharma-king consecration jewel-crown of all the Tathāgatas, transcending the three realms. He had well attained the all-pervading vajra-wisdom of all the Tathāgatas, the mahā-yoga of sovereign freedom without obstruction, the decisively pure and wondrous Dharma-wisdom of all the Tathāgatas. He had well and perfectly realized the seal of the equality of the ultimately empty and quiescent nature of all the Tathāgatas. All undertakings were skillfully and well accomplished, and the various rare aspirations of all other sentient beings, insofar as they were without transgression, could all be fulfilled. He had well established himself in the equality of the three times, with the perpetual, unending, vast, and universal nature of body, speech, and mind, like vajra, equal to all the Tathāgatas, unmoving and indestructible. This Bhagavān dwelt at the summit of the desire-realm, in the palace of the Paranirmita-vaśavartin Heavenly King, the place where all the Tathāgatas constantly abide, which all praise together as the Great Jewel-Treasury Hall. This hall was made of priceless maṇi gems, adorned with all manner of rare and precious ornaments interspersed and decorating it, with sentient beings reflecting and emitting great radiance. Jeweled bells and golden chimes hung everywhere, and when stirred by gentle breezes produced harmonious and elegant sounds. Embroidered canopies, silk pennants, flower-banners, and colorful fly-whisks, jeweled pearl necklaces, half-moon and full-moon ornaments, and all manner of various adornments embellished it, beloved by worthy sages, devas, and ṛṣis. Together with eighty koṭis of great bodhisattvas, all possessing dhāraṇī-gates, samādhi-gates, and unobstructed wondrous eloquence; such and similar immeasurable merits could not be fully praised even in many kalpas. Their names were: Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva-mahāsattva, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva-mahāsattva, Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva-mahāsattva, Vajramuṣṭi Bodhisattva-mahāsattva, Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva-mahāsattva, Prathamacittotpāda-dharmacakrapravartana Bodhisattva-mahāsattva, Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva-mahāsattva, and Sarvamārapramardin Bodhisattva-mahāsattva. With these as the foremost, eighty koṭis of great bodhisattvas surrounded them. He proclaimed the right Dharma, excellent in the beginning, middle, and end, with skillfully wondrous meaning and text, purely perfect, complete, and of the pure Brahma-conduct.

At that time, the World-Honored One, for the bodhisattvas, taught the Dharma-gate of the Prajñāpāramitā principle-reality of the intrinsic purity of all dharmas. This gate is the sentence-meaning of the bodhisattva. What is called the sentence-meaning of the bodhisattva? Namely: "The sentence-meaning of the supremely wondrous and pure is the sentence-meaning of the bodhisattva. The sentence-meaning of the eternal quiescence and purity of all views is the sentence-meaning of the bodhisattva. The sentence-meaning of the subtle, wondrous, and delightful purity is the sentence-meaning of the bodhisattva. The sentence-meaning of the eternal cessation and purity of craving is the sentence-meaning of the bodhisattva. The sentence-meaning of the transcendence and purity beyond the womb is the sentence-meaning of the bodhisattva. The sentence-meaning of the adornment and purity of manifold virtues is the sentence-meaning of the bodhisattva. The sentence-meaning of the supreme delight and purity of the mind is the sentence-meaning of the bodhisattva. The sentence-meaning of the purity of mind is the sentence-meaning of the bodhisattva. The sentence-meaning of the purity of form is the sentence-meaning of the bodhisattva. The sentence-meaning of the purity of sound is the sentence-meaning of the bodhisattva. The sentence-meaning of the purity of fragrance is the sentence-meaning of the bodhisattva. The sentence-meaning of the purity of taste is the sentence-meaning of the bodhisattva. The sentence-meaning of the purity of touch is the sentence-meaning of the bodhisattva. Why? Because the intrinsic nature of all dharmas is pure. Since the intrinsic nature of all dharmas is pure, Prajñāpāramitā is the supreme purity."

Having taught these sentence-meanings of the bodhisattva, this Prajñā principle-reality of the pure Dharma, the Buddha addressed Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva and the others: "Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva! If anyone hears this Dharma-gate of the Prajñāpāramitā principle-reality of the intrinsic purity of all dharmas, and it passes even once through the ear, all the gravest transgressions of affliction-obstructions and karmic obstructions will be spontaneously extinguished, and up to the attainment of bodhi one will not be born in an evil destiny. If one can daily receive, uphold, read, recite, and contemplate it with right mindfulness, then in this very life one will assuredly attain the vajra-samādhi of the equality of all dharmas. After passing through the lives of sixteen great bodhisattvas, one will assuredly attain the vajra-holding nature of the Tathāgata and swiftly realize unsurpassed, right, and equal bodhi."

He then spoke the secret syllable:

"*Hūṃ*"

At that time, the World-Honored One, further assuming the aspect of Vairocana Tathāgata, taught the bodhisattvas the gate of manifest perfect awakening through the Prajñāpāramitā of the profoundly deep principle-reality of the quiescent dharma-nature of all the Tathāgatas. Namely: "Vajra-equality accomplishes right awakening, because great bodhi is firm in nature like vajra. Meaning-equality accomplishes right awakening, because great bodhi is of one-meaning nature. Dharma-equality accomplishes right awakening, because great bodhi is intrinsically pure in nature. All-karma equality accomplishes right awakening, because great bodhi is free from all discrimination."

Having taught this gate of dharma-nature, the Buddha addressed Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva: "Vajrapāṇi! If anyone hears this gate of manifest perfect awakening through the Prajñāpāramitā principle-reality of the four quiescent natures accomplishing right awakening, and with faith and understanding receives, upholds, reads, recites, and contemplates it with right mindfulness, one will be able to transcend all evil destinies and swiftly realize unsurpassed, right, and equal bodhi."

He further spoke the secret syllable:

"*Āṃ*"

At that time, the World-Honored One, further assuming the aspect of Śākyamuni, who among all the Tathāgatas is able to tame those difficult to tame, taught the bodhisattvas the Dharma-gate of the Prajñāpāramitā principle-reality of the universal victory and equality of all dharmas. Namely: "Desire is without elaboration in nature. Anger is without elaboration in nature. Attachment is without elaboration in nature. Why? Because all is without elaboration in nature. Since all is without elaboration in nature, Prajñāpāramitā is without elaboration in nature."

Having taught this Prajñā principle-reality Dharma of universal victory for taming all evil, the Buddha addressed Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva and the others: "Vajrapāṇi! If anyone hears this Dharma-gate of the Prajñāpāramitā principle-reality of the universal victory and equality of all dharmas, and with faith and understanding receives, upholds, reads, recites, and contemplates it with right mindfulness, even if one were to kill all sentient beings of the three realms, one would ultimately not fall into an evil destiny because of this. Why? Because one has already received the discipline of the tamed mind. One will always be born in favorable destinies, cultivate the practices of bodhi, and swiftly realize unsurpassed, right, and equal bodhi."

He further spoke the secret syllable:

"*Hūṃ*"

At that time, the World-Honored One, further assuming the aspect of the intrinsic purity of all the Tathāgatas, taught the bodhisattvas the Dharma-gate of the Prajñāpāramitā principle-reality of the Avalokiteśvara wisdom-seal of the equality of all dharmas. Namely: "The nature of all desire is pure, and the nature of anger is pure. Because the nature of all desire is pure, the nature of all defilement is pure and the nature of transgression is pure. Because the nature of all defilement and transgression is pure, the nature of all dharmas is pure and the nature of sentient beings is pure. Because the nature of all dharmas and sentient beings is pure, the nature of all-knowledge is pure. Because the nature of all-knowledge is pure, Prajñāpāramitā is the supreme purity."

Having taught this pure Dharma of the Prajñā principle-reality of the equality wisdom-seal, the Buddha addressed Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva and the others: "Vajrapāṇi! If anyone hears this Dharma-gate of the Prajñāpāramitā principle-reality of the Avalokiteśvara wisdom-seal of the equality of all dharmas, and with faith and understanding receives, upholds, reads, recites, and contemplates it with right mindfulness, even while amid the five sense-desires, one will not be stained by the faults of adventitious afflictions, just as a lotus, though in the mire, is never stained. One will swiftly realize unsurpassed, right, and equal bodhi."

He further spoke the secret syllable:

"*Hrīḥ*"

At that time, the World-Honored One, further assuming the aspect of all the Tathāgatas as lords of the three realms, taught the bodhisattvas the Dharma-gate of the Prajñāpāramitā principle-reality of the wisdom-treasury of the consecration and manifestation of all the Tathāgatas. Namely: "Consecration-giving, causing one to attain the position of Dharma-King of all the three realms. Treasure-giving, causing one to attain the fulfillment of all wishes. Pure Dharma-giving, causing one to attain the jewel-nature of all dharmas. Food-giving, causing all of body, speech, and mind to obtain peace and happiness."

Having taught this consecration Prajñā principle-reality wisdom-treasury, the Buddha addressed Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva and the others: "If anyone hears this Dharma-gate of the profoundly deep principle-reality wisdom-treasury of consecration, and with faith and understanding receives, upholds, reads, recites, and contemplates it with right mindfulness, one will swiftly fulfill the bodhisattva's practices and swiftly realize unsurpassed, right, and equal bodhi."

He further spoke the secret syllable:

"*Trāṃ*"

At that time, the World-Honored One, further assuming the aspect of the eternal wisdom-seal secret treasury of all the Tathāgatas, taught the bodhisattvas the Dharma-gate of the Prajñāpāramitā of the profoundly deep principle-reality of the vajra-wisdom-seal of all the Tathāgatas. Namely: "Holding the vajra body-seal of all the Tathāgatas, one attains the true and real essential nature of all the Tathāgatas. Holding the vajra speech-seal of all the Tathāgatas, one attains sovereign freedom of all gates. Holding the vajra mind-seal of all the Tathāgatas, one attains the completeness of all samādhis. The vajra-holder, holding the vajra wisdom-seal of all the Tathāgatas, attains the supreme body, speech, and mind as vajra."

Having taught this wisdom-seal Dharma, the Buddha addressed Vajrapāṇi and the others: "Vajrapāṇi! If anyone hears this Dharma-gate of the Prajñāpāramitā of the profoundly deep principle-reality of the vajra-wisdom of all the Tathāgatas, and with faith and understanding receives, upholds, reads, recites, and contemplates it with right mindfulness, one immediately accomplishes the supreme vajra-wisdom-seal. In all-knowledge and in all undertakings, one will attain perfect fulfillment. The nature of body, speech, and mind will be like vajra, indestructible. One will swiftly realize unsurpassed, right, and equal bodhi."

He further spoke the secret syllable:

"*Aḥ*"

At that time, the World-Honored One, further assuming the aspect of the Tathāgata of all dharmas free from elaboration, taught the bodhisattvas the Dharma-gate of the Prajñāpāramitā principle-reality of the syllable-turning chapter. Namely: "All dharmas are empty, because they are without self-nature. All dharmas are without marks, because they are free from all marks. All dharmas are without aspiration, because they are free from all aspirations. All dharmas are intrinsically pure in nature; thus Prajñāpāramitā is intrinsically pure in nature."

Having taught this Dharma of syllables free from all elaboration, the Buddha addressed Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva and the others: "If anyone hears this Dharma-gate of the principle-reality syllable-turning Prajñā free from elaboration, and with faith and understanding receives, upholds, reads, recites, and contemplates it with right mindfulness, one will attain unobstructed wisdom regarding all dharmas and swiftly realize unsurpassed, right, and equal bodhi."

He further spoke the secret syllable:

"*Āṃ*"

At that time, the World-Honored One, further assuming the aspect of all the Tathāgatas entering the vast, great turning, taught the bodhisattvas the gate of equality of the Prajñāpāramitā of the profoundly deep principle-reality of entering the vast, great turning. Namely: "By entering vajra-equality, one enters the turning of all the Tathāgatas. By entering meaning-equality, one enters the dharma-turning of all the bodhisattvas. By entering dharma-equality, one enters the turning of the wondrous Dharma. By entering the equality of all dharmas, one enters the turning of all dharma-wheels."

Having taught this equality of entering the vast, great turning Prajñā principle-reality, the Buddha addressed Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva and the others: "If anyone hears this gate of equality of the profoundly deep principle-reality of the wheel-nature, and with faith and understanding receives, upholds, reads, recites, and contemplates it with right mindfulness, one will be well able to awaken to all equalities and swiftly realize unsurpassed, right, and equal bodhi."

He further spoke the secret syllable:

"*Hūṃ*"

At that time, the World-Honored One, further in accordance with the vast, great offering-implements and various offering-substances of all the Tathāgatas, taught the bodhisattvas the unsurpassed Dharma-gate of the Prajñāpāramitā of the profoundly deep principle-reality of the supreme and foremost vast, great offering-implements and various offering-substances. Namely: "Generating the bodhi-mind itself constitutes a vast, great offering to all the Tathāgatas. Rescuing and protecting all sentient beings itself constitutes a vast, great offering to all the Tathāgatas. Sustaining and upholding the wondrous Dharma itself constitutes a vast, great offering to all the Tathāgatas. Writing, receiving, upholding, reading, reciting, contemplating with right mindfulness, and making offerings to Prajñāpāramitā itself constitutes a vast, great offering to all the Tathāgatas."

Having taught this unsurpassed Dharma of the profoundly deep principle-reality of the true and pure offering, the Buddha addressed Vajrapāṇi and the others: "If anyone hears this unsurpassed Dharma-gate of the offering Prajñā principle-reality, and with faith and understanding receives, upholds, reads, recites, and contemplates it with right mindfulness, one will swiftly fulfill the practices of all the bodhisattvas and swiftly realize unsurpassed, right, and equal bodhi."

He further spoke the secret syllable:

"*Oṃ*"

At that time, the World-Honored One, further assuming the aspect of all the Tathāgatas able to tame all sentient beings, taught the bodhisattvas the Dharma-gate of the Prajñāpāramitā principle-reality of the secret wisdom-treasury that tames all sentient beings. Namely: "The equality-nature of all sentient beings is the equality-nature of wrath. The taming-nature of all sentient beings is the taming-nature of wrath. The true dharma-nature of all sentient beings is the true dharma-nature of wrath. The vajra-nature of all sentient beings is the vajra-nature of wrath. Why? Because all sentient beings are tamed and caused to attain bodhi."

Having taught this profoundly deep principle-reality wisdom-treasury of skillful taming, the Buddha addressed Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva and the others: "If anyone hears this Dharma-gate of the wisdom-treasury of taming Prajñā principle-reality, and with faith and understanding receives, upholds, reads, recites, and contemplates it with right mindfulness, one will be able to tame the faults of wrath and the like in oneself, and will also be able to tame all sentient beings so that they are always born in favorable destinies and enjoy wondrous happiness. In this present life, even enemies will give rise to compassionate minds. One will be well able to cultivate the practices of the bodhisattvas and swiftly realize unsurpassed, right, and equal bodhi."

He further spoke the secret syllable:

"*Haḥ*"

At that time, the World-Honored One, further assuming the aspect of all the Tathāgatas abiding in equality, taught the bodhisattvas the Dharma-gate of the Prajñāpāramitā of the profoundly deep principle-reality of the supreme equality of all dharmas. Namely: "Since all dharmas are of an equality-nature, the profound Prajñāpāramitā is also of an equality-nature. Since all dharmas are of the nature of the supreme meaning, the profound Prajñāpāramitā is also of the nature of the supreme meaning. Since all dharmas are of the nature of dharma-ness, the profound Prajñāpāramitā is also of the nature of dharma-ness. Since all dharmas have a nature of functional activity, one should know that the profound Prajñāpāramitā also has a nature of functional activity."

Having taught this supreme Dharma of the profoundly deep principle-reality of the equality of equality-nature, the Buddha addressed Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva and the others: "If anyone hears this supreme Dharma-gate of the profoundly deep Prajñā of the principle-reality of equality, and with faith and understanding receives, upholds, reads, recites, and contemplates it with right mindfulness, one will be able to penetrate the dharma-nature of equality and the profound Prajñāpāramitā, and regarding all sentient beings the mind will be without obstruction. One will swiftly realize unsurpassed, right, and equal bodhi."

He further spoke the secret syllable:

"*Hrīḥ*"

At that time, the World-Honored One, further assuming the aspect of all the Tathāgatas empowering all sentient beings, taught the bodhisattvas the Dharma-gate of the Prajñāpāramitā principle-reality of the empowerment of all sentient beings. Namely: "All sentient beings are the tathāgatagarbha, because the nature of Samantabhadra Bodhisattva pervades all. All sentient beings are vajra, because they are of the nature of the perfection of consecration. All sentient beings are the treasury of the right Dharma-nature, because they can turn the wheel of all the right Dharma-speech. All sentient beings are the treasury of karma-nature, because they can perform all activities in correspondence."

Having taught this supreme treasury Dharma of the profoundly deep principle-reality of the empowerment of sentient beings, the Buddha addressed Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva and the others: "If anyone hears this supreme treasury Dharma-gate of the all-pervading Prajñā principle-reality, and with faith and understanding receives, upholds, reads, recites, and contemplates it with right mindfulness, one will be able to penetrate the dharma-nature of the supreme treasury and swiftly realize unsurpassed, right, and equal bodhi."

He further spoke the secret syllable:

"*Trīḥ*"

At that time, the World-Honored One, further assuming the aspect of all the Tathāgatas that is immeasurable, boundless, and ultimately exhaustive, taught the bodhisattvas the Dharma-gate of the Prajñāpāramitā principle-reality of the equality of the immeasurable, boundless, and ultimately exhaustive nature of all dharmas. Namely: "Since the profound Prajñāpāramitā is immeasurable, all the Tathāgatas are also immeasurable. Since the profound Prajñāpāramitā is boundless, all the Tathāgatas are also boundless. Since the profound Prajñāpāramitā is of one nature, all dharmas are also of one nature. Since the profound Prajñāpāramitā is ultimately exhaustive, all dharmas are also ultimately exhaustive."

Having taught this vajra of the boundless, edgeless, and ultimate principle-reality, the Buddha addressed Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva and the others: "Vajrapāṇi! If anyone hears this vajra Dharma-gate of the Prajñāpāramitā of the ultimate principle-reality, and with faith and understanding receives, upholds, reads, recites, and contemplates it with right mindfulness, all obstructions will be extinguished ultimately and without remainder. One will assuredly attain the vajra-holding nature of the Tathāgata and swiftly realize unsurpassed, right, and equal bodhi."

He further spoke the secret syllable:

"*Viṣaya*"

At that time, the World-Honored One, further assuming the aspect of all the Tathāgatas' Vairocana, the secret dharma-nature free from all elaboration, taught the bodhisattvas the Dharma-gate of the Prajñāpāramitā of the profoundly deep principle-reality of Samantabhadra's Great Bliss Amoghavajra Divine Spell, the immeasurable decisive entry into all dharma-natures, without beginning, middle, or end, the supreme and foremost. Namely: "Because bodhisattvas are able to perform vast, great service and offerings, they attain the supreme great bliss. Because they attain the supreme great bliss, they attain the unsurpassed great bodhi of all the Buddhas. Because they attain the unsurpassed great bodhi of all the Buddhas, they are able to subdue all the armies of māra. Because they subdue all the armies of māra, they attain sovereign, supreme accomplishment in the three realms. Because they attain sovereign, supreme accomplishment in the three realms, they are able to universally benefit all sentient beings and bestow upon all the ultimate, supreme peace and happiness."

He further spoke the secret syllable:

"*Sarva*

> "Those of the most supreme wisdom
> constantly abide in birth and death,
> broadly ferrying all sentient beings across,
> and do not enter nirvāṇa.
> Prajñāpāramitā,
> the ultimate skillful-means wisdom,
> can accomplish pure karma
> and universally purify all states of existence.
> Furthermore, through desire and the like,
> they tame all the world,
> up to the summit of existence,
> attaining the pure, unconditioned fruit.
> Abiding in birth and death,
> worldly dharmas cannot stain them,
> like the wondrous color of a lotus
> that dust and filth cannot pollute.
> One of great desire is a person of great purity.
> One of great giving is a person of great happiness.
> With sovereign freedom in the three realms,
> one creates firm and lasting benefit."

He further spoke the secret syllable:

"*Haḥ*"

"*Oṃ namaḥ sarva-buddha-bodhi-sattvānāṃ*"

"*Oṃ vajra-sattva visphūṭāya sarva-pāpa-bandhana-nipra-mokṣāya sarva-pāpa-gati-bhaye svasatvaṃ sarva-tathāgata-samaya vara hūṃ trāṭ*"

When the Buddha had spoken these secret syllables, he addressed Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva, the Lord of Secrets: "If any sentient beings hear these twenty-five Dharma-gates of the profoundly deep Prajñāpāramitā principle-reality secret teaching, and with faith and understanding receive, uphold, read, recite, and contemplate them with right mindfulness, in this present life they will immediately attain the supreme accomplishment of the vajra-secret of all the Tathāgatas. Before long, they will attain the dharma-nature body of the great vajra-holder of all the Tathāgatas."

At that time, the World-Honored One, having taught the bodhisattvas all the Dharma-gates described above, further addressed Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva: "Vajrapāṇi! This sūtra is difficult to hear. If anyone hears even a single syllable, one should know that this person has already made offerings to Buddhas in past lives and planted roots of goodness in the presence of those Buddhas. How much more so for one who fully hears, receives, upholds, reads, recites, and contemplates it with right mindfulness. One should know that this person has assuredly already made offerings, revered, honored, and praised eighty koṭi-nayuta Ganges-sands of Buddhas. Wherever this sūtra abides, that place contains the stūpa of the Buddhas. If sentient beings cherish this sūtra and constantly carry and guard it, never separating it from their person, that person should be received by all the world with reverence and offerings. That person will attain the supernatural knowledge of past lives and be able to know the events of immeasurable past kalpas. One will not be disturbed by any of the deva-māras or Pāpīyas. The Four Heavenly Kings and all other devas will constantly guard and protect one. All the Buddhas and bodhisattvas will constantly make offerings, embrace, and accept one. One may be reborn at will in the pure lands of the ten directions. Vajrapāṇi! I have now briefly described the merit of this Dharma-gate of the Prajñāpāramitā principle-reality. If I were to describe it in full, even an entire kalpa would not exhaust it."

When the Buddha had finished teaching this sūtra, Vajrapāṇi and all the bodhisattvas, devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, humans, and non-humans, the entire assembly, all greatly rejoiced, faithfully received it, and practiced accordingly.
