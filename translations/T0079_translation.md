# 鸚鵡經
## The Parrot Sūtra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0079

Translated from the Chinese. Liu Song dynasty, translated by Guṇabhadra (求那跋陀羅)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Bhagavān was in Śrāvastī, in Jeta's Grove, in the park of Anāthapiṇḍada. At that time, the World-Honored One rose at dawn, put on his robes, and together with his robes and bowl went to the city of Śrāvastī to collect alms. While walking through Śrāvastī for alms, he came to the house of the Parrot, the son of Mālutodeyya. At that time, the Parrot, the son of Mālutodeyya, had gone out on some minor business and was not at home. In the house of the Parrot, the son of Mālutodeyya, there was a dog named Jù, sitting on a fine cushion, eating polished rice and meat from a golden bowl. The white dog saw the World-Honored One coming from afar and barked at him. The World-Honored One then said: "Stop, white dog! You should not make such a sound. In your former life you used to chant and recite!" Thereupon, the white dog became extremely angry and displeased. It got down from its bed and cushion, went to the threshold of the door, lay there pressed against it, and remained still.

Later, the son of Mālutodeyya returned home and saw the white dog had gotten down from its bed and cushion, was lying pressed against the door threshold, still and sorrowful. Seeing this, he asked the people nearby: "Who has disturbed this white dog, causing it to be sorrowful and unhappy, to get down from its bed and cushion and lie still against the door threshold?" "Young master, no one has disturbed this dog to make it sorrowful and unhappy, to get down from its bed and cushion and lie still against the door threshold. Young master, today the śramaṇa Gautama came to this house to beg for food. The white dog barked at him, and the śramaṇa Gautama said: 'Stop, white dog! You should not make such a sound. In your former life you used to chant and recite!' Therefore, young master, the white dog became angry and displeased, got down from its bed and cushion, and has been lying silently against the door threshold." Thereupon, the Parrot, the son of Mālutodeyya, became angry and displeased toward the World-Honored One. He reviled the World-Honored One from afar, slandered the World-Honored One from afar, and raged at the World-Honored One from afar: "This śramaṇa Gautama speaks such falsehoods!" He left Śrāvastī and went to Jeta's Grove, the park of Anāthapiṇḍada.

At that time, the World-Honored One was surrounded by an assembly of hundreds and was teaching the Dharma for them. The World-Honored One saw the Parrot, the son of Mālutodeyya, coming from afar, and told the bhikṣus: "Bhikṣus, do you see the Parrot, the son of Mālutodeyya, coming from afar?" "Indeed, World-Honored One." "If the Parrot, the son of Mālutodeyya, were to die at this moment, in the time it takes to bend or extend an arm, he would be reborn in hell. Why is this? Because he harbors such extreme anger toward me. On account of that anger, when his body perishes at death, he would be reborn in an evil destiny, in hell."

At that time, the Parrot, the son of Mālutodeyya, came to the World-Honored One and addressed him: "Śramaṇa Gautama, did you go to my house today to beg for food?"

"Young master, I went to your house today to beg for food."

"Śramaṇa Gautama, what offense had my white dog committed against you, that you caused it to become angry and displeased, to get down from its bed and cushion and lie still against the door threshold?"

The Buddha replied: "Young master, I rose at dawn, put on my robes, and together with my robes and bowl went to the city of Śrāvastī to collect alms. While walking through Śrāvastī for alms, I came to your house. Your white dog saw me coming from afar and barked at me. I then said: 'Stop, white dog! You should not make such a sound. In your former life you used to chant and recite!' Therefore, young master, your white dog became angry and displeased, got down from its bed and cushion, and has been lying silently against the door threshold."

"Gautama, what was this white dog to me in the past?"

"Stop, young master. Do not ask. If you were to hear it, you would be sorrowful and displeased."

The Parrot, the son of Mālutodeyya, asked the World-Honored One three times: "Gautama, what was my white dog to me in the past?"

"Young master, you have asked three times, so I shall tell you. Young master, the white dog was in its previous life your father, whose name was Todeyya."

Thereupon, the Parrot, the son of Mālutodeyya, became even more greatly angered and displeased toward the World-Honored One. He reviled the World-Honored One, raged at the World-Honored One, and slandered the World-Honored One: "This śramaṇa Gautama speaks falsehoods!" He addressed the World-Honored One: "Gautama, my father Todeyya constantly practiced giving, constantly practiced banner-offerings, and constantly tended the sacred fire. When his body perished at death, he was reborn in the sublime Brahmā heaven. Why would he be reborn as a dog?"

"Young master, it was because of your overweening pride. Your father Todeyya was the same, and therefore he was reborn as a wretched dog." The Buddha then spoke this verse:

> "The brahmin with overweening pride,
> At death is reborn in the six destinies:
> As rooster, pig, dog, or wild fox,
> As donkey, or in the womb of hell."

"Young master, if you do not believe what I have said, then you may return home. When you arrive, say to the white dog: 'White dog, if it is true that in your former life you were my father Todeyya, then get back up on the bed and cushion.' Young master, the white dog will then get back up on the bed and cushion. 'White dog, if in your former life you were my father Todeyya, then eat polished rice and meat from the golden bowl.' Young master, the white dog will then eat polished rice and meat from the golden bowl. 'White dog, if in your former life you were my father Todeyya, then show me the wealth that my father left behind, the treasure that you hid which I do not know about.' Young master, the white dog will then show you the wealth left by your father that you do not know about."

Thereupon, the Parrot, the son of Mālutodeyya, having heard what the World-Honored One said, carefully reflected upon it, recited it, circumambulated the World-Honored One, and then departed from the World-Honored One and returned home. Upon arriving, he said to the white dog: "White dog, if it is true that in your former life you were my father Todeyya, then get back up on the bed and cushion." The white dog then got back up on the bed and cushion. "White dog, if in your former life you were my father Todeyya, then eat polished rice and meat from the golden bowl." The white dog then ate polished rice and meat from the golden bowl. "White dog, if in your former life you were my father Todeyya, then show me the wealth that my father left behind, the treasure that you hid which I do not know about." Thereupon, the white dog got down from the bed and cushion and went to the place where it used to sleep. Having arrived at its old sleeping place, at the four legs of the bed, it dug at the ground with its mouth and paws, and the Parrot, the son of Mālutodeyya, obtained a great deal of money and treasure. Having obtained this great wealth and great profit, the Parrot was exceedingly joyful. A wholesome mind arose in him. He placed his right knee on the ground, pressed his palms together facing toward Jeta's Grove, the park of Anāthapiṇḍada, and three times declared his own name and lineage: "The śramaṇa Gautama speaks truly! The śramaṇa Gautama's words are true! The śramaṇa Gautama does not speak falsely!" Having three times declared his name, he left the city of Śrāvastī and went to Jeta's Grove, the park of Anāthapiṇḍada.

At that time, the World-Honored One was surrounded by an assembly of hundreds and was teaching the Dharma for them. The World-Honored One saw the Parrot, the son of Mālutodeyya, coming from afar, and told the bhikṣus: "Bhikṣus, do you see the Parrot, the son of Mālutodeyya, coming from afar?" "Indeed, World-Honored One." "If the Parrot, the son of Mālutodeyya, were to die at this moment, in the time it takes to bend or extend an arm, he would be reborn in a good destiny. Why is this? Because he has a wholesome mind toward me. On account of that wholesome mind, when his body perishes at death, he would be reborn in a good destiny, in heaven."

At that time, the Parrot, the son of Mālutodeyya, went to the World-Honored One. Upon arriving, he exchanged cordial greetings face to face with the World-Honored One and then sat to one side. After he had sat to one side, the World-Honored One said: "Young master, was what I said about the white dog true or not?"

"As the śramaṇa Gautama said, it was truly as stated, without difference. Śramaṇa Gautama, I wish to ask you something further. Please hear my question."

"Young master, ask whatever you wish."

"Gautama, what is the cause and condition by which human beings, all having received a human body, have differences of high and low, good and bad, pure and impure? Gautama, why are some long-lived and some short-lived? Why are some free from illness and some afflicted with illness? Why are some handsome and some ugly? Why are some of high station and some of low station? Why are some capable and some incapable? Why do some have great wealth and some have little wealth? Why are some foolish and some wise?"

"Young master, it is because of the causes and conditions of sentient beings, because of their actions, conditioned by their actions, and made by their actions. According to the actions that sentient beings perform, they come to have differences of high and low, good and bad."

"Śramaṇa Gautama, you have spoken briefly without explaining in detail. I do not understand the meaning. May the śramaṇa Gautama be so kind as to explain it well for me, so that I may follow it. May the śramaṇa Gautama explain in detail what was said briefly, so that I may understand the meaning."

"Therefore, young master, listen well and reflect carefully. I shall explain."

"Indeed, Gautama." The Parrot, the son of Mālutodeyya, received the World-Honored One's teaching.

The World-Honored One said: "Young master, what is the cause and condition by which some men or women are short-lived? Young master, there are some men or women whose hands are stained with blood, who draw near to evil and lack compassion, who take the lives of all sentient beings down to the smallest ant. On account of such actions, due to such causes and such conduct, when their bodies perish at death they are reborn in an evil destiny, in hell. Coming to be reborn among humans, their lives are short. Why is this? Young master, because their actions were of the kind that shortens life. Thus, young master, when some men or women engage in killing, you should know this is the result of such actions. Young master, what is the cause and condition by which some men or women are long-lived? Young master, there are some men or women who have abandoned killing and separated themselves from killing, who have cast aside weapons and clubs, who always feel a sense of shame, who wish for the safety and well-being of all sentient beings, and whose minds are purified of any intention to kill. On account of such actions, due to such causes and such conduct, when their bodies perish at death they are reborn in a good destiny, in heaven. Coming to be reborn among humans, their lives are long. Why is this? Young master, because their actions were of the kind that lengthens life. Thus, when some men or women have separated themselves from killing and abandoned killing, you should know, young master, this is the result of such actions. Young master, what is the cause and condition by which some men or women have much illness? Young master, there are some men or women who harm sentient beings. In harming sentient beings, they use their hands, stones, clubs, or weapons. On account of such actions, due to such causes and such conduct, when their bodies perish at death they are reborn in an evil destiny, in hell. Coming to be reborn among humans, they have much illness and pain. Why is this? Young master, because their actions were of the kind that produces illness. Thus, when some men or women harm sentient beings, you should know, young master, this is the result of such actions. Young master, what is the cause and condition by which some men or women are free from illness? Young master, there are some men or women who do not harm sentient beings. In not harming sentient beings, they do not use their hands, stones, weapons, or clubs. On account of such actions, due to such causes and such conduct, when their bodies perish at death they are reborn in a good destiny, in heaven. Coming to be reborn among humans, they are free from illness and pain. Why is this? Young master, because their actions were of the kind that produces freedom from illness. Thus, when some men or women do not harm sentient beings, you should know, young master, this is the result of such actions. Young master, what is the cause and condition by which some men or women are ugly? Young master, there are some men or women who have much anger and resentment, much sorrow and displeasure. At the slightest word they become angry and resentful, sorrowful and displeased. They abide in anger and give rise to anger, and they speak slanderously at length. On account of such actions, due to such causes and such conduct, when their bodies perish at death they are reborn in an evil destiny, in hell. Coming to be reborn among humans, their form and complexion are poor and ugly. Why is this? Because their actions were of the kind that produces ugliness. Thus, when some men or women are angry and resentful and sorrowful, you should know, young master, this is the result of such actions. Young master, what is the cause and condition by which some men or women have a fine form? Young master, there are some men or women who are not often angry and not often sorrowful. Even if someone speaks to them with coarse and harsh words, they are neither angered nor resentful nor sorrowful. They do not abide in anger, do not give rise to anger, and do not harbor resentment. On account of such actions, due to such causes and such conduct, when their bodies perish at death they are reborn in a good destiny, in heaven. Coming to be reborn among humans, their form and complexion are excellent. Why is this? Young master, because their actions were of the excellent kind. Thus, when some men or women are free from anger and free from sorrow, you should know, young master, this is the result of such actions. Young master, what is the cause and condition by which some men or women have little ability? Young master, there are some men or women who give rise to covetous jealousy. Seeing others receive gifts and offerings with respect, they give rise to covetous jealousy, wishing that what others have might become theirs. On account of such actions, due to such causes and such conduct, when their bodies perish at death they are reborn in an evil destiny, in hell. Coming to be reborn among humans, they have little ability. Why is this? Young master, because their actions were of the kind that produces little ability. Thus, when some men or women give rise to covetous jealousy, you should know, young master, this is the result of such actions. Young master, what is the cause and condition by which some men or women have great ability? Young master, there are some men or women who are free from covetous jealousy and do not give rise to covetous jealousy. Seeing others receive gifts and offerings with respect, they do not give rise to covetous jealousy, nor wish that what others have might become theirs. On account of such actions, due to such causes and such conduct, when their bodies perish at death they are reborn in a good destiny, in heaven. Coming to be reborn among humans, they have great ability. Why is this? Young master, because their actions were of the kind that produces great ability. Thus, when some men or women are free from covetous jealousy and do not give rise to covetous jealousy, you should know, young master, this is the result of such actions. Young master, what is the cause and condition by which some men or women are born into a lowly family? Young master, there are some men or women who are proud and arrogant. They do not show respect where respect is due, do not serve where service is due, do not bow where bowing is due, do not make offerings where offerings are due, do not yield their seat where it should be yielded, do not show the way where guidance is due, and do not rise in respect with palms pressed together where such reverence is due. On account of such actions, due to such causes and such conduct, when their bodies perish at death they are reborn in an evil destiny, in hell. Coming to be reborn among humans, they are born into a lowly family. Why is this? Young master, because their actions were of the lowly kind. Thus, when some men or women are proud and arrogant, you should know, young master, this is the result of such actions. Young master, what is the cause and condition by which some men or women are born into a noble family? Young master, there are some men or women who are not proud and not arrogant. They show respect where respect is due, serve where service is due, bow where bowing is due, make offerings where offerings are due, yield their seat where it should be yielded, show the way where guidance is due, and rise in respect with palms pressed together where such reverence is due. On account of such actions, due to such causes and such conduct, when their bodies perish at death they are reborn in a good destiny, in heaven. Coming to be reborn among humans, they are born into a noble family. Why is this? Young master, because their actions were of the noble kind. Thus, when some men or women are not proud and not arrogant, you should know, young master, this is the result of such actions. Young master, what is the cause and condition by which some men or women have little wealth? Young master, there are some men or women who do not give and are not generous. They do not give to śramaṇas, brahmins, the poor and destitute, or to those who come seeking alms. They do not give food and drink, clothing, garlands, perfumes, bedding, lodging, lamps, or supplies. On account of such actions, due to such causes and such conduct, when their bodies perish at death they are reborn in an evil destiny, in hell. Coming to be reborn among humans, they have little wealth. Why is this? Young master, because their actions were of the kind that produces little wealth. Thus, when some men or women have little wealth, you should know, young master, this is the result of such actions. Young master, what is the cause and condition by which some men or women have great wealth? Young master, there are some men or women who give and are generous. They give to śramaṇas, brahmins, the poor and destitute, and to those who come seeking alms. They give food and drink, clothing, garlands, perfumes, bedding, lodging, lamps, and supplies. On account of such actions, due to such causes and such conduct, when their bodies perish at death they are reborn in a good destiny, in heaven. Coming to be reborn among humans, they have great wealth. Why is this? Young master, because their actions were of the kind that produces great wealth. Thus, when some men or women have great wealth, you should know, young master, this is the result of such actions. Young master, what is the cause and condition by which some men or women are foolish? Young master, there are some men or women who, for the sake of sentient beings, do not go to ask questions. There are śramaṇas and brahmins of renown, yet such persons do not go to them to ask about the meaning at the proper time, nor do they inquire of these worthies: 'What is wholesome and what is unwholesome? What is good and what is not good? What is ugly and what is fine? What is dark and what is bright? What is the result of dark and bright actions? What is the meaning of the Dharma as directly seen? What is the meaning of the precepts for the next life? What is good and what is evil?' Having heard from them, they do not study accordingly. On account of such actions, due to such causes and such conduct, when their bodies perish at death they are reborn in an evil destiny, in hell. Coming to be reborn among humans, they are foolish. Why is this? Young master, because their actions were of the foolish kind. Thus, when some men or women do not go to ask questions for the sake of sentient beings, you should know, young master, this is the result of such actions. Young master, what is the cause and condition by which some men or women are wise? Young master, there are some men or women who, for the sake of sentient beings, go to ask questions. There are śramaṇas and brahmins of renown, and such persons go to them to ask about the meaning at the proper time, and they inquire of these worthies: 'What is wholesome and what is unwholesome? What is good and what is not good? What is ugly and what is fine? What is dark and what is bright? What is the result of dark and bright actions? What is the meaning of the Dharma as directly seen? What is the meaning of the precepts for the next life? What is good and what is evil?' Having heard from them, they study accordingly. On account of such actions, due to such causes and such conduct, when their bodies perish at death they are reborn in a good destiny, in heaven. Coming to be reborn among humans, they are wise. Why is this? Young master, because their actions were of the wise kind. Thus, when some men or women go to ask questions for the sake of sentient beings, you should know, young master, this is the result of such actions. Young master, if one performs actions that shorten life, having performed them one receives a short life. If one performs actions that lengthen life, having performed them one receives a long life. If one performs actions that produce illness, having performed them one has much illness. If one performs actions that produce freedom from illness, having performed them one is free from illness. If one performs ugly actions, having performed them one receives ugliness. If one performs actions that produce a fine form, having performed them one receives a fine form. If one performs actions that produce little ability, having performed them one has little ability. If one performs actions that produce great ability, having performed them one has great ability. If one performs lowly actions, having performed them one receives a lowly station. If one performs noble actions, having performed them one receives a noble station. If one performs actions that produce little wealth, having performed them one has little wealth. If one performs actions that produce great wealth, having performed them one has great wealth. If one performs foolish actions, having performed them one receives foolishness. If one performs wise actions, having performed them one receives wisdom. This is what I said, young master. Young master, it is according to the actions that sentient beings perform, caused by actions, conditioned by actions, made by these actions, that sentient beings come to have differences of high and low, good and bad."

"Enough, Gautama! Enough, Gautama! World-Honored One, I now take refuge in the Dharma and in the Bhikṣu Saṅgha. World-Honored One, I now declare myself an upāsaka. From this day forward, for the rest of my life, I shall abstain from killing. I now take refuge. World-Honored One, from this day forward, just as I would enter the home of any other upāsaka in Śrāvastī, so too shall I enter the home of the Todeyya family, and I shall bring lasting benefit and well-being to the Todeyya household."

Thus spoke the Buddha. The Parrot, the son of Mālutodeyya, having heard what the World-Honored One said, rejoiced and withdrew.
