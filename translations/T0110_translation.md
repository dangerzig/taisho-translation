# 佛說三轉法輪經
## Sūtra on the Three Turnings of the Dharma Wheel Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0110

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Yijing (義淨)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Bhagavān was in the Deer Park at the Ṛṣipatana in Vārāṇasī. At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the five bhikṣus: "Bhikṣus! Regarding this noble truth of suffering, when one attends wisely to the Dharma that has been heard, the eye, knowledge, clarity, and awakening arise. Bhikṣus! Regarding the noble truths of the arising of suffering, the cessation of suffering, and the path leading to the cessation of suffering, when one attends wisely to the Dharma that has been heard, the eye, knowledge, clarity, and awakening arise.

"Bhikṣus! This noble truth of suffering is a Dharma to be fully understood. Thus it should be known. When one attends wisely to the Dharma that has been heard, the eye, knowledge, clarity, and awakening arise.

"Bhikṣus! This noble truth of the arising of suffering is a Dharma to be fully understood. Thus it should be abandoned. When one attends wisely to the Dharma that has been heard, the eye, knowledge, clarity, and awakening arise.

"Bhikṣus! This noble truth of the cessation of suffering is a Dharma to be fully understood. Thus it should be realized. When one attends wisely to the Dharma that has been heard, the eye, knowledge, clarity, and awakening arise.

"Bhikṣus! This noble truth of the path leading to the cessation of suffering is a Dharma to be fully understood. Thus it should be cultivated. When one attends wisely to the Dharma that has been heard, the eye, knowledge, clarity, and awakening arise.

"Bhikṣus! This noble truth of suffering is a Dharma to be fully understood. Thus it has been known. When one attends wisely to the Dharma that has been heard, the eye, knowledge, clarity, and awakening arise.

"Bhikṣus! This noble truth of the arising of suffering is a Dharma to be fully understood. Thus it has been abandoned. When one attends wisely to the Dharma that has been heard, the eye, knowledge, clarity, and awakening arise.

"Bhikṣus! This noble truth of the cessation of suffering is a Dharma to be fully understood. Thus it has been realized. When one attends wisely to the Dharma that has been heard, the eye, knowledge, clarity, and awakening arise.

"Bhikṣus! This noble truth of the path leading to the cessation of suffering is a Dharma to be fully understood. Thus it has been cultivated. When one attends wisely to the Dharma that has been heard, the eye, knowledge, clarity, and awakening arise.

"Bhikṣus! If I had not fully understood the three turnings and twelve aspects of these Four Noble Truths, the eye, knowledge, clarity, and awakening would not have arisen. Then I would not have been able, among all the gods, māras, brahmās, śramaṇas, brāhmaṇas, and all worldly beings, to abandon afflictions, liberate the mind, and realize unsurpassed enlightenment.

"Bhikṣus! Because I have fully understood the three turnings and twelve aspects of these Four Noble Truths, the eye, knowledge, clarity, and awakening have all arisen. Then I was able, among all the gods, māras, brahmās, śramaṇas, brāhmaṇas, and all worldly beings, to abandon afflictions, liberate the mind, and realize unsurpassed enlightenment."

At that time, when the World-Honored One taught this Dharma, the Elder Kauṇḍinya and eighty thousand celestials became far removed from dust and defilement, attaining the purity of the Dharma eye. The Buddha asked Kauṇḍinya: "Do you understand this Dharma?"

He answered: "I have understood, World-Honored One!"

"Do you understand this Dharma?"

He answered: "I have understood, Sugata!"

Because Kauṇḍinya understood the Dharma, he was therefore named Ājñāta-Kauṇḍinya.

At that time, the earth-dwelling yakṣas, having heard the Buddha's teaching, proclaimed in a great voice to gods and humans: "You should know! The Buddha, at the Ṛṣipatana in Vārāṇasī, in the Deer Park, has extensively taught the Dharma wheel of three turnings and twelve aspects. By this, among gods, humans, māras, brahmās, śramaṇas, brāhmaṇas, and all worldly beings, he brings great benefit, causing those of the same brahma conduct to swiftly reach the place of peaceful nirvāṇa. Gods and humans increase while the asuras decrease." Because the yakṣas proclaimed thus, the celestials of the sky and the hosts of the Four Great Kings all heard and knew. Thus it was transmitted, and in an instant it reached all the six desire heavens. In a moment it reached even the Brahmā heaven, where the sound was universally heard. The hosts of Brahmā, having heard, also universally proclaimed it, elaborating as before. For this reason, this sūtra is called the Three Turnings of the Dharma Wheel.

At that time, the five bhikṣus and the gods and humans, having heard what the Buddha taught, joyfully practiced in accordance.
