Twenty-seventh day of the second month, eighteenth year of Engi [918 CE].

Office of the Left Imperial Secretariat [issues this directive] to Tō-ji:

Regarding the matter of placing in the sūtra repository the thirty booklets of Dharma texts sought and obtained in Tang China by Kūkai, the posthumously designated Grand Monk Superintendent, Fundamental Ācārya of the Mantra [Shingon] tradition:

The Minister of the Right, transmitting the imperial edict: the aforementioned Dharma texts are to be fully received into the sūtra repository and not removed from the country. The head of the sect is ordered to protect them in perpetuity. The temple shall acknowledge this and act in accordance with this directive without negligence.

---

Second day of the eleventh month, nineteenth year of Engi [919 CE].

Chief Scribe: Sugano no Ason Kiyokata.

Grand Counselor: Tachibana no Ason Chōsei.

Petition regarding the receipt of the Great Preceptor's honored booklets.

Total: thirty booklets.

---

Sixth day of the sixth month, eighteenth year of Jōgan [876 CE]. Acting Vinaya Master Shinnen.

Petition to supply: booklets, the *Sūtra of the Great Peacock Queen of Dhāraṇīs* (*Mahāmāyūrī-vidyārājñī-sūtra*), and other ritual texts, eight types in total, along with paper for copying.

The matters involving Esuku have been returned and concluded; received by Shinnen.

---

Second day of the eleventh month, nineteenth year of Engi [919 CE]: the aforementioned booklets were delivered from the Imperial Palace and stored in one wicker basket fitted with a standing cover of brocade-sewn brown cloth. Also included: one imperial edict document directed from the Office of the Left Imperial Secretariat to Tō-ji.

---

Twenty-fourth day of the seventh month, second year of Tenmei [1782 CE]: copied from the Hōkodai exemplar; copying completed. Zenshō.
