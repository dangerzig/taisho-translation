# 受持七佛名號所生功德經
## Sūtra on the Merit Produced by Receiving and Upholding the Names of Seven Buddhas

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0436

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Xuanzang (玄奘)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha Bhagavān was in Śrāvastī, dwelling in the Jeta Grove of Anāthapiṇḍada's park, together with immeasurable, countless śrāvakas and bodhisattva-mahāsattvas, as well as devas, humans, asuras, and the rest, all in the great assembly gathered around him.

At that time, the World-Honored One told Śāriputra: "Out of compassion for all sentient beings, I shall now briefly teach the merit produced by receiving and upholding the names of seven Buddhas, so that those who receive and uphold them may attain extraordinary benefits and happiness. You should listen carefully and reflect well upon this! I shall explain it for you in full."

Śāriputra said: "Indeed, World-Honored One! I gladly wish to hear."

The Buddha told Śāriputra: "In the east from here, there is a buddha-land called Stainless, Adorned with Manifold Virtues. In that land there is a Buddha named Sun-Wheel Pervading Radiance Auspicious Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, Perfect in Knowledge and Conduct, Well-Gone One, Knower of the World, Unsurpassed Worthy, Tamer of Persons, Teacher of Devas and Humans, Buddha Bhagavān. He is now present there, with compassion for the world, teaching the wondrous Dharma for the great assembly, revealing teachings that are good in the beginning, good in the middle, and good in the end, with skillful and subtle meaning, purely and perfectly complete, purifying the holy life. If there are those who hear that Buddha's name, they will transcend the intense suffering of flowing through birth and death in the long night for a hundred thousand koṭi great kalpas, and wherever they are born they will always remember their past lives. Those who can receive and uphold that Buddha's name will not lose the merit previously attained, and moreover for a hundred thousand koṭi great kalpas, wherever they are born, their minds will be free from delusion, and they will enjoy wondrous pleasures among devas and humans. Those who can establish an image of that Buddha and make offerings with reverence, honor, and praise will not lose the merit previously attained, and moreover for immeasurable koṭi great kalpas, wherever they are born, they will always encounter a Buddha and swiftly realize unsurpassed, perfect enlightenment.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra! In the east from here, there is another buddha-land called Wondrous Awakening, Adorned with Manifold Virtues. In that land there is a Buddha named Wondrous Merit Abiding Auspicious Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, Perfect in Knowledge and Conduct, Well-Gone One, Knower of the World, Unsurpassed Worthy, Tamer of Persons, Teacher of Devas and Humans, Buddha Bhagavān. He is now present there, with compassion for the world, teaching the wondrous Dharma for devas and humans, revealing teachings that are good in the beginning, good in the middle, and good in the end, with skillful and subtle meaning, purely and perfectly complete, purifying the holy life. If there are those who hear that Buddha's name, they will transcend all the eight difficult conditions, and wherever they are born they will always remember their past lives. Those who can receive and uphold that Buddha's name will not lose the merit previously attained, and moreover for immeasurable koṭi great kalpas, wherever they are born, their minds will always be wise, they will constantly abide in good destinies, and they will enjoy wondrous pleasures. Those who can establish an image of that Buddha, make offerings with reverence, honor, and praise will not lose the merit previously attained, and moreover for immeasurable koṭi great kalpas, wherever they are born, they will always encounter a Buddha, cultivate all the pāramitās, become a great guide, and liberate immeasurable beings.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra! In the east from here, there is another buddha-land called Lord of Beings. In that land there is a Buddha named Single Jewel Canopy King Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, Perfect in Knowledge and Conduct, Well-Gone One, Knower of the World, Unsurpassed Worthy, Tamer of Persons, Teacher of Devas and Humans, Buddha Bhagavān. He is now present there, with compassion for the world, teaching the wondrous Dharma for the great assembly, revealing teachings that are good in the beginning, good in the middle, and good in the end, with skillful and subtle meaning, purely and perfectly complete, purifying the holy life. If there are those who hear that Buddha's name, they will be freed from all sorrow and suffering, and their rank, wealth, and treasures will not diminish, except as a consequence of past evil karma already determined. Those who can receive and uphold that Buddha's name will not lose the merit previously attained, and wherever they are born they will possess great awesome virtue, supernatural powers, and sovereign mastery; their bodies will radiate light, and their appearance will be dignified and beautiful, delightful for all to see. Those who can establish an image of that Buddha and make offerings with reverence, honor, and praise will not lose the merit previously attained, and moreover for immeasurable koṭi great kalpas they will never be vainly born in a world without a Buddha. Cultivating the bodhisattva practices, adorned with great vows, they will attain unsurpassed enlightenment and benefit all beings.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra! In the east from here, there is another buddha-land called Sovereign Power. In that land there is a Buddha named Well-Gone, Settled Traces Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, Perfect in Knowledge and Conduct, Well-Gone One, Knower of the World, Unsurpassed Worthy, Tamer of Persons, Teacher of Devas and Humans, Buddha Bhagavān. He is now present there, with compassion for the world, teaching the wondrous Dharma for the great assembly, revealing teachings that are good in the beginning, good in the middle, and good in the end, with skillful and subtle meaning, purely and perfectly complete, purifying the holy life. If there are those who hear that Buddha's name, their minds will become peaceful and tranquil, free from clamor and disturbance. Those who can receive and uphold that Buddha's name will not lose the merit previously attained, and moreover for immeasurable koṭi great kalpas, wherever they are born, in all four deportments of the body, whether speaking or silent, their minds will always be in samādhi. Those who can establish an image of that Buddha and make offerings with reverence, honor, and praise will not lose the merit previously attained, and moreover for immeasurable koṭi great kalpas, wherever they are born, they will always encounter a Buddha, swiftly master all the gates of samādhi, and become a Two-Footed Honored One, liberating immeasurable beings.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra! In the east from here, there is another buddha-land called Supreme Jewel. In that land there is a Buddha named Jewel Flower Radiance Auspicious Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, Perfect in Knowledge and Conduct, Well-Gone One, Knower of the World, Unsurpassed Worthy, Tamer of Persons, Teacher of Devas and Humans, Buddha Bhagavān. He is now present there, with compassion for the world, teaching the wondrous Dharma for the great assembly, revealing teachings that are good in the beginning, good in the middle, and good in the end, with skillful and subtle meaning, purely and perfectly complete, purifying the holy life. If there are those who hear that Buddha's name, they will be endowed with superior mindfulness and wisdom-practice, and when they teach the Dharma before the four assemblies they will be fearless; their words will be awesome and dignified, and all who hear them will respect and accept them. Those who can receive and uphold that Buddha's name will not lose the merit previously attained, and moreover for immeasurable koṭi great kalpas, wherever they are born, they will be endowed with mindfulness and wisdom-practice, achieve keen and powerful wisdom, and attain superior retention of what they hear. Those who can establish an image of that Buddha and make offerings with reverence, honor, and praise will not lose the merit previously attained, and moreover for immeasurable koṭi great kalpas, wherever they are born, they will always encounter a Tathāgata. With unobstructed eloquence they will broadly proclaim the wondrous Dharma, and gradually cultivating the provisions of merit and wisdom, they will become a Honored One of Devas and Humans, liberating immeasurable beings.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra! In the south from here, there is a buddha-land called Tranquil Pearl. In that land there is a Buddha named Transcending Boundless Traces Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, Perfect in Knowledge and Conduct, Well-Gone One, Knower of the World, Unsurpassed Worthy, Tamer of Persons, Teacher of Devas and Humans, Buddha Bhagavān. He is now present there, with compassion for the world, teaching the wondrous Dharma for the great assembly, revealing teachings that are good in the beginning, good in the middle, and good in the end, with skillful and subtle meaning, purely and perfectly complete, purifying the holy life. If there are those who hear that Buddha's name, their minds will become serene and undisturbed. Those who can receive and uphold that Buddha's name will not lose the merit previously attained, and moreover they will swiftly realize the Moonlight Victorious Samādhi, and having realized this samādhi, they will then be able to realize Ganges-sand numbers of samādhi gates. Those who can establish an image of that Buddha and make offerings with reverence, honor, and praise will not lose the merit previously attained, and moreover they will be able to universally receive, uphold, and deeply penetrate the meaning of all Dharma-gates taught by all Buddhas, illuminating them without obstruction, like the light of the sun. Wherever they are born, they will always encounter a Buddha, and by this means they will swiftly realize unsurpassed bodhi.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra! In the south from here, there is another buddha-land called Supreme Fragrance. In that land there is a Buddha named Wondrous Fragrance King Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, Perfect in Knowledge and Conduct, Well-Gone One, Knower of the World, Unsurpassed Worthy, Tamer of Persons, Teacher of Devas and Humans, Buddha Bhagavān. He is now present there, with compassion for the world, teaching the wondrous Dharma for the great assembly, revealing teachings that are good in the beginning, good in the middle, and good in the end, with skillful and subtle meaning, purely and perfectly complete, purifying the holy life. If there are those who hear that Buddha's name, their bodies and minds will be harmonious and at ease, free from all heaviness and dullness; evil karma will be consumed, and afflictions will grow faint. Those who can receive and uphold that Buddha's name will not lose the merit previously attained, and moreover in future lives, wherever they are born, they will be endowed with the thirty-two marks of a great person, beheld and admired by all sentient beings. Those who can establish an image of that Buddha and make offerings with reverence, honor, and praise will not lose the merit previously attained, and moreover in future lives, wherever they are born, they will always be able to leave home, possess the pure precepts, travel to all buddha-lands, hear the Dharma and uphold it, fully cultivate all merit, and by this means they will swiftly realize anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi."

The Buddha told Śāriputra: "If sentient beings hear the names of these seven Buddhas, receive, uphold, and make offerings to them, they will certainly attain the merit described above. Why? The names and forms of these seven Buddhas were all accomplished through their original vows and great compassion. Therefore, when sentient beings hear, uphold, and make offerings to them, they all attain such benefits and happiness."

At that time, when the Bhagavān had finished teaching this sūtra, Śāriputra and all the great śrāvakas, together with all the bodhisattva-mahāsattvas, and all the devas, humans, asuras, and the entire great assembly heard what the Buddha had taught. All greatly rejoiced, believed, accepted, and faithfully practiced it.
