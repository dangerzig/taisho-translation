# 諸佛集會陀羅尼經
## Dhāraṇī Sūtra of the Assembly of All Buddhas

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1346

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Devaprajñā (提雲般若) and others.

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was beside the Ganges River, surrounded by the four world-protecting heavenly kings. At that time the World-Honored One addressed Vaiśravaṇa and the other four heavenly kings: "You should know that all sentient beings, whether male or female, whether old or young, are bound by four kinds of great terror: birth, old age, sickness, and death. Among these, the terror of death is the most difficult to eliminate. I shall now teach you the method for eliminating it."

At that time the four heavenly kings rose from their seats, joined their palms in reverence, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! Today we have obtained a great benefit, to meet the Tathāgata who embraces the world and bestows life upon it."

At that time the World-Honored One rose from his seat, faced east, snapped his fingers, and proclaimed: "All the buddhas in that direction, tathāgatas, arhats, samyak-saṃbuddhas, who without exception have all, for the sake of all sentient beings, achieved anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi: May the buddhas know that I now, wishing to compassionately rescue and protect all sentient beings, shall turn the second Dharma-wheel which was not previously turned, and shall cause the physical strength and lifespan of sentient beings to increase and grow, so that they will forever be free from untimely death. May all the buddhas come to this assembly and, for the protection of sentient beings, together use their majestic spiritual power to remove their untimely suffering."

Likewise toward the south, west, north, the four intermediate directions, above, and below. At that time the Tathāgata, with the Buddha-eye, surveyed the worlds of the ten directions. In each and every world there were buddhas filling every space, like sesame or hemp seeds, all coming to this assembly as companions. He clearly saw that in all worlds of the ten directions, in each and every region, the buddhas and world-honored ones were likewise completely filling all, plainly manifest. All these buddhas and tathāgatas spoke simultaneously and proclaimed the mantra:

*Care ca-rā care vī-nā-ciṃ svasti-ke cakra-laṃ-ga-ji prā-śā-maṃ-tu sarva-ru-ga anā-ṇi ku-nā-ṇi mahā-naṇi ca-ṇe ca-ṇe he-ma-gu-ṇe he-ma-ni śaṃ-dhi he-ma-śi-ni ki-rā-be ki-rā-be he-ku-rā-ṇe ku-rā-ṇe ku-ma-ti vi-śā-ma-ni śu-śu vi-bha a-ca-re vi-ca-re mā-vi-laṃ-bha mu-hu mu-hu.*

At that time, around each of the buddhas of the ten directions there was a vajra-guhyapati king personally attending and surrounding them. These innumerable vajra-king hosts then spoke together in unison the mantra:

*Ci ci śi-se svāhā.*

At that time Vaiśravaṇa, the Heavenly King, addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I too now wish to proclaim a dhāraṇī for the protection of sentient beings, so that they may be free from untimely death. May the Tathāgata graciously grant me power." He then spoke the mantra:

*Śvete śve-ta-rī ri-rī.*

At that time Virūpākṣa, the Heavenly King, also said: "I now wish to proclaim a dhāraṇī for the protection of sentient beings." He then spoke the mantra:

*Mā-daṃ-gī mā-daṃ-gī ni-śu mā-śu mu.*

At that time Dhṛtarāṣṭra, the Heavenly King, also spoke a mantra for the protection of sentient beings:

*Ca-re ca-re svāhā.*

At that time Virūḍhaka, the Heavenly King, also spoke a mantra:

*Ba-liṃ-bha-bha.*

At that time the World-Honored One addressed the four heavenly kings: "Devas! This dhāraṇī has been born from all the buddhas and is known and seen by all the buddhas. If a good man or good woman can read it even once in a single day, that person will never fall into the evil destinies. One should regard such a person as a great teacher. If a person, wishing to benefit all sentient beings, reads and recites this mantra day after day, it can cause the lifespans of sentient beings to increase and grow. It can also cause that person's calamities, untimely terrors, and fears to be eliminated. There will be no evil omens, nor any diseases or suffering. Water cannot sweep them away, fire cannot burn them, blades cannot wound them, and poisons cannot harm them.

"Wherever this dhāraṇī is read and recited, know that in that place all the buddhas of the ten directions constantly protect and remember. If a person copies it by hand or has others copy it, that person has thereby served and made offerings to all the buddhas. Why? Because benefiting sentient beings constitutes offering to all the buddhas.

"If one specifically wishes to protect one's body, one should copy this mantra and wear it on one's person. If in any place there are good men and good women of deep faith who encounter calamity and wish to eliminate it, they should swiftly receive and uphold this mantra in accordance with the method. The method of upholding the mantra is as follows: First, select a clean place and plaster the ground with sandalwood paste, making a square altar seven cubits on each side. The person should begin on the eighth day of the month, bathe in fragrant water, put on new clean garments, receive the eight precepts and fasting, and eat only unhusked rice, rock-sugar, and cow's milk. Take black aloeswood and camphor, a full ounce combined, and place them on the altar. Also take white sandalwood, or aloeswood, or camphor, or cloves, or kākola incense, and place them on the altar. That person should recite this dhāraṇī mantra over these incenses forty-nine times each day, for a full seven days.

"On the fifteenth day, fast for the entire day. At midday, with saffron, make twenty-one small altars upon the main altar. One of these is called the Tathāgata Altar, and the remaining twenty are called the Vajra-King Altars. Furthermore, outside the altar make four small altars, called the Four Heavenly King Altars. Take musk, camphor, white sandalwood, saffron, and red sandalwood powder, and at the Tathāgata Altar, scatter or apply them as offerings. For the remaining altars, select any one incense and offer it. Also take milk, curds, ghee, and sugar, and in that order, using four new vessels, fill each separately and place them on the Four Heavenly King Altars. Also place clean water in vessels, gather the flowers of twelve kinds of fruit-bearing trees, and place them inside. Also light ten lamps with fragrant oil and place them on the Tathāgata Altar, as offerings to the buddhas of the ten directions. On each of the remaining altars, light one lamp.

"From the previously empowered incenses, take camphor and aloeswood and burn them on the Tathāgata Altar as an offering. On the remaining altars, burn the other incenses. When burning the incense, the incenses of the Tathāgata Altar and the remaining altars should each be separately empowered by reciting this divine mantra over them. If any sentient being smells this incense, all untimely death will be eliminated. When the previously empowered incenses have all been burned, then collect the food of the Four Heavenly King Altars and scatter it in a clean place."

When the Buddha had spoken this sūtra, the four great heavenly kings and all the beings of the world, the devas, humans, asuras, gandharvas, and the rest, joyfully received it and put it into practice.
