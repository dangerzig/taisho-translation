# 佛說十一面觀世音神呪經
## Sūtra of the Eleven-Faced Avalokiteśvara Divine Spell Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1070

Translated from the Chinese. Northern Zhou dynasty, translated by Yaśogupta (耶舍崛多).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was on Vulture Peak in Rājagṛha, together with a boundless assembly of bodhisattva-mahāsattvas, surrounded on all sides. At that time Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, together with countless spell-holding sages, surrounded on all sides, came to the place of the Buddha. Having arrived, he prostrated with all five limbs touching the ground in homage at the Buddha's feet. Having paid homage, he circumambulated the Buddha three times and took a seat to one side. Then Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I have a heart-spell called 'Eleven-Faced,' spoken by one hundred and ten million buddhas. I now speak it for the sake of all sentient beings: to cause all sentient beings to be mindful of wholesome dharmas, to cause all sentient beings to be free from worry and affliction, to eliminate all sentient beings' illnesses, to eliminate all obstacles, calamities, evil omens, and bad dreams, to eliminate all untimely illness and death, to cause all those with evil minds to become gentle and pliant, to cause all māra-ghosts and spirit-obstacles to cease. World-Honored One! I have never seen any deva, māra, brahmā, Indra, śramaṇa, brāhmaṇa, or any other being who, having received and upheld this spell, whether reading, reciting, copying, or circulating it, or protecting their body with this spell, or empowering water for bathing with this spell, or entering a battle, or having been poisoned, could be harmed by any adversity through reciting this spell, except for residual karma from former lives. This spell is held in mind by all buddhas; this spell of mine is spoken by all buddhas. World-Honored One! I recall that beyond the sands of the Ganges in past kalpas, there was a buddha named Hundred-Lotus-Eyed Supreme Unobstructed Merit-Light King Tathāgata. At that time, in the presence of that buddha, I was the king among the great spell-holding sages. It was from that buddha that I first received this spell. When I received this spell, all the buddhas of the ten directions appeared before my eyes. Having seen them, I instantly attained the patient endurance of the birthless. Know therefore that this spell has such divine power and can also benefit countless sentient beings. For this reason, know that good men and good women who can diligently recite it day and night without letting it be forgotten, when reciting this spell, should not attend to other matters. In the early morning, bathe the body. If one does not bathe, one should rinse the mouth and wash the hands before reciting this spell one hundred and eight times. One who upholds this spell will obtain ten kinds of reward in their present body. What are the ten? First, the body will always be free from illness. Second, one will always be held in the minds of the buddhas of the ten directions. Third, all material goods, clothing, food, and drink will naturally be abundant and never lacking. Fourth, one can vanquish all enemies. Fifth, one can cause all sentient beings to generate minds of loving-kindness. Sixth, all sorcery and febrile illness will be unable to cause harm. Seventh, all weapons will be unable to cause harm. Eighth, all dangers from water will be unable to drown one. Ninth, all dangers from fire will be unable to burn one. Tenth, one will not suffer any untimely death. These are the ten. In one's present body, one will further obtain four kinds of reward. What are the four? First, at the moment of death, one will see the boundless buddhas of the ten directions. Second, one will never fall into the hells. Third, one will not be harmed by any bird or beast. Fourth, after death, one will be reborn in the land of Amitāyus. World-Honored One! I recall that beyond the sands of the Ganges in past kalpas, and again beyond the sands of the Ganges, and again beyond countless sands of the Ganges, at that time there was a buddha named Mandarava-Fragrance Tathāgata. I was at that time in the body of an upāsaka and again received this spell from that buddha. Having received this spell, for forty thousand kalpas I transcended the realm of birth and death. When I spoke this spell, I attained the great compassion, great loving-kindness, great sympathetic joy, and great equanimity wisdom-treasury dharma-gate of all buddhas. By the power of this dharma-gate, I can rescue all sentient beings, and from all manner of imprisonment, fetters, stocks, chains, and impending execution, as well as dangers from water, fire, and various sufferings, I always rescue and protect them, bringing them liberation. As for all yakṣas and rākṣasas, by the power of this spell I cause these rākṣasas to all generate wholesome minds with merit perfected, and they immediately generate the mind of *anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi*. My divine spell has such power. Even if a person has committed the four pārājika offenses and the five heinous crimes, if they can read and recite this spell even once, all fundamental grave sins will be completely eliminated. One who recites this spell has such merit; how much more so for one who receives, upholds, reads, and recites it according to this sūtra's teaching. Know that such a person, before myriads of koṭis of nayutas of nayutas of buddhas, has previously heard this dharma and now hears it again; how much more so for one who receives, upholds, reads, and recites it day and night without forgetting. Whatever such a person's mind wishes, I will fulfill their vow. If furthermore a person, on the morning of the fourteenth or fifteenth day, bathes with fragrant water, puts on fresh clean clothing, and every time after using the latrine bathes again, and these clean garments are not worn to the latrine; when practicing this method, one does not eat for the entire day until the next morning. In the practice hall, place an image of Avalokiteśvara and hang multicolored pennants and canopies, with incense and flowers as offerings. Upon first entering the practice hall, one must earnestly and wholeheartedly repent to the buddhas of the ten directions. When the repentance is complete, before the image spread a sitting mat, kneel reverently, and with utmost sincerity recite and uphold this spell. One who practices this method should know that they will transcend the realm of birth and death for forty thousand kalpas. World-Honored One! If one calls upon the names of one hundred thousand koṭis of buddhas, or if one calls upon the name of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, the merit of those two persons is equal and without difference."

At that time Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! If good men, good women, and all sentient beings call upon my name diligently day and night, they will all attain the stage of non-retrogression. In their present body they will be freed from all suffering and affliction; all obstacles, difficulties, and fears, as well as the sins of the three karmas, will all be eliminated. How much more so for one who practices according to this sūtra's teaching; know that such a person will attain the mind of *anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi* as if holding it in the palm of their hand." The Buddha told Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva Mahāsattva: "Excellent, excellent! Good man! Know that by your divine power in rescuing and protecting all sentient beings, you will certainly accomplish the mind of *anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi*; there is no doubt." The Buddha told Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva Mahāsattva: "Good man! I also receive and uphold this divine spell, and I also give my seal of approval. Good man! You may now speak it." At that time Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva Mahāsattva rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, knelt on his right knee with palms joined, prostrated with all five limbs touching the ground in homage at the Buddha's feet, and having paid homage, took a seat to one side. He addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I shall now, empowered by the Buddha's divine power, speak the spell:

*Namo buddhāya namo dharmāya namo saṃghāya namo jñāna-sāgara-vairocana-tathāgatāya namaḥ ārya-avalokiteśvarāya bodhisattvāya mahā-sattvāya mahā-kāruṇikāya namaḥ sarva-tathāgatebhyaḥ arhadbhyaḥ samyak-saṃbuddhebhyaḥ tadyathā oṃ dhara dhara dhiri dhiri dhuru dhuru itti vitti cale cale pracale pracale kusume kusuma-vare ili mili citi jala-mapa-nāya bodhi-sattva mahā-kāruṇika svāhā*

At that time Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! This spell has such divine power and is the most supreme." He further spoke the spell:

*Namaḥ ārya-avalokiteśvarāya bodhisattvāya mahā-sattvāya tadyathā ha ha ha ha ili mili cili vili kili hili svāhā*

Then Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! This spell is the spell for empowering water and clothing. If a person upon entering the practice hall first empowers water with this spell seven times and bathes with it, and then empowers the clothing with this spell seven times before putting it on to enter the practice hall."

*Namo ārya-avalokiteśvarāya bodhisattvāya mahā-sattvāya tadyathā juru juru ha ha ha ha svāhā*

"This is the spell for empowering incense. Upon first entering the practice hall, empower the incense seven times, and only then burn it."

*Namo buddhāya namo dharmāya namo saṃghāya namaḥ ārya-avalokiteśvarāya bodhisattvāya mahā-sattvāya mahā-kāruṇikāya tadyathā śrī śrī dhiri dhiri dhiri śrī svāhā*

"This is the spell for empowering flowers and oil. Empower flowers for scattering before the buddha and empower oil for lighting lamps, each one hundred times."

*Namo buddhāya namo dharmāya namo saṃghāya namaḥ ārya-avalokiteśvarāya bodhisattvāya mahā-sattvāya tadyathā sādhe sādhe sādhi sādhi saudhā saudhā svāhā*

"This is the spell for empowering food. When offering food to the Buddha, empower all food, drink, and various fruits with twenty-one recitations before offering them."

*Namo buddhāya namo dharmāya namo saṃghāya namaḥ ārya-avalokiteśvarāya bodhisattvāya mahā-sattvāya mahā-kāruṇikāya tadyathā hasida-si-huru huru juru suru suru svāhā*

"This is the spell for empowering fire. Use *sumana* wood to kindle a fire, and separately take *sumana* wood cut into thirty-one pieces of one-inch length. Mix *dadhi*, ghee, and honey together, and take one piece of *sumana* wood, smear it with the *dadhi*, ghee, and honey mixture, empower it with one recitation, and cast it into the fire. Finish all thirty-one pieces, empowering each one and casting it in. After finishing, practice according to the method."

*Namo buddhāya namo dharmāya namo saṃghāya namaḥ ārya-avalokiteśvarāya bodhisattvāya mahā-sattvāya mahā-kāruṇikāya tadyathā ili mili cili mili dhili hili svāhā*

"This is the boundary-establishing spell. Empower water seven times and scatter it in the four directions, or empower mustard seeds and scatter them in the four directions, or empower clean ash and scatter it in the four directions, each empowered seven times."

*Namo buddhāya namo dharmāya namo saṃghāya namaḥ ārya-avalokiteśvarāya bodhisattvāya mahā-sattvāya mahā-kāruṇikāya tadyathā raiti-dhili-citi-gaccha-gaccha-bhaga-vaṃ ārya-avalokiteśvarāya svabhāvana svāhā*

"When the circumambulation is complete, recite this spell seven times while going around Avalokiteśvara. This spell has additional divine powers. If a person suffers from intermittent fever, whether occurring daily, every other day, or every third day, empowering with this spell will bring complete cure.

"If one has been struck by an evil ghost, or struck by Hārītī, or struck by a rākṣasī, or struck by a piśāca ghost, or struck by a pūtana ghost, or struck by a lunacy ghost, or if one suffers from boils, fistulae, sores, or pustules, or if one has been stung by a snake or scorpion or any venomous insect: empower yellow earth made into mud seven times with the spell and apply it to the sting; all will be completely cured. If one suffers from wind illness, empower ghee seven times and apply it to the affected area and also consume it; one will be immediately cured. If one suffers from paralysis with ears and nose blocked, take blue wood-incense and heat it with sesame oil on birch bark, and empower it seven times; apply to the affected area for immediate cure. All such illnesses, when treated with this spell, will all be cured. The divine powers of this spell are beyond exhaustion; I have only briefly described them according to the sūtra's teaching."

At that time Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva Mahāsattva addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! If good men or good women can practice according to Avalokiteśvara's teaching, those good men and good women should use white sandalwood to carve an image of Avalokiteśvara. The wood must be solid and not decayed. The body should be one foot three inches tall, with eleven heads. The front three faces should be bodhisattva faces. The left three faces should be wrathful faces. The right three faces should resemble bodhisattva faces with fangs protruding upward. Behind should be one face with a great laughing expression. The top should be one buddha face. All faces should look forward, with a halo behind them. Each of the eleven faces wears a flower crown, and within each crown is Amitābha Buddha. Avalokiteśvara's left hand holds a water-sprinkling vessel, with lotus flowers emerging from the vessel's mouth. The right hand is extended, with a strand of jewels, forming the fearlessness-bestowing mudrā. The image's body should have jeweled necklaces and ornaments carved upon it. When the person has completed making this image, and wishes to have their heart's desire fulfilled, from the first day of the month they should enter the practice hall until the fifteenth. Upon entering the practice hall, every time after using the latrine one must bathe, and three sets of clean garments are needed, changing garments three times a day: one set in the early morning, one set at midday, and one set in the evening. The clean garments are not to be worn to the latrine. The practitioner should eat only one meal a day of a strict vegetarian fast, taking no other flavors, eating only barley and milk porridge. The room for the practice hall must be a clean room, wiped and freshly plastered with fragrant mud, and further sprinkled with fragrant water. Within the room, mark out an area seven cubits square. At the four corners, erect posts, and hang pennants all around. In the center, set up a high seat and place the image of Avalokiteśvara upon it, facing west. Scatter various flowers throughout the practice hall, and burn only aloeswood, styrax, and similar incense. From the first through the seventh day, recite the spell three times daily: one hundred and eight times in the early morning, one hundred and eight times at midday, and one hundred and eight times in the evening; food offerings are not yet necessary. From midday of the eighth day through the thirteenth day, offer various food, drink, and fruits once daily. The food offerings should not be placed on a tray but only upon clean grass spread on the ground. On the fourteenth and fifteenth days, double the finest incense and flowers as offerings. Various delicacies and assorted fruits, superior to those before, should be offered to the Buddha. The practitioner should spread only *kuśa* grass for a seat, kneel reverently, facing directly toward the image. On the fourteenth and fifteenth days, before the image light a sandalwood fire. One *sheng* of *sumana* oil should be placed in a clean copper vessel before the practitioner. Also needed is aloeswood, coarsely cut to the thickness of a chopstick, cut into inch-long pieces totaling one thousand and eight pieces. The practitioner, from the afternoon of the fifteenth day, takes one piece of aloeswood, smears it with *sumana* oil, empowers it with one recitation, and casts it into the sandalwood fire. In this manner, complete all one thousand and eight pieces in sequence. During those two days the practitioner must not eat at all. On the night of the fifteenth, Avalokiteśvara will come and enter the practice hall, and the sandalwood image will move of its own accord. When the image moves, the three-thousand great-thousand world-system will shake simultaneously. The buddha face at the top of the image will emit a voice praising the practitioner: 'Excellent, excellent! Good man! I have come to see you. Whatever vows you have will now all be fulfilled.' At that time there are four vows. What are the four? First, the vow to ascend into space from one's seat at will, unobstructed. Second, the vow to be unobstructed among all sages and noble ones. Third, the vow to become the king among spell-holding sages. Fourth, the vow to immediately follow and attend upon Avalokiteśvara in this very body. These are the four vows. The practitioner may request whichever of the four they wish, and Avalokiteśvara will immediately grant one. If the four vows have not been obtained, on the fifteenth day of the following month, set up the practice hall again. Within the practice hall, place one image that contains relics. Then place the Eleven-Faced Avalokiteśvara image beside the relic image. One thousand and eight flower stems are needed. The practitioner, before the images, spreads grass for a seat and kneels reverently. Take one flower, empower it with one recitation, and scatter it upon the image. In this manner, complete all one thousand and eight flowers in sequence. When the flowers are finished, the front bodhisattva face of the Avalokiteśvara image will emit a great thunderclap. At that time the practitioner should keep the mind calm and steady and not be frightened. When the thunder sounds, all things will shake. The practitioner's mouth should constantly recite the spell. When the thunder sounds, one should immediately make one's petition, calling out: 'Homage to Avalokiteśvara! When will this disciple be able to rescue all sentient beings from suffering? When will I be able to fulfill the wishes of all sentient beings?' Avalokiteśvara will then grant the vow as wished. When the vow is granted, the devas, nāgas, eight classes of beings, and all ghosts and spirits will be unable to create any obstacles.

"At the time of a lunar eclipse, use a red copper bowl containing three *liang* of cow's ghee. On a roadside, before an image of Avalokiteśvara, smear the ground with yellow earth in a circle one foot five inches across, and place the ghee bowl upon it. From the beginning of the eclipse, recite the spell until the moon returns to its full brightness, and then stop. Take the ghee and eat it all; none may be left over. Having eaten this ghee, all illnesses within the body will be completely cured.

"Take equal parts of realgar and orpiment, place them on a grass leaf before an image of Avalokiteśvara, and recite the spell one thousand and eight times. After empowering them, dissolve in warm water and bathe the body. All obstacles, evil dreams, and epidemic illnesses will be completely cured.

"If enemies from another region are about to invade one's territory, face the Avalokiteśvara image directly toward them, make offerings of various incense and flowers, and take lamp soot the size of a large bean. Recite the spell one thousand and eight times and apply it to the wrathful face on the image's left side. The enemy forces will be unable to advance. If an epidemic breaks out among the people of a kingdom, or if various livestock and animals die of plague, set up a practice hall. Take white mustard seeds, press them to obtain one *sheng* of oil, and take purple *jiāng* wood the thickness of a writing brush, cut into one-inch pieces totaling one thousand and eight. First kindle the purple *jiāng* wood before the image. The practitioner takes one piece of *jiāng* wood, smears it with white mustard seed oil, empowers it with one recitation, and casts it into the fire. Complete all the pieces, each empowered once and cast into the fire, and all epidemic illnesses will be completely cured. If a person has been afflicted with illness through another's sorcery, evil spells, or cursing magic, the afflicted person should be placed before the image. Take a scarlet thread, and with each recitation tie one knot, reciting seven times to make seven knots. Tie it to the image's crown for one night, then take it and tie it to the sick person's neck for immediate cure. If a person suddenly becomes afflicted with madness, take white thread and make twenty-one knots with twenty-one recitations. Before the image, recite an additional one hundred and eight times, and tie the thread to the topknot of the image's front center face for one night. Take it and tie it to the sick person's neck. If after two days there is no improvement, take the empowered cord, recite over it an additional one hundred and eight times, and twist it around the image's neck for one more night. Then take it and tie it to the sick person for immediate cure. If evil ghosts enter a person's dwelling, take one hundred and eight pieces of frankincense. Before the image, empower each piece with one recitation and place it in the fire. When the incense is finished, all evil ghosts will naturally scatter and flee, not daring to remain.

"If an enemy is seeking an opportunity against someone, take white thread. Before the image, tie it into one hundred and eight knots, empowered one hundred and eight times. Tie it to the topknot of the wrathful face on the image's left side for one night. Take the cord and, calling out the enemy's name, cut one knot for each call. Continue through all one hundred and eight knots, constantly calling the person's name, and whatever they attempt will fail. If people harbor anger toward each other, take a five-colored thread, make an empowered cord before the image, and tie it to the topknot of the wrathful face on the left side for one night. Take it and wear it on one's person, and the angry party will naturally be reconciled. If a person wishes to seek good fortune, take a five-colored thread, tie it into an empowered cord before the image, recite over it seven times, and tie it to the topknot of the front center face for one night. Take it and tie it to one's own body, and whatever one seeks will be fulfilled. If a person knows that obstacles exist within their body, take various blended incense and anoint the image's body. Further, bathe the image with fragrant water. After bathing the image, take this water, empower it with one hundred and eight recitations, and bathe one's own body with it. Having bathed, all obstacles will naturally be eliminated."

When this chapter was spoken, the entire great assembly praised in unison: "Excellent, excellent! Avalokiteśvara Mahāsattva! You are able to rescue and protect all sentient beings by speaking this great divine spell. We, the great assembly, shall also receive and uphold it."

When this sūtra had been spoken, the entire great assembly rose at once, circumambulated the Buddha three times, made obeisance, and departed.
