# 佛說大愛陀羅尼經
## Sūtra of the Great Love Dhāraṇī Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1379

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmabhadra (法賢)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was dwelling at Śrāvastī, in the Jetavana, Anāthapiṇḍada's garden. At that time there was a sea deity named Mahāprīti, who came to where the Buddha was. Having arrived, he touched his head and face to the ground in reverence at the Buddha's feet, then stepped back to stand to one side and said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One, I have a wish that would bring benefit to sentient beings. May the Buddha in his compassion permit me to proclaim it."

At that time the World-Honored One, knowing that Mahāprīti's heart harbored great benefit, said to him: "Excellent, excellent! For the sake of benefit, speak according to your intention."

At that time Mahāprīti, having received the Buddha's permission, said to the Buddha: "I and my retinue dwell in the great ocean, and we witness many sentient beings falling into peril at sea. Out of compassion for such beings, I wish to proclaim a dhāraṇī, so that those sea perils may be rendered harmless. World-Honored One, if good men and good women, and bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, upāsikās, and others hear this dhāraṇī, recite it, and uphold it, and if they but hear the names of myself and my retinue, then all great dangers of the ocean shall be wholly overcome."

At that time Mahāprīti, empowered by the Buddha's might, proclaimed the dhāraṇī:

> *tathā tsuru tsuru tulān dhāri mahā bhaṅga sthite kuniṣka niṣke anāhu tsapari tsuru tsuru samanta vandari agni cakri cakra talaṃ purāgni svāhā*

At that time the sea deity Mahāprīti, having proclaimed this dhāraṇī in accord with the Buddha's sacred dispensation, rejoiced and exulted, paid reverence to the Buddha, and withdrew.
