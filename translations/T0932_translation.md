# 金剛頂經瑜伽觀自在王如來修行法

## The Vajraśekhara Sūtra: Yoga Method for Cultivating the Practice of Avalokiteśvara-rāja Tathāgata

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0932

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Vajrabodhi (金剛智).

---

I now expound the essentials of the Vajra-Lotus Dharma according to the *Vajraśekhara Sūtra*. One must first enter the abhiṣeka samaya maṇḍala, generate the great bodhicitta, relinquish body, life, and wealth to benefit all beings, advance with vigor in accordance with mindfulness, and practice compassion, sympathetic joy, and equanimity without interruption. Only such a person should cultivate and train in this practice. As for the methods for painting the maṇḍala and its images, these are explained in detail elsewhere.

Whenever one enters the shrine to begin practice, one should first touch the five limbs to the ground and prostrate to the principal deity, Avalokiteśvara-rāja Tathāgata. Next, prostrate to Amoghasiddhi Tathāgata of the north, then to Akṣobhya, Ratnasambhava, and Vairocana Tathāgata, performing reverent prostrations to each according to the proper rite. Then kneel on both knees with palms joined in lotus mudrā and confess all transgressions of the three karmas:

> "Through countless kalpas I have drifted,
> Sinking in the sea of birth and death.
> Now with purified mind
> I openly confess and repent.
> As all buddhas have confessed,
> So too do I now confess.
> May I and all beings
> All become entirely pure."

Recite this mantra:

*Oṃ sarva-bhava-śuddha sarva-dharma sarva-bhava-śuddho 'haṃ*

Next, one should rejoice in the merit and wisdom accumulated by all buddhas, bodhisattvas, and beings of past, present, and future:

> "The buddhas of past, present, and future,
> Bodhisattvas and all beings:
> In all the wholesome roots they have gathered,
> I join palms and fully rejoice."

Next, one should place the right knee on the ground and raise joined palms above the crown, visualizing prostration at the feet of all buddhas and bodhisattvas, and recite the mantra:

*Oṃ padma-me*

Having prostrated to all buddhas, one sits according to the seat-mudrā and enters concentration, contemplating innumerable tathāgatas pervading the dharmadhātu, with oneself present in that very assembly. Then one forms the secret samaya mudrā: with the six fingers of both hands touching tip to tip, the ring fingers slightly bent pressing over the middle fingers from the sides, the thumbs pressing beneath the ring fingers, and place it upon the crown. The mantra is:

*Oṃ tathāgatodbhavāya svāhā*

Next, form the Lotus Family samaya dhāraṇī mudrā. Join both palms in lotus mudrā with the thumbs and little fingers touching tip to tip and the six fingers separated by about an inch. Place this at the right ear and recite the mantra:

*Oṃ padmodbhavāya svāhā*

Next, form the Vajra Family samaya dhāraṇī and mudrā. Hook the little fingers and thumbs over and under each other in alternation. First form the mudrā at the heart, then place the mantra at the left ear. Move the index fingers three times while reciting, and when finished, scatter from the crown. The mantra is:

*Oṃ vajrodbhavāya svāhā*

Next, form the vajra body-protection dhāraṇī and mudrā. Interlace the ring and little fingers and the thumbs inward. Raise the middle fingers upright like a banner. Bend the index fingers against the backs of the middle fingers, leaving a gap of about three-tenths like a hook shape. Place the thumbs upright at the heart. The mantra is:

*Oṃ vajrāgni-praṇapatāya svāhā*

Next, form the vajra fire-flame earth-boundary dhāraṇī and mudrā. Insert the middle finger into the space between the index and ring fingers of the opposite hand, and the ring finger into the space between the little and ring fingers of the opposite hand. Cross the remaining fingers with thumbs touching tips, turning downward, with the thumbs touching the ground like a nail being driven in. Recite the mantra three times, visualizing a single-pronged vajra of blazing fire penetrating down to the vajra foundation. The mantra is:

*Oṃ kiri kiri vajra vajrī bhūr bandha bandha hūṃ phaṭ*

For the vajra fire-city blazing-lightning enclosure boundary, use the same mudrā as before but open the thumbs and rotate them rightward through eight directions while reciting:

*Oṃ sara sara vajra-prakāra hūṃ phaṭ*

For the vajra fire-flame net boundary, also use the previous earth mudrā but open and raise the thumbs upright. Bring them over the crown and back three times, visualizing a vajra fire-flame net reaching up to the summit of existence. The mantra is:

*Oṃ viṣphurad-rakṣa-vajra-pañjara hūṃ phaṭ*

Next, form the vajra blazing-fire boundary mudrā. Place both palms side by side with thumbs upright about three inches apart. Rotate rightward above the crown, visualizing the wall and net. Recite the mantra:

*Oṃ asamāṅgini svāhā*

When this boundary is complete, the six classes of Māra and all vināyaka demons flee in terror with nowhere to hide.

Next, form the Avalokiteśvara jewel-chariot mudrā. Cross both palms face up, and with the thumbs hook the middle fingers inward three times while reciting the mantra. Visualize the principal deity arriving according to your wish. Keep your mind focused on the deity's secret heart; illusory bubbles cannot rest in mind. The mantra is:

*Oṃ ṭuru ṭuru hūṃ*

Next, form the eight-petaled great lotus mudrā. Cross both hands with the index fingers and little fingers touching tip to tip, the thumbs and middle fingers each touching their counterpart, forming eight petals of a white lotus about one cubit across. Visualize placing it upon the chariot and recite the mantra three times:

*Oṃ kamala svāhā*

Next, form the yoga secret samaya mudrā. Clasp both hands firmly with little fingers and thumbs each joined and raised upright. Strike the mudrā to awaken attention and recite:

*Oṃ vajra-padma-samaya-stvaṃ*

Next, form the mudrā of the samaya of joy and delight of all tathāgatas.

> Clasp all ten fingers firmly,
> Cross the middle fingers within;
> The little fingers and thumbs
> Join tips together and stand upright.

The mantra is:

*Oṃ samaya-hṛ-surata-stvaṃ*

Next, open the heart-door and inscribe the vajra wisdom syllables. Visualize upon the two breasts the syllable *trāṭ* on the right and *ṭa* on the left, like the panels of a palace door. Form the supreme vajra-bond mudrā and with all three karmas acting simultaneously, point to the heart and open the two syllables.

The mantra is:

*Oṃ vajra-bandha trāṭ*

> From beginningless time, habitual seeds
> Have accumulated dust and affliction.
> Now with the sin-summoning mudrā
> I gather them to be crushed.
> Clasp all ten fingers firmly,
> Extend the middle fingers straight like needles;
> Bend the index fingers like hooks,
> And in mind, summon all transgressions.
> Visualize those sins in their form:
> Hair bristling, naked, black in shape.
> Reverse the mudrā and thrust it at the heart;
> When it touches, recite the mantra.
> Because the three karmas correspond,
> All accumulated sins can be summoned.
> Having recited this summoning,
> Then perform the method of crushing.

The mantra is:

*Oṃ sarva-bhava-kariṣaṇa-viśodhana-samaya vajra hūṃ jaḥ*

> Having summoned them into the palms,
> Now perform the method of crushing.
> Cross the previous mudrā over,
> And pronouncing *vaṃ*, bind the sins.
> Extend the middle fingers straight
> With the syllables *trāṭ* upon them;
> Visualize them as a vajra pestle
> And clap together as if crushing mountains.
> Wrathful hooks and furious forms
> Can exhaust all evil destinies.
> After reciting, clap the middle fingers
> Three times seven, as appropriate.

*Oṃ vajra-pāṇi visphoṭaya satva-pāya-bandhana-nipra-mokṣaya sarva-pāpa-gati-vighāya sarva-sattvān sarva-tathāgata-vajra-samaya hūṃ trāṭ*

> With compassion universally assembling,
> Summon in all dust and defilement;
> Clap and crush all māras,
> Everything is removed.
> Pure as the full moon,
> Contemplate an eight-petaled lotus
> And visualize it within the heart.
> Place both palms with elbows touching,
> Raised upright like a jeweled lotus,
> All ten fingers widely separated,
> Gently curving the eight petals.

The mantra is:

*Oṃ kamala svāhā*

> Through this gate of correspondence,
> By the skillful means of former buddhas,
> Sins accumulated by the three karmas,
> Immeasurable and extremely heavy obstacles,
> Having performed this crushing,
> Are like fire burning withered grass.
> Sentient beings are forever deluded
> And do not know this principle;
> Through the great compassion of the tathāgatas,
> This secret and wondrous gate is opened.

Next, form the entering mudrā. Inscribe the tathāgata's wisdom syllable within: clasp both hands in vajra-bond with the thumbs entering the center and the index fingers touching tip to tip in a ring shape. Contemplate an eight-petaled lotus before you. Upon it, place the syllable *aḥ*, adorned with two dots. This wondrous syllable summons evil. Its color is white as conch and snow, radiating a thousand beams of light. Visualize using the index fingers to pick up the syllable and place it within the heart. With the three karmas acting in unison, recite this mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-veśa aḥ*

Having visualized it entering the heart, the syllable grows ever more brilliant and radiant. This is the very essence of the dharmadhātu. The practitioner should contemplate it thus. Before long, one will realize purity, for dharmas are originally unborn. The three-world tathāgatas hold the vajra of body, speech, and mind in the vajra fist through wondrous skillful means. Now close the heart-door. The wisdom syllable attains firmness. Then bend the index fingers to press against the backs of the thumbs. Touch the mudrā to the chest and recite:

*Oṃ vajra-muṣṭi vaṃ*

Next, form the mudrā for closing the heart-door of all buddhas' vajra. Interlace all eight fingers inward. Starting from the right, rotate left, then right, passing back and forth three times like turning door panels. Visualize closing the heart-door and recite the mantra three times:

*Oṃ padma-tubhe aḥ*

Next, form the mudrā for dispelling and establishing the boundary. Join both palms at the heart. Cross the ring fingers externally with the right over the left. Raise the middle fingers upright like a banner. Then hook the index fingers over the ring fingers, and join the thumbs to the little fingers at the tips. The mantra is:

*Oṃ amṛtodbhava hūṃ phaṭ*

This method is that of the Great Compassionate Avalokiteśvara transformed into Hayagrīva Vidyārāja, who empowers the practitioner to quickly attain buddhahood and establishes this boundary. Rotate left three times to dispel the unwholesome; rotate right three times to establish the boundary at whatever distance you wish.

Next, having performed the blessing, distribute syllables and visualize the true form. Place upon the visualized Avalokiteśvara the clearly manifest syllable *hrīḥ*. All these syllables must appear within a moon disk. White light streams from between the brows, then gradually draws back in. Join both palms at the heart with all ten fingers interlaced inward. Draw out the thumb and press it upright. Stamp the mudrā on the crown, forehead, both eyes starting from the right, both arms, the heart, the navel, the knees, then scatter from the left.

Recite the mantra:

*Oṃ ārulik svāhā*

Form this mudrā and distribute syllables upon the body. One's own body then becomes like Great Compassionate Avalokiteśvara Tathāgata, with the thirty-two major marks and eighty minor characteristics, a body of purple-gold radiance with perfect halo, blessed by the tathāgata. Having visualized thus, touch the mudrā to the crown and distribute the syllable *oṃ*; touch the forehead and distribute *a*; touch the eyes and distribute *ru*. Continue rightward in sequence, distributing syllables all the way down to both feet.

> *Oṃ*, visualized upon the crown,
> Its color white as the moon,
> Radiating measureless light,
> Destroying all obstacles;
> One becomes like a buddha or bodhisattva
> Touching the crown of this person.
> *A*, placed upon the forehead,
> Its color entirely golden,
> Illuminating all darkness of ignorance,
> Able to arouse profound wisdom.
> *Ru*, placed upon both eyes,
> Its color like indigo lapis lazuli,
> Able to reveal all forms and marks,
> Gradually perfecting tathāgata wisdom.
> *Li*, visualized upon both shoulders,
> Its color white as gleaming silk,
> Because the mind is pure,
> One swiftly reaches the path of bodhi.
> *Svā*, placed upon heart and navel,
> Its appearance is red in color;
> One who constantly visualizes this syllable
> Swiftly attains turning the dharma wheel.
> *Hā*, placed upon both feet,
> Its color like the full moon;
> The practitioner who visualizes thus
> Swiftly attains perfect nirvāṇa.
> Visualizing each root syllable and its color
> Constitutes the supreme dharma gate of great compassion;
> Each is also the true form of the principal deity,
> Able to extinguish all sins and attain auspiciousness.
> Like an indestructible vajra mass,
> This is called the supreme method of great compassion;
> One who constantly cultivates this practice
> Should know that such a person will swiftly attain siddhi.

Recite three times to purify the mudrā. Then visualize in your own heart that the Great Compassionate Thousand-Eyed Avalokiteśvara emits from his mouth the wondrous syllables of the Great Compassion mantra in five-colored light, which enters the practitioner's mouth and arranges itself rightward around the moon in the heart. Then recite the principal deity's root mantra once. With the right hand's ring finger, pass one bead of the rosary, proceeding continuously from beginning to end, neither hurried nor slow, neither too loud, pronouncing each syllable clearly so that one can hear it oneself. The visualization of the principal deity, the syllables distributed on the body, the mantra recitation, and the counting must all be observed simultaneously in a single moment of thought, without any gap that would cause the mind to become scattered. If the visualization becomes fatiguing, recite according to one's mental capacity, whether one hundred, two hundred, three hundred, up to seven hundred or one thousand. Keep the number constant. Even when there are circumstances requiring attention, one must still recite without reducing the count. If one seeks liberation from birth and death, there is no set number for practicing samādhi yoga contemplation. For the contemplation, visualize one's own heart as a full moon, limpid and pure, clear within and without. Place the syllable *oṃ* at the center of the moon. Arrange *a-ru-li-svā-hā* rightward in sequence around the rim of the disk. Carefully observe each syllable's meaning in correspondence with the mind, without error.

The syllable *oṃ* means "flowing continuity" and also "unborn and undying"; among all dharmas it is the supreme meaning. The syllable *a* means "unborn" and is the quiescent wisdom within the dharma of all tathāgatas. The syllable *ru* means that all dharmas are without movement and also without arising or abiding. The syllable *li* means the unequaled awakening of all tathāgatas and also no grasping or rejecting. The syllable *svā* means the unequaled and inexpressible nature of all tathāgatas. The syllable *hā* means quiescence without cause and also no abiding in nirvāṇa.

The syllable meanings explained above are all meanings of syllables that have nothing to be expressed. Having understood that there are no syllables, one contemplates that the meaning and principle are truly without anything to express. Continue continuously from beginning to end, without counting or recording, and without interruption. "Without interruption" is the meaning of flowing continuity, unborn and undying. Because there is no birth or death, there is no movement. Because there is no movement, there is no opposition. Because there is no opposition, there is no arising or abiding. Because there is no arising or abiding, there is unequaled awakening and no grasping or rejecting. Because there is no grasping or rejecting, there is equality and inexpressibility. Because of equal inexpressibility, there is quiescence without cause and no abiding in nirvāṇa. Because of quiescence without cause and no abiding in nirvāṇa, this is the unborn, undying, supreme dharma gate of all tathāgatas, called samādhi recitation.

Next, form the samādhi mudrā. Cross both palms face up. Raise the index fingers back to back and upright. Press the thumbs against the tips of the index fingers. Place upon the crossed legs.

The practitioner next cultivates ānāpānaka samādhi. Sit upright and still with the body unmoving, the tongue touching the upper palate. Calm the breath, making it subtle. Carefully contemplate that all dharmas arise from one's own mind, and that all afflictions are like flames, like illusions, without true reality. Having contemplated thus, pray: "May all buddhas show me the path of practice." Recite this mantra:

*Oṃ samaya-padme hrīḥ*

While sitting in correct posture with everything clearly perceived, contemplate the innumerable buddhas in space, who all at once snap their fingers to awaken the practitioner. They say: "Excellent! You are able to practice thus. Visualize the syllable *aḥ* distributed in your heart, shining through it with brilliance." Recite this mantra:

*Oṃ citta-prativedinaṃ karomi*

> "You must see the mind:
> Full and perfect like the pure moon.
> Then reflect further:
> From where does this mind arise?
> Habitual seeds of affliction,
> Good and evil, all come from mind.
> Mind is the ālaya;
> Cultivating purity is the cause.
> When obscured by adventitious defilements,
> One cannot achieve bodhi."

Then recite this mantra:

*Oṃ bodhicitta-mutpādayāmi*

"Having recited this, contemplate the mind with utmost clarity and purity, as if dwelling in great space without any obstruction. Then within the heart, visualize a lotus that causes the moon disk of the mind to become full, increasingly bright and clear, abiding in firm bodhi." Then receive the heart-mantra:

*Oṃ tiṣṭha vajra-padma*

"Visualize that lotus and moon disk gradually expanding, extending to fill the dharmadhātu, equal to space without obstruction. This is the gate of equal suchness. Upon the pure moon disk, contemplate the five-wisdom vajra pervading the dharmadhātu. There is only one great vajra. You should know that your own body is the vajradhātu." The mantra is:

*Oṃ sphara-vajra-padma*

"Recite this mantra and visualize that from within the lotus, measureless light streams forth. From that measureless light, measureless worlds appear. Each world is adorned with wondrous jewels, and in each, Avalokiteśvara-rāja Tathāgata is surrounded by assemblies of sacred beings. This confirms that your own mind has attained purity. Know that your own body has become that very buddha, complete with all marks." Then within samādhi, prostrate to all buddhas and pray for firm empowerment. Recite the vajra-heart mantra:

*Oṃ siṃhara-padma*

Having recited this mantra, one is immediately endowed with sarvajñā wisdom and attains perfect, complete awakening!

Next, perform the empowerment secret mudrā. Cross the middle, ring, and six outer fingers externally. Raise the index fingers touching like a lotus. Place the thumbs together upright like a banner. Stamp the mudrā on heart, forehead, throat, and crown. The mantra is:

*Oṃ vajra-dharma hrīḥ*

Then visualize the tathāgatas in space holding a celestial jewel and pouring consecration upon your crown. Join the wisdom and concentration hands in vajra-bond, with the index fingers and thumbs forming a jewel shape. Having stamped this mudrā on the forehead, one receives the five-buddha crown upon the head. The mantra is:

*Oṃ padma-ratna-abhiṣeca sarva-vaṃ hrīḥ-tvaṃ*

Next, form the lotus garland mudrā. As before, join both palms at the heart. Bend the thumbs into the center of the base. Make the middle and ring fingers and six outer fingers each into fists, like tying a flower garland, and slowly lower them. The mantra is:

*Oṃ padma-ratna-māle vaṃ*

Next, form the all-compassion armor mudrā.

> With the little fingers clasped firmly at the heart,
> Cross the index fingers to the sides;
> On the face of the index finger, visualize *oṃ*,
> On the face of the ring finger, visualize *cīṃ*.

Green light streams forth, unbroken like silk drawn from a lotus root. When the heart is settled, the wisdom fist ties the garland behind the forehead, then extends forward again. Extend the index fingers. The *oṃ* and *cīṃ* on the two fingers wind around each other. The unbroken green light wraps like armor around the heart, back, waist, both knees, chest, back, throat, neck, crown, and behind the forehead. At each point, the index fingers circle three times. Scatter the palms forward and downward like celestial robes. This universally protects all sentient beings; no heavenly māra can harm them. The mantra is:

*Oṃ apāya-padma-kavace-bandhara-kṣa vaṃ hūṃ haṃ*

Next, form the tathāgata joy mudrā. Join both palms at the heart with the index fingers crossed externally and the thumbs upright at the heart. Gently clap three times to summon all sacred beings. The mantra is:

*Oṃ padma-tuṣya ho*

One should now, through the wisdom of accomplishing action, visualize in samādhi before oneself a vast milk-ocean from which arises a great lotus king with a vajra stem, filling the dharmadhātu. Upon it is a jeweled pavilion with a lion throne, and within a full moon, a wondrous white lotus appears. Contemplate the syllable *hrīḥ* in the ocean transforming into an open lotus, which then transforms into Avalokiteśvara-rāja Tathāgata! Countless bodhisattvas surround him, forming the retinue, all assembled in the gathering.

Next, form the mudrā of Vajrarāja Bodhisattva for summoning all sacred beings. Make vajra fists with both hands, with the index finger bent three times like a hook. The mantra is:

*Oṃ padma-aṅkuśa hūṃ jaḥ*

Having recited this mantra three times, beckon with the index finger three times. The true bodies of bodhisattvas fill space and arrive from the ten directions in response to this thought.

Next, form the great mudrā of the vajra lasso. Draw the sacred body into the wisdom body. Cross the middle and ring fingers externally. Join the thumbs and index fingers tip to tip. Recite this mantra:

*Oṃ padma-amogha-pāśa hūṃ*

Then visualize the bodhisattva's dharma-body entering the painted image, which thereupon becomes the dharma-body.

Next, form the vajra hook-and-chain mudrā. This causes the principal deity to abide firmly. Hook the thumbs and index fingers together with the ring fingers and little fingers, forming the vajra stopping mudrā. The mantra is:

*Oṃ padma-sphoṭa vaṃ*

Having performed this, the sacred being's true body remains empowered and does not disperse.

Next, form the vajra wondrous-bell mudrā. Making this mudrā causes all sacred beings to rejoice. Bend the thumbs into the vajra-bond, with the index fingers bent and touching tip to tip like a ring. This is the vajra joy mudrā. The mantra is:

*Oṃ padma-veśa ho*

Through this mantra and mudrā, all buddhas, bodhisattvas, and the sacred principal deity are all delighted.

Next, form the argha water mantra and mudrā. Join both palms at the heart. Extend the middle, ring, and six outer fingers upward with palms open. Bend the index fingers to press against the thumbnails. The mantra is:

*Oṃ ṭire ṭire mudhā svāhā*

With this, make offerings to all tathāgatas and vajra beings. With amṛta, anoint the crowns of all sentient beings, extinguishing immeasurable karmic obstructions. Through this argha offering, one bathes the sacred assembly in their stainless bodies. The merit of this is incalculable. One shall attain the dharma-cloud stage of abhiṣeka.

Next, recite the hundred-syllable mantra of all tathāgatas, with its mudrā:

*Oṃ padma-sattva-samayam anupālaya padma-sattva-tvenopatiṣṭha dṛḍho me bhava sutoṣyo me bhava anurakto me bhava supoṣayo me bhava sarva-siddhiṃ me prayaccha sarva-karmasu ca me cittaṃ śreyaḥ kuru hūṃ ha ha ha ha hoḥ bhagavan sarva-tathāgata-padma mā me muñca padme bhava mahā-samaya-sattva aḥ*

Through the empowerment of this Mahāyāna hundred-syllable mantra, even if one has committed the five ānantarya sins or slandered all buddhas and Mahāyāna sūtras, all such sins are completely extinguished. Because the principal deity firmly abides within one's own body, one swiftly attains the stage of Vajrasattva and achieves the stages of bodhisattva and buddhahood.

Next, form the vajra play mudrā. Join both hands in vajra fists with the thumbs upright at the heart. In joyful play, gradually raise them in a spinning motion to the crown and scatter them, in accord with the sacred intention. This offering to all tathāgatas' wisdom serves as the play and amusement offered to all buddhas. The mantra is:

*Oṃ padma-lāse hoḥ*

Next, form the vajra garland mudrā. Without changing the previous mudrā, extend it forward as if presenting a jeweled garland to adorn the heads. The mantra is:

*Oṃ padma-māle trāṭ*

Through this mudrā and mantra offering, one shall attain the stage of dharma-king of abhiṣeka.

Next, form the vajra song mudrā. From the previous mudrā position, raise gradually from the navel to the mouth and scatter, producing sublime pure sounds to delight the sacred assembly. The mantra is:

*Oṃ padma-gīte gī*

Through this vajra song offering, one shall swiftly perfect the eloquence of a tathāgata.

Next, form the vajra dance mudrā. Contemplate wondrous clouds of celestial performers making universal offerings. With the index fingers of both hands, perform a whirling dance along the left side, the right side, and then at the heart. At each point, the index fingers circle three times, with the mantra recited without ceasing, and scatter at the crown.

*Oṃ padma-nṛtye kṛṭa*

Through this wondrous dance offering, one shall attain the tathāgata's mind-born body.

Next, form the incense outer-offering mudrā. This universally perfumes the great assembly of all buddhas. Release the vajra-bond and scatter the palms downward, visualizing wondrous clouds of incense pervading the dharmadhātu. The mantra is:

*Oṃ padma-dhūpe aḥ*

Through this incense offering, one attains the tathāgata's unobstructed wisdom. Offering wondrous incense pervading all worlds, one attains the supreme wisdom of all buddhas.

Next, form the vajra flower-scattering mudrā. With this, adorn all worlds. Release the bond-mudrā and scatter upward, like clouds of fragrant blossoms pervading the dharmadhātu. The mantra is:

*Oṃ padma-puṣpe*

Through the vajra flower offering, one swiftly realizes the tathāgata's thirty-two marks. May all sentient beings also achieve this, identical to the thirty-two marks of all buddhas.

Next, form the vajra lamp mudrā. Cross the middle and ring fingers externally. Bend the index fingers to touch tips like a jewel shape. The thumbs each bend in a pair. Maṇi lamp-light illuminates the dharmadhātu.

The mantra is:

*Oṃ padma-dīpe dī*

One who performs this practice offers the tathāgata's wisdom lamp, enabling all sentient beings to swiftly attain the wisdom of a tathāgata. Through this offering, one attains the purity of the five eyes.

Next, form the vajra perfume mudrā. This is used to make offerings to the great assembly of all buddhas. Release the vajra-bond and scatter as if applying perfume. The fragrance flows throughout the ten directions, universally perfuming the sacred assembly and the śrāvakas. The mantra is:

*Oṃ padma-gandhe gaḥ*

One who performs this practice offers the fragrance of the tathāgata's wisdom and moral conduct, enabling all sentient beings to swiftly attain purity. Through this merit, one perfects the fivefold dharma-body.

Next, form the root mudrā. One should now carefully visualize oneself as identical to the principal deity, surrounded by the retinue, abiding in great perfect-mirror wisdom. Make vajra-bond with both hands and raise the middle fingers upright like a banner. Then recite the Avalokiteśvara root mantra, with one's body dwelling in the moon disk, identical to the sattva. The mantra is:

*Oṃ lokeśvara-rāja hrīḥ*

Next, one should sing various praises and hymns. When offering praise in the early morning, wash, anoint the hands, and form the wheel-mudrā, singing praises in sublime and elegant tones. The four daily sessions are: dawn, noon, dusk, and midnight. For rosary recitation in each session, follow the methods of one's own family. Then offer argha water. With the Trailokyavijaya mudrā, rotate leftward to release the boundary. Then form the vajra liberation mudrā and reverently send off all sacred beings.

Next, form the dismissal mudrā. From the root mudrā, raise from the navel to the face, then scatter. Join palms above the crown. Visualize Avalokiteśvara-rāja Tathāgata returning to his original palace. Recite the mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-padma muḥ*

Having dismissed the principal deity, make whatever vows you wish. Then apply the armor mudrā for protection. With the Hayagrīva Vidyārāja boundary mudrā, rotate leftward to release the boundary, and move about freely. Maintain purity, offer incense and flowers, read and recite the Mahāyāna, and in all four deportments constantly be mindful of the principal deity without interruption. If any sentient being cultivates this teaching, in the present life they will realize the Ground of Joy. After sixteen lifetimes, they will attain perfect, complete awakening!
