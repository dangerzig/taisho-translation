# 佛說佛印三昧經
## Sūtra of the Buddha-Seal Samādhi, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0621

Translated from the Chinese. Later Han dynasty, translated by An Shigao (安世高)

---

The Buddha was in Rājagṛha on Mount Gṛdhrakūṭa. At that time there were twelve thousand great bhikṣus, all of whom were arhats. There were also four hundred billion bodhisattvas, all worthy ones of the caliber of Maitreya. Bodhisattvas from the eight directions, above and below, arrived in ever-increasing numbers beyond calculation. They all flew to the Buddha's presence, bowed their heads to the Buddha's feet, rose, circumambulated the Buddha seven times, and sat down, tier upon tier above and below, in hundreds of billions of rows beyond counting. The Bodhisattva Mañjuśrī was the most talented, foremost in all. His radiance and wisdom were utterly different from and surpassed all the other bodhisattvas, beyond anyone's reach.

At that time the Buddha sat in samādhi. The Buddha's body, his spirit, his outer robe, and his inner robe, all became invisible as the Buddha sat. His radiance illuminated the eight directions, above and below, and innumerable buddha-lands all became bright. In all those buddha-lands, spontaneous offerings appeared before all the Buddhas, bodhisattvas, pratyekabuddhas, arhats, and bhikṣu-saṃghas. The innumerable Buddhas of the eight directions, above and below, each sent bodhisattvas flying to the Buddha's presence, who sat beside the Buddha's seat. All the bodhisattvas assembled; their number was like the grains of sand on the banks of the Ganges, each grain of sand representing one bodhisattva. Thus it was for the sand of forty Ganges rivers, all arranged tier upon tier above and below. Each bodhisattva spontaneously sat upon a great lotus flower. The Buddha had transformed the entire world for thousands of millions of miles, and all the bodhisattvas sat filling it.

The Bodhisattva Maitreya and Śāriputra came forward with palms joined and asked the Bodhisattva Mañjuśrī: "Honored one of the highest talent! The Buddha just entered samādhi, but now he has entirely vanished. We do not know where he has gone. There must be a reason. We wish to hear about it."

Mañjuśrī said to Maitreya: "Honored one of high talent! You are about to become a Buddha in the future. And you, Śāriputra, are the Buddha's disciple, an arhat of the most powerful wisdom. Why do you not each sit in singleminded meditation and search for the Buddha's body and spirit to see what kind of practice he is engaged in?" Then all the bodhisattvas and arhats, in innumerable numbers, all sat in singleminded concentration, searching through the eight directions, above and below, through innumerable buddha-lands without end, yet none were able to know the whereabouts of the Buddha's body and spirit. The Bodhisattva Maitreya, Śāriputra, and the others again knelt with palms joined and asked the Bodhisattva Mañjuśrī: "We have singlemindedly searched for the Buddha's body and spirit, yet none of us can find where he is. We wish to know the explanation."

Mañjuśrī said: "All of you, please sit at ease for a moment. He will return of his own accord."

When they heard the Buddha was returning, they all sat upright. The entire assembly then saw the Buddha come back. At that time all were filled with joy. They rose and made obeisance to the Buddha. The Bodhisattva Maitreya, Śāriputra, and the others came forward, knelt with palms joined, and asked the Buddha: "Just now, at the time of your samādhi, the Buddha's body, spirit, outer robe, and inner robe all became invisible. We did not know where you had gone. We all joined in singleminded meditation and searched for the Buddha's body and spirit through the eight directions, above and below, without end, yet we could not find the Buddha at all. We wish to hear from the Buddha."

The Buddha said: "The place I went to is extremely profound. It is not something you can know. Only the Buddhas themselves know it." The Buddha said: "This samādhi is extremely rare to encounter. To encounter it and hear of it and know it brings a joy beyond words. Why? Because it is extremely rare to hear of."

The Buddha said: "Bodhisattvas who seek the Way and practice the six perfections without transgression for a thousand billion kalpas still cannot attain it. Even if they encounter the Buddha's samādhi at that time, they still cannot hear or know the name of the Buddha's samādhi. If they further practice the six perfections with diligence and without slackening for three thousand billion kalpas, and hear the name of the Buddha's samādhi, and encounter it, they still will not believe in its existence. If they further practice the six perfections without transgression for seven thousand billion kalpas, and hear of the Buddha's samādhi, they still will not believe in its existence. If they further practice the six perfections without transgression for eight thousand billion kalpas, and hear the name of the Buddha's samādhi, only then will they believe in it and encounter it. For one who believes in its existence, if even once they encounter it, their heart is filled with joy and delight. The merit of that one day and one night surpasses the merit of practicing the six perfections for a further three thousand billion kalpas. Why? Because one who hears and knows this samādhi will afterward seek the Way and attain buddhahood swiftly. The name of the Buddha's samādhi is the Wisdom-Seal of the Mahāprajñāpāramitā Sūtra. For a bodhisattva seeking the Way, to hear the Wisdom-Seal of the Mahāprajñāpāramitā Sūtra is a great and rare good fortune. A bodhisattva seeking the Way who wishes to become a Buddha must obtain the Mahāprajñāpāramitā Sūtra. The Mahāprajñāpāramitā Sūtra is the great father and mother of all the Buddhas of the eight directions, above and below. Only by obtaining the Mahāprajñāpāramitā Sūtra can one become a Buddha. A bodhisattva seeking the Way must first accumulate merit to the full before hearing the Mahāprajñāpāramitā Sūtra. A bodhisattva seeking the Way who does not obtain the Mahāprajñāpāramitā Sūtra cannot become a Buddha."

The Buddha said: "If a good man or good woman believes in the existence of this sūtra of the Buddha's samādhi, and kneels before it with a compassionate and joyful heart, that person immediately obtains merit from it, and in future lives also obtains merit from it. When their lifespan ends, they will be born above in the heavens as a heavenly king. When their heavenly lifespan ends, they will descend and be born in the world as a wheel-turning king. Thus, when their lifespan ends, they are born in heaven; when their heavenly lifespan ends, they descend and are born into the family of a prince or lord. Going back and forth between the heavenly path and the human path, they will never again pass through Naraka, the animal realm, the preta realm, the ghost and spirit realm, or the nāga realm. After countless kalpas, they shall all become Buddhas. If anyone hears the name of this samādhi and has even the slightest doubt or disbelief, as small as a hair, when that person's life ends, they will enter the eighteen Narakas, where they are burned and boiled, with no end in sight. Even if afterward they emerge and seek the Way, their attainment of buddhahood will be endlessly delayed. Why? Because the sūtra of the Buddha's samādhi is the essential seal and attestation of all the Buddhas of the eight directions, above and below."

The Buddha said: "What I have spoken is thus. All of you should believe it and have no doubts. Who shall bear witness? Only the bodhisattvas as numerous as the sands of forty Ganges rivers, who are my younger siblings, shall all bear witness."

When the Buddha had spoken this sūtra, the Bodhisattva Mañjuśrī, the Bodhisattva Maitreya, the Arhat Śāriputra, and all the devas and people, having heard the sūtra, were all filled with great joy. They rose and made obeisance to the Buddha.
