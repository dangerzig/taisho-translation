# 佛說滅十方冥經
## Sūtra of Dispelling the Darkness of the Ten Directions Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0435

Translated from the Chinese. Western Jin dynasty, translated by Dharmarakṣa (竺法護)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was traveling in Kapilavastu, at the Śākya clan's retreat beneath the nyagrodha tree, together with a great assembly of bhikṣus, one thousand two hundred and fifty bhikṣus, and bodhisattvas beyond number.

At dawn, the Buddha donned his robes and took up his bowl. Surrounded by the bhikṣus and their retinue, he entered the city for alms. At that time, there was a young boy of the Śākya clan named Mukhasukhaprasanna. He had prepared his chariot and was setting out from the city at dawn. Seeing from afar the World-Honored One approaching with the holy assembly, he descended from his chariot, went forward on foot, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, circumambulated him three times to the right, and stood to one side.

At that time, Mukhasukhaprasanna was sorrowful and downcast. The Buddha, knowing this in advance, asked him: "Young one, why have you come out of the city so early, with such worry and distress upon your face?"

Mukhasukhaprasanna addressed the Buddha: "O Heaven among Heavens! My two parents are unwell. They are tormented by humans and non-humans, and day and night, whether sleeping or waking, they find no rest. Even when going about, they are harassed. Moreover, though I live in a great nation, I am beset by such hardship and misery, with no one to rely on. I have been thinking to myself and do not know what to do. I do not know what means might be devised. Should I encounter enemies or thieves, or be afflicted by non-humans, sorcery, or evil spirits, I have no way to protect myself. I beg the World-Honored One to instruct me in the Dharma, to provide timely rescue and relief, so that there may be no further harm."

The Buddha told Mukhasukhaprasanna: "Listen carefully! Receive this well! Reflect upon it and uphold it! I shall teach you the Dharma of protection."

The young Śākya boy received the teaching and listened.

The Buddha said: "In the east, beyond eight thousand nayutas of buddha-lands from here, there is a world called Removing All Affliction. Its Buddha is named Equal Practice Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, teaching the Dharma. If one is going east, one should first bow one's head and take refuge in the eastern Buddha, making offerings. Then there will be no fear, and none will dare to harass or harm. In whatever one undertakes, all will be fulfilled according to one's wishes. The mind will never be confused, and whatever the heart intends will be accomplished."

The Buddha then spoke in verse:

> First pay homage to the Perfectly Awakened One,
> Equal Practice, the great sage.
> Then set out toward the east,
> And you will be free from fear.

The Buddha told the boy: "In the south, beyond a hundred million thousand buddha-lands from here, there is a world called Dispelling Darkness, Equally Essential Liberation. Its Buddha is named First Aspiration of Mind, Free from Fear, Taking Refuge, Transcending Chief Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, teaching the Dharma. If you wish to go south, you should bow your head from afar and take refuge in that Buddha, and only then set out, with single-minded concentration, the mind never leaving the Buddha. Then there will be no fear and you will encounter no calamity."

The Buddha then spoke in verse:

> Having left behind all perceptions,
> Young one, you should cultivate this.
> When traveling to the south,
> You will encounter no fear.

The Buddha told the boy: "In the west, beyond Ganges-sand buddha-lands from here, there is a world called Excellent Selection. Its Buddha is named Vajra Footsteps Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, teaching the Dharma. If you wish to go west, you should first contemplate and reflect upon the Dharma of original purity, which has nothing to be perceived: no arising and no ceasing, tranquil and pure. First bow your head and pay homage to that Buddha, take refuge with single mind, and only then set out. There will be no fear and you will encounter no calamity."

The Buddha then spoke in verse:

> All dharmas have no arising,
> Nor is there any ceasing.
> One who clearly understands this
> Will have no fear.

The Buddha told the boy: "In the north, beyond twenty thousand buddha-lands from here, there is a world called Awakened Discernment. Its Buddha is named Jewel Wisdom Chief Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, teaching the Dharma. If you wish to go north, whether at home or in your dwelling, bow your head and pay homage, taking refuge in that Buddha, and only then set out. There will be no fear and you will encounter no calamity."

The Buddha then spoke in verse:

> Whether at home,
> In all household occupations,
> Whatever you carry going north,
> You will have nothing to fear.

The Buddha told the boy: "In the northeast, beyond a million billion buddha-lands from here, there is a world called Upholding What Is Thought. Its Buddha is named Destroying Māra's Pride, Walking Alone Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, teaching the Dharma. When that Tathāgata went to the bodhi tree, the moment he sat down with single mind, he transformed all the māra officials, their retinues, and the māra heavens throughout the three-thousand great-thousand world-systems, exhorting and establishing them all in non-retrogression, so that they would attain the unsurpassed, true path. This is what that Tathāgata accomplished when he went to the tree. If you go northeast, bow your head from afar and take refuge in that Buddha, and then proceed. Wherever you go, you will find safety and will have nothing to fear."

The Buddha then spoke in verse:

> From the first arising of the aspiration,
> He vanquished the māra armies.
> If your mind constantly remembers this Buddha,
> You will immediately be free from fear.

The Buddha told the boy: "In the southeast, beyond two Ganges-sands of buddha-lands from here, there is a world called Constant Illumination. Its Buddha is named First Aspiration of Mind, Non-Retrogressing Wheel, Accomplished Chief Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, teaching the Dharma. When that Tathāgata was originally a bodhisattva, he constantly cultivated all practices of the non-retrogressing wheel: generosity, upholding precepts, patience, diligent effort, dhyāna concentration without disturbance, and the accomplishment of wisdom. This is what that Tathāgata accomplished when he was originally a bodhisattva. If you go southeast, first bow your head with all five limbs touching the ground, take refuge with single mind, and then proceed. There will be no fear."

The Buddha then spoke in verse:

> First prostrate with all five limbs,
> Then set forth from home.
> Wherever you wish to go,
> You will not encounter thieves or harm.

The Buddha told the boy: "In the southwest, beyond eighty thousand buddha-lands from here, there is a world called White Canopy of Covering. Its Buddha is named Jewel Canopy Illuminating Space Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, teaching the Dharma. If you go southwest, first bow your head and pay homage to that Buddha, scatter flowers from afar while contemplating signlessness, and then proceed. There will be no fear."

The Buddha then spoke in verse:

> Make offerings to the Perfectly Awakened One,
> Scattering flowers in veneration.
> With a mind of signlessness,
> There will be no fear.

The Buddha told the boy: "In the northwest, beyond six Ganges-sands of buddha-lands from here, there is a world called Abiding in Purity. Its Buddha is named Enlightening Bodhisattvas Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, teaching the Dharma. That Buddha's land is pure and without defilement, without craving and desire, without women, and free from the five desires. If you go northwest, first pay homage to that Buddha, take refuge, repent of your faults, purely cultivate the holy life, and then leave home. There will be no fear."

The Buddha then spoke in verse:

> First cultivate the holy life,
> Then leave the household.
> Safely, without encountering thieves,
> Wherever you go, free from all difficulties.

The Buddha told the boy: "In the direction below, beyond ninety-two niyutas of buddha-lands, there is a world called Mindfulness Without Inversion. Its Buddha is named Mindful of the First Aspiration, Severing Doubt, Removing Desire Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, teaching the Dharma. When you wish to sit or lie down at night, remember this Tathāgata, bow your head in refuge, and always with universal compassion think of saving all beings. Then sit or lie down, and there will be no fear. Whatever you wish will be fulfilled."

The Buddha then spoke in verse:

> With universal compassion, think of all beings.
> Whether sitting in meditation or lying to sleep,
> In dreams or while awake,
> There will be no fear.

The Buddha told the boy: "In the direction above, beyond sixty Ganges-sands of buddha-lands, there is a world called Free from All Fear, Without Fixed Abode. Its Buddha is named Dispelling Darkness, Equally Transcending King Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, teaching the Dharma. Whenever you rise from sitting, always pay homage to that Buddha, take refuge, and make offerings. Always harbor a compassionate mind, pitying all sentient beings. Then rise and proceed, and there will be no fear. Wherever you go, you will find auspicious peace. By this mindfulness, you will always be protected."

The Buddha then spoke in verse:

> Always harbor a mind of equal compassion,
> Pitying all categories of beings.
> Through this care for all living beings,
> There will be no fear.

The Buddha told the boy: "You should faithfully receive these Buddhas' names, uphold them, reflect upon them, and embrace them in your heart. Wherever you wish to go, there will be no danger or difficulty."

Mukhasukhaprasanna, the young Śākya boy, came forward and addressed the Buddha: "I have already received these Buddhas' names, embraced them in my heart, and am reflecting upon and practicing them. Their significance is immeasurable. Having established my own mind, I see the ten directions without any obstruction. What the World-Honored One has just proclaimed, conveying the sūtras and the Buddhas' names, is just as I have heard it, true and without discrepancy."

At that time, the entire assembly of a hundred thousand people heard what the Buddha had taught. All rose from their seats, straightened their garments, and addressed the Buddha kneeling: "Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, your great compassion covers all! You have taught this sūtra for our sake. We and all present now see the Buddhas; within and without, all is transparent and without darkness, just as the Buddha has said, without any difference."

Thereupon, Śāriputra came forward and addressed the Buddha: "How swiftly these sons of good family have attained the pure eye, seeing all the Buddhas without obstruction! Is the power of this merit truly so great? Is it through the awesome spiritual power of these Buddhas, or through the merit of their own past lives?"

The Buddha said to Śāriputra: "It is both the Buddhas' awesome spiritual power and also their own past merit."

At that time, the Buddha emitted a great radiance that universally illuminated the ten directions. The earth shook greatly. The devas scattered flowers and incense. Musical instruments sounded without being played. The Worthy Ānanda rose from his seat, straightened his garments, knelt, and with palms joined addressed the Buddha: "What is the reason for this great radiance illuminating everywhere and this great shaking of the earth, with the devas scattering flowers and incense?"

The Buddha told Ānanda: "Did you see the hundred thousand people, with palms joined, taking refuge before the Buddha?"

Ānanda replied: "I have seen them, World-Honored One!"

The Buddha said: "These hundred thousand people, through the roots of virtue they have established and the practices they have undertaken, will for immeasurable kalpas not fall into evil destinies. Each will successively serve as a cakravartin holy king, sovereign of the four continents, throughout as many worlds as there are sands in the Ganges. As cakravartins, they will always see the Buddhas, and through their own vows and aspirations, all their wishes will be fulfilled. Afterward, they will all attain Buddhahood in the world, with the name Pure Eye Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, Perfect in Knowledge and Conduct, Well-Gone One, Knower of the World, Unsurpassed Worthy, Master of the Dharma Path, Teacher of Devas and Humans, Buddha, World-Honored One. All will share the same name, each in a different world where they accomplish their path."

As soon as the Buddha bestowed this prediction upon all these sons of good family, the earth spirits and the spirits of empty space all raised their voices in unison, proclaiming: "The merit of these sons of good family is immeasurable!" Their voices reached up to the four heavenly kings, the Trāyastriṃśa heaven, the Yāma heaven, the Tuṣita heaven, the Nirmāṇarati heaven, the Paranirmitavaśavartin heaven, up to the māra realm, and further to the Brahmā heaven and the Akaniṣṭha heaven, the twenty-fourth. All together they sang in praise: "How rare! How rare! Truly unprecedented! The awesome grace of the Buddhas, World-Honored Ones, is able to save all! How majestic and magnificent! To exhort, illuminate, and transform these sons of good family, and bestow upon them the prediction that they will attain the unsurpassed, true path, with wisdom unobstructed, universally seeing and accomplishing all!"

Mukhasukhaprasanna, the young Śākya boy, came forward and addressed the Buddha: "What is this sūtra called? How should it be upheld?"

The Buddha told the boy: "This sūtra is called 'Dispelling the Darkness of the Ten Directions.' Why? By being mindful of the Buddhas of the ten directions and taking refuge with single mind, the heart becomes open and serene, as if one in darkness beholds light, with no more fear. Therefore it is called 'Dispelling the Darkness of the Ten Directions.' You should uphold it. It is also called 'What the Tathāgata Praises'; you should uphold it. It is also called 'Comprehending the Dharma of Signlessness.' It is also called 'Emptiness, Having Nothing.' Diligently uphold it."

The Buddha told the boy: "If there are persons who receive this sūtra, uphold, recite, and read it, and explain it for others fully and completely without any omission, their wishes will be swiftly fulfilled. Bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, and upāsikās who receive and uphold this sūtra and recite and read it will never know fear. If before the magistrates, they will not be wrongly accused. If among thieves, they will not be endangered. If amid a great fire, it will be extinguished. If in water, they will never drown. Devas, nāgas, ghosts, spirits, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, evil spirits, and other various ghosts, humans and non-humans, none will dare touch them. Lions, tigers, wolves, fierce beasts, and bears will not dare approach them. Hungry ghosts, goblins, backward-footed ghosts, and latrine-dwelling ghosts will not be able to trouble them, and there will be nothing to fear. Whether dwelling in seclusion, in the open wilderness, under a tree, or in an open field alone, one will be established and protected by the Tathāgata. If sons of good family and daughters of good family receive this sūtra, uphold, recite, read, and transcribe it, they have already made offerings to the Buddhas of the past and have planted manifold roots of virtue; the merit they have cultivated is about to be fulfilled."

At that time, the Buddha told the Worthy Ānanda: "If there were a person who now met the Buddha face to face and with sincere heart made offerings of robes, food, bedding, and medicine for illness, this would not compare with a person who receives and upholds this sūtra, transcribes, recites, and reads it, and teaches it to others. The latter is equivalent to having fully made offerings to the Buddha."

At that time, Śakra, Lord of the Devas, came with immeasurable devas, each bearing heavenly flowers, and made offerings by scattering them before the Buddha. He addressed the Buddha: "I shall protect and guard all who uphold this sūtra."

The four heavenly kings and the devas above each brought flowers and incense to make offerings to the Buddha, and each addressed the Buddha: "We shall protect and guard the sons and daughters of good family who receive this sūtra, uphold, recite, and read it, and teach it to others. If they transcribe and carry the sūtra-scroll, wherever they dwell we shall surround and guard them, and none who lie in wait shall be able to find an opening."

When the Buddha had taught this, Śakra, Lord of the Devas, and his assembly, the young Śākya boy Mukhasukhaprasanna, the four heavenly kings, devas, nāgas, ghosts, spirits, asuras, and worldly humans heard the sūtra and rejoiced. They bowed in reverence and departed.
