# 阿闍梨大曼荼攞灌頂儀軌

## Ritual Manual for the Ācārya's Great Maṇḍala Abhiṣeka

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0862

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown.

---

At that time, Vajrapāṇi said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! If good men or good women enter the samaya of this great king of maṇḍalas born from the great compassion treasury, how much accumulation of merit do they acquire?"

When he had spoken thus, the Buddha addressed Vajrapāṇi: "O Secret Lord! From the initial arousing of the aspiration all the way to becoming a tathāgata, all the accumulated merit: the merit of those good men and good women is equal to that. Secret Lord! Through this Dharma-gate, one should know it thus: those good men and good women are born from the mouth of the Tathāgata; they are children of the Buddha's heart. Wherever those good men and good women are, in that very place there is a buddha performing buddha-activities. Therefore, Secret Lord, if one wishes to make offerings to the Buddha, one should make offerings to those good men and good women. If one wishes to see the Buddha, one should contemplate them, for they can crush the māra-armies and bring benefit and joy to all. Therefore, those of you who wish to attain the true wisdom of all tathāgatas should single-mindedly practice this Dharma, for it swiftly accomplishes omniscience."

Empower a five-colored cord and bind it to the disciple's left arm. The mantra is:

*Oṃ mahā-vajra-kavaca vajrī kuru vajra vajra haṃ.*

"Homage to the great holy Vairocana Tathāgata of the Vajradhātu. Homage to Akṣobhya Tathāgata of the east. Homage to Ratnasaṃbhava Tathāgata of the south. Homage to Avalokiteśvara-rāja Tathāgata of the west. Homage to Amoghasiddhi Tathāgata of the north.

"Homage to Samantabhadra, Vajrasattva, and all the bodhisattva mahāsattvas throughout the dust-mote lands of empty space and the dharma-realm, layer upon layer like Indra's net, in the three times."

Next, recite this secret mantra mentally to protect all the disciples. The secret mantra is:

*Oṃ mahā-vajra-kavaca vajrī kuru vajra vajra haṃ.*

Next, empower unguent and apply it to the disciples' palms. The mantra is:

*Oṃ vajra-gandhe jāḥ.*

When applying the unguent, say: "May you all be fully endowed with the fragrance of the morality, concentration, wisdom, liberation, and knowledge and vision of liberation of all tathāgatas."

Next, empower a white flower and bestow it upon the disciples. The mantra is:

*Oṃ vajra-puṣpe oṃ.*

Say: "May you all attain the inexhaustible ocean of marks of all tathāgatas."

Next, empower a censer and fumigate the disciples' hands with it. The mantra is:

*Oṃ vajra-dhūpe aḥ.*

Say: "May you all attain the wondrous form moistened by the inexhaustible great compassion of all tathāgatas."

Next, empower a lamp and have the disciples gaze upon it. The mantra is:

*Oṃ vajra-āloke īḥ.*

Say: "May you all attain wisdom equal to the space-realm of all tathāgatas."

The mantra of the Radiant Vajra Sword empowers the five products of the cow:

*Oṃ vajrotkāṭa.*

Have the disciples drink. The mantra:

*Oṃ mahā-jvāla hūṃ.*

Next, having empowered with the vajra sword of the profound wisdom of all buddhas that destroys all afflictions and secondary afflictions, or with the division's heart-mantra, take an udumbara or aśvattha tooth-stick measuring ten finger-widths, wash it with fragrant water, anoint and fumigate it, wind flowers around its root, offer one to all buddhas, and give the rest to the practitioner.

Empower with the Acala vidyā and with the one hundred and eight tathāgata-tooth empowerments.

Next, bestow the tooth-stick upon the disciple. The disciple then goes to the precept-conferring place, chews the small end, and is asked: "In which direction did you throw the tooth-stick? That is the direction." A holy being speaks this verse:

> "You have obtained peerless benefit,
> Equal in rank to the Great Self.
> All the tathāgatas
> And the bodhisattvas instructed by this teaching
> All embrace you
> To accomplish the great work.
> Tomorrow you shall
> Attain birth in the Mahāyāna."

The stick is thrown and its sign is examined.

Three: bind a sūtra-thread and tie it to the disciple's samādhi-arm.

Next, for the night practice, a red cloth covers the disciple's head, closing all gates to the evil destinies and opening the accomplishment of the pure five eyes.

Bind the samaya-mudrā and orally transmit this secret mantra:

*Samaya-stvaṃ.*

Then have the disciple extend the patience and vow fingers upright like a needle. Leading them into the maṇḍala gate, transmit this secret mantra three times:

*Samaya hūṃ.*

Then say: "You have now entered the retinue-division of the Tathāgata. I now cause you to give rise to vajra-wisdom. Through this wisdom you shall attain the supreme accomplishment of all tathāgatas, and all worldly and supramundane siddhis and activities shall all be accomplished. Furthermore, you must not reveal these ritual matters before anyone who has not entered the maṇḍala and received abhiṣeka. If you do, not only will you lose your samaya, but you will also bring calamity upon yourself."

The master should firmly bind the wisdom-mudrā of Vajrasattva and place it on the disciple's crown, saying: "This is the samaya-mudrā of Vajrasattva. If you carelessly reveal it to one who has not received abhiṣeka, it will cause your head to split open. You must not be disrespectful toward me. You should give rise to deep faith. You should regard my body as Vajrasattva, and carry out all my instructions fully. If you do not, you will bring calamity upon yourself, or die prematurely and fall into hell. You should take heed."

When the master wishes to cause Vajrasattva to enter the disciple's body and mind, he binds the Vajrasattva-mudrā and says: "This is the samaya of Vajrasattva. May it enter your body and mind and become the supreme vajra-wisdom."

Recite this secret mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-āveśa aḥ.*

Next, bind the wrathful vajra-fist, with the patience and vow fingers hooked together, and recite the above Mahāyāna samaya hundred-syllable secret mantra. After chanting, pull open the mudrā. Through the power of this secret mantra, the disciple enters vajra-wisdom and realizes supreme insight. Through this wisdom, one can know the various minds of all sentient beings, know the three kinds of worldly activities, firmly establish the bodhicitta, extinguish all suffering, be free from all fears, not be harmed by any evil, be empowered by all tathāgatas together, have all siddhis manifest before one, and obtain unprecedented peace and joy without seeking.

"You should deeply rejoice. I now briefly explain for you the meritorious excellences: in all grounds, all liberation-gates, supernatural-power gates, samādhi-gates, dhāraṇī-gates, perfection-gates, the ten powers, fearlessnesses, and unique qualities, through this Dharma all shall be attained. All the profound meanings of hundreds of thousands of sūtras you have never before seen or heard, you will naturally comprehend. Before long you shall realize the true wisdom of all buddhas; how much more the lesser siddhis?"

Having spoken thus, next ask: "What kind of vision have you seen?" Next, having sequentially destroyed the sins, further visualize the light of all buddhas purifying the disciple's body and mind. Akṣobhya and the others of the four directions, Mahāvairocana Tathāgata and others above, all emit pure light; below, visualize the vajra-heroic syllable emitting wrathful light to crush and destroy them.

When performing this rite, the disciple will certainly see auspicious signs, and one should know that their karmic obstructions have been eliminated. If their sins are extremely heavy and they see no good signs, the master should recite a verse of truth to awaken them.

The verse says:

> "Samantabhadra's true body pervades all;
> He can be the sovereign king of the world.
> Without beginning, without end, without birth or death,
> His nature and marks abide forever, equal to empty space.
> The minds of all sentient beings,
> Firm in bodhicitta, are called sattva;
> The mind abides in the immovable samādhi,
> Diligent and resolute, called vajra.
> I now speak these words of truth;
> May the World-Honored One, fulfilling his original vow,
> For the sake of all beings' siddhis,
> With compassion and pity, grant his empowerment."

Having spoken thus, again bind the vajra-entry mudrā and recite the syllable-mantra one hundred and eight times.

Bind the vajra-bond: with the dhyāna-wisdom fingers pressing the base of the dāna-prajñā fingers, and the vīrya-bala fingers slightly bent and touching each other.

Having performed the rite thus, if no good signs appear, one should simply introduce the disciple and confer the samaya, but should not give them abhiṣeka.

Next, transmit this secret mantra to the disciple three times: "The vidyā is not added; simply recite the buddha-samaya vidyā with empowerment."

*Oṃ praticcha vajra guh.*

After reciting, have the disciple cast their flower. Whichever deity the flower lands upon, the disciple receives that division's deity-mantra and shall swiftly attain accomplishment.

Next, recite this secret mantra three times, and release the samaya-mudrā that the disciple had bound upon their heart. The secret mantra:

The vajra-liberation mantra:

*Oṃ tiṣṭha vajra nirjime bhava śaśvato me bhava hṛdayaṃ me adhiṣṭha sarva-siddhiṃ ca me prayaccha hūṃ ha ha ha ha guh.*

Next, recite this secret mantra and remove what covers the disciple's eyes. The secret mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-sattva svayaṃ tine cakṣur daghaṭanā tapārotkāṭayati sarva-akṣu vajra-cakṣur anuttrāṃ he vajra-pāśa.*

Next, call the disciple and show them all the ritual implements of all the divisions within the maṇḍala. Through this Dharma, one is embraced and guarded by all tathāgatas, and Vajrasattva constantly abides in their heart. Whatever they seek, up to the very body of Vajradhara, nothing will not be attained, and they will gradually enter the essential dharma-nature of all tathāgatas.

Next, the one to be consecrated is led forward: their left foot steps on the flower-gate, their right foot steps on the flower-center. Entering through the gate of Śakra's direction, they sit upon the flower-platform. Offerings are made with various flowers, unguents, burnt incense, lamps, banners, canopies, and exquisite music. If one cannot prepare all of these, one does what one can. The reason is that this person sits in the Buddha's seat. Further, with various praises, songs, and hymns, one causes them to feel solemn joy. Recite this verse:

> "When the buddhas descend from Tuṣita to be born,
> Śakra, Brahmā, nāgas, and spirits attend as retinue;
> The various supreme and wondrous auspicious events:
> May you today fully obtain them all.
> Born in the Śākya palace at Kapilavastu,
> The nāga kings bathed him with sweet-dew water;
> The auspicious events of the devas' offerings:
> May your abhiṣeka be just like this.
> On the vajra-seat, for the sake of all beings,
> In the last watch of the night, he vanquished Māra and attained perfect awakening;
> The rare and auspicious events that manifested:
> May you accomplish them all upon this seat.
> Deer Park at Vārāṇasī, adorned by the river,
> He opened the wondrous Dharma for the five ascetics;
> The immeasurable auspicious events accomplished:
> May you today fully obtain them all.
> The ocean of great compassion and skillful means of all buddhas
> Universally benefits the ocean of sentient beings in the dharma-realm;
> To the end of future time, without fatigue,
> The four unimpeded wisdoms you shall attain."

If there are further praises and verses, compose them as one wishes, encouraging the supreme aspiration and bringing joy.

Next, one should confer abhiṣeka. First, visualize the syllable on the disciple's crown, emitting great blazing flames, brilliant and radiant. Also visualize within the disciple's heart a moon-disk, and within the moon-disk an eight-petaled lotus; upon the lotus-platform, also visualize the syllable. If the Vajra division is obtained, within the syllable visualize a five-pronged vajra; for the other divisions this can be known accordingly. If Mahāvairocana is obtained, visualize a stūpa, and one should visualize one's own body as the image of Vairocana. Take the flask belonging to the disciple's division, and visualize the object of their division within the water of the flask, such as a vajra or lotus. Have them each bind the mudrā of their obtained division. Place it upon their crown, recite the mantra of the corresponding division seven times, and perform the abhiṣeka.

The mantra is:

*Oṃ vajra-sattva abhiṣiñca hūṃ. Oṃ vajra-ratna abhiṣiñca trāḥ. Oṃ vajra-padma abhiṣiñca hrīḥ. Oṃ vajra-karma abhiṣiñca aḥ.*

Upon the disciple's forehead, visualize the syllable *LA*, colored like true gold; upon both eyes, each a syllable *RA*, colored like fire, with light and flames above; between the two feet, visualize various colors forming the shape of a dharma-wheel adorned with eight spokes.

Next, recite the heart-mantra of Vajrasattva, empower unguent, and apply it to the disciple's chest. The reason for performing this empowerment is to cause the disciple to become like Vajrasattva.

Next, bind the Mahāvairocana-mudrā, recite the root-mantra, and place the mudrā on the disciple's heart, then forehead, then throat, then crown. Then one should truly visualize the supreme secret crown of all tathāgatas being placed on the disciple's head. Then bind the four kinds of garland-mudrās as described above, and tie them to the disciple's forehead according to the rites of their division.

If performing the ācārya abhiṣeka rite, one should use all five flasks in the order described above, and bind the four kinds of garlands layer by layer upon the disciple's forehead.

Next, the master takes a five-pronged vajra with the mudrā-feather and bestows it upon the disciple's hands. With various skillful and encouraging words, one comforts and guides them, reciting this verse:

> "The buddhas' vajra-abhiṣeka rite:
> You have now been consecrated in accordance with the Dharma.
> In order to accomplish the tathāgata's essential nature,
> You should receive this vajra."

Having recited this verse, the mantra is:

*Oṃ vajra-adhipati vidvān abhiṣiñca mitiṣṭha vajra-samaya-stvaṃ.*

Then take back the vajra. If it is the Jewel division, visualize a jewel upon the vajra, and so forth for the other divisions. When reciting the above mantra, change the initial word "vajra" to "jewel," and so forth for the other divisions.

Next, to the disciple's original name, add the word "Vajra," and address them by this name. Recite this mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-sattva abhiṣiñca mi vajra-nāma abhiṣekte he vajra-nāma.*

Also, with incense, flowers, and various offerings, make offerings to the one who has received abhiṣeka. The master should take a small vajra and, as if treating the eyes, wipe the disciple's eyes, and say: "Good man! A physician of the world can remove cataracts from the eyes. Today the buddhas and tathāgatas remove the cataract of your ignorance in the same way, so that you may attain the vajra-wisdom eye and see the true nature of dharmas."

Next, take a mirror and have them look into it, to reveal that the nature and marks of all dharmas are empty and quiescent. Recite this verse:

> "The nature of all dharmas:
> Neither defilement nor purity can be found;
> Neither real nor unreal,
> All appear from causes and conditions.
> You should know that all dharmas
> Have no intrinsic nature to depend upon.
> You are now a true child of the Buddha;
> Broadly benefit all sentient beings."

The master should feel reverence toward the disciple, for this person is able to continue the lineage of all buddhas.

The master should bestow a conch shell upon the disciple and say: "From this day forward, you should turn the Dharma-wheel of all buddhas. You should blow the unsurpassed Dharma-conch, causing the great Dharma-sound to pervade all places. You should not harbor doubt or fear regarding this Dharma. In the ultimately pure practice of all mantras, you should broadly reveal and show skillful means for sentient beings. Good man, listen well: if you can practice in this way, all tathāgatas will know that you can repay the kindness of the Buddha. Therefore, at all times and in all places, all vajra-holders will protect you and grant you peace and joy."

Next, lead the disciple before the great maṇḍala, explain the samaya to make it firm, and say: "Good man! You should firmly uphold the true Dharma. Even if you encounter hardship and affliction, even unto death, you should not abandon the cultivation of bodhicitta. Toward those who seek the Dharma and toward material things, you should not be miserly. Toward sentient beings, you should not commit even the slightest harmful act. This is the supreme essential meaning, the domain of the holy. I have now fully explained it for you. You should practice in accordance with what has been taught."

The disciple should feel deep gratitude, join their palms, and prostrate.

Again, take the five-pronged vajra and bestow it, saying: "This is the essential nature of all buddhas, held in the hand of Vajrasattva. You should firmly uphold the precepts and always carry it."

After the disciple receives it, transmit this definitive vow-mantra and have them recite it:

*Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-siddhi vajra-samaya-tiṣṭha eṣa tvam dhāraya mi vajra-sattva hi hi hi hi hūṃ.*

Having performed the rite thus, the master should instruct the disciple in all the secret samaya-wisdom of all the maṇḍalas.

If the disciple has a lapse in the samaya, the master should restrain them and not allow them to cause damage. The disciple should respect and honor the master, never notice the master's faults, and not harbor dislike toward fellow students.

Say to them: "You should always feel loving-kindness and compassion toward all sentient beings, sympathetically instruct and guide them, and never feel aversion."

Recite this verse:

> "In the three realms, the gravest sin
> Is nothing other than aversion;
> In the domain of desire,
> Do not give rise to aversion."

Next, to make the disciple firmly maintain joy, recite this verse:

> "These samayas
> The buddhas have taught for you;
> Guard and cherish them well,
> As you would protect your own life."

After the disciple receives the master's instruction, they prostrate at the master's feet and say: "As the master has instructed, I vow to practice."

Dedication of merit and vows: "The disciple so-and-so, having come before the great maṇḍala of the great compassion womb-treasury, heard the true Dharma and given rise to pure faith. Having reverently invited the holy assembly of the ocean-gathering of sages, taken refuge in the Three Jewels, repented and purified sins, and received the Buddha's pure precepts; having cast the flower and formed a connection, received the embrace of the holy ones, and obtained the vajra title of abhiṣeka. In accordance with the merit of the holy stages, boundless and incalculable as grains of sand, I dedicate all of it to sentient beings throughout the dharma-realm. May they all leave suffering and attain peace and joy, abandon wrong and return to right, arouse bodhicitta, practice the bodhisattva-path and never retrogress, and in a future time all attain buddhahood together."

### Colophon

> "Eliminating the three karmas of ignorance and delusion,
> One directly attains the moon-disk of the sattva's mind;
> With the definitive non-retrogression of the three secret practices,
> Self and other completely attain siddhi."

In the twelfth year of Kansei, gēngshen, late autumn, to supplement the printing blocks at Buzan, this was collated against four or five variant copies based on the transmitted text and annotated.

Recorded by the Śramaṇa Shinjō of Kawachi, Yakujusan.

In the first year of Kyōwa, kanototori, fifth month, this was further collated and corrected against the text of Chishakuin in Kyoto. A note at the end of that text states: "The Sanskrit phrases and meanings are not found in the old text. Following the oral transmission of the master, I privately added them. However, those phrases and meanings entirely follow the commentary on the four-fascicle *略出經*. In the course of copying and reviewing, the literary garden was overgrown and the path of meaning was impassable. Considering this, the ritual matters are kept secret and frequently corrupted; I have thus ventured to annotate a portion."

Recorded by Ekō, a disciple of Naishū Kyōkō: "The following Sanskrit phrases and meanings were later additions; yet through long transmission, they came to lack phrases and meanings, or have only the Sanskrit with the Chinese translation missing. This awaits further collation with good copies." Recorded by the Śramaṇa Kaidō of Buzan Sōjiin.
