# 文殊菩薩獻佛陀羅尼名烏蘇吒
## Dhāraṇī Presented to the Buddha by Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva, Named Ususaṭa

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1178

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown (失譯)

---

At that time, the Buddha commanded Ānanda: "For the sake of future sentient beings who are submerged in suffering, proclaim this widely and spread it abroad."

At that time, Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva first spoke a verse, saying:

> All classes of sentient beings
> Revolve and sink in the realm of lustful demons;
> None has the capacity to awaken them...
> Only I am able to rescue and deliver them,
> Permanently severing the root of birth and death,
> So that all may abide in the bliss of nirvāṇa.

He then recited the dhāraṇī:

*kuli kuli dena ucuṭa ucuṭa dena duhu duhu dena guṭa guṭa dena ñamidu ñamidu dena guṭahu guṭahu dena uśu uśu dena yamiñya yamiñya dena duhūṭa guṭata svāhā*

"The foregoing spell is to be recited three times; a cord is to be knotted with seven knots and tied around the ankle. This spell can cause all those who have lost their minds to regain right mindfulness; it extinguishes the fire of lustful desire, removes the fetter of pride, exhausts the defilements of the three poisons, and naturally brings about coolness and purity.

"If there are those whose spirit is sunken beneath the deep abyss of ignorance, long submerged in saṃsāra and unable to find the way out, alas! This is deeply lamentable. If a person's mind attains awakening and is utterly repelled by defilement, knowing that this is the domain of ordinary foolish persons, of spirits, demons, and animals; it is not fitting for the holy life, nor is it what the sages desire. Only such a person may receive and be given this dhāraṇī.

"Do not transmit it to those who are hypocritical, who doubt and slander, or who have little faith; even if they recite it, they will see no efficacy and will instead regress and fall. But if one recites it with utmost sincerity, the fire of desire will gradually be extinguished; once the fetters are eliminated, the mind attains liberation. When the mind is liberated, one then attains the fruit of the Path. This is the true and non-deceptive efficacy of its spiritual power.

"Those who practice this method must abstain from alcohol, the five pungent vegetables, and meat. Men should practice for nine times nine, eighty-one days; women for seven times seven, forty-nine days. Day and night, throughout the six periods, one should diligently and wholeheartedly recite; burn aloeswood and white sandalwood incense, scatter flowers, and make offerings to all Buddhas of the ten directions, three times by day and three times by night, taking great care never to neglect or forget.

"In the remaining time before the number of days is completed, one should dwell alone in a mountain cave, monastery, or village, keeping oneself far from people in all four directions. One should give rise to deep joy and spontaneously rejoice, thinking: 'Now I have encountered the spiritual power of a great sage; this very body may attain the body of a Buddha, and I need not fear submersion in saṃsāra.' With a mind thus purified, in thought after thought maintaining faith and devotion, one should sever all evil deeds, refrain from idle talk and divisive speech, and not dwell on fame or gain. When such a number of days has been completed, liberation is certain; the mind becomes serene and is no longer afflicted by desire or pride."
