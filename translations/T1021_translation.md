# 華嚴經心陀羅尼
## Heart Dhāraṇī of the Avataṃsaka Sūtra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1021

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Śikṣānanda (實叉難陀), Tripiṭaka Master of Khotan.

---

[Dhāraṇī:] oṃ kina kina tathāgathu ṭibhave śānti bhārati udā mudāme oṃ sarvathe svāhā

This dhāraṇī was spoken by Samantabhadra Tathāgata and translated by Śikṣānanda, Tripiṭaka Master of the Kingdom of Khotan.

Another version of this scripture states: If a person recites this dhāraṇī once, it is equivalent to reciting the *Avataṃsaka Sūtra* in all three recensions; upper, middle, and lower; as preserved in the Dragon Canon, one full time each. How much more so for one who upholds it continuously. If a person is able to complete one hundred thousand recitations, that person will become keen and perceptive, subtle in understanding, penetrating all the scriptural treasuries. As for the accumulation of merit and the transformation of obstacles, these cannot be fully enumerated.

Transmitted by the Elder Mahāyāna at Gaogong Temple in Shangyuan County.

---

**Translator's note:** The dhāraṇī is rendered here in phonetic romanization based on the Chinese transcription. The text attributes both the composition of the dhāraṇī to Samantabhadra Tathāgata and its Chinese translation to Śikṣānanda (652–710 CE), the Khotanese monk who produced the eighty-fascicle recension of the *Avataṃsaka Sūtra* (T0279) under Empress Wu Zetian. The closing attribution to the Elder Mahāyāna at Gaogong Temple in Shangyuan County (near present-day Nanjing) appears to be a later colophon added by a copyist or transmitter.
