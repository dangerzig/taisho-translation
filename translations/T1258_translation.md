# 摩利支菩薩略念誦法
## Abbreviated Recitation Method for Bodhisattva Māricī

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1258

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空)

---

First, one should form the mudrā of the Buddha Vairocana.

For this mudrā configuration: interlace both hands inward, and raise both thumbs upright together; this completes [the mudrā]. Recite the mantra seven times, then release [the mudrā] above the crown of the head. The mantra is:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ a vi ra hūṃ khaṃ*

Next, form the root mantra mudrā of Bodhisattva Māricī.

For this mudrā configuration: interlace both hands inward, raise and join both index fingers upright, have each middle finger curve around and grip the respective index finger so they press firmly together, then raise both thumbs upright together; this completes [the mudrā]. Recite the mantra to consecrate the five places of the body: touch the forehead, then the right shoulder, then the left shoulder, then the heart, then the throat, reciting once at each place. The mantra is:

*Namo ratna-trayāya tadyathā arka-marīci marīci adū-marīci citra-marīci mahā-citra-marīci antardhāna-marīci māricī-marīci namo 'stu te rakṣa rakṣa māṃ sarva-sattvānāṃ ca sarvatra sarva-pāyu-padravebhyaḥ svāhā*

Next, form the heart mudrā of Bodhisattva Māricī. The mantra is:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ oṃ māricye svāhā*

For the mudrā configuration for the heart mantra: form a hollow fist with the left hand, pressing the index finger and thumb together to form a ring. Visualize one's own body entering into the hollow of the left fist. Cover the left fist with the right hand. Then visualize this mudrā as the form of Bodhisattva Māricī, and continuously visualize one's own body dwelling within the heart of Bodhisattva Māricī without interruption. Recite the bodily mantra.

One will immediately obtain supreme consecration. One will not be seen by any evil persons or obstructors. All disasters and calamities will not attach to one's body. All slander and verbal disputes will be eliminated. Insects, wolves, tigers, leopards, water, fire, thieves, and bandits will all be unable to cause harm. All worldly fruits and rewards one seeks will be attained.

If one practices the supramundane Dharma, dhyāna-samādhi will immediately manifest. Wisdom will increase and one will attain śrutadhāraṇa; one will not forget or lose the Dharma of bodhicitta. When reciting the mantra, deeply arouse compassion to relieve all sentient beings from suffering. All will attain unobstructed liberation and swiftly realize the unsurpassed path of bodhi.

This must be kept secret; do not transmit it indiscriminately.
