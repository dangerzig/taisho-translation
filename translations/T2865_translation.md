# 護身命經
## Sūtra on Protecting Body and Life

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 2865

Translated from the Chinese. Author unknown.

---

*The Buddha Speaks the Sūtra on Rescuing and Protecting Body and Life, and Saving People from the Suffering of Illness and Calamity*

At that time, the Buddha was between the twin śāla trees, at the time he was about to enter parinirvāṇa. Śāriputra, Ānanda, and immeasurable, boundless great bodhisattvas, great disciples, and all the devas had come to assemble. The Buddha sat upright and told Ānanda: "I am about to enter parinirvāṇa. After my extinction, in the evil world of the five turbidities, all sentient beings will be harassed and disturbed by evil māras and evil spirits: those that suck people's vital essence, those that seek people's weaknesses, those that come to kill people unjustly. Ānanda, you must diligently circulate this sūtra so that all sentient beings in the evil world will be free from illness and suffering, free from unjust death, and so that all evil sorcery and poisons will be utterly eliminated."

"Ānanda, what I entrust and commission is only this sūtra. If any sentient being, whether man or woman, can read and recite even a single phrase or verse of this sūtra, all evil spirits cannot approach them. Whether in the wilderness, or in urgent danger, or in a great fire, or in a great flood, one should constantly read and recite this sūtra. It can completely sever all such calamities. Why? Because this sūtra possesses great spiritual power; therefore constantly read it. If one cannot read it, one should merely copy it and keep it against the breast, receiving and upholding it with utmost mind. This sūtra carries the spiritual power of all buddhas of past, future, and present. When one wishes to travel far, one should always carry it along. No evil venomous beast can approach. If one arrives at any village or settlement, one should single-mindedly expound it for people. Those who are able and willing to listen will have all their wishes fulfilled."

"Ānanda, the Buddha does not speak falsely. This sūtra is the Buddha's secret essential teaching, exceedingly difficult to obtain. It is like a wondrous medicine that can cure poisonous illness, can ward off poisonous vapors, and can sever evil poisons. If a person carries it while traveling, all evil venomous creatures and all sorcerous poisons that would come to harm them, upon smelling the fragrance of this medicine, scatter in the four directions and dare not look back. This sūtra is likewise. If there is one who is ill, they should wash and bathe in purity, then single-mindedly read and recite it, and all afflictions will be eliminated."

The Buddha told Ānanda: "If any evil māra or sorcerous poison does not follow my words, I shall cause that māra to encounter all manner of sorcerous poison, and like pressing oil, all evil will be utterly eliminated with nothing remaining."

The Buddha then pronounced the names of the Seven Buddhas. The first is Vipaśyin Buddha. The second is Śikhin Buddha. The third is Viśvabhū Buddha. The fourth is Krakucchanda Buddha. The fifth is Kanakamuni Buddha. The sixth is Kāśyapa Buddha. The seventh is Śākyamuni Buddha. If anyone who is in suffering, distress, or illness reads and recites the names of these Seven Buddhas, all evil sorcery and poisons will be utterly eliminated and cannot approach.

After the Buddha spoke this sūtra, he further told Ānanda: "Now, out of compassion for sentient beings, I shall further speak the names of six spirits. The first is named Panaila. The second is named Kanaila. The third is named Chandaka. The fourth is named Qinjia. The fifth is named Motou. The sixth is named Moqi. These are the names of the six spirits. Ānanda, if any sentient being, whether man or woman, whether noble or humble, is in suffering or distress, they should all recite the names of these six spirits. Their afflictions will be eliminated. All evil pestilent vapors cannot approach. All will be utterly extinguished with nothing remaining."

The Buddha told the immeasurable, boundless great bodhisattva-mahāsattvas, the deva-spirit kings, and all devas and humans: "After my extinction, if any person receives and upholds the Dharma I have entrusted, you should all constantly protect them day and night, enabling them to be at peace."

Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One, after the Buddha's extinction, I shall lead twenty-five bodhisattvas. In the evil world, wherever anyone reads and recites this sūtra, we shall be at their left and right, day and night, protecting that person. All evil spirits and phantoms cannot approach. We shall always ensure that person is at peace whether sleeping or awake, cultivating the wholesome Dharma."

The Buddha praised Mañjuśrī and the others: "Excellent, excellent! You are able to protect the aspiration for anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi that I have cultivated and accumulated throughout a hundred thousand ten-thousand koṭis of kalpas."

At that time, the Four Heavenly Kings bared their right shoulders, placed their right knees on the ground, and with palms joined said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One, after the Tathāgata's extinction, each of us shall constantly lead our retinues to patrol the borders of the kingdoms. Wherever anyone reads, recites, copies, and upholds this sūtra, our retinues will constantly follow that person, protecting them day and night, ensuring they see no evil. When that person wishes to travel through the wilderness, we shall constantly follow and escort them from the front. Diligently we shall protect them, not leaving that person, not allowing evil māras to approach. They will always be provided for, never suffering hunger or thirst, lacking nothing. Whatever they wish for, we spirit-kings shall fully supply. According to their wishes, nothing will be lacking. Why? Because that person circulates this sūtra, cultivates the wholesome Dharma, and makes offerings to the Three Treasures, and should not be allowed to cease."

At that time, the gandharva king, the asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, humans, non-humans, and others all knelt before the Tathāgata, and with palms joined said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One, we devas and humans shall constantly fly through the evil world. Wherever anyone reads, recites, copies, and upholds this sūtra, we devas and humans, together with our retinues, shall go to the place where that person dwells, listen to and receive this sūtra-dharma, and constantly guard and protect them day and night, not departing, surrounding that person on all four sides. No evil māras or ghosts can approach. They cannot steal that person's vital essence. They cannot unjustly take that person's life. They cannot unjustly harass or harm them. They cannot seek their weaknesses. They cannot transgress against them. They shall cause poisonous vapors not to spread."

"Our retinues shall constantly come to the place where that person dwells. We shall be in the sky. If that person encounters a great fire, our retinues, by whatever means expedient, shall protect their body and not allow the fire to burn them. If they encounter a great flood with rapid, sweeping currents, our retinues shall come from the sky to receive that person, ensuring they are not drowned. They shall immediately be stopped and brought safely past the peril of water. If they encounter great bandits, we shall protect that person from all four sides, causing the bandits' hearts to turn, their swords and weapons to remain unraised, and giving rise to compassion. If they encounter imprisonment and shackles under the law, suffering day and night, our retinues in the sky shall cause the officials' hearts to become joyful and order their release, so all will be liberated. Our retinues shall single-mindedly rescue and protect them, not allowing others to harass and disturb them. Throughout immeasurable, boundless kalpas, we shall constantly be mindful of this sūtra. Why? Because this is the Dharma that the World-Honored One has earnestly entrusted, to circulate for a long time."

The Buddha further praised the devas, humans, and others: "Excellent, excellent! Your retinues, throughout asaṃkhyeya kalpas, have encountered a hundred thousand ten-thousand koṭis of buddhas. You are further able to protect my disciples throughout a hundred thousand ten-thousand koṭis of kalpas, to circulate this sūtra, to read and recite and copy it, to rescue and save through skillful means, to ensure they see no evil, and to constantly practice the wholesome mind."

At that time, the gandharva king and the others, together with their retinues, bowed their heads to the Buddha's feet and single-mindedly carried out his instructions.

The Buddha told Ānanda: "I place my right hand upon the crown of your head. Apply your mind well. What I entrust and commission is only this sūtra. Ānanda, diligently circulate this sūtra so that all sentient beings may hear and know of it. Ānanda, you are foremost among my heart-disciples. All the Dharma I have spoken I entrust to you and the others. Because I now have compassion for all sentient beings and wish to enable their liberation..." The Buddha then sat upright and entrusted this Dharma.

"Ānanda, this sūtra is majestic and powerful, possessing extreme spiritual power. Urge all good men and women to make offerings with incense, flowers, colored cloth, and lamps kept burning. Moreover, if one can circulate and recite it, rescuing people from the suffering of illness and calamity, in this present life they will have peace and auspiciousness. In the life to come, they will be born in the land of Amitāyus, born upon a lotus, with a golden body, marks complete, wisdom and courage, like those of the highest grade. Such merit is incalculable."

The Buddha told Ānanda: "You should use fine paper, fine brushes, and fine ink. With utmost mind, copy the Dharma I have spoken, each phrase and verse exactly as the Buddha taught, not losing a single stroke or dot. Ānanda, because I have compassion for sentient beings, I entrust this Dharma to you alone. May all beings with form hear and know of it, their minds opened and understanding clear, constantly practicing the wholesome Dharma."

At that time, Ānanda, before the World-Honored One, with palms joined, every hair on his body standing on end, trembling and shaking, single-mindedly listened to the Buddha's words, not daring to lose a single phrase or verse. With flowing tears he said: "The World-Honored One's entrustment I shall receive and uphold with utmost mind, broadly circulating this sūtra."

Ānanda further said: "I receive the Heavenly Honored One's teaching." He bowed his head to the Buddha's feet and single-mindedly carried out the instructions.

> Cultivating merit, one receives the reward of happiness;
> All one desires comes about naturally.
> Transcending the stream of birth and death,
> One ascends to the words of nirvāṇa.
>
> If a person delights in performing merit,
> The heavenly spirits naturally protect them.
> All wishes come about naturally;
> The hosts of māras cannot destroy them.
>
> With scant merit, many afflictions abound;
> Merit can eliminate all calamities.
> When merit and virtue are firmly established,
> One swiftly attains firm and solid samādhi.
>
> Born in heaven, one receives joy and happiness;
> Among humans, one is also at ease.
> This is all due to meritorious virtue;
> Wherever one goes, all comes about naturally.
>
> Through this merit as skillful means,
> One forever departs from the suffering of birth and death.
> Attaining the Way, one reaches nirvāṇa:
> Neither arising nor ceasing.
