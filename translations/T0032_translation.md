# 四諦經
## Sūtra on the Four Noble Truths

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0032

Translated from the Chinese. Later Han dynasty, translated by An Shigao (安世高)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the country of Śrāvastī, in the Jetavana, the garden of Anāthapiṇḍada. At that time, the Buddha addressed the bhikṣus: "Bhikṣus!" They responded: "Yes, we are here." The bhikṣus then listened to the Buddha. The Buddha spoke thus: "Bhikṣus! The true and correct teaching of the Dharma consists of these Four Noble Truths, fully contemplated and seen, understood, distinguished, and revealed. Whatever bhikṣus of past ages, from the Tathāgata, the Worthy One, Perfectly Awakened: it was through this correct teaching, these Four Noble Truths, fully contemplated and seen, understood, distinguished, and revealed. From these Four Noble Truths, whatever bhikṣus of future ages, from the Tathāgata, the Worthy One, Perfectly Awakened: it is through this correct Dharma, truly these Four Noble Truths of the worthy ones, fully as stated above. Now the bhikṣus of the present, from the Tathāgata, the Worthy One, Perfectly Awakened, also through this correct truth, teach these Four Noble Truths, fully contemplated and seen, understood, distinguished, and revealed."

The Buddha further addressed the bhikṣu Śāriputra: "Bhikṣu! You possess wisdom, quick wisdom, swift wisdom, sharp wisdom, skillful wisdom, profoundly penetrating wisdom, aversion-overcoming wisdom, tireless seeing wisdom, with rare treasures following. The bhikṣu Śāriputra is able; the bhikṣu Śāriputra is capable. This worthy one can thoroughly teach the Four Noble Truths with balanced explanation. The bhikṣu Śāriputra is an extraordinary person who can fully explain through contemplation, able to see, able to open understanding, able to comprehend, able to distinguish, able to reveal and see. Now in many ways he follows the Dharma of the path. This bhikṣu Śāriputra is unsurpassed, able to turn others back from wrong paths. The bhikṣu Śāriputra can lead one to follow the path; the bhikṣu Maudgalyāyana can lead one to completion of the path. The bhikṣu Śāriputra is like a mother who gives birth; the bhikṣu Maudgalyāyana is like a mother who nurtures. One should regard the bhikṣu Maudgalyāyana as thus awakened. Śāriputra and Maudgalyāyana should be served, should be honored, should be visited and consulted. The bhikṣu Śāriputra and the bhikṣu Maudgalyāyana, being fellow practitioners, bring joy and mindfulness, so that there is nothing beyond this."

The Buddha, having spoken thus, rose from his seat and entered his chamber to reflect in contemplation. Then the venerable bhikṣu Śāriputra, for the benefit of the assembly and because the Buddha was present in the world, now taught these Four Noble Truths: "What are the four? First is suffering, second is origination, third is cessation, fourth is the path. The Four Noble Truths, when practiced, bring about the cessation of suffering.

"What, noble ones, is the noble truth of suffering? From birth there is suffering, from aging there is suffering, illness is suffering, death is suffering; encountering what one does not love is suffering, separation from what one loves is suffering, not obtaining what one seeks is also suffering; in brief, the five aggregates of clinging produce suffering.

"Noble ones, what is the suffering of birth? Whatever person of whatever kind, from birth onward increasing, through following and having, through desire coming into existence, the five aggregates already arisen, the life faculty already obtained: this is called birth. Birth, noble ones, is suffering. Why is birth suffering? Because for those who are born, the body experiences further suffering, contact upon contact, pain upon pain, causing the mind further suffering. Contact upon contact, feeling upon feeling, causing body and mind further suffering. Contact upon contact, knowing and feeling again and again, causing the body to undergo affliction. Contact upon contact, sensing and feeling again and again, the mind burning with distress. Contact upon contact, knowing and feeling again and again, causing body and mind to burn with distress. Contact upon contact, from suffering upon suffering, the body burns with weary distress. Contact upon contact, feeling upon feeling, the mind burns with weary sorrow and distress. Contact upon contact, feeling upon feeling, causing body and mind to burn with weariness and distress. From mindfulness burning with distress, contact upon contact, feeling upon feeling: birth, noble ones, is suffering. As stated above regarding suffering, therefore it is said that from this there is aging.

"Noble ones, what is the suffering of aging? As all people grow weary and exhausted, such is aging: wrinkled, white-haired, strength trembling with age, stooped and leaning on a staff, hair falling out, dark spots appearing and features changing, faculties already ripened, body about to decay, complexion already altered, aging having reached the end of life. This is called aging. Aging, noble ones, is suffering. Why is aging said to be suffering? When a person grows old, the body experiences further suffering, contact upon contact, practicing and feeling again and again, the mind further suffering. Contact upon contact, acting and feeling again and again, body and mind also suffering. Contact upon contact, practicing and feeling again and again, the body burning with distress. Contact upon contact, feeling upon feeling, the mind burning with distress. Contact upon contact, feeling upon feeling, body and mind burning with distress. Contact upon contact, feeling upon feeling, the body burning with weary sorrow and distress. Contact upon contact, feeling upon feeling, the mind burning with weary distress and sorrow. Contact upon contact, feeling upon feeling, body and mind burning with weary sorrow and distress. Contact upon contact, feeling upon feeling. Therefore, noble ones, aging is said to be suffering. As stated above regarding suffering, therefore it is said.

"Illness, noble ones, is suffering. What is illness? There are headaches, stomach ailments, ear diseases, nose diseases, mouth diseases, lip diseases, tongue diseases, throat diseases, retching, bowel disorders, lower body ailments, fevers, urinary diseases, madness, goiter, recurring diseases, bone and joint diseases, skin diseases, diseases of the fat, blood-heat diseases, and phlegm diseases. There are also various others, all arising from dependency, unable to be separated from, all attached to the body. Illness, noble ones, is suffering. Why is illness suffering? When a person is afflicted, the body experiences further suffering, contact upon contact, feeling upon feeling, the mind suffering. Contact upon contact, feeling upon feeling, both body and mind suffering. Contact upon contact, feeling upon feeling, the body burning with distress. Contact upon contact, feeling upon feeling, the mind burning with distress. Contact upon contact, feeling upon feeling, body and mind burning with distress. Contact upon contact, feeling upon feeling, the body burning with weary sorrow and distress. Contact upon contact, feeling upon feeling, the mind burning with weary sorrow and distress. Contact upon contact, feeling upon feeling, body and mind burning with weary sorrow and distress. Contact upon contact, feeling upon feeling. What is said about illness, noble ones, is suffering. Therefore it is said, and from these conditions it arises.

"Death, noble ones, is suffering. What is death? Whatever person exists, whatever person exists within birth and death, everywhere the body is abandoned, broken down, destroyed, and seen no more; the life is already ended, the five aggregates already relinquished, the life faculty already extinguished at the time of death: this is called death. Death, noble ones, is suffering. Why is death suffering? For the dying person, the body experiences further suffering, contact upon contact, feeling upon feeling, the mind further suffering. Contact upon contact, feeling upon feeling, body and mind further suffering. Contact upon contact, feeling upon feeling, causing the body to burn with distress. Contact upon contact, feeling upon feeling, causing the mind to burn with distress. Contact upon contact, feeling upon feeling, causing body and mind to burn with distress. Contact upon contact, feeling upon feeling, the body burning with weary remorse and distress. Contact upon contact, feeling upon feeling, causing body and mind to burn with weary remorse and distress. Contact upon contact, feeling upon feeling. Death, noble ones, is suffering. For these reasons it is said, and from these conditions it is taught.

"Encountering what one does not love, noble ones, is suffering. What is encountering what one does not love? Noble ones, a person's six internal sense bases, which are unloved and unwanted, come into contact with one another. When these hateful things converge, gather together, associate, and meet, this is suffering. So too with external sense bases, consciousness, thought, sensation, perception, attention, and craving, and the six elements likewise. Noble ones, there are for a person six kinds of elements that are unloved. What are the six? The earth element, water element, fire element, wind element, space element, and consciousness element. When these converge, gather, meet, and associate, this is suffering. Encountering what one does not love, noble ones, is suffering. Why is encountering what one does not love suffering? When unloved things meet and associate, noble ones, the person's body experiences further suffering, contact upon contact, feeling upon feeling, the mind further suffering. Contact upon contact, feeling upon feeling, body and mind further suffering. Contact upon contact, feeling upon feeling, body and mind burning with distress. Contact upon contact, feeling upon feeling, the body burning with weary, bitter distress. Contact upon contact, feeling upon feeling, the mind burning with weary, bitter distress. Contact upon contact, feeling upon feeling, body and mind burning with weary, bitter distress. Contact upon contact, feeling upon feeling. Encountering what one does not love, noble ones, is suffering. Therefore it is said, and from this it is taught.

"Separation from what one loves, noble ones, is suffering. What is separation from what one loves? Noble ones, a person's own sense bases that are loved: from these one is separated, lost, parted, divided, not together, not meeting, not dwelling together, not encountering, not in contact. This is suffering. So too with external sense bases, consciousness, contact, craving, sensation, attention, love, and the six elements likewise. Noble ones, a person has six beloved elements: earth, water, fire, wind, space, and consciousness. From these one is separated, lost, parted, divided, not meeting, far apart, not dwelling together, not encountering, not in contact. This is suffering. This separation from what one loves, noble ones, is suffering. Therefore it is said, and from these conditions it is taught.

"Not obtaining what one seeks is also suffering, and therefore it is further explained. Noble ones, for those in the world who are subject to the law of birth, when the thought arises, 'May I not be born,' this too cannot be avoided. For those subject to the law of aging, noble ones, when they similarly wish, 'May I not grow old,' this thought cannot be avoided. For those subject to the law of illness, noble ones, when they have already received illness and wish, 'May I not have this,' this suffering cannot be avoided. For those subject to the law of death, noble ones, when they must receive death and wish, 'May I not die,' this cannot be obtained or avoided. Noble ones, when a person experiences painful feelings that are unwanted, undesirable, and displeasing to the mind, they wish that these feelings would become wanted, desirable, and pleasing to the mind, but this cannot be obtained; from such desire comes only severing. Noble ones, when a person has thoughts and reflections that are unwanted, undesirable, and displeasing, they wish that these thoughts and reflections would become wanted, desirable, and pleasing, but this cannot be obtained; from this comes only severing. Noble ones, when a person has contacts that are wanted, desirable, and pleasing to the mind, should the thought arise that these wanted, desirable, and pleasing contacts should be permanent and never separated from, this wish will not be fulfilled. Should noble ones give rise to perceptions and attention with craving for what is wanted, desirable, and pleasing, wishing that these should be permanent, firm, and never separated from, this wish will not be fulfilled. Not obtaining what one seeks is also suffering; therefore it is said, and from these conditions it is taught.

"The suffering of the five aggregates of clinging is therefore further explained, and from this the teaching follows. These phenomena are impermanent, beset by affliction and illness, subject to decay, deterioration, aging, instability, and unreliability; they are subject to desire, change, and separation. Therefore the five aggregates of clinging are suffering. In past ages, noble ones, this too was the noble truth of suffering; in future ages, noble ones, this is also the noble truth of suffering; in the present age, noble ones, this is also the noble truth of suffering. This is without difference, neither inverted nor confused. As there is truth, as it truly is, as it accords with the noble truth, the noble truth. Noble ones, this truth is known, seen, understood, obtained, and accorded with; such is the truth and awakening. Therefore it is called the noble truth.

"What, noble ones, is the noble truth of the origination of suffering? Noble ones, when a person, through the six internal sense bases of the body, develops craving, and whatever is loved and clung to, approached and pursued: this is origination. Just as with the internal body, so too with the external body, consciousness, contact, knowledge, practice, and attachment. Noble ones, there are for a person six elements toward which craving arises: earth, water, fire, wind, space, and consciousness. Whatever is loved and clung to, approached and pursued and developed: this is origination. And how should this be understood? When a person has children, a spouse, servants, attendants, fields, houses, places of rest and bedding, they find ease in craving, clinging, approaching, contacting, pursuing, and seeking. One should know that this craving, this origination, is the noble truth of the origination of suffering. In past ages, noble ones, this craving and origination was also the noble truth of the origination of suffering; in future ages, this craving and origination is also the noble truth of the origination of suffering; in the present age, this craving and origination is also the noble truth of the origination of suffering. Thus without difference, as it truly is, neither inverted nor confused, true truth, rightly so. Noble ones, this truth is the noble truth, further seen, understood, obtained, accorded with, as there is awakening. Therefore the origination of suffering is called the noble truth.

"What, noble ones, is the noble truth of the cessation of suffering? Noble ones, when a person, within the six internal sense bases of the body, does not accept, obtains liberation from them, is no longer in contact with them, has cut them off, abandoned them, separated from them, exhausted them, no longer yearns for them, has extinguished them, and is at peace: this is the cessation of suffering. Just as with the internal body, so too with the external, consciousness, proximity, contact, perception, attention, practice, yearning, and craving. Noble ones, when a person has no craving for the six elements of earth, water, fire, wind, space, and consciousness, obtains liberation from them, is no longer in contact with them, has cut them off, abandoned them, discarded them, differentiated from them, has no use for them, and is at peace: this is the cessation of suffering. And what does this mean? When a person has no craving or clinging regarding children, family, servants, attendants, fields, houses, dwellings, places of rest and bedding, buying and selling and profit, has no craving or clinging, does not approach, and has no arising of pursuit or seeking regarding these: one should know that this cessation of craving is the noble truth of the cessation of suffering. In past ages, noble ones, this cessation of craving was the noble truth of the cessation of suffering; in future ages likewise; in the present age, this cessation of craving is also the noble truth of the cessation of suffering. Thus without difference, as it truly is, not confused, not inverted, true truth, this is as it is. Therefore, suffering having been exhausted, this is called the noble truth.

"What, noble ones, is the noble truth of the path leading to the cessation of suffering? Noble ones, there is the Eightfold Path: first, right view; second, right intention; third, right speech; fourth, right action; fifth, right livelihood; sixth, right effort; seventh, right mindfulness; eighth, right concentration.

"What, noble ones, is right view? Noble ones, when a disciple of the noble path contemplates suffering as suffering, origination as origination, cessation as cessation, the path as the path, able to distinguish and observe, able to attain the observation of the Dharma, able to receive and reflect, able to contemplate and consider, able to accept and practice: this is called right view. Also observing past karma, also mindful of the noble path, early practice and seeing, practice of remorse accepted and ceased, unconditioned, mindful, quiescent, ceased and detached, as one obtains liberation, the mind distinguishes, contemplation and practice accord, the mind dwells in the Dharma, contemplating characteristics without departing from their meeting and acceptance: this is called right view, this is called the noble truth of the path.

"What, noble ones, is right intention? Noble ones, when a disciple of the noble path contemplates suffering as suffering, origination as origination, cessation as cessation, the path as the path, whatever practice of dedication, mindfulness upon mindfulness: this is called right intention. Also observing past lives, also that which is studied, practice and accord with mindfulness, from practice contemplating remorse, unconditioned, quiescent acceptance, ceased and detached, obtaining the mind of liberation, contemplation and cessation, what is sought and dedicated to, mindfulness practiced in accord: this is called right intention, this is called the noble truth of the path.

"What, noble ones, is right speech? Noble ones, disciples of the noble path! Contemplating suffering as suffering, origination as origination, cessation as cessation, the path as the path: ceasing the four verbal transgressions and whatever remaining evil speech, from these one obtains cessation and separation. Ceasing, separating, guarding, restraining, not committing, no guilt incurred from actions, free from guilt, having ceased: this is right speech. Also maintaining observation of past karma, having entered practice and mindful of the path, from practice repentance and cessation of mind, unconditioned, transcending the world, quiescent, pleasing to the mind, ceased, without clinging, as one obtains the mind of liberation, distinguishing and contemplating: removing the four evil verbal actions, separating, ceasing, withdrawing, guarding, restraining, not committing, no guilt incurred from actions, free from guilt, having ceased: this is called right speech, this is called the noble truth of the path.

"What, noble ones, is right action? Noble ones, when a disciple of the noble path contemplates suffering as suffering, origination as origination, cessation as cessation, the path as the path: removing the three bodily evil actions and whatever remaining bodily evil actions, from these ceasing, separating, guarding, restraining, not committing, no guilt incurred from actions, free from guilt, having ceased: this is called right action. Also maintaining observation of past karma, also from the noble path of practice, mindful of worldly conduct and seeing remorse, ceasing, unconditioned, transcending the world, seeing quiescence and cessation, from the unconditioned transcending the world, without clinging, as one obtains the mind of liberation through contemplation: removing the three bodily evil actions and removing bodily evil actions, separating, ceasing, withdrawing, guarding, restraining, not committing, no guilt incurred from actions, guilt having ceased: this is called right action, this is called the noble truth of the path.

"What, noble ones, is right livelihood? Noble ones, when a disciple of the noble path contemplates suffering as suffering, origination as origination, cessation as cessation, the path as the path: what should not be sought one does not seek, what should not be practiced one does not practice, the various animal-like trades and wrong pursuits for self-sustenance: this is called wrong livelihood. Also maintaining observation of past karma, from practice obtaining the path, mindful of worldly conduct and contemplating remorse, ceasing, transcending the world, unconditioned contemplation, quiescent, ceased and transcending the world, without clinging, as one obtains the mind of liberation through contemplation: what should not be sought one does not seek, the various animal-like trades for self-sustenance, separating, ceasing, withdrawing, guarding, restraining, not committing, no guilt incurred from actions, guilt having ceased: this is called right livelihood, this is called the noble truth of the path.

"What, noble ones, is right effort? Noble ones, when a disciple of the noble path contemplates suffering as suffering, origination as origination, cessation as cessation, the path as the path: whatever energy one exerts, whatever skillful means, whatever emerging, whatever abiding in cessation, whatever capability, whatever spreading of virtue, whatever rejoicing, not destroying or extinguishing, mindfully and rightly ceasing: this is called right effort. Also maintaining observation of past karma, also from obtaining practice and mindfulness, from worldly conduct seeing remorse, ceasing, transcending the world, unconditioned, quiescent, ceased and detached, having obtained the contemplation of the path and the mind of liberation: whatever energy, whatever skillful means, whatever emerging, whatever abiding in cessation, whatever spreading of virtue, whatever rejoicing, mindfully gathering and ceasing without destroying or extinguishing: this is called right effort, this is called the noble truth of the path.

"What, noble ones, is right mindfulness? Noble ones, when a disciple of the noble path contemplates suffering as suffering, origination as origination, cessation as cessation, the path as the path, being mindful and following mindfulness, mindfulness upon mindfulness not forgotten, speaking little, mindfulness not departing: this is called right mindfulness. Also observing the maintenance of past karma, also practicing in accordance with the obtained path, mindfulness of worldly conduct beyond repentance, gathering, ceasing, transcending the world, unconditioned, quiescent, seeing cessation, unified virtue, without clinging, as the mind of liberation contemplates mindfully, following mindfulness upon mindfulness, mindfulness not forgotten, speaking little, mindfulness not departing: this is called right mindfulness, this is called the noble truth of the path.

"What, noble ones, is right concentration? Noble ones, when a disciple of the noble path contemplates suffering as suffering, origination as origination, cessation as cessation, the path as the path, the mind resting and not moving, not running astray, having gathered and ceased, mind and mindfulness dwelling in oneness: this is called right concentration. Also observing the maintenance of past karma, also being mindful through the mind obtaining liberation, seeing repentance in worldly conduct, gathering, ceasing, transcending the world, unconditioned, seeing what is acceptable as it truly is, obtaining non-clinging, from the conditions of liberation the mind directed toward contemplation, the mind at rest, rightly settled in oneness, not confused, not running astray, gathered and at rest, mindfulness and concentration abiding in twofold mindfulness: this is called right concentration, this is called the noble truth of the path.

"In past ages, noble ones, this too was the noble truth of practicing the cessation of suffering; in future ages yet to come, this too is the noble truth of practicing the path; in the present age, this too is the noble truth of practicing the cessation of suffering. Thus without difference, as it truly is, without loss, without confusion, true truth, as it originally is, possessing the virtue of the noble truth of the path. Noble ones, this truth, further seeing, obtaining, and according with liberation: therefore it is the cessation of suffering. Practicing in accordance with this is called the noble truth of the path. In summary: suffering, the origination of suffering, cessation, and seeing the path: the practice proclaimed by the Buddha is immeasurable."

Thus Śāriputra taught, and the bhikṣus received and practiced it.

Maintaining the contemplation of past karma.
