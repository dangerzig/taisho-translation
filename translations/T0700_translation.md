# 右繞佛塔功德經
## Sūtra of the Merit of Circumambulating a Buddha Stūpa Clockwise

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0700

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Śikṣānanda (實叉難陀)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was in the land of Śrāvastī, at the Jeta Grove of Anāthapiṇḍada, together with a great assembly of bhikṣus and an innumerable multitude, surrounding him in attendance.

At that time, the Elder Śāriputra rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, placed his right knee on the ground, joined his palms toward the Buddha, and made his request in verse:

> World-Honored One of great authority and virtue,
> I wish you to tell us:
> When one circumambulates a Buddha stūpa clockwise,
> What rewards does one obtain?

At that time the World-Honored One answered in verse:

> The merits obtained
> By circumambulating a Buddha stūpa clockwise:
> I shall now speak of a small portion;
> All of you, listen well.
> All the devas, nāgas,
> Yakṣas, ghosts, and spirits
> Will draw near and make offerings:
> This is due to circumambulating the stūpa clockwise.
> In every place where one is born,
> One is far from the eight difficulties,
> Always born where there is no hardship:
> This is due to circumambulating the stūpa clockwise.
> In every life,
> Mindfulness and wisdom are never lost;
> One is endowed with wondrous marks and features:
> This is due to circumambulating the stūpa clockwise.
> Coming and going among devas and humans,
> One's merit and lifespan are long and far-reaching;
> One always obtains great renown:
> This is due to circumambulating the stūpa clockwise.
> In Jambudvīpa,
> One is always born among the most honored,
> In families of pure lineage:
> This is due to circumambulating the stūpa clockwise.
> One's bearing is always upright and handsome;
> One is wealthy with many treasures
> And always enjoys great estates:
> This is due to circumambulating the stūpa clockwise.
> Wealth and treasure are always abundant,
> Yet one has no miserly heart;
> Courageous and broadly generous:
> This is due to circumambulating the stūpa clockwise.
> One's form is pure, subtle, and wondrous;
> All who see one admire and look up;
> Wherever one dwells, one is always at peace:
> This is due to circumambulating the stūpa clockwise.
> One may become the King of the Trāyastriṃśa,
> With wife and children all complete,
> With power and might, sovereign and free:
> This is due to circumambulating the stūpa clockwise.
> One may become a brahmin,
> Keeping the precepts and mastering all,
> Including mantras and the Vedas:
> This is due to circumambulating the stūpa clockwise.
> One may become a great elder,
> Noble and wealthy with many possessions,
> With granaries always full and abundant:
> This is due to circumambulating the stūpa clockwise.
> One may become a righteous king,
> Sovereign ruler of Jambudvīpa,
> With all the land submitting to one's rule:
> This is due to circumambulating the stūpa clockwise.
> One may become a wheel-turning king,
> Endowed with the seven treasures and great power,
> Governing all beings through the ten wholesome deeds:
> This is due to circumambulating the stūpa clockwise.
> From here one ascends to the heavens,
> Always possessing great authority and virtue,
> With pure faith in the Buddha-Dharma:
> This is due to circumambulating the stūpa clockwise.
> When pure faith is swiftly accomplished,
> One has no confusion about the Dharma
> And sees that all conditioned things are empty:
> This is due to circumambulating the stūpa clockwise.
> Having left the heavens at life's end,
> One is born again among humans;
> Entering the womb without confusion:
> This is due to circumambulating the stūpa clockwise.
> While in the mother's womb,
> One is unstained by filth and impurity,
> Like a pure maṇi gem:
> This is due to circumambulating the stūpa clockwise.
> In the womb and at the time of birth,
> The mother is always at ease and happy;
> So too when nursing:
> This is due to circumambulating the stūpa clockwise.
> Parents and relatives
> All join in nurturing;
> The nurse never leaves one's side:
> This is due to circumambulating the stūpa clockwise.
> Kinfolk all love and cherish one,
> Surpassing even father and mother;
> Wealth naturally increases:
> This is due to circumambulating the stūpa clockwise.
> Yakṣas and evil ghosts
> Cannot startle or frighten one for a moment;
> All one needs comes naturally:
> This is due to circumambulating the stūpa clockwise.
> Through a hundred thousand kalpas,
> One's body grows ever more pure;
> Wondrous marks and features become complete:
> This is due to circumambulating the stūpa clockwise.
> One's pure eyes are long and wide,
> Like the blue lotus flower;
> One also obtains the pure divine eye:
> This is due to circumambulating the stūpa clockwise.
> Wondrous marks are always complete;
> All features naturally adorn one;
> One achieves great power:
> This is due to circumambulating the stūpa clockwise.
> One may be born in the palace of Śakra,
> With great authority and sovereign power,
> Honored among the Trāyastriṃśa devas:
> This is due to circumambulating the stūpa clockwise.
> One may be born in the Yāma,
> Tuṣita, Nirmāṇarati,
> Or Paranirmitavaśavartin heavens:
> This is due to circumambulating the stūpa clockwise.
> Or one may be born in the Brahmā heaven,
> The most sovereign in the Brahmā world,
> Honored by all the devas:
> This is due to circumambulating the stūpa clockwise.
> For billions of nayutas of kalpas,
> One is always honored and served
> By the wise with reverence:
> This is due to circumambulating the stūpa clockwise.
> One's body and garments
> Are spotless for billions of kalpas,
> Fully endowed with pure, white dharmas:
> This is due to circumambulating the stūpa clockwise.
> Endowed with great vigor,
> One diligently cultivates every kind of practice,
> Never wearying or slackening:
> This is due to circumambulating the stūpa clockwise.
> Courageous and constantly diligent,
> Firm and indestructible;
> All one undertakes is swiftly accomplished:
> This is due to circumambulating the stūpa clockwise.
> With a deep, far-reaching, subtle voice,
> All who hear it rejoice;
> One is always at peace and free from illness:
> This is due to circumambulating the stūpa clockwise.
> As I have taught,
> Weary of and abandoning the suffering of the three realms,
> One accomplishes the wisdom of transcendence:
> This is due to circumambulating the stūpa clockwise.
> One always abides in the four foundations of mindfulness,
> The four right efforts,
> And the four bases of spiritual power:
> This is due to circumambulating the stūpa clockwise.
> One thoroughly comprehends the Four Noble Truths,
> The faculties, powers, and seven factors of awakening,
> The noble path and the holy fruits:
> This is due to circumambulating the stūpa clockwise.
> One extinguishes all afflictions,
> Is endowed with great authority and virtue,
> And the six outflow-free supernatural powers:
> This is due to circumambulating the stūpa clockwise.
> Forever free from greed, anger, and delusion,
> And all obstructions,
> One realizes the awakening of a pratyekabuddha:
> This is due to circumambulating the stūpa clockwise.
> Obtaining the wondrous purple-gold color,
> A body adorned with marks and features,
> One appears as the teacher of devas and humans:
> This is due to circumambulating the stūpa clockwise.
> All this is due to bodily deeds
> And to praise with the speech;
> By circumambulating the Buddha stūpa clockwise,
> One obtains these great benefits.
> The merits obtained
> By circumambulating the stūpas of all the Buddhas:
> What I have now spoken from what I have heard
> Is but a brief account; how could it be exhaustive?

When the World-Honored One had spoken these verses, Śāriputra and the entire assembly were all filled with great joy, faithfully received the teaching, and practiced it.
