# 佛說帝釋巖祕密成就儀軌
## Ritual Manual for the Secret Accomplishments of the Indra Cave, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0940

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dānapāla (施護)

---

At that time, Śākyamuni Buddha addressed the Great Secret Lord Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva: "You should now know that in the land of Magadha, to the north of the Āmra settlement, on Mount Vaidehī, there is the Cave of Indra. Within that cave there are ninety-nine palaces. A koṭi of great bodhisattvas, along with devas, nāgas, the eight divisions, and various immortals dwell therein. Maitreya Bodhisattva is presently there, having entered a samādhi called Supreme Adornment. Having emerged from this samādhi, he teaches the Dharma to the assembly of bodhisattvas.

"Vajrapāṇi! This Cave of Indra is the most supreme and excellent. It is a display of the Buddhas' spiritual transformations, beyond the comprehension of those of small wisdom. It is the Dharma-gate of inconceivable correspondence. The great bodhisattvas dwell within it, and there are various kinds of accomplished sacred methods of correspondence. If there are any worldly beings who generate a resolute mind to seek bodhi, who through innumerable kalpas diligently practice the multitude of deeds without fearing the length of the time, only for them is this Dharma of the accomplishments of the Indra Cave taught. Furthermore, if there is a person who has abandoned desire, anger, and delusion, and the marks of self-view, who has tamed the sense-faculties, generated a mind of pure faith, and follows the Buddha's right teaching: only for such a one is it taught. But if there is a person who creates evil karma, who has not planted the seeds of bodhi, who is far from the right Dharma, who does not believe in cause and effect, who slanders the ācārya, or who is unfilial to parents: do not teach it to them."

At that time, the Great Secret Lord Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! As the Buddha has taught, there is great merit, and this is a great skillful means. Without special exertion, in this very life one can see Maitreya Bodhisattva. I now wish to hear it. May the Buddha teach it."

At that time, the Buddha addressed the Great Secret Lord Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva: "I will now truly teach it for you. Vajrapāṇi! If there are practitioners of the corresponding practice who wish to enter the Cave of Indra without special exertion, who in this very life seek to see Maitreya Bodhisattva, or who wish to obtain the sacred sword, sacred wheel, cintāmaṇi jewel, the Dharma texts, sacred medicine, añjana medicine, *pānuka*, sacred adornments, or such accomplished methods: the practitioner must first, at the ācārya's place, generate a mind of faith and respect, receive transmission according to the Dharma, and then seek the empowerment and protection of their own principal deity. Having made extensive offerings to all the Buddhas and Tathāgatas, and having made great gifts universally for all sentient beings, the practitioner should face east and walk the distance of ten bow-lengths. Then face west and walk thirty bow-lengths. They will then enter a palace. Its form is square with windows. The practitioner will immediately see, through the windows, various trees, all of which are sacred medicines.

"They will also see the palace of the terrestrial world. The practitioner should rest there briefly. Not far away, they will see a well. The well is about half the depth of a person's body. There is an iron chain that one may grasp as a ladder. The practitioner should firmly hold this chain and descend the distance of two cubits, reaching level ground. The practitioner should grope along the ground with their hands, and gradually they will see a pathway. The practitioner should walk along this pathway downward. Next, they will see an *āmra* tree. The tree bears fruit shaped like a *vīṇā*. The practitioner should take one fruit, carry it outside, and eat it. Upon eating it, they will attain a life-span of one kalpa, like Nārāyaṇa-deva, with beautiful marks, a handsome form, and great power. They will obtain the power of complete retention and will fully comprehend all the Buddhas' Dharma.

"If the practitioner does not take this fruit, they should walk another hundred bow-lengths, and they will see the palace of Maheśvara-deva. Before that palace is a great pool, filled with sacred medicines. To the north of this pool is a great tāla tree. The practitioner should take one fruit from that tree, carry it outside, and eat it; they will attain a life-span of one kalpa. If the practitioner does not wish to take this fruit, they should take one sacred medicine from the pool and carry it outside; whatever it touches will turn to gold. If the practitioner also does not wish to take this sacred medicine, they should take one myrobalan fruit from the four sides of that celestial palace, carry it outside, and eat it; they will also attain a life-span of one kalpa.

"Then, leaving that celestial palace, to the south and north they will see two paths. The southern path must not be taken; one should take the northern path. Not far from there, they will see a copper city, tall and vast. The practitioner should knock at the city gate. A *māṭaṅgī* woman will open the gate. Upon entering, they will see two paths. On the left path is a great tree called Aśoka; beneath that tree, many *māṭaṅgī* women play in all four directions. Those many women will point the practitioner to the right path.

"The practitioner should go to the right and walk one *yojana*. As far as the eye can see along the distant road, they will see a silver city. The city is tall and vast, and all its gates are locked. There are gate-hooks in the form of serpents, placed beside each gate. The practitioner, upon seeing them, should not be afraid. Take one hook and open the gate yourself.

"Upon entering, the first thing they will see is a sacred sword. If the practitioner wishes to take it, take the sword. Upon grasping it, they will become a great vidyādhara-king. After death, they will be reborn in Tuṣita Heaven. If they do not wish to take the sword, they should take the sacred wheel. Upon receiving it, they will become a cakravartin. After death, they will be reborn in the Heaven of the Four Kings. If they do not wish to take the wheel, they should take the cintāmaṇi jewel. Upon receiving it, they will attain the five supernatural powers and perform the eighteen transformations.

"Spontaneously, a voice will proclaim throughout the great earth to all sentient beings, saying: 'If any sentient being has any desire, I will give it to them and fulfill every wish.' They will then establish the banner of the cintāmaṇi.

"If they see the Dharma texts of the Buddhas, the practitioner should open and read them. They will obtain complete dhāraṇī-retention of all the Buddhas' Dharma without loss, and will penetrate the supreme, ultimate truth.

"If they see sacred medicine, the practitioner should take it. With one *liǎng* of the medicine, one can produce a koṭi of gold. If one ingests this medicine, one's life-span will equal that of Brahmā-deva.

"If they see *pānuka* boots, the practitioner should take them and put them on. They will be able to traverse the four great continents from sunrise to sunset and return to the original place within a short time.

"If they see añjana medicine, the practitioner should take a small amount and place it in their own eye. They will then see everything throughout the three realms.

"If they see sacred adornments, the practitioner should take them and wear them upon the body. They will immediately obtain handsome features, the power of invisibility, and sovereign freedom.

"If the practitioner does not wish to seek any of these sacred accomplishments, they should stay and rest there overnight. Within that cave, when the lotuses close, one knows it is night; when the asura-women come out to gather flowers, one knows it is day.

"At that time, the practitioner should proceed further. Not far from there, they will see a golden city. The practitioner should enter that city. They will see a palace made of the seven treasures, with maṇi pillars, jeweled wheels, jeweled nets, and various wondrous flowers as adornments. True pearls are strung and draped over it. The ground is covered with golden sand, pure, clean, and delightful.

"Within that palace is a lion-throne, tall, vast, and beautifully adorned. Maitreya Bodhisattva sits upon it, surrounded by innumerable devas, nāgas, the eight divisions, and various hosts of immortals, all paying reverence, hearing the Bodhisattva expound the wondrous Dharma.

"At that time, the practitioner, having arrived, should generate the most supreme and extraordinary mind, approach the Bodhisattva, and stand before him with reverence.

"Maitreya Bodhisattva will then say to the practitioner: 'Welcome, good man!' The practitioner should reply: 'I have come well.' The Bodhisattva will say: 'Excellent, excellent! Good man! You have been able to generate a courageous and resolute mind to come here. Why is this? The sentient beings of southern Jambudvīpa have firm and resolute faith. They are able to be vigorous and courageous in the Buddha-Dharma. They are able to generate a mind of reverence toward me. You should know: if any sentient being wishes, in this present life or in a future life, to see my body, they should generate a devoted mind and recite my root great-vidyā. They will thereby obtain peace and happiness, be free from all difficulties, and see my body.' The root great-vidyā:

"*Namo ratna-trayāya namo vairocana-sāminī-tathāgatāya arhate samyak-saṃbuddhāya tadyathā oṃ maitri-maitri-maitreya-vaḥ-siddhani maitri-sya-kaṭaya samara-samara saṃkha-pratigīya sara-sara visara-visara boddhaya-boddhaya boddhānugate mahā-boddhi parivartitāṃ mānase svāhā*

"The Bodhisattva says: 'Good man! This great-vidyā has great awesome power. If practitioners of the yoga practice can generate a devoted mind and maintain this vidyā, such a person will see my body without any doubt.'

"Then Maitreya Bodhisattva says to the practitioner: 'You have now come here. What do you seek? Do you seek to see my body and to receive the right Dharma? Do you seek various accomplished methods? Or do you seek great renown in southern Jambudvīpa? Whatever you seek, I will grant your wish.' The practitioner replies: 'Bodhisattva! When I first generated the mind to come and enter this palace, it was solely to see you, Bodhisattva. Now that I have seen you, my mind is fully satisfied. I seek nothing else. I will remain here until the time when you, Bodhisattva, attain perfect enlightenment in the future. May I hear and receive the right Dharma at your side.'"

At that time, Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva, having heard Śākyamuni Buddha teach this Dharma, was filled with joy, faithfully received it, and practiced in accordance with the teaching.
