# 妙法蓮華經度量天地品第二十九
## Chapter Twenty-Nine on Measuring Heaven and Earth, from the Sūtra of the Lotus of the Wonderful Dharma

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 2872

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown (失譯)

---

At that time, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva adjusted his robe, baring the right shoulder, and with his head and face touching the ground in prostration, he performed obeisance, then knelt on one knee with joined palms and addressed the Buddha, saying: "World-Honored One, we, the immeasurable assembly of bodhisattvas together with all the gods, humans, and others, having broadly heard the teachings expounded before the Buddha, the unfolding and explanation of the Sūtra of the Lotus of the Wonderful Dharma, find our minds pure and leaping with joy at what we had never before experienced. Again we address the Buddha, saying: 'World-Honored One, I constantly travel day and night, going and returning throughout all the immeasurable worlds of the ten directions, teaching and transforming sentient beings. I do not yet know the depth or shallowness, the nearness or distance, of heaven and earth. I humbly pray that the World-Honored One will explain this for me, so that I and the great assembly may all hear it completely.'"

At that time, the Buddha addressed Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva and the bodhisattvas as numerous as the sands of the Gaṅgā: "Listen carefully now. I shall analyze and explain this for you.

"This great earth is twenty hundred-million myriads of li in depth. Next there is a layer of moisture, also twenty hundred-million myriads of li in depth. Next there is a layer of the various wondrous precious jewels; gold, silver, lapis lazuli, crystal, agate, coral, and amber; forty hundred-million myriads of li in depth. Next there is a layer of golden granules, eighty hundred-million myriads of li in depth. Next there is a layer of hard silver, also eighty hundred-million myriads of li in depth. Next there is a layer of silver granules, one hundred hundred-million myriads of li in depth. Next there is a layer of vajra, two hundred hundred-million myriads of li in depth. Below all this is a boundless great wind that sustains heaven and earth and prevents them from tilting or moving.

"As for the great earth, it is the beam and pillar of heaven. Why is this? All the ten thousand things beneath heaven depend upon the earth: rivers, the great seas and small seas, broad pools, wells, valleys, grasses, trees, forests, medicinal herbs, and all trees; all are born and grow from the earth. Earthen mountains and hill-mountains, the small Iron-Encircling Mountains, Mount Meru, the Vajra Mountains, Mount Sumeru; all these various wondrous jewel mountains, the kings among all mountains, welling up from the four treasures and towering above all the various wondrous jewel mountains, all arise and are formed together from the precious jewels that well forth from the great earth.

"Furthermore, Mount Sumeru, the king of mountains, wells up from the four treasures and rises above all the worlds. What are the four? To the south it is called lapis lazuli; to the west it is called crystal; to the north it is called agate; to the east it is called gold. The radiant flames of the four treasures illuminate and shine upon the four directions. The sentient beings beneath heaven all gaze up at this and call it 'heaven.'

"As for Mount Sumeru, the Lord of Heaven and the Four Guardian Kings who protect the world all dwell in reliance upon it. Concerning Mount Sumeru: to the south there are immeasurable palaces of the seven treasures, at a height of one hundred myriads of li above the earth. Within them dwells a Heavenly King named Vi; "
