# 種種雜呪經
## Sūtra of Various Miscellaneous Mantras

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1337

Translated from the Chinese. Northern Zhou dynasty, translated by Jñānagupta (闍那崛多).

---

## Six Mantras from the Lotus Sūtra

### The First: Spoken by Bhaiṣajyarāja Bodhisattva

*Tadyathā: ani mani mani mamani kiti caritī śame śami-tā bhiṣaṇtī mokati moka-tā-tāme same abhisame asama-same jaye-kṣa-ye akṣaye akṣa-ni śaṇtī śaritī śami-tī araṇi aroka-bhaśi-parāti bhikṣaṇi bhipāra abhendara-ni bhiṣṇa atinda-pari-śuddhi ukule mokule araṭi parati śukaṇi asamasame buddhavilokiti dharma-parikṣiti saṃgani-ghośa-ni bhayabhaya-bhaya-śudhani manti mantra-kṣāyati huru-ṭī huru-ta kaupya-śariye akṣaye akṣaye abhyandā-niye aba-rā-ta-ni amana-nā-tā-ye.*

This dhāraṇī divine mantra was spoken by buddhas as numerous as sixty-two koṭi Ganges-sand-numbers. If anyone violates and insults this Dharma-teacher, they will have violated and insulted all these buddhas. This dhāraṇī greatly benefits sentient beings.

### The Second: Spoken by Pradānaśūra Bodhisattva

*Tadyathā: jale-bhare mahā-jale-bhare uke-moke ati-aṭa-bhati naritāye naritāya-bhati iti-ni-bhiti-ni kiti-ni naritini nari-ṭā-bhati.*

"World-Honored One! This dhāraṇī was spoken by buddhas as numerous as the Ganges sands. If anyone violates and insults this Dharma-teacher, they will have violated and insulted all these buddhas."

### The Third: Spoken by Vaiśravaṇa, the Heavenly King

*Tadyathā: aṭṭe naṭṭe nāṭṭe anāḍe-nāḍi kunaḍi-kunaḍi.*

### The Fourth: Spoken by Dhṛtarāṣṭra, the Heavenly King

*Tadyathā: agane gane gauri gandhāri caṇḍāli mātaṅgi pukkasi siṃhakūle pūruṣa-sūle.*

"World-Honored One! This dhāraṇī was spoken by forty-two koṭi buddhas. If anyone violates and insults this Dharma-teacher, they will have violated and insulted all these buddhas."

### The Fifth: Spoken Jointly by the Ten Rākṣasī

*Tadyathā: iti-me nime huru-he-stāhe.*

> If one does not comply with our mantra
> And disturbs the Dharma-teacher,
> Their head shall split into seven parts,
> Like the branches of an arjaka tree.

### The Sixth: Spoken by Samantabhadra Bodhisattva

*Tadyathā: adaṇḍe daṇḍe-pati-daṇḍe bhara-tani-daṇḍe kuśale-daṇḍe sudhari su-dhari su-dharā-pati-buddha-paśyane dharāṇi abhara-tani abhara-tani saṃgha-parikṣiti saṃgha-ni-gata-ne dharma-parikṣiti sarva-sattva bhā huru-ta kaupya-śariye anu-gati praśa-gna-bhikriti.*

If any bodhisattva hears this dhāraṇī, they should know it is Samantabhadra's power of supernatural penetration.

## Dhāraṇī for Circumambulating Stūpas to Eliminate Sins

*Namo buddhāya, namo dharmāya, namo saṃghāya. Namo āryāvalokiteśvarāya bodhisattvāya mahāsattvāya mahā-kāruṇikāya. Tadyathā: khara-vati śu-ha-vati gabhati svāhā.*

If a person is able to single-mindedly circumambulate a stūpa in walking practice for seven days, reciting this mantra a full twelve thousand times, they will see face to face the majestic power of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva. All their sin-obstructions will be eliminated and all their wishes fulfilled.

## Mantra for Bowing in Worship to Eliminate Sins, with All Buddhas Coming to Welcome at the End of Life

*Namo buddhāya. Oṃ huru huru siddha-locani sarva-artha-sādhani svāhā.*

This mantra comes from the Ratnaketu Sūtra and was spoken by one billion bodhisattvas. Reciting the mantra once and bowing to the Buddha once surpasses the merit of bowing to the Buddha thousands of millions of billions of times. It is also called "the mantra for all buddhas to come and welcome at the end of life." It should be recited one hundred times each at dawn and at the hour of settling in. Brahmin monks frequently recite this mantra.

## Mantra for Making Offerings to the Three Treasures

*Namo sarva-buddha-dharma-saṃgha-ṣya. Namo āryāvalokiteśvarāya bodhisattvāya mahāsattvāya mahā-kāruṇikāya. Tadyathā: dūbhi dūbhi gāya-dūbhi vīra-vīra-ni svāhā.*

If one recites this mantra once each day at dawn before a buddha image with palms joined and a sincere mind, it surpasses making offerings to ten trillion buddhas with various incenses and flowers.

## Avalokiteśvara Repentance Mantra

*Namo ratna-trayāya. Namo āryāvalokiteśvarāya bodhisattvāya mahāsattvāya mahā-kāruṇikāya. Tadyathā: turu turu aśi-ma śi-ma śi-mahā māri-ni dūpa-māri-ni dūbhi dūbhi namo-nama svāhā.*

Before an image of Avalokiteśvara, burn incense, confess one's sins openly, and recite three times with a sincere mind. This eliminates sins from beginningless time, and whatever one wishes for will certainly be fulfilled.

## Vajra Mantra for Snake Bites

*Namo vajrapāṇaye mahā-yakṣa-senāpataye. Ṭiṇi ṭiṇi svāhā.*

If a person is bitten by a poisonous snake, recite this mantra twenty-one times over them and the poison will immediately disperse and be eliminated.

## Mantra for Peaceful and Secure Seated Meditation

*Namo buddhāya, namo dharmāya, namo saṃghāya. Tadyathā: mahā-bhadre ati-bhadre vikṛtā-harāja-śi mahā-vikṛtā-harāja-śi svāhā.*

If when first sitting one has not yet settled and the body and mind are unsettled, first recite this mantra seven times, then sit in the cross-legged position and one will not be startled or disturbed.

## Mantra for Swellings

*Namo ratna-trayāya. Namo āryāvalokiteśvarāya bodhisattvāya mahāsattvāya mahā-kāruṇikāya. Tadyathā: kiri kiri kiri kiri bhi-kiri bhi-kiri svāhā.*

For malignant swellings or wind-swellings, empower sesame oil by reciting the mantra over it and apply. It will be cured.

## Vajra Mantra for Treating Ghost-Illness

*Namo ratna-trayāya. Namo śrī-caṇḍa-vajrapāṇaye mahā-yakṣa-senāpataye. Tadyathā: sara sara siri siri suru suru vandha vandha gari-hāya hūṃ jaliṃ-he-he vajrapāṇi kuru kuru dharma dharma namo-stuṭi svāhā.*

If a person is afflicted by a ghost-illness, empower water by reciting the mantra seven times, hold it in the hand, and facing the sick person, splash it on their face while shouting a command. They will be immediately cured.

## Thousand-Turn Dhāraṇī

*Namo ratna-trayāya. Namo āryāvalokiteśvarāya bodhisattvāya mahāsattvāya mahā-kāruṇikāya. Tadyathā: jaye jaye jaya-bhāhe-ni jayuttāri-kāla kāla māla māla cāla cāla kiṇa kiṇa sarva-karma bhāra-ṇa-nime bhagavati saha-sarva-kati sarva-buddha-vilokiti cacu-śūra-tā-ra-garaṇa svaha-vā-kāya mānu-bhiśudhani sura sura parasura parasura sarva-buddhādhiṣṭhite svāhā. Dharma-dhātu-garbhe svāhā. Abhava-bhava-bhava-ti-dharma-vā-buddha-ni-ye svāhā.*

## Four Following-the-Mind Mantras of Avalokiteśvara

### The First: Mantra for Eliminating Sins and Purification

*Namo ratna-trayāya. Namo āryāvalokiteśvarāya bodhisattvāya mahāsattvāya mahā-kāruṇikāya. Tadyathā: oṃ-tūre tu-tūre tu-vajra-mokṣaṇi svāhā.*

If one recites this a full one hundred thousand times, all sins will be eliminated.

### The Second Mantra

*Namo ratna-trayāya. Namo āryāvalokiteśvarāya bodhisattvāya mahāsattvāya mahā-kāruṇikāya. Tadyathā: atu-tutu helaṃ-ciṃ-gara-bhi harakṣa rakṣa mohuṣya sarva-duḥkhe-bhi sara-pūpa-dara-bhi svāhā.*

If one recites this a full one hundred thousand times, one attains fearlessness, and all those of evil paths who wish to harm one will be unable to succeed.

### The Third Mantra

*Namo ratna-trayāya. Namo āryāvalokiteśvarāya bodhisattvāya mahāsattvāya mahā-kāruṇikāya. Tadyathā: siddhi-caṇḍe hūṃ namuki-ni-ha hūṃ-tutu oṃ-bhiśudhani svāhā.*

If one recites this a full one hundred thousand times, all karmic obstructions will be purified.

### The Fourth Mantra

*Namo ratna-trayāya. Namo āryāvalokiteśvarāya bodhisattvāya mahāsattvāya mahā-kāruṇikāya. Tadyathā: oṃ-parasanna-ni-riyati-tata-parasandhe parasandhe parasanna-kāra-ṇi sarva-sattvā-na hūṃ-kāru-dha-praśama-ni svāhā.*

This is a mantra for removing anger. If recited seven times, it will cause the angry person to become joyful.

## Mantra of Seventy Koṭi Buddhas

*Namaḥ saptānāṃ samyak-saṃbuddha-koṭīnāṃ. Tadyathā: cale cule cunde svāhā.*

## Mantra That Follows the Intent of All Tathāgatas

*Namaḥ sarva-tathāgatāhṛdayānugata. Tadyathā: oṃ ku-kini svāhā.*

If a person is able to recite each of these two divine mantras once, they can eliminate the sins committed in nine koṭi great kalpas.

## Six-Syllable Dhāraṇī Mantra

*Oṃ vāgī-da namaḥ.*

Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva explained this mantra in six thousand verses. The Tripiṭaka master said: reciting this mantra eliminates all sins and generates all good.

## Mantra for Taking Refuge in the Three Treasures

*Namo buddhāya guruve, namo dharmāya tayane, namo saṃghāya mahate. Siddhi tri-bhyoḥ pi-sadā namo.*

If one recites this mantra, in every place of rebirth one will constantly encounter the Three Treasures and attain the four intimate practices, which will follow one like a shadow follows form, never departing even for a moment.
