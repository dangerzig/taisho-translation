# 大雲經祈雨壇法
## Altar Ritual for Requesting Rain from the Mahāmegha Sūtra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 990

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空)

---

When the sky is in severe drought and one wishes to pray for rain: construct an altar in the open air. Remove all rubble and impurities. Hang blue curtains and suspend blue banners. Apply fragrant clay to create a square altar. In the center of the altar, paint a pool of seven jewels. Within the pool, paint the palace of the Dragon King of the Sea. In the dragon palace, paint Śākyamuni Tathāgata in the appearance of expounding the Dharma. To the right of the Buddha, paint Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva; to the left of the Buddha, paint Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva and other attendants. To the right before the Buddha, paint the Wheel-Canopy Dragon King, Lord of the Trichiliocosm; to the left before the Buddha, paint the two Dragon Kings Nanda and Upananda.

On the four sides of the altar, using gomaya juice, paint one Dragon King on each side: in the east, paint one Dragon King with one body and three heads, three cubits in length, surrounded by his retinue; in the south, paint one Dragon King with one body and five heads, five cubits in length, with his retinue; in the west, paint one Dragon King with one body and seven heads, seven cubits in length, surrounded by his retinue; in the north, paint one Dragon King with one body and nine heads, nine cubits in length, surrounded by his retinue. All appear amid thick, dark blue-black clouds; from the waist down they take the form of serpents, with their tails in the pool; from the waist up they take the form of bodhisattvas, all with palms joined, rising up from the pool.

At the four corners of the altar, place four vases of clean water. According to one's means, dye all food, fruit, and other offerings blue, and arrange them as offerings with a sincere and pure heart. Burn incense and scatter blue flowers. Everything used within the ritual space should be made blue.

Those who pray for rain, if they are renunciant bhikṣus, should fully observe the Vinaya. If they are laypeople, they should take the eight precepts. During the performance of the ritual, eat only the three white foods. Each day, bathe in fragrant water and wear new, clean blue garments. On the western side of the altar, use blue materials as a seat, then anoint the hands with fragrance.

First, perform the three mysteries to empower oneself and protect the ritual space. Place this Mahāmegha Sūtra on the altar table. Arouse great compassion for all sentient beings. Sincerely invoke the empowerment of all Buddhas and Bodhisattvas. Day and night, devoutly recite this Mahāmegha Sūtra, or have two, three, or up to seven people take turns reciting, so that the sound of the sūtra is never interrupted.

In times of severe drought, when this Mahāmegha Sūtra is recited according to this method for one day, two days, or up to seven days, sweet rain will certainly descend. If the calamity is severe and rain still does not fall, perform the ritual again, and sweet rain will surely come. Even if the sea overflows its bounds or tides exceed their limits, when this ritual is performed and the sūtra is recited, there will be none that does not respond with effect.

One should make a vow to dedicate to all the dragon kings the merit generated through reciting this sūtra: may they all be freed from all suffering and difficulties, may they arouse the mind of unsurpassable bodhi, and for the sake of all sentient beings, may they cause sweet rain to descend.
