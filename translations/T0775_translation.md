# 佛說四無所畏經
## Sūtra of the Four Fearlessnesses, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0775

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dānapāla (施護)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was in the land of Śrāvastī, in the Jeta Grove of Anāthapiṇḍada. At that time the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣus: "Listen carefully! The Tathāgata, Worthy One, Perfectly Awakened One has accomplished the four fearlessnesses. In the midst of the great assembly he turns the great Dharma-wheel, roaring the lion's roar, sovereign and without fear."

When the bhikṣus heard these words, they prostrated with the five parts of their bodies on the ground, bowed at the Buddha's feet, joined their palms, and said: "What are the Tathāgata's four fearlessnesses?"

The Buddha told the bhikṣus: "In the past, in the midst of the great assembly, I made this statement: 'The Tathāgata has accomplished perfect and complete enlightenment.' When I said this, śramaṇas, brahmins, māras, Brahmā, and devas and humans all declared: 'What is proclaimed here does not accord with the Dharma's teaching. We have never seen or heard of such a thing.' At that time the World-Honored One, in the midst of that assembly, was without alarm, without fear. His mind found peace and happiness, and he abided in fearlessness.

"Furthermore, I declared: 'My body is pure; all outflows are exhausted.' When I said this, śramaṇas, brahmins, māras, Brahmā, and devas and humans all declared: 'What is proclaimed here does not accord with the Dharma's teaching. We have never seen or heard of such a thing.' At that time the World-Honored One, in the midst of that assembly, was without alarm, without fear. His mind found peace and happiness, and he abided in fearlessness.

"Furthermore, I declared: 'The single obstacle of defilement obstructs the path and its fruit. When this obstacle is destroyed, the holy path naturally appears.' When I said this, śramaṇas, brahmins, māras, Brahmā, and devas and humans all declared: 'What is proclaimed here does not accord with the Dharma's teaching. We have never seen or heard of such a thing.' At that time the World-Honored One, in the midst of that assembly, was without alarm, without fear. His mind found peace and happiness, and he abided in fearlessness.

"Furthermore, I declared: 'Cultivating morality, concentration, and the like can lead one out of birth-and-death and bring an end to all retribution of suffering.' When I said this, śramaṇas, brahmins, māras, Brahmā, and devas and humans all declared: 'What is proclaimed here does not accord with the Dharma's teaching. We have never seen or heard of such a thing.' At that time the World-Honored One, in the midst of that assembly, was without alarm, without fear. His mind found peace and happiness, and he abided in fearlessness."

The Buddha told the bhikṣus: "These are called the Tathāgata's four fearlessnesses in turning the great Dharma-wheel.

"Furthermore, bhikṣus, the Tathāgata also fully accomplishes the four fearlessnesses in the eight great assemblies. What are these eight? In the past, in a certain place, there were innumerable hundreds of śramaṇas, standing, sitting, walking, or at rest, saying to one another: 'The Buddha once taught such characteristics of the Dharma, profound and difficult to understand, never before seen or heard. Is it so or is it not? Is it true or is it false?' At that time the Tathāgata, hearing their words, was without timidity or dread, without the least hair standing on end, sovereign and fearless, like a mountain that does not move.

"Furthermore, innumerable hundreds of brahmins, innumerable hundreds of kṣatriyas, innumerable hundreds of householders, innumerable hundreds of the retinue of the Four Heavenly Kings, innumerable hundreds of the Trāyastriṃśa devas, innumerable hundreds of the māra kings, and innumerable hundreds of the Brahmā devas: in these eight assemblies, standing, sitting, walking, or at rest, they said to one another: 'The Buddha once taught such characteristics of the Dharma, profound and difficult to understand, never before seen or heard. Is it so or is it not? Is it true or is it false?' At that time the Tathāgata, hearing their words, was without timidity or dread, without the least hair standing on end, sovereign and fearless, like a mountain that does not move."

The Buddha said: "Bhikṣus, this is called being without fear amid the eight great assemblies." He then spoke this verse:

> "The Tathāgata, sovereign, turns the Dharma-wheel;
> In the past he accomplished the four fearlessnesses.
> Devas, humans, māras, Brahmā, and śramaṇas,
> Hearing the Teacher's words, harbored doubt.
> Body and mind unmoving, he attained fearlessness,
> Benefiting and bringing joy to all sentient beings,
> Causing them to generate the mind of unsurpassed bodhi,
> To practice with reverence and reach the other shore."

At that time all the great bhikṣus, hearing what the Buddha had taught, received it with faith and practiced accordingly.
