# 如來方便善巧呪經
## Sūtra of the Tathāgata's Skillful Means Mantras

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1334

Translated from the Chinese. Sui dynasty, translated by Jñānagupta (闍那崛多).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Bhagavat was on the peak of Mount Kailāsa, at the dwelling place of sages frequented by the devas, together with a great assembly of five hundred bhikṣus, and also five hundred great bodhisattva mahāsattvas, whose names were: Maitreya Bodhisattva, Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva, Samantabhadra Bodhisattva, Anantakusuma Bodhisattva, Samantakusuma Bodhisattva, and others. All these bodhisattvas abided as ekajāti-pratibaddha, successors to the honored position. At that time the World-Honored One was in that grove, surrounded by the great assembly. One bhikṣu had been seized by a ghost; his body was naked and he was unaware of it, raising his hands and crying out, his speech coarse and wild. Another bhikṣu was afflicted by a demonic spirit and, with his back turned to the Buddha, cried out mournfully in the sky, unable to control himself. When Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva saw and heard this, he immediately rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, placed his right knee on the ground, joined his palms toward the Buddha, and said: "World-Honored One! Who is causing these sounds in the sky? And for what reason is this bhikṣu naked, raising his hands and crying out?"

Then the World-Honored One addressed Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva Mahāsattva: "Good man! This one bhikṣu has been seized by an evil ghost, and the other bhikṣu is afflicted by a demonic spirit." Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva then addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! Out of compassion for all sentient beings and in order to eliminate the suffering of all illnesses, the Tathāgata should today speak divine mantras."

Then the World-Honored One, by his supernatural power, caused six tathāgatas to suddenly appear in the sky, each speaking a mantra. They were: first, Vipaśyin Buddha; second, Śikhin Buddha; third, Viśvabhū Buddha; fourth, Krakucchanda Buddha; fifth, Kanakamuni Buddha; and sixth, Kāśyapa Buddha. At that time Vipaśyin Buddha, abiding in the sky, for the protection of all sentient beings and the elimination of all evil demonic ghosts, spoke this mantra:

*Namo buddhāya, namo dharmāya, namo saṃghāya. Oṃ hara-hara hīre-hīre hīrāya. Namaḥ kilī-kilāya. Jāghan-mocana-ya. Namo nama svāhā.*

When Vipaśyin Buddha had spoken this mantra, he addressed Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva: "Good man! If any faithful man or faithful woman can receive, uphold, read, and recite this dhāraṇī, no blade or staff can harm them, no water or fire can injure them, non-human evil ghosts cannot trouble them, and they will never die an untimely death. If they are poisoned, the poison will transform into delicious food, easy to digest and causing no illness. Before eating, one should first take one spoonful, empower it seven times with the mantra, and then eat; this can eliminate all curses and demonic afflictions, and all illnesses will be healed. Lifespan will be extended, and whatever the ears hear will be remembered without forgetting. In sleep, one will dream of seeing the buddhas. If there is a person afflicted by a demonic spell, recite the mantra seven times beside their ear, and upon hearing it they will immediately be cured. If there is a person suffering from epilepsy or vitiligo, take sumanā flowers, fumigate sesame to make oil, take agaru, blue lotus, fennel grass, and gate-facing herbs in equal parts, grind them to powder, combine with the oil and cook, empower one hundred and eight times, and apply to the body; the person will immediately be cured. All manner of illnesses can be overcome by this."

Then Śikhin Buddha, abiding in the sky, for the protection of all sentient beings, the elimination of all illnesses, and the severing of all ghosts and evil spirits, spoke this divine mantra:

*Namo buddhāya, namo dharmāya, namo saṃghāya. Namaḥ śikhi-nirdita-tathāgatāya. Oṃ paca-paca pacāya. Sarva-bhūta-nāṃ cchandha-rāya. Pra-abhidena. Svāhā.*

When Śikhin Tathāgata had spoken this mantra, he addressed Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva: "Good man! This dhāraṇī was spoken by immeasurable hundreds of thousands of koṭi nayutas of buddhas. I too now speak it, wishing to show compassion for all sentient beings, to eliminate all illness, to repel all ghosts, and to remove evil dreams and untimely death. Ākāśagarbha! If any good man or good woman can day and night recollect and recite this divine mantra, they will never be troubled by evil illness or bewitched by evil people, and in sleep they will dream of seeing the buddhas. When they leave this body, they will encounter that buddha and in his presence attain realization of the path. Ākāśagarbha! The power of this dhāraṇī is immeasurable. I shall now explain it to you in brief. If any good man or good woman wishes to sever all illness, they should take five-colored thread and tie it as a mantra-cord. For epidemic illness, take sumanā flower oil, empower it one hundred and eight times, and apply it to the body; they will immediately be cured. If one wishes to protect oneself and be at peace, empower water one hundred and eight times, sprinkle it in the four directions to establish the boundary, and take five-colored thread and tie it as a mantra-cord to wear. For a child afflicted by an evil spirit, take blue-colored thread and tie it as a mantra-cord. If one is troubled by an evil spirit and falls unconscious, recite this dhāraṇī beside their ear up to seven times and they will immediately be cured. For all such matters, apply as appropriate."

Then Viśvabhū Buddha, abiding in the sky, for the protection and rescue of all sentient beings from all illness and suffering and the severing of all evil ghosts, spoke this dhāraṇī:

*Namo buddhāya, namo dharmāya, namo saṃghāya. Namaḥ viśvabhū-svid-tathāgatāya. Oṃ karā-karā-karā kuru-kuru-kuru-ccha-dānaṃ kuru. Sarva-kāraṇā. Svāhā.*

When Viśvabhū Buddha had spoken this mantra, he addressed Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva: "Good man! This dhāraṇī was spoken by all the buddhas of the Bhadrakalpa and by the buddhas of the three times. I too now speak it. Ākāśagarbha! You should protect and uphold it. If any good man or good woman receives, upholds, reads, and recites this dhāraṇī, no blade or staff can harm them, no evil poison can injure them, all malarial fevers will be kept far away, water cannot sweep them away, fire cannot burn them, they will never die an untimely death, and no evil illness can afflict them, with the sole exception of fixed karmic retribution from past deeds. If any bhikṣu, bhikṣuṇī, upāsaka, or upāsikā, at dawn, bathes and puts on clean garments, and before a buddha image burns fine incense and recites this mantra a full one hundred and eight times, all demonic spells and curses will be unable to cause harm, all illness and suffering will be kept far away, all obstacles will be removed, lifespan will be extended, and whatever money, goods, and possessions the heart desires will all be obtained. If there are evil people, evil bandits, or hostile officials and rulers, they will first be pacified and appeased, and all sentient beings will be of one accord. If one is fearful because of quarrels or facing officials in a dispute, take white-colored thread, empower it one hundred and eight times, and tie it as a mantra-cord to wear; in all matters, one will gain victory. If one wishes for longevity, take yogurt, honey, and rice-flower, combine them, take a spoonful, empower it once, and cast it into the fire, up to one thousand and eight times; one's wish will be fulfilled. If one wishes to remove one's own and others' obstacles, gain all manner of wealth and treasures, be free from all evil, accomplish all skillful crafts, uphold all mantras, and subdue all enemies, take fennel, grass incense, powdered incense, śirīṣa flowers, tagara incense, stone flowers, kuṅkuma incense, cyperus, indra-hand grass incense, and the white sap of tree branches; take equal parts, grind to powder and combine, empower one thousand and eight times, and apply to the body; one's wish will be fulfilled. If one wishes to see officials and ministers, take a tree branch that exudes white sap and make a fire; empower sesame, take a pinch, empower once, and cast it into the fire, up to one thousand and eight times; one's wish will be fulfilled. If one wishes to cure poisoning, before a buddha image, apply mud to the ground, take purple-steel wood branches cut to two inches in length, one hundred and eight pieces, soak them in ghee and honey, name each one after the person, and cast them into the fire while reciting the mantra; one's wish will be fulfilled. If one wishes to eliminate all evil ghosts, take one hundred and eight remnant fire-brands from a cremation, empower each one once, and cast them into the fire; the ailment will immediately be cured. For all such matters in all circumstances, apply as appropriate, except that one must never cause harm to the bodies or lives of sentient beings."

Then Krakucchanda Buddha, abiding in the sky, for the protection of all sentient beings, the elimination of all illness, and the severing of all evil and demonic spirits, spoke this mantra:

*Namo buddhāya, namo dharmāya, namo saṃghāya. Namaḥ krakucchanda-tathāgatāya arhat-sammyak-saṃbuddhāya. Oṃ kaṭa-kaṭa-kaṭa-kaṭa khicchi-khicchi-khicchi kāya. Namaḥ sarva-tathāgate-bhyo arhat-addhiya-sammyak-saṃbodhi-vijñā. Svāhā.*

When Krakucchanda Buddha had spoken this mantra, he addressed Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva: "Good man! This dhāraṇī was spoken, praised, and rejoiced in by buddhas equal in number to the sands of the Ganges who share the name Krakucchanda, and by the buddhas of the three times. If a person receives and upholds this divine mantra, in the future, in the final age, they will establish the Three Treasures and give rise to right faith. If any bhikṣu, bhikṣuṇī, upāsaka, or upāsikā, at dawn, bathes, puts on fresh clean garments, prepares various fine incenses and flowers, makes offerings to the Buddha, and recites this mantra one hundred and eight times, that person will be able to know the events of seven past lives. If born in heaven, they will be honored among the devas; if born among humans, they will become a ruler. If they receive and uphold it throughout their life according to the dharma, at the time of death they will be born in the Land of Peace and Happiness. If one constantly recites and upholds it thus, in this very body one will be freed from all manner of illness and suffering and will attain dhāraṇī, hearing and retaining without forgetting. Before eating, empower the food seven times and then eat; thus one will be freed from all poison and illness. If one wishes to eliminate others' calamities and afflictions, empower clean water one thousand and eight times, bathe the body, and then proceed. For treating evil sores, use a red copper knife. For all such matters, recite the mantra seven times and one's wish will be fulfilled. If one constantly recites and upholds it, wealth will come of itself, all poisons will be destroyed, and all obstructing evil ghosts and demonic spirits will stay far away. If one wishes to see the body of a buddha, go to a pure place, whether a place of practice or before a stūpa, apply cow-dung to the ground, burn agaru incense, and recite this mantra one thousand and eight times. Then lie down with the head facing east, and in a dream one will see the buddhas, and whatever the heart wishes will all be fulfilled. That buddha tathāgata will reveal whether one's life will be long or short, or whether there are deficiencies, showing all such matters."

Then Kanakamuni Buddha, abiding in the sky, for the protection of all sentient beings from confusion and bewilderment, the elimination of all illness, and the severing of all evil ghosts and demonic spirits, spoke this mantra:

*Namo buddhāya, namo dharmāya, namo saṃghāya. Namaḥ kanaka-munaye tathāgatāya arhat-sammyak-saṃbuddhāya. Oṃ sara-sara-sara-sara śira-pāya dharma-dharma-dharma-dharma dhumu-dhumu-dhumāya. Namaḥ kanaka-munaye tathāgatāya arhat-sammyak-saṃbuddhāya. Siddhantu mantra-pada. Svāhā.*

When Kanakamuni Buddha had spoken this mantra, he addressed Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva: "Good man! This dhāraṇī: if any good man or good woman constantly reads, recites, receives, and upholds it, that person will forever be freed from fear and all enemies. Water cannot sweep them away, fire cannot burn them. If they accidentally consume poison, it will transform into sweet and delicious nourishment for the body, extending their life. They will not suffer untimely illness or untimely death. Their lifespan will be extended, their official rank increased, and they will constantly be protected and remembered by all the buddhas. If obstacles from prior karma arise, recite it three times daily and all will be eliminated. If one wishes to heal others' illness and suffering, prepare food and drink as one is able, together with various incenses and flowers, whether agaru or frankincense, and make a fine clean decoction. Empower it one thousand and eight times, bathe the body, and then perform the mantra practice; all illness and suffering, and those afflicted by evil demonic ghosts, will be cured. For treating leprosy, bodily swelling, and disorders of wind and cold, take calamus powder mixed with white honey, recite the mantra one thousand and eight times before the Buddha, and take it on an empty stomach; one will immediately be cured. If one has malarial fever of one, two, three, or four days, also before the Buddha, apply cow-dung to the ground of the practice-site, take sumanā flowers and fashion them into a garland, place it on the head, and the disease will immediately depart. If one has been bewitched and falls unconscious, recite this dhāraṇī beside their ear one hundred and eight times and they will immediately be cured. If one has epilepsy or madness, or has been seized by kaṭapūtana, piśāca ghosts, or the like, before a buddha image apply cow-dung to the ground, burn fine incense as above, take the wilted flowers that were offered before the Buddha, empower them one thousand and eight times, burn them in the fire, and fumigate the nostrils; the person will immediately be cured. For all such illnesses, apply as appropriate."

Then Kāśyapa Buddha, abiding in the sky, for the protection of all immeasurable sentient beings, the elimination of all illness, and the severing of all evil demonic spirits, spoke this divine mantra:

*Namo buddhāya, namo dharmāya, namo saṃghāya. Namaḥ kāśyapa-tathāgatāya. Oṃ hara-hara-hara-hara ha-ha-ha. Namaḥ kāśyapāya arhat-sammyak-saṃbuddhāya. Siddha-ghu-mantra-pada. Svāhā.*

When Kāśyapa Buddha had spoken this mantra, he addressed Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva: "Good man! This dhāraṇī was spoken by buddhas equal in number to thirty-three times the sands of the Ganges. You should receive, uphold, read, recite, and practice it. Ākāśagarbha! If any good man or good woman recites this mantra three times daily and nightly, they will immediately dream of seeing the buddhas and be freed from all karmic obstacles, with the sole exception of the fixed retribution of past lives. In all matters, whatever the heart desires will be obtained. If one has leprosy or vitiligo, before a buddha image, apply cow-dung to the ground, burn fine incense, take the wilted flowers offered before the Buddha, empower them one thousand and eight times, grind to powder, mix with water, and take it before the Buddha; one will immediately be cured. For chronic illness and epidemic diseases, the treatment is the same. For headache, also apply the flower powder to the forehead. For a woman having a difficult birth, cook this flower powder in oil and apply it to the birth canal. For all such cases, apply as appropriate, and all will receive benefit. For scabies and damp sores, also take wilted flower fine powder mixed with ghee, cook over fire, empower one thousand and eight times, and apply; one will immediately be cured. If one wishes to stop rain, also take wilted flowers, empower one thousand and eight times, and burn them in the open; as the smoke rises, the sky will immediately clear. If a great wind arises with thunder, lightning, and hail, these too can be stopped. If one has malarial fever of one, two, three, or four days, also take wilted flowers, empower one thousand and eight times, finely grind to powder, mix with wine, and drink it; one will immediately be cured. For epilepsy, empower the flowers as before; whether burned or taken as medicine, all will be cured. If one has been bewitched or seized by piśāca ghosts and spirits, or has disorders of wind and cold, or diseases related to eclipses, mix with water and drink. For all such illnesses, before the Buddha, apply cow-dung to the ground, empower wilted flowers one thousand and eight times, whether burning or taking them as medicine; all will be cured, and each will attain peace and happiness. Do not give rise to doubt; one should believe with certainty and apply as the heart directs."

Then Śākyamuni Tathāgata, also abiding in the sky, for the protection of all immeasurable sentient beings, the elimination of all illness, and the severing of all evil demonic ghosts, spoke this divine mantra:

*Namo buddhāya, namo dharmāya, namo saṃghāya. Namaḥ Śākya-munaye tathāgatāya arhat-sammyak-saṃbuddhāya. Tadyathā: gume-gume mahā-gume tari-tari mahā-tari cari-cari mahā-cari dāṃ-dāṃ mahā-dāri temi-temi mahā-temi ha-ha khiri-khiri mahā-khiri juru-juru mahā-juru miri-miri mahā-miri tiri-tiri mahā-tiri dhume-dhume mahā-dhume cari-cari mahā-cari kiri-kiri mahā-kiri cari-cari mahā-cari. Svāhā.*

When Śākyamuni Tathāgata had spoken this mantra, he addressed Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva: "Ākāśagarbha! If any good man or good woman receives, upholds, reads, and recites this dhāraṇī, no blade or staff can harm them, no water or fire can injure them, and no evil illness can trouble them. They will never die an untimely death. This is no different from the divine mantras spoken by the six buddhas above."

Then Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva Mahāsattva addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! The divine mantras spoken by the buddhas above, I shall protect and uphold them. I too now wish to speak a mantra to protect all immeasurable sentient beings, to eliminate all obstacles, sever all karma, extinguish all sins, dispel all misfortune, remove all ghosts, repel all evil spirits, and heal all illness. For these reasons, I speak this divine mantra:"

*Athā khāra-ri-ka garā bodhisattva mahāsattva bhagavarī tame-ta-dhū-cata a-ha-ma bhagava-yubha-vanse sa-bhe śaṃ-pari-pa-pācīya-naṃ-ni-bhāra-ṇi. Sarva-bhūta-ni-bhāra-ṇi sarva-roga-ni-bhāra-ṇi. Tadyathā: ciri-ciri mahā-ciri dhuru-dhuru mahā-dhuru śvari-śvari mahā-śvari hiri-hiri mahā-hiri. Svāhā.*

When Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva Mahāsattva had spoken this mantra, he addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! This divine mantra has immeasurable supernatural power, inconceivable. If any good man or good woman has been seized by an evil ghost, make a mantra-cord with threads, empower it one hundred and eight times, and wear it while sitting; the evil ghosts will immediately depart. Furthermore, take sumanā flowers, fine pure cow's ghee, and white mustard seed, combine them in one place, empower once and cast into the fire with each recitation, up to one hundred and eight times and one hundred and eight casts; the ailment will immediately be cured. If an evil person wishes to come and cause harm, take an iron knife, empower it one hundred and eight times, and strike the path that the evil person travels upon; they will be unable to cause harm. Alternatively, empower water and sprinkle it in the four directions to establish a four-directional boundary; staying within it, no evil will be able to prevail. If one wishes to protect oneself from harm, take five-colored thread and make a cord, empower it one hundred and eight times, and wear it whether sitting or walking. If one wishes to remove poison, hold medicinal herbs in the hand and empower them. If one wishes to remove illness, empower oil one hundred and eight times and apply it; the ailment will be healed. For ear pain, take agaru, finely grind to powder, cook in oil, and place in the ear. For abdominal illness, empower ginger water one hundred and eight times and drink it; the ailment will immediately be healed. For toothache and dental decay from worms or wind, take a sumanā wood branch, empower it one hundred and eight times, chew on it and rub the teeth; the ailment will immediately be healed. For sick children, tie white thread as a mantra-cord and have the child wear it. For headache, use either a knife or the hand, empower one hundred and eight times, and the ailment will immediately be healed. If one has been bewitched, empower a knife and use it to undo the spell. To stop evil wind, hail, and rain, empower a pomegranate branch and use it to ward them off. To counter evil poisons, empower water and use it. For all such matters in all circumstances, apply as appropriate. In all places, before a buddha image, on a mud-prepared ground, scatter flowers, burn incense, and receive and uphold according to the dharma." When the buddhas had thus finished speaking this mantra-sūtra, Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva together with all the great bodhisattvas and arhats, having heard what the Buddha taught, joyfully put it into practice.
