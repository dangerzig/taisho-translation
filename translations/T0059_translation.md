# 諸法本經
## Sūtra on the Root of All Dharmas

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0059

Translated from Chinese by Zhi Qian of Wu

---

## Fascicle 1

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was dwelling in Jeta's Grove, Anāthapiṇḍada's Park, in the kingdom of Śrāvastī. The Buddha addressed the bhikṣus: "Listen as I expound the root of all dharmas." They replied: "So be it."

The World-Honored One said: "If there are outsiders and practitioners of other teachings who come to ask: 'What is the root of dharmas?' you should answer: 'Desire is the root of all dharmas. What is their accumulation? Contact is their accumulation. What is their common destination? Feeling is their common destination. What brings them into existence? Mindfulness brings them into existence. What illumines the path? Contemplation illumines the path. What is foremost? Samādhi is foremost. What is supreme? Wisdom is supreme. What is unshakeable? Liberation is unshakeable. What is ultimate? Nirvāṇa is ultimate.'

"Thus, bhikṣus: desire is the root of all dharmas, contact is the accumulation of all dharmas, feeling is the common destination of all dharmas, mindfulness brings all dharmas into existence, contemplation illumines the path of all dharmas, samādhi is foremost among all dharmas, wisdom is supreme among all dharmas, liberation is the unshakeable ground of all dharmas, nirvāṇa is the ultimate end of all dharmas.

"Bhikṣus, you should train in this way: always cultivate the perception of having gone forth from household life, the perception of impermanence, the perception of suffering in impermanence, the perception of non-self in suffering, the perception of loathsomeness in food, the perception of impurity, the perception of death, the perception of the absence of delight in all the world, the perception of knowing the crooked and straight in the world, the perception of discerning existence and non-existence in the world. The pleasures that the world habitually grasps, their change and loss, and their final destination — all of these you should know through right view as they truly are. Bhikṣus, being mindful of this is for severing craving, abandoning desire, entering right wisdom, and reaching the end of suffering."

When the Buddha had finished teaching this sūtra, all rejoiced and undertook to practice it.
