# 佛說轉法輪經
## Sūtra on Turning the Dharma Wheel Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0109

Translated from the Chinese. Later Han dynasty, translated by An Shigao (安世高)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the country of Vārāṇasī, seated beneath a tree in the Deer Park. At that time, a thousand bhikṣus and all the celestial spirits were gathered in a great assembly, filling the sky. Then a spontaneous Dharma wheel flew forth and came before the Buddha, turning. The Buddha touched the wheel with his hand and said: "Stop! In the past, through numberless kalpas, I turned through birth and death for the sake of name-and-form, receiving suffering without measure. Now the intention of delusion and craving has ceased, the bonds of the outflows have been released, the faculties are settled, birth and death have been severed. I shall no longer turn through the five destinies." The wheel then stopped.

Thereupon the Buddha told the bhikṣus: "In the world there are two things that lead to extreme conduct. A disciple of the path who has left the household life should never engage in either of them throughout life. What are the two? The first is having the mind set on sensual desire without aspiration for purity. The second is clinging to the body and its pleasures, unable to be vigorous. These are the extreme conducts that cause one to regress; one cannot encounter the Buddha's path, virtue, and the complete person. If a bhikṣu does not set the mind on sensual desire or engage in clinging to bodily pleasures, one can receive the middle way. The Tathāgata's supreme and perfect awakening attains the eye, attains wisdom, crossing over from both extremes, and personally reaches nirvāṇa.

"What is the middle way? It is the practice of the eightfold right path: first, right view; second, right intention; third, right speech; fourth, right action; fifth, right livelihood; sixth, right effort; seventh, right mindfulness; eighth, right concentration.

"Bhikṣus! If you had not previously heard the path, you should now know: 'This is suffering' is a noble truth. Having single-mindedly received the eye, received meditative reflection, received the wisdom-vision, awakened to what the mind contemplates, and caused the mind to understand, you should know: 'The arising of suffering, the cessation of suffering' are noble truths. Having received the eye, contemplated, reflected on the wisdom-vision, awakened to what the mind contemplates, and caused the mind to understand: thus is the truth of cessation.

"What is suffering? Birth is suffering, old age is suffering, illness is suffering, sorrow, grief, and affliction are suffering, meeting what one hates is suffering, separation from what one loves is suffering, not obtaining what one seeks is suffering. In short, clinging to the five aggregates is suffering.

"What is the arising of suffering? It is craving that leads to renewed existence, accompanied by delight: not departing from it, desiring everywhere. Craving for sensual pleasures, craving for form, craving for formlessness: this arising is suffering.

"What is the cessation of suffering? It is awakening to the fact that from craving comes renewed existence and its pleasures: not receiving lustful thoughts, not being mindful, without remainder, without lust, abandoning it, without further meditation. Thus is the cessation of arising.

"What is the path leading to the cessation of the arising of suffering? It is the practice of the eightfold right path: right view, right intention, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, right concentration. This is the noble truth of the path for the cessation of the arising of suffering.

"Furthermore, bhikṣus! 'This is suffering' is a noble truth. 'Suffering has an arising' is a noble truth. 'The arising of suffering ceases' is a noble truth. 'There is a path leading to the cessation of the arising of suffering' is a noble truth. If those who had not previously heard this Dharma should receive the eye, contemplative practice, the wisdom-vision, and the awakened mind, causing the mind to be liberated. If those now present who have not heard this Dharma of the four truths should receive the eye of the path, receive meditative reflection, receive the wisdom-awakening, causing the mind to practice liberation. If those elsewhere who have not been able to hear this Dharma of the four truths should also receive the eye, receive meditation, receive wisdom, receive awakening, causing the mind to be liberated.

"This is the four truths, three turnings combined making twelve aspects. Until knowledge was complete and purified, I did not declare it. Among all the world's gods and humans, whether brahmās, māras, śramaṇas, or brāhmaṇas, having personally known and realized it, one practices morality, concentration, wisdom, liberation, and the knowledge and vision of liberation. This constitutes the four ultimates. This birth is the last; there will be no further existence. One is forever separated from the world, without sorrow or affliction."

When the Buddha taught this, the Venerable Ājñāta-Kauṇḍinya and the others, together with eighty thousand celestials, all became far removed from dust and defilement, and the Dharma eye arose in them. The thousand bhikṣus, their outflows exhausted and minds liberated, all attained arhatship. And all remaining habitual dharmas that should be exhausted were all turned. The sound of the World-Honored One's Dharma wheel resounded through three turnings. All gods and worldly beings abiding on the ground of the Dharma heard it universally, reaching the first heaven of the Four Great Kings, the Trāyastriṃśa Heaven, the Yāma Heaven, the Tuṣita Heaven, the Nirmāṇarati Heaven, the Paranirmita-vaśavartin Heaven, up to all the Brahmā realms, universally heard in an instant. At that time, in the Buddha's realm of three thousand suns and moons and twelve thousand heavens and earths, all trembled greatly. This was the World-Honored One, beginning at Vārāṇasī, turning the supreme Dharma wheel that had never been turned before, illuminating countless beings, and all the gods and humans who from this attained the path.

The Buddha having spoken thus, all greatly rejoiced.
