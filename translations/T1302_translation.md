# 諸星母陀羅尼經
## Dhāraṇī Sūtra of the Mother of All Stars

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1302

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Facheng (法成).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Bhagavān was dwelling in a great settlement in the wilderness. The devas, nāgas, yakṣas, rākṣasas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, the māras, the spirits of the sun and moon, Mars, Venus, Saturn, the remaining stars, Jupiter, Rāhu, and the Comet-Spirit, the twenty-eight lunar mansions, and all the great assemblies were all praising the vow-phrases of the great vajras. Upon the lion-throne adorned with majestic splendor, assembled in one place with all the bodhisattvas: Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, Vajra-Krodha Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, Vajra-Kula Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, Vajra-Cāpa Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, Vajra-Pati Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, Vajrālaṃkāra Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, Vajra-Prabhā Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, Sarva-Darśin Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, Loka-Maṅgala Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, Padma-Dhvaja Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, Vipula-Mukha Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, Puṣpa-Netra Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, and Maitreya Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, surrounded before and behind by all the great bodhisattva-saṃgha, gazing up as the Dharma was taught. The Dharma was called the Vast and Majestic Wish-Fulfilling Jewel, excellent in the beginning, middle, and end, beautiful in meaning and expression, unmixed and pure, purified and brahmic conduct.

At that time, Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva observed the great assembly, rose from his seat, and by his own spiritual power circumambulated the World-Honored One several hundred thousand times. Having bowed, he stood before the Buddha, supporting himself with his own power, sitting properly in padmāsana, gazing upon the great assembly. Placing his vajra-palm upon his own heart, he addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! There are evil stars whose forms and appearances are extremely malignant. They possess fierce and sharp minds, wrathful in form and expression. They afflict sentient beings, stealing their vital essence, their possessions, or their very lives. Those of long life they cause to die young. Thus they torment all sentient beings. For their sake, I humbly pray that the World-Honored One will reveal a Dharma-gate to guard and protect all sentient beings." The World-Honored One replied: "Excellent! Excellent! You have raised great compassion. For the benefit of all sentient beings, you ask the Tathāgata about this profound and secret meaning. Listen carefully now, consider well, and I shall teach the method for destroying the wrath and devastation of the evil stars, and also speak the secret meaning of making offerings, practicing giving, and mantra-recitation."

> "Those to be worshipped, one should worship;
> Those that create evil, one should repel.
> These stars of various forms and colors,
> How may they be caused to rejoice?
> The devas and the non-devas,
> The kiṃnaras, the nāgas,
> The yakṣas and the rākṣasas,
> Humans, kaṭapūtanas and the rest,
> The fiercely powerful great spirits,
> How may their wrath be extinguished?
> The secret words and offering methods
> I shall now proclaim in sequence."

At that time, Śākyamuni Tathāgata emitted from his own heart the playful radiance of the compassionate mind, which entered the topknots and feet of all the stars. Immediately, the sun, moon, and all star-spirits rose from their seats and with celestial offerings made worship to Śākyamuni Tathāgata. Kneeling with knees touching the ground, they joined their palms in reverence and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! The Tathāgata, Worthy of Offerings, Perfectly and Truly Awakened One, has benefited us. We humbly pray that the World-Honored One will proclaim a Dharma-gate so that when we are assembled, we may guard, protect, and defend the teacher of the Dharma, granting auspiciousness, keeping them far from weapons, destroying poisons, and establishing boundaries."

At that time, Śākyamuni Tathāgata then taught the method of offering to the stars and spoke the secret dhāraṇī:

*Oṃ mohurā-kāya svāhā. Oṃ śitāṃśu-bhe svāhā. Oṃ lola-daṅga-kumārāya svāhā. Oṃ baudhya baudhya svāhā. Oṃ bhoga-asibhaṇḍe-śe svāhā. Oṃ hasura-sattamāya svāhā. Oṃ kṛṣṇa-paranāya svāhā. Oṃ amita-priyā svāhā. Oṃ jacchī-kata-bhe svāhā.*

"Vajrapāṇi! These are the secret heart-mantras of the eight stars. Merely reading them brings accomplishment. One should construct a single-colored incense-altar twelve finger-spans in size, install the offerings within it, and present offerings in vessels of clay, copper, gold, or silver. With each individual offering, recite 108 times.

"Vajrapāṇi! Afterward, recite this secret dhāraṇī of the Mother of All Stars a full seven times. All the stars will then provide protection. Whatever poverty one suffers will be entirely liberated. Even one whose life is about to end will obtain long life.

"Vajrapāṇi! If any bhikṣu, bhikṣuṇī, upāsaka, or upāsikā, or any other sentient being merely hears it with their ears, they will not suffer untimely death.

"Vajrapāṇi! Having established offerings within the star-altar, if one recites it daily, all the stars will fulfill every wish of that Dharma-teacher, and all poverty and scarcity among their kind will be eliminated."

At that time, Śākyamuni Tathāgata then taught the Dhāraṇī of the Mother of All Stars, speaking the mantra:

*Namo buddhāya. Namo vajra-dharāya. Namo padma-dharāya. Namaḥ sarva-grahāṇāṃ. Namaḥ sarva-āśā-pura-kānāṃ. Namo nakṣatrāṇāṃ. Namo dvādaśa-rāśīnāṃ. Tadyathā buddhi buddhi pattra pattra pamin pamin sara sara prasara prasara saṃbhara saṃbhara kṛtāya kṛtāya māra māra makṛda makṛda gaṇḍīya sarva-vighna-kuru kuru jina jina kṣapaya kṣapaya śānti dīrgha-māya dīrgha-māye dhruta-ni-daśa-ya-karmānaṃ bhagavati rakṣaya rakṣaya māna-bha-pari-pālaṃ sara-ba-satuṅga-siddhya sarva-kṛhana-kṣatra-peta-māpaṇi-parā-ni-parā bhagavati mahā-māye sādhaya dhu-siddhya-māśaya pāpa-ni māṃ cānti cānti turu turu cānti moyu moyu cimoyu cimoyu habhā-habe ukri-ukra-teme-purāya-me manudarāṃ sarva-tathāgatā-dhiṣṭhite samāya svāhā. Oṃ svāhā. Hūṃ svāhā. Hrīḥ svāhā. Hūṃ svāhā. Haṃ svāhā. Padma-dhara svāhā. Ādityāya svāhā. Somāya svāhā. Dhara-ni-sudāya svāhā. Buddhāya svāhā. Budha-aspatīye svāhā. Grahāya svāhā. Kṣaṇa-bhanāya svāhā. Rāhu-be svāhā. Citra-be svāhā. Buddhāya svāhā. Vajra-dharāya svāhā. Padma-dhara svāhā. Kumārāya svāhā. Nakṣatrānāṃ svāhā. Sarvopadrava-nāṃ svāhā. Oṃ sarva-pāpiti phaṭ phaṭ svāhā.*

"Vajrapāṇi! This is the secret mantra-phrase of the Dhāraṇī of the Mother of All Stars, the root that accomplishes all matters.

"Vajrapāṇi! This secret dhāraṇī mantra-phrase: beginning from the seventh day of the bright fortnight of the ninth month, maintain complete purity. On the fourteenth day, make offerings to the stars. Receive and uphold this dhāraṇī. On the fifteenth day of the month, if one can recite it day and night, after a full nine years one will have no fear of death, no fear of shooting stars or falling celestial bodies, no fear of evil from the lunar mansions. One will remember past lives and be able to make offerings to all the stars, and all the stars will grant whatever one wishes."

At that time, all the stars bowed to the World-Honored One, praised him saying "Excellent!" and suddenly vanished.
