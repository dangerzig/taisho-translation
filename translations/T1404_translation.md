# 佛說如意寶總持王經
## Sūtra on the Wish-Fulfilling Jewel Dhāraṇī King Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1404

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dānapāla (施護).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the Tuṣita heavenly palace, together with Suthita and countless thousands of bodhisattva-mahāsattvas. These bodhisattva-mahāsattvas had all fully attained the turning of the irreversible wheel of all dharmas, and in one more lifetime would attain unsurpassed, perfect, and complete enlightenment. At that time, there were also immeasurable heavenly beings with their retinues who came to the assembly.

Then the World-Honored One, seated upon his great jeweled throne amidst the great assembly, surveyed the gathered multitude and was about to proclaim the wondrous Dharma. At that time, Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Suthita rose from his seat, approached the World-Honored One, placed his five limbs on the ground in prostration, and said: "World-Honored One! If a good man obtains these sentences of the Wish-Fulfilling Jewel Dhāraṇī and single-mindedly upholds them, yet neither sees, hears, nor encounters the World-Honored Ones of past, present, and future, how can this be? What is the cause of this? May the Tathāgata universally explain it to us one by one, so that all sentient beings of the future will be free from doubt." Having spoken, he stood in respectful attendance.

Then the World-Honored One addressed Bodhisattva Suthita: "Your great skillful means enable you to ask about this matter. Listen carefully, and I shall explain it to you."

The Buddha said: "Suthita! If that good man upholds these sentences of the Wish-Fulfilling Jewel Dhāraṇī, yet regarding the past, present, and future, although he practices beneficial deeds and constantly abides in meritorious karma, he has not yet severed doubt regarding these dhāraṇī sentences. Suthita! Although this is called upholding, it is not truly upholding. Why? Because this person abides in a conditioned mind, lacks skillful wisdom, and is without skillful means.

"If that good man, regarding these dhāraṇī sentences, has a mind free from doubt, with definitive and single-pointed focus, this is called truly upholding. This is called knowing the names of the Dharma teachers of the past, future, and present. This is called seeing the characteristics of the Dharma teachers of the past, future, and present. It is also called seeing the perfectly and universally awakened ones of the past, future, and present. Why? Suthita! Because this person has wondrous understanding of the gate of dhāraṇī. Because, moreover, he does not abide in either belief or disbelief. Because he also understands that there is no going and no coming, no arising and no ceasing, neither emptiness nor non-emptiness, neither auspicious signs nor absence of auspicious signs, neither aspiration nor absence of aspiration. Because, moreover, he does not grasp at such dharmas.

"Suthita! If that good man can practice in this way, accumulating deeds while abiding in non-abiding, this is called abiding, this is attaining nirvāṇa. This is called upholding, serving, and making offerings to these sentences of the Wish-Fulfilling Jewel Dhāraṇī. This is called attaining the meaning of no destination and no destruction. This is called attaining the power of no doubt. This is called attaining the purity of śīla. This is called attaining the equality of all dharmas, ultimately becoming a buddha. He sees that the Dharma being taught is without self and without other, neither conjoined nor unconjoined.

"Suthita! If that good man constantly cultivates this practice, he attains freedom from defilement of self. If he attains freedom from defilement, he also attains freedom from bondage. If he is free from bondage himself, he frees others from bondage. If both self and others are free from bondage, he also does not abide in liberation.

"Suthita! If he is neither defiled nor bound and does not abide in liberation, this is called attaining the Wish-Fulfilling Jewel Dhāraṇī. It is also called, within the Tathāgata's lineage, knowing the supreme light of the buddhas of the past. It is also called knowing the supreme light of the buddhas of the future. It is also called knowing the supreme light of the buddhas of the present.

"Suthita! These sentences of the Wish-Fulfilling Jewel Dhāraṇī are the mother of the buddhas of the past, future, and present. For what reason? Because they abide in benefit for the past, future, and present.

"Suthita! If a bodhisattva-mahāsattva upholds these sentences of the Wish-Fulfilling Jewel Dhāraṇī, one should know that he is called one who has made offerings to and praised countless buddhas, one who has heard the unsurpassed Dharma from countless buddhas, and one who is praised and protected by countless buddhas, for he is born from the immeasurable merit and wisdom of the buddhas.

"Suthita! If one upholds these sentences of the Wish-Fulfilling Jewel Dhāraṇī, whatever one seeks will be obtained without exception: seeking intelligence, one obtains intelligence; seeking dhāraṇī, one obtains dhāraṇī; seeking the seven treasures, one obtains the seven treasures.

"Suthita! If a good man who seeks such things wishes to obtain them, he should, for half a month, purify himself with single-minded resolve, take only milk as food, make great offerings to our Buddha lineage, and then recite these sentences of the Wish-Fulfilling Jewel Dhāraṇī with single-pointed focus without interruption. Whatever he seeks will be obtained without exception.

"Suthita! If one upholds these dhāraṇī sentences, by the majestic power of these dhāraṇī sentences, even if in worlds as numerous as the sands of the Ganges there are devas, nāgas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, kumbhāṇḍas, and other beings including humans and non-humans, even if such beings harbor the most evil intentions, they will be unable to cause disturbance. Even the constellations and Vaināyaka spirits will be unable to cause harm. Why? Because of the protection of the Dharma's power, and because Bodhisattva Bhadraruci, Bodhisattva Mahāvajrapāṇi, and others provide their protection. These bodhisattvas provide constant protection from this Tuṣita Heaven up to the Akaniṣṭha Heaven; how much more so for those who recite it in the human realm!

"Suthita! Not only these bodhisattvas, but the minds of the buddhas also constantly protect and keep in mind such persons. Why? Because they uphold these sentences of the Wish-Fulfilling Jewel Dhāraṇī."

When the World-Honored One had finished proclaiming this Dharma, Bodhisattva Suthita, the countless thousands of bodhisattvas in the assembly, the heavenly beings, and their retinues, having heard the Dharma, were overjoyed and elated, bowed to the Buddha, and withdrew.
