# 佛說鬼問目連經
## Sūtra of Ghosts Questioning Maudgalyāyana, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0734

Translated from the Chinese. Later Han dynasty, translated by An Shigao (安世高)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was dwelling in Rājagṛha at the Kalandaka Bamboo Grove. At that time, in the late afternoon, Maudgalyāyana arose from meditative absorption and wandered along the banks of the Ganges River, where he saw various hungry ghosts suffering different punishments. When the hungry ghosts saw the Venerable Maudgalyāyana, they all gave rise to a mind of respect and came to ask about their karmic causes.

One ghost asked: "Ever since I was born, I have constantly suffered from headaches. What sin has caused this?"

Maudgalyāyana answered: "When you were a human, you enjoyed striking sentient beings on the head with a staff. Now you are receiving the flower of this retribution; its fruit will be in the hells."

One ghost asked: "Ever since I was born, though I possess immeasurable wealth, I am compelled to wear tattered clothing. What sin has caused this?"

Maudgalyāyana answered: "When you were a human, you practiced generosity and created merit, but then regretted and begrudged it. Now you are receiving the flower of this retribution; its fruit will be in the hells."

One ghost asked: "Ever since I was born, I have had no fixed dwelling place and must always rest along alleyways and roadsides. What sin has caused this?"

Maudgalyāyana answered: "When you were a human, when travelers came seeking lodging, you would not give them a place to stay; and when you saw others hosting travelers, you were angry about that as well. Now you are receiving the flower of this retribution; its fruit will be in the hells."

One ghost asked: "I cannot eat even a whole bushel of food and still be satisfied. What sin has caused this?"

Maudgalyāyana answered: "When you were a human, you fed sentient beings but never let them eat their fill. Now you are receiving the flower of this retribution; its fruit will be in the hells."

One ghost asked: "Ever since I was born, my belly has been as large as a jug and my throat as thin as the eye of a needle, so that no food can pass through. What sin has caused this?"

Maudgalyāyana answered: "When you were a human, you were a village headman who relied on your power and authority, looked down upon and cheated the common people, forcibly beat and struck them, and demanded fine food for yourself. Now you are receiving the flower of this retribution; its fruit will be in the hells."

One ghost asked: "Ever since I was born, I have constantly suffered from sores rotting my private parts, with pain beyond words. What sin has caused this?"

Maudgalyāyana answered: "When you were a human, in the pure precincts of a Buddhist temple and monastery, you engaged in sexual misconduct. Now you are receiving the flower of this retribution; its fruit will be in the hells."

One ghost asked: "Ever since I was born, I have had many sons, all handsome and lovable, yet they all died young. Thinking of them breaks my heart; the pain is beyond words. What sin has caused this?"

Maudgalyāyana answered: "When you were a human, you saw your children killing living creatures and you approved with delight, eating their flesh. Because of killing, their lives are short; because of your delight, you suffer this poison of pain. Now you are receiving the flower of this retribution; its fruit will be in the hells."

One ghost asked: "Ever since I was born, there has been a dog with a great body, sharp teeth, and blazing red eyes that constantly comes to devour me. What sin has caused this?"

Maudgalyāyana answered: "When you were a human, you enjoyed taking dogs to hunt, cruelly harming sentient beings without any compassion. Now you are receiving the flower of this retribution; its fruit will be in the hells."

One ghost asked: "Ever since I was born, there has been a person holding various sharp knives who constantly cuts my flesh. When all the flesh is gone he takes it away, but it soon grows back and he comes to cut again. The pain is beyond words. What sin has caused this?"

Maudgalyāyana answered: "When you were a human, you enjoyed slaughtering sentient beings, never with the least compassion. Now you are receiving the flower of this retribution; its fruit will be in the hells."

One ghost asked: "Ever since I was born, I have constantly suffered from pain throughout my entire body that is unbearable. What sin has caused this?"

Maudgalyāyana answered: "When you were a human, you enjoyed fishing. The fish you caught in your nets you threw upon the sand to die in agony. Now you are receiving the flower of this retribution; its fruit will be in the hells."

One ghost asked: "Ever since I was born, I have been dull-witted and without understanding. What sin has caused this?"

Maudgalyāyana answered: "When you were a human, you forcibly urged others to drink wine, causing them to become senseless. Now you are receiving the flower of this retribution; its fruit will be in the hells."

One ghost asked: "Ever since I was born, I have constantly suffered from feverish thirst. Walking along I see the Ganges River and hope to enter it to relieve the fever and thirst. But when I enter, my body is scorched and ruined, with flesh separating from bone. When I try to drink, a single mouthful entering my belly scorches and destroys my five organs. The pain is beyond words. What sin has caused this?"

Maudgalyāyana answered: "When you were a human, you enjoyed setting fire to mountains and marshes, killing sentient beings. Now you are receiving the flower of this retribution; its fruit will be in the hells."

One ghost asked: "Ever since I was born, I have constantly suffered from hunger and thirst. I wish to go to the latrine to eat the excrement there. But at the latrine there is a powerful ghost who beats me with a staff, so I can never get near. What sin has caused this?"

Maudgalyāyana answered: "When you were a human, you were the head of a Buddhist temple. When visiting bhikṣus came, you were stingy and would not give them food. Only after the visitors had left did you distribute food to the resident monks. Because of being stingy with the Saṃgha's property, now you are receiving the flower of this retribution; its fruit will be in the hells."

One ghost asked: "Ever since I was born, I have constantly dwelt in impurity, with stench and foulness enveloping my body, unable to escape. When hungry and thirsty, I eat this very impurity. What sin has caused this?"

Maudgalyāyana answered: "When you were a human, you were the son of a brahmin. A practitioner came one afternoon to your door to beg for food. At that time you devised a scheme so this practitioner would never come begging again. You took his bowl, placed excrement at the bottom, and covered it with rice on top. When the practitioner received the bowl, he returned to his dwelling and set it aside. After he had washed his hands and rinsed his mouth, he took up the bowl intending to eat, but the stench from the bowl was so foul he could not approach it. Because of this, you fell into the hells. In a future life you will fall into the Hell of Excrement."

One ghost asked: "Ever since I was born, I have had a copper vessel on my shoulder, filled with molten copper. One hand holds a copper ladle that scoops from it and pours it over my head. The pain is beyond words. What sin has caused this?"

Maudgalyāyana answered: "When you were a human, you served as the Saṃgha's duty-officer overseeing monastic affairs. There was a jar of ghee that you hid in another place and did not distribute to the Saṃgha. Only after the visitors had left did you distribute it to the resident monks. This ghee was the property of the universally shared Saṃgha, in which all have a share. Because of being stingy with the Saṃgha's property, now you are receiving the flower of this retribution; its fruit will be in the hells."

One ghost asked: "Ever since I was born, I have had to climb mountains of knives and forests of swords, or fall into fiery pits and cauldrons of boiling water, suffering every kind of torment without cease. What sin has caused this?"

Maudgalyāyana answered: "When you were a human, you were a priest in charge of a temple to the heavenly gods. You slaughtered the three kinds of sacrificial animals and offered them to the heavenly spirits, pouring blood and flesh in all four directions. You told the people: 'If you make such sacrifices, you will obtain great fortune and benefit!' By speaking such demonic and perverse words, such deceptive and monstrous speech, you lightly cheated the common people and deluded fathers and mothers. Because of this, its fruit will be in the hells."

One ghost asked: "Ever since I was born, I have constantly been made to swallow iron pellets. What sin has caused this?"

Maudgalyāyana answered: "When you were a human, you were a young temple attendant who drew clean water to make a rock-sugar drink. The piece of rock sugar was large and hard. You stole a small piece by breaking it off; before the Saṃgha had eaten, you stole and ate a mouthful. Because of this cause and condition, its fruit will be in the hells. In a future life you will constantly swallow iron pellets."

At that time Maudgalyāyana, having finished telling the hungry ghosts about the causes and conditions of their past lives, returned to Mount Gṛdhrakūṭa.

The entire great assembly, hearing what the Buddha had taught, bowed their heads and practiced it.
