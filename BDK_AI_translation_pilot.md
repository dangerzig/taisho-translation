# AI-Assisted Translation Pilot for BDK English Tripiṭaka

## 1. Context and Alignment

The BDK English Tripiṭaka represents one of the most important and sustained efforts to make the Buddhist canon accessible in English. The scope and ambition of this work are extraordinary, and its scholarly standards set a global benchmark.

This proposal is intended to support and extend that mission by exploring how recent advances in AI might assist, but not replace, traditional scholarly translation.

---

## 2. The Challenge

Despite decades of progress, a substantial portion of the Chinese Buddhist canon remains untranslated. Current translation efforts are necessarily:

- Time-intensive  
- Dependent on limited scholarly capacity  
- Focused on producing publication-quality editions  

This creates a structural gap between:
- The scale of the canon  
- The pace of translation  

---

## 3. Proposed Approach

Recent work suggests that AI systems can generate high-quality *working drafts* of untranslated texts very quickly.

These drafts are not intended as final translations. Instead, they serve as:

- A starting point for scholars  
- A way to reduce time spent on initial drafting  
- A tool to make more texts accessible for study  

The goal is to support scholars by allowing them to focus on:
- Interpretation  
- Annotation  
- Philological analysis  

---

## 4. Pilot Proposal

A small pilot could explore this approach in a controlled and collaborative way.

### Scope:
- 10–20 untranslated texts  
- Selected in consultation with BDK  

### Process:
- AI-generated draft translation  
- Light human review for clarity and accuracy  
- Delivery in structured, readable format  

### Timeline:
- Several weeks  

---

## 5. Potential Benefits to BDK

This pilot would allow BDK to:

- Evaluate the quality of AI-assisted drafts directly  
- Assess how such drafts might support scholarly workflows  
- Explore whether this approach could extend the reach of existing translation efforts  

All work would be aligned with BDK’s standards and priorities.
