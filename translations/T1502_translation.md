# 菩薩受齋經
## Sūtra on the Bodhisattva's Observance of the Purificatory Fast

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1502

Translated from the Chinese. Western Jin dynasty, translated by Nie Daozhen (聶道真).

---

The rite for the bodhisattva's observance of the purificatory fast states: "I, [name], take refuge in the Buddha, take refuge in the Dharma, take refuge in the Bhikṣu Saṃgha. Whatever evil I, [name], have committed by body, spoken by mouth, or thought by mind, I now cast away and abandon. I, [name], for such-and-such days and such-and-such nights, observe the bodhisattva's purificatory fast, taking refuge in the bodhisattvas. As the sixty thousand bodhisattvas who came before all observed this fast: I am a bodhisattva. As the bodhisattvas who practiced before, Mañjuśrī, Ratnakūṭa, Amita, Dharmika, Maitreya, Avalokiteśvara, Samantabhadradāna, Śūraṅgama, and Pūrvanivāsa Bodhisattva observed this fast: I am a bodhisattva, and I observe the bodhisattva's fast. If I share in generous giving, I shall attain the perfection of giving. If I receive the prediction, I shall attain the perfection of vigor. If I single-mindedly sit in meditation, I shall attain the perfection of meditation. If I expound the sūtras, I shall attain the perfection of prajñā. This is skillful means, and from this one attains the great perfection of prajñā. As when one is mindful of beings in the hells, beings among the hungry ghosts, and beings in the animal realm, causing them to attain liberation and be reborn as humans: from such generous giving, one shall reach the presence of Amitābha Buddha in Sukhāvatī and receive samādhi meditation." This is the rite for the bodhisattva's observance of the purificatory fast.

The Buddha addressed Subhūti: "A bodhisattva has ten contemplations that should be maintained. What are these ten contemplations? One should be mindful of the buddhas of the past: this is the bodhisattva's practice. One should be mindful of the buddhas of the future: this is the bodhisattva's practice. One should be mindful of all the present buddhas of the ten directions: this is the bodhisattva's practice. One should be mindful of the perfection of morality and keeping the precepts: this is the bodhisattva's practice. One should be mindful of the perfection of meditation: this is the bodhisattva's practice. One should be mindful of skillful means: this is the bodhisattva's practice. One should be mindful of the perfection of prajñā: this is the bodhisattva's practice. One should be mindful of the sixty thousand bodhisattvas in meditation-samādhi at the abode of Amitābha Buddha: this is the bodhisattva's practice. One should be mindful of the preceptors and teachers of the past, future, and present: this is the bodhisattva's practice. These are the ten contemplations. If one who has generated the aspiration to seek the bodhisattva path and meditation daily reflects on these ten matters, failure to be mindful of them constitutes impure practice.

"On a bodhisattva's fast day there are ten precepts. First, on a bodhisattva's fast day one must not apply cosmetics, flowers, or fragrances. Second, on a bodhisattva's fast day one must not sing, dance, beat drums, play music, or adorn oneself. Third, on a bodhisattva's fast day one must not sleep on a high bed. Fourth, on a bodhisattva's fast day, after midday one must not eat again. Fifth, on a bodhisattva's fast day one must not handle money, knives, gold, silver, or precious objects. Sixth, on a bodhisattva's fast day one must not ride in carriages or on oxen or horses. Seventh, on a bodhisattva's fast day one must not strike children, servants, or animals. Eighth, all bodhisattvas observe this fast. From sharing in generous giving one obtains merit. I am a bodhisattva. As I am mindful of beings in the hells, beings among the hungry ghosts, and beings in the animal realm, causing them to attain liberation and be reborn as humans: from such generous giving, one shall reach the presence of Amitābha Buddha in Sukhāvatī and receive samādhi meditation. This is the rite for the bodhisattva's breaking of the fast. On a bodhisattva's fast day, before retiring, one should stand before the Buddha with palms joined and say: 'Today, among all beings in the ten directions who observe the fast and precepts, I, [name], rejoice immeasurably. Today, among all who keep the precepts, I, [name], rejoice immeasurably. Today, among all who practice patience and are mindful of the people of the world, I, [name], rejoice immeasurably. Today, among all who practice vigor, I, [name], rejoice immeasurably. Today, among all who expound the sūtras with wisdom, I, [name], rejoice immeasurably. I take this vicarious encouragement and sympathetic joy, this merit of giving, and dedicate it to all people, non-humans, and bodhisattvas of the ten directions, wherever they are in places of toil, hardship, and distress, that all may obtain merit, be liberated from grief and suffering, be reborn as humans, and abide in peace, prosperity, and boundless happiness.' On a bodhisattva's fast day one must not be seen sweeping or cleaning. Ninth, on a bodhisattva's fast day one must not eat or drink everything in one's vessel. Tenth, on a bodhisattva's fast day one must not joke face to face with women or share a seat with them, and likewise for women. These are the ten precepts. One must not violate them, must not cause others to violate them, and must not encourage others to violate them."

The rite for the bodhisattva's breaking of the fast states: "Namo to the Buddha, Namo to the Dharma, Namo to the Bhikṣu Saṃgha! I, [name], for such-and-such days and such-and-such nights, have observed the bodhisattva's fast. From sharing in generous giving, I shall attain the perfection of giving. As I have kept the precepts, I shall attain the perfection of morality. As I have been mindful of all beings in the worlds of the ten directions, wishing them peace, I shall attain the perfection of patience. As I have received the prediction, I shall attain the perfection of vigor. As I have sat in meditation, I shall attain the perfection of meditation. This is skillful means, like the great perfection of prajñā." As the sixty thousand bodhisattvas practiced the bodhisattva fast, on fast days one divides the day and night into three parts: one part for meditation, one part for reading sūtras, and one part for sleeping. This is the rule for the bodhisattva's fast day. On the fourteenth of the first month one begins, and on the seventeenth one breaks. On the eighth of the fourth month one begins, and on the fifteenth one breaks. On the first of the seventh month one begins, and on the sixteenth one breaks. On the fourteenth of the ninth month one begins, and on the sixteenth one breaks.

The above are the dates for the fast days.

I take refuge in Amitābha, the Perfectly Awakened One of the West, and in the great Bodhisattva Mahānāga. May the three poisons be extinguished, and may we be reborn in the honored land.

> The pure and honored divine land
> Rests in peace in the West.
> May I take refuge and devote myself
> To serving the Unsurpassed King.
> His supernatural powers and sagely wisdom
> Illuminate and perceive the thoughts of my heart.
> I take refuge in all the great protectors;
> For a hundred kalpas they do not waver.
