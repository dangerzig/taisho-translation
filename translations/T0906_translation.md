# 佛頂尊勝心破地獄轉業障出三界祕密三身佛果三種悉地真言儀軌
## Ritual Manual of the Secret Mantras of the Three Bodies, Buddha-Fruit, and Three Kinds of Siddhi of the Uṣṇīṣavijaya Heart for Shattering Hell, Transforming Karmic Obstructions, and Exiting the Three Realms

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0906

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Śubhakarasiṃha (善無畏)

---

Mahāvairocana Tathāgata's golden drum speaks. When the mouth opens and the tongue is raised, it shakes the Dharma-realm palace. The Tathāgatas of the Lotus Platform Treasury World emerge from samādhi and, in order to shatter the hells and extinguish the calamities of the seven lives, instruct the bodhisattvas to teach the five-syllable secret. These five syllables are: *a vaṃ raṃ haṃ khaṃ*.

The syllable *a* is the vajra division, governing the liver. The syllable *vaṃ* is the lotus division, governing the lungs. The syllable *raṃ* is the jewel division, governing the heart. The syllable *haṃ* is the karma division, governing the stomach. The syllable *khaṃ* is the space division, governing the spleen. Mountains, seas, and the great earth arise from the syllable *a*. Rivers and ten thousand streams arise from the syllable *vaṃ*. Gold, jade, precious jewels, the sun, moon, stars, fire-pearls, and radiance are accomplished from the syllable *raṃ*. The five grains, five fruits, and all flowers bloom and bear fruit through the syllable *haṃ*. Excellent fragrance, beautiful people, celestial longevity, nourishing complexion, flavor, handsome appearance, merit, wealth, and nobility are adorned through the syllable *khaṃ*. The syllable *a* is Akṣobhya Tathāgata of the east. The syllable *vaṃ* is Amitābha Tathāgata of the west. The syllable *raṃ* is Ratnasaṃbhava Tathāgata of the south. The syllable *haṃ* is Amoghasiddhi Tathāgata of the north. The syllable *khaṃ* is Vairocana Mahā-sūrya Tathāgata of the zenith. The meaning of the syllable *a* is the most profound: the body of empty quiescence. Grasping it, one cannot grasp it; abandoning it, one cannot abandon it. It is the mother of the ten thousand dharmas, the great abhiṣeka king. This syllable *a* is such. It is a Dharma difficult to believe. Hīnayāna vinaya masters in particular should not be shown this text. The Sanskrit originals of the five divisions comprise four hundred thousand words, drawn from the *Vairocana Sūtra* and the *Vajraśekhara Sūtra*, selecting the essential and wondrous. The most supreme field of merit is this five-syllable mantra alone. The merit obtained by one who recites it is beyond comparison, beyond conception, and beyond description. Contemplating and illuminating the nature of principle causes one to acquire blessings: the bones and body become robust, forever free from disasters, obstructions, and all manner of illness, and one's life is nourished and prolonged. If one writes these five syllables on a drum and strikes it so that near and far hear its sound, its blazing power spreads for a thousand *lǐ*; seedlings and crops will be abundantly nourished, and people will be free from plague and pestilence. This is the Tathāgata's contemplation of the nature of non-arising. These five-division mantras are the nectar and rare elixir of all the Tathāgatas' non-arising, the ambrosia of ghee, the wondrous medicine of buddha-nature. When a single syllable enters the five organs, ten thousand diseases will not arise. How much more so when one cultivates the sun contemplation and moon contemplation: in that very moment one realizes the Buddha-body of empty quiescence. These are the five syllables *a vaṃ raṃ haṃ khaṃ*, the Dharma-body mantra. If one recites even a single time daily, or seven times, or twenty-one times, or forty-nine times, comparing the merit: a single recitation equals the merit of chanting all the scriptures one million times. How much more so for one who sits in meditative quiescence and enters the gate of concentration! From the contemplation of the syllable *a*, carefully and clearly like the sun shining in empty space: this is directly seeing the buddha-nature, and the merit obtained is beyond comparison. The passages and phrases of the Secret Treasury are truly inconceivable. I only fear that Hīnayāna Dharma-teachers and vinaya-holders of the lesser vehicle will give rise to doubt and disbelief, which would instead increase their transgressions. It is like a king who has a young child he especially dotes upon: he will spare nothing from his treasury of precious jewels, but the one thing he will not give is the sword Ganjiang and the blade Moye, lest the child, not knowing how to wield them, injure his own body. Therefore the Tathāgata secretly transmits this to great bodhisattvas, and does not give it to śrāvakas of inferior wisdom.

*A*: Vajra Earth Division, first.

*Vaṃ*: Vajra Water Division, second.

*Raṃ*: Vajra Fire Division, third.

*Haṃ*: Vajra Wind Division, fourth.

*Khaṃ*: Vajra Space Division, fifth.

Earth, water, fire, wind, and space: the seeds of the five wheels. *A vaṃ raṃ haṃ khaṃ*. The five syllables have an earth-wheel. Above the earth-wheel is a water-wheel. Above the water-wheel is a fire-wheel. Above the fire-wheel is a wind-wheel. Above the wind-wheel is a space-wheel. Within the space-wheel, visualize the syllable *haṃ*: its color is deep and dark. It gradually expands and takes form. Above the wind-wheel, visualize the syllable *vaṃ*, which transforms into the water-wheel. Above it, visualize the syllable *prā* in golden color, which transforms into a golden turtle. On the turtle's back, visualize the syllable *su*, which transforms into the wondrous Mount Sumeru, composed of four treasures. There is also the syllable *khaḍga*, which transforms into golden mountains encircling seven times. In empty space, visualize Vairocana Buddha's body-pores streaming forth fragrant milk, raining upon the seven golden mountain ranges, forming the eight-virtue fragrant water-milk ocean between them. In the heart, visualize upon the summit of Mount Sumeru the auspicious syllable *hrīḥ*, which transforms into an eight-petaled lotus pervading the Dharma-realm. Upon the lotus is the syllable *a*, which transforms into an eight-flowered, eight-pillared jeweled pavilion, vast and boundless in height and breadth, adorned with every kind of subtle and wondrous jewel. Sixty Ganges-sands of koṭis of Tathāgatas, together with all the devas, dragons, eight divisions, and inner and outer offering-bodhisattvas, surround this Dharma-realm palace. There is also the syllable *hrīḥ* gate, which transforms into a great lotus petal. Upon it is a maṇḍala. Upon the maṇḍala is a lion-throne. Upon the lion-throne is a Lotus King. Above is a pure full moon-disc. Upon the full moon-disc is the auspicious syllable *hrīḥ*, which transforms into the Wondrous Moon Great Lotus. Above it is the syllable *vaṃ*, radiating great light that universally illuminates the Dharma-realm. All sentient beings of the three realms, six destinies, four modes of birth, and eight difficulties who encounter this light and are touched by it attain liberation. This syllable *vaṃ* transforms into a stūpa, with square, round, triangular, half-moon, and spherical forms, because it is composed of the five great elements: earth, water, fire, wind, and space.

This stūpa transforms into Mahāvairocana Tathāgata, whose body-color is like the moon. Upon his crown he wears a five-buddha coronet. His body is adorned with wondrous gauze and celestial garments and jeweled necklaces. His radiance universally illuminates the Dharma-realm in the ten directions. All lean upon the moon-disc. The four Buddhas, four pāramitā bodhisattvas, sixteen bodhisattvas, eight offering bodhisattvas, four summoning bodhisattvas, the thousand Buddhas of the Bhadrakalpa, twenty devas, and boundless, limitless bodhisattvas form the retinue. The four Buddhas are: Akṣobhya Buddha of the vajra-firm self-nature body; Ratnasaṃbhava Buddha of the merit-adorned body; Amitābha Buddha of the enjoyment-wisdom body; and Śākyamuni Buddha of the transformation body. The four bodhisattvas are: Vajra-pāramitā Bodhisattva, Ratna-pāramitā Bodhisattva, Dharma-pāramitā Bodhisattva, and Karma-pāramitā Bodhisattva. The sixteen bodhisattvas are: Vajrasattva, Vajrarāja, Vajrarāga, Vajrasādhu, Vajraratna, Vajratejas, Vajraketu, Vajrahāsa, Vajradharma, Vajratīkṣṇa, Vajrahetu, Vajrabhāṣa, Vajrakarma, Vajrarakṣa, Vajrayakṣa, and Vajramuṣṭi. The eight offering bodhisattvas are: Vajralāsyā, Vajramālā, Vajragītā, Vajranṛtyā, Vajradhūpā, Vajrapuṣpā, Vajrālokā, and Vajragandhā. The four summoning bodhisattvas are: Vajrāṅkuśa, Vajrapāśa, Vajrasphoṭa, and Vajraghaṇṭā. Having thus contemplated with such a retinue, through the realization of a purified mind, one's own mind is the Buddha, with all marks fully complete. One then realizes sarvajña and is endowed with the thirty-seven holy and complete deities. Then contemplate in empty space: all the Buddhas, like sesame seeds, pervade the realm of space. Visualize one's body realizing the ten stages. The syllable *vaṃ* transforms into the water of great compassion. Prepare to scatter and sprinkle it upon myself and all sentient beings upon the great earth of the bodhicitta, washing clean the defilements and idle discourse of the one hundred and sixty minds. All afflictions and karmic defilements are completely severed. Without discarding the body born of one's father and mother, in this very body one realizes the great bodhi and attains the rank of buddha-fruit.

Furthermore, contemplate within one's own body a great ocean. At its bottom is the syllable *prā*, golden in color. This syllable transforms into a golden turtle, which is the buddha-nature. Upon the turtle is the syllable *su*, which transforms into Mount Sumeru. Upon the mountain is the syllable *a*, which transforms into a multicolored, wondrous vajra earth-wheel. Upon the wheel is a practice-site of thirty-eight cubits. The syllable *aṃ* transforms into a three-layered maṇi-jewel palace, which is the desire realm, form realm, and formless realm, adorned with the seven treasures. Within the wondrous palace is a ten-cubit altar, which is the ten Dharma-realms. In the center of the altar is the great enlightened one's lion-throne. In the center is the syllable *a*, which transforms into a four-cubit gem-stone, which is the layered maṇḍala. The layering represents: generating the mind, practicing, bodhi, and nirvāṇa. Upon it is a great white lotus. Upon the lotus is the syllable *a*, which transforms into the Dharma-body Mahāvairocana Tathāgata, who teaches *a vaṃ raṃ haṃ khaṃ*. These five syllables transform into the five wisdom Tathāgatas. They further become the eight-petal nine-deity forms. They further become the five great wisdom-king forms. Mahāvairocana transforms into the syllable *haṃ*; the syllable transforms into a sword; the sword transforms into the body of Acala Vidyārāja. The Vidyārāja transforms into the great dragon Kulikā, manifesting a wrathful form coiling around the sharp sword. The Dragon King transforms into two attendant messengers: the messenger Kiṃkara and the messenger Ceṭaka.

These five syllables are called the Secret Siddhi, also called the Accomplishment Siddhi, also called Susiddhi. Susiddhi means: pervading the Dharma-realm, accomplishing the buddha-fruit, and realizing the great bodhi. The secret words of the Dharma-realm are filled with radiance. Only a Buddha together with a Buddha can enter this gate. Pratyekabuddhas and śrāvakas cannot illuminate it. This is also called the Secret Siddhi. If one recites it a single time, it is equivalent to chanting all the scriptures one million times. The Secret Siddhi proceeds from the heart to the crown. The Secret Siddhi is the accomplishment of the Dharma-body, which is the true Dharma-treasury of the three kinds of eternal body. Therefore, with bowed head, I pay homage to Vairocana Buddha:

> Homage to Vairocana Buddha, whose pure eyes open like lotuses,
> Teacher of devas and humans, sovereign ruler of the three realms,
> Savior of the world with the great bodhicitta,
> Whose profound and wondrous mantra and empowerment Dharma
> Flows into the gate of the unborn syllable *a*.

The syllable *a* is like the amalavijñāna, the immaculate consciousness. The essence of the amalavijñāna is the ālayavijñāna. The use of the syllable *a* contains the ten thousand dharmas, just as the store-consciousness contains all dharmas. Therefore, "Vairocana" in four syllables contains the meaning of the four teachings, and the nine layers of moon-discs represent the eight-petal nine deities.

The lower-grade siddhi of Mañjuśrī's mantra:

"*A ra pa ca na*." The single syllable "*oṃ cirim*" is universally applied to all matters. This is called the Exiting Siddhi. It can produce roots and stems pervading the four directions. Reciting it once is like chanting the complete canon one hundred times. The Exiting Siddhi proceeds from the feet to the waist.

"*A vi ra hūṃ kha*," the mantra of Mahāvairocana Tathāgata: the middle-grade siddhi. This is called the Entering Siddhi. It can produce branches and leaves. Furthermore, the Entering Siddhi proceeds from the navel to the heart, pervading the four directions with blazing radiance, entering the Buddha's Dharma-realm. This is called the Entering Siddhi. If one recites it once, it is like chanting the complete canon one thousand times.

"*A vaṃ raṃ haṃ khaṃ*," the mantra of Vairocana. These five syllables are called the Secret Siddhi, also called Susiddhi, also called the Accomplishment Siddhi. The Secret Siddhi proceeds from the heart to the crown. Susiddhi is the accomplishment of the Dharma-body. Furthermore, the Accomplishment Siddhi accomplishes the buddha-fruit and realizes the great bodhi. The secret words of the Dharma-realm are filled with radiance. Only a Buddha together with a Buddha can enter this gate. Pratyekabuddhas and śrāvakas cannot illuminate it. This is called the Secret Siddhi. If one recites it once, it is equivalent to chanting all the scriptures one million times.

Such are the three kinds of siddhi mantras. Although they are the mantras of the Uṣṇīṣavijaya Heart, they are all mantras of the three bodies of Mahāvairocana Tathāgata. From this one should know: the Uṣṇīṣavijaya Buddha-uṣṇīṣa is none other than the body of Vairocana Tathāgata, and is the three-division buddha-uṣṇīṣa body.

The three consciousnesses: first, *padana*, the sixth consciousness; second, ādānavijñāna, the seventh consciousness; third, ālayavijñāna, the eighth consciousness. Now adding a fourth, the amala immaculate pure consciousness, this becomes the meaning of the nine-layered heart-moon-disc. The Exiting Siddhi, Entering Siddhi, and Accomplishment Siddhi all flow into the syllable *a*; one should practice the three grades of contemplation. The upper grade: one's body is identical with the body of the great-thousand world, identical with the Dharma-body Mahāvairocana Tathāgata. Within the great-thousand world, for all sentient beings, one teaches the eighty-four thousand Dharma-treasury scriptures, wishing all to simultaneously attain Buddhahood. The middle grade: contemplate that my body equals the middle-thousand world, identical with the response-body Mahāvairocana Tathāgata. Within the middle-thousand world, for all sentient beings, one teaches the eighty-four thousand Dharma-treasury scriptures, wishing all to simultaneously attain Buddhahood. The lower grade: contemplate that my body equals the small-thousand world, identical with the transformation-body Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva. Within the small-thousand world, for all sentient beings, one teaches the eighty-four thousand treasury scriptures, wishing all to simultaneously attain Buddhahood.

But these three kinds of mantras are the secret within the secret, the most esoteric within the esoteric. Those of the two vehicles, violators of the precepts, and assemblies without faith cannot enter this gate. Bodhisattvas and assemblies of faith may recite them day and night, and will surely attain anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi. Throughout immeasurable and innumerable kalpas, they can rescue and liberate all suffering sentient beings of the six destinies, causing them all to enter this syllable *a*, sever the afflictive karmic defilements of immeasurable and innumerable kalpas, generate the unsurpassed bodhicitta, and all realize the buddha-fruit. If there is a person of the most superior faculties who constantly maintains recitation in the three daily periods, recollecting at every moment, this person will certainly, without discarding the body born of father and mother, in this present body attain the inconceivable and rarely attained Buddha-body.

If anyone harbors indeterminate doubt about this teaching, for immeasurable, inconceivable Ganges-sands of innumerable great kalpas, they will fall into the Avīci Hell. Should they finally exhaust the kalpas of that hell and emerge, they will then enter the eighteen hells and the eighty-four thousand hells. Thus, transmigrating through each, they will each undergo the full duration of all those kalpas. They will then fall into the hungry ghost realm and the animal realm, each enduring the full extent of those kalpas. When those kalpas are exhausted and they are born in the human realm, because of their disbelief in the Dharma, they will be destitute and suffer extreme poverty, afflicted with innumerable grave illnesses throughout their entire life, day and night without cessation. After their life ends, they will again fall into the Avīci Hell. Therefore they constantly undergo great suffering and are never born in favorable destinies. For this reason, one must not teach these three mantras of the three-body buddha-fruit and three-bodhi medicine to those who do not believe.

If someone hears this mantra even once, in their past lives through countless generations they have planted immeasurable roots of goodness. It is by the power of those past roots of goodness that they are now able to hear it. Such a person, at the end of their life, will certainly be reborn in accordance with their vows in the pure lands of the ten directions. For the beings of that land and for all sentient beings of the three-thousand great-thousand world, they will teach this Dharma, extracting suffering and bestowing happiness, causing all to realize the great bodhi-fruit. Know that such a person is one transformed by the Tathāgata; this is a bodhisattva. How much more so for one who personally recites and transmits the teaching to others: the merit is inconceivable. If each, with pure and faithful mind, completes five hundred thousand recitations, the immeasurable evil karmic transgressions accumulated since beginningless time throughout innumerable kalpas within one's body will be eternally extinguished. After the end of one's life, in accordance with one's vows, one will be born in the Lotus Platform Treasury World. One will constantly abide in the primordial awakening of the syllable *a*, the original Buddha, and behold Vairocana Tathāgata's wondrous body. Constantly in that world, one will receive the bliss of the Dharma. This is what Vairocana Tathāgata spoke with his golden mouth. The five wisdom Tathāgatas arise from within the syllable *a*, transforming into immeasurable bodies. One who in the past heard even the single syllable *a* is now Vairocana Tathāgata.

> The white ūrṇā-mark of the Samyak-saṃbuddha, fully illuminating like the sun and moon.
> Akṣobhya, Ratnasaṃbhava, Savior of the World, Amitābha, Amoghasiddhi, the Unfailing King.
> All upon the siddhi auspicious wheel, transmitting this wondrous Dharma to transform all realms of existence.
> The compassionate mind, sovereign in subjugation of the three worlds: Vajrasattva, Acala,
> Whose pledged vows are never violated, responding to the proper time. When the yoga rites are completed, they return to the vajra.
> I rely upon Vairocana Buddha to open the heart-wisdom seal and establish this standard ritual.
> Immeasurable merit universally adorns all, entering together into the dhāraṇī of all the Sugatas.
> May all those with karmic affinity who study and practice abide in the unsurpassed ocean of purity.

## Dharma for Shattering Hell with the Uṣṇīṣavijaya Heart, One Fascicle

Colophon: The inner colophon note states: "In the spring, second month, of the second year of Enpō, the year *kinoetora* [1674 CE], written at the Sōji-kyō-ji temple on the northern shore of the lake, by the monk Chōnen. On the tenth day of the eighth month of the fifth year of Tenmei, the year *kinotomi* [1785 CE], copied from Master Chōnen's exemplar. The monk Jinin of Bushū." In the autumn, seventh month, of the first year of Kyōwa [1801 CE], these copies were sought at Chishaku-in in eastern Kyoto. The monks of Aizen-in at the mountain collated and added punctuation and vernacular glosses. The above two texts on the Uṣṇīṣavijaya shattering-hell are variant editions of the three previously printed works on the Uṣṇīṣavijaya shattering-hell and the three-siddhi ritual. They are now re-collated and carved in woodblock, awaiting comparison with better copies. Recorded by Kaidō of Sōji-in, Toyoyama.

The five organs:

The liver: red lotus.

The lungs: blue lotus.

The heart: yellow lotus.

The kidneys: crimson lotus.

The spleen: white lotus.

The liver's seed-syllable: five-pronged vajra.

The heart's seed-syllable: cintāmaṇi jewel.

The lungs' seed-syllable: vajra upon a lotus.

The kidneys' seed-syllable: crossed vajra.

The spleen's seed-syllable: golden stūpa.

Central, the stomach: the wisdom of the great perfect mirror. The heart: the wisdom of the equality of nature. The lungs: the wisdom of wondrous contemplation. The kidneys: the wisdom of accomplishing what is to be done. The gallbladder: Trailokyavijaya. The large intestine: Kuṇḍalī. The bladder: Yamāntaka. The small intestine: Vajrayakṣa. The stomach: Acala. The triple burner: Samantabhadra. The six bowels are: gallbladder, small intestine, stomach, bladder, and triple burner.
