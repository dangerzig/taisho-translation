# 佛說堅意經
## Sūtra of Firm Resolve, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0733

Translated from the Chinese. Later Han dynasty, translated by An Shigao (安世高)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was in the land of Śrāvastī, in the Jeta Grove of Anāthapiṇḍada.

The Buddha told Ānanda: "I am now in meditative absorption, feeling compassion for the people of the world who do not know that the Buddha's path is right, true, vast, and profound. With shallow, false, and frivolous words they wish to give rise to jealousy and slander the root of the path, making baseless and extreme challenges to trouble my disciples. You should set your mind aright and know that such transgressors are either perverse, demonic, and wicked teachers, or ignorant and crafty worldly people. If any bodhisattvas, bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, or upāsikās who are learned in the sūtras, lofty and pure, open the understanding of the ignorant and benighted, and teach them about the retribution of merit and sin in the cycle of birth and death, and if those people immediately understand and know to submit to the truth of the path, then they are people whose sins are extinguished and whose merit is born. But if one points and demonstrates as if building a bridge for them, yet their minds are confused and agitated and they do not wish to listen, and though they try to force themselves to listen their minds are drowsy; or if they destroy the Dharma, disparage śramaṇas and upāsakas, and speak with foul mouths and false words, then know clearly that such people are dragged along by their sins. The śramaṇa, the worthy one, takes patience as foremost. One should be like clear water, which purifies all things: dead people, dead dogs, dead snakes, excrement, and urine it washes as well, yet the purity of the water is not impaired. One should also hold one's mind like a broom sweeping the ground: clean or unclean, dead people, dead dogs, dead snakes, excrement, and urine it sweeps as well, yet the broom is not impaired. One should also be like the power and radiance of wind and fire: dead people, dead dogs, dead snakes, excrement, and urine the wind blows and the fire burns, yet the power and radiance of wind and fire are not impaired. If someone comes wishing to kill you, do not become angry. If someone comes wishing to slander you, do not become angry. If someone comes wishing to accuse you falsely, do not become angry. If someone comes wishing to mock you, do not become angry. If someone comes wishing to ruin you and prevent you from serving the Buddha-Dharma, do not become angry. Simply maintain a compassionate mind and right intention: sins will be extinguished and merit will be born. The crooked cannot enter into the straight, and ten thousand evils will disintegrate and dissolve."

The Buddha told Ānanda: "If there are people of good mind and wholesome intention who hear the Buddha's clear Dharma and listen with a concentrated mind, even for one day, that is good. If they cannot manage one day, half a day is good. If they cannot manage half a day, one period is good. If they cannot manage one period, half a period is good. If they cannot manage half a period, even a moment is good. The merit of this is immeasurable and incalculable.

"You should broadly teach this to all the bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, upāsikās, and lay people, and should further broadly teach them about generosity, planting provisions for the journey through birth and death. On days of fasting, let food and drink be prepared, and invite the worthy and learned of the four groups, the śramaṇas and practitioners, to sit upon high seats and discourse on the Buddha's sūtras. Burn incense and light lamps, and let the radiance reach the heavens. The devas will rejoice and laugh, and all will descend through empty space, inclining their ears to listen, none without delight. If there are those who break through their miserliness, practicing generosity to create merit, the good spirits will immediately descend to protect their households, warding off calamity and dispelling misfortune. Going forth they will encounter auspicious meetings; their fortunes will be auspicious, and they will never encounter resentment or evil. It is like planting grain: as one plants, so it grows. Planting good, one reaps merit; planting evil, one reaps calamity. There has never been anyone who reaped the fruit without planting the seed. Set your minds aright, and merit will naturally come to you. Be careful not to consult diviners, lest you be dragged along by the perverse. If the mind harbors doubt and suspicion, the good spirits will distance themselves, and one will constantly fall into the realm of sin; nothing one does will succeed. Not knowing that one has violated the precepts, one blames the Buddha and the spirits instead, worshipping them to no benefit, and never correcting one's mind. Any man or woman who hears this sūtra, and who receives, upholds, reads, and recites it, will without exception receive merit."

When the Buddha had spoken this sūtra, Ānanda was filled with joy and rose to bow to the Buddha.
