# 使呪法經
## Sūtra of the Method of Employing Spells

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1267

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Bodhiruci (菩提留支).

---

At that time, Vināyaka, on Mount Kailāsa, assembled all the great hosts: Great Brahmā, Maheśvara, Śakra, and innumerable koṭis of ghosts and spirits. Rising from his seat, he bowed in homage to Maheśvara and requested: "I now wish to proclaim a one-syllable spell to benefit sentient beings. May you grant your approval and hear what I say." The devas said: "Excellent! As you say." Vināyaka, given permission to speak, was joyful and elated, and he spoke the Vināyaka one-syllable spell:

*Oṃ jah ga-hrīḥ oṃ hah hūṃ phaṭ*

"If one wishes to perform this method, one must first make an image. White pewter, gold, silver, copper, or birchwood may be used; each should be carved into the image's form. The husband and wife in two bodies are harmoniously embracing while standing, both having elephant heads and human bodies. The price for making the image must not be haggled over. When the image is complete, on the first day of the bright fortnight, in a pure room, plaster a round altar with pure cow dung, of whatever size desired. Take one *sheng* of sesame oil and recite the above spell over the pure oil one hundred and eight times, then warm the oil. Place the image in a clean copper vessel filled with the warm oil, and install it within the altar. Using a clean copper spoon or ladle, pour the oil over the bodies and crowns of both images one hundred and eight times. From the following day onward, recite the spell over the previous oil one hundred and eight times. During the day, pour the oil seven times: four times at dawn, three times at noon, making seven in all. Perform this method continuously for seven days, and whatever one's heart wishes will be accomplished as desired. During the pouring of the oil, repeatedly make aspirations. Use ghee and honey mixed with roasted flour to make balls, and offer radish root and a cup of vinegar-wine. Each day present fresh offerings of food. The offerings must be consumed by the practitioner for one to receive their potency."

At that time, Vināranāga, leading nine thousand eight hundred great ghost kings, roamed throughout the three-thousand worlds. "We exercise supernatural power and sovereignty, traversing all directions to guard and protect the Three Jewels, benefiting sentient beings with great compassion." Facing the World-Honored One, they spoke in unison: "By my sovereign supernatural power, I am called Vināranāga. I am also named Vināyaka, also Vivīvanānaga, and also Mahā-Vināyaka. Thus in the four quarters beneath heaven, the names differ. In the transcendent world I have yet another name." Then by supernatural transformation he rose into space and spoke this verse:

> "I possess a wondrous and subtle dharma,
> Extremely rare in the world.
> Those sentient beings who uphold it
> Will all have their wishes fulfilled.
> I act in accord with worldly ways,
> Manifesting rare occurrences in the world.
> I can follow their every wish.
> If one seeks fame or promotion,
> I cause the king to summon them.
> If one seeks rare worldly treasures,
> I cause the world to amass precious wealth,
> The household abundant in the seven treasures,
> All that the world considers rare.
> If one seeks beautiful form,
> Upon making vows it arrives at once.
> Speak not of far or near,
> High rank or difficulty;
> For those who devote their minds to me,
> I accomplish it in an instant.
> If sentient beings suffer illness,
> Madness, scabies, or leprosy,
> Poisons and all adversities,
> A hundred kinds of harmful afflictions:
> By reciting my dhāraṇī
> There is none who will not be liberated.
> Walking alone in dark and gloomy places,
> Relying on me, all are without fear.
> If bandits suddenly attack,
> I cause them all to bind themselves.
> If one wishes for natural blessings,
> I will certainly cause mutual love.
> Those who oppress and bully in the world,
> I cause them all to be subdued.
> Wandering carefree in joyful ease,
> Lacking nothing whatsoever,
> Every thought will be fulfilled,
> And whatever one has will be satisfying.
> If a host of evils comes to invade,
> I cause things to accord with one's will.
> I am able to guard and protect all:
> Dwellings will all be auspicious,
> Homes will be peaceful and tranquil,
> Sons and daughters will earn renown,
> Husband and wife will be harmonious.
> Those who uphold me at the highest level,
> I grant them sovereignty among humans.
> Those who uphold me at the middle level,
> I make them teachers of emperors.
> Those who uphold me at the lowest level
> Will enjoy wealth and honor without end,
> Always wishing to delight and entertain,
> With nothing that is not fully satisfied.
> Servants and maids arranged in groups,
> Beautiful women filling the courtyards.
> Roaming freely with mastery,
> Appearing and hiding as one wishes,
> Going out and entering without obstruction,
> Unfathomable to any measure.
> Within the three realms,
> I have attained supernatural sovereignty.
> Descending into the world, the rare things I do
> Are all accomplished by me.
> If I were to tell of all I can do,
> Exhausting all kalpas would not suffice.
> Those who uphold my dhāraṇī,
> I will always appear before them.
> Husband, wife, and all their retinue
> I shall follow and protect.
> When I am away on my travels,
> Recite my name and I arrive at once.
> Through perilous and dangerous places,
> Great oceans and rivers,
> Deep mountains and narrow passes,
> Lions, elephants, tigers, and wolves,
> Venomous insects and spirit-perils:
> Upholding me, all find safety.
> If anyone invades or torments them,
> Their heads will split into seven pieces.
> Lifespans will all be long,
> And blessings and fortune will come of their own."

At that time, Vināyaka, having spoken this verse, told the worldly people: "The dhāraṇī dharma I have proclaimed for abiding in the world supremely protects sentient beings, and all their wishes will be fulfilled. One should recite and uphold it day and night to a full one hundred thousand or even two hundred thousand times, and all will be as described." Then rising into space, he spoke the spell:

*Namo vināyaka-śya asti-mukha-śya tadyathā ajīya-najīya-śu-bha-tīya usta-ṃkāya siva-tha-paya-bhadha-sa-śya-ya bhari-pacī svāhā*
