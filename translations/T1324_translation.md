# 能淨一切眼疾病陀羅尼經
## Dhāraṇī Sūtra for Purifying All Eye Diseases

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1324

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Bhagavat was dwelling in Kapilavastu, in a settlement of the Śākya clan. At that time there was a certain Śākya who lived in the settlement of Carṇimākā. He had pure faith in the Buddha, pure faith in the Dharma, and pure faith in the Saṃgha. He had taken refuge in the Buddha, taken refuge in the Dharma, and taken refuge in the Saṃgha. He had no doubts about the Buddha, no doubts about the Dharma, and no doubts about the Saṃgha. He was wholeheartedly devoted to the Buddha, the Dharma, and the Saṃgha. He was determined upon the supreme destiny of perfect enlightenment. Yet his eyes, though able to perceive forms and appearances, could not see.

At that time Kṣemākara the Śākya called to mind the Tathāgata and spoke these words: "Homage to the Buddha, the World-Honored One! To the Torch of Wisdom! To the Dhāraṇī that creates light! I take refuge in the Sugata, the Great Compassionate One! May you protect, care for, and embrace me, and cause my eyes to become pure."

At that time the World-Honored One, transcending the ears and eyes of the world, heard with the divine ear and saw with the divine eye. Then the World-Honored One addressed Ānanda: "Go to where the Śākya is. Use this dhāraṇī-vidyā to empower him, to purify his eyes, to rescue him, to embrace him, to nourish him, to seal the boundary around him, to remove the impurities and cataracts from his eyes, and to free him from disease. Broadly disseminate this to the fourfold assembly of bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, upāsikās, and other sentient beings. The mantra states:

*Tadyathā: hiri miri līki hiri kete huyu huyu huyā-māni huru huru nuru nuru svāhā.*

"Ānanda! This dhāraṇī-vidyā-rāja: for eye impurities, wind-impurities, jaundice, phlegm-disease, and the diseases arising from the conjunction of these three, may my eyes and those of so-and-so not suffer pain, and may they not shed tears. By the truth of the words of the arhats, by the truth of the words of the precepts and restraints, by the truth of the words of austere practices, by the truth of the words of the sages, by the truth of the words of dependent arising, the truth of suffering, the truth of arising, the truth of cessation, the truth of the path, and the truth of the words of the pratyekabuddhas: may the eyes of myself, so-and-so, be purified. The seven buddhas of perfect enlightenment have spoken this. I, Śākyamuni, Worthy of Offerings, Perfectly and Truly Awakened, now speak it. The Four Great Heavenly Kings also speak it. Śakra Devendra also speaks it. The lord of the Sahā world, Brahmā King, also speaks it. Ānanda! I see no one in the worlds of devas, māras, śramaṇas, or brahmins who, holding this Eye-Purifying Dhāraṇī, with eye cataracts, floating films, or dimness of vision caused by devas, nāgas, yakṣas, rākṣasas, rākṣasīs, piśācīs, kumbhāṇḍas, kumbhāṇḍa women, vetāla ghosts, human sorcery, or brahmin sorcery, would dare to defy it, or for whom it would fail to be effective. Venerable Ānanda! Receive this dhāraṇī now, go to the Śākya settlement, and give it to Kṣemākara. Convey my words: let him recite and uphold this dhāraṇī three times during the day and three times during the night." Then Ānanda went there and gave it to Kṣemākara. As soon as Kṣemākara heard this dhāraṇī, the channels of his eyes were purified, his eyes could see, and he was freed from all impurities.

At that time the World-Honored One, having spoken this sūtra, the devas, humans, asuras, gandharvas, and all the rest, hearing what the Buddha taught, joyfully received it and put it into practice.
