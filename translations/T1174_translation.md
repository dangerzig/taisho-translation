# 五字陀羅尼頌
## Verses on the Five-Syllable Dhāraṇī

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1174

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

> Among the hundred-thousand yogas, the Vajra Great Master taught
> The holy Mañjuśrī Youth's five-syllable secret method.

> Those who cultivate this samādhi swiftly enter all buddhas' wisdom
> And, with an ordinary person's body, visibly achieve the body of a buddha.

> This method is the most secret; the Great Master transmits it orally.
> One should don the armor of diligence: rely on the Dharma, not on persons.

> The Tathāgata's Dharma is nondual, attained by those of pure faith,
> Just as when rain falls universally, moist soil is first to grow.
> The World-Honored One's secret teaching: the wise should cultivate it.

> Beings, narrow and weak by nature, stray into the suffering of the three realms.
> Though they hear the supreme teaching, they do not generate a courageous mind.
> The wise, generating compassion, seek awakening for their sake.

> It is as though near a mountain of jewels: the wise go to gather them,
> While the foolish, knowing this, do not go, and endure suffering all their days.

> If any hear this Dharma and thus know the supreme path,
> Those who dwell in great vows: such persons are fit to learn it.

> If any hear this Dharma and tremble with deep sorrow and joy,
> Weeping, their body-hairs standing on end: such persons are fit to learn it.

> If any hear this Dharma and their single mind is undisturbed,
> All faculties pure and delighted: such persons are fit to learn it.

> If any hear this Dharma and directly taste the flavor of meditation's joy,
> And no longer delight in worldly pleasures: such persons are fit to learn it.

> If any hear this Dharma and do not wait for a special time or day,
> And do not seek elaborate preparations, but with the mind alone press straight ahead,
> And regarding various offering implements do not create the suffering of seeking without finding,
> But skillfully understand Dharma-offering: such persons are fit to learn it.

> When reciting and practicing the mantra, it is like tasting celestial ambrosia.
> Singlemindedly one must wish to hear it: such persons are fit to learn it.

> Forming the secret mudrā with both hands, generating a thought of great earnestness,
> As if lifting Mount Sumeru: such persons are fit to learn it.

> This Dharma was taught by all buddhas for those of the supreme vehicle.
> Those of inferior and weak faculties will doubt and be unable to believe.

> Because all beings are pure by nature, by the original vow-power of all buddhas,
> Through the corresponding dharma-mudrā, one manifests as all holy forms.
> Right there in a single sitting, one achieves the most perfect enlightenment.
> Those who follow this Dharma should generate faith in this way.

> If even a single thought arises saying "I am an ordinary being,"
> This is the same as slandering the buddhas of the three times: one incurs grave sin within the Dharma.

> Those who have not received the abhiṣeka rank, and those who are not fellow practitioners,
> Should not be told of it recklessly: guard it like the jewel in the topknot.

> As the sūtras describe the place: perhaps in a forest hermitage,
> By a river, a pond, or an ocean shore, on a pure and renowned mountain peak,
> Where one has attained the path, turned the Dharma wheel, or where sages achieved accomplishment.
> Choose the ground and build a shrine, adapting to the environment.
> Coat the ground smooth and level, and scatter seasonal flowers.

> The supporting disciplines and rules are as described in the various traditions.
> Purify the body with a bath, put on fine garments,
> Apply fragrant paste for adornment, and approach the door of the shrine.

> First visualize one's own form as Vajrasattva:
> Left hand holding a vajra, right hand holding a luminous bell.
> At the threshold, intone the syllable *hūṃ*; with wrathful eyes, ward off all inauspiciousness.

> Then prostrate with the five limbs and reverently bow at the World-Honored One's feet.
> Singlemindedly take refuge, visualizing the Buddha as eternally abiding.

> Next, kneel with both knees and confess all transgressions.
> With these pure phrases, earnestly perform repentance.

*Oṃ svabhāva-śuddha sarva-dharma svabhāva-śuddho 'haṃ.*

> Silently recite once and pronounce the syllable *a*.
> Immeasurable accumulated sins are purified without remainder.

> Next, with a mind of devoted invitation, form the Vajra Arising mudrā.
> Silently recite this mantra to summon the buddhas of the ten directions.
> Both hands in Vajra Fists, hook the charity-wisdom fingers,
> The progress-power tips touching, at the heart face upward and beckon three times.
> Then know that all tathāgatas have arisen from samādhi.

*Oṃ vajra tiṣṭha.*

> One should contemplate throughout space the buddhas and the holy assembly,
> Filling the ocean of the dharma-realm with no gaps between them.
> All, by the power of their vows, come and descend into the practice site.
> Form and hold the Vajra mudrā, visualizing prostration at all buddhas' feet.
> Both hands back-to-back, hook the charity-wisdom and concentration-knowledge fingers.
> Visualize bowing to all tathāgatas, kneeling, then scatter above the crown.

*Oṃ vajra hūṃ.*

> There are four kinds of seated posture; use them as appropriate.
> Sit upright, settle the joints, in lotus posture upon a pure moon disc.

> Then with the syllable *māṭha*, the two eyes become sun and moon,
> Emitting vajra flames, gazing upon all the tathāgatas.
> Next, turning to survey all directions, burn away all who create obstacles.
> From the heart, tongue, and both hands, the syllable *hūṃ* sends forth golden light,
> Just as all tathāgatas manifest the sublime marks of teaching the Dharma.

> Next, facing the buddhas of the ten directions, form the Great Vow Mudrā.
> The ten fingers in Vajra Bond, raise the twin patience-vow peaks,
> Showing all buddhas and holy ones, entreating them to remember their former vows.

*Oṃ samaya sattvaṃ.*

> Next form the Joy Mudrā, offering this samādhi-delight.
> The ten fingers interlaced outwardly, the patience-vow fingers crossed within,
> The charity-wisdom and concentration-knowledge fingers each paired and raised.

*Oṃ samaya hoḥ surata sattvaṃ.*

> Visualize upon the two breasts: *trāṭ* on the right, *ṭa* on the left,
> Like the two wings of a palace door. Recite this secret phrase,
> Then with the vajra bond, strike three times to open them.

*Oṃ vajra bandha trāṭ.*

> Visualize before you an eight-petaled lotus, with the syllable *a* in pale light.
> Both hands in Vajra Bond, concentration-knowledge fingers within.
> Recite this secret mantra; the syllable flows into the palace of the heart.

*Oṃ vajra āveśa aḥ.*

> With the previous entry mudrā, bend the progress-power fingers to press down.
> With this, close the heart-gate; the wisdom syllable is made firm.

*Oṃ vajra muṣṭi vaṃ.*

> Next form the Trailokyavijaya and abide in the Wrathful samādhi.
> Those who wish to perform this method must first dwell in great compassion.
> Both hands in Vajra Fists, the charity-wisdom fingers hooking in reverse,
> The progress-power fingers raised and spread, dwelling in a scolding posture,
> Brows furrowed, laughing yet wrathful, four *hūṃ*s like the sound of thunder.
> Visualize the Guhyapati assembly receiving the teaching and standing in attendance.
> Rotate left to accomplish warding; rotate right to accomplish the boundary.

*Oṃ sumbhani sumbhani hūṃ gṛhṇa gṛhṇa hūṃ gṛhṇapaya hūṃ ānaya hoḥ bhagavan vajra hūṃ phaṭ.*

> Next form the Samādhi mudrā; the practitioner abides in samādhi.
> Both hands interlaced outwardly, placed face-up upon the crossed legs.
> Sit upright, close mouth and teeth, count the breaths to settle the mind.
> The tathāgatas previously invited, pervading the realm of space,
> Snap their fingers to awaken me, commanding me to contemplate the gate of *a*.
> Silently recite this mantra, receiving the teaching and standing in attendance.

*Oṃ citta-prativedhaṃ karomi.*

> Silently reciting once, one should then visualize it as a moon disc.
> Wishing to further purify it, one recites this secret mantra.

*Oṃ bodhicitta-utpādayāmi.*

> Upon the pure moon disc, contemplate the seed-syllable *dhaṃ*:
> It becomes the Vajra Sword. Recite this secret mantra:

*Oṃ tiṣṭha vajra-tīkṣṇa.*

> Upon the pure moon disc, sharp and penetrating with radiance,
> One should gradually expand it to pervade, its measure equal to space.

*Oṃ sphara vajra-tīkṣṇa.*

> One no longer sees one's own body or any mark whatsoever.
> Next, gradually contemplate the sword. Recite this gathering mantra:

*Oṃ saṃhara vajra-tīkṣṇa.*

> The tathāgatas throughout space all follow the sword as it contracts.
> When its measure equals one's own body, one has become the original holy form:
> Body of purple-gold color, in the sublime form of a youth,
> Five topknots adorned with head ornaments, crowned with the jewels of the five directions,
> Right hand holding the Vajra Sword, from which flame-colored fire blazes,
> Left hand holding a blue lotus upon which rests a Prajñā Sanskrit text.
> Dwelling in all sublime colored marks, the body seated in a pure moon disc.

> The practitioner, having established this, should reflect in this way:
> "I now firmly abide as the body of the Vajra Sword,
> The samaya body, the mahāsamaya.
> The tathāgatas of the three times manifest perfect enlightenment;
> I, abiding in this samādhi, am the Vajra Sword body."

> Having so reflected, while simultaneously reciting the mantra,
> One should form the presiding deity's mudrā to empower the samādhi form.
> Both hands interlaced outwardly, the patience-vow fingers straight,
> Bending the upper joints like the tip of a sword.
> At the heart, forehead, throat, and crown, recite once at each.

*Oṃ mukhā-cinna dhaṃ.*

> Also form the Five-Topknot mudrā, to fully endow with all marks.
> The precept-wisdom, charity, progress-concentration, power-knowledge fingers,
> And the patience-vow fingers all touching: the mudrā shaped like five peaks.
> Stamp the heart, both shoulders, the throat, and finally upon the crown.
> This is called the Five-Topknot mudrā. Recite this root mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ apratihatā-śāsanānāṃ. Tadyathā: oṃ rara smara apratihata-śāsanānāṃ kumāra-rūpa-dhāriṇī hūṃ saphaṭ saphaṭ svāhā.*

> Next form the Abhiṣeka mudrā: join both palms together,
> Concentration-knowledge fingers within, progress-power fingers in maṇi shape.
> Place upon the forehead and recite the mantra; visualize the Buddha anointing one's crown.

*Oṃ ratna-kuśa āghriya hūṃ.*

> Next form the Jewel-Garland mudrā: open and separate the previous mudrā.
> Circle the forehead and crown behind, the mudrā circling three times each.
> First open from the charity-wisdom fingers, hanging down like garland ribbons.

*Oṃ ratna-kuśa āghriya māle.*

> Next don the Firm Armor: both hands in Vajra Fists,
> Extend and cross the progress-power fingers. On the finger-faces visualize *oṃ* and *dhaṃ*.
> Green light unbroken, like drawing lotus fibers.
> Heart, back, navel and waist, both knees and behind the seat,
> Gradually reaching throat and neck, then forehead and behind the crown:
> The progress-power fingers circle three times each; in front, scatter from the charity-wisdom,
> Both hands trailing celestial garments. This is called the Compassion Armor.

*Oṃ vajra-kavace vajrī-kuru vajra-vajrī haṃ.*

> Next, at the painted image's heart, contemplate the syllable *dhaṃ* as a sword,
> Which again becomes the presiding deity's body, as previously contemplated.
> Then with the Hook mudrā invite: both hands in Vajra Fists,
> Charity-wisdom fingers hooking in reverse, power raised, progress bent in beckoning.

*Oṃ vajrāṅkuśa jaḥ.*

> Next with the Lasso mudrā draw in; the mudrā the same as before,
> Only the progress-power fingers touching like a ring.

*Oṃ vajrapāśa hūṃ.*

> Next with the Chain mudrā halt: both hands in Vajra Fists,
> Progress-power fingers interlocking like hooks and chains. With this one halts.

*Oṃ vajrasphota vaṃ.*

> Next with the Bell mudrā delight; again with the previous mudrā,
> The charity-wisdom and progress-power fingers each hooking in reverse.

*Oṃ vajraghaṇṭā hoḥ.*

> Next one should offer the arghya: a fine vessel filled with fragrant water,
> Together with exquisite flowers. Lift it to the forehead and present it.

*Oṃ vajra-nāda-gātha.*

> Next form the four inner offerings, emanated by the Universally Illuminating One:
> Mahārati, the goddess of delight, offering pleasure to all holy ones.
> Both hands in Vajra Bond, the concentration-knowledge fingers standing together.
> Contemplate clouds of sublime attendant goddesses pervading all the lands of the ten directions.

*Oṃ mahārati.*

> Next offer with the Garland mudrā: extend the arms and lift as if presenting.
> Contemplate clouds of sublime jeweled garlands pervading the realm of space.

*Oṃ rūpa-śubhe.*

> Next form the Song mudrā, making this offering.
> The previous mudrā rises from the navel, gradually upward to the mouth and scatters.
> Visualize the sound of kiṃnara music, offering to all holy beings.

*Oṃ śurutra-sāṃgīye.*

> Next offer with dance, presenting to the holy ones of the ten directions.
> Both hands in Vajra Fists, rotating right and scattering above the crown.
> Visualize clouds of sublime musicians pervading all world-realms.

*Oṃ sarva-pūjye.*

> Next, with the Incense-Burning mudrā, pervade all worlds with fragrance.
> Vajra Bond scattered downward: fragrant clouds filling the dharma-realm.

*Oṃ vajra-dhūpe.*

> Next, with the Flower-Scattering mudrā, adorn all world-realms.
> Vajra Bond scattered upward: flower-nets pervading space.

*Oṃ vajra-puṣpe.*

> Next offer the Wisdom-Lamp mudrā, illuminating all dark places.
> The concentration-knowledge fingers pressing forward: this universal wisdom-light.

*Oṃ vajra-ruke.*

> Next offer the Perfume mudrā, before the chest in an anointing gesture.
> With clouds of liberation-fragrance, universally purifying the realm of beings.

*Oṃ vajra-gandhe.*

> Inner and outer offerings complete, one should now recite in sequence.
> Form the secret root mudrā and recite the Hundred-Syllable Mantra:

*Oṃ ghṛghā-sattva-samaya-manu-pālaya ghṛghā-sattva-tvenopatiṣṭha dṛḍho me bhava sutoṣyo me bhava anurakto me bhava supoṣyo me bhava sarva-siddhiṃ me prayaccha sarva-karmasu ca me citta-śreyaḥ kuru hūṃ ha ha ha ha hoḥ bhagavān sarva-tathāgata-ghṛgha mā me muñca ghṛghī bhava mahā-samaya-sattva aḥ.*

> Without releasing the root mudrā, then recite the dhāraṇī one has been practicing:

*A ra pa ca na.*

> There are four kinds of recitation: first, samādhi recitation,
> Meaning one contemplates the mantra being recited flowing from the presiding deity's mouth.
> Following the light, it enters my mouth, rotates right, and is arranged upon the heart-moon,
> As if placing crystal beads upon a bright mirror.
> *A* means "unborn"; *ra* means "without dust or defilement";
> *Pa* means "no ultimate truth": the nature of all dharmas is equal.
> *Ca* means "without activities"; *na* means "without nature or marks."
> Though the five phrases differ, their nature is nondual.
> When the mind unites with this nature, there is no need for further discrimination.
> What the Buddha praises is non-thought; non-thought too is not thought.
> Not thinking, thinking has been thought: this reaches the dhāraṇī.
> Such are the four phrases' meaning, taught in accordance with the sūtras.

> Second, spoken recitation: visualize the syllables as before,
> Neither high nor low, neither slow nor fast, the sound's rhythm like a ringing bell.

> Third, vajra recitation: visualize and enter the syllables as before,
> Seal the lips and teeth together, allowing only the tongue to move slightly.

> Fourth, māra-subduing recitation: with compassion as the foundation,
> Outwardly displaying a wrathful appearance, brows furrowed, voice stern.

> Though the four kinds differ, in a single thought they are nondual.
> Both hands hold the rosary: bodhi-seeds or lotus-seeds.
> One should use the Lotus mudrā, or abide in the Dharma-teaching mudrā.
> Morning, noon, dusk, and midnight: four periods as the fixed standard.

> This Dharma is the foremost, the most supreme among all secrets.
> One should be willing to give up even one's life, singlemindedly relying on the definitive meaning.

> Those who practice in accord with principle, who dwell in meditation practice,
> Should contemplate this Dharma to give rise to samādhi's function
> And swiftly attain omniscient wisdom.
> Those of inferior, weak faculties, confused and entangled in desire,
> Are also encouraged to cultivate this Dharma,
> To consume the obstacle of afflictions and enter serene wisdom.

> When the count is complete, again offer the arghya water.
> One should, with a joyful heart, recite praise in sublime melody.
> Again present the eight offerings, with loving devotion.
> Form the secret root mudrā; from the heart, scatter above the crown.
> Visualize the deity in space, returning along the path to the original palace.

*Oṃ vajra-tīkṣṇa muḥ.*

> One who abides in this samādhi is the most honored, with none above,
> Except for buddhas and bodhisattvas: there is no one worthy of one's reverence.
> If one wishes to accord with the world and show outward marks of respect,
> One should visualize atop that person's head a buddha or bodhisattva form.

> The Hundred-Syllable Mantra is what is most secret within the Dharma.
> If the mind is about to become scattered, one should secretly recite it.

> In the past, before the Great Master, I received these essentials orally.
> My foolish power cannot express them: it is like one drop from the ocean.
> Fearing I might offend the Great Sage, I tremble with awe and dread,
> Like a foolish, lowly person offering a small taste of ambrosia by hand.
> Do not, because you think lightly of it, consider this supreme medicine ineffective.
> Though the form be a jackal or a rākṣasa, for the sake of the Dharma one should accept it.
> May by this merit all beings universally awaken.
> May I leave the net of the world and practice in accordance with what has been taught.
