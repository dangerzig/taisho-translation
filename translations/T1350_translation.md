# 佛說一切如來名號陀羅尼經
## The Buddha's Teaching: Dhāraṇī Sūtra of the Names of All the Tathāgatas

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1350

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmadeva (法賢).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was in the land of Magadha, at the great Bodhi-seat of the Dharma-field, having just attained perfect enlightenment. He was together with an assembly of eighty thousand bodhisattva mahāsattvas, and also with eighty-four thousand great Brahmā-princes who were present at the site of awakening, all surrounding him and gazing upon the World-Honored One.

At that time, from within the assembly, a bodhisattva mahāsattva named Avalokiteśvara rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, placed his right knee on the ground, joined his palms toward the Buddha, and addressed him: "World-Honored One! There is a Dhāraṇī of the Names of All the Tathāgatas. This Dhāraṇī of the Names of All the Tathāgatas has already been taught and will yet be taught by the buddhas and tathāgatas of the Glorious Kalpa, the Fortunate Kalpa, and the Constellation Kalpa. I now, by the majestic power of the Buddha, and also for the benefit and joy of all sentient beings, wish to proclaim it. We humbly pray that the World-Honored One will graciously protect us."

At that time the World-Honored One praised Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva Mahāsattva: "Excellent, excellent! Avalokiteśvara! You are able, for the benefit of all sentient beings, to give rise to the mind of great compassion. Proclaim it as you wish." Then Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, having received the Buddha's permission, further addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! If a person wishes to recite that dhāraṇī, they should first sincerely recite the names of the buddhas and tathāgatas, namely: Jewel-Lion-Sovereign Tathāgata, Jewel-Cloud Tathāgata, Jewel-Ornament-Treasury Tathāgata, Lion-Great-Cloud Tathāgata, Cloud-Lion Tathāgata, Sumeru Tathāgata, Lion-Roar Tathāgata, Lion-Splendor Tathāgata, Brahma-Voice Tathāgata, Beneficent-Love Tathāgata, Lotus-Exalted Tathāgata, Dīpaṃkara Tathāgata, Lotus-Born Tathāgata, Sunarā Tathāgata, Flower-Holding Tathāgata, Jewel-Holding Tathāgata, Dharma-Born Tathāgata, Sunlight Tathāgata, Sun-Illuminating Tathāgata, Moonlight Tathāgata, Immeasurable-Treasury Tathāgata, Immeasurable-Ornament-Treasury Tathāgata, Immeasurable-Light Tathāgata, Lotus-Treasury Tathāgata, Celestial-Wondrous-Voice Tathāgata, and Kokila-Voice Tathāgata. If a person hears these names of the buddhas and tathāgatas, or proclaims them for others, that person will not hear the name of the evil destinies for sixty thousand kalpas, let alone fall into the Avīci Hell." When Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva had spoken these names of the buddhas and tathāgatas, he then spoke the dhāraṇī:

*Tathā catura-śiti-yujana-śata-sahasra-ṇi jāṭa-bhara mā-kuṭa laṃ-kṛta-dhāraṇī svāhā. Sarva-tathāgata-mūrtita-dhāraṇī svāhā. Avalokiteśvarāya svāhā. Sarva-tathāgatoṣṇīṣa-dhāraṇī svāhā. Sarva-tathāgata-bhāṣita-dharma-skandha-dhāraṇī svāhā. Sarva-tathāgata-bhāṣita-sapta-dhāraṇī svāhā. Padma-abhanikāya svāhā. Aṣṭa-mahā-bhaya-dhāraṇī svāhā. Śveta-varaṇāya svāhā. Sarva-tathāgata-nāma-dhāraṇī svāhā. Aśiti-padma-śanikāya tathāgata-dhāraṇī svāhā. Padma-hastāya svāhā. Sarva-mantra-dhāraṇī svāhā.*

At that time, when Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva had spoken this dhāraṇī, he further addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! This Dhāraṇī of the Names of All the Tathāgatas: if any good man or good woman receives, upholds, reads, recites, contemplates, remembers, and teaches it to others, that person's five sins of immediate retribution will all be eliminated. After their life ends, they will be born in a heaven as a king, with a lifespan of eighty-four hundred thousand koṭi kalpas. After that, they will attain the position of a cakravartin king, with a lifespan of sixty intermediate kalpas. After those kalpas have passed, they will attain buddhahood, with the name Padmagarbha Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha."

At that time, when the World-Honored One had spoken this sūtra, the eighty thousand bodhisattva mahāsattvas and the eighty-four thousand great Brahmā-princes, having heard what the Buddha taught, all greatly rejoiced, believed, received it with faith, and put it into practice.
