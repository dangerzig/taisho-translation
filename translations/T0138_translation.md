# 佛說十一想思念如來經
## Sūtra on the Eleven Contemplations for Mindfulness of the Tathāgata

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0138

Translated from the Chinese. Liu Song dynasty, translated by Guṇabhadra (求那跋陀羅)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Bhagavān was dwelling on Gṛdhrakūṭa Mountain near Rājagṛha, together with a great assembly of two hundred and fifty bhikṣus. At that time the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣus: "One should contemplate the Tathāgata through eleven mindfulness-contemplations. Having contemplated, one should give rise to a mind of loving-kindness toward the Tathāgata. What are the eleven? Purity of precepts and mind. Complete dignity of deportment. The sense faculties undisturbed. Faith and mind undistracted. Always maintaining a courageous and vigorous mind. Not being troubled by the vicissitudes of suffering and pleasure. Mindfulness not lost. Śamatha and vipaśyanā present before one. The mind of samādhi without cessation. The mind of wisdom immeasurable. Contemplating the Buddha without satiation. Thus, bhikṣus, one should contemplate the Tathāgata through these eleven mindfulness-contemplations. Having contemplated the Tathāgata, one should give rise to a mind of loving-kindness toward the Tathāgata. This is called the bhikṣu who, among bhikṣus, cultivates the practice of Buddha-mindfulness. That bhikṣu who has cultivated the practice of Buddha-mindfulness should seek one of two fruits: attaining liberation in the present life, or becoming an anāgāmin without remainder."

At that time the bhikṣus, having heard what the Buddha taught, rejoiced and undertook to practice it.

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Bhagavān was dwelling in the Jetavana, Anāthapiṇḍada's garden, at Śrāvastī. At that time the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣus: "If the liberation of loving-kindness is approached, widely cultivated, practiced and brought to fulfillment, and the wholesome qualities it produces are fully acquired, then eleven rewards and eleven fruits will follow. What are the eleven? Sleeping peacefully. Waking peacefully. Seeing no evil dreams. Being protected by devas. Being beloved by humans. Being revered by non-humans. Not being harmed by poison. Not being harmed by weapons. Not perishing by water or fire. Not being subject to punishment or execution. And at the breaking up of the body, at death, being reborn in a good place in the Brahma heavens above. One swiftly and quickly attains all wholesome dharmas. The wisdom that exhausts all āsravas is able to act. Bhikṣus! With the liberation of loving-kindness, approached, widely cultivated, practiced and brought to fulfillment, and the wholesome qualities it produces fully acquired, these eleven dharmas will follow. Therefore, bhikṣus, you should seek the means to the liberation of loving-kindness. Thus, bhikṣus, you should undertake this training."

At that time the bhikṣus, having heard what the Buddha taught, rejoiced and undertook to practice it.
