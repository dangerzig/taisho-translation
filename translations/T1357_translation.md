# 佛說師子奮迅菩薩所問經
## The Buddha's Teaching: Sūtra on the Questions of Siṃhavikrīḍita Bodhisattva

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1357

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown.

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was beside Lake Anavatapta, in the Nāga King's palace, together with five hundred great bhikṣus. There was also a full assembly of one thousand great bodhisattvas, all of whom had attained all dhāraṇīs, dwelt on the tenth bhūmi, were due to succeed to the honored position, and in one more birth would attain anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi. They had penetrated all dharma-realms without obstruction, adorned themselves with great adornments, and harbored no doubts about the Buddha's qualities of wisdom.

Then a bodhisattva named Siṃhavikrīḍita, who was in the assembly, rose from his seat, arranged his robes, bared his right shoulder, placed his right knee on the ground, joined his palms toward the Buddha, and addressed him: "World-Honored One! If sentient beings make offerings to the Buddha with a mind of faith and reverence, how much merit do they obtain?" The Buddha addressed Siṃhavikrīḍita Bodhisattva: "Good man! Do not say such a thing. Why? If one makes offerings to the Buddha, the accumulation of merit is immeasurable and boundless. Why? Because the Tathāgata is accomplished in the immeasurable body of discipline, the body of concentration, the body of wisdom, the body of liberation, and the body of the knowledge-and-vision of liberation.

"Siṃhavikrīḍita! If sentient beings see the All-Knowing One with a pure mind of faith, make offerings with reverence, honor and praise him, and present to the Tathāgata whatever brings comfort: robes, food and drink, bedding, and medicine, making such offerings during his lifetime; and if another person, after the Buddha's parinirvāṇa, makes offerings to his relics, even so small as a mustard seed: the merit of these two persons is exactly equal, without any difference.

"Furthermore, Siṃhavikrīḍita! If a lay bodhisattva piles up seven kinds of jewels as high as Mount Sumeru and makes offerings to those of the śrāvaka and pratyekabuddha vehicles; and if a renunciant who cultivates the path, having aroused the mind of enlightenment, offers a single small coin in generosity: the merit of the latter surpasses the former a hundredfold, a thousandfold, a hundred thousand myriad koṭi-fold, an asaṃkhyeya-fold, beyond all calculation, computation, or analogy.

"Furthermore, Siṃhavikrīḍita! If sentient beings for hundreds of thousands of years present every kind of pleasurable object to the Buddha, the World-Honored One; and if another person, after the Buddha's parinirvāṇa, offers even a single flower to the Tathāgata, or sweeps and sprinkles a buddha-stūpa, or plasters the ground of the stūpa, and offers incense, lights lamps, and presents banners, canopies, and music to the Buddha, saying: 'Homage to the Buddha, who throughout innumerable asaṃkhyeya kalpas cultivated the full range of ascetic practices, accumulated all merits, attained perfect enlightenment, and benefited and brought to maturity innumerable sentient beings,' if one can thus give rise to a true mind and make offerings to the Buddha, that person will not fall into the evil destinies for one kalpa, a hundred kalpas, or thousands of myriads of kalpas.

"Furthermore, Siṃhavikrīḍita! There is a dhāraṇī called Puṣpakūṭa. I now wish to teach it for the benefit, joy, and enrichment of the great multitude of sentient beings, and out of compassion for the world, for the benefit and peace of devas and humans. Siṃhavikrīḍita! If a person receives and upholds this Puṣpakūṭa Dhāraṇī, that person will constantly obtain the supernatural knowledge of past lives. In the end they will never fall into the three evil paths. They will be born in places free from difficulty and will not be separated from the Three Treasures. They will never be born in a lowly family. They will never be separated from mindfulness of the Buddha. In every place of rebirth, their faculties will be unimpaired. Life after life they will not lose the mind of enlightenment. They will be born with various kinds of profound eloquence. They will constantly see the innumerable buddhas of the ten directions." Then the World-Honored One spoke this dhāraṇī:

*Tadyathā: tu-rā-ṇī tha-rā-ni mu-ṇī pā-ra-sā saṇ-ṇi siddhi jaṇ-dī ji-na mu-ji nī-hā ji ru-ga-pa-dī bud-dha-pa-dī ji-kā-rā u-ka-ṭa-rā ju-pa-ka ji ji ju-pa-dī pha-śa-rā bud-dhi daṇ-ma-bha-sī a-kṣa-jā hī pa-ga ji a-mi-ta hī-pa kṣu-ta ti ji ju-va dī ṇi dī jā sa-ma a ji ji ju-ga-rā-va dī dī ṇa bud-dhi in-dri-jā bud-dhi svāhā.*

"Siṃhavikrīḍita! If anyone hears the name of this Puṣpakūṭa Dhāraṇī and, with a pure mind of faith, remembers, recites, upholds, and wishes to practice it, they should do so during the second, third, and eighth months. From the eighth day, cultivate a mind of mindfulness of the Buddha, up to and including the fifteenth day. Three times during the day and three times during the night, single-mindedly contemplate the Buddha. Offer incense, flowers, lamps, and light to the Three Treasures. On the fifteenth day, they will see all the buddhas seated on lotus thrones teaching the Dharma for the assembly. They will obtain the dhāraṇī; their memory will be firm and their understanding lucid. From that point forward until awakening, all that they hear will be remembered without forgetting. All sūtras and texts, all arts and skills, and all samādhis will be purified in their minds, except for seeing the Four Noble Truths. Why? Because this Dharma belongs to the category of the undefiled."

When this Dharma was spoken, Siṃhavikrīḍita Bodhisattva and all the bodhisattvas, bhikṣus, devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, and the people of the world, both human and non-human, having heard what the Buddha taught, joyfully received it and put it into practice.
