# 金剛頂勝初瑜伽普賢菩薩念誦法
## Recitation Method for Samantabhadra Bodhisattva from the Vajraśekhara Supreme Initial Yoga

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1123

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

> I bow in homage to Samantabhadra, the dharmadhātu's true suchness body!
> I now, in accordance with the great teaching,
> The Vajraśekhara's Supreme Initial,
> Briefly describe the practice rites.
> May the Supreme Initial Vajradhātu
> Ocean-assembly holy host
> Bestow compassion and grant their protection,
> Benefiting all practitioners.
> For this reason I have compiled it.

> If one wishes to seek liberation,
> One should go to an ācārya
> And request the abhiṣeka.
> Having received permission,
> Then practice in accordance with the root teaching.
> Select an excellent location
> And establish a wheel-altar.
> Then visualize one's own body
> As identical to Samantabhadra's body,
> White as conch shell and snow,
> Seated upright, entering samādhi.
> Upon the tongue, visualize a five-pronged
> Pure, wondrous vajra pestle.
> Secretly recite this true word,
> Becoming one with the dharmadhātu's purity.
> Recite the true word:

*Oṃ svabhāva-śuddhaḥ haṃ*

> By reciting this true word,
> Body and vessel are both purified.
> Then contemplate the buddhas of space,
> Pervading like sesame seeds.
> Recite the Vairocana vidyā,
> And see all the buddhas clearly.
> The buddha-contemplation true word:

*Khaṃ vajra-dhātu*

> One should fulfill Samantabhadra's conduct,
> Seeking to accomplish the most perfect awakening.
> Body and mind unmoving,
> In samādhi bow to all buddhas.
> Recite the true word:

*Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-pāda-vandanaṃ karomi*

> The principal deity's great seal is complete.
> Next, form the vajra clasp:
> Stand upright, join, and cross at the first joints.
> The secret word is thus recited:

*Vajrāñjaliḥ*

> This then becomes the vajra bond.
> Its vidyā is proclaimed hereafter:

*Vajra-bandha*

> Open the bond, strike and press the heart.
> One should recite the secret word:

*Vajra-bandha trāṭ*

> Next, with the preceding vajra bond,
> Open the samādhi-wisdom and ring-little fingers.
> Recite the universal-entry true word;
> The deity descends, granting constant protection.

*Vajra-āveśa aḥ*

> Without changing the preceding mudrā,
> Add the samādhi-wisdom and index-strength fingers.
> This is named the samādhi fist.
> Recite the true word thus:

*Vajra-muṣṭi māṃ*

> Divide that fist into two:
> The left at the hip, the right at the heart.
> Body, speech, and mind are vajra;
> The form and body follow the initial visualization.
> Using the preceding bond as the standard,
> With the wisdom and index-strength fingers at the gate,
> The samādhi finger pressed between them:
> This is the secret samaya mudrā.
> Seal the heart, forehead, throat, and crown,
> All while reciting this true word:

*Surata-sattvaṃ*

> The vajra bond is again proclaimed.
> The mindfulness-vow fingers form a blade shape.
> The index-strength fingers press the blade's side
> At the first joint as before.
> The samādhi finger presses the wisdom-finger's tip,
> Like forming the full-lotus posture.
> Sealing the crown and heart, one should visualize
> Vairocana Buddha,
> Solemnly radiant, brilliant white.
> Recite this true word:

*Oṃ bhuḥ khaṃ*

> Next, seal the forehead.
> One should visualize Akṣobhya,
> Blue in color, positioned to the front.
> Recite this secret vidyā phrase:

*Vajra-sattva*

> Next, press upon the right of the crown.
> His name is Ratnasambhava,
> Yellow in color, dignified in form.
> Recite the true word thus:

*Vajra-ratna*

> Raise and place upon the back of the crown.
> This is Amitāyus Tathāgata,
> Red in color, of supreme form.
> Recite this true word:

*Vajra-dharma*

> Again, to the left of the crown:
> Amoghasiddhi,
> Green in color. The five buddhas are now complete.
> Recite this secret vidyā phrase:

*Vajra-karma*

> Next, form the jewel-garland seal
> For the abhiṣeka to adorn the body.
> With both vajra fists,
> Circle them before the forehead,
> Then separate the fists behind the head,
> And as before, tie the garland again.
> Slowly open the ring-little fingers of both hands,
> Lowering them to both sides,
> As if hanging silk streamers.
> Recite the following true word:

*Oṃ vajra-māla abhiṣiñca māṃ*

> Spread open the samādhi and wisdom hands,
> Clap together: the mudrā of accomplishment.
> This delights all the holy ones.
> Recite the true word thus:

*Vajra-tuṣya hoḥ*

*Sattvaṃ kurya-thā sukhaṃ*

> Again, with the vidyā as above,
> Don the Vajrasattva crown.
> With the five buddha-colored bodies,
> Abide in the corresponding seals.
> Vairocana Bhagavān:
> The mudrā abides in the Tathāgata fist.
> Next proclaim Akṣobhya:
> The samādhi hand grasps the garment's corner,
> Forming a fist pressed upon the heart;
> The wisdom hand touches the earth as prescribed.
> Ratnasambhava, the bestower of wishes:
> The wisdom palm faces upward at the chest.
> Amitābha's supreme seal:
> The samādhi fist at the hip holds a lotus;
> The wisdom fist is like an unfolding flower,
> And also like Akṣobhya Buddha.
> The wisdom hand's three-banner form,
> As if rescuing sentient beings,
> Palm raised at the side of the chest:
> This is Amoghasiddhi's seal.

> Again form the vajra bond.
> Open the palms, join the samādhi-wisdom fingers.
> The ring-little fingers stand straight as peaks.
> The mindfulness-vow fingers enter the palms,
> Joined together in an arrow shape.
> Seal the heart, forehead, and throat;
> At the crown likewise.
> All use the heart secret word:

*Hūṃ*

> Then take up the fragrant water
> And recite the following arghya vidyā:

*Parama-sukha-śāya-sālārita-vilāsana-mi-tairā namo-mi bhagavān taṃ jyak hūṃ māṃ hoḥ hi hi hi hi praticcha kusu-mañjali-rī-nātha*

> Raise it near the forehead and present the offering.
> As prescribed, bathe all the holy ones.
> As before, visualize a full moon,
> Brilliant white, appearing in the altar.
> The Great Holy One abides therein,
> In the mudrā of the vajra-pride posture.
> Then proclaim the four attendant positions.
> Eye-Arrow is to the front,
> Red in color, garments likewise,
> Adorned with crown and garland.
> Both hands bend a bow and arrow,
> Gazing toward the Sattva.
> Kīlikilarāja is to the right,
> White in color.
> Both vajra fists
> Cross and embrace: the samaya.
> To the rear, named Love,
> Form and garments all blue.
> Both fists stand with the wisdom arm upright,
> The elbow supported by the samādhi hand,
> Together holding the makara banner.
> To the left, named Pride,
> Yellow in color, fists at the hips,
> Head slightly tilted to the left.

> The karma-seal true words
> Empower and establish each firmly.
> For the Great Holy Vajra-Pride,
> One should recite the heart secret word:

*Hūṃ*

> The attendant deities are next proclaimed,
> Beginning with Eye-Arrow.
> Both fists are each firm,
> Bending a bow and releasing an arrow.
> The secret mudrā is now complete.
> Recite the secret vidyā hereafter:

*Jaḥ vajra-dṛṣṭi-sāya-ke-māṭha*

> As before, with fists and both hands,
> Cross the arms and embrace at the chest.
> For Kīlikilarāja,
> Recite this following phrase:

*Hūṃ vajra-kilikilaḥ hūṃ*

> Again, changing the preceding seal:
> The wisdom arm stands straight as a banner,
> The samādhi fist supports the elbow.
> Recite the vidyā named the Love Mudrā:

*Māṃ vajrī niḥ-smara rāṭ*

> Both fists each placed at the hips,
> With the head tilted to the left:
> The Pride Mudrā is thus complete.
> Recite the true word hereafter:

*Hoḥ vajra-kāme-śvarī tāṃ*

> Next proclaim the inner offerings.
> Beginning at the southeast corner:
> Holding flowers, form and garments white,
> The Vajra Delight.
> Vajra Delight's nature:
> Black in color, holding an incense burner.
> Vajra Eye offers a lamp,
> Red in color, adorned thus.
> Last is the unguent position,
> Vajra Great Auspiciousness,
> Form and substance all yellow.
> Next proclaim their mudrā forms.
> All first dance with both fists,
> Then complete the mudrā as prescribed.
> With both vajra fists,
> Side by side, toss upward and scatter.
> Visualize wondrous flower offerings.
> Recite the true word hereafter:

*He vajra-rati*

> With fists side by side, cast downward.
> Contemplate clouds and oceans of incense
> Pervading everywhere.
> Recite the following true word:

*Mahā-rata vajra hoḥ*

> Both fists with samādhi-wisdom fingers joined,
> Like a lamp: one should apply visualization,
> Broadly giving as a buddha's deed.
> The secret word is thus recited:

*Oṃ vajra-locani*

> With both fists turned over,
> Scatter them to both sides from the chest,
> Like the gesture of fine unguent.
> Recite this true word:

*Mahā-śrī vajrī hi*

> The outer offering deities,
> Visualized at the four corners:
> Southeast is named Sport,
> Both fists at the heart.
> Smile is at the southwest,
> Both hands scatter from beside the mouth.
> Song is at the northwest,
> Strumming and holding the harp.
> Northeast is named Dance,
> Whirling and spinning as prescribed.
> Form and garments all golden.
> True words and mudrās are now proclaimed.
> Turn both fists over and join them,
> Circling the heart to the right:
> This is the Sport seal.
> Its vidyā is recited hereafter:

*He rati vajra-vilāsi-ni trāṭ*

> As before, the seal beside the mouth:
> Slowly scatter from the ring-little fingers,
> Raise the palms and extend the arms fully.
> With a smiling face, recite the true word:

*He rati vajra-hāse svāhā*

> The samādhi arm like a harp,
> Changing the index-strength fingers to slight bending,
> The wisdom hand strums the strings.
> For Song, recite this vidyā:

*He rati vajra-gīte tī tī*

> Both fists dance at the heart,
> Joining above the crown, then scattering.
> Named the Dance Mudrā:
> The true word phrase is thus proclaimed:

*He rati vajra-nṛtte vai-pa vai-pa*

> The four door-keepers reside at the gates.
> Their forms and bearing are now described.
> First, holding a hook, blue in color,
> Splendidly adorned, positioned between.
> South carries a lasso, all yellow.
> West holds chains, all red.
> North is named Bell,
> With green crown and garland.
> The secret mudrās and true words
> Are now next established.
> Both fists, backs pressed together,
> The ring-little fingers hook in reverse.
> The index-strength fingers are fully extended.
> Then slightly bend the index finger,
> Gently beckon: this is the Hook Mudrā.
> Its vidyā is recited hereafter:

*Vajra-kuśe jaḥ*

> The preceding seal: cross the index-strength fingers,
> Reversing so the tips hang from each other.
> Within, like a loop-lasso.
> Recite the following true word:

*Vajra-pāśe hūṃ*

> Change the index-strength fingers to hook each other.
> Open the fists, cross the arms behind:
> This is named the Chain Mudrā.
> The secret word is thus recited:

*Vajra-śaṅkale māṃ*

> Like the chain, backs touching,
> Shake and ring: the Bell vidyā:

*Vajra-ghaṇṭe hoḥ*

> The Great Holy One's adornments:
> Flower seat, garments,
> And all else seen before the honored one.
> Their colors follow the body's hue.
> Of supreme form, endowed with all virtues,
> Wearing a crown of the five buddhas.

> When about to construct the maṇḍala,
> First visualize all the positions.
> A moon-wheel, round and bright, appears.
> Within it, contemplate:
> Half-lotus, facing the principal deity,
> Joyfully gazing upward in reverence.

> Again proclaim the samaya seal:
> The principal deity's seal proclaimed before.
> That mudrā and its true word
> Should be followed as prescribed without error.
> The remaining deities are next established,
> Also beginning with Eye-Arrow.
> Following the corresponding protection-body,
> The Great Bliss Mind-Following Seal:
> Extremely bend the index-strength fingers,
> First joints, backs touching.
> The samādhi-wisdom fingers press them together.
> The mudrā is accomplished.
> Press the samādhi upon the wisdom,
> Deeply crossing: the seal is complete.
> This is named Kīlikilarāja.
> Next proclaim the makara form:
> The index hooks the vow-finger,
> The strength grasps the mindfulness-tip.
> The precept finger extends straight as a needle.
> The ring-little fingers join and stand straight.
> The samādhi-wisdom fingers are each side by side,
> Pressing beside the index-strength fingers.
> The Love Mudrā is now complete.
> Without changing the preceding seal,
> From outside, touch the hip:
> First right, then left likewise.

> The inner offerings are again made clear:
> Twist the vajra clasp sideways.
> Toss upward for the flower seal;
> Scatter downward for the incense;
> Samādhi-wisdom pressed together for the lamp;
> Unguent opens from the chest.
> Sport whirls in circumambulation;
> The Smile Mudrā near the mouth;
> Slowly open from the ring-little fingers.
> Again form the Song seal:
> Change the strength finger to slight bending,
> The index plucks in the space between.
> The samādhi hand in khaṭaka,
> The wisdom hand in three-banner form,
> Whirling and dancing at the heart.
> The eight offerings are now complete.
> Next form the vajra bond.
> Change the index-strength fingers to slight bending,
> Slowly beckon: this is the Hook Mudrā.
> From the bond, the Lasso is born.
> The samādhi finger between the wisdom and strength:
> The seal complete, this becomes the Chain.
> Loop the index with the samādhi-wisdom-strength,
> Press and then hook each other.
> Again make a firm bond.
> The samādhi-wisdom and precept fingers lower,
> Shake at the chest:
> This becomes the Bell. The four attractions are complete.
> The samaya true words
> Are the same as the preceding karma phrases.

> At the Vajrasattva position
> And the practitioner's station,
> All have sixteen deities
> Surrounding, abiding in dignified beauty.
> Again recite the following:
> The supreme true praise.
> By merely reciting it,
> Siddhi is swiftly brought to perfection.

*Mahā-sukha mahā-rāga mahā-vajra mahā-dhana mahā-jñāna mahā-karma vajra-sattva niḥ-siddhaya me*

> Having finished reciting the praise,
> Contemplate the principal honored one.
> Body and mind undistracted,
> Take up and hold aloft the rosary.
> At the heart, empower it,
> And recite the true word:

*Oṃ vajra-guhya-japa samaye hūṃ*

> Next, recite for the principal honored one.
> Holding the rosary, abide in equanimity.
> Do not excessively move the tip of the tongue.
> Lips and teeth are both closed.
> The vajra speech is beyond sound,
> Clearly contemplating the marks and excellences.
> At the four periods, without interruption,
> Hundreds of thousands as the limit,
> Or even exceeding this.
> Recite the true word thus:

*Oṃ vajra-sattva aḥ*

> When the recitation quota is complete,
> Hold up the rosary and make great vows.
> Then form the root seal
> And recite the root vidyā seven times.
> Again perform the eight offerings.
> With melodious voice recite the praise.
> Present the arghya water.
> Dissolve the boundary and all the seals.
> Then form the samādhi fist,
> Recite once and pull it apart.
> Next form the karmaṇa:
> Recite three times and open the hand three times.
> Each corresponding birth-seal
> Should each be individually released.
> Recite the true word:

*Oṃ vajra muḥ*

> Next form the sending-off seal.
> Both hands in vajra bond,
> The mindfulness-vow fingers standing straight as needles.
> Having recited, toss upward.
> Recite the true word:

*Oṃ kṛtu-va sarva-sattvā-rtha-siddhi-datā yathā-nu-gaccha-dhvaṃ buddha-viṣayaṃ puna-rāga-manāya-tu oṃ vajra-sattva muḥ*

> Next form the jewel seal.
> Both hands in vajra bond.
> The index-strength fingers in jewel shape.
> The samādhi-wisdom fingers likewise.
> The seal form rises from the heart,
> Placed at the consecration point.
> Separate the hands as if tying a garland;
> This also forms the armor seal.
> Recite the true word thus:

*Oṃ vajra-ratna abhiṣiñca māṃ sarva-mudrā-me dṛḍhī kuru vara-kavacanā*

> Having empowered and donned the armor,
> Clap the palms together three times
> To delight the holy assembly.
> With this true word and seal,
> Release the bond and attain joy.
> Recite the true word:

*Oṃ vajra-tuṣya hoḥ*

> Having sent off the holy assembly
> And completed one's own empowerment,
> Leave the practice-hall.
> At leisure, walk about freely,
> Read the Mahāyāna scriptures,
> Regulate the breath and settle body and mind.
> The mind should always contemplate the principal deity.
> Benevolent practitioner, you should diligently follow this.
