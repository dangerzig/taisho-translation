# 供養十二大威德天報恩品
## Chapter on Offerings to the Twelve Great Majestic Devas to Repay Their Grace

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1297

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

At that time, Samantabhadra Bodhisattva observed the humans and devas with compassion for all, and of his own accord spoke: "All sentient beings undergo transformations of the four great elements. There arise various illnesses, or ghosts and māras come and create various diseases, deluding the world and diminishing their lifespan. How should one counteract these various internal and external harms? It is because sentient beings do not know gratitude that such adversities occur. What constitutes this grace? The earth, water, fire, wind, sun, moon, and all the devas each have the grace of internal and external nourishment and sustenance. How may one repay such grace? The four great elements have their essences, which are the devas and the sun and moon. Making offerings to these devas brings various benefits; both the vessel-world and the living-world are strengthened. Who are they, and how many? There are twelve such devas: Pṛthivī Deva, Varuṇa Deva, Agni Deva, Vāyu Deva, Īśāna Deva, Śakra Deva, Yama Deva, Brahmā Deva, Vaiśravaṇa Deva, Rākṣasa Deva, Sūrya Deva, and Candra Deva.

"When Pṛthivī Deva is pleased, there are two benefits: first, the human body becomes firm and its color and strength increase; second, the earth-element's flavor and power in the vessel-world increase. When this deva is angered, there are also two harms: first, the human body becomes disordered and its color and strength diminish; second, the earth's flavor and power in the vessel-world are all disrupted.

"When Varuṇa Deva is pleased, there are two benefits: first, the human body does not suffer thirst; second, rain falls in due season. When this deva is angered, there are also two harms: first, the human body becomes parched with thirst; second, drought demons desiccate all things in the vessel-world, or great rains flood the world, sweeping away vegetation without reaching all beings.

"When Agni Deva is pleased, there are two benefits: first, the body's heat increases and decreases seasonally; second, the seasons proceed in order. When this deva is angered, there are also two harms: first, the body's heat increases and decreases out of season; second, spontaneous fires arise and burn all things.

"When Vāyu Deva is pleased, there are two benefits: first, the human body is light and at ease, with all movements following the mind's intent; second, the vessel-world is stable and free from upheaval, with gentle breezes that neither harm sentient nor insentient beings. When this deva is angered, there are also two harms: first, the human body and voice do not obey one's will; second, great winds fill and scatter the world. Or when winds fail to blow, vegetation does not follow the seasons.

"If the four elemental devas become wrathful and bring calamity, the king and people should enter a stūpa of the Tathāgata's five wheels, receive and uphold the precepts, take refuge in the Three Treasures, collect their minds, and contemplate thus: Earth depends on water for its arising; because the nature of water is empty, earth too is impermanent. Water depends on wind for its arising; because the nature of wind is empty, water too is impermanent. Fire depends on water and wind for its arising; because water and wind are empty, fire too is impermanent. Wind depends on space; because space has no substance, wind too is impermanent. Since wind is empty, fire is empty; since fire is empty, water is empty; since water is empty, earth is empty; since earth is empty, all sentient and insentient beings upon the earth are entirely impermanent. When one contemplates thus, the devas' anger is extinguished, having nowhere to abide. Then the practitioner should further contemplate: Although space has no substance, there is still space; therefore it can be called space. A name must have a substance; therefore since space exists, wind also exists. Since wind exists, fire, water, earth, and the rest all have substance. When one contemplates thus, the devas rejoice, their majestic radiance increases twofold, and they abide in stability.

"When Īśāna Deva is pleased, the other devas are also pleased, and the māra-hosts cause no disturbance. He was formerly called Maheśvara. The Buddha said: if one makes offerings to Maheśvara, one has thereby made offerings to all the devas. When this deva is angered, the māra-hosts all manifest, and the land falls into chaos.

"Śakra Devendra is the lord of all who dwell on the earth. He records the good and evil deeds of sentient beings. When this deva is pleased, the land is secure and the people live in peace. When this deva is angered, weapons clash in war, and all the kings who dwell on the earth are unsettled.

"When Yama Deva is pleased, people do not suffer untimely death, and epidemics do not break out. When this deva is angered, people die before their time, and epidemics prevail everywhere.

"Brahmā Deva is the lord of the upper heavens and the father of all sentient beings. When this deva is pleased, the vessel-world is secure and free from disturbance. Why? Because at the beginning of the kalpa, this deva established the vessel-world. Sentient beings are not in disorder, and the world is governed justly. Why? Because the father-king is pleased. When this deva is angered, the world is insecure, with various diseases. Even trees and vegetation all wither and fall. Sentient beings are confused, as if intoxicated.

"When Vaiśravaṇa is pleased, the yakṣa hosts are pleased and do not harm the people or spread poisonous swellings. When angered, all become disordered.

"When Rākṣasa Deva is pleased, all the flesh-eating ghosts are also pleased, not spitting poison or committing evil deeds. When this deva is angered, they all appear in disorder.

"When Sūrya Deva is pleased, his light does not harm things, people's anger does not become dull, and both sentient and insentient beings are all happy. When this deva is angered, he loses measure and his light fails. Though people have eyes, they cannot see. Cold and suffering press upon them suddenly.

"When Candra Deva is pleased, his cool light nourishes all things, and people suffer no febrile diseases. When angered, he withdraws from all.

"The mutual illumination of Sūrya and Candra brings great benefit. The seasons are harmonious, and sentient beings find joy in all their undertakings. At the beginning of the kalpa, humans were like the devas of the form realm. Gradually craving flavor, they lost their bodily radiance, increasingly abandoned wholesome minds, and performed evil karma. The world became disordered, and heat and cold arose. Therefore these devas of the sun and moon appeared to transform the world through their manifestations.

"When do these devas rejoice? When do they become wrathful? When kings and their people govern unjustly, performing unwholesome deeds, constantly killing and stealing, and abandoning the True Dharma, then all the devas feel grief and sorrow. When grief and sorrow have passed, they give rise to wrath. When celestial calamities are about to arise, there are first portentous signs: rain falls from a cloudless sky, and the stars move in disorder. If evil deeds are halted and the world is governed justly, the devas rejoice, all come to protect, wondrous fragrance pervades, virtue increases, and evil diminishes.

"If a person understands these great holy majestic devas, first with material giving they adorn their physical bodies, then with Dharma-giving they reveal their Dharma-bodies, and further practice compassion by not taking life: this constitutes the offering that repays their grace.

"Among the devas and spirits of the world, their numbers are exceedingly many. Why offer only to these twelve devas to secure the land and bring peace to all people? Because the twelve devas comprehensively include all devas, nāgas, ghosts, spirits, constellations, and officials of the dark realm. Therefore, by making offerings to them, understanding these twelve devas, one obtains the protection of all devas, nāgas, and the rest. The reason is as follows:

"Pṛthivī Deva, together with all the spirits on the earth, the spirits beneath the trees, and the spirits of the wilderness, all enter the altar together to receive offerings at the same time.

"Varuṇa Deva, together with the spirits of all rivers, streams, and the great ocean, and all the nāga hosts, all enter the altar together to receive offerings at the same time.

"Agni Deva, together with all fire spirits, vidyādharas, and sage hosts, all enter the altar together to receive offerings at the same time.

"Vāyu Deva, together with all wind spirits and formless roaming spirits, all enter the altar together to receive offerings at the same time.

"Īśāna Deva, together with all the māra hosts, enters this altar together to receive offerings at the same time.

"Śakra Deva, together with all the devas and ghosts dwelling on Mount Sumeru and all other mountains, all enter the altar together to receive offerings at the same time.

"Yama Deva, together with the officials of the five paths of the dark realm, the Great Mountain Lord, the life-officers, the plague-sending spirits, and the hungry ghosts, all enter the altar together to receive offerings at the same time.

"Brahmā Deva, together with all the devas of the dhyāna heavens of the form realm, all enter the altar together to receive offerings at the same time.

"Vaiśravaṇa Deva, together with all the yakṣas and devouring spirits, all enter the altar together to receive offerings at the same time.

"Rākṣasa Deva, together with all rākṣasas and blood-eating ghosts, all enter the altar together to receive offerings at the same time.

"Sūrya Deva, together with all the star hosts, the seven luminaries, all the grahas, and all the light-spirits who roam through the sky, all enter the altar together to receive offerings at the same time.

"Candra Deva, together with all the twenty-eight mansions, the twelve zodiacal spirits, and all the mansion hosts who dwell in the sky, all enter the altar together to receive offerings at the same time.

"One should contemplate in this manner. These deva-lords personally vowed before the Buddha that they would bear the burden of all sentient beings, protect the boundaries of the nation, and guard the True Dharma. When summoned and invited, they will not transgress their original vows, and will certainly respond like an echo. Until they are dispatched, they remain at the altar and do not oppose the practitioner. Therefore one should summon, invite, and make offerings, and afterward should dismiss them with gratitude, acknowledging that the rite was simple, and beseech their joy.

"When summoning and inviting, use each deva's individual mudrā and mantra, or the universal invitation mudrā and mantra may also be used.

"How can a small offering satisfy so many deva hosts and ghosts and spirits? It is through three principles that a small offering can be received by all. First, through the empowerment of the three mysteries: the mind contemplates the Dharma-realm and the reality-nature as a wondrous offering. Second, through great compassion. Third, because the merit-power of these deva-lords is vast, just as a nāga who obtains a small amount of water, through its own merit-power, can sprinkle rain upon all lands. For making offerings to the devas: the aggregate merit of all people of the four continents does not equal the merit-wealth of a single being in the Cāturmahārājakāyika heaven. The wealth increases or decreases in accordance with the lord's intentions. Because the devas' merit is vast, they can receive a meager offering and distribute it to all. Just as a small fire increases or decreases according to the fuel, so too with a small offering: because the devas' merit is great, what is entrusted to them becomes vast. Furthermore, even if the color and flavor are inferior, through the three principles mentioned above, all is transformed into pure and exquisite amṛta-ghee. Just as Jīvaka, through compassion for all, caused all things to become medicine. And Nirgrantha's curse-poison was transformed back into medicine. If even worldly cases are thus, how much more so when the practitioner follows the Buddha's teaching, arises great compassion, and performs the empowerment of the three mysteries! Moreover, through the devas' merit, even inferior offerings become pure and exquisite offerings. Like the man who turned stone into a king with a philosopher's stone, or the woman who turned sand into gold. The noble ones of the heavens do not cling to worldly wealth but only delight in what accords with the True Dharma. Therefore they do not scorn a small offering but rejoice in compassionate giving.

"If a deva is not pleased, invite the outer vajra division lord, the four-armed Acala Vidyā-Rāja, and make offerings and take refuge. At that time, all the devas will follow his command with joy and provide protection.

"When making offerings, from which direction should they come? In the east, Śakra. In the southeast, Agni. In the south, Yama. In the southwest, Rākṣasa. In the west, Varuṇa. In the northwest, Vāyu. In the north, Vaiśravaṇa. In the northeast, Īśāna. At the center, the honored one. At the four corners, Brahmā, Pṛthivī, Sūrya, and Candra. Thus they are situated.

"How should offerings be made, and which deva comes first? Either follow the directional positions, beginning from the east and proceeding clockwise. Or if one has a specific prayer, make offerings according to the methods of pacification and the like. For seeking pacification, Śakra is first. For seeking increase, Brahmā is primary. For seeking subjugation, Maheśvara is first. For seeking love and respect, Vaiśravaṇa is first, with the rest proceeding clockwise. Or if one wishes to stop warfare, Śakra is primary. To remove yakṣa and rākṣasa difficulties, use Vaiśravaṇa and Rākṣasa Deva. To remove epidemics, use Yama. To remove drought or flood, use Varuṇa for both. To remove enemy calamities, use Vāyu, and also petition this deva for wind-related difficulties. To harmonize people, use Brahmā. For water calamities, use Agni. For fire calamities, use Varuṇa. To subdue māras, use Īśāna or Agni. To suppress evil places, use Pṛthivī, and also petition for good harvests of the five grains. For seeking official rank, use Śakra. For seeking wisdom, use Sūrya. For seeking samādhi, use Candra. To remove febrile or cold diseases, use Sūrya or Candra accordingly. For diseases of the four elements, use the corresponding elemental deva. Make that deva primary, with the rest offered to in clockwise order. For wealth, use Vaiśravaṇa. For nobility, also use Brahmā.

"The offering implements should each be prepared in one vessel: fragrant water, fragrant paste, seasonal flowers, five-grain rice, candles, and so forth. Place them all in one vessel for the offering. Burn incense to perfume all, pure and in accordance with the Dharma. The ritual procedure for offerings, the individual mudrās and mantras, the painted images, and various other doctrinal matters are explained in other texts."

Samantabhadra Bodhisattva, for the sake of liberating sentient beings, asked and answered of his own accord: such causes and conditions, such repayment of grace, such prayers, such contemplation-methods, such three mysteries, such skillful means, such great power. He expounded at will and presented it to the World-Honored One. The World-Honored One said: "The principle is indeed thus. Excellent! Excellent! What you have said is true and not false. These twelve devas are in fact buddhas of the ancient past who have come to manifest for the liberation of sentient beings. Therefore the practitioner should not rely on their physical forms as devas, but only contemplate the Dharma-body adornments of those devas. If a person follows what I and you have taught and makes offerings to these devas, and before the images of these devas lectures on and reads the Prajñā and other profound sūtras, causing all these devas to rejoice greatly, then because the devas rejoice, all sentient beings will also rejoice. Because all sentient beings rejoice, all the buddhas also rejoice. Because all the buddhas rejoice, all worldly and supramundane siddhis will be perfected."
