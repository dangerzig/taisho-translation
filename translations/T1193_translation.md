# 妙吉祥平等瑜伽祕密觀身成佛儀軌
## Ritual of the Secret Yoga of Mañjuśrī's Equality for Contemplating the Body and Attaining Buddhahood

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1193

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Cixian (慈賢).

---

Whenever one wishes to perform liturgical recitation, ritual practice, or offerings, one should first take refuge in the Buddha and recite the Eight Great Vows. The mantra:

*Oṃ buddha-dhara aḥ ca saṃgha ca tri-ratna-gara-manutre mutu-cittāṃ karumiyā-śaṃ svapra-rātha-prasiddhaye niśāṇa-sarva-pāpanāṃ puṇyānāṃ cānu-moda-nāṃ kṛtu-pava-saṃcari-śayamī āryāṣṭa-ga-pāśi-da-idaṃ vaira-mupanāyā yavatra-tiryāga-mī-nīya-va-jaisurye ati-yaṃ-dāri-praṇi-vadha-praśya-harānāṃ amu-bhraṃ-hacarya-tanā-vakā-bhinu-matu-pāja-naṇa-paṇa vikāla-saṇa-māla-varaṇa-kāri-nītya-gi-tavā-nīda anīyā-haṃ-nī-virāpe-tri-rāpe.*

Upon first entering the practice site, face toward the Buddha. Seated, visualize this body shattering into particles like motes of dust, then gather and reconstitute the body as a vajra body.

The mantra:

*Oṃ raju-bhagata sarva-dharma.*

Purification of the four activities of speech:

*Oṃ vākiya-śuddha sarva-dharma vākiya-śuddho 'haṃ.*

Purification of the three activities of mind:

*Oṃ citta-śuddha sarva-dharma citta-śuddho 'haṃ.*

Purification of the three activities of body:

*Svabhāva-śuddha sarva-dharma svā-svā-śuddho 'haṃ.*

> Having purified the three activities, extend the mind to all sentient beings
> Throughout the six paths.

Retribution-obstacles, karmic obstacles, and affliction-obstacles are all eliminated, and purity is obtained. Next, visualize the Buddha-altar above and extend the mind in offering. Then visualize one's own hands as Vajrapāṇi's hands; the feet, eyes, heart, and so forth are likewise. With this vajra body, make reverent worship to all buddhas. Then further visualize one's own five fingers taking up the five-fold vajra-wisdom amṛta water and self-anointing the crown, washing away the five bodies and ten kinds of karma and six sense-objects. Visualize the ordinary person's body as entirely nonexistent, like a pure moon disc. Upon the disc, one's own body appears as the syllable *hūṃ*, blazing like a flame, resting upon the moon disc with the measure of a lamp's light. Recite this mantra to empower seven times. The mantra:

*Oṃ bodhicitta-utpādayāmi.*

Again visualize the *hūṃ* syllable form resting upon the moon disc, like a great mass of radiance. Visualize the body scattering as sparks of fire; after a long while, gather them back. Recite the mantra seven times:

*Oṃ sukha-ma-vajra.*

> Again visualize upon the moon disc a small radiance abiding.
> Recite this mantra; one should recite it seven times.

The mantra:

*Oṃ tiṣṭha vajra.*

> The small radiance does not move; visualize the body spinning away.
> Recite this mantra; again one should recite it seven times.

The mantra:

*Oṃ sphara vajra.*

> Again visualize the body returning. Recite the mantra seven times.

The mantra:

*Oṃ saṃhara vajra.*

> Again visualize the body entering, about to become the Great Awakened Body.
> Recite this secret mantra and empower seven times.

The mantra:

*Oṃ vajra āveśa hūṃ.*

> Visualize the small radiance abiding, now identical to the Great Awakened Body.
> Recite this dhāraṇī; again one should recite it seven times.

The mantra:

*Oṃ vajrātmako 'haṃ.*

> Furthermore, contemplate one's own body as a vajra body,
> Just like the Tathāgata's body, identical without any difference.
> Recite this mantra three times:

*Oṃ yathā sarva-tathāgata-sattva-sukha ahaṃ.*

Next, one should hold a vajra in the right hand and a bell in the left, acting as the Pāramitā Bodhisattva. Holding the vajra in ritual practice represents the Buddha-mudrā: the five-pronged vajra represents the Tathāgata, and the bell represents the four Pāramitā Bodhisattvas. Attentively visualize one's own body as Ratnasambhava Buddha. Cast the vajra three times, then spin and dance, forming the Lotus mudrā. After the mudrā, prostrate; after prostrating, take up the vajra and touch it to the crown, mouth, and heart, reciting the three wisdom-syllables *oṃ āḥ hūṃ*.

Visualize the crown as an altar. First, visualize placing a lotus within the crown, with the lotus having eight petals. After placing the lotus, lay upon it a vajra. Upon the vajra are sun and moon discs. Upon the sun disc are twelve syllables; upon the moon disc are the sixteen vowels, the mothers of Sanskrit. Because the dharma of production is unattainable, upon the moon disc visualize placing the syllable *oṃ*. Then contemplate the *oṃ* syllable spinning away; after a long time it returns, taking the form of a wheel-syllable. Then visualize it transforming once into Vairocana Buddha. The remaining four directional buddhas are installed in sequence, all identically equal. The four Pāramitā Bodhisattvas should be contemplated with their seed-wisdom syllables, dwelling in the four corners seated upon triple-tiered thrones without moon discs. Down to the vidyā-kings, all should be done in this manner. These nine positions abide in the first court.

The eight inner bodhisattvas, contemplated with their seed-wisdom syllables, are distributed in sequence in the second court. The twelve outer deities, the ten vidyā-kings, and others, also contemplated with their seed-wisdom syllables and secret names, are installed in the third court according to their positions.

When the visualization of the altar is complete, contemplate the body as the Buddha and recite the Buddha-Altar Mantra:

*Buddha-vajra-dhara-śrī-mandana-tri-vajra-bhinīya-bha-vina-adhiṣṭhāna-pānumī-niyāṃ-karu-toṃ-kāya-vajrī-nāda-śaṇdhi-saṃsthita-buddha-kuśa-vaṃ-tu-kāya-vajrī-nā. Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-kāya-vajra-svā-svātmako 'haṃ.*

Next, visualize in the mouth the Dharma-Altar position. Visualize the syllable *a* going out and returning, becoming a jeweled lotus. Then visualize it transforming once into Amitābha. Perform the same contemplation as before, and recite the Dharma-Altar Mantra:

*Dharma-vaivāka-pātha-śrī-mandana-tri-vajra-bhinīya-bha-vina-adhiṣṭhāna-pānumī-niyāṃ-karu-toṃ-vāga-vajrī-nāda-śaṇdhi-saṃsthita-buddha-kuśa-vaṃ-tu-vāga-vajrī-nā. Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-vajra-svā-bhavātmako 'haṃ.*

Next, visualize within the heart the Saṃgha-Altar position. Contemplate the syllable *hūṃ* going out and returning, becoming a vajra. Visualize it transforming once into Akṣobhya. Perform the same contemplation as before, and recite the Saṃgha-Altar Dhāraṇī:

*Citta-vajra-dhara-śrī-mandana-tri-vajra-bhinīya-bha-vina-adhiṣṭhāna-pānumī-niyāṃ-karu-toṃ-citta-vajrī-nāda-śana-ṇdhi-saṃsthita-buddha-kuśa-vaṃ-tu-citta-vajrī-nā. Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-citta-vajra-svā-bhavātmako 'haṃ.*

> When the Three Jewels are visualized complete, settle the mind in stillness and immobility.
> Then with a vajra lasso bind and close the six sense-gates.
> With each thought, each binding, as if donning vajra armor.

The mantra:

*Oṃ dhaṃ hūṃ vajra-cakra.*

Next, one should with earnest mind take refuge in the Three-Jewel Altar. Recite the dhāraṇī:

*Namo buddhāya, namo dharmāya, namo saṃghāya. Abhavai-bha-vanā-bha-vanā-bha-vanā-deva-bha-vanā iti bhavai-mā-bha-vasiyāṃ-bhavānām-anu-pāra-bhiyate anīyāmī-sapharaṃ-jarā-vaṃ anīyāmī-sapharan-tā-pa anīyāmī-sapharan-dhiyānāṃ anīyāmī-vinī-mokṛ-tāṃ.*

> Having taken refuge in the Three Jewels, visualize before you a single altar.
> Below are the Wind, Fire, and Water wheels; the Earth wheel sits uppermost.
> Upon the earth, four-colored iron; upon the iron, a triangular earthen platform:
> This is the ground of mixed jewels. Next, with both hands interlaced,
> Place the palms upon the ground: a vajra altar needs no further construction;
> Install it in this manner. One should contemplate the syllable *a*
> Abiding within the altar center. Visualize the syllable going and returning,
> Resting upon a jeweled lotus. Next, visualize the syllable *hūṃ*
> Abiding upon a jeweled vajra. Then visualize *bhrūṃ oṃ*:
> The syllable becomes a seven-jeweled pavilion. Inside are three tiers of altars
> And furthermore four gates. The four gates are each of four colors:
> East is white, south is blue, west is yellow, north is gold.
> Visualize within that pavilion sun and moon discs.
> Then recite *oṃ āḥ hūṃ* and visualize upon the moon disc.
> Contemplate the *oṃ* syllable body transforming into a Buddha-wheel form.
> Then contemplate the syllable *a* becoming a jeweled lotus.
> Then contemplate the syllable *hūṃ* body becoming a wisdom-vajra.
> Then visualize the three syllables together going out and returning,
> Abiding within the altar center. The five buddhas, the pāramitās,
> The twelve inner and outer deities, the ten great vidyā-kings and others,
> With their seed-wisdoms and sequence: all as extensively described above.

Next, with both hands forming a lotus shape, visualize reaching upward to receive the Tathāgata, and recite this mantra three times:

*Oṃ ṭake ākāśaya-praveśaya bandhaya tuṣya hūṃ jaḥ hūṃ vaṃ hoḥ haha hūṃ.*

> Visualize inviting the buddhas above, who have now descended into the altar.
> Recite this mantra, and with mind directed, bow at the eastern gate.

*Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-pūja-upasthānāya ātmānaṃ niryātayāmi sarva-tathāgata-vajra-sattva-adhiṣṭha svā vaṃ hūṃ.*

> Visualize the buddhas having reached the altar; seek the jeweled-vase abhiṣeka.
> Recite this mantra, and with mind directed, bow at the southern gate.

*Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-pūja-abhiṣekāya ātmānaṃ niryātayāmi sarva-tathāgata-vajra-ratna-abhiṣiñca svā vaṃ trāṭ.*

> Having visualized the abhiṣeka complete, take refuge in the Tathāgata
> And cultivate according to the method. Present extensive Dharma-offerings.
> Recite this mantra, and visualize bowing at the western gate.

*Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-pūja-pravartanāya ātmānaṃ niryātayāmi sarva-tathāgata-vajra-dharma-pravartāya svā vaṃ hrīḥ.*

> Next, visualize one's own body as firm, without arising or perishing,
> Accomplishing works without end. One should make offerings to the Buddha
> And vow that all sentient beings may all obtain the fruit of siddhi.
> Recite this mantra, and visualize bowing at the northern gate.

*Va sarva-tathāgata-pūja-karmaṇi ātmānaṃ niryātayāmi sarva-tathāgata-vajra-karma-kuru svā vaṃ aḥ.*

> Again earnestly address the Buddha: "I now with sincere mind,
> According to my ability, make offerings and take refuge in the Tathāgata."
> Recite this gāthā, and at the eastern gate bow to the central one.

*Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-kāya-mī-vāci-citta-vajra-praṇame vajra-mandanāṃ karomi. Oṃ vajra hūṃ.*

> Next, with a five-buddha hymn, sing praise to the Tathāgata.
> Bow to the four directional buddhas in the four directions, and to the central one in the east.
> He is called the Wisdom-Vajra Body.

*Aḥ-kṣobhya vajra-mahānīyāna vajra-dhātu mahā-buddha tri-maṇḍala tri-vajra-gara-kuśa vajra namastute.*

The Vajra Body of Vairocana Tathāgata:

*Vairocana mahā-śuddha vajra-sāṃta mahā-rati prakārti-prabhāsvara-garā-garaṃ-nīvaisa vajra namastute.*

The Jewel-Vajra Body of Ratnasambhava Tathāgata:

*Ratna-rāja sugāṃbhira-kha vajra-kaśa nirmāla svabhāva-śuddha nīre-pakāya vajra namastute.*

The Vajra Amṛta Body of Amitābha Tathāgata:

*Vajra-amṛta mahānīyāna nīri-vikāla-pākha vajra-dhari-karāga-pāramitā-pratipat-bha-sa vajra namastute.*

The Vajra Awakening-of-Beings Body of Amoghasiddhi Tathāgata:

*Amogha-vara-saṃbhūta sarva-śa-paripūraka svabhāva-śuddha-nīre-pāvara-sattva namastute.*

> Having praised the five buddhas, extend the mind to the ground-altar.
> Raise and place it upon the crown, as if wearing a crown upon the head.
> The two altars like water and milk, merged and unified in one seat,
> Each indistinguishable from the other.
> Seated in stillness, contemplate attentively;
> Form the mudrā and recite the root mantra. For each deity's position,
> What follows shall be explained in sequence.

> Akṣobhya Tathāgata's mudrā: left fist placed at the navel,
> Right hand hanging down to touch the earth.

*Oṃ akṣobhya hūṃ.*

> Vairocana Buddha: right hand in Vajra Fist,
> With the tip of the index finger.

*Oṃ vajra-dhātu vaṃ.*

> Ratnasambhava Tathāgata's mudrā: left fist at the navel,
> Right hand in the wish-granting gesture.

*Oṃ ratna-saṃbhava trāṭ.*

> Amitābha Tathāgata: both hands face-up interlaced,
> Progress-power fingers raised back-to-back, concentration-knowledge fingers lying across their tips.

*Oṃ amitābha hrīḥ.*

> Amoghasiddhi Buddha: left fist again at the navel,
> Right hand in the fearlessness gesture.

*Oṃ amogha-siddhi aḥ.*

> Vajra-Pāramitā: both hands in Vajra Bond,
> Patience-vow fingers raised like needles.

*Oṃ siddha-locanī hūṃ svāhā.*

> Ratna-Pāramitā's mudrā: without changing the previous mudrā form,
> The progress-power fingers in a jewel shape.

*Oṃ kelikeli māmake hūṃ svāhā.*

> Dharma-Pāramitā's mudrā: both hands in Vajra Palms,
> Open the patience-vow and progress-power fingers, the progress-power against the upper joints of patience-vow,
> The concentration-knowledge fingers curved and slightly open.

*Oṃ śvete paṇḍara-vāsinī sarva-dāsa-dhanī hūṃ svāhā.*

> Karma-Pāramitā: both hands in Vajra Bond,
> The patience-vow and charity-wisdom fingers raised.

*Oṃ tāri tuttāri tāri svāhā.*

> The twenty bodhisattvas' mudrā forms should now be explained.
> Both hands in Vajra Bond, pulling left and right to open.
> Kṣitigarbha Bodhisattva's mudrā: both hands in a spinning dance form,
> Then forming the Vajra Bond, hands opening face-up and scattering.
> For all the following deity positions: first bond, then spin and dance.

*Oṃ kṣiti-gabha-ya svāhā.*

> Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva: left fist at the waist-side,
> Right hand as if granting wishes, with the concentration finger pressing the patience finger.

*Oṃ vajra-pāṇi svāhā.*

> Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva's secret mudrā: left fist again at the waist,
> Right hand before the heart, with the concentration finger pressing the patience finger.

*Oṃ khagarbhāya hūṃ svāhā.*

> Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva: left fist at the waist-side,
> Right hand bending the precept finger, spinning right and scattering at the heart.

*Oṃ lokeśvara-rājāya svāhā.*

> Sarvanivaraṇaviṣkambhin Bodhisattva: both hands with the progress-power fingers hooking,
> Pulling open and scattering the mudrā.

*Oṃ sarva-nīvaraṇa-viṣkaṃbha hūṃ.*

> Samantabhadra Bodhisattva's mudrā: left fist at the waist,
> Right hand at the navel-side, bending the patience finger, the concentration finger pressing the progress finger.

*Oṃ samanta-bhadrāya hūṃ.*

> Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva: left fist again at the waist,
> Right hand with the concentration finger pinching the patience finger; both eyes follow the hand's gaze.

*Oṃ mañjuśrīṇātha hūṃ.*

> Maitreya Bodhisattva's mudrā: left fist at the waist-side,
> Right hand before the heart, pressing the patience finger at its middle joint.

*Oṃ maitrīya hūṃ svāhā.*

> Garment-Offering Bodhisattva: left fist at the waist,
> Right hand before the face, flicking the patience finger and scattering.

*Oṃ vara aḥ hūṃ.*

> Garland-Stringing Bodhisattva's mudrā: right and left hands at the bosom,
> As if gathering flowers, visualizing placing them atop the Buddha's crown.

*Oṃ vajra-mārya hūṃ.*

> Flower-Scattering Bodhisattva's mudrā: left hand with the knowledge finger pinching the vow finger,
> Right hand with the concentration finger pinching the patience finger, scattering outward before the face.

*Oṃ vajra-puṣpa hūṃ.*

> Incense Bodhisattva's mudrā: both hands holding an incense burner,
> As in the buddha-offering mudrā.

*Oṃ vajra-dhūpe aḥ.*

> Lamp-Offering Bodhisattva: left hand supporting the right,
> The patience finger extended in offering.

*Oṃ vajra-lokanī.*

> Amṛta Bodhisattva's mudrā: visualize in the left palm
> Amṛta water, and with the right ring finger, flick it.

*Oṃ vajra-udana-katuṣa-sa.*

> Mirror-Offering Bodhisattva: left fist at the waist,
> Right hand before the face, as if holding a mirror.

*Oṃ vajra-viśa aḥ.*

> Dance-Offering Bodhisattva: both hands in a dancing gesture.

*Oṃ vajra-te hūṃ.*

> Song-Offering Bodhisattva: left fist at the waist-side,
> Right hand with the patience and progress fingers raised straight, clapping together.

*Oṃ vajra-gīte hūṃ.*

At this point, one should employ the mantras and mudrās of the ten great vidyā-kings, as described extensively in a separate volume.

> When the secret offerings are complete, further present food and drink,
> Flowers, fruit, and perfume paste, making offerings of various kinds.
> If the mind grows weary, and one cannot complete all the ritual forms
> And wishes to leave the practice site, one should recite the farewell mantra.
> Again, with earnest reflection, one humbly addresses all tathāgatas:
> "In my mind-offerings, if there have been any errors,
> May the Buddhas have compassion on me and again bestow amṛta water
> To wash away all sins and completely eliminate them."

The farewell mantra:

*Oṃ kṛtu-va sarva-sattva-rathana-siddhi-na-tatvāya-thāṇa-ga-tagaccha-dhvaṃ sarva-buddha-viṣayaṃ-viharā-dhvaṃ yathā-sukhaṃ yatra yatra samutpannāṃ mudra-sarva-samaśośa-mūrttiye-vasamu-caryat-tatra-tatrī-va mokṣa-samapatsa-bhṛṇā-nīyāna-sattva-vasthāya-mantrī-vihārya-rathā-sukhaṃ yat-kiñci-karamaṇi-kāri-tānī-punīyāṃ-tāṃ sarva-sattvāṃ-pārṇa-māye-tā-kṛtā-nī-karamāṇi-su-nā-rūṇā-nī-prakāśaye-na pratī-cchāna ye-tā-kumitra-saṃpaka-vaśā-kana-ci-rā-kṛtye-na muṅkhi-na vimohite-na kāye-na vākiyā-mānasa-kāri-tāṃ-mī-tāṃ sarva-pāpāṃ-pratī-niṣṭhāyāmī.*

> The vajra ācārya, when performing the abhiṣeka for a disciple,
> Should first visualize the altar as above and also recite the mantras.
> These are the four vidyā-kings:

The Praṇīya-takamantra is used to pluck flowers and strike the disciple's five obstacles. The Padma Vidyā-king.

> Then burn white mustard seeds to fumigate the one being given the Dharma.
> All afflictions: the Ṭakkirāja Vidyā-king
> Empowers pure water and sprinkles the disciple's body
> To remove all sinful impurities. The Patāla mantra
> Is used to empower cooked rice, offering it to all devas,
> Nāga spirits, the eight classes of beings, and others.

> This Dharma is extremely secret:
> It must not be recklessly disseminated.
> Only when one encounters a person of wisdom
> May it be taught to them.
