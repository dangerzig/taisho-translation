# 馬鳴菩薩大神力無比驗法念誦軌儀
## Recitation Ritual for the Dharma of Great Supernatural Power and Incomparable Efficacy of Aśvaghoṣa Bodhisattva

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1166

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Vajrabodhi (金剛智).

---

Long ago, Śākyamuni Tathāgata was seated beneath the Bodhi tree, in the midst of the great assembly. At that time Aśvaghoṣa Bodhisattva came forward and addressed the Buddha: "I wish to speak my dharma for adorning the Buddhadharma, and to provide clothing for the poor, lowly, and naked sentient beings of the degenerate age. It is a great divine mantra obtained through practicing the bodhisattva path for countless kalpas. It possesses great awesome virtue and incomparable form and power, able to perform excellent deeds in the world that none can obstruct." At that time the Buddha smiled faintly and told Aśvaghoṣa Bodhisattva: "Excellent! Great man! Quickly speak, speak!"

"So be it. World-Honored One, have compassion on me and hear my teaching." Joining his palms joyfully, from his mouth he emitted great radiance and within the radiance spoke the divine mantra:

[Dhāraṇī]

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ kaka-aśvaghoṣa kṛṭa-rākha ṭarākha svāhā*

"If one wishes to receive and uphold this dharma, one should first give all one's wealth to the teacher as offering, and receive and study the mudrā and true word. Then paint an image of the bodhisattva: white flesh-colored, with palms joined, seated on a white lotus, riding a white horse, wearing white clothes, adorned with necklaces, wearing a flower crown, with the right foot pendant.

"When the painting is complete, prepare a clay altar. Beginning from the third day of the third month, with the practitioner facing east and the image facing west, make offerings with various extensive offering articles, ghee, milk, and ghee lamps. For twenty-eight days, up to one hundred days, one must maintain purity and cleanliness.

"For bhikṣus and dharma-transmitting teachers who benefit beings, their kindness is never in vain. At that time I shall always emit great radiance upon the patron's household and the national territory, accomplishing silks, brocades, gauze, cotton, wealth-fabrics, and fine materials of every kind, with gold and treasures piled up like mountains, for worldly enjoyment in abundance.

"If even one person in one country practices this, because of my original vow, all households under heaven will be exactly as described above: wealth, profit, and ornamental goods will all be accomplished, and no one will lack or suffer affliction.

"My original vow is entirely for adorning beings with wealth and treasures. If one develops a mind of doubt toward this dharma and gives rise to thoughts of disbelief, one will not accomplish the wishes of this lifetime, and the fruits of future lives will be uncertain. With firm determination, take refuge in this dharma, and do not begrudge the virtue of transmission. I shall surely protect and watch over you day by day, hour by hour, and bring to fulfillment the world's foremost wonderful wealth and happiness.

"As explained above, keeping in mind the original vow, before the treasure-altar form the mudrā and recite. Bend the left middle finger and ring finger and press their nails with the thumb. Extend the index finger and little finger and place the hand upon the heart. The right hand holds a mālā while reciting the true word. This is called the Jade Ring Mudrā for Accomplishing All Wishes. This mudrā, lest the foolish and unlearned of future ages recklessly misuse it, must not be recorded in writing. From ten thousand persons, select ten; from those ten, select one, and grant permission."
