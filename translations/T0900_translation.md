# 十八契印
## The Eighteen Mudrā-Contracts

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0900

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, composed by Huìguǒ (惠果)

---

Having received abhiṣeka from one's teacher and been granted authorization, one will before long attain accomplishment. When a disciple possesses these qualities, one may transmit the teaching. This is like a wish-fulfilling jewel that can accomplish all undertakings. As described in the sūtra regarding the proper place: in the mountains, beside flowing water, a pure forest retreat, or a valley one delights in, free from all dangers and terrors. Adorn the offering articles according to one's means. The practitioner faces the direction of the principal deity and bows in homage, then bows to the Buddhas of the remaining directions, placing the five limbs on the ground. Having made obeisance as taught, one kneels on both knees, joins the palms with hollow centers, and with a sincere mind fully confesses all transgressions of the three karmas:

> "From my past lives, transmigrating through birth-and-death,
> Now before the Great Holy One, I confess with all my heart.
> As the former Buddhas confessed, so do I likewise now.
> May you bestow the power of empowerment, that all beings be purified.
> By this great vow, may self and others obtain freedom from defilement."

The mantra:

*Oṃ sarva-bhāva-śuddha sarva-dharma sarva-bhāva-śuddho haṃ*

Next, before the principal deity, sit in full cross-legged posture or half cross-legged, and give rise to the mind of great compassion: "I cultivate this Dharma for all sentient beings, that they may swiftly realize unsurpassed, perfect awakening." First, grind various incenses and apply them to the hands. Then form the Buddha Division Samaya Dhāraṇī Seal: join both palms with hollow centers; bend the two index fingers to rest against the upper joints of the two middle fingers; bend the two thumbs to rest against the lower joints of the index fingers. When the seal is formed, place it at the heart. Visualize the Tathāgata's thirty-two major marks and eighty minor marks, clearly and distinctly as if present before one's eyes. With utmost sincerity, recite the mantra seven times.

*Oṃ tathāgato-dbhavāya svāhā*

By forming this seal and reciting this mantra, one immediately alerts all the Tathāgatas, who will all protect, keep in mind, and empower the practitioner, illuminating and touching them with radiance. All karmic obstructions are extinguished. Life is prolonged, merit and wisdom increase. The holy assembly of the Buddha Division will guard and protect with delight. In life after life, one will be free from the evil destinies, born from a lotus, and swiftly realize unsurpassed, perfect awakening.

Next, the Padma Division Samaya: join both palms with hollow centers, then spread apart the two index fingers, two middle fingers, and two ring fingers, bending them into a lotus shape. Place it at the heart. Visualize Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva with all marks complete. Recite the mantra seven times, then release at the crown to the right.

*Oṃ padmo-dbhavāya svāhā*

By forming this seal and reciting this mantra, one immediately alerts Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva and the other holy assembly of the Padma Division, who all rejoice and empower and protect the practitioner. All the bodhisattvas illuminate and touch with radiance; all karmic obstructions are extinguished. All the bodhisattvas become one's constant spiritual friends.

Next, form the Vajra Division Samaya Seal: turn the left palm outward; place the back of the right palm against the back of the left hand. With the thumbs and little fingers of both hands, hook each other to form a vajra shape. Place it at the heart and visualize Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva. Recite the mantra seven times, then release at the crown to the left.

*Oṃ vajro-dbhavāya svāhā*

By forming this seal and reciting this mantra, one immediately alerts all the holy assembly of the Vajra Division, who empower and protect the practitioner. All karmic obstructions are completely extinguished. All illness and suffering cannot touch the body. One obtains a body as firm as vajra.

Next, form the Body-Protection Samaya: interlace both hands inward with the right pressing the left. Erect both middle fingers. Bend both index fingers into hook shapes against the backs of the middle fingers without touching them. Join both thumbs pressing on the ring fingers. This completes the seal. Seal the five places of the body: the forehead, then the right shoulder, then the left shoulder, then the heart, then the throat. Release above the crown, reciting the mantra once for each place.

*Oṃ vajra-agni-pradipta-tāya svāhā*

By forming this seal and reciting this mantra for empowerment, one is immediately clad in vajra armor. All Vināyaka demons and celestial māras who create obstructions will retreat and flee, for they see the practitioner's radiance and body-clad majestic power and sovereignty. Whether dwelling in mountain forests or in perilous and difficult places, one will be entirely without fear. Disasters of water, fire, and all calamities, tigers, wolves, lions, blades, clubs, cangues, and chains, all such things are completely extinguished. Those who see the practitioner will be delighted. After death, one will not fall into the evil destinies but will be born in the pure, wondrous lands of all the Buddhas.

Next, form the Earth-Boundary Mantra Seal: insert the right ring finger between the left ring finger and little finger; insert the right middle finger between the left middle finger and index finger; the left hand does likewise. The remaining fingers join at the tips. Visualize the seal becoming a flaming vajra shape. Touch the thumb to the ground and pull it upward; with each pull recite once, up to three times. According to one's intention, large or small, mark the boundary in the mind: this establishes the firm ground-boundary.

*Oṃ kiri kiri vajra vajri bhūrli-bandha bandha hūṃ phaṭ*

By forming this seal and reciting this mantra to empower the ground-boundary, the earth down to the water's edge becomes like a vajra seat. Celestial māras and other obstructers cannot cause harm. With little added effort, accomplishment is swiftly attained.

Next, form the Directional Vajra Wall Mantra Seal: following the earth-boundary seal above, open and erect both thumbs to the sides like a wall. Visualize the seal as a vajra shape. Circumambulate the body to the right three times, marking the boundary in the mind, large or small: this establishes a vajra fortress of adamantine firmness. Even the Buddhas and bodhisattvas would not transgress it; how much less all those difficult to tame, the Vināyaka demons, and venomous creatures with sharp fangs and claws, who cannot come near.

*Oṃ sara sara vajra-prakāra hūṃ phaṭ*

Next, the practitioner should visualize within the platform an eight-petaled great lotus with a lion-throne upon it, and above the throne a seven-jeweled pavilion draped with jeweled necklaces, colored silks, banners, and canopies, with jeweled pillars in rows, hung with wondrous celestial garments. Clouds of fragrance spread all around, and a universal rain of assorted flowers descends, with all manner of music playing. Jeweled vases of argha water, celestial and wondrous food and drink, and maṇi-jewel lamps appear. Having completed this contemplation, recite this verse:

> "By the power of my merit, the power of the Tathāgata's empowerment,
> And the power of the Dharma-realm,
> I make universal offerings and abide therein."

Having spoken this verse:

Next, form the Great Ākāśagarbha Universal Offering Seal: join both palms, cross the two middle fingers outward, join the two index fingers at the tips and press them inward into a jewel shape. When the seal is formed, recite the mantra four times.

*Oṃ gagana-saṃbhava*

By reciting this mantra with empowerment, the visualized offerings and articles become true and without difference, and all the worthy and holy assemblies can enjoy them.

Next, form the Jeweled Carriage Seal: interlace both hands inward, palms facing up; the index fingers touch each other horizontally. Each thumb presses the base of the index finger. Visualize a seven-jeweled carriage with a vajra charioteer, the jeweled carriage riding through the sky to the principal deity's Land of Utmost Bliss. Recite the mantra three times.

*Oṃ turu turu hūṃ*

By this mantra and seal's empowerment, the seven-jeweled carriage arrives at the principal deity's land. Visualize the principal deity and all the holy assembly with their retinues surrounding them, mounting the jeweled carriage and coming to the practice-hall, abiding in the sky.

Next, form the Invitation Carriage Seal: following the previous seal, draw the thumbs toward the body and flick the middle fingers. Recite the mantra three times.

*Namaḥ stri-yādhvikānāṃ tathāgatānāṃ oṃ vajra-agni-yakṛṣāya svāhā*

By forming this seal with mantra empowerment, the holy assembly comes from their own land to the practice-hall, abiding in the sky.

Next, form the seal for inviting the principal deity's samaya to descend to the practice-hall: interlace both hands inward to make fists. Tuck the left thumb inside the palm. With the right thumb, beckon toward the body.

*Oṃ jina-jika oṃ ārulika oṃ vajra-dṛlika āgaccha āgaccha svāhā*

By this mantra and seal's empowerment, the principal deity, not transgressing the original vow, immediately gathers at the practice-hall.

Next, one should expel all obstructers. Form the seal of Hayagrīva, the Vidyā-King of the Padma Division, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva: join both palms; bend both index fingers and both ring fingers inside the palms with nails back-to-back. Erect and spread both thumbs. Rotate the seal to the left three times, mentally expelling all obstructers. All the māras, upon seeing this seal, retreat and flee. Rotate the seal to the right three times: this establishes the firm fire-boundary.

*Oṃ amṛto-dbhava hūṃ phaṭ svāhā*

Next, form the Upper Vajra-Net Seal: following the wall seal above, press the two thumbs against the lower joints of the two index fingers. Recite the mantra three times, then rotate three times above the head to the right.

*Oṃ viśphurad-rakṣa vajra-pañjara hūṃ phaṭ*

By this net-seal and mantra empowerment, an adamantine, indestructible net is established.

Next, form the Fire-Enclosure Sealed-Seam Seal: place the left hand over the back of the right hand, erect both thumbs. Recite the mantra three times and circumambulate the body three times to the right. Visualize a fire-enclosure surrounding the outside of the vajra wall.

*Oṃ asamaṅginī hūṃ phaṭ*

Next, present the argha fragrant-water with the mantra seal: cup both hands holding the vessel. Visualize bathing the feet of the holy assembly. Recite the mantra three times.

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ gagana-sama-sama svāhā*

By presenting the argha fragrant-water, the practitioner's three karmas are purified and the defilement of afflictions is washed away.

Next, form the Lotus Seat Offering Seal: join both palms with hollow centers; spread and bend the left and right ring fingers, middle fingers, and index fingers outward like a barely opened lotus. Within the jeweled pavilion, visualize all the holy assembly and the principal deity each seated in their proper positions, with their retinues surrounding them, clearly and distinctly. Recite the mantra three times.

*Oṃ kamala svāhā*

By forming the flower-seat seal and reciting the mantra, the practitioner shall attain the fulfillment of the ten bhūmis and shall attain the vajra seat.

Next, form the Universal Offering Seal: join both palms with the right pressing over the left, fingers interlaced. This completes the seal. Recite the mantra three times. Visualize immeasurable, boundless cloud-oceans of unguent, cloud-oceans of flower-garlands, cloud-oceans of incense, and cloud-oceans of food, drink, and lamplight, all becoming pure, vast, and abundant universal offerings.

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ sarvathā-kham-udgate spharahimaṃ gaganakam svāhā*

---

Regarding the Eighteen Mudrā-Contracts: tradition states they were composed by Ācārya Huìguǒ, or alternatively by the Great Master Kūkai. Now, according to the catalog of the Thirty Fascicle Collection: "Mahā-gaṇapati Sūtra, one fascicle," and also: "The Eighteen Methods in Siddhaṃ Script." Considering this, the Great Master likely compiled them based on these two texts.

A summary verse on the sequential arising of the Eighteen Mudrā-Contracts:

> The first, adorning the practitioner, has five kinds:
> The pure Buddha, Padma, Vajra, and the donning of armor.
> The second, establishing boundaries, has two kinds:
> The earth-boundary and the vajra wall.
> The third, adorning the practice-hall, has two:
> The practice-hall and the Ākāśagarbha universal offering.
> The fourth, inviting, has three kinds:
> Sending the carriage, requesting the carriage, and making the invitation.
> The fifth, protective seals, has three kinds:
> The division's Vidyā-King, the net, and the fire-enclosure.
> The sixth, offerings, has three kinds:
> The argha, the flower-seat, and the universal offering.
> The first two offerings are individual offerings,
> Separately presented to the principal deity alone.
> The last offering is a general offering,
> Presented to the principal deity and all the holy assembly.
> In total, six categories and eighteen practices:
> These are called the Eighteen Mudrā-Contracts.
> What is the sequence of their arising?
> Wishing to enter the gate of the secret dhāraṇī,
> One must first purify and adorn one's own body.
> Once the body is purified and adorned,
> One should also empower and purify the boundary and ground.
> Once the boundary and ground are empowered and prepared,
> One should then adorn the practice-hall.
> Once the practice-hall is adorned,
> One should invite the principal deity, the Great Holy One.
> Once the invitation to the Holy One is made,
> With courageous vigor, establish the protective seals.
> With inner and outer protection free from obstruction,
> Only then should one present the offerings.
> Without rising from one's seat, one accomplishes the siddhi.
