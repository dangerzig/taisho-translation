# 祈願文
## Prayer of Aspiration

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 2846

Original Chinese composition. Five Dynasties, Later Tang dynasty, 2nd year of the Changxing era (931 CE). Author unknown.

---

[Ritual directions:] An altar is established at this location for five days; at each altar, the monks are supplied with food. Seven persons receive food distributions; lamps are lit. Each night the Great [Sūtra] is chanted.

One bolt of cloth. One piece of camel hide. One folio of paper.

The foregoing; establishing the altar, reciting the sūtras, supplying the monks, chanting the Buddha's name, lighting lamps, and making offerings and donations; these are the intentions hereby set forth:

May the dragon-heaven and the eight classes of beings guard and protect Dunhuang. May Brahmā, Śakra, and the Four Heavenly Kings bring peace to the people and tranquility to the frontier. May all auspiciousness drive the old year far away to distant lands. May all good omens and auspicious signs attend the lord of the prefecture throughout the realm.

May the imperial lord safeguard his great undertakings and return to the capital. May envoys from the eight directions all arrive and present tribute. May the Great King maintain his position; may his blessings and honors be daily renewed, equal to the Five Sacred Mountains in loftiness, enduring without being superseded even by Heaven itself.

May the Princess forever flourish, as fragrant as peach and plum blossoms. May the Lady also dwell in peace, ever flourishing like spring flowers, gifted with every literary accomplishment in full measure. May all the young lords be filial and devoted to the state. May the young ladies and sisters govern the inner chambers with excellent virtue. May the entire household, spreading like branches, preserve joyful countenances and receive blessings in abundance.

Thereafter, may herdsmen rejoice in the tranquil reign of Shun. May village elders spread the celebrations of Yao's golden years throughout the fields. May the five grains be doubled at a thousand households, filling the eastern ridges to overflowing, all abundantly harvested in the southern acres.

May the court's envoys soon be received in the imperial presence. May the frontier conditions they report receive gracious attention. May the sage sovereign quickly dispel all difficulties and offer encouragement. May the howling of enemies be stilled and their weapons laid down; may beacon fires and frontier snares be completely extinguished.

Now, on the occasion of the altar's conclusion, all who have gathered at this site, thirsting for the Way, venerate the Three Treasures. We humbly pray that this merit be dedicated to these aspirations.

26th day of the 12th month of the 2nd year of the Changxing era [931 CE], the western Guiyi Army.
