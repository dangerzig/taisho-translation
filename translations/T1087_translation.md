# 觀自在如意輪菩薩瑜伽法要
## Essentials of the Yoga Method of Avalokiteśvara Cintāmaṇicakra Bodhisattva

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1087

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Vajrabodhi (金剛智).

---

> I now follow the yoga
> Of the Vajraśekhara Sūtra to teach
> The maṇi-lotus division's
> Wish-fulfilling recitation method.
> By cultivating this samādhi,
> One becomes like Avalokiteśvara.
> First select the disciple:
> One of good family who reveres the dharma,
> Beloved and respected by many,
> Wise and courageously diligent,
> Of resolute perseverance,
> Whose awakened wisdom never wavers,
> Who is filially devoted to parents,
> Of pure faith in the Three Treasures,
> Delighting in bodhisattva practice,
> In the four immeasurables
> Without even a moment's interruption,
> Ever delighting in the Mahāyāna dharma,
> Abiding in the bodhisattva precepts,
> Respecting the ācārya
> And all the holy ones,
> Possessing the power of firm resolve,
> The courage of a great person,
> Skilled in the gate of concordant absorption,
> Ever delighting in the practice of tranquility,
> Wise and free from fear,
> Adorning the body with precepts,
> Diligently cultivating the secret vehicle,
> Reverently following the principle and its path,
> Single-minded and without stinginess,
> Ever delighting in hearing the wondrous dharma,
> Having once entered the samaya,
> Received the consecration from a teacher,
> And obtained the seal of approval:
> Such a one will soon attain accomplishment.
> A disciple possessing these qualities
> May be given this transmission.
> This is the wish-fulfilling jewel
> That can accomplish all activities.
> As the sūtra describes the place:
> Among mountains and by flowing waters,
> In a pure araṇya,
> In valleys where one delights to dwell,
> Free from all dangers and fears,
> Adorned with offerings according to one's means.
> The practitioner faces west
> Toward the Sovereign Lord of Mastery,
> Then pays homage to the buddhas of all directions,
> Placing the five limbs on the ground
> In prostration as the teaching prescribes.
> Having knelt on both knees,
> With palms joined and mind at rest,
> With sincere heart, fully confess
> All sins of the three karmas:
> "From past lives throughout saṃsāra
> I have flowed in birth and death.
> Now before the great holy ones
> I confess with all my heart.
> As former buddhas confessed,
> So too do I now.
> May your blessing power be bestowed
> That all sentient beings be purified.
> By this great vow,
> May self and others attain purity."

The secret words:

*Oṃ svabhāva-śuddha sarva-dharma svabhāva-śuddho 'haṃ*

Next the practitioner should rejoice in all the merit and wisdom accumulated by all buddhas and bodhisattvas.

> The buddhas of the past, present, and future three ages,
> The bodhisattvas and all the holy ones,
> Whatever roots of merit they have accumulated:
> With palms joined I rejoice in them all,
> As in those I myself have accumulated,
> With joy that makes no distinction.

Next, place the right knee on the ground, form a lotus-bud mudrā with joined palms and place it upon the crown of the head, and visualize paying homage at the feet of all tathāgatas and bodhisattvas. The secret words:

*Oṃ padma-vi*

Having paid homage to all the buddhas, sit in full lotus, half lotus, or royal-ease posture as one wishes.

> Having formed this seated mudrā,
> Contemplate the buddhas pervading space,
> Each appearing before oneself,
> Abiding in that assembly of holy ones.
> From the knees upward,
> Bring the rotating hands to the heart and join them
> Like an unopened lotus,
> Visualizing homage to all the buddhas.
> Next form the samādhi mudrā:
> At the heart, make the vajra-fist bond,
> With wisdom and concentration fingers raised upright.
> This is the vajra-lotus mudrā,
> Universally used by those who hold the lotus.
> Having awakened the holy ones,
> Recite this secret word:

*Oṃ vajra-padma-samaya stvaṃ*

> By forming this mudrā,
> The buddhas and the sons of the sugatas,
> All those of great renown,
> Will with discerning observation receive one.
> Recalling their original vow
> Before the Universally-Illuminating One,
> Because they do not violate the command,
> Their blessing will bring fulfillment.

Next form the mudrā of the All-Buddhas' Ease, Delight, and Joy Samaya.

> Bind the ten fingers firmly,
> Cross the patience and vow fingers at the center,
> With charity-wisdom and meditation-knowledge
> Each paired and standing upright.

The secret words:

*Oṃ samaya husura-ta stvaṃ*

> By showing this mudrā,
> All buddhas and bodhisattvas
> And all the vajra-holders
> Will be wondrously delighted.
> Next one should open the heart-gate
> And admit the vajra wisdom-syllable.
> Contemplate above the two breasts:
> *Traṃ* on the right and *ṭa* on the left,
> Like the door-panels of a palace.
> Form the supreme vajra-bond,
> Activate the three karmas simultaneously,
> And clap the heart to open the two syllables.

The secret words:

*Oṃ vajra-bandha traṃ ṭa*

> The seeds planted since beginningless time,
> All the gathered afflictions:
> Now with the sin-summoning mudrā
> I gather them to be crushed.
> Bind the ten fingers firmly,
> With patience and vow extended like needles,
> Effort and strength bent like hooks,
> And with the mind visualize summoning all sins.
> Visualize the form of those sins:
> Hair standing on end, naked, black in form.
> Reverse the mudrā and pierce the heart;
> Upon contact, recite the secret words.
> Because the three karmas are in accord,
> All accumulated sins can be summoned.
> Having recited to summon them,
> Then perform the crushing method.

The secret words:

*Oṃ sarva-pāpa-karṣaṇa-viśodhana-samaya vajra hūṃ jaḥ*

> Having summoned them into the palms,
> Perform the crushing method.
> Cross the previous mudrā inward,
> Calling *hūṃ* to bind the sins.
> With patience and vow both extended straight,
> Bearing the syllables *traṃ ṭa*,
> Visualize them as a vajra pestle.
> Clap together as if crushing mountains,
> With wrathful words and fierce form,
> Able to purify the evil destinies.
> Having recited, clap patience and vow
> Seven or three times as appropriate.

*Oṃ vajra-pāṇi viśaphūṭa-ya sata-pāya-bandhana nipra-mukṣa-ya sarva-pāya-gati-viyoga sarva-sattvānāṃ sarva-tathāgata vajra-samaya hūṃ traṃ ṭa*

> Through this gate of concordant absorption,
> By the skillful means of former buddhas,
> The sins accumulated by the three karmas,
> Immeasurably heavy obstructions,
> Having been crushed and destroyed,
> Are like fire burning dry grass.
> Sentient beings are ever deluded,
> Not knowing this principle and path.
> Through the great compassion of the tathāgatas,
> This secret and wondrous gate is opened.
> Next one should form the entry mudrā
> And admit the tathāgata wisdom-syllable.
> With both hands in vajra-bond,
> Meditation and wisdom enter within,
> And the two effort-strength fingers
> Touch at the tips like a ring.
> Contemplate before you an eight-petaled lotus
> Upon which rests the syllable *a*,
> Adorned with two dots,
> Making the wondrous syllable *āḥ*.
> White as shell or snow,
> Radiating a thousand rays of light.
> Visualize picking it up with the effort-strength fingers
> And placing it within the heart.
> Activate the three karmas simultaneously
> And recite this secret word:

*Oṃ vajra-veśa āḥ*

> Having visualized it entering the heart,
> The syllable shines even more brightly:
> This is the essence of the dharma-realm.
> The practitioner should contemplate thus,
> And before long will awaken to tranquility,
> Because dharmas are originally unborn.
> The three-age tathāgatas,
> With their vajra body, speech, and mind,
> All through wondrous skillful means,
> Hold all within the vajra fist.
> Thus close the heart-gate;
> The wisdom-syllable becomes firmly established.
> Bend the effort-strength fingers
> And press them against the backs of the meditation-wisdom fingers.
> Touch the mudrā to the chest,
> Then recite this wondrous word:

*Oṃ vajra-muṣṭi vaṃ*

> The practitioner abides in equanimity.
> With both hands in vajra-bond,
> Place them face-up below the navel.
> The meditation-wisdom fingers form a lotus-leaf shape:
> This is called the samādhi mudrā.
> Recite this secret word:

*Oṃ samādhi-padme hrīḥ*

> On the out-breath and in-breath,
> Abide in ānāpāna.
> Visualize the buddhas pervading space
> Snapping their fingers to awaken me:
> "Child of the buddhas! How is it that
> You seek supreme and equal awakening
> Yet do not know the tathāgatas'
> Wondrous dharma of true reality?"
> Having heard this awakening,
> The practitioner responds:
> "What is this called 'true reality'?
> May the most honored one explain."
> All the buddhas are delighted
> And speak this supreme word:
> "Excellent, mahāsattva!
> You are able to ask thus.
> Visualize within your heart
> The *āḥ* syllable-gate you placed within.
> Let the syllable pervade the heart."
> Recite this secret word:

*Oṃ citta-prativedikaṃ karomi*

> Silently recite once,
> Then visualize it as a moon disc.
> Wishing to redouble one's diligence,
> Further recite the wondrous word:

*Oṃ bodhicitta-mutpādayāmi*

> This causes the moon disc of the mind
> To become perfectly round and pure.
> Within it visualize a wondrous lotus,
> Upon which rests a jeweled vajra.

The secret words:

*Oṃ tiṣṭha-maṇi vajra-padma*

> Extending to the measure of space,
> Pervading all three realms.
> Further recite this wondrous word,
> The vajra speech beyond sound:

*Oṃ spharaṇa-maṇi vajra-padma*

> From this extended wondrous lotus
> Stream forth a thousand rays of flame.
> Within each and every ray of light,
> Countless buddha-fields appear.
> Within each field there are wondrous lotuses.
> Visualize the one who holds the jewel-lotus,
> Holding the jewel-lotus victory banner.
> From within the banner a wondrous voice emerges:
> "Whoever possesses meager merit,
> I shall fulfill all their wishes."
> Abiding in this samādhi of tranquility,
> For the benefit of all sentient beings,
> All such bodhisattvas
> Abide in meditative equipoise.
> From the lotus-womb
> Wondrous rays of a thousand lights stream forth,
> All for the benefit of sentient beings,
> Through the perfection of generosity and the other pāramitās,
> Pervading all samādhis,
> Through the gate of skillful means of principle.
> Out of compassion for sentient beings,
> They create limitless skillful means,
> Manifesting as various transformation bodies,
> From birth through nirvāṇa,
> Turning the great and wondrous dharma-wheel:
> All issue from the wish-fulfilling jewel.
> The wondrous dharma that is spoken
> Is all accomplished by the wheel.
> As the wheel is wondrous wisdom,
> It can sever all the fetters.
> Like turning the great dharma-wheel,
> This is the path of merit and wisdom.
> Next, correctly contemplate all,
> And gradually gather in the wisdom-lotus.

The secret words:

*Oṃ saṃhara-maṇi vajra-padma*

> All the tathāgatas everywhere
> All enter and become one body,
> Like a bright mirror
> That can reflect ten thousand images.
> The self-nature body of the dharma-realm,
> Abiding in the vajra-lotus,
> Now transforms the jewel-lotus
> Into the true Cintāmaṇicakra Bodhisattva:
> Hand holding the wish-fulfilling jewel,
> Six-armed, golden in body color.
> Visualize all of this as one's own body,
> With a crown-jewel in the topknot,
> The Sovereign of Mastery in the crown,
> Abiding in the posture of teaching the dharma.
> The first hand rests in contemplation,
> Out of compassion for sentient beings.
> The second holds the wish-fulfilling jewel,
> Able to fulfill all wishes.
> The third holds a rosary
> For liberating beings in the animal realm.
> The left hand presses upon the Radiant Mountain,
> Accomplishing unshakable stability.
> The second holds a lotus,
> Able to purify all transgressions.
> The third hand holds a wheel,
> Able to turn the supreme dharma.
> The six arms' vast and expansive body
> Can travel through the six destinies.
> Through the skillful means of great compassion,
> It severs the suffering of all sentient beings.
> The practitioner contemplates thus,
> Seated within a moon disc,
> With a thousand rays streaming from the body,
> And a circular halo behind head and back.
> Further visualize a moon disc at the heart
> With a jeweled lotus upon it.
> Through this, one can become firmly established,
> Contemplating one's own body without wavering.
> To be free from all delusive thoughts,
> Recite this secret word:

*Oṃ nṛtti-tiṣṭha ratna-vajra-padma-ātma-kuhaṃ samayo 'haṃ mahā-samayo 'haṃ sarva-tathāgata-abhisaṃ-bodhi ratna-vajra-padma-ātma-kuhaṃ*

> Empowered by this dharma,
> Join the ten fingers in a lotus-bud,
> With effort-strength bent like jewels.
> Seal the heart, forehead, throat, and crown.
> Visualize *hūṃ* at the heart,
> *Traṃ* at the forehead,
> *Hrīḥ* at the throat,
> And *āḥ* at the crown.
> Through this placement of visualization,
> This body becomes like vajra.
> Further recite this secret word,
> The lotus-speech as sound:

*Oṃ ratna-vajra-dharma hrīḥ*

> Next one should form the consecration mudrā.
> The wise one joins the lotus-palms,
> With effort-strength in jewel shape,
> Charity-wisdom open and close together,
> Placed at the forehead while reciting the secret words,
> Visualizing the buddhas performing the consecration:

*Oṃ padma-abhikṛta-tiratna-padma-viśe-kailā-viśiñca vaṃ taṃ*

> Then with this wondrous mudrā,
> Separate the two hands to either side,
> As if fastening a lotus garland,
> Gently lowering them forward and dispersing,
> Visualizing white ribbons descending.
> Recite this wondrous word:

*Oṃ padma-māle vaṃ hrīḥ taṃ*

> Next one should form the armor mudrā.
> With both hands in lotus-fists,
> Circle from the heart to the back,
> From the back around the navel,
> To the waist and both knees,
> Gradually up around the back of the neck,
> From the back of the neck to the throat,
> Then again around the back of the neck,
> Returning to the forehead,
> Then around the back of the crown,
> Gently dispersing below the navel.
> Recite this secret word:

*Oṃ abhaya-padma-kavaca-bandhara-kṣa vaṃ hūṃ haṃ*

> To delight all the buddhas,
> One should clap the lotus mudrā:
> With both hands form the lotus-palms,
> And clap wondrously to express delight.

The secret words:

*Oṃ padma-tuṣi hoḥ*

> Visualize before one's own body
> The syllable *hrīḥ*,
> Which transforms into the Lotus King.
> Within it is the syllable *hrīḥ*,
> With *traṃ* on either side,
> Forming the vajra jewel-lotus,
> Which together transform into the honored one
> Holding the *cintā*-maṇi wondrous jewel,
> As previously visualized as one's own body.
> Now the visualization is the same.
> To make the two bodies one,
> Next perform the summoning method.
> With ten fingers in an unopened lotus,
> Bend the effort-strength fingers like hooks,
> And recite this secret word
> In the voice of the lotus:

*Oṃ padma-cija-na kuśa hūṃ*

> Having summoned the deity,
> Next form the lasso mudrā.
> As before, join the lotus-palms,
> With effort-strength touching at the tips like a ring:
> This is the lotus-lasso,
> Able to fulfill all wishes.
> Recite this secret word
> To draw the deity into the wisdom-body:

*Oṃ padma-cija-na mukha-pāśa hūṃ*

> Having entered the wisdom-body,
> To make it unshakable,
> Further form the lotus-chain.
> One should make a resolute determination.
> As before, join the lotus-palms,
> With effort and meditation fingers forming a ring,
> Strength and wisdom likewise,
> Interlocking like a hook-chain:

*Oṃ padma-cija-na sphūṭa hūṃ*

> To bring about wondrous delight,
> Form the lotus-bell mudrā.
> With the lotus-offering posture,
> Meditation-wisdom entering within,
> Effort-strength resting like rings.
> Recite this secret word:

*Oṃ cija-na padma-vijaya hūṃ*

Next one should recite the Lotus Hundred-Syllable Secret Word, holding an arghya vessel with *kuṅkuma*, white sandalwood, and camphor fragrant water, offering it in reverence:

*Oṃ padma-sattva samayam anupālaya padma-sattva tvenopatiṣṭha dṛḍho me bhava sutoṣyo me bhava anurakto me bhava supoṣyo me bhava sarva-siddhiṃ me prayaccha sarva-karmasu ca me citta-śreyaḥ kuru hūṃ ha ha ha ha hoḥ bhagavan sarva-tathāgata padma mā me muñca padmi bhava mahā-samaya-sattva āḥ hrīḥ*

> Next, with inner and outer offerings,
> Make offerings to the Lotus King.
> For the inner offerings:
> Form the lotus-palm at the heart,
> With meditation-wisdom both extended straight:
> This is called the Lotus-Joy.
> Recite this secret word:

*Oṃ cija-na padma-lase hūṃ*

> Next form the garland mudrā
> And make this offering.
> Without changing the previous joy mudrā,
> Offer it forward with both hands,
> Visualizing various jeweled garlands
> Filling all of space.

The secret words:

*Oṃ cija-na padma-māle hūṃ*

> Next, with the song mudrā,
> Make an offering to the Wisdom-Lotus One.
> Again with the previous wondrous mudrā,
> Bend the palms to touch all fingers,
> Moving from the navel gradually to the mouth,
> Dispersing downward as if pouring,
> Visualizing kiṃnara music
> As offerings to all the holy ones.

The secret words:

*Oṃ cija-na padma-gīte hūṃ*

> Next form the dance mudrā:
> With the previous mudrā rotating left and right,
> Join the wondrous lotus-palms
> And disperse above the crown.
> Through these four offerings,
> One can attain great supernatural powers.

The secret words:

*Oṃ cija-na padma-nṛtte hūṃ*

> Having made these four offerings,
> One can accomplish the most supreme undertakings.
> Next form the outer offerings,
> Presenting them with a joyful mind.
> Extend the mind without limit.
> The lotus incense-burning method
> Is taught by all the buddhas' true words
> For the benefit of all sentient beings.
> Lower the lotus-palms and disperse
> As if burning incense,
> Reciting this secret word,
> Visualizing clouds of offering-incense
> Pervading all of space,
> Making offerings to all the holy assembly:

*Oṃ padma-cija-na dhūpe āḥ*

> Next form the flower mudrā,
> With the thirty-two marks
> Adorning all the tathāgatas.
> Contemplate clouds of wondrous flowers
> Extending throughout all realms.
> As before, join the lotus-palms
> And scatter upward like flowers,
> Making offerings to all the tathāgatas
> And all the sons of the sugatas.
> Visualize filling all of space
> With clouds of fragrant flowers,
> And kalpa-trees supremely adorned.
> Recite this secret and wondrous word,
> Activating the three karmas simultaneously:

*Oṃ padma-cija-na puṣpe hūṃ*

> Sentient beings are shrouded in ignorance,
> Separated from the light of wisdom.
> To purify and remove this for them,
> One should form the wisdom-lamp mudrā.
> With the previous lotus-palms,
> Raise meditation-wisdom upright, close together.
> Visualize a cintāmaṇi lamp
> Illuminating all of space
> With boundless rays of light.
> Recite this secret word:

*Oṃ padma-cija-na dīpe*

> The wise one should next form
> The liberation perfume-incense mudrā,
> To purify sentient beings,
> Offering this fragrance of moral discipline.
> Open both hands in dispersing lotus-palms,
> At the heart in a perfuming gesture.
> The ten fingers create a fragrant infusion,
> A sea of fragrance pervading all of space,
> Offered to the Buddha and the honored one.
> Recite this secret word:

*Oṃ padma-cija-na gandhe hūṃ*

> Having completed inner and outer offerings,
> One should then recite in proper sequence,
> Forming the secret root mudrā
> As one faces the Lord of the Secret Words.
> First recite the root words
> Clearly seven times.
> Place the flat palms at the heart,
> With patience-vow fingers like lotus leaves,
> Effort-strength in jewel shape,
> All remaining fingers upright like a banner.
> Recite the root secret words,
> Contemplating the fulfillment of all sentient beings' wishes.

The secret words:

*Namo ratna-trayāya namaḥ ārya-avalokiteśvarāya bodhisattvāya mahā-sattvāya mahā-kāruṇikāya tadyathā oṃ cakra-varti cintā-maṇi mahā-padme ruru-tiṣṭha jvala ākarṣāya hūṃ phaṭ svāhā*

> Next form the secret heart mudrā:
> Based on the previous root mudrā,
> Bind the precept-direction and charity-wisdom fingers.
> This is called the original heart mudrā.
> Whatever wishes of the mind one has,
> Whatever the heart contemplates:
> By forming this mudrā,
> All will be accomplished.

The secret words:

*Oṃ padme cintā-maṇi jvala hūṃ*

> Next form the following-heart mudrā:
> With both hands in vajra-bond,
> Effort-strength in jewel shape,
> Meditation-wisdom both extended straight,
> Precept-direction also extended straight,
> Charity-wisdom crossed and upright.
> Recite this heart-within-heart:

*Oṃ varada-padme hūṃ*

> Next visualize from the honored one's mouth
> Flowing forth the secret words,
> Clearly forming a path of syllables,
> Shining in five colors of light.
> With alternating splendid colors,
> They enter through the practitioner's mouth,
> Arranged within the moon disc of the heart,
> Gleaming like a red crystal.
> Contemplate each one carefully,
> Following the principle toward awakening.
> Abide in samādhi and cultivate,
> Entering through the gate of the syllable *a*.
> Then enter the wheel-syllable contemplation,
> Contemplating all the syllables throughout.
> This is called samādhi-recitation.
> One attains wisdom and liberation.
> Through this concordant absorption,
> Before long one accomplishes omniscience.
> If one recites aloud in proper sequence,
> The result is supremely wondrous and extraordinary.
> Abide in the contemplation of the honored one.
> One should not have an agitated mind:
> Neither too high nor too low,
> Neither too slow nor too fast.
> The wise one abandons discrimination
> And all deluded thoughts.
> If one recites one hundred thousand times,
> All one seeks will be accomplished.
> Hold the rosary in both hands;
> Use crystal or lotus seeds,
> Conch beads or other precious materials,
> Unblemished and lustrous,
> Strung to one hundred and eight.
> Empower each bead with seven recitations,
> Passing each at the heart,
> With *svāhā* sounding in unison.
> One thousand and one hundred and eight,
> Reciting according to one's capacity.
> Four times or three times daily;
> This method is best practiced in the last watch of night,
> As taught by the Buddha
> In the Wish-Fulfilling Wheel Sūtra.
> If one cultivates in this way,
> In this present life one will realize the first bhūmi.
> After sixteen more lifetimes,
> One will attain supreme enlightenment.
> How much more so for worldly accomplishments?
> Could they not be fulfilled in this very life?
> When the recitation is complete,
> Re-form the samādhi mudrā,
> Again make the eight offerings,
> Then dispatch the Lord of the Secret Words.
> With both hands in vajra-bond,
> Patience-vow in lotus-leaf shape,
> Disperse from heart to face,
> And join the flower-palms above the crown.
> Visualize the honored one in space
> Returning to his original palace.

The secret words:

*Oṃ padma-sattva hrīḥ muḥ*

> Having dispatched the holy one,
> Continue to abide in the visualization of the honored one.
> Or in a quiet place,
> Read and recite the Mahāyāna:
> The Laṅkāvatāra, the Avataṃsaka,
> The Prajñāpāramitā, and the Adhyardhaśatikā,
> And other such sūtras and teachings.
> Contemplate and cultivate them.
> When reading of the sūtras is complete,
> Move freely in walking, standing, sitting, or lying;
> Even in sleep,
> Never interrupt the mind of enlightenment,
> And one will soon attain accomplishment.
> As spoken by Vajragarbha,
> This ritual of great compassion
> Does not require selecting days or stars,
> Nor particular times for meals or bathing.
> Whether pure or impure,
> One should always practice without interruption,
> Free from distraction,
> Not engaging in worldly affairs.
> When the recitation is complete, make vows,
> Form the samaya mudrā,
> Pay homage to the buddhas and bodhisattvas,
> Then walk about at ease.
> Through this accumulated merit,
> I now join in compiling this teaching.
