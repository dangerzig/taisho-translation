# 佛說如意輪蓮華心如來修行觀門儀
## Ritual of the Contemplation-Gates for the Practice of the Wish-Fulfilling Wheel Lotus-Heart Tathāgata, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1090

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Cixian (慈賢).

---

> At that time the Bhagavān, together with the great bodhisattvas,
> Upon the summit of Mount Sumeru,
> For the sake of all sentient beings,
> Expounded the Wish-Fulfilling Wheel,
> The secret method of practice.
> The Buddha said: "This Lotus
> Maṇi Wish-Fulfilling Wheel,
> Vajra King Tathāgata,
> Possesses immeasurable and boundless
> Inconceivable practices,
> Awesome virtue and supreme power.
> It subdues all māras;
> No evil can enter.
> You should all listen well;
> I shall now explain in detail.
> In the latter age to come,
> If there are seekers of the dharma,
> They should first pay homage to the ācārya
> And receive the three kinds of consecration.
> First listen to and receive all the dharmas,
> Causing the faculties to become fully matured.
> Petition the ācārya,
> Who will transmit them in due order:
> The heart-dharma of the Wish-Fulfilling Wheel.
> When the disciple has received the dharma,
> They should generate a most sincere mind.
> With incense, flowers, and lamp-light,
> Perfume-incense, food and drink, and other offerings,
> First make offerings to all the buddhas.
> Having made offerings according to the method,
> Hold a jewel in the mouth,
> Visualize petitioning all the buddhas:
> 'May I, through this dharma-gate,
> Swiftly attain accomplishment.'
> Then form the wheel-altar mudrā:
> With both hands first perform a rotating dance,
> Then cross the ten fingers behind the back,
> Rotating to the crown and dispersing.
> By forming this mudrā,
> One universally pervades all altars
> And can enter all the secret words."

The wheel-altar mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-cakra hūṃ*

> Next form the samaya
> Lotus secret mudrā:
> Join both hands together,
> With the effort-strength and meditation-wisdom fingers
> Bent like an opening lotus.
> This is called the Wish-Fulfilling Wheel
> Lotus Samādhi Mudrā.

The samādhi mantra:

*Namo ratna-trayāya namaḥ ārya-avalokiteśvarāya bodhisattvāya mahā-sattvāya mahā-kāruṇikāya oṃ samaye saukhye śānte nandhi sarva-samaya-anupraviṣṭa-anurage svāhā*

> Form the above samaya
> And recite this mantra.
> Upon one's own crown,
> The five directions and four corners,
> Empower the nine positions.
> Next empower the six sense faculties
> And both knees,
> Then disperse the mudrā at the forehead.
> Next form the invitation mudrā:
> With both hands in vajra fists,
> Cross the precept-direction and effort-strength fingers
> Behind each other, hooking,
> With meditation-wisdom pressing patience-vow.
> With the two effort-strength fingers
> Moving upward back and forth,
> Hook and invite all the holy assembly.
> Having invited them, perform a rotating dance
> With both hands joined like a lotus.

The invitation mantra:

*Namo mahā-śrīyaye oṃ śāntiye sma-saukhye siddhi-siddhi sādhaya-svāmi oṃ śakari-svākha avaha sarva-ratha sādhaya svāhā*

> Having recited this mantra,
> Before performing the ritual,
> First enter a river.
> Take soil from in front of oneself
> And from the right side,
> Place it in the left hand,
> And mold it into one's own bodily form.
> With the hand, before one's face,
> Circle one's own body three times.
> Then gaze upon it,
> Visualizing one's body, speech, and mind karmas
> And the six defilements of contact
> All entering into that clay body.
> Go to a hidden place
> And, performing the ritual, cast it away.
> With one's own two hands,
> At that hidden place,
> Wash nine times with water,
> Then use bean-powder
> To cleanse the two hands.
> With both hands scoop up water,
> Visualize bathing the Buddha,
> All the bodhisattva assembly,
> And the dharma-ācārya,
> Offering three times.
> With both hands scoop water again.
> Between the right thumb,
> Pour the water from the palm
> To bathe one's parents,
> Bathing nine times.
> Then cleanse one's own body.
> Finally, with the right hand,
> Take water and pour it over the crown,
> Visualizing one's own body
> As pure both within and without.
> Having bathed as above,
> Proceed to the altar entrance.
> Before the altar gate,
> First wash the feet with water,
> Then wash both hands.
> With the right hand turned upward like a vessel,
> Bend the right precept-finger
> And place it in the palm.
> Take water and pour it into the palm.
> Sip the water from the palm three times,
> Visualizing the three karmas becoming pure.
> Next take pure water
> And sprinkle it toward the inside of the altar gate,
> Signifying the method of purifying the altar.
> Recite the root mantra
> To empower the pure water.

The pure-water mantra:

*Oṃ śuddhe-anuśudhanāya svāhā*

> Having recited this mantra,
> Visualize inner and outer purity.
> Next one should invite the buddhas
> And bodhisattvas of the ten directions.
> Then carefully contemplate,
> Visualizing a universal invitation within the mind.
> Prostrate with all five limbs
> And visualize paying homage to all the holy assembly.

The homage and invitation:

*Namo sarva-buddha-bodhisattvebhyo'dhinā-gata-pari-ya loka-dhātu-viyā-vasiddhi-te-bhiyoḥ-nara-veśa-satva-dhātu-pari-trāṇa-kale-bhiyāyethi*

> Having recited this invitation,
> Visualize all things prepared within.
> Then perform the ritual again.
> Sprinkle water within the altar,
> In the four directions and four corners.
> Sprinkle and purify all objects used within the altar.
> Next the practitioner should visualize
> A radiant jeweled pavilion.
> Then visualize a golden lotus,
> Visualizing the lotus expanding broadly,
> Level and even on all four sides,
> Forming the foundation of the jeweled pavilion.

The mantra:

*Oṃ acala-dhire svāhā*

> Next the practitioner should visualize
> The four sides of the pavilion's foundation.
> Then further visualize an ocean
> Whose water transforms into milk.

The ocean-water mantra:

*Oṃ vimala-udadhi hūṃ*

> Then visualize within the milk-ocean,
> Upon a golden lotus,
> Four corner-grounds are placed.
> Upon these, Mount Sumeru is set,
> Eight-cornered and perfectly square.
> Above, visualize a jeweled pavilion
> Adorned with various precious ornaments.

The immovability mantra:

*Oṃ acala hūṃ*

> Next visualize the jeweled pavilion
> With tiers all splendidly adorned,
> Four doors set on all four sides,
> Furnished with various jeweled necklaces
> And a canopy of the seven treasures,
> Placed within the pavilion.
> Further, maṇi pearls
> Form the windows and lattices.
> Then various jeweled beads
> Are strung and hung all around.
> All these varieties of jewels
> Emit various kinds of light,
> Making universal offerings
> To all the tathāgatas of the ten directions
> And all the bodhisattva assembly.

The offering mantra:

*Namo sarva-tathāgatānāṃ sarva-thakhaṃ udgate spharaṇa gagana-khaṃ svāhā*

> Having made the mental offering,
> Within the jeweled pavilion,
> First spread golden ground.
> Above, visualize a lion throne
> With a jeweled lotus placed upon it,
> Complete with stems, leaves, stamens, and pistils.
> The lotus petals have six colors.
> Within the petals,
> Adorn them with hundreds of thousands of jewel-beads
> Interspersed as decorations.
> Outside the central lion throne,
> Visualize hundreds of thousands of kinds
> Of lotus lion thrones
> Surrounding it on all sides,
> Complete with all adornments.

The lotus mantra:

*Oṃ kamale hūṃ*

> Having recited the mantra and visualized,
> When all is laid out according to the method,
> Use jeweled vessels
> Filled with the seven treasures,
> Five grains, fragrant flowers, and other items.
> Visualize them before the holy assembly,
> Scattering and offering with the right foot,
> Extending one's homage.
> From Sukhāvatī,
> Mentally invite the holy assembly
> Together with Avalokiteśvara
> Bodhisattva and his retinue.
> With devoted mind, universally invite them
> To descend into the altar.
> Before the holy assembly,
> Set out various kinds of incense and flowers,
> White whisks, banners, pennants, and canopies,
> Music and lamp-light,
> To welcome all the holy assembly forward:
> "May you compassionately descend and arrive."
> Next form the offering mudrā:
> Cross the precept-direction fingers of both hands,
> With charity-wisdom standing straight upright,
> Patience-vow and effort-strength fingers
> Slightly curved like lotus petals,
> Meditation-wisdom slightly curved.

The offering mantra:

*Oṃ padma-citta-maṇi mahā-padme hūṃ argha-sapratīccha svāhā*

> Next form the invitation mudrā:
> Cross the precept-direction fingers of both hands
> Behind each other, hooking,
> With meditation-wisdom pressing patience-vow,
> Effort-strength hooking back and forth.

The invitation mantra:

*Oṃ ihi ihi cakra-varti-citta-maṇi mahā-padme atra sannihita kuśa svāhā*

> Having welcomed and invited as above,
> Request the holy assembly to be seated.

The seating mantra:

*Oṃ svāgata bhagavaṃ ihi ye hi prasannaṃ dasiya tamihā grahana pūjam asmaka prasannacittiya guru svāhā*

> Next form the seat-offering mudrā:
> With both meditation-wisdom fingers curved,
> Precept-direction fingers slightly open and curved,
> All remaining fingers open and dispersed.

The seat-offering mantra:

*Oṃ cakra-varti mahā-padme ātmanaṃ pratīccha svāhā*

> Next visualize washing the Buddha's feet.

The foot-washing mantra:

*Oṃ pravara-sakṣāra-pratīccha svāhā*

> Having visualized washing the feet
> Of the holy assembly of the ten directions,
> Present all offerings
> Equally and universally.
> Next form the universal offering,
> The mudrā of equal perfection.
> With the right hand's precept, patience, and effort fingers,
> Three fingers standing straight upright,
> Bend the meditation finger to press the charity finger,
> And place it at the side of the chest.
> The left hand rests face-up at the heart,
> Visualizing holding offering items,
> Mentally making universal offerings
> To the tathāgatas of the ten directions
> And all the holy assembly.

The offering mantra:

*Oṃ śaṅkale samaya svāhā*

> Form the wish-fulfilling jewel mudrā:
> Both hands in vajra fists,
> With the effort-strength fingers hooked behind each other.
> Visualize the mind as a jewel form,
> And pull open the effort-strength fingers:
> The wish-fulfilling wheel heart-jewel.

The mantra:

*Oṃ cakra-varti citta-maṇi hūṃ*

> Next, with the wish-fulfilling wheel
> Lotus heart-jewel mudrā,
> Empower the five places:
> The crown, the forehead,
> The throat, the heart, and both shoulders.
> Seal them with the mudrā.
> Visualize at these five places
> The positions of the holy assembly installed,
> And recite the root mantra three times.
> Next, with the heart-jewel mudrā,
> Establish the boundary in the four directions,
> Four corners, above, and below.
> Visualize the inner boundary as complete.

The boundary mantra:

*Oṃ cakra-varti citta-maṇi vatva vatva-tva-niśa hūṃ phaṭ svāhā*

> Form the mudrā and recite the mantra;
> Inner and outer boundaries are complete.
> Next recite the armor-spell
> To protect one's own body.

The armor mantra:

*Oṃ cakra-varti citta-maṇi mahā-padme ruru-tiṣṭha jvala ākarṣāya rakṣa vaṃ hūṃ phaṭ svāhā*

> Having empowered as above,
> Form the vajra-stake mudrā:
> Cross both hands inward,
> With effort-strength fingers upright and touching,
> Meditation-wisdom joined together.
> First use this mudrā
> To empower and protect the body,
> Reciting the previous armor-spell.
> Having empowered the body-protection,
> Point the mudrā toward the ground,
> Visualizing it like driving in a stake.
> Turn the mudrā to the right,
> Visualizing the establishment of the altar-boundary.
> Visualize it like a vajra citadel,
> Inner and outer firmly secured.
> Having visualized according to the method,
> The practitioner should, with devoted sincerity,
> Make the five kinds of offerings,
> Presenting them to all the holy assembly.
> "May you compassionately accept them."
> First form the perfume-incense mudrā:
> With both hands first performing a rotating dance,
> Cross the two meditation-wisdom fingers,
> With the remaining eight fingers standing upright,
> Rubbing outward,
> Visualizing anointing the holy assembly's feet
> And rubbing the Buddha's chest.

The perfume-incense mantra:

*Oṃ padma-citta-maṇi gandhe svāhā*

> Next form the flower-scattering mudrā:
> Both hands touching at the tips,
> In the shape of scooping something up,
> Visualizing placing flowers upon the Buddha's crown.

The flower-scattering mantra:

*Oṃ padma-citta-maṇi puṣpa-pratīccha svāhā*

> Next form the incense-burning mudrā:
> Both hands face-up and crossed,
> With patience finger standing upright.

The incense-burning mantra:

*Oṃ cakra-varti mahā-padme dhūpaya hūṃ*

> Next form the lamp-lighting mudrā:
> Both hands face-up and crossed,
> Patience-vow fingers touching at the tips,
> Meditation-wisdom placed at the sides
> Of the patience-vow fingers.

The lamp-light mantra:

*Oṃ cakra-varti nidīpe svāhā*

> Next form the food-offering mudrā:
> The precept-direction fingers of both hands
> Touch at the tips,
> Remaining fingers slightly curved,
> Like the gesture of holding a bowl of food.

The buddha-food mantra:

*Oṃ cakra-varti mahā-padme piṇḍa-pātraṃ grahana svāhā*

> Having made offerings in this way,
> The practitioner should, with devoted mind,
> Visualize making universal offerings
> To all the holy assembly of the ten directions.

The offering mantra:

*Namo sarva-buddha-bodhisattvānāṃ sarva-thakhaṃ udgate spharaṇa vaṃ gagana-khaṃ svāhā*

> Having made mantra-offerings,
> Visualize paying homage to the eight bodies at the forehead:
> The five directions above the crown,
> And the forehead, mouth, heart, and other places.
> With devoted sincerity, visualize paying homage.

The buddha-homage mantra:

*Namo sarva-buddha-bodhisattvānāṃ sarva-tra-saṃkusu-mitā-bhiñjāna-raśi-nā namo stute svāhā*

> Having paid homage to the holy assembly,
> One should relinquish one's own body,
> Visualizing it as a jeweled pavilion.
> Upon one's inner heart,
> Visualize a lotus-vajra,
> Upon which a sun-moon disc is set.
> Above, visualize the body of true suchness,
> Abiding in serene tranquility
> Within the moon disc.
> Next, upon the moon disc,
> Further visualize the Wish-Fulfilling Wheel
> Lotus King Tathāgata,
> Sitting in stately composure.
> The visualization should be single-minded;
> Do not give rise to distraction.
> Announce to the holy assembly:
> "May you bestow your great compassion
> And show me the way of skillful practice.
> Whatever supreme deeds I accomplish,
> I bestow upon all sentient beings,
> That they may all ascend to true enlightenment."
> Having addressed the holy assembly,
> Further, at the Wheel King's heart,
> Visualize a moon disc installed within.
> Upon the moon, visualize the syllable *hūṃ*,
> The size of a grain of barley,
> With light radiating all around,
> Pervading all the ten directions.

The mantra thus recited:

*Hūṃ*

> Having recited this *hūṃ* syllable,
> Next visualize the *hūṃ* syllable
> Transforming into six-colored light.
> Within this light,
> It transforms into six bodhisattvas,
> Each holding offering items,
> Filling the three-thousand world-system,
> Making universal offerings to all the buddhas,
> The bhagavāns of the three ages.
> When the light-offering is complete,
> Gather the *hūṃ* syllable back
> And return it to the heart's moon disc.
> Upon the *hūṃ* syllable,
> Further transform it into a moon-disc altar,
> A lotus resting upon a vajra.
> As before, with the sun-moon disc
> And the lion throne as before,
> Adorned identically in all respects.
> Above, visualize the Wish-Fulfilling Wheel
> Great Lotus Tathāgata,
> Golden in complexion.
> The right hand holds up
> The wish-fulfilling maṇi jewel.
> The left hand holds
> A great golden lotus.
> The body wears supreme garments,
> With maṇi pearls
> Strung as necklaces.
> Next form the Wish-Fulfilling Wheel
> Maṇi root mudrā:
> Both hands first join palms.
> The effort-strength fingers bend like jewels,
> Patience-vow extended like needles,
> Charity-wisdom and precept-direction fingers
> Standing straight and open.
> Meditation-wisdom slightly curved,
> Visualizing an open lotus.
> Then recite the root vidyā.

The mantra:

*Namo ratna-trayāya namaḥ ārya-avalokiteśvarāya bodhisattvāya mahā-sattvāya mahā-kāruṇikāya tadyathā oṃ cakra-varti cintā-maṇi mahā-padme ruru-tiṣṭha jvala ākarṣāya hūṃ phaṭ svāhā*

> Next form the heart mudrā:
> The tips of both hands' six fingers
> Are each placed between the finger-gaps.
> The two effort-strength fingers are bent,
> With tips half an inch apart.
> Meditation-wisdom fingers open and upright,
> In the shape of an opening lotus.
> Further visualize this mudrā
> Installed within the heart.

The heart mantra:

*Oṃ padma-citta-maṇi jvala hūṃ*

> Form the heart-within-heart mudrā:
> Both meditation-wisdom fingers bent,
> With fingertips touching face to face.
> The remaining eight fingers stand upright,
> Lowering the mudrā downward,
> Visualizing it placed upon the heart-mudrā.

The heart-within-heart mantra:

*Oṃ varada-padme hūṃ*

> Having thus upon one's body
> Arranged and installed with visualization,
> The Wish-Fulfilling Wheel heart-dharma
> And the heart-within-heart mudrā,
> Further, from the heart-within-heart,
> Emanate Kuruḍha,
> Named the Wish-Fulfilling Wheel
> Lotus Heart Vidyā-King.
> His complexion is pale red,
> With strung flowers as necklaces,
> Adorned with various ornaments.
> His right hand holds a hook-staff,
> His left hand holds a lasso.
> He stands to the right of the Buddha
> And addresses the World-Honored One:
> "May the Buddha command and instruct me
> To accomplish all tasks."
> Next, to the left of the Buddha,
> Visualize the Buddha's Rosary-Mother.
> She kneels on the left knee,
> Reddish-white in complexion,
> A rosary hanging from her neck,
> Both hands as if counting beads,
> In the posture of keeping the daily recitation.

The Rosary-Body mantra:

*Oṃ amṛtaṃ ghaṃ-śrīye-śrī-māriṇi svāhā*

> Having recited this mantra,
> Thread it through the eight consciousnesses.
> Hold the rosary in both hands
> And first offer homage above the crown.

The rosary mantra:

*Oṃ vasumati-śrīye svāhā*

> Having empowered with the mantra,
> Further upon the heart,
> Visualize a moon-altar installed.
> Upon it place the rosary,
> Visualize the beads as the Buddha-Mother,
> The vidyā-king, and the mantra
> All merged as one without distinction.
> Whenever performing the daily recitation,
> To the left of the Buddha,
> First visualize the Rosary-Mother
> Seated upon the moon disc.
> Within the mind, extend homage,
> And recite the mantra seven times.
> Having visualized in this way,
> Further within the mind visualize
> Incense, flowers, and lamp-light,
> Mentally making offerings again
> To the Wish-Fulfilling Wheel Tathāgata,
> The Buddha-Mother, and the vidyā-king.
> Next recite the Buddha-Mother's name.

The Buddha-Mother mantra:

*Oṃ śvete-bhadre rāvaśiṇī jaṭā-makuṭa-dhāraṇī svāhā*

> When the empowerment ritual is complete,
> Mentally make offerings again
> To all the buddha and holy assembly.
> Announce and invite them, extending homage.
> Form the previous lotus mudrā,
> Both hands like an open lotus.
> Visualize escorting all the holy assembly
> Back to their original positions.

The dispatch mantra:

*Oṃ cakra-varti citta-maṇi gaccha gaccha svāhā*

> Recite this dispatch vidyā,
> Visualizing all the buddhas and holy assembly
> Each returning to their pure lands.
> Having dispatched the holy assembly,
> Next form the boundary-releasing mudrā:
> The precept-direction fingers of both hands
> And the effort-strength fingers
> All cross and hook,
> Meditation-wisdom pressing patience-vow.
> The two effort-strength fingers separate,
> Visualizing opening the altar gate.
> Turn left three times to release.
> All the vajra stakes
> And the boundary cords
> Are gathered in with the mudrā.

The boundary-releasing mantra:

*Oṃ asamāṅginī vidhe hūṃ phaṭ svāhā*

> With the mudrā reciting the mantra,
> When the inner and outer boundaries have been released,
> At the time of dispatch,
> Further visualize all the holy assembly
> Descending sweet-dew water
> To wash one's own body,
> All karmic sins eliminated,
> Inner and outer completely pure.
> The Buddha then tells the practitioner:
> "Diligently practice this method,
> Do not give rise to weariness.
> Recite and uphold without interruption,
> And before long you will attain accomplishment
> No different from a buddha."
