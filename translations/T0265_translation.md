# Saddharma-puṇḍarīka Sūtra Excerpt

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 265

Translated from the Chinese. Anonymous translator, Western Jin dynasty.

---

Thus have I heard:

The Buddha was dwelling on Mount Gṛdhrakūṭa in Rājagṛha, together with a great assembly of forty-two thousand bhikṣus. There were also eighty-four thousand bodhisattvas including Samantabhadra and Mañjuśrī, and one thousand bodhisattvas of the Bhadrakalpa including Maitreya. Śakra and the gods of Trāyastriṃśa were countless in number. Brahmā and the Brahmā gods were beyond reckoning. King Ajātaśatru attended with the kings of Jambudvīpa, likewise too numerous to count.

The Buddha, amid the four assemblies of bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, and upāsikās, preached the *Saddharma-puṇḍarīka*, expounding innumerable verses.

At that time, a stūpa of the seven treasures surged up from the earth, rising to the Brahmā heaven. In the center of the stūpa was a great lecture hall of the seven treasures, adorned with banners, pennants, and flower canopies, fragrant and pure. Within that splendid hall was a golden seat, and upon the seat sat a buddha named Prabhūtaratna, who praised Śākyamuni Buddha, saying: "Excellent! Excellent! Since my parinirvāṇa, throughout kalpas as numerous as the sands of the Ganges, in buddha-fields as numerous as the sands of the Ganges, I have remained in space. Though buddhas as numerous as the sands of the Ganges have passed, I have through all those kalpas never returned to that land. I have seen Śākyamuni Buddha diligently seeking the buddha path for the sake of living beings, giving without satiation, sparing neither hands, nor eyes, nor head, nor wife and children, elephants, horses, and carriages, nor precious jewels, having no thought of greed or attachment. Therefore I have come forth, wishing to make offerings to Śākyamuni Buddha and to save all the lowly. I ask that Śākyamuni Buddha sit upon my golden seat and further expound the *Saddharma-puṇḍarīka Sūtra*."

Thereupon Śākyamuni Buddha ascended the lecture hall and sat upon the golden seat, then preached the *Saddharma-puṇḍarīka Sūtra*, and again spoke innumerable verses, saying:

> Those who hear of the Buddha Joyful Treasure
> And know his name will not fear birth and death,
> Nor suffer hardship again.
> Those who hear of the Medicine King Buddha
> And know his name
> Shall be healed of illness and recall past lives.

Then Śākyamuni Buddha spoke of innumerable asaṃkhyeya kalpas, and again of innumerable asaṃkhyeya kalpas:

"When I was practicing the bodhisattva path, I sought the *Saddharma-puṇḍarīka Sūtra* and gave it to others. In all my seeking, whether for food, clothing, the seven treasures, wife, or children, I never had the slightest thought of attachment.

"When I was a king in a world of exceedingly long life, I established the crown prince as ruler and abandoned affairs of state. Beating a drum and ringing a bell, I offered myself, declaring: 'Who wishes to take me as a slave? I seek the *Saddharma-puṇḍarīka Sūtra* and wish to make offerings.' At that time, a brāhmaṇa said to me: 'Come and be my slave. I have the *Saddharma-puṇḍarīka Sūtra*.' I then followed the brāhmaṇa, single-mindedly serving as a slave: drawing water, sweeping floors, gathering flowers and fruit, and providing food for the brāhmaṇa, for a thousand years without rest."

The Buddha then spoke in verse:

> Beating the drum and ringing the bell, he vowed,
> Offering himself, asking: "Who wants me?"
> "Take me as your slave,
> For I wish to make offerings,"
> With a servant's heart and virtuous intent.

The Buddha said: "The king at that time was I myself. The brāhmaṇa at that time was Devadatta. Who was it whose kindness enabled me to fulfill the six pāramitās, the thirty-two marks, and the eighty excellent characteristics? It was all through the grace of Devadatta. Devadatta was my good teacher. Through the kindness of this good teacher, I was able to perfect the six pāramitās, the thirty-two marks, and the eighty excellent characteristics, majestic and honored, liberating beings in the ten directions. All this was through the grace of Devadatta. After innumerable asaṃkhyeya kalpas henceforth, Devadatta shall become a buddha named Devarāja, who shall attain the ten powers, the thirty-two marks, and the eighty excellent characteristics. The buddha-land of Devarāja shall be called Devasopāna. Devarāja Buddha shall teach the dharma to beings throughout the entire kalpa without ceasing. In his first turning of the dharma, he shall guide beings as numerous as the sands of the Ganges to attain arhatship, beings as numerous as the sands of the Ganges to attain the path of pratyekabuddha, and beings as numerous as the sands of the Ganges to arouse the aspiration for anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi.

"At that time, Devarāja Buddha's lifespan shall be twenty kalpas, and after his parinirvāṇa, the dharma shall abide for twenty kalpas. After the parinirvāṇa of Devarāja Buddha, his relics shall not be dispersed. A single stūpa of the seven treasures shall be erected, sixty *li* in breadth and eighty *li* in length. All the people of Jambudvīpa shall go to make offerings at the buddha's relics. At that time, innumerable beings shall attain arhatship, innumerable beings shall arouse the aspiration for pratyekabuddha, and innumerable beings shall arouse the aspiration for anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi.

"Good sons and good daughters! Those who hear this Lotus Sūtra and have faith without slander shall have their past and future offenses extinguished. The gates of the three evil paths shall be closed, and the gates of the three good paths shall be opened. They shall always be first among those born in the heavens, always be first among those born in the human realm, and shall be reborn before the buddhas of the ten directions, naturally taking birth within lotus flowers of the seven treasures."

Thereupon, from the buddha-land below, a bodhisattva named Prajñākūṭa addressed his own buddha, wishing to return to his original land. Śākyamuni Buddha said: "Prajñākūṭa! I have a bodhisattva named Mañjuśrī. You should meet with him before returning to your land."

At that moment, Mañjuśrī emerged from the pool of the Nāga King Sāgara, seated upon a great lotus flower as large as a chariot wheel with a thousand petals, accompanied by exceedingly numerous bodhisattvas. Mañjuśrī descended from the great lotus, paid homage to the two buddhas, and then exchanged greetings with Bodhisattva Prajñākūṭa.

Prajñākūṭa asked Mañjuśrī: "In the pool you entered, how many did you guide? What was the number?"

Mañjuśrī replied: "The number is exceedingly great, beyond anyone's ability to reckon. If I were to speak of it, the mind would not believe it. Let the proof come of itself." At that moment, lotus flowers surged up from the pool, entirely produced by all that had been scattered within the pool. Those who had originally aroused the bodhisattva mind rode flowers in the air, speaking only of Mahāyāna matters. Those who had originally aroused the śrāvaka aspiration rode flowers in the air, speaking only of the matter of ending birth and death. Mañjuśrī, seeing the flowers thus, answered Bodhisattva Prajñākūṭa in verse:

> By the Venerable One's own discernment,
> Distinguish their number for yourself.

Bodhisattva Prajñākūṭa again asked Mañjuśrī: "What dharma did you teach that you could guide so many?"

Mañjuśrī replied: "In that pool, I taught only the *Saddharma-puṇḍarīka*."

Prajñākūṭa asked again: "That dharma is supremely honored, with nothing that can equal it. Is there anyone who can swiftly attain buddhahood through it?"

Mañjuśrī replied: "The Nāga King Sāgara has a daughter, eight years of age, of exceedingly great wisdom. Her aspiration is not slight, and she can swiftly attain buddhahood."

Bodhisattva Prajñākūṭa said to Mañjuśrī: "I have seen the Venerable One's teacher seek buddhahood through great effort, accumulating merit over exceedingly many kalpas. I cannot believe that this girl can swiftly attain buddhahood."

The girl in the pool immediately surged forth, circumambulated the Buddha three times, joined her palms together, and addressed the Buddha: "The Buddha's marks are dignified and beautiful, his merit magnificent and lofty, honored by all the gods, revered by all nāgas, spirits, human beings, and bodhisattvas. The dharma he teaches is supremely honored. I now make a vow and wish to attain buddhahood."

Śāriputra then said to the girl: "Though you have made this vow, buddhahood cannot be attained. Moreover, you are a woman. Your accumulated practices do not yet fulfill the bodhisattva path."

The girl held a single *maṇi* jewel, its value equal to that of an entire great kingdom. She swiftly offered it to the Buddha, and the Buddha swiftly accepted it. The girl said to Śāriputra and Bodhisattva Prajñākūṭa: "Was my offering of the jewel to the Buddha swift or slow?"

They replied: "Exceedingly swift!"

The girl asked again: "Was the Buddha's acceptance of my jewel swift or slow?"

They replied: "Exceedingly swift!"

The girl said: "My offering of the jewel to the Buddha was slow; the Buddha's acceptance of my jewel was also slow. My attainment of buddhahood now shall be swift." At that instant, the girl's body transformed into that of a bodhisattva. The entire assembly was astonished. She then transformed into a buddha body, complete with all marks and excellent characteristics, together with a buddha-land and disciples, just as a buddha would have.

The entire assembly of gods, nāgas, spirits, and innumerable beings all aroused the aspiration for the supreme, true path. The great trichiliocosm trembled in six ways. Thirty thousand stream-enterers attained the stage of non-retrogression.
