# Verses in Praise of Amitābha Buddha

## *Zàn Āmítuó Fó Jì* (讚阿彌陀佛偈)

Taishō 1978

Composed by Tánluán (*Tánluán*, 曇鸞) of the Northern Wei dynasty.

*Note: This is a liturgical hymn by Tánluán (476-542 CE), the foundational figure of Chinese Pure Land Buddhism and author of the celebrated Commentary on the Treatise on Rebirth (Wǎngshēng Lùn Zhù). The text consists of 195 verses of praise with 51 prostrations, structured as a devotional liturgy centered on Amitābha Buddha. Each set of verses is preceded by the formula "Homage, with utmost sincerity I take refuge and bow to Amitābha Buddha of the Western [Pure Land]" and followed by the refrain "May I, together with all sentient beings, be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss." The hymn proceeds through Amitābha's twelve light-names (Immeasurable Light, Boundless Light, Unhindered Light, Incomparable Light, King of Blazing Light, Pure Light, Joyful Light, Wisdom Light, Unceasing Light, Inconceivable Light, Ineffable Light, Light Surpassing Sun and Moon), the qualities of the Pure Land assembly (śrāvakas, bodhisattvas, Avalokiteśvara, Mahāsthāmaprāpta), the adornments of the land (jeweled ground, bodhi-tree, music, lotus pools), and concludes with homage to Nāgārjuna as the ancestral master and a personal confession and dedication of merit. The text is an important example of early Chinese Pure Land devotional literature.*

---

Homage to Amitābha Buddha.

Homage: with utmost sincerity I take refuge and bow to Amitābha Buddha of the Western [Pure Land].

> In the west, beyond this world, past ten trillion buddha-lands,
> Lies the Land of Peace and Bliss.
> The World-Honored Buddha named Amitābha:
> Aspiring to rebirth, I bow in refuge.

May I, together with all sentient beings, be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

---

Homage: with utmost sincerity I take refuge and bow to Amitābha Buddha of the Western [Pure Land].

> Since attaining buddhahood, ten kalpas have passed;
> His lifespan stretches on without measure.
> His dharma-body's light-wheel pervades the dharma-realm,
> Illuminating the world's blind darkness: therefore I bow.

May I, together with all sentient beings, be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

---

### The Twelve Light-Names

Homage: with utmost sincerity I take refuge and bow to Amitābha Buddha of the Western [Pure Land].

> His wisdom-light is immeasurable;
> Therefore the Buddha is also called Immeasurable Light.
> All limited forms receive this light's illumination:
> Therefore I bow to the True and Real Radiance.

May I, together with all sentient beings, be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

---

> His liberating light-wheel knows no boundary;
> Therefore the Buddha is also called Boundless Light.
> Those touched by this light depart from existence and non-existence:
> Therefore I bow to the Awakened One of Equality.

May I, together with all sentient beings, be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

---

> His light-cloud is unhindered, like empty space;
> Therefore the Buddha is also called Unhindered Light.
> All that is obstructed receives this light's nourishment:
> Therefore I bow to the Inconceivable One.

May I, together with all sentient beings, be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

---

> His pure radiance is without compare;
> Therefore the Buddha is also called Incomparable Light.
> Those who encounter this light have their karmic bonds removed:
> Therefore I bow to the Ultimate Refuge.

May I, together with all sentient beings, be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

---

> The Buddha's light shines supreme, the very first;
> Therefore the Buddha is also called King of Blazing Light.
> The darkness of the three evil paths receives this light and opens:
> Therefore I bow to the Great Worthy of Offerings.

May I, together with all sentient beings, be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

---

> The light of the Way is brilliant, its color transcendent;
> Therefore the Buddha is also called Pure Light.
> Once bathed in this light, sins and defilements are removed,
> And all attain liberation: therefore I bow.

May I, together with all sentient beings, be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

---

> His compassionate light reaches far, bestowing peace and joy;
> Therefore the Buddha is also called Joyful Light.
> Wherever the light reaches, dharma-joy is obtained:
> I bow to the Great Consoler.

May I, together with all sentient beings, be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

---

> The Buddha's light can shatter the darkness of ignorance;
> Therefore the Buddha is also called Wisdom Light.
> All buddhas and the assemblies of the three vehicles
> Together praise him: therefore I bow.

May I, together with all sentient beings, be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

---

> His radiance universally illumines at all times;
> Therefore the Buddha is also called Unceasing Light.
> Those who hear of this light's power, their minds unceasing,
> All attain rebirth: therefore I bow.

May I, together with all sentient beings, be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

---

> His light, apart from the Buddha, none can fathom;
> Therefore the Buddha is also called Inconceivable Light.
> The buddhas of the ten directions praise rebirth [in his land],
> Extolling his merit: therefore I bow.

May I, together with all sentient beings, be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

---

> His divine light transcends characteristics and cannot be named;
> Therefore the Buddha is also called Ineffable Light.
> Through this light he attained buddhahood, his radiance blazing;
> Praised by all buddhas: therefore I bow.

May I, together with all sentient beings, be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

---

> His radiance surpasses the sun and moon;
> Therefore the Buddha is called Light Surpassing Sun and Moon.
> Even Śākyamuni's praise cannot exhaust [his virtues]:
> Therefore I bow to the Unequaled Among Equals.

May I, together with all sentient beings, be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

---

> In compassion, shelter and protect me;
> Let the seeds of Dharma grow.
> In this life and lives to come,
> May the Buddha always embrace me.

May I, together with all sentient beings, be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

---

Homage: with utmost sincerity I take refuge and bow to Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva (*Guānshìyīn Púsà*) of the Western Land of Ultimate Bliss.

May I, together with all sentient beings, be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

Homage: with utmost sincerity I take refuge and bow to Mahāsthāmaprāpta Bodhisattva (*Dàshìzhì Púsà*) of the Western Land of Ultimate Bliss.

May I, together with all sentient beings, be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

Homage: with utmost sincerity I take refuge and bow to the bodhisattvas and the pure ocean-wide assembly of the Western Land of Ultimate Bliss.

May I, together with all sentient beings, be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

---

Universally, for teachers, saṃgha, parents, spiritual friends, and all sentient beings of the dharma-realm: may the three obstructions be severed, and may all attain rebirth in the land of Amitābha Buddha. Taking refuge, I repent.

---

### The Assembly of the Pure Land

> Amitābha Buddha's initial assembly:
> Śrāvakas and bodhisattvas, numberless in count.
> Their supernatural powers, skillful and wondrous, beyond reckoning:
> Therefore I bow to the Vast Great Assembly.

May I, together with all sentient beings, be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

---

> The immeasurable mahāsattvas of the Land of Peace and Bliss
> Shall all, in one more birth, fill the Buddha's seat,
> Except those whose original vows of great compassion
> Are to universally liberate all sentient beings.
> These are jeweled forests of assembled merit:
> With one mind, palms joined, I bow.

May I, together with all sentient beings, be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

---

> The śrāvakas of the Land of Peace and Bliss each shine one fathom, like shooting stars.
> The bodhisattvas' light-wheels span four thousand leagues, like the full autumn moon reflecting purple gold.
> They gather the Buddha's dharma-treasury for sentient beings:
> Therefore I bow to the Great Ocean of Mind.

May I, together with all sentient beings, be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

---

> Furthermore, Avalokiteśvara and Mahāsthāmaprāpta
> Are foremost among the holy assembly.
> Their compassionate light illumines the great-thousand world;
> Attending the Buddha, left and right, they display divine majesty.
> Liberating all with whom they have affinity, never pausing for a moment,
> Like the great ocean's tides that never miss their time.
> To such Great Compassion and Great Power,
> With one mind I bow.

May I, together with all sentient beings, be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

---

### Qualities of Those Reborn

> All sentient beings born in the Land of Peace and Bliss
> Are endowed with the thirty-two marks.
> Their wisdom is full, they enter the profound Dharma,
> Thoroughly penetrating the essentials of the Way, without obstruction.
> According to their faculties, sharp or dull, they accomplish patience:
> Two kinds of patience, and even incalculable [further levels].
> Recollection of past lives and the five supernatural powers are always at hand;
> Until buddhahood, they never again fall into evil destinies,
> Except when born in other realms of the five turbidities,
> Manifesting just as the Great Muni [Śākyamuni] did.
> To be born in the Land of Peace and Bliss, accomplishing great benefit:
> Therefore, with utmost sincerity, I bow.

May I, together with all sentient beings, be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

---

### The Bodhisattvas' Activity

> The bodhisattvas of the Land of Peace and Bliss, sustained by the Buddha's divine power,
> In the space of a single meal visit the ten directions,
> Paying reverent homage to the buddhas of
> Incalculable, innumerable buddha-worlds.
> Flowers, incense, music manifest from thought;
> Jeweled canopies and banners appear at will.
> Rare and extraordinary treasures beyond the world's naming
> Are scattered as offerings, wondrous and precious.
> Transformed into flower-canopies, their radiance dazzling;
> Their fragrance pervades universally, reaching everywhere.
> The smallest canopies span four hundred leagues,
> And some cover an entire buddha-land.
> As each [canopy] dissolves, the next transforms in sequence;
> These bodhisattvas all rejoice.
> In empty space they play celestial music,
> Singing elegant praises, extolling the Buddha's wisdom.
> Having heard the Dharma and made offerings,
> Before the meal is done they soar back through the void.
> Their divine powers, free and sovereign, are inconceivable:
> Therefore I bow to the Supreme Honored One.

May I, together with all sentient beings, be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

---

### Qualities of the Inhabitants

> The bodhisattvas of the Land of Amitābha
> May indeed be described according to their wisdom.
> Toward themselves and all things they have abandoned "mine";
> Pure as a lotus unstained by dust.
> Coming and going, advancing and stopping, as if drifting in a boat,
> Benefit and peace are their sole concern, indifferent to comfort.
> Self and other alike are empty; the two conceptions severed;
> Yet the torch of wisdom illumines the long night.
> The three insights and six supernatural powers are all complete;
> The bodhisattva's myriad practices penetrate the mind's eye.
> Such merit is boundless:
> Therefore, with utmost sincerity, I bow.

May I, together with all sentient beings, be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

---

> The śrāvakas, bodhisattvas, devas, and humans of the Land of Peace and Bliss
> All possess penetrating wisdom.
> Their bodily forms and adornments are without distinction;
> The names are listed only in accord with other lands' [conventions].
> Their countenances are dignified beyond compare;
> Their subtle, wondrous forms are neither human nor divine.
> Bodies of emptiness, bodies without limit:
> Therefore I bow to the Power of Equality.

May I, together with all sentient beings, be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

---

> All who are born in the Land of Peace and Bliss
> Dwell entirely in the assembly of right determination.
> Wrong determination and indeterminate [states] do not exist there:
> All buddhas praise this; therefore I bow.

May I, together with all sentient beings, be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

---

> All who hear the name of Amitābha Buddha,
> With trusting hearts rejoicing and celebrating what they have heard,
> Even with a single thought of utmost sincerity,
> Transferring [this merit] and aspiring to rebirth, shall all attain it,
> Except for those who commit the five heinous offenses or slander the correct Dharma:
> Therefore I bow, aspiring to rebirth.

May I, together with all sentient beings, be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

---

### Adornments of the Pure Land

[The hymn continues with verses praising: the surpassing beauty of the Pure Land inhabitants compared to worldly beings (from beggars to wheel-turning kings to the six heavens, each a thousand-million-fold inferior); the wish-fulfilling provisions (jeweled halls, food, clothing all manifesting according to desire); the Pure Land's sustenance (seeing color and smelling fragrance suffice as food, which then vanishes when finished); the innumerable bodhisattvas and śrāvakas from the ten directions who have been born, are being born, and will be born there; the merit of hearing Amitābha's name (even a single thought yields great benefit); the coming of bodhisattvas from the eastern and all other directions to pay homage and make offerings.]

---

### The Dharma Assembly

> When the Holy Lord, the World-Honored One, teaches the Dharma,
> The great assembly gathers like clouds in the seven-jeweled hall.
> Hearing the Buddha reveal and explain, all awaken and enter;
> Joy fills them completely, and all attain the Way.
> At that time, gentle breezes arise on all four sides,
> Stirring the jeweled trees, producing wondrous sounds,
> Harmonious and clear, surpassing strings and woodwinds,
> Exceeding bells and chimes beyond compare.
> Celestial flowers drift and swirl, following the fragrant wind,
> A natural offering, ceaseless.
> The devas further bring celestial flowers and incense,
> With hundreds of thousands of instruments to pay their respects.
> Such merit of the Three Treasures assembled:
> Therefore I turn my thoughts and bow to the Dharma hall.

May I, together with all sentient beings, be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

---

### The Land, the Bodhi-Tree, and the Pools

> The wondrous land, vast beyond reckoning,
> Is naturally formed of the seven treasures combined.
> Adorned through the Buddha's original vow-power:
> I bow to the Great Embracer of Purity.

> The world's radiance is wondrously supreme;
> Delightful, peaceful, and serene, without the four seasons.
> The power of benefiting self and others is perfectly complete:
> I take refuge in the Skillfully Adorned One through Expedient Means.
> The jeweled ground is clear, still, and level as the palm,
> Without mountains, rivers, ridges, or valleys.
> If, through the Buddha's divine power, they are needed, they then appear:
> I bow to the Inconceivable Honored One.

> The bodhi-tree is four million leagues in height,
> Fifty yojanas in circumference.
> Its branches and leaves spread two hundred thousand [yojanas],
> Naturally formed of assembled treasures.
> Moon-light maṇi gems and ocean-wheel jewels,
> The kings of many treasures, adorn it.
> All around, jeweled garlands hang in layers;
> Hundreds of thousands of myriads of colors shift and change.
> Its radiance outshines a thousand suns;
> An infinite jeweled net covers it above.
> All adornments manifest as needed:
> I bow to the Bodhi-Tree of the Place of Awakening.

> Gentle breezes blow through the trees, producing sounds of Dharma,
> Streaming universally to all buddha-lands in the ten directions.
> Those who hear these sounds attain the profound dharma-patience;
> Until attaining buddhahood, they encounter no suffering.
> Divine power, vast and great, inconceivable:
> I bow to the Bodhi-Tree of the Place of Awakening.

> The tree's fragrance, tree's color, tree's sound,
> Tree's touch, tree's taste, and tree's dharma:
> The six faculties encountering these all attain dharma-patience:
> Therefore I bow to the Bodhi-Tree of the Place of Awakening.

[Additional verses praise the bodhi-tree's power to purify the six faculties, tracing its efficacy to the Tathāgata's five powers (divine power, original vow, fulfilled vows, clear understanding and firmness, and ultimate vow); the music of the Pure Land surpassing that from earthly kings through the six heavens; the jeweled trees and their mutual reflection; the jeweled nets with golden threads and pearl-jewels covering the land; the gentle wind scattering flowers; and the lotus flowers filling the world with their radiance.]

---

### The Lotus Flowers and Emanation Buddhas

> Each and every flower sends forth light:
> Thirty-six hundred thousand million [rays].
> Within each ray there is a buddha-body,
> In number also like the rays emitted.
> The buddha-bodies, with marks like golden mountains,
> Each again emit hundreds of thousands of rays,
> Universally teaching the wondrous Dharma to the ten directions,
> Each establishing sentient beings on the buddha-way.
> Such divine power is boundless:
> Therefore I take refuge in Amitābha.

May I, together with all sentient beings, be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

---

### The Pools of Eight Virtues

> Pavilions and halls, not built by artisans,
> Are carved from the seven treasures, formed by transformation.
> Bright moon-pearl pendants and interlaced dew-curtains;
> Each has a bathing pool of fitting shape.
> Water of the eight virtues fills the pools,
> Its color, taste, and fragrance pure as sweet dew.
> Golden pools have white silver sand;
> The seven-jeweled pools, sands interchanging likewise.
> Fragrant trees line the banks, their branches drooping;
> Sandalwood's fragrance constantly wafts.
> Celestial flowers, dazzling, serve as ornament;
> Above the water, they glimmer like luminous clouds.
> The fruit of the uncontaminated is inconceivable:
> Therefore I bow to the Treasury of Merit.

May I, together with all sentient beings, be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

---

> Bodhisattvas and śrāvakas enter the jeweled pools;
> The depth responds to each one's wish.
> If one wants the water to pour over the body, it pours naturally;
> If one wants it to flow back, the water returns at once.
> Warmth and coolness are perfectly suited, without exception;
> The spirit opens, the body is refreshed, and the mind's defilements are cleansed.
> Clear, bright, limpid, and pure, as if formless;
> The jeweled sands shine through as if without depth.
> Gently rippling, turning, the waters pour into one another;
> Graceful and at ease, harmonizing body and spirit.
> Subtle waves, measureless, produce wondrous sounds;
> Each hears the Dharma-words suited to their needs.
> Some hear the wondrous chapter of the Three Treasures;
> Some hear of quiescence, emptiness, and non-self.
> Some hear of the immeasurable pāramitās,
> The [Buddha's] exclusive powers, unshared dharmas, and all supernatural wisdoms.
> Some hear of non-action and the patience of non-arising,
> Even up to the dharma of anointing the crown with sweet dew.
> According to each one's faculties and dispositions, all rejoice,
> Conforming to the true meaning of the characteristics of the Three Treasures.
> The path practiced by bodhisattvas and śrāvakas:
> All of this is fully heard here.
> The name of suffering in the three evil paths is permanently closed;
> There are only the natural sounds of happiness and bliss.
> Therefore this land is called "Peace and Bliss":
> I bow to the Infinite Honored One.

May I, together with all sentient beings, be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

---

### Homage to Nāgārjuna

> Our ancestral master, Nāgārjuna Mahāsattva,
> Born into this world, he restored the crumbling framework of the Dharma.
> Closing the doors of the heterodox, he opened the correct track:
> He is the eye of all in Jambudvīpa.
> I humbly hear that he joyfully [dwells on] the Stage of Joy,
> And will take refuge in Amitābha to be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

May I, together with all sentient beings, be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

---

> Just as when the dragon moves, clouds must follow,
> And in Jambudvīpa, a hundred flowers bloom:
> Homage to the compassionate Nāgārjuna,
> With utmost sincerity, I take refuge and bow.

May I, together with all sentient beings, be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

---

### Personal Dedication

> Since beginningless time I have cycled through the three realms,
> Turned by the wheel of false delusion.
> The karma created in a single thought, a single moment,
> Is enough to bind one to the great earth and trap one in the three evil paths.
> May the compassionate light protect and be mindful of me,
> That I may not lose the mind of bodhi.
> I praise the Buddha's wisdom and merit in these sounds;
> May all with affinity throughout the ten directions hear them.
> All who wish to be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss:
> May all fulfill their wishes without obstruction.
> Whatever merit I possess, whether great or small,
> I transfer to all, to be reborn together.
> Homage to the Inconceivable Light:
> With one mind I take refuge and bow.

May I, together with all sentient beings, be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

---

### Conclusion

> The buddhas of the ten directions, in the three times, of immeasurable wisdom,
> All ride the single Suchness, called "Perfectly Awakened."
> Their two wisdoms perfectly complete, the Way is equal;
> Their transformative guidance follows conditions, hence is various.
> My taking refuge in Amitābha's pure land
> Is taking refuge in the lands of all buddhas.
> With one mind I praise one buddha;
> May this reach the unhindered ones of the ten directions.
> To all such buddhas of the ten directions, immeasurable,
> With utmost sincerity I bow.

May I, together with all sentient beings, be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

---

[Closing prostrations to Avalokiteśvara, Mahāsthāmaprāpta, and the bodhisattvas and pure ocean-wide assembly of the Western Land of Ultimate Bliss, with universal dedication of merit.]

195 verses of praise, 51 prostrations.
