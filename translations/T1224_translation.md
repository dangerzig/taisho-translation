# 金剛童子持念經
## Sūtra of the Vajra Youth's Recitation Practice

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1224

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown.

---

The true word for purifying the three karmas:

*Oṃ svadhi-nu-śuddha-nāya svāhā*

Join both palms in lotus añjali and recite the preceding true word seven times to empower the three karmas of body, mind, and speech. All deluded thoughts and past transgressions are entirely purified.

Next, form the universal homage to all tathāgatas seal. Place the right knee on the ground. With both hands, left palm down and right palm up, backs touching, cross both ring fingers and little fingers inversely and place them upon the crown. Recite the true word with the mouth, maintain this posture with the body, and with the mind visualize one's own body filling the dharmadhātu, bowing with utmost sincerity before the buddhas and bodhisattvas. The true word:

*Oṃ vajra-vīta*

Having thus employed the three karmas in unison to bow before and contemplate all the holy assembly and the maṇḍala's principal deity image, sit in full lotus or half lotus, settle the body and mind, and form the Buddha-family samaya seal. Join both palms in lotus añjali, opening the hands as if scooping. Place the two samādhi-fingers alongside the upper joints of the two mindfulness-fingers, and with the two wisdom-fingers pinch the first joint below the two samādhi-fingers. The seal is complete. Then recite the Buddha-family samaya true word:

*Oṃ tathāgato-dbhavāya svāhā*

Place the seal upon the crown, recite the true word three times, and visualize the seal as the Buddha's form. Next, form the Lotus-family samaya seal. Join both palms in añjali as before, slightly open. The six fingers, consisting of the two middle, two index, and two samādhi fingers, are slightly bent, their tips about one inch apart from each other. The two ring fingers and two little fingers each stand upright side by side, like an open lotus flower. Recite the true word three times, raise the seal to the right of the crown above the right ear, and visualize it as Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva. The Lotus samaya true word:

*Oṃ padmo-dbhavāya svāhā*

Next, form the Vajra-family samaya seal. Use the universal homage seal as before and place it upon the heart. Recite the true word three times, raise the seal to the left of the crown above the left ear, and visualize it as Vajragarbha Bodhisattva. The Vajra-family samaya true word:

*Oṃ vajro-dbhavāya svāhā*

Then form the protection seal. Cross both hands, right over left, internally interlaced and standing upright. The two mindfulness-fingers touch tip to tip. The two samādhi-fingers are slightly bent behind the mindfulness-fingers without touching, in the form of a three-pronged vajra. The two wisdom-fingers stand side by side, pressing down in the palm upon the index and ring fingers. The seal is complete. In the original sūtra, the root seal may also be used. Here we follow the universal method from the Susiddhikara Sūtra so as not to be restricted. The protection true word:

*Oṃ sarva-duṣṭa-vāśaṃ-kara-kaṇi kuru-dharakṣa-rakṣa māṃ svāhā*

By forming this seal and reciting the preceding true word, empower the five points of one's own body: first the forehead, then the right shoulder, left shoulder, heart, and throat. These are the five points. By the awesome power of the true word, empowering one's own body, one is clad in vajra armor.

Next, form the seal for expelling obstacles and binding Vināyakas. Interlock all ten fingers internally. Bend both wisdom-fingers into the palms with nails back to back, each pressing the face of the mindfulness-fingers. Grip tightly into fists. With the mind, visualize the Vināyakas and all obstacle-makers being bound in fivefold bondage. The expulsion true word:

*Oṃ dhuna vidhuna trāsaya śuṣaya sarva-vināyakānāṃ hūṃ phaṭ svāhā*

Next, form the vajra armor seal. Using the previous seal, stand both samādhi-fingers upright with tips touching. With both wisdom-fingers, each pinch the first joint below the samādhi-fingers. The seal is complete. Empower the five points of one's body as before with this seal, and the entire body is naturally clad in vajra armor, its radiance blazing. The true word:

*Oṃ hrīḥ kaṇi-yama-daha hūṃ phaṭ svāhā*

Next, form the vajra wall seal. Using the preceding armor seal, separate the two samādhi-finger tips by about one inch and open the palms in a wall shape. The seal is complete. The true word:

*Oṃ hrīḥ kaṇi-daha-daha paca-paca hūṃ phaṭ svāhā*

With the seal above the crown, rotate clockwise three times while reciting the true word three times. By the empowered spiritual power, a vajra wall is established all around the four directions, near or far as the mind determines. The boundary radiates with light, and all māras dare not approach.

Next, form the invitation seal for the holy honored one. Interlock both hands internally and stand upright. The two samādhi-finger tips touch. With both wisdom-fingers, beckon toward oneself. The invitation true word:

*Oṃ dhuni bhagavān kaṇi-ṭhunru-dhā ehi-hi hūṃ phaṭ*

Without changing this seal, recite the following true word to invite the holy honored one's retinue. The true word:

*Oṃ saparivāra mahā-vāhaya bhagavān kaṇi-kuru-dha samaya manusmara svāhā*

Next, form the seal of the sovereign vidyārāja Kuṇḍalī, to expel māras who might be hiding among the holy assembly. By the vidyārāja's awesome power, search out and dispatch the obstacle-makers, retaining only the holy ones and their retinue. The seal: cross both ring fingers, right over left, internally. Bend both index fingers together, pressing them upon the crossed ring fingers. Both mindfulness-fingers stand upright with tips touching. Both samādhi-fingers are each slightly bent behind the mindfulness-fingers without touching, in the form of a three-pronged vajra. Both wisdom-fingers stand side by side pressing upon the index fingers. The seal is complete. While reciting the true word, rotate the seal counterclockwise three times in the air to expel and dispatch deceptive māras. Then rotate clockwise three times to establish the vajra radiant, firm great boundary. No being, common or holy, may cross this boundary. Those who transgress will certainly find no peace through the samaya; the holy assembly punishes transgressors, for such is the oath of the holy assembly. Recite the Kuṇḍalī true word:

*Namo ratna-trayāya namaś-caṇḍa-vajra-pāṇaye mahā-yakṣa-senā-pataye oṃ amṛta-kuṇḍali khakha khāhi khāhi tiṣṭha tiṣṭha bandha bandha hana hana garja garja visphoṭaya visphoṭaya sarva-vighnān vināyaka mahā-gaṇa-pati jīvitānta-karāya hūṃ phaṭ*

Then form the upper-direction vajra net seal. Using the previous wall seal, spread and stand both wisdom-fingers upright. The seal is complete. Rotate the seal clockwise three times above the crown while reciting the true word three times. A vajra net is then established covering everything above. The true word:

*Oṃ vajra-pañjara hūṃ phaṭ*

Next, offer the arghya fragrant water to bathe the holy honored one. Using the previous invitation seal, with both wisdom-fingers each pinch the first joint at the base of the samādhi-fingers. Hold the seal level at the heart like a flat cup, then raise the seal to the forehead to present the offering. If there is a fragrant water vessel, press this seal upon it and empower it three times or once, then lift and present it. The true word:

*Oṃ vajro-daka hūṃ*

Next, form the jewel mountain seal. Using the single-pronged vajra seal, flatten the upper joint like the shape of a summit. Both wisdom-fingers stand side by side, pressing down in the palm upon the other fingers. The seal is complete. Recite the jewel mountain true word:

*Oṃ acala hūṃ*

Next, offer the lotus seat seal. Using the preceding arghya seal, turn both wisdom-finger tips outward and stand them apart by about half an inch in a lotus shape. The true word:

*Oṃ vajra-virāya svāhā*

Next, offer the five offerings. First, form the unguent seal. Using the previous single-pronged vajra seal, slightly bend both wisdom-fingers to pinch the first joint below the samādhi-fingers, about one inch apart. The seal is complete. The unguent true word:

*Oṃ kaṇi kuru-dha sarva-sattvā-bhayaṃ-kara-rakṣa-rakṣa sarva-gandhī svāhā*

Next, form the flower offering seal. Using the previous single-pronged vajra seal, extend the right wisdom-finger. The seal is complete. Recite the flower offering true word:

*Oṃ kaṇi puṣpa-mudbhava hūṃ*

Next, form the incense seal. Using the previous single-pronged seal, extend the left wisdom-finger. The seal is complete. Recite the incense true word:

*Oṃ kaṇi kuru-dha uru-niṣṭhā-rutkaṭa-paira-vagṛhṇa-gṛhṇa dhūpa svāhā*

Next, form the food offering seal. Using the preceding single-pronged vajra seal, extend both wisdom-fingers and both ring-fingers with tips touching, while the remaining four fingers interlock internally as fists. The seal is complete. The true word:

*Oṃ kaṇi hūṃ hūṃ svāhā*

Next, form the lamp seal. Using the previous single-pronged vajra seal, turn both wisdom-fingers toward oneself and stand them upright side by side. The seal is complete. Recite the lamp true word:

*Oṃ kaṇi dīpa-nidipāya hūṃ phaṭ svāhā*

Next, form the universal offering seal. Join both palms with right over left, cross at the upper joint. The seal is complete. Visualize flowing forth from this seal various offering clouds and oceans, transforming into palaces, towers, and pavilions of the seven treasures, garments, food and drink, and the finest celestial music filling the dharmadhātu, making offerings to all the holy assembly. Here only one or two are briefly mentioned; if described in full, they are as stated in the Avataṃsaka Sūtra regarding offerings. Recite this offering true word three or seven times:

*Oṃ amogha-maṇi-padma-jvāla-pravartāya hūṃ*

Having completed all the offerings above, with the right hand cast the vajra pestle. Recite the root true word. Before the holy assembly, with the mind visualizing as if in the presence of the most honored, perform the trampling dance, imitating Vajrasattva casting and tossing his pestle before the Tathāgata. Then empower the five points of one's own body. With the left hand take the vajra bell and shake it three times before the forehead, signifying the offering of celestial music to the holy assembly to bring them delight. Also recite the root true word, with the mind abiding in Sattva's wondrous samādhi.

The practitioner, fearing that during the course of attendance and offerings there may have been forgetfulness, errors, or transgressions of the samaya precepts, or that karmic obstacles from many past lives may not yet be eliminated, should recite the hundred-syllable true word of the Vajra family to supplement and remedy, extinguishing all faults, so that body and mind become pure and in accord with the dharma. The hundred-syllable true word:

*Oṃ vajra-sattva samayam anupālaya vajra-sattva tvenopatiṣṭha dṛḍho me bhava sutoṣyo me bhava anurakto me bhava supoṣyo me bhava sarva-siddhiṃ me prayaccha sarva-karma su ca me cittaṃ śreyaḥ kuru hūṃ ha ha ha ha hoḥ bhagavan sarva-tathāgata-vajra mā me muñca vajrī-bhava mahā-samaya-sattva āḥ*

The seal: cross both hands externally, right over left, forming fists. Stand both middle fingers upright and join them. This is called the samaya seal. By forming this seal and empowering the four places of the body, consisting of heart, forehead, throat, and crown, while reciting the hundred-syllable true word, one can eliminate all grave offenses from beginningless time: breaking the four major prohibitions, the five heinous offenses, slandering the Vaipulya sūtras, and all such offenses are entirely eradicated. How much more so minor errors and inadvertent impurities!

Then form the principal deity's seal. Join both palms. Place the left ring finger in front and below, the right ring finger behind and above pressing the left ring finger's back. Cross the fingers behind both middle fingers. Both index fingers each hook the tips of the ring fingers. Both middle fingers stand upright and joined. Both thumbs also stand upright side by side, pressing the middle joint of the middle fingers. Both little fingers join upright. The seal is complete. Recite the root true word and empower the four places of the body with the seal. The true word:

*Namo ratna-trayāya namaś-caṇḍa-vajra-pāṇaye mahā-yakṣa-senā-pataye tadyathā oṃ kaṇi-dhuni hūṃ phaṭ svāhā*

Next, form the second root seal. This is the very first protection seal described above, also called the single-pronged vajra seal. It is the same as the Tārā Bodhisattva seal. The second root true word:

*Namo ratna-trayāya namaś-caṇḍa-vajra-pāṇaye mahā-yakṣa-senā-pataye tadyathā oṃ dhuna vidhuna kaṇi kuru-dha sarva-yantrāṇi hūṃ*

Next, form the head seal. Using the preceding single-pronged vajra seal, with both thumbs each hook the ring-finger tips, pressing the inner upper joint within the palm. The seal is complete. The true word:

*Oṃ hūṃ hūṃ mama phaṭ*

Next, form the crown seal. Using the preceding single-pronged vajra seal, stand both thumbs upright with tips touching. The seal is complete. The true word:

*Oṃ hrīḥ caṇḍa-kaṇi-daha-paca hūṃ phaṭ svāhā*

Next, form the heart seal. Using the preceding root seal, place it upon the heart and empower once with the heart true word:

*Oṃ kaṇi hūṃ phaṭ*

Next, hold the rosary at the heart and empower it seven times with the accompanying heart true word:

*Oṃ kaṇi-dhuma hūṃ phaṭ*

With both hands hold the rosary at the heart and recite. Sit with the body upright and still, contemplating intently. Let the mind abide in the awesome virtue and wondrous contemplation of the principal deity, as a painted image appears like a reflection in a mirror: not one, not different, pure and without blemish. Outwardly visualize the radiant light; let the mind abide in the wondrous contemplation of compassion, pitying the beings of the six destinies and four modes of birth, ceaselessly turning in saṃsāra. "I now generate the mind of fierce, valiant faith. With utmost sincerity I recite the divine and wondrous phrases, the mind abiding in the yoga contemplative wisdom of the principal deity. Now that my body and mind are purified, I dedicate this merit and spiritual power to pervade and benefit the entire dharmadhātu, universally aiding all sentient beings. May they receive this empowerment and become as my own body."

If one follows the original sūtra, there are fifteen additional true words and seals, all of which should be recited and practiced until thoroughly mastered, to be selected and used when circumstances require, in preparation for urgent situations to supplement any deficiency. They are not for constant use. They are fully described in the great sūtra and are not repeated here. If needed in an emergency, one may look them up and use them.
