# 大乘寶月童子問法經
## Mahāyāna Sūtra of the Boy Ratnacandra's Inquiry into the Dharma

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0437

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dānapāla (施護)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the city of Rājagṛha, on Vulture Peak Mountain, together with a great assembly of fifty-five thousand koṭi bhikṣus; the Bodhisattva Invincible and the rest, eighty thousand hundred-thousand innumerable koṭi nayutas of those single-mindedly practicing the conduct of bodhi; and devas, humans, and others numbering a hundred thousand nayutas.

At that time, the World-Honored One, as the time for the meal arrived, donned his robes and took up his bowl. Together with the bhikṣu assembly, the bodhisattvas, devas, nāgas, spirits, and the rest, respectfully surrounding him, he entered the great city of Rājagṛha to beg for food.

At that time, Prince Ratnacandra, the son of King Bimbisāra, was going out of the great city of Rājagṛha on some business, mounted upon a great dragon-elephant. Seeing the World-Honored One from afar, he descended from the dragon-elephant and went to the Buddha's place. When he arrived, he paid his respects, bowed his head to the Buddha's feet, and stood to one side. The boy Ratnacandra addressed the Buddha: "Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha! I beg the World-Honored One to proclaim the names of the Tathāgatas of the ten directions. If there are faithful good men and good women who, having heard these names, have all their sins of the five acts of immediate retribution and all karmic obstructions entirely eliminated and swiftly attain non-retrogression from unsurpassed, perfect enlightenment, may the World-Honored One proclaim them."

At that time, the World-Honored One told the boy Ratnacandra: "Excellent! Excellent! That you delight in hearing the meaning of the Tathāgatas' names is an excellent intention. All sins and karmic obstructions will certainly be eliminated. Young one! Listen carefully and reflect well upon this! I shall explain it for you.

"Young one! Beyond a hundred thousand koṭi nayutas of Ganges-sand buddha-lands to the east, there is a world called Without Sorrow. In that world there is a Tathāgata named Worthy Auspicious, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, free from illness and affliction, who constantly teaches the wondrous Dharma for all sentient beings. That Buddha's lifespan is sixty thousand hundred-thousand koṭi nayuta innumerable kalpas. In that world there is no sun or moon, day or night; only the Buddha's radiance universally illuminates all, and also illuminates the beings of the hells, the animal realm, and the Yama realm, causing them to attain liberation. Immeasurable beings attain the patience of non-arising."

The Buddha said: "Young one! In the south, beyond a hundred thousand koṭi nayutas of Ganges-sand buddha-lands, there is a world called Tranquil. In that world there is a Tathāgata named Boundless Radiance, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who constantly teaches the wondrous Dharma for all sentient beings."

The Buddha said: "Young one! In the west, beyond a hundred thousand koṭi nayutas of Ganges-sand buddha-lands, there is a world called Joyful. In that world there is a Tathāgata named Joy Auspicious, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who constantly teaches the wondrous Dharma for all sentient beings."

The Buddha said: "Young one! In the north, beyond a hundred thousand koṭi nayutas of Ganges-sand buddha-lands, there is a world called Immovable. In that world there is a Tathāgata named Jewel Banner, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who constantly teaches the wondrous Dharma for all sentient beings."

The Buddha said: "Young one! In the southeast, beyond a hundred thousand koṭi nayutas of Ganges-sand buddha-lands, there is a world called Correct Practice. In that world there is a Tathāgata named Sorrowless Auspicious, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who constantly teaches the wondrous Dharma for all sentient beings."

The Buddha said: "Young one! In the southwest, beyond a hundred thousand koṭi nayutas of Ganges-sand buddha-lands, there is a world called Jewel Banner Auspicious. In that world there is a Tathāgata named Jewel Banner, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who constantly teaches the wondrous Dharma for all sentient beings."

The Buddha said: "Young one! In the northwest, beyond a hundred thousand koṭi nayutas of Ganges-sand buddha-lands, there is a world called Wondrous Sound. In that world there is a Tathāgata named Auspicious Flower, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who constantly teaches the wondrous Dharma for all sentient beings."

The Buddha said: "Young one! In the northeast, beyond a hundred thousand koṭi nayutas of Ganges-sand buddha-lands, there is a world called Peaceful Happiness. In that world there is a Tathāgata named Lotus Radiance Playful Wisdom, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who constantly teaches the wondrous Dharma for all sentient beings."

The Buddha said: "Young one! In the direction below, beyond a hundred thousand koṭi nayutas of Ganges-sand buddha-lands, there is a world called Vast. In that world there is a Tathāgata named Radiance Auspicious, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who constantly teaches the wondrous Dharma for all sentient beings."

The Buddha said: "Young one! In the direction above, beyond a hundred thousand koṭi nayutas of Ganges-sand buddha-lands, there is a world called Moonlight. In that world there is a Tathāgata named Wealth Auspicious, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who constantly teaches the wondrous Dharma for all sentient beings.

"Young one! All these worlds and buddha-lands have pure candana pavilions, and the names of all these Tathāgatas, if any person hears them, respectfully receives and upholds them, copies, reads, and recites them, and broadly explains them for others, all sins including the five acts of immediate retribution will be entirely eliminated. They will not fall into the hells, the animal realm, the Yama realm, and they will swiftly attain non-retrogression from unsurpassed, perfect enlightenment.

"What do you think, young one? In the past, in the great incalculable and vastly boundless incalculable kalpa, there was a world called Jewel-Producing. In that world there was a Tathāgata named Diligent Auspicious, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha. These ten Tathāgatas, when they were in the bodhisattva stage before the Buddha Diligent Auspicious, made offerings and vowed: 'When each of us attains unsurpassed, perfect enlightenment in our respective buddha-lands, if there are sentient beings who, even for an instant or a moment, hear the names of us ten Buddhas, and having heard them, respectfully receive, uphold, copy, read, and recite them, and broadly explain them for others, all their sins including the five acts of immediate retribution will be entirely eliminated. They will not fall into the hells, the animal realm, the Yama realm, and they will swiftly attain non-retrogression from unsurpassed, perfect enlightenment.'"

At that time, the boy Ratnacandra, having heard this, asked: "World-Honored One! What is the lifespan of that Buddha Tathāgata?"

The World-Honored One answered: "That Buddha's lifespan is ten asaṃkhyeya hundred-thousand koṭi nayuta world-systems' atoms of dust in number of kalpas."

The boy, having heard this, addressed the Buddha: "That Buddha Tathāgata is truly extraordinary! With such a compassionate aspiration and vow, for the sake of all sentient beings he attained such a lifespan."

The Buddha said: "Young one! If there are sentient beings who hear the names of these ten Buddhas, respectfully receive, uphold, copy, read, recite, and practice them with joyful faith, then all immeasurable, boundless merit will be fully achieved, and the sins of the three karmas will be unable to arise.

"Young one! If good men and good women were to fill Ganges-sand buddha-lands with the seven treasures and use them as offerings to the Tathāgatas for a hundred thousand years, the merit would be immeasurable. Yet if good men and good women hear the names of these ten Buddhas, respectfully believe, receive, copy, read, recite, and explain them for others, the merit attained surpasses the former offerings without limit."

At that time, the Lord of the Sahā world, the great Brahmā king, Śakra Lord of the Devas, the four great heavenly kings, the Suyāma deva prince, the Maheśvara deva prince, using the finest candana powder, scattered it before the World-Honored One as offerings, and addressed the World-Honored One: "If there are sentient beings who copy, read, recite, and faithfully receive and uphold this correct Dharma, they will be respected and bowed to by all devas, humans, and asuras. All those in the hells, the animal realm, the Yama realm, the asura realm, and the hungry ghost realm will all be liberated."

The Buddha said: "If this correct Dharma reaches the ears and is received, upheld, read, and recited, the evil of sinful māras and enemies will not be able to invade or harm, and one will swiftly attain non-retrogression from anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi."

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> Upholding the names of these ten World-Honored Ones,
> With lifespans of koṭi kalpas,
> The Buddha has proclaimed their inconceivable merit:
> In the future they will accomplish the Two-Footed Honored One.

At that time, the boy Ratnacandra, son of King Bimbisāra, addressed the World-Honored One and spoke in verse:

> Great Fearless One who teaches well,
> Boundless and immeasurable Buddhas!
> Supreme teacher of sentient beings,
> I now bow my head in homage.
> Thus I now study
> The knowledge and vision of all Buddhas.
> May I sever all afflictions
> And swiftly accomplish bodhi.

At that time, having spoken these verses, the boy addressed the World-Honored One: "I now practice the Dharma-gate of patience, the great wisdom and comprehensive retention of the bodhisattva, the vaipulya teachings, and the conduct of the Buddha's bodhi. Thus I study and practice the conduct of bodhi."

At that time, the World-Honored One again spoke in verse:

> If a person receives and upholds the Buddhas' names,
> Water, fire, and thieves cannot invade them;
> Poison, swords, weapons, royal persecution, and the rest:
> All such sufferings will naturally be eliminated.
> Thus one swiftly attains bodhi;
> Broadly circulate and spread the Buddhas' names.

"If in the final age, there are people who receive, uphold, read, and recite this correct Dharma, such persons at the end of their lives will swiftly accomplish the Buddha's path. If one were to make offerings of incense and flowers to all the Buddhas for many kalpas, this would not compare with a person who briefly copies, reads, and recites this correct Dharma; the merit attained is immeasurable and boundless. If a person were to take the most beloved seven treasures and fill all the lands of the universe with them to make offerings to all the Buddhas, this too would not compare with reading and reciting this sūtra; the merit attained is far greater."

When the Buddha taught this sūtra, immeasurable hundred-thousand koṭi nayutas of sentient beings gave rise to the aspiration for anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi. Immeasurable and boundless sentient beings attained the patience of non-arising and became non-retrogressing from anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi.

When the Buddha had spoken these words, the boy Ratnacandra and all present greatly rejoiced, bowed in reverence, and departed.
