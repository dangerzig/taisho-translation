# 佛說彌勒下生成佛經
## Sūtra of Maitreya's Descent and Attainment of Buddhahood Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0454

Translated from the Chinese. Yao Qin dynasty, translated by Kumārajīva (鳩摩羅什)

---

The greatly wise Śāriputra, able to follow the Buddha in turning the Dharma-wheel, the great general of the Buddha-Dharma, out of compassion for sentient beings, addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! As stated in former and later sūtras, Maitreya will descend and become a Buddha. I wish to hear in detail about Maitreya's merit, supernatural powers, and the adornments of his land. By what generosity, what moral discipline, and what wisdom can sentient beings come to see Maitreya?"

At that time, the Buddha told Śāriputra: "I shall now explain this for you in detail. Listen with your whole heart. Śāriputra! The water of the four great oceans will gradually diminish by three thousand yojanas. At that time, the land of Jambudvīpa will be ten thousand yojanas long and eight thousand yojanas wide, flat as a mirror, covered everywhere with renowned flowers and soft grasses, with trees of every kind, their flowers and fruits in luxuriant abundance, all growing thirty li tall. Cities and towns will be so close together that chickens can fly from one to the next. The people will live to eighty-four thousand years, endowed with wisdom, awesome virtue, fine complexion, and great strength, at ease and in happiness. They will have only three illnesses: first, excretion; second, hunger and thirst; third, old age. Women will not marry until the age of five hundred.

"At that time, there will be a great city named Ketumatī, twelve yojanas long and seven yojanas wide, elegant, wonderfully adorned, and clean. People of merit will fill it, and because of their merit, it will be abundant, joyful, and peaceful. The city walls will be of the seven treasures, with towers, windows, and balconies above, all of various jewels. Nets of true pearls will drape and cover it. The streets and alleys will be twelve li wide, swept clean. There will be a great and powerful nāga king named Tārā Śikhin, whose pool will be near the city. The nāga king's palace will be in this pool, and every midnight he will send down a fine, gentle rain to settle the dust. The ground will be moist and smooth, as if oiled. People coming and going will raise no dust. The people of that time, blessed by their merit, will have luminous pearl pillars at every crossroads, all ten li tall. Their radiance will shine so that day and night are no different, and the light of lamps and torches will no longer be of use. In the cities, houses, and every lane, there will not even be the finest grain of earth; the ground will be entirely covered with golden sand. Everywhere there will be heaps of gold and silver. There will be a great yakṣa spirit named Bhadrapāla-Śāsakārya, who will always guard and sweep this city clean. Whenever there is excrement or filth, the earth will split to receive it, then close again. When people are about to die, they will naturally go to the charnel ground to die. At that time, the world will be at ease and happy, without enemies, bandits, or theft. Cities and villages will not even close their gates. There will be no decay or affliction, no water, fire, or weapons, no famine or poison. The people will constantly be of loving-kindness, respectful and harmonious, with their faculties tamed, their speech modest and yielding.

"Śāriputra! I now roughly describe for you the wealth and happiness of that country and its cities. In the various gardens and pools, there will naturally be water of eight meritorious qualities. Blue, red, crimson, and white lotuses of various colors will cover them all. On all four sides of the pools will be stairways of the four treasures. Flocks of birds will gather in harmony: geese, ducks, mandarin ducks, peacocks, kingfishers, parrots, śāri birds, kuṇāla birds, jīvajīvaka birds, and other birds of wondrous voice, constantly among them. There will also be other kinds of birds with wondrous voices, beyond number. Fruit trees and fragrant trees will fill the entire country. At that time, Jambudvīpa will always have fine fragrances, like the Fragrant Mountain. The flowing waters will be beautiful, their taste sweet and healing. Rains will fall at the proper time, and crops will grow abundantly, without weeds growing among them. A single planting will yield seven harvests, requiring little labor but producing much. The food will be fragrant and delicious, nourishing and strengthening.

"In that country at that time, there will be a wheel-turning king named Saṃkha, with four kinds of armies, who will govern the four continents not by martial force. The king will have a thousand sons, valiant and powerful, able to defeat all enemies. The king will have the seven treasures: the golden wheel, the elephant, the horse, the jewel, the queen, the treasurer, and the general. In that land there will also be a seven-jeweled terrace, one thousand zhang tall, with a thousand heads and a thousand wheels, sixty zhang wide. There will also be four great treasuries, each surrounded by four hundred million lesser treasuries: the Elāpatra great treasury in the country of Gandhāra; the Pāṇḍuka great treasury in the country of Mithilā; the Piṅgala great treasury in the country of Sūrāṣṭra; and the Saṃkha great treasury in the country of Vārāṇasī. These four great treasuries, each a thousand yojanas in length and breadth, will be filled with treasures, each with four hundred million lesser treasuries attached. Four great nāga kings will each guard them. These four great treasuries and their lesser treasuries will spontaneously spring forth in the shape of lotus flowers. Countless people will all go to behold them. At that time, these many treasures will have no one to guard them. The people who see them will have no covetous attachment in their hearts, but will cast them to the ground like tiles, stones, grass, wood, or clods of earth. Those who see them will all give rise to weariness, thinking: 'In the past, sentient beings for the sake of these treasures mutually killed and harmed each other, stole, deceived, and spoke falsely, causing the conditions for sin in birth and death to grow and multiply.' The city of Ketumatī will be draped with nets of various jewels, adorned with jeweled bells. When gentle breezes blow and stir them, their sound will be harmonious and elegant, like the striking of bells and chimes.

"In the city there will be a great brahmin lord named Subrahmā. The brahmin lady will be named Brahmāvatī. Maitreya will be conceived and born to them as his father and mother. His body will be the color of purple gold, with the thirty-two marks. Sentient beings will gaze upon him without satiation. His bodily strength will be immeasurable and inconceivable. His radiance will shine without obstruction, and the sun, moon, fire, and jewels will all pale. His body will be a thousand feet tall, his chest thirty zhang wide, his face twelve zhang and four feet long. His body will be perfectly complete, upright and without compare, with all marks accomplished, like a cast golden image. His fleshly eyes, pure and clear, will see for ten yojanas. His constant radiance will illuminate a hundred yojanas in all four directions, and the sun, moon, fire, and jewels will no longer appear; there will be only the Buddha's light, subtle and supreme.

"Maitreya Bodhisattva, contemplating the five desires of the world and seeing how deeply they bring affliction and how sentient beings sink and drown in the great ocean of birth and death, most pitiable, will, through this correct contemplation, take no delight in household life. At that time, King Saṃkha, together with his great ministers, will take this jeweled terrace and offer it to Maitreya. Maitreya, having accepted it, will give it to the brahmins. The brahmins, having accepted it, will immediately destroy it and divide the pieces among themselves. Maitreya Bodhisattva, seeing this wondrous terrace become impermanent in an instant, will know that all dharmas are likewise subject to destruction. He will cultivate the perception of impermanence, renounce the world, and study the path. He will sit beneath the Dragon-Flower Bodhi Tree, whose trunk, branches, and leaves will be fifty li tall, and on the very day he goes forth, he will attain anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi. At that time, all the devas, nāga kings, and spirit kings, without revealing their forms, will rain down flowers and incense to make offerings to the Buddha. The three-thousand great-thousand world system will quake mightily. Light will stream from the Buddha's body, illuminating immeasurable lands, and all who are ready to be saved will see the Buddha.

"At that time, the people will each think: 'Though we might enjoy the five desires for a hundred billion years, we cannot escape the suffering of the three evil paths. Neither wife nor children nor wealth can save us. The world is impermanent, and life cannot last long. We should now cultivate the holy life within the Buddha-Dharma.' Having thought thus, they will renounce the world and study the path. At that time, King Saṃkha, together with eighty-four thousand great ministers, reverently surrounding him, will also renounce the world and study the path. Furthermore, eighty-four thousand brahmins, brilliant and greatly wise, will also go forth in the Buddha-Dharma. There will also be an elder named Sudatta, the present-day Elder Sudatta, who together with eighty-four thousand people will also go forth. There will also be the brothers Rṣidatta and Pūrṇa, who with eighty-four thousand people will also go forth. There will also be two great ministers, one named Candana and the other Sumanas, beloved and valued by the king, who with eighty-four thousand people will also go forth in the Buddha-Dharma. King Saṃkha's precious queen, named Śyāmāvatī, the present-day Viśākhā, with eighty-four thousand ladies-in-waiting, will also go forth together. King Saṃkha's crown prince, named Devāsana, the present-day Devāsana, with eighty-four thousand people will also go forth. Among Maitreya Buddha's kinsmen, a brahmin's son named Sumati, sharp-witted and wise, the present-day Uttara, with eighty-four thousand people will also go forth in the Buddha-Dharma. Countless millions such as these, seeing the suffering and affliction of the world, will all go forth in the Dharma of Maitreya Buddha.

"At that time, Maitreya Buddha, seeing the great assembly, will think: 'All these people have not come to my place for the happiness of heavenly rebirth, nor for the happiness of this present world, but only for the cause of the eternal happiness of nirvāṇa. All these people have planted various good roots within the Buddha-Dharma. The Buddha Śākyamuni sent them to me; that is why they have now all come to me, and I now receive them. These people, whether through reading and reciting, distinguishing and determining the sūtra, vinaya, and abhidharma collections, or through cultivating various merits, have come to me. Or through giving clothing and food to others, observing precepts, and practicing wisdom, and cultivating these merits, they have come to me. Or through offering banners, canopies, flowers, and incense to the Buddha, and cultivating these merits, they have come to me. Or through generosity, keeping fasting days, and cultivating loving-kindness, and performing these merits, they have come to me. Or by causing suffering beings to be happy, and cultivating these merits, they have come to me. Or through observing precepts and practicing patience, cultivating pure loving-kindness, and by these merits, they have come to me. Or through offering constant meals to the saṅgha, holding Dharma lectures, and making offerings of food, and cultivating these merits, they have come to me. Or through observing precepts, being learned in many teachings, cultivating dhyāna meditation, and practicing the wisdom free of outflows, and by these merits, they have come to me. Or through building stūpas and making offerings to relics, and by these merits, they have come to me. Well done, the Buddha Śākyamuni! He was well able to teach and transform these hundreds of billions of sentient beings and cause them to come to me.'

"Maitreya Buddha will thus praise the Buddha Śākyamuni three times, and then teach the Dharma, saying: 'All you sentient beings were able to do what is difficult. In that evil world of greed, anger, delusion, confusion, and short-lived people, you were able to observe the precepts and perform various meritorious deeds. This is extremely rare. At that time, sentient beings did not recognize their parents. Śramaṇas and brahmins did not know the Dharma of the path. They mutually harmed one another. It was near the kalpa of swords. They were deeply attached to the five desires, filled with jealousy, flattery, crookedness, sycophancy, turbidity, deviousness, and falsity, without compassionate hearts, mutually killing each other, eating flesh and drinking blood. Yet you were able to cultivate good deeds among them. This is truly rare! Well done, the Buddha Śākyamuni! With his great compassionate heart, amid suffering and afflicted sentient beings, he spoke sincere and true words, indicating that in the future I would come and liberate you all. Such a teacher is extremely hard to encounter. With deep compassion he pitied the sentient beings of the evil age, rescued them from suffering and affliction, and caused them to be at peace. The Buddha Śākyamuni, for your sake, gave his head in charity, allowed his ears, nose, hands, feet, and limbs to be cut away, and endured various sufferings, all to benefit you.'

"Maitreya Buddha will thus guide and comfort immeasurable sentient beings, causing them to rejoice, and then teach the Dharma. People of merit will fill the assembly, receiving and accepting with reverence, longing for the great teacher, each wishing to hear the Dharma. They will all think: 'The five desires are impure, the root of manifold suffering. Moreover, they can remove sorrow, grief, and worry. The dharmas of suffering and happiness are all impermanent.' Maitreya Buddha will observe the great assembly and see that their minds are purified and supple. He will teach them the Four Noble Truths, and all who hear will simultaneously attain the path of nirvāṇa.

"At that time, in the Flower-Grove Park, the park being a hundred yojanas in length and breadth, filled with the great assembly: at the first assembly of teaching the Dharma, nine billion six hundred million people will attain arhatship. At the second great assembly of teaching the Dharma, nine billion four hundred million people will attain arhatship. At the third great assembly of teaching the Dharma, nine billion two hundred million people will attain arhatship. After Maitreya Buddha has turned the Dharma-wheel and saved devas and humans, he will lead his disciples to enter the city to beg for food. Immeasurable hosts of Pure-Abode devas will reverently follow the Buddha into the city of Ketumatī. As they enter the city, he will display various supernatural powers and immeasurable transformations. Lord Śakra and the devas of the desire realm, the Brahmā king and the devas of the form realm, will play hundreds of thousands of kinds of music, sing hymns of the Buddha's virtues, rain down heavenly flowers, candana powder, and incense to make offerings to the Buddha. Along the streets, banners and canopies will be raised and various famous incenses burned, their smoke rising like clouds. When the World-Honored One enters the city, the great Brahmā king and Lord Śakra will join their palms in reverence and praise him in verse:

> "'The Perfectly Awakened One, the Two-Footed Honored One,
> Among devas and humans, none can compare.
> The World-Honored One of the Ten Powers, so rare!
> Unsurpassed, most supreme, the excellent field of merit.
> Those who make offerings to him will be born in the heavens.
> We bow our heads to the one of great, incomparable diligence.'

"At that time, devas, humans, and rākṣasas, seeing the great-powered Māra subdued by the Buddha, will be greatly delighted, countless millions beyond measure. They will all join their palms and exclaim: 'How extraordinary! How extraordinary! The Tathāgata's supernatural power and complete merit are inconceivable!' At that time, devas and humans will scatter various many-colored lotus flowers and māndārava flowers before the Buddha's path, accumulating to the knees. The devas in the sky will play hundreds of thousands of kinds of music, singing praises of the Buddha's virtue. Then the māra king, in the first and last watches of the night, will rouse the people, saying: 'You have obtained human bodies and encounter a good time. You should not let sleep cover your minds through the whole night. Whether standing or sitting, you should constantly and diligently practice with correct mindfulness, carefully contemplating the five aggregates as impermanent, suffering, empty, and without self. Do not be negligent and fail to practice the Buddha's teaching. If you give rise to evil karma, you will surely have regret later.' The men and women in the streets will all repeat these words: 'Do not be negligent and fail to practice the Buddha's teaching. If you give rise to evil karma, you will surely have regret. You should strive diligently and with effort seek the path. Do not lose the benefit of the Dharma, living and dying in vain. A great teacher such as this, who uproots suffering and affliction, is extremely hard to encounter. Be firm in your diligence, and you will attain the eternal bliss of nirvāṇa.'

"At that time, Maitreya Buddha's disciples will all be upright and handsome, with complete deportment, weary of birth, old age, sickness, and death, broadly learned, guardians of the Dharma-treasury, practicing dhyāna, having attained freedom from desires, like birds emerging from their shells. At that time, Maitreya Buddha will wish to go to the Elder Mahākāśyapa's place. Together with the fourfold assembly, he will go to Mount Gṛdhrakūṭa. On the summit of the mountain, they will see Mahākāśyapa. At that time, the men and women of the great assembly will all be astonished and amazed. Maitreya Buddha will praise him: 'The bhikṣu Mahākāśyapa was a great disciple of the Buddha Śākyamuni. Among the great assembly, the Buddha Śākyamuni always praised him as foremost in ascetic practices. He penetrated dhyāna concentration and liberation samādhi. Though this person possessed great supernatural powers, he had no pride. He was able to cause sentient beings to attain great joy. He constantly had compassion for the lowly, poor, and afflicted sentient beings, rescuing them from suffering and affliction and causing them to be at peace.' Maitreya Buddha will praise the skeletal remains of Mahākāśyapa: 'Well done, one of great spiritual virtue! Great disciple Mahākāśyapa of the Lion of the Śākyas! In that evil age, he was able to cultivate his mind.' At that time, the people of the assembly, seeing Mahākāśyapa praised by Maitreya Buddha, hundreds of billions of people, on account of this, will become weary of the world and attain the path. All these people, mindful of the Buddha Śākyamuni, who in that evil age taught and transformed immeasurable sentient beings, causing them to be complete in the six supernatural powers and become arhats. At that time, the place of teaching the Dharma will be eighty yojanas wide and one hundred yojanas long. Among the people therein, whether sitting or standing, near or far, each will see the Buddha before them, teaching the Dharma for them alone.

"Maitreya Buddha will dwell in the world for sixty thousand years, with compassion for sentient beings, causing them to attain the Dharma-eye. After his passing, the Dharma will abide in the world for another sixty thousand years. All of you should diligently generate the pure mind and give rise to good deeds. You will certainly, without doubt, come to see Maitreya Buddha, the lamp of the world."

When the Buddha had finished teaching this sūtra, Śāriputra and the rest joyfully received and upheld it.
