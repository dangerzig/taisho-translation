This is T2181, a very brief Japanese document in literary Chinese. 藏俊 (Zōshun) was a Japanese Hossō (Yogācāra) scholar-monk of the late Heian period. 安元二年 = 1176 CE. 院宣 refers specifically to a decree from the retired emperor's court (the *in*). The text is a cover note submitting a catalog of Hossō school commentarial literature.

---

# 注進法相宗章疏
## A Submitted List of Commentaries and Subcommentaries of the Hossō School

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 2181

Composed in Japan by Zōshun (藏俊)

---

On a day of the eleventh month of the second year of An'gen [1176], the Provisional Vinaya Master Zōshun respectfully submits this in compliance with a decree of the Retired Emperor's court.
