# Catalog of Items Brought Back by Master Chishō

## *Chishō Daishi Shōrai Mokuroku* (智證大師請來目錄)

Taishō 2173

Compiled by Enchin (*Enchin*, 圓珍) of Japan. A bibliographic catalog.

*Note: This is a catalog compiled by the Japanese Tendai monk Enchin (814–891), also known posthumously as Master Chishō (Chishō Daishi), listing texts and objects he brought back from Tang China during his journey to seek the dharma (853–858). He traveled through the two capitals (Chang'an and Luoyang), Zhejiang, Lingnan, and Fujian, obtaining 441 works in 1,000 fascicles.*

Guóqīng Monastery (*Guóqīng Sì*, 國清寺) on Mount Tiāntái (*Tiāntái Shān*, 天台山), Tángxīng County (*Tángxīng Xiàn*, 唐興縣), Tái Prefecture (*Táizhōu*, 台州), Zhèjiāng East Circuit (*Zhèjiāng Dōngdào*, 浙江東道), Great Tang.

General catalog of texts sought by the bhikṣu Enchin of Enryaku-ji (*Enryakuji*, 延曆寺) on Mount Hiei (*Hieizan*, 比叡山) in the upper capital of Japan, who entered Tang to seek the dharma.

Traveling through the two capitals, the two Zhè regions, Lǐngnán, and the Fújiàn circuit, he obtained and transmitted sūtras, vinaya, treatises, and biographies of the Great and Small Vehicles, as well as dhāraṇī teachings and maṇḍala paintings, Tiāntái perfect and sudden teachings, and various commentaries, digests, miscellaneous texts, Sanskrit manuscripts, and catalogs from different schools. The total of all items obtained, before and after, comes to four hundred and forty-one works in one thousand fascicles. Together with dharma implements and ritual objects, there are altogether sixteen categories. Each item is listed by name below.

The above three items were all brought to the Great Tang from the western buddha-lands of India by a Brahmin tripiṭaka master. For one of these, the journey covered one hundred and sixty-eight thousand *lǐ*, with hardships beyond description. When Enchin arrived in Fúzhōu, he met the tripiṭaka master and received instruction in the Siddhaṃ script, along with three kinds of dharma tokens. These are to be transmitted to our home country and offered in perpetual veneration. May all who see, hear, and rejoice share in this karmic connection.

The above three items: the Japanese monk Tián Yuánjué (*Tián Yuánjué*, 田圓覺) came to Tang in the fifth year of Kāichéng (*Kāichéng*, 開成) [840 CE] and resided for a long time at Wǔtái. He later traveled to Cháng'ān. In the ninth year of Dàzhōng (*Dàzhōng*, 大中) [855 CE] they met in the city. He personally assisted Enchin in copying sacred teachings and painting maṇḍalas. In the sixth month of the tenth year he accompanied Enchin to Mount Tiāntái. After remaining through the rest of the summer, in the autumn months he traveled to Guǎngzhōu, where he encountered Japanese merchants Lǐ Yīngjué (*Lǐ Yīngjué*, 李英覺), Chén Tàixìn (*Chén Tàixìn*, 陳太信), and others, and entrusted the aforementioned tokens to them for delivery. They are now brought to our home country for perpetual veneration.

The above two items: the Dharma-Transmitting Great Worthy, Monk Cúnshì (*Cúnshì*, 存式), of Kāiyuán Monastery in the Fúzhōu capital commandery, sent these to Enchin as dharma tokens.

## General Catalog of Dharma Sought in Tang by the Bhikṣu Enchin of Enryaku-ji on Mount Hiei in the Upper Capital of Japan, in One Fascicle

The foregoing Kaiyuan and Zhenyuan Buddhist canon, together with the commentaries, digests, and collected works of both the Northern and Southern schools, dhāraṇī recitation texts, sacred images, dharma implements and ritual objects, and the various preceding and subsequent catalogs, are each detailed item by item as above. May they be universally transmitted throughout the ten directions, carrying out the Buddha's intention. With this merit, may it serve to benefit the myriad chariots above and relieve the multitudes below. May it extend vertically through the three realms and horizontally across a thousand buddha-lands. Whether through faith or slander, all alike plant the cause of right awakening. Whether friend or foe, all equally ride the vehicle of the vast and lofty. All shall reach the ultimate position of Mahāvairocana (*Dàrì*, 大日). Respectfully recorded.

The fifteenth day of the fifth month, the twelfth year of Dàzhōng (*Dàzhōng*, 大中) of the Great Tang [858 CE].

Recorded by the bhikṣu Enchin of Enryaku-ji on Mount Hiei in the upper capital of Japan.

Interpreter: Dīng Shèngnán Mǎn (*Dīng Shèngnán Mǎn*, 丁勝男滿), of the junior seventh rank.

Junior disciple: Xiánjìng Tànzōng (*Xiánjìng Tànzōng*, 閑靜撢宗).

Attendant: Ōya Zenkichi (*Dàzhái Quánjí*, 大宅全吉).

This one-fascicle catalog of dharma-seeking is respectfully submitted to the Grand Chancellor. May it serve to forge a karmic bond.

The eighteenth day of the fourth month of the third year of Tennan (*Tennan*, 天安) of Japan, the year *tsuchinoto-u* [859 CE].

Respectfully recorded and submitted by the monk Enchin.
