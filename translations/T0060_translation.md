# 瞿曇彌記果經
## The Sūtra on the Prediction of the Fruits for Gautamī

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0060

Translated by Huijiǎn of the Liu Song dynasty

---

## Fascicle 1

### The Sūtra Spoken by the Buddha on the Prediction of Fruits Concerning Gautamī

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Bhagavān was dwelling in the Nyagrodha Grove in the Śākyan city of Kapilavastu, together with a great assembly of bhikṣus, observing the pravāraṇā. At that time, the great woman Gautamī came to where the World-Honored One was, and having arrived, she paid reverence to the World-Honored One's feet and stood to one side. Having stood to one side, the great woman Gautamī said to the World-Honored One: "World-Honored One, is it possible for women to attain the four fruits of the śramaṇa? May women go forth in this Dharma and discipline with faith and delight, leaving the household life to train in the path?"

"Enough, Gautamī! There is no need for that. Women may not go forth in this Dharma and discipline with faith and delight, leaving the household life to train in the path. You, Gautamī, may always shave your head, put on the kaṣāya robe, and practice the pure brahmacarya to the end of your life."

Thereupon the great woman Gautamī, having been restrained by the World-Honored One, paid reverence to the World-Honored One's feet, circumambulated the World-Honored One, and departed.

At that time the bhikṣus were making robes for the World-Honored One, and the World-Honored One was soon to go to the Śākyas to observe the pravāraṇā. When the pravāraṇā was concluded and the three months of robe-making were finished and the robes completed, he would travel among the people with his robes and bowl.

The great woman Gautamī heard that the bhikṣus were making robes for the World-Honored One, that the World-Honored One would soon go to the Śākyas to observe the pravāraṇā, and that when the pravāraṇā was concluded and the three months of robe-making had produced completed robes, he would travel among the people with his robes and bowl. Having heard this, the great woman Gautamī came to where the World-Honored One was, and having arrived, she paid reverence to the World-Honored One's feet and stood to one side. Having stood to one side, the great woman Gautamī said to the World-Honored One: "World-Honored One, is it possible for women to attain the four fruits of the śramaṇa? May women go forth in this Dharma and discipline with faith and delight, leaving the household life to train in the path?"

"Enough, Gautamī! There is no need for that. Women should not go forth in this Dharma and discipline with faith and delight, leaving the household life to train in the path. You, Gautamī, shave your head, put on the kaṣāya robe, and practice the pure brahmacarya to the end of your life."

Thereupon the great woman Gautamī, having been restrained a second time by the World-Honored One, paid reverence to the World-Honored One's feet, circumambulated the World-Honored One, and departed.

At that time the World-Honored One was observing the pravāraṇā among the Śākyas; when the pravāraṇā was concluded and the three months of robe-making had produced completed robes, he traveled among the villages with his robes and bowl. The great woman Gautamī heard that the World-Honored One was observing the pravāraṇā among the Śākyas, that the pravāraṇā had concluded and the three months of robe-making had produced completed robes, and that he was traveling with his robes and bowl among the villages. Having heard this, the great woman Gautamī together with the elder women followed after the World-Honored One. As they followed, the World-Honored One traveled in stages and arrived at Nāvati, where he stayed at the Giṇī-āvasatha. At that time the great woman Gautamī came to where the World-Honored One was, and having arrived, she paid reverence to the World-Honored One's feet and stood to one side. Having stood to one side, the great woman Gautamī said to the World-Honored One: "World-Honored One, is it possible for women to attain the four fruits of the śramaṇa? May women go forth in this Dharma and discipline with faith and delight, leaving the household life to train in the path?"

"Enough, Gautamī! There is no need for that. Women may not go forth in this Dharma and discipline, leaving the household life to train in the path. You, Gautamī, shave your head, put on the kaṣāya robe, and practice the pure brahmacarya to the end of your life."

Thereupon the great woman Gautamī, having been restrained a third time by the World-Honored One, paid reverence to the World-Honored One's feet, circumambulated the World-Honored One, and departed.

At that time the great woman Gautamī stood weeping before the gateway, her feet unwashed, her body covered with dust and dirt, her bearing dazed and overwhelmed. The Venerable Ānanda, seeing from afar the great woman Gautamī standing before the gateway weeping, her feet unwashed, her body covered with dust and dirt, her bearing dazed and overwhelmed, spoke thus: "Why is it, Gautamī, that you stand before the gateway weeping, your feet unwashed, your body covered with dust, your bearing dazed and overwhelmed?"

"It is so, Venerable Ānanda. Women may not go forth in this Dharma and discipline; they are not permitted to go forth with faith and delight, leaving the household life to train in the path."

"You, Gautamī, remain here. I shall go to where the World-Honored One is, and having arrived, I shall present this matter to the World-Honored One."

Thereupon the Venerable Ānanda went to where the World-Honored One was, and having arrived, paid reverence to the World-Honored One's feet and stood to one side. Having stood to one side, the Venerable Ānanda said to the World-Honored One: "World-Honored One, is it possible for women to attain the four fruits of the śramaṇa? May women go forth in this Dharma and discipline with faith and delight, leaving the household life to train in the path?"

"Ānanda, enough! There is no need for that. Women may not go forth in this Dharma and discipline with faith and delight, leaving the household life to train in the path. Ānanda, if women were permitted to go forth in this Dharma and discipline with faith and delight, leaving the household life to train in the path, the brahmacarya would not long endure. Just as, Ānanda, in a household with many women and few men, can it broadly prosper?"

"No, World-Honored One."

"Just so, Ānanda. If women were permitted to go forth in this Dharma and discipline with faith and delight, leaving the household life to train in the path, the brahmacarya would not long endure. Just as, Ānanda, there is a flourishing rice field and a flourishing wheat field, and in their midst a hailstorm descends bringing harm, and because of that hailstorm they are ruined and destroyed, just so, Ānanda, if women were permitted to go forth in this Dharma and discipline with faith and delight, leaving the household life to train in the path, the brahmacarya would not long endure."

"World-Honored One, the great woman Gautamī has been of great benefit. When the World-Honored One's mother passed away, she nurtured and nursed him."

"So it is, Ānanda. So it is, Ānanda. This great woman Gautamī has indeed been of great benefit; when my mother passed away, she nursed and nurtured me with her milk. And I too, Ānanda, have been of benefit to the great woman Gautamī: relying on me, she has taken refuge in me, taken refuge in the Dharma, and taken refuge in the Saṃgha of bhikṣus; she has no doubt about the Buddha, no doubt about the Dharma, no doubt about the Saṃgha; no doubt about suffering, its origin, its cessation, and the path. She is endowed with faith, morality, learning, and generosity; she is endowed with wisdom. She has abandoned and renounced killing, taking what is not given, sexual misconduct, and false speech, and has abandoned and renounced drinking intoxicants. Ānanda, if any person, through reliance on another, takes refuge in the Buddha, takes refuge in the Dharma, takes refuge in the Saṃgha of bhikṣus, has no doubt about the Buddha, no doubt about the Dharma, no doubt about the Saṃgha of bhikṣus, no doubt about suffering, its origin, its cessation, and the path, is endowed with faith, morality, learning, and generosity, endowed with wisdom, and has abandoned and renounced killing, taking what is not given, sexual misconduct, and false speech, and has abandoned and renounced drinking intoxicants, then, Ānanda, even if that person were to provide robes, bedding, sleeping accommodations, and medicine for illness for the whole of their life, they could not repay that person.

"Furthermore, Ānanda, eight heavy rules are to be laid down for women, which women must not transgress, and women should uphold these precepts for the whole of their lives. It is as if, Ānanda, a person skilled in walking along the bottom of the water, or a skilled disciple, enters deep water and casts a net, guarding the water and damming it so that it does not flow away. Just so, Ānanda, women should practice these eight heavy rules, which women must not transgress, and women should uphold these precepts for the whole of their lives. What are the eight?

"Ānanda, a bhikṣuṇī must seek full ordination from the bhikṣus. This, Ānanda, is the first heavy rule I lay down for women, which women must not transgress, and which women should uphold in conjunction with the precepts for the whole of their lives.

"Ānanda, a bhikṣuṇī must receive exhortation from the bhikṣus every fortnight. This, Ānanda, is the second heavy rule I lay down for women, which women must not transgress, and which women should uphold in conjunction with the precepts for the whole of their lives.

"Ānanda, if there are no bhikṣus present, bhikṣuṇīs may not perform the pravāraṇā. This, Ānanda, is the third heavy rule I lay down for women, which women must not transgress, and which women should uphold in conjunction with the precepts for the whole of their lives.

"Ānanda, when a bhikṣuṇī comes to perform the pravāraṇā, she must do so before both saṃghas, conducting the pravāraṇā with the three matters: what was seen, what was heard, and what was suspected. This, Ānanda, is the fourth heavy rule I lay down for women, which women must not transgress, and which women should uphold in conjunction with the precepts for the whole of their lives.

"Ānanda, if the bhikṣus do not permit it, bhikṣuṇīs may not question bhikṣus about the sūtras, the Vinaya, and the Abhidharma; Ānanda, if the bhikṣus permit it, bhikṣuṇīs may question bhikṣus about the sūtras, the Vinaya, and the Abhidharma. This, Ānanda, is the fifth heavy rule I lay down for women, which women must not transgress, and which women should uphold in conjunction with the precepts for the whole of their lives.

"Ānanda, bhikṣuṇīs may not censure bhikṣus for offenses seen, heard, or suspected; Ānanda, bhikṣus may censure bhikṣuṇīs for offenses seen, heard, or suspected. This, Ānanda, is the sixth heavy rule I lay down for women, which women must not transgress, and which women should uphold in conjunction with the precepts for the whole of their lives.

"Ānanda, if a bhikṣuṇī commits a saṃghāvaśeṣa offense, she must perform penance before both saṃghas for a fortnight. This, Ānanda, is the seventh heavy rule I lay down for women, which women must not transgress, and which women should uphold in conjunction with the precepts for the whole of their lives.

"Ānanda, a bhikṣuṇī who has received full ordination, even if she has been ordained for a hundred years, must pay reverence by touching the feet of a newly ordained bhikṣu, and must honor and serve him with respect. This, Ānanda, is the eighth heavy rule I lay down for women, which women must not transgress, and which women should uphold in conjunction with the precepts for the whole of their lives. These, Ānanda, are the eight heavy rules I have laid down for women, which women must not transgress, and which women should uphold in conjunction with the precepts for the whole of their lives. Ānanda, if the great woman Gautamī accepts these eight heavy rules, she may train in the path within this Dharma and discipline and receive full ordination as a bhikṣuṇī."

Thereupon the Venerable Ānanda, having heard what the World-Honored One said, carefully reflected upon it, recited it, and committed it to memory. He paid reverence to the World-Honored One's feet, circumambulated the World-Honored One, departed, and went to where the great woman Gautamī was. Having arrived, he spoke these words to the great woman Gautamī: "It has been granted, Gautamī! Women may go forth in this Dharma and discipline with faith and delight, leaving the household life to train in the path. Gautamī, the World-Honored One has said: 'You must practice the eight heavy rules, which women must not transgress, and which women should uphold in conjunction with the precepts for the whole of their lives.' What are the eight? Gautamī, a bhikṣuṇī must seek full ordination from the bhikṣus. This, Gautamī, is the first heavy rule the World-Honored One has laid down for women, which women must not transgress, and which women should uphold in conjunction with the precepts for the whole of their lives. And, Gautamī, extending to the eighth: a bhikṣuṇī who has received full ordination for a hundred years must pay reverence to a newly ordained bhikṣu by touching his feet, and must honor and serve him with respect. This, Gautamī, is the eighth heavy rule the World-Honored One has laid down for women, which women must not transgress, and which women should uphold in conjunction with the precepts for the whole of their lives. These, Gautamī, are the eight heavy rules the World-Honored One has laid down for women, which women must not transgress, and which women should uphold in conjunction with the precepts for the whole of their lives. Gautamī, if you can accept these eight heavy rules, you may train in the path within this Dharma and discipline and receive full ordination as a bhikṣuṇī."

"So it is, Venerable Ānanda! Pray hear my simile. A wise person, upon hearing a simile, understands its meaning. It is as if, Venerable Ānanda, a woman of the kṣatriya class, a brāhmaṇa woman, a woman of the artisan class, or a woman of the common people, having bathed thoroughly and anointed herself with fragrant perfume and put on clean white robes, and if someone out of compassion, wishing to benefit her and bring her ease, were to offer her a garland of utpala flowers, or a garland of campaka flowers, a garland of vārṣika flowers, or a garland of atimuktaka flowers, she would receive it with both hands and place it upon her head. Just so, Venerable Ānanda, the eight heavy rules laid down by the World-Honored One I shall receive and hold upon my head for the whole of my life."

"Great woman Gautamī, you may now train in the path within this Dharma and discipline and receive full ordination as a bhikṣuṇī."

After this, at a later time, the great woman Gautamī, together with an assembly of senior elder bhikṣuṇīs, the most venerable and senior among them, all practicing the brahmacarya, came to where the Venerable Ānanda was. Having arrived, she paid reverence to the Venerable Ānanda's feet and stood to one side. Having stood to one side, the great woman Gautamī said to the Venerable Ānanda: "Venerable Ānanda, these bhikṣuṇīs here are senior elders who have all long practiced the brahmacarya, while these bhikṣus are newly ordained, not long on the path, not long entered into this Dharma and discipline. These bhikṣus should treat these bhikṣuṇīs as elders, paying them reverence and serving them accordingly."

"Enough, Gautamī! I shall go to where the World-Honored One is, and having arrived, I shall present this matter to him." Then she followed the Venerable Ānanda. Thereupon the Venerable Ānanda went to where the World-Honored One was, and having arrived, paid reverence to the World-Honored One's feet and stood to one side. Having stood to one side, the Venerable Ānanda said to the World-Honored One: "World-Honored One, today the great woman Gautamī, together with bhikṣuṇīs who are senior elders all practicing the brahmacarya, came to where I was; having arrived, she paid reverence to my feet and stood to one side. Having stood to one side, the great woman Gautamī said to me: 'Venerable Ānanda, know that these bhikṣuṇīs are senior elders who have all long practiced the brahmacarya, while these bhikṣus are newly ordained, not long on the path, not long in this Dharma. May these bhikṣus treat these bhikṣuṇīs as elders, paying them reverence and serving them accordingly.'"

"Enough, Ānanda! Beware of such words; do not speak thus again. You, Ānanda, should know that as I understand it, not a single phrase of this can be justified, how much less could such a statement be made! Ānanda, if women had not gone forth in this Dharma and discipline with faith and delight, leaving the household life to train in the path, brāhmaṇas and householders would lay out their robes upon the ground, and having laid them out, would speak thus: 'These śramaṇas are possessed of virtue and discipline; let the śramaṇas walk above. The śramaṇas' diligence is most extraordinary; may we gain benefit and welfare throughout the long night.' Ānanda, if women had not gone forth in this Dharma and discipline with faith and delight, leaving the household life to train in the path, brāhmaṇas and householders would spread their hair upon the ground and speak thus: 'Let the śramaṇas walk above, let the śramaṇas stand above. The śramaṇas' virtue and discipline is most sublime; may we gain benefit and welfare throughout this long night.' Ānanda, if women had not gone forth in this Dharma and discipline with faith and delight, leaving the household life to train in the path, brāhmaṇas and householders standing along the road, holding in their hands various bags filled with goods, would speak thus: 'Honored ones, please take these and eat and drink as you wish; may we gain benefit, welfare, and ease throughout the long night.' Ānanda, if women had not gone forth in this Dharma and discipline with faith and delight, leaving the household life to train in the path, brāhmaṇas and householders, trusting in the virtue and conduct of those śramaṇas, would carry them in their arms into their homes and make every manner of offering: 'Honored ones, take and use whatever you wish; may we gain benefit, welfare, and happiness throughout the long night.' Ānanda, if women had not gone forth in this Dharma and discipline with faith and delight, leaving the household life to train in the path, just as the sun and moon possess the greatest spiritual power and capacity, the radiance of those śramaṇas of virtuous conduct would have surpassed even theirs, how much more would it have surpassed the feeble heterodox practitioners! Ānanda, if women had not gone forth in this Dharma and discipline with faith and delight, leaving the household life to train in the path, the Dharma legacy would have endured for a thousand years; now it has already been diminished by five hundred years, and only five hundred years remain.

"Ānanda, there is no such possibility, it cannot be, that women could ever attain five things: they cannot become a Tathāgata, an Arhat, a Samyaksaṃbuddha; they cannot become a cakravartin; they cannot become Śakra; they cannot become Māra; they cannot become Brahmā. This is impossible. It is possible, however, that men can attain these five things: they can become a Tathāgata, an Arhat, a Samyaksaṃbuddha; they can become a cakravartin; they can become Śakra, Māra, and Brahmā. This is possible."

Thus spoke the Buddha. The Venerable Ānanda, having heard what the World-Honored One said, rejoiced and was gladdened.
