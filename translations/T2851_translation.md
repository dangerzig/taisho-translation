# Harmonizing Text for the Bodhisattva Precepts

## *Hé Púsà Jiè Wén* (和菩薩戒文)

Taishō 2851

Author unknown. A Dunhuang liturgical text, dated to the fourth year of Qiánfú (*Qiánfú*, 乾符, 877 CE), copied at Bàoēn Temple (*Bàoēn Sì*, 報恩寺).

*Note: This is a call-and-response liturgy ("harmonizing text," *hé*, 和) for conferring the bodhisattva precepts. The preceptor chants the precept and the assembly responds. Each section describes the karmic consequences of violating a precept.*

With deep mind and thirsty longing, attend single-mindedly to the sound of the dharma. May the precept master broadly bestow and expound.

[Harmonize:] All bodhisattvas, do not kill. Killing will certainly lead to falling into the fire pit. Killing brings the retribution of a short life in the next birth. In this sahā world, both eyes will suffer the wound of double blindness. I urge all in this practice-hall assembly to cut off the karma of killing and not to engage in it. Disciples of the Buddha:

All bodhisattvas, do not steal. If one takes others' possessions, just as a mother must eventually die, after death one becomes an animal's body, wearing fur and bearing horns to repay the debt. All day long driven and tugged without rest, with no time even to eat water or grass, yet the deluded mind does not perceive or know. Therefore I earnestly report this again. Disciples of the Buddha:

All bodhisattvas, do not commit sexual misconduct. The sin of sexual misconduct and moral confusion has deep roots. The iron bed will come face to face. Blazing iron pillars will compete to burn the body; the entire body will be soaked in rot. Why not give rise to the bodhi-mind? Disciples of the Buddha:

All bodhisattvas, do not speak falsely. In the future, who will: not having seen, claim to have seen? For deceitful and false words, iron plows will till the tongue. Additionally, saws will cut for the sake of false testimony. If sentient beings deceive and delude their teachers, monks, and parents, if they can repent and do not persist, they will surely be freed from the suffering of the Tapana (*Bōzhà*, 波吒) hell. Disciples of the Buddha:

All bodhisattvas, do not sell liquor. Selling liquor brings molten copper poured into the mouth. Fire shoots from beneath the feet, flames reaching to heaven. Hell-guards holding spears will sever both hands. All because of drunken stupor and moral confusion: what one does with this body, one's own body must endure. Then one is driven into the Avīci (*Ābí*, 阿鼻) hell, with a thousand layers of iron walls and nowhere to turn. Disciples of the Buddha:

All bodhisattvas, do not praise yourself and disparage others. Like washing snow with hot water, creating sins: in a single kṣaṇa one enters the Tapana hell and falls unconscious. Through the night until dawn, long nails are driven down. Eyes and ears all weep blood. The causes of sin, the fruits of sin, and the roots of sin are all cool. Then the ox-headed guards come to pull out the tongue, not permitting even a brief plea for mercy. The hell-guards wielding weapons force a release. Disciples of the Buddha:

All bodhisattvas, do not slander others. Slandering others leads to entering the Naraka (*Nài*, 奈) hell together. Swords and blades inflict crosswise punishment through the destinies. Entering the hell of mud and water, one is overwhelmed. Confusion and dullness are like a boiling cauldron. One passes through every hell. Skin, blood, and flesh flow like water. When will one ever leave the Tapana hell? Disciples of the Buddha:

All bodhisattvas, do not be excessively miserly. Hoarding treasures like mountains, yet when the poor come begging, not a needle or blade of grass can be given. The greedy mind does not know satisfaction. In the future one goes empty-handed into the Yellow Springs. Disciples of the Buddha:

All bodhisattvas, do not be excessively angry. Excessive anger will certainly bring the retribution of a snake, body twisting and writhing, crawling without hands or feet. Because of anger in a past life, eighty thousand tubes come to suck and feed. Leaving behind only bone and skin. The suffering is unbearable. When in what kalpa will one ever again attain a human body? Disciples of the Buddha:

All bodhisattvas, do not slander the Three Jewels. If one slanders the Three Jewels, one falls into the evil destinies. Three hundred shells, long nails, fixed and driven into one's body. Screaming reaches to heaven; the sound is vast. Slandering the Buddha, slandering further in anger: copper opens, iron railings come in opposition. How painful! How bitter! It cannot be expressed. When will one ever encounter the palm-of-heaven way? Disciples of the Buddha:

[Harmonize:] The dust-motes of violations darken a thousand lifetimes. The supreme fruit of bodhi is thus entirely and suddenly lost. Playing as an infant through the six destinies is truly pitiable. Between heaven and the human realm, which path of rebirth will you take?

[Harmonize:] Having committed violations and knowing their retribution, one has already fallen into the three evil paths. How many years have been spent within them, unable to return?

[Harmonize:] Having fallen into the evil destinies and reached the utmost suffering, passing through further kalpas and eons is truly heartrending. The months and years feel endlessly long. Does one not even hear the names of father, mother, or the Three Jewels?

[Harmonize:] How bitter! How bitter!

[Harmonize:] The lotus-treasury precepts are heard today for the first time. May the assembly all incline their hearts and pray for the broad exposition.

[Harmonize:] I now repent and dare not conceal. I only pray that the precept master will compassionately expound in full.

[Harmonize:] The Great Teacher's secret treasury, the sweet-dew elixir of truth: joyfully we hear it today. May you broadly expound it.

[Harmonize:] The mind-ground of the Tathāgata, blocked from hearing throughout vast kalpas, is now proclaimed. May you broadly expound it.

[Harmonize:] The precepts of Ganges-sands of categories are rarely proclaimed. I only pray that the precept master will compassionately expound them in full.

With deep mind, shame, and remorse, I dare not conceal. Laying bare my innermost heart, I openly confess and repent. The sinful obstructions of vast kalpas, numerous as the sands of the Ganges, upon hearing these precepts today, are all eliminated. I am ashamed and grateful to the precept master for this gift of joyful generosity.

Written in the fourth month of the fourth year of Qiánfú [877 CE] at Bàoēn Temple.
