# 佛說大乘聖吉祥持世陀羅尼經
## Mahāyāna Sūtra of the Holy Auspicious Vasudhārā Dhāraṇī Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1164

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmadeva (法天).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the World-Honored One was in the great thorn-grove of Kauśāmbī, together with a great assembly of four hundred and fifty bhikṣus. All were great arhats well known to the assembly, together with bodhisattva mahāsattvas. The assemblies of all buddha-dharmas came together, surrounding him with reverence, listening to the Buddha expound the dharma.

At that time, in the assembly there was a householder named Sucandra, who dwelled in the great city of Kauśāmbī. This householder possessed great wisdom, skillful means, many sons and daughters, servants and attendants, a complete retinue, all endowed with wholesome roots and generating the great wholesome mind.

At that time, the householder Sucandra went to see the World-Honored One. Having arrived at the Buddha's presence, he reverently joined his palms, bowed at the Buddha's feet, and circumambulated hundreds of thousands of times. Then he withdrew and sat to one side. Having seated himself in composure, the householder Sucandra addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! Today I wish to ask the Tathāgata, Arhat, Perfectly Awakened One a question. I have doubts in my mind. I beseech the World-Honored One, with boundless great compassion, to expound the dharma for me and resolve my doubts."

At that time, Śākyamuni World-Honored One told the householder Sucandra: "I now grant you permission to ask whatever doubts you have in your mind. Whatever your doubts, you should not keep them harbored within."

The householder Sucandra, hearing what the Buddha said, again addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! If good men and good women suffer greatly from poverty, how can they avoid suffering from poverty? If people have many illnesses, how can they avoid suffering from illness?"

At that time, the World-Honored One, who possesses all-knowledge, told the householder Sucandra: "Why do you now, solely on account of poverty, harbor doubts and ask such a question?"

The householder Sucandra addressed the World-Honored One: "I am now poor, World-Honored One! I am now poor, Sugata! I have many dependents: sons, daughters, maids, and servants filling the household. Please speak for me, World-Honored One! In accordance with my question, expound the dharma: by what means can poor sentient beings leave poverty and suffering? By what means can sick sentient beings be free from the affliction of illness? How can livelihood, wealth, grain, and wheat fill the storehouses? How can one obtain the beloved gold, silver, maṇi-gems, pearls, lapis lazuli, conch shells, jade discs, coral, giant clam shell, and vajra jewels, with treasuries overflowing, enabling me to give without end? How can one's household dependents, sons, daughters, young and old, all attain noble independence?"

Having thus spoken, the householder Sucandra addressed the World-Honored One.

At that time, the Buddha further told the householder Sucandra: "In the distant past, throughout countless kalpas, in that age, a buddha appeared in the world named Vajradhara-Sāgaramahāghoṣa Tathāgata, Arhat, Perfectly Awakened, Complete in Knowledge and Conduct, Well-Gone, Knower of the World, Unsurpassed, Trainer of Persons to Be Tamed, Teacher of Devas and Humans, Buddha, World-Honored One.

"I, at that World-Honored One's presence, heard a dhāraṇī named Auspicious Vasudhārā. Having heard this dharma, I constantly received, upheld, read, and recited it, made offerings with utmost sincerity, and further broadly explained this dhāraṇī for others.

"Good man! I now proclaim this dhāraṇī for you, to protect you. Humans cannot harm you; non-humans also cannot harm you; yakṣas cannot harm you; rākṣasas cannot harm you; pretas cannot harm you; bhūtas cannot harm you; piśācas cannot harm you; kumbhāṇḍas cannot harm you; ostārakas cannot harm you; pūtanas cannot harm you; kaṭa-pūtanas cannot harm you; eaters of excrement cannot harm you; eaters of urine cannot harm you; eaters of various pure and impure substances also cannot harm you.

"Householder Sucandra! This Auspicious Vasudhārā Dhāraṇī: if there are good men or good women who obtain this dhāraṇī sūtra and keep it in their own homes, or hold it in their hands with reverent offerings, or contemplate this sūtra-dharma in their minds, or are able to hear this sūtra-dharma, and then receive, uphold, read, recite, make offerings, or broadly explain its meaning for others, these good men and good women will be constantly protected day and night, will be at ease and happy, with abundant food and drink, and will obtain great merit.

"This Auspicious Vasudhārā Dhāraṇī Sūtra: if one can make offerings to it with utmost sincerity, one is making offerings to all the tathāgatas, arhats, and perfectly awakened ones of past, present, and future. If one can, in accordance with the dharma, recite this dhāraṇī four times during the middle of the night, the devas will come to guard and protect this person and will be delighted. They will also personally descend and rain down supreme, sweet, delicious food and drink. The celestial hosts, toward all tathāgatas, are filled with joy in their hearts; toward all buddha-dharmas, filled with joy; toward the Buddha's signless wisdom, filled with joy; toward the harmonious assembly, filled with joy; toward teachers of the dharma, filled with joy."

The Buddha further told the householder Sucandra: "I now, for your sake and for all sentient beings of the future, to bring vast benefit and happiness, proclaim this Auspicious Vasudhārā Dhāraṇī:

*Namo ratna-trayāya namo bhagavate vajradhara-sāgara-mahā-ghoṣāya tathāgatāya arhate samyak-saṃbuddhāya tadyathā surūpe suvadani bhaṇḍarī subhadrī bhadravatī candravatī muṃgale muṃgale sumuṃgale muṃgalavatī ale acāle acā-pāle unugatā-vaś uniyu-tāvaś unubhidāvaś uccheda-nāvaś sasyavatī dhānavatī dhānīyavatī śrīmatī prabhamatī amale vimale vaśe-male ruru-ruru-ruru-ruvatī surūpe capalī muṃgale ardhā-pannā-siddhi vitanā-siddhi avināsiddhi avi-tanā-siddhi avi-dhānā-siddhi citrā-nā-siddhi viśvakeśi-vaś viśvarūpi viśunāśi viśundhaśīle viguṇini anutārī aṃkurī muṃkurī prabhaṃkurī virame vidhamī dhumī dhudhumī rirīmī khakhale kikhimī ṅaṅamī tattarī tara-tara tattara-tattara tāraya-tāraya vajrī-vajrī vajrūpame ṭhake-ṭhake ṭhāke-ṭhāra-ke uke-ke-phuke dake-dhake-dharake āvarttā-vaś pravartā-vaś varṣaṇi-varṣaṇi-vaś spada-vaś vajradhara-sāgara-mahā-ghoṣa tathāgata manusmara sarva-tathāgata-satya manusmara dharma-satya manusmara saṃgha-satya manusmara taṭṭa-taṭṭa pūraya-pūraya pūraṇi-pūraṇi sarvā-vaś sarva-sattvānāṃ ca bhara-bhara muṃgale śānta-matī śubha-matī mahā-matī muṃgala-matī prabhā-vatī bhadra-vatī sucandra-matī āgaccha āgaccha samaya manusmara svāhā āvaraṇa manusmara svāhā ādhāra manusmara svāhā prabhāva manusmara svāhā svabhāva manusmara svāhā dhṛti manusmara svāhā teja manusmara svāhā jaya manusmara svāhā vijaya manusmara svāhā hṛdaya manusmara svāhā sarva-sattva manusmara svāhā*

Then he proclaimed the Auspicious Vasudhārā root vidyā:

*Namo ratna-trayāya oṃ vasudhārī svāhā oṃ śrī-vasumūḥ-vaś svāhā*

The heart vidyā:

*Namo ratna-trayāya oṃ vasu-śrī svāhā oṃ śrī-vasu-matī-śrī svāhā oṃ vasu svāhā oṃ rakṣa-mi bhūtā-raṃ-vaś vāsinī svāhā namo vajrapāṇi śrī-śrī-kāri-dhāna-kāri-dha-kāri svāhā*

The Buddha further told the householder Sucandra: "This Auspicious Vasudhārā Dhāraṇī possesses great awesome virtue. If you can recite it at the proper times, all illnesses, famines, and calamities of untimely death will be unable to intrude.

"If there are people who, in their own homes or others' homes, wherever they may be, make offerings to the Vajradhara-Sāgaramahāghoṣa Tathāgata, Arhat, Perfectly Awakened One, and with focused mind recite this Auspicious Vasudhārā Dhāraṇī for a full six months with various offerings, then whatever they wish and whatever increasing benefit they seek will all be accomplished.

"If there are people who, in accordance with the dharma, select a pure place, or within a storehouse, and with white sandalwood construct a four-sided maṇḍala, then invite and make offerings to the Vajradhara-Sāgaramahāghoṣa Tathāgata, all buddhas, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, and all the bodhisattva holy assembly, and constantly during the night recite this Auspicious Vasudhārā Dhāraṇī with focused mind without interruption until accomplishment is reached, these good men and good women will receive from a person of great awesome virtue the bestowal of blessings, and whatever they wish will be fulfilled: all wealth, grain, wheat, gold, silver, and precious treasures. All fears and disturbances will be eliminated.

"Householder Sucandra! If you can diligently receive and uphold this Auspicious Vasudhārā Dhāraṇī, reciting it, and can broadly explain it for others, it will bring you benefit and happiness among humans and devas through the long night."

The householder Sucandra praised: "Excellent! Well spoken, World-Honored One!" The householder Sucandra, in the World-Honored One's presence, having heard this Auspicious Vasudhārā Dhāraṇī, his mind gave rise to love and joy. Joyful and elated, his face serene, he bowed at the Buddha's feet and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! This Auspicious Vasudhārā Dhāraṇī, from this day forward I shall never forget! I shall recollect, receive, uphold, read, recite, explain its meaning, make offerings with reverence, and also broadly proclaim this dharma for others."

At that very moment, in the household of the householder Sucandra, in the snap of a finger, there suddenly appeared various gold, silver, precious treasures, grain, wheat, and possessions filling the storehouses. At that time, the householder Sucandra gazed at the World-Honored One, his eyes never leaving him. He again circumambulated hundreds of thousands of times, bowed at the Buddha's feet, and having bowed, withdrew.

At that time, the World-Honored One told Ānanda: "Go today to the household of the householder Sucandra and observe all the possessions, grain, wheat, gold, silver, and precious treasures, the various great storehouses and granaries filled throughout."

At that time, Ānanda, having heard the Buddha's instruction, swiftly went to the great city of Kauśāmbī, to the household of the householder Sucandra. Having arrived and entered within, he saw that all the possessions, grain, wheat, measures of gold, silver, and precious treasures, throughout the various great storehouses, were completely filled. At that time, Ānanda, suddenly seeing this, was inwardly astonished and amazed. His face bore a gentle smile, his mind gave rise to love and joy, and he was joyful and elated. At that time, Ānanda, having fulfilled the Buddha's instruction, returned to the Buddha's presence. Having arrived, he bowed his head at the World-Honored One's feet and addressed the Buddha: "By what causes and conditions has the householder Sucandra obtained such vast merit, vast wealth and honor? His vast storehouses are filled with various possessions, grain, wheat, gold, silver, and precious treasures. Excellent, World-Honored One! I beseech you to explain this matter for me."

The Buddha told Ānanda: "This householder Sucandra possesses great wisdom, being able to generate the mind of recollection and upholding, never forgetting. By receiving, upholding, reading, reciting, making offerings with reverence, contemplating the wondrous truth, generating great compassion, and explaining this dharma for other sentient beings, he causes you to obtain inconceivable supreme merit. Throughout immeasurable ages, he constantly proclaims this dharma for devas and worldly people.

"Ānanda! This dhāraṇī is truly unprecedented. All devas, māras, great Brahmā kings, śramaṇas, brāhmaṇas, devas, humans, asuras, and others should always generate the aspiration toward this Auspicious Vasudhārā Dhāraṇī, honoring and revering it, and should not give rise to minds of contempt or destruction.

"Ānanda! This Auspicious Vasudhārā Dhāraṇī cannot be destroyed by any mantra. Sentient beings without wholesome roots, sentient beings of meager merit, cannot even momentarily hear it with their ears, let alone comprehend this dhāraṇī. In this sūtra, how could they recollect it in their minds, proclaim it with their mouths, receive, uphold, read, and recite it? Why is this so? Because this Auspicious Vasudhārā Dhāraṇī was proclaimed by all tathāgatas together, empowered by all tathāgatas, confirmed, explained, offered to, honored, and praised by all tathāgatas. It is the most supreme, subtle, wondrous, profoundly deep, and difficult-to-understand dharma. All tathāgatas, each with the same mind without difference, with one voice proclaim this wondrous dharma, causing all sentient beings to easily understand.

"I now, for the sake of sentient beings who greatly suffer from poverty, illness, evil terrors, and all disturbances and unpleasant things, cause those who experience such things to attain abundance, ease, and happiness."

At that time, Ānanda, having heard the Buddha proclaim this Auspicious Vasudhārā Dhāraṇī, generated the mind of recollection, receiving, upholding, reading, reciting, contemplating, understanding, honoring, and making offerings. Ānanda rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, placed his right knee on the ground, and joined his palms toward the Buddha. At that time, Ānanda, having reverently bowed to the Buddha, generated a mind of utmost sincerity and spoke this gāthā in praise:

> "Inconceivable is the Buddha, the World-Honored One;
> Inconceivable is the dharma the Buddha has proclaimed.
> Inconceivable the arising of the joyful mind;
> Inconceivable the retribution received in the present life.
> The Teacher of Devas and Humans, with all-knowledge and all-wisdom,
> Having reached the other shore, is freed from birth and death.
> Having realized the supreme fruit, he became the Dharma King.
> I now praise the Buddha's fearlessness."

At that time, having spoken this praise, Ānanda was joyful and elated. He addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! What is the name of this sūtra? How should I receive and uphold it?"

The World-Honored One told Ānanda: "As you previously asked about the householder Sucandra's obtaining of all wealth, treasures, and storehouses, this sūtra on the method of upholding is named 'Proclaimed by All Tathāgatas,' and is named the 'Auspicious Vasudhārā Dhāraṇī Sūtra.' You and others should thus receive and uphold it."

When the Buddha had finished speaking this sūtra, Ānanda, the bhikṣus, the bodhisattva mahāsattvas, and all the devas, humans, asuras, gandharvas, and others of the world were all greatly delighted. They faithfully received and practiced the teaching, bowed in reverence, and withdrew.
