# 聖觀自在菩薩一百八名經
## Sūtra of the One Hundred and Eight Names of Holy Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1054

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Devaśānti (天息災).

---

Thus have I heard. At one time the Buddha was on Mount Potalaka, in the palace of Holy Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva. That mountain, with its towering peaks formed of many jewels, was immaculate and pure, with jāmbūnada gold, maṇi-gem kings, and all manner of precious jewels, their wondrous radiance always shining universally. Furthermore, there were wish-fulfilling heavenly kalpa-trees, from which constantly flowed incense of innumerable kinds, including surabhī, candana, and agaru, along with kusuma flowers, soft and pleasant, of wondrous color and fragrance, adorning every place. Furthermore, immeasurable hundreds of thousands of millions of billions of nayutas of devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, humans, and non-humans went to where the Buddha was, bowed their heads in homage, made offerings with respect and reverence, praised him, and with palms joined in single-minded silence listened to the dharma.

At that time the World-Honored One told Brahmā: "The Tathāgata's teaching of the dharma is good in the beginning, good in the middle, and good in the end. Its meaning is profound, its language skillful, purely unmixed, perfectly pure, with the marks of the brahma-conduct. He teaches the dharma as appropriate to benefit sentient beings. This is the One Hundred and Eight Names of Holy Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva. If anyone hears them, throughout hundreds of thousands of millions of billions of innumerable kalpas they will not fall into evil destinies. If that person has committed the five heinous crimes, all shall be completely extinguished. In every life, world after world, they shall attain the knowledge of former lives. The secret vidyā of the One Hundred and Eight Names of Holy Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva says:

At that time the Buddha told Brahmā and Śakra: "If anyone receives, upholds, reads, and recites this secret vidyā of the One Hundred and Eight Names of Holy Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, that person shall, world after world, in every life, always behold Holy Avalokiteśvara. If one constantly upholds it, one shall gain great wealth, intelligence, valor, physical beauty, a fine voice, eloquence, and shall always know the meaning of all dharmas and gain entry into the maṇḍala. Whatever one prays for and all mantras shall attain accomplishment. If one recites it as a morning practice, one shall be forever free from illness, including scabies, leprosy, and wind disorders. At the end of one's life, one shall be reborn in the Western Land of Ultimate Bliss."

At that time the World-Honored One told Brahmā: "If a person receives and upholds the names of buddhas numbering sixty-two billion Ganges-river-sands, and furthermore makes the four kinds of offerings for their entire life, does that person obtain much reward?" Brahmā said: "Exceedingly much, World-Honored One! Exceedingly much, Sugata!" The Buddha said: "If a person receives and upholds the One Hundred and Eight Names of Holy Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, and even for a brief moment pays homage and makes offerings, the reward of these two persons is exactly equal, without difference. Brahmā, you should know that by receiving, upholding, reading, and reciting the One Hundred and Eight Names of Holy Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, one gains such immeasurable and boundless rewards. How much more so for one who receives, upholds, reads, and recites them for an entire lifetime: the merit gained, world after world, in every life, can never be exhausted."

When the World-Honored One had spoken these words, Great Brahmā, the heavenly lord Śakra, the eight classes of devas and nāgas, and the entire great assembly, having heard what the Buddha taught, accepted it with faith and put it into practice.
