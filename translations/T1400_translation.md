# 佛說消除一切災障寶髻陀羅尼經
## Sūtra on the Jeweled Crest Dhāraṇī for Eliminating All Calamities and Obstacles Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1400

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmabhadra (法賢).

---

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed Ānanda: "There is a dhāraṇī called Jeweled Crest that can bring great benefit to sentient beings and can extinguish the extremely heavy karmic offenses of sentient beings. Ānanda, in the past, when Indra and the asuras were at war, the lord of the devas was defeated and fled, his terror immeasurable. He swiftly came to the place where the Buddha was and cried out: 'World-Honored One, of great compassion, may you look upon me with pity. I am terrified of the asuras and cannot abide in peace. I pray that your great compassion will grant me safety.' Ānanda! Having heard Indra speak thus, I addressed him: 'You need not be afraid. I shall grant you protection. Lord of the Devas! In a past kalpa, there was a buddha-world called Illuminating Contemplation. In that land there was a buddha named Avalokiteśvara Tathāgata. That buddha bestowed upon me the Jeweled Crest Dhāraṇī. This dhāraṇī was proclaimed with one voice by one koṭi of buddhas. It has great majestic power and can bring about auspiciousness. If a person writes it, wears it on the crown of the head, reads and recites it, or receives and upholds it, I know that person's every sin will be extinguished and immeasurable merit will increase. It will constantly provide protection and will also benefit others. Lord of the Devas! You should always remember it, write it, and wear it on your crown. It will cause all your undertakings to accord with your wishes. This dhāraṇī king is also called the Wish-Fulfilling Dhāraṇī. It can bring sentient beings the most supreme benefit. Those asuras will then calm their minds of their own accord. Should they come to battle, they will be defeated and subdued.' I then proclaimed the Jeweled Crest Dhāraṇī:

Translator's note: This dhāraṇī invokes the Three Jewels and the seven perfectly awakened ones. Identifiable terms include: jaya-kari (victory-bringing), buddha-tāraṇī (buddha-deliverance), pavitra (pure), maṅgala (auspicious), sahasra-bhuje (thousand-armed), sahasra-netre (thousand-eyed), candra-sūrya (moon and sun), ākāśa-gagana-cāriṇī (wandering through space), stambhani (immobilize), mohani (confound), amogha-pāśa (infallible noose), hasti (elephant), sarva-bhaya-vimocani (liberating from all fears), maṇi (jewel), tri-loka (three worlds), various ritual commands including praśamani (pacify), nāśanī (destroy), rakṣa (protect), and invocations against specific fears (viṣa-bhiyā/poison, yuddhi-bhiyā/war, graha-uccāṭanī/expelling seizers).

"When the lord of the devas received this dhāraṇī, he was overjoyed. He wore it on his crown, remembered and upheld it, and always dwelt in peace and happiness with his retinue. Ānanda! The majestic power of this dhāraṇī is vast. It not only subjugates the asura king, but even all devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, bhūtas, vetālas, and others who disturb sentient beings are unable to cause harm due to the protection of this dhāraṇī's majestic power. Furthermore, it can eliminate famine, epidemic, poisoning, untimely death, inauspicious dreams, and the terrors of evil māras. If a person who seeks wealth or seeks children recites this dhāraṇī, all will be obtained according to their wishes: wealth will be immeasurable and their retinue will flourish. Ānanda! The merit of this dhāraṇī is supremely excellent. Were I to describe it fully, it would be inexhaustible."

At that time, Ānanda, having heard the Buddha proclaim this dhāraṇī, received and upheld it with devoted mind, bowed to the Buddha, and withdrew.
