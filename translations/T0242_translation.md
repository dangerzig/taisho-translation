# 佛說遍照般若波羅蜜經
## Sūtra of Universal Illumination Prajñāpāramitā Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0242

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dānapāla (施護)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the World-Honored One had attained the vajra-samādhi wisdom of all Tathāgatas, had attained the most supreme jeweled crown of all Tathāgatas' various perfections, had attained the vajra-wisdom corresponding to the great sovereign mudrā of all Tathāgatas, had received the wisdom-seal of all Tathāgatas consecrated as lord of the three realms, and had fulfilled the wishes of all sentient beings through various transformations with the great mantra of equal wisdom. This great Vairocana, eternally abiding in the three ages, praised all the Tathāgatas through the vajra body, speech, and mind-activities of all Tathāgatas equally throughout the three times.

At that time, the World-Honored One was dwelling in the Paranirmitavaśavartin heaven's palace in the desire realm. That palace was adorned with various wondrous great maṇi jewels of many colors, various strings of true pearls, various banners and jeweled canopies, and hung with various jeweled bells, all perfectly adorned. Present were bodhisattva-mahāsattvas: Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva, Vajramuṣṭi Bodhisattva, the Bodhisattva Who Generates the Same Mind and Turns the Dharma-Wheel, Gaganaganjadhvaja Bodhisattva, the Bodhisattva Who Crushes All Māra Kings, and Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva. Such sixty-eight koṭi bodhisattva-mahāsattvas reverently surrounded him, and he taught the Dharma for them. Good in the beginning, good in the middle, and good at the end, with deep meaning, skillful language, purely unmixed and fully pure, he proclaimed the gate of all pure dharmas for bodhisattvas: "If sentient beings are purified with respect to desire, it is because of the bodhisattva. If the bonds of craving are purified, it is because of the bodhisattva. If all actions are purified, it is because of the bodhisattva. If the nature of views is purified, it is because of the bodhisattva. If delight is purified, it is because of the bodhisattva. If greed is purified, it is because of the bodhisattva. If anger is purified, it is because of the bodhisattva. If delusion is purified, it is because of the bodhisattva. If the ālaya is purified, it is because of the bodhisattva. If writing and language are purified, it is because of the bodhisattva. If aspiration is purified, it is because of the bodhisattva. If contemplation is purified, it is because of the bodhisattva. If the body is purified, it is because of the bodhisattva. If speech is purified, it is because of the bodhisattva. If the mind is purified, it is because of the bodhisattva. If form is purified, it is because of the bodhisattva. If sound is purified, it is because of the bodhisattva. If scent is purified, it is because of the bodhisattva. If taste is purified, it is because of the bodhisattva. If touch is purified, it is because of the bodhisattva. What do you think? The self-nature of all dharmas is empty and the self-nature is pure; the prajñāpāramitā is also empty in self-nature and pure in self-nature."

The Buddha told Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva: "Within this gate of all pure dharmas of the prajñāpāramitā, the secret syllable *hūṃ* encompasses all dharmas."

The Buddha told Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva: "As for the prajñāpāramitā, the gate of all pure dharmas, if a person hears, receives, reads, and recites it, that person enters the seat of bodhi. All karmic obstructions, retribution obstructions, affliction obstructions, the evil destinies of the hells, and all veils and entanglements will not arise, and all sufferings will be entirely extinguished. If, furthermore, a person daily receives, upholds, reads, recites, and single-mindedly contemplates it, that person in this very life will attain the samādhi of the equality of all dharmas, the vajra-samādhi. They will attain all sovereignty, delight, and bliss, enter the bodhisattva stage, and swiftly realize the fruit of Buddhahood. Therefore they are named 'vajra-holding Buddhas.' All such dharma-practices are encompassed within the gate of the secret syllable *hūṃ*."

At that time, the Tathāgata Universal Illumination told Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva: "This prajñāpāramitā sūtra teaches the bodhi of the quiescent Dharma of all Tathāgatas. Namely: the vajra-equality bodhi; the bodhi of the equality of the vajra's firm meaning; the bodhi of the firmness and equality of the one meaning and the one Dharma; the bodhi of the inherent purity of the self-nature of all dharmas. These are all the great bodhi, able to eliminate all conceptual proliferations. If any person hears, receives, reads, recites, reverences, and makes offerings, all their karmic obstructions will be eliminated, and they will even attain the seat of bodhi and swiftly realize anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi. All such dharma-practices are encompassed within the gate of the secret syllable *āṃ*."

At that time, the Tathāgata Śākyamuni, in order to tame all evil, taught the prajñāpāramitā sūtra of all dharmas equal and most supreme: "One should see the greed in what greed proliferates about; one should see the delusion in what delusion proliferates about; one should see all dharmas in what all dharmas proliferate about. The prajñāpāramitā should be known in this way. All such dharma-practices are encompassed within the gate of the secret syllable *trāṃ*."

The Buddha told Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva: "If any person hears, receives, upholds, reads, recites, and contemplates this prajñāpāramitā, even if that person were to kill all the sentient beings of the three realms, the sins so created being immeasurable and boundless, through the power of the merit of this recitation they will be swiftly eliminated, and that person will realize anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi. All such dharma-practices are encompassed within the gate of the secret syllable *hūṃ*."

At that time, the Tathāgata of Inherent Purity further taught the prajñāpāramitā sūtra of the wisdom-seal of Avalokiteśvara, the collection of all dharmas: "Because all worldly craving is pure, all anger is pure, all defilements are pure, all sins are pure, all sentient beings are pure, all dharmas are pure, all wisdom is pure, and the prajñāpāramitā is pure."

The Buddha told Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva: "If any person hears, receives, upholds, reads, recites, and contemplates this prajñāpāramitā, even though that person is in the midst of all the mire of craving, the afflictions of craving cannot defile them, for they are like a red lotus unstained by mud. Such a person will before long accomplish anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi."

At that time, the Tathāgata who is Lord of All Three Realms taught the prajñāpāramitā sūtra of the consecration-born wisdom-treasury of all Tathāgatas: "If one gives the consecration, one will attain the body of all kings within the three realms. If one gives beneficial action, one will attain the complete fulfillment of all wished-for desires. If one gives the gift of Dharma, one will swiftly realize the equality of all dharmas. If one gives the gift of material goods, one will have complete happiness of body, speech, and mind. All such dharma-practices are encompassed within the gate of the secret syllable *trāṃ*."

At that time, the Tathāgata who Attains the Wisdom-Seal of All Tathāgatas and Constantly Upholds All Secret Dharmas taught the prajñāpāramitā sūtra of the vajra-wisdom-seal of all Tathāgatas: "If one receives the body-seal of all Tathāgatas, one accomplishes all Tathāgatas. If one receives the speech-seal, one accomplishes all Dharma-gates. If one receives the mind-seal, one accomplishes all samādhis. If one receives the vajra-seal, one accomplishes the most supreme vajra activities of body, speech, and mind of all Tathāgatas."

The Buddha told Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva: "If any person hears, receives, reads, recites, and contemplates this sūtra, that person will swiftly accomplish complete wealth and nobility, swiftly accomplish the secret of vajra body, speech, and mind, and before long realize the most supreme anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi. All such dharma-practices are encompassed within the gate of the secret syllable *aḥ*."

At that time, the Tathāgata of All Non-Proliferation further taught the prajñāpāramitā of the revolving syllable-wheel: "Because all dharmas are without self-nature, they are without marks. Because all dharmas are without marks, they are without activity. Because they are without activity, they are without aspiration. Because they are without aspiration, all dharmas are originally pure, and the prajñāpāramitā is pure. All such dharma-practices are encompassed within the gate of the secret syllable *aṃ*."

At that time, the Tathāgata Who Enters the Great Wheel of All further taught the prajñāpāramitā of entering the great wheel: "By entering the equality of the vajra, one also enters the wheel of all Tathāgatas. By entering the equality of the vajra's benefit, one also enters the wheel of all great bodhisattvas. By entering the equality of the Dharma, one also enters the wheel of all dharmas. By entering universal equality, one also enters all wheels. All such dharma-practices are encompassed within the gate of the secret syllable *raṃ*."

At that time, the Tathāgata of All Offering Rites Vastly Arisen further taught the prajñāpāramitā sūtra of the most supreme offering to all Tathāgatas: "Give rise to the bodhi-mind and broadly offer to all Tathāgatas. Rescue all sentient beings and cause them to receive the wondrous Dharma of all Tathāgatas, copying, offering, receiving, upholding, reading, and reciting it. One will then receive the vast offering of all Tathāgatas. All such dharma-practices are encompassed within the gate of the secret syllable *oṃ*."

At that time, the Tathāgata of Equal Taming of All further taught the prajñāpāramitā sūtra of Vajrapāṇi's taming of all sentient beings, the treasury of correct wisdom: "Because all sentient beings are equal, anger is also equal. Because all sentient beings are tamed, anger is also tamed. Because all dharmas are equal, anger is also equal. Because all sentient beings are equal, the vajra is also equal. What do you think? For the sake of bodhi, one thus tames all sentient beings. All such dharma-practices are encompassed within the gate of the secret syllable *haṃ*."

At that time, the Tathāgata Who Abides in the Equality of All Dharmas further taught the prajñāpāramitā sūtra of the most supreme equality of all dharmas: "Because all is equal, the prajñāpāramitā is also equal. Because all is beneficial, the prajñāpāramitā is also beneficial. Because all is Dharma, the prajñāpāramitā is also Dharma. Because all is karma, the prajñāpāramitā is also karma. All such dharma-practices are encompassed within the gate of the secret syllable *hrīṃ*."

At that time, the Tathāgata who is Lord of the World further taught the prajñāpāramitā sūtra of all sentient beings' abiding: "The tathāgatagarbha of all sentient beings is the treasury of the great bodhisattva Samantabhadra, because of the self-nature of all. It is the vajra-treasury, because of the vajra consecration. It is the Dharma-treasury, because of turning all speech. It is the karma-treasury, because of performing all skillful deeds. All such dharma-practices are encompassed within the gate of the secret syllable *hrīṃ*."

At that time, the Tathāgata of Immeasurable, Boundless, and Ultimate, abiding in the boundless and ultimate Dharma, taught the prajñāpāramitā sūtra of all dharmas abiding in the equality of the ultimate vajra: "Because the prajñāpāramitā is boundless, all Tathāgatas are also boundless. Because all Tathāgatas are boundless, the prajñāpāramitā is also boundless. And because the prajñāpāramitā is one, all dharmas are one. Because the prajñāpāramitā is ultimate, all dharmas are also ultimate."

The Buddha told Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva: "If any person hears, receives, upholds, reads, recites, and contemplates this prajñāpāramitā, that person will enter the ultimately pure bodhisattva stage. All veils and obstructions will ultimately not arise, and that person will be named a vajra-holding Tathāgata. All such dharma-practices are encompassed within the gate of the secret syllable *vyāṃ*."

At that time, the great Tathāgata Vairocana, having attained the secret Dharma of all Tathāgatas and awakened to all dharmas of proliferation, taught the prajñāpāramitā sūtra of the great bliss vajra non-empty samādhi of equality: "The most supreme Dharma-gate is originally without anything, without high, middle, or low. If one can accomplish the Dharma-bliss of the bodhi of all Tathāgatas, subdue the māra enemies, be sovereign in the three realms, and even rescue all sentient beings, causing them to obtain the most supreme benefit and bliss, such a person is named a great-earth bodhisattva. All such dharma-practices are encompassed within the gate of the secret syllable *svaṃ*.

"Furthermore, Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva! That great bodhisattva, even while dwelling in saṃsāra, performs all beneficial deeds to ferry sentient beings across. Abiding in skillful means and wisdom, all their undertakings are purified. The afflictions of craving cannot invade them, just as a lotus in water is not stained by mud. They are sovereign, at ease, joyful, firm, and non-retrogressing. All such dharma-practices are encompassed within the gate of the secret syllable *ha*.

"Furthermore, Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva! If any person single-mindedly contemplates, daily reads and recites, or even joyfully listens to this prajñāpāramitā sūtra, that person will attain all happiness and joyful bliss and accomplish the non-empty great bliss of the vajra-samādhi. All such dharma-practices are encompassed within the gate of the secret syllable *hūṃ*."

At that time, the World-Honored One, the Tathāgata Vairocana, told Vajrapāṇi, the Great Secret Lord: "I shall now further teach the twenty-five kinds of secret Dharma-gates of the prajñāpāramitā. Listen carefully! The mantras are:

*Oṃ namo sarva-buddha-bodhisattvānāṃ*

*Oṃ bodhicitta-vajri*

*Oṃ surata-stvaṃ*

*Oṃ samantabhadra-caryā vibhūmaṇi*

*Oṃ aniruddhi*

*Oṃ jāti-vivṛte*

*Oṃ mahārāga-dharmi*

*Oṃ vīrya-kāvaci*

*Oṃ sarva-gāmi*

*Oṃ vajra-dṛḍha-kāvacit hūṃ*

*Oṃ sarva-tathāgate*

*Oṃ svabhāva-śuddhi*

*Oṃ dharmata-jñāna-viśuddha*

*Oṃ karma-viśodhani hūṃ*

*Oṃ niṣumbha-vajriṇī hūṃ phaṭ*

*Oṃ kāmarāgi*

*Oṃ jaha-vajri*

*Oṃ sarva-dāyeni*

*Oṃ hrīṃ*

*Oṃ ākāra-mukhe*

*Oṃ prajñā-pāramitā-jakaṃ āṃ hūṃ*

*Oṃ āṃ oṃ sarva-tathāgata-kāya-gṛ*

*Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-viśodhani*

*Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-citta-vajra a*

*Oṃ saṃvigya sarva-sattvānāṃ mokṣāya sarva-pāpa-gate-vyā sarva-samaya-vajra hūṃ traṭ*"

At that time, the World-Honored One, having spoken these mantras, told Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva: "These seals and mantras can destroy all the darkness of sin, can produce all auspiciousness, and are the most supreme accomplishment of the vajra secrets of all Tathāgatas. If a person obtains this and upholds, recites, and hears it, that person is named a vajra-holding, pure Tathāgata. If there are sentient beings who receive, uphold, read, recite, joyfully hear, and attend to this prajñāpāramitā sūtra, such persons have already, in the presence of immeasurable Buddhas, planted various good roots and cultivated many foundations of virtue.

"Furthermore, Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva! If a person hears even a single four-line verse of this prajñāpāramitā sūtra, they will receive the reverent offerings of eighty koṭi nayuta Ganges-sands of Tathāgatas, how much more so if they understand its meaning and expound it for others. The place where that person holds this sūtra is like a Buddha's stūpa-temple, and all devas, humans, asuras, and others will constantly come to pay their respects. If a person circulates this sūtra and successively reads and recites it, they will obtain the knowledge of past lives and be able to know the events of koṭis of kalpas in the past. All māras, evil spirits, and calamities will be unable to invade or harm them. They will constantly be protected by the four great heavenly kings and the worthy sages. At the end of their life, their mind will not be confused, and all the Buddhas and great bodhisattvas will come to welcome them. They may be reborn at will in the pure lands of the ten directions.

"Furthermore, Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva! This prajñāpāramitā sūtra, the sacred and essential Dharma-gate, accomplishes such most supreme dharma-practices and excellent, wondrous merit."

When the Buddha had finished teaching this sūtra, Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva, and devas, humans, asuras, gandharvas, and the rest, all greatly rejoiced, believed, accepted, and respectfully practiced it.
