# 佛說熾盛光大威德消災吉祥陀羅尼經
## Sūtra of the Dhāraṇī of Blazing Radiance and Great Awesome Virtue for Averting Calamity and Bringing Auspiciousness, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0963

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空)

---

Preface:

The teaching of the Benevolent One is this: what it accords with is the Way; what it embodies is the spirit. It governs the dharma-realm through conditioned actions and exhausts the three times without ceasing. There is nowhere it does not pervade. Therefore, heaven serves as covering, earth as support, sun, moon, and stars as illumination, and all living beings and things as indicators of fortune and misfortune. When the waning and waxing respond in mutual connection, misfortune can be averted and fortune can be pursued. Heaven, earth, sun, moon, stars, and the myriad things can each be brought to their proper state. This is the essential function of this sūtra. Its averting of calamities, guiding toward auspiciousness, ritual procedures, altar protocols, essential regulations, and time limits are all luminously set forth without omission, sufficient to bring blessings to the nation above and protection to the people below, reaching from the near to the far and from phenomena to truth. It is truly a Way of breadth and concision. Since Amoghavajra first transmitted and translated it, successive dynasties have treasured it. Those who recite and uphold it according to the Dharma have benefited beings of all capacities. Should it not have an exegetical commentary to aid its circulation? In former times, Master Jian of Yunjian, though he once composed a sub-commentary, it was uneven in its verbosity and brevity. Without presuming to measure my own abilities, I have gathered the authoritative sayings of the patriarchs of the Tiantai lineage, edited and revised, to perhaps offer some small aid to posterity. In the second year of Zhizhi [1322 CE], by imperial summons I proceeded to the capital, traveling by relay along the Huai River. This preface was written on the day of the Mid-Autumn Festival.

---

At that time, Śākyamuni Buddha was in the Pure Abode Heaven. He addressed the hosts of asterisms and celestial beings who traverse empty space, the nine planetary lords, the twenty-eight lunar mansions, the twelve zodiacal deities, and all the holy assembly: "I will now teach the Dharma of the Blazing Radiance and Great Awesome Virtue Dhāraṇī for averting calamities, as spoken by Sāla-rāja Tathāgata in the past. If there are national kings and their great ministers, and in the places where they dwell and throughout all the realms under their rule, the five planets encroach upon them, or Rāhu, comets, baleful stars, or ominous stars shine upon their natal mansions, or the zodiacal positions of their ruling stars come under influence, or they approach the imperial throne, and in the nation, in the household, and in their territorial domains, there is encroachment, whether advancing or retreating, creating various obstructions, one should simply set up a practice-hall in a clean place and recite this dhāraṇī one hundred and eight times or one thousand times. Whether for one day, two days, three days, or up to seven days, one should adorn the altar according to the Dharma and receive, uphold, read, and recite it with utmost sincerity. All calamities will be entirely eliminated and will be unable to cause harm. If Venus or Mars enters the Southern Dipper, creating various obstructions for the nation, the household, and the territorial domains, one should set up before an image of a wrathful deity, draw the form of the offending planet, and recite this dhāraṇī with a fierce voice to empower it. That calamity will immediately be removed, transferred to the body of one who is disobedient and rebellious against the king's command." Then he spoke the dhāraṇī:

"*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ apratihata-śāsanānāṃ tadyathā oṃ kha kha khahi khahi huṃ huṃ jvala jvala prajvāla prajvāla tiṣṭha tiṣṭha sthiri sthiri sphura sphura śāntika-śrīye svāhā*"

"This dhāraṇī was proclaimed in unison by all the Tathāgatas. If there are bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, good men, or good women who receive, uphold, read, and recite this dhāraṇī, they can accomplish eighty thousand kinds of auspicious matters and can eliminate eighty thousand kinds of inauspicious matters. If there are national kings, great ministers and their retinues, all the common people, and they are encroached upon by the five planets, Rāhu, Ketu, comets, ominous portents, or baleful stars approaching the imperial throne; if in the nation, the household, and the territorial domains, the ruling mansions are beset by calamities arising in succession; if Saturn encroaches, whether advancing or retreating; if enemies from past lives seek to harm them; if various evil and untoward events, slander, hostile spells, and curses create calamities: have all sentient beings receive and uphold this according to the Dharma, and all calamities will be entirely eliminated, unable to cause harm. Calamity will be transformed into fortune, and all will obtain auspiciousness. I now teach this dhāraṇī of inconceivable and incomparable merit. Receive and uphold it in secret, and do not recklessly transmit it." At that time, the Tathāgata addressed the four assemblies: "If there are national realms that are unsettled and calamities arise in profusion, invite a pure assembly and establish a practice-hall according to the Dharma. Install a Buddha image, establish the boundary and maintain protection, and make offerings of incense, flowers, lamps, and candles according to one's ability. This will cause all sentient beings to obtain immeasurable merit, and the calamities will immediately be eliminated." At that time, when the Tathāgata had finished teaching this dhāraṇī sūtra, Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva, all the śrāvakas, the four assemblies, the great celestial beings who traverse empty space, and all the asterisms and the entire holy assembly, all following the Buddha's command, bowed their heads in reverence, received and upheld it, and each returned to their own celestial palace. And the devas, nāgas, the eight divisions, and the entire great assembly, having heard what the Buddha taught, were all greatly delighted, faithfully received it, and practiced accordingly.
