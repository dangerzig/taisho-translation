# 須摩提經
## Sumatī Sūtra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0336

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Bodhiruci (菩提流志)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the city of Rājagṛha, on Mount Gṛdhrakūṭa, together with one thousand two hundred and fifty bhikṣus and ten thousand bodhisattva-mahāsattvas.

At that time, in the city of Rājagṛha there was an elder's daughter named Sumatī, eight years of age. Her face was upright and proper, her appearance beautiful and fine, and she was complete in all marks. All who saw her were delighted. She had in past lives drawn near to and made offerings to immeasurable Buddhas, planting various good roots.

At that time, this young woman went to the Tathāgata's place, prostrated at the Buddha's feet, circumambulated him three times to the right, knelt with palms joined, and spoke in verse:

> "Unsurpassed, perfectly awakened one,
> Great lamp of illumination for the world:
> What are the practices of a bodhisattva?
> I beg you to permit my question."

The Buddha told Sumatī: "You may now freely ask, and I shall explain, cutting through the net of your doubts."

At that time, Sumatī spoke before the Buddha, asking in verse:

> "How does one obtain a body that is beautiful,
> Wealthy, noble, and honored?
> And by what causes and conditions
> Does one gain retainers who cannot be undermined?
> How does one come to see oneself
> Receiving a transformation birth
> Upon a thousand-petaled lotus flower,
> Face to face with all the World-Honored Ones?
> How can one realize
> The sovereign, supreme supernatural powers,
> Traveling to immeasurable lands
> To pay reverence to all the Buddhas?
> How does one become free from enmity,
> With words that others trust and accept,
> Purified of Dharma-obstructions,
> And forever free from all māra deeds?
> How can one, at the time of death,
> Come to see all the Buddhas,
> Hear the teaching of the pure Dharma,
> And not suffer from affliction?
> O Great Compassionate, Unsurpassed Honored One!
> I beg you to explain this for me."

At that time, the Buddha told the girl Sumatī: "Excellent! Excellent! You are well able to ask about this profound and wondrous meaning. Listen carefully, reflect well upon it, and I shall explain it for you."

Sumatī said: "So it is, World-Honored One! I delight in hearing."

The Buddha said: "Sumatī! A bodhisattva who accomplishes four dharmas obtains a beautiful body. What are the four? First, not giving rise to anger toward evil companions. Second, abiding in great loving-kindness. Third, deeply delighting in the correct Dharma. Fourth, making images of the Buddha."

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> "Anger destroys good roots; do not let it grow.
> Loving-kindness, delight in the Dharma, and making Buddha images:
> One will obtain a body adorned with all marks,
> Which all sentient beings will always delight in seeing.

"Furthermore, Sumatī! A bodhisattva who accomplishes four dharmas obtains a body of wealth and nobility. What are the four? First, giving at the proper time. Second, giving without a mind of contempt. Third, giving with joy. Fourth, giving without hoping for reward."

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> "Giving at the proper time, without contempt,
> Joyfully bestowing without seeking return:
> One who constantly and diligently cultivates these deeds
> Will in future births obtain great wealth and station.

"Furthermore, Sumatī! A bodhisattva who accomplishes four dharmas obtains retainers who cannot be undermined. What are the four? First, skillfully abandoning and avoiding divisive speech. Second, causing beings of wrong views to abide in right views. Third, when the correct Dharma is about to perish, protecting it and causing it to long endure. Fourth, teaching sentient beings to proceed toward the Buddha's bodhi."

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> "Abandoning divisive speech and wrong views,
> Protecting the correct Dharma when it is about to perish,
> Establishing beings in the great bodhi:
> One will achieve retainers that cannot be undermined.

"Furthermore, Sumatī! A bodhisattva who accomplishes four dharmas will, before the Buddha, receive a transformation birth and be seated upon a lotus flower. What are the four? First, offering flowers, fruits, and fine powdered incense, scattering them upon the Tathāgata and upon stūpas. Second, never wrongly harming others. Third, making images of the Tathāgata and placing them upon lotus flowers. Fourth, giving rise to deep and pure faith in the Buddha's bodhi."

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> "Scattering flowers and incense upon the Buddha and stūpas,
> Not harming others and making images,
> With deep faith and understanding in the great bodhi:
> One will be born before the Buddha upon a lotus flower.

"Furthermore, Sumatī! A bodhisattva who accomplishes four dharmas travels from one buddha-land to another. What are the four? First, seeing others cultivate goodness and not creating obstacles for them. Second, never obstructing others when they teach the Dharma. Third, lighting lamps as offerings at the Tathāgata's stūpas. Fourth, constantly and diligently cultivating the various dhyāna meditations."

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> "Seeing others cultivate good and teach the correct Dharma,
> Not giving rise to slander or creating obstacles,
> Offering lamps at the Tathāgata's stūpas,
> Cultivating the dhyānas: one will travel to buddha-lands.

"Furthermore, Sumatī! A bodhisattva who accomplishes four dharmas will dwell in the world without enmity. What are the four? First, drawing near to good friends without a flattering mind. Second, having no jealous mind toward others' superior dharmas. Third, constantly rejoicing when others gain renown. Fourth, not having a mind that belittles or slanders the bodhisattva practices."

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> "Not drawing near good friends with flattery,
> Having no jealous mind toward others' superior dharmas,
> Always rejoicing in others' renown,
> Not slandering bodhisattvas: one obtains freedom from enmity.

"Furthermore, Sumatī! A bodhisattva who accomplishes four dharmas will have words that others trust and accept. What are the four? First, one's speech and practice always correspond. Second, not concealing one's faults from good friends. Third, not seeking faults in the Dharma one hears. Fourth, not giving rise to an evil mind toward those who teach the Dharma."

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> "Speech and practice always in accord,
> Not hiding one's faults from good friends,
> Hearing the sūtras without seeking faults in persons or Dharma:
> All one's words will be trusted and accepted.

"Furthermore, Sumatī! A bodhisattva who accomplishes four dharmas can be free from Dharma-obstructions and swiftly attain purity. What are the four? First, with deep aspiration, embracing the three sets of pure precepts. Second, not slandering the profound sūtras when hearing them. Third, seeing newly resolved bodhisattvas and giving rise to a mind of omniscience toward them. Fourth, having great loving-kindness equally toward all sentient beings."

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> "With deep aspiration embracing the precepts,
> Hearing profound sūtras and being able to believe and understand,
> Reverencing the newly resolved as one would the Buddha,
> With loving-kindness pervading all: obstructions are removed.

"Furthermore, Sumatī! A bodhisattva who accomplishes four dharmas can be free from all māras. What are the four? First, understanding the equality of the nature of all dharmas. Second, giving rise to diligent effort. Third, constantly being mindful of the Buddha. Fourth, dedicating all good roots."

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> "Knowing the equal nature of all dharmas,
> Always giving rise to diligence and being mindful of the Tathāgata,
> Dedicating all good roots:
> The māra hosts cannot take advantage.

"Furthermore, Sumatī! A bodhisattva who accomplishes four dharmas will have all the Buddhas appear before them at the time of death. What are the four? First, when others have requests, giving so as to satisfy them. Second, giving rise to deep faith and understanding in all good dharmas. Third, offering adornments and implements to the bodhisattvas. Fourth, diligently cultivating offerings to the Three Treasures."

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> "Satisfying others' requests when they seek,
> Having deep faith and understanding in the Dharma, offering adornments,
> Diligently making offerings to the field of merit of the Three Treasures:
> At the time of death, the Buddhas will appear before you."

At that time, the girl Sumatī, having heard the Buddha's teaching, said: "World-Honored One! As the Buddha has taught the bodhisattva practices, I shall carry them out. World-Honored One! If among these forty practices I should fail to cultivate even one, I would be violating the Buddha's teaching and deceiving the Tathāgata."

At that time, the Venerable Mahāmaudgalyāyana told Sumatī: "The bodhisattva practices are extremely difficult to carry out. You have now made this supreme and great vow. Can you truly be sovereign over this vow?"

At that time, Sumatī said: "Venerable one! If my great vow is true and not false, and can cause all these practices to be perfected, may this three-thousand great-thousand world system quake in six ways."

Then Sumatī further addressed Maudgalyāyana: "By the power of my true words, in a future life I shall attain Buddhahood just as the present Śākyamuni Tathāgata, and in my land there will be no māra deeds, no evil destinies, and no word for 'woman.' If my words are not false, may the bodies of this entire great assembly turn golden." When she had spoken, the entire assembly turned golden.

At that time, the Venerable Mahāmaudgalyāyana rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, prostrated at the Buddha's feet, and said: "World-Honored One! I now first pay homage to the newly resolved bodhisattvas and all the bodhisattva-mahāsattvas."

At that time, the Dharma-Prince Mañjuśrī told Sumatī: "In what dharma do you abide to make such a sincere vow?"

Sumatī answered: "Mañjuśrī! That is not a proper question. Why? Because within the dharma-realm, there is nothing to abide in."

He asked again: "What is called bodhi?"

She answered: "The dharma of non-discrimination is called bodhi."

He asked again: "What is called a bodhisattva?"

She answered: "All dharmas being equal to the nature of empty space is what is called a bodhisattva."

He asked again: "What is called the practice of bodhi?"

She answered: "Like the actions of a mirage and a valley echo: such is the practice of bodhi."

He asked again: "Based on what hidden meaning do you speak thus?"

She answered: "In this matter, I do not see even the slightest dharma that is hidden or not hidden."

He asked again: "If that is so, then all ordinary beings should be bodhi itself."

She answered: "Do you say that bodhi is different from ordinary beings? Do not hold such a view. Why? Because these are all of the same dharma-realm nature: neither grasped nor released, without arising or perishing."

He asked again: "Among those in this teaching, how many can understand it?"

She answered: "As many as the quantity of the minds and mental factors of illusory, conjured beings: that many illusory, conjured beings can understand this meaning."

Mañjuśrī said: "Illusions and conjurations are fundamentally non-existent. How can there be such minds and mental factors?"

She answered: "The dharma-realm is also thus: neither existent nor non-existent. Even the Tathāgata is likewise."

At that time, Mañjuśrī addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! This Sumatī is truly remarkable. She has been able to accomplish such Dharma-patience."

The Buddha said: "So it is, so it is. It is truly as you say. Moreover, this girl has already in the past given rise to the bodhi-mind. Thirty kalpas after her, I then resolved upon unsurpassed bodhi. It was she who also caused you to abide in the patience of non-arising."

At that time, Mañjuśrī rose from his seat, made obeisance to her, and told Sumatī: "Immeasurable kalpas ago, I once made offerings to you. I did not expect that today I would again be in your presence."

Sumatī said: "Mañjuśrī! Do not give rise to such discrimination. Why? Because it is through non-discrimination that one attains the patience of non-arising."

He asked again: "Sumatī! Have you still not transformed your female body?"

Sumatī answered: "The marks of a woman are utterly unobtainable. What then is there to transform?

"Mañjuśrī! I shall now remove your doubts for you. By the power of my true words, when in a future life I attain anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi, in my Dharma all bhikṣus will receive ordination by the call 'Come, well come!' and go forth to study the path. All the beings in my land will have bodies of golden color. Their clothing, provisions, and necessities will be like those of the Sixth Heaven. Food and drink will be abundant, appearing at the mere thought. There will be no māra deeds and no evil destinies. There will also be no word for 'woman.' There will be forests of the seven treasures covered with jeweled nets above, and seven-jeweled lotus flowers covered with jeweled canopies, just like the pure land accomplished by Mañjuśrī, adorned and embellished in exactly the same way.

"If my words are not false, may the bodies of this great assembly all turn golden, and may my female body transform into a male, like a thirty-year-old bhikṣu learned in the Dharma."

When she spoke these words, the bodies of the entire great assembly all turned golden, and the bodhisattva Sumatī transformed from female to male, appearing as a thirty-year-old bhikṣu learned in the Dharma.

At that time, the earth-dwelling hosts of devas praised one another, saying: "Extraordinary! Extraordinary! The bodhisattva-mahāsattva Sumatī, when attaining bodhi in a future life, will adorn and uphold a buddha-land with merit and virtue like this."

At that time, the Buddha told Mañjuśrī: "This bodhisattva Sumatī will in a future life attain perfect awakening with the name Supremely Excellent Merit-Treasury Tathāgata, appearing in the world." When the Buddha taught this sūtra, thirty koṭi of sentient beings abided in non-retrogression from anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi. Eighty koṭi of sentient beings were freed from dust and defilement and attained the pure Dharma-eye. Eight thousand sentient beings all realized wisdom.

Five thousand bhikṣus who were practicing the bodhisattva vehicle and whose minds were about to retrogress, having seen the bodhisattva Sumatī's wholesome roots of aspiration and surpassing awesome virtue, each removed the upper garments from their bodies and offered them to the Tathāgata.

Having made this offering, they made a great vow: "By this good root, we are determined to accomplish anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi." Because those good men dedicated this good root to unsurpassed bodhi, they transcended ninety kalpas of birth-and-death suffering and became non-retrogressing from anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi.

At that time, the World-Honored One gave them a prediction: "All of you, in a future life, after one thousand kalpas have passed, in the Immaculate Radiance Kalpa, in the Mirage World, in the Hard-to-Endure Buddha-land, will one after another within a single kalpa attain Buddhahood, all bearing the same name: Eloquence-Adornment Tathāgata, appearing in the world.

"Mañjuśrī! This Dharma-gate is of great awesome power, able to bring great benefit to bodhisattva-mahāsattvas and those of the śrāvaka vehicle. Mañjuśrī! If there are good men or good women who, seeking bodhi, practice the six pāramitās without skillful means for a full thousand kalpas, and if another person in half a month's time copies and reads and recites this sūtra once, the merit attained by the latter surpasses the former by a hundredfold, a thousandfold, a hundred-thousand koṭi-fold, even beyond what calculation or analogy can express.

"Therefore, Mañjuśrī! This subtle and wondrous Dharma-gate is the very foundation of the bodhisattvas' sūtra-treasury. I now entrust it to you. In the future, you should receive, uphold, read, recite, and explain it for others. Just as when a wheel-turning holy king appears in the world, all the seven treasures appear before him, yet after the king passes, the treasures all vanish: so too when this subtle and wondrous Dharma-gate circulates in the world, the illumination of the Tathāgata's seven factors of awakening and other dharmas does not perish; but if it ceases to circulate, the correct Dharma will perish.

"Therefore, Mañjuśrī! If good men and good women seeking bodhi should give rise to diligent effort, copy this sūtra, receive, uphold, read, and recite it, and explain it for others. This is my teaching. Do not in future lives give rise to a mind of regret."

When the Buddha had finished teaching this sūtra, the bodhisattva Sumatī, the bodhisattva Mañjuśrī, and all the great assembly, devas, humans, asuras, gandharvas, and the rest, having heard what the Buddha taught, all greatly rejoiced, believed, accepted, and respectfully practiced it.
