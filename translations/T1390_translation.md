# 佛說洛叉陀羅尼經
## Sūtra of the Lakṣa Dhāraṇī Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1390

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmabhadra (法賢)

---

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed Ānanda, saying: "Listen carefully. I shall now proclaim to you the Lakṣa Dhāraṇī. This dhāraṇī is rare and difficult to encounter, just as the Buddhas appearing in the world are rare. Ānanda! If any sentient being obtains this dhāraṇī and is able to receive and uphold it, the merit that person acquires is no different from that of upholding a lakṣa of Buddhas; it can produce a great accumulation of merit for sentient beings, and it can extinguish immeasurable grave sins of sentient beings."

At that time, the World-Honored One then proclaimed the Lakṣa Dhāraṇī:

[Dhāraṇī] *namas samanta-buddhānāṃ* / *oṃ namo bhagavate vipulacanānaṃ* *gaṃcanudita-pada-prabhāsaketu-mūrdhni* *tathāgatāya arhate samyak-saṃbuddhāya* / *tathā bodhi bodhi sarva-tathāgata-gocari* / *dhara dhara hara hara prahara prahara* / *mahābodhi-citta-dhāri curu curu muru muru* / *śatasahasra-rāśmi-saṃcodite* *sarva-tathāgata-vasite guṇaguṇavati* *buddha-guṇāvabhāse mili mili* *sarva-tathāgata-adhiṣṭhite nabhastare* / *śama śama praśama praśama* *sarva-pāpa-praśamanī sarva-pāpa-viśodhanī* / *huru huru bodhi-mārga-saṃprasthite* *sarva-tathāgata-pratiṣṭhite viśuddhe svāhā* / *oṃ sarva-tathāgata-avalokite jaya jaya svāhā* / *oṃ huru huru jaya-mukhe svāhā*

At that time, having heard the Buddha proclaim this dhāraṇī, Ānanda rejoiced and received it with faith, paid reverence, and withdrew.

---

**Translator's note:** The dhāraṇī is preserved in the Chinese as a phonetic transliteration of Sanskrit. The above represents a scholarly reconstruction toward probable Sanskrit originals; elements such as *vipulacanānaṃ*, *gaṃcanudita-pada*, and *nabhastare* remain uncertain. Clearly identifiable formulae include the homage sequence (*namas samanta-buddhānāṃ ... tathāgatāya arhate samyak-saṃbuddhāya*), the purification triad (*sarva-pāpa-praśamanī sarva-pāpa-viśodhanī*), and the three closing *svāhā* formulas.
