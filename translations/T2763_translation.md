# Interlinear-Annotated Śrīmālā Sūtra

## *Jiázhù Shèngmán Jīng* (挾注勝鬘經)

Taishō 2763

Author unknown. A Dunhuang manuscript.

*Note: This is a Dunhuang manuscript fragment preserving portions of the Śrīmālādevī-siṃhanāda-sūtra (Queen Śrīmālā's Lion's Roar Sūtra, cf. T353) with interlinear annotations (jiázhù). In the surviving extraction, only the base sūtra text is preserved; the interlinear notes (which would have appeared in smaller characters between the lines of the main text) have apparently been lost. The text covers key doctrinal passages on the Mahāyāna, the inadequacy of the arhats' and pratyekabuddhas' attainment compared to the Buddha's complete nirvāṇa, the two kinds of death, and the doctrine of the four abodes of affliction (zhùdì fánnǎo), including the crucial concept of "beginningless ignorance" (wúshǐ wúmíng zhùdì). A colophon identifies the text as offered by the disciple Tánshòu Kědūn.*

[Opening lost] ...the dharma has immeasurable, boundless merit.

The Buddha addressed Śrīmālā: "You should now further expound on the embracing of the true dharma as taught by all buddhas." Śrīmālā addressed the Buddha: "Excellent, World-Honored One. I humbly receive your teaching." She then addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One, the embracing of the true dharma is the Mahāyāna. Why? Because the Mahāyāna gives birth to all the wholesome dharmas of śrāvakas, pratyekabuddhas, and the worldly and supramundane. World-Honored One, just as the great Lake Anavatapta gives rise to eight great rivers, so too the Mahāyāna gives birth to all the wholesome dharmas of śrāvakas, pratyekabuddhas, and the worldly and supramundane.

"World-Honored One, just as all seeds depend upon the earth to grow, so too all the wholesome dharmas of śrāvakas, pratyekabuddhas, and the worldly and supramundane depend upon the Mahāyāna to increase and grow. Therefore, World-Honored One, abiding in the Mahāyāna and embracing the Mahāyāna is itself abiding in the two vehicles and embracing the two vehicles and all wholesome dharmas of the world and beyond the world.

"As the World-Honored One taught, there are six matters. What are the six? They are: the abiding of the true dharma, the extinction of the true dharma, the prātimokṣa, the vinaya, leaving home, and receiving full ordination. These six matters are taught for the sake of the Mahāyāna. Why? The abiding of the true dharma is taught for the sake of the Mahāyāna: when the Mahāyāna abides, the true dharma abides. The extinction of the true dharma is taught for the sake of the Mahāyāna: when the Mahāyāna is extinguished, the true dharma is extinguished. The prātimokṣa and the vinaya: these two dharmas have the same meaning but different names. The vinaya is itself the Mahāyāna training. Why? Because one leaves home in dependence on the Buddha and receives full ordination. Therefore it is said that the Mahāyāna code of conduct is the vinaya, is leaving home, is receiving full ordination. Therefore the arhat has no separate leaving home or receiving of full ordination. Why? Because the arhat depends on the Tathāgata to leave home and receive full ordination.

"The arhat takes refuge in the Buddha. The arhat has fear. Why? The arhat, with respect to all actionless states, abides with thoughts of dread, like a person with a sword drawn wishing to come and harm him. Therefore the arhat has no ultimate bliss. Why? World-Honored One, those who are without a refuge seek a refuge. Just as beings without a refuge have fear of this and that, and because of fear they seek a refuge, so too the arhat has fear. Because of fear, he depends on the Tathāgata.

"World-Honored One, arhats and pratyekabuddhas have fear. Therefore arhats and pratyekabuddhas have remaining births. Because the dharma of birth is not exhausted, they have birth. Because remaining practices of the holy life are yet to be completed, their practice is not pure. Because their task is not completely finished, there is still something to be done. Because they have not crossed beyond, there is still something to be severed. Because it is not severed, they are far from the realm of nirvāṇa. Why? Only the Tathāgata, the Worthy One, the Perfectly Enlightened One, attains parinirvāṇa and accomplishes all merits. Therefore arhats and pratyekabuddhas do not accomplish all merits. To say they attain nirvāṇa is the Buddha's skillful means.

"Only the Tathāgata attains parinirvāṇa and accomplishes immeasurable merits. Therefore arhats and pratyekabuddhas accomplish limited merits. To say they attain nirvāṇa is the Buddha's skillful means.

"Only the Tathāgata attains parinirvāṇa and accomplishes inconceivable merits. Therefore arhats and pratyekabuddhas accomplish conceivable merits. To say they attain nirvāṇa is the Buddha's skillful means.

"Only the Tathāgata attains parinirvāṇa, having severed and extinguished all faults that should be severed, accomplishing the foremost purity. Arhats and pratyekabuddhas have remaining faults and are not of the foremost purity. To say they attain nirvāṇa is the Buddha's skillful means.

"Only the Tathāgata attains parinirvāṇa, gazed upon by all sentient beings, surpassing the realm of arhats, pratyekabuddhas, and bodhisattvas. Therefore arhats and pratyekabuddhas are far from the realm of nirvāṇa.

"To say that arhats and pratyekabuddhas contemplate liberation and possess the four wisdoms of ultimate attainment and obtain a place of rest: this too is the Tathāgata's expedient teaching with a remainder, an incomplete teaching. Why? Because there are two kinds of death. What are the two? They are segmented death (*duànduàn sǐ*, 分段死) and inconceivable transmutation death (*bùsīyì biànyì sǐ*, 不思議變易死). Segmented death pertains to deluded sentient beings. Inconceivable transmutation death pertains to arhats, pratyekabuddhas, and bodhisattvas of great power who have mind-made bodies, extending up to the ultimate unsurpassed bodhi.

"Of the two kinds of death, regarding segmented death, it is said that the arhat and pratyekabuddha have the wisdom that 'my births are ended.' Because they have attained the fruit with a remainder, it is said that 'the holy life is established.' Because what ordinary people and the seven classes of trainees had not previously accomplished, namely the severance of deluded afflictions, it is said that 'what was to be done has been done.' Because the afflictions that arhats and pratyekabuddhas sever can no longer receive future existence, it is said that 'I shall not receive future existence.' This does not mean they have exhausted all afflictions, nor that they have exhausted all births. Therefore to say 'I shall not receive future existence' is because there are afflictions that arhats and pratyekabuddhas cannot sever.

"Afflictions are of two kinds. What are the two? They are abode afflictions (*zhùdì fánnǎo*, 住地煩惱) and active afflictions (*qǐ fánnǎo*, 起煩惱). The abodes are of four kinds. What are the four? They are: the abode of views in one place, the abode of desire-love, the abode of form-love, and the abode of existence-love. These four abodes give rise to all active afflictions. Active afflictions are momentary mind, momentarily corresponding.

"World-Honored One, the mind-unassociated beginningless ignorance abode (*wúshǐ wúmíng zhùdì*, 無始無明住地): World-Honored One, the power of these four abodes is such that all higher afflictions depend on them as seeds, yet compared to the ignorance abode, they cannot be reckoned by calculation or analogy. World-Honored One, this ignorance abode's power, among the abodes counted as existence-love and the four abodes, is the most powerful. It is like the evil Māra Pāpīyān, who in the Paranirmitavaśavartin Heaven surpasses all in form, power, lifespan, retinue, and sovereignty. So too the ignorance abode's power, among the abodes counted as existence-love and the four abodes, is the most victorious. It is the basis for afflictions equal to the sands of the Ganges in number, and it also causes the four kinds of affliction to abide for a long time.

"The wisdom of arhats and pratyekabuddhas cannot sever it. Only the bodhi-wisdom of the Tathāgata can sever it. Thus, World-Honored One, the ignorance abode is the most powerful. World-Honored One, furthermore, just as conditioned by grasping, defiled karma produces the three realms of existence, so too conditioned by the ignorance abode, undefiled karma produces the three kinds of mind-made bodies of arhats, pratyekabuddhas, and bodhisattvas of great power. These three grounds and the three kinds of mind-made body, as well as undefiled karma, all depend on the ignorance abode as their condition, not being without condition. Therefore the three kinds of mind-made body and undefiled karma are conditioned by the ignorance abode.

"World-Honored One, thus the four abodes counted as existence-love are not the same as the ignorance-abode karma. The ignorance abode is separate from the four abodes and is severed at the buddha-ground, severed by the bodhi-wisdom of the Buddha. Why? Because arhats and pratyekabuddhas sever the four abodes, but the undefiled is not exhausted. Being unable to attain sovereign power, they also do not realize it.

"The undefiled that is not exhausted is precisely the ignorance abode. World-Honored One, arhats, pratyekabuddhas, and bodhisattvas of the final body are covered and obstructed by the ignorance abode. Therefore, regarding this and that dharma, they do not know and do not perceive. Because they do not know and see, what should be severed is not severed and is not ultimate. Because it is not severed, they are called 'having remaining faults.' Their liberation is not liberation from all faults. Their purity is not complete purity. Their merit is not complete merit. Because they have accomplished liberation with a remainder, purity with a remainder, and merit with a remainder, they know the suffering with a remainder, sever the accumulation with a remainder, realize the cessation with a remainder, and cultivate the path with a remainder. This is called attaining a partial nirvāṇa. Those who attain a partial nirvāṇa are called 'those who face toward the realm of nirvāṇa.'"
