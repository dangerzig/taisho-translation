# 瑜伽集要焰口施食起教阿難陀緣由
## Yoga Compendium: The Story of Ānanda and the Origin of the Teaching on Giving Food to the Flaming-Mouth Hungry Ghost

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1319

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

At that time the World-Honored One was in the city of Kapilavastu, at the Nyagrodha Saṃghārāma, together with many bhikṣus and bodhisattvas, an innumerable assembly surrounding him on all sides, as he taught the Dharma.

At that time Ānanda was dwelling alone in a quiet place, contemplating the teachings he had received. During that very night, after the third watch, he saw a hungry ghost named Flaming-Mouth. Its form was ugly and repulsive, its body withered and emaciated, fire blazing from its mouth, its throat like the point of a needle, its hair tangled and disheveled, its fangs and claws long and sharp, extremely frightening. Standing before Ānanda, it said to him: "In three days your life will come to an end, and you will be born among the hungry ghosts."

When Ānanda heard these words, his heart was filled with terror and dread. He asked the hungry ghost: "Great being! If after my death I am to be born as a hungry ghost, what skillful means can I now employ to be freed from this suffering?" Then the hungry ghost said to Ānanda: "If tomorrow morning you are able to give food and drink to hungry ghosts as numerous as hundreds of thousands of nayutas of Ganges-sand numbers, and also to innumerable brahmins and sages, the officials of Yama's bureau of karmic retribution, the administrators of the paths of the underworld, as well as all manner of ghosts and spirits, those who have died long ago and in the distant past, and all their food and drink according to the measure of Magadha, giving each one seven times seven hu of food and drink, and furthermore make offerings to the Three Treasures on our behalf, then your lifespan will be increased, and we will be freed from the suffering of the hungry ghosts and reborn in the heavens."

When Ānanda saw the Flaming-Mouth hungry ghost, emaciated, withered, scorched, and extremely ugly, with fire blazing from its mouth, throat like a needle, hair tangled and disheveled, nails and claws long and sharp, and heard these words, he was greatly terrified, every hair on his body standing on end.

At dawn he rose from his seat and went to where the Buddha was. He circumambulated three times to the right, bowed at the Buddha's feet, and with his body trembling, addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One of great compassion! I beg you to save me from this suffering. Why? Last night during the third watch, while walking in meditation at a quiet place and contemplating the teachings I had received, I saw the Flaming-Mouth ghost, who said to me: 'In three days your life will certainly end, and you will be born among the hungry ghosts.' I asked the ghost: 'How can I be freed from this suffering?' The hungry ghost answered: 'If you are able to give food and drink equally and universally to hungry ghosts as numerous as hundreds of thousands of nayutas of Ganges-sand numbers, to brahmins and sages, to the officials of Yama's bureau of karmic retribution, the administrators of the paths of the underworld, and to all ghosts, spirits, attendants, retainers, and those who have died long ago and in the distant past, then your lifespan will be increased.'" He then addressed the World-Honored One: "How can I possibly prepare enough food and drink to satisfy them all?"

The Buddha addressed Ānanda: "Do not be afraid. I recall that in a past life, immeasurable kalpas ago, when I was a brahmin, I received from Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva Mahāsattva a dhāraṇī called the Dharma of the Dhāraṇī of the Tathāgata of Immeasurable Majestic Virtue, Sovereign Radiance."

The Buddha addressed Ānanda: "If you are able to properly perform this dhāraṇī method, empowering it seven times, you can cause a single offering of food to transform into all manner of sweet-dew food and drink, sufficient to satisfy all hungry ghosts, brahmins, sages, and various ghosts and spirits as numerous as hundreds of thousands of koṭi-nayutas of Ganges-sand numbers with supreme and wondrous food and drink. All will be fully satisfied. Each and every one of these beings will receive food in the amount used in Magadha as its measure. This food and water will be equal in quantity to the Dharma-realm, inexhaustible to eat. All will attain the fruit of sagehood and be liberated from their suffering bodies."

The Buddha addressed Ānanda: "You should now receive and uphold this dhāraṇī method. It will cause your merit, virtue, and lifespan to increase. The hungry ghosts will be reborn in the heavens or in a pure land and receive human or heavenly bodies. It can cause the donor to transform obstacles, eliminate calamities, extend their years, and increase their lifespan. In this present life they will attract supreme merit, and in the future they will realize awakening. With a vast mind, universally for all sentient beings, for the parents of many lifetimes throughout accumulated kalpas, for the officials of the constellations in heaven, for the dark bureaus and earth courts below, for the realms of Yama and the ghosts, for tiny insects and all that creeps and moves, for all beings with consciousness, set out an unrestricted and vast offering. May all come to this assembly. By the Buddha's majestic radiance, may their bodies and fields be washed clean, and may they obtain this supreme benefit and enjoy human and heavenly happiness. May all buddhas, prajñā, bodhisattvas, vajra-devas, and all the immeasurable sages of the karmic paths, with their unconditional compassion, bear witness to what I practice. Therefore, we wish to fulfill our great vows; we wish to broadly protect and save all sentient beings without loss or regression; we wish to crush all karmic obstacles and make them pure; we wish to diligently seek the unsurpassed path and swiftly accomplish it; we wish to rescue beings in the evil destinies and have them forever cast off the sea of suffering and reach the other shore. As the sūtras describe, in the boundless worlds and the six destinies and four modes of birth, those among them who serve as lords, leaders, and chiefs are all bodhisattvas abiding in inconceivable liberation, who through their compassionate vows have divided their forms and cast their shadows to manifest transformation-bodies. In the six destinies they share in the suffering of their kind, and by establishing skillful means they are not destroyed by afflictions or secondary afflictions. They distinguish the various kinds of karma and cause beings to arouse the aspiration for the Way. They constantly reproach themselves and repent of the deeds their bodies have committed. Taming and teaching all sentient beings, they serve as great guides, crushing the three evil paths, purifying the karmic paths, severing the stream of craving, never abandoning their vows and practices. In the sea of suffering they serve as wise friends, bringing to maturity, benefiting, and bringing joy to all sentient beings, leading them to realize great nirvāṇa.

"If any donor deeply believes in the Mahāyāna and thirsts with longing for yoga, wishing to see and hear the sweet-dew Dharma-gate of the dhāraṇī treasury, and for the sake of all sentient beings arouses a mind of rescue and relief, earnestly offers praise, gives great treasures, and three times requests the teacher before the altar-rite is permitted: they should be equally impartial, free from thoughts of enmity and hatred, constantly practice giving without regret, draw near to good friends, be brave and diligent without timidity, earnestly seek the great path, praise the Three Treasures, nurture living beings, save them by skillful means, and enable them all to attain liberation. They should not sustain their lives through evil pursuits, but constantly benefit themselves and others. Such a good man is a true good friend, practicing the bodhisattva path. Universally, for all in the three evil paths and evil destinies, for all hungry ghosts, King Yama, brahmins and sages, devas of the sky, Śakra, Brahmā, the four kings, the officials of the constellations, the eight classes including nāgas and spirits, the sun and moon, Mount Sumeru, asuras and non-Buddhist practitioners, the demon hosts of the six desire realms, the elements of water, fire, wind, and space, the spirits of mountains, forests, caves, dwellings, palaces, and monasteries, the spirits roaming the great earth, rivers, flowing springs, bathing pools, shrines, and places auspicious and inauspicious, the spirits who record good and evil with unobstructed supernatural powers, the feathered creatures flying in the sky, the fish and scaled creatures swimming in the water, the furred and horned animals, all insects that creep and move, all beings with consciousness, the wandering souls exposed in the wilderness, those beaten and tormented in bitter affliction, those bound by enmity and hatred through many lives not yet freed, wronged souls through accumulated kalpas who were robbed of wealth or life, deceased monks and nuns who have not yet attained the fruit, parents, relatives, and kin of many lifetimes: by the Tathāgata's teaching may they be brought out of the three evil paths. In the immeasurable hells may they arouse the mind of enlightenment. May each be willing to release and free themselves from the bonds of enmity, praising and being mindful of one another as they would of their own parents, and may they come to this place of practice to bear witness, guard, and be mindful. With hearts leaping for joy, as rare to encounter as the udumbara flower, knowing that this has come about through their own past deeds, dwelling in the human world where the dispositions of consciousness are hard to settle, mostly following delusion and accumulating the source of suffering, not yet having attained the fruit of sagehood and therefore still giving rise to faults. Moreover, relying on the king's land and water and dwelling in the Buddha's compassionate light, always thinking of past conditions and still bearing the fruit of the present, reproaching themselves day and night: 'How have I been repaid thus?' Perhaps for the sake of relatives, kin, parents, how many times have they changed sides and clung in delusion, changing form and appearance so that who could recognize them? May they today, by the Buddha's power, fly through the distant sky to arrive at this place of practice. May the compassionate light brush their bodies, each according to their form and kind. May they repent and wash away the dust of their faults, arouse the mind of enlightenment, and receive this offering."

The Buddha addressed Ānanda: "If one wishes to receive and uphold the method of giving food, one must rely upon the profound samādhi and ācārya method of yoga. Those who wish to cultivate this practice should study under a yoga ācārya, arouse the mind of unsurpassed great enlightenment, receive the samaya precepts, enter the great maṇḍala, and receive consecration. Only then may they receive this teaching. Having received the five-wisdom consecration of the great Mahāvairocana Tathāgata and succeeded to the position of ācārya, only then may they transmit this teaching. If this is not done, the transmission is not permitted from one person to another. Even if one were to practice it, one would invite calamity upon oneself, commit the crime of stealing the Dharma, and achieve no efficacy whatsoever.

"If one has received consecration and, following the teacher's instruction, cultivates the yoga practice, deportment, and ritual procedures, and is well able to distinguish and thoroughly comprehend the characteristics of dharmas, such a person is called a Tripiṭaka Ācārya, and only then may they transmit this teaching.

"If one wishes to perform the rite, one must first protect oneself, and the disciples likewise. Having determined the date, select a clean site: a fine and spacious hall, a quiet garden or grove, a place beloved by ghosts and spirits, a flowing spring, bathing pool, river, sea, mountain, or marsh, a place of merit, or a hall or building will also do. Plaster the ground properly with fragrant water-paste, making it square or round, large or small, according to the donor's means. At the four corners erect banner-poles and adorn them properly with five-colored silks. Mount flaming jewels, and within the jewels install the Buddhauṣṇīṣa, the Great Compassion, the Wish-Fulfilling, and the Uṣṇīṣavijayā dhāraṇīs: Buddhauṣṇīṣa in the northeast, Great Compassion in the southeast, Wish-Fulfilling in the southwest, Uṣṇīṣavijayā in the northwest. Adorn the four pillars properly with exceptional beauty. These are called Auspicious Victory Banners. Within a hundred yojanas they eliminate all adversity and misfortune, and thus the boundary is established. When the wind blows and their shadows sweep, when earth is scattered and water sprinkled, karmic obstructions are destroyed and great merit is obtained. All who see with their eyes or hear with their ears will universally receive benefit.

"Next, hang silk banners and canopies all around. Jeweled fans and white whisks are arranged in their proper positions. Argha water and fragrant water, wondrous flowers, lamps, scented paste, food, drink, medicines, various fruits and delicacies, and all other items should be purified by the Dharma and not allowed to become impure. When the adornment is complete, hold a censer and circumambulate the place of practice to the right, surveying everything. Where things are not fully arranged, attend to them again.

"When the adornment is finished, together with the disciples bathe in fragrant water and put on new, clean garments. Go outside to the central courtyard and sweep it properly. Plaster the ground with fragrant paste and adorn it properly. This is called the Samaya Altar. Outside the place of practice, spread clean mats and arrange them with proper deportment. Make three prostrations, kneel facing east, hold a censer, and perform the invitation rite."
