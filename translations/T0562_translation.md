# 佛說無垢賢女經
## Sūtra of the Worthy Girl Vimalā, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0562

Translated from the Chinese. Western Jin dynasty, translated by Dharmarakṣa (竺法護)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in Rājagṛha, on Mount Gṛdhrakūṭa, together with bodhisattvas, great disciples, learned men and women, devas, humans, asuras, spirits, and nāgas in countless numbers, gathered for a Dharma assembly. At that time the Buddha was teaching the sūtra.

In the assembly there was an elder brahmin named Sudānta, whose wife was named Vairūyā. Together with nine hundred and seventy-five billion women, they sat with joined palms listening to the sūtra. At that time Vairūyā was pregnant, and the girl within the womb, her body fully formed, was also sitting in the womb with joined palms listening to the sūtra.

The worthy Anuruddha, through the power of his meritorious divine sight, saw this girl within the womb sitting with joined palms listening to the sūtra. He thought to himself: "I imagine that among those gathered here, what their eyes can see is limited. They are unable to probe hidden matters as I can." He congratulated himself, his countenance bright with delight.

The Buddha said to Anuruddha: "What have you seen that makes your expression so changed and delighted?"

Anuruddha said: "Through my divine sight, I saw the girl in the womb sitting with joined palms listening to the sūtra. That is why I am pleased and rejoicing."

The Buddha said: "Excellent, excellent! It is as you say. But it is like comparing the light of the many stars to the brilliance of the sun and moon: can they be equal? Among the śrāvakas, your sight is foremost, with none to surpass you. But what the Tathāgata observes, seeing equally in the ten directions, includes every flying bird and running beast and every creature underground that bears young. The offspring within those wombs are all, like you, equally listening to the sūtra."

At that time Anuruddha and all in the assembly harbored doubts. The Buddha then emitted radiance that illuminated everywhere without limit, above and below and in the eight directions, without obstruction. He caused the beings and things in countless lands to be visible, as if looking into a mirror where inside and outside can both be seen.

Anuruddha and the others looked up into the sky and saw flying birds hovering with folded wings, pausing in flight to listen to the Buddha's teaching. Within eggs not yet hatched, the unborn within the birds' wombs were also spreading their wings to listen to the sūtra. Looking down, they saw running beasts and four-footed creatures that had stopped eating grass and drinking water, standing upright to listen to the sūtra. The offspring they carried in their wombs were also within the womb, bending their front legs, listening with one-pointed mind. Worms, snakes, earthworms, and all creatures born from the earth had stilled their bodies and did not move, listening to the sūtra with focused attention. Among them, those bearing unborn offspring had their young within the womb, raising their heads and coiling their bodies, listening with one-pointed mind.

Then Anuruddha, supported by the Buddha's supernatural power, asked the girl within the womb in the eight kinds of voice: "The offspring of birds in eggs, creatures in wombs, and worms: for what reason do they join their palms, spread their wings, bend their legs, and coil their bodies to listen to the sūtra with one-pointed mind?"

At that time the girl and all the beings within their respective wombs answered Anuruddha: "Because all living beings are confused about the five places and do not recognize the true path, for this reason we listen to the sūtra. Because all beings are given to lust, anger, and ignorance, and birth and death do not cease, for this reason we listen to the sūtra. Because all people do not show filial devotion to their parents and do not make offerings to the Buddha and the Saṃgha of bhikṣus, for this reason we listen to the sūtra."

At that time Anuruddha, hearing what was spoken, knelt and said to the Buddha: "I acknowledge the World-Honored One's merit and supernatural power, penetrating and illuminating all things. I would rather have my body sink into Naraka and endure the many torments through countless kalpas than take arhatship. Why? Those still in the womb who have not yet seen the light of day already generate the great resolve to save all beings. Yet I, with this body, out of fear of death, am bound by perception and consciousness, like a dead person who is of no benefit to the living."

Then the girl was born, emerging from her mother's right side. The three-thousandfold world shook mightily. Countless devas halted in the sky and rained down heavenly flowers and played celestial music. A spontaneous thousand-petaled lotus appeared, as large as a cartwheel, with a stem like blue lapis lazuli, and the girl sat upon it.

At that time the devas and humans, the flying birds and running beasts, and the worms, snakes, and earthworms that were pregnant all gave birth as well. It was like when a king goes on a military campaign and all his officials, great and small, follow along.

Thereupon Śakra took heavenly garments and descended from above to give them to the girl, saying: "Being unclothed is unseemly. Take these garments and wear them."

The girl replied: "Those who have not yet been freed from desire and nirvāṇa: we shall not accept anything from them. You are an arhat; I aspire to be a bodhisattva. You are not of my kind; our aspirations are not the same."

Śakra then said: "I see that your female body is unclothed and unseemly. That is why I brought garments to give you."

The girl replied: "In the Dharma of the Great Vehicle, there is neither male nor female. I shall have garments come to me of their own accord."

The Buddha said to Śakra: "This is not about adorning a female body. One who generates the bodhisattva mind will of themselves attain the excellent marks. What is revealed without limit: this is what adorns the bodhisattva's body."

Then Śāriputra, deeply astonished by the girl's transformations, came forward and said to the Buddha: "From what land has this girl come to this place, and who will bring her garments?"

The Buddha said: "This girl comes from the southeast, from the land of the Buddha Vairūyā Dharmābhyāsa, in a realm called Jambūnada, one hundred thousand buddha-lands from here. Having come from her homeland to see the Buddha, garments will come to her of their own accord from her homeland." The garments then came of themselves through the sky with a rustling sound. A voice from the sky spoke to the girl: "Put on these garments, and you shall attain the five supernatural powers." (In the girl's homeland, all had attained the five supernatural powers.)

The girl put on the garments and descended from the lotus to come before the Buddha. With one step of her foot, heaven and earth shook six times. All the women generated the unsurpassed mind of equal liberation. The flying birds, creatures, and beasts all transformed their bodies and became human, clothed in heavenly garments and adorned with pearl necklaces and ornaments.

The girl touched her head to the Buddha's feet, three times said, "Homage to the Samyaksaṃbuddha," and knelt before the Buddha: "I wish that for all beings gathered here, you would broadly teach the sūtra and the Dharma, so that they may attain their aspirations." The Buddha, following her wish, taught the sūtra.

At that time the girl and the nine hundred and seventy-five billion women, hearing what the Buddha taught, leaped with joy. They no longer changed their bodies but stood before the Buddha and transformed into men. Each removed their pearl necklaces and jewels and scattered them upon the Buddha. Through the Buddha's supernatural power, what they scattered spontaneously transformed into a jeweled lattice canopy. Within it was a seven-jeweled lion throne, upon which a seated Buddha raised his hand and praised them. At that very moment, all attained the stage of non-retrogression. The beasts, creatures, worms, and snakes that had become human also removed their pearl necklaces and jeweled ornaments and scattered them upon the Buddha. The seated Buddha within the canopy caused their scatterings to form a jeweled canopy as well, equal in every way to the first, and gave teachings to them. All attained the seventh stage.

The Buddha said to the Bodhisattva Vimalā: "You, from within the womb, served as a guide for all beings. The Tathāgata, the Perfectly Awakened One, likewise serves as a guide for all beings in the five destinies."

When the Buddha had finished speaking this sūtra, the entire assembly was filled with great joy. They bowed and withdrew.
