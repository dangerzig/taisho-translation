# 尊上經
## The Sūtra of the Exalted (Bhaddekaratta)

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0077

Translated from the Chinese. Western Jin dynasty, translated by Dharmarakṣa

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Bhagavān was in Śrāvastī, in Jeta's Grove, in the park of Anāthapiṇḍada. At that time, the Venerable Lomasakaṅgiya was among the Śākyas, in a forest cave. At that time, the Venerable Lomasakaṅgiya rose at dawn and, having left the cave, spread a rope seat on the open ground, laid out his sitting cloth, and sat cross-legged. Then a certain deity of supremely beautiful form, when the night was over, came to the Venerable Lomasakaṅgiya. Having arrived, the deity bowed at the feet of the Venerable Lomasakaṅgiya and stood to one side. By the light of that deity, the entire cave was illuminated with a wondrous radiance. Standing to one side, the deity addressed the Venerable Lomasakaṅgiya: "Bhikṣu, bhikṣu! Do you retain the Verses of the Auspicious and understand their meaning?"

When this was said, the Venerable Lomasakaṅgiya replied to the deity: "Deity, I do not retain the Verses of the Auspicious or understand their meaning. Deity, do you retain these Verses of the Auspicious and understand their meaning?"

When this was said, the deity replied to the Venerable Lomasakaṅgiya: "Bhikṣu, I retain the Verses of the Auspicious but do not understand their meaning."

"How is it, deity, that you retain the Verses of the Auspicious but do not understand their meaning?"

"Bhikṣu, at one time the World-Honored One was in Rājagṛha, in the Kalandaka Bamboo Grove. There he spoke the Verses of the Auspicious for the bhikṣus:

> 'Do not recall the past;
> Have no longing for the future.
> The past has already perished and ceased;
> The future cannot be obtained.
> As for the present dharmas,
> One should contemplate them, each and every one.
> What is grasped is not firmly established;
> The wise person can awaken to this.
> Having attained this, one can progress.
> What wise person would grieve over the ending of life?
> My mind does not depart from this;
> The great multitude cannot dislodge [me].
> Thus firmly and steadfastly abiding,
> Day and night without abandoning this.
> Therefore, the Verses of the Auspicious:
> A person should contemplate them in this way.'

"Thus, bhikṣu, I retain the Verses of the Auspicious but do not understand their meaning."

"Then, deity, who retains the Verses of the Auspicious together with their meaning?"

"Bhikṣu, the World-Honored One is in Śrāvastī, in Jeta's Grove, in the park of Anāthapiṇḍada. He retains these Verses of the Auspicious together with their meaning. Therefore, bhikṣu, you should receive from the World-Honored One the Verses of the Auspicious and their meaning, reflect upon them well, recite and retain them. Why is this? Because, bhikṣu, these Verses of the Auspicious and their meaning accord with the meaning and the Dharma, lead to the practice of the holy life, bring the attainment of spiritual powers, lead to the noble path, and correspond with nirvāṇa. Any son of good family who joyfully aspires to study the Way, who joyfully aspires to leave the household life and study the Way, should retain these Verses of the Auspicious and their meaning, reflect upon them well, and reverently uphold them." After the deity had spoken, it bowed at the feet of the Venerable Lomasakaṅgiya, circumambulated the Venerable Lomasakaṅgiya, and then vanished from that very spot.

The Venerable Lomasakaṅgiya, not long after the deity had departed, observed the rains retreat among the Śākyas. After the three months of the rains retreat were completed, having made and completed his robes, he set out with his robe and bowl toward Śrāvastī. Traveling by stages, he arrived at Śrāvastī and stayed in Jeta's Grove, in the park of Anāthapiṇḍada. The Venerable Lomasakaṅgiya then went to the World-Honored One. Having arrived, he bowed at the World-Honored One's feet and sat to one side. Seated to one side, the Venerable Lomasakaṅgiya addressed the World-Honored One: "World-Honored One, at one time I was in a quiet cave among the Śākyas. World-Honored One, at dawn I rose, left the cave, spread a rope seat on the open ground, laid out my sitting cloth, and sat cross-legged. At that time, a deity of supremely beautiful form, when the night was over, came to my presence. Having arrived, the deity bowed at my feet and stood to one side. By the light of that deity, the entire cave was illuminated with a wondrous radiance. Standing to one side, the deity said to me: 'Bhikṣu, bhikṣu! Do you retain the Verses of the Auspicious and their meaning?' When this was said, I replied to the deity: 'Deity, I do not retain the Verses of the Auspicious or their meaning. Deity, do you retain the Verses of the Auspicious and their meaning?' When this was said, the deity replied to me: 'Bhikṣu, I retain the Verses of the Auspicious but do not understand their meaning.' 'How is it, deity, that you retain the Verses of the Auspicious but do not understand their meaning?' 'Bhikṣu, at one time the World-Honored One was in Rājagṛha, in the Kalandaka Bamboo Grove. There he spoke the Verses of the Auspicious for the bhikṣus, but I do not understand their meaning. He spoke these verses. Thus, bhikṣu, I retain the Verses of the Auspicious but do not understand their meaning.' 'Then, deity, who retains the Verses of the Auspicious together with their meaning?' 'Bhikṣu, the World-Honored One is in Śrāvastī, in Jeta's Grove, in the park of Anāthapiṇḍada. He retains the Verses of the Auspicious and their meaning. Therefore, bhikṣu, you should receive from the World-Honored One the Verses of the Auspicious and their meaning, reflect upon them, and reverently practice them. Why is this? Because, bhikṣu, these Verses of the Auspicious and their meaning accord with the meaning, lead to the practice of the holy life, bring the attainment of spiritual powers, lead to the path of equality, and correspond with nirvāṇa. Any son of good family who joyfully aspires to study the Way, who joyfully aspires to leave the household life and study the Way, should retain these Verses of the Auspicious and their meaning, reflect upon them well, and reverently uphold them.' After the deity had spoken, it bowed at my feet, circumambulated me, and then vanished from that very spot."

"Lomasakaṅgiya, do you know the name of that deity?"

"World-Honored One, I do not know the name of that deity."

"Lomasakaṅgiya, that was the devaputra named Praṇamadana, a great general of the Heaven of the Thirty-Three."

"Now is the time, World-Honored One! Now is the time, Well-Gone One! May the World-Honored One speak the Verses of the Auspicious and their meaning for the bhikṣus. Having heard them from the World-Honored One, the bhikṣus shall reverently uphold them."

"Therefore, Lomasakaṅgiya, listen well and reflect carefully. I shall now explain."

"Just so, World-Honored One." The Venerable Lomasakaṅgiya received the World-Honored One's teaching.

The World-Honored One then spoke this explanation of the verses: "How, Lomasakaṅgiya, does a bhikṣu recall the past? Lomasakaṅgiya, when a bhikṣu takes delight in past form, becomes attached, and dwells therein; when past feeling, perception, formations, and past consciousness are delighted in, clung to, and dwelt in: thus, Lomasakaṅgiya, does a bhikṣu recall the past.

"How, Lomasakaṅgiya, does a bhikṣu not recall the past? Lomasakaṅgiya, when a bhikṣu does not delight in past form, does not become attached, and does not dwell therein; when past feeling, perception, formations, and consciousness are not delighted in, not clung to, and not dwelt in: thus, Lomasakaṅgiya, does a bhikṣu not recall the past.

"How, Lomasakaṅgiya, does a bhikṣu long for the future? Lomasakaṅgiya, when a bhikṣu takes delight in future form, becomes attached, and dwells therein; when future feeling, perception, formations, and consciousness are delighted in, clung to, and dwelt in: thus, Lomasakaṅgiya, does a bhikṣu long for the future.

"How, Lomasakaṅgiya, does a bhikṣu not long for the future? Lomasakaṅgiya, when a bhikṣu does not delight in future form, does not become attached, and does not dwell therein; when future feeling, perception, formations, and consciousness are not delighted in, not clung to, and not dwelt in: thus, Lomasakaṅgiya, does a bhikṣu not long for the future.

"How, Lomasakaṅgiya, does a bhikṣu contemplate present dharmas? Lomasakaṅgiya, when a bhikṣu delights in present form, clings to it, and dwells therein; when present feeling, perception, formations, and consciousness are delighted in, clung to, and dwelt in: thus, Lomasakaṅgiya, does a bhikṣu contemplate present dharmas.

"How, Lomasakaṅgiya, does a bhikṣu not contemplate present dharmas? Lomasakaṅgiya, when a bhikṣu does not delight in present form, does not become attached, and does not dwell therein; when feeling, perception, formations, and consciousness are not delighted in, not clung to, and not dwelt in: thus does a bhikṣu not contemplate present dharmas."

Thus spoke the Buddha. The Venerable Lomasakaṅgiya, having heard what the World-Honored One said, rejoiced and was delighted.
