# 摩醯首羅大自在天王神通化生伎藝天女念誦法
## Recitation Method of the Arts-Celestial Maiden Supernaturally Born from Maheśvara, the Great Sovereign Deva-King

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1280

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown.

---

At that time, Maheśvara Deva-King was in the Great Sovereign Heaven, surrounded before and behind by various celestial maidens, sporting with supernatural powers and performing divine music. Suddenly, from his hairline there appeared a celestial maiden, surpassing in beauty and foremost in the arts, whom none among all the devas could equal. In the midst of the great assembly she spoke these words: "I now wish to benefit all beings. Whatever they pray for, whether prosperity, auspiciousness, wealth, or happiness, I can fulfill according to their heart's desires. I can also swiftly bring accomplishment in all arts and skills. I possess a secret dhāraṇī, an essential method, which I shall now teach." She then spoke the dhāraṇī:

*Namo ujjhiśa-mahā-viṣa-śikha-vipra-pratīya śikara cāruṃ tadyathā śvajatī vairamā-jarīnī hūṃ phaṭ svāhā.*

At that time, the arts-celestial maiden, having spoken this dhāraṇī, addressed the celestial assembly: "If one wishes to recite and uphold this dhāraṇī of mine, one must first establish a ritual site, adorned according to the proper method, with various fragrant flowers as offerings. For fourteen days or seven days, observe the precepts and fasting, abstain from sexual activity, and recite this dhāraṇī a full 10,000 or 100,000 times. During this period, do not consume wine, meat, or pungent foods. Recite with utmost sincerity, and the celestial maiden will appear in person to bestow various blessings; all wishes and prayers will be completely fulfilled. From that point forward, there will be no obstructions. Even if one has a spouse or consumes pungent foods, these need not be restricted. However, if one can maintain purity, the verification will be swiftest. Afterward, whatever application is needed, accomplishment will follow.

"When performing the recitation, one must first establish boundaries. Empower water or ash seven times, sprinkle it in the four directions, and wherever one wishes, near or far, the boundary is established. All ghosts and spirits will be unable to approach. If one recites 1,000 times, devas, nāgas, humans, and non-humans who see the practitioner will become joyful. If one recites 2,000 times, all ghosts and spirits will guard and protect. If one recites 3,000 times, the essences of herbs and medicinal plants will all appear before the practitioner, and the spirits of flower and fruit groves will joyfully attend. If one recites 4,000 times, all vetāla ghosts will generate deep reverence. If one recites 5,000 times, all nāga spirits and their retinues, male and female, will rejoice with love and admiration without wearying. If one recites 6,000 times, all yakṣa retinues, male and female, will joyfully follow and obey, serving at one's command. If one recites 7,000 times, all deva-kings, the mātaras, and their retinues will attend and serve, performing various tasks without tiring. If one recites 8,000 times, all vidyādharas and five-supernatural-power sages will generate deep joy and boundless admiration. If one recites a full 10,000 or up to 100,000 times, all devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, humans and non-humans, and even Skandha Deva and all ghosts and spirits will generate great joy, attending and guarding the practitioner, constantly granting the practitioner prosperity and auspiciousness.

"Another method: if one wishes to pray for rain and stop drought, it is not difficult. When there is drought, establish a boundary-altar according to the method, perform homa and recitation, and recite this mantra a full 10,000 times. The rain will immediately fall. If the rain is excessive and one wishes to stop it, empower clean ash 108 times and cast it into the sky. The rain will immediately stop.

"Another method: if an enemy harbors evil intent, write their name or draw their form with cinnabar or red earth. Tread upon it with the left foot and recite. Perform this after nightfall, 108 times. Regardless of distance, the enemy will come of their own accord seeking to resolve the enmity, their heart becoming harmonious, and they will instead generate love and respect.

"Another method: if one wishes to command and employ all ghosts and spirits, summon them together, generate a compassionate mind, empower food and drink 108 times according to one's means and scatter it as offerings. Thereafter command them, and they will swiftly accomplish whatever is needed, constantly joyful and never departing.

"Another method: if one wishes to summon all manner of people, regardless of distance, empower water seven times, sprinkle it in the four directions, face toward the person, call their name, and recite 108 times. Whether acquaintance or stranger, they will all come running, obeying one's will without daring to refuse.

"Another method: if an enemy comes intending harm, recite the vidyā with utmost sincerity 108 times. The enemy will immediately be struck, and their body will burn as if on fire. If they relent and submit, release them: recite the vidyā seven times saying 'Release,' and their body will immediately return to normal.

"Another method: if a husband and wife are unfaithful to each other and cannot bear to see one another, hating each other like fire and water, secretly write their names (without their knowledge) and tread upon them with the left foot as before. Call their names and recite 1,008 times. They will immediately become harmonious and love each other as if glued together, never again generating feelings of hatred or estrangement.

"Another method: if one desires a beloved woman, or even the celestial maidens, consorts, and queens of the retinue of Śakra Devendra, one should summon them by recitation according to the previous method, and they will come of their own accord, requesting to serve. If one does not reject them, they will never leave.

"Another method: if one wishes to summon yakṣa maidens to obtain their treasures, summon them at will; they will come swiftly. Whatever is needed, they will present it all, offering to become wives, concubines, or attendants.

"Another method: if one wishes to gain the love and respect of all noble persons, call their names and recite 1,008 times. They will come of their own accord to pay respects and serve.

"Another method: when entering a government office, recite the vidyā seven times with utmost sincerity before arriving, and from afar create auspicious conditions to make things go as desired. When meeting face to face, the official will be immeasurably pleased and will help with whatever is requested.

"Another method: if one desires a beloved woman or man, empower rouge or safflower juice, apply it to the soles of both feet, heat them over fire, cover with both hands, call the person's name and recite. That person will immediately come of their own accord. Even from a deeply hidden place they will come directly. They will not part from you even until the end of life. If a woman wishes to obtain a man, follow the same method, and the person will come as desired.

"Another method: if one wishes to see ghosts and spirits in bodily form, take charred firewood remaining from cremation in a charnel ground and perform homa. With each recitation, burn one piece, up to 108 times. All ghosts and spirits will manifest their forms, and one will see them.

"Another method: whatever treasures and wealth one desires, if one recites with utmost sincerity, they will naturally be obtained.

"Another method: if one wishes to bind a person and question them about matters, regardless of whether they are great or small, pure or impure, visualize the honored one and recite with utmost sincerity. The person will immediately be bound. Ask about past, present, and future affairs, and all will be revealed with nothing unknown. Not only can one bind a single person; even hundreds, thousands, or tens of thousands of people can be bound with utmost recitation, all responding instantly to one's thought.

"Another method: if a household member has fled to a very distant place, write their name as before, tread upon it and recite. That person will turn back and come running, arriving immediately. If they are slow to come, draw their form in ash, perform the method three times daily, and with each recitation of the mantra, strike it once, up to three times seven repetitions. The person will become terrified and come as swiftly as the wind.

"Another method: to cure all illnesses, take khadira wood and cut it into twenty-one pieces. With each recitation, cast one piece into the fire. When complete, the illness will be cured.

"Another method: for spirit-affliction, take asafoetida, benzoin, realgar, white mustard seeds, costus root, and chinaberry leaves. Pound and mix them together into forty-nine pills. With each recitation, cast one into the fire. The illness will be cured.

"Another method: for fox-enchantment affliction, take realgar, white mustard seeds, ligusticum, single-headed garlic, rhinoceros horn, ram's urine, white horse's hoof-scrapings, and donkey hoof-scrapings. Pound and sieve into pills, empower 108 times, burn them as fumigation into the nostrils, and apply to the body. The fox-enchantment will be destroyed and the illness cured.

"All the beneficial methods described above are supernaturally efficacious and swiftly accomplished. Whenever performing the method, one must be utterly sincere. If one is not sincere or treats it with contempt, the recitation will be fruitless and the method will not succeed. But if one is perfectly sincere, efficacious results will be obtained from a single recitation. I have here spoken only briefly; the remaining methods are inexhaustible. If one wishes to recite and seek verification, first paint my altar and paint my image and install it within the altar. Make offerings according to the method, and verification will come swiftly; accomplishment will come swiftly."

The method for painting the image of the Great Sovereign Celestial Maiden:

"First paint Maheśvara Deva-King with three faces and six arms, of extraordinary and surpassingly beautiful countenance, both lovely and fearsome. From his hairline there appears a celestial maiden, supremely wonderful and delightful, surpassed by none in heaven or the human world. She wears celestial garments, adorned with jeweled necklaces, with bangles and bracelets on both wrists. Her left hand is raised, presenting a celestial flower; her right hand reaches down as if grasping her skirt. Her body may be about three feet tall, or may be made larger or smaller as appropriate."

When the painting is completed, install it within the altar according to the method and make offerings.

The method for the prayer-altar:

"The altar should be four finger-spans high and one cubit in measurement, sized according to the available space. The altar has four gates. Inside the east gate, burn benzoin incense. Inside the south gate, burn agarwood incense. At the west gate, burn frankincense. At the north gate, burn tagara incense. At the center of the altar, install the image of the honored one. At the four corners, install one knife each, and suspend a bright mirror. At each of the four gates, install two arrows. On both sides of the south gate, establish positions for Vināyaka. Around the four edges of the altar, according to one's means, arrange sweet cakes, fruits, and flowers as offerings."

The method for the fire-altar:

"Construct a homa furnace twelve finger-spans deep, one cubit across. Beside the altar, make a likeness of the enemy's form. Cut it with a knife, perform homa and recitation, burning various toxic substances with soap-pod thorned firewood. With each recitation, burn one piece, up to forty-nine or 108 times. Great verification will immediately follow, and the enemy will be destroyed. For curing all diseases, follow the same method: make a likeness of the ghost or spirit's form, sever it, and burn it. Even a certainly fatal illness will be instantly cured."

The mudrā of the arts-celestial maiden born from the crown of the supernaturally sporting Maheśvara:

"Join both palms. Cross both ring fingers and middle fingers externally with the finger surfaces resting on the backs of the hands. Both index fingers slightly curl at the tips as if forming a jewel. Both thumbs are placed together and move back and forth. This completes the mudrā. The invocation and summoning vidyā:

*Namo maheśvarāya ujjhiśa-mauśikhāya ehi hi svāhā.*

"Having formed this mudrā, recite this vidyā seven times to invoke and summon the deva with all retinues to descend, and they will bestow various blessings.

"Next, form the supernatural all-summoning mudrā. With the right hand, extend the middle finger straight up. Place the ring finger behind the middle finger. Hook the index finger upon the ring fingertip. Press the thumb horizontally upon the index and little fingernails. Move the middle finger in a hooking gesture, pulling outward then toward the body. Recite the root vidyā, adding the name of the one to be summoned before the 'svāhā.'

"When this mudrā is formed and the vidyā recited, all summoned beings will immediately arrive. This supernatural summoning mudrā may be used universally in all situations.

"If one encounters an enemy or evil person intending harm, form the mudrā and extend the middle finger, pointing at them. They will naturally be scattered and destroyed. Evil ghosts and spirits, when pointed at, will be annihilated.

"Form this mudrā and recite the root vidyā. Stamp the heart, forehead, throat, and crown to form a body-protection. All ghosts and demons will not dare approach. This method must be personally received from a teacher and secretly upheld in a pure room. After each session of recitation, cover the celestial image with clean silk. One must never reveal this method to others. If recklessly divulged, it will be ineffective for life. Be cautious! Be cautious!

"The celestial image may be painted or sculpted. At the center of the altar, install the image of the honored one. To the left and right of the deva, install two celestial maidens each. If seats are arranged within the altar, their forms should be lovely, like celestial maidens. Behind the image, also place positions for two attendant celestial maidens. Only in the front, leave space for a fire-furnace to burn various incense and medicines as offerings to the honored one. If a fire-furnace cannot be installed, place a jeweled basin filled with fragrant water and various wonderful flowers as an alternative offering. At the four corners and four gates of the altar, if one cannot install actual knives and arrows, they may be drawn outside the altar. Move the mirrors inside to the four inner corners of the altar; where other ritual systems would place karma-vajras, place mirrors face up instead.

"When painting the altar, on the ground at all four sides, paint five-colored auspicious clouds, as if supporting the altar and surging up from the earth."
