# 佛頂尊勝陀羅尼念誦儀軌法

## The Buddhōṣṇīṣa Vijaya Dhāraṇī: Ritual Manual for Recitation

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0972

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

## Ritual Manual for the Recitation of the Buddhōṣṇīṣa Vijaya Dhāraṇī, in One Fascicle

The method of reciting dhāraṇīs requires that one has first seen the sacred assembly in the samaya maṇḍala, received abhiṣeka, and come to know one's principal deity. Having received the samaya from one's teacher, one should go to a secluded mountain place or a clean room. Paint an image of the principal deity, the Vijaya Dhāraṇī figure, and mount it on the eastern wall. The practitioner should sit facing it.

At the place of recitation, dig the ground to a depth of one and a half cubits. If there are tiles, gravel, bone, ash, hair, or any impurities in the soil, all must be removed. If none are found, refill with the original earth and tamp it flat. If there is earth left over, the site is auspicious. Mix cow dung with good earth and plaster the surface smooth. Then take cow dung mixed with water and recite the Aparājita dhāraṇī twenty-one times to empower the cow dung. The Aparājita mantra is:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ oṃ huru huru caṇḍāli mātaṅgi svāhā*

After empowering it, begin applying from the northeast corner and proceed rightward. Next, take mallow leaves or lotus leaves and wipe the surface clean and bright. Then, using white powder mixed with water, divide the surface into nine positions with cords. Grind white sandalwood on a stone and apply it to each of the nine positions.

The nine positions are as follows: in the center, place the position of Vairocana Buddha; to his right, the position of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva; behind Avalokiteśvara, the position of Maitreya Bodhisattva; behind the Vairocana position, the position of Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva; to the left of Ākāśagarbha, the position of Samantabhadra Bodhisattva; to the left of Vairocana, the position of Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva; below Vajrapāṇi, the position of Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva; in front of Vairocana, the position of Sarvanīvaraṇaviṣkambhin Bodhisattva; to the right of Sarvanīvaraṇaviṣkambhin, the position of Kṣitigarbha Bodhisattva. These are called the nine positions. All are anointed with white sandalwood, serving as the positions for welcoming and inviting the sacred assembly. Above, hang canopies and streamers. On all four sides of the practice hall, in the morning offer milk porridge. At the midday meal, offer yogurt rice, sweet rich foods, various beverages, and fruits. At the four gates place four incense burners. At the four corners place four pure water vases filled with fragrant water, with flowers or green leafy branches inserted as offerings. At the four corners light four ghee lamps. Before the practice hall, at the reciter's seat, place two bowls of argha fragrant water.

For the vessels containing offerings, use gold, silver, wrought copper, porcelain, or new earthenware, or conch cups, or fresh clean leaves. All others are unsuitable. Before filling the food vessels, first wash them clean, cover them, and fumigate the inside with incense smoke. After filling them with food, fumigate them again with incense. Sprinkle them with water empowered by the Aparājita dhāraṇī, and then arrange them rightward within the altar.

Before the altar, place a low bench half an inch above the ground, or a mat of kuśa grass, or spread some other clean material for the reciter to sit upon.

The reciter should bathe and purify the body. The bathing method is as described in the *Susiddhikara*. Alternatively, one may use the dharma bathing method: contemplate the true mark of dharmas as one's bath. Or, whether lay or monastic, guard and maintain one's discipline and precepts without violation, taking that as one's bath. Or, three times daily before the Buddha, prostrate, openly confess, rejoice in merit, entreat, make vows, and dedicate merit, taking that as one's bath. Or recite the purification mantra with empowerment seven times as one's purification. At the time of bathing and main recitation, contemplate: "All dharmas are originally pure in nature; I too have attained purity." Forming this intention, then recite the bathing mantra:

*Oṃ svabhāva-śuddha sarva-dharma svabhāva-śuddho 'haṃ*

Each day upon entering the practice hall for recitation, establish a fixed schedule: either two sessions (morning and dusk), or three sessions (adding noon), or four sessions, following the yoga method (adding midnight).

According to the teaching of the Vijaya Dhāraṇī Sūtra itself, on each full-moon day of the bright fortnight, one should recite to extinguish karmic obstructions and increase merit and extend life. The required number is one thousand recitations, through which one realizes the supramundane samādhi and attains the dhāraṇī of unforgetting.

On that day, fasting for the entire day and night is the superior practice. Or one may eat the "three white foods" (milk, yogurt, and non-glutinous rice, either as cooked rice or porridge) as the middle practice. Or one may eat a normal vegetarian meal as the lower practice. Wear clean garments, and the mind should be reverently sincere and pure in faith, without doubt.

When about to begin recitation, upon reaching the practice hall, first kneel on both knees and prostrate to Vairocana Buddha and the Eight Great Bodhisattvas. Openly confess, then pronounce the Five Great Vows: first, "Sentient beings are boundless; I vow to save them all"; second, "Merit and wisdom are boundless; I vow to accumulate them all"; third, "Dharma gates are boundless; I vow to learn them all"; fourth, "Tathāgatas are boundless; I vow to serve them all"; fifth, "Unsurpassed bodhi; I vow to attain it."

Sit in full lotus. Anoint the hands with incense and form the samaya mudrā. Recite the mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ asamī-tri-samī-samaye svāhā*

The mudrā: join both palms with the two thumbs upright. Stamp the mudrā on five places: forehead, right shoulder, left shoulder, heart, and throat, reciting the mantra once at each point, then scatter at the crown. Through the divine power of the samaya mudrā and mantra, one is able to purify the ground of the tathāgata, perfect the earth pāramitā, and accomplish both mundane and supramundane siddhi.

Next, form the dharmadhātu-born mudrā and recite the mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānaṃ dharma-dhātu-svabhāva-ko 'haṃ*

The mudrā: place both thumbs within the palms, each making a fist. Raise the two index fingers, placing them side by side. Place the mudrā upon the crown, then slowly lower and scatter downward. Recite the mantra three times. Then contemplate one's own body as equal to the dharmadhātu, free from all material characteristics, like empty space.

Next, form the Vajrasattva Dharma Wheel mudrā and recite the mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-vajrāṇāṃ oṃ vajrātma-ko 'haṃ*

The mudrā: cross both hands back to back. Place the left thumb in the palm of the right hand, touching it to the right thumb. Recite the mantra three times. One thereby attains a body like that of Vajrasattva.

Next, form the vajra armor mudrā. The mantra is:

*Namaḥ samanta-vajrāṇāṃ oṃ vajra-kavaca hūṃ*

The mudrā: join both palms in a hollow, with the index fingers each placed on the back of the middle finger, and both thumbs joined together resting on the middle knuckle of the middle fingers. Stamp the mudrā on the same five places as before, reciting the mantra once at each. Through this empowerment, one's body becomes clad in vajra armor, and no heavenly māra can approach.

Next, form the Acala mudrā. The mantra is:

*Namaḥ samanta-vajrāṇāṃ caṇḍa-mahā-roṣaṇa sphaṭaya hūṃ trāṭ hāṃ māṃ*

The mudrā: with each hand, press the thumb against the nails of the little finger and ring finger. Raise the middle and index fingers, holding them together. The left hand forms the sheath and the right hand the sword, inserting the sword into the sheath. Rotate the right sword-mudrā leftward to dispel all māras causing obstructions within the practice hall. Rotate rightward to establish the boundary in all eight directions and above and below.

Then visualize within the practice hall Mount Sumeru. Upon its summit, visualize a seven-jeweled pavilion. Within the pavilion, Vairocana Buddha is surrounded by a retinue of eight hundred million bodhisattva mahāsattvas who have fulfilled the tenth bhūmi. At the four gates and four corners, place four bodhisattvas each, along with eight offerings. Visualize and present both inner and outer offerings, each one clear and distinct.

Next, form the tathāgata-hook mudrā for inviting the sacred assembly. Recite the mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ aḥ sarva-tatra-pratihate tathāgatāṅkuśa-bodhicaryā-paripūraka svāhā*

The mudrā: interlace both hands inward and raise them. Extend the right index finger and bend it into a hook shape. Recite three times. Through the divine power of the mudrā and mantra, all buddha tathāgatas and the sacred assembly, not violating their original vow, all gather together.

Next, form the mudrā for offering seats. Recite the mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ aḥ*

The mudrā: join both palms in a hollow, with the little fingers and thumbs touching. Spread the remaining six fingers slightly bent, like an open lotus. Recite three times. Through the power of this mudrā and mantra, all manner of wondrous jeweled seats flow forth like a sea of clouds, offered to the tathāgata and the entire sacred assembly.

Next, offer argha water. Recite the mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānaṃ gagana-sama-sama svāhā*

Hold the vessel with both hands at forehead level, kneeling. Recite three times and offer the argha water to bathe the sacred assembly.

Next, form the mudrā for offering perfume. Recite the mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānaṃ viśuddha-gandhodbhavāya svāhā*

The mudrā: raise the right palm outward and grasp the right wrist with the left hand. Recite three times. Through the power of this mudrā and mantra, a sea of clouds of all perfumes flows forth, making offerings to all tathāgatas and the sacred assembly.

Next, form the mudrā for offering flower garlands. Recite the mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānaṃ mahā-maitrya-viyūna-gate svāhā*

The mudrā: interlace both hands inward with palms facing up, with the index fingers touching tip to tip. Recite three times. Through the power of this mudrā and mantra, a sea of clouds of all flower garlands flows forth, making offerings to all tathāgatas and the sacred assembly.

Next, form the mudrā for offering burnt incense. The mantra is:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānaṃ dharma-dhātv-anugate svāhā*

The mudrā: hold both palms face up, with the little, middle, and ring fingers bent upright, backs touching. Recite three times. Through the power of this mudrā and mantra, a sea of clouds of all burnt incense flows forth, making offerings to all tathāgatas and the sacred assembly.

Next, form the mudrā for offering food and drink. Recite the mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānaṃ arara karara-malindī-mahā-bali svāhā*

The mudrā: cup the hollow palms together and open them like a vessel. Recite three times. Through the power of this mudrā and mantra, a boundless sea of clouds of food and drink flows forth, making offerings to all tathāgatas and the sacred assembly.

Next, form the mudrā for offering lamps. Recite the mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānaṃ tathāgata-arcis-spharaṇi-avabhāsa-gaganaudāryā svāhā*

The mudrā: make a fist with the right hand, raise the middle finger, and press the thumb against the middle knuckle. Recite three times. Through the power of this mudrā and mantra, a sea of clouds of all lamp-light flows forth, making offerings to all tathāgatas and the sacred assembly.

Furthermore, as stated in the Mahāyāna sūtras of the tathāgata, one should also visualize banners, canopies, streamers, nets, jeweled necklaces, garments, silks, and other offerings, filling the dharmadhātu as seas of clouds of offerings. Then recite this verse of truth in praise:

> "Through the power of my merit,
> The power of the tathāgata's empowerment,
> And the power of the dharmadhātu,
> May universal offerings be made and abide."

Next, form the Ākāśagarbha Vidyā-rājñī mudrā. The mantra is:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānaṃ sarva-tham udgate spharahimaṃ gagana-kaṃ svāhā*

The mudrā: interlace both hands in joined palms, right pressing left. Recite seven times. Then visualize within one's heart a bright, full moon disk, clear and distinct. Upon the moon disk, visualize the syllable *khaṃ*, white in color, radiating great light that illuminates all worlds in the ten directions. Contemplate the true meaning of the syllable *khaṃ*: all dharmas are equal to space, free from all material marks, free from all obstructions.

Then, within the realm of true principle, visualize oneself as the image of Vajrapāramitā Buddha-Mother Bodhisattva: the left hand holding a lotus upon which rests a five-pronged vajra; the right hand palm up, lowered in the gesture of granting wishes; adorned with crown and jeweled necklaces, with a compassionate countenance, in the posture of delivering all sentient beings.

Having visualized thus, cross both hands externally in fists. Raise the two middle fingers. With this Vajrapāramitā mudrā, stamp upon four places: heart, forehead, throat, and crown. After stamping, scatter the hands. Recite the Vajrapāramitā mantra:

*Oṃ satva-vajrī hūṃ*

When stamping each of the four places, recite once. Then place the previous mudrā upon the forehead and recite three times for the abhiṣeka. After the abhiṣeka, separate the mudrā hands to both sides and with vajra fists tie behind the crown. Recite the mantra:

*Oṃ vajrābhiṣeca*

Recite three times, then form the armor mudrā: with each hand make a vajra fist with index fingers upright, and at the heart, wind them around each other as if fastening armor. Do likewise behind the back, then at the navel, both knees, around the waist, back to the heart, both shoulders (the same winding motion), then below the crown, behind the neck, before the forehead, and behind the head tie both vajra fists. Gradually lower the hands to both sides, slowly descending like hanging ribbons. Then clap both palms together three times. Recite the armor mantra:

*Oṃ cīṃ*

Then the clapping mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-tuṣya hoḥ*

Now visualize oneself as having become the principal deity. Join both palms, bend the index fingers with their nails back to back, and press both thumbs on the tips of the index fingers in the gesture of snapping. Then recite the Vijaya Dhāraṇī:

*Namo bhagavate trailokya-prativiśiṣṭāya buddhāya bhagavate tadyathā: oṃ viśodhaya samasamanta-avabhāsa-spharaṇa-gati-gahana sarva-viśuddhe abhiṣiñcatu-māṃ sugata-vara-vacana amṛta-abhiṣeke ahara ahara āyuḥ-sandhāraṇi śodhaya śodhaya gagana-viśuddhe uṣṇīṣa-vijaya-viśuddhe sahasra-raśmi-saṃcodite sarva-tathāgata-adhiṣṭhāna-adhiṣṭhite mudre vajra-kāya-saṃhātana-śuddhe sarva-āvaraṇa-viśuddhi-pratinivartaya āyuḥ-śuddhe samaya-adhiṣṭhite maṇi maṇi tathā-tabhūta-koṭi-pariśuddhe visphuṭa-buddhi-śuddhe jaya jaya vijaya vijaya smara smara sarva-buddha-adhiṣṭhita-śuddhe vajre vajra-garbhe vajraṃ bhavatu mama sarva-sattvānāṃ ca kāya-viśuddhe sarva-gati-pariśuddhe sarva-tathāgata-samāśvāsa-adhiṣṭhite budhya budhya bodhaya bodhaya samanta-pariśuddhe sarva-tathāgata-adhiṣṭhāna-adhiṣṭhita mahā-mudre svāhā*

The voice of recitation should be neither loud nor soft, neither slow nor fast. With single-minded attention, visualize Vairocana Buddha, clear and distinct. After reciting seven times, scatter the mudrā above the crown. Take a bodhi-seed rosary and place it in the palm. Recite the rosary empowerment mantra:

*Oṃ vairocana-mālā svāhā*

After reciting seven times, place the rosary upon the crown. With the left hand at the heart supporting the beads and the right hand moving them, pass one bead each time with the syllable *svāhā*. Recite from one hundred and eight up to one thousand times. Then gather the rosary into the palm, join the palms together, and place them upon the crown. Make whatever pure and wondrous vows you wish, praying that all sentient beings may also attain them.

Then form the principal deity's mudrā again and recite seven or three times. Then form the Vajrapāramitā mudrā again, and also form the five kinds of offering mudrās, reciting the five mantras for offerings, all as described above.

Then form the Acala mudrā, recite once, and rotate the mudrā leftward to release the boundary. Hold the argha vessel and make the offering. Then form the previous samaya mudrā above the crown to send off the assembly, reciting the samaya mantra once. Without releasing this mudrā, recite the vajra liberation mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-mokṣa muḥ*

Send off all buddhas and the sacred assembly, and prostrate as before. Openly confess, rejoice, entreat, make vows, and dedicate merit. After leaving the practice hall, go to a quiet place and read Mahāyāna sūtras. Contemplate the truth of supreme meaning. Dedicate this wondrous merit toward what you seek, to assist in accomplishing siddhi.

If performing the rite of pacification, face north. The altar is round. Visualize the sacred assembly in white. All offerings in the practice hall should be white. Wear white garments. Sit facing north. Burn aloeswood incense.

If performing the rite of increase, face east. The principal deity, offerings, and one's own garments should all be yellow. Burn white sandalwood incense.

If performing the rite of subjugation, face south. The principal deity, offerings, and garments should all be blue or black. Burn benzoin incense.

If performing the rite of attraction, face west. Visualize the principal deity in red. Food, drink, and garments should all be red. Burn a blend of ghee and incense.
