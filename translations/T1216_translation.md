# Vaipulya Mañjuśrī Kumārabhūta Bodhisattva Avataṃsaka Root Teaching: Praise of the Wrathful King Yamāntaka, Mantra Abhicāruka Ritual Manual

## *Dàfāngguǎng Mànshūshìlì Tóngzhēn Púsà Huáyán Běnjiào Zàn Yánmàndéjiā Fènnù Wáng Zhēnyán Āpízhēlújiā Yíguǐ Pǐn*

Taishō 1216

Translator unknown.

*Note: This is a tantric ritual manual (yíguǐ) comprising chapters 31-32 of a larger Mañjuśrī Avataṃsaka collection. Chapter 31, spoken by Vajrapāṇi (the Secret Lord), provides detailed instructions for the abhicāruka (destructive magic) rites of Yamāntaka, the wrathful emanation of Mañjuśrī. It includes: (1) iconographic specifications for painting Yamāntaka (six-faced, six-armed, black, riding a water buffalo, with skull garlands and tiger-skin skirt); (2) elaborate homa (fire-offering) procedures using poisonous substances, blood, dog fat, human bone, and other charnel-ground materials for subduing enemies, destroying kingdoms, and causing death; and (3) consecration rites involving charnel-ground ash for various purposes. Chapter 32, featuring Śāntamati Bodhisattva, raises the ethical objection to such violent practices, to which Vajrapāṇi responds that these are taught from the perspective of ultimate reality (shíjì) and are practiced with skillful means and great compassion for taming sentient beings, not from malice.*

## Chapter Thirty-One: The Abhicāruka Ritual of the Wrathful King Yamāntaka

At that time, Vajrapāṇi, the Secret Lord, surveyed the great assembly and those seated in the Pure Abode celestial palace, and made this proclamation: "You should all listen. The wrathful king of incomparable ferocity, as taught by Mañjuśrī, punishes and chastises those difficult to tame, even severing their lives, in order to make them submit."

"First I shall explain the ritual for painting the image."

At that time, Vajrapāṇi, the Secret Lord, proclaimed in verse:

> "Without choosing auspicious days or stars,
> And without requiring fasting precepts,
> One who fears vengeful enemies
> Should paint the wrathful image.
> On the eighth day of the dark fortnight,
> And on the fourteenth day,
> Go to a charnel ground and obtain
> A corpse-wrapping cloth of a brāhmaṇa.
> During the middle watch of night,
> Stain the cloth with blood,
> Then wash it with water
> And let it dry in the sun.
> A painter of fierce and evil disposition
> Should create the fearsome wrathful form.
> In the dark fortnight, at a charnel ground,
> The painting must be completed in three nights.
> On the eighth or fourteenth night,
> Light a lamp with dog fat.
> The painter should stand
> Facing south,
> Seated on a skull-cushion,
> With mind and body firmly settled in concentration,
> Or the practitioner may paint it himself
> If oppressed by fearful enemies.
> During the first watch of night,
> The enemy's body will burn with fever.
> In the second watch, chills and fevers strike,
> And spirits possess and confuse them.
> In the third watch, they relinquish their life
> And, dying, depart to another existence.
> How could such a one rest at ease
> While harboring malice toward the practitioner?
> The adversary's body withers and rots,
> And their entire household is extinguished.
> Through painting this image:
> The Yamāntaka image
> Has six faces, six hands, and six feet,
> Black in color, belly like a wolf,
> Bearing a skull-garland in wrath,
> With tiger skin as a skirt,
> Holding various weapons,
> With clasped hands that are fearsome.
> Eyes red, form ferocious and evil,
> Three eyes as emblems,
> Hair standing upright, blazing with flames,
> Or the dark color of black smoke,
> Also like añjana [collyrium],
> Like the dark clouds of summer rain.
> Its appearance is like the conflagration at the end of a kalpa.
> It should be painted riding a water buffalo,
> Wrathful and terrifying,
> Able to destroy rākṣasas
> And even sever the life of Yama.
> Fierce fury is its constant activity,
> Frightful and supremely malevolent,
> The most fearsome among the fearsome,
> Able to slay all sentient beings.
> One should paint this wrathful [form]
> Using one's own blood as pigment,
> Blended and shaded,
> With dog fat mixed with cow ghee,
> Contained in a skull vessel.
> Dead man's hair makes the brush,
> With a dog-bone handle.
> One should fast while painting,
> Whether doing it oneself or having another do it.
> Offer food, flowers, and the like abundantly,
> Red garlands, purple sandalwood incense,
> Dog meat for burnt incense,
> Human fat for the lamp's adornment.
> While painting the image,
> Make offerings at the beginning, middle, and end.
> The painted image should be distinct and clear.
> Reward the painter for his work;
> Give him a generous price
> And make his heart rejoice.
> Without delay, one should perform
> The great and fierce rites.
> The materials needed should be purchased;
> A brave one does not haggle over the price.
> All undertakings will certainly succeed.
> With various offerings,
> Reward the painter of the image
> Until he is satisfied and his hopes fulfilled.
> One should protect the painter's person;
> Otherwise, harm will come to him
> And to his family and dependents.
> One should also protect oneself.
> Mantra recitation should accompany the painting;
> This is the proper ritual procedure.
> When this image is clearly made,
> Merely seeing it fulfills one's wishes
> And accomplishes all tasks.
> Against violent enemies who do harm,
> One should take this excellent image along
> And seek whatever one desires.
> Those of great power, rebellious kings,
> The greatly wealthy and self-indulgent,
> Those especially swollen with pride,
> Violent evil-doers,
> Those who are not beneficial to the Three Jewels,
> Annihilationists who disparage mantras,
> Those who disrespect mantra-masters,
> Or who disparage those who revere them,
> Who also disparage mantra-reciters,
> Who slight and scorn mantras,
> Who never delight in wholesome dharmas,
> Who oppress and torment sentient beings:
> For such ones, the rite should be performed,
> And before long their lives will end.
> Take the bark, leaves,
> Root, and fruit of the neem tree,
> Mix with sour liquid,
> And with human bone powder,
> Mustard-seed oil and poison,
> Sour tamarind and ginger,
> And red mustard powder.
> Mix all with human blood
> And place before the image.
> The practitioner faces south,
> The honored image faces north.
> Before the image, make a kuṇḍa [fire pit],
> And light a fire with bitter wood,
> Or burn thorn-wood.
> In that furnace, place
> The mixture for the homa offering.
> One who fully knows the ritual
> Should then summon the fire deity
> With the wrathful vidyā-king,
> And form the śūla-mudrā,
> Which is effective for all rites.
> Casting [offerings] into the fire one thousand and eight times,
> All with great wrath:
> First, the children will wither and perish,
> Then the retainers and kin will die.
> Second, the lord, his wife,
> And their relatives will be extinguished.
> Third, all of them will die.
> One should know this according to the teaching.
> Before this image,
> In the middle of the night, one should recite mantras
> To harm those enemies.
> Thus one should proceed accordingly:
> Their kingdom will be destroyed,
> Their armies struck by plague,
> Fires will arise, great winds will blow,
> Torrential rains will pour,
> All their great armies
> Will be attacked and punished by outside enemies.
> Various calamities will occur
> And manifold diseases will arise.
> All their bodies will wither.
> For that rebellious king, perform this;
> Success should not be doubted.
> Non-human beings will fill [their lands],
> Their households will be in turmoil and strife,
> And they will find no peace in sleep.
> Their very ground will tremble,
> Rākṣasas will suck their vital essence,
> Surrounding their homes,
> Oppressing them with terror.
> Anxiety and anguish to the point of agony,
> And none can protect them.
> Maheśvara and the earth deities,
> Brahmā and the world-protectors,
> Indra lord of the Trāyastriṃśa heaven,
> All the mantra-deities,
> And all the worldly gods:
> Upon merely seeing this wrathful [form],
> Their lives will be cut short.
> In the middle of the night and at midday,
> If the mantra-holder is wrathful,
> King Yama himself
> Will cause their bodies to split asunder.
> On any day of the dark fortnight,
> Set up this honored image,
> Make abundant offerings of food,
> At a wilderness charnel ground,
> At a solitary tree, a Liṅga shrine,
> In a mountain cave or grotto.
> Without companions, one should dwell alone,
> Always performing this rite.
> In a tranquil great forest hermitage,
> In the open sky, an empty cave, or by a river,
> On a seashore, one should go to such places.
> At such kinds of places,
> Dwelling there as one pleases,
> Within a radius of one hundred yojanas,
> One should perform this rite.
> The scope of these matters has been explained.
> One should perform them with purity,
> Dwelling in diligence,
> Pure and free from desire.
> The domain of mantras is inconceivable,
> The practice of mantras is inconceivable,
> The supernatural powers of mantras are inconceivable,
> The accomplishments of the practitioner are inconceivable,
> The deeds performed are inconceivable,
> The fruits obtained are inconceivable.
> Now manifesting the wrathful king Yamāntaka:
> This is the great and awesome spiritual power.
> The realm of spiritual play that arises from it,
> The practitioner's accomplishment, is inconceivable.
> Manifesting here in Jambudvīpa,
> The great awesome power of all bodhisattvas:
> Even they cannot provide protection against this,
> Let alone the worldly mantras,
> All the planetary deities and the Mother goddesses,
> Īśāna and the rest, down to Viṣṇu.
> The divine youth Vasu,
> Even Indra lord of the gods,
> Cannot protect that person
> By means of their samaya.
> Sons of the Buddha and bodhisattvas,
> Those of awesome power on the ten stages,
> Pratyekabuddhas and śrāvakas,
> Those of great power freed from desire,
> Cannot protect that person.
> The original vow that was first sought:
> I now briefly proclaim it. Listen, you who seek prosperity.
> One who harms a mantra-holder
> Cannot be restrained by anyone.
> One who dislikes a vidyā-holder:
> How can their calamities be quelled?
> If one generates pure faith
> Along with a compassionate mind,
> And recites the mantra of the wrathful king,
> Great and awesome Yamāntaka,
> At that time calamities are removed
> And their life is protected.
> White cloth, mustard-seed oil,
> Five kinds of viṣa medicine,
> Dog blood and dog meat,
> Three pungents, salt, mustard,
> Conch powder, tamarind,
> Sea salt, dhattūra root,
> And kuṣṭhaka,
> Millet, hemp root, hemp ash,
> Safflower root and thorns,
> And madana root,
> Onion, garlic, palāśa,
> Kuṭaja and chives,
> Surā liquor and medicinal wine.
> These ingredients in equal parts
> Cast into the furnace before the image,
> Burn a full one thousand and eight.
> The enemy's lineage will perish,
> Along with relatives and friends,
> Protective deities and retainers:
> Their entire line will be annihilated.
> At the second round,
> When the mantra-holder performs the homa,
> Their territory and settlements
> Will suffer famine,
> Drought and epidemic,
> Rākṣasas will fill the land,
> Conflagration and hailstones,
> Lightning and frost
> Upon their villages and towns.
> Throughout the rebellious king's domain,
> Many afflictions will arise,
> Enemy armies will come to punish them,
> Calamities and ill omens of every kind
> Will appear in their territory.
> Burn dhattūra root, and that person goes mad.
> Constantly burn pungent substances,
> And their whole body burns as if afire.
> If you burn extremely sour substances,
> They will be struck by intermittent fever
> Arising in their body.
> For a proud and rebellious king,
> With a great violent faction,
> Supported by a large army:
> In two nights or seven nights,
> Their lives will all be ended.
> Take the deity that person worships,
> And their ruling constellation.
> Using the ash of a cremated corpse,
> Fashion images of all of them.
> Before the honored image,
> Trample their heads with your feet,
> Reciting mantras in wrath.
> That false and rebellious king
> Will suddenly be afflicted with various diseases,
> Assailed by great illness.
> In a moment they will perish:
> Killed by wild beasts,
> Or their limbs will be severed,
> Or they will be devoured by rākṣasas.
> Filthy non-human beings,
> Flesh-eating pūtanas,
> Piśāca hungry ghosts,
> And the various Mother goddesses:
> Both that person and their attendants
> Will be destroyed in an instant."

The auspicious Vajra-holder,
Having spoken thus in the assembly,
Bowed to all the buddhas
And remained silent.

Then, for the benefit of the world, he spoke again:

> "All the yakṣa hosts,
> The yakṣiṇī mantras,
> As taught by the bodhisattvas,
> And the yakṣa generals,
> The yakṣiṇī teaching-wheel:
> All are freely enjoyed,
> For summoning and for love-rites.
> But afflictions that are not cured,
> Those who seek mantras out of desire,
> Whose wisdom is darkened by the gloom of passion,
> Cannot apply the antidote;
> It is cut off by the Buddha's precepts.
> Since beginningless saṃsāra,
> Deeply habituated and pitiable,
> From suffering to yet more suffering:
> Therefore the Buddha taught of evil destinies.
> If one guards the sense-faculties,
> Pure conduct brings a fortunate rebirth.
> Therefore the wise and tranquil
> Ultimately realize nirvāṇa.
> With the three karmas riding on equanimity,
> One attains perfect quiescence.
> But the deluded swallow evil and foolish views,
> The ignorant are stained and beclouded,
> In the dense forest of birth and death,
> Revolving through the five destinies.
> Out of compassion for their suffering,
> [The teaching] permits the use of desire,
> Able to ward off all transgressions
> And sever the three kinds of fault,
> Reverently following the Dharma King's teaching,
> Gaining liberation from all fetters."

## Chapter Thirty-Two: The Wrathful King Yamāntaka

At that time, Śāntamati ("Tranquil Wisdom") Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, seated in that great assembly, rose from his seat, bowed to all the tathāgatas, and stood in the assembly. He circumambulated Śākyamuni Buddha three times, clasped the Buddha's feet, and knelt reverently. He then gazed at Vajrapāṇi, Lord of the Yakṣas, and spoke thus: "You, extremely violent Vajrapāṇi, have proclaimed to sentient beings the killing and harming of all sentient beings, and have permitted all mantra teachings involving desire and passion. Son of the Buddha! Such is not the dharma of bodhisattvas. The great bodhisattva, born from great compassion, practices the bodhisattva path for the benefit [of beings]. Because of the intention of supreme correct practice, he does not depart from the bonds of existence. Son of the Buddha! The Tathāgata, the Worthy One, the Perfectly Enlightened One, teaches methods for the benefit and ease of all sentient beings. Because he has perfected great compassion, his supreme intention is for the benefit and happiness of all sentient beings."

At that time, Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva Mahāsattva addressed Śāntamati Bodhisattva: "Śāntamati Bodhisattva! One should train thus and abide thus. It is as you have said, as you have shown. As all buddhas, bodhisattvas, and those of great awesome power have taught, I too teach in this way. I teach in accordance with ultimate reality.

> "Ultimate reality is inconceivable,
> Karmic maturation is inconceivable,
> The Buddha-dharma is inconceivable,
> Bodhisattvas are inconceivable.
> The practice of taming sentient beings,
> Every practice, is inconceivable.
> What the bodhisattva practices
> Is therefore called inconceivable.
> In all the mantra teachings,
> The awesome power is inconceivable.
> The wrathful king's mantra,
> Great and awesome Yamāntaka:
> His supernatural realm is inconceivable,
> His great power is inconceivable."

"Śāntamati! The inconceivable bodhisattva mahāsattvas and the rest manifest in the realm of sentient beings in this way. Śāntamati! The bodhisattva who practices mantras should generate this mind: 'If one engages in sexual desire, one incurs immeasurable transgression toward sentient beings and falls into the great hells. One who creates wrathful sentient beings also incurs immeasurable transgression. Do not cause sentient beings to be rendered incapable of attaining the three kinds of bodhi.' Śāntamati! Thus the bodhisattva who holds mantras generates this mind: 'By skillful means, I perform abhicāruka [rites]. In all activities, one should not grasp at characteristics, should not seize upon what is unwholesome, but should learn the skillful means for taming sentient beings, with a mind wrapped in great compassion.' Furthermore, Son of the Buddha! Dharma and non-dharma, purity and non-purity, wholesome and non-wholesome: these are the skillful means of responding and transforming sentient beings. All buddhas and bodhisattvas, flowing forth from the dharma-realm, practice and teach the Dharma. Through this very teaching, they speak with skillful means to sentient beings, in order to bring them to maturity. One should abide thus correctly. Son of the Buddha! We should train in this way: that is, to tame sentient beings, to bring sentient beings to maturity, and to bring sentient beings to tranquility. Those sons of the Buddha who have entered the maṇḍala assembly should all listen with pure faith and wholesome [intent]. They should examine what is wholesome and what is unwholesome. That is to say, they should generate deep love and delight in the Dharma taught by the Tathāgata, and should not doubt or slander it."

At that time, Śāntamati Bodhisattva Mahāsattva reflected and abided silently [in contemplation of] the inconceivable Buddha-dharma. With this mindfulness, he gazed up at the Tathāgata. Then Vajrapāṇi, the Secret Lord, surveying the great assembly, again taught the dharma of the wrathful king, instructing the assembly: "All you hosts of devas and sentient-realm-dependent ghosts and spirits: the practitioner should first protect his own body. Take the image of the wrathful king and dwell in one place, namely at a Maheśvara liṅga shrine. Smear [the liṅga] with a mixture of poison, mustard seeds, dog blood, and sour liquid. If there is a liṅga, take white cloth and leaves for offerings. Take a human intestine as a sacred thread and wind it around [the liṅga]. With the right hand, hold a human skull and hurl it at the liṅga. Point with the index finger of the left hand, and abide in great wrath toward that rebellious king who insults and scorns [the practitioner], as well as other evil people and their great violent factions. At the place where the rite is performed, close the door, be naked with hair unbound, and trample the Maheśvara liṅga with the left foot, splitting it in two. When you hear the great sound 'Hūṃ,' do not be afraid. On that very day, the rebellious king, the other great evil factions, and the vengeful enemies will be seized by great chills and fever, or possessed by non-human beings or rākṣasas. If you continue to recite mantras for a short while more, those enemies will perish in an instant. If you recite through consecutive nights, their entire household will be destroyed.

"Another method: at midday, go to a Maheśvara shrine. Take bitter neem leaves and offer them. Burn dog meat as incense and recite mantras. The enemy's household will be burned by fire, and they will be struck by malarial chills. If the mantra recitation continues without ceasing, [the practitioner] stands wrathfully at the right side of the Maheśvara [liṅga], and that enemy's household will be extinguished. If you wish to restore things to their original state, wash the liṅga with water, then bathe it in cold milk, and it will return to normal.

"Another method: on the right side of the Maheśvara liṅga, burn madana and thorn-wood fuel. Dip a piece of myrobalan wood in poisoned blood and mustard-seed oil, and cast it into the fire one thousand and eight times. The enemy will be afflicted with a great illness that no one can cure. On the second day, they will be seized by great chills and fever and major illness, or struck by various diseases, or possessed by non-human beings unto death. On the third day, with mantra recitation at all three times, all of their lives will be relinquished. If you wish to restore things to normal, perform a homa with milk, and that settlement and the enemies will all obtain peace and happiness. In this way, for whatever deity or ghost that person worships, trample upon it and recite mantras. Write the star-constellation to which that person belongs and trample it with the left foot. Except for the mantras taught by the Tathāgata, all other worldly mantras should be trodden upon with the big toe of the left foot while performing the rite and reciting. Even before completing the practice of the wrathful king, merely reciting can accomplish all tasks, can also destroy all [opposing] mantras, can harm all enemies, and can break all mantra-methods. I have now spoken in brief. Whatever the practitioner may practice, whether worldly or supramundane mantra rituals, even if the original teaching does not mention it, borrowing from other divisions will still achieve all accomplishments. Merely reciting achieves the fulfillment of all wishes. Merely reciting the wrathful king obtains the supreme accomplishment, arising according to one's desire. It can also crush all adversaries. By forming the śūla-mudrā, it correspondingly accomplishes all tasks.

"Another method: at noon, go to a charnel ground where corpses are burned. Fast for one day and one night. On the fourteenth day of the dark fortnight, light a fire with charnel-ground fuel. Mix poison, mustard seeds, and blood together. Recite the mantra once and cast [the mixture] once into the fire. You will hear the sound 'Ha ha!' All the hungry ghosts will come. Do not be afraid. Tell them: 'Harm my enemy for me.' The ghosts, hearing this, will accept the command, then vanish from sight. Even at a distance of a thousand yojanas, they will arrive in an instant to harm that enemy and their household. Thus many kinds of siddhi can be accomplished.

"Another method: in a clean, quiet place, take white cloth and recite the mantra, burning it once per recitation, one thousand and eight times. Take the ash separately with the left and right hands. Divide it onto two pieces of clean material, wrap each portion, and place each in an earthen bowl, covered with another earthen bowl. Recite mantras to consecrate the materials. Go to a great charnel ground on the fourteenth night of the dark fortnight, or on the eighth night. Stand at the place where corpses are burned, facing south. Place the two vessels before you. Be naked with hair unbound, wrathful and without fear. Recite the mantra twelve thousand times to consecrate the materials, and accomplishment will be obtained. If non-human beings demand the materials of accomplishment, do not give them. If they try to seize the ash, recite the wrathful king's mantra and pronounce the syllable 'Hūṃ,' and they will instantly vanish. Clearly distinguish the ash taken by the left and right hands. Do not be careless in consecrating them. At dawn, bathe and put on clean clothes, then return to your original place. The ash taken and consecrated by the right hand: scatter it on the heads of all ghosts, spirits, devas, nāgas, and yakṣas, and it will produce love and devotion. The ash consecrated by the left hand: scatter it on the heads of all men and women, and they will all become devoted and loving."

"Take the right-hand ash and scatter it on the navel, and the person will become a eunuch. Scatter it on the genitals, and they will be unable to engage in worldly conjugal relations, being given to improper conduct. If a man dotes upon a certain woman, scatter the ash on her private parts, and she will be unable to act improperly with other men; her faculty will be damaged. Yet when she joins with her own husband, her faculty will be restored to normal. Likewise, scatter the ash on a man's genitals, and he will become impotent; that man will be unable to enjoy relations with other women. Yet with his own wife, his faculty will again be capable of worldly functions. If one takes the original ash and scatters it on the genital openings of both a man and a woman, they will feel deep mutual devotion. If another man or woman forcibly approaches them, that person's faculty will fester and be consumed by worms, and they will be reduced to debility. Within a month, their body will reek like a corpse. Beset by grave illness, the man's organ will swell; from this cause, even unto death, no one will be able to heal them. Whatever is done with this ash achieves accomplishment.

"Furthermore, by smearing the ash on one's hand and touching the person, all will be accomplished. Whether one acts personally or instructs another to act, accomplishment follows according to one's will. If one cannot touch the person directly, take the ash and blow it toward whatever part of their body the ash may reach; or scatter it, or merely visualize scattering it, and all tasks will be accomplished. Whether acting personally or instructing another, accomplishment follows without fail, and no effort is wasted. Furthermore, bedding, quilts, various ornaments, various implements, footwear, parasols, and all manner of personal possessions, including food, drink, household items, flower baskets, vines, fruits, perfume, and incense: scatter the ash upon all of them. The targeted enemy will be afflicted with fleas, lice, bed-bugs, and other vermin that bite and torment them with extreme suffering. After seven days they will perish; no physician will be able to heal them. Even the other devas cannot intervene, and no mantra can protect them. Only if the person who performed the rite undoes it can things be restored to normal. Take licorice root, blue lotus flower, and white sandalwood; grind them together with clean water and smear the mixture on that person's body from crown to feet, then empower it with the holy Mañjuśrī root mantra, and they will be healed."

"Another method: standing upwind of all ḍākinīs and haughty women (this method is not for other places), scatter the ash. With this intention, cause that woman to lose her faculties. If done for a man, he will lose his virility and facial hair. This can accomplish various tasks. Teaching men and women to perform [the rites] also achieves accomplishment. By merely thinking of giving that person ash and instructing them to act, the same results follow, causing them to suffer great illness. If you touch their head while mentally focusing, they will suffer headaches. Touching the mouth causes mouth sores. In sequence, touching the heart causes heart pain, the belly causes stomach pain, the feet cause foot pain, the shins cause shin pain, causing bleeding and evil diseases. This can even cause death. Withering, destruction, summoning, and subjugation: whatever that person desires to accomplish, all succeed at once. Even reduction, summoning, and love-rites performed from afar also achieve accomplishment.

"Another method: stand upwind at a deep well. With both hands, cup the ash and scatter it upon city walls, which will cause the enemy's ramparts to collapse. The general's dwelling will be consumed by fire, attacked and defeated by outside enemies, plunged into dire peril, abandoning their homes and fleeing in disarray. Another method: when an enemy army approaches, scatter the ash downwind. Even if that army is extremely powerful, they will destroy themselves, be struck by great fever, and their elephants, horses, chariots, and infantry will scatter and be captured by the other side. Countless kinds of affairs like these can all be accomplished as desired to crush enemies. This method can also protect oneself and one's own army and retainers. If you wish to restore things to normal, before the wrathful king's image, perform a homa with milk one thousand and eight times, and they will be at peace, with no one able to harm them."
