# 青龍寺軌記
## Record of the Ritual Procedures of Qinglong-si

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0855

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown

---

First, the yogin should constantly abide in the contemplation of the principal deity. When walking, visualize treading upon lotus flowers and abide therein. Next, upon reaching the door of the vihāra, snap the fingers three times and pronounce *hūṃ*. With the right eye contemplate the syllable *ma*; with the left eye contemplate the syllable *ṭa*. *Ma* is the sun; *ṭa* is the moon. Enter the door and look about: this accomplishes the five benefits, namely: all the Buddhas rejoice, defilements are removed, radiance is revealed, obstacles are expelled, and the boundary is sealed.

Next, with the left hand form the vajra-fist at the heart, raising the wind-finger. The right hand likewise. Rotate above the crown to the left and right, pointing upward and downward, all while reciting the *hūṃ* syllable mantra.

Next, circumambulate three times and bow to the Buddha.

Next, recite words of confession.

Next, take one's seat and again bow to the Buddha.

Next, purify the three karmas.

Next, empower fragrant water and sprinkle for purification.

Next, recite the spirit-offering invocation.

Next, announce the request for empowerment and protection.

Next, form the seal for awakening the Buddha.

Next, chant the nine expedient homages. If the mind is scattered, recite a Mahāyāna scripture.

Next, the four immeasurables.

Next, the supreme-mind mantra.

Next, the vajra wheel.

Next, the earth boundary.

Next, the four directional boundaries.

Next, sit as appropriate. In the heart, contemplate the initial syllable-wheel: nine concentric circles, empty, round, and white.

Next, enter the Buddha's samaya.

Next, the Dharma-realm arising.

Next, the turning-the-Dharma-wheel seal: rotate it three times clockwise above the forehead, seal the five places, and visualize turning the Dharma-wheel throughout the three-thousand worlds.

Next, don the armor.

Next, abide in meditative contemplation. Place a pure white *ra* syllable upon the crown; karmic defilements are extinguished and merit and wisdom become complete.

Next, the seal and mantra of Invincible Endurance.

Next, startle and awaken the earth spirit.

Next, the earth spirit's support.

Next, the sequential mantras.

Next, the altar-making mantra.

Next, the purification-sprinkling mantra.

Next, the verse addressed to the earth spirit.

Next, invite the earth deity.

Next, the earth-supporting mantra.

Next, the compassion-born maṇḍala.

Next, establish the five syllables.

Next, contemplate the hundred rays of Universal Illumination at the crown.

Next, place the unborn phrase in the heart.

Next, inscribe the syllable of freedom from defilement at the chest.

Next, establish the immaculate eye.

Next, form the fire-wheel seal.

Next, form the earth-wheel seal. Recite the mantra and place it between the two knees. Water, fire, wind, and space are each placed at their respective positions in sequence.

Next, with vajra-palms joined at the heart, recite the fulfillment verse.

Next, establish the vessel-world. Visualize the space-wheel in the lower realm, its extent immeasurable. Wind, fire, and water function in sequence.

Next, abide in the concentration seal. Contemplate a syllable on the earth-wheel becoming an ocean. Within the ocean, a syllable becomes a five-wisdom vajra. Upon it, a syllable becomes a karmic-action emblem. Upon the karmic-action emblem, a syllable becomes an eight-petaled lotus. The two dragon-kings Nanda and the other support the lotus on the left and right.

Next, the great ocean seal and mantra.

Next, Vajrapāṇi holding a flower.

Next, the great mantra-king.

Next, the eight-petaled lotus seal.

Next, the lion-throne seal.

Next, upon the lotus platform, a syllable transforms into a vast palace with stairs, balustrades, and full adornment of the seven treasures. Contemplate it in full according to the ritual manual.

Next, the Ākāśagarbha mantra.

Next, the great wheel-altar seal.

Next, the seal for spreading the various colors and boundary paths. External binding with the five kinds of seed-syllables and so forth. In sequence: white, red, yellow, blue, and black, corresponding to faith, vigor, mindfulness, concentration, and wisdom, as explained in the commentary. Then recite the five-color seed-syllables and rotate the seal five times.

Next, recite *namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ hūṃ*, and rotate once more.

Next, abide in the concentration seal. Contemplate the seed-syllables of each deity in the central court, each one clearly and distinctly arranged. First, visualize a circle of light, the great enlightened one's lion-throne, and the eight petals. Upon the throne is a pure luminous moon-disc with the long *āḥ* syllable placed in the center. It transforms into Mahāvairocana, the great sage, pure and free from all defilements, of wondrous form transcending the three realms, and so forth. Surrounded by the eight-petaled maṇḍala retinue. In the moon-discs on each of the eight petals are syllables that transform into Ratnaketu, Saṃkusumitarāja, Amitābha, and Divyadundubhimegha-nirghoṣa Buddha. In the pure moon-discs at the four corners are syllables that transform into Samantabhadra, Mañjuśrī, Avalokiteśvara, and Maitreya, each with forms, colors, adornments, countenances, hand-seals, and emblems all as specified in the ritual manual. Each seed-syllable corresponds to each deity's color, each form to each samaya-emblem: contemplate according to the Dharma without omission. If one cannot manage this, contemplate the syllable in the central court becoming radiant, emitting golden light that universally illuminates the three courts. The gate of the syllable *a* transforms into all the deities; the seed-syllables transform into all forms and colors. Having thus contemplated, this realm preserves the thirteen assemblies. The pure luminous moon-disc is the body of meditative correspondence, surrounded by the eight maṇḍala retinues and the rest: this clearly represents the central court.

Next, the east: the seal of Universal Knowledge, called the second assembly. North: Avalokiteśvara, the third assembly. South: Vajrapāṇi, the fourth assembly. In the Nirṛti direction: Acala, the Tathāgata's messenger, and in the Wind direction: Trailokyavijaya, the fifth assembly. The four great guardians of the four directions are the sixth assembly. At the first gate, Śākyamuni is the seventh assembly. The third is Mañjuśrī, the eighth assembly. In the south, Sarvanivaraṇa-viṣkambhin is the ninth assembly. In the Victorious direction, Kṣitigarbha is the tenth assembly. In the Dragon direction, Ākāśagarbha is the eleventh assembly. The Susiddhikara retinue is the twelfth assembly. The world-protecting devas of great majesty are the thirteenth assembly. They are arrayed in sequence.

Next, the jeweled-crown raised-hand seal.

Next, the armor seal. At each court, bow three times in refuge. Recite the twenty-eight syllables beginning with *ka* and so on, slowly encircling toward the jade-pillow, throat, heart, navel, and the rest, contemplating each in sequence.

Next, the thirty-six syllables beginning with *i* and so on; with the previous seal, encircle the navel.

Next, the universal seal at the chest. Recite the two expedient wheels, meaning the two kinds of wheels, adding the nose, thirty-four each, as the body's radiance.

Next, the lotus and vajra division wheels, following the same pattern as the Buddha-division wheel. In total, the three-court maṇḍala resides in the respective places on the body, and so forth.

Next, the seed-syllable concentration seal: distribute the thirty-two syllables.

Next, the universal seal at the heart. Recite the four mantras: bodhicitta samādhi, bodhicaryā prajñā, bodhisiddhi restoration, and śānta nirvāṇa.

Next, abide in the concentration seal. Contemplate the inner-heart maṇḍala.

Next, the great mantra-king Dharma-realm equality contemplation: arrange the twelve syllables, also corresponding to the twelve links of dependent origination.

Next, the eight secret seals.

> "All who hear my voice, or see me, or touch me:
> Their minds are settled in Arhat,
> In Samyak-saṃbuddha.
> All merit and virtue
> Is equal to that of the Great Sun World-Honored One,
> Without any difference."

Next, contemplate the syllable in the heart. The syllable's great void-point is the gate of liberation through emptiness. The syllable's freedom from causes and conditions is the gate of liberation through signlessness. The syllable *u* and its samādhi-strokes are the gate of liberation through wishlessness. By relying on this contemplation of liberation, one can remove the obstacles of practice. Then leave the practice-hall and follow the rules of training.
