# 深沙大將儀軌
## Ritual of the Deep Sand General

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1291

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

At that time, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva rose from his seat in the great assembly, prostrated with all five limbs touching the ground, bowed at the Buddha's feet, and addressed the Buddha: "I possess a method of vast accomplishment using one true-word, one mudrā, and three messenger true-words. For the benefit and happiness of the good men and good women of Jambudvīpa, I humbly entreat the World-Honored One to mercifully permit me to speak it now." The mantra:

*Namo ratna-trayāya namaḥ āryāvalokiteśvarāya tadyathā jaye jaye jayā-vahe ni jayū-dhari-kara-kara māra-māra cala-kṛṇa sarva-karma-dhāraṇī-me-pati svāhā.*

"Having recited this mantra, evil karma is eliminated. Reciting it seven times, the five heinous sins are destroyed. When one has recited it a full 1,000 times, there is no sin that is not eliminated. When one has recited a full 100,000 times, one will see Avalokiteśvara face to face in all manner of adornments. During seven days of initial practice, one should eat only milk-porridge and ghee. On the final day, do not eat. On the night of the fifteenth, recite before a buddha image without a fixed number. When the image is seen to move and audibly proclaim, 'Excellent! Excellent!' emitting radiant light, and furthermore places pearl treasures upon the mantra-practitioner's crown, know that accomplishment has been verified. Having seen Avalokiteśvara face to face, all virtuous wishes are fulfilled. Furthermore, if one dreams of Avalokiteśvara in all manner of adornments during sleep, all wholesome matters are accomplished, all evil karma is eliminated, and through continuous recitation one obtains the elimination of sins.

"Next, the one mudrā, also called 'Benefiting Sentient Beings.' With one mudrā, the thirty-two uses and various accomplishments are performed. Cross the backs of both hands against each other. Press both thumbs upon both index fingers to form a sword. Directing the mudrā toward empty space serves as a canopy-boundary; in the ten directions, above in the heavens and everywhere, boundaries are established. Directing it toward the honored one serves as universal offering. Performing the method serves as universal reverence. At the conclusion, it serves as dedication of merit, and also for dispatching and sending forth. If one wishes to subjugate evil persons, form the hand-mudrā without duality; recite with a wrathful mind 108 times, and all deeds of non-Buddhist practitioners are destroyed. If a person has a heart illness, empower yellow earth and apply it to the heart; the illness is cured.

"If a person suffers from headache: earth and fire.

"If a person has an eye ailment, empower apricot-seed oil and apply it; the illness is cured.

"If a person has swelling, empower flour 108 times and apply it; the swelling is healed.

"If a person wishes to drive away māra-afflictions from afar, empower ruomu wood eight times, and the affliction departs.

"Next, the messengers. The three masters comprehensively govern the twenty-eight divisions.

"The left palm held flat facing upward toward the sky, with the thumb moving back and forth.

"The heavenly messenger is Vaikara, also called Jñānapūrṇa. The secret words:

*Vaikara ehi hūṃ svāhā.*

"Both palms joined, both earth-fingers moving back and forth. The space messenger is a great sage, also called Vāsi. The secret words:

*Siddhya svāhā.*

"Both hands directed toward empty space, with both thumbs moving back and forth. The earth messenger commands water, fire, and thunder at will, and is also called Mahārāja. The secret words:

*Oṃ jaye jaye svāhā.*

"These three kinds of messengers can extinguish the three calamities, can eliminate the three kinds of attachment, can destroy the karmic obstructions of lifetime after lifetime, and achieve siddhi in lifetime after lifetime.

"Furthermore, these three kinds of messengers are without mutual conflict. Their power instantly arises and ceases; they overturn bowls, bind and fetter, join wood, and empty bowls.

"If a person completes the full mantra practice, they will ride through the realm of space as a sage, able to summon and dispatch devas, nāgas, yakṣas, ghosts, spirits, and others at will. Such is the power of the Dharma."

*Akāda-mī.*

The One-Mudrā, Thousand-Kinds, Thousand-Turning, Three-Messengers Accomplishment Method, in one fascicle.
