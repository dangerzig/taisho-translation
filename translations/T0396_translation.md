# 佛說法滅盡經
## Sūtra of the Extinction of the Dharma Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0396

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the country of Kuśinagara. The Tathāgata would enter parinirvāṇa in three months, and the bhikṣus and bodhisattvas were with him.

An immeasurable multitude came to the Buddha and bowed their heads to the ground. The World-Honored One was silent and did not speak, and his radiance did not appear. The Worthy Ānanda bowed and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! In the past, when you taught the Dharma, your awesome radiance shone forth alone. Now the great assembly has gathered, yet the radiance no longer appears. For what reason is this? There must be a cause. May we hear its meaning."

The Buddha remained silent and did not respond. Ānanda asked in this way three times.

The Buddha told Ānanda: "After my nirvāṇa, when the Dharma is about to be extinguished, in the age of the five turbidities, the way of Māra will flourish. Māra will become śramaṇas and ruin my path. They will wear secular clothes yet delight in fine kāṣāya robes of five colors. They will drink wine and eat meat, kill living beings and crave flavors, lacking all compassion, and they will hate and envy one another.

"At that time there will be bodhisattvas, pratyekabuddhas, and arhats who practice with diligence and cultivate virtue, whom all people respect and look up to. Their teaching will be impartial; they will have compassion for the poor and be mindful of the elderly, caring for the destitute and suffering. They will constantly use sūtras and images to cause people to venerate and serve them, and will cultivate all kinds of merit. Their disposition will be kind and benevolent; they will not harm others. They will sacrifice themselves to aid others, not cherishing their own lives, patient, humane, and gentle.

"If there are such people, the multitude of māra-bhikṣus will all be jealous of them, slandering and spreading evil about them, expelling and banishing them, not allowing them to remain. Thereafter, among themselves, they will not cultivate the path and virtue. Temples and monasteries will stand empty and desolate, no longer repaired, and will gradually fall to ruin. They will only covet wealth and goods, hoarding them without distributing, and will not create merit. They will buy and sell servants, plow fields and plant crops, burn mountains and forests, harming living beings, without any compassion. Male servants will become bhikṣus; female servants will become bhikṣuṇīs. They will lack morality and virtue, engaging in lustful and impure conduct, with no distinction between men and women. This will cause the Dharma to become thin and diluted, all because of such people. Some will flee from the authorities and rely on my path, seeking to become śramaṇas without cultivating precepts or discipline. At the end of each half-month, though they recite the precepts in name, they will be weary and lazy, unwilling to listen. They will abbreviate and skip the beginning and end, unwilling to recite the full text. They will not study or practice the sūtras. Even if there are some who read, they will not recognize the characters or phrases. Insisting they are correct, they will not consult the learned, arrogantly seeking fame, making an outward display of elegance while hoping for others' offerings. When such māra-bhikṣus die, their spirits shall fall into the Avīci Hell, suffering the sins of the five acts of immediate retribution, cycling through the hungry ghost and animal realms without exception, for kalpas as numerous as the sands of the Ganges. Only when their punishment is exhausted will they be born in a border land where the Three Treasures are absent.

"When the Dharma is about to be extinguished, women will be diligent, always cultivating merit; men will be lazy and negligent, paying no heed to the Dharma. Seeing śramaṇas, they will look upon them as they would upon dirt, having no faith. When the Dharma is about to die out, at that time all the devas will weep tears. Rainfall will be irregular; the five grains will not ripen. Epidemics will spread, and many will die. The people will toil in hardship, and the magistrates will oppress them, paying no heed to principle. All will think only of disorder. Evil people will multiply like sand in the sea, while the good will be very few, perhaps one or two. As the kalpa draws to its end, the sun and moon will grow shorter, and human lifespans will grow brief. At forty, hair will turn white. Men's licentiousness will exhaust their vitality and shorten their lives; some may live to sixty. Men's lives will be short, while women's lives will be long: seventy, eighty, ninety, or even a hundred years.

"Great floods will suddenly arise, coming without warning. The people of the world will not believe it, thinking things are as they always have been. Beings of all kinds, regardless of high or low status, will be submerged and carried away, devoured by fish and turtles.

"At that time there will be bodhisattvas, pratyekabuddhas, and arhats, driven out by the māra-multitudes and excluded from the assemblies. The three vehicles will retreat into the mountains, to places of merit and virtue, and in peaceful contentment will guard themselves, considering this their joy. Their lifespans will be extended, and the devas will protect them. When the Bodhisattva Candraprabha appears in the world, they will meet one another and together revive my path for fifty-two years. The *Śūraṅgama Sūtra* and the *Pratyutpanna-samādhi* will be the first to disappear. The twelve divisions of the canon will follow and also be entirely extinguished, no longer appearing. The written texts will not be seen. The robes of the śramaṇas will naturally turn white.

"When my Dharma is extinguished, it will be like an oil lamp about to go out: the flame flares brighter for a moment, and then it is extinguished. When my Dharma is extinguished, it will be like the dying of a lamp. After this, it is difficult to describe what will follow. After many millions of years, Maitreya will descend into the world and become a Buddha. The world will be at peace, the poisonous vapors will be dispelled, the rains will be timely and harmonious, the five grains will flourish, the trees will grow tall, and humans will be eight zhang in height and will all live eighty-four thousand years. The beings who will be liberated will be beyond reckoning."

The Worthy Ānanda bowed and addressed the Buddha: "What should this sūtra be called? How should it be upheld?"

The Buddha said: "Ānanda! This sūtra is called 'The Extinction of the Dharma.' Proclaim it to all and cause it to be understood and distinguished. Its merit is immeasurable and beyond reckoning."

The disciples of the four assemblies, hearing this sūtra, were stricken with sorrow and grief. All gave rise to the supreme, true aspiration for the path, and all bowed to the Buddha in reverence and departed.
