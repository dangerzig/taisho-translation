# 阿閦如來念誦供養法

## The Recitation and Offering Method for Akṣobhya Tathāgata

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0921

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

> Homage to the All-Illuminating One.
> I now, relying on the sūtra-agreements,
> Briefly explain the practice of Akṣobhya Buddha:
> The ritual of cultivation and recitation.
> The practitioner should pay homage
> To the Tathāgatas of the five directions,
> Imagining throughout empty space
> Buddhas as numerous as sesame seeds.
> Before each and every Buddha,
> Confess with all one's heart,
> Rejoice and exhort and request.
> The merit I have accumulated
> I dedicate to all sentient beings.
> Next, facing the honored one,
> One should sit in full-lotus posture,
> Body upright and perfectly straight.
> Close the eyes, abandon clinging,
> And then arouse the mind of compassion,
> Contemplating the boundless realms.
> First form the samaya;
> Next recite the Vajra Wheel
> To extinguish all transgressions.
> Then one should form the Armor Mudrā,
> Empowering the five places.
> Next make the Vajra Stake
> To make firm the ground of the bodhi-site.
> Then form the directional boundary.
> Within the altar, contemplate the great ocean.
> In the center, visualize Mount Sumeru.
> Above, contemplate a jeweled pavilion.
> Upon the pavilion, a lion-throne
> With manifold offering implements,
> Adorned with many jewels.
> Next form the Chariot Mudrā,
> Visualizing an offering to the Abhirati pure land.
> Then clear the path through empty space.
> Next form the mudrā to request the jeweled chariot,
> And with the division-heart mudrā, make the invitation.
> Then one should perform the expulsion
> And display the samaya.
> Then form the vajra net.
> Offer the *arghya* water,
> Visualizing bathing the stainless body.
> Then offer the honored one's seat.
> In sequence, present the five offerings.
> Then form the Ākāśagarbha mudrā,
> Extending to boundless realms;
> For each, visualize clouds and oceans,
> Offering them personally with one's own body.
> Then one should recite praises,
> Or praise the 108 Names.
> Then form the Division-Mother Mudrā,
> Empowering the honored one
> And also protecting oneself.
> Next form the Honored One's Mudrā,
> Then take up the rosary.
> After empowering, place it on the crown,
> And, abiding steadfastly, perform the recitation.
> Then enter the syllable-wheel contemplation.
> With this supreme merit,
> Dedicate it to sentient beings.
> Then form the Honored One's Mudrā.
> Next recite the Division-Mother Vidyā.
> As before, the five offerings
> And praise of the honored one's virtues.
> Then offer the *arghya* water.
> One should form the Outer Court Mudrā,
> Turn left to dissolve the boundary.
> Then form the Jeweled Chariot Mudrā,
> Push outward to send off,
> Visualizing the honored one returning to his palace.
> Re-form the samaya.
> The five repentances as before.
> Then rise, following one's own intention.
> Read and recite Mahāyāna sūtras,
> Or abide in samādhi.
> Stamp stūpas, contemplate the six recollections.
> With this merit, support the siddhi.
> This is called the bodhisattva practice.

The practitioner enters the shrine of the honored one. Facing east, kneeling with palms joined, one sincerely visualizes all Tathāgatas and all great bodhisattvas, as numerous as atoms, pervading the ten-direction realms like sesame seeds, as though present before one's eyes. Among them, further visualize the Tathāgatas of the five directions, and bow once to each. The mantra for paying homage to all Tathāgatas:

*Oṃ sarvatathāgata kāya vāk citta pūja maṇḍanaṃ karomī*

> By reciting this mantra
> And making obeisance to all Buddhas,
> In all the lands of the ten directions,
> The worship is fully complete.

Then, with the right knee on the ground and palms joined at the heart, confess all transgressions: "From beginningless time until this present body, the many sins I have committed, the ten evils, the four grave offenses, the five acts of immediate retribution, and boundless other wrongs: I now, before all Buddhas and great bodhisattvas, with deep remorse and regret, openly confess and repent. Once confessed, I shall never commit them again." The mantra of confession:

*Oṃ sarvapāpa sphuṭa dahana vajrāya svāhā*

> By reciting this mantra,
> In accord with the principle of true reality,
> All sins, like dried grass,
> Are burned away without remainder.

Next one should reflect: all Tathāgatas, all great bodhisattvas, pratyekabuddhas, śrāvakas, and all ordinary beings have accumulated merit and wisdom. I now fully rejoice in all of it. Just as all Tathāgatas rejoice in all merit and wisdom, so do I now also rejoice. The mantra of rejoicing in all merit and wisdom:

*Oṃ sarvatathāgata puṇyajñāna anumodanā pūjameghā samudra spharaṇa samaye hūṃ*

> By reciting this mantra,
> In the merit of all Buddhas, bodhisattvas,
> The Two Vehicles, and ordinary beings,
> One obtains supreme rejoicing.

Next, sincerely contemplate: all Tathāgatas pervading the dharma-realm have just attained perfect enlightenment. Visualize oneself present at that ocean-assembly, before each and every Buddha, sincerely exhorting and requesting: "May all Tathāgatas, out of compassion for us, turn the unsurpassed Dharma-wheel." The mantra for requesting the turning of the Dharma-wheel:

*Oṃ sarvatathāgata adhyeṣaṇā pūjameghā samudra spharaṇa samaye hūṃ*

> By reciting this vidyā,
> All Tathāgatas,
> In both defiled and pure lands,
> Turn the unsurpassed Dharma-wheel.

Next, one should exhort and request the Buddhas and Tathāgatas of the ten directions not to enter parinirvāṇa: "May all Tathāgatas, out of compassion for sentient beings, dwell long in the world without entering parinirvāṇa, and for immeasurable kalpas widely perform benefit." The mantra for requesting non-parinirvāṇa:

*Oṃ sarvatathāgatāna adhyeṣayame sarvasattvahitārthāya dharmadhātu sthitibhavatu*

> By reciting this mantra,
> All Tathāgatas
> Remain for immeasurable kalpas,
> Widely benefiting sentient beings.

The practitioner reflects thus: "Through my worship of the Buddhas, confession, rejoicing, and exhortation, having thus accumulated immeasurable merit and wisdom, may all of it be dedicated to all sentient beings. May the supreme siddhi praised by the Buddhas be fully attained by all sentient beings." The mantra for dedication and aspiration:

*Oṃ sarvatathāgata saṃśiṣṭa sarvasattvānāṃ sarvasiddhiyoga saṃprāhidaṃ tathāgata jñādhiṣṭhidaṃ*

> By reciting this mantra,
> Sincerely making the supreme vow,
> All classes of sentient beings
> Swiftly attain siddhi.

The practitioner, before the image of the honored one, sits in full-lotus, or half-lotus, or the auspicious posture, or even the universal-monarch posture, according to preference. Then visualize all Tathāgatas, all bodhisattvas, and the vajra assembly, arousing great compassion, rescuing and bringing peace and joy to all sentient beings, vowing that all beings may swiftly attain the siddhi of unsurpassed bodhi.

> Then form the Buddha Division Mudrā:
> Join the palms hollow at the center,
> Open the palms with the left thumb alongside the right index,
> The right thumb alongside the left,
> Concentrating on a single object.
> Contemplate the Buddha's excellent marks.
> Recite the mantra three times,
> Place at the crown, then release.

The Buddha Division Samaya Mantra:

*Oṃ tathāgatodbhavāya svāhā*

> By reciting and forming this mudrā,
> All of the Buddha Division assembly
> Empower the practitioner,
> Not violating their original vow.
> Next form the Lotus Division:
> Join the palms hollow at center,
> Slightly open the index and thumb of the left,
> And visualize Avalokiteśvara,
> Fully marked, holding a lotus,
> Abiding in yoga samādhi.
> Recite clearly three times,
> Release at the crown to the right.

The Lotus Division Samaya Mantra:

*Oṃ padmodbhavāya svāhā*

> By reciting and forming the mudrā,
> All of the Lotus Division
> Holy assembly gather like clouds,
> Empowering by their original vow.
> Next form the Vajra Division:
> Cross the hands inward,
> The remaining fingers in three-pronged form.
> In the mind, visualize Vajrapāṇi
> With awesome power, holding the vajra,
> Fully marked, body adorned.
> Recite three times,
> Release at the crown to the left.

The Vajra Division Samaya Mantra:

*Oṃ vajrodbhavāya svāhā*

> By reciting and forming the mudrā,
> All the vajra-holders
> Gather and appear before one,
> Granting wishes, not violating their vow.
> Next form the Armor Mudrā:
> Both hands interlaced inward,
> Index and ring fingers standing together,
> Thumbs like a vajra-shape.
> Forehead, shoulders, heart, and throat:
> Once at each of five places.
> Contemplate the body's awesome radiance,
> Blazing and pervading all around.
> All māras and obstructors
> Scatter and flee, not daring to look.

The Vajra Armor Mantra:

*Oṃ vajrāgni pradipta padāya svāhā*

> By forming the Armor Mudrā,
> One is far removed from all obstacles,
> Able to block the gate to evil destinies,
> And also protect all sentient beings.
> Next form the Vajra Wheel,
> The mudrā of great awesome power.
> Both hands interlaced inward,
> Raise both index fingers and thumbs,
> The index fingers twined with the thumbs,
> The ring fingers joined and standing,
> Place the mudrā at the heart,
> And sincerely recite seven times.

The Vajra Wheel Mantra:

*Namas triyādhvikānāṃ sarvatathāgatānāṃ aṃ viraji viraji mahāvajra satva satva sārate sārate trāyi trāyi vidhamani sambhañjani tramati siddhāgri trāṃ svāhā*

> By reciting this mantra,
> It is as if one re-enters the Wheel-altar.
> Those who have lost mindfulness or broken samādhi,
> Whether bodhisattvas or śrāvakas,
> The two kinds of bodily and verbal discipline,
> The four grave offenses, the five acts of immediate retribution:
> All such karmic obstructions
> Are entirely purified.
> Next one should form the Earth Boundary:
> Cross the index and ring fingers,
> The thumbs, ring, and little fingers upright together.
> With the little fingers, press toward the ground.
> Tap three times, visualizing downward
> A blazing single-pronged vajra
> Penetrating to the vajra-foundation,
> Visualizing the removal of earth-faults.

The Vajra Stake Mantra:

*Oṃ kīli kīli vajra vajrī bhūr bandha bandha hūṃ phaṭ*

> By forming the Earth Mudrā,
> The entire ritual arena
> Becomes vajra ground.
> Māras find no opportunity.
> With the slightest merit of practice,
> One swiftly realizes samādhi.
> Body and mind are free of fatigue,
> Far removed from torpor.
> Next form the Vajra Wall:
> Based on the previous downward mudrā,
> Spread open and raise the little fingers.
> Circle rightward three times.
> Visualize a vajra wall,
> Blazing with awesome flames,
> Protecting the entire arena,
> Completing the directional boundary.

The Vajra Wall Mantra:

*Oṃ sara sara vajra prakāra hūṃ phaṭ*

> By forming the Wall Mudrā,
> All māras and obstructors,
> Vināyakas and the like,
> Scatter in all directions and flee.
> Next form the Great Ocean Mudrā:
> Both hands face-up and interlaced.
> This completes the Ocean Mudrā.
> Rotate it at the heart.
> Visualize the great ocean,
> Deep and vast without limit,
> Pure with eight excellent qualities,
> All born from the dharma-realm.

The Great Ocean Mantra:

*Oṃ vimalodadhi hūṃ*

> Next form the Sumeru Mudrā:
> Both hands interlaced inward, in fists.
> Recite the mantra three times.
> Visualize the Wondrous High Mountain
> Made of four jewels,
> Encircled by the seven golden mountains.
> On the mountaintop, visualize a pavilion
> Adorned with many jewels.

The Mount Sumeru Mantra:

*Oṃ acala hūṃ*

> Next form the Ākāśagarbha
> Vidyā-queen great secret mudrā:
> Both hands in vajra-bond,
> Index fingers in jewel-shape,
> Other fingers upright like banner-staffs,
> Left and right interlaced.
> This completes the offering ritual,
> To be cultivated in this sequence.
> Next visualize within the hall
> The honored one with retinue,
> Each in their flower-seat position.
> Perfumed ointment, flower garlands,
> Incense, wish-fulfilling lamps,
> *Arghya* and auspicious vases,
> Exquisite celestial food and drink,
> Jeweled pillars arrayed in rows.
> By the power of my merit,
> The Tathāgata's empowering force,
> And the power of the dharma-realm,
> I make universal offerings and abide.

The Ākāśagarbha Great Vidyā-Queen Mantra:

*Oṃ gaganasaṃbhava vajra hoḥ*

> By reciting and forming this mudrā,
> Ākāśagarbha, the honored one,
> Without exceeding his original vow-power,
> Makes all offerings become real.
> Next one should form the Jeweled Chariot:
> Both hands face-up and interlaced,
> The two thumbs bracing each other at the sides,
> The little fingers alongside the thumbs.
> Recite the mantra three times,
> And send it to the honored one's pure land.

The Chariot Mantra:

*Oṃ turu turu hūṃ*

> The practitioner, holding a censer,
> Clears the path through empty space.
> Recite the mantra three times,
> Shattering Māra's net.

The Mantra for Clearing the Path:

*Oṃ susiddhikāri javalita nanda-mukhyataye javala javala bandha bandha hana hana hūṃ phaṭ*

> Visualize a seven-jeweled chariot
> Adorned with a jeweled canopy,
> Silk pennants, jeweled bells,
> Pearl garlands interlacing everywhere.
> Immeasurable celestial music
> Plays spontaneously without being struck,
> All producing harmonious sounds.
> Visualize it reaching the Abhirati pure land.
> The honored one and retinue
> Board this jeweled chariot.
> Then form the mudrā to request the chariot:
> Based on the previous Chariot Mudrā,
> With the little fingers, push the index fingers,
> Visualizing the chariot arriving in the sky.

The Mantra for Requesting the Chariot:

*Namas triyādhvikānāṃ sarvatathāgatānāṃ oṃ vajrāṅkuśa ākarṣaya svāhā*

> Next form the Division-Heart Mudrā:
> Both hands interlaced inward,
> Left little finger beckons toward the body.
> Three times, adding the summoning phrase.

The Division-Heart Invitation Mantra:

*Oṃ vajradhṛk ehy ehi svāhā*

> By reciting this mantra,
> The honored one and retinue
> Joyfully come to the assembly,
> Granting wishes and accomplishment.
> Then form the Expulsion Mudrā:
> Both hands in vajra-shape.
> First raise the left hand
> And push outward to expel.
> All the māras
> Flee in terror.

The Expulsion Mantra:

*Oṃ kīli kīli vajra hūṃ phaṭ*

> Through recitation and expulsion,
> All māra-obstructors
> Flee and hide from the holy assembly,
> Scattering in all four directions.
> Then raise the right hand
> To display the samaya.
> The holy assembly remembers their former vows
> And comes again to the assembly.

The Samaya Display Mantra:

*Oṃ saṃkale samaya svāhā*

> Next form the Vajra Net:
> Based on the previous Vajra Wall,
> The little fingers press the thumb-sides,
> Rotate rightward above the crown.
> This creates a firm net.
> The māras above
> Cannot invade or harm.
> Practice swiftly attains accomplishment.

The Vajra Net Mantra:

*Oṃ visphūra-dara-rekṣa vajrapañjara hūṃ phaṭ*

> Then form the Sealed-Seam Mudrā:
> Left palm alongside right hand-back,
> Both little fingers extended straight.
> Recite the mantra three times,
> Circle rightward and up and down.
> Visualize vajra flames
> Sealing tight the directional boundary,
> Making the site awesome and powerful.

The Vajra Fire-Court Mantra:

*Oṃ asamāgni hūṃ phaṭ*

> Next, with a devout mind,
> Offer the *arghya* water.
> Hold the vessel before the forehead,
> Visualizing bathing the holy assembly.

The *Arghya* Offering Mantra:

*Namo samantabuddhānāṃ gagana-samāsama svāhā*

> Next one should offer the flower-seat:
> Both hands joined hollow,
> Index, ring, and thumbs slightly curved,
> Rotating with the mind.
> The honored one and retinue
> Are visualized seated on the flower-platforms,
> Each in their proper position.
> Contemplate them clearly and distinctly.

The Flower-Seat Mantra:

*Namo samantabuddhānāṃ aḥ*

> Next form the Perfume Mudrā:
> The right palm faces outward, upright.
> The left hand grasps the right wrist.
> Visualize clouds of perfumed ointment
> Anointing the ocean of holy assembly.

The Perfume Offering Mantra:

*Oṃ gandha-madhuṇi vara-ṇi-pratikṛhṇa svāhā*

> Upon forming the Perfume Mudrā,
> Throughout the mudrā-domain,
> Immeasurable perfume-celestial maidens,
> Each holding vessels of ointment,
> Throughout all boundless lands
> Make offerings to the Buddha and holy assembly.
> Before long, one shall attain
> The Dharma-body endowed with five divisions.
> Next form the Flower-Garland Mudrā:
> Both hands face-up and interlaced,
> Both thumbs curved like rings,
> The little fingers alongside the thumb-joints.
> Visualize offering flower-garlands
> To the holy retinue.

The Flower-Garland Offering Mantra:

*Oṃ māla-dhare vajradhara svāhā*

> Upon forming the Flower-Garland Mudrā,
> Throughout the mudrā-domain,
> Immeasurable flower-celestial maidens,
> Each holding garland vessels,
> Throughout all boundless lands
> Make offerings to the Buddha and holy assembly.
> Before long, one shall attain
> Freedom from defilement, like a lotus.
> Then form the Incense Mudrā:
> Both hands with palms face-up,
> Thumbs, index, and ring fingers upright with backs touching,
> The middle and little fingers bracing each other.
> Visualize clouds of burning incense
> Offered to the holy retinue.

The Incense Offering Mantra:

*Oṃ dhūpa-śikhe kuruvajriṇi svāhā*

> Upon forming the Incense Mudrā,
> Throughout the mudrā-domain,
> Immeasurable incense-celestial maidens,
> Each holding seven-jeweled censers,
> Throughout all boundless lands
> Make offerings to the Buddha and holy assembly.
> Before long, one shall attain
> The Tathāgata's unobstructed wisdom.
> Next form the Food Offering Mudrā:
> Both hands joined hollow,
> The little fingers alongside the thumbs,
> In the shape of a food vessel.
> Visualize clouds of food and drink
> Offered to the holy retinue.

The Food Offering Mantra:

*Oṃ mara mara megha maghi-ni-pratikṛhṇa vajriṇi svāhā*

> Upon forming the Food Offering Mudrā,
> Throughout the mudrā-domain,
> Immeasurable celestial maidens,
> Each holding jeweled food vessels,
> Throughout all boundless lands
> Make offerings to the Buddha and holy assembly.
> Before long, one shall attain
> The food of dharma-joy and dhyāna-bliss.
> Next form the Lamp Mudrā:
> The right hand makes a tight fist,
> Index finger upright, little finger alongside.
> Recite the mantra three times.
> Visualize wish-fulfilling jewel-lamps
> Offered to the holy retinue.

The Jewel-Lamp Offering Mantra:

*Oṃ javala-māle nidhi-paśike svāhā*

> Upon forming the Lamp Mudrā,
> Throughout the mudrā-domain,
> Immeasurable lamp-celestial maidens,
> Each holding wish-fulfilling lamps,
> Throughout all boundless lands
> Make offerings to the Buddha and holy assembly.
> Before long, one shall attain
> The pure five kinds of eye.
> Extend the mind to pervade
> All immeasurable Buddha-lands,
> Making manifold offerings:
> Boundless offering rites.
> Then form the Ākāśagarbha
> Great Bodhisattva Secret Mudrā:
> Both hands in vajra-bond,
> Both thumbs in jewel-shape,
> Index fingers like banner-staffs,
> Both little fingers joined and upright.
> Recite the mantra three times.

The Ākāśagarbha Mantra:

*Namas sarvatathāgatebhyo viśvamukhebhyaḥ sarvathākham udgate sphara hīmaṃ gaganakhaṃ svāhā*

> Then praise the honored one,
> His immeasurable merit-aggregation,
> Or recite the 108 Names,
> Making vocal offerings in song.
> Within the practitioner's body,
> At the heart, one should contemplate
> A perfectly full, pure moon-disk,
> Concentrating until it is clearly distinct.
> Above it, visualize a vajra,
> Golden, in the form of five wisdoms,
> Its radiance streaming forth
> Illuminating and touching boundless realms,
> Awakening the palace of Māra,
> Performing vast Buddha-activities.
> Through this samādhi,
> One becomes Akṣobhya Buddha,
> Fully marked, with the earth-touching mudrā,
> Surrounded by the retinue.
> Then form the Root Mudrā,
> Empowering the four places.

The Akṣobhya Tathāgata Mantra:

*Oṃ akṣobhya hūṃ*

> Next form the Māmakī mudrā,
> The Division-Mother, the greatly compassionate one.
> Both hands interlaced inward,
> Thumbs, index, and little fingers like needles.
> Three times, empower the honored one.
> Then protect one's own body,
> Each reciting once,
> Empowering the five places.

The Māmakī Mantra:

*Namo ratnatrayāya namas caṇḍa vajrapāṇaye mahāyakṣa-senāpataye oṃ kula-ndhari bandha bandha hūṃ phaṭ*

Next form the Tathāgata Akṣobhya Great Body Mudrā, and recite the root vidyā seven times. The Great Body Mantra:

*Namo bhagavate akṣobhyāya tathāgatāya arhate samyaksaṃbuddhāya tadyathā kākani kākani rocani rocani troṭani troṭani trāsani trāsani pratihānani pratihānani sarvakarma-param-pārāṇi me akṣobhya tu svāhā*

> Next one should purify the rosary:
> Both hands cradle the bead-garland.
> Empower three times, place on the crown.

The Rosary-Purification Mantra:

*Oṃ vairocana-mālā svāhā*

> Next form the Rosary-Holding Mudrā:
> Both hands in half-vajra.
> With this, hold the rosary.
> Recite the mantra three times.

The Rosary-Holding Mantra:

*Oṃ vasumatī śrīye padma maṅgala-ṇi svāhā*

> Then recite the honored one's vidyā.
> Before the body, contemplate the honored one's form.
> One's own body likewise.
> Concentrate, free of distraction.
> Or by the principle of true reality,
> Be in accord with the Dharma Body.
> The mantra syllables clearly distinct,
> Neither too slow nor too fast.
> Either a thousand or 108,
> One count always fixed.
> When recitation is complete,
> Cradle the rosary above the crown,
> And hand the count to the Division-Mother.
> Re-form the samaya,
> Recite the root vidyā three times,
> Then enter the syllable-wheel contemplation.
> Upon the moon-disk at the heart,
> Arrange the mantra syllables in a row,
> Golden, endowed with awesome radiance.
> Contemplate the principle of true reality.
> Contemplate the gate of the syllable *oṃ*:
> All dharmas are without outflow.
> Next contemplate the gate of the syllable *a*:
> All dharmas are fundamentally unborn.
> Third, the gate of the syllable *kṣo*:
> All dharmas are without exhaustion or cessation.
> Fourth, the gate of the syllable *bhya*:
> All dharmas are without self-nature.
> Fifth, the gate of the syllable *hūṃ*:
> All dharmas are without cause or condition.
> Each and every mantra syllable:
> Contemplate and illumine the dharma-realm nature,
> From beginning to end,
> Concentrating the mind without interruption.
> Re-form the Division-Mother Mudrā,
> Recite the mantra three times.
> With melodious singing voice,
> Praise the honored one's virtues.
> Re-form the five offerings,
> Presenting them to the honored one.
> Again offer the *arghya* water,
> Earnestly seeking one's original aspiration.
> According to the mind's upper, middle, or lower grade,
> One obtains siddhi according to the teaching.
> Then form the Outer Court Mudrā,
> Turn right to dissolve all boundaries.
> Next form the Jeweled Chariot Mudrā
> And the Division-Heart Mudrā,
> Sending off the honored one, each pushing outward.
> Re-form the Three Division Mudrās,
> Protect the body and the five repentances,
> All as performed before.
> Bow to the Buddha as one wishes.
> Read and recite the Vaipulya Vehicle.
> The ten practices of the Dharma invite
> Immeasurable, boundless merit.
> The sūtras, contemplate the six recollections,
> Each through the principle of true reality.
> One by one, contemplate them
> In accord with the yoga teaching.
> If one wishes to remove karmic obstacles,
> One should stamp Buddha-stūpas,
> Using sand or fragrant clay,
> Each containing the verse of dependent origination.
> Accumulate the number as the sūtras teach.
> At completion, wondrous signs will appear.
> Cultivating the recitation method,
> With this supreme field of merit,
> All sentient beings
> Swiftly become Akṣobhya Buddha.

The Sending-Off Mantra:

*Oṃ vajradhṛk yahi yahi svāhā*

> By reciting this mantra,
> The sending-off of the honored one is accomplished.

The Mantra for Removing Wilted Flowers:

*Oṃ hara hara graharaṇāya svāhā*

*Oṃ kālare mahākālare svāhā*
