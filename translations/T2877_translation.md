# 如來在金棺囑累清淨莊嚴敬福經
## Sūtra on the Tathāgata in the Golden Coffin, Entrusting, Pure, Adorned, and Venerating Merit

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 2877

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown (失譯)

---

Thus have I heard. At one time, the Buddha was between the twin Śāla trees in the city of Kuśinagara. Having finished expounding the nirvāṇa teaching, he was about to enter the golden coffin. Wishing to have his body cremated, he sat upon the railing of the golden coffin, and tears flowed from both his eyes as he emitted light and the earth trembled. The multitude of immeasurable great bodhisattvas, gods, dragons, and the eightfold assembly all gave rise to doubt.

At that time, Subhūti addressed the Buddha, saying: "World-Honored One, now wishing on behalf of the great assembly to reverently request the resolution of their doubt, I wonder whether you will grant this or not?"

The Buddha said: "Make whatever request you wish."

Subhūti addressed the Buddha, saying: "World-Honored One, the Tathāgata has always taught that nirvāṇa is permanent and blissful, and that there is forever no birth and death. How is it, then, that you now sit upon the railing of the golden coffin with tears streaming down your face, causing doubt to arise among the assembly?"

---

**Translator's note:** This text breaks off here, as the extant Chinese version (139 characters) concludes at this point without a response from the Buddha. The text appears to be a fragment of what would originally have been a longer exchange. The scene depicts the moment just after the parinirvāṇa teaching at Kuśinagara, with Subhūti's question, why does the Buddha weep if nirvāṇa is truly deathless?, left unanswered in the surviving text.
