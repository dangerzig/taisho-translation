# 佛為年少比丘說正事經
## Sūtra of the Buddha Teaching the Young Bhikṣus about Right Practice

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0502

Translated from the Chinese. Western Jin dynasty, translated by Faju (法炬)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was dwelling in the land of Śrāvastī, at the Jetavana Monastery of Anāthapiṇḍada, during the summer retreat. At that time, many senior śrāvaka disciples were observing the retreat in caves and beneath trees to the left and right of the World-Honored One. A number of young bhikṣus came to the Buddha, prostrated at the Buddha's feet, and sat to one side. The Buddha taught the young bhikṣus various dharmas, instructing, guiding, illuminating, and gladdening them. Having instructed, guided, illuminated, and gladdened them, he remained silent. The young bhikṣus, hearing the Buddha's teaching, rejoiced and were delighted. They rose from their seats, made obeisance, and departed.

The young bhikṣus then went to the senior bhikṣus, prostrated at their feet, and sat to one side. At that time, the senior bhikṣus had this thought: "We should take these young bhikṣus under our care: perhaps one person taking charge of one, or one person taking charge of two, three, or many." Having thought this, they did so: some took charge of one person, some of two, three, or many. Some senior bhikṣus took charge of as many as sixty persons.

Then, on the fifteenth day, at the time of the poṣadha observance, the World-Honored One took his seat before the great assembly. At that time, the World-Honored One surveyed the bhikṣus and told them: "Excellent! Excellent! I am now pleased that the bhikṣus are practicing right conduct. Therefore, bhikṣus! You should exert yourselves with diligence."

In the land of Śrāvastī, when the month of Kārttika was complete, the bhikṣus who had been traveling among the people in various places heard that the World-Honored One had completed the summer retreat in Śrāvastī during the month of Kārttika. Having finished their robes, they took up their robes and bowls and traveled from place to place among the people, gradually arriving at Śrāvastī. They put away their robes and bowls, washed their feet, went to the World-Honored One, prostrated at his feet, and sat to one side. At that time, the World-Honored One taught these traveling bhikṣus various dharmas. Having instructed, guided, illuminated, and gladdened them, he remained silent.

At that time, the traveling bhikṣus, hearing the Buddha's teaching, rejoiced and were delighted. They rose from their seats, made obeisance, and departed. They went to the senior bhikṣus, prostrated at their feet, and sat to one side. At that time, the senior bhikṣus had this thought: "We should take these traveling bhikṣus under our care: perhaps one person taking charge of one, or two, three, or many." They then did so: some took charge of one person, some of two, three, or even as many as sixty. The senior bhikṣus, having taken charge of the traveling bhikṣus, instructed and guided them, skillfully knowing the proper sequence and order.

Then, on the fifteenth day, at the time of the poṣadha observance, the World-Honored One took his seat before the great assembly. Surveying the bhikṣus, he told them: "Excellent! Excellent! Bhikṣus, I am delighted by the right conduct you have practiced, and by the Dharma-delighting right conduct you have practiced. Bhikṣus! The Buddhas of the past also had assemblies of bhikṣus whose right conduct was just like that of this present assembly. The Buddhas of the future will also have assemblies whose right conduct will be just like that of this present assembly. Why? Because in this present assembly, among the elder bhikṣus, there are those who have attained the first dhyāna, the second dhyāna, the third dhyāna, the fourth dhyāna, loving-kindness, compassion, joy, and equanimity, the base of infinite space, the base of infinite consciousness, the base of nothingness, and the base of neither-perception-nor-non-perception, abiding in full attainment. There are bhikṣus who, having exhausted the three fetters, have attained the fruit of stream-entry, are no longer destined for the evil paths, are certain to progress toward the three kinds of bodhi, and after seven rebirths among devas and humans will reach the end of suffering. There are bhikṣus who, having exhausted the three fetters and weakened craving, aversion, and delusion, have attained the fruit of once-returning. There are bhikṣus who, having exhausted the five lower fetters, have attained the fruit of non-returning, will attain parinirvāṇa in a higher realm, and will not return to be born in this world. There are bhikṣus who have attained immeasurable spheres of supernatural power, the divine ear, the knowledge of others' minds, the knowledge of past lives, the knowledge of birth and death, and the knowledge of the exhaustion of outflows. There are bhikṣus who cultivate the contemplation of impurity to sever craving, who cultivate the mind of loving-kindness to sever aversion, who cultivate the perception of impermanence to sever the conceit of self, and who cultivate mindfulness of breathing to sever discursive thought. How does a bhikṣu cultivate mindfulness of breathing to sever discursive thought? A bhikṣu, relying on a village, up to and including observing cessation with the out-breath, and the awareness of observing cessation with the out-breath: this is called cultivating mindfulness of breathing to sever discursive thought."

When the Buddha had finished speaking this sūtra, all the bhikṣus, hearing the Buddha's teaching, joyfully practiced it.
