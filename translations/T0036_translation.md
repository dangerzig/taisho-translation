# 本相猗致經
## 本相猗致經

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0036

Translated from the Chinese. 後漢 安世高譯

---

## Fascicle 1

Thus have I heard.

At one time, the Buddha was dwelling in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍada's Park, in the country of Śrāvastī. The Buddha then addressed the bhikkhus: "Originally, craving for existence was neither seen nor understood. Now it is seen that existence arises from craving for existence. If this original craving for existence did not exist, neither would the present existence arise — this is now clearly seen. From this fundamental condition, craving for existence is brought about. Craving for existence, O bhikkhus, arises from a cause; it does not arise without a cause. And what, O bhikkhus, is the cause from which craving for existence arises? It is said to be delusion.

"Delusion also, O bhikkhus, arises from a cause; it does not arise without a cause. And what, O bhikkhus, is the cause from which delusion arises? It is said to be the five hindrances.

"The five hindrances also, O bhikkhus, arise from a cause; they do not arise without a cause. And what, O bhikkhus, is the cause from which the five hindrances arise? It is said to be the three unwholesome actions.

"The three unwholesome actions also, O bhikkhus, arise from a cause; they do not arise without a cause. And what, O bhikkhus, is the cause from which the three unwholesome actions arise? It is said to be non-restraint of the sense faculties.

"Non-restraint of the sense faculties also, O bhikkhus, arises from a cause; it does not arise without a cause. And what, O bhikkhus, is the cause from which non-restraint of the sense faculties arises? It is said to be the absence of right mindfulness.

"The absence of right mindfulness also, O bhikkhus, arises from a cause; it does not arise without a cause. And what, O bhikkhus, is the cause from which the absence of right mindfulness arises? It is said to be lack of faith.

"Lack of faith also, O bhikkhus, arises from a cause; it does not arise without a cause. And what, O bhikkhus, is the cause from which lack of faith arises? It is said to be the hearing of false and non-Dhamma teachings.

"The hearing of non-Dhamma teachings also, O bhikkhus, arises from a cause; it does not arise without a cause. And what, O bhikkhus, is the cause from which the hearing of non-Dhamma teachings arises? It is said to be association with ignoble persons.

"Ignoble persons also arise from a cause; they do not arise without a cause. And what, O bhikkhus, is the cause giving rise to ignoble persons? It is said to be delight in gathering together with ignoble persons.

"Thus, O bhikkhus, when one has fully gathered with the ignoble, the conduct of the ignoble becomes complete; when the conduct of the ignoble is complete, hearing of non-Dhamma teachings becomes complete; when hearing of non-Dhamma teachings is complete, lack of faith becomes complete; when lack of faith is complete, absence of right mindfulness becomes complete; when absence of right mindfulness is complete, non-restraint of the sense faculties becomes complete; when non-restraint of the sense faculties is complete, transgression through the three unwholesome actions becomes complete; when transgression through the three unwholesome actions is complete, the five hindrances become complete; when the five hindrances are complete, delusion becomes complete; when delusion is complete, worldly craving for existence becomes complete. Thus, when craving and attachment are complete, they accumulate and increase, step by gradual step.

"Transcendent wisdom and liberation also have a cause; they do not arise without a cause. And what is the cause of transcendent wisdom and liberation? It is said that the seven factors of awakening are the cause.

"The seven factors of awakening arise from a cause; they do not arise without a cause. And what is the cause from which the seven factors of awakening arise? It is said to be the four foundations of mindfulness.

"The four foundations of mindfulness arise from a cause; they do not arise without a cause. And what, O bhikkhus, is the cause from which the four foundations of mindfulness arise? It is said to be the three purities of conduct.

"The three purities of conduct also, O bhikkhus, arise from a cause; they do not arise without a cause. And what, O bhikkhus, is the cause of the three purities? It is said to be guarding and restraining the sense faculties.

"Guarding and restraining the sense faculties also arises from a cause; it does not arise without a cause. And what, O bhikkhus, is the cause from which guarding and restraining of the sense faculties arises? It is said to be right mindfulness.

"Right mindfulness also, O bhikkhus, has a cause; it does not arise without a cause. And what, O bhikkhus, is the cause of right mindfulness? It is said to be faith as the foundation.

"Faith as the foundation also, O bhikkhus, has a cause; it does not arise without a cause. And what, O bhikkhus, is the cause of faith as the foundation? It is said to be hearing the Dhamma-sūtras as the cause.

"Hearing the Dhamma-sūtras also has a cause; it does not arise without a cause. And what is the cause of hearing the Dhamma-sūtras? It is said to be serving noble persons as the cause.

"Serving noble persons also has a cause; it does not arise without a cause. And what is the cause of serving noble persons? It is said to be the gathering of noble persons as the cause.

"Thus, O bhikkhus, by gathering with noble persons one is able to serve noble persons; having served noble persons, one then hears the words of the Dhamma; having heard the words of the Dhamma, one then establishes the foundation of faith; having established the foundation of faith, one then attains right mindfulness; having attained right mindfulness, one then guards and restrains the sense faculties; having guarded and restrained the sense faculties, one then attains the three purities of conduct; having attained the three purities of conduct, one then attains the four foundations of mindfulness; having attained the four foundations of mindfulness, one then has the seven factors of awakening; having the seven factors of awakening, one then attains unconditioned liberation and transcends the world. Thus liberation and transcendence of the world, step by step, through their causes, lead to the full attainment of transcendence of the world."

The Buddha having spoken thus, the disciples received and practiced his teaching.
