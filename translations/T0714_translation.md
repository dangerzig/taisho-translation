# 緣起聖道經
## Sūtra of Dependent Arising and the Noble Path

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0714

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Xuanzang (玄奘)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Bhagavān was in the land of Śrāvastī, dwelling in the Jeta Grove of Anāthapiṇḍada, together with a great assembly of bhikṣus, twelve hundred and fifty in number, as well as bodhisattva mahāsattvas and an immeasurable great assembly.

At that time the World-Honored One addressed the great assembly: "Before I had realized the three forms of bodhi, while I was alone in a deserted place, sitting silently in meditation, I gave rise to this thought: 'How extraordinary! The beings of the world have sunk into the sea of suffering, yet they are entirely unaware of the dharma of liberation. This is deeply worthy of compassion. For although there is birth, old age, and death, disappearing here and appearing there, sentient beings are unable to truly know the dharma of liberation from birth, old age, and death.'

"I further reflected: 'Because of what is there old age and death? And what is the condition for old age and death?' When I thought about this in accordance with principle, there arose this true direct insight: 'Because there is birth, there is old age and death. Thus old age and death has birth as its condition.'

"I further reflected: 'Because of what is there birth? And what is the condition for birth?' When I thought about this in accordance with principle, there arose this true direct insight: 'Because there is becoming, there is birth. Thus birth has becoming as its condition.'

"I further reflected: 'Because of what is there becoming? And what is the condition for becoming?' When I thought about this in accordance with principle, there arose this true direct insight: 'Because there is grasping, there is becoming. Thus becoming has grasping as its condition.'

"I further reflected: 'Because of what is there grasping? And what is the condition for grasping?' When I thought about this in accordance with principle, there arose this true direct insight: 'Because there is craving, there is grasping. Thus grasping has craving as its condition.'

"I further reflected: 'Because of what is there craving? And what is the condition for craving?' When I thought about this in accordance with principle, there arose this true direct insight: 'Because there is feeling, there is craving. Thus craving has feeling as its condition.'

"I further reflected: 'Because of what is there feeling? And what is the condition for feeling?' When I thought about this in accordance with principle, there arose this true direct insight: 'Because there is contact, there is feeling. Thus feeling has contact as its condition.'

"I further reflected: 'Because of what is there contact? And what is the condition for contact?' When I thought about this in accordance with principle, there arose this true direct insight: 'Because there are the six sense bases, there is contact. Thus contact has the six sense bases as its condition.'

"I further reflected: 'Because of what are there the six sense bases? And what is the condition for the six sense bases?' When I thought about this in accordance with principle, there arose this true direct insight: 'Because there is name-and-form, there are the six sense bases. Thus the six sense bases have name-and-form as their condition.'

"I further reflected: 'Because of what is there name-and-form? And what is the condition for name-and-form?' When I thought about this in accordance with principle, there arose this true direct insight: 'Because there is consciousness, there is name-and-form. Thus name-and-form has consciousness as its condition.'

"At this point my mind turned back from consciousness and did not proceed further, meaning: consciousness is the condition for name-and-form; name-and-form is the condition for the six sense bases; the six sense bases are the condition for contact; contact is the condition for feeling; feeling is the condition for craving; craving is the condition for grasping; grasping is the condition for becoming; becoming is the condition for birth; because of birth as the condition, there is old age and death, sorrow, lamentation, suffering, grief, and vexation. Thus the purely great mass of suffering accumulates.

"I further reflected: 'When what is absent is old age and death absent? And by the cessation of what does old age and death cease?' When I thought about this in accordance with principle, there arose this true direct insight: 'When there is no birth, there is no old age and death. When birth ceases, old age and death ceases.'

"I further reflected: 'When what is absent is birth absent? And by the cessation of what does birth cease?' When I thought about this in accordance with principle, there arose this true direct insight: 'When there is no becoming, there is no birth. When becoming ceases, birth ceases.'

"I further reflected: 'When what is absent is becoming absent? And by the cessation of what does becoming cease?' When I thought about this in accordance with principle, there arose this true direct insight: 'When there is no grasping, there is no becoming. When grasping ceases, becoming ceases.'

"I further reflected: 'When what is absent is grasping absent? And by the cessation of what does grasping cease?' When I thought about this in accordance with principle, there arose this true direct insight: 'When there is no craving, there is no grasping. When craving ceases, grasping ceases.'

"I further reflected: 'When what is absent is craving absent? And by the cessation of what does craving cease?' When I thought about this in accordance with principle, there arose this true direct insight: 'When there is no feeling, there is no craving. When feeling ceases, craving ceases.'

"I further reflected: 'When what is absent is feeling absent? And by the cessation of what does feeling cease?' When I thought about this in accordance with principle, there arose this true direct insight: 'When there is no contact, there is no feeling. When contact ceases, feeling ceases.'

"I further reflected: 'When what is absent is contact absent? And by the cessation of what does contact cease?' When I thought about this in accordance with principle, there arose this true direct insight: 'When there are no six sense bases, there is no contact. When the six sense bases cease, contact ceases.'

"I further reflected: 'When what is absent are the six sense bases absent? And by the cessation of what do the six sense bases cease?' When I thought about this in accordance with principle, there arose this true direct insight: 'When there is no name-and-form, there are no six sense bases. When name-and-form ceases, the six sense bases cease.'

"I further reflected: 'When what is absent is name-and-form absent? And by the cessation of what does name-and-form cease?' When I thought about this in accordance with principle, there arose this true direct insight: 'When there is no consciousness, there is no name-and-form. When consciousness ceases, name-and-form ceases.'

"I further reflected: 'When what is absent is consciousness absent? And by the cessation of what does consciousness cease?' When I thought about this in accordance with principle, there arose this true direct insight: 'When there are no formations, there is no consciousness. When formations cease, consciousness ceases.'

"I further reflected: 'When what is absent are formations absent? And by the cessation of what do formations cease?' When I thought about this in accordance with principle, there arose this true direct insight: 'When there is no ignorance, there are no formations. When ignorance ceases, formations cease. When formations cease, consciousness also ceases. When consciousness ceases, name-and-form ceases. When name-and-form ceases, the six sense bases cease. When the six sense bases cease, contact also ceases. When contact ceases, feeling also ceases. When feeling ceases, craving also ceases. When craving ceases, grasping also ceases. When grasping ceases, becoming also ceases. When becoming ceases, birth also ceases. When birth ceases, old age and death, sorrow, lamentation, suffering, grief, and vexation all cease. Thus the purely great mass of suffering is forever extinguished.'

"I further reflected: 'I have now realized the ancient path, the ancient trail, the ancient footprints that the sages of old once traveled.'

"It is like a person wandering through a vast, overgrown wilderness of tangled forest who suddenly comes upon an ancient path, an ancient trail, ancient footprints that people of old once traveled. He follows along. Having followed along, he sees an ancient city, a royal capital of old, with gardens, pools, and ponds complete in every way, with pure and lovely streets most delightful to behold. That person, seeing this, thinks: 'I should now go swiftly to the king and report this matter.' Then that person goes to the king and reports: 'May the great king know that I had occasion to wander through the vast, overgrown wilderness of tangled forest, and I suddenly came upon an ancient path, an ancient trail, ancient footprints that people of old once traveled. I followed along. Having followed along, I saw an ancient city, a royal capital of old, with gardens, pools, and ponds complete in every way, with pure and lovely streets most delightful to behold. Great king! If you were now to make your capital in that city, it would surely bring your majesty's realm to great prosperity, security, abundance, happiness, and a flourishing populace.' The king then made his capital in that city. Afterward the royal capital did indeed grow into great prosperity, security, abundance, happiness, and a flourishing populace.

"I too am like this. I have now realized the ancient path, the ancient trail, the ancient footprints that the sages of old once traveled. What is this ancient path, ancient trail, ancient footprints that the sages of old once traveled? Know that it is the noble Eightfold Path: first, right view; then right thought, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, and right concentration, up to the eighth. This is called the ancient path, the ancient trail, the ancient footprints that the sages of old once traveled. I followed this path of old. Having followed it, I saw old age and death, saw the accumulation of old age and death, saw the cessation of old age and death, and saw the footprints of the path leading to the cessation of old age and death. So too I saw birth, becoming, grasping, craving, feeling, contact, the six sense bases, name-and-form, consciousness, and formations. I saw the accumulation of formations, saw the cessation of formations, and saw the footprints of the path leading to the cessation of formations. Having naturally penetrated this dharma and realized direct, complete enlightenment, I proclaimed it to the bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, and upāsikās, and further proclaimed it to the various non-Buddhist śramaṇas, brahmins, and miscellaneous renunciants, as well as to an immeasurable great assembly. If these bhikṣus can rightly cultivate and accomplish realization within this, they will be able to realize the excellence of right principle and right Dharma. So too with the bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, upāsikās, and the immeasurable great assembly: if within this they can rightly cultivate and accomplish realization, they will be able to realize the excellence of right principle and right Dharma. In this way the holy life may be widely expanded, and it will benefit immeasurable sentient beings, rightly and skillfully revealing it for all devas and humans."

At that time the bhikṣus, the bodhisattva mahāsattvas, and the immeasurable great assembly, hearing what the Buddha had said, praised it as unprecedented. All were filled with great joy and faithfully received and practiced the teaching.
