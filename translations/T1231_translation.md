# 大輪金剛修行悉地成就及供養法
## Method of Practice, Siddhi Attainment, and Offerings of Mahācakra Vajra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1231

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown.

---

Based on the *Vajraśekhara Sūtra*, I briefly describe the sequential procedure of Mahācakra recitation practice. One should first prepare a clean room, adorn the practice hall, and establish the maṇḍala, with flower banners, streamers, canopies, burning incense, and scattered flowers. One should first receive the abhiṣeka from a master; only afterward, having personally received the teaching of the recitation method, may one begin to practice. Three times daily, each session consisting of 1,080 recitations, without variation. When the full count of 300,000 is reached, one attains siddhi. Each time before entering the practice hall, first visualize the syllable *hūṃ* in one's heart. Contemplate the syllable transforming into a great vajra, and then enter the practice hall. Arriving before the presiding deity, stand upright with palms joined and repent with all sincerity, reflecting: "From beginningless time I have transmigrated in birth and death, not awakening to true reality, constantly dwelling in the three realms. I now repent all my karmic obstructions, and pray that they may all be extinguished." Then recite the mantra for repentance and removal of transgressions:

*Oṃ sarva-pāpa-visphūṭa-dahana vajrāya svāhā.*

Through the empowering force of reciting this mantra, one obtains the samādhi of purity of the three karmas.

Next, join the palms above the crown and recite the mantra of universal homage to all buddhas and the holy assembly:

*Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-kāya-vāk-citta-pūjana-karomi.*

> Homage to the Equally and Perfectly Awakened Ones of the ten directions,
> All buddhas of the three times who possess the three bodies.
> Homage to all the great Mahāyāna dharmas,
> Homage to the assembly of non-retrogressing bodhisattvas,
> Homage to all the vidyās and true words,
> Homage to all the secret mudrās.
> May you with compassion guard and remember me,
> And deliver me from the mire of birth and death.
> My fleshly eyes cannot see or know;
> The eye of the path sees all; to it I take refuge.

The refuge mantra:

*Namo diśānāṃ buddha-koṭīnāṃ oṃ huru huru siddha-locanī sarva-artha-sādhanī svāhā.*

> From past lives onward,
> Transmigrating in the cycle of birth and death,
> Indulging body, speech, and mind in wrong karma,
> Through jealousy and miserliness,
> And wrong views that obscured the mind,
> I transgressed the samaya.
> Now before the great holy lord,
> I repent with all my heart.
> As the former buddhas repented,
> So too do I now.
> May the power of empowerment
> Purify all sentient beings.
>
> Join both hands in a hollow clasp,
> Visualize the body as lapis lazuli,
> Luminous and translucent within and without.
> Through the three mysteries of vajra,
> Empower and become the presiding deity.
> Next, with the purification-of-three-karmas mudrā,
> Seal the five places in turn,
> Accomplishing the pure ablution.

The mantra:

*Oṃ svabhāva-śuddha sarva-dharma svabhāva-śuddho 'haṃ.*

> Place the vajra palms atop the crown,
> Extend the mind throughout the dharma-realm.
> Personally before the buddhas of the ten directions,
> The bodhisattvas, and the vajra assembly,
> Prostrate with the whole body touching the ground.
>
> I purify this body, free from all defilement,
> Together with the body, speech, and mind of the three times.
> Surpassing the dust-motes of great ocean-realms in number,
> I offer them all to every tathāgata.

The mantra:

*Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-pūjā-pravarttanāya ātmānaṃ niryātayāmi sarva-tathāgata-śca-adhitiṣṭhantu sarva-tathāgata-jñāna-me āviśatu.*

> Sit upright, still the body and mind,
> With right mindfulness contemplate the presiding deity.
> First form the samaya mudrā:
> Samādhi and wisdom hands joined in a hollow clasp,
> Offering and power fingers alongside the patience and vow fingers,
> Pressing the back at the first joint.
> Dhyāni and wisdom fingers alongside the offering and power fingers,
> Each pinching the lower joint.
> Contemplate all tathāgatas
> With marks and signs clearly manifest.

The buddha-division samaya mantra:

*Oṃ tathāgatodbhavāya svāhā.*

> Scatter from the crown seven times.
> Through this one can alert and awaken
> The buddhas, who come to guard and remember.
> One obtains purity of bodily karma.
>
> Next, present the lotus division:
> As before, join both hands,
> Spread and extend the remaining six fingers,
> Slightly bent, forming an eight-petaled lotus.
> Secretly visualize Avalokiteśvara,
> Whose marks and signs are supremely dignified,
> Together with immeasurable koṭis
> Of the lotus-family holy assembly
> Surrounding and empowering.
> One obtains purity of verbal karma.

The mantra:

*Oṃ padmodbhavāya svāhā.*

> The vajra samaya mudrā:
> Left hand face-down over the right hand face-up,
> Fingertips touching each other's backs.
> Thumbs and little fingers, dhyāni and wisdom fingers,
> Each crossed in reverse.
> The rest are like a three-pronged vajra.
> Visualize Vajragarbha,
> Wrathful and blazing with radiant light.
> Contemplate the vajra family
> And retinue surrounding.
> Through this empowerment,
> One obtains purity of mental karma.

The mantra:

*Oṃ vajrodbhavāya svāhā.*

> Next, don the armor:
> Merit and wisdom hands crossed internally,
> Patience and vow fingers erect and joined,
> Offering and power fingers bent like hooks,
> Dhyāni and wisdom fingers press the precept fingers.
> Seal the five places to form armor.
> Extending compassion to all sentient beings,
> Swiftly delivering them from obstacles and difficulties.
> All māras and evil persons
> Cannot gain any advantage.

The mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-agni-praṇapta-pādāya svāhā.*

> Next form the earth-boundary mudrā:
> With the precept and patience fingers
> Placed between the vow and square fingers,
> Each wound into the palm.
> Thumbs, little fingers, offering, and power fingers,
> With dhyāni and wisdom fingers each joined,
> Forming a shape like a vajra.
> Dhyāni and wisdom fingers press the ground.
> Through the empowerment of mudrā and mantra,
> Down to the vajra-wheel below,
> An indestructible boundary is established,
> A vajra adamantine seat.
> Heavenly māras cannot move it;
> Defilement and evil become pure.

The mantra:

*Oṃ kiri kiri vajra-bhūr bandha bandha hūṃ phaṭ.*

> Next form the vajra wall:
> Following the previous earth-boundary mudrā,
> Open the palms, erect the dhyāni and wisdom fingers.
> From the mudrā, blazing flames flow forth,
> Circling rightward three times,
> Establishing an indestructible boundary.

The mantra:

*Oṃ sara sara vajra-prahāra hūṃ phaṭ.*

> Next form the "initial arousal of intent"
> Turning-the-dharma-wheel secret mudrā:
> Each hand in a vajra fist,
> Offering and power fingers with thumbs and little fingers hooked.
> Through this empowerment,
> Body and vessel become purified,
> Forming a dharma-maṇḍala.
> Touch the mudrā to the altar
> And it becomes the vajra-division
> Subjugation maṇḍala.
> Touch the mudrā to the presiding deity's image
> And place it in space;
> All become maṇḍalas.
> If the practitioner transgresses the method
> Or inadvertently breaks the samaya,
> Through the empowering force of the mantra,
> The precept-ranks are restored to perfect purity.

The mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-cakra hūṃ jaḥ hūṃ vaṃ hoḥ.*

> Next form the Ākāśagarbha
> Vast offering mudrā:
> Join the samādhi and wisdom hands,
> Patience and vow fingers crossed externally,
> Offering and power fingers forming a maṇi-jewel.
> From the mudrā flow forth all offerings:
> Garments, food and drink in clouds,
> Palaces and multi-storied pavilions,
> Incense, flowers, and music,
> Various jeweled streamers and banners,
> Clouds and oceans throughout the ten directions,
> Becoming true offerings,
> Just as in the realm of Sukhāvatī.
> Within the altar, the syllable *hrīḥ*
> Radiates great radiance,
> Like the color of red crystal,
> Illuminating the buddha-lands of the ten directions.
> Those who encounter this radiance
> Have all karmic obstructions entirely extinguished.
> Through the power of my merit,
> The empowering power of the tathāgatas,
> And the power of the dharma-realm,
> I make universal offerings and abide.

The mantra:

*Oṃ gagana-saṃbhava vajra hoḥ.*

> Next form the chariot mudrā:
> Both hands face-up and crossed,
> Each dhyāni and wisdom finger
> Pinching the lower joint of the offering and power finger.
> A chariot of the seven jewels appears,
> Proceeding to the vajra palace
> To invite the presiding deity and retinue
> To ride this jeweled chariot.

The sending-chariot mantra:

*Oṃ turu turu hūṃ.*

> Without releasing the previous mudrā form,
> With the dhyāni and wisdom fingers
> Pull the offering and power fingers toward the body;
> This becomes the invitation chariot.

The mantra:

*Namaḥ stīrya-divikānāṃ tathāgatānāṃ oṃ vajra-aṅga ākarṣaya svāhā.*

> The chariot rests in space.
> Now welcome all the holy assembly.
> Merit and wisdom hands in internal-bond fists,
> Wisdom finger in a hook shape,
> Drawn toward the body three times to summon
> The presiding deity and retinue.
> Not forsaking their original vow,
> They answer the invitation and descend to the practice hall.

The mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-ādhṛk mahā-cakra-vajre ehyehi svāhā.*

> Next form the Trailokyavijaya
> Wrathful vajra mudrā:
> Both hands in vajra fists,
> Thumbs and little fingers hooked in reverse,
> Offering and power fingers erect at the heart.
> In the mind, visualize becoming the presiding deity.
> The right foot treads upon Maheśvara,
> The left treads upon Umā.
> The entire body blazes with fierce flames.
> Seal the heart, forehead, throat, and crown.
> Circling leftward dispels obstacles;
> Turning rightward fortifies the boundary.
> Wrathful on all four sides,
> Eight arms holding weapons,
> Standing in a T-shaped stance.
> Entering this samādhi,
> One should abide in the mind of enlightenment,
> Deeply arousing compassion,
> Extinguishing inner and outer obstacles.

The mantra:

*Oṃ sumbha-niṣumbha hūṃ gṛhṇa gṛhṇa hūṃ gṛhṇa-paya hūṃ ānaya hoḥ bhagavan vajra hūṃ phaṭ.*

> The upper vajra net:
> Use the previous earth-boundary mudrā.
> Dhyāni and wisdom fingers pinch the offering and power fingers.
> Circle three times above the crown,
> Forming the vajra adamantine net.

The mantra:

*Oṃ viṣphurad-rakṣa vajra-pañjara hūṃ hūṃ phaṭ.*

> The vajra fire-enclosure boundary:
> Left hand covers the back of the right hand.
> Erect the dhyāni and wisdom fingers
> And visualize blazing, fierce flames.
> From the mudrā, radiance streams forth,
> Circling rightward outside the walls,
> With three layers of fire encircling.
> Even the assemblies of buddhas and bodhisattvas
> Would not transgress this boundary;
> How much less could heavenly māras
> Approach to cause disturbance!

The mantra:

*Oṃ asamāgni-hara hūṃ phaṭ.*

> Next offer the arghya mudrā,
> Visualizing bathing the feet of the holy assembly.
> This enables the practitioner
> To obtain purity of the three karmas.

The mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ gagana-samasama svāhā.*

> Next offer the lotus seat:
> Join the palms and extend the precept and square fingers,
> Together with the patience, vow, offering, and power fingers,
> Rounded like an eight-petaled lotus.
> Visualize from the mudrā flowing forth
> Immeasurable vajra flowers,
> Offered to all the holy lords.

The mantra:

*Oṃ.*

> The vast Amoghavajra-rāja
> Maṇi offering mudrā:
> Join the vajra palms,
> Offering and power fingers pressed together like a jewel.
> Upon reciting the secret mantra,
> Throughout the ten directions,
> A great rain of offerings falls universally:
> Clouds of paste-incense, flowers, and garlands,
> Clouds of burning incense and garments,
> Maṇi-jewel radiant lamps,
> Clouds of streamers, banners, and jeweled canopies,
> Various celestial music,
> Pervading all buddha-lands,
> Becoming true offerings.
> One obtains immeasurable aggregations of merit,
> Boundless as space,
> Always born in the assemblies of the buddhas,
> Delivering all sentient beings.

The mantra:

*Oṃ amogha-pūjā-maṇi-padma-vajri tathāgata-vilokite samanta-prasāra hūṃ.*

> With single-pointed attention contemplate the presiding deity,
> Clearly manifest as if before one's eyes,
> With a compassionate and wrathful countenance,
> Retinue surrounding,
> Generating admiration thought after thought.
> One obtains samādhi in the present.
>
> Offer a sincere vajra praise:

*Mahā-cakrāya caṇḍāya vidyā-rājāya sādhave dundanta-namakāyāya namaḥ-stu vajrapāṇaye.*

> All the holy ones rejoice.
> Visualize above the presiding deity's crown
> A vajra wheel
> Revolving in space.
> Below the seat, two vajra wheels;
> Next, the vajra-division mother;
> Next, Mahācakra Vajra.
>
> The root repentance mudrā:
> Precept and square fingers with offering and power fingers internally crossed,
> Thumbs, little fingers, patience, and vow fingers, and wisdom fingers joined,
> Like a wheel with three-cornered blazing flames.
> Dhyāni and wisdom fingers at the crown, requesting joyful acceptance.
>
> I have now performed the protection according to the method;
> Where there have been infractions of the samaya,
> Secretly holding the Soma Vajra mantra,
> I repent the transgressions of the four times.

The Mahācakra Vajra mantra:

*Namaḥ stīrya-divikānāṃ sarva-tathāgatānāṃ oṃ viraji viraji mahā-cakra-vajri sata sata sārati sārati trayi trayi vidhamani saṃbhañjani tramati siddha-agriye tāṃ svāhā.*

> Next clap the vajra palms
> To make the holy assembly rejoice.

The mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-tuṣya hoḥ.*

> Both hands hold up the rosary
> To the crown, then place it at the heart.

The mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-guhya japa-samaye hūṃ.*

The rosary-holding mantra:

*Oṃ kiri kiri budhiri ti svāhā.*

> Contemplate the wondrous mind of enlightenment,
> A perfectly bright and pure moon-disc.
> The secret syllable *aṃ*,
> Radiant with the light of pure gold.
> Whether walking, standing, sitting, or lying down,
> Always keep it manifest before one.
>
> Body and mind unmoving,
> Lightly observe all things.
> Recite in a soft voice that one's own ears can hear,
> Neither too slow nor too fast.
>
> If during recitation an urgent matter
> Requires leaving the practice hall,
> One should settle the holy assembly.

The verse:

> Respectfully invited to descend into samādhi,
> Not violating original vows, you pervade the world.
> The unfailing activities I temporarily set at rest;
> May the holy assembly return to perfect quiescence.

Then ring the vajra bell.

The mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-ghaṇṭā aḥ.*

> Leave with calm and deliberate steps.
> Upon entering and exiting, recite the *hūṃ* syllable mantra.
> When recitation is finished and one wishes to dismiss the assembly,
> Again form the universal offering.
> Next present the division-mother mantra
> And the entrusting mantra phrase.
>
> I now, for the sake of all,
> Pursue the fruit of awakening.
> May the great holy lord
> Fulfill my complete count.
>
> Always form the division-mother mudrā,
> Empowering with the hundred-syllable mantra
> To make the presiding deity rejoice.
> Circle the fire-enclosure leftward;
> This becomes the releasing of the great mudrā.

Recite the verse:

> The tathāgatas of the present world,
> The bodhisattvas who save the world,
> May the great Mahāyāna teaching never be cut off,
> And may all reach the supreme ground of wisdom.
> May the holy heavenly assembly
> Certainly bear witness for me.
> May each return to their proper abode,
> And hereafter again come in compassion.

Next recite the dismissal verse:

> Having accomplished this supreme benefit
> And bestowed it upon all sentient beings,
> May all the holy ones return to their native realms.
> Ring the bell, not violating the great compassionate vow.
> Then present the chariot and farewell mudrā.
>
> Next make the dedication of merit and vows.
> Next, the three-division body protection.
> Bow and repent according to the regular liturgy,
> Then go freely about one's activities.

---

*Colophon*: During the Kan'en era, second year, *tsuchinoto-mi* [1749], seventh month, an auspicious morning.
