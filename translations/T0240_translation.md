# 實相般若波羅蜜經
## Sūtra of the Prajñāpāramitā of True Reality

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0240

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Bodhiruci (菩提流志)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Bhagavān, having well accomplished the vajra true wisdom of all Tathāgatas, wearing the consecration jeweled crown adorned with various extraordinary features that transcend the three realms, in the sovereign unobstructed state of mahāyoga, having attained profound and subtle wisdom, having realized the Dharma of equality, having completed all works and deeds, satisfying the hearts of all sentient beings, being equal across the three periods of time, ever unmoving and indestructible, with the three karmas as firm as vajra, his body of universal radiance, was dwelling in the palace of the king of the Paranirmita-vaśavartin heaven in the desire realm. That royal palace was adorned with various splendors, all made of great maṇi jewels. Silk canopies, banners, and pennants of many colors reflected upon one another. Pearl garlands and jeweled bells sounded in the wind. All Tathāgatas ever frequent this place, all praising it as the most auspicious. Eighty million bodhisattva-mahāsattvas surrounded the Buddha in front and behind, making offerings with reverence. The Buddha taught them the Dharma, good in the beginning, middle, and end, profound in meaning, subtle in expression, pure and complete, unmixed and unalloyed.

Their names were: Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva, Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva, Dharmacakrapravartana Bodhisattva, and Sarvamārapramardin Bodhisattva. These bodhisattva-mahāsattvas and others served as the leaders.

At that time, the World-Honored One, within the great assembly, taught all the bodhisattvas the Dharma-gate of the Prajñāpāramitā of the True Reality of the Inherent Purity of All Dharmas. It states: "The pure station of desire is the bodhisattva's station. The pure station of seeing is the bodhisattva's station. The pure station of deep attachment is the bodhisattva's station. The pure station of delight is the bodhisattva's station. The pure station of the treasury is the bodhisattva's station. The pure station of adornment is the bodhisattva's station. The pure station of radiance is the bodhisattva's station. The pure station of body is the bodhisattva's station. The pure station of speech is the bodhisattva's station. The pure station of mind is the bodhisattva's station. The pure station of form is the bodhisattva's station. The pure station of sound is the bodhisattva's station. The pure station of smell is the bodhisattva's station. The pure station of taste is the bodhisattva's station. The pure station of touch is the bodhisattva's station. Why? Because the self-nature of all dharmas is inherently pure. The inherent purity of all dharmas is itself the purity of prajñāpāramitā."

At that time, when the World-Honored One had taught this Dharma-gate, he addressed Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva: "Vajrapāṇi! If there is anyone who hears this Dharma-gate of the Prajñāpāramitā of the True Reality of the Inherent Purity of All Dharmas, and it passes once through their ears, that person's obstructions of affliction, obstructions of karma, and obstructions of the Dharma, even the most grave offenses, shall all be extinguished of themselves, and until they reach enlightenment they shall not be born in the evil destinies. Furthermore, if there is a person who can daily receive, uphold, read, recite, contemplate, and practice it, in this very body they shall attain the vajra samādhi of the equality of all dharmas. In the remaining sixteen lifetimes, they shall attain sovereignty and joyful play in all Dharma-gates, and shall ultimately attain the vajra body of all Tathāgatas."

At that time, the Tathāgata then spoke the mantra:

*Hūṃ*

At that time, the World-Honored One, further manifesting the characteristic of the universal radiance of all Tathāgatas, taught all the bodhisattvas the Dharma-gate of the Prajñāpāramitā of the True Reality of the Quiescent Nature of All Buddhas' Attainment of true enlightenment. It states: "The equality of vajra attains true enlightenment, because the nature of great enlightenment is as firm as vajra. The equality of meaning attains true enlightenment, because great enlightenment is of a single meaning in nature. The equality of Dharma attains true enlightenment, because great enlightenment is inherently pure in self-nature. The equality of all attains true enlightenment, because great enlightenment is free from all discrimination."

At that time, when the World-Honored One had taught this Dharma-gate, he again addressed Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva: "Vajrapāṇi! If there is anyone who hears these four kinds of Dharma-gates of the Prajñāpāramitā of the True Reality of the Quiescent Nature of Attaining true enlightenment, and receives, upholds, reads, recites, contemplates, and practices them, one should know that this person has already transcended all evil destinies and shall swiftly realize the supreme, perfect enlightenment."

At that time, the Tathāgata again spoke the mantra:

*Oṃ*

At that time, the World-Honored One, further manifesting the characteristic of all Tathāgatas' ability to tame beings difficult to tame, the characteristic of Śākyamuni, taught all the bodhisattvas the Dharma-gate of the Prajñāpāramitā of the True Reality of the Equality of All Dharmas. It states: "Desire is without elaboration in nature. Anger is without elaboration in nature. Delusion is without elaboration in nature. Why? Because all dharmas are without elaboration in nature. The nature of all dharmas being without elaboration is itself the nature of prajñāpāramitā being without elaboration."

At that time, when the World-Honored One had taught this Dharma-gate, he again addressed Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva: "Vajrapāṇi! If there is anyone who hears this Dharma-gate of the Prajñāpāramitā of the True Reality of the Equality of All Dharmas, and receives, upholds, reads, recites, contemplates, and practices it, even if that person were to kill all sentient beings in the three realms, they would never on that account fall into the evil destinies. Why? Because they have already received the mental discipline of taming. One should know that this person shall swiftly attain the supreme, perfect enlightenment."

At that time, the Tathāgata again spoke the mantra:

*Haṃ*

At that time, the World-Honored One, further manifesting the characteristic of the inherent purity of all Tathāgatas, taught all the bodhisattvas the Dharma-gate of the Prajñāpāramitā of the True Reality of the Wisdom-Seal of Avalokiteśvara of the Equality of All Dharmas. It states: "The nature of desire in all worlds is pure, and the nature of anger is pure. Because the nature of desire and the nature of anger in all worlds are pure, the nature of defilement in all worlds is pure, and the nature of offense is pure. Because the nature of defilement and the nature of offense in all worlds are pure, the dharma-nature of all worlds is pure, and the nature of sentient beings is pure. Because the dharma-nature of all worlds and the nature of sentient beings are pure, the nature of wisdom in all worlds is pure. The purity of the nature of wisdom in all worlds is itself the purity of prajñāpāramitā."

At that time, when the World-Honored One had taught this Dharma-gate, he again addressed Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva: "Vajrapāṇi! If there is anyone who hears this Dharma-gate of the Prajñāpāramitā of the True Reality of the Wisdom-Seal of Avalokiteśvara of the Equality of All Dharmas, and receives, upholds, reads, recites, and practices it with right mindfulness, this person, though dwelling amid the five sense-desires, shall not be defiled by the faults of desire, just as a lotus, though growing in mud, is not stained by it, and shall swiftly attain the supreme, perfect enlightenment."

At that time, the Tathāgata again spoke the mantra:

*Hrīḥ*

At that time, the World-Honored One, further manifesting the characteristic of all Tathāgatas as lords of the three realms, taught all the bodhisattvas the Dharma-gate of the Prajñāpāramitā of the True Reality of the Treasury of Wisdom of Consecration and Manifestation of All Buddhas. It states: "The gift of consecration, causing all to attain the royal position of the three realms. The gift of material treasures, causing all to have their wishes fulfilled. The gift of the pure Dharma, causing all to attain the true nature of all dharmas. The gift of food and drink, causing all to attain ease and happiness of body and mind."

At that time, the Tathāgata again spoke the mantra:

*Trāṃ*

At that time, the World-Honored One, further manifesting the characteristic of the eternal wisdom-seal and secret treasury of all Tathāgatas, taught all the bodhisattvas the Dharma-gate of the Prajñāpāramitā of the True Reality of the Profound Abode of the Vajra Wisdom-Seal of All Buddhas. It states: "The vajra body-seal upheld by all Buddhas attains the true essential nature of all Tathāgatas. The vajra speech-seal upheld by all Buddhas attains sovereignty over all Dharma-gates. The vajra mind-seal upheld by all Buddhas attains the completeness of all samādhis. The vajra wisdom-seal upheld by all Buddhas attains the supreme body, speech, and mind, which are like vajra."

At that time, when the World-Honored One had taught this Dharma-gate, he again addressed Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva: "Vajrapāṇi! If there is anyone who hears this Dharma-gate of the Prajñāpāramitā of the True Reality of the Profound Abode of the Vajra Wisdom-Seal of All Buddhas, and receives, upholds, reads, recites, and contemplates it with right mindfulness, one should know that this person shall accomplish the supreme vajra seal. In all wisdom and all undertakings they shall be complete. Their body, speech, and mind shall be like vajra, and they shall ultimately attain the supreme, perfect enlightenment."

At that time, the Tathāgata again spoke the mantra:

*A*

At that time, the World-Honored One, further manifesting the characteristic of all Tathāgatas' being forever free from elaboration, taught all the bodhisattvas the Dharma-gate of the Prajñāpāramitā of the True Reality of the Chapter on the Turning of the Wheel of Letters. It states: "All dharmas are empty, because they are without self-nature. All dharmas are signless, because they are free from all characteristics. All dharmas are wishless, because they are free from all wishes. All dharmas are inherently pure in self-nature, because prajñāpāramitā is pure."

At that time, the Tathāgata again spoke the mantra:

*A*

At that time, the World-Honored One, further manifesting the characteristic of all Tathāgatas' entering the vast turning of the wheel, taught all the bodhisattvas the Dharma-gate of the Prajñāpāramitā of the True Reality of Entering the Vast Turning of the Wheel. It states: "By entering the equality of vajra, one enters the turning of the wheel of all Tathāgatas. By entering the equality of meaning, one enters the turning of the wheel of all bodhisattvas. By entering the equality of Dharma, one enters the turning of the wheel of the wondrous Dharma. By entering the equality of all, one enters the turning of all wheels."

At that time, the Tathāgata again spoke the mantra:

*Hūṃ*

At that time, the World-Honored One, further manifesting the characteristic of the great skillful means of all Tathāgatas, taught all the bodhisattvas the Dharma-gate of the Prajñāpāramitā of the True Reality of the Foremost and Most Extensive Offerings to All Buddhas. It states: "To generate the aspiration for enlightenment is itself the great skillful means of extensively making offerings to all Buddhas. To rescue and protect sentient beings is itself the great skillful means of extensively making offerings to all Buddhas. To uphold and maintain the true Dharma is itself the great skillful means of extensively making offerings to all Buddhas."

At that time, when the World-Honored One had taught this Dharma-gate, he again addressed Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva: "Vajrapāṇi! If there is anyone who hears this Dharma-gate of the Prajñāpāramitā of the True Reality of the Foremost and Most Extensive Offerings to All Buddhas, whether they copy it themselves or have others copy it, whether they uphold it themselves or have others uphold it, whether they read and recite it themselves or have others read and recite it, whether they contemplate it themselves or have others contemplate it, whether they make offerings themselves or have others make offerings: whatever they do is itself the great skillful means of extensively making offerings to all Buddhas."

At that time, the Tathāgata again spoke the mantra:

*Oṃ*

At that time, the World-Honored One, further manifesting the characteristic of all Tathāgatas' ability to tame, taught all the bodhisattvas the Dharma-gate of the Prajñāpāramitā of the True Reality of the Secret Wisdom Treasury That Can Tame and Gather All Sentient Beings. It states: "The equality-nature of all sentient beings is the equality-nature of anger. The taming-nature of all sentient beings is the taming-nature of anger. The true dharma-nature of all sentient beings is the true dharma-nature of anger. The vajra-nature of all sentient beings is the vajra-nature of anger. Why? Because the taming-nature of all sentient beings is itself enlightenment."

At that time, the Tathāgata again spoke the mantra:

*Haḥ*

At that time, the World-Honored One, further manifesting the characteristic of all Tathāgatas' abiding in equality, taught all the bodhisattvas the Dharma-gate of the Prajñāpāramitā of the True Reality of the Supreme Equality-Nature of All Dharmas. It states: "Because all dharmas are of an equality-nature, prajñāpāramitā is of an equality-nature. Because all dharmas are of an ultimate-truth nature, prajñāpāramitā is of an ultimate-truth nature. Because all dharmas are of a dharma-nature, prajñāpāramitā is of a dharma-nature. Because all dharmas are of a functional nature, prajñāpāramitā is of a functional nature."

At that time, the Tathāgata again spoke the mantra:

*Hrīḥ*

At that time, the World-Honored One, further manifesting the characteristic of all Tathāgatas as the refuge for sentient beings, taught all the bodhisattvas the Dharma-gate of the Prajñāpāramitā of the True Reality of the Refuge for All Sentient Beings. It states: "All sentient beings are the tathāgata-garbha, because the essential nature of the Bodhisattva Samantabhadra pervades all. All sentient beings are the vajra-treasury, because they are sprinkled with the water of the vajra-treasury. All sentient beings are the treasury of the true Dharma, because they are spoken of through true and proper words. All sentient beings are the treasury of wondrous activity, because they are moved by skillful and wondrous activity."

At that time, the Tathāgata again spoke the mantra:

*Trīḥ*

At that time, the World-Honored One, further manifesting the characteristic of all Tathāgatas' being immeasurable, boundless, and ultimately exhaustive, taught all the bodhisattvas the Dharma-gate of the Prajñāpāramitā of the True Reality of the Equality of All Dharmas Being Immeasurable, Boundless, and Ultimately Exhaustive. It states: "Because prajñāpāramitā is immeasurable, all Buddhas are also immeasurable. Because prajñāpāramitā is boundless, all Buddhas are also boundless. Because prajñāpāramitā is of one nature, all dharmas are also of one nature. Because prajñāpāramitā is ultimately exhaustive, all dharmas are also ultimately exhaustive."

At that time, when the World-Honored One had taught this Dharma-gate, he again addressed Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva: "Vajrapāṇi! If there is anyone who hears this Dharma-gate of the Prajñāpāramitā of the True Reality of Being Immeasurable, Boundless, and Ultimately Exhaustive, and receives, upholds, reads, recites, and contemplates it with right mindfulness, all the obstructions and burdens of this person shall be extinguished completely without remainder. They shall swiftly reach enlightenment, attain the vajra body of the Tathāgatas, and gain sovereignty."

At that time, the Tathāgata again spoke the mantra:

*Vyāḥ*

At that time, the World-Honored One, further manifesting the characteristic of the dharma-nature that is the secret of all Tathāgatas, free from elaboration and universally radiant, taught all the bodhisattvas the Dharma-gate of the Prajñāpāramitā of the Foremost True Reality of the Great Bliss Vajra, Unerring, Unobstructed, Definitive Entry into the Dharma-Nature, Without Beginning, Middle, or End. It states: "Because bodhisattvas can extensively serve and make offerings, they attain the supreme great bliss. Because they attain the supreme great bliss, they attain the supreme great enlightenment of all Buddhas. Because they attain the supreme great enlightenment of all Buddhas, they can vanquish all the armies of Māra. Because they vanquish all the armies of Māra, they attain sovereignty in all three realms. Because they attain sovereignty in all three realms, they can universally benefit all sentient beings and give them all the ultimate and supreme bliss. Why? In verse:

> "Those who possess the supreme wisdom
> Always dwell within birth and death,
> Broadly liberating all beings,
> Yet they do not enter nirvāṇa.
> Prajñāpāramitā,
> The ultimate skillful wisdom,
> Can accomplish the pure karma
> And purify universally all existences.
> Furthermore, by means of desire and such,
> They tame all the worlds,
> Even up to the highest heaven,
> Purifying them without violence.
> Dwelling in the world of birth and death,
> Worldly dharmas cannot defile them,
> Like the wondrous color of the lotus,
> Unsoiled by dust and filth.
> Those of great desire are the pure ones;
> Those of great giving are the blissful ones.
> Sovereign in the three realms,
> They accomplish firm and lasting benefit."

At that time, when the World-Honored One had taught this Dharma-gate, he again addressed Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva: "Vajrapāṇi! If there is anyone who hears this Dharma-gate of the Prajñāpāramitā of the True Reality of the Great Bliss Vajra Dharma-Nature, and daily, each morning, listens to it or recites it without interruption, one should know that this person's offenses and obstructions shall all be extinguished of themselves. Their mind shall always be at ease and harmonious, in the foremost bliss. In this very body, they shall accomplish the vajra, unerring, unobstructed, definitive entry into the Dharma. They shall furthermore accomplish the vajra, secret, and firm body of all Tathāgatas."

At that time, the Tathāgata again spoke the mantra:

*Svāhā*

At that time, when the World-Honored One had taught all the above Dharma-gates for the bodhisattvas, he again addressed Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva: "Vajrapāṇi! This sūtra of mine is exceedingly difficult to hear. If there is anyone who hears it, even a single syllable, one should know that this person has in past lives already made offerings to the Buddhas and has planted roots of merit in the presence of all the Buddhas. How much more so for one who hears, reads, and recites it in full! One should know that this person has definitively already made offerings to, honored, revered, and praised eighty billion nayutas of Ganges-sands of Buddhas. Wherever this sūtra is, that place has a stūpa of all the Buddhas. Furthermore, if there is a person who cherishes this sūtra and always guards and protects it, keeping it ever upon their person, this person should receive the reverence and offerings of all the world. This person shall attain the supernatural knowledge of past lives and shall be able to know the events of immeasurable kalpas past. They shall not be disturbed by any heavenly Māra Pāpīyas. The Four Great Heavenly Kings and all the other devas shall always guard and protect them. All Buddhas and all bodhisattvas shall constantly embrace them. They shall be reborn in any of the pure lands of the ten directions according to their wish. Vajrapāṇi! I have now briefly described the merit of this Dharma-gate of the Prajñāpāramitā of True Reality. Were I to describe it in full, I could not exhaust it even in an entire kalpa."

When the Buddha had spoken this sūtra, Vajrapāṇi and all the bodhisattvas, the devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, humans, non-humans, and the entire assembly were all greatly joyful, and they accepted it with faith and practiced it.
