# 文殊師利發願經
## Mañjuśrī's Aspiration Sūtra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0296

Translated from the Chinese. Eastern Jin dynasty, translated by Buddhabhadra (佛陀跋陀羅)

---

> With body, speech, and mind purified,
> All defilements removed and cleansed,
> With single-minded reverence I bow
> To the Buddhas of the ten directions and three times.

> By the power of Samantabhadra's vows
> I behold all the Buddhas;
> Before each and every Tathāgata
> I bow as many times as there are atoms in all lands.

> Within a single atom
> I see all the Buddhas,
> Surrounded by assemblies of bodhisattvas;
> So too in every atom of the dharma-realm.

> With all manner of wondrous voices
> I proclaim and praise the most victorious ones;
> Their ocean of immeasurable merit
> Can never be exhausted.

> By the power of Samantabhadra's practice,
> With supreme and unsurpassed offerings,
> I make offerings to all the Buddhas
> Of the ten directions and three times.

> With wondrous incense, flowers, and garlands,
> And various kinds of music,
> With all manner of wondrous adornments,
> I universally offer to all the Buddhas.

> Through greed, hatred, and delusion
> I have committed every evil deed;
> The unwholesome of body, speech, and mind:
> I repent and eliminate them all.

> In the merit of all sentient beings,
> Of all śrāvakas and pratyekabuddhas,
> Of bodhisattvas and of all Buddhas,
> I rejoice completely.

> To all Buddhas of the ten directions
> Who first accomplish perfect enlightenment,
> I urge and invite them all:
> Turn the unsurpassed Dharma-wheel.

> To those who manifest nirvāṇa,
> With joined palms I reverently request:
> Abide for all the kalpas of dust-motes,
> Bringing peace and joy to all beings.

> Whatever merit I have gathered,
> I dedicate to all sentient beings,
> To complete the bodhisattva practice
> And reach the supreme enlightenment.

> I make offerings to all past
> And present Buddhas of the ten directions;
> May all future World-Honored Ones
> Swiftly accomplish the path of enlightenment.

> May all buddha-lands of the ten directions
> Be universally adorned;
> With Tathāgatas seated at the place of enlightenment,
> And assemblies of bodhisattvas filling them.

> May all beings of the ten directions
> Have their afflictions eliminated,
> Deeply understand the true meaning,
> And always abide in peace and joy.

> When I practice the bodhisattva conduct
> And perfect the wisdom of past lives,
> May I eliminate all obstructions,
> Forever exhausting them without remainder.

> Completely freed from birth and death,
> From māras, afflictions, and karmic deeds,
> Like the sun dwelling in empty space,
> Like a lotus untouched by water.

> Traveling universally through the ten directions,
> Teaching and transforming all beings,
> Eliminating the suffering of the evil paths,
> Fulfilling the bodhisattva practice.

> Though I follow along with the world,
> I do not abandon the bodhisattva path;
> To the end of all future kalpas
> I fully cultivate Samantabhadra's practice.

> If there are those who practice together,
> May we always gather in one place;
> In wholesome deeds of body, speech, and mind,
> May we all become equal.

> When encountering a good teacher
> Who reveals Samantabhadra's practice,
> From this bodhisattva
> May I always be near and never parted.

> Always seeing all the Buddhas,
> Surrounded by assemblies of bodhisattvas,
> To the end of all future kalpas
> I reverently make offerings to them all.

> Guarding and protecting the Buddhas' Dharma,
> Praising the bodhisattva practice,
> Cultivating to the end of future kalpas,
> I complete Samantabhadra's path.

> Though in the midst of birth and death,
> I possess inexhaustible merit;
> Wisdom, skillful means,
> And all samādhis and liberations.

> Within a single atom
> I see inconceivable lands;
> In each and every land
> I see inconceivable Buddhas.

> Seeing in this way the ten directions,
> All the oceans of worlds,
> In each and every ocean of worlds
> I behold all the oceans of Buddhas.

> Within a single voice
> Are all wondrous sounds complete;
> Within each wondrous sound
> Is the most supreme sound complete.

> By the power of profound wisdom,
> Entering endless wondrous sounds,
> Turning the pure Dharma-wheel
> Of all the Buddhas of the three times.

> All kalpas of the future
> Can be contained in a single thought;
> All kalpas of the three times
> Become a single instant.

> In a single thought one beholds
> All the Tathāgatas of the three times,
> And universally discerns and knows
> Their liberation and their realms.

> Within a single atom
> Are produced the pure lands of the three times;
> In every atom of the ten directions,
> Adorned lands are likewise.

> I behold all future Buddhas
> Accomplishing the path and turning the Dharma-wheel;
> Having completed all Buddha-deeds,
> They manifest entry into nirvāṇa.

> With supernatural power traveling everywhere,
> The great vehicle's power of universal access,
> The power of compassion covering all,
> The power of practice and merit complete.

> The power of merit purified,
> The power of wisdom unobstructed,
> The power of samādhi and skillful means,
> Attaining the power of enlightenment.

> The power of pure wholesome karma,
> The power to eliminate afflictions,
> The power to crush all māras,
> The power to fulfill Samantabhadra's practice.

> Adorning and purifying the ocean of buddha-lands,
> Liberating the ocean of sentient beings,
> Discerning the ocean of all karmas,
> Exhausting the ocean of wisdom.

> Purifying the ocean of all practices,
> Fulfilling the ocean of all vows,
> Beholding the ocean of all Buddhas:
> I practice throughout the ocean of kalpas.

> The practices of all Buddhas of the three times,
> And their immeasurable great vows:
> I fulfill them all completely,
> Accomplishing Buddhahood through Samantabhadra's practice.

> The bodhisattva named Samantabhadra,
> Foremost among all the Buddhas' children:
> I dedicate my roots of merit,
> Aspiring to become equal to him.

> With body, speech, and mind purified,
> Freely adorning all lands,
> Accomplishing perfect enlightenment:
> May all be the same as Samantabhadra.

> Like Mañjuśrī
> And Samantabhadra's practice,
> All my roots of merit
> I dedicate likewise.

> The path of dedication
> Praised by all Tathāgatas of the three times:
> I dedicate my roots of merit
> To fulfill Samantabhadra's practice.

> When my life comes to an end,
> May all obstructions be eliminated;
> May I see Amitābha face to face
> And be reborn in the land of Sukhāvatī.

> Having been born in that buddha-land,
> I shall fulfill all great vows;
> The Tathāgata Amitābha
> Shall appear before me and bestow my prediction.

> Adorning and purifying Samantabhadra's practice,
> Fulfilling Mañjuśrī's vows,
> To the end of all future kalpas
> I complete the bodhisattva practice.
