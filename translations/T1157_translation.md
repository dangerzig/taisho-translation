# 香王菩薩陀羅尼呪經
## Dhāraṇī Mantra Sūtra of Gandharāja Bodhisattva

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1157

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Yijing (義淨).

---

[Dhāraṇī]

*Namo ratna-trayāya namo ārya-avalokiteśvarāya bodhisattvāya mahāsattvāya namaḥ gandharāja ya bodhisattvāya mahāsattvāya icchimi micchimi hānti mānti sarvārtha-sādhani kṛṣa-paṇa-dhi pāme paryeṣṭi svāhā*

For this dhāraṇī, one must first recite it sincerely one hundred thousand times. Then before an image of Avalokiteśvara, make a square altar of four cubits. Take flowers and other fine incenses as offerings. At night, burn a lamp of pure cow's ghee and do not let the light be extinguished. At midnight the mantra master must rise, put on clean garments and dress properly, and recite this dhāraṇī one thousand and eight times before the image. It must be completed by dawn, without drowsiness or sleep. On that day, someone will come of their own accord to deliver money and goods to the mantra master.

If one further seeks a great matter, one should paint Gandharāja Bodhisattva on silk cloth. The painter must not eat past midday and must observe the eight-precept fast. The painting may be any size. The body and flesh should all be white, with a dignified countenance, wearing a celestial crown, with garlands at the neck. The right arm hangs down with all five fingers extended in the abhaya mudrā, and from each of the five fingertips amṛta rains down upon sentient beings of the five destinies. Below the hand, paint three to five black ghosts. The left arm is bent at the elbow, with the hand at the left breast holding a lotus. The flower grows upward from a lotus beneath the feet. The flower is white-pink in color, and the lotus beneath the feet is also white-pink. There is a circular halo behind the neck, and above is a parasol canopy. The garments are of five-colored brocade. Two strands of pearl necklaces drape over the shoulders, one red and one yellow. The image must be completed in one day, and skin-glue must not be used.

Before the image, burn incense and recite one hundred thousand times, fasting for one day and one night. First make a large square water-altar, then anoint it with sandalwood paste. Make eight small square altars of twelve finger-widths each. The first three altars are: one for offering to the Buddha, one for offering to the Dharma, and one for offering to the Saṃgha. The fourth altar is for offering to Amitābha Buddha, the fifth for offering to Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, the sixth for offering to Mahāsthāmaprāpta Bodhisattva, the seventh for offering to Gandharāja Bodhisattva, and the eighth for offering to all the good spirits. Place fine pure food, fruits, incense, and flowers around all four sides. This method may be performed on the fourteenth or fifteenth day of any month.

Then take one thousand and eight stems of jāti flowers. With each recitation, cast one stem onto the chest of the image. After performing the method, each midnight one must rise and constantly recite one thousand and eight times before the image, until someone comes of their own accord to deliver money and goods. The money and goods must not be hoarded with miserliness; they should be used and given to the poor and destitute. This is the dharma of Gandharāja Bodhisattva.

Furthermore, there is a separate abbreviated essential method.

The method for upholding the mantra: the practitioner should bathe, put on fresh clean garments, and observe the eight-precept fast. Purify the area according to the altar method, arrange offerings in sequence, scatter flowers, burn incense, and light lamps. From midnight, sit upright within the altar and recite the mantra one thousand and eight times. Take one thousand and eight campaka flower stems. With each recitation, cast one stem of flower upon the bodhisattva's body. While reciting the mantra, one must not hear the sound of birdsong. Break the fast with three kinds of white food. Burn benzoin incense as an offering. Each day, filter water and bathe in fragrant water, and change into separate garments. After going to the latrine, always wash thoroughly. At dawn and midnight when entering the altar, one must chew a willow twig before entering. Then enter the altar and single-mindedly recite the mantra without thinking of external matters. One will then see divine efficacy and radiance. When the sign appears, stop. Even if no light appears, there will be signs of movement, and wherever one goes, one will obtain great rewards. In the method of upholding the mantra, one must not break the precepts. One's body must not come near or touch a woman; if one touches a woman, the method will have no efficacy.
