# 都表如意摩尼轉輪聖王次第念誦祕密最要略法
## Abbreviated Most Essential Secret Method for the Sequential Recitation of the Wish-Fulfilling Maṇi Cakravartin Holy King

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1089

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Vimuktisiṃha (解脫師子).

---

## Chapter One: Characteristics

> "I shall now explain the method of contemplation.
> The three kinds of mantra are in sequence:
> First, use the great heart for outer empowerment;
> Second, the root mantra, with inner visualization;
> Third, the lesser heart, for both inner and outer.
> One should hold them in mind without confusion.
> These three marvelous mantra-paths
> Establish the practitioner on the ground of the Vidyā-King.
>
> If one cultivates this samaya,
> One must possess seven kinds of supreme characteristics,
> Just as the cakravartin king holds the seven treasures
> And all the four continents submit.
>
> Next, I shall explain the proper recitation method.
> First, select a clean and auspicious place,
> Whether in the mountains or beside a pond,
> Or in a monastery's quiet chamber,
> Or in a miraculous stūpa of buddha-relics.
> One should hold this in mind and prepare all requisites.
> First, establish the ground as instructed.
> Next, install the principal deity.
> The practitioner must prepare their own seat.
> The deity faces west and is placed before one's eyes.
> The practitioner sits facing east before the image.
> Bathe cleanly, anoint the body with incense,
> And also wear fresh, clean, fine garments.
> Hold all manner of offering implements.
> With every step, visualize an eight-petaled lotus.
> Visualize transforming the ground to be free of impurity.
> Empower the body-protection and enter the chamber.
> Take one's place before the image and begin recitation."

## Chapter Two: The Great Heart Secret Phrase

> "First, use the great heart mantra-king
> For bathing, washing, purification, and protection rites.
> Empower water and sprinkle the body, empower the garments.
> Further, visualize and recollect, and all is accomplished.
> These five procedures are in sequence,
> All using the great vidyā and its mudrā."

*Oṃ padma cintā-maṇi mahā-jvala hūṃ*

Empower the bathing powder and washing implements, each with the spell seven times, then bathe and wash.

Visualize the water as white, like nectar. Then visualize one's own body: all defilements and afflictions, upon receiving this water, all dusts and toils completely fall away. When bathing is complete, one should wash with clean water. Take a small amount of earth, empower it seven times, and use it to wash as appropriate. Then put on garments.

At that time, empower the garments seven times as well, making this visualization: "My body is now clad in vajra armor." When this is completed according to the method, one should sprinkle the body. Take a handful of water, empower it seven times, and scatter it upon the body. Visualize the sky raining jeweled flowers, filling the entire world. Then one must perform body-protection: form this secret mudrā, recite and empower seven times, and seal seven places: heart, both shoulders, both eyes, both knees. Release the mudrā above the head.

Having completed the great body-protection, one must then seal the boundary. If establishing an altar-boundary with four open gates, one need not seal the boundary. If performing a protective rite, one must use the mind throughout. Then arrange and set in order, and perform the invocation and other procedures.

## Chapter Three: The Root Secret Phrase

> "Next, I explain the root ritual method.
> Visualize and recollect, invoking the Great Holy King.
> Recite this secret phrase and form the mudrā.
> One by one, visualize yourself going to that mountain.
> Hold the thought, bow in homage, and make the invocation.
> Welcome and guide the Honored One into the bodhimaṇḍa.
> Then use argha water to present to him,
> With flowers, food, drink, anointing perfume,
> Lamps, canopies, banners, pennants, and medicines:
> Make offerings according to the method to that Vidyā-King.
> Having visualized making offerings to the Honored One,
> Then take up the beads and recite the secret phrase.
> Confess past-life transgressions and make vows.
> With utmost sincerity, sit upright and contemplate the Vidyā-King."

The practitioner sits upright before the image in contemplation, holding in mind and carefully visualizing Mount Potalaka, adorned with all manner of jewels, with flowers and fruits flourishing.

On the summit of that mountain, the palace of the Holy One is within. One should visualize the Holy One seated serenely within the palace, surrounded by supreme and wondrous offerings adorning his body. Within this mental visualization, the practitioner goes to that mountain in person, inviting the Honored One, guiding him into the bodhimaṇḍa, and installing the Holy One upon a lotus platform at the center of the altar.

Next, one offers argha water. In a new copper vessel or white porcelain, fill with fragrant water and present it to the Holy One, reciting the mantra. Then visualize: the Holy One, having received this water, sprinkles it in the four directions and upon his own wondrous body.

The practitioner carefully observes as the Honored One sprinkles the water; the great trichiliocosm becomes like lapis lazuli, clear and transparent inside and out, with nothing within.

Having offered the argha, next make offerings of flowers, food, drink, anointing perfume, powdered incense, and lamps and canopies, each empowered with the mantra and mudrā.

When the offerings are complete, before the principal deity, one confesses one's bodily faults and reproaches one's own transgressions. Next, one makes vows, expressing whatever the heart seeks. Within the visualization, seal the altar on three sides, leaving only the western gate open. Then seal the great boundary on all four sides around the body and the sky above. Next, anoint the hands and arms with perfume, and then begin recitation.

Take the prayer beads, perfume them with incense, join both palms and raise them to the crown three times, speaking thus:

"I recollect with devoted mind, attending upon the Holy One, upholding this secret phrase. I beseech the Honored One not to forsake great compassion, not to transgress the original vow. May you generate the great vow and swiftly manifest your wondrously adorned body. When I behold it, I shall in this very body attain realization of the Dharma-Cloud Bhūmi, and always attend the Honored One without parting."

Having made this vow, one recites the root secret mantra:

*Namo ratna-trayāya namaḥ ārya-avalokiteśvarāya bodhisattvāya mahāsattvāya mahā-kāruṇikāya tadyathā oṃ cakra-vartī cintā-maṇi mahā-padme ruru tiṣṭha jvala ākarṣāya hūṃ phaṭ svāhā*

Recite this mantra three hundred thousand times within seven days, and the Holy One will appear before the reciter. Whatever the heart seeks will all be accomplished.

If one constantly recites, three times each day, one thousand and eight or one hundred and eight times per session, and thus completes three hundred thousand recitations, accomplishment is attained. At each session, one performs the welcoming, sending-off, and entrusting procedures.

The great heart secret phrase says:

*Oṃ varada padma cintā-maṇi mahā-jvala hūṃ phaṭ svāhā*

## Chapter Four: The Lesser Heart Secret Phrase

> "Next, I explain the most supreme lesser heart-king,
> Which can accomplish all manner of siddhi.
> The three karmas are employed together to form the wondrous mudrā,
> Reciting the secret mantra while entrusting.
> Then within the visualization, release the boundary.
> Next offer argha and sprinkle the four directions.
> Then confess one's own faults with deep shame.
> Guide the Great Holy One back to the original palace.
> Having installed the Honored One in that palace,
> Take leave and visualize oneself returning to one's own place."

Furthermore, each time after recitation, the practitioner must perform the entrusting. Form this secret mudrā and recite the mantra, entrusting to the Holy One, speaking thus: "All the accumulated recitations and the merit of practice I entrust to the Honored One. I beseech the Honored One to accept and guard them, and not let them be dispersed or lost." Also visualize the hand-mudrā as a treasure-vault: all the recitation-counts are placed within the vault and given to the Holy One. The Holy One, having accepted them, places them within a lotus. When the entrusting is complete, one must also confess transgressions and make great vows. Then one performs the sending-off, guiding the Holy One back to the palace, releasing the boundary. Then take fragrant water and sprinkle it in the four directions, also presenting it to the Holy One, and then proceed with the sending-off. Form the secret mudrā and recite the mantra, visualizing oneself escorting the Holy One back to the original palace and installing him in the palace. When the Holy One is seated, one takes leave and returns to one's own place.

The lesser heart secret phrase says:

*Oṃ varada padme hūṃ*

I shall now describe the characteristics of the secret mudrās herein. First, the great mudrā: anoint the hands and arms with various perfumes, then form the mudrā and raise it to the crown. Join the palms at the heart. Hook both little fingers and ring fingers into the palm. Press both index fingers upon the back of the middle fingers at the second joint. Extend both thumbs upright side by side.

This is the great heart mantra secret mudrā, which must always be used together. For all empowerments, recite each secret phrase seven times and seal with each mudrā. Whether casting or holding, one must visualize it as complete, each one according to the method. One must not allow the mind to stray to other objects or follow worldly ways. Whether walking, standing, sitting, or lying down, one abides in the profound realm of all the buddhas. If any practitioner cultivates this method, before long they will accomplish sarvajña-jñāna.

## The Second: Root Mantra Mudrā

Place both wrists together before the chest. Spread all ten fingers. Extend both little fingers and both thumbs upright side by side, like a lotus shape. Follow the same method for sealing and holding as before. For the invocation alone, move the thumbs back and forth.

## The Third: Lesser Heart Mantra Mudrā

Join both hollow palms like an unopened lotus. When entrusting, follow the visualization as before. To send off the Honored One, open both thumbs. This lesser heart secret mudrā is used universally throughout the Lotus division. I have here briefly extracted from the great Lotus-division samaya this most secret and profound sequential recitation. The practitioner must not transmit it erroneously; guard it as one guards the eyes. If one can do so, all the holy ones will greatly rejoice and keep that person in mind, and all the wholesome spirits will guard and protect day and night, never departing.

## Chapter Five: Accomplishing Worldly and Supramundane Disaster-Averting Homa Recitation

> "I now follow the Lotus division
> And briefly explain the Wish-Fulfilling Maṇi Wheel,
> Which surpasses all divisions as the most secret,
> Accomplishing both worldly and supramundane dharmas.
> There are four kinds of recitation in all.
> Practitioner, listen well as I explain:
> First, the pacification empowerment method;
> Second, the increase of merit and wisdom;
> Third, the method of love and recollection according to the heart;
> Fourth, the subjugation of all enemies.
> These four kinds of homa practice,
> In upper, middle, and lower methods, I set forth in order.
>
> If one wishes to accomplish pacification,
> First generate a patient and compassionate heart.
> One should seek the ground of unsurpassed awakening
> And not delight in the two-vehicle path of the śrāvakas.
> If one further seeks to accomplish a great matter,
> One must first perform the obstacle-removing method.
> Take white sandalwood sticks of sixteen finger-widths.
> With each recitation, burn one, calling one's own name.
> Count to one hundred and eight or one thousand and eight.
> All original wishes will be fully satisfied.
> When the practitioner removes another's illness,
> Or extinguishes a great calamity in the nation,
> Whether one's own or another's calamity, or that of livestock,
> One should hold the mind and invoke the Great Saint.
> At each great city gate,
> Visualize installing the principal deity,
> Adorned with all manner of supreme jewels.
> Contemplate the six arms as fully described.
> Also visualize from between the brows a white light
> Gradually and broadly extending throughout the world.
> All the calamities of all sentient beings
> Are all suddenly dispelled into nothing.
> Or visualize this light entering one's own body:
> All karmic obstructions are entirely extinguished.
> With offenses extinguished and merit born through empowerment,
> Redouble diligent recitation, and accomplishment is attained."

## Chapter Six: Increasing Merit

> "I now briefly explain the method for generating merit.
> Through great compassion, following sentient beings,
> The bodhisattva manifests division-bodies everywhere,
> Responding to whatever sentient beings delight in.
> He also establishes all manner of treasure-stores.
> The various dharma-gates each differ.
> In a hundred thousand myriad lands, all who follow
> Will, by diligent seeking, accomplish each one.
> He may teach the dharma of characteristics or the characteristicless.
> Following the natures of sentient beings and their inclinations,
> Establishing the buddha-dharma to protect the world:
> The Great Saint's power is inconceivable.
> Presenting one dharma, one swiftly transcends.
> Within one dharma, three further appear.
> Each of the three divisions has three kinds,
> And among them, the first is disaster-averting.
> Having briefly explained disaster-averting in all divisions,
> Next comes increase, which I now explain.
>
> If one seeks longevity and wealth,
> Or delights in intelligence and the gate of dhāraṇī,
> Or seeks past-life knowledge of future and past,
> Or desires in this life strength like a nāga:
> If performing increase for oneself,
> First one should eat the three white foods daily.
> If seeking longevity and freedom from illness,
> Construct a fire-pit in lotus shape,
> One cubit in depth, breadth, and circumference.
> Take the five pure substances and apply them within.
> Then take ghee, honey, and sandalwood,
> One spell, one burning, calling one's own name.
> Count to one hundred and eight, one thousand and eight,
> Or up to three thousand, following the previous method.
> If there are extremely heavy karmas from past lives,
> No more than ten thousand recitations will extinguish them all.
> With offenses extinguished, merit and wisdom increase,
> And one attains freedom from illness and long life.
> If performing this for another's sake,
> Simply call that person's name; the method is the same.
>
> The practitioner, practicing as instructed and holding the mind,
> Each morning at dawn takes a willow stick,
> Empowers it with eight hundred recitations, then chews it.
> Before long, within one month or three months,
> One's mind will open and one gains intelligence.
> For the homa, use the lesser heart spell.
> For recitation, use the root great mantra.
> I have briefly explained the increase of merit.
> For sentient beings without merit, swiftly empower them.
>
> Setting aside these various methods,
> If one merely wishes for longevity and great body-protection,
> By reciting one thousand and eight times daily,
> In this very life one obtains past-life knowledge.
> By diligent practice day and night without ceasing,
> All the siddhi sought will be accomplished.
>
> If one takes medicine seeking vigor and strength,
> Empower it with the root mantra.
> When recitation reaches a full one hundred thousand,
> Take the medicine on an empty stomach each time.
> After three weeks, five weeks, or beyond seven,
> One's strength will equal that of nine nāgas.
> If one takes the amṛta medicine of longevity,
> One may live for one kalpa or beyond a middle kalpa.
> If one holds medicinal herbs seeking accomplishment,
> The result surpasses that of all other divisions.
> Cow's ghee, orpiment, realgar, and the like:
> One must place one substance upon the altar.
> On the first or fifteenth of the bright half of the month,
> Begin recitation at the first watch of the night.
> Recite without interruption for one full period,
> Until the three signs appear as the limit.
> The first sign, warmth, brings intelligence.
> The second sign, smoke, makes the body invisible.
> The third sign, fire, causes one to ascend into the sky.
> This is the accomplishment of a great immortal.
> For the siddhi of cow's ghee, one should consume it.
> For realgar and similar medicines, apply them.
> I have now explained this increase.
> Practitioner, exert yourself and guard it well."

## Chapter Seven: Recollection According to the Heart

> "I now further explain the method of following the heart.
> Whether humans, non-humans, or livestock:
> Take salt mixed with barley flour
> And shape it as desired according to the heart's wish.
> Male from the left, female from the right, starting from the feet.
> One must use a steel knife to cut finely.
> Three times each day, one hundred and eight each time,
> Or up to one thousand and eight, as one wishes.
>
> If one wishes to captivate and cause recollection,
> Holding the formed figure, call that person's name.
> In the homa also, one must call the name.
> Within eight hundred recitations, the heart submits.
>
> If subduing all nāgas to compliance,
> First make a nāga in serpent shape.
> If seeking rain, it should be blue.
> If stopping excess rain, it should be red.
> For the fire, use milky-sap wood.
> Within three days it is accomplished.
> The method requires utmost sincerity.
> One must not have a lazy or heedless mind.
>
> If subduing devas, yakṣas, and the like,
> For each class, call its proper name.
> If wishing enemies to become happy,
> Recitation can also turn their hearts.
> Whether performing homa or sustained recitation,
> Upper, middle, and lower classes of enemies differ.
> For superior beings with good fortune, three hundred times.
> For middling and inferior without fortune, double that.
> I have briefly explained recollection according to the heart.
> The wise should contemplate this carefully.
> All divisions are entirely gathered herein,
> The most secret and essential lotus heart."

## Chapter Eight: Abhicāruka

> "I now bow to that Great Saint,
> Who manifests all manner of skillful gates.
> To protect the buddha-dharma, he appears wrathful,
> Subjugating and destroying those who harm the holy dharma.
> The fire-pit herein must be triangular.
> Burn female wood and wet firewood.
> This method must be performed during the dark half of the month,
> On the first day or at the new moon.
> Expose the body, prefer dark places, and generate wrath.
> In a loud voice, recite while calling the person's name.
> Cast rancid ghee and rancid oil into the pit.
> Within one thousand and eight times, it is naturally accomplished.
>
> To subjugate violent ghosts and spirits,
> One should also burn hellebore root,
> Or burn neem or raw wood,
> And with flour mixed with human blood, make a figure.
> One cut, one spell, one hundred and eight times.
> Within three days, they are tamed.
>
> If further subjugating those who destroy the dharma,
> Redouble offerings to the great Vidyā-King.
> Burn various poisons in the pit,
> And use wet firewood for the homa.
> Within three days, eight hundred times:
> The evil person will turn their mind.
>
> If one does not perform the homa,
> Simply recite the root mantra-vidyā.
> For all one wishes to subjugate,
> In every phrase call that person's name.
> When the count reaches eight hundred,
> That person will come of their own accord and show respect.
>
> I have briefly explained this subjugation according to the method.
> The practitioner should contemplate it carefully.
> This most profound secret oral teaching
> Is the sequential recitation's all-encompassing gate.
> If one can attend upon this Honored One,
> It is not from planting wholesome roots in one life alone,
> But from having been in the presence of immeasurable buddhas,
> Cultivating the ultimate gate of bodhi.
> If within that one meets a good teacher,
> In one life one enters the indestructible gate.
> If I were to explain these merits extensively,
> In immeasurable koṭis of kalpas their praise would be endless.
> I have now briefly set forth this small portion.
> Practitioner, with diligent mind, cultivate it earnestly."
