# 大悲心陀羅尼修行念誦略儀
## Abbreviated Rite for the Practice and Recitation of the Great Compassion Heart Dhāraṇī

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1066

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

According to the *Abhiṣeka-Bodhimaṇḍa Sūtra*, those who practice the dhāraṇī dharma-gate and seek to swiftly leave the great ocean of birth and death and quickly realize unsurpassed bodhi should first enter the abhiṣeka-bodhimaṇḍa, the oceanic assembly of all buddha tathāgatas. Having received the abhiṣeka, one generates a joyful mind and personally receives from the master the rules of recitation practice. Afterward, in a clean room, a mountain forest, or by flowing water, which are the most superior settings, one establishes the bodhimaṇḍa and installs the principal deity. The mantra practitioner faces east. One should smear the ground with gomaya and grind white sandalwood into fragrant paste to apply to the altar, either square or round, of whatever size one wishes. Upon the altar, scatter flowers and burn incense as offerings. Take two clean vessels filled with fragrant water and place them upon the altar as offerings. Whether the practitioner bathes or does not bathe, there is no obstruction. One should simply direct the mind to contemplate and observe: the original nature of all sentient beings is pure; it is obscured by adventitious defilements, so that the pure dharma-nature of true suchness is not seen. In order to purify them, one should wholeheartedly recite this mantra three or seven times. The mantra says:

*Oṃ svabhāva-śuddhaḥ sarva-dharma svabhāva-śuddho 'haṃ*

By the empowerment of this mantra, the karmas of body, speech, and mind are all purified. Then, touching the five limbs to the ground, one bows in homage to all the buddhas of the ten directions, all the great bodhisattvas, and the vast Mahāyāna. Kneeling on the right knee, one confesses, rejoices, exhorts, and makes vows:

> "I bow in homage to the buddhas of the ten directions, the truly awakened,
> To the most supreme and wondrous dharma, and the assembly of bodhisattvas.
> With the pure karma of body, speech, and mind,
> Earnestly, with palms joined, I reverently bow.
> Throughout beginningless saṃsāra in all the realms of existence,
> The offenses born of karma of body, speech, and mind:
> As the buddhas and bodhisattvas have confessed,
> So now do I confess likewise.
> In the practices and vows of the buddhas and bodhisattvas,
> The merit born of the vajra three karmas,
> That of pratyekabuddhas, śrāvakas, and sentient beings:
> In all the wholesome roots they have gathered, I fully rejoice.
> All the lamps of the world who sit at the bodhimaṇḍa,
> Whose awakened eye opens and shines upon the three realms of existence:
> I now kneel and first exhort them
> To turn the unsurpassed and wondrous dharma-wheel.
> All the tathāgatas, lords of the three realms,
> Who are about to enter nirvāṇa without remainder:
> I exhort them all to abide long,
> Not forsaking their compassionate vow to save the world.
> May the merit of confession, exhortation, and rejoicing
> Cause me never to lose the mind of bodhi.
> Among the wondrous assemblies of buddhas and bodhisattvas,
> May I always be a good friend, never rejected.
> Free from the eight difficulties, born where there is no difficulty,
> With the wisdom of past lives, adorned in bodily marks,
> Far from delusion, endowed with compassion and wisdom,
> May I fully perfect the pāramitās.
> Wealthy, prosperous, and born in a noble clan,
> With an extensive retinue, ever flourishing,
> The four unobstructed eloquences, the ten sovereign masteries,
> The six penetrations, and all dhyānas perfectly complete.
> Like Vajradhvaja and Samantabhadra,
> May my vows of praise and dedication be likewise."

Next, one bows to the principal deity and all the holy assembly. The mantra says:

*Oṃ padma-bhe*

By making obeisance with this mantra, the principal deity and the holy assembly accept one as their charge. Next, before the principal deity, one sits in full or half lotus posture and generates the heart of great compassion: "I practice this dharma so that all sentient beings may swiftly leave the great ocean of birth and death and quickly realize unsurpassed samyak-saṃbodhi." First, one grinds various incenses and applies them to the hands. Then one forms the Buddha-division samaya mudrā.

With both hands in a hollow-palm joined position, open both index fingers and bend them against the upper joints of the middle fingers. Bend both thumbs against the lower joints of the index fingers; the mudrā is thus formed. Place the mudrā at the heart. Visualize the tathāgata's thirty-two marks and eighty minor excellences, clear and distinct as if directly before the eyes, and wholeheartedly recite the mantra seven times. The mantra says:

*Oṃ tathāgato 'dbhavāya svāhā*

By this mudrā and mantra recitation, all the tathāgatas are aroused; they all protect, remember, and empower the practitioner. Their radiance shines upon my body; all karmic obstructions are extinguished, lifespan is extended, and merit increases. The holy assembly of the Buddha division protects and rejoices. In every life, one is freed from the evil destinies, born from a lotus, and swiftly realizes unsurpassed samyak-saṃbodhi.

Next, one forms the Lotus-division samaya mudrā.

With both hands in a hollow-palm joined position, spread both index fingers, middle fingers, and ring fingers, bending them into a lotus shape. Place the mudrā at the heart, visualize Avalokiteśvara with complete marks and excellences, and recite the mantra seven times. Release the mudrā at the crown to the right. The mantra says:

*Oṃ padma-udbhavāya svāhā*

By this mudrā and mantra recitation, Avalokiteśvara and all bodhisattvas and the holy assembly of the Lotus division who hold the lotus are aroused, and all rejoice, empower, and keep the practitioner in mind. The radiance of all bodhisattvas shines upon one's body; all karmic obstructions are extinguished. All bodhisattvas always serve as good friends.

Next, one forms the Vajra-division samaya mudrā.

Turn the left hand palm outward. Place the back of the right palm on the back of the left hand. Hook the thumbs and little fingers of both hands together in a vajra shape. Place the mudrā at the heart, visualize Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva, and recite the mantra seven times. Release at the crown to the left. The mantra says:

*Oṃ vajra-udbhavāya svāhā*

By this mudrā and mantra recitation, the holy assembly of the Vajra division is aroused. All vajra-holders empower and protect, all karmic obstructions are extinguished, all pain and suffering do not attach to the body, and one attains the indestructible vajra body.

Next, one forms the body-protection samaya mudrā.

Cross the fingers inward, right over left. Extend both middle fingers upright with tips touching. Bend both index fingers in a hook shape behind the middle fingers without touching them. Place both thumbs upon the ring fingers; the mudrā is formed. Seal the five places of the body: forehead, then left and right shoulders, then heart, then throat. Release the mudrā above the crown. Recite the mantra once at each point. The mantra says:

*Oṃ vajrāgni-pra-ṇidhaptāya svāhā*

By forming this mudrā and reciting the mantra, one is as if clad in vajra armor. All vināyakas and māras who create obstacles retreat and flee. They see the practitioner's body radiant with majestic virtue and sovereign power. Whether in mountain forests or amidst dangers, one is entirely without fear. Disasters of water, fire, and all calamities; tigers, wolves, and lions; blades, cudgels, fetters, and shackles: all such things are extinguished, and all who see one are delighted. After death, one does not fall into the evil destinies but is born in the pure and wondrous buddha-lands.

Next, one forms the Vajracakra Great Bodhisattva great power seal. Having entered the maṇḍala, one receives the three unobstructed samaya disciplines of the three ages. By entering the maṇḍala, in body and mind one is endowed with the anākṣarika samaya precepts equal to the dust-motes of world-systems in ten directions of dust-mote realms. Whether through bending the body, bowing, speaking, breathing, stirring the mind, or shifting one's thoughts, should one forget the mind of bodhi or lose the wholesome roots, by the supreme skillful means of this mudrā-seal and secret mantra, through recitation and directed attention, one can remove all transgressions. The samaya is restored and shines even more brightly. Because it purifies body, speech, and mind, it constitutes entering all maṇḍalas and obtaining the abhiṣeka samaya. One should form the mudrā and recite the mantra seven times. Cross both hands inward. Extend both middle fingers. Wind both ring fingers around the middle fingers. Extend both index fingers straight. Place the mudrā at the heart. Sincerely recite seven times. The mantra says:

*Namaḥ strīya-dhvikānāṃ sarva-tathāgatānāṃ āṃ viraji viraji mahā-cakra-vajri sata sata sārate sārate trayi trayi vidhamani saṃbhañjani tramati siddha-gṛtāṃ svāhā*

When reciting this mantra, make this contemplation: throughout the entirety of space and the dharma-realm, may all sentient beings in the six destinies of birth and death swiftly enter the great maṇḍala of the universal assembly and become equal to the holy ones.

Next, one forms the meditation mudrā and enters the contemplation of the four immeasurable minds. First, one enters the samādhi of immeasurable loving-kindness. With a pure and earnest mind, one universally contemplates all sentient beings of the six destinies and four modes of birth: they all possess the tathāgata-garbha and are endowed with the three kinds of vajra of body, speech, and mind. By the power of my practice of the three mysteries, may all sentient beings become equal to Samantabhadra Bodhisattva. Having thus contemplated, one recites the mantra of the samādhi of great loving-kindness:

*Oṃ mahā-maitrāya sphara*

Next, one enters the wisdom of the samādhi of immeasurable compassion. With a compassionate mind, one universally contemplates all sentient beings of the six destinies and four modes of birth, drowning in the ocean of suffering of birth and death, unaware of their own mind, vainly generating discriminations and giving rise to all manner of afflictions and karma, and therefore unable to realize the equal true suchness like space, which gives rise to merit as numerous as the sands of the Ganges. By the power of my practice of the three mysteries, may all sentient beings become equal to Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva. Having thus contemplated, one recites the mantra of the samādhi of great compassion:

*Oṃ mahā-kāruṇāya sphara*

Next, one enters the wisdom of the samādhi of immeasurable joy. With a pure mind, one universally contemplates all sentient beings of the six destinies and four modes of birth: they are originally pure like a lotus, unstained by adventitious defilements, their self-nature being pure. By the power of my practice of the three mysteries, may all sentient beings become equal to Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva. Having thus contemplated, one recites the mantra of the samādhi of great joy:

*Oṃ śuddha-pramudita sphara*

Next, one enters the wisdom of the samādhi of immeasurable equanimity. With an equal mind, one universally contemplates all sentient beings of the six destinies and four modes of birth: they are all free from self and possessions, free from the aggregates and elements, free from subject and object. All dharmas are equal, for the mind is originally unborn and its nature and characteristics are empty. By the power of my practice of the three mysteries, may all sentient beings become equal to Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva. Having thus contemplated, one recites the mantra of the samādhi of great equanimity:

*Oṃ mahā-upekṣā sphara*

By the mantra practitioner's cultivation of the four immeasurable minds and recitation of the mantras, all manner of human and celestial māra-difficulties and karmic obstructions are extinguished. Immeasurable merit and wisdom are suddenly gathered, and body and mind become supple and capable of sovereign mastery.

Next, one forms the wheel-altar mudrā.

With both hands each making a vajra fist, the index and little fingers of each hand hook each other. Seal the mouth and recite the mantra; this constitutes entering all maṇḍalas. Then place the mudrā at the crown. At the place where the bodhimaṇḍa has been established, it all becomes the great maṇḍala, as if the principal deity has personally established the wheel-altar. The mantra says:

*Oṃ vajra-cakra hūṃ jaḥ hūṃ vaṃ hoḥ*

By forming this mudrā and reciting the mantra, even if the practitioner has transgressed the dharma, accidentally erred in the three karmas, or broken the samaya precepts, all transgressions can be removed and all brought to perfection.

Next, one forms the mudrā for invoking the principal deity.

Cross both hands inward to make fists. The left thumb enters the palm. With the right thumb, beckon toward oneself. The mantra says:

*Oṃ ārolik ehyehi svāhā*

By the empowerment of this mantra and mudrā, the principal deity bodhisattva does not transgress the original vow, and leads all the holy assembly to come to the bodhimaṇḍa, empowering the practitioner to swiftly fulfill the original vow.

Next, one forms the Hayagrīva Vidyā-King mudrā for dispelling obstacles and sealing the boundary.

Join both palms. Bend both index fingers and ring fingers into the palms with nail-backs facing each other. Extend both thumbs upright; the mudrā is formed. Rotate the mudrā leftward three times, mentally contemplating the dispelling of all obstacle-making māra-spirits, ghosts, and vināyakas, who retreat and flee. Rotate the mudrā rightward three times and wave it up and down; this establishes the firm great boundary. The mantra says:

*Oṃ amṛtodbhava hūṃ phaṭ svāhā*

Next, the argha fragrant-water offering mudrā.

With both hands, hold the argha vessel filled with fragrant water with fresh flowers floating in it, and present it at the level of the forehead. The mantra says:

*Maṃsa maṃsa svāhā*

By offering the argha fragrant-water, the practitioner's three karmas are purified, and all the defilements and obstructions of affliction are washed away. From the stage of resolute practice through the ten bhūmis to the tathāgata-bhūmi, when one is about to realize the pāramitā of each bhūmi, all the tathāgatas anoint one's head with the nectar dharma-water.

Next, one forms the seat-offering mudrā.

With both hands in a lotus-palms joined position, spread and open both ring fingers, bending their tips to form an open lotus shape. The mantra says:

*Oṃ kamala svāhā*

By the empowerment of this mantra and mudrā, the principal deity and the holy assembly truly receive and take their seats upon the lotus jewel-thrones.

Next, one forms the universal offering mudrā.

Join both palms with fingers slightly interlaced, right over left. Place the mudrā upon the heart and recite the mantra five times. From the mudrā there stream forth all manner of offering cloud-oceans: anointing perfume, flower garlands, burnt incense, food and drink, lamps, jeweled vases, and celestial music, universally offered to all buddhas, bodhisattvas, the principal deity, and the holy assembly. The mantra says:

*Namaḥ sarva-tathāgatebhyo viśva-mukhebhyaḥ sarva-thā-kham udgāte spharaṇa imaṃ gagana-khaṃ svāhā*

By the empowerment of this mantra and mudrā, all buddhas, bodhisattvas, the principal deity, and the holy assembly receive true and vast offerings, for so it is by the nature of dharma. Next, one recites the praise:

*Kamala-mukha kamala-locana kamala-saṃna kamala-hasta kamala-bhāmuni kamala kamala-saṃbhava sakara mala kṣa-lana namo 'stu te*

> "Next, form the principal deity's heart secret mudrā,
> And in accord with the teaching, perform the recitation.
> Carefully contemplate the marks and excellences of the principal deity's body,
> Universally radiating immeasurable great light.
> All sentient beings who suffer
> Are touched by this light and brought to peace.
> Having recited the root mantra seven times,
> Release the mudrā above the crown, letting it descend like a streamer."

The heart mudrā: both hands in hollow-palm joined position, wrists together. Move both index fingers back and forth.

The great dhāraṇī of the Holy Thousand-Handed Thousand-Eyed Avalokiteśvara Mahāsattva, the Vast, Perfect, Unobstructed Great Compassion Heart Dhāraṇī, is as stated in the text.

Next, one takes up the prayer beads, holds them at the crown, and empowers them with seven recitations. The mantra says:

*Oṃ virocana-mālā svāhā*

Next, one empowers them with the thousand-turning mantra seven times. The mantra says:

*Oṃ vajra-guhya japa-samaye hūṃ*

Having empowered them, one makes this vow: "May I and all sentient beings swiftly accomplish all supreme wishes, whether worldly or supramundane." With both hands, hold the beads at the heart. Recite the mantra once, and with the last syllable move one bead. Neither too fast nor too slow, neither too loud nor too soft. Pronounce each syllable of the mantra clearly and distinctly. Or practice the vajra recitation, with the tongue-tip slightly moving while the lips and teeth are closed. Free from all distraction, with single-pointed focus on the principal deity, do not attend to any other mental image. Whether a thousand or a hundred times, always fix the count. When the recitation is complete, hold the beads up and bow, sincerely making vows. Return the beads to their place and again form the principal deity's heart secret mudrā for recitation. Release the mudrā as before.

> "With praise and offerings, redouble one's earnestness.
> Offer the argha once more and declare the original vow.
> Again with the Hayagrīva Vidyā-King mudrā,
> Turn it three times with the mantra to release the boundary.
> As with the previous mudrā for invoking the deity,
> Push the thumb outward to complete the sending off."

The mantra says:

*Oṃ ārolik gaccha gaccha svāhā*

> "Having sent off the holy assembly, the practitioner
> Again forms the meditation mudrā, generating loving-kindness and compassion.
> The three divisions' empowerment becomes the vajra armor.
> When bowing is complete, one takes leave and freely walks about.
> One who thus practices in accord with the dharma
> Swiftly gathers merit and wisdom and attains spiritual powers.
> In this present life, one enters the bhūmi of joy,
> And after sixteen lifetimes, one attains true awakening."
