# 九品往生阿彌陀三摩地集陀羅尼經
## Sūtra of the Dhāraṇī Collection of the Amitābha Samādhi for the Nine Grades of Rebirth

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0933

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空)

---

At that time, Vairocana Tathāgata was in the great vihāra of the gate of the great samādhi, together with a great assembly of eighty-nine thousand bhikṣus, all great arhats whose wisdom was fully accomplished and whose tasks were complete. Their names were: Spiritual-Power Wisdom-Eloquence Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, Mahāsthāmaprāpta Bodhisattva, Supernatural-Sovereignty-King Bodhisattva, Pure-Light Immaculate-Dhāraṇī Bodhisattva, Great-Power Universal-Hearing Bodhisattva, Great-Adornment-Power Bodhisattva, Immeasurable-Light Bodhisattva, and Wisdom-Virtue Universal-Radiance-King Bodhisattva. Such great bodhisattvas, śrāvakas, and the great assembly all proceeded to the Buddha's place and addressed the World-Honored One: "The land of Amitāyus resides in the pure consciousness samādhi of the nine grades. This is the realm of all the Buddhas, the dwelling of the Tathāgata. All the Buddhas of the three times attain perfect enlightenment from this, endowed with the three knowledges, and increase merit and wisdom.

"The nine grades of realms are: Upper-grade, upper-birth: the Ground of True Form. Upper-grade, middle-birth: the Immaculate Ground. Upper-grade, lower-birth: the Ground Free from Defilement. Middle-grade, upper-birth: the Ground of perfect enlightenment. Middle-grade, middle-birth: the Ground of Luminous Power. Middle-grade, lower-birth: the Ground Free from Outflows. Lower-grade, upper-birth: the Ground of true enlightenment. Lower-grade, middle-birth: the Ground of Worthy Awakening. Lower-grade, lower-birth: the Ground of the Gate of Bliss. These are called the nine grades of pure consciousness, the realm of true suchness.

"Within them are seated the twelve great maṇḍala images of the great-perfect-mirror-wisdom treasures. Their names are: Buddha of Immeasurable Light, the All-Penetrating Three Knowledges; Buddha of Boundless Light, the Universal-Awakening Three Knowledges; Buddha of Unobstructed Light, the Wisdom-Path Three Knowledges; Buddha of Unopposed Light, the Six-Truth-Principle-Wisdom Three Knowledges; Buddha of Blazing Radiance King Light, the Form-Virtue Three Knowledges; Buddha of Pure Light, the One-Awakening Three Knowledges; Buddha of Joyful Light, the Universal-Gate Three Knowledges; Buddha of Wisdom Light, the Entering-Wisdom Three Knowledges; Buddha of Uninterrupted Light, the Radiant-Form Three Knowledges; Buddha of Inconceivable Light, the Luminous-Penetration Three Knowledges; Buddha of Ineffable Light, the Five-Virtue Three Knowledges; Buddha of Light Surpassing Sun and Moon, the Wisdom-Power Three Knowledges.

"All such Buddhas and Tathāgatas are endowed with true form. They are the support of the compassionate aspects of all the Tathāgatas of the three times. If sentient beings wish to be reborn in such a pure land of the nine grades, and to behold and serve the twelve wondrous radiances, they should, three times daily and nightly, recite the names of the nine grades of pure land and praise the titles of the twelve light-Buddhas. They will thereby permanently escape the burning house of the three realms and will certainly be born in true suchness, leaving behind all outflows and permanently entering the outflow-free state.

"If a person wishes to enter the realm of this samādhi, with the Buddha's wisdom and a purified mind and cleansed body, contemplate and recollect the true-form mantra of the great samaya."

The mantra:

*Oṃ amṛta-teje galaṃ hūṃ*

"Good men! This true-word Brahmic mantra: this seat is the root of the ultimate principle-wisdom of all the Buddhas and Tathāgatas of all the ten directions and three times, and of the twelve vows of emptiness. If my disciples, wishing to benefit and bring happiness to the three realms and to devas and humans, write this sūtra, receive, uphold, read, and recite it, their merit and happiness will increase, their wisdom and eloquence will grow, their life-span and vitality will be extended, their karmic obstructions will be eliminated, their ten thousand illnesses will be extinguished, and their compassion and love will increase. All excellent qualities will be fulfilled without exception. How much more so for those who write and recite with utmost sincerity: they will transcend the three times, attain the unborn, and transform the burning house of the three realms. Through their recitation, they will be reborn in the assembly of the Land of Ultimate Bliss, in the nine grades of the pure land."

At that time, the great assembly, having heard what the Buddha taught, were all greatly delighted, faithfully received it, and practiced accordingly.

---

Colophon: On the eighth day of the fourth month of the fourth year of Kenkyū [1193 CE], at Mount Toganoo, copied and collated from the exemplar of the Ācārya's chamber at the Jison-in of Kanshu-ji, by Kanchō. In the autumn, eighth month, of the first year of Kyōwa [1801 CE], the copy was obtained from Bishop Jijun of Chisan and checked against the printed edition. The Yugashi Amida Daishinshō states: "This great-heart mantra appears in the Amitāyus Ritual and also in the Sūtra of the Nine Grades of Rebirth." It further states: "The ritual explains the supreme power of the great-heart mantra, and the Sūtra of the Nine Grades of Rebirth teaches the same." The Jūshinron Gusō states: "The Sūtra of the Nine Grades of Rebirth says: this is the root great-mantra of Amitābha Tathāgata. If one recites it even once, all the transgressions of the ten evils and five heinous crimes and all karmic obstructions within the body are entirely extinguished." However, this text has only the short mantra and no great-mantra teaching. It is certain that the great-mantra passage has been lost. There are passages in the text that are not fully clear; the reader should be aware. We await a complete copy.

Recorded by Kaidō of Toyoyama Hase, the ascetic practitioner. First collation with additions completed by Jijun.
