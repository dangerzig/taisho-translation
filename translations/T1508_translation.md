# Oral Explanation of the Twelve Links of Dependent Arising from the Āgamas

## *Āhán Kǒujiě Shí'èr Yīnyuán Jīng* (阿含口解十二因緣經)

Taishō 1508

Translated by An Xuan (*Ān Xuán*, 安玄) and the lay-upāsaka Yán Fótiáo (*Yán Fótiáo*, 嚴佛調) of the Later Han dynasty.

*Note: This is one of the earliest Chinese Buddhist texts, a second-century oral instruction on the twelve links of dependent arising (pratītyasamutpāda) and their relationship to the five aggregates, eighteen elements, the Four Noble Truths, and the ten unwholesome actions. It also contains remarkable early material on embryology, the mechanics of rebirth consciousness, parent-child karmic relations, and a closing passage on why women are more frequently reborn in the lower realms. The style is characteristically archaic, reflecting the collaborative translation method of the Parthian monk An Xuan and his Chinese partner Yan Fotiao, who worked together at Luoyang around 181 CE.*

---

Those who wish to cut off birth-and-death and cross over to the way beyond the world should contemplate backward through the twelve links of dependent arising. What are the twelve?

(1) At the root is ignorance (*chī*, 癡). (2) From ignorance arise volitional formations (*suǒ zuò xíng*, 所作行). (3) From formations arises consciousness (*shí*, 識). (4) From consciousness arise name-and-form (*míng sè*, 名色). (5) From name-and-form arise the six deteriorations (*liù shuāi*, 六衰, i.e., the six sense-bases). (6) From the six deteriorations arises contact (*suǒ gèng*, 所更). (7) From contact arises feeling (*tòng*, 痛). (8) From feeling arises craving (*ài*, 愛). (9) From craving arises seeking (*qiú*, 求). (10) From seeking arises obtaining (*dé*, 得). (11) From obtaining arises birth (*shēng*, 生). (12) From birth arise old age, sickness, and death. These are the twelve events of dependent arising.

### The Four Noble Truths

When these twelve events are about to arise, one should extinguish them using the four impermanences. What are the four impermanences? (1) Knowing suffering. (2) Abandoning the accumulation [of causes]. (3) Knowing cessation. (4) Practicing the path. Furthermore, contemplating birth, old age, sickness, and death: by contemplating these four, one turns back the twelve links of dependent arising and the path is accomplished. A practitioner who contemplates these four, wishing to cross beyond the world, should sever the twelve events of dependent arising; this is to sever the root of birth-and-death.

### Internal and External Dependent Arising

The twelve links of dependent arising have internal and external [aspects]: (1) internally, ignorance; externally, earth. (2) Internally, formations; externally, water. (3) Internally, consciousness; externally, fire. (4) Internally, name-and-form; externally, wind. (5) Internally, the six sense-entrances; externally, space. (6) Internally, calamity [i.e., contact]; externally, the seed. (7) Internally, feeling; externally, the root. (8) Internally, craving; externally, the stem. (9) Internally, grasping; externally, the leaf. (10) Internally, becoming; externally, the node. (11) Internally, birth; externally, the flower. (12) Internally, old age and death; externally, the fruit. The birth-and-death of human beings arises from the internal twelve links; the birth-and-death of all things arises from the external twelve links.

### Ignorance Explained

What is ignorance? It means not paying respect to father and mother, not distinguishing between good and evil. From this causal condition, when one obtains suffering, one does not wish to abandon it; one does not believe in this present life or the life to come. Having done such things, one then follows into formations. Without doing them, one also does not attain [release]. Thus, having ignorance, there are formations; having formations, there is consciousness; having consciousness, there is name-and-form; having name-and-form, there are the six sense-entrances; having the six sense-entrances, there is planting [contact]; having planting, there is feeling; having feeling, there is craving; having craving, there is grasping; having grasping, there is becoming; having becoming, there is birth; having birth, there are old age and death.

Therefore, human beings are born through the twelve links of dependent arising; with the twelve links, they are born; without the links, they are not born. All things are likewise. If one does not sever the twelve links of dependent arising, one is not freed from birth-and-death. Practicing the thirty-seven factors of awakening, one attains the Way through this.

### The Five Aspects of the Twelve Links

The twelve links of dependent arising have five aspects: (1) Ignorance; (2) the vital force of birth-and-death and formations: these are the causal conditions of previous lives. (3) Consciousness: from consciousness one receives a body and is born. (4) Name-and-form: the physical body reconstitutes the five aggregates: these are the causal conditions of the present life. (5) The six deteriorations: one again performs the vital force of birth-and-death, planting seeds: these are the causal conditions of future lives. Because the three times, past, present, and future, are mutually linked as causal conditions, there are five aspects.

### The Ten Bodily Deeds and the Twelve Links

The twelve links of dependent arising originate from the ten bodily deeds. How? Seven deeds constitute one [group]; three deeds arise from four. The seven deeds that constitute one group are: killing, stealing, sexual misconduct, divisive speech, harsh speech, false speech, and frivolous speech; together they belong to form as one [aggregate]. The three deeds arising from four are: envy, anger, and ignorance; these arise from feeling, perception, formations, and consciousness. These ten deeds combine into the five aggregates, and thus constitute the twelve links of dependent arising.

Earth is called ignorance. Formations are the flourishing aggregate (*shèng yīn*, 盛陰), constituting the activity of birth-and-death. The seeds one plants oneself are called formations. Having formations, there is consciousness receiving a body in birth-and-death. Having consciousness, there is name-and-form. The body again performs the ten deeds, completing the five aggregates. Having name-and-form, there are the six entrances, again performing the flourishing aggregate, planting seeds; hereafter one must again receive [rebirth]. Because of this mutual linking as causal conditions through birth-and-death, they are called "dependent arising."

The ten bodily deeds constituting the twelve links are: envy is ignorance; anger is the vital force of birth-and-death; doubt is consciousness; killing is name-and-form; stealing is the six deteriorations; sexual misconduct is contact; divisive speech is feeling; false speech is craving; harsh speech is becoming; frivolous speech is aspiration. Because of the existence and non-existence of birth, there are the twelve links of dependent arising.

Why? Sexual misconduct is ignorance; anger is the vital force of birth-and-death; doubt is consciousness. These three internal matters are the root. Having these three, there are the seven [external] matters, completing the five flourishing aggregates. Envy is the root of the five aggregates and therefore constitutes doubt. The five aggregates' actions may be pleasing (then good) or displeasing (then angry), and thereby one performs the vital force of the ten deeds of birth-and-death, externally from the body, internally from the mind. Doubt means not distinguishing good and evil, not knowing birth-and-death; therefore it constitutes consciousness.

A practitioner wishing to sever the twelve links of dependent arising should first sever the ten bodily deeds. This constitutes [severing] ignorance; from [severing] ignorance, the five aggregates are extinguished, and the twelve links are also extinguished.

To sever the ten bodily deeds: externally from the body, internally from the mind; hence "body and mind." To uphold means not killing. This also means externally from the mouth, internally from envy. To sever means: when the mind does not arise toward any of the ten thousand things, then anger stops. When anger stops, killing stops. When killing stops, craving stops. When craving stops, weeping stops.

Externally, the mouth means sound. When sound stops, divisive speech, harsh speech, false speech, and frivolous speech also stop. Without doubt, one enters the path. This is how one reverses the five aggregates and severs the root of the twelve links.

### Three Modes of Receiving a Body

Human beings receive a body in three distinct ways: (1) the five aggregates and the flourishing aggregate; (2) the eighteen elements; (3) the twelve links of dependent arising and their activity. Through these three things, one obtains a body in three distinct ways.

The first, the five aggregates and the flourishing aggregate: the five aggregates arise from the ten bodily deeds. From the eye comes the form aggregate; from the ear, the feeling aggregate; from the nose, the perception aggregate; from the mouth, the formations aggregate; from the mind, the consciousness aggregate. The mind, as master, thinks in response to these six matters as the root: this is the ground of the five aggregates.

The second, eighteen, entering into nineteen faculties: the eighteen elements arise because the five aggregates in their activity become the flourishing aggregate, which has objects and entrances constituting the twelve entrances as the basis. The six sense-faculties become eighteen; since there is consciousness between them, they become nineteen faculties. "Eighteen elements" is said because consciousness does not [separately] arise; hence eighteen elements. This explains the flourishing aggregate giving rise to the eighteen elements and nineteen faculties.

The third, the twelve links of dependent arising, means the five aggregates. The five flourishing aggregates in their activity seek the twelve links, and thereby one has a body. This is the same as the ten bodily deeds, merely distinguished differently. From form one obtains a body; from the [other] four aggregates one obtains a name. From name-and-form one obtains craving and grasping. From grasping comes the activity of ignorance; acting in ignorance completes the twelve links of dependent arising. The practitioner should sever them.

Form is not the body; only name constitutes the body. Although one has eyes, ears, nose, tongue, body, and mind, these also are not the body. Why? If the ear were the person, it should be able to hear everything. From shape one obtains a name, like [the word] "gold." Just as when gold is made into an object, it receives its name from that [shape]. Earth, water, fire, wind, and space: these five things make the body, but they are also not the body. Why? Because the body knows subtle sensation. After a person has died, earth does not know subtle sensation. Knowing that earth, water, fire, wind, and space are not the body, the body is also empty, and the mind is also empty; both are empty and there is nothing that exists, and there is also no feeling. Why? After a person has died, one no longer perceives feeling. This is emptiness. When the mind has already departed from the body, there is also no feeling; it is only because of conditions combining together that [things exist], and they return to nothingness.

### The Twelve Winds of the Body

Within the body there are twelve winds: the upward wind, the downward wind, the eye-wind, the ear-wind, the nose-wind, the back-wind, the side-wind, the navel-wind, the arm-wind, the foot-wind, the bending-wind, and the cutting-wind. When the cutting-wind reaches a sick person, it kills. The killing cutting-wind severs a person's life.

### Birth, Aging, Sickness, and Death

Birth, aging, sickness, and death: "birth" means the first moment of falling into the mother's womb. Once born, one is already aging. "Stopping" means the abiding within the mind. Because of stopping, the breath deteriorates as it goes in and out. Seeing clearly that the body is a combination of ten things is deterioration.

### The Five Elements of the Body

Within the body there are five things: (1) earth, (2) water, (3) fire, (4) wind, (5) space. What is hard is earth; what is soft is water; what is hot is fire; what is breath is wind; [the passages through which] food and drink enter and exit are space. These and other conditions combine to make a person, who then thinks of it as "my body."

If one wishes [to understand] all things, one should examine whether one has a body. There are also five conditions that combine: (1) form, (2) feeling, (3) perception, (4) formations, (5) consciousness. These ten things combining, one then sees birth-and-death.

### Good and Evil Deeds

Deeds are of two kinds: good and evil. Good deeds are of two classes: (1) not committing the three [evils] of the body, the four of speech, and the three of the mind; (2) giving, upholding the precepts, patience, diligence, and freedom from doubt. These are the two kinds of good. Evil is also of two classes: (1) committing the three of the body, the four of speech, the three of the mind, and drinking alcohol; (2) doubt, envy, miserliness, and greed. These are the two kinds of evil. Guarding body, speech, and mind is the Way. A practitioner is born through merit.

Through activity comes old age, sickness, and death, and the body then deteriorates and decays. When a person lies down and sleeps, the mind separates from the body due to earthly conditions. Why does one not die? Because the four things (*sì shì*, 四事) are still combined and have not yet dispersed.

### The Ten, Three, Two, and One

A person performs ten deeds that converge into three; three converge into two; two converge into one. What are the ten? The ten evils, which produce the three deeds of body, speech, and mind. Having three, there are the two of name-and-form. Having name-and-form, one falls into ignorance for a hundred kalpas before one can again become a human being, and it is hard to obtain a complete body.

When a human being is born, the vital essence constitutes the earth, and consciousness constitutes the seed. In the five destinies, consciousness has different names, but they merge into one consciousness. Entering one consciousness, one loses one's former knowledge. For example, when a person is born in a heavenly realm, the human consciousness is taken in and receives the heavenly consciousness, and one then forgets the affairs of the human world. A person comes from non-existence into birth, and from existence into death. Why does one come to birth from non-existence? Because one does not know non-existence, therefore one is born. Why does one die from existence? Because one does not know existence, therefore one dies. If one knows non-existence, one is no longer born. If one knows existence, one no longer dies.

### The Mechanism of Rebirth

When the mind has a thought and that [thought-moment] has ceased, [consciousness] immediately arrives at the place of rebirth. If the thought has not ceased, one also cannot travel to be born in a subsequent [mental moment]. The tip of the thought arises and then ceases; another thought arises again. Although the thoughts are many, one must always receive the causal conditions from the initial thought.

Human beings are born originally through women. The impure secretions of man and woman produce the body. Growing up, one falls under the influence of bad companions, is deceived by the five aggregates, the six abodes, and the twelve deteriorations, and thereby there is old age, sickness, death, grief, and suffering. Birth-and-death is ignorance; the wise person should sever it.

The six abodes are earth, water, fire, wind, space, and consciousness. The twelve deteriorations are form, sound, smell, taste, touch, and thought-desire (*niàn yù*, 念欲), [each having an] internal hidden thief: these are the twelve deteriorations.

### The Three Conditions of Birth

A person's birth has three conditions: (1) combination (*héhuì*, 合會); (2) assemblage (*jù*, 聚); (3) mind, thought, and consciousness (*xīn yì shí*, 心意識). Feeling, perception, formations, and consciousness constitute "combination." All cravings and desires constitute "assemblage." The first [moment] is "mind" (*xīn*), the middle is "thought" (*yì*), and the end is "consciousness" (*shí*).

When a person first falls into the mother's womb, it is like the yolk within an egg. After thirty-seven days, the head, face, hands, feet, and fingers are all complete. Four days before birth, the [fetus] turns downward.

A person in the mother's womb suffers beyond description. While in the womb, life grows daily and consciousness grows daily. The body gradually ages; by half a year, body and consciousness diminish daily. Obtaining a body is both merit and transgression. Why? Obtaining a human body is merit; hunger, thirst, cold, heat, craving, lust, envy, and jealousy are transgression.

### Parent-Child Karmic Connections

A person gives birth to a child through five karmic connections: (1) a prior-life aspiration; (2) shared karma; (3) understanding propriety; (4) coming to collect a debt; (5) repaying a debt.

What is a prior-life aspiration? In a previous life, upon seeing another's handsome child, one made the aspiration: "May my child be like this." Shared karma means jointly engaging in business, obtaining profit, and calling upon each other. Understanding propriety means mutually respecting and loving one another. Coming to collect a debt means the parents manage the livelihood, but the child squanders it. Repaying a debt means the child manages the livelihood and gives it to the parents: this is repaying a debt.

A child is born through three karmic connections: (1) The parents in a previous life owed the child a debt. (2) The child in a previous life owed the parents a debt. (3) An enemy comes to be born as one's child. When the parents toil and seek wealth, having accumulated it, they die, and the child uses it: this is the parents having previously owed the child. When the child goes out and seeks a livelihood, having accumulated it, dies, and the parents use it: this is the child owing the parents. Sometimes a child is born and dies after a hundred days or a thousand days, and the parents grieve and are distressed: this is an enemy following along to be born [as their child].

### Three Classes of Children

Children born are of three classes: (1) meritorious children; (2) true children; (3) untrue children. What are meritorious children? When the parents uphold the precepts, practice giving, patience, diligence, and cultivate the Way, and the child is likewise: these are meritorious children. True children: when the parents do not believe in the Way, but the child alone follows the Way's teaching: this is a true child. Untrue children: when the parents follow the Way's work and practice according to the dharma, but the child only drinks wine and does evil, such that others do not wish to see [the child]: this is an untrue child.

A child is born from parents with shared intention, shared action, or shared thought. When all enjoy longevity, wealth, and beauty: this is shared action. Craving, desire, and anger: this is shared intention. Diligence in practicing the Way: this is shared thought. If one's prior actions were more with the father, the child resembles the father more; if more with the mother, the child resembles the mother more; without shared action, there is no resemblance. When a person is born and placed on the ground, although having no knowledge yet, the child is naturally drawn toward its mother: this is because of the consciousness's original causal connection.

### Rebirth Consciousness

When a person comes to be born, there is a causal connection. The body can enter and exit without interval. After seven days, the body again deteriorates. It also has earth, water, fire, wind, and space, but these are subtle and hard to see.

When a person is old or young, the consciousness increasingly forgets, because the consciousness is gradually turning toward the place of its next birth. Why does one not know in advance the place of one's future birth? Because one has not yet arrived. Others are born, others come; others arrive at others' places; others grieve at others' places. This is because of long habituation from the distant past.

### Suffering of Body and Mind

Without study, the body has three pains, and the mind also has three pains. The body's pains are: (1) being struck by weapons, tiles, and stones, or being kicked and trampled; (2) illness and emaciation; (3) death. The mind's pains are: (1) worry about parents, siblings, spouse, children, and acquaintances; (2) worry about wealth, treasures, and enemies.

Why is there pain? Because the five aggregates are out of balance, therefore there is pain. Why does the aggregate "die"? Because it matures and decays, therefore there is death. All illnesses are produced by the mind; the body has no knowledge and therefore does not produce illness. Why is the body ill? Because the four conditions are out of balance. Why is there grief? Because nothing is as one wishes. Why is there instability? Because illness swiftly passes. Why is there urgency? Because [life] inevitably deteriorates. Why does it crumble? Because the body is aged and toxic. Why are there old age and death? Because [the body] deteriorates. Why is there impermanence? Because one has no self-mastery. Why is there suffering? Because of urgency. Why is there emptiness? Because there is no owner. To say "I made this" is to ask: why is there non-self? Because one cannot be separated from suffering and cannot receive suffering [at will]. These are the ten aspects.

### Three Desires and Three Enemies

People's desires are generally of three kinds, which people love and always wish to obtain: (1) strength and health; (2) peace and security; (3) long life. But there are also three enemies: (1) old age is the enemy of strength and health; (2) sickness is the enemy of peace and security; (3) death is the enemy of long life. There are also three refuges: (1) taking refuge in the Buddha; (2) taking refuge in the Dharma; (3) taking refuge in the Bhikṣu Saṃgha.

### Various Observations

There are four things to be feared: (1) birth, (2) old age, (3) sickness, (4) death.

A person's face turns red from five conditions: (1) being near fire; (2) drinking alcohol; (3) being frightened; (4) thinking in anger; (5) feeling great shame.

Hair turns white from four conditions: (1) excess internal heat; (2) excess worry; (3) much illness; (4) a family tendency toward early graying.

A person becomes thin and emaciated from four conditions: (1) eating little; (2) having worries; (3) much anxiety; (4) illness, with the body not yet balanced.

There are four things that give no advance warning: (1) graying hair; (2) old age; (3) sickness; (4) death. These four things cannot be avoided, cannot be left behind, and cannot be pushed away.

There are four things that cannot be endured: (1) hunger, (2) thirst, (3) cold, (4) heat.

The body has four further aspects: (1) insufficiency, (2) lack of fullness, (3) lack of satiety, (4) lack of contentment.

The body has four pains: (1) the pain of birth, (2) the pain of old age, (3) the pain of sickness, (4) the pain of death.

### The Eight Tastes

All tastes do not exceed eight kinds: (1) bitter, (2) astringent, (3) pungent, (4) salty, (5) bland, (6) sweet, (7) sour, (8) indistinct. Bitterness increases [both] cold and heat. Astringency increases wind and removes cold. Pungency removes water. Sourness removes wind and causes the eyes to dim.

There are three kinds of food that enter completely into the bones, marrow, and blood vessels: (1) fats and oils, (2) toxins, (3) alcohol. These three all pervade the entire body without exception. All food and drink contains fats and oils, differing only in whether thin or thick, much or little.

### Four Classes and Four Kinds of Birth

People are of four classes: (1) the householder class, (2) the practitioner class, (3) the shaman-diviner class, (4) the farmer class.

Births are of four kinds: (1) womb-born (*fù shēng*, 腹生), (2) moisture-born (*hán rè hé shēng*, 寒熱和生), (3) transformation-born (*huà shēng*, 化生), (4) egg-born (*luǎn shēng*, 卵生). Womb-born means humans and animals. Moisture-born means insects, moths, fleas, and lice. Transformation-born means devas and hell-beings. Egg-born means birds, fish, and turtles.

### The Bones and Channels

A person's head has forty-five bones. From the waist upward, fifty-one bones. The four limbs have one hundred and four bones. In total, two hundred bones. A person's body has seven hundred thousand channels and nine hundred and ninety thousand pores. If one attains contemplation, one can see and distinguish all of them oneself.

### Women and the Lower Realms

There was an arhat who, with the divine eye's penetrating vision, saw that the number of women falling into the hells was extremely great. He then asked the Buddha: "Why is this?"

The Buddha said: "Because of four causal conditions: (1) Greed for precious objects, clothing, and ornaments, always wanting more. (2) Mutual jealousy and envy. (3) Excessive gossip and quarrelsome speech. (4) Making oneself attractive through adornment, with excessive sexual desire. For these reasons, the number [of women] falling into the hells is great."
