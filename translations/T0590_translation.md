# 佛說四天王經
## Sūtra of the Four Heavenly Kings, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0590

Translated from the Chinese. Liu Song dynasty, translated by Zhiyan and Baoyun (智嚴共寶雲)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was in Śrāvastī, at the Jetavana Monastery of Anāthapiṇḍada.

The Buddha said to his disciples: "Guard your minds and thoughts well. Do not love the six desires. Cleanse your feelings and remove defilement. Let non-seeking be foremost. Be pure within. Outwardly fulfill filial duty. With the four immeasurable minds, support and nourish those who gave you life. At dawn enter the temple of the honored one, bow your head, and repent of your faults. In the morning receive instruction and in the evening recite; contemplate the wondrous meaning of the sūtras. With the Buddha's grave precepts, cure the ailments of the mind's defilement. In a solemn and quiet place, practice the counting of breaths and meditation. Reverse the flow and exhaust the source, seeking the truth of the Way. Life is like lightning: in a flash it is extinguished.

"On the fast days, examine the mind, be cautious with the body, and guard the mouth. On these days the devas inspect the good and evil of human beings. Atop Mount Sumeru is the second Trāyastriṃśa heaven. The lord of that heaven is named Indra. His merit is lofty and majestic; he presides over the four heavens. The four heavenly spirit-kings are Indra's four garrison commanders, each governing one direction. On the eighth day of each month, they dispatch their messengers below to inspect the world and observe the good and evil of emperors, ministers, the people, nāgas, ghosts, insects, crawling creatures, and all that wriggles and moves, in both thought and deed. On the fourteenth day they dispatch their princes below. On the fifteenth day the four heavenly kings descend in person. On the twenty-third day the messengers descend again. On the twenty-ninth day the princes descend again. On the thirtieth day the four kings descend in person once more.

"When the four kings descend, the sun, moon, five planets, and twenty-eight constellations, together with all the devas among them, all descend together. The four kings command: 'Diligently observe all beings and inspect their auspicious and inauspicious conduct. If on these days beings take refuge in the Buddha, take refuge in the Dharma, take refuge in the bhikṣu-saṃgha; if with pure hearts they keep the fast, give to the poor and destitute; uphold the precepts, practice patience, are diligent, meditate; study the sūtras and explain their meaning, illuminate the blind and darkened; are filial toward both parents, revere and serve the Three Treasures; bow and receive the Dharma, practice the four immeasurable minds, and with kindness nurture all beings: report all of this in detail to Indra.' If many cultivate merit and are diligent without negligence, Indra and his thirty-three counselors all rejoice together.

"Indra then commands the life-recorders to increase their lifespans and add to their allotted years. He dispatches good spirits to guard and protect their persons. According to the number of precepts upheld: if one keeps one precept, five spirits guard that person; if one keeps all five precepts, twenty-five spirits guard the gates of the household. Epidemics and pestilence, all manner of evil, sinister plots: all are extinguished. At night there are no bad dreams. Officials, thieves, floods, fires, and disasters all in the end cause no harm. Calamities and portents are dispelled. Only through these four immeasurable minds, five precepts, and six monthly fasts, like a great flood extinguishing a small fire, is there any fire that is not extinguished? When their lifespan ends, their spirits are welcomed and ascend to be born in the heavens, in palaces of the seven treasures, with no wish unfulfilled.

"But if there are those who take the lives of beings, who are filthy and corrupt, who steal, who violate others' wives in sexual misconduct, who speak with a forked tongue, who curse harshly, who speak falsely or frivolously, who practice sorcery and curses, who are jealous, angry, and deluded, who go against the Way and are unfilial, who defy the Buddha, defy the Dharma, and slander the bhikṣu-saṃgha, who argue good and evil with reversed logic: for all who engage in such conduct, the four kings report it. Indra and all the devas are displeased. The good spirits no longer guard and protect them, and the sun and moon lose their light, the constellations lose their courses, and the wind and rain come at the wrong times.

"It is to show the people of this present world that they should reform the past and cultivate the future, washing their hearts and being solemn and respectful. They should confess before the Three Treasures, nourish their parents with the four immeasurable minds, be loyal to the emperor, offer gentle counsel with a compassionate heart, be utterly sincere and without deceit, reform what went before and cultivate what comes after, abandon the impure and corrupt ways and follow the path of purity.

"If there are those who reform their crooked conduct and follow the true and right, Indra and the four kings all rejoice without exception. The sun and moon will be clear and bright, the constellations will follow their courses, the wind and rain will come in due season, poisonous vapors will dissipate, sweet dew will descend from the heavens, and clear springs will flow from the earth. The grains will be nourishing and flavorful, and those who eat them will have few illnesses. Their countenance will be radiant and their lifespans will grow longer. In life they will not know prison; in death they will be born above in the heavens. By their merit they will accomplish whatever they wish. They will fly about freely; whether living or dead, they will be at ease. Their heads will be encircled by sunlight. Their food will digest of itself, without the affliction of bodily waste. Their bodies will be fragrant and pure, and their breath sweetly scented. Today the sun, moon, and constellations are the celestial mansions of the devas! Their palaces of the seven treasures hang in the sky, manifesting according to one's aspiration. When their lifespan ends and they descend to be reborn, they will be born into the household of a prince or lord. Their countenance will be splendid and radiant, and all who see them will rejoice in their hearts. They will encounter the Buddha and meet the Dharma, connected to one worthy sage after another, practicing with vigor. They will not encounter wrongdoing, and they will certainly attain nirvāṇa. All of this comes about through the five precepts, the ten good deeds, restraining the passions, mastering desire, and the six monthly fasts!

"In the time of Krakucchanda Buddha, people lived sixty thousand years, and their nature was effortless. They protected others as they would themselves, equal and without distinction. After that Buddha departed the world and the true teaching declined, the people lacked right conduct, and gradually turned to evil. Their lifespans diminished day by day, until they reached a hundred years. After my passing, the people will violate the Buddha's teaching, and there will no longer be filial children. The life-recorders will reduce their allotted years, and their lifespans will diminish day by day. The heavenly spirits will not protect them, and malignant epidemics and evil spirits will come to harm them daily. Disasters and calamities will follow one after another, and their wishes will be thwarted, and unprovoked misfortune will strike. In life they will be caught in the prison of the king's law; in death they will enter Naraka, the realm of hungry ghosts, and the animal realm. If they emerge to be human, they will certainly be of low station. Good and evil follow the body like the five grains: one reaps the harvest of what one has sown. It is like writing at night: when the fire is extinguished, the writing remains. When the body dies and the name perishes, the consequences of merit and wrongdoing do not decay. Guard your minds carefully, restrain the body, guard the mouth. Through the five precepts, the ten good deeds, one can from this attain the Way. My attainment of buddhahood today is the result of accumulated practice!"

The bhikṣus, hearing this sūtra, were all filled with great joy. They bowed to the Buddha and departed.
