# 佛說兜沙經
## Duśa Sūtra Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0280

Translated from the Chinese. Later Han dynasty, translated by Lokakṣema (支婁迦讖)

---

Through the awesome supernatural power of all the Buddhas: so it is for all Buddhas of the past, future, and present.

The Buddha was in the country of Magadha, in a place of Dharma-purity, a place called the Field of Questioning and Purity.

When the Buddha first attained Buddhahood, the light and radiance were exceedingly bright. There was a natural vajra lotus, vast and magnificent. There was a natural lion throne; all the Buddhas of the past also sat upon it, with deportment and manner identical without difference.

Having already gone beyond the realm of supreme characteristics, the bodily conduct of all Buddhas was entirely equal and complete; their radiance was without any further obstruction.

In the abodes of immeasurable Buddhas, the abodes of immeasurable Dharmas, the abodes of immeasurable great assemblies of the ten directions, and the abodes of immeasurable buddha-lands: this is what has been produced by the Buddhas of past, future, and present equally.

The bodhisattvas all came from various lands for a great assembly. Their number was as numerous as the atoms of ten buddha-lands: each atom representing one bodhisattva, to that extent.

All the bodhisattvas were of the rank of one-birth-supplement. All entered into the domain of peace and comfort for the peoples of the ten directions; all entered into the abode of the Dharma; all entered into the lands of the ten directions; all entered into the wisdom of nirvāṇa of the ten directions; all entered into the past-life deeds of the world's beings of the ten directions; all entered into the progressive deepening of bodhisattvas; all entered into all the dharmas of wisdom; all entered into the dharmas of inner and outer; all entered into the dharmas that neither move nor waver; all entered into the dharmas of past, future, and present.

The bodhisattvas deliberated thus: "The Buddha loves us and our kind."

What the bodhisattvas thought was: "Reveal and show us the buddha-lands, the places where the Buddha has traveled and dwelt. Let us hear all that there is: show us the purity of the buddha-lands, show us the purity of the Buddha's Dharma.

"The Dharma that the Buddha teaches, may he cause us all to see it. Show us the formation and destruction of the buddha-lands, and let us see them all. Show us the arising and appearing of all the Buddhas, and show us the good and evil of the buddha-lands; may the Buddha reveal all of this to us.

"Show us all the existing lands of the ten directions; show us the immeasurable Dharma taught by all the Buddhas; show us the ten Dharma-abodes of the bodhisattvas; show us the ten Dharma-practices of the bodhisattvas; show us the ten Dharma-confessions of the bodhisattvas; show us the ten stages of the bodhisattvas; show us the ten samādhi-powers of the bodhisattvas; show us the ten dwelling-places and vows of the bodhisattvas; show us the ten wisdoms of the bodhisattvas; show us the ten samādhis of the bodhisattvas; show us the ten methods of supernatural flight of the bodhisattvas; show us the ten seals of the bodhisattvas; show us when all the bodhisattvas come flying; show us the names of the Buddhas, which are inexhaustible.

"May the Buddha then cause us to protect the peoples of the world; may all the buddha-lands of the ten directions be entirely pure and without blemish. Show us what the Buddhas know: the existence and non-existence of things. Show us the roots of all evil, and cause them all to be entirely purified.

"May all dharmas be taught to us; may all doubts and difficulties be resolved for us; may all perplexities be entirely resolved; may all desires be severed.

"Show us the places where the Buddhas dwell; show us the divisions and realms of the Buddhas' Dharma; show us the awesome supernatural power of the Buddhas; show us the deeds of the Buddhas; show us the strength of the Buddhas.

"Show us the four fearlessnesses of the Buddhas; show us the samādhis the Buddhas enter; show us the transformations the Buddhas perform.

"Show us that none can surpass the Buddhas; show us the honored titles of the Buddhas, which none can equal or reach.

"Show us the faculties of the Buddhas; show us the Buddhas' flights; show us the Buddhas' radiance; show us the Buddhas' wisdom; show us the four fearlessnesses of the Buddhas."

The Buddha fully knew what all the bodhisattvas were thinking, and the Buddha fully manifested his radiance and supernatural power.

In the east, at an extremely distant, immeasurable number of buddha-lands, there was a Buddha named Akṣobhya. His land was called Pure Joy. The Bodhisattva Mañjuśrī came from that land, together with bodhisattvas as numerous as the atoms of the lands of the ten directions. All came forward, bowed to the Buddha in homage, and each sat upon a natural lion throne beneath a jeweled canopy.

In the south, at an extremely distant, immeasurable number of buddha-lands, there was a Buddha named Unimpeded. His land was called Exalted Joy. The Bodhisattva Buddhaśrī came from that land, together with bodhisattvas as numerous as the atoms of ten buddha-lands. All came forward, bowed to the Buddha in homage, and each sat upon a natural lion throne beneath a jeweled canopy.

In the west, at an extremely distant, immeasurable number of buddha-lands, there was a Buddha named Unmoving. His land was called Wave-Joy. The Bodhisattva Ratnaśrī came from that land, together with bodhisattvas as numerous as the atoms of ten buddha-lands. All came forward, bowed to the Buddha in homage, and each sat upon a natural lion throne beneath a jeweled canopy.

In the north, at an extremely distant, immeasurable number of buddha-lands, there was a Buddha named Aja. His land was called Abiding Joy. The Bodhisattva Dānaśrī came from that land, together with bodhisattvas as numerous as the atoms of ten buddha-lands. All came forward, bowed to the Buddha in homage, and each sat upon a natural lion throne beneath a jeweled canopy.

In the northeast, at an extremely distant, immeasurable number of buddha-lands, there was a Buddha named Aśūnya. His land was called Superior Joy. The Bodhisattva Guṇaśrī came from that land, together with bodhisattvas as numerous as the atoms of ten buddha-lands. All came forward, bowed to the Buddha in homage, and each sat upon a natural lion throne beneath a jeweled canopy.

In the southeast, at an extremely distant, immeasurable number of buddha-lands, there was a Buddha named Acala. His land was called Firm Joy. The Bodhisattva Nairaśrī came from that land, together with bodhisattvas as numerous as the atoms of ten buddha-lands. All came forward, bowed to the Buddha in homage, and each sat upon a natural lion throne beneath a jeweled canopy.

In the southwest, at an extremely distant, immeasurable number of buddha-lands, there was a Buddha named Utsiṃha. His land was called Beautiful Joy. The Bodhisattva Vidyāśrī came from that land, together with bodhisattvas as numerous as the atoms of ten buddha-lands. All came forward, bowed to the Buddha in homage, and each sat upon a natural lion throne beneath a jeweled canopy.

In the northwest, at an extremely distant, immeasurable number of buddha-lands, there was a Buddha named Apara. His land was called Active Joy. The Bodhisattva Dharmaśrī came from that land, together with bodhisattvas as numerous as the atoms of ten buddha-lands. All came forward, bowed to the Buddha in homage, and each sat upon a natural lion throne beneath a jeweled canopy.

In the lower direction, at an extremely distant, immeasurable number of buddha-lands, there was a Buddha named Vaiśramaṇa. His land was called Excellent Joy. The Bodhisattva Kṣāntiśrī came from that land, together with bodhisattvas as numerous as the atoms of ten buddha-lands. All came forward, bowed to the Buddha in homage, and each sat upon a natural lion throne beneath a jeweled canopy.

In the upper direction, at an extremely distant, immeasurable number of buddha-lands, there was a Buddha named Dharśa. His land was called Full Joy. The Bodhisattva Nāradaśrī came from that land, together with bodhisattvas as numerous as the atoms of ten buddha-lands. All came forward, bowed to the Buddha in homage, and each sat upon a natural lion throne beneath a jeweled canopy.

The Bodhisattva Mañjuśrī, bearing the Buddha's supernatural power, surveyed all the bodhisattvas everywhere, and with skillful wisdom spoke: "The great assembly of bodhisattvas, how magnificent it is! The buddha-lands of all the immeasurable Buddhas, the places where they dwell, all the places; the robes of the Buddha-dharma, the Dharma the Buddhas teach, the supernatural power of the buddha-lands, the deeds of the Buddhas, the strength of the Buddhas, the good and evil of the buddha-lands, the immeasurable Dharma of the Buddhas.

"Why is this? The Buddhas' teachings in all the buddha-lands of the ten directions, and their liberation of the beings of the ten directions: all dharmas are profoundly deep and without limit, like empty space, with nothing whatsoever that obstructs.

"Why is this? In this sahā world-realm, in all its regions, the various kinds of people each have different bodies, different names, different appearances, different heights, different lifespans, different forms, different thoughts, different ideas, different voices. Each hears the Buddha's voice differently.

"Why is this land called Sahā? In its various regions, some call the Buddha Jinada, some call him Viśvamandana, some call him Indrānīti, some call him Śākyamuni, some call him Dundubhisaṃghoṣa, some call him Nārāyaṇa, some call him Gautama-Jñānapti, some call him Mahāśramaṇa, some call him Śrīmān-Āśvāsana, some call him Citrāśvāsana. In all the regions thus, each calls the Buddha Śākyamuni by different names, altogether numbering ten thousand appellations.

"Thus in all the ten directions, in immeasurably distant buddha-lands, the various peoples of all nations, each with their different languages, call upon the name of the Buddha Śākyamuni. In each and every buddha-land, there are altogether one hundred million different names.

"From the beginning, when the Buddha Śākyamuni first began to cultivate the path, those whom he taught and trained as disciples were people such as these."

The Buddha emitted radiance. First, light came forth from beneath his feet and illuminated one entire buddha-realm with extreme brilliance, revealing ten billion Jambudvīpas to the south, ten billion great oceans, ten billion Mount Sumerus, ten billion Cakravāḍa Mountains, ten billion Pūrvavidehas to the east, ten billion Aparagodānīyas to the west, ten billion Uttarakurus to the north, ten billion Cāturmahārājakāyika heavens, ten billion Trāyastriṃśa heavens, ten billion Yāma heavens, ten billion Tuṣita heavens, ten billion Nirmāṇarati heavens, ten billion Paranirmitavaśavartin heavens, ten billion Brahma heavens, ten billion Brahmakāyika heavens, ten billion Brahmapurohita heavens, ten billion Brahmapāriṣadya heavens, ten billion Mahābrahmā heavens, ten billion Parīttābha heavens, ten billion Apramāṇābha heavens, ten billion Ābhāsvara heavens, ten billion Parīttaśubha heavens, ten billion Apramāṇaśubha heavens, ten billion Śubhakṛtsna heavens, ten billion Anabhraka heavens, ten billion Puṇyaprasava heavens, ten billion Bṛhatphala heavens, ten billion Avṛha heavens, ten billion Atapa heavens, ten billion Sudṛśa heavens, ten billion Sudarśana heavens, ten billion Akaniṣṭha heavens, and ten billion Bhavāgra heavens.

All such heavenly abodes were each illuminated and made visible. This entire buddha-realm was completely illuminated, for the Buddha Śākyamuni presides over it all.

In the ten directions, each direction contains one hundred million minor lands, each with one great ocean and one Mount Sumeru, reaching up to the thirty-three heavens. One minor land encompasses all such domains.

In total there are ten billion minor lands, which together form one buddha-land called Sahā.

The Buddha distributed his emanation-bodies throughout all ten billion minor lands. In each minor land there was one Buddha; altogether there were ten billion Buddhas, all together with the bodhisattvas, seated in the ten billion minor lands.

All the devas and humans could see the Buddha. All the bodhisattvas, devas, and humans, bearing the Buddha's supernatural power, regarded each other as though meeting face to face, and saw the Bodhisattva Mañjuśrī.

There were further: the Bodhisattva Mañjuśrī, the Bodhisattva Ratnaśrī, the Bodhisattva Dānaśrī, the Bodhisattva Guṇaśrī, the Bodhisattva Buddhaśrī, the Bodhisattva Nairaśrī, the Bodhisattva Vidyāśrī, the Bodhisattva Kṣāntiśrī, the Bodhisattva Nāradaśrī, and the Bodhisattva Dharmaśrī.

The buddha-lands where these bodhisattvas dwell are all supremely wondrous and beautiful. Those lands each have their own names: the Buddha of Acala-Joy, the Buddha of Unimpeded-Joy, the Buddha of Aja-Joy, the Buddha of Exalted-Joy, the Buddha of Acala-Joy, the Buddha of Utsiṃha-Joy, the Buddha of Indra-Joy, the Buddha of Brahma-Joy, and the Buddha of Vijñāna-Joy.
