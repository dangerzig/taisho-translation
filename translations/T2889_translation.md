# 佛說續命經
## Sūtra on Prolonging Life, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 2889

Translated from the Chinese. Dynasty and translator unknown (失譯)

---

> Homage to the greatly compassionate, greatly merciful Avalokiteśvara who relieves suffering.
> May [your] holy hand stroke my crown,
> Saving and protecting those heavy with sin in the Sahā world,
> Establishing [them] in the ground of nirvāṇa and liberation.
> Unceasingly making offerings to all buddhas,
> Ferrying all beings across; like a shadow following a form...
> May [I] always uphold [this] and never abandon it.

Amitābha Buddha of the Western [Pure Land], Bodhisattva Avalokiteśvara, and Bodhisattva Mahāsthāmaprāpta possess this power: those who recite the names of these one Buddha and two Bodhisattvas will be liberated from the suffering of birth and death and will never enter the hells. They will always encounter good spiritual friends. Those who have doubts or face difficulties; if they recite this sūtra one thousand times, they will immediately attain liberation.

> First vow: May the Three Treasures always stand firm.
> Second vow: May wind and rain follow their proper seasons.
> Third vow: May the king live ten thousand years.
> Fourth vow: May the borderlands be free from mounds of blades.
> Fifth vow: May the three lower realms be freed from suffering.
> Sixth vow: May all hundred illnesses be completely eliminated.
> Seventh vow: May all sentient beings practice compassion and filial piety.
> Eighth vow: May slaughterers refrain from killing living beings.
> Ninth vow: May those imprisoned be set free.
> Tenth vow: May the dharmadhātu be universally at peace.
>
> Eye vow: May [my] eyes never see the flash of blades.
> Ear vow: May [my] ears never hear sounds of resentment and misfortune.
> Mouth vow: May [my] mouth not speak words contrary to the heart.
> Hand vow: May [my] hands never kill a single sentient being.
> Universal vow: In the future, may [we] encounter Maitreya, and hand in hand with our companions, enter the Phantom City.
