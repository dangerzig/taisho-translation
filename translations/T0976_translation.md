# 佛頂大白傘蓋陀羅尼經
## Sūtra of the Great White Parasol Buddha-Uṣṇīṣa Dhāraṇī

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0976

Translated from the Chinese. Yuan dynasty, translated by Śarabha (沙囉巴)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Bhagavān was in the Good Dharma Hall of the Trāyastriṃśa Heaven, together with bhikṣus, great bodhisattvas, Śakra-devānām-indra, and an immeasurable assembly. At that time, the Bhagavān spread his seat and sat, then entered the samādhi of universal contemplation of the uṣṇīṣa. Then the Bhagavān suddenly, from his fleshly uṣṇīṣa, proclaimed the secret and subtle Dharma-practice:

"Homage to all the Buddhas and all the great bodhisattvas. Homage to the Buddha. Homage to the Dharma. Homage to the Saṃgha. Homage to the Buddhas of seven koṭis and all the great śrāvaka assemblies. Homage to all the arhats of the world. Homage to all the stream-enterers. Homage to all the once-returners. Homage to all the non-returners. Homage to all the right-practice assemblies of the world. Homage to the assemblies proceeding toward right practice. Homage to the great devaṛṣi assemblies who by means of spells and imprecations can also benefit beings. Homage to the accomplished vidyādhara assemblies. Homage to the assembly of Mahābrahma Kings. Homage to the assembly of Śakra-devānām-indra. Homage to the assembly of Rudra, Umāpati, and Sahāya. Homage to the assembly of the Loveless Son, who reveres the five great mudrās. Homage to the assembly of Mahākāla, who destroys the fortress of the three realms, who delights in the charnel ground, and whom the mother-ghosts revere. Homage to the Bhagavān Tathāgata-kula. Homage to the Padma-kula. Homage to the Vajra-kula. Homage to the Ratna-kula. Homage to the Mahānāga-kula. Homage to the Kumāra-kula. Homage to the Nāga-vaṃśa-kula. Homage to the Bhagavān Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha, the Heroic Steadfast Armament King Buddha. Homage to the Bhagavān Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha, Amitābha Buddha. Homage to the Bhagavān Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha, Akṣobhya Buddha. Homage to the Bhagavān Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha, Bhaiṣajyaguru-vaiḍūrya-prabhā-rāja Buddha. Homage to the Bhagavān Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha, Śāla-puṣpa-samantakoṭi-vikasita-rāja Buddha. Homage to the Bhagavān Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha, Śākyamuni Buddha. Homage to the Bhagavān Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha, Ratna-ketu-rāja Buddha. Homage to the Bhagavān Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha, Samantabhadra-rāja Buddha. Homage to the Bhagavān Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha, Vairocana Buddha. Homage to the Bhagavān Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha, Viśāla-netra-utpala-gandha-ketu-rāja Buddha.

"Having thus paid homage to the Buddhas and others, the Bhagavatī, the Great White Parasol Buddha-Uṣṇīṣa, the peerless Pratyaṅgirā Mother, can extinguish all bhūta-demons and ghosts, sever all vidyā-spells, release all bonds, avert untimely death, grief, affliction, and evil dreams, crush eighty-four thousand demon-spirits and the eight seizers. Further, she can cause the twenty-eight nakṣatras to produce great joy, and can divert all enmity and harm. All who raise poisonous intent and all evil dreams are annihilated.

> "Peerless one of great power,
> the violent and mighty mother,
> the blazing and radiant mother,
> the great powerful white-parasol mother,
> the flame-garlanded white-robed mother,
> Tārā of the frowning countenance,
> the Victorious Vajra renowned in garlands,
> of lotus and vajra aspect,
> the garlanded peerless one,
> the Vajra-Wall Destroyer Mother,
> revered by all the good devas,
> of auspicious form and great awesome radiance,
> the Rescuing Mother of great power,
> Vajra-śṛṅkhalā and the rest,
> the Vajra-youths bearing the lineage,
> holding vajras, of the golden-garland lineage,
> the tawny-hued jewel-mother,
> the radiant vajra-garlanded one,
> the graceful vajra-holding mother,
> whose eyes shine like golden light,
> the blazing white vajra mother,
> of lotus, sun, and moonlight.

"All you mudrā-hosts, may you protect me! May you protect me!

"*Oṃ ṛṣi-gaṇa praśasta-sitātapatroṣṇīṣa sarva-tathāgata-uṣṇīṣa-sitātapatrī hūṃ ṭrūṃ jambhana-kari hūṃ ṭrūṃ stambhana-kari hūṃ ṭrūṃ mahānāga-kari hūṃ ṭrūṃ mahā-vidyā-saṃrakṣana-kari hūṃ ṭrūṃ pratividyā-saṃvāsana-kari hūṃ ṭrūṃ sarva-duṣṭānāṃ stambhana-kari hūṃ ṭrūṃ sarva-yakṣa-rākṣasa-graha-nāṃ vidhvaṃsana-kari hūṃ ṭrūṃ catur-aśīti-nāṃ graha-sahasra-nāṃ vidhvaṃsana-kari hūṃ ṭrūṃ aṣṭa-viṃśati-nāṃ nakṣatrāṇāṃ prasādana-kari hūṃ ṭrūṃ aṣṭānāṃ mahā-grahāṇāṃ vidhvaṃsana-kari hūṃ ṭrūṃ rakṣa rakṣa māṃ*

"May you protect me! May you protect me! The Bhagavatī, the Great White Parasol Buddha-Uṣṇīṣa, Vajra-uṣṇīṣa-śikhin Pratyaṅgirā Mother, with a thousand hands and a thousand arms, a hundred thousand koṭi-nayuta eyes, of the aspect of non-dual blazing glory, the Vajra Great and Vast Mother, who has attained great sovereignty within the three realms: may you protect me! May you protect me!

"*Oṃ*: fear of kings, fear of bandits, fear of fire, fear of water, fear of poison, fear of weapons, fear of invasion by enemy armies, fear of famine, fear of enmity and harm, fear of plague, fear of thunderbolts, fear of untimely death, fear of earthquakes, fear of shooting stars, fear of imprisonment, fear of devas, fear of nāgas, fear of lightning, fear of sky-travelers, fear of wild beasts. Furthermore, there are deva-possessions, nāga-possessions, asura-possessions, wind-spirit possessions, sky-traveler possessions, gandharva-possessions, human and non-human possessions, great-serpent possessions, obstacle-causing possessions, rākṣasa-possessions, hungry-ghost possessions, ḍākinī possessions, flesh-eating possessions, bhūta-possessions, kumbhāṇḍa-possessions, stinking-ghost possessions, foul-smelling ghost possessions, skanda-possessions, stupor-possessions, madness-possessions, nightmare possessions, those that make one fall, revati-possessions. Against all such ghosts and possessions, may I be at peace! Furthermore, those who eat vitality, those who eat vital essence, those who eat embryos, those who eat blood, those who eat fat, those who eat flesh, those who eat tallow, those who eat marrow, those who eat afterbirth, those who steal life, those who eat vomit, those who eat filth, those who eat urine, those who eat leaking water, those who eat leftover food, those who eat spittle, those who eat saliva, those who eat mucus, those who eat pus, those who eat offerings, those who eat garlands, those who eat incense, those who eat fragrance, those who steal thought, those who eat flowers, those who eat fruit, those who eat grain, those who eat fire-offerings: against all such beings, may I be at peace!

"All the vidyā-spells cast by such assemblies and by all ghosts and possessions are entirely severed. Nail them with the vajra-peg! The vidyā-spells cast by all wanderers are entirely severed. Nail them with the vajra-peg! The vidyā-spells cast by sky-travelers and ḍākinīs are entirely severed. Nail them with the vajra-peg! The vidyā-spells cast by Mahāpaśupati are entirely severed. Nail them with the vajra-peg! The vidyā-spells cast by the Loveless Son are entirely severed. Nail them with the vajra-peg! The vidyā-spells cast by the sky-traveling true assemblies are entirely severed. Nail them with the vajra-peg! The vidyā-spells cast by the hosts of Mahākāla mother-ghosts are entirely severed. Nail them with the vajra-peg! The vidyā-spells cast by skull-bearers are entirely severed. Nail them with the vajra-peg! The vidyā-spells cast by those who accomplish honey-rites for victory are entirely severed. Nail them with the vajra-peg! The vidyā-spells cast by the four sisters are entirely severed. Nail them with the vajra-peg! The vidyā-spells cast by Vṛkṛtī's assemblies of desire-masters and gathering-lords are entirely severed. Nail them with the vajra-peg! The vidyā-spells cast by the unclothed virtue-exhorters are entirely severed. Nail them with the vajra-peg! The vidyā-spells cast by arhats are entirely severed. Nail them with the vajra-peg! The vidyā-spells cast by the passionless are entirely severed. Nail them with the vajra-peg! The vidyā-spells cast by Guhyaka Vajrapāṇi are entirely severed. Nail them with the vajra-peg! Homage to the Bhagavatī, the Great White Parasol Buddha-Uṣṇīṣa Mother! May you protect me! May you protect me!

"*Oṃ asita-analārka-prabha-sphuṭa-vikasita-sitātapatrī jala jala gata gata dhara dhara vidhara vidhara cinda cinda bhinda bhinda hūṃ hūṃ phaṭ phaṭ svāhā hehe phaṭ huhu phaṭ amukīya phaṭ apratihata phaṭ prapratāya phaṭ asura-vidrāvaṇa-karīya phaṭ sarva-deva-vidyā phaṭ sarva-nāga-vidyā phaṭ sarva-asura-vidyā phaṭ sarva-māruta-vidyā phaṭ sarva-garuḍa-vidyā phaṭ sarva-gandharva-vidyā phaṭ sarva-kiṃnara-vidyā phaṭ sarva-mahoraga-vidyā phaṭ sarva-yakṣa-vidyā phaṭ sarva-rākṣasa-vidyā phaṭ sarva-pretī-vidyā phaṭ sarva-piśācī-vidyā phaṭ sarva-bhūta-vidyā phaṭ sarva-kumbhāṇḍa-vidyā phaṭ sarva-pūtanā-vidyā phaṭ sarva-kaṭapūtanā-vidyā phaṭ sarva-skandha-vidyā phaṭ sarva-unmāda-vidyā phaṭ sarva-jvarī-vidyā phaṭ sarva-apasmārī-vidyā phaṭ sarva-uṣṭarākṣa-vidyā phaṭ sarva-dhārī-vidyā phaṭ sarva-duḥsvapna-vidyā phaṭ sarva-cārī-vidyā phaṭ sarva-kṛtyā-karma-māṇikā-kuruṭa-vidyā phaṭ kṛṣṇa-vaidyā-trā-vidyā phaṭ cija-praśaka sarva-duṣṭa-jighārti-vidyā phaṭ durūpa-vidyā phaṭ sarva-tīrthika-vidyā phaṭ sarva-śramaṇa-vidyā phaṭ sarva-vidyā-dhārī-vidyā phaṭ sarva-rāja-vidyā phaṭ caya-karama-dhukara-vidyā phaṭ sarva-artha-sādhaka-vidyā phaṭ vidyā-cārī-vidyā phaṭ caburu-viṣa-bhakṣa-nī-vidyā phaṭ sarva-gāmari-vidyā phaṭ vidyā-rājaye-vidyā phaṭ mahā-pratyaṅgirā-vidyā phaṭ vajra-śaṅkala-pratyaṅgirā rācaya phaṭ mahā-kālāya-mātrī-gaṇa-namaskṛtaye phaṭ kaiśa-naveye phaṭ brahma-muṇiye phaṭ agniye phaṭ mahā-kāriye phaṭ kāla-daṇḍiye phaṭ indriye phaṭ raudriye phaṭ gāmari phaṭ varahiye phaṭ cāmuṇḍiye phaṭ rudriye phaṭ kāla-rātriye phaṭ yama-daṇḍiye phaṭ kāpāliye phaṭ adhimukti-śmaśāna-vāsiniye phaṭ*

"If there are sentient beings who raise poisonous intent against me, who raise violent intent, those who eat vitality, those who eat vital essence, those who eat embryos, those who eat blood, those who eat fat, those who eat flesh, those who eat tallow, those who eat marrow, those who eat afterbirth, those who steal life, those who eat vomit, those who eat filth, those who eat urine, those who eat leaking water, those who eat leftover food, those who eat spittle, those who eat saliva, those who eat mucus, those who eat pus, those who eat offerings, those who eat garlands, those who eat incense, those who eat fragrance, those who steal thought, those who eat flowers, those who eat fruit, those who eat grain, those who eat fire-offerings;

"And furthermore those who raise hatred, those who raise poisonous intent, those who raise violent intent: deva-possession hosts, nāga-possession hosts, asura-possession hosts, wind-spirit possession hosts, sky-traveler possession hosts, gandharva-possession hosts, human and non-human possession hosts, great-serpent possession hosts, yakṣa-possession hosts, rākṣasa-possession hosts, hungry-ghost possession hosts, ḍākinī-possession hosts, flesh-eating possession hosts, bhūta-possession hosts, kumbhāṇḍa-possession hosts, stinking-ghost possession hosts, foul-smelling ghost possession hosts, skanda-possession hosts, stupor-possession hosts, madness-possession hosts, nightmare-possession hosts, those-that-make-one-fall possession hosts, revati-possession hosts, Yama-possession hosts, bird-possession hosts, mother-ghost-joy possession hosts, wanderer-possession hosts, thorn-remover possession hosts;

"Or those with febrile illnesses, one-day fevers, two-day fevers, three-day fevers, four-day fevers, or constant fevers, extremely burning fevers, intermittent fevers, bhūta-ghost fevers, wind-bile-phlegm fevers, combined-humor fevers: may all fevers, headaches, and half-headaches be eliminated. Furthermore, those with choking and gagging, eye diseases, nose diseases, mouth diseases, neck diseases, heart diseases, throat pain, ear pain, tooth pain, heart pain, brain pain, flank pain, back pain, abdominal pain, waist pain, pain in the private parts, thigh pain, calf pain, hand pain, foot pain, joint pain: may all be eliminated. May you protect me! The Great White Parasol Vajra-Uṣṇīṣa-śikhin Pratyaṅgirā Mother, within a twelve-yojana vajra boundary, all bhūtas, vetālas, ḍākinīs, and fever-ghosts, as well as sores, scabies, ringworm, leprosy, fistulas, epilepsy, abscesses, carbuncles, itching, ulcers, desiccation, wind-tremors, jewel-poison, insect-poison, sorcery-curses, water and fire, enmity and harm from enemies, untimely death. Furthermore, the poisons of timapugabees, vipers, scorpions, and rats, as well as nāgas, tigers, lions, bears, wolves, and all manner of poisonous wasps that steal life: all such are bound by the vidyā-bond, bound by divine power. All vidyā-spells are bound; all ghosts and possessions are likewise bound.

"*Tadyathā oṃ anale anale viṣade viṣade vajra-dhari bandha bandha vajra-pāṇi phaṭ hūṃ hūṃ phaṭ phaṭ hūṃ ṭrūṃ bandha phaṭ svāhā*

"If a person carries this sūtra of the Great White Parasol Buddha-Uṣṇīṣa Pratyaṅgirā Mother Dhāraṇī, or writes it on birch bark, palm leaf, or white silk, and either wears it on the body or ties it about the neck, one should know that for the rest of that person's life, poison cannot harm them. Weapons, water, fire, jewel-poison, gu-poison, and sorcery-curses cannot harm them either. There will be no untimely death. All ghosts and possessions who see this person will have no choice but to produce great joy. Eighty-four thousand vajra-division beings will produce great joy and constantly guard and protect this person. For eighty-four thousand great kalpas, one will obtain knowledge of past lives. Life after life, one will not be born among yakṣas, rākṣasas, pūtanas, kaṭapūtanas, bhūta-ghosts, or any evil destiny. Nor will one be born into poverty and low station. One will obtain the immeasurable, boundless merit-aggregate of Ganges-sands of Buddhas.

"If a person upholds this sūtra of the Great White Parasol Buddha-Uṣṇīṣa Pratyaṅgirā Mother Dhāraṇī: one who does not practice celibacy will attain celibacy; one who does not keep the precepts will attain precept-keeping; one who is not pure will attain purity; one who does not observe poṣadha will attain poṣadha; one who does not keep a pure fast will attain a pure fast. If one has committed the five heinous crimes and all past calamities, old karmic debts, and accumulated transgressions from beginningless time, all will be extinguished without remainder.

"If a woman wishes to obtain a son, and can receive and uphold this sūtra of the Great White Parasol Buddha-Uṣṇīṣa Pratyaṅgirā Mother Dhāraṇī, she will give birth to a wise son, perfect in merit, longevity, beauty, and strength. At the end of her life, she will be reborn in the land of Sukhāvatī.

"If one encounters human illness, livestock disease, plague and pestilence, conflict and strife, or invasion by enemy armies, and all such calamities, bring this Great White Parasol Buddha-Uṣṇīṣa Peerless Pratyaṅgirā Mother Dhāraṇī and tie it atop a banner-pole. Make extensive offerings and perform the great Buddhist rites. Receive and welcome this dhāraṇī, placing it at the four gates of the city, or in all villages, towns, hamlets, and fields. With worship, reverence, and single-minded offerings, all invading armies will immediately be dispelled. Plague and illness, harmful conflicts, invasion by enemy forces, and all disasters will all be eliminated.

"*Tadyathā oṃ stūrūṃ bandha bandha: may you protect me, may you protect me, svāhā. Rakṣa rakṣa māṃ. Oṃ hūṃ stūrūṃ bandha bandha vajra: may you protect me. Rakṣa rakṣa māṃ. Vajra-pāṇaye hūṃ phaṭ svāhā. Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-uṣṇīṣa-avalokite mūrdha-te jala jala gata gata dhara dhara vidhara vidhara cinda cinda bhinda bhinda hūṃ hūṃ phaṭ phaṭ svāhā. Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-uṣṇīṣa hūṃ phaṭ phaṭ svāhā. Tadyathā oṃ anale anale kasame kasame viṣade viṣade svāme svāme sarva-buddhādhiṣṭhāna-adhiṣṭhite sarva-tathāgata-uṣṇīṣa-sitātapatrī hūṃ phaṭ svāhā. Hūṃ mama hūṃ ni svāhā.*"

"If one can practice according to the Buddha's instructions, all the deva-kings and nāga-kings will follow the proper seasons and send down sweet rain."

At that time, all the Buddhas, all the great bodhisattvas, the devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, humans, and non-humans, the entire great assembly, having heard what the Buddha taught, all greatly rejoiced, faithfully received it, and practiced accordingly.
