# 聖六字增壽大明陀羅尼經
## Holy Six-Syllable Life-Extending Great Vidyā Dhāraṇī Sūtra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1049

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dānapāla (施護).

---

Thus have I heard. At one time the Buddha was in the country of Śrāvastī, in the Jetavana grove of Anāthapiṇḍada's park. At that time the venerable Ānanda was gravely ill. The Buddha, knowing this himself, went to where Ānanda was, spread a seat, sat down, and told Ānanda: "Listen carefully. I possess the Six-Syllable Great Vidyā Dhāraṇī, which can dispel calamities and extend lifespan. If you uphold it, not only will it benefit yourself, but it will also bring peace through the long night to the fourfold assembly of bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, and upāsikās, keeping them far from all suffering.

"Furthermore, Ānanda, this Six-Syllable Great Vidyā Dhāraṇī was proclaimed in unison by seventy-seven koṭis of buddhas together with six great teachers of mighty virtue. The six great teachers are: first, the Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha; second, Śakra, Lord of the Devas; third, Vaiśravaṇa, King of Many Hearings; fourth, Dhṛtarāṣṭra, King of the Upholding State; fifth, Virūḍhaka, King of Increase and Growth; sixth, Virūpākṣa, King of the Wide-Seeing Eye. These sages and worthies, with one voice in unison, spoke this dhāraṇī:

*Nandire nandire nanduri dumari paṇḍari kuṣṭhati madu-mati svāhā*

The Buddha told Ānanda: "This Six-Syllable Great Vidyā formula has great majestic power. If any person is terrified by danger from royal law, danger from great floods, danger from great fires, danger from robbery, danger from enemies, danger from all manner of evils, danger from warfare, or danger from malign celestial bodies, when such dangers threaten their lives, they should single-mindedly recite the Great Vidyā formula: 'Protect so-and-so, and grant deliverance.' Having spoken thus, these various dangers will swiftly be dispelled.

"Furthermore, Ānanda, if any sentient being suffers from various pains, including headaches, neck pain, eye pain, ear pain, nose pain, tooth pain, tongue pain, lip pain, mouth pain, cheek pain, chest pain, rib pain, back pain, heart pain, stomach pain, waist pain, hip pain, pain throughout the body, as well as dysentery, hemorrhoids, fistula, wind disease, bile disease, phlegm disease, and all grave illnesses, one should recite the Great Vidyā formula as before: 'By the Buddha's great majestic virtue, may all the sun, moon, and stars, and the arhat-sages, speak true words of protection for the disciple so-and-so, eliminating calamities and granting peace and happiness.' All weapons, swords, poisons, tigers, wolves, lions, venomous snakes, scorpions, and evil beasts will cause no harm. Malarial fever will not afflict, nor will one die an untimely death. Even apasmāra spirits, bhūtas, piśācas, kumbhāṇḍas, and all ghost generals will stay far away and dare not cause trouble.

"Furthermore, Ānanda, if any sentient being is possessed by ghosts or spirits that persist for years and months without releasing their grip, empower a thread with this mantra and tie it on the afflicted person's hand. Then Vajrapāṇi, the Great Yakṣa Lord, will with his wrathful power shatter the ghost's head into seven parts. Furthermore, the greatly wise Śāriputra, the greatly powerful Maudgalyāyana, the precept-holding Rāhula, and you, Ānanda, shall all come to protect and bring peace.

"If this is not so, then King Sumeru shall leave its place, the great ocean shall dry up, the sun and moon shall fall, and the great earth shall crack apart. The Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha never speaks falsehood.

"Ānanda, the supernatural power and majestic virtue of this Six-Syllable Great Vidyā Dhāraṇī is unprecedented. If one merely hears it with rejoicing, that person will always enjoy long life, freedom from illness, and immunity from all evils. How much more so for one who upholds, recites, copies, and makes offerings to it. This is called the accomplishment of the supreme method of increase and benefit."

Ānanda, having heard this, accepted it with faith and put it into practice.
