# Sūtra of the High King Avalokiteśvara

## *Gāowáng Guānshìyīn Jīng* (高王觀世音經)

Taishō 2898

Translator unknown. An apocryphal devotional text.

*Note: This is one of the most widely recited apocryphal sūtras in East Asian Buddhism. According to legend, a man condemned to death was instructed in a dream to recite this text a thousand times, after which the executioner's sword broke upon his neck.*

Avalokiteśvara (*Guānshìyīn*, 觀世音) Bodhisattva. Homage to the Buddha. Homage to the Dharma. Homage to the Saṃgha.

The buddha-land has its affinities. The buddha-dharma is mutually arising. Permanence, bliss, selfhood, and purity. Through affinity with the buddha-dharma:

Homage to the great prajñāpāramitā (*móhē bōrě bōluómì*, 摩訶般若波羅蜜). This is the great divine mantra. Homage to the great prajñāpāramitā. This is the great bright mantra. Homage to the great prajñāpāramitā. This is the unsurpassed mantra. Homage to the great prajñāpāramitā. This is the unequaled mantra.

Homage to Pure Light Secret Buddha (*Jìngguāng Mìmì Fó*, 淨光祕密佛). Dharma-Treasury Buddha (*Fǎzàng Fó*, 法藏佛). Lion's Roar Spiritual Power King Buddha (*Shīzihǒu Shénzú Yóuwáng Fó*, 師子吼神足由王佛). The Buddha said to Sumeru Peak King Buddha (*Xūmí Dēngwáng Fó*, 須彌登王佛). Dharma-Guardian Buddha (*Fǎhù Fó*, 法護佛). Vajra-Treasury Lion's Sport Buddha (*Jīngāngzàng Shīzi Yóuxì Fó*, 金剛藏師子游戲佛). Jewel-Victory Buddha (*Bǎoshèng Fó*, 寶勝佛). Spiritual Powers Buddha (*Shéntōng Fó*, 神通佛). Medicine Master Lapis Lazuli Light King Buddha (*Yàoshī Liúlíguāng Wáng Fó*, 藥師琉璃光王佛). Universal Light Merit Mountain King Buddha (*Pǔguāng Gōngdé Shānwáng Fó*, 普光功德山王佛). Well-Abiding Merit Jewel King Buddha (*Shànzhù Gōngdé Bǎowáng Fó*, 善住功德寶王佛). The seven buddhas of the past. The thousand buddhas of the future Bhadrakalpa (*Xiángjié*, 賢劫). The fifteen hundred buddhas. The fifteen thousand buddhas. The five hundred Flower-Victory Buddhas. The hundred billion Vajra-Treasury Buddhas. Dīpaṃkara (*Dìngguāng*, 定光) Buddha.

The names of the six buddhas of the six directions: In the east, Jewel-Light Moon-Palace Moon-Wonderful Honored-Sound King Buddha. In the south, Tree-Root Flower King Buddha. In the west, Sovereign King Spiritual-Powers Flame-Flower King Buddha. In the north, Moon-Palace Pure Buddha. Above, Innumerable Vigor Jewel-Crown Buddha. Below, Good-Stillness Moon-Sound King Buddha.

Innumerable buddhas. Prabhūtaratna (*Duōbǎo*, 多寶) Buddha. Śākyamuni (*Shìjiāmóuní*, 釋迦牟尼) Buddha. Maitreya (*Mílè*, 彌勒) Buddha. Akṣobhya (*Āchù*, 阿閦) Buddha. Amitābha (*Mítuó*, 彌陀) Buddha. All sentient beings in the center, those in the buddha-worlds, those walking and abiding upon the earth, and those in empty space: may compassion extend to all sentient beings, may each be at peace and rest. Day and night cultivate and uphold, and let the heart constantly seek to recite this sūtra, which can extinguish the suffering of birth and death and quell all poisons and harms.

Homage to Great-Bright Avalokiteśvara. Observing-Bright Avalokiteśvara. High-Bright Avalokiteśvara. Opening-Bright Avalokiteśvara.

Medicine King (*Yàowáng*, 藥王) Bodhisattva. Medicine Superior (*Yàoshàng*, 藥上) Bodhisattva. Mañjuśrī (*Wénshūshīlì*, 文殊師利) Bodhisattva. Samantabhadra (*Pǔxián*, 普賢) Bodhisattva. Ākāśagarbha (*Xūkōngzàng*, 虛空藏) Bodhisattva. Kṣitigarbha (*Dìzàng*, 地藏) Bodhisattva. The hundred million bodhisattvas of Cool Jewel Mountain (*Qīngliáng Bǎoshān*, 清涼寶山). The Bodhisattva of Universal Light Tathāgata's Transformative Victory (*Pǔguāng Rúlái Huàshèng*, 普光如來化勝).

Reciting this verse thought after thought, the seven world-honored buddhas then spoke this dhāraṇī:

*Lí bō lí bō dì. Qiúhē qiúhē dì. Tuóluóní dì. Níhēluó dì. Pílíní dì. Móhē jiā dì. Zhēnlíng qián dì. Suōpóhē.*

> Avalokiteśvara of the ten directions
> And all the bodhisattvas
> Vow to save sentient beings.
> By calling their names, all are freed.
> If there is a person of wisdom,
> Earnestly explain this for others.
> Wherever there is the condition,
> Recite it without cease.
> Reciting the sūtra a full thousand times,
> Thought after thought, the mind unbroken:
> Fire and flame cannot harm;
> Weapons are immediately shattered.
> Anger transforms into joy;
> The dead are changed into the living.
> Do not say this is false:
> The buddhas' words are not untrue.

Sūtra of the High King Avalokiteśvara Spoken by the Buddha.

> The High King Avalokiteśvara
> Can save from all dangers and perils.
> In the midst of imminent crisis and difficulty,
> The dead are changed into the living.
> The buddhas' words are not untrue;
> Therefore one should bow in reverence.
> Upholding and reciting a full thousand times,
> Heavy sins are all extinguished.
> Those of little merit who do not believe:
> Devote yourself to receiving and upholding this sūtra.

Recite the names of the eight great bodhisattvas:

Homage to Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva. Homage to Maitreya Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva. Homage to Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva. Homage to Samantabhadra Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva. Homage to Vajrapāṇi (*Jīngāngshǒu*, 金剛手) Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva. Homage to Mañjuśrī (*Miàojíxiáng*, 妙吉祥) Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva. Homage to Sarvanīvaraṇaviṣkambhin (*Chúgàizhàng*, 除蓋障) Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva. Homage to Kṣitigarbha King Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva. Homage to all the honored bodhisattva-mahāsattvas.

> May this merit
> Extend universally to all.
> Having recited a full thousand times,
> Heavy sins are all extinguished.
