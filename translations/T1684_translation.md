# 八大靈塔梵讚
## Sanskrit Praise of the Eight Great Sacred Stūpas

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1684

Composed by King Śīlāditya of India (戒日王). Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmabhadra (法賢).

---

*[Translator's note: This text consists entirely of phonetically transcribed Sanskrit (梵讚, "Brahmic verse"). The Chinese characters serve exclusively as phonograms, bearing no semantic content in Chinese. The hymn, attributed to King Śīlāditya (Harṣavardhana, r. 606–647 CE) of India, praises the eight great stūpas marking the principal sacred sites of the Buddha's life. Clearly identifiable Sanskrit terms and place names include: Lumbinī, Kapila(vastu), Kuśinagara, Kauśāmbī, Mathurā, Bodhi, Vaiśālī, Sindhu, dharmacakra, bhuvana, śrīmat, sthāna, varṇa, vimocana, Meru, sūrya, maṇi, stūpa, ratna, buddha, sarva, dhātu-garbha, prakāśa, mahā-caitya, vandana, and samāpta. Portions of uncertain reconstruction are enclosed in brackets.]*
