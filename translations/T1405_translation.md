# 佛說息除賊難陀羅尼經
## Sūtra of the Dhāraṇī Spoken by the Buddha for Quelling the Danger of Bandits

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1405

Translated from the Chinese. Song Dynasty, translated by Dharmabhadra (法賢)

---

Thus have I heard.

At one time, the Buddha was in the kingdom of Magadha, walking in meditation surrounded by a great assembly, and arrived at Indrashāla Rock on Mount Vediya, beside the Āmravana.

At that time, the Venerable Ānanda suddenly saw a great host of vicious bandits approaching from afar. Upon seeing them, he was seized by great terror; his heart was filled with distress and the hairs on his body all stood on end. The Venerable Ānanda quickly went to where the Buddha was, and upon arriving, joined his palms and addressed the Buddha, saying: "World-Honored One! I have just seen from afar a great host of vicious bandits. I implore the World-Honored One to act as my protector."

At that time, the World-Honored One, having heard the words of the Venerable Ānanda, addressed Ānanda, saying: "Are you afraid of bandits?" Ānanda said to the Buddha: "I am greatly afraid, World-Honored One!" The Buddha said: "Ānanda! You need not be afraid. I have a dhāraṇī that can eliminate the danger of bandits." At that time, the Venerable Ānanda, having heard the Buddha's words, his heart arose with joy, and he spoke these words: "I implore the World-Honored One to proclaim it for me."

At that time, the World-Honored One thereupon proclaimed the Great-Wheel Boundary-Sealing Dhāraṇī:

*tadyathā āvaloke prāvaloke | ihe adhipati uri | gandhāri caṇḍāri māṭaṅgi | jāguri phukkase*

At that time, having proclaimed this dhāraṇī, the World-Honored One addressed the Venerable Ānanda: "You may now depart. With this dhāraṇī, establish a protective boundary extending twelve yojanas in every direction, so that peace and security will be attained, the danger of bandits will be quelled and eliminated, and swords, blades, weapons, staffs, and the like will all be unable to intrude. Furthermore, all those vicious bandits will be unable to leave their present location, spinning in place like a potter's wheel.

"Furthermore, Ānanda! If a person encounters the danger of bandits, one should take a length of clean white thread, intone this dhāraṇī over it seven times to empower it, and tie seven knots. When the danger has passed, undo the knots. All the host of bandits will be as though bound and shackled, unable to cause harm."

At that time, having proclaimed this sūtra, the Venerable Ānanda and the great assembly, having heard what the Buddha had proclaimed, all greatly rejoiced and, full of faith, received and practiced it.

---

**Note on the dhāraṇī:** The Sanskrit reconstruction (*tadyathā āvaloke prāvaloke | ihe adhipati uri | gandhāri caṇḍāri māṭaṅgi | jāguri phukkase*) is approximate, based on the Chinese phonetic transcription. Caṇḍārī, Māṭaṅgī, and Jāgulī are names of protective yakṣiṇī figures commonly invoked in tantric dhāraṇī literature.
