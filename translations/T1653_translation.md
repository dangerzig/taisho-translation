# T1653 大乘緣生論

# Mahāyāna Treatise on Dependent Origination

*Note: This treatise (lùn 論) was composed by Uḍḍalaka (Yùlèngqié 欝楞迦) and translated by Amoghavajra (Bùkōng 不空) during the Tang dynasty. It presents a systematic Mahāyāna analysis of the twelve links of dependent origination (shí'èr yīnyuán 十二因緣, pratītyasamutpāda) in a root-verse and auto-commentary format. The root text consists of thirty verses (Yuánshēng sānshí lùn 緣生三十論) that map the twelve links through various analytical frameworks: the three categories (affliction, karma, suffering), three time periods (past, present, future), cause and effect relationships, the fourfold emptiness of cause and fruit, two roots (ignorance and craving), two joints (karma-fruit junctures), four summaries, five positional functions, eight kinds of fruit, and the ten internal and external characteristics (non-permanence, non-annihilation, non-transference, etc.). The treatise concludes with the middle way between eternalism and annihilationism, referencing the City Simile Sūtra (Chéng yù jīng 城喻經), the Kātyāyana Sūtra (Jiāzhānyán jīng 迦栴延經), and the Sūtra Breaking Rāhula's Questions (Pò Luóyùná jīng 破邏遇拏經). It is followed by three concluding verses and a brief identification of the twelve links with the Four Noble Truths.*

---

## Auto-Commentary

I shall now explain the Thirty Verses on Dependent Arising in sequential order.

### The Numerical Unfolding of the Twelve Links

"From one arise three; from three, in turn, arise six":

"From one arise three" refers to ignorance (wúmíng 無明). This ignorance is called "not-knowing." Because one does not know suffering, its accumulation, its cessation, and the path, it is called "not-knowing." From this arise merit, demerit, and the imperturbable: these are called the "three formations" (sānxíng 三行), together with bodily formation, verbal formation, and mental formation, which turn and arise from it.

"From three, in turn, arise six": from the three formations arise the six consciousness-groups (liùshí shēn 六識身), namely eye-consciousness, ear-consciousness, nose-consciousness, tongue-consciousness, body-consciousness, and mental consciousness.

"Six and two": these six consciousness-groups turn and produce two kinds: name (míng 名) and form (sè 色).

"Two more and six": from name-and-form, two kinds, turn and produce the six sense-bases (liùchù 六處): eye-base, ear-base, nose-base, tongue-base, body-base, and mental base.

"From six likewise arise six": from those six sense-bases turn and produce six contacts (liùchù 六觸): eye-contact, ear-contact, nose-contact, tongue-contact, body-contact, and mental contact.

"From six there are three": from those six contacts turn and produce three feelings (sānshòu 三受): pleasant feeling, painful feeling, and neither-pleasant-nor-painful feeling.

"These three again have three": from those three feelings in turn arise three kinds of craving (sānài 三愛): craving for the desire realm, craving for existence, and craving for non-existence.

"Three again produce four": from those three kinds of craving turn and produce four graspings (sìqǔ 四取): grasping at desires, grasping at views, grasping at precepts and practices, and grasping at the doctrine of self.

"Four again produce three": from those four graspings turn and produce three existences (sānyǒu 三有): desire-realm existence, form-realm existence, and formless-realm existence.

"From three arises one": from those three existences as conditions, one kind of future birth arises.

"That one again produces seven": from one future birth arise old age, death, grief, lamentation, suffering, sorrow, and vexation, seven kinds.

"Whatever suffering is therein, the Sage declares is all encompassed": therein, beginning with ignorance and ending with suffering, the immeasurable kinds of suffering are all encompassed in the World-Honored One's abbreviated statement.

### The Twelve Links Are Empty

"Twelve kinds of differentiation, the wise declare to be empty": these twelve links, from ignorance onward, differentiated into twelve, are all empty of self-nature. One should know that what is described here is only emptiness. Dharmas are born from emptiness; emptiness is born from dharmas. Dharmas arise through the power of the dependent-arising links. "One should know the twelve dharmas": through the successive arising by the power of the links, these twelve dharmas should thus be known.

Among them: the characteristic of delusion is ignorance. In the link of formations, the characteristic of accumulating future existence is the formations. In the link of consciousness, the characteristic of the arising link's turning out is consciousness. In the link of name-and-form, the characteristic of the combination of the name-body and form-body is name-and-form. In the link of the six sense-bases, the characteristic of establishing the faculties is the six bases. In the link of contact, the characteristic of the gathering of eye, form, and consciousness is contact. In the link of feeling, the characteristic of experiencing and using the inversion of the agreeable and disagreeable is feeling. In the link of craving, the characteristic of insatiability is craving. In the link of grasping, the characteristic of seizing and appropriating is grasping. In the link of existence, the characteristic of the name-body and form-body is existence. In the link of birth, the characteristic of the arising of the aggregates is birth. In the link of aging, the characteristic of maturation is aging. In the link of death, the severance of the life-faculty is death. Grief's characteristic is urgency and haste. Lamentation's characteristic is the sound of weeping. Suffering's characteristic is physical affliction. Sorrow's characteristic is mental affliction. Vexation's characteristic is the aspect of harm and damage.

### The Three Categories

"Ignorance, karmic action, consciousness ...": the synonyms for the twelve links are: "not-knowing" is ignorance; "karma" is the formations; "consciousness" is discrimination; "name-and-form" is the five-aggregate assemblage; "bases" is the sense-bases; "contact" is the three-way meeting; "feeling" is reception; "craving" is thirst; "grasping" is holding; "enjoyment" is existence; "arising" is birth; "maturation" is aging; "the final boundary" is death.

These are further categorized into affliction, karma, and suffering:

"The first, eighth, and ninth are affliction; the second and tenth are karma; the remaining seven are all suffering: three encompass the twelve dharmas."

The three afflictions are: ignorance, craving, and grasping. The two karmas are: formations and existence. The seven kinds of retribution are: consciousness, name-and-form, the six sense-bases, contact, feeling, birth, and aging-and-death. These twelve dharmas are encompassed by three categories.

### The Three Time Periods

"The first two are the past; the last two, the future; the remaining eight are the present: these are called the three-time dharmas."

Ignorance and formations, the first two, are past. Birth and aging-and-death, the last two, are future. Consciousness, name-and-form, the six sense-bases, contact, feeling, craving, grasping, and existence, eight kinds, are present.

### The Causal Chain and Cessation

"Affliction produces karma, karma evokes retribution; retribution in turn produces affliction; affliction again produces karma; and through karma there is retribution."

These three, as previously described: from affliction there is karma; from karma there is retribution; again from retribution there is affliction; from affliction there is karma; from karma there is retribution.

Question: Through the exhaustion of affliction, each ceases. What does this mean?

Answer: "Apart from affliction, how could there be karma? Apart from karma, how could there be retribution? Without retribution, there is no affliction: these three are each quiescent."

If this mind is without the stain of affliction, then one does not accumulate karma. If one does not create karma, then one does not receive retribution. If retribution is extinguished, affliction also does not arise. Thus these three are each quiescent.

### Cause and Fruit

"Five links as cause produce the fruit, called affliction and karma; seven links serve as fruit: seven kinds of suffering, one should know."

The five causes are affliction and karma, as previously stated: ignorance, formations, craving, grasping, and existence. The seven kinds of fruit, also as previously stated, are seven kinds of suffering: consciousness, name-and-form, the six sense-bases, contact, feeling, birth, and aging-and-death.

### The Fourfold Emptiness

"In the cause, emptily there is no fruit; in the fruit, there is also no cause; in the cause there is also no cause; in the fruit there is also no fruit: the wise are in accord with emptiness."

If these previously described causes and fruits, in which the cause is empty and the fruit also empty, the fruit empty and the cause also empty, the cause empty and the cause also empty, the fruit empty and the fruit also empty: in these four propositions one should be in accord.

### Divisions, Joints, and Summaries

"In the world, four kinds of divisions": the previously described five causes of the three times and seven fruits are summarized into four divisions, in sequential order. Division one: ignorance and formations (two past dharmas). Division two: consciousness, name-and-form, the six bases, contact, and feeling (five present dharmas). Division three: craving, grasping, and existence (three present dharmas). Division four: birth and aging-death (two future dharmas).

"Affliction, karma, and fruit combined: one should acknowledge six divisions": affliction, karma, and retribution, three categories, are tied into two roots, making six divisions. The ignorance root contains: ignorance (affliction), formations (karma), consciousness through feeling (retribution). The craving root contains: craving and grasping (affliction), existence (karma), birth and aging-death (retribution).

"Two joints and three summaries": existence is the foundation. Two joints arise: between existence and birth is the first joint; between formations and consciousness is the second joint. Both are karma-fruit joints. Between feeling and craving, cause and fruit are mixed: this is the third joint. These three joints become four summaries. Ignorance and formations (two) are the first summary. Consciousness through feeling (five) are the second. Craving, grasping, and existence (three) are the third. Birth and aging-death (two) are the fourth.

### Positional Functions

"Two, three, two, three, two: in the suffering-position there are five dharmas: agent, treasury, realm, activation, arising, and continuation."

Ignorance and formations (two) are the agent in the suffering-position. Consciousness, name-and-form, and the six bases (three) are the treasury. Contact and feeling (two) are the realm. Craving, grasping, and existence (three) are the activation. Birth and aging-death (two) are the continuation.

### Eight Kinds of Fruit

In the ignorance root, the first division has four kinds of fruit: delusion fruit, arising fruit, retribution fruit, and equal-flow fruit. The numbers are one, one, three, two. Ignorance is the delusion fruit. Formations is the arising fruit. Consciousness, name-and-form, and the six bases are the retribution fruit. Contact and feeling are the equal-flow fruit.

In the craving root, the second division has four kinds of fruit: affliction fruit, deficiency fruit, turning-out fruit, and equal-flow fruit. The numbers are two, one, one, one. Craving and grasping are the affliction fruit. Existence is the deficiency fruit. Birth is the turning-out fruit. Aging-and-death is the equal-flow fruit. Thus there are eight kinds of fruit in all.

### No Sentient Being, No Life, Empty

"These have twelve kinds; because of their combination, dependent arising. No sentient being, no life: empty, without; by wisdom one knows."

Thus, from ignorance at the beginning to aging-and-death at the end, there are twelve links. Because of the power of their combination, each arises in dependence on conditions. Yet there is no sentient being, no life-span, and they are empty and without substance: this should be known through wisdom. "No sentient being" is because they are not solid. "No life-span" is because there is no self. "Empty" means without an agent, because there is no agent.

"No self, no mine; no self, no self within: four kinds of ignorance are empty. The remaining links are likewise."

Ignorance is no-self. Within this, ignorance is no-mine, because there is no self. Since there is no self, within the self there is no ignorance. These four kinds, in which ignorance is empty of self, mine, self-within, and ignorance-within, are empty. As with ignorance, the remaining links of formations and the rest are all likewise empty. One should know this.

### The Middle Way

"Eternalism and annihilationism, the two extremes, abandoned: this itself is the middle way. If awakened, one has accomplished: the awakened body is all the buddhas."

"Existence" is the eternalist grasping; "nonexistence" is the annihilationist grasping. These two extremes arise from this: because of conditions, that arises. Among those existences, if one abandons both extremes, one accords with the middle way. If one does not know this meaning, then like the non-Buddhist schools, one falls into the two extremes. If one is awakened, one is all the buddhas. As the Buddha in the world is able to accomplish, not others.

"Awakened, among the assembly, the holy sage declares no-self. Once, in the City Simile Sūtra, the Guide taught this meaning."

This too is this middle way. Awakened, among the assemblies, the Buddha declares no-self and no-mine. "You monks should know: saying 'I' and 'mine,' the foolish ordinary person of little learning, in the conventional designation, says 'I' and 'mine.' Monks! When birth occurs, only suffering arises; when cessation occurs, only suffering ceases." This is as the Guide taught in the City Simile Sūtra.

"The Kātyāyana Sūtra teaches right view and the view of emptiness. The Sūtra Breaking Rāhula's Questions also teaches supreme emptiness."

These three sūtras, and in other places, such characteristics have been extensively taught by the World-Honored One.

"If dependent arising is rightly known, that knowledge accords with emptiness. If dependent arising is not known, one also does not know that emptiness."

If there is right knowledge of the previously described dependent arising, that knowledge is no different. What is that knowledge? Knowing emptiness. If dependent arising is not known and emptiness is also not known: if one does not know this dependent arising, one also cannot enter that emptiness; this should be known.

"If regarding emptiness one generates pride, one does not become weary of the aggregates. If one has that nihilistic view, one is confused about the meaning of dependent arising."

If regarding emptiness one generates pride, one does not become weary of the five aggregates. If one has that nihilistic view and is confused about this meaning of dependent arising, then among the four kinds of views one grasps whichever: first, annihilationism; second, eternalism; third, the doctrine of a sovereign creator; fourth, the doctrine that all is done by past karma.

"Because dependent arising is not confused, one abandons pride and knows emptiness, and through weariness of the aggregates, one is not confused about karmic fruit."

If, among the previously described dependently arising links, one has no confused mind, and regarding the grasping of self and mine one abandons pride, then one enters emptiness in accord with the Dharma. Through weariness of the aggregates, one is not confused about karmic fruit: because in the five aggregates one grasps self and mine, one circulates endlessly throughout the world. Through weariness of those aggregates, there is no inversion or confusion regarding the continuity of karmic fruit.

### Karma, Emptiness, and Continuity

"Karma creates conditions for continued birth, and not without depending on these conditions. With empty conditions there will be this: karmic retribution and the means of enjoyment."

As previously described, affliction, karma, and retribution: by such wholesome and unwholesome karma, sentient beings are driven laterally, upward, and downward in continued birth. Without this karma, conditions would not be created. Otherwise, there would be the fault of receiving retribution for karma not done, and the loss of karma already done. If through these wholesome and unwholesome karmas there is retributional enjoyment, the self-nature is empty and originally without self. That conditions arise from empty nature should also be known. This meaning is now further summarized.

### The Wheel of Existence

"From three arise two; from two arise seven; from seven again arise three: the wheel of existence thus turns."

Ignorance, craving, and grasping (three) produce formations and existence (two). Those two produce consciousness, name-and-form, the six bases, contact, feeling, birth, and aging-death (seven). From those seven, as previously described, three again arise, and from those three again two, and again seven. Therefore, in unbroken succession, this wheel of existence thus turns.

"All are cause and fruit. Relying on conditions, affliction arises; relying on conditions, karma also arises; relying on conditions, retribution also arises: not one is without conditions."

Ignorance, formations, craving, grasping, and existence (five) are called causes. Consciousness, name-and-form, the six bases, contact, feeling, birth, and aging-death (seven) are called fruits. All of these throughout the world: whether "I," "sentient being," "life-span," "born one," "man," "person," or "agent," such discriminations are only deceptive and should be known as such. How then are they born? "Only empty dharmas give rise to empty dharmas": within the self-nature emptiness, the conventionally designated affliction, karma, and fruit, only the empty, conventionally designated dharmas of affliction, karma, and fruit arise. This is the meaning.

### The Ten Analogies

"Recitation, lamp, seal, mirror, sound, sunlight, seed, and vinegar: the aggregates continue without transfer. The wise should observe those two."

Like recitation: there is one who teaches the recitation and one who receives it. What is taught does not transfer to the receiver. Why? Because the teacher remains as before, the teaching also does not constitute continuity. Why? Because the self does not produce from itself.

Like the successive production of lamp-flames: it is not the first flame that transfers, yet the second flame does not arise without a cause.

Likewise with a seal and its impression, a face and a mirror, a sound and its echo, the sun and fire, a seed and a sprout, vinegar and salivation: in each pair, there is no transfer, yet there is arising, and it is not without a cause.

The two aggregates in their successive continuity turn without the first aggregate transferring, yet the second aggregate does not fail to arise, and does not arise without a cause. The wise should rightly observe this successive continuity of the aggregates without transfer.

### Ten Internal and External Characteristics

There are also ten internal and ten external characteristics that accord with each other, which should all be known.

The ten external characteristics are: (1) not permanent, because ceasing; (2) not annihilated, because arising; (3) not transferring, because after complete cessation the new arises as something previously non-existent; (4) no gap between cause and fruit, because the succession is unbroken; (5) not that very entity, because there is differentiation; (6) not a different entity, because arising from the former; (7) no agent, because of the combination of causes and conditions; (8) not without a cause, because the seed serves as cause; (9) momentary destruction, because seed, sprout, stalk, branch, leaf, flower, and fruit successively produce one another; (10) same-class fruit linked, because sweet, sour, salty, bitter, pungent, and astringent follow the differentiation of the cause, with the fruit turning out accordingly.

The ten internal characteristics are: (1) not permanent, because the aggregates at the boundary of death cease without remainder; (2) not annihilated, because the aggregates of the next birth are obtained; (3) not transferring, because after the aggregates at the boundary of death cease without remainder, the aggregates of the next birth, previously non-existent, newly arise; (4) no gap, because the succession of aggregates is unbroken, with cause and fruit continuing; (5) not that very entity, because the aggregates at death and the aggregates of the next birth are different; (6) not a different entity, because the latter arises from the former; (7) no agent, because of the combination of causes and conditions; (8) not without a cause, because affliction and karma serve as causes; (9) momentary destruction, because the kalala, arbuda, peśī, ghana, praśākhā, and the stages from birth through infancy, childhood, youth, and old age successively produce one another; (10) same-class fruit linked, because wholesome and unwholesome imprints follow the differentiation of cause, with the fruit turning out accordingly.

### Three Concluding Verses

> The consciousness-body is likewise.
> The prior limit and the later limit
> Are also without any accumulation.
> Not arising yet there is arising;
> Destruction without combination.

### The Four Noble Truths

Among these, ignorance, formations, craving, grasping, and existence constitute the truth of accumulation (jídì 集諦). Consciousness, name-and-form, the six bases, contact, feeling, birth, and aging-death constitute the truth of suffering (kǔdì 苦諦). The twelve links: the truth of the path (dàodì 道諦) is the skillful means for causing their cessation and realization, namely the foundations of mindfulness, right exertions, bases of psychic power, faculties, powers, factors of awakening, and the noble Eightfold Path. These are called the truth of the path.
