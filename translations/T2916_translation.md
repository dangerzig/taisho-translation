A note before the translation: T2916 appears in Taishō vol. 85 among questionable and apocryphal texts. It is a Chinese-composed folk religious document of the "chain letter" type (*灵签经*), with no Indian source text. The language is at points garbled from repeated copying, as is typical of this genre.

---

# 勸善經
## Sūtra on Encouraging Goodness

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 2916

Chinese composition; no Indian source text. Dynasty unknown. Translator unknown (失譯)

---

By imperial edict to the Left Prime Minister Jia: urgently promulgate this to the virtuous sentient beings of all provinces. Each day, recite the name of Amitābha Buddha one thousand times. With your mouth, cease evil and practice good.

This year there is great heat, and no one can bring in the harvest. Countless multitudes have died of illness. The first [disease] is malaria. The second is epidemic disease. The third is death from dysentery with blood and white discharge. The fourth is death from red-eye disease. The fifth is death in childbirth. The sixth is death from watery dysentery. The seventh is death from wind illness.

Now I encourage sentient beings to copy this scripture and thereby exempt one household from calamity. Copy it for the benefit of one's six relatives. Those who see this scripture but do not copy it: their household will be destroyed. Those who post it alongside their gate may pass through this calamity. Those without merit cannot obtain the chance to see this scripture.

This scripture comes from the south. On the eighth day of the first month, there was thunder, lightning, and thunderbolts. In the sky appeared a child of four years of age. Also seen was an old man standing on the road. [The old man] saw a serpent ten thousand feet in length, with a human head and a bird's body. [The serpent] then called out to the old man, saying: "Mount Tai is about to collapse. Tens of millions of women are required [as souls]. Whoever obtains this sheet [of scripture] — for the tens of millions who are afflicted with difficult-to-cure illness, [salvation is possible]."

Those who copy this scripture will be exempted from this calamity. For those who do not believe, just wait and observe: on the first day of the fourth month, three households will be forced to share one ox; five men will share one wife; monks and nuns will wander door-to-door in distress.

Copy and circulate this scripture, the true words of the sage. If you perform exorcism rituals alone, you cannot escape this calamity. Encourage one another to recite Amitābha Buddha; before long, a time of great peace will be seen.
