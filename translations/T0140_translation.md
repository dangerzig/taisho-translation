# 阿那邠邸化七子經
## Sūtra on Anāthapiṇḍada Converting His Seven Sons

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0140

Translated from the Chinese. Later Han dynasty, translated by An Shigao (安世高)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Bhagavān was in Śrāvastī, in the Jetavana, the garden of Anāthapiṇḍada. At that time, Anāthapiṇḍada had seven sons, none of whom had sincere faith in the Buddha, the Dharma, or the Saṃgha. They did not take refuge in the Buddha, the Dharma, or the Saṃgha. They also did not refrain from killing, did not refrain from stealing, did not refrain from sexual misconduct, did not refrain from false speech, and did not refrain from drinking alcohol.

At that time, the householder Anāthapiṇḍada told his seven sons: "You should now take refuge in the Buddha, the Dharma, and the Saṃgha. Do not kill, do not steal, do not engage in sexual misconduct, do not speak falsely, and do not drink alcohol. Refrain from all of these."

The sons said: "We are not able to take refuge in the Buddha, the Dharma, or the Saṃgha. Refraining from killing, stealing, sexual misconduct, false speech, and drinking alcohol: none of this are we able to do."

The householder Anāthapiṇḍada said: "I shall give each of you one thousand gold coins. Take refuge in the Buddha, the Dharma, and the Saṃgha, and refrain from killing, stealing, sexual misconduct, false speech, and drinking alcohol. Refrain from all of them."

At that time, the seven sons, having received one thousand gold coins each, took refuge in the Buddha, the Dharma, and the Saṃgha, and refrained from killing, stealing, sexual misconduct, false speech, and drinking alcohol.

The householder Anāthapiṇḍada, having given each of his seven sons one thousand gold coins and instructed them in the three refuges and the five precepts, went to the garden and came to where the World-Honored One was. He bowed his head at the Buddha's feet and sat to one side. Anāthapiṇḍada then said to the World-Honored One: "I have seven sons here who had no sincere faith and no joyful mind toward the Buddha, the Dharma, or the Saṃgha. They did not take refuge in the Buddha, the Dharma, or the Saṃgha. They did not refrain from killing, stealing, sexual misconduct, false speech, or drinking alcohol. World-Honored One! I gave each of the seven sons one thousand gold coins to have them take refuge in the Buddha, the Dharma, and the Saṃgha, and to receive the five precepts. World-Honored One! Will these seven sons gain any merit, good, or virtuous accomplishment that will bear fruit for them hereafter?"

The World-Honored One said: "Excellent! Excellent! Householder! You bring great benefit to many beings, wishing to bring peace and safety to living beings so that devas and humans may attain ease. Householder! By this merit, your seven sons will gain all good merits in full. Listen carefully to the fruit these seven sons will receive from their meritorious actions. I shall now explain.

"To the north there is a country with a city called Stone Chamber. The land is fertile and the people are thriving. There is a treasury called Irāvata, containing countless hundreds of thousands of gold, silver, precious gems, mother-of-pearl, agate, pearls, amber, crystal, and lapis lazuli, as well as various wonderful treasures. Even if the people of the Gandhāra country were to carry and bear away treasures as they wished for seven years, seven months, and seven days, whether in wrapping cloths, in their arms, or on their heads, the Irāvata treasury would not be diminished.

"Furthermore, householder! If those seven thousand gold coins given to your seven sons were compared to this great Irāvata treasury: a hundredfold, a thousandfold, a hundred-thousandfold, or immeasurably: none of it could equal the merit obtained by your seven sons.

"Householder! There is also a country called Kaliṅga, with a city called Mithilā. The grain is abundant and the people are thriving. There is a treasury called Pāṇḍu, containing countless gems, gold, silver, mother-of-pearl, agate, pearls, crystal, lapis lazuli, coral, and amber. Even if the people of the Kaliṅga country were to carry away treasures as they wished for seven years, seven months, and seven days, whatever amount they bore, the treasury would not be diminished. Yet the seven thousand gold coins of your seven sons: a hundredfold, a thousandfold, a hundred-thousandfold, immeasurably: none of it could equal the merit obtained by the seven sons' seven thousand gold coins. Their merit is without equal.

"Furthermore, householder! In the country of Videha, there is a city called Suraṭṭha, with a great treasury called Piṅgala, containing countless hundreds of thousands of gems, gold, silver, mother-of-pearl, agate, pearls, amber, crystal, and lapis lazuli. Even if the people of the Videha country were to carry away as they wished for seven years, seven months, and seven days, bearing away whatever amount, the Piṅgala treasury would not be diminished. Yet the seven sons' seven thousand gold coins: a hundredfold, a thousandfold, a hundred-thousandfold, immeasurably: none of it could equal the merit obtained by the seven sons. Their merit is beyond reckoning.

"Furthermore, householder! In the country of Kāśi, in the city of Vārāṇasī, there is a treasury called Nāgaka, containing countless gold, silver, gems, mother-of-pearl, agate, crystal, lapis lazuli, pearls, and amber. The seven sons' seven thousand gold coins, compared to this great Nāgaka treasury: the merit obtained from the seven sons' seven thousand gold coins exceeds it a hundredfold, a thousandfold, a hundred-thousandfold, or immeasurably.

"Householder! Set aside the Gandhāra people, the Kaliṅga people, the Videha people, and the Kāśi people. Even if all the men, women, old, and young of this entire continent of Jambudvīpa were to carry away from these four great treasuries whatever gold, silver, gems, mother-of-pearl, agate, pearls, amber, crystal, and lapis lazuli they wished for seven years, seven months, and seven days, the four great treasuries would not be diminished. Yet, householder, the seven sons' seven thousand gold coins: a hundredfold, a thousandfold, a hundred-thousandfold, or immeasurably: none of it could equal the merit of your seven sons."

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke this verse:

> "Irāvata of Gandhāra, Mithilā and Pāṇḍu,
> Piṅgala and Suraṭṭha, Nāgaka of Vārāṇasī:
> These four great treasuries, filled with all manner of precious gems,
> Immeasurably, cannot match the fruit of the merit they have created."

At that time, the World-Honored One taught Anāthapiṇḍada the wonderful and subtle Dharma, encouraging him and making him glad. The householder Anāthapiṇḍada, having heard the wonderful Dharma from the Tathāgata, rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, placed his right knee on the ground, and with palms joined said to the World-Honored One: "I beseech the World-Honored One to accept my invitation, together with the Saṃgha of bhikṣus. I wish to prepare a meal of sweet-dew food and drink, for the sake of those seven sons."

The World-Honored One silently accepted Anāthapiṇḍada's invitation. When Anāthapiṇḍada saw the World-Honored One silently accept the invitation, he bowed his head at the Buddha's feet and departed. He returned home, and on that very day prepared the sweet and delicious food and drink. Having prepared the food and arranged the seats for the Buddha and the Saṃgha of bhikṣus, he announced that the time had come: "The time is right. We beseech the World-Honored One to grace us with his presence."

At that time, the World-Honored One, knowing the time had arrived, put on his robes and took up his bowl. With the Saṃgha of bhikṣus escorting him before and behind, he entered the city of Śrāvastī and went to Anāthapiṇḍada's house. He took his seat, together with the Saṃgha of bhikṣus. The householder Anāthapiṇḍada and his seven sons went to where the World-Honored One was, bowed their heads at the Buddha's feet, and sat to one side.

The householder Anāthapiṇḍada then said to the World-Honored One: "I have these seven sons. I gave each one thousand gold coins to have them take refuge in the Buddha, the Dharma, and the Saṃgha, and to receive the five precepts. Now I beseech the World-Honored One to teach them the Dharma, so that my seven sons may, before the Tathāgata, attain right view."

The World-Honored One told the householder Anāthapiṇḍada: "Just so, householder! Just so, householder!"

The householder Anāthapiṇḍada, seeing the Buddha and the Saṃgha of bhikṣus settled in their seats, together with his seven sons served the sweet and delicious food and drink as offerings. The householder Anāthapiṇḍada and his seven sons, having served the food to the Buddha and the Saṃgha of bhikṣus, and seeing the World-Honored One finish eating and put away his bowl, Anāthapiṇḍada then went to where the World-Honored One was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, and sat to one side.

The Tathāgata then taught the seven sons the wonderful and subtle Dharma. The World-Honored One, knowing that Anāthapiṇḍada's seven sons were listening to the Dharma with wholehearted attention, taught them the Dharma that all Buddha-World-Honored-Ones always teach: suffering, its arising, its cessation, and the path. When the World-Honored One had taught this Dharma to Anāthapiṇḍada's seven sons, each one, right there upon his seat, had all the dust and defilement exhausted, without any blemish or impurity, and the Dharma-eye arose. Having seen the Dharma and attained the profound Dharma, without any doubt or hesitation, having attained fearlessness, having understood the Tathāgata's profound Dharma, they took refuge in the Buddha, the Dharma, and the Saṃgha, and received the five precepts.

The World-Honored One, having again taught the Dharma to the householder Anāthapiṇḍada and his seven sons, rose from his seat and departed.

At that time, the householder Anāthapiṇḍada and his seven sons, having heard what the Buddha taught, joyfully practiced in accordance.
