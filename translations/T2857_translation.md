# Requesting a Dharma-Name: Meanings and Recitation Texts

## *Suǒ Fǎhào Yì* (索法號義)

Taishō 2857

Author unknown. A Dunhuang liturgical text.

*Note: This is a Dunhuang collection of formulaic benediction and eulogy texts used when conferring Buddhist dharma-names (fǎhào) on laypeople, probably composed for professional liturgists. It includes separate blessing formulas for men and women, the ill, children, the deceased, officials, and others. The text reflects the highly literate, parallel-prose style of Dunhuang ritual compositions, dated to the seventh year of Yuánhé (812 CE).*

### For a Gentleman Recovering from Illness

We humbly note that you have been briefly parted from restful meals and bedridden for some time. Morning winds and daytime breezes have stirred the curtains; clouds of sorrow at dusk have gathered over the courtyard. Beneath the mandarin-duck curtains, the color of worry arose secretly with a faint hum. Under the kingfisher eaves, clear smoke rose in disorder. Now that your illness is healed:

Gentleman Yìgōng Qiān (*Yìgōng Qiān*, 意公謙) is a man of fine talent from Luò (*Luò*, 洛). He has always cherished the three principles of righteousness in his heart and has ever possessed the beauty of unity strong as metal. But then, due to a disturbance of the jade constitution and an affliction of the precious body, his frame wasted as thin as a flower of the sixth-month snow, his countenance withered as an autumn leaf of the ninth month. Therefore he looked upward and relied upon the Imperial Awakening, and with sincere azure-eyed devotion addressed the Golden Father. Through benevolence he received the distant illumination of the white brush. Joyfully, the six sense-faculties became pure and tranquil, like the moon shining upon an autumn river. The three obstacles dispersed like clouds, like a red lotus opening in a jade pond. Such is the truth of sincere faith, not dimmed; the divine mirror shines clearly. The cloud of compassion spreads and the fever of affliction is cooled; the shadow of wisdom draws near and the deep illness subsides. Having received the protection and mindful care of all buddhas, how could there not be a heart of celebration? We express this celebration on this morning.

We pray that emerald mountains grant long life and crimson trees increase the spring. May you surely obtain the jade-carving and expand the celebration of the star-river.

### For a Woman

We pray that a lotus dot the sea of the nature and a bright moon illumine the terrace of the mind. May you dwell forever in the years of the green willow and be eternally sealed within the curtain of the lotus.

We pray that black silk remain unchanged and rosy powder increase the spring. May your virtue equal the pine beneath the cliff and your longevity match the cassia in the moon.

We pray that green eyebrows be shaped like the moon, forever displaying the freshness of the character *bā* ("eight"). May your jade countenance be like spring, uniquely possessing spring's color.

### For a Young Gentleman

Bright Gentleman (*Lǎng Jūnzǐ*, 朗君子): we pray that his writing may wield the five-colored brush, looking down upon the great current from on high. May his learning encompass the nine classics, and may he look down upon Bào and Xiè (the great poets). May he pluck the immortal cassia of the eastern hall, whose fragrance issues from within. May his fame spread throughout the realm, his emoluments and glories reflecting one another. May the jade trees and lotus fragrances share the same courtyard rites, forever illuminating those before his knees.

### For a Woman's Adornment

May the pines and vines increase their green and the phoenix-colored tapestries be forever rolled in the courtyard. May the jade lotuses be fragrant and the immortal cassia forever flourishing.

### For a Matron

We pray that the vast sea flow forever, nourishing prosperity and good fortune to reach farther. May the mountain of longevity rise loftily, equaling all peaks and turning ever higher.

### For a Military Gentleman's Adornment

May the golden tablet of merit be forever honored, the purple cord of office always glorious. May wealth fill the treasures of the four seas and blessings match the emoluments of the five marquises.

In affairs, purity and auspiciousness. In glory and salary, daily renewal. In all that is planned, lasting success. In movement and stillness, eternal well-being and primal auspiciousness.

### For a Deceased Father

Knowing the departed father: when the north wind blew, one should have been fanning the pillow and rejoicing in the warmth of the bed-covers. But unexpectedly the boat was suddenly capsized by crashing waves, and the courtyard candle was abruptly extinguished by the wind. The orphaned son imagines the shadowy realm, his thoughts beneath his father's knees compounding his grief. Before he could present the offering of tangerines in sincerity, he can only grasp at the meaning of the transmitted robe. Thinking of the filial way of establishing oneself, there is no way to pursue it from the earth. Grieving that the wind-blown tree does not stop; aching that the cold spring is forever sealed.

We pray that within the lapis lazuli hall, you tread upon the fragrant steps in circumambulation. In the forest beneath the jeweled trees, may you pluck immortal flowers and make offerings. May you receive your prediction beside the jade pool and be born from transformation upon the red lotus. May dharma-water moisten the mind and fragrant wind stir the consciousness.

We pray that beside the sweet-dew terrace, you may be born hearing the sound of prajñā. In the liberation grove, may you joyfully appear at the assembly of the Dragon-Flower.

We pray that the five-colored clouds bear your feet and you wander freely, returning to the palace of liberation. May a hundred blessings nourish your spirit and you roam unhindered, leaving the suffering of the bonds of craving.

### For a Deceased Child Under Ten

A lotus, blazing; a face of such beauty. An autumn moon, poised; a form of rare loveliness. Before the gates of kin, we rejoice in the reputation of the family tradition; the neighbors marvel at the treasure of the lane. Just as the vital breath was forming, it was suddenly extinguished. Just as the flower was at its most fragrant, it abruptly fell. This has caused the stern father to weep in compassion for his beloved child with endless sorrow. The calyx-brothers and lotus-branch siblings grieve at the emptiness of the row of geese.

### For a Deceased Woman

Evening clouds trailing: her nature was serene as the bright moon; her body was pure as autumn frost. Her tender form possessed the elegance of southern lands; her propriety surpassed the beauty of Xī Shī. It was hoped that the gates would flourish with tinkling jade, endlessly adorning the branches of the clan, that the four womanly virtues would transmit their fragrance and illumine the nine degrees of kinship. But alas, indifferent heaven did not protect, and her allotment abruptly ended. The lotus clouds dimmed and the cassia shadow sank from its radiance. The fragrant tree withered and the orchid grace ceased its luxuriance. We think of Cháng'é (*Cháng'é*, 嫦娥) departing forever to the moon palace. We lament the wandering water flowing back to the sea, never to return. Grief and sorrow for father and mother; the bitter pain settles into the bonds of marriage kin.

### Praise of Virtue

Breadth of virtue and depth of magnanimity, esteemed in propriety and music. His will surpasses past and present; his actions accord with the spirits and the luminous. His integrity rises high as the clouds above the jade ramparts; his benevolence is as vast as the waters of the surging sea.

### Praise of Military Virtue

Wind and clouds in his nature, stars in his birth. His fidelity stands firm, his righteousness is deep, his benevolence is profound, his virtue is weighty. Wielding both the civil and the martial, he is a fine talent of the purple palace. Utterly loyal and perfectly upright, he serves as a trusted heart for our lord. If it were not so, how could he be esteemed in the garrison-city, his rank and office proclaimed, his renown shaking the human realm, his fame fragrant throughout the district?

### For an Official's Adornment

We pray that the light of virtue shine upon the golden tablets, serving as the pillar of the age. May his renown resound through the jade terraces, his distinguished name shining beneath the sun. May the water of prajñā-wisdom irrigate the field of the body. May the fragrant wind of sandalwood nourish the sea of the nature. May glorious fame be firmly established, and may emoluments and advancement be preserved and attained. May the golden-purple office contend in radiance with the sun and moon. May blessings and longevity be as enduring as mountains and rivers.

### For a Woman's Recitation

Upon a lofty terrace, the precious mirror: clouds approach the face of a beauty surpassing all ages. On a day of laughter, the jeweled flower: mist envelops the countenance of a southern beauty. Know that the merit of her adornment and purity rests upon the Buddha's power, secretly dissolved.

In making the above-named dharma-garment: expressing wholehearted sincerity at the garden of Jetavana, addressing the Golden One. In assembly with the seven ranks of the ordained community, the thousand-bodied transformation-buddhas are proclaimed. This may be used to cultivate the dust and toil of the kalpas, and to transcend the fever and affliction of this present morning. The profound vow having been aroused within the inner heart, the merit shall surely nourish the honored body.

We pray that the sweet dew of all the heavens, responding to earnest entreaty, draw near, and that the great sages add their authority, pouring ambrosia upon the crown. If there are obstacles from many past lives, may they be dissolved and eliminated by reliance on mindfulness. May the transgressions and errors of the present life be annihilated by reliance upon this exceptional cause.

The seventh year of Yuánhé (*Yuánhé*, 元和) [812 CE], the sixth month. Words of consolation spoken at the appropriate time. Examined twice.
