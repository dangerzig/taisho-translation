# 佛說十八臂陀羅尼經
## Sūtra of the Dhāraṇī of the Eighteen Arms, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1118

Translated from the Chinese. Song Dynasty, translated by Dharmabhadra (法賢)

---

At that time, the World-Honored One said to Ānanda: "The sentient beings of the world are deluded with regard to true wisdom; they transmigrate through the three realms without knowing the root of suffering, and indulge body, speech, and mind in committing the four grave offenses. Such beings are deeply worthy of compassion. I possess the Great Dhāraṇī of the Eighteen Arms. If sentient beings obtain this dhāraṇī and constantly uphold and recite it, all the fundamental karmic offenses such a person has committed will be entirely eliminated, and they will furthermore be able to accumulate immeasurable merit."

At that time, the World-Honored One then proclaimed the Dhāraṇī of the Eighteen Arms:

[Dhāraṇī] "Namo Amitābhāya Tathāgatāya Arhate Samyaksaṃbuddhāya. Namaḥ Āryāvalokiteśvarāya Bodhisattvāya Mahāsattvāya Mahākāruṇikāya. Tathā oṃ samantabandhari samantāvalokite viśeṣakari visphūṭani duṣṭa pāpavitari karma mama sarvasattvānāṃ ca Bhagavān Āryāvalokiteśvara varamale varamale sphuṭani sphuṭani dhage dhage udduṇi kamoke jvaśikaraṇi sarvaduṣṭānāṃ varjaya vivarjaya sarvaduṣṭāṃ praśamanāya svāhā. Hara hara cara cara paca paca vaśīkuru sarvabhūtānāṃ svāhā."

At that time, having heard the World-Honored One proclaim this Dhāraṇī Sūtra, Ānanda rejoiced, received it with faith, paid obeisance, and withdrew.

---

**Translator's note:** The dhāraṇī is rendered here as a best-effort Sanskrit reconstruction from the Chinese phonetic transcription. The invocation opens with homage to Amitābha Tathāgata and Āryāvalokiteśvara, consistent with the text's dedication to the Avalokiteśvara of the Eighteen Arms. The precise original Sanskrit cannot be recovered with certainty from the transliteration alone.
