# 佛說堅固女經
## Sūtra of the Steadfast Girl, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0574

Translated from the Chinese. Sui dynasty, translated by Narendrayaśas (那連提耶舍)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the land of Kosala, in the city of Śrāvastī, at the Jetavana Monastery of Anāthapiṇḍada.

At that time the Buddha told the bhikṣus: "Listen carefully! I shall now teach you. If a woman wishes to generate the mind of anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi, she should first make this reflection: 'All the flattery, deceit, jealousy, greed, anger, falsehood, and other evils found in women: because of the cause and condition of generating the mind of anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi, in future lives they shall never again arise.' For this reason, all women should assuredly generate the mind of anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi."

When the Buddha spoke these words, in the assembly there was an upāsikā named Sthirā. She rose from her seat, straightened her robes, joined her ten fingers together, and said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! For the sake of benefiting all beings, for the sake of giving peace and happiness to all beings, for the sake of having compassion on all beings in all the worlds, and for the sake of liberating all devas and humans, I now generate the mind of anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi.

"World-Honored One! In the past, through beginningless birth and death, and in the future through endless transmigration, I do not give rise to fear. Only for the sake of not severing the Buddha-seed, not severing the Tathāgata-seed, and not severing the seed of omniscience do I generate the mind of anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi and cultivate the bodhisattva practice.

"World-Honored One! I now generate the mind of enlightenment for the sake of bringing peace to all beings: to serve as a rescuer for those without a rescuer, as a friend for those without a friend, as a refuge for those without a refuge, and as a shelter for those without a shelter."

At that time the Elder Śāriputra, in the great assembly, thought to himself: "This girl Sthirā, because she wishes to be a friend to all beings, has generated the mind of anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi. Because of this generation of mind, the world will have śrāvakas and pratyekabuddhas." Having thought this, he told the girl: "Sister! Because you have generated the mind of enlightenment, in future lives you will see śrāvakas and pratyekabuddhas."

Then the girl Sthirā said to Śāriputra: "From the distant past, throughout my transmigration in birth and death, I have never generated such a mind of enlightenment as this. Only now have I first generated it. Today I have obtained a great benefit. I have well obtained a human body and well obtained a lifespan. In past lives I encountered good teachers, yet I never once generated the mind of a śrāvaka or pratyekabuddha. Because of this cause, now again I am able to generate the mind of anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi for all beings."

Then Śāriputra told the girl Sthirā: "You have well obtained benefit, well obtained a human body, well obtained a lifespan, to be able to generate the mind of anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi. As you have spoken, so should you practice."

The girl Sthirā then said to the Elder Śāriputra: "As I have spoken, I shall assuredly practice."

Then Śāriputra asked the girl Sthirā: "How does a bodhisattva practice in accordance with what is taught?"

The girl said: "Śāriputra! A bodhisattva does not delight in the ground of a śrāvaka, does not delight in the ground of a pratyekabuddha, but delights only in the body of the Tathāgata, the ground of the Tathāgata, and the ground of omniscience. Thus, Elder Śāriputra, this is called the bodhisattva mahāsattva's practice in accordance with what is taught.

"Furthermore, Elder Śāriputra! When a bodhisattva mahāsattva practices generosity, she aspires to anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi, not seeking the ground of a śrāvaka or pratyekabuddha. When keeping precepts, when practicing patience, when practicing diligence, when practicing meditation, and when cultivating wisdom, she aspires to anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi, not seeking the ground of a śrāvaka or pratyekabuddha. This is called the bodhisattva mahāsattva's practice in accordance with what is taught."

Śāriputra said to the girl Sthirā: "What benefit does one who practices in accordance with what is taught obtain? Tell me."

When the girl Sthirā heard this, she said to Śāriputra: "What benefit do you wish to see?"

Śāriputra said: "Sister! I wish to see the benefit of generating the mind of anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi."

The girl said: "The characteristic of that mind is like an illusion. How can one see benefit in it?"

Śāriputra said: "If you have wisdom, then in this very body you should be able to exhaust suffering and attain arhatship. If in this body you cannot exhaust suffering and attain arhatship, then your wisdom is a great error."

The girl said: "Elder Śāriputra! If in this body I were to attain arhatship, then my wisdom would be inverted. Because I do not take arhatship in this body, in a future life I shall attain anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi. Therefore you should know that my wisdom is not inverted."

Then Śāriputra said to the girl Sthirā: "How long will it be before you attain anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi?"

The girl said: "Elder Śāriputra! I am neither a Buddha nor an arhat. I do not know how long until I attain anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi. Since Śāriputra has now attained the undefiled wisdom of an arhat, he should know how long until I attain anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi."

Śāriputra said: "I do not have the wisdom to know how long until you attain anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi."

The girl said: "In life after life, I would never wish for wisdom like Śāriputra's, which depends on another for understanding and receives faith from another. I wish for the wisdom of the Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, which knows and awakens of itself."

At that time Pūrṇamaitrāyaṇīputra thought: "We abide in the ground of the Lesser Vehicle and have attained only small wisdom. If one sees the Tathāgata's wisdom, then one knows that the wisdom we have attained is the most narrow and inferior, abiding in a small ground with small wisdom."

At that time the Elder Śāriputra thought: "Although we have attained the fruit of arhatship, we cannot broadly benefit beings and perform the deeds of a great person. Only a great person, endowed with the qualities of a great person, can greatly benefit immeasurable beings. Just as the girl Sthirā, for the benefit of herself and all beings, has generated the mind of anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi." Having thought this, he asked the girl: "Anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi: what kind of dharma is it called?"

The girl Sthirā said: "Elder Śāriputra! Anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi is the highest and most supreme among all dharmas, with nothing that surpasses it. This is called anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi. Furthermore, Śāriputra! What is called anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi: I do not see any dharma that is anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi."

Śāriputra said: "If you do not see any dharma called anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi, then how do you generate the mind of enlightenment and wish to awaken to awakening?"

The girl said: "I wish to cause those beings who walk the wrong path to abide in the right path. That is why I generate the mind of anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi."

Having heard this, Śāriputra asked the girl again: "When the Buddha attained anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi, he did not see dharma, did not see path, did not see fruit. What benefit do you see, that you wish to awaken to awakening?"

Hearing this, the girl said to Śāriputra: "Just so. When I attain anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi, in my land there shall be disciples such as Śāriputra and Maudgalyāyana. It is for the sake of seeing such things that I wish to awaken to the unsurpassed enlightenment."

Śāriputra said: "Sister! In the unobtainable Dharma, how do you wish to awaken?"

The girl said: "Just so! Just so! Śāriputra! I wish precisely to awaken to this unobtainable Dharma. The unobtainable Dharma is neither obtained nor not obtained. For this reason I wish to awaken to anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi. Having awakened to anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi, I shall turn the great Dharma wheel. When I turn the Dharma wheel, the beings of the great trichiliocosm shall all hear and know of it."

At that time Śāriputra asked the girl Sthirā: "How shall I know that in the future, when you attain anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi and turn the great Dharma wheel, its sound will pervade the great trichiliocosm?"

Then the girl Sthirā pressed the ground with the big toe of her right foot. At that moment the great trichiliocosm shook mightily. Only the place where Śāriputra sat did not move.

Then Śāriputra thought: "This woman has performed a great supernatural feat, causing the entire great trichiliocosm to shake. Why is it that only the ground where I sit does not move? Is it the Buddha's power? Or is it my own power?"

At that time the World-Honored One, knowing Śāriputra's thought, told Śāriputra: "In this very ground, in the distant past, a thousand women all bearing the name Sthirā generated the mind of anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi at the very spot where you sit, displayed great supernatural powers, and received the prediction of awakening. In the future, when Maitreya appears, at this same place a thousand women all bearing the name Sthirā shall generate the mind of anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi, display great supernatural powers, and receive the prediction of awakening. They shall become Buddhas named Viśiṣṭasthirā Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha."

Then the girl Sthirā said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! If the Buddha's awakening is not obtained in the past, not obtained in the present, and not obtained in the future, then how does the World-Honored One speak of past, present, and future Buddhas of the three times?"

The Buddha said: "Just so! Just so! Buddhahood is not obtained in the past, not obtained in the present, and not obtained in the future. The reason I speak of past, present, and future Buddhas is that these are merely conventional names used to designate the three times. It does not mean that the Buddhas have a past, a future, or a present."

Then the girl Sthirā said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! Because this Dharma is self-awakened and self-known, not heard from another, for this reason one must generate the mind of anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi."

The Buddha said: "Excellent! Excellent! Being able to know thus, in the future you shall attain anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi."

The girl said: "World-Honored One! There is no one who sees this Dharma and does not attain enlightenment. Therefore I shall assuredly attain anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi."

The Buddha said: "Sister! In the future, will you teach and transform beings?"

The girl said: "World-Honored One! There is no one who sees this Dharma and does not teach. Therefore I shall assuredly be able to teach and transform beings."

The Buddha said: "In the future, will you become a great guide?"

The girl said: "World-Honored One! There is no one who sees this Dharma and does not become a guide. Therefore I shall assuredly become a great guide."

At that time Śakra, holding māndārava flowers, stood before the Buddha. He gave the flowers to the girl Sthirā, saying: "Take these flowers and scatter them upon the Buddha." The girl Sthirā received the flowers and scattered them upon the Buddha. Through the Buddha's supernatural power, the flowers halted in the sky.

At that time the World-Honored One gently smiled. It is the nature of all Buddhas that when they smile, light of blue, yellow, red, white, and purple crystal, in various mingled colors, issues from their mouths. The light illuminated everywhere up to the Brahmā heaven, returned to the Buddha, circled him three times on the right, and entered through the crown of his head.

At that time Ānanda said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! The Buddhas do not smile without cause or for small reasons. For what cause and condition does the Buddha now smile?"

The Buddha told Ānanda: "Do you see this girl Sthirā? When this girl's life in this world ends, she shall cast off her female body and become a man. In the Nakṣatra kalpa she shall attain anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi, and shall be called Samantadarśin Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha.

"At the first assembly when Samantadarśin Buddha teaches the Dharma, twenty trillion people shall exhaust their defilements, cast off their heavy burdens, and realize arhatship. At the second teaching, fifteen trillion people shall attain arhatship. At the third teaching, ten trillion people shall attain arhatship. Thereafter, immeasurable hundreds of thousands of trillions of beings shall attain the fruit of arhatship.

"Thus, Ānanda, in the land of Samantadarśin Tathāgata there shall be no Naraka, no hungry ghosts, and no animals. The beings of that land shall all be accomplished in the ten good deeds. They shall not engage in commerce, farming, or labor. That buddha-land shall have immeasurable merit. Therefore, if a good man or good woman generates the mind of anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi, one should not give rise to a mind of contempt toward such a person. Only the Tathāgata clearly knows this matter; it is not something that śrāvakas or pratyekabuddhas can comprehend. Therefore, Ānanda, if anyone loves me and reveres me, they should not give rise to a mind of disdain toward a bodhisattva."

When the Buddha had finished teaching this sūtra, the Elder Ānanda, the Elder Śāriputra, the upāsikā Sthirā, and all the devas and humans, hearing the Buddha's teaching, were all filled with great joy.
