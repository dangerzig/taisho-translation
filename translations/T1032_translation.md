# 瑜伽蓮華部念誦法
## Yoga Lotus Division Recitation Method

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1032

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

Having first entered the ritual space, one wholeheartedly prostrates, confesses, makes vows, and dedicates merit. Then one forms the lotus-palms mudrā: join both hands with palms cupped hollow, resembling an unopened lotus bud. Recite the all-dharmas purification mantra, touching the seal to the heart, forehead, throat, and crown once each, thereby becoming purified. The mantra says:

*Oṃ svabhāva-śuddhaḥ sarva-dharma svabhāva-śuddho 'haṃ*

Then, with the right knee on the ground and the lotus-palms placed atop the head, recite the mantra and visualize prostrating at the feet of all buddhas, bodhisattvas, and the principal deity. The mantra says:

*Oṃ padma bhuḥ*

Having done this, all worship and offerings to the buddhas and bodhisattvas are fully accomplished. Then one sits in full or half lotus posture and forms the lotus samaya mudrā: interlock both hands with fingers crossed outward, raising both thumbs and both little fingers side by side. Present this to all buddhas, bodhisattvas, and the principal deity, and they will recall their original vows and bestow empowerment. The mantra says:

*Oṃ vajra-padma-samaya stvaṃ*

Next, form the supreme joy mudrā: using the same hand position as before, lower both middle fingers into the palms and press the fingertips together. The mantra says:

*Oṃ samaya hūṃ surata-stvaṃ*

Next, form the opening-the-heart mudrā: visualize on the two nipples the syllable TRA on the right and ṬA on the left, like door pivots. With the vajra-bond, pull apart three times to open one's heart, as if opening a folding fan. The mantra says:

*Oṃ vajra-bandha trāṭ*

Next, form the entering-wisdom mudrā: using the vajra-bond as before, tuck both thumbs into the palms. Visualize one cubit ahead a white lotus with the syllable AḤ upon it, emitting white light that flows into the heart and rests on a white lotus there. The mantra says:

*Oṃ vajra-āveśa aḥ*

Next, form the closing-the-heart-door mudrā: as before, but press both index fingers against the joints of both thumbs and touch the seal to the heart. The mantra says:

*Oṃ vajra-muṣṭi vaṃ*

Next, form the samādhi mudrā: interlock both hands palms upward and place them below the navel, with both thumbs pointing upward and touching. Recite the entering-samādhi mantra:

*Oṃ samādhi padme hrīḥ*

Next, sit upright with right mindfulness and enter samādhi. Press the tongue against the palate and cease all mental grasping. Contemplate that all dharmas, internal and external, are entirely without substance. If stray thoughts are many, one should first count breaths: count from one breath to seven, then from one to seven again, continuing without interruption. When the mind is free of grasping, breath counting is unnecessary.

Entering deeply into the pure realm of nothingness, one visualizes all buddhas filling space, their bodies small as sesame seeds yet complete with all marks and excellences. They address the practitioner: "Good man, contemplate your own mind. Also contemplate your own body prostrating at all buddhas' feet." One replies: "World-Honored Ones, how should I contemplate the mind? What is its appearance?" The buddhas answer: "Good man, the mind's appearance is difficult to fathom." They bestow a mantra, to be secretly recited while contemplating:

*Oṃ citta-prativediṃ karomi*

The practitioner secretly recites this while contemplating their own mind as a pure moon. The buddhas say: "Good man, your mind is originally just like this, merely obscured by adventitious defilements. Know that this is the mind of enlightenment." They bestow another mantra:

*Oṃ bodhicittam utpādayāmi*

Reciting this, one contemplates the moon in the heart becoming ever more clear and unblemished. Within the moon-disc, contemplate the syllable HRĪḤ, which becomes an eight-petaled white lotus, brilliant and radiant. The mantra says:

*Oṃ tiṣṭha vajra-padma*

Next, contemplate this white lotus expanding throughout the dharma-realm, equal in measure to space. The mantra says:

*Oṃ sphara vajra-padma*

Next, contemplate the white lotus gradually contracting, with all the buddhas in space entering it, until it is equal in measure to one's own body. The mantra says:

*Oṃ saṃhara vajra-padma*

The lotus then transforms into Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, adorned with a jeweled crown and necklaces, complete in marks and excellences, emitting great radiance pervading the dharma-realm. Amitāyus Buddha, the principal deity, sits atop the crown. In the left hand is a white lotus; the right hand makes the gesture of opening a lotus. The mantra says:

*Oṃ samayohaṃ mahāsamayohaṃ sarva-tathāgata-abhisambodhi-vajra-padmātmakuhaṃ*

One who enters this samādhi is seen by all devas, nāgas, and the eight classes as no different from Avalokiteśvara. This eliminates the practitioner's immeasurable kalpas of grave karmic offenses. All supreme aspirations are without exception fulfilled. One presently obtains the complete vajra dharma-body, and contemplating this body, one attains perfect enlightenment.

Next, form the empowerment mudrā: with the outward-crossing vajra-bond, bend both index fingers to press against both thumbs. Touch the seal to the heart, forehead, throat, and crown, reciting once each. The mantra says:

*Oṃ hrīḥ sarva-kārya-adhiṣṭhita sarva vaṃ*

Next, form the coronation mudrā: with palms joined, raise both thumbs and bend the eight fingers inward like a jewel shape. Place atop the head and recite three times, visualizing the five tathāgatas crowning one's head. The mantra says:

*Oṃ hrīḥ maṇi-padma-abhiṣiñca sarva vaṃ trāḥ hrīḥ*

Next, form the garland-tying mudrā and recite the mantra. Starting from the coronation mudrā at the forehead, separate both hands and circle them three times around the back of the head as if tying a garland. Then bring them forward past the ears and let the little fingers hang down like garland streamers. The mantra says:

*Oṃ vajra-padma-māle vaṃ hrīḥ*

Next, form the armor mudrā: make vajra fists as a child grips, pressing both index fingers against the joints of both thumbs. Place at the heart, then extend both index fingers overlapping about one joint's width, with the right atop the left. Visualize the syllable OṂ on the face of the right index finger and HŪṂ on the left. While continuously reciting these two syllables, visualize them emitting green light like unbroken lotus-root threads. Circle the light three times before the heart, three times behind the back, then at the navel, both knees, the waist, back to the heart, the right shoulder, left shoulder, the forehead, and three times around the back of the head. Then form vajra fists and open from the little fingers, letting them hang down like celestial garments.

Without releasing the bond, clap the palms together three times. The mantra says:

*Oṃ vajra-padma-tuṣya hūṃ*

Next, within the altar, visualize the syllable AḤ becoming a moon-disc. Within the moon-disc, the syllable HRĪḤ becomes the principal deity's body, emitting great radiance, with countless retinue appearing within the circle of light.

Form the hook mudrā: with the vajra-bond, raise the right index finger like a hook and beckon three times, one recitation each. The mantra says:

*Oṃ āyahi jaḥ*

Next, the lasso mudrā: as before, touch both index fingertips together in a ring, drawing the principal deity into the visualized wisdom-body. The mantra says:

*Oṃ āḥ he hūṃ hūṃ*

Next, the chain mudrā: as before, interlink both index fingers and both thumbs like chain links, causing the principal deity to remain. The mantra says:

*Oṃ he sphota vaṃ*

Next, the bell mudrā: as before, tuck both thumbs into the palms and shake the hands three times like ringing a bell, causing the principal deity to rejoice. The mantra says:

*Oṃ viśaya hūṃ*

Next, form the scented-water offering mudrā and recite the hundred-syllable mantra or other praises. Cross both hands outward and open the palms, touching both middle fingertips together. Slightly bend both index fingers, leaving about one wheat-grain's gap from the middle finger backs without touching, forming a shape like a three-pronged vajra head. Both thumbs press against the lower sides of the index fingers. Visualize the cupped hands filled with fragrant water, recite the mantra, and pour forward to wash the principal deity's feet. The mantra says:

*Oṃ vajra-ḍāka cchaḥ*

The principal deity having arrived, form the karma mudrā: place the left vajra fist at the heart, visualizing holding a white lotus. The right vajra fist rotates above, visualizing opening the lotus petals. One's body becomes identical to the principal deity. The activity mantra says:

*Oṃ vajra-dharma hrīḥ*

Next, form the samaya mudrā: vajra-bond with both index fingers bent and thumbs pressing down (same as the empowerment mudrā above), causing the principal deity to recall their original vow and bestow empowerment. The mantra says:

*Oṃ sarva-kāri*

Then form the vajra play inner offering mudrā: vajra-bond with both thumbs extended horizontally toward the body, side by side at the heart. The mantra says:

*Oṃ mahā-rati*

Next, the garland: same mudrā unchanged, but extend both arms straight forward at forehead level. The mantra says:

*Oṃ rūpa-śubhe*

Next, the song: without changing the mudrā, bring it from the forehead to the mouth, release, and extend both hands forward and down, as if producing song. The mantra says:

*Oṃ śruta-saukhye*

Next, the dance: rotate both vajra fists around each other, then open from the little fingers and form vajra-palms joined together, placing them atop the head. The mantra says:

*Oṃ sarva-pūri*

These are the four inner offerings.

Next, the incense mudrā: with the vajra-bond, release downward, as if burning incense. The mantra says:

*Oṃ pradhāraṇini*

Next, the flower mudrā: with the vajra-bond, release upward, as if scattering flowers. The mantra says:

*Oṃ phalagami*

Next, the lamp mudrā: as with the play mudrā, but bend both thumbtips forward toward each other without touching. The mantra says:

*Oṃ sute jagari*

Next, the perfume mudrā: release the vajra-bond and rub the chest as if applying fragrance. The mantra says:

*Oṃ sugandhi*

These are the four outer offerings.

Next, form the division's heart root mudrā: palms joined, with both ring fingers and both index fingers initially crossed, both little fingers and both thumbs spread apart. Recite the hundred-syllable mantra three times, then the three-and-a-half syllable mantra seven times. Release the mudrā above the head, then take up the recitation beads. Hold the beads at the heart with both hands while reciting, contemplating the principal deity emitting pure radiance that flows into the crown and illuminates the moon in the heart, where a white lotus rests. One's body and the principal deity become identical in color and appearance, like seeing oneself in a clear mirror, the practitioner and deity forming each other's reflections. When the prescribed number of recitations is complete, confess, make vows, and again offer the samaya and the eight offerings. Then dismiss the holy one to return to their original palace. For the dismissal, use the samaya mudrā at the mouth and release. The mantra says:

*Oṃ padma-sattva muḥ*

Then again form the empowerment, coronation, armor, and clap mudrās. Afterward, exit the ritual space. One may read Mahāyāna sūtras or print buddha images and build stūpas. At all times, the samādhi of the principal deity should not be interrupted. When in impure places, visualize atop the head the dharmadhātu-udbhava syllable emitting red light, namely the syllable RAṂ. For all food consumed, empower it with this syllable, and it will not become impure. For all offerings of incense and flowers, empower them with this syllable emitting white light, and they will be free of impurity, and the offerings will pervade the dharma-realm.

The hundred-syllable mantra of the Lotus Division says:

*Oṃ padma-sattva samayam anupālaya padma-sattva-tvenopatiṣṭha dṛḍho me bhava sutoṣyo me bhava anurakto me bhava supoṣyo me bhava sarva-siddhiṃ me prayaccha sarva-karmasu ca me cittaṃ śreyaḥ kuru hūṃ ha ha ha ha hoḥ bhagavan sarva-tathāgata-padma mā me muñca padmī bhava mahā-samaya-sattva hrīḥ*
