# 觀自在大悲成就瑜伽蓮華部念誦法門
## Dharma Gate of Recitation for the Yoga of Avalokiteśvara's Great Compassion Accomplishment in the Lotus Division

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1030

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

All who wish to practice within this gate must know the proper sequence of procedures. If one does not thoroughly understand the ritual, one's efforts will be wasted, squandering time. Therefore, I have examined and verified the methods from various sūtras, ritual texts, and yoga manuals, combining them into a single compilation, so that those of initial aspiration who seek liberation may ascend to the stage of attainment. Hence I have composed this to accomplish that practice.

All practitioners should first enter the great samaya altar of consecration, receive the bodhisattva precepts, generate the mind of great compassion, and relinquish body, life, and wealth to benefit sentient beings. With single-pointed aspiration for the buddhas and bodhisattvas, the body free from desire and anger, the mind diligent, courageous, gathered and undistracted, and loving-kindness, compassion, joy, and equanimity never for a moment ceasing, one leads immeasurable beings to the other shore. Internally and externally pure, maintaining the utmost cleanliness, one should always show respect toward elders, preceptors, and ācāryas. The mind should be without duplicity and one's words always sincere. When beggars come, give according to one's means and never send them away empty-handed. In all four postures and while attending to various tasks, at every moment one should settle the mind in stillness and carefully observe that all conditioned things are impermanent. In brief, all dharmas are like reflections in a mirror.

Having understood this, one should don the armor of diligence, wield the secret vidyā-mantra sword that destroys the army of afflictions, shatter the bonds of the hindrances, emerge from the ocean of delusion in birth and death, arrive at the site of awakening and sit upon the vajra seat, firmly resolving not to allow retrogression. Only such a person should practice this supreme dharma gate within the Mahāyāna realm. Therefore I shall now explain in proper order.

Those who wish to recite should first purify the ground. The method of ground purification: the ground may be four cubits, eight cubits, twelve cubits, or sixteen cubits square; choose the measurement according to one's ability. Dig to knee depth or one cubit deep, removing insects, ants, potsherds, stones, hair, bones, ashes, charcoal, chaff, thorns, tree roots, and all impure objects. Fill with clean earth, pack it firm and level. Then upon it build the practice chamber and apply pure plaster to the room.

First determine the orientation: for disaster-dispelling rites, the room should face south with the door opening southward; sit facing north with legs crossed. For merit-increasing rites, open the door to the west, face east, and sit in full lotus. For wrathful rites, open the door to the north, face south, and squat with the left foot pressing upon the right. For rites of friendship and mutual affection, open the door to the east, face west, and sit with both feet together upright and knees spread apart. This is the method for building the practice chamber and the sitting positions for each type of activity. The mudrā methods follow accordingly.

If a clean room already exists, or one practices on open ground, a stone mountain, or similar, one may simply construct the altar and sit according to the appropriate position for one's purpose. Having built the room, first apply cow dung plaster according to the proper method, empowering it with the mantra before use. When first sweeping the ground, recite:

*Oṃ hara hara rāju-grahāraṇāya svāhā*

Recite three times while sweeping. After sweeping, when removing the swept dirt, recite:

*Oṃ śvete mahā-śvete khadani svāhā*

Recite three times to remove the dirt. After removing the dirt, smear the altar with cow dung while reciting:

*Oṃ karāle mahā-karāle svāhā*

Recite this while applying the plaster. When washing all offering vessels and scented-water vessels, use this mantra:

*Namaḥ stri-ye dvikānāṃ sarva-tathāgatānāṃ oṃ viraji viraji mahā-vajri sata sata sarate sarate taile vidhamani sambhajani dharamati siddha-kriya-triyaṃ svāhā*

Recite three times while washing all offering implements. This mantra is recited in order to purify the offenses of having violated the samaya and all defilements from contact. For all offering materials not yet presented, place them together in a clean spot and recite this vidyā-mantra while forming the mudrā:

*Oṃ śikhari vajri aḥ*

The mudrā for this is: with the right hand, grip the three fingers from the middle finger down around the thumb forming a peak, and extend the index finger straight upward. With this mudrā, stamp the objects and wave it rightward over them to protect all incense, flowers, lamps, food, and drink. Then proceed to the bathing place. These preparations may be done by oneself or by one attendant disciple.

When about to form mudrās, one must first form the three samaya mudrās before forming any others.

The first, the Buddha Division samaya mudrā: place both hands side by side, middle fingers touching as if scooping water. Each index finger rests on the upper segment of the middle finger, then both thumbs press the lower segment of the index fingers. The mantra says:

*Oṃ tathāgatodbhavāya svāhā*

Recite this and place the mudrā atop the head. Visualize this mudrā as the tathāgata's true body, no different at all; to see this mudrā is to see the Buddha.

Next, the Lotus Division samaya mudrā: press both wrist-bases together, raise both hands with all ten fingers spread open. Both thumbs touch tip to tip, and both little fingers likewise. Raise the mudrā above the head slightly to the right, and visualize this mudrā as the holy Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva. The mantra says:

*Oṃ padmodbhavāya svāhā*

Next, the Vajra Division samaya mudrā: place both hands palm-down, right over left in reverse overlap. Hook the right little finger over the left thumb, and the right thumb over the left little finger. Raise the mudrā above the head slightly to the left, and visualize this mudrā as Vajragarbha Bodhisattva. The mantra says:

*Oṃ vajrodbhavāya svāhā*

Having formed these three samaya mudrās, one may then form all other mudrās. Those who follow the secret treasury vidyā-mantras should observe this order. I shall now explain the essentials.

All practitioners, first thing in the morning before doing anything else, when about to go out, should first form the mudrā and recite the vidyā-mantra, and only then proceed. The mantra says:

*Namo ratna-trayāya. Namaś caṇḍa-vajrapāṇaye mahā-yakṣa-senāpataye. Namo vajra-krodhāya duṣṭa-kaṭa-bhairavāya. Tadyathā: oṃ amṛta-kuṇḍali khākha khāhi khāhi tiṣṭha bandha bandha hana hana garjha garjha visphoṭaya visphoṭaya sarva-vighnān vināyaka-gaṇapati-dvi-daṇḍa-karāya hūṃ phaṭ svāhā.*

The mudrā: with each hand, press the thumb upon the little fingernail and extend the other three fingers. Then cross both arms, right over left, resting each hand upon the opposite upper arm. Complete the mudrā at the heart. Assume this posture: stand with feet in a V-shape, puff the cheeks, bite the right lip, and assume an angry expression. Recite seven times. If one wishes to bind obstacle-making ghosts and spirits, make fists and bind with the three fingers of each hand while saying "bandha bandha." Having done this, then go out.

When entering impure places or going to the privy, one should also protect the body. The mudrā: interlock both little fingers and ring fingers right over left inward, stand both middle fingers upright touching at the tips, place both index fingers on the upper segments of the middle fingers separated by a slight gap, and press both thumbs on the middle segment of the middle fingers. This is the body-protection mudrā. The mantra says:

*Oṃ vajra-gīni praṇitāya svāhā*

Use this mudrā to stamp the five points: crown, both shoulders, heart, and throat. This is the body-protection method. When going to the privy, snap the fingers three times as a warning. The finger-snapping mantra says:

*Oṃ kili kili rāva-laudra hūṃ phaṭ*

One recitation, one snap, up to three times. When washing the hands, visualize in the mind the Kuṇḍali mantra and form, then wash. After washing, rinse the mouth. The mouth-rinsing mudrā: with the right hand, bend the middle and ring fingers into the palm, extend the thumb, index, and little fingers to hold water, and rinse three times. The mantra says:

*Oṃ jule jule kuru kuru svāhā*

Having rinsed with this mudrā and mantra, next purify the body of hidden obstacle-making ghosts and spirits. The mudrā: make fists with both hands, then extend both index fingers. Insert the right index fingertip into the left fist and grip it, and likewise insert the left index fingertip into the right fist. The mantra says:

*Oṃ hana hana amṛte hūṃ phaṭ*

Recite three times and slowly stroke downward from the head, three times, causing all māras and obstacles within the body to be entirely eliminated. Visualize that the māras have departed. Then form the binding-māras mudrā: with the left hand, beckon outward. Then place the right hand palm-down upon the back of the left hand, interlock all eight fingers, twist leftward, turn toward the heart forming a combined fist, and stand both thumbs upright at the heart. Then hook the right elbow inside the left elbow and extend it outward. Place the mudrā atop the head, then open and slowly lower it. The mantra says:

*Oṃ kili kili bhīla-udāra-prakṛti mahā-krodha vijaya-nikṛnta hūṃ phaṭ bandha bandha svāhā*

By the power of this mudrā and mantra, all māras are bound. Then visualize one's body clad in vajra armor. Having completed these procedures, when bathing one should follow the mantra-code and siddhi bathing methods. Know that one may use only the Kuṇḍali small heart mantra for self-bathing and self-consecration:

*Oṃ amṛte hūṃ phaṭ*

The mudrā common to all three divisions: with the right hand, press the thumb upon the little fingernail and extend the other three fingers. Use this mudrā and mantra to sprinkle the body and clothes, empower water for bathing, and for putting on garments.

When bathing, one should single-mindedly recollect the buddhas and bodhisattvas without distraction, visualizing the principal deity as no different from oneself. First visualize the principal deity and the Three Jewels as present before one's eyes, then offer three cupped-handfuls of bathing water. The mudrā: cup both hands as if holding water, but touch both thumbs to both index fingertips. First offer to the Buddha:

*Oṃ tīre tīre buddhāya svāhā*

Next, offer to the Dharma:

*Oṃ tīre tīre dharmāya svāhā*

Next, offer to the Sangha:

*Oṃ tīre tīre saṅghāya svāhā*

Next, offer to the principal deity, using the general mantra for all three:

*Oṃ arghaṃ bhagavan praticcha imaṃ svāhā*

When offering water to the principal deity, one may also recite the principal mantra. Having completed these procedures, when bathing in a river, recite the principal mantra, then slowly emerge. When taking up garments, recite this mantra and sprinkle water on the clothing before putting it on:

*Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-adhiṣṭhita amṛta-vara svāhā*

After sprinkling, when putting on the garments, recite:

*Oṃ vimala-parivarta vajri hūṃ*

The mudrā is simply both hands in fists. When putting on all garments, ornaments, crowns, earrings, bracelets, and all bodily adornments, recite this mantra throughout. While performing these procedures, one should not give rise to anger, wrong thoughts, or impurities, and should not look at anything inauspicious.

After bathing, when walking to the practice chamber, one should not go barefoot. Visualize an eight-petaled lotus supporting one's feet. One's body is identical in form to the principal deity, flanked left and right by devas, nāgas, and the eight classes, with an escort surrounding one in front and behind. Further visualize the principal deity clearly present before one. Along the path, do not step over living plants, any images, or even animal forms. Do not step on offering materials, stūpa shadows, image shadows, or the shadows of teachers and monks.

Arriving before the practice chamber, wash and rinse according to the proper method, then enter. When first entering, at the threshold make a single "hūṃ" sound and enter. Before the buddha image in the room, generate this thought: "The true bodies of the buddhas, bodhisattvas, and great dharma-kings of the three times are permanently abiding. My fleshly eyes cannot perceive them. May they, with the eye of the Way, see my taking of refuge." Having generated this thought, prostrate with the three karmas, pressing all five limbs to the ground with reverence, also saying aloud: "I now pay homage." After prostrating, confess, rejoice in others' merit, make extensive vows, and pledge to cultivate virtue, then burn incense. By the fragrance of this incense, all evil ghosts and spirits are dispelled. The incense mantra, used by the Vajra Division and all other divisions from this point onward:

*Oṃ padma-muni mohaya mohaya jaya-mohani svāhā*

Having done this, empower water with a mantra and sprinkle it in the four directions to establish a protective boundary. The mantra says:

*Oṃ ārolik svāhā*

Having established the boundary, then perform the visualization practice. First contemplate a single syllable HRĪḤ. Amitāyus Tathāgata arises from this syllable, with form complete and perfect. From the tathāgata's body flows wondrous fragrant milk-water, forming a great ocean. Within this ocean, visualize a syllable PHRĀṂ, which transforms into a turtle, vast beyond measure in yojanas, golden in color. Upon the turtle, visualize a lotus with eight petals in three tiers, arising from a syllable HRĪḤ. Within the lotus, visualize a syllable SU, with a syllable HŪṂ on each side. These syllables together form Mount Sumeru, with eight peaks composed of many jewels. Within this mountain, contemplate five chambers, externally appearing as five but internally a single space. Within the chambers, visualize eight great golden vajra pillars, composed of wondrous jewels, inlaid and bejeweled. Above are makara-fish heads holding jeweled chains in their mouths, hung with golden bells and draped with ornamental canopies, adorned with jeweled canopy-flowers, silk streamers, net-ribbons cascading and intertwining, emitting pure radiance. Crystal and other jewels form the ground, strewn with renowned flowers such as kusuma. Pure morality serves as the perfume, its fragrance surpassing all. Liberation serves as the burning incense, its clouds rising sublime. Wisdom-maṇi lamps shine brilliantly. Jeweled trees stand in rows; fragrant breezes gently touch, releasing all fragrances together. Silk banners flutter amid jeweled plates interspersed with jeweled vases. Countless yakṣas, rākṣasas, devas, and others surround the space, playing music, performing vajra dances, and singing vajra songs. Auspicious clouds fill the air, raining innumerable transcendent fragrant flowers from within, drifting gently through space. Various fragrant delicacies and scented drinks are arrayed as offerings.

The practitioner should make this visualization serene, calm, and clearly vivid, in full correspondence with the mind. When entering this contemplation, recite:

*Namo samanta-buddhānāṃ sarvathā-udgate sphara hīmaṃ gagana-khaṃ svāhā*

By reciting this vidyā-mantra and entering the samādhis, whatever the mind contemplates is accomplished. These are the truthful words of the Buddha and should not be doubted. The reason for performing this visualization first is to detach from all internal and external objects and attain purity, like space unattached to anything. To further purify the body, recite this mantra together with the heart mudrā:

*Oṃ svabhāva-śuddhaḥ sarva-dharma svabhāva-śuddho 'haṃ*

Having purified the body by reciting this mantra, then make this declaration: "Through immeasurable kalpas I have drifted in birth and death, mired in the mud of afflictions, never meeting a good friend. Following deluded mind, I have been unable to escape. I have never sought the unsurpassed Way. Therefore, let me now generate the mind of awakening." One should then pronounce aloud the syllable A. The reason for pronouncing this syllable is that A signifies the unborn, the gate of emptiness and quiescence. This gate alone can distance one from dust and defilement. Those who follow this dharma can eliminate the sins of immeasurable kalpas, numerous as dust-motes. It is like a bright sun in clear sky illuminating universally; all darkness naturally disperses. The sun is the sun of wisdom; the sky is the sky of the mantra. This wisdom-sun illuminates the mantra-sky.

With deep afflictions as darkness, contemplate the realm of space with a serene and pure mind. Throughout space are tathāgata forms, small as sesame seeds, yet complete with all marks and excellences. These tathāgatas are all before the practitioner. They snap their fingers as a wake-up call and say: "Good man, if you wish to generate the mind of awakening, you should contemplate your own mind."

Hearing these words, the practitioner should leap up joyfully from the seat and prostrate with all five limbs before each tathāgata with single-minded devotion. When prostrating, recite:

*Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-vandana karomi*

After reciting this and visualizing prostration to the tathāgatas, contemplate one's own mind. When contemplating the mind, recite:

*Oṃ citta-prativeda karomi*

Reciting this vidyā-mantra, within one's own mind contemplate a moon, round and white in form but not yet fully clear. Address the Buddha: "I have seen the mind. It is like the moon, but not yet clear." The Buddha says: "Good man, excellent! You have seen the mind." Then recite this mantra to contemplate the mind again, making it extremely bright and pure:

*Oṃ bodhicittam utpādayāmi*

Reciting this and contemplating the heart-moon until it is extremely pure, then, to make the mind of awakening firm, contemplate upon the moon a vajra-lotus. When contemplating this flower, recite:

*Oṃ tiṣṭha vajra-padma*

Reciting this mantra while contemplating the flower: upon the flower is a vajra, and because of this form it is called the vajra-lotus. Generate this thought: "This mind of mine is no different from the vajra-lotus." It gradually opens and blazes with light, full and inexhaustible. When entering this vajra-lotus-blossoming samādhi, recite:

*Oṃ sphara padma*

Reciting this mantra, contemplate the flower. Its radiance illuminates the wondrous lands of buddhas as numerous as the sands of immeasurable Ganges rivers. Within this light, throughout the Lotus Division, in accord with the practitioner's practice, a clear, pure celestial voice resounds. All the tathāgatas of every direction enter this flower. After the tathāgatas have entered, the wondrous flower gradually contracts as before, and one remains in a body of whatever size one can manifest. When performing this sword-flower samādhi, recite:

*Oṃ saṃhara vajra-padma*

Reciting this mantra, the flower contracts and settles. Then enter the vajra-lotus body: "This vajra samādhi-lotus has become my body." When entering this contemplation, recite:

*Oṃ vajra-padma-kuhaṃ samayohaṃ mahāsamayohaṃ*

Having contemplated this vajra-lotus body, visualize one's body as identical to that celestial form. The reason for first entering the visualization of Avalokiteśvara is to firmly and swiftly develop the vajra body, and to vow that in life after life one will certainly obtain the holy one's body. The mudrā for entering this contemplation: interlock the three fingers from the index finger downward outward, touch both index fingertips together like lotus petals, and extend both thumbs upright. The mantra says:

*Oṃ hrīḥ sarva-kāri-adhiṣṭha samaṃ hrīḥ*

With this mudrā and mantra, stamp four points: heart, between the eyebrows, throat, and crown. Next, perform the self-consecration method, which enables the practitioner to swiftly attain the samādhi-body in complete accomplishment. The mudrā: join both palms, then compress the four fingers from the index finger down, pressing the fingertips and bending the finger-backs into a jeweled-lotus shape. The mantra says:

*Oṃ dhrī maṇi padma-abhiṣiñca samaṃ hrīḥ*

Recite this mantra and raise the mudrā to the forehead, then separate both hands and circle around the head to the back of the crown, then slowly lower them along the body to the heart and rest there. Then make vajra fists with each hand and extend both index fingers straight. Visualize on the face of the right index finger a syllable OṂ, and on the left index finger a syllable AḤ. Circle both hands around the body as an armor-donning method. Having done this, all devas, nāgas, humans, and non-humans will see the practitioner as a vajra body clad in vajra armor. All māras and heretics will not dare look directly, and will scatter in retreat, unable to cause harm.

Having done this, next visualize before oneself the principal deity's image, with the usual marks and excellences. As described above, within the Sumeru mountain visualization of the five jeweled chambers, in the central chamber single-mindedly contemplate Amitāyus Tathāgata, complete with all marks, blazing with radiance. To the buddha's right is Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva; to the left, Mahāsthāmaprāpta Bodhisattva. If one additionally upholds other bodhisattvas of the division, place them slightly forward on the right, as described in the original method. All deva-spirits, messengers, and bodhisattvas should be arranged according to one's preference.

Having completed this visualization, take the syllables of whichever vidyā-mantra one upholds and visualize them distributed from head to feet, each in its proper place according to meaning and skillful arrangement. After distributing the syllables, stand and form the jeweled-chariot mudrā to welcome the holy ones. The visualization made earlier represents the dharma-body; the ones now invited represent the wisdom-body. One bends the wisdom-body to come and join the dharma-body; therefore one first visualizes and then invites. The mudrā: place both hands palms-up, facing inward, and interlock the fingers. Touch both index fingertips together sideways. Use the thumbs to flick the middle fingertips inward. This is the jeweled-chariot mudrā. Visualize the jeweled chariot riding five-colored clouds, traveling to the wondrous buddha-land to welcome the holy ones. The mantra says:

*Oṃ turu turu hūṃ*

Recite this mantra, visualizing the chariot traveling with the mudrā. Then, using the division's heart mantra and mudrā, invite the holy ones to descend. The heart mudrā: interlock all ten fingers inward into a fist, then draw out the right thumb and beckon inward with it. The mantra says:

*Oṃ ārolik svāhā*

The jeweled chariot goes to welcome them, then the dharma invitation brings them along with the chariot to the practitioner's place of offering. Upon arriving, they leave the chariot and enter the chamber. Use the hook mudrā and mantra to summon them, then lead them into the chamber. Once inside, perform the joy mudrā, causing the holy ones to rejoice. After that, set out a seat and invite them to sit. The seat mudrā is the lotus samādhi mudrā. The mantra says:

*Oṃ mira mirāya svāhā*

After setting the seat, offer arghya water using the principal deity's mudrā and mantra. After offering, immediately seal the boundary: first the ground boundary, then the four directional boundaries, then the sky boundary, all the same as in other divisions. After sealing the boundary, perform the various offering rites, further offering incense, flowers, food, and drink. The food-offering mantra says:

*Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-rasa-rasāgra-utra pūja-samaye hūṃ*

Having made these offerings, confess, make vows, and dedicate merit before the holy ones, as usual. Then take up the rosary, settle the mind, and recite. The rosary mantra says:

*Oṃ vajra-pramitra-samaya hūṃ*

Recite seven times, then take up the rosary and recite the main mantra. After recitation, confess again. After confessing, make vows. After vows, recite various praises and hymns, singing of the immeasurable merit of the tathāgatas, the Three Jewels, and the principal deity. Then form the offering mudrās again and skillfully present the arghya offering. After the offering, dissolve the boundary. After dissolving the boundary, recite the dismissal mantra:

*Oṃ kṛtau-ba sarva-sattvārtha-siddhi-natāye-tha anugage-ca-tu-haṃ buddha-viṣayaṃ punar-āgamanāya oṃ padma muḥ*

Reciting this mantra, form the lotus mudrā, raise it to the crown, and release it. Then, as before, use the jeweled chariot to send the holy ones away. When using the mudrā and mantra for dismissal, flick outward. While reciting, also dissolve the syllables visualized on one's body and all the methods employed. Then bow to the Buddha and withdraw. The recitation gate is complete.

> May I transmit this wondrous dharma gate;
> May diligent practitioners swiftly succeed.
> May this small virtue pervade the dharma-realm,
> Benefiting and delighting beings inexhaustibly through the three times.
