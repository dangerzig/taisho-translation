# 般若波羅蜜多心經
## Heart Sūtra of Prajñāpāramitā

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0253

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Prajñā (般若) and Liyan (利言) and others

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the city of Rājagṛha, on Mount Gṛdhrakūṭa, together with a great assembly of bhikṣus and an assembly of bodhisattvas. At that time, the Buddha, the World-Honored One, entered a samādhi called Vast and Profound.

At that time, within the assembly there was a bodhisattva-mahāsattva named Avalokiteśvara. When practicing the deep prajñāpāramitā, he illuminated the five aggregates and saw that they are all empty, and he was freed from all suffering and adversity. Then Śāriputra, empowered by the Buddha's might, joined his palms in reverence and addressed the Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Avalokiteśvara: "Good man! If there are those who wish to study the practice of the profound prajñāpāramitā, how should they cultivate it?" Having asked thus,

the Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Avalokiteśvara then addressed the venerable Śāriputra: "Śāriputra! If good men and good women wish to practice the practice of the profound prajñāpāramitā, they should contemplate the nature of the five aggregates as empty. Śāriputra! Form is not different from emptiness; emptiness is not different from form. Form is itself emptiness; emptiness is itself form. Feeling, perception, volitional formations, and consciousness are likewise. Śāriputra! All these dharmas have the characteristic of emptiness: they are not produced and not destroyed, not defiled and not pure, not increased and not decreased. Therefore, in emptiness there is no form, no feeling, perception, volitional formations, or consciousness; no eye, ear, nose, tongue, body, or mind; no form, sound, smell, taste, touch, or mental object; no eye-element and so on up to no mind-consciousness element. There is no ignorance and no extinction of ignorance, and so on up to no old age and death and no extinction of old age and death. There is no suffering, origination, cessation, or path. There is no wisdom and no attainment. Because there is nothing to attain, the bodhisattva, relying on prajñāpāramitā, has a mind without obstruction. Because there is no obstruction, there is no fear. Far removed from inverted dream-thinking, one reaches ultimate nirvāṇa. All Buddhas of the three periods of time, relying on prajñāpāramitā, attain the supreme, perfect enlightenment. Therefore know that prajñāpāramitā is the great divine mantra, the great luminous mantra, the unsurpassed mantra, the mantra equal to the unequaled, which can remove all suffering, true and not false. Therefore the mantra of prajñāpāramitā is spoken."

The mantra is then spoken:

*Gate gate pāragate pārasaṃgate bodhi svāhā*

"Thus, Śāriputra! All bodhisattva-mahāsattvas, in the practice of the profound prajñāpāramitā, should practice in this way." Having spoken thus,

at that time the World-Honored One arose from the Vast and Profound samādhi and praised the Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Avalokiteśvara: "Excellent, excellent! Good man! It is so, it is so, as you have spoken. The practice of the profound prajñāpāramitā should be practiced in this way. When it is practiced thus, all Tathāgatas rejoice."

At that time, when the World-Honored One had spoken these words, the venerable Śāriputra was filled with great joy, and the Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Avalokiteśvara was also greatly joyful. Then all the devas, humans, asuras, gandharvas, and others of that assembly, hearing what the Buddha taught, were all greatly joyful, and they accepted it with faith and practiced it.
