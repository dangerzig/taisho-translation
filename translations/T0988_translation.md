# 孔雀王呪經

## The Peacock King Dhāraṇī Sūtra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0988

Translated from the Chinese. Later Qin dynasty, translated by Kumārajīva (鳩摩羅什).

---

## The Peacock King Dhāraṇī Sūtra, in One Fascicle

The great divine nāga king of the east: for seven leagues, establish the vajra boundary.

The great divine nāga king of the south: for seven leagues, establish the vajra boundary.

The great divine nāga king of the west: for seven leagues, establish the vajra boundary.

The great divine nāga king of the north: for seven leagues, establish the vajra boundary.

The great divine nāga king of the center: for seven leagues, establish the vajra boundary.

Proclaim this three times.

The great divine nāga king of the east, the Blue Emperor, each commands eighty-four thousand ghosts to guard the eastern quarter.

The great divine nāga king of the south, the Red Emperor, each commands eighty-four thousand ghosts to guard the southern quarter.

The great divine nāga king of the west, the White Emperor, each commands eighty-four thousand ghosts to guard the western quarter.

The great divine nāga king of the north, the Black Emperor, each commands eighty-four thousand ghosts to guard the northern quarter.

The great divine nāga king of the center, the Yellow Emperor, each commands eighty-four thousand ghosts to guard the central quarter.

The Sandalwood Palace Army of the east: their heads are a hundred paces wide, their mouths open like valley-mountains, and in groups of ten and five together they swallow.

The Sandalwood Palace Army of the south: their heads are a hundred paces wide, their mouths open like valley-mountains, and in groups of ten and five together they swallow.

The Sandalwood Palace Army of the west: their heads are a hundred paces wide, their mouths open like valley-mountains, and in groups of ten and five together they swallow.

The Sandalwood Palace Army of the north: their heads are a hundred paces wide, their mouths open like valley-mountains, and in groups of ten and five together they swallow.

The Sandalwood Palace Army of the center: their heads are a hundred paces wide, their mouths open like valley-mountains, and in groups of ten and five together they swallow.

From the deep mountains of Bhakula in the east, Śālākha seizes all you hundred ghosts and places cangues upon your necks!

From the deep mountains of Bhakula in the south, Śālākha seizes all you hundred ghosts and places cangues upon your necks!

From the deep mountains of Bhakula in the west, Śālākha seizes all you hundred ghosts and places cangues upon your necks!

From the deep mountains of Bhakula in the north, Śālākha seizes all you hundred ghosts and places cangues upon your necks!

From the deep mountains of Bhakula in the center, Śālākha seizes all you hundred ghosts and places cangues upon your necks!

In the east, the great divine nāga king, the vajra secret-trace guardian, Samantabhadra Bodhisattva, Bhaiṣajyarāja, Bhaiṣajyasamudgata, and Mokṣadatta Bodhisattva: enter!

In the south, the great divine nāga king, the vajra secret-trace guardian, Samantabhadra Bodhisattva, Bhaiṣajyarāja, Bhaiṣajyasamudgata, and Mokṣadatta Bodhisattva: enter!

In the west, the great divine nāga king, the vajra secret-trace guardian, Samantabhadra Bodhisattva, Bhaiṣajyarāja, Bhaiṣajyasamudgata, and Mokṣadatta Bodhisattva: enter!

In the north, the great divine nāga king, the vajra secret-trace guardian, Samantabhadra Bodhisattva, Bhaiṣajyarāja, Bhaiṣajyasamudgata, and Mokṣadatta Bodhisattva: enter!

In the center, the great divine nāga king, the vajra secret-trace guardian, Samantabhadra Bodhisattva, Bhaiṣajyarāja, Bhaiṣajyasamudgata, and Mokṣadatta Bodhisattva: enter!

The great divine nāga king of the east, named Haṭuha, at the great assembly of the Buddha personally declared: "I shall protect this Mahāprajñāpāramitā divine dhāraṇī of yours."

The great divine nāga king of the south, named Hārūlekṣādi, at the great assembly of the Buddha personally declared: "I shall protect this Mahāprajñāpāramitā divine dhāraṇī of yours."

The great divine nāga king of the west, named Naṭuhuā, at the great assembly of the Buddha personally declared: "I shall protect this Mahāprajñāpāramitā divine dhāraṇī of yours."

The great divine nāga king of the north, named Hārilekṣādi, at the great assembly of the Buddha personally declared: "I shall protect this Mahāprajñāpāramitā divine dhāraṇī of yours."

Pure Cultivation Bodhisattva enters the body to seek out māras. Pure Treasury Bodhisattva splits the marrow to seek out māras. Fire Light Bodhisattva grasps fire to seek out māras. Moon Light Bodhisattva emits radiance to seek out māras. Earth Holding Bodhisattva digs the ground to seek out māras. Samantabhadra Bodhisattva welcomes the vital spirit. Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva follows the sound and goes to the rescue.

There are eight nāga kings: Nāga King Nanda, Nāga King Upananda, Nāga King Sāgara, Nāga King Vāsuki, Nāga King Takṣaka, Nāga King Anavatapta, Nāga King Manasvin, and Nāga King Utpala. Each, together with many hundreds of thousands in their retinue, comes and enters this chamber. May my dhāraṇī words be fulfilled as wished!

There are four kiṃnara kings: Kiṃnara King Dharma, Kiṃnara King Wonderful Dharma, Kiṃnara King Great Dharma, and Kiṃnara King Dharma-Holder. Each, together with many hundreds of thousands in their retinue, comes and enters this chamber. May my dhāraṇī words be fulfilled as wished!

There are four gandharva kings: Gandharva King Music, Gandharva King Wonderful Music, Gandharva King Beauty, and Gandharva King Beautiful Sound. Each, together with many hundreds of thousands in their retinue, comes and enters this chamber. May my dhāraṇī words be fulfilled as wished!

There are four asura kings: Asura King Bali, Asura King Kharaskandhā, Asura King Vemacitrin, and Asura King Rāhula. Each, together with many hundreds of thousands in their retinue, comes and enters this chamber. May my dhāraṇī words be fulfilled as wished!

There are four great garuḍa kings: Garuḍa King Great Power, Garuḍa King Great Fullness, Garuḍa King Great Body, and Garuḍa King As-You-Wish. Each, together with many hundreds of thousands in their retinue, comes and enters this chamber. May my dhāraṇī words be fulfilled as wished!

In the south, Dīpaṃkara Buddha. In the north, the Seven-Jeweled Hall. In the west, Amitāyus. In the east, Bhaiṣajyaguru Vaiḍūryaprabha. Above, eight bodhisattvas. Below, the Four Heavenly Kings. In the south, Maheśvara, the great sovereign heavenly king, together with his retinue, the yakṣa generals, spirit-kings, and others: listen! I now wish to recite these dhāraṇī passages. May my dhāraṇī words be fulfilled as wished!

I announce to all ghosts and spirits: from above and below, from east, west, south, north, the four intermediate directions, above and below, all come and assemble to follow my command!

Homage to the Great Golden Peacock King divine dhāraṇī! Homage to the Mahāprajñāpāramitā divine dhāraṇī! Homage to the Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva Dhāraṇī divine dhāraṇī!

Up to the devas of the Formless Realm; the Śuddhāvāsa Heaven, the Śubhakṛtsna Heaven, the Ābhāsvara Heaven, the Nirmāṇarati Heaven, the Paranirmitavaśavartin Heaven, the Tuṣita Heaven, the Yāma Heaven, the Trāyastriṃśa Heaven; Śakra Devānām Indra; the Heavenly King Dhṛtarāṣṭra, the Heavenly King Virūḍhaka, the Heavenly King Virūpākṣa, the Heavenly King Vaiśravaṇa; as well as the six kings everywhere; sun, moon, and the five planets; the twenty-eight lunar mansions; Hārītī the Mother of Demons; the five judges and their retinues; Pañcika, the great general of the spirit hosts; the twenty-eight divisions of Maheśvara; all the great nāga kings: protect this body!

The Great Brahmā King, the Thirty-Three Heavens, the World-Protecting Four Heavenly Kings, Brahmā the Lord of the Sahā World, Śikhin Mahā-Brahmā, Jyotiṣ-prabhā Mahā-Brahmā, the Vajra Secret-Trace Guardian, Maheśvara, the Great Golden Peacock King, the Kumbhāṇḍa King, the Great Eloquence Spirit-King, the Nārāyaṇa King, and King Ajātaśatru, son of Vaidehī; the Mountain Spirit-King, the Tree Spirit-King, the River Spirit-King, the Sea Spirit-King, the Earth Spirit-King, the Water Spirit-King, the Fire Spirit-King, the Wind Spirit-King; the yakṣa general and rākṣasa king; Mānibhadra, Pūrṇabhadra, Cakravāla-prabha, Dharmarāja-prabha, Daṇḍara, Agali, and the other eight great spirit-kings; Ṭaki and the other seven great spirit-kings; Khakhalūda and the other great spirit-kings; Ḍhara and the other six great spirit-kings; Śikṣakara and the other ten great spirit-kings; the ṛṣis, ghosts, and great conjurer dhāraṇī kings: all shall protect this body!

Then he spoke the dhāraṇī:

"Homage to the Buddha. Homage to the Dharma. Homage to the Saṅgha. Homage to the seven buddhas of the past and their right, perfect awakening. Homage to the pratyekabuddhas. Homage to all buddhas. Homage to all arhats. Homage to Maitreya and all bodhisattvas. Homage to all anāgāmins, homage to sakṛdāgāmins, homage to srotāpannas. Homage to those in the world who follow the right Dharma and walk the right path. Having paid reverence to that assembly, I wish to practice the Great Golden Peacock King Dhāraṇī Sūtra. May all wishes be fulfilled auspiciously!"

Announce to all ghosts and spirits and listen: whether dwelling in the sky, on the earth, or in the water: devas, nāgas, asuras, garuḍas, gandharvas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, yakṣas, rākṣasas, hungry ghosts, piśācas, bhūtas, kumbhāṇḍas, pūtanas, gandhas, unmaḍas, cāyas, apasmāras, uttarakas: listen to what I declare!

Ghosts that feed on various things: ghosts that consume breath, ghosts that consume sugar, ghosts that consume blood, ghosts that consume stomachs, ghosts that consume flesh, ghosts that consume fat, ghosts that consume life-force, ghosts that consume strength, ghosts that consume garlands, ghosts that consume flowers, ghosts that consume fruit, ghosts that consume grain, ghosts that consume scent, ghosts that consume ill will, ghosts that consume secret plotting, ghosts of harmful intent, and ghosts that love to take the lives of others: all ghosts and spirits, listen!

I wish to expound the Great Golden Peacock King Dhāraṇī Sūtra. I present incense, flowers, food, and drink for you all to receive. Whether you come with harmful minds, plotting minds, or injuring minds to consume incense and foul breath, or with compassionate minds, wholesome minds, and minds of faith in the Buddha, the Dharma, and the Bhikṣu Saṅgha: listen to what I declare!

The fourteen rākṣasī: first, named Darkness; second, named Maker-of-Darkness; third, named Kumbhāṇḍī; fourth, named White-Abode; fifth, named Flower-Eye; sixth, named Child-Taker; seventh, named Hair-Taker; eighth, named Maker-of-Yellow; ninth, named Drooping; tenth, named Extremely-Drooping; eleventh, named Watching-for-Opportunity; twelfth, named Yama's-Messenger; thirteenth, named Yama's-Rākṣasī; fourteenth, named Flesh-Eater. You all, receive my incense, flowers, food, and drink! Protect this person and their family. May my dhāraṇī words be fulfilled auspiciously!

Then the Buddha told Ānanda: "You should take the Tathāgata's Great Peacock King Dhāraṇī and use it to protect Bhikṣu Maṅgala. Bind the boundary and bind the dhāraṇī for him, so that poison cannot harm him, weapons cannot touch him, and all evils are completely removed and healed. Whether caused by devas, nāgas, marutas, garuḍas, gandharvas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, yakṣas, rākṣasas, hungry ghosts, piśācas, bhūtas, kumbhāṇḍas, pūtanas, black pūtanas, gandhas, unmāras, cāyas, apasmāras, uttarakas, kīṭas, karmaṇas, khakkhudas, kīlaṇas, viṭaḍas, jīmas, or vaiśākas; whether from improper eating, improper sitting, improper shadow, improper looking, improper lifting, improper transgressing, or improper touching; whether from fevers of one day, two days, three days, four days, five days, six days, or seven days, of half a month or one month, occurring once at a given time of day; whether from ghostly fever, wind disease, fire disease, water disease, cholera with burning heat; whether from half-body pain, qi-reversal chest-coughing pain, throat pain, head and ear pain, toothache, heart pain, side pain, back pain, abdominal pain, waist pain, hip pain, genital pain, stomach pain, liver pain, hand and foot pain, or pain in all joints: may all be completely removed and healed. Protect this body!"

Then he spoke the dhāraṇī:

"Safe by day, safe by night, safe always in between. Safe and secure day and night, protected by all buddhas."

The Four Heavenly Kings and all the great spirit-kings also use the Peacock King Dhāraṇī to protect this body. Then they spoke the dhāraṇī:

Samantabhadra, auspicious Samantabhadra, Accomplisher-of-All, Stainless, Pure-and-Wondrous, Candragupta, Sūryakānta, Ḍambi, Jaḍambi, Vīṃkāle: protect this person's life, granting a lifespan of a hundred years, to see a hundred autumns.

Then he spoke the dhāraṇī:

The Great Golden Peacock King Dhāraṇī Sūtra.

At that time the Four Heavenly Kings addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! We shall now speak this dhāraṇī, to be used for treating all the illnesses and misfortunes of sentient beings."

Then they spoke the dhāraṇī:

*Samī samā samī abaṭi viśuddhi baṭini sāle*

The Buddha then spoke the Āṭavaka Wilderness Spirit dhāraṇī sūtra, to remove the suffering, afflictions, and illnesses of sentient beings. Then the spirit spoke the dhāraṇī:

"World-Honored One! These dhāraṇī words are for the sake of the fourfold assembly, to bring them peace and ease and free them from all afflictions. Against the hosts of māras and evil ghosts, thieves, flood and fire, violent storms, rākṣasas and evil spirits, fever, cold disease, wind disease, and all manner of illness, decline of household fortune, misfortune in all directions, and sudden attacks by fierce beasts: urgently recite this dhāraṇī, and all will be liberated."

"I shall now speak the dhāraṇī again:

*Sāli śāli viliṃbhā svāhā. Depart swiftly! Depart swiftly! Swiftly return to your original abode!*

For the Peacock King Dhāraṇī altar: plaster the ground with cow dung. Scatter seven-colored flowers. Provide forty-nine streamers, four knives, four mirrors, one hundred arrows, one bow, seven jars filled with liquid, sixteen fathoms of black sheep-wool cord, twenty-five sets of thin cakes, seven oil lamps lit. Also: one vessel of yogurt, one vessel of parched-grain drink, one vessel of cooked rice, one vessel of thin cakes, one vessel of pomegranates, and one vessel of flowers.
