## Key Findings and Differences from Our Glossary

### 1. Areas of Strong Agreement with Our Conventions

Our glossary aligns well with scholarly consensus on:
- **emptiness** (shunyata) -- universal
- **impermanence** (anitya) -- universal
- **consciousness** (vijnana) -- universal
- **form** (rupa) -- universal
- **feeling** (vedana) -- universal
- **perception** (samjna) -- universal
- **Blessed One** (Bhagavat) -- matches 84000, Bodhi, Sujato, Harrison (differs from BDK)
- **afflictions** (klesha) -- good match with 84000 and S-H
- **craving** (trishna) -- universal
- **ignorance** (avidya) -- universal
- **mindfulness** (smriti) -- universal
- **compassion** (karuna) -- universal
- **equanimity** (upeksha) -- universal

### 2. Terms Where We Differ from Majority Consensus

| Our Rendering | Majority Rendering | Chinese | Sanskrit | Recommendation |
|---------------|-------------------|---------|----------|----------------|
| **formations** (samskara) | formations / volitional formations | 行 | samskara | **Keep**: aligns with Bodhi and majority |
| **dependent arising** | split: "dependent arising" vs "dependent origination" | 緣起 | pratityasamutpada | **Keep**: both are standard; "arising" is newer preference |
| **becoming** (bhava) | split: "becoming" vs "existence" | 有 | bhava | **Keep**: "becoming" is defensible and conveys the dynamic sense |
| **grasping** (upadana) | split: "grasping" vs "clinging" | 取 | upadana | **Keep**: both are standard |

### 4. Terms Where Analayo Differs Significantly

Bhikkhu Analayo, as the most prominent translator of Chinese Agama texts (Madhyama-agama T0026, Samyukta-agama T0099), makes several distinctive choices relevant to our project since we are also translating from Chinese:

| Bhagavat | Blessed One | Blessed One | 世尊 | Bhagavat |

### 5. BDK-Specific Conventions

Since BDK is the other major project translating the Chinese Taisho canon, their conventions are directly relevant:

- **World-Honored One** for Bhagavat (世尊): This is the most significant difference from our "Blessed One." The BDK rendering is a calque of the Chinese, while ours follows the Sanskrit. Both approaches are defensible. Our choice aligns with 84000, Bodhi, Sujato, and Harrison.
- BDK publications generally leave technical terms untranslated (bodhisattva, nirvana, samadhi, etc.), which aligns with our approach.

## Recommendations for Standard Renderings

Based on this research, the following recommendations are offered for our project's standard renderings:

### Keep Current Renderings (strong consensus support)

1. **Blessed One** for Bhagavat -- matches 84000, Bodhi, Sujato, Harrison
2. **afflictions** for klesha -- well-supported; close to 84000's "affliction"
3. **formations** for samskara -- matches Bodhi; widely understood
4. **dependent arising** for pratityasamutpada -- matches 84000 and newer scholarship
5. **emptiness** for shunyata -- universal
6. **suffering** for duhkha -- majority practice (though Analayo's objection is noted)
7. **Tathagata** transliterated -- matches 84000, Bodhi, BDK
8. **bodhisattva** transliterated -- universal
9. **nirvana** transliterated -- matches most authorities
10. **loving-kindness** for maitri -- matches 84000, Bodhi

### Consider Updating

1. **bodhi**: consider adding "awakening" as a parenthetical gloss, since the trend in scholarship is toward "awakening" rather than "enlightenment"
2. **asrava**: consider translating as "taint" (Bodhi) or "outflow" rather than leaving purely transliterated
3. **dana, kshanti, virya, shila**: consider providing English equivalents in parentheses for reader accessibility (generosity, patience, energy, ethical conduct)

### Adopt for New Terms

When these terms first appear in future translations, use:
1. **mind of awakening** for bodhicitta (84000 standard)
2. **skillful means** for upaya (near-universal)
3. **dharma body / reward body / emanation body** for trikaya (widely used)
4. **buddha-nature** for tathagatagarbha (most common, though "tathagata-essence" is the 84000 preference)
5. **dharma realm** for dharmadhatu (widely used)
6. **pure land** for buddhakshetra / jingtu (widely used in East Asian context)
7. **true suchness** for tathata (standard in Chinese Buddhist translation)
8. **merit** for punya (universal)
9. **wholesome roots** for kushala-mula (Bodhi standard)
10. **vow** for pranidhana (widely used)

## Source Details and Access

### Digital Dictionary of Buddhism (DDB)
- URL: http://www.buddhism-dict.net/ddb/
- Access: Guest access available; institutional subscriptions for full access
- 73,000+ entries covering East Asian Buddhist terminology
- The most authoritative online resource for Chinese Buddhist terms
- Incorporates and corrects Soothill & Hodous

### BDK English Tripitaka
- URL: https://www.bdkamerica.org/the-translation-project/
- Digital texts: https://bdk-seiten.com/
- 139 works in first series, translating from the Taisho canon
- Uses "World-Honored One" for Bhagavat (most distinctive convention)

### Soothill & Hodous
- Online: https://mahajana.net/texts/soothill-hodous.html
- DILA edition: https://glossaries.dila.edu.tw/glossaries/SHH?locale=en
- PDF: https://glossaries.dila.edu.tw/data/soothill-hodous.dila.pdf
- Historical reference (1937); some renderings now outdated
- Useful for obscure terms and variant transliterations

### Princeton Dictionary of Buddhism
- Print: Princeton University Press, 2014
- Online via Oxford Reference
- 5,000+ entries covering all Buddhist languages
- Entry headwords use Sanskrit forms; provides equivalents in Chinese, Tibetan, Japanese, Korean

### 84000 Glossary
- URL: https://84000.co/glossary-search
- Individual entries: https://84000.co/glossary/entity-XXXXX.html
- Trilingual (Tibetan-Sanskrit-English) with some Chinese
- Particularly strong for Mahayana terminology
- Editorial team: John Canti, Tom Tillemans, Gavin Kilty, Larry Mermelstein

### Fo Guang Shan
- Glossary: https://www.fgsitc.org/glossary/
- Dictionary: https://foguangdictionary.org/
- Glossary of Humanistic Buddhism: 4,000+ terms
- Chinese-English bilingual; good for modern Chinese Buddhist usage

### Bhikkhu Bodhi (Pali Nikayas)
- Standard reference translations: Middle Length Discourses, Connected Discourses, Numerical Discourses
- Key distinctive renderings: "formations" (sankhara), "taints" (asava), "defilements" (kilesa)

### Bhikkhu Sujato (SuttaCentral)
- URL: https://suttacentral.net
- Most distinctive renderings: "immersion" (samadhi), "mendicant" (bhikkhu), "extinguishment" (nibbana), "choice" (sankhara)
- Prioritizes natural English over traditional Buddhist English

### Bhikkhu Analayo (Agama translations)
- Madhyama-agama (T26): BDK English Tripitaka, with Bingenheimer and Bucknell
- Samyukta-agama (T99): ongoing translation
- Key distinctive renderings: "benevolence" (metta), "influx" (asava), "awakening" (bodhi), dukkha left untranslated

### Paul Harrison (Prajnaparamita)
- Diamond Sutra and other Prajnaparamita translations
- Uses "Blessed One" for Bhagavat
- Translates Tathagata as "Realized One" in some contexts
- Prioritizes natural English; avoids "Buddhist Hybrid English"
