# 六門陀羅尼經
## Sūtra of the Six-Gate Dhāraṇī

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1360

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Xuanzang (玄奘)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Bhagavān was dwelling in the Heaven of Pure Abodes, abiding in space, in a place of practice adorned with the seven precious substances in all their splendor, together with a boundless assembly of bodhisattvas. At that time the World-Honored One addressed all the bodhisattvas: "Good men! If you wish to benefit and bring happiness to sentient beings, you should receive this dharma of the Six-Gate Dhāraṇī, which is as follows:

"The sufferings I have undergone while transmigrating through saṃsāra; may sentient beings not experience those same sufferings. Whatever prosperity and worldly happiness I have enjoyed; may all sentient beings likewise enjoy such happiness. If I have not first confessed the evil I have committed, I will never speak in praise of the unsurpassed Dharma. If I have not first become aware of all the māra-deeds within me, I will never raise my mind toward the unsurpassed Dharma. All the vast wholesome roots, both worldly and supramundane, encompassed by the pāramitās that I possess; may all sentient beings swiftly realize the fruit of unsurpassed wisdom. As I attain liberation, may all sentient beings likewise attain liberation, and may they not become attached to either saṃsāra or nirvāṇa."

The dhāraṇī:

*Chàn mí chàn mí, chǎn dì chǎn dì, bá dié lì bá dié lì, sū bá dié lì sū bá dié lì, dì shì dì shì, zhàn dié lì zhàn dié lì, zhàn dié luó fá dǐ, dī shū fá dǐ, dá mó fá dǐ, sà pó jié lì, shuò pí shū dá nǐ, sà pó ā là tuō, suō dá nǐ, mò nuò sēng shū dá nǐ, suō hē.*

"If there are faithful men of pure faith and women of pure faith who are able to recite this Six-Gate Dhāraṇī at the six periods of day and night, all the karmic hindrances of such persons will be entirely extinguished, and they will swiftly awaken to anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi."

When the Bhagavān had finished expounding this sūtra, all the bodhisattva-mahāsattvas and the assembly of devas, having heard what the Buddha had proclaimed, all rejoiced greatly, accepted it with faith, and undertook to practice it.
