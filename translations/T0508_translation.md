# 阿闍世王問五逆經
## Sūtra of King Ajātaśatru's Question about the Five Heinous Acts

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0508

Translated from the Chinese. Western Jin dynasty, translated by Faju (法炬)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Bhagavān was on Vulture Peak Mountain in the city of Rājagṛha, together with an assembly of five hundred great bhikṣus.

Devadatta went to the dwelling of King Ajātaśatru. When he arrived, he took his seat. King Ajātaśatru then rose from his seat, bowed his head to Devadatta's feet, and returned to his seat.

King Ajātaśatru said to Devadatta: "I have heard that the Venerable Devadatta reports that the śramaṇa Gautama constantly makes this statement: 'There are five heinous sins. If any clan-son or clan-daughter commits these five unpardonable sins, they will certainly enter hell without doubt. What are the five? Killing one's father, killing one's mother, harming an arhat, causing a schism in the saṅgha, and harboring malicious intent toward the Tathāgata. These are the five unpardonable sins. If any man or woman commits these acts, they will certainly enter hell without doubt.' Now, Devadatta, I have personally killed my father the king. Shall I too enter hell?"

Devadatta then told King Ajātaśatru: "Great King! Do not be fearful. What calamity is there? What fault is there? Who suffers calamity and receives retribution? Who creates calamity and must receive its fruit? Moreover, the great king has not committed an evil transgression. Whoever commits evil will receive their own retribution."

At that time, many bhikṣus, at the proper hour, put on their robes, took up their bowls, and entered the city of Rājagṛha to beg for alms. These many bhikṣus, entering the city to beg, heard King Ajātaśatru say to Devadatta: "Venerable Devadatta! I have heard the śramaṇa Gautama make this statement: 'There are five unpardonable sins. If any man or woman commits these five acts, they will certainly enter hell without doubt.' Without just cause, I personally killed my father the king. Shall I enter hell?" And they heard Devadatta reply: "Do not fear, great king! Who creates calamity? From where does calamity arise? Who commits evil and afterward receives retribution? The king has not created calamity. Whoever creates calamity will receive their own retribution."

The many bhikṣus, having begged their food in the city of Rājagṛha, after eating gathered their robes and bowls, placed their sitting cloths over their shoulders, and went to the World-Honored One. They bowed their heads to his feet, sat to one side, and reported the discussion between King Ajātaśatru and Devadatta, relating it in full to the World-Honored One.

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke this verse:

> The fool, knowing what he has done,
> Says that calamity brings no retribution.
> Now I observe what is to come:
> The place of retribution is determined.

Then the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus: "Though King Ajātaśatru of Magadha has killed his father the king, before long he will come to my dwelling and develop equal faith in me. After his life ends, he will fall into hell like the bouncing of a ball."

At that time, a bhikṣu addressed the World-Honored One: "When his life ends in that hell, where will he be reborn?" The World-Honored One replied: "When his life ends in that hell, he will be reborn in the realm of the Four Heavenly Kings."

The bhikṣu asked: "When his life ends there, where will he be reborn?" The World-Honored One replied: "Bhikṣu! When his life ends there, he will be reborn in the Heaven of the Thirty-Three."

The bhikṣu asked: "When his life ends in the Heaven of the Thirty-Three, where will he be reborn?" The World-Honored One replied: "Bhikṣu! When his life ends in the Heaven of the Thirty-Three, he will be reborn in the Yāma Heaven."

The bhikṣu asked: "World-Honored One! When his life ends there, where will he be reborn?" The World-Honored One replied: "When his life ends in the Yāma Heaven, he will be reborn in the Tuṣita Heaven."

The bhikṣu asked: "World-Honored One! When his life ends in the Tuṣita Heaven, where will he be reborn?" The World-Honored One replied: "When his life ends in the Tuṣita Heaven, he will be reborn in the Nirmāṇarati Heaven."

The bhikṣu asked: "When his life ends in the Nirmāṇarati Heaven, where will he be reborn?" The World-Honored One replied: "Bhikṣu! When his life ends in the Nirmāṇarati Heaven, he will be reborn in the Paranirmitavaśavartin Heaven."

The bhikṣu asked: "World-Honored One! When his life ends in the Paranirmitavaśavartin Heaven, where will he be reborn?" The World-Honored One replied: "Bhikṣu! When his life ends in the Paranirmitavaśavartin Heaven, he will be reborn in the Nirmāṇarati Heaven, then in the Tuṣita Heaven, the Yāma Heaven, the Heaven of the Thirty-Three, and the Heaven of the Four Heavenly Kings. He will then be reborn among human beings."

The bhikṣu asked: "World-Honored One! When his life ends from there, where will he be reborn?" The World-Honored One replied: "Bhikṣu! The King of Magadha, Ajātaśatru, for twenty kalpas will not fall into the three evil destinies. He will transmigrate among devas and humans. At last, in his final body, he will shave his head and beard, don the three Dharma robes, and with firm faith go forth from the household life to study the path. He will become a pratyekabuddha named Stainless."

The bhikṣu said: "How remarkable! How extraordinary, World-Honored One! Though he committed such grievous sin, he will receive such happiness and become a pratyekabuddha named Stainless."

The World-Honored One replied: "King Ajātaśatru of Magadha will give rise to intention and achieve fulfillment, and all goodness will come to him. Bhikṣu! If intention is sufficiently developed, one can be rescued from hell. If one's intention is not sufficiently developed but one's conditions are fulfilled, then even though one has not yet been born in hell, it is still possible to employ skillful means to avoid reaching hell."

The bhikṣu asked: "If a person has both things fulfilled, where will they be reborn?" The World-Honored One replied: "If both are fulfilled, they will be reborn in two places. What are the two? Among devas and among humans."

The bhikṣu asked: "If intention is fulfilled but conditions are not fulfilled, what is the difference between these two?"

The World-Honored One replied: "Bhikṣu! Intention fulfilled but conditions not fulfilled: this is dull faculties. Intention not fulfilled but conditions fulfilled: bhikṣu, this is sharp faculties."

The bhikṣu asked: "What is the difference between dull faculties and sharp faculties?"

The World-Honored One replied: "Dull faculties, bhikṣu, means that one does not advance in one's endeavors. Sharp faculties, bhikṣu, means being wise, intelligent, and discerning."

The bhikṣu asked: "What is the difference between these two? What actions do they return to?"

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke this verse:

> Wisdom is the highest thing in the world;
> It leads one to the place of security.
> Those who can understand the equality of karma
> Sever the cycle of birth and death.

"This, bhikṣu, is the difference."

At that time, the bhikṣu, having heard what the Buddha taught, joyfully practiced it. He rose from his seat, bowed his head to the Buddha's feet, circumambulated three times, and withdrew.

That very day, at the proper hour, the bhikṣu put on his robes, took up his bowl, and entered the city of Rājagṛha to beg for alms, going to the outer gate of the Magadha king's palace.

King Ajātaśatru saw the bhikṣu approaching from afar. Seeing him, he commanded the gatekeeper: "Gatekeeper! I previously ordered that no Śākya bhikṣu be allowed in, except the Venerable Devadatta."

The gatekeeper seized the bhikṣu's hand and drove him outside the gate. The bhikṣu raised his right hand and said to the King of Magadha: "I am the great king's good spiritual friend, a place of peace and security, free from all troubles."

The king replied: "Bhikṣu! On what grounds do you say, 'I am the great king's good friend, a place of security'?"

The bhikṣu then told King Ajātaśatru: "The World-Honored One has spoken about the king, saying thus: 'Though the King of Magadha has killed his father the king, when his evil life ends, he will fall into hell like the bouncing of a ball. When his life ends there, he will be reborn in the realm of the Four Heavenly Kings; then in the Heaven of the Thirty-Three; then in the Yāma Heaven, the Tuṣita Heaven, the Nirmāṇarati Heaven, and the Paranirmitavaśavartin Heaven. When his life ends there, he will be reborn again in the Nirmāṇarati Heaven, the Tuṣita Heaven, the Yāma Heaven, the Heaven of the Thirty-Three, and the realm of the Four Heavenly Kings. He will then be reborn here in the human realm. Thus, for twenty kalpas the great king will not fall into the three evil destinies, transmigrating among humans. At last, in his final human body, he will shave his head and beard, don the three Dharma robes, and with firm faith go forth from the household life to study the path. He will become a pratyekabuddha named Stainless.' For this reason, great king, you will attain the baseless faith." When the bhikṣu had spoken these words, he withdrew and departed.

When King Ajātaśatru heard what the bhikṣu said, he was neither pleased nor angry, and he did not accept what was spoken. He then told the prince Jīvaka: "Jīvaka! A śramaṇa came to my dwelling and said these words, that the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Samyaksaṃbuddha has given me a prediction: though I killed my father the king and committed this heinous transgression, after death I will fall into hell like the bouncing of a ball, then be reborn in the Heaven of the Four Heavenly Kings, the Heaven of the Thirty-Three, the Yāma Heaven, the Tuṣita Heaven, the Nirmāṇarati Heaven, the Paranirmitavaśavartin Heaven; then again be reborn in the Nirmāṇarati Heaven, the Tuṣita Heaven, the Yāma Heaven, the Heaven of the Thirty-Three, the realm of the Four Heavenly Kings; then be reborn in the human world; and in my final human body, I will shave my head and beard, don the three Dharma robes, with firm faith go forth to study the path, and become a pratyekabuddha named Stainless. Go, Jīvaka, to the dwelling of the śramaṇa Gautama and verify whether these words are true or not." He replied: "Yes, great king!"

Then the prince Jīvaka, having received the King of Magadha's order, left the city of Rājagṛha and went to Vulture Peak Mountain, to the World-Honored One's dwelling. Arriving, he bowed his head to the Buddha's feet and sat to one side. The prince Jīvaka then related in full to the Tathāgata everything the King of Magadha had said. The World-Honored One replied: "It is so, Jīvaka! The words of the World-Honored Buddha are without duality; what is spoken accords with the matter. Why? Jīvaka, King Ajātaśatru will attain the baseless faith. Jīvaka! For all men and women, this destination is the same without exception."

Then the prince Jīvaka, having received this teaching from the Tathāgata, rose from his seat, bowed his head to the Buddha's feet, and withdrew. He went to the dwelling of the King of Magadha. Arriving, he told King Ajātaśatru: "The Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Samyaksaṃbuddha has indeed spoken these words. Why? Because for all those who attain the baseless faith, there is no exception. May the king go to the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Samyaksaṃbuddha."

The king replied: "Jīvaka! I have heard that the śramaṇa Gautama possesses such spells that he can subdue the people, so that none among the heterodox teachers fails to accept his teaching. Therefore I am not able to go and see the śramaṇa Gautama. Wait, Jīvaka! I shall observe and investigate whether the śramaṇa Gautama truly possesses omniscience or not. If he does possess omniscience, then I will go to see the śramaṇa Gautama."

Then the prince Jīvaka, having heard these words from the King of Magadha, left the city of Rājagṛha and went to Vulture Peak Mountain, to the World-Honored One's dwelling. Arriving, he bowed his head to the Buddha's feet, sat to one side, and reported this matter to the World-Honored One.

The World-Honored One then said: "Jīvaka! Before long, the King of Magadha will come to my dwelling and attain the baseless faith. Even if I enter nirvāṇa before that day, he will afterward make offerings to my relics." The prince Jīvaka was overjoyed and unable to contain himself. Then the World-Honored One taught the prince Jīvaka the subtle and wondrous Dharma, causing him to be filled with joy.

Then the prince Jīvaka, having heard this profound Dharma from the Tathāgata, rose from his seat, bowed his head to the Buddha's feet, circumambulated three times, and withdrew. The prince Jīvaka, having heard what the Buddha taught, joyfully practiced it.
