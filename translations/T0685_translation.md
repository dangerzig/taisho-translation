# 佛說盂蘭盆經
## Ullambana Sūtra, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0685

Translated from the Chinese. Western Jin dynasty, translated by Dharmarakṣa (竺法護)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was in the land of Śrāvastī, at the Jeta Grove of Anāthapiṇḍada. Mahāmaudgalyāyana had just attained the six supernatural powers and wished to save his father and mother to repay the kindness of their nourishing and nursing. With the divine eye he surveyed the world and saw that his deceased mother had been born among the hungry ghosts. She had nothing to eat or drink, and her skin clung to her bones. Maudgalyāyana was filled with grief. He filled his bowl with rice and went to offer it to his mother. When his mother received the bowl of rice, she covered it with her left hand and with her right hand scooped up the rice. But before the food entered her mouth, it turned to burning coals, and she could not eat. Maudgalyāyana cried out in anguish, weeping and wailing, and rushed back to tell the Buddha, relating everything in full.

The Buddha said: "The root of your mother's transgressions is deeply bound. It is not something you alone have the power to change. Although your filial devotion moves heaven and earth, neither the heavenly spirits, the earth spirits, Māra and the non-Buddhist masters, nor the spirits of the Four Heavenly Kings can do anything about it. Only the awesome spiritual power of the assembled Saṃgha of the ten directions can bring about her liberation. I shall now teach you a method of rescue, so that all those in difficulty may be freed from sorrow and suffering, and their karmic obstructions may be eliminated."

The Buddha said to Maudgalyāyana: "On the fifteenth day of the seventh month, the day of the Saṃgha's self-examination, one should prepare food of a hundred flavors and the five kinds of fruit, vessels for washing, fragrant oil and lamps, bedding and sitting-cloths, and the finest things in the world, and place them all in basins to make offerings to the great and virtuous Saṃgha of the ten directions, for the sake of one's parents of seven generations and one's present parents who are in difficulty. On that day, all the noble assembly, whether practicing meditation in the mountains, or having attained the four fruits of the path, or practicing walking meditation beneath trees, or having attained the six supernatural powers and teaching śrāvakas and pratyekabuddhas, or being great bodhisattvas of the ten grounds who provisionally appear as bhikṣus among the great assembly: all with one mind receive the pravāraṇā meal. Those who possess the pure precepts and the way of the noble assembly, whose virtue is vast and deep: if anyone makes offerings to this self-examining Saṃgha, their present parents and parents of seven generations and six kinds of relatives will be freed from the suffering of the three evil paths and will be liberated at once. Food and clothing will come to them naturally. If one's parents are still alive, they will enjoy a hundred years of happiness. If one's parents of seven generations have already passed away, they will be born in heaven, freely transforming into heavenly birth, entering the light of heavenly flowers, and receiving immeasurable happiness." Then the Buddha instructed the Saṃgha of the ten directions to first recite prayers and blessings for the donor's parents of seven generations and practice meditative concentration, and then to receive the food. When first receiving the basin, it should be placed before the Buddha's stūpa. When the Saṃgha has completed the prayers, they may then receive the food.

At that time, the bhikṣu Maudgalyāyana and the great assembly of bodhisattva mahāsattvas were all filled with great joy. Maudgalyāyana's sorrowful weeping and wailing ceased at once. On that very day, his mother was freed from a kalpa's suffering as a hungry ghost.

Then Maudgalyāyana again said to the Buddha: "My parents have received the merit and power of the Three Treasures and the awesome spiritual power of the Saṃgha. If in the future all disciples of the Buddha who practice filial devotion should also offer this ullambana to rescue and save their present parents and parents of seven generations, would this be permissible?"

The Buddha said: "An excellent and timely question! This is exactly what I wished to say, and now you ask. Good man! If any bhikṣu, bhikṣuṇī, king, crown prince, prince, great minister, prime minister, the three high officials, the hundred officials, or any of the myriad common people practice filial devotion and compassion, they should all, for the sake of their living parents and parents of seven generations, on the fifteenth day of the seventh month, the day of the Buddha's rejoicing and the day of the Saṃgha's self-examination, place food and drink of a hundred flavors in the ullambana basin and offer it to the self-examining Saṃgha of the ten directions. They should pray that their present parents may live a hundred years free from illness and free from all suffering and vexation, and that their parents of seven generations may be freed from the suffering of the hungry ghosts and be born among humans and devas, with happiness without limit."

The Buddha said to all good men and good women: "All disciples of the Buddha who practice filial devotion should, in every moment of thought, constantly remember their parents. They should make offerings to their parents of seven generations. Every year on the fifteenth day of the seventh month, with filial devotion and compassionate recollection of the parents who gave them birth and their parents of seven generations, they should prepare the ullambana offering and present it to the Buddha and the Saṃgha, in order to repay the kindness of their parents' nurturing and loving care. All disciples of the Buddha should reverently uphold this dharma."

At that time, the bhikṣu Maudgalyāyana and the fourfold assembly of disciples, having heard what the Buddha said, joyfully practiced it.
