# 焰羅王供行法次第
## Sequential Procedure for Offerings to King Yama

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1290

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, compiled by Amoghavajra (阿謨伽).

---

If a practitioner wishes to perform this method, they should first know the five transformations of this king, along with the origins of his great palace. The five transformations are: first, Yama Dharma-King, which is his original title; second, the King of Death; third, Saṃghāla-King of the Yellow Springs Realm; fourth, the Wrathful King Who Judges Sins; fifth, Daṇḍa, the Minor Wrathful King. His palace is beneath the earth to the north of the Iron-Ring Mountains; this is the Palace of the Dark Path. He is surrounded by a retinue of 50,000. In the courtyard of the palace stands the Daṇḍa Pillar. Upon its head is a minor wrathful face. The king constantly gazes upon this face to know the gravity of sins and the good and evil of the human world. When someone in the human world has committed a grave sin, fire-light blazes forth from its mouth, and within the light a black cord surges out as a warning. Seeing the wooden tally, the name is recorded. When someone has done good, a white lotus unfurls from the mouth, and its fragrance pervades. The Great Mountain Lord and the Five-Path General constantly obey the king's instructions and adjudicate good and evil.

Whenever performing this method, prepare the offerings. For emperors, princes, all officials, ministers, and common people, the offerings should suit the person: sesame oil, five grains, paper money, silk, incense, and medicines are used. Mix ghee, honey, and anasona incense with medicines into porridge and cook it. Seek an open and quiet outdoor place to perform the offerings, away from the sight of those who do not understand. Use willow branches five feet long to mark the positions. This method should be performed for epidemics, qi-illnesses, and all ailments and afflictions. It can also be used when one's allotted lifespan has been exhausted and one's name has been entered in the register of death, to petition the king to strike the name from the death register and enter it in the register of life. When arriving at a household afflicted by plague, recite the mantra of the Great Mountain Lord abundantly.

The practitioner takes their seat and applies fragrant paste to their hands and arms. Recite the name of Śākyamuni Tathāgata seven times. Sprinkle the ground with fragrant water. The mantra for empowering fragrant water:

*Oṃ amṛta-hana hana hūṃ svāhā.*

The officiating teacher brings forth a single thought: transcending the distinction between self and other is called "unobstructed"; when the mind is free from thoughts of enmity or kinship, it is called "equanimity." To practice this method is the bodhisattva's practice of vast aspiration. To exercise such compassion is the compassion of the Tathāgata, the king of all merit. Surpassing the three kinds of giving, this is because the power of empowerment by the inexhaustible, majestic, sovereign, luminous, supremely wonderful true-word, as taught by the Buddha, can transform one portion of food into immeasurable food, and one measure of wealth into inexhaustible wealth. Each portion of food and wealth becomes equal to the Dharma-realm in extent, universally offered to all sentient beings, who are all satisfied with amṛta Dharma-food. Filled with Dharma-food, their sins are extinguished and their merit is born; they leave behind evil-destiny bodies and receive human and heavenly reward. This enables the patron to increase their lifespan, eliminate karmic obstructions, and immediately fulfill the pāramitā of giving. In the present they receive excellent fruition, and in the future they realize bodhi. The wondrous power is inconceivable; trust in it without doubt. Today the patron reverently makes this unobstructed, vast offering for the sake of the emperor, ministers, and all beings of the Dharma-realm. Beginning with the buddhas, bodhisattvas, and vajra devas, down to all classes of ghosts, the spirits of the dark path, hungry ghosts, and the spirits of departed monks and nuns, record their names on tablets, establish each upon the altar, place offerings before each tablet, and recite the Amṛta-King mantra to empower the offerings. Visualize within the palm of the right hand the syllable [vaṃ]. From this syllable flows amṛta water, filling all the offerings.

Mantra for sprinkling purifying water:

*Oṃ vajra-śani hūṃ phaṭ.*

Sprinkle the above fragrant water with a bamboo branch. If performing this method outdoors, do not face toward a peach or willow tree.

Next, invite the buddhas and bodhisattvas:

With single-minded devotion, I invite our original teacher, Śākyamuni Buddha.

With single-minded devotion, I invite the Vast Majestic Sovereign Radiant Deva of the World.

With single-minded devotion, I invite Amitābha Buddha of the Western Land of Utmost Bliss.

With single-minded devotion, I invite the future-born Maitreya, the Honored Buddha.

With single-minded devotion, I invite all buddhas of the entirety of empty space.

With single-minded devotion, I invite the Great Holy Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva Mahāsattva.

With single-minded devotion, I invite the Great Holy Samantabhadra Bodhisattva Mahāsattva.

With single-minded devotion, I invite the Great Compassionate Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva Mahāsattva.

With single-minded devotion, I invite Mahāsthāmaprāpta Bodhisattva Mahāsattva.

With single-minded devotion, I invite Bhaiṣajyarāja and Bhaiṣajyasamudgata Bodhisattva Mahāsattva.

With single-minded devotion, I invite Sarvanīvaraṇaviṣkambhin Bodhisattva Mahāsattva.

With single-minded devotion, I invite Kṣitigarbha Bodhisattva Mahāsattva.

With single-minded devotion, I invite Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva Mahāsattva.

With single-minded devotion, I invite Vajragarbha Bodhisattva Mahāsattva.

With single-minded devotion, I invite all bodhisattva mahāsattvas of the ten directions, throughout empty space.

With single-minded devotion, I invite all the worthy and holy ones of the ten directions, throughout empty space, of the Śrāvaka and Pratyekabuddha vehicles.

With single-minded devotion, I invite all Dharma-protecting vajra secret-trace benevolent spirit hosts.

I humbly pray that our original teacher Śākyamuni Tathāgata, and all buddhas, all great bodhisattva mahāsattvas, and all worthy and holy ones of the ten directions, throughout empty space and pervading the Dharma-realm, today at this time, not forsaking their great vow, for the sake of sentient beings, accept our invitation, descend to this ritual site, and receive the offerings presented by today's patron. We now invoke them with the summoning mantra. I humbly pray that the Three Jewels have compassion and accept, protect, and eliminate calamity and difficulty. If a person's allotted lifespan is exhausted, though their name has been entered in the death register, may the king extend his compassion and by skillful means strike the name from the death register, and in accordance with his great vow, enter it in the register of life, granting a lifespan of one hundred years, ever beholding one hundred autumns.

The mantra for summoning all buddhas and bodhisattvas:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ asarva-trā-pratihata-tathāgataṃ-kuśa-bodhi-caryā-paripūraka svāhā.*

The mudrā: both hands in the wrathful bond, both wind-index fingers bent like hooks. After the invitation, present the offerings: first Śākyamuni, then Samantabhadra and Mañjuśrī. Next invite Brahmā Deva, Śakra, and the Four Deva-Kings. Each of the four deva-king seats has one attendant ghost: the gandharva king of the east, the kumbhāṇḍa king of the south, the nāgas of the west, and the twenty-eight great yakṣas of the north. After the offerings, next the mudrās and mantras of Brahmā Deva, Śakra, and the Four Kings.

The common heart-mantra mudrā of the Four Kings:

The left hand with thumb, middle finger, and little finger all standing upward. Bend the index and ring fingers into a fist against the palm. The palm faces forward, the elbow bent upward. The mantra:

*Oṃ jaṃbhara-jalendrāya svāhā.*

The mudrā of Brahmā Deva:

The left hand forms a fist against the hip. The right hand has five fingers touching and standing slightly bent, raised above the shoulder. Visualize a lotus within the palm. The mantra:

*Oṃ brahmaṇe svāhā.*

The mudrā of Śakra Deva:

The right hand as before, placed at the hip. The left hand has five fingers extended straight and touching. Bend the earth-pinky and water-ring fingers at the middle joint. Place the wind-index behind the fire-middle. Bend the space-thumb at the middle joint. The mantra:

*Oṃ indrāya svāhā.*

The mudrā and mantra for summoning Yama Daṇḍa:

With both hands, cross the middle fingers, ring fingers, and little fingers. Both index fingers stand erect with tips touching. Both thumbs are placed alongside the index fingers. Open the palms as if scooping water. The thumbs move back and forth. The mantra:

*Tadyathā oṃ yama-daṇḍāya śipaṃ-karīṇī vajrī-duti hiṃ hiṃ svāhā.*

The dharma-body mudrā of King Yama:

Bend the three fingers of the left hand, extending the index finger about three-tenths. Press the thumb alongside the lower joint of the index finger. The index finger moves back and forth. The mantra:

*Oṃ yama-rāja ugra-vihīya ākarccha svāhā.*

If one uses this mudrā-mantra to construct a great altar and make offerings to all buddhas and devas, form this mudrā and recite the mantra seven times. King Yama will then come to receive the offerings with great joy.

Next, invite the Five-Path General. In vajra-añjali, call the names: "Five-Path General, Left Life-Officer, Right Life-Officer."

Next invite the Heavenly Bureau Lord, all officials of the Heavenly Bureau, bureau emissaries, and all their divisions to descend to this site and receive my offerings. Invoke the Underworld Spirit-Lord, the Equanimity Great King, all officials of the Underworld Bureau, bureau emissaries, and all their divisions to descend to this site and receive my offerings. Invoke the thirty-six wardens of the hells, the horse-headed rākṣasas, ox-headed rākṣasas, and all their retinues to descend to this site and receive my offerings. Invoke the Land-Master Vidyā-King, the spirits of mountains, rivers, peaks, and waterways, city-wall and community shrine spirits, and all other spirit hosts with their retinues, asking them to come to this site and receive my offerings.

Invoke all departed monks and nuns not yet liberated, the patron's ancestors of seven generations and from ages past, all their spirits, and all karmic creditors from beginningless time, to descend to this site and receive my offerings, and to take refuge in the Three Jewels.

Invoke the Great-Power Burning-Face King of the Wilderness, and the immeasurable, incalculable, countless hungry ghosts as numerous as the sands of the Ganges, each with their retinues, to descend to this site, receive my offerings, and take refuge in the Three Jewels.

Next, the mantra for summoning the hungry ghosts:

*Oṃ bhū-prī-ka-tri-tri-tathāgatāya.*

Next, the mudrā and mantra for opening the throat:

*Oṃ bhū-pati-rī-ka-tā-ri-tathāgatāya.*

After the invitation, form the hook, lasso, chain, and bell mudrā.

Next, the mudrā for making the offerings radiant. The mantra:

*Oṃ jvalāya maṃdurī svāhā.*

Next, sprinkle purified fragrant water with a bamboo leaf.

Next, recite the amṛta mantra. Extend the right hand above the right shoulder, fingers together with the middle finger separated. Recite three times seven repetitions.

*Namo surūpāya tathāgatāya tadyathā oṃ suru suru prasuru prasuru svāhā.*

Next, recite the heart of Vairocana and contemplate, transforming it into a sea of milk. Extend the right hand with five fingers facing down over the food vessel. Visualize the syllable vaṃ within the palm.

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ vaṃ.*

Next, recite this mantra to make the offerings subtle and delicious.

Next, invite the Great Mountain Lord, who is the king's attendant. Behind the mountain there is a Valiant Ghost-King who in an instant traverses the world, distributing the wooden tallies of illness. In vajra-añjali, recite the mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ citragupta-ya svāhā.*

Next, invite and make offerings to all the spirits of the dark path.

Next, wholeheartedly chant the names of the Five Tathāgatas:

*Namo bhagavate prabhūta-ratnāya tathāgatāya.*

Homage to Ratnasaṃbhava Tathāgata, who removes the karma of miserliness, and whose merit is perfected.

*Namo bhagavate surūpāya tathāgatāya.*

Homage to Surūpa Tathāgata, who destroys ugly forms, and whose marks are perfected.

*Namo bhagavate amṛta-rājāya tathāgatāya.*

Homage to Amṛta-Rāja Tathāgata, who bathes the Dharma-body's mind and bestows bliss.

*Namo bhagavate vipula-gatrāya tathāgatāya.*

Homage to Vipula-Gātra Tathāgata, who widens the throat and bestows wondrous flavor.

*Namo bhagavate abhayaṃ-karāya tathāgatāya.*

Homage to Abhayaṃkara Tathāgata, who removes all fear and frees from the hungry-ghost destiny.

Next, in vajra-añjali, reverently invite Kṣitigarbha Bodhisattva, praying that through compassion all spirits of the dark path may be guided in proper order without any being lost.

Next, in vajra-añjali, recite: "We, so-and-so, reverently present these fragrant and clean food and drink, money, silk, and other offerings, first to the buddhas of the ten directions, to the innumerable spirits and officials of the dark path throughout a hundred thousand koṭi myriad nayuta unspeakably many lands as numerous as atoms, the spirits of departed monks and nuns, the five hundred host of hungry ghosts, the host of demi-brahman hungry ghosts; may each receive food equal to sixteen pecks of the measure of Magadha. May this food all transform into the joy of Dharma, the bliss of dhyāna, the purity of amṛta ghee, a pure sea of milk, pervading the Dharma-realm, vast and immeasurable, satisfying all without exception, so that all may realize liberation and bodhi, and take refuge in the eternally abiding Three Jewels."

Next, in vajra-añjali, chant the hymn:

*Ayaṃ tunīvacā-gasura-kiṃnarā-nakṣaka-rāla-dāyu-pravara-dharma-gīrta-dhigāla-vidharma-ca-praśama-cokya-nīmita-bhūta-nīmita-bhāra-kāśaya-tanniha-śramaṇāya dhāṃ.*

Next, chant the confession verse: "The offerings prepared today were largely not in accord with the Dharma, laughably crude, and have troubled all the officials of the dark path and the ḍākinī hosts. I humbly pray that you will grant your permission, that you will have compassion, and that you will be pleased to bestow your blessings."

Next, if one wishes to eliminate epidemics, qi-illnesses, or febrile diseases, make separate offerings to the Great Mountain Lord. If one wishes to gain liberation from curses cast by enemies, make separate offerings to Brahmā Deva and the Four Deva-Kings, and liberation will follow. If one wishes to obtain merit, make separate offerings to the twenty-eight great yakṣas. If one wishes to rescue a life whose allotted span has been exhausted, make separate offerings to King Yama and the Five-Path General, and the name will be struck from the death register and entered in the register of life. All of these may be performed according to the practitioner's intention, but money and silk need not be offered. The ācārya should also summon and invite the local nāga-spirits and all other spirits and make offerings to them; all is according to one's wishes.

Always wear fresh garments. In the center of the altar, dig a small hole and place fresh clothing inside. At regular intervals, using the mantra of King Yama, bring forth from the altar fried cakes, five-grain porridge, and various fragrant flowers mixed with water in a clean vessel. With a joyful heart, scatter them in the four directions. Beginning with the four attendant ghosts, all ghosts will joyfully accept. Afterward, one should recite Mahāyāna sūtras.
