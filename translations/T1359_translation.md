# 佛說花積樓閣陀羅尼經
## The Buddha's Teaching: Puṣpakūṭa Pavilion Dhāraṇī Sūtra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1359

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dānapāla (施護).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the World-Honored One was at Lake Anavatapta, in the palace of the Nāga King Without-Heat-Affliction, together with a great assembly of four hundred and fifty bhikṣus. There was also Siṃhavikrīḍita Bodhisattva and a full assembly of one thousand great bodhisattvas, all of whom had attained the dhāraṇīs, perfected the tenth bhūmi, received consecration, occupied the position of successor, donned the great armor, advanced toward the qualities of a buddha, were irreversible in mind, and were universally renowned. These bodhisattva mahāsattvas had all come from other regions to assemble here.

At that time Siṃhavikrīḍita Bodhisattva Mahāsattva rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, came before the World-Honored One, placed his right knee on the ground, joined his palms with reverence, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! How may one obtain the store of merit of the Buddha? And for those who make offerings to the Tathāgata, how are the causes and fruits immeasurable and boundless? May the Tathāgata compassionately reveal this." Having spoken, he settled his mind and waited.

At that time the Buddha addressed Siṃhavikrīḍita Bodhisattva Mahāsattva: "Excellent, excellent! Good man! You now ask me, 'How may one obtain the Tathāgata's store of merit? And how are the causes and fruits of making offerings to the Tathāgata immeasurable and boundless?' Listen carefully, and I shall now teach you."

The Buddha said: "Good man! The Tathāgata is endowed with five dharmas of immeasurable and boundless merit. What are the five? They are discipline, concentration, wisdom, liberation, and the knowledge-and-vision of liberation. Good man! Because the Tathāgata is endowed with these five dharmas of immeasurable merit, he has obtained a store of merit that is immeasurable and boundless. Good man! Those who make offerings to the Tathāgata will attain nirvāṇa. Those of the three vehicles, according to the mind they have aroused, will each see nirvāṇa in their own way.

"Good man! If one sees the Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha, and with a pure mind sincerely takes refuge, honors and praises him, and offers robes, bedding, medicines for illness, and all necessities, the fruit obtained is immeasurable and boundless. Good man! Furthermore, after the Tathāgata's parinirvāṇa, if one obtains relics of the Tathāgata as small as a mustard seed and makes offerings, the fruit obtained is the greatest of all. Furthermore, of all kinds of meritorious deeds, making offerings to the Tathāgata's relics yields the most supreme merit.

"Siṃhavikrīḍita! Furthermore, if a person takes a heap of jewels as high as Mount Sumeru and gives it in offering to śrāvakas and pratyekabuddhas, the fruit obtained does not equal that of a householder or renunciant bodhisattva who has aroused the mind of unsurpassed, perfect awakening and gives a single gold coin in offering to the Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha. The former skillful wholesome root does not equal even one part in a hundred, one part in a thousand, one part in a hundred thousand of the latter, and is beyond all calculation, number, comparison, or analogy.

"Good man! Furthermore, when the Tathāgata, having dwelt in the world for a hundred years, a thousand years, or even a hundred thousand years, has completed his teaching activities and entered parinirvāṇa, and a wondrous stūpa has been raised over his relics: if a person at this stūpa plasters the ground with fragrant clay to make a maṇḍala, sets out flower-garlands, fragrant paste, burning incense, and lamps, and makes offerings with a devoted mind, or even offers but a single flower or a single lamp, or takes a broom and sweeps away the dust, or with a single handful of water sprinkles and purifies it, such a person, through the sincerity of their devotion, obtains immeasurable merit. Whatever sins this person committed in past lives that would have caused them to fall into the Avīci Hell will, because they made offerings at the stūpa, be entirely eliminated.

"Good man! Furthermore, if a person arouses even a single thought of taking refuge in the Buddha, and does not subsequently regress or become contemptuous, that person in the future, for a hundred kalpas, a thousand kalpas, or even a hundred thousand kalpas, will constantly experience supreme blessings, be born among devas and humans, and will never again fall into any evil destiny.

"Good man! I now teach this Puṣpakūṭa Pavilion Dhāraṇī Sūtra because I wish to benefit and bring joy to all sentient beings in the heavens and the human world. Good man! If a person obtains this Puṣpakūṭa Pavilion Dhāraṇī Sūtra and with a devoted mind receives, upholds, reads, recites, and makes offerings to it, and furthermore expounds its true meaning broadly for others, that person will never fall into any evil destiny, nor be born in a lowly lineage. They will always be born in supreme places among devas and humans, their faculties will be complete, and they will obtain the knowledge of past lives. They will be endowed with various kinds of profound eloquence, will constantly encounter the jewels of the Buddha, Dharma, and Saṃgha, and will also be remembered and protected by the Tathāgata. They will obtain the mind of awakening and never regress."

At that time the World-Honored One spoke the dhāraṇī:

*Tadyathā: dhāraṇi dhāraṇi muni prabhā-svāri siddhi candrī-nā-thā ji-ni-ri hā-ri a-ru-gā-ya-vā-ti buddha-vā-ti vi-ri u-kā-ci rā-ju pa-ga-te te-ju vā-ti viśā-rā buddhi dharma-vā-bha-se a-kṣaya ka-la-pe ka-la-pa vā-ti amṛta-ka-la-pe hu-tā śa-ni-te-ju vā-ti ni-tya-sa-mā-hi-te te-ju vā-ti ti-kṣa-ṇa-ṇi driya buddhi svāhā.*

"Good man! This dhāraṇī has been proclaimed in common by innumerable buddhas and tathāgatas of the ten directions. If a person hears the name of this dhāraṇī sūtra and, with a mind of utmost sincerity, reverently believes in it and makes offerings, that person will in the future obtain immeasurable and boundless merit. Furthermore, when a person has made this dhāraṇī their preliminary practice and then, during the second, third, or eighth month, beginning on the eighth day of the bright fortnight, arouses a mind of utmost sincerity, and six times during the day and night recites this dhāraṇī and contemplates all the buddhas and tathāgatas with an undistracted mind, and further offers fragrant paste, exquisite flowers, burning incense, and lamps of various kinds without interruption: then on the fifteenth day they will certainly see the World-Honored One seated on a lotus-treasury throne teaching the Dharma of the dhāraṇī. The practitioner will at that very time have their aspirations perfectly fulfilled, all defilements of the mind removed, and will realize the knowledge of past lives. They will attain the various dhyāna-concentrations, abide in the Four Noble Truths, and understand the meaning of all doctrines, treatises, arts, and crafts. Even at the end of their life, their faculty of hearing will not deteriorate, and they will clearly discern all supreme matters, even to hearing the Tathāgata teach such undefiled truths as the Four Noble Truths."

When the World-Honored One had spoken this dhāraṇī sūtra, Siṃhavikrīḍita Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, and all the great bodhisattvas, the great assembly of bhikṣus, devas, humans, asuras, gandharvas, and the rest, having heard what the Buddha taught, all greatly rejoiced, bowed to the Buddha, and withdrew.
