# 氷揭羅天童子經
## Sūtra of the Piṅgala Celestial Youth

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1263

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

If a practitioner upholds this vidyā, whether sustaining himself by begging for food, or subsisting solely on milk porridge, or eating his own food, he should recite before the image to a full three hundred thousand times. Afterward, perform the fire method. Take fine resin and cut it into one thousand and eight pieces. Recite once and cast one piece into the fire. Three times daily, for a full forty days, accomplishment will be attained, and all wishes will be fulfilled.

Another method: take fine resin mixed with frankincense and form it into pills. Beginning on the eighth day of the dark fortnight and continuing through the fourteenth, three times daily, reciting one thousand and eight times per session, cast the pills into the fire. The youth will appear, either by day or by night, manifesting in person as a companion. He will fulfill all wishes and carry out whatever commands are given, with nothing that does not accord.

Another method: take frankincense and form it into pills. For seven days, three times daily, reciting one thousand and eight times per session, recite once and cast one pill into the fire. One will certainly obtain the lordship of a domain, and nothing will fail to bear fruit.

The practitioner who wishes to perform this method should first recite seven hundred thousand times.

**Method for Making the Image**

The image should be made of white sandalwood, six finger-widths in height, in the form of a youth. The left hand holds a fruit, and the right hand hangs down in the wish-granting gesture with palm facing outward. Install it within the practice-hall and make offerings of various food and drink, milk porridge, incense, and flowers. Take red flowers; with each recitation cast one flower upon the youth's body, to a full one hundred thousand times. The youth will certainly manifest in person, and one will obtain the fulfillment of all wishes, daily bestowing upon a thousand people the finest garments and food, lacking nothing.

Another method: three times daily, each session take one thousand and eight pills of storax and cast them into the fire. After a full seven days, the youth will manifest in person and point out hidden treasures. If one can uphold this daily, one will obtain great benefit, and the treasures will be revealed. He will likewise point out the entrances to asura caves, and will certainly fulfill one's wishes and bestow great wealth and treasures.

If a person is imprisoned and bound, reciting this vidyā will bring immediate release.

Another method: if one wishes all people to be delighted upon seeing them, recite the person's name to a full one hundred thousand times, and all will be delighted.

Another method: take aśoka wood and burn it on a mountaintop, mixing frankincense into one thousand and eight pills and casting them into the fire. One will attain peace and accomplishment; whatever one wishes to see will appear, and one will live for a thousand years.

The dhāraṇī mantra:

*Oṃ ṭhiṭh-bhi ṭhiṭh-nī svāhā*

Another version:

*Oṃ ṭhi-ja ṭhi-nī svāhā*

The root mudrā: join both palms with hollow center. When making the invitation, move both thumbs three times toward and away from the body. When dispatching, push outward three times.

Invitation of the fire-deva, vidyā and mudrā. The mantra:

*Oṃ agnaye āga-āga svāhā*

The dispatch vidyā:

*Agnaye gaccha-gaccha svāhā*

The mudrā: with the right hand, bend the thumb into the palm. Bend the index finger like a hook, bracing it against the back of the thumb at the lower joint. The remaining fingers are all extended. When inviting, hook the index finger toward the body three times. When dispatching, push outward three times.

Body-protection mudrā and vidyā:

*Oṃ kuru-dhana hūṃ jah*

The mudrā: make a fist with the right hand, raise the thumb and touch it to the five places of the body. This constitutes complete protection.
