# 觀自在菩薩說普賢陀羅尼經
## Sūtra of the Samantabhadra Dhāraṇī Spoken by Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1037

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

Thus have I heard. At one time the Bhagavān was dwelling at Rājagṛha on Vulture Peak, together with a great assembly of bhikṣus and ninety-nine koṭis of great bodhisattva-mahāsattvas.

At that time, Holy Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, present in that assembly, rose from his seat and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One, I wish to proclaim the Samantabhadra Dhāraṇī. World-Honored One, I received this dhāraṇī from Candrottamaprabhā Tathāgata. If one who follows the bodhisattva vehicle merely hears this dhāraṇī, they immediately attain non-retrogression and swiftly attend upon all tathāgatas, arhats, samyak-saṃbuddhas. It can extinguish all karmic obstructions, bring peace, happiness, and abundance, and purify the body, speech, and mind. One penetrates the great ocean of all mysteries, fulfills the great ocean of all vows, and immediately sees all tathāgatas.

"Through hearing this dhāraṇī, I realized the acceptance of the non-arising of dharmas. I attained the śūraṅgama samādhi, the jewel-seal samādhi, the blazing-torch samādhi, the ocean-seal samādhi, the all-pervading-space samādhi, and samādhi-gates equal in number to the sands of the Ganges. I further attained eighty-four thousand dhāraṇī-gates including the opening-the-inexhaustible-casket gate. Through these I attained complete wisdom and complete practice. Gaining such wisdom, I accomplished the dharma I had heard, attending upon and making offerings to the buddhas without interruption. I humbly beseech the World-Honored One to permit me to proclaim it for the fourfold assembly."

The Buddha said: "You may proclaim it."

Then Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva Mahāsattva entered the vajra-maṇḍala samādhi and spoke the dhāraṇī:

*Namo ratna-trayāya. Namaḥ ārya-avalokiteśvarāya bodhisattvāya mahāsattvāya mahākāruṇikāya. Tadyathā: khagave khagave khagave cakṣu-khagave sūtra-khagave karaṇa-khagave jihva-khagave kāya-khagave mana-khagave svara-khagave vaima-khagave śūnya-khagave nirmita-khagave praṇidhāna-khagave samatha-khagave pāramitā-khagave bodhi-khagave sara sara sara sarva-buddha-adhiṣṭhite bhara bhara bhara dharma-adhiṣṭhite kara kara kara sangha-adhiṣṭhite. Namaḥ ārya-avalokiteśvarāya bodhisattvāya mahāsattvāya mahākāruṇikāya. Namo dvānavatīnāṃ bodhisattva-koṭīnāṃ adhiṣṭhite tu vaṃ ārya-mālā-candranandantu. Tadyathā: surabhī surabhī muni muni mahāmuni mati mati mahāmati. Namaḥ ārya-avalokiteśvarāya bodhisattvāya mahāsattvāya mahākāruṇikāya. Siddhyantu samanta-bhadra-dhāraṇī svāhā.*

At that time, when Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva spoke this dhāraṇī, ninety-two koṭis of bodhisattvas attained the śūraṅgama samādhi, and bodhisattvas equal in number to the sands of the Ganges attained the subtle and wondrous dhāraṇī samādhi.

"I shall now explain the merits of this dhāraṇī. If one recites this dhāraṇī one hundred and eight times each morning, completing twenty-one days, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva will manifest in person and all wishes will be fulfilled.

"If a person is imprisoned in stocks and fetters, after seven days of recitation they will be freed. If a person suffers from malaria, reciting beside their left ear will heal them. If a person suffers from wind-affliction or demonic possession, mix butter and oil, empower it with twenty-one recitations, and have the patient take it for an immediate cure. If one suffers from toothache, empower a tooth-stick with twenty-one recitations and have the patient chew it for an immediate cure. If one suffers from earache, take jasmine-flower oil and place it in a fired copper vessel, empower it with seven recitations, and drip it into the ear for an immediate cure. If one suffers from headache or stomach pain, empower the hand with recitations and massage the area for an immediate cure. If one is afflicted by demons, tie a spell-empowered cord with one hundred and eight recitations and have the patient wear it for an immediate cure. If one is possessed by a ḍākinī spirit, empower oil with seven recitations and anoint the joints for an immediate cure. For all other illnesses, empower either the hand or a willow branch and rub or sweep the affected area for an immediate cure.

"For all the above methods, even without having established a formal practice, reciting on the spot is immediately effective.

"If one wishes to see buddhas and bodhisattvas, attain dhāraṇī-gates and samādhi-gates, obtain supernatural powers, invisibility, eye-medicine, or realgar accomplishments, the practitioner should go before a reliquary stūpa or buddha image, spread and prepare a maṇḍala, hang silk banners and canopies, scatter various flowers, burn sandalwood, aloeswood, and frankincense, light one hundred lamps, and make extensive offerings. The reciter should bathe in purity, wear clean garments, and maintain celibacy, then recite this dhāraṇī and seal the boundaries of the cardinal and intermediate directions. The boundary-sealing dhāraṇī says:

*Namo ratna-trayāya. Namaḥ ārya-avalokiteśvarāya bodhisattvāya mahāsattvāya mahākāruṇikāya. Tadyathā: ciri-bandha-me miri-bandha-mi ciri-miri-bandha-mi sīmā-bandha-mi sīmā-me kaṣṭhina-ti-kramatu svāhā.*

"Empower water with twenty-one recitations of this dhāraṇī and sprinkle it throughout the ritual space in the ten directions to establish the boundary.

"I shall now speak the invitation dhāraṇī. The mantra practitioner should first recite this dhāraṇī to make the invitation, and only then begin the main recitation. The dhāraṇī says:

*Namo ratna-trayāya. Namaḥ ārya-avalokiteśvarāya bodhisattvāya mahāsattvāya mahākāruṇikāya. Tadyathā: ciri ciri miri miri cirile ehi bhagavan nārya-avalokiteśvara svāhā.*

"This dhāraṇī is my heart mantra. Reciting this mantra accomplishes the invitation. The practitioner should begin on the eighth day of the bright half of the month and continue to the fifteenth, reciting one hundred and eight times at each of the three daily periods. Bathe three times daily and change garments three times. On the fifteenth day, make extensive offerings and recite without limit. At midnight on that day, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva will come to the ritual space, manifesting a golden body of perfect marks and splendid beauty, emitting hundreds of thousands of rays of light. The reciter should not be afraid but generate a courageous mind. Upon seeing Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, one immediately attains a bhūmi and realizes the dhāraṇī samādhi. One then sees Akṣobhya Tathāgata in the east, Ratnadhvaja Tathāgata in the south, Amitāyus Tathāgata in the west, and Divyadundubhisvara-rāja Tathāgata in the north. Seeing these four tathāgatas and the vast, majestic virtue of the tathāgatas of the ten directions, sustained by the power of the buddhas' great compassionate vows, one remains long in the world. After this life ends, one will be born in a pure, wondrous buddha-land, attending upon and making offerings to tathāgatas in every place."

When the World-Honored One had spoken this sūtra, the bodhisattva-mahāsattvas, devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, humans, and non-humans all rejoiced greatly, accepted it with faith, and put it into practice.
