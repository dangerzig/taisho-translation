# 挾註波羅蜜多心經
## Heart Sūtra of the Perfection of Wisdom with Interlinear Annotations

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 2747

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown (失譯)

---

The Bodhisattva Avalokiteśvara, while practicing the deep Perfection of Wisdom, clearly perceived that the five aggregates are all empty, and thereby transcended all suffering and distress.

Śāriputra, form is not different from emptiness, emptiness is not different from form; form is precisely emptiness, emptiness is precisely form. Sensation, perception, mental formations, and consciousness are likewise.

Śāriputra, the empty nature of all phenomena is: neither arising nor ceasing, neither defiled nor pure, neither increasing nor decreasing. Therefore, in emptiness there is no form, no sensation, perception, mental formations, or consciousness; no eyes, ears, nose, tongue, body, or mind; no form, sound, smell, taste, touch, or mental objects; no eye realm, and so forth up to no mind-consciousness realm.

No ignorance, and no exhaustion of ignorance; and so forth up to no old age and death, and no exhaustion of old age and death. No suffering, origination, cessation, or path. No wisdom and no attainment, because there is nothing to be attained.

The Bodhisattva, relying on the Perfection of Wisdom, the mind has no obstruction; because there is no obstruction, there is no fear, and one is far removed from confused and inverted thinking, arriving at ultimate nirvāṇa.

All Buddhas of the three ages, relying on the Perfection of Wisdom, attain anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi.

Therefore know that the Perfection of Wisdom is the great mantra of divine power, the great mantra of illumination, the incomparable mantra, capable of removing all suffering, true and not empty. Therefore recite the mantra of the Perfection of Wisdom, which is spoken thus:

> *gate gate pāragate pārasaṃgate bodhi svāhā*
