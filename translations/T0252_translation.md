# 普遍智藏般若波羅蜜多心經
## Heart Sūtra of the Prajñāpāramitā of the Treasury of Universal Wisdom

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0252

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, retranslated by Dharmacandra (法月)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the great city of Rājagṛha, on Vulture Peak Mountain, together with a great assembly of bhikṣus numbering a full hundred thousand, and seventy-seven thousand bodhisattva-mahāsattvas, whose leaders were the Bodhisattva Avalokiteśvara, the Bodhisattva Mañjuśrī, the Bodhisattva Maitreya, and others. All had attained samādhi and dhāraṇī and abided in the inconceivable liberation.

At that time, the Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Avalokiteśvara, who was seated in that assembly, rose from his seat and went before the World-Honored One. Facing him with palms joined, bowing with reverence, gazing up at the Honored One's countenance, he said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I wish, in this assembly, to speak the Heart of the Prajñāpāramitā of the Treasury of Universal Wisdom for all bodhisattvas. I only pray that the World-Honored One will permit what I speak and proclaim the essential secret Dharma for all bodhisattvas." At that time, the World-Honored One, with his wondrous Brahmā-voice, addressed the Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Avalokiteśvara: "Excellent, excellent! O one endowed with great compassion! I permit what you speak, and you shall make a great radiance for all sentient beings."

Thereupon the Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Avalokiteśvara, having received the Buddha's permission and being sustained by the Buddha's mindfulness, entered the samādhi called Radiance of Wisdom. Having entered this absorption, by the power of samādhi he practiced the deep prajñāpāramitā and illuminated the five aggregates, seeing that their self-nature is entirely empty. Having realized that the self-nature of the five aggregates is entirely empty, he calmly arose from that samādhi. He then addressed the venerable Śāriputra: "Good man! The bodhisattva has a Heart of Prajñāpāramitā called the Treasury of Universal Wisdom. Listen now with careful attention, reflect well upon it, and I shall analyze and explain it for you." Having spoken thus, the venerable Śāriputra said to the Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Avalokiteśvara: "Yes! O Great Pure One! Please speak it. Now is the right time."

He then addressed Śāriputra: "All bodhisattva-mahāsattvas should study thus: The nature of form is emptiness; the nature of emptiness is form. Form is not different from emptiness; emptiness is not different from form. Form is itself emptiness; emptiness is itself form. Feeling, perception, volitional formations, and consciousness are likewise. The nature of consciousness is emptiness; the nature of emptiness is consciousness. Consciousness is not different from emptiness; emptiness is not different from consciousness. Consciousness is itself emptiness; emptiness is itself consciousness. Śāriputra! All these dharmas have the characteristic of emptiness: they are not produced and not destroyed, not defiled and not pure, not increased and not decreased. Therefore, in emptiness there is no form, no feeling, perception, volitional formations, or consciousness; no eye, ear, nose, tongue, body, or mind; no form, sound, smell, taste, touch, or mental object; no eye-element and so on up to no mind-consciousness element. There is no ignorance and no extinction of ignorance, and so on up to no old age and death and no extinction of old age and death. There is no suffering, origination, cessation, or path. There is no wisdom and no attainment. Because there is nothing to attain, the bodhisattva, relying on prajñāpāramitā, has a mind without obstruction. Because there is no obstruction, there is no fear. Far removed from inverted dream-thinking, one reaches ultimate nirvāṇa. All Buddhas of the three periods of time, relying on prajñāpāramitā, attain the supreme, perfect enlightenment. Therefore know that prajñāpāramitā is the great divine mantra, the great luminous mantra, the unsurpassed mantra, the mantra equal to the unequaled, which can remove all suffering, true and not false. Therefore the mantra of prajñāpāramitā is spoken."

The mantra is then spoken:

*Gate gate pāragate pārasaṃgate bodhi svāhā*

When the Buddha had spoken this sūtra, all the bhikṣus, the assembly of bodhisattvas, and all the devas, humans, asuras, gandharvas, and others of the world, hearing what the Buddha taught, were all greatly joyful, and they accepted it with faith and practiced it.
