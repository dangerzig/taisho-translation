# The Garuḍa King Chapter from the Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva Root Great Teaching King Sūtra

## *Wénshūshīlì Púsà Gēnběn Dàjiào Wáng Jīng Jīnchì Niǎo Wáng Pǐn*

Taishō 1276

Translated by Amoghavajra (*Bùkōng*), Tang dynasty.

*Note: This is a chapter from the great Mañjuśrī tantric compendium, presenting the ritual methods of the Garuḍa King (Jīnchì Niǎo Wáng, "Golden-Winged Bird King"). Set in the Pure Abode celestial palace, the Buddha instructs Mañjuśrī that mastery of the five elements through the Garuḍa King's mantra-practice enables one to comprehend the languages of all beings and achieve both worldly and supramundane accomplishments, including power over all serpents and nāgas. The Garuḍa King himself then rises to teach his root mantra and "one hundred methods of the past" (guòqù bǎi zhǒng fǎ), an extensive collection of ritual procedures. These include: summoning, controlling, and curing snake bites and dragon-inflicted poisons; various abhicāra (destructive) rites using homa with animal parts, human remains, and other substances; weather-control methods; subjugation and love-rites; and methods for breaking opponents' mantras. The text concludes with mudrā instructions, criteria for selecting and initiating worthy disciples via maṇḍala, and the solemn oath required of recipients.*

---

At that time, Śākyamuni Buddha again gazed upon the Pure Abode celestial palace and addressed Mañjuśrī Kumārabhūta ("Eternally Youthful") Bodhisattva: "Mañjuśrī! Within your great teaching, praised and rejoiced in by all the tathāgatas, following the practice of the great mantras, and in order to cause all sentient beings to enter the samaya-maṇḍala: by understanding the five elements of earth, water, fire, wind, and space, one penetrates the inconceivable domain of the languages of all sentient beings, accomplishes the practice of the Garuḍa King's mantra, and through supernatural powers comprehends this penetrating skillful means. All worldly and supramundane dharmas are accomplished, even to the thorough comprehension of all the embodied forms of animals and the Garuḍa King."

Therefore, in the midst of the bodhisattva assembly, the Garuḍa King, surrounded by countless garuḍas, rose from his seat, went before Mañjuśrī the great bodhisattva, bowed his head to the ground at [Mañjuśrī's] feet, knelt with palms joined, and said to Mañjuśrī: "I abide in the stage of a great bodhisattva. For the benefit and happiness of sentient beings, within this Teaching King, I wish to expound the one hundred methods of the past. May the great bodhisattva rejoice and permit me to speak."

Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva said: "For the benefit of sentient beings, speak now."

Then the Garuḍa King, empowered by the Buddha's spiritual force, rose from his seat with joy and delight. Mañjuśrī said: "You should proclaim the one hundred methods of the past together with their profound and secret essentials."

At that time, the Garuḍa King spoke the mantra:

[Dhāraṇī]

### The One Hundred Methods

Having spoken the mantra, the Garuḍa King next proclaimed the one hundred methods of the past: summoning dragons, taming dragons, capturing dragons; whether one has been bitten by a dragon or not, all are caused to become possessed by spirits, who reveal all events past and future; summoning snakes, punishing snakes, extracting venom for play; protection from all poisons; methods accomplished by mere thought or mental intention.

"The mantra-holder should maintain fasting precepts for three days without eating, or fast for twelve days. On a riverbank, build a six-cubit maṇḍala using powders of five colors. Draw eight eight-petaled lotuses. In the center, paint the Buddha in the posture of teaching the Dharma. To the Buddha's right, draw the great saint Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva in powder, with palms joined, gazing at the Buddha. To the Buddha's left, draw Nārāyaṇa [Viṣṇu] with four arms, holding the four emblematic implements. Near Nārāyaṇa, draw the Garuḍa King in an extremely fearsome and terrifying form. Near the Garuḍa King, draw Aruṇa. Behind the great saint Mañjuśrī, draw Aprajña Bodhisattva, Sudhana the youth, and Subhūti, standing with palms joined. This is the central altar.

"Next, in the outer court to the east, draw a vajra in white lime. On the left, draw a sword in charcoal. On the north, draw a club in yellow. On the west, draw a lasso in red. Thus the outer court of the maṇḍala is established.

"When the drawing is complete, offer three kinds of sweet food on the four sides. Scatter various flowers, and apply various unguent-incenses and powdered incenses upon the altar. Place arghya [offering-water] vessels filled with scented water. Burn benzoin incense. Recite the root mantra to invite all the holy assembly.

"Before this altar, construct a homa furnace. Light a fire with smokeless charcoal. Feed it with khadira wood. Generating great compassion for all sentient beings, sit on a nāga-seat. Using snake-thorn wood sticks dipped in the three sweets, cast them into the furnace one thousand and eight times. The sign of accomplishment will then appear: many snakes will come and gather. Offer them arghya. Know that the mantra-method has succeeded. Recite the mantra and make this vow: 'May the mantra-method attain siddhi.' Then recite the root mantra for the dismissal. Sprinkle arghya water while reciting the mantra over all the offerings of food, drink, flowers, and incense, and cast them into the river water. From then on, all summoning and binding of poisonous creatures, and whatever you recite, will be accomplished.

### Subsidiary Ritual Methods

"Another method: if you wish to make two people despise each other, take white cotton flowers mixed with poison and perform homa twenty-one times. They will immediately despise each other.

"Another method: if you wish to destroy an enemy's household, take a piece of snake skin, recite the mantra once, and cast it into the fire twenty-one times. That enemy will be destroyed.

"Another method: take a bird's wing and perform homa twenty-one times. The enemy will run about wildly, jumping like a crow.

"Another method: if you wish a man and woman to be mutually devoted, perform homa with white mustard seeds and ghee twenty-one times. They will immediately become obedient to each other.

"Another method: if you wish a king to be well-disposed toward you, perform homa with milk porridge twenty-one times. You will get your wish.

"Another method: consecrate a clod of earth and cast it into a fire. The fire will immediately lose its heat. To dispel this, consecrate grass and cast it into the fire, and it will return to normal.

"Another method: recite the mantra seven times and consecrate a river or spring. All the fish will be immune to nets.

"Another method: snap the fingers and recite the mantra. All fish can be summoned.

"Another method: if anyone suffers from illness, consecrate water twenty-one times and pour it over their head. The illness will be cured.

"Another method: consecrate a staff twenty-one times. Strike any door with it, and the door will open by itself.

"Another method: place the staff in a blue-colored bag and display it before an enemy army. That army will scatter.

"Another method: consecrate your garment-hem twenty-one times and wave it at the enemy. All the swords, staffs, bows, and crossbows of that army will break by themselves, and all will be immobilized. To release them, recite the mantra once and they will be freed.

"Another method: take a snake's head, consecrate ash and stop its mouth. Speak that person's name and recite the mantra twenty-one times. That person will immediately become mute.

"Another method: if someone has been struck by a poison arrow, consecrate water and sprinkle it from afar. The arrow will come out by itself.

"Another method: if you wish to break another person's mantra, recite this mantra and the other's will become ineffective. To release it, simply release it.

"Another method: consecrate a brick twenty-one times and hurl it at someone who is in shackles or fetters. The shackles will break and fall off.

"Another method: if you wish to bring rain, establish the altar as before, make offerings, and construct a homa furnace. Light a fire with bitter neem wood. Perform homa with white mustard seeds one thousand and eight times. Sweet rain will immediately fall. If you want more, perform additional homa; even a thousandfold increase is possible at will.

"Another method: take long pepper, consecrate it twenty-one times, and hold it in your hand. Wherever you wave it, the evil hail and frost will move elsewhere.

"Another method: if a house is on fire, perform the above method, and the fire will move elsewhere.

"Another method: if it is snowing, also perform this method, as described above.

"Another method: consecrate a clay snake twenty-one times and speak: 'Bite so-and-so.' It will follow the instruction and bite that person.

"Another method: make a snake from charcoal powder and use the same method as above. To release the person, recite the mantra saying 'release,' and they will be released.

"Another method: if you wish to summon snakes, consecrate white mustard seeds seven times and scatter them in the four directions. Snakes will immediately come; establish a protective boundary. Sprinkle water on them and it becomes a dismissal.

"Another method: consecrate a clod of earth twenty-one times and cast it into a dragon pool. The dragon will immediately come.

"Another method: consecrate earth and cast it into water. The dragon will surge up, and the mantra-holder may stand upon the dragon's back, moving freely.

"Another method: if someone has been bitten by a poisonous snake, make four garuḍa birds from clay. Recite the mantra and sprinkle water on the victim's body. Cast the garuḍa birds in the four directions. Each will seize a snake and bring it. The practitioner speaks: 'Drink the poison from this person.' The poison will immediately disperse, and the person will be able to stand.

"Another method: consecrate small beans one hundred and eight times and scatter them in the four directions. Dragons will come, transformed into bee-forms. Say: 'Sting so-and-so.' The bees will sting, and no other mantra-holder can release it. Only the original practitioner, by consecrating water and sprinkling it, can release it and disperse the poison.

"Another method: take a bow and consecrate it. Shoot arrows in the four directions. Snakes will coil around the arrows and return. The practitioner speaks: 'Drink this poison.' The bitten person will stand up immediately. The arrows should not have iron tips.

"Another method: if someone has been struck by a poison arrow, consecrate water and [sprinkle it on] the arrow. The arrow will come out by itself.

"Another method: make four mongooses from anthill clay. Consecrate water and sprinkle it on the victim's body. The mongooses will go and seize snakes and bring them. The practitioner speaks: 'Drink the poison.' The poison is dispelled, and the bitten person stands up.

"Another method: consecrate charcoal. On the ground, draw a snake and use an arghya-wood staff to whip it. The drawn snake will go, seize a [real] snake, and bring it, then drink its poison by itself. Even a person who has died from a bite will revive.

"Another method: consecrate a banner or canopy. Wave it over a person who has died from poison, and they will immediately revive.

"Another method: consecrate musical instruments, then play, strike, or blow them. All who have died from poison will revive.

"Another method: if you wish to summon dragons and snakes, use earth dyed in five colors to draw a maṇḍala. Clap your hands and recite the mantra. Dragons and snakes will come from the four directions and enter the altar. Do not be afraid. Protect your body and tie a topknot. They will not dare to do harm.

"Another method: recite the mantra and consecrate your eyes. If you gaze at an angry person, they will instantly fall to the ground and be transformed into a snake. To release them, simply release them.

"Another method: in this manner, reciting the mantra, whether a person has been poisoned or not yet poisoned, all are made to manifest miraculous effects at the practitioner's will.

"Another method: take withered flowers that were offered to Nārāyaṇa, recite the mantra to consecrate them one hundred and eight times, and scatter them at a crossroads or into someone's house. The flowers will transform into snakes that can bite people. If you consecrate water and sprinkle it on the bitten person, the poison will be neutralized.

"Another method: consecrate your own hand. For six months, you will be immune to all poisons.

"Another method: consecrate your own body and speak: 'Snake, bite that person.' It will immediately bite.

"Another method: consecrate your headdress, necklaces, and adornments and wear them. They will protect your body from all poisons.

"Another method: if a person has died from poison, recite the mantra beside their body. Whether you apply mud, sprinkle water, fan them, or mentally recite the mantra, the person who died from poison will revive.

"Another method: if someone is thin and emaciated, consecrate mahā-māṃsa mixed with ghee, and fumigate that person. They will immediately become plump and full.

"Another method: take human bone ground to powder, and burn owl and horned-owl [flesh]. Recite the mantra, adding that person's name. That person will perish.

"Another method: recite the mantra and consecrate madana medicine mixed with chaff and burn it. It will cause that person to go mad.

"Another method: mix white mustard seeds and burn them, reciting the mantra with that person's name added. They will be afflicted with an incurable malaria.

"Another method: burn Job's tears mixed with cat dung, reciting the mantra to consecrate them. The two people will despise each other.

"Another method: burn skull powder, dried toad powder, dried fish powder, and honey, reciting the mantra to consecrate them. That person's life will be severed.

"Another method: burn ox gall and human bone mixed with ghee. This crushes an enemy household.

"Another method: mix fish roe, wine, and cotton-flower seeds, recite the mantra to consecrate them. If the person is separated and far away, speak their name, and before long they will return.

"Another method: grind mung beans to powder, mix with chicken meat and eggs, pound into pellets the size of jujubes, burn them while speaking the name. This accomplishes a summoning.

"Another method: pound radish seeds and mix with sesame oil and burn. This is the quick-summoning incense method.

"Another method: burn benzoin mixed with ghee, speaking that person's name. This cures all illnesses.

"Another method: perform homa with sesame and white mustard seeds for seven nights, speaking that person's name. You will gain their love and obedience.

"Another method: mix salt and mustard seeds, perform homa one thousand and eighty times, three times daily, for seven full days. You will gain the love of persons of rank.

"Another method: take a skull, finely pound it to powder, consecrate it one thousand and eighty times, apply it to your hands, and touch a person. You will gain their love and devotion.

"Another method: take charnel-ground ash, consecrate it one hundred and eight times, and scatter it on a person's body. They will suffer from severe malaria. Later, if they generate a penitent mind, consecrate [water] with this mantra and the illness will be released.

"Another method: mix mongoose hair, white mustard seeds, and snake skin into powder. Speak the person's name, consecrate one hundred and eight times, and burn. Everyone will despise that person. To release it, consecrate sesame seeds and burn them, which will restore love and bring wealth.

"Another method: burn sesame, husked rice, and ghee mixed together, reciting the mantra to consecrate. You will gain a woman's devotion and love.

"Another method: burn barley, sesame, and toad fat beside a dragon pool, performing homa for three nights. The heavens will send a great rain.

"Another method: make a garuḍa form from clay, hold it in your joined palms, enter water up to your chest, and at midnight speak that person's name. Recite the mantra one hundred and eight times. You will gain their love and devotion.

"Another method: scatter husked rice in a charnel ground, then pick it up. With each grain you pick up, recite the mantra once, and strike the heart of the garuḍa [image]. You will obtain official rank, salary, and a full retinue.

"Another method: burn mongoose hair, mouse hair, and cotton-flower seeds together while reciting mantras. All ghosts and spirits will love and obey you, and you may command them at will, and they will accomplish everything.

"Another method: burn poisonous bālā mixed with honey. You will gain everyone's love and devotion.

"Another method: burn red eggs, skull powder, and red mustard-seed oil. This produces love and devotion.

"Another method: burn palāśa, orchid incense, madana-flower seeds together. This produces love and devotion.

"Another method: burn fennel seeds, divine wood, and toad dung together. This produces love and devotion.

"Another method: burn barley, sesame, and thatch-grass mixed with cow urine. This produces love and devotion.

"Another method: burn orpiment, crow's tongue, and one's own rudra-earth together, speaking that person's name. This causes muteness.

"Another method: burn human hair, beef, and sesame. This causes illness in another.

"Another method: burn crow wings, owl wings, and neem oil together, speaking the evil person's name. This causes banishment. That person will have no choice but to go far away.

"Another method: burn benzoin and ghee mixed with the juice of three fruits, reciting mantras. Everyone will love and obey you, following all commands.

"Another method: burn líng-líng incense, cinnamon, and storax (these three combined), reciting mantras. Everyone will comply and obey your commands.

"Another method: burn ghee combined with white sandalwood, camphor, and añjana medicine, reciting mantras. Persons of rank will be pleased.

"Another method: before a Nārāyaṇa image, prepare mahā-māṃsa. First perform eight rounds of homa as an offering, then recite the mantra one thousand and eight times. Perform the rite for three nights. All that is sought will be obtained.

"Another method: in a charnel ground, make a figure of that person from charnel ash. Burn tiger meat as incense. Sit on a grass mat and recite one thousand times. All that is sought will be obtained; all who are summoned will come; all commands will be carried out.

"Another method: perform homa with cotton-flower seeds, chaff, and crow wings. In an instant, that person will be banished.

"Another method: in a charnel ground, light a fire with udumbara wood. Use a kapāla [skull-cup] as a seat. Burn snake skin. The household [you protect] will never lack food.

"Another method: grind charnel-ground bones to powder, mix with white mustard seeds, and perform homa one thousand and eight times, speaking that person's name. Within a hundred yojanas, anyone can be summoned, and regarding desire, sensual contact, and transgressions..."

### Image of the Garuḍa King

"Another method: carve from white sandalwood the form of a Garuḍa King with wings spread, adorned with nāga ornaments, with extremely sharp beak and claws, in a fearsome and terrifying form, standing upon a lotus pedestal, gazing downward. Or use another wood, or paint on a wall. The craftsman must observe the eight precepts, and should be paid generously and made content. The image should be approximately one span in size. Before this image, perform all rites. All that is sought will be accomplished without fail.

"Another method: if you seek increase and prosperity, make a Garuḍa King image from vīva wood. Recite before it, and all wishes will be granted.

"Another method: if you seek love and devotion, make the image from udumbara wood. Recite before it, and love and devotion will be achieved.

"Another method: if you seek children or cattle herds, make the image from śirīṣa [silk-tree] wood.

"Another method: if you seek wealth, make the image from madhūka wood.

"Another method: perform homa with pork. Karmic fruits will be accomplished.

"Another method: if you seek an official position, perform homa with horse meat.

"Another method: perform homa with black sāra-bird meat. This brings auspicious fortune.

"Another method: if you seek fame, women, or land, burn tiger meat for homa.

"Another method: if you seek to win a legal dispute, also perform homa with tiger meat and mahā-māṃsa. Accomplishment will follow.

"Another method: if you seek victory in battle, perform homa with tiger meat. Victory is assured.

"Another method: if you seek the love of a powerful king, perform homa with elephant meat. You will gain his love and submission.

"Another method: if a proud and haughty person of power, or a minister, [is to be tamed], perform homa with horse-incense grass. You will gain their love and submission.

"Another method: to sever that person's life, burn elephant hair, speaking their name for homa.

"Another method: carve a Garuḍa image from śmaśāna wood. Recite before this image. That person's life will be severed.

"Another method: recite before a gold image. This accomplishes the increase method.

"Another method: recite before a silver image. This brings widespread fame.

"Another method: perform homa with crow wings. This can cause harm.

"Another method: perform homa with eagle feathers. This can cause killing.

"Another method: perform homa with owl wings. This causes mutual hatred.

"Another method: perform homa with peacock feathers. This brings abundant wealth.

"Another method: perform homa with pheasant feathers. This brings many wives and concubines.

"Another method: perform homa with sparrow wings. This brings many children.

"Another method: if you seek gold, burn bird feathers.

"Another method: perform homa with kite wings. This causes the other to become dazed and confused.

"Another method: perform homa with dog meat. This can sever another's life.

"Another method: perform homa with water-buffalo meat. This accomplishes a summoning.

"Another method: if you wish to harm another, perform homa with tiger meat.

"Another method: if you wish to quell disasters, perform homa with deer hair.

"Another method: if you wish to destroy a city, perform homa with ram's wool.

"Another method: if you wish to make people despise each other, perform homa with human hair.

"Another method: if you wish to harm another, also perform homa with human hair, which can also destroy an enemy household.

"All such methods are performed with homa three times daily for seven days."

"If you merely think of me, I can remove all poisons. If you constantly recite, I can accomplish all tasks. World-Honored One! If anyone, within this great Teaching King, recites this mantra at three times daily, I shall constantly remove all calamities for them and shall always follow behind that person."

### The Mudrās

Then the Garuḍa King explained his own hand-mudrā:

"Wind the two thumbs around each other, with the two hands in a bent-wing posture. Forming this mudrā completes the body-mudrā. This was previously taught by the great Maheśvara. All dragons, upon seeing this mudrā, will all melt away and not dare to disobey. By lowering this mudrā and beckoning with the remaining fingers, it becomes the dragon-summoning mudrā."

The mantra is:

[Dhāraṇī]

"This mudrā and mantra can tame dragons and can also tame all who are difficult to tame. Another mudrā: join both palms like an unopened lotus. Insert the two thumbs inward, then each hand clenches into a fist, joining them together. This mudrā is called 'the mudrā for taming all dragons of heaven and the human realm,' and it can accomplish everything.

"Another mudrā: join both palms. Cross the two ring fingers outward behind the middle fingers. The two thumbs are parallel and slightly curved like a mouth. The two index fingers each hook the ring fingers. The two little fingers are parallel and upright. Use the root mantra. This is called the Garuḍa King's 'Universal Light Mudrā,' also called the 'Mudrā that Terrifies All Dragons.'

### Transmission Requirements

"In all the mantra-practice methods I have taught, and in the worldly Garuḍa sūtra rituals, this mantra and mudrā are used to assist and accomplish all tasks. Out of compassion for all sentient beings, this Root Teaching King has been spoken. One should always seek accomplishment through this supreme teaching, always performing the most excellent benefit for sentient beings. In the future age of the Dharma's decline, use this to protect the Buddha-dharma, guard the king and the kingdom, and cause all sentient beings to obtain happiness.

"This dharma-gate requires that one select a proper vessel [worthy recipient]: one who has pure faith in the Three Jewels, who abides in the mind of bodhi and has deep compassion for sentient beings, who is filial to parents, loyal to the king, respectful to preceptors and ācāryas, whose faculties are complete, who has deep faith in the mantra-dharma, who can achieve accomplishment in this present life without doubt, who generates intense longing and diligently seeks this dharma without sparing body or life. If the ācārya sees such a person earnestly requesting it, then have them prepare the maṇḍala materials and establish the maṇḍala as described above. Have the disciple purify and fast, bestow the three refuges and the bodhicitta precepts, and lead them into the maṇḍala to cast flowers. If the flowers land on the Buddha, bodhisattvas, Nārāyaṇa, the Garuḍa King, or the devas, that person is a worthy vessel and should receive this dharma. If the flowers do not land on the holy assembly, that person has no share in this dharma's accomplishment.

"Once [the flowers] have landed, before this altar, consecrate scented water with the root mantra three times, and make this vow: 'After receiving this dharma, I must never reveal it to one who has not entered the maṇḍala. I shall not be disrespectful or ungrateful toward my ācārya. If in this lifetime I do not violate this oath, siddhi will manifest before me, and in the next life I shall be born in the palace of the great saint Mañjuśrī. If I leak the secret or betray my teacher's kindness, I shall surely die young and meet with many calamities, and in the future I shall fall into evil destinies.' Having made this vow, have them drink the oath-water. Then receive the teaching from the ācārya point by point, upholding, reading, and reciting it. One should not perform harmful rites on account of minor offenses. All rites should be performed for the benefit of many people, to protect the kingdom and uphold the Buddha-dharma. One who generates such a mind will achieve accomplishment swiftly with little effort and obtain immeasurable, vast merit. This dharma is extremely profound and must be greatly treasured. Among all Garuḍa methods, this is the king, the most supreme.

### Additional Methods

"If you wish to tame all unruly youths, burn deer meat.

"If you wish to make people despise each other, burn pelican wing-feathers.

"If you wish to tame unruly youths, burn pheasant wing-feathers.

"If you wish to summon a distant person, burn water-buffalo meat. Three times daily, one thousand and eight times per session. Accomplishment will be achieved."
