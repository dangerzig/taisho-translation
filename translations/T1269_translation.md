# 佛說金色迦那鉢底陀羅尼經
## Sūtra of the Golden Gaṇapati Dhāraṇī Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1269

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Vajrabodhi (金剛智).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was in the Kalandaka Bamboo Grove in Śrāvastī, greatly expounding the dharma. Out of compassion for all sentient beings, he spoke this dhāraṇī, belonging to the Golden Gaṇapati. At that time the World-Honored One spoke the mantra:

*Namo stuṭi-kaṭa-kaṭa-ṭiṭha-maṭha kirī-vaṇa-kirī-vaṇa pañca-pañca namaḥ stuṭe huru-dapa-jani-ye svāhā abhuta-rāṇa-kṣa-citta-hrasa-ma-ga-cāti mahā-yama-hasti-nakna-pra-kūpa-yami kuru-kuru juru-juru muru-muru namo-namo*

The Buddha told Śāriputra: "This is the Golden Gaṇapati's obstacle-removing mantra. If sentient beings receive and uphold this mantra, all dharma-practices they undertake will be accomplished. If there are great results, one should know that there have certainly been obstacles. On the second, eighth, or fifteenth day of the bright or dark fortnight, plaster the ground with cow dung to make an altar, and make various offerings. All undertakings will be accomplished."

The Golden Gaṇapati addressed the Buddha: "I shall guard and protect those who uphold this mantra. If sentient beings uphold this mantra, whatever material resources, bedding, clothing, food and drink, gold, silver, precious treasures, or servants they need will never be old, lacking, or scarce. All mantra-methods will be swiftly brought to accomplishment, and all undertakings will accord with one's wishes."

"The image should have a human body with an elephant head and six arms. It should be painted on white cotton cloth; carving is also acceptable. White sandalwood, purple sandalwood, or neem wood may all be used; other woods are not permitted. For pigments, do not use animal-based glue; use fragrant juices and sap from aromatic trees. The body stands upright with the trunk curving to the right. The upper left hand holds a sword; the next hand holds a modaka ball; the lower hand holds a sword-club. The next right hand holds a vajra; the lower hand holds a lasso. The body is golden in color. The feet stand upon a golden mountain. Above the head are five-colored clouds, within which the four heavenly kings and various ṛṣis scatter flowers. On the left is Kumāra Viśuddhasena; on the right is Āṭavaka, the foremost great general. Below on the left, paint beautiful women making music offerings, standing. On the right, paint four great yakṣas, each holding weapons: one with a half-head, one with a boar's head, one with an elephant's head, and one with a horse's head, all wearing tiger-skin loincloths.

"When making the image, do not allow unauthorized persons to observe. When the painting is complete, burn incense for purification. Do not eat the five pungent herbs. In a pure room, install the altar. The altar may be square or round, of whatever size desired. Within the altar burn frankincense, white rosin, and aloeswood. Offer various cakes, milk porridge, modaka balls, ghee, honey, fruits, and various offerings, and all undertakings will follow one's heart.

"If there are ghosts or spirits difficult to subdue, twist a white thread while reciting the spell, tying one knot per recitation until forty-nine knots are complete. Tie it to the left arm, and all ghosts will be bound.

"The upholder must not engage in illicit sexual conduct or tell lies and deceive. As for dietary prohibitions, abstain from rapeseed oil and coriander, and the five pungent herbs must not be eaten. Do not enter the homes of those in mourning or places of childbirth. The mudrā methods may be used universally, and are shared across all Vināyaka methods. Do not associate with evil persons. For those who practice together, sharing with them is without hindrance."
