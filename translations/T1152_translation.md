# 佛說佛母般若波羅蜜多大明觀想儀軌
## Visualization Ritual for the Great Vidyā of Prajñāpāramitā, Mother of Buddhas, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1152

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dānapāla (施護).

---

At that time the World-Honored One, for all those who cultivate the supreme supramundane siddhi, proclaimed the visualization method for the Great Vidyā of Prajñāpāramitā, Mother of Buddhas.

That great vidyā says:

*Namo bhagavate ārya-prajñā-pāramitāye aparimita-guṇāye bhakti-vatsalāye sarva-tathāgata-jñāna-paripūrtāye sattva-vatsalāye tadyathā oṃ dhī śruti smṛti vijaye svāhā*

This great vidyā has great merit, is supreme and most excellent, and is inconceivable. If yoga practitioners wish to visualize the syllables of this great vidyā and seek the siddhi method, they should first generate the great bodhicitta. Then, in accordance with the dharma, upon one's own heart visualize a pure moon-disc. Within the moon-disc, visualize the syllable *Dhī*, which turns from the navel-cakra and abides at the heart. The syllable is yellow in color. This is the root heart-syllable of Prajñāpāramitā Bodhisattva.

Having visualized this, further visualize this syllable transforming into Prajñāpāramitā Bodhisattva. Visualize this bodhisattva's body as the color of true gold, with six arms, perfect in all the excellent marks, adorned with various ornaments, wearing a jeweled crown. The topmost two hands form the teaching mudrā. For this mudrā, join both palms together, with both middle fingers entering the palms so their flat surfaces touch, and both thumbs and both index fingers each touching tip to tip. The remaining fingers do not move: this completes the mudrā. The other hands hold a Prajñāpāramitā sūtra, an utpala lotus, a śakti weapon, and so forth.

Visualize in accordance with the dharma. Having visualized thus, further contemplate attentively that the Great Vidyā of Prajñāpāramitā is supreme and profoundly deep, arising from no-self. Because of thus contemplating Prajñāpāramitā as arising from no-self, all dharmas are equal in no-self. Because of this equality, from Prajñāpāramitā there arise boundless forms of syllables. These syllable-forms encompass all forms.

If yoga practitioners hear:

*Oṃ Dhī* (two syllables),

they should visualize them upon the crown of the head. If they hear:

*Oṃ Ghe* (two syllables),

they should visualize them upon the tip of the tongue. If they hear:

*Oṃ Jñī* (two syllables),

they should visualize them upon both ear-lobes.

Furthermore, if yoga practitioners wish to visualize the syllables of this Great Vidyā of Prajñāpāramitā, Mother of Buddhas, they should sincerely, upon their own heart, visualize a great red lotus of eight petals forming a maṇḍala. At the eight petal positions, distribute and visualize the syllables of the great vidyā, each syllable at its proper position without mixing.

On the eastern petal, visualize and place: *Namo* (two syllables).

On the southeastern petal: *Bhagavate* (four syllables).

On the southern petal: *Ārya-prajñā-pāramitāye* (nine syllables).

On the southwestern petal: *Aparimita-guṇāye* (eight syllables).

On the western petal: *Bhakti-vatsalāye* (six syllables).

On the northwestern petal: *Sarva-tathāgata-jñāna-paripūrtāye* (fourteen syllables).

On the northern petal: *Sattva-vatsalāye* (six syllables).

On the northeastern petal: *Tadyathā* (three syllables).

Having visualized the eight petals in sequence, at the center of the lotus, visualize and place: *Oṃ Dhī* (two syllables).

Furthermore, within the inner ring of the lotus, visualize eight positions distributed. At the eastern position: *Śru* (one syllable). At the southeastern position: *Ti* (one syllable). At the southern position: *Smṛ* (combined). At the southwestern position: *Ti* (one syllable). At the western position: *Vi* (one syllable). At the northwestern position: *Ja* (one syllable). At the northern position: *Ye* (one syllable). At the northeastern position: *Svā-hā* (two syllables).

Having thus visualized all the syllables of the great vidyā and distributed them in their positions, one should then, in accordance with the dharma, attentively contemplate the true principle of the syllables as a dharma gate, or in accordance with the dharma, remember and recite. At the three times of day, in accordance with the dharma, arrange the altar and recite one hundred and eight times, or one thousand and eighty times. If one does this for one month, six months, or up to one year without interruption, one will attain the supreme and excellent siddhi method.

Furthermore, if one hears, upholds, or recites this, that person will attain the fulfillment of retentive memory and realize the samādhi of emptiness. This Great Vidyā of Prajñāpāramitā, Mother of Buddhas, is supreme, profoundly deep, and of great merit. Yoga practitioners should visualize it in accordance with the dharma in this way.
