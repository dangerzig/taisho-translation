# 佛說無能勝大明王陀羅尼經
## Sūtra of the Great Vidyārāja Aparājita Dhāraṇī Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1233

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmadeva (法天).

---

At that time the World-Honored One told Śāriputra, Ānanda, and others: "I now proclaim the great vidyārāja Aparājita dhāraṇī, for the benefit and happiness of devas and humans. If there are vetāḍas, bhūtas, or others who create obstacles through root-karmic acts against this great vidyārāja, or if yakṣas and rākṣasas perform violent evil deeds wishing to destroy this vidyārāja, none can destroy it." He then spoke the mantra:

*Tadyathā hasani vihasani huru-juru-ru svāhā*

"If there are wise people who, without doubt or misgiving toward this vidyārāja, honor and uphold it, whether at the king's gate, among lions, on the road or off the road, constantly recollecting it in mind, and even at the māra-king's abode or when encountering enemies, this vidyārāja will constantly provide protection, free from all terrors." He then spoke the mantra:

*Tadyathā dhumi-dhudhumi dhumāvate svāhā*

"If there are women who single-mindedly receive and uphold it, and who can also write it and wear it upon their heads, their possessions will always be abundant and never diminish, and all their family members will enjoy peace and happiness." He then spoke the mantra:

*Tadyathā javā-javā-javani svāhā*

"If there are women who are pregnant and after a long time cannot give birth and suffer greatly, by receiving and upholding this vidyā they will be able to give birth easily." He then spoke the mantra:

*Tadyathā kaśa-kaśa-vikaśa-mahā-kaśa svāhā*

"If there are people on the battlefield when both sides are killing each other, this Aparājita will provide protection and all will be kept safe." He then spoke the mantra:

*Tadyathā ṭha-ṭha-ṭhaci-ṭha svāhā*

"If there are people who are intoxicated, or in their sleep and dreams, or while walking or standing, the bhūta hosts will be unable to disturb them, and they will constantly enjoy peace and happiness. Furthermore, if there are people who with earnest mind constantly receive, uphold, chant, recite, and proclaim this Aparājita, they will be protected day and night. Listen with single mind." He then spoke the mantra:

*Tadyathā hara-hara-ra svāhā*

"I now proclaim how the evil Māra, relying on his power, manifested in various forms and came to confront the Bodhisattva. The Bodhisattva, having seen this, halted his army and, as if addressing brothers, spoke these words: 'For a thousand koṭis of kalpas I have sought bodhi, giving up my own body and life, and even sons and daughters, and even wealth and treasures, without any reluctance. I have made great vows and practiced this vigor and difficult austerities. For the sake of sentient beings I have undertaken altruistic conduct, with a compassionate and pitying mind, hoping to attain perfection.' When the evil Māra saw this, he came to create obstacles for me. At that time the Bodhisattva recollected this great vidyārāja Aparājita dhāraṇī, and by the power of that vidyā, caused Māra and his great army to be completely routed." He then spoke the mantra:

*Tadyathā iti-miti-tira-mira-miti-mileni svāhā*

"Thereupon the vidyārāja manifested in the form of a woman and came before the Bodhisattva. With palms joined above her head, she spoke this verse:

> "'Great wisdom has proclaimed it, like a great light illuminating all.
> And I, on this very day, have subdued Māra's army.
> As you in the past sought supreme perfect awakening,
> You attained great fearlessness, and Māra's army was subdued.'

"I now, by the power of that vidyā, have destroyed Māra, his great army, and all his retinue." He then spoke the mantra:

*Tadyathā kavaci-kavaṭṭha-mule na-kula-kuhara-māleni svāhā*

At that time the World-Honored One spoke this verse:

> "The Tathāgata, Perfectly Awakened One,
> Causes all the world
> To attain the supreme fruit.
> Poison cannot harm them,
> Weapons cannot wound them,
> Fire cannot burn them.
> Even in their sleep,
> They never have evil dreams.
> Whether imprisoned by a king,
> Or in the hands of bandits,
> Or on perilous mountain cliffs,
> Or even at rivers,
> From all fearful difficulties
> They are completely liberated.
> From all offenses, suffering, and torment,
> They are freed and at peace.
> The power of vidyā and the power of wisdom
> Constitute great awesome virtue.
> Whatever deeds are performed
> Can be swiftly accomplished."

He then spoke the mantra:

*Tadyathā sule-susule vara-va-vara manabhava-smara-hule svāhā māle svāhā*

The Buddha said: "When I formerly sought bodhi, in the life when I was a six-tusked white elephant, I protected the hunter: 'If this is true, may all sentient beings have all their sufferings completely removed.' In a past life when I was a turtle in the ocean, though my body was cut and sliced, my mind bore no anger: 'If this is true, may all sentient beings have all their illnesses completely eliminated.'" He then spoke the mantra:

*Tadyathā hile-mile-ni dhuru-ṃbha panuṃ svāhā*

The Buddha said: "In a past life when I sought bodhi, I gave sentient beings all manner of happiness. In my former life as a quail, captured by a hunter, I gave whatever was wanted, contemplating with a compassionate mind without generating anger." He then spoke the mantra:

*Tadyathā bhiri-dhu-dhu-dhuni mahā-nāte svāhā dhu-dhu-dhuni hici-mici-bhiciri svāhā*

The Buddha said: "When I was the Patience Ṛṣi, cultivating the pāramitā of patience, my mind was free from affliction. I regarded all sentient beings as newborn infants. When blood flowed from my body, it transformed into milk. When I practiced the perfection of giving in seeking bodhi, a brāhmaṇa came before me and begged for my head. I gave my head and even had my hair bound to a tree with a cord. At that time I generated the mind of compassion without anger, regarding the brāhmaṇa as a newborn infant, and I even gave away my wife and consorts. I did not seek the five desires, did not seek the sovereignty of a king, did not seek the wealth of heaven. Truly, for the sake of subduing Māra and his retinue, and even taming all enemies to attain the supreme position. To destroy them, I proclaim the great vidyā of great power, which gives birth to the bodhisattva conduct of the past. To subdue Māra, I now proclaim it again." He then spoke the mantra:

*Tadyathā kikiṇi vimadhaṇi vidhu-dhu-dhunī mahā-nāti raṇāvati mahā-varathāvati he-he-he-mimi-le śiśaṇi-śiśaṇi-śiśaṇi sīriṇi-mīriṇi mahā-mīriṇi lele-ṇi mīriṇi-ṇi svara-pavaniṇi titi-ṇi mittiriṇi sarva-kāma-ratha-sādhaṇi śatru-darśaṇi śvadāle-mahā-dāle-ni-pata-dāle kṣurūpa-me sahasa-radhale vimale ugrāti kṣiṇi mahā-tāpe śatru-bhayaṃ-kari pūjita-triśūla-ke śuni-ti yama-lūke śunīti viśūrūta siddha-jauti-triśūla-ke śvāṇi-jita brahma-mānaṇḍa-śupame siddhi-śakti-skandha-śāya viśūrūte*

"I proclaim for you the awesome virtue and power of this great vidyā. When the vigorous devas and the asuras faced each other in battle, the devas recollected this Aparājita, and the asuras were subdued of their own accord. When you vigorous devas confronted all the asuras, though the devas' lifespan was a thousand years, the asuras watched for an opportunity but could not prevail. If the true power of Aparājita is accomplished, one may go to the Brahmā Heaven. Thus, constantly performing various deeds, the awesome virtue of Aparājita cannot be surpassed even by Brahmā Heaven, and therefore it is worthy of offerings. This was proclaimed by all buddhas." He then spoke the mantra:

*Viśāle-vipule tikṣiṇi sutikṣiṇi agni-saṃ-nirśe amokhe apratihate me khavāyu-smat-māke-mahā-prābhe viniyu-ṇā-bhe viniyūta-rāṇā-bhe kṣāṇi agni-prābhe pravṛtte vāyu-vat-saṃ-pramāradhanī jvālita-jvālita vāyu-vat-saṃ-pramāradhanī jvālita-gni viṣṇu-vivara-vale anāle amokhe gule bhakta-tatri-kṣi biccha-gaṭsa-mānāya vaipanti bhūta-ruvanti deva-sura-yakṣa-rakṣasa-biśaca mahā-giri-sāgara-ṇi pravāta-cālanti kampanti kṣupanti śruta-vāṇi-da-nanuṃ mahā-māṭha-śaktira-śaktira kṣiṇi aniya-bhūtānāṃ māda-bhūtānāṃ trāsa-trāsa-tānāṃ śarādi visrā-cariṇi hari-hiṃga-lakṣira-kṣita-kṣirakta satra-dhāriṇi mahā-garva-varade patatai-juśakti-skandha-vālūya me vināśikā me bhava-śatru svāhā hari-hari-hiṃga-lakṣiya svini-yūtāya praviniyūta-bhāye dhumā-māriṇi-ye vaiśvānara-dharāye ajitāya pūrṇi-vadana-na-vaṃ-saṃ-graha-ya cicirici-ri-ciṇi-ci kiriti-ri-ciṇi he-he-he-he-he-he-he-he dhuru-vi-dhuru-ciriṇi-ci ke-sara-visara-paravāla jaja-jaja bhūñjaṃ prasarya-ca svāhā*

The Buddha said: "In the last watch of the night, at the first appearance of the sun, for the sake of the world I performed the most supreme practice and perfectly accomplished the buddha's deeds: I attained bodhi, subdued Māra's army, and turned the dharma wheel. Furthermore, a thousand koṭis of pure-abode devas came before me, generating the most profound aspiration, and with gentle voices requested the turning of the dharma wheel."

The Buddha told Śāriputra: "Listen carefully, Śāriputra! This great vidyārāja Aparājita benefits and brings happiness to all sentient beings. Śāriputra! In the past, the devas and asuras fought in battle. At that time the celestial host was routed and the asuras were victorious. Thereupon Śakra, Lord of the Devas, wrote this Aparājita dhāraṇī upon a golden leaf and placed it atop his banner, and again engaged in battle. At that time the celestial host was victorious, and the asura army was immediately routed." He then spoke the mantra:

*Tadyathā butsume-vasu-karate na-guṇāricā-kapāriṇi svāhā*

The Buddha told Śāriputra: "I see no devas, māras, or others, nor any human śramaṇas, brāhmaṇas, asuras, or any ghosts or specters, apasmāras, gandharvas, yakṣas, rākṣasas, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, vināyakas, pūtanas, kumbhāṇḍas, kaṭa-pūtanas, flesh-eaters, or others who would dare to transgress against this great vidyārāja Aparājita. As for this great vidyārāja dhāraṇī, those who neither see nor hear it are obstructed by karmic hindrances from past and present lives."

At that time, when the World-Honored One had proclaimed all the accomplishments of this dhāraṇī, Ānanda, Śāriputra, devas, humans, asuras, and others all single-mindedly received and upheld it, joyfully putting it into practice.
