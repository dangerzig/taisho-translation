# 佛說八大靈塔名號經
## Sūtra on the Names of the Eight Great Sacred Stūpas Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1685

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmabhadra (法賢).

---

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣus: "I shall now proclaim the names of the Eight Great Sacred Stūpas. Listen carefully, and I shall explain them to you. What are the eight? They are as follows: first, the Lumbinī Garden in the city of Kapilavastu, the place where the Buddha was born; second, beneath the Bodhi tree on the banks of the Nairañjanā River in the kingdom of Magadha, the place where the Buddha attained the fruit of the path; third, the city of Vārāṇasī in the kingdom of Kāśī, the place where the great Dharma wheel was turned; fourth, Jetavana in the kingdom of Śrāvastī, the place where great supernatural powers were displayed; fifth, the city of Kanyākubja, the place of descent from Trāyastriṃśa Heaven; sixth, the city of Rājagṛha, the place where the disciples were divided and the Buddha undertook their transformation; seventh, the sacred stūpa in the city of Vaiśālī, the place where the lifespan was contemplated; eighth, between the great twin trees in the śāla grove of the city of Kuśinagara, the place of entry into nirvāṇa. These are the Eight Great Sacred Stūpas." He then spoke again in verse:

> In King Śuddhodana's capital, the city of Kapilavastu,
> Lumbinī Garden, where the Buddha was born;
> Beside the Nairañjanā River in Magadha,
> Beneath the Bodhi tree, supreme awakening was attained;
> In the city of Vārāṇasī in the kingdom of Kāśī,
> The great Dharma wheel was turned in twelve aspects;
> Within Jetavana in the great city of Śrāvastī,
> Supernatural powers pervaded the three realms;
> In the city of Kanyākubja of the kingdom of Sāṃkāśya,
> Descent was made from the palace of Trāyastriṃśa Heaven;
> In the great city of Rājagṛha, the sangha was divided,
> And the Tathāgata skillfully guided them, practicing compassion;
> In the sacred stūpa of the great city of Vaiśālī,
> The Tathāgata contemplated the measure of his lifespan;
> In the city of Kuśinagara, the land of great power,
> Between the twin śāla trees, nirvāṇa was entered.

"These are the Eight Great Sacred Stūpas. If there are brāhmaṇas, good men, or good women who generate great faith and build stūpas and temples, serving and making offerings, such persons will obtain great benefit, attain great rewards, and receive widespread praise. Their renown will spread far and wide, profoundly and extensively. All bhikṣus should also study this.

"Furthermore, bhikṣus, if faithful good men and good women are able to make sincere offerings at these Eight Great Sacred Stūpas during this present life, upon the end of their lives they will swiftly be reborn in the heavenly realms."

At that time, the World-Honored One again addressed the bhikṣus: "Listen carefully. I shall now explain my travels and sojourns in various kingdoms and cities, and the duration of my stay in the world." He then spoke in verse:

> Twenty-nine years dwelling in the royal palace,
> Six years practicing austerities in the snowy mountains,
> Five years in Rājagṛha, teaching and transforming,
> Four years abiding in Vaiśālī,
> Two years in retreat at Jarī Cliff,
> Twenty-three years dwelling in Śrāvastī,
> Vaiśālī and the Deer Park,
> Makulī and Trāyastriṃśa Heaven,
> Śiśunāga and Kauśāmbī,
> The peak of Stūpa Mountain and the Great Wilderness,
> Veṇu village and Verañjā,
> King Śuddhodana's capital, the city of Kapilavastu...
> One year each at these holy sites,
> Śākyamuni Tathāgata sojourned and dwelled.
> Thus for eighty years remaining in the world,
> Thereafter the Muni entered nirvāṇa.
