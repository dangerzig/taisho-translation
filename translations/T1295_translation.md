# 供養護世八天法
## Method for Making Offerings to the Eight World-Protecting Devas

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1295

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, compiled by Faquán (法全).

---

The *Subāhu-paripṛcchā-sūtra* says: The practitioner who cultivates mantra practice should daily make offerings to the direction-protecting devas, and then they will be free from calamities and difficulties. Outside the place of practice, in the eight directions, spread kuśa grass, lotus leaves, or apply paste to round altars, making ten positions. On the left and right of Śakra, place Brahmā and the Earth Deva. If there is no place outside the practice-site to set up positions, then in a clean and open area before the place of practice, create a directional boundary. Within this, in the eight directions, with Brahmā and the Earth Deva placed at the center, make offerings to the devas of the ten directions. For the food offering, use mixed congee consisting of glutinous rice and sesame. First place a clean leaf, making it extremely clean, fragrant, and fine, and fill one vessel. Before each session, first place a clean leaf or a clean tea bowl, and first fill a clean vase with fragrant water. Then kneel with palms joined and invoke the world-devas of the ten directions.

I respectfully invite the Lord of the Eastern Direction, Kauśika, together with his retinue, to descend to this place. I have prepared these offerings; may you graciously accept them.

I respectfully invite Agni, Deva of Fire of the Southeast, together with his retinue, to descend to this place. I have prepared these offerings; may you graciously accept them.

I respectfully invite Yama, Lord of the Southern Direction; the Rākṣasa Lord; the Bhūta Deva King; the Water Deva; the Nāga King; the Wind Spirit King and others; Vaiśravaṇa, Heavenly King of Much Hearing; Īśāna, Heavenly King; and Mahābrahmā, Heavenly King.

I respectfully invite all the great spirit kings dwelling on the earth, together with their retinues, to descend to this place. I have prepared these offerings; may you graciously accept them.

I humbly pray that you may invisibly bestow protection upon me. Visualize each direction in your mind and make your petition.

After the invitations, next invoke the world-devas using their Sanskrit names.

**Īśāna** of the northeast, with his retinue, the bhūta hosts. Trident mudrā: samādhi fist with fire finger upright and wind finger bent back. The mantra of Īśāna:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ rudrāya svāhā.*

Visualize the direction-protecting heavenly kings each descending from their heavenly palaces to this place. Fix them in their proper directional positions with the mind.

**Śakra.** Mudrā: inner bond, with the two wind fingers extended straight like needles. The mantra of Śakra, Heavenly King:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ śakrāya svāhā.*

**Agni,** the Fire Sage. Concentration-palm: fire and space fingers pinched together in a triangle. Wisdom hand: four wheel fingers upright, space finger placed horizontally across the palm, wind finger bent in three beckoning gestures. The mantra of the Fire Deva:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ agnaye svāhā.*

**Yama.** Concentration and wisdom hands joined, earth and wind fingers turned back into the moon. The mantra of King Yama:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ vavasvatāya svāhā.*

**Rākṣasa.** In a fearsome form, in the southwest direction, holding a sword. The mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ rākṣasa-adhipataya svāhā.*

**Water Deva.** Holding a lasso. The mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ apāpataye svāhā.*

**Vāyu,** the Wind Deva. Banner: wisdom fist, with earth and water fingers upright. The mantra of the Wind Deva:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ vāyave svāhā.*

**Vaiśravaṇa.** Hollow joined palms, two earth fingers crossed entering the palms, space fingers upright, wind fingers bent sideways about an inch apart without touching. The mantra of Vaiśravaṇa, Heavenly King:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ vaiśravaṇāya svāhā.*

**Brahmā.** Holding a red lotus. Samādhi hand: space finger holds water finger. The mantra of the Brahmā King:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ prajāpataye svāhā.*

**Earth Deva.** Holding a jeweled vase. The mantra of the Earth Deva:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ pṛthivyai svāhā.*

**Sun Deva.** Fortune and wisdom hands face up, water fingers enter space fingers holding a banner, fire and wheel fingers about to touch, water and fire fingers hold each other. The mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ ādityāya svāhā.*

**Moon Deva.** Samādhi hand holding a white lotus flower, space finger pinching the first joint of the fire finger. The mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ candrāya svāhā.*

Invocation by name and respectful address: Direction-protecting heavenly kings, the twelve zodiacal spirits, the nine grahas and great devas, the twenty-eight lunar mansions, the karmic lords of the dark courts and the lords of one's natal constellation: I have now encountered this calamity and am being afflicted by such-and-such a matter. I respectfully salute the hosts of devas and, in accordance with the Buddha's teaching and command, invite you to accept my welcome and all come to attend this gathering. With single-minded sincerity, give rise to joyful hearts, and on behalf of me, so-and-so, eliminate calamitous obstacles and increase blessings and longevity. Or one may say: Beginning from today, I undertake the practice of mantra. By the power of the great sage's mindful protection, may I deliver myself and all sentient beings from the suffering karma of saṃsāra. I humbly pray that the world-protecting heavenly lords and the constellations traveling through space, in accordance with the Buddha's command, may bestow fearlessness upon me and allow me to dwell in peace and happiness.

Next, one should offer clean fragrant water, pouring a little water onto a leaf to present. The mantra:

*Oṃ amṛte hūṃ phaṭ.*

Mantra for scented paste. Flick with the middle and ring fingers, and offer a small amount of scented paste as a presentation.

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ viśuddha-gandha-udbhava svāhā.*

Mantra for flower garlands. Inverted lotus palms, placed at the forehead, rotating clockwise.

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ mahā-maitrīya-abhyudgate svāhā.*

Mantra for burning incense. Earth, water, and fire fingers touching back to back, two wind fingers joined at the sides, space finger pinching the upper joint of the wind finger wheel and opening.

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ dharma-dhātv-anugate svāhā.*

Mantra for food and drink. Sealed palms joined.

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ arara karara malin-danda malin-dade mahā-bali svāhā.*

Mantra for lamp-light. Right feather hand forms a fist with the fire finger upright, space finger pressing the wind finger to pinch the middle joint of the fire finger.

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ tathāgata-arci-spharaṇa-avabhāsana-gagana-udaya svāhā.*

The mantra of the universal offering of Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva:

*Namaḥ sarva-tathāgatebhyaḥ viśva-mukhebhyaḥ sarva-thā-khaṃ udgate spharaṇa-imaṃ gagana-khaṃ svāhā.*

Visualize the direction-protecting heavenly kings each descending from their heavenly palaces to this place. Fix them in their proper directional positions with the mind. Invocation by name and respectful address: Direction-protecting heavenly kings, the twelve zodiacal spirits, the nine grahas and great devas, the twenty-eight lunar mansions, the karmic lords of the dark courts and the lords of one's natal constellation: I have now encountered this calamity and am being afflicted by such-and-such a matter. I respectfully salute the hosts of devas and, in accordance with the Buddha's teaching and command, invite you to accept my welcome and all come to attend this gathering. With single-minded sincerity, give rise to joyful hearts, and on behalf of me, so-and-so, eliminate calamitous obstacles and increase blessings and longevity. By the power of the great sage's mindful protection, may I deliver myself and all sentient beings from the suffering karma of saṃsāra. I humbly pray that the world-protecting heavenly lords and the constellations traveling through space, by reason of the Buddha's command, may bestow fearlessness upon me and allow me to dwell in peace and happiness. After the invocation is finished, next one should offer clean fragrant water from the vase, presented on a leaf. The mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ indrāya arghaṃ.*

Next, flick with the middle and ring fingers and offer a small amount of scented paste, saying upon presentation "gandhaṃ."

Next, place one flower upon the seat and present it, calling it "puṣpaṃ." Burn stick incense. Before the seat, the Sanskrit term for burning incense is "dhūpaṃ."

Next, ladle one spoonful of congee and place it on the leaf, and say "naivedyaṃ." Next, insert a small wax candle into the congee. In Sanskrit: *nīpaṃ pūjā-śrī-deva-putra-śye śāntiṃ kuru svāhā.*

For each position, from the water to the candle, after the offering is complete, move on to the next. For the candle, one should intend that it not go out until all positions have been served. It may be necessary to have assistants and attendants, several persons, each carrying out one task to assist with the offering. If one takes each item by oneself, the candle may not last through the entire ritual, which would seem inauspicious. Then repeat the petition for one's request. One may circumambulate, or else sit quietly and recite the Mahāśrī mantra twenty-one times, and further recite the Tathāgata's Compassionate Protection mantra one hundred and eight times. Dedicate the merit of this recitation to benefit the devas, praying that the devas' happiness may increase and become ever more excellent. During the offerings, many things may not be done according to proper form; may you with compassion bestow joy upon me. The master should hold the censer and universally request joy and ask for freedom from calamity. Speak a verse to clearly send off the heavenly lords: "By the sovereign and unimpeded divine power of the devas, may what is now sought be swiftly accomplished. May the heavenly lords return to their original palaces, and when invoked again, may you compassionately attend."

*Oṃ kili-kili vajra-muṣṭi svāhā.*

Next, distribute food to all ghosts and spirits. The *Subāhu-paripṛcchā-sūtra* says: "Furthermore, Subāhu! If a person who recites mantras wishes to achieve accomplishment and practice all dharma-methods free from obstacles and difficulties, attaining siddhi, they should sacrifice various foods and beverages to the devas, asuras, yakṣas, nāga kings, garuḍas, jīvañjīvakas, kaṭapūtanas, gandharvas, bhūtas, and various ghost-spirits, whether dwelling on the ground or in space, so that the mantra-practitioner will be free from all difficulties and all their wishes and aspirations will be completely fulfilled." Firmly form the mudrā and seal of protection. Place the right knee on the ground and speak the invocation:

"Those dwelling on Mount Sumeru, the deva and bhūta hosts; those dwelling in the Joyous Garden and other heavenly palaces; those dwelling in the sun and moon; those dwelling in rivers, seas, ponds, marshes, springs, and wells; those dwelling in villages; those dwelling in spirit temples; those dwelling in empty rooms; those dwelling in deva chambers; those dwelling in saṃghārāmas and caityas; those dwelling in non-Buddhist hermitages of grass; those dwelling in homes; those dwelling in storehouses; those dwelling in streets and alleys; those dwelling at crossroads; those depending on solitary trees or on main roads; those dwelling among graves; those dwelling in śītavana charnel grounds; those sojourning in great forests; those dwelling in places where lions and tigers roam; those dwelling in great sandy deserts; those dwelling on sea islands and other excellent places: all of you with your retinues, descend and come to this place! I have prepared flower garlands, scented paste, food and drink, and fine lamp-light. May you graciously be present. May the result I seek be fulfilled." Then, in a copper vessel or clean basin, place various sweet and delicious foods, fruits, sweet and crisp items, all broken into pieces. Recite the mantra for giving food to flaming-mouth hungry ghosts, giving rise to a mind of compassion. Press the basin with one's own hand and empower it twenty-one times. Then recite the mantra of the Tathāgata of Wondrous Form and empower water to sprinkle. Then recite the universal offering seal and mantra, sincerely visualizing the offered flowers, incense, food, and drink as vast, numerous, and exquisitely fine. Then recite the verse:

> By the merit I have cultivated,
> May it be universally bestowed upon the ghost realm.
> Having eaten and been freed from the suffering of hunger,
> May they abandon their bodies and be reborn in places of happiness.

Then take it outside the barrier or to the direction of the dwelling and scatter it as an offering, or offer it into flowing water, giving it to the nameless ghosts and spirits to eat. The mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ indrāya gṛhṇa imaṃ arghaṃ gandhaṃ puṣpaṃ dhūpaṃ naivedyaṃ nīpaṃ pūjā-śrī-deva-putra-śye śāntiṃ kuru svāhā.*

In summary: "I take refuge in all the buddhas and in Śakra. Accept this argha, scented paste, flower garland, burnt incense, congee, and fine lamp-light as offerings. May the Auspicious Deva-son bring peace from calamity to so-and-so, increase blessings and wisdom, and bring auspicious fulfillment." Therefore it concludes with svāhā.

Offering mantras, mudrās, and seals for the ten directional heavenly kings, etc.

Whenever one has received the dharma-methods, or has completed a session of mantra recitation, or has finished a homa or abhiṣeka, one must always make offerings to the devas, and one will swiftly be free from difficulties. Verses:

> Contemplate the mind arising from the dharma-dhātu,
> Accomplishing the original wrathful altar:
> A three-cornered nāga form,
> Fiercely blazing, reaching up to the Brahmā heavens.
>
> The three families in sequence; draw in with the lasso and summon from the four directions.
> Accomplish the body with three recitations,
> Or alternatively, just once.
>
> When the affair is finished and the vow has been made,
> Afterwards form the mudrā of the lord of each direction.
> Recite each one's original mantra
> To send off the holy direction-protecting devas.
>
> When the recitation is completed according to the method,
> And the hymns of praise and vows are finished,
> Release the boundary and send off the holy devas,
> Then exit the place of practice.
>
> In the eight corners, apply paste to round altars
> To serve as ten holy positions.
> On the left and right of Śakra,
> Place Brahmā and the Earth Deva.
>
> Or in the dragon direction,
> Place Brahmā and Earth facing each other.
> The positions of the Sun Deva and Moon Deva,
> With grahas and mansions all following their courses.
>
> In the central position, visualize the four-armed
> Acala, Wrathful King:
> Both hands in vajra fists,
> Wind and earth fingers curved like hooks,
> Placed at either side of the mouth.
> The right hand hooks, the left holds a lasso.
> Mount Sumeru serves as his seat,
> With eight peaks appearing outside the body.
>
> Sharp vajra wheels,
> Vast and full, of deep blue color,
> Disheveled hair hanging down like a lasso,
> Majestic flames like the conflagration at the end of a kalpa.
>
> His power is like Garuḍa, blazing and brilliant, none able to withstand him.
> Māra and Pāpīyas are terrified and flee.
> By the power of his ancient great vow,
> He manifests this fierce and wrathful form,
> Swallowing and destroying all obstacles,
> Comforting the practitioner.
>
> When the directional positions have been arranged,
> Make offerings with mixed congee:
> Glutinous rice and sesame
> Cooked together with mung beans.
>
> Then at each altar position
> Place one ceramic vessel.
> First, with clean fragrant water,
> Pour a little into the vessel.
>
> Next, present the scented paste,
> Flicking it with the fingers.
> Then present the various fine flowers,
> And next, burn the incense.
>
> Then ladle one spoonful of congee,
> All with a sincere and weighty mind,
> Empowering each three times.
> Next take a small wax candle
> And insert it into the congee.
> Until the affair is finished,
> Do not let this candle go out.
>
> Upon the line of consummation,
> Add the words of one's prayer.
> By the holy one's secret empowerment,
> All that is done will achieve siddhi.
> This is an aid to the practice;
> Even when practiced alone, the prayer will be fulfilled.
>
> When one wishes to invite and to offer,
> Fix the mind to secure each position.
> Make offerings according to the method,
> With determination and without doubt;
> All prayers will be accomplished.
>
> Among the nine grahas, the Sun is chief.
> Among the constellations, the Moon is chief.
> Brahmā, Earth, and the mudrā:
> These will now be explained in order.
>
> Eight-petaled padma,
> Brahmā's mudrā:
> Ten fingers like an argha vessel.
> The Earth Deva's mudrā:
> With the concentration hand, grasp the wrist of the wisdom hand.
> The wisdom hand extends all five fingers upright,
> The space finger enters the moon horizontally,
> The wind finger hooks back and forth.
>
> The Fire Deva's invitation and dismissal mudrā:
> Take the meditation finger and pinch the discipline finger,
> Flicking outward three times.
> This is the dismissal mudrā.

The mantras are as stated in the sūtras.

Praising the merit of the Tathāgata's ten epithets in Sanskrit:

*Tathāgata-arhat-samyak-saṃbuddha-vidyā-caraṇa-saṃpanna-sugata-lokavid-anuttara-puruṣa-damya-sārathi-śāstā-deva-manuṣyānāṃ-ca buddha-bhagavān.*

## Order of the Deva Offerings

First, outside the place of practice, spread kuśa grass, lotus leaves, or green grass, and apply paste to round altars while preparing the six kinds of implements including wax candles. Next, take one's seat. Perform the three-family armor and body-protection. Next, empower the fragrant water. Next, use the karma mudrā and empower seven times. Next, empower the altar. Next, purify the ground. Next, make the five great vows, state the reason for the affair, and recite the Prajñā sūtra to benefit the devas. Next, perform the Mahācakra Vajra mudrā and mantra. Visualize within the ground-altar the syllable transforming into a single-pronged vajra, and the vajra transforming into the four-armed Vidyārāja. Next, the four-syllable mantra: visualize the originally abiding honored one empowering the altar-honored one, merging into nonduality. Next, invoke from the appropriate direction, reciting the great summoning-hook mudrā and mantra. The mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ aḥ sarva-tatra pratihatiye tathāgata-kuśa-cchaḍi carya paripūraka svāhā.*

Next, recite the Sanskrit invocation. Next, kneel and recite the Chinese petition. Next, recite the Four Wisdoms hymn of praise.

Next, present the argha offering to Acala Vidyārāja. Next, the seat mudrā. Next, the net. Next, the flames. Next, the scented paste, flowers, and burnt incense, as in the usual recitation and offering to the Acala Honored One. Next, present the offerings one by one to each of the devas.

Next, the universal offering mantra. Next, form the mudrā and empower the five bodily locations. Next, recite Acala's Compassionate Rescue mantra one hundred and eight times. Pray earnestly. When the count is complete, as before, form the protective seal and make confession. Next, recite the mantra of the presiding deva one hundred and eight times, or one thousand and eighty times; this may be recited as an abhiṣeka to protect the patron. Next, recite the Tathāgata's Compassionate Protection mantra one hundred and eight times. Then recite the Mahāśrī Deva mantra one hundred and eight times.

Next, make the final offering and presentation to Acala Vidyārāja. Next, recite the universal offering mantra to make offerings to the Vidyārāja, the ocean-assembly of the dust-realm honored ones, and the world-protecting devas. Next, the Four Wisdoms hymn and the hymn of praise to the devas, nāgas, and eightfold assembly. Next, the dedication of merit. Next, release the boundary, undoing each element as in the usual procedure. Next, the argha. Next, the dismissal. Next, the three-family body-protection as at the beginning, and then exit the place of practice.
