# 佛說禪行三十七品經
## Sūtra of Meditation Practice on the Thirty-Seven Factors, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0604

Translated from the Chinese. Later Han dynasty, translated by An Shigao (安世高)

---

Thus have I heard. At one time the Buddha was traveling in the land of Śrāvastī, at the Jetavana Monastery of Anāthapiṇḍada. The Buddha said: "Bhikṣus! If one can, in the time of a finger-snap, practice contemplation of the body within the body, contemplation of the body without, and contemplation of the body within and without, discriminating with mindfulness and understanding the delusion and affliction of the world: this is called diligence, practicing in accordance with the Buddha's teaching, and not being a fool who eats others' offerings in vain. How much more so for one who can practice extensively! To summarize the essential: if in the time of a finger-snap one practices contemplation of feelings, contemplation of the mind, and contemplation of dharmas, within and without, discriminating with mindfulness and understanding the delusion and affliction of the world, all is as stated above. How much more so for one who can practice extensively! Therefore one should be mindful of practicing the four foundations of mindfulness.

"Bhikṣus! If in the time of a finger-snap one practices preventing evil dharmas that have not yet arisen from arising, exhorting the mind and governing one's conduct, being diligent and focused: this is called diligently practicing meditation, practicing in accordance with the Buddha's teaching, and not being a fool who eats others' offerings in vain. How much more so for one who can practice extensively! To summarize the essential: if in the time of a finger-snap one practices severing evil dharmas that have already arisen, or practices causing good dharmas that have not yet arisen to arise, or practices establishing good dharmas that have already arisen so they are not forgotten, increasing one's practice to bring them to fullness, exhorting the mind and governing one's conduct, being diligent and focused: all is as stated above. How much more so for one who can practice extensively! Therefore one should be mindful of practicing the four right efforts.

"Bhikṣus! If in the time of a finger-snap one practices the concentration of desire, severing birth and death, contemplating the supernatural powers: this is the most diligent practice of meditation, practicing in accordance with the Buddha's teaching, and not being a fool who eats others' offerings in vain. How much more so for one who can practice extensively! To summarize the essential: if in the time of a finger-snap one practices the concentration of diligence, or the concentration of the mind, or the concentration of investigation, severing birth and death, contemplating the supernatural powers: all is as stated above. How much more so for one who can practice extensively! Therefore one should be mindful of practicing the four bases of supernatural power.

"Bhikṣus! If in the time of a finger-snap one practices the faculty of faith, seeing the four occasions of joy, not departing from the Buddha, the Dharma, the assembly, and the precepts: this is called diligently practicing meditation, practicing in accordance with the Buddha's teaching, and not being a fool who eats others' offerings in vain. How much more so for one who can practice extensively! To summarize the essential: if in the time of a finger-snap one practices the faculty of diligence, seeing the four right efforts; or the faculty of mindfulness, seeing the four foundations of mindfulness; or the faculty of concentration, seeing the four dhyānas; or the faculty of wisdom, seeing the Four Noble Truths: all is as stated above. How much more so for one who can practice extensively! Therefore one should be mindful of practicing the five faculties.

"Bhikṣus! If in the time of a finger-snap one practices the power of faith, through which one obtains the four occasions of joy and becomes indestructible: this is called diligently practicing meditation, practicing in accordance with the Buddha's teaching, and not being a fool who eats others' offerings in vain. How much more so for one who can practice extensively! If in the time of a finger-snap one practices the power of diligence, the power of mindfulness, the power of concentration, or the power of wisdom: all is as stated above. How much more so for one who can practice extensively! Therefore one should be mindful of practicing the five powers.

"Bhikṣus! If in the time of a finger-snap one practices the awakening factor of mindfulness, being mindful of what should be minded, with loving-mindfulness, with right mindfulness, with mindfulness of wholesome dharmas, attaining aspiration without forgetting: this is called diligently practicing meditation, practicing in accordance with the Buddha's teaching, and not being a fool who eats others' offerings in vain. How much more so for one who can practice extensively! To summarize the essential: if in the time of a finger-snap one practices the awakening factor of investigation of dharmas, with a mind that discriminates sūtra after sūtra, understands and follows the teaching; or the awakening factor of diligence, with body diligent and mind also diligent; or the awakening factor of joy, knowing what should be delighted in, causing the mind to obtain happiness; or the awakening factor of tranquility, causing the body to be at rest and the mind also at rest; or the awakening factor of concentration, causing the mind to abide and mindfulness also to abide, with aspiration undistracted and free from wrong thought; or the awakening factor of equanimity, practicing equanimity and knowing what is to be minded, knowing how to stabilize the body, causing one to see the Way, guarding against evil thoughts, and practicing in peace: all is as stated above. How much more so for one who can practice extensively! Therefore one should be mindful of practicing the seven factors of awakening.

"Bhikṣus! If in the time of a finger-snap one practices right view, knowing the ancient, knowing the beginning and the end, knowing the inner and the outer, knowing suffering, knowing its accumulation, knowing its cessation, knowing the path, knowing the Buddha, knowing the Dharma, knowing the bhikṣu-saṃgha, knowing the practices to be learned, as the six directions and what is to be cultivated and grasped, and the arising, transformation, and loss of delight, and its destination, and knowing the virtue of non-attachment: this is called right view, diligently practicing meditation, practicing in accordance with the Buddha's teaching, and not being a fool who eats others' offerings in vain. How much more so for one who can practice extensively! To summarize the essential: if in the time of a finger-snap one practices right intention, intending renunciation, intending non-contention, intending non-killing; or right speech, not speaking falsely, not speaking with a forked tongue, not speaking harshly, not speaking mockingly; or right livelihood, not making a living through greed, not making a living through anger, not making a living through delusion; or right action, not killing, not stealing, not engaging in sexual misconduct; or right effort, cultivating and governing the matters of the four right efforts; or right mindfulness, receiving and practicing the four foundations of mindfulness; or right concentration, contemplating and mindful of the matters of the four dhyānas: all is the same as stated above. The merit of even a finger-snap is such as this. How much more so for one who can practice extensively! Therefore one should be mindful of practicing the noble Eightfold Path."

When the Buddha had spoken this, all received it with joy.
