# 淨瑠璃淨土摽
## Description of the Pure Lapis Lazuli Pure Land

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0929

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown

---

Bhaiṣajyaguru Vaiḍūrya-prabhā Tathāgata! His entire body is of a light blue-green color. He sits upon a hundred-jeweled lotus lion-throne within a jeweled cave, in the samādhi of great emptiness, radiating innumerable rays of light. Within each ray, the innumerable emanation-Buddhas all display the mark of the samādhi of great emptiness. His right arm is raised with the palm open, the hand relaxed with fingers spread like opening petals. His left hand rests below the navel, the little finger hooked. He is called the Great Jewel Lapis Lazuli Medicine.

The ground is the color of dark lapis lazuli. Innumerable jeweled flower-trees, as many as dust-motes, bear leaves of various shapes. Some are in the form of Buddhas, radiating immeasurable light, called Buddha-pāramitā trees. Some are in the form of bodhisattvas, producing immeasurable music, called samādhi-dhāraṇī trees. Some are in the form of golden halls, emitting immeasurable clouds of a hundred treasures, called bodhi-majesty trees. Some are in the form of jeweled stūpas, producing immeasurable celestial hosts, called awakening-wisdom trees and vast sun-rain-wheel trees. Immeasurable hundreds of thousands of banners and pennants stand in rows. Immeasurable jeweled nets, jeweled garlands, bells, and chimes provide adornment. Before the Buddha, to the left and right, monastics of the four types who have gone forth are all in samādhi.

**To the left, first tier:** Vajragarbha-rāja Bodhisattva! His entire body is a light reddish-blue color. He sits upon a jeweled lotus platform. In his right hand, at the center of the palm, stands upright a three-pronged vajra. His left arm is bent with five fingers raised, the thumb pressing upon the nail of the little finger. Bodhisattvas as numerous as dust-motes serve as his attendants, namely: Cloud-Fragrance Bodhisattva, Flower-Jade Bodhisattva, Vajra-Añjali Bodhisattva, Fire-Head Celestial-Form Bodhisattva, Immortal-Form Bodhisattva, and others.

**To the right, first tier:** Padma-dhvaja Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva! His entire body is a light blue color. He sits upon a jeweled lotus platform. In his left hand he holds a cintāmaṇi, with a vajra placed upon a white jeweled lotus platform. His right hand forms the wish-granting mudrā. Bodhisattvas as numerous as dust-motes serve as his attendants, namely: Blue-Lotus Bodhisattva, Three-Pronged Vajra Bodhisattva, Single-Pronged Vajra-Bell Bodhisattva, and others.

**To the left, second tier:** Sūryaprabha Bodhisattva! His entire body is a reddish-crimson color. In his left palm rests a sun-disc. In his right hand he holds red māñjūṣaka flowers, some fully open, some half-open, some still in bud, or their jeweled fruits. Bodhisattvas as numerous as dust-motes serve as his attendants, namely: bodhisattvas holding censers, bodhisattvas holding great māñjūṣaka red flowers, and others.

**To the right, second tier:** Candraprabha Bodhisattva! His entire body is a pinkish-white color. In his left palm rests a moon-disc. In his right hand he holds red and white lotuses. Bodhisattvas as numerous as dust-motes serve as his attendants, namely: bodhisattvas holding hooks, bodhisattvas holding hook-bells, and others.

Behind and near the Tathāgata are the ten great disciples and twenty-one, thirty-five, and other numbers of arhats, each with innumerable śrāvaka assemblies as retinue. The chief figures each hold their own implements according to their characteristic forms. The retinues stand to the right of the Dharma-recipient, bearing the Dharma-form, arranged to the left, right, and behind, looking upward. To the left, right, and behind are innumerable immortals and celestial maidens offering seasonal flowers.

In the outer court before the Buddha, to the left and right, are innumerable celestial hosts in musical forms: gandharva women offering the music of celestial harps; kiṃnara figures playing golden drums from the heart; garuḍa women offering the music of celestial flutes; earth-goddess women all playing drums; asura women offering celestial drum music; mahoraga women offering the music of mouth-organs; and the great host of Māra's retinue offering celestial dance music. In the outer court, above and below and in the eight directions, are the Twelve Yakṣa Generals, with innumerable vajra-youth hosts as retinue. The forms of the Twelve Yakṣa Generals are as follows:

**First: Kumbhīra.** In the Īśāna direction. The terrifying great general! His color is yellow. He rides a tiger, holds a khaḍga sword, and his right fist presses against his left waist. (Some say he is placed in the northeast.)

**Second: Vajra.** In the Indra direction. The Vajra Great General! His color is blue with red eyes. He rides a monkey and holds a three-pronged vajra. His left hand is bent with fingers raised and facing left. (Some say he is placed in the east.)

**Third: Mihira.** In the Agni direction. The Dharma-Protecting Great General! His color is tinged with flames above and blue smoke. He rides a dragon and holds a flaming-bannered spear, wielded with both hands. (Some say he is placed in the southeast.)

**Fourth: Aṇḍīra.** In the direction of Agata. The Companionship-Protecting Great General! His color is tinged with flames above and yellow smoke. He rides a serpent and holds a khaḍga battle-axe. His left fist extends the wind-finger toward the fire-jewel. (Some say he is placed in the south.)

**Fifth: Anila.** In the Yama direction. The True-Dharma Great General! His color is red with blazing flames, thunder-eyes and fire-hair. He rides a two-winged horse. In his left hand he holds a mirror-stand; in his right he holds a flaming mirror. (Some say he is placed in the southwest.)

**Sixth: Śāṇḍilya.** In the Nirṛti direction. The Rākṣasa Great General! His color is yellowish-red, in rākṣasa form. He rides a ram. In his left hand he holds a mirror; in his right a khaḍga sword, or he holds a trident. (Some say he is placed in the west.)

**Seventh: Indra.** In the Nirṛti direction. The Imperial-Messenger Great General! His color is yellow. He rides a mount. In his left hand he holds a white whisk; in his right he holds a bell with a gazing jewel. (Some say he is placed between the west and north.)

**Eighth: Pajra.** In the Cāmuṇḍā direction. The Wolf-Dragon Great General! His color is mottled white. He rides a garuḍa bird. In his left hand he holds a wish-fulfilling jewel; in his right he holds a vajra bell. (Some say he is placed in the north.)

**Ninth: Mahoraga.** In the west direction. A great general! (Some call him the Wind-Conquering Great General!) He rides a dog, a fearsome dog known as the celestial hound. His left fist extends the wind-finger toward the wheel. His right hand bends the wind-finger. His color is like a black wind. (Some say he is placed above.)

**Tenth: Sindūra.** In the binding direction. The Water-Conquering Great General! He rides a boar. In his right hand he holds a hook; his left bends toward the earth and water. His color is like black water. (Some say he is placed below.)

**Eleventh: Catura.** In the Vaiśravaṇa direction. The World-Protecting Great General! Also called the Maṇi Great General! He rides a rat. In his right hand he holds a wish-fulfilling jewel; in his left he holds a lasso. His color is like a summer day's black clouds. (Some say he is placed in the west.)

**Twelfth: Vikarāla.** In the Īśāna direction. The Diligent-Wrathful Great General! He rides an ox, a water-buffalo. In his hands he holds a jeweled bow and arrows. His color is dark blue, slightly lighter than the preceding color. (Some say he is placed in the Guan direction.)

Before the Buddha, as inner guardians, are kalaviṅka birds and white swans. These birds speak the Dharma in the same voice as the five chief figures above; hence they are called birds of shared life.

This concludes the description of the Pure Lapis Lazuli Land.
