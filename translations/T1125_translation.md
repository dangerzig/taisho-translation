# T1125 金剛頂瑜伽金剛薩埵五祕密修行念誦儀軌

# Vajraśekhara Yoga Vajrasattva Five Secrets Practice Recitation Ritual Manual

*Note: This is an esoteric ritual manual (yìguǐ 儀軌) translated by Amoghavajra (Bùkōng 不空) during the Tang dynasty. It presents the Five Secrets (wǔmì 五密) practice of Vajrasattva (Jīngāng sàduǒ 金剛薩埵), a rapid-attainment method from the Vajraśekhara (Jīngāngdǐng 金剛頂) tradition. The text argues that esoteric maṇḍala initiation enables realization in a single lifetime, in contrast to the three incalculable kalpas required by the exoteric path. The five secrets are embodied in Vajrasattva and four attendant deities: Desire Vajra (Yù jīngāng 欲金剛), Kelikila Vajra (Jìlǐjìluó jīngāng 計里計羅金剛), Love Vajra (Ài jīngāng 愛金剛), and Pride Vajra (Màn jīngāng 慢金剛), corresponding to the five wisdoms and five buddhas. The manual includes extensive mudrā and mantra sequences for the complete sādhana.*

---

As the *Vajraśekhara Sūtra* (Jīngāngdǐng jīng 金剛頂經) in one hundred thousand verses and eighteen assemblies of yoga sets forth, it reveals the inner merit and secret essentials of the Tathāgata's sudden realization. Those who practice the bodhisattva path and attain supreme bodhi take benefiting and bringing peace to all sentient beings as the wondrous path. All sentient beings are submerged in and transmigrate through the five destinies and three realms. If they do not enter the maṇḍala of the five divisions and five secrets and do not receive the three kinds of secret empowerment, then with their defiled three karmas they could deliver boundless sentient beings: this is impossible. The sentient beings of the five destinies are encompassed by the three realms, namely the desire realm, form realm, and formless realm. In the form and formless realms, the path of transcending the three realms is practiced, with separate liberation, concentration, and wisdom as superior conditions, for those upper two realms are subsumed under the ground of concentration. The desire realm lacks dhyāna and is the ground of scattered good. Even if there are rules for practicing concentration, one must still rely on ascetic austerities and the seven expedient means. Due to weak faculties, even the fruit of an arhat or pratyekabuddha is difficult to achieve; how much less the great Samantabhadra ground of the ten bhūmis and the realization of Vairocana's three-body universal-light stage! Persons of the two vehicles, though they realize the fruits of the path, cannot bring benefit and peace to boundless sentient beings. Those who practice within the exoteric teaching do so for a very long time, through three great incalculable kalpas, and only afterward realize supreme bodhi. During that interval, nine out of ten fall back; some, upon reaching the seventh bhūmi, redirect the merit and wisdom they have accumulated toward the śrāvaka or pratyekabuddha path and still cannot realize supreme bodhi.

If one relies on the inner realization and self-awakened holy wisdom Dharma spoken by Vairocana Buddha's self-enjoyment body, and the wisdom of the great Samantabhadra Vajrasattva's enjoyment-for-others body, then in this present life one encounters a maṇḍala ācārya, enters the maṇḍala, and receives the complete karmic rites. By means of the Samantabhadra samādhi, Vajrasattva is drawn to enter the practitioner's body. Through the power of empowerment and divine might, in an instant one realizes immeasurable samaya and immeasurable dhāraṇī gates. By inconceivable Dharma, the disciple's innate self-grasping and dharma-grasping seeds are transformed, and immediately one accumulates the merit and wisdom of one great incalculable kalpa within the body. One is then born into the Buddha's family; such a person is born from the hearts of all tathāgatas, born from the Buddha's mouth, born from the Buddha-Dharma, born through transformation from the Dharma, and receives the Buddha's Dharma-wealth. Merely seeing the maṇḍala, if one can produce pure faith for even an instant and gaze upon it with a joyful mind, then in the ālaya-consciousness the vajradhātu seed is planted. One receives the abhiṣeka and investiture with a vajra name. From this point onward, one receives the vast, profound, inconceivable Dharma and transcends the two vehicles and the ten bhūmis.

This Dharma gate of the great Vajrasattva Five Secrets Yoga should be practiced with uninterrupted mental engagement throughout the four periods, in all four postures of walking, standing, sitting, and lying down. In the experiential domains of seeing, hearing, sensing, and knowing, the two graspings of self and dharma are entirely equalized. In this very life one realizes the first bhūmi and gradually advances. Through practicing the five secrets, one is neither stained by nirvāṇa nor attached to saṃsāra. Throughout the boundless five destinies and births and deaths, one broadly performs benefit and joy, dividing the body into a hundred koṭis to travel among the destinies and bring sentient beings to accomplishment, enabling them to realize the stage of Vajrasattva.

## The Practice

The yogin, in a quiet mountain forest, or in a meditation chamber, or in any place of delight, should bow to the four directional tathāgatas and make bodily offerings while reciting the root mantra. By offering the body, one relinquishes the defiled substance of the three karmas and thereby receives the precept of unobstructed discipline across the three times.

Next, one visualizes in space the assembly of all buddhas and bodhisattvas. Then, with right knee on the ground, one forms the vajra-arising mudrā and recites its mantra. One should contemplate, urging all tathāgatas not to abide in the present enjoyment of tranquility, beseeching their compassion, asking them not to abandon their original vows but to empower and protect.

Before the assembly of noble ones, one confesses, rejoices, and entreats, then makes the five great vows. One then forms the Vajrasattva cross-legged posture, with the right foot pressing the left.

One should form the concentration mudrā and recite the supreme, perfect bodhi-mind mantra:

"Oṃ sarva-yogacitta-utpādanāya mi."

By reciting this mantra, all tathāgatas cause the yogin to attain irreversibility, to vanquish all māra-enemies. Such a person is equal to the great bodhisattvas and all tathāgatas.

The yogin should contemplate thus: "I should generate the great courageous mind of Vajrasattva. All sentient beings possess the tathāgata-garbha nature, for Samantabhadra Bodhisattva pervades all sentient beings. I shall cause all beings to realize the stage of Vajrasattva."

One further contemplates: "All sentient beings possess the vajra-treasury nature and will certainly receive the vajra abhiṣeka in the future. I shall cause all sentient beings to swiftly attain the great bodhisattva abhiṣeka ground and realize the stage of Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva."

One further contemplates: "All sentient beings possess the wonderful Dharma-treasury nature and can turn all speech. I shall cause all beings to hear the entire Mahāyāna sūtra-piṭaka and realize the stage of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva."

One further contemplates: "All sentient beings possess the karma-treasury nature and are skilled in accomplishing all activities. I shall cause all beings to make vast offerings to all tathāgatas and realize the stage of Viśvakarman Bodhisattva."

One further contemplates: "All sentient beings already possess these four treasury natures and have obtained the bodies of the four great bodhisattvas. By the power of my merit, the power of the Tathāgata's empowerment, and the power of the dharmadhātu, may all sentient beings swiftly realize the pure Vairocana body." One recites the mantra:

"Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-saṃsthita sarvasattvānāṃ sarvasiddhayo saṃpanaye tathāgatāc ca adhiṣṭhitaṃ."

## Mudrā Sequences

One then forms the vajra añjali mudrā: both palms joined, ten fingers interlaced with right pressing left. Mantra: "Oṃ vajrāñjali."

By forming this mudrā, the ten pāramitās are perfected and the two provisions of merit and wisdom are accomplished.

Next, the vajra-binding mudrā: as before with the vajra añjali, then interlace outwardly to form fists. Mantra: "Oṃ vajra-bandha."

By forming this mudrā, one achieves vajra-liberation wisdom.

Next, with the vajra-binding, strike the heart three times. Mantra: "Oṃ vajra-bandha trāṭ."

By forming this mudrā, one crushes the ten afflictions covering body and mind, and all mudrā-positions are summoned to reside in body and mind, following the practitioner to accomplish all affairs. The "all mudrās" are: the great wisdom mudrā, the samaya wisdom mudrā, the Dharma wisdom mudrā, and the karma wisdom mudrā.

Next, the vajra-āveśa mudrā: with both hands in vajra-binding, bend the thumbs and place each between the ring and middle fingers. Mantra: "Oṃ vajra-āveśa aḥ."

By forming this mudrā, the four wisdom mudrās manifest with great power, and accomplishment is swiftly attained.

Next, the vajra-fist samaya mudrā: from the previous mudrā, bend the index fingers to touch the backs of the thumbs. Mantra: "Oṃ vajra-muṣṭi vaṃ."

By forming this mudrā, all mudrās are firmly bound and always remain within the practitioner's body and mind without dispersing.

Next, the samaya mudrā: with both hands in vajra-binding, raise and join the two middle fingers and place them at the heart. Mantra: "Samaya-sattvaṃ."

Having formed the mudrā and recited the mantra, one visualizes behind oneself a moon disc as a circular halo, the body dwelling within it, visualizing Vajrasattva.

By forming this mudrā and reciting this mantra, the great wisdom mudrā and all the mudrās of every division, and all the vajra mudrās of the body, speech, and mind of all tathāgatas, are never performed in vain nor violated. If one recites one thousand times, all mudrās one forms will be accomplished.

Next, the great samaya true-reality mudrā: with both hands in vajra-binding, the ring fingers enter the palms and interlock, the little fingers and thumbs face to face and joined like a single-pronged vajra. Touch the heart with the ring fingers. Mantra: "Samaya-he-sūrata-sattvaṃ."

By forming this mudrā and touching the heart, Vajrasattva pervades body and mind and swiftly grants accomplishment; whatever wish or aspiration is sought will be fulfilled.

Next, the Vajrasattva great wisdom mudrā: releasing the previous mudrā, each hand forms a vajra fist. Place the left hand on the hip and the right hand in the gesture of wielding a vajra, placed at the heart. Right foot presses left. Mantra: "Vajrasattva hūṃ."

Having recited, one visualizes one's own body as Vajrasattva dwelling in a great moon disc, seated on a great lotus, wearing the five-buddha jeweled crown, with a pleased and joyful countenance. The body is the color of the moon, luminous within and without. One generates great compassion, rescuing and delivering endless and inexhaustible sentient beings, causing them to attain the body of Vajrasattva. The three secrets operate simultaneously, equal in measure to space.

By maintaining the yoga great wisdom mudrā in correspondence, even if one has transgressed the Dharma, committed grave offenses, or created obstructions, by maintaining that great wisdom mudrā all tathāgatas will offer worship and reverence. If any person bows, makes offerings, shows respect, or offers praise, it is as though they see all tathāgatas and Vajrasattva himself.

Abiding in this great wisdom mudrā, one visualizes before one's body the wisdom body of Vajrasattva, identical to one's own body. One contemplates the four mudrās surrounding it, sharing the same moon disc and same lotus. Each abides in its own posture, holding its distinctive emblem, each wearing the five-buddha jeweled crown.

The yogin focuses single-pointedly on the Vajrasattva before the body, with mind unmoving. Mantra: "Vajrasattva aḥ."

By reciting this mantra, Vajrasattva appears through āveśa. Then one recites: "Vajrasattva dṛśya."

By reciting this mantra, in concentration one sees Vajrasattva clearly and distinctly. Then one recites the four-syllable vidyā: "Jaḥ hūṃ vaṃ hoḥ."

By reciting this mantra, the wisdom body of Vajrasattva is summoned, caused to enter, caused to bind, and caused to rejoice, merging into one body with the yogin's meditation body.

## The Sūrata and Crown Mudrās

Next, the sūrata mudrā: with both hands in vajra-binding, the right thumb enters the left tiger-mouth. One empowers the four places of heart, forehead, throat, and crown, reciting the mantra once at each: "Sūrata-sattvaṃ."

By this mudrā-empowerment, the four pāramitā bodies each abide in their own positions and constantly protect.

Next, the five-buddha jeweled crown mudrā: with both hands in vajra-binding, ring fingers raised and joined with bent upper joints like a sword shape, index fingers touching the backs of the ring fingers. Place the mudrā at the crown, then at the hairline, then at the right of the crown, then the back, then the left, reciting the mantra once at each position: "Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-ratna-abhiṣeka aḥ."

By forming this mudrā, one obtains the abhiṣeka stage of all tathāgatas and Vajrasattva.

Next, the vajra garland mudrā, then the armor mudrā enveloping the entire body, then the joy mudrā with both palms clapping: "Vajra-tuṣya hoḥ."

## The Four Secret Karma Mudrās

Next, one forms the Vajrasattva great wisdom mudrā again and recites the root mantra. Then one should form the four secret karma mudrās, reciting the vajra song of praise. This praise has four lines; with each mudrā one recites one line.

The Desire Vajra mudrā: both hands in vajra fists; the left hand imagines holding a bow and the right hand draws an arrow as if shooting. This forms the body of this deity. Mantra verse: "In all sufferings, the bliss of the self-nature of sattva."

The Kelikila mudrā: from the previous mudrā, cross the two fists embracing the chest. This forms the body of this deity. Mantra verse: "Sattvaṃ vajrasattva, the supreme bliss of sūrata."

The Love Vajra mudrā: from the previous two vajra fists, place the left fist supporting the right elbow, raising the right arm like a banner. This forms the body of this deity. Mantra verse: "For all, the great bliss of nirvṛtti."

The Pride Vajra mudrā: both vajra fists placed on the hips, the head slightly inclined to the left as if bowing. This forms the body of this deity. Mantra verse: "The attainment of siddhi and the filling of the lotus."

## The Five Secrets Samaya Mudrās

Next, the Vajrasattva samaya mudrā: form the vajra-binding with ring fingers entering the palms and interlocking as before, thumbs and little fingers each touching like a single-pronged vajra. Mantra: "Sūrata-sattvaṃ."

By forming this mudrā and reciting this mantra, one's supernatural powers, lifespan, might, and marks become equal to Vajrasattva's.

The Desire Vajra samaya mudrā: from the previous mudrā, bend the upper joints of the index fingers so their nail-backs meet, with the thumbs pressing upon them. Mantra: "Jaḥ vajra-dṛṣṭi-sya-kema-ṭa."

By forming this mudrā, one severs the subtle afflictions of the ground of fundamental ignorance.

The Kelikila samaya mudrā: from the previous mudrā, the right thumb presses the left, crossing. Mantra: "Hūṃ vajra-kelikili hūṃ."

By forming this mudrā, one rescues and protects all sentient beings who suffer, and they all attain the great bliss samādhi.

The Love Vajra samaya mudrā: from the previous mudrā, the index fingers mutually grasp the ring fingers, the index fingers joined like an eye shape, ring fingers and little fingers raised and joined. Mantra: "Vaṃ vajriṇī smaraṃ rāṭ."

By forming this mudrā, one attains great compassionate liberation, cherishing all sentient beings as one's only child, always generating the mind of rescue and peace.

The Pride Vajra samaya mudrā: from the previous, touch both thighs, right first then left. Mantra: "Hoḥ vajra-kāme-śphṛ-traṃ."

By forming this mudrā, one attains the great vigor pāramitā, and in an instant can make vast offerings to all tathāgatas throughout boundless worlds.

## The Hundred-Syllable Mantra

Next, abiding in the Vajrasattva samaya, one recites the great Mahāyāna direct-realization hundred-syllable mantra:

"Oṃ vajrasattva samayam anupālaya, vajrasattva tvenopatiṣṭha, dṛḍho me bhava, sutoṣyo me bhava, anurakto me bhava, supoṣyo me bhava, sarva-siddhiṃ me prayaccha, sarva-karmasu ca me cittaṃ śreyaḥ kuru hūṃ, ha ha ha ha hoḥ, bhagavan sarva-tathāgata vajra mā me muñca, vajrī-bhava, mahā-samaya-sattva aḥ."

One then enters the Vajrasattva samādhi and, forming the great wisdom mudrā, recites the Mahāyāna direct-realization Vajrasattva mantra: "Vajrasattva."

Abiding in the great wisdom mudrā, or holding a rosary with unlimited recitation, one should not allow fatigue. By abiding in samādhi and reciting this mantra, in this present life one realizes immeasurable samādhis, can accomplish the body of the principal deity, and all tathāgatas appear before one. One attains the five supernatural powers, travels through all worlds in the ten directions, and broadly performs the work of benefit and peace for boundless sentient beings.

The yogin, in walking, standing, sitting, and lying down, should always be surrounded by the four attendants, dwelling in a great lotus within the same moon disc.

## Doctrinal Explanation

Vajrasattva is Samantabhadra Bodhisattva (Pǔxián púsà 普賢菩薩), the eldest son of all tathāgatas, the bodhi-mind of all tathāgatas, the ancestral teacher of all tathāgatas. Therefore all tathāgatas pay reverence to Vajrasattva. As the scripture says:

> "The samādhi of Vajrasattva
> Is named the Dharma of all buddhas.
> This Dharma can accomplish the buddha-way;
> Apart from this, there are no buddhas."

Desire Vajra is the prajñāpāramitā. It penetrates all buddha-dharmas without obstruction, like vajra, and can give birth to all buddhas.

Kelikila Vajra is the ākāśagarbha samādhi. It gives peace to boundless sentient beings and rescues those mired in poverty and want, fulfilling all their worldly and transcendent wishes.

Love Vajra is Tārā Bodhisattva. She abides in great compassionate liberation, pitying boundless suffering sentient beings and always harboring the thought of rescue and the bestowal of peace.

Pride Vajra is the great vigor pāramitā. It abides in unobstructed liberation, performing vast buddha-work for boundless tathāgatas and bringing benefit to sentient beings.

Desire Vajra holds the vajra bow and arrow, shooting all the defiled seeds in the ālaya-consciousness, transforming them into the great mirror wisdom (mahādarśana-jñāna). Kelikila Vajra embraces Vajrasattva, signifying the purification of the seventh consciousness's false grasping of the eighth consciousness as self, with its ego-delusion, ego-view, ego-pride, and ego-love, transforming it into the equality wisdom (samatā-jñāna).

Vajrasattva abiding in the great wisdom mudrā represents, from the vajradhātu through to Vajraghaṇṭā Bodhisattva, the thirty-seven wisdoms accomplishing the self-enjoyment and enjoyment-for-others fruit-bodies. Love Vajra holds the makara banner and purifies the mental consciousness's discursive engagement with pure and defiled states of the defiled mind, transforming it into the discriminating wisdom (pratyavekṣaṇā-jñāna). Pride Vajra, with two vajra fists placed on the hips, signifies the purification of the five sense-consciousnesses' material obstruction of the body, generating great vigor to cause all sentient beings without remainder to simultaneously become buddhas, transforming them into the action-accomplishing wisdom (kṛtyānuṣṭhāna-jñāna).

Desire Vajra is the wisdom eye, observing the dependently originated nature in its pure and defiled aspects, knowing all dharmas as neither existent nor nonexistent. Kelikila Vajra, with unstained wisdom, observes the pure aspect of the dependently originated nature, knowing that in the fruit stage it is neither identical with nor different from the perfectly realized nature, and that all dharmas are neither identical with nor different from bodhi and nirvāṇa. Vajrasattva is the self-nature body, unborn and unceasing, equal in measure to space, the body pervading the dharmadhātu. Love Vajra, with the great compassion divine eye, sees the Samantabhadra essence within all sentient beings as neither increasing nor decreasing. Pride Vajra, with the pure unobstructed fleshly eye, sees all sentient beings dwelling in the ordinary stage and knows that, though covered by afflictions, their original nature is pure; if they engage in great vigor, they will attain stainless purity.

Vajrasattva is the body of Vairocana Buddha. Desire Vajra is the Vajra Pāramitā, Kelikila is the Jewel Pāramitā, Love Vajra is the Dharma Pāramitā, and Pride Vajra is the Karma Pāramitā.

Vajrasattva is the Bhagavat Akṣobhya Tathāgata. Desire Vajra is Vajrasattva, Kelikila is Vajrarāja, Love Vajra is Vajrarāga, and Pride Vajra is Vajrasādhu.

Vajrasattva is the Bhagavat Ratnasaṃbhava Tathāgata. Desire Vajra is Vajraratna, Kelikila is Vajrasūrya, Love Vajra is Vajraketu, and Pride Vajra is Vajrahāsa.

Vajrasattva is the Bhagavat Avalokiteśvararāja Tathāgata. Desire Vajra is Vajradharma, Kelikila is Vajratīkṣṇa, Love Vajra is Vajrahetu, and Pride Vajra is Vajrabhāṣa.

Vajrasattva is the Bhagavat Amoghasiddhi Tathāgata. Desire Vajra is Vajrakarma, Kelikila is Vajrarakṣa, Love Vajra is Vajrayakṣa, and Pride Vajra is Vajramuṣṭi. The four inner offerings are those same four attendants. The four outer offerings are also those same four attendants.

Desire Vajra, with the bodhi-mind arrow, hooks and summons all sentient beings and places them on the buddha path. Kelikila's embracing mudrā is the great skillful-means vajra vehicle, causing realization of unstained wisdom. Love Vajra's makara banner is the great compassion vajra lasso: throughout immeasurable kalpas abiding in birth and death, the mind never shifts, for delivering all beings is its path. Pride Vajra, with great vigor, is the prajñā vajra bell, rousing sentient beings who slumber in the cave of ignorance.

The Samantabhadra maṇḍala does not depart from the five bodies. The Trailokyavijaya maṇḍala is the same as the Vajradhātu. The Lotus Division universal-subjugation maṇḍala follows this pattern. The Jewel Division all-meaning-accomplishment is also explained in the same way.

## Conclusion

The five secrets of Vajrasattva constitute the Tathāgata Division, and they are also the Vajra Division, the Lotus Division, and the Jewel Division. That the five bodies share one great lotus signifies great compassionate liberation. That they share one moon disc with circular radiance signifies great wisdom. Therefore the bodhisattva, through great wisdom, is not stained by saṃsāra; through great compassion, does not abide in nirvāṇa. As the scripture says: "There are three kinds of sattvas: the foolish sattva, the wise sattva, and the vajra-sattva." Vajrasattva is distinguished from the other two. A practitioner who attains this vajra vehicle is called a vajra-sattva.

Therefore, the bodhisattva who possesses transcendent wisdom, even unto the end of birth and death, always works for the benefit of beings and does not hasten toward nirvāṇa. By what dharma is this attained? Therefore, through prajñā and upāya, empowered by the perfection of wisdom, all dharmas and all existences are entirely purified. "All dharmas and all existences" means the two graspings of self and dharma. Therefore, to regulate the world through desire and cause it to be purified, from the Summit of Existence to the evil destinies, one tames and exhausts all existences.

By abiding in the ākāśagarbha samādhi, regarding the two graspings of self and dharma, one awakens to their equality and purity, like a lotus. Therefore, as the nature of the lotus is originally pure and undefiled by impurities, so too the nature of desires is the same: unstained, it benefits all beings. Benefiting beings means performing works of peace and benefit, dwelling in the position of great sovereignty. Therefore, great desire attains purity, great bliss and abundant riches; one attains sovereignty over the three realms and can perform firm and steadfast benefit. Firm benefit has the bodhi-mind as its cause. The cause is twofold: delivering boundless sentient beings is the cause, and supreme bodhi is the fruit. Furthermore, great compassion is the root; concurrently abiding in the great compassionate mind, one cannot be shaken by the wind of the two-vehicle realm, all due to great skillful means. Great skillful means is the three secret vajras as the supreme condition, enabling realization of Vairocana's pure three-body fruit stage.
