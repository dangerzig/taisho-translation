# 大方廣佛華嚴經普賢菩薩行願王品
## Chapter on the King of Aspirational Prayers of Samantabhadra Bodhisattva, from the Great Vast Buddha Avataṃsaka Sūtra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 2908

Translated from the Chinese. Author unknown.

---

> Throughout the buddha-fields of the ten directions,
> The lions among humans who traverse the three times:
> Without exception, to all those buddhas,
> With pure body, speech, and mind I bow down.
>
> To cultivate Samantabhadra's aspirational prayers,
> All buddhas appear within the mind's visualization.
> Bowing my body, as numerous as dust-motes of buddha-fields,
> With reverent mind I pay homage to all the supreme ones.
>
> Upon a single dust-mote, buddhas numerous as dust-motes,
> Surrounded by their buddha-sons in their midst:
> Throughout the dharma-realm, without remainder,
> Buddhas fill all. One should have faith and understanding.
>
> The oceans of their virtues are praised without end;
> Wondrous sounds in branch-streams of ocean-vast words.
> I extol the merit of all buddhas;
> All the sugatas I now praise.
>
> Supreme, wondrous flowers and garlands of flowers,
> Small bells, unguent-incense, and jeweled canopies,
> Supreme lamps and burning incense:
> These I offer to all the supreme ones.
>
> Many wondrous garments and aromatic incense,
> Supreme, variegated adornments,
> Wondrous powdered incense heaped as high as Mount Sumeru:
> These I offer to all the supreme ones.
>
> Unsurpassed, vast offerings:
> With faith I present them to all the supreme ones.
> Through the power of delighting in Samantabhadra's practices,
> I bow down and make offerings to all the supreme ones.
>
> Whatever transgressions I have committed,
> Through body, speech, and mind,
> Held by the power of greed, anger, and delusion:
> Each and every one I separately confess.
>
> All supreme ones and buddha-sons of the ten directions,
> Pratyekabuddhas, śrāvakas in training and beyond training,
> And whatever merit sentient beings have cultivated:
> I rejoice in all of these.
>
> Whoever in the ten directions serves as a lamp of the world,
> Gradually attaining bodhi and realizing non-attachment:
> Before all those guides,
> I request them to turn the unsurpassed wheel.
>
> Those who delight in proceeding to nirvāṇa,
> For the benefit and happiness of all sentient beings,
> Or those who delight in remaining for kalpas as numerous as dust-motes of buddha-fields:
> I join my palms and request them all.
>
> Prostration, offering, and confession,
> Requesting, rejoicing, and exhortation:
> Whatever small wholesome roots I have accumulated,
> All of these I dedicate to bodhi.
>
> All buddhas of the past throughout the ten directions,
> And whoever abides in the world: I should make offerings.
> All the world-honored ones of the future:
> May they realize bodhi and swiftly reach fulfillment.
>
> Throughout all buddha-fields of the ten directions,
> May all become vast and pure.
> Those who approach the bodhi tree as equally awakened ones:
> May all the buddha-sons fulfill their aspirations.
>
> May all sentient beings of the ten directions
> Be free from illness and suffering and always at peace.
> May all beings, in the meaning of the Dharma,
> Fulfill their hopes and jointly achieve.
>
> May I cultivate the practices of bodhi;
> May I know my former lives wherever I am born.
> World after world, in abandoning and receiving bodies,
> May I always go forth and attain full accomplishment.
>
> Studying and cultivating in accord with all the supreme ones,
> May the practices of Samantabhadra be fulfilled.
> May my precept-conduct, stainless and most pure,
> Be always upheld without flaw or blemish.
>
> Devas, nāgas, yakṣas, kumbhāṇḍas,
> Humans, non-humans, and all other classes:
> In whatever languages they have,
> I shall teach the Dharma to them all.
>
> Genuinely virtuous, diligently cultivating the perfections,
> May I never forget the mind of bodhi.
> Whatever transgressions have become obstacles:
> May all of them be eliminated, with nothing remaining.
>
> From karma, afflictions, māras, and wrong paths,
> Liberated and at ease, I move through the world,
> Unattached like a lotus upon water,
> Practicing without obstruction like the sun and moon.
>
> Throughout all buddha-fields and directions,
> May the suffering of the evil destinies be eliminated.
> May sentient beings be placed in peace and happiness,
> With abundant benefit for all beings of every destiny.
>
> May I perfectly cultivate the practices of bodhi,
> Entering into the practices of sentient beings.
> Revealing Samantabhadra's aspirational prayers,
> May I practice to the end of future kalpas.
>
> Whoever practices this practice together with me:
> May I always follow alongside them.
> In body-karma, speech-karma, and mind-karma,
> May my practices be one with theirs.
>
> Whoever delights in benefiting me,
> The good friends who reveal Samantabhadra's aspirational prayers:
> May I always encounter them,
> And toward them never have a contrary thought.
>
> The teachers of the Way, enriched by their buddha-sons,
> May I always behold them directly before me.
> Before them I should perform great offerings,
> Without weariness to the end of future kalpas.
>
> Protecting and upholding the supreme, wondrous Dharma of all buddhas,
> Universally revealing the cultivation of the bodhi-practices:
> To purify Samantabhadra Bodhisattva's practices,
> To the end of future kalpas I should practice.
>
> While revolving through the various realms of existence,
> May I attain inexhaustible merit and wisdom.
> Samādhi, wisdom, skillful means, and liberation:
> May I accomplish the inexhaustible treasuries of all virtues.
>
> Upon one dust-mote, dust-mote numbers of buddha-fields;
> In those fields, boundless buddhas, immeasurable,
> Surrounded by their buddha-sons in their midst:
> Practicing the bodhi-practices, I gaze up at them.
>
> Thus, throughout boundless realms,
> Upon the tip of a hair, the measure of the three times.
> Oceans of buddhas, and buddha-fields like oceans;
> Oceans of kalpas of practice: one should enter.
>
> One sound, with branches of speech as vast as oceans of language:
> All of these are the pure speech-branches of the buddhas.
> Following the sounds that delight all destinies,
> May I always cause them to enter the sound of the buddhas.
>
> When the supreme ones traversing the three times
> Turn the various Dharma-wheels in accord with the Dharma,
> Into all the inexhaustible sounds of those buddhas,
> Through the power of wisdom I should enter.
>
> If entering the many kalpas of the future,
> I should enter them in an instant's fraction.
> Whoever, in kalpas equal to the three times,
> In each instant's fraction enters and practices:
>
> Whoever traverses the three times, the lions among humans,
> In every instant I gaze up at them.
> Always in the realms they have traveled,
> Through the power of illusion-like liberation I enter.
>
> Whoever adorns buddha-fields with the three times:
> All are established upon a single dust-mote.
> Throughout immeasurable realms,
> I enter the adornment of all buddha-fields.
>
> All the future lamps of the world,
> Attaining buddhahood, turning the Dharma-wheel,
> And displaying nirvāṇa, proceeding to the true limit:
> To the places of all those guides I should go.
>
> Universally swift, with the power of supernatural abilities;
> Universally entering the universal gate, with the power of the vehicles;
> Universally meritorious, with the power of practices;
> Universally pervading, with the power of compassion and the rest.
>
> Universally, with the power of all good deeds and merit;
> Through the power of great wisdom, free from attachment;
> Through the power of wisdom, skillful means, samādhi, and wisdom;
> Through the power of truly accomplishing bodhi.
>
> Through the power of completely pure karma,
> Destroying the power of all affliction;
> Subjugating the power of all māras:
> Fulfilling the power of Samantabhadra's practices.
>
> May oceans of buddha-fields be purified;
> May oceans of sentient beings be liberated.
> May oceans of dharmas be perceived;
> May oceans of wisdom be contemplated.
>
> May oceans of practices be purified;
> May oceans of vows be fulfilled.
> Oceans of buddhas should receive offerings;
> Through oceans of kalpas of practice, may there be no time of weariness.
>
> Whoever traverses the three times, the supreme ones,
> Whose practices and aspirations of bodhi differ in many ways:
> Through the practice of Samantabhadra, having attained equal awakening,
> May I bring all of these, without remainder, to fulfillment.
>
> Most powerful among all buddha-sons,
> Therefore named "Samantabhadra":
> The wise can practice in accord with his conduct,
> And such virtues should be dedicated.
>
> Body, speech, and mind pure,
> All practices pure, all buddha-fields pure:
> However Samantabhadra thoroughly dedicates merit,
> May I also follow in that practice.
>
> To practice the entirely good conduct of Samantabhadra,
> The practices of Mañjuśrī should also be performed.
> To the end of future kalpas, without a time of weariness:
> All their deeds should be fulfilled.
>
> May all that is practiced be without measure;
> May all merit also be without limit.
> Though abiding in immeasurable supreme practices,
> May all their spiritual transformations be known.
>
> Even if there were an end to space,
> An end to sentient beings likewise,
> And an end to karma and afflictions:
> My vows would be equally boundless.
>
> Throughout the immeasurable buddha-fields of the ten directions,
> Adorned with jewels and offered to the buddhas,
> And the supreme joys of humans and worthy ones,
> Offered for kalpas as numerous as dust-motes of buddha-fields:
>
> Yet whoever hears this sūtra of dedication
> And gives rise to faith and understanding in accord with supreme bodhi,
> If one can produce even a single instant of faith,
> That person accomplishes the supreme accumulation of merit.
>
> Whoever makes these aspirational prayers of Samantabhadra,
> That person casts off the evil destinies.
> They are also called one who has departed from evil companions;
> That person will swiftly see Amitābha.
>
> Such persons attain the most blissful lifespan;
> In this present life they also attract joy as they wish.
> Like Samantabhadra Bodhisattva,
> Such persons will not be long in attaining likewise.
>
> Whoever, turned by the power of ignorance,
> Has committed the five heinous crimes and other transgressions:
> If they can recite Samantabhadra's practices,
> All will swiftly be eliminated, with nothing remaining.
>
> In wisdom, fine features, and all marks,
> Lineage, and subtle form, all will be perfected.
> Māras and non-Buddhists will be unable to prevail;
> All in the three realms will make offerings.
>
> Swiftly proceeding to the sovereign bodhi tree,
> For the benefit of sentient beings, sitting there,
> Attaining perfect, complete awakening, turning the Dharma-wheel,
> Vanquishing all the hosts of māras.
>
> Whoever upholds, recites, and explains
> These aspirational prayers of Samantabhadra's practices:
> The fruition thereof is known only to the buddhas.
> Do not be doubtful about supreme bodhi.
>
> Mañjuśrī's heroic valor and his knowledge,
> And Samantabhadra Bodhisattva likewise:
> Following their example, I cultivate and practice.
> All these virtues I dedicate.
>
> All the supremely good ones traversing the three times,
> Their dedications among the most excellent:
> All my wholesome roots like these
> I dedicate for the practice of Samantabhadra's conduct.
>
> When I approach the time of death,
> May all obscuring obstacles be removed.
> May I behold Amitābha directly before me
> And swiftly travel to Sukhāvatī.
>
> Having arrived there, may all these aspirational prayers
> Clearly and completely appear before me.
> Without remainder, may I bring them all to fulfillment,
> Benefiting beings throughout all worlds.
>
> In the supremely beautiful bodhimaṇḍa of Sukhāvatī,
> Born within that supreme, wondrous lotus,
> Before the most excellent Amitābha:
> May I receive the prediction of awakening.
>
> Having received the prediction there,
> Attaining immeasurable hundreds of millions of supernatural powers,
> Through the power of wisdom pervading the ten directions,
> May I bring abundant benefit to all sentient beings.
