# 摩訶毘盧遮那如來定惠均等入三昧耶身雙身大聖歡喜天菩薩修行祕密法儀軌
## Ritual of the Secret Method for Practicing the Dual-Bodied Great Holy Joyous Deva Bodhisattva, the Samaya Body of Equal Samādhi and Wisdom of Mahāvairocana Tathāgata

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1271

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

If there are disciples of the Buddha, whether bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, or upāsikās, or any good men or good women who wish to practice the method of this holy deva bodhisattva and achieve accomplishment, fulfilling all that they seek and desire according to their hearts, they must first exhaust their treasures in service to the dharma-transmitting master and ācārya, so as to make them joyful. With a hundred flavors of food and drink, necessities, wealth, garments, bedding, and personal requisites, serve and make offerings until the master's heart is pleased. Only then may one inquire about and fully learn the subtle and secret, most profound essential principles, and undertake the practice of the secret method. The essential path of accomplishment requires that one dedicate one's body in pursuit of the path, exhausting one's treasures in service. This means offering the very garments one would wear and the very food one would eat to the dharma-transmitting master and ācārya who knows the method. This is what is meant by "exhausting one's treasures in service."

The method requires first preparing one *shēng* of sesame oil, a warm charcoal brazier, a fire-screen, and a white copper vessel. Empower with the great mantra 108 times; warm the oil while empowering. The mudrā is simply the three-pronged vajra.

The root great mantra (body mantra):

*Namo vināyaka-śahā-asti-mukha-śa tadyathā oṃ nāyaka nāyaka vināyaka vināyaka trāyaka paritrāyaka śaṃkha-hasti śaṃkha-kā-citta śānti-kara svāhā.*

Bathe the image: three times at midday and four times at dawn. The bathing period is limited to seven days. Pray for the attainment of siddhi. Empower the offering materials with the lesser heart mantra. The offering materials are: radish, joy-balls, cakes, seasonal fruits, and the like. Present them as offerings to the presently manifesting noble sage. Within seven days, results will certainly be obtained.

The lesser heart mantra:

*Oṃ kiri ñak svāhā.*

After each offering is complete, recite the praise verse:

*Oṃ gaṇa-bhati-jūti svā-siddhi mahā-gaṇdha-jyoti-kārāya svāhā.*

Because one's desires and wishes are fulfilled to perfect and complete satisfaction, one should make the dharma-transmitting ācārya joyful. Afterward, one may undertake the practice for one period of seven days and obtain the four kinds of siddhi: pacification, increase, attraction, and subjugation. These are the four kinds of siddhi attainment.

The *Vināyaka Sūtra* states that offerings to this holy deva bodhisattva yield three grades of results. Superior offerings cause one to obtain the majestic power of a king. Middling offerings cause one to possess the seven jewels and become wealthy. Inferior offerings cause one never to lack food and clothing, and to be naturally satisfied and content.

For superior offerings: one makes the dharma-transmitter's heart joyful, receives instruction in the most profound secret principles, and makes offerings according to the proper method. For middling offerings: one can see the ritual but does not fully comprehend it; one practices in abbreviated sequence according to the essential path. For inferior offerings: one does not bathe the image with oil but merely installs it in a separate hall. One makes offerings only during the first time-period each day. This is called inferior.

Disciples of the Buddha in the Age of the Final Dharma who harbor miserliness, pride, and possessive attachment in their hearts, and who fail to make the dharma-transmitting ācārya truly joyful and pleased, who do not inquire into the detailed and profound essential principles and do not awaken to them, will obtain only the crude and abbreviated practice. Those who slander the profound and supreme path invite the seeds of poverty and lack of merit.

The Tiantai Master Zhizhe Ācārya's *Record of the Essential Points of Calming and Contemplation* states: "One who has merit but lacks wisdom is a fool no different from a nāga-bodied ghost. One who has wisdom but lacks merit is a perverse, deluded person, not of the mind of a noble sage. In the world, one who truly lacks merit has no equal in wretchedness. Among those who conduct themselves in the world, there is nothing that surpasses self-adornment through merit and virtue."

Therefore, when making offerings and practicing this holy deva bodhisattva, one must achieve the perfect fullness of both merit and wisdom. *Samādhi* is merit; *prajñā equally balanced* (the samaya body) means that this method has two times at beginning and end: first, the early morning; second, midday. The early morning is the time of increase; midday is the time of auspiciousness. Therefore, one who makes offerings to this holy deva achieves the perfect fullness of both merit and wisdom.

Furthermore, the Vinaya school's provisional teachers of a hundred generations lack equality. This method uses wine as an offering. Aconite is a poison, yet when a physician obtains it, it cures disease and brings bodily ease; those who compound medicines obtain joy from it. Wine, this "water of joy," is a wondrous medicine that gladdens the farmer. This wine: for those who can drink, it becomes medicine; for those who drink wrongly, it becomes poison. This deva bodhisattva makes the human heart joyful, and therefore wine is used as an offering. This is why it is called "water of joy." When present-day kings and nobles make offerings, they will certainly obtain spiritual verification. The offerings remaining may be consumed by the mantra-master without fault. The drinking restrictions of other methods do not apply to this method.

Disciples of the Buddha in the Age of the Final Dharma should give rise to a sincere mind of faith, making offerings with wholehearted diligence. One must not give rise to doubt. One should seek out and learn from those who transmit and know the Dharma, faithfully accepting and practicing it. One must not be crude or negligent. The sequential method received and held by the ācārya, complete in every detail, is not recorded in the written ritual text. One who is able to receive the sequential method, learn it, awaken to it, practice the offerings, and faithfully accept and carry it out, will attain great joy in all things.
