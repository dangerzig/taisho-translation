# Record of the Merits Cultivated by the Buddhist Teaching Master Hóngbiàn of Shazhou under the Great Tibetan Empire

## *Dàfān Shāzhōu Shìmén Jiàofǎ Héshàng Hóngbiàn Xiū Gōngdé Jì* (大蕃沙洲釋門教法和尚洪辯修功德記)

Taishō 2862

Translator unknown. A Dunhuang manuscript.

Based on the Gate Spirit Mother and the *Suvarṇaprabhāsa-sūtra* (*Jīnguāngmíng Jīng*, 金光明經, "Sūtra of Golden Light"), this is a summary of the merits of Vaiśravaṇa (*Píshāmén*, 毘沙門) and Śrīdevī (*Gōngdé Tiān*, 功德天, "Goddess of Merit").

At that time the World-Honored One was at Gandhamādana ("Fragrant Mountain," *Xiāngshān*, 香山). While in meditation during the first watch of the night, the assembly of the Trāyastriṃśa (*Sānshísān*, 三十三) heaven and Vaiśravaṇa, the Heavenly King, received a prediction and descended, bringing various music, incense, and flowers to make offerings to the World-Honored One. When the offerings were complete, the World-Honored One gave Vaiśravaṇa, the Heavenly King, a prediction that in a future life he would become a buddha with the title Nemūrazhē Píshāmén (*Nìwùluózhē Píshāmén*, 泥勿羅遮毘沙門). Having received this prediction, Vaiśravaṇa made a great vow before the Buddha: "Until I attain buddhahood, I vow to serve as a great and powerful yakṣa king, protecting all sentient beings in this southern continent of Jambudvīpa (*Nán Yánfútí*, 南閻浮提), as well as kings and great ministers, princes, and all the people of every land. Whether male or female, may they have faith in and receive the *Suvarṇaprabhāsa-sūtra*. May the fourfold assembly read and recite it for me, and within that land may there be awakening.

"If the people suffer from famine, epidemic, or disease, if neighboring enemies attack one another, if violent rains and evil winds do not cease, if the seasons are disrupted and the sun and moon are eclipsed, if wells emit strange sounds and stars fall and the earth shakes, if the people within the land suffer various afflictions, if such immeasurable hundreds of thousands of calamities and strange evil events arise, then may the king of that land wish to protect his country. Let the king, within a prominent place in the land, sprinkle fragrant water upon the ground and establish a practice site. Let him have a single-word master (*yīzì shī*, 一字師) read and recite the *Suvarṇaprabhāsa-sūtra* for me. At that time, immeasurable hundreds of thousands of celestial spirits, together with the guardian deities of the land and all the virtuous spirits of old, and my retinue, will each conceal their forms and provide protection and assistance. The enemies will naturally be subdued, and sorrow, sickness, and pestilence will also be removed. All the soldiers will be brave and strong, and the people within the land will receive peace and happiness."

The World-Honored One said: "Is this true?" Vaiśravaṇa, the Heavenly King, replied to the World-Honored One: "If there is a king or minister who summons me thus, and I do not appear at that place or protect that king, may I never see the great nation's brethren. May I be shattered into seven pieces before the future buddhas." Having made this vow, he returned to his own heaven. When he arrived in heaven, the great assembly gathered together. Ānanda (*Ānán*, 阿難) rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, joined his palms reverently, and said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One, I am an ordinary person and do not know the holy intent. During the night watch, there were heavenly incense and flowers..."

Record of the merits cultivated by the Buddhist Teaching Master Hóngbiàn (*Hóngbiàn*, 洪辯) of Shazhou (*Shāzhōu*, 沙州) under the Great Tibetan Empire (*Dàfān*, 大蕃). Written by Xiànjì (*Xiànjì*, 憲驥), Doctor of Letters under the Great Tibetan Empire.

By imperial order to the Governor of Hexi (*Héxī*, 河西).
