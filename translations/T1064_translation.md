# 千手千眼觀世音菩薩大悲心陀羅尼
## Great Compassion Heart Dhāraṇī of the Thousand-Armed Thousand-Eyed Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1064

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

> Homage to the Lord of Great Compassion, Avalokiteśvara,
> Whose deep vow-power manifests a body of excellent marks,
> Whose thousand arms adorned in splendor protect universally,
> Whose thousand eyes of radiance observe all things.
> From words of truth he proclaims secret words;
> From a mind of non-action he gives rise to a compassionate mind.
> May he swiftly grant the fulfillment of all hopes,
> And forever eliminate all karmic offenses.
> The hosts of devas, nāgas, and noble ones together offer their compassionate protection;
> Hundreds of thousands of samādhis are cultivated in an instant.
> The body of one who upholds this is a pillar of radiance;
> The mind of one who upholds this is a treasury of supernatural powers.
> Washing away the dust of afflictions, may it ferry across the ocean of vows;
> Transcending and realizing awakening through this gate of skillful means.
> I now chant and vow to take refuge:
> May all wishes from the heart be perfectly fulfilled.
> Homage to Avalokiteśvara of Great Compassion: may I swiftly know all dharmas.
> Homage to Avalokiteśvara of Great Compassion: may I quickly obtain the eye of wisdom.
> Homage to Avalokiteśvara of Great Compassion: may I swiftly ferry across all beings.
> Homage to Avalokiteśvara of Great Compassion: may I quickly obtain skillful means.
> Homage to Avalokiteśvara of Great Compassion: may I swiftly board the ship of prajñā.
> Homage to Avalokiteśvara of Great Compassion: may I quickly cross the ocean of suffering.
> Homage to Avalokiteśvara of Great Compassion: may I swiftly attain the path of precepts and samādhi.
> Homage to Avalokiteśvara of Great Compassion: may I quickly ascend the mountain of nirvāṇa.
> Homage to Avalokiteśvara of Great Compassion: may I swiftly reach the abode of the unconditioned.
> Homage to Avalokiteśvara of Great Compassion: may I quickly become one with the dharma-nature body.

> If I should face a mountain of swords, the mountain of swords will crumble of itself.
> If I should face a cauldron of boiling liquid, the cauldron will be extinguished of itself.
> If I should face the hells, the hells will dry up of themselves.
> If I should face hungry ghosts, the hungry ghosts will be sated of themselves.
> If I should face asuras, their evil minds will be tamed of themselves.
> If I should face animals, they will of themselves attain great wisdom.

Having made these vows, with utmost sincerity call upon my name. One should also single-mindedly call upon my original teacher, Amitābha Tathāgata. Then one should recite this dhāraṇī divine spell. If one recites it five times in a single night, it will eliminate the grave sins accumulated over hundreds of thousands of myriads of koṭis of kalpas of birth and death.

Homage to Amitābha Tathāgata. Homage to Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva Mahāsattva.

Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva further addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! If any human or deva recites and upholds the verses of the Great Compassion dhāraṇī, at the time of death all the buddhas of the ten directions will extend their hands. Whatever buddha land one wishes to be born in, one will be reborn there according to one's vow." He further addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! If any sentient being who recites and upholds the Great Compassion divine spell should fall into the three evil destinies, I vow not to attain complete awakening. If one who recites and upholds the Great Compassion divine spell is not reborn in a buddha land, I vow not to attain complete awakening. If one who recites and upholds the Great Compassion divine spell does not attain limitless samādhis and eloquence, I vow not to attain complete awakening. If one who recites and upholds the Great Compassion Heart divine spell does not have all their wishes fulfilled in this present life, then it shall not be called the Great Compassion Heart Dhāraṇī. The only exceptions are those whose intentions are unwholesome or whose sincerity is lacking. If any woman despises the female body and wishes to become a man, and recites and upholds the verses of the Great Compassion Dhāraṇī but does not transform the female body into a male body, I vow not to attain complete awakening. If one harbors even the slightest doubt, one will certainly not succeed. If any sentient being has misappropriated the food, drink, or property of the permanent community, even a thousand buddhas appearing in the world could not grant absolution; even if they could repent, it would not be eliminated. But now, by reciting the Great Compassion divine spell, it is immediately eliminated. If one has misappropriated the food, drink, or property of the permanent community, one must repent before the teachers of the ten directions before it can be eliminated. But now, when one recites the Great Compassion Dhāraṇī, the buddhas of the ten directions come at once to serve as witnesses, and all karmic obstructions are completely eliminated. All ten evil deeds, the five heinous crimes, slandering others, slandering the dharma, breaking fasts, breaking precepts, destroying stūpas, damaging monasteries, stealing saṅgha property, defiling pure conduct: all such grave sins and evil karma are completely eliminated. The only exception is one who harbors doubt toward the spell; for such a person, even small offenses and light karma cannot be eliminated, how much less grave sins. Yet even without immediate elimination of grave sins, it can still serve as a distant cause for awakening." He further addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! If any human or deva recites and upholds the Great Compassion Heart spell, they will obtain fifteen kinds of fortunate births and will not suffer the fifteen kinds of evil deaths. The evil deaths are: first, not dying of hunger and hardship; second, not dying by the stocks, chains, or beating; third, not dying at the hands of enemies; fourth, not dying in battle between armies; fifth, not being killed by tigers, wolves, or evil beasts; sixth, not dying from venomous snakes, vipers, or scorpions; seventh, not dying by drowning or burning; eighth, not dying from poison; ninth, not dying from parasitic poisons; tenth, not dying of madness or loss of mindfulness; eleventh, not dying by falling from mountains, trees, or cliffs; twelfth, not dying from evil persons' sorcery; thirteenth, not dying at the hands of evil spirits or wicked ghosts; fourteenth, not dying from evil illness afflicting the body; fifteenth, not dying an untimely, self-inflicted death. One who recites and upholds the Great Compassion divine spell will not suffer these fifteen kinds of evil death. The fifteen kinds of fortunate birth are: first, wherever one is born, one will always encounter a virtuous ruler; second, one will always be born in a virtuous kingdom; third, one will always encounter favorable times; fourth, one will always meet good companions; fifth, one's sense faculties will always be complete; sixth, one's mind for the path will be fully mature; seventh, one will not violate the precepts; eighth, all one's kin will be harmonious and grateful; ninth, material goods, food, and wealth will always be abundant; tenth, one will always receive the respect and support of others; eleventh, one's wealth and treasures will never be stolen; twelfth, whatever one wishes for will all be fulfilled; thirteenth, nāgas, devas, and good spirits will always guard and protect one; fourteenth, wherever one is born, one will see the buddhas and hear the dharma; fifteenth, whatever correct dharma one hears, one will awaken to its profound meaning. If one recites and upholds the Great Compassion Heart Dhāraṇī, one will obtain these fifteen kinds of fortunate birth. All devas and humans should constantly recite and uphold it, and never be negligent." When Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva had finished speaking, before the assembly he joined his palms and stood upright. With a heart of great compassion for all sentient beings, he opened his face in a smile and then spoke the vast, perfectly complete, unobstructed Great Compassion Heart Dhāraṇī, its divine and wondrous verses. The dhāraṇī:

*Namo ratna-trayāya namaḥ ārya-avalokiteśvarāya bodhisattvāya mahā-sattvāya mahā-kāruṇikāya oṃ sarva-rabhaye sudhanadasya namas-kṛtvā imaṃ ārya-avalokiteśvara-raṃdhava namo narakindi hrīḥ mahā-vat-svāme sarva-arthato-śubhaṃ ajeyaṃ sarva-sata namo-vastu namo-vāka mavitāto tadyathā oṃ avaloki lokate karate e hrīḥ mahā-bodhisattva sarva sarva mala mala mahima hṛdayaṃ kuru kuru karmaṃ dhuru dhuru vijayate mahā-vijayate dhara dhara dhrīṇi śvarāya cala cala mama vimala muktele ehi ehi śina śina āraṣaṃ pracali viṣa viṣaṃ praśaya huru huru mara huru huru hrīḥ sara sara siri siri suru suru bodhiya bodhiya bodhaya bodhaya maitreya nārakindi dhṛṣṭina bhayamāna svāhā siddhāya svāhā mahā-siddhāya svāhā siddhā-yoge-śvarāya svāhā nārakindi svāhā māra-nara svāhā śira-siṃha-mukhāya svāhā sarva-mahā-asiddhāya svāhā cakra-asiddhāya svāhā padma-kastāya svāhā nārakindi-vagalāya svāhā mavari-śaṅkarāya svāhā namo ratna-trayāya namaḥ ārya-avalokiteśvarāya svāhā oṃ sidhyantu mantra-padāya svāhā*

(Forty-one) "If one wishes to benefit all hungry and thirsty beings and hungry ghosts by granting them coolness, one should use the Sweet Dew Hand. The mantra:

*Oṃ suru suru prasuru prasuru suru suru ya svāhā*

(Seven) "If one wishes to be free from fear at all times and places, one should use the Fearlessness-Bestowing Hand. The mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-nāya hūṃ phaṭ*

(Eight) "If one suffers from eyes that are dark and without light, one should use the Sun-Essence Maṇi Hand. The mantra:

*Oṃ dūpikāya dūpi-prāvarṇi svāhā*

(Nine) "If one suffers from febrile toxic illness and seeks coolness, one should use the Moon-Essence Maṇi Hand. The mantra:

*Oṃ susiddhikari svāhā*

(Ten) "If one seeks advancement in official position and rank, one should use the Jewel Bow Hand. The mantra:

*Oṃ ācavile svāhā*

(Eleven) "If one wishes to meet good companions quickly, one should use the Jewel Arrow Hand. The mantra:

*Oṃ kāmala svāhā*

(Twenty-four) "If one wishes to be reborn in the Brahmā heavens, one should use the Kuṇḍikā Hand. The mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-śekhara-ruṭṭaṃṭa*

(Twelve) "If one suffers from various bodily ailments, one should use the Willow Branch Hand. The mantra:

*Oṃ susiddhikari-vartānaṃ-tamukta-ye vajra vajra-bandha-hana-hana hūṃ phaṭ*

(Thirteen) "If one wishes to eliminate all evil obstructions and difficulties, one should use the White Whisk Hand. The mantra:

*Oṃ padmanī-bhagavati-mohaya-jaga-mohanī svāhā*

(Fourteen) "If one wishes for all kin to be harmonious, one should use the Jewel Vase Hand. The mantra:

*Oṃ kali-saṃ-manyam svāhā*

(Fifteen) "If one wishes to repel all tigers, wolves, and evil beasts, one should use the Shield Hand. The mantra:

*Oṃ yakṣa-sannāya-candra-dhanupari-ya paśa paśa svāhā*

(Sixteen) "If one wishes to be free from official difficulties at all times and places, one should use the Battle-Axe Hand. The mantra:

*Oṃ viraya viraya svāhā*

(Twenty-eight) "If one wishes to command all ghosts and spirits to comply without resistance, one should use the Skull Staff Hand. The mantra:

*Oṃ dhūna-vajra*

(Twenty-nine) "If one wishes the buddhas of the ten directions to swiftly extend their hands, one should use the Rosary Hand. The mantra:

*Namo ratna-trayāya oṃ anabhadī-nijaye-siddhi-siddha-ṛṣiṃ svāhā*

(Four) "If one wishes to subdue all demons and malevolent spirits, one should use the Jewel Sword Hand. The mantra:

*Oṃ diśe-diśe-jaṭu-vinī-jaṭu-divā-dhāya hūṃ phaṭ*

(Six) "If one wishes to vanquish all enemies, one should use the Vajra Pestle Hand. The mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-ghinī-praṇi-padāya svāhā*

(Thirty-two) "If one wishes for good spirits and nāga kings to always come and protect one, one should use the Kuśa Iron Hook Hand. The mantra:

*Oṃ aṅkuṣa-tāraka-rā-viśaye namo svāhā*

(Thirty-three) "If one wishes to compassionately protect all sentient beings, one should use the Monk's Staff Hand. The mantra:

*Oṃ narti-narti-narṭa-pati-narte-dāya-paṇi hūṃ phaṭ*

(Eighteen) "If one wishes to attain various kinds of merit, one should use the White Lotus Hand. The mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-viraya svāhā*

(Nineteen) "If one wishes to be reborn in the pure lands of the ten directions, one should use the Blue Lotus Hand. The mantra:

*Oṃ kili kili vajra-bhūra-bandha hūṃ phaṭ*

(Twenty-one) "If one wishes to see all the buddhas of the ten directions face to face, one should use the Purple Lotus Hand. The mantra:

*Oṃ sara sara vajra-kara hūṃ phaṭ*

(Twenty-five) "If one wishes to be reborn in the celestial palaces, one should use the Red Lotus Hand. The mantra:

*Oṃ śaṅkale svāhā*

(Twenty) "If one wishes to attain vast and great wisdom, one should use the Jewel Mirror Hand. The mantra:

*Oṃ vispu-ranara-kṣa-vajra-maṇḍa hūṃ phaṭ*

(Thirty-one) "If one wishes to perfect eloquent and wonderfully skillful speech, one should use the Jewel Seal Hand. The mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-nīḍhaṃ-jaye svāhā*

(Thirty-nine) "If one wishes the buddhas of the ten directions to swiftly come and bestow the mark of prophecy by touching one's crown, one should use the Crown-Transformation-Buddha Hand. The mantra:

*Oṃ vājri-vivajraṃ-gi svāhā*

(Thirty-four) "If one wishes all ghosts, spirits, nāgas, snakes, tigers, wolves, lions, humans, and non-humans to always be respectful and devoted, one should use the Joined Palms Hand. The mantra:

*Oṃ visara visara hūṃ phaṭ*

(Twenty-two) "If one wishes to find various treasures hidden underground, one should use the Jewel Casket Hand. The mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-pāśa-kari-gana-ṃra hūṃ*

(Twenty-three) "If one wishes to swiftly attain the path of buddhahood, one should use the Five-Colored Cloud Hand. The mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-kari-raṭṭaṃṭa*

(Twenty-six) "If one wishes to repel invading enemies and adversaries from other regions, one should use the Jewel Halberd Hand. The mantra:

*Oṃ samaye-ghinī-hari hūṃ phaṭ*

(Twenty-seven) "If one wishes to summon all devas and good spirits, one should use the Jewel Conch Hand. The mantra:

*Oṃ śaṅkale mahā-saṃ-manyam svāhā*

(One) "If one wishes to be endowed with abundant merit, resources, and provisions, one should use the Wish-Fulfilling Jewel Hand. The mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-vatara hūṃ phaṭ*

(Two) "If one seeks peace and safety amid various kinds of distress, one should use the Lasso Hand. The mantra:

*Oṃ kili-lā-moṇḍra hūṃ phaṭ*

(Three) "If one suffers from various abdominal ailments, one should use the Jewel Bowl Hand. The mantra:

*Oṃ kili kili vajra hūṃ phaṭ*

(Seventeen) "If one desires sons, daughters, or servants, one should use the Jade Ring Hand. The mantra:

*Oṃ padma-ṃ-viraya svāhā*

(Thirty) "If one wishes to attain the most supreme and wondrous Brahma-voice, one should use the Jewel Bell Hand. The mantra:

*Namo padma-pāṇaye oṃ amṛtaṃ gaṃbhī-śriye-bhūri-kṣirṇi svāhā*

(Five) "If one wishes to vanquish all celestial māras and non-Buddhist practitioners, one should use the Vajra-Pestle Hand. The mantra:

*Oṃ nibhe nibhe nipāya-mahā-śriye svāhā*

(Thirty-five) "If one wishes never to be apart from the buddhas in every lifetime, one should use the Transformation-Buddha Hand. The mantra:

*Oṃ candra-bhaṃ-ṭṭari-kari-dhī-giri-dhī-giri-ni hūṃ phaṭ*

(Thirty-six) "If one wishes in every lifetime to be born in the palaces of the buddhas and never receive a body through a womb, one should use the Transformation-Palace Hand. The mantra:

*Oṃ visara visara hūṃ phaṭ*

(Thirty-seven) "If one wishes to be intelligent, learned, broadly educated, and never forgetful, one should use the Jewel Sūtra Hand. The mantra:

*Oṃ aharaḥ sarva-vidyā-dhara-pūṇite svāhā*

(Thirty-eight) "If one wishes that from this body to the attainment of buddhahood, the mind of awakening should never retrogress, one should use the Non-Retrogression Golden Wheel Hand. The mantra:

*Oṃ śanamī-ca svāhā*

(Forty) "If one wishes for the fruition and ripening of all crops and grains, one should use the Grape Hand. The mantra:

*Oṃ amala-khaṇḍenī-nī svāhā*

When Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva had finished speaking this spell, the great earth shook in six ways, jeweled flowers rained down from the sky, the buddhas of the ten directions were all filled with joy, the celestial māras and non-Buddhist practitioners were terrified with their hair standing on end, and all in the assembly attained realization: some attained the fruit of stream-entry, some the fruit of once-returning, some the fruit of non-returning, some the fruit of arhatship, and some attained the first, second, third, fourth, fifth, and up to the tenth bhūmi. Countless sentient beings generated the mind of awakening.
