# 佛說無量壽佛化身大忿迅俱摩羅金剛念誦瑜伽儀軌法
## The Buddha's Teaching on the Ritual Method of Vajra Recitation Yoga for the Great Wrathful Swift Kumāra, Transformation Body of Amitāyus Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1223

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Vajrabodhi (金剛智).

---

> I now, following the yoga,
> Expound from the Vajraśekhara Sūtra
> The blazing vajra division's
> Western method of recitation.
>
> If there are practitioners
> Who receive this upon the crown of their heads,
> They should first from an ācārya
> Have received the abhiṣeka.
>
> Next, one should select a pure place:
> Amid rivers, streams, and springs,
> On mountain peaks, in araṇya retreats,
> Places where sages attained the path,
>
> Or within a monastery,
> Pure and broadly adorned,
> Or any place one delights in,
> Wishing to practice the method of siddhi,
> Upholding it with diligence.
>
> Among all the vajra divisions,
> This method is most profound and subtle.
> Through the power of mantra empowerment,
> It subjugates and attracts.
>
> Contemplating the buddhas,
> Reflecting upon the presiding deity,
> The power of the mantras is beyond compare,
> Able to crush great obstacles.
>
> Merely holding the mantra phrases,
> Transformation buddhas emerge from the mouth.
> Various auspicious events
> Come of their own accord as one contemplates.
>
> Homage to Amitāyus!
> May the power of this merit
> Swiftly bring transcendent siddhi.
>
> Having broadly made great vows,
> Next one should bow to all buddhas
> And all great bodhisattvas.
>
> Confess the transgressions of the three karmas:
> "From past lives onward,
> I have revolved in birth and death.
> Now before the great holy lord,
> I repent with all my heart.
> As the former buddhas repented,
> So too do I now."
>
> The stainless mantra:

*Oṃ svabhāva-śuddha sarva-dharma svabhāva-śuddho 'haṃ.*

> Next the practitioner should bow
> To all the holy assembly.
> With one mantra and one bow,
> The gathered merits of prostration
> Are like turning all the sūtras;
> Doubts are dispelled and the mind is settled.
>
> The prostration mantra:

*Namo buddhāya namo dharmāya namaḥ saṃghāya huru huru siddho ruci kiri bhā kiri bhā taśi sittani bhu bhuruni svāhā.*

> Next form the gate-opening mudrā:
> Both hands in vajra fists,
> With the sound *hūṃ*, raise the mudrā
> And strike the gate to alert and awaken.
> This can crush inner and outer māras.

The mantra:

*Hūṃ hūṃ hūṃ.*

> The kuṇḍikā water-holding mudrā:
> The samādhi hand spread flat,
> The dhyāni finger presses the patience-precept nail.
> Empower with the secret mantra seven times.

The mantra:

*Oṃ amṛte hūṃ phaṭ.*

> Take a willow branch,
> Both inside and outside the body,
> Purifying the defilements of affliction.
>
> The sprinkling-purification mantra:

*Oṃ turu turu śakuru śakuru svāhā.*

> The buddha-division half-open lotus:
> The offering and power fingers assist the patience and vow fingers;
> Dhyāni and wisdom fingers below the offering and power fingers.
> At the heart, visualize the Tathāgata.

The mantra:

*Oṃ tathāgatodbhavāya svāhā.*

> The lotus samādhi mudrā:
> Ten fingers slightly bent and spread,
> Thumbs and little fingers joined.
> With one-pointed mind contemplate
> The eight-petaled Avalokiteśvara.
>
> The secret mantra:

*Oṃ padmodbhavāya svāhā.*

> Next form the vajra-division mudrā:
> Both hands with backs facing each other,
> Thumbs and little fingers hooked.
> At the heart, visualize Vajradhara.
> Scatter to the left and recite the mantra:

*Oṃ vajrodbhavāya svāhā.*

> The body-protection mudrā: internal cross,
> Patience and vow fingers erect like a banner,
> Offering and power fingers attached like hooks,
> Dhyāni and wisdom fingers press the precept fingers.
> Seal the five places to form armor.
>
> The mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-agni-praṇapta-pādāya svāhā.*

> Next, the earth-boundary mudrā:
> Patience and precept fingers wrap around the square vow finger,
> Entering the palm. Dhyāni and wisdom fingers join,
> Together with the thumbs and little fingers and the offering and power fingers.
> Dhyāni and wisdom fingers touch the ground.

The mantra:

*Oṃ kiri kiri vajra-vajri bhūr bandha bandha hūṃ phaṭ.*

> The directional vajra wall:
> Open the previous mudrā's dhyāni and wisdom fingers,
> Standing them sideways like a wall.

The mantra:

*Oṃ sara sara vajra-prakāra hūṃ phaṭ.*

> Next, invite the presiding deity:
> Samādhi and wisdom hands crossed internally,
> Wrists joined with the middle segment protruding,
> Wisdom finger erect to form the summoning gesture.

The mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-ādhṛk ihyehi bhagavan vajra-ādhṛk.*

> To dispel obstacles:
> The right wisdom hand presses the wisdom finger,
> Forming a three-pronged shape at the hip.
> The samādhi fist circles above the crown,
> Left and right completing the boundary.

The mantra:

*Namo vajrāya ha naḍuna vidmaṃ suyotsarāya hūṃ phaṭ.*

> The net mudrā follows the previous wall;
> Dhyāni and wisdom fingers pinch the offering and power fingers
> At their base, circling three times above the crown
> To form the upper net.
>
> The vajra-net mantra:

*Oṃ viṣphurad-rakṣa vajra-pañjara hūṃ phaṭ.*

> The fire-enclosure: wisdom hand covers the samādhi hand,
> Erect the dhyāni and wisdom fingers, circle rightward
> Three times to form fierce flames.

The mantra:

*Oṃ asaṃ-cagni hūṃ phaṭ.*

> Next form the flower-seat offering:
> Both hands cupped in a lotus shape,
> Slightly bent, opening eight petals.

The mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-mālāya svāhā.*

The arghya water-offering mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ gagana-samasama svāhā.*

> Paste-incense: spread the samādhi palm,
> The wisdom hand grasps the right wrist.

The mantra:

*Oṃ visara visara hūṃ phaṭ svāhā.*

> Flower offering: palms upward, crossed,
> Ring fingers joined with thumbs alongside.

The mantra:

*Oṃ bhūr ijvala takye svāhā.*

> Burning incense: backs upright and joined,
> Dhyāni and wisdom fingers alongside the offering and power fingers,
> Joined sideways. Recite the mantra:

*Oṃ visāra visara hūṃ phaṭ svāhā.*

> Food offering: palms upward and joined,
> Fire and wind wheels slightly bent,
> Two thumbs alongside the wind fingers.

The mantra:

*Oṃ vajriṇī vajra-ākye svāhā.*

> Lamp mudrā: samādhi hand in a fist,
> Patience finger erect, dhyāni finger pinching it.

The mantra:

*Oṃ candanī roca-nakari hūṃ phaṭ.*

> Universal offering throughout space:
> Vajra palms placed atop the crown,
> The mind pervading the dharma-realm.

The mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ sarvathā-kham udgate sphuraheṃ gagana-kaṃ svāhā.*

> Next, with a clear and elegant voice,
> Praise and extol the holy lord:

*Vajrasattva-saṃgraha vajra-ratna manutra vajra-dharma gāyanī vajra-karma kāru bhāvanī gāma-sugha-sa.*

> Next form the topknot mudrā:
> Samādhi fist, offering finger and dhyāni finger pinch together,
> Place at the crown and recite the mantra:

*Oṃ śikhāya svāhā.*

> Seated and unmoving,
> Evenly composed in meditative absorption,
> Contemplate within the lotus of one's own heart
> A blazing sun-disc of majestic radiance.
>
> The syllable *hūṃ* becomes a single-pronged vajra,
> Transforming into the wrathful lord.
> Right arm straight, left slightly bent,
> Treading upon a blue lotus.
>
> Body colored like yellow clouds,
> Hair red and standing straight upward,
> Adorned with jeweled necklaces,
> Waist wrapped in tiger skin.
>
> The wisdom hand raised, holding a vajra above,
> The samādhi hand below, granting fearlessness,
> Crushing the armies of heavenly māras,
> Spiritual powers beyond compare.
>
> At the first utterance of the mantra,
> Five-colored auspicious clouds appear.
> Transformation buddhas fill the sky;
> The devas come to scatter flowers.
>
> Gandharvas, kiṃnaras,
> The twenty-eight divisions,
> And all the other great sages
> Reverently join their palms and bow.
>
> Contemplating with undistracted mind,
> The great Vajra Kumāra.

The body mantra:

*Namaḥ ratna-trayāya namaś caṇḍa-vajrapāṇaye mahā-yakṣa-senāpataye tadyathā oṃ mahā-vajra-kumāra kaṇi-dhuni hūṃ hūṃ phaṭ svāhā.*

> The attendant-heart mudrā:
> The wisdom hand in a hollow fist,
> The square finger against the back of the vow finger,
> Power finger's phrase alongside the skillful-means finger,
> Pressing the back of the wisdom finger,
> Which in turn presses the back of the wisdom finger likewise.
> The index finger erect like a banner.
> Through this empowerment,
> One commands sovereign power.
> No phrase surpasses this supreme one.
>
> The attendant-heart mantra:

*Namaḥ ratna-trayāya namaś caṇḍa-vajra-kumāra oṃ kaṇi-dhuni hūṃ hūṃ phaṭ phaṭ svāhā.*

> The wrathful-heart mudrā:
> Both hands joined in a hollow clasp,
> Water fingers crossed entering the tiger-mouth,
> Wind fingers hooked, pinching the space fingers below,
> Earth fingers erect like fangs.
>
> With the true mind of great compassion,
> Rescue those in the three evil paths,
> Swiftly attaining siddhi.

The mantra:

*Hūṃ vajra-kumāra kaṇi-dhuni hūṃ hūṃ phaṭ.*

> Next, the inauspiciousness-breaking seal:
> Internally cross the earth and water fingers,
> Fire fingers bent with backs touching like armor,
> Wind and space fingers erect; the seal is complete.
>
> Whether walking or sleeping,
> While holding the mind in contemplation,
> Calamities, obstacles, and evil dreams are dispelled.
>
> Next, the spiritual-powers mudrā (also used alternatively):
> Open the two wind fingers of the previous mudrā.
> This accomplishes the vajra dharma,
> Penetrating the gate of sovereign freedom.
>
> The demon-subjugation mudrā:
> Earth fingers crossed externally,
> Wind fingers hook the two earth fingers,
> Fire and water fingers joined erect,
> Two space fingers hold the fire-finger tips.
>
> Merely forming this empowered seal,
> Celestial music and clouds of offerings appear.
> Whatever the mind desires becomes reality;
> The wrathful one subdues all māras.
>
> Water fingers crossed with fire and wind protruding,
> Fire and wind fingers bent into fists,
> Earth and space fingers erect, raised to the heart.
> This secret seal of awesome spiritual power
> Crushes the sharp fangs of fierce beasts;
> Hostile māras cannot stand against it.
>
> Next form the seal of the Vidyā King:
> Following the previous demon-subjugation mudrā,
> Two space fingers pinch the base of the water fingers.
> This commands the four kings
> And all the great, powerful, and benevolent spirits,
> Shaking the great thousandfold world.
>
> The vajra-king body-protection:
> Fire finger bent, right space finger presses it,
> Water finger holds the space finger,
> Earth finger stands against the back of the water finger.
> This summons the vajra
> And grants an indestructible body,
> Imperishable across the three realms,
> Able to protect the practitioner.

The mantra:

*Namaḥ sarva-karma oṃ gaṇi-dhuni oṃ hūṃ phaṭ phaṭ svāhā.*

> To cure ailments of body and mind:
> Samādhi and wisdom hands in vajra fists,
> Wind-finger tips joined in a circle,
> Two space fingers placed beside the fire fingers.
>
> Recite the mantra seven times.
> When one contemplates with a wrathful mind,
> Ghosts and spirits that create obstacles
> Are subdued and retreat in disarray.

The mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-kumāra hana hana nāhana nāhana paca paca cinda cinda bhinda bhinda gṛhṇa gṛhṇa hūṃ hūṃ phaṭ phaṭ.*

> The vajra-joy mudrā:
> Right hand in a hollow fist,
> Space finger presses the backs of the water and wind fingers,
> Thumb and patience fingers extended; the mudrā is complete.
> This is called "Vajra Constant Mindfulness."
>
> All devas and worldly beings
> Generate love and sympathetic joy,
> Able to crush inner and outer obstacles.
>
> Next, the vajra-palm cross:
> The wisdom hand in a single-pronged form,
> The vow and square fingers enter the palm,
> The wisdom finger is spread open,
> Circling and raising to form the mudrā.
>
> The wrathful vajra-cross,
> Like battling against the asuras.
>
> The heart mantra:

*Oṃ guru guru saphan saphan.*

> Join the rosary in the palms;
> Empower it with the mantra.

The mantra:

*Oṃ kiri kiri mudri ca svāhā.*

> When the practitioner has finished recitation,
> Hold up the rosary and make great vows.
> Form the samādhi mudrā
> And enter the dharma-dhātu samādhi.
>
> Cultivate the revolving dhāraṇī:
> All dharmas are fundamentally unborn,
> Their self-nature beyond verbal expression,
> Pure and free from all defilement,
> Karma and its causes like empty space.
>
> Revolving, deeply contemplate:
> Syllable by syllable, realize true reality.
> Though beginning and end may differ,
> What is realized all returns to one.
>
> Without forsaking this samādhi,
> Also abide in unconditioned compassion.
> May all sentient beings universally
> Attain this very ground.
>
> Emerging from samādhi, form the root mudrā.
> After reciting the root mantra seven times,
> Make universal offerings and wonderful praises.
>
> The fire-enclosure and boundaries:
> From the crown, circle leftward to release them.
> Dismiss the invitation with an outward gesture,
> Adding "gaccha gaccha."
>
> Then form the vajra-bond:
> Two fire fingers in a lotus shape,
> From the heart to the face, scatter;
> At the crown, join in lotus palms.
>
> The farewell mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-mokṣa muḥ.*

> As before, protect the body again,
> Bow universally to all the holy assembly,
> Then go freely about one's activities.
>
> A brief account of the homa
> And the method for accomplishing siddhi:
> The hearth faces the maṇḍala,
> Positioned opposite and separately made,
> Square or round according to what is sought.
>
> Fill the hearth halfway with blazing coals.
> With ghee, honey, benzoin,
> Mustard seeds, sandalwood, frankincense,
> Agarwood, storax, white glue,
> Milk-porridge mixed with fragrant medicines,
> Form one hundred and eight pills.
>
> The detailed methods are as in the sūtra.
> One mantra, one casting into the fire.
> The presiding deity and the retinue
> Rejoice and fulfill one's prayers.
>
> Furthermore, with milk-porridge,
> White mustard seeds, sesame,
> Rice, realgar, and similar,
> Mixed with white-sap wood and ghee,
> Cast into the hearth to obtain manifest results.
>
> The messenger fire-food method:
> Ghee, honey, milk, curd, and similar,
> Frankincense, white glue incense,
> Carrots, rice,
> Costus-root incense, cane sugar,
> Rock sugar, cypress leaves, and such.
> Pound and mix into one hundred and eight pills.
> One mantra per pill cast
> Into the hearth as offerings to the messengers.
>
> All devas, nāgas, yakṣas,
> Gandharvas, asuras,
> The four kings and their retinues,
> And the vināyakas,
> All rejoice and grant their protection.
>
> On the eighth or fifteenth of each month,
> Regularly prepare the hearth for offerings,
> With various flowers, incense, and food.

The mantra for summoning the messengers:

*Namaḥ ratna-trayāya namaś caṇḍa-vajrapāṇaye mahā-yakṣa-senāpataye māteṅga-kumāra oṃ kaṇi-dhuni hūṃ phaṭ svāhā.*

> Next, the method for painting the image of the presiding deity.
> Height of one *chi* and five *cun*,
> Standing in a T-shaped pose,
> Feet treading upon blue lotus flowers.
>
> Body colored like yellow clouds,
> Hair red and wildly twisted upward,
> Adorned with various jeweled necklaces,
> Rings and bracelets to ornament the body,
> Waist wrapped in tiger skin.
>
> The left hand holds a vajra,
> The right hand below grants fearlessness,
> Depicted in a most swift form.
>
> To the left, paint one vajra attendant,
> Four-armed, yellow-cloud colored,
> Holding a vajra, wheel, lasso, and knife.
> To the right, two attendant messengers:
> One hand forming a fist mudrā,
> The next holding a skull-cup,
> With nāgas coiled around waist, arms, and thighs.
>
> In front, the kumāra assembly;
> The eight divisions surround them.
> Above, five-colored auspicious clouds;
> The devas scatter wondrous flowers.
> Indra and his heavenly army,
> The four kings and their messengers,
> And the vināyakas
> All receive the command and serve.
>
> The painter should observe pure fasting.
> When the image is complete, make extensive offerings.
>
> Next, a separate method for painting the image:
> Body five *chi* and eight *cun* tall,
> Colored like a red lotus,
> Eyes two *cun* long.
>
> Garments of ochre-yellow,
> Feet treading upon golden lotus flowers.
> The right hand holds a vajra
> Raised above as if about to strike;
> The left hand holds a golden lotus.
>
> The hair is sparse, yellowish, and spiraling to the right,
> With a single topknot as a crown.
> Above the hairline by two *cun*,
> Within the hair stands a transformation buddha.
>
> To the right, a staff; to the left, a water-flask.
> On either side behind, one buddha each;
> The one holding the staff is golden.
>
> The holy ones surround his body;
> Swirling clouds of five-colored radiance.
>
> When performing the practice and recitation,
> Face the image and complete the required count
> As prescribed in the sūtra,
> With compassion for sentient beings.
>
> Should one give rise to wrath,
> The Vajra Being will immediately manifest
> In his most wrathful and swift body,
> Able to make all who see him tremble.
>
> The practitioner should at all times
> Keep body, speech, and mind pure.
