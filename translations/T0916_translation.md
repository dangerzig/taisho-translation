# 受五戒八戒文
## Text for Receiving the Five Precepts and the Eight Precepts

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0916

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown

---

## The Eight Precepts

### Section One: Bowing in Homage and Requesting Witnesses

We, your disciples, bow with joined palms to the unsurpassed, all-knowing wisdom throughout the Dharma-realm of empty space, to those who have taken form and manifested bodies to attain true enlightenment, to the Mahāyāna of the one vehicle of Mahā-prajñā-pāramitā, to the holy ones of the ten bhūmis and the great beings of the three stages of worthiness, to the various spirits governing the visible and invisible realms of empty space, to the devas of the form and formless realms, to Brahmā and his retinue, to the world-protecting Śakra and his assisting ministers, and to Yama, lord of the courts of the unseen realm. We humbly pray that today, with your pure divine eye, you will behold this assembly of people who, with reverent sincerity and single-minded devotion, respectfully invite you.

### Section Two: Cleansing and Confessing Past Karma

We, your disciples, together with all those in this practice-hall: from beginningless time until the present day, our accumulated transgressions are immeasurable and impossible to fathom. The offenses amassed over countless lifetimes can scarcely be reckoned. Toward the Three Treasures we have given rise to the mind of disbelief. Before the honored ones of the ten directions we have engaged in impure conduct. We have given free rein to the body in killing, stealing, and sexual misconduct; we have given free rein to the mouth in false speech, frivolous talk, and harsh words; we have given free rein to the mind in greed, anger, and delusion. From these have proliferated boundless evil karma. We have cycled through the three realms of existence and been submerged in the four torrents. The five hindrances have veiled our minds and the ten entanglements have confused our nature, leaving us long resident among ordinary beings with no prospect of liberation. The Sanskrit word *kṣamā* is rendered in Chinese as "repentance of faults." From this day forward, we sever the mind of continuity, and pray that all past transgressions be entirely extinguished.

### Section Three: Obtaining the Precepts through the Three Refuges

We, your disciples, together with all those in this practice-hall: take refuge in the Buddha, honored as supreme among those with the two perfections. Take refuge in the Dharma, honored as supreme in freedom from desire. Take refuge in the Saṃgha, honored as supreme among assemblies. We humbly pray that the Three Treasures will compassionately bear witness, take pity upon us, and embrace us. We, your disciples, together with all those in this practice-hall: having taken refuge in the Buddha, having taken refuge in the Dharma, having taken refuge in the Saṃgha, we receive the eight precepts, the discipline of proximate dwelling. We pray that the honored ones will remember and uphold us, and with compassion keep us in their thoughts.

### Section Four: Questioning and Explanation of the Precepts

Sons of the Buddha! Listen carefully. The eight precepts are as follows: first, not killing; second, not stealing; third, not engaging in any sensual desire; fourth, not speaking falsely; fifth, not drinking alcohol or eating meat; sixth, not anointing the body with fragrant oils or adorning oneself with garlands and flowers; seventh, not deliberately going to watch or listen to singing, dancing, or musical performances; eighth, not sitting or lying on high or luxurious beds, and not eating after midday. Beginning from this moment until the next morning's dawn, for one day and one night, following the Buddha in going forth from home, during this time you must not transgress. Can you uphold them?

### Section Five: Dedication and Making of Vows

We, your disciples, together with all those in this practice-hall: all the wholesome conditions of receiving the precepts we now dedicate entirely to all sentient beings throughout the Dharma-realm. May the delusions of false attachment be dispelled and the obstacles of ignorance exhausted. May the non-discriminating wisdom clearly manifest before us. May the practice-gates of phenomenal cultivation be vigorously studied. May we travel throughout and journey across the Flower-Treasury World. May we deeply accord with the fundamental Tathāgata-garbha mind. May those who have not yet realized it realize it; may those who have not yet perfected it perfect it. Throughout the ocean of sentient beings, may all attain Buddhahood.

## The Five Precepts

All ordinary beings, deluded about the nature of original awakening, follow false thoughts and transmigrate within the three realms of existence. Thus the six destinies are differentiated, and the realms of humans and devas are separated. The Buddha established five precepts for people to receive and uphold, entering the principle of true reality through the gate of skillful means.

### Section One: Sincere Invocation and Request

We, your disciples, together with all those in this practice-hall: we respectfully address the perfectly awakened ones, those who turn the Dharma-wheel, and those who enter parinirvāṇa, throughout the Dharma-realm of empty space in the ten directions. You are our true spiritual friends, our great physician-teachers, who embrace us. The boundless Dharma-treasury, the single flavor of the finest elixir, the full and complete sound of the Mahāyāna teaching, is our good medicine, is our boat and raft, which ferries us across the river of birth-and-death. The holy assembly of the Three Vehicles, the bhikṣus of the eight grades, the śrāvakas tending toward quiescence, and the bodhisattvas overflowing with compassion: toward us they give rise to the mind of pity and sympathy, caring for us as for the sick, saving and protecting us. We humbly pray that through the inconceivable power of the Three Treasures' great vows, from within the true nature you will give rise to unconditioned compassion and today accept our invitation.

### Section Two: Repentance of Past Transgressions

We, your disciples, together with all those in this practice-hall: through kalpas of delusion until the present day, because we have not clearly understood the truth of ultimate meaning, the gates of obstruction have arisen, the afflictions of both emotional and cognitive kinds. Confused about phenomena and confused about principle, encountering objects we create karma, following conditions we commit offenses. The body gives rise to three branches of offense; the mouth commits four kinds of faults; the three mental karmas grow ever deeper and deeper. Like grains of sand and specks of dust, their number is immeasurable. Unaware that the casket of the body contains the four poisonous snakes, the five outcastes, and the six empty villages of the sense-gates, we have accepted the deceptive friendship of countless unwholesome conditions, driving the discriminating mind through the five destinies in endless cycles. From this we have accumulated eighty-four thousand kinds of human failings and committed every manner of evil. Beginning from today, we examine our body and mind, lay everything bare before the Buddha, and pray that all may be extinguished.

### Section Three: Turning from Error to Reverence

We, your disciples, together with all those in this practice-hall: take refuge in the Buddha, the unsurpassed honored one. Take refuge in the Dharma, honored as supreme in freedom from desire. Take refuge in the Saṃgha, honored as supreme among assemblies. From this day forward, we take the Buddha as our teacher and will never again take refuge in other paths of evil māras or non-Buddhist teachings. We humbly pray that the Three Treasures will compassionately bear witness, take pity upon us, and embrace us.

We, your disciples, together with all those in this practice-hall: having taken refuge in the Buddha, having taken refuge in the Dharma, having taken refuge in the Saṃgha. The Buddha is the Bhagavān, the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Samyak-saṃbuddha. He is our great teacher. We now take refuge. May the Buddha embrace us.

### Section Four: Explanation of the Precept-Marks and Combined Practice

Sons of the Buddha! Listen carefully. The precepts of the upāsaka are as follows: first, not killing; second, not stealing; third, not engaging in sexual misconduct; fourth, not speaking falsely; fifth, not drinking alcohol or eating meat. Beginning from this moment until the end of future lives, during this time you must not transgress. Can you uphold them?

### Section Five: Dedication to the Ocean of Vows

We, your disciples, together with all those in this practice-hall: the precepts we have just received, we dedicate toward the awakening of the ocean of true suchness nature. May all sentient beings throughout the four torrents and three realms of existence generate the mind of enlightenment and cultivate the practice of Samantabhadra. May the Buddha-seed never be severed; may we be born into the family of the Tathāgata. May we take up great compassion and pass on the teaching to others in turn. May we obtain the jewel hidden in the topknot and discover the pearl concealed in the robe. May we take our seat upon the place of awakening, and may all attain Buddhahood.
