# 寂照神變三摩地經
## Sūtra on the Samādhi of Tranquil Illumination and Spiritual Transformation

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0648

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Xuanzang (玄奘)

Bodhisattva-mahāsattvas numbering as many as the atoms in ten koṭi buddha-lands were present, and all had reached the stage of non-retrogression. All had accomplished the boundlessness of the realm of space. They had accomplished the unobstructed equality of the dharma-realm. They had faith in and understanding of the varied fruition arising from karma, and faith in and understanding of the various results arising from causes, with all dharmas arising like an imprint from an imprint. Through the wisdom of equality of nature, they saw all dharmas as being like reflections and images, and through the nature of equality, they saw all dharmas as being like the sound of an echo in a valley, whose nature is one of expressive equality. All had attained the inconceivable liberation and superior concentration, and were established in the heroic-progress samādhis. They were established in the ability to manifest boundless forms of buddha-bodies. Having perfected all dhāraṇīs, in a single pore they could universally manifest all buddha-lands; in a single pore they could universally manifest both death and birth: descent from Tuṣita, entering the womb, birth, leaving the city to go forth, demonstrating the practice of difficult austerities, approaching the seat of enlightenment, vanquishing Māra's army, attaining perfect enlightenment, turning the wheel of the true dharma, and finally manifesting entry into mahāparinirvāṇa. Having accomplished the ability, while seated in a single cross-legged posture, to pervade all worlds in the ten directions with the wisdom of omnipresence, they could universally manifest in all assemblies of all tathāgatas in all worlds in the ten directions a single tathāgata, and in the assembly of one tathāgata could universally manifest all tathāgatas. In expounding the boundlessness of all dharmas, they were entirely skillful, having reached the boundless limit of all dharmas. Entering the boundless net of various illusions, they could universally manifest beings of boundless and limitless kalpas. Within their own bodies they could universally realize and sustain all sentient beings' bodies with skillful conviction. Within a single body they could universally realize and sustain all tathāgatas' bodies with skillful conviction. Within a single buddha-body they could universally realize and manifest all tathāgatas' bodies, and were entirely skillful with regard to all other sentient beings' bodies without remainder. Within their own bodies they could universally realize and manifest all the worlds of the ten directions with full skillfulness. Within a single dharma-body they could universally manifest and pervade all sentient beings of the three times, and entering samādhi in a single body, they could manifest boundless sentient beings' bodies emerging from it. Manifesting the realization of perfect enlightenment in a single body, they could universally manifest bodies resembling all sentient beings. In each and every sentient being's body they could universally manifest one sentient being's body, and furthermore, within a single sentient being's body they could universally manifest all sentient beings' bodies. Within sentient beings' bodies they could manifest the dharma-body, and within the dharma-body they could manifest sentient beings' bodies. Within all bodhisattvas' vows they could realize and sustain the skillfulness of vow-lessness. They could manifest for sentient beings the buddhas' realization of perfect enlightenment, and the realization of perfect enlightenment at the places of vow-power. For sentient beings whose faculties had already matured, in accordance with the transformations appropriate to them, they could manifest supreme perfect enlightenment and bring to rest the vow-lessness of all kalpas. Within all sentient beings' bodies they could universally manifest the sovereignty of vow-power, transferring the consciousness-body and establishing the wisdom-body. They could universally manifest the cessation of their own bodies and the fulfillment of the vows of other sentient beings' bodies. They could universally manifest the accomplishment of the great vows of all sentient beings. In each and every world they could individually manifest all the kalpas, practicing the bodhisattva conduct without interruption. From a single pore, by the power of great vows, they could manifest journeying through all buddha-lands. In each and every one of the inexpressibly many worlds, they could universally manifest becoming a perfectly awakened buddha. In a single phrase of dharma they could universally manifest and expound without remainder, pervading all the dharma-realms without exception. They could rain down the taste of the marvelous amṛta arising from the vast dharma-stream. They could universally illuminate all the illuminations of liberation, roaring the sound of true dharma-thunder, fulfilling the great vows that fill all realms of sentient beings. They could universally realize the domains of meditation, liberation, superknowledges, illumination, and wisdom in a single arising of thought. In all worlds of the ten directions, at the places where living beings die and are reborn in the cycle of existence, they could manifest all the differentiated bodily forms of the sentient beings already born there. Regarding the unobstructed wisdom of knowing one's own mind, the wisdom of knowing others' minds, the mind of one sentient being, and the wisdom that perceives the mental activity of all sentient beings, they were entirely skillful. Realizing the marvelous wisdom of the Tathāgata's ten powers in a single kṣaṇa, they were entirely skillful. They could universally realize the unobstructed wisdom that reaches all kalpas of the three times, and the marvelous wisdom that manifests the continuity of other sentient beings, all with full skillfulness. In each and every mental kṣaṇa they could manifest all sentient beings in all the places of activity in all worlds of the ten directions, all with full skillfulness. Furthermore, from the perception of a single sentient being, they could exhaustively realize the marvelous wisdom that directly perceives the actions of all sentient beings, all with full skillfulness. From the speech-sounds of a single sentient being, they could realize and manifest the marvelous wisdom of the speech-sounds of all sentient beings, all with full skillfulness. Taking one body as the basis, they could universally manifest all the bodies in all worlds, all with full skillfulness. In the assembly of one tathāgata, they could realize and manifest the teaching and reception of dharma in all tathāgatas' assemblies, all with full skillfulness. In all tathāgatas' assemblies, they could realize and manifest the teaching and reception of dharma in the assembly of a single tathāgata, all with full skillfulness. They had all attained all the marvelous dhāraṇīs, realized and upheld excellent decisive eloquence, and could expound the faculties of all sentient beings' realms, all with full skillfulness. Taking the mind of a single sentient being as their object, the inexpressible turning toward supreme enlightenment and the wisdom that awakens all sentient beings' minds, they were entirely skillful. With a single vocal utterance they could universally instruct all worlds, could fully understand the differentiated inclinations of all sentient beings, and could illuminate the continuity of other sentient beings, all with full skillfulness. With a single mental moment of recollection they could realize the kalpas of the prior extent of all sentient beings, universally manifest the karmic fruition of what they had done, and in accordance with what was appropriate awaken sentient beings and cause them all to see directly, all with full skillfulness. They could universally adorn all worlds, all with full skillfulness. They were skillful in realizing all worlds, in the equality-awakening of all buddhas, and in the self-nature practice-vows of the bodhisattva's great vows, and could fully understand the radiance of the dharma-light they cast, all with full skillfulness. They could cause inexpressibly many worlds to enter a single atom, all with full skillfulness. They could cause a single atom's extent to equal all worlds, all with full skillfulness. In one buddha-land they could universally manifest all buddha-lands, all with full skillfulness. They could contain the vast water of all oceans within a single pore, and travel through worlds spanning the dharma-realm without harming sentient beings, all with full skillfulness. They could cause the contents of inexpressibly many worlds to enter their own bodies and universally manifest all sentient beings' activities, all with full skillfulness. They could contain countless, inconceivable, incalculable, limitless, inexpressible small iron-ring mountains, great iron-ring mountains, and other great mountains within a single pore, and travel through all worlds without frightening sentient beings, all with full skillfulness. They could compress inexpressibly many kalpas into a single kalpa and extend a single kalpa into inexpressibly many kalpas, manifesting therein all the differentiations of formation and destruction, all with full skillfulness. For sentient beings to be guided, universally in all the various worlds, in accordance with what was fitting, they could manifest water-disasters, fire-disasters, or wind-disasters, all with full skillfulness. With the tip of the right foot they could toss countless, inconceivable, immeasurable worlds without harming any sentient being, all with full skillfulness. All had already reached the stage of the dharma-cloud, and could bear the vast calamities, anxieties, famines, and perils of all sentient beings to be guided in the ten directions, all with full skillfulness, without harming any other sentient being. By the power of superknowledges they could manifest the arising of a buddha in worlds where there was no buddha. Such immeasurable and boundless merits were all fully accomplished.

Also present were five hundred bodhisattva-mahāsattvas, with the bodhisattva Bhadrapāla as their chief. All had reached the stage of non-retrogression.

Also present were the lord of the gods Śakra, the great Brahmā king who is sovereign of the sahā world, the four world-protecting kings, the deva-prince Maheśvara, the deva-prince of the Sun, the deva-prince of the Moon, the deva-prince Suvīra-manas, the deva-prince Suśrīma, and countless other inconceivable, incalculable, boundless deva-princes. At that time, from each and every one of the World-Honored One's pores, from each minor mark, and from each major mark, there issued forth lights of various colors, as many as the atoms in ten buddha-lands. Each of these lights universally illuminated in each of the ten directions worlds as many as the atoms in ten koṭi of buddha-lands, pervading them completely. From each of these worlds, countless koṭi-nayuta-hundreds of thousands of bodhisattvas were gathered. Each of these bodhisattvas rode on a marvelous jeweled platform, surrounded by jeweled pavilions measuring koṭi-hundreds of yojanas in length and breadth, adorned with cords of maṇi-pearls and precious jewels, magnificent with tall banners, pennants, and parasols. Immeasurable, countless, inconceivable koṭi-nayuta-hundreds of thousands of celestial maidens, surrounded in front and behind, went to Vulture Peak Mountain and approached the Tathāgata. They bowed at his feet and offered celestial flower-clouds, jewel-clouds, garment-clouds, sandalwood-clouds of firm-bodied nāga incense, and clouds of celestial music, songs, and praises, all measuring the extent of worlds, raining them down as offerings. They then withdrew to sit at one side, gazing up at the World-Honored One, their eyes never leaving him, abiding with respect.

At that time, in this great trichiliocosm, the devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, and mahoragas of vast and awesome virtue, together with Śakra, Brahmā, the world-protectors, humans, non-humans, and the assemblies of bodhisattvas, were packed together to overflowing, so that not even a space the size of a hair-tip was left unfilled.

Then, in the assembly, the bodhisattva Bhadrapāla rose from his seat, covered one shoulder, placed his right knee on the ground, pressed his palms together reverently, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I wish to ask the Tathāgata, Worthy One, Perfectly Awakened One a few questions. May you please grant permission to answer my inquiry." Having spoken thus, the World-Honored One addressed the bodhisattva-mahāsattva Bhadrapāla: "Bhadrapāla! Ask whatever you wish. I shall answer accordingly, making your heart glad."

Having said this, the bodhisattva-mahāsattva Bhadrapāla addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! Through what abiding, through what practice, through what discipline, through what roots of merit, through what diligence, through what foundation, through what skillful wisdom, through what marvelous knowledge, through what mindfulness, through what course, through what guidance, through what upholding of truth, through what armor, do the bodhisattva-mahāsattvas become irreversible with respect to supreme perfect enlightenment, without turning, without retreat, without falling away, and instead courageously advance toward supreme perfect enlightenment? How does the Tathāgata have his practice of blazing diligence? How does the Tathāgata have his marvelous knowledge? How is there great wisdom and skillful knowledge? How is there pure discipline? How is there full mindfulness, so that whatever dharma is heard is never forgotten? How does mindfulness arise so that one can fully understand the roots of merit cultivated in all past lives? How does one become skillful in recollecting past abodes and fully awaken other sentient beings? How does one fully perceive the superiority of the faculties? How does one possess the marks so as to see the Buddha, hear the dharma, and serve the sangha? How does one, while abiding in other worlds, universally behold the buddhas of boundless and limitless worlds, hear their dharma, fully uphold all of it with thorough mastery, and also broadly proclaim and explain it for others? How does one become like a flame of fire that can burn away all unroots of merit? How does one become like the bright moon that can realize all fresh, white, and pure dharmas? How does one become like the king of mountains that can uphold all supreme roots of merit? How does one become like vajra, profoundly enduring and indestructible? How does one become fearless like a mountain peak? How does one obtain excellent and pure vocal sounds with unimpeded eloquence? How does one become fully learned, analyzing all dharmas with decisive skillfulness? How does one keep one's countenance pure and always gently smiling, free from frowning? How does one become free from jealousy and miserliness? How can one's voice instruct boundless worlds? How can one place all beings of boundless and limitless worlds within a single pore, so that the sentient beings within cannot perceive where they are, and only those who should be liberated can correctly understand? How can one, in the great assemblies of all tathāgatas in the ten directions, universally manifest the ripening of sentient beings without leaving one's original place? How can one manifest within a single pore the descent from the Tuṣita heaven, dwelling in the womb, birth, leaving the city to go forth, manifesting the practice of difficult austerities, approaching the seat of enlightenment, vanquishing Māra's army, attaining perfect enlightenment, turning the marvelous wheel of dharma, entering mahāparinirvāṇa, and manifesting the abiding of the true dharma? How does one, in a single kṣaṇa of mind, fully know the mental activities of all sentient beings?"

Having spoken thus, the World-Honored One addressed the bodhisattva-mahāsattva Bhadrapāla: "Excellent, excellent! Bhadrapāla! Excellent! You are now able to ask the Tathāgata about such profound meaning. The practice you now undertake is for the benefit of many beings, for the happiness of many beings, out of compassion for the great multitudes of the world, and for the sake of bringing great benefit and happiness to devas and humans. You have now accomplished great compassion. Therefore, Bhadrapāla, listen carefully, apply your mind with utmost attention, and I shall analyze and explain it for you." The bodhisattva-mahāsattva Bhadrapāla said: "So it is, World-Honored One! We wish with joy to hear."

The Buddha addressed the bodhisattva-mahāsattva Bhadrapāla: "Bhadrapāla! There is a samādhi called Tranquil Illumination and Spiritual Transformation. It is practiced by bodhisattvas and encompasses the buddha-stage. Bodhisattva-mahāsattvas who abide in it can attain these and other immeasurable supreme merits. Bhadrapāla! What is meant by the samādhi of Tranquil Illumination and Spiritual Transformation? It is the ability to truly realize all dharmas, to penetrate their characteristics, to penetrate the characteristics of non-inversion, to penetrate the characteristics of inversion, to increase the characteristics of non-inversion, to diminish the characteristics of inversion, not to cling to one's own abiding stage, not to grasp another's abiding stage, not to rely on one's lifespan, not to cycle in saṃsāra, to fully know all matters, to cultivate śamatha so that it is manifest, to cultivate vipaśyanā contemplating karma so that it is manifest, to keep the mind settled, with mindfulness undistracted and with vitarka and vicāra stilled, far from unwholesome qualities and close to wholesome qualities, ceasing craving, hatred, and delusion, removing ignorance and drawing near to illumination, fully knowing cause and effect and distancing oneself from unknowing, exhausting craving and cutting off delight-and-attachment, with certainty regarding the Buddha, without doubt regarding the dharma, with deep faith in the sangha, with speech that is indestructible and that harmonizes with hidden meanings and inclines toward seclusion, with beautiful and pleasing words and a dignified appearance, free from attachment to flavors and not creating evil, supporting disengagement and distancing oneself from bondage, taking no pleasure in the miscellaneous affairs of the world; seeing the deep danger in saṃsāra, seeing the supreme merit in nirvāṇa, delighting in parinirvāṇa through superior aspiration; being free from flattery, free from deception, free from pretense, free from deceitful speech, free from deceitful displays, free from deceitful inquiry, always delighting in avoiding wealth, gain, and reverence; being courageously diligent and supremely enduring, free from laziness, having forever severed all hindrances, constantly delighting in encountering the ten wholesome courses of action; with the aggregate of discipline unbroken and the aggregate of concentration undisturbed, entering samāpatti and samādhi without any basis; never being sated with fulfilling the pāramitās, with mastery and transformation in the dhyānas, liberations, samādhis, and samāpattis, sporting freely in the bases of spiritual power, with the wisdom of omniscience turning freely and with good discriminating awareness. Being by nature neither dull nor mute, not under others' control, and by nature able to remain distant from prolonged affairs, attaining the abode of the good person and not regarding the evil person, distancing oneself from the foolish and delighting in meeting the wise, upholding the power of mindfulness and the power of knowledge, taking no pleasure in the miscellaneous gatherings of householders and renunciants, delighting in dwelling in seclusion, being able to endure correctly the dharmas of emptiness, signlessness, and wishlessness, and truly penetrating all dharmas. Bhadrapāla! This is called the samādhi of Tranquil Illumination and Spiritual Transformation. A bodhisattva who diligently cultivates and studies this samādhi attains the unobstructed wisdom of all dharmas.

"Furthermore, Bhadrapāla! The samādhi of Tranquil Illumination and Spiritual Transformation is the wisdom of the equality of all dharmas and the wisdom in which all verbal expression does not operate. Abandoning household affairs, taking no pleasure in the three realms, without any weakening, with the mind unattached to any dharma, embracing the true dharma and secretly guarding all dharmas, with deep faith and understanding of the fruition of dharmas, with skillfulness in the vinaya, stilling all disputes without opposition or contention, with the patience of equality inclining toward the nature of equality, with skillfulness in discerning dharmas, deciding dharmas, and understanding dharma-phrases, with wisdom in analyzing dharma-phrases, wisdom of the prior extent, wisdom of the posterior extent, wisdom of the purity of the three spheres, wisdom of bodily composure, wisdom of mental composure, wisdom of guarding deportment, wisdom of dharmic purity surpassing all objects, omniscient wisdom of the aggregates, wisdom of the equality of the elements, illumination of the sense-bases, forever severing all craving and inclining toward the realization of the unborn, illuminating causes and the cessation of karmic results, seeing the dharma and cultivating the path with delight in approaching the Tathāgata, being of keen wisdom-nature and with the discerning wisdom of letters, fully knowing sounds, and realizing joy, dharma-joy, and unfailing delight. Being gentle and upright, free from frowning, soft, agreeable, pleasing, and speaking first with welcome, greeting others with 'Welcome!' and free from all laziness, respectful and reverent, following and heeding the teacher's instructions; never complacent about saṃsāra, fully endowed with the pure white dharmas, with a pure livelihood that does not abandon tranquil abiding, established in the superior stages without damaging right mindfulness, skillful in the aggregates, skillful in the elements, skillful in the sense-bases, inclining toward the realization of superknowledges, diminishing afflictions, forever destroying the continuity of all habitual tendencies, inclining toward progress and cultivating accomplishment; skillful in the means of escaping from all offenses, able to permanently vanquish the bonds of all views, severing the latent tendencies without them arising again, possessing recollection of former lives, without doubt regarding karmic fruition, not relying on any dharma that arises in the mind, not engaging in any enterprise, not attending to the internal sense-bases, not activating the external sense-bases, neither exalting oneself nor demeaning others, without attachment to any good quality, never relying on ordinary beings; accomplishing what is difficult to accomplish as the outflow of discipline, possessing the great aggregate of radiance with self-knowledge, free from agitation and maintaining all deportments, free from anger and far from harsh speech, not harming others, attending to and protecting good friends, far from enmity, endowed with pure discipline, doing no harm, with gentle speech, not relying on the three realms, protecting intimate companions, patiently accepting the nature of emptiness and non-self in all dharmas, with keen aspiration for the wisdom of omniscience, with wisdom's light illuminating firm discipline; entering samāpatti and always delighting in solitude, with non-discriminating wisdom joyfully fulfilled, with the mind undisturbed and free from the actions of views; attaining dhāraṇī and inclining toward marvelous knowledge, with correct understanding and practice regarding what is possible and what is not, instructing and admonishing through the gateway of cause and principle, able to correctly practice in accordance with the stage of patience, far from impatience and established in the stage of knowledge, forever severing unknowing and established in marvelous knowledge, at the yogācāra stage as practiced by bodhisattvas, reaching the marvelous knowledge of the intrinsic nature of all dharmas, burning and purging the mind so that it neither arises nor continues, with unobstructed knowledge and bearing no heavy burden; the Tathāgata's marvelous wisdom curing craving, removing hatred, forever severing delusion, harmonizing with right principle and distancing from wrong principle, aspiring to wholesome dharmas and practicing with superior aspiration, drawing near to awakening, not abandoning the severing of evil and the protection of white dharmas, with roots of merit as the foremost through skillful means; forever severing all characteristics and transforming all perceptions, eliciting the sūtras and the excellent vinaya, discerning the truths with a tendency toward the realization of liberation, with definitive and singular speech that cannot be swayed by any condition, giving rise to true knowing and seeing, delighting in seeking much learning with an insatiable thirst for wisdom.

"The mind being pure, the body being pure, speech being pure and free from doubt, drawing near to emptiness, close to signlessness, without grasping the nature of wishlessness, attaining fearlessness, not demeaning those who suffer in the realms of existence, and also bestowing upon them material wealth. Not reproaching or banishing the poor, arousing compassionate awareness for transgressors, bringing about beneficial matters, embracing them through dharma and bestowing material gifts. Praising without flattery those who uphold the precepts. Able to relinquish all one's possessions, extending invitations with superior aspiration, and acting as one speaks. Repeatedly giving rise to keen and vigorous application, receiving with earnestness and delight, and accomplishing the wisdom of analogy. Skillful in the designations and conceptual labels of former times, with the realizing wisdom that forever destroys designations, not hoping for reverence, forgiving disrespect, without seeking gain, without sorrow at loss, not elated at praise, not angered by censure, not attached to acclaim, not diminished by blame, not indulging in pleasure, not turning from pain, not clinging to formations. Not being attached to true praise, not accepting untrue praise, avoiding improper conduct and practicing proper conduct, drawing near to exemplars and distancing from non-exemplars, never demeaning sentient beings with few roots of merit, and correctly upholding the Buddha's sacred teaching. With speech that is concise and a nature that is gentle, skillful in the conventions of worldly speech, able to vanquish adversaries and acting at the appropriate time. With pure deportment and dignified bearing, accomplished in the wisdom of what is beneficial and what is not. Understanding the world, understanding the treatises, with eloquent and articulate speech, delighting in practicing generosity and always extending one's hand, with the mind free from attachment and endowed with a sense of shame and conscience. Constantly repudiating unwholesome states of mind, never abandoning the virtues of the dhūta practices, upholding right conduct and practicing with rectitude, rising and going to meet elders with respect, offering seats and vanquishing arrogance, with an equanimous and motivated mind penetrating the meaning, embracing knowledge and stilling unknowing, realizing the wisdom of mind, the wisdom that can follow and perceive the intrinsic nature of mind, the skillful wisdom of what leads and what does not lead, the marvelous wisdom of the speech of all sentient beings, the marvelous wisdom of establishing various kinds of speech, the wisdom of discerning meaning far from what is meaningless, analyzing the various dhyānas without relishing any of them. Observing the minds of all sentient beings, the marvelous wisdom of knowing the superiority and inferiority of sentient beings' faculties, able to correctly observe what is the case and what is not, able to correctly discriminate all actions, the marvelous wisdom that realizes what is not karma and not fruition. Various convictions, realization and non-forgetting, able to directly perceive the various elements and non-singular elements, the vajra-like samādhi that observes nothing, possessed of the brahmā-voice in samādhi and samāpatti. The recollecting wisdom that can recall each and every event of past abodes, whether named or unnamed, the wisdom that correctly observes all pervasive conduct, and the wisdom of the time of realization when the outflows are forever exhausted. With unimpeded divine vision universally and correctly observing, manifesting all forms through the sport of superknowledges, the wisdom of equal entry into form and non-form. Understanding the various limbs of vocal sounds, the dhāraṇī-wisdom that can follow and realize, and all forms of images, echoes, and sounds. The wisdom of the equality of nature that expounds the true dharma in accordance with what is appropriate, the wisdom of turning the faculties that brings joy to all sentient beings through excellent exposition, the wisdom of observing the timely and untimely that enters the true reality. Whatever dharma is spoken is never in vain, able to fulfill all the pāramitās, the skillful wisdom that exhorts and disciplines all sentient beings. Without discrimination regarding any deportment, the marvelous wisdom that flows uncontaminated into the dharma-realm, destroying all discrimination and various discriminations."
