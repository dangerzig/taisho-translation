# 息除中夭陀羅尼經
## Dhāraṇī Sūtra for Averting Untimely Death

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1347

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dānapāla (施護).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was beside the Ganges River, together with a great assembly, including the four great world-protecting heavenly kings, with Vaiśravaṇa at their head.

At that time the World-Honored One addressed the four kings: "There is a great terror, deeply to be dreaded. You should know this. Whether male or female, youths or maidens, whether human or divine, I observe untimely death and give rise to great dread. At all times it causes disturbance, and none can rescue one another. Even old age and death are a dread exceedingly great, without end. I shall now teach you a Dharma of rescue and protection." Then the four great heavenly kings in the assembly addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! Today, before the World-Honored One, having heard this teaching, we leap with joy. We offer our bodies, lives, and possessions to the Tathāgata. May the Buddha take compassionate pity and proclaim it for us."

At that time the World-Honored One, with a snap of his fingers, stood facing east and spoke these words: "Homage to all the tathāgatas of the ten directions, of unsurpassed, perfect, and true enlightenment, vast in compassion, who take pity on sentient beings. They have all realized anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi. May you compassionately observe together, grant power to our sentient beings, and protect them with empowerment to avert untimely death." In this manner, east, west, south, north, and even above and below, all the tathāgatas proclaimed these words: "Homage to all the tathāgatas of unsurpassed, perfect, and true enlightenment, who do not forsake compassion and who, for the sake of sentient beings, have realized anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi. May you compassionately observe together, and for our sentient beings proclaim and explain the teaching.

"At that time all the buddhas together bore witness. I now proclaim the Dharma for rescuing from terror and averting difficulty. When the second turning of the Dharma-wheel is being proclaimed, all in this assembly will obtain lifespan, physical appearance, and fullness thereof. They will all be freed from the terrors of birth and death, with no untimely death, and likewise with regard to old age and sickness."

At that time all the buddhas of the ten directions illuminated every world, and the images of the tathāgatas, like sesame seeds in quantity, filled everything. Furthermore, each and every tathāgata of the ten directions spoke in unison and proclaimed the mantra:

*Tadyathā: cale calā cale viṇi-ḍiṃ svasti-ke cakra-ji-nā praśa-mantu sarva-hega anā-ku-nā cu-ca-re ca-re ke-ma-ṇi śaya-ni ma-ni śaya-ni hi-ma-śi-si-jñau-rā vi-ye hi-ku-rā-re ku-ma-ti viṣa-ma-ṇi ma-ṇi śi-śu-he-va a-ca-re vi-ca-re mā-vi-laṃ-va mu-hu mu-hu svāhā.*

At that time, when all the tathāgatas of the ten directions had spoken this secret divine mantra, they declared: "All the tathāgatas of unsurpassed, perfect, and true enlightenment have all spoken thus."

Then Vaiśravaṇa, the Heavenly King of the North, addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! Today, having heard the empowering and protecting majestic spiritual power of the tathāgatas of the ten directions, averting untimely death and quelling terrors, I shall now speak a mantra before the Buddha."

*Tadyathā: śvete śvete ri ri ri ri.*

Then Dhṛtarāṣṭra, the Heavenly King of the East, spoke a mantra before the Buddha:

*Care care svāhā.*

Then Virūḍhaka, the Heavenly King of the South, spoke a mantra before the Buddha:

*Vāri vā vā vā.*

Then Virūpākṣa, the Heavenly King of the West, standing before the Buddha, also spoke a mantra:

*Ma-daṃ-gi-ni su-ma su-mu su-mu.*

At that time the World-Honored One addressed the four heavenly kings: "This divine mantra has been attested and observed by all the buddhas. If any men or women of good family day and night receive and uphold it, sincerely remembering it, they will certainly obtain long life without untimely death. After their lives end, they will not fall into the evil destinies. Furthermore, if a person, wishing to benefit sentient beings, arouses a mind of sincere truth, receives, upholds, reads, and recites it, at the time of their death there will be no terrors, no strange and ominous signs, and no evil portents. In their present life they will be free from white leprosy, madness, and all diseases. They will be separated from dangers of water, fire, swords, weapons, epidemics, and poisons of insects and drugs, as well as thunder and hail. All manner of difficulties will be entirely kept at a distance.

"Wherever this sūtra is found, all the buddhas of the ten directions and all the bodhisattvas will come together to protect it and prevent any obstruction or difficulty. Furthermore, if a person copies and adorns this sūtra, keeps it clean and reads it aloud, the merit obtained will be no different from creating a buddha image and adorning it with various ornaments. Furthermore, if a person makes offerings to the buddhas with a focused mind free from arrogance, they will obtain auspicious blessings and disasters and difficulties will be averted. If a person receives, upholds, and copies this sūtra, constantly free from indolence, the merit obtained will likewise be the same. All the holy and worthy ones will constantly protect them and they will be separated from all calamities."

When the World-Honored One had spoken this sūtra, the four great heavenly kings, nāgas, spirits, the eight classes of beings, asuras, and all humans and devas, the entire great assembly, having heard the Dharma, joyfully believed, received it with faith, and put it into practice.
