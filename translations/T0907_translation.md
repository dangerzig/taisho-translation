# 佛頂尊勝心破地獄轉業障出三界祕密陀羅尼
## Secret Dhāraṇī of the Uṣṇīṣavijaya Heart for Breaking Open the Hells, Transforming Karmic Obstructions, and Transcending the Three Realms

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0907

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Śubhakarasiṃha (善無畏)

---

It is called the Vajra Drum. When the mouth opens and the tongue rises, it shakes the Dharma-realm palace of the lotus treasury. All the Tathāgatas emerge from samādhi and thereby crush and break open the hells, extinguishing the calamities of the seven rebirths. It initiates the teaching of the bodhisattvas, who proclaim the five-syllable secret. One receives the root-transmission and distributes the syllables according to the method. If a sovereign wears it within his crown, the myriad nations will be at peace. If a military governor or regional inspector writes the mantra upon his banners, the four quarters will be tranquil. If a city governor or garrison commander inscribes the syllables upon war-drums, the stern warning will be heard from afar. Evil influences will vanish; blazing prosperity will spread a thousand leagues. Crops will be abundantly moistened, people will be free from calamity and illness, and the local earth-spirits will bring gentle winds and timely rains. The methods of yogic practice number roughly a thousand; I shall describe only a small portion. If one writes the mantra upon war-drums during recitation and empowerment, the enemy army will surrender of its own accord, and not a single person will be harmed.

The Buddha said: "The syllable *a* is the lord of the Vajra Division, corresponding to the liver. The syllable *vaṃ* is the lord of the Padma Division, corresponding to the lungs. The syllable *raṃ* is the lord of the Ratna Division, corresponding to the heart. The syllable *haṃ* is the lord of the Karma Division, corresponding to the stomach. The syllable *khaṃ* is the lord of the Space Division, corresponding to the spleen.

"Mountains, seas, and the great earth arise from the syllable *a*. Rivers and ten thousand streams arise from the syllable *vaṃ*. Gold, jade, precious jewels, sun, moon, stars, fire-pearls, and radiance are accomplished from the syllable *raṃ*. The five grains, five fruits, and all the flowers that bloom and open bear fruit through the syllable *haṃ*. Beautiful and fragrant celestial beings, long-lived with flourishing complexion, delicious flavors, upright appearance, merit, wealth, and honor are adorned by the syllable *khaṃ*.

"The syllable *a* is Akṣobhya Tathāgata of the east. The syllable *vaṃ* is Amitābha Tathāgata of the west. The syllable *raṃ* is Ratnasambhava Tathāgata of the south. The syllable *haṃ* is Amoghasiddhi Tathāgata of the north. The syllable *khaṃ* is Vairocana Mahā-sūrya Tathāgata above."

The syllable *a* is the profoundly deep essence of emptiness and quiescence. One cannot grasp it by grasping and cannot abandon it by abandoning. It is the mother of the myriad dharmas, the great abhiṣeka king: this is the syllable *a*. The syllable *a* is a dharma difficult to believe; Hīnayāna vinaya-masters especially should not be allowed to see it.

This original text of the five divisions, in the Sanskrit, comprises four hundred thousand words. It is drawn from the Mahāvairocana Sūtra and the Vajraśekhara Sūtra, gathering together the essential and wondrous, the supreme field of merit. These five-syllable mantras alone suffice. The merit obtained by one who recites them is beyond comparison, beyond conception, and beyond description.

The five-division mantras of the Vajraśekhara Sūtra: by receiving, upholding, reading, reciting, and contemplating their principled nature, they enable people to obtain merit; the bones become firm and the body healthy. One is forever free from calamity, obstruction, and all illness and suffering. They nourish and prolong life. *A* is the Vajra Earth Division, one. *Vaṃ* is the Vajra Water Division, two. *Raṃ* is the Vajra Fire Division, three. *Haṃ* is the Vajra Wind Division, four. *Khaṃ* is the Vajra Space Division, five.

The Tathāgata's essential nature, the contemplation of the unborn. The above five-division mantras are the precious amṛta elixir of the unborn and the wondrous medicine of Buddha-nature for all the Tathāgatas. When a single syllable enters the five organs, the myriad illnesses do not arise. How much more so when one cultivates the sun-contemplation and moon-contemplation: one immediately cultivates and attains the Buddha-body of emptiness and quiescence.

The meaning of the fivefold dharma-body: *a vaṃ raṃ haṃ khaṃ*. The above five dharma-body mantras: if one recites them once daily, or seven times, twenty-one times, or forty-nine times, the merit of a single recitation is comparable to turning the canon one million times. How much more so for one who sits in meditative quiescence and enters the gate of samādhi, contemplating the syllable *a* with clear and distinct examination like the sun illuminating the sky: this is clearly seeing the Buddha-nature. The merit obtained is beyond comparison. The textual passages of the secret treasury are truly inconceivable. I only fear that Śrāvaka Dharma-teachers and Hīnayāna vinaya-practitioners will give rise to doubt and disbelief, which would instead increase their transgressions. Consider the analogy of a king who has a young child whom he loves above all: though he would empty the treasury of its precious jewels without reluctance, he alone would not give the child the swords Ganjiang and Moye, fearing that the child, not knowing how to wield them, would injure his own body. For this reason, the Tathāgata secretly transmits them to the great bodhisattvas and does not give them to the Śrāvakas of lesser wisdom.

The three mantras below represent the three kinds of siddhi. Within the method of accomplishment, the distinctions of upper, middle, and lower grades are established.

*A ra pa ca na*: this is called the exiting siddhi. It can produce roots and stems that pervade the four directions. One recitation is equivalent to turning the canon one hundred times.

*A vi ra hūṃ kha*: in the siddhi chapter of the Mahāvairocana Sūtra, it is called "the vajra word-phrase that vanquishes the four māras, liberates from the six destinies, and fulfills the wisdom of all wisdoms." This is called the entering siddhi. It can produce branches and leaves that pervade the four directions. Its radiant brilliance enters the buddha dharma-realm; hence it is called the entering siddhi. One recitation is equivalent to turning the canon one thousand times.

*A vaṃ raṃ haṃ khaṃ*: this is called the secret siddhi, also called the accomplishment siddhi, also called the susiddhi. Susiddhi means "pervading the dharma-realm." It accomplishes the fruit of Buddhahood and realizes the great enlightenment. The dharma-realm's secret means that its radiance pervades everything. Only a Buddha together with a Buddha can enter this gate; pratyekabuddhas and śrāvakas cannot illuminate it. This is also called the secret siddhi. One recitation is equivalent to turning the canon one million times.

The exiting siddhi extends from the feet to the waist. The entering siddhi extends from the navel to the heart. The secret siddhi extends from the heart to the crown. These three siddhis are as follows: the exiting siddhi accomplishes the nirmāṇakāya; the entering siddhi accomplishes the saṃbhogakāya; the secret siddhi, the susiddhi, accomplishes the dharmakāya. These are the three kinds of eternal body, the treasury of the true Dharma. Therefore, with bowed head I pay homage to Vairocana Buddha.

> With bowed head I pay homage to Vairocana Buddha,
> Whose open, pure eyes are like lotuses.
> Tamer of the three realms, teacher of devas and humans,
> The great bodhicitta, savior of the world.
> The profoundly wondrous mantra and its empowering Dharma
> Flow into the unborn gate of the syllable *a*.
> The white curl, markless, perfectly and universally enlightened,
> Perfectly full, constantly illuminating like sun and moon.
> Akṣobhya, Ratnasambhava, saviors of the world,
> Amitābha, Amoghasiddhi, the unfailing kings:
> All upon the auspicious wheel of siddhi
> Transmit this wondrous Dharma to transform all existence.
> Compassionate and sovereign Trailokyavijaya,
> Vajrasattva and Acala:
> Without violating the vow, responding at the appointed time,
> When the yoga is complete, they return to the vajra.
> I rely on Vairocana Buddha
> To open the wisdom-seal of the heart and establish the standard.
> With immeasurable merit universally adorned,
> Together entering the dhāraṇī of all the Sugatas,
> May all who have the affinity to study and practice
> Abide in peace upon the unsurpassed ocean of purity.
