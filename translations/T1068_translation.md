# 千手觀音造次第法儀軌
## Ritual Manual for the Sequential Construction Method of the Thousand-Armed Avalokiteśvara

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1068

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Śubhakarasiṃha (善無畏).

---

The principal deity's true body is of a great golden-yellow color, seated in full lotus posture upon a great jeweled lotus platform. The lotus has thirty-two petals, and between each pair of petals are various small petals, adorned with immeasurable hundreds of thousands of great maṇi jewels. The principal deity's front face has a triple-tiered crown, with a total of five hundred heads and faces. To the left and right of the central face are two additional faces: the right one is called the Lotus Face and the left is called the Vajra Face. The right face is blue-green and the left is dark-white. The front face represents the Buddha Division; because the Great Being possesses a great body, the ocean-assemblies of all three divisions are fully present. Eight great bodhisattvas form the retinue. Before the Great Being is Tóngmù, the celestial maiden, holding a beautiful flower. There is also a boy holding a sūtra in a monastic seat, appearing as a seven-year-old child. The third tier has the twenty-eight divisions, each in their own true form. The mantra says:

1. Guhyaka Vajrapāṇi, red in color, with three eyes. Right hand holds a vajra; left hand in a fist at the waist.

*Oṃ agna agna sāyata tathāgatāya svāhā*

2. Ucchūṣma Kuṇḍalī Aṅkuśa, left hand holds a single-pronged vajra, right hand in a fist at the waist. The Eight-Division Warrior Śaṅkara is green; right hand holds the wisdom-sword, left hand forms the three-pronged mudrā.

*Oṃ viṣṭāya laṃtarā ārakṣatāya svāhā*

3. Maheśvara-nārada, black-reddish in color, with three eyes and a wrathful expression. He wears a three-pronged trident as a crown and vajra jewels as a necklace. Left hand holds a mace; right hand holds a jeweled dish, red inside and black outside.

*Oṃ asaṃmuci laṃtana svāhā*

4. Kumbhīra-dhara Kapila, pinkish-white in color. Left hand holds a jeweled bow; right hand holds a jeweled arrow.

*Oṃ vaśinī ārya-samanta svāhā*

5. Bāṣpa-varuṇa, pinkish-white in color. Left hand holds a lasso; right hand at the waist.

*Oṃ ucchūṣma samaya viṭara viṭara svāhā*

6. Mānasvin Cakra-pracaṇḍara, left hand holds a vajra wheel, right hand in the fist-mudrā, red in color.

*Oṃ pūrūsata sata gaṇa gaṇa āruṣṇīya svāhā*

7. Satyamahāvīra, left hand holds a jeweled banner surmounted by a phoenix-bird; right hand forms the wish-granting mudrā.

*Oṃ vedānci gati ārakṣa svāhā*

8. Kaurāṇḍa Paṅkīra, left hand holds a vajra bell, right hand in a vajra fist, pinkish-white in color.

*Oṃ ganāya bhautāya rākṣa svāhā*

9. Pippala-garā King, left hand holds a sword; right hand at the waist.

*Oṃ māraga kṣāya ajirākṣāya namaḥ śrīyā ye svāhā*

10. Aṅgadavīta-sāhavara, left hand holds a bow; right hand holds a trident-arrow. Color is dark yellow.

*Oṃ mahā-dīpaye svāhā*

11. Brahmā Saṃpara, reddish-white in color. Left hand holds a jeweled vase; right hand holds a three-pronged vajra.

*Oṃ vara vara yakṣuti yakṣuti svāhā*

12. Pañca-vibhū Śuddhāvāsa Yamārāja, purplish-white in color. Left hand holds the Yama-banner; right hand holds a female staff.

*Oṃ vara vara hūṃ haṭa haṭa svāhā*

13. Lord Śakra of the Thirty-Three, pinkish-white in color. Left hand at the waist; right hand holds a vajra.

*Oṃ maṇika-śākya vajra-vaiḍūrīya svāhā*

14. Sarasvatī of Great Eloquent Merit, daughter of the king of the heavenly lord Śakra, a celestial maiden of great virtue, great consort of Vaiśravaṇa. Left hand holds a wish-fulfilling jewel of dark purple color; right hand holds a vajra sword.

*Oṃ mahā-guliṃ-gṛta ye svāhā*

15. King Dhṛtarāṣṭra, reddish-red and also blue-white in color. Left hand holds a wish-fulfilling jewel-king of yellow-blue color with eight facets; right hand holds a sword.

*Oṃ kṣatrīyā ye sarvahā sarvahā satā viśeṣṭha svāhā*

16. The Divine Mothers and their mighty retinue. Color as described.

*Oṃ hiri hiri tahī tuhī svāhā*

17. King Virūḍhaka, red in color. Left hand holds a mace; right hand holds a sword.

*Oṃ he i hūṃ hūṃ svāhā*

18. King Virūpākṣa, white in color. Left hand holds a mace; right hand holds a golden lasso, blue in color.

*Oṃ mādhūgī pukuṣatī svāhā*

19. Heavenly King Vaiśravaṇa, dark blue in color. Left hand holds a jeweled stūpa; right hand holds a mace.

*Oṃ namo te nam vajra-gīkṣa-vara mitānāṃ nam-viśrā-paṇḍāya varāya svāhā*

20. Golden Peacock King, body the color of gold. Left hand holds a jeweled banner surmounted by a peacock bird of exquisite color that speaks immeasurable wondrous words.

*Oṃ guguaṃg haṭa śarā-kara fāṃguaṃg haṭa śarā-kara jōubiaṃn hūṃ rā-ka oṃ haṭa svāhā*

21. The Twenty-Eight Divisions of Great Ṛṣis, being the twenty-eight celestial deities. Īśāna is their chief, body colored black-reddish-white. Left hand holds a mace; right hand holds a vermilion dish. Adorned with vajra jewels as a necklace.

*Oṃ namo buddha-dharma-saṅgha indrāya vaśinī ye svāhā*

22. Maṇibhadra, pinkish-white in color. Left hand holds a jeweled banner surmounted by a wish-fulfilling gem; right hand forms the wish-granting mudrā.

*Oṃ dhāra-kara-kyā mandā svāhā*

23. General Pāñcika, body reddish-red in color. Left hand holds a vajra; right hand at the waist.

*Oṃ ārya samāye svāhā*

24. Nanda and Upananda. Upper body is red, left hand holds a red lasso, right hand a sword, each head has five nāgas. Lower body is dark blue-green, left hand holds a blue lasso, right hand a sword. Sāgara Nāga and Elapatra: upper body reddish-white, left hand holds a red nāga, right hand a sword; lower body blue-white, left hand holds a white nāga.

*Oṃ kumānī bhau-śāṃ svāhā*

25. Asura, namely the Great-Bodied Asura. Body reddish-red. Left hand holds a sun-disc; right hand holds a moon-disc. Gandharva: left hand holds a singing lute, right hand forms the dancing-mudrā, body pinkish-white. Garuḍa King, golden, with both wings complete, left hand holds a conch, right hand holds a jeweled conch-flute. Kiṃnara and Mahoraga: these two kings are white in form, like rākṣasī women, with two, three, four, or five eyes, holding various medicine vessels, with two, four, six, or eight arms complete, adorned with celestial crowns, celestial garments, and all manner of jeweled pearls.

*Oṃ jañjā jañju mālikā svāhā*

26. The Water, Fire, Thunder, and Lightning deities, these four deities all have spouses. Thunder is the celestial thunder deity; lightning is the earth-lightning deity. The remaining ones have water and fire as their body's adornment.

*Oṃ guravā ye yu svāhā*

27. Kumbhāṇḍa King, with a long nose and wrathful visage, black in color. Left hand holds a great battle implement; right hand holds a lasso.

*Oṃ jāye ajāye svāhā*

28. Piśāca, with large eyes and wrathful visage, black-reddish in color. Left hand holds a fire-jewel.

*Oṃ tīye tīye rinda rinda svāhā*

Thus the good deities, with different mouths but a single voice, each spoke their oath, proclaiming and explaining their names and secret mantras. Leading their retinues, they surround and protect from left and right, before and behind, those who receive and uphold them. Svāhā. If any good men or good women who hold the spell recite the mantras of these good deities, they shall possess great majestic power and virtue, and so forth.
