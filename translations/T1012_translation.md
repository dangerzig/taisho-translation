# 佛說出生無量門持經

## The Sūtra on Producing Infinite Gateways of Dhāraṇī, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1012

Translated from the Chinese. Eastern Jin dynasty, translated by Buddhabhadra (佛陀跋陀羅).

---

Thus have I heard. At one time the Buddha was in Vaiśālī, in the Great Forest Monastery's multi-storied lecture hall, together with a great assembly of forty thousand bhikṣus. At that time the World-Honored One wished to relinquish the continuation of his lifespan; after three more months he would enter parinirvāṇa. He then told the Elder Mahāmaudgalyāyana: "Go forth and announce throughout the great trichiliocosm to the śrāvaka bhikṣus, those on the pratyekabuddha path, and all bodhisattvas that they should all come and gather at the Great Forest Monastery's multi-storied lecture hall."

Maudgalyāyana received the instruction and went to the summit of Mount Sumeru. He entered the as-you-wish samādhi and with a great voice announced throughout the great trichiliocosm, so that all heard and knew.

The Elder Śāriputra then thought: "I too should enter the as-you-wish samādhi and universally invite all bhikṣus dwelling throughout Jambudvīpa to come and gather at the Great Forest Monastery's multi-storied lecture hall." Having formed this intention, he entered samādhi accordingly and invited all bhikṣus dwelling in Jambudvīpa to come and assemble. At that time, three million bhikṣus gathered at the Great Forest Monastery's multi-storied lecture hall.

Then the World-Honored One addressed Akṣayamati Bodhisattva, Mañjuśrī Kumārabhūta, Apāyadurgati Bodhisattva, Aśoka Bodhisattva, Vigata-skandha-āvaraṇa Bodhisattva, Śāntaviṣayagocarī Bodhisattva, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, Gandhahastin Bodhisattva, Anantapratibhāna Bodhisattva, and Maitreya Bodhisattva: "Good sons! Go forth to the buddha-lands of the ten directions, as numerous as the sands of the Ganges, and announce to all bodhisattvas who are one-birth-from-buddhahood, bodhisattvas who have attained non-retrogression, bodhisattvas who have attained the patience of the non-arising of dharmas, and bodhisattvas of understanding-through-faith: all should come and gather at the Great Forest Monastery's multi-storied lecture hall."

These bodhisattvas immediately received the instruction and went forth, announcing throughout the buddha-lands of the ten directions as numerous as the sands of the Ganges to all bodhisattvas who were one-birth-from-buddhahood, those who had attained non-retrogression, those who had attained the patience of the non-arising of dharmas, and those of understanding-through-faith, that all should come and gather at the Great Forest Monastery's multi-storied lecture hall.

At that time, at the Great Forest Monastery's multi-storied lecture hall there gathered eighty billion hundred-thousand bodhisattvas who were one-birth-from-buddhahood, one billion hundred-thousand bodhisattvas who had attained non-retrogression, thirty billion bodhisattvas who had attained the patience of the non-arising of dharmas, and sixty billion bodhisattvas who had attained understanding-and-liberation. All these great beings gathered at once at the Great Forest Monastery's multi-storied lecture hall.

At that time, the Venerable Śāriputra, seeing the great multitude of bodhisattvas assembling like clouds, thought: "I should, relying on the Buddha's divine power, respectfully question the World-Honored One, so that these bodhisattvas may be freed from the net of doubt and swiftly achieve the profound unobstructed eloquence: hearing the dharma spoken by all buddhas throughout the ten directions and lands as numerous as the sands of the Ganges, remembering and not forgetting, and swiftly accomplishing the unsurpassed, right, and true path of most perfect enlightenment.

"Also, may the bodhisattvas attain the four inexhaustible purities of dharma-gates. What are the four? First, purification of sentient beings; second, purification of dharma; third, purification of eloquence; fourth, purification and adornment of buddha-lands.

"May they attain the four supreme joys and harmonies. What are the four? First, harmony of body; second, harmony of speech; third, harmony of mind; fourth, harmony of skillful means.

"May they swiftly accomplish the four kinds of wondrous dhāraṇī gates. What are the four? First, the dhāraṇī of producing infinite gateways; second, the dhāraṇī of patience with the profound dharma; third, the dhāraṇī of skill regarding the faculties of sentient beings; fourth, the dhāraṇī of skill regarding the causes and effects of sentient beings."

The Venerable Śāriputra, contemplating these purifications and the immeasurable wisdom-ground of the bodhisattvas, wished the Buddha to expound upon them, and so questioned the World-Honored One with this meaning.

Thereupon the World-Honored One praised Śāriputra: "Excellent, excellent! Śāriputra! You are able to ask such a question on behalf of the bodhisattvas. Śāriputra! Listen attentively, listen attentively! Reflect well upon it. I shall now explain the profound merit of the bodhisattva's practice that swiftly accomplishes the unsurpassed, right, and true path.

"Śāriputra! If one wishes to attain omniscience and know all dharmas, one should study this dhāraṇī gate, should study these dhāraṇī phrases: the so-called 'no-phrase, right phrase, universal phrase.' They accomplish eloquent delight, luminous accordance with the path, skillful analysis, ultimate analysis, firm speech, firm *ilā ilī*, *ilā sthiti jani*.

"They tread the supreme path, unmoving and quiescent, free from all enemies, blazing and eternally extinguished. They produce the stainless, pure self-nature. Those who skillfully expound all existences are without possession, without attachment. They skillfully subdue, are luminous and free from defilement, skillfully self-controlled, bear fruit and are greatly courageous, and attain great renown. Moving the unmoving with right movement, difficult to make firm; well-abiding and secure, roaming without obstruction or attachment; opening all dharma gates, according with what is appropriate, strong and powerful, vigorous and courageous, attaining the power of skillful means, great light universally illuminating, brilliant and stainless, with understanding equalized, universally entering without exception, accomplishing everything without fail. This excellent and wondrous dhāraṇī is the dwelling of all buddhas.

"Thus, Śāriputra! The bodhisattva who practices this dhāraṇī transcends counting and is beyond enumeration, without discrimination and without attainment. Regarding all dharmas that have been cut off, one advances in merit without doing anything, yet nothing is left undone. One does not see dharmas as having union or separation, arising or ceasing, nor any knowledge of past, present, or future. One does not know dharmas as having power or being without power.

"Cultivating mindfulness of the Buddha, one does not contemplate the marks, does not contemplate the lineage, does not contemplate the retinue; neither precepts, nor concentration, nor wisdom, nor understanding, nor the liberation-knowledge-and-vision contemplation. Without abiding, without attainment, not exhausting afflictions. Not knowing through knowledge or through non-knowledge. Not speaking the dharma, not purifying sentient beings. Not for oneself, not for the other. Not dharma, not vinaya, not deportment. Without purification. Not contemplating body, not contemplating mind. Without the ultimate nirvāṇa. Not contemplating past, future, or present. Not from oneself, not from another. In all dharmas there is no receiver of practice.

"Śāriputra! This is called 'embracing and receiving all dharmas, mindfulness of the Buddha in accordance.' It is called 'the fearless dhāraṇī gate entered by all dharmas, with wondrous phrases and meanings, all wishes completely fulfilled.' It is called 'accordance with the one-path samādhi.' It is also called 'all roots of merit not obtained through another; lineage and marks arise from the dharma itself.' It is called 'right awakening, crossing beyond all māra-events.'

"Śāriputra! This is the bodhisattva's dhāraṇī of producing infinite gateways. Whoever hears this dhāraṇī will attain non-retrogression from *anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi*. Why? From this dhāraṇī, the merits of all buddhas are brought to ultimate completion, and one can discern the courses of action of all sentient beings. Because one attains what is without attainment, it is called the dhāraṇī of producing infinite gateways."

At that time the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> "Dharmas are empty, without doubt or confusion;
> For the path, be without heedlessness.
> The dharma-nature is without deluded thought;
> This practice, through effort, attains the dhāraṇī.
> This sūtra, as has been explained:
> That dhāraṇī is inexhaustible.
> From it is born wondrous wisdom;
> Swiftly accomplish the unsurpassed path.
> Whoever attains this dhāraṇī,
> The bodhisattva has nothing to fear.
> The dharma spoken by buddhas of the ten directions,
> All that is spoken, one entirely hears.
> One can receive and hold it all;
> Meaning and flavor are completely perfect.
> As the sun produces measureless light,
> So too this dhāraṇī produces wisdom.
> Up to the unsurpassed dharma,
> The wondrous and subtle dhāraṇī gates:
> All arise from these phrases.
> One should well guard this sūtra.
> Even if all people throughout an entire kalpa
> Exhausted themselves in difficult questioning,
> One could decisively resolve all doubts;
> Such wisdom is inexhaustible.
> Drawing near to the seat of the unsurpassed path,
> Becoming a true dharma-prince;
> The dhāraṇī-holder is beloved,
> Through constantly remembering this sūtra.
> Honored and revered by all beings,
> Protected and remembered by the buddhas of the ten directions;
> Renown pervading the ten directions,
> Through embracing and holding this sūtra.
> Eighty billion buddhas
> Can all be seen at the time of death;
> Every one extends a hand
> To welcome the one who holds this sūtra.
> If throughout billions of thousands of kalpas
> One created unwholesome karma,
> In a single month it can be removed
> By receiving and reciting this sūtra.
> The merit a bodhisattva has generated,
> Accumulated over billions of kalpas:
> Skillfully reciting this dhāraṇī sūtra
> For a single month equals all of that.
> The power of mindfulness, the power of vigor,
> Samādhi and supreme wondrous dhāraṇī:
> All appear before one;
> Swiftly accomplish the most perfect enlightenment.
> The sentient beings of the three realms,
> All of them becoming māras,
> Cannot cause any interruption
> For one who cultivates this sūtra.
> From that true and sincere speech,
> From that ultimate dharma,
> From that one attains the path
> And achieves omniscience.
> Because long ago I heard this dhāraṇī,
> Dīpaṃkara gave me the prediction.
> Buddhas as numerous as the sands of the Ganges
> Can all appear at once.
> If you wish to hear the buddhas of the ten directions
> And understand everything they teach,
> Devote yourself to practicing this dhāraṇī;
> All will be swiftly accomplished.
> Adorning buddha-lands,
> Purifying assemblies and their accomplishments,
> Radiant marks and lineage:
> All shall be obtained from this sūtra.
> Having removed mental heedlessness,
> For seven days with focused contemplation,
> Eighty billion buddhas
> All come together to bestow this sūtra.
> Not thinking what is thought by thought,
> Nor thinking the unthought;
> This thinking too is unthought:
> From this, swiftly achieve accomplishment.
> Revere and honor this sūtra's teaching;
> On the path, do not have doubt.
> Just as in the great ocean
> One should not doubt that there are jewels.
> Wishing to bring peace to devas and humans,
> No wish is difficult.
> This is where the mind of the path arises;
> Simply practice with right intention."

"Furthermore, Śāriputra! A bodhisattva who accomplishes four dharmas will swiftly attain this dhāraṇī. What are the four? Weariness with and renunciation of desires; not giving rise to jealous mind; giving all possessions to all sentient beings; and day and night seeking the dharma with an unwearying mind."

At that time the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> "Weary of and renounce sensual desires;
> Māra's realm and hell are their conditions.
> Heedlessness creates all evils;
> Evil causes one to fall into evil paths.
> Do not give rise to envy toward others;
> Do not act for the sake of reputation.
> Look upon all beings with tender compassion;
> Noble, dignified, and with fine appearance.
> The quarrels and disputes of sentient beings
> All have craving as their root.
> Therefore one should renounce attachment;
> When attachment is renounced, the wondrous dhāraṇī is accomplished.
> Day and night, always delight in the dharma;
> Investigate and inquire, seeking the path.
> The one who concentrates on seeking the path
> Swiftly attains the wondrous dhāraṇī."

"Furthermore, Śāriputra! A bodhisattva who accomplishes another four dharmas will swiftly attain this dhāraṇī. What are the four? Dwelling in seclusion with a tranquil mind; attaining patience with the profound dharma; not seeking profit and support; and giving away all inner and outer possessions without remainder. These are the four."

At that time the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> "Dwelling in seclusion with a tranquil mind is praised by buddhas;
> Guarding oneself in emptiness and stillness, not disparaging others.
> Always delight in cultivating patience with the profound dharma,
> As urgently as if your head were on fire, without heedlessness.
> Do not seek reputation or profit and support,
> For from these come all manner of flattering and crooked conduct.
> Everywhere know contentment, like a bird in flight;
> Restrain the body, diminish the self, and cultivate benevolent practice.
> How joyful to receive the Buddha's teaching!
> To leave the household life and receive the full precepts,
> Renouncing home and departing from all suffering,
> Following and cultivating the Buddha's teaching.
> If one does not receive the Buddha's teaching,
> Leaving home is merely for the precepts' benefit;
> Faith, precepts, giving, learning, and wisdom
> Are all left unaccomplished.
> This fault comes from being far from the Buddha;
> Therefore renounce craving for profit."

"Furthermore, Śāriputra! A bodhisattva who accomplishes another four dharmas will swiftly attain this dhāraṇī. What are the four? First, the eight syllables, whose meanings and attendant meanings all enter therein.

"The eight syllables are: *pa*, *ra*, *bha*, *ka*, *ja*, *da*, *śa*, *kṣa*.

"*Pa* means the supreme meaning: all dharmas are without self, and all come entering through this gate. *Ra* means that marks and excellences are without marks, like the tathāgata's body entering the dharma-nature. *Bha* means that the dharma of ignorance and the dharma of wisdom both accord and enter into meaning. *Ka* means that karmic actions and their fruits, as well as non-karmic-action results, are all ultimately in accord and enter into meaning. *Ja* means that birth, conditions, old age, and death all accordingly enter into non-arising and non-ceasing. *Da* means that all dhāraṇīs accordingly enter into emptiness, marklessness, and wishlessness. *Śa* means that all dhāraṇīs all accordingly enter. *Kṣa* means that all dharmas entirely enter into suchness.

"These eight syllables: all meanings enter therein.

"Second, skillfully copy this sūtra, embrace and hold it without letting go. Third, every half-month, recite and study this sūtra. Fourth, constantly provide for and make offerings to those who hold this sūtra."

At that time the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> "Contemplate the meaning of the eight syllables;
> Skillfully copy and hold this sūtra.
> Every half-month, come and recite;
> Encourage and inspire those who practice the dhāraṇī.
> Throughout all Ganges-sand lands in the ten directions,
> With immeasurable buddhas present before one,
> Attaining the seat of enlightenment with wondrous marks complete:
> Diligently cultivate and practice this dhāraṇī."

"Furthermore, Śāriputra! A bodhisattva who studies this dhāraṇī attains four benefits of merit. What are the four? First, being remembered by the buddhas of the ten directions; second, being free from all māra-events; third, swiftly purifying evil karmic obstructions; fourth, fully accomplishing unobstructed and conclusive eloquence. These are the four."

At that time the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> "All buddhas constantly protect and remember;
> The hosts of māras cannot find an opening.
> Sinful karmic obstructions are all removed;
> One attains the wondrous immeasurable dhāraṇī."

The Buddha told Śāriputra: "Long ago in the past, countless kalpas ago, there was a buddha and world-honored one named Ratnāgni-śikhā-rāja Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha, Vidyācaraṇa-saṃpanna, Sugata, Lokavid, Anuttara, Puruṣa-damya-sārathi, Śāstā devamanuṣyānāṃ, Buddha, Bhagavān.

"Śāriputra! When that Ratnāgni-śikhā-rāja Tathāgata was about to enter nirvāṇa, there was a cakravartin king named Dhṛta-prabhāsa, who ruled the four continents with sovereign ease. The king had a crown prince named Asmṛti-guṇa-mārga, whose body at birth appeared as that of a sixteen-year-old youth. From that World-Honored One he heard this dhāraṇī. Having heard it, he devoted himself to studying it for seven thousand years without being overcome by sleep. Throughout those seven thousand years, he never once arose in bodily desire and never once thought of royal affairs. For another seven thousand years, his body never reclined; he sat upright in correct concentration. Thereupon he saw ninety billion nayutas of buddhas, heard the dharma from each, and was able to receive and hold it all. He then left the household life to study the path.

"For ninety thousand years, using this dhāraṇī of producing infinite gateways, he widely taught the people. In a single lifetime, he established eighty billion nayutas of beings in the aspiration for *anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi*, encouraging and guiding them to non-retrogression.

"Among that assembly there was a householder's son named Candradhvaja, who heard this dhāraṇī and rejoiced with his whole heart. Through the wholesome-root conditions of that rejoicing, he always drew near to ninety billion buddhas, attained the supreme dhāraṇī, attained the supreme stage, and gained immeasurable eloquence. After three more kalpas of drawing near to buddhas, at the end of the third kalpa, Candradhvaja became a buddha named Dīpaṃkara Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha.

"That prince Asmṛti-guṇa-mārga is now Amitāyus Buddha of the west.

"Śāriputra! I too, together with the bodhisattvas of this Fortunate Kalpa, once heard this dhāraṇī and, having heard it, rejoiced. Through the wholesome-root conditions of that rejoicing, I was freed from four hundred thousand kalpas of the sin of birth and death, and moreover drew near to ninety billion buddhas.

"Therefore, Śāriputra! If a bodhisattva mahāsattva wishes to swiftly accomplish *anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi*, one should diligently rejoice in this wondrous dhāraṇī. The merit of rejoicing serves as a great condition even for the stage of non-retrogression for a bodhisattva. How much more so for one who copies it, holds it, receives it, recites it, and practices as it teaches! No human being can conceive of or express such merit."

At that time the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> "Hearing this sūtra and rejoicing in the heart,
> Copying it, holding it, reverently reciting it:
> No deva or human can calculate
> The measure of that merit-reward.
> In life after life one always sees the Buddha;
> One's faith and aspiration never waver.
> Deeply understanding the essentials of the profound dharma,
> One swiftly awakens to the unsurpassed path.
> Samādhi that does not diminish,
> The three knowledges and the dhāraṇī gate,
> Wealth and beauty, always seeing the Buddha,
> Up to the unsurpassed path.
> I recall that in a past life,
> A householder's son heard this dhāraṇī
> And immediately saw ninety billion
> Nayutas of buddhas.
> He sought as he wished and attained,
> Accomplishing the unsurpassed path.
> If you wish to swiftly become a buddha,
> With the path's power subduing the hosts of māras,
> Wishing to fulfill the hundred-merit marks:
> Practicing this is not at all difficult.
> If one filled Ganges-sand lands
> With piled-up treasures and gave them in charity,
> Compared to copying and holding this sūtra,
> That giving could never match it."

"Furthermore, Śāriputra! For a bodhisattva who practices this dhāraṇī, there are eight great spirits who are deities of the Himālaya mountains and who together guard and watch over the practitioner. Their names are: the Spirit of Courageous Strength, the Spirit of Powerful Force, the Spirit of Sovereign Freedom, the Spirit of Heroic Valor, the Spirit of Knowing-Action, the Spirit of Invincibility, the Kumāra Spirit, and the Spirit of Good Arms. These are the eight.

"If one wishes these spirits to come, one should bathe the body, purify the garments, walk with dignified bearing, contemplate all beings with compassion, and reflect on the essentials of this dharma. As one remembers and contemplates, those spirits immediately appear before one.

"There are also eight bodhisattvas born in the heavens of desire who also constantly protect and remember the practitioner. Who are the eight? The Celestial Son Illuminating-the-Ten-Directions, the Celestial Son Free-from-Desire, the Celestial Son Wisdom-Light, the Celestial Son Like-the-Sun, the Celestial Son True-Meaning, the Celestial Son Wish-Fulfilled, the Celestial Son Star-King, and the Celestial Son Knowing-Action. These are the eight.

"These bodhisattvas constantly come to protect and remember the practitioner, enabling them to concentrate and swiftly attain the wondrous dhāraṇī.

"One who practices this dhāraṇī should take truth as the foundation, always be respectful, be mindful of gratitude, study patience with the profound dharma, contemplate equally the dharma of non-arising and non-ceasing, equally uphold the precepts, and protect both oneself and others."

When this dharma was spoken, bodhisattvas as numerous as thirty-two times the sands of the Ganges immediately attained this dhāraṇī and non-retrogression from *anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi*. Six hundred thousand people attained the patience of the non-arising of dharmas. Thirty-two thousand devas and humans all gave rise to the aspiration for the unsurpassed, right, and true path.

At that time the great trichiliocosm trembled in six ways. Heavenly flowers rained down everywhere throughout the world. Celestial musicians played, their instruments sounding of themselves without being struck.

Then Śāriputra addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! What should this sūtra be called?"

The Buddha told Śāriputra: "The essential name of this sūtra is 'Producing Infinite Gateways of Dhāraṇī.' It is also called 'The Path-Practice of One-Birth-from-Buddhahood.' It is also called 'Accomplishing the Path, Subduing Māra, and Attaining Omniscience.' You should receive and uphold it."

When this dharma was spoken, the Venerable Śāriputra and all the bodhisattvas who had come from the ten directions, together with the entire assembly of devas, humans, gandharvas, asuras, and the rest, having heard what the Buddha had spoken, were all greatly joyful.
