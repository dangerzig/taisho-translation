# Yogic Essentials: Dhāraṇī Ritual for Rescuing Ananda from the Flaming-Mouth Hungry Ghost (瑜伽集要救阿難陀羅尼焰口軌儀經)

## Taisho T1318

**Translator:** Amoghavajra (不空 Bùkōng, 705-774), Tang dynasty
**Fascicles:** 1

*Note: This text is the foundational scripture for the "Feeding the Flaming-Mouth Hungry Ghosts" (焰口 yànkǒu) ceremony, one of the most widely practiced Buddhist rituals in East Asia. The narrative frame tells of the hungry ghost Yankou ("Flaming Mouth") appearing to Ananda and threatening him with death and rebirth as a hungry ghost within three days unless he feeds immeasurable hungry ghosts. The Buddha then teaches Ananda a dhāraṇī that can transform a single vessel of food into inexhaustible sustenance for all hungry ghosts, spirits, departed ancestors, and beings of the six realms. The ritual integrates yogic (瑜伽 yúqié) esoteric practices including mudras, mantras, and visualization, requiring proper initiation (灌頂 guàndǐng, abhiseka) from a qualified acarya (阿闍梨 āshélí). Seven tathagatas are invoked by name, each bestowing specific benefits.*

---

At that time the World-Honored One was dwelling in the city of Kapila (迦羅城 *Jiāluó chéng*), at the Nyagrodha Monastery (尼俱律那僧伽藍 *Níjùlǜnà sēngqiélán*), together with monks, bodhisattvas, and a countless assembly, surrounded before and behind, teaching the Dharma.

At that time Ananda was dwelling alone in a quiet place, recollecting the teachings he had received. During the third watch of that very night, he saw a hungry ghost named Flaming Mouth (焰口 *Yànkǒu*). Its form was hideous and ugly, its body emaciated and withered, its mouth ablaze with fire and its throat as narrow as a needle's point. Its hair was disheveled and tangled, its teeth and claws were long and sharp: most terrifying to behold. Standing before Ananda, it addressed him: "Three days from now your life will end, and you will be reborn among the hungry ghosts."

When Ananda heard these words, his heart filled with alarm and dread. He asked the hungry ghost: "Great being! If after my death I am to be born as a hungry ghost, what skillful means can I employ to avoid this suffering?"

The hungry ghost replied: "Tomorrow at dawn, if you can distribute food and drink to hungry ghosts numbering as many as hundreds of thousands of *nayutas* (那由他) of Ganges River sands, and also to the immeasurable brahmana sages, the officers of Yama's jurisdiction, the administrators of the karmic path and underworld officials, as well as all ghosts, spirits, and the long-departed ancestors, providing each with seven times seven bushels of food as measured by the standard of Magadha, and if you also make offerings to the Three Treasures on our behalf, then your lifespan will be increased, and we will be freed from the suffering of hungry ghosts and reborn in heaven."

When Ananda saw the emaciated, withered, scorched, and extremely hideous body of this Flaming-Mouth hungry ghost, with fire blazing from its mouth, its throat like a needle, its hair tangled, and its claws long and sharp, and when he further heard these distressing words, he was greatly frightened, with every hair on his body standing on end. At dawn he rose from his seat and went to the Buddha. He circumambulated the Buddha three times to the right, bowed at the Buddha's feet, and with his body trembling, addressed the Buddha:

"O greatly compassionate World-Honored One! We pray you rescue me from this suffering. Last night during the third watch, while walking in meditation in a quiet place and recollecting the teachings, I saw a ghost called Flaming Mouth who told me: 'In three days your life will certainly end, and you will be reborn among the hungry ghosts.' I asked the ghost: 'How can I be freed from this suffering?' The ghost answered: 'If you can distribute food to countless billions of *nayutas* of Ganges River sands of hungry ghosts, brahmana sages, Yama's officers and underworld administrators, as well as all ghosts, spirits, attendants, and long-departed ancestors, giving equally to all hungry ghosts, then your lifespan will be increased.'"

Ananda asked: "World-Honored One! How can I possibly procure enough food and drink for such immeasurable beings?"

The Buddha told Ananda: "Do not be afraid. I recall that in a past life, immeasurable kalpas ago, when I was a brahmana, I received from Avalokitesvara Bodhisattva-Mahasattva a dhāraṇī called the Dhāraṇī Method of the Tathāgata of Immeasurable Majestic Virtue, Self-Mastery, and Radiance."

The Buddha told Ananda: "If you can skillfully perform this dhāraṇī method and empower it with seven recitations, you can cause a single portion of food to be transformed into various kinds of sweet-dew food and drink, sufficient to satisfy hundreds of thousands of *kotis* of *nayutas* of Ganges River sands of all hungry ghosts, brahmana sages, beings of different kinds, and ghosts and spirits, with supremely fine food and drink so that all are fully sated. Each and every one of them receives an amount equal to the standard bushel of Magadha. This food and water are equal in measure to the dharma-realm, and eating them is inexhaustible. All will obtain the sacred fruit and be liberated from their suffering bodies."

The Buddha told Ananda: "You should now receive and uphold this dhāraṇī method. It will cause your merit and lifespan to increase, cause hungry ghosts to be reborn in heaven or in pure lands, to receive human or celestial bodies, and enable the sponsor to have obstacles transformed, calamities eliminated, lifespan extended, and blessings enhanced. In this life one will attract superior merit and in the future will realize bodhi.

"Arouse the vast aspiration and universally act for all sentient beings. For parents of many lifetimes, for the celestial constellations, for the ghostly judges of the netherworld, for Yama's ghost realm, for crawling insects and all beings with consciousness: establish a boundless, unrestricted, vastly generous offering so that all may come to the assembly. Riding on the Buddha's awesome radiance, cleanse and wash their bodily fields, and obtain this supreme benefit of receiving human and celestial happiness.

"We pray that all buddhas, prajñā bodhisattvas, vajra devas, and all the administrators of the karmic path and immeasurable sacred worthies, with unconditional compassion, bear witness to what I practice. Therefore, because we wish to fulfill our great vows, because we wish to broadly protect and deliver sentient beings without regression, because we wish to crush all karma and make it pure, because we wish to diligently seek the unsurpassed Way and quickly accomplish it, and because we wish to rescue beings in the evil paths so they may forever cast aside the ocean of suffering and reach the other shore:

"As the sūtra teaches, in the boundless worlds and six destinies of four kinds of birth, those among them who serve as sovereigns, rulers, and leaders are all bodhisattvas abiding in inconceivable liberation, who through their compassionate vows distribute their forms and spread their shadows, manifesting transformation-bodies in the six destinies, undergoing suffering of the same kind as the beings there. They establish skillful means without being corrupted by afflictions or secondary afflictions. They distinguish all forms of karma and cause beings to arouse the aspiration for the Way. They constantly reproach themselves and repent of their past actions. They tame and teach all sentient beings, serving as great guides, crushing the three evil destinies, purifying the karmic paths, severing the stream of craving, never abandoning their vows of practice, dwelling in the ocean of suffering as good spiritual friends, bringing all beings to maturity, benefit, and joy, and realizing great nirvāṇa.

"If there is a patron who deeply trusts the Mahayana and thirsts for yoga, who desires to hear the sweet-dew dharma-gate of the dhāraṇī treasury, who for all sentient beings arouses the aspiration to rescue and deliver, who earnestly praises and donates great wealth, who makes three requests to the master before being permitted to perform the ritual, who maintains an attitude of equality free from thoughts of enmity or partiality, who constantly practices giving without regret, who draws close to good friends, who advances with vigor without timidity, who seeks the great Way, who praises the Three Treasures and nurtures life, who by skillful means rescues and delivers all so they are liberated, who does not sustain his life through evil seeking, who constantly benefits both self and others: such a good man is truly a good friend who practices the bodhisattva path.

"Universally, for all the evil destinies and realms of the three paths, for all hungry ghosts, for Yama and his retinue, for brahmana sages, for the devas of empty space, for Sakra, Brahma, and the four kings, for the celestial constellations, for the eight classes of dragons and spirits, for the sun and moon, for Mount Sumeru, for the asuras, for non-Buddhist practitioners, for the demon hosts of the six desire heavens, for the elements of water, fire, wind, and space, for the spirits of mountains, forests, caves, dwellings, palaces, monasteries, and the great earth, rivers, flowing springs, and bathing pools, for the various traveling spirits of shrines and auspicious and inauspicious places, for the spirits who record good and evil with unobstructed supernatural powers, for feathered beings flying in the sky, for water creatures swimming with fins, for fur-bearing and horned animals, for crawling insects and all beings with consciousness, for wandering souls in the wilderness and the tormented spirits bound to their corpses, for those with multi-life enmities still bound to one another, for ancestral spirits of many kalpas who owe debts of property and life, for deceased monks and nuns who have not yet attained the fruit, for parents and relatives of many lifetimes:

"Riding on the Tathāgata's teaching, may they emerge from the three evil paths. In the immeasurable hells, may they arouse the aspiration for bodhi. May each be willing to release and liberate their karmic bonds. May they think of one another with recollection, as a parent thinks of a child. Coming to this ritual site, may they bear witness and offer protective mindfulness, their hearts surging with joy, like encountering the rare *udumbara* flower. Through their own past actions they find themselves in the human realm, where mental awareness is difficult to stabilize and mostly follows delusion, accumulating as the source of suffering. Not yet having attained the sacred fruit, they repeatedly generate faults.

"Furthermore, depending on the king's land and water and abiding in the Buddha's compassionate light, constantly reflecting on past connections while still experiencing present fruits, day and night reproaching themselves: how can we repay such extraordinary kindness? Whether for relatives, family, or parents: how many times have we been inverted and entangled in grasping, changing form and face so that we would not recognize each other? We pray that today, by this Buddha's power, they may fly through the sky from afar and arrive at this ritual site. Touched by the compassionate light, may each according to their form repent and wash away the dust of their faults. May they arouse the aspiration for bodhi and accept these offerings."

## Instructions for the Ritual

The Buddha told Ananda: "If one wishes to receive and uphold the method of offering food, one must rely on the profound samādhi of the yogic acarya method. Those who wish to cultivate this should study under a yogic acarya (瑜伽阿闍梨), arouse the unsurpassed great aspiration for bodhi, receive the samaya precepts, enter the great mandala (曼拏羅 *mànnáluó*), and obtain abhiseka (灌頂 *guàndǐng*, initiation). Only then may one receive this practice. One should receive the five-wisdom abhiseka of the great Vairocana Tathāgata, succeed to the position of acarya, and only then transmit this teaching. If this is not done, those not properly authorized must not perform it. If one practices without authorization, one will bring calamity upon oneself and incur the offense of stealing the Dharma, and in the end there will be no effect.

"If one has received abhiseka and follows the master's instruction, cultivating the yogic dignified ritual procedures, and is well able to distinguish and thoroughly understand the characteristics of the Dharma, one is called a Tripitaka Acarya and may transmit this teaching."

### Setting Up the Ritual Space

"If one wishes to perform the ritual, first protect oneself, and the disciples likewise. Having determined the date, select a clean site: a splendid great hall, a quiet garden or grove, a place beloved of ghosts and spirits, near flowing springs or bathing pools, or by rivers, mountains, or marshes, at a site of merit. A hall or building will also do. Plaster it properly with fragrant water and mud, its dimensions square or round according to the patron's means. Erect markers at the four corners, adorned properly with five-colored silk.

"Install a flame-jewel, and within the jewel place the *Buddhosnisa*, *Mahakarunika*, *Suiqiu*, and *Vijaya* [dharanis]: *Buddhosnisa* in the northeast, *Mahakarunika* in the southeast, *Suiqiu* in the southwest, and *Vijaya* in the northwest. Adorn the four pillars properly in a marvelous and distinctive way, calling them 'Auspicious Banners.' For a hundred *yojanas* around there will be no misfortune. The boundary is thereby established. Where the wind blows, the shadows fall, the dust scatters, or the water touches, karmic obstacles are eliminated and great merit obtained. All who see with their eyes or hear with their ears will universally benefit.

"Next, hang silk streamers, canopies, precious fans, and white whisks arranged in order. Then prepare *argha* water (scented water), marvelous flowers, lamps, incense-paste, food and drink, herbal medicine, various kinds of fruit and other items, and purify them with the Dharma method so they are free from defilement."

### The Ritual Procedure

[After adorning the space, inspecting it, the master and disciples bathe in fragrant water, put on new clean garments, sweep and purify the outer courtyard, plaster the ground, and establish the samaya altar. The practitioner kneels facing east, holding an incense burner, and performs the invocation.]

The invocation announces to all buddhas, prajñā bodhisattvas, vajra devas, and all karmic-path administrators and sacred worthies. It invokes, by the great compassion and the Buddha's spiritual power, all hungry ghosts of the three evil paths throughout empty space, Yama's officers, celestial and underworld officials, brahmana sages, long-departed ancestors, wilderness spirits, celestial beings of empty space, and all their retinues and attendant ghosts and spirits. [The invocation requests that all these beings, carried by the Tathāgata's power, come at dawn to receive the supremely fine Dharma-flavor of sweet-dew nectar.]

[After the threefold invocation, the master makes offerings of incense, flowers, lamps, and ointment, then confesses his own offenses, bows, and anoints his palms with fragrant paste before beginning the seal-and-mantra procedures.]

### Breaking Open the Hells

"Next, form the seal for breaking open the hells. Both hands make vajra fists, with the little fingers hooked together. The index fingers stand upright, side by side. The mind visualizes the hells opening. Recite three times, pulling open three times."

[Dhāraṇī for breaking open the hells]

"By the awesome power of this seal and mantra, all the hell-gates in every destiny are flung open."

### Summoning the Hungry Ghosts

"Next, form the seal for summoning the hungry ghosts. The left hand makes the fearlessness gesture. The right hand extends forward, with four fingers slightly curved and the index finger beckoning."

[Dhāraṇī for summoning]

"Having summoned them, all gather like clouds. With a heart of compassion, praise and console them, causing them to rejoice and thirst for the Dharma."

### Summoning and Crushing Offenses

[Seals and dharanis for summoning karmic offenses and crushing them, followed by the seal for purifying fixed karma and the seal for repentance and destruction of offenses.]

"Children of the Buddha! Having now repented:

> 'Offenses accumulated over a hundred kalpas
> Are in a single thought-moment completely swept away,
> Like fire burning dry grass,
> Destroyed completely with nothing remaining.'"

### The Sweet-Dew Offering

"Next, form the seal of the Tathāgata of Wondrous Form-Body bestowing sweet-dew nectar. With the left hand, turn the wrist forward. Snap the ring finger and thumb."

[Dhāraṇī for bestowing sweet-dew nectar]

"While reciting the mantra, visualize on the tips of the ring finger and thumb a *vam* syllable flowing out prajñā sweet-dew Dharma-water. Snap and sprinkle it into the air. All hungry ghosts and beings of different kinds universally obtain cooling refreshment, their fierce fires extinguished, their bodily fields moistened, and their sensations of hunger and thirst ended."

### Opening the Throats

"Next, form the seal for opening the throats. The left hand visualizes holding a lotus. The right hand snaps the ring finger and thumb, snapping while reciting."

[Dhāraṇī for opening throats]

"Tell the children of the Buddha: 'Now that I have made the seal and spell for you, your throats are opened of their own accord, penetrating without obstruction, free from all hindrances.'"

### The Seven Tathagatas

"'Children of the Buddha! I now recite for you the auspicious names of the tathagatas, which can cause you to forever leave the sufferings of the three evil destinies and the eight difficulties, and to always be true and pure disciples of the Tathāgata.

"'Homage to Ratnaketu Tathāgata (寶勝如來 *Bǎoshèng rúlái*). Children of the Buddha! If you hear the name of Ratnaketu Tathāgata, the karmic fires of your worldly defilements will all be extinguished.

"'Homage to Abhayamkara Tathāgata (離怖畏如來 *Lí bùwèi rúlái*). Children of the Buddha! If you hear the name of Abhayamkara Tathāgata, you will always attain peace and happiness, forever free from alarm and dread, in pure and blissful ease.

"'Homage to Vipulakaya Tathāgata (廣博身如來 *Guǎngbó shēn rúlái*). Children of the Buddha! If you hear the name of Vipulakaya Tathāgata, the needle-like throats and karmic fires of you hungry ghosts will cease burning, becoming cool and unobstructed, and the food you receive will have the flavor of sweet-dew nectar.

"'Homage to Surupa Tathāgata (妙色身如來 *Miàosè shēn rúlái*). Children of the Buddha! If you hear the name of Surupa Tathāgata, you will not receive ugly forms. Your faculties will be complete, your marks and excellences perfectly fulfilled, supremely dignified and beautiful, foremost among heavenly and human beings.

"'Homage to Prabhutaratna Tathāgata (多寶如來 *Duōbǎo rúlái*). Children of the Buddha! If you hear the name of Prabhutaratna Tathāgata, you will be fully endowed with wealth and treasures, receiving whatever you wish without end.

"'Homage to Amitabha Tathāgata (阿彌陀如來 *Āmítuó rúlái*). Children of the Buddha! If you hear the name of Amitabha Tathāgata, you will be reborn in the Western Land of Ultimate Bliss, born by transformation from a lotus, entering the stage of non-retrogression.

"'Homage to the Tathāgata of Immeasurable Majestic Virtue, Self-Mastery, and Radiance in the World (世間廣大威德自在光明如來). Children of the Buddha! If you hear the name of this tathagata, you will obtain five kinds of merit: first, being foremost in all worlds; second, having the dignified and beautiful eyes of a bodhisattva; third, having majestic virtue so vast it surpasses all non-Buddhist practitioners and heavenly demons, like the sun illuminating the world and shining upon the great ocean, with towering merit; fourth, attaining great self-mastery, going wherever one wishes like a bird flying through the sky without obstruction; fifth, attaining the great firm radiance of wisdom, with body and mind luminous and transparent like a lapis lazuli jewel.

"'Children of the Buddha! These seven tathagatas, by the power of their vows, rescue sentient beings, causing them to forever leave afflictions, to be freed from the suffering of the three evil destinies, and to abide in safety, comfort, and eternal happiness. By a single recitation of their names, a thousand lifetimes of suffering are left behind, and the unsurpassed Way is realized.'"

### Three Refuges, Bodhicitta, and Samaya Precepts

"'Next I give you the refuge in the Three Treasures. Take refuge in the Buddha, honored among those with two [kinds of] perfection. Take refuge in the Dharma, honored for being free from desire. Take refuge in the Sangha, honored among all communities.

"'You children of the Buddha, having taken refuge in the Buddha, in the Dharma, and in the Sangha, depend on the Three Treasures and firmly uphold them according to the Dharma.'"

"'Next, for your sake I arouse the aspiration for bodhi. Listen carefully.'" Form the vajra-palm seal with the ring finger like a lotus petal, and press the seal to the heart. [Dhāraṇī for arousing bodhicitta]

"'I have now aroused the aspiration for bodhi on your behalf. Children of the Buddha! You should know that the bodhicitta arises from great compassion. It is the direct cause of buddhahood and the root of wisdom. It can break through ignorance, afflictions, and evil karma, without being stained or corrupted.'"

"'Next I bestow upon you the samaya precepts.'" Form the seal with the two hands bound, ring fingers extended like needles. [Dhāraṇī for samaya precepts]

"'I have now bestowed the samaya precepts upon you. From now on, this causes you to enter the Tathāgata's position. You are true children of the Buddha, born from the Dharma through transformation, receiving your share of the Buddha-dharma.'"

### Transforming and Distributing the Food

"Next, form the seal of the Tathāgata of Immeasurable Majestic Virtue, Self-Mastery, and Radiance. The left hand visualizes holding a vessel. The right hand snaps the ring finger and thumb. Visualize a *vam* syllable in the palm of the left hand, flowing out various kinds of inexhaustible sweet-dew Dharma-food."

[Dhāraṇī for offering food]

"'Tell the children of the Buddha: Now that I have made the seal and spell for you, this single portion of food is transformed into immeasurable food, as great as Mount Sumeru, as vast as the dharma-realm, and can never be exhausted.'"

[Second dhāraṇī for further empowerment of the food]

"'Visualize within the seal a flow of sweet-dew nectar becoming an ocean of milk, pouring into the dharma-realm, universally sustaining all of you and all sentient beings until fully satisfied.'"

The practitioner then takes the sweet-dew vessel in the right hand, stands facing east, and pours it before the altar or into a spring, pool, river, or ocean of flowing water. One must not pour it beneath a pomegranate or peach tree, as ghosts and spirits fear these and cannot partake.

### Timing of the Ritual

For the sacred assembly's altar of wisdom kings and celestial beings, and for offering food and making offerings to all buddhas and sacred assemblies: the proper time is the fifth watch at dawn when the sun rises. For ghost and spirit rituals: the proper time is at the settling of people, and the *zi* hour (11pm-1am) is also acceptable. According to the original acarya method, during mealtime throughout the entire day, one may simply empower food and water for distribution to birds, beasts, aquatic creatures, and the like, regardless of the time. For performing the hungry-ghost food-offering ritual, the proper time is the *hai* hour (9-11pm). If one offers food to hungry ghosts during mealtime, the effort is wasted and in the end has no effect. If the timing is wrong, it is a false deception, and the ghosts and spirits cannot eat. Those who have not received the transmission from a master bring calamity upon themselves and incur the offense of stealing the Dharma.

### Final Instructions to the Hungry Ghosts

"'Children of the Buddha! Though you are gathered by kind, do not harbor anger or resentment. What I offer is entirely without obstruction, without high or low, equal and universal, not choosing between enemies and friends. Today, do not look down on the lowly because you are exalted, or bully the weak because you are strong, or block the orphaned and young from eating, or cause unequal distribution, or transgress the Buddha's compassionate relief. You must love and care for one another as a parent thinks of an only child.'

"'You children of the Buddha! Each of you has parents, brothers, sisters, spouses, children, relatives, good friends, and family. If any of them could not come due to circumstances, you children of the Buddha, with compassionate love, should each carry food, money, and goods to distribute to one another until all are fully satisfied, lacking nothing. May all arouse the aspiration for the Way, forever leave the three evil destinies, long cross the four floods, and soon cast off these bodies and quickly surpass to the fruit of the Way.

"'Furthermore, I divide this pure food into three portions on your behalf: first, to be given to aquatic creatures, so they may attain the emptiness of persons; second, to be given to fur-bearing animals, so they may attain the quiescence of dharmas; third, to be given to beings of other realms who receive consciousness and take form, so that all may be fully satisfied and attain the acceptance of the non-arising of dharmas.'"

### Universal Offering and Dedication

[The universal offering seal is formed, placed at the heart, with its dhāraṇī.]

"'Children of the Buddha! All the food you have ever received was obtained from the human world through the trading of lives, wine, meat, and the reeking, pungent, and foul. Though you received such food, it was like poison harming the body, only increasing the root of suffering, sinking in the ocean of suffering with no time of liberation. I, so-and-so, relying on the Tathāgata's teaching, sincerely and exhaustively make offerings, establishing this unrestricted, vastly generous Dharma-assembly.

"'Today you have encountered this supreme goodness. The precepts have touched your bodies. In past lives you extensively served the buddhas, drew near to good friends, and made offerings to the Three Treasures. Through these causes and conditions, you have met a good spiritual friend who aroused the aspiration for bodhi, vowing to attain buddhahood and not seek any lesser fruit. Those who first attain the Way should help deliver the others in turn. I also pray that you will day and night constantly protect me and help me fulfill my vows.

"'With the merit generated by this offering of food, I universally transfer it to all sentient beings of the dharma-realm. Together with all sentient beings, I transfer all this merit to the unsurpassed bodhi, to the wisdom of all wisdoms, seeking no lesser fruit, and praying for the swift attainment of buddhahood.'"

### Sending Off

"Next, form the sending-off seal. Both hands make vajra fists, index fingers hooked together; open while reciting."

[Dhāraṇī of Vajra Liberation for sending off]

The Buddha told Ananda: "If in the future monks, nuns, laymen, and laywomen, each morning or at mealtime or at any time, constantly practice this method with these mantras and the seven tathagatas' names to empower food and give it to hungry ghosts and others, then the practitioner, at mealtime or any time, should set out food for the hungry ghosts and other spirits in a clean vessel, wait until the settling of people, empower it, and distribute it to immeasurable hungry ghosts and other spirits. This will generate immeasurable merit, equal to making offerings to hundreds of thousands of *kotis* of tathagatas with no difference whatsoever. One's lifespan will be extended, one's vigor and vitality will increase, one's roots of merit will be complete, all non-human beings, yaksas, raksasas, and evil ghosts and spirits will not dare to trouble one, and one will also accomplish immeasurable majestic virtue.

"If one wishes to offer food to hungry ghosts, brahmana sages, Yama's officers and underworld administrators, all ghosts and spirits, and long-departed ancestors, fill a clean vessel with pure food and drink, form the above seals and recite the mantras, and cast it into clean flowing water. Having done so, this becomes celestial ambrosia and fine food. It will supply *kotis* of Ganges River sands of hungry ghosts, brahmana sages, underworld administrators, and spirits, as well as the long-departed. Through the awesome power of the empowered food, seals, and mantras, each will accomplish their fundamental wishes and all good merits. Together they will simultaneously make a vow, blessing the sponsor with long life, merit, peace, and happiness, and further causing that person's mind to have correct understanding, purity, complete roots of merit, and the swift realization of unsurpassed, perfect, and complete enlightenment. This is also equal to making offerings to hundreds of thousands of Ganges River sands of tathagatas, with no difference. All enemies will be unable to cause harm.

"If monks, nuns, laymen, or laywomen wish to make offerings to the Buddha, Dharma, and Sangha, they should use incense, flowers, lamps, ointment, and supremely fine food and drink. Empowering these with the above seals and mantras and reverently offering them, all buddhas, bodhisattvas, and sacred worthies will rejoice and praise various merits. Such persons will be constantly remembered and praised by all buddhas. Virtuous devas and spirits will always come to protect them. Such a person has thus fulfilled the *dana-paramita* (perfection of giving)."

The Buddha told Ananda: "Follow my words and practice according to the Dharma. Propagate this widely so that all beings of short lifespan and meager merit may universally hear of it and constantly practice this method. Their lifespans will be extended and their merit will increase."

At that time the Buddha said: "This sūtra is taught for Ananda's sake and for rescuing the Flaming-Mouth hungry ghost and all sentient beings, and is called the Dhāraṇī Sūtra. By this title you should receive and uphold it."

The entire great assembly, together with Ananda, having heard what the Buddha taught, received it with single-minded faith and practiced it with joy.
