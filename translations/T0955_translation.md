# 一字頂輪王瑜伽觀行儀軌
## Ritual of Yoga Contemplation Practice for the One-Syllable Uṣṇīṣa-cakravartin

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0955

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空)

---

If the true-word practitioner seeks the siddhi of invisibility, they should contemplate as follows: "How can I swiftly attain accomplishment? I should practice this samādhi. All dharmas are without form, their nature self-accomplished like empty space." Having made this supreme resolution, one should purify the ground and so forth as in the root teaching, and enter this samādhi.

> Pervading the three-thousand-fold world,
> Filled with the water of wisdom-accomplishment,
> There is a great jeweled lotus
> Blooming and unfolding upon the water.
> Its jeweled stem is like Mount Sumeru,
> Covered above with a net of pearls.
> Upon the lotus is a great jewel,
> With eight pillars as its adornment.
> Visualize the accomplished jeweled tower-pavilion
> With four gates facing the four directions,
> Pearl-nets, lotus-bells, and the like,
> And wondrous fly-whisks as ornaments.
> Necklaces of half and full moon pearls,
> Draped with jeweled sashes in variegated patterns.
> This great jeweled tower-pavilion
> Pervades up to the summit of existence.
> One should, according to one's power, contemplate
> The cloud-ocean of offerings.
> Within the previously visualized tower,
> There is a delightful white parasol
> Adorned with various jewels at its finial,
> Jeweled necklaces of half and full moons,
> Wondrous fly-whisks and other ornaments.
> Above the parasol is a great jewel
> That universally emits radiance
> And rains down various treasures.
> The true-word wisdom contemplation-practice:
> Within that tower-pavilion,
> A vajra lion-throne adorned with jeweled lotuses,
> Pearl-nets, lotus-banners, chimes,
> White fly-whisks, and other ornaments.
> All joyful desires
> Surround and dwell therein.
> Not far before one's body,
> One should visualize a second seat,
> Entirely as above
> Except without the lion-throne.
> At the corner-gates in the four places,
> Playful attendants with incense, etc.
> Next, perform the method of complete equipment.
> How does one make the complete equipment?
> First, contemplate that all dharmas
> Have no-nature as their nature.
> Object and self are one body.
> Then empower the various dharmas.
> How does one empower the dharmas?
> Birth, old age, sickness, grief, and death
> Disturb and agitate the field of the mind.
> Having contemplated thus,
> One should give rise to great compassion.
> From this arises the wisdom-mind,
> Which is the mind of radiance.
> Perfumed and accomplished through great compassion,
> Its essence is like the full moon,
> Free from grasper and grasped.
> The mind of bodhi is now born.
> One should abide in body, speech, and mind,
> Empowered by the luminous essence.
> This is called the method of complete equipment.
> Having understood the supreme sequence of the clan-vidyā,
> Having understood the awesome virtue of all the true words,
> One abides in the method of the division's king,
> And becomes the king of that clan.
> First, having accomplished the seal,
> Next contemplate the vidyā.
> Through the essence of body, speech, and mind,
> Visualize the body as all the Buddhas.
> In the heart, visualize a moon-disc,
> Undefiled, just like a Buddha.
> The true-word practitioner should know:
> Various rays of light
> All arise from the moon-form,
> Pervading immeasurable realms,
> Then returning to enter the practitioner's heart.
> They solidify into a five-peaked form
> And transform into a vajra shape.
> Visualize it resting in the palm,
> Again emitting various rays of light.
> That radiance pervades and fills
> Boundless realms.
> Performing the play of the Buddhas,
> It returns to the yogin's body.
> When all have thus entered,
> One becomes the great form of Samantabhadra.
> One should contemplate the great bodhisattva
> With all marks fully accomplished,
> With all adornments,
> Crowned with a lotus-garland consecration.
> One has become a mahāsattva,
> Born from the yogin's heart.
> Holding the true-word bow and arrows,
> Abiding upon the moon-disc,
> Or holding a garland and standing,
> Or dwelling peacefully in the four directions.
> According to one's power, contemplate before the body
> The great bliss of all sentient beings.
> The most supreme, bring to accomplishment
> All the seal-adorned hands.
> Generously bestow upon all
> Sentient beings as they wish,
> And with joyful display as a dance,
> The vajra-garland flows forth.
> The left hand at the waist,
> The right in a dancing posture,
> Establishing benefit for sentient beings
> According to one's own vidyā-form.
> Whatever sentient beings behold,
> All are made to be tamed.
> Making such empowerments,
> One benefits sentient beings.
> And other benefits besides.
> Writing-out and other offerings:
> Having thus set forth offerings
> To one's own body and one's own honored deity,
> With the true-word syllables in correspondence,
> According to one's power, perform the recitation.
> As sound and form correspond,
> Using syllables as flower-garlands.
> The wise should contemplate
> The yoga of correspondence.
> Regarding sound, I now teach
> The accomplishment of all the most supreme.
> The Tathāgata Lotus Division:
> Recitation in the voice of a conch.
> My praise is like the sound of thunder,
> Clearly pronouncing the syllable *hūṃ*.
> I call this the Vajra Division.

This also applies to Maheśvara. The reciter accomplishes homage and karmic rites. For this division's recitation, the sound is like bells and chimes, like a harp, like a flute, like the sound of dancing necklaces. Its sound is like the cry of a peacock. As in the methods of all divisions, the corresponding meaning is accomplished through such sounds. One performs the recitation in correspondence with the true word. The true-word practitioner, following the sound, should contemplate its meaning. Before long, accomplishment will come. This applies to all divisions. This is the ritual of voice-recitation.

I will teach form-recitation. I will now teach all. "Form" is called "seal." In correspondence with this, one turns and skillfully contemplates the vidyā. Place it at one's chest. Observe the seal-flame and vidyā. From within the body, a vajra emerges and showers amṛta. This causes the principal deity to rejoice. One obtains the power to conceal one's own form. The great and diligent hero: I teach form-recitation. By this, the true-word practitioner knows that through the seal, accomplishment is brought about. Before long, one easily transforms to another body. I will teach yoga-recitation. As previously taught, the vidyā should be contemplated and placed at its proper location. Make offerings as taught. This is the purely corresponding recitation: apart from the mind, the throat, the crown, the tongue, the nose, and apart from internal and external thought, one relies solely on dharma-correspondence. One should not depend on sound. This is called the vajra yoga-recitation ritual. If one knows the true and real essence, one should know that accomplishment is obtained and one realizes permanence. By this yoga-method, abiding in correspondence, with the supreme mind of bodhi, accomplishment will come before long. All will be as one wishes. Among these, syllable-recitation, I will now teach as follows: thus one accumulates provisions. The true word should be recited and upheld. Taking written syllables as form, one should discern and contemplate them, performing the recitation practice. The moon, arrayed in the mind, generates the vidyā-syllables together. Place them at the principal deity's chest. Before long one obtains the siddhi of invisibility. With moon upon moon joined together, contemplate all the syllable-forms upon them: the radiant wheel-adornment, arrayed without interruption, like pearls strung on a thread, golden in color as light, blazing upon the principal deity's body. As those moon-syllables, the supremely wondrous true words, accomplish the true-word practitioner, so does joy arise in each case. Power, life-span, and increase blaze ever more brilliantly. The mind, the radiance, in correspondence: I call this the syllable-array recitation. I have now pervaded all the rituals, accomplishing the radiance of objects. I now teach the recited vidyā. If the reciter performs it thus, accomplishing objects through radiance, one should give rise to the ritual of recitation-correspondence. All true words can be accomplished. One ritual, in correspondence, one should make one's cognition pure. Pure is the mind-cognition. The wise should then enter accomplishment. By body, by speech, by mind, abiding thus in objects, one should know the four-limb method. The yoga-method is accomplished. Herein, what is called "mind": one obtains supernormal powers and stages, etc. By body, one manifests various bodies. By speech, one discriminates all. Smoke, flame, and other accomplishments: I call these object-accomplishments. Among these, body-accomplishment has many kinds and categories. The four seals and other maṇḍalas: the yogin should, to the full extent of their understanding, create a great maṇḍala.

When the maṇḍala ritual is accomplished, at the central samādhi-form, one should set up a soft seat and sit. In correspondence with all the yoga samādhis, day and night in equipoise, the true-word practitioner abides in correspondence with wisdom and mindfulness. At midnight, or when the sign of dawn appears, or at the third watch, or at the sign of dawn, accomplishment will certainly come: either stages or supernormal powers, as the Tathāgata has taught. I now teach mind-accomplishment, obtained in this very body. Empower the body with this seal. I now present the great seal; one should contemplate as follows. For the accomplishment, maṇḍala, and recitation practitioner: with a purified mind, accomplishment is attained. I teach the signs of accomplishment: from the mouth or body there emanates radiance, warmth, smoke, or increase. If one sees oneself ascending into empty space, one should know this as the sign of accomplishment. I have now broadly taught body-accomplishment.

I will teach the method of speech-accomplishment, teaching in sequence as it is obtained. Having first performed the preliminary practices, one should proceed as before. Upon the mouth-heart seal, place a lotus. One should know that at the heart, upon it rests a conch. From within the conch, sound issues forth, continuous and uninterrupted. Within the lotus, a vajra-tongue arises. Upon the white tongue, visualize either a Buddha, or a jewel, or a lotus, or a karma-vajra, or another division's seal-implement. For one month, two months, three, four, five, or up to eight months, maintain the contemplation. Upon this altar, sit in cross-legged posture, constantly in correspondence with the fundamental quiescence. Having clearly contemplated the sound of the conch, then rely upon the lotus-realm. From the flower-pistil, sound issues forth, in volume like an atom. That sound emerges to the throat, then to the tongue, and thereby forms the syllable. The tongue-syllable emits a blazing light, fiercely pervading everywhere. With its sound, it fills empty space. The practitioner should recite with fixed concentration for one day and one night, or during the middle or latter watches of the night. From the tongue, radiance issues forth. That radiance carries a great sound. Or from the heart, from the lips, from the teeth, variegated radiance issues forth. If one sees such signs, one should know that the siddhi has been obtained. By this speech-correspondence, one can crush all heterodox schools, cause others to generate pure faith, and bring benefit to sentient beings. By such yoga-correspondence recitation, the bhikṣu named Vāka-jaṭa will obtain speech-accomplishment. Emerging from variegated signs, in seven days the ritual of speech-accomplishment is complete.

I now teach object-accomplishment. Following the previous yoga, one should attain accomplishment. Take objects from other divisions, or use other divisions' seals and true words, performing the threefold action-aspect. The wise, within this maṇḍala, enters the karma-samaya and personally performs all the karmic deeds, sovereign within them as their form. For each division's lord, one should place the seal-object in the right hand in sequence. Within the vajra-bond, place the object-materials, stacking them within the bond-seal at the navel or at the heart. With both hands, bind the object-offering and place it at one's own heart with one's own vidyā. The wise, by this method, contemplates the body as a mass of fire-light. Using the previously accumulated merit-provisions of body, speech, and mind, place the body-object in the vidyā-hand. Recite with the true-word syllable fire-flame sound. The true-word practitioner should perform this throughout the night without breaking the sitting posture. By this ritual method, warmth arises in the first watch, smoke in the middle watch, and flame at the sign of dawn. Thus, gradually increasing, when the flame accomplishes the object, one obtains the power to fly through empty space and sovereignty over the three realms.

I will teach the method of añjana. As I have previously taught, one should sever all grasping and rely upon one's root vidyā, extending even to one's own body, speech, and mind likewise. With one's own true word, one should become invisible and take on the form of the root-vidyā lord, performing the recitation. The one called the Great Vajra Name, by the great vajra-body, the wise should stand firm, summon, cause to enter, bind, and bring joy. Place it at the heart. According to one's heart's desire, I contemplate my own body through my vidyā: "I am empty space." By this correspondence-accomplishment, up to the lord of the entire desire-realm, none can see one's form. In an instant, one reaches the Brahma-heaven. Exhausting its dharmas, perform the summoning. One should create a four-seal maṇḍala. Place the mind-intent in the left hand. Place one's own body there. With the fist, hold firmly. The fist becomes empty space. With this siddhi of invisibility, even the Trāyastriṃśa Heaven cannot see one. One roams in the palace of the Paranirmita-vaśavartin Heaven, enjoying sovereign pleasure at will, for as long as one desires. This is the pill-in-mouth invisibility method.

I now briefly teach the method of accomplishment. Thus, contemplate one's own vidyā: "I am the essence of all things." One's own body is identical with that seal. Abiding in the subtle vajra-samādhi, one enters into the heart of one's own vidyā. For the pill-accomplishment, contemplate placing the pill in the mouth. One can roam upon the summit of Mount Sumeru. All the world cannot see one. The medicine-form accomplishes great power. One enjoys the celestial pleasures of the Trāyastriṃśa Heaven. At will, one obtains bliss. One mingles with other worldly beings. I now teach the invisibility pill: the pill is accomplished according to the root teaching. The true-word wisdom should be applied. Before the wisdom-body of one's own vidyā, one should seek and offer it to the accomplished one. Pure as empty space: the vidyā-body, the object, and the form. With the vidyā-syllable array, abiding in correspondence with the blazing flame, one enters the principal deity's body. As if invisibly abiding in mind, from the vidyā's mouth flows forth the blazing awesome radiance of the true word. The pill should be well accomplished. When smoke and vapor rise from it, one should contain it and place it in the mouth for the invisible state. With sovereign freedom, one roams the four great continents. In an instant, through all the worlds, one returns to one's original place. Going wherever one wishes. Various accomplishments: yakṣa hosts constantly serve as one's retinue. One can also play upon Mount Sumeru and the four yakṣa worlds in the lower tier of the Four Heavenly Kings, performing immeasurable benefit for sentient beings. For those who have lost the right path, those adrift in the wilderness, those beset by bandits, kings, water, fire, and other calamities, one should give rise to compassionate pity. In all places of bondage, "Having accomplished the siddhi, I shall liberate them all."

I have now briefly taught this. The extensive method is as in the great sūtra. One should contemplate the moon of one's own heart, the moon-form endowed with radiance. Within this moon, one syllable, golden in color, difficult to behold like the sun's disc, its radiance universally spreading. The yogin visualizes the radiance, and that syllable becomes a wheel. That wheel becomes a turning wheel, holding wondrous forms surrounded by the seven treasures. From all the pores of the body, immeasurable Buddhas flow forth. The yogin should contemplate, using the Vajradhātu seal, empowering four places with the true-word recitation. By the power of diligent heroism, accomplishment is attained. Mindfulness, concentration, and diligence serve as firewood to burn away all transgressions. By the true-word form-fire, one should leave behind doubt and discrimination. Seek the great bliss. Abandon small pleasures. With earnest wisdom-bodhi, abide in the true-word ritual.
