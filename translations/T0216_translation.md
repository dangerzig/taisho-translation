# 大魚事經
## Sūtra of the Great Fish

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0216

Translated from the Chinese. Eastern Jin dynasty, translated by Zhu Tanwulan (竺曇無蘭)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Bhagavān was in the city of Śrāvastī, in the Jeta Grove, the Anāthapiṇḍada Monastery.

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣus: "Long ago in the past, there was a body of water with many great fish.

"At that time, the great fish commanded the small fish: 'Do not leave this place and go elsewhere, lest you be caught by evil people.'

"At that time, the small fish did not follow the great fish's instruction and went elsewhere.

"At that time, a fisherman cast a net of rice-bait and line to catch fish. When the small fish saw it, they hurried back to where the great fish were.

"At that time, the great fish saw the small fish returning and asked them: 'Did I not tell you not to leave this place and go elsewhere?' The small fish answered the great fish: 'We had already gone elsewhere and have just returned.'

"The great fish then asked the small fish: 'When you went elsewhere, were you not caught by nets?'

"The small fish answered the great fish: 'When we went there, we were not caught by anyone. However, from afar we saw a long line following behind us.'

"The great fish then said to the small fish: 'You have already been harmed. The reason is this: the line you saw from afar following behind you is the very same line that killed your ancestors, your grandparents, and your parents, every one of them. You will now surely be killed by it. You are no children of mine.'

"At that time, all the small fish were caught by the fisherman and cast upon the bank. Thus a great many small fish perished.

"It is the same in this case. Suppose there is a bhikṣu traveling in another village, wearing his robe and holding his bowl, going about to beg for alms, intending to benefit and ferry across sentient beings. But he does not guard his body, does not guard his speech or mind, his faculties are not restrained, and his attention is not focused. He begs for alms in that village.

"Then he sees women of surpassing beauty, with complexions like peach blossoms. Seeing them, he gives rise to lustful desire, and with this lustful mind, his body, speech, and mind blaze with passion.

"With body, speech, and mind blazing with passion, he begs for alms in that village and returns to his lodging, still giving rise to thoughts of desire.

"He then goes to the senior bhikṣus and explains the whole matter to them.

"The venerable senior bhikṣus tell this bhikṣu: 'You have given rise to lustful thoughts. This is impure. Bhikṣu! You should practice the contemplation of impurity.'

"The venerable senior bhikṣus admonish him thus two or three times.

"But that bhikṣu, with body, speech, and mind still blazing intensely, again goes to that village to beg for alms. From afar he sees the women of surpassing beauty, with complexions like peach blossoms. Seeing them, he gives rise to lustful desire, and with this lustful mind, his body, speech, and mind blaze with passion. Because his body, speech, and mind blaze with passion, he begs for alms in that village, returns to his lodging, and goes to the venerable senior bhikṣus, explaining the whole matter to them.

"The venerable bhikṣus tell this bhikṣu: 'Depart! You are no bhikṣu of our assembly.'

"At that time, this bhikṣu did not formally relinquish the precepts but put on lay clothing and took delight among sensual pleasures. This is called a bhikṣu for whom Māra has gained an opening, who follows Pāpīyas's wishes, and who is not freed from birth, old age, sickness, death, sorrow, grief, suffering, and affliction. Thus, bhikṣus! Gain and material provisions are extremely difficult, extremely painful, and extremely fearful. They cause one to fall into the evil destinies and prevent one from being born in the supreme realm."

"Therefore, bhikṣus, you should train thus: gains already obtained should be relinquished; gains not yet obtained should not arouse greed. Thus, bhikṣus, should you train."

At that time, the bhikṣus, hearing what the Buddha taught, joyfully accepted and practiced it.
