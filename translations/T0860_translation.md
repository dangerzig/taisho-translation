# 大日經持誦次第儀軌

## Sequential Ritual Manual for Recitation of the Mahāvairocana Sūtra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0860

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown.

---

### Chapter on Pure Conduct and Protection

> With utmost sincerity, reverence, and single-minded abiding,
> Prostrate with five limbs touching the ground in homage.
> Take refuge in the perfectly enlightened ones of the ten directions,
> All those of the three times possessing the three bodies.
> Take refuge in all Mahāyāna teachings,
> Take refuge in the assembly of those irreversible from awakening.
> Take refuge in all vidyā and true words,
> Take refuge in all secret mudrās.
> With purified karma of body, speech, and mind,
> Offer earnest and boundless reverent homage.

Mantra of skillful means for taking refuge:

*Oṃ namaḥ sarva-tathāgatākāya-vāk-citta-vandanāṃ karomi*

> Kneel on the right knee with palms joined,
> Contemplate and confess previous evil karma:
> "Through ignorance I have accumulated
> Evil deeds of body, speech, and mind.
> Because desire, anger, and delusion covered my heart,
> Toward the Buddha, true Dharma, and noble Sangha,
> Parents, both teachers, and spiritual friends,
> And toward immeasurable sentient beings,
> Throughout the cycle of birth and death since beginningless time,
> I have committed extremely grave and endless transgressions.
> Before all the buddhas presently in the ten directions,
> I confess all and vow never to commit them again."

Mantra of skillful means for removing transgressions:

*Oṃ sarva-pāpa-sphaṭana-hana vajrāya svāhā*

> "Homage to the buddhas of the ten directions and three times,
> The three kinds of permanent bodies and the treasury of true Dharma,
> The assemblies of superior vows and great bodhicitta:
> I now take complete and true refuge in all."

Mantra of skillful means for taking refuge:

*Oṃ sarva-buddha-bodhi-sattvebhyaḥ śaraṇa-gacchāmi vajra-dharmahṛd*

> "I purify this body, freeing it from all defilements,
> Together with the body, speech, and mind of the three times.
> Surpassing in number the sand-grains of the great ocean and atoms of buddha-fields,
> I offer all to every tathāgata."

Mantra of skillful means for offering the body:

*Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-pūja-pravartana-ātmānaṃ niryātayāmi sarva-tathāgata-kiñcana me abhiṣiñcatu sarva-tathāgata-kiñcana me*

> "The jewel of the supreme vow of pure bodhicitta:
> I now arouse it to deliver all beings.
> Those entangled by the collection of suffering from birth onward,
> And those whose bodies are harmed by ignorance,
> I shall rescue, shelter, and liberate,
> Always benefiting all sentient beings."

*Oṃ bodhicittam utpādayāmi*

> "Throughout the boundless worlds of the ten directions,
> The ocean assemblies of all perfectly enlightened ones,
> With their many kinds of skillful means and powers,
> And all the buddha-children for sentient beings,
> Whatever meritorious deeds they have practiced,
> I now rejoice in all of them."

Mantra of skillful means for rejoicing:

*Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-puṇyajñānānumodanā-pūja-megha-samudra-spharaṇa-samaye hūṃ*

> "I now entreat all tathāgatas,
> Those of great bodhicitta who rescue the world:
> May you throughout all worlds of the ten directions
> Constantly rain the great cloud of Dharma."

Mantra of skillful means for entreaty:

*Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-adhyeṣaṇa-pūja-megha-samudra-spharaṇa-samaye hūṃ*

> "May all sentient beings in their places of dwelling
> Swiftly abandon bodies that accumulate suffering,
> Attain the stage of the stainless ground,
> And abide peacefully in the pure dharmadhātu essence."

*Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-niddhyāyāmi sarva-sattvahitārthāya dharma-dhātu-sthityarbhava tu*

> "Whatever various meritorious deeds I have practiced,
> For the benefit of all sentient beings,
> I now dedicate all of them correctly:
> May they eliminate the suffering of birth and death and reach awakening."

Mantra of skillful means for dedication:

*Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-vāk-citta-niryātana-pūja-megha-samudra-spharaṇa-samaye hūṃ*

Mudrā for entering the buddha samaya:

> To purify the defilements of the three karmas,
> Form the samaya mudrā.
> First join the wisdom and concentration hands,
> And extend both space wheels upright.
> Touch all the limbs throughout the body,
> While reciting the true words.

Mantra for entering the buddha samaya:

*Namaḥ sarva-tathāgatebhyo viśva-mukhebhyaḥ oṃ asami trī samaya samaye svāhā*

Dharmadhātu-born mudrā:

> With the prajñā-samādhi hands,
> Both form vajra fists.
> The two space wheels rest within the palms,
> The wind banners standing straight upright.
> Place the mudrā upon the forehead,
> Circle the body and scatter downward.
> Contemplate the body as equal to the dharmadhātu,
> Stainless like empty space.

Dharmadhātu-born mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ dharmadhātu-svabhāvako 'haṃ*

> The vajrasattva mudrā:
> Form the vajra-jñāna seal.
> The samādhi and wisdom hands back to back,
> Earth, water, fire, and wind wheels,
> Left and right mutually grasping,
> The two space wheels each revolving,
> Joined within the wisdom palm.
> This is called the Dharma wheel,
> The most victorious auspicious mudrā.

Vajrasattva mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-vajrāṇāṃ oṃ vajrātmako 'haṃ*

> Having recited this mantra,
> Abide in meditative equipoise.
> Contemplate that this very body
> Is itself Vajradhara.
> Armor vajra mudrā:
> First form the añjali,
> The samādhi and wisdom wind wheels
> Twisted upon the fire wheels,
> Touching the upper joint of the middle fingers.
> The two space wheels parallel,
> Resting within the palms.
> The fire wheels visualize the syllable *raṃ*.
> The syllable *raṃ* like a luminous jewel,
> Placed upon the crown of the head.

Armor mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-vajrāṇāṃ oṃ vajra-kavaca hūṃ*

> Next contemplate the Unbearable One,
> Who subdues all great obstacles.
> The syllable *raṃ*, blazing with brilliant flames,
> Encircles and adorns the entire body.

Mantra of the Unbearable One:

*Namaḥ sarva-tathāgatebhyo viśva-mukhebhyaḥ sarva-pāya-vigatebhyaḥ viśva-mukhebhyaḥ sarva-thā hūṃ khaṃ hūṃ rakṣa mahā-vāle sarva-tathāgata-puṇya-nidhate hūṃ hūṃ trāṭ trāṭ aparāhate svāhā*

### Chapter Three: Offering Ritual

> First form the samaya,
> Then the Acala sword mudrā,
> And offer arghya water.
> Next present the vajra seat,
> Then offer incense, flowers, and such.
> The Defilement-Removing Immovable Lord,
> Visualize before you the syllable *raṃ*,
> At a distance of one cubit,
> Blazing with the radiance of the morning sun.
> Recite the true meaning of the mantra,
> Which can remove all obstacles.
> First purify the ground of your own mind,
> Then purify the ground of the ritual space.
> Eliminate all faults completely;
> Its aspect is like empty space.
> Below, contemplate the *ha* wind wheel,
> Black flames streaming forth.
> Next, the water wheel with the syllable *va*,
> Its color like a milky ocean.
> Next, contemplate the earth with the syllable *a*,
> Golden in all four directions.
> For the above four syllable contemplation gates,
> Always first recite the homage.

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ vīraṃ ha va a*

> From this vajra wheel,
> All dharma aspects universally manifest.
> Contemplate the Lord Muni
> Seated upon a hundred-jeweled lotus.
> Wondrous flowers surge all around,
> Guarded by hundreds of millions of lotuses,
> Each upon its lotus flower
> Bearing a lord of immeasurable majestic power,
> Within a vast celestial palace,
> Jeweled pillars in rows,
> Banners, streamers, flower garlands, and the like,
> Jeweled vases and arghya offerings.
> Raining universally clouds of fragrant flowers,
> Precious treasures streaming everywhere.
> Nets of light hang like delicate canopies,
> Mutually reflecting wondrous images.
> The palace soars with tall, broad trees,
> Bright and luminous throughout the dharmadhātu.
> Spreading canopies of precious jade branches,
> Flowers and leaves fragrant everywhere.
> Fruits of a thousand splendid varieties,
> Scattered abundantly across the Dharma assembly.
> Skillfully colored maṇi lamps,
> Draped with trailing celestial garments.
> Fragrant herbs full of sweetness,
> Fragrant foods of every rare flavor,
> Celestial music playing wondrous tones,
> Harmonious and pleasing to the mind.
> Cloud terraces without limit,
> Wondrously displaying all offering implements.
> By one's own power, the Buddha's blessing,
> And the mind pervading the dharmadhātu:
> Simply recite this mantra
> To fulfill the vast offering.

Ākāśagarbha vidyā-consort mantra:

*Namaḥ sarva-tathāgatebhyo viśva-mukhebhyaḥ oṃ sarva-thākhaṃ udgata spharahimaṃ gagana-khaṃ svāhā*

> All dharmas are unborn,
> Because their own nature is originally quiescent.
> Contemplate this mantra and
> Place the syllable *a* at its center.
> It transforms into Mahāvairocana,
> Universally manifesting an assembly like sand-grains.
> Radiant flames pervade the dharmadhātu,
> According to their natures, bringing awakening.
> Upon the great lotus king,
> Contemplate the Lord Muni.
> Between the Lord's white ūrṇā,
> A bright lord again appears.
> Having contemplated this bright lord,
> Then perform the empowerment practice.

Heart mantra of Mahāvairocana Tathāgata:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ a*

Place the wisdom hand in vajra fist at the space between the eyebrows.

Tathāgata ūrṇā mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ āḥ haṃja*

Next, the mantras of all deities. Universal seed-syllable heart mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ ka*

> The *ka* syllable gate spoken in the sūtras:
> All dharmas are without action.
> The cintāmaṇi jewel king seal:
> The ten wheels of concentration and wisdom interlaced,
> The vajra añjali form,
> Pervading all bodhisattva practices.

Mantra of all bodhisattvas:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ sarva-thā-vimati-vikiraṇa-dharma-dhātu-jāta-saṃsāha svāhā*

> Acala, the Defilement-Remover,
> Clears away and makes brilliant.
> To quell all obstacles,
> He abides in the fire-born samādhi.

Mantra of Acala, the Immovable Messenger:

*Namaḥ samanta-vajrāṇāṃ caṇḍa-mahāroṣaṇa sphaṭaya hūṃ trāṭ hāṃ māṃ*

Use the wisdom sword to seal all directions and boundaries.

> Next perform the invocation practice.
> The firm vajra-bandha:
> Straighten the two wind wheels
> And bend their upper joints.
> This is named the vajra hook,
> Which summons all assemblies.
> The holy ones come according to their vows,
> Not violating their original pledges.

Mantra for summoning the sacred assembly:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ āḥ sarva-trā-prahatitathāgataṃ kuśa-bodhicyā-prapūraka svāhā*

> Next offer the samaya
> Complete with mantra and mudrā;
> The mudrā form is as previously explained,
> As in the samaya teachings.

Samaya mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ a-samayat-rī-samaya-samaye svāhā*

> Next offer arghya water,
> Presented to all the sugatas,
> For bathing their stainless bodies.

Arghya mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ gagana-sama-sama svāhā*

> Next offer the lotus seat,
> Placed universally everywhere.
> The two space wheels and earth wheels
> Joined together form the base.
> The remaining wheels spread apart:
> This is the lotus seal.

Tathāgata seat mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ āḥ*

> Next perform purification
> With the great wisdom-sword seal.
> The mantra of Holy Acala:
> Contemplate oneself as identical to him.
> The supreme vajra flame
> Burns all obstacles without remainder.
> Then immediately transform into
> The body of Vajrasattva.
> Mantra, mudrā, and contemplation in accord,
> Pervade and adorn all the limbs.

Vajra seed-syllable heart mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ vaṃ*

> Contemplate this true meaning:
> All dharmas are beyond words.
> Because one possesses the seal and so forth,
> One becomes identical to Vajradhara.
> Know that the seal's form
> Is the water wheel within the five-pronged vajra.

Vajrasattva mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-vajrāṇāṃ caṇḍa-mahāroṣaṇa hūṃ*

> Next, pervading the entire body,
> Don the vajra armor.
> The body and speech secret seals
> Were previously explained according to the teaching.
> Next, the syllable *kha* with a bindu
> Is placed upon the crown of the head.
> Contemplate this mantra:
> All dharmas are like empty space.

That mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ khaṃ*

> Next, with single-minded abiding,
> Employ the māra-subduing seal.
> The wise one should turn it universally,
> In accord with the mantra.
> All those with malicious minds
> Shall see the entire ground
> Blazing with vajra flames.
> With the wisdom hand,
> Form a vajra fist,
> Extend the wind wheel straight,
> And apply it to the ūrṇā spot,
> In the form of Bhṛkuṭī:
> This is that emblem.
> This seal is named the great seal;
> Contemplating it eliminates all māras.

Bhṛkuṭī mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ sarva-bhaya-trāsani hūṃ sphaṭaya svāhā*

> Next use the Unbearable One's
> Secret seal and mantra
> To seal the surrounding boundary:
> Ferocious, none can behold it.
> First form the añjali,
> The wind wheels within the palms.
> The space and earth wheels
> Curve inward like hooks.
> The fire wheels joined at the peak,
> Open and spread the water wheels,
> Revolving the fingers toward the ten directions:
> This is named sealing the great boundary.

Mantra of the Unbearable One:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ rī ru pu vī ku rī svāhā*

> Or use the Immovable Lord
> To accomplish all tasks:
> Protecting the body, purifying the space,
> And sealing all the boundaries.

Seed-syllable heart mantra of the Immovable Lord:

*Namaḥ samanta-vajrāṇāṃ haṃ*

> Form the wisdom-sword seal,
> And sprinkle purification everywhere.
> The incense, flowers, and such,
> All offering implements prepared,
> Again universally establish everywhere
> The pure dharmadhātu heart,
> Namely the syllable-gate of *raṃ*,
> As previously instructed.

Incense mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ viśuddha-gandha-udbhava svāhā*

Flower mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ mahā-maitryā-abhyudgate svāhā*

Burning incense mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ dharma-dhātva-anuga te svāhā*

Lamp mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ tathāgata-arci-spharaṇa-vabhāsa-dhāri svāhā*

Food offering mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ arara karara malinandami malinande mahā-māle svāhā*

> All remaining offering implements
> That should be presented:
> Follow this same procedure,
> Purified with the Immovable Lord.
> Join the concentration and wisdom palms,
> The five wheels interlaced:
> This is the universal offering seal
> For holding all articles.
> Then perform the mental ceremony:
> All offerings are fully replete.
> In this direction and in all others,
> Great offering ornament clouds,
> Each raining all offering substances,
> Offering to the worlds of the ten directions,
> All buddhas and bodhisattvas,
> Using the Ākāśagarbha universal seal.
> As the practice is duly performed,
> All accumulated merit
> Is dedicated toward accomplishment
> For the benefit of all sentient beings.
> If practitioners of true words
> Seek accomplishment in this lifetime,
> First recite according to the proper method;
> When the practice session is complete,
> Then with the Vajradhara's
> Supreme hymn of praise,
> Offer to buddhas and bodhisattvas
> And swiftly achieve accomplishment.

Vajra hymn verse:

> "Because Vajrasattva accepts and sustains us,
> We become the supreme vajra jewel.
> Through vajra speech and song of praise,
> May we accomplish the supreme vajra activity."

Verse for turning the vidyā:

> "Through the adornment of my own merit,
> Born from the pure dharmadhātu,
> By the empowering force of the Tathāgata,
> May the welfare of all beings be fulfilled.
> Recite the Ākāśagarbha turning vidyā three times;
> The secret seal is as previously explained."

### Chapter Four: Recitation Procedure

> Having thus performed the proper offering,
> Arouse the mind to benefit boundless sentient beings.
> Pay homage to all buddhas and celestial beings,
> Take the appropriate meditation seat and enter samādhi.
> The ritual of the four types of dhyāna
> Can generate joy and bliss within the mind.
> Through empowerment with true meaning,
> The mantra becomes meditative equipoise.
> The wise one, as previously instructed,
> Directly contemplates the honored one.
> Within the clear and bright moon-disk of the mind,
> Clearly perceive all the mantra syllables.
> The practitioner should generate firm resolve,
> First single-pointedly contemplate the honored one,
> Hold that one's mantra and secret seal,
> And make oneself the yoga-body of that honored one.
> By abiding in the body of inherent correspondence,
> Even those of little merit achieve accomplishment.
> If the practitioner recites
> The mantra and seal of Mahāvairocana,
> The Perfectly Enlightened One,
> It should be done according to this method.

Seed-syllable heart mantra of Mahāvairocana Tathāgata:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ a*

> The body-secret seal herein
> Is the Perfectly Enlightened One's white ūrṇā mark:
> The wisdom hand in vajra fist
> Placed at the space between the eyebrows.

Tathāgata ūrṇā mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ āḥ haṃja*

> As before, turn the syllable *a*
> And become Mahāvairocana.
> Through the power of the Dharma,
> Indistinguishable from one's own body.
> Abiding in the yoga of the honored one,
> Empower with the five-limb syllables:
> Lower body, navel, heart, crown, and between the brows.
> In samāhita meditation,
> Visualize and establish them.
> By abiding according to this method,
> One becomes identical to the Lord Muni.
> The syllable *a* empowers the lower body: the golden wheel yoga seat.
> The syllable *vaṃ* empowers the navel: the pure white light of great compassion water.
> The syllable *raṃ* empowers the heart: the red-cornered wisdom fire light.
> The syllable *haṃ* empowers the ūrṇā: the black wind of sovereign power.
> The syllable *khaṃ* at the crown: the five colors named great space.

The five syllables adorning the body bring majestic accomplishment and blaze with great wisdom light, eliminating all karmic transgressions. Those who see this person are awed; the armies of celestial māras retreat.

**Equal to great compassion, the space-crown**: Because the nature of boundless space is unobstructed. Like the realm of space, free from all discrimination and non-discrimination; thus, the wondrous contemplation of all-knowledge and omniscience is equal, free from all discrimination and non-discrimination. Therefore it is called "equal without equal."

**The wind of great compassion at the forehead**: Because the wisdom-wind of obstacle-removing can open and develop. Just as wind can purify and remove dust, fog, smoke, and clouds, so the great wisdom-wind purifies and removes the defilements of ignorance, afflictions, and torpor.

**The fire of great compassion at the heart**: Because the torch of samādhi-wisdom nurtures and fosters. Just as fire can burn all firewood, so the fire of all-knowledge burns the firewood of ignorance, extinguishes delusional karmic defilements, leaving no remainder, and in the darkness of ignorance serves as a great illumination.

**The water of great compassion at the navel**: Because the milky ocean of merit nourishes and moistens. Just as water can flow from high to low, greatly benefiting and moistening grasses, trees, flowers, and fruits, its nature originally pure; so the wisdom-water of the Tathāgata flows from the dharmadhātu of true words into the worldly realm, moistening the Dharma practices that sustain and assist the path, producing great fruits, constantly washing the mind of awakening so that it remains undefiled.

**The seat of great compassion at the earth boundary**: Because the vajra ground beneath the vajra seat upholds and supports. Just as earth can give birth to all things, bearing and supporting the entire world without being toppled; so the ground of all-knowledge is born from the Tathāgata's vajra nature. That which is born is ultimately unborn, yet it can give birth to the myriad virtues of awakening, upholding the mind-ground of all worthy and holy ones, indestructible.

> Also, place at the head
> The King of Hundred Lights Universally Illuminating.
> At the eyes, establish the stainless syllable,
> Bright as a lamp, clearly illuminating.
> The wise one contemplates the self-body
> As equal to the Tathāgata's body.
> At the mind's moon-disk, bright and clear,
> The garland of sounds in correspondence.
> Syllable after syllable without interruption,
> Like the ringing of melodious bells.
> The mantra of the Perfectly Enlightened One:
> Select and uphold according to one's practice.
> Know that this skillful means
> Quickly achieves accomplishment.
> Or use an alternative ritual
> Through the universal gate of the three mysteries.
> If one understands the turning and revolving,
> All endeavors will be accomplished.

Universal seed-syllable heart mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ ka*

> With this light of principle,
> Contemplate the true meaning of this sound.
> The maṇi jewel king seal:
> Concentration and wisdom interlaced.

All bodhisattvas mantra: (as above)

> The syllable *kha* containing all colors,
> Enhanced with the great space bindu,
> Placed upon the crown of the head,
> One becomes equal to empty space.
> Within the head,
> Contemplate the primordial syllable,
> Adorned with a pure white bindu:
> The supreme heart of a hundred vidyā.
> The eye realm, like a bright lamp:
> The great space stainless syllable.
> Abiding in the seat of the honored one,
> The Perfectly Enlightened One manifests directly.
> Again contemplate the heart center,
> The full and pure moon-disk.
> Brightly manifest the *a* syllable gate,
> Entirely vajra-colored.
> Its sound and true meaning:
> All dharmas are fundamentally unborn.
> With the *ka* syllable as the leader,
> Or whatever true words are upheld,
> Arrayed in a circle of brightness.
> Single syllables and sentence-causes
> Enter and exit as one wishes.
> Only then perform the recitation,
> Continuing until exhaustion brings rest.
> Or arrange the mantra syllables
> Within the moon-disk of the mind.
> According to the deep secret meaning,
> Contemplate the true nature of each sound.
> Or offer incense, flowers, and such,
> In sequence cultivating the offerings.

### Chapter Five: True Practice

> As in the previous practice procedure,
> Empower oneself and transform
> Into the body of Vajrasattva.
> Contemplate the buddhas and bodhisattvas,
> Their immeasurable merits.
> Toward boundless sentient beings,
> Arouse the great mind of compassion.
> With the vajra hymn of praise
> And other wondrous words,
> Extol the tathāgatas'
> True merit. Dedicate all good done,
> Making this aspiration:
> "Just as the Great Enlightened World-Honored Ones
> Dedicated their realized merit
> Toward great awakening,
> So too do I now
> Take all my accumulated merit
> And share it with all dharmadhātu beings,
> Together crossing the ocean of birth and death,
> Attaining the path of perfect enlightenment,
> Self-benefit and benefit of others,
> Abiding in the Tathāgata's great abiding,
> Not solely for myself.
> Even returning to birth and death,
> Delivering all sentient beings,
> Attaining the wisdom of all aspects,
> Reaching the foremost peace and bliss,
> And offering arghya water."
> Form the añjali of homage
> And place it at the crown of the head,
> Contemplating all buddhas and bodhisattvas,
> Their true and great merit,
> And with utmost sincerity pay homage.

Verse of homage:

> "Those who have forever abandoned all faults,
> Whose bodies are adorned with immeasurable merit,
> Who single-mindedly benefit all sentient beings:
> I now pay homage to all of you."

Next, address the assembly of holy ones with this verse:

> "All tathāgatas now present,
> All bodhisattvas who rescue the world,
> Who do not sever the great vehicle teaching,
> Who have reached the supreme stages:
> May the assembly of sacred celestials
> Definitively know and witness me.
> May each return to your abodes,
> And later again come with compassion."

Next, release the samaya mantra and secret seal at the crown of the head, generating this thought: "May all bonds, protections, and empowerments be released and liberated."

Through this skillful means, each of the honored ones previously invited returns to their own abode, not detained by the unobstructed great vow.

Again, use the dharmadhātu's fundamental nature to empower the self-body, contemplate the pure bodhicitta, and abide in the body of Vajrasattva. The vidyā and seal herein have been previously explained.

If, after completing the recitation, one holds the body with these three seals, all activities of the mantra path are concluded, and the ritual procedures are all perfectly complete.

Furthermore, one should, as previously instructed, contemplate the dharmadhātu syllable as the crown mark, don the vajra armor, and through this secret adornment, become indestructible like the nature of vajra itself. All who hear the sound, or see, or touch this practitioner will certainly attain anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi. All merits will be accomplished, equal to Mahāvairocana Tathāgata without any difference.

Next, arouse the superior mind to practice supreme activities. In a pure place adorned with incense and flowers, first transform the self-body into Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, or abide in the Tathāgata's own nature. Following the previous methods, empower with mantra and secret seal. Then, with the mind of Dharma-giving, read and recite Mahāyāna vaipulya sūtras, or recite mentally, inviting celestial spirits and others to listen and receive the teaching. As the verse says:

> "The Vajraśekhara Sūtra teaches:
> Avalokiteśvara, the lotus-eyed one,
> Is identical to all buddhas,
> The body of inexhaustible adornments.
> Or take the world's guide,
> The sovereign lord of all dharmas,
> Select any one epithet,
> And use it for the fundamental-nature empowerment."

Seed-syllable heart mantra of Avalokiteśvara:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ sa*

> The syllable-gate's true meaning:
> All dharmas are unattached.
> The sound that flows forth:
> Contemplate it in this manner.
> The body-secret aspect herein
> Is the so-called lotus seal.
> As in the previously offered seat,
> I have already explained it fully.

Next, the mantra of Avalokiteśvara:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ sarva-tathāgata-avalokita-karuṇā-maya rara ra hūṃ jaḥ svāhā*

Having placed the dharmadhātu heart syllable at the crown as before, and additionally empowered with this mantra, secret seal, and contemplation, read and recite sūtras according to one's ability, or construct caitya maṇḍalas. When the activities are complete, next rise from the seat and with a harmonious and respectful manner attend to worldly affairs.

Furthermore, to sustain the body-wheel, next go on alms round, or accept a patron's invitation, or receive what is given in the sangha. One should avoid fish, meat, and pungent vegetables. After offering first to the honored one and all buddhas, one should not eat various leftover, overnight, or impure foods. All liquors and fruit-juice beverages that can cause intoxication should not be consumed.

Next, take a portion of food and offer it to the honored one. Then perform the eating-as-one-wishes ritual. If there is a remainder, set aside another portion for the starving and those who beg for alms, generating this thought: "I accept this morsel of food to sustain the vessel of the body and practice the Way in peace, like greasing a wheel-axle to prevent damage and reach the destination. I should not partake for the sake of flavor." Neither increase nor decrease the mind's attachment, and do not generate thoughts of beautifying the body. Then contemplate the dharmadhātu heart syllable to purify all food, and empower the self-body with the karma vajra.

The seed syllable herein is as explained for the syllable *vaṃ* mantra. Then recite the ten-powers vidyā eight times before eating.

That vidyā:

*Namaḥ sarva-buddha-bodhisattvānāṃ oṃ mālindati rumāṅgare svāhā*

Thus abide, first accomplishing the yoga of the honored one. After the meal, the remaining tangible food is offered with the accomplishment mantra heart to those who should eat. Enhance with the Infallible Wrathful One, and recite the mantra of Holy Acala once. The recipient will be delighted and will constantly follow and protect the practitioner.

That mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-vajrāṇāṃ trāṭ amogha-caṇḍa-mahāroṣaṇa sphaṭaya hūṃ trāṭ māṃ trāṭ māṃ hūṃ trāṭ haṃ māṃ*

After eating, rest for a short while, then pay homage to all buddhas and confess all transgressions.

To purify the mind, practice the regular observances in this manner, including reading sūtras as before.

Always abide thus. In the afternoon, practice likewise. During the first and last watches of the night, contemplate the Mahāyāna without interruption. In the middle watch, use the karma vajra as before, don the vajra armor, pay homage to all buddhas and great bodhisattvas. Then direct the mind to offer according to the Dharma, generating this thought: "For the sake of all sentient beings and the great cause, I should protect this body and rest briefly. It is not for the sake of indulging in the pleasure of sleep." First sit with proper posture, cross both feet, and lie on the right side. If the limbs are fatigued, turn to any side without fault. To awaken quickly, always keep the mind fixed on the vidyā. Furthermore, do not lie flat on the bed. On subsequent days, practice likewise.

The mantra-holder, by not neglecting the ritual procedures and practicing continuously without interruption, earns the title of "one who practices the bodhisattva path through the mantra gate."

If, after a period of time, signs appear during recitation practice and one has performed the preliminary methods through the superior practice without achieving accomplishment, one should arouse self-awareness, redouble one's diligence, and not generate thoughts of inferiority such as: "This teaching is beyond my capacity." Thus, by extending one's resolve, benefiting self and others, never letting time pass in vain. Because the practitioner is earnest and never rests, all the holy ones secretly observe the mind and bestow the empowerment of their spiritual power, enabling the practitioner to be free from all obstacles.

Herein are two things one should never abandon: never abandon all buddhas and bodhisattvas, and never abandon the mind that benefits boundless sentient beings. By maintaining the aspiration for all-knowledge without wavering, this cause and condition will certainly be established, and accomplishment of the appropriate class of siddhi will follow.

Next, the mantra of Trailokyavijaya:
