# 謗佛經
## Sūtra on Slandering the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0831

Translated from the Chinese. Northern Wei dynasty, translated by Bodhiruci (菩提流支)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Bhagavān was dwelling at Rājagṛha on Mount Gṛdhrakūṭa, together with a great assembly of twelve hundred and fifty bhikṣus and eighty thousand bodhisattvas. At that time, in the assembly there were bodhisattva-mahāsattvas seated in the great gathering: Free-from-Sorrow (離憂悲), Lion's Sport (師子遊戲), Wisdom-Radiance (智光明), Brahma-Thunder Cloud-Voice (梵雷音響雲聲), Excellent-Merit Jewel-Flower Radiance (善作功德寶華光明), Lion's Stride Cloud-Thunder Voice (師子遊步雲雷音聲), Splendor-of-Majestic-Virtue and Renown (光明威德名聞), Boundless-Wisdom Contemplation-Adornment (無邊智聚思惟莊嚴), Boundless Jewel-Flower Renown (無邊寶華名稱), and Wisdom-Radiance Eloquence (智慧光明辯才說意). These ten bodhisattva-mahāsattvas had gathered in the assembly. For seven years they had diligently cultivated the practice of dhāraṇī but were unable to attain it. When the seven years were complete, they grew sorrowful, thinking, "Our minds cannot even become settled, let alone attain dhāraṇī!" Having failed in their quest, their minds grew weary. Although for seven years they had cast off the veil of delusion, constantly practicing walking meditation in pursuit of the dhāraṇī, they were unable to attain it. They abandoned the precepts, returned to lay life, departed from the Buddha-Dharma, and engaged in base conduct, giving rise to doubt and confusion regarding the Buddha-Dharma. Such was their transgression. King Ajātaśatru was also seated in the great assembly.

At that time, the World-Honored One had previously eliminated King Ajātaśatru's doubt and remorse. Having cast off his doubt and remorse, King Ajātaśatru attained liberation from all sorrow and affliction. For seven days he dispensed great gifts of wealth. When this great giving over seven days was complete, seven hundred million beings came together to the Buddha's place to hear the Dharma. The ten bodhisattvas, those good men who had already incurred transgression within the Buddha-Dharma, were also seated in the assembly.

At that time, in the assembly there was a bodhisattva-mahāsattva named Fearless-Practice, who had attained dhāraṇī, accomplished dharma-patience through asaṃkhyeya kalpas, attained the patience of the unborn, possessed various kinds of eloquence, could know the gate of omniscience, could teach the Dharma-gates, and could know the deep-minded faith and understanding of sentient beings in order to teach them.

At that time, Fearless-Practice Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva rose from his seat, adjusted his robe over one shoulder, placed his right knee on the ground, joined his palms and faced the Buddha, saying: "Venerable One! In this assembly, ten good men have diligently cultivated the practice of dhāraṇī for seven years, yet their aspirations have not been fulfilled. They have abandoned the Buddha-Dharma and become laypeople. Excellent, World-Honored One! Please explain, by your skillful means, so that these good men may swiftly come to understand."

At that time, the World-Honored One said to Fearless-Practice Bodhisattva: "Good man! You should know that these ten good men have never heard the Dharma-gate of slandering the Buddha. These good men once slandered the Buddha, and for this reason they cannot swiftly attain supernatural penetration."

At that time, Fearless-Practice Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva questioned the Tathāgata in verse:

> "I now ask the Benevolent One, the radiant King of Dharma:
> When practicing the bodhisattva path, how is evil karma purified?
> O One of immeasurable, supreme wisdom, of the ten powers and unobstructed knowledge,
> Liberated and free from idle speculation: please teach the bodhisattva's pure practice.
> I ask the One of boundless wisdom, I ask the One who severs evil intentions,
> I ask the incomparable Honored One: what is the bodhisattva's practice?
> Already freed from all bondage, apart from afflictions, having vanquished Māra,
> Knowing the minds and thoughts of sentient beings: please teach the bodhisattva's practice.
> With a smiling face like a blossoming flower, who would sever the conduct of existence,
> Who teaches the Dharma to sever the thought of existence: please teach the bodhisattva's practice.
> Who can bestow immeasurable joy, with excellent merit and excellent practice,
> Who throughout all the worlds brings benefit and happiness:
> Already through immeasurable ages, having given up beloved possessions,
> Elephants, horses, and a hundred jeweled heads, immeasurable wives and children given away;
> Who has tamed himself through patience, delighting in the merit of precept-practice,
> With resolute and diligent vigor, whose mind is never sorrowful;
> Whose hundred kinds of suffering are already exhausted, whose sought-after aims are all fulfilled:
> Please teach the Dharma as is fitting, to sever the suffering of sentient beings.
> Please teach how to leave the three defilements; how does one leave unwholesomeness?
> Please teach the bodhisattva's practice, to tame and benefit sentient beings."

At that time, the World-Honored One said to Fearless-Practice Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva: "Good man! These ten bodhisattvas once slandered the Buddha."

Fearless-Practice Bodhisattva said: "World-Honored One! How did they slander the Buddha?"

The Buddha said: "Fearless-Practice! These ten bodhisattvas, in a past age thirty kalpas ago, when there was a Buddha named Avalokiteśvara Tathāgata, Worthy of Offerings, Perfectly Enlightened, in a world called Blazing Flames: Fearless-Practice! The ten good men now in this assembly, after that Avalokiteśvara Tathāgata entered nirvāṇa, were all sons of great families and wealthy elders. They built five hundred monasteries, and in each monastery installed a thousand bhikṣus. At that time there was a Dharma-teacher named Eloquence-Accumulation, who had attained dhāraṇī and sat upon the Dharma-seat to teach the assembly. Five thousand Buddhas all granted him eloquence, and eighty million devas guarded and made offerings to him. In a single Dharma-teaching by the Dharma-teacher Eloquence-Accumulation, seventy thousand sentient beings all became irreversible from supreme, perfect awakening, and ten thousand sentient beings attained the fruit of stream-entry.

"Good man! At that time there was a king named Moon-Attainer, who together with five hundred attendant women went to make offerings to the Dharma-teacher Eloquence-Accumulation. Out of reverence for the Dharma, they performed songs and music, scattered various jeweled flowers upon that bhikṣu, anointed his body with sandalwood and other fragrances, covered him with five hundred fine garments, and for seven days offered every kind of material comfort with their whole hearts, making offerings to him as befits a Dharma-teacher.

"At that time, those sons of elders said that bhikṣu had broken the pure precepts. As retribution for that evil karma, they fell into the great hells for ninety thousand years. For five hundred lifetimes, although born as humans, they received the body of a eunuch. They were born among barbarian peoples and in families of wrong views. For six hundred lifetimes they were born blind and tongueless. For seven hundred lifetimes, although they went forth as monastics and sought dhāraṇī, they were unable to attain it. Why? Because of the obstruction of their evil karma from past lives.

"Good man! You should well understand this Dharma-gate that I have now taught. Having heard it, even if one sees a Dharma-teacher who has truly broken the precepts, one must not give rise to anger. One should not even speak of it; how much less should one speak of it based merely on hearsay? Good man! If we gather together the transgression of gouging out the eyes of all sentient beings, the evil karma of one who looks upon a Dharma-teacher with a mind of anger exceeds that accumulated transgression. If we gather together the transgression of taking the lives of all sentient beings, one who gives rise to an evil thought toward a Dharma-teacher, even for the time it takes to turn one's face away, accumulates a transgression that, compared with the former, does not equal one part in a hundred, not one part in a thousand, not one part in a hundred thousand, not one asaṃkhyeya part, not one kalā part, not one numerical part, not one fraction part, nor even one upaniṣad part. Why? Because to slander a Dharma-teacher is to slander the Buddha.

"Good man! If one wishes to make offerings to the Buddha, that person should make offerings to the Dharma-teacher. If one wishes to attend upon the Buddha, that person should attend upon the Dharma-teacher. If one wishes to bow to the Buddha, that person should bow to the Dharma-teacher. Why? Because omniscience is born from bodhisattvas, and all Buddhas are born from bodhisattvas.

"Good man! When does a bodhisattva generate the mind of awakening? Having generated that mind, one does not dwell in desire and pleasure, is not stained by afflictions, and does not dwell in any acts contrary to the pure conduct. One should understand this. Such a bodhisattva enters the formless concentrations yet is not reborn in the formless realm. Why? Because the bodhisattva is not born by the power of samādhi, but is born by the power of vows. Such a bodhisattva is liberated from the deluded conduct of all hair-pore beings, ordinary people, and the foolish. If anyone could see the color of empty space, that person could see the afflictions of a bodhisattva.

"Good man! Consider the Nāga King Anavatapta: though counted among the nāga host and no different in bodily form from all other nāgas, he is far removed from the three afflictions of nāgas. What are the three? First, hot sand does not fall upon his head. Second, he does not engage in desire in the form of a serpent. Third, he has no fear of garuḍas. Thus, good man, these three afflictions of nāgas cannot affect him. The bodhisattva-mahāsattva is likewise: though possessing all worldly pleasures, the afflictions and suffering of desire in the three realms of existence cannot overwhelm him. One should understand this.

"Furthermore, good man! Consider aquatic creatures such as fish: they move through water, see through water, and enter water without dying. Good man! The bodhisattva-mahāsattva is likewise: traversing birth-and-death and dwelling among ordinary conduct, yet constantly seeking the practice of wisdom and cultivating the true Dharma, with a mind that is never confused. The affairs of ordinary people cannot defile the bodhisattva, and the sufferings of the three realms cannot affect the bodhisattva. Therefore, the bodhisattva-mahāsattva should always protect oneself and never slander a Dharma-teacher."

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> "If one wishes to make offerings to the Buddha, if one wishes to bow to the Tathāgata,
> One need only make offerings to the Buddha's sons: this is the foremost offering to the Buddha.
> I teach that robes and bowls, seats, couches, and places for walking meditation,
> All such things should be given to those who have generated the mind of awakening.
> Thus does one make offerings to the Buddha; thus does one make offerings to the Dharma.
> The Buddha is supreme in the world; all are born from the Dharma.
> This person of solitary wisdom gives sight to those in darkness,
> Shows the true path to the lost, and leads them to the deathless place.
> If one constantly offers all treasures to all the Buddhas,
> Though making such offerings, one cannot repay the Buddha's kindness.
> But if from this day forward one constantly makes offerings to Dharma-teachers,
> And neither slanders nor speaks against them, then one repays the kindness of all the Buddhas."

At that time, Fearless-Practice Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! Is there a skillful gate that can purify the evil karma of these bodhisattvas?"

The Buddha said: "There is. Fearless-Practice! There is a dhāraṇī. If these good men can go forth from household life and single-mindedly recite the phrases of this dhāraṇī, their evil karma will be purified.

*Tadyathā ajite acāra-bhati anā-virī aśrī-ṣa akṛte anāyu-ke aśe abhi-haḷi abhāva-dhūru taṅgā mayu-tā jā-tā nadā-para prā-bheni gati ugāra-ke hau hau mecā-pari sādi māsī samā-dhiyū jinā-ya pari-śuddhi-te*

"Good man! If these ten good men can recite the phrases of this dhāraṇī, for seven days completely relinquishing everything, not eating mixed foods, with minds undistracted, not attached to any form, with minds free from discrimination, abandoning the thought of arrogance, constantly reciting without ceasing, doing nothing else, not mingling with others, practicing with an equal mind and always bringing benefit, with minds constantly cultivating the impermanence of the five aggregates without abandoning them, constantly cultivating the mindfulness of the Buddha, if they practice in this way, they will directly see a thousand Buddhas in the ten directions."

At that time, the ten good men in the assembly, having heard the Buddha's teaching, immediately went forth from household life. Single-mindedly reciting the phrases of this dhāraṇī, they practiced vigor for seven days, according with and gathering in the teaching. They also constantly cultivated the samādhi of mindfulness of the Buddha and saw one thousand Buddhas. All their evil karmic obstructions were confessed and completely extinguished. At that time they immediately attained the collection-of-all-wisdom-gates dhāraṇī, extinguished the karma of birth-and-death for thirty kalpas, and became irreversible from supreme, perfect awakening.

"Fearless-Practice! You should know that the king who made offerings to the Dharma-teacher at that time, Moon-Attainer, is now Amitāyus Tathāgata, Worthy of Offerings, Perfectly Enlightened. Akṣobhya Tathāgata, Worthy of Offerings, Perfectly Enlightened, is the Dharma-teacher Eloquence-Accumulation of that time. The ten sons of the great families, those elder's sons who at that time slandered the Dharma-teacher Eloquence-Accumulation, saying he had violated the pure precepts, are the ten good men now in this assembly.

"Thus, good man! In whatever monastery a bodhisattva may dwell, whether for good or ill, even at the cost of one's own life and body, one must not perceive their faults. Why? Good man! A bodhisattva possesses four kinds of pure dharmas. What are the four? First, cultivating emptiness. Second, never harboring a mind of harm toward any sentient being. Third, always bringing benefit to bodhisattvas. Fourth, teaching the Dharma without seeking livelihood. Good man! These are the four dharmas of the bodhisattva-mahāsattva's pure awakening."

At that time, the World-Honored One, to clarify this meaning, spoke in verse:

> "If one can faithfully understand emptiness, the Buddha teaches this is the foremost Dharma.
> Such pure practice, practiced with constant non-negligence:
> Not harming sentient beings, not speaking words of harm,
> That person will attain Buddhahood, with radiance illuminating the world.
> This is the second pure path. Having heard, one acts to benefit others;
> Practicing patience toward sentient beings, never engaging in harmful conduct.
> There is a Dharma, wholesome and stainless: give it to those who have generated the mind of awakening.
> If one does not seek reward or gratitude, this is the third path to awakening.
> With a mind that resolutely gives rise to compassion, teaching the Dharma without seeking gain,
> With deep-hearted sympathy and pity: this is the fourth path of pure wisdom."

"Furthermore, good man! The bodhisattva-mahāsattva who cultivates dhāraṇī should practice in secluded forests, draw near to hear the Dharma, wear fresh and clean garments, dwell alone in quiet places, constantly contemplate and practice in the four deportments, offer various things to Dharma-teachers, give rise to right faith without following others' words, never grow weary, constantly practice vigor with a mind free from flattery and deceit, never depart from mindfulness of the Buddha, with a mind of non-attainment, well cultivate the markless, and practice as one teaches. With a mind of faith and understanding, invite the Buddhas and confess. Rejoice, transfer merit, and constantly practice what is right. Never transgress the deportments, never fear admonishment, depend upon and abide with the honored ones in all conducts, and in this Dharma cultivate without weariness."

When this Dharma was taught, thirty thousand sentient beings who had never before generated the mind of awakening, upon hearing this Dharma, generated the mind of awakening. Five thousand sentient beings were freed from dust and defilement, and in all dharmas attained the purity of the Dharma-eye. Thirty thousand bodhisattvas all attained the patience of the unborn.

"Good man! If bodhisattva-mahāsattvas hear this Dharma-gate, they will gain immeasurable merit. If one were to fill the three-thousand great-thousand worlds with the seven treasures and give them to the Three Treasures day after day for as many kalpas as there are grains of sand in the Ganges, and if another person were to hear this Dharma-gate, that person's merit would be superior. If, furthermore, one were to practice the five pāramitās for a thousand kalpas, apart from the gate of prajñā-pāramitā, and if another person were to hear this Dharma-gate, that person's merit would be superior. Good man! If, furthermore, a person were to make offerings morning, noon, and evening to a thousand Buddhas, honoring and praising them and attending to their wishes, and if another person were to hear this Dharma-gate, that person's merit would be superior.

"Thus, good man! You should guard and cherish this Dharma-gate. I now entrust it to you for protection: receive it, uphold it, read it, recite it, explain its meaning, and cause it to be copied. Even at the cost of your life and body, you should guard and cherish it and practice in accord with it."

When the Tathāgata had finished speaking, Fearless-Practice Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva, the other bodhisattvas, all the bhikṣus, and the entire assembly, together with the devas, humans, asuras, gandharvas, and all the rest, having heard what the Buddha taught, were all greatly delighted.
