# 大乘方廣曼殊室利菩薩華嚴本教閻曼德迦忿怒王真言大威德儀軌品
## Great Vaipulya Mahāyāna Ritual Manual Chapter on the Mantra of Yamāntaka Wrathful King from the Avataṃsaka Root Teaching of Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1215

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown.

---

### Chapter Thirty

At that time, Vajrapāṇi, the great yakṣa general, observed the great bodhisattvas and devas in the assembly, rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, placed his right knee on the ground, and with palms joined in reverence addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! Mañjuśrī, the youthful true one, has not extensively explained Yamāntaka Wrathful King. I observe that in the future, after the Tathāgata's nirvāṇa, when the Buddha's teaching is hidden and obscured, in the time of great terror in the final dharma age, when there are no śrāvakas or pratyekabuddhas and buddha-fields are far distant: for the sake of protecting the true dharma, for the sake of the dharmadhātu's long abiding, for the sake of subduing all unruly, rebellious, and illegitimate kings, and for those who do not benefit the Three Treasures, the bodhisattva through skillful means uses this inconceivable dharma to discipline and subdue them to satisfaction. Through inconceivable dharma, beings are matured and freed from evil destinies. When the Buddhadharma is being destroyed, those who uphold the Buddhadharma should, in accordance with the teaching, recite and uphold Yamāntaka Wrathful King, and all will certainly attain accomplishment. For sentient beings who do not benefit the Buddha's teaching, and for rebellious and illegitimate kings who do not benefit the Buddha's teaching, one should employ Yamāntaka Wrathful King; no other class of being is appropriate."

At that time the World-Honored One silently entered the samādhi of playful supernatural empowerment. Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva also abided in silent stillness. The entire assembled multitude experienced the six kinds of trembling. The heavenly hosts were all startled, terrified, and fearful. All the devas, nāgas, asura kings, all the mother-goddesses, all the planets and constellations, and the assemblies of devas and humans all trembled, their minds giving rise to anguish. The evil-minded pūtanas and violent ones all took refuge in the Dharma King's teaching, in the Secret Lord Vajrapāṇi yakṣa of great awesome virtue, and in the samaya of Mañjuśrī's youthful true words. They all spoke thus: "Mañjuśrī! We beseech your compassionate protection. We are being scorched by this wrathful true word; we all suffer in agony and collapse upon the ground."

Then the Great Awesome Virtue One, in the form of the youth Mañjuśrī, spoke these words:

> "Devas and others, fear not! Yakṣas, rākṣasas,
> And all the asura hosts: I proclaim the samaya
> That none can transgress. Humans, non-humans, spirits,
> And all the bhūtas: generate minds of loving-kindness
> And be mindful of perfect enlightenment, the Dharma King,
> The honored one with two perfect feet, the Lion of the Śākyas, the benevolent king.
>
> "That Buddha proclaimed true words:
> Buddhôṣṇīṣa, Buddha-eye, and others,
> The wheels within the three realms,
> The Light-Cluster Supreme Crown,
> The Supreme Crown True Word.
>
> "Lotus-Handed Avalokiteśvara,
> The holy sovereign Avalokiteśvara,
> Bhṛkuṭī, Tārā,
> The renowned vidyā-queen,
> White-Robed and Great White,
> Hayagrīva of awesome virtue,
> The vidyārāja of the Lotus Family,
> Flowing forth from the Supreme Buddha.
>
> "The One-Syllable Cakravartin,
> Sovereign among true words:
> Be mindful of the deva among devas,
> The vidyā-lord of great fierce power,
> The great wrathful Yamāntaka.
> Be mindful also of Avalokiteśvara,
> Who from the heart gives rise to compassion.
> The former buddhas proclaimed
> Tārā who rescues sentient beings.
>
> "As Avalokiteśvara declared,
> Abiding in the samādhi of compassion,
> The holy one manifests a female form:
> This is a bodhisattva
> Practicing the conduct of bodhi.
> Throughout a thousand worlds
> And in countless buddha-fields,
> She journeys through all sufferings and hardships,
> Delivering sentient beings,
> Bearing a wondrous, beautiful female appearance
> To subdue and guide all into compliance,
> Establishing them in the vehicle of bodhi.
>
> "For the sake of all bodhisattvas,
> Be mindful of Vajrapāṇi,
> The diligent and heroic true-word king.
> The bearer of the family, Māmakī,
> Worthy of offerings in the three realms.
> Hook, chain, Vikhara,
> Vajra-fist of renown,
> Wrathful Wondrous-Moon,
> Blue-staff terrifying form.
> Such messenger-hosts,
> Wrathful ones named heavenly teachers,
> Supreme of the Vajra Family:
> If one is mindful of them, they grant protection.
>
> "The world's noble elephant,
> The bodhisattva of great awesome virtue,
> Mahāsthāmaprāpta Bodhisattva,
> The eldest son of Great Awesome Virtue,
> Samantabhadra of dignified beauty:
> If at that time one calls them to mind,
> One is freed from all fears.
>
> "One should also constantly be mindful of
> Ratnabhadra, the great sovereign,
> The supreme yakṣa king,
> The śrāvakas and pratyekabuddhas:
> All are freed from fears.
> If one makes offerings to them,
> One obtains great empowered protection.
> The Vast-Fruit and Merit-Born,
> The devas of form and formless realms,
> Those of great awesome virtue who have left desire,
> Those who make offerings to the Three Treasures,
> Those of pure faith in the Buddha's teaching:
> All of these are without possessions.
> The fear of friend and foe alike
> Is called the samaya.
>
> "The true words are difficult to transgress.
> This wrathful one is supreme among them.
> What Yamāntaka has proclaimed
> Universally protects all sentient beings.
> All the hosts of devas and humans
> Are filled with joy.
> Each abides in their original vow,
> And the sons of the Buddha all offer praise.
>
> "Having heard these commanding words,
> The yakṣa lord in wrath
> At that time caused to tremble
> Countless thousands of worlds.
> The wrathful king brings no benefit;
> Why then, sons of the Buddha,
> Have you already proclaimed him?
> The wrathful king's awesome virtue
> Subdues violent sentient beings.
> In the great assembly he
> Proclaims with a loud voice.
>
> "Having spoken, Vajrapāṇi
> Cast down the vajra pestle.
> Then the Great Awesome Virtue One,
> The lord of wondrous sound and wisdom,
> In the form of a youth, mocked and said:
> 'Do not be alarmed, great yakṣa!
> Vajrapāṇi of awesome virtue!
> This holy wrathful king of great sovereign power
> That I have proclaimed,
> I now bestow upon you.
> Expound it freely as you wish.
> Can you fully describe
> The wrathful king's awesome virtue?
> This is what you uphold and maintain,
> As if it manifests within your body.
> You can summon and gather them all,
> With heart-vidyā make inquiry and response,
> But you cannot manifest transformations.
> The wrathful āveśa:
> I permit you to expound it broadly.
> But those who abandon the samaya,
> Who do not bathe, who indulge in sleep,
> Who follow desire,
> Who apply oil and crave adornment,
> Whose minds are constantly wrathful,
> Who abandon the supreme true words,
> Who lack pure faith in the Buddha's teaching,
> Who doubt the true dharma,
> Who perform evil deeds without pure faith,
> Who slander the sublime Dharma and Saṃgha jewels,
> Whose minds are distracted and defiled,
> Who pollute the Three Treasures,
> Who dwell in impure realms,
> Who do not keep secret the true-word teaching,
> Who are constantly impure and without compassion,
> Who in celestial halls, temples, and practice-halls
> Perform defiling acts:
> All such classes of beings
> The wrathful one utterly destroys.
>
> "'Those who break the samaya precepts,
> Who do not know the corresponding vidyā,
> Even for the slightest deficiency in practice,
> The wrathful one disciplines and punishes.
> In all places among humans
> One cannot be exempt from heedlessness.
> If through a heedless, attached mind
> One damages the samaya,
> The wrathful king seizes the opportunity.
> Foolish people in all places
> Follow heedlessness.
> Those who practice true words
> Should leave desire and attain liberation,
> Like śrāvakas and pratyekabuddhas.
> The wrathful king can compel them;
> Mañjuśrī's compassionate mind.'
>
> "Having spoken thus,
> The inconceivable deeds of all buddhas
> And the great awesome power of the bodhisattvas
> Having been thus proclaimed,
> He abided in silent stillness."

At that time, the auspicious Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva of great awesome virtue, having received this authorization, leaped with joy and again reached out his hand to grasp the vajra pestle.
