# 智炬陀羅尼經
## Jñānolkā Dhāraṇī Sūtra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1397

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Devaprajñā (提雲般若) and others.

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was in the Palace of the Sun and Moon, together with an immeasurable assembly of great bodhisattvas: Samantabhadra Bodhisattva, Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva, Dhāraṇī-Sovereign-King Bodhisattva, Vajradhara Bodhisattva, and such bodhisattva mahāsattvas at their head.

At that time, in the east there was a buddha named Jñānolkā Tathāgata. In the south there was a buddha named Suvarṇa-Raśmi-Kūṭa Tathāgata. In the west there was a buddha named Satya-Vacana Tathāgata. In the north there was a buddha named Megha-Garjita-Rāja Tathāgata. Each came from his own land to this assembly.

At that time the World-Honored One, together with all the tathāgatas and the assembly of bodhisattvas, was in the Golden-Banner Pavilion, each seated upon a jewel-adorned lion throne. Then Sūrya and Candra, the celestial princes, came to where the Buddha was, bowed their heads in reverence to all the buddhas and bodhisattvas, and offered various exquisite objects in worship. When the offerings were complete, they withdrew to one side, and addressed each other: "How can we obtain from the tathāgatas and bodhisattvas the Jñānolkā Dhāraṇī that ignites the light of all sentient beings, destroys all darkness, and fills the ten directions with wisdom? Through the power of this dhāraṇī, may we be able to serve as a great torch of illumination for sentient beings." When they had spoken these words, all the tathāgatas and the assembly of bodhisattvas spoke the mantra together in unison:

*Sāya-te daṃ cakṣu-da-thā cakṣu prabhā-tu rā-mi-ta kāra-ta i-tī-daṃ-sa su-rā-ṭa su-rā-ṭhā su-tā-sa i-daṃ-sa be-rā be-rā pā-ṇi cā-ru mu-ta-ni ā-ra-ṇi ā-ra-ṇi kāra-pā-ṇi kāra-pā-ṇi tu-ru-ṭu-si tu-ru-ṭu-si thā-su-ti thā-su-ti dhi-ṇi dhi-ṇi thu-ru thu-ru thu-rā thu-rā kā-rā kā-rā si-thā-sa si-thā-sa gi-rā gi-rā gi-rā pā-ya tu-su tu-su sun-tu sun-bu si-thā su an-ta su e-thā su i-thā-sa pā-ṇi i-su-ṇi ti-su-ṇi ka-rā ka-rā ki-ṇi ki-ṇi ku-ru ku-ru ku-ma ku-ma ka-rma ka-rma ka-rma-pā-ṇi ke-ru ke-ru ke-rā-pā-ṇi kā-ka-ṇi kā-ka-ṇi rā-ru bu-ti tu-ru-ti mahā-tu-ru-ti ka-rā ka-rā ki-ki bi-ju-si bi-ju-si ṭhā-su ṭhā-su hā-su hā-su hā-sa-pā-ṇi svāhā.*

*Tadyathā: tu-nā-ciṃ mahā-tu-nā-ciṃ su-ru su-ru svāhā. Śuklā viśuddha-ni trā trā svāhā. Śuddhi-pra-ti-bhī tu-ru tu-ru svāhā. Padma-māri-ṇi sattva-hṛta bodhi hu-ru hu-ru svāhā. Sattva-bodhi sattva-lokā-ni tu-ru tu-ru svāhā. Lakṣaṇa acite tumaṃ parihāṇi ku-ru ku-ru svāhā. Dhāra dhāra mahā-dhāra dhāra dhāra-dhara yantī-ye svāhā. Subhrate su-dabhī aprati-hata bodhi dhāra dhāra dhāra-yantī-ye svāhā. Namo jñānolkāya tathāgatāya. Namo subharaṇa-prabhā-kūṭa-nibhāsāya tathāgatāya. Namo sattvā-dhi-nā tathāgatāya. Namo viṣma-svara-garjitodaraśībyāsa tathāgatebhyaḥ. Siddhantu mantra-padāni svāhā.*

At that time Samantabhadra Bodhisattva addressed Sūrya and Candra: "Celestial princes! This dhāraṇī has been proclaimed by eighty-eight koṭis of buddhas and tathāgatas for the benefit of all sentient beings. Celestial princes! The udumbara flower may yet easily appear, but the appearance of this dhāraṇī is exceedingly rare. Celestial princes! One may yet encounter a buddha's appearance in the world, but to encounter this dhāraṇī is even more difficult. This dhāraṇī may yet be obtained, but to find one who recites and upholds it is rarer still.

"Celestial princes! If one wishes to rescue and deliver those who have committed the five rebellious acts, who have slandered the true Dharma, and who are destined to suffer in the Avīci Hell, one should go to a secluded place, coat the ground with clean plaster, and offer various incense and flowers according to one's ability. For three sets of seven days, six times daily, day and night, one should recite this dhāraṇī mantra. Through the majestic spiritual power of this dhāraṇī, the Avīci Hell will immediately be shattered into hundreds of thousands of pieces, and the sentient beings within it will be liberated at once. How much more so for a person in the human realm who hears it! Know that such a person is already being protected and remembered by all the buddhas and by us bodhisattvas as well. Celestial princes! Do not give rise to doubt concerning what has been taught here."

When the Buddha had spoken this sūtra, the celestial hosts of Sūrya and Candra joyfully received it and put it into practice.
