# 無能勝大明心陀羅尼經
## Sūtra of the Great Vidyā Heart Dhāraṇī of Aparājita

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1235

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmadeva (法天).

---

> I take refuge in the Supreme Awakened One,
> The Dharma, and the assembly of holy worthies.
> The great vidyā heart true word
> Was proclaimed by Aparājita herself.

The Tathāgata, Arhat, Perfectly Awakened One also proclaimed this vidyā for the benefit of all sentient beings, to accomplish all auspicious deeds, to eliminate grave offenses and prevent untimely death, to extend the lifespan, and to keep far from all illnesses.

If there are good men, good women, boys, or girls who revere and honor this sūtra, then all humans and non-humans who harbor evil minds, rākṣasas, ostārakas, hungry ghosts, skandas, apasmāras, those who practice the arts of Ucchuṣma-mātra, bhūta-specters, and all such evil beings who perform sorcery, who curse sentient beings and cause various calamities, who produce hunger, disease, and all manner of evil illnesses, boils, carbuncles, swellings, scabies, leprosy, and warts: all such sufferings will be unable to cause harm.

If there are karmic misfortunes from past lives, or mutual killing, litigation, quarrels, and disputes, or imprisonment by royal law, or dangers from flood, fire, or bandits: when such difficulties oppress and torment, if one recollects and recites this dhāraṇī, they will certainly be dispersed. One will be freed from calamity and harm and will enjoy auspicious peace and happiness.

If there are people who write this vidyā on birch bark, silk, or paper, or write and make offerings to it in other places, or wear it upon the crown of the head, upon the neck, upon the arm, upon the hand, or carry it within the garments, or bind it into a garland and wear it in the topknot: by thus receiving and upholding it, one will forever be free from calamity and will constantly enjoy peace and happiness.

If one constantly, at early dawn, with body and mind pure and clean, recites it with utmost sincerity, then after death, for seven lifetimes one will obtain knowledge of past lives, a dignified and beautiful physical form, constant happiness, and complete observance of the precepts. From one's mouth will constantly issue the fragrance of utpala flowers. One's words will be truthful and sincere, and all people will love and respect one. One will further be reborn in the Heaven of the Thirty-Three. If one contemplates this true word in accordance with the dharma, accomplishment will certainly be attained.
