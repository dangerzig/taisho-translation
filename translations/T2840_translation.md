# 三界圖
## Diagram of the Three Realms

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 2840

Translated from the Chinese. Dynasty unknown; translator unknown (失譯)

---

In the desire realm (kāmadhātu), there are thirty-two defilements (kleśa) abandoned through seeing (darśana-heya) and four abandoned through cultivation (bhāvanā-heya).

In the form realm (rūpadhātu), there are twenty-eight abandoned through seeing and three through cultivation.

The formless realm (ārūpyadhātu) is likewise. Together, the three realms total ninety-eight.

This refers to the differentiation of sixteen aspects under the Four Noble Truths (catvāry āryasatyāni), distinguished by their opposition and established according to their truth. The sixteen aspects are the four mental aspects (ākāra) under each of the Four Noble Truths.

The four aspects of the truth of suffering (duḥkhasatya) are: impermanence (anitya), suffering (duḥkha), emptiness (śūnya), and non-self (anātman). The four aspects of the truth of origination (samudayasatya) are: cause (hetu), origination (samudaya), arising (prabhava), and condition (pratyaya). The four aspects of the truth of cessation (nirodhasatya) are: cessation (nirodha), pacification (śānta), excellence (praṇīta), and liberation (niḥsaraṇa). The four aspects of the truth of the path (mārgasatya) are: path (mārga), suchness (nyāya), practice (pratipad), and deliverance (nairyāṇika).

The commentary explains: Because there is arising and cessation, [conditioned phenomena are] impermanent (anitya). Because of their oppressive nature, [they are] suffering (duḥkha). Because they contradict the view of what belongs to self (ātmīyadṛṣṭi), [they are] empty (śūnya). Because they contradict the view of self (ātmadṛṣṭi), [they are] non-self (anātman). Because it produces dharmas, [it is] cause (hetu). Because there are manifold kinds, [it is] origination (samudaya). Because it perpetually generates, [it is] arising (prabhava). Because each [factor] individually assists, [it is] condition (pratyaya). Because all aggregates (skandha) are exhausted, [it is] cessation (nirodha). Because the three marks [of conditioned existence] are stilled, [it is] pacification (śānta). Because there are no multitudinous afflictions, [it is] excellence (praṇīta). Because one is freed from multitudinous calamities, [it is] liberation (niḥsaraṇa). Because it has the meaning of universal passage, [it is] path (mārga). Because it accords with correct principle, [it is] suchness (nyāya). Because it rightly advances toward [the goal], [it is] practice (pratipad). Because it is able to transcend forever, [it is] deliverance (nairyāṇika).

Regarding the truth of suffering, there are four wisdoms: (1) patient acceptance of the dharma of suffering (duḥkha-dharma-kṣānti), (2) wisdom of the dharma of suffering (duḥkha-dharma-jñāna), (3) patient acceptance of the analogue of suffering (duḥkha-anvaya-kṣānti), and (4) wisdom of the analogue of suffering (duḥkha-anvaya-jñāna). These counteract ten defilements, including the view of the body (satkāyadṛṣṭi). The remaining three truths are likewise.

The patient acceptance of the dharma of origination (samudaya-dharma-kṣānti), the wisdom of the dharma of origination (samudaya-dharma-jñāna), the patient acceptance of the analogue of origination (samudaya-anvaya-kṣānti), and the wisdom of the analogue of origination (samudaya-anvaya-jñāna) counteract seven latent tendencies (anuśaya), including wrong view (mithyādṛṣṭi).

The patient acceptance of the dharma of cessation (nirodha-dharma-kṣānti), the wisdom of the dharma of cessation (nirodha-dharma-jñāna), the patient acceptance of the analogue of cessation (nirodha-anvaya-kṣānti), and the wisdom of the analogue of cessation (nirodha-anvaya-jñāna) counteract the defilements pertaining to the truth of cessation.

The patient acceptance of the dharma of the path (mārga-dharma-kṣānti), the wisdom of the dharma of the path (mārga-dharma-jñāna), the patient acceptance of the analogue of the path (mārga-anvaya-kṣānti), and the wisdom of the analogue of the path (mārga-anvaya-jñāna) counteract the defilements pertaining to the truth of the path.

Regarding those [defilements] abandoned through cultivation (bhāvanā-heya): upon each of the Four Noble Truths, the analogical patient acceptance (比忍, anvaya-kṣānti) and analogical wisdom (比智, anvaya-jñāna) counteract the four defilements of the desire realm: greed (lobha), aversion (dveṣa), pride (māna), and ignorance (avidyā).
