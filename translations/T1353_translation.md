# 東方最勝燈王陀羅尼經
## Dhāraṇī Sūtra of the Supreme Lamp-King of the East

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1353

Translated from the Chinese. Sui dynasty, translated by Jñānagupta (闍那崛多).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was in the country of Śrāvastī, in the Jeta Grove, at Anāthapiṇḍada's monastery, together with a great assembly of one thousand two hundred and fifty bhikṣus, and a great assembly of forty thousand bodhisattvas. The lord of the sahā world, Brahmā, Śakra Devānām Indra, and the four great heavenly kings (Dhṛtarāṣṭra, Virūḍhaka, Virūpākṣa, and Vaiśravaṇa) were present, together with the generals of the twenty-eight classes of ghosts and spirits. Eighty-four thousand hāritī devas with their children and retinues also came to join the assembly.

At that time, beyond this buddha-world, past one hundred thousand koṭi buddha-lands, there was a world named Flowers in Abundance. The buddha of that world was named Supreme Lamp-King Tathāgata, Samyak-saṃbuddha, who is presently there teaching the Dharma. He dispatched two bodhisattvas, one named Mahāprabhā and the other Amitābha, and told them: "Good men! Take these dhāraṇī phrases to the sahā world and give them to Śākyamuni Buddha. This mantra greatly benefits beings, enabling sentient beings to be at peace throughout the long night, obtaining great wholesome benefit, physical beauty, strength, and fame." He then spoke the mantra:

*Re mahā-re rāṇi akhi-makhi samanthi mahā-samanthi sāyi so-laṃ-mi svāhā.*

The two bodhisattvas received and upheld these dhāraṇī phrases from the Buddha, and in the time it takes a strong man to bend and extend his arm, they vanished from the Flowers in Abundance land and arrived in the country of Śrāvastī, in the Jeta Grove, at Anāthapiṇḍada's monastery. The two bodhisattvas bowed at the Buddha's feet, stood to one side, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! Beyond this buddha-world, past one hundred thousand koṭi buddha-lands, there is a world named Flowers in Abundance. The buddha of that world is named Supreme Lamp-King Tathāgata, Samyak-saṃbuddha, who is presently teaching the Dharma. He sent us here to inquire after the World-Honored One's health, to ask whether you are free from illness and affliction, and to ensure that your disciples are not being troubled or frightened by any māras, ghosts, devas, nāgas, yakṣas, bhūtas, pūtanas, piśācas, hungry ghosts, dried-up-corpse ghosts, madness-causing ghosts, mind-confusing ghosts, road-blocking ghosts, corpse-raising ghosts, disease-bringing ghosts of one, two, three, or four days, the sun, moon, or constellations, lions, tigers, leopards, poisonous snakes, wolves, dogs, bats, humans, or non-humans. Such disturbances are impossible here."

"Ānanda! If any good man or good woman receives, upholds, reads, recites, copies, explains, and widely disseminates these dhāraṇī phrases, fire cannot burn them, they cannot drown in water, blades cannot wound them, poison cannot harm them, the king cannot execute them, Brahmā and the other devas will not despise them, and they will be able to remember the events of ten past lives. Ānanda! This mantra was spoken by seventy-seven koṭi buddhas. If anyone violates this mantra or robs the vital energy of one who upholds it, they will have violated and acted contrary to all those buddhas."

Then Maitreya Bodhisattva rose from his seat, arranged his robes, bared his right shoulder, placed his right knee on the ground, joined his palms, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I too now wish to speak dhāraṇī phrases for the benefit of sentient beings, so that they may obtain wholesome benefit, physical beauty, strength, and fame." He then spoke the mantra:

*Abha-na ku-na a-nu-bhati bhāra-ti-bha-ti dūru-mi rū-ru-mi anā-ka-śi pa-nā-ka-śi kā-śi mahā-kā-śi a-nu-ta parā-ṇu-ta kha-khi mahā-kha-khi si-dha-ti ri-ta dan-ti ti-ri mi-ri ci-ri śi-ri hū-mi mahā-hū-mi hū-hū-mi hū-śā-bhati svāhā.*

Then the Buddha further addressed Ānanda: "You should receive, uphold, read, recite, copy, explain, and widely disseminate these dhāraṇī phrases. Why? Ānanda! This mantra enables one to remember the events of thirteen past lives. Ānanda! This mantra was spoken by eighty-four koṭi buddhas. If anyone violates this mantra or robs the vital energy of one who upholds it, they will have violated and acted contrary to all those buddhas."

Then Mañjuśrī, prince of the Dharma, Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, rose from his seat, arranged his robes, bared his right shoulder, placed his right knee on the ground, joined his palms, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I too now wish to speak dhāraṇī phrases, wishing to benefit sentient beings, so that they may obtain wholesome benefit, physical beauty, strength, and fame." He then spoke the mantra:

*Akṣa-ki sa-ki nā-gi nā-gi ni-nā-gi sā-re bha-na ni-ri-ha-gi ha-ra-bha-ti dhyān-de-gi dhyān-ti-ra-bha-ti svāhā.*

Then the Buddha further addressed Ānanda: "You should receive, uphold, read, recite, copy, explain, and widely disseminate these dhāraṇī phrases. Why? Ānanda! This mantra enables one to remember the events of thirteen past lives. Ānanda! This mantra was spoken jointly by ninety-nine koṭi buddhas of the past. If anyone violates this mantra or robs the vital energy of one who upholds it, they will have violated all those buddhas."

Then Śākyamuni Buddha addressed the great assembly: "I too now wish to speak dhāraṇī phrases for the benefit of sentient beings, so that they may obtain wholesome benefit, physical beauty, strength, and fame." He then spoke the mantra:

*Abha-na ku-na dan-gi ko-dan-gi rā-lu-re rū-ru-re ki-re bi-re a-re svāhā.*

Then the Buddha further addressed Ānanda: "You should receive, uphold, read, recite, copy, explain, and widely disseminate these dhāraṇī phrases. Why? Ānanda! This mantra was spoken by the buddhas of the three times. It universally eliminates all calamities, demonic affairs, and bonds, protecting so-and-so and enabling them to be at peace. Whether for poisonous diseases, wind and cold diseases, or diseases of entanglement, whether for pain in the heart, belly, or spine, pain in the eyes, ears, or body, or headache: for all such afflictions, the Tathāgata Supreme Lamp-King sent these dhāraṇī phrases to the World-Honored One, also wishing to enable sentient beings of this sahā world to be at peace throughout the long night, obtaining great wholesome benefit, physical beauty, strength, and fame." The mantra is as spoken above.

At that time the four heavenly kings, by the Buddha's divine power, ensured that life would not end prematurely. The Buddha addressed Ānanda: "If you enter a place of bandits, you should recite this mantra. If you encounter water or fire or wish to go to the royal palace, you should also recite this mantra. If there are any places of quarrel, conflict, illness, or fear, you should also remember this mantra. Whether there are kings, bandits, water, fire, devas, nāgas, yakṣas, bhūtas, pūtanas, piśācas, hungry ghosts, dried-up-corpse ghosts, madness-causing ghosts, mind-confusing ghosts, road-blocking ghosts, corpse-raising ghosts, vitality-draining ghosts, disease-bringing ghosts of one, two, three, or four days, the sun, moon, or constellations, lions, tigers, leopards, poisonous snakes, wolves, dogs, bats, humans, or non-humans that come to cause harm or fright, such a thing is impossible.

"Ānanda! If any bhikṣu, bhikṣuṇī, good man, or good woman receives, upholds, reads, recites, copies, explains, and widely disseminates these dhāraṇī phrases, fire cannot burn them, they cannot drown in water, blades cannot wound them, poison cannot harm them, and they will not be harmed by road-blocking spirits. If one ties this mantra-empowered cord upon a withered tree and prays for compassionate rescue and protection, the tree will bloom and bear fruit again. How much more so for human beings! The only exception is fixed karmic retribution from past deeds. Ānanda! If one recites and practices at the three times of dawn, midday, and dusk without interruption, heavy sins will become light. One who deserves the death penalty will be released with a caning. One who deserves caning will be released with a rebuke. Whatever one seeks, one will obtain the desired result, by the power of this divine mantra and through the protection and mindfulness of all the buddhas and sages."

Then the four great heavenly kings rose from their seats, arranged their robes, bared their right shoulders, placed their right knees on the ground, joined their palms, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! We too now wish to speak dhāraṇī phrases for the benefit of sentient beings, so that they may obtain wholesome benefit, physical beauty, strength, and fame. If any bhikṣu, bhikṣuṇī, upāsaka, or upāsikā receives, upholds, reads, recites, copies, explains, and widely disseminates these dhāraṇī phrases, no evil person or non-human will be able to gain any advantage over them." They then spoke the mantra:

*Tathā-ta danta danta-ñe bi-danta-ñe tata-daya pāṇi-dān hu-ñe kwo-kaṭ-ni kyū-ka-ni kyū-ka-ni dhan-māre chan-māre svāhā.*

Then the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> This dhāraṇī sūtra
> Was spoken by all the buddhas before.
> I now also proclaim and expound it
> For the benefit of all sentient beings.
>
> If a person with a mind of joyful faith
> Hears and upholds this mantra,
> Their eloquence will increase.
> In every place they go,
> They will not encounter calamity or adversity.
> No human or non-human,
> No kumbhāṇḍa or pūtana
> Will be able to cause them harm,
> Because they recite and uphold this mantra.
>
> They will not be killed by others,
> Because they recite and uphold this mantra.
> Māra Pāpīyas, king of the heavenly demons,
> The preeminent lord of the desire realm,
> Will be unable to create obstacles,
> Because they recite and uphold this sūtra.
>
> Their parents, brothers and sisters,
> Relatives, and dear ones
> Will not encounter decline or calamity,
> Because they recite and uphold this sūtra.
>
> Through hundreds of thousands of koṭi kalpas,
> Whatever unwholesome karma was committed,
> The ten evils and the five acts of immediate retribution,
> In seven days all will be eliminated,
> Because they recite and uphold this mantra.
>
> The merit that bodhisattvas
> Accumulate over koṭi kalpas
> Can be surpassed in seven days
> Because they recite and uphold this mantra.
>
> The honored four great heavenly kings,
> Virūpākṣa, Dhṛtarāṣṭra,
> Virūḍhaka, Vaiśravaṇa,
> With their ministers and retinues,
> Will joyfully praise
> And reverently guard and protect them always,
> Because they recite and uphold this mantra.

When the Buddha had spoken this sūtra, Śāriputra and all the bhikṣus, and all beings of the world including devas, humans, asuras, and the rest, having heard what the Buddha taught, all greatly rejoiced, received it with reverence, put it into practice, bowed, and departed.

## Dhāraṇī for Requesting Dreams to Know Good and Ill Fortune

*Namo the Three Jewels. Namo Maṇibhadra, the great ghost-spirit general. Mahā-taṭa-dhāraṇī ni-ru-shi-na biṃ-ga-ri a-ri-ga-ri kaṃ-ta biṃ-ga-ri a-bha-ta-jā a-bha-ta-jā Maṇibhadra-liṃ mahā-gandhu-pa gandha.*

The four heavenly kings: Dhṛtarāṣṭra, Virūḍhaka, Viśramaṇa, Vaiśravaṇa. Trāyastriṃśa Heaven, Yāma Heaven, Tuṣita Heaven, Nirmāṇarati Heaven, Paranirmitavaśavartin Heaven, Māra's Transformation-Freedom Heaven. The Brahmā-Assemblage Wondrous Heaven, Brahmā-Ministers' Fortune Heaven, Mahābrahmā Heaven, Water-Practice Brahmā Heaven, Water-Subtle Brahmā Heaven, Water-Immeasurable Brahmā Heaven. Water-Responsive Grove Heaven, Moderate Purity Heaven, Universal Purity Brightness Heaven, Guarding-the-Wondrous Heaven, Subtle-Wondrous Heaven, Ultimate-Wondrous Heaven, Merit-Beloved-Victory Heaven, Near-the-Boundary Heaven, Universal Survey Heaven, Joyful-Vision Heaven, No-Bound-Reception Heaven, Akaniṣṭha Heaven, Pure-Radiance Heaven, Universal-Equality Heaven. These are the thirty-three.

*Ha-ni ha-ni ha-ni kha-ha-ni kha-du kha-ni kha-ci kha-ci kha.*

Empower water with the mantra and give it to the person to drink.

*Akati parā-kati parā-saṃ-kati bhāra-vīrā-yanti parā-parā-ma-mūja-ma-mūja-rāti saṃ-parā-jā-rāti bi-ti-ti hī-hī nī-nī u-kure mū-ku-dūti danā-kati danā-rāti danā-saṃ-tāra i-pa-ha. Mahā-yo bha-ghṛ bhāri mahā-bhāri jāri mahā-jāri gau-mū-ha sabhati mahā-sabhati apāṭa saṃ-kiṃ-ṭa dhāraṇī tata-ha agha-jāni bha-bhi sarva-dharmani-bha-bhi iśā-madha-bi-bha-śana vi-māla-śu-pari dharma-ni khāna bhāru-ni vanya-ta māre cāre huru-huru śau-bhi-ti mantra-mantra svāhā.*

*Ci-pa-cu ci-pa-cu hunu-pa-cu hunu-pa-cu pūru-pa-cu pūru-pa-cu ci-pa-cu ci-pa-cu ajña-pa-cu dūhuna-pa-cu śamānu-pa-cu huśuti-pa-cu migi-huna-pa-cu i-hu-hina-pa-cu nīrīgi-ti-tina-pa-cu bhāja-ti-na-pa-cu miyānu-hu-ti-na-pa-cu uśyochū-ti-na-pa-cu svāhā.*

## Mantra for Turning the Bowl

*Namo mātiṃ-gi-na, namo mātiṃ arā-ja-ra. Namo mā-cai-ga ga-ri ga-ta-ri ga-na tatśa-na mā-sikri-dhu iṃ-mā bi-bhara bhakṣa-me saṃ-bi-jā saṃ-bi-jāti aṭa-ni bha-ṭa-ni bha-manda aru-śu dū-ṭa saṃ-ga-bhi-me a-sita-ta-ri aṃ-ku-ṣṭa pa-śān-ti ta-ri-ga da-ri-ga na-ti-bhi-na-caku-sa. Buddha-satyan, dharma-satyan, saṃgha-satyan, pratyekabuddha-satyan, arhan-satyan, deva-satyan, ṛṣi-satyan. Pa-śān-ti ta-ri-ga ta-ri-ga na-ti-bhi-na-caku-sa svāhā.*

## Mantra for Empowering a Mirror (Requires a Boy)

If one sees a monastic of dignified deportment or a foreigner, this is entirely auspicious. If one sees a monastic of ugly appearance, or sees a person weeping and wearing tattered garments, this is an inauspicious sign.
