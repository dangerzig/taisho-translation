# 佛說馬有三相經
## Sūtra Spoken by the Buddha on the Three Marks of a Horse

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 114

Translated from the Chinese. Eastern Han, translated by Zhī Yào (支曜)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the country of Śrāvastī, in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍada's Park. The Buddha then addressed the bhikṣus:

"A fine horse has three marks. When it enters royal service, it can serve as a mount and please the king's mind, and it earns the name of a royal horse. What are the three marks? First, a fine horse has good spirit and can run on its own; second, it has strength; third, it has an upright form and fine color. These are the three marks by which a fine horse enters royal service.

"A good person also has three marks. Having attained a good mind, one's fame is illustrious; all people respect and hold one in esteem; one can receive the reverence of others and bring blessings to all under heaven. What are the three marks? A good person with an attained mind can run, has strength, and has an upright appearance.

"What is meant by a good person with an attained mind being able to run? A good person with an attained mind truly knows 'this is suffering,' knows 'this is the origin,' knows 'this is the cessation,' and recognizes 'this is the path.' In this way, the good person with an attained mind runs.

"What is meant by a good person with an attained mind having strength? Whatever harmful and evil tendencies exist, one should cut them off, seeking this with full effort. Striving diligently, one's mind abandons evil. Harmful evil tendencies not yet arisen one prevents from arising. Wholesome intentions not yet arisen one brings forth. Wholesome intentions already arisen one maintains, without forgetting or diminishing, gradually increasing them. In conduct and mind alike, one pursues wholesome action with full effort; one controls the mind and abandons evil. In this way, the good person attains strength.

"What is meant by a good person with an attained mind having an upright appearance? In this world, a good person with an attained mind avoids the five sensual pleasures and avoids harmful evils, and is able to attain the four dhyānas and practice them. In this way, the good person with an attained mind has a fine appearance.

"These are the three causal conditions for genuine practice. The good person with an attained mind: their fame spreads, they become a leader whom all people hold in esteem, and they can receive the reverence of all under heaven. It is thus."

The Buddha spoke thus.
