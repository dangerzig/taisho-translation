# 佛說佛頂尊勝陀羅尼經

## The Buddhōṣṇīṣa Vijaya Dhāraṇī Sūtra Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0971

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Yìjìng (義淨).

---

Thus have I heard. At one time the Bhagavān was in the city of Śrāvastī, in the Jeta Grove, in Anāthapiṇḍada's park, together with a great assembly of twelve hundred fifty bhikṣus, and twelve thousand bodhisattva mahāsattvas.

At that time, in the Hall of Good Dharma of the Thirty-Three Heavens, there was a deva named Supratiṣṭhita. Together with the great devas and deva maidens surrounding him in front and behind, he enjoyed supreme honor and happiness, delighting in various celestial pleasures, mutually entertaining one another in every kind of bliss.

Then, during the night, Supratiṣṭhita heard a voice saying: "Supratiṣṭhita! In seven days you will certainly die. After passing from here, you will be reborn in Jambudvīpa and undergo seven successive births in animal form, followed by the sufferings of hell. After emerging from hell, you may with difficulty obtain a human body, but you will be born in a poor and lowly family, and while still in your mother's womb you will lack both eyes."

When Supratiṣṭhita heard this voice, every hair on his body stood on end. He was filled with great terror, sorrow, and despair, panic-stricken and without any plan. He immediately rushed to the palace of Śakra, Lord of the Devas, crying out in grief. Having prostrated at the feet of the Lord of the Devas, he said: "May the Lord of the Devas hear what I have to say. While I was surrounded by deva maidens enjoying bliss, I heard a voice saying: 'Supratiṣṭhita! In seven days your life will end. After death, you will be reborn in Jambudvīpa and undergo seven successive births in animal form, followed by the sufferings of hell. After emerging from hell, you may with difficulty obtain a human body, but you will be born in a poor and lowly family, and while still in your mother's womb you will lack both eyes.' Hearing these words, I am panic-stricken and without any plan. How can I be freed from such suffering?"

At that time, when Śakra, Lord of the Devas, heard the words of Supratiṣṭhita, he was greatly alarmed and astonished. He reflected carefully: "What seven successive evil-destiny bodies will this Supratiṣṭhita receive?" After sitting quietly for a moment, he concentrated his mind and observed clearly. He saw that Supratiṣṭhita would receive seven successive evil-destiny bodies, namely: pig, dog, jackal, monkey, python, crow, and vulture, eating various foul and impure substances.

When Śakra saw that Supratiṣṭhita would fall into seven successive evil-destiny bodies, he was deeply moved by compassion, filled with sorrow and anguish, the pain piercing his body and mind. He thought: "What plan is there? To whom can I turn for refuge? Who can shelter and protect him, freeing him from this suffering?" Then he reflected: "Only the Tathāgata, the Worthy One, the Perfectly Enlightened One, the father of great compassion, can free Supratiṣṭhita from this suffering!"

Having reflected thus, during the first watch of the night, adorning himself with various flowers and celestial garments, carrying flowers and incense, Śakra went to the Jeta Grove, to the place of the World-Honored One. He reverently made offerings, prostrated at the Buddha's feet, circumambulated seven times, then knelt with palms joined and addressed the Buddha: "Supratiṣṭhita heard a voice saying: 'In seven days your life will end, and you will undergo seven successive births in evil animal destinies.' Hearing this, he is filled with deep sorrow and anguish, the pain piercing his heart. The World-Honored One, with great compassion, pity, and beneficence: how can he be freed from this suffering?"

At that time, when the World-Honored One heard these words, he smiled. From the crown of his head, he radiated light of many colors, illuminating the entire trichiliocosm, then returning to the Buddha's presence. When the Buddha World-Honored One speaks of past events, the light enters from behind. When he speaks of future events, the light enters from the chest. When he speaks of hell, the light enters from the soles of the feet. When he speaks of animals, the light enters from the heels. When he speaks of hungry ghosts, the light enters from the toes. When he speaks of human affairs, the light enters from the knees. When he speaks of a ruler of strength, the light enters from the left palm. When he speaks of a wheel-turning king, the light enters from the right palm. When he speaks of celestial affairs, the light enters from the navel. When he speaks of śrāvakas, the light enters from the mouth. When he speaks of pratyekabuddhas, the light enters from the space between the eyebrows. When he speaks of anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi, the light enters from the crown.

At that time, the light returned to the Buddha's presence, circumambulated the Buddha three times, and entered the Buddha's mouth.

Then the World-Honored One told Śakra: "Lord of the Devas! There is a dhāraṇī called 'Tathāgata Buddhōṣṇīṣa Vijaya,' which can purify the suffering of birth and death in all evil destinies, can purify and remove all the sufferings of hell, Yama's realm, and the animal realm, and can break all hell-bound karma and redirect beings to the good paths.

"Lord of the Devas! This Buddhōṣṇīṣa Vijaya Dhāraṇī is inconceivable and possesses great supernatural power. If any person hears it even once, all evil karma created in former lives will be entirely eliminated. They will obtain a pure and excellent body. Wherever they are born, they will remember and never forget. From one buddha-field to another, from one celestial realm to another, even traversing all the Thirty-Three Heavens, wherever they are born they will remember and never forget.

"Furthermore, Lord of the Devas! If a person is about to die and for even a moment calls to mind this dhāraṇī, their lifespan will be restored. Body, speech, and mind will be purified, and they will suffer no pain. According to their merit, they will enjoy complete peace. All tathāgatas will watch over them. All deva spirits will constantly attend and guard them. They will be respected by all people. Evil obstacles will be eliminated. All bodhisattvas will jointly shelter and protect them.

"If any man or woman reads, recites, and upholds this dhāraṇī even for a moment, know that all the sufferings of the three evil paths for that person will be destroyed and eliminated without any remainder. The pure lands of all buddhas and the palaces of all devas, and the profoundly deep practices and vows of all bodhisattvas: they may freely enter all of these without any obstruction."

At that time, Śakra listened single-mindedly with delight.

Then the Bhagavān, observing the thoughts in Śakra's mind, with a mind of great compassion spoke this dhāraṇī Dharma, reciting the mantra:

*Namo bhagavate trailokya-prativiśiṣṭāya buddhāya bhagavate tadyathā oṃ viśodhaya samanta-avabhāsa-spharaṇa-gati-gahana svabhāva-viśuddhe abhiṣiñcatu māṃ sugata-vara-vacana amṛta-abhiṣeke ahara ahara āyuḥ-sandhāraṇi śodhaya śodhaya gagana-viśuddhe uṣṇīṣa-vijaya-viśuddhe sahasra-raśmi-sañcodite sarva-tathāgata-adhiṣṭhāna-adhiṣṭhite mudre vajra-kāya-saṃhatana-viśuddhe sarva-āvaraṇa-viśuddhe pratinivartaya āyuḥ-śuddhe samaya-adhiṣṭhite maṇi maṇi mahā-maṇi tathāta-bhūta-koṭi-pariśuddhe visphuṭa-buddhi-śuddhe jaya jaya vijaya vijaya smara smara sarva-buddha-adhiṣṭhita-śuddhe vajre vajra-garbhe vajraṃ-bhavatu mama amukasya sarva-sattvānāṃ ca kāya-viśuddhe sarva-gati-pariśuddhe sarva-tathāgata-samāśvāsa-adhiṣṭhite buddha buddha buddhaya buddhaya samantu pariśuddhe sarva-tathāgatānām adhiṣṭhāna-adhiṣṭhite svāhā*

At that time, having spoken this dhāraṇī, the World-Honored One told Śakra: "This dhāraṇī-seal is called 'The Buddhōṣṇīṣa Vijaya Dhāraṇī that Purifies and Removes All Evil Destinies.' It can also eliminate all karmic obstacles from transgressions, and can purify all the sufferings of the foul evil paths.

"Lord of the Devas! This dhāraṇī was jointly proclaimed by eighty-eight Ganges-sand koṭis of hundreds of thousands of buddhas, who rejoiced in and upheld it, and sealed it with the wisdom seal of Mahāvairocana Tathāgata. It was spoken to break through the net of the three evil destinies for all sentient beings, to liberate all sentient beings in hell, the animal realm, and Yama's realm, so that beings oppressed by suffering and drowning in the ocean of birth and death may attain liberation. It was also spoken for the benefit of those with short lives, little merit, and no present protection, and for sentient beings who delight in creating mixed, defiled evil karma. This dhāraṇī abides with sustaining power in Jambudvīpa. Furthermore, beings of wrong views who do not believe in good and evil, who cycle through the three evil destinies in various transmigrations enduring the suffering of birth and death, beings of little merit who have lost the right path: all such beings without exception receive its benefit, and all are liberated from the heavy burden of suffering."

The Buddha told Śakra: "I now proclaim this dhāraṇī Dharma and entrust it to you. You should bestow it upon Supratiṣṭhita, causing him to uphold, recite, contemplate, cherish, revere, and make offerings to it, remembering and never forgetting. You should also cause it to be widely disseminated among all sentient beings in Jambudvīpa. The benefits of this dhāraṇī are immeasurable! It is also spoken for all deva-sons. This great dhāraṇī-seal I entrust to you. Lord of the Devas! You should remember and uphold it with right mindfulness, diligently guard and protect it, and never allow it to be lost.

"Furthermore, Lord of the Devas! If any person hears this dhāraṇī even for a moment, all the grave and obstructing evil karma accumulated over a thousand kalpas, for which they should receive the suffering of birth and death in hell, the hungry-ghost realm, the animal realm, Yama's realm, or bodies of asuras, yakṣas, ghosts, spirits, pūtanas, kaṭapūtanas, apasmāras, mosquitoes, gnats, turtles, dogs, pythons, and other such bodies, all kinds of birds, fierce beasts, and all sentient beings down to the body of an ant: they will never again receive such bodies but will immediately attain a transformed rebirth. They will be together in one place with all buddha world-honored ones and one-more-birth bodhisattvas, or be born in a great brāhmaṇa family, or in the kṣatriya class, or in the most eminent and noble families.

"Lord of the Devas! That such persons are born in such superior places is entirely because they heard this dhāraṇī. Wherever they are born, they will obtain a pure and excellent body. Lord of the Devas! Such rewards, even to the supreme place of awakening, all come from praising the supremely meritorious qualities of this dhāraṇī.

"Lord of the Devas! This dhāraṇī is called 'auspicious.' Why? Because it eliminates all three evil paths. This Buddhōṣṇīṣa Vijaya Dhāraṇī is like the sun-treasury maṇi jewel, pure and without flaw, like empty space, with radiant flames illuminating and penetrating everywhere without exception. If sentient beings uphold this dhāraṇī with a pure mind, they likewise become like fine gold, bright, pure, and supple, pleasing to behold, unstained by any defilement, like a lotus unstained by dust.

"Lord of the Devas! Those who hold this dhāraṇī are likewise. Borne by this pure karma, they will certainly be reborn in the good destinies. Lord of the Devas! Wherever this dhāraṇī abides, if one can write it, uphold it, listen to it, praise and recite it, revere and make offerings to it, all evil paths will be purified, and all the sufferings of hell will swiftly be eliminated.

"Lord of the Devas! If any man or woman writes this dhāraṇī and places it on a tall banner, or on a high mountain, or upon a tower, or even within a stūpa: Lord of the Devas! If there are bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, upāsikās, or men and women of good family who see such a banner from afar, or approach near to it and its shadow falls upon their body, or if the wind blows light dust from the banner bearing the dhāraṇī onto their body: Lord of the Devas! All the transgressions of such beings, all the evil karma by which they should fall into the evil destinies, the sufferings of hell, the hungry-ghost realm, the animal realm, Yama's realm, and asura bodies: all such suffering they will never endure, and they will never be stained by karmic defilement.

"Lord of the Devas! Such beings will all receive the prediction of all buddhas and will all become irreversible from anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi.

"Lord of the Devas! How much more so for those who further make offerings of various flower garlands, unguent incense, powdered incense, banners, streamers, flower canopies, garments, and jeweled necklaces, who perform various kinds of music and adornments, who at a crossroads build a stūpa, install the dhāraṇī within it, join palms in reverence, circumambulate it, and pay homage with bowed head! Lord of the Devas! Those who can make offerings in this way are called mahāsattvas, true children of the Buddha, pillars of the Dharma, and indeed are stūpas of the Tathāgata's complete-body relics."

At that time, King Yama, during the first watch of the night, came to the Buddha's presence. With various fine garments, various flowers, incense, unguent, powdered incense, sandalwood, and aloeswood, he reverently made offerings, circumambulated the Buddha seven times, and having prostrated at the Buddha's feet, said: "I have heard the Tathāgata expound this dhāraṇī Dharma of great power and praise its inconceivable merit of upholding. The World-Honored One, with great compassion, pities and benefits suffering sentient beings. World-Honored One! I shall constantly follow and protect them, not allowing those who uphold it to fall into hell, for they follow the Tathāgata's words, and I shall guard and remember them."

At that time, the four guardian deva kings circumambulated the Buddha three times, stood to one side, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! We only wish the Tathāgata would again explain the dhāraṇī Dharma in detail for us."

At that time, the World-Honored One told the four great deva kings: "Listen carefully! I shall explain for you the method of upholding this dhāraṇī, also for the sake of those beings with short lives. If one wishes to uphold it, one should first bathe in pure water and put on new, clean garments. From the first day of the bright half of the month through the full moon on the fifteenth day, observe the fast and single-mindedly recite the dhāraṇī one thousand times. This will cause those with short lives to have their lifespan restored and extended, forever freed from the suffering of illness. All karmic obstacles will be entirely eliminated. All the sufferings of hell: all birds, beasts, and living creatures who hear the sound of this dhāraṇī even once, at the end of their present life, will never again endure any suffering."

The Buddha said: "If any person suddenly encounters a severe illness and is oppressed by manifold suffering, hearing this dhāraṇī will immediately free them forever from all evil illnesses, and all suffering will be eliminated. If they should have fallen into the evil paths, they will also be liberated and will immediately be reborn in the World of Wondrous Joy. At the end of their present life, they will never again receive a body from the womb. Wherever they are, they will be born from a lotus. Wherever they are born, they will always have the memory of past lives and never forget."

The Buddha said: "If any person has previously created extremely grave evil karma and then dies, and because of this evil karma should fall into hell, the animal realm, Yama's realm, or the hungry-ghost destiny, or even into the Naraka hells, or be born in water, or become various kinds of birds or beasts: take the bones from the deceased's body, take a handful of clean earth, recite the dhāraṇī twenty-one times over it, and scatter it upon the bones. The deceased will be reborn in heaven."

The Buddha said: "If a person can daily recite this dhāraṇī twenty-one times, they should receive the most excellent offerings in the world. At the end of their life, they will be reborn in the Land of Ultimate Bliss. If they constantly recite it, their lifespan will increase and they will enjoy every kind of bliss. At the end of their life, they will immediately be reborn in the various wondrous and subtle buddha-fields, always gathered together with all buddhas in one place. All tathāgatas will constantly expound the subtle and wondrous Dharma for them. All buddhas will bestow the prediction of awakening. Their bodily light will illuminate all buddha-fields, and they will ultimately attain the supreme nirvāṇa.

"Furthermore, if one wishes to recite this dhāraṇī Dharma, before a buddha image, first take clean earth and make an altar, of whatever size, square in shape. Scatter various grasses and flowers upon the altar. Burn various fine incenses. Place both knees on the ground, or squat. The mind should constantly contemplate the Buddha. Form the mudrā of the dhāraṇī: first bend the index finger and press it with the thumb. Then join the palms at the heart and recite the dhāraṇī one hundred eight times. Within the altar, like a king of clouds, it will rain various flowers, able to make offerings universally to eighty-eight koṭi-Ganges-sand nayutas of hundreds of thousands of buddhas. Those buddha world-honored ones will all praise in unison: 'Excellent! Excellent! This is rare indeed. You are truly a child of the Buddha!' The practitioner will realize the samādhi of unobstructed wisdom and the samādhi of bodhicitta adornment. Those who hold this dhāraṇī should practice in this manner."

The Buddha said: "Lord of the Devas! Through my skillful means, if any sentient being should fall into hell, they will immediately be liberated. All evil paths will also be purified, eliminated without any remainder. Furthermore, I cause those who uphold it to have their lifespan increased. Lord of the Devas! You should now take my dhāraṇī Dharma and bestow it upon Supratiṣṭhita. Have him recite and uphold it for a full seven days, and then you and Supratiṣṭhita should both come to see me."

At that time, Śakra, Lord of the Devas, personally received the dhāraṇī Dharma before the World-Honored One. He upheld and remembered it, returned to his own palace, and bestowed it upon Supratiṣṭhita. When Supratiṣṭhita received this dhāraṇī Dharma, after six days and six nights of practicing and upholding it according to the method, all his wishes were fulfilled. The suffering of all the evil destinies that he should have received was immediately resolved and he was liberated. He abided in the factors of awakening with an extended lifespan, and immeasurable meritorious karma gathered like clouds in an instant.

Filled with boundless joy, he cried out in a loud voice: "How rare is the Tathāgata, who has spoken this wondrous Dharma! How rare are these efficacious results, so great and immeasurable! The greatly compassionate World-Honored One has been able to speak this divine mantra for us and all sentient beings, liberating me from the suffering of the evil destinies!"

At that time, after the seventh day, Śakra together with Supratiṣṭhita and the celestial assembly, adorned with flower garlands, carrying wondrous incense, jeweled banners, streamers, canopies, celestial garments, and jeweled necklaces, wondrously adorned, came to the Buddha's presence. Having made offerings, they presented celestial garments and various jeweled necklaces to the World-Honored One. They circumambulated hundreds of thousands of times, leaping with joy, and sat to one side, gazing upon the Honored One's countenance, their eyes never turning away.

At that time, the World-Honored One extended his golden arm and, with his right hand, adorned with a hundred blessings, endowed with all marks, and free from fear, touched the crown of Supratiṣṭhita and taught him the Dharma, bestowing the prediction of awakening. The Buddha told Supratiṣṭhita: "This dhāraṇī-seal is called 'The Buddhōṣṇīṣa Vijaya Dhāraṇī that Purifies and Removes All Evil Destinies.' You should uphold it."

At that time, Supratiṣṭhita, Śakra the Lord of the Devas, all the celestial assembly that had come, and the entire great assembly, having heard the Buddha's teaching, all rejoiced greatly, faithfully accepted it, and departed to put it into practice.
