# 佛說諸行有為經
## Sūtra on All Conditioned Phenomena, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0758

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmadeva (法天)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the World-Honored One was in the land of Śrāvastī, in the Jeta Grove of Anāthapiṇḍada, together with a great assembly of one thousand two hundred and fifty bhikṣus.

At that time the World-Honored One told the assembly of bhikṣus: "Bhikṣus! All conditioned phenomena are in constant flux, illusory and unreal. They cannot endure, have no fixed characteristics, and are inverted dharmas. Bhikṣus! Moreover, when all conditioned phenomena have their defilements exhausted, they are without stain and separated from all impurity. All sentient beings, even down to crawling creatures and bhūtas, when their lifespans are exhausted, will certainly perish. If there were no birth, there would be no death. Whether they are elders, brahmins, or kṣatriyas of noble clans, supremely powerful and wealthy in their own right, with immeasurable gold, silver, jewels, and all manner of possessions, lacking nothing; though they have parents, family, relatives, acquaintances, officials, servants, and attendants, all fully provided: when their lifespans are exhausted, none can be exempted. Furthermore, a kṣatriya who has received the consecration anointing and become a great king with great sovereignty, possessing great power and influence, with innumerable people, having conquered all the lands and territories of the great earth: when his lifespan is exhausted, he too cannot be exempted. Furthermore, the ascetics in the forests, those who practice austerities, who do not crave flavors and eat only fruits, and who further renounce even fruits to practice austerities: when their lifespans are exhausted, they too cannot be exempted.

"Furthermore, those who cultivate the ten wholesome deeds and are born in the heaven of the Four Great Kings, the Trāyastriṃśa heaven, the Yāma heaven, the Tuṣita heaven, the Nirmāṇarati heaven, or the Paranirmitavaśavartin heaven; and those who further cultivate dhyāna meditation and are born in the Brahmakāyika heaven, the Brahmapurohita heaven, the Mahābrahmā heaven, the Parīttābha heaven, the Apramāṇābha heaven, the Ābhāsvara heaven, the Parīttaśubha heaven, the Apramāṇaśubha heaven, and the Śubhakṛtsna heaven; those who attain the Anabhraka heaven, the Puṇyaprasava heaven, the Bṛhatphala heaven, and the Asaṃjñisattva heaven; and those non-returners who attain the Avṛha heaven, the Atapa heaven, the Sudṛśa heaven, the Sudarśana heaven, and the Akaniṣṭha heaven; and those who, wearied of the obstructing physical body, cultivate the samādhi of boundless space and the like, and are born in the sphere of boundless space, the sphere of boundless consciousness, the sphere of nothingness, and the sphere of neither-perception-nor-non-perception: all these devas, though supremely exalted, are not exempt from birth; they too must perish.

"Those in the three realms whose outflows are exhausted, who have done what was to be done, who have set down the heavy burden and attained their own benefit, who have ended all the fetters of existence and attained arhatship: though they too possess this body, it too must eventually be relinquished. And those who, in the kalpa of swords and weapons, cultivate a single body and dwell in stillness, who realize the causes and conditions and attain the realization of the middle vehicle, called pratyekabuddhas: their bodies too must eventually be relinquished. And even the Tathāgata, Worthy One, Perfectly Awakened One, whose ten powers are vast and great, whose four kinds of wisdom are perfectly clear, who teaches the Dharma without fear with the true lion's roar, who through countless exertions attained the body of Nārāyaṇa: this too must be relinquished.

"Bhikṣus! Just as a potter fashions unfired vessels, pots, and jars: though they may be completed, they are destined to break. And as ripe fruit must naturally fall. The dharma of arising and ceasing is also like this. Bhikṣus! All sentient beings, even down to the bhūtas, all those with consciousness: when their lifespans are exhausted, none are exempt from this. Just as if there were no birth, there would be no death." He then spoke these verses:

> "The Tathāgata, Deva above Devas,
> Has taught this dharma of impermanence.
> Like unfired vessels that are not solid,
> They will ultimately come to ruin.
> Like ripe fruit that naturally falls,
> The lives of sentient beings are just so.
> Bhikṣus, you should now understand:
> Arising and ceasing are to be feared."

When the World-Honored One had spoken this sūtra, all the bhikṣus received it with one mind and joyfully practiced accordingly.
