# Sūtra of the Holy Adornment Dhāraṇī Spoken by the Buddha

## *Fó Shuō Shèng Zhuāngyán Tuóluóní Jīng* (佛說聖莊嚴陀羅尼經)

Taishō 1376

Translated by Shīhù (*Shīhù*, 施護) of the Song dynasty.

*Note: This is a protective dhāraṇī sūtra set in Kapilavastu, in which the Buddha teaches the Great Adornment Dhāraṇī for the protection of the young Rāhula, who has been tormented by evil rākṣasas during the night. The sūtra contains a dramatic frame story: the child Rāhula, weeping, comes before the Buddha seeking protection, prompting the Buddha to teach this dhāraṇī not only for his sake but for the benefit of all future sentient beings. Subsequently, Maitreya Bodhisattva, Mahābrahmā, the Four Heavenly Kings, the stars, dragons, yakṣas, and various spirit-classes each pronounce their own protective mantras. The text concludes with the Great Brahmā affirming the collective protection and the Buddha confirming its truth.*

---

Thus have I heard. At one time, the World-Honored One was in the city of Kapilavastu (*Jiāpílúo chéng*, 迦毘羅城), in the Garden of the Sorrowless Tree, together with Ānanda and other great bhikṣus, and with Maitreya (*Císhì*, 慈氏) Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva, all assembled in one place.

At that time there were also gathered: deva hosts, nāga hosts, yakṣa hosts, rākṣasa hosts, asura hosts, garuḍa hosts, gandharva hosts, kiṃnara hosts, piśāca hosts, preta hosts, kumbhāṇḍa hosts, pūtana hosts, seizing-spirit hosts, the twenty-eight great yakṣa generals, the four great yakṣa generals, all the star-constellation hosts, and even the ascetic sages of the Snow Mountains. All such hosts came to the assembly.

There were also rākṣasa hosts from other regions, including護迦, 儞隷, 布寫, 那哩, 枳吒, 迦囉拏, 穌摩, 娜摩賀, 賀哩, 摩怛哩, and other such hosts who also came to the assembly.

Furthermore, in the four directions there were four great rākṣasas: in the east, the rākṣasa Harita; in the south, the rākṣasa Guṇa; in the west, the rākṣasa Kiśorī; in the north, the rākṣasa Maṇitānta. These four rākṣasas were extremely violent in nature, each with sixty retainers. These rākṣasas also came to the assembly.

## Fascicle One

At that time, in the palace of King Śuddhodana, the child Rāhula (*Luóhóuluó*, 羅睺羅), while sleeping at night, was tormented by evil rākṣasas who came to disturb him, repeatedly frightening him so that he could not rest in peace. Rāhula's mother Yaśodharā (*Yēshū tuóluó*, 耶輸陀羅) and all the attendants knew that the World-Honored One and the holy assembly were at the Garden of the Sorrowless Tree, not far from the palace. In the last watch of the night, she had Rāhula, together with the other children and attendants as escorts, proceed to the Buddha's presence. When the child Rāhula arrived at the Buddha's assembly, he prostrated at the Buddha's feet and stood alone before the Buddha, weeping.

The World-Honored One then addressed Ānanda: "Ānanda, do you see that the child Rāhula has come to my presence weeping?"

Ānanda replied: "Indeed, I have seen this."

The Buddha said: "Ānanda, when the child Rāhula lies down to sleep at night, evil rākṣasas and various ghosts and spirits come to torment him so that he cannot rest in peace. This is why he has come to me seeking protection."

"Ānanda, it is not only today that the child Rāhula is tormented and unable to rest. After my passing, in the final age, all sentient beings will have meager merit; their unwholesome karma will increase and blaze. Because of this, evil rākṣasas and ghosts and spirits will roam the world, constantly acting harmfully toward sentient beings, tormenting them in various ways and allowing them no peace. I observe this matter and feel deep compassion."

"Ānanda, I now wish to protect the child Rāhula and also to care compassionately for future sentient beings, and so I shall teach the Great Adornment Dhāraṇī. Listen carefully."

Thereupon Ānanda and the great assembly all fell silent and wished the Buddha to expound.

The World-Honored One then spoke the Great Adornment Dhāraṇī:

*Tadyathā gaṇi gagara gānāpati gumī gagara gumā pati sparika gāri ṇi garapati sarapati dhānapati surapati are pan are pan pati mapati apati vāhu pati hura harahā hiri garahuru utrile puhire oṃ citrile pata patita ṭa ṭa vaihara oṃ ravai maṇi sarhu mahā hire huhu samantā sasari asamantā sari parekhā sari avantī mahā vantri gaṇi mī khale harasva svāhā*

"Ānanda, this Great Adornment Dhāraṇī was proclaimed by all past buddhas. I now teach it again to protect the child Rāhula and also to benefit sentient beings of the final age."

"Ānanda, if there are sentient beings tormented by rākṣasas and evil ghosts and spirits in various ways, they should write this Adornment Dhāraṇī on paper or silk, make an amulet, and wear it on the body or arm. It will cause all evil rākṣasas and all blood-drinking evil ghosts and spirits of the world not to dare approach, but to flee and scatter to the four directions and the four intermediate directions, to a distance of ten yojanas, so that sentient beings may obtain peace and happiness."

"Ānanda, if sentient beings constantly recite this Great Adornment Dhāraṇī, all weapons, poisons, evil spells, snares and bindings, and all harmful acts by humans and non-humans will be completely liberated. Within a radius of ten yojanas, all sentient beings will be protected, and all calamities will be extinguished."

"Ānanda, if sentient beings wish to recite and hold this Great Adornment Dhāraṇī for the protection of their own bodies and for the benefit of all sentient beings, they should first select a pure and excellent site, set up markers beforehand, observe purification and bathe with fragrant water, so that body and mind are pure within and without. Using thread, they should demarcate a boundary, apply fragrant paste to the ground to make a maṇḍala. In the maṇḍala, they should arrange various fine flowers, incense for smearing and burning, lighted lamps, and various offerings. Facing north, sitting upright with right mindfulness, they should visualize the Buddha, the World-Honored One, descending to the maṇḍala. Then they should present various offerings, take refuge and praise, generate a firm and courageous mind of non-regression, for the purpose of benefiting and protecting all sentient beings and for the present accomplishment of their aims."

"They should first recite the boundary-establishing Adornment Dhāraṇī, alerting and summoning Brahmā, Indra, and all devas, nāgas, yakṣas, rākṣasas, and ghost-spirits to come and assemble, so that all evil yakṣas, rākṣasas, pūtanas, and others in the world who harm sentient beings, having heard this dhāraṇī, will be frightened and scattered, without any obstacles, and one's wishes will be fulfilled."

The boundary-establishing dhāraṇī:

*Tadyathā nire nire vimale tatmale hucī hucī siritīri tatri sanira pū svāhā*

"Ānanda, after reciting this boundary-establishing Adornment Dhāraṇī, one should then recite the Holy Great Adornment Dhāraṇī. All undertakings will be accomplished without fail."

---

## Fascicle Two

At that time, Maitreya Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva rose from his seat, stood before the Buddha, and said: "World-Honored One, I also wish to protect the child Rāhula and grant him peace, and to benefit all future sentient beings. For those tormented by various afflictions, evil persons, weapons, poisons, evil spells, snares and bindings, and all such matters, I wish to cause their complete liberation, so that day and night they may rest in peace and happiness. I shall also speak an Adornment Dhāraṇī. May the World-Honored One permit me to speak it."

The Buddha said: "Speak as you wish."

Maitreya Bodhisattva then spoke the Adornment Dhāraṇī:

*Tadyathā avanāti pānani avinā nujuru juru jarī dhananī nanī adhananī samantā nanī nirmuṇi nirmī muknī śānti svastiyānāṃ mama sarvasattvānāṃ ca rakṣa kuru svāhā*

Then the lord of the sahā world, the Great Brahmā King, addressed the Buddha: "I also wish to protect the child Rāhula and all future sentient beings, and to speak my own Adornment Mantra. May the Tathāgata permit me to speak it."

The Buddha said: "Excellent! Excellent! Speak as you wish."

The Great Brahmā King then spoke the Adornment Mantra:

*Samyāthi nandāṃ aṅgurī pan gurī anur nūnur śikhe yuṃ kaśikharī nandur nūr nūrāyati acā acā vimuca śānti yuṃ svastiyāyānāṃ kuru mama sarvasattvānāṃ ca svāhā*

The World-Honored One, knowing that the deva hosts, nāga hosts, yakṣa hosts, and all the ghosts and spirits each wished to protect the child Rāhula and future sentient beings by speaking their own mantras, said: "You deva hosts, nāga hosts, and all ghosts and spirits who wish to speak your own mantras for the protection of sentient beings, speak them each as you wish."

[Then follow the protective mantras spoken by:]

- Supratiṣṭhita Deva (*Shàn Shífēn Tiān*, 善時分天)
- Indra, Lord of the Devas (*Dìshì Tiānzhǔ*, 帝釋天主)
- Vaiśravaṇa, Heavenly King (*Píshāmén Tiānwáng*, 毘沙門天王)
- Dhṛtarāṣṭra, Heavenly King (*Chíguó Tiānwáng*, 持國天王)
- Virūḍhaka, Heavenly King (*Zēngzhǎng Tiānwáng*, 增長天王)
- Virūpākṣa, Heavenly King (*Guǎngmù Tiānwáng*, 廣目天王)
- Candradeva, the Wondrous Moon (*Miàoyuè Tiānzǐ*, 妙月天子)
- All the deva hosts, speaking with one voice
- All the star-constellation devas, speaking with one voice
- All the nāga hosts, speaking with one voice
- The twenty-eight great yakṣa generals, speaking with one voice
- The pretas, piśācas, kumbhāṇḍas, and others, speaking with one voice
- The pūtanas and kaṭapūtanas, speaking with one voice
- All the mātṛ spirits, speaking with one voice
- The four directional rākṣasas, speaking with one voice
- The rākṣasī Naḍamitā

---

Then the lord of the sahā world, the Great Brahmā King, addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One, we deva hosts, nāga hosts, yakṣa hosts, and all, including Naḍamitā and such, have all at this time, in the World-Honored One's presence, each spoken our own mantras, wishing to protect the child Rāhula so that no evil ghosts or spirits may approach to torment him, so that he may have peace by day, peace by night, and happiness at all times."

"World-Honored One, our mantras are spoken not only to protect the child Rāhula, but also to protect all future sentient beings."

"World-Honored One, if sentient beings hear these mantras of ours, they should sincerely remember and uphold them. This will cause all those harmful types, including evil yakṣas, rākṣasas, and pūtanas, within one yojana, two yojanas, three or four yojanas, and up to ten yojanas, not to dare approach, but to be frightened and scattered far away. Within those ten yojanas, all evils will be extinguished, pestilence will not spread, weapons and poisons will all be kept far away, and they will not be obstructed by any devas, nāgas, yakṣas, rākṣasas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, or any pūtanas and non-human types."

"World-Honored One, among these hosts, if any disobey and do not comply, their own assemblies will not tolerate them and will expel them from their clans."

Then the World-Honored One addressed Ānanda: "This Great Brahmā King has spoken these true words in my presence; I have verified them. Ānanda, the Great Adornment Dhāraṇī that I have taught has great power, can subdue all evils, and has beneficent effect; I have already taught it."

"Ānanda, if anyone, using the evil spells of any human or non-human beings, attempts to seize, bind, and torment sentient beings in various ways, whether on the ground, in the sky, in mountain valleys, on mountain peaks, or in any place whatsoever, by the awesome power of my Holy Adornment Dhāraṇī, all will be destroyed and unable to harm sentient beings."

The Buddha further said to Ānanda: "The Buddha is true, the Dharma is true, the Saṃgha is true, the devas are true, the ṛṣis are true. These five cannot be contradicted. Ānanda, as I have spoken, receive and uphold this well, so that in the future it may circulate in the world and greatly benefit all sentient beings."

When the Buddha had finished speaking this sūtra, the child Rāhula, having heard the World-Honored One teach the Great Adornment Dhāraṇī and having received the protection of all these mantras, obtained peace and happiness without leaving the assembly. His mind was joyful and delighted. He touched his head to the ground and prostrated at the Tathāgata's feet, then returned to the royal palace with his attendants.

Maitreya Bodhisattva, Ānanda, and all the devas, nāgas, yakṣas, and all humans and non-humans, having heard what the Buddha taught, joyfully believed and accepted it, bowed to the Buddha, and withdrew.
