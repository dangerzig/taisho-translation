# Glossary: Brilliance Praise Sūtra (光讚經)

## Key Terms

### Core Prajñāpāramitā Terms

| Chinese | Sanskrit | English | Notes |
|---------|---------|---------|-------|
| 般若波羅蜜多 | prajñāpāramitā | perfection of wisdom | The central subject of the entire sūtra. Dharmarakṣa's title renders it as "brilliance praise" (光讚), an interpretive translation of prajñā as brilliance. |
| 般若 | prajñā | wisdom | The capacity for penetrative insight into the nature of all dharmas; the sixth and highest of the six pāramitās. |
| 空 | śūnyatā | emptiness | The fundamental teaching that all dharmas are empty of intrinsic existence. The sūtra devotes an entire chapter ("Distinguishing Emptiness") to this theme and enumerates multiple categories of emptiness. |
| 無相 | animitta | signlessness | One of the three gates of liberation. The absence of distinguishing marks or characteristics in all dharmas. |
| 無願 | apraṇihita | wishlessness | One of the three gates of liberation. The absence of any goal-directed aspiration based on false views. |
| 假名 | prajñapti | conventional designation | A central concept in the sūtra: all dharmas, including "bodhisattva" and "prajñāpāramitā," are merely conventional designations that do not arise and do not cease. |
| 戲論 | prapañca | conceptual proliferation | The mental activity of making distinctions and elaborations that obscures the true nature of dharmas. |
| 真如 | tathatā | suchness | The ultimate nature of reality; the way things truly are, beyond conceptual elaboration. Referenced as "naturalness" or "the natural" throughout the sūtra. |
| 法性 | dharmatā | nature of dharmas | The essential nature of phenomena; often used synonymously with suchness and emptiness. |
| 法界 | dharmadhātu | dharma-realm | The totality of all dharmas viewed as a single reality; the sphere of dharmas in which all phenomena abide. |
| 緣起 | pratītyasamutpāda | dependent arising | The principle that all conditioned phenomena arise in dependence on causes and conditions. |
| 勝義諦 | paramārtha-satya | ultimate truth | Truth as realized through direct yogic perception, beyond conceptual elaboration. |
| 我見 | ātma-dṛṣṭi | self-view | The mistaken view that a permanent, independent self exists. The sūtra repeatedly demonstrates that self, person, and lifespan are unobtainable. |

### The Categories of Emptiness

| Chinese | Sanskrit | English | Notes |
|---------|---------|---------|-------|
| 內空 | ādhyātma-śūnyatā | internal emptiness | Emptiness of the six internal sense faculties (eye, ear, nose, tongue, body, mind). |
| 外空 | bahirdhā-śūnyatā | external emptiness | Emptiness of the six external sense objects (form, sound, smell, taste, touch, dharmas). |
| 內外空 | ādhyātma-bahirdhā-śūnyatā | emptiness of internal and external | Emptiness of both the internal faculties and external objects together. |
| 空空 | śūnyatā-śūnyatā | emptiness of emptiness | The emptiness of emptiness itself; even the concept of emptiness lacks inherent existence. |
| 大空 | mahā-śūnyatā | great emptiness | Emptiness of the ten directions; the spatial dimensions are themselves empty. |
| 有為空 | saṃskṛta-śūnyatā | emptiness of the conditioned | Emptiness of all conditioned phenomena. |
| 無為空 | asaṃskṛta-śūnyatā | emptiness of the unconditioned | Emptiness of unconditioned dharmas; even nirvāṇa and cessation are empty. |

### Abhidharma Categories

| Chinese | Sanskrit | English | Notes |
|---------|---------|---------|-------|
| 五蘊 | pañcaskandha | five aggregates | Form, feeling, perception, formations, and consciousness. The sūtra exhaustively demonstrates the emptiness of each aggregate. |
| 色 | rūpa | form | Material or physical phenomena; the first of the five aggregates. |
| 受 | vedanā | feeling | The quality of pleasantness, unpleasantness, or neutrality in experience; the second aggregate. |
| 想 | saṃjñā | perception | Cognition that identifies and labels objects; the third aggregate. |
| 行 | saṃskāra | formations | Volitional and conditioning factors; the fourth aggregate. |
| 識 | vijñāna | consciousness | Awareness or discernment; the fifth aggregate. Also one of the eighteen elements. |
| 六入 | ṣaḍāyatana | six sense bases | The six internal bases of cognition: eye, ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind. |
| 十八界 | aṣṭādaśadhātu | eighteen elements | The six faculties, six objects, and six consciousnesses. Systematically shown to be empty throughout the sūtra. |
| 四大 | catvāri mahābhūtāni | four elements | Earth, water, fire, and wind; the basic material constituents of phenomena. |
| 十二因緣 | dvādaśāṅga-pratītyasamutpāda | twelve links | The twelve links of dependent arising from ignorance through aging-and-death. The sūtra states these are "without root" and the bodhisattva comprehends them as empty. |
| 法 | dharma | dharma | A term with many meanings: the Buddha's teaching, a constituent of experience, a mental object, or reality itself. |
| 有為法 | saṃskṛta-dharma | conditioned phenomena | Dharmas produced by causes and conditions, contrasted with the unconditioned. |
| 無為 | asaṃskṛta | unconditioned | That which is not produced by causes and conditions, such as space, cessation, and suchness. |

### Afflictions

| Chinese | Sanskrit | English | Notes |
|---------|---------|---------|-------|
| 煩惱 | kleśa | afflictions | Mental factors that disturb the mind and perpetuate suffering. |
| 無明 | avidyā | ignorance | Fundamental misunderstanding of reality; the first of the twelve links and root of all affliction. |
| 貪 | rāga | greed | Desire for or clinging to pleasant objects; one of the three poisons. |
| 瞋 | dveṣa | anger | Hostility toward unpleasant objects; one of the three poisons. |
| 癡 | moha | delusion | Fundamental confusion about reality; one of the three poisons. |
| 憍 | mada | arrogance | Inflated self-regard; listed among the afflictions to be abandoned. |
| 疑 | vicikitsā | doubt | Uncertainty regarding the truths, the Three Jewels, or the path. |
| 漏 | āsrava | outflows | The "outflows" or taints of desire, existence, and ignorance. The sūtra frequently speaks of those "whose outflows are exhausted." |

### The Six Pāramitās

| Chinese | Sanskrit | English | Notes |
|---------|---------|---------|-------|
| 六波羅蜜 | ṣaṭpāramitā | six pāramitās | The six perfections that constitute the bodhisattva's mahāyānic path: giving, morality, patience, vigor, meditation, and wisdom. |
| 布施波羅蜜 | dāna-pāramitā | perfection of giving | The first pāramitā. The bodhisattva gives without any conception of giver, gift, or recipient. |
| 戒波羅蜜 | śīla-pāramitā | perfection of morality | The second pāramitā. Observing the ten wholesome courses of action while establishing others therein. |
| 忍辱波羅蜜 | kṣānti-pāramitā | perfection of patience | The third pāramitā. Forbearance in the face of hardship, harm, or difficult teachings, without any resentment. |
| 精進波羅蜜 | vīrya-pāramitā | perfection of vigor | The fourth pāramitā. Joyful, tireless effort in wholesome activities. |
| 禪定波羅蜜 | dhyāna-pāramitā | perfection of meditation | The fifth pāramitā. Practicing samādhi through skillful means without following concentration to be reborn in higher realms. |
| 布施 | dāna | giving | Generosity; the first of the six perfections. |
| 戒 | śīla | morality | Ethical conduct; the second perfection and one of the three trainings. |
| 忍 | kṣānti | patient endurance | Forbearance; the sūtra also uses this for the "patient endurance of the non-arising of dharmas" (anutpattikadharmakṣānti). |
| 精進 | vīrya | vigor | Diligent effort in wholesome activities; the fourth perfection. |
| 禪定 | dhyāna | meditation | States of deep concentration; the fifth perfection. |
| 慧 | prajñā | wisdom | The capacity to discern and understand; the sixth perfection. |

### Meditative Attainments

| Chinese | Sanskrit | English | Notes |
|---------|---------|---------|-------|
| 三摩地 | samādhi | concentration | One-pointed focus of mind; the sūtra enumerates 110 named samādhis as part of the bodhisattva's mahāyānic vehicle. |
| 四禪 | catvāri dhyānāni | four dhyānas | The four meditative absorptions of the form realm. |
| 四無色定 | caturārūpyadhyāna | four formless absorptions | The four meditative absorptions of the formless realm. |
| 空三昧 | śūnyatā-samādhi | emptiness samādhi | Concentration on the emptiness of all dharmas; one of the three gates of liberation. |
| 無相三昧 | animitta-samādhi | signless samādhi | Concentration on the signlessness of all dharmas; one of the three gates of liberation. |
| 無願三昧 | apraṇihita-samādhi | wishless samādhi | Concentration on the wishlessness of all dharmas; one of the three gates of liberation. |
| 八解脫 | aṣṭavimokṣa | eight liberations | Eight progressive stages of meditative liberation, culminating in the cessation of perception and feeling. |
| 陀羅尼 | dhāraṇī | dhāraṇī | Mnemonic formulas or incantations; the sūtra speaks of "dhāraṇī gates" alongside "samādhi gates." |
| 首楞嚴三昧 | śūraṅgama-samādhi | Śūraṅgama Samādhi | The "heroic march" samādhi, listed first among the 110 samādhis of the bodhisattva's mahāyānic vehicle. |

### The Thirty-Seven Factors of Awakening

| Chinese | Sanskrit | English | Notes |
|---------|---------|---------|-------|
| 四念處 | catvāri smṛtyupasthānāni | four foundations of mindfulness | Mindfulness of body, feeling, mind, and dharmas. Repeatedly invoked throughout the sūtra. |
| 四正勤 | catvāri samyakpradhānāni | four right efforts | Preventing unarisen unwholesome dharmas, abandoning arisen ones, generating unarisen wholesome dharmas, and maintaining arisen ones. |
| 四如意足 | catvāra ṛddhipādāḥ | four bases of supernatural power | Aspiration, vigor, mind, and investigation; the bases for developing supernatural abilities. |
| 五根 | pañcendriyāṇi | five faculties | Faith, vigor, mindfulness, concentration, and wisdom as spiritual capacities. |
| 五力 | pañcabalāni | five powers | The same five qualities as the faculties, but as active forces that overcome their opposites. |
| 七覺支 | saptabodhyaṅga | seven factors of awakening | Mindfulness, investigation of dharmas, vigor, joy, tranquility, concentration, and equanimity. |
| 八正道 | āryāṣṭāṅgikamārga | eightfold path | Right view, thought, speech, action, livelihood, effort, mindfulness, and concentration. |

### Buddha Qualities

| Chinese | Sanskrit | English | Notes |
|---------|---------|---------|-------|
| 十力 | daśabala | ten powers | The ten special powers of a tathāgata, including knowledge of what is possible and impossible, of karmic results, and of the paths leading to all destinations. |
| 四無畏 | catvāri vaiśāradyāni | four fearlessnesses | The four kinds of confidence possessed by a buddha in teaching. |
| 四辯才 | catasraḥ pratisaṃvidaḥ | four analytical knowledges | The four kinds of unhindered knowledge: of dharma, meaning, expression, and eloquence. Frequently paired with the ten powers in this sūtra. |
| 十八不共法 | aṣṭādaśa āveṇika-buddhadharma | eighteen unique buddha-dharmas | Eighteen qualities exclusive to a fully awakened buddha, not shared with śrāvakas or pratyekabuddhas. |
| 五眼 | pañcacakṣus | five eyes | The fleshly eye, heavenly eye, wisdom eye, dharma eye, and buddha eye. |
| 天眼 | divyacakṣus | divine eye | The supernatural ability to see beings' births, deaths, and karmic destinations. |
| 慧眼 | prajñācakṣus | wisdom eye | The eye of wisdom that penetrates the emptiness of all dharmas. |
| 法眼 | dharmacakṣus | dharma eye | The eye that sees the differentiation of dharmas and the paths suited to beings. |
| 佛眼 | buddhacakṣus | buddha eye | The all-encompassing eye of a fully awakened buddha. |
| 六神通 | ṣaḍabhijñā | six supernatural powers | The divine eye, divine ear, knowledge of others' minds, recollection of past lives, supernatural abilities, and the exhaustion of outflows. |
| 大悲 | mahākaruṇā | great compassion | The buddha's or bodhisattva's universal compassion for all sentient beings. A foundational motivation for the bodhisattva path. |
| 四無量心 | catvāry apramāṇāni | four immeasurables | Loving-kindness, compassion, sympathetic joy, and equanimity. |
| 慈 | maitrī | loving-kindness | The wish for all beings to be happy; one of the four immeasurables. |
| 悲 | karuṇā | compassion | The wish for all beings to be free from suffering; one of the four immeasurables. |
| 喜 | muditā | sympathetic joy | Rejoicing in others' happiness and merit; one of the four immeasurables. |
| 捨 | upekṣā | equanimity | Impartial regard for all beings; one of the four immeasurables. |

### Path and Practice

| Chinese | Sanskrit | English | Notes |
|---------|---------|---------|-------|
| 菩提 | bodhi | awakening | The enlightenment sought by bodhisattvas; specifically, anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi (supreme, correct, and perfect awakening). |
| 阿耨多羅三藐三菩提 | anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi | supreme, correct, and perfect awakening | The full awakening of a buddha. Transliterated from Sanskrit throughout the text. |
| 一切智 | sarvajñā | omniscience | The "wisdom of sarvajñā" (all-knowing wisdom) is a key goal throughout the sūtra, appearing over 100 times. |
| 不退轉 | avaivartika | non-retrogression | The stage from which a bodhisattva can no longer fall back from the path to buddhahood. |
| 無生法忍 | anutpattikadharmakṣānti | patient endurance of the non-arising of dharmas | The insight that all dharmas are unborn; a decisive attainment on the bodhisattva path. |
| 方便 | upāya | skillful means | The various methods and means used by bodhisattvas to guide beings according to their capacities. |
| 方便善巧 | upāya-kauśalya | skill in means | The adept use of appropriate teaching methods and compassionate strategies. |
| 十善業 | daśakuśalakarmapatha | ten wholesome deeds | The ten courses of wholesome action: abstaining from killing, stealing, sexual misconduct, lying, divisive speech, harsh speech, idle talk, covetousness, malice, and wrong views. |
| 念 | smṛti | mindfulness | Attentive awareness; one of the five faculties and a component of many practice lists. |
| 金剛 | vajra | vajra | "Diamond" or "thunderbolt"; used as a simile for indestructible concentration (vajra-samādhi). |
| 三藏 | Tripiṭaka | Tripiṭaka | The "three baskets" of Buddhist scripture: sūtra, vinaya, and abhidharma. |

| 欲界 | kāmadhātu | desire realm | The lowest of the three realms, where beings are driven by sensory desire. |

| 閻浮提 | Jambudvīpa | Jambudvīpa | The southern continent in Buddhist cosmology, corresponding to the human world. |

### Persons and Vehicles

| Chinese | Sanskrit | English | Notes |
|---------|---------|---------|-------|

| 轉輪王 | cakravartin | wheel-turning king | A universal monarch who rules by righteousness; the sūtra mentions bodhisattvas appearing as cakravartins. |
