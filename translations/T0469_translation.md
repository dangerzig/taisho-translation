# 文殊問經字母品第十四
## Chapter Fourteen on the Syllabary, from the Mañjuśrī-paripṛcchā Sūtra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0469

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空)

---

At that time, Mañjuśrī addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! As for all the letters of the syllabary, how do all dharmas enter into these and the dhāraṇī syllables?"

The Buddha told Mañjuśrī: "All dharmas enter into the letters of the syllabary and the dhāraṇī syllables. Mañjuśrī! As follows:

"When one intones the syllable *a*, it is the sound of impermanence.

"When one intones the syllable *ā*, it is the sound of the absence of self.

"When one intones the syllable *i*, it is the sound of the sense faculties being vast and broad.

"When one intones the syllable *ī*, it is the sound of worldly calamity.

"When one intones the syllable *u*, it is the sound of manifold oppression.

"When one intones the syllable *ū*, it is the sound of diminishing the many beings of the world.

"When one intones the syllable *ṛ*, it is the sound of beings who are upright, gentle, and continuous.

"When one intones the syllable *ṝ*, it is the sound of severing the play of defilement.

"When one intones the syllable *ḷ*, it is the sound of the marks of arising dharmas.

"When one intones the syllable *ḹ*, it is the sound of the marks of defilement in the three realms of existence.

"When one intones the syllable *e*, it is the sound of arousing what is sought.

"When one intones the syllable *ai*, it is the sound of the supreme deportment.

"When one intones the syllable *o*, it is the sound of grasping.

"When one intones the syllable *au*, it is the sound of transformation birth.

"When one intones the syllable *aṃ*, it is the sound of the absence of 'mine.'

"When one intones the syllable *aḥ*, it is the sound of sinking and submersion.

"When one intones the syllable *ka*, it is the sound of entering the ripening of karma.

"When one intones the syllable *kha*, it is the sound of the emergence of all dharmas being equal to empty space.

"When one intones the syllable *ga*, it is the sound of the profound Dharma.

"When one intones the syllable *gha*, it is the sound of crushing the dense darkness of ignorance.

"When one intones the syllable *ṅa*, it is the sound of the purification of the five destinies.

"When one intones the syllable *ca*, it is the sound of the Four Noble Truths.

"When one intones the syllable *cha*, it is the sound of not concealing desire.

"When one intones the syllable *ja*, it is the sound of transcending old age and death.

"When one intones the syllable *jha*, it is the sound of subduing evil speech.

"When one intones the syllable *ña*, it is the sound of subduing the māras of others.

"When one intones the syllable *ṭa*, it is the sound of cutting off speech.

"When one intones the syllable *ṭha*, it is the sound of producing a definitive reply.

"When one intones the syllable *ḍa*, it is the sound of producing the subduing of māra-disputes.

"When one intones the syllable *ḍha*, it is the sound of extinguishing defiled realms.

"When one intones the syllable *ṇa*, it is the sound of eliminating all afflictions.

"When one intones the syllable *ta*, it is the sound of suchness being uninterrupted.

"When one intones the syllable *tha*, it is the sound of the power of fearless advance.

"When one intones the syllable *na*, it is the sound of taming, discipline, tranquility, and peace.

"When one intones the syllable *dha*, it is the sound of the seven kinds of noble wealth.

"When one intones the syllable *na*, it is the sound of thoroughly knowing name and form.

"When one intones the syllable *pa*, it is the sound of the supreme meaning.

"When one intones the syllable *pha*, it is the sound of attaining the fruit and realizing it.

"When one intones the syllable *ma*, it is the sound of liberation from bondage.

"When one intones the syllable *bha*, it is the sound of emerging from the three realms of existence.

"When one intones the syllable *ma*, it is the sound of quelling arrogance and pride.

"When one intones the syllable *ya*, it is the sound of the Buddha's penetrating comprehension.

"When one intones the syllable *ra*, it is the sound of the supreme meaning of pleasure and displeasure.

"When one intones the syllable *la*, it is the sound of cutting the limbs of craving.

"When one intones the syllable *va*, it is the sound of the most supreme vehicle.

"When one intones the syllable *śa*, it is the sound of the emergence of faith, diligence, mindfulness, concentration, and wisdom.

"When one intones the syllable *ṣa*, it is the sound of subduing the six sense-bases and attaining the wisdom of the six supernatural powers.

"When one intones the syllable *sa*, it is the sound of directly realizing omniscience.

"When one intones the syllable *ha*, it is the sound of destroying afflictions and being free from desire.

"When one intones the syllable *kṣa*, it is the sound of the ultimate wordlessness of all syllables.

"Mañjuśrī! These are the meanings of the letters of the syllabary, and all syllables enter into them."
