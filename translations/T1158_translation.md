# 地藏菩薩儀軌
## Ritual Manual for Kṣitigarbha Bodhisattva

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1158

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Śubhakarasiṃha (輸婆迦羅).

---

At that time Śākyamuni Buddha was on Mount Rātijeya, together with innumerable Kṣitigarbha bodhisattvas, ten koṭis of bhikṣus and bhikṣuṇīs, and devas, nāgas, and the eight classes of spirits. An immeasurable assembly of hundreds of thousands surrounded him as he expounded the dharma.

At that time in the great assembly there was a great being named Kṣitigarbha, who bared his right shoulder, placed his right knee on the ground, joined his palms in reverence, and addressed the Buddha: "I wish to speak divine mantras in order to benefit all sentient beings. I beseech the World-Honored One to compassionately grant permission to speak the divine mantras."

Then the Buddha told Kṣitigarbha Bodhisattva: "Speak quickly, to benefit all sentient beings."

Then Kṣitigarbha Bodhisattva rose into the sky and uttered praises, paying homage to the World-Honored One. While in the sky he also manifested immeasurable supernatural powers, and with his divine voice spoke the mantra:

*Oṃ aṃ mātha māṇi kumbhi kumbhi samanta svāhā*

Next he spoke the heart mantra:

*Oṃ yaṃ mātha jaḥ svāhā*

Next the heart-within-the-heart mantra:

*Oṃ kṣi hī*

As soon as this mantra was spoken, the great bodhisattvas of the ten bhūmis and others lost their composure and were bewildered. The devas, nāgas, eight classes, yakṣas, and spirits were startled, fled, and fell to the ground. The sun, moon, and stars lost their light and were hidden. Jeweled flowers rained profusely from the sky. Even through the exhaustion of kalpas, the power of the mantra cannot be fully described.

Next, the method for painting the image. Make a figure in the form of a śrāvaka, wearing a kāṣāya covering the left shoulder. The left hand holds a flower in full bloom, and the right hand forms the abhaya mudrā. Seat it upon a lotus. Alternatively, for the image of a great being seated upon a throne, the crown bears a celestial diadem, wearing a kāṣāya. The left hand holds a lotus-cintāmaṇi, and the right hand is as before. Seat it upon a nine-grade lotus pedestal.

Next, the mudrā-mantra seal. Both hands in saṃpuṭa, with the ring fingers moving inward and outward within the palms. The mantra:

*Oṃ yaṃ māja rī svāhā*

Next, the universal offering mudrā. Both hands in saṃpuṭa, with the thumbs standing upright, moving back and forth.

*Oṃ naṃ jama ṇi svāhā*

Next, the comprehensive general mudrā. Both hands in saṃpuṭa, with the index fingers standing upright, moving back and forth. The mantra:

*Oṃ jinīya svāhā*

Next, the invocation and praise mudrā. Both hands in saṃpuṭa, with the index fingers moving back and forth. Next, the dismissal: with the contemplation hand, snap the thumb and index finger.

Furthermore, the methods of accomplishment. If one wishes to attain great merit, perform a homa with akā-wood seventy thousand times. If one wishes the five grains to be accomplished, perform a homa with rice grains and flowers. If one wishes merit for another person, take earth from that household's hearth and perform a homa. If one wishes to attain high position and twofold virtue, perform a homa with bilva flowers. If one wishes enemies from life after life to be subdued, perform a homa with bitter neem-wood. If one wishes to subdue evil spirits and sorcery, perform a homa with poisonous substances. If one wishes to cure madness and illness, perform a homa with lotus-seed grass thirty thousand times. If one wishes to extinguish offenses and generate goodness, and to be reborn in Sukhāvatī in this body and hereafter, perform a homa with grass thirty thousand times. If one wishes the curses and spells of evil persons to revert to those persons, cast bitter grass into the fire and perform a homa thirty thousand times. If one wishes to attain immeasurable prosperity and emolument, perform a homa with white-flower bird-grass thirty thousand times. If one wishes the virtue of samādhi and eloquence, perform a homa with kulū-grass thirty thousand times. If one wishes to convert all sentient beings and cause them to leave suffering and attain happiness, perform a homa with white mustard seeds thirty thousand times. If one wishes to convert sentient beings in worlds without a buddha, perform a homa with śikā-grass thirty-seven myriads of times. If one wishes dry fields to produce the five grains, empower old turnip greens and scatter them. If one is troubled by myriad illnesses, perform a homa with achyranthes grass thirty thousand times. If one is troubled by the three yin and four heat fevers and various illnesses, perform a homa with gandhaka and various gate-grasses. If husband and wife are not in harmony, perform a homa with reconciliation-grass thirty thousand times and they will be harmonized. If evil friends and good friends are at odds, perform a homa with vine-leaf grass thirty thousand times. If performed on the eighth, fourteenth, or fifteenth day of the bright half of the month, the above methods will certainly be accomplished.
