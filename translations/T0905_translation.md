# 三種悉地破地獄轉業障出三界祕密陀羅尼法

## The Secret Dhāraṇī Method of the Three Kinds of Siddhi for Breaking Hell, Transforming Karmic Obstacles, and Transcending the Three Realms

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0905

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Śubhakarasiṃha (善無畏).

---

The practical methods of yoga number approximately a thousand. Here I briefly describe a small portion. When one opens the mouth and raises the tongue, the palace of the dharma-realm trembles, and the Tathāgata of the Lotus Treasury World emerges from samādhi, instantly crushing hell, transforming karmic obstacles, and transcending the three realms. One should know that if the syllables are properly arranged, when a sovereign wears them on the crown of the head, ten thousand lands will be peaceful and pure. When regional governors and military inspectors inscribe the true words on their banners and pennants, the four quarters will be tranquil. When prefects and garrison commanders inscribe the syllables on drums and horns to sound a solemn warning, the drum-sound resounds far and wide, banishing baleful influences for a thousand leagues, causing crops to flourish with ample rain, freeing the people from calamity and plague, and making the local earth-spirits bring gentle winds and timely rain. When one recites and empowers the syllables, inscribing them on war-drums, enemy forces will surrender of their own accord and not a single person will be harmed. This is called the Vajra Drum.

> Homage to Vairocana Buddha,
> Whose pure eyes bloom open like lotuses,
> Tamer of the three realms, teacher of devas and humans,
> Savior of the world through the great bodhicitta.
> The profound and wondrous true words and empowerment dharma
> Flow into the gate of the unborn, the syllable *A*;
> The white ūrṇā, signless, perfectly omniscient,
> Full and ever-shining like sun and moon.
> Body, speech, and mind become the three mysteries,
> The three mysteries accomplish the dharma of response and transformation.
> The five wheels and five wisdoms are the five divisions,
> The five divisions fully encompass the dharma-realm wheel.
> Akṣobhya, Ratnasambhava, Savior of the World,
> Amitābha, Amoghasiddhi, the Unerring King:
> They accomplish the auspicious wheel of siddhi,
> Transmitting this wondrous dharma to transform all beings.
> With compassionate mind, sovereign and subduing the three worlds,
> Vajrasattva and Acala,
> Without violating their vows, responding at the appointed time,
> When the yoga work is complete, they return to the vajra realm.

The Buddha said: "The syllable *A* is the Vajra Division lord of the liver. The syllable *A* is the Dharma Body of principle of Mahāvairocana Tathāgata, the seed of the vajra earth-wheel whose fundamental nature is pure, whose ultimate principle is utterly ungraspable emptiness. It is the maṇḍala of the Vajra Division. If one speaks in terms of name-and-form: earth is form-dharma. Among the five aggregates, the consciousness-aggregate mind sustains earth, and its seed is impure. The five viscera are form-dharma. Among the five aggregates, the consciousness-aggregate mind gives rise to them; therefore, in terms of name-and-form, earth is form-dharma. Now the liver governs the ethereal soul (*hun*). The spirit-energy of the ethereal soul corresponds to the east and wood. Wood is form-emptiness. Wood governs spring; its color is blue-green. Blue-green arises from wood, wood arises from water, and the liver arises from blue-green energy and the kidneys. Its shape resembles an upright lotus petal with a pearl set in its center; its flesh is on the left side of the chest. The liver extends outward as the eyes and governs the sinews; the sinews terminate as the nails. Now, because the five syllable-gates govern the five viscera and six bowels, inner and outer are intermingled. Furthermore, sour flavors enter the liver most readily, strengthening the liver but harming the spleen. If the spleen lacks the ethereal soul, one becomes frequently dull and confused. The lungs attack the liver and cause disease, just as metal overcomes wood. When the lungs are strong and the liver weak, one should settle the mind upon the lungs and use blue-green energy to absorb white energy; then the liver disease will be cured, by means of blue-green energy.

"The syllable *Vaṃ* is the Lotus Division lord of the lungs. The syllable *Vaṃ* is the eleventh conjugation of the syllable *Va*, and the syllable *Vi* is the third conjugation. It is a transformative explanation of the meaning of the syllable *A*: the seed of the great ocean water-wheel of Mahāvairocana Tathāgata's wisdom. The dharma of sovereign supernatural powers is called the Wisdom Dharma Body, also called the Reward Body. It is the maṇḍala of the Lotus Division. The lung-viscera governs the bodily soul (*po*); the bodily soul is the physical form. Its shape resembles a flower. It governs the nose and corresponds to the west and metal. Metal governs autumn; its color is white. White arises from wind, wind arises from the yang-energy of earth. Among the five aggregates, the perception-aggregate mind sustains wind. Perception-mind arises from consciousness, consciousness-mind arises from past formations, past formations arise from ignorance, ignorance arises from deluded thinking, and deluded thinking arises again from deluded thinking: this is the cycle of the twelve links of dependent origination. The lungs arise from white energy and the spleen. Pungent flavors enter the lungs most readily, strengthening the lungs but harming the liver. If the lungs lack the bodily soul, one suffers fear and madness. The heart attacks the lungs and causes disease, just as fire overcomes metal. When the heart is strong and the lungs weak, one should settle the mind upon the heart and use white energy to absorb red energy; then the lung disease will be cured, by means of white energy, the syllable *Vaṃ*.

"The syllable *Raṃ* is the Jewel Division lord of the heart. The syllable *Raṃ* is the seed of the fire-element of Mahāvairocana Tathāgata's heart-ground, the dwelling-place of the Buddhas of the three times, burning away the beginningless dust and defilements of deluded attachment of all sentient beings, producing the sprout-seed of the bodhicitta. It is a transformative explanation of the meaning of the syllable *A*: it is the Nirmāṇakāya Tathāgata, but in truth it is the Wisdom Dharma Body fire-born maṇḍala. The heart governs the spirit (*shen*). Its shape resembles a bird; it corresponds to the south and fire. Fire governs summer; its color is red. Red arises from fire, fire arises from wood. Among the five aggregates, the feeling-aggregate mind sustains fire. The feeling-mind arises from the perception-mind. Furthermore, the heart arises from red energy and the liver. The heart extends outward as the tongue and governs the blood; blood terminates as milk. It also governs the ears, and extends to the nose-bridge, throat, forehead, and chin. Bitter flavors enter the heart most readily, strengthening the heart but harming the lungs. If the heart lacks the spirit, one frequently forgets what came before and after. The kidneys attack the heart and cause disease, just as water overcomes fire. When the kidneys are strong and the heart weak, one should settle the mind upon the kidneys and use red energy to absorb black energy; then the heart disease will be cured, by means of red energy, the syllable *Raṃ*.

"The syllable *Haṃ* is the Karma Division lord of the kidneys. The syllable *Hūṃ* is a conjugation of the syllable *Ha*: the seed of the wind-element of Mahāvairocana Tathāgata's eternal lifespan, the meaning of the three gates of liberation and the ungraspable nature of the three periods, the Dharma Body great power maṇḍala. Wind is sustained by the perception-aggregate mind. The five viscera are: liver, lungs, heart, spleen, and kidneys. The stomach is one of the six bowels. The stomach is the belly; grain belongs to the spleen-bowel. It is the ocean of the five viscera and six bowels. All water and grain enter the stomach, and the five viscera and six bowels all receive sustenance from it. The five flavors each flow to their destinations; bland flavors enter the stomach, and therefore the kidneys receive from the stomach. The kidneys are below the navel and waist: the left is called the kidney, the right is called the Gate of Life. The kidneys support the heart and abdomen, and at rest they release vital essence. The kidneys govern the will and correspond to the north and water. Water governs winter; its color is black. Among the five aggregates, the formation-aggregate mind sustains water. The formation-mind arises from the feeling-mind; the feeling-mind arises from perception. The kidneys arise from black energy and the lungs; they govern the ears. The kidneys extend outward as the bones and govern the marrow; marrow terminates as the ear-milk, and bones terminate as the teeth. Salty flavors enter the kidneys most readily, strengthening the kidneys but harming the heart. If the kidneys lack the will, one frequently weeps in sorrow. The spleen attacks the kidneys and causes disease, just as earth overcomes water. When the spleen is strong and the kidneys weak, one should settle the mind upon the spleen and use black energy to absorb yellow energy; then the kidney disease will be cured. Black energy is the syllable *Haṃ*.

"The syllable *Khaṃ* is the Space Division lord of the spleen. This syllable is Mahāvairocana Tathāgata's invisible crown-protuberance, the place of the great emptiness-wisdom realized by the five Buddhas, the supreme maṇḍala of the quiescent true suchness of principle and wisdom, the bodhi-site realized by all Buddhas of the ten directions and three times. The spleen governs the intellect and corresponds to the center and earth. Earth governs late summer; its color is yellow. Yellow arises from earth, earth arises from fire, as explained above; among the five aggregates, the consciousness-aggregate mind sustains earth. Or it may be considered a wood-viscera; the blue-green of wood is emptiness. The spleen arises from yellow energy and the heart; it governs the mouth and is the seat of the will. Sweet flavors enter the spleen most readily, strengthening the spleen but harming the kidneys. If the spleen lacks the intellect, one frequently becomes confused and disoriented. The liver attacks the spleen and causes disease, just as wood overcomes earth. When the liver is strong and the spleen weak, one should settle the mind upon the liver and use yellow energy to absorb blue-green energy; then the spleen disease will be cured. Yellow energy is the syllable *Khaṃ*. The five viscera are like lotus petals curving downward."

"The inner five viscera and the outer five elements emerge to form the physical body: this is 'name.' Form is the four great elements and five sense-faculties; name is the four aggregates of perception, formation, and so forth, which are mind. These are the sun and moon, the five planets, the twelve zodiacal mansions, and the twenty-eight constellations, forming the human body. Mountains, seas, and the great earth emerge from the syllable *A*. Rivers and streams emerge from the syllable *Vaṃ*. Gold, jade, precious jewels, sun, moon, stars, fire-pearls, and radiance are accomplished from the syllable *Raṃ*. The five grains and manifold flowers bloom and open because of the syllable *Haṃ*. Fragrant and beautiful realms, the nurturing of devas and humans, their complexion, savor, handsome appearance, merit, wealth, and nobility are adorned by the syllable *Khaṃ*. The syllable *A* is Akṣobhya Tathāgata of the east. The syllable *Vaṃ* is Amitābha Tathāgata of the west. The syllable *Raṃ* is Ratnasambhava Tathāgata of the south. The syllable *Haṃ* is Amoghasiddhi Tathāgata of the north. The syllable *Khaṃ* is Mahāvairocana Tathāgata above."

The meaning of the syllable *A* is profoundly deep: the body of empty quiescence. Grasping it, it cannot be grasped; releasing it, it cannot be released. The mother of the Dharma, the great abhiṣeka: this is the syllable *A*. The syllable *A* is a dharma difficult to believe. Hīnayāna Vinaya masters especially should not be allowed to see it. The original five-division Sanskrit text comprises four hundred thousand words, drawn from the *Mahāvairocana Sūtra* and the *Vajraśekhara Sūtra*, gathering the most essential and wondrous, the supreme field of merit: these five-syllable true words alone. The merit obtained by one who recites them is beyond comparison, beyond thought, beyond expression. The five-division true words of the Vajraśekhara Sūtra, when upheld, recited, and contemplated for their principle-nature, cause a person to obtain merit, with strong bones and a healthy body, forever free from calamity, obstacles, and all manner of illness. These five syllable-gates are the crest-jewels of the five wisdoms, the liver and heart of the five Buddhas, the quiescent wisdom-mother of all Buddhas of the ten directions and three times, the nurturing father and mother of all sentient beings, and the storehouse of the dharma-realm of the ten directions. Because they can, for the sake of the enemy-Māras of the three times, win the triple victory, they are like armor, halberds, shields, bows, arrows, and weapons. Therefore the Buddhas and bodhisattvas of the three times are immersed within them and are born from within them. The sprout is the seed.

Furthermore, the seat of this yoga is a golden vajra square wheel, which is the vajra seat.

*A*: the Vajra Earth Division. First, contemplate the syllable *A* as the earth, contemplating the vajra seat.

*Vaṃ*: the Vajra Water Division. Second, contemplate the syllable *Vaṃ* as water, contemplating the lotus.

*Raṃ*: the Vajra Fire Division. Third, contemplate the syllable *Raṃ* as the sun.

*Haṃ*: the Vajra Wind Division. Fourth, contemplate the syllable *Haṃ* as the moon.

*Khaṃ*: the Vajra Space Division. Fifth, contemplate the syllable *Khaṃ* as space.

Contemplate the unborn nature of the Tathāgata's essential body.

These five-division true words are the precious nectar of immortality of all Tathāgatas, the wondrous medicine of the clarified-butter buddha-nature. If a single syllable enters the five viscera, ten thousand diseases will not arise; how much more so when one cultivates the sun contemplation and moon contemplation, at which time one immediately realizes the Buddha-body of empty quiescence.

*A*, *Vaṃ*, *Raṃ*, *Haṃ*, *Khaṃ*: these five syllables above are the Dharma Body true words. If one recites them once daily, or seven times, or twenty-one times, or forty-nine times, the merit of a single recitation is equal to revolving the entire canon one million times. How much more so when one sits in meditative quiescence and enters the gate of samādhi! When one contemplates the syllable *A* with careful clarity, like the sun illuminating the sky, this is the clear contemplation of the buddha-nature. The merit obtained is beyond comparison. The passages of the secret treasury are truly inconceivable. I only fear that Śrāvaka Dharma-masters and Hīnayāna Vinaya-holders may give rise to doubt and disbelief, which would instead increase their transgressions. It is like a king who has a young child whom he loves most dearly: he freely empties his treasury of jewels and treasures without the slightest regret, but he alone will not give the child the swords Gan Jiang and Mo Ye, fearing the child does not know how to use them and will injure his own body. Therefore the Tathāgata secretly transmits this to great bodhisattvas and does not give it to śrāvakas of lesser wisdom.

*Dharma-dhātu*, Dharma Body Tathāgata, true reality. From the armpits to the crown is the upper portion, from the navel to the armpits is the middle portion, from the feet to the navel is the lower portion. Within the true words, one should distinguish the three kinds of siddhi accordingly.

**Lower Siddhi**: *A, Ra, Pa, Ca, Na*. This is called "emerging siddhi," capable of generating roots and stems that pervade the four directions. Reciting once equals revolving the entire canon one hundred times, immediately entering the equality of all dharmas of the Tathāgata. All written characters are also equal, and one swiftly accomplishes mahāprajñā. Reciting twice eradicates the grave transgressions of eons of birth-and-death. Mañjuśrī and Samantabhadra follow in attendance; the fourfold assembly surrounds one as guards. Compassionate and fearless dharma-protecting good spirits stand before this person. If one recites three times, samādhi manifests. If one recites four times, one attains complete retention (*dhāraṇī*) and never forgets. If one recites five times, one swiftly accomplishes unsurpassed bodhi.

**Middle Siddhi**: *A, Vi, Ra, Hūṃ, Khaṃ*. This is called "entering siddhi," capable of generating branches and leaves that pervade the four directions, with radiant splendor entering the Buddha's dharma-realm: this is called "entering siddhi." If one recites once, it equals revolving the entire canon one thousand times.

**Upper Siddhi**: *A, Vaṃ, Raṃ, Haṃ, Khaṃ*. This is called "secret siddhi," also called "accomplishment siddhi," also called *susiddhi*. *Susiddhi* means "pervading the dharma-realm." Accomplishing the Buddha-fruit and realizing great bodhi, the secret words of the dharma-realm fill all with radiance. Only a Buddha together with a Buddha can enter this gate. Pratyekabuddhas and śrāvakas cannot illumine it. This is also called "secret siddhi." If one recites once, it is equivalent to revolving the entire canon one million times.

The emerging siddhi extends from the feet to the waist. The entering siddhi extends from the navel to the heart. The secret siddhi extends from the heart to the crown. These three kinds of siddhi are: the emerging siddhi is the accomplishment of the Nirmāṇakāya, the entering siddhi is the accomplishment of the Saṃbhogakāya, and the secret siddhi, the *susiddhi*, is the accomplishment of the Dharmakāya. These are truly the three kinds of eternal body, the treasury of the true Dharma. The Dharmakāya Vairocana's fully endowed body, the true source of the five divisions and three divisions: therefore I pay homage to Vairocana Buddha. These three sets of five syllables make fifteen syllables, which are fifteen kinds of vajra samādhi. One syllable is fifteen syllables, and fifteen syllables are one syllable. One syllable is five syllables, and five syllables are one syllable. Forward and reverse, spinning and turning, beginning and end are non-dual. Now within these eight gates, all dharmas are encompassed. Within a single syllable, all syllables are contained; within all syllables, a single syllable is contained. With a single syllable, all syllables are explained; with all syllables, a single syllable is explained. With a single syllable, all syllables are established; with all syllables, a single syllable is established. With a single syllable, all syllables are demolished; with all syllables, a single syllable is demolished. This meaning is: forward once, reverse once, then forward rotation four times, then reverse rotation four times, benefiting all sentient beings in the ten-direction realms, all accomplishing the meaning of Buddhahood.

The *hṛdaya* heart of an ordinary person has a shape like a lotus flower, closed and not yet bloomed, with sinews and vessels binding it into eight segments: in men it faces upward, in women it faces downward. Contemplate this lotus flower, causing it to bloom open as an eight-petaled white lotus. On this platform, contemplate the syllable *A*, colored like vajra-gold. Then contemplate the syllable *A* as a square yellow altar, with one's body situated within it. From the syllable *A* produce the syllable *Ra*, and burn the body until it is entirely reduced to ash. From within this ash, the syllable *Va* arises, pure white in color. From this, produce the five wheel-syllables *A*, *Vaṃ*, *Raṃ*, *Haṃ*, *Khaṃ*, establishing them at five places on the body from waist below to crown above. This is called purifying the bodhicitta. Using these five syllable-gates as conditions, one generates the root of great compassion, and the Buddha's *śāla*-tree king grows and increases, spreading throughout the dharma-realm. Yet all dharmas, through these five syllable-gates, are fundamentally unborn, beyond speech, pure by nature, without cause or condition, and like empty space.

As for the eight-petal arrangement: from navel to heart is the vajra platform. The navel is the great ocean. Below the navel, this ground is where the assembled honored ones dwell, on the ocean shore. This means: from the ocean of great compassion of all Buddhas, vajra wisdom is born; from vajra wisdom, all Buddha-assemblies are produced.

On this heart's eight-petaled lotus platform, contemplate the syllable *A*. From this syllable, emit immeasurable light. From the center of the heart, it scatters in the four directions and gathers into a garland of light, like a flower garland, pervading all Buddha-fields. This light, from crown to feet, encircles the practitioner's body all around. Then contemplate the syllable *Āṃ* on the crown of the head. It transforms and becomes the central womb. From this syllable, three layers of light-flames are born. The first layer of light encircles above the throat, from throat to crown, and wherever it illumines, honored ones spontaneously appear, forming the first court maṇḍala. The next layer of light encircles above the heart, from navel upward to throat, and honored ones spontaneously appear, forming the second court maṇḍala. The next layer of light encircles above the navel, from navel downward, and honored ones spontaneously appear, forming the third court maṇḍala, which is the worldly deva court. The honored ones differ in form, color, and marks of excellence, each vividly complete within one's own body, as though one had personally entered the Buddha-assembly. One's own body entirely becomes a maṇḍala-body: this is the universal-gate dharma-realm body.

The central womb is Vairocana's own eight-petaled heart-lotus. On this heart-lotus platform is the maṇḍala's central womb, and the eight outer petals are also arranged according to the Buddha's positional sequence. The four directions are the Tathāgata's four wisdoms, and the four corner petals are the four methods of attraction. In the southeast, Samantabhadra is the bodhicitta, the wondrous cause. In the southwest, Mañjuśrī is great wisdom. In the northeast, Maitreya is great compassion. Great compassion and great mercy both belong to the second level of meaning. In the northwest, Avalokiteśvara is realization: the practice and vow are accomplished, entering this lotus-platform samādhi. Among the four directional petals: first, the syllable *A* in the east symbolizes the bodhicitta, which is foremost among all practices. Yellow is the vajra nature; this one is named Jewel Banner, also called Akṣobhya Buddha. Next, the syllable *Ā* in the south is practice; red signifies fire, sharing the meaning of Mañjuśrī. This is Flower Blooming, also called Ratnasambhava Buddha. Next, the syllable *Āṃ* in the west is bodhi: through myriad practices one accomplishes perfect awakening. White signifies the meaning of complete luminous ultimacy, and also means water. This Buddha is named Amitābha. Next, the syllable *Aḥ* in the north is the fruit of perfect awakening. This Buddha is named Drum-Sound; he is Śākyamuni. This is great nirvāṇa: the trace returns to the source, therefore nirvāṇa. The sun of the Buddha has already set behind the mountain of nirvāṇa, therefore its color is black.

Next, entering the center: the syllable *Aḥ* is skillful means. One then knows that the body of this heart's dharma-realm is fundamentally and eternally quiescent in nature. This is Vairocana's original-ground body, the lotus-platform's essential body, transcending the eight petals, beyond direction and location, not a realm accessible to conditioned mind. Only a Buddha together with a Buddha can know it. Through this skillful means, being the same as great space yet manifesting all forms, the central space possessing all colors: this is the empowered world. The universal-gate assembly of the maṇḍala exists everywhere. Yet it is simply the Tathāgata's one body, one wisdom, one practice. Therefore the eight petals are all the single body of Mahāvairocana. Therefore the central honored one, Mahāvairocana, is the Dharmakāya. The Lord of Secrets, Vajrasattva's wisdom-mudrā, is Prajñā. Avalokiteśvara's lotus-mudrā is Liberation. Thus body-mystery is the merit of the Dharmakāya, speech-mystery is the merit of Prajñā, and mind-mystery is the merit of Liberation. Through prajñā one attains liberation; liberation depends on prajñā. These two rely on the body of the Dharmakāya, neither identical nor separate, and neither can be lacking. They are like the three dots of the Sanskrit syllable *I*: the bodhicitta is the Vajra Division; great compassion is the Lotus Division; skillful means is the Response Body. Therefore the syllable *A* is inside the womb, indicating the stage of virtual awakening and before; the syllable *Sa* is outside the womb, indicating the stage of marvelous awakening; the syllable *Va* is function, upon which all dharmas turn and revolve, and through this gate the two kinds of transformation, manifest and free, save and deliver all ten realms.

This Tathāgata wisdom-mudrā is the true nature of mind, the fruit of all-knowledge wisdom. The bodhicitta is the cause, great compassion is the root, and skillful means is the ultimate. Through the gate of all-knowledge, the five kinds of meaning serve as conditions. If there are sentient beings who know this dharma-teaching, people in the world should make offerings to them as though reverencing a *caitya*. A *caitya* is where the bodily relics reside, and therefore all devas and worldly people who seek blessings and protection all make offerings to it. If a practitioner faithfully accepts this meaning, then they are where the Dharma Body relics reside. Furthermore, in Sanskrit, the word *caitya* shares the same base as *citta*; the secret meaning here is that the mind is the Buddha's stūpa. Just as the third maṇḍala takes one's own mind as foundation and builds up layer by layer to the central womb, with the nirvāṇa-color highest of all, therefore this *caitya* is extremely tall. And from the central womb's eight petals, building outward layer by layer to the third court, universally manifesting gate-bodies pervade everywhere without exception, therefore this *caitya* is extremely vast. The lotus platform is the *dharmadhātu*, the so-called Dharma Body relics. If sentient beings understand this mind-bodhi mudrā, they are identical with Vairocana. Therefore it says: "People of the world should make offerings as though reverencing a *caitya*."

Vairocana's body and land, dependent and direct retribution, interpenetrate perfectly; nature and characteristics are one and the same. True suchness pervading the dharma-realm is the great self. Body, speech, and mind are equal, like great empty space. Taking empty space as the bodhi-site and the dharma-realm as the seat, Mahāvairocana Tathāgata, in order to enable beings to know and see this path, manifests two kinds of Dharma Body: the Wisdom Dharma Body Buddha, dwelling in the principle of true characteristics, for self-enjoyment, manifests thirty-seven honored ones, causing all to enter the path of non-duality; the Principle Dharma Body Buddha, dwelling in the dharma of suchness-as-it-is and quiescent illumination, yet ever abiding unmoved, manifests upon the eight petals, for both self- and other-enjoyment, manifesting the three-layered maṇḍala, causing all ten realms to realize great emptiness. Although there are distinctions of principle and wisdom, of broad and condensed, fundamentally they are a single dharma with no difference whatsoever. The ten thousand dharmas return to the single syllable *A*; the five divisions are one and the same Vairocana.

> I rely upon Vairocana Buddha,
> Opening the wisdom-mudrā of the mind, establishing its meaning.
> Immeasurable merits universally adorn all,
> Entering together into the dhāraṇī of all Sugatas.
> May we, together with all who have the affinity to study and practice,
> Abide in peace in the unsurpassed ocean of purity.
