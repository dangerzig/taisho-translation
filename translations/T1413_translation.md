# 佛說尊勝大明王經
## Sūtra on the Victorious Great Vidyārāja Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1413

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dānapāla (施護).

---

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed Ānanda: "Ānanda! There is a Victorious Great Vidyārāja that was proclaimed by all the buddhas of the past. Ānanda! Seven koṭis of tathāgatas also rejoiced in proclaiming it. I now proclaim and reveal it again for all sentient beings of the future. You should listen carefully. Ānanda! If good men or good women hear this Victorious Great Vidyā and wish to receive, uphold, read, and recite it, they must first purify body and mind, taking refuge in all buddhas, all honored Dharma, and all noble saṃgha. They should further take refuge in the Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva of Great Compassion, Avalokiteśvara, and also take refuge in the protector-lord of the sahā world, and all vidyādhara devas. Having taken refuge in these buddhas, bodhisattvas, and the noble saṃgha, they may then recite." He then proclaimed the Victorious Great Vidyārāja:

*tadyathā dhara dhara dhara dhara dhara dhara dhara dhiri dhiri dhiri dhiri dhiri dhiri dhiri dhuru dhuru dhuru dhuru dhuru dhuru dhuru suru suru suru suru suru suru suru mara mara mara mara mara mara mara mara miri miri miri miri miri miri miri miri muru muru muru muru muru muru muru muru* [Protect me and all sentient beings!] *hili hili hili hili hili hili hili hili hili huru huru huru huru huru huru huru huru huru kili kili kili kili kili kili kili kili kili mili mili mili mili mili mili mili mili mili cili cili cili cili cili cili cili cili cili cili curu curu curu curu curu curu curu curu curu curu sarva-bhūta-pratisedhaṃ karomi svāhā*

"All enemies who generate evil minds, those of the most extreme venom and harm, who wish to inflict evil dharmas upon me or other sentient beings, causing various diseases or obstructions, blocking the eyes from seeing, the ears from hearing, the nose from discerning scent, the mouth from speaking, and binding all the limbs of the body: whether those enemies act themselves, cause others to act, or even merely rejoice in such acts, all shall be destroyed."

*ṭuru ṭuru ṭuru ṭuru svāhā*

"All sprites, phantoms, vetāla-whirlwind ghosts, and all such ghosts and spirits who wish to generate evil minds and act harmfully shall retreat and scatter to the four directions, departing far away."

*cara cara svāhā mara mara svāhā miri miri svāhā dhuru dhuru svāhā*

"All humans and non-humans who generate evil minds and attempt to bind me or other sentient beings, causing madness, confusion, or daze, whether already done or about to be done, shall all be dispelled and released."

*nile nilā-keśe svāhā pete petā-keśe svāhā rohite rohitā-keśe svāhā avadate avadatā-keśe svāhā śvete śvetā-vastra-dhāraṇīye svāhā*

"The terror of kings, the terror of bandits, the terror of water and fire, the terror of ḍākinīs, the terror of piśācas, the terror of kumbhāṇḍas, the terror of kaṭapūtanas, the terror of shadow-ghosts, and all such terrors of evil ghosts and spirits: whether traveling on a road or in the wilderness, by day or by night, one will always be safe. Furthermore, when enemies see one's face, their enmity will be dispelled; when they hear one's words, their enmity will be dispelled. They will be like close relatives meeting with joy."

*tadyathā huru huru niki nikiri nikirini svāhā*

"All evil spells are severed and destroyed. They either burn to ashes of their own accord, or harm themselves, or confound themselves, or their stakes and pegs topple and collapse, or they vanish and become invisible, or spells cast in the sky naturally fall, or written spells are obstructed and rendered ineffective. Even the realm of Yama trembles in terror."

*tadyathā saṭa saṭa phaṭ phaṭ phaṭ phaṭ phaṭ bhuru bhuru muru muru svāhā*

"If māras or māra-folk approach with evil intent, or if Maheśvara devas, yakṣas, yakṣiṇīs, or any beings that feed on blood, flesh, fat, vital energy, or saliva seize or bind a person, all are released. Even at the time of conception, dwelling in the womb, and first birth, all will be safe and peaceful."

"Ānanda! This Victorious Great Vidyārāja is the mother of the world. All sentient beings are her children. Ānanda! If good men or good women hear this Victorious Great Vidyā and can receive, uphold, read, and recite it, all the karmic offenses of that person from beginningless time will be extinguished. All auspiciousness will increase. Those who see them will be filled with joy, and all their undertakings will succeed. Furthermore, if good men or good women write this vidyārāja on paper or silk and always wear it on the crown of the head or the arm, all that is inauspicious will be kept far away. Even in dreams, no evil visions will appear. Fire cannot burn them, water cannot sweep them away, and all evils cannot harm them. Furthermore, if one scoops up water and empowers it by reciting this vidyārāja, and then draws it into the nose, all latent karma residing in the body will also be extinguished. One's skillfulness will increase and one will always obtain peace and happiness. All evil spells, curses, and harmful acts can be dispelled by empowering pure water, thread, or white mustard seeds with this vidyārāja, and they will retreat of their own accord. Ānanda! I have already told you that this great vidyārāja was proclaimed in unison by the tathāgatas of the past, seven koṭis in number. I now proclaim it again for sentient beings of the future who are without merit and without virtue, who are troubled and disturbed by various evil ghosts and cannot abide in peace. You should receive and uphold it well, and cause it to be transmitted without interruption in the future, serving as a refuge for all sentient beings."

At that time, when the World-Honored One had proclaimed this Victorious Great Vidyārāja, the Venerable Ānanda joyfully accepted it with faith, bowed to the Buddha, and withdrew.
