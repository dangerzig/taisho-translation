# 佛說最上意陀羅尼經
## Sūtra of the Supreme-Intention Dhāraṇī, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1408

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dānapāla (施護).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the city of Dove-Rescue, in the Ox-Head Sandalwood Vihāra, together with a great assembly of bhikṣus, as well as devas, nāgas, and the eight classes of beings, who reverently surrounded him and gazed upon him as they dwelt.

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed Ānanda and the great assembly: "I observe that in the southern continent of Jambudvīpa, in the age of the declining Dharma, all sentient beings, because of meager merit, will be troubled by evil ghosts and spirits who create various calamities and difficulties, tormenting sentient beings and preventing them from being at peace. There is a dhāraṇī that can extinguish these and also increase auspiciousness. I shall now proclaim it. Listen carefully."

Ānanda and the great assembly, receiving the Buddha's instruction, listened in silence.

The Buddha said: "Ānanda, in a past age, in this Jambudvīpa there was a bhikṣu named Transmission-of-the-Teaching. On the fifteenth day of the dark fortnight of the ninth month, he was traveling northward, not far from the kingdom of China, about four yojanas distant, when suddenly along the road he saw a divine being standing at a distance. This divine being was thirty cubits tall, with a face four cubits in circumference.

"The bhikṣu Transmission-of-the-Teaching saw this divine being, magnificent in stature and with a warm, joyful countenance. Examining him carefully, he realized this was none other than the youth Mañjuśrī himself. The bhikṣu approached the divine being, placed his five limbs on the ground, and extended full prostrations. Rising from his obeisance, he joined his palms, gazed up, and addressed the divine being: 'How is it that the youth Mañjuśrī manifests such a form? I now know with certainty that this is none other than the sage himself.'

"The divine being told the bhikṣu: 'It is as you say. I am Mañjuśrī.'

"The bhikṣu again addressed the divine being: 'Why do you manifest this form today?'

"The divine being told the bhikṣu: 'Do you not know? The southern continent of Jambudvīpa is about to experience calamities and difficulties. Sentient beings of meager merit will contract various diseases and suffer all manner of torment.'

"The bhikṣu said: 'Why will such calamities suddenly befall Jambudvīpa?'

"The divine being said: 'On the southern face of Mount Sumeru, there are asuras who have given rise to evil intentions and, leading their retinues, sought battle with the heavenly hosts. The asuras blocked the sun, moon, and stars, so that their light did not appear. Furthermore, immeasurable extremely evil yakṣas, gandharvas, garuḍas, and others dwelling on the southern face of Mount Sumeru, seeing the asuras manifest these signs, also joined their side. The heavenly hosts were defeated, and the asuras were victorious.

"'Thereupon, in southern Jambudvīpa, the light of the sun, moon, and stars was suddenly concealed and hidden. Various inauspicious signs appeared everywhere. Winds and rains came at the wrong times; droughts and floods occurred simultaneously. The five grains and seedlings blossomed but bore no fruit; even if there were seeds, the food had no flavor. This caused sentient beings' complexion, strength, and majestic radiance to all diminish.

"'Furthermore, earth-dwelling piśācas and other evil ghost-classes changed their original forms and manifested strange appearances: some as lions, tigers, and wolves; some as dogs; and some as female forms. They tormented sentient beings, causing them to contract various diseases and suffer. These include goiter, wind-diseases, phlegm and vomiting diseases, eye diseases, headaches, stomachaches, and even hemorrhoid and fistula diseases. There are also malarial fevers: some recurring daily, some every other day, some momentarily, and some lasting a month. Some suffered from sores and ringworm; some from scabies and leprosy. All manner of evil diseases spread throughout Jambudvīpa, causing sentient beings to suffer extreme torment. Those of meager merit had no escape. Whether elderly, middle-aged, young, or children, because of this pestilence, many died prematurely.

"'Bhikṣu, it is because of this that I feel compassion and sorrow. Therefore I manifest this form to make it known. Bhikṣu, I now tell you: if there are sentient beings who, for themselves and their families, and even for their villages and the entire nation, wish to dispel pestilence and increase auspicious merit, they should first deeply generate faith and reverence for the Three Treasures. Then they should prepare fine flowers and precious fruits, burning incense and anointing incense, and all manner of offerings to make their offerings. They should with resolute mind not eat for seven days, and eight times daily, four during the day and four during the night, with courageous and focused attention, recite the Supreme-Intention Dhāraṇī. The calamities and difficulties will certainly be extinguished, and all auspicious merit will be increased.

"'Bhikṣu, if one wishes to recite the Supreme-Intention Dhāraṇī, one should first bow one's head and recite the name: Homage to the Tathāgata King of Vast, Profound Wisdom's Resounding Roar. Having recited this name, one should then recite the dhāraṇī.'

"The divine being then proclaimed the Supreme-Intention Dhāraṇī for the bhikṣu Transmission-of-the-Teaching:

*[Dhāraṇī]*

*Tadyathā vipuragārīṃ te vipulaśvāri vipulayuniśe aragate svāhā. Namo sarvanivāraṇaviṣkambhine tathāgatāya. Tadyathā hima hima mahāhima svāhā. Namo guṇākarāya tathāgatāya. Tadyathā gaganākāri gaganasaṃbhave gaganakīrtite svāhā. Namo ratnadagatāya namo tathāgatāya. Tadyathā ratnodgate ratnasaṃbhave svāhā. Namo mahāmitasarvamini tathāgatāya. Tadyathā amala mahī svāhā. Namo mānasatambhāya tathāgatāya. Tadyathā mānasatvaṃ śuddhapadma sarīpadmasaṃbhave karihita kāraye svāhā. Namo sarvabuddhabodhisattvānāṃ. Namo āryamañjuśrīya. Tadyathā jaye jayabuddhi hima hima mahāhima svāhā. Namo āryāvalokiteśvarāya. Tadyathā gaganādhāye gaganavikrāntadhmī svāhā. Namo āryasamantabhadrāya tathāgatāya. Tadyathā bhadri mahābhadri amṛtabhadri vigatarājaśe svāhā. Namo āryavajradhipataye tāya. Tadyathā kīrtitasarvadhmibhi atikīrtitasarvadhmibhi vajrasaṃbhave vajrabhidanakāya svāhā. Namo sarvabuddhabodhisattvānāṃ. Tadyathā śvetakārbhi jvāraniye svāhā. Satambha nimoha nipañcanī puṇḍarikāya svāhā. Mahānanti nandabhaniye svāhā. Nuranudurīye svāhā. Ravikṛte svāhā. Śāntisvenivāpanīye svāhā. Laṃmodarīye kalāpāśe kīrtita svāhā. Ajananividhamanīye svāhā. Dhātukāraye svāhā. Meghakāraye svāhā. Māravantiye svāhā. Bodhiyuṅga nantiye svāhā. Karmasādhanikāya svāhā. Ekaramāya svāhā. Āyugacāriṇīye svāhā. Brahmamopaghūḍhāya svāhā. Sarvakarmāvaraṇabhiṣiṣṭaktāya svāhā. Sarvabuddhabhisaṃskṛtāya svāhā. Ekasiliṅgāya svāhā. Asiliṅgāya svāhā. Bhūtāya svāhā. Abhūtāya svāhā. Sarvānugupasaṃmāya svāhā. Mama sarvānāṃ ca svāhā.*

"The divine being, having spoken the Supreme-Intention Dhāraṇī, told the bhikṣu Transmission-of-the-Teaching: 'You should now take this dhāraṇī and throughout the ten directions of southern Jambudvīpa, in every nation, city, and village, transmit and proclaim it, causing all sentient beings to hear and know it, and causing this dhāraṇī to be widely disseminated. Why? Because it can increase the merit of sentient beings, because it can remove the sufferings and torments of sentient beings, because it can eliminate the calamities and difficulties of the world, because it can cause all evil ghosts and spirits to extinguish their evil intentions, and because it can cause the kings, ministers, and people of every nation, city, and village to all attain peace, stability, and happiness.

"'Bhikṣu, if good men or good women wish to reverently make offerings, receive, uphold, read, and recite this dhāraṇī in order to extinguish calamities and increase merit, they should generate a most sincere mind and take refuge in the Three Treasures. For seven days, they should each day prepare various incenses, flowers, food, and drink as offerings to the Buddha. They should also each day provide for seven monks. At midnight, in their own courtyard, they should light a great torch for illumination and recite this dhāraṇī. All calamities will be resolved and liberation obtained.

"'Bhikṣu, if good men and good women can do this, they will attain liberation for themselves.

"'Bhikṣu, if for fourteen days they similarly prepare various incenses, flowers, food, and drink, making offerings to the Buddha and saṃgha double the previous amount, that person's parents will also attain liberation.

"'Bhikṣu, if for twenty-one days they similarly, with doubled offerings of various incenses, flowers, food, and drink, reverently make offerings to the Buddha, Dharma, and Saṃgha, that person's sons, daughters, and all family members will also attain liberation.

"'Bhikṣu, if for twenty-eight days they similarly cultivate and uphold this practice, doubling it beyond the previous with unwavering devotion, all the people of that person's village will attain liberation.

"'Bhikṣu, to summarize: if for up to forty-nine days, with offerings ever increasing beyond the previous, they make offerings to the Buddha, Dharma, and Saṃgha, the merit that person obtains will extend from the king above to the people below, so that everyone within the nation's borders will attain universal liberation.

"'Bhikṣu, know that I now tell you: take this Dharma and in this realm and other realms, swiftly transmit and proclaim it so it may be widely disseminated. Why? Because it can eliminate all manner of suffering and torment for sentient beings, and increase all manner of merit.

"'Bhikṣu, if there is a person who, having heard this Dharma and knowing its benefits, does not show it reverence, does not broadly proclaim it for others, and also does not personally and diligently read and recite it: bhikṣu, know that this person incurs the transgression of concealing the Dharma, equivalent to the five heinous crimes.

"'Bhikṣu, if there are sentient beings who hear this Dharma and do not transgress my teaching but personally practice according to the Dharma, receiving, upholding, reading, reciting, making offerings, and showing reverence, and who also practice meritorious cultivation for others according to the Dharma, and who can disseminate it to broadly benefit sentient beings: bhikṣu, I shall know from afar. First, on behalf of this person, I shall go to Mount Sumeru, to the devas, humans, asuras, gandharvas, and garuḍas, and with a mind of benefit, subdue and exhort them, teaching the Dharma and causing all to rejoice. I shall cause those devas, humans, asuras, and others to extinguish their contentious minds and show mutual reverence. Then the calamities of the Jambudvīpa realm will be extinguished, pestilence will not arise, all evil ghosts will retreat into hiding, and all sentient beings will attain peace and happiness.'

"The divine being, having spoken these matters, vanished from sight. The bhikṣu Transmission-of-the-Teaching reverently transmitted and proclaimed it."

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the great assembly: "The youth Mañjuśrī, in the past, through the power of skillful means, spoke such a Dharma for sentient beings. I now proclaim it again for all of you. Receive, uphold, read, and recite it with focused mind, and in the future, cause it to be widely disseminated, eliminating calamities and increasing auspiciousness."

Ānanda and the great assembly, having heard the Buddha's teaching, were all filled with joy. With focused mind they received and upheld it, bowed to the Buddha, and withdrew.
