# 大乘四齋日
## The Four Mahāyāna Fast Days

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 2849

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown (失譯)

---

The [four special fast days are]: the 8th day of the 2nd month, the 8th day of the 4th month, the 8th day of the 5th month, and the 15th day of the 7th month.

The three long fasting months of the year: the 1st month, the 5th month, and the 9th month.

The six fast days [each month]: the 8th, 14th, 15th, 23rd, 28th, and 30th.

The ten fast days [each month]. On the 1st day of the month, the Lads of Good and Evil descend to take note. On the 14th, the Life-Examiners and Record-Watchers descend. On the 15th, the Great God of the Five Paths descends. On the 18th, Yama King descends. On the 23rd, the Heavenly Great General descends. On the 24th, Śakra descends. On the 28th, the General of Taishan Prefecture descends. On the 29th, the Four Heavenly Kings descend. On the 30th, the Heavenly Bureau and the Earth Office descend.

On the 1st, the Lad descends to take note. Contemplate Dīpaṃkara Tathāgata Buddha. By observing this fast, sins are eliminated; for forty kalpas one will not fall into the Hell of Swords and Spears.

On the 8th, the Prince descends. Contemplate Bhaiṣajyaguru Vaiḍūryaprabhā Buddha. By observing this fast, sins are eliminated; for thirty kalpas one will not fall into the Hell of Pulverized Grass.

On the 14th, the Life-Examiner descends. Contemplate the Thousand Buddhas of the Bhadrakalpa. By observing this fast, sins are eliminated; for one thousand kalpas one will not fall into the Hell of Boiling Cauldrons.

On the 15th, the Great General of the Five Paths descends. Contemplate Amitābha Buddha. By observing this fast, sins are eliminated; for two hundred kalpas one will not fall into the Hell of Cold Ice.

On the 18th, Yama King descends. Contemplate Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva. By observing this fast, sins are eliminated; for ninety kalpas one will not fall into the Hell of Sword Trees.

On the 23rd, the Heavenly Great General descends. Contemplate Vairocana Buddha. By observing this fast, sins are eliminated; for one thousand kalpas one will not fall into the Hell of Hungry Ghosts.

On the 24th, the Lord of Taishan Prefecture descends. Contemplate Kṣitigarbha Bodhisattva. By observing this fast, sins are eliminated; for ninety kalpas one will not fall into the Hell of Instantaneous Cutting.

On the 28th, Śakra, Lord of Heaven, descends. Contemplate Amitābha Buddha. By observing this fast, sins are eliminated; for one thousand kalpas one will not fall into the Hell of Iron Saws.

On the 29th, the Four Heavenly Kings descend. Contemplate Bhaiṣajyarāja and Bhaiṣajyasamudgata Bodhisattvas. By observing this fast, sins are eliminated; for seven thousand kalpas one will not fall into the Hell of the Grinding Millstone.

On the 30th, the Great Brahma King descends. Contemplate Śākyamuni Buddha. By observing this fast, sins are eliminated; for eight thousand kalpas one will not fall into the Hell of Cold Ice.

---

**Translator's notes:**

- **Structure:** The text presents a layered liturgical calendar: four annual special days, three long fasting months, six monthly fast days, and the ten monthly fast days (the 1st, 8th, 14th, 15th, 18th, 23rd, 24th, 28th, 29th, 30th) with associated devotional practices.

- **Textual discrepancy:** The summary list of the ten fast days assigns the 24th to Śakra (帝釋) and the 28th to the General of Taishan Prefecture (太山府軍), but the detailed benefits section reverses this, assigning the 24th to the Lord of Taishan Prefecture and the 28th to Śakra (天帝釋). This inconsistency appears to be a scribal error in the received text; both sections are translated as they stand.

- **定光如來佛** (Dīpaṃkara Tathāgata): An alternate Chinese rendering of the Buddha Dīpaṃkara (the "Lamp-Lighter"), typically rendered 然燈佛.

- **盧舍那佛** (Vairocana): Transliteration of Sanskrit *Locana/Rocana*, the "adornment body" Vairocana of the *Brahma Net Sūtra*, here rendered as Vairocana for clarity.

- **餓鬼地獄** ("Hell of Hungry Ghosts"): The text anomalously conflates the preta realm (hungry ghosts) with the hell realm (naraka), a doctrinal irregularity reflecting the syncretic popular Buddhist milieu of this text.

- **纔截地獄** ("Hell of Instantaneous Cutting"): Literally "the hell of just/barely cutting off," likely referring to a hell where beings are repeatedly severed.
