# 布薩文等
## Poṣadha Texts and Other Liturgies

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 2853

Author unknown.

---

All buddhas manifested in the world, transforming beings through skillful means. The multitudes looked up in reverence, and delusion and confusion vanished. Although the Dharma is expounded through the three vehicles, the precepts differ in their opening and closing according to whether faculties are sharp or dull. Yet all who see and hear equally receive surpassing benefit. This is the occasion of our assembly. The instructing ācārya serves as the leader who exhorts us upon the Way. He causes the true wind to blow far and wide and the Sun of the Buddha to shine again, so that within this excellent land, one may extend the merit of hearing. At this time, the jeweled ground is opened and the golden banner is raised. Fragrant incense and auspicious chanting join with the music of pipes and songs in harmonious resonance. The tallying-sticks proclaim liberation; we receive and uphold them upon our heads. The precept-name "guarding against wrong" deepens the treasury of bodhi and receives the sweet dew. Thus we draw forth the fragrant blossoms of the tree of awakening and bring to fruition the flavorful fruits of the garden of incense. Each and every one of these is first used as adornment. We pray that Brahmā, Śakra, the kings, nāgas, devas, and the eight classes of beings may have their bodily radiance increased and their holy power enhanced. May they silently keep in mind all sentient beings and support this Dharma-teaching. May yin and yang be in proper order, wind and rain be harmonious, harvests be abundant, and the people be content in their livelihoods. Furthermore, may the surpassing merit adorn the Holy Divine Zanpu. We humbly pray that his lifespan may equal the sacred stone and his life match the numinous cedar. May his officials and ministers be well, enveloped in boundless blessing. May the senior worthies have merit to sustain all sentient beings. Thereafter, may weapons and blades be forever abolished and the Dharma-teaching circulate freely. May all sentient beings together ascend the path of awakening.

### Poṣadha Text

The Dharma-king manifested in response, his awesome power shaking the great thousandfold world. The flourishing of the Dharma-teaching, its propagation and spread, is our duty. How much more so the proclamation of the precept-treasury: twice each month, we diligently observe it without transgression, bringing merit to sustain the family and the nation. Thereupon, the banner and bell summon the assembly, the brahma-chant invites the saṅgha. Incense rises through the five clouds; pennants gleam in manifold colors. All these many virtues and limitless good conditions are used as adornment. We pray that the celestial immortals of the upper realms, nāgas, spirits, and the eight classes may silently guard with their awesome power and invisibly bestow their holy virtue. May the sun and moon shine with constant clarity. May yin and yang be in proper order. May gentle winds come in due season and sweet rains fall at the proper time. May the people of the four directions have the joy of secure borders. May there be no cause for worry across ten thousand li throughout the endless years. We pray that the Dharma may blow forever, the Buddha's teaching spread wide. May all sentient beings together ascend the path of awakening.

### Poṣadha Text

I humbly observe that in this region beyond the flowing sands, the black-robed monastics number in the hundreds. That one is able to hold high the torch of wisdom and raise the banner of the Dharma, propagating the teaching in the present age and establishing a bridge for ages to come: is this not what we call our instructing master? Therefore he is able to cause the vinaya rules of the twofold assembly to be diligently maintained without weariness, and the three paths to continue in unbroken succession. The method of poṣadha washes clean the cage of afflictions. The merit of upholding the Dharma perpetuates the light for all beings, both moving and still. We pray that this boundless merit may first be used as adornment for Brahmā, Śakra, the four kings, nāgas, devas, and the eight classes. May their merit and virtue increase ever more, may their awesome radiance grow ever more splendid. May epidemics be dispelled and living beings be benefited and given joy. May the three borders be free from the anxiety of strange portents. May the hundred grains bring the joy of abundant harvest. We further dedicate this merit to adorn the Holy Divine Zanpu. We humbly pray that his brightness may equal Shun's domain and accord with Yao and Tang. May he extend benevolence and kindness to the eight directions and care for the common people as his own children. We next dedicate this merit to adorn our Jie'er, the supreme counselor. We humbly pray that his glory may exceed that of past years and his blessings increase in the present. We further dedicate this to adorn the Military Governor Duke She. May his blessings grow year by year and his longevity exceed metal and stone. Thereafter, may it sprinkle and spread throughout the dharma-realm, reaching universally to all sentient beings.

### Text for the Conclusion of a Seasonal Assembly

The Sun of the Buddha sheds its light: no calamity that it does not dispel. The flow of prajñā: no blessing that it does not bring. Now we have opened the treasure-house of the eight nāga kings, unrolled the true texts of the five regions of India, assembled two hundred for the wheel of brahma-chanting, and over thirty days completed the recitation. This is for our present Holy Divine Zanpu: the occasion for his merit is herewith set forth. We humbly submit that the Holy Divine Zanpu surpasses in the Way the righteous sovereigns of old, his accomplishments exceeding Yao and Shun. Grasping the charts and governing the calendar, holding the register and rectifying the times, he has purified and pacified all within the eight directions. He has broadly promoted the ten wholesome deeds, and the household has been especially elevated; the wondrous merit stands especially tall. He has caused the seasonal assemblies to be established, hoping the myriad undertakings may endure for all time. Thus, from the capital court to the distant reaches of Dunhuang, year after year these practices are cultivated as a constant observance. This gathering marks the completion of the spring season. On this day, the rice fields first turn green and the peach orchards blush with red. The jade-colored banners approach their end, and the golden words declare the conclusion. May all this surpassing and boundless merit first be used as adornment.

Transformative response is without fixed direction; spiritual function is tireless. Its kindness nourishes all beings, both moving and still; its blessings pervade all living creatures. The highest among the heavenly ones alone undertakes this work. As for its wondrous affairs, how could excessive words suffice? The arising of this auspicious occasion surely has its reason. Now, in the middle month of the three spring months, at the beginning of the four seasons, catkins burst on the southern branches and ice opens on the northern banks. We spread abroad the transformative traces of the Dharma-king, hoping to avert a thousand calamities. We proclaim the glory of the Great Sage's words, aspiring to reach ten thousand virtues. Thereupon, not confined to the moonlit hall, at night we strike the frosty bell, gathering the assembly of monastics. Vying to bear pennants and canopies, they form a grand procession at the four gates, circling the capital of the entire prefecture. The golden countenance of the image is set forth; it is as if the clouds were not above Vulture Peak. The ūrṇā opens like a lunar halo; flowers mark the path to the lotus palace. The whole marketplace and the whole city are moved; mountains shake and valleys resound. At Kapilavastu, the weariness of desire: did it require a year? Our hope is that the four heavenly kings guard the world, and a hundred blessings are silently bestowed. May comets be swept from the gates of heaven. May epidemics hide beneath the gates of the earth. May auspicious clouds display their good colors, gathering in the sky. May weapons be turned upside down, culture cultivated and warfare stilled. All this merit we dedicate to the nāgas, devas, and the eight classes. May their awesome radiance be ever glorious and their spiritual power without limit. May disasters not arise and calamities not occur. Furthermore, we dedicate this surpassing merit to the Holy Divine Zanpu. May ten thousand nations bring tribute and the four seas come to court. May the precious calendar endure forever and his longevity rival metal and stone. We further dedicate this surpassing merit to adorn the Jie'er and the Military Governor. May their lifespans extend ever further and their emoluments reach ten thousand measures. May the tribal envoys and the various officials establish loyalty and uprightness in this sagely age. Thereafter, may it sprinkle and spread throughout the dharma-realm, reaching universally to all sentient beings. Relying on this surpassing cause, may all ascend to the fruition of bliss.

### City-Procession Text

How great is the beneficial manifestation of our Dharma-king! Descending from Tuṣita Heaven, he cast his reflection in the Royal City. Observing the wondrous forms, he saw that suffering is like boils and sores. Recognizing the precious abode, he then crossed beyond the palace walls. Conquering the four māras, he ascended to right awakening. Dwelling in the three realms, he alone is called the Honored One. His spiritual transformations are beyond measure and cannot be fully described. Now, in this spring month, the earth opens and the shoots sprout. The birds hold the sounds of spring; the wind sways. Thereupon, the Nairañjanā park is thrown wide open and the lotus palace thoroughly revealed. The golden form shines resplendent at the four crossroads; the silver ūrṇā radiates to the eight directions. In solemn and stately procession, it is as if circling the city of the White Rice King. Lofty and majestic, it is as if circling the ramparts of Kapilavastu. High and low all gather; great and small assemble like clouds. Pipes and songs compete, playing the Dharma-melody. Offerings vie with one another, and our hope is that a hundred blessings may descend. May a thousand obstacles be dispelled like clouds. Beholding the surpassing marks, may we gain their cause; gazing upon the wondrous form, may we produce merit. All these many virtues and limitless good conditions we first use as adornment for Śakra, Brahmā, the four kings, nāgas, devas, and the eight classes. May their awesome power increase and their supernatural abilities grow. May they drive out noxious vapors and dispel epidemics. We next dedicate this surpassing merit to adorn our present Holy Divine Zanpu. We humbly pray that his sacred person may be strong and enduring, as the sun goes and the moon comes. May the precious abode be everlastingly prosperous, as enduring as heaven and earth. May the Jie'er and Military Governor have lifespans rivaling the pines. May blessings and prosperity sustain one another. May the tribal envoys and the various officials assist in governance with harmony, purity, and uprightness. Thereafter, may the four seasons be in order, the five grains flourish. May a hundred calamities be removed and ten thousand auspicious signs gather. The prajñā dhāraṇī is the teacher of all buddhas. May the great assembly of the entire city all recite it universally.

### City-Procession Text

Responsive transformation is without end; spiritual function is tireless. Its kindness nourishes all beings, both moving and still; its blessings pervade all living creatures. The highest among the heavenly ones alone masters its power. As for its wondrous affairs, how could excessive words suffice? The auspicious sign of this assembly surely has its reason. Now, as the four seasons draw to a close and the three months of spring begin, we bid farewell to the old and welcome the new, dispelling calamity and establishing merit. Our hope is to purify the realm's borders and preserve the teeming multitude. Thereupon, not confined to the moonlit hall, at night we strike the frosty bell, gathering the assembly of monastics. Bringing forth all pennants and flowers, we bring out the buddha-image at the four gates and circle the doubled city walls once around. Solemn in marks and excellences, it is as if the clouds above Vulture Peak were not there. Resplendent in ūrṇā-radiance, the lotus peak descends its steps. The whole city and the whole marketplace are stirred; valleys resound and mountains shake. At Śrāvastī, the crossing beyond the city: did it require a year? Our hope is that the four heavenly kings guard the world and a hundred blessings are silently bestowed. May comets be swept from the gates of heaven. May epidemics hide beneath the gates of the earth. May the manifold clouds spread their joyous colors, gathering in the sky. May weapons be turned upside down, culture cultivated and warfare stilled. All this merit we dedicate to the nāgas, devas, and the eight classes. May their awesome radiance be ever glorious and their spiritual power without limit. May disasters not arise and calamities not occur. Furthermore, we dedicate this excellent merit to the Holy Divine Zanpu. May ten thousand nations bring tribute and the four seas come to court. May the precious calendar endure forever and his longevity rival metal and stone. May His Highness the Crown Prince resound like thunder far and near, and the lesser sea be forever clear. May the Consort's orchid and osmanthus be forever fragrant. May the ladies of the inner court offer hymns of divine flowers. We further dedicate this surpassing merit to adorn our Military Governor Duke Du in all ways. May his emoluments reach ten thousand measures. Thereafter...

### Text for Military Sūtra-Recitation

All buddhas arise in compassion: without conditions, they prepare universally for sentient beings. Those who perceive different marks respond according to their conditions. Therefore, one who turns toward them has blessings following their aspirations; one who shows disrespect has misfortune arising in accordance with their mind. Thus we know that misfortune and blessings come from oneself, not from any love or hatred of the sages. Now, the purpose of this assembly's sūtra-recitation is as follows: our Eastern Army's State Minister, the Counselor Chebo, reverently builds merit for the generals and officers on the western campaign. We humbly submit that the State Minister is one whom heaven has sent down with outstanding spirit and the earth has endowed with surpassing energy. He is a bulwark like the sacred mountains, with stratagems laid out at his seat. Repeatedly, the brave warriors of the north have raised armies and invaded the borders, plundering people and livestock, ravaging fields and crops. The people are unable to dwell in peace, and the alarm beacons are frequently raised. Our State Minister, stirred to sudden wrath, his hair standing on end, has selected fine officers and dispatched a force to campaign in the west. Although his planning is as clear as the palm of his hand, with not one matter overlooked, nevertheless he must rely on the aid of merit to ensure safety and auspiciousness. Therefore he has sent afar to the companions beyond the Three Perils, praying from a distance upon the texts of the eight treasuries. His hope is that the soldiers and horses be at peace and the family and nation be forever tranquil. Therefore, with utmost sincerity and single-minded purpose, we chant the *Vajracchedikā*. The orderly assembly of monks and nuns proclaims the prajñā. One can only imagine this surpassing excellence: what could be added to it? First, we use it as adornment for the four world-guarding kings, nāga spirits, and the eight classes. May their awesome radiance be splendid and their power of merit increase. May the western formations command awe and the northern barbarians submit in allegiance. We further dedicate it to adorn the military generals and ministers. We humbly pray that their talent and wisdom grow daily and their merit accumulate like mountains. May their lifespans extend far and may they be firmly established in the highest office. May all the generals and officers be protected by the Three Jewels and adorned by ten thousand virtues. Thereafter...

### Sūtra-Recitation Text

The beneficial manifestation of our Dharma-king is difficult to fully describe. For those who take refuge, there is no goal that is not achieved. Now, we open the nāga-treasury, concentrate our minds, strike the great bell, and invite the twofold assembly: by whose initiative is this done? It is the merit established by our State Minister, the Counselor Chebo, reverently on behalf of the generals and officers of the western campaign. We humbly submit that the State Minister is one descended from the heights, divinely powerful and inspiring, compassionate in his kindness. Going beyond the frontier, he soothes the people and secures the borders. Earlier in the beginning of spring, brigands raised their banners and plundered people and livestock. Therefore, a great military force was dispatched to eliminate the savage enemy. Although the troops are strong and the officers brave, nevertheless merit is the master of misfortune. Therefore, from afar, relying on the monastics beyond the flowing sands, we pray earnestly with recitation and mindfulness. Now the excellent undertaking is laid out from afar, and the surpassing merit is fully complete. All is used to adorn the military generals and ministers in their very persons. May the buddhas keep them in mindful protection, that there be no anxiety of injury or harm. May the eight classes silently bestow their aid and give rise to thoughts of harmony and submission. Thereafter, may men and horses all be safe and officers and troops preserved in health. May each keep to his post forever and warfare be eliminated. Thereafter, may it sprinkle and spread throughout the dharma-realm, reaching universally to all sentient beings. Relying on this excellent cause, may all ascend the path of awakening.

### Canopy-Erection Text

For removing calamity and quelling difficulty, nothing surpasses the secret words of the buddha-uṣṇīṣa. For gathering blessings and extending good fortune, the matter relies on the city-procession dhāraṇī-recitation. Now, in this spring month, while the cold still holds its power, we invite the monks and nuns of the twofold assembly to erect white banners at five locations. By whose initiative is this done? It is the merit established by our Jie'er, the Military Governor, on behalf of all the people of the district. We humbly submit that the Jie'er, the Military Governor, governs the people fairly and devotes himself to securing the borders. Fearing the spread of pestilence and the outbreak of calamity, he has in advance cultivated this great vow and established this excellent cause. The city-procession will annihilate noxious vapors; the erection of banners will bring the benefit of merit. Now the excellent undertaking is prepared and the surpassing vow is fulfilled. May the blessings be as vast as empty space, beyond comparison. We dedicate this surpassing benefit first as adornment for Brahmā, Śakra, the four kings, nāga spirits, and the eight classes.

### Canopy-Erection Text

For beholding marks and arousing virtue, nothing surpasses the body of responsive transformation. For averting calamity and dispelling misfortune, nothing exceeds the buddha-uṣṇīṣa-hṛdaya dhāraṇī. The bodyless body manifests a body to meet difficulty. Without speaking, it speaks the heart-dhāraṇī to sustain those in peril. The grandeur of this occasion is unsurpassed. Now, within the prefecture of Dunhuang, the white Dharma-banners of victory are erected, and the buddha-uṣṇīṣa is established at the four gates, so that the dark karma may be swept away. By whose initiative is this assembly done? It is established by the two Jie'er, the Provincial Governor, Duke Du, and others, on behalf of the people of the city, for peace and security. The Jie'er and Military Governor have considered that Dunhuang is at the extreme west, bordering the northern barbarians. Mounted warriors with drawn bows raid without warning. They fear that trampled crops will reduce the source of food and clothing by a hundredfold, that the nine stages of agriculture will not flourish, and that ten thousand people will be burdened with anxiety. Therefore they have conferred together, with the sole purpose of securing the people. For protecting the nation and rectifying the realm, nothing surpasses the erection of these banners and canopies. Our hope is to dispel calamity from the barren lands and drive away epidemics to a land of nothingness. May the five grains be free from the disaster of frost and hail. May the ten thousand beings ascend to the city of human longevity. We first dedicate this merit to adorn the Holy Divine Zanpu. We humbly pray that the precious throne be forever firm and his years rival metal and stone. May the four seas be clear and calm and ten thousand lands bring tribute. We also dedicate this virtue to adorn the Jie'er and Military Governor, that as frost and rain they may relieve the parched lands. May the tribal envoys and the various officials of the dawn court establish loyalty and uprightness in this sagely age. We further dedicate this virtue as adornment for the two instructing ācāryas. We humbly pray that they may be utterly delivered from suffering and transcend the cycle of rebirth. May their lifespans equal the cold pine and their blessings be like spring grass. Thereafter, may it sprinkle and moisten all beings, both moving and still, reaching universally without limit. Relying on this surpassing cause, may all ascend the path of right awakening.

### Canopy-Erection Text

For extending auspiciousness and displaying celebration, one must rely on the victorious banners. For sweeping away evil and eliminating calamity, one needs the power of the dhāraṇī. Therefore, Supratiṣṭhita, upon hearing it, had his lifespan extended. Nandī achieved his original aspiration. Chimei-demons fear it and flee from punishment. Celestial māras dread it and beg for rescue. Great indeed is the divine dhāraṇī; its praise is beyond attainment. Now, as the warm winds of spring stir all beings and the doors of hibernation are about to open, pennants and canopies are arrayed and the black-robed monastics stand in solemn order. Why is this? It is the time when our Jie'er, the Supreme Counselor, together with Military Governor Duke Du and others, who nurture the people as their own children and worry for the nation as for their own home, fear that noxious vapors may wreak evil within the city and that the people may repeatedly suffer adverse calamity. Therefore, in this auspicious month of the three yang, they establish the three blessings before the World-Honored One. At the beginning of the four seasons, they erect beneficial banners at the four gates. All this surpassing and wondrous merit, the supreme field of blessings, is entirely used as adornment for Brahmā, Śakra, the four kings, nāga spirits, and the eight classes. We humbly pray that their awesome radiance increase and their power of merit grow. May they nurture the common people, guard them without failing, and protect the military and the nation. May our Holy Divine Zanpu's sacred person be firm and enduring. As the sun goes and the moon comes, may the precious abode be everlastingly prosperous, as enduring as heaven and earth. May the Jie'er and Military Governor have lifespans rivaling the pines. May blessings and prosperity sustain one another. May the tribal envoys and the various officials assist in governance with harmony, purity, and uprightness. Thereafter, may the four seasons be in order and the five grains flourish. May a hundred calamities be removed and ten thousand auspicious signs gather. The prajñā dhāraṇī is the teacher of all buddhas. May the great assembly of the entire city all recite it universally.
