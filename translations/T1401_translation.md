# 佛說大金剛香陀羅尼經
## Sūtra on the Great Vajra Incense Dhāraṇī Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1401

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dānapāla (施護).

---

At that time, the World-Honored One proclaimed this great vidyā capable of subjugating the constellations of the heavens: "This Vajra Incense, of great power and supreme might like a vajra jewel, has great utility and can shatter mountains. If there are supreme constellations that will not submit, one need only recite this Vajra Incense mantra, and the heavenly constellations will tremble in terror and submit of themselves, as when wind blows upon tree branches and they bend and fall with the wind." He then proclaimed the dhāraṇī:

Translator's note: This dhāraṇī is addressed to Vajra Gandhārī, a protective yakṣiṇī deity. Identifiable Sanskrit terms include: homage to the Three Jewels (ratna-traya), Caṇḍa Vajrapāṇi as great general of the yakṣas, epithets of Vajra Gandhārī (fierce, terrifying, six-faced, twelve-eyed, disheveled-haired, of many forms), invocations of Māra, Maheśvara, Garuḍa, Kumāra, Brahmā, Indra, Candra, Āditya, Agni, Yama, Varuṇa, Vāyu, Kubera, the Four Heavenly Kings (Dhṛtarāṣṭra, Virūḍhaka, Virūpākṣa, Vaiśravaṇa), Mahākāla, Bhṛṅgīriṭi, Vināyaka, Pañcika, the Mātṛkā goddesses (Indrāṇī, Brahmaṇī, Kaumārī, Māheśvarī, Vaiṣṇavī, Vārāhī), and numerous ritual commands including gṛhṇa (seize), jambhani (paralyze), stambhani (immobilize), ākarṣaṇīye (attract), mardana (crush), āveśaya (enter/possess), puraya (fulfill), śānti (peace), puṣṭi (nourishment), and dhṛti (firmness).

"This dhāraṇī enables the Tathāgata's lineage to subjugate the three realms, to protect and uphold the Buddha-Dharma, and to bring about great auspiciousness."
