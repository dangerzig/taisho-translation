# 佛說校量數珠功德經
## Sūtra on Comparing the Merit of Prayer Beads, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0788

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Ratnacitta (寶思惟)

---

At that time the Bodhisattva Mahāsattva Mañjuśrī, Dharma-Prince, wishing to benefit all sentient beings, with a mind of great compassion addressed the great assembly: "Listen well! I shall now explain the merit of receiving and holding prayer beads, comparing the differences in blessings and benefits. If there are those who recite the various dhāraṇīs and the names of the Buddhas, and for the sake of benefiting themselves and protecting others wish to swiftly accomplish the various dharmas and obtain their efficacy, the method of prayer beads should be as follows: one should receive and hold them. If iron is used for the prayer beads, reciting and turning them one round yields merit multiplied fivefold. If red copper is used, reciting and turning them one round yields merit multiplied tenfold. If pearls, coral, or such are used, reciting and turning them one round yields merit multiplied a hundredfold. If wooden rudrākṣa seeds are used, reciting and turning them one round yields merit multiplied a thousandfold. If one wishes to be born in the pure lands of the Buddhas or in the heavenly palaces, one should receive these beads. If lotus seeds are used, reciting and turning them one round yields merit multiplied ten-thousandfold. If indra-rudrākṣa seeds are used, reciting and turning them one round yields merit multiplied a million-fold. If ulūdra-rudrākṣa seeds are used, reciting and turning them one round yields merit multiplied ten-million-fold. If crystal is used, reciting and turning them one round yields merit multiplied a hundred-million-fold. If bodhi seeds are used, whether one turns and recites or merely holds them in the hand, reciting one round yields merit that is immeasurable, incalculable, and beyond comparison.

"Good men! As for bodhi seeds: if a person holds these bodhi-seed beads in hand but is unable to recite the names of the Buddhas or dhāraṇīs according to the proper method, yet this good man is able simply to hold them in hand and carry them on his person, then whether walking, standing, sitting, or lying down, whatever words he speaks, whether good or evil, because this person holds the bodhi seeds, the merit obtained is equal to reciting the names of all the Buddhas and chanting the dhāraṇīs, with no difference. The blessings obtained are immeasurable. As for the prayer beads themselves, they should have a full complement of one hundred and eight. If these are difficult to obtain, then fifty-four, or twenty-seven, or fourteen may also be used. These are the differences in the characteristics of the prayer-bead Dharma.

"Good men! For what reason do I now particularly praise the use of bodhi seeds as yielding the supreme benefit? Listen well, all of you, for I shall explain the causes of the past. Long ago, a Buddha appeared in the world and attained perfect enlightenment beneath this tree. At that time, a non-Buddhist who trusted in wrong and inverted views slandered the Three Treasures. That non-Buddhist had a son who was suddenly struck and killed by non-human spirits. The non-Buddhist thought: 'My wrong views have been extreme. I wonder what spiritual power the Buddhas truly possess. Since the Tathāgata attained perfect enlightenment beneath this tree, if the Buddha is truly holy, the tree should have the power to respond.' He then laid his dead son beneath the bodhi tree and said: 'If the Buddha's tree is truly holy, my son must be restored to life.' After seven days of chanting the Buddha's name, the son was indeed restored to life. The non-Buddhist praised: 'The spiritual power of the Buddhas is something I have never seen! The tree where the Buddha attained the path has manifested this wonder. Its great spiritual power is difficult to conceive.' When the other non-Buddhists heard this, they all abandoned their wrong views, turned to what is right, generated the mind of bodhi, and came to trust and know that the Buddha's power is inconceivable. The people all called it the Life-Extending Tree. Because of this it came to have two names; you should know them. Having seen what you need, I have spoken these words."

The Buddha said: "Excellent, excellent, Mañjuśrī, Dharma-Prince! It is as you have said, without any difference."

The entire great assembly, having heard this sūtra on comparing the merit of holding prayer beads, were all greatly filled with joy. They received it with faith and practiced accordingly.
