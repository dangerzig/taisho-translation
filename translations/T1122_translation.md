# T1122 金剛頂瑜伽他化自在天理趣會普賢修行念誦儀軌

# Vajraśekhara Yoga Paranirmitavaśavartin Nayasaṃgraha Samantabhadra Practice Recitation Ritual Manual

*Note: This is an esoteric ritual manual (yìguǐ 儀軌) translated by Amoghavajra (Bùkōng 不空) during the Tang dynasty. It presents the complete sādhana for the Samantabhadra (Pǔxián 普賢) practice from the Nayasaṃgraha assembly (Lǐqù huì 理趣會) of the Paranirmitavaśavartin heaven (Tāhuà zìzài tiān 他化自在天) in the Vajraśekhara (Jīngāngdǐng 金剛頂) tradition. The text is composed almost entirely in verse and covers the full sequence of practice: purification, the awakening mudrā, prostrations to the four directional buddhas, the four immeasurables, vajra-binding, the heart-gate opening and seed-syllable installation, the Trailokyavijaya boundary, a distinctive karma-obstacle crushing ritual, the lotus samādhi, bodhicitta visualization with the seed-syllable transforming in a sun disc, the five-stage body transformation, abhiṣeka, armor and garland, the eight inner and outer offerings, the hundred-syllable mantra, and the dismissal with the liberation mudrā.*

---

> I, with the three purified karmas,
> For the benefit of all sentient beings,
> That they may attain the three bodies,
> Pay homage and take refuge in the Three Treasures.
> The vajra body, speech, and mind
> Pervade the realm of birth and death;
> The secret vajradhātu,
> The great compassionate protector.
> Heroic Akṣobhya,
> Most victorious Ratnasaṃbhava,
> Great compassionate Amitābha,
> Accomplisher of unobstructed activity:
> To these unsurpassed honored ones
> I bow my head in reverence,
> Together with Vajrasattva,
> Who subdues all,
> Supreme Ākāśagarbha,
> Who bestows all abhiṣekas,
> World-Saving Avalokiteśvara,
> Who reveals the yoga of samādhi,
> Skillful Viśvakarman,
> Adept in secret skillful means:
> To all these holy ones above
> I bow my head in reverence.

## Entering the Practice

> The practitioner of this Dharma
> Should always abide in the visualization of the principal deity.
> Walking, one treads upon lotuses.
> Arriving at the door of the meditation chamber,
> One snaps the fingers three times, reciting "hūṃ."
> At the right eye, place the syllable "ma";
> At the left eye, place the syllable "ṭa."
> The right becomes the sun, the left the moon,
> Streaming forth vajra light.
> Upon entering the door and gazing about,
> All māras are scattered.
> With the left hand in vajra fist
> At the heart, index finger raised,
> The right hand likewise,
> Above the crown, rotating leftward three times,
> Pointing to the sky and the lower realms,
> Then rotating rightward,
> All while reciting the "hūṃ" syllable vidyā.
> Next, contemplate that the buddhas eternally abide.
> Having bowed and paid homage to the Three Treasures,
> One then recites the purification vidyā,
> With both hands forming the unopened lotus,
> Empowering the heart, forehead, throat, and crown.

"Oṃ svabhāva-śuddha-sarva-dharma-svabhāva-śuddho'haṃ."

> The secret meaning of this vidyā is:
> All dharmas are by nature pure,
> And I too am by nature pure.
> Through this empowerment,
> Self and other attain stainlessness.
> Then, within one's own heart,
> One contemplates the nature becoming vajra;
> The three karmas have undergone transformation
> And become the three secret gates.

## The Awakening Mudrā and Prostrations

> Next, form the awakening mudrā:
> Two fists, the little fingers hooking,
> The index fingers touching at the sides,
> Raised twice in a hooking gesture.
> Recite this secret speech:

"Oṃ vajrotiṣṭha."

> Through this mantra-mudrā empowerment,
> The buddhas do not savor the taste of quiescence
> But all arise from samādhi to gather at the assembly,
> Observing and embracing the practitioner.

> Next, form the vajra-holding great mudrā:
> The little fingers and thumbs cross in reverse.
> With right knee on the ground, place the mudrā on the crown,
> Visualize the body pervading oceans of buddha-lands,
> Bowing at the feet of each and every tathāgata.

Mantra: "Oṃ vajra-huḥ."

> Through this vajra-holding mudrā,
> All the perfectly awakened ones will be pleased.
> In order to serve all tathāgatas,
> One offers the body to Akṣobhya Buddha:
> Vajra añjali extended above the crown,
> The entire body prostrated to the ground, bowing with the heart.

Mantra: "Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-pūja-upasthānāya ātmānaṃ niryātayāmi, sarva-tathāgata-vajra-sattva adhiṣṭha svāhā hūṃ."

> Through this mantra and body-mudrā,
> One perfects the mind of bodhi.
> Next, one should bow to Ratnasaṃbhava,
> To serve and request abhiṣeka.
> Vajra añjali placed below at the heart,
> With the forehead to the ground, making the offering.

Mantra: "Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-pūja-abhiṣekāya ātmānaṃ niryātayāmi, sarva-tathāgata-vajra-ratna-abhiṣiñca svāhā trāḥ."

> Through this offering of the body,
> One obtains the dharma-king's abhiṣeka stage.
> In order to request the turning of the Dharma wheel,
> One offers the body to Amitāyus.
> Vajra añjali placed upon the crown,
> With the mouth to the ground, offering one's body.

Mantra: "Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-pūja-pravartanāya ātmānaṃ niryātayāmi, sarva-tathāgata-vajra-dharma-pravartaya svāhā hrīḥ."

> Through this mantra-request,
> One obtains the wisdom of turning the wondrous Dharma wheel.
> Vajra añjali at the heart,
> With the crown of the head to the ground, making the request.

Mantra: "Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-pūja-karmaṇi aḥ ātmānaṃ niryātayāmi, sarva-tathāgata-vajra-karma kuru svāhā aḥ."

> Through this sincere request of offering the body,
> One can manifest various bodies.
> Next, visualize oneself before the ocean of buddhas,
> Confessing, rejoicing, exhorting, and transferring,
> All following the abbreviated yoga.

## The Four Immeasurables

> Then one enters the principal deity's samaya,
> Sitting cross-legged with upright body in right absorption.
> The four immeasurable minds pervade the dharmadhātu,
> Cultivated and applied according to the Dharma teaching.

Great kindness mantra: "Oṃ mahā-maitrī-sphara."

> When reciting this mantra,
> Extend the mind throughout the three realms,
> Universally bestowing joy upon sentient beings.

Great compassion mantra: "Oṃ mahā-karuṇā-sphara."

> When reciting this mantra,
> The mind pervades the realm of beings,
> Universally rescuing and relieving through all gates.

Great joy mantra: "Oṃ sarva-śuddha-pramoda-sphara."

> Having rescued and saved each according to their kind,
> One empowers with this vidyā:
> All types of sentient beings
> Are all bestowed bodhi.
> Having brought such benefit and joy,
> One returns to the nature of the dharmadhātu.

Great equanimity mantra: "Oṃ mahopekṣā-sphara."

> When reciting this mantra,
> The mind abides in equality,
> Not seeing self or other.
> Only this one nature and characteristic,
> Which is the nature of Samantabhadra,
> The mind of great bodhi.

## The Binding, Heart-Gate, and Boundary

Next, with both hands performing a spinning dance, one forms the vajra añjali mudrā, the ten fingers interlaced at the first joints. Mantra: "Oṃ vajrāñjali."

This mudrā then becomes the firm binding, called the supreme vajra-binding, from which all mudrās arise. Mantra: "Oṃ vajra-bandha."

Then, the thumbs enter the moon, the index fingers rest on their backs: the vajra-fist great samaya mudrā. Mantra: "Oṃ vajra-muṣṭi vaṃ."

Separate into two and form the principal deity's samaya mudrā. Mantra: "Hoḥ vajrasattva sūrata-sattvaṃ."

> As soon as the vow-mudrā and mantra are formed,
> The body dwells in a sun disc, identical to the principal deity.
> Next, present the joyful samaya,
> Which makes all the holy ones rejoice.
> The binding mudrā with the ring fingers and little-finger moon-discs,
> And the little fingers and thumbs standing upright and joined.

Mantra: "Oṃ samaya hoḥ sūrata-sattvaṃ."

> Next, one should open the heart gate.
> Visualize upon the two breasts,
> "Trāḥ" on the right, "ṭa" on the left.
> Pull open the vajra-binding at the heart three times,
> Like opening door-leaves.

Mantra: "Oṃ vajra-bandha trāṭ."

> Visualize one cubit before oneself
> The syllable "aḥ" in pure white light.
> The thumbs enter the moon-palm,
> And with the index fingers
> One plucks the syllable and places it within the heart.

Mantra: "Oṃ vajra-āveśa aḥ."

> With the previous fist-mudrā vidyā,
> One seals shut the heart-palace gate.
> Having secretly sealed the heart gate,
> Divide into two at the heart.
> Reciting "hūṃ," raise the right with a shout,
> Then "hūṃ" raising together with the left,
> Three "hūṃ"s hooking and interlocking,
> "Hūṃ" extending the index fingers.
> Rotating leftward is expulsion;
> Rotating rightward is boundary-creation.
> When performing this method,
> Inwardly abide in the great compassionate mind,
> Outwardly display great wrathful power:
> Four faces with upright sharp fangs,
> Eight arms wielding sharp weapons,
> The entire body emitting fierce flames,
> In the aspect of a great roaring shout.
> The Guhyapati vajra hosts
> Receive the command and stand in attendance.

## The Lotus Samādhi and Karma-Obstacle Crushing

> Next, form the lotus samaya.
> The root binding with little fingers and thumbs upright.
> Through this mantra and secret mudrā,
> Cultivating samādhi, it swiftly manifests.

Mantra: "Oṃ vajra-padma-samaya-sattvaṃ."

> Next, remove the deviation and turn the mind
> To return to the wisdom of equality.
> The previous mudrā with the little fingers crossing,
> And at the final syllable of the mantra,
> Pull open the secret mudrā.

Mantra: "Hūṃ ṭaki sphoṭa-ya mahā-vīra-vajra vajradhara sthāna-ṭa."

> As soon as this vidyā-mudrā is performed,
> Those of the two vehicles generate compassion and rescue.
> Ordinary beings enter the buddha-realm,
> Buddhas emerge to deliver sentient beings:
> All through this empowerment,
> One obtains the Samantabhadra mind.

Mantra: "Oṃ sūrata vajra jaḥ hūṃ vaṃ hoḥ. Oṃ mahāsukha vajra sādhaya sarva-sattva-veya jaḥ hūṃ vaṃ hoḥ."

> Through this mantra,
> All abide in the great compassionate mind.
> Holy and ordinary alike receive joy:
> This is called the great equality of ordinary and holy.
> Next, summon the karma created
> Since beginningless time through false views.
> The binding mudrā with ring fingers like needles,
> The index fingers alongside like hooks.
> The mind visualizes summoning all sins,
> Which take the form of hungry ghosts.
> Reverse the mudrā toward the heart and summon,
> Reciting this true speech:

Mantra: "Oṃ sarva-pāpa-ghaṭana-viśodhana-samaya vajra hūṃ jaḥ."

> Through these three secret gates,
> All karmic actions of self and other are gathered.
> Reciting "hūṃ," the index finger presses down:
> This is the lasso drawing them into the palm.
> Reciting "vaṃ," they cross inwardly,
> Each pair hooking together.
> Having bound all sinful karma,
> The ring fingers extend upright like a banner,
> Inscribed with the syllables "trāḥ" and "ṭa,"
> Visualized as becoming a vajra.
> Clap them together as if crushing a mountain.
> With wrathful speech and wrathful form,
> One can purify all evil destinies.
> Recite the vidyā and clap the ring fingers
> Three or seven times.

Mantra: "Oṃ vajra-pāṇi-visphoṭaya sarva-pāya-bandhana-nipramokṣaya sarva-pāya-gati-viśodhaya sarvasattvān sarva-tathāgata-vajra-samaya hūṃ trāṭ."

> Through this corresponding method,
> The sins accumulated by the three karmas,
> The extremely heavy obstructions from beginningless time,
> Having been thus crushed,
> Are like dry grass and sesame
> Burned by the fire at the end of a kalpa.
> Through the Tathāgata's great compassion,
> This supremely secret gate is opened.
> Next, form the secret mudrā,
> Scatter and destroy the karmic obstacle wheel,
> And thereby accomplish buddha-activities.
> The vajra añjali,
> The index fingers bent with backs touching,
> The thumbs pressing beside them.

Mantra: "Oṃ vajra-karma-viśodhana sarva-varaṇa-nibuddha sthāna hūṃ."

> In order to reveal one's own nature,
> One should use the lotus samādhi mudrā,
> Place it at the left side of the head, and recite:

Mantra: "Oṃ caṇḍarośanī samanta-bhadra-keraṇī mahā-vajriṇī hūṃ."

> Through this Dharma empowerment,
> The three poisons are entirely extinguished,
> And the great bodhi of one's own mind
> Is accomplished at that very moment.

## The Bodhicitta Visualization

> Next, enter the samādhi of discriminating wisdom.
> The binding mudrā face-up, placed on the crossed legs.
> The index fingers with backs upright, tips touching.
> The thumbs touching and pressing the index fingers.
> This is called the Avalokiteśvararāja mudrā.
> Sitting upright with the body motionless,
> The tongue pressed against the upper palate, lips and teeth closed,
> The mind abiding in great emptiness without discrimination.
> The buddhas, filling space, come to rouse and awaken,
> Saying: "You have realized the one path of purity
> But have not yet realized the vajra yoga samādhi."
> Then, within samādhi, one bows at the buddhas' feet.

For the remaining details, follow what is extensively explained in the Yoga Sūtra. Reciting this mantra, the syllable "aḥ" previously installed within the heart emits immeasurable red light, rotating rightward to become a sun disc. Mantra: "Oṃ citta-prativedikaṃ karomi."

Through this mantra-empowerment, one's own heart becomes like the sun. As it is not yet clear, one further recites: "Oṃ bodhi-citta-utpādayāmi."

Through this mantra-empowerment, it becomes like the sun disc at the height of summer, blazing with radiance. Within the sun disc, one further visualizes a white lotus and upon it the principal deity's heart-syllable, pure white in color, which becomes the principal deity's mudrā. This is the vajra wisdom. Mantra: "Oṃ tiṣṭha-vajra."

Through this mantra-empowerment, one's own heart-wisdom becomes clear. One further recites: "Oṃ vajrātma-kuhaṃ."

The secret meaning is: "I am vajra." Thus the three karmas have become vajra. Further: "I am the vajra body." When making this contemplation, this vajra extends to pervade the dharmadhātu, and all buddhas merge into this vajra as one body. One further empowers it to become firm. Mantra: "Oṃ nirdha-tiṣṭha-vajra."

Through this empowerment, it becomes supremely firm and immovable. One then knows oneself to have become the indestructible vajra body. In order to accomplish the body of the principal deity, one recites: "Oṃ yathā-karma-kuladha-sattha-haṃ."

Next, with the previous principal deity's samaya mudrā and mantra, one empowers the heart, forehead, throat, and crown.

## Abhiṣeka, Armor, and Crown

Next, perform the abhiṣeka method. With the Vajradhātu sovereign mudrā placed on the crown, then the forehead, then right, then back, then left:

"Oṃ vajrasattva." Then at the right of the crown, the jewel: "Oṃ vajraratna." Then at the back, the Dharma: "Oṃ vajradharma." Then at the left, karma: "Oṃ vajrakarma."

The secret mudrā: the root binding with ring fingers like a knife, index fingers attached. Through this empowerment, the five-tathāgata crown rests on one's head.

Divide into two fists, bring them to the back of the crown, the index fingers wrapping around three times, then releasing downward in the gesture of hanging streamers. This is the garland abhiṣeka, signifying that the stainless streamer has been tied. Mantra: "Oṃ vajra-māle abhiṣiñca vaṃ."

Then with both hands in fists, the index fingers extended, one visualizes the syllable "oṃ" on the right index finger-face and "vaṃ" on the left, with green-white light like drawing a lotus fiber. This becomes the armor-lasso. From the heart, wrap three times around the back, then the navel, waist, both knees, then the navel again, waist, heart, back, throat, back of the neck, forehead, back of the crown, all three times, then releasing downward like draping celestial garments.

Having performed this empowerment, one is clad in the great vow-adorned, compassionate armor of the tathāgatas of the three times. All heavenly māras cannot create obstacles, and one can defeat all māra armies, performing the buddha-work of benefiting sentient beings.

To delight the holy ones and swiftly attain accomplishment, clap both hands three times. Mantra: "Oṃ vajra-tuṣya hoḥ."

> Through the clapping-mudrā empowerment,
> All holy ones are delighted.
> Next, again visualize the principal deity's heart
> Transforming into the wonderful body of the principal deity.
> As one previously contemplated oneself, so now again,
> Revealing that the Dharma and wisdom bodies are not two.
> The previous visualization through the five aspects of accomplishment
> Is what is called the self-nature dharma body.
> What is now visualized is the wisdom body.
> Abiding in this samādhi's secret gate,
> The root binding with the thumbs entering the moon.

Mantra: "Oṃ vajrasattva aḥ. Vajrasattva dṛśya."

> Through this empowerment,
> The honored body becomes clearly manifest.
> One then summons it to enter one's own body.
> Next, at one's dwelling place,
> One further visualizes the syllable "a"
> Becoming Mount Sumeru, the king of mountains,
> With a jeweled pavilion above it.
> Within this palace,
> One further installs the principal deity's body,
> Surrounded by the retinue,
> With all kinds of offering implements.
> All things in the dharmadhātu
> Completely fill that space.
> When making this visualization,
> Recite this mantra:

"Oṃ gaganasaṃbhava vajra hoḥ."

> Through the power of this mantra,
> All offering implements are fully provided,
> Whatever is desired arising from the space-treasury.

## Summoning, Offerings, and Attendant Mudrās

To serve the principal deity, one should enter the action-accomplishing wisdom samādhi. Having thus visualized and empowered, one summons the assembly of holy ones with the Amoghavajrarāja.

> The wisdom and method hands in vajra fists,
> Arms crossed embracing the chest, index fingers bent,
> Snapping the fingers, the sound pervading the worlds.
> Contemplate the ocean of buddhas universally gathering like clouds.

Mantra: "Oṃ vajra-samāja jaḥ."

> Through the mantra and secret mudrā,
> The principal deity and all holy ones
> Descend and fill all of space.
> Next, abiding in the equality-nature wisdom samādhi,
> One holds and presents argha, the variously scented waters,
> Bathing the stainless bodies of the holy ones.
> One should use the vajra añjali mudrā
> To empower the scented water while reciting the mantra.

Then, using form to summon the holy ones into the palace's wisdom body, one forms the Trailokyavijaya mudrā with the index finger beckoning like a hook. Mantra: "Oṃ vajra-rūpe jaḥ."

Having summoned, one draws them in as one with sound, the index fingers touching like a lasso. Mantra: "Oṃ vajra-śabde hūṃ."

Having become secretly one, one further causes them to remain with scent, the index fingers hooking like a chain. Mantra: "Oṃ vajra-gandhe vaṃ."

Having firmly bound them, one then delights them with taste, the index finger-faces meeting. Mantra: "Oṃ vajra-rase hoḥ."

One should make verbal praises as song. Next, form the principal deity mudrā and mantra.

With both hands as if shooting: this is "Mind-Born." Mantra: "Oṃ manud-bhava vajra jaḥ."

With both arms crossing as if embracing, the wisdom hand covering the method hand: this is "Touch." Mantra: "Oṃ vajra-kelikila hūṃ."

With the wisdom elbow resting firmly, the method thumb and index finger upright: this is "Love Binding." Mantra: "Oṃ sneha vajra vaṃ."

With both fists at the hips: this is "Spirit." Mantra: "Oṃ vajra-garva hoḥ."

The same gestures performed softly produce the four female deities: Mind-Born Woman ("Oṃ manud-bhava vajrī hi"), Touch-Joy Woman ("Oṃ vajra-kelikile hūṃ"), Love-Binding Woman ("Oṃ sneha vajriṇī ha"), and Sovereign Woman ("Oṃ vajra-garvī hūṃ").

The palms upward and open like flowers: this is Spring. Mantra: "Oṃ madhu vajrī ku oṃ ku oṃ."

The palms downward as if scattering incense: this is Summer. Mantra: "Oṃ vajra-meghaye ṭa ṭa."

Both hands at the eyes: this is Autumn. Mantra: "Oṃ śara vajrī aḥ aḥ."

Both hands turning at the heart: this is Winter. Mantra: "Oṃ vajra-śeṣire aḥ aḥ."

The four attractors are used as before in sequence. Next, with the four vidyās one summons the deity to enter the body.

## The Hundred-Syllable Mantra and Offerings

One again forms the principal deity's samaya great mudrā and recites the Mahāyāna hundred-syllable mantra. Through this empowerment, even unsurpassed bodhi is not difficult to attain; how much less all other accomplishments! Even if one has committed the five unpardonable transgressions, upon reciting, they are completely eliminated. Why? Because the principal deity firmly abides within one's own body.

"Oṃ vajrasattva samayam anupālaya, vajrasattva tvenopatiṣṭha, dṛḍho me bhava, sutoṣyo me bhava, anurakto me bhava, supoṣyo me bhava, sarva-siddhiṃ me prayaccha, sarva-karmasu ca me cittaṃ śreyaḥ kuru hūṃ, ha ha ha ha hoḥ, bhagavan sarva-tathāgata vajra mā me muñca, vajrī-bhava, mahā-samaya-sattva aḥ."

Next, one makes inner and outer offerings to the secret mantra king:

The firm binding with thumbs upright: this is the Play Bodhisattva (Xìxī púsà 戲嬉菩薩). Mantra: "Oṃ mahā-rati."

As soon as the vidyā-mudrā is formed, the Mahārati woman delights all the holy ones with her blissful mudrā.

Extending both arms forward with wrists joined: this is the Garland Bodhisattva. Mantra: "Oṃ rūpa-śobhe."

Through this mudrā and mantra, the garland woman pervades the oceans of buddha-lands, raining down vajra garlands.

From the navel to the mouth, scattering: this is the Song Offering Bodhisattva. Mantra: "Oṃ śabda-trā-svake."

Through this secret mudrā and mantra, the vajra song woman, with as many music maidens as atoms in all worlds, pervades the ten directions with beautiful sounds as offerings. One thereby attains the Tathāgata's unobstructed eloquence.

Both hands performing a spinning dance above the crown, then releasing: this is the Dance Bodhisattva. Mantra: "Oṃ sarva-pūjī."

Through this offering, one obtains the six superknowledges.

The binding released downward: this is the Incense Bodhisattva. Mantra: "Oṃ praharaṇi ni."

Through this method, clouds of incense pervade all buddha-lands.

Scattering upward like flowers: this is the Flower Bodhisattva. Mantra: "Oṃ phalāgami."

Through this, one attains clouds of flowers pervading all buddha-lands and the thirty-two marks of the Tathāgata.

The thumbs upright pressing against the vajra-binding: this is the Lamp Bodhisattva. Mantra: "Oṃ sutījagri."

Through this, the light of wisdom universally illuminates the buddha-realms as an offering, and one attains the five eyes of the Tathāgata.

The binding at the heart in the gesture of applying unguent: this is the Unguent Bodhisattva. Mantra: "Oṃ sugandha-aṅgī."

Through this, the scent of the precepts universally anoints the buddha-lands, and one attains the five-fold dharma-body wisdom.

One then places the vajra añjali on the crown and recites the Ākāśagarbha mantra, producing clouds of all kinds of offerings from the ocean. One abides in the principal deity's karma ritual and recites to one's capacity. Holding the rosary in añjali above the crown, one recites the root vidyā empowering the heart, with each recitation aligned with the syllables of the mantra, whether ten thousand, one thousand, or one hundred, practicing diligently in the four periods day and night.

## Dismissal and Conclusion

When recitation is complete, one again makes the eight offerings and the universal offering mudrās. One again forms the principal deity and retinue mudrās, presents the samaya, and makes vows.

With the Trailokyavijaya mudrā, rotating leftward to release the boundary, one forms the binding mudrā with the ring fingers extended like needles, pulls open at the heart, then joins the palms above the crown, visualizing the holy assembly returning to their original abode. This is the liberation mudrā. Mantra: "Oṃ kṛtva sarvasattva-ratha-siddhi-nutaya-tha-nuga-gaccha-dhvan-buddhaviṣayaṃ punarāgamanāya tu oṃ vajrasattva muḥ."

Having performed this method, one again empowers as before with the abhiṣeka, armor, and prostrations to the four tathāgatas, making vows and confession. With body abiding in the principal deity's visualization and uninterrupted bodhi-mind, one then exits the ritual arena and, as one wishes, circumambulates sūtras, stamps stūpas and images, and walks about at ease.
