# 鸚鵡經
## Sūtra of the Parrot

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 79

Translated from the Chinese. Liu Song Dynasty, translated by Guṇabhadra

---

Thus have I heard.

At one time, the Bhagavān was in the city of Śrāvastī, at the Jetavana in the garden of Anāthapiṇḍada. At that time, the World-Honored One rose in the morning, put on his robes, and together with his robes and bowl went to the city of Śrāvastī on his alms round. While going on his alms round through Śrāvastī, he arrived at the home of Śuka Māṇava, son of Todeyya. At that time, Śuka Māṇava, son of Todeyya, had gone out and was away, attending to some minor business. At that time, in the home of Śuka Māṇava, son of Todeyya, there was a white dog named Complete, sitting on a fine cushion and eating polished rice with meat from a golden bowl. The white dog saw the World-Honored One approaching from afar, and upon seeing him, began to bark. The World-Honored One then spoke these words: "Stop, white dog! You should not make such sounds. You used to recite and chant!" Thereupon, the white dog became extremely angry and displeased, got down from the bed-cushion, went to the base of the door-threshold, lay down against it, and remained there in silence.

Later, when the son of Todeyya returned home, he saw the white dog had gotten down from the bed-cushion and was lying silently against the door-threshold, looking grieved and unhappy. Seeing this, he asked the people nearby: "Who disturbed this white dog, causing it to be grieved and unhappy, to get down from the bed-cushion and lie silently against the door-threshold?" "Māṇava! No one disturbed this dog or caused it to be grieved and unhappy, to get down from the bed-cushion and lie silently against the door-threshold. Māṇava! Today the śramaṇa Gautama came to this house to beg for alms. The white dog barked at him, and the śramaṇa Gautama spoke these words: 'Stop, white dog! You should not make such sounds. You used to recite and chant!' For this reason, Māṇava, the white dog became angry and unhappy, got down from the bed-cushion, and now lies silently against the door-threshold." Thereupon, Śuka Māṇava, son of Todeyya, became angry and displeased with the World-Honored One. From afar he reviled the World-Honored One, from afar he slandered the World-Honored One, from afar he raged against the World-Honored One: "This śramaṇa Gautama! How he speaks such false and empty words!" He departed from Śrāvastī and went to the Jetavana, the garden of Anāthapiṇḍada.

At that time, the World-Honored One was encircled by a countless assembly of hundreds, teaching them the Dharma. The World-Honored One saw Śuka Māṇava, son of Todeyya, approaching from afar. Seeing him, the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣus: "Bhikṣus! Do you see Śuka Māṇava, son of Todeyya, approaching from afar?" "Yes, World-Honored One!" "If Śuka Māṇava, son of Todeyya, were to die at this moment, in the time it takes to bend and extend one's arm, he would be born in niraya. Why? Because he is filled with such extreme anger toward me. Due to that anger, when the body perishes and life ends, he would be born in an evil destiny, in niraya."

At that time, Śuka Māṇava, son of Todeyya, came before the World-Honored One, and upon arriving, addressed the World-Honored One: "Śramaṇa Gautama! Did you go to my home today to beg for alms?"

He replied: "Māṇava! I went to your home today to beg for alms."

"Śramaṇa Gautama! What offense did that white dog commit against you, that you caused my white dog to become angry and unhappy, to get down from the bed-cushion and lie silently against the door-threshold?"

He answered: "Māṇava! In the morning I put on my robes and, together with my robes and bowl, went to the city of Śrāvastī on my alms round. While going on my alms round through the city of Śrāvastī, I arrived at your home. Your white dog saw me approaching from afar, and upon seeing me, barked. I then spoke these words: 'Stop, white dog! You should not make such sounds. You used to recite and chant!' For this reason, Māṇava, the white dog became angry and unhappy, got down from the bed-cushion, and now lies silently against the door-threshold."

"Gautama! What former relative of mine was this white dog?"

"Stop, Māṇava! Do not ask. If you hear it, you may become grieved and unhappy."

Śuka Māṇava, son of Todeyya, addressed the World-Honored One three times: "Gautama! What former relative was the white dog?"

"Māṇava! You have asked three times, so I shall explain. Māṇava! The white dog in its former life was your father, named Todeyya."

Thereupon, Śuka Māṇava, son of Todeyya, became doubly enraged and displeased with the World-Honored One. He reviled the World-Honored One, raged against the World-Honored One, and slandered the World-Honored One: "This śramaṇa Gautama! Speaks false and empty words!" He addressed the World-Honored One: "Gautama! My father Todeyya constantly practiced giving, constantly made banner offerings, and constantly tended the sacred fire. When his body perished and life ended, he was born in the sublime Brahmā heaven above. How could he possibly have been born as a dog?"

"Māṇava! It is because of your excessive pride, and your father Todeyya's as well, that he was born as a base and lowly dog." He then spoke this verse:

> The brahmin of excessive pride,
> At death is born in the six destinies:
> As rooster, pig, dog, or wild fox,
> As donkey, egg-born, or in hell.

"Māṇava! If you do not believe what I have said, then, Māṇava, you may return home. When you arrive, speak to the white dog as follows: 'If it is true, white dog, that in your former life you were my father Todeyya, then get back up onto the bed-cushion.' Māṇava, the white dog will then get back up onto the bed-cushion. 'If you, white dog, in your former life were my father Todeyya, then eat polished rice with meat from the golden bowl.' Māṇava, the white dog will then eat polished rice with meat from the golden bowl. 'If you, white dog, in your former life were my father Todeyya, then show me the treasure my father left behind, which you hid away and whose location I do not know.' Māṇava, the white dog will then show you the treasure your late father left behind, whose location you do not know."

Thereupon, Śuka Māṇava, son of Todeyya, having heard what the World-Honored One said, reflected on it well, committed it to memory and recited it, circumambulated the World-Honored One, took leave of the World-Honored One, and returned home. Upon arriving, he spoke to the white dog as follows: "White dog! If in your former life you were my father Todeyya, then get back up onto the bed-cushion and sit." The white dog then got back up onto the bed-cushion and sat. "White dog! If in your former life you were my father Todeyya, then eat polished rice with meat from the golden bowl." The white dog then ate polished rice with meat from the golden bowl. "White dog! If in your former life you were my father Todeyya, then show me the treasure my late father left behind, which you hid away and whose location I do not know." Thereupon, the white dog got down from the bed-cushion and went to its former resting place. Upon reaching its former resting place, at the base of the four legs of the bed, it dug at the ground with its mouth and paws, and caused Śuka Māṇava, son of Todeyya, to obtain a great store of money and treasure. Thereupon, Śuka Māṇava, son of Todeyya, having obtained the great store of money and treasure and having obtained great profit, was exceedingly joyful. A virtuous mind arose in him, and placing his right knee on the ground, he joined his palms facing toward the Jetavana, the garden of Anāthapiṇḍada, and three times proclaimed his own name, saying: "The śramaṇa Gautama speaks truth! The śramaṇa Gautama's words are true! The śramaṇa Gautama does not speak falsely!" Having three times proclaimed his own name, he departed from the city of Śrāvastī and went to the Jetavana, the garden of Anāthapiṇḍada.

At that time, the World-Honored One was encircled by a countless assembly of hundreds, teaching them the Dharma. The World-Honored One saw Śuka Māṇava, son of Todeyya, approaching from afar. Seeing him, the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣus: "Bhikṣus! Do you see Śuka Māṇava, son of Todeyya, approaching from afar?" "Yes, World-Honored One!" "If Śuka Māṇava, son of Todeyya, were to die at this moment, in the time it takes to bend and extend one's arm, he would be born in a good destination. Why? Because he has a virtuous mind toward me. Sentient beings, because of a virtuous mind, when the body perishes and life ends, are born in a good destination, in heaven above."

At that time, Śuka Māṇava, son of Todeyya, went before the World-Honored One. Upon arriving, he and the World-Honored One exchanged greetings and pleasantries. After exchanging greetings and pleasantries, he sat to one side. After Śuka Māṇava, son of Todeyya, had sat to one side, the World-Honored One addressed him: "Māṇava! It is exactly as I said. Regarding the white dog, is it truly as I said, or not?"

"Just as the śramaṇa Gautama said, regarding the white dog, it is truly as you said, without any difference. Śramaṇa Gautama! I wish to ask a further question. Please hear what I ask."

"Māṇava! Ask whatever you wish."

"Gautama! What is the cause and what is the condition that among those who all receive a human body, there are differences of high and low, good and bad, pure and impure? Gautama! Why are there those with long life and those with short life, those without illness and those with illness, those with fine appearance and those with ugly appearance, those of noble birth and those of lowly birth, those with great influence and those with little influence, those with much wealth and those with little wealth, those with wrong wisdom and those with right wisdom?"

"Māṇava! It is because of the causes and conditions of sentient beings, because of their actions, conditioned by their actions, made by their actions. According to the actions that sentient beings perform, those actions cause them to have differences of good and bad, high and low."

"Śramaṇa Gautama! What you have said is brief and has not been explained in detail; I do not understand its meaning. I wish that the śramaṇa Gautama would explain it well for me, so that I may follow it. What the śramaṇa Gautama has stated only briefly, without explaining in detail, please make it known to me so that I may understand its meaning."

"Therefore, Māṇava, listen well and reflect carefully. I shall explain it for you."

"Yes, Gautama!" Śuka Māṇava, son of Todeyya, received the World-Honored One's instruction.

The World-Honored One said: "Māṇava! What is the cause and what is the condition that certain men or women have short lives? Māṇava! There are certain men or women who are extremely given to bloodshed, who stain their hands with blood, who dwell close to evil and are without compassion, who take the lives of all sentient beings, down to the smallest ant. Because of such actions, due to such causes and such conduct, when the body perishes and life ends, they are born in an evil destiny, in niraya. If they come to be born in the human realm, their lives are short. Why? Māṇava! Because their conduct cuts life short. Therefore, when certain men or women practice killing, this, Māṇava, should be known as the retribution of such action.

"Māṇava! What, then, is the cause and what is the condition that certain men or women have long lives? Māṇava! There are certain men or women who abandon killing, abstain from killing, set aside the knife and the staff, who always possess a sense of shame, who wish for the peace and safety of all sentient beings, and who purify their minds of the intention to kill. Because of such actions, due to such causes and such conduct, when the body perishes and life ends, they go to a good destination, in heaven above. If they come to be born in the human realm, their lives are long. Why? Māṇava! Because their conduct is that which leads to long life. Therefore, when certain men or women abstain from killing and abandon killing, this, Māṇava, should be known as the retribution of such action.

"Māṇava! What is the cause and what is the condition that certain men or women have much illness? Māṇava! There are certain men or women who afflict and harm sentient beings. They afflict sentient beings with their hands, with stones, with staffs, or with knives. Because of such actions, due to such causes and such conduct, when the body perishes and life ends, they are born in an evil destiny, in niraya. If they come to be born in the human realm, they have much illness and pain. Why? Māṇava! Because their conduct is that which produces illness. Therefore, when certain men or women afflict sentient beings, this, Māṇava, should be known as the retribution of such action.

"Māṇava! What, then, is the cause and what is the condition that certain men or women are free from illness? Māṇava! There are certain men or women who do not afflict or harm sentient beings. They do not afflict sentient beings with their hands, with stones, with knives, or with staffs. Because of such actions and such causes, because of such conduct, when the body perishes and life ends, they are born in a good destination, in heaven above. If they come to be born in the human realm, they are free from illness and pain. Why? Māṇava! Because their conduct is that which produces freedom from illness. Therefore, when certain men or women do not afflict sentient beings, this, Māṇava, should be known as the retribution of such action.

"Māṇava! What, then, is the cause and what is the condition that certain men or women have an ugly appearance? Māṇava! There are certain men or women who are given to much anger and much distress. At the slightest word, they become angry, distressed, and unhappy. They dwell in anger, give rise to anger, and extensively engage in slander. Because of such actions and such causes, because of such conduct, when the body perishes and life ends, they are born in an evil destiny, in niraya. If they come to be born in the human realm, their appearance is base and ugly. Why? Because their conduct is base and ugly. Therefore, when certain men or women are angry and distressed, this, Māṇava, should be known as the retribution of such action.

"Māṇava! What, then, is the cause and what is the condition that certain men or women have a fine appearance? Māṇava! There are certain men or women who are not given to much anger, not given to much distress. Even if someone speaks to them with coarse and rough words, they do not become angry, do not harbor resentment, do not become distressed. They do not dwell in anger, do not give rise to anger, and do not respond with angry resentment. Because of such actions and such conduct, because of such actions, when the body perishes and life ends, they are born in a good destination, in heaven above. If they come to be born in the human realm, their appearance is fine. Why? Māṇava! Because their conduct produces a fine appearance. Therefore, when certain men or women are without anger and without distress, this, Māṇava, should be known as the retribution of such action.

"Māṇava! What, then, is the cause and what is the condition that certain men or women have little influence? Māṇava! There are certain men or women who are given to covetous jealousy, who give rise to covetous jealousy. When they see that others receive reverent offerings of wealth and goods, they give rise to covetous jealousy, thinking, 'What others have, let me obtain it.' Because of such actions, because of their actions and conditioned by their actions, when the body perishes and life ends, they are born in an evil destiny, in niraya. If they come to be born in the human realm, they have little influence. Why? Māṇava! Because their conduct produces little influence. Therefore, when certain men or women are covetously jealous and give rise to covetous jealousy, this, Māṇava, should be known as the retribution of such action.

"Māṇava! What, then, is the cause and what is the condition that certain men or women have great influence? Māṇava! There are certain men or women who are free from covetous jealousy, who do not give rise to covetous jealousy. When they see that others receive reverent offerings of wealth and goods, they do not give rise to covetous jealousy, thinking, 'What others have, let me obtain it.' Because of such actions, because of their actions, and conditioned by their actions, when the body perishes and life ends, they are born in a good destination, in heaven above. If they come to be born in the human realm, they have great influence. Why? Māṇava! Because their conduct produces great influence. Therefore, when certain men or women are free from covetous jealousy and do not give rise to covetous jealousy, this, Māṇava, should be known as the retribution of such action.

"Māṇava! What, then, is the cause and what is the condition that certain men or women are born into lowly families? Māṇava! There are certain men or women who are arrogant and conceited. Those who should be revered, they do not revere. Those who should be served, they do not serve. Those who should be honored, they do not honor. Those who should receive offerings, they do not make offerings to. Those who should be offered a seat, they do not offer a seat to. Those who should be shown the way, they do not show the way to. Those who should be honored with rising, reverence, and joined palms, they do not honor with rising, reverence, and joined palms. Because of such actions, conditioned by such actions, having such conduct, when the body perishes and life ends, they are born in an evil destiny, in niraya. If they come to be born in the human realm, they are born into lowly families. Why? Māṇava! Because their conduct is that of lowliness. Therefore, when certain men or women are arrogant and conceited, this, Māṇava, should be known as the retribution of such action.

"Māṇava! What, then, is the cause and what is the condition that certain men or women are born into noble and distinguished families? Māṇava! There are certain men or women who are not arrogant, not conceited. Those who should be revered, they revere. Those who should be served, they serve. Those who should be honored, they honor. Those who should receive offerings, they make offerings to. Those who should be offered a seat, they offer a seat to. Those who should be shown the way, they show the way to. Those who should be honored with rising, reverence, and joined palms, they honor with rising, reverence, and joined palms. Because of such actions, conditioned by such actions, having such conduct, when the body perishes and life ends, they are born in a good destination, in heaven above. If they come to be born in the human realm, they are born into noble and distinguished families. Why? Māṇava! Because their conduct is that of nobility and distinction. Therefore, when certain men or women are not arrogant and not conceited, this, Māṇava, should be known as the retribution of such action.

"Māṇava! What, then, is the cause and what is the condition that certain men or women have little wealth? Māṇava! There are certain men or women who do not give, who are not donors. They do not practice giving to śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas, to the poor and lowly, and to those who come begging; they do not give food and drink, clothing and garments, garlands and perfumes, bedding, lodging, lamps, or attendants. Because of such actions, conditioned by such actions, having such conduct, when the body perishes and life ends, they are born in an evil destiny, in niraya. If they come to be born in the human realm, they have little wealth. Why? Māṇava! Because their conduct produces little wealth. Therefore, when certain men or women have little wealth, this, Māṇava, should be known as the retribution of such action.

"Māṇava! What, then, is the cause and what is the condition that certain men or women have much wealth? Māṇava! There are certain men or women who give, who are donors. They practice giving to śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas, to the poor and lowly, and to those who come begging; they give food and drink, clothing and garments, flower garlands and perfumes, bedding, lodging, lamps, and attendants. Because of such actions, conditioned by such actions, having such conduct, when the body perishes and life ends, they are born in a good destination, in heaven above. If they come to be born in the human realm, they have much wealth. Why? Māṇava! Because their conduct produces much wealth. Therefore, when certain men or women have much wealth, this, Māṇava, should be known as the retribution of such action.

"Māṇava! What, then, is the cause and what is the condition that certain men or women have wrong wisdom? Māṇava! There are certain men or women who, among sentient beings, are unable to go and inquire from those renowned śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas. Having gone to them, they do not ask questions at the proper time about the meaning, nor do they discuss with these worthy ones: 'What is wholesome and what is unwholesome? What is good and what is bad? What is evil and what is ugly? What is dark and what is bright? What is the retribution of dark and bright? What is the meaning of the Dharma seen in this life? What is the meaning of precepts for the next life? What is good and what is evil?' Having heard from them, they do not practice accordingly. Because of such actions, conditioned by such actions, having such conduct, when the body perishes and life ends, they are born in an evil destiny, in niraya. If they come to be born in the human realm, they have wrong wisdom. Why? Māṇava! Because their conduct produces wrong wisdom. Therefore, when certain men or women, among sentient beings, are unable to go and inquire, this, Māṇava, should be known as the retribution of such action.

"Māṇava! What, then, is the cause and what is the condition that certain men or women have right wisdom? Māṇava! There are certain men or women who, among sentient beings, are able to go and inquire from those renowned śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas. Having gone to them, they ask questions at the proper time about the meaning, and they discuss with these worthy ones: 'What is wholesome and what is unwholesome? What is good and what is bad? What is ugly and what is fine? What is dark and what is bright? What is the retribution of dark and bright? What is the meaning of the Dharma seen in this life? What is the meaning of precepts for the next life? What is good and what is evil?' Having heard from them, they practice accordingly. Because of such actions, conditioned by such actions, having such conduct, when the body perishes and life ends, they are born in a good destination, in heaven above. If they come to be born in the human realm, they have right wisdom. Why? Māṇava! Because their conduct produces right wisdom. Therefore, when certain men or women, among sentient beings, are able to go and inquire, this, Māṇava, should be known as the retribution of such action.

"Māṇava! If one performs actions leading to a short life, having performed them, one receives a short life. If one performs actions leading to a long life, having performed them, one receives a long life. If one performs actions leading to illness, having performed them, one has much illness. If one performs actions leading to freedom from illness, having performed them, one is free from illness. If one performs actions leading to ugliness, having performed them, one receives ugliness. If one performs actions leading to a fine appearance, having performed them, one receives a fine appearance. If one performs actions leading to little influence, having performed them, one receives little influence. If one performs actions leading to great influence, having performed them, one receives great influence. If one performs actions leading to lowliness, having performed them, one receives lowliness. If one performs actions leading to nobility, having performed them, one receives nobility. If one performs actions leading to little wealth, having performed them, one receives little wealth. If one performs actions leading to much wealth, having performed them, one receives much wealth. If one performs actions leading to wrong wisdom, having performed them, one receives wrong wisdom. If one performs actions leading to right wisdom, having performed them, one receives right wisdom. This, Māṇava, is what I have previously explained. Māṇava! According to the actions that sentient beings perform, because of their actions, conditioned by their actions, through these actions, because sentient beings act, there come to be these differences of high and low, good and bad."

"It is finished, Gautama! It is finished, Gautama! World-Honored One! I now take refuge in the Dharma and the Sangha of bhikṣus. World-Honored One! From this day forward, I take and uphold the precepts of an upāsaka. From this day forward, for the rest of my life, I will abstain from killing. I now take refuge. World-Honored One! From this day forward, just as you enter the homes of other upāsakas in the city of Śrāvastī, so too may you enter the home of Todeyya. May the household of Todeyya, throughout the long night, receive benefit and gain peace."

Thus spoke the Buddha. Śuka Māṇava, son of Todeyya, having heard what the World-Honored One said, departed joyfully.
