# 金剛頂普賢瑜伽大教王經大樂不空金剛薩埵一切時方成就儀
## Ritual of Accomplishment in All Times and Places for Mahāsukha Amogha-Vajrasattva from the Vajraśekhara Samantabhadra Yoga Great Teaching King Sūtra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1121

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown.

---

> I bow in homage to the great secret, born from the vajra-holder.
> The mantras are easy to accomplish; I shall explain the recitation ritual.
> The mantras have prior response; generate the mind to benefit and bring happiness
> To all sentient beings without remainder. Recite this at will:
> The mantra of innate accomplishment. One should generate the bodhicitta.

*Oṃ sarva-yoga-citta-utpādayāmi*

> By merely reciting the mantra, generating the bodhicitta,
> All obstacles are entirely removed, and one obtains all joy and delight of mind.
> One is not hindered by the hosts of Māra, and becomes equal to the Samyak-saṃbuddha,
> Receiving the offerings of all the worlds. Further contemplate that all dharmas,
> And forms are without self-nature, empty.

> Then see through to the original mind, round and white like a full moon.
> A five-pronged white vajra rises upright upon the face of the moon-disc.
> Then contemplate the wisdom-vajra transforming into the Bhagavān,
> The body of Vajrasattva, whose form and garments are like the bright moon,
> Seated upon a white lotus pedestal, with a crown adorned by the five buddhas,
> Replete with all ornamental adornments, with blazing red light behind,
> Abiding within the moon-disc. Furthermore, with five vajras
> Adorn the heart moon-disc. Thus contemplate one's own body.
> With two vajra fists, the left at the hip and the right at the heart:
> This is called the Vajrasattva pride-mudrā, the hūṃkāra.
> The wisdom leg pressing upon the samādhi thigh is called the Samantabhadra cross-legged posture.
> Next form the wondrous bliss samaya secret mudrā:
> All fingers should be interlocked; with the ring and little fingers as the base,
> The thumb enters that gateway. Gently move and press together.
> Recite the surata, the mantra of staṃbha.
> Through this mudrā and mantra, the holy one enters one's body.
> Seal the heart, forehead, throat, and crown. Then form the samādhi fist:
> The thumbs and ring fingers both enter the center;
> The index fingers bend and press against the backs.
> Recite: *Oṃ vajra-muṣṭi vaṃ.* Then
> Spread into two karma fists and recite *sarvasiddhi.*
> Again present the previous garland mudrā, the Mahāsukha Vajra Heart,
> With the mantra hūṃkāra. Next join the vajra palms:
> The middle fingers touch like a lotus closing;
> The index fingers rest at the first joint.
> Place at the crown, forehead, rotating to the right:
> This forms the five-buddha crown.
> The mantras shall now be proclaimed. At the crown's center, Vairocana Buddha:
> Recite *Oṃ svāṃ khaṃ.* At the hairline, Akṣobhya Buddha:
> Recite *Vajra-sattva,* completing the mantra.
> At the crown's right, Ratnasaṃbhava Buddha,
> Likewise called *Vajra-ratna,* completing the phrase.
> At the back, Amitāyus Buddha:
> Recite again *Vajra-dharma,* completing the mantra.
> At the crown's left, recite the Amoghasiddhi
> Buddha's mantra: *Vajra-karma.* The mudrā that distributes the buddha-body's supreme seal:
> Form two vajra fists, cross the arms at the forehead,
> Separate the fists to the jade ears, the index fingers winding around each other.
> Bind the vajra garland three times, separate the hands to both sides of the ears,
> And from the little fingers and thumbs scatter in succession, with grace letting fall the silk ribbons.
> Recite *Vajra-mālā svaṃ vaṃ.* Through
> This mudrā and the rest, one receives the complete abhiṣeka.
> To summon the holy assembly, clap staggered palms three times
> And recite *Vajra-toṣya guhya,* the mantra.
> By clapping the vajra palms, one swiftly becomes the sattva's body.
> Next one should universally establish
> The retinue of sixteen honored ones surrounding the Great Bliss.
> The eye is placed before, in front:
> Two hands hold bow and arrow, form and garments in lotus-red.
> With two vajra fists, bend the bow as if to shoot,
> And recite *Jaḥ vajra-dṛṣṭisāyake māṭa.*
> To the right, Kīlīkilā:
> Cross the arms in vajra fists, the right holding a vajra within,
> Moon-colored with lotus eyes. Recite *Hūṃ vajra-kīlīkile hūṃ.*
> Behind, holding banner and pennant,
> With makara as the crest, the wisdom fist raises arm and elbow,
> The samādhi fist with the ring and little fingers in water gesture,
> Form and eyes like blue lotuses. *Vaṃ vajriṇī-smaraṃ raṭa.*
> Having recited this mantra, place both fists at the waist,
> Lean the head slightly to the left, and recite *Guhya vajra-kāme-nirvṛttraṃ,*
> The mantra completed. The Vajra Garland dwells at the left, mind-body in lotus-yellow.
> At the front right corner, the season of spring:
> Two hands hold an offering tray of flowers,
> Form and garments in lotus-white. With two fists palm-up, scatter upward:
> Named "flowers filling the realm of space." Recite *Oṃ madhu vajra-kuṃ oṃ.*
> At the back right corner, the season of rain:
> Holding a censer offering burning incense, body-color like a hanging cloud.
> Cover both hands and press downward: fragrant clouds pervade with scent.
> Recite the mantra: *Oṃ vajra-meghi guru guru.* At the back left corner, the season of autumn:
> Red-colored, holding lamp-candles. The thumbs and ring fingers press together:
> The vajra lamp raised all around. Recite *Oṃ śara vajra-aṃ.*
> Scatter in rain and wipe one's own chest:
> This forms the perfume seal. Recite *Oṃ vajra-śeṣire hūṃ hūṃ.*
> Holding and offering the perfume vessel,
> Abide at the front left corner, winter, body in lotus-yellow garments.
> At the outer maṇḍala, at the four corners, establish:
> In the flower direction, named "play": cover two fists at the heart,
> Rotate and recite *He rati vajra-vilāsinī trāṭ.*
> Raise palms with fingers beside the mouth and,
> Smiling, recite *He rati vajra-hāse hāhā-kārāya,*
> Named "laughter," dwelling outside the cloud.
> Autumn's outer, Vajra Song: the samādhi hand holds a Brahmā harp,
> The samādhi fist with the ring finger slightly bent, the curved arm like a harp;
> The wisdom fist's index likewise, between the samādhi forearms.
> Singing: *He rati vajra-gīte tītī,* the mantra completed. Spinning dance, turning all fingers,
> Scatter the fists and re-form them, above the left and right cheeks rotating upward,
> Vajra palms scattered above the crown. Recite *He rati vajra-nṛte vepavepa,*
> The dance place at winter's outer corner.
> Thus the four offerings: all are of golden body-color.
> The little fingers and thumbs hook and clasp, arms cross with the ring fingers extended,
> The index fingers bent at the first joint like hooks.
> Recite *Vajra-kuśe jaḥ,* the mantra:
> Holding the hook, guarding the front gate.
> Then with the index and ring fingers standing
> Like a ring forming a lasso, recite again *Vajra-pāśe hūṃ,* the mantra:
> Holding the lasso, guarding the right gate.
> The index and ring fingers tightly knotted, pressed together, then recite
> *Vajra-śaṅkhale vaṃ,* the mantra completed:
> With the chain, stationed at the rear gate.
> Without changing the previous mudrā form,
> At the heart gently shake: named "ringing the vajra bell."
> *Vajra-ghaṇṭe guhya,* the mantra recited:
> Holding the bell, guarding the left gate. The four gathering deities' forms and colors
> Should be internalized as the four mudrās.

As above, the sixteen honored ones complete the wondrous installation as guests, beautiful and graceful in every posture, seated on lotuses in the Samantabhadra cross-legged posture, all abiding upon moon-discs, with all ornamental adornments decorating their bodies, all wearing five-buddha crowns, garments of lotus-color matching their body-color, all dyed to delight the eye, gazing at the vajra-holder.

> Having thus established, the maṇḍala is complete.
> Therein, like a wheel shape, eight pillars adorned,
> Between the supreme vajra pillars, moon-discs serve as ornament.
> The outer maṇḍala, in four directions, should have four gates,
> With four outer gates corresponding. Gate-doors interlocking in harmony,
> Filled and adorned with vajra jewels. At the corners of the maṇḍala,
> Half-moons serve as ornament. Garlands, bells, silk banners,
> Hung and arrayed everywhere: a gentle breeze blows and stirs them,
> Producing harmonious and pleasant sounds. Surrounded by the vajradhātu,
> Space itself serves as the palace, with the maṇḍala at the center.
> Thus contemplate with careful attention: at all times and in all places, abiding,
> Established by one's own mind, one's own body as the main deity.
> All such offerings surround and are presented to one's own body,
> Following constantly in walking and resting. All forms that are seen:
> Contemplate that they are all empty. Then think again of the main deity's body.
> Recite *Oṃ kālu muḥ kha-sarva-dharma-nāma-aji-nu paṃ nātu,* the mantra,
> Or simply recite the syllable Oṃ. Through one's own superior understanding, contemplate:
> Perfume, lamps, incense, flower garlands, food and drink,
> Clothing, jeweled banners, canopies, fans, whisks, pennants, bells appearing,
> Song, dance, supreme palaces, pervading all the realms of space.
> "I now transform all these into the most supreme offerings,
> For all the holy ones to enjoy." Then recite the four praise-kings:
> The arrow, embrace, banner, and garland mudrās. In chorus sing the Vajra Desire:
> *Sarva-anurāga sukha-sattva-māṃ-nābha-praveśa-nirmāṇa.*
> *Vaṃ vajra-sattva-parama-surata-mitha-bhava-me mahā-sukha-nṛtya-
> Sārāyaṇa-dhvaja. Pratipattana-
> Siddhi-calagu-praṇata-māna.*
> Through this vajra song, great bliss follows what is beloved,
> Blissful ease becomes firm, and all wishes are accomplished.
> With wondrous words of bliss, recite according to one's portion the syllable *Ā*:
> All dharmas are originally unborn; one should know this meaning.
> The great bliss body is not empty; constantly recite hūṃkāra.
> The vajra that holds the dharma-mudrā: place any mantra at will,
> Hūṃkāra or another. With each recitation, emit blazing light
> Pervading from every pore, like sphaṭika crystal,
> The sun manifesting bright moonlight. The vajras of body, speech, and mind:
> All should be thus contemplated; accomplishing all the teachings.
> The vajra speech recitation: lips and teeth closed without sound,
> Gently moving the tongue in recitation. Cultivating in this way,
> Until a full month is complete, or two or six months:
> All visualized signs will manifest; one attains the sattva's body,
> Abiding before the holy assembly. With *Oṃ kāla* make offerings,
> Again present the eight offerings, sing the four praise-kings in chanting.
> The vajra palms joined together, the middle fingers upright like a banner.
> Recite: *Oṃ kṛṣṭa va-sarva-sattva-nadatā anu-gasā-dra-bhaṃ
> Buddha-viṣayaṃ punarāgama-nāstu oṃ vajra-sattva muḥ.*
> Having recited this mantra, scatter the mudrā above the crown,
> And with fists send off the holy assembly, flowing forth from the crown.
> If one wishes at all times to practice this ritual with intention,
> Seeing all forms and so forth, visualize them all as offerings to the main deity.
> Contemplate one's body as the empty mark of form: this is my main deity.
> Contemplate in this way: this is called the eye of superior understanding.
> All those forms beheld are empty; contemplate and contemplate again:
> The body of Vajrasattva. This is already one with the honored one.
> Know that all is originally empty: thus they are the same, one body.
> Abiding in this yoga, whether walking, standing, sitting, or lying down,
> Recite the radiant mantra at will, completing one hundred thousand recitations,
> Through the night without sleeping, reciting until dawn.
> Furthermore, at all times, using the previous wondrous bliss
> Samaya and garland mudrās, each reciting their respective mantras,
> One's own body becomes the main deity, the heart bound in yoga.
> Afterward, at the four times, recite within the ritual site,
> Altogether completing one hundred thousand recitations. Until the last watch of the night,
> As before, without sleeping, recite until dawn.
> Before long one will attain accomplishment. The main deity's body is the buddha's body:
> To obtain it is not even difficult; how much more so all the siddhis.
> The excellent ācārya compiled this subtle meaning;
> I have briefly explained the great sūtra, gathering the most secret essentials.
> Those who have not entered the maṇḍala and presumptuously use it accumulate the defilement of offense;
> As stated in all the teachings, they commit great grave karmic obstacles.
> Yet nothing can remove these except forming the Vajrasattva
> Great Mudrā, which can extinguish them all. Those who cultivate this samādhi,
> With proximate intention performing all dharmas, which are not spoken of in any teaching:
> All will be accomplished as one wishes. As soon as one forms the great mudrā,
> All the tathāgatas pay homage. The mantra-holder thereby becomes
> The supreme lord of all the buddhas. The awesome power of the great mudrā
> Is supremely quiescent, without peer. Without performing all the ritual procedures,
> Only forming this one great mudrā: nothing can obstruct it,
> And one swiftly attains accomplishment. Protecting the original samaya,
> Not causing body or mind to suffer, receiving bliss to attain accomplishment.
> May all sentient beings universally have faith, receive, and enter this dharma,
> And may all swiftly accomplish the body of Vajrasattva,
> The firm body of Vairocana, and all bodies as wished.

Colophon: Written in Brahmic and Chinese characters by the monk Yuanli at Qinglong Temple in the Western Capital on the fourteenth day of the eighth month of the third year of Dali [768 CE].

Copied at the Keirin Hall on the twenty-eighth day of the ninth month of the first year of Ōtoku [1084 CE], from a Tang-dynasty original at the Zentōin. The Tang original of the Thousand-Arm Ritual was kept at the Taiei. Noted by Ryōki.
