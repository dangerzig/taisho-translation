# 北斗七星念誦儀軌
## Ritual Manual for the Recitation of the Seven Stars of the Northern Dipper

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1305

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Vajrabodhi (金剛智)

---

At that time, the Tathāgata, for the sake of all beings of meager merit in the final age of the Dharma, expounded the teachings of the dhāraṇīs. At that time, all the suns, moons, and stellar constellations gathered like clouds, surrounding him on all sides, and with one voice spoke, saying: "We humbly beseech the Tathāgata to expound for us the divine dhāraṇī."

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke the Dhāraṇī of the Eight Stars:

> *oṃ satvanāya pañcamijaya jambhutama svāminā rakṣa bhavatu svāhā*

The mudrā and mantra derive from the *Vajraśekhara Sūtra*, "Chapter on the Seven Stars."

The Buddha addressed Tān Láng, Pò Jūn, and the others, saying: "If there are good men and good women who uphold and recite this divine dhāraṇī, will you protect them?"

At that time, the Eight Ladies spoke to the World-Honored One, saying: "If there are people who recite this divine dhāraṇī every day, their definite karmic offenses will all be extinguished and all their prayers and wishes will be fulfilled. Furthermore, if there are people who are able to recite this divine dhāraṇī one hundred and eight times every day, they will immediately obtain protection for themselves and all their dependents. If they are able to recite five hundred times, through great spiritual power and authority, [the protectors] will universally surround them within five hundred yojanas. All māra kings, all māra hordes, all obstructors, and countless evil spirits will not dare to approach, and [we] will constantly protect them. Any among the Eight Ladies of the Northern Dipper, all the suns, moons, and stellar constellations, and all devas, nāgas, and yakṣas who are capable of creating obstacles and difficulties will at once be cut off and destroyed.

"If a person wishes to make offerings, they should first give rise to a mind of rescue and deliverance, and at a pure and quiet place make offerings of incense, flowers, food, and drink, while holding and reciting the divine dhāraṇī and forming the mudrā seal. When making offerings in this manner, the Eight Ladies and all their retinues will manifest in bodily form, attending upon [the practitioner] according to their wishes, and fulfilling countless prayers and aspirations. The highest stations will be immediately obtained; how much more so the minor official posts and worldly honors. If they seek long life, [we] will strike their names from the register of fixed karma and return them to the register of life.

"If various kings, princes, great ministers, and ladies of the inner palace construct a maṇḍala within their own palaces and perform homa rites, prostrations, and offerings according to the Dharma, the Eight Ladies of the Northern Dipper will all be greatly pleased. Therefore, [such rulers] will long abide in superior positions and constantly receive peace and happiness; all officials, high and low, will be harmonious and will not practice unlawful deeds; the people will flourish, crops and harvests will be abundant, and the national territory will be at peace. There will be no disasters or calamities, no strange omens will appear, and epidemics and deaths will not arise; enemies and bands of thieves within the kingdom's borders will naturally retreat and disperse."

Therefore, this Dharma is supremely secret. Among those without faith and those without wisdom, do not recklessly propagate it. Because those without wisdom have unclean minds, they do not grasp the meaning of the Dharma and give rise to doubt and slander. However, among those without wisdom, those born of the Vajra lineage and Vajra sons who constantly recite and uphold the vidyā of Buddhalocanā should propagate it. Even if Vajra sons are without wisdom, they do not give rise to doubt and slander; therefore, they accomplish the Dharma. Although practitioners may enjoy worldly pleasures, they should deeply dedicate [all merit] toward unsurpassed, perfect enlightenment (*anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi*).

---

**Notes:** The star names Tān Láng (貪狼, "Greedy Wolf") and Pò Jūn (破軍, "Army-Breaker") are the Chinese names for the first and seventh stars of the Northern Dipper (Ursa Major), here personified as deity-rulers addressed by the Buddha. The "Eight Stars" and "Eight Ladies" likely refer to the seven stars of the Northern Dipper plus an auxiliary star (輔星). The dhāraṇī is reconstructed from its Chinese phonetic transcription; the "二合" notations in the original indicate ligature syllables formed by combining two phonetic elements.
