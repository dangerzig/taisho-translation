# 勝幢臂印陀羅尼經
## Dhāraṇī Sūtra of the Victorious Banner Arm-Seal

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1363

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Xuanzang (玄奘).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Bhagavat was on the summit of Mount Gṛdhrakūṭa, in the divine palace of celestial sages, together with a great assembly of twelve hundred and fifty bhikṣus and also with innumerable bodhisattva mahāsattvas. Mañjuśrī and Bhadrapāla, the sixteen great men, were at their head, along with devas, humans, asuras, and others, with the Great Brahmā King at their head, a boundless great assembly surrounding him on all sides.

At that time the World-Honored One, abiding in the wisdom of right mindfulness, was teaching the wondrous and subtle Dharma for the great assembly. Then the Great Brahmā King, leading the host of devas and asuras, bowed at the Buddha's feet, joined their palms in reverence, stood before the Tathāgata, and together addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! In the past, wishing to benefit and bring joy to all sentient beings, you sought to realize unsurpassed, perfect enlightenment. With a compassionate vow permeating your mind, you achieved perfect enlightenment. You possess great supernatural power and are endowed with great compassion. Why then do we now see sentient beings falling into the hells, the realm of hungry ghosts, the animal realm, and enduring extreme suffering among humans and devas, yet you do not employ skillful and skillful means to rescue and deliver them? We humbly pray that you will take compassionate pity and enable them to escape their suffering and hardship." Then Avalokiteśvara also led innumerable mantra-holding celestial sages, bowed at the Buddha's feet, joined their palms in reverence, stood before the Tathāgata, and together addressed the Buddha: "We humbly pray that, out of compassionate pity for all sentient beings, you will reveal the skillful Dharma for rescuing them from suffering and hardship."

At that time the World-Honored One addressed the two great beings and their retinues: "Take your seats. I shall now, out of compassion for all sentient beings, briefly teach the skillful Dharma for rescuing them from suffering and hardship. You should listen carefully and reflect upon it well. There is a great dhāraṇī called the Victorious Banner Arm-Seal. If one constantly recites it, one can eliminate the five rebellious acts, the ten evils, and other such sins. One will never again be reborn in the evil destinies or suffer the extreme pain found among humans and devas. Those who constantly uphold it will obtain wealth and position in this present life, and in future lives will be born into noble families. Whatever they desire will appear before them, and they will enjoy all happiness. They will constantly encounter the buddhas, obtain the recollection of past lives, and attain unsurpassed, perfect enlightenment." The dhāraṇī states:

*Kara kara kiri kiri kuru kuru sara sara siri siri suru suru sarva-buddha-avalokite dhara dhara sarva-dharma-loka-mukhe cara cara sarva-saṃgha-adhiṣṭhite bhara bhara anika-buddha-koṭi-bhāsite cīṇa cīṇa sarva-karma-varaṇa-nirmama sarva-sattvānāṃ ca sarva-pāpāni sarva-duḥkha-padāya bṛṭa bṛṭa dṛṭa dṛṭa naṭa naṭa kaṭa kaṭa sasphuṭāya sasphuṭāya sarva-varaṇa-pāya-duggatini svāhā.*

"Good man! This Dhāraṇī of the Victorious Banner Arm-Seal has been spoken by buddhas as numerous as the sands of the Ganges. I now, out of compassion for all sentient beings, and because of your request, also teach it for the assembly. Good man! The appearance of buddhas in the world is exceedingly rare. To obtain a human body well-endowed is even rarer. To hear this divine mantra is rarer still. If a good man or good woman can properly receive and uphold it, this is yet more difficult to obtain. How much more so to copy it, diligently recite it, and encourage others to receive and uphold it! Good man! I recall that in the past, from the Medicine Master Tathāgata of Lapis Lazuli Light and Sudarśana and other buddhas, I heard this divine mantra, received, upheld, read, recited, and correctly taught it to others. Through this I realized unsurpassed enlightenment. Therefore you too should diligently study it, encourage all sentient beings to receive, uphold, read, and recite it, and enable them to escape suffering and hardship and obtain supreme benefit and joy."

When the Bhagavat had spoken this sūtra, Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva, Bhadrapāla Bodhisattva, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, the Great Brahmā King, and all the devas, humans, asuras, and the rest of the entire great assembly, having heard what the Buddha taught, all greatly rejoiced, believed, received it with faith, and put it into practice.
