# 清淨法身毘盧遮那心地法門成就一切陀羅尼三種悉地

## The Pure Dharmakāya Vairocana: The Mind-Ground Dharma Gate for Accomplishing All Dhāraṇī and the Three Kinds of Siddhi

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0899

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown.

---

At that time, Vairocana Buddha was in the Lotus Treasury World, together with hundreds of billions of emanation-body Śākyamuni Buddhas, teaching the bodhisattva Dharma of the mind-ground śīla pure-conduct chapter for realizing the path of awakening.

At that time, the hundreds of billions of Śākyamunis spoke with different mouths but in one voice, saying: "Dharmakāya World-Honored One! Although all sentient beings have received the mind-ground Dharma gate, they are unable to devote themselves to its diligent study. Even if they briefly hold it in mind, they soon let it go, give free rein to body and mind, and indulge in unwholesome acts. Some may think: 'I have received the profoundly wondrous Dharma gate of all buddhas, but I cannot practice diligently. How can I avoid regression and re-arouse the aspiration for the Way?' Day and night they are diligent, cultivating single-mindedly, seeking the unsurpassed Way. But some regress, create more evil karma, and enter the stream of birth and death, no longer contemplating the wondrous Dharma of the mind-ground, cycling through evil destinies with no prospect of escape. How can such sentient beings be tamed and disciplined?"

At that time, Vairocana Buddha addressed the hundreds of billions of Śākyamuni Buddhas: "Listen carefully now! I shall explain the method of taming and disciplining, to bring peace and happiness to all sentient beings. Regarding the mind-ground Dharma, whether beings have heard it or not, all can be tamed. Those who have heard it should be spurred to greater diligence. Those who have not should be caused to arouse the aspiration for the Way.

"You should know that when sentient beings who have received the mind-ground Dharma are not diligent, who take pleasure in creating evil and follow their desires, obstacles should be created for them, causing their bodies to be ill at ease. When such beings are oppressed by suffering, they will surely reflect deeply and re-arouse the aspiration for the Way, practicing diligently day and night in all their study. Why? It is like a wild horse that cannot be tamed: only by adding the whip can it be controlled.

"Furthermore, there are sentient beings who have never heard the Buddha-Dharma. Through slight merit from former lives, they have now obtained a human body and have meager food and clothing. They delight in creating all manner of worldly evil karma and do not seek the causes of liberation and transcendence. Such beings are ablaze with ignorance. They do not know that this body is like an illusion, like a magical transformation, born and dying in the same instant. They only create evil, and at death they enter hell, enduring all manner of bitter suffering with no prospect of escape. For such people, the Buddha has deep compassion.

"You should know: at the very moment when such a person is creating evil, you should know the method of taming. From time to time, afflict them with serious illness that worldly medicine cannot cure. When such beings are oppressed by suffering, they will surely arouse the aspiration for the Way. Only then can they be drawn in and led to enter the Buddha-Way."

When the Śākyamunis heard this teaching, they felt ashamed and abashed. They prostrated, took their leave, and returned to their own source-worlds. Sitting at their respective bodhimaṇḍas, they reflected: "The realm of the buddhas is difficult to understand and enter, inconceivable. Through various skillful means they guide sentient beings, but ordinary beings are ignorant and difficult to discipline." They then addressed the great assembly: "You should all know that the mind-ground śīla pure-conduct Dharma gate is difficult to hear and difficult to see. All of you bodhisattva mahāsattvas, śrāvakas, devas, nāgas, ghosts, spirits, and others should study it, practice diligently with single-minded focus, remember and uphold it without forgetting, and you shall attain buddhahood."

At that time, the Great Wrathful Vajra rose from his seat, manifested great auspicious marks, and radiated inconceivable light illuminating one hundred thousand buddha-fields. He prostrated in homage and addressed Vajrapāṇi: "Great Being! I have heard that all buddhas at their bodhimaṇḍa seats all praise the dhāraṇī gate and its essential methods of comprehensive retention, which establish immeasurable and inconceivable matters beyond conception. Yet thinking of sentient beings, the majority have little merit. Even if they uphold the practice, they do not achieve accomplishment. I only wish that the Great Being would devise great skillful means for such people, enabling them to achieve accomplishment. How can they attain the three kinds of siddhi? How should the nine kinds of altar be constructed? How should body and mind be settled for reciting mantras? When first beginning recitation, what signs appear by which one knows siddhi will be attained? How should one choose a dwelling place? How should one choose food? How should one make various offerings? How should one maintain proper deportment in walking, standing, sitting, and lying down, and what should one contemplate? For impoverished beings who have nothing for offerings, how should they hold the proper visualization to attain mahā-siddhi?"

Then Vajrapāṇi said to the Wrathful Kuṇḍalī: "This should be asked of the Buddha. I cannot answer it."

Then the two vajra-holders addressed the Buddha in unison: "World-Honored One! The Buddha has taught the comprehensive dhāraṇī Dharma gate. How should sentient beings study and practice it?" They asked each question in detail as above.

At that time, the Buddha told the two vajra-holders: "This should be asked of Vairocana Buddha, who can answer these matters."

Then the Buddha entered samādhi and, through supernatural power, caused the entire great assembly, whether devas, nāgas, ghosts, spirits, or any other beings, all to travel to the Lotus Treasury World. They prostrated in homage and said: "Dharmakāya World-Honored One! Among my sentient beings, those who practice the essential dhāraṇī methods mostly do not achieve accomplishment. Even if they gain some small effect, vināyakas immediately create various obstacles, preventing accomplishment. They further cause such persons to revert to their original nature, generate a mind of weariness and aversion, regress, and abandon the practice. Such beings simply do not know the wondrous mind-ground Dharma and the realm of the buddhas, and therefore they do not accomplish the three kinds of siddhi and are instead deluded and confused by ghosts and spirits. We only wish that the World-Honored One would have compassion for sentient beings and, for our sake, explain the essential rules of pure practice for the comprehensive method, so we may know: How does one attain the three kinds of siddhi? How should body and mind be settled for mantra recitation so as not to be obstructed by evil ghosts and spirits?"

At that time, Vairocana Buddha said: "You should know that I recall in the distant past, when I first aroused the aspiration for the Way, I was in a forest hermitage, sitting upright in contemplation, cultivating the mind-ground Dharma gate. Because I lacked wisdom, my mind was unsettled and the dharmas did not manifest before me. Since my mind was unsettled, various delusive thoughts and grasping attachments arose. For this reason, I was deluded and confused by ghosts and spirits, and mistakenly entered the realm of Māra, thinking it was the Buddha-Dharma. I generated thoughts of attachment and desire, taking them for the ultimate attainment. Unaware and unknowing, I passed through incalculable kalpas, harmed by Māra.

"Later, I suddenly recovered my original mind and knew that this was the work of demons and not the Buddha-Dharma. Even so, I had no means to remedy it. I cried out loudly, calling to all buddhas: 'The buddhas possess the eye of wisdom! Why do you not see that I am tormented by Māra?' At that time, innumerable emanation buddhas in empty space immediately told me: 'Excellent, bodhisattva! Listen carefully now! We shall explain the method for removing Māra. There is a great divine mantra called the mind-ground mantra. One who recites and upholds it will swiftly attain sarvajña-jñāna, the wisdom of all aspects, and will not be exploited by any māra.'

"Hearing these words, my heart was filled with great joy. I said: 'O Buddhas, please speak it for me.' Then the emanation buddhas spoke it for me. Having heard it, I remembered and upheld it without forgetting. At that very moment, all the māras retreated and scattered. At that time, I attained the patience of the unborn (anutpattika-dharma-kṣānti), and the great path of awakening was naturally perfected.

"You should know that this mind-ground divine mantra is used by all buddhas who cultivate the mind-ground Dharma. That any buddha could achieve accomplishment without reciting this mantra is impossible. That one could sit at the bodhimaṇḍa without reciting this mantra and be free from all māra-difficulties is impossible. That any buddha could possess sarvajña without reciting this mantra is impossible. That one could be sovereign over all dharmas without reciting this mantra is impossible. That any humans or devas using other mantras could attain siddhi without first reciting this mantra is impossible. That one who first recites this mantra and then uses other mantras would fail to achieve results is impossible. That one who recites this mantra could be afflicted by vināyakas is impossible. Why? Because this mantra is the essential mind-ground Dharma of all buddhas. Whether worldly or supramundane, what dharma does not arise from the mind-ground? From what else could it be established? Therefore, the profoundly deep realm of all buddhas cannot be entered by anyone."

At that time, Śākyamuni Buddha and the entire great assembly prostrated and said: "World-Honored One! We only wish you would speak it."

At that time, Vairocana Buddha then spoke the mind-ground divine mantra to Śākyamuni, reciting the mantra:

*Oṃ sutiṣṭha vajra*

Having spoken this mantra, jeweled flowers rained from the sky. All the finest incense, flowers, and music from the worlds of the ten directions gathered like clouds as offerings. Celestial musicians filled the sky. All devas and nāgas declared: "Never before has such a thing occurred!" Although the Buddha spoke the Dharma, among the assembly some heard and some did not. Why? Those whose minds had attained liberation, who had comprehended the three kinds of emptiness, who had attained the Dharma-eye, who could enter the realm of the buddhas without fear, and who had no obstacles regarding the mind-ground Dharma: all of these heard it. Those who well understood the skillful means spoken by all buddhas and comprehended that all dharmas are like illusions also heard it. Those who did not possess the wisdom described above did not hear it, just as a person drunk on wine who lies down is unaware and unknowing.

At that time, having spoken this mantra, Vairocana Buddha suddenly vanished, entering the pure dharmadhātu as one body, pervading the buddha-fields of the ten directions, equal to great empty space with no difference whatsoever. The entire assembly could not see him.

Then Śākyamuni Buddha also followed Vairocana Buddha, entering the dharmadhātu as one true essence, and the entire assembly could no longer see him either.

Then the five great bodhisattvas, Mañjuśrī, Samantabhadra, Avalokiteśvara, Maitreya, and Vajragarbha, all followed in attendance upon Śākyamuni, entering the deep dharmadhātu to hear Vairocana Buddha explain the profoundly deep realm of the essential mind-ground Dharma gate. The entire assembly did not know where our original teacher Śākyamuni Buddha and the great bodhisattvas had gone.

At that time, Vairocana Buddha was in the pure dharmadhātu, having entered a place that is no place, and spoke only to the five great bodhisattvas, explaining the rules, deportment, and signs of siddhi for upholding the mind-ground divine mantra Dharma gate.

The Buddha declared: "You should know that if one upholds this mind-ground divine mantra and wishes to study dhyāna, samādhi, and wisdom, to enter all samādhis, and to realize the patience of the unborn, one should know that one must first recite the mind-ground mantra one million times. After that, sit in full lotus with the right hand pressing upon the left hand. Close the eyes and contemplate no-place. Cut off all thoughts, yet do not depart from thought. Cut off all conditions, yet do not depart from conditions. First contemplate the four great elements and five aggregates as having no existence. Having contemplated thus, recite this mind-ground mantra twenty-one times. One will then naturally enter immeasurable samādhis and attain the patience of the unborn. Such a realm of realization can only be known by one who has experienced it; it cannot be described. When reciting this mantra, do not allow the mouth, tongue, or throat to move. Let the mind recite it. Furthermore, one must enter the thought of no-thought. This is called true recitation."

He further declared: "You should know that for those who delight in the Mahāyāna and study dhyāna and wisdom, if they are persons of dull faculties from past lives without the proper causes, who cannot settle the mind according to the previously described sequence, they need only recite the mind-ground divine mantra one million times, and the nature of inherent wisdom will open. They will swiftly realize the patience of the unborn. If one has previously studied and practiced but has temporarily forgotten one's former wisdom, there is no need for extensive recitation. Simply follow the previous method of settling the mind-ground and recite twenty-one times, and one will attain dhyāna, samādhi, and wisdom. Such a person can enter all realms without fear, and prajñāpāramitā is naturally perfected. Such a realm of realization can only be known by one who has experienced it."

He further said: "If there are people who hold the divine mantras of the three divisions and wish to use this mind-ground divine mantra as an auxiliary for accomplishment, I shall now explain the distinctions for you. Those who hold buddha-division mantras should first recite the mind-ground mantra one million times. Those who hold bodhisattva-division mantras should recite the mind-ground mantra two million times. Those who hold vajra-division mantras should recite the mind-ground mantra three million times. If a person completes these numbers of recitations, whatever root mantra they hold will be accomplished. Why? Because this mind mantra is the mother of all mantras. Therefore, all mantra deities dare not disobey. If what one does is not going as wished, simply recite the mind-ground mantra twenty-one times mentally, and great efficacious results will come. All mantra deities will rush forward like shooting stars, not daring to disobey or resist instructions."

He further said: "You should know that if a person holds the mantras of the three divisions but has not yet attained siddhi, because as an ordinary being they are ablaze with ignorance, they may suddenly generate a mind of regression, engage in improper conduct, become attached to the five desires, and allow the mantra they hold to be interrupted. Perhaps after a long time, they suddenly awaken on their own, arouse the mind of enlightenment, and resume holding the original mantra. For such people, I shall now explain the continuation mantra, enabling sentient beings to have no interruption in nature, making the continuation and the original practice one, without regression." He then spoke the continuation mantra:

*Namo bhagavate uṣṇīṣa oṃ bhūriṃ bandha ye svāhā tathāgatū svābhāye svāhā padmanī svābhāye svāhā vajra-maṇi svābhāye svāhā maṇi maṇi maṇi kūrāye svāhā tadyathā hūṃ huṃ phaṭ maṇi-dhari hūṃ phaṭ oṃ hūṃ hūṃ gaga phaṭ maṇi-vajrī hūṃ phaṭ*

"This is the continuation divine mantra, also called the mind-ground root divine mantra. It can extinguish grave transgressions. Whatever karmic offenses exist, reciting this mantra can extinguish them all. It can also destroy the efficacy of all other mantras, and it can also restore the efficacy of all mantras. It is unnecessary to elaborate, as both aspects of recitation and upholding yield immeasurable efficacious results.

"If a person who holds a mantra has had an interruption and wishes to resume, they should first recite the original mantra they were holding according to their ability, for a lesser or greater number of times. Then they should make aspirations and prostrate to Vairocana Buddha: 'Disciple so-and-so previously recited such-and-such a mantra for such-and-such a number of times. There was an interruption. Now I have again recited such-and-such a number. I request all buddhas to join my previous recitation practice with my current recitation, making them continuous without interruption.' Then recite the continuation divine mantra one thousand and eight times. Again make aspirations as before. This will make the practice identical to having been unbroken and without regression from the beginning."

Vairocana said: "Guard and protect this mantra. Do not transmit it to persons without wisdom. Why? Because persons without wisdom, hearing the skillful means spoken by all buddhas, will generate attachment and indolence, will not practice diligently, will give rise to thoughts of the five desires, and will regress from the mind of enlightenment. Why? Because such persons have faculties that are not firm and they possess little wisdom. They will enter the stream of birth and death and, though they may practice further, it will ultimately be of no benefit. Why? It is like a person who deliberately soils a new garment and then tries to wash it clean. Why not simply not soil it in the first place? Therefore, this mantra is the same. For those who have never regressed from the beginning, reciting this mantra as an auxiliary will bring swift accomplishment."

At that time, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva said: "Dharmakāya World-Honored One! For those who hold mantras and seek the signs of the three kinds of siddhi, what are the signs of the upper siddhi? What are the signs of the middle siddhi? What are the signs of the lower siddhi? We only wish the World-Honored One would explain, so that all bodhisattva mahāsattvas and all humans and devas may universally attain accomplishment, reach the stage of non-regression, attain the great samādhi, realize the path of awakening, achieve perfect enlightenment, guide the assemblies of devas and humans, lead them to enter nirvāṇa, and forever leave birth and death, no longer enduring suffering."

At that time, Vairocana Buddha declared: "If you wish to know the three kinds of siddhi, I shall explain them for you. You should know that the three divisions share the same upper, middle, and lower siddhi signs, and each of the three divisions has its own three kinds of siddhi.

"If good men wish to attain the upper siddhi, they must maintain inner and outer purity: purified of the three bodily, four verbal, and three mental afflictive karmas. First, recite the mantra they hold, completing the number of recitations prescribed in the original sūtra. Then recite the mind-ground mantra, also completing the prescribed number. In a quiet, secluded place, sit in full lotus on a seat of pure grass. Burn the finest incense as an offering. Make vast and great vows: 'I announce to all buddhas of the ten directions, all great bodhisattvas, all great vajra-holders, all devas, invisible spirit officials, and holy ones: may all witness and verify that disciple so-and-so wishes to uphold such-and-such a mantra. May the World-Honored Ones, bodhisattvas, vajra-holders, and devas truly witness and verify this and grant swift accomplishment.' Having made this aspiration three times, close the eyes and sit.

"First recite the mantra one holds eight hundred times. Visualize one's own body as the body of the presiding mantra deity, with every bodily feature adorned with marks and signs, noting whether the body has an aura or not, the posture of sitting or standing, expressions of wrath or joy, and every movement, all following exactly the painted image as described in the original sūtra. Make this visualization of oneself extremely clear and distinct. Next visualize immeasurable spirit-hosts, retinue members, and messengers, surrounding one in front and behind, reverently attending, each according to what is described in the original text. Make this visualization completely clear.

"When this is complete, recite the mantra one holds twenty-one times mentally. With each recitation, visualize light of patterned radiance streaming from one's own mouth and entering the mouths of all the deities. When the light has fully entered, visualize all the deities in turn entering one's own mouth and being established within the mind-king.

"Practice this visualization three times daily. It must be made clear and distinct. Over a total of three periods of seven days, practice according to this sequence each day, and one will certainly accomplish the upper siddhi.

"Afterwards, not only the matters described in the original sūtra, but whatever inconceivable deeds one wishes to accomplish will all be accomplished. All one's actions, intentions, movements, and activities will be identical with the original mantra deity. One attains great sovereign freedom that cannot be fully described. Practitioners will know this.

"Whenever one wishes to accomplish something, contemplate and think: 'I am the body of the great sage, sovereign and free. I now temporarily transform into an ordinary person, dwelling among the common multitude, to deliver suffering sentient beings while remaining unrecognized.' If one needs to direct the spirit-hosts, visualize them emanating from one's own mind-king, transforming into hundreds of billions of beings, surrounding one in front and behind, carrying out instructions of all kinds without obstruction. This is not through one's own power, but through the power of the mind-ground divine mantra, which can cause all deities to merge with the sovereign divine consciousness as one body.

"Therefore know: those who hold buddha-division or bodhisattva-division mantras may seek the upper siddhi. Those who hold vajra-division or lower mantras should not seek the upper siddhi. Why? Because vajra-holders, devas, yakṣa mantra deities are fierce and violent by nature. If they were to accomplish the upper siddhi, because they would gain sovereign freedom, they would not generate compassion and would harm all ghosts and spirits. However, if one can arouse great compassion, universally pity all beings without generating harmful thoughts or anger, then one may also seek the upper siddhi. If one cannot do this, one should not violate the Buddha's words, or one will incur great transgression. Know this well.

"If one holds the buddhōṣṇīṣa mantra and attains the upper siddhi, one becomes identical with all buddhas. Why? Although the body is that of an ordinary being, the mind attains sovereign freedom, with unhindered eloquence and unobstructed wisdom. One can serve as teacher to all devas, humans, and the world, possessing sarvajña-jñāna, all supernatural powers, which cannot be fully described. Therefore, one should know that there is no difference from a buddha. There are further inconceivable matters that cannot be fully described; only one who has realized them can know.

"If one holds a bodhisattva-division mantra and attains the upper siddhi, one becomes identical with the bodhisattva who originally spoke that mantra, without any difference. Why? Because this ordinary mind, through the bodhisattva's delivering activity and the adornment of mantra power and various skillful means, causes this ordinary being to become identical in mind with the bodhisattva. The bodhisattva has ten thousand practices for benefiting sentient beings and supernatural powers of great sovereign freedom. The mantra-holder likewise attains all of this. Therefore, know that one is identical with the bodhisattva without any difference, protecting the Buddha-Dharma and subjugating all māras.

"If one holds a vajra-division mantra and attains the upper siddhi, one likewise becomes identical with the vajra-holder who originally spoke the Dharma. Why? Because vajra means an indestructible body. The mantra-holder's body and mind become indestructible, and the spiritual power attained is identical with the vajra-holder who originally spoke the mantra. One can protect the Buddha-Dharma, subjugate all māras, and lead them to enter the right path. The mantra-holder can attain all of this. Are there further matters?"

At that time, Samantabhadra Bodhisattva said: "Dharmakāya World-Honored One! Those who hold the mantras of the three divisions and accomplish siddhi each become identical with their respective honored one. How can an ordinary being thus attain the bodies of buddhas, bodhisattvas, and vajra-holders while remaining among ordinary mortals?"

Vairocana Buddha told Samantabhadra: "You should know that buddhas who transform into ordinary beings are not truly ordinary beings. Mantra-holders who attain siddhi are likewise."

At that time, Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva said: "Dharmakāya World-Honored One! The upper siddhi is now understood. What is the middle siddhi? We only wish you would explain it, so that sentient beings may universally attain peace and happiness."

Vairocana Buddha told Mañjuśrī: "For the middle siddhi, whatever mantra of the three divisions one holds, each has its own method for painting the image of the honored one. Follow the original sūtra in every detail for the bodily features, adornments, retinue members, and so forth, all made clear and committed to memory. First complete the prescribed number of recitations, then also complete the prescribed number of mind-ground divine mantra recitations. Whether in the mountains, in the open wilderness, in a city or marketplace, or within a monastery, one needs a quiet place, whether on open ground or within a room. One need only sit quietly, burn various fine incenses, and make offerings to all buddhas, bodhisattvas, vajra-holders, devas, and the presiding mantra deity. Then make great vows and repent of transgressions from former lives and the present, abiding in deep-hearted repentance. After prostrating to the Three Treasures, sit in full lotus, holding the rosary, closing the eyes, settling the visualization, and contemplating the presiding deity standing directly before one, face to face. This visualization must be made completely clear.

"When the visualization is complete, recite the mantra one holds eight hundred times. Recite only mentally, without moving the mouth or tongue. With each recitation, visualize white light emerging from one's own mouth and entering the mouth of the presiding mantra deity. Do not let the light be interrupted. When the recitation is complete, the visualization of light from the deity's mouth must be clear and unbroken. After completing the recitation, make further aspirations, offer incense and flowers with the seal, and when all offerings are complete, rise, prostrate, and dismiss the deity according to the proper method.

"Practice this three times during the day and three times during the night. Each session follows this procedure. Over a total of seven days, the middle siddhi is accomplished.

"If a person who holds the mantras of the three divisions attains this middle siddhi, the great inconceivable accomplishments described in the original sūtra will all be attained. If accomplished within the buddha division, all three divisions are accomplished. If accomplished within the bodhisattva division, all bodhisattva mantras are accomplished, as well as all vajra-division mantras and those of devas, yakṣas, ghosts, and spirits below. If accomplished within the vajra division, not only the original method but all mantras of vajra-holders and below, including devas, nāgas, ghosts, and spirits, are all accomplished. However, the bodhisattva division cannot accomplish buddha-division mantras, and vajra-division mantras cannot accomplish bodhisattva-division mantras. Why? From the heart to the crown is upper; from the navel to the heart is middle; from below to the navel is lower. Therefore, the dharmas cannot be practiced in reverse order. Mantra-holders must clearly understand upper, middle, and lower mantras. This is the general rule of middle siddhi for all three divisions. Know this well."

At that time, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva said: "Dharmakāya World-Honored One! The middle siddhi is now understood. What is the lower siddhi?"

Vairocana Buddha declared: "You should know that if a person who holds the mantras of the three divisions wishes to attain the lower siddhi, first complete the prescribed number of recitations and the mind-ground mantra recitations as previously described. When the number is complete, sit in a quiet place. Burn storax incense as an offering. Then recite the great-wheel vajra seal-mantra twenty-one times. Prostrate and announce: 'May the vajra swiftly descend here! This disciple holds such-and-such a mantra and seeks the accomplishment of such-and-such a wish for great efficacious results. Because this disciple is poor and has nothing for offerings, I only wish the Great Saint would, for this disciple, here arrange and establish a great maṇḍala altar, with every detail according to the sūtra and nothing lacking.'

"Having spoken thus, close the eyes and sit. Visualize the vajra-holder before you, arranging a great maṇḍala altar with the finest articles as offerings. Having visualized this altar, next visualize the four walls and the ground all made of the seven precious substances. Then form the hand-seal. The presiding mantra deities descend and raise the ritual space. The disciple makes offerings and recites the mantra one holds one hundred eight times. Make aspirations from the heart. Practice this six times day and night, visualizing clearly in each session and in proper sequence. Over a total of two periods of seven days, the lower siddhi is accomplished. The visualization of this ritual space and altar must be perfectly clear, without error."

Vairocana Buddha then declared to the hundreds of billions of emanation-body Śākyamuni Buddhas and the five great bodhisattvas: "Regarding the signs of accomplishment of these three kinds of siddhi: if a person who holds the mantras of the three divisions attains these three siddhis, know that such a person will not be long in attaining buddhahood. Why? Because this is the great skillful means of all buddhas, the most essential method of the comprehensive dhāraṇī gate, a profoundly deep realm that is difficult to understand, difficult to enter, and inconceivable."

He further declared: "The meritorious capabilities attained by those who achieve siddhi cannot be fully described; only one who has realized them can know. Why? If I were to fully explain this Dharma gate, some people upon hearing it would become mentally confused and filled with doubt and disbelief. Why? The realm of the buddhas is not fully known even by those at the equal-enlightenment stage. How much more would ordinary beings and devas be terrified?"

At that time, Samantabhadra Bodhisattva rose from his seat and said: "Dharmakāya World-Honored One! All tathāgatas take great compassion as their foundation. Why then do the dhāraṇīs speak of fierce and violent qualities, with majestic sovereign power that can harm ghosts, spirits, non-Buddhist practitioners, devas, and asuras?"

Vairocana said: "Listen carefully now! I shall explain. There are two meanings that should be well understood. What are the two? First, all buddhas use skillful means to teach and guide sentient beings. Second, this fierce and violent form manifests to subjugate all māras and lead them to enter the Buddha-Way.

"You should know that this too is a skillful means. When mantra-holders see such things and generate thoughts of anger, subjugating ghosts and spirits without having attained siddhi, they will not succeed. Furthermore, they do not understand the skillful means spoken by the buddhas. To understand this: 'subjugating' means subjugating the various inversions, delusive thoughts, and grasping attachments within one's own mind, and the various unwholesome actions. Sometimes thoughts of hungry ghosts arise, sometimes thoughts of non-Buddhist practitioners, sometimes thoughts of asuras, sometimes thoughts of evil ghosts, spirits, and rākṣasas. For this reason, thought after thought, all of these are produced as evil ghosts, spirits, devas, asuras, non-Buddhist practitioners, and rākṣasa-ghosts, all arising from the mind. Although one does not immediately experience such retributions, what the sūtras describe as 'crushing and subjugating ghosts and spirits' means that through the power of the mantra, one can extinguish such evil thoughts within the mind. Without these evil thoughts, one will not receive an evil body. Therefore, know that this is what 'subjugation' means.

"If one can first subjugate the evil ghosts and spirits of one's own mind, then all celestial māras, non-Buddhist practitioners, devas, asuras, yakṣas, rākṣasas, and evil ghosts and spirits will naturally submit and not dare to disobey. But if one does not subjugate one's own evil mind and tries to subjugate external devas, ghosts, and spirits, that is impossible."

Samantabhadra Bodhisattva further said: "Why does the Dharma speak of healing illness and rescuing sentient beings from suffering, while in other texts it says one should not treat illness or compound medicines? What is the meaning?"

Vairocana Buddha said: "Healing illness is likewise, no different from the above. If one can heal the illness of one's own mind, one can also heal all illnesses. But if one is oneself ill and tries to heal others' illnesses, that is impossible. The teaching that forbids treating illness refers to one's own illness. The teaching that permits it refers to the power of the mantra that enables liberation, for the mind of one who is liberated has no illness. One who has illness is not called liberated. If a mantra-holder has illness of the mind, they can never heal others' illnesses; even if they tried, it would not work."

Samantabhadra Bodhisattva further said: "World-Honored One! Why do we hear that dhāraṇīs can extinguish grave transgressions and can also rescue beings from the suffering of hell? What is the meaning?"

Vairocana said: "There are two meanings. First, true hearing. Second, ear-hearing. True hearing means deeply penetrating the dharma-nature, knowing that dharmas are like illusions, and the substance of transgressions is likewise, ultimately unobtainable. Such a person has true siddhi and can rescue beings from hell, so what is strange about it? Ear-hearing means hearing through the coming together of various conditions. Through this skillful means, all buddhas cause those who hear to gradually cultivate and refine their own recognition of original nature. Through these causes and conditions, all transgressions are extinguished. Through the power of all buddhas, this too can rescue beings from the suffering of hell."

Vairocana Buddha said: "If a person cannot accomplish the three kinds of siddhi, they need only recite the mind-ground divine mantra one million times, and the mantra they hold will produce great efficacious results. If mantra-holders do not practice according to the Dharma and the presiding mantra deity becomes angered, reciting the mind-ground mantra will naturally make all deities joyful again."

At that time, Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, Samantabhadra Bodhisattva, Vajragarbha-rāja Bodhisattva, and Maitreya Bodhisattva spoke in unison, praising Vairocana Buddha with this verse:

> All buddhas are inconceivable;
> The profoundly deep Dharma is likewise.
> We have now heard it
> And also understand its deep meaning.
> The dharmakāya does not speak of "body";
> The reward and response bodies are likewise.
> The three bodies all do not speak:
> This is called the meaning of true speaking.
> Speaking and not-speaking are both empty,
> Neither one nor two.
> The speaker and the listener:
> These are all like illusions.
> Like illusions, they are unobtainable.
> Such are the buddhas' skillful means.

When the bodhisattvas had spoken this verse, they sat and listened to the Dharma.

Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva said: "World-Honored One! For those who seek siddhi and may be in deep mountains or vast wilderness, practicing mantra recitation, not yet having achieved accomplishment, and lacking provisions and food, causing them to regress from the mind of enlightenment: for such good men, I now wish to speak the divine mantra of the celestial kitchen." He then spoke the mantra:

*Namo ratna-trayāya jala-mahā-jala dūru mahā-dūru hūṃ kṣipra-hanisap oṃ kṣipra-hanisap śīghra*

"First wash the bowl with clean water and place it on a clean cloth. Place clean ash beneath the cloth, spread the cloth upon the ash, hold water in the mouth and spray the bowl. Close the eyes and recite the mantra one hundred eight times, mentally visualizing the finest celestial food. At that time, the devas will send celestial youths to bring and present the finest food, filling the bowl. Immediately rise and prostrate to Maitreya, the World-Honored One, then prostrate to Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, and eat. Any remainder should be distributed to all sentient beings. However many people eat of it, it cannot be exhausted. Those who eat this food will naturally realize siddhi.

"If one uses this mantra to bless ordinary human food and drink twenty-one times and offers it to hungry ghosts, the ghosts who receive this food will be freed from the suffering of the hungry-ghost realm and will be reborn in Maitreya's celestial palace. In times of famine, if one blesses ordinary food and drink with this mantra one thousand and eight times, many people can eat of it, and it will not be exhausted.

"In a year of famine, on the fifteenth day when the white moon is full, burn incense and look upward at the moon. Recite this mantra over the moon one thousand and eight times, making this aspiration: 'May all sentient beings illuminated by the moonlight universally become satisfied, free from thoughts of hunger and thirst.' At noon on the fifteenth day, one may also bless the sun, making the same aspiration.

"Each day, constantly recite this mantra one thousand and eight times, making this aspiration: 'May all deva-kings, with their leftover food, send the deva spirits to give it to all hungry ghosts throughout the ten direction realms. Having eaten, may they be freed from that suffering.'

"If a person can perform this practice for three years, not missing a single day, the benefits are vast and many. After leaving this body, one will be reborn in the Western Pure Land. In this present life, one will gain increasing merit and blessings. All other mantras one holds will be accomplished."

At that time, Vajragarbha-rāja Bodhisattva said: "For all sentient beings who hold the mantras of the three divisions and have not yet achieved accomplishment, but have gained some small effect, I shall now provide protection." He then spoke the mantra:

*Tadyathā oṃ vajra-svabhāya svāhā*

"If a mantra-holder senses any unease, know that it is māras and vināyakas causing disturbance. Immediately and urgently recite this mantra. I, together with all Garbha-rājas of the ten directions and all their retinues, will go there to provide protection. All vināyakas, māras, ghosts, and demons will flee in terror as if dying. If they recover, they will run far away to other places and not dare to remain."

At that time, Śākyamuni Buddha said: "Excellent! Excellent! All you bodhisattvas, you are able to ask these questions on behalf of all sentient beings and to speak these divine mantras, bringing universal peace and happiness to all sentient beings."

He further said: "All should know that this mind-ground Dharma gate and its profoundly deep scripture was spoken by immeasurable, innumerable, boundless Ganges-sand buddhas. It is difficult to see and difficult to hear. If a person has, in past immeasurable kalpas, made offerings to all buddhas, deeply delighted in the Buddha-Dharma, and become weary of the worldly cycle of birth and death, and has heard the profound Dharma spoken by all buddhas, then through these causes and conditions they are now able to hear this mind-ground scripture and dhāraṇī. If they further practice diligently, reading and reciting single-mindedly, such people are truly children of the Buddha. Why? Because such a person will soon attain anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi. Know that this sūtra is the king among all sūtras. This mantra is the king among all mantras. All of you bodhisattva mahāsattvas should disseminate it throughout Jambudvīpa, bringing great benefit and happiness to all sentient beings, enabling mantra-holders to swiftly accomplish siddhi."

At that time, Vairocana Buddha said: "When disseminating this sūtra, first examine the person's faculties and nature, and only then entrust it. Why? A person of wisdom who hears it will deeply believe. A person without wisdom will surely be alarmed and frightened, and will also harbor doubt. This will cause such beings to incur transgressions in return, falling into hell at death for having slandered this sūtra and all buddhas, receiving such retribution."

When this sūtra was spoken, the assembly did not hear, because it was spoken within the pure dharmadhātu. Then Śākyamuni Buddha and the five great bodhisattvas, having prostrated to Vairocana Buddha, took their leave and returned to Jambudvīpa beneath the bodhi tree, where they taught the Dharma for the assembly.

After the subsequent parinirvāṇa, this was entrusted to the five great bodhisattvas to be transmitted throughout the world. Because the śrāvakas had not heard the Buddha speak it, when they saw the bodhisattvas disseminating this sūtra, they were deeply alarmed and doubtful, and did not believe or accept it.

Then Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva said: "You śrāvakas! This sūtra is not within your realm and not within your knowledge. It was spoken by Vairocana Buddha to the hundreds of billions of emanation-body Śākyamunis and the great bodhisattvas. Therefore, it is beyond your knowledge."

At that time, the entire assembly heard together. Having heard, they wept with sadness and tears, exclaiming that never before had such a thing occurred.

Then the assembly rejoiced, faithfully accepted it, prostrated in homage, and departed to put it into practice.
