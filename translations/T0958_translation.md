# 金剛頂經一字頂輪王儀軌音義
## Phonetic Glossary and Commentary on the Ritual of the One-Syllable Cakravartin of the Vajraśekhara Sūtra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0958

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空)

---

From the text beginning "homage" to the line "therefore I constantly take refuge": this praise consists of eight stanzas. In the first, there are five lines with five meanings. First, in the opening line, "Samantabhadra" refers to principle, meaning the one syllable that subsumes all the Buddhas below. Alternatively, it may be divided into two Buddhas: Samantabhadra and the Buddhas' Cakravartin. Or it may be divided into three Buddhas: the previous two plus "the one who has realized great bodhi." Or it may be divided into the four wisdoms. "Samantabhadra" is the great-perfect-mirror wisdom, which is Akṣobhya Buddha. The next line is the discriminating wisdom, which is Amitābha Buddha. The next line is the equality wisdom, which is Ratnasaṃbhava Buddha. "Turning the teaching-command wheel" in one line is the all-accomplishing wisdom, which is Amoghasiddhi Buddha. Or it may be divided into the five wisdoms: the previous four plus the dharmadhātu-essential wisdom, namely "receiving the name Vajradhātu" in one line. From this line downward, they correspond to the four directions and the central Vairocana Buddha. "From the crown flows forth the great Vajra Vidyārāja" in two lines is the eastern Buddha. From "awesome light" to "surrounded" in two lines is the southern Buddha. "For the sake of all" to "Cakravartin" in two lines is the western Buddha. "Barely appearing" to "all perish" is the northern Buddha. "To the one body" is the central Buddha. The next two lines are the eastern Buddha. The next two, the southern Buddha. The next two, the western Buddha. The next two, the central Buddha. "Attaining Buddhahood" to "equal to empty space" is the northern Buddha. The next one, the central Buddha. The next two, the eastern Buddha. The next two, the western Buddha. "Causing sudden" in one line is the southern Buddha. "Therefore I constantly take refuge," one line, is the northern Buddha. Now follows the explanation of the text's phonetic glossary.

The sequential order of the One-Syllable Cakravartin practice: Upon going to the hall, first purify the hands and mouth. Arrive at the chamber, take your seat, and form the three-mystery seals.

Next, use the syllable *raṃ* to purify the body and the place, burning away all impurities.

Next, form the Buddha-Division Heart Seal at the crown and recite three times.

Next, form the Lotus-Division Heart Seal, raising the right thumb beside the right ear, and recite three times.

Next, form the Vajra-Division Heart Seal, raising the left thumb beside the left ear, and recite three times.

Next, empower with the Aparājita Seal. Afterward, perform the ritual of entering the hall: contemplate oneself as Vajrasattva, then enter the hall. Bow to the Buddha, offer burned incense and unguent incense, and form the three-division seals.

Next: the Universal Prostration Seal; recite "homage," etc.; the four immeasurable contemplations; the bodhicitta words and seal; the Aparājita boundary-protection words and seal; the three-liberation contemplation; the five-phase contemplation; the principal deity's root true-word and seal; Mahāvairocana's three-syllable secret word; the empowerment-consecration words and seal; the offering words and seal; the praise and seal; the offering of argha; the Buddha-Eye words and seal; then the Wisdom-Fist with the root true word; contemplate the principal deity and attendants; the rosary words and seal; then the counted recitation is complete.

Next, empower the four places with the root true-word seal, and also apply the body-protection seal. Then form the concentration seal and enter contemplation: contemplate the syllable-meaning and the moon-disc seed, etc. When emerging, empower with the root true-word, then the praise words and seal, offerings, argha, "homage," etc.; the Aparājita boundary-release; the liberation seal and words; and finally the three-division heart seals. The session is complete.

Colophon: On the third day of the tenth month of the third year of Tenpuku [i.e., 1235 CE, actually Tengyō/Tenbuku era], at Ninna-ji Kaimyō-ji, copied from the Toganoo-san Hokku-dai library text. Completed.

Chiseki-in monk, Tōbu Jinin.

On the night of the sixth of the eighth month, autumn of the first year of Kyōwa [1801 CE], this text was requested and copied. This phonetic glossary was composed by a Japanese person who applied Japanese readings, and therefore some of the phonetic glosses are difficult to reconcile. Errors of the "lu-yu" type are not few. Now corrected with punctuation and Japanese readings, it is committed to woodblock printing. At that time, the winter, tenth month of the same year.

Recorded by Kaidō of Hōsho-ji, Toyoyama Hase, Yamato Province.
