# 邪見經
## Sūtra on Wrong Views

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 93

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown (失譯)

---

Thus have I heard.

At one time, the Venerable Ānanda was dwelling at Rājagṛha, in the Kalandaka Bamboo Grove. Not long after the World-Honored One had passed into parinirvāṇa, a certain Ājīvika ascetic who was a childhood friend of the Venerable Ānanda wandered about after his midday meal and came to where the Venerable Ānanda was. Having arrived, he and the Venerable Ānanda exchanged cordial greetings. After exchanging cordial greetings, he sat down to one side. Having sat down to one side, that Ājīvika said to the Venerable Ānanda: "I wish to ask something; please hear my question."

"Ask, good sir!" The Ājīvika, having heard this, understood.

"Ānanda, that Śramaṇa Gautama abandoned wrong views and cleared away wrong views. He did not declare: 'the world is eternal,' 'the world is not eternal,' 'the world is finite,' 'the world is infinite,' 'the soul is identical to the body,' 'the soul is different from the body,' 'after death one exists,' 'after death one does not exist,' 'after death one both exists and does not exist,' 'after death one neither exists nor does not exist.' Ānanda, did that Śramaṇa Gautama know wrong views, and should he be understood to know them thus?"

"Brahmin, that World-Honored One, possessed of wisdom, knowledge, and vision, the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly and Fully Enlightened One, abandoned wrong views and cleared away wrong views, and did not declare: 'the world is eternal' down to 'after death one neither exists nor does not exist.' Brahmin, that World-Honored One, possessed of wisdom and vision, the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly and Fully Enlightened One, he should be understood to know wrong views thus."

"Ānanda, that Śramaṇa Gautama abandoned wrong views and cleared away wrong views, and did not declare: 'the world is eternal' down to 'after death one neither exists nor does not exist.' Then how, Ānanda, did that Śramaṇa Gautama know these wrong views? In what way did he know them?"

"Brahmin, that World-Honored One, possessed of wisdom and vision, the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly and Fully Enlightened One, abandoned wrong views and cleared away wrong views, and did not declare: 'the world is eternal' down to 'after death one neither exists nor does not exist.' Brahmin, these wrong views are associated and fully associated, leading to such destinations, such rebirths, and to future existences. Brahmin, the World-Honored One knows this, possessed of knowledge and vision, the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly and Fully Enlightened One: he knows wrong views, and should be understood to know them in this way."

"Therefore, Ānanda, I now take refuge in you."

"Brahmin, do not take refuge in me. Just as I have taken refuge in the World-Honored One, so too should you take refuge in him."

"Therefore, Ānanda, I now take refuge in that World-Honored One, the Dharma, and the Sangha of bhikṣus. I shall be an upāsaka of that World-Honored One. From this day forward I abstain from taking life. Today I pay my respects to the Venerable One."

Thus spoke Ānanda. That Ājīvika, having heard what the Venerable Ānanda had said, rejoiced and was delighted.

---

**Translator's notes:** The interlocutor is identified as an Ājīvika (邪命, lit. "wrong livelihood"), a member of the deterministic ascetic sect contemporary with early Buddhism, described as a childhood friend of Ānanda. Ānanda addresses him as "Brahmin" (婆羅門), suggesting he was of brahmin caste. The ten undeclared positions (avyākata) listed here concern the eternity and finitude of the world, the relation of soul to body, and the question of existence after death, representing the classic set of speculative views the Buddha refused to affirm. The final item in the sequence appears to be textually corrupt in the received Taishō edition; it has been rendered according to the standard fourfold formula.
