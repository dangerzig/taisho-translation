# 大方廣菩薩藏經中文殊師利根本一字陀羅尼經
## Sūtra of Mañjuśrī's Fundamental One-Syllable Dhāraṇī from the Mahāvaipulya Bodhisattva Treasury Sūtra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1181

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Ratnacintā (寶思惟).

---

Thus have I heard. At one time the Buddha was in the Śuddhāvāsa heaven, in the jeweled and splendidly adorned place of practice, together with great bodhisattvas and the Śuddhāvāsa devas. At that time the World-Honored One, with great kindness and great compassion, addressed all the devas in the assembly: "All of you, listen well to the wheel-practice and mantra-dharma of Mañjuśrī, the Youth, which is for the protection of all sentient beings. It is the heart great divine mantra-king, the secret of all tathāgatas. I shall now reveal and explain it for you all. If anyone recites it, I declare that such a person has thereby upheld all mantras and brought them to full completion, and everything they undertake will be accomplished, with nothing surpassing it.

"You should know that this dhāraṇī, among all mantras, is the great divine mantra-king, possessing great supernatural power. If good men or good women are able to receive and uphold it, Mañjuśrī the Youth, Bodhisattva, will always come to protect them. Whether waking or dreaming, he will manifest his bodily form and various auspicious things, causing that person to be filled with great joy.

"Good men! This mantra-king can even summon Mañjuśrī the Youth, Bodhisattva, to come, to say nothing of other bodhisattvas and all worldly and supramundane worthy and holy beings.

"Furthermore, good men! This mantra can extinguish all disasters and hindrances, all evil dreams, all enemies, all five heinous offenses, four grave transgressions, and ten evil deeds, as well as all evil, heretical, and inauspicious incantations. It can also accomplish all good deeds and possesses great vigor. Know that this mantra, among all worldly and supramundane mantras, is the most excellent. It is the heart of all buddhas and can cause all wishes to be completely fulfilled.

"If one takes five-colored thread, ties it into a mantra-cord, and binds it upon one's neck, it will protect one's body and cause all wishes to be perfectly fulfilled, without anything surpassing it."

He then spoke the mantra:

*Oṃ chrīṃ*

"Children of the Buddha! This mantra can destroy all evil, heretical wāng-liǎng ghosts and spirits. It is the auspicious dharma of all buddhas and can also bring all divine mantras to accomplishment. One who recites this mantra can cause beings to give rise to great kindness and great compassion, and all hindrances will be extinguished, and all wishes will be fulfilled.

"Even before performing any ritual, it can accomplish things as one desires. If one generates the mind of unsurpassed great bodhi and recites it once, its power can protect oneself. If one recites it twice, its power can protect one's companions. If one recites it three times, its power can protect all persons in one household. If one recites it four times, its power can protect all persons in one city. If one recites it five times, its power can protect all persons in one country. If one recites it six times, its power can protect all persons in one world. If one recites it seven times, its power can protect all persons in the four continents.

"If at dawn one recites it once over water and washes one's face, all who see one will be filled with delight. If one empowers incense and perfumes one's clothing, all who see one will likewise be delighted.

"If one suffers from toothache, empower a tooth-cleaning stick with the mantra, chew it and rub the teeth, and the pain will immediately cease.

"If a woman is in difficult labor, take aṭalūśakā root or lāṅgalikā root, empower it seven times, mix it with water free of insects, and apply it to the laboring woman's navel, and the child will be easily born.

"If a man has been struck by an arrow and the arrowhead has entered the sinew and bone and cannot be extracted, take ten-year-old ghee, three liang, empower it one hundred and eight times, place it on the wound and also have the patient eat it, and the arrowhead will come out.

"If a woman has not borne children for five, or even twenty or thirty years, whether due to her own illness, or the man's illness, or affliction by ghosts and spirits, or various other illnesses, or having been poisoned: take ten-year-old or older ghee, five liang, and one liang of peacock feather. Place the feather in the ghee and empower it twenty-one times. Fry it and pound it to powder. Add one liang of rock sugar and the flesh of three haritaki fruits with seeds removed, and mix together. Empower it one hundred and eight times. Each morning on an empty stomach, have her take the full dose. Within seven days she will conceive.

"If one suffers from headache, take a crow's wing feather, empower it seven times, and brush it over the afflicted person's head, and the ailment will be healed.

"If one suffers from malaria, whether recurring every one, two, or up to seven days, or chronic: cook porridge with pure milk, add one liang of fine ghee, empower it one hundred and eight times, and feed it to the patient, and they will be healed.

"Good men! In brief, if one wishes to compound any medicine or take any remedy, one should first sincerely recite the mantra twenty-one times, and it will swiftly take effect as desired.

"Furthermore, good men! If any being is seized by a flying-head ghost, rub your own face with your hand, recite the mantra one hundred and eight times while assuming a fearsome expression, then with the left hand form the birth-mudrā (tucking the thumb into the palm with the other four fingers pressed over it, making a tight fist). Then, with eyes glaring, silently recite the mantra while gazing at the patient, and the affliction will be removed.

"If someone suffers from any ghost-illness, empower the right hand by reciting the mantra one hundred and eight times, burn benzoin incense for fumigation, form the birth-mudrā with the left hand, and rub the patient's head with the right hand, and the ailment will be healed.

"If one has enemies, or evil dreams, or various terrors causing unease of body and mind, take seven-colored thread and tie a mantra-cord in the shape of a lotus, or a wheel, or a vajra. Empower it one hundred and eight times and fumigate it with benzoin incense. Within seven days of wearing it on one's neck, all calamities and difficulties will be entirely dispersed.

"Alternatively, grind gorocana into ink and on clean paper or silk draw the form of whatever is feared. On all four sides, write the syllables of the mantra surrounding it, or draw lotuses, or wheels, or svastika marks, conch shells, fish, vajra hooks, treasure vases, and other symbols surrounding it. Empower it seven times, and the fear will be removed.

"Good men! If one wishes to pass through places of danger with lions, tigers, wolves, venomous snakes, hostile bandits, and all manner of peril, one should purify body and mind, avoiding women, the five pungent vegetables, alcohol, meat, garden cress, and coriander. One should give rise to thoughts of great compassion for all sentient beings and sincerely recite the mantra forty-nine times. All hostile evils will naturally retreat, and even if one encounters them, they will all be delighted.

"Good men! This mantra is such that all sentient beings, even throughout one kalpa or immeasurable kalpas, may not even be able to hear its name, let alone see it or single-mindedly recite it. Even if the seven treasures, elephants, and horses filled all of Jambudvīpa, these are still worldly things subject to destruction. But this dhāraṇī mantra can cause beings in this present life and in lives to come to always obtain peace and security, to always be among the retinue of tathāgatas and great bodhisattvas, and whether for oneself or for others, all wishes will be obtained. Therefore, with earnest effort and the thought that this is rarely encountered, do not treat it lightly or give rise to doubt."
