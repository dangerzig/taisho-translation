# 佛說毘奈耶經

## The Vinaya Sūtra Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0898

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown.

---

At that time the Buddha was on Vulture Peak Mountain in Rājagṛha, together with immeasurable bodhisattvas, mantra-holding sages, the Great Worthy Brahmā King, earth spirits, and others, expounding the Dharma of mantras. Then Vajrapāṇi rose from his seat, prostrated at the Buddha's feet, and said: "I now wish to explain the vinaya for mantra-holding practice. I further request that the World-Honored One and all the great worthy ones each explain the essentials, demonstrating them to all who recite and uphold mantras." He further said: "I only wish the World-Honored One would permit me to speak."

The Buddha told Vajrapāṇi: "You of great majestic virtue, who harbor compassion within, you are able to speak these words and wish to explain the wondrous Dharma. This will surely bring great benefit. Speak as you will; we in this assembly will all follow the Dharma you expound."

Then Vajrapāṇi said: "World-Honored One! If there are good men or women who recite mantras and wish to achieve efficacious results, such persons must first and foremost be diligent in the mantras they uphold. They must know how to make offerings to buddha stūpas, honored images, and the presiding mantra deity. According to their ability, they should constantly make offerings without any thought of interruption. Before the Three Jewels, they should openly confess, make vows, and dedicate. They should dedicate this merit to all sentient beings, wishing that all be freed from suffering.

"Having spoken these words, they should wash their hands, join their palms, and before the honored image, sit kneeling on kuśa grass, saying: 'May all the buddhas and bodhisattvas of the ten directions, and all mantra deities, have compassion and think of me. I now wish to practice this mantra and achieve efficacious results from the deity. If I am able to accomplish this practice, may I see signs of success. If I cannot accomplish it, may I also see signs of failure.' Having made this aspiration, they should recite single-mindedly. From among the practices to be accomplished, whether the root mantra or the heart mantra, recite one hundred eight times, then immediately fall asleep right there on the seat.

"If in a dream one makes offerings to buddhas, Dharma teachers, monastics, spiritual friends, or parents; or sees oneself wearing clean white garments with head and body adorned; or sees rivers, oceans, great mountains, pavilions, palaces, and halls; or sees people giving one silk and sashes; or rides elephants, horses, or oxen; or obtains swords, spears, battle-axes, bows, arrows, copper wheels, hooks, or lassos; or sees someone bestowing clean white garments, flower garlands, and jeweled necklaces; or sees oneself conversing with kings, great ministers, or elders, speaking wholesome words; or sees dignified women holding banners, canopies, flower vases; or sees oneself holding tridents; or ascends a lion throne or other high seats; or sees blood flowing from one's own head: if one sees such signs, know that one can accomplish this practice.

"Or if in a dream one sees painted images of honored ones or deity-forms crumbling and decaying; or sees one's parents sorrowful and weeping; or sees naked non-Buddhist practitioners; or sees oneself without clothing; or sees evil-doing people or caṇḍālas dragging one along; or sees oneself walking through filthy places; or sees great waters dried up; or sees eating at improper times; or sees one's own hands, feet, and body filthy with foul odors; or sees oneself running in terror; or sees oneself pulling out one's own hair; or sees snakes, scorpions, rats, or wolves; or sees oneself falling into a pit and being injured: if one sees such signs, know that this person has great obstacles to this mantra practice, and it will be difficult to accomplish.

"If such good men still wish to love and uphold the practice and achieve results, they should add diligence and vigor without scattering the mind, never generating weary or indolent thoughts. In every period, they should recite without failing. They should always maintain a compassionate mind, keep far from sensual desire, and cultivate pure conduct. They should bathe, be clean, and be gentle and supple. They should properly study the methods and enter the mantra altar. To seek the great fruit, they should cut off wrong speech and all playful, idle talk. They should have compassion for the orphaned, weak, poor, old, and young, supporting and cherishing them like infants. They should be moderate in food and clothing. Companions on the path should encourage one another. Toward the Three Jewels, they should generate deep faith and reverence. They should listen to the true Dharma as a thirsty person craves water, always seeking wisdom and heeding spiritual friends.

"As for the rules of the mantra altar, one must clearly understand the signs of vināyaka obstacles and the methods for their removal. One must also properly study the fire-offering method, the method for offering food to non-human beings, the hand-seal mantras for inviting, welcoming, and dismissing various deities, and related practices. Furthermore, one must know the proper dwelling place: mountain retreats facing water on terraced banks, or fine flower gardens and groves that are not dry and withered, or under a single great tree, or within a buddha relic stūpa, or within a pure saṅghārāma. All such places are suitable. Places where there are bandits, prostitutes, widows, caṇḍālas, fierce beasts, or venomous snakes, as well as leather-working households, butchers' shops, homes that raise camels, donkeys, pigs, dogs, or fowl, or those who keep hawks for hunting, and places near charnel grounds, physicians, or non-Buddhist practitioners: a mantra reciter should never dwell in any such place. Furthermore, one should examine the dwelling place: the water should be free of insects and ants, there should be no floating corpses, excrement, or filth, and there should be no malodorous vapors from dark cliffs. Such places are also unsuitable. One should move far away and seek a superior site with sandy or stony ground, flowing springs, and flourishing pools before settling there to practice recitation.

"When the outer environment is pure, the inner person should also be upright and clear. One must deeply abandon thoughts of desire and covetousness. Toward anger and resentment, one should cultivate compassion and patience instead. All afflictions should be subdued. Three times daily, one should enter the stūpa or go to the open field where the practice is performed, openly confess, rejoice in all merit, dedicate to unsurpassed perfect awakening, and keep the aspiration for buddhahood always on one's lips. In the early and late watches of the night, be diligent in contemplation, reading and reciting Mahāyāna sūtras and maintaining the altar rules without forgetting. Recite the mantras of the great wrathful vajra kings, arouse great leaping joy, visualize the honored one's form as if directly before your eyes, cultivate with a joyful mind, and single-mindedly contemplate the mantra syllables, causing the mind's eye to see impermanence, suffering, emptiness, and the absence of any solid reality, without following the five sense-desires. The breath should be regulated and gentle, not leaning to one side. Practice the ritual carefully. When about to rise and walk, one should deliberately lift the feet. The syllables, sounds, and essential character of the mantra phrases should all be made clear. If while reciting a mantra one needs to cough, one must restrain it. Whether at the beginning, middle, or end of the recitation, whether more or less, if one coughs one must start over from the beginning.

"World-Honored One! If mantra masters can practice in accordance with this Dharma, they will soon attain great efficacious power. All vināyakas will be unable to create obstacles and will flee far away. But if mantra masters, while reciting, have incorrect pronunciation, omit syllables, have a dry and rough throat, or frequently cough, causing interruptions in the mantra's sound; or if the body is not clean: at such times the vināyakas will seize the opportunity. The celestial devas and good spirits will not offer protection. They may encounter great illness, calamity, and difficulty, and the practice will not produce results. Mantra masters should never engage in such conduct. With single-minded focus, they should sit in full lotus on kuśa grass and recite mantras with utmost sincerity. Inwardly harboring compassion, the aspirational mind should not give rise to idle thoughts: 'I am performing this practice for the sake of all sentient beings.' They should sit and recite until the body does not become weary. When they wish to rise, they may walk, make offerings, read sūtras, and praise the World-Honored One. As explained above, in all periods, one should practice in this manner."

At that time the World-Honored One praised him: "Excellent! Excellent! O Vajrapāṇi! You are able to speak in this way, protecting those who read and recite mantras and showing them the essential and wondrous mantra vinaya."

Then Vajrapāṇi further said: "I now wish to explain the method for making offerings to the presiding mantra deity-king. The mantra-holder must first bathe the body according to the Dharma, without scattering the mind, and contemplate the presiding mantra deity. Prostrating with the five limbs touching the ground, one should arouse great faith: 'All that I seek in the Dharma is accomplished through the majestic power and blessing of the great deity.' Recite the mantra and direct the mind.

"Facing south, form the vajra-pāśa seal or the buddhōṣṇīṣa seal. With great wrathful mind, silently recite the great vajra-pāśa mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-pāśa hrīḥ*

"If forming the buddhōṣṇīṣa seal, recite this mantra:

*Oṃ gaganā-amala hūṃ*

"Having completed the southern visualization, next visualize the west. Mentally form the vajra-banner seal, and with wrathful mind silently recite the mantra, saying: 'I now seal the western boundary.' Mantra:

*Oṃ ṭaraṃ aṅginī-rāṭ paṃ hu*

"Next visualize the north. Form the vajra-crushing seal. Recite the mantra as before:

*Oṃ hrīḥ vajra-kāli-maṭ paṃ hu*

"Next visualize the east. Form the vajra-peak seal. Recite the mantra as before, saying: 'Seal the eastern boundary.' Mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-śikhali-rū-maṭ*

"Having performed this ritual, the boundaries of the four directions are sealed. Then one should rise from the seat and contemplate the presiding mantra deity, inviting and summoning the deity-spirit. Holding an incense burner, burn incense as an offering. Visualize: 'By the command of the great majestic spiritual power I now invoke, I prostrate and pay homage.' Contemplate the presiding mantra. Take pure water and sprinkle it over the body. Sit peacefully with hands at the chest, silently recite while counting the rosary. Before becoming weary, if fatigue arises, one may rise and take incense and flowers for offerings.

"When about to leave through the door, recite the mantra single-mindedly. If there is any distraction, at that moment all vināyakas, ghosts, and spirits will scheme and plot, causing the mantra master's mind to become confused so that the practice fails. They will create flowers of strange colors and unusual fragrances to make the mantra master's heart stir with desire and delight. One should not take pleasure in them but dismiss them with rebuke. The mind should constantly recite and contemplate the presiding mantra deity. In walking, standing, sitting, and lying down, one must never forget.

"World-Honored One! Such a person is constantly followed by vināyakas seeking an opportunity. If one does not practice according to the Dharma, they will create obstacles. If one practices in accordance with the Dharma, nothing can find an opening. We vajra-holders, wielding the divine vajra, constantly provide protection and ensure swift efficacious results. Those who practice wrongly and against the rules, we abandon. When possessed by māras and vināyaka spirits, or when wind enters the body, one often harbors anger, arouses greed and delusion. If not treated, it can lead to death. But if one later feels remorse and practices according to the Dharma, one can still subdue all māras, evil spirits, and vināyakas."

The Buddha declared: "So it is, so it is. As you have said, there is nothing false in your words. One should follow the mantra Dharma in this way to achieve accomplishment."

At that time, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva rose from his seat and said: "World-Honored One! If there are people who cherish their lives and wish to achieve the realization of efficacious results, such mantra masters should all cut off all evil actions, not gaze at women's forms or bodily beauty. If they see someone unclothed, they should not look. They should not think about places of former unwholesome connections. They should fix the mind on a single object, constantly contemplating the great majestic spiritual power of the Three Jewels. They should always abide in true reality: all conditioned things of the world are impermanent, suffering, empty, and without self. They should dwell single-mindedly in reciting the mantra rules without distraction, with right contemplation and honesty, without grasping at delusive thoughts. They should also read and recite the profound and wondrous Mahāyāna sūtras, diligently make offerings to the Buddha, Dharma, and Sangha, and build koṭi stūpas and images, not letting time pass in vain. If one can practice in this way, this good man will soon achieve great efficacious results, and no ghost or spirit will be able to create obstacles."

At that time the Great Brahmā King, empowered by the Buddha's spiritual force, addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I constantly observe those who uphold the mantra practice and seek accomplishment. Why is it that their vital energy becomes weak, they are excessively drowsy, they cannot eat or drink, their body and head become feeble, they become lazy and indolent, afflicted by illness, frequently generate anger, are attached to sensual desire, give rise to unwholesome thoughts about their own practice, are insatiably greedy, constantly harbor doubt, and though they have already obtained the subtle and secret Dharma, do not practice in accordance with it but instead seek after other mantras, discussing and debating right and wrong with others? They also say: 'What kind of people have achieved results through mantra recitation? Which mantras are effective? Whose wishes are fulfilled? In what direction and in what place does recitation produce results?' World-Honored One, why do such people waste their time in vain?

"Furthermore, there are people who strenuously seek to learn and practice such mantra methods, who are briefly diligent but then generate a mind of retreat, who slander the Buddha's pure precepts, who serve as temple administrators, whose evil karmic thoughts I observe, who engage in activities they should not do and eat things they should not eat. The World-Honored One is the excellent condition of all sages, the guide who opens the path of humans and devas, who shows the causes of right action and enables the attainment of wondrous fruits. I only wish you would have compassion and benefit humans and devas by explaining this for me."

At that time the World-Honored One told Brahmā: "Listen carefully, listen carefully, and reflect well! Great Brahmā, know this: if a person who wishes to study mantra recitation has not yet generated deep bodhicitta, still harbors doubt about the Mahāyāna, receives the mantra text without proper transmission from a teacher, with the syllables and sentences jumbled and confused, who frequently sees and hears those who recite the subtle and secret teachings yet knows nothing about them, who does not follow the proper times and seasons for reciting mantras, and whose knowledge of altar procedures has never been made clear, what kind of person is this? It is like one born blind from the mother's womb. Whatever they grasp only leads them into darkness and wrong conduct. They understand nothing of the methods within the mantras, yet because of their attachment to ignorance, delusion, and the inverted sense of self, they make foolish claims, asserting their wisdom and understanding of all dharmas.

"Great Brahmā! If such a mantra master, while performing mantra practice and generating such thoughts, there is a vināyaka named Vajra-Fury who will follow this person and create obstacles, causing physical discomfort and failure in mantra practice.

"Great Brahmā! If a mantra master, when bathing, does not follow the proper ritual of forming seals and reciting mantras, does not contemplate the presiding deity, and does not sprinkle water, there is a vināyaka named Tongxū who will then cause harm and injury.

"When a mantra master is harmed and obstructed by a vināyaka, the vital energy becomes weak, drowsiness increases, doubts and suspicions arise during practice, laziness sets in, and illness and suffering afflict the body. Why? Simply because the mantra master is not single-minded, seeks too much without satisfaction, and does not practice diligently.

"Great Brahmā! If this is the case, what method should be used to eliminate it? The mantra practitioner should seek a teacher who clearly understands the mantra Dharma methods, establish an altar, and enter it. From among the four seal methods, study and receive one seal. After the ācārya confers the abhiṣeka, then before a sūtra or image, take sesame and ghee, recite the mantra one hundred eight times, and burn them in the fire. The vināyakas will all be dispersed and eliminated.

"Great Brahmā! Entering the altar and receiving the Dharma has great power. But if the mantra practitioner's understanding of the Dharma is still incomplete, lacks bodhicitta, does not revere the field of merit, does not know about vināyakas, and rashly performs great rituals of mantra recitation and offering, or if there are people who have not yet entered the altar, have not made offerings to the ācārya, have not received the seal method, yet presume to teach others this secret Dharma, saying: 'This mantra has such and such spiritual power; such and such ghosts and spirits should be bound, subdued, summoned, and dismissed using this method.' They also teach others to construct altars and various ground-seals, commanding them to perform interrogation rituals: 'If you do thus, there will be this laughing; if you do thus, there will be this crying. The mantra deity also has such and such messengers.'

"Great Brahmā! Such people, through ignorance, serve as teachers. Although they read the texts, they do not understand the meaning, the profound Dharma gate, or the ritual procedures. They take up teaching others to perform such practices. Such people are possessed by a vināyaka named Liṭṭa-heṅgla, who causes many calamities and an unsettled mind.

"Great Brahmā! If there are such obstacles, one should again enter the lotus Dharma altar assembly, and at the ācārya's place receive the abhiṣeka Dharma. If one can do this, the vināyaka can be expelled.

"Great Brahmā! Within my Dharma, there are some among the śramaṇas who do not know the subtle and wondrous rules of mantra practice. Toward the honored teacher and the pure śīla, they harbor contempt and mutual criticism. In the monastery, they seek status and are puffed up with pride. Some even disparage the Buddha's teaching and slander the true Dharma.

"Great Brahmā! Know that such people, because they recklessly show contempt for my pure Dharma, are immediately possessed by a vināyaka named Able-Obstructor, who causes them to commit various evils without remorse. If one wishes to expel this, one should build koṭi stūpas and images, kneel before the honored teacher, and again receive the rules and commitments. One must also enter the lotus altar and receive the abhiṣeka. Only then can the possessing spirit be eliminated, and the study of mantra practice will easily reach accomplishment."

At that time the firm-standing earth deity emerged from the ground, prostrated at the Buddha's feet, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! How should a mantra master purify body and mind? How should they walk in meditation? How can they know what should and should not be done? World-Honored One! At what times should a mantra master recite mantras? How can they know the proper time for recitation? I only wish the Tathāgata would compassionately explain."

At that time the Buddha told the earth deity: "Listen well. The practices that a mantra master should follow are these. If one wishes to uphold any mantra Dharma, one must have deep, reverential faith and earnestness, abandon wrong views and unwholesome dharmas, even idle laughter, and attentively practice the four kinds of pure conduct. What are the four? First, purity of bodily conduct. Second, purity of verbal conduct. Third, purity of mental conduct. Fourth, purity of water and food. The purity of body, speech, and mind constitutes inner purity.

"As for outer purity: when the mantra master bathes and enters the water, first put on bathing garments, contemplate this mantra, and rub the body. Mantra:

*Oṃ dhupe dhupe kāya-dhupe prajvali ni svāhā*

"Then wash the head and body. Rub the feet with brick or stone. Take earth, divide it into three portions, and with the left hand take the first portion, reciting this mantra:

*Oṃ bhuja-bhala hūṃ*

"Silently reciting the mantra, wash the lower body. Next take the second portion, silently reciting the mantra to wash the feet. After washing the hands clean, take the third portion, recite the mantra, and wash the head. After washing, recite the previous mantra and sprinkle water over the body. Then fill both palms with clean water, recite the previous mantra again, and declare: 'This water I offer to the presiding mantra deity.' Put on new clean garments. According to one's ability, take incense and flowers and make offerings to all buddhas, tathāgatas, and all worthy and holy ones. Openly confess: 'I now offer this body to the Three Jewels.' Following the method described above, recite mantras with true faith, sitting upright and single-minded until not weary. When fatigued, one may rise and walk, beg for food, or drink. Having obtained food, recite this mantra to bless the food before eating. Mantra:

*Namaḥ sarva-buddha-bodhisattvānāṃ oṃ bhālaṃ-dhāte-tiśe mālinī svāhā*

"Recite the mantra three times. First take a small pinch and offer it to the presiding mantra deity, then eat one's fill. After washing the bowl and rinsing the mouth, following the same bathing procedure, return to the practice hall. Make offerings, confess, read and recite sūtras, then sit and recite mantras until not fatigued. When the body is weary, one may rise and walk. At evening, do likewise. If weary from recitation, walk, make offerings to all buddhas and bodhisattvas, prostrate, and offer praise. When about to sleep, lie on the right side. Contemplate the body as impermanent, without self, ultimately suffering, empty, and impure, a false combination of skin and bones. Recite this mantra and then fall asleep. Mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-rakṣaṇi hūṃ*

"If one recites this mantra, all evil dreams will be eliminated, good spirits will provide protection, and efficacious results will come quickly. Such are the rules for pure conduct, timing, and ritual."

At that time the earth deity again addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! When a mantra master practices and recites the teachings, what dreams indicate good or evil regarding the efficacious results?"

The Buddha told the earth deity: "If mantra masters lovingly uphold and recite mantras, practicing diligently day and night without a mind of retreat, when such a person sleeps, if in their dream they see boys or girls beautifully adorned, or see the forms of parents, siblings, or sisters, know that the mantra deity has turned to look upon me. If they see the aforementioned males or females holding incense, flowers, food and drink, or fruits, know that the mantra deity has drawn near to me. If they see their own body adorned in clean white garments, one should think: 'The mantra deity protects me with compassion.' They should then add diligence and vigor. If in a dream they see the aforementioned males or females holding tall banners, streamers, canopies, jeweled necklaces, crowns, conches, swords, and the like, know that the mantra's efficacious results are near. If they see śramaṇas or the aforementioned males and females making offerings to the Three Jewels, giving alms, or being in flower groves, saṅghārāmas, or stūpas, one should think: 'I have now been accepted and sustained by the mantra deity.' If they see themselves climbing a mountain, ascending a tall pavilion, sitting on a lion throne, or about to leap into empty space, one should think: 'My efficacious results are extremely near.' If they see themselves sitting on a mountain peak or upon a lion throne, riding a lion or white elephant, wearing a celestial crown, honored by all with reverence: upon seeing these signs, the great efficacious results have arrived.

"Earth deity, know this: mantra masters should be skilled in recognizing these signs. If in a dream one sees butchers, caṇḍālas, naked non-Buddhist practitioners or Nirgrantha ascetics, mourners with loose hair, oneself without clothing, running in terror, holding impure things, grasping cooked meat or fish, eating sesame dregs, falling into a deep pit, or being trampled by elephants or horses: know that a vināyaka named Vajra-Fury is creating obstacles. If one wishes to expel it, follow the altar methods, seals, and so forth as described above, and additionally recite this mantra one hundred eight times. It will protect the body, remove the obstacles, and naturally the dreams will show all manner of auspicious signs. Mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-nārāhaṇa-mātha-pañjaraṇa hūṃ phaṭ*

"Furthermore, Earth deity! There is a vināyaka named Valiant-Crown. If it sees a mantra master intent on diligent accomplishment of mantra practice, it generates thoughts of advance and retreat, delays the days, causes physical discomfort, and makes the practitioner wish to go to such-and-such a mountain to practice. Because of this affliction, the practitioner becomes lazy and not diligent. They then ask others about various places, and if someone answers: 'That place is good, though there are some difficulties,' this only increases doubt and hesitation, the mind becomes unsettled, and the original aspiration is lost.

"How can one recognize the signs of this obstacle? If the mantra master, though reciting mantras, in sleep and dreams sees men with their hands cut and injured, or speaking words of blame and fault; or sees stūpas and temple images crumbling and decaying; or sees fine things that cannot be entered: know that this is the work of a vināyaka creating obstacles. If upon seeing such evil signs one suddenly generates a mind of retreat and lets body and mind run wild, then to expel it, one should plaster the altar and make offerings. Place fine incense, flowers, fruits, food, and drink within the altar. Take a new bottle filled with water and place it at the center. Recite the previous bathing mantra over the fragrant water-bottle one thousand and eight times. Use this water to bathe the body. This protects the body and can disperse the vināyakas.

"Furthermore, Earth deity! There is a vināyaka, also named Vajra-Fury, that causes the mantra master's vital energy to become weak. Upon hearing the mantra's sound, it generates weariness and headaches, constantly harbors doubt and suspicion, always follows its own habits. Even when admonished by others, it responds with anger. It is arrogant and self-important, acts freely and recklessly, does not respect elders or juniors, and shows contempt for everything.

"If one recognizes the signs of this obstacle, one should receive and hold the great vajra-wheel seal, construct an altar, and enter it. Then use the tathāgata buddhōṣṇīṣa dhāraṇī mantra, described below. Recite it over fragrant water one hundred eight times and use it to bathe the body. This will eliminate the vināyaka. The tathāgata buddhōṣṇīṣa mantra:

*Namo buddhāya hrīḥ uṣṇīṣa-sadhāra vajra-jvālini bhabhayālaka-jvālini rā-labra-ata-jisphura-hara saṃhara-hara-hara nikālidṛṇa-tāṃ kiṃ madaṃ hūṃ phaṭ*

"Also recite the six-syllable heart mantra:

*Oṃ rāṃ bandha svāhā*

"Reciting this mantra many times is foremost. The vināyaka will immediately flee far away.

"There is also a vināyaka named Vajra-Pestle, which causes people to lose their minds and become confused in their fundamental nature. It may cause a person, while reciting, to suddenly think of other things when they should be concentrating. Former deeds and scenes appear before them as if they were possessed by a ghost, and they lose awareness. Whether they recite many or few mantras, alien thoughts arise. At that time, the mantra deity departs far away, and evil signs appear in dreams.

"If one wishes to purify and eliminate such obstacles, one should make great vows, arouse the bodhisattva mind and the vow to broadly rescue beings. For one day and one night, observe a proper fast on an empty stomach according to the Dharma, single-mindedly receiving and upholding the pure precepts. Recite the previous buddhōṣṇīṣa mantra without limit on the number of recitations. Take a five-colored thread and tie it into a mantra cord. Recite the mantra seven more times over it and tie it on the left arm. If one performs this practice, the evil vināyakas can be eliminated.

"Next I shall explain the altar method. The mantra master must first confess and purify, arouse the bodhicitta, and search for good ground. For one day and one night, do not eat and keep the precepts. Using five colors, paint the altar's interior. With fragrant paste, paint the Buddha seated in full lotus. On the right side, paint Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, with tiger skin around the waist and hips, the right hand holding a precious staff and the left hand holding a water vessel. On the left side, paint Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva, both hands holding fly-whisks. Below this, paint Amṛtakuṇḍalī in wrathful blazing form. Below this, paint a three-pronged flaming trident, then an axe, hook, mace, vajra, club, lasso, conch, and other ground-seal implements, all arranged in adornment around the perimeter.

"Below Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, paint Hāritī Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, Kurīpaṇḍa-bhadanī, Mahāśvetanī, and others, each with hair dressed, celestial crowns, garments, water vessels, flowers, and fly-whisks, each according to their own Dharma. Paint the form of the solitary-topknot rākṣasī, with terrifying fangs protruding from the mouth, wearing a crown of human skulls, seated on a rock, entwined with snakes, having four arms: the upper right hand holding a battle-axe, the next hand holding a vajra, the upper left hand holding a freshly severed head dripping blood, the next hand holding a vessel filled with blood, representing asuras and vināyakas. Below this, paint the deity Lambinī, the body wearing freshly flayed elephant skin.

"At each of the four corners of the altar, place a vajra. At the eastern gate of the altar, paint the Great Terror deity along with a vajra and Mudra-jaṭa. At the southern gate, paint Great Vajra, hands holding a battle-axe, thundering at the vināyakas: this is the vajra general. At the western gate, paint a lotus; at the center, Keśa-dhāriṇī Nandūtī, hands holding a fire torch. At the northern gate, paint Mahā-vajra-śikhara-nandana, meaning the great vajra-peak attendant.

"When the painting is complete, according to one's ability, prepare the various implements and carry out the practice accordingly."

Colophon from the Réiun collation:

The above is a faithful transcription of the Vinaya Sūtra of the Secret Treasury in one fascicle. We humbly hope that future practitioners of the esoteric path will reverently uphold purity, so that the myriad beings throughout the Ganges-sand worlds may be moistened by the rain of the Dharma.

Collated on the twenty-fourth day of the first month of the autumn, the jiǎzǐ year of Jōkyō [1684].

Written by the bhikṣu Jōgon of Enmyōji temple, Nishiki County, Kawachi Province, Great Japan.
