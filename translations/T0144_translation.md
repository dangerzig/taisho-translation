# 佛說大愛道般泥洹經
## Sūtra on the Parinirvāṇa of Mahāprajāpatī Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0144

Translated from the Chinese. Western Jin dynasty, translated by Bai Fazhu (白法祖)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the country of Vaiśālī, staying at the Kūṭāgāraśālā Hall at the Monkey Pond. At that time, Mahāprajāpatī Gautamī was also in the country of Vaiśālī, together with five hundred bhikṣuṇīs. All were arhats, all possessed great supernatural powers. Among them were Sundarī, Mahālī, Viśākhā, Utpalavarṇā, and Prajāpatī Gautamī. These senior bhikṣuṇīs, the great disciples, were staying at the bhikṣuṇī monastery in the Royal Garden of Vaiśālī. At that time, Mahāprajāpatī Gautamī reflected to herself: "I cannot bear to see the Buddha enter parinirvāṇa, nor Ānanda, Śāriputra, Maudgalyāyana, and the other worthies. I shall first relinquish my lifespan and enter nirvāṇa." At that time, the Buddha perceived this and said to Ānanda: "Mahāprajāpatī Gautamī is now thinking: 'I cannot bear to see the Buddha enter parinirvāṇa, nor Ānanda, Śāriputra, Maudgalyāyana, and the other worthies. I shall first relinquish my lifespan and enter nirvāṇa.' And the five hundred bhikṣuṇīs have likewise reflected, wishing to relinquish all suffering: 'We cannot bear to see the Buddha enter parinirvāṇa, nor the worthy Ānanda, Śāriputra, Maudgalyāyana, and the other worthies. We too shall relinquish our lifespans and enter nirvāṇa.'" Thus spoke the Buddha.

Ānanda said to the Buddha: "My body cannot bear this. I cannot distinguish the four directions. What I have heard of the Dharma, I can no longer recollect. Why is this? It is because I have heard that Mahāprajāpatī Gautamī is about to enter parinirvāṇa."

The Buddha then told the Venerable Ānanda: "Ānanda! Do you think that Mahāprajāpatī Gautamī, in entering parinirvāṇa, will take the seed of diligence with her, or the seed of concentration, the seed of loving-kindness, the seed of liberation, the seed of the knowledge and vision of liberation, or the Dharma that I myself have realized through direct knowledge: the four foundations of mindfulness, the four right efforts, the four bases of supernatural power, the five faculties, the five powers, the seven factors of awakening, or the eightfold noble path? Are you afraid that Mahāprajāpatī Gautamī will take these teachings away?"

When the Buddha had spoken thus, Ānanda said: "Mahāprajāpatī Gautamī will certainly not take the seed of purity away in parinirvāṇa, nor the seed of concentration, the seed of wisdom, the seed of liberation, the seed of the knowledge and vision of wisdom, nor the seed of enlightenment. The teachings that the Buddha himself has realized through wisdom: the four foundations of mindfulness, the four right efforts, the four bases of supernatural power, the five faculties, the five powers, the seven factors of awakening, the eightfold noble path, she will certainly not take these teachings away in parinirvāṇa."

Ānanda said: "I was reflecting that Mahāprajāpatī Gautamī has great kindness toward the Buddha. When the Buddha's mother passed away, Mahāprajāpatī Gautamī nursed and raised the Buddha."

The Buddha said: "It is so, Ānanda. Mahāprajāpatī Gautamī has great kindness toward me. When my mother passed away, she nursed and raised me." The Buddha said: "Ānanda! I also have immeasurable kindness toward Mahāprajāpatī Gautamī. Why is this? It is because of me that Mahāprajāpatī Gautamī took refuge in the Buddha, took refuge in the Dharma, took refuge in the Saṃgha of bhikṣus, and took refuge in the practice of the path to its end. She no longer has doubts about the Buddha, no longer has doubts about the Dharma, no longer has doubts about the Saṃgha of bhikṣus, and no longer has doubts about the practice of the path to its end. She has understood all of this completely. Ānanda! If a person can teach another to take refuge in the Buddha, to take refuge in the Dharma, to take refuge in the Saṃgha of bhikṣus, and to take refuge in the practice of the path to its end, and the recipient keeps this for the span of a lifetime, and one receives and gives clothing, food, bedding, and medicine, never refusing what is asked for, doing this for the span of a lifetime, one would still be unable to repay the teacher's kindness." The Buddha said: "Therefore, Ānanda, my kindness toward Mahāprajāpatī Gautamī is great and immeasurable."

At that time, Mahāprajāpatī Gautamī, together with the five hundred bhikṣuṇīs, departed from the country of Vaiśālī and went to a great tree. They came to where the Buddha was, placed their heads at the Buddha's feet, and stood to one side. The five hundred bhikṣuṇīs likewise bowed to the Buddha and stood to one side. Mahāprajāpatī Gautamī then joined her palms and said to the Buddha: "I cannot bear to see the Buddha enter parinirvāṇa, nor Ānanda, Śāriputra, Maudgalyāyana, and the other worthy bhikṣus. I wish to first relinquish my lifespan and enter nirvāṇa." Having spoken thus, the Buddha silently accepted what Mahāprajāpatī Gautamī had said. Mahāprajāpatī Gautamī then touched the Buddha's feet with her hands and said: "Today is the last time I shall see the Refuge of the World. Today is the last time I shall see the Light of the World. Today is the last time I shall see the Unsurpassed One of the World. From this day forward, I shall never again see the Honored One of the three realms." Having bowed to the Buddha, she sat to one side. The five hundred bhikṣuṇīs likewise joined their palms and said to the Buddha: "We cannot bear to see the Buddha enter parinirvāṇa, nor the worthy Ānanda, Śāriputra, Maudgalyāyana, and the other worthy bhikṣus. We wish to relinquish our lifespans and enter nirvāṇa." The Buddha silently accepted what the five hundred bhikṣuṇīs had said. The five hundred bhikṣuṇīs then placed their heads at the Buddha's feet and said: "Today is the last time we shall see the Refuge of the World. Today is the last time we shall see the Light of the World. Today is the last time we shall see the Unsurpassed One of the World. From this day forward, we shall never again see the Honored One of the three realms." Having spoken thus, they each returned to their seats.

The Buddha taught Mahāprajāpatī Gautamī and the five hundred bhikṣuṇīs various categories of the Dharma. When this was finished, they all rose joyfully from their seats, bowed to the Buddha, circumambulated the Buddha three times, and placed their heads on the ground. They returned to the country of Vaiśālī and entered the bhikṣuṇī monastery in the Royal Garden. They spread out five hundred seats in one place. Mahāprajāpatī Gautamī and the five hundred bhikṣuṇīs each took their seats. At that time, Mahāprajāpatī Gautamī manifested her supernatural powers. From her seat she sank into the earth and disappeared, then emerged in the east, in the empty sky. She ascended from the height of one tree up to seven trees, manifesting the four kinds of supernatural powers. In the empty sky she walked about. Having walked about, she stood still. Having stood still, she sat down. Having sat down, she reclined. Having reclined, five-colored fire issued from her body: from the upper body came five-colored fire and from the lower body came water; from the lower body came five-colored fire and from the upper body came water. Thus she disappeared in the east and emerged in the west, disappeared in the south and emerged in the north. From the height of seven trees she descended to the ground, manifesting supernatural transformations as before. Then she extinguished her supernatural powers and entered nirvāṇa. At that time, the five hundred bhikṣuṇīs all likewise sank into the earth from their seats, emerged in the east, in the empty sky, and ascended from the height of one tree up to seven trees, manifesting the four kinds of supernatural powers. In the empty sky they walked about. Having walked about, they stood still. Having stood still, they sat down. Having sat down, they reclined. Having reclined, five-colored fire issued from their bodies: from the upper body came five-colored fire and from the lower body came water; from the lower body came five-colored fire and from the upper body came water. Thus they disappeared in the east and emerged in the west, disappeared in the south and emerged in the north. From the height of seven trees they descended to the ground, manifesting supernatural transformations as before. Then they extinguished their supernatural powers and entered nirvāṇa.

At that time, the Buddha told the Venerable Ānanda: "Go tomorrow at dawn into the city of Vaiśālī, to the house of the householder Yaśoda. When you arrive, tell the householder Yaśoda: 'The Buddha's mother has entered parinirvāṇa, together with the five hundred bhikṣuṇīs. The Buddha urges the householder to prepare five hundred biers, five hundred jars of sesame oil, five hundred portions of incense, five hundred portions of firewood, and various kinds of flowers, incense, and music. Bring them to the bhikṣuṇī monastery in the Royal Garden. The reason is that the Buddha's mother has entered parinirvāṇa together with the five hundred bhikṣuṇīs. They are all arhats, all of great supernatural powers. Their merit and virtue are complete. They should be given a proper funeral.'" The Buddha said to Ānanda: "Tell the householder: the Buddha urges this."

Ānanda heard the Buddha's words and replied: "So be it." He rose, placed his head at the Buddha's feet, and at dawn entered the city of Vaiśālī. He went to the house of the householder Yaśoda and, upon arriving, told the gatekeeper: "Announce that I have come, and tell the householder that Ānanda is outside." The gatekeeper, hearing Ānanda's words, went in and announced it. At that time, the householder Yaśoda was in a high tower with his attendants, enjoying music together. Hearing the gatekeeper's announcement, he was immediately alarmed, his hair standing on end. He descended from the tower and came out to meet Ānanda. He placed his head at the feet of the Venerable Ānanda in greeting and said to the Venerable Ānanda: "This is not an ordinary or trivial matter. Why has the Venerable come into the city so early?" The householder Yaśoda asked: "What has happened?" Ānanda then replied: "The Buddha has sent me to urge the householder to prepare five hundred biers, five hundred jars of sesame oil, five hundred portions of incense, five hundred portions of firewood, and various kinds of fine flowers and incense, and music. Bring them to the bhikṣuṇī monastery in the Royal Garden. The reason is that the Buddha's mother has entered parinirvāṇa together with the five hundred bhikṣuṇīs. They are all arhats, all of great supernatural powers. Their merit and virtue are complete. They should be given a proper funeral. The Buddha urges the householder thus."

When the householder heard Ānanda's words, he immediately fainted and fell to the ground, crying: "Venerable Ānanda! What offense have our people committed against the bhikṣuṇīs? What grievance did the bhikṣuṇīs hold against our people, that they entered parinirvāṇa without informing us? Venerable Ānanda! From this day forward, the practice halls will be empty, the seats will be empty, the walking paths will be empty. The crossroads and streets will all be empty. The city of Vaiśālī is already as if empty. Venerable Ānanda! From this day forward, we will never again see the bhikṣuṇīs going on their alms round into the city of Vaiśālī. How great is this pain!"

When the householder Yaśoda had finished speaking, Ānanda told the householder: "The Buddha himself has said: 'All things held dear must eventually be parted from and scattered. Each goes to a different place, each follows a different path. What is born, what arrives, what is thought of, each has its own actions, each has its own causes and conditions. All must eventually come to an end, all must eventually be parted. To wish that they not be parted is forever impossible. The wise should simply protect and practice the Dharma.'" At that time, the Venerable Ānanda cited various sūtras and essential teachings to ease the householder's mind. Having eased, gladdened, and encouraged the householder, Ānanda then went into the city of Vaiśālī.

The brāhmaṇas and householders had gathered in the lecture hall for a separate meeting. The Venerable Ānanda arrived and took a seat. When he was seated, he addressed the brāhmaṇas and householders of Vaiśālī: "You should all prepare five hundred biers, five hundred jars of sesame oil, five hundred portions of incense, five hundred portions of firewood, and various kinds of fine flowers and incense, and music. Bring them to the bhikṣuṇī monastery in the Royal Garden. The reason is that the Buddha's mother has entered parinirvāṇa together with the five hundred bhikṣuṇīs. They are all arhats, all of great supernatural powers. Their merit and virtue are complete. They should be given a proper funeral. The Buddha urges all the brāhmaṇas and householders thus." The brāhmaṇas and householders of Vaiśālī immediately fainted and fell to the ground, and said to the Venerable Ānanda: "What offense have our people committed against the bhikṣuṇīs? What grievance did the bhikṣuṇīs hold against our people? What have we done to offend the bhikṣuṇīs, that they entered parinirvāṇa without informing us? Venerable Ānanda! From this day forward, the practice halls will all be empty, the seats will all be empty. The crossroads and streets will all be empty. The city of Vaiśālī is already as if empty. From this day forward, we will never again see the bhikṣuṇīs entering the city of Vaiśālī on their alms round."

At that time, the Venerable Ānanda told the brāhmaṇas and householders of Vaiśālī: "The Buddha himself has said: 'All things held dear must eventually be parted from and scattered. Each goes to a different place, each follows a different path. What is born, what arrives, what is thought of, each has its own actions, each has its own causes and conditions. All must eventually be parted. To wish that they not be parted is forever impossible. The wise should simply protect and practice the Dharma.'" At that time, the Venerable Ānanda cited various sūtras and essential teachings for the brāhmaṇas and householders of Vaiśālī, to ease their minds, to gladden their minds, and to encourage their minds. Having eased, gladdened, and encouraged them, the Venerable Ānanda rose from his seat and went to where the Buddha was.

At that time, the householder Yaśoda, together with the five hundred brāhmaṇas and householders, brought the five hundred biers, five hundred jars of sesame oil, five hundred portions of firewood, five hundred portions of incense, and various kinds of fine flowers and incense and music, and came to the bhikṣuṇī monastery in the Royal Garden. When they arrived, the gate of the Royal Garden bhikṣuṇī monastery was closed. The householder Yaśoda told one person: "Come, climb onto another's shoulders, scale the wall, enter the garden, and open the gate." The person received the householder's instruction, climbed onto another's shoulders, scaled the wall, and opened the gate. The householder Yaśoda and the five hundred brāhmaṇas all entered the bhikṣuṇī monastery in the Royal Garden. At that time, the five hundred bhikṣuṇīs had together six śrāmaṇerīs. The six śrāmaṇerīs told the householder Yaśoda: "Worthy householder! Do not disturb the five hundred bhikṣuṇīs. They have all entered the meditative concentration of settled mind." At that time, the householder told the six śrāmaṇerīs: "The five hundred bhikṣuṇīs are not in meditative concentration. They have relinquished their lifespans and entered nirvāṇa." When the six śrāmaṇerīs heard these words, they immediately fainted and fell to the ground, crying: "Who will henceforth teach and admonish us? Who will henceforth correct and guide us? Whose robes and bowl shall we follow?"

At that time, the householder told the worthy śrāmaṇerīs: "The Buddha himself has said that all things held dear must eventually be parted. Worthy śrāmaṇerīs, do not be sorrowful. You should only strive diligently and increase your effort." At that time, the householder collected the relics of Mahāprajāpatī Gautamī. With various kinds of incense, flowers, and music, they reverently collected the relics and placed them on a golden bier. The relics of the five hundred bhikṣuṇīs were likewise treated. The householder Yaśoda, together with the five hundred brāhmaṇas and householders, brought the relics of Mahāprajāpatī Gautamī and the five hundred bhikṣuṇīs to where the Buddha was.

At that time, the Buddha told the Venerable Śāriputra: "Come! Face the east, join your palms, lower your right knee to the ground, and speak thus: 'Those in the east who are of right faith and right practice, who have attained the three kinds of supernatural knowledge, the six kinds of superknowledge, and the great supernatural powers, whose merit and virtue are complete, let them all come here. The reason is that the Buddha's mother has entered parinirvāṇa together with the five hundred bhikṣuṇīs. They are all arhats, all of great supernatural powers. Their merit and virtue are complete. Together we should give them a proper funeral.' Likewise for the south, the west, and the north, likewise for the east." The Venerable Śāriputra received the instruction and faced the east, the south, the west, and the north, inviting the arhats. Immediately, from the east came three hundred and fifty arhats, and likewise from the south, the west, and the north, making a total of one thousand arhats before the Buddha. The Buddha then, together with the one thousand bhikṣu monks, went to where the relics of Mahāprajāpatī Gautamī were. The Buddha took his seat, and the one thousand bhikṣus all took their seats.

At that time, the Buddha told the Venerable Ānanda: "Rise, take the relics of Mahāprajāpatī Gautamī, place them in a bowl, and bring them here into my hand." Ānanda replied: "So be it." He rose from his seat, took the relics of Mahāprajāpatī Gautamī, placed them in the bowl, and handed it to the Buddha. The Buddha received it with both hands. Having received the relics of Mahāprajāpatī Gautamī, the Buddha addressed the assembly of bhikṣu monks: "These are the relics of a woman. Originally this was an evil body: impetuous, violent, frivolous, fickle, and jealous. But Mahāprajāpatī Gautamī has already cast off the body of a woman. What a man should attain, Mahāprajāpatī Gautamī has already attained."

At that time, the Buddha commanded the householder Yaśoda and the assembly of bhikṣu monks to together erect a stūpa for Mahāprajāpatī Gautamī and the five hundred bhikṣuṇīs. When the stūpa was erected, the people of Vaiśālī, as well as the devas and humans, all together made offerings at the stūpa of Mahāprajāpatī Gautamī and the five hundred bhikṣuṇīs.

Thus spoke the Buddha. All the bhikṣus were joyful. They rose, came forward, bowed to the Buddha, and departed.
