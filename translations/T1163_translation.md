# 佛說雨寶陀羅尼經
## Sūtra of the Rain of Treasures Dhāraṇī Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1163

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

Thus have I heard. At one time the Bhagavān was in the kingdom of Kauśāmbī, in the Kaṇṭaka Forest, together with a great assembly of five hundred bhikṣus and also many bodhisattva mahāsattvas.

At that time in the kingdom of Kauśāmbī there was an elder named Sucandra, whose faculties were tranquil and whose mind was serene. He had many sons, daughters, and servants, and had accomplished pure faith. He went to the Buddha's place, bowed his face to the Buddha's feet, circumambulated hundreds of thousands of times, stood to one side, joined his palms in reverence, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I wish to ask the Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha about a small matter of doubt. I beseech you, out of great compassion, to graciously hear and grant permission."

At that time the World-Honored One told the elder: "Ask whatever you wish. I shall explain it for you and cause your heart to rejoice."

Then the elder, hearing these words, was joyful and elated: "World-Honored One! How can good men and good women who are poor and destitute become wealthy and prosperous? How can those who are ill be freed from illness?"

At that time the World-Honored One told the elder Sucandra: "What reason prompts such a question?"

Then the elder again addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! We who live as householders have many dependents, yet our resources are scarce and difficult to sustain. Furthermore, there is much illness. I beseech the World-Honored One to reveal the essential dharma, so that those who are poor may forever leave poverty, their storehouses and treasuries may all be filled to overflowing, their wives, sons, daughters, and dependents at home may be sustained, and whoever comes to ask will be met with joy, becoming a great patron of giving. May their storehouses of gold, silver, precious treasures, wish-fulfilling jewels, vajra gems, conch shells, crystal, red pearls, carnelian, and all manner of gold and treasures be abundant and inexhaustible, providing for relatives, broadly cultivating generous giving, and benefiting sentient beings."

At that time the World-Honored One told the elder Sucandra: "Good man! In the past, countless asaṃkhyeya kalpas ago, I encountered a buddha world-honored one named Vajradhara-sāgara-ghoṣa Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha. From that tathāgata I received this Rain of Treasures Dhāraṇī. I received, upheld, read, recited, contemplated, remembered, rejoiced in, and broadly proclaimed it for others. Through the awesome power of this dhāraṇī, if any good man, human or non-human, yakṣa, rākṣasa, preta, piśāca, kumbhāṇḍa, ostāraka, pūtana, kaṭa-pūtana, or other such beings with evil intentions, none can cause harm. Furthermore, there are various ghosts who devour human fat, marrow, pus, blood, mucus, saliva, urine, and feces: if they wish to cause disturbance, they cannot create obstruction."

The Buddha told Sucandra: "If any good man thinks of it in mind, holds it in hand, writes it down, or merely hears the name, receives, upholds, rejoices in, or broadly expounds it for others, that good man or good woman will be at peace throughout the long night, receiving all kinds of happiness, for the sake of abundant resources and peace for the yoga practitioner. If any person wishes to receive and uphold this Rain of Treasures Dhāraṇī, they should make offerings to all the tathāgatas. For one night, two nights, or three nights, single-mindedly recite and uphold it. Having received pure faith in the Three Jewels, all devas will rejoice and rain down wealth, treasures, and grain for the sake of that reciting dharma master." He then spoke the dhāraṇī:

[Dhāraṇī]

*Namo bhagavate vajradhara-sāgara-nirghoṣāya tathāgatāya tadyathā oṃ surūpe bhadra-vati maṃgale acchare accha-pāre udghaṭani udbhedani saśya-vati dhānya-vati dhana-vati śrīmati prabhā-vati amāre vimāre ruru surūpe vimāre ananta-siddhi vita-siddhi viśvake aṃ-kure maṃ-kure didi-me dudu-me tata-re tara-tara vajre aṭani bhuke ukke ṭake ṭake raṣaṇi niṣpadani bhagavan vajradhara-sāgara-nirghoṣaṃ tathāgatam anusmara smara smara sarva-tathāgata-satyam anusmara saṃgha-satyam anusmara taṭṭa taṭṭa pura pura purāya purāya bhara bhara bharaṇi sumaṃgale śānta-mati maṃgala-mati prabhā-mati mahā-mati subhadra-vati agaccha agaccha samayam anusmara svāhā ādharaṇam anusmara svāhā prabhāvam anusmara svāhā dhṛtim anusmara svāhā vijayam anusmara svāhā sarva-sattva-vijayam anusmara svāhā*

The Buddha told the elder Sucandra: "This is called the Rain of Treasures Dhāraṇī. Through the awesome power of this dhāraṇī, illness, famine, epidemic, and karmic obstacles are all extinguished. If any good man or good woman first makes offerings to all tathāgatas and for one day and one night without interruption recites and upholds this dhāraṇī, in that household treasures will rain down in the measure of a great person. All calamities will be entirely extinguished. Therefore, good man! You should receive and uphold this Rain of Treasures Dhāraṇī and broadly expound and explain it for others."

"Excellent! World-Honored One!" The elder Sucandra, hearing what the Buddha taught, was joyful and elated: "I now receive from the Buddha this Rain of Treasures Dhāraṇī, and shall receive, uphold, read, recite, and broadly explain it for others."

At that time the elder Sucandra, having received the Buddha's instruction, circumambulated the World-Honored One hundreds of thousands of times, joined his palms in reverence, bowed his face to the Buddha's feet, and departed with joy.

At that time the Buddha told the long-lived Ānanda: "Go to the home of the elder Sucandra and see that in all his storehouses, all varieties of wealth, grain, precious treasures, and resources are now completely filled to overflowing."

At that time the long-lived Ānanda, having received the Buddha's instruction, went to the great city of Kauśāmbī and to the home of the elder Sucandra. Upon entering, he saw that all the storehouses were filled to overflowing with wealth and treasures. Seeing this, he was greatly joyful and elated and returned.

At that time the long-lived Ānanda, marveling at what had never been seen before, was greatly joyful and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! What cause and condition has filled the storehouses of the elder Sucandra's home to overflowing?"

The Buddha said: "Good man! The elder Sucandra had pure faith in me and received and upheld this Rain of Treasures Dhāraṇī, proclaiming it for all sentient beings. Therefore, Ānanda! Receive and uphold this dhāraṇī and broadly proclaim it for others. I observe with the Buddha's eye throughout all the worlds that devas, humans, māras, brahmās, śramaṇas, and brāhmaṇas cannot create obstruction or difficulty for one who receives and upholds the Rain of Treasures Dhāraṇī. Why? Because the Tathāgata speaks no false words, and these true word phrases cannot be destroyed. This dhāraṇī cannot even be heard by sentient beings of scant wholesome roots, let alone being copied, received, upheld, read, or recited. Why? Because all tathāgatas have spoken it with true words, all tathāgatas have rejoiced in it, all tathāgatas have praised it, all tathāgatas have proclaimed it, and all tathāgatas have planted it."

Ānanda addressed the Buddha: "Excellent! World-Honored One!" And he praised in wondrous verses:

> "The buddhas are inconceivable; the buddhadharma is likewise so.
> Pure faith is inconceivable; its rewards are also thus.
> The tranquil wisdom, the omniscient dharma king, is beyond birth and death.
> Having reached the excellent further shore, I bow to the courageous buddha."

At that time the long-lived Ānanda, hearing the Buddha proclaim this Rain of Treasures Dhāraṇī Sūtra, was elated and joyful, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! What shall this essential dharma be named? How shall we now receive and uphold this sūtra?"

The Buddha told Ānanda: "This sūtra is called 'What the Elder Sucandra Asked,' and you should receive and uphold it. It is also called 'That Which Can Obtain All Wealth, Treasures, and Hidden Stores,' and it is also called 'The Teaching of the Rain of Treasures Dhāraṇī Praised by All Tathāgatas.' You should receive and uphold it."

When the Bhagavān had spoken this sūtra, the immeasurable bhikṣus and bodhisattvas, and all the devas, humans, asuras, and others, and the entire great assembly, hearing what the Buddha taught, all greatly rejoiced, accepted it with faith, and put it into practice.

The heart true word:

*Oṃ vasudhāre svāhā*

The heart-within-the-heart true word:

*Oṃ śrī-vasu svāhā*

The lesser heart true word:

*Oṃ vasu svāhā*
