# 佛說目連所問經
## Sūtra on Maudgalyāyana's Questions Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1468

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmadeva (法天).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was dwelling at the Bamboo Grove Monastery in the city of Rājagṛha. At that time, the Venerable Mahāmaudgalyāyana, during the last watch of the night, went from his own dwelling to the place where the Buddha was. Upon arriving, he prostrated, touching his head to the Buddha's feet, and sat to one side.

Then the Venerable Mahāmaudgalyāyana rose from his seat and addressed the Buddha, saying: "World-Honored One! If there are bhikṣus or bhikṣuṇīs who, confused and intoxicated, violate the precepts, without shame or remorse, who slight the rules of discipline and engage in unlawful conduct, World-Honored One, what consequences will befall them?"

The World-Honored One told the Venerable Mahāmaudgalyāyana: "If there are bhikṣus or bhikṣuṇīs who, confused and intoxicated, violate the precepts, without shame or remorse, who slight the rules of discipline and engage in unlawful conduct, those persons, upon the end of their lives, will be reborn in hell for a duration equal to five hundred years in the Heaven of the Four Great Kings, which in human years amounts to nine million years."

The Venerable Mahāmaudgalyāyana again addressed the Buddha, saying: "World-Honored One! If there are bhikṣus or bhikṣuṇīs who, confused and intoxicated, violate the precepts, without shame or remorse, who slight the rules of discipline and do not follow the preaching of the Dharma, what consequences will befall them?"

The World-Honored One told the Venerable Mahāmaudgalyāyana: "If there are bhikṣus or bhikṣuṇīs who, confused and intoxicated, violate the precepts, without shame or remorse, who slight the rules of discipline and do not follow the preaching of the Dharma, those persons, upon the end of their lives, will be reborn in hell for a duration equal to one thousand years in Trāyastriṃśa Heaven, which in human years amounts to thirty-six million years."

The Venerable Mahāmaudgalyāyana addressed the Buddha, saying: "World-Honored One! If there are bhikṣus or bhikṣuṇīs who, confused and intoxicated, violate the precepts, without shame or remorse, who slight the rules of discipline and commit pāyattika offenses, what consequences will befall them?"

The World-Honored One told the Venerable Mahāmaudgalyāyana: "If there are bhikṣus or bhikṣuṇīs who, confused and intoxicated, violate the precepts, without shame or remorse, who slight the rules of discipline and commit pāyattika offenses, those persons, upon the end of their lives, will be reborn in hell for a duration equal to two thousand years in Yāma Heaven, which in human years amounts to one hundred forty-four million years."

The Venerable Mahāmaudgalyāyana addressed the Buddha, saying: "World-Honored One! If there are bhikṣus or bhikṣuṇīs who, confused and intoxicated, violate the precepts, without shame or remorse, who slight the rules of discipline and commit sthūlātyaya offenses, what consequences will befall them?"

The World-Honored One told the Venerable Mahāmaudgalyāyana: "If there are bhikṣus or bhikṣuṇīs who, confused and intoxicated, violate the precepts, without shame or remorse, who slight the rules of discipline and commit sthūlātyaya offenses, those persons, upon the end of their lives, will be reborn in hell for a duration equal to four thousand years in Tuṣita Heaven, which in human years amounts to five hundred seventy million and sixty thousand years."

The Venerable Mahāmaudgalyāyana addressed the Buddha, saying: "World-Honored One! If there are bhikṣus or bhikṣuṇīs who, confused and intoxicated, violate the precepts, without shame or remorse, who slight the rules of discipline and commit saṃghāvaśeṣa offenses, what consequences will befall them?"

The World-Honored One told the Venerable Mahāmaudgalyāyana: "If there are bhikṣus or bhikṣuṇīs who, confused and intoxicated, violate the precepts, without shame or remorse, who slight the rules of discipline and commit saṃghāvaśeṣa offenses, those persons, upon the end of their lives, will be reborn in hell for a duration equal to eight thousand years in Nirmāṇarati Heaven, which in human years amounts to two billion three hundred four million years."

The Venerable Mahāmaudgalyāyana addressed the Buddha, saying: "World-Honored One! If there are bhikṣus or bhikṣuṇīs who, confused and intoxicated, violate the precepts, without shame or remorse, who slight the rules of discipline and commit pārājika offenses, what consequences will befall them?"

The World-Honored One told the Venerable Mahāmaudgalyāyana: "If there are bhikṣus or bhikṣuṇīs who, confused and intoxicated, violate the precepts, without shame or remorse, who slight the rules of discipline and commit pārājika offenses, those persons, upon the end of their lives, will be reborn in hell for a duration equal to sixteen thousand years in Paranirmitavaśavartin Heaven, which in human years amounts to nine billion one hundred fifty-six million years."

When the Venerable Mahāmaudgalyāyana heard the Buddha's teaching, he was greatly overjoyed, made obeisance, and departed.
