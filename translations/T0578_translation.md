# 無垢優婆夷問經
## Sūtra of the Questions of the Upāsikā Vimalā

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0578

Translated from the Chinese. Northern Wei dynasty, translated by Gautama Prajñāruci (瞿曇般若流支)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Bhagavān was dwelling in the city of Śrāvastī, in the Peaceful Palace Hall of the Tiered Pavilion.

At that time, the upāsikā Vimalā, the upāsikā Bhadrā, and other upāsikās went to the place of the Buddha. Having arrived, they bowed their heads at his feet, and after paying respects they sat to one side.

Then the World-Honored One said to the upāsikā Vimalā: "Upāsikā! In your practice of diligence, is your mind not negligent?"

The upāsikā said: "I am not negligent and not lax in my practice."

The Buddha said to the upāsikā Vimalā: "How is it that you are always not negligent? And how is it that you are not lax in your practice?"

The upāsikā Vimalā said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I always rise early and sweep the ground of the Buddha's stūpa. Having swept, I anoint and clean the four sides and four corners. When the ground is clean, I scatter flowers and burn incense. Having made such offerings, I then enter my room. Having entered my room, I next enter meditation and cultivate the four brahmavihāras. I do not depart from the three refuges, and I uphold the five precepts. I am always thus. I am not negligent and not lax in my practice."

When the upāsikā Vimalā had spoken thus, she again said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I do not yet know what merit and reward come from the good roots of sweeping the ground of the Buddha's stūpa. What merit and reward come from the good roots of anointing the four sides? What merit and reward come from the good roots of scattering flowers, burning incense, and making offerings to the Buddha's stūpa? What merit and reward come from the good roots of meditation, the four brahmavihāras, the three refuges, and the five precepts? May the World-Honored One explain this for me."

The Buddha said to the upāsikā Vimalā: "Sweeping the ground of the Buddha's stūpa yields five rewards. What are these five? First, one's own mind becomes pure, and others who see this generate a mind of purity. Second, one becomes beloved by others. Third, the devas rejoice. Fourth, one accumulates the karma of a dignified appearance. Fifth, at the end of one's life, one is born on a good path among the devas. Vimalā, you should know: the rewards of sweeping the ground of the Buddha's stūpa are such as these.

"Vimalā, you should know: if a person has faith in the Buddha and anoints the ground of the Buddha's stūpa in a circular shape, scatters flowers and burns incense, making offerings in this way, I say that when that person's body perishes and life ends, they will be born in Pūrvavideha, with abundant happiness and freedom. When their life there ends, they will be born in the Nirmāṇarati heaven.

"Vimalā, you should know: if a person has faith in the Buddha and anoints the ground of the Buddha's stūpa in a half-moon shape, scatters flowers and burns incense, making offerings in this way, I say that when that person's body perishes and life ends, they will be born in Aparagodānīya, with abundant happiness and freedom. When their life there ends, they will be born in the Tuṣita heaven.

"Vimalā, you should know: if a person has faith in the Buddha and beside the Buddha's stūpa anoints the ground in a square shape, scatters flowers and burns incense, making offerings in this way, I say that when that person's body perishes and life ends, they will be born in Uttarakuru, with abundant happiness and freedom. When their life there ends, they will be born in the Yāma heaven.

"Vimalā, you should know: if a person has faith in the Buddha and anoints the ground of the Buddha's stūpa in a human-face shape, scatters flowers and burns incense, making offerings in this way, I say that when that person's body perishes and life ends, they will be born in Jambudvīpa, with abundant happiness and freedom. When their life ends, they will be born in the Trāyastriṃśa heaven.

"Vimalā, you should know: the rewards of anointing the stūpa ground, scattering flowers, and burning incense through these good roots are such as these.

"Vimalā, you should know: if a person enters meditation, cultivates the four brahmavihāras, takes refuge in the Buddha, the Dharma, and the Saṃgha, and upholds the five precepts, I say that the good roots of that person are immeasurable and innumerable, and the rewards are inexhaustible, ultimately leading to nirvāṇa.

"Vimalā, you should know: if a person takes refuge in śrāvakas and pratyekabuddhas and cultivates the aggregate of precepts, they cannot attain this inexhaustible nirvāṇa. Why? Because the rewards obtained from upholding the five precepts, meditating, and cultivating the four brahmavihāras can only be received in nirvāṇa and nowhere else, since the merit is so great."

When the World-Honored One had spoken thus, the upāsikā Vimalā's mind gave rise to doubt, and she was silent, saying nothing.

At that time, the World-Honored One, knowing her thoughts, extended from his face his broad, long tongue, which covered his entire face, both ears, both eyes, and both nostrils, and then covered the sky. Having covered the sky, he retracted it into his mouth. Having retracted it, he again spoke to the upāsikā Vimalā: "Have you ever seen a person who speaks falsely, who speaks with a forked tongue, who speaks harshly, or who speaks frivolously, who has a tongue of such form and appearance?"

Then the upāsikā Vimalā, having heard these words, rose from her seat, joined her palms toward the Buddha, and said: "World-Honored One! I have never seen such a thing. Even a truthful person has never had such a tongue, let alone a person who speaks falsely. Only the Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, because from beginningless time he has always spoken truthfully, has obtained such a tongue."

Then the upāsikā Vimalā, knowing the Buddha speaks truth, was freed from doubt and filled with joy. She praised him in verse:

> "For a thousand billion kalpas
> I have never once seen
> Such a supreme Dharma-jewel,
> Nor have I ever made offerings to one.
> Today I have seen
> Such a lamp of the world.
> Having seen thus,
> I have heard the foremost Dharma."

Then the World-Honored One replied to the upāsikā Vimalā in verse:

> "Having heard, you have become a noble one.
> This is the true, supreme Dharma-jewel,
> The pure and tranquil Dharma.
> You have entered the city of nirvāṇa."

When the World-Honored One had spoken, the upāsikā Vimalā's heart was filled with great joy. The bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, upāsikās, and the entire assembly of devas, humans, asuras, gandharvas, and others, hearing the Buddha's teaching, praised it saying: "Excellent!"

> This sūtra is the cause of human and heavenly birth,
> And also the path to nirvāṇa.
> Practitioners proceed toward the human and heavenly realms,
> And also approach the gate of nirvāṇa.
> May the merit of translating this sūtra
> Be given universally to all beings.
> May they swiftly cultivate the path of causes
> And attain the human realm, the heavenly realm, and nirvāṇa.
