# 修習般若波羅蜜菩薩觀行念誦儀軌
## Ritual of Contemplative Practice and Recitation for Cultivating the Prajñāpāramitā Bodhisattva

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1151

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

> I pay homage to the Prajñā Mother, the pure and sublime dharma-body.
> I shall now, according to yoga, expound the method of contemplative practice.

> Extend the mind to pervade the dharma-realm, the ocean of all buddha-lands.
> Visualize the body circling everywhere; through this one attains liberation.

> Visualize the body circling all buddhas; before each and every buddha,
> Surrounded by assemblies of bodhisattvas, holding sublime offering implements.

> The tathāgatas of the three times, the great bodhisattva assemblies,
> Pratyekabuddhas and śrāvakas, all the treasuries of the true Dharma:
> All these without remainder visualize before oneself,
> Before each and every assembly, clear and vivid as if face to face.

> All arising from the pure mind, vast and manifold offering implements,
> Making immeasurable offerings, bowing without cease.

> Palaces, sublime whisks, canopies, countless pavilions and towers,
> Flower-garlands, powdered and paste incense, adorned with various jewels.

> All those offering implements pervade and fill the realm of space,
> With earnest mind empowered and offered to all the tathāgatas.

> As space, so are the buddha-lands; as the lands, so too the buddhas.
> All are immeasurable, the ten directions' boundless realms.

> Having understood, one speaks these words: "I bow to all tathāgatas."

The mantra for bowing to all tathāgatas:

*Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-pāda-vandanāṃ karomi.*

> The various supreme and immeasurable dhāraṇīs taught in Mahāyāna sūtras,
> The heart of the buddhas: one should earnestly recite them.

> Generate a mind of pure faith, concentrate on a single object,
> For the sake of all sentient beings, contemplate the supreme bodhi.

> Then one should bare one shoulder, place the right knee on the ground,
> Dwell with sincere mind, palms joined, and speak these words:

> "What I have done, caused others to do, or what self and others have rejoiced in,
> All sins of body, speech, and mind: may all buddhas grant their compassion.
> All offenses of this life and the billions of past lives since beginningless time,
> And until I sit upon the seat of bodhi: I shall never again transgress."

> Such supreme confession, by truly contemplating,
> With the fire of the buddhas' vow-wisdom, burns all away without remainder.

> The grasped and the grasper are all nothing but mind;
> Buddha-lands, offerings: all are the grasper's and grasped's intention.

> The bodhisattva abiding in meditative absorption sees the mind as a reflected image.
> The grasper, conception, and discrimination all abide in emptiness.

> The two natures are without nature; this is called the mark of emptiness.
> Without nature is not non-existence; it is inseparable from the one nature.

> Agent, recipient, and offering: these three are free from discrimination.
> Because there is no discrimination, this offering is supreme.

> Such supreme offering and the method of circumambulation:
> All grave offenses and sins are eliminated without remainder.

> Knowing that the nature of desire is without desire, one enters the emptiness of desire's nature.
> Knowing that the nature of anger is without anger, one enters the emptiness of anger's nature.
> Knowing that the nature of delusion is without delusion, one enters the emptiness of delusion's nature.
> Thus, regarding all afflictions, one contemplates the principle of their true nature.

> Those who possess this wisdom, through the emptiness of non-discrimination,
> Are like a lotus dwelling in water: untouched by any offense.

The practitioner, having thus contemplated and purified body and mind, then forms the Samaya mudrā.

> Join both hands together, place them upon the heart.
> Contemplate the buddhas throughout space and recite the mantra three times.

The mantra:

*Oṃ vajrāñjali.*

Next, form the Vajra Bond: the ten fingers interlaced outwardly, tightly bound to form the mudrā. The Vajra Bond mantra:

*Vajra bandha.*

> With the previous Vajra Bond, strike upon the heart.
> This can destroy all fetters and reveal the palace of liberation.

Then recite the Heart-Opening mantra:

*Vajra bandha trāṭ.*

> Next, form the Wisdom-Generating mudrā. As with the previous Vajra Bond,
> The samādhi and wisdom fingers enter the palms, summoning the wisdom of perfect nirvāṇa,
> Summoning the omniscient wisdom.

The mantra:

*Oṃ vajra āveśa aḥ.*

> Next, form the Vajra Fist: the progress fingers press upon the meditation-wisdom fingers.
> The vajra body, speech, and mind are sealed together, unshakable.

Recite the Vajra Fist mantra:

*Vajra muṣṭi aḥ.*

> Then form the Samaya: one naturally becomes Samantabhadra.
> Having formed it, raise the patience-vow fingers; the body dwells in the moon disc.

Recite the Samaya mantra:

*Oṃ samaya sattvaṃ.*

> Next, form the Great True Vow: the patience-vow fingers reverse into the bond;
> The charity-wisdom and concentration-knowledge fingers stand upright.
> Touch the heart two or three times;
> The defiled seeds in the storehouse consciousness are repelled, and compassion grows.

Then recite the mantra:

*Oṃ samaya hoḥ surata sattvaṃ.*

> Next, one should perform the warding-off: form the Vajra Yakṣa.
> Both hands in Vajra Fists, the charity-wisdom and progress-power fingers bent,
> Placed at both sides of the mouth. Visualize the body as identical to that deity.
> In a fierce voice, recite the mantra while looking left and right.

The Vajra Yakṣa mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-yakṣa hūṃ.*

> All obstructing beings scatter and flee in the four directions.
> Visualize the two eyes as māṭha, spinning like the sun and moon discs.
> The śamatha and vipaśyanā fists are each placed upon the hips;
> Following the sun's direction, perform a sweeping gaze to establish the ten-directional boundary.

Recite the Vajra Eye mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-nṛṣṭi māṭha.*

> Enter samādhi: the bond mudrā as before,
> Placed face-up upon the crossed legs, body upright and straight.
> Close the eyes and enter right samādhi; contemplate the buddhas throughout space,
> Filling it like sesame seeds. All those tathāgatas,
> Remembering their former vows, with different mouths but one voice,
> Teach and instruct with mantras. Concentrate upon the principle of true nature.

*Oṃ sarva-yoga-citta-utpādayāmi.*

> Concentrate upon the principle of true nature, reciting this mantra frequently.
> Settle the mind in inner contemplation, gradually pervading the dharma-realm.
> All appearances of empty space, even empty space has no appearance;
> Through the yoga of empty space, extend and pervade the ten directions.

The practitioner, having entered the profoundly deep samādhi of the essential nature of prajñāpāramitā, within the true nature realizes a great moon disc whose measure equals the dharma-realm, its light universally illuminating, serene and cool. Arising from great compassion for all sentient beings in the three realms ablaze with suffering, regarding them as an only child and deeply aspiring to rescue them, the practitioner reflects: "How shall I now cause those who have not yet crossed over to cross over, those not yet liberated to be liberated, those not yet comforted to be comforted, and those who have not yet attained nirvāṇa to attain nirvāṇa?"

One then enters the yoga samādhi of the presiding deity, visualizing the body as the Prajñāpāramitā Buddha-Mother, wearing the crown of the five buddhas, in celestial garments and jeweled necklaces, with golden radiance streaming from every pore as innumerable buddhas. One further reflects: "My body and the Prajñāpāramitā Bodhisattva are not two and not different." Then, with the Vajra-Rakṣa Bodhisattva mudrā, one empowers the four places.

> Both hands in Vajra Bond, the progress-power fingers raised straight like needles.
> Stamp the heart, nose, throat, and crown: this completes the deity yoga.

Recite the mantra:

*Vajra-rakṣa haṃ.*

> Next, form the Five-Buddha Crown: both hands in Vajra Bond.
> The patience-vow fingers like swords, the progress-power fingers against the upper joints.
> Place it on the crown, then the four directions.
> Both fists circle before the forehead,
> Tied at the back of the head like a garland, trailing down like ribbons.

The mantra for the Crown of All Tathāgatas:

*Oṃ bhukhaṃ.*

Next recite the Vajra Family mantra:

*Vajrasattva.*

Next recite the Jewel Family mantra:

*Vajraratna.*

Next recite the Lotus Family mantra:

*Vajradharma.*

Next recite the Karma Family mantra:

*Vajrakarma.*

Next recite the Vajra Garland Consecration mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-māle abhiṣiñca maṃ.*

Next, form the Armor mudrā: both hands in Vajra mudrā, don the armor for self and others. The index fingers circle and tie; this constantly produces great protection. Recite the Armor mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-kavaca vajrī kuru vajra haṃ.*

With this Armor mudrā and mantra, one dons the armor and obtains a body of vajra nature, and furthermore obtains a vajra lifespan. For the duration of one's life, one receives great empowered protection; in all places, nothing can obstruct or destroy. After donning the armor, clap the palms together, producing joy.

Next recite the Vajra Joy mantra:

*Vajra-tuṣya hoḥ.*

> Through this heart mantra, release the bonds and attain joy,
> Obtaining a vajra body, like Vajrasattva himself.

> Having completed the Universal Entry of Vajra, the great mudrā, following the proper ritual,
> Should be formed before the body while contemplating the Great Being.
> See that wisdom-being and contemplate it as oneself:
> Summon with a hook, draw in, bind, and cause delight for accomplishment.

Recite the Prajñāpāramitā Universal Entry mantra:

*Oṃ dharma vajra aḥ.*

Next recite the Prajñāpāramitā Contemplation Heart mantra:

*Oṃ dharma vajre nṛśaya jaḥ hūṃ vaṃ hoḥ.*

This is the Great Being's hook-summoning, drawing-in, binding, and heart-delighting. Recite "samaya sattvaṃ," and the universal entry enters behind through the moon disc. Within it, one should contemplate the being behind. "I am the samaya sattvaṃ": following that mudrā-being, cultivate and observe one's own body. The vajra speech is now accomplished and can accomplish all mudrās.

The practitioner then, before the image of the presiding deity within the altar, visualizes a pavilion of seven jewels with four open gates. Within that hall, contemplate the syllable *a*, which becomes the Prajñāpāramitā Buddha-Mother, surrounded by the ten pāramitās, together with the eight offering deities and the four gate hook-and-chain attendants, and the guardian deities of the eight directions. All are visualized and formed from their own seed-syllables. Then visualize oneself in the Akaniṣṭha heaven of the form realm, and perform the summoning mudrā.

> With a snap of the fingers, one should invite all the buddhas.
> In an instant, all buddhas and vajra bodhisattvas
> Fill every altar, gathering in the maṇḍala.
> Then swiftly form the great mudrā. Contemplate Vajrasattva,
> Reciting once the 108. Through forming the gathering mudrā with joy,
> All tathāgatas become firm, and one naturally becomes Vajrasattva,
> Abiding as a compassionate friend in all the gates and every place.
> Hooks and other implements perform their work through the great karma mudrā.

The Summoning mantra:

*Oṃ vajra samāja jaḥ.*

> Next, form the four attractions: both hands in Vajra Fists,
> Cross the wrists, bind the charity-wisdom fingers. The index finger like a great hook,
> Like a lasso, like a chain; the backs of the hands pressing close.

The Vajra Hook mantra:

*Vajrāṅkuśe jaḥ.*

The Vajra Lasso mantra:

*Vajrapāśe hūṃ.*

The Vajra Chain mantra:

*Vajrasphote vaṃ.*

The Vajra Bell mantra:

*Vajraghaṇṭe hoḥ.*

> Through the Vajra Hook's summoning, all undertakings are accomplished;
> Through the Vajra Lasso's ritual, all are drawn in;
> Through the Vajra Chain's correspondence, all are bound as appropriate;
> Through the Vajra's entry and binding, all universal entries are accomplished.

Then lift the arghya vessel and offer it while reciting the Hundred-Syllable Mantra:

*Oṃ vajrasattva samayam anupālaya vajrasattva-tvenopatiṣṭha dṛḍho me bhava sutoṣyo me bhava anurakto me bhava supoṣyo me bhava sarva-siddhiṃ me prayaccha sarva-karmasu ca me cittaṃ śreyaḥ kuru hūṃ ha ha ha ha hoḥ bhagavān sarva-tathāgata-vajra mā me muñca vajrī bhava mahā-samaya-sattva āḥ.*

Through offering the arghya with the Hundred-Syllable Mantra, all that one wishes is swiftly accomplished.

Next recite the Vajra Play mantra:

*He rati vajra-vilāsinī trāṭ.*

Next the Vajra Garland mantra:

*He rati vajra-hāse haha.*

Next the Vajra Song mantra:

*He rati vajra-gīte titi.*

Next the Vajra Dance mantra:

*He rati vajra-nṛtye vaipa vaipa.*

Then perform the four external offerings and present them.

Next the Vajra Flower-Garland mantra:

*He vajra-rati.*

Next the Vajra Incense mantra:

*Mahā-ratna vajrī hoḥ.*

Next the Vajra Lamp mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-locanī.*

Next the Vajra Perfume mantra:

*Mahā-śrī vajrī hī.*

When the practitioner abides in the samādhi of these eight offering bodhisattvas, they can produce the offering rites of vast cloud-oceans of offerings pervading all buddha-lands throughout the ten directions. Then form the presiding deity's samaya mudrā: both hands in vajra añjali, with the thumbs each pressing the side of the index fingers. Recite the presiding deity's mantra seven times.

The mantra:

*Namo bhagavatyai prajñāpāramitāyai. Oṃ hrīḥ dhī śrī śrutī vijaye svāhā.*

After reciting the mantra, release the mudrā above the crown.

Next recite the Rosary mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-guhya-japa-samaye hūṃ.*

Through reciting this mantra to empower the rosary seven times, each single recitation becomes one thousand. After completing this empowerment, during the four periods, three periods, or at least two periods of the day, recite either 108 times or 1,000 times. The time period and number of recitations should each remain constant. When the recitation count is complete, raise the rosary to the crown as before, then enter the Prajñāpāramitā samādhi.

Within the heart, visualize a perfectly full moon disc whose measure pervades the dharma-realm. Within it, contemplate the syllable *a*, clearly and distinctly, white as conch shell, concentrating upon it. This is called śamatha. Having gathered all scattered and agitated thoughts so the mind is settled, one then corresponds with wisdom, contemplating the meaning of the syllable *a*: because the nature and characteristics of all dharmas, like the dharma-realm, cannot be apprehended, this is called vipaśyanā.

Whether corresponding with samādhi or corresponding with wisdom, when this has been long practiced and fully matured, samādhi and wisdom operate in tandem. The contemplated and the contemplator become equal, and the grasped and the grasper should both be completely abandoned. By constantly performing this contemplation, in this very life one enters the first bhūmi, and after sixteen great births, one becomes Samantabhadra Bodhisattva.

Again form the presiding deity's mudrā and recite the Hundred-Syllable Mantra. Again form the eight offering mudrās, lift the arghya water, and with the Vajra Eye gaze to the left to release the boundary. Then form the Dharma-Pāramitā mudrā to bid farewell to the holy assembly. Recite the Vajra Liberation mantra:

*Oṃ kṛtu-va sarva-sattva-ratha-siddhi-raṇadatayathā-nuga-gaccha-dhvaṃ buddha-viṣayaṃ punarāgamanāyatu. Oṃ vajra-ghāga-sattva muḥ.*

Then, with the Vajra Jewel mudrā, perform the consecration, don the armor, bow to the five directional buddhas, and exit the practice site. Walk about at will and read Mahāyāna scriptures. Transfer all this supreme merit to all sentient beings for the swift realization of the unsurpassed, perfect enlightenment of Prajñāpāramitā.

Afterward, the following explanation of the powers is given, which is truly sublime.

The Root Mantra of Prajñāpāramitā:

*Namo bhagavatyai prajñāpāramitāyai. Oṃ hrīḥ dhī śrī śrutī vijaye svāhā.*

The Buddha said: "This dhāraṇī-mudrā has four names: first, the Inexhaustible Treasury of Prajñā; second, the Eye of Prajñā; third, the Root of Prajñā; fourth, the Vajra Prajñā Heart. This dhāraṇī-mudrā possesses great merit. If one can, with the utmost sincerity, uphold it according to the method, each recitation produces 18,000 sūtra-piṭaka collections. Furthermore, within each of those sūtra collections, 25,000 more sūtra-piṭaka collections are produced. Furthermore, within each of those sūtra collections, one million sūtra-piṭaka collections are produced. Furthermore, within each of those sūtra-piṭaka collections, immeasurable hundreds of thousands of millions of billions of nayutas of asaṃkhyeyas of sūtra-piṭaka collections are produced. Thus, cascading forth to produce immeasurable and inexhaustible sūtra-piṭaka collections, each of whose titles, names, phrases, and flavors of meaning are each different, never repeated. Thus, thought after thought, they arise without end. Therefore it is called the Inexhaustible Treasury Dhāraṇī-Mudrā.

"This dhāraṇī-mudrā is the ancestral source of all buddhas of the ten directions and three times. It is also the inexhaustible dharma-treasury of all buddhas of the ten directions and three times. It is the mother of all prajñāpāramitā. The buddhas and bodhisattvas of past, present, and future constantly make offerings to it, revere it, and praise it. If any good man or good woman, with the utmost sincerity, copies, reads, recites, and practices as taught, all the grave sins accumulated over hundreds of thousands of millions of billions of Ganges-sands of kalpas of birth and death will, in a brief moment, be entirely eliminated without remainder. The merit of this dhāraṇī-mudrā, even if I were to dwell for hundreds of thousands of millions of billions of asaṃkhyeya kalpas praising it, I could not exhaust it. How much less could any other person exhaust its praise?

"If one wishes to practice the prajñā method, one should eat only one meal, observe the precepts, bathe in fragrant water, put on fresh clean garments, and enter the practice site. One must first recite this dhāraṇī and form this mudrā a full one million times. Afterward, in practicing other prajñā methods, one will certainly attain accomplishment. Therefore it is called the Root of Prajñā.

"This dhāraṇī-mudrā can completely illuminate all the dharmas of prajñāpāramitā; therefore it is called the Eye of Prajñāpāramitā.

"This dhāraṇī-mudrā can completely destroy all obstacles and can completely uphold all the merits of buddhas and bodhisattvas; therefore it is called the Vajra Prajñā Heart."
