# 般若波羅蜜多理趣經大樂不空三昧真實金剛薩埵菩薩等一十七聖大曼荼羅義述
## Exposition of the Meaning of the Great Maṇḍala of the Seventeen Holy Ones Including Vajrasattva Bodhisattva of the Great Bliss Amoghavajra Samādhi of True Reality, from the Prajñāpāramitā-naya Sūtra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1004

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空)

---

At that time, Vairocana Tathāgata, in the palace of the Paranirmita-vaśavartin Heaven King, for the sake of all the great bodhisattvas, taught the seventeen pure sentence-gates of the profoundly deep principle-reality of this Prajñāpāramitā. These are the sentence-meanings of the samādhis of the seventeen great bodhisattvas. In order to cause those who can uphold them to swiftly reach bodhi, he expounded this great maṇḍala of the seventeen holy positions. The secret words spoken by the Tathāgata and the great beings: by cultivating in accordance with these, one swiftly accomplishes. What are the seventeen holy ones?

The first is the Great Bliss Amoghavajra Samādhi True-Reality Vajra Bodhisattva. He represents the Samantabhadra-body of all the Buddhas, pervading the vessel-world and the sentient-being-world. Because of his boundless sovereign freedom, because principle is ever quiescent in essence, neither false nor destructible, he bears this name. In his left hand he holds a vajra-bell, which is the meaning of delight. Placed at the left of the waist, it represents the great self. In his right hand he holds a five-pronged vajra, which is the meaning of the five wisdoms. The fist is turned outward, indicating sentient beings. In the maṇḍala, he occupies the central position and unifies all the assemblies' aspects. Apart from him, there are sixteen positions, which are the direct path to right awakening.

The second is Mano-janma Vajra Bodhisattva (Mind-Born). With the arrow of great compassionate desire, he destroys the minds of the two vehicles. Therefore, he holds this arrow and manifests the equally illusory wisdom-body of desire-free union.

The third is Kelikila Vajra Bodhisattva. In the language of the central country, his name means "Touch." Because he does not abandon sentient beings and must cause them to be liberated, and to illuminate that the nature of touch is itself bodhi, he abides in the embracing posture and manifests the equally illusory wisdom-body of pure touch.

The fourth is Compassion Vajra Bodhisattva. Out of compassion, with the rope of loving attachment, he universally binds all sentient beings. Until they reach bodhi, he will never let go. He is also like the great makara fish that swallows everything it encounters; once entered into its mouth, there is no escape. Therefore he holds this makara-fish banner and manifests the equally illusory wisdom-body of love-bondage-and-release.

The fifth is Vajra-Māna Bodhisattva (Vajra Pride). With unsurpassable wisdom, he causes all sentient beings to realize the body of Vairocana Tathāgata, gaining sovereign freedom in both worldly and supramundane matters. Therefore he abides in a posture of proud dignity and manifests the equally illusory wisdom-body of self-and-non-self.

The sixth is Vajra-Dṛṣṭi Bodhisattva (Vajra Vision). With the eye of quiescent-illumination great wisdom, in the defiled realm, the pure land, and even in ultimate truth and conventional truth, he sees only the truth of the supreme meaning of all dharmas, without scattering and without movement. Therefore he holds the seal of the mind-born and manifests the body of samādhi.

The seventh is Vajra-Rati Bodhisattva (Vajra Delight). Regarding the sense-object of the body, he attains delightful purity. In birth-and-death and liberation, he neither loathes nor clings. Therefore he holds the seal of Touch Vajra and manifests the body of samādhi.

The eighth is Vajra-Rāga Bodhisattva (Vajra Desire). Through desire itself, he attains purity. Therefore he can use desire to accumulate all merit, wisdom, and swiftly realize bodhi. Because he abides in the nature of desire-attachment, he holds the seal of Compassion and manifests the body of samādhi.

The ninth is Vajra-Īśvara Bodhisattva (Vajra Sovereignty). He enters and exits the three realms with sovereign freedom and without fear. In birth-and-death and nirvāṇa, he obtains the great-self body. Therefore he abides in the Vajra-Pride posture and manifests the body of samādhi.

The tenth is Vajra-Vasanta Bodhisattva (Vajra Spring). He can use the flowers of bodhi-awakening to produce cloud-oceans of offerings. Also, through skillful means, he bestows them upon sentient beings as merit-benefit. Because flowers are the characteristic of spring, he is named accordingly. Therefore he holds flowers as his seal-implement.

The eleventh is Vajra-Megha Bodhisattva (Vajra Cloud). He can use the Dharma-moisture compassion-cloud to nourish sentient beings. Also, through skillful means, he bestows body and mind, transforming the foul unwholesomeness of beginningless ignorance into immeasurable offering-clouds of incense. Because censer-smoke resembles clouds, he is named accordingly. Therefore he holds a censer as his seal-implement.

The twelfth is Vajra-Śarad Bodhisattva (Vajra Autumn). He constantly uses the lamp of wisdom to shatter all darkness. Also, through skillful means, he bestows upon sentient beings immeasurable cloud-oceans of radiant offerings. Because the clear and refreshing quality of space and form is nowhere more apt than in autumn, and to represent the essence of wisdom-light, he is named accordingly. Therefore he holds a lamp as his seal-implement.

The thirteenth is Vajra-Tuṣāra Bodhisattva (Vajra Frost-and-Snow). He can use the five undefiled skandha-fragrance to anoint the mind-essence of sentient beings, extinguishing the foul heat of afflictions, accomplishing the fragrance of the five-part dharma-body. Also, through skillful means, he bestows upon sentient beings cloud-oceans of unguent-offerings. Because sandalwood unguent that dispels all toxic heat resembles frost and snow, he is named accordingly. Therefore he holds unguent as his seal-implement.

The fourteenth is Vajra-Rūpa Bodhisattva (Vajra Form). With the wisdom of purity of form, in the pure wondrous realm he manifests the enjoyment-body, and in the defiled realm he manifests the transformation-body, performing the work of summoning. Therefore he holds a hook as his seal-implement.

The fifteenth is Vajra-Śabda Bodhisattva (Vajra Sound). With the wisdom of purity of sound, he can express sixty-four kinds of Brahma-voice, pervading the dharma-realm, performing the work of leading in. Therefore he holds a lasso as his seal-implement.

The sixteenth is Vajra-Gandha Bodhisattva (Vajra Fragrance). With the wisdom of purity of fragrance, he emits the natural renown-fragrance of the Vajradhātu, entering all scattered and agitated minds, performing the work of halting and retaining. Therefore he holds a chain as his seal-implement.

The seventeenth is Vajra-Rasa Bodhisattva (Vajra Taste). With the wisdom of purity of taste, he upholds the unsurpassed Dharma-flavor of the yoga-samādhi, performing the work of joy. Therefore he holds a bell as his seal-implement.

The seventeen pure samādhi-wisdoms of these great bodhisattvas, if expounded broadly according to the text, have immeasurable names, meanings, essences, functions, principles, phenomena, and gates of accomplishment and realization. Here, only a rough outline of the main headings has been given. Extracted from the thirteenth assembly of the Vajraśekhara Sūtra, the Great Samaya True-Reality Yoga, a summary of the main points.
