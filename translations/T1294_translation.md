# 施八方天儀則
## Rules for Offerings to the Devas of the Eight Directions

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1294

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown.

---

Whenever making offerings to the devas of the eight directions, first prepare the food to be offered. Cook a thick porridge of five flavors, not thin. The five flavors are: non-glutinous rice, mung beans, sesame, cow's milk, and ghee. Measure amounts according to judgment, making it palatable. Add a small amount of salt. One must take great care that it is ritually pure. Place it in a porcelain basin. Prepare twelve small wax candles, ground white sandalwood paste, seasonal flowers, and clean water. At the place of offering, in each of the eight directions, smear a small altar. Upon each small altar first place a clean bowl. The porridge, fragrant paste, and flowers are each carried by an attendant who follows behind the mantra-reciter. The mantra-reciter should first invoke with utmost sincerity the deva-kings of the eight directions together with their retinues, requesting them to come to the ritual site and take their respective positions.

Beginning with the east, recite the mantra of Śakra. First sprinkle a little clean water, then apply the fragrant paste, then offer seasonal flowers, then porridge, then insert a candle into the porridge, then present burning incense three times, chanting the mantra while requesting protection and beseeching the deva-king's joy and the blessing of the ritual patron. Next, proceed to the southeast, the quarter of Agni Deva, in the same manner, reciting each deva's own mantra and making offerings in sequence. Next the south, Yama Deva. Next the southwest, Rākṣasa Deva. Next the west, Varuṇa Deva. Next the northwest, Vāyu Deva. Next the north, Vaiśravaṇa Deva. Next the northeast, Īśāna Deva. At the end, retain a small amount of the remaining porridge, add a generous amount of clean water, and place in it small cakes, fruit, fragrant paste, and seasonal flowers. When everything is assembled, recite the amṛta mantra, empower it with the hand-mudrā, and have someone carry it to a clean place and pour it upon the ground as an offering to the various malevolent ghosts and spirits, praying that they protect the patron and be pleased. Do not look back, but proceed straight to return.

The mantra of Śakra Deva of the east:

All mantras should be prefixed with "namaḥ."

*Oṃ indrāya svāhā.*

The mantra of Agni Deva of the southeast:

*Oṃ agnaye svāhā.*

The mantra of Yama Deva of the south:

*Oṃ yamāya svāhā.*

The mantra of Rākṣasa Deva of the southwest:

*Oṃ nairṛtaye svāhā.*

The mantra of Varuṇa Deva of the west:

*Oṃ varuṇāya svāhā.*

The mantra of Vāyu Deva of the northwest:

*Oṃ vāyave svāhā.*

The mantra of Vaiśravaṇa Deva of the north:

*Oṃ vaiśravaṇāya svāhā.*

The mantra of Īśāna Deva of the northeast:

*Oṃ īśānāya svāhā.*

At each position, recite the respective mantra while clearly recording the order of items offered. First water, then fragrant paste, then flowers, then porridge, then a candle, then burning incense. When all positions have been completed, recite the Sumeru-king mantra and empower it three times seven or five times seven repetitions. The mantra:

*Oṃ sumerū-kalpāya oṃ suru suru prasuru prasuru svāhā.*

When all offerings are finished, wash one's hands and make further offerings. Dispatch and respectfully send off the holy assembly from the ritual site, collect the offering items, and then coat the altar.

The mantra of Brahmā Deva above:

*Oṃ brahmaṇe svāhā.*

The mantra of Pṛthivī Deva below:

*Oṃ pṛthivīye svāhā.*

The water and other six items should each be carried by one person, following behind the officiant. When an offering at one position is finished, that person stands at the next unoffered position. In this manner proceed in sequence, and take care. The candles must be carefully watched; they must not be allowed to go out before all offerings are complete.

The mantra of Sūrya Deva:

*Oṃ sahasra-kiraṇa hūṃ ture svāhā.*

The mantra of Candra Deva:

*Oṃ soma-prabhā hūṃ svāhā.*

The amṛta mantra:

*Namo surūpāya tathāgatāya tadyathā oṃ suru prasuru suru svāhā.*

The mantra for offering food and drink:

*Namaḥ sarva-tathāgatāvalokite oṃ bhara bhara saṃbhara saṃbhara hūṃ.*

If a person seeks all manner of auspicious affairs and on the day when the established ritual site's practice is completed, during the first watch of the night, one should first make offerings to the deva-kings of the eight directions as well as Brahmā Deva, Pṛthivī Deva, the constellations, luminaries, ghosts, and spirits. Only afterward should one dispatch the holy assembly and dismantle the ritual site. Those who perform the method in this way will please all the holy and wise ones, who will bestow blessings, and one will obtain auspiciousness.
