# 一髻文殊師利童子陀羅尼念誦儀軌
## Recitation Ritual for the Dhāraṇī of Ekajāṭa Mañjuśrī Youth

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1183

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Vajramaṇāyuṣa (金剛福壽).

---

[Dhāraṇī]

*Namo mañjuśrīye kumāra-bhūtāya tadyathā oṃ śrī-galave mahā-mukha-galave ala-mābhi-galave sama-samanta-galave svāhā*

At that time Mañjuśrī, the Dharma Prince, had spoken this dhāraṇī in the great assembly, and the tathāgatas of the ten directions, the devas, nāgas, eight classes, and the entire great assembly all praised together: "Excellent, excellent! Mañjuśrī, Youth, Bodhisattva Mahāsattva! By the power of your great compassionate vow, in the degenerate age, out of pity for beings of meager merit, you have skillfully proclaimed this inconceivable wish-fulfilling jewel, this wondrous dharma treasury, to benefit beings and create inexhaustible merit."

At that time Mañjuśrī addressed the great assembly: "I received this dhāraṇī from a past buddha. Each time a declining kalpa arrives and the merit of beings is exhausted, I proclaim it, causing good men and good women of the degenerate age, bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, and upāsikās who seek the path of bodhi to guide all beings and swiftly bring to accomplishment all good deeds, crossing over the great ocean of suffering of birth and death. Therefore I urge them to receive and uphold these divine and wondrous phrases.

"If good men or good women practice with vigor day and night, constantly reciting this dhāraṇī without forgetting, or even merely praise its inconceivable power and encourage others to faith, receive it sincerely, uphold and recite this dhāraṇī, and practice deeds of benefit: such good men and good women will have all the evil karmic deeds accumulated since beginningless time, including the five heinous offenses, entirely extinguished. They will never again sink into the great ocean of birth and death, and will have no more fear of the three evil destinies.

"Wherever they are reborn, they will attain knowledge of past lives, their six faculties will be purified, they will be endowed with all excellent marks, wonderfully beautiful with blazing majesty. They will obtain whatever they seek. All mantras previously upheld will all be accomplished, and whatever they undertake will be surpassingly excellent.

"If one who receives and upholds this dhāraṇī has previously violated the precepts and their three kinds of karma are deficient, all will be purified, all sins from broken precepts will be extinguished, they will forever leave the evil destinies and always be reborn among humans and devas, their faculties will be gentle and pliant, their wisdom pure, they will gain mastery in all affairs, attain deep faith in the true dharma, and all who hear their words will believe and accept them.

"Among enemies they will always be victorious; all hostile parties will forever cease their evil intentions and be unable to cause harm. All devas and nāgas will constantly follow and protect them. They will naturally accomplish vast and great merit, and all who see them will submit.

"Because my compassionate vow is deep and vast, with inexhaustible merit and wisdom, I benefit all sentient beings; they are protected and mindful by the tathāgatas and great bodhisattvas of the ten directions, and through the empowerment and vow-power of all the holy ones, every kind of liberation is accomplished.

"I shall now further explain the method for painting the image, constructing the ritual space, making offerings, and performing the accomplishment practice for one who receives and upholds this dhāraṇī.

"The practitioner seeking accomplishment should first choose an auspicious day according to the lunar mansions and stellar conjunctions. The painter should receive the eight precepts and maintain purity. On white felt or silk, paint Ekajāṭa Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva in the form of a youth, golden in color, sitting in half-lotus upon a thousand-petaled white lotus. The left hand holds a blue lotus, upon which rests a wish-fulfilling jewel. The right hand faces outward with the five fingers forming the wish-granting mudrā. He wears a gentle smile, adorned with various pearl garlands, a crown, and necklaces.

"When the painting is complete, in a pure room, apply plaster to make a square altar. From the inside outward, arrange the positions of the three precincts and place the seventeen honored ones, exactly as described in the Five-Syllable Wheel Yoga method. One should offer excellent, fragrant cakes and fruits with utmost sincerity.

"Place the image on the eastern side of the altar. The practitioner sits on the western side, facing east toward the image. Begin the recitation on the eighth day of the bright fortnight, reciting the foregoing mantra eight hundred or one thousand times each day. Then sleep before the image, single-mindedly thinking of Mañjuśrī as one falls asleep. In a dream, one will certainly see Mañjuśrī in the form of a small child. If one sees this sign, know that one has received his empowerment; all vows and aspirations will be fulfilled, all that one seeks will be obtained, and all words and teachings one speaks will be believed and accepted by others.

"Another method: take mṛttikā medicine, empower it with the foregoing mantra eight thousand times, then hold it in the mouth. If one goes to the king's palace or to the offices of the hundred officials, one's words will be heeded and all requests granted; one's teachings will be believed, obeyed, and never defied.

"Another method: take tejovati medicine, empower it with the foregoing mantra eight thousand times, and place it in a bowl, then go begging for alms while holding the bowl. Whatever food, drink, clothing, or even gold, silver, and the seven treasures one needs will all be obtained. All people will love and revere one and compete to make offerings, and in all disputes one will be victorious.

"Another method: on the eighth day of the bright fortnight, before this image of Mañjuśrī, light lamps, eat the three white foods, and offer white foods, various white flowers, and white sandalwood incense. Recite the mantra eight thousand times. When the recitations are complete, lie down and sleep before the image. Mañjuśrī will manifest his pure and wondrous body, all wishes will be fulfilled, and each day he will bestow one thousand gold coins. One must not accumulate them; all should be used to make offerings to the Three Treasures and cultivate merit.

"Each day recite eight hundred times. According to one's ability, make offerings without interruption.

"Another method: take a silver bowl, empower it one hundred and eight times, or eight hundred times, or twenty-one times. Place it before the image of Mañjuśrī on a white cloth, and cover it with another white cloth. After one night, in the morning reach into the bowl, and one will obtain eight hundred gold coins.

"All the above methods should be performed in secrecy. Not even one person may learn of it, except for a close companion who shares one's practice and aspiration. One must never reveal it to outsiders, or the power of empowerment will immediately be lost. One must exercise the utmost discretion. If one follows the teaching with care, the daily supply of gold coins will continue without cease.

"All the above methods should not be practiced simultaneously. Choose whichever one wishes to accomplish. One must first purify body and mind, frequently drink the five pure products of the cow, give rise to the mind of compassion, and practice with vigor day and night without any lapse in accordance with the teaching. One will certainly attain the supreme siddhi in this very life."

The heart mantra:

*Oṃ smana-śrī hūṃ svāhā*

The following-heart mantra:

*Oṃ aśa-maṇi hūṃ svāhā*

The altar-boundary mantra:

*Oṃ deśa-māni hūṃ svāhā*

The four-direction boundary mantra:

*Oṃ samānanta-kimāni hūṃ svāhā*

"Empower water with this mantra twenty-one times and sprinkle it on the four sides; this establishes the boundary.

"If one constantly recites this mantra before Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva, eight hundred times daily without interruption, one will daily receive five hundred gold coins. These must be offered in service to the Three Treasures; one may use only one's own share for sustenance. One must not seek it for purposeless or teaching-contrary uses, and must never accumulate it.

"One should each day offer various cakes and fruits to Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva, replacing them at the proper times so they do not become impure. One's own garments must be kept extremely clean, with frequent bathing to prevent uncleanliness.

"If one encounters an unorthodox-appearing śramaṇa or brāhmaṇa, or one whose complexion is haggard, whose bearing is undignified, or whose form is ugly, one must never laugh at, scorn, or disparage them. The great holy Mañjuśrī manifests various forms and unusual appearances to test and discipline the minds of practitioners, wishing them always to give rise to compassion and universal equanimity, to hold all sentient beings in reverence without discrimination, without attachment, without partiality, vast and unobstructed, always regarding all as future buddhas.

"If a practitioner can thus follow the teaching, abide in non-discrimination, and practice with vigor, Mañjuśrī the Great Holy One will swiftly fulfill all their aspirations, and the transformations within the bowl will be endlessly abundant.

"Another method: take calamus root, empower it with the body mantra eight thousand times, and place it in the bowl. Take this bowl and enter the city to beg; whatever clothing one needs will all be obtained as desired.

"Another method: take three kinds of incense (benzoin, white sandalwood, and storax), and always use them for offerings. At all places, always burn this incense without interruption, and whatever one seeks will be swiftly fulfilled.

"Another method: take five-colored thread one arm-length long. Recite the mantra to empower it one hundred and eight times, tying one knot, until there are twenty-one knots. Place the knotted cord in the bowl before the image, and make offerings according to one's ability. Each day Mañjuśrī will produce twenty-one liang of the finest gold within the bowl. This gold must never be stored or accumulated; it should be used for merit and offerings to the Three Treasures, and it will never be exhausted. If one hoards and accumulates it, it will immediately vanish and never appear again.

"Another method: there are medicines called suśapatra (also called saśapa) and mahāvidā. If there is a great wind, heavy rain, or prolonged downpour without clearing: take both medicines, place them together, and empower them with the body mantra eight thousand times. Cast them into the sky, and the wind and rain will cease.

"If there is scorching drought without rain, take suśapatra, empower it eight thousand times, and scatter it into water where dragons dwell. Great rain will immediately fall.

"Another method: if one takes the empowered medicine and scatters it upon a great battlefield, all swords, staffs, bows, and arrows will become useless, and even if used, they will certainly cause no injury.

"If scattered amid grass, trees, or any crop fields, no insects will be able to damage them. If scattered in stone caves, bamboo groves, or among rice and other grain fields, the harvest will multiply and be taken without end.

"If scattered upon one's own head, one will be victorious in all disputes. If one enters the king's palace or the homes of great ministers and officials, all will obey and show reverence.

"If scattered among merchants and traders, their goods will not be stolen, and no thief will be able to plunder them.

"If cast in the four directions, all ghosts, spirits, yakṣas, rākṣasas, and demons will be unable to cause harm.

"If scattered on a fire, the fire will extinguish itself and be unable to burn anything. If scattered in water, the water will stop flowing and become still.

"There are many more such methods that cannot be fully described. Once the foregoing method is accomplished and proven effective, whatever one subsequently wishes to do will all succeed, without needing to describe every case. There is absolutely no doubt about this.

"This mantra possesses great awesome virtue, radiance, and inconceivable power that is difficult to fathom. Anyone who recites it to accomplishment will succeed, unless they lack sincerity."

Colophon: This sūtra was first [brought] on the eighteenth day of the ninth month of the first year of Shangyuan [674 CE] by the brāhmaṇa Vajramaṇāyuṣa (in Tang, Jingang Fushou), at the Kaiyuan Temple in Qinzhou. In the seventeenth year of Zhenyuan [801 CE], third month, the translation-monk again passed through Zhongzhou within the eight gorges, at the residence of Commissioner Sa. There he saw an Indian monk's sealed and verified record for inclusion in the canon.

Mañjuśrī Compassion-Protection True Word:

*Oṃ buddha-maitrīya kṣa haṃ*

Mañjuśrī's Wrathful Dhāraṇī:

*Oṃ vajra-tīkṣṇa-krodha china china hūṃ phaṭ*

Use these mantras to protect the body, expel [evil], establish boundaries, and purify all incense, flowers, and offering articles. May all beings universally encounter this teaching, know Mañjuśrī, swiftly realize the merits of prajñā-wisdom, and quickly accomplish the unsurpassed, supreme honored one.

Mañjuśrī's Sore-Healing Mantra:

*Namaḥ sarva-buddhānāṃ ajitāya nabhutarūbi oṃ rī svāhā*

Heart Mantra for Malaria from the Seven Buddhas Medicine Master Sūtra:

*Tadyathā aghu-mā-trā-ghu-māmā-ghu-ku-re-ha-hu-he-mara-mara-mara-śū-śu-ni-pura svāhā*

After reverently reciting the names of the Seven Buddhas and the Twelve Spirit Generals, write this mantra and tie it behind the elbow. When recovered, place it in a clean location.

Goodly-Named Supreme Auspicious King Tathāgata. Jewel-Moon Wisdom-Adornment Radiant Sound Sovereign King Tathāgata. Golden-Hued Jewel-Light Wondrous-Practice Accomplishment Tathāgata. Sorrowless Supreme Auspicious King Tathāgata. Dharma-Ocean Supreme-Wisdom Sporting-in-Supernatural-Powers Tathāgata. Medicine Master Lapis Lazuli Light Tathāgata.

Yakṣa Mantra:

*Oṃ kuvera svāhā*
