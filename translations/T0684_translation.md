# 佛說父母恩難報經
## Sūtra Spoken by the Buddha on the Difficulty of Repaying One's Parents' Kindness

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 684

Translated from the Chinese. Eastern Han, translated by An Shigao

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Bhagavān was dwelling in Śrāvastī, in the Jetavana Grove of Anāthapiṇḍada.

At that time the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣus: "Parents bring great benefit to their children. They nourish and raise them with milk and food, tending to their upbringing in due season, until the four great elements are fully formed. Even if a son were to carry his father on his right shoulder and his mother on his left for a thousand years, even if they were to relieve themselves upon his back, and yet he harbored no resentment toward his parents, still such a son would not be sufficient to repay the kindness of his parents. But if his parents lack faith, he teaches them to have faith, instructs them, and brings them to a place of peace and security; if they lack precepts, he gives them precepts, instructs them, and brings them to a place of peace and security; if they have not heard the teachings, he causes them to hear, instructs them, and brings them to a place of peace and security; if they are miserly and greedy, he teaches them to delight in giving, encourages and instructs them, and brings them to a place of peace and security; if they lack wisdom, he teaches them to become wise, encourages and instructs them, and brings them to a place of peace and security. In this way, [he leads them to] have faith in the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Perfectly and Completely Enlightened One, the One Accomplished in Knowledge and Conduct, the Sugata, the Knower of the World, the Unsurpassed One, the Trainer of Those to Be Tamed, the Teacher of Gods and Humans, called Buddha, the World-Honored One; and teaches them to have faith in the Dharma, instructs them, and brings them to a place of peace and security. The Dharma is exceedingly profound; one attains its fruit in this very body, and its meaning and flavor are very deep. In this way, the wise person who clearly comprehends and penetrates this practice teaches them to have faith in the Holy Saṃgha. The Holy Saṃgha of the Tathāgata is supremely pure; their conduct is straight and without deviation; they are always in harmony; they are accomplished in the Dharma in every respect: accomplished in morality, accomplished in samādhi, accomplished in wisdom, accomplished in liberation, accomplished in the knowledge and vision of liberation. The so-called Holy Saṃgha of four pairs and eight types: this is the Holy Saṃgha of the Tathāgata, most honored and most noble, worthy of veneration and reverence, the unsurpassed field of merit in this world. In this way, sons should teach their parents to practice loving-kindness. Bhikṣus! There are two kinds of sons: sons born of the body, and sons nurtured by care. These, bhikṣus, are the two kinds of sons. Therefore, bhikṣus should train as born sons, with the flavor of the Dharma coming forth from their mouths. Thus should all bhikṣus train."

At that time, having heard what the Buddha had said, the bhikṣus rejoiced and reverently put it into practice.
