# 大聖天歡喜雙身毘那夜迦法
## Method of the Great Holy Celestial Joyful Double-Bodied Vināyaka

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1266

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

The body spell:

*Namo vināyaka-śya hasti-mukha-śya tadyathā oṃ nāyika-nāyika vinā-yika-vinā-yika trapa-ritrā-yika śaṅkha-hasti-śaṅkha-kā-citta-śānti-kara svāhā*

The heart spell:

*Oṃ gīḥ jah*

The heart-within-heart spell:

*Oṃ jah-jah hūṃ svāhā*

If one wishes to perform this celestial method, one must first make an image. White pewter, copper, wood, or flower-wood may be used; casting or carving are both acceptable. The image should depict the husband and wife as two bodies in mutual embrace, standing. Each should be five inches or seven inches tall, both having elephant heads and human bodies. The price for making the image must not be disputed.

When the image is complete, on the first day of the bright fortnight, in a pure room, plaster a round altar with pure cow dung, of whatever size desired. Take one *sheng* of fine, clear sesame oil and place it in a clean copper vessel. Recite the above body-spell over the oil one hundred and eight times, then warm the oil. Place the image in the oil and install it within the altar. Using a clean copper spoon or ladle, pour the oil over the crowns and bodies of both images one hundred and eight times. During the day, pour the oil seven times: four times at dawn, three times at noon, making seven in all. From the following day onward, recite the spell over the previous oil and pour it over the image again. Perform this method continuously for seven days, and whatever one's heart wishes will be fulfilled as desired. During the pouring of the oil, repeatedly make aspirations. Further, mix ghee and honey with roasted flour to make balls, and offer radish root, one cup of wine, joyful-balls, seasonal fresh flowers and fruits. Each day replace these with fresh offerings. All wholesome undertakings will be accomplished as desired, and all calamities will be completely eliminated. The food that has been offered must be consumed by the practitioner; only then will one receive its potency.

**Invitation Mudrā**

Hook both little fingers and both ring fingers inward. Both middle fingers stand upright and cross. Both index fingers each stand upright, placed against the middle fingers. Both thumbs likewise stand upright, placed beside the index fingers; move the thumbs back and forth.

The spell:

*Oṃ pakarā-cuṇa-devātāya*

Recite seven times.

The praise spell:

*Oṃ gaṇapati-śānti-svasti mahā-gaṇapati svāhā*

The dispatch spell:

*Oṃ pakarā-cuṇa-devātāya svāhā*

Recite seven times.

The body-protection spell with water:

*Oṃ kili-kili vajra hūṃ phaṭ*

When wishing to perform the method, first recite the above spell over water seven times and use it to rinse the mouth. Then take more water, recite the spell seven times, and sprinkle it over the body with a willow branch. This constitutes body-protection. Then enter the recitation room where the method is performed.

The bathing spell:

*Oṃ anava-jari bha-parī-ṇa hah*

Recite the above spell over water seven times and use it for bathing.

## Method for Pacifying Vināyaka

To invite all celestial beings, form the Indra-giving mudrā. Cross both little fingers and ring fingers within the palms. Both middle fingers stand upright with fingertips touching. Both index fingers each rest on the back of the middle finger at the halfway point below the first joint. Move the thumbs back and forth. The spell:

*Oṃ śatru-pa-parama-dhanīye svāhā*

Recite seven times. Whenever a spell-practitioner is frightened in dreams, or sees animals and evil visions, one should know that the Vināyaka King is angry. Upon waking from the dream, immediately express shame and contrition, asking him not to be angry, and promise that the next day there will be food and drink as recompense.

Smear the ground with water to make a round water-altar of two cubits, or as large as a platter will also suffice. Take five rice-cakes, three radish roots roasted until cooked, and if flowers are available, add flowers. Also burn white rosin, frankincense, and similar incense and install them within the altar. The practitioner sits west of the altar facing east, and recites the Maheśvara spell one hundred and eight times. Then say aloud: "I express my contrition. Please depart in peace." Having spoken thus, gather all the miscellaneous items from the altar into a tray, carry them out the door, and discard them to the west, or to the northwest. Say aloud: "*Sarva-yakṣa-rāja ai-eṣa-svāhā sapa-jā-gaccha*." Having spoken thus, discard them and return.

The Maheśvara spell:

*Oṃ vidhara-sani para-mārī-danī chinda-nī chinda-nī bhinda-nī bhinda-nī svāhā*

Recite the spell one hundred and eight times. The mind will then become joyful. Not only in dreams but whenever one senses māra-disturbances, performing the preceding method will certainly bring relief.

## Method for Making the Image

The image should be standing upright, with an elephant head and human body. The left tusk protrudes and the right tusk is broken. The face turns slightly to the left, with the trunk curving outward. It has six arms. The upper left hand holds a sword; the next hand holds a fruit platter; the lower hand holds a wheel. The upper right hand holds a club; the next hand holds a lasso; the lower hand holds a tusk.

The price for making this image must not be disputed; the full amount must be paid. Casting, carving, or painting are all acceptable.

This great holy and supremely secret method is rare in the human world. It should truly not be transmitted carelessly. One should be admonished and exercise caution.
