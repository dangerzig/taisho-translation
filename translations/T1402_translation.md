# 消除一切閃電障難隨求如意陀羅尼經
## Sūtra on the Wish-Fulfilling Dhāraṇī for Eliminating All Lightning Obstacles and Difficulties

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1402

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dānapāla (施護).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the kingdom of Śrāvastī, in a monastery. At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the Venerable Ānanda: "Listen carefully. There is a Dhāraṇī for Eliminating All Lightning Obstacles and Difficulties, a Wish-Fulfilling Dhāraṇī Sūtra, which was proclaimed by the tathāgatas, arhats, and perfectly awakened ones of the past. I now, with constant compassion, also proclaim it, wishing to benefit all living beings in the heavens and human realm and bring them happiness."

Ānanda said: "So be it, World-Honored One! I am delighted to hear it."

The World-Honored One said: "Ānanda! In the east there is a thunder-lightning named Agha. In the south there is a thunder-lightning named Śatiru. In the west there is a thunder-lightning named 'Emitting Light.' In the north there is a thunder-lightning named Saunamanī. Ānanda! If good men or good women know these thunder-lightning names, they will have no fear from any lightning in the directions where they dwell. If there are such lightning flashes at any place of dwelling, one need only write this mantra and place it there, and all lightning will be unable to strike."

At that time, the World-Honored One proclaimed these *naramiṇa* protective mantra phrases:

*tadyathā nimi nimi nimiṃ-dhāri trailokya-lokinī śūla-pāṇinī rakṣa rakṣaṃ sarva-viniyoga-bhaye bhiyāt svāhā*

At that time, the Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Avalokiteśvara, together with his retinue, had all come to the assembly and was seated to one side. The Bodhisattva Avalokiteśvara then rose from his seat and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I too shall proclaim this true Dharma."

The *naramiṇa* protective mantra phrases:

*tadyathā vināśa pratyādhikā pratyāmitrī śuddhi-mukte vimale prabhāsa-kari aṇḍāri paṇḍāri śvete paṇḍaravāsinī māte kārālā kapī-la-piṅgalā kṛṣṇā-[ādi] mukṣi rakṣi rakṣiṃ sarva-kāla-mṛtyu-[abhi]yu-bhaye bhiyāt māṃ paśa-mantu sarva-kāla-mṛtyuvaṃ ārya-avalokiteśvara-tejasā svāhā*

At that time, the Lord of Secrets, Vajrapāṇi, approached the Buddha, placed his palms together in reverence, and addressed the World-Honored One: "I too shall proclaim dhāraṇī mantra phrases for this true Dharma."

*tadyathā muni muni muni mate muni-mate su-mate mahā-mate ha ha ha ha [mātṛ]-nāṃ-sthite [prapa]-vajra-pāṇi rahaṃ-[nir]dha svāhā*

"World-Honored One! These phrases of mine are called 'Invincible.' If a person remembers, receives, upholds, writes, and makes offerings to them, I will always protect them, and all terrors and all untimely deaths will be resolved and dispelled."

At that time, Mahābrahmā, Lord of the Sahā World, addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I too shall proclaim dhāraṇī mantra phrases for this true Dharma, wishing to benefit and bring happiness to all sentient beings." He then proclaimed the dhāraṇī:

*tadyathā hili mili cili svāhā brahma-pūri mahā-brahmaṇī brahma-garbhi puṣpa-[śās]tāri svāhā*

"World-Honored One! This dhāraṇī of mine is called the Staff of Brahmā. It can rescue and protect against all untimely death, granting long life, and all karmic offenses will be extinguished."

At that time, Indra, Lord of the Devas, approached the World-Honored One, placed his palms together in reverence, and addressed the World-Honored One: "I too shall now proclaim dhāraṇī mantra phrases for this true Dharma."

*tadyathā buddha-[maṇḍa]ni māninī [gau]rī gandhāri caṇḍāle [mā]ḍhaṃge puṣpākṣi [sāra]-brate hīnā-madhya-[uṃga] dhāraṇī maṇḍule [nī]ke cakara [vā]ki śavari śavari svāhā*

"World-Honored One! These phrases of mine are called the Vajra Seat. All terrors and all untimely deaths are obstructed and dispelled without hindrance."

At that time, the Four Heavenly Kings, Dhṛtarāṣṭra, Virūḍhaka, Virūpākṣa, and Vaiśravaṇa, placed their palms together toward the Buddha and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! We too shall proclaim dhāraṇī phrases for this true Dharma."

*tadyathā puṣpe su-puṣpe [durba]-parihāri ārya-praśaste śāntini [nir]-mukte maṅgale [ye] hiraṇya-garbhi stute [sthā]vite svāhā*

"World-Honored One! As we four great heavenly kings have proclaimed above, these mantra phrases are called 'Fearlessness.' Amid all terrors they provide protection, and all lifespans will be free from untimely death."

At that time, the Nāga King Sāgara, the Nāga King Manasvin, the Nāga King Vidyutprabhā, the Nāga King Anavatapta, the Nāga King Vidyujjihva, the Nāga King Śataprabhā, and others also approached the World-Honored One, placed their palms together in reverence, and stood to one side.

The World-Honored One said: "Wherever these wish-fulfilling phrases are present, they protect against all lightning obstacles and difficulties, and can also rescue and protect against all untimely death, remove all poisons, and destroy all kaṭapūtanas, vetāḍas, and those who perform inauspicious acts. Listen carefully and generate a mind of compassion. Among you of the nāga destiny, each gives rise to anger, arrogance, and pride. You should subdue these." The nāgas said: "How should we subdue them?" The World-Honored One answered: "I have a dhāraṇī that will cause the arrogance and pride in the minds of you nāgas to cease." The mantra:

*tadyathā ajari māmari amṛte akṣaye avyaye puṇya-pariyāye sarva-pāpa-praśamanī svāhā ārya-puṇḍa-[rau]pakiye svāhā indra-[śa]nīye svāhā megha-[śa]nīye svāhā*

The World-Honored One said: "If any man or woman receives, upholds, reads, recites, writes, and makes offerings to these mantra phrases, all nāga spirits and lightning terrors will be unable to cause harm. Neither root poisons nor compounded poisons nor any manufactured poisons will be able to cause harm, for our sake and for the sake of all sentient beings."

At that time, Mahābrahmā, Indra Lord of the Devas, the four world-protecting Heavenly Kings, the nāga kings, and the others praised him: "Excellent! Excellent! O Worthy One! Wishing to benefit and bring happiness to the multitude of living beings, you have well proclaimed these *naramiṇa* protective mantra phrases for this true Dharma. We single-mindedly receive and uphold them."

When the Buddha had finished proclaiming this sūtra, the entire assembly of devas, humans, asuras, gandharvas, and all the rest heard what the Buddha had proclaimed, rejoiced greatly, and faithfully received and practiced it.

---

*Translator's note: The dhāraṇīs in this sūtra appear to be a variant edition of the same text as Taishō No. 1403. The Sanskrit IAST reconstructions above follow the same identifications. For the dhāraṇī of the World-Honored One, identifiable terms include: nimi (measuring), dhāri (holding), trailokya (three worlds), lokinī (world-protectress), śūla-pāṇinī (trident-holder), rakṣa (protect), sarva (all), bhaye (in fear). For Avalokiteśvara's dhāraṇī: vināśa (destruction), śuddhi (purity), mukte (liberated), vimale (pure), prabhāsa (radiant), paṇḍaravāsinī (white-robed one), kārālā (fierce), kapila (tawny), piṅgalā (reddish), kṛṣṇā (dark), rakṣi (protect), mṛtyu (death), ārya-avalokiteśvara (noble Avalokiteśvara), tejas (radiance). For the remaining dhāraṇīs, see the notes to T1403. Uncertain reconstructions are indicated with brackets.*
