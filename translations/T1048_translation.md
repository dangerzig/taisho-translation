# 佛說大護明大陀羅尼經
## Great Protective Vidyā Great Dhāraṇī Sūtra Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1048

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmadeva (法天).

---

At that time the World-Honored One was staying in the village of Mṛtica. The World-Honored One told the venerable Ānanda: "Ānanda, I now wish to go to the city of Vaiśālī." The venerable Ānanda said: "So be it, World-Honored One. I now hear your command." Thereupon the World-Honored One and Ānanda went to the city of Vaiśālī. Upon arriving at the Āmrapālī Garden, the Buddha told Ānanda: "Ānanda, you should enter the city, choose an auspicious site, set up an Indra-pillar, stand with right mindfulness, and speak this Great Protective Vidyā Great Dhāraṇī along with its gāthās:

*Visarata visarata visarata visarata visarata*

> "'The past, present, and future
> Samyak-saṃbuddhas, with one accord,
> Cross over the world.
> All the buddhas, all the pratyekabuddhas,
> The arhats, and the śrāvakas,
> The power of all precepts, all words of truth,
> Down to Great Brahmā,
> Kāmeśvara,
> Śakra and all the devas,
> All the asuras,
> The asura messengers,
> Down to the bhūtas and the rest,
> All are in one accord.'

*Visarata visarata visarata visarata visarata*

> "'All the buddhas of past, present, and future,
> By their perfect and complete knowledge, ferry the world across. Know this.'

*Muñcata muñcata muñcata muñcata matiṣṭhatu iti viyupaśamitā niḥsarata niḥsarata*

"The Deva among Devas, honored by all the heavens, entered Vaiśālī. The World-Sovereign Brahmā, the heavenly lord Śakra, and the four world-protecting kings also entered. Innumerable thousands of celestial hosts, innumerable asura kings and asura hosts also entered. Down to innumerable thousands of bhūta hosts, all followed in, paying homage to the World-Honored One. Observing the disasters and difficulties that had arisen among all sentient beings, the epidemics that were spreading, he performed the ritual of accomplishment to swiftly cause these to retreat and scatter, bringing peace and happiness. The mantra says:

*Nirgacchata nirgacchata nirgacchata nirgacchata kṣipraṃ parāyata ya niyuktam aduṣṭacittanāśayatā yai maitracittanā paraṃ duḥkamāra kṣāṃ cānuvṛttikāṃ ati siddhiṣṭhatu mākaṃ pramukhato lokānukaṃpā kujanā payati sumu sumu sumu sumu rusumu rusumu rusumu sumu suru suru suru suru mumu mumu mumu mumu praviśati rusumu muru muru muru muru muru muru muru muru hūṃ miri miri miri miri miri miri muru miri muru miri muru miri muru miri muru miri muru miri muru miri muru miri muru miri muru miri muru miri muru miri muru miri muru miri riti muru riti riri riri riri riri miri miri miri miri miri miri tihasmi miri ti miri ri mimi kaṭa kara kaṭa kaṃ kara kara kaca kaṃ kara kaṃ kara kaṃ kara kaṃ kara kaṃ kara kaṃ kara kaṃ karu kariti kurīśe kaṃ kari kaṃ kari śeti riti riri riri riri phāsari puri puri puri puri puri puri purānānā thariri puri bhari pusata anāthaś cari pra nirgacchata parāyata rī praṃ parāyata*

"'The Tathāgata, Samyak-saṃbuddha, has entered this place of disaster-removal for all sentient beings in the world, and with great compassion, great joy, great equanimity, great loving-kindness, speaks this mantra of accomplishment and these gāthās. All the sagely assembly, down to the bhūta hosts, knowing this dharma to be the most supreme in all the world: if furthermore, without sparing body or life, for the sake of all beings, they eliminate epidemics, shatter disasters, increase good minds, and bring peace and happiness; if for the sake of the world they teach the path of liberation, teach the dharma of reverence, bring peace and happiness to all, and act as teachers who ferry the world across; if for all the world they give rise to minds of loving-kindness, establishing all beings as if they were their own infants, always following all sentient beings, like lamplight that increases its illumination, causing those in saṃsāra to find the true path; if the wisdom of all dharmas is never lacking, and their speech is pure, giving joy to all; then in each life they will be born into noble and wealthy families, attaining great fearlessness and accomplishment of meritorious karma. The earth of their birthplace will universally quake, and all beings will rejoice. At the site of their awakening the earth quakes in six ways, and Māra and Māra's retinue are all filled with distress, knowing that the Muni will turn the dharma-wheel and teach the Four Noble Truths, using the dharma of training to subdue non-Buddhist practitioners and all sentient beings, who shall certainly become buddhas. He brings peace and happiness to humans, devas, and the heavenly lord Śakra, and even to all the bhūta hosts and the rest. At all times he enables them to see the Buddha, to attain the mind of the noble and worthy, and to acquire immeasurable merit. Whatever they seek shall all be accomplished, and by attaining bodhi they gain peace along the four paths. Furthermore, he causes all beings, by day, by night, at midday, and in all places, to all be at peace and happy, down to every crawling creature, each made joyful. Furthermore, he causes all beings to refrain from creating evil karma and to meet the noble and the worthy. When the bhūtas gather at the place where one dwells and in the space above, they always extend loving protection toward all beings, by day and by night, at all times, abiding in accordance with the dharma.'"

Thereupon the venerable Ānanda addressed the World-Honored One: "So be it, World-Honored One. I now hear your command." Having set up the Indra-pillar, for the sake of all beings eliminating disasters and difficulties, standing with right mindfulness, he spoke this Great Protective Vidyā Great Dhāraṇī and its gāthās of accomplishment:

*Visarata visarata visarata visarata visarata*

> "Past, present, and future, all the buddhas,
> By their perfect and complete knowledge, may they grant their protection.
> May the power of the pratyekabuddhas grant protection,
> May the power of all the arhats grant protection,
> May the power of all the śrāvakas grant protection,
> May the power of all precepts grant protection,
> May the power of all words of truth grant protection,
> May the solitary-dwelling Brahmā's power grant protection,
> May Kāmeśvara's power grant protection,
> May Kauśika's power grant protection,
> May the hosts of devas' power grant protection,
> May the asura kings' power grant protection,
> May all the asuras' power grant protection,
> May the asura messengers' power grant protection,
> May all the bhūtas' power grant protection.
> May the Buddha and the noble worthies,
> For the sake of all sentient beings,
> Extend the power of their majestic virtue,
> And cause all disasters and difficulties to be removed."

*Nirgacchata nirgacchata nirgacchata nirgacchata kṣipraṃ praparāyata ya niyuktam aduṣṭacittanā śayatā yai maitracittanā paraṃ duḥkamāra kṣāṃ cānuvṛtti tukamā siddhitiṣṭhatu mākaṃca*

> "The Samyak-saṃbuddha, for the world's sake,
> To remove disasters, has entered this place."

*Sumu sumu sumu sumu rusumu rusumu rusumu rusumu sumu muru muru muru muru muru muru muru muru muru muru muru muru muru muru muru miri visarata visarata visarata visarata mukhato lokānukaṃpā kujanā payati muñcata muñcata vinaya praviśati iti yupaśamitu nirgacchata nirgacchata*

"The World-Sovereign, Great Brahmā, most exalted and supreme among the heavens, the heavenly lord Śakra, and the four world-protecting kings also entered. Innumerable noble and worthy ones, innumerable asura kings, innumerable asura hosts, and even innumerable bhūta hosts all beheld the World-Honored One and proclaimed their homage, in order to eliminate disasters for all sentient beings."

*Nirgacchata nirgacchata nirgacchata nirgacchata*

"If any person or non-person gives rise to an evil mind, acting harmfully and creating disasters, they should swiftly retreat and scatter, and never again bring about evil. If they do not retreat, they shall bring about their own destruction. May loving-kindness be always aroused, and may they constantly protect and guard. Know that the Tathāgata, savior of the world, is thus."

*Sumuru sumuru sumuru sumuru sumuru muru muru muru muru muru suru suru suru suru suru suru suru suru miri miri miri miri muru miri muru miri muru miri muru miri muru miri muru miri muru miri muru miri muru miri muru miri muru miri muru miri muru miri rusu mimi riti miri sisi mi kaṃ kara kara kaṭa kara kaca kaṃ kara kaṃ kara kaṃ kara kaṃ kara kaṃ kara kaṃ kara kaṃ kara kaṃ kara kaṃ karu kariti kurīśe kaṃ kari kari śeti riri riri riri riri ti ri phāsari hiri hiri hiri hiri hiri hiri hiri ya niyuktam anātha nātha nī muri muri nirgacchata nirgacchata*

"May all enemies swiftly retreat and scatter, and may all sentient beings have their wishes fulfilled. The Tathāgata, with great compassion, great joy, great equanimity, and great loving-kindness, speaks this mantra of accomplishment and these gāthās, so that all the celestial hosts and the bhūta hosts may attain the most supreme wisdom and the meaning of the dharma. He removes all disasters and calamities from the world, destroys the faults of self and others' greed and poison, and with a virtuous mind teaches all dharmas, causing all sentient beings in the world to see the path of liberation and attain peace and happiness. The Tathāgata, teacher of the three realms, guards and protects all beings as his own infants, and like a lamp illuminates all beings, enabling those in saṃsāra to see the true path. He skillfully teaches the dharma-essentials, his speech pure and clear, with all spirits as witnesses. Furthermore, in each life one attains great fearlessness, dwelling in wealth and prosperity with abundant resources, benefiting all, attaining the accomplishment of meaning, attaining the accomplishment of merit. Furthermore, at one's birth the earth universally quakes, and all beings rejoice and attain happiness. At the site of awakening the earth quakes in six ways, and Māra and Māra's retinue are all stricken with grief, knowing that the Muni turns the wheel of the true dharma and teaches the Four Noble Truths, using the dharma of training to subdue non-Buddhist practitioners and all sentient beings, bringing peace and happiness to the devas, the heavenly lord Śakra, and even all the bhūta hosts. He causes all sentient beings at all times and in all directions, by the Buddha's merit and the celestial intent, to accomplish whatever they seek, gaining peace and happiness and attaining bodhi. May all sentient beings become virtuous and good, far removed from all wrongdoing, with none who commit offenses, down to every crawling creature. May all disasters and calamities be removed and all illness and distress eliminated. Wherever one goes and wherever one dwells, by day, by night, and at midday, may all be at peace and happy. When the bhūtas gather, whether dwelling upon the earth or in the sky, may they always give rise to loving minds and abide in accordance with the dharma. May all the buddhas of this world and other worlds, and even all the devas, always come to guard and protect, and may great peace and happiness be attained."

When this dhāraṇī had been spoken, the celestial hosts and all the rest rejoiced and put it into practice.
