## Fascicle 1

### The Sūtra Spoken by the Buddha on the Questions of Śakra

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was dwelling in the Āmra Garden to the east of Rājagṛha in the kingdom of Magadha, north of the great brahmin settlement, in the Cave of Śakra on Mount Vaidehī, together with a great assembly.

At that time, Śakra, lord of the devas, heard that the Buddha was dwelling in the Cave of Śakra on Mount Vaidehī in the kingdom of Magadha. He addressed Pañcaśikha, prince of the gandharvas, saying: "Do you know? I have heard that the Buddha is dwelling in the Cave of Śakra on Mount Vaidehī in the kingdom of Magadha. I wish to go with you to the Buddha's presence to draw near and make offerings." Upon hearing these words, Pañcaśikha, prince of the gandharvas, said to Śakra: "Excellent, lord of the devas!" Having spoken thus, he took up his lapis lazuli-adorned lute and followed after Śakra. At that time, the heavenly host, hearing that Śakra, lord of the devas, and Pañcaśikha, prince of the gandharvas, had resolved to go to the Buddha's presence to draw near and make offerings, also each resolved to follow and go to the Buddha's presence to draw near and make offerings.

At that time, Śakra, lord of the devas, Pañcaśikha, prince of the gandharvas, and that heavenly host departed from their celestial realm, and as swiftly as a strong man might flex and extend his arm, arrived at the foot of Mount Vaidehī in the kingdom of Magadha. Then suddenly a great radiance appeared on the mountain, illuminating all in every direction. The people on all four sides of the mountain, seeing that light, said to one another: "Why does a great fire blaze upon this mountain, its original appearance hidden as though it were a mountain of jewels?"

At that time, Śakra, lord of the devas, addressed Pañcaśikha, prince of the gandharvas: "Do you see how extraordinary and wondrous is the appearance of this mountain? It is because the Blessed One, the World-Honored One, abides within it, pure in the four requisites. Moreover, all the halls and palaces on this mountain are made entirely of precious gems; those who dwell there exhaust all defilements and realize the fruits of holiness, and even the great and powerful devas constantly abide here." He further said: "Therefore, this occasion of drawing near and making offerings, as I spoke of before, is difficult to encounter and difficult to meet with — now is precisely the right time. You, Pañcaśikha, prince of the gandharvas, should make an offering with the music you bear. Why so? From this point onward, it will truly be difficult to encounter such an opportunity."

Upon hearing these words, the prince of the gandharvas said to Śakra: "Excellent! Excellent!" Having spoken thus, he arose and reflected: "All Tathāgatas, Buddhas, and Thus-Come Ones possess the divine ear, and whether near or far, they can all hear." Having thought thus, he struck the lapis lazuli-adorned lute he held, and from within its sound he brought forth a gāthā, and within the gāthā he expressed what he longed for. The gāthā said:

> O Sunlight, daughter of radiance,
> Pray entreat your royal father —
> Take me as your companion,
> And thus proclaim your virtue.
> My longing and love for you
> Is like one afflicted by heat
> Yearning for coolness,
> Like the thirsty thirsting for water.
> Like the sick one yearning for medicine,
> Like the hungry one thinking of food,
> Like a great elephant held by a hook
> Unable to go forward.
> And like the arahant
> Who joyfully seeks the Dhamma of cessation —
> Such too is the nature
> Of my present aspiration.
> Craving increases defilement;
> This holds no true reality
> And fulfills no wished-for aim,
> But brings forth every kind of suffering.
> The merit I have performed,
> Offering to the arahants —
> Whatever fruit that has ripened
> I would share with you.
> My seeking of Sunlight's daughter
> Is a resolve most firm.
> O Śakra, lord of the devas,
> Grant me my heart's desire.

At that time, the World-Honored One, within the Cave of Śakra, heard that sound from afar through the divine ear, and with his spiritual power called out from afar to Pañcaśikha, prince of the gandharvas: "Well done! Well done, Pañcaśikha, prince of the gandharvas! You are skilled in music. When you pluck the strings, the subtle and wondrous sound is like the voice of beautiful song, and when you sing, the voice is again like the sound of strings. Why, having long played music, did you bring forth a gāthā from within the strings? And within the gāthā, why did you speak three kinds of sound — the sound of love and longing, the sound of the nāgas, and the sound of the arahants?"

At that time, Pañcaśikha, prince of the gandharvas, supported by the Buddha's spiritual power, heard the Buddha's words from afar, and said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I recall that at one time there was a gandharva king named Timbaru whose daughter was named Sunlight. She was the one my heart longed for and whom I wished to take as my companion. Though I employed many expedients, my wish was not fulfilled, so I played this music before her, bringing forth a gāthā from within the strings and speaking three kinds of sound within the gāthā. World-Honored One! When I was playing this music, there were devas gathered at that assembly of the Dharma who said to one another: 'This Pañcaśikha, prince of the gandharvas, neither sees nor hears of our Buddha World-Honored One, who possesses the ten epithets in full: Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, Vidyācaraṇasampanna, Sugata, Lokavid, Anuttarapuruṣa, Puruṣadamyasārathi, Śāstā Devamanuṣyāṇāṃ, Buddho Bhagavān.' Thereupon I said to those devas: 'Good devas, well have you praised the Buddha's virtues.' The devas replied: 'Pañcaśikha, prince of the gandharvas! We share with you all the merit of praising the Buddha's virtues.' Hearing the devas' words, Pañcaśikha, prince of the gandharvas, suddenly awakened and replied: 'Noble ones! From this day I take refuge in the Blessed One, the World-Honored One, the Buddha.' For this reason, I played that music for the Buddha just now."

At that time, Śakra, lord of the devas, thought to himself: "The spiritual faculties of Pañcaśikha, prince of the gandharvas, are now mature — even before reaching the Buddha's presence, he has already made his offering." Having thought thus, he addressed Pañcaśikha: "Carry my words and go to the Buddha's presence. Bow your head to his feet, and in my name say: 'Śakra, lord of the devas, bows his head at your two feet and inquires after the World-Honored One: Are you free from illness and affliction? Do you move and rest with ease? Are your strength and health well? Do you go and stop without distress? Today I and the Trāyastriṃśa heavenly host wish to come to the Buddha's presence to draw near and make offerings. We await the Buddha's permission.'"

Upon hearing these words, Pañcaśikha, prince of the gandharvas, said to Śakra: "Excellent, lord of the devas!" Having spoken thus, he went to the Buddha's presence, bowed his head to the Buddha's feet, stood to one side, and said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! Śakra, lord of the devas, together with the Trāyastriṃśa heavenly host, has sent me here to bow at the Buddha's two feet and inquire after the World-Honored One: Are you free from illness and affliction? Do you move and rest with ease? Are your strength and health well? Do you go and stop without distress? Today we wish to come to the Buddha's presence to draw near and make offerings, and so he has sent me here to await the Buddha's permission."

The Buddha replied: "You may return and tell Śakra and that heavenly host — now is precisely the right time." Pañcaśikha, prince of the gandharvas, received the holy command of the Buddha and returned to Śakra's place, conveying the World-Honored One's words that now was precisely the right time.

At that time, Śakra and the Trāyastriṃśa heavenly host proceeded to the Buddha's presence. Having arrived, they bowed at the Buddha's two feet and stood to one side. At that moment, the lord of the devas arose in thought: "This Cave of Śakra is narrow and confined — how can the innumerable heavenly host be seated?" The Buddha knew his thought, and by spiritual power caused the cave to expand and become spacious, so that all the devas could be accommodated without obstructing one another. Śakra, lord of the devas, and that heavenly host each bowed to the Buddha and took their seats in order. When all were seated and settled, Śakra, lord of the devas, joined his palms and said: "World-Honored One! Throughout the long night I have joyfully wished to see the Buddha and joyfully wished to hear the true Dharma. World-Honored One! I recall that at one time the Buddha was at the Jetavana Anāthapiṇḍikārāma in the kingdom of Śrāvastī, having entered the fire-element samādhi. At that time, I was in the palace of Vaiśravaṇa, and I saw that in that palace there was a consort named Śubhabhujā who, seeing the Buddha enter the fire-element samādhi, joined her palms in reverence and fixed her mind on the Buddha. Seeing that the World-Honored One had not yet emerged from samādhi, I said to Śubhabhujā: 'When the Blessed One, the World-Honored One, emerges from samādhi, convey my sincere inquiry to the Buddha — is he free from illness and affliction? Does he move and rest with ease? Are his strength and health well? Does he go and stop without distress?' And again I said: 'When the Buddha emerges from concentration, convey my sincere message — do not let it be forgotten.' World-Honored One! Is this indeed so?"

The Buddha said: "Śakra! This is indeed so. That consort at one time conveyed your respectful inquiry. The Buddha further said: "Lord of the devas! While I was in samādhi, I also heard your words; not long thereafter, I emerged from samādhi."

At that time, Śakra said: "World-Honored One! I have heard in former times that when a Buddha, Tathāgata, Samyaksaṃbuddha appears in the world, he is of great benefit, and through great skillful means he leads beings according to their kind — sometimes concealing the human form, sometimes manifesting the heavenly body. I now know for myself that the Buddha's appearance in the world is of great benefit, and that through skillful means he leads beings according to their kind, sometimes concealing, sometimes manifesting. World-Honored One! There are śrāvakas who have gone forth from home under the Buddha and practiced the holy life; upon death they were reborn in the Trāyastriṃśa heaven, where those heavenly beings take delight in three things: lifespan, physical form, and renown. World-Honored One! In former times there was a Śākya woman named Sucarita who had gone forth from home under the Buddha and maintained the holy life. She constantly lamented her female body and sought the form of a man. Upon death she was reborn in the Trāyastriṃśa heaven, became my son, was named Sucarita, and possessed great majesty and power — a great man. World-Honored One! There were also three bhikṣus who practiced the śrāvaka path but had not been able to cut off the mind of craving and desire; upon death they were reborn in the heavenly realm as sons of the gandharva Viṇa, and they constantly came to serve as attendants to Sucarita the devaputra. At that time, Sucarita the devaputra spoke a gāthā to the sons of the gandharva Viṇa:

> "'I was formerly a woman,
> Wise, named Sucarita,
> Loathing the female form I sought a man's body,
> Constantly making offerings to the Buddha, Dharma, and Saṅgha.
> At that time I saw your three sons
> Practicing the śrāvaka path —
> Now you are reborn in a lower lineage
> And serve as my attendants.
> You should now know this:
> I speak truly to you.
> In former times when you were human
> You were abundantly supplied in the four requisites.
> You did not rely on the Buddha's precepts —
> Now you may well feel shame.
> To understand the mind is itself the Dharma;
> Only the wise can understand.
> In former times we practiced the same path,
> Drew near the Buddha, heard the true Dharma,
> Aroused faith, upheld the Buddha's precepts,
> And made offerings to the holy community.
> Through my practice of right conduct
> I became a son of Śakra,
> Possessed of great heavenly majesty,
> Knowing my own name as Sucarita,
> Dwelling in a supremely excellent palace,
> Transformed from female to male form.
> You sons of the gandharvas, who under the Buddha
> Maintained the holy life
> And heard the Buddha's supreme Dharma,
> Have become attendants instead.
> In the heavenly realm I had not seen this —
> Now I see it:
> Practicing the śrāvaka path,
> Yet being reborn in a lower lineage.
> O sons of the gandharvas, receive my instruction —
> The life you have received
> Is not that of the sons of those Buddhas.'
> The sons of the gandharvas said: 'What the deva says is true.
> We, on account of craving and desire,
> Have fallen into the gandharva realm.
> We now arouse diligence
> And contemplate only the Buddha's true Dharma.
> Knowing that craving produces faults,
> We cut off the mind of craving.
> Craving is bound by defilement;
> Its power surpasses Māra's host.
> Abandoning the Buddha's true Dharma,
> We did not attain birth among the superior devas.
> Śakra and Brahmā
> Sit in the assembly of the good Dharma,
> Observing the excellent practices of the devas,
> Wandering through the heavenly realms.
> Seeing us reborn in a lower lineage
> And wandering through the heavenly realm —
> Because our conduct was not upright,
> We have not obtained the superior fruit.'
> Then Sucarita the devaputra
> Said to his father Śakra:
> 'Royal father, now know this:
> Our Buddha, the most supreme,
> Has appeared in the world,
> Subduing the host of Māra —
> Named Śākyamuni.
> These three gandharvas
> Are sons of that Buddha
> Who forgot right mindfulness
> And fell into the gandharva realm.
> Among those three,
> One alone lacks right understanding;
> The other two have returned to the right path,
> Constantly turning toward the Buddha's bodhi
> And walking the true Dharma.
> Among all the śrāvakas observed,
> None can compare with them.
> They have put aside all desire,
> Are able to sever defilement,
> Think only of the Blessed One,
> And no longer give rise to other thoughts.
> All the Dharma not yet understood —
> Those two know it rightly.
> They shall obtain the superior fruit
> And be reborn in the Brahma heaven.'

"World-Honored One! At that time, having heard Sucarita the devaputra speak these verses, I had some unresolved questions about this matter, so I have come to the Buddha's presence wishing to respectfully inquire. May the Buddha in compassion expound for me."

At that time, the World-Honored One thought: "Throughout the long night Śakra, lord of the devas, has been without slackening, without neglect, without dust, without defilement. What he asks, he truly does not know — it is not a demonic undertaking. What he asks, I should expound." Having thought thus, he spoke a gāthā, saying to Śakra:

> Śakra, now know this:
> What your heart desires
> And wishes to ask about in meaning —
> Ask, and I shall expound it for you.

At that time, Śakra, lord of the devas, spoke a gāthā, saying to the World-Honored One:

> Now receiving the Buddha's permission,
> As my mind desires —
> I now raise my request:
> May the Buddha expound it for me.

Having spoken this gāthā, Śakra said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! By what are all devas, humans, asuras, gandharvas, and all ordinary beings afflicted?"

The Buddha said: "They are afflicted by enmity and affection. Śakra, lord of the devas! All devas, humans, asuras, gandharvas, and all ordinary beings think to themselves: 'Alas! I had first done no harm to others, harbored no resentment, engaged in no quarreling, no disputing, no complaint, no litigation, and held nothing against anyone — why have they treated me thus in return?' Lord of the devas! Such matters arise from enmity and affection; because enmity and affection arise, affliction ensues."

Śakra said: "World-Honored One! So it is, so it is, as the Buddha has said. I now understand this meaning from the Buddha — that enmity and affection are the affliction. My doubt is severed, and my heart's desire is fulfilled."

At that time, Śakra, lord of the devas, rejoiced at hearing the Buddha's teaching, received it with faith, and again said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! This affliction of enmity and affection — what is its cause, what its origin? What is its arising, what its condition? By what cause does it exist? By what cause does it not exist?"

The Buddha said: "Lord of the devas! This affliction of enmity and affection has enemies and loved ones as its cause, originates from enemies and loved ones, arises from enemies and loved ones, has enemies and loved ones as its condition. Because of enemies and loved ones, the affliction of enmity and affection exists; if there are no enemies and loved ones, enmity and affection do not exist."

Śakra said to the Buddha: "So it is, so it is, as the Buddha has said. I now understand this meaning from the Buddha — that the affliction of enmity and affection has enemies and loved ones as its cause; if there are no enemies and loved ones, there is no enmity and affection." He further said: "World-Honored One! By what does the existence of enemies and loved ones arise? From what does it originate? From what does it arise? On what condition does it depend? By what cause does it exist? By what cause does it not exist?"

The Buddha told Śakra: "Desire is its cause; from desire it originates; from desire it arises; on desire it depends as its condition. Because of desire there are enemies and loved ones; if there is no desire, there are no enemies and loved ones."

Śakra said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! So it is, so it is, as the Buddha has said — enemies and loved ones exist because of desire." He further said: "World-Honored One! This desire — what is its cause? From what does it originate? From what does it arise? On what does it depend as its condition? By what cause does it exist? By what cause does it not exist?"

The Buddha said: "Śakra! Desire has doubt and uncertainty as its cause; from doubt and uncertainty it originates; from doubt and uncertainty it arises; on doubt and uncertainty it depends as its condition. Because of doubt and uncertainty there is desire; if there is no doubt and uncertainty, there is no desire."

Śakra said: "World-Honored One! So it is, so it is, as the Buddha has said — desire exists because of doubt and uncertainty." He further said: "World-Honored One! Doubt and uncertainty — what is their cause? What their origin? What their arising? What their condition? By what cause do they exist? By what cause do they not exist?"

The Buddha told Śakra: "False construction is their cause; from false construction they originate; from false construction they arise; on false construction they depend as their condition. Because of false construction there is doubt and uncertainty; because of doubt and uncertainty there is desire; because of desire there are enemies and loved ones; because of enemies and loved ones there arises enmity and affection; because of enmity and affection there is taking up of weapons, litigation, fighting, and disputing; duplicity of feeling arises, and speech becomes untrue — such various kinds of unwholesome actions and unskillful dharmas come to be, and thereby a great mass of suffering is gathered. Lord of the devas! If there is no false construction, there is no doubt and uncertainty; if there is no doubt and uncertainty, there is no desire; if desire does not exist, what enemies and loved ones can there be? If enemies and loved ones are not established, enmity and affection are automatically removed; because enmity and affection are absent, the taking up of weapons, litigation, fighting, and disputing, duplicity of feeling and untruthful speech — such various kinds of unwholesome actions and unskillful dharmas — all cease; thus a great mass of suffering is extinguished."

Śakra said to the Buddha: "So it is, so it is, as the Buddha has said — because of doubt and uncertainty there is false construction." He further said: "World-Honored One! By what dharma is false construction extinguished? And how should bhikṣus practice?"

The Buddha told the lord of the devas: "What extinguishes false construction is the noble eightfold path. The noble eightfold path comprises: right view, right intention, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, and right concentration. Through these eight dharmas, false construction is extinguished. If bhikṣus practice these dharmas, this is what is called the practice that extinguishes false construction."

Śakra said to the Buddha: "So it is, so it is. World-Honored One! What extinguishes false construction is the noble eightfold path." Śakra further said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! Among the dharmas of individual liberation, in the dharmas that extinguish false construction that bhikṣus practice — how many kinds of dharmas are there?"

The Buddha said: "Lord of the devas! Among the dharmas of individual liberation, the dharmas of false construction comprise six kinds. What are the six? They are: the eye perceiving form, the ear hearing sound, the nose smelling fragrance, the tongue discerning flavor, the body sensing contact, and the mind discriminating dharmas. Lord of the devas! The eye perceiving form has two aspects: what may be perceived and what may not be perceived. What may not be perceived refers to the domain of all defiled dharmas, which should not be perceived; what may be perceived refers to the domain of all wholesome dharmas, which may be observed. So it is with the eye perceiving form, and likewise up to the mind discriminating dharmas."

"World-Honored One! I now understand this meaning from the Buddha. What may not be perceived — all the unwholesome dharmas within the domain of the eye, if perceived, increase defiled dharmas and diminish wholesome dharmas. What may be perceived — all the wholesome dharmas within the domain of the eye, if perceived, increase wholesome dharmas and diminish defiled dharmas. Likewise up to the mind discriminating dharmas. World-Honored One! Having heard this Dharma from the Buddha, my aspiration is fulfilled and my doubt is severed." He further said: "World-Honored One! If a bhikṣu wishes to extinguish false construction, how many dharmas should he abandon, and how many dharmas should he practice?"

The Buddha said: "Lord of the devas! If a bhikṣu wishes to extinguish the dharmas of false construction, he should abandon three dharmas and practice three dharmas: (1) doubt and uncertainty, (2) hankering, (3) meaningless speech. These three kinds of dharmas likewise have what is to be practiced and what is not to be practiced. What is not to be practiced: regarding these three kinds of unwholesome dharmas, one should abandon and not practice them; if one practices them, unwholesome dharmas increase and wholesome dharmas diminish. What is to be practiced: regarding these three kinds of unwholesome dharmas, one diligently practices their elimination, and thus unwholesome dharmas diminish and wholesome dharmas increase."

Śakra said: "World-Honored One! So it is, so it is. I now understand this meaning from the Buddha. Doubt and uncertainty, hankering, and meaningless speech — these three kinds of dharmas, if practiced, diminish wholesome dharmas and increase the unwholesome. If a bhikṣu diligently practices the elimination of these three dharmas, unwholesome dharmas diminish and wholesome dharmas increase." He further said: "World-Honored One! If a bhikṣu practices the dharmas that extinguish false construction, how many kinds of body are there?"

The Buddha said: "Lord of the devas! If a bhikṣu practices the dharmas that extinguish false construction, there are three kinds of body. The three kinds of body are: the body of ease and satisfaction, the body of suffering and distress, and the body of equanimity. The body of ease and satisfaction has two aspects: what is to be practiced and what is not to be practiced. What is to be practiced refers to all wholesome dharmas; what is not to be practiced refers to all unwholesome dharmas. So too with the body of suffering and distress and the body of equanimity."

Śakra said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! So it is, so it is. I now understand this meaning from the Buddha. If a bhikṣu practices the dharmas that extinguish false construction, with regard to the body of ease and satisfaction, the body of suffering and distress, and the body of equanimity — among these three bodies, all wholesome dharmas are to be practiced, all unwholesome dharmas are not to be practiced."

At that time, Śakra further said: "World-Honored One! Are the desires, recollections, and physical forms of all sentient beings identical?"

The Buddha said: "No. Lord of the devas, all sentient beings do not share one desire, nor one recollection, nor one physical form. Lord of the devas! All sentient beings, though dwelling each in their own realm and destiny, do not know the distinctions among realms and destinies. Because they do not know these distinctions among realms and destinies, they walk the dark path and in return grasp foolish dharmas as being true and real. These sentient beings, not knowing the various distinctions of realms and destinies, understand only the realm of darkness, and though they understand even this, they constantly walk the dark path and firmly grasp foolish dharmas as being true and real."

Śakra said: "World-Honored One! So it is, so it is, as the Buddha has said. I now understand this meaning from the Buddha — all sentient beings do not share one desire, nor one recollection, nor one physical form; because those sentient beings do not know distinctions, they grasp the foolish and the dark as being true and real."

At that time, Śakra further said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! Have all śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas attained the ultimate purity of the holy life?"

The Buddha said: "No. There are two aspects to this. Lord of the devas! If those śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas are unable to exhaust affection for dharmas, they will decidedly not attain the ultimate purity of the holy life. If those śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas are able to exhaust affection for dharmas, they attain the unsurpassed liberation, the right liberation of mind — this is called attaining the ultimate purity of the holy life."

Śakra said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! So it is, so it is, as the Buddha has said. I now understand this meaning from the Buddha. If śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas do not exhaust affection for dharmas, they will decidedly not attain the ultimate purity of the holy life. If śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas exhaust affection for dharmas, they will decidedly attain the unsurpassed liberation, the right liberation of mind — this is called attaining the ultimate purity of the holy life."

At that time, Śakra further said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! How may I now permanently depart from the illness of all views, so that it no longer arises? This illness of views arises from mind-consciousness; what is to become of this mind-consciousness of mine? Though I have asked the Buddha about various meanings, why can I not obtain the holy fruit, attaining Buddhahood, Tathāgathood, Arhatship, and Samyaksaṃbodhi? I beseech the World-Honored One: please sever for me the doubt and the root illness of all views."

The Buddha said: "Lord of the devas! Do you know? In former times there were śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who also asked about this matter."

Śakra said: "World-Honored One! I now recall that at one time, devas of great majesty and power assembled at the assembly of the good Dharma in the Trāyastriṃśa heaven. At that time, some devas in the assembly, not knowing the Dharma, hastily wished to become Buddhas, and with this intention made their declaration to the World-Honored One. The Buddha, discerning their foolishness and ignorance, did not grant them the prediction. At that time, those devas, their wish unfulfilled and their minds in discord, rose from their seats and each returned to their own realm. Their own realm did not appear, and they knew they would fall. Those devas, having fallen, were greatly alarmed and terrified, and their minds gave rise to doubt and uncertainty. Each thought: 'Our own realm has not appeared — we know we have fallen. If we can find any śramaṇa or brāhmaṇa, we will go and ask: are you the Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha?' At that time, those devas — some who saw me walking alone — came to my presence and asked me: 'Noble one! Who are you?' I replied: 'I am Śakra, lord of the devas.' Those devas, their minds in distress, said: 'Lord of the devas! Do you not see our suffering? Because we should have inquired of the Dharma from the Buddha but did not inquire, should have taken refuge but did not take refuge — with a discordant mind we returned to our own realm, our own realm did not appear, and we knew we had fallen. Therefore we suffer. We beg your protection and aid. Lord of the devas! From this day forward we vow to take refuge in the Buddha as śrāvaka disciples.' At that time I spoke a gāthā in reply to those devas:

> "'You all arose in wrong thought;
> Your words too were not right.
> Seeking the Buddha with discordant minds,
> Because of this you long endure suffering.
> Perhaps you met some śramaṇa
> Or some brāhmaṇa
> Wandering in walking meditation, and asked:
> Are you the Perfectly Enlightened One?
> If you are the Perfectly Enlightened One,
> I take refuge and make offerings.
> I then asked them:
> How should one make offerings?
> The questions could not be answered —
> The Buddha's true and correct path.
> At that time those devas,
> In their minds' desires,
> With mind and mental factors
> Wavering in doubt and discrimination —
> I know their mental dharmas,
> As the World-Honored One has taught.
> I have already spoken to them:
> In those three realms,
> Only the Blessed One, the World-Honored One,
> Is the great teacher of the world.
> Skillfully subduing the great host of Māra,
> Able to ferry all sentient beings
> To the further shore of nirvāṇa.
> The Tathāgata, the great and awakened one,
> Among devas and humans,
> Has no equal.
> The fearless great man
> Has skillfully severed the illness of craving and affection.
> O great solar lord Tathāgata —
> Bow your head now and pay homage.'"

At that time, after Śakra, lord of the devas, had related this matter, the Buddha further told him: "Lord of the devas! Do you know that past matter — the benefit of discrimination and the benefit of ease and satisfaction?"

Śakra said: "World-Honored One! I now recall that at one time devas and asuras engaged in battle; the devas were victorious and the asuras were defeated and retreated. I thought: 'All the pleasure of the devas and all the pleasure of the asuras — I alone receive it and obtain the ease and satisfaction.' Through obtaining such a benefit of ease and satisfaction, throughout this life there would be fighting and disputing and mutual harm by weapons — this is called the benefit of ease and satisfaction. The benefit of discrimination means that throughout this one life there is no fighting and disputing, and even weapons do not harm one another — this is called the benefit of discrimination." Śakra further said: "World-Honored One! Having now heard this true Dharma from the Buddha, my faith is deepened even further, and I give rise to this aspiration and vow: may I at the end of my life, if reborn among humans, be born into a noble family of great wealth and abundant grain, with many precious gems accumulated, with carriages and vehicles and playthings, with a flourishing retinue fully equipped, always without deficiency. May I be reborn into such a superior family and enter the womb of one wise, with my body and limbs complete, my form and appearance outstanding, eating the finest flavors, honorable and unrestrained, with a long lifespan. May I arouse right faith and go forth from home to the Buddha, shave off hair and beard, don the dharma robes, and become a bhikṣu; may I constantly maintain the holy life without any breach or transgression, and realize the fruits of srotāpanna and sakṛdāgāmin, up to attaining the very end of suffering. World-Honored One! I have further heard that there is the Akaniṣṭha heaven — may I, when I die among humans, be reborn in that heaven."

The Buddha said: "Lord of the devas! Excellent, excellent. Lord of the devas! It shall be as you aspire. By what cause and condition has this distinguished attainment come about?"

Śakra said: "World-Honored One! I have no other cause — it is precisely from having heard the true Dharma from the Buddha that I have aroused deep faith, and through the power of aspiration I have attained such a fruit. World-Honored One! Today, in this assembly, having heard the true Dharma, through the power of the Dharma my wisdom has increased and my lifespan too has increased."

At that time, having made this aspiration, Śakra attained the pure Dharma eye, far from dust, apart from defilement. Moreover, eighty thousand heavenly beings also obtained the purity of the Dharma eye. At that time, Śakra, lord of the devas, having heard the Dharma and seen the Dharma and being able to understand it, was established in the Dharma and firm, with all doubts severed. Having thus realized this, he rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, joined his palms, and bowing his head said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I am liberated! I am liberated! From this day onward, for the rest of my life, I take refuge in the Buddha, the Dharma, and the Saṅgha, and uphold the precepts of an upāsaka."


At that time, Śakra, Lord of the Devas, turned before the Buddha and spoke to the Gandharva prince Pañcaśikha, saying: "You have now generated great benefit for me and for the welfare of many beings. Because you came before and offered that wondrous music as a gift to the Buddha, I and the others were thereby enabled to hear the Dharma and attain the fruit. When I return to my palace, I shall fulfill your every wish."

At that time, Śakra, Lord of the Devas, further addressed the assembly of the Trāyastriṃśa Heaven, saying: "Venerable ones, you should now intone with brahmic voice the Three Refuges in the Buddha. What think you? The Blessed Lord Buddha has already attained the brahmic abode of tranquil nirvāṇa." At that time, the assembly of devas followed Śakra in circumambulating the Buddha three times, then prostrated with their heads at the Buddha's feet, stood before the Buddha, and with one voice in unison, they intoned with brahmic voice the Three Refuges in the Buddha:

[Dhāraṇī] *namo namas sattvasame bhagavate tathāgatāya arhate samyaksaṃbuddhāya*

Śakra, Lord of the Devas, together with that assembly of devas, having three times taken refuge in the Buddha, and together with the Gandharva prince Pañcaśikha and the others, vanished from the assembly and returned to the heavenly realm.

At that time, the Great Brahmā King, Lord of the Sahā World, after that day had passed and in the watches of the night, came to the place of the Buddha, his bodily radiance blazing brilliantly, illuminating the Cave of Śakra. Having arrived before the Buddha, he prostrated at the Buddha's feet, then sat to one side, joined his palms in homage, and spoke a gāthā:

> Śakra, for the welfare of many,
> Came to the Buddha to ask about the true Dharma;
> The Buddha, with his wondrous voice,
> Caused all doubt and confusion to be cut away.

At that time, having spoken the gāthā, the Brahmā King said: "World-Honored One, when the Buddha expounded the true Dharma, Śakra, Lord of the Devas, attained the pure eye of the Dharma, far from dust and free from defilement; and eighty thousand devas also attained the pure eye of the Dharma." The Buddha said: "It is so, it is so." At that time, the Great Brahmā King, Lord of the Sahā World, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and received them in faith, prostrated at the Buddha's feet, vanished from sight, and returned to the heavenly realm.

At that time, the World-Honored One, during the watches of the night, came to where the assembly of bhikṣus sat surrounding him, and addressed the bhikṣus, saying: "After that day had passed, in the watches of the night, the Great Brahmā King, Lord of the Sahā World, came to my place. Having prostrated at my feet, with palms joined in reverence, he spoke a gāthā:

> 'Śakra, for the welfare of many,
> Came to the Buddha to ask about the true Dharma;
> The Buddha, with his wondrous voice,
> Caused all doubt and confusion to be cut away.'

"He then said to me: 'When Śakra, Lord of the Devas, heard the true Dharma, he attained the pure eye of the Dharma; and eighty thousand devas also attained the pure eye of the Dharma.' I then replied: 'It is so, it is so.' At that time, having heard my words, that Brahmā King rejoiced and received them in faith, prostrated at my feet, vanished from sight, and returned to the heavenly realm."

At that time, the entire assembly of bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha expound this Dharma, all rejoiced greatly, prostrated before the Buddha, and withdrew.
