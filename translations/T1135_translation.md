# 佛說一切如來金剛壽命陀羅尼經
## Sūtra of the Vajra Longevity Dhāraṇī of All Tathāgatas Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1135

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

Thus have I heard. At one time the Buddha was beside the Ganges River, together with bhikṣus, great bodhisattvas, and a great assembly of innumerable devas and humans. At that time the World-Honored One addressed Vaiśravaṇa and the other Four Heavenly Kings: "There are four kinds of dharmas that are greatly fearsome. Whether man or woman, boy or girl, no sentient being can escape them: they are birth, old age, sickness, and death. Among these, one dharma is the most universally tormenting and most difficult to counter, namely the fear of death. Out of compassion, I shall proclaim the dharma that counters it."

At that time the Four Heavenly Kings addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! Today we have obtained great benefit. We beseech the World-Honored One to proclaim this dharma for the sake of sentient beings."

At that time the World-Honored One, facing east, snapped his fingers to summon all the tathāgatas, and made this vow: "May all the tathāgatas, arhats, and samyak-saṃbuddhas of the ten directions who have realized bodhi for the sake of sentient beings all assist me. Through the spiritual power of all the tathāgatas, may I cause all sentient beings to transform the karma of untimely death and increase their lifespans. Previously I had not turned this dharma wheel for sentient beings; now I shall turn it, enabling sentient beings to accomplish lifespan, complexion, and strength, free from the fear of premature death." In the south, west, north, four intermediate directions, above, and below, he likewise summoned and proclaimed.

At that time, throughout the ten directions as far as the Buddha's eye could reach, throughout worlds beyond measure, all the tathāgatas gathered, filling space in numbers like motes of dust. Then all the buddhas, in order to bestow empowerment, with different mouths but the same voice spoke the Vajra Longevity Dhāraṇī of All Tathāgatas:

[Dhāraṇī]

*Tadyathā cale calā cale mināci svastikeḥ cakraṃgaṇaṃ praśamantu sarvaloka sarvasattvānāṃ ānāḍi kunāḍi cāre cāre hemakāuri hemaniśāni hemaśikhi koravi korame hekura veśikura vaiśimakuri viśamaṇi śuśubhiva ācale micale māvilaṃ mahūṃ muhūṃ oṃ vajrāyuṣe svāhā*

At that time, in all the buddha-lands of the ten directions, all the vajradhara bodhisattvas with different mouths but the same voice also spoke a life-extending dhāraṇī:

*Hūṃ hūṃ śikhi svāhā*

When all the tathāgatas and all vajradhara bodhisattvas of the ten directions had spoken this dhāraṇī, they became invisible and were no longer seen.

At that time Vaiśravaṇa Heavenly King addressed the Buddha: "I too, through the Buddha's spiritual power, for the sake of empowering and protecting all sentient beings and removing untimely death, speak a dhāraṇī:

*Śivite śivitale rili*

At that time Virūḍhaka Heavenly King also addressed the Buddha: "I too, through the Buddha's spiritual power, for the sake of removing premature death from the multitude of sentient beings, speak a dhāraṇī:

*Matangī matangīni śumā śumāmu*

At that time Dhṛtarāṣṭra Heavenly King also addressed the Buddha: "I too, for the sake of removing the fear of death from all sentient beings, speak a dhāraṇī:

*Jala jala jarala*

At that time Virūpākṣa Heavenly King also addressed the Buddha: "I too, through the Buddha's spiritual power, for the sake of removing untimely death from all sentient beings, speak a dhāraṇī:

*Milima mama ma*

The Buddha told the Four Heavenly Kings: "If any person reads and recites this sūtra, receiving and upholding it daily, even just once, one should revere that good man or good woman as one would think of a buddha. They will never fall into the three evil paths and will certainly have their lifespan increased. If a person daily turns and reads this sūtra for the sake of all sentient beings, they will never have the fear of premature death or short life, nor will they have evil dreams, nightmares, spells, and curses, nor the fear of evil-formed rākṣasas and ghosts. Nor will they be harmed by water, fire, weapons, or poison. All buddhas and bodhisattvas will gather them in and protect them, and that place will also be protected by the buddhas."

When the World-Honored One had spoken this sūtra, Vaiśravaṇa Heavenly King and all the great assembly greatly rejoiced, accepted it with faith, and put it into practice.
