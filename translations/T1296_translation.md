# 十天儀軌
## Ritual of the Ten Devas

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1296

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown.

---

The mudrā of Maheśvara Deva of the northeast:

The right hand forms a fist placed at the right hip. The left hand has five fingers extended straight and touching. Bend the earth-pinky and water-ring fingers at the middle joint. The fire-middle, wind-index, and space-thumb fingers are each slightly separated. This completes the mudrā. The mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ oṃ īśānāya svāhā.*

*Oṃ rudrāya svāhā.*

The mudrā of Śakra Deva of the east:

The right hand is as before. The left hand has five fingers extended straight and touching. Bend the earth-pinky and water-ring fingers at the middle joint. Place the wind-index behind the fire-middle. Bend the space-thumb at the middle joint. The mantra:

*Oṃ indrāya svāhā.*

Also:

*Oṃ śakrāya svāhā.*

The mudrā of Agni Deva of the southeast:

The left hand forms a fist placed at the left hip. The right hand has five fingers extended straight and touching. Bend the space-thumb into the palm. Bend the wind-index at the middle joint, beckoning with "svāhā." The mantra:

*Oṃ agnaye svāhā.*

The mudrā of Yama Deva of the south:

First join the palms. Both earth-pinky fingers bend at the middle joint with backs touching. Both wind-index fingers also bend with backs touching. Press both thumbs upon the respective wind-index middle joints to complete the mudrā. The mantra:

*Oṃ yamāya svāhā.*

The mudrā of Rākṣasa Deva of the southwest:

The right hand forms a fist placed at the hip. The right hand has five fingers extended straight and touching. Bend the earth-pinky and water-ring fingers at the middle joint. Press the space-thumb tip upon the earth-pinky and water-ring fingernails to complete the mudrā. The mantra:

*Oṃ nairṛtaye svāhā.*

The mudrā of Varuṇa Deva of the west:

The right hand as before. The left hand in a hollow fist with the space-thumb not entering the palm. The wind-index finger stands straight, slightly bent at the middle joint, to complete the mudrā. The mantra:

*Oṃ varuṇāya svāhā.*

The mudrā of Vāyu Deva of the northwest:

The right hand as before. The left hand has five fingers extended straight and touching. Bend the earth-pinky and water-ring fingers at the middle joint to complete the mudrā. The mantra:

*Oṃ vāyave svāhā.*

The mudrā of Vaiśravaṇa Deva of the north:

First join the palms with all ten fingers internally crossed. Both water-ring fingers stand erect with tips touching. Both wind-index fingers stand erect, slightly bent. The left space-thumb enters the right palm and presses the left fire-middle fingernail. The right space-thumb passes over the left space-thumb's back, enters the left palm, and presses the right fire-middle fingernail. Both wind-index fingers beckon with "svāhā." The mantra:

*Oṃ vaiśravaṇāya svāhā.*

The mudrā of Brahmā Deva above:

The right hand forms a fist placed at the right hip. The left hand has five fingers touching and erect, slightly bent, raised just slightly above the shoulder to complete the mudrā. The lotus-form mantra:

*Oṃ brahmaṇe svāhā.*

The mudrā of Pṛthivī Deva below:

First join the palms. From the wind-index fingers downward, all four fingertips touch, widely open in front. Both space-thumbs are placed alongside the wind-index fingers. The mantra:

*Oṃ pṛthivīye svāhā.*

The mudrā of Sūrya Deva:

No different from the previous earth-deva mudrā, except that both space-thumb tips rest upon the lower joints of the water-ring fingers. The mantra:

*Oṃ ādityāya svāhā.*

The mudrā of Candra Deva:

Like the previous Brahmā Deva mudrā, but with a pure white moon-form visualized within the palm. The mantra:

*Oṃ candrāya svāhā.*

The mudrā of the seven luminaries:

First join the palms with all ten fingers touching. Both wind-index and both space-thumb fingers are spread widely apart. The mantra:

*Grahā-īśvarīya-prapada-jyotiṣa-māyā svāhā.*

The mudrā of the twenty-eight lunar mansions:

First form a hollow-palmed añjali. Both fire-middle fingers cross externally. Both space-thumbs also cross. The mantra:

*Nakṣatra-neṣu-nādīye svāhā.*
