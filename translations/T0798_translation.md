# 佛說進學經
## Sūtra on Advancing in Learning, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 798

Translated from the Chinese. Liu Song dynasty, translated by Juqu Jingsheng (沮渠京聲)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was dwelling in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍada's Park at Śrāvastī, with a great assembly of twelve hundred and fifty worthy ones. The Buddha addressed the bhikṣus: "There are four refined practices that the wise constantly follow, that men of virtue cultivate, that accomplished persons perpetually uphold, and that the untalented and foolish do not delight in. What are these four? First, serving one's parents with filial piety, with a cheerful countenance and adequate provision. Second, maintaining benevolence and practicing compassion, refraining from killing at all times. Third, giving generously to relieve those in want, never being stingy or contrary. Fourth, having encountered a holy age, renouncing worldly glory and treading the path. These are the four refined practices that the wise follow, that men of virtue cultivate, that accomplished persons uphold, and that the untalented and foolish do not delight in."

The Buddha then recited in verse:

> The wise are praised for filial piety;
> Compassionate toward living beings, they nurture with kindness.
> Giving freely, they provide for all universally;
> Transcending the mundane, they honor stillness.
> Such is right conduct,
> Practiced by those of clear understanding.
> With the vision of the holy ones fully possessed,
> One surely attains the unconditioned.

The Buddha addressed the bhikṣus: "There are furthermore two dharmas in which, whether in quiet seclusion or amid a great assembly, one's mental practice should never slacken: first, stillness and silence, the serene composure of the virtuous and holy; second, broad learning and the discussion of profound meanings. There are also two kinds of giving: fine food and drink to sustain bodily life; and spreading and expounding the sūtras to reveal their subtle meanings, bringing delight to those who hear them. The gift of food sustains the body; the gift of Dharma transforms the spirit. Though the former may bring pleasure, the gift of Dharma is supreme. Therefore, bhikṣus, reflect upon and expound the wondrous Dharma; proclaim wisdom and do not abide in delusion. Having cleansed yourselves, also purify the dust of attachment. Such is the Dharma of the path, enabling liberation without end. This is truly called going forth from home, with awakening and understanding fully possessed."

When the Buddha had finished speaking this sūtra, the bhikṣus were joyful, paid reverence, and received the teaching.
