# 佛說彌勒來時經
## Sūtra of Maitreya's Coming Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0457

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown

---

Śāriputra, the Buddha's foremost disciple, with a compassionate mind thinking of the world, went to the Buddha's place, knelt before him, and with palms joined asked: "The Buddha has always said: 'After the Buddha departs, Maitreya will come.' I wish to hear about this from the Buddha."

The Buddha said: "When Maitreya Buddha is about to appear, the mountains, trees, grasses, and plants within Jambudvīpa will all have withered and burned away. The present Jambudvīpa measures six hundred thousand li in circumference. When Maitreya appears, Jambudvīpa will be four hundred thousand li from east to west and three hundred and twenty thousand li from north to south. The land will produce five kinds of fruits. Within the four seas there will be no mountains, ridges, ravines, or valleys; the ground will be level as a whetstone, and the trees will all grow tall.

"At that time, among the people there will be few who are greedy, lustful, angry, or deluded. The people will be numerous, and the villages and households will be so close that the crowing of roosters can be heard from one to the next. The people will all live to be eighty-four thousand years old. Women will not marry until the age of five hundred. Among the people there will be no illness or pain. All the people of the world will have only three afflictions: first, the desire to obtain things; second, hunger and thirst; third, old age. The people's complexions will all be like the color of peach blossoms, and all people will be treated with respect.

"There will be a city called Ketumatī. The ruler of Ketumatī will be king over the nation. The city will be four hundred and eighty li in circumference, built of earth, then planked with boards, then adorned with gold, silver, lapis lazuli, crystal, and precious jewels. On each of its four sides there will be twelve gates, all with carvings, and further adorned with gold, silver, lapis lazuli, crystal, and precious jewels. The king will be named Saṃkha. All within the four seas will be subject to Saṃkha. When he travels, he flies through the air, and wherever he goes, the people and spirits all bow in reverence.

"The city will have four treasures: first, gold, guarded by a nāga named Nīlakesara, who protects the gold; the mountain where the nāga dwells is called Gandhāra. Second, silver; in that country there is a guardian nāga named Pāṇḍuka. Third, a bright-moon pearl; the place where it is found is called Śuddha, and the guardian nāga is named Piṅgala. Fourth, lapis lazuli; the city where it is found is called Vāraṇasī.

"There will be a brahmin named Subrahmā, who will be Maitreya's father. Maitreya's mother will be named Mahāvaidehī, and Maitreya will be born as their son. Maitreya will be of the brahmin caste. His body will possess the thirty-two marks and eighty minor characteristics, and he will be sixteen zhang tall. When Maitreya is born and touches the ground of the city, his eyes will see clearly for ten thousand li, and the light from his head will illuminate four thousand li. When Maitreya attains the path and becomes a Buddha, he will sit beneath the dragon-flower tree, which is forty li tall and forty li wide. When Maitreya becomes a Buddha, eighty-four thousand brahmins will all go to Maitreya's place to serve him as teacher, and they will renounce their homes to become śramaṇas. When Maitreya sits beneath the tree, he will attain the Buddha's path on the eighth day of the fourth month, at the hour when the morning star appears.

"King Saṃkha, hearing that Maitreya has attained Buddhahood, will then lead eighty-four kings, all of whom will abandon their kingdoms, entrusting their nations to their crown princes, and together go to Maitreya Buddha's place. They will all shave their beards and hair and become śramaṇas. In addition, one thousand eight hundred brahmins will all go to Maitreya Buddha's place and become śramaṇas. Maitreya's parents will also be among them. In addition, one thousand and eighty-four holy brahmins will all go to Maitreya Buddha's place and become śramaṇas.

"In the country there will be a great and wealthy benefactor named Sudatta, also called by the people Sudatta. He will bring out gold and give it to Maitreya Buddha and all the śramaṇas, and his fame will spread far and wide. Sudatta will also lead fourteen thousand worthy and good people to Maitreya Buddha's place to become śramaṇas.

"There will also be two brothers. The elder brother will be named Dundubha, and the younger will be named Pulāṇa. The brothers will both say: 'Why should we remain in this world? Would it not be better for us both to go to the Buddha's place and seek to become śramaṇas?' The brothers will both say: 'Excellent! Let us go to Maitreya Buddha's place and become śramaṇas.'

"There will also be eighty-four thousand young women, all wearing fine garments, white pearls, gold, silver, and jeweled necklaces. They will all go to Maitreya Buddha's place, remove the jewels from their bodies and place them on the ground, and address the Buddha: 'We wish to offer these to the Buddha and all the śramaṇas. We wish to become bhikṣuṇīs under the Buddha.' The Buddha will then permit them to become bhikṣuṇīs.

"Maitreya Buddha, seated before the bhikṣu saṅgha and the bhikṣuṇīs, will teach them, saying: 'All of these were, during the time of the Buddha Śākyamuni, those who recited the sūtras, those with compassionate minds, those who practiced generosity, those free from anger, those who built buddha-temples, those who placed the Buddha's relics in stūpas, those who burned incense, those who lit lamps, those who hung silk banners, those who scattered flowers, and those who read the sūtras. These bhikṣuṇīs were all people from the time of the Buddha Śākyamuni who upheld the precepts and were sincere. All of them have now come to this assembly. Those who heard the sūtras taught by the bhikṣus will all attain the path beneath the dragon-flower tree.'

"At Maitreya Buddha's first assembly, when he teaches the Dharma, ninety-six hundred million people will all attain arhatship.

"At the second assembly, when he teaches the Dharma, ninety-four hundred million bhikṣus will all attain arhatship.

"At the third assembly, when he teaches the Dharma, ninety-two hundred million śramaṇas will all attain arhatship. All the devas in the heavens will take flowers and scatter them upon Maitreya's body.

"Maitreya Buddha will lead all the arhats to the city governed by King Ketumatī. The king will receive them in his inner palace with food and drink. The entire city will be bright; at night it will be as daytime. Maitreya, seated in the palace, will teach the Dharma, saying: 'Words must not go unacted upon; the path must not go unstudied; the sūtras must not go unpraised.' When the Buddha has finished teaching the sūtra, all the bhikṣus, the king, and the hundred officials will uphold the Buddha's sūtras and precepts, and all will cross beyond the world."

The Buddha spoke thus. Maitreya Buddha will come down after sixty billion and six hundred thousand years from now.
