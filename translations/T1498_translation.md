# 佛說八種長養功德經
## Sūtra Spoken by the Buddha on the Eight Kinds of Virtue that Nourish Growth

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1498

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmapāla et al. (法護等譯)

---

Homage to all Buddhas! May all Buddhas and the assembly of bodhisattvas receive and protect me! Then the following gāthās are recited:

> I now take refuge in supreme bodhi,
> In the most exalted pure Buddha, Dharma, and Saṅgha.
> I arouse the vast and great mind of bodhi,
> That the benefit of self and others may both be accomplished.
> I repent and remove all unwholesome karma,
> And rejoice in the boundless accumulation of merit.
> First I shall fast for one full day,
> Then I shall cultivate the eight-fold practice of nourishing growth.

Know that the eight practices of nourishing growth are the so-called Eight Precepts. The disciple should recite the following two or three times before the ācārya. Having recited these gāthās, one should then state one's own name:

"I am named [such-and-such]. I beg the ācārya to receive and protect me. From this moment forth I arouse a mind of pure faith, until I sit at the bodhimaṇḍa and attain perfect and complete awakening. I vow to take refuge in the Buddha, the most honored of the two-footed; I vow to take refuge in the Dharma, the most honored among those free from desire; I vow to take refuge in the Saṅgha, the most honored among those who have tamed themselves. These Three Jewels are the objects of my refuge. I, [such-and-such], a lay devotee (upāsaka) of pure faith, beg the ācārya to hold me in mind and protect me. From this day and this very moment, arousing a pure mind, until this night has passed and until the sun first rises at the dawn of the following morning, during that interval I shall uphold the Eight Precepts: namely, (1) not killing living beings, (2) not stealing, (3) not engaging in conduct that is not brahmacarya, (4) not speaking falsely, (5) not drinking intoxicants, (6) not eating at improper times, (7) not adorning the body with garlands and flowers, and not engaging in singing, dancing, or entertainments, and (8) not sitting or lying on high, wide, or large beds. I now renounce all such things and vow never to abandon the eight virtuous qualities of the pure precepts."

This is said two or three times. Then one says: "I uphold the practice of the precepts, adorn the mind, cause the mind to rejoice, and broadly cultivate all appropriate and excellent practices, seeking to attain the fruit of Buddhahood and bring it to perfect completion."

Then the following gāthās are recited:

> I arouse the supreme non-dual mind,
> As an unsolicited friend to all sentient beings.
> Practicing excellently the path of supreme bodhi,
> Upon becoming a Buddha I shall bring broad benefit to the world.
> May I, by means of this wholesome karma,
> Before long in this very life attain perfect awakening,
> Teach the Dharma for the benefit of the world,
> And explain to sentient beings the suffering of the three realms of becoming.
