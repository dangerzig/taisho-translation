# Secret Dhāraṇī Methods of the Immovable Messenger (不動使者陀羅尼祕密法)

## Taisho T1202

**Translator:** Vajrabodhi (金剛智 Jīngāngzhì), Tang dynasty
**Fascicles:** 1

*Note: This is an esoteric ritual manual for practices centered on Acalanatha (不動明王 Bùdòng míngwáng, "Immovable Wisdom King"), described here as the messenger (使者 shǐzhě) of Vairocana Buddha. The text covers four preliminary austerities, homa fire-offering methods, three different image-painting protocols, elaborate mudra (seal) and mantra sequences, and instructions for summoning spirit-attendants called kimkara (矜羯羅 jīnjiēluó) and cetaka (制吒迦 zhìzhājiā). Vajrabodhi (671-741) was one of the "three great masters" of Tang-dynasty esoteric Buddhism.*

---

## [Preliminary Practices]

These are the methods for accomplishing all beneficial results through the heart of the holy Immovable One of Measureless Power, the messenger of Vairocana.

One who wishes to uphold these practices should first perform four kinds of diligent austerities to discipline body and mind, keeping one's thoughts from scattering and one's resolve firm, so as to quickly obtain verification and fulfill one's vows. What are the four kinds of self-discipline? First, fasting from food. Second, subsisting on breath (服氣 *fúqì*). Third, eating only vegetables. Fourth, reducing food intake.

Having disciplined oneself according to one's ability, one should exclusively recite the root dhāraṇī until one completes one *laksa* (洛叉 *luòchā*, 100,000 recitations) or up to three *laksas*. Then, for one full day and night, taking not even water, one should make extensive offerings and adorn the ritual space. Before the painted image, burn pieces of chinaberry wood (苦練木 *kǔliàn mù*), each the size of a thumb and twelve finger-widths long, with both ends dipped in ghee. With each recitation of the spell, burn one piece. When 1,008 pieces have been burned, minor worldly matters will be fulfilled.

Furthermore, after the recitation count is complete, one should enter a river, ocean, or great waterway, standing chest-deep, facing east. Reciting daily until three *laksas* are completed, all cherished fields of merit in one's heart will be fulfilled. The water must be a place free from crocodiles, dragons, and fierce creatures, for if the spell's power is not yet accomplished, one may be harmed by such beings. One must therefore establish a protective boundary.

If one constantly increases one's diligent recitation, the Immovable Messenger will appear in person. His power can bind all ghosts and spirits. He can also shatter all trees, cause flying birds to fall from the sky at one's thought, and dry up dragon pools. In debates and confrontations with heretics and evil people, all can be subdued.

Furthermore, one should watch for the time when a lunar eclipse is about to occur and complete one's recitation count. For one day and night before the eclipse, do not eat. Take fresh cow-dung that has not yet touched the ground and make a square altar two cubits across. "Not touched the ground" has two meanings: first, catching it in a vessel so it does not reach the ground; second, even if it falls, taking just the upper portion before the dust contaminates it, and using that to plaster the altar. After plastering the altar, scatter and offer various fine flowers upon it. Place the *Mahaprajnaparamita Sūtra* at the center. Take milk from a cow whose calf is the same color as its mother, make it into yogurt, and extract one *liang* of ghee from it by churning. Place this in a cooked-copper bowl, and use a *karira*-wood spatula to stir the ghee. From the moment the eclipse begins, commence recitation until one of three signs appears: warmth, smoke, or flame. If the warmth sign appears, consuming the ghee will cure all diseases. If the smoke sign appears, smearing it on one's body will render one invisible, so that evil people cannot see you. If the flame sign appears, consuming the ghee will grant supernatural powers and the ability to fly through the air: this is the power of bodily supernormal travel. Regarding the calf-milk: the calf must be over one year old, as large as its mother yet still nursing, and its coat must be the same color as the mother's. Only such milk is suitable for making the ghee.

Furthermore, after the practitioner has completed one *laksa* of recitations, he should go to the summit of a high mountain deep in the wilderness. Abstaining from grains and food, he should recite another *laksa*, with mind continuously focused and undistracted by other concerns. Heavenly hidden treasures will then naturally appear. There are three kinds of hidden treasures: celestial, divine, and human. Treasures buried by humans are called great human treasures. Those guarded by ghosts and spirits are called divine treasures, also called earth treasures. Those protected by the devas are called celestial treasures. If even celestial treasures can be seen, how much more so earth treasures and human treasures! One should use them for meritorious deeds and employ them as one wishes.

## [Homa Fire Offering Methods]

Furthermore, according to the *homa* (護魔 *hùmó*) ritual method: fill a ladle with cow's milk, and with each recitation of the spell, pour one offering into the fire. When 1,000 recitations are completed, this can eliminate great plagues throughout the land. The *homa* method involves digging a pit in the ground to make a furnace and kindling a blazing fire. The ladle should be carved from hard wood, with a bowl-shaped head capable of holding an amount of milk no larger than an egg-yolk, and a straight handle about two feet long. Keep the milk in a separate clean vessel, and use this ladle to dip and pour while reciting the spell. In India, plagues would sometimes infect an entire household, with all who caught the disease dying. The same occurs in the Wu, Shu, and Lingnan regions of China. This method can control such epidemics.

Furthermore, take flowers of a hundred varieties and mix them with ghee, yogurt, and honey. With each recitation, burn one offering. Whatever color of clothing one desires will correspond to the color of flowers: if one seeks crimson, burn red flowers; others follow accordingly. Though the inconceivable spiritual power fulfills the practitioner's wishes, one should measure one's station and request proportionally, and there will be no future difficulties. If one greedily takes what is beyond one's station, the spirit will despise the person, and though one obtains something, it will not be valued.

If one burns *milava* fruit for one *laksa*, with a recitation for each one, one will obtain the highest official position in the land and accomplish whatever is within one's capability. *Milava* is a foreign fruit.

Furthermore, burning *pityang* fish-flowers brings the love and delight of all people. Burning pine-wood anointed with the three substances and reciting 100,000 times brings an immeasurable retinue of followers. The pine-wood should be seven inches long and finger-thick, split open. Burning barley while reciting brings wealth, nobility, and sovereignty for a great hero. Barley here means horse-barley, the kind with husks.

## Section One: Image-Painting Method for Subjugation

If one wishes to perform rituals, one should face the image, for what the mind envisions, the spirit responds to through the image. On fine silk, paint the Immovable Messenger wearing red-colored garments with a diagonal scarf, with a red waist-cloth, a single topknot on the left side hanging down to the ear, the left eye slightly squinting to the side, the left hand holding a lasso and the right hand holding a sword standing upright. The sword-head should be shaped like a lotus petal, the sword-hilt inlaid with jewels, and he should be seated upon a precious stone. With knitted brows and glaring eyes, his body a reddish-yellow color, in a wrathful posture that causes all beings to feel fear and dread.

After painting this image, one should go to a pure hermitage on the bank of a river or seashore, or within a clean room. The practitioner should purify his body and wear red-colored clothing, visualizing his entire body as red-colored, and silently beg for food without speaking. When five *laksas* of recitations are completed, take soybean stalks, five inches long, cut into 10,000 pieces. Burn them silently, anointed with the three substances, before this image with complete sincerity. The Immovable Messenger will then appear in person for the practitioner to see. Upon seeing him, one attains the Tathāgata's samādhi-mind and dwells constantly with the bodhisattvas.

Furthermore, before this image, recite the root mantra three times daily for six months, making offerings of flowers, incense, food, and drink according to one's ability. All kinds of wishes will be fulfilled.

If enemy soldiers or bandits approach, the practitioner should hold a banner in hand, recite 1,000 times, and plant it at the point of their approach. The invaders will naturally retreat, fleeing in terror. For enemies who damage the country or harm the Buddha-dharma, mix salt and earth together to make their likeness, inscribe their name on the heart of the figure, make it one cubit long, and recite the spell over it. With each recitation, cut off and burn one segment until the entire figure is consumed. If that person does not submit, they will certainly die.

Taking *mandara* leaves and burning them while reciting, calling the evil person's name once per recitation for 1,000 recitations, that person will certainly lose their mind. Burning cow's milk for 1,000 recitations will restore them to their original state. Burning salt while calling a person's name, one recitation per burning for 1,000 recitations, will summon anyone within a thousand *li* to come. Burning *asa* incense three times daily without interruption will obtain the highest position in the land. All the above methods are performed before this painted image to achieve their results.

## Section Two: Another Image-Painting Method

First, paint Śākyamuni Buddha (釋迦牟尼佛 *Shìjiāmóuní fó*) in the center. On the left, paint the youth Manjusri Bodhisattva (曼殊室利童子菩薩 *Mànshū shìlì tóngzǐ púsà*). On the right, paint Vajrapani Bodhisattva (執金剛菩薩 *Zhí jīngāng púsà*) with a beautiful smile, holding a vajra in the right hand. At the bottom, paint the Immovable Messenger adorned with precious jeweled ornaments.

Before this painted image, complete five *laksas* of recitations, and then perform various ritual tasks. Take 100,000 lotus stems, anoint each one with ghee, honey, and yogurt, recite the spell once over each flower, and then burn it. When all the lotuses have been burned, the auspicious Laksmi (蓮華吉祥天 *Liánhuá Jíxiáng Tiān*) will appear in person and ask the practitioner: "What is your wish? Whatever you seek, all will be fulfilled."

Taking 100,000 *sumanas* flowers, with one recitation and burning of each until all are consumed, a *yaksini* (female yaksa) will appear in person and can be directed to various tasks.

If someone in a terrifying and dangerous situation comes seeking help, take ash from a charnel ground, recite over it seven times, and give it to them for bodily protection, and they will attain peace and ease. Taking ox-bezoar, reciting over it seven times before the image, and mixing it with pure water to make a single dot on the forehead will cause all who see that person to feel delight, and will subdue all Vinayaka (毘那夜迦 *pínàyèjiā*) demons. For snake, scorpion, and similar poisons, make mud from clean earth, recite over it seven times, and apply it to the painful wound; the cure will be immediate.

## Section Three: Separate Method for Painting the Messenger

If one wishes to see the Immovable Messenger and have all manner of worldly affairs fulfilled to one's satisfaction, paint the Immovable Messenger with a reddish-yellow body, an upper garment with a blue diagonal scarf, a red lower skirt, a single topknot on the left side the color of black clouds, with the appearance of a youth. In the right hand he holds a vajra and in the left hand a lasso. From both sides of the mouth, small fangs slightly protrude. His wrathful eyes are red, and he sits on a stone mountain amid flames. Before this painted image, one performs various seal-formings and recitations, and all will be accomplished: emitting light, becoming invisible, binding all ghosts and spirits.

Even without a painted image, one may practice in any clean place or in a temple, in a quiet room where no one will disturb. For all worldly ailments of ghosts, spirits, and malarial fevers, reciting seven times or up to twenty-one times will without exception bring immediate cure.

Before this painted image, plaster the ground with clean mud, burn *asa* incense, and place a clear mirror at the center of one's chest. While adding recitations, have a young child look into the mirror and ask what they see. The child will then speak of the matters being sought. To summon dragon-spirits, simply obtain their names and station a pure boy or girl to stand while reciting the spell over them. The spirits will enter the child's mind and converse with the practitioner about matters of the three times, answering all questions.

### Summoning the Kimkara

If one wishes to accomplish the summoning of a *kimkara* (矜羯羅 *jīnjiēluó*), begin on the first day of the new moon. Rise early, purify oneself, and scatter flowers before the painted image. Plaster the ground with sandalwood powder to make an altar, and recite 108 times. At noon and dusk, recite 108 more each time; reciting more continuously is best. If at other times one cannot recite, simply sit in the ritual space with single-minded right thought, and recite when the time comes. On the fifteenth day, when the count is full, prepare various foods and drinks as offerings. Before the painted image, dig a pit one cubit square and one hand-span deep, and burn *arka*-wood (or chinaberry-wood if unavailable). Take one *dou* and five *sheng* of white mustard seeds, and from dusk onward, take an apricot-kernel-sized amount of mustard seeds, recite one time, and throw them into the fire. Mix the mustard seeds with ghee so they are moist. Continue burning and reciting in this way. After midnight, the *kimkara* will appear and say: "What tasks do you need performed?" The practitioner should reply: "I need the *kimkara* from this day forward, whenever there are matters to inquire about, to always follow me and not go elsewhere."

"*Kim*" means "to inquire about matters." "*Kara*" means "to carry out tasks."

If the spirit does not appear, recite with determined mind to the Immovable Messenger; you will certainly see it. Do not have doubts. Continue straight through until dawn, and without fail it will come. Once it appears, you may direct it to various tasks. It can even wash your hands or fetch willow branches for you. If you wish to ascend to heaven or enter the mountains, it will also carry the practitioner there.

If one wishes to see the celestial women of the Desire Realm, it can bring them for a meeting. How much more so for obtaining people and objects in the human world, as well as all manner of food and drink.

This spirit takes the form of a small child, and there are two kinds: one called *kimkara*, who is respectful and careful; and one called *cetaka* (制吒迦 *zhìzhājiā*), who is difficult to speak with and has a bad temper. Like ill-tempered servants in the human world, though they carry out tasks, they constantly make mistakes.

When there are no tasks, say to it "Go for now and come back later." Do NOT say "Nothing to do, you may go." If you say "Nothing to do, you may go," it will leave permanently and never return. This is the most important point to remember and must not be forgotten. In India, there was a monk who had directed one for many years. One day he misspoke, and it never came again. He wept and regretted bitterly, but it never returned.

### Summoning the Kulika Dragon King

If one wishes to employ the Kulika Dragon King (古力迦龍王 *Gǔlìjiā lóngwáng*), paint a sword on the wall with the Kulika Dragon King coiled around it. The dragon takes the form of a serpent. In the middle of the sword, write the syllable "A." In your mind, also visualize this sword and syllable with perfect clarity, and while mentally focusing on the Immovable Messenger, recite 108 times. Three times daily for six months; more recitations are better. After the full months have passed, the Kulika Dragon King will manifest in human form, constantly following and available for direction.

## Root Mantra of the Immovable Messenger

[Dhāraṇī]

*Namah samanta-vajranam vikrta-vikritamaha-bhairava-dista-cista-hara antara-mara-dhara caturmukha-svara naraka-kapilu-dhi vajra-kesam hum vajra-vajri garha hum phat svaha*

One who upholds this mantra should, for six months, recite it three times daily without interruption, eating only clean food each day. Before each meal, set aside one portion in a clean vessel, recite over it twenty-one times, and after finishing one's own meal, pour the offering-food in a clean place. After the full months, the Immovable Messenger will fulfill all manner of wishes. The three times are: morning, noon, and dusk. Each session consists of 108 recitations. This is called the method of upholding the root mantra. The heart-mantra and food-offering mantra follow separately.

## [Nature of the Immovable Messenger]

This Immovable Messenger is a transformation-body of Vairocana Buddha (毘盧遮那佛 *Pílúzhēnà fó*). Once one has upheld his practice, he will protect one life after life. If one seeks the unsurpassed, world-transcending bodhi, one should maintain pure celibate conduct and be single-mindedly diligent. One will attain various inconceivable samadhis, inconceivable realms, inconceivable supernatural powers, inconceivable eloquence, and inconceivable power and function. Such matters are known only through direct realization and cannot be fully described. For worldly people whose habitual tendencies are not yet severed, even if they commit various grave offenses a thousand times, the Messenger will always permit their repentance and will not immediately abandon them.

## Mudra Sequences

**Boundary-Setting and Body-Protection: the Conch-Shell Seal.** With the left hand, the middle, ring, and little fingers grip the same fingers of the right hand. Each thumb presses its own ring finger. Clasp them, then raise the right index finger straight, and bend the left index finger to press the second joint of the right index finger. Place it at the mouth and recite the root mantra seven times. Circle it three times clockwise above the head. The boundary is set to whatever distance the mind determines; nothing can violate it.

**The Armor Seal.** Join both hands, with the two index fingers and two ring fingers interlaced inside the palms. Raise the two thumbs, two middle fingers, and two little fingers straight up touching each other, forming the shape of a three-pronged vajra. This is called the Armor Seal. After reciting the root mantra seven times, touch the seal first to the forehead, then the right shoulder, then the left shoulder, then the heart, then the throat: these five points to protect the body. Before every recitation session, first perform this body-protection method. Also do it before going out.

**The Sword Seal.** With the left hand, press the thumb against the tips of the ring and little fingers, and extend the index and middle fingers straight as a sword-sheath. With the right hand, press the thumb against the nails of the ring and little fingers, extend the middle and index fingers straight as a sword, and insert it into the left palm. This is called the Sword Seal. Place it at the heart with the middle fingertip pointing straight up, and recite the Immovable Messenger's All-Evil-Repelling Mantra seven times. When done, move and place it atop the head. The mantra says:

[Dhāraṇī]

*Om acarakana-bhuta-cetaka hum hum ka-ika-i itam gairihi maha-ribhi-sa-sata ahri-cim phat*

If someone has consumed poison and is near death, forming this seal and reciting seven times will give immediate relief. For setting boundaries, repelling ghosts and spirits, or dispersing evil storm-clouds, place the seal on the left knee, then draw it out forcefully like unsheathing a sword. Circle it three times clockwise above the head at whatever distance desired, then stand the sword upright at the point between the eyebrows for a moment and stop. Nothing will dare to violate. Before reciting, first form this seal, then open the hands to take up the beads and proceed with regular recitation.

**The Fearlessness-Purification Seal.** With the right hand, press the upper joint of the thumb against the nail of the index finger, and extend the remaining three fingers straight. Recite seven times. To purify any incense, flowers, or other offerings, dip these three fingers in clean water and sprinkle; they are instantly purified. If a frightened person comes seeking protection, form this seal beneath your robe and speak their name seven times with the recitation. That person will immediately be free of fear. This is called the Fearlessness-Purification Seal.

**Invitation Mantra of the Immovable One.** [Dhāraṇī] Place the Sword Seal atop the head. Bend the left middle finger three times and recite the invitation mantra three times to beckon; the Messenger will immediately come.

**The Lasso Seal and Mantra.** [Dhāraṇī] After reciting seven times, this is used to subdue all ghosts and spirits, and to summon people from one or two thousand *li* away, as well as devas, nagas, and the eight classes of beings.

**The Lion's Powerful Advance Seal.** Form the Armor Seal and extend both index fingers straight and apart. Stand in a vajra stance, wave the seal left and right, with glaring eyes and wrathful intent, making the sound "hum" while reciting the Lion Mantra: [Dhāraṇī] Recite seven times. This can subdue all evil demons. Using the seal against demonic evil clouds and rain will immediately scatter them. After scattering, release the seal. If evil wind and rain do not stop, take thorns and mix them with white mustard seeds, burn them while reciting 108 times, then recite the root mantra 108 more times. Not only will the wind and rain stop, but the dragon-spirits will come to protect the practitioner.

**The Root Heart-Mantra Seal and Eye Seal.** First form the Eye Seal: with the right hand, grip the thumb with the ring and little fingers, extend the index and middle fingers at the forehead between the eyebrows, and draw them downward then toward the hairline and upward. This is called the Immovable Messenger's Celestial Eye Seal. Recite the heart-mantra: [Dhāraṇī] *Om ciraku-bhavaya svaha*. With wrathful intent, making the sound "hum," call out the enemy's name. A great spirit will seize that person's heart and force submission. If one constantly recites without interruption, one will certainly attain the divine eye, seeing throughout the three-thousand great-thousand worlds and the three realms as clearly as if before one's eyes.

**The Root Heart Seal.** Join both palms, interlace all ten fingers inside the palms, extend the two index fingertips touching each other, and hook the two thumbs over the two ring-finger nails. This is the Root Seal. Heart-mantra: [Dhāraṇī]

Every day before eating, set aside a portion of various foods in a small vessel. After one's own meal, recite seven times over it and pour it in a clean place. Day after day, wherever one goes, one will always be followed and protected.

**One-Syllable Mantra:** [Dhāraṇī] Use the Root Seal with recitation. Constantly reciting and forming the seal without interruption, the Messenger will also never depart from one's side.

**The Immovable Precious Mountain Seal.** Interlace all ten fingers inward and grip tightly into a fist. This is called the Immovable Precious Mountain Seal.

**The Head Seal.** With the right hand, tuck the thumb into the palm, and grip the four fingers into a fist. Place it atop the crown; this is called the Head Seal.

**The Single Topknot Seal.** From the Head Seal, extend the index and middle fingers together. Place it on the left side of the crown, then draw it downward past the ear; this is the Messenger's Single Topknot Seal.

**The Mouth Seal.** Place both hands side by side. Cross the two little fingers, bend the two ring fingers to grip the second joint of the little fingers, and press the two thumbs onto the ring-finger nails. Touch the two middle fingertips together, and bend the two index fingers to press the third joint of the middle fingers (counting from the palm upward). Place this seal over the mouth; this is called the Mouth Seal.

**The Heart Seal.** From the Mouth Seal, bend the two index fingers into the base of the two thumbs. Place it on the heart, forming a single-pronged vajra shape; this is called the Heart Seal.

**The Flame Seal.** With the right hand, press the thumb onto the little-finger nail. With the left hand, grip the thumb into a fist and extend the index finger into the right palm. Sweep from the right side over the head toward the left, like a swirling halo of light; this is called the Flame Seal.

**The Fire-Blocking Seal.** Place both hands side by side, each gripping the thumb into a fist. Bend both index fingers into the base of the thumbs, then bring the fists together side by side. This can eliminate all fire calamities.

All the above seals should be used in sequence during each recitation session while continuously reciting the mantra. Once one has accomplished the root mantra, the remaining mantras and seals will be effective upon recitation and forming alone, without requiring further separate practice.

**The Comprehensive Compassionate-Rescue Immovable Mantra:** [Dhāraṇī] This mantra comes from the *Mahavairocana Sūtra* and can incorporate all the seal-methods. After completing the preceding methods, recite this mantra seven or more times while visualizing the Immovable One, and also form the Sword Seal, touching the forehead, left and right shoulders, heart, and throat (five points). Recite this mantra 108 times, then visualize oneself in the form of Kumara (a youth), snap one's fingers, and disperse.

## [Charnel Ground Practice]

Furthermore, there is a method: in a charnel ground, take clothing from a dead person and paint the Immovable Messenger on it, using blood from the practitioner's own body to consecrate it. Place the image facing west, with the practitioner facing east. Before this image, recite. Three times daily, bathe and wear wet garments, and silently recite one *laksa*. When complete, on the twenty-third of the month, prepare a feast for all ghosts and spirits. The food method: take various rice, various beans, sesame, and mix them all together to make rice, and scatter it in the eight directions. Fast for one day and night. Find a corpse with all features intact, wash it clean, clothe it, and lay it supine before the painted image with feet facing west. Burn incense, protect one's own body, and establish boundaries on all four sides. The practitioner sits upon the dead person's chest and recites. When 10,000 recitations are complete, the corpse will move. Do not be afraid; simply press down firmly and recite urgently. When the corpse spits a lotus flower from its mouth, take and hold the flower. One's body will then transform into the appearance of a fifteen- or sixteen-year-old youth, and one will ascend through the air, reaching even the Brahma heavens, traveling freely everywhere.

This is a separate practice method. If one is in a dangerous or fearful place, or oppressed by official matters, one should single-mindedly recite the root mantra. One may also write the mantra and drive it into the ground in one's courtyard; no evil will dare stir. Writing the compassionate-rescue mantra works even better.

Simply in a clean room, set boundaries and protect the body, fix the mind on the Immovable Messenger, and recite without interruption. The more one recites the better, up to ten *laksas*. At each meal, set aside one portion to offer to the Immovable Messenger, and mentally maintain diligent visualization without interruption. This is the most fundamental method and yields the swiftest verification. The benefits will come naturally; do not speak of them to others. One *laksa* is 100,000 recitations. The essential methods of the Immovable Messenger are fully contained herein. Below: receive in faith and practice.

## Method for Fulfilling Wishes [Appendix]

If good men and women, monks and nuns, encounter calamity, seek official appointment, wish to meet a person of rank, or have requests to make, they should burn the five incenses and recite until reaching 100,000 without interruption. If time is short and the wish urgent, many people may recite together, combining their counts toward the total, and the wish will likewise be fulfilled. The spell says:

[Dhāraṇī: invocation of the Bodhisattva of Returning Light, Bodhisattva of Returning Joy, the great deva Anuttara, the Bodhisattva of Aspiring Virtue, and others, requesting that the petitioner's official, legal, or other matters be resolved, and that all buddhas, bodhisattvas, and foreign arhats rescue and aid them.]

The method of practice: first memorize the spell thoroughly and recite it silently, without the voice emerging. When 100,000 recitations are completed, one's wish will be obtained. If one seeks quick results, recite while walking and standing without interruption. For major matters, have many people recite together. When going out, incense is not required. When sitting at home to recite, one must burn the five incenses: *asa* incense (安息香), *lingling* incense (零陵香), *patchouli* (霍香), aloeswood (沈香), and frankincense (熏陸香). If aloeswood is unavailable, white sandalwood may substitute. But *asa* incense, *lingling*, and patchouli must not be omitted.
