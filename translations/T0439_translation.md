# 佛說諸佛經
## Sūtra on the Buddhas Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0439

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dānapāla (施護)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the city of Rājagṛha, on Vulture Peak Mountain. At that time, the Venerable Mahāmaudgalyāyana, as the time for the meal was approaching, donned his robes and took up his bowl, about to go into the royal city. He suddenly had this thought: "Today, I shall first go to the Akaniṣṭha heaven to inquire about a small matter, and then return to beg for food." Thereupon the Venerable Mahāmaudgalyāyana entered samādhi and, in the time it takes a strong man to flex and extend his arm, arrived at the Akaniṣṭha heaven. Having arrived, he met with the devas there, and they exchanged various gentle words of greeting. Then the Venerable Mahāmaudgalyāyana asked the devas: "How long ago did a Buddha, World-Honored One, appear in the world and realize unsurpassed bodhi?" A hundred Akaniṣṭha heavenly lords answered in unison: "After a full hundred thousand kalpas, a Buddha appears in the world and realizes unsurpassed bodhi."

At that time, the Venerable Mahāmaudgalyāyana, having heard and accepted this, entered samādhi. In the time it takes a strong man to flex and extend his arm, he returned from the Akaniṣṭha heaven to the royal city. As was his custom, he went begging in regular order. When the meal was finished and he had washed and rinsed, he put away his robes and bowl and went to the Buddha. He bowed his head to both of the Buddha's feet, and having paid his respects, sat to one side. With palms joined and gazing up, he addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One, today as the meal-time was approaching, I entered samādhi and went to the Akaniṣṭha heaven. I asked the devas there: 'How long ago did a Buddha appear in the world and realize unsurpassed bodhi?' A hundred heavenly lords answered me in unison: 'After a full hundred thousand kalpas, a Buddha appears in the world and realizes unsurpassed bodhi.' World-Honored One! Though I believe and accept this, what is the truth of this matter? I beg the World-Honored One to explain it for me."

At that time, the World-Honored One told Mahāmaudgalyāyana: "Listen carefully now, and I shall explain it for you. Mahāmaudgalyāyana! Those Akaniṣṭha heavenly lords know and see very little. They told you: 'After a full hundred thousand kalpas, a Buddha appears in the world and realizes unsurpassed bodhi.' This is not correct; it should not be deeply believed. Mahāmaudgalyāyana! I recall that in the distant past, the very first Buddhas I encountered were sixty koṭi Tathāgatas who appeared in the world. These Buddhas each remained in the world to benefit beings for a longer or shorter time, and following their respective conditions, entered nirvāṇa in succession. After that, there were again eighty koṭi Buddhas who appeared in the world, all sharing the name Wondrous Flower. Before each and every one of these Buddhas, I made great vows and practiced the holy life. After these Buddhas, five hundred Buddhas appeared in the world, all sharing the name Correct Brahmā. After these Buddhas, eight hundred Buddhas appeared in the world, all sharing the name Dīpaṃkara. After these Buddhas, fifteen thousand Buddhas appeared in the world, all sharing the name Samyaksambuddha. After these Buddhas, one thousand Buddhas appeared in the world, each with different names and clan names. Before these Buddhas I again made vows and cultivated the holy life. After these Buddhas, six thousand Buddhas appeared in the world, all sharing the name Supratipanna. After these Buddhas, ninety thousand Buddhas appeared in the world, all sharing the name Wondrous Kāśyapa. After these Buddhas, one thousand Buddhas appeared in the world, all sharing the name Sun. After these Buddhas, one thousand Buddhas appeared in the world, all sharing the name Dyutimat. After these Buddhas, eighty thousand Buddhas appeared in the world, all sharing the name Airavat. After these Buddhas, seventy thousand Buddhas appeared in the world, all sharing the name Indra. After these Buddhas, one Buddha appeared in the world named Virtue. Before this Buddha, together with all the śrāvaka disciples, I made vows, honored, and made offerings over many years. After this Buddha, eighty koṭi nayutas of pratyekabuddhas appeared in the world.

"Mahāmaudgalyāyana! Those pratyekabuddhas made great vows before me. There was also a cakravartin holy king named Pervading Radiance who, before me, first encountered forty Buddhas all sharing the name Invincible. Mahāmaudgalyāyana! After the Buddha named Virtue had entered nirvāṇa, I encountered one Buddha also named Invincible. During that Buddha's time, I was a cakravartin holy king named Hundred Names. Together with the śrāvaka disciples of the Invincible Buddha, I made vows, honored, and made offerings over many years. After the Buddha entered nirvāṇa, I used the seven treasures to build a stūpa and make offerings to his relics. Practicing in this way over an immeasurable time, Mahāmaudgalyāyana, I then attained bodhi."

"Furthermore, Mahāmaudgalyāyana! Those Akaniṣṭha devas know and see very little. After the Invincible Buddha's parinirvāṇa, yet another Buddha appeared in the world named Holder of the World. After him appeared a Buddha named Holder of the Earth. After him, a Buddha named Great Able Sage. After him, a Buddha named Wondrous Manifestation. After him, a Buddha named Lion. After him, a Buddha named Supreme Rarity. After him, a Buddha named Severing All Sorrow. After him, a Buddha named Accomplishing All Aims. After him, a Buddha named Victor. After him, a Buddha named Jewel Radiance. After him, a Buddha named Mind's Renown. After him, a Buddha named Upatiṣya. After him, a Buddha named Tiṣya. After him, a Buddha named Perfect Radiance. After him, a Buddha named Moonlight. After him, a Buddha named Deva Radiance. After him, a Buddha named Atibhu. After him, a Buddha named Atiyaśas. After him, a Buddha named Imperishable Powers. After him, a Buddha named Supreme Victor. After him, a Buddha named Tiṣyadaru. After him, a Buddha named Supreme Flower. After him, a Buddha named Ariṣṭa. After him, a Buddha named Atibhu. After him, a Buddha named Dīpaṃkara. After him, a Buddha named Vanquishing Enemies. After him, a Buddha named Golden Radiance. After him, a Buddha named Golden Light. After him, a Buddha named Jewel Eye. After him, a Buddha named Lotus Eye. After him, a Buddha named Supreme Lotus. After him, a Buddha named Great Lotus. After him, a Buddha named Lotus. After him, a Buddha named Vipaśyin. After him, a Buddha named Śikhin. After him, a Buddha named Viśvabhū. After him, a Buddha named Krakucchanda. After him, a Buddha named Kanakamuni. After him, a Buddha named Kāśyapa. And now I, Śākyamuni, have appeared in the world. Mahāmaudgalyāyana! Having first made vows, I made offerings to all these Buddhas, scattering seven-jeweled flowers, and only then did I accomplish samyak-saṃbodhi.

"Mahāmaudgalyāyana! Each and every one of these Buddhas, from the time of their first aspiration until they attained perfect awakening, passed through innumerable kalpas. The number of kalpas from my first aspiration until my present attainment of Buddhahood is extremely great, beyond calculation. Those Akaniṣṭha devas know and see very little. You should have no doubts."

At that time, when the World-Honored One had finished teaching this sūtra, Mahāmaudgalyāyana's doubts were immediately dispelled. Joyful and elated, he bowed to the Buddha and withdrew.
