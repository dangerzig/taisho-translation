# 囉嚩拏說救療小兒疾病經
## Sūtra Spoken by Rāvaṇa on Rescuing and Healing Childhood Illnesses

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1330

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmabhadra (法賢).

---

At that time Rāvaṇa surveyed all the children of the world, from newborn through twelve years of age. All being in the stage of infancy, foolish and unknowing, their vital spirit not yet sufficient, ghosts and sprites gained advantage over them. There were twelve luminous mother-ghosts who roamed the world, constantly watching for opportunities day and night. Whether the child was sleeping, or alone, walking, or sitting, at such moments these ghosts would manifest various different forms to frighten the children and cause them to lose their normal state. They would siphon off the child's vital essence, thereby causing illness and even early death. "Seeing these things, I was deeply moved to compassion. I shall now describe the twelve luminous mother-ghosts, the months and years and times at which they seize and possess children, the symptoms of the resulting illnesses, and the great vidyā methods for rescue and healing, as well as the ritual procedures for making offerings and sacrificial ceremonies. If anyone hears what I teach and there is one suffering from illness, if the vidyā-holder follows my method to perform the healing and approaches it with utmost sincerity, the patient will certainly attain ease, happiness, and auspiciousness. The names of the twelve luminous mother-ghosts are: Mātṛnandā, Sunandā, Revatī, Mukhamaṇikā, Viṇālikā, Śakunī, Pūtanā, Śuṣkā, Āryakā, Jambhakā, Piṅgikā, and Skandha.

"These twelve luminous mother-ghosts possess children in order to obtain sacrificial offerings. I shall now describe the symptoms of each one's possession. If a child is possessed on its birth day, birth month, or birth year, it is the mother-ghost Mātṛnandā who has seized it. The child first suffers from alternating chills and fever; the body becomes thin and weak, gradually withering; the mind becomes confused and disturbed; the body constantly trembles; and the child cries and refuses to eat. The vidyā-holder should take earth from both banks of a river and fashion an image of the sick child, placing it facing west within a four-directional maṇḍala. In the maṇḍala, set out various incenses and flowers, and white-colored food and drink, as well as wine and meat. Set up seven banners and light seven lamps. Take white mustard seed, fox dung, cat dung, benzoin incense, and snake skin. Combine these medicines with yellow cow's ghee to make incense and fumigate the child. Take castor-bean plant, sesame, vitex, or its leaves, pippali tree leaves, and vāraka medicine. Boil these five medicines in water and bathe the child. Then recite the great vidyā, empowering the sacrificial food and various items within the maṇḍala. The great vidyā:

*Oṃ namo rāvaṇāya trailokya-vidra-paṇāya vajriṇa-brahma-nandani-namāri-karūpe-ṇa mātṛnandā vara-vara śuṣka-śuṣka pañca-pañca kumāra-ghaṃ svāhā.*

"After reciting and empowering the great vidyā, go outside the city. At midday, facing east, make the sacrificial offering to the mother-ghost Mātṛnandā. Discard all the sacrificial food, incense, flowers, and other items wherever convenient. The sick child will quickly be cured."

"Next, if a child becomes ill on the second day, second month, or second year after birth, it is the mother-ghost Sunandā who has seized it. The child first suffers from alternating chills and fever and displays a confused and disturbed appearance, keeping the eyes closed and refusing to eat, with the hands and feet contracting and clenching, pain in the belly, vomiting, and labored breathing. The vidyā-holder should take one dou of rice flour and fashion an image of the sick child, placing it facing west within the maṇḍala. In the maṇḍala, set out various supreme and fine incenses, flowers, food, drink, wine, and meat. Set up four white banners and light four lamps. Take benzoin incense, garlic, snake skin, white mustard seed, cat dung, and ghee; combine them to make incense and fumigate the child. As before, use the five-medicine water to bathe the child. Then recite the great vidyā, empowering the sacrificial food and various items within the maṇḍala." The great vidyā:

*Oṃ namo rāvaṇāya trailokya-vidra-paṇāya candra-hasa-dhāriṇī jvālita-hasa-tāya hana-hana daha-daha triśūle-na-nuṣṭha-graha-ni-kṛtāya-ni-kṛtāya juṣāya-juṣāya hana-hana sunandā pañca-pañca kumāra-ghaṃ svāhā.*

"After reciting and empowering the great vidyā, go outside the city. At the hour of the dog [7-9 PM], facing west, make the sacrificial offering to the mother-ghost Sunandā. Discard all the sacrificial food, incense, flowers, and other items wherever convenient. The sick child will quickly be cured."

"Next, if a child becomes ill on the third day, third month, or third year after birth, it is the mother-ghost Revatī who has seized it. The child suddenly becomes startled and fearful, screaming and crying, with bodily pain, irregular chills and fever, trembling of the head and face, turning to look at its own body while gradually becoming emaciated and weak, losing desire for food and drink until wasting away. The vidyā-holder should use various fine-flavored meat dishes, raw meat, raw fish, wine, and the like. Set up eight red banners and light eight lamps. Take vilva tree leaves, benzoin incense, snake skin, garlic, cat dung, and white mustard seed, and combine with ghee to fumigate the child. As before, use the five medicines boiled in water to bathe the child. Then recite the great vidyā, empowering the sacrificial food and various items within the maṇḍala." The great vidyā:

*Oṃ namo rāvaṇāya daśa-vadana candra-hasya-pra-jvālita-hasa-tāya hana-hana daha-daha mardanāya-mardanāya nuṣṭha-graha-moṭāya-moṭāya svāhā.*

"After reciting and empowering the great vidyā, go outside the city. At the hour of the dog, facing north, make the sacrificial offering to the mother-ghost Revatī. Discard all the sacrificial food and various items wherever convenient. The sick child will quickly be cured."

"Next, if a child becomes ill on the fourth day, fourth month, or fourth year after birth, it is the mother-ghost Mukhamaṇikā who has seized it. The child first suffers from alternating chills and fever, coughing and sneezing, vomiting, bodily trembling and drooping head, crying and scratching with the hands, looking about at people's faces with both eyes, losing desire for food and drink, and having excessive urination and bowel movements. The vidyā-holder should take earth from both banks of a river and fashion an image of the sick child, placing it facing west within the maṇḍala. Set out various incenses and flowers, raw and cooked meat dishes, wine, and fruit. Set up four red banners and light four lamps. Take tūla seeds, snake skin, cat dung, ox horn, tiger claws, and white mustard seed; combine them to make incense and fumigate the child. As before, use the five-medicine water to bathe the child. Then recite the great vidyā, empowering the sacrificial food and various items within the maṇḍala." The great vidyā:

*Oṃ namo brahma-vipanu-maheśvara-skanda-huta-śana mukhamaṇikā hana-hana mardanāya-mardanāya ni-kṛtāya-ni-kṛtāya khāhi-khāhi bhagavatī mukhamaṇikā svāhā.*

"After reciting and empowering the great vidyā, go outside the city. At the hour of the dog, facing south, make the sacrificial offering to the mother-ghost Mukhamaṇikā. Discard all the sacrificial food and various items wherever convenient. The sick child will quickly be cured."

"Next, if a child becomes ill on the fifth day, fifth month, or fifth year after birth, it is the mother-ghost Viṇālikā who has seized it. The child first suffers from mental confusion and distraction, frequent outbursts of anger, irregular chills and fever, coughing and sneezing, vomiting, sudden appearance of sores on the body resembling water blisters, gazing vacantly into space, losing desire for food and drink, gradually wasting away until the belly is sunken and no longer visible. The vidyā-holder should prepare white-colored food and drink, wine, meat, and the like. Set up five white banners and light five lamps, with various incenses and flowers. Take benzoin incense, garlic, snake skin, cat dung, and white mustard seed; combine with ghee to make incense and fumigate the child. As before, use the five-medicine water to bathe the child. Then recite the great vidyā, empowering the sacrificial food and various items within the maṇḍala." The great vidyā:

*Oṃ namo rāvaṇāya trailokāya-vidra-paṇāya viṇālikā-viṇālikā mokṣāya-mokṣāya hana-hana candra-hase-na pañca-pañca viṇālikā svāhā.*

"After reciting and empowering the great vidyā, go outside the city. At midday, facing west, make the sacrificial offering to the mother-ghost Viṇālikā. Discard all the sacrificial food and various items wherever convenient. The sick child will quickly be cured."

"Next, if a child becomes ill on the sixth day, sixth month, or sixth year after birth, it is the mother-ghost Śakunī who has seized it. The child first suffers from alternating chills and fever, sometimes laughing and sometimes crying, bodily trembling with a foul odor, losing desire for food and drink, and gradually wasting away. The vidyā-holder should take one dou of rice flour, fashion an image of the sick child, and place it facing west within the maṇḍala. Set out various incenses and flowers, food and drink, wine and meat, and also milk porridge. Set up four white banners and light four lamps. Take benzoin incense, garlic, snake skin, cat dung, white mustard seed, and ghee; combine them to make incense and fumigate the child. As before, use the five-medicine water to bathe the child. Then recite the great vidyā, empowering the sacrificial food and various items within the maṇḍala." The great vidyā:

*Oṃ namo bhagavate rāvaṇāya laṅkeśvarāya preta-vidra-paṇāya vajriṇa hana-hana śakunī pañca-pañca kumāra-ghaṃ svāhā.*

"After reciting and empowering the great vidyā, go outside the city. At the hour of the dog, facing south, make the sacrificial offering to the mother-ghost Śakunī. Discard all the sacrificial food and various items wherever convenient. The sick child will quickly be cured."

"Next, if a child becomes ill on the seventh day, seventh month, or seventh year after birth, it is the mother-ghost Pūtanā who has seized it. The child first suffers from alternating chills and fever with bodily pain, excessive urination and bowel movements, constantly clenching both fists, losing desire for food and drink, and gradually wasting away. The vidyā-holder should use kuśa grass to fashion an image of the sick child, and place it facing west within the maṇḍala. Use various red-colored flowers and prepare red-colored food and drink, wine, and meat. Set up eight white banners and light eight lamps. Take benzoin incense, snake skin, corpse hair, tiger claws, nimba tree leaves, cat dung, white mustard seed, and ghee; combine them to make incense and fumigate the child. As before, use the five-medicine water to bathe the child. Then recite the great vidyā, empowering the sacrificial food and various items within the maṇḍala." The great vidyā:

*Oṃ namo bhagavate rāvaṇāya laṅkeśvarāya preta-vidra-paṇāya kumāra-graha-ni-kṛtu hana-hana cūraṇḍa-cūraṇḍa svāhā.*

"After reciting and empowering the great vidyā, go outside the city. At the hour of the dog, facing west, make the sacrificial offering to the mother-ghost Pūtanā. Discard all the sacrificial food and various items wherever convenient. The sick child will quickly be cured."

"Next, if a child becomes ill on the eighth day, eighth month, or eighth year after birth, it is the mother-ghost Śuṣkā who has seized it. The child first suffers from alternating chills and fever with a confused and disturbed appearance, bodily pain, inability to see, drooping head without strength, foul odor from the body, and loss of desire for food and drink. The vidyā-holder should take one dou of rice flour and fashion a black ram, placing it with the head facing west within the maṇḍala. Use incense and flowers, milk porridge, fine-flavored food and drink, wine, and meat. Set up five white banners and light five lamps. Take benzoin incense, sajārasā, snake skin, garlic, white mustard seed, cat dung, and ghee; combine them to make incense and fumigate the child. As before, use the five-medicine water to bathe the child. Then recite the great vidyā, empowering the sacrificial food and various items within the maṇḍala." The great vidyā:

*Oṃ namo rāvaṇāya trailokāya-vidra-paṇāya jvala-jvala pra-jvala-pra-jvala hana-hana hūṃ phaṭ svāhā.*

"After reciting and empowering the great vidyā, go outside the city. At the hour of the dog, facing south, make the sacrificial offering to the mother-ghost Śuṣkā. Discard all the sacrificial food and various items wherever convenient. The sick child will quickly be cured."

"Next, if a child becomes ill on the ninth day, ninth month, or ninth year after birth, it is the mother-ghost Āryakā who has seized it. The child first suffers from alternating chills and fever, bodily trembling, crying, pain throughout the body, drooling and salivating from the mouth, vomiting without ceasing, sometimes drooping the head and neck, sometimes rolling the eyes upward, and losing desire for food and drink. The vidyā-holder should take one dou of rice flour and fashion a white ram, apply white sandalwood incense to the ram, and place it with the head facing west within the maṇḍala. Set out various incenses and flowers, fine-flavored food and drink, wine, and meat. Set up four white banners and light four lamps. Use snake skin to make incense and fumigate the child. As before, use the five-medicine water to bathe the child. Then recite the great vidyā, empowering the sacrificial food and various items within the maṇḍala." The great vidyā:

*Oṃ namo laṅkādhipataye laṅkeśvarāya hana-hana paca-paca hūṃ-hūṃ phaṭ svāhā.*

"After reciting and empowering the great vidyā, go outside the city. At the hour of the dog, facing north, make the sacrificial offering to the mother-ghost Āryakā. Discard all the sacrificial food and various items wherever convenient. The sick child will quickly be cured."

"Next, if a child becomes ill on the tenth day, tenth month, or tenth year after birth, it is the mother-ghost Jambhakā who has seized it. The child first suffers from alternating chills and fever, suddenly makes terrible sounds, vomiting without ceasing, with excessive urination and bowel movements, frequent pain in the eyes and teeth, and loss of desire for food and drink. The vidyā-holder should take earth from both banks of a river and fashion an image of the child, apply gorocanā to the image, and place it facing west within the maṇḍala. Set out various incenses and flowers, fine-flavored food and drink, wine, and meat. Take benzoin incense, chicken wings, ox horn, snake skin, human bone, cat dung, white mustard seed, and ghee; combine them to make incense and fumigate the child. As before, use the five-medicine water to bathe the child. Then recite the great vidyā, empowering the sacrificial food and various items within the maṇḍala." The great vidyā:

*Oṃ namo bhagavate vasunivāya rāmaṇa-bhima-rūpāya hana-hana gauḥ phaṭ svāhā.*

"After reciting and empowering the great vidyā, go outside the city. At the hour of the dog, facing south, make the sacrificial offering to the mother-ghost Jambhakā. Discard all the sacrificial food and various items wherever convenient. The sick child will quickly be cured."

"Next, if a child becomes ill on the eleventh day, eleventh month, or eleventh year after birth, it is the mother-ghost Piṅgikā who has seized it. The child first suffers from alternating chills and fever, bodily trembling, pain in the finger joints, crying, vomiting, loss of desire for food and drink, gazing upward into space, and gradually wasting away. The vidyā-holder should take one dou of black bean flour, fashion an image of the sick child, apply red sandalwood incense to it, and place it facing west within the maṇḍala. Divide the various incenses, flowers, food, drink, wine, and meat into twenty-five portions. Set up twenty-five banners and light twenty-five lamps. Take pigeon dung, pigeon and wagtail wings, human hair, ram horn, cat dung, and white mustard seed; combine with ghee to make incense and fumigate the child. As before, use the five-medicine water to bathe the child. Then recite the great vidyā, empowering the sacrificial food and various items within the maṇḍala." The great vidyā:

*Oṃ namo bhagavate rāvaṇāya candra-hasvāya-graha-hasa-tāya jvala-jvala pra-jvala-pra-jvala hana-hana nuṣṭha-gṛ-hasa svāhā.*

"After reciting and empowering the great vidyā, go outside the city. At the hour of the dog, facing west, make the sacrificial offering to the mother-ghost Piṅgikā. Discard all the sacrificial food and various items wherever convenient. The sick child will quickly be cured."

"Next, if a child becomes ill on the twelfth day, twelfth month, or twelfth year after birth, it is the mother-ghost Skandha who has seized it. The child first suffers from alternating chills and fever, glaring angrily at people with an appearance as if threatening them, clenching and contracting the hands and feet and contracting the belly, gradually wasting away, and losing desire for food and drink. The vidyā-holder should use barley flour to fashion an image of the child, and place it facing west within the maṇḍala. Set out various incenses and flowers, fine-flavored food and drink, wine, and meat. Set up eight red banners and light four lamps. Take ox horn, white mustard seed, benzoin incense, garlic, snake skin, cat dung, and ghee; combine them to make incense and fumigate the child. As before, use the five-medicine water to bathe the child. Then recite the great vidyā, empowering the sacrificial food and various items within the maṇḍala." The great vidyā:

*Oṃ namo rāvaṇāya tri-pura-vināśanāya nuṣṭha-ratna-kara-rāla-vasu-rāya candra-hase-na hana-hana mardana-mardana nandini-vidra-pāya-vidra-pāya skandha pañca-pañca kumāra-ghaṃ hūṃ-hūṃ phaṭ-phaṭ svāhā.*

"After reciting and empowering the great vidyā, go outside the city. At the hour of the dog, starting from the east and rotating through the four directions, make the sacrificial offering to the mother-ghost Skandha. Discard all the sacrificial food and various items wherever convenient. The sick child will quickly be cured."

At that time, Rāvaṇa, having spoken this Sūtra on Rescuing and Healing Childhood Illnesses, joyfully withdrew.
