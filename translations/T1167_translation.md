# 八大菩薩曼荼羅經
## Sūtra of the Maṇḍala of the Eight Great Bodhisattvas

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1167

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

Thus have I heard. At one time the Bhagavān was on Mount Potalaka, in the palace of the holy Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, surrounded by hundreds of thousands of koṭis of nayutas of bodhisattvas. At that time in the assembly there was a bodhisattva named Ratnacandra-prabhā, who rose from his seat, arranged his robes, bared his right shoulder, bowed, joined his palms, and said: "Bhagavān! I have a small question. May the Tathāgata hear and permit me to ask." Then the Bodhisattva Ratnacandra-prabhā said: "If good men and good women construct eight maṇḍalas, how should they be established? And upon what dharma should they rely to generate immeasurable merit, enabling practitioners to swiftly realize bodhi?"

At that time the Tathāgata praised the Bodhisattva Ratnacandra-prabhā: "Excellent, excellent! Good man! You are able to ask about such a profound meaning, for the benefit and happiness of immeasurable, boundless sentient beings, for the purification of the three evil destinies, and for the realization of the incomparable, unsurpassed wisdom. Listen well now. If any sentient being merely hears this secret mantra, they will attain longevity and happiness. Good man! There are eight maṇḍalas, which are the profoundly essential dharma of the eight great bodhisattvas. If any sentient being constructs these eight maṇḍalas in accordance with the dharma even once, all their ten evil acts, five heinous offenses, and slander of the vaipulya sūtras will be entirely extinguished, and all that they seek in benefit, advantage, and supreme aspiration will be accomplished." He then spoke the Tathāgata's secret mantra:

*Oṃ mahā-vīra svāhā*

"Within the maṇḍala, visualize the Tathāgata with a body of true gold, endowed with the thirty-two marks, seated upon a lotus pedestal."

Next he spoke the secret mantra of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva:

*Hūṃ hrīḥ haḥ padma-śrīye svāhā*

"Visualize within the maṇḍala the holy Avalokiteśvara with a red-colored body; the left hand holds a lotus and the right hand grants wishes. In his crown is Amitāyus Tathāgata."

Next he spoke the secret mantra of Maitreya Bodhisattva:

*Mahārīye svāhā*

"Behind Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, visualize Maitreya Bodhisattva with a golden body; the left hand holds a kuṇḍikā and the right hand grants fearlessness. In his crown is a stūpa, and he sits in half-lotus."

Next he spoke the secret mantra of Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva:

*Agha-bhaya svāhā*

"Behind the Buddha, visualize Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva; the left hand holds a jewel placed upon the heart, and the right hand grants a flow of immeasurable treasures."

Next he spoke the secret mantra of Samantabhadra Bodhisattva:

*Hrīḥ jaya svāhā*

"To the left of Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva, visualize Samantabhadra Bodhisattva wearing a five-buddha crown, with a golden body; the right hand holds a sword and the left hand grants wishes. He sits in half-lotus."

Next he spoke the secret mantra of Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva:

*Oṃ vaṃ-rava svāhā*

"To the left of the Tathāgata, visualize Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva; the right hand holds a vajra and the left hand rests upon the hip. He wears a five-buddha crown, his body is blue, and he sits in half-lotus."

Next he spoke the secret mantra of Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva:

*Śrī-jaṃga svāhā*

"Before Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva, visualize Mañjuśrī as a youth bodhisattva with five topknots; the left hand holds a blue lotus with a five-pronged vajra within the flower, and the right hand grants wishes. His body is golden, and he sits in half-lotus."

Next he spoke the secret mantra of Sarvanivaraṇaviṣkambhin Bodhisattva:

*Nivaraṇa svāhā*

"To the right of Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva, visualize Sarvanivaraṇaviṣkambhin Bodhisattva with a golden body; the left hand holds a cintāmaṇi-banner and the right hand grants wishes. He sits in half-lotus."

Next he spoke the secret mantra of Kṣitigarbha Bodhisattva:

*Kṣāharaja svāhā*

"Before the Tathāgata, visualize Kṣitigarbha Bodhisattva wearing a crown and necklaces, with a joyful and serene countenance, contemplating compassion for all sentient beings. The left hand rests below the navel holding a bowl, and the right hand faces downward with the thumb pressing the index finger, contemplating the comforting of all sentient beings."

"This is the method of offering and contemplation for the maṇḍala of the eight great bodhisattvas. If good men or good women receive and uphold this Sūtra of the Eight Maṇḍalas, all their karmic hindrances will be entirely extinguished, and they will swiftly realize unsurpassed samyak-saṃbodhi."

When the Buddha had spoken this sūtra, all the great bodhisattvas, the śrāvaka assembly, and all the devas, humans, and the eight classes of beings, hearing what the Buddha taught, joyfully accepted and put it into practice.

Praise of the Eight Great Bodhisattvas:

> He who can shatter the gates of nirvāṇa's palace-city,
> Dharma-enjoyment of all buddhas, savior of the world: to him I bow.

> From his own hands flows pure water, removing hungry ghosts' thirst;
> Wish-fulfilling tree of the three realms: to the Lotus-Handed One I bow.

> Great compassion-water as his heart, extinguishing the fire of anger;
> I bow to Lord Maitreya, who severs the bowstring of desire.

> Ākāśagarbha of wondrous wisdom, tranquil lord of space;
> Liberation from the stream of birth and death: to the Buddha's heart-son I bow.

> The boundless delusions of beings: he can still the unprofitable mind.
> To Samantabhadra I bow, foremost son of the Sugata.

> He exhausts the servants of defilement, transcends and conquers Māra's armies.
> To Vajrapāṇi I bow, proclaimer of all vidyās.

> I bow to Mañjuśrī, who holds the form of a wondrous youth,
> Spreading the lamp of wisdom everywhere, dispelling the darkness of the three realms.

> Remover of all hindrances, therefore I bow:
> Lord of inexhaustible wisdom, able to produce tireless eloquence.

> Like the earth on which all beings depend without cease,
> Treasury of firm wisdom and compassion: to Kṣitigarbha I bow.

> These true sons of the Sugata: the merit gained by praising them;
> May all beings thereby become vessels worthy of such praise.

Colophon: The positions of the eight [bodhisattvas] in this text are confused. They have now been corrected according to the directional positions set forth in the Uṣṇīṣavijayā Recitation Method in the Heng fascicle.
