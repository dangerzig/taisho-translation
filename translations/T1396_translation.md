# 佛說拔除罪障呪王經
## The Buddha's Teaching: Sūtra of the Mantra-King for Removing Sin-Obstacles

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1396

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Yijing (義淨).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Bhagavat was in the Śuddhāvāsa Heaven, teaching the wondrous and subtle Dharma for the great assembly. At that time Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, from within the great assembly, rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, placed his right knee on the ground, joined his palms toward the Buddha, gazed up at the Honored One's countenance without ever turning away, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! This great Dharma-gate is profound, subtle, and truly rare. However, in the future age of the five defilements, sentient beings of evil karma will be full of greed, hatred, and delusion. Deceitful and lacking faith, they will delight in practicing what is unwholesome, not cultivating the precepts, and devoting themselves to flattery and pretense. As for the majestic power, supernatural transformations, and miraculous displays of the mantra-Dharma, and the benefits and accomplishments of merit obtained by seekers in the various lands and regions, and the liturgies of recitation, the rites of ritual worship, they will refuse to believe or practice them. They will say these are not the Buddha's teaching and will slander them. With evil obstacles enveloping their minds, they will die and fall into the great hells, where they will suffer extreme torment with no prospect of escape. World-Honored One! Since these sentient beings have encountered such great suffering, how can they be rescued and protected? We humbly pray that you will compassionately deliver them." At that time Śākyamuni Tathāgata extended his hand and touched the crown of Mañjuśrī, the Dharma-Prince, and said: "Excellent! Mañjuśrī! Wishing to benefit all sentient beings, you are able to ask the Tathāgata about such a matter of profound import. Listen carefully and give your utmost attention. I shall now teach you this mantra-Dharma to bring peace and joy to devas and humans and benefit all. Among all mantra-Dharmas, it is the most supreme. It is secret and profoundly deep, accords with the path of awakening, and relies on the true Dharma-realm to obtain great merit. At the time of death, one will certainly be able to recall this great and mighty mantra-king. With right mindfulness present before one, one will be born in a wholesome destination." He then spoke the mantra:

*Namaḥ sarva-tathāgatebhyaḥ arhadbhyaḥ. Oṃ kumāra huru vīṇa viśva-saṃbhava agaccha agaccha raku raku valāṃ valāṃ haṃ haṃ. Śīna-śī Mañjuśrīya su-śrīya tārāya mā-sarva-duṣkebhyaḥ phaṭ phaṭ śama-ya śama-ya amṛtodbhava udbhava pāpa-pavinaśaya svāhā.*

"Mañjuśrī! This is your most supreme fundamental mantra-treasury, the heart-king divine mantra. It can eliminate all suffering, distress, and hardship. All evil sin-obstacles will be entirely destroyed. One will enjoy long life without illness, wealth and happiness without end, eloquence and intelligence in speech, and the love and respect of all people. It can also open the way to mastery of all mantra-kings." When Śākyamuni Tathāgata spoke these words, all the buddha-lands as well as this great earth with its mountains, rivers, streams, and seas, and the entire realm of sentient beings trembled with the six kinds of earthquakes. All sentient beings in the evil destinies, among the hungry ghosts, in the animal realm, in the domain of King Yama, and in the hells had their suffering and distress suspended and all obtained peace and joy.

The Buddha further addressed Mañjuśrī: "Whether devas or humans, whether male or female, whether householders or renunciants, if any sentient beings can single-mindedly receive and uphold this mantra-king, they will not be deceived or slandered by evil persons. Māras will find no opportunity, enemies will be unable to cause harm, and poisons will be unable to injure them. All obstacles and vināyakas will of themselves retreat and scatter. Those who receive and uphold it should give rise to this thought: 'The inconceivable Dharma of the buddhas, the world-honored ones, can be realized and known only by a buddha together with a buddha. How could I presume to give rise to slander?'"

At that time, when the World-Honored One had taught this Dharma, the entire great assembly together with the Śuddhāvāsa devas and all the other devas, numbering hundreds of thousands of koṭis, the devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, and the rest, the mantra-accomplished great sages, and Mañjuśrī the Dharma-Prince, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, Maitreya Bodhisattva, Mahāsthāmaprāpta Bodhisattva, Vajradhara Bodhisattva, and all such great bodhisattvas, as well as all the tathāgatas and bodhisattvas in the buddha-lands of asaṃkhyeya worlds, together bore witness: This great mantra-king is an inconceivable Dharma. It is a great divine mantra, a great vidyā-mantra, an unsurpassed mantra, an unequaled mantra. It can remove the obstacles of sin and suffering. It is true and not false. At that time the great assembly of devas, having heard the mantra-king proclaimed, all greatly rejoiced and reverently received and upheld it.
