# 呪五首
## Five Dhāraṇīs

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1034

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Xuanzang (玄奘)

---

### The Dhāraṇī That Extinguishes All Sins Through a Thousand Repetitions

[Dhāraṇī] *namaḥ ratnatrayāya | namo āryāvalokiteśvarāya bodhisattvāya mahāsattvāya mahākāruṇikāya | tadyathā | jaye jaye jayavāhinī jayottarī | kara kara mara mara cara cara | cini cini | sarvakarmāvaraṇi me | bhagavati sahasravarte | sarvabuddhāvalokite | cakṣuḥ-smṛti-grahaṇa-jihvākāya-mano-viśodhani | sura sura prasura prasura | sarvabuddhādhiṣṭhite svāhā | dharmadhātugarbhe svāhā | svabhāva-svabhāva-sarvadharmavibodhanī svāhā*

### The Six-Syllable Dhāraṇī

[Dhāraṇī] *namo āryamañjuśrīye | oṃ vakhe daṃ namo*

### The Dhāraṇī of the Seven Koṭi Buddhas

[Dhāraṇī] *namas saptānāṃ samyaksaṃbuddha-koṭīnāṃ | tadyathā | oṃ cale cule cunde svāhā*

### The Dhāraṇī Following the Mind of All Tathāgatas

[Dhāraṇī] *namo sarvatathāgata-hṛdayāya anugate | tadyathā | oṃ kuṭhagini svāhā*

### The Dhāraṇī Following the Mind of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva

[Dhāraṇī] *namo ratnatrayāya | namo āryāvalokiteśvarāya bodhisattvāya mahāsattvāya mahākāruṇikāya | tadyathā | oṃ tare tuttāre ture svāhā*

---

**Translator's note:** The Chinese text consists entirely of phonetic transcriptions of Sanskrit dhāraṇī formulae. The Sanskrit reconstructions above are based on Tang-period phonology and comparison with parallel recensions. Several passages in the first dhāraṇī remain uncertain (notably *cakṣuḥ-smṛti-grahaṇa-jihvākāya* and *svabhāva-svabhāva*), and the fourth dhāraṇī's central syllables (*oṃ kuṭhagini*) are tentative. The third dhāraṇī is the well-attested Cundī (準提) mantra. The fifth dhāraṇī's *oṃ tare tuttāre ture svāhā* is the standard Tārā mantra, here attributed to Avalokiteśvara, likely reflecting an early stage before Tārā's full differentiation as an independent deity.
