# 金光明最勝懺儀
## Liturgy of the Supreme Repentance of the Golden Light

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1946

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, compiled by Zhili (知禮).

---

All reverence!

With single mind, I bow my head to the ground before the eternally abiding Three Jewels of the ten directions.

> May this cloud of incense and flowers
> Pervade and fill the realms of the ten directions,
> Making offerings to all buddhas,
> The honored Dharma and all bodhisattvas,
> The assemblies of śrāvakas and pratyekabuddhas,
> And all celestial immortals.
> May they accept and use them for the work of the buddhas.

Having made offerings, all reverence!

With single mind, I reverently invite: Homage to our original teacher, Śākyamuni Buddha; the World-Honored Ones, Akṣobhya of the East and the four buddhas; the Buddha Jewel-Flower Lapis Lazuli, the Buddha Jewel-Victory, and others; all buddhas throughout the *Golden Light Sūtra* and all buddhas of the ten directions and three times.

With single mind, I reverently invite: Homage to the Mahāyāna *Suvarṇaprabhāsa* Ocean of the Twelve Divisions of the Canon.

With single mind, I reverently invite: Homage to Bodhisattva Faith-Marks, Bodhisattva Avalokiteśvara, Bodhisattva Mahāsthāmaprāpta, Bodhisattva Golden-Light, Bodhisattva Golden-Treasury, Constant-Sorrow, Dharma-Supreme, and all bodhisattvas, śrāvakas, pratyekabuddhas, worthies, and sages throughout the *Golden Light Sūtra* and of the ten directions and three times.

With single mind, I reverently invite: Homage to Great Brahmā, the Thirty-Three Devas, the Four World-Protecting Kings, Vajrapāṇi, General Saṃjñeya, the great deity Sarasvatī, Goddess Mārīcī, the deity Skanda, the firm Earth Goddess, the Bodhi Tree Spirit, Hārītī the Mother of Demon-Children with her five hundred retinue, all of whom are great bodhisattvas, as well as all sacred shrines of the famous mountains and great rivers within this nation, all ghosts and spirits belonging to this province and district, the guardians of the monastery at this place of residence, those who protect the correct Dharma, and all the holy assembly.

With single mind, I reverently invite: Homage to the Foremost-in-Majestic-Virtue, Accomplisher-of-All-Affairs, Great Merit Goddess.

*[Dhāraṇī]*

*Namo Śrī-Mahādevyai tadyathā prīpūrṇa cale samandarā śatani mahā vipulā argati samanda vidamba mane mahā kārya pratiṣṭhā pane sarvārtha sādhani supratipriye ayāna dharma-tama mahā vikupite mahā maitrī upasaṃhite mahā hṛdaya susaṃgṛhīte samanta patha anupalane svāhā.*

> The Buddha's face is like the pure full moon,
> And also like a thousand suns emitting radiance.
> His eyes are pure, broad, and elegant like blue lotuses;
> His teeth are white, even, and close-set like conch shells and snow.

> The Buddha's virtue is boundless as the great ocean,
> With limitless wondrous treasures accumulated within.
> The waters of wisdom and virtue are always brimming full;
> Hundreds of thousands of supreme samādhis fill it completely.

> The wheel-marks beneath his feet are all adorned;
> Hubs, nets, and a thousand spokes are perfectly even.
> His hands and feet are webbed throughout with adornment,
> Like a goose-king, complete with all the marks.

> The Buddha's body gleams like a golden mountain,
> Pure, exceptional, and without compare,
> Also like the wondrous summit, replete with merit.
> Therefore I bow my head to the Buddha, the Mountain King.

> His marks and excellences, like space, are immeasurable,
> Surpassing a thousand suns in emitting radiance.
> All are like flame and illusion, inconceivable.
> Therefore I bow my head; my mind is without attachment.

Homage to the Buddha Jewel-Flower Lapis Lazuli. Homage to the Mahāyāna *Suvarṇaprabhāsa Sūtra*. Homage to the Foremost-in-Majestic-Virtue, Accomplisher-of-All-Affairs, Great Merit Goddess.

Now in this place of practice I have arranged and set forth offerings. I light various lamps, burn various incenses, and present various food and drink, pure and in accord with the Dharma. I reverently offer them to all buddhas, the World-Honored Ones, the Mahāyāna canon, the bodhisattvas, worthies, sages, and all the Three Jewels. Furthermore, I separately prepare incense, flowers, food, and drink, and present them to the Great Merit Goddess, Sarasvatī, the Four Kings, Brahmā, Śakra, the eight classes of devas and nāgas, and the holy assembly. I further distribute food and drink, scattering them in all directions to give to the various spirits. I humbly pray that the Three Jewels and celestial immortals may show compassion toward me and all sentient beings and accept these offerings. Through the power of the Golden Light and the majestic spiritual power of all the buddhas, in a single thought-moment may they appear throughout all buddha-lands of the ten directions, pervading like clouds and moistening like rain, broadly performing the work of the buddhas, equally perfuming all sentient beings, arousing the mind of bodhi, and together perfectly completing all-knowledge.

I now, in accordance with the teaching, make offerings to the Mahāyāna Three Jewels and the Auspicious Great Goddess. Holding these various foods and drinks, I scatter and sprinkle them in all directions, universally giving to the various spirits. May all the spirits, with majestic power and sovereign freedom, in a single thought gather universally, each receiving the Dharma-food, fully satisfied and without want, with bodily strength courageous and keen, guarding and protecting with firmness. Knowing what I seek, may they willingly join me, turning this merit and benefit to universally moisten all living beings, so that the fruits of retribution may naturally be of constant and supreme bliss.

With single mind, I bow my head to our original teacher, Śākyamuni Buddha.

With single mind, I bow my head to Akṣobhya Buddha of the east.

With single mind, I bow my head to Ratnaketu Buddha of the south.

With single mind, I bow my head to Amitāyus Buddha of the west.

With single mind, I bow my head to Sūkṣmadhvani Buddha of the north.

With single mind, I bow my head to the Buddha Jewel-Flower Lapis Lazuli.

With single mind, I bow my head to the Buddha Jewel-Victory.

With single mind, I bow my head to the Buddha Immaculate-Blazing-Jewel-Radiance-King-of-Marks.

With single mind, I bow my head to the Buddha Golden-Flame Radiance.

With single mind, I bow my head to the Buddha Golden-Hundred-Radiance-Illuminating-Treasury.

With single mind, I bow my head to the Buddha Golden-Mountain Jewel-Canopy.

With single mind, I bow my head to the Buddha Golden-Flower-Flame-Radiance-Marks.

With single mind, I bow my head to the Buddha Great Torch.

With single mind, I bow my head to the Buddha Jewel-Marks.

With single mind, I bow my head to all buddhas throughout the *Golden Light Sūtra* and all buddhas of the ten directions and three times.

With single mind, I bow my head to the Mahāyāna *Suvarṇaprabhāsa* Ocean of the Twelve Divisions of the Canon.

With single mind, I bow my head to Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Faith-Marks.

With single mind, I bow my head to Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Avalokiteśvara.

With single mind, I bow my head to Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Mahāsthāmaprāpta.

With single mind, I bow my head to Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Golden-Light.

With single mind, I bow my head to Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Golden-Treasury.

With single mind, I bow my head to Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Constant-Sorrow.

With single mind, I bow my head to Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Dharma-Supreme.

With single mind, I bow my head to all bodhisattva-mahāsattvas throughout the *Golden Light Sūtra* and of the ten directions and three times.

With single mind, I bow my head to the assemblies of śrāvakas, pratyekabuddhas, worthies, and sages such as Śāriputra.

Universally, for the sake of all sentient beings of the dharmadhātu, may all sever and remove the three obstructions. Taking refuge, I repent.

I, bhikṣu [name], take refuge and bow my head to the ground before all buddhas now present in the ten directions, those who have attained anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi, who turn the wondrous Dharma-wheel, who uphold and illumine the Dharma-wheel, who rain the great Dharma-rain, who beat the great Dharma-drum, who blow the great Dharma-conch, who raise the great Dharma-banner, who hold aloft the great Dharma-torch; who, for the benefit and ease of all sentient beings, constantly bestow the gift of the Dharma, guiding the deluded multitudes and causing them to attain the great fruit and to realize permanence and bliss. To such buddhas, the World-Honored Ones, with body, speech, and mind I bow my head in sincere devotion, with utmost mind paying reverence.

Those World-Honored Ones, with true wisdom and true eyes, with true testimony and true equanimity, fully know and fully see the wholesome and unwholesome karma of all sentient beings. I, from beginningless saṃsāra until the present, have followed evil currents, and together with all sentient beings have committed karmic-obstruction transgressions. Bound by greed, anger, and delusion, when I did not know the Buddha, when I did not know the Dharma, when I did not know the Saṃgha, when I did not know good from evil, through body, speech, and mind I committed the uninterrupted transgressions: with an evil mind I drew blood from a buddha's body, I slandered the correct Dharma, I broke the harmony of the Saṃgha, I killed an arhat, I killed my father and mother. The three of body, four of speech, and three of mind: I committed the ten unwholesome deeds. I did them myself, taught others to do them, and seeing others do them, I rejoiced on their behalf. I wrongly slandered virtuous persons. With scales and measures I deceived, using the false as true. I gave impure food and drink to all. In the six destinies, toward fathers and mothers of various lives, I caused mutual harm. I stole the property of stūpas and the property of the saṃgha of the four directions and of the community present. I used them at my own discretion. The World-Honored One's Dharma and Vinaya I did not delight in following. When teachers and elders instructed me, I did not comply. Seeing practitioners of the śrāvaka, pratyekabuddha, and Mahāyāna paths, I was pleased to revile and insult them, causing the practitioners' minds to be filled with regret and vexation. Seeing those superior to me, I harbored jealousy. Toward gifts of Dharma and gifts of wealth, I constantly gave rise to stinginess. Covered by ignorance, confused by wrong views, I did not cultivate the causes of good but caused evil to increase. Toward all buddhas I gave rise to slander. What is Dharma I called non-Dharma; what is non-Dharma I called Dharma.

Such transgressions as these the buddhas, with true wisdom and true eyes, with true testimony and true equanimity, fully know and fully see. I now take refuge and, before all buddhas, fully confess and reveal them, not daring to conceal. Transgressions not yet committed I shall never commit again. Transgressions already committed I now entirely repent. The karmic obstructions I have created, which ought to lead to rebirth in the evil destinies of the hells, among animals, and among hungry ghosts, among the asura hosts, and in the eight places of difficulty: may all the karmic obstructions of this present life be eliminated and extinguished, and may all evil retributions not be received in the future. Just as the great bodhisattvas of the past, cultivating the practices of bodhi, fully repented of all their karmic obstructions, so too do I now repent of my karmic obstructions. I fully confess and reveal them, not daring to conceal. Transgressions already committed, may they be eliminated. Future evils I shall never dare to commit again. Just as the great bodhisattvas of the future, cultivating the practices of bodhi, will fully repent of all their karmic obstructions, so too do I now repent of my karmic obstructions. I fully confess and reveal them, not daring to conceal. Transgressions already committed, may they be eliminated. Future evils I shall never dare to commit again. Just as the great bodhisattvas now present in the ten directions, cultivating the practices of bodhi, have fully repented of all their karmic obstructions, so too do I now repent of my karmic obstructions. I fully confess and reveal them, not daring to conceal. Transgressions already committed, may they be eliminated. Future evils I shall never dare to commit again.

I, bhikṣu [name], take refuge and bow my head to all buddhas, the World-Honored Ones, of the ten directions, who have newly attained right awakening and have not yet turned the Dharma-wheel, and who are about to relinquish their responsive bodies and enter nirvāṇa. I bow my head before all these World-Honored Ones and exhort them to turn the Dharma-wheel. I request that they abide long in the world to deliver and bring ease to all sentient beings.

I, bhikṣu [name], take refuge and bow my head to all buddhas, the World-Honored Ones, of the ten directions. I now rejoice in all the wholesome practices of body, speech, and mind cultivated by all sentient beings: giving, precepts, mind, and wisdom. I rejoice in the roots of goodness of the worthies and sages of the Two Vehicles and bodhisattvas. I rejoice in the merit of all buddhas of the ten directions who have realized wondrous bodhi and bestowed the Dharma upon all. With utmost sincerity I share in their joy and offer praise.

I, bhikṣu [name], take refuge and bow my head to all buddhas, the World-Honored Ones, of the ten directions. May they bear witness to my humble sincerity and this present aspiration. All the good I have cultivated through the three activities of body, speech, and mind, from the beginningless past until today, including giving, precepts, goodness, and wisdom, and even this repentance, exhortation, and sympathetic joy: gathering and taking up what is now present, I turn it over and bestow it upon all sentient beings of the dharmadhātu, that together they may realize bodhi and become like all the buddhas.

I, bhikṣu [name], take refuge and bow my head to all buddhas, the World-Honored Ones, of the ten directions. May they verify my humble sincerity and this present aspiration. May the devas and eight classes of beings increase their majestic spiritual power and constantly come to protect this nation. May winds be auspicious and rains timely, grains and fruits abundantly harvested. May the sage emperor and benevolent king extend compassion without limit. May ministers and officials always maintain their honored glory. May all the people of the four classes forever enjoy peace, prosperity, and happiness. May the buddha-dharma patrons, our parents, teachers, and monastics, throughout successive lifetimes, along with former enemies and dear ones, the dharmadhātu and all beings, all give rise to right faith and arouse the mind of bodhi. May they equally cultivate the six pāramitās, with both merits and wisdom fully prepared. Furthermore, may we receive the hidden blessings of all the sages. May we always encounter the Great Vehicle and good teachers who open our buddha-wisdom. May vow and practice manifest, and may we shoulder and transmit the buddha-dharma of the three times, guiding and transforming all, lighting the inexhaustible lamp, universally gathering all sentient beings to return together to the secret treasury.

Homage to the Buddha. Homage to the Dharma. Homage to the Saṃgha. Homage to our original teacher, Śākyamuni Buddha. Homage to the four buddhas of the four directions. Homage to the Buddha Jewel-Flower Lapis Lazuli. Homage to the Mahāyāna *Suvarṇaprabhāsa Sūtra*. Homage to Bodhisattva Faith-Marks. Homage to Bodhisattva Golden-Light. Homage to Bodhisattva Golden-Treasury. Homage to Bodhisattva Constant-Sorrow. Homage to Bodhisattva Dharma-Supreme. Homage to the Foremost-in-Majestic-Virtue, Accomplisher-of-All-Affairs, Great Merit Goddess.

Taking refuge in the Buddha, may all sentient beings embody and understand the great Way and arouse the unsurpassed mind.

Taking refuge in the Dharma, may all sentient beings deeply enter the treasury of sūtras, with wisdom like the ocean.

Taking refuge in the Saṃgha, may all sentient beings lead and govern the great assembly, all without obstruction. Homage to the holy assembly.
