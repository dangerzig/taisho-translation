# 佛說聖大總持王經
## The Buddha's Teaching: Sūtra of the Holy Great Dhāraṇī King

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1371

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dānapāla (施護).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the World-Honored One was in the country of Śrāvastī, in the Jeta Grove, the garden of Anāthapiṇḍada. At that time Ānanda had been dwelling separately for the rains retreat. When the summer retreat was complete, he came silently to the Buddha's assembly. Upon arriving at the assembly, he prostrated with all five limbs touching the ground and bowed at the Buddha's feet. He then withdrew and sat to one side, eagerly awaiting the Buddha's instruction.

Then the World-Honored One addressed Ānanda: "Ānanda! I now wish to benefit all sentient beings, and therefore I shall teach the verses of the great dhāraṇī. You should listen carefully." At that time Ānanda received the Buddha's command. With body and mind composed, he stood with palms joined.

The Buddha said: "Ānanda! I have a mahā-dhāraṇī, a great dhāraṇī king, which was proclaimed by seventy-seven koṭi tathāgatas of the past, and I too rejoice in it. Ānanda! These verses of the great dhāraṇī can enable sentient beings to attain great peace and happiness, and to be endowed with majestic virtue, power, and strength. If any good man or good woman has the fortune to encounter it, they should think thus: 'This great dhāraṇī is rare and difficult to encounter, just as the appearance in the world of a tathāgata, arhat, samyak-saṃbuddha is rare and difficult to encounter, and just as the udumbara flower is rare and difficult to see.' They should give rise to such thoughts, sincerely believe in it, and make offerings to it."

Then the World-Honored One further addressed Ānanda: "If any good man or good woman receives, upholds, reads, recites, and makes offerings to these verses of the great dhāraṇī with a focused mind, blades cannot wound them, poisons cannot harm them, fire cannot burn them, water cannot sweep them away, the law of kings cannot be imposed upon them, and they will never die an untimely death. As for devas, humans, asuras, maruts, garuḍas, gandharvas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, yakṣas, rākṣasas, pretas, piśācas, kumbhāṇḍas, pūtanas, apasmāras, and all such beings who create evil dharmas and act without benefit, they too will be unable to harm them. Even sorcery, heterodox spells, and all manner of evil dharmas will be unable to cause disturbance. Furthermore, malarial fevers of one day, two days, three days, four days, or many days, or even momentary ones, whether caused by wind, bile, or phlegm, and even headaches and similar diseases, will also be unable to cause harm."

Then the World-Honored One spoke the dhāraṇī:

*Tadyathā: jvale mahā-jvale jvala-ni mahā-jvala-ni tapa-ni mahā-tapa-ni jvala-ti ukti mukti saṃbhani mahā-saṃbhani svāhā.*

"Ānanda! I have already told you that these verses of the great dhāraṇī were proclaimed by seventy-seven koṭi buddha-tathāgatas, and I now proclaim it again. Ānanda! If any good man or good woman receives and upholds these verses of the great dhāraṇī with a mind of faith, whether reading, reciting, or contemplating them, and makes various offerings, they will attain the knowledge of past lives, knowing the events of seven lifetimes, and will never regress from the mind of enlightenment."

Then the World-Honored One spoke another dhāraṇī:

*Tadyathā: aṇi maṇi kṣami kṣa-maye mānane anānu-hure mahā-hure tanake gaṃ-bhīri-nu prasahi sahi svāhā.*

"Ānanda! These verses of the great dhāraṇī were proclaimed by eighty-eight koṭi buddha-tathāgatas of the past, and I too rejoice in them and now proclaim them again. Ānanda! If any good man or good woman receives and upholds these verses of the great dhāraṇī with a mind of faith, whether reading, reciting, contemplating, or making offerings, that person will attain the knowledge of past lives, knowing the events of fourteen lifetimes, and will never regress from the mind of enlightenment."

Then the World-Honored One spoke yet another dhāraṇī:

*Tadyathā: aṃ-ci-vanti chaṭa-ke chaṭa-rake chaṭa-ke chaṭa-rake asika-sma-kasi ire mire tire mire nau-rave mahā-nau-rave bhūtaṃ-gami bhūta-parāyaṇa-timi aṃ-gale mahā-timi-aṃ-gale saṃ-bhave mahā-saṃ-bhave svāhā.*

"Ānanda! These verses of the great dhāraṇī possess great majestic virtue, immeasurable power and strength. If any good man or good woman receives, upholds, reads, recites, contemplates, and makes offerings to them with a mind of faith, they will attain the knowledge of past lives, knowing the events of twenty-one lifetimes, and will never regress from the mind of enlightenment."

Then the World-Honored One spoke yet another dhāraṇī:

*Tadyathā: akere vākere rure mahā-rure uhani muhani muhani caṃbhani stambhani svāhā.*

"Ānanda! This great dhāraṇī possesses great majestic virtue and great power and strength. Its merit is immeasurable, surpassing all those before it, and it can bring great benefit to sentient beings. If any good man or good woman arouses a mind of sincere faith without giving rise to doubt, and receives, upholds, reads, and recites this great dhāraṇī, whether for the protection of others, for rescue and relief, or for the cessation of calamities, that person will be unharmed by blades and weapons, unaffected by poisons, and all evil things will be unable to cause damage. Furthermore, if a person wishes to protect themselves, they should write this dhāraṇī on white silk and wear it on their body or arm. The merit obtained will be immeasurable and boundless. Moreover, if a wise person upholds this dhāraṇī and performs a rescue for a withered tree, that withered tree will soon revive. Its roots and leaves will grow anew, turning the color of coral, and its flowers and fruits will flourish abundantly, delighting all who see them.

"Ānanda! If a wise person constantly receives and upholds this dhāraṇī, repenting of the evil karma committed in past lives, and if they sincerely confess, there is no sin that will not be eliminated. Why? Ānanda! Consider the sun and moon, whose majestic radiance is vast and can illuminate the world: yet by the power of the majestic virtue of this dhāraṇī, even they could be made to fall. Consider the waters of the four great oceans, gathered from countless streams, deep and vast beyond compare: yet by the power of the majestic virtue of this dhāraṇī, they too could be dried up. Consider the great earth, immeasurable and boundless: yet by the power of the majestic virtue of this dhāraṇī, it could be split into a thousand pieces. How then could any sin-karma fail to be eliminated?

"Ānanda! In whatever place these verses of the great dhāraṇī abide, one should honor, revere, believe in, and make offerings to them, and must not treat them with contempt or act in opposition. If any devas, nāgas, yakṣas, rākṣasas, pretas, piśācas, kumbhāṇḍas, pūtanas, kaṭapūtanas, apasmāras, vetāḍas, humans and non-humans, or any such beings who create evil dharmas and act without benefit in the world should act in opposition to and not comply with these verses of the great dhāraṇī, then Dhṛtarāṣṭra, the Heavenly King, will manifest a form of extreme wrath, his radiant flames gathered into a single mass, and with a blazing iron wheel will split the offender's head into seven parts like the branches of an arjaka tree. Furthermore, Śakra, the lord of the devas, will also become greatly wrathful, his radiant flames blazing and gathered into a single mass, and with the vajra will split the offender's head into seven parts like the branches of an arjaka tree. And Vajrapāṇi, the great yakṣa lord, will also strike and split the offender's head with the vajra like the branches of an arjaka tree. Furthermore, if any beings act in opposition to these verses of the great dhāraṇī, even though they dwell in the heavens, they will never again be able to enjoy the merit-water of the Crystal King Palace."

When the Buddha had spoken this sūtra, Ānanda and the great assembly of that gathering, the devas, humans, asuras, gandharvas, humans and non-humans, and the rest, having heard what the Buddha taught, joyfully put it into practice, bowed to the Buddha, and withdrew.
