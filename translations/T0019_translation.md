# 大三摩惹經
## Sūtra of the Great Assembly (Mahāsamāja Sūtra)

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0019

Translated by Dharmadeva of the Song dynasty

---

## Fascicle 1

### Sūtra of the Great Assembly, Spoken by the Buddha

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was dwelling in the Kapila Grove, together with a great assembly of bhikṣus, all of them arhats in whom all outflows had been extinguished, their deeds accomplished, their own benefit attained, all fetters of existence severed, their minds free — five thousand five hundred in all.

At that time, the Śakras and Brahmās of the ten directions, together with gods of great majesty and virtue, surrounded with reverence by their retinues, their bodies dignified and majestic in appearance, blazing with luminous splendor, came to the Kapila Grove, approached the World-Honored One, and with foreheads touching his feet paid homage, then stood to one side. At that time, four great Brahmā kings each proclaimed the Buddha's virtues in gāthā verses. The first Brahmā king spoke this verse:

> "At this great assembly,
> The wondrous sound of Dharma is proclaimed.
> My Buddha, the Invincible —
> Gods and humans gather from all around."

The second Brahmā king spoke this verse:

> "Through vast asaṃkhyeya kalpas,
> He cultivated a mind of deep faith,
> Guarding the sense faculties of eye and the rest,
> Not abiding in the realms of sense-objects."

The third Brahmā king spoke this verse:

> "Precepts, samādhi, and wisdom — genuine and true,
> Pure and free from all defilement;
> Like Indra's vajra,
> Firm and indestructible."

The fourth Brahmā king spoke this verse:

> "Whoever takes refuge in the Buddha
> Will never fall into evil destinies;
> When that person's life comes to its end,
> Swiftly they will be reborn in the heavens."

At that time, the World-Honored One, with his pure divine eye, universally surveyed the great assembly — the multitude of gods and humans, immeasurable and innumerable — and addressed the assembly of bhikṣus: "The Tathāgatas of the past, the Worthy Ones, the Perfectly Enlightened, assembled the multitudes of gods and humans and expounded the Dharma for them, even as I do now. Today I universally gather gods and humans, wishing to expound the Dharma. You should receive and uphold it. Those who are courageous, resolute, and fearless, like a lion, firm in deep faith without attachment — throughout the great earth, the mountains, and even unto the Brahmā world — all shall attain nirvāṇa." When this Dharma was proclaimed, another seventeen hundred gods and humans in the stage of learning, and innumerable further gods, blazing with luminous splendor, came to where the Buddha was. The Buddha told the bhikṣus: "Listen well. I observe those gods who have arrived: they should be delivered by the Dharma delightful to śrāvakas."

At that time, seven thousand great yakṣas, endowed with great supernormal powers and majestic virtue, blazing with luminous splendor, surrounded with reverence by their retinues, came to the Kapila Grove.

Also, six thousand great yakṣas dwelling on the Golden Mountain, endowed with great supernormal powers and majestic virtue, blazing with luminous splendor, surrounded with reverence by their retinues, came to the Kapila Grove.

Also, three thousand great yakṣas dwelling on Mount Sāta, endowed with great supernormal powers and majestic virtue, blazing with luminous splendor, surrounded with reverence by their retinues, came to the Kapila Grove.

Also, Kumbhīra and a hundred thousand yakṣas dwelling on Vipula Mountain at Rājagṛha, endowed with great supernormal powers and majestic virtue, blazing with luminous splendor, surrounded with reverence by their retinues, came to the Kapila Grove.

Also, the yakṣa generals Śvāmitra, Pañcaśikha, Viśvameva, and others, endowed with great supernormal powers and majestic virtue, blazing with luminous splendor, together with their retinues, came to the Kapila Grove.

Also, the Guardian King of the East, lord of the gandharvas, named Dhṛtarāṣṭra, endowed with great supernormal powers and immeasurable majestic virtue, his body of wondrous appearance, blazing with radiant splendor, surrounded with reverence by his retinue, came to the Kapila Grove to assemble and hear the Dharma. The Guardian King of the South, lord of the kumbhāṇḍas, named Virūḍhaka, endowed with great supernormal powers and immeasurable majestic virtue, his body of wondrous appearance, blazing with radiant splendor, surrounded with reverence by his retinue, came to the Kapila Grove to assemble and hear the Dharma. The Guardian King of the West, great lord of the nāgas, named Virūpākṣa, endowed with great supernormal powers and immeasurable majestic virtue, his body of wondrous appearance, blazing with radiant splendor, surrounded with reverence by his retinue, came to the Kapila Grove to assemble and hear the Dharma. The Guardian King of the North, great lord of the yakṣas, named Kubera, endowed with great supernormal powers and immeasurable majestic virtue, his body of wondrous appearance, blazing with radiant splendor, surrounded with reverence by his retinue, came to the Kapila Grove to assemble and hear the Dharma. Furthermore, the spirits attending the four great heavenly kings — namely Māyā, Kāci, Vikṛti, Baligu, Balikuci, and others — all possessing supernormal powers and might, proud and ignorant, their forms coarse and fearsome, undergoing various transformations, surrounded with reverence by their retinues, came to the Kapila Grove to assemble and hear the Dharma.

Also, yakṣa spirit generals — namely Indra, Movaruṇa, Prajāpati, Bharadvāja, Īśāna, Candana, Kāma, Śrīṣṭha, Kuṇikaṇṭha, Nikaṇṭha, Maṇimaṇimaṇicara, Prāṇada, Upapañcaka, Sātagiri, Hemavata, Pūrṇaka, Khenira, Kuviṭa, Gupara yakṣa, Aṭavikumāra-rāja, Nāriṣabha, Cittrasena, Gandharva, Dīrghaśati, Mātali, Pañcāla, Gaṇḍa, Sumomānamirgu — together with their retinues, Triphalī, Trikaṇṭakī, and all those who walk in the world, all possessed of supernormal powers and majestic virtue, blazing with luminous splendor, surrounding with reverence, came to the Kapila Grove to assemble and hear the Dharma.

Also, the great venomous nāgas — namely Takṣaka, Kambala, Aśvataru, Prāpta, Prajāgu, Sāguna, Sahasauna, Saku, Dhṛtarāṣṭra, Kucura, Airāvaṇa, and other nāgas — fierce and violently malevolent, endowed with great supernormal powers and majestic virtue, blazing with radiant splendor, surrounded with reverence by their retinues, came to the Kapila Grove to assemble and hear the Dharma.

Also, the garuḍa named Abhaya, together with the birds Viśuddhanetra and other flying creatures and their retinues, likewise endowed with supernormal powers and majestic virtue, blazing with luminous splendor, surrounding with reverence, came to the Kapila Grove to assemble and hear the Dharma.

Also, the Invincible Vajrapāṇi, those dwelling within the sea, all the fearsome yakṣas, together with their retinues, all endowed with supernormal powers and majestic virtue, blazing with luminous splendor, surrounding with reverence, came to the Kapila Grove to assemble and hear the Dharma.

Also, the assembly of asuras — namely Vemacitra, Sucitra, Prahlāda, Namuci, together with a hundred Māli asura sons, and all their retinues — endowed with great supernormal powers and immeasurable majestic virtue, blazing with luminous splendor, surrounding with reverence, came to the Kapila Grove to assemble and hear the Dharma.

Also, the gods of the Four Great Heavenly Kings, the gods of Trāyastriṃśa, the gods of Yāma, the gods of Tuṣita, the gods of Nirmāṇarati, the gods of Paranirmitavaśavartin, and the various assemblies of gods, all endowed with supernormal powers and majestic virtue, blazing with luminous splendor, surrounding with reverence, came to the Kapila Grove to assemble and hear the Dharma.

Also, the named gods — namely the god Varuṇa, the god Vāruṇī, the god Soma, the god Pṛṣṭha, the god Āgama, the god Mitrāvaruṇī, the Earth God, the Water God, the Fire God, the Wind God — these ten gods, endowed with great majestic virtue, manifesting supernormal transformations, together with their retinues, blazing with luminous splendor, surrounding with reverence, came to the Kapila Grove to assemble and hear the Dharma.

Also, the god Nārāyaṇa, the god Sahālica, the Sun God, the Moon God, the Constellation God, the god Śakra, the god Maghavān, the supremely honored god of all sages and worthies, together with the gods of two retinues — these ten gods, endowed with great supernormal powers and immeasurable majestic virtue, blazing with luminous splendor, surrounding with reverence, came to the Kapila Grove to assemble and hear the Dharma.

Also, various goddesses — namely the goddess Sāhikā, the goddess Like-a-Flame-of-Fire, the goddess Ariṣṭā, the goddess Somā, the goddess Uttamā, the goddess Puṣpavasinī, the goddess Cārakā, the goddess Subhadrā, the goddess Kakṣācyutā, the goddess Prādyumnā, Sānikā, and others — innumerable goddesses, together with their retinues, endowed with great supernormal powers and immeasurable majestic virtue, their forms majestic and dignified, blazing with luminous splendor, surrounding with reverence, came to the Kapila Grove to assemble and hear the Dharma.

Also, great yakṣa women — namely the yakṣiṇī Śamā, the yakṣiṇī Mahāśamā, the yakṣiṇī Mānuṣā, the yakṣiṇī Mānuṣottamā, the yakṣiṇī Kṛṣṇā, the yakṣiṇī Pramoṣā, the yakṣiṇī Mānapradūṣikā, the yakṣiṇī Mālahā, the yakṣiṇī Mahāmālā, the yakṣiṇī Bhikṣumunikā — these ten great yakṣa women, likewise endowed with supernormal powers and majestic virtue, blazing with luminous splendor, surrounded with reverence by their retinues, came to the Kapila Grove to assemble and hear the Dharma.

Also, ten more great yakṣa women — namely the yakṣiṇī Śuklā, the yakṣiṇī Karṇamālā, the yakṣiṇī Kāruṇā, the yakṣiṇī Nīlakavasinī, the yakṣiṇī Āvanatakeśā, the yakṣiṇī Pramukhipītakavasinī, the yakṣiṇī Sānamātā, the yakṣiṇī Hārītī, the yakṣiṇī Rucikā — these and such yakṣa women, likewise endowed with supernormal powers and majestic virtue, blazing with luminous splendor, surrounded with reverence by their retinues, came to the Kapila Grove to assemble and hear the Dharma.

Also, Hārītī together with her retinue of boys and girls, likewise endowed with majestic virtue and supernormal powers, blazing with luminous splendor, surrounded with reverence by their retinues, came to the Kapila Grove to assemble and hear the Dharma.

Thus the Brahmā kings and Indras of the ten directions, the gods, humans, and the eight classes of beings, the great bhikṣus — immeasurable and innumerable — all came and gathered in assembly.

At that time, within the assembly there was a great black spirit named Cudūna, endowed with great supernormal powers, fierce and violently malevolent, tormenting and harming gods and humans, obstructing the cultivation of wholesome deeds. He slapped the ground with his hand, sending forth a great terrible sound, and in the sky above conjured great wind, clouds, lightning, hail, thunder, and flashes — every manner of evil omen — so that the multitude was seized with terror. The Buddha at once observed this and proclaimed aloud: "Hear the Dharma!" When the māra heard this, he took refuge and ceased his malice, and together with the bhikṣus, dwelt within the śrāvaka vehicle. At that moment, the assembled multitude, witnessing the Buddha subdue the māra, leapt for joy, received the teaching in faith, and reverently practiced it.
