# 佛為娑伽羅龍王所說大乘經
## Mahāyāna Sūtra Spoken by the Buddha for Sāgara, the Nāga King

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0601

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dānapāla (施護)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the great ocean, in the magnificent hall of awakening in the palace of Sāgara, the Nāga King, together with a great assembly of 7,500 bhikṣus and bodhisattva-mahāsattvas endowed with great wisdom who had come from the ten directions to gather in assembly. Also present were hundreds of thousands of koṭis of nayutas of Brahmā kings, Śakra and the World-Protectors, and devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, and others who had also come to the assembly.

At that time, the World-Honored One, seeing all these great hosts gathered, addressed Sāgara, the Nāga King: "Lord of Nāgas, observe this world. All its various actions arise from delusion. The various dharmas of the mind will bring forth various kinds of fruition. If those beings do not understand this, they will be born in various destinies.

"Lord of Nāgas, you should observe the hosts within this great ocean and see the various forms and appearances they take. Lord of Nāgas, all these forms and appearances arise from the wholesome and unwholesome karmic deeds of body, speech, and mind, each individual mind producing various transformations. Yet these dharmas of the mind, though called forms and appearances, are like magical illusions, for nothing can be grasped.

"Lord of Nāgas, these forms and appearances, and all dharmas whatsoever, have no fundamental arising, no master or controller. There is no self, and nothing that obstructs. All these various actions and deeds, and the inherent nature of all dharmas, are all illusory appearances, inconceivable.

"Lord of Nāgas, if there are bodhisattvas who know that all dharmas are unborn, unceasing, without form, without characteristics, and having thus known in accordance with reality, who cultivate all wholesome karmic deeds without any notion of cultivation, and regarding all forms, appearances, aggregates, sense-fields, and elements, see no arising in any dharma whatsoever; if they have attained this vision in accordance with reality, they should then contemplate the excellent and marvelous forms and appearances. Lord of Nāgas, how should these excellent and marvelous forms be contemplated? One should contemplate the bodily characteristics of the Tathāgata. The Tathāgata's body is born from hundreds of thousands of koṭis of nayutas of meritorious virtues. Furthermore, how are such characteristics maintained? How should one have faith in them? How does one attain such characteristics, and further attain in the human and heavenly realms a state without aging and without death? Further, one attains the body of hundreds and thousands of Paranirmitavaśavartin devas, and even the body of Mahābrahmā. This is achieved when the mind is undistracted and is focused in contemplation, gazing upon the Tathāgata's most wondrous body. One should truly know that this body, with all its excellent and wondrous adornments, is entirely obtained through the accumulation of wholesome karmic deeds.

"Lord of Nāgas, just as all the adornments of the palace in which you dwell are born from merit, so too with Brahmā kings, Śakra, the World-Protectors, and all the devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, humans, non-humans, and the rest: all their adornments are born from merit.

"Lord of Nāgas, furthermore, among all the beings in this great ocean, some are vast and some are minute, and most dwell in ugly and repulsive forms. All these bodies are the product of the transformations of their various minds. Lord of Nāgas, this is why it is said: 'It is obtained in accordance with the karmic deeds of body, speech, and mind.' Lord of Nāgas, such retribution has karma as its cause and karma as its master. You should cause all sentient beings to give rise to a mind of wisdom, and in all they do and cultivate, to study and follow wholesome karmic deeds. Regarding all wrong views, they should neither act upon them nor abide in them, knowing that wrong views are not ultimate. Having thus known, all sentient beings should seek them as teachers and come to make offerings, and they will also receive the faith and offerings of all in heaven and the human realm.

"Lord of Nāgas, there is one dharma that can enable sentient beings to cut off all karmic deeds leading to evil destinies. What is this one dharma? It is the contemplation of wholesome dharmas. And how should one contemplate wholesome dharmas? One should observe one's own self: 'In my daily activities of walking, standing, sitting, and lying down, throughout the day and night, whatever thoughts my mind gives rise to are nothing but faults.' Having thus become aware, one causes the unwholesome dharmas within the four dignified postures not to arise. Having thus cut off all unwholesome dharmas, one causes wholesome dharmas to become fully present. Furthermore, one enables all sentient beings who share in the good to attain the positions of śrāvaka, pratyekabuddha, bodhisattva, and even supreme, perfect enlightenment.

"Lord of Nāgas, what are wholesome dharmas? I shall now explain them. They are the ten wholesome courses of action, which are the fundamental abode of all. They are the fundamental abode for birth in heaven and the human realm. They are the fundamental abode of all excellent dharmas, both mundane and supramundane. They are the fundamental abode of śrāvakas, pratyekabuddhas, and bodhisattvas. They are the fundamental abode of supreme, perfect enlightenment. What is this fundamental abode? It is the ten wholesome courses of action. When one is able to keep far from killing, stealing, sexual misconduct, false speech, frivolous speech, harsh speech, divisive speech, and further from covetousness, ill-will, and wrong views: when one is able to keep far from all these, this constitutes the ten wholesome courses of action, which are the fundamental abode of the mundane and the supramundane.

"Lord of Nāgas, when a person keeps far from killing, they obtain ten kinds of wholesome dharmas. What are the ten? They are: bestowing fearlessness; all other sentient beings dwell in a mind of loving-kindness toward them; attaining right conduct; not giving rise to thoughts of finding fault with any sentient being; attaining freedom from illness; gaining long life; being protected by various non-human beings; whether asleep or awake, always being at peace; further being guarded by noble and holy beings, with the mind never wearying or rejecting; in dreams, never seeing the distressing events of evil karma; naturally not fearing evil destinies; and after death, being born in heaven. Lord of Nāgas, when a person obtains these ten kinds of wholesome dharmas and practices the bodhisattva path, they dwell in a wholesome mind. When their roots of merit mature, they shall attain supreme, perfect enlightenment.

"Lord of Nāgas, when a person keeps far from stealing, they obtain ten reliant dharmas. What are the ten? They are: attaining great wealth and self-mastery; being free from the troubles of rulers; being free from the troubles of water, fire, thieves, and enemies; having many relatives who are harmonious and compliant; having many people who take delight in them without causing them suffering, and whatever they say being trusted by all; having immeasurable treasures, all gathered together; receiving praise from all in this land and other lands; in all their activities being without fear or dread; being praised by others for their good name and their wisdom; further attaining vigor, long life, and eloquence in harmony with their character; and toward both kin and strangers, the mind making no distinction and giving rise to no harm. After death, they are born in the heavenly realm.

"Lord of Nāgas, when a person keeps far from stealing and obtains these ten reliant dharmas, by those roots of merit they can directly realize the Buddha-Dharma and shall attain supreme, perfect enlightenment.

"Lord of Nāgas, when a person keeps far from sexual misconduct, they obtain four kinds of wise and wholesome dharmas. What are the four? They are: subduing the sense faculties; being free from distraction; receiving the praise of all the world; and obtaining innumerable followers.

"Lord of Nāgas, when a person keeps far from sexual misconduct and obtains these four kinds of wise and wholesome dharmas, by these roots of merit they shall attain supreme, perfect enlightenment, and further realize the mark of the great being's concealed organ.

"Lord of Nāgas, when a person keeps far from false speech, they obtain eight kinds of wholesome dharmas in the heavenly and human realms. What are the eight? They are: the mouth being pure, always like the fragrance of a blue lotus; further, all the world having right views of them; all in the heavenly and human realms taking delight in them; body, speech, and mind being pure, transforming all sentient beings so they abide in the practice of the three pure karmas; having attained purity, all being filled with joy; attaining truthful speech, so that one's words are necessarily sincere and trustworthy; attaining surpassing eloquence, so that all one's words contain skillful means; and being free from all faults in the heavenly and human realms. Lord of Nāgas, when a person keeps far from false speech and obtains these eight kinds of wholesome dharmas in the heavenly and human realms, by those roots of merit they attain purity of speech-karma, sincere and upright conduct, and shall attain supreme, perfect enlightenment.

"Lord of Nāgas, when a person keeps far from frivolous speech, they shall attain three kinds of definitive dharmas. What are the three? They are: being known by all who know the Dharma, who take definitive delight in them; being known as definitively truthful, thereby giving rise to wisdom; and being definitively known as a teacher of devas and humans, in whom all in heaven and the human realm place their trust and delight. Lord of Nāgas, when a person keeps far from frivolous speech and obtains these three kinds of definitive dharmas, by these roots of merit, dedicating them to enlightenment, they receive the prediction of all tathāgatas and shall realize supreme, perfect enlightenment.

"Lord of Nāgas, when a person keeps far from harsh speech, they are purified of the eight kinds of faults of speech and obtain eight kinds of wholesome dharmas. What are the eight? They are: truthful speech, loving speech, speech based on meaning, gentle speech, speech free from grasping, speech that many people delight in, good speech, and speech that has benefit. Lord of Nāgas, when a person keeps far from harsh speech and obtains these eight kinds of pure speech-karma, by these roots of merit, dedicating them to enlightenment, they shall in the future realize supreme, perfect enlightenment and further attain the supreme pure voice of Brahmā.

"Lord of Nāgas, when a person keeps far from divisive speech, they shall attain five kinds of firmness. What are the five kinds of firmness? They are: firmness of body, being far removed from all frightening difficulties; firmness of retinue, not being coveted by others; firmness of faith, obtaining the rewards of trusting in karmic consequences; firmness of Dharma, obtaining firm realization of the fruits of the path; and firmness of good friends, always receiving their loving words and guidance. Lord of Nāgas, when a person keeps far from divisive speech and obtains these five kinds of firmness, by those roots of merit, dedicating them to enlightenment, they shall realize supreme, perfect enlightenment, and all heretics and Māra kings shall be unable to destroy them.

"Lord of Nāgas, when a person keeps far from the poison of covetousness, they obtain eight kinds of wholesome dharmas. What are the eight? They are: the covetous mind is eliminated; the mind of killing does not arise; the mind of jealousy does not arise; one takes joy in being born in a noble family, and is honored by holy persons; one has a mind of loving-kindness, benefiting all sentient beings through wholesome karmic deeds; one's body is handsome; one is honored by many people; and one is born in the world of Brahmā. Lord of Nāgas, when a person keeps far from the poison of covetousness and obtains these eight kinds of wholesome dharmas, by these wholesome dharmas, dedicating them to enlightenment, without backsliding of mind, they shall realize supreme, perfect enlightenment.

"Lord of Nāgas, when a person keeps far from the poison of ill-will, they shall attain five kinds of supreme vows fulfilled. What are the five? They are: body, speech, and mind do not regress, and the sense faculties are not disordered, and one attains all manner of vast wealth and honor fulfilled; one attains the submission of enemies, and all manner of vast merit fulfilled; one receives the supreme offerings of humans and devas, and all manner of vast meritorious virtue fulfilled; whatever one's highest desires of body and mind, all are fulfilled; and as with wealth and honor, any hundred or thousand supreme vows one makes are all fulfilled. Lord of Nāgas, when a person keeps far from the poison of ill-will and obtains these five kinds of fulfillment, by these roots of merit, dedicating them to enlightenment, they realize supreme, perfect enlightenment, and are honored by the three realms.

"Lord of Nāgas, when a person keeps far from wrong views, they obtain ten kinds of meritorious dharmas. What are the ten? They are: attaining a mind that is at ease and wholesome, together with wholesome companions; deeply trusting in cause and effect, and not committing wrongdoing even for the sake of one's life; before long, attaining the position of the noble and holy; not being confused about wholesome dharmas, and cultivating the practices of humans and devas; not falling into the animal realm or the domain of Yama; walking the holy path and attaining the supreme merit; being free from all wrong dharmas; being free from the view of a self; seeing that all wrongdoing is empty in nature; and attaining right practice without deficiency in the heavenly and human realms. Lord of Nāgas, when a person keeps far from wrong views and obtains these ten kinds of meritorious dharmas, by these roots of merit, dedicating them to enlightenment, they swiftly come to know all the Buddha-Dharma and shall attain supreme, perfect enlightenment.

"Lord of Nāgas, furthermore, observe the subtle workings of the ten unwholesome dharmas, which for the most part lead to the destinies of hell, hungry ghosts, and animals.

"Lord of Nāgas, observe those sentient beings: if one commits killing, one falls into hell, the animal realm, or the domain of Yama. Later born among humans, because of residual karma one receives two kinds of retribution: first, a short life; second, suffering and affliction.

"If one commits stealing, one falls into hell, the animal realm, or the domain of Yama. Later born among humans, because of residual karma one receives two kinds of retribution: first, one lives in poverty and low station; second, one cannot obtain the wealth of others.

"If one commits sexual misconduct, one falls into hell, the animal realm, or the domain of Yama. Later born among humans, because of residual karma one receives two kinds of retribution: first, stupidity; second, one's spouse is unfaithful.

"If one commits false speech, one falls into hell, the animal realm, or the domain of Yama. Later born among humans, because of residual karma one receives two kinds of retribution: first, one's words are not truthful; second, people do not trust or follow one.

"If one commits frivolous speech, one falls into hell, the animal realm, or the domain of Yama. Later born among humans, because of residual karma one receives two kinds of retribution: first, one's words are not correct; second, one's words have no certainty.

"If one commits harsh speech, one falls into hell, the animal realm, or the domain of Yama. Later born among humans, because of residual karma one receives two kinds of retribution: first, one's words frequently cause quarrels; second, people who hear one give no weight to what is said.

"If one commits divisive speech, one falls into hell, the animal realm, or the domain of Yama. Later born among humans, because of residual karma one receives two kinds of retribution: first, one obtains inferior retinue; second, one's own relatives become separated.

"If one is covetous, one falls into hell, the animal realm, or the domain of Yama. Later born among humans, because of residual karma one receives two kinds of retribution: first, one is unable to benefit others; second, one is constantly encroached upon and harmed by others.

"If one is given to ill-will, one falls into hell, the animal realm, or the domain of Yama. Later born among humans, because of residual karma one receives two kinds of retribution: first, the mind is constantly unhappy; second, much turns out contrary to one's wishes.

"If one holds wrong views, one falls into hell, the animal realm, or the domain of Yama. Later born among humans, because of residual karma one receives two kinds of retribution: first, wrong views; second, indolence.

"Lord of Nāgas, if one engages in such ten unwholesome dharmas, one will certainly obtain such consequences, and will further separately receive boundless great masses of suffering.

"Lord of Nāgas, if a bodhisattva keeps far from killing and practices the bodhisattva path through the practice of giving, they obtain great wealth, long life, and immeasurable merit, and are freed from all fear of being harmed by others.

"Lord of Nāgas, if a bodhisattva keeps far from stealing and practices the bodhisattva path through giving, they obtain great wealth, honor, and immeasurable merit. Toward all things, the mind is without any reluctance, and they realize the profound wisdom of the supreme Dharma proclaimed by all the buddhas.

"Lord of Nāgas, if a bodhisattva keeps far from sexual misconduct and practices the bodhisattva path through giving, they obtain great wealth, honor, and immeasurable merit, and are blessed with a wholesome retinue, parents, spouse, and children, all free from wrong views.

"Lord of Nāgas, if a bodhisattva keeps far from false speech and practices the bodhisattva path through giving, they obtain great wealth, honor, and immeasurable merit. All their words are wholesome and gentle, and whatever sincere vows they make are firm and unwavering.

"Lord of Nāgas, if a bodhisattva keeps far from frivolous speech and practices the bodhisattva path through giving, they obtain great wealth, honor, and immeasurable merit. Their words are truthful and heard with trust. Whatever they say dispels all doubts.

"Lord of Nāgas, if a bodhisattva keeps far from harsh speech and practices the bodhisattva path through giving, they obtain great wealth, honor, and immeasurable merit. Their words are worthy of acceptance, and hearers do not turn away. Among all assemblies, they are without fault.

"Lord of Nāgas, if a bodhisattva keeps far from divisive speech and practices the bodhisattva path through giving, they obtain great wealth, honor, and immeasurable merit. Toward all their retinue, the mind abides in equanimity, cherishing all equally, without any separation.

"Lord of Nāgas, if a bodhisattva keeps far from the poison of covetousness and practices the bodhisattva path through giving, they obtain great wealth, honor, and immeasurable merit. They attain a handsome body, with all faculties complete. Those who see them take delight, and the mind never wearies of them.

"Lord of Nāgas, if a bodhisattva keeps far from the poison of ill-will and practices the bodhisattva path through giving, they obtain great wealth, honor, and immeasurable merit. Toward enemies, the mind gives rise to nothing at all. Hearing the essence of the Buddha-Dharma, they can give rise to deep faith.

"Lord of Nāgas, if a bodhisattva keeps far from wrong views and practices the bodhisattva path through giving, they obtain great wealth, honor, and immeasurable merit. Toward the Three Treasures they hold right views. They are always near the Buddha and hear the wondrous Dharma. They make offerings to the Sangha without ever wearying or retreating, and they teach and transform sentient beings, causing them to arouse the aspiration for enlightenment.

"Lord of Nāgas, if one cultivates these ten wholesome karmic deeds and practices the bodhisattva path, first adorned by giving, the rewards are fulfilled: one attains great wealth and honor.

"If adorned by keeping the precepts, the rewards are fulfilled: one attains all the Buddha-Dharma, with vows fully complete.

"If adorned by patience, the rewards are fulfilled: one attains the Buddha's enlightenment, the thirty-two marks and eighty minor excellences, and further attains the complete voice of Brahmā.

"If adorned by diligence, the rewards are fulfilled: one is able to vanquish Māra and heretics, and rescue and liberate beings through the Buddha-Dharma.

"If adorned by meditative concentration, the rewards are fulfilled: one attains right mindfulness, purity, and the complete practice of the Dharma.

"If adorned by wisdom, the rewards are fulfilled: one permanently cuts off all wrong views.

"If adorned by great loving-kindness, the rewards are fulfilled: one enables all sentient beings to subdue all subtle afflictions.

"If adorned by great compassion, the rewards are fulfilled: one attains a mind that never wearies of or rejects any sentient being.

"If adorned by great joy, the rewards are fulfilled: one attains single-mindedness without distraction.

"If adorned by great equanimity, the rewards are fulfilled: all subtle afflictions are entirely eliminated.

"Lord of Nāgas, and if adorned by the four means of gathering others, the rewards are fulfilled: one is able to lead and transform all sentient beings in accordance.

"If adorned by the four foundations of mindfulness, one is able to comprehend body, sensation, mind, and dharmas.

"If adorned by the four right efforts, one is able to entirely eliminate all unwholesome dharmas and attain the fulfillment of all wholesome dharmas.

"If adorned by the four bases of spiritual power, one is able to attain lightness and ease of both body and mind.

"If adorned by the five faculties, one attains unretreating faith and diligence, a mind free from confusion, full comprehension of karmic causes, and the permanent elimination of afflictions.

"If adorned by the five powers, one attains freedom from folly and delusion, and the permanent elimination of the faults of poverty.

"If adorned by the seven factors of awakening, one attains the awakening to all dharmas in accordance with reality.

"If adorned by the noble Eightfold Path, one is able to realize right wisdom.

"If adorned by śamatha, one attains the cutting off of all afflictions.

"If adorned by vipaśyanā, one attains the wisdom that comprehends all dharmas.

"If adorned by the right path, one is able to know all skillful means, both conditioned and unconditioned.

"Lord of Nāgas, I have now briefly described the ten wholesome dharmas and their adornments. As for the ten powers, the four wisdoms, the eighteen unshared dharmas, and indeed every aspect of the Tathāgata's Dharma, all are fulfilled through these.

"Lord of Nāgas, the full and extensive explanation of the adornments of the ten wholesome courses of action should be studied and practiced. Lord of Nāgas, just as the great earth can serve as the abode for all the kingdoms, cities, towns, forests, trees, and medicinal herbs of the human realm, and further, all karmic deeds have their seeds, and once there are seeds, they are formed by the four great elements, just as when grain is planted it first produces a sprout and stem, and then matures, all depending on the earth: so too, Lord of Nāgas, these ten wholesome courses of action serve as the excellent and marvelous abode for all sentient beings in the heavenly and human realms. They enable all conditioned and unconditioned dharmas to attain the fruit of wisdom. They serve as the abode for all śrāvakas, pratyekabuddhas, bodhisattvas, and supreme, perfect enlightenment. They also serve as the fundamental abode of all the Buddha-Dharma.

"Lord of Nāgas, regarding all that I have said, all of you should give rise to right-minded faith and understanding."

At that time, Sāgara the Nāga King, together with the bodhisattva-mahāsattvas in the assembly, all the śrāvakas, and the devas, humans, asuras, gandharvas, and all the rest of the great assembly, having heard what the Buddha taught, received it with joy and practiced it.
