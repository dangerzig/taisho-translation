# Hymn of the Three Majesties of the Luminous Religion for Attaining Salvation

## *Jǐngjiào Sānwēi Méngdù Zàn* (景教三威蒙度讚)

Taishō 2143

Translator unknown. A Nestorian (Church of the East) text from Tang-dynasty China.

*Note: This is a Christian text included in the Taishō canon from Dunhuang manuscripts. The "Luminous Religion" (*Jǐngjiào*, 景教) is the Church of the East (Nestorian Christianity) as known in Tang-dynasty China. The "Three Majesties" (*sānwēi*, 三威) refers to the Trinity. Key terms use Syriac transliterations: Allāhā (*Āluóhē*, 阿羅訶) for God the Father, Messiah (*Míshīhē*, 彌施訶) for Christ the Son, and "Pure Wind King" (*Jìng Fēng Wáng*, 淨風王) for the Holy Spirit.*

### Hymn

> The supreme heavens bow in deep reverence;
> The great earth holds all in universal peace.
> The true nature of humankind relies upon and rests in
> The merciful Father of the three powers, Allāhā.
>
> All the assemblies of the good offer sincere worship;
> All natures of wisdom sing praises.
> All who hold the true take refuge entirely;
> By receiving the holy merciful light, they are saved from the Evil One.
>
> Difficult to seek, without equal, the truly eternal:
> The Merciful Father, the Bright Son, the Pure Wind King.
> Among all sovereign lords, he is Teacher and Lord;
> Among all the world-honored ones, he is Dharma-Emperor.
>
> He forever dwells in the realm of wondrous brightness without limit;
> His glorious power surveys all the bounded realm.
> From the beginning, no person has ever seen him;
> Neither through form can his appearance be discerned.
>
> His alone is the pure, crystalline virtue of absolute purity;
> His alone is the divine power without equal;
> His alone is the unchanging, majestic, ever-abiding.
> He is the root of all goodness, without limit.
>
> I now, with all thought, remember his merciful grace,
> And praise that wondrous joy that illumines this land.
> The Messiah, universally honored, the Great Holy Son,
> Broadly saves the realm of suffering, rescuing countless beings.
>
> The ever-living King of Life, the Merciful and Joyful Lamb,
> Broadly bears all suffering without shirking the toil.
> May he forgive the heavy accumulated sins of all living beings,
> And skillfully protect the true nature so that it attains the uncaused.
>
> The Holy Son sits upright at the Father's right hand;
> His seat, moreover, transcends all height.
> The Great Teacher, may he grant the requests of all who beseech him,
> And send down the raft to save them from drifting on the river of fire.
>
> The Great Teacher is our Merciful Father;
> The Great Teacher is our Holy Lord;
> The Great Teacher is our Dharma-King;
> The Great Teacher is able to universally save and deliver.
>
> The Great Teacher's power of wisdom aids all who are weak;
> All eyes gaze upward without a moment's turning away.
> He further sends down sweet dew upon the parched and scorched;
> All who receive its moisture have their good roots nourished.
>
> The Great Saint, Universally Honored Messiah:
> I praise the Merciful Father's ocean-treasury of mercy.
> The Great Saint, humble, and the Pure Wind's nature:
> Purely crystalline, the dharma-ear, inconceivable.

### Honored Scriptures

Reverence to the Wondrous-Body Imperial Father, Allāhā; the Response-Body Imperial Son, Messiah; and the Witness-Body Rūḥā d-Qudšā (*Lúhēníng Jùshā*, 盧訶寧俱沙). These three bodies together return to one substance.

[There follows a list of "Dharma-Kings" (prophets and apostles), including:]

Yōḥanān (*Yúhànnán*, 瑜罕難) Dharma-King, Luke (*Lújiā*, 盧伽) Dharma-King, Mark (*Mójǔcí*, 摩矩辭) Dharma-King, Matthew (*Míngtài*, 明泰) Dharma-King, Moses (*Móushì*, 牟世) Dharma-King, David (*Duōhuì*, 多惠) Dharma-King, and others, together with the Twenty-Four Holy Dharma-Kings, Paul (*Bàoxìn*, 報信) Dharma-King, and others.

[There follows a catalog of honored scriptures of the Luminous Religion, including the *Sūtra of the Ever-Bright Imperial Joy*, the *Sūtra of the Primordial Ultimate*, the *Sūtra of the Heavenly Treasury*, and many others.]
