# Record of the Meaning of the Ten Stages, Fascicle One (十地義記卷第一)

## Taisho T2758

**Author:** Anonymous
**Fascicles:** 1

*Note: This is a philosophical commentary on the Dasabhumika (十地 shídì, "Ten Stages") chapter of the Avatamsaka Sutra, analyzing the proof-sequence of the assembly, the meaning of the bodhisattva names, and the doctrinal structure of the opening passage. The commentary employs a highly systematic hermeneutical method characteristic of Chinese Huayan (華嚴) exegesis, distinguishing between "teaching" (教 jiào), "practice" (行 xíng), and "self-substance" (自體 zìtǐ) levels of interpretation, and between "self-portion" (自分 zìfēn) and "other-portion" (他分 tāfēn) stages of realization. The text is fragmentary, beginning mid-discussion without a formal opening, and concluding with the colophon "Copied by Baozang" (寶藏寫).*

---

[The text begins in the midst of discussing the setting and the assembly of the *Dasabhumika* chapter.]

...therefore it is placed in the celestial dwelling to indicate that the obstacles of going and coming are removed, drawing conditions to enter reality. It is placed in a setting of waiting without defilement. The remainder concerns the true nature and true characteristics; at this point one should clarify the reward-body. Taking the Dharma as its substance, we understand there is also a body.

## The Phrase "Together with Great Bodhisattvas"

"Together with great bodhisattvas" is the sixth phrase in the proof-sequence, clarifying the assembly that heard together. Within this there are two passages: first, a brief praise of their qualities; second, from "these bodhisattvas" onward, an extensive praise of their qualities.

The detailed and the summary can be explained in three ways. First, the detailed and summary of the teaching. Second, the detailed and summary of skillful-means practice. Third, the detailed and summary of the self-substance.

Regarding the detailed and summary of the teaching: the first two lines with a single annotation briefly raise the essential theme, called the "summary"; from "these bodhisattvas" onward, the extensive praise is called the "detailed."

Regarding skillful-means practice: counteracting [obstacles] to reveal the substance is called "summary virtue." The extensive body-substance [discussion] is about skillful means that, though they accord with the substance, remain within the counteracting [level] and are called "summary" for the self-substance's true practice. What is outside the substance constitutes the obstacle, called "extensive."

### The Four Phrases of the Brief Praise

Within the brief praise there are four phrases. "Together with the assembly of great bodhisattvas" states the number of those gathered. The second phrase, "non-retrogression," states that their practice is firm. The third phrase states the function of their practice. The fourth phrase, "coming from other world-systems," states their place of origin.

The word "together" (與 *yǔ*) has two senses: first, the Buddha is "together with" [them]; second, Ananda is "together with" [them]. Taking the Buddha's perspective: Ananda, being within the Buddha's embrace, cannot be said to be "together with" in that sense. From the perspective of principle and teaching, Ananda is a reflected-image great being and can be said to be "together with." From the perspective of practice and teaching, the Buddha's "together with" means taking the self-substance cause as "together with." From the perspective of practice and teaching, Ananda's "together with" means Ananda attained the wisdom of retention, incorporating the self-substance's skillful-means practice, hence "together with."

### Three Kinds of Assembly

"Together with the assembly of great bodhisattvas" involves three kinds [of assembly]: first, the reflected-image assembly; second, the assembly addressed by the teaching; third, the assembly of those who are forthcoming. [Extended analysis of the distinctions between these three kinds of assembly, their relationship to the stages of practice, and how they correspond to the gradual and sudden approaches to teaching.]

### The Meaning of "Great"

"Great" (大 *dà*) means surpassing the three vehicles. It is called "great" because, in the shared teaching and extending through the shared lineage, it surpasses the counteractive level.

### The Meaning of "Bodhisattva"

"Bodhisattva" (菩薩 *púsà*) means in Chinese "a being with the aspiration for the Way" (道心眾生 *dàoxīn zhòngshēng*). It is precisely because these persons have their minds set on the Way that they are called "Way-aspiration beings." There are also lesser bodhisattvas of the two vehicles, in whom birth-and-death and nirvana cannot operate with both quiescence and function simultaneously. Great beings operate with both quiescence and function simultaneously, benefiting both self and others, penetrating without obstruction: these are called "great Way-aspiration beings."

There are also two kinds of bodhisattvas: nominal and real. Nominal bodhisattvas, though they have understanding and practice, cling to names and characteristics and cannot penetrate fully. Real bodhisattvas embody and realize the dharma-realm, penetrating without obstruction: therefore they are called "great."

### The Meaning of "Non-Retrogression"

"Non-retrogression" (不退轉 *bùtuìzhuǎn*) has two kinds: first, non-retrogression in everything; second, non-retrogression within specific stages. [Extended analysis of how non-retrogression applies differently at the initial entry into the dharma-realm, at the first stage where one is born into the Buddha's family, at the eighth stage and above where one enters the stream of the Dharma, and at the tenth stage of receiving the Dharma-cloud.]

### The Meaning of "One More Birth"

"One more birth" (一生 *yīshēng*) has four kinds: first, counteractive birth; second, practice birth; third, positional birth; fourth, response-transformation birth.

Regarding counteractive birth: there are three sub-types: (1) fractional-segment birth, (2) transformation birth, and (3) birth of self-nature that is not truly established. "One more birth" here means that fractional-segment and transformation births have been eliminated; only the one birth of self-nature-not-truly-established remains.

Regarding practice birth: of the three kinds of mind-made bodies (意生身 *yìshēngshēn*), namely the samadhi-bliss-practice mind-made body, the awakening-to-dharma-self-nature mind-made body, and the category-of-kinds-dwelling-without-action mind-made body, "one more birth" here refers to the non-action position.

Regarding positional birth: this is the single birth of the vajra [concentration] at the eleventh stage.

Regarding response-transformation birth: the bodhisattva, with shallow yet skillful expedient means, receives birth in Tusita Heaven (兜率天 *Dōushuài tiān*) to teach and transform. When he descends to the human realm, he immediately attains buddhahood. Since there is only the one birth in Tusita remaining, it is said "with one more birth, attaining bodhi."

### The Meaning of "Bodhi"

"Bodhi" (菩提 *pútí*) means the unsurpassed true and correct Way. "Unsurpassed" because it surpasses the three vehicles' shared teaching. "Correct" because principle in its absolute nature is correct. "True" because it is free from the defilements of delusion and ignorance. "Way" because the self-substance penetrates fully and completely.

### "Coming from Other Lands"

"Coming from other lands" means: from the substance, function arises. For the sake of other realms, function is exercised. Coming from the substance is also "other." [Extended analysis of how "buddha" represents the wisdom-world of self-mastery, "world-system" represents the vessel-world of self-mastery, "together" represents the convergence of three kinds of conditions, and "coming" reveals that practice is all-gathering.]

## The Extensive Praise of Virtues

From "these bodhisattvas" onward is the second major section: the extensive praise of virtues. Within this there are four sub-sections: first, the positional firmness of the bodhisattvas; second, from "well able to teach and transform" onward, the firmness of practice; third, from "constantly able to cultivate and gather" onward, the firmness of karmic action; fourth, from "such bodhisattvas" onward, the firmness of breadth.

### Positional Firmness

Position is not established in vain; it encompasses practice as its substance. Practice does not operate alone; it relies on position to advance. Hence practice is discussed next. Practice has no separate creation; its substance is precisely the karma of body, speech, and mind. Hence karmic action is discussed next. Yet practice-virtue is vast and all-pervading, exhausting the nature of the Dharma. Hence the firmness of breadth is discussed next.

Within position there are two aspects: first, the practice-characteristics of the self-portion position; second, from "the realm of all tathagatas" onward, the practice-characteristics of the other-portion position.

"All bodhisattvas" refers to the above-mentioned bodhisattvas of one more birth. "All practices of all bodhisattvas" reveals the causal practices from the primordial beginning. "Realm of wisdom" means the substance is revealed, outwardly luminous, and fully illuminates the dharma-realm: this is called "wisdom." The substance illuminates inwardly with equal, one-flavor [awareness]: this is called "insight." "Realm" means the scope or range. "Self-mastery" means that obstacles are exhausted, fetters are gone, pure and ripened unhindered practice. "Realm of all tathagatas" means that the other-portion is well accomplished. "All able to enter" means deep entry into the superior portion with practice corresponding to the substance of reality.

[The commentary continues with detailed analysis of the firmness of practice (divided into self-portion and other-portion), the firmness of karmic action, and the firmness of breadth. It analyzes the six phrases describing benefit to others through the three wheels of body, speech, and mind, the beginning and mature stages of self-benefit and other-benefit practice, and the relationship between the bodhisattva's self-substance dharma-realm practice and the other-portion progression into the buddhas' practices.]

### The Names of the Bodhisattvas

From "their names were" onward is the second major section: listing the names of those with these virtues. There are three ways to explain the names.

First, according to the conversion-teaching: names are given as appropriate for the beings who hear them, whether corresponding or not corresponding [to the bodhisattvas' actual qualities]. Just as in proper meaning one may use conventional language, and in conventional language one may embed proper meaning. Thus, a bodhisattva's name is like that of Avalokitesvara, who manifests the name of virtue for the sake of wholesome beings. Yet the bodhisattva's actual virtue is not necessarily exactly this: this is meaning embedded within language. And yet it simultaneously is the bodhisattva's actual virtue: this is language expressing meaning.

Second, according to the principle-teaching: this is language-following-meaning. Knowing the flavor, clothing the flavor; knowing the meaning, relying on phenomena to express meaning; following characteristics to know reality, thereby manifesting the bodhisattvas' actual virtue.

Third, according to the practice-teaching: the intent is to reveal the person through the Dharma. Those who hear understand the Dharma and also know the person. The intent is to reveal the Dharma through the person. Those who hear understand the person and also know the Dharma. Just as hearing "Vimalakirti" (維摩詰 *Wéimójié*, "Stainless Reputation") immediately conveys the gate of inconceivable unobstructed liberation. Merely hearing "Samantabhadra Bodhisattva" (普賢菩薩 *Pǔxián púsà*) immediately conveys the dharma-realm practice-gate. Manjusri (文殊師利 *Wénshūshīlì*), the celestial maiden, and others are all gates of wondrous-wisdom practice. Vajragarbha (金剛藏 *Jīngāng zàng*) and the rest also convey that all bodhisattva practices are vast as the dharma-realm, ultimate as empty space, equal and non-dual, revealing the self-substance's true and complete profound Dharma-gate.

### Five Ways of Establishing Names

There are broadly five kinds of name-establishment: (1) from causes and conditions, as in Sadaprarudita Bodhisattva (常啼 *Chángtí*, "Ever-Weeping"), who was called this because as a child his parents gave him that name, and now as a bodhisattva he always grieves for sentient beings; (2) from the practice-gate originally cultivated, as in Maitreya Bodhisattva (彌勒 *Mílè*, "Kindness"), who in a past life as a king, upon seeing a monk's meditation-light surpassing all others and learning it came from cultivating the kindness-samadhi, vowed to always cultivate kindness, and thus is now called "Kindness Clan"; (3) from the distinctive counteractive practice for self-benefit; (4) from the virtue of benefiting others according to different karmic connections for transformation, like a versatile artisan named according to the task; (5) from the self-substance dharma-gate, all embodying and realizing the dharma-realm, manifested through different causal practices.

### Individual Names Explained

**Vajragarbha** (金剛藏 *Jīngāng zàng*): Virtue exhausting the depths of the ocean; hence "Vajra Treasury."

**Ratnagarbha** (寶藏 *Bǎozàng*): Benefit inexhaustible, like a treasure used in service.

**Padmagarbha** (蓮華藏 *Liánhuá zàng*): The self-substance's causal practice entering the dharma-realm, like a lotus treasury.

**Jayagarbha** (勝藏 *Shèngzàng*): The cultivation that clarifies severance of delusion. It is not called "victorious" because of cutting off afflictions, but because of entering nirvana-nature without cutting off afflictions. As the *Vimalakirti Sutra* says: "Like a victorious wrestler who can overcome all." This bodhisattva encounters all afflictions without obstruction, hence "victorious." "Treasury" signifies that unconditioned practice can dispel the darkness of birth and death.

**Suryagarbha** (曰藏 *Yuē zàng*): Conditioned-arising gathering that does not abandon worldly practice; illumination within darkness.

**Suddhasuryagarbha** (淨圓藏 *Jìng yuán zàng*): The self-substance's worldly [practice], illumination within darkness yet always pure and luminous, surpassing in excellence.

**Sarvalokavyuhalamkaragarbha** (照一切世間莊嚴藏): The self-substance's inner luminous, perfectly pure great wisdom blazes with a distinctive stream; hence "illuminating all worlds." Skillful and wondrous and outstanding, hence "adornment." Virtue that produces, hence "adornment." Hence named "treasury." The self-substance's causal practice is called "bodhisattva."

**Vimukticandra** (解脫月 *Jiětuō yuè*): This is a bodhisattva at the culmination of the understanding-and-practice [stage], who has not yet achieved actual realization. As the person addressed by the teaching, this leading questioner has his name singled out.

### The Number of Bodhisattvas

"Such bodhisattvas" introduces the third major section: the number of those with these virtues. "Immeasurable" means their position exhausts the bounded. "Boundless" means their practice pervades the dharma-realm. "Asamkhyeya" (阿僧祇 *āsēngqí*) means beyond all limits. "Inconceivable" means their substance is beyond discrimination. "Incalculable" explains "immeasurable" as profoundly weighty. "Immeasurable" explains "boundless" as vastly great. "Without divisions" [corresponds to] *asamkhyeya*. "Inexpressible" explains the substance as beyond discrimination, meaning not one-by-one. "Inexpressible" explains being beyond discrimination, meaning not different-from-different.

[Extended discussion citing the *Asamkhyeya Chapter* of the Avatamsaka Sutra, where Bodhisattva Heart-King asks the Buddha about these numerical terms, and the Buddha provides a sequence of a hundred levels of exponentially increasing numbers leading up to *asamkhyeya*, followed by twenty-four more levels. These all refer to the dharma-realm practice-and-function of the buddhas and bodhisattvas. The commentary discusses how these terms point to the self-substance dharma-gate, the practice-and-virtue dharma-gate, the principle-fusing conditioned-arising dharma-gate, the wisdom dharma-gate, the causal-practice dharma-gate, the merit dharma-gate, and the true-teaching dharma-gate.]

**"Vajragarbha Bodhisattva as the foremost"** has two meanings: first, his position is ultimate, his practice foremost, and his virtue covers the great assembly, hence he is foremost; second, he is the substance of reality for all bodhisattvas' practice-virtue, hence he is foremost. Furthermore, he is foremost because the essential purport arises through this person, and so the teaching's salvific benefit is displayed at the beginning.
