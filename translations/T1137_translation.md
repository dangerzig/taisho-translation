# 佛說善法方便陀羅尼經
## Sūtra of the Good Dharma Skillful Means Dhāraṇī Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1137

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown.

---

Thus have I heard. At one time the Buddha was dwelling in the land of Magadha at the site of quiescent awakening beneath the bodhi tree, together with a great assembly of bhikṣu monks, including the greatly wise Śāriputra, the elder Maudgalyāyana, the elder Ānanda, and other great śrāvakas, as well as a multitude of bodhisattva mahāsattvas: Vajradhvaja Bodhisattva, Vajragarbha Bodhisattva, Maitreya Bodhisattva, and such great bodhisattva mahāsattvas of this Fortunate Kalpa.

Then the Vajradhara Spirit addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! The Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha truly knows and sees the radiant power of this Good Skillful Means Dhāraṇī Mantra. Its force can crush and extinguish all evils. For those without protection, it can provide protection. If one encounters all manner of evil companions, poisonous nāgas, ghosts, yakṣas, rākṣasas, dānavas, kumbhāṇḍas, humans, and non-humans, the fears of blades and cudgels, harm from enemies, sorcery, and curses, none of these can cause disturbance. In all four dignified postures of walking, standing, sitting, and lying down, it never abandons sentient beings, just as I now attend and guard the Tathāgata and never leave his side for even a moment. This dhāraṇī at all times protects sentient beings, benefits sentient beings, and never forsakes them, in just the same way. Therefore I now entreat the Tathāgata to proclaim this dhāraṇī. This dhāraṇī is itself the bodhisattva, is itself the Mahāyāna. Why? Because for good men and good women who lack protection, it can provide protection and extinguish all quarrels, imprisonment, and unjust calamities."

At that time the Buddha told the Vajradhara Spirit: "Excellent, excellent! Vajradhara Spirit! As you have said, you are indeed able to entreat the Tathāgata for the sake of sentient beings. Listen attentively now, and I shall proclaim for you the Good Dharma Skillful Means Dhāraṇī Mantra." Then the World-Honored One spoke the mantra:

*Ukumu kumā viṇi mādani vārā-cāle naśuśurī uttā svāhā*

At that time, when the Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha had spoken this Adornment Great Uṣṇīṣa-vijaya-rāja Dhāraṇī, he told the Vajradhara: "You should remember and uphold it in this way. The Tathāgata shall now further proclaim for you." He then spoke the mantra:

*Vajre vajre vajre tāri vajre bhāti vajre bhedī vajre datti cakra vajre cakra-tāri tāri tāri bhāṃ mūri cāri śuśurī paṃga viviri rīri śīri śīri rūrū ci cāre cūri mūri maṇḍari sarva-pāpa-viśā ṭūṭi ṭūṭi biri mare hatani bhāre bhāre bhādī vaṭi vīti vīre rū sāre śaravera sanī rāvā nāre yemā cārina indra-vaṭi titi rāyaṇa namo maheśvarāya nirīśi vāṇi pāpa-campāni karāvaṭi bhūtāvaṭi sattā-kaṭi sumāvaṭi sumāṃ svāhā*

The Buddha told the Vajradhara: "I shall further proclaim for you a divine mantra that can enable the practitioner to attain longevity in this present life." He then spoke the mantra:

*Hara hara hira*

Then the Vajradhara addressed the Buddha: "Yes, World-Honored One! I shall receive and uphold it."

*Kati tapacita ayinā hāri campāni kakadani adā ahani mārga virūhani śu-svabhāti pusvabhāti hiri hiri yathā-śī yathā-ki nī yathā-pārañca yathā-bhadaṃ yathā-kīdadaṃ*

Having spoken this mantra, he told the Vajradhara: "This Good Dharma Dhāraṇī Mantra has been spoken by the Tathāgata for the benefit of all sentient beings and out of great compassion. I shall now further proclaim it for you. As the tathāgatas, arhats, and samyak-saṃbuddhas of the past have wondrously proclaimed, and as the buddhas of past, future, and present have sealed and confirmed, it is true and not false."

Then the World-Honored One emitted the great person's light from between his brows, universally illuminating all buddha-lands. Wherever this light shone, it universally benefited immeasurable sentient beings. The buddhas of those regions, seeing this light, each rose from their seats and together followed the light to the Sahā World. Having arrived, they praised in unison: "Excellent! Śākyamuni, World-Honored One, you are well able to entrust and benefit all sentient beings, bringing them peace and happiness. Excellent, excellent! Śākyamuni Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha! For the benefit of all sentient beings, for their protection, for their illumination, and for the sake of causing them to obtain joy, you have spoken this Good Dharma Dhāraṇī Mantra. The phrases spoken by us buddhas are likewise the same. Excellent! Śākyamuni, you who give generously! If any good man or good woman hears this Good Dharma Dhāraṇī Mantra and wishes to obtain its benefits, they should receive, uphold, read, and recite it each morning, and they will be protected by us buddhas. Wherever this mantra is recited, that place is auspicious, for we buddhas are all present there. Whatever extremely grave evil karma sentient beings have previously created is all extinguished without remainder. It can cause these good men and others to fully obtain twenty benefits: first, attaining longevity in the present life; second, peace and security in their dwelling; third, fame that spreads far; fourth, attaining honor and nobility in the present; fifth, abundant wealth and treasure; sixth, a majestic and outstanding appearance; seventh, courage and fearlessness; eighth, freedom from all illness; ninth, peace, security, and happiness; tenth, advancing without weariness; eleventh, being protected by all buddhas; twelfth, being guarded by all devas; thirteenth, being beloved and respected by others; fourteenth, seeing the buddhas in person; fifteenth, being protected by good friends; sixteenth, poisons and harm cannot reach them; seventeenth, evil ghosts are subdued; eighteenth, enemies naturally disappear; nineteenth, their retinue is accomplished; twentieth, wholesome wishes follow their heart.

"Thus, Vajradhara! This dhāraṇī mantra is the subtle and secret treasury of all buddhas, protected by all buddhas, true and not false, wondrous and well spoken."

Then the Vajradhara Spirit addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I shall also rejoice in, have faith in, and joyfully proclaim the divine mantra spoken by the Tathāgata. Why? Because the power of this dhāraṇī mantra can provide protection for those without protection, and its benefits are great. For the sake of such good men and good women, I shall now further proclaim the Good Dharma Dhāraṇī Mantra. Those who wish to obtain peace and happiness and seek liberation should, each morning, receive, uphold, read, and recite it, and they will obtain the protective power of the mantra and will certainly be forever freed from all suffering." He then spoke the mantra:

*Śikhi śikhi kici kici vāvā vāvā rūrū rūrū śiśi śiśi gara gara hīhī phapha phapha mase mase hana hana hana dhaha dhaha dhaha paca paca svāhā*

At that time the World-Honored One told the Vajradhara: "Excellent, excellent! Vajradhara Spirit! This dhāraṇī is most divine and most efficacious, and is therefore proclaimed for sentient beings. The divine power of this dhāraṇī mantra can block and shield every single pore, so that nāgas, ghosts, all epidemics, and all evil poisonous vapors cannot enter. It can also serve as the cause and condition for liberation."

At that time the Lord of the great trichiliocosm, Mahābrahmā Deva Rāja, rose from his seat, made obeisance to the Buddha, joined his palms toward the Buddha, and addressed the Buddha: "Excellent, World-Honored One! I too am pleased to praise, rejoice in, and assist in the accomplishment of benefits. Good men who practice this mantra should each morning receive, uphold, read, and recite this dhāraṇī, just as the World-Honored One now, wishing to benefit, bring peace, and protect all sentient beings, has spoken this Good Dharma Dhāraṇī Mantra." Then Mahābrahmā Deva Rāja spoke the mantra:

*Rimi rici ri svāhā māphuri svāhā mā svāhā pakapa-bha saṃtari svāhā*

Having spoken this mantra, he addressed the Buddha: "Great Virtuous Supreme Honored One! This dhāraṇī mantra as proclaimed can fully protect these good men and good women, and can also increase their lifespan. Therefore practitioners should each morning, in accordance with the dharma, read, recite, cultivate, and uphold it."

Then Śakra Devānām Indra also addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I too wish to benefit and protect such good men, and therefore proclaim this dhāraṇī, which is protected by the buddha world-honored ones. Therefore, if practitioners wish to obtain the merit and benefits of this dhāraṇī mantra, they should each morning read, recite, receive, and uphold it." Then Śakra spoke the mantra:

*Viṇi vāranī vādā-māraṃti cici ci kuri gandhāri māra-māra-vāhana mandāraṇī dhāra-māriṇī cakra-vāṣi śaṃbhāri śaṃvāri svāhā*

At that time there were also the Four Great Heavenly Kings: Vaiśravaṇa, Dhṛtarāṣṭra, Virūḍhaka, and Virūpākṣa. They joined their palms in reverence and addressed the Buddha: "We four kings shall also protect such good men and good women. Those who delight in cultivating this Good Skillful Means Dhāraṇī should each morning receive, uphold, read, and recite it." Then the Four Kings spoke the mantra:

*Phabha-śu phabha tūma pari hāre ari parā-śete śānti nimute maugāre tūti tūti svāhā*

At that time Śākyamuni Tathāgata raised his right hand and told Śakra Devānām Indra, Mahābrahmā Deva Rāja, and the Four World-Protectors: "Excellent, excellent! Great Virtuous Devas! You have been able to rejoice in and proclaim this Good Dharma Skillful Means Dhāraṇī King. This Good Dharma Dhāraṇī is protected by all buddhas. You should all constantly and well receive and uphold it."

At that time the World-Honored One further told the Vajradhara Spirit: "Excellent, excellent! Vajradhara Spirit! This Good Skillful Means and Longevity Dhāraṇī Mantra: these three mantras are all protected, praised, and confirmed by the tathāgatas of the ten directions, and guarded by the deva kings and powerful spirits. If any good men or good women wish to practice, they should first purify their body, speech, and mind, and each morning with earnest heart receive, uphold, and with a pure heart read and recite in accordance with the dharma. Those who can do this will find that no evil devas, nāgas, humans, or ghosts can inflict harm upon them or find any advantage."

At that time the bodhisattvas, the great assembly of śrāvakas, the Vajradhara Spirit, and all the deva kings, spirits, nāga kings, yakṣas, humans, and non-humans all joined in reverence, greatly rejoiced, and received, upheld, and put it into practice.
