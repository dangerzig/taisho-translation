# 大聖歡喜雙身毘那夜迦天形像品儀軌
## Ritual of the Image Chapter of the Great Holy Joyous Dual-Bodied Vināyaka Deva

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1274

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, composed by Jingse (憬瑟).

---

I now set down this secret ritual based on the oral transmission of Hanguang. The dual-bodied deva-king image: husband and wife, two devas, standing in mutual embrace. The height is seven *cun* or five *cun*. Both devas have elephant heads and human bodies.

The male deva's face rests against the right shoulder of the female deva so that the female deva's back is visible. Likewise, the female deva's face rests against the right shoulder of the male deva so that the male deva's back is visible. The soles of both feet are exposed. Their hands and feet are soft, as those of a plump and beautiful young woman.

The male deva wears no flower garland on the head; a red kāṣāya is draped over the shoulder. The female deva has a flower garland on the head but does not wear a kāṣāya; she has jeweled necklaces and bangles on hands and feet, and places both her feet upon the tips of the male deva's feet.

Both devas are white, the color of flesh, wearing red skirts. Each clasps the other's waist with both hands, the right hand covering the back of the left hand. The tip of the right middle finger of each deva reaches to the back of the middle joint of the other's left middle finger.

This embracing image represents the love of the six sense-bases. The love of the six sense-bases means: first, touching each other's backs with the nose in love; second, joining in love with the chest; third, embracing the waist in love with the hands; fourth, joining in love with the belly; fifth, treading in love with both feet; sixth, wearing a red skirt, which specifically represents love and reverence. If a practitioner makes and offers this Six-Loves-and-Reverence image, that person will certainly gain the love and reverence of kings, great ministers, queens, court ladies, and all people.

Generate a mind of courageous faith and do not give rise to a single thought of doubt or hesitation. Time and again the verified results are very many indeed.

If one wishes to practice the harmonizing method and remove obstacles, one should make and offer the six-armed deva image. Its form is elephant-headed with a human body, complete with six arms. The six arms are: left upper hand holding a knife, middle hand holding a fruit tray, lower hand holding a wheel; right upper hand holding a staff, middle hand holding a vajra, lower hand holding a lasso.

There is also a four-armed image: elephant-headed with a human body, complete with four arms. The right first hand holds a battle-axe; the second hand holds a tray of joy-balls. The left first hand holds a tusk; the second hand holds a jeweled staff.

Furthermore, according to the *Sūtra of Amoghapāśa Wrathful King Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva*, the four-armed deva form is described as this Vināyaka Vajra. Its form is elephant-headed with a human body, complete with four arms. The left first hand holds a vajra, the next hand holds a battle-axe; the right first hand holds a lasso, the next hand holds a trident.

This image-chapter ritual should be kept secret and transmitted only to chosen suitable vessels. This Great Holy Deva-king is a body of sovereign transformation of Maheśvara. The secret teachings say: because of sovereign mastery of the six supernatural powers, he is called the Holy Deva; because of sovereign mastery of wisdom, he is called Maheśvara; because he accomplishes love and reverence, he is called the Dual-Bodied Vināyaka King; because he accomplishes the five grains, he is called the Six-Armed Deva.

Moreover, as the sūtra states: "Maheśvara is sovereign in wisdom. When the great ocean nāga-kings send down rain, he can distinguish and count every single drop, understanding all within a single thought." Based on this holy statement, because of his sovereign mastery of wisdom, he is named Maheśvara.

Furthermore, the four-directional six-armed Vināyaka images are described as follows:

**East**: Cuisuì Deva (Crusher), also named the Great General Without-Sorrow. His form is like a deva in human shape with an elephant head set atop a celestial crown. The left hand holds a parasol canopy; the right hand holds a sword.

**South**: Yǐnshí Deva (Food and Drink), also named the Great General Adorned-Topknot. His form is like a deva in human shape with an elephant head set atop a celestial crown. The left hand holds a lasso; the right hand holds a flower garland.

**West**: Yīfu Deva (Garments), also named the Great General Crown-Walker. His form is like a deva in human shape with a nāga head set atop the crown. The left hand holds a bow; the right hand holds an arrow.

**North**: Xiàngtóu Deva (Elephant-Head), also named the Golden-Colored Gaṇapati. His form is elephant-headed with a human body. The left hand holds a white lapis lazuli jewel; the right hand holds a jeweled staff. Alternatively, he is complete with six arms, as previously described.

There is also a six-faced, six-armed image, as described in the sūtra. This method should be kept secret.
