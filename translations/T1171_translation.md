# The Vajraśekhara Sūtra Yoga: Methods of Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva

## *Jīngāng Dǐng Jīng Yújiā Wénshūshīlì Púsà Fǎ*

Taishō 1171

Translated by Amoghavajra (*Bùkōng*), Tang dynasty.

*Note: This is an esoteric ritual manual (yíguǐ) in two parts, both attributed to the Vajraśekhara Yoga tradition of Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva. The first part presents the famous five-syllable dhāraṇī of Mañjuśrī (a ra pa ca na), together with four types of maṇḍala (great maṇḍala, samaya maṇḍala, dharma maṇḍala, and karma maṇḍala), instructions for painting Mañjuśrī's image (five-topknotted youth, golden colored, holding the prajñā sword and Prajñāpāramitā manuscript, seated on a moon-disc), and the recitation counts required for various accomplishments (500,000 recitations for unlimited eloquence; one koṭi for freedom from suffering; five koṭi for anuttara-samyak-saṃbodhi). The second part is a detailed sādhana (念誦儀軌) in verse with mantras, presenting a complete practice session: preliminary prostrations to the four directional buddhas (Akṣobhya, Ratnasambhava, Avalokiteśvara-rāja, Amoghasiddhi), the purification of three karmas, vajra-binding, opening the heart-gate, visualization of the moon-disc and the seed-syllable "dhaṃ" transforming into the prajñā sword, identification with Mañjuśrī, and an extensive set of mudrā-mantra sequences (the vajra-sword mudrā, crown-mudrā, armor-mudrā, the four gathering-mudrās, the eight inner and outer offerings, and the one-hundred-syllable mantra of purification), culminating in the promise that diligent four-session daily practice leads to the first bhūmi in this life and full enlightenment within sixteen rebirths.*

---

## Part One: The Five-Syllable Dhāraṇī

At that time, Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva, in the great assembly of Vairocana [Buddha], rose from his seat, bowed at the Buddha's feet, and said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I now wish to speak the original five-syllable dhāraṇī. If any good man or good woman recites it even once, the merit equals that of receiving, upholding, reading, and reciting all the sūtras and Dharma-meanings spoken by all the tathāgatas."

Vairocana Buddha told Mañjuśrī: "Speak as you wish."

Thereupon Mañjuśrī spoke the vidyā:

[Dhāraṇī: a ra pa ca na]

As soon as this dhāraṇī was spoken, the Dharma taught by all the tathāgatas was absorbed into the five-syllable dhāraṇī, enabling sentient beings to accomplish the Prajñāpāramitā.

### The Four Maṇḍalas

"I shall now explain the maṇḍala method. On the fourteenth or fifteenth day [of the fortnight], select an extremely pure place and construct a maṇḍala. Apply cow dung to the ground, then spread white sandalwood paste, of whatever size you wish. In the center of the maṇḍala, paint Mañjuśrī in the form of the five-topknotted youth. His body is the color of turmeric gold, adorned with various necklaces and ornaments. His right hand holds a vajra-sword; his left hand holds a manuscript [of the Prajñāpāramitā]. He sits within a moon-disc. Around the four sides of the moon-disc, write the five-syllable dhāraṇī. The ācārya, before this altar, forms the vajra-sword mudrā and recites. At that time, Mañjuśrī empowers this ācārya, who immediately obtains unobstructed eloquence. Moreover, [Mañjuśrī] manifests in person and explains, one by one, the profound meanings of this dhāraṇī.

"The ācārya should then bow and exit the ritual space. For the disciples, he confers the bodhisattva precepts, covers their eyes with red silk, and leads them to the entrance of the altar, where they stand in order. The ācārya then tells the disciples: 'You have now obtained the Prajñāpāramitā of all the tathāgatas. From now on, you must not reveal this vidyā to others, lest they violate your samaya. This dhāraṇī must be kept utterly secret.'

"'A ra pa ca na' means 'the fulfillment of all wishes.' Why?

"'A' means 'aspiring toward bodhi.' 'Ra' means 'deeply attached to and not abandoning sentient beings.' 'Pa' means 'the truth of the supreme meaning' [paramārtha]. 'Ca' means 'wondrous practice.' 'Na' means 'without self-nature' [niḥsvabhāva]. Aspiring toward bodhi, not abandoning sentient beings, deeply entering the truth of the supreme meaning to practice, and cultivating [the understanding that] all dharmas have no self-nature: if one cultivates thus, all wishes are fulfilled. Among all these wishes, one attains the Tathāgata's rank and the Vajra-holder's [rank] without specifically seeking them.

### Contract-Mudrā Maṇḍala

"I now also explain the contract-mudrā maṇḍala (*qìyìn màntuóluó*). In the center of the altar, paint a vajra-sword. On each of the four sides, in their respective directions, paint the eight offering-mudrās and the four gathering-mudrās. By reciting before this altar, accomplishment will soon be attained.

### Samaya Maṇḍala

"I now also explain the samaya maṇḍala. In the center of the altar, write the five syllables, and [around them] the seed-syllables of the eight offerings and four gatherings. By reciting before this altar and making this statement: 'The gate of the syllable "a": all dharmas are originally unborn,' and reciting daily, before long all karmic obstructions will be eliminated and accomplishment will be swiftly attained.

### Karma Maṇḍala

"I now also explain the karma maṇḍala. In the center of the altar, place a volume of the Prajñāpāramitā Sūtra. Read and recite daily, and make various offerings. Before long, accomplishment will be attained.

### Painting the Image

"I now explain the method for painting the image. On white cloth or silk, paint Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva seated within a moon-disc. Inside the disc, write the five syllables around the circumference. On the four sides, paint the eight offerings and four gatherings, as in the great maṇḍala method. Before this image, recite properly, making this statement: 'All dharmas are accomplished by their own nature.' When the recitation count reaches five hundred thousand, one immediately obtains inexhaustible eloquence equal to Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva's, without any difference. One will fly through space, and all worldly and supramundane affairs will be accomplished.

"Further, when the count reaches one koṭi, one is freed from all sufferings. At two koṭi, all transgressions including the five heinous acts are exhausted without remainder. At three koṭi, one realizes all samādhi-gates. At four koṭi, one obtains the great power of unfailing memory. At five koṭi, one attains anuttara-samyak-saṃbodhi.

"Another method: around a śarīra stūpa, write the five-syllable dhāraṇī in a rightward circumambulation. Walk around the stūpa reciting without interruption, until completing five lakṣa [five hundred thousand] recitations. At that time, the Tathāgata, Mañjuśrī, Vajradhara, and others will manifest in the sky and furthermore teach the Dharma."

## Part Two: The Yoga Ritual and Offering Procedures for Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva

> Homage to the eternally youthful Wondrous Auspicious One!
> I teach the mantra-recitation according to Yoga,
> The vajra-recitation of body, speech, and mind:
> The Tathāgata's profound gate of the three mysteries.
> The practitioner should generate the Samantabhadra-mind,
> Receive the vajra-precepts from a teacher,
> Not sparing body or life, arousing compassion:
> Only then may one enter the wheel of liberation.
> One should receive the samaya from a teacher,
> The mudrās and secret words as stated in the sūtra.
> Revere the ācārya as one would the Buddha,
> And toward fellow students maintain a respectful mind.
> Whether on a mountain, in a forest hermitage,
> By a flowing spring or bathing pool, under a pleasant tree,
> On a mountain peak, in a stone cave, or by a solitary tree:
> Establish the altar according to the original method.
> Adorn a pure chamber and install the principal deity.
> According to your ability, make offerings and abide single-mindedly.
> Contemplate the ocean of buddhas in the ten directions
> And offer worship at the feet of all tathāgatas.
> To perfect the vajra of the three karmas,
> On both hands, the tongue, and the heart,
> Visualize the five-wisdom vajra,
> For through this empowerment, all siddhi [is attained].
> Next form the mudrā called "Awakening":
> Both hands form vajra-fists,
> Hook the index fingers and raise the middle fingers,
> Pressing the tips together sideways: this accomplishes awakening.

The awakening mantra:

[Dhāraṇī]

### Prostrations to the Four Directional Buddhas

> Reverently bow to Akṣobhya in the East,
> Offering body and life, seeking non-retrogression.
> Prostrate with the whole body touching the ground, bowing with the heart.
> With vajra-añjali above the crown.

Mantra for offering body and life:

[Dhāraṇī]

> Next reverently bow to Ratnasambhava,
> Offering body and life, seeking the abhiṣeka rank.
> With vajra-añjali at the heart,
> Touch the forehead to the ground in sincere worship.

Mantra for seeking abhiṣeka:

[Dhāraṇī]

> Next bow to Avalokiteśvara-rāja,
> Offering body and life, seeking samādhi.
> With vajra-añjali placed above the crown,
> Touch the mouth to the ground in sincere worship.

Mantra for seeking samādhi:

[Dhāraṇī]

> Next bow to Amoghasiddhi,
> Offering body and life, seeking skillful wisdom.
> With vajra-añjali placed at the heart,
> Touch the crown to the ground in reverent worship.

Mantra for seeking skillful means:

[Dhāraṇī]

> Next reverently bow to the buddhas of the ten directions.
> Visualize your body present before all the buddhas.
> Contemplate the five limbs touching the ground in worship.
> Then form the vajra-samaya [mudrā].

Mantra for universal worship:

[Dhāraṇī]

> Next recite the mantra for accomplishing all beings.
> For all sentient beings who seek the supreme:
> May all the tathāgatas empower them,
> Swiftly accomplishing the supreme Way.

Mantra for ripening all beings:

*Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-uṣṇīṣa-sarva-sattvānāṃ sarva-siddhayoḥ saṃpratyayantāṃ tathāgata-śāsana-adhiṣṭhāntāṃ.*

### Purification and Vajra-Binding

> Next sit in cross-legged posture with body upright.
> Purify the three karmas, making them clean.
> Because the original nature of all dharmas is pure,
> Let this body of mine be pure and stainless.

Mantra for purifying the three karmas:

[Dhāraṇī]

Next, with vajra-añjali, cross the ten fingers at the tips and recite:

[Dhāraṇī]

Deeply interlace all fingers into fists, forming the vajra-binding, and recite:

[Dhāraṇī]

### Opening the Heart-Gate

> Next, open the heart to enter the Buddha's wisdom.
> On both breasts, visualize two syllables,
> "Traṭ" in white,
> Imagining them as two door-panels.
> With both hands form the vajra-binding
> And strike the heart three times to open the door.

Mantra for opening the heart:

[Dhāraṇī]

> Contemplate the wondrous lotus of the "a" gate,
> And with the mudrā summon [the deity] into the heart-palace.
> Concentration and wisdom become the moon, the vajra-binding.
> In dhyāna-wisdom palms, visualize the syllable entering.

Mantra for vajra-entry:

[Dhāraṇī]

> Next form the mudrā to close the heart-door:
> As the previous entry-mudrā's emblem,
> Bend the middle fingers onto the ring-finger tips.
> This achieves adamantine non-retrogression.

Vajra-fist mantra:

[Dhāraṇī]

### The Mañjuśrī Samaya and Visualization

> Next form the Mañjuśrī samaya:
> Interlace the ten fingers into a full moon,
> Extend the ring fingers straight like a vajra-sword.
> Visualize your body as equal to Wondrous Auspicious One.

Samaya mantra:

[Dhāraṇī]

> Next form the joyful samādhi-mudrā:
> Concentration and wisdom as the moon, firmly bound.
> The patience and vow fingers cross in the middle,
> Charity-wisdom and dhyāna-wisdom fingers stand touching.

Mantra:

[Dhāraṇī]

> Next form the Vajra Trailokyavijaya [mudrā].
> Visualize your body as identical to him, without difference.
> Both hands form vajra-fists,
> Hook the index fingers and raise the middle fingers.
> Turn left to dispel; turn right to bind.
> With a compassionate mind, display the wrathful form.

Trailokyavijaya mantra:

[Dhāraṇī]

> Next form the lotus samaya,
> To accomplish the contemplative practice.
> Interlace the ten fingers to make the moon,
> With dhyāna-wisdom and charity-wisdom fingers upright and touching.

Mantra:

[Dhāraṇī]

### The Ānāpāna Visualization

The practitioner should cultivate the method of ānāpāna [mindfulness of breathing]. In practicing this, keep the limbs still, stop the in-breath and out-breath, making them subtle, and do not allow the mind to scatter. Then contemplate all the buddhas in space, like sesame seeds filling the ten directions. With a vajra finger-snap, [the buddhas] address the practitioner: "Good man! Contemplate your original mind."

Hearing this, the practitioner visualizes himself bowing at the feet of all the buddhas. After bowing, he contemplates his original mind attentively and says to all the buddhas: "The characteristics of the mind have no substance. How can one cultivate and realize [anything]?"

All the buddhas tell the practitioner: "Good man! Contemplate the moon-disc in your heart, as if in a light mist."

Then recite the mantra for polishing and clarifying bodhi:

[Dhāraṇī]

The practitioner should contemplate clearly and attentively. Before long, he will see the pure bodhicitta, free from all defilements, pure as the full moon. Then recite the bodhicitta mantra:

[Dhāraṇī]

Visualize within the bodhi-moon the syllable "dhaṃ" in golden color, radiant as the sun, emitting great light. It then transforms into the Prajñāpāramitā sword, free from all discrimination, able to sever afflictions. Visualize it as the wisdom-sword:

[Dhāraṇī]

Visualize the wisdom-sword gradually expanding until it pervades the entire dharmadhātu:

[Dhāraṇī]

Visualize the wisdom-sword gradually contracting until it equals the size of one's own body:

[Dhāraṇī]

To make the wisdom-sword firm and unscattering:

[Dhāraṇī]

Visualize all the tathāgatas in space entering the wisdom-sword, becoming one body. Reflect: "As is the nature of those buddhas, so too am I":

[Dhāraṇī]

Visualize the wisdom-sword gradually transforming into Mañjuśrī Kumārabhūta Bodhisattva, endowed with great awesome virtue. His body is adorned with various necklaces and ornaments. On his crown, visualize five topknots. His right hand holds the wisdom-sword; his left hand holds a blue lotus flower, upon which rests a Prajñāpāramitā manuscript. His body is the color of turmeric gold. Mentally recite "a ra pa ca na" once.

### The Vajra-Sword Mudrā and Subsequent Mudrās

> Next form the vajra wisdom-sword mudrā:
> Interlace [the fingers] into the full moon,
> Extend the ring fingers upright like a sword.
> Seal the heart, forehead, throat, and crown:
> This accomplishes body-protection and establishes the principal deity.

The true-word:

[Dhāraṇī]

### The Crown Mudrā

> Next form the crown jewel-seal:
> Interlace both hands into the full moon,
> Bend the index fingers back like a jewel shape,
> With thumbs pressing beneath;
> Place it on the forehead, then part to both sides.
> Then form the wisdom-fist as if tying a cloth;
> Slowly extend both hands forward and unfurl them:
> Know that one has now bound the stainless silk.

The true-word:

[Dhāraṇī]

> Next form the jewel-sword self-consecration seal:
> Join both palms, bend the index fingers,
> Tuck the thumbs into the palms;
> Place it on the forehead, then part to both sides.

The consecration true-word:

[Dhāraṇī]

### The Armor Mudrā

> Next form the armor mudrā:
> Both hands make vajra fists,
> Place them before the heart with index fingers upright.
> On left and right, visualize the syllable "oṃ";
> Visualize streaming emerald light.
> Circle three times from the heart around to the back,
> Down to the navel, the knees, back up around the waist,
> From the waist to the heart, across both shoulders,
> From the shoulders to the throat and around the neck,
> From the back of the neck to the forehead,
> From the forehead to the crown; form the wisdom-fist,
> Slowly unfurl downward like hanging ribbons,
> Then gaze upward and revolve as in a dance;
> Interlace both hands into the full moon.

The armor true-word:

[Dhāraṇī]

### The Joy Mudrā

> Next perform the vajra hand-clap rite:
> Bring both hands together and clap once.
> By performing this clapping seal and true-word together,
> One causes the holy assembly to generate great joy
> And swiftly obtains the principal deity's adamantine body.

The joy true-word:

[Dhāraṇī]

### Visualization and the Four Gathering Mudrās

> Next, before the practitioner's seat, visualize
> An eight-petaled lotus complete with pistils and stamens.
> Above it, visualize a lion's supreme throne;
> Upon the throne, a seven-jeweled pavilion.
> Within, visualize the seven-jeweled lotus king;
> Above, visualize the syllable "dhaṃ" endowed with majestic radiance
> Illuminating the dharma-realm without exception.
> That syllable transforms into the vajra-sword;
> Contemplate it clearly in its original form.
> Summon the bodhisattva to enter the visualized body:
> Both hands form the full moon, index fingers hooked;
> Visualize oneself as identical to that bodhisattva.

The Vajra-Hook Bodhisattva true-word:

[Dhāraṇī]

> Next form the lasso seal to enter the honored one's body:
> Form the full moon, thumbs pressing wisdom-fingers into the palms.
> Through the empowerment of this secret seal,
> One transforms into a single body, without difference.

The Vajra-Lasso true-word:

[Dhāraṇī]

> Next form the chain seal to make it firm:
> Form the full moon with four fingers interlocked like rings.
> Through the power of this secret seal,
> All is made adamantine and unchanging.

The Vajra-Chain true-word:

[Dhāraṇī]

> Next form the bell seal to generate joy:
> Thumbs and little fingers within the palms, like a hand-bell.
> Causing the principal deity and the assembly to rejoice,
> The empowerment swiftly brings wondrous accomplishment.

The Vajra-Bell true-word:

### The Arghya Offering

The practitioner should next cup arghya water in both hands, visualizing the washing of the feet of the Vajra-Sword Bodhisattva and all the retinue. Alternatively, one may empower the arghya with the hundred-syllable mantra and offer it. The arghya true-word:

[Dhāraṇī]

### The Hundred-Syllable Mantra and Conclusion

The practitioner should next recite the one-hundred-and-eight-name praise as an offering to the principal deity. Then form the vajra-sword mudrā at the heart and recite the hundred-syllable mantra to empower oneself. Even if, in past lives, one has committed all manner of evil karma including the five heinous acts and all other transgressions, through the empowerment of this hundred-syllable mantra, all karmic obstructions are completely eliminated. In this very body, one attains the Śūraṅgama Samādhi. If the mind is scattered, recite this vidyā seven times, twenty-one times, forty-nine times, or one hundred and eight times. The mind will leave its entanglements and swiftly attain samādhi.

The hundred-syllable mantra:

[Dhāraṇī]

Without dissolving the previous mudrā, contemplate the principal deity before you, and visualize your own body as identical to the principal deity, without difference. Contemplating clearly, recite the five-syllable dhāraṇī, either in vajra-voice or in clear lotus-voice, seven times or twenty-one times, then release the mudrā above the crown. Next, take up the prayer beads and hold them at the heart for mantra recitation, neither too fast nor too slow: one hundred and eight times, one thousand, or up to ten thousand times. When the count is complete, hold the beads in both hands and place them atop the crown, then return them to their place.

For samādhi recitation: contemplate within the great perfect-mirror wisdom the five syllables arrayed. Contemplating clearly, in accordance with the meaning, the mind unites with the Prajñāpāramitā. This is called samādhi recitation.

If the body grows weary, form the principal deity's sword-mudrā, recite the five-syllable dhāraṇī seven times, then make the eight great offerings to all the buddhas, praising them with wondrous words. Offer arghya water. With the Trailokyavijaya mudrā, turn leftward to dissolve the boundary. Form the vajra-sword mudrā and respectfully send all the holy ones back to their original palaces:

[Dhāraṇī]

Having done this, again form the samaya mudrā, recite the empowerment mantra, and seal the four places [heart, forehead, throat, crown]. Then perform the abhiṣeka, don the vajra armor, and make the four prostrations to the buddhas of the four directions as before, with confession and vow-making. Then, in a quiet place, adorned with incense and flowers, abiding in the samādhi of the principal deity, read and recite the Vaipulya and all Mahāyāna sūtras, the Mahāprajñāpāramitā, the large text, and even the Mañjuśrī Prajñā, walking in meditation at will.

> If a wise person practices according to this method,
> Diligently cultivating four sessions day and night,
> In this very life they will realize the Stage of Joy [first bhūmi],
> And after sixteen rebirths, attain perfect enlightenment.

### The Mañjuśrī Wrathful Dhāraṇī

[Dhāraṇī]

This mantra is used for body-protection, dispelling [evil], establishing boundaries, and purifying all incense, flowers, and offering implements.

> May all beings encounter this teaching,
> With Mañjuśrī as their constant good friend,
> Swiftly realizing the skillful wisdom of prajñā,
> And quickly attaining the supreme, the Two-Footed Honored One.

The Five-Syllable Dhāraṇī [with variant forms]:

*Arapacana. Oṃ vajra-tīkṣṇa. Oṃ nukha-cida. Oṃ khaḍga-graha-khaṃ. Oṃ khaḍga-sattva.*
