# Siddhi Methods of the Great Yakṣiṇī Joyful Mother and Her Beloved Son

## *Dà Yàochā Nǚ Huānxǐ Mǔ bìng Àizǐ Chéngjiù Fǎ*

Taishō 1260

Translated by Amoghavajra (*Bùkōng*), Tang dynasty.

*Note: This is an esoteric ritual manual (chéngjiù fǎ, "siddhi methods") centered on the yakṣiṇī Hārītī (Huānxǐ Mǔ, "Joyful Mother") and her son Priyaṅkara (Bìlǐyùnjiā). The text opens with a narrative of the Buddha at the Bamboo Grove in Rājagṛha, where Hārītī, the beautiful consort of the yakṣa general Pañcika and mother of five hundred sons, takes refuge, receives the five precepts, and pledges to protect all beings rather than devour children. In exchange, the Buddha instructs his disciples to set aside food for her at every meal. Hārītī then teaches her own dhāraṇī and provides elaborate ritual instructions for invoking her presence: painting her image as a celestial woman seated on a jeweled platform with children at her knees, constructing a secret altar with specific offerings, and performing mantra recitation until she manifests. The text includes numerous subsidiary methods for finding hidden treasures, winning the love of others, curing poison, resolving lawsuits, assisting difficult childbirth, and summoning distant persons. The second half provides parallel rituals for her son Priyaṅkara, including instructions for carving his sandalwood image and homa procedures for obtaining his aid.*

---

At that time, the Buddha was dwelling in the Bamboo Grove Monastery in Rājagṛha, teaching the essentials of the Dharma for devas and humans. There was a great yakṣiṇī named Joyful [Hārītī], beautiful in appearance, with five thousand attendants. She constantly dwelt in the land of China, protecting the world. She was the daughter of the great yakṣa general Sāta and was married to the great yakṣa general Pañcika. She had given birth to five hundred sons, all of great power. Together with her retinue, she came to the Buddha's presence and sat to one side.

The Buddha addressed Joyful Mother: "You should now accept the Tathāgata's instruction. I wish you to abandon your violent ways and protect all sentient beings. In this city of Rājagṛha and throughout Jambudvīpa, you shall grant fearlessness to all the sons and daughters born to women."

Joyful Mother said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! If that is so, what shall I and my children eat?"

The Buddha said: "Simply be compassionate and do not harm sentient beings. I shall instruct my śrāvaka disciples, at every meal, to always give you food. At the end of each row [of monks' meals], a portion of food shall be set out, and your name shall be called along with those of your children, so that you may all be satisfied. If there is food remaining, you may redistribute it to all the ghosts and spirits, transferring your merit with a sincere mind so that they too may be satisfied."

Joyful Mother said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I now take refuge in the Tathāgata. I respectfully accept the Buddha's instruction and dare not transgress it. In the great city of Rājagṛha and in all the lands, I shall protect all the sons and daughters born to the people, causing them to be at peace and happy, not permitting any evil ghosts or spirits to take advantage of them. I only beg that the Tathāgata protect and be mindful of me."

The Buddha said: "Excellent, excellent! Joyful Mother! You should now, within the Tathāgata's true Dharma and Vinaya, receive and uphold the three refuges and five precepts, so that throughout the long night you may be liberated from all suffering and attain great happiness. That is to say: taking refuge in the Buddha, the Dharma, and the Sangha; not taking life; and up to not drinking intoxicants: these are your training rules. You should uphold them."

At that time, Joyful Mother, having received the three refuges and the five training rules, leaped with joy, circumambulated the Buddha three times, and said: "World-Honored One! I have now received the Tathāgata's protection. I have a dhāraṇī of my own mind that can remove all calamities, difficulties, and terrors. If anyone receives and upholds these syllables, my attendants will constantly guard and protect them, causing them to obtain happiness. I beg the Tathāgata to allow me to proclaim it."

The Buddha said: "Speak as you wish."

Joyful Mother then, before the Buddha, spoke the dhāraṇī:

[Dhāraṇī]

"World-Honored One! This dhāraṇī of mine has great awesome power, like a true cintāmaṇi jewel, able to fulfill the wishes of all beings. Only the Tathāgata and the bodhisattvas can bear witness to me."

The Buddha said: "Joyful Mother! You are able to benefit sentient beings and protect my Dharma by speaking this dhāraṇī. In future ages, I entrust my disciples to your protection, so that they may all be at peace and happy."

### Ritual Instructions for Painting and Worship

Joyful Mother said to the Buddha: "As the Buddha has holy commanded, I shall carry it out. World-Honored One! If one wishes to accomplish the method of this dhāraṇī, first, on white cloth or on silk, of whatever size, paint my image as Joyful Mother in the form of a celestial woman, made extremely beautiful. Her body is white tinged with red, wearing heavenly silks and jeweled garments, with a crown, earrings, and white conch-shell bracelets, adorned with various necklaces and ornaments. She sits on a jeweled platform with her right foot hanging down. On either side of the platform, beside her knees, paint two children each. In her left arm, she cradles a child named Priyaṅkara, made extremely handsome. Her right hand, near her breast, holds a pomegranate [literally, 'auspicious fruit']. To her left and right, also paint attendant women and retinue, some holding white whisks and some holding adornments.

"The painter should be pure, having bathed and put on new clean clothes, and should maintain the eight precepts. When the painting is properly completed, in a private room that has been thoroughly cleaned, construct a square altar of four cubits. Take the dung of a cow of pure color, catch it in a vessel, mix it with incense, and apply it according to the proper method. Then apply white sandalwood paste. On the square altar, make a round altar and place the image in the center. Above, set up a celestial canopy and hang various colored banners. Scatter various seasonal flowers upon the altar. Also offer milk porridge, yogurt rice, sweet and crisp food and drink, and various fruits, along with arghya [scented water]. Burn fine aloeswood incense as offerings. The image should face west. The mantra-holder should sit west of the altar, facing east, before the image, and recite mantras at four times, three times, or at least once daily without interruption. If there is any break, the method will be difficult to accomplish. One must not recite Vajra-division mantras or miscellaneous mantras, lest accomplishment be hindered. At each session, recite one hundred and eight times as the standard.

"During the actual recitation, one should not engage in casual conversation, for it would break the mantra's continuity, waste the count, and squander one's effort. If you wish to accomplish this method, begin on the first, fifth, sixteenth, or twentieth day of the bright fortnight. Following the above ritual, recite before this image until the count reaches ten thousand, which completes the preliminary practice. You must be secretive; do not let anyone know. Do not recite this mantra in front of others.

"If one can practice with dedication and secrecy according to the teaching, Joyful Mother will certainly manifest in person and fulfill the practitioner's wishes. When performing this recitation, one must absolutely not make enemies with anyone, lest it harm that person and bring about non-benefit. Once accomplishment is obtained, whatever wealth and treasures are gained should be broadly given in dāna [generosity]; one should not be stingy.

"If you wish Joyful Mother to quickly manifest, the practitioner should constantly think of this Mother, never letting her leave the mind. At the place of recitation and wherever you dwell, everything must be kept clean. Do not use lamplight. Always burn aloeswood incense, dwell alone, and recite without interruption. Do not be afraid; Joyful Mother will certainly come and manifest. Once she has come, if you reveal this to others, you will certainly meet with misfortune. When she manifests, do not speak to her, but only recite mantras with utmost sincerity and make additional proper offerings. Later she will certainly come frequently and tell the practitioner: 'What would you have me do?' She may also leave necklaces, bracelets, jewels, and adornments. If she leaves them, you must use them immediately; do not store them. Still, do not speak to her. After a long time of growing familiarity, you may then speak, and ask her to be your mother, or your elder or younger sister. You should not generate desire toward her. Even if the holy one shows amorous intent, you must absolutely not accept; if you do, you will be born into her clan and will find it difficult to gain liberation.

"The mantra-holder, at each of his own meals, should first reduce one portion of clean food and consecrate it with the heart-mantra seven times. After eating, take it to a clean place and set it out. Then you will constantly receive her protection and she will never leave your side."

The heart-mantra is:

*Oṃ nunu mālikā hrīḥ te svāhā.*

### Accomplishment through Homa

"After the practitioner's preliminary practice is complete, choose an auspicious stellar day, or a time of solar or lunar eclipse, and make offerings according to the method. Take fine aloeswood incense, dip it in ghee, and perform homa one thousand and eighty times. Accomplishment will certainly be obtained, or she may manifest in person. One may also consecrate prayer beads or offering implements and pray for upper, middle, or lower accomplishments. If the ground where one dwells trembles, or if one sees a radiance, know that accomplishment has been gained. From then on, all that you do will go as you wish, and you may then employ the various siddhi methods that follow.

### Subsidiary Methods

"If you seek a hidden treasure: take a virgin girl, have her bathe in purity and wear fresh, clean clothes. Take fragrant flowers and have the girl hold them with both hands, covering her face, and stand beside the altar. The mantra-holder should recite the mantra and consecrate turmeric-scented water one hundred and eight times, then sprinkle it on the girl's body. In the mantra formula, add the petition. In a short time, the girl will speak and tell the location of the hidden treasure. Or simply recite mantras single-mindedly and request the holy one to manifest and ask her the location. Or simply petition and ask Joyful Mother to daily provide whatever is needed. If the petition is granted, whatever wealth and treasures are obtained should be used broadly for meritorious works and dāna to benefit sentient beings. They should not be hoarded, or you will lose your accomplishment.

"Another method: if you wish a person of rank to be pleased with you, take earth from beneath that person's gate, mix it with saliva to form a pellet, consecrate it one hundred and eight times, and place it in a latrine. That person will certainly be respectful and pleased with you.

"Another method: if you wish a woman to be devoted and loving, consecrate a fruit twenty-one times and give it to her to eat. She will immediately become devoted and loving.

"Another method: if there are doubtful matters, consecrate a girl as before and ask her. She will explain all the doubtful matters in detail.

"Another method: simply recite mantras and make petitions. You will hear a voice from the empty sky in response, or in dreams you will certainly be told. All doubtful matters will be known.

"Another method: if someone is possessed by ghosts and spirits, causing fear and unease, take benzoin, asafoetida, white mustard seeds, and ghee. Perform homa one thousand and eighty times, and all possessing spirits will be utterly destroyed.

"Another method: if there is an evil person who obstructs you, take rudra-earth, consecrate it twenty-one times or one hundred and eight times, and secretly bury it beneath the threshold of that person's house. The obstructing person will certainly fall ill. If you wish them to recover, retrieve the earth, and the illness will immediately be cured.

"Another method: if husband and wife are not in harmony, secretly consecrate clothing or food that they use twenty-one times, and give it to them without their knowledge. They will certainly become harmonious.

"Another method: if someone has been poisoned, consecrate white pigeon droppings, mix with water, and give it to them to drink. They will immediately recover.

"Another method: if someone is imprisoned in shackles or entangled in legal disputes, take peach wood gathered on the fifth day of the fifth month. Secretly write that enemy's name on it. Consecrate it one hundred and eight times. In the mantra formula, add that person's name, consecrate it with a prayer, and drive it into the ground. The legal dispute will be resolved without further trouble.

"Another method: if you wish to win a debate, take a piece of wild kudzu vine, consecrate it twenty-one times, and hold it in your hand while debating. The opponent will be rendered speechless.

"Another method: if a woman has difficulty finding a marriage partner, make a human figure from ash, consecrate it one hundred and eight times, and have the woman bow to this ash figure seven times each day. The marriage will certainly come together; it never fails.

"Another method: if you wish to summon a beloved person from afar, make a figure from ash, or from salt. Place a knife on the figure's heart, speak that person's name, and at three times daily form the summoning mudrā and recite the mantra one hundred and eight times to consecrate it. Within seven days at most, that person will arrive. If they do not come, that person will certainly be stricken with serious illness.

"Another method: if someone owes a debt and will not repay it, form that person's figure from salt. Form the subjugation mudrā and recite the dhāraṇī one hundred and eight times. In the mantra formula, speak that person's name and score the figure with a knife. That person will come of their own accord and prostrate, begging for mercy.

"Another method: if someone suffers from possession by ghosts, consecrate a girl as before and ask about it. Once you learn which spirit is causing the illness, use the proper method to dispatch the ghost, and the patient will invariably be cured.

"Another method: if you wish to determine the outcome before going to treat an illness, first light a lamp and place it inside the stove, consecrate it with the mantra twenty-one times, and seal the stove door. The next morning at dawn, check: if the oil is exhausted, go; if not exhausted, do not treat, for recovery will be difficult. This method is very exacting.

"Another method: at the time of a solar or lunar eclipse, before the image, fill a cooked-copper bowl with ghee and consecrate it until it overflows. Eating this consecrated ghee, one will obtain the power of unfailing memory, able to recite a thousand words daily.

"Another method: if there is someone who is not obedient, secretly recite the mantra and consecrate fruit or food, and contrive to give it to that person to eat. They will certainly become devoted and loving.

"Another method: if a house where someone has died causes people fear, recite this dhāraṇī with a wrathful mind twenty-one or one hundred and eight times, and the fear will be removed. It is best not to go to houses where death has occurred.

"Another method: if you face a legal proceeding, recite this mantra one hundred and eight times, or simply recite the mantra secretly. You will certainly prevail in your case.

"Another method: if you wish to have an audience with a person of rank or an official, recite the mantra one hundred and eight times. When you meet them, they will certainly be pleased.

"Another method: if a woman is having a difficult birth, consecrate cow ghee one hundred and eight times, or twenty-one times, and give it to the woman to eat and apply to the birth canal. The delivery will certainly be easy.

"If the mantra-holder single-mindedly recites this mantra exclusively without mixing in other practices, Joyful Mother will constantly follow and protect, never leaving their side, and will before long manifest in person. If one practices this Joyful Mother method and wishes for swift results, one should set up a separate quiet room and maintain the utmost secrecy. The rite must not be performed in a buddha-hall or monastery, lest accomplishment be hindered. Others must not see this image or know that the rite is being performed, or the efficacy will surely be lost.

"Another method: if there is an enemy who intends to harm you, take sesame seeds and ghee, and before the image perform homa one thousand and eighty times. In the mantra formula, speak that person's name. They will certainly become well-disposed.

"Another method: if you wish someone to think of you constantly, write that person's name and place it under your bed-leg. That person will certainly think of you constantly.

"Another method: if you wish to cause terror and unease in an enemy's household, take a piece of skull bone, consecrate it twenty-one or one hundred and eight times, and secretly place it inside the enemy's dwelling. Their household will certainly be terrified and uneasy. Whenever you take skull bone, first consecrate your own body with the mantra twenty-one times, then form the summoning mudrā, and then take it. Secretly consecrate and use it, and it will be unfailingly effective. If you wish to remove the terror, recite the mantra twenty-one times, retrieve the skull bone and perform a dismissal, sending it back to its original place. That household will then be free of fear. Once a bone has been used, it cannot be used a second time and will have no effect. Furthermore, any food, fruit, or other items that have been offered must absolutely not be eaten, or the mantra-holder's practice will become ineffective.

"Whenever offering food to Joyful Mother, it must always be freshly prepared; leftover or stale food must not be used. Each time you set out offerings, you must deliver them personally; do not have another person deliver them, or they will have no effect.

### The Skull-Servant Method

"Another method: if you wish to accomplish the method of commanding a servant, first complete the mantra recitation so that your practice is established. Then select a skull. If you know that this skull belonged to a capable and resolute person, accomplishment will be more easily achieved. First, at the place where you found it, consecrate your own body. Then consecrate that skull with the mantra one hundred and eight times, form the summoning mudrā to summon the spirit, and secretly wrap and carry it away. First wash it with clean water, then bathe it in scented water. Make a tongue of silver. Before the image of Joyful [Mother], consecrate it one thousand and eighty times. Beside the altar, cover the skull with a steamer lid. That very night, [the spirit] will announce its name, or manifest in person, and offer its services. From then on, whatever commands you give will be unfailingly obeyed, and if you ask about doubtful matters, it will have foreknowledge. This method requires the utmost secrecy; if leaked, not only will it fail but it will bring calamity upon you. Whenever you send a skull [spirit] to another person, you must first assess that person. If the enemy is someone who practices Vajra-division methods, or who maintains strict precepts and understands dharma-methods, you must absolutely not disturb them, or it will rebound and harm you. Otherwise, it will certainly be effective."

### The Mudrās

"Next, the explanation of the mudrās. First, form the summoning mudrā: with the fingers of the right hand, cross over and insert them into the back of the left hand, grasping the left palm. With the left hand facing the body, beckon three times. This completes the mudrā. Next, form the subjugation mudrā: with both hands interlaced inwardly, hook the two little fingers together, insert the two ring fingers each into the tiger's mouth [the space between thumb and forefinger] on the opposite side, join the two middle fingers upright, with the two index fingers each pressing on the back of the middle fingers, and the two thumbs each pressing on the middle joint of the middle fingers. This completes the mudrā. Next, form the mudrā for striking evil persons: with the right hand, press the thumb on the nail of the ring finger. With each recitation of the mantra, flick once toward the evil person. This completes the mudrā.

"Next, form the mudrā for dismissing the holy one: form the previous summoning mudrā, then flick the right hand outward. This mudrā is thus complete."

---

At that time, Joyful Mother, having spoken this dhāraṇī method in the great assembly, said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I have now received the Tathāgata's mindfulness and protection. I and my retinue accept the Buddha's instruction. In future ages, I shall protect all sentient beings and cause them to be at peace and happy, free from all terrors. If anyone can practice this dhāraṇī in purity according to this method, all that they seek will be fulfilled."

## The Dhāraṇī and Siddhi Methods of the Beloved Son Priyaṅkara

Joyful Mother again said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I now wish to speak the dhāraṇī method of my beloved son Priyaṅkara, for the benefit and protection of all those who seek."

The mantra is:

[Dhāraṇī]

"The mudrā is formed by joining both hands in añjali [palms together], with the two thumbs bent inward into the palms. This completes it. This single mudrā is used for all purposes: obtaining protection, summoning, and dismissal. By consecrating the five places on one's body with the mudrā (crown, right shoulder, left shoulder, heart, and throat), protection of the body is accomplished. Beckoning with the two thumbs is the summoning. Opening outward is the dismissal.

"If anyone receives and upholds this dhāraṇī method, I and my beloved son Priyaṅkara, together with all attendants, will protect that person and not allow any evil ghosts or spirits to trouble them.

"If one holds this dhāraṇī, one must be pure and abstain from pungent foods. Recite the mantra to a count of three hundred thousand, and also make offerings of food to the beloved son Priyaṅkara. This completes the preliminary practice.

"The food-offering method: first, in a clean open-air place, apply cow dung to make a circular altar. Scatter seasonal flowers upon the altar, or simply offer on a clean stone. Each time you are about to eat, first take a small portion of each food item and place them together in a clean vessel. Consecrate with the above mantra seven times. Offer it upon the altar and call out the name of Priyaṅkara, saying: 'Accept my food and grant me your protection.' Doing this without fail for six months, the beloved son will constantly follow and protect you.

### Homa Procedures for Priyaṅkara

"Another method: whether begging for food or subsisting on milk, recite the mantra to a count of three hundred thousand. Then take beef formed into pellets. Consecrate one pellet and burn one, three times daily, one thousand and eighty times per session. After forty-nine days, Priyaṅkara will certainly manifest in person and fulfill the practitioner's wishes.

"Another method: the practitioner eats the three white foods [milk, yogurt, rice]. Form pellets of beef mixed with benzoin for homa. Beginning on the first day of the bright fortnight, continue for a full month, three times daily, one thousand and eighty times per session. On the last day, make abundant offerings. Fast for one day and one night, abstaining from casual speech. Perform homa according to the teaching without interruption, reciting with utmost sincerity. The beloved son Priyaṅkara will certainly manifest and become a constant companion to the mantra-holder, providing all that is needed.

"Another method: take bright, fine benzoin, dip it in ghee, and perform homa three times daily at the rate described above. With this unbroken, uninterrupted homa, [Priyaṅkara] will constantly deliver gold coins for the practitioner's use.

"Another method: if you seek a hidden treasure, perform the preliminary practice as described above. Once accomplished, then use storax mixed with ghee for homa, three times daily, one thousand and eighty times per session. If this continues without interruption, the hidden treasure will manifest, and you may take and use it without any obstruction. If one constantly recites this dhāraṇī and makes food-offerings, one's wealth will flourish. All wishes will be fulfilled, and Priyaṅkara will guard and protect you.

"Another method: if you wish to subdue an enemy, write that enemy's name and place it beneath the mantra-holder's left foot. With a vigorous, wrathful voice and intention, recite the mantra, adding the enemy's name to the mantra formula. After reciting a full one hundred thousand times, all adversaries will submit.

"Another method: if someone is imprisoned, constantly recite this dhāraṇī and you will gain release.

"Another method: if you wish for peace and security, take āmra tree leaves mixed with milk, consecrate and perform homa one hundred thousand times, and you will obtain peace and security.

### Carving the Image of Priyaṅkara

"I now further explain the method for carving an image of Priyaṅkara. Take fine white sandalwood without flaws. The image should be six finger-widths or one span in height. Have a skilled craftsman carve it in the form of a boy, with five reddish-purple topknots on his head, with perfect features and adorned with various necklaces and ornaments, sitting cross-legged upon a lotus leaf. The right hand holds a pomegranate in a gesture of giving. The left hand is raised, palm outward, with the five fingers spread and hanging down: this is called the 'wish-fulfilling hand.'

"When this image is completed, in a private, clean room, use benzoin-scented water mixed with clay to make an altar of one or three cubits square. Apply cow dung according to the proper method, then apply white sandalwood paste to make a circular seat. Place the image in the center. Scatter various seasonal flowers upon the altar. Offer milk porridge, yogurt rice, sweet and crisp fruits, and modaka [sweet dumplings] according to the proper method. Burn aloeswood incense. Before this image, recite the dhāraṇī to a count of one hundred thousand. The beloved son Priyaṅkara will certainly come and manifest, asking the practitioner: 'Why have you summoned me? What do you seek?' The practitioner may then make whatever request he wishes, and all will be fulfilled. From then on, every rite performed will be unfailingly effective.

### Dream Divination

"Another method: if you wish to know future events, good or bad, apply cow dung to make an altar as before, make offerings as before, and burn benzoin incense. Consecrate sesame seeds one at a time and burn them, one thousand and eighty in total. Then, at that very place, lie down to sleep. Do not engage in casual speech. With single-minded concentration, think of the holy deva Priyaṅkara and request a dream-vision. In a short time, you will dream of Priyaṅkara, and whatever doubtful matters you ask will be explained."

The dream-requesting mantra is:

[Dhāraṇī]

---

At that time, Joyful Mother, having spoken her own heart-dhāraṇī and the siddhi methods of her beloved son Priyaṅkara, prostrated with her five limbs touching the ground, bowed at the Buddha's feet, and said: "World-Honored One! I now, through this dhāraṇī and these siddhi methods, seek to benefit sentient beings. I beg the Tathāgata and all the holy assembly to bear witness to me."

The Buddha said: "Excellent, excellent! Joyful Mother! I now once again entrust [my disciples] to you. You and your retinue, in [the monasteries of] my Dharma, wherever my renunciant disciples dwell, and among all the people: guard and protect them diligently. Do not allow evil ghosts and spirits to create obstacles. Cause them to obtain happiness. Until my Dharma has not yet been extinguished, you should act thus throughout Jambudvīpa."

At that time, Joyful Mother and her five hundred sons, together with all her retinue of yakṣas, having heard the Buddha's instruction, all rejoiced greatly, bowed in homage, and departed.
