# 佛說北斗七星延命經
## Sūtra on the Seven Stars of the Northern Dipper for Extending Life, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1307

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown.

---

Those born in the year of the Rat are born under this star. Their sustaining grain is millet. When afflicted, they should make offerings to this sūtra and wear the talisman of their natal star. Great auspiciousness.

Those born in the years of the Ox and the Boar are both born under this star. Their sustaining grain is foxtail millet. When afflicted, they should make offerings to this sūtra and wear the talisman of their natal star. Great auspiciousness.

Those born in the years of the Tiger and the Dog are both born under this star. Their sustaining grain is non-glutinous rice. When afflicted, they should make offerings to this sūtra and wear the talisman of their natal star. Great auspiciousness.

Those born in the years of the Rabbit and the Rooster are both born under this star. Their sustaining grain is red beans. When afflicted, they should make offerings to this sūtra and wear the talisman of their natal star. Great auspiciousness.

Those born in the years of the Dragon and the Monkey are both born under this star. Their sustaining grain is hemp seeds. When afflicted, they should make offerings to this sūtra and wear the talisman of their natal star. Great auspiciousness.

Those born in the years of the Snake and the Sheep are both born under this star. Their sustaining grain is soybeans. When afflicted, they should make offerings to this sūtra and wear the talisman of their natal star. Great auspiciousness.

Those born in the year of the Horse are born under this star. Their sustaining grain is red beans. When afflicted, they should make offerings to this sūtra and wear the talisman of their natal star. Great auspiciousness.

Homage to the Greedy Wolf Star: this is the Tathāgata Who Conveys Intentions and Realizes Penetration, in the Most Victorious World of the East.

Homage to the Great Gate Star: this is the Tathāgata of Radiant Sound and Sovereign Freedom, in the Wondrous Jewel World of the East.

Homage to the Emolument Preserver Star: this is the Tathāgata of Golden-Colored Accomplishment, in the Perfected World of the East.

Homage to the Literary Curve Star: this is the Tathāgata of Supreme Auspiciousness, in the Sorrowless World of the East.

Homage to the Honest and Correct Star: this is the Tathāgata of Vast Penetrating Wisdom and Eloquence, in the Pure Abode World of the East.

Homage to the Military Curve Star: this is the Tathāgata Who Plays in the Dharma-Sea, in the Dharma-Mind World of the East.

Homage to the Army Breaker Star: this is the Medicine Master Tathāgata of Lapis Lazuli Light, in the Lapis Lazuli World of the East.

When one encounters a year of calamity and misfortune, bow to this sūtra seven times.

At that time, the Buddha addressed Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva: "This sūtra I have taught possesses great supernatural might and great power. It can save all sentient beings from grave sins and can eliminate all karmic obstructions. If any bhikṣu, bhikṣuṇī-saṃgha bhikṣu, official, householder, good man, good woman, whether noble or humble, whether great or small: all living beings are governed by the seven stars of the Northern Dipper.

"If they hear this sūtra, receive, uphold, and make offerings to it, read and recite it, and urge their friends, relatives, and kin to receive and uphold it, in this present life they will obtain merit, and in future lives they will be born in the heavens.

"If good men and good women have ancestors who have passed away and fallen into the hells, suffering various torments, and they hear this sūtra, have faith, and make offerings to it with reverence, those ancestors will be liberated from the hells and born in the world of utmost bliss.

"If good men or good women are afflicted by ghosts and demons, harassed by evil māras, plagued by nightmares, strange occurrences, and terrified souls, and they hear this sūtra and receive, uphold, and make offerings to it, their souls will become peaceful and they will never again know fear.

"If good men or good women who hold official posts, serve in government, or are dispatched on military campaigns encounter this sūtra and make offerings to it with faith and reverence, they will receive promotions in office. Great auspiciousness.

"If good men or good women who are afflicted by illness wish to recover, they should burn incense and make offerings to this sūtra in a clean room. Their illness will be healed.

"If good men or good women wish to advance in their livelihood, in their travels and business ventures, in seeking wealth to their satisfaction, or in commerce and trade, and they encounter this sūtra and make offerings to it with faith and reverence, their pursuit of wealth will be satisfied. Great auspiciousness in going out and coming in.

"If good men or good women find their silkworms failing and their livestock unwell, they should burn incense and make offerings to this sūtra in a clean room. Their fields and silkworms will prosper according to their wishes, their livestock will thrive and multiply, and there will never be loss or calamity.

"If a woman has difficulty in pregnancy or labor and encounters this sūtra, having faith and making offerings to it with reverence, both mother and child will be safely delivered, the crisis resolved. The children she bears will all be beautiful and blessed with long life.

"If good men and good women wish to know: the seven stars of the Northern Dipper govern the lives of all people. Throughout one's entire life, whatever calamities, official disputes, quarrels, ominous sounds from the hearth, and all manner of strange phenomena may arise, if one encounters this sūtra and makes offerings to it with faith and reverence, not one will cause harm."

At that time, Mañjuśrī said: "Good men and good women should receive this with reverence and faith." All made reverence and departed.
