# 藥師琉璃光如來消災除難念誦儀軌
## Ritual Manual for the Recitation of Bhaiṣajyaguru Vaiḍūrya-prabhā Tathāgata for Eliminating Disasters and Removing Calamities

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0922

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, composed by Yixing (一行)

---

> First purify body, speech, and mind; take refuge in the Buddha, Dharma, and Saṃgha.
> Pay reverence to the Universally Illuminating Honored One and all the holy assembly of the ten directions.
> I now briefly open and expound the secret method for eliminating disasters.
> This method was taught by the World-Honored One: it is the most supreme, the foremost.
> It swiftly leads one out of birth-and-death and brings the rapid realization of great enlightenment.
> To accord with the realm of sentient beings, it also teaches the removal of calamity and disaster,
> And the methods of increase, attraction, and subjugation. For women in difficult pregnancy,
> For those in perilous childbirth unable to deliver, and for those afflicted with illness,
> When spirits and ghosts cause misfortune: establish a maṇḍala,
> Sculpt or paint an image of the principal deity, and light forty-nine lamps.
> Make offerings to the Lapis Lazuli Honored One, keeping the lamps burning day and night.
> Offer fragrance, flowers, fruits, and food, and make five-colored banners and canopies.
> Release water and land creatures; when the days reach forty-nine,
> Empower a five-colored thread and tie it around the neck of the sick person.
> If one wishes to practice such a method, the wise should first approach a teacher,
> Receive the samaya precepts, generate the great mind of enlightenment,
> Obtain the vidyādhara abhiṣeka, and be authorized by an ācārya.
> Only then should one begin to practice. In a quiet mountain forest,
> Beside a river, pool, or ocean shore, or in one's own dwelling place,
> Smear and clean a maṇḍala, round or square as one prefers.
> Place two vessels of argha water, arrange the seven offerings below.
> Burn incense, present flowers, lamps, food, drink, unguent, and so on,
> Arranged on the four sides of the altar. Confess, rejoice,
> Exhort and make vows, and transfer all merit.
> Correspond and accomplish the siddhi. Practice in accord with the teaching.
> The wheel of mudrā-contract methods:

> The Buddha Division heart-secret seal: samādhi and wisdom in inner binding,
> Both wisdom-fingers erect and upright. Contemplate the Buddhas filling empty space.
> Recite the mantra seven times, then release the seal above the crown.

The mantra:

*Oṃ jina-jika svāhā*

> By this empowerment, all the Buddhas gather like clouds.
> Their radiance illuminates the practitioner, removing obstructions and swiftly bringing accomplishment.
> Next, form the Padma Division heart-seal as before,
> Bending the samādhi-wheel into the palm, erecting the wisdom-wheel.
> Contemplate Avalokiteśvara with his lotus-division retinue
> At the right of all the Tathāgatas. Recite the mantra seven times,
> Then release the seal at the crown to the right.

The mantra:

*Oṃ ārulika svāhā*

> By this empowerment, the bodhisattva assembly gathers like clouds.
> Becoming the practitioner's fellow worker, all wishes are fulfilled.
> Next, form the Vajra Division heart-seal, also as before,
> Bending the wisdom-wheel into the palm, erecting the samādhi-wheel.
> Contemplate Vajrapāṇi and all vajra-holders
> Dwelling at the right of the Tathāgata. Recite the mantra seven times,
> Then release the seal at the crown to the left.

The mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-dhṛlika svāhā*

> By this empowerment, the vajra assembly gathers like clouds.
> By the Buddha's original compassionate vow, they guard the practitioner.
> The three karmas become like vajra: firm and indestructible.
> Next, form the armor seal. Samādhi and wisdom each make a fist;
> Hold the wheel-fingers horizontally in the palm; rest the samādhi-fist at the heart.
> Seal the five places with the wisdom-fist: forehead, both shoulders, heart, and throat.
> Empower and release above the crown.

The mantra:

*Oṃ bhūḥ-jvalati-jaḥ hūṃ*

> By this seal and mantra, empowered with the armor,
> All māras cannot press near; accomplishment is swiftly obtained.
> Among all the Buddha's uṣṇīṣas, this seal has great awesome virtue.
> As soon as the seal is formed to protect the body, devas and humans all pay reverence.
> In all places of peril and danger, and in all times of calamity,
> One will obtain peace and happiness in every case. Next, with a sincere and devoted mind,
> Transform this defiled place into a pure and wondrous buddha-land.

The mantra:

*Oṃ bhūḥ khaṃ*

> By this empowerment, the principal deity's pure land is accomplished.
> A great jeweled palace appears, adorned with every kind of treasure.
> Within the jeweled palace, visualize Bhaiṣajyaguru Tathāgata
> Surrounded by the bodhisattva assembly, and contemplate the maṇḍala.
> Next, form the invitation seal, using the Buddha Division heart as before.
> With both wheel-fingers, beckon toward the body. Recite the mantra three times.

The mantra:

*Oṃ jina-jika ehy ehi bhagavan uṣṇīṣāya svāhā*

> By this empowerment, all obstructing beings
> Will flee far away, paying respect to the practitioner.
> Next, present the argha water with seasonal flowers floating on top,
> Precious medicines, fragrance, and seeds placed within the water.
> Raise it above the crown to present. Recite the mantra seven times.
> Visualize bathing the feet of the holy assembly and state one's wishes.

The mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ oṃ gagana-sama-sama svāhā*

> By presenting the fragrant water, one is freed from defilement and obtains purity.
> One shall attain the stage of abhiṣeka and realize the Tathāgata's dharma-body.
> Next, form the seat-offering seal: the five fingertips, a blossoming flower.
> Visualize that from the seal flow forth lotus lion-thrones.

The mantra:

*Oṃ kamala-siddhaṃ svāhā*

> By this mantra and seal, the principal deity and retinue
> Each receive a jeweled lotus vajra lion-throne.
> Next, form the universal offering great seal and its procedures.
> The fingertips initially cross, and from the seal flow forth
> All manner of offerings: incense, flowers, lamps,
> Unguent, food, drink, and cloud-oceans of music,
> Platforms, parasols, jeweled pavilions, jeweled seats and adornments,
> Supreme and wondrous banners, pennants, and canopies, worthy vases of fragrant water.
> All flow from the seal and pervade the empty space of the Dharma-realm,
> Making offerings to all the Tathāgatas, bodhisattvas, and the holy assembly.

The mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ sarvathā-kham-udgate spharahimaṃ gaganakam svāhā*

> Then, with the power of the three equalities and the true and wondrous gāthā,
> Sincerely recite three times; all will be accomplished.
> "By the power of my merit, the power of the Tathāgata's empowerment,
> And the power of the Dharma-realm: I make universal offerings and abide therein."
> Then form the Buddha's great compassion protective-seal and its procedures:
> The five fingertips in firm binding, the supreme wheel-fingers erect and joined together.
> Seal the heart, forehead, throat, and crown: become equal to the self-nature of all the Buddhas.

The mantra:

*Oṃ buddha-maitrī vajra-rakṣa haṃ*

> By this empowerment, all māra-obstructions and calamities,
> And those who would cause harm with evil intent, will immediately give rise to the mind of compassion.
> Next, form the Buddha-Eye Seal: with the *saṃpuṭa* mudrā,
> Two canopy-fingers on the light-finger's back, like the shape of a smiling eye.
> Bend both wheel-fingers, each holding the middle joint of the light-fingers.
> Seal the five places of one's own body. The Buddha-Mother will constantly empower and protect.
> Recite the Buddha-Eye root mantra.

The mantra:

*Namo bhagavate uṣṇīṣāya oṃ ruru sphuru-jvala tiṣṭha siddha-locanī sarva-artha-sādhane svāhā*

> By this seal and mantra, with the power of empowerment,
> One can fulfill all wishes and remove all misfortune,
> Produce all merit and extinguish all karmic obstructions,
> And enable all sentient beings who see or hear to obtain peace and happiness.
> Next, praise and extol the merit of the principal deity.
> "Homage to the realm of the full moon, the honored pure and wondrous lapis lazuli!
> With Dharma-medicine you save devas and humans; through twelve causal vows,
> Your compassion and great vows are vast. May you liberate all sentient beings.
> I now offer praises and, with sincere mind, bow my head to the ground."
> The root secret seal: both wings internally cross,
> Both wrists slightly apart, spread two or three inches.
> The meditation and wisdom fingers move back and forth.

The great mantra:

*Namo bhagavate bhaiṣajya-guru-vaiḍūrya-prabhā-rājāya tathāgatāya arhate samyak-saṃbuddhāya tadyathā oṃ bhaiṣajye bhaiṣajye bhaiṣajya-samudgate svāhā*

> By the power of this principal deity, recite the mantra the prescribed number of times,
> Seven times up to one hundred and eight. Release the seal above the crown.
> State the prayers of one's heart and, before the principal deity,
> Pray that he will graciously watch over and annihilate disaster and untimely death.
> Next, take the prayer beads and coil them within the double palms.
> Empower them with seven recitations, then place the beads upon the crown.

The mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-guhya-japa-samaye hūṃ*

> By this mantra and seal, empowering the prayer beads,
> Starting from the mother-bead, with each recitation, turn one bead.
> When the last syllable of the mantra is complete, upon reaching the mother-bead, turn back.
> One must not cross over the mother-bead; to pass over it is a violation of the Dharma.
> The extensive teaching is eighteen thousand; the abbreviated is one hundred and eight.
> When the set count is completed, again hold up the beads and empower them.
> State the matter of one's prayer and, with sincere mind, universally dedicate the merit.

Regarding these Dharma-seals and mantras: if good men or good women have many obstructions and calamities, or any kind of illness; if a woman has a difficult pregnancy or perilous childbirth; if one wishes to avert disaster and seek blessings; or if one is afflicted by spirits and ghosts with illnesses difficult to cure: take a five-colored thread and twist it into a cord. Empower it with the mantra and tie it around the neck of the sick person, or upon their hands, feet, waist, or abdomen. Furthermore, invite seven monastics and establish a practice-hall. Create an image of the principal deity, copy the Bhaiṣajyaguru Sūtra, perform the six-session practice, and light seven tiers of lamps. Make five-colored banners forty-nine feet in length. Recite the sūtra forty-nine times daily. Release forty-nine water and land creatures. With seasonal flowers and fruits, make offerings diligently. Empower the five-colored thread with the mantra and make vows. Then press the seal upon the thread and recite the mantra forty-nine more times, tying forty-nine knots. Then recite the following empowerment mantra:

*Namo ratna-trayāya namaḥ Kumbhīra Vajra Mihira Aṇḍīra Maṇila Saṃdīra Indra Pajra Mahoraga Kinnara Catura Vikarāla namaḥ piśāca-guru-bhiṣyā-liyā-prā-pra-jāya tadyathā oṃ viśāśi viśāśi viśāca-samagate svāhā*

> Again praise and bow to the principal deity. Form the principal deity's secret seal,
> Recite the mantra seven times, and again present argha water.
> Make offerings as before. Complete the rite with the Buddha's uṣṇīṣa seal,
> Recite the vidyā three times, and turn to the left to release the boundary.
> Send off the holy assembly as with the previous invitation seal,
> But with both wheel-fingers flicking outward. Visualize them in the sky,
> Returning to the pure land and residing in the jeweled palace.

*Oṃ jina-jika gaccha gaccha bhagavan uṣṇīṣāya svāhā*

> Having sent off the principal deity, form the Buddha's compassion-protection
> Samaya secret-contract and empower the four places with the seal.
> Again form the Buddha-Eye Seal, sealing the five places with the mantra.
> Form the three-division heart-seals, each with its own vidyā three times.
> Again form the armor body-protection, sealing the five places with the mantra.
> Bow to the Buddha and make great vows. Walk about at will.
