# 金輪王佛頂要略念誦法
## Abbreviated Recitation Method for the Golden Wheel King Buddha-Uṣṇīṣa

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0948

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空)

---

The various offering rites are broadly taught in other scriptures. Now, for those who practice morning and evening, or who constantly maintain practice at four or three times daily without interruption, the redundancies are removed and only the essentials are presented. When the practitioner wishes to perform the recitation of the various buddha-uṣṇīṣas, first form the three-division heart seals and recite the true words to empower the three karmas. The Buddha-Division Heart Seal: the two hands, right pressing over left, fingers interlaced inward, with the two thumbs standing upright side by side. The true word:

*Oṃ jina-jik ca*

Next, form the Lotus-Division Heart Seal. Use the previous seal, but bend the left thumb into the palm. The true word:

*Oṃ arolika*

Next, form the Vajra-Division Heart Seal. Use the previous Buddha-Division Heart Seal, but bend the right thumb into the palm. The true word:

*Oṃ vajra-dhṛk ca*

Next, form the Tathāgata-Fist Seal. The left hand covers the topknot, raised four finger-widths high, making a fist with the left thumb standing upright. The right hand forms a vajra-fist and grips the nail of the left thumb. The true word:

*Oṃ bhūṃ jvala hūṃ*

While reciting the true word, empower the five places of one's own body with the seal. The bodily vessel becomes purified and corresponds with the Dharma. Then empower the ground of the practice-hall with the seal, reciting the true word seven times. That place is transformed into a Vajradhātu palace, naturally adorned with various jewels, like a Buddha's pure land.

Next, form the Vajra Armor Protection Seal. Use the previous Buddha-Division Heart Seal, with the two thumbs standing upright, tips touching, upper joints bent like a sword shape. The two little fingers are each placed against the back of the two thumbs, without touching them. Empower the forehead, right shoulder, left shoulder, heart, and throat, five places, reciting once at each. The true word:

*Oṃ cakravarti praśamita ra-nadra ra-nadra bhasmāṃ caluṣṇīṣa rakṣa rakṣa māṃ hūṃ phaṭ svāhā*

Next, form the Seal for Inviting the Holy Assembly. The two hands face upward with fingers interlaced. The two little-finger tips touch each other like a carriage-shaft. While reciting the true word, use the two thumbs to each hook the two middle fingers toward the body, three or seven times, visualizing the holy assembly riding this carriage and descending to the practice-hall. The Invitation True Word:

*Namaḥ satreyā-dhivikānāṃ sarva-tathāgatānāṃ oṃ vajra-gīñjā-kṛṣāya svāhā*

Next, form the Duty-Fulfilling Buddha-Uṣṇīṣa Seal for dispelling obstacles and establishing the boundary. Use the previous Buddha-Division Heart Seal, with the two thumbs standing upright, tips touching, upper joints bent like hooks. While reciting the true word, place the seal above the crown and turn it leftward three times, dispelling all inauspiciousness and all who create obstacles. Then turn it rightward three times, thereby establishing the boundary for the duty-fulfilling rite. The true word:

*Namo samanta-buddhānāṃ oṃ ṭaru oṃ bandha svāhā*

Next, form the Great Samaya Seal. Use the previous Armor Seal, but slightly open the two little fingers into a vajra shape, with the two thumbs each placed at the first joint below the two little fingers. The true word:

*Oṃ śaṅkale mahā-samayaṃ svāhā*

While reciting the true word, turn the seal rightward three times as before, establishing the great boundary as far as the practitioner wishes.

Next, form the seal of the Division-Mother, Buddha-Eye. The two hands form a joined-palm mudrā, with the two little fingers each pinching the back of the two middle fingers to form an eye shape. Bend the two thumbs into the palms. The true word:

*Namo samanta-buddhānāṃ oṃ ruru sphura jvālā-tiṣṭha siddha-locanī sarva-artha-sādhanī svāhā*

Next, form the seal of the principal deity, the Cakravartin King. Use the previous Duty-Fulfilling Buddha-Uṣṇīṣa Seal, with the two thumbs standing side by side. Flatten the two little fingers, bending them at two joints, their tips touching upon the nails of the two thumbs. Form the seal at the heart and recite the true word seven times or one hundred and eight times. Also empower the five places of the body once each. By the power of this empowerment, one's body merges as one with the principal deity. The One-Syllable Cakravartin-King Dhāraṇī:

*Namo samanta-buddhānāṃ bhruṃ oṃ*

Next, form the Seal for Offering Argha Fragrant Water. Use the previous Great Samaya Seal, with the two little fingers pressed against the back of the two middle fingers. The two thumbs are placed at the first joint on the side below the two little fingers, forming the shape of a conch-shell cup. Visualize the seal as filled to the brim with fragrant water. Raise the seal to the level of the forehead and offer the fragrant water to bathe the principal deity and all the holy assembly. The true word:

*Namo samanta-buddhānāṃ oṃ argha raha argha-bhrīya-pratīcena makaṃ svāhā*

Next, form the Universal Offering Seal. The two hands form a hollow joined-palm mudrā, with all five fingertips of the left and right hands each crossing at the upper joints. Visualize that from the seal flows an inexhaustible stream of incense, flowers, food and drink, palaces, garments, and offerings for all the holy assembly. The true word:

*Namaḥ sarva-buddha-bodhi-sattvānāṃ oṃ sarva-tra saṃkusu-mitā-bhikñā-raśi-nāṃ namo stute svāhā*

Next, form the Universal Buddha-Uṣṇīṣa Seal. Use the previous Universal Offering Seal, but interlace the fingers somewhat deeper to the middle joints, as though a flower rests in the palms. If the practitioner, due to urgent circumstances, cannot form the seals of all ten buddha-uṣṇīṣas individually, simply forming this seal and reciting the universal true word of all the buddha-uṣṇīṣas will also incur no fault for the practitioner. The true word:

*Namo samanta-buddhānāṃ ma-pratihata-śāsanānāṃ oṃ cakravarti oṃ hūṃ*

Next, recite the praise:

*Maṇi-ṣṇī sarva-jagad-dhitai-ṣṇī yaśas-vibhūra-bhuvanaika-bandhai samastuṃ-vidyādhi-pacakra-māriṇāṃ namo stute tra-tri-cakravartinī*

For each recitation session, first use the five-limb or five-phase or eight-phase visualization of wisdom to become the principal deity in yoga. Alternatively, visualize arranging the one syllable at three places on one's own body. By the awesome power of the syllable, one becomes the Uṣṇīṣa-cakravartin. Then visualize: within the maṇḍala there is a great ocean of fragrant water. At the center of the ocean is Mount Sumeru, surrounded by seven rings of golden mountains. Upon the summit of Mount Sumeru is an eight-petaled white lotus. Upon each petal, arranged clockwise, are placed the seven treasures of the cakravartin: the golden wheel, elephant, horse, jewel, queen, general, and treasurer. On the eighth petal in front, visualize the placement of Buddha-Eye. The ten buddha-uṣṇīṣa attendants surround on all sides. Having first established this visualization, then take up the rosary for corresponding recitation: one hundred, one thousand, or up to ten thousand times. When the regular session's quota is completed, hold the rosary above the crown and recite to the Division-Mother, asking the Honored One to guard all merit and works. Then again form the root seal and empower the five places of the body. Again form the Universal Offering Seal and make offerings as before. Then, with joyful song, praise the principal deity: "With immeasurable compassion and vows, protect us and bring us to liberation, corresponding with the siddhis." Broadly make vast vows to benefit and bring happiness to sentient beings and to vow to attain right awakening. Next, hold up the argha and offer it as before. Then form the Duty-Fulfilling Buddha-Uṣṇīṣa Seal and turn it leftward to release the boundary. Then form the previous Invitation Seal, but push the two middle fingers outward, reciting the previous true word with the phrase "kṛṣāya" replaced by "visarjaya," thereby performing the sending-off. Then form the Armor Seal, recite the previous true word, and empower the five places of the body. Then form the Three-Division Heart Seals and sincerely bow, making vows. One may then leave the practice-hall, or circumambulate, or read and chant the Mahāyāna sūtras, performing various wholesome deeds to assist accomplishment.
