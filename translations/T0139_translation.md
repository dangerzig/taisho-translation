# 佛說四泥犁經
## Sūtra on the Four Great Hells Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0139

Translated from the Chinese. Eastern Jin dynasty, translated by Zhu Tanwulan (竺曇無蘭)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Bhagavān was in the city of Śrāvastī, in the Jetavana, the garden of Anāthapiṇḍada. At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣus:

"There are four great hells. What are the four? Bhikṣus! The great hell of Tiṣya, the great hell of the bhikṣu Kokālika, the great hell of Devadatta, and the great hell of Maskarī.

"Bhikṣus! In the great hell of Tiṣya, flames burst forth from his body twenty cubits in length. Bhikṣus! In the great hell of Kokālika, flames burst forth from his body thirty cubits in length. Bhikṣus! In the great hell of Devadatta, flames burst forth from his body forty cubits in length. In the great hell of Maskarī, flames burst forth from his body sixty cubits in length.

"If some person wishing for peace and benefit were to pour the water of thirty great oceans upon Tiṣya's body, the ocean's water would be exhausted but the fire would not be extinguished. It is like molten copper. If someone were to place twenty drops of water into the molten copper, the drops would instantly evaporate. It is the same with the bhikṣu Tiṣya: the flames are not extinguished. If again some person wishing for peace and benefit were to pour the water of twenty great oceans upon his body, the water would quickly be exhausted. Why? Because the bhikṣu Tiṣya, that foolish person, obstructed the Saṃgha of bhikṣus and caused them to go without food for one day. By this cause and condition, the bhikṣu Tiṣya entered the great hell.

"Bhikṣus! Regarding the bhikṣu Kokālika, if a person wishing for his peace and benefit were to pour the water of thirty great oceans upon his body, the ocean's water would quickly be exhausted. It is like molten copper heated by two suns: if someone were to place thirty drops of water into the molten copper, it would be consumed completely without remainder. It is the same with this. The bhikṣu Kokālika, that foolish person: if some person wishing for his peace and benefit were to pour the water of thirty great oceans upon his body, the great ocean's water would quickly be exhausted. Why? Because the bhikṣu Kokālika, that foolish person, slandered the bhikṣus Śāriputra and Maudgalyāyana. When his body was destroyed and his life ended, he was born in the three evil destinies, falling into the Padma hell. By this cause and condition, the bhikṣu Kokālika entered the great hell.

"In the great hell of Devadatta, if again some person wishing for his peace and benefit were to pour the water of forty great oceans upon his body, the great ocean's water would quickly be exhausted but the fire would not be extinguished. It is like molten copper heated by three suns: if someone were to place forty drops of water into the molten copper, it would be instantly consumed completely without remainder. It is the same with this. Devadatta, that foolish person: if some person wishing for his peace and benefit were to pour the water of forty great oceans upon his body, the great ocean's water would quickly be exhausted but the fire would not be extinguished. Why? Because Devadatta, that foolish person, attempted to harm the Tathāgata, killed an arhat bhikṣuṇī, and disrupted the Saṃgha of bhikṣus. When his body was destroyed and his life ended, he fell into the three evil destinies and was born in the Avīci hell. By this cause and condition, the bhikṣu Devadatta entered the great hell, with flames bursting forth from his body forty cubits in length.

"Bhikṣus! In the great hell of Maskarī, if a person wishing for his peace and benefit were to pour the water of sixty great oceans upon his body, the ocean's water would quickly be exhausted but the fire would not be extinguished. It is like molten copper heated by four suns: if someone were to place sixty drops of water into the molten copper, it would be instantly consumed completely without remainder. It is the same with Maskarī. If a person wishing for his peace and benefit were to pour the water of sixty great oceans upon his body, the great ocean's water would quickly be exhausted but the fire would not be extinguished. Why? Because Maskarī, that foolish person, taught and led a hundred koṭi of people to practice wrong views. By this cause and condition, Maskarī's body emits flames sixty cubits in length.

"Thus, bhikṣus, are the four great hells."

At that time, the bhikṣus, having heard what the Buddha taught, joyfully practiced in accordance.
