Continuing from the verse about the eighty thousand people:

> Within the city of Bandhumatī, a multitude of eighty thousand:
> Hearing of Khaṇḍa and the others going forth and realizing the noble path,
> All gave rise to pure minds and came to the presence of the Buddha.
> Having heard the Dharma with rejoicing hearts, they swiftly gave rise to courageous minds;
> With joined palms they addressed the World-Honored One: "Please allow us to go forth
> And uphold the precepts and rules of discipline." Thereupon they were received:
> Their heads were shaved and the precepts were conferred; the spiritual powers were further manifested.
> All bonds and fetters were severed, like extinguishing the Śirīṣa grove,
> Its blazing flames arising nevermore. They accomplished the great liberation;
> Thus the basis of all suffering shall be extinguished and exist no more.

At that time, having spoken this verse, the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣus: "When the Buddha Vipaśyin had guided that assembly, he departed from the Pleasurable Deer Park and went to the city of Bandhumatī; the eighty thousand bhikṣus also went to the city of Bandhumatī. They came before the World-Honored One, prostrated at his feet, and sat to one side. The Buddha then fully explained the causes and conditions of attaining the Way, so as to strengthen their resolve."

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> Difficult to do, supremely difficult to do; caught in saṃsāra, they exhausted saṃsāra.
> These eighty thousand people forever severed all bonds and fetters,
> As did Tiṣya and the sage-prince Khaṇḍa:
> All diligently sought to go forth, and together attained the fruit of liberation.

At that time, having spoken this verse, the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣus: "Then the Buddha Vipaśyin formed this thought: 'This great assembly of bhikṣus dwells in the city of Bandhumatī; it would be fitting to reduce their number. Let sixty-two thousand bhikṣus go to various directions, wandering among villages to practice cultivation as they please. After six years they shall return to the city of Bandhumatī to receive and uphold the Prātimokṣa.' As he formed this thought, a deva in the sky, knowing the Buddha's intention, addressed the Buddha Vipaśyin: 'Excellent! If the sixty-two thousand bhikṣus are to wander among the villages to practice cultivation as they please, and after six years return to their home country to receive and uphold the Prātimokṣa, now is indeed the right time.' The Buddha thereupon proclaimed: 'Among you in the assembly of bhikṣus, let sixty-two thousand persons wander among the villages to practice cultivation as they please; after six years return to your home country to receive and uphold the Prātimokṣa.' The sixty-two thousand persons, having heard this, went out of the city of Bandhumatī to wander and dwell in various directions."

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> Without outflows, equal to the unequaled, the tamer, the great man,
> Who guides the multitudes of beings, leading them to the path of tranquility.
> Now he dispatches the assembly of bhikṣus, the supreme śrāvakas of the Buddha,
> Sixty-two thousand in number, going out from the city of Bandhumatī,
> Wandering among the villages, possessed of great dragon-like majesty,
> Practicing cultivation as they please; after six years, returning to their home country.

At that time, having spoken this verse, the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣus: "Those sixty-two thousand people thereupon went out of the city and wandered to various villages to practice cultivation as they pleased. Thus one year passed, then two years; by the sixth year, those bhikṣus said to one another: 'Six years are now complete; we should return to our home country.' As they spoke these words, the deva in the sky again addressed them: 'Now is indeed the right time; return to the city of Bandhumatī to receive and uphold the Prātimokṣa.' Thereupon the sixty-two thousand bhikṣus, by their own spiritual powers and the majesty of the devas, arrived at the city of Bandhumatī in a brief moment."

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> Those great bhikṣus of that Buddha, sixty-two thousand in assembly,
> Wandering among the villages as they pleased, the six years now complete.
> Reflecting that they wished to return, the devas all gave notice,
> Bidding them return to the city of Bandhumatī to receive the pure and cooling precepts.
> Hearing this, they rejoiced greatly, the hairs of their bodies standing upright in joy;
> Immediately employing their spiritual powers, like riding great dragon-elephants,
> In a brief moment they returned to their home city, at ease without any hindrance.
> The unsurpassed one honored among two-footed beings, arisen in this world,
> Who has excellently proclaimed the assemblies of precepts and rules, liberating all sentient beings:
> Now he shall expound and set forth the Prātimokṣa.

At that time, having spoken this verse, the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣus: "Those sixty-two thousand people arrived at the city of Bandhumatī, came to the presence of the Buddha Vipaśyin, prostrated at his feet, and sat to one side. The Buddha said: 'Listen carefully! I shall now recite the Prātimokṣa:

> Patience is supreme;
> One who can be patient attains nirvāṇa.
> As the Buddhas of the past have declared:
> Those who go forth to become śramaṇas
> Must keep far from harm and killing,
> And the seven transgressions of body and speech.
> Upholding these precepts completely,
> They give rise to great wisdom,
> Attaining the pure body of a Buddha,
> Without equal in this world,
> Bringing forth the wisdom free of outflows,
> Bringing to an end the suffering of birth and death.'

"At that time, as the World-Honored One proclaimed these precepts, various devas, by their divine power, descended from their heavenly palaces and came before the Buddha Vipaśyin; they made obeisance with joined palms and listened to receive the Prātimokṣa."

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> Without outflows, inconceivable, breaking through darkness to reach the further shore:
> Śakra, Brahmā, and all the devas together listened to the precepts of the great Ṛṣi.

At that time, having spoken this verse, the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣus: "On one occasion I was dwelling in a quiet abode near the Seven-Leaf Cave at Rājagṛha, when I suddenly reflected: 'When the past Buddha Vipaśyin taught the Vinaya Piṭaka, it may be that some devas did not come to hear the great ṛṣi's precepts. Let me now go to the heavens and inquire of the Brahmā assembly.' Having formed this thought, I at that time entered samādhi, and like a mighty man extending his arm, arrived in an instant at Sudṛśa Heaven. The devas there bowed their heads to my feet and said: 'Welcome, World-Honored One! It has been long since you came here. We are śrāvaka disciples of the samyak-saṃbuddha Vipaśyin. That Buddha was of Kṣatriya caste, of the Kauṇḍinya clan; he went forth in faith. His lifespan was eighty thousand years. His father was King Bandhumat; his mother was Queen Bandhumatī. Prince Khaṇḍa and Tiṣya went forth, received the precepts, and attained arhatship. The greatly worthy foremost attendant was named Aśoka. At three assemblies he taught the Dharma and broadly liberated śrāvakas: at the first great assembly, sixty-two thousand persons attained arhatship; at the second great assembly, one hundred thousand persons attained arhatship; at the third great assembly, eighty thousand persons attained arhatship. Such was the Buddha Vipaśyin: such was his supreme distinction, such was his going forth, such was his realization of bodhi, such was his teaching of the Dharma, such was his discipline. He caused his disciples, wearing robes and bearing bowls, to practice the brahma-conduct, to be far removed from the five desires, to sever the afflictions and attain liberation, to realize the dharma of non-arising, and to become anāgāmins. Likewise with the Buddha Śikhin, the Buddha Viśvabhū, the Buddha Krakucchanda, the Buddha Kanakamuni, and the Buddha Kāśyapa: through teaching the Dharma and discipline, wearing robes and bearing bowls, practicing the brahma-conduct, being far removed from the five desires, severing the afflictions and realizing the dharma of non-arising, becoming anāgāmins, it was exactly the same.'

"At that time, the World-Honored One, accompanied by countless hundreds of thousands of devas reverently surrounding him, proceeded to Sudarśana Heaven. The devas there, upon seeing the Buddha, bowed their heads to his feet and, together with countless hundreds of thousands of devas reverently surrounding him, proceeded to Akaniṣṭha Heaven. At that time the heavenly king, seeing the World-Honored One from afar, prostrated with all five limbs to the ground, bowing to the World-Honored One's feet, and said: 'Welcome, World-Honored One! It has been long since you came here. We are śrāvaka disciples of the samyak-saṃbuddha Vipaśyin. That Buddha was of Kṣatriya caste, of the Kauṇḍinya clan; his lifespan was eighty thousand years. His father was King Bandhumat; his mother was Queen Bandhumatī; the city was likewise named Bandhumat. Prince Khaṇḍa and Tiṣya went forth, received the precepts, and attained arhatship. The greatly worthy foremost attendant was named Aśoka. At three assemblies he taught the Dharma and broadly liberated śrāvakas: at the first great assembly, sixty-two thousand persons attained arhatship; at the second great assembly, one hundred thousand persons attained arhatship; at the third great assembly, eighty thousand persons attained arhatship. Such was the Buddha Vipaśyin: such was his supreme distinction, such was his going forth, such was his brahma-conduct, such was his realization of bodhi, such was his teaching of the Dharma, such was his discipline. He caused his disciples, wearing robes and bearing bowls, to practice the brahma-conduct, to be far removed from the five desires, to sever the afflictions and realize the dharma of non-arising, and to become anāgāmins. Likewise with the Buddha Śikhin, the Buddha Viśvabhū, the Buddha Krakucchanda, the Buddha Kanakamuni, and the Buddha Kāśyapa: wearing robes and bearing bowls, teaching the Dharma and discipline, practicing the brahma-conduct, being far removed from the five desires, realizing the dharma of non-arising, becoming anāgāmins, it was exactly the same. Now the Great Muni also teaches the Dharma and the brahma-conduct, disciplines beings, and it is exactly the same.'

"At that time, the devas spoke these verses:

> The Unsurpassed Honored One among Two-Footed Beings, entering samādhi,
> Swiftly wielding great supranormal power, departing the realm of Jambudvīpa,
> Came to Sudṛśa Heaven, as a mighty man
> Swiftly extends his arm, arriving here in an instant.
> The World-Honored One is most rare: without outflows, without obstruction,
> A pure, liberated body, like a lotus untouched by water.
> Throughout a hundred thousand worlds, none is equal to the Buddha;
> He subdues the great king Māra as a river sweeps away fine grass.
> The devas of Sudṛśa and all the rest come together to bow their heads,
> Taking refuge in the Highest Honored One, the Perfectly Enlightened One of great compassion,
> Who disciplines all beings, with the six faculties entirely pure,
> Bringing forth unsurpassed wisdom, striving diligently in accordance with the Dharma.
> The past Buddha Vipaśyin, the Honored One of complete and perfect enlightenment,
> The subtle and wondrous Dharma he proclaimed, at three assemblies liberated śrāvakas;
> The rules of discipline and the brahma-conduct, guarded without deficiency or transgression,
> Pure and complete, like the moon on the fifteenth night.
> The World-Honored Buddha Śikhin, the Tathāgata Viśvabhū,
> Krakucchanda of the Bhadrakalpa, Kanakamuni,
> And the Buddha Kāśyapa: all these Tathāgatas,
> The multitudes of śrāvakas they liberated have all attained the exhaustion of outflows,
> With no further afflictions. Ever cultivating the seven factors of awakening,
> And practicing the eightfold right path, able to depart from the faults of the five desires,
> Penetrating to great wisdom, known to all assemblies.
> As Vaiśravaṇa ever partakes of the taste of ambrosia,
> As a king receives consecration, forever shattering all darkness,
> As the sun radiates its light: each and every Buddha, World-Honored One,
> Through dignified conduct and Dharma-practice, benefits all beings;
> Opening various skillful means to guide them, there is no difference whatsoever."

At that time, the World-Honored One, having spoken these verses, addressed the bhikṣus: "When I went to those heavens and heard these things, I understood that devas and humans all come with joyful assent to the Dharma-assemblies of all Buddhas. If there is any person who delights in receiving and upholding this, who in walking, standing, sitting, and lying down reflects upon and recites it, they will have no confusion, will permanently sever the cycle of rebirth, and will attain liberation and bliss."

When the Buddha had finished expounding this sūtra, all were greatly joyful, received it with faith, and reverently put it into practice.
