# 大方廣師子吼經
## Mahāvaipulya Sūtra of the Lion's Roar

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0836

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Divākara (地婆訶羅)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Bhagavān was in the Palace of the Sun and Moon, in the Victory-Treasury Hall, together with a great assembly of ninety billion bhikṣus and countless bodhisattva mahāsattvas.

At that time the Buddha told the Bodhisattva Victory-Accumulation: "To the north of here, past Buddha-lands equal in number to the dust-motes of sixty Ganges-rivers' worth of billions of nayutas of hundreds of thousands of lands, there is a world called Joyful. The Buddha there, named Dharma-Arising Tathāgata, Worthy of Offerings, Perfectly Awakened One, is presently teaching the Dharma, establishing, upholding, and universally benefiting all. He now wishes to teach the Great Vaipulya, named 'The Lion's Roar,' which is difficult to encounter and difficult to hear. You should go there and listen to the essential Dharma."

At that time the Bodhisattva Victory-Accumulation, having received the Buddha's instruction, immediately went to the Joyful World. He beheld the Buddha Dharma-Arising, bowed at his feet, circumambulated him seven times to the right, then withdrew and stood to one side.

The Buddha Dharma-Arising, seeing the Bodhisattva Victory-Accumulation and knowing the answer yet purposely asking, spoke thus: "Good man! Where have you come from?"

The Bodhisattva Victory-Accumulation then abided in his own mind, silently, without speaking a word. The devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, humans, non-humans, and the entire great assembly all thought: "The Bodhisattva Victory-Accumulation! The Honored One of the three realms has asked a question. How can he remain silent and not answer, just abiding in his own mind?"

At that time the Buddha, with his broad, bright, lustrous, blue-lotus eyes, stretched like a lion, surveyed the ten directions universally and, knowing the assembly's doubt, manifested a gentle smile and emitted a great golden light. This light was interspersed with countless hundreds of thousands of various different colors, universally illuminating the ten directions. All the lands throughout the cosmos trembled and shook.

At that time the assemblies of bodhisattvas in the ten directions, seeing this spiritual transformation, in various forms and various garments, came to the Buddha's place, bowed at the Buddha's feet, and each sat upon lotus-flower seats adorned with their own merit.

At that time the Bodhisattva Lightning-Garland rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, placed his right knee on the ground, joined his palms toward the Buddha, and, joyful and amazed as never before, addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I have seen countless spiritual transformations in the past, but never have I seen a radiance and earth-trembling such as I now behold. Excellent, World-Honored One! We pray you will explain the cause. Why did you smile? We beg your compassion and pity to resolve this assembly's doubt." At that time the Bodhisattva Lightning-Garland made this request in verse:

> "Great compassionate guide and teacher,
> Your smile is not without cause.
> For the benefit of sentient beings, we pray the Buddha
> Will graciously give a definitive explanation."

At that time the Tathāgata Dharma-Arising, dignified and resplendent, his body shining with the radiance of Jambudvīpa gold, radiated countless hundreds of thousands of billions of nayutas of subtle and wondrous beams of light, like a great golden pillar. The Bodhisattva Lightning-Garland addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I have seen countless spiritual powers and lights, but none compared to today. Never have I seen the like before."

The Buddha told Lightning-Garland: "Just so, just so! This great radiance and spiritual power of the Tathāgata is rarely manifested. Without a great cause, this sign would not be shown. Listen carefully, listen carefully! Reflect well upon it, and I shall explain the cause of my smile. Do not be alarmed or frightened, do not entertain other hopes, strengthen your mind, and do not give rise to doubt. Why? The realm of the Buddhas is inconceivable; the power of their vows and spiritual penetration is inconceivable. Contemplate this deeply and by no means harbor doubt. Lightning-Garland! Did you see the Bodhisattva Victory-Accumulation, the emissary of Śākyamuni Buddha?"

Lightning-Garland said: "I did."

"Good man! When I asked this Bodhisattva Victory-Accumulation 'Where have you come from?' he abided in his own mind in silence and did not answer me. Seeing this, I manifested a smile."

"The Tathāgata asked and he did not answer. Now the entire assembly finds this strange and puzzling."

"Good man! The Bodhisattva Victory-Accumulation understands it thus: 'All dharmas are without coming and without going. How can the World-Honored One ask me where I have come from? He knows that all dharmas are without speech and that a place cannot be found. How then can one say where one has come from?' Good man! This is already a brief statement of the true nature of all dharmas.

"Good man! The Bodhisattva Victory-Accumulation! Among all dharmas there are no words and no speech, because the nature of words has been left behind. All dharmas are without arising, because the nature of arising has been left behind. All dharmas are without any destination, because destination has been rightly cut off. All dharmas are without manifestation, because there is nothing upon which to depend. They transcend mind, thought, and consciousness; they depart from all causes and conditions. They are without name, without words, without action, without indication. They surpass the domain of the eye and the rest. There is no accumulation, no arising, no concept of departure. There is no location, departed from all locations. The Dharma has only one word, namely 'no word.' Originally there was no speech; what is there to speak of? Good man! You should know that non-speech is true speech."

At that time the Bodhisattva Pure-Body, empowered by the Buddha's spiritual force, addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! If non-speech is true speech, then should not the mute and the silent all be teaching the Dharma?"

The Buddha said: "Just so, good man! As you say. Not only do the mute and silent teach the Dharma, but those who are not mute also all teach the Dharma, yet they do not know the Dharma."

The Bodhisattva Pure-Body addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! We pray you will clearly explain: how do all sentient beings teach the Dharma yet not know the Dharma?"

The Buddha told the Bodhisattva Pure-Body: "Good man! It is like a person born blind standing in sunlight yet unable to see the sun. A bystander tells him about it, and only through another's voice does he know the sun exists. In the same way, all dharmas fully enter the Dharma-realm. The Dharma-realm is without words, apart from the nature of all words. It is not something that sentient beings can explain or express. Yet because of causes and conditions there is speech. It is like an echo in a deep valley: the valley is empty and without sound, yet because of causes and conditions the sound of an echo arises. In this way, good man, through the coming together of causes and conditions, words and sounds appear, yet the realm of sentient beings is empty and without any words.

"Good man! The speech and language that sentient beings possess should all be known as entering the four kinds of unobstructed wisdom. What is spoken enters the unobstructed wisdom of Dharma. What is not spoken enters the unobstructed wisdom of meaning. Through words one analyzes and dissects, entering the unobstructed wisdom of phrasing. Corresponding to facts, resolving without impediment, one enters the unobstructed wisdom of eloquent teaching. The speech and language that sentient beings possess should all be known as entering these four sentences of Dharma. The sentence of true meaning has originally never moved. It is like the person born blind who follows another's words and grasps at them, but this is not true seeing. Therefore, good man! Those who wish to seek the Dharma should seek it within their own person. Those who wish to seek bodhi should seek it within the five aggregates."

When this sentence of true meaning was taught, the great trichiliocosm universally quaked in six ways, and a great light pervaded everything. The Buddha extended his broad, long tongue-mark, which covered the entire great trichiliocosm. From this tongue-mark he emitted countless billions of nayutas of hundreds of thousands of beams of light. From the great hells below to the pinnacle of existence above, all the worlds were universally illuminated by this light. Then he withdrew the tongue-mark and addressed the great assembly: "All of you should know that the Tathāgata's broad, long tongue-mark was obtained through truthful speech. What the Tathāgata teaches should be received with respect and given true and genuine faith. Do not harbor hesitation or give rise to doubt."

At that time the assembly of bodhisattvas of the ten grounds and all the rest, together with the devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, humans, non-humans, and the entire great assembly, all rose from their seats, joined their palms, and with one voice addressed the Buddha: "We pray the World-Honored One will teach the true meaning! We pray the Sugata will teach the true meaning! We of this assembly wish only for the Dharma that the Tathāgata has realized; we do not wish for any other dharma. We of this great assembly are entirely without doubt."

At that time the World-Honored One surveyed the entire great assembly a second and a third time, and spoke thus: "Out of compassion and pity for all the world, for the benefit, peace, and joy of the many, with the wealth of Dharma I bring peace to all devas and humans. For this reason I now make the great lion's roar. Good men! You should know that in the Sahā world, Śākyamuni Tathāgata, Worthy of Offerings, Perfectly Awakened One, who presently teaches the Dharma and establishes, upholds, and delivers sentient beings, is myself, the Tathāgata Dharma-Arising. In the Sahā world I take on various forms to benefit sentient beings, taming and delivering each according to what is fitting."

At that time the great assembly, hearing these words, was filled with amazement. They leapt with joy as never before, and with one voice exclaimed: "Excellent, excellent! The World-Honored One, wishing all sentient beings to make the lion's roar, has taught the true and real Dharma of the lion's roar for this great assembly. Those who are able to hear it should know that their good roots are not few, to say nothing of those who receive and uphold it, read and recite it, broadly proclaim and circulate it, and make offerings with various garlands, various garments, banners, pennants, parasols, and burning, anointing, and powdered incenses with respect and reverence! Such persons are guarded and protected by all the Buddhas."

At that time the Buddha praised the bodhisattvas, saying: "Excellent, excellent! Good men! As you say, the merit of such good men and good women is not small. If there are those who hear this true lion's roar Dharma of the Buddha and, with purified minds, even once exclaim 'Excellent!', I shall embrace and protect them all. They will also be attended to by Maitreya. These persons carry my bodhi upon their two shoulders. In the five turbidities, receiving and believing this sūtra, in life after life wherever they are born, I shall bring them to maturity, and they will also be embraced and protected by Maitreya. These persons will be able to dry up the ocean of birth-and-death, subdue the hosts of māras, extinguish all afflictions, strike the great Dharma-drum, forever leave behind the female body, crush all hostile obstacles, and extinguish all aggregations of fetters.

"If there are good men and good women who, for ten asaṃkhyeya kalpas through dust-motes of great trichiliocosms, supply and make offerings to all the Tathāgatas with every kind of enjoyment, yet if they hear this sūtra of true and real spiritual power and harbor doubt and disbelief, such persons have committed a fault toward the Buddha and do not truly make offerings to all the Buddhas. If there are good men who hear of the Tathāgata's true and real virtue and, with pure faith, offer praise, the merit of this surpasses the former a hundred-thousand-fold. Such persons are those who truly make offerings.

"Good men! If among you there are those whose minds are purified by faith in me, you should carefully write, receive, and uphold this sūtra. Wherever this sūtra abides, there all the Buddhas dwell."

At that time the Bodhisattva Victory-Accumulation, the Bodhisattva Lightning-Garland, the Bodhisattva Constant-Light, the Bodhisattva Pure-Eye, the Bodhisattva Maitreya, the Bodhisattva Fearless-Action, the Bodhisattva Avalokiteśvara, the Bodhisattva Mahāsthāmaprāpta, the Bodhisattva Mañjuśrī, the Bodhisattva Accumulation-of-Eloquence, the Bodhisattva Courageous-Eloquence, the Bodhisattva Remover-of-All-Obstacles, the Bodhisattva Light-Maker, and the Bodhisattva Samantabhadra, with these as the foremost, eighty-four billion nayutas of hundreds of thousands of bodhisattva mahāsattvas all addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! In the latter age to come, we shall broadly circulate this sūtra and cause sentient beings to awaken to great nirvāṇa. World-Honored One! Those who have not long planted good roots will not even hear this sūtra enter their ears. If there are those who receive and uphold this sūtra and broadly circulate it, the praise of their merit, even throughout hundreds of thousands of billions of nayutas of kalpas, cannot be exhausted."

At that time the World-Honored One told the bodhisattvas: "Excellent, excellent! All of you should thus honor and revere the Buddha's teaching, and receive and uphold the right Dharma."

When the Buddha had finished teaching this sūtra, the Bodhisattva Victory-Accumulation, together with all the devas, humans, asuras, gandharvas, and the entire great assembly, joyfully received and practiced it.
