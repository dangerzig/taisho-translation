# 毘盧遮那五字真言修習儀軌
## Ritual Manual for Practicing the Five-Syllable Dhāraṇī of Vairocana

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0861

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空)

---

To practice and accomplish the Dharma of Vairocana Buddha, one should first find a quiet, clean place, smear the ground with cow-dung, sprinkle it with fragrant water, burn various renowned incenses, and scatter various seasonal flowers. One should be in a place that is tranquil and free from clamor. Having bathed and purified oneself, one puts on fresh, clean clothing and sits in the cross-legged posture. Or one may sit according to the type of practice: for pacification, in the auspicious posture facing north; for increase, in the lotus posture facing east; for attraction, in the vajra posture facing west; for subjugation, in the squatting posture with feet pressing feet, facing south.

According to one's preference, sit facing the appropriate direction. Anoint the hands with fragrant paste and join the palms above the crown in homage to all the Buddhas. Then, from below, beginning with the wind-wheel, visualize the syllable *vaṃ*, adorned with two dots, golden in form, emitting blazing, fiercely radiant light. The vajra fire breaks through the great earth and surges upward, streaming forth. Then visualize this vajra fire burning one's own body until only ashes remain, their color according to the four types of practice. Visualize the ashes becoming a maṇḍala; within the maṇḍala, visualize a lotus; within the lotus, visualize the syllable *vaṃ* transforming into a vajra form. Visualize one's own body as composed of vajra.

Then form the Samaya Seal: make the *sampuṭa* (cupped-palm) mudrā with both hands, erect both thumbs, and empower the five places. The dhāraṇī:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ asame trisame samaye svāhā*

Next form the Dharmadhātu Seal: make fists with both hands, thumbs inside the palms, and erect both index fingers. Visualize the syllable *raṃ* above the crown and contemplate the true meaning: all dharmas are like dust, unobtainable. The dhāraṇī:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ dharmadhātu-svabhāvako hūṃ*

Next form the Wheel Seal: interlace both hands facing outward, with both thumbs touching inside the right palm. Contemplate one's own body as Vajrasattva, and empower with this seal. The dhāraṇī:

*Namaḥ samanta-vajrāṇāṃ vajrātmako hūṃ*

Next form the Armor Seal: make the *sampuṭa* mudrā with both hands, bend both index fingers to press on the backs of the middle fingers at the upper joint, erect both thumbs side by side, and seal the five places. The dhāraṇī:

*Namaḥ samanta-vajrāṇāṃ vajra-kavaca hūṃ*

Then, before one's own body, visualize the Great Lotus King. Within the lotus, visualize the golden syllable *kha*. The syllable *kha* transforms into the Bhagavān, the Great Courageous Honored One, abiding in samādhi, forming the sword seal. Then form the Tathāgata Hook Seal to invite the principal deity: interlace both hands inward to make fists, erect the right index finger like a hook, and beckon toward oneself. The dhāraṇī:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ aḥ sarvatra-pratihata tathāgata-kuśa-bodhicaryā-paripūraka svāhā*

When the holy assembly has arrived, present the argha water. Cup both hands and hold it before the forehead, visualizing bathing the holy assembly. The dhāraṇī:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ gagana-sama-sama svāhā*

Next present the seat-offering seal: join both hands in a hollow-heart mudrā, spread and bend the six fingers outward like a lotus shape. The dhāraṇī:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ aḥ*

Next form the seal of the Holy Immovable One: make fists with both hands, erect the index and middle fingers of each hand, and with both thumbs press the ring and little fingers. The left hand is the sheath, the right hand is the sword. Turn the sword to the left to banish all māra-obstacles; turn to the right, up and down, to establish the boundaries of all directions. The dhāraṇī:

*Namaḥ samanta-vajrāṇāṃ caṇḍa-mahāroṣaṇa sphaṭaya hūṃ trāṭ hāṃ māṃ*

Then with the sword seal, touch the incense and flowers, seeking the stainless to purify them, thus accomplishing the empowerment and protection. Place within the sheath.

Next form the Vairocana Five-Syllable Sword Seal: join both palms, bend both index fingers to touch each other in the shape of a sword. The dhāraṇī:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ a vi ra hūṃ khaṃ*

Next form the Unguent Seal: extend the right palm outward, and with the left hand grasp the right wrist like a ring. The dhāraṇī:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ viśuddha-gandhā-udbhave svāhā*

Next form the Flower Seal: interlace both hands inward, palms facing up with index fingers touching, and rest the thumbs at the lower joints of the index fingers. The dhāraṇī:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ mahā-maitryā-abhyudgate svāhā*

Next form the Incense Seal: join the backs of the middle, ring, and little fingers of both hands, erect the index fingers straight and slightly inclined together, with the thumbs at the lower joints of the index fingers. The dhāraṇī:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ dharmadhātu-anudgate svāhā*

Next form the Food and Drink Seal with dhāraṇī: "Join the samādhi and wisdom palms, mutually interlace all five wheels: this is the seal for holding all things, the universal offering seal."

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ arara karara maliṃ-dandame svāhā*

Next form the Lamp Seal: make a fist with the right hand, erect the middle finger straight, bend the index finger with the thumb pressing it against the middle finger. The dhāraṇī:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ tathāgatārcis-spharaṇā-avabhāsana-gaganodaryā svāhā*

The offered unguent, flower-garlands, incense, food and drink, and lamplight should each follow the colors of the four types of practice in making the offerings. Through these five offering seals, one accomplishes the universal offering of fragrant flower cloud-oceans for the assembled holy ones.

Next form the Fulfillment Seal: join both hands in a hollow-heart mudrā and place it above the crown. The dhāraṇī:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ oṃ vismaye spharahimaṃ gaganakam dharmadhātu-avakaśa-samata-ye sarvathā-gatā-parikṣa-maṇḍala mama-pūjā-maliṇa tathāgata-adhiṣṭhāna maliṇa-cāme svāhā*

Through this dhāraṇī seal and the cloud-ocean of fragrant-flower offerings, the truth is thus without substance. Then, using the five syllables, distribute them from the lower body to the crown: earth, water, fire, wind, space. Visualize one's own body as the golden-hued Vairocana Buddha, with universally radiant light, crowned and adorned with necklaces, wearing gauze garments. Form the sword seal, rest it at the heart, and abide in samādhi. Hold the beads with both hands, join them within the palms, and recite the dhāraṇī seven times. Empower and place upon the crown. The dhāraṇī:

*Oṃ vairocana-mālā svāhā*

Then, holding the beads with both hands at the heart, recite the principal deity's mantra for the prescribed number of repetitions. When finished, rest the beads within the palms, join the palms above the crown, and transfer merit with the aspiration: "By this merit, may all sentient beings swiftly realize Vairocana Buddhahood!" Having set down the beads, form the meditation seal, sit upright with correct posture. Above the crown, visualize the white syllable *aṃ*. Upon both pupils, visualize the syllable *raṃ*. Then slightly bow the head and fix the mind on the space within the chest, visualizing a perfectly full moon-disc. Upon the moon-disc, arrange the five syllables. Visualize the syllable *a* in the center, golden in color; the remaining four syllables are arranged clockwise.

The true meaning of the five syllables:

The syllable *a*: because all dharmas are originally unborn. The syllable *vi*: because, in terms of meaning, all dharmas are unobtainable through speech. The syllable *ra*: because all dharmas are apart from defilement. The syllable *hūṃ*: because the cause of all dharmas is unobtainable. The syllable *khaṃ*: because all dharmas are like space, unobtainable. This is called the mental recitation.

Then form the sword seal, recite seven times, and release it above the crown. Again form the five offering seals, present the argha, and recite praises. Form the Acala sword seal, turn to the left to release the boundary. Form the samaya seal, place it above the crown, recite three times, and visualize sending off the holy assembly.

Bow to the Buddha and rise. Walk about at will, read and recite Mahāyāna vaipulya sūtras. Transfer the merit of these blessings to all sentient beings. May one swiftly perfect the wishes of the mind, and may all worldly and world-transcending siddhis be accomplished.
