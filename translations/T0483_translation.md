# 三曼陀跋陀羅菩薩經
## Sūtra of Samantabhadra Bodhisattva

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0483

Translated from the Chinese. Western Jin dynasty, translated by Nie Daozhen (聶道真)

---

## Chapter One: The Five Coverings

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the land of Magadha, at the Pure Dharma Site, upon the self-arising vajra seat. Its brilliant light was exceedingly bright, universally illuminating all. He was together with an innumerable assembly of mahāsattvas and bodhisattvas, with Samantabhadra Bodhisattva and Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva foremost among them.

Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva asked Samantabhadra Bodhisattva: "If there are those who seek the bodhisattva path, good men and good women who wish to attain the state of being free from coverings and pure, what dharma should they practice to achieve it?"

Samantabhadra replied to Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva: "If good men and good women wish to seek the bodhisattva path, they should arrange their garments, and three times each day and night prostrate to the Buddhas of the ten directions, make obeisance, repent, and confess all the evil they have done. Whatever should be patiently accepted, they should patiently accept. Whatever should be venerated with obeisance, they should venerate with obeisance. Whatever should be delighted in with aspiration, they should delight in with aspiration. Whatever should be exhorted and invited, they should exhort and invite. In this way, all coverings of wrongdoing, all coverings of defilement, and all coverings of dharmas will be entirely removed, and all merits will be fully perfected.

"The Prajñāpāramitā, the Daśabhūmika Sūtra, all samādhis, all dhāraṇīs, and all upāyakauśalya: these are the foremost guides among all sūtras. One who practices in this way has already paid homage to all the Buddhas, with a mind that is utterly sincere."

## Chapter Two: Repentance

Samantabhadra Bodhisattva said: "All evil done by body, committed by speech, or conceived by mind, all evil thoughts that arise equal to the dust-motes in all the buddha-lands of all the Buddhas: I repent of all these evils for their sake: 'Whatever evil I have done since the beginning, whether against the Buddhas, the bodhisattvas, the kalyāṇamitras, parents, arhats, pratyekabuddhas, the Tathāgatas, the spirits of the Tathāgatas' temples, or the Dharma of the Tathāgatas, all the transgressions and evils I have committed; whether regarding Sukhāvatī, the buddha-land of Amitābha Buddha, or all the Buddhas, all the buddha-lands, and all the Buddhas' Dharma, if I have harbored doubt, given rise to disbelief: I repent of all these sins. For whatever slander I have committed against all the Buddhas, the bodhisattvas, the kalyāṇamitras, parents, arhats, pratyekabuddhas, and all people; if I have indulged in desire, indulged in delusion, indulged in willfulness; if I have been obstinate and refused to speak with others; if I have been led by lust, led by stinginess and jealousy, led by gluttony, led by flattery, led by the seven hundred and fifty desires: when my mind was disordered and I could not concentrate, being covered by all coverings and fearful of all fears, with all the faults arising in the mind, I now repent of all these sins for their sake.

"'Since asaṃkhyeya kalpas, I have harbored evil intentions toward the Buddha, or caused dissension in the Saṅgha of bhikṣus, or harmed arhats, or harmed my parents, or upon seeing the correct Dharma declared it to be non-Dharma, or upon seeing non-Dharma declared it to be the Dharma, or mocked all people, or conceived thoughts always connected with wrongful matters. Whatever transgressions others have committed, whatever I was about to commit, or whatever I had already committed; whatever precepts the Tathāgata taught that I have violated, whether in this life or in former lives when I did not know the Dharma of the Buddha and the Saṅgha of bhikṣus, all the transgressions and evils I have committed: I now repent of all these sins for their sake.

"'Whatever acts of envious obstruction I have committed: if there was a Buddha, obstructing people from seeing him; if there was one learned in the sūtras and teaching the Dharma, obstructing people from hearing; if there was a kalyāṇamitra, obstructing people from attending the assembly; if people made offerings of robes, alms-food, bedding, or medicine for the sick, and I scolded and obstructed them from giving, creating innumerable offenses without end, further causing others to commit sins: I now repent of all these sins for their sake.

"'Whatever sins I have committed, if I saw others commit transgressions and aided and encouraged them, and on account of this was bound by sin and born in a degenerate age; if I was born into a poor family; if I was separated from kalyāṇamitras; if there was a Buddha and I could not see him; if there were bodhisattva kalyāṇamitras and I could not meet with them in assembly, and could not hear the sūtras and the Dharma: because of all the evils I have done, I could not encounter these noble ones in person. I now repent of all these sins for their sake.

"'Whatever sins I have committed that prevented me from hearing the Dharma; or if, upon hearing the Dharma, my mind could not receive it; or if, having received it, I forgot and lost it; if I could not firmly uphold the Dharma, could not truly uphold the Dharma, and was timid and without courage; if my physical appearance could not become dignified; if in my births I always lacked wealth and treasures; if I could not attain the practice of dhāraṇī, could not attain the practice of samādhi, could not attain the practice of the prajñāpāramitā, could not attain the practice of the wisdom of non-conceptual mind, could not attain the wisdom entering into upāyakauśalya, and could not attain the wisdom entering into the unobstructed Daśabhūmika: all the sins I have committed that prevented me from attaining these, I now repent of all these sins for their sake.

"'Whatever sins I have committed that prevented me from attaining the merit of wisdom entering into all dharma-practices, the merit of the wisdom of the mental activities of all people, the merit of the wisdom entering through the five faculties of all people, the merit of the wisdom of the moral discipline of all people, the merit of wisdom entering into all dharma-wisdom, and the merit of the nirvāṇa-wisdom of all people: all the sins I have committed that prevented me from attaining these, I now repent of all these sins for their sake.

"'Whatever sins I have committed that prevented me from attaining the divine eye and divine ear, spiritual powers of flight, the knowledge of my own former lives, the knowledge of past and future events, the voice of Brahmā, the merits of body, speech, and mind, and the pure and lofty practice, so that I could not become complete in all merits: all the sins I have committed that prevented me from attaining these, I now repent of all these sins for their sake.

"'If others have harbored evil intentions toward me, or if there have been armed conflicts, or if I have given rise to loving-kindness toward others, or if there have been armed conflicts, or if I have brought about all coverings and fears: I now gather together before all the Buddhas, before all the truths of the eye, the truths of wisdom, and the truths of immediate acceptance, before these truths I take refuge and repent. Further I expose and reveal; having revealed, I reproach myself and do not dare to conceal. From this day forward, I do not dare to commit them again.'"

## Chapter Three: Aspiration and Delight

Samantabhadra Bodhisattva said: "Good men and good women who seek the bodhisattva path should make this aspiration and delight: 'Now I take refuge in the All-Knowing One. Before all the Buddhas, bodhisattvas, kalyāṇamitras, parents, arhats, pratyekabuddhas, and all people, I sincerely seek compassion. Those I have not yet awakened, I now awaken them, as the Buddhas know. Such is the refuge I take; I have already taken such refuge.

"'Furthermore, I now make obeisance to all the Buddhas, all the bodhisattvas, the kalyāṇamitras, parents, arhats, and pratyekabuddhas, making obeisance to them all as the highest, most supreme, unsurpassed, as the bright among the bright, without peer and without equal, as the Buddhas know. Such is the obeisance I should make; I have already made such obeisance.

"'Furthermore, I now wish to venerate the merits of all the Buddhas, the merits of all the bodhisattvas and kalyāṇamitras, the merits of all the arhats and pratyekabuddhas, and the merits made by all people of the ten directions, as the Buddhas know. Such are the merits I should venerate; I have venerated them all.

"'This is the bodhisattva's wisdom. If good men and good women have such merit, I aspire to and delight in aiding their joy. Whatever attains the Buddha's wisdom, I should aspire to and delight in; I have already aspired and delighted. For those who have not yet made merit, now that they make merit, I aspire to and delight in it all. For the most honored, I also aspire to and delight in the merit of what the most honored has done. Holding this merit of aspiration and delight, may all in the ten directions obtain it.'"

## Chapter Four: Exhortation and Invitation

Samantabhadra Bodhisattva said: "Good men and good women who seek the bodhisattva path should make this exhortation and invitation: 'I sincerely exhort and invite all the Buddhas. For the Buddhas presently existing, who have attained anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi, those who have already become fully awakened but have not yet turned the Dharma-wheel: I exhort and invite all the Buddhas to turn the Dharma-wheel. Through this exhortation and invitation, may the sūtras and Dharma now being taught cause all people each to obtain their proper place and all to be at peace. May the devas, nāgas, spirits, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, humans, and non-humans, and those in the hells, the hungry ghost realm, and the animal realm who labor in suffering all attain liberation. May those who have not yet awakened all abandon their deluded minds, all attain right understanding, and enter the Buddha's path.

"'Furthermore, for those Buddhas who wish to enter parinirvāṇa, I exhort and invite them: please do not enter parinirvāṇa yet. For the sake of all people, please abide for immeasurable kalpas, abiding in the dharma-body as non-abiding. May the sūtras and Dharma being taught cause all people each to obtain their proper place. May all be valiant and complete in the practices of the Dharma of Samantabhadra Bodhisattva. May all people, taking this as their foundation, each attain peace. May the devas, nāgas, spirits, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, humans and non-humans, those in the hells, the hungry ghost realm, and the animal realm, and all who labor in suffering, swiftly attain liberation. May those who have not yet awakened abandon their deluded minds, all attain right understanding, and enter the Buddha's path. May those who walk the crooked path all abandon the crooked path, enter the right path, and all abide in the original dharma of non-arising.'"

Samantabhadra Bodhisattva said: "Good men and good women who seek the bodhisattva path should make offerings and dedications: 'All the merit of my repentance, my patient acceptance, my obeisance, my aspiration and delight, and my exhortation and invitation, whether I intend to do, am in the midst of doing, or have already done, all the merit of these deeds: may it all be gathered together and unified, becoming one single flavor of merit. As is the Dharma of the Buddhas, as the Buddhas know, this merit can produce the marks of the Buddhas in the place of one's birth, and enable one to attain the dharma of freedom at will. All offerings already received, those being received, and those yet to be received: these offerings are right offerings, non-attached and severed. I hold this merit of Dharma-offerings and dedications, that all people may attain the Dharma, that all may give rise to this aspiration. Just as the offering of sarvajñā is an offering of equality, so too I now make offerings: may they be like the practices of Samantabhadra Bodhisattva, holding this merit and giving it to all. May I not fall into the hells, the hungry ghost realm, the animal realm, or the eight unfavorable conditions. May I always be born where there are Buddhas and bodhisattvas. May I be born in the Sukhāvatī realm of Amitābha Buddha. Holding this merit, and through my good aspiration, may I fully and universally give rise to the mind of anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi.

"'I hold this merit of Dharma-offerings and dedications, on behalf of all people, to serve as shelter and protection. Receiving their refuge, I serve as a ferryman. In the darkness I serve as a light, and among lights the brightest light. Among supports I serve as a support, and among supports the most honored support. For all people not yet ferried across, I shall ferry them across. For those not yet liberated, I shall liberate them. For those not yet in parinirvāṇa, I shall cause them to enter parinirvāṇa. Working to benefit all people, may all give rise to the mind of anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi.

"'I hold this merit of Dharma-offerings and dedications, that all people, together with me, in all our places of birth, in all our aspirations, may always make offerings to all the Buddhas, make offerings to all the bodhisattvas. Holding the merit of these prior offerings to the Buddhas and bodhisattvas, may all people, together with me, never be separated from the bodhisattva Dharma, never separated from the kalyāṇamitras, from Mañjuśrī, from Vimalakīrti, and from Samantabhadra Bodhisattva and his equals. May the practices of these bodhisattvas be fully perfected in dhāraṇī, pure samādhi, single-pointed and unmovable mind. May all have accomplished the practice of the prajñāpāramitā, fully understood upāyakauśalya, and the wisdom entering into all dharmas with discrimination. May all people, together with me, attain and fully perfect the wise conduct of these bodhisattvas.

"'I hold this merit of Dharma-offerings and dedications: for those in the hells, the hungry ghost realm, the animal realm, and those bound and imprisoned, may all attain liberation. May those without eyes gain sight, may the deaf gain hearing, may all who labor in suffering attain peace. Whether in this buddha-land or buddha-lands of other regions, from the lowest Avīci hell to the highest realm, and all the crawling creatures in between, whether with feet or without, whether unborn, soft-born, or transformation-born, whether with form or formless, whether with thought or without thought, and all people and non-people who depend upon each other: may they, at the proper time, be able to hold the Buddha-eye, to see, know, and become aware of all. May all attain human form and enter the Buddha's path, hearing the Dharma and fully understanding and receiving it. May all attain the mind of anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi.

"'I hold this merit of Dharma-offerings and dedications, giving it to all. I hold this merit: may all attain the practices equal to the Buddhas, the practices equal to the bodhisattvas, the practices of the kalyāṇamitras. May all people make offerings and give rise to vows, attain the buddha-lands, and be able to purify them. Regarding the dharmas of the three times, may they understand and fully know them. Like a vajra that penetrates all things, may all people, together with me, attain the Buddha's wisdom, fully perfected, able to move and affect all equally. Among all the profound wisdoms, may they all attain realization. Among all dharmas, may they be without doubt. Holding this merit, may I fully perfect my vows, like the Dharma-practice of Samantabhadra Bodhisattva, fully attaining the ten powers and the stages. Let this serve as witness. Holding this merit, may all people and I attain this fortune.'"

## Chapter Five: Comparing Merit

Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva asked Samantabhadra Bodhisattva: "If good men and good women who seek the bodhisattva path practice the dharma of exhortation, delight, and so forth three times each day and night as described above, what is the merit of such a person?"

Samantabhadra Bodhisattva replied to Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva: "If good men and good women who practice the bodhisattva path were to fill Jambudvīpa with the seven treasures and offer them to the Tathāgatas, Arhats, Samyaksaṃbuddhas, this would not compare to the merit of good men and good women who, three times each day and night, practice the dharma of exhortation and delight, repenting what should be repented, patiently accepting what should be accepted, paying obeisance to what should receive obeisance, aspiring to and delighting in what should inspire aspiration and delight, exhorting and inviting what should be exhorted and invited, making offerings and dedications of what should be offered and dedicated, practicing day and night according to the teaching given above. The merit of this surpasses that of offering the seven treasures filling Jambudvīpa to the Tathāgatas by a hundredfold, a thousandfold, a ten-thousandfold, a hundred-millionfold, a trillion-fold, and is utterly beyond comparison, beyond calculation, and beyond analogy."

When this Dharma was taught, innumerable devas, abiding in the sky, held heavenly flowers, incense, and music as offerings, scattering them upon the Buddha and all the bodhisattvas.

When Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva and Samantabhadra Bodhisattva had finished teaching this sūtra, the devas, nāgas, spirits, asuras, humans, and non-humans, hearing the sūtra, greatly rejoiced, made obeisance before the Buddha, and departed.
