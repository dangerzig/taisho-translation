# 降三世忿怒明王念誦儀軌
## Recitation Ritual for the Trailokyavijaya Wrathful Vidyārāja

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1210

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

At that time all buddhas, bodhisattvas, and all the worthy and holy ones manifested in the world for the benefit of all sentient beings. For those whose ways were contrary to the true dharma, who damaged the country, and who were māras harming sentient beings: Akṣobhya-Samantabhadra, because of his profoundly deep great vow, manifested a wrathful form and addressed the Buddha: "My true words are extremely rare in the world. May all the tathāgatas hear and permit me to proclaim them. We shall guard and protect the teaching of all buddhas and benefit all sentient beings."

At that time the Buddha declared: "Excellent, excellent! Wrathful King! Listen as I speak the subjugation true word."

Then he manifested the form of the most extremely evil, great wrathful one, with four faces and eight arms, and spoke the verses of the true word:

*Oṃ sumbhani sumbha hūṃ gṛhṇa gṛhṇa hūṃ gṛhṇa-paya hūṃ ānaya hoḥ bhagavan-vajra hūṃ phaṭ*

At that time, when Samantabhadra Mahāsattva and the others spoke this true word, the great trichiliocosm quaked in six ways. All the heavenly māras and ghost realms that caused harm to sentient beings were filled with terror and could find no peace. They all fled and gathered together to address the Wrathful Lord: "We beseech you to have compassion and free us from fear."

At that time the Wrathful King granted them all peace and security. His right foot treads upon the great sovereign māra king below, and his left foot treads upon the māra's wife below. He causes all sentient beings in the country to attain happiness.

"Reciting this true word even once causes immeasurable, boundless māra-realms to suffer tormenting heat. Those who create obstacles for the practitioner will have no power; they will become the practitioner's servants and even their obstructions will be turned aside.

"The practitioner should guard the three karmas, inner and outer. This mantra also serves for protection, boundary-establishment, and body-sealing at the five points. For the mudrā ritual procedures, one should consult the Vajraśekhara Yoga Teaching; they are not retranslated here. No separate mudrā seal-methods are needed; the awesome efficacy resides entirely in this true word. There is no additional separate mantra.

"If one wishes to cultivate this true word, sit facing east before the image in the wrathful posture. Visualize one's own body as identical to the honored Wrathful King himself, then purify the three karmas. Recite the syllable *Hūṃ* three times to establish the dharmadhātu. Then with both hands in firm bond, raise them above the forehead and turn left and right three times; this also constitutes protection and boundary-establishment.

"Next, with utmost sincerity in añjali, bow in worship to the Wrathful Lord and all the holy assembly.

"If one wishes to subjugate all evil persons, construct a triangular hearth. The altar faces south. Take evil wood and burn one piece per recitation, for a total of three hundred and twenty-four recitations. The evil person will of themselves be subjugated and come to take refuge.

"If one wishes to cause an evil person to fall ill, construct a subjugation altar. Recite the root true word one thousand and eighty times, and burn red and black mustard seeds, one thousand and eighty grains, burning one per recitation. Within the mantra phrases, recite the person's name. This will cause a long illness without causing death.

"If one wishes to heal illness, construct a pacification altar facing east. Burn one thousand grains of white rice, one per recitation. Within the mantra phrases, insert the words 'remove illness,' and the illness will be pacified. The person will submit to the Buddhadharma without any contrary intent, like a cut branch.

"If one wishes to suddenly destroy an evil person, place that person's name and image in the hearth. With a malicious mind, recite the mantra one hundred and eight times, and throw sand at the image and burn it; the person will die. If one wishes to immediately revive them, face the Wrathful King and with a mind of compassion recite one hundred and eight times; without delay they will revive, completely unharmed.

"If one wishes to be victorious in military battles, take one hundred and eight pieces of fragrant flower-wood, recite the true word over each and burn them; even against a demonic enemy army, one will immediately be victorious. One should also use the five grains and five incenses; if these are not available, use whatever is at hand, without being troubled.

"If one wishes to enter a mountain forest without encountering dangerous birds, beasts, or venomous insects, hold any convenient stone in both palms, empower it with fourteen recitations, and throw it onto the path ahead. All birds, beasts, and venomous insects will be subdued, and none will have the intention to harm.

"If one wishes to win the love and respect of the king and great ministers, construct a love-and-respect altar-hearth. Burn one hundred and eight chrysanthemum roots, empowering each one per recitation; love and respect will come of their own accord.

"If one wishes to save a person with a critical illness, empower fragrant water before the image one hundred and eight times. Quickly sprinkle it upon the patient's abdomen and chest, and they will awaken.

"If one wishes to cure madness or epilepsy, take dragon-spring water and empower it one hundred and eight times. Wash both eyes, mouth, tongue, and so forth, and the illness will naturally be extinguished without any remaining symptoms.

"If one wishes to bind a man or woman and have them openly answer all questions, take a pomegranate-wood staff one zhang and two chi long. Empower it ten thousand times, then separately empower each stroke and strike the person fourteen times. They will answer truthfully whatever is asked, and after the interrogation is concluded, visualize releasing them from the binding.

"If a practitioner cultivates this dharma, for forty-nine days they must abstain from the five grains, flavoring, and salt, with single-minded concentration on nothing else. When the mantra reaches one hundred thousand recitations, at that time the honored deity will manifest in form, appearing as a great, dreadful, terrifying māra-body, to test the practitioner's resolve. At this moment, take care not to become agitated or fearful, and the dharma will be accomplished."

At that time, when the Wrathful King had spoken this true word, all the heavenly māras, evil ghosts, and others were all greatly subdued. They believed, accepted, and took refuge in the Buddha's path, and each became a guardian and protector of the practitioner.

Next, dissolve the boundary, bow to the Buddha, and leave the altar-hall. Then read the Prajñāpāramitā and other sūtras at one's leisure.
