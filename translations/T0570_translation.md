# 佛說賢首經
## Sūtra of Bhadraśrī, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0570

Translated from the Chinese. Western Qin dynasty, translated by Sheng Jian (聖堅)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the land of Magadha, at the Pure Dharma Seat. At that time there was a great assembly: the bodhisattvas, including the Bodhisattva Maitreya, bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, upāsikās, devas, nāgas, spirits, and asuras. The Buddha told Śāriputra: "The Buddhas of the ten directions are today also holding great assemblies, teaching the sūtras for the bodhisattvas."

At the Pure Dharma Seat there was an upāsikā named Bhadraśrī, the consort of King Bimbisāra. She joined her palms and said to the Buddha: "I wish to hear the names of the Buddhas of the ten directions, and the names of the bodhisattvas and their lands."

The Buddha said: "Excellent! Excellent! I shall tell you. In the east there is a Buddha named Entering Diligence, in a land called Steadfast Tree, with a bodhisattva named Reverence Chief. In the south there is a Buddha named Never-Abandoning Joyful Diligence, in a land called World-Remembrance Tree, with a bodhisattva named Awakening Chief. In the west there is a Buddha named Long Diligence, in a land called Lotus Tree, with a bodhisattva named Jewel Chief. In the north there is a Buddha named Sun Diligence, in a land called Radiant Tree, with a bodhisattva named Generosity Chief. In the northeast there is a Buddha named Compassion Diligence, in a land called Blue Lotus Tree, with a bodhisattva named Merit Chief. In the southeast there is a Buddha named Hundred Lotus Diligence, in a land called Fragrance Tree, with a bodhisattva named Command Chief. In the southwest there is a Buddha named Supreme Diligence, in a land called Jewel Tree, with a bodhisattva named Diligence Chief. In the northwest there is a Buddha named Single-Crossing Diligence, in a land called Contemplation Tree, with a bodhisattva named Virtue Chief. Below there is a Buddha named Brahmā Diligence, in a land called Crystal Tree, with a bodhisattva named Wisdom Chief."

Bhadraśrī, hearing the names of the Buddhas of the ten directions, the bodhisattvas, and their lands, leaped with joy. She came forward, placed her head on the ground, paid respects to the Buddha, and said: "Although I have now generated the mind of anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi, by what practice can I leave behind the female body?"

The Buddha said: "Excellent! Excellent! This is a question worthy of a Buddha."

The Buddha said: "Bhadraśrī! By practicing ten groups of virtues, one can leave behind the female body.

"There is one thing by which a woman can quickly attain a male body and realize anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi. What is this one thing? To generate the aspiration for omniscience, to create immeasurable merit, and to be able to never forget it. This is the one thing.

"There are two things by which a woman can quickly attain a male body and realize anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi. What are these two? First, to act in accordance with one's words, not worshipping the devas but taking refuge only in the Buddhas. Second, to act without believing in heterodox views. These are the two things.

"There are three things by which a woman can quickly attain a male body and realize anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi. What are these three? First, to always purely guard the three actions of the body. Second, to purely guard the four actions of speech. Third, to purely guard the three actions of the mind. These are the three things.

"There are four things by which a woman can quickly attain a male body and realize anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi. What are these four? First, to give without flattery or deceit. Second, to keep the precepts without flattery or deceit. Third, to guard one's own purity. Fourth, to hear the six dharmas without flattery or deceit. These are the four things.

"There are five things by which a woman can quickly attain a male body and realize anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi. What are these five? First, to act in accordance with the Dharma. Second, to make all one's deeds accord with the Dharma. Third, upon hearing the Dharma, to abide firmly. Fourth, not to delight in the female body. Fifth, to aspire to become a man. These are the five things.

"There are six things by which a woman can quickly attain a male body and realize anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi. What are these six? First, not to be lazy, and never to forget one's deeds. Second, to have a gentle mind. Third, to be simple and honest. Fourth, not to be flattering or deceitful. Fifth, to be without vanity. Sixth, to be sincere in all one's actions. These are the six things.

"There are seven things by which a woman can quickly attain a male body and realize anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi. What are these seven? First, to always be mindful of the Buddha, the Dharma body. Second, to always be mindful of the Dharma, attaining the Buddha's wisdom. Third, to always be mindful of the Saṃgha, who will come to be one's assembly. Fourth, to always be mindful of the precepts, seeking purity. Fifth, to always be mindful of generosity, removing all defilements. Sixth, to always be mindful of the heavens, so as to develop the bodhisattva mind. Seventh, to always be mindful of human beings, wishing to liberate all. These are the seven things.

"There are eight things by which a woman can quickly attain a male body and realize anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi. What are these eight? First, not to take pleasure in food and fine garments. Second, not to adorn oneself with flowers. Third, not to use fragrances. Fourth, not to use mixed perfumes. Fifth, not to go to places of entertainment and amusement. Sixth, not to enjoy music and performance. Seventh, not to engage in singing and dancing. Eighth, to observe the six monthly fasts. These are the eight things.

"There are nine things by which a woman can quickly attain a male body and realize anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi. What are these nine? First, not to cut off anything. Second, not to be attached to anything. Third, to have no self. Fourth, not to conceive of a person. Fifth, not to conceive of a lifespan. Sixth, not to conceive of a life-force. Seventh, not to conceive of a place of birth. Eighth, not to conceive of no place of birth. Ninth, only to depart from the twelve links of dependent origination. These are the nine things.

"There are ten things by which a woman can quickly attain a male body and realize anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi. What are these ten? First, to have kindness toward all. Second, not to covet any person's possessions. Third, not to think of another person's male body. Fourth, not to speak with a forked tongue. Fifth, not to speak harshly. Sixth, not to speak falsely. Seventh, not to speak frivolously. Eighth, when others offer music and entertainment, not to take pleasure in it. Ninth, neither to give rise to resentful thoughts nor to harbor hatred, but simply to abide in equanimity. Tenth, not to form connections with others through wrongful causes and false blessings. These are the ten things."

When the Buddha had finished teaching the sūtra, Bhadraśrī and sixty thousand upāsikās in Magadha, hearing these practices, all generated the mind of anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi. The bodhisattvas, including Maitreya, the bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, upāsikās, devas, nāgas, spirits, and asuras all came forward, placed their heads on the ground, paid respects to the Buddha, and departed with joy.
