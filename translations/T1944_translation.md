# 禮法華經儀式
## Rite for Paying Homage to the Lotus Sūtra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1944

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown (失譯)

---

With one mind, I bow in homage to the eternally dwelling Three Jewels throughout all dharma-realms of the ten directions. May this cloud of incense and flowers pervade all realms of the ten directions, making offerings to all buddhas, to the Wondrous Dharma Lotus Sūtra, and to the assembly of bodhisattvas and śrāvakas, to be received and used for the work of buddhahood.

> I bow my head to the buddhas of the ten directions, who perfect the supreme and complete vehicle.
> Original ground and traces open two gates; through teachings and parables, the true and secret is expounded.
> Universally causing all provisional and lesser practitioners to fully realize the buddha-bodhi.
> I now vow to take refuge; may I transcend the ocean of birth and death.

With one mind, I bow in homage to the Original Teacher, Śākyamuni Buddha.

With one mind, I bow in homage to Prabhūtaratna Buddha of the past.

With one mind, I bow in homage to the emanation bodies of Śākyamuni Buddha throughout the ten directions.

With one mind, I bow in homage to all the buddhas mentioned throughout the Lotus Sūtra, and to all buddhas of the ten directions and three times.

With one mind, I bow in homage to the Wondrous Dharma Lotus Sūtra, the Dharma-treasure of the wondrous word.

With one mind, I bow in homage to all the bodhisattvas, śrāvakas, and pratyekabuddhas mentioned throughout the Lotus Sūtra, and to all the Saṅgha of worthy and noble ones who have attained the path throughout the ten directions and three times.

With one mind, I bow in homage to Bodhisattva Mahāsattva Samantabhadra.

With sincere heart, I confess and repent. I and all sentient beings, from beginningless time, have lost the true mind and wandered in birth and death. The obstructions of wrongdoing arising from the six sense faculties are immeasurable and boundless. The perfect and wondrous buddha-vehicle has not been opened and understood. All our wishes have not been fulfilled.

Now I pay homage and reverence to the Wondrous Dharma Lotus Sūtra. With these roots of virtue, I expose and confess all dark and evil deeds. May all the boundless and grievous sins committed through body, speech, and mind across past, present, and future be entirely extinguished. May body and mind become pure, with the obstructions of delusion removed. May the adornments of merit and wisdom increase and the pure causes grow. May the practices and vows of self and others be swiftly and completely fulfilled.

May all the tathāgatas always be present, expounding the Dharma. For all merits, may we give rise to a mind of sympathetic joy. May we dedicate these merits toward bodhi and attain the fruit of eternal bliss. At the day of death, may right-mindfulness appear before us. May we behold face to face Amitābha and all the holy assembly. In an instant may we be born within a lotus flower. May all sentient beings universally attain the buddha-path.

Namo Buddha. Namo Dharma. Namo Saṅgha. Namo Śākyamuni Buddha. Namo the Wondrous Dharma Lotus Sūtra. Namo Bodhisattva Mañjuśrī. Namo Bodhisattva Samantabhadra.
