# 佛說呪時氣病經
## Sūtra Spoken by the Buddha on a Spell for Seasonal Illness

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1326

Translated from the Chinese. Eastern Jin, translated by Dharmanandin (曇無蘭)

---

Homage to the Buddha, homage to the Dharma, homage to the Saṃgha of bhikṣus. Homage to the Seven Buddhas of the Past, homage to all Buddhas of the present, homage to all Buddhas of the future, homage to all disciples of the Buddhas. May the spell I pronounce be fulfilled according to my wish.

*akhani nikhani akhayani khani abhirā mandalī vācinī pādelī*

Homage to the Buddha, homage to the Dharma, homage to the Saṃgha of bhikṣus. Homage to the Seven Buddhas of the Past, homage to all Buddhas of the present, homage to all Buddhas of the future, homage to all disciples of the Buddhas. Homage to all teachers, homage to all disciples of the teachers. May the spell I pronounce be fulfilled according to my wish. If a person contracts a seasonal illness, tie a thread and recite the spell seven times over it. Also write the names of the demons and spirits mentioned above on paper or on bark of the Chinese scholar tree, and bind this to the end of the thread. When reciting this spell, one should observe the fast, be pure in body, bathe and rinse the mouth, burn incense, rectify the mind, and only then pronounce it.
