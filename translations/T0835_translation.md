# 如來師子吼經
## Sūtra of the Tathāgata's Lion's Roar

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0835

Translated from the Chinese. Northern Wei dynasty, translated by Buddhaśānta (佛陀扇多)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Bhagavān was in the Palace of the Sun and Moon, in the Victory-Treasury Hall, together with a great assembly of ninety-nine billion bhikṣus and bodhisattva mahāsattvas numbering eighty-four thousand billion nayutas of hundreds of thousands.

At that time the World-Honored One told the Bodhisattva Victory-Accumulation: "To the north of here, past six thousand billion Ganges-rivers' worth of Buddha-worlds, and again past hundreds of thousands of billions of incalculable worlds beyond, there is a Buddha-world called Joyful. The Buddha there, named Dharma-Superior Tathāgata, Worthy of Offerings, Perfectly Awakened One, presently lives and dwells, teaching the Dharma for sentient beings. That World-Honored One now wishes to teach a great lion's roar vaipulya Dharma-gate. You should go there and listen to this supreme Dharma. Good man! That supreme Dharma-gate is exceedingly difficult to hear."

At that time the Bodhisattva Victory-Accumulation received the Buddha's instruction and, practicing as instructed, went to that world. He bowed at the feet of the Tathāgata Dharma-Superior, circumambulated him seven times to the right, then withdrew and sat to one side.

After the Bodhisattva Victory-Accumulation had sat to one side, the Tathāgata Dharma-Superior, knowing the answer yet purposely asking, spoke thus: "Good man! Where have you come from?"

The Bodhisattva Victory-Accumulation remained silent and did not answer.

At that time the devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, humans, non-humans, and all the rest had this thought: "For what reason does this Bodhisattva Victory-Accumulation, when questioned by the Honored One of the three realms, not answer but remain silent?"

At that time the World-Honored One, with eyes like vast, broad blue lotus flowers, surveying the ten directions like a lion at play, knowing the great assembly harbored doubt, emitted a radiance. The Tathāgata Dharma-Superior emitted a golden light that universally illuminated the ten directions, interspersed with countless hundreds of thousands of various colors, and this world trembled and shook in six ways.

Bodhisattvas from the assemblies of the ten directions, seeing this spiritual transformation, manifesting in various forms and various wondrous appearances, having witnessed the various events of the light emitted by the Tathāgata, gathered in that land. They all bowed at the Tathāgata's feet, and each sat upon a lotus-flower seat according to the merit and virtue of their own portion of good roots.

At that time the Bodhisattva Lightning-Flame rose from his seat, arranged his robe over his left shoulder, came before the Buddha, placed his right knee on the ground, joined his palms toward the Buddha, and, joyful and exultant, addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! The countless radiances that the World-Honored One has emitted, which I now behold, I have never before seen since time without beginning. Excellent, World-Honored One! We pray you will explain this matter. Why did the Great Compassionate One emit this radiance? We pray you will explain its meaning."

At that time the Bodhisattva Lightning-Flame asked in verse:

> "All unsurpassed great beings
> Do not emit this radiance without cause or condition.
> We pray you will explain for us:
> Truly, out of compassion and to benefit sentient beings,
> You have emitted this great radiance.
> What is the cause? We pray you will explain."

At that time the Bodhisattva Lightning-Flame beheld the Tathāgata's body, the color of Jambudvīpa gold, emitting countless hundreds of thousands of billions of rays of light, like a golden pillar, manifesting freely. Having seen this, he addressed the Tathāgata Dharma-Superior: "World-Honored One! I have seen countless radiances before, but never have I seen such supreme and wondrous events of radiance as these."

That Buddha replied: "Just so, Lightning-Flame! As you say. Only in a few places do the Buddhas, the Tathāgatas, emit such radiance, and without a cause such a sign would not be shown. Listen now, Lightning-Flame! Listen carefully, reflect well upon it, and I shall explain the reason why the Buddhas, the Tathāgatas, emit this radiance. Good man! When you have heard it, do not be alarmed or doubtful, do not be frightened or fearful. Why? The realm of the Buddhas is inconceivable. The power of their immeasurable supreme vows is inconceivable. Their acts of adornment are inconceivable. Good man! Having seen this, do not be alarmed or doubtful. Lightning-Flame, you should know: the Bodhisattva Victory-Accumulation is an emissary of the Buddha Śākyamuni, sent to my Joyful world. Did you see him?"

The Bodhisattva Lightning-Flame answered that Buddha: "I saw him, World-Honored One! I saw him, Sugata!"

The Buddha said: "Good man! When I asked this Bodhisattva Victory-Accumulation, 'Good man! Where have you come from?' he did not answer me but remained silent. Good man! Seeing this, I emitted the radiance. Good man! For what reason, when I asked, did he not answer? The great assembly, hearing this, was startled and puzzled, and he remained silent."

The Buddha said: "Good man! Why did this son of good family not answer when the Tathāgata asked?"

The Bodhisattva Victory-Accumulation addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! How can the World-Honored One, regarding the dharma-nature of the future, ask me, 'Good man! Where have you come from?' The dharma that the Buddha has awakened to is inexpressible, and the nature of all dharmas is inexpressible. I have no eloquence with which to say from where I have come."

At that time the Tathāgata told the great assembly: "Good men! This good man has briefly stated all dharmas and shown the place of the true and real path. This Bodhisattva Victory-Accumulation: regarding the peerless dharmas, they are inexpressible and indemonstrable, apart from letters, apart from above and below, apart from coming and going. All paths are cut off, and nothing can be seen at the place of the ācārya. They transcend mind, thought, and consciousness, and depart from all causes and conditions. They are without name, without words, indemonstrable, inexpressible, invisible, and beyond the domain of the eye and the rest. There is no accumulation, no speech, no precedent, apart from marks. What is called a single word cannot be expounded. How then could he answer me?"

At that time the Bodhisattva Light-Sound, empowered by the Buddha's spiritual force, addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! If all dharmas are inexpressible and yet there is speech, then all the words of the foolish and deluded would also be teaching the Dharma."

The Buddha said: "Good man! As you say. Not only do all the foolish and deluded teach the Dharma; those who are not foolish also teach the Dharma, yet they do not realize it."

At that time the Bodhisattva Light-Sound addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! We pray the World-Honored One will explain: how do all sentient beings teach the Dharma yet not realize it?"

At that time the World-Honored One told the Bodhisattva Light-Sound: "Good man! It is like a person born blind in this world who dwells in sunlight, yet cannot know what the sun's disc looks like or what color the sunlight is. If another person tells the blind man about it, only through another's voice does he know the sun's radiance has such-and-such a mark. Yet the sun's radiance is apart from all names and words, and is not something that sentient beings can express or explain. Because of causes and conditions, the sound is heard.

"Good man! It is like echoes in a deep mountain valley in this world that arise because of causes and conditions. Good man! The mountain valley is empty and the echo is empty, yet because of causes and conditions it arises.

"Good man! All the speech and language that sentient beings possess enters into the [four kinds of] eloquence. What has not yet been spoken, when the mind has not yet been aroused, is called the unobstructed wisdom of Dharma. When the mind has been aroused but words have not yet been spoken, it is called the unobstructed wisdom of meaning. When one reaches the point of analyzing and expounding, it is called the unobstructed wisdom of phrasing. When one speaks without obstruction or impediment, it is called the unobstructed wisdom of eloquent teaching. Therefore, good man! All the speech and language that sentient beings possess should be known as not departing from these four Dharma-gate meanings and not departing from the ultimate reality. It is like the blind person who, when taught by another, then knows the sun's disc and the sun's radiance, and abandons his conceit.

"Good man! Those who wish to seek meaning should seek it within their own persons. Good man! Those who wish to seek bodhi should seek it within the five aggregates."

When this meaning of the sentence of true and real speech was taught, the great trichiliocosm universally trembled and shook in six ways, and a great radiance pervaded and illuminated everything. The Tathāgata Dharma-Superior extended his broad, long tongue, covering the entire great trichiliocosm. From within that radiance, countless hundreds of thousands of billions of further rays of light emerged, illuminating everywhere from the great hells below to the pinnacle of existence above.

At that time that Buddha withdrew his radiance and told the great assembly: "Good men! Never could such a tongue-mark and radiance speak falsely. You should all have true faith and good mindfulness: I speak what is true and real."

At that time the bodhisattvas of the ten grounds were the foremost of the assembly. Together with all the devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, humans, non-humans, and all the rest, they rose from their seats, joined their palms, and with one voice addressed the Buddha: "We pray the World-Honored One will teach the true and real meaning! We pray the Sugata will teach the true and real Dharma-meaning! We of this great assembly, by the Buddha's spiritual power, have no mind of doubt or confusion."

At that time the World-Honored One told the great assembly: "Good men! This is the great lion's roar. I now wish to speak it for the peace, benefit, and joy of all sentient beings, and even of devas and humans. Good men! In the Sahā world, there is a World-Honored One named Śākyamuni Tathāgata, Worthy of Offerings, Perfectly Awakened One, who presently lives and dwells, teaching the Dharma for sentient beings. Good men! That is my present body. The Tathāgata Dharma-Superior, for the benefit of sentient beings, manifests various forms in that Sahā world and teaches the Dharma for them."

At that time the great assembly, hearing these words, was filled with exceedingly great joy. Leaping and exulting beyond measure, they exclaimed in praise of the Tathāgata: "Excellent, excellent! World-Honored One! May all sentient beings make such a great lion's roar, as the World-Honored One now makes the great lion's roar in this assembly. World-Honored One! If there are those who hear this lion's roar, those sentient beings cannot be called ones who have planted meager good roots. If there are those who hear the Tathāgata's great lion's roar and, having heard it, receive it with faith, how much more those who read, recite, receive, and uphold it, broadly teach it for others, and make offerings to this sūtra with incense, flowers, unguents, powdered incense, burning incense, various garments, jeweled banners, and jeweled parasols! The World-Honored One should know: these sentient beings are empowered by the Buddha. If there are those who hear even one phrase of this sūtra of the great lion's roar enter their ears, those sentient beings have not planted merely meager good roots and merit."

At that time the World-Honored One praised the bodhisattvas: "Excellent, excellent! Good men! As you say: those sentient beings did not plant merely meager good roots and merit to be able to hear this. If they can hear the Tathāgata's teaching of even a phrase of this sūtra of the great lion's roar and, with joyful minds, speak even a single word of praise saying 'Excellent!': good men! They are all protected by me and also protected by the Bodhisattva Maitreya. Those persons carry my bodhi upon their shoulders. If in the five turbidities they can receive and believe this sūtra, they are those whom I have already brought to maturity in the past, and they are also protected by the Bodhisattva Ajita. Those persons have already dried up the great ocean of birth-and-death, already subdued the hosts of māra, already extinguished all afflictions, already sounded the great Dharma-thunder, already struck the great Dharma-drum, already left behind the female body, already vanquished all hostile obstacles, and already extinguished all the masses of fetters.

"Good men! If there are good men and good women who, filling ten asaṃkhyeya great trichiliocosms with all the Buddhas, the Tathāgatas, supply and make offerings to them with every kind of offering; yet if there are those who hear even a phrase of this sūtra of true and real meaning and can receive it with faith, and because of their faith exclaim 'Excellent!', the merit of this surpasses the former offering to all the Buddhas, the Tathāgatas, of three thousand great thousand worlds. For this reason, good men! If among you there are those who believe my words, you should write, receive, and uphold this sūtra. Good men! Wherever this sūtra is written and offerings are made, you should know that in that household the Tathāgata dwells."

At that time the Bodhisattva Victory-Accumulation, the Bodhisattva Lightning-Flame, the Bodhisattva Constant-Light, the Bodhisattva Pure-Eye, the Bodhisattva Fearless, the Bodhisattva Avalokiteśvara, the Bodhisattva Mahāsthāmaprāpta, the Bodhisattva Mañjuśrī, the Bodhisattva Accumulation-of-Eloquence, the Bodhisattva Remover-of-All-Obstacles, the Bodhisattva Light-Maker, and the Bodhisattva Samantabhadra, with these as the foremost, eighty-four thousand billion hundreds of thousands of bodhisattva mahāsattvas, addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! In the future age to come, we shall broadly proclaim and teach the phrases of this Dharma-gate. World-Honored One! Those sentient beings who hear this sūtra may also be called those who have attained nirvāṇa. World-Honored One! If there are those who can believe this sūtra, those sentient beings have not merely planted meager good roots. World-Honored One! If this sūtra enters their ears even once, how much more for those who fully read, recite, receive, and uphold it! The merit attained by those sentient beings cannot be exhausted even through hundreds of thousands of billions of kalpas."

At that time the World-Honored One told the bodhisattva mahāsattvas: "Excellent, excellent! Good men! All of you should thus protect and uphold the Tathāgata's right Dharma."

When the Buddha had finished teaching this Dharma-gate, the Bodhisattva Victory-Accumulation and the entire great assembly all rejoiced greatly.
