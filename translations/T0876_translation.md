# 金剛頂經瑜伽修習毘盧遮那三摩地法

## The Vajraśekhara Sūtra: Yoga Method for Cultivating the Samādhi of Vairocana

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0876

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Vajrabodhi (金剛智).

---

### Preface from the Newly Obtained Zhenyuan Catalog of Mahāyāna and Hīnayāna Sūtras

In the fourth year since the Emperor's accession, all things were properly ordered and the people were at peace. Civil and military cultivation were both complete, and the governance of distant regions was well established. Military accomplishments were secured, and literary principles flourished. Hardly any lost texts or abandoned documents remained ungathered.

There was a monk named Héng'ān, who had studied emptiness in the way of the Buddha and whose practice was deeply firm. He reflected that the essential teachings of tathatā must rely on ancestral transmission, yet among what had survived through the ages of loss and destruction, much was damaged and incomplete. He thought: "Even after the Qin burning of books, texts of high antiquity still survive. Even after the wall of Confucius's house crumbled, the old master's documents were still partly recovered. How could the sacred teachings perish entirely?" With lasting feeling, he resolved to fill these gaps. Whether famous monasteries or distinguished sites, he went to each as if returning home. He traveled north to Mount Wutai and west through the passes and ranges, chanting by mouth and copying by hand, leaving nothing uncollected. In the end he obtained one hundred thirty-two works totaling three hundred forty-three fascicles from the newly translated Zhenyuan catalog of Mahāyāna and Hīnayāna sūtras, vinaya, and śāstra, together with catalogs of all sūtra sources. Through perilous terrain he bore these himself on his back, bringing them to the capital and reporting all to the Emperor. The Emperor commended him and ordered that all be deposited in the official monastic repositories. Without a white horse the palm-leaf manuscripts gathered like clouds; without crossing the desert the Sanskrit texts arrived. Although this was due to the diligence and vows of a son of the Śākyas, it was truly accomplished through the illumination and inspiration of the sagely ruler. The unification of carriages and writing begins from this.

As for the sūtras of the Zhenyuan catalog, these were translated by the Indian monk Ajitasena and six others, commissioned by our Emperor Dezong, the Divinely Martial and Filially Cultured, in the jiǎxū year of the Zhenyuan era.

The way of Maitreya and its Dharma are non-dual. The wise grasp its greatness while the unwise grasp only its smallness. It is like a single rain moistening the earth equally, yet the plants differ in bitterness and sweetness; like a single sun illuminating the sky without distinction, yet creatures differ in experiencing day and night. The profoundly deep scriptures flourished from this, and the meanings of the complete and partial teachings can clearly be seen. Words are the flowers of the mind; without flowers, there can be no fruit. Sūtras are the root of the teaching; without roots, nothing can grow. Disclosing the essential principles, expounding all wonders, departing far from the three realms, existing before heaven and earth were formed; liberating from all sufferings, transcending all arising and ceasing. Those who awaken attain it in an instant; those who are deluded lose it through kalpas of sand. Boundless and immeasurable, unmoving and unstained, existing upon existing, empty beyond empty. With sincere faith, one's nature is entirely capable. Through dedication, all things become merit. In every place, all look up in reverence. Each and every kind obtains benefit. It is truly the acupuncture needle for sentient beings and the great ordering principle of the world. If one discards the footprints to seek the rabbit, what rabbit can be found? If one abandons the raft to cross the water, what water can be crossed? The sages grow ever more distant, yet their subtle words remain. What person of virtue who propagates the Way would not be moved to reverence?

Written in the bǐngwǔ year of the Bǎodà era, winter, tenth month.

> I take refuge in Vairocana Buddha,
> Whose body, speech, and mind pervade empty space,
> Who expounds the Tathāgata's gate of the three mysteries,
> The supremely profound teaching of the vajra one vehicle.
> I rely on the most supreme yoga method
> To reveal the true place of practice,
> To enable sentient beings to manifest the real,
> And suddenly realize unsurpassed perfect enlightenment.
> The disciple with firm bodhicitta,
> Having received the abhiṣeka position from the teacher,
> Wondrously cultivates concentration and wisdom, constantly observing,
> Deeply entering the gate of skillful application.
> Guiding sentient beings to supreme awakening,
> Embracing them with the four methods of attraction,
> Never tiring of great compassion, never abandoning,
> Upon seeing even small virtue, offering praise.
> Non-abiding dāna giving, equal to empty space,
> Able to shatter ignorance and darkness with wisdom's light,
> When beings desire something, never going against them,
> Speaking first with a smile to gladden the heart.
> Within the wondrous Dharma, unstained,
> Skillfully using prajñā to sever all fetters,
> The supreme Dharma wheel never retreating,
> Expounding fearlessly with the four kinds of eloquence.
> In all activities of buddhas and sentient beings,
> Always donning the armor of the great compassion vow,
> Defeating Māra's victorious armies,
> Firmly upholding all the secrets of the buddhas.
> One who possesses such manifold virtues
> Is worthy of being approved for transmission.
> In places where former buddhas and holy sages have dwelt,
> Various excellent sites or mountain retreats,
> Establish a meditation chamber and lay out the wheel-maṇḍala.
> Smear with fragrant paste to create the sacred positions.
> Arrange lamps, arghya water, and all implements.
> Scatter wondrous flowers to adorn the ground.
> To transform the worlds of sentient beings
> Into a pure and wondrous buddha-land,
> With these phrases of self-and-other purity,
> Contemplate according to principle and silently recite.

Mantra:

*Oṃ svabhāva-śuddha sarva-dharma svabhāva-śuddho 'haṃ*

> Next direct the mind to pervade the dharmadhātu.
> Buddha-oceans in every dust-mote land fill empty space.
> Empower the three karmas with the seed-syllable *hūṃ*.
> Form the vajra-rising seal to universally awaken.
> The dāna-wisdom hook, forming vajra fists,
> The vigor fingers joined, raised three times.

Mantra:

*Oṃ vajra tiṣṭha*

> Through the empowerment of this mantra and seal,
> All buddhas, not attached to the bliss of quiescence,
> Arise from samādhi to attend the assembly,
> Observing the practitioner and embracing them together.
> Next form the great seal of Vajradhara.
> One by one, visualize prostrating at each tathāgata's feet.
> The dhyāna-wisdom and dāna-jñāna fingers inversely interlaced,
> The right knee on the ground, place the seal upon the crown.

Mantra:

*Oṃ vajra bhūḥ*

> Having just formed the Vajradhara seal,
> All perfectly enlightened ones accord with you.
> Before the buddhas of the ten directions,
> Homage, service, and offerings are all perfected.
> To serve and attend all tathāgatas,
> Abandon the body and offer it to Akṣobhya Buddha.
> Prostrate the entire body on the ground with heartfelt homage.
> The vajra añjali extended above the crown.

Mantra:

*Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-pūja-upasthānāya ātmānaṃ niryātayāmi sarva-tathāgata-vajra-sattva adhiṣṭha sarva-thāṃ*

> Through this mantra and body-seal,
> One attains the perfected bodhicitta.
> Next pay homage to Ratnasambhava,
> For the offering of abhiṣeka.
> The vajra añjali placed below the heart,
> Touch the forehead to the ground as an offering.

Mantra:

*Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-pūja-abhiṣekāya ātmānaṃ niryātayāmi sarva-tathāgata-vajra-ratna-abhiṣiñca sarva-thāṃ*

> Through this wondrous offering of the body,
> One will soon become the lord of the three realms.
> To seek the offering of turning the Dharma wheel,
> Next pay homage to Amitāyus.
> The vajra añjali placed upon the crown,
> Touch the mouth to the ground, offering the body.

Mantra:

*Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-pūja-pravartanāya ātmānaṃ niryātayāmi sarva-tathāgata-vajra-dharma-pravartāya thāṃ*

> Through this sincere offering of the body,
> One will join the world-saviors in turning the Dharma wheel.
> Next pay homage to Amoghasiddhi,
> For the offering of karmic action.
> The vajra añjali at the heart,
> Touch the crown to the ground as an offering.

Mantra:

*Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-pūja-karmaṇi ātmānaṃ niryātayāmi sarva-tathāgata-vajra-karmaṇi kuruḥ thāṃ*

> Through this skillful offering of the body,
> One can manifest all kinds of bodies.
> Next, before the buddha-ocean,
> Join palms and kneel to confess all faults:
> "Throughout beginningless saṃsāra in all existences,
> Transgressions born of body, speech, and mind:
> Just as all buddhas and bodhisattvas have confessed,
> I now likewise make confession.
> Furthermore, arouse deep joy of heart
> And rejoice in all accumulations of merit and wisdom:
> In the practices and vows of buddhas and bodhisattvas,
> Merit born from the three vajra karmas,
> And the wholesome roots gathered by pratyekabuddhas,
> Śrāvakas, and all sentient beings, I fully rejoice.
> Contemplate all buddhas seated beneath the bodhi tree;
> In person, I entreat each to turn the Dharma wheel.
> All the world-lamps seated at the bodhimaṇḍa,
> Whose eyes of awakening open to illuminate the three existences:
> I kneel before all and first entreat them
> To turn the unsurpassed wondrous Dharma wheel.
> I also entreat all world-honored ones
> Not to enter nirvāṇa but to remain always in the world.
> All tathāgatas, lords of the three realms,
> Who are about to enter final nirvāṇa without remainder:
> I entreat all to remain forever,
> Not forsaking the vow of compassion to rescue the world.
> The merit of confession, rejoicing, and entreaty:
> May I never lose the bodhicitta.
> Among the wondrous assemblies of buddhas and bodhisattvas,
> May I always be a good support, never wearied or abandoned.
> Free from the eight difficulties, born where there is no difficulty,
> With past-life knowledge, abiding in wisdom, adorned in body,
> Far from delusion, endowed with compassion and wisdom,
> Fully perfecting all pāramitās.
> Wealthy, abundant, born in a noble lineage,
> With a vast and flourishing retinue,
> The four unhindered knowledges, ten sovereignties,
> Six supernatural powers and all dhyānas perfected:
> Like Vajraketu and Samantabhadra,
> May my praise and dedication be likewise."
> The practitioner next cultivates samādhi.
> In full lotus, upright body, entering right absorption,
> The four immeasurables pervading the dharmadhātu,
> Cultivated and applied according to the Dharma teaching.
> Enter the Samantabhadra samaya:
> Because the essence is identical to Sattvavajra,
> Concentration and wisdom in harmony, the vajra-bandha.
> The patience-vow fingers raised like a banner.
> Upon reciting the original-vow seal mantra,
> The body abides in a moon-disk, identical to the Sattva.

Mantra:

*Oṃ samayaḥ stvaṃ*

> Next form the supreme-joy samādhi seal,
> To delight all the holy ones with this bliss.
> The patience-vow fingers enter the full-moon palm.
> The dhyāna-jñāna and dāna-prajñā fingers both extended and parallel.

Mantra:

*Oṃ samayaḥ hūṃ surata stvaṃ*

> Through this wondrous seal and mantra,
> All sacred assemblies are filled with joy.
> Next, open the heart and enter the Buddha's wisdom.
> Visualize the syllable *trāṭ* above the breast.
> Pull apart the vajra-bandha before the heart;
> The two syllables turning like hinges opening a fan.

Mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-bandha trāṭ*

> An eight-petaled white lotus opens to one cubit,
> Brightly manifesting the syllable *a* in pure white light.
> The dhyāna-jñāna fingers both enter the vajra-bandha.
> Summon and draw in the Tathāgata's quiescent wisdom.

Mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-viśa aḥ*

> Next form the Tathāgata's firm fist.
> The vigor fingers bent, bracing the dhyāna-jñāna fingers' backs.
> Through correspondence with this wondrous seal,
> One firmly upholds all the buddhas' wisdom.

Mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-muṣṭi thāṃ*

> Next, with wrathful Trailokyavijaya,
> Purify and remove all inner and outer obstacles.
> Both hands crossed at the arms in vajra fists,
> The dāna-prajñā fingers hooked, the vigor fingers standing.
> The practitioner visualizes the body blazing with fierce flames,
> Eight-armed, four-faced, with sharp upright fangs.
> Thundering the syllable *hūṃ* like the sound of thunder,
> Revolving clockwise above the crown to seal the boundary.

Mantra:

*Oṃ sumbha niḥsumbha hūṃ gṛhṇa gṛhṇa hūṃ gṛhṇa apāya hūṃ ānāya hoḥ bhagavan vajra hūṃ phaṭ*

> Next form the lotus samaya,
> To bring about the accomplishment of samādhi.
> The concentration and wisdom hands in vajra-bandha,
> The dāna-prajñā and dhyāna-jñāna fingers harmoniously upright.
> Through this mantra and secret seal,
> The cultivation of samādhi swiftly manifests.

Mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-padma-samayaḥ stvaṃ*

> The practitioner, wishing to enter vajra concentration,
> First abides in the investigative wisdom seal.
> The concentration and wisdom hands facing up, interlaced.
> The vigor-dhyāna and strength-jñāna fingers each bracing the other.
> Cultivating meditative equipoise with this wondrous seal,
> One attains the Tathāgata's immovable wisdom.

The practitioner should next cultivate the ānāpānaka samādhi. Sit upright in proper posture. The body should not sway. The tongue touches the upper palate. Stop the incoming and outgoing breath, making it extremely subtle. Contemplate that all dharmas arise from one's own mind. All afflictions and subsidiary afflictions, the aggregates, elements, and sense-fields are all like illusions, mirages, a gandharva city, a whirling fire-ring, or an echo in an empty valley. Having contemplated thus, one does not see body or mind. Abide in quiescent extinction, signless equality, taking this as the ultimate true wisdom.

At that time, contemplate innumerable buddhas in empty space, as numerous as sesame seeds filling the great earth. All extend golden arms and snap their fingers in warning, saying: "Good man! The place you have reached is the one path of purity, but you have not yet realized the vajra yoga samādhi and sarvajña wisdom. Do not be content with this. You should fulfill the practice of Samantabhadra and achieve the most perfect enlightenment."

> Having heard the warning, the practitioner
> In samādhi universally prostrates at their feet:
> "May all tathāgatas
> Show me the path to follow."
> All the buddhas speak in unison:
> "You should observe your own mind."
> Having heard this teaching,
> One observes one's own mind as instructed.
> After long and careful observation,
> One does not see the form of one's own mind.
> Again visualizing prostration at the buddhas' feet,
> One says: "Most Victorious One!
> I do not see my own mind.
> What is the form of this mind?"
> All the buddhas declare:
> "The form of the mind is difficult to fathom.
> We bestow the heart mantra;
> Contemplate the mind according to principle."

*Oṃ citta-prativediṃ karomi*

> In an instant one sees the mind,
> Full and perfect like a pure moon.
> Again one reflects:
> "What is this mind?
> It is the seed of affliction and habitual tendencies;
> Both good and evil arise from the mind.
> The mind is the ālaya consciousness;
> Purifying it serves as the cause.
> Through the six pāramitās' cultivation,
> That mind becomes the great mind.
> The storehouse consciousness is fundamentally unstained,
> Pure and without blemish.
> Long ages of accumulated merit and wisdom
> Are like the full and pure moon.
> Without substance and without function,
> What is spoken of is also not the moon."
> Because of possessing merit and wisdom,
> One's own mind is like the full moon.
> Leaping with joy in the heart,
> One again addresses the world-honored ones:
> "I have seen my own mind,
> Pure as the full moon,
> Free from all afflictive defilements,
> Free from subject and object and their like."
> All the buddhas declare:
> "Your mind has always been thus.
> It was obscured by adventitious dust;
> The bodhicitta is what purifies it.
> You have contemplated the pure moon-disk
> And realized the bodhicitta.
> We bestow this heart mantra:
> Recite it secretly and contemplate."

*Oṃ bodhicittam utpādayāmi*

> "This can cause the mind's moon-disk
> To become perfectly full and ever more radiant."
> All the buddhas again declare:
> "Bodhi is firm and indestructible.
> Because one abides well in firmness,
> We again bestow the heart mantra:

*Oṃ tiṣṭha vajra*

> "Upon the pure moon-disk,
> Contemplate the five-wisdom vajra.
> Cause it to pervade the dharmadhātu:
> There is only one great vajra.
> You should know that your own body
> Is itself the Vajradhātu.

*Oṃ vajrātmako 'haṃ*

> "The self-body is vajra,
> Firm, solid, and indestructible."
> Again one addresses all the buddhas:
> "I have become the vajra body."
> Then all those tathāgatas
> Instruct the practitioner:
> "Contemplate the body as the Buddha's form."
> And bestow this mantra:

*Oṃ yathā sarva-tathāgatāḥ tathā 'haṃ*

> Having realized the mind's purity,
> One sees one's own body as the Buddha,
> All marks perfectly complete,
> Immediately realizing sarvajña.
> In samādhi, universally prostrating to all buddhas,
> Requesting firm empowerment.
> All the buddhas, hearing
> The words of the Vajradhātu,
> All enter into the vajra
> And then speak the vajra heart:

*Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-abhisaṃbodhi niryāṭa vajra tiṣṭha*

> All the buddhas of great renown,
> Having just spoken this vidyā,
> The equally enlightened Vajradhātu
> Realizes the true wisdom.
> Then all those tathāgatas,
> Having conferred the firm empowerment,
> Emerge from the vajra
> And universally abide in empty space.
> The practitioner reflects:
> "Having realized the vajra samādhi,
> I possess sarvajña;
> I have achieved perfect enlightenment."
> To cause entry into the buddha stage,
> One should form the vajra samaya.
> The ten fingers fully interlaced outwardly,
> The patience-vow fingers upright like a banner.
> Seal the heart, forehead, throat, and crown,
> Reciting once at each to empower.

Mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-sattvādhiṣṭha sarva-thāṃ*

> Then visualize all tathāgatas in empty space
> Holding the ākāśa jewel to anoint one's crown.
> Concentration and wisdom in harmony, the vajra-bandha.
> The vigor and strength, the dhyāna and jñāna fingers like a jewel shape.
> Having empowered the seal at the forehead,
> The crown of the five buddha wisdoms rests upon the head.
> Then split the jñāna fists and circle behind the crown:
> Know that the stainless silk ribbon has been tied.

Mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-ratna abhiṣiñca thāṃ sarva-mudrā me niryāṭi kuru varaṇa-vacena thāṃ*

The practitioner should then reflect: "I have now achieved perfect enlightenment. I should arouse the great mind of compassion toward all sentient beings. Throughout endless saṃsāra, I shall always don the armor of the great vow of adornment. In order to purify buddha-lands, mature sentient beings, and serve all tathāgatas, I shall enable all sentient beings to sit beneath the bodhi tree, defeat the celestial māras, and achieve the most perfect enlightenment. Therefore, I should don the armor of compassion of the tathāgatas of the three times."

> Having tied the garland seal behind the crown,
> Extend it forward, stretching the vigor fingers.
> The two *oṃ* points wind and encircle each other,
> Unbroken green light like binding armor.
> Heart, back, navel, waist, both knees,
> Throat, crown, forehead, and back of the neck:
> At each, the vigor fingers revolve three times.
> Scatter the palms forward and down, trailing celestial garments.
> This universally protects all sentient beings;
> No celestial māra can destroy it.

Mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-kavaca vajra-kuru vajra vajra-haṃ*

> Next form the joy seal.
> The concentration and wisdom hands clap three times.
> Through the finger-seal empowerment,
> All sacred assemblies are filled with joy.

Mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-tuṣya hoḥ*

The practitioner should next, with the accomplishing wisdom samādhi, visualize before oneself an inexhaustible milky ocean giving birth to a great lotus king with a vajra stem, pervading the dharmadhātu in size. Above it, visualize a pavilion of seven precious wonders adorned with wish-fulfilling jewels, with clouds of flowers, oceans of incense, music, and song. Within the jeweled pavilion, upon a lion throne, in a pure full moon, a wondrous white lotus appears. Contemplate the syllable *thāṃ* gate, radiating great light universally illuminating the dharmadhātu, becoming Vairocana Tathāgata. His body is the color of the full moon, wearing the crown of the five tathāgatas, with draped silk gauze, celestial garments and jeweled necklaces adorning the body, light universally illuminating. Innumerable great bodhisattvas surround him as his retinue. The practitioner, wishing to cause all tathāgatas to assemble, next uses the samādhi of Vajrarāja Bodhisattva to summon the sacred hosts.

> Concentration and wisdom in vajra fists,
> Arms crossed embracing the chest, vigor fingers bent,
> Snapping fingers with sound pervading all worlds,
> Contemplate the buddha-ocean universally gathering like clouds.

Mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-samāja jaḥ*

> Next form the great seal of the vajra hook,
> The summoning wisdom of all tathāgatas.
> Concentration and wisdom in outward interlacing,
> The vigor finger alone curved three times like a hook.

Mantra:

*Oṃ āḥ hye jaḥ*

> Next form the great seal of the vajra lasso,
> Drawing the honored one's body into the wisdom essence.
> From the previous seal, the dhyāna finger enters the concentration palm.
> The strength and jñāna fingers pinched together in a ring shape.

Mantra:

*Oṃ āḥ hye hūṃ hūṃ*

> Next form the vajra-hook-chain seal,
> Causing the honored one to abide firmly.
> The dhyāna-jñāna and vigor-strength fingers hooked together:
> This is named the vajra restraining seal.

Mantra:

*Oṃ hye sabhūṭa thāṃ*

> Next form the wondrous vajra-bell seal,
> Causing all holy ones to rejoice.
> The dhyāna-jñāna fingers bent into the vajra-bandha:
> This is named the vajra joy seal.

Mantra:

*Oṃ ghaṇṭā aḥ aḥ*

> Next enter the equality-nature wisdom samādhi,
> Offering arghya water of many fragrances,
> Visualizing bathing the stainless bodies of all holy ones.
> One will attain the dharmamegha-bhūmi of abhiṣeka.

Mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-arghaṃ hūṃ*

> Next, with the vajra-dharma hymn of praise,
> Extol the merit and wisdom of all tathāgatas.
> Contemplate the marks and signs, uttering a pure voice
> To accord with the principle of true tathatā nature.

Mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-sattva-saṅgraha vajra-ratna-manustuṣya vajra-dharma-gāyana vajra-karma-kuru bhava*

> Next form the vajra-play seal
> To accomplish the Tathāgata's inner retinue.
> Concentration and wisdom in harmony, the vajra-bandha.
> The dhyāna-jñāna fingers standing upright before the heart.

Mantra:

*Oṃ mahā-rati*

> Through the play-offering,
> One will soon realize the vajra samādhi.
> Next form the vajra-garland seal.
> Contemplate wondrous garland-clouds pervading the dharmadhātu.
> Without changing the previous seal, hold it forward.
> Visualize offering a precious garland to adorn the head.

Mantra:

*Oṃ rūpa-śobhe*

> Through the vajra-garland offering,
> One will receive the abhiṣeka Dharma-King position.
> Next form the vajra-song seal,
> Praising the Buddha's wisdom with wondrous sound.
> The previous seal moves from the navel to the mouth, then scatters.
> Wondrous music delights the sacred assembly.

Mantra:

*Oṃ śruta-trā saukhye*

> Through the vajra-song offering,
> One will soon possess the Tathāgata's eloquence.
> Next form the wondrous vajra-dance seal.
> Contemplate wondrous music-clouds as universal offering.
> At the heart, concentration and wisdom each revolve in dance.
> The vajra añjali is placed upon the crown.

Mantra:

*Oṃ sarva-pūraye*

> Through the wondrous dance offering,
> One will attain the Tathāgata's mind-born body.
> Next form the burning-incense outer offering.
> With this, universally perfume the buddha-ocean assembly.
> The harmonious vajra hands without scattering.
> Visualize wondrous incense-clouds pervading the dharmadhātu.

Mantra:

*Oṃ prahladani ni*

> Through the burning-incense offering,
> One attains the Tathāgata's unhindered wisdom.
> Next form the vajra flower-scattering seal
> To adorn all worlds with this.
> The bound seal scatters upward like offering flowers.
> Fragrant flower-clouds pervade the dharmadhātu.

Mantra:

*Oṃ phalagami*

> Through the vajra-flower offering,
> One swiftly realizes the Tathāgata's thirty-two marks.
> Next, with the vajra-lamp seal,
> Universally illumine the buddha assembly with brilliant light.
> The dhyāna-jñāna fingers pressed forward in the vajra-bandha.
> Maṇi lamp-light illuminates the dharmadhātu.

Mantra:

*Oṃ sutija-gri*

> Through this vajra-lamp offering,
> One swiftly attains the Tathāgata's five purified eyes.
> Next form the vajra-unguent seal
> To offer to all buddha assemblies.
> Scatter the vajra-bandha as if applying unguent.
> The fragrance flows throughout the ten direction realms.

Mantra:

*Oṃ sugandha-aṅgī*

> Through the vajra-unguent seal,
> One attains the five-part dharmakāya wisdom.
> Having thus broadly performed all buddha activities,
> Next apply the mind to mantra recitation.
> First, single-pointedly contemplate the honored one.
> The four vidyā draw the honored one into one's body.
> Know that body and honored one are nondual;
> Form, appearance, and dignified bearing are all equal.
> The retinue of the assembly surrounds oneself.
> Abide in the great mirror wisdom of perfect quiescence.
> Concentration and wisdom in the vajra-bandha.
> The patience-vow fingers like a blade, vigor fingers bracing.
> First recite the vajra hundred-syllable vidyā
> To ensure the empowerment remains unshaken.

Mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-sattva samayam anupalaya vajra-sattva tvenopatisṭha dṛḍho me bhava sutoṣyo me bhava anurakto me bhava supoṣyo me bhava sarva-siddhiṃ me prayaccha sarva-karmasu ca me cittaśrīyaḥ kuru hūṃ ha ha ha ha hoḥ bhagavan sarva-tathāgata-vajra mā me muñca vajrī bhava mahā-samaya-sattva āḥ*

Through the empowerment of this Mahāyāna hundred-syllable mantra, even one who has committed the five unpardonable sins or slandered all buddhas and the Mahāyāna sūtras, if a mantra practitioner causes the honored one to abide firmly within the body, will in this present life obtain all siddhi sought, namely the supreme siddhi, the Vajrasattva siddhi, and even the Tathāgata's supreme siddhi. Without changing the great seal of the Vajradhātu, recite the honored one's root vidyā mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-dhātu thāṃ*

> With both concentration and wisdom hands, hold up the rosary.
> Empower with the root mantra seven times,
> Hold it to the crown, then place it at the heart.
> Firmly abide in meditative equipoise and recite.
> The tip of the tongue moves slightly, lips and teeth closed.
> Forward and backward, circulate around the body contemplating the marks.
> Practice diligently at all four periods without interruption.
> A thousand or a hundred as the minimum, or exceeding this.
> All supernatural powers, merit, and wisdom:
> In this very life, one becomes equal to the All-Illuminating Lord.

When the practitioner has completed the designated recitation, hold the rosary above the crown and diligently make great vows. Then form the samādhi seal, enter the dharmadhātu essential nature samādhi, and cultivate the five-syllable revolving dhāraṇī.

> All dharmas are fundamentally unborn;
> Their own nature is beyond words.
> Pure and free from all defilement,
> Causes and karmic actions are equal to empty space.
> Revolving, carefully contemplate:
> Syllable by syllable, awaken to the truth.
> Though first and last differ,
> What is realized all returns to one.
> Do not abandon this samādhi;
> Also abide in unconditioned compassion.
> May all sentient beings universally
> Become like me, without any difference.

When the practitioner emerges from samādhi, form the root seal and recite the root vidyā seven times.

Then make the eight great offerings to all buddhas. With wondrous words, extol and praise. Offer arghya water. With the Trailokyavijaya seal, revolve leftward to release the boundary. Then form the vajra liberation seal and escort all holy ones back to their original realms. The seal is the previously described samaya seal; the patience-vow fingers support the flower to the crown and scatter. Mantra:

*Oṃ kṛtavā sarva-sattva-arthā-siddhi-nirdatāya thānugacchata svī bhaṃ buddha-viṣayaṃ punarāgamanāya tu oṃ vajra-sattva mūḥ*

Having performed this ritual, again form the samaya seal and recite the empowering vidyā. Seal the four places and then perform the abhiṣeka. Don the vajra armor. As before, perform the four prostrations to the buddhas of the four directions, confession, aspiration, and so forth. Then, in a quiet place adorned with incense and flowers, abide in the honored one's samādhi and read Mahāyāna vaipulya sūtras, walking in meditation as one wishes.

> If any sentient being encounters this teaching
> And practices diligently day and night in all four periods,
> In this very life they will realize the Stage of Joy,
> And after sixteen lifetimes achieve perfect enlightenment.
