# 佛說持明藏八大總持王經
## The Buddha's Teaching: Sūtra of the Eight Great Dhāraṇī Kings of the Vidyādhara Treasury

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1370

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dānapāla (施護).

---

At that time the World-Honored One, for the sake of sentient beings of the final age, proclaimed the Sūtra of the Eight Great Dhāraṇī Kings of the Vidyādhara Treasury, so that in the future, the practitioners among the fourfold assembly might bring benefit to all sentient beings and attain accomplishment in seeking the various siddhis.

The Buddha said: "If any practitioner arouses a brave and resolute mind, firm and without regression, and cultivates these eight great dhāraṇīs, they must first purify their body and mind, making themselves clean inside and out, continuing without interruption until accomplishment is attained. If they stop even briefly, they will not attain accomplishment. When one wishes to recite and practice, before each recitation one should first call upon and pay homage to all the buddhas, the Dharma, and the holy assembly. Having thus taken refuge in the Three Treasures, one should then recite with a devout mind." He then spoke the dhāraṇī:

*Namo ati-vati-nāti ṭhake chaṭarake ṭhake uru-materu-rumate turu-hile mile sarva-jana-vipadanaga. Namaḥ sarva-sama-saṃbuddhanāṃ. Siddhantu mantra-padāni svāhā.*

"This dhāraṇī was proclaimed by immeasurable samyak-saṃbuddha world-honored ones of the past. If one wishes to practice for the purpose of pacifying calamities, increasing benefit, and providing protection for sentient beings, one must first recite it until thoroughly proficient. Having completed the preliminary practice and gained great power, one may then act on behalf of sentient beings. This dhāraṇī method should be practiced during mid-autumn, when it has great power. If performed at that time, the recitation will easily succeed, and it can eliminate the eight great terrors. Furthermore, if sentient beings constantly recite it, they will always be protected, will be at peace and happy day and night, all inauspicious things will be far removed, their merit will increase, and they will be free from suffering and affliction. Whether in the royal capital or encountering war, if one wishes to escape from various dangers, one should choose an auspicious morning, recite and perform the rite at noon, and one will attain liberation. If one is possessed by devas, nāgas, ghosts, or spirits, the possession can also be removed. By the power of recitation, even a withered tree can be made to bloom and bear fruit again. Moreover, the practitioner who recites it will attain the knowledge of past lives, knowing the events of immeasurable lifetimes."

He further spoke a dhāraṇī:

*Puku-jvale puku-jvale samyati mahā-samanta mahā-jvale ti-huke muke huke muke mahā-śaśṇi-rā-mitāya śaśi-śatu mahā-jvale jvalati siddhi-ccā saṃ-ca maraṇī-ṇa svāhā.*

"This dhāraṇī was proclaimed by the child Cakra-saṃsāra-artha-siddhi. It should be recited during the tenth month for the benefit of sentient beings, and one will obtain immeasurable pure karmic rewards."

He further spoke a dhāraṇī:

*Sunati suvena-nāci unāci nairici bhajani-dha mālī-ccā mana-ci-vāci ta-puruna ṭa-vaṭani-nāti eka-ccā-ra bōdhi sai-ceṇa rākha-siddhi-ccāne hī-śāne vi svāhā.*

"This dhāraṇī was proclaimed by pratyekabuddhas. It should be recited during winter in a clean place, and it can protect all sentient beings."

He further spoke a dhāraṇī:

*Tara-ciru-ru-ciru veru-veira ṭhake hile mile tire śire nāti-vāti ku-nāti anā-mati manā-mati rule ka-sa-ka-si-ka-sa śira-rura madha u-māya nipha-sa-phā-śe. Oṃ vibhavati svāhā.*

"This dhāraṇī was proclaimed by koṭi arhats and can protect all sentient beings, freeing them from all terrors. This method should be recited during spring for pacifying calamities and increasing benefit, and accomplishment will certainly be attained. If one follows the method without error and does not regress in one's practice, one can become invisible, unseen by anyone."

He further spoke the heart-dhāraṇī:

*Oṃ an-ta-nda-vami vibhava-su svāhā.*

"With this heart-vidyā, additionally naming the matter one seeks, recite nine lakṣas. Whatever one seeks will certainly be accomplished. When the count is complete, prepare various incenses and flowers, light lamps, and make offerings to the Buddha, Dharma, Saṃgha, bodhisattvas, pratyekabuddhas, and śrāvakas. If one begs for food, one should take the bowl and walk north to the village, begging from house to house in order, with seven houses as the limit, not skipping any. The merit of what those seven households give will extend to seven clans. For the flowers used in offering, one should seek kāravīra flowers and nāga flowers, as these are the most supreme."

He further spoke a dhāraṇī:

*Svāni svāni svāna-mukeru-bhami saṃ-hi mahā-ka-pa-ne. Oṃ cire mire. Oṃ ka-i-ti vini-guha hūṃ ccā-nāya svāhā.*

"This dhāraṇī was proclaimed by stream-enterers. This method should be recited during summer in pursuit of accomplishment. The place for performing the rite should be a charnel ground, between two rivers, or established on a bridge. If performing protection for another person, first have a pure maiden spin thread and string beads while reciting one lakṣa. What is sought will be accomplished. Or take blood from one's own left side, dip a white lotus in it as a homa offering, and recite eight hundred times. Whatever is sought will be obtained. Or take the left eyes of a black cat, a dead pig, and a dead person, grind and combine them together, mix with three metals to form pills, and take them together with the juice of the sāla tree. What is sought will certainly be accomplished."

He further spoke a dhāraṇī:

*Akā-śi-kāśi nā-kāśi gaṇa-kā-la-śi praṇadanta praṇadā-vatva. Oṃ vidha-mahā-vidha-śi nā-bhita-nā-bhi ruru-mihi-cire mile a-śau-ta-śau-ṇa-riya-mi-tu-ya. Namaḥ sarva-buddha-saṃbodhi-saṃbhavānāṃ svāhā.*

"This dhāraṇī was proclaimed by Aparājita Bodhisattva. If anyone recites it with a devoted mind for one full year, they will certainly attain surpassing physical beauty and strength, auspicious peace and happiness, perfect merit, and their fame will be heard far and wide."

He further spoke a dhāraṇī:

*Namaḥ ratna-trayāya. Namo nāgami-bhina. Namo rākuśa-variye. Namo dhātubhiṣyākaye. Ye anābhi-vijani kuku u-buddha-khāga-cakra svāhā.*

"This dhāraṇī was proclaimed by anāgāmins and can cure all illness and suffering. If there is a sick person, the practitioner should take their hand and recite this mantra to empower milk, which the patient should drink and use to wash their body. Even one on the verge of death can be cured. Or empower manaḥśilā medicine and place it in the sick person's ear. This too can effect a cure. If a practitioner who has completed the preliminary practice wishes to seek the siddhi, they should take a rosary or wheel and simply recite according to the method. Even if the practitioner has no merit of their own, by the power of the method they will still attain accomplishment."

He further spoke a dhāraṇī:

*Pasanni kārari pasanni makāri pasanni makāri pasanni takāri takāri pasanni m��rikāri pasanni rurāri pasanni pasannā-kāri-te. Namaḥ ācārya-padānāṃ. Oṃ pasanni-surūmike svāhā.*

"This dhāraṇī was proclaimed in the past when I was a bodhisattva named Sumantra Vidyādhara Deva. Manifesting in animal form, I spoke this dhāraṇī for the benefit of beings. If anyone devoutly receives and upholds it, all their wishes will be fulfilled. If any sentient being who has committed various evil deeds is bound to fall into the animal realm, by the majestic power of this dhāraṇī their former sins will be eliminated and they will certainly be reborn among humans or devas. This dhāraṇī can accomplish all manner of undertakings.

"If any person wishes to attain accomplishment in various undertakings, they must first complete the preliminary practice to perfection. Then, taking the object whose accomplishment is sought, they should recite before the great vidyārāja either three times or six times daily, with one thousand recitations per session as the standard, without any deficiency, continuing until the siddhi is attained. Once the siddhi is attained, the work will be perfectly accomplished.

"If a person wishes to subdue an enemy, they should sit facing south, draw the enemy's head and face with their hand, press it with the hand while reciting the mantra, and the enemy will naturally submit. If a person recites three times daily without fail, all people will respect and defer to them. If a person faces south and performs a homa with black sesame, whatever the heart desires will be obtained in every case.

"If one places a bowl on the ground and presses the hand upon it while empowering it, one will certainly receive abundant alms. If one empowers ghee and eats it, one will never suffer from malaria and will always feel light and at ease. If a woman has no breast milk, she should empower ghee and salt and eat them, and she will produce milk. If a woman has no children, she should empower sandalwood flower powder and apply it to her forehead three times daily, or tie medicine around her neck or arm, and she will certainly bear a blessed child. If a woman has a difficult temperament, her husband should recite and empower her, and her nature will become gentle and good. If a husband has a difficult temperament, his wife should recite the mantra, mix sandalwood flower powder, and have him eat it, and they will respect and value each other.

"If a person is crossing water and reaches a deep place in the middle, their physical strength failing and fearing they may drown, if their mind recalls the mantra, they will immediately reach shallow water. If one empowers a withered tree, it will bloom and put forth leaves again. If one empowers a dried-up pond, water will naturally fill it to the brim."

He further spoke a dhāraṇī:

*Sati akāti vikāti puraṇi agati-samahiti-siddhi-sa-santi saṃ-padanāṃ. Namo-stu ācāri-anāṃ.*

"This dhāraṇī was proclaimed in the past when I was the greatly wrathful Vidyārāja. If a person constantly receives and upholds it, they will attain auspicious and supreme accomplishments. If a person who has completed the preliminary practice takes aśvattha flowers and viḍaṅga fruit-kernel powder, mixes them with ghee and honey, empowers milk and takes it together, and then recites for seven days and nights, they will attain great intelligence.

"If one takes the eyes of a tiger, fox, cat, and pig, grinds them to powder in equal portions, combines and forms pills, empowers them, and places them in the nose, one will become invisible. If one takes slightly bitter root medicine, mixes it with milk and honey, empowers it, and eats it, one's appearance will not age.

"If in a charnel ground, beneath a solitary tree, or at a crossroads, one fasts and recites, and if accomplishment is attained, one will be able to walk and stand in the sky and may also travel through the desire and form heavenly realms. If one attains the sword-siddhi, that is the most supreme.

"If on the seashore or a great riverbank one constantly eats milk or meat broth while reciting for ten months, with daily correspondence of body and mind without regression, one will certainly obtain rare treasures from the sea or river. If on a mountain where nāgas dwell one constantly eats mung beans and ghee while reciting, the nāgas will bring pearls and gold. If beneath an udumbara tree one recites without interruption while begging for food, and the siddhi is attained, empowered ghee will instantly turn to water. Drinking it, one will understand all manner of worldly methods.

"If one fashions a lion from clay and empowers it, and the siddhi is attained, one may ride it to the heavenly realms. For each of these methods, if one practices them one by one and seeks accomplishment, having completed the preliminary practice to perfection, one should further recite eight thousand times according to the method, and the siddhi will certainly be attained."

At that time, when the World-Honored One had spoken the Eight Great Dhāraṇī Kings of the Vidyādhara Treasury, he addressed Ānanda: "This secret method you should well receive and uphold, so that in the future it may be transmitted without interruption, enabling practitioners to bring great benefit to all sentient beings." Then Ānanda, having received the Buddha's command, leapt for joy, bowed in reverence, and withdrew.
