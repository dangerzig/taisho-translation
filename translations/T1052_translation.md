# 讚觀世音菩薩頌
## Hymn in Praise of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1052

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Huìzhì (慧智).

---

> The bodhisattva known as Avalokiteśvara,
> Whose spiritual powers, unobstructed, are beyond measure,
> Who shakes mountains, dries seas, and quakes the great earth,
> Who with compassion regards all beings as one body.
> Recollecting his name, the merit is not in vain;
> Therefore one should always invoke him.
> I now with utmost sincerity recall his virtues,
> And with this reverent mind offer praise.

> The bodhisattva's wisdom is deep as the sea,
> His skillful means and expedients beyond all fathoming.
> All the devas, asuras,
> Mahoragas, and human assemblies
> With subtle and wondrous pure verses
> Praise him through kalpas without weariness.
> I now proclaim this Hymn of Springtime Radiance,
> Describing but a small portion of his merits.

> Of all noble and beautiful forms in the world,
> Among the devas none surpasses Great Brahmā;
> Yet compared to the Holy Sovereign's true form,
> Not even a hundred-thousand-ten-thousandth part can match it.

> The Honored One's head adornments are supremely fine,
> Crowned with mandāra flowers and gold blossoms;
> The rainbow's beauty serves as ornament,
> Like a half-moon reflected on the mountain king,
> Or a Mount Sumeru fashioned of a hundred jewels.

> The Honored One's bodily marks are most wondrous,
> Like gentle rain veiling a jeweled peak.
> An eṇeya deerskin drapes his shoulder,
> His radiance brilliantly pervades all directions,
> Towering and magnificent like a golden mountain.

> Like the full moon dwelling in the sky,
> Or the color of the campaka flower,
> He surpasses even the body of Maheśvara,
> Who merely wears white nāgas as ornaments.

> In his right hand he holds a golden lotus,
> Its stem formed of vaiḍūrya jewels.
> With compassion it opens, supremely fragrant and pure,
> Delighting all the wise with love and joy.

> The holy flower the Honored One holds
> Is so adorned and wondrously resplendent,
> Like moonlight gleaming atop Mount Sumeru.

> The lotus platform on which the holy body sits,
> Arisen from the Honored One's merit:
> With utmost sincerity and earnest mind
> I bow in homage to the Wish-Granter,
> Praised and worshipped by all the devas.
> I now with a pure mind bow in homage.

> The Honored One's spiral topknot is the color of dark bees;
> The wondrous image of the Muni dwells therein.
> Its radiance illumines and adorns the Honored One's head,
> Like burning coral with the flash of lightning,
> Or like orpiment blazing upon a dark mountain.

> Above and below, adorning the Great Sage's body,
> I boldly compose this text in praise.

> When raging elephants with sharp tusks on four sides
> Threaten to crush a person caught among them,
> If that person single-mindedly invokes the bodhisattva,
> They leave behind suffering and have no fear.

> When a violent dragon roars and spews smoke and venom,
> Snarling with furious rage and a terrible visage,
> About to devour a person in dreadful terror,
> By recalling Avalokiteśvara one is delivered.

> When ocean tides and wind-driven waves raise their roar,
> And makara great fish are all set churning,
> If a person's boat breaks and they fall amid the waters,
> By invoking Avalokiteśvara they gain safety.

> In high mountains, perilous valleys, and cliff faces,
> With rushing torrents splashing on rocks and many dangers,
> If a person falls in with no one to rescue them,
> By invoking Avalokiteśvara in the mind, they reach the other shore.

> When fierce flames with violent wind arise,
> Spreading to burn dwellings and engulf a person,
> By wholeheartedly invoking the Treasury of Merit,
> The fire will cause no harm in that moment.

> If a person is bound and imprisoned,
> With cangue, fetters, manacles, and shackles upon their body,
> By wholeheartedly invoking Avalokiteśvara,
> That person is swiftly freed.

> If a person falls into a great pit of fire,
> By single-mindedly calling the name of Avalokiteśvara,
> The heat is removed and purity obtained,
> Like a lotus blossoming in a pond.

> If a person passes through a great forest
> And suddenly encounters thunderbolts and torrential rain,
> When lightning is about to strike in extreme peril,
> By invoking Avalokiteśvara one is spared the disaster.

> If a person encounters a fierce lion
> With teeth and claws sharp as knives and swords,
> Eyes blazing, roaring as it comes to devour,
> By invoking Avalokiteśvara one is freed from harm.

> When evil bandits, Turks, or mlecchas,
> Ferocious, merciless as rākṣasas,
> Bind a person with iron chains,
> By invoking and recalling the name, all bonds are loosed.

> In deep mountains, great marshes, and perilous places,
> When brigands lie in wait to rob and seize,
> By invoking the name and the power of recollection, praying for rescue,
> That person is at ease, free from distress.

> When spells, slanders, and false accusations aim to harm,
> And enemies constantly seek means with evil intent,
> Because one always invokes Avalokiteśvara,
> They ultimately cannot cause any harm.

> When battle drums and banners face off on both sides,
> With weapons crossing and blades competing,
> If at that moment one invokes and calls the name,
> The bodhisattva bestows fearlessness in the midst of it.

> When powerful evil spirits seize a person,
> When kumbhāṇḍas and others drink a person's marrow,
> If one can recollect and invoke Avalokiteśvara,
> He protects that person and dispels the plague.

> Ghost-sickness, fever, bloating,
> Wasting disease, jaundice, heart pain,
> Leprosy, epilepsy, cholera,
> Hemorrhages, emaciation, and all manner of diseases,
> When evil dark karma entwines one's body,
> By invoking the name and holding it in mind, all are healed.

> When hungry ghosts with bellies like mountains,
> Lips and mouths parched, tormented by hunger and thirst,
> The bodhisattva, seeing them, gives rise to compassion
> And one by one causes them to be satisfied.

> When hungry ghosts are afflicted by bitter cold,
> Their skin and flesh broken and ruined,
> Raising both hands in extreme suffering,
> Through spiritual powers and skillful means he warms them.

> When garuḍa birds with beak and claw
> Snatch up nāgas on water and land,
> If those nāgas single-mindedly invoke the bodhisattva,
> They are freed from suffering and obtain peace and joy.

> If one wishes to obtain superior rewards,
> Elephants, horses, carriages, servants,
> Clothing, food, drink, and precious treasures,
> Because one always invokes Avalokiteśvara,
> All wishes naturally come to fulfillment.

> If one desires the art of longevity,
> To sit and rest among the clouds, roaming freely through space,
> Because one always invokes Avalokiteśvara,
> One will obtain divine spells and celestial medicines.

> I behold a painted image of Avalokiteśvara,
> Contemplating all the excellences and merits of his form,
> And seeing his spiritual powers and sovereign mastery,
> I am moved to utter this praise with utmost sincerity.

> All meritorious virtues whatsoever,
> Among the bodhisattvas he is foremost.
> With boundless skillful means and great expedients,
> He manifests a pure and wondrous form-body,
> Free from all discriminations of existence and non-existence,
> Benefiting beings without limit, like space itself.

> By this praise of the Treasury of Merit,
> May I realize the omniscience of the tathāgatas.

By this praise of the merits of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, may the merit ascend to benefit the Sage-Spirit Emperor, Our Sovereign Lord, that he may skillfully turn the golden wheel, his form, strength, and lifespan without end, his wisdom and reward beyond conception, ever ruling Jambudvīpa and abiding in the land of China, protecting the Three Jewels and benefiting all beings, so that all sentient beings may share in the sage's blessings.
