# 佛說五大施經
## Sūtra on the Five Great Gifts, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 706

Translated from the Chinese. Song Dynasty, translated by Dānapāla et al.

---

At one time the Buddha, the World-Honored One, was dwelling in the Jetavana, the garden of Anāthapiṇḍada, in Śrāvastī, together with a community of bhikṣus.

The Buddha addressed the bhikṣus, saying: "There are five kinds of great gifts. I will now explain them for you. What are these five? They are: first, refraining from killing; this is a great gift; second, refraining from stealing; third, refraining from sexual misconduct; fourth, refraining from false speech; fifth, refraining from intoxicants. These are the great gifts.

"For what reason is the practice of refraining from killing called a great gift? Because through refraining from killing, one is able to give fearlessness to immeasurable sentient beings. Through fearlessness, there is no enmity, no hatred, no harm. When those immeasurable sentient beings have obtained fearlessness, and are free from enmity, hatred, and harm, they then attain peace and happiness in the heavens and among humans. Therefore, refraining from killing is called a great gift. Refraining from stealing, refraining from sexual misconduct, refraining from false speech, and refraining from intoxicants are likewise the same."
