# 千轉陀羅尼觀世音菩薩呪
## Thousand-Turn Dhāraṇī: Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva's Spell

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1035

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Zhìtōng (智通).

---

*Namo ratna-trayāya. Namaḥ ārya-avalokiteśvarāya bodhisattvāya mahāsattvāya mahākāruṇikāya. Tadyathā: jaye jaye jaya-vāhini jayottari kara kara mara mara cara cara kiṇa kiṇa sarva-karma-āvaraṇāni me bhagavati svāhā. Sarva-bhakti-sarva-buddha-avalokita-cakṣu-sūtra-karaṇa-svāhā-kāya-mano-viśuddhani sura sura prasura prasura sarva-buddha-adhiṣṭhite svāhā. Dharma-dhātu-garbhe svāhā. Abhāva-svabhāva-dharma-buddhāniye svāhā.*

The mudrā for the Thousand-Turn is the same as the heart mudrā of Avalokiteśvara: the four fingers from the index finger onward are interlocked and pressed inward, the left thumb is bent into the space of the index finger, and the right thumb is extended straight inward without bending. Both wrists are pressed together. The feet form a "T" shape, with the right foot standing straight and the left knee bent outward in a straddling posture, the body bent toward the left. Hold the heart mudrā before the right breast without touching it, with a smiling expression and the head turned to the right.

On the fifteenth day of each month, bathe and enter a clean room. Form the heart mudrā and recite the spell to extinguish the four grave offenses and the five heinous crimes.

The Thousand-Turn text says: Having recited this spell, evil karma is extinguished. At seven recitations, the five heinous crimes are destroyed. At a full ten recitations, there is no sin that is not destroyed. At one hundred thousand recitations, one sees Avalokiteśvara face to face in various adornments.

During the seven-day practice period, one may eat only milk gruel, butter, cheese, white cakes, and polished rice. One may not eat salt, sauce, or vegetables. On the final day, one must fast entirely. On the night of the fifteenth, on an empty stomach, before a buddha image, recite the spell continuously without a fixed count. When the image moves, speaks aloud "Well done!", emits radiant light, and places true jewels and precious objects atop the practitioner's head, one knows the practice has been accomplished. Having seen Avalokiteśvara face to face, all wholesome aspirations are fulfilled. Furthermore, if in sleep one dreams of Avalokiteśvara in various adornments, all good things are accomplished and all evil karma is extinguished. Even a simple, straightforward recitation destroys sins.

When about to travel east or west, first recite the spell seven times over the hand and rub the face. Wherever one goes, there will be no calamities or misfortunes. If one can maintain purity and recite according to the method without ceasing, one attains the first bhūmi. If any woman can recite and uphold this spell, she will afterward become a man and never again receive a female body.

First, construct an altar. After arranging and completing all offerings, then begin reciting the spell. The altar is sixteen cubits on each of its four sides, with four concentric borders, the outer ones white and the inner ones of four colors, each one layer, like walls, making eight layers in total, combining the five directional colors. Open a gate on each of the four sides, aligned north-south and east-west. The innermost enclosure need not have a gate. Place eight large earthen jars and four water vessels, all filled with water and with cedar or tree branches inserted. Place the water jars inside the vessels. Prepare white cakes, polished rice, milk, cheese, butter, honey, fragrant fruits, and other offerings. Light sixteen lamps. Outside each of the four gates, plant sixteen unused arrows and hang five-colored threads from the arrows.

Take one thousand eight hundred pieces of khadira wood, each one foot long. Using either an earthen or a copper bowl, anoint the wood pieces with butter and milk. Empower each piece with one recitation of the spell and cast it into the fire. If khadira wood is unavailable, one may substitute.

One must also paint a white image of the holy Avalokiteśvara, of whatever size one's resources allow.

An earlier translation says: The karma accumulated over a thousand kalpas is entirely extinguished in one session of recitation, and one gains the roots of merit amassed by a thousand buddhas. One transcends a thousand kalpas of cyclic wandering through birth, old age, sickness, and death. After leaving this body, one will see a thousand wheel-turning holy kings and forever uphold the ten wholesome precepts. If one wishes to be reborn in a buddha's pure land, one should recite twenty-one times at each of the three periods of day and night, completing twenty-one days. As one desires, one will in dreams see a golden buddha image or bodhisattva forms.

The above spell and its powers were all translated from the Sanskrit original by Master Tōng at Zǒngchí Temple. The mudrā methods were translated by Master Gupta.

An alternative translation says: For the practice method, take five pounds of turuṣka incense together with its plant, one thousand pieces of birch wood each one foot long, and butter. Empower each with one spell recitation, for a total of one thousand recitations. When ready to burn them, anoint each piece of wood and incense with butter, empower each with one recitation, and burn it. During the fifth and sixth months, collect cow dung and dry it in the shade. Mix turuṣka incense-water with it to make a paste for the altar surface. The altar should be circular, two cubits across. Arrange various flowers and incense on the altar; if out of season, carve wooden flowers instead. Burn frankincense. The spell-master faces east, sits, and recites a full one thousand recitations to complete the practice.

Before beginning the spell practice, take this spell-empowered water, recited twenty-one or seven times, and drink it. Spell-empowered water may also be used to purify impure things, clean the ritual room, purify the buddha hall, and wash the body. A spell-empowered willow branch may be used to strike away illness. Spell-empowered water heals illness. All of these require twenty-one recitations each. If one can maintain purity throughout one's life, reciting twenty-one times at each of the three daily periods, the sins destroyed are inconceivable. This practice is effective for all purposes.
