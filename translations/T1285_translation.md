# 佛說寶賢陀羅尼經
## The Buddha's Teaching: Sūtra of the Dhāraṇī of Maṇibhadra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1285

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmabhadra (法賢).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was in the kingdom of Śrāvastī, in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍada's Park, together with a great assembly of bodhisattvas. At that time there was a great yakṣa lord named Maṇibhadra, who came to the Buddha's dwelling. Having arrived, he bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, joined his palms in reverence, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I possess a great secret heart-dhāraṇī, and I wish to proclaim it. May the World-Honored One graciously extend his protective care.

"World-Honored One! This dhāraṇī: if any bhikṣu, bhikṣuṇī, upāsaka, or upāsikā recites it with a sincere heart three times daily, I shall always serve as their protector. In all affairs they shall obtain auspiciousness. Even food, garments, bedding, gold, silver, precious jewels, and grain, I shall constantly provide. I shall also bring about the accomplishment of all excellent benefits, and cause all people everywhere to love and respect them. Only the unwholesome dharmas of lust, anger, and delusion are excepted; in all other undertakings, everything shall accord with their wishes."

At that time the World-Honored One, knowing that this great yakṣa lord Maṇibhadra was sincere in his faith and devotion, and wished to bring peace and happiness to all sentient beings afflicted by poverty and suffering, addressed him: "Excellent! Maṇibhadra, great yakṣa lord! You are able to act for the benefit of all sentient beings. I too am glad to hear it. Proclaim it as you wish."

Then the great yakṣa lord Maṇibhadra spoke the dhāraṇī:

*Namo ratna-trayāya namo maṇi-bhadrāya mahā-yakṣa-senāpataye sāye te nāhi-rī maṇi-bhadra hiri hiri maṇi-bhadra kiri maṇi-bhadra kiri kiri maṇi-bhadra ciri maṇi-bhadra ciri ciri maṇi-bhadra juru maṇi-bhadra juru juru maṇi-bhadra curu maṇi-bhadra curu curu maṇi-bhadra turu maṇi-bhadra turu turu maṇi-bhadra kuru maṇi-bhadra kuru kuru maṇi-bhadra suru maṇi-bhadra suru suru maṇi-bhadra sarvārtha mī sādhaya svāhā tathā-pata-nī su-pata-nī surumi su-mati su-rati sukṣma-bhe hiri ke hiri kāri pūra-ṇi siddha-bhadri hiri hiri svāhā ihi kuni-ṣke ihi seni-ṣke ihi guṇi-ṣke svāhā.*

Then the great yakṣa lord Maṇibhadra, having spoken this dhāraṇī, again addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! This dhāraṇī: if a person recites it seven times according to the method, all the excellent benefits that person undertakes will be accomplished. Furthermore, if a person on the fifteenth day of the bright fortnight observes purity and keeps the precepts, burns agarwood and sandalwood incense three times daily, and recites this dhāraṇī a full 8,000 times, that person will obtain gold, silver, and precious jewels according to their wishes, all as desired."

Then the great yakṣa lord, having spoken this dhāraṇī, bowed to the Buddha and withdrew.

When the Buddha had spoken this sūtra, the assembly of bodhisattvas, hearing what the Buddha had said, all greatly rejoiced, faithfully accepted, and put it into practice.
