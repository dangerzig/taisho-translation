# 大乘無量壽經

## The Mahāyāna Sūtra of Infinite Life

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0936

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Fǎchéng (法成).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Bhagavān was dwelling at the Jetavana monastery in the garden of Anāthapiṇḍada in the state of Śrāvastī, together with a great assembly of twelve hundred and fifty bhikṣus and a great assembly of bodhisattva-mahāsattvas, all gathered together. At that time the World-Honored One addressed the youth Mañjuśrī: "Mañjuśrī, in the direction above there is a world called Infinite Merit Assembly. In that land there is a buddha named Amitāyus Jñāna-viniścaya-rāja Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now teaching and expounding the Dharma for sentient beings. Mañjuśrī, listen well. The people of Jambudvīpa in the south all have short lives. The maximum is one hundred years, and among them, many die untimely and premature deaths. Mañjuśrī, regarding the merit, renown, and essential Dharma of this Amitāyus Tathāgata: if there are sentient beings who hear his name, and either copy the scripture themselves or have others copy it, making it into sūtra scrolls, and receive, uphold, read, and recite it, and at their place of dwelling make offerings with various flower garlands, necklaces, perfumed ointments, and powdered incense, then if their lifespan has come to an end, they will receive an extension of life to the full measure of one hundred years. Thus, Mañjuśrī, if there are sentient beings who hear the one hundred and eight names of this Amitāyus Jñāna-viniścaya-rāja Tathāgata, it will increase their longevity. If there are sentient beings whose great lifespan is about to end, and they call to mind the names of this Tathāgata, they will gain an increase of life. Thus, Mañjuśrī, if good men or good women wish to seek long life, and there are those who hear the one hundred and eight names of this Amitāyus Tathāgata, or copy the scripture themselves or have others copy it, and receive, uphold, read, and recite it, they will obtain such karmic fruits with complete merit and blessings." The dhāraṇī reads:

*Namo bhagavate aparimitāyur-jñāna-suviniścitateja-rājāya tathāgatāya. Tadyathā: oṃ sarva-saṃskāra-pariśuddha dharmate gagana svāhā. Mucikati sarva-bhāva-viśuddhi mahānaya-parivāri svāhā.*

The World-Honored One further addressed Mañjuśrī: "If anyone copies the one hundred and eight names of this Tathāgata, either personally or by having others copy them, and makes them into sūtra scrolls, and receives, upholds, reads, and recites them, then even if their lifespan has ended, they will again receive a full one hundred years of life. After the end of this body, they will be reborn in the world of Infinite Merit and Wisdom, the Pure Land of Infinite Life." The dhāraṇī reads:

*Namo bhagavate aparimitāyur-jñāna-suviniścitateja-rājāya tathāgatāya. Tadyathā: oṃ sarva-saṃskāra-pariśuddha dharmate gagana svāhā. Mucikati sarva-bhāva-viśuddhi mahānaya-parivāri svāhā.*

At that time, ninety-nine koṭis of buddhas all simultaneously spoke this Essential Sūtra of Infinite Life with one voice. The dhāraṇī reads:

*Namo bhagavate aparimitāyur-jñāna-suviniścitateja-rājāya tathāgatāya. Tadyathā: oṃ sarva-saṃskāra-pariśuddha dharmate gagana svāhā. Mucikati sarva-bhāva-viśuddhi mahānaya-parivāri svāhā.*

At that time, a further one hundred and four koṭis of buddhas all simultaneously spoke this Essential Sūtra of Infinite Life with one voice. The dhāraṇī reads:

*Namo bhagavate aparimitāyur-jñāna-suviniścitateja-rājāya tathāgatāya. Tadyathā: oṃ sarva-saṃskāra-pariśuddha dharmate gagana svāhā. Mucikati sarva-bhāva-viśuddhi mahānaya-parivāri svāhā.*

At that time, a further seven koṭis of buddhas all simultaneously spoke this Essential Sūtra of Infinite Life with one voice. The dhāraṇī reads:

*Namo bhagavate aparimitāyur-jñāna-suviniścitateja-rājāya tathāgatāya. Tadyathā: oṃ sarva-saṃskāra-pariśuddha dharmate gagana svāhā. Mucikati sarva-bhāva-viśuddhi mahānaya-parivāri svāhā.*

At that time, a further sixty-five koṭis of buddhas all simultaneously spoke this Essential Sūtra of Infinite Life with one voice. The dhāraṇī reads:

*Namo bhagavate aparimitāyur-jñāna-suviniścitateja-rājāya tathāgatāya. Tadyathā: oṃ sarva-saṃskāra-pariśuddha dharmate gagana svāhā. Mucikati sarva-bhāva-viśuddhi mahānaya-parivāri svāhā.*

At that time, a further fifty-five koṭis of buddhas all simultaneously spoke this Essential Sūtra of Infinite Life with one voice. The dhāraṇī reads:

*Namo bhagavate aparimitāyur-jñāna-suviniścitateja-rājāya tathāgatāya. Tadyathā: oṃ sarva-saṃskāra-pariśuddha dharmate gagana svāhā. Mucikati sarva-bhāva-viśuddhi mahānaya-parivāri svāhā.*

At that time, a further forty-five koṭis of buddhas all simultaneously spoke this Essential Sūtra of Infinite Life with one voice. The dhāraṇī reads:

*Namo bhagavate aparimitāyur-jñāna-suviniścitateja-rājāya tathāgatāya. Tadyathā: oṃ sarva-saṃskāra-pariśuddha dharmate gagana svāhā. Mucikati sarva-bhāva-viśuddhi mahānaya-parivāri svāhā.*

At that time, a further thirty-six koṭis of buddhas all simultaneously spoke this Essential Sūtra of Infinite Life with one voice. The dhāraṇī reads:

*Namo bhagavate aparimitāyur-jñāna-suviniścitateja-rājāya tathāgatāya. Tadyathā: oṃ sarva-saṃskāra-pariśuddha dharmate gagana svāhā. Mucikati sarva-bhāva-viśuddhi mahānaya-parivāri svāhā.*

At that time, a further twenty-five koṭis of buddhas all simultaneously spoke this Essential Sūtra of Infinite Life with one voice. The dhāraṇī reads:

*Namo bhagavate aparimitāyur-jñāna-suviniścitateja-rājāya tathāgatāya. Tadyathā: oṃ sarva-saṃskāra-pariśuddha dharmate gagana svāhā. Mucikati sarva-bhāva-viśuddhi mahānaya-parivāri svāhā.*

At that time, a further number of buddhas equal to the sands of the Ganges all simultaneously spoke this Essential Sūtra of Infinite Life with one voice. The dhāraṇī reads:

*Namo bhagavate aparimitāyur-jñāna-suviniścitateja-rājāya tathāgatāya. Tadyathā: oṃ sarva-saṃskāra-pariśuddha dharmate gagana svāhā. Mucikati sarva-bhāva-viśuddhi mahānaya-parivāri svāhā.*

"Good sons! If anyone copies this Essential Sūtra of Infinite Life, either personally or by having others copy it, and their lifespan has ended, they will again receive long life and complete their years." The dhāraṇī reads:

*Namo bhagavate aparimitāyur-jñāna-suviniścitateja-rājāya tathāgatāya. Tadyathā: oṃ sarva-saṃskāra-pariśuddha dharmate gagana svāhā. Mucikati sarva-bhāva-viśuddhi mahānaya-parivāri svāhā.*

"If anyone copies this Essential Sūtra of Infinite Life, either personally or by having others copy it, and receives, upholds, reads, and recites it, they will ultimately never fall into the hells, and wherever they are reborn they will gain the knowledge of past lives." The dhāraṇī reads:

*Namo bhagavate aparimitāyur-jñāna-suviniścitateja-rājāya tathāgatāya. Tadyathā: oṃ sarva-saṃskāra-pariśuddha dharmate gagana svāhā. Mucikati sarva-bhāva-viśuddhi mahānaya-parivāri svāhā.*

"If anyone copies this Essential Sūtra of Infinite Life, either personally or by having others copy it, and receives, upholds, reads, and recites it, it is the same as copying all eighty-four thousand scriptures." The dhāraṇī reads:

*Namo bhagavate aparimitāyur-jñāna-suviniścitateja-rājāya tathāgatāya. Tadyathā: oṃ sarva-saṃskāra-pariśuddha dharmate gagana svāhā. Mucikati sarva-bhāva-viśuddhi mahānaya-parivāri svāhā.*

"If anyone copies this Essential Sūtra of Infinite Life, either personally or by having others copy it, it is the same as copying eighty-four thousand sections and building stūpas and temples." The dhāraṇī reads:

*Namo bhagavate aparimitāyur-jñāna-suviniścitateja-rājāya tathāgatāya. Tadyathā: oṃ sarva-saṃskāra-pariśuddha dharmate gagana svāhā. Mucikati sarva-bhāva-viśuddhi mahānaya-parivāri svāhā.*

"If anyone copies this Essential Sūtra of Infinite Life, either personally or by having others copy it, it can extinguish the five sins of immediate retribution and all other grave transgressions." The dhāraṇī reads:

*Namo bhagavate aparimitāyur-jñāna-suviniścitateja-rājāya tathāgatāya. Tadyathā: oṃ sarva-saṃskāra-pariśuddha dharmate gagana svāhā. Mucikati sarva-bhāva-viśuddhi mahānaya-parivāri svāhā.*

"If anyone copies this Essential Sūtra of Infinite Life, either personally or by having others copy it, and receives, upholds, reads, and recites it, then even if their transgressions were as great as Mount Sumeru, all would be completely extinguished." The dhāraṇī reads:

*Namo bhagavate aparimitāyur-jñāna-suviniścitateja-rājāya tathāgatāya. Tadyathā: oṃ sarva-saṃskāra-pariśuddha dharmate gagana svāhā. Mucikati sarva-bhāva-viśuddhi mahānaya-parivāri svāhā.*

"If anyone copies this Essential Sūtra of Infinite Life, either personally or by having others copy it, and receives, upholds, reads, and recites it, then māras, the retinues of māra, yakṣas, and rākṣasas will find no opportunity against them, and they will never die by violence." The dhāraṇī reads:

*Namo bhagavate aparimitāyur-jñāna-suviniścitateja-rājāya tathāgatāya. Tadyathā: oṃ sarva-saṃskāra-pariśuddha dharmate gagana svāhā. Mucikati sarva-bhāva-viśuddhi mahānaya-parivāri svāhā.*

"If anyone copies this Essential Sūtra of Infinite Life, either personally or by having others copy it, and receives, upholds, reads, and recites it, then at the time of death, ninety koṭis of buddhas will appear before that person. Receiving the buddhas' guiding hand, that person will be able to journey through all buddha-fields. Do not harbor doubt about this sūtra." The dhāraṇī reads:

*Namo bhagavate aparimitāyur-jñāna-suviniścitateja-rājāya tathāgatāya. Tadyathā: oṃ sarva-saṃskāra-pariśuddha dharmate gagana svāhā. Mucikati sarva-bhāva-viśuddhi mahānaya-parivāri svāhā.*

"If anyone copies this Essential Sūtra of Infinite Life, either personally or by having others copy it, and receives, upholds, reads, and recites it, they will always have the four great heavenly kings accompanying and protecting them." The dhāraṇī reads:

*Namo bhagavate aparimitāyur-jñāna-suviniścitateja-rājāya tathāgatāya. Tadyathā: oṃ sarva-saṃskāra-pariśuddha dharmate gagana svāhā. Mucikati sarva-bhāva-viśuddhi mahānaya-parivāri svāhā.*

"If anyone copies this Essential Sūtra of Infinite Life, either personally or by having others copy it, and receives, upholds, reads, and recites it, they will be reborn in the Western Land of Ultimate Bliss, the Pure Land of Amitābha." The dhāraṇī reads:

*Namo bhagavate aparimitāyur-jñāna-suviniścitateja-rājāya tathāgatāya. Tadyathā: oṃ sarva-saṃskāra-pariśuddha dharmate gagana svāhā. Mucikati sarva-bhāva-viśuddhi mahānaya-parivāri svāhā.*

"If in any place this Sūtra of Infinite Life is copied, either personally or by having others copy it, that place is a stūpa, and all should pay reverence and bow before it. If animals or birds hear this sūtra, all such beings will before long attain omniscience." The dhāraṇī reads:

*Namo bhagavate aparimitāyur-jñāna-suviniścitateja-rājāya tathāgatāya. Tadyathā: oṃ sarva-saṃskāra-pariśuddha dharmate gagana svāhā. Mucikati sarva-bhāva-viśuddhi mahānaya-parivāri svāhā.*

"If anyone copies this Sūtra of Infinite Life, either personally or by having others copy it, they will ultimately never receive a woman's body." The dhāraṇī reads:

*Namo bhagavate aparimitāyur-jñāna-suviniścitateja-rājāya tathāgatāya. Tadyathā: oṃ sarva-saṃskāra-pariśuddha dharmate gagana svāhā. Mucikati sarva-bhāva-viśuddhi mahānaya-parivāri svāhā.*

"If anyone can give even a small portion of this sūtra to others, it is equal to filling the entire three-thousandfold great-thousandfold world with the seven treasures and giving them in charity." The dhāraṇī reads:

*Namo bhagavate aparimitāyur-jñāna-suviniścitateja-rājāya tathāgatāya. Tadyathā: oṃ sarva-saṃskāra-pariśuddha dharmate gagana svāhā. Mucikati sarva-bhāva-viśuddhi mahānaya-parivāri svāhā.*

"If anyone can make offerings to this sūtra, it is the same as making offerings to all the sūtras, without any difference." The dhāraṇī reads:

*Namo bhagavate aparimitāyur-jñāna-suviniścitateja-rājāya tathāgatāya. Tadyathā: oṃ sarva-saṃskāra-pariśuddha dharmate gagana svāhā. Mucikati sarva-bhāva-viśuddhi mahānaya-parivāri svāhā.*

"Thus, regarding Vipaśyin Buddha, Śikhin Buddha, Viśvabhū Buddha, Krakucchanda Buddha, Kanakamuni Buddha, Kāśyapa Buddha, and Śākyamuni Buddha: if someone were to make offerings of the seven treasures to these seven buddhas, the merit of that would be limited. But the merit of copying, receiving, and upholding this Sūtra of Infinite Life is immeasurable." The dhāraṇī reads:

*Namo bhagavate aparimitāyur-jñāna-suviniścitateja-rājāya tathāgatāya. Tadyathā: oṃ sarva-saṃskāra-pariśuddha dharmate gagana svāhā. Mucikati sarva-bhāva-viśuddhi mahānaya-parivāri svāhā.*

"If someone were to give in charity seven treasures equal in quantity to Mount Sumeru, the merit of that could still be measured. But the merit of this Sūtra of Infinite Life is beyond all counting." The dhāraṇī reads:

*Namo bhagavate aparimitāyur-jñāna-suviniścitateja-rājāya tathāgatāya. Tadyathā: oṃ sarva-saṃskāra-pariśuddha dharmate gagana svāhā. Mucikati sarva-bhāva-viśuddhi mahānaya-parivāri svāhā.*

"Just as the drops of water in the four great oceans can be counted, the karmic fruits produced by this Sūtra of Infinite Life are beyond all reckoning." The dhāraṇī reads:

*Namo bhagavate aparimitāyur-jñāna-suviniścitateja-rājāya tathāgatāya. Tadyathā: oṃ sarva-saṃskāra-pariśuddha dharmate gagana svāhā. Mucikati sarva-bhāva-viśuddhi mahānaya-parivāri svāhā.*

If anyone copies this Sūtra of Infinite Life, either personally or by having others copy it, and is further able to protect, uphold, and make offerings to it, it is exactly the same as reverently making offerings to the Tathāgatas in all the buddha-lands of the ten directions, without any difference. The dhāraṇī reads:

*Namo bhagavate aparimitāyur-jñāna-suviniścitateja-rājāya tathāgatāya. Tadyathā: oṃ sarva-saṃskāra-pariśuddha dharmate gagana svāhā. Mucikati sarva-bhāva-viśuddhi mahānaya-parivāri svāhā.*

> The power of generosity can accomplish perfect awakening.
> One who realizes the power of generosity is a lion among humans.
> The power of generosity can make its sound heard everywhere.
> Through the gradual steps of compassion, one enters most fully.
>
> The power of upholding precepts can accomplish perfect awakening.
> One who realizes the power of precepts is a lion among humans.
> The power of precepts can make its sound heard everywhere.
> Through the gradual steps of compassion, one enters most fully.
>
> The power of patience can accomplish perfect awakening.
> One who realizes the power of patience is a lion among humans.
> The power of patience can make its sound heard everywhere.
> Through the gradual steps of compassion, one enters most fully.
>
> The power of diligence can accomplish perfect awakening.
> One who realizes the power of diligence is a lion among humans.
> The power of diligence can make its sound heard everywhere.
> Through the gradual steps of compassion, one enters most fully.
>
> The power of meditation can accomplish perfect awakening.
> One who realizes the power of meditation is a lion among humans.
> The power of meditation can make its sound heard everywhere.
> Through the gradual steps of compassion, one enters most fully.
>
> The power of wisdom can accomplish perfect awakening.
> One who realizes the power of wisdom is a lion among humans.
> The power of wisdom can make its sound heard everywhere.
> Through the gradual steps of compassion, one enters most fully.

At that time, when the Tathāgata had finished speaking this sūtra, all the beings of the world, including devas, humans, asuras, gandharvas, and others, having heard what the Buddha taught, all greatly rejoiced, accepted it with faith, and put it into practice.
