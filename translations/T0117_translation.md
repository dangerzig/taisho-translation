# 佛說戒香經
## Scripture of the Fragrance of Precepts, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0117

Translated from the Chinese. Song Dynasty, translated by Dharmabhadra

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was dwelling in the Jetavana Monastery of Anāthapiṇḍada in the country of Śrāvastī, together with a great assembly of bhikṣus. At that time, the Venerable Ānanda came to where the Buddha was, and upon arriving, bowed his head to the ground at the Buddha's feet, joined his palms in reverence, and spoke to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I have a small doubt I wish to raise, and I humbly ask the World-Honored One to explain it to me. I observe that in the world there are three kinds of fragrance: the fragrance of roots, the fragrance of flowers, and the fragrance of seeds. These three kinds of fragrance pervade everywhere, smelled when there is wind and smelled also when there is no wind. What is the nature of such fragrance?"

At that time, the World-Honored One said to the Venerable Ānanda: "Do not speak as you have, saying that these three kinds of fragrance pervade everywhere, smelled whether there is wind or no wind. These three kinds of fragrance, whether there is wind or no wind, though pervading everywhere, cannot in fact be smelled in all places. Ānanda! If you wish to hear of a fragrance that is genuinely pervasive, you should listen carefully, and I will proclaim it to you."

Ānanda said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I now rejoice to hear it. I humbly ask you to proclaim it."

The Buddha said to Ānanda: "The fragrance that pervades the ten directions whether there is wind or no wind belongs to those in the world who are upāsakas or upāsikās, who uphold the pure precepts of the Buddha and practice all virtuous actions, namely: not killing, not stealing, not engaging in sexual misconduct, not speaking falsely, and not drinking alcohol. The fragrance of precepts belonging to such upāsakas and upāsikās is heard throughout the ten directions, and in all those ten directions they are universally praised, with others saying: 'In such-and-such a city there are upāsakas and upāsikās of this kind, who uphold the pure precepts of the Buddha and practice all virtuous actions, namely: not killing, not stealing, not engaging in sexual misconduct, not speaking falsely, and not drinking alcohol, and so forth.' Those who possess these precepts obtain such a fragrance, pervading the ten directions whether there is wind or no wind, universally praised, beloved, and revered."

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke these verses:

> All the flowers and fruits in the world, and all the aloeswood, sandalwood, ambergris, and musk...
> Such fragrances as these do not pervade everywhere; only the fragrance of precepts pervades all.
> Candana, saffron, and styrax, utpala lotus and mālatī blossoms...
> Among all these wondrous floral fragrances, only the fragrance of precepts is supreme.
> All the aloeswood and sandalwood of the world; their scent is faint and does not pervade everywhere;
> If a person upholds the fragrance of the Buddha's pure precepts, all the devas hear it throughout and cherish and revere them.
> Thus having complete and pure precepts, and always practicing all virtuous actions,
> Such a person can release the bonds of the world, and all māras will always keep far away.

At that time, the Venerable Ānanda and the assembly of bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced, received them in faith, paid homage to the Buddha, and withdrew.
