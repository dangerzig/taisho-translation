# 佛說善樂長者經
## The Buddha's Teaching: Sūtra of the Elder Sunanda

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1380

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmadeva (法賢).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was in the city of Kapilavastu, together with a great assembly. At that time there was a Śākya elder named Sunanda who dwelt in his home. This elder revered and trusted the Three Jewels. He had taken refuge in the Buddha, taken refuge in the Dharma, and taken refuge in the Saṃgha. He trusted only the Buddha, Dharma, and Saṃgha and no longer placed faith in any heterodox teachings. He sought only the Buddha, Dharma, and Saṃgha. He well understood suffering, its arising, its cessation, and the path, had perceived the truth of reality, and had obtained the great fruit. It was certain that, if not immediately, he would realize awakening. At that time, the elder suddenly lost the ability to see the forms he had been seeing.

Then the elder Sunanda, facing in the direction of the World-Honored One from afar, made this aspiration: "My Buddha, the World-Honored One, of great compassion and great mercy! May you grant me eyes, grant me the light of sight, grant me purity of vision, and remove the darkness from my eyes." Having made this aspiration, he further said: "I now take refuge in the Buddha, take refuge in the Sugata."

At that time the World-Honored One, with his pure divine ear surpassing human hearing, heard from afar the elder's words of aspiration. He then addressed the Venerable Ānanda: "Ānanda! Take my secret divine mantra now and go to where the Śākya elder Sunanda is. Rescue, deliver, and protect him, and bring him tranquility and joy. Do this also for the benefit and joy of the fourfold assembly."

At that time the World-Honored One, for the sake of the elder Sunanda and others, spoke this secret great divine mantra for purifying the eyes:

*Tadyathā: hiri miri kiri hi-ke-ta hu-yu hu-yu hu-yā vi-ti hu-ru hu-ru hu-ru nu nu nu nu-ru.* By the power of these sentences of the secret divine mantra for purifying the eyes, may the eyes be purified, darkness removed, cataracts eliminated, and even the eye-diseases caused by wind-poison, jaundice-poison, phlegm-poison, and congestion-poison all be removed: no pain, no swelling, no distress, no suffering, and no discharge or tears. May all such diseases be entirely eliminated.

Furthermore, by the truth-power of the precepts, the truth-power of cultivation, the truth-power of ascetic practices, the truth-power of the secret divine mantra, the truth-power of dependent arising, the truth-power of the truth of suffering, the truth-power of the truth of arising, the truth-power of the truth of cessation, the truth-power of the truth of the path, the truth-power of the stream-enterer, the truth-power of the once-returner, the truth-power of the non-returner, the truth-power of the arhat, the truth-power of the pratyekabuddha, the truth-power of the bodhisattva, the truth-power of the Buddha, the truth-power of the Dharma, and the truth-power of the holy Saṃgha: by all these truth-powers, may the eyes be purified, darkness removed, cataracts eliminated. All eye-diseases caused by wind-poison, jaundice-poison, phlegm-poison, and congestion-poison shall be unable to invade: no pain, no swelling, no distress, no suffering, and no discharge or tears.

The Buddha addressed Ānanda: "This secret divine mantra was proclaimed in common by the six samyak-saṃbuddhas of the past, namely: Vipaśyin Tathāgata Samyak-saṃbuddha, Śikhin Tathāgata Samyak-saṃbuddha, Viśvabhū Tathāgata Samyak-saṃbuddha, Krakucchanda Tathāgata Samyak-saṃbuddha, Kanakamuni Tathāgata Samyak-saṃbuddha, and Kāśyapa Tathāgata Samyak-saṃbuddha. I, the seventh, Śākyamuni Tathāgata Samyak-saṃbuddha, likewise proclaim it. Even the four great heavenly kings, namely Dhṛtarāṣṭra, Virūḍhaka, Virūpākṣa, and Vaiśravaṇa, as well as Śakra Devendra and Mahābrahmā, the lord of the Sahā world, all rejoiced and proclaimed it as well."

The Buddha addressed Ānanda: "I have not seen, in the realms of devas, Brahmā, and māras, nor among śramaṇas, brahmins, and the rest, anyone who, having recited this secret great divine mantra three times, could be invaded by any illness or suffering, nor by any internal or external darkness or cataracts, nor by diseases caused by devas, nāgas, yakṣas, asuras, garuḍas, gandharvas, kumbhāṇḍas, pretas, pūtanas, kaṭapūtanas, piśācas, rākṣasas, karkuḍas, vetāḍas, stellar afflictions, poisonings, or evil sorceries: all are eliminated.

"Furthermore, Ānanda! If one with this ailment recites this secret great divine mantra three times daily, that person's eyes will be purified and they will see things as before. Because this secret great divine mantra for purifying the eyes has great power, the eyes will be thus purified, darkness removed, and internal and external cataracts eliminated. Even wind-poison obstructions, jaundice-poison obstructions, phlegm-poison obstructions, and congestion-poison obstructions will all be eliminated: no pain, no suffering, no discharge or tears.

"Furthermore, by the truth-power of pure discipline, the truth-power of cultivation, the truth-power of the five-powered sages, the truth-power of the worthy and holy, the truth-power of holy medicine, the truth-power of the secret phrases, the truth-power of dependent arising, the truth-power of the truth of suffering, the truth-power of the truth of arising, the truth-power of the truth of cessation, the truth-power of the truth of the path, the truth-power of the stream-enterer, the truth-power of the once-returner, the truth-power of the non-returner, the truth-power of the arhat, the truth-power of the pratyekabuddha, the truth-power of the bodhisattva, and the truth-power of the Buddha: by all these truth-powers, may the eyes be purified."

At that time the World-Honored One further spoke a secret divine mantra:

*Namo buddhāya bhagavate. Siddhantu mantra-padāṃ svāhā.*

The Buddha addressed Ānanda: "This secret great divine mantra: if a person recites it three times daily, together with the previous mantra, in accordance with the method, all that person's eye-diseases will be entirely eliminated."

At that time the Venerable Ānanda addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I now, by the Buddha's holy command, shall take this secret great divine mantra and go to where the elder Sunanda is, broadly proclaiming it, bringing great benefit to the elder Sunanda and the fourfold assembly."

When the Buddha had spoken this sūtra, the Venerable Ānanda and the entire great assembly, having heard what the Buddha taught, all greatly rejoiced, believed, received it with faith, and put it into practice.
