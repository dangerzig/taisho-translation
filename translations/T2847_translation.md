# 祈願文
## Prayer of Aspiration

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 2847

Translated from the Chinese. Tang/Five Dynasties period; translator unknown.

---

In this very Sahā world, with hearts vowed to benefit all beings, we establish the gate of skillful means. Benevolence extends to the eight directions; sounds of peace and joy compete in welcoming; before the Brahma-assembly, the mind is led forward. It is said that the hardships of forced labor are dispelled, demons and malevolent spirits are banished a thousand li from the household gate. Gathering all these many virtues, with boundless excellent conditions:

First, using these merits to adorn the Four Heavenly Kings, dragon-devas, and the Eight Divisions of protective beings — we humbly pray that their awesome radiance blazes brilliantly and their meritorious power increases ever more, that they may bring rescue to the people and protection to the nation.

Again, carrying these supreme merits, next to adorn our Hexi Festival.

May the Great King's honored position be adorned. We humbly pray that he, venerable as the guiding stars of north and south, may as a bodhisattva among kings of men forever fulfill the Tathāgata's entrustment.

Again, carrying these supreme merits, next to adorn the honored position of our Hexi Chief Saṃgha Controller, the Venerable Preceptor. We humbly pray that his brilliant governance and administration may firmly subdue and restrain all; his physical strength be solid as hills and mountains; his benevolent lifespan extend to far-reaching eminence.

Carrying these supreme merits, next to adorn the honored position of the Directing Secretary. We humbly pray that his golden branches ever flourish and jade leaves be fragrant in their season; solid as a great rock, his renown grows and is venerated; may he alone stand as city and fortress.

Again, carrying these supreme merits, next to adorn the honored positions of the Yan Commander's Office, the Xun Commander's Office, and the various Commanders-in-Chief. We humbly pray that those of extraordinary talent who surpass the multitude, those of inexhaustible skill who have drawn fortune back to their hearts, with the capacity to garrison and protect all things, together assist the path of the enlightened king.

Thereafter, may all under heaven be settled and the realm within the seas be at peace. May the name of campaigns and warfare be unheard. May the people have the blessing of being upheld and supported.

Mahāprajñā: its benefits and joy are boundless. May the entire assembly with sincere reverence universally recite this.

*Recorded by Daoji*

---

**Translator's notes:** This is a Dunhuang liturgical merit-dedication prayer, likely composed in the late Tang or Five Dynasties period (9th–10th century). The references to the Hexi (河西) region, the Chief Saṃgha Controller (都僧統), and the military command structure reflect the institutional context of the Guiyijun (Return-to-Allegiance Army) polity centered at Dunhuang. Several passages appear textually corrupt or lacunose, most notably the abrupt ending of the petition for the Chief Saṃgha Controller. The text is preserved as a single Dunhuang manuscript fragment. "Daoji" (道記) at the close appears to be a scribal colophon rather than a translator attribution.
