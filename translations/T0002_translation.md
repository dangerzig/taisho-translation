# 佛說七佛經
## The Sūtra of the Seven Buddhas

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 2

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Fātian (法天).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the country of Śrāvastī, in the Jeta Grove, the Garden of Anāthapiṇḍada. At that time there was a great assembly of bhikṣus. At mealtime, carrying their bowls, they went to the Kālī assembly hall and sat together in contemplation, thinking: "In past ages, which Buddhas appeared in the world? What were their clan names and lifespans, and what is the meaning of these?" Having thought thus, they questioned one another but could not find the answers.

At that time, the World-Honored One knew that these bhikṣus were contemplating this matter. He arose from his seat and went to the Kālī assembly hall, where he sat in full lotus posture. The bhikṣus bowed their heads to his feet and stood to one side, palms joined in reverence, gazing upon him single-mindedly.

The World-Honored One asked: "Bhikṣus, what are you thinking about?" The bhikṣus replied: "We were contemplating which Buddhas appeared in the world during past ages, but we could not determine their clan names and lifespans." The Buddha told the bhikṣus: "Would you like to hear about this?" The bhikṣus answered: "Now is the right time. We wish you would explain it for us."

The Buddha said: "Listen carefully, and I shall explain. Ninety-one kalpas ago, Vipaśyin Buddha, Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened One, appeared in the world. Thirty-one kalpas ago, Śikhin Buddha and Viśvabhū Buddha, Worthy Ones, Perfectly Enlightened Ones, appeared in the world. In the Fortunate Kalpa, in the sixth kalpa, Krakucchanda Buddha, Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened One, appeared in the world. In the seventh kalpa, Kanakamuni Buddha, Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened One, appeared in the world. In the eighth kalpa, Kāśyapa Buddha, Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened One, appeared in the world. In the ninth kalpa, I, Śākyamuni Buddha, appeared in the world as Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened One. Furthermore, in past kalpas, Vipaśyin Buddha, Śikhin Buddha, and Viśvabhū Buddha proclaimed the śīla of pure discipline and accomplished the supreme practice of wisdom.

"Furthermore, in the Fortunate Kalpa, Krakucchanda Buddha, Kanakamuni Buddha, and Kāśyapa Buddha also taught the pure rules of conduct and the Dharma of meditation, liberation, and concentration; and what I teach is likewise. Bhikṣus! In the past, Vipaśyin Buddha was of the kṣatriya caste and, having given rise to pure faith, sought to leave the household life and attained the path of perfect enlightenment. Śikhin Buddha and Viśvabhū Buddha were also of the kṣatriya caste. Krakucchanda Buddha, Kanakamuni Buddha, and Kāśyapa Buddha were of the brāhmaṇa caste. I was born in the palace of King Śuddhodana, of the kṣatriya caste."

At that time, the World-Honored One, wishing to further proclaim this meaning, spoke in verse:

> I speak of the past world, ninety-one kalpas ago,
> When there was Vipaśyin, who appeared in the world.
> Thirty-one kalpas ago, Śikhin and Viśvabhū,
> These Perfectly Enlightened Ones were all of the kṣatriya caste.
> Krakucchanda Tathāgata was of the brāhmaṇa caste;
> Kanakamuni and Kāśyapa, their caste was likewise so.
> I dwell in Jambudvīpa as the son of Śuddhodana;
> Though I attained the bodhi of a Buddha, I too am of the kṣatriya caste.

At that time, the World-Honored One, having spoken this verse, told the assembly of bhikṣus: "Listen carefully! I shall now further explain the clan names of the seven Tathāgatas, Worthy Ones, Perfectly Enlightened Ones. Vipaśyin Buddha, Śikhin Buddha, and Viśvabhū Buddha were of the Kauṇḍinya clan. Krakucchanda Buddha, Kanakamuni Buddha, and Kāśyapa Buddha were of the Kāśyapa clan. The Tathāgata Śākyamuni is of the Gautama clan."

At that time, the World-Honored One again spoke in verse:

> Vipaśyin Tathāgata, Śikhin, and Viśvabhū,
> These three Buddhas were all of the Kauṇḍinya clan.
> Krakucchanda Tathāgata, Kanakamuni, and Kāśyapa,
> These three Buddhas were all of the Kāśyapa clan.
> I dwell in the realm of Jambudvīpa, born in the palace of Śuddhodana;
> Having left home and attained bodhi, I am of the Gautama clan.

At that time, the World-Honored One, having spoken this verse, told the assembly of bhikṣus: "Listen carefully! I shall now further explain the length of lifespan for the seven Tathāgatas, Worthy Ones, Perfectly Enlightened Ones. Vipaśyin Buddha, Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened One, appeared in the world when the human lifespan was eighty thousand years. Śikhin Buddha, Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened One, appeared in the world when the human lifespan was seventy thousand years. Viśvabhū Buddha, Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened One, appeared in the world when the human lifespan was sixty thousand years. Krakucchanda Buddha, Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened One, appeared in the world when the human lifespan was forty thousand years. Kanakamuni Buddha, Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened One, appeared in the world when the human lifespan was thirty thousand years. Kāśyapa Buddha, Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened One, appeared in the world when the human lifespan was twenty thousand years. I transform beings in the five turbidities, when the human lifespan is one hundred years."

At that time, the World-Honored One again spoke in verse:

> Vipaśyin Tathāgata, Śikhin, Viśvabhū,
> Krakucchanda World-Honored One, Kanakamuni, Kāśyapa,
> When these appeared in the world, the respective human lifespans were:
> Eighty thousand, then seventy thousand, sixty thousand and forty thousand,
> Thirty thousand down to twenty thousand. As for Śākyamuni Buddha,
> He appeared in the five turbidities, when the human lifespan was one hundred years.

At that time, the World-Honored One, having spoken this verse, told the assembly of bhikṣus: "Listen carefully! I shall now further explain the various names of the fathers, mothers, countries, and cities of the seven Tathāgatas. Vipaśyin Buddha: his father was King Bandhumā, his mother was Bandhumatī, and the royal city was also named Bandhumatī. Śikhin Tathāgata: his father was King Aruṇa, his mother was also named Aruṇā, and the city was named Aruṇavatī. Viśvabhū Tathāgata: his father was King Supratīta, his mother was Prabhāvatī, and the city was named Anupamā. Krakucchanda Buddha: his father was Yajñadatta, his mother was Viśākhā, the king was Kṣemakṣama, and the city was named Kṣema. Kanakamuni Buddha: his father was Yajñadattu, his mother was Uttarā, the king was Śubha, and the city was named Śubhavatī. Kāśyapa Tathāgata: his father was Brahmagupta, his mother was Brahmagupta-guptā, the king was Kṛkin, and the city was Vārāṇasī. For me, the present Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened One: my father was King Śuddhodana, my mother was Mahāmāyā, and the city was named Kapilavastu."

At that time, the World-Honored One again spoke in verse:

> Vipaśyin Tathāgata, the place of that Buddha's birth:
> Bandhumā was his father, Bandhumatī his mother,
> And the great city where he dwelt was also named Bandhumatī;
> At that time the land was prosperous and rich, and its people were always joyful.
> Śikhin Buddha, the World-Honored One: King Aruṇa was his father,
> Aruṇāvatī was the name of that Buddha's mother;
> The city where he dwelt had the same name, Aruṇavatī;
> Its people were greatly flourishing, wealthy and always at peace.
> Viśvabhū Tathāgata: his father the king and his mother were named
> Supratīta and Prabhāvatī;
> The city of that country was named Anupamā;
> That age was also peaceful, and all was free from calamity.
> Krakucchanda, the World-Honored One: his father's given name
> Was Yajñadatta; Viśākhā was his mother;
> King Kṣemakṣama ruled from the city of Kṣema;
> At that time those beings all honored virtue and goodness.
> Kanakamuni: Yajñadattu
> Was the name of his father; his mother was called Uttarā;
> The king's name was Śubha; the city, Śubhavatī;
> It was vast and beautifully adorned, and beings were free from affliction.
> Kāśyapa Buddha's father was Brahmagupta;
> His mother's name was established as Brahmagupta-guptā;
> The king of the country was Kṛkin; the capital, the city of Vārāṇasī;
> The beings within it were always at peace, day and night.
> As for my own birthplace: King Śuddhodana was my father,
> Mahāmāyā my mother; the city was named Kapilavastu.
> These Perfectly Enlightened Ones, the seven Tathāgatas,
> Their fathers, mothers, countries, and cities, with their various names, are thus.

At that time, the World-Honored One, having spoken this verse, told the assembly of bhikṣus: "Listen carefully! I shall now further explain the śrāvaka disciples of the seven Tathāgatas, Worthy Ones, Perfectly Enlightened Ones. Vipaśyin Tathāgata, Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened One, had a great-wisdom disciple named Khaṇḍadīpya, foremost among the śrāvakas. Śikhin Tathāgata, Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened One, had a great-wisdom disciple named Bhūsaṃbhava, foremost among the śrāvakas. Viśvabhū Tathāgata, Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened One, had a great-wisdom disciple named Yaśottara, foremost among the śrāvakas. Krakucchanda Tathāgata, Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened One, had a great-wisdom disciple named Saṃjīva, foremost among the śrāvakas. Kanakamuni Tathāgata, Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened One, had a great-wisdom disciple named Surūpottara, foremost among the śrāvakas. Kāśyapa Tathāgata, Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened One, had a great-wisdom disciple named Bharadvāja, foremost among the śrāvakas. I, the present Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened One, have a great-wisdom disciple named Śāriputra, foremost among the śrāvakas."

At that time, the World-Honored One again spoke in verse:

> Vipaśyin Tathāgata had a son of great wisdom
> Named Khaṇḍadīpya. Śikhin Buddha, the World-Honored One,
> Had a son of great wisdom named Bhūsaṃbhava.
> Viśvabhū Tathāgata had a son of great wisdom
> Named Yaśottara. Krakucchanda Tathāgata
> Had a son of great wisdom named Saṃjīva.
> Kanakamuni had a son of great wisdom,
> Surūpottara. Kāśyapa Buddha, the World-Honored One,
> Had a son of great wisdom, Bharadvāja.
> I, the present Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened, have a son of great wisdom
> Named Śāriputra. These sons of the seven Buddhas
> Were each, among all the śrāvakas, foremost.

At that time, the World-Honored One, having spoken this verse, told the assembly of bhikṣus: "Listen carefully! I shall now further explain the attendant disciples of the seven Tathāgatas. Vipaśyin Tathāgata, Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened One, had an attendant named Aśoka. Śikhin Tathāgata, Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened One, had an attendant named Kṣamakāru. Viśvabhū Buddha, Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened One, had an attendant named Upaśānta. Krakucchanda Buddha, Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened One, had an attendant named Buddhiyu. Kanakamuni Buddha, Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened One, had an attendant named Surūpatrīya. Kāśyapa Tathāgata, Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened One, had an attendant named Sarvamitra. I, the present Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened One, have an attendant named Ānanda." At that time, the World-Honored One again spoke in verse:

> The Buddha's son Aśoka, Kṣamakāru and the rest,
> Together with Upaśānta and Buddhiyu,
> Surūpatrīya and Sarvamitra,
> And that Ānanda: all were attendants to the Buddhas.
> Always practicing the mind of loving-kindness and compassion,
> Accomplishing samādhi,
> Penetrating the marks of all dharmas, endowed with great wisdom,
> Learned extensively and of keen intelligence, great teachers of the Dharma,
> Foremost among the assembly, renowned throughout the ten directions.
> Gods and humans all paid them respect; the power of their diligence was firm;
> They severed all afflictions and realized the unborn and undying.
> Attending upon the World-Honored Buddhas, they always obtained benefit for themselves.
> These Tathāgatas accomplished true sons of the Buddha.

At that time, the World-Honored One, having spoken this verse, told the assembly of bhikṣus: "Listen carefully! I shall now further explain the assemblies of śrāvaka disciples liberated by the seven Tathāgatas, Worthy Ones, Perfectly Enlightened Ones. When Vipaśyin Tathāgata taught the Dharma at the first assembly, sixty-two thousand bhikṣus attained the fruit of arhatship. When he taught the Dharma at the second assembly, one hundred thousand bhikṣus attained the fruit of arhatship. When he taught the Dharma at the third assembly, eighty thousand bhikṣus attained the fruit of arhatship. When Śikhin Tathāgata taught the Dharma at the first assembly, one hundred thousand bhikṣus attained the fruit of arhatship. When he taught the Dharma at the second assembly, eighty koṭi of bhikṣus attained the fruit of arhatship. When he taught the Dharma at the third assembly, seventy thousand bhikṣus attained the fruit of arhatship. When Viśvabhū Tathāgata taught the Dharma at the first assembly, eighty thousand bhikṣus attained the fruit of arhatship. When he taught the Dharma at the second assembly, seventy thousand bhikṣus attained the fruit of arhatship. When he taught the Dharma at the third assembly, sixty thousand bhikṣus attained the fruit of arhatship.

"When Krakucchanda Tathāgata taught the Dharma at a single assembly, forty thousand bhikṣus attained arhatship. When Kanakamuni Buddha taught the Dharma at a single assembly, thirty thousand bhikṣus attained the fruit of arhatship. When Kāśyapa Tathāgata taught the Dharma at a single assembly, twenty thousand bhikṣus attained the fruit of arhatship. When I taught the Dharma at a single assembly, one thousand two hundred and fifty bhikṣus attained the fruit of arhatship."

At that time, the World-Honored One again spoke in verse:

> Vipaśyin Tathāgata, Śikhin, Viśvabhū,
> Krakucchanda World-Honored One, Kanakamuni, Kāśyapa,
> And Śākyamuni: when each appeared in the world,
> The śrāvaka assemblies they liberated numbered seventy koṭi,
> Plus ninety-three thousand two hundred and fifty persons;
> All attained arhatship and would not receive further existence.

At that time, the World-Honored One, having spoken this verse, told the assembly of bhikṣus: "Listen carefully! Vipaśyin Buddha, Śikhin Buddha, Viśvabhū Buddha, and so on down to my own present appearance in the world: in maintaining the teachings, proclaiming the Dharma, subduing sentient beings, and establishing the precepts and rules of conduct, as well as in holding the robes and bowls and seeking to realize bodhi, there is not the slightest difference among us."

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> In these past and present kalpas, Vipaśyin and the other Buddhas,
> The many bhikṣus they liberated all accomplished great wisdom.
> They diligently cultivated the correct path, the factors of bodhi:
> The five faculties and five powers, the four mindfulnesses and four bases of spiritual power,
> The seven factors of enlightenment and the eightfold noble path, together with samādhi.
> Serene were their faculties such as the eye; they penetrated the treasury of the Dharma,
> Awakening all living beings and nurturing the life of wisdom.
> In this Fortunate Kalpa, all praised this as unprecedented.
> The Buddhas, with great compassion and wisdom, themselves awakened and awakened others;
> With majestic virtue and great supernatural powers, all that they taught was thus.

At that time, the World-Honored One, having spoken this verse, together with the bhikṣus rose from their seats and returned to the Garden of Anāthapiṇḍada. When this night had passed, at the break of dawn,

the assembly of bhikṣus went from their dwellings to the Kālī assembly hall and questioned one another: "The Tathāgatas of the past entered the great nirvāṇa, departed from all idle speculation, and forever severed the cycle of rebirth, without any fault. Such great beings, with such wisdom, such discipline, such samādhi, such liberation, such majestic virtue, such lineage, descended into the world to benefit beings. This is extremely rare and inconceivable."

At that time, the World-Honored One knew what the bhikṣus were thinking. He arose from his seat, went to the Kālī assembly hall, sat in full lotus posture, and told the assembly of bhikṣus: "What are you thinking about?" The bhikṣus then addressed the World-Honored One: "We have heard that the Tathāgatas of the past entered the great nirvāṇa, departed from all idle speculation, forever severed the cycle of rebirth, and were without any fault. Such great beings, with such wisdom, such discipline, such samādhi, such liberation, such majestic virtue, such lineage, descended into the world to benefit beings. This is extremely rare and inconceivable." The Buddha said: "Bhikṣus, what you have said, what is the reason for it?" The bhikṣus said: "The Buddha possesses the pure Dharma-realm and has realized the wisdom of true enlightenment; there is nothing he does not fully know. We wish you would explain it for us."

The Buddha said: "Bhikṣus, listen carefully, and I shall explain. In a past age, there was a great king named Bandhumā, and that king's queen was named Bandhumatī. At that time, Vipaśyin Buddha descended from Tuṣita Heaven to Jambudvīpa and entered his mother's womb, dwelling in the womb-treasury. He emitted a great radiance that illuminated all the worlds, leaving no place in darkness. Even the evil destinies and all the hells, where the majesty of the sun and moon cannot reach, were suddenly flooded with great brightness by the Buddha's light. The beings there could see one another and cried out: 'Why are there other beings here in this place?'"

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> When the Bodhisattva descended from Tuṣita to Jambudvīpa,
> Like a cloud and like the wind, in an instant he reached his mother's womb.
> His body emitted a great radiance, illuminating the realms of gods and humans;
> The hells and the Iron Ring Mountains were all freed from darkness.
> The manifold realms of the Buddha-lands, as he dwelt within his mother's body;
> Thus the great assembly of sages also came together to gather there.

At that time, the World-Honored One, having spoken this verse, told the assembly of bhikṣus: "Listen carefully! When this Bodhisattva Mahāsattva descended from Tuṣita Heaven to Jambudvīpa and entered his mother's womb, the yakṣa Bhūma cried out in a loud voice: 'This great Bodhisattva, great being of majestic virtue, has relinquished the bodies of gods, humans, and asuras, and now dwells in this mother's womb to receive a human body.' Likewise, the gods of the Four Heavenly Kings, the Trāyastriṃśa Heaven, the Yāma Heaven, and so on up to the Brahmā Attendant gods, hearing this proclamation that the Bodhisattva had descended into his mother's womb, all came to gather there."

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> When the Bodhisattva descended from Tuṣita into his mother's womb,
> The great yakṣa Bhūma proclaimed that the Bodhisattva
> Had abandoned his celestial body and his asura form
> To dwell in this mother's womb and receive the retribution of a human life.
> The Four Kings, Trāyastriṃśa, Yāma, and Tuṣita,
> And so on up to the Brahmā world, all heard this news:
> The Bodhisattva has descended to the human realm, of subtle, true golden hue.
> All the gods came to gather there, their hearts filled with great joy and celebration.

At that time, the World-Honored One, having spoken this verse, told the assembly of bhikṣus: "Listen carefully! When this Bodhisattva Mahāsattva descended from Tuṣita Heaven to Jambudvīpa, four great heavenly sons, endowed with majestic virtue, their bodies clad in armor and wielding bows and swords, guarded the Bodhisattva so that neither humans nor non-humans could do him harm."

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> When the Bodhisattva was born, Indra of Trāyastriṃśa Heaven
> Dispatched those four heavenly kings, each endowed with great power;
> Their bodies clad in golden armor, wielding bows, swords, and spears,
> They constantly stood guard. Rākṣasas and non-humans
> Dared not cause any harm. Dwelling peacefully in his mother's womb,
> As if in a great palace, he constantly enjoyed bliss.

At that time, the World-Honored One, having spoken this verse, told the bhikṣus: "Listen carefully! When this Bodhisattva Mahāsattva descended from Tuṣita Heaven to Jambudvīpa and dwelt in his mother's womb, his body was pure and radiant, like a maṇi jewel, and his mother's mind was at peace, free from all feverish affliction."

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> When the Bodhisattva dwelt in the womb, he was pure and without blemish,
> Like a vaiḍūrya gem, like a maṇi jewel,
> His radiance illuminated the world, like the sun emerging from the clouds;
> He accomplished the highest meaning and gave rise to the supreme wisdom.
> His mother was free from worry and affliction and always practiced meritorious deeds;
> All beings looked up to her with reverence. She abided peacefully among the kṣatriyas.

At that time, the World-Honored One, having spoken this verse, told the assembly of bhikṣus: "Listen carefully! When this Bodhisattva Mahāsattva descended from Tuṣita Heaven to Jambudvīpa and dwelt in his mother's womb, it was never heard that the mother had any attachment to sensual desire. She was free from attachment to the five sense-objects such as form." At that time, the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> When the Bodhisattva dwelt in the womb, he caused his mother's mind to be pure;
> She did not hear the name of defilement, and was far removed from the faults of the five desires;
> The roots of craving and attachment were severed, and she did not experience suffering or affliction;
> Her body and mind were always at peace, and she constantly enjoyed happiness.

At that time, the World-Honored One, having spoken this verse, told the assembly of bhikṣus: "Listen carefully! When this Bodhisattva Mahāsattva descended from Tuṣita Heaven to Jambudvīpa and dwelt in his mother's womb, the mother herself upheld the five precepts of a lay devotee: first, not killing; second, not stealing; third, not engaging in sexual misconduct; fourth, not speaking falsely; fifth, not drinking intoxicants. The Bodhisattva was born from her right side. After the mother's life ended, she was reborn in the heavenly realm."

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> While the Bodhisattva dwelt in the womb, his mother herself upheld the five precepts;
> The child was born from her right side, free from all suffering and affliction.
> Just as the heavenly lord Indra enjoys the exquisite five pleasures,
> After her life ended, she quickly attained rebirth in heaven.

At that time, the World-Honored One, having spoken this verse, told the assembly of bhikṣus: "Listen carefully! When this Bodhisattva Mahāsattva was born from the right side, the great earth trembled. His body was the color of true gold, free from all defilement. He emitted a great radiance that universally illuminated all the realms of the world. All the evil destinies and dark hells were suddenly flooded with great brightness, and the beings within them could see one another. Each wondered: 'Why are there other beings here in this place?'"

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> When the Bodhisattva was born, the great earth trembled;
> His body's marks were like true gold, unstained by any dust or impurity.
> With majestic virtue and great supernatural powers, his radiance illuminated all;
> Beings shrouded in karmic darkness could see one another.

At that time, the World-Honored One, having spoken this verse, told the assembly of bhikṣus: "Listen carefully! When this Bodhisattva Mahāsattva was born from the right side, his mother felt no fatigue; she neither sat nor reclined. At that time, the Bodhisattva, the great being of majestic virtue, whose mind was free from confusion and whose feet did not touch the ground, was received by four great heavenly sons who held up the child's body."

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> When the Bodhisattva was born, his mother's mind was free from distraction;
> She neither sat nor reclined, but was at ease and content.
> The great being of majestic virtue, his mind free from all darkness,
> Four heavenly sons held up his body; his feet did not touch the ground.

At that time, the World-Honored One, having spoken this verse, told the assembly of bhikṣus: "Listen carefully! When this Bodhisattva Mahāsattva was born from the right side, his body was pure like a vaiḍūrya jewel, far removed from all impure substances such as pus, blood, mucus, and saliva. Like a maṇi jewel or a Kauśika garment, no dust or filth could adhere to it."

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> When the Bodhisattva was born, his body and vessel were completely pure,
> Far removed from all foul and impure things such as pus, blood, mucus, and saliva.
> Just as a Kauśika garment or a maṇi jewel,
> Lustrous and bright of body, no dust or filth could cling to it.

At that time, the World-Honored One, having spoken this verse, told the assembly of bhikṣus: "Listen carefully! When this Bodhisattva Mahāsattva was born from the right side, two heavenly sons poured down two kinds of water from the sky, one cool and one warm, to bathe the child."

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> When the Bodhisattva was born, two gods appeared in the sky
> To bathe the child's body, pouring down two kinds of water,
> Warm and cool, each perfectly suited, signifying the perfection of merit and wisdom,
> Accomplishing great fearlessness and regarding all beings universally.

At that time, the World-Honored One, having spoken this verse, told the assembly of bhikṣus: "Listen carefully! When this Bodhisattva Mahāsattva was born from the right side, he was endowed with the thirty-two marks. His form was dignified and beautiful, his eye-faculty was pure, and he could see for ten yojanas."

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> When the Bodhisattva was born, all his marks were complete;
> His eyes were pure and his form dignified; he could see afar for ten yojanas.

At that time, the World-Honored One, having spoken this verse, told the assembly of bhikṣus: "When this Bodhisattva Mahāsattva was born from the right side, he could see the Trāyastriṃśa Heaven. The lord Indra of that heaven, seeing that this child was a true Buddha-body, took up a white parasol to cover the child's body, so that cold, heat, wind, dust, and all manner of harm could not touch him."

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> When the child was first born, he gazed afar toward Trāyastriṃśa;
> Indra beheld him and, holding a white parasol,
> Came at once to cover his body. Cold, heat, wind, sun,
> And all manner of poisons and evils could not touch him at all.

At that time, the World-Honored One, having spoken this verse, told the assembly of bhikṣus: "Listen carefully! When this Bodhisattva Mahāsattva was born from the right side, his birth mother, wet nurse, and nursemaid, along with the palace women, surrounded and protected him, bathing him, anointing him with fragrance, and attending to him in every way."

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> When the child was first born, three mothers nursed and raised him,
> And the palace women on all sides constantly surrounded him,
> Bathing him and anointing him with fragrance, keeping him always at peace;
> Thus day and night, they never left him for a moment.

At that time, the World-Honored One, having spoken this verse, told the assembly of bhikṣus: "Listen carefully! When this Bodhisattva Mahāsattva was born from the right side, his form was dignified and beautiful, endowed with the thirty-two marks. King Bandhumā summoned physiognomists to examine the child. The brāhmaṇas said: 'If he remains a householder, he will receive the consecration of a wheel-turning king, ruling over the four continents, endowed with a thousand sons, majestic and fearless, able to defeat rival armies without resort to weapons, bows, or swords. If he leaves the household life and practices with firm resolve, he will attain perfect, complete enlightenment.'"

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> The physiognomist brāhmaṇas examined this heavenly child
> And told his father the king: "He is endowed with the thirty-two marks,
> Like the moon emerging among the stars; in the world this is extremely rare.
> If he always remains in the palace, he will surely succeed to the position of wheel-turning king,
> Ruling over the four great continents and fathering a thousand princes.
> If he leaves the household life, he will swiftly attain the unsurpassed enlightenment."

At that time, the World-Honored One, having spoken this verse, told the assembly of bhikṣus: "Listen carefully! When this Bodhisattva Mahāsattva was born from the right side, his body was the color of true gold, with dignified and beautiful marks. Like a water-born lotus, no dust or filth could adhere to it; all sentient beings gazed upon him insatiably. His voice was subtle, exquisite, refined, and melodious. Just as the kalaviṅka bird on the Snowy Mountains, intoxicated from feeding on flowers, produces sounds that are elegant, exquisite, clear, and resonant, and no being who hears them does not delight in them, so too was the child's voice."

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> When the child was first born, his bodily marks were the color of true gold,
> Like a red lotus flower, unstained by dust or filth.
> His voice was subtle and exquisite, like the sound of the kalaviṅka;
> All who heard it loved it insatiably.

At that time, the World-Honored One, having spoken this verse, told the assembly of bhikṣus: "Listen carefully! When this Bodhisattva Mahāsattva was a child, he was far removed from falsehood and delusion. His mind and intentions were pure and straightforward; he awakened himself and awakened others, always practicing the correct Dharma. The people attended upon him as upon the heavenly lord Indra, and he honored his parents. For this reason he was called Vipaśyin."

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> Vipaśyin Tathāgata, when he was a child,
> Penetrated great wisdom, far removed from falsehood and delusion.
> Awakening himself and awakening others, he cultivated the practice of the correct Dharma;
> The people always loved and respected him, as they do the heavenly Indra.
> He waited upon his parents, and his renown filled the world;
> This is the one named Vipaśyin, who benefits all sentient beings.
