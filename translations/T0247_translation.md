# 佛說了義般若波羅蜜多經
## Sūtra of the Definitive Meaning of Prajñāpāramitā Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0247

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dānapāla (施護)

---

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the venerable Śāriputra: "You should now know that all bodhisattva-mahāsattvas who wish to practice the conduct corresponding to prajñāpāramitā should truly understand all dharmas as they really are, and in all their actions, be free from all characteristics."

At that time, the venerable Śāriputra joined his palms in reverence and said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! As the World-Honored One has said, for all bodhisattva-mahāsattvas who wish to practice the conduct corresponding to prajñāpāramitā, how should they understand the self-nature of all dharmas? And in all their actions, how should they be free from characteristics?"

The Buddha said: "Śāriputra! If bodhisattva-mahāsattvas wish to perfect the corresponding supreme conduct and be free from the characteristics of action with respect to all dharmas, they should understand that all dharmas abide in non-abiding. Then they can perfect the corresponding supreme conduct.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra! If bodhisattva-mahāsattvas, with respect to all dharmas, practice the practice of giving, there is no giver, no recipient, and giving obtains nothing. If it is thus, they can perfect the pāramitā of generosity. Furthermore, those who practice the dharma of morality: there is no upholder, nothing upheld, and no arising of action. If it is thus, they can perfect the pāramitā of morality. Furthermore, those who practice the dharma of patience: among all dharmas there is no movement or turning, and they are free from all arising of action. If it is thus, they can perfect the pāramitā of patience. Furthermore, in the corresponding practice, one should diligently cultivate: whether of body or mind, there is no weariness or laxity, no characteristic of arising action. If it is thus, one can perfect the pāramitā of diligence. Furthermore, among all dharmas there is no scattering, and one is free from the characteristic of attainment. If it is thus, one can perfect the pāramitā of meditation.

"Śāriputra! If bodhisattva-mahāsattvas wish to abide in the conduct corresponding to prajñāpāramitā, they should perfect the four foundations of mindfulness, the four right efforts, the four bases of supernatural power, the five faculties, the five powers, the seven factors of awakening, and the dharmas of the eightfold noble path.

"Furthermore, one should contemplate the samādhi of emptiness, the samādhi of signlessness, the samādhi of wishlessness, the four dhyānas, the four immeasurables, the four formless absorptions, the eight liberations, the nine preliminary practices, and the nine contemplations. What are the nine contemplations? They are: the contemplation of internal dharmas, the contemplation of the bloated corpse, the contemplation free from redness, the contemplation free from blueness, the contemplation of the putrefied and scattered, the contemplation of non-abiding, the contemplation free from scattering, the contemplation free from burning affliction, and the contemplation free from food and drink. These are called the nine contemplations.

"Furthermore, the contemplation of recollecting the Buddha, the contemplation of recollecting the Dharma, the contemplation of recollecting the Saṅgha, the contemplations of recollecting morality, generosity, and the heavens, the contemplation free from afflictions, the contemplation of recollecting arising and ceasing, the contemplation of recollecting impermanence, suffering, and non-self, the contemplation of recollecting that all worldly things are not ultimate, the contemplation of the knowledge of suffering, the knowledge of origination, the knowledge of cessation, the knowledge of the path, the knowledge of exhaustion, the knowledge of non-arising, the knowledge of dharmas, the knowledge of non-self, the knowledge of harmony, the knowledge of reality, the contemplation of verbal discrimination, the contemplation free from verbal discrimination, the contemplation of the faculty of knowing-what-is-not-yet-known, the contemplation of the faculty of already-knowing, the contemplation of the faculty of complete knowledge, the contemplation of impurity, the contemplation of purity, the contemplation of śamatha and vipaśyanā, the contemplation of the three knowledges, the contemplation of the four comprehensions, the contemplation of the four fearlessnesses, the contemplation of the five supernatural powers, the contemplation of the six pāramitās, the contemplation of the seven kinds of mental abiding, the contemplation of the eight dharmas of the great person, the contemplation of the nine abodes of sentient beings, the contemplation of the Tathāgata's ten powers, the contemplation of the eighteen unshared dharmas, the contemplation of great loving-kindness, the contemplation of great compassion, and up to the contemplation of the knowledge of all-knowledge. With respect to all such dharmas, one should contemplate in this way.

"Furthermore, bodhisattva-mahāsattvas who wish to perfect all-knowledge and the knowledge of all aspects should truly contemplate prajñāpāramitā.

"Furthermore, if one wishes to perfect the knowledge of the path and its aspects, the knowledge of all aspects, to comprehend the mental dispositions and characteristics of all sentient beings, and to sever all the defilements of all sentient beings, one should practice the supreme conduct corresponding to prajñāpāramitā.

"Śāriputra! As I have explained above regarding the gateways of contemplating all dharmas, bodhisattva-mahāsattvas should study in this way."

At that time, the venerable Śāriputra again said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! For bodhisattva-mahāsattvas who practice prajñāpāramitā, what dharmas should they sever?"

The Buddha said: "Śāriputra! Those who practice prajñāpāramitā should sever ten kinds of doubt. What are the ten? They are: doubt about existence, doubt about non-existence, doubt about the differentiation of dharmas, doubt involving slander, doubt about a single dharma, doubt about many dharmas, doubt about sameness and difference, doubt of the highest grade, doubt about names, and doubt about names and meanings. These ten doubts should be severed. If it is thus, then the bodhisattva-mahāsattva has nothing to observe with respect to all characteristics. Because characteristics are without observation, names are also without observation. Prajñāpāramitā is also without observation. Mental formations are without observation. Form is without observation. Feeling, perception, volitional formations, and consciousness are all without observation. Why? Because the self-nature of form is empty, and emptiness is apart from nature. The essence of form is itself emptiness; apart from form there is no separate emptiness. The essence of emptiness is itself form; apart from emptiness there is no separate form. What does this mean? It is due to the discrimination of names.

"Śāriputra! You should know that the self-nature of form does not arise and does not cease, is neither defiled nor pure. The self-nature of that name is also not a conditioned dharma. It is free from all doubts, comes from nowhere and abides nowhere, arises from reality, and is free from the three periods of time. Form is thus, and feeling, perception, volitional formations, and consciousness are likewise. Therefore, bodhisattva-mahāsattvas have nothing to observe with respect to all names and characteristics. Because there is nothing to observe, there is nothing to enter. To have nothing to enter is to perfect the supreme conduct corresponding to prajñāpāramitā."
