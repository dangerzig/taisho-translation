# 千臂軍荼利梵字真言
## Brahmi-Script Dhāraṇī of the Thousand-Armed Kuṇḍali

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1213

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, recorded by Yicao (義操)

---

The dhāraṇī of the Thousand-Armed Kuṇḍali:

*[Brahmi script: Dhāraṇī of the Thousand-Armed Kuṇḍali]*

Recorded in Brahmi script by the late Mahācārya Yicao, Inner Imperially Appointed Preceptor of Dharma Transmission and Abhiṣeka, of the Eastern Stūpa Courtyard of Qinglong Monastery of the Great Tang.

---

**Translator's note:** This text is essentially a colophon frame. The mantra itself was transmitted in Siddham (Brahmi) script rather than Chinese characters; the Taishō edition preserves only the surrounding Chinese apparatus. Yicao (義操, d. 820) was a Tang esoteric master at Qinglong Monastery in Chang'an, lineage disciple of Huiguo (惠果) and thus a Dharma-brother of Kūkai. Kuṇḍali (軍荼利) is one of the Five Wisdom Kings (*pañcavidyārāja*) of the Vajrayāna pantheon, associated with the south and with purification.
