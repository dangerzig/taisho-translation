# 佛說百佛名經
## Sūtra of One Hundred Buddha Names Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0444

Translated from the Chinese. Sui dynasty, translated by Narendrayaśas (那連提耶舍)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the country of Śrāvastī, in the Jetavana grove of Anāthapiṇḍada's park, together with great bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, upāsikās, a great assembly of bodhisattvas, and the great deva Śakra, the great Brahmā king, the four great heavenly kings, devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, humans and non-humans, and an immeasurable multitude of hundreds of thousands, all gathered around, making offerings with respect, honor, and praise. At that time, the World-Honored One was teaching the wondrous Dharma for the great assembly.

Then the Venerable Śāriputra rose from his seat, straightened his garments, placed his right knee on the ground, joined his ten fingers together, and addressed the Buddha: "May the World-Honored One proclaim the names of the presently existing Buddhas of the worlds in the ten directions. Why? Because if good men and good women hear the names of these presently existing Buddhas, they will produce great merit, give rise to the aspiration for anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi, attain non-retrogression, and also swiftly accomplish anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi."

At that time, the Buddha told Śāriputra: "Excellent! Excellent! You now, wishing to benefit and bring happiness to the great assembly, to shelter and have compassion for all sentient beings, to cause all sentient beings to have their wishes fulfilled, to cause all to give rise to joy, and also for the sake of bodhisattvas of the future to increase their wholesome roots: excellent! Excellent! You have given rise to a good and discerning intention and asked such a question. Such a question arises entirely from the Tathāgata's awesome spiritual power. Śāriputra! Listen carefully. If good men and good women hear the names of these presently existing Buddhas and can receive and uphold them, all the māra multitudes will not be able to take advantage of such persons, nor will any evil people. They will attain immeasurable, boundless, and profoundly deep merit. Wherever they are born, they will be complete in the bodhisattva practices, attain the knowledge of past lives, be beautiful in appearance and complete in all marks, and always be able to draw near to and make offerings to the Buddhas, until they swiftly accomplish anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi. Why? Śāriputra! Because if there are those who hear these Buddhas' names and receive, uphold, read, recite, bow in reverence, copy, and make offerings, and also instruct others to do so, the merit attained is immeasurable and boundless."

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> If one can uphold these Buddhas' names,
> One will not be harmed by swords;
> Poison cannot harm, fire cannot burn,
> And one will not fall into the eight difficult conditions.
> One will see the great-wisdom golden radiance,
> The Dharma-kings with their thirty-two marks,
> And having beheld all these Buddhas,
> One will make immeasurable offerings to them.
> One's eyes will not be blind, red, or yellow;
> The body will not be crooked or one-eyed.
> One attains Nārāyaṇa's great strength:
> Such is the reward of upholding the Buddhas' names.
> One is always honored and offered to
> By devas, nāgas, and yakṣas,
> Gandharvas and the rest.
> Enemies and evil people cannot harm:
> Such is the reward of upholding the Buddhas' names.
> Listen carefully now, Śāriputra!
> To the wondrous words I speak:
> If there are those who hear these Buddhas' names,
> They draw near to the path of bodhi.
> Therefore with your whole heart listen now
> To the great Dharma-kings of the ten-direction worlds,
> Who can pluck out the thorns of sentient beings' afflictions,
> Like a medicine tree that cures all illness.
> To the deluded, blind, and ordinary beings,
> They bestow the bodhisattva's eye of wisdom.
> Like a bodhisattva who practices supreme generosity
> In immeasurable thousands of billions of Ganges-sand worlds,
> Filling them all with Jambu gold,
> Day and night, six times, without rest,
> Giving to the great compassionate guide;
> And also filling a hundred lands with candana,
> With banners, canopies, and garments like the Ganges sands,
> For immeasurable thousands of millions of billions of kalpas,
> Making offerings single-mindedly;
> And moreover in a single buddha-land
> Filling it with stūpas of the Buddhas,
> Making offerings for ten kalpas
> To Ganges-sand numbers of Buddhas;
> And also building tall stūpas like Mount Sumeru,
> All made of the seven treasures,
> Filling ten thousand lands in this way,
> Numbering thirty-six billion,
> With red candana and true pearls
> Making parasol canopies and offering implements,
> Each parasol covering
> A hundred buddha-worlds and their lands,
> With pearl tassels reaching to the buddha-lands,
> Their number like the Ganges sands,
> And in the sky making golden parasol canopies,
> Also like the Ganges sands in number,
> Their color like purple-gold images,
> Each illuminating the three-thousand worlds,
> With offerings at each stūpa
> For immeasurable, boundless, innumerable kalpas:
> All this merit
> Does not equal giving rise to the bodhi-mind.
> And giving rise to the bodhi-mind,
> Abiding in non-negligence and pure precepts,
> Does not equal upholding these Buddhas' names.
> And upholding these Buddhas' names
> While also having compassion for sentient beings,
> Broadly circulating in all directions,
> Causing others to receive and uphold the Buddhas' names:
> Such a person among all sentient beings
> Becomes a field of merit, equal to the Tathāgata.

At that time, the Buddha told Śāriputra: "If there are those who single-mindedly receive, uphold, read, recite, and never forget these Buddhas' names, then any greed, anger, delusion, and fears that have arisen will be immediately eliminated, and any greed, anger, delusion, and fears not yet arisen will be prevented from arising."

At that time, the World-Honored One proclaimed the Buddhas' names:

> These Buddhas' names and the rest
> Can save and protect the world.
> In the first watch, recite once;
> Contemplate the Buddhas and then sleep.
> In the middle watch, recite once;
> In the last watch, likewise.
> So too during the day's three periods,
> In the first, middle, and last,
> Recite once each time,
> With diligence and without negligence.

> Through reciting these Buddhas' names,
> One always has good dreams.
> Evil ghosts and evil people
> Cannot take advantage.
> Therefore these Buddhas' names
> Can save and protect the world.
> All music-loving devas, yakṣas, kumbhāṇḍas,
> Rākṣasas and various ghosts and spirits
> Cannot cause obstruction.

> Those who constantly recite and uphold
> These one hundred Buddhas' names:
> All works of the māra
> Cannot take advantage of them.

> At that time, Śakra, Lord of the Devas,
> King of the Trāyastriṃśa,
> Straightened his garments,
> Knelt and joined his palms,
> And addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One!
> We shall always guard and protect
> Those who uphold the Buddhas' names.
> And the four great heavenly kings
> Will also constantly protect
> Those who uphold the Buddhas' names.
> Only fixed karma
> Cannot be rescued;
> Among all devas and humans,
> None can cause them evil."

> At that time, the All-Knowing One
> Emitted a great, subtle voice,
> With eight kinds of harmonious and elegant tones,
> Beautiful and delightful to hear,
> And universally told the great assembly:
> "Swiftly receive these Buddhas' names!
> In the ten-direction worlds,
> Circulate and spread them everywhere,
> Performing the great gift of Dharma,
> And severing the doubts of sentient beings."

> When the great sage Śākyamuni
> Proclaimed this Dharma,
> Three hundred bhikṣus
> All attained the complete exhaustion of the outflows.
> Also bhikṣuṇīs,
> Numbering forty,
> All exhausted the outflows
> And attained arhatship.
> Also upāsakas,
> Numbering a full five thousand,
> With supremely joyful minds,
> Attained the fruit of stream-entry.
> Also upāsikās,
> Numbering eleven hundred,
> Were all freed from dust and defilement
> And attained the pure Dharma-eye.
> Also great deva kings,
> In immeasurable thousands of myriads,
> In the Dharma-king's Dharma,
> Attained the pure Dharma-eye.
> Like the sands of the Ganges,
> Divided into three parts:
> Bodhisattvas equal to one part
> All attained the patience of non-arising.

> The three-thousand great-thousand land
> At that time shook six ways.
> All mountains and high peaks,
> And the great earth all trembled and roared.

> In the sky above,
> Wondrous heavenly flowers rained down.
> Devas, nāgas, non-humans, and the rest:
> Joyful voices filled all directions.

> At that time, Śakra Lord of the Devas,
> Maudgalyāyana, Revata,
> Śroṇaparāntaputra, Kapphiṇa,
> Mahākātyāyana,
> And Mahākāśyapa, Uruvilvā-Kāśyapa,
> Up to Nadī-Kāśyapa,
> Pūrṇamaitrāyaṇīputra,
> Subhūti, Aniruddha,
> Ānanda, Bhadrika,
> and Dravya-Mallaputra:
> Such great ones in the assembly,
> Twenty-five thousand persons,
> Addressed the Buddha in unison:
> "Lion of the Śākyas, Dharma-king!
> We shall exhaust our diligent powers,
> All without remainder,
> And with all our supernatural powers,
> In immeasurable worlds,
> Manifesting immeasurable bodies,
> Passing through immeasurable buddha-lands,
> We shall constantly proclaim these Buddhas' names,
> Just as the Buddha has taught."

> At that time, the Buddha World-Honored One,
> To cause the assembly to see,
> Displayed great supernatural powers,
> Manifesting immeasurable billions of lands.
> Through the Buddha's supernatural power,
> They saw the Lion of the Śākyas
> Pervading all places,
> Teaching this sūtra,
> Just as the past Buddhas
> Had taught without any difference.

> The Buddha told the great assembly:
> "Throughout immeasurable kalpas,
> I have long and well cultivated supernatural powers.
> Concerning the unsurpassed Buddha-Dharma,
> You should have no doubt.
> For the peace and security of sentient beings,
> I have swiftly proclaimed these Buddhas' names,
> Causing all sentient beings
> To forever leave the suffering of birth and death."

At that time, the Buddha told the Venerable Śāriputra: "If good men and good women, having heard the Buddhas' names, develop deep and pure faith, give rise to the bodhi-mind, delight in receiving and upholding them, and with day and night diligence read, recite, copy, and broadly teach them to others, they will attain immeasurable, boundless, and vast merit, attain non-retrogression, and swiftly accomplish anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi. All the māra multitudes will be unable to disturb them."

When the Buddha had finished teaching this sūtra, the Venerable Śāriputra, Śakra, Brahmā, the four heavenly kings, bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, upāsikās, devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, and the rest, the entire great assembly, having heard this Dharma, all greatly rejoiced.
