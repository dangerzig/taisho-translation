# 八吉祥經
## Sūtra of the Eight Auspicious Ones

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0430

Translated from the Chinese. Liang dynasty, translated by Sanghapāla (僧伽婆羅)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was dwelling in the country of Śrāvastī, in the Jetavana grove of Anāthapiṇḍada's park, together with one thousand two hundred and fifty great bhikṣus, eighty thousand bodhisattva-mahāsattvas, and various devas, nāgas, ghosts, spirits, and others.

At that time, the World-Honored One told Śāriputra: "From this buddha-world, going east past one Ganges-sand of worlds, there is a world called Heaven-Victorious. In that land there is a Buddha named Excellent Speech Auspicious Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, teaching the Dharma.

"Śāriputra! From that buddha-world, going east past two Ganges-sands of worlds, there is a world called Mindful Intention. In that land there is a Buddha named Universal Radiance Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, teaching the Dharma.

"Śāriputra! From that buddha-world, going east past three Ganges-sands of worlds, there is a world called Beloved Delight and Play. In that land there is a Buddha named Victorious in Battle Auspicious Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, teaching the Dharma.

"Śāriputra! From that world, going east past four Ganges-sands of worlds, there is a world called Excellent Purity Assembly. In that land there is a Buddha named Sovereign Banner King Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, teaching the Dharma.

"Śāriputra! From that buddha-world, going east past five Ganges-sands of worlds, there is a world called Dustless Assembly. In that land there is a Buddha named Boundless Merit Radiance Auspicious Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, teaching the Dharma.

"Śāriputra! From that buddha-world, going east past six Ganges-sands of worlds, there is a world called Unobstructed Play. In that land there is a Buddha named Unobstructed Pillar of Deeds Auspicious Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, teaching the Dharma.

"Śāriputra! From that buddha-world, going east past seven Ganges-sands of worlds, there is a world called Gold Assembly. In that land there is a Buddha named Wondrous Flower Courageous Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, teaching the Dharma.

"Śāriputra! From that buddha-world, going east past eight Ganges-sands of worlds, there is a world called Beautiful Sound. In that land there is a Buddha named Precious Lotus Peacefully Abiding King Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, teaching the Dharma.

"Śāriputra! These eight buddha-lands are all pure. There are no women and no five turbidities.

"Śāriputra! If good men and good women who abide in the Great Vehicle hear the names of these eight Buddhas, receive and uphold them without forgetting, and also explain them for others, that they should fall into the three evil paths or the eight difficult conditions is impossible. If there are women who hear the names of the eight Buddhas and can remember and uphold them, and also explain them for others, that they should again receive a female body is impossible.

"Śāriputra! If good men and good women who abide in the Great Vehicle hear the names of these eight Buddhas, and having heard them, receive, uphold, and explain them for others, then in this life and future lives they will always have supernatural powers, attain the eloquence of joyful teaching, deeply cultivate dhyāna concentration, and be complete in dhāraṇī. Their six sense-faculties will be pure, and they will always encounter sages. They will have no fear of devas, nāgas, yakṣas, humans or non-humans, thieves, water, fire, poison, or any other terrors. All fears will be entirely eliminated. In sleep and waking they will always be at peace, without any nightmares, and the devas will always protect them."

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke these verses:

> If there are good men
> And also good women
> Who hear the eight Buddhas' names,
> Remember, uphold, and explain them for others,
> Their bodies and minds will always be at peace,
> Without any terrors.

> Keeping mindful without forgetting,
> Immeasurable kalpas of sin are eliminated.
> Later born among devas and humans,
> Far from all evil destinies.

> The six sense-faculties always pure,
> With dignified and majestic virtue,
> The eight divisions of good spirits
> Constantly protect them through the night.

> Honored by devas and humans,
> Offerings are made with joyful delight.
> Yakṣas and non-humans,
> Thieves with swords and weapons,
> Water, fire, poison, and the like:
> These fears are all entirely absent.
> In sleep and waking, always at peace,
> Without any nightmares.

> Sharp in faculties and endowed with wisdom,
> Always delighting in hearing the correct Dharma,
> Having heard it, they give rise to faith
> And attain the deep and wondrous Dharma.
> Their breath is free of foul odor;
> With diligence they teach others.
> The māras and non-Buddhist practitioners
> Dare not cause disturbance.

> Women who hear the Buddhas' names,
> Remember, uphold, and teach them to others,
> Will exhaust their female retribution
> And never receive it again.

> Reciting, upholding, and teaching others
> The names of the eight Buddhas,
> One attains the fruits of all merit
> As described above.
> Therefore those with wisdom
> Should be mindful, receive, and uphold them,
> With single-minded joyful delight,
> And should not give rise to negligence.

When the Buddha had finished teaching this sūtra, the Elder Śāriputra and all the great bhikṣus, together with all the bodhisattva-mahāsattvas, devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, humans and non-humans, heard what the Buddha had taught, rejoiced, and faithfully practiced it.
