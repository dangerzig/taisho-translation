# 佛說延壽妙門陀羅尼經
## Sūtra of the Wondrous Gate Life-Extending Dhāraṇī Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1140

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmabhadra (法賢).

---

Thus have I heard. At one time the Buddha was in the land of Magadha, at the site of true enlightenment, at the vajra seat of the great sacred stūpa, in the Hall of Universal Radiance, at the great bodhimaṇḍa, together with a great assembly of twelve hundred fifty śrāvaka monks, including Venerable Śāriputra, Venerable Mahāmaudgalyāyana, Venerable Mahākātyāyana, Venerable Mahākāśyapa, Venerable Ānanda, Venerable Rāhula, Venerable護國, Venerable Revata, Venerable Cūḍapanthaka, Venerable Gavāṃpati, Venerable Bharadvāja, Venerable Kālodāyin, and Venerable Aniruddha. All these venerable ones were great arhats. There were also immeasurable and boundless great bodhisattva assemblies, including:

Vajradhvaja Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, Vajragarbha Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, Vajrapāda Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, and Maitreya Bodhisattva Mahāsattva. All these bodhisattva mahāsattvas were great bodhisattvas of the Fortunate Kalpa.

At that time Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva Mahāsattva rose from his seat in the great assembly, went before the World-Honored One, joined his palms, bowed his head, and addressed the Buddha: "We beseech the Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha, for the benefit and happiness of all sentient beings, to proclaim the correct dharma of the Wondrous Gate Life-Extending Dhāraṇī. This correct dharma of the dhāraṇī serves as illumination and protection for me and all sentient beings, so that all evil ones who seek advantage, including those with evil hearts, angry hearts, extremely evil hearts, sinful hearts, afflicted hearts, and heartless hearts, as well as devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, mahoragas, rākṣasas, bhūtas, piśācas, pretas, kumbhāṇḍas, humans, and non-humans, none of these evil-hearted ones can invade. Furthermore, blades, weapons, poisons, and all diseases also cannot cause harm. Furthermore, in walking, standing, sitting, lying down, speaking, or silence, in all places there is rescue and protection. We beseech you to proclaim the Wondrous Gate Life-Extending Dhāraṇī for us. Why? Because this dhāraṇī has great awesome power. Sustained by this power, I constantly revere, serve, and make offerings to the Tathāgata, and rescue and protect all sentient beings. World-Honored One! I now wish to bring great benefit to all sentient beings, and therefore I entreat the World-Honored One. If any good man or good woman who seeks the bodhisattva vehicle obtains this dhāraṇī and upholds it with utmost sincerity, that person will receive the Tathāgata's protection, and calamities such as battle, quarrel, illness, and untimely death will be unable to invade or harm them."

At that time the World-Honored One, having received Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva's request, praised him: "Excellent, excellent, Vajrapāṇi! Very good, very good, Vajrapāṇi! You have sincerely entreated for the benefit of all sentient beings. Listen attentively now, and I shall proclaim for you the correct dharma of the Wondrous Gate Life-Extending Dhāraṇī. Vajrapāṇi! If any good man or good woman wishes to receive, uphold, read, and recite the correct dharma of the Wondrous Gate Life-Extending Dhāraṇī, they must first recite the Tathāgata Abhiṣeka Dhāraṇī." He then spoke the Abhiṣeka Dhāraṇī:

*Rukhu bukhu ru-kalā maṇi bandhani mālica māni māhu māhuri ko svāhā*

At that time, when the World-Honored One had proclaimed the Tathāgata Abhiṣeka Dhāraṇī, he told Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva Mahāsattva: "This Tathāgata Abhiṣeka Dhāraṇī can purify the vessel of the body of sentient beings. Afterward, when one recites the Wondrous Gate Life-Extending Dhāraṇī, one will be free from obstacles and the merit will be accomplished." He then spoke the Wondrous Gate Life-Extending Dhāraṇī:

[Dhāraṇī]

*Tatha vajri vajri vajri vajradhāri vajravati vajravati vajravati cakradhāri paritibhini bhiri mūri kāri huhu ri paṃgari viviri rī śirūrū cāri cāri curi paṇḍari svāhā tatha maṇinī gagāriṇī maṇḍani supramaṇḍani yekeciṃ mama pāpakāriṇī yastisthā naśani praṇaśani svāhā mama sarva-pāpa-praṇaśani akāla-mṛtyu-pratiśidhani sarva-śatru-ni-vāraṇi sarva-duṣpanna-pratiśidhani tatha yekeciṃ ṭū-pāpakāriṇa-stisthā naśani vinaśani praṇaśani bandhani chindani chinda-chinda virimiri haṭani vāri vāri viḍi viḍi viri viri bhindi bhindi aparisāraṇi carā-carā-ṇi carā-kaṇa ye brahma-vāriṇī indravati titirāyaṇī namo maheśvarāya nirīśi pañcani pañcani kāravāni ni bhūtavāni ni sattavāni ni satte-kaṭi samāvati sumā-prabhi svāhā mama dīrghāyuṣkatāya tatha hara hara hīce svāhā mama śatru-pratiśidhanaktatāya tatha gatita-patita ayena arjambhina kāri kārina ni buṭana ni mārga-virūhani hu-svabhāti pu-svabhāti hiri hiri yathā agni yathā parāñci yathā abhayaṃ yathā hṛdayaṃ mama upariduṣṭa-sarva-duṣṭa-pratiśidhanaktatāya*

At that time, when the World-Honored One had spoken the Wondrous Gate Life-Extending Dhāraṇī, he told Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva: "This Wondrous Gate Life-Extending Dhāraṇī was spoken by the buddhas, samyak-saṃbuddhas of the past, for the benefit of all sentient beings, and should always be received, upheld, and proclaimed for others. The buddhas of the past mutually rejoiced and made sincere vows. I likewise, wishing to benefit all sentient beings, have spoken this correct dharma of the dhāraṇī."

At that time, when the World-Honored One had spoken these words, from between his brows he emitted a great person's ray of light that universally illuminated all the buddha-lands of the ten directions, and all those buddha-lands were unified in one light. Wherever the light shone, the buddha world-honored ones each left their own lands and in an instant all came to this Sahā World, gathering like clouds at the place where the Tathāgata was expounding the dharma. The buddhas of the ten directions all praised: "Excellent, excellent! World-Honored One Śākyamuni Buddha! For the benefit of all sentient beings, you have proclaimed the correct dharma of the Wondrous Gate Life-Extending Dhāraṇī, providing illumination for all sentient beings, causing them to rejoice and obtain mindful remembrance, and also extinguishing all māras and enemies. This dhāraṇī, as the Buddha has spoken, we too receive and uphold.

"Furthermore, if any good man or good woman generates a sincere heart and constantly receives and upholds this dhāraṇī, reading and reciting it in accordance with the dharma at dawn, that person obtains twenty kinds of merit. If any person who loves this dharma-medicine wishes for such merit, they should be diligent and vigorous, reading and reciting in accordance with the dharma, and will certainly obtain the merits described above. What are the twenty kinds of merit? First, one upholds all buddhas; second, one serves and makes offerings to all buddhas; third, all karmic offenses are entirely extinguished; fourth, one deeply understands the wondrous dharma; fifth, one obtains longevity; sixth, one's fame spreads universally; seventh, one obtains great wealth and honor; eighth, one obtains great power; ninth, one is always free from illness; tenth, one possesses great vigor; eleventh, all the devas hold one in esteem; twelfth, all the buddhas cherish one; thirteenth, one obtains wholesome conduct; fourteenth, one obtains wholesome benefit; fifteenth, one serves as illumination; sixteenth, one obtains joy; seventeenth, one obtains mindful remembrance; eighteenth, the body possesses excellent marks; nineteenth, one obtains fearlessness; twentieth, one is replete with all roots of merit."

Furthermore, the Buddha told Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva: "This is the supreme secret dharma dhāraṇī of the phrases of all tathāgatas, samyak-saṃbuddhas. I have proclaimed this dhāraṇī to well remove all māras and enemies, and all buddhas receive and uphold it."

At that time Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva, generating a sincere heart, joined his palms toward the Buddha and said: "World-Honored One! I now, sustained by the Buddha's spiritual power, wishing to rescue, protect, and benefit all sentient beings, also rejoice in proclaiming the phrases of the Wondrous Gate Life-Extending Dhāraṇī. World-Honored One! If furthermore any person sincerely receives and upholds it, reading and reciting in accordance with the dharma at dawn, that person will constantly receive protection, and even māras who seek advantage will be unable to invade. In all places one will constantly obtain peace and happiness." He then spoke the dhāraṇī:

*Tatha śikhi śikhi rici rici caca caca vibhivibhivibhivibhi vāvā vāvā ruru ruru rara rara haha haḥhaḥhaḥhaḥ jaja jaja giri giri giri giri graha graha graha graha hṛhṛhṛhṛ haha haha mama mama hana hana sarva-pratīriti gaṃ ṇa daha daha sarvaśatru va paca paca sarvahita-iṣṭana svāhā*

At that time the World-Honored One praised Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva Mahāsattva: "Excellent, excellent! Vajrapāṇi! This Wondrous Gate Life-Extending Dhāraṇī is the supreme accomplishment. For the benefit and happiness of all sentient beings, I too receive and uphold it. If furthermore any person recites and reads this correct dharma of the Wondrous Gate Life-Extending Dhāraṇī in accordance with the dharma at dawn, and rejoices in it, that person will constantly receive protection and have their lifespan increased."

At that time in the assembly, the Lord of the Sahā World, Mahābrahmā Deva Rāja, joined his palms toward the Buddha and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I too, sustained by the Buddha's spiritual power, rejoice in proclaiming the correct dharma of the Wondrous Gate Life-Extending Dhāraṇī. If furthermore any person reads, recites, receives, and upholds it in accordance with the dharma at dawn, I shall constantly protect such good men and good women in all places, granting them peace and security." He then spoke the dhāraṇī:

*Tatha hiri miri ciri svāhā brahmā-sāri svāhā brahmā-bhi svāhā subhagī svāhā puṣpa-saṃsitāri svāhā*

"World-Honored One! If any good man or good woman receives and upholds this dhāraṇī, all evil māras and ghosts and spirits who seek advantage will be unable to gain it. In all places I shall constantly provide protection. World-Honored One! I now, wishing to benefit and bring happiness to all sentient beings, further speak a dhāraṇī:

*Tatha ciri vāraṇi ḍa mālaṇi nīriti-cici-ji gāuri gaṇdhāri sarma-hāna va-ntaruṇi jvāra-māriṇi cakravakeśvari svāhā*

At that time in the assembly, Vaiśravaṇa Heavenly King, Dhṛtarāṣṭra Heavenly King, Virūḍhaka Heavenly King, and Virūpākṣa Heavenly King each bowed before the Buddha and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! We four kings, sustained by the Buddha's spiritual power, also wish to benefit and bring happiness to all good men and good women. We rejoice in proclaiming the Wondrous Gate Life-Extending Dhāraṇī. If furthermore any person reads and recites with a sincere heart in accordance with the dharma at dawn, we four kings shall constantly protect them, removing all obstacles and granting them peace and security." They then spoke the dhāraṇī:

*Tatha puṣpe supuṣpe tūma pari hāri ārya-praśasti śānti maṃgalāya nimukti stuti stuti svāhā*

At that time the World-Honored One extended his wondrous right arm and touched the crowns of Mahābrahmā Deva Rāja and the Four World-Protectors, and spoke these words: "Excellent, excellent! Great Brahmā King and World-Protectors! Wishing to benefit and bring happiness to all sentient beings, and to protect and uphold the buddhadharma, you have well spoken these wondrous phrases of the dhāraṇī."

Furthermore, the Buddha told Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva Mahāsattva: "If any good man or good woman generates a sincere heart and constantly reads and recites in accordance with the dharma at dawn these Wondrous Gate Life-Extending Dhāraṇīs, that person will receive the empowerment and protection of all tathāgatas, and all deva kings, nāga kings, yakṣa kings, asura kings, garuḍa kings, gandharva kings, kiṃnara kings, mahoraga kings, vidyādhara deva kings, and all humans and non-humans will all cherish and esteem them, and will constantly protect them in all places, granting them peace and security."

When the Buddha had finished speaking this sūtra, Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva Mahāsattva and all the śrāvakas, as well as Mahābrahmā and the World-Protectors, all the devas, humans, asuras, gandharvas, humans, and non-humans, hearing what the Buddha taught, all greatly rejoiced, accepted it with faith, and put it into practice.
