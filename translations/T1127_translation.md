# 佛說普賢菩薩陀羅尼經
## Dhāraṇī Sūtra of Samantabhadra Bodhisattva Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1127

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmadeva (法天).

---

> Taking refuge in the universal realm of space,
> The pure and equal cause of the three times,
> The path of true awakening whose nature equals the light of dharma,
> The body of transformation that benefits and brings happiness to devas and humans,
> Dwelling in the stage of sovereignty over the ten bhūmis,
> Practicing great giving and vows to liberate sentient beings:
> Adorned with the marks of merit and virtue, named Samantabhadra,
> I now proclaim the secret teaching of the true words.

At that time Samantabhadra Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, wishing to manifest vast transformations, entered a samādhi called Great Sovereignty over the Three Realms. Having entered this samādhi, from the pure realm of space he manifested innumerable bodies like dust and sand, pervading all worlds of the great trichiliocosm.

At that time, all the tathāgatas of all buddha-lands extended their right hands to anoint the crown of Samantabhadra Bodhisattva Mahāsattva and praised him: "Excellent, excellent! Son of the Buddha! You should now proclaim the supreme dhāraṇī-dharma that is the mother of all buddhas." Then Samantabhadra Bodhisattva emitted a thousand rays of light from his body, universally illuminating the ten directions, and all buddha-lands trembled. He then spoke the dhāraṇī:

[Dhāraṇī]

*Tadyathā oṃ namo namas satva-bhuvana-īśvara mahā-bhogāya asamāsama amitāsama ānandasama gaganasama tribhavasama viśvasama paramarathasama svabhāvasama tathāgatasama arajasama śuddhisama buddhasama dharma-tathāgatasama siṃha-viṣamasama ekanayasāgara-viśuddha-loka hīhīra buddha-ra-buddha vijaya vijaya aṃ praśakta naya naya haṃ vajra-diṣṭhāna-saṃbhoga aṃ sanāraya raya dharma-gara dhara dhara jaya jaya hūṃ hūṃ dhama dhama svāhā*

When Samantabhadra Bodhisattva Mahāsattva had spoken this dhāraṇī, the Buddha observed that bodhisattva's body and saw that he was a former buddha of the past who, out of compassion for all sentient beings, had manifested transformation bodies filling the three realms and all worlds. At that time, all the great bodhisattvas, devas, humans, asuras, and others rose from all the vajra peaks, left their own jeweled and splendidly adorned great thrones of wealth and honor, came before the bodhisattva, and proclaimed: "Homage to the great compassionate, great merciful one of the boundless ocean of great merit! This is the supreme great dhāraṇī-dharma for attaining buddhahood. It well benefits the innumerable sentient beings of the three realms throughout past, future, and present, causing them to abide securely in the stage of the great dharma."

The Buddha said: "If any person hears this great dhāraṇī-dharma, the offenses committed over boundless kalpas are all extinguished. It can also dry up the great ocean of afflictions and demolish the lofty mountain of self-view. Suppose a person were to see a hundred thousand buddhas and accumulate wholesome roots over boundless kalpas: even that could not equal the merit of this true word. This great dhāraṇī is the ocean of perfect great merit of the heart of all tathāgatas. It can give birth to the seed of bodhi-wisdom, accomplish the ground of omniscience, and all buddha-dharmas cannot equal it. This great dhāraṇī's name, even in a hundred thousand nayutas of koṭis of kalpas, would be difficult to hear. If any person receives, upholds, reads, recites, respectfully honors, and makes offerings to this sūtra, such beings will well understand dhāraṇī, be able to enliven the life of wisdom, abide in the stage of the Tathāgata, and accomplish all merits.

"If any person reads and recites this sūtra once, twice, or up to three times, that person obtains the supreme merit, can extinguish all offenses, can sever all afflictions, and furthermore encounters the buddha tathāgatas and does not undergo any grief or suffering. If at a quiet place beneath a solitary tree one single-mindedly recites it, that person attains the dharma of all accomplishments and is beloved and respected by all. If any person in a great forest or wilderness sits cross-legged and recites this sūtra a full seven times, that person will before long attain great dhyāna-samādhi, sever all afflictions, and furthermore Samantabhadra Bodhisattva will appear before them, seated cross-legged. If a person, according to their means, makes offerings of incense and flowers while reciting this dhāraṇī, and even when exhausted falls asleep, in their dreams they will see Samantabhadra Bodhisattva extend his right hand, emit the light of dharma to illumine their body and mind, and speak these words: 'Excellent, excellent! Son of the Buddha! Through the great maṇḍala of the Tathāgata, you will turn your body and attain the stage of a great bodhisattva, with body and mind at peace, possessing great merit, virtue, and wisdom, and will always see Samantabhadra Bodhisattva Mahāsattva.' If a person with a heart of loving-kindness and compassion reads and recites this sūtra for all sentient beings, that person will perfectly fulfill the ten pāramitās, extinguish all afflictions and defilements, and furthermore be guarded by devas and humans. The Tathāgata will praise them, saying: 'Son of the Buddha! If you practice accordingly, before long you will enter the ground of Samantabhadra.'"
