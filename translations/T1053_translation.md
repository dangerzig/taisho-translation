# 聖觀自在菩薩功德讚
## Praise of the Merits of Holy Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1053

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dānapāla (施護).

---

> I bow to the most holy Avalokiteśvara,
> Whose wondrous form is like the full moon, born from the lotus,
> Who bestows fearlessness upon all beings.
> I now praise his merits:

> "All good dharmas he possesses in full,
> Upon his crown he bears the great wisdom-diadem of all buddhas.
> Adorned with merit and wisdom, the most honored one:
> Therefore I bow to Lotus-Hand.

> Both cheeks are round and full, rosy and white,
> His nose is straight and wondrously dignified,
> His teeth like shell-white snow, close-set and even,
> His four canines level and well-formed.

> His Brahma-voice is clear and resonant, deep and wondrous;
> All who hear it are moved to love.
> Whatever speech and language he proclaims
> Is gentle, kindly, and sweet.

> Always by the power of great compassion and skillful means,
> He saves and liberates all suffering beings.
> A guide for the benefit of self and others:
> Therefore I bow to Lotus-Hand.

> His brows are like the newly risen moon,
> His body like true purple-gold.
> Adorned with garlands of many jewels,
> All who behold him are never sated.

> Seated upon a spread deerskin,
> The most sovereign, honored among all assemblies,
> All his bodily marks perfectly complete:
> Therefore I bow to Lotus-Hand.

> A fine, subtle cloth wraps his chest,
> Maṇi gems serve as earrings.
> Adorned with worldly and supramundane jewels,
> He is the most sovereign and supreme.

> All virtuous merits fully complete,
> The great dharma-king of all the world,
> He opens forth all manner of skillful gates:
> Therefore I bow to Lotus-Hand.

> Sinful beings, numerous as dust-motes,
> Have wandered in saṃsāra through immeasurable kalpas.
> The bodhisattva ever gives rise to a mind of skillful means,
> Transforming and guiding all to enter the assembly of wisdom.

> He radiates the light of compassion to illuminate all,
> Bestowing the supreme dharma upon all beings,
> Causing all sentient beings to realize bodhi:
> Therefore I bow to Lotus-Hand.

> In all the great Avīci hells,
> The bodhisattva compassionately goes and manifests his body,
> Shattering those destinies without remainder,
> So that Yama's jailers are struck with terror.

> All those beings of karmic retribution
> Have their suffering removed and their sins extinguished.
> He can burn away the evil destinies with the fire of wisdom:
> Therefore I bow to Lotus-Hand.

> Endowed with the power of compassion, wisdom, practice, and vows,
> He manifests his body throughout the desire and form realms.
> Sovereign over the dharma, he benefits all beings,
> And even Yama's retinue rejoices.

> He shatters all realms of karmic wrongdoing,
> Causing all suffering and distress to arise no more,
> Bringing beings relief from suffering and cool refreshment:
> Therefore I bow to Lotus-Hand.

> I now praise the Great Sage,
> Whose supreme form has no equal,
> Unobstructed and sovereign, lord of the three realms,
> Who universally bestows fearlessness upon all beings.

> He ferries beings across the sea of suffering,
> Severing the three poisons at their root,
> Causing all to dwell in peace and happiness:
> Therefore I bow to Lotus-Hand.

> Among all the hosts of hungry ghosts,
> Those with needle-throats, great bellies, or foul hair,
> Such beings of many kinds beyond number,
> Tormented by hunger and thirst day and night;
> Driven by the fire of hunger and thirst,
> Devouring one another in anguish:
> Upheld by the bodhisattva's vow of compassion,
> He goes there and manifests his body to bring relief.

> First he transforms various fine foods and drink,
> Causing each one to be satisfied and nourished.
> Then he proclaims the gate of the true dharma,
> And all are freed from every suffering.

> By the bodhisattva's power of compassion, wisdom, and skillful means,
> He can enter into every destiny and class of being.
> Through benefiting, shared action, and gathering in beings:
> Therefore I bow to Lotus-Hand.

> Upon his body is a great maṇi jewel
> That constantly emits pure and wondrous radiance,
> Illuminating all the world's darkness,
> Outshining the sun and moon so they are not seen.

> All yakṣas, rākṣasas, and such,
> Bathed in this light, take refuge in him.
> Observing the minds of beings, he causes them to sever doubt:
> Therefore I bow to Lotus-Hand.

> The powerful asura kings and their hosts,
> Contentious, brave, and difficult to tame:
> The bodhisattva also manifests in their destinies,
> Teaching the dharma with skillful means to convert them.

> Having heard the dharma, they sever doubt and delusion,
> Each giving rise to a compassionate and virtuous mind.
> He bestows the most supreme and wondrous dharma-gate:
> Therefore I bow to Lotus-Hand.

> The bodhisattva once traveled through the heavenly realms
> And entered the palace of a certain celestial prince.
> Opening the gate of teaching by skillful expedients,
> He found that prince had nothing at all to give.

> At that time the prince was stricken with distress.
> The bodhisattva then manifested precious treasures for him,
> Enabling the prince to fulfill his wish to give:
> Therefore I bow to Lotus-Hand.

> All those consumed by desire,
> Ever burning in the fire of affliction,
> Upon seeing the bodhisattva's gracious and virtuous countenance
> Can turn their minds to contemplate the true dharma.

> Those in the world aflame with greed, anger, and delusion,
> Respectfully bowing to the Great Holy One,
> Have the three poisons and afflictions extinguished:
> Therefore I bow to Lotus-Hand.

> The realm of sentient beings is without boundary,
> Insects, worms, and ants beyond count.
> The bodhisattva with a mind of compassion
> Everywhere uses skillful means to teach and convert them.

> When the world suffers famine,
> He transforms food to sustain all beings,
> Causing the fire of hunger and thirst to be quenched:
> Therefore I bow to Lotus-Hand.

> If he sees beings in the great ocean
> Whose boats and rafts are wrecked and about to sink,
> The bodhisattva compassionately manifests a horse-body,
> Enabling them to cling to it and cross safely.

> When beings sail in boats upon the great ocean,
> The bodhisattva sends the wind as they desire,
> Whether favorable or contrary, to ferry them across,
> Causing all to leave behind every terror.

> The bodhisattva has already left the three realms' suffering,
> Perfectly completing the gate of samādhi.
> He further gathers in and transforms all beings,
> As numerous as space, without limit.

> The bodhisattva has already left behind all terrors,
> Namely rākṣasas, bhūtas,
> Piśācas, ḍākinīs,
> And all manner of evil spirits leading astray,
> Great floods, great fires, bandits,
> Tigers, wolves, venomous creatures, weapons,
> Diseases, poisons, black magic,
> And even royal punishment and imprisonment.

> The bodhisattva, having already left all these behind,
> Yet again acts for all those beings,
> A guide who benefits self and others,
> Supreme in great compassion and fearlessness.

> The bodhisattva has perfected the brahma-conduct
> And like the sun and moon illuminates the world.
> Praised by humans and devas, the Great Holy One:
> This is the most supreme true brahma-conduct.

> The bodhisattva ever abides in samādhi,
> With boundless merit as his support.
> By skillful expedients he manifests his dwelling place:
> Mount Potalaka is where he resides.

> That mountain, lofty and vast, supremely wondrous,
> Is adorned with all manner of precious jewels.
> It has jeweled trees beyond number,
> Tāla trees and campaka trees and more.

> Various rare birds perch upon them,
> Always producing pure and wondrous sounds.
> Such is the adornment of the sage's dwelling;
> By paying homage and gazing upon it, one gains merit.

> Kubera, Varuṇa,
> And Yama, these three celestial lords,
> The gandharva king, the asura king,
> The yakṣa king, and all the nāga lords,
> Down to the bhūtas and all such beings,
> Whether devas, spirits, or every manner of king:
> Day and night each gives rise to a reverent mind,
> Taking refuge in and praising the True Holy One.

> Having praised him thus,
> All their wishes are fulfilled as they desire.
> Great wealth, joy, and long life,
> Valor, diligence, and majestic virtue,
> With innumerable sufferings extinguished,
> Not even fearful dreams arising,
> Until the very end of one's lifespan,
> The bodhisattva appears in person to give comfort."

> The merit-ocean of Holy Avalokiteśvara
> Is immeasurable, boundless, and inexhaustible.
> Even with a hundred tongues through a thousand kalpas,
> One could not finish praising and extolling it.
