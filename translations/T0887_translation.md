# 無二平等最上瑜伽大教王經
## Sūtra of the Supreme Yoga Great Teaching King of Non-Dual Equality

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0887

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dānapāla (施護)

---

# Fascicle One

## Chapter 1: The Image Section of the Supreme Great Ritual King of Non-Dual Equality

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the World-Honored One was dwelling in the palace of the Paranirmitavaśavartin Heaven, together with bodhisattva-mahāsattvas numbering as the dust-motes of ninety-nine Mount Sumerus. All these bodhisattvas were Vajrasattva's vajra retinue. Their names were: Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva, Samantabhadra Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva, Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva, Vajramuṣṭi Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva, Bodhisattva who Turns the Dharma Wheel with an Equal Mind, Ākāśasaṃbhava Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva, and Māravimardaka Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva. Such great bodhisattva-mahāsattvas were the foremost among them.

At that time, the celestial palace, empowered by the divine might of all tathāgatas, was fully adorned with the most excellent ornaments: banners, pennants, parasols, canopies, wondrous jewels, hanging bells, and pearl garlands draped throughout. Great maṇi jewels radiated brilliant light of various colors, shining vastly like a full moon, pure and luminous.

The Buddha World-Honored One constantly abided in the samādhi of the right wisdom of all tathāgatas' vajra empowerment. He had received the vajra consecration of all tathāgatas and wore a jeweled crown upon his head. He occupied the throne of dharma king of the three realms, realized the sovereign mastery corresponding to the omniscience of all tathāgatas, and fulfilled the equality-seal of all tathāgatas. He had accomplished the supreme activities of all tathāgatas and abided in the eternally present body, speech, and mind activities of the vajra of Great Compassion Mahāvairocana Tathāgata, equal across the three times. Extending to all sentient beings, he could fulfill all wishes. He was praised in common by all tathāgatas and received their unanimous invitation to teach the dharma.

Furthermore, tathāgatas numbering as the sands of the Ganges came from the ten directions to assemble at this gathering, filling Jambudvīpa like sesame seeds. These tathāgatas each also constantly taught this secret dharma-gate of non-dual equality in their own buddha-fields.

At that time, Vajrapāṇi, the Great Vajra-Holder, abided in the mind of all tathāgatas, cultivated the practice of all tathāgatas, followed the bodily conduct of all tathāgatas, and accorded with the mental actions of all tathāgatas. Thus, gathering together the correspondences of his own division, he abided in his own dharma, and within this assembly concealed his body so that he was not visible.

Then, all the tathāgatas gathered from the ten directions, not seeing Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva in the assembly, spoke in unison with one voice this gāthā:

> "Great is Vajrapāṇi, like our father and mother!
> He does not appear in this assembly; where has the great being gone?"

After the tathāgatas had spoken thus, each abided silently within their great vajra-jewel empowerment dharma.

At that time, Vajrapāṇi, the Great Vajra-Holder, emerged from the five places of all tathāgatas and manifested with his own body the appearance of Mahāvairocana Buddha. Furthermore, from the mind of all tathāgatas he manifested a great moon maṇḍala, radiating with hundreds of thousands of circular lights. Having thus appeared, he further manifested from the mind of all tathāgatas a great sun maṇḍala, vast as innumerable hundreds of thousands of worlds, then returned to his original body and sat peacefully in the midst.

Then all the assembled tathāgatas each joined their palms and bowed to Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva, inviting him to teach the dharma, and spoke this gāthā:

> "Excellent, the supreme vajra dharma! Excellent, the pure vajra eye!
> Excellent, the supreme vajra wisdom, gathering the omniscience of all buddhas!
> We invite Vajrapāṇi to benefit and bring joy to all buddhas,
> And proclaim the supreme secret dharma of the Great Vehicle, born of self-and-other wisdom."

Then Vajrapāṇi, the Great Vajra-Holder, addressed the tathāgatas: "The buddha-tathāgatas should not invite me to proclaim the secret dharma-gate."

The tathāgatas asked: "For what reason will you not teach?"

Vajrapāṇi said: "Buddhas, know this: in this great assembly, among the bodhisattva-mahāsattvas, there may be those who, upon hearing this supreme secret dharma of the first principle of the Great Vehicle, would manifest many kinds of destructive signs."

The tathāgatas said: "Each of us shall empower the assembly with our divine power and cause all to become a great vajra assembly. You need only carry out the buddha's work."

Then Vajrapāṇi, the Great Vajra-Holder, smiled gently and gazed up at the buddhas, praising them: "Excellent! Excellent!" He then entered the samādhi of the great wheel light of all tathāgatas illuminating the minds of all tathāgatas. Having emerged from this samādhi, he manifested four great sun maṇḍalas in the four directions: in the east, the Great Sun Maṇḍala Holding Inexhaustible Colors; in the south, the Wish-Fulfilling Jewel Vajra Great Sun Maṇḍala; in the west, the Immeasurable Vajra Great Sun Maṇḍala; in the north, the Infallible Samādhi Vajra Great Sun Maṇḍala. Having thus manifested, he proclaimed to all the tathāgatas and all the bodhisattvas the secret dharma-gate of the Supreme Great Ritual King of Non-Dual Equality. What he proclaimed corresponded with the principles of all bodhisattvas, causing all bodhisattvas to accomplish the supreme meritorious, miraculous activities of all tathāgatas, pervading the assembly and extending throughout the ten directions, so that all buddhas and bodhisattvas abided in accordance with principle. Just as the light of space illumines a great trichiliocosm, and the light of every sun disk shines proportionally yet pervades everywhere, so too the light of all tathāgatas and the light of Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva merged equally, illuminating in equality, so that those who truly perceived abided in accordance with principle.

Then all the tathāgatas offered Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva an ocean-cloud of Samantabhadra offerings born from all tathāgatas. After making offerings, the tathāgatas abided in silence.

Vajrapāṇi then entered the samādhi of observing the mind-wisdom divisions of all bodhisattvas, the self-samādhi that produces the supreme secret of all tathāgatas and destroys the causes of darkness. Having emerged from this samādhi, he proclaimed the wisdom-division samādhi dharma-gate:

[The verse teaching follows, describing visualization of a pure milk-ocean with a great lotus like moonlight of crystal, observing one's own mind-seed corresponding to one's own dharma, thereby producing the mind-maṇḍala of one cubit's measure, four-sided with four corners, adorned with four jewels. Through proper visualization, light surrounds it blazing and shining everywhere. Within, one visualizes one's own body as pure, free from distinctions of self, and transforms it into a buddha body.]

*Dhāraṇī:* **Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-jñāna-svabhāva-ātma-kuhaṃ**

[The teaching continues with further visualizations: establishing the vajra-empowerment dharma-gate, the master dharma-gate of all tathāgatas, Samantabhadra's vajra dharma-gate, and the dharma-king dharma-gate of Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva. Each describes progressively larger maṇḍala visualizations: one visualizes one's own image corresponding to the buddha image, the four-seal maṇḍalas, and ultimately the identity of self-image with buddha-image through five-place observation. The chapter culminates in Samantabhadra's holding-vajra dharma-gate.]

Thereupon Samantabhadra Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva surveyed the assembly, rained an ocean-cloud of offerings from all bodhisattvas filling the realm of space, and offered them to Vajrapāṇi. After making offerings, he bowed in homage and abided according to the dharma.

[Samantabhadra then manifests as Mahāvairocana and teaches the wisdom-division's true samādhi, with visualizations of sun maṇḍalas, the letter *vaṃ*, the letter *oṃ* in the four directions, and progressively expanding visualizations from one cubit to one *tāla* tree height to one *krośa* measure. He teaches the sixteen honored ones, the method of the letter *hūṃ* turning leftward and producing light that destroys all māras, and the arising of noble beings from seed syllables.]


## Chapter 2: The Wisdom-Division Samādhi Section

[This chapter continues the verse teachings of the wisdom-division (jñāna-kula). It describes visualization of a white lotus as large as a chariot wheel containing a sun maṇḍala, one's body arising from the karma-vajra as identical to Vajrasattva. Further instructions describe: visualizing the pure milk-ocean; one's body as buddha-body pervading space; increasingly vast maṇḍalas containing buddha images, Vajrasattva, and noble beings; the lotus maṇḍala with the letter *oṃ*; and the various dharma-gates including the Samantabhadra Secret Dharma-Gate, the Vajra Samādhi Dharma-Gate, and the Holding Vajra Dharma-Gate. Each gate involves progressively expanding maṇḍalas and the visualization of self-image as identical with buddha-image, all permeated by the true nature of bodhicitta and great vidyā syllables.]


# Fascicle Two

## Chapter 3: The Reality-Gathering Division Samādhi Section

At that time, Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva, the Great Secret Lord, again entered the wondrous moon maṇḍala of all tathāgatas and manifested with his own body the appearance of Mahāvairocana Buddha, abiding within the dharmadhātu great maṇḍala. Having thus abided, he returned to his own form and proclaimed the reality-gathering division samādhi dharma-gate:

[The verse describes visualization in the pure milk-ocean with *utpala* flowers, vidyā syllables, and the correspondence of the wisdom-division maṇḍala. One visualizes Vajrasattva's image within light arising from wrathful forms, with one's own image also abiding in the light.]

*Dhāraṇī:* **Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-saṃgraha-jñāna-svabhāva-ātma-kuhaṃ**

[The teaching explains that the self-image is the *hūṃ* syllable, from which all visualization arises. The chapter continues with dharma-gates: Vajrapāṇi's Supreme Son Dharma-Gate, the Self-Image Wisdom Dharma-Gate, the Self-Nature Benefit Dharma-Gate, the Master Dharma-Gate of All Tathāgatas, the Vajra Self-Nature Dharma-Gate, the Firm Wisdom Dharma-Gate, the Great Vajra Being Dharma-Gate, the Vajra Eye Dharma-Gate, and the Great Secret Lord Dharma-Gate. Each describes maṇḍala visualizations of increasing complexity, from one cubit to one *tāla* tree to one *krośa*, with five colors representing the five divisions (white for wisdom, black for reality-gathering, red for dharma, yellow for karma, and the supreme color of *caru* representing the finest adornment), and the Three Vajra Breaking Dharma-Gate.]


## Chapter 4: The Dharma-Division Samādhi Section

Vajrapāṇi then proclaimed the dharma-division samādhi dharma-gate:

*Dhāraṇī:* **Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-dharma-jñāna-svabhāva-ātma-kuhaṃ**

[This chapter describes the Avalokiteśvara Dharma-Gate and the Samantabhadra Dharma-Gate within the dharma division. The lotus, representing the purity of desire, becomes the maṇḍala. Vidyārājas arise from Avalokiteśvara, born from Vajrasattva. The Lotus-Holding Dharma-Gate teaches the correspondence of self-mind maṇḍala with the dharma maṇḍala. The chapter maps the four implements to the four divisions: the vajra wheel for wisdom, the vajra sword for dharma, the vajra mace for reality-gathering, and the trident shared across three divisions. The five maṇḍalas correspond to the five divisions: self-mind to wisdom, great wisdom to reality-gathering, wondrous dharma to dharma, wondrous moon to karma, and sun to all divisions universally.]


## Chapter 5: The Karma-Division Samādhi Section

Vajrapāṇi placed the great vajra mace of all tathāgatas within the secret divisions of wisdom and the others, entered the great wisdom correspondence maṇḍala, and proclaimed the karma-division samādhi dharma-gate:

*Dhāraṇī:* **Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-kāya-jñāna-svabhāva-ātma-kuhaṃ**

[This chapter teaches the karma-flame (kāmāgni) visualizations. From the self-image arise three kinds of images: those of Brahmā, those of Nārāyaṇa, and those of the karma-flame. These correspond to specific origins: buddha-images produce Brahmā's image transformations; dharma-images produce Nārāyaṇa's image; Vajrapāṇi's firm activity produces the karma-flame. Maṇḍalas expand from one cubit to one hundred eight cubits to one thousand cubits, with buddha images arising within each.]


## Chapter 6: The Section on Supreme Wisdom Samādhi for All Accomplishments

Vajrapāṇi proclaimed:

[The verse teaches that practitioners seeking *siddhi* should visualize within their own division's maṇḍala, abiding in samāhita (meditative equilibrium). Those with doubt about the samādhi are said by the Buddha to be of inferior disposition; those who understand it possess great merit. The teaching uses the analogy of *utpala* flowers growing from mud to illustrate the immaculate nature of mind. Bodhicitta, firm and accepted by the buddhas, is born from Vajrapāṇi; apart from this, there is no other origin.]


## Chapter 7: The Section on the Profound Secret Offering of All Buddhas

Samantabhadra Bodhisattva entered the samādhi of all vajra producing great wisdom empowerment, then transitioned into the samādhi of all correspondence wisdom empowerment. Having emerged, he proclaimed the dharma-gate of the birthless, born from all-correspondence samādhi secret accomplishment wisdom:

[This extensive verse describes specific *siddhi* practices: visualization of buddha-images within maṇḍalas; Vajrasattva visualized as small as a grain of wheat with blazing garlands; five-pronged vajra visualized at the tip of the nose; lotuses visualized as small as a date. The four divisions use different implements and flowers: *utpala* for wisdom and gathering divisions; *padma* for karma and dharma divisions. The timing is specified: ten for wisdom, twelve for gathering, sixteen for dharma, and twenty for the karma division. Detailed ritual instructions follow for drawing four-cubit maṇḍalas, reciting five *lakṣa* (500,000) times, and practicing at the ocean shore or in mountain retreats during the first and middle watches of the night. Even those with the heaviest transgressions can succeed through this dharma. Through Vajrapāṇi's empowerment, practitioners can manifest buddha bodies the size of Mount Sumeru.]


# Fascicle Three

## Chapter 8: The Section on All Tathāgatas' Wisdom-Realization, the Supreme Secret

The tathāgatas from the ten directions again invited Vajrapāṇi to proclaim the samādhis of all tathāgatas' wisdom-realization, the great vidyā samādhi, and the lion-transformation samādhi of all tathāgatas' accomplishment, the dharma-gate of swift realization of *anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi*, great wisdom, and equality.

Vajrapāṇi replied: "The buddhas should not again invite me to proclaim these samādhi dharma-gates. Why? This dharma is exceedingly profound and supremely secret. It is the dharma-gate of the true wisdom-seal accomplishment of all tathāgatas. If those of inferior disposition hear this dharma, a thousand kinds of destruction would arise."

The tathāgatas said: "We buddhas jointly empower you. Simply teach as appropriate."

[The verse teaching declares that practitioners who do not violate the true correspondence gate will attain a buddha-body in this very life. All beings are gathered like one vast lotus; all samādhi dharma-gates can be accomplished. The teaching emphasizes self-nature purity: the minds of self and all beings are originally pure and luminous. All buddhas' supreme activities are accomplished through authentic practice of the great vidyā. Desire, hatred, and delusion are known as empty in nature; without abandoning them, purity is attained of itself.]


## Chapter 9: The Section on Vajrasattva's All-Secret Samādhis

[This chapter teaches the relationship between practitioners and the ācārya (teacher). Practitioners of yoga should prostrate only to tathāgatas, not to śrāvakas. Those who violate this secret will have their heads split into seven pieces and lose bodhicitta. The buddha is the supreme ācārya; one who knows the secret truth is no different from the buddha. The vidyā-holder should practice three times daily. The Great Bodhicitta Dharma-Gate and Vajradhara Dharma-Gate are taught. A further section explains the three vajras of body, speech, and mind: the mind-vajra enables one to enter the wisdom-vajra; the speech-vajra is observed through the pure nature of vajra self-nature; the body-vajra is the indestructible vajra empowered by vajra light. Through six months of practice according to this method, one attains the accomplishment-dharma of all buddhas. Desire-dharma, unborn and unstained, arises entirely from the vajra.]

Vajrapāṇi then taught the samādhi of all tathāgatas' great wisdom ācārya:

[The verse describes the buddha-ācārya who visualizes innumerable buddhas filling space, born from great vidyā, abiding in samāhita with a mind of delight. The ācārya enters the dharma-maṇḍala, contemplates the five-place correspondence, offers the supreme jeweled crown, and practices the supreme bliss gate of the buddha-ācārya. "If one's dharma corresponds in an instant, one attains buddhahood in this present life."]


## Chapter 10: The Section on the Meditation Maṇḍala of All Samādhi Accomplishments

The tathāgatas from the ten directions again invited Vajrapāṇi to proclaim the supreme great maṇḍala of meditation for all samādhi-wisdom accomplishment. Vajrapāṇi entered the samādhi of all tathāgatas' equality vajra empowerment and proclaimed:

[A detailed maṇḍala description: sixteen cubits in measure, four-sided with four corners and four gates. Within the central moon maṇḍala stands a *jambu* tree, with five-pronged vajras at the four outer corners. The buddha-seats are arranged by direction: Akṣobhya in the east, Ratnasambhava in the south, Amitābha in the west, Amoghasiddhi in the north, and Mahāvairocana at the center. Buddha-eye bodhisattvas are placed at the inner four corners, and Amṛtakuṇḍalin and other vidyārājas at the four gates. Five lights produce the five dharmas. The entire space is visualized as filled with buddhas, offered through the secret dharma.]


## Chapter 11: The Section on the Great Meditation

Vajrapāṇi proclaimed the great meditation maṇḍala of the infallible samādhi:

[Four maṇḍalas for the four divisions are described: the sun maṇḍala correspondence (all directions filled with Vajrasattva dharma), the wondrous dharma maṇḍala (with dharma lords in five directions), the karma maṇḍala (with Indra, devas, and kāmāgni), and the illuminating Vajrasattva mind-summit maṇḍala (atop a crystal mountain in a milk-ocean, with vidyā-consorts). The chapter culminates in the lion-transformation great meditation maṇḍala, featuring a mountain of four jewels, maṇḍala with moon-wheel and jeweled throne, vidyā-consorts born from five-place correspondence, and elaborate directional offerings: *utpala* flowers in the east, *padma* in the south, a red parasol in the west, and golden wisdom-light in the north.]


# Fascicle Four

## Chapter 12: The Section on Arising from the Great Meditation

Vajrapāṇi entered the samādhi of wisdom-light illuminating all of space. Having emerged, he proclaimed the great vidyā of the wisdom division:

*Dhāraṇī:* **Oṃ jñānod-bhavāya svāhā**

Upon speaking this great vidyā, asaṃkhyeya hundreds of thousands of buddha-fields all trembled greatly. Whoever hears this great vidyā attains vast powers of memory, eloquence, and wisdom.

[Three more division-dhāraṇī follow the same pattern: reality-gathering, dharma, and karma divisions each have their own dhāraṇī, each producing vast light illuminating all buddha-fields. The chapter then teaches the mudrā (seal) correspondences for each division: two arms joined and fingers merged is the universal seal; vajra-joining is the wisdom seal; jewel-binding is the reality-gathering seal; lotus-arising is the dharma seal; the karma division is identical to the dharma; and trident-binding is the karma-division seal.]


## Chapter 13: The Section on the Initial Rites of the Supreme Vidyā Teaching

Vajrapāṇi entered the samādhi of fulfilling all wishes. Having emerged, he proclaimed the great samādhi accomplishment maṇḍala:

"This maṇḍala is supremely vast, like the vastness of space itself. If one wishes to paint this maṇḍala, one should follow the ritual procedure of one's own division. First spread the painting cloth, empower the five-colored threads with consecration, measure and lay out the boundary paths, and paint proportionally as prescribed. The maṇḍala has three concentric levels, with four sides, four corners, four gates, and four pavilions fully adorned. Each gate is decorated with *niyūha* ornaments, and the sky is painted raining down jewels.

"In the first (inner) level: paint the lord of one's own division at the center, the wisdom-division lord in the east, the reality-gathering lord in the south, the dharma-division lord in the west, and the karma-division lord in the north.

"In the second (middle) level: paint Mahāśvetacchatra (Great White Parasol) Bodhisattva in the east, Vajradhara Bodhisattva in the south, Padmadhara Bodhisattva in the west, and Bhrūkuṭī Bodhisattva in the north. At the four corners paint Buddha-eye Bodhisattva, Māmakī Bodhisattva, Pāṇḍaravāsinī Bodhisattva, and others.

"In the third (outer) level: paint Indra with desire-realm and pure-abode devas in the east; five-superknowledge great ṛṣis and Agni's retinue in the southeast; the lord of piśācas and the seven mother-spirits in the south; piśāca lords and bhūta hosts in the southwest; Varuṇa and nāga hosts in the west; Vāyu and vidyādhara hosts in the northwest; Kubera and yakṣa hosts in the north; and bhūta lords in the northeast.

"At the four gates paint Vajraśṛṅkhalā Bodhisattva. Outside the third level at the four corners, paint five-pronged vajras."

[Detailed ritual instructions follow for placing consecration vases, offering incense, flowers, lamps, and unguents, making secret offerings, invoking the lord deity's approval, making offerings to Piśācī-karī and Hārītī, and performing *homa* (fire offering) with the division's great vidyā.]

"If a person hears the name of Vajrasattva or sees his body, they will attain the supreme stage within seven lifetimes."

Vajrapāṇi then requested Mahāvairocana Tathāgata to teach the supreme accomplishment dharma-gate. Mahāvairocana proclaimed:

[A verse describing the maṇḍala of thirty *yojanas*, filled with *indranīla* (sapphire) light, visualizing the milk-ocean with great lotuses containing Yamāntaka (the wrathful laughing king), Nīladaṇḍa, and other forms. Three kinds of images fill space. Practitioners visualize their own mind as containing noble beings, with the *kalpa*-fire's great light blazing, and transformation buddhas emerging from every pore, pervading all of space.]


## Chapter 14: The Section on Accomplishing Tasks

Mahāvairocana taught the dharma-gate of differentiating all secret great vidyā teachings:

[The verse describes visualizing four-directional maṇḍalas in space, asaṃkhyeya *yojanas* in extent. One's own image appears within as Mahāvairocana, with five-place correspondence. Sun maṇḍalas with noble beings are visualized, born from Vajrasattva's own mind.]


## Chapter 15, Part One: The Section on Mahāvairocana Tathāgata's All Great Vidyā Vajra Arising

Mahāvairocana taught the recitation dharma of all tathāgatas' true teachings:

[An extensive verse describes the mind as vast as space, external maṇḍalas no different from one's own maṇḍala. Detailed instructions for great vidyā practice follow: one-syllable, three-syllable, and five-syllable vidyā formulations; five colors corresponding to different ritual activities (sapphire for certain rites; red for lotus-bliss; yellow and blazing for increase); specific maṇḍala visualizations from heart to crown; and the identity of self-image with buddha-image. Practitioners should enter a quiet dwelling, abide in meditation, and single-mindedly visualize the accomplishment of body, speech, and mind.]

Mahāvairocana then entered the samādhi of all tathāgatas' wish-fulfilling jewel equality vajra and proclaimed the all-tathāgatas-arising vajra maṇḍala:

[A painted maṇḍala is described with the five directional buddhas: Akṣobhya (east), Ratnasambhava (south), Amitāyus (west), Amoghasiddhi (north), and the buddha-ācārya (center). Five-pronged and eleven-pronged vajras are placed, with *utpala* flowers at the inner corners and four jewels representing the four great seals. The root syllables of the four divisions are taught: *hūṃ* for the buddha division, *hūṃ-ja* for the vajra division, *ja-a* for the lotus division, and *a-aḥ* for the karma division.]

Mahāvairocana then taught the three levels of accomplishment: regular accomplishment through one month's practice; near-accomplishment; and great-accomplishment through three months. He further taught the wisdom-division accomplishment-treasury: practitioners visualize themselves as Mahāvairocana wielding the vajra wheel, vajra sword, trident, vajra hook, and vajra lasso in succession. Within moon-light images, one visualizes Vajrapāṇi, knowing "all desires are pure as space itself"; lotus-eyes granting sovereignty over all stages; five buddhas radiating five-colored light with sun-light arising, producing the karma-vajra; five-pronged vajras granting the indestructible vajra body.

[Specific summoning rites follow: in the middle of the night, at a quiet place or wilderness or mountain, practitioners recite the division's vidyā and form the division's mudrā. The vajra hook is visualized at the heart. Lists of wrathful kings who can be summoned are given: Yamāntaka, Hayagrīva, Acala, Mahābala, Amṛtakuṇḍalin, Vajragandha, Vajranakhī, and Vajracaṇḍa.]


# Fascicle Five

## Chapter 15, Part Two (continued)

Mahāvairocana entered successive samādhis and proclaimed great vidyā for each wrathful king and bodhisattva:

**Yamāntaka's great vidyā** (lengthy dhāraṇī): Upon proclaiming this, Mahāvairocana manifested in moon maṇḍalas of dust-mote-number buddhas, pervading space with various colors and his own image.

> "Great are all the great vidyā dharmas, supreme vajra indestructible!
> All tathāgatas' firm gate, attaining the incomparable nectar-gathering."

**The supreme vajra-heart vidyā** (short dhāraṇī ending *hūṃ phaṭ*): Upon this, even all tathāgatas felt awe.

**The light-pervading-all-space vidyā** (Acala/Caṇḍamahāroṣaṇa's dhāraṇī): A lengthy mantra. Upon recitation, all tathāgatas manifested a great wheel of light pervading space.

**Acala (Immovable One, the great wrathful king) vidyā** (lengthy dhāraṇī).

**Bhrūkuṭī (Frowning One) Bodhisattva's great vidyā.**

**Mahābala (Great Power) wrathful king's great vidyā.**

**Amṛtakuṇḍalin (Nectar-Vessel) great wrathful king's great vidyā** (the longest dhāraṇī in the text): a vast mantra of destruction and subjugation against all obstacles, concluding with *hūṃ hūṃ hūṃ hūṃ phaṭ phaṭ*.

**Vajragandha (Vajra Incense) Bodhisattva's great vidyā** (the most extensive dhāraṇī): Upon its proclamation, all tathāgatas manifested a great vajra correspondence-cloud filling space, and spoke in unison:

> "Great are all the great vidyā dharmas, the vast true treasury of secrets!
> Having heard this supreme vajra, all buddhas abide in *āveśa* (possession)."

**Hayagrīva (Horse-Head) great wrathful king's great vidyā**: Upon recitation, all great poisonous beings were terrified to the point of destruction.

**Aparājita (Unconquered) great wrathful king's vidyā.**

**Vajra-Uṭkāṭaka vidyārāja's vidyā.**

**Bhrūkuṭī's vidyā.** **Vajra-Kīla vidyā.** **Buddha-eye Bodhisattva's vidyā.** **Pāṇḍaravāsinī (White-Robed) Bodhisattva's vidyā.** Additional vidyā for: nectar-arising, affliction-destruction, great dharma-summit, light-gathering, supreme summit, supreme buddha-summit, white parasol great buddha-summit, supreme vajra Tārā, and wrathful-summoning-of-the-three-realms.

[After the dhāraṇī catalogue, a verse summarizes the ritual applications of each wrathful king: Yamāntaka for the wisdom division, Hayagrīva for the reality-gathering division, Mahābala and Amṛtakuṇḍalin for subduing the difficult-to-tame. Practitioners who recite one *lakṣa* (100,000) of any wrathful king's vidyā can summon all tathāgatas and perform all rites of attraction.]


## Chapter 16: The Section on All Great Vidyā Samādhis

Mahāvairocana taught all great vidyā's correspondence samādhis:

[The verse covers pacification (*śāntika*), increase (*pauṣṭika*), and other ritual activities. All dharmas arise from the mind-wisdom; one visualizes buddha-images producing various jewel-lights. Each division's lord and vidyā are visualized at the supreme crown of the head. All great vidyā arise from the great vidyā samādhi, proclaimed by Vajra Fearlessness out of the buddhas' compassionate concern.]


## Chapter 17: The Section on Dispelling Māra through All Tathāgatas' Great Vidyā

Mahāvairocana taught all great vidyā for dispelling māras:

[The verse describes the three wrathful ones of the three divisions used for dispelling māra: Yamāntaka for wisdom, Hayagrīva for reality-gathering, and the dharma division following wisdom's procedure. Practitioners recite hundreds of thousands or even *ayuta* (billions) of vidyā, with mind fixed on the buddhas. Specific instructions include writing vidyā on palm-leaves with thorns, performing rites in charnel grounds or in temples of Maheśvara. If māras are not subdued within seven days and nights, the wrathful vajra will swiftly destroy them, splitting their heads into seven pieces.]


## Chapter 18: The Section on Serving Vajrasattva, the Secret of All Tathāgatas

Mahāvairocana taught the protocols for disciples serving the ācārya according to Vajrasattva's dharma:

"One should regard one's own ācārya as no different from the buddha-ācārya, born from vajra empowerment, and revere him for one's entire life.

"A disciple's conduct should be firm: one should not sit or lie on the teacher's seat or bed. One should arrange the teacher's clothing and shoes. One should not joke or laugh before the teacher, nor speak useless words.

"If one transgresses against even one extremely small matter, the foolish one shall fall into the Avīci Hell. If one slanders the ācārya or violates the profound secret dharma, one shall certainly fall into the Avīci Hell for many kalpas of suffering.

"The disciple should follow the teacher's instructions as if they were the buddha's command, carrying them out whether in joy or suffering, never transgressing for the duration of one's life.

"One should not place one's hands on one's hips before the teacher. When seeking accomplishment, one should not speak the teacher's name. One who does so shall fall into hell for many kalpas.

"One who regards the teacher as the buddha is truly called a disciple. The teacher's words should be heard without doubt, for they are the same as those of Ratnasambhava Buddha. One who hears contrary words is a fool and shall fall into the Raurava (Screaming) Hell.

"One who transgresses the teacher destroys the samādhi and falls into hell for sixty kalpas."


# Fascicle Six

## Chapter 19: The Section on the Wish-Fulfilling Jewel of All Tathāgatas' Great Vidyā

Mahāvairocana taught all-accomplishment practice:

[The verse declares that all secret great vidyā bestow joy upon all beings. Vajradhara the Vidyā-King is the supreme sovereign of all. The verse addresses desire, hatred, and delusion: "All desire-dharmas are originally boundless, bestowing great benefit and joy"; "All hatred-dharmas are originally boundless, proclaimed by the great vajra wisdom"; "All delusion-dharmas are originally boundless, proclaimed by omniscience." Suffering and joy are equally boundless, with equality and non-discrimination as the foundation.]

The tathāgatas from the ten directions, having heard Mahāvairocana's teaching, all rejoiced and said to Vajrapāṇi: "How extraordinary! These dharma-gates are called the Master of All-Knowing Wisdom. How could one who does not know this dharma know any other?"

Vajrapāṇi then declared his wish to proclaim the samādhi dharma-gate of all tathāgatas' wish-fulfilling jewel. The tathāgatas approved.

[The verse teaches the Three Vajra Correspondence of Mahāvairocana's samādhi: visualization of a pure moon maṇḍala with crystal moonlight, a sun maṇḍala with four colors and the *oṃ* syllable in yellow, five lights corresponding to one's own dharma, the *hūṃ* syllable corresponding to one's pure self-image radiating *jāmbūnada* gold light with all marks complete, and five lights from the buddha-image pervading space.]

Vajrapāṇi then taught the Vajrasattva three-vajra-wisdom samādhi: the *oṃ* syllable as Vairocana, the *hūṃ* syllable as Vajrasattva wearing Vairocana's crown, holding a vajra, surrounded by sapphire light.

The Amitāyus samādhi: the *dhaṃ* syllable as the lord Amitāyus holding an eight-petaled lotus.

The Kāmāgni (Karma-Flame) samādhi: the *sa* syllable as the kāmāgni image holding a trident, with *jāmbūnada* gold light.

The Secret Homa Dharma-Gate: detailed instructions for fire-offering visualization, including specific measurements (thirty-two finger-widths for the great *utpala*, sixteen finger-widths for the five-pronged vajra, twenty-six finger-widths for the trident). Four *homa* furnace shapes: circular for pacification (using honey and milk), four-sided for increase, bow-shaped for attraction, and triangular for subjugation. One visualizes the *hūṃ* syllable at the tip of the tongue.


## Chapter 20: The Section on Collecting All Great Vidyā Samādhis

Vajrapāṇi entered the samādhi of all tathāgatas' unconditioned wisdom vajra empowerment and proclaimed the dharma-gate of collecting all samādhi's essential great vidyā:

[Four division-samādhis are described: the all-buddha samādhi of the wisdom division (white, four-armed, jeweled crown, *oṃ* syllable); the all-vajradhara correspondence samādhi of the reality-gathering division (black, wrathful, four-armed); the all-dharma supreme universal auspicious samādhi (red, holding a red lotus); and the karma-division vajra-trident supreme samādhi (yellow, peaceful light, three-eyed). Each is the supreme meditation dharma of its division.]

Vajrasattva's concluding verse:

> "Throughout all realms of experience, all noble beings offer universal praise;
> All these dharmas are in correspondence; from the arising ritual, accomplishment is attained."

[A detailed catalogue of vidyā applications follows: self-mind vidyā, division lords' vidyā, wrathful kings' vidyā, all karma vidyā, and buddha-summit vidyā. Specific uses: pacification uses the division's own vidyā; increase also uses the division's vidyā; attraction uses the great buddha-summit visualization; subjugation uses the wrathful kings; dispelling māra uses the great buddha-summit and vajra-stake. Five pure lights are distinguished by color for specific activities. The *hūṃ* syllable serves the three divisions; *oṃ* is supreme for the wisdom division. Vidyā syllables are placed at the forehead and heart.]

Samantabhadra Bodhisattva, the Vajra-Holder, then spoke in verse:

> "Corresponding to the four times according to the dharma, visualize the image of vajra nectar;
> Near-accomplishment this can achieve, and this accomplishment-dharma is the most supreme.
> When the lord deity's image corresponds, great accomplishment is fully achieved;
> When the buddha-summit image corresponds, it achieves the correspondence-accomplishment.
> When the division's noble ones correspond, transformations arise in the karma division;
> Distinguish the minute vajra, and well visualize it among the buddhas of three times.
> Through practice according to the dharma, one attains buddhahood; all accomplishments are as one wishes;
> These accomplishments have nothing higher; vast accomplishment arises from merit."


## Chapter 21: The Section on the Great Wisdom of All Tathāgatas' Body, Speech, and Mind Purity, the Self-Nature Vajra

The tathāgatas from the ten directions manifested a cloud of wondrous bliss and great adornment born from all tathāgatas, offering it to Vajrapāṇi. Then, from the speech-vajra, they uttered a wondrous dharma-sound praising the great vajra-teaching secret dharma-gate:

> "The secret, pure Great Vehicle dharma, supreme among wisdoms, a treasury of merit,
> The all-pervading great wisdom, the selfless gate: I bow and praise the secret wisdom.
> The secret, true, pure meaning, the supreme practice-dharma vast as the ocean,
> Born from Samantabhadra's body: I bow and praise the vajra wisdom.
> The supreme great wisdom as the vehicle, abiding in the three vajras, indestructible,
> Having reached the further shore, the great dharma-gate: I bow and praise the path to enlightenment.
> For the supreme birth of all beings, causing all to enter the wisdom of the buddhas,
> The buddhas' supreme self-nature purity: I bow and praise the saving of beings.
> The pure dharma-body, great liberation, like a wish-fulfilling jewel, sovereign and free,
> The liberation-gate of all beings: I bow and praise the pure practice."

At that time, a bodhisattva-mahāsattva in the assembly named Wish-Fulfilling Jewel Samādhi Vajra addressed Vajrapāṇi: "Great being! If a bodhisattva desires to seek such great merit-dharma, how should they seek it?"

Vajrapāṇi answered: "If a bodhisattva arouses a thought free from doubt, they should seek thus: with a thought free from doubt, seek the buddha-dharma; with a thought free from doubt, seek all dharmas; with a thought free from doubt, truly visualize within all practices. Just as a bodhisattva dwelling on the Dharma-Cloud Stage perceives the dharma-body, reward-body, and transformation-body of all tathāgatas, free from all discrimination and all doubt, abiding in true vision of all dharmas, so too one who truly knows this secret great merit-dharma. In brief, this secret dharma is the vajra great wisdom of the bodhisattva."

Then all the tathāgatas, having heard this, exclaimed that it was unprecedented, and a great person appeared in the assembly who bowed to Vajrapāṇi and asked: "Regarding this secret great vidyā practice dharma-gate of the first principle, among those who have faith and those who do not, what is the meaning?"

Vajrapāṇi replied: "Suppose all sentient beings in a great trichiliocosm as numerous as dust-motes could all realize supreme enlightenment. Could this be believed? The secret dharma-gate should be seen in just this way. To see it thus is to see free from discriminative views."

The great person manifested by all the tathāgatas then felt great joy, exclaiming it was unprecedented, and spoke this gāthā:

> "Great is wisdom, great wisdom! The great vajra has appeared!
> The blind of the world attain purified eyes of wisdom."

Then Vajrapāṇi entered the samādhi of all vajras' non-dual equality vajra. From this samādhi arose hundreds of thousands of koṭi-nayutas of lights numbering as Ganges-sands, illuminating buddha-fields of unspeakable dust-mote numbers in the southeast direction. The great bodhisattvas in those buddha-fields who were touched by the light all attained the perfection of the great wisdom of the three bodies. All tathāgatas abided in bodhicitta. All beings entered the fearless practice-gate of bodhicitta's self-nature, abiding in the dharma-gate of the vajra self-nature, great wisdom, equal as space, the equality-vajra awakening, all dharmas without marks, free from conceptual elaboration, unborn, pure, supreme.

Then Vajrapāṇi arose peacefully from samādhi and universally addressed the great assembly: "Great beings! You should regard all sentient beings with an equal view, as you would regard the buddhas."

At that time, the great person manifested by transformation withdrew into the treasury of all tathāgatas' body, speech, and mind jewels and suddenly vanished. The tathāgatas from the ten directions and all the great bodhisattvas each abided in their own secret correspondence dharma in accordance with principle. Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva likewise abided in his own body, speech, and mind secret correspondence true dharma.
