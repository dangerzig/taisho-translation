# 普賢菩薩行願讚
## Praise of the Vows and Practices of the Bodhisattva Samantabhadra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0297

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空)

---

> In all the worlds of the ten directions,
> All the lion-among-humans of the three times:
> I now bow to them all without remainder,
> With purified body, speech, and mind.

> Bodies as numerous as atoms in all lands,
> To every Tathāgata I bow in reverence;
> With mind and thought directed to all Buddhas,
> By the power of Samantabhadra's vows and practices.

> Upon the tip of one atom, Buddhas as numerous as atoms,
> With Buddha-sons seated in their midst:
> Thus the dharma-realm without remainder,
> I have faith that all Buddhas fill completely.

> With an ocean of every kind of voice,
> And an ocean of merit-sounds,
> When proclaiming the merit of the Tathāgatas,
> I constantly praise all the Sugatas.

> With supreme flower-garlands, paste-incense,
> Music, supreme parasols,
> And all the most supreme adornments,
> I offer them all to the Tathāgatas.

> With supreme robes and all manner of incense,
> Powdered incense heaped high as Sumeru,
> Supreme lamps and burning incense,
> I offer them all to the Tathāgatas.

> All supreme and vast offerings:
> I direct my faith toward all the Tathāgatas.
> By the power of faith in Samantabhadra's practice,
> I bow in reverence and make offerings to all the Tathāgatas.

> Whatever evil deeds I have committed,
> All through greed, hatred, and delusion,
> Through body, speech, and mind alike:
> I now confess them all.

> In the merit of all beings of the ten directions,
> Of learners, non-learners, and pratyekabuddhas,
> Of all the Buddha-sons and all the Tathāgatas:
> I completely rejoice in all of it.

> To all the lamps of the worlds in the ten directions,
> Who have realized enlightenment and attained non-defilement:
> I now urge and invite all the World-Honored Ones
> To turn the unsurpassed, wondrous Dharma-wheel.

> To all those who wish to manifest nirvāṇa:
> I join my palms and beseech them all:
> May they abide for kalpas as numerous as atoms in all lands,
> For the benefit and joy of all beings.

> Bowing, making offerings, confessing evil,
> Rejoicing in merit, urging, and inviting:
> All the merit I have accumulated
> I dedicate entirely to enlightenment.

> From all the Tathāgatas I learn and practice;
> When perfecting Samantabhadra's vows and practices,
> May I make offerings to all past Buddhas
> And all those now dwelling in worlds of the ten directions.

> May all those of the future swiftly accomplish
> Their wish and realize enlightenment.
> May all the lands of the ten directions
> Become vast, purified, and adorned.

> May Buddhas all arrive at the king of enlightenment-trees,
> And may all their Buddha-sons fill them.
> May all beings of the ten directions
> Be at peace, free from all afflictions.

> May all beings obtain the benefit of the Dharma
> And gain the mind that accords with their wishes.
> When I cultivate on the path of enlightenment,
> In all destinies may I remember past lives.

> In every life, through birth and death,
> May I always go forth as a renunciant.
> May my precept-conduct be stainless and always pure,
> My practice always without flaws or gaps.

> In the languages of devas, nāgas, yakṣas,
> Kumbhāṇḍas, humans,
> And all the languages of every kind of being:
> May I teach the Dharma in all their voices.

> May I always apply myself to the wondrous pāramitās,
> Never becoming confused about the mind of enlightenment.
> May all sins and obstructions
> Be entirely eliminated without remainder.

> From karma, afflictions, and māra-realms,
> In the paths of the world may I gain liberation,
> Like a lotus untouched by water,
> Like the sun and moon unattached to space.

> May the suffering of the evil paths be stilled,
> And all beings brought to peace and joy.
> May I bring benefit to all beings
> Throughout all the lands of the ten directions.

> Always following along with sentient beings,
> May the wondrous conduct of enlightenment be perfected.
> Samantabhadra's vows and practices I cultivate;
> Through future kalpas I practice them.

> All those who practice together with me:
> May we always gather together with them.
> In deeds of body, speech, and mind,
> May we cultivate the same vows and practices.

> All good friends who benefit me
> And reveal to me Samantabhadra's practice:
> May I always gather together with them
> And never have a mind of weariness toward them.

> Always seeing all the Tathāgatas,
> Surrounded by their Buddha-sons,
> May I offer vast offerings to them all,
> Without weariness through all future kalpas.

> Always holding the wondrous Dharma of all Buddhas,
> Causing the practice of enlightenment to shine forth,
> Entirely purifying Samantabhadra's practice:
> May I cultivate it through all future kalpas.

> When cycling through all existences,
> May I gain inexhaustible merit and wisdom.
> Prajñā, skillful means, samādhi, and liberation:
> May I obtain the inexhaustible treasury of merit.

> Upon one atom-tip are lands as numerous as atoms,
> With inconceivable buddha-lands therein;
> Buddhas and Buddha-sons seated in their midst:
> May I always see the supreme, wondrous practice of enlightenment.

> Thus in all directions without measure,
> Upon the tip of a single hair, the measure of the three times,
> The ocean of Buddhas and the ocean of lands:
> I enter and practice through the ocean of kalpas.

> Within the merit-ocean of a single voice,
> The pure voice of all Tathāgatas,
> The voice that delights all beings:
> May I always enter the eloquence of the Buddhas.

> Within those inexhaustible voices,
> All the Tathāgatas of the three times,
> When turning the wondrous wheel of truth:
> By the power of my wisdom may I universally enter.

> In a single instant, all the future:
> I enter all the kalpas of the future.
> All the immeasurable kalpas of the three times:
> In an instant I can enter koṭi kalpas.

> All the lion-among-humans of the three times:
> In a single instant I behold them all.
> I constantly enter their realms,
> With the power of conduct like illusory liberation.

> All the wondrous, adorned lands of the three times
> Can be manifested upon the tip of one atom.
> Thus all directions without limit:
> I can enter the adorned lands of all the Buddhas.

> All the future lamps of the world:
> They all awaken and turn the Dharma-wheel.
> Having shown ultimate, tranquil nirvāṇa:
> I go to attend upon all the World-Honored Ones.

> By the power of supernatural swiftness universally,
> By the power of the vehicle's universal access,
> By the power of conduct with equal merit,
> By the power of compassion universally pervading,

> By the power of merit, universally adorned,
> By the power of wisdom, free from attachment,
> By the power of prajñā, skillful means, and samādhi:
> I gather the power of enlightenment.

> Purifying the power of karma,
> I now crush the power of afflictions,
> And completely subdue the power of Māra:
> I perfect all the powers of Samantabhadra.

> Universally purifying the ocean of lands,
> Universally liberating the ocean of beings,
> Completely perceiving the ocean of dharmas,
> And reaching the source of the ocean of wisdom.

> Universally purifying the ocean of practices,
> And fulfilling the ocean of vows,
> Making offerings at all the ocean-assemblies of Buddhas:
> I practice Samantabhadra's conduct through kalpas without weariness.

> All the Tathāgatas of the three times,
> With their various vows and practices of enlightenment:
> May I fulfill them all without remainder,
> Awakening to enlightenment through Samantabhadra's practice.

> Of all the Buddhas and Tathāgatas, there is an eldest son:
> His name is the honored Samantabhadra.
> With wisdom and wondrous practice equal to his,
> I dedicate all my wholesome roots.

> May body, speech, and mind be purified,
> All practices purified, and all lands purified.
> Like the wisdom of the one named Samantabhadra:
> May I now become entirely equal to him.

> Samantabhadra's vows and practices universally adorn;
> I practice the conduct of Mañjuśrī.
> Through all future kalpas without weariness,
> I completely fulfill all without remainder.

> The supreme practices needed are immeasurable;
> The merit therein is beyond reckoning.
> Having dwelt in immeasurable cultivation,
> I fully know all their supernatural powers.

> When space reaches its ultimate end,
> When beings are without remainder and likewise end,
> When karma and afflictions reach their end:
> Only then do my vows also end.

> If there are those in the ten directions' boundless lands
> Who adorn them with jewels and give them to the Buddhas,
> With supreme joy of devas and humans,
> Giving for kalpas as numerous as atoms in those lands,

> Yet if a person, upon hearing this king of supreme vows,
> Gives rise to a single thought of supreme faith,
> Yearning and seeking the supreme enlightenment:
> They obtain a mass of merit surpassing the former.

> Such a person is far removed from the evil paths,
> And far removed from all evil friends.
> They will swiftly see the Buddha Amitāyus.
> Simply by recalling Samantabhadra's supreme vows and practices,

> They obtain great benefit and a supreme lifespan.
> Well come, for this person's life is born.
> Like the great bodhisattva Samantabhadra himself:
> That person will before long attain it.

> Whatever sins of the five acts of immediate retribution,
> Committed through lack of wisdom:
> When that person recites Samantabhadra's vows and practices,
> They are swiftly eliminated without remainder.

> Wisdom, appearance, marks, and excellent qualities,
> Clan and family are all accomplished.
> By māras and non-Buddhist paths one cannot be crushed,
> And in the three realms one is always honored with offerings.

> Swiftly proceeding to the bodhi-tree,
> Arriving there and sitting for the benefit of beings,
> Awakening to enlightenment and turning the Dharma-wheel,
> Subduing Māra together with his retinue.

> If there are those who uphold these Samantabhadra vows,
> Who read, recite, receive, uphold, and explain them:
> The Tathāgata fully knows their fruition.
> They will attain the supreme enlightenment; let there be no doubt.

> Like Mañjuśrī's courageous wisdom,
> And like Samantabhadra's wisdom too:
> When I learn and practice as they do,
> I dedicate all my wholesome roots.

> All the Tathāgatas of the three times
> Praise this supreme vow of dedication.
> All my wholesome roots
> I have fully dedicated to Samantabhadra's practice.

> When, at the time of death, I relinquish this life,
> May all karmic obstructions be transformed.
> May I see Amitābha face to face
> And swiftly go to that land of Sukhāvatī.

> Having arrived there, may these supreme vows
> All be fully and completely manifest before me.
> May I fulfill them all without remainder,
> Bringing benefit to beings in the world.

> In that supremely adorned Buddha-assembly,
> Born within a supreme lotus flower,
> May I there receive a prediction,
> Face to face with the Tathāgata Amitābha.

> Having there received the prediction,
> I shall transform into koṭi immeasurable forms,
> Vastly bringing benefit and joy to sentient beings,
> Throughout the worlds of the ten directions by the power of wisdom.

> If a person recites and upholds Samantabhadra's vows,
> All the wholesome roots they have accumulated:
> In a single instant their wishes are fulfilled,
> And thereby all beings attain the supreme vow.

> Having obtained this Samantabhadra practice,
> With its supreme, immeasurable accumulation of merit:
> May all beings drowning in evil habits
> All proceed to the palace of the Buddha Amitābha.

---

> The gate of the palace of perfect nirvāṇa,
> He who can break open its doors:
> The Buddhas' Dharma-enjoyment,
> The savior of the world, to you I bow my head.

> From his own hands flows pure water
> That can quench the thirst of hungry ghosts.
> The wish-fulfilling tree of the three realms:
> I bow my head to Padmapāṇi.

> Great compassion, water as his heart,
> He can extinguish the fire of anger.
> I bow my head to the honored Maitreya,
> Who can sever the bowstring of desire.

> Ākāśagarbha of wondrous wisdom,
> The honored one tranquil as empty space,
> Liberation from the stream of birth and death:
> I bow my head to this son of the Buddha's heart.

> The delusions of boundless sentient beings,
> He can still their useless minds.
> Samantabhadra, to you I bow my head,
> Foremost son of the Sugatas.

> Ending the servitude of defilements,
> Overcoming the army of Māra:
> I bow my head to Vajrapāṇi,
> Who can proclaim all the vidyā-mantras.

> I bow my head to Mañjuśrī,
> Who bears the form of a wondrous youth,
> Extending the lamp of omniscient wisdom,
> Outshining the brilliance of the three realms.

> Sarvanīvaraṇaviṣkambhin, Remover of All Obstructions:
> Therefore I bow my head to you,
> Honored one of inexhaustible wisdom,
> Who can produce inexhaustible eloquence.

> Like the earth, upon which all beings
> Depend without interruption:
> Treasury of firm wisdom and compassion,
> Kṣitigarbha, to you I bow my head.

> The merit obtained by praising
> These true sons of the Sugatas:
> By this, may all sentient beings
> Likewise become vessels worthy of such praise.

---

The dhāraṇī for swiftly fulfilling Samantabhadra's vows and practices:

*Samāsthiryā-adhivikānāṃ tathāgatānāṃ. Oṃ aśvara-vigini svāhā.*

After reciting the Praise of the Vows and Practices of the Bodhisattva Samantabhadra each day, one should immediately recite this dhāraṇī. Having recited it even once, the vows and practices of Samantabhadra are all entirely fulfilled. For a person of samādhi, they will swiftly attain samādhi manifesting before them. Merit and wisdom, the two kinds of adornment, obtain a firm and solid Dharma, and are swiftly accomplished.
