# 賢劫十六尊
## The Sixteen Deities of the Bhadrakalpa

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0881

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown

---

> Outside the wheel-maṇḍala, among the thousand Buddhas of the Bhadrakalpa,
> Sixteen are the foremost. Maitreya holds a water-flask;
> Amogha-padma-netra has a lotus-eye; Apagata-śoka holds the palm-leaf book;
> Apagata-pāpa holds a three-pronged vajra; Gandhahastin holds a lotus;
> Mahāvīrya holds a spear and halberd; Ākāśagarbha has jewel-radiance;
> Jñānadhvaja holds a banner-standard; Amitābha holds a lotus;
> Bhadrapāla holds a jewel-vase; Jālinīprabha holds a net-parasol;
> Candraprabha holds a half-moon banner; Anantamati holds a palm-leaf book;
> Vāk-saṃcaya holds a flower-cloud; Vajragarbha holds a single-pronged vajra;
> Samantabhadra has the five-wisdom seal. The seed-syllable begins with *a*;
> The syllable *hūṃ* presses the left hand to the earth.

Their respective mantras are:

*Oṃ maitreyāya svāhā a*

*Oṃ amogha-nayanāya svāhā ga*

*Oṃ sarva-pāpāya jahāya svāhā bha*

*Oṃ sarva-śoka-tamo-nirgatāya svāhā aṃ*

*Oṃ gandha-hastini svāhā ṅa*

*Oṃ śūrāya svāhā vi*

*Oṃ gaganagañjāya svāhā ak*

*Oṃ jñāna-ketu-me svāhā traṃ*

*Oṃ amita-prabhāya svāhā dhvaṃ*

*Oṃ bhadra-pālāya svāhā pri*

*Oṃ jālinī-prabhāya svāhā ja*

*Oṃ candra-prabhāya svāhā ma*

*Oṃ akṣaya-mataye svāhā kiniya*

*Oṃ pratibhāna-kūṭāya svāhā laṃ*

*Oṃ vajra-garbhāya svāhā va*

*Oṃ samanta-bhadrāya svāhā ak*

> In the outer court of the Vajradhātu, the earth-dwelling and sky-roaming devas,
> Briefly described, are of five classes. The wrathful ones have the syllable *hūṃ*.
> Arrayed are twenty devas, with water-chestnut lotuses as their seats.
> In the northeast, Nārāyaṇa holding a wheel, in rākṣasa form;
> Kumāra with a bell-contract; Maheśvara who crushes;
> With a parasol-canopy and jewel at the east gate; Brahmā holding a red lotus;
> Indra with a single-pronged vajra; Agni in the southeast,
> With a triangle as emblem; Sūrya in youthful form;
> Vajra-āhāra holding a garland; Yama with a daṇḍa-seal;
> Aṅgāraka with a cluster of fire-light; In the southwest, the lord of rākṣasas
> Holding a hammer with his retinue; Bṛhaspati, the deva, holding a staff;
> Vajra-vāsas, the yakṣa, at the west gate holding bow and arrows;
> Varuṇa holding a lasso; next is placed Candra;
> Vāyu-dhvaja in the northwest corner; Vajra-mukha holding a hook;
> Vinayaka with a vajra-sword; at the gate's east, Vaiśravaṇa
> Holding a stūpa with a jewel-staff; Īśāna with a halberd-seal.

Their respective mantras are:

*Oṃ māla-vajra*

*Oṃ vajra-ghaṇṭa*

*Oṃ vajra-guṇa*

*Oṃ vajra-mūna*

*Oṃ vajra-yudha*

*Oṃ vajra-nāla*

*Oṃ vajra-kuṭali*

*Oṃ vajra-maṇila*

*Oṃ vajra-kāla*

*Oṃ vajra-piṅgala*

*Oṃ vajra-daṇḍa*

*Oṃ vajra-musala*

*Oṃ vajra-vāśi*

*Oṃ nāga-vajra*

*Oṃ vajra-prabha*

*Oṃ vajra-nīla*

*Oṃ vajra-kuśa*

*Oṃ vajra-jaya*

*Oṃ vajra-bhairava*

*Oṃ rudrāya vajra*

> These great deva hosts, their majestic virtues have many gates.
> Their queens and retinues are all within the maṇḍala.
> One should fully reveal and explain them. For the offering to the yakṣa-deva,
> He should reside east of the north gate.

His mantra is:

*Oṃ vajra-vināyaka svāhā*

---

Colophon: In the fifth month of Kyōwa 2 [1802 CE], a year of *mizunoe-inu*, collated from the Chishaku-in edition of Kyoto against the Kominebō edition of Buzan, and printed for distribution. Recorded by the Shingon monk Kaidō, residing at the Myōon-in of Hase.
