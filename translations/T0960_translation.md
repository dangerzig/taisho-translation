# 一切如來說佛頂輪王一百八名讚
## Hymn of the One Hundred and Eight Names of Uṣṇīṣacakravartin, as Spoken by All Tathāgatas

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 960

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dānapāla (施護)

---

At that time, all Tathāgatas proclaimed the One Hundred and Eight Names of Uṣṇīṣacakravartin, Wheel-Turning King and Lord of the Great Luminous Dhāraṇī of Immeasurable Merit. They then spoke this hymn of praise:

> I take refuge in the supreme teacher, Lord of all worlds,
> Who ever harbors great compassion and manifests great renown.
>
> Great hero of the ten powers, whose merit transcends the three realms,
> Endowed with all auspicious marks, ever revered by gods and humans,
> Embracing all sentient beings, granting them a great accumulation of merit.
>
> Also named the Great Sovereign (Maheśvara), King of all Buddha-crowns,
> Who turns the wheel of all dhāraṇīs and perfects the phrases of the true Dharma.
>
> With holy wisdom penetrating thoroughly, liberating all completely,
> Ever the great sovereign, transcending all devas.
>
> The embodiment of great meditative absorption, regarding all beings with equanimity,
> Endowed with boundless wisdom, neither real nor unreal,
> Neither one nor two, called the Great Celestial Sage.
>
> Most rare in all the world, whose very nature is primordial cessation,
> Pure and unstained, skillfully entering into the inexhaustible nature.
>
> Vast and open, without fear, neither severed nor destroyed,
> Without conceptual thought yet not empty, able to relinquish the ten thousand phenomena,
> Unattached like empty space, wondrous form that is itself empty form.
>
> The auspicious mark is an auspicious omen, serene and ever resolute,
> From noble aspiration arises wondrous Brahmic sound, the image among those devas.
>
> Sovereign and vast beyond limit, merit of immeasurable extent,
> Mind and speech inconceivable, without equal and equal to the without-equal.
>
> True image vast and boundless, ultimately unsurpassed,
> Throughout worlds without limit, all pay reverence.
>
> Lord of all dhāraṇī-images, this image is unprecedented,
> Auspicious image rarely found, possessing the divine eye and the three eyes.
>
> The true three liberations, true speech and true serenity,
> The true Dharma abiding in ultimate reality, contemplating the wondrous dharma-body,
> Serene, ever firm and enduring, of pure nature that is resolute.
>
> Vāyu-deva and Candra-deva, the light of the sun and wondrous mind,
> Letters in sets of three and six, one dhāraṇī and one syllable-king,
> One wisdom that is all wisdom, sovereign and great sovereign.
>
> Brahmā and Nārāyaṇa, one Buddha and one dharma-form,
> One deva dwelling in one assembly, one samādhi and one accord,
> Throughout all worlds, proclaiming the many wondrous dharmas from on high.
>
> With this supernatural luminance, transforming with light throughout all places,
> Great light of awesome virtue and power, Lord of all devas,
> Lord of all Buddha-dharmas, Lord of the wisdom of all dharmas,
> An inexhaustible accumulation of merit: these are the One Hundred and Eight Names.
