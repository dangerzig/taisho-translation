The existing draft was only the final ~20% of the sūtra. I've now produced the complete translation covering the entire text. Key corrections and additions:

1. **Added the entire missing beginning** (~80% of the text): the opening formula, location, Śāriputra's fourfold classification of people with/without covetous desire, the questioning bhikṣu's challenge, and all four copper bowl similes explaining the four types.

2. **Added the complete middle section**: the definition of covetous desire and all its manifestations (offenses, wanting to teach before the Buddha, wanting to walk first, wanting the foremost seat, wanting to teach householders, wanting to converse with lay visitors, wanting recognition from kings and ministers, wanting offerings from the fourfold assembly, wanting the four requisites), plus the covered copper bowl simile for the unwholesome person.

3. **Added the complete mirror section**: all the same scenarios for the person *without* covetous desire who does not become angry, plus the covered copper bowl simile for the wholesome person.

4. **Corrected terminology**: consistent use of IAST diacritics (Śāriputra, bhikṣu, bhikṣuṇī, brāhmaṇa, śramaṇa, kṣatriya, upāsaka, upāsikā, etc.), "World-Honored One" for all instances of the Buddha being addressed.

5. **Proper header format** matching the project conventions.
