# 佛說一切佛攝相應大教王經聖觀自在菩薩念誦儀軌
## Recitation Ritual of the Holy Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva from the Sūtra of the Great Teaching King of All Buddhas' Concordant Absorption, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1051

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmadeva (法賢).

---

> I now take refuge in the buddhas and bodhisattvas,
> Who expounded the great teaching of concordant absorption.
> I briefly describe the ritual of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva,
> Which accomplishes all aims and benefits living beings.

At that time the World-Honored One, wishing to benefit all sentient beings, expounded the great teaching of all buddhas' concordant absorption. The Buddha said: "If an ācārya or practitioner wishes to cultivate the factors of awakening and various accomplishments, they should practice the method of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva. When practicing this method, first seek a quiet place. Following the ritual, with single-minded diligence, practice the preliminary exercises until they are fully mastered. Then, according to the method, perform the visualization and recite the syllable *hūṃ*. After selecting the ground, establishing the boundary, performing the rites, and dispatching the māras, spread grass at that place and sit down. When the practitioner is about to direct the mind to visualize the noble ones, they must first purify the three karmas. Recite the mantra for purifying the three karmas:

*Oṃ kāya-vāk-citta-vajra-svabhāva-trāṃ*

"Having recited this mantra, further speak the illustrative gāthā:

> "Just as the pure and wondrous lotus,
> Though born in mud, cannot be stained,
> So too all sentient beings amid afflictions
> May attain purity throughout the three realms."

"Having recited the gāthā, visualize a jeweled crown upon one's head containing Amitāyus Buddha. Next, from the heart, visualize an open white lotus emerging. Upon that lotus, visualize a moon disc. Within the moon disc, visualize the syllable *oṃ* appearing, its nature like space, being solely true and great wisdom. Then recite the wisdom-opening mantra:

*Oṃ citta-prativedikaṃ karomi*

"Having recited this mantra, further visualize various jewels transforming into an eight-petaled lotus. Upon that lotus, visualize a moon disc. Within the moon disc, the syllable *hrīḥ* appears, which is the original body of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, seated in full lotus posture, red in body color with two arms. To the bodhisattva's left, visualize Pāṇḍaravāsinī Bodhisattva, hand holding a lotus. Next, visualize the eight directional noble ones: First, on the eastern petal of the lotus, visualize Vilokini Bodhisattva appearing, hand holding a red lotus. The bodhisattva's mantra:

*Oṃ vilokini avalokaya svāhā*

"On the southern petal of the lotus, visualize Tārā Bodhisattva appearing, hand holding a green lotus. The bodhisattva's mantra:

*Oṃ tāre tuttāre ture svāhā*

"Next, on the western petal of the lotus, visualize Bhṛkuṭī Bodhisattva, hand holding an utpala flower with an eight-spoked wheel inside. The bodhisattva's mantra:

*Oṃ bhṛkuṭi mahā-cakra-vartini hūṃ*

"Next, on the northern petal of the lotus, visualize Bhrūṃkuṭī Bodhisattva appearing, hand holding a yellow lotus. The bodhisattva's mantra:

*Oṃ bhṛkuṭi tatkṛti maṭṭa hūṃ*

"Next, on the northeastern petal of the lotus, visualize Padmavāsinī Bodhisattva appearing, hand holding a scarlet lotus. The bodhisattva's mantra:

*Oṃ mahā-śriye maṇi-padme hūṃ*

"Next, on the southeastern petal of the lotus, visualize Vajrapadma-śrī Bodhisattva appearing, hand holding a white lotus. The bodhisattva's mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-padme śvari-dhara-padme hūṃ*

"Next, on the southwestern petal of the lotus, visualize Viśvapadma Bodhisattva appearing, hand holding a black lotus. The bodhisattva's mantra:

*Oṃ viśva-padme hrīḥ*

"Next, on the northwestern petal of the lotus, visualize Viśvavajra Bodhisattva appearing, hand holding a karma-vajra. The bodhisattva's mantra:

*Oṃ viśva-vajre hūṃ*

"Next, at the center of the lotus, again visualize Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva manifesting a great and wondrous body. The mantra:

*Oṃ hrīḥ padma-nārtteśvara hūṃ*

"All these great bodhisattvas and their subtle mantras: if the practitioner performs the visualizations according to the method and recites the mantras with focused, unwavering attention, that person will swiftly attain buddhahood in the future. Even while enjoying the five sense pleasures, they will be able to perfect the ten pāramitās and the gate of dhāraṇī. Up through the stage of pratyekabuddha, the ten bhūmis of a bodhisattva, and the peaceful stage of buddhahood, all will be swiftly accomplished. Do not harbor any doubt.

"Furthermore, the practitioner should also regularly take the five kinds of great vehicle medicine, namely:

*Bodhi-citta-kari-pūra-rakta-candana-vajra-unaka*

"If one can regularly take these medicines and recite without ceasing, one will swiftly be freed from old age, illness, and suffering, one's lifespan will increase, one's appearance will be transformed, and all who see one will love and respect one. In this very life, one will attain the supreme wisdom.

"Furthermore, all the methods of pacification, enrichment, attraction, and subjugation, as well as summoning, elimination, dispatching of māras, binding of enemies, and striking methods, up to flying through space at will and various samādhis: if one practices the method of Avalokiteśvara, all will be accomplished.

"The root mantra of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva:

*Oṃ hrīḥ padma-nārtteśvara hūṃ*

"Furthermore, if the practitioner constantly recites this root mantra of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva one hundred and eight times daily, all sins committed by that person day and night will be completely eliminated. If one recites five hundred times daily, it can eliminate all illness. If one recites one thousand times daily, one's lifespan will increase. If one recites two thousand times, one will gain the respect of all people. If one recites three thousand times, all people will love and respect one and one's wealth and honor will increase. If one recites four thousand times, one will be freed from poverty and hardship. If one recites five thousand times, one can cause all enemies to become confused in body and mind. If one recites six thousand times, one can subdue all enemies. If one recites seven thousand times, one can subdue the four māras. If one recites eight thousand times, one can subdue all devas and celestial women. If one recites nine thousand times, one can subdue yakṣas and yakṣa women. If one recites ten thousand times, one can subdue devas, nāgas, and humans. If one recites twenty thousand times, the hidden noble ones will grant accomplishment. If one recites thirty thousand times, one can fulfill all wishes. If one recites forty thousand times, one will attain the power of flight at will. If one recites fifty thousand times, one will attain the accomplishment of the sacred sword. If one recites sixty thousand times, one will attain the rank of a vidyādhara. If one recites seventy thousand times, one will become lord of the world. If one recites eighty thousand times, one will attain the position of an iron wheel-turning king. If one recites ninety thousand times, one will attain the position of a gold wheel-turning king. If one recites one hundred thousand times, one will attain the stage of a bodhisattva. If one recites one million times, one will attain mastery and fulfill the ten bhūmis. If one recites five million times, one will attain the stage of a buddha. If one recites ten million times, one will attain the complete body, speech, and mind of Amitāyus Buddha of the Land of Ultimate Bliss, benefiting and delighting sentient beings." At that time the World-Honored One further spoke the mantra of Pāṇḍaravāsinī Bodhisattva:

*Oṃ paṇḍara-vāsinī varade hūṃ*

"This subtle mantra of Pāṇḍaravāsinī Bodhisattva: if a person can constantly recite it, all sins and karmic obstructions will be completely eliminated. If one can recite with focused attention according to the method for a full seven days, all the people within the kingdom will revere and honor one. If one recites for one month, one will obtain great wealth and honor. If one recites to a full one hundred thousand times, one will attain power equal to that of a bodhisattva."

Furthermore, the mantra of Vilokini Bodhisattva:

*Oṃ vilokini avalokaya svāhā*

"This bodhisattva's subtle mantra: if a person recites it according to the method ten thousand times, they will attain the accomplishment of the sacred sword. If the practitioner has something they wish to know, they should take a tooth-stick, empower it with twenty-one recitations, clean the teeth, and then sleep. Whatever one wishes to know will be revealed in dreams, and all that is told will be true."

Furthermore, the mantra of Tārā Bodhisattva:

*Oṃ tāre tuttāre ture svāhā*

"This bodhisattva's subtle mantra: if a person recites it according to the method one hundred thousand times, that person can subdue all devas, nāgas, asuras, and vidyādharas of the three realms, and their women will come as servants, all in accord with one's mind. If the practitioner wishes to summon those devas, nāgas, and their women, one should visualize their bodies as red, and within their hearts a vajra hook blazing with light, hooking their hearts. Further visualize a lasso binding their necks, with a fearsome appearance. Whatever one summons, during the recitation they will certainly arrive swiftly, and whatever one seeks will accord with one's mind."

Furthermore, the mantra of Bhṛkuṭī Bodhisattva:

*Oṃ bhṛkuṭi mahā-cakra-vartini hūṃ*

"This bodhisattva's subtle mantra: if a person wishes to achieve accomplishment through recitation, they should first use medicines such as *viḍaṅga*, *vajruṇaka*, and others to smear a maṇḍala in the shape of a full moon, then according to their means, prepare incense, flowers, and other items to make offerings within it. The practitioner should then spread a seat and sit, reciting with single-minded focus without ceasing. Whatever one seeks will certainly be accomplished and one will experience happiness. After death, one will be reborn in the Land of Ultimate Bliss."

Furthermore, the mantra of Bhrūṃkuṭī Bodhisattva:

*Oṃ bhṛkuṭi tatkṛti maṭṭa hūṃ*

"This bodhisattva's subtle mantra: if a person wishes to perform the subjugation method through recitation, they should take soil from beneath the enemy's feet and soil from the two banks of a river, and further use poison, mustard seeds, salt, blood, and *viḍaṅga*, *vajruṇaka*, and other substances, mixing them into clay to form an effigy of the enemy. Use human bone as a stake and pierce the effigy's body and joints. Place it inside a skull and cover with the skull lid. In the charnel ground, roast it over a cremation fire while reciting the mantra and empowering it for three periods of seven days and nights. The subjugation will certainly be accomplished."

Furthermore, the mantra of Padmavāsinī Bodhisattva:

*Oṃ mahā-śriye maṇi-padme hūṃ*

"This bodhisattva's subtle mantra: when reciting, one should first make offerings of incense, flowers, and the five kinds of offerings before beginning. It can provide protection in all places. If one engages in debate, one will certainly be victorious and attain great mastery equal to Avalokiteśvara. If one performs a homa with ten thousand lotuses, one will become lord of the land. If one performs a homa with one hundred thousand lotuses, one will attain the position of a wheel-turning king."

Furthermore, the mantra of Vajrapadma-śrī Bodhisattva:

*Oṃ vajra-padme śrī-vara-padme hūṃ*

"This bodhisattva's subtle mantra: when the practitioner directs the mind in recitation, it can subdue and summon. If one recites seven times, it can protect one's own body. If one recites one thousand times, it can eliminate dangers from poison, weapons, lions, elephants, snakes, fire, thieves, imprisonment, sea demons, and other calamities, and can eliminate diseases such as *kharkhoda*, *kūṇaka*, *ducchari*, *mṛtāṅga*, and others, as well as repel all evil and venomous creatures. If one recites one hundred thousand times, one's lifespan will increase, old age will revert to youth, one's appearance will be perfect, and all people will love and respect one. If a region suffers from drought, one should select a site and paint a nāga pool. Before the pool, with the mind focused on the nāga's name, perform a homa with white mustard seeds and salt, and a great rain will fall, bringing abundance to the entire region."

Furthermore, the mantra of Viśvapadma Bodhisattva:

*Oṃ viśva-padme hrīḥ*

"This bodhisattva's subtle mantra: if a person recites it with focused attention, they will perfect the karmas of body, speech, and mind, and can accomplish various things, such as binding methods, ghost-quelling methods, staking methods, methods for hooking others' minds, illusory transformation methods, and up to opening asura caves, invisibility, and flying through space at will; all can be accomplished."

Furthermore, the mantra of Viśvavajra Bodhisattva:

*Oṃ viśva-vajre hūṃ*

"This bodhisattva's subtle mantra: if the practitioner recites it with focused, unwavering diligence according to the method, they will constantly be able to protect self and others and gain the love and respect of all. They will also obtain great eloquence and surpass all others in debate. Furthermore, this bodhisattva can rescue sentient beings from the realms of hell, hungry ghosts, and animals with great diligence, just like Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva. If there are those who have faith in and reverence for the Buddha-dharma, the bodhisattva will protect them always, like a mother." The Buddha said: "If furthermore there are people who, regarding this Sūtra of the Great Teaching King of All Buddhas' Concordant Absorption and the Recitation Ritual of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, are free from doubt and cultivate it with firm resolve, they will attain the ultimate dharma of accomplishment. Furthermore, even one who has not entered the maṇḍala, who is still beset by afflictions and has meager merit, may freely engage in all activities, whether walking, standing, sitting, or lying down, whether speaking or laughing, and regardless of circumstance, so long as they constantly generate the mind of awakening, contemplate true reality, and maintain their recitation; all methods they practice will be accomplished. Furthermore, the practitioner who generates the mind of awakening and takes the five kinds of holy medicine, namely *mahā-rakta*, *kari-pūra*, *rakta-candana*, and other holy medicines:

"By following the Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva ritual in recitation and making offerings to the noble ones, even without making a buddha image, one can attain the supreme concordant absorption and the stage of Vajrasattva Bodhisattva, swiftly realizing awakening. What signs indicate such accomplishment? The factors of awakening, pāramitās, gate of dhāraṇī, ten bhūmis, and all else will naturally be accomplished. If a person can recite in accordance with the dharma of all buddhas' concordant absorption, they will be no different from all the buddhas.

"Furthermore, if the practitioner's mind is not yet tranquil and they cannot comprehend the dharma of concordant absorption, I shall now explain the method for painting the image. If the practitioner wishes to paint the image, first seek a skilled painter and have them purify body and mind in advance and observe the precepts. Use a fine piece of silk with selvedge on both ends, two cubits in length, and with the finest pigments, paint a moon disc on the silk. Within the moon disc, paint a multicolored eight-petaled lotus. At the center of the lotus, paint Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, red in body color, with three faces and six arms, wearing a jeweled crown containing Akṣobhya Buddha. The right face is black with a slightly wrathful expression; the left face is white. The six arms hold lotuses according to the method. On the eastern petal, paint Vilokini Bodhisattva, white in body color, with the left hand holding a red lotus and the right hand holding a vajra. On the southern petal, paint Tārā Bodhisattva, green in body color, with the left hand holding a green lotus and the right hand holding a jewel. On the western petal, paint Bhṛkuṭī Bodhisattva, golden in body color, holding an utpala flower containing a wheel. On the northern petal, paint Bhrūṃkuṭī Bodhisattva, reddish-white in body color, holding a yellow lotus and a sharp sword. On the northeastern petal, paint Padmavāsinī Bodhisattva, golden in body color, holding a scarlet lotus. On the southeastern petal, paint Vajrapadma-śrī Bodhisattva, body colored like space, holding a white lotus and a crystal rosary. On the southwestern petal, paint Viśvapadma Bodhisattva, body colored like an autumn moon, holding a black lotus and a scripture. On the northwestern petal, paint Viśvavajra Bodhisattva, body of various colors, holding a lotus. Next, at the four corners outside the petals, paint the four attendant bodhisattvas of Play, Garland, Song, and Dance. Next, at the four gate positions, visualize the four subtle syllables *jaḥ*, *hūṃ*, *vaṃ*, *hoḥ*, which transform into the four bodhisattvas of Hook, Lasso, Chain, and Bell, guarding the four gates.

"Furthermore, the method for painting the image. As before, select a skilled painter. On white silk, paint Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, body colored like an autumn moon, with perfect marks, wearing a jeweled crown containing Amitāyus Buddha. He has eight arms: the first right hand holds a rosary, the second hand holds a jeweled staff, the third hand holds a trident, the fourth hand forms the wish-granting mudrā. The first left hand holds a lotus, the second hand holds a kuṇḍikā, the third hand holds a lasso, the fourth hand holds a Prajñāpāramitā sūtra. He wears a tiger skin as a garment and a nāga as a sash. He sits or stands in full lotus posture upon a lotus. To the bodhisattva's right, paint the practitioner holding an arghya vessel and incense burner, with a posture of reverence.

"Further method for painting the image. Following the appearance of Mount Potalaka, paint Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, white in body color with perfect marks, adorned with white garments. The right hand holds a rosary; the left hand supports the cheek in a posture of contemplating the benefit of all sentient beings. He sits upon a lion-lotus throne, with the left foot hanging down stepping upon a lotus, and the right foot resting upon the lotus seat.

"Further method for painting the image. As before, on white silk, paint Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, reddish-white in body color, with ten arms. The first right hand holds a rosary; the remaining four hands form the fearlessness mudrā for rescuing sentient beings from danger. The first left hand holds a double-stemmed red lotus with a Prajñāpāramitā sūtra atop it; the remaining four hands also form the fearlessness mudrā for rescuing sentient beings from danger. He sits upon a lion-lotus throne with the left foot hanging down, supported by a lotus. To the bodhisattva's right, paint a lion, elephant, fire, and snake; to the left, paint bandits, imprisonment, the sea, and demons. These are the eight perils, and the appearance should show the bodhisattva rescuing sentient beings from them.

"Further method for painting the image. As before, on white silk, paint Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva within a moon disc, manifesting various colors, with thirteen heads, each wearing a jeweled crown containing Amitāyus Buddha, with the main face perfectly round. The thirteenth face is green and appears as a horse's head. He has one hundred arms, with hands holding various karma implements. Or one may paint one hundred heads and one thousand arms, likewise with hands holding various karma implements. In all these painted images, to the right side paint the practitioner in a kneeling posture of reverence, hands holding an arghya vessel and incense burner.

"Regarding all the above images, the practitioner should choose whichever form accords with their heart and paint that one image. For each and every one, one must generate the most supreme and excellent aspiration. When the painting is complete, prepare the finest varieties of incense, flowers, food, drink, and all offering implements, and set them before the image. Invite the ācārya to perform the consecration according to the method. The practitioner should then purify themselves and sit before the image with a settled mind, reciting the root subtle mantra of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva. If one is free from all doubts and maintains focused, unwavering attention, all undertakings will certainly be accomplished.

"Furthermore, after the ācārya has performed the consecration and transmitted the root subtle mantra, the practitioner, with the propriety of a disciple, should offer various precious jewels, gold, silver, and wealth, and even their kingdom, city, and very life, all to be presented to the ācārya as thanks. Why? Because the above dharma is exceedingly rare and difficult to encounter, how much more so its transmission. Therefore, with devoted heart, seek the ācārya and willingly offer one's delight. Furthermore, this dharma must not be transmitted to those who are casual about it. If such a person's mind is not devout, it may bring about negative consequences."
