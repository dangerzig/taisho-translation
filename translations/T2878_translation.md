# 救疾經
## Sūtra on Rescuing from Illness

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 2878

Translated from the Chinese. Author unknown.

---

At that time, the Buddha was at the twin śāla trees. Ānanda and an immeasurable number of devas, together with all the devas and humans, had come to assemble. The Buddha sat upright and told Ānanda: "My disciples and all the bodhisattvas, the great kingdoms are at peace, the people are without disorder, the world is harmonious, and all reverently honor the Three Treasures. Those who violate and harm the Three Treasures..." The Buddha, while walking peacefully, saw three people whose bodies were covered with sores, foul and disintegrating, unable to speak clearly, their garments stained and filthy, bearing burdens as they walked, looking like diseased people who begged for a living.

The Buddha asked these three people: "Where have you come from? Whose children are you?" They were all silent. The Buddha earnestly pressed them. After three times of asking, they did not answer. The Buddha then used skillful means to convert them. The three people answered: "We are children of darkness and affliction. Why do you trouble yourself to ask?" The Buddha asked the three people: "Your illness, from where did it arise? How did you contract it?" The three people answered: "We do not know the cause of our affliction. Without feeling the pain or itch, the sores gradually spread. We pray the World-Honored One will explain the root causes of this illness so that we may be enlightened."

The Buddha then told Ānanda to assemble the retinue, and even the names of the Seven Buddhas all came to sit in assembly. "I shall observe the past karma of these people," and he then called upon the Seven Buddhas. The first is Vipaśyin Buddha. The second is Śikhin Buddha. The third is Viśvabhū Buddha. The fourth is Krakucchanda Buddha. The fifth is Kanakamuni Buddha. The sixth is Kāśyapa Buddha. The seventh is Śākyamuni Buddha. These Seven Buddhas all came and sat in assembly.

The Buddha further asked the Seven Buddhas: "These three people's illness, who gave it to them?" The Seven Buddhas, from the west, each answered: "I did not give it to them." The Buddha then entered the samādhi of dhyāna concentration and clearly observed the three illnesses, knowing the root causes. The Seven Buddhas said: "World-Honored One, this illness did not come from others. They themselves invited the affliction that produced it. It was the Vajra Secret-Trace One who, seeing these evil persons, struck them with his vajra-pestle and spat upon their faces, producing sores. Their eyebrows and beard fell out. This is because they offended the Three Treasures, causing it to be so."

The Buddha then asked: "Vajra Secret-Trace One, for what reason did you give these three people this illness?" The Vajra One answered: "World-Honored One, I have vowed to always remain at the Buddha's side, to protect the Three Treasures, and not to allow evil persons to damage or harm the Tathāgata's wholesome mind, causing it to be submerged. I saw that among these three people, one offended the Tathāgata by damaging and defacing the honored images. The second offended the right Dharma by stealing sūtra-images and severing the holy teaching, causing the wholesome Dharma to be obstructed. The third offended the holy saṃgha by cheating and harming the assembly, causing the saṃgha of the four paths to be diminished and destroyed."

The Vajra Secret-Trace One said to the Buddha: "If they had offended other spirits, the illness would not be like this. Their eyebrows and beard would not have fallen out. Their sores would itch and produce pus, and worldly treatments could cure them. But those who offend the Three Treasures cannot be treated by any worldly physician. Their faces emit light and fat oozes from their bodies."

The Vajra Secret-Trace One said to the Buddha: "My original vow, at the time the World-Honored One attained the Way, was to always remain at his side, to protect the Three Treasures, and to prevent māras, evil ghosts, and evil persons from coming to disturb and harm them. If any evil person humiliates, steals from, or damages the Three Treasures, my vajra-pestle will shatter their heads like the arjaka fruit splitting into seven pieces upon hitting the ground."

The Vajra Secret-Trace One said to the Buddha: "As for these three people: first, from their parents and even seven generations, the karmic debts pull upon one another, and they receive this evil retribution. Or from their own bodies, they committed subtle offenses without awareness. If the offense was intentional and they did not confess in time, the transgressions accumulated heavily. To cure this evil illness, the only remedy is to sincerely take refuge in the buddhas, to repent before the Seven Buddhas, to openly confess before the Vajra One, and with utmost sincerity to pray that the power of the Seven Buddhas may cause these grave transgressions to be eliminated."

The Buddha told all sentient beings: "Regarding the property of the Three Treasures, if anyone takes it, regardless of whether it was taken secretly or openly, once it enters their hands, it must be repaid in multiples." The Seven Buddhas answered: "If it is Buddha-property, once it enters one's hands, it must be repaid tenfold. If not returned within ten years, the Secret-Trace One becomes wrathful and can cause the taker to fall ill with evil disease. If it is the property of sūtra-images, once it enters one's hands, it must be repaid sevenfold. If not returned within seven years, this can cause the taker to fall ill with evil disease. If it is the permanently held property of the saṃgha as a whole, once it enters one's hands, it must be repaid fivefold. If not returned within five years, and one intentionally resists, this can cause the taker to fall ill with evil disease."

Ānanda said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One, people of Jambudvīpa are largely without faith. They say the Three Treasures do not exist. Those who offend them are many, and they invite evil illness. Their accumulated transgressions are countless. The people of Jambudvīpa kill their parents and destroy the Dharma rituals. And their paternal and maternal kinsmen and those under sworn contract, they kill. This directly causes evil illness."

"If anyone serves as guarantor who swears that this is true, and on the six purification days takes an oath before the Buddha, it causes a reciprocal retribution upon that person. Either the Four Heavenly Kings descend, or their prince descends, or their emissaries descend, or the Thirty-Three Devas descend, or great sages descend, or the one who severs life descends, or the vajra warriors descend. On the days they descend, they record people's good and evil. It is fitting to do good deeds and not fitting to do evil."

"If anyone steals the property of sūtra-images, knowing it to be so and deliberately doing it, this causes evil illness. If one handles gold, silver, copper, or iron, or if one secretly takes property of the Three Treasures, including money, grain, silk, or cloth, and years pass without returning it, this can cause evil illness."

"If anyone curses and swears while burning a buddha-image, pushes against a buddha's body, or burns or boils a holy visage; if one mars or extinguishes passages of a sūtra; or brings a woman inside the saṃghārāma to sleep; or brings a woman inside a buddha-stūpa and exchanges words and pledges with her; or together with a woman engages in lustful conduct; or commits such acts in person: these can cause evil illness. Such matters, after a long time, will result in illness, within no more than three years."

"If anyone secretly takes another's fasting rice and uses it to prepare a purification meal, knowing this and deliberately taking it, this can cause evil illness."

"If anyone takes another's colored cloth and gives it to a woman, the receiver who knows the situation and the giver are both guilty. Both persons become ill. If one secretly takes miscellaneous implements of monks or nuns, knowing this and not returning them, this can cause illness. If anyone, together with sūtra-images or oxen and donkeys, commits impure acts, this can cause illness. Though these are animals, if done in proximity to sūtra-images, illness results within three years. If anyone brings a woman into a Three Treasures building to commit impure acts, this can cause illness. If anyone abducts a nun who observes pure conduct, this can cause illness."

"If anyone, in a room where sūtra-images are kept, without a wooden case to store them below, sleeps together with a woman, this can cause illness. Within three years, all manner of evil illness will afflict the body. One should confess with bitter earnestness. Transgressions arise from the mind; transgressions are extinguished by the mind. The mind is like a heavenly palace; the mind is like a hell. With the palm facing up, it is a heavenly palace; with the palm facing down, it is a hell. To extinguish the grave transgressions within the body, confess with utmost sincerity and do not become lax or retreat."

The Buddha told the Seven Buddhas, the Vajra Secret-Trace One, all the great bodhisattvas, and the retinue: "These three sick people, how can they be saved? The great Vajra Warrior has revealed the root causes. Now they deserve compassion. Through skillful means, let us rescue and save them from the suffering of illness. The world calls the Tathāgata 'great compassion, great kindness.'"

The Seven Buddhas each said: "World-Honored One, all sentient beings, ignorant as they are, possess the Buddha-nature. The illness of these people is easy to eliminate and extinguish. Now let us ask them directly. The Vajra Secret-Trace One is my elder brother. Ānanda is my younger brother. My retinue is beyond count. The seer Asita can suppress poisons. The seer Arāḍa can spell away evil spirits. The Thirty-Three Devas can send down Dharma-water. The great physician of the Snow Mountains can bestow wondrous medicine. The sweet-dew Dharma-nectar can moisten what is withered and parched. King Ajātaśatru committed grave transgressions against his own body, yet even these could be eliminated: his bodily sores were removed. Through the Buddha's skillful means, his body was restored to its original state. What cannot be eliminated, what cannot be extinguished, is illness born from within one's own mind."

The Buddha then, through the contemplation of the mind's truth and falsehood, created by transformation a great pit, four thousand paces in circumference, filled with fire and coals. He asked King Ajātaśatru: "Can you enter this great pit to eliminate your transgressions and heal your sores?" King Ajātaśatru said: "If it can eliminate my transgressions, I shall enter immediately." King Ajātaśatru then, before the Buddha, burned incense and made a vow, and leaped into the fire. Upon entering, water welled up beneath his arms, transforming it into a bathing pool. All his transgressions were eliminated. It is like someone damming a long-flowing river: if the dam is above, the flow below is stopped. If one takes refuge in and repents before all buddhas, grave transgressions are eliminated. If one does not believe the words of the sūtra, even light transgressions are hard to eliminate.

The Buddha told the sick people: "I shall instruct you. You should, with utmost sincerity, for a period of a hundred days, invite a great virtuous Dharma-master and arrange purification meals. Each day, bow to the names of the Seven Buddhas. Each day, bow to the Vajra Secret-Trace One. Each day, bow to Amitāyus Buddha. Each day, produce one copy of the Sūtra on Rescuing from Illness. Within a hundred days, practice the Way and perform confession. When a hundred copies are completed, hold a ceremony for the salvation sūtra. This can free you from these accumulated calamities and afflictions."

"Do not be without faith. If you are, the sores will spread further. The Buddha's words are not false. The sūtra's teaching is not in error. These are words of the right Dharma, exceedingly profound, exceedingly good."

All the buddhas told the great disciples: "This sūtra is called the Sūtra on Rescuing and Protecting Sentient Beings from Evil Illness. Let it circulate widely among the people of Jambudvīpa so that those who are ill may hear of it."
