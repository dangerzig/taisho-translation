# 觀自在菩薩大悲智印周遍法界利益眾生薰真如法
## Method of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva's Great Compassion Wisdom-Seal Pervading the Dharma-Realm, Benefiting Beings, and Perfuming Suchness

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1042

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

Having received the holy command of Vairocana, I proclaim the dharma of Avalokiteśvara's mahā-jñāna-mudrā. If any yoga practitioner wishes to be reborn in the Western Land of Ultimate Bliss to benefit sentient beings, one should receive the Lotus-Vajra ritual method from a wise and accomplished ācārya, make extensive offerings, and practice the recitation method.

Within the altar, place an incense burner. This incense burner contains and embodies the form of Avalokiteśvara pervading the dharma-realm. What is this form? The incense seal should be made in the shape of the syllable HRĪḤ.

The principle that wisdom-karma is unattainable gathers four meanings into one syllable, which is this Sanskrit letter. The syllable HA signifies that the causes of all dharmas are unattainable. The syllable RA signifies purity without defilement. The syllable I signifies that freedom is unattainable. The syllable AḤ signifies original non-arising and non-perishing. This is the forward meaning.

Original non-arising and non-perishing, freedom unattainable, purity without defilement, causes of all dharmas unattainable: this is the reverse meaning.

The correspondence of reverse and forward reveals the incense-seal pattern. I have made its diagram.

This wondrous incense seal is called "Great Compassion Removing Suffering." Why? Because as it burns in sequence, it reveals true reality. When it has burned completely, it shows that whether forward or reverse, all ultimately returns to the dharma of emptiness.

In this place, one should contemplate: from the single syllable HRĪḤ arise the five syllables OṂ VA JRA DHA RMA. Within each syllable arise infinite syllable-gates. Each syllable-gate transforms into all buddha and bodhisattva bodies. Each transformation-body pervades the dharma-realm, benefiting sentient beings.

Therefore the practitioner gains immeasurable merit, all siddhis are perfected, and one receives the empowerment of all buddhas. The practitioner thereby obtains peace and security in this present life, free from all obstructions, like a wondrous lotus flower that all who see it cherish. At death one gains rebirth in the upper grade of the lotus in the Land of Ultimate Bliss.

Those of sharp faculties, with wisdom and skillful means, see the Buddha in this very body and attain a dhāraṇī called "Unstained by the World." In every place of rebirth, the body emits wondrous fragrance pervading the lands of the ten directions. Sentient beings who receive this perfuming all realize non-retrogression. Such merit cannot be fully described.

On the lid of the incense burner, carve the syllables VA JRA DHA RMA, with OṂ added at the beginning, making five syllables. At the center of the lid, a samaya form should be erected: a single-pronged vajra topped with an open eight-petaled lotus. The five syllables described above encircle this samaya form.

The samaya is the form of the original vow. If one sees this form, pays homage, and contemplates it single-mindedly, one immediately realizes the lotus nature. This is why those reborn in the Land of Ultimate Bliss are never again stained by the world. Even should they return to the world to liberate beings, like a lotus flower they are unstained by any defilement. This is entirely due to the power of past original vows, by which one realizes this fruit-realm.

Therefore the practitioner should erect this samaya form, contemplate it single-mindedly, and make this visualization: the incense smoke from this pattern becomes this samaya form, and this form further becomes the body of the principal deity. This shows how the original vow of the causal stage becomes the form and appearance of the fruition stage: this is the meaning of samaya.

When burning the incense, form the principal deity's mudrā and recite the principal mantra to empower it, and accomplishment is attained. The lid diagram is as described.

One who enters this wheel attains unsurpassed awakening. If one wishes to recite the mantra constantly without interruption, yet has not yet abandoned mental grasping and tends toward laxity, one should simply rely on this wondrous seal and burn sandalwood, lotus, and other incenses. Performing this incense-burning method daily is equivalent to the constant practice of reciting the vajra dharma-vidyā. Why? Because all the syllable-meanings of the mantra described above are revealed through this incense seal.

The root mudrā: form the vajra-bond with both hands, touch both index fingertips together like lotus petals, and stand both thumbs upright side by side. The mantra says:

[*Oṃ ārolik svāhā*]

If a person upholds this one-syllable mantra, it can eliminate all calamities, misfortunes, and diseases. After death, one attains upper-grade rebirth in the Land of Ultimate Bliss. All other worldly and supramundane great aspirations are fulfilled through upholding it. How much more so for one who practices according to this teaching: all siddhis will be perfected before long.

The dharma of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva's perfuming-suchness incense-seal has been fully proclaimed.
