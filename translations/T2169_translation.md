# 開元寺求得經疏記等目錄
## Catalog of Sūtras, Commentaries, and Records Obtained at Kaiyuan Temple

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 2169

Compiled during the Tang dynasty by Enchin (圓珍) of Japan

---

Catalog of the Dharma-seeking monk Enchin from Japan

At Kaiyuan Temple in Fuzhou, the following sūtras, śāstras, commentaries, and records were obtained, totaling one hundred and fifty-six fascicles.

The above were copied out.

The above were donated and given by the Brahmin Tripiṭaka master Āsāryamañjusūtra.

The above were donated and presented by the śramaṇas Cháng Zhēn, Cún Shì, Huì Guàn, Zhì Hǎi, Yì Xióng, Cān Liáo, Lǚ Quán, and others.

On the twenty-first day of [the ninth] month, having received authorization from the prefectural judge to reside at Kaiyuan Temple.

The foregoing Dharma-gate items have been recorded here as they were obtained.

Recorded by Enchin, dharma-seeking monk from Japan, on the twenty-first day of the ninth month of the seventh year of the Dazhong era of the Great Tang [853 CE].

---

**Translator's note:** The Brahmin Tripiṭaka master's name 阿娑阿哩耶曼蘇悉怛羅 is a phonetic transliteration of a Sanskrit name; the reconstruction *Āsāryamañjusūtra* is approximate. The Chinese monk names (Cháng Zhēn, Cún Shì, etc.) are rendered in Mandarin reading as no Sanskrit originals are recoverable.
