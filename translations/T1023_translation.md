# 一切如來正法祕密篋印心陀羅尼經
## All Tathāgatas' True Dharma Secret Casket Seal Heart Dhāraṇī Sūtra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1023

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dānapāla (施護).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was in the land of Magadha, in the Immaculate Garden beside the Jeweled Radiance Pool, together with great bodhisattvas, śrāvakas, devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, humans and non-humans, as well as kings, elders, and householders. Hundreds of thousands surrounded him, paying reverence and listening to the dharma.

At that time in the assembly there was a great wealthy brāhmaṇa named Vimalaprabha, who could comprehend subtle and wondrous teachings, was learned in the scriptures, eloquent, fond of broad learning, cultivated the ten virtues, revered the Three Jewels, and was filled with joyous enthusiasm. He thought: "All sentient beings who cultivate virtue as their cause will have immeasurable wealth and abundant provisions. I should cultivate this." Then the great brāhmaṇa Vimalaprabha rose, joined his palms, circumambulated the Buddha seven times, and with various flowers, incense paste, powdered incense, and other offerings honored the World-Honored One. He also presented garments, pearl necklaces, and other gifts worth a hundred thousand in value, and prostrating at the Buddha's feet, addressed him: "World-Honored One! May you rouse great compassion and accept my food offering." He made this request three times.

Then the World-Honored One silently accepted the invitation. When the great brāhmaṇa Vimalaprabha saw the World-Honored One silently accept, he quickly returned home. Over the course of one night, he prepared various abundant fine foods of the six flavors, fully complete. With the food prepared, he adorned his dwelling with balconies, bathing pools, silk banners in great abundance, flowers, incense paste, powdered incense, various fragrances, anointing oils, and other offerings, leaving nothing unprepared. Then the brāhmaṇa and his retinue, bearing flowers and pennants, playing music, proceeded with his household to the Immaculate Garden beside the Jeweled Radiance Pool to welcome the World-Honored One. Having arrived, he addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! The meal time has come and the food is prepared. May the World-Honored One and the great assembly come to my home and accept my offering."

Then the World-Honored One surveyed the assembly and comforted the great brāhmaṇa Vimalaprabha, saying: "Excellent! Excellent! As you wish." He also told the assembly and the devas to all proceed there. Then the World-Honored One rose from his seat, and with the great assembly of bodhisattvas, śrāvakas, devas, nāgas, the eightfold assembly, humans and non-humans, kings, ministers, and retinue, hundreds of thousands reverently surrounding him, all gathered and set out for the home of the great brāhmaṇa Vimalaprabha.

At that time, the World-Honored One's body emitted golden light, radiating manifold rays of immeasurable aspects, transforming into a net of various radiance that illuminated the buddha-fields of the ten directions. It further illuminated the assemblies gathered at the teaching sites of the tathāgatas in those buddha-fields, transforming into various offering implements. Having illuminated those realms, the light returned and entered the pores of Śākyamuni's body. Then the great brāhmaṇa Vimalaprabha and his retinue, with single-minded reverence wishing to make offerings to the Buddha, swept the road clean and adorned it in purity.

Then the World-Honored One, with the great assembly of devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, Śakra, Brahmā, the Four World-Protector Kings, Maheśvara, Nārāyaṇa, and hundreds of thousands more, reverently surrounding him, proceeded toward that place.

As the World-Honored One walked along the road, in the middle of the route there was a garden called Ānanda. Within that garden was a great ancient stūpa, extremely damaged and ruined. On all four sides, thorns, brush, and dense woodland had grown hard and impenetrable, so that it resembled a mere mound. As the World-Honored One drew near that stūpa, the mound blazed with flames on all four sides, with lights of various colors and aspects shining brilliantly. From near and far everyone could see the ruined stūpa. Within the light, a wondrous voice spoke in praise: "Excellent! Excellent! Śākyamuni Buddha, pure, subtle, with immeasurable auspicious signs beyond reckoning! The brāhmaṇa Vimalaprabha has invited the Tathāgata, and now a rare, inconceivable great event appears."

Then the Tathāgata Śākyamuni circumambulated the ruined mound of a stūpa to make offerings. He removed his own garments and adornments and draped them over the old stūpa-mound. When the covering was complete, he wept with tears of compassion. He further contemplated relics emerging and the buddhas of the ten directions all appearing. Then relics surged forth from within the stūpa and came to rest in the palms of all the tathāgatas of the ten directions. Then all the tathāgatas of the ten directions also wept tears, and those buddhas emitted light from their bodies that illuminated the ancient stūpa, forming a net of radiance, making its interior and exterior brilliant and translucent, pure beyond compare. Afterward, the light and the buddha-relics returned and entered the stūpa.

At that time the entire assembly wept, astonished and awestruck, exclaiming that this was unprecedented. Then in the assembly there was a great yakṣa lord named Vajrapāṇi, whose body blazed with light, whose arms were straight and strong, soft and radiant like an elephant's trunk, and who bore a vajra mace. He swiftly approached the World-Honored One, prostrated at his feet, and addressed him: "World-Honored One! Why have you manifested these rare auspicious signs? And why have the buddhas of the ten directions all wept tears and emitted radiance? May the Tathāgata expound this for me and resolve the assembly's doubts."

Then the World-Honored One told the great yakṣa lord Vajrapāṇi: "This contains the full-body relics of all the tathāgatas, gathered within this stūpa. The True Dharma Heart Seal Dhāraṇī of all the tathāgatas, in the form of sesame-seed-sized images of all the buddhas, is also within this stūpa.

"You should know, Vajrapāṇi, that these sesame-seed-quantity buddha images and the True Dharma Heart Seal Dhāraṇī abide within this stūpa. Because of this, the full-body relics of hundreds of thousands of koṭis of tathāgatas also dwell herein. If this True Dharma Heart Seal Dhāraṇī is within this stūpa, then the eighty-four thousand dharma gates are also herein, and the uṣṇīṣa prophecies of ninety-nine hundred-thousand koṭis of tathāgatas are also herein.

"You should know, Vajrapāṇi, that the merits of the stūpa containing all the tathāgatas' relics and this True Dharma Heart Seal Dhāraṇī are immeasurable, boundless, inconceivable, and beyond description. You should know, Vajrapāṇi, that through this True Dharma Casket Seal Heart Dhāraṇī, all true dharma and all merit are increased and perfected."

When the World-Honored One taught this dharma, the great assembly heard and rejoiced, their defilements were eliminated, and they attained the pure dharma eye. Among them, some attained the fruits of stream-entry, once-returning, non-returning, and arhatship, as well as the pratyekabuddha vehicle. Among them, some realized the first ground, second ground, and so on through the tenth ground. Among them, some perfected the six pāramitās. Among them, some received the prophecy of enlightenment. Among them, some attained non-retrogression. At that time, the great brāhmaṇa Vimalaprabha, right there in the assembly, was freed from all afflictions, his obstructions were eliminated, and he attained the five supernatural powers.

Then the great yakṣa lord Vajrapāṇi, witnessing these rare transformations and sovereign supernatural powers, leapt with joy, exclaiming that this was unprecedented, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! This True Dharma Seal Heart Dhāraṇī has such vast benefits. If sentient beings write it out beautifully, uphold, recite, honor, and praise it, what reward will they receive? May the Buddha expound this."

Then the World-Honored One told the great yakṣa Vajrapāṇi: "Listen attentively! I shall explain for you. If there are bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, or upāsikās who uphold, recite, write out, and honor this True Dharma Seal Heart Dhāraṇī, they will obtain merit and good-root rewards equal to ninety-nine hundred-thousand koṭis of sesame-seed-quantity tathāgata images. They will also encounter those tathāgatas and receive their prophecy of enlightenment. Why? Because this True Dharma Seal Heart Dhāraṇī was jointly proclaimed by ninety-nine hundred-thousand koṭis of nayutas of sesame-seed-quantity tathāgata images.

"Furthermore, if a person upholds and recites this True Dharma Seal Heart Dhāraṇī Sūtra, they will encounter the ninety-nine hundred-thousand koṭis of nayutas of sesame-seed-quantity tathāgata images of the ten directions. If bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, upāsikās, good men, or good women can daily make reverent offerings to this True Dharma Seal Heart Dhāraṇī Sūtra with flowers, incense paste, powdered incense, flower garlands, anointing oils, fine garments, and adornments, the merit they obtain is no different from making offerings to ninety-nine hundred-thousand koṭis of sesame-seed-quantity tathāgata images of the ten directions. They will also receive from each tathāgata vast and magnificent adornments, and obtain immeasurable heaps of the seven treasures, like Mount Sumeru."

Then the entire assembly of devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, humans and non-humans were all astonished, exclaiming that this was unprecedented: "Such a decayed, ruined, broken stūpa, perpetually blocked by thorns, with dense brush and trees filling it like a mound. Yet today the Tathāgata has roused great compassion and sovereign supernatural power to manifest these rare auspicious signs, an inconceivable stūpa of the seven treasures!" The entire assembly praised it without end.

Then in the assembly, the great yakṣa lord Vajrapāṇi addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! How did this ruined mound of a stūpa transform to manifest such marvelous adornments?"

Then the Buddha told Vajrapāṇi: "Apart from this ruined mound of a stūpa, there is no separate seven-treasured, marvelously adorned stūpa. Why? It manifests according to perception. Some see a thorny mound of a stūpa; others see a seven-treasured, marvelously adorned stūpa. What the buddhas have revealed is a stūpa of the ten directions containing all tathāgatas' full-body relics, adorned with a hundred precious jewels and the seven treasures in marvelous splendor. It is the evil karma of beings that causes them to see a thorny, broken stūpa.

"You should know, Vajrapāṇi, great yakṣa lord! In the final age, at the very last period, sentient beings with heavy karmic obstructions who have not planted good roots, who broadly commit unwholesome deeds and create karma leading to the three evil paths, by this cause will be born in a world without a buddha, will never hear the names of the buddhas, and will always be far from the sangha and the dharma. This is true and not false. This is why all the buddhas and I wept tears of sorrow.

"Furthermore, Vajrapāṇi! When the true dharma is about to be extinguished, it abides in this stūpa. All tathāgatas' full-body relics are also stored within this stūpa, guarded and protected by the dharma spoken by all the tathāgatas."

Then the great yakṣa lord Vajrapāṇi addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! How can sentient beings plant good roots? How can the true dharma be installed in this stūpa?"

Then the World-Honored One told Vajrapāṇi: "If a person with faith understands, upholds, recites, writes out, and explains this dhāraṇī to others, adorning it beautifully and placing it within a stūpa, this is the same as placing all the tathāgatas' full-body relics within that stūpa. It will be guarded by the secret *dhāraṇī* of all tathāgatas, guarded by the stūpas containing sesame-seed-quantity buddha images of the ninety-nine hundred-thousand koṭis of tathāgatas of the ten directions, and guarded by the uṣṇīṣa and eye of all tathāgatas.

"Furthermore, if a person uses the seven treasures in accordance with the dharma, consecrating and carving images of buddhas and bodhisattvas, adorning them beautifully and placing them within the stūpa; and further uses parasols, wheel-finials, jeweled nets, bells, and various wonderful adornments, bells, chimes, cymbals, and various instruments; flowers, incense, necklaces, garments, food and drink, and other offerings; and further consecrates them with the true dharma of all tathāgatas, maintaining purity and diligence, always free from laziness; and when the stūpa deteriorates over time, repairs and adorns it with various offerings, with sincere faith and reverence free from doubt or slander, that person will attain supreme perfect enlightenment.

"Furthermore, if a person circumambulates this stūpa once or twice, or makes even a single prostration, they will be freed from the sin of the uninterrupted hells and will surely attain non-retrogression, up to and including the ultimate fruit of buddhahood.

"Wherever this stūpa abides, all the tathāgata images of the ten directions are also present there. Furthermore, wherever this stūpa is, by the protective power of all the tathāgatas, the four directions of that land will be free of poisonous nāgas, thunderstorms, hail, untimely storms, and other terrors. That place will also be free of poisonous insects and creatures, including poisonous snakes, wolves, rats, locusts, vipers, scorpions, yellow wasps, black bees, and even centipedes and millipedes, all of which will stay far away. That land will be free of fierce and violent tigers, wolves, and lions. Flying birds and running beasts will be unable to cause harm. All disasters in that land will cause no disturbance. There will be no yakṣas, rākṣasas, bhūtas, vetāḍas, piśācas, or mad ghosts able to cause harm. There will be no malaria, boils, tumors, hemorrhoids, fistulas, leprosy, scabies, or any disease able to invade. If a person sees this precious stūpa and joyfully pays reverence, no such harms can touch them.

"Furthermore, in that land, cattle, horses, sheep, dogs, and various animals, as well as men and women, will suffer no untimely death. There will be no fear of swords, weapons, or armaments, floods, or fires. That land will also be free from famine, epidemics, and various terrors.

"That place will always be surrounded and protected by the Four Great Heavenly Kings, and the twenty-eight great yakṣa lords will secretly come to guard it in hidden form. The Thirty-Three Devas, the twenty-eight lunar mansions, Rāhu, the planets, and even the sun and moon will course in their proper order day and night. All the nāga kings will constantly watch over the land, sending timely rains. Devas of the desire realm, form realm, and even the formless realm will come at the three periods of the day to this stūpa to make prostrations and offerings. All the tathāgatas will honor and respect it, remembering it in contemplation.

"Furthermore, if a person constantly adorns a buddha-stūpa and its images, whether made of wood, stone, clay, painting, gold, silver, bronze, or other materials, and beautifully writes out this True Dharma Seal Heart Dhāraṇī Sūtra, and further consecrates it with the dharma of all tathāgatas, and then places it within the precious stūpa; and further adorns the four surrounding stairways of the stūpa, with wheel-finials, banners, pennants, and bells hanging all around, jeweled nets, whether large or small, tall or wide, adorned as one wishes; and further consecrates it with the true dharma of the tathāgatas of the ten directions, and performs all the rites of protection by the holy ones, and then makes extensive offerings of various incense, flowers, food and drink, necklaces, and other pure, devoted offerings, then immeasurable merit will be obtained."

Then in the assembly, the great yakṣa lord Vajrapāṇi again addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! For what reason does writing out, upholding, making offerings to, and honoring this True Dharma Seal Heart Dhāraṇī yield such immeasurable merit?"

Then the World-Honored One told Vajrapāṇi: "This is the Secret Casket Seal Heart Dhāraṇī of the full-body relics of all tathāgatas in the dharmadhātu. That is why such immeasurable merit is obtained."

When the great yakṣa lord Vajrapāṇi heard the Buddha describe such immeasurable merit, he joyfully accepted it with faith and again addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! May the Buddha compassionately expound this dharma. We wish to hear it."

Then the World-Honored One told Vajrapāṇi: "Listen attentively! I shall now explain for you. This True Dharma Seal Heart Dhāraṇī was jointly proclaimed by all the buddhas of the past, future, and present. If there are those who receive it with faith, honor, and make offerings, they will enter the stages of the śrāvakas, pratyekabuddhas, and the ten grounds, and attain the dharma body, reward body, and transformation body. Therefore it is named the Full-Body True Dharma Secret Casket Seal."

Then the World-Honored One spoke the dhāraṇī:

*Namaḥ stri-yadhi-vikānāṃ sarva-tathāgatānāṃ oṃ bhuvi-ṣṭi bhuvanāvale vavale curu curu dhara dhara sarva-tathāgata-dhātu-dhari padma-saṃbhave jayathe vimana smaradharmacakra pravartana vajri-bodhi-maṇḍa-alaṅkṛte sarva-tathāgata-adhiṣṭhite bodhaya bodhaya bodhi bodhi budhya saṃbodhaya saṃbodhaya cala cala calantu sarva-sattvānāṃ sarva-varaṇāni sarva-pāpa-vigate huru huru sarva-śuka-vigate sarva-tathāgata-hṛdaya-vajriṇi saṃbhara saṃbhara sarva-tathāgata-sudhatu-dhāraṇī-mudrā-siddhite subuddhe sarva-tathāgata-adhiṣṭhita-dhātu-garbhe svāhā samaya-adhiṣṭhite svāhā sarva-tathāgata-hṛdaya-dhātu-mudrā svāhā apratisthita-stūpe tathāgata-adhiṣṭhite huru huru hūṃ hūṃ svāhā oṃ sarva-tathāgata-uṣṇīṣa-dhāraṇī-mudrā sarva-tathāgata-dhātu-vibhūṣita-adhiṣṭhite hūṃ hūṃ hūṃ svāhā*

When the World-Honored One had spoken this dhāraṇī, he told Vajrapāṇi: "This Full-Body Secret Casket Seal Heart Dhāraṇī was proclaimed by all tathāgatas universally. Throughout the ten directions, in each and every direction, ninety-nine sesame-seed-quantity tathāgata images of hundreds of thousands of koṭis of nayutas of tathāgatas, hearing this dharma taught, all came here together and spoke in praise: 'Excellent! Excellent! Śākyamuni! You have well expounded the True Dharma Casket Seal Heart Dhāraṇī, causing it to circulate broadly throughout Jambudvīpa, and further causing all sentient beings to place full-body relics within this stūpa.' Through this, all the tathāgatas of the ten directions guard and protect it with their samādhi.

"Furthermore, if a person upholds, recites, explains, writes out, and beautifully adorns this Full-Body Relics Secret Casket Seal Heart Dhāraṇī Sūtra, and places it within a stūpa made of gold, silver, sandalwood, or decorated materials, then all the tathāgatas of the ten directions will constantly come to guard and protect it."

When the World-Honored One taught this True Dharma Casket Seal Heart Dhāraṇī, from within the old, broken mound there appeared a seven-treasured, wondrously adorned stūpa with wheel-finials, banners, pennants, and various ornaments. The entire assembly of humans and devas saw it clearly, and it remained visible for a long time as before.

At that time, the great assembly of bodhisattvas, śrāvakas, devas, nāgas, the eightfold assembly, humans and non-humans, witnessing this marvel, all rejoiced.

When the World-Honored One taught this dharma, the entire assembly, the great yakṣa lord Vajrapāṇi, the great brāhmaṇa Vimalaprabha, all the bodhisattvas, great śrāvakas, devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, humans and non-humans, kings, great ministers, elders, and householders, the whole assembly having heard the Buddha's teaching, received it with single-minded faith, and joyfully departed.
