# 拔濟苦難陀羅尼經
## Dhāraṇī Sūtra for Rescuing from Suffering and Hardship

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1395

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Xuanzang (玄奘).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Bhagavat was in Śrāvastī, dwelling in the Jeta Grove at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park, together with an incalculable number of śrāvakas and bodhisattva mahāsattvas, as well as devas, humans, asuras, and others, a boundless great assembly surrounding him on all sides.

At that time, from within the assembly, a bodhisattva named Inexpressible-Merit-Adornment rose from his seat, bowed at the Buddha's feet, joined his palms in reverence, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! In this world, innumerable sentient beings, through the causes and conditions of their afflictions, create various evil karma. They are bound to fall into the hells, the realm of hungry ghosts, and the animal realm, or to suffer extreme pain in the realms of devas and humans. We humbly pray that you will take compassionate pity and, through skillful means, rescue and deliver them."

The Buddha said: "Good man! Excellent, excellent! You are able to show compassion for all sentient beings and make this request. Listen carefully, listen carefully. I shall now briefly teach you the skillful means for rescuing beings from all suffering. Good man! There is a buddha, a world-honored one, named Akṣobhya Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha. For the sake of benefiting and bringing joy to all sentient beings, he taught a dhāraṇī and bade the assembly recite it. The dhāraṇī states:

*Kaka-ni kaka-ni rucani rucani truṭa-ni truṭa-ni trāsa-ni trāsa-ni prati-hana prati-hana sarva-karma-paraṃ-para-pra-ṇa-mī svāhā.*

"If any good man or good woman reverently pays homage to Akṣobhya Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha, and receives and upholds this mantra, then the five sins of immediate retribution, the four heavy transgressions, the ten evils, the defamation of holy sages, and the sin of slandering the true Dharma that they have previously committed will all be eliminated. When their life draws to its end, Akṣobhya Buddha together with the bodhisattvas will appear before them, praise and console them, and cause them to rejoice. He will further say: 'I have now come to welcome you. Follow me to the buddha-land from which I come.' After their death, they will certainly be reborn in the pure buddha-land of Akṣobhya Tathāgata.

"Good man! There is another world-honored one named Durgati-Pariśodhana-Rāja Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha. For the sake of benefiting and bringing joy to all sentient beings, he taught a dhāraṇī and bade the assembly recite it. The dhāraṇī states:

*Śuddha-ni śuddha-ni sarva-pāpa-viśuddha-ni śudhī viśudhī sarva-karma-viśuddha-ni svāhā.*

"If any good man or good woman reverently pays homage to Durgati-Pariśodhana-Rāja Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha, and receives and upholds this mantra, for fourteen thousand kalpas they will constantly remember their past lives. In every place of rebirth they will obtain a male body, their faculties will be complete, and they will deeply believe in cause and effect. They will be skilled in all arts and techniques, and will perfectly understand all treatises. They will delight in practicing generosity and will be weary of and renounce all desires. They will not create evil karma, and they will be free from all dangers and terrors. They will possess right livelihood and wisdom and will be loved and respected by all. They will constantly be near good friends and will always hear the true Dharma. They will seek the mind of awakening and never abandon it even for an instant. They will adorn themselves with all merits and virtues, will be endowed with wholesome discipline and restraint, and will be apprehensive of all evil karma. They will never suffer want, and will be gentle, joyful, and serene. Among devas and humans they will constantly enjoy happiness. They will swiftly realize unsurpassed, perfect awakening and will never regress from the ten pāramitās. They will constantly aspire to benefit and bring joy to all sentient beings, and all their practices will not be solely for their own benefit. In every place of birth they will constantly see the Buddha, uphold the true Dharma, and belong to the assembly of the worthy and holy."

When the Bhagavat had spoken this sūtra, the śrāvakas, bodhisattvas, and all the devas, humans, asuras, and the rest, having heard what the Buddha taught, all greatly rejoiced, believed, received it with faith, and put it into practice.
