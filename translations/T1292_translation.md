# 法華十羅剎法
## Method of the Ten Rākṣasīs of the Lotus Sūtra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1292

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown.

---

Before forming the mudrās, one should first enter the samādhi-mudrā of Śākyamuni.

First, the altar mudrā: both dāna and prajñā fingers cross internally. With both water-ring fingers, press both space-thumbs upon both fire-middle fingernails. The remaining fingers touch at the tips to complete the mudrā. Recite the twenty-eight chapters.

Second, the Lotus Sūtra mudrā: internally cross both water-ring and fire-middle fingers. Press the dāna finger upon the power-middle fingertip. Press the wind-index upon the wisdom-index. Join the dhyāna-thumb to the prajñā finger to complete the mudrā. The mantra:

*Sarva-dharma-puṇḍarīka añjali-maṇi.*

Third, the rākṣasī mudrā: with both hands, hook each water-ring finger behind the fire-middle finger. Then press each space-thumb upon the water-ring fingernail. Place the right wind-index upon the left prajñā finger, then the right fire-middle upon the left power-middle fingertip, and the right dāna finger upon the left vow-ring finger to complete the mudrā. The mantra:

*Tadyathā mili mili mulu mulu hu svāhā.*

Fourth, using the previous mudrā with the left hand on top, it is complete. The mantra:

*Tadyathā mili mili mu hu svāhā.*

Fifth, using the previous mudrā with each pair separated and facing away, the dāna and prajñā fingers face each other, and the right wind-index hooks the left power-middle to complete the mudrā. The mantra:

*Oṃ aṣṭa-sahasra-vajra-kumāra kana kana husu husu phaṭ phaṭ phaṭ.*

Sixth, the rākṣasī-binding mudrā: using the previous mudrā, cross both thumbs. Between both water-ring fingers, press the earth-pinky fingers on top. Then bend both wind-index fingers, hooking and pressing upon the space-thumb tips, to complete the mudrā. The mantra:

*Iṭhili.*

Seventh, the Sūtra-piṭaka mudrā: externally cross both fire-middle fingers. Hook both water-ring fingers on top. Then touch both space-thumb tips together. Both earth-pinky fingers cross and enter inside. The mantra [not given in the text].

Eighth, the rākṣasī-guarding-the-Lotus-piṭaka mudrā: with both hands, each fire-middle finger passes behind and over, then both water-ring fingers hook. The wind-index fingers cross. Then each earth-pinky and space-thumb form a linked ring to complete the mudrā. The mantra:

*Oṃ bhe yuṃ vaṃ ūṃ ṭha.*

Ninth, the rākṣasī-particle-dhāraṇī mudrā: with both hands, each pair of fire-middle fingers hooks behind the water-ring finger from above. Then left and right clasp together. Place the right earth-pinky upon the left power-middle finger surface, and the right wind-index upon the left wisdom-index. Place the left prajñā finger upon the right dhyāna-thumb to complete the mudrā. The mantra:

*Oṃ jū phaṭ jñā svāhā.*

Tenth, the heaven-and-earth māra-eye great-binding mudrā: left hand face down, right hand face up. The right earth-pinky hooks the left prajñā finger. Cross the right water-ring to hook the left vow-ring. Then each space-thumb locks behind and joins together. The right fire-middle hooks the left power-middle. Then each wind-index finger meets together to complete the mudrā. The mantra:

*Oṃ jāṃ yeja tura bandha bandha tu kīṃ bandha kīṃ va ak ak.*

The forms of the ten rākṣasīs:

Lambā: her form is like a yakṣa. Her garments are blue. Her right hand holds a single-pronged vajra at her right shoulder; her left hand holds a rosary. She stands with left knee raised, situated upon it. Her face is flesh-colored.

Vilambā: her form is like a nāga king, like the full moon, as if gazing upon the great sea. Her right hand grasps wind and clouds; her left hand holds a rosary. Her garments are blue-green. Her face is white. Before her stands a mirror-platform.

Kūṭadantī: her form is like a celestial maiden. Her sage-garments are blue. Her face is cast downward. Before her she presents fragrant flowers. She kneels in a half-crossed-leg position.

Puṣpadantī: her form is like a bhikṣuṇī. Her garments are purple. Her right hand holds a flower; her left hand holds a flower tray. Her face is slightly downcast.

Makuṭadantī: her form is like a divine maiden. Her garments are of every marvelous color. Her right hand takes a trident; her left hand holds a kuṇḍikā. She is in the posture of a guardian. She sits in half-lotus.

Keśinī: her form is like a child, full-moon flesh-colored.

The Gandharva Maiden: her right hand takes a copper ring; her left hand is in a dancing posture. She kneels.

Acalā: her form is like the dhāraṇī-crown bearer, constantly guarding. Her garments are of a pale color.

Māladharī: her form is like Śrī Devī. Her left and right hands hold necklaces. Her garments are gold. Her face is flesh-colored. She sits in full-lotus.

Kuntī: her form is like the crown-sounding maiden. Her garments are pink-blue. Her right hand grasps her skirt; her left hand holds a single-pronged vajra, as if about to strike. She kneels.

Sarvasattvōjohārī: her form is like a maiden of Brahmā or Śakra. She wears armor, with emanations emerging from her crown.

Hayagrīva: wrathful in form. His right hand holds a club; his left hand holds a triśūla. His garments are of various mixed colors. He sits in full-lotus.

The mantra of the eight rākṣasīs:

*Oṃ tiri tiri hūṃ hūṃ hī hī bhī hūṃ vaṃ hūṃ phaṭ.*

The protection-body mantra of the ten rākṣasīs and eight nāga kings: Nanda, Upananda, and all their retinues and kindred shall protect me. One should say: *ja ja ja.*

The root heart-mantra of the ten rākṣasīs:

*Oṃ tadyathā oṃ iti-miṃ iti-miṃ ti-miṃ lumī lumī sādhahu sādhahu sarva svāhā.*
