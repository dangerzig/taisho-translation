# 佛說布施經
## Sūtra of Generosity, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0705

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmabhadra (法賢)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was in the land of Śrāvastī, at the Jeta Grove of Anāthapiṇḍada. Together with the great assembly of bhikṣus, he taught the Dharma of generosity. There are thirty-seven kinds:

First, if one gives with a heart of faith and respect, one will be free from envy and jealousy and will be esteemed by all.

Second, if one gives at the proper time, one's three karmas will be purified and the four seasons will be peaceful and secure.

Third, if one constantly gives, one's body and mind will be at ease and happy, free from scattering and loss.

Fourth, if one gives with one's own hand, one obtains long and slender fingers and an upright, handsome body.

Fifth, if one gives for the sake of others, one will in turn receive the practice of great generosity from others.

Sixth, if one gives in accordance with the teaching, one's mind is free from attachment to characteristics and one obtains the merit of the unconditioned.

Seventh, if one gives fine and beautiful things, one obtains an upright and splendid body, loved and delighted in by all.

Eighth, if one gives the finest incense, one always enjoys the fragrance of sandalwood and offerings.

Ninth, if one gives the finest flavors, one obtains the supreme among tastes, nourishing one's entire body.

Tenth, if one gives in accordance with the Dharma, with respect and honor, one obtains peace and happiness, and all are glad to see one.

Eleventh, if one gives with a vast and broad mind, one obtains immeasurable and vast merit.

Twelfth, if one gives fine food, one is free from famine and one's storehouses overflow.

Thirteenth, if one gives beverages and drinks, wherever one goes one is free from hunger and thirst.

Fourteenth, if one gives garments, one obtains the finest clothing and an adorned body.

Fifteenth, if one gives a dwelling place, one obtains broad fields and estates, and splendid pavilions.

Sixteenth, if one gives bedding, one is born in a noble family with fine and radiant furnishings.

Seventeenth, if one gives elephants, horses, chariots, and palanquins, one obtains the four bases of spiritual power and their wondrous, unobstructed function.

Eighteenth, if one gives medicines, one obtains peace and happiness, free from all illness.

Nineteenth, if one gives the sūtras and the Dharma, one obtains the knowledge of past lives and other penetrations.

Twentieth, if one gives flowers and fruit, one obtains the flowers of the seven factors of awakening.

Twenty-first, if one gives garlands, one is freed from the defilements of greed, anger, and delusion.

Twenty-second, if one gives incense, one is freed from the stench of afflictions.

Twenty-third, if one gives parasols and canopies, one obtains sovereignty over the Dharma.

Twenty-fourth, if one gives bells and chimes, one obtains a beautiful voice.

Twenty-fifth, if one gives music, one obtains the deep and wondrous Brahmā voice.

Twenty-sixth, if one gives by lighting lamps, one obtains the pure divine eye.

Twenty-seventh, if one gives silk, gauze, and bolts of cloth, one obtains the garments of liberation.

Twenty-eighth, if one sprinkles the Tathāgata's stūpas and temples with fragrant water;

Twenty-ninth, if one bathes the Tathāgata's body with fragrant water;

Thirtieth, if one anoints and adorns the Buddha image with fragrant oil: together these three produce the thirty-two marks and the eighty minor features.

Thirty-first, if one gives fragrant bathing water to the Saṃgha, one is born in a wealthy family, with few illnesses and happiness.

Thirty-second, if one gives with a mind of loving-kindness, one's countenance is pleasant and peaceful, free from all anger.

Thirty-third, if one gives with a mind of compassion, one is freed from killing and harm.

Thirty-fourth, if one gives with a mind of sympathetic joy, one is free from fear and far from grief and vexation.

Thirty-fifth, if one gives with a mind of equanimity, one is freed from obstructions and realizes the joy of quiescence.

Thirty-sixth, if one gives in various ways, one obtains various kinds of merit.

Thirty-seventh, if one gives with a mind free from attachment and free from characteristics, one attains anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi.

The Buddha told the bhikṣus: "These thirty-seven kinds are the subtle and wondrous practices of generosity performed by the wise. You should now receive and uphold them."

At that time, the King of Śrāvastī said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! How should we practice generosity?"

The Buddha said: "Great king! If you wish to obtain supreme and wondrous rewards of merit when practicing generosity: be compassionate and do not kill; be free from all envy; hold to right views and be far from unwholesomeness; firmly uphold the precepts; draw near to good friends; close the doors of the evil destinies and open the path to heavenly birth. Benefit yourself and benefit others with an impartial mind. If one gives in this way, it is true generosity, a great field of merit. Furthermore, when practicing generosity, whatever your heart desires, you will obtain the corresponding reward. If with fine colors, famous incense, precious flavors, and soft textures you give with your own hand, you will be honored by all, your family will be complete, and you will receive the reward of wealth, nobility, and happiness. If you give food, you will obtain great strength. If you give ghee-oil lamps, you will obtain the divine eye. If you give music, you will obtain the divine ear. If you give medicines, you will obtain long life. If you give a dwelling, you will obtain pavilions, fields, and gardens. If you give the Dharma in teaching, you will obtain the sweet dew."

The Buddha said: "Great king! If one gives while practicing the ten wholesome deeds, one further obtains ten kinds of reward. The ten wholesome deeds are: not killing, not stealing, not engaging in sexual misconduct, not speaking falsely, not speaking frivolously, not speaking harshly, not speaking divisively, not being covetous, not being angry, and not being deluded. Through these one obtains: a life not cut short, wealth that is not scattered or lost, a pure family, truthful speech, freedom from envy, being a delight for all to see, harmony with friends and relatives, not falling into poverty, an upright and handsome countenance, and wisdom in accord with the Dharma. Such are the rewards obtained."

The Buddha said: "Great king! If one makes offerings of the finest food and drink to the Three Treasures, one obtains five kinds of benefit: an upright and splendid body, increased strength, a long life, peace and happiness, and the accomplishment of eloquence. In this way, all the sentient beings of Jambudvīpa, along with their parents, wife, children, sons, daughters, and family, by practicing generosity as above, will have all their wishes fulfilled without exception."

When this Dharma had been spoken, all were filled with great joy, bowed, and departed.
