# 持世陀羅尼經
## Vasudhārā Dhāraṇī Sūtra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1162

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Xuanzang (玄奘).

---

Thus have I heard. At one time the Bhagavān was in the kingdom of Kauśāmbī, in the Kaṇṭaka Forest, together with a great assembly of five hundred bhikṣus, koṭis of bodhisattva mahāsattvas, and devas, humans, asuras, and other innumerable great assemblies, surrounding him on all sides.

At that time in that kingdom there was an elder named Sucandra, of warm and noble bearing, with an elegant and serene temperament. He had numerous sons, daughters, and servants, and held deep reverence and faith in the Buddha, Dharma, and Saṃgha. He came to the Buddha's place, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, circumambulated hundreds of thousands of times, stood to one side, joined his palms in reverence, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I wish to ask the Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha about a small matter of doubt. I beseech you, out of great compassion, to graciously hear and grant permission."

At that time the World-Honored One, out of compassion for him, told him in a gentle voice: "Elder! Ask whatever you wish. I shall explain it for you with skillful distinctions and cause your heart to rejoice."

Then the elder, joyful and elated, bowed his head in obeisance, joined his palms, and asked: "World-Honored One! How can good men and good women who are poor and of low station become wealthy and honored? How can those who are ill be cured of their illness? How can those who have committed offenses have their offenses extinguished? How can those in danger and fear find peace and happiness?"

At that time the World-Honored One, knowing already yet asking nonetheless, said: "Elder! What reason prompts such a request?"

Then the elder again addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! We who live as householders have many dependents, yet our resources are scarce and difficult to make ends meet. Furthermore, there is much illness, karmic burden, and fearful danger. Therefore I beseech the World-Honored One to reveal skillful means, so that those who are poor and of low station may attain great wealth and position, provide for their relatives, broadly cultivate generous giving, benefit all beings, and have storehouses that never run empty; so that those who are ill may be restored to the harmony of the four great elements, diligently cultivating wholesome karma with body and mind free from weariness; so that those who have offenses may have them swiftly extinguished, and at the breaking of the body and the end of life be reborn in a good destiny; and so that those in danger and fear may have peace and happiness of body and mind, draw near and make offerings to the Buddha, Dharma, and Saṃgha, and swiftly realize unsurpassed samyak-saṃbodhi."

At that time the World-Honored One told the elder: "Good man! In the past, countless kalpas ago, I encountered a buddha world-honored one named Vajradhara-sāgara-ghoṣa Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha, Perfect in Knowledge and Conduct, Well-Gone, Knower of the World, Unsurpassed, Trainer of Persons, Teacher of Devas and Humans, Buddha, Bhagavān. Wishing to benefit all sentient beings, he spoke a dhāraṇī called Vasudhārā. At that time, upon hearing it, I was filled with joy. I received, upheld, read, recited, and broadly proclaimed it for others, benefiting and bringing happiness to immeasurable sentient beings. Through this cause and condition, my merit and wisdom increased, and I swiftly realized unsurpassed samyak-saṃbodhi and expounded the subtle dharma for all devas and humans. Now I shall proclaim this dhāraṇī for you. You, devas, humans, and others should all listen attentively. Having heard it, receive, uphold, and broadly proclaim it for others. The spiritual power of this mantra is inconceivable, causing all sentient beings to obtain benefit and happiness." The dhāraṇī says:

[Dhāraṇī]

*Tadyathā surūpe bhadra-vati maṃgale acchale accha-pāle udghaṭani udbhedani saśya-vati dhāna-vati dhana-vati śrīmati prabhā-vati aṃ-māle vimāle ruru surū-pa vimāle ananta-siddhye vita-siddhye viśva-ke siṃ-kure maṃ-kure vivime dudu-me tata-le tarāṭ tarāṭ vajre vajre kāṭi kāṭi pārṣaṇi niṣpadane vachra-dara tathāgata-satya-sā-garanī ghuśaṃ tathāgatam anusmara anusmara dharma-satyam anusmara saṃgha-satyam anusmara taṭṭa taṭṭa pura pura purāya barā barā-ṇi sumaṃgale śānta-mati maṃgala-vati subhadra-vati agaccha agaccha samayam anusmara svāhā ā-vacchānām anusmara svāhā prabhāvam anusmara svāhā dhṛtim anusmara svāhā vijayam anusmara svāhā sarva-sattva-vijayam anusmara svāhā*

"This dhāraṇī possesses great spiritual power. If any good man or good woman sincerely receives and upholds it and broadly proclaims it for others, evil spirits, devas, nāgas, yakṣas, humans, and non-humans will all be unable to harm them, and all beneficial and joyful matters will increase day and night. If one can sincerely make offerings to the Three Treasures and recite this great dhāraṇī sūtra for seven days and nights without even a moment's gap, all devas, nāgas, and spirits will be filled with joy, and of themselves will mysteriously rain down all needed wealth and grain. Famine and epidemic will all be removed, all karmic obstacles will be annihilated, all danger and fear will all obtain peace, merit and wisdom will gradually increase, whatever is sought will come as wished, and one will swiftly realize unsurpassed enlightenment and bodhi."

At that time the Buddha told the elder Sucandra: "You should have faith in and receive this dhāraṇī, remember it, recite and uphold it, and broadly proclaim it for others. Whatever benefit and happiness you seek will all be harmoniously fulfilled."

Then the elder, hearing what the Buddha taught, was joyful and elated, and addressed the Buddha: "I am able to receive and uphold it, broadly proclaim it for others, and bring benefit and happiness to immeasurable sentient beings. I beseech the World-Honored One to protect and care for me with compassion."

The World-Honored One said: "Just so! Just so!"

Then the elder, joining his palms in reverence, circumambulated the World-Honored One hundreds of thousands of times, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, and departed with joy.

At that time the World-Honored One told Ānanda: "In all the storehouses of the elder Sucandra, all varieties of wealth and grain are now completely filled to overflowing."

Venerable Ānanda joyfully addressed the Buddha: "What cause and condition has suddenly filled the storehouses of the elder Sucandra to overflowing?"

The Buddha told Ānanda: "The elder Sucandra, having heard me proclaim the great dhāraṇī, deeply believed and rejoiced. He received, upheld, read, and recited it, and vowed to proclaim it for immeasurable sentient beings. Through the merit of this, all his storehouses are filled. You too should receive, uphold, read, recite, and broadly proclaim this dhāraṇī for others, causing all sentient beings in this great trichiliocosm to obtain benefit and happiness. I observe with the eyes of a buddha that among all the worlds' devas, māras, brahmās, and others, none can violate or surpass this dhāraṇī, for its phrases are true and correct and cannot be destroyed. Those of scant merit cannot even hear it. Why? Because these phrases have been praised in common by the buddhas of the three times, empowered with inconceivable spiritual power, causing all who hear and uphold them to obtain benefit and happiness."

Venerable Ānanda, with deep joy, praised in wondrous verses:

> "The buddhas are inconceivable; the dharma they teach is likewise so.
> Those who can rightly practice it: their rewards are also thus.
> The omniscient dharma king has extinguished birth, old age, sickness, and death.
> Having reached the excellent further shore, I bow to the great awakened honored one."

At that time Ānanda, elated and joyful, bowed to the Buddha, joined his palms, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! What shall this dharma gate be named? How shall we now receive and uphold it?"

The Buddha told Ānanda: "This is called 'What the Elder Sucandra Asked.' It is also called 'That Which Can Bring All Wealth and Position.' It is also called 'Healing Illness.' It is also called 'Extinguishing Offenses.' It is also called 'That Which Can Remove All Danger and Fear.' It is also called 'That Which All Buddhas Praise in Common.' It is also called 'That Which Is Empowered by the Spiritual Power of All Buddhas.' It is also called 'The Vasudhārā Dhāraṇī Sūtra.' You should practice it and not let it be forgotten, to benefit and bring happiness to all sentient beings."

When the Bhagavān had spoken this sūtra, the immeasurable śrāvakas and bodhisattvas, and all the devas, humans, asuras, and others, and the entire great assembly, hearing what the Buddha taught, all greatly rejoiced, accepted it with faith, and put it into practice.
