# 佛說祕密八名陀羅尼經
## The Buddha's Teaching: Dhāraṇī Sūtra of the Secret Eight Names

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1366

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmadeva (法賢).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was in the country of Śrāvastī, in the Jeta Grove at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park, together with a great assembly of twelve hundred and fifty bhikṣus, as well as an assembly of bodhisattva mahāsattvas, with Vajrapāṇi at their head.

At that time the World-Honored One addressed Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva, the Great Lord of Secrets: "Your original division is known as the Vajra Division. Within the vidyā-dhara treasury, it contains the most supreme, most profound, and most secret Dharma. The knowledge of sentient beings is shallow and their faculties narrow and inferior; they find it difficult to believe and understand the meaning of this secret. The difficult-to-believe Dharma that you have previously taught does not accord with the method and cannot be called holy. It earns the name 'unwholesome' and is also called 'difficult to tame.' Vajrapāṇi! I shall now, within your Vajra Division, briefly teach your secret eight names and their dhāraṇī. This dhāraṇī, if a person recites and upholds it, will make it easy to accomplish whatever they seek. If a person hears this dhāraṇī sūtra and, with a mind of utmost sincerity, receives, upholds, reads, and recites it, all their sins of the five acts of immediate retribution will be entirely eliminated. When this sūtra is upheld, through the power of the Dharma's majesty, all eighty-four thousand koṭi-nayuta hosts of evil māras will retreat, scatter, and flee to the ten directions of the world. If a person hears this dhāraṇī and rejoices in it with joyful aspiration, then one who does not hold the precepts will obtain complete precepts, one who is not practicing pure conduct will accomplish pure conduct, one who is not in quiescence will attain quiescence, and they will even come to see the buddhas and bodhisattvas, who will joyfully console them and grant their wishes to be fulfilled."

At that time Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva, having received the Buddha's admonition, bowed his face to the ground and said: "World-Honored One! What I previously taught was indeed as the Holy One declared. We humbly pray that the World-Honored One will not forsake great compassion and, for the sake of all sentient beings of the present and future, will proclaim my eight secret names and the dhāraṇī, enabling all sentient beings to obtain great benefit."

The Buddha addressed Vajrapāṇi: "Listen carefully, listen carefully. Within the most supreme vidyā-dhara treasury of the Vajra Division, I shall teach your secret eight names. The first is Elephant-Ear. The second is Wondrous Ornament. The third is Jewel-Ocean of Merit. The fourth is Immovable. The fifth is Cloud of Truth. The sixth is Lovely Form. The seventh is Flame-Light. The eighth is Wondrous Color. Vajrapāṇi! These are your secret eight names." He then spoke the dhāraṇī:

*Namo buddhāya. Namo dharmāya. Namo saṃghāya. Namo vajra-pāṇaye. Tadyathā: atkha-ratkhi su-ma-mukhe sarva-kārya-ni sādhaya hiri miri tidani sidhantu mantra-padāṃ svāhā.*

The Buddha addressed Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva: "If any person receives, upholds, reads, and recites these secret names and dhāraṇī, that person will not fall into the hells for seven koṭi-nayuta hundreds of thousands of kalpas. When their life draws to its end, the buddhas and bodhisattvas will appear before them, console them, and teach the Dharma. After their death they will be born in Tuṣita Heaven."

When the Buddha had spoken this sūtra, Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva and all the bodhisattvas, as well as all the devas, humans, asuras, gandharvas, humans and non-humans in the world, having heard what the Buddha taught, all greatly rejoiced, believed, received it with faith, and put it into practice.
