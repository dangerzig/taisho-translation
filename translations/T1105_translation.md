# 聖多羅菩薩一百八名陀羅尼經
## Dhāraṇī Sūtra of the One Hundred and Eight Names of Holy Tārā Bodhisattva

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1105

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmadeva (法天).

---

Homage to the supremely wondrous Holy Tārā Great Bodhisattva, adorned with all manner of maṇi jewel necklaces and silk canopies of the most supreme great world.

At that time, Tārā Great Bodhisattva, desiring to proclaim the great dhāraṇī dharma never before seen or heard in ages past, graciously set forth her teaching.

[Dhāraṇī]

When this spell was spoken, at that time the devas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, rākṣasas, bhūtas, and vināyakas, upon hearing the great spell-phrases, covered their faces in terror and, exerting great spiritual power, sought refuge and protection. They took refuge in and praised Tārā Bodhisattva: her eyes are pure and expansive, her mind practices truth. She gazes equally upon the three realms. From her eyes she radiates the light of compassion, like Avalokiteśvara, benefiting sentient beings like a lotus opening.

At that time Maheśvara, baring his right shoulder and placing his right knee on the ground, together with all the devas, asuras, vidyā-holders, and other assemblies, gathered and knelt in the same manner. Then Maheśvara spoke the spell:

[Dhāraṇī]

When Maheśvara had praised and spoken this one hundred and eight names, he further declared: "You, Tārā Bodhisattva! You have skillfully proclaimed them."

At that time Tārā Bodhisattva extended her hands, and a radiant flame universally illuminated the ten directions. She then recalled the buddhas of the past and said: "Maheśvara and all the devas, listen with earnest minds. All the tathāgatas possess the great ten powers and always act as protectors. With such vast and great majestic virtue, they practice the path of bodhi, liberating you and others from the great terror of saṃsāra's ocean of suffering and death, enabling you to reach the ultimate other shore of nirvāṇa, and causing your minds to give rise to faith and practice and to accept the bright dharma."

At that time there was a buddha named Jyotiḥ-prabha-vicitrālaṃkāra Tathāgata, who further declared: "This great vidyā-power provides great rescue and protection for all sentient beings on water and land, provides great illumination in the darkness, and brings forth roots of merit from all karmic obstructions. This great vajra-dhāraṇī was heard from the buddhas of the past; those who practice in correspondence and know it attain purity of mind, transforming into the most supreme auspicious samādhi. All transgressions are spontaneously extinguished and all offenses spontaneously cease to arise. If furthermore any person hears, receives, and upholds it, concentrating the mind in reading and reciting, then as the Buddha has declared, all undertakings are accomplished, all afflictions are liberated, and the wishes of all sentient beings are fulfilled. The wisdom-dharma of the tathāgata is without equal in the world. Furthermore, by reading and reciting this one hundred and eight names, Tārā Bodhisattva will rescue, protect, and guide one to face true enlightenment and accomplish all that is sought. Whether king, great minister, elder, or householder, all sentient beings before one will submit, and it can shatter all the world's delusions and afflictions."

At that time the Buddha Jyotiḥ-prabha declared: "That person enters the family of the tathāgata and is a true lion. The power of that radiance can awaken devas and humans. Possessed of the power of diligence, he obtains the most supreme and exalted great being of the tathāgata, renowned in name, who skillfully comprehends the characteristics of the dharma and is supreme in benefit."

Furthermore, that buddha declared: "Free from desire, the mind is joyful; afflictions do not arise; one dwells well in ease. One generates the vast mind and enjoys long nights of peace. Tārā Bodhisattva delights in one and guards one. Furthermore, all the devas, nāgas, ghosts, and spirits marvel as they have never seen before, rejoicing and delighting, joining palms and standing in praise."
