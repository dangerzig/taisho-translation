# 拔一切業障根本得生淨土神呪
## Divine Dhāraṇī for Uprooting All Karmic Obstructions and Attaining Rebirth in the Pure Land

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0368

Translated from the Chinese. Liu Song dynasty, translated by Guṇabhadra (求那跋陀羅)

---

*Namo amitābhāya tathāgatāya tadyathā amṛtabhave amṛtasaṃbhave amṛtavikrānte amṛtavikrānta-gāmini gagana-kīrtikare svāhā.*

If there are good men and good women who can recite this dhāraṇī, the Buddha Amitābha will constantly dwell upon the crown of their head, day and night protecting them, preventing any enemy from gaining advantage over them. In the present life, they will always obtain peace and comfort. At the end of their lives, they will naturally be reborn in the Pure Land.

---

## Account of the Inconceivable Supernatural Power of the Amitābha Sūtra

In the past, Dharma Master Ruì of Chang'an, as well as Huìchóng, Sēngxiǎn, Huìtōng, and more recently, in the Later Zhou, Meditation Master Shí, Meditation Master Jǐng, and Dharma Master Luán of Xihe, numbering several hundred altogether, were all reborn in the Western Land. The meditation master Chuò of Xihe, having seen that Dharma Master Luán had been reborn in the Pure Land, led his associates in dedicating themselves exclusively to the practice of Pure Land cultivation. Master Chuò also compiled a record of verifications of the Western Land titled the *Collection on the Land of Peace and Joy*, which circulated widely.

Furthermore, in the Jin dynasty, the Dharma Master Yuǎn entered Mount Lu and did not leave for thirty years. He assembled like-minded companions, one hundred and twenty-three monastics and laypeople, and together they made a vow aspiring to the Western Land, carving their vow upon the mountain rock. In the Tiānjiā era of the Chen dynasty, the meditation master Zhēn of Mount Lu, while seated in meditation, saw several hundred people together boarding a seven-jeweled lotus-vessel heading west. Master Zhēn then asked to be taken aboard. The people on the vessel answered: "Dharma Master, although you have lectured well on the *Nirvāṇa Sūtra*, which is indeed supremely inconceivable, you have not yet mastered the recitation of the *Amitābha Sūtra* and its dhāraṇī, and therefore you cannot come with us." The Dharma Master then abandoned his lecturing and, day and night, devoted himself exclusively to reciting the *Amitābha Sūtra* and the dhāraṇī. He calculated that he should complete twenty thousand recitations. Twenty-eight days before his death, at about four in the morning, a divine being from the western direction brought a white silver platform through the sky, brighter than the sun, and announced: "Dharma Master, when your life comes to an end, you shall ride upon this platform to be reborn in the land of the Buddha Amitābha. I have come to show you this, so that you may know your rebirth is certain." At the time of his death, both monastics and laypeople all heard what sounded like music playing in the sky, and they also perceived a strange fragrance, which lingered for several months without fading. That night, the monks at the Peak-Top Monastery all saw dozens of torches within the valley, each as large as a cart-wheel.

Examining the records of past and present, those who have been reborn in the World of Peace and Joy are not few. Many witnessed transformation-Buddhas and their retinues coming to welcome them, along with auspicious signs, as the fuller accounts make clear and cannot all be recorded here. Because Meditation Master Zhēn had a verified experience with this sūtra, this single account is briefly recorded to awaken future sages and to assist the aspiration for rebirth.

The Divine Dhāraṇī for Uprooting All Karmic Obstructions and Attaining Rebirth in the Pure Land was translated at imperial command by Guṇabhadra near the end of the Yuánjiā era of the Song dynasty, comprising fifty-nine characters in fifteen phrases. The Bodhisattva Nāgārjuna, aspiring to be reborn in the Land of Peace and Nourishment, received this dhāraṇī in a dream. The tripiṭaka master Yaśas recited this dhāraṇī. The Dharma Master Xiù of Tianping Temple received this dhāraṇī orally from the tripiṭaka master Yaśas. That person said: "The original text has not come from abroad. If one wishes to receive and uphold the dhāraṇī method: chew a willow twig, wash with bean-powder, rinse the mouth, light incense, and before a Buddha-image, kneel in the foreign manner with palms joined. Six times each day and night, recite it three times seven repetitions each time. This will eliminate the four grave offenses, the five acts of immediate retribution, the ten unwholesome deeds, and the sin of slandering the vaipulya sūtras; all will be entirely eliminated. In the present life, whatever one seeks will be obtained, and one will not be confused or troubled by evil ghosts or spirits. When the count reaches two hundred thousand recitations, one will obtain the sprout of the awakening of bodhi. When it reaches three hundred thousand recitations, one will see the Buddha Amitābha face to face."
