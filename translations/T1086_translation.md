# 觀自在菩薩如意輪瑜伽
## Yoga of the Wish-Fulfilling Wheel of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1086

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

> I now, following the yoga
> Of the Vajraśekhara Sūtra, expound
> The wish-fulfilling recitation method
> Of the Maṇi-Padma Division.
> By cultivating this samādhi,
> One can become like Avalokiteśvara.
>
> First, select the disciple:
> One of good family who reveres the Dharma,
> Loved and respected by many,
> Wise and courageously diligent,
> With resolute vīrya,
> Whose awakened wisdom is never abandoned,
> Filial to their parents,
> Of pure faith in the Three Jewels,
> Delighting in the practice of bodhi,
> In the four immeasurable minds
> Without even a moment's interruption,
> Always delighting in the Mahāyāna Dharma,
> Abiding in the bodhisattva precepts,
> Reverently serving the ācārya.
>
> All the holy ones
> Accomplish their steadfast power.
> A person of heroic courage,
> Well versed in the gate of correspondence,
> Always delighting in tranquil practice,
> With wisdom and fearlessness,
> The body ever adorned by precepts,
> Diligently cultivating the secret vehicle,
> Reverently following the path of principle,
> Single-minded and without stinginess.
>
> Always delighting in hearing the wondrous Dharma,
> Having once entered the samaya,
> Having received abhiṣeka from a teacher,
> And having been given the seal of approval:
> Before long they will attain accomplishment.
> A disciple possessing these marks
> May then receive the transmission.
> This is the wish-fulfilling jewel,
> Able to accomplish all undertakings.
>
> As the sūtras teach regarding the site:
> Among mountains and beside flowing water,
> A pure araṇya,
> A pleasant valley as one likes,
> Free from all dangers and fears,
> With offerings adorned according to one's means.
>
> The practitioner faces west
> Toward the Sovereign King of Compassion.
> Next, bow to the buddhas of the other directions,
> Placing the five limbs on the ground,
> Paying reverence as taught.
> Kneeling on both knees,
> Abide with palms joined and heart open.
> With sincere mind, fully confess
> All sins of the three karmas.
>
> "From past lives I have
> Transmigrated through birth and death.
> Now, before the Great Holy One,
> I confess with all my heart.
> As the buddhas before have confessed,
> So too do I now.
> May, by the power of empowerment,
> All sentient beings be purified."
> By this great vow,
> Self and others attain purity.

The mantra:

*Oṃ sarva-bhāva-śuddha sarva-dharma-svabhāva-śuddho 'haṃ.*

The practitioner should next rejoice in the merit and wisdom accumulated by all buddhas and bodhisattvas.

> The buddhas of the past, present, and future,
> The bodhisattvas and all the holy ones:
> All the wholesome roots they have accumulated,
> With palms joined, I fully rejoice in them all,
> Just as in the merit I myself have gathered,
> With joy that is no different.

Next, place the right knee on the ground, join the palms in a lotus shape and place them above the crown, and visualize bowing at the feet of all tathāgatas and bodhisattvas.

The mantra:

*Oṃ padma-vi.*

Having bowed to the buddhas, sit in full lotus, half lotus, or the cross-legged posture of a cakravartin, as one pleases.

> Having formed this seated mudrā,
> Contemplate the buddhas pervading space,
> Each before one's own body,
> Abiding in that assembly of holy ones.
>
> Śamatha and vipaśyanā, from the knees upward,
> Spiraling and dancing, joined at the heart,
> Like a lotus not yet bloomed:
> Visualize bowing to the buddhas.
>
> Next, form the samaya:
> The steadfast bond at the heart,
> With the generosity and wisdom fingers, and the meditation and gnosis fingers, standing upright.
> This is the vajra-lotus seal,
> The universal seal of those who hold the lotus.
> Having thus alerted the holy ones,
> Recite this mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-padma-samaya stvaṃ.*

> By forming this mudrā,
> The buddhas and the sons of the sugatas,
> All those of great renown,
> With their wondrous discerning wisdom, take one in.
> Recalling their original vow
> Before the All-Illuminating One,
> Because they do not violate the teaching,
> They empower one to perfect accomplishment.

Next, form the samaya-mudrā that delights and pleases all buddha tathāgatas.

> The ten fingers firmly bound,
> The patience and vow fingers crossed in the middle,
> The generosity and wisdom, and meditation and gnosis fingers,
> Each paired and standing upright.

The mantra:

*Oṃ samaya hoh su-rata stvaṃ.*

> By displaying this mudrā,
> All the buddhas and bodhisattvas,
> And all the vajra-holders
> Are all wondrously delighted.
>
> Next, open the gate of the heart
> And introduce the vajra-wisdom syllable.
> Contemplate upon the two breasts:
> On the right, traṃ; on the left, ṭaḥ,
> Like the doors of a palace.
> With the supreme vajra-bond,
> The three karmas activated simultaneously,
> Strike the heart to open the two syllables.

The mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-bandha traṃ-ṭaḥ.*

> Since beginningless time, the seeds of conditioning
> And the defilements thus accumulated:
> Now with the sin-summoning mudrā,
> Gather them together to be crushed.
>
> The ten fingers firmly bound,
> The patience and vow fingers extended like needles,
> The vigor and strength fingers curved like hooks.
> With the mind, visualize summoning all sins:
> Imagine the mass of sins
> As figures with bristling hair, naked, and black.
> The mudrā pierces the heart;
> Upon touching, recite the mantra.
> Because the three karmas correspond,
> All accumulated sins can be summoned.
> Having recited the summoning,
> Then perform the method of crushing.

The mantra:

*Oṃ sarva-pāpa-karṣaṇa-viśuddha-na samaya vajra hūṃ jaḥ.*

> Having summoned them into the palms,
> Perform the method of crushing.
> Cross the previous mudrā inward,
> Pronouncing hūṃ to bind the sins.
> The patience and vow fingers both extend straight,
> Bearing the syllables traṃ and ṭaḥ.
> Visualize them as a vajra pestle,
> Clapping together as if crushing a mountain.
> With wrathful words and fierce form,
> Purify all the evil destinies.
> After reciting, clap the patience and vow fingers
> Three times seven, as appropriate.

The mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-pāṇi visphūṭaya sarva-pāya-bandhana nipra-mukṣāya sarva-pāya-gati-vijaya sarva-sattvān sarva-tathāgata vajra-samaya hūṃ traṃ ṭaḥ.*

> By this correspondence,
> The former buddhas taught through skillful means:
> The sins accumulated by the three karmas,
> Immeasurably heavy obstructions,
> Having been thus crushed and destroyed,
> Are like fire burning dry grass.
>
> Sentient beings are ever deluded,
> Not knowing this principle.
> Out of the tathāgata's great compassion,
> This secret and wondrous gate is opened.
>
> Next, form the entering mudrā
> To introduce the tathāgata's wisdom-syllable.
> The two wings firmly bound,
> The meditation and gnosis fingers entered within,
> The two vigor and strength fingers
> Pressing together like a ring.
> Visualize before you an eight-petaled lotus,
> Upon which is placed the syllable aḥ,
> Adorned with two dots,
> The wondrous syllable then called āḥ,
> White as shell or snow,
> Radiating a thousand rays of light.
> Visualize using the vigor and strength fingers
> To pick up the syllable and place it within the heart.
> The three karmas operating simultaneously,
> Recite this mantra:

*Oṃ vajra āviśa aḥ.*

> Having visualized entering the heart,
> The syllable's form becomes even more luminous.
> This is the essence of the dharma-dhātu;
> The practitioner should contemplate thus.
> Before long, one will awaken to quiescence,
> Because dharmas are fundamentally unborn.
>
> The tathāgatas of the three times,
> The vajra body, speech, and mind,
> All through wondrous skillful means
> Are held within the vajra fist.
> With this, close the gate of the heart;
> The wisdom-syllable becomes firmly established.
> Bend the vigor and strength fingers
> To rest on the backs of the meditation and gnosis fingers.
> Touch the chest with the mudrā,
> And recite this wondrous mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-muṣṭi oṃ.*

> The practitioner abides in equanimity.
> The two wings firmly bound,
> Placed face-up below the navel,
> The meditation and gnosis fingers in lotus form:
> This is called the samādhi-mudrā.
> Recite this mantra:

*Oṃ samādhi padme hrīḥ.*

> With the outbreath and inbreath,
> Abide in ānāpāna.
> Visualize the buddhas pervading space
> Snapping their fingers to awaken me:
>
> "Child of the Buddha! How will you
> Achieve unsurpassed perfect awakening
> Without knowing the tathāgatas'
> Wondrous Dharma of true reality?"
>
> Having heard the awakening call,
> The practitioner again says:
> "What is called true reality?
> May the most victorious one teach it."
>
> All the buddhas rejoice
> And speak these excellent words:
> "Well said, mahāsattva!
> You are able to ask such a question.
> Visualize within your heart
> The aḥ syllable-gate that was placed inside.
> Let the syllable penetrate the heart."
> Recite this mantra:

*Oṃ citta-prativīdaṃ karo mi.*

> Silently recite it once,
> Then visualize it becoming a moon-disc.
> Wishing to increase one's vigor,
> Further recite this wondrous mantra:

*Oṃ bodhi-citta-mutpādayāmi.*

> This causes the heart's moon-disc
> To become perfectly full and pure.
> Within it, visualize a wondrous lotus,
> Upon which rests a jewel-vajra.

The mantra:

*Oṃ tiṣṭha-maṇi vajra-padma.*

> Expanding to the measure of space,
> Pervading the three realms.
> Further recite this wondrous mantra,
> The vajra speech beyond sound:

*Oṃ spharaṇa-maṇi vajra-padma.*

> From this expanded wondrous lotus,
> A thousand rays of light stream forth.
> Within each ray of light,
> Immeasurable buddha-fields appear.
> In each field there is a wondrous lotus;
> Visualize the one who holds the jewel-lotus.
>
> Holding the jewel-lotus victory-banner,
> From within the banner a wondrous sound emerges:
> "Who among those of little merit
> Wishes to have all their aspirations fulfilled?
> Abiding in this samādhi of quiescence
> Is for the benefit of all sentient beings."
>
> Such bodhisattvas as these
> All abide in meditative equipoise.
> From the lotus womb,
> A thousand wondrous rays of light stream forth,
> All for the benefit of sentient beings:
> Dāna-pāramitā and the rest,
> Entering all samādhis throughout,
> Through the gate of skillful means in principle.
>
> Out of compassion for sentient beings,
> Creating immeasurable skillful means,
> Manifesting bodies in various forms,
> From birth to nirvāṇa,
> Turning the great wondrous Dharma-wheel:
> All this issues from the wish-fulfilling jewel.
>
> The wondrous Dharma that is taught
> Is all accomplished through the wheel.
> The wheel serves as wondrous wisdom,
> Able to sever all fetters.
> By turning the wondrous Dharma-wheel,
> This is the path of merit and wisdom.
> Next, rightly contemplate all,
> And gradually gather in the wisdom-lotus.

The mantra:

*Oṃ saṃhara-maṇi vajra-padma.*

> All the tathāgatas in every place
> Enter and become one body,
> Like a bright mirror
> Able to reflect ten thousand images.
>
> The self-nature body of the dharma-dhātu,
> Abiding upon the vajra-lotus,
> Transforms the jewel-lotus
> Into the true Cintāmaṇicakra Bodhisattva,
> Holding a wish-fulfilling jewel, with six arms and a golden body.
>
> Visualize all this as one's own body:
> The crown adorned with jewels,
> In the crown sits the Sovereign King [Amitābha],
> Abiding in the posture of teaching the Dharma.
>
> The first right hand is in the thinking posture,
> Out of compassion for sentient beings.
> The second holds the wish-fulfilling jewel,
> Able to fulfill all aspirations.
> The third holds a rosary,
> For delivering beings from the suffering of the animal realm.
> The first left hand presses Mount Potalaka,
> Accomplishing unshakeable stability.
> The second holds a lotus,
> Able to purify all that is unwholesome.
> The third holds a wheel,
> Able to turn the unsurpassed Dharma.
>
> The six arms and vast body
> Can travel through the six destinies.
> Through the skillful means of great compassion,
> The suffering of all sentient beings is severed.
>
> The practitioner, contemplating thus,
> Sits upon the moon-disc,
> The body streaming a thousand rays of light,
> With halos behind the head and back.
>
> Further visualize, upon the heart's moon-disc,
> A jewel-lotus also appearing.
> By this one becomes firmly established,
> Unmoved, having contemplated one's own body.
> To be free from all false thoughts,
> Recite this mantra:

*Oṃ nirdhiṣṭha-ratna vajra-padma-ātmakūhaṃ samayo 'haṃ mahā-samayo 'haṃ sarva-tathāgata-abhisaṃbodhi-ratna vajra-padma-ātmakūhaṃ.*

> By thus holding the Dharma,
> Join the ten fingers in a lotus shape,
> The vigor and strength fingers curved like jewels,
> And seal the heart, forehead, throat, and crown.
>
> The syllable hūṃ is visualized at the heart,
> Traṃ is placed at the forehead,
> Hrīḥ at the throat,
> And aḥ at the crown.
> By distributing this visualization,
> This body becomes like vajra.
> Further recite this mantra,
> With the lotus-voice as the sound:

*Oṃ ratna-vajra-dharma hrīḥ.*

> Next, one should form the abhiṣeka.
> The wise one joins lotus-palms,
> The vigor and strength fingers in jewel form,
> The meditation and gnosis fingers opened wide apart.
> Place at the forehead, recite the mantra,
> And visualize the buddhas performing abhiṣeka.

*Oṃ padma-bhṛkuṭī-dhāci-ratna-padma-abhiṣekaṃ-aṃ-traṃ.*

> With this wondrous mudrā,
> Separate the two hands to either side,
> As if stringing a lotus-garland,
> Slowly lowering and scattering before you.
> Visualize hanging white ribbons.
> Recite this wondrous mantra:

*Oṃ padma-māle aṃ hrīḥ traṃ.*

> Next, form the armor.
> The two hands in lotus form,
> From the heart, circle around to the back,
> From the back, circle to the navel,
> Then to the waist and both knees,
> Gradually up and around behind the neck,
> From the neck again to the throat,
> Then around behind the head,
> Returning to the forehead,
> Then around behind the crown,
> Slowly lowering and scattering in front.

Recite this secret mantra:

*Oṃ abhaye padma-kavaca-bandha-rakṣa oṃ hūṃ haṃ.*

> To delight all the buddhas,
> One should clap the lotus-mudrā.
> The two hands form the lotus-palm,
> Wondrously clapping to create joy.

The mantra:

*Oṃ padma-tuṣṭi ghoḥ.*

> Visualize before one's body,
> Contemplate the hrīḥ syllable-gate
> Transforming into the Lotus King,
> Within which is a hrīḥ syllable.
> Traṃ is placed on either side
> As vajra-jewel lotuses,
> All transforming into the honored one,
> Holding the Cintāmaṇi wondrous jewel.
>
> As with the earlier contemplation of one's body,
> So too is this contemplation now.
> To make the two bodies nondual,
> Next perform the summoning method.
>
> The ten fingers as an unopened lotus,
> The vigor and strength fingers in a hook shape.
> Recite this mantra,
> Making it a lotus-voice:

*Oṃ padma-cintana nākuśa hūṃ. Oṃ padma-vijñāna puṣpe hūṃ.*

> Having made the summoning,
> Next form the lasso-mudrā.
> As before, join the lotus-palms,
> The vigor and strength fingers touching like a ring.
> This is called the lotus-lasso,
> Able to fulfill all aspirations.
> Recite this mantra
> To summon entry into the wisdom-body:

*Oṃ padma-cintana amogha-pāśa hūṃ.*

> Having entered the wisdom-body,
> To prevent it from wavering,
> Next form the lotus-chain.
> With resolute mind,
> As before, join the lotus-palms.
> The vigor and meditation fingers pinching like a ring,
> The strength and gnosis fingers likewise,
> Interlocked like a chain of hooks:

*Oṃ padma-cintana sphūṭa hūṃ.*

> To create wondrous delight,
> Form the lotus-bell mudrā.
> With the lotus-offering hand,
> The meditation and gnosis fingers enter the palm,
> The vigor and strength fingers resting like rings.
> Recite this secret mantra:

*Oṃ cintana padma āviśaya hūṃ.*

> Next, recite the lotus
> Hundred-syllable secret mantra.
> Offer and present argha water:
> With saffron, white sandalwood,
> Camphor, and various flowers,
> Hold them with both hands and make offerings.

*Oṃ padma-sattva-samaya manupālaya padma-sattva-tvenopaṭiṣṭha nirdriḍho me bhava sutoṣyo me bhava anurakto me bhava supoṣyo me bhava sarva-siddhiṃ me prayaccha sarva-karma su-ca-me cittaṃ śrīyaṃ kuru hūṃ ha-ha-ha-ha hoḥ bhagavan sarva-tathāgata-padma mā me muñca padma-me bhava mahā-samaya-sattva hrīḥ.*

> Next, with inner and outer offerings,
> Make offerings to the Lotus King.
> The so-called inner offerings:
> Lotus-palms at the heart,
> Meditation and gnosis fingers both extended straight.
> This is called Lotus Joy.
> Recite this mantra:

*Oṃ cintana padma-lase hūṃ.*

> Next, form the garland-mudrā
> And make offerings with this.
> Without changing the previous joy-mudrā,
> Hold out both hands before you.
> Visualize various jeweled garlands
> Filling the entire realm of space.

The mantra:

*Oṃ cintana padma-māle hūṃ.*

> Next, with the song-mudrā,
> Make offerings to the wisdom-lotus holder.
> With the previous wondrous mudrā,
> Bend the palms and hook the fingers.
> From the navel gradually to the mouth,
> Lowering and scattering as if pouring.
> Visualize the sound of kiṃnaras
> Making offerings to the holy ones.

The mantra:

*Oṃ cintana padma-gīte hūṃ.*

> Next, form the dance-mudrā.
> Rotate the previous mudrā left and right,
> Join the wondrous lotus-palms,
> Place on the crown, and scatter.
> By these four offerings,
> One attains great supernatural powers.

The mantra:

*Oṃ cintana padma-nṛtte hūṃ.*

> Having made these four offerings,
> One can accomplish the most supreme affairs.
> Next, form the outer offerings.
> With a joyful heart, present them.
> Extend the mind without limit.
>
> The lotus incense-burning method:
> As the buddhas have truly taught,
> For the benefit of all sentient beings.
> The lotus-palms face downward and scatter,
> As if burning incense.
> Recite this secret mantra,
> Visualizing clouds of incense as offerings
> Pervading the realm of space,
> Offering to all the holy assembly:

*Oṃ padma-cintana dhūpe hūṃ.*

> Next, form the flower-mudrā,
> With the thirty-two marks
> Adorning all the tathāgatas.
> Contemplate wondrous clouds of colored flowers;
> Extend the mind to pervade all.
> As before, join the lotus-palms,
> Scatter upward as if casting flowers,
> Making offerings to all the tathāgatas
> And the sons of the sugatas.
> Visualize the realm of space
> Filled with fragrant clouds of flowers
> And jeweled trees of supreme beauty.
> Recite this secret mantra,
> The three karmas operating simultaneously:

*Oṃ padma-cintana puṣpe hūṃ.*

> Sentient beings, covered by ignorance,
> Are separated from the light of wisdom.
> To purify and remove this,
> One should form the wisdom-lamp mudrā.
> With the previous lotus-palm,
> The meditation and gnosis fingers standing upright and close together.
> Visualize the maṇi-lamp
> Illuminating the entire realm of space
> With immeasurable light streaming forth.
> Recite this mantra:

*Oṃ padma-cintana dīpe hūṃ.*

> The wise one should next form
> The liberation scented-paste mudrā,
> To purify sentient beings,
> Offering this fragrance of śīla.
> The two hands scatter in lotus-palm form,
> At the heart in a paste-applying gesture.
> The ten fingers accomplish the infusion.
> A sea of fragrance pervades space,
> Offered to the Buddha and the honored one.
> Recite this secret mantra:

*Oṃ padma-cintana gandhe hūṃ.*

> Having completed the inner and outer offerings,
> One should then engage in sequential recitation,
> Forming the secret root-mudrā
> To correspond with the mantra-king.
>
> First, recite the root mantra
> Clearly seven times.
> The palms flat at the heart,
> The patience and vow fingers like a lotus,
> The vigor and strength fingers in maṇi form,
> The remaining fingers all like a banner.
> Recite the root mantra,
> Contemplating the fulfillment of sentient beings' aspirations.

The mantra:

*Namo ratna-trayāya. Nama ārya-avalokiteśvarāya bodhisattvāya mahāsattvāya mahā-kāruṇikāya. Tadyathā: oṃ cakra-vartī cintāmaṇi mahā-padme ruru-tiṣṭha jvala ākṛṣāya hūṃ phaṭ svāhā.*

> Next, form the heart secret.
> Based on the previous root mudrā,
> The precept and direction, generosity and wisdom fingers bound:
> This is called the root-heart mudrā.
> All aspirations
> And what the heart contemplates,
> By forming this mudrā,
> Will all be accomplished.

The mantra:

*Oṃ padme cintā-maṇi jvala hūṃ.*

> Next, form the following-heart mudrā.
> The two hands firmly bound,
> The vigor and strength fingers in maṇi form,
> The meditation and gnosis fingers both extended,
> The precept and direction fingers also extended straight,
> The generosity and wisdom fingers crossed and standing upright.
> Recite this heart-within-the-heart:

The mantra:

*Oṃ varada padme hūṃ.*

> Next, visualize from the honored one's mouth
> The secret mantra streaming forth,
> Clearly forming a path of syllables,
> Radiating light of five colors,
> With alternating supreme hues,
> Entering the yogin's mouth,
> Arrayed upon the heart's moon-disc,
> Bright as red crystal.
>
> Contemplate each syllable one by one,
> Following principle to gradual awakening.
> Abide in samādhi and cultivate,
> Entering the gate of the syllable aḥ.
> Then enter the wheel-syllable contemplation,
> Universally contemplating all syllables.
> This is called samādhi-recitation,
> Attaining wisdom and liberation.
>
> Through this correspondence,
> Before long one achieves omniscience.
> If one constantly recites aloud in sequence,
> It is supremely wondrous and extraordinary.
>
> Abide in the contemplation of the honored one.
> One should not have an agitated mind,
> Not too high, not too low,
> Not too slow, not too fast.
>
> The wise one abandons discrimination
> And all false-thought mind.
> If one recites one lakṣa times,
> All that is sought will be accomplished.
>
> Hold the rosary with both hands:
> Crystal, lotus seeds,
> Conch beads, and other jewels,
> Flawless and lustrous.
> Thread one hundred and eight beads.
> Empower each one with seven recitations
> Of the heart and heart-within-heart mantras,
> Or the supplementary mantra.
>
> After empowering and stringing the rosary,
> Pass each bead at the heart,
> Sounding svāhā with each bead.
> One thousand one hundred and eight,
> Reciting according to one's capacity.
> Four times daily, or three,
> The after-midnight session being most supreme,
> As taught by the Buddha
> In the Cintāmaṇicakra Sūtra.
>
> If one cultivates in this way,
> In the present life one realizes the first bhūmi.
> After sixteen more lifetimes,
> One achieves unsurpassed bodhi.
> How much more so for worldly siddhis:
> How could they not be obtained in this very life?
>
> After reciting according to one's capacity,
> Again form the samaya,
> Perform the eight offerings once more,
> And send off the lord of the mantras.
>
> The two wings firmly bound,
> The patience and vow fingers in lotus-leaf form,
> From the heart to the face, scatter,
> And atop the crown join lotus-palms.
> Visualize the honored one in space
> Returning to their palace.

The mantra:

*Oṃ padma-sattva hrīḥ mūḥ.*

> Having sent off the holy one,
> Abide in the contemplation of the honored one.
> Or in a tranquil place,
> Read and recite the Mahāyāna:
> The Laṅkāvatāra, the Avataṃsaka,
> The Prajñā, and the Naya,
> And such sūtras and teachings,
> Contemplating and cultivating them.
>
> Having finished reading the sūtras,
> Walk, stand, or sit as one pleases,
> And even while sleeping,
> Never interrupt the mind of bodhi.
> Before long, one will attain siddhi.
>
> As Vajragarbha has taught,
> This great compassion ritual procedure:
> It does not require choosing days or mansions.
> For eating, drinking, and bathing,
> Whether pure or impure,
> One should always practice without interruption.
>
> Far from distraction,
> In an empty and tranquil place,
> Not pursuing worldly affairs.
> After recitation, make a vow,
> Form the samaya-mudrā,
> Bow to the buddhas and bodhisattvas,
> And then walk in meditation as one pleases.
