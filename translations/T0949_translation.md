# 奇特最勝金輪佛頂念誦儀軌法要
## Essential Ritual Methods for the Recitation of the Extraordinary Most Victorious Golden Wheel Buddha-Uṣṇīṣa

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0949

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown

---

One who wishes to practice the method of the One-Syllable Uṣṇīṣa-cakravartin must first enter the great maṇḍala ocean-assembly and receive consecration directly from a master. Having been granted authorization, one may then study the subtle and wondrous ritual procedures and the true-word teachings. Establish the practice-hall and paint the image of the principal deity entirely according to the rites of the division, and install it properly. If one wishes to swiftly accomplish the siddhis, one must first bathe and put on fresh, clean garments. If a renunciant, one must strictly maintain the vinaya without any breach. If a layperson, one should receive the eight precepts of the upavāsa. Furthermore, one must earnestly generate the vast mind. Toward the ācārya, one should have reverence as toward a Buddha. Out of compassion for sentient beings, generate the great vow of compassion, not sparing one's own life, constantly seeking to deliver them, causing all to leave suffering and attain great benefit and happiness. Having entered the practice-hall, touch the five limbs to the ground and sincerely make universal prostration to all the Tathāgatas. Then, before the principal deity and the Ganges-sands of holy assembly, with purified three karmas confess and remove karmic obstructions. Request all the Buddhas to turn the wondrous Dharma-wheel. Rejoice in the merit and wisdom cultivated by the three vehicles. Dedicate these wondrous wholesome deeds toward bodhi, that all sentient beings of the dharma-realm may together ascend to the Buddha-fruit.

Next, apply unguent to the hands and join the palms like a lotus. Contemplate that the original source is self-nature purity, obscured by adventitious defilements. Having made this contemplation, extend the mind broadly: I and all sentient beings are equally pure. To bring about this purity, recite the supremely excellent bathing true word:

*Oṃ sarva-bhāva-śuddhaṃ*

By reciting three times, the three karmas of body, speech, and mind, extending throughout the entire dharma-realm, are all entirely purified.

Next, form the Samaya Seal of the Heart of All the Buddha-Division:

> The ten fingers of both hands interlace inwardly in a fist.
> The two thumbs stand upright, joined together.
> Contemplate all the Buddhas filling empty space.
> Recite the true word three times and release at the crown.

The true word of all Buddhas' division-heart:

*Oṃ jina-jik svāhā*

By forming this seal and reciting the true word, the Buddhas of the ten directions, all the Tathāgatas, by their fundamental vows, all assemble and pervade empty space. They extend their radiance to illumine and empower the practitioner, freeing them from all obstructions and afflictions. The three karmas become purified, and one swiftly realizes bodhi.

Next, form the Samaya Seal of the Heart of All Bodhisattvas' Lotus Division:

> Following the previous Buddha-Division Heart Seal, bend the left thumb into the palm.
> The right thumb stands upright. Hook three times.
> Direct the mind to recollect Avalokiteśvara.
> The bodhisattva assembly of all atoms, with their lotus retinue, fills empty space.
> With utmost sincerity, recite the true word three times,
> Then release the seal at the right of the crown.

The true word of the Lotus-Division Heart:

*Oṃ ārolik svāhā*

By forming this seal and reciting the true word, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, by his fundamental vows, leads the lotus assembly and immeasurable bodhisattvas, who pervade empty space and all assemble. They emit great radiance to empower the practitioner, causing them to attain purity, have all wishes fulfilled, be freed from all obstructions and difficulties, and swiftly realize bodhi.

Next, form the Samaya Seal of the Heart of All Vajra-Division:

Following the previous Buddha-Division Heart Seal:

> Bend the right thumb into the palm. The left thumb stands upright. Hook three times.
> The retinue of atom-like multitudes surround on all sides,
> Their awesome radiance blazing throughout empty space.
> With utmost sincerity, recite the true word three times,
> Then release the seal at the left of the crown.

The true word of the Vajra-Division Heart:

*Oṃ vajra-dhṛk svāhā*

By forming this seal and reciting the true word, all the Vajrapāṇis of the ten directions, by their fundamental vows, gather like clouds, manifesting great awesome virtue to empower the practitioner, removing obstructions, crushing māras, with sovereign freedom and no obstruction, swiftly accomplishing the siddhis and quickly reaching bodhi.

Next, form the Vajra Armor Protection Seal:

> Both hands form vajra-fists. The two thumbs bend horizontally into the palms.
> The patience and vigor fingers form hooks in the gesture of donning armor.
> Empower the heart, forehead, two shoulders, and throat.
> With utmost sincerity, recite the true word three times,
> Seal the five places of the body and release at the crown.

The true word of the Armor Protection:

*Oṃ jvala hūṃ*

By forming the armor seal and reciting the true word, empowering the five places becomes the great body-protection, making one equal to the adamantine and indestructible body. Vajragarbha King, by his fundamental vows, leads his retinue, as numerous as atoms, immeasurable vajra-beings surrounding and attending. Their radiance blazes as they manifest wrathful forms, empowering the practitioner with awesome sovereign virtue, dispelling all who create obstructions, crushing all māras. On a small scale it can protect one's own body; on a large scale it can protect the national realm. All calamities, floods, fires, swords, armies, poisons, gu-sorcery, tigers, wolves, and fierce beasts all flee far away, not daring to look. Vināyakas naturally flee in terror, and there will never be any affliction or harm. One swiftly realizes bodhi.

Next, form the Duty-Fulfilling Seal:

> Both hands interlace inwardly in a fist. The two thumbs bend into the palms.
> Then extend the patience and vow fingers, tips touching.
> Three revolutions above the crown establish the boundary-protection.
> Turning left three times effects the dispelling.
> Wherever the mind directs, it establishes the direction.
> This seal of power can accomplish all deeds.

The true word of the Duty-Fulfilling Buddha-Uṣṇīṣa:

*Namo samanta-buddhānāṃ oṃ ṭaru oṃ bandha svāhā*

By forming this seal and reciting the true word, one can dispel obstacles and establish the protective boundary. This is called the awakening of the holy hearts of all the Buddhas. It can destroy others' hostile dharmas, crush and annihilate Māra. The devas, nāgas, eight divisions, rākṣasas, yakṣas, and vināyakas all flee in terror. By forming this seal, all worldly and supramundane deeds can be swiftly accomplished with sovereign freedom and no obstruction, and the siddhis manifest at once.

Next, form the Argha Offering Seal:

Both palms face upward in the shape of a vessel.

> The patience and vigor fingers bend beside the vow fingers.
> The thumbs curve to fill beside the patience and vigor fingers.
> Hold the vessel, revolve three times before the forehead,
> Visualizing the bathing of the holy assembly, pure as lotuses.

The true word for argha-bathing of the holy ones:

*Namo samanta-buddhānāṃ gagana-samāśa-ma svāhā*

By forming this seal, reciting the true word, and offering the argha to bathe the holy ones, this signifies that the practitioner's beginningless defilements and karmic impurities are cleansed and purified, like a wondrous lotus. In the future, one will obtain the undefiled wisdom as their fruit.

Next, form the Universal Offering of Empty Space Seal:

> The two palms join with fingers crossing at the first joints,
> Right pressing over left, bound like a vajra.
> Visualize the true and wondrous offerings that manifest,
> Immeasurable cloud-oceans offered to the holy assembly,
> Making offerings to all the Tathāgatas.
> Then speak the four lines of the wondrous gāthā.
> Recite the true word five times and release at the crown.

The true word of the great vidyā of empty space:

*Namo samanta-buddhānāṃ sarva-thaṃ medhā-gaite sphāra-hīmaṃ gagana-kaṃ svāhā*

By forming this seal and reciting the true word, through inconceivable power, from the seal flow forth all offerings. Immeasurable celestial beings each hold celestial jeweled vessels filled with wondrous offerings that pack the entirety of empty space: unguent, incense, lamps, food and drink, garments, banners, parasols, and necklaces, all offered to the Tathāgata. By reciting once, unguent flows forth; in the future one obtains the unguent of the precept-perfection. By reciting twice, incense flows forth; in the future one obtains the fragrance of concentration. By reciting three times, flower-garlands flow forth; in the future one obtains the wondrous lotus free from defilement. By reciting four times, precious food flows forth; in the future one obtains the joy of Dharma and the bliss of dhyāna. By reciting five times, precious lamps flow forth; in the future one obtains the five eyes of the Tathāgata. One thereby obtains all the above merits and pāramitā gates in their fullness. Furthermore, with the mind pervading the dharma-realm, with words of truth, recite the wondrous gāthā:

> "By the power of my merit,
> The empowering power of the Tathāgata,
> And the power of the dharma-realm,
> I make universal offerings and abide therein."

Next, with Brahmic praise, sing of the principal deity's auspicious marks, golden body, and supremely excellent merit:

*Maṇi-ṣṇī sarva-jagad-dhitai-ṣṇī yaśas-vibhūra-bhuvanaika-bandhai samastuṃ-vidyādhi-pacakra-māriṇāṃ namo stute tra-tri-cakravartinī*

By praising the three bodies of the Buddha, the merits of the six faculties, the eighty-four thousand kinds of supremely excellent inconceivable qualities: by the cause of this song of praise, one will in the future obtain the wondrous fruit. The ocean of accumulated merit is immeasurable, equal to the Extraordinary Honored One.

Next, form the Great Seal of the Buddha-Eye Vidyā:

> Both hands join in a hollow palm, not yet opened.
> The two thumbs bend into the palms.
> The patience and vigor fingers each press against the back of the vow fingers,
> Like eyes opening to illuminate all.
> Place at the brow, heart, forehead, two shoulders, and throat.
> Give rise to great compassion and remove all fears.

Recite the true word and release at the crown.

The true word of the Buddha-Eye Great Vidyā Mother:

*Namaḥ sarva-tathāgatebhyo viśuddha-bhyaḥ samyak-saṃbuddhebhyaḥ oṃ ruru sphuru jvālā tiṣṭha siddha-locanī sarvārtha-sādhanī svāhā*

This is called the Great Vidyā of All Buddha-Eyes. It can pacify what is fearsome and difficult to tame. For the sake of accomplishing worldly and supramundane deeds, and all positions within the Dharma of the Great Wheel King of all Buddha-Uṣṇīṣas, it extinguishes all disputes. It is also the clan-mother of all the Buddhas, and the birth-mother of all the great bodhisattvas. When the awesome virtue and extraordinary power of the Uṣṇīṣa-Cakravartin true word is taught, all the great bodhisattvas lose their composure, and the awesome power of the devas, nāgas, and spirits is eclipsed. Whenever one recites the one-syllable buddha-uṣṇīṣa vidyā, one must first recite the Buddha-Eye vidyā, and after the recitation session is complete, recite it again seven times. The holy assembly will rejoice, and sentient beings will be at peace and happy.

Next, form the One-Syllable Root Seal:

> Both hands tightly interlace inwardly in a fist.
> The patience and vow fingers form a sword shape.
> The thumbs, patience, and vigor fingers stand with nails touching.
> This is called the Vajra Buddha-Uṣṇīṣa Seal.
> Visualize one's body with awesome virtue equal to the principal deity,
> With flowing radiance shining throughout the ten directions.

Seal the five places of the body and release at the crown.

The true word of the Golden Wheel Cakravartin King:

*Namo samanta-buddhānāṃ oṃ hūṃ truṃ hūṃ*

> This is the mahā-seal, the so-called Uṣṇīṣa of the Tathāgata.
> By forming and reciting it, one becomes equal to the World-Honored One.
> When the uṣṇīṣa-cakravartin vidyā is proclaimed,
> The three-thousand worlds shake six times.
> Mountains and rivers surge and boil,
> Great fire pervades the palaces of Māra.
> Terrified, they all take refuge.
> The sufferings of hell are stilled.
> The bodhisattvas, nāgas, and celestial hosts
> Stand in awe as before a Buddha's body.

Next, form the Great Compassion Universal Protection Seal:

The ten fingers interlace in a crescent-moon shape. The thumbs and little fingers touch each other. Seal the heart, forehead, and throat, then release at the crown.

The true word of compassionate protection:

*Oṃ buddha-maitri vajra-rakṣa haṃ*

> Forming the seal and reciting the vidyā removes fears.
> Giving rise to the great compassionate mind pervading all,
> May all enemies and hostility that encroach upon us
> Obtain great peace and happiness.
> By this threefold recitation of compassionate protection,
> The minds of hatred and harm will awaken of themselves.
> They will deeply repent and turn toward bodhi,
> And with joy and reverence approach the practitioner.
> If one constantly recites this vidyā at the beginning and end,
> Terrified beings will find comfort.
> This protects great benefit and supreme deeds,
> Swiftly fulfilling all wishes and realizing the siddhis.

Next, form the Rosary-Purification Seal:

> Coil the rosary within the palms,
> Like an unopened lotus bud.
> Hold them joined above the heart
> And empower with three times seven recitations.
> Then raise them to the crown with a devoted mind,
> And make offerings according to the Dharma.

The rosary-purification true word:

*Oṃ amṛte jayā siddhi siddhāṃ te svāhā*

Next, form the Counting Seal:

> Both hands form half vajra-fists. The thumbs pinch the precept area.
> The patience and vigor fingers are behind the vow fingers.
> Recite the counting vidyā seven times.

Then proceed with the recitation.

The rosary-holding true word:

*Namo bhagavate susiddhi sādhaya sādhaya sādharāte svāhā*

> First, purify the three karmas and recite according to the method.
> Be single-minded, without distraction, with attention fixed on the principal deity.
> Pronounce each syllable clearly,
> Neither too slow nor too fast.
> Do not give rise to sloth;
> Coughing and yawning,
> Defilement and drowsiness:
> All such faults,
> Even the slightest loss of mindfulness,
> Will prevent accomplishment.
> When the recitation quota is complete, place the rosary back in its original spot.
> Then enter the samaya and abide in the principal-deity contemplation.
> After the duration of a meal, emerge from concentration.
> Then re-form the root seal.
> Next, form the Buddha-Eye vidyā.
> With song, praise in melodious tones.
> After universal offerings and argha,
> Dispel by turning left and release.
> Re-apply the three-division body-protection.
> Last of all, the compassionate protection seal.
> Prostrations and confession follow the previous format.
> Read and recite the Mahāyāna sūtras.
> Circumambulate as one wishes.
> At all times, never break the continuity.

## Essential Methods of the Extraordinary Most Victorious Golden Wheel Buddha-Uṣṇīṣa Recitation Ritual

If one is unable to recite the previous great Buddha-Eye true word, reciting this heart true word is also acceptable. The Buddha-Eye short mantra:

*Namo samanta-buddhānāṃ oṃ buddha-locanī svāhā*

Gyōshun. Second collation completed.

## Water-Altar Method

Take fresh, clean cow dung. Dung from a calf that has not eaten grain or beans is the very best. Do not use raw cow dung directly. Take a new, clean earthenware pot and draw clean water. Do not use leftover water; pour it into a clean vessel. Mix the dung with the water, stir and remove the dregs, and add sandalwood powder. Take one willow branch. Hold it in the right hand; in the left hand hold a vajra and a rosary. Sit facing east and recite the buddha-uṣṇīṣa heart mantra, stirring the water with the willow branch, empowering with one hundred and eight recitations. Carry the mantra-water vessel into the practice-hall. Starting from the northeast corner, sprinkle the fragrant water, and with the right hand rub the ground. Rub following the direction of the sun. As you rub, let it dry naturally; do not let water pool. When the entire floor of the practice-hall has been rubbed once, extend to the area one step beyond each of the four sides of the practice-hall. Set up posts at the four corners and connect them with ropes to form the boundary. This is then called the boundary-ground of the Buddha-Uṣṇīṣa Samādhi Dhāraṇī. After the first rubbing, perform a second and third in the same manner. Do not use leftover fragrant water for rubbing the ground. Each day, take fresh, clean cow dung and prepare it as before. Having done this, the ground is called the ground of the eight meritorious waters of the Buddha-Uṣṇīṣa Samādhi Dhāraṇī. The altar ground is now dry and complete.

Colophon: Second collation completed.
