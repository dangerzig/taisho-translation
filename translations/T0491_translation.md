# 六菩薩亦當誦持經
## Sūtra on the Six Bodhisattvas Who Should Also Be Recited and Upheld

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0491

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown (失譯)

---

Siṃhavikrīḍita Bodhisattva, Siṃhavikrānta Bodhisattva, Siṃhadhvaja Bodhisattva, Siṃhakāra Bodhisattva, Dṛḍhavīravīrya Bodhisattva, Vajraghātaprajñā Bodhisattva.

> Majestic Buddhas of the ten directions,
> Magnificent kings among the holy,
> With the thirty-two wondrous marks
> And the eighty excellent characteristics,
> Emanating wondrous radiance from their bodies,
> Illuminating all the ten directions ...
> With all my being I take refuge
> And bow my head to the kings of the Dharma.

> Majestic Buddhas of the ten directions,
> With boundless great compassion,
> Arousing and establishing great vows,
> Saving and liberating all sentient beings ...
> Those who hear much and rejoice,
> Their merit cannot be measured or recounted.
> With all my being I take refuge
> And bow my head to the greatly compassionate ones.

> Majestic Buddhas of the ten directions,
> Whose merit cannot be measured,
> Whose wisdom is like a boundless sea,
> Pouring its grace upon all ten directions ...
> Those who hear their names
> Eliminate faults and dissolve all calamities.
> I take refuge in the great wisdom
> And bow my head to the king of perfect enlightenment.

> Majestic Buddhas of the ten directions,
> Whose buddha-lands are supremely pure,
> Adorned with the seven treasures,
> Arrayed with wondrous fragrant sandalwood,
> Inhabited entirely by bodhisattvas,
> With no mention of the two vehicles,
> Speaking only of non-retrogression ...
> The excellence of the path of Prajñāpāramitā.

> Majestic Buddhas of the ten directions,
> Treasures of the path across the three ages ...
> Those who hear, believe, and rejoice
> Swiftly attain the supreme truth,
> Roaming upon the bodhisattva path,
> Steadfastly abiding without retrogression,
> In each life always encountering Buddhas,
> Swiftly meeting all World-Honored Ones.

> Majestic Buddhas of the ten directions,
> Teachers and guides across the three ages ...
> With sincere mind harboring reverence,
> With faith and joy, free from all doubt,
> In each life always dignified and fair,
> With a countenance of brilliant splendor,
> With eloquence and wisdom uniquely transcendent ...
> I vow to pay homage to the teacher of gods and humans.

There are also four bodhisattvas.

These four great beings were all teachers of the Buddhas of the ten directions in past ages. When these four great beings first began their practice, they made great vows, saying: "I shall liberate all sentient beings until every one of them has attained nirvāṇa, and only then shall I attain buddhahood." For this reason, one should recite, contemplate, and uphold their names, thereby attaining immeasurable and boundless merit, reaching the stage of non-retrogression. Suppose that the entire trichiliocosm, filled throughout all of space with the finest gold, were offered as a gift in generosity; yet this would not compare with reciting and contemplating the names of these four great beings for even a single moment. The merit thereby obtained surpasses that golden offering by a thousandfold, a hundred-millionfold, a ten-thousandfold times over.

Their names are:

Tyaktaskandhanīvaraṇa Bodhisattva, Śāntêndriya Bodhisattva, Prajñātejas Bodhisattva, Avivikta Bodhisattva.
