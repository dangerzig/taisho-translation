# 無垢淨光大陀羅尼經
## Great Immaculate Pure Light Dhāraṇī Sūtra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1024

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Mitāśan (彌陀山).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was in Kapilavastu, in the great monastery, together with a great assembly of immeasurable monks; and also immeasurable hundred-thousand billion nayutas of bodhisattva-mahāsattvas, whose names were: Sarva-nīvaraṇa-viṣkambhin Bodhisattva, Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva, Samantabhadra Bodhisattva, Akṣayamati Bodhisattva, and Maitreya Bodhisattva, among others, as their leaders. There were also immeasurable devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, humans and non-humans, an immeasurable great assembly surrounding him in reverence as he taught the dharma.

At that time in the city there was a great brāhmaṇa named Kapilacaṇḍa, who paid homage to non-Buddhist paths and did not believe in the buddha-dharma. A physiognomist told him: "Great brāhmaṇa! After seven days you will certainly die." When the brāhmaṇa heard these words, his heart was filled with sorrow and dread, and he thought: "Who can save me? Whom should I rely on?" Then he further thought: "The śramaṇa Gautama is said to be omniscient and to have realized omniscience. I should go to him. If he truly is omniscient, he will surely speak of my fearful situation." Having thus resolved, he went to the Buddha's presence. From afar, before the assembly, he beheld the Tathāgata. He wanted to ask questions but was still hesitant.

Then the Tathāgata Śākyamuni, who clearly sees all dharmas of the three times, knew the brāhmaṇa's thoughts, and with a compassionate, gentle voice told him: "Great brāhmaṇa! After seven days you will certainly die. You will fall into the terrifying Avīci Hell, and from there enter the sixteen hells. Having emerged, you will then receive a caṇḍāla body. After dying, you will be reborn among pigs, always dwelling in foul mud and constantly eating filth, with a long lifespan of much suffering. Later, when you obtain a human body, you will be poor, of low caste, impure and foul, ugly, dark, emaciated, dried up, and afflicted with leprosy. No one will be pleased to see you. Your throat will be as narrow as a needle, and you will always lack food and drink. You will be beaten and suffer great misery."

When the brāhmaṇa heard these words, he was filled with great terror, weeping with grief. He rushed to the Buddha's presence, prostrated at his feet, and addressed the Buddha: "The Tathāgata is the one who rescues all sentient beings! I now repent and take refuge in the World-Honored One. I beg you to save me from the sufferings of the great hells!"

The Buddha said: "Great brāhmaṇa! At the three-way crossroads in this city of Kapilavastu there is an ancient buddha-stūpa, within which there are presently tathāgata relics. That stūpa has collapsed. You should go there and thoroughly repair it, construct a wheel-finial post, write out the dhāraṇī and place it inside, make great offerings, and in accordance with the dharma recite the divine mantra seven times. This will cause your life-root to be restored and extended. Long afterward, when your life ends, you will be reborn in the Land of Ultimate Bliss, where for a hundred thousand kalpas you will experience the greatest joy. After that, in the Abhirati world, for another hundred thousand kalpas you will experience joy as before. After that, in the Tuṣita heavenly palace, for another hundred thousand kalpas you will experience joy in succession. In every birth you will always remember past lives, all obstructions will be eliminated, all sins extinguished, and you will be forever free from the hells and all such sufferings, always seeing the buddhas, always protected by the tathāgatas.

"Brāhmaṇa! If any monks, nuns, laymen, laywomen, good men, or good women have short lives or many illnesses, they should repair old stūpas or make small clay stūpas, write the dhāraṇī in accordance with the dharma, consecrate the cord and make an altar. By this merit, those whose lives are about to end will have their lifespans extended. All those suffering from illness will be healed. They will be forever free from the hells, the animal realm, and the realm of hungry ghosts. Their ears will never even hear the sounds of hell, let alone their bodies suffer there."

When the brāhmaṇa heard these words, his heart was filled with joy, and he wished to go immediately to the site of the ruined stūpa to repair it according to the teaching.

Then from among the assembly, Sarva-nīvaraṇa-viṣkambhin Bodhisattva rose from his seat, joined his palms, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! What is this dhāraṇī dharma that can generate and increase merit and good roots?"

The Buddha said: "There is a great dhāraṇī named 'The Most Victorious Immaculate Pure Light Great Altar Dharma.' All the buddhas use it to comfort sentient beings. If anyone hears this dhāraṇī, the five heinous sins are extinguished and the gates of hell are closed. It eliminates the stains of greed, jealousy, and all sins. Those with short lives all have their lifespans extended, and all auspicious matters are accomplished."

Then Sarva-nīvaraṇa-viṣkambhin Bodhisattva again addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! May the Buddha teach this dhāraṇī dharma so that all sentient beings may attain long life, so that all sins and obstructions may be purified, and so that it may serve as a great light for all sentient beings."

Then the World-Honored One, hearing this request, emitted great radiance from the top of his head that illuminated the three-thousandfold great-thousandfold world system. Having awakened all the tathāgatas everywhere, the light returned to its source and entered through the top of the Buddha's head. Then the Buddha, with the beautiful, pleasing, *kalaviṅka* melodious voice, spoke this mantra:

### Root Dhāraṇī

*Namaḥ saptānāṃ samyak-saṃbuddha-koṭīnāṃ pariśuddha-mānasa-bhāgavat-cita pratiṣṭhitānāṃ namaḥ bhagavate amitāyuṣe tathāgatāya oṃ tathāgata-śuddhe ayur-viśuddhani saṃhara saṃhara sarva-tathāgata-vīrya-bale-na pratisamhara ayuḥ-smara smara sarva-tathāgata-samaya-bodhi bodhi-buddhi vibuddhi buddhaya buddhaya sarva-pāpa-avaraṇa-viśuddhe vigata-māla-paya-subuddha-buddhe huru huru svāhā*

The Buddha said: "Sarva-nīvaraṇa-viṣkambhin! This is the root dhāraṇī mantra. If one wishes to perform this dharma, on the eighth, thirteenth, fourteenth, or fifteenth day of the month, circumambulate a relic stūpa seventy-seven times, reciting this dhāraṇī also seventy-seven times. One should construct an altar with protective purification, and write this mantra seventy-seven copies. Out of respect for the dharma, one should make offerings to the scribe with fragrant flowers, food and drink, clean garments, bathing, anointing incense, and burning incense, or give the seven treasures or whatever one can. One should place the mantra copies inside a stūpa and make offerings to that stūpa. Or one may make seventy-seven small clay stūpas, placing one copy within each, and make offerings. When the dharma is properly performed, those whose lives are about to end will have their lifespans extended. All past obstructions and evil-destiny karma will be entirely extinguished. One will be forever free from the hells, the realm of hungry ghosts, and the animal realm. In every birth one will always remember past lives. All aspirations will be fulfilled. This is equivalent to having planted good roots in the presence of seventy-seven billion tathāgatas. All diseases and afflictions will be eliminated.

"If a person is gravely ill and about to die, one should construct a square altar and draw various figures upon it. Fill vases with fragrant water and place them at the four corners. Arrange incense burners and burn various fine incenses. Fill five-colored bowls with various foods and the three white foods, and place five more bowls on the altar as offerings. Fill one vessel with various food and drink and place one vase of water at the center of the altar. Near the edge of the altar, draw an image of Vināyaka with a lamp placed above its head. Bring the sick person to the west side of the altar, facing the altar. Fill one vessel of food and place it on the altar before the sick person. The mantra-master must be pure and in accordance with the dharma. Recite the mantra over the sick person seventy-seven times. The person who is about to die will fall into a coma for seven days, after which their life will be continued and their consciousness will return, as if waking from a dream.

"If one maintains purity and recites this mantra once daily for a full hundred years, that person at death will be reborn in the Land of Ultimate Bliss. If one recites it constantly at all times, until enlightenment one will always remember past lives and be forever free from untimely death and all evil destinies.

"Furthermore, if a person recites the mantra seventy-seven times on behalf of a deceased person, calling their name with sincere mind, and if that deceased person has fallen into evil destinies, they will immediately be freed from the suffering of the evil paths and be reborn in heaven to enjoy bliss. Or if one calls the deceased person's name and writes this dhāraṇī in accordance with the dharma and places it within a buddha-stūpa, making offerings according to the dharma, the deceased will also be freed from evil destinies and be reborn in heaven, or even in the Tuṣita heavenly palace, and until enlightenment will not fall into evil paths.

"If good men or good women circumambulate this buddha-stūpa, or prostrate, or make offerings, they will receive the prophecy of supreme perfect enlightenment and non-retrogression. All past obstructions and sins will be entirely eliminated. Even flying birds and animals that come within the shadow of this stūpa will be forever freed from the animal realm and evil destinies.

"If one who has committed the five heinous sins enters the shadow of the stūpa or touches it, all those sins will be eliminated. Where the stūpa is placed, there will be no evil spirits, yakṣas, rākṣasas, pūtanas, piśācas, evil beasts, evil nāgas, poisonous insects, poisonous plants, nor any phantoms or evil ghosts that steal vital force. There will be no swords, weapons, water, fire, frost, hail, famine, untimely death, evil dreams, or inauspicious sufferings. If evil omens appear in that land, the stūpa will manifest divine transformations and emit great blazing light, causing all those evil and inauspicious things to be completely destroyed. If hostile beings, enemies, enemy companions, robbers, bandits, or invaders wish to harm that land, the stūpa will also emit great blazing light, and at that place weapons and armor will appear. When the evil bandits see this, they will naturally flee and scatter. All good devas and spirits will always guard that land. Within a hundred yojanas in all four directions, a great protective boundary will be formed. Men and women within it, and even animals, will be free from epidemics, illness, suffering, and conflict, and will not commit any unlawful acts. No other mantra-arts can overcome this. This is the root dhāraṇī dharma.

### Wheel-Finial Post Dhāraṇī

"Good sons! I shall now teach you the dhāraṇī dharma for the wheel-finial post."

Then he spoke the mantra:

*Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-vipulāye-ṣṭha-maṇi-kanaka-hāra-jātā-vipuṣṭhāye-ṣṭha ṭuru ṭuru samanta-virukite sara sara pāpa-viśudhani buddhani saṃbuddhani pravara-yeṣṭha vare-maṇi-toṣe huru cira-māla-viśuddhe hūṃ hūṃ svāhā*

"Good sons! One should write this mantra ninety-nine copies in accordance with the dharma and place them around all four sides of the wheel-finial post. Also write this mantra and the description of its powers, and place them secretly covered in the center of the post.

"Having done this, it is equivalent to having erected ninety-nine thousand wheel-finial posts, equivalent to having placed ninety-nine thousand buddha-relics, equivalent to having constructed ninety-nine thousand buddha-relic stūpas, equivalent to having constructed ninety-nine thousand great stūpas of the eight jewels, and equivalent to having constructed ninety-nine thousand enlightenment-seat stūpas.

"If one makes even a single small clay stūpa and places this dhāraṇī within it, it is equivalent to having made ninety-nine thousand small jeweled stūpas.

"If any sentient being circumambulates this stūpa, or makes even one prostration or one palm-joining, or offers one flower, one stick of incense, burning incense, anointing incense, a bell, a pennant, or a canopy, it is equivalent to having made offerings to ninety-nine thousand buddha-stūpas. This constitutes the accomplishment of vast good roots and an accumulation of merit.

"If flying birds, mosquitoes, gnats, or flies come within the shadow of the stūpa, they will receive the prophecy of supreme perfect enlightenment and non-retrogression. If one merely sees this stūpa from afar, or hears the sound of its bells, or hears its name, all the five heinous sins and every karmic obstruction will be eliminated. One will always be protected by all the buddhas and attain the pure path of the tathāgatas. This is the wheel-finial dhāraṇī dharma.

### Stūpa Construction Dhāraṇī

"Good sons! I shall now teach you the dhāraṇī dharma for constructing buddha-stūpas."

Then he spoke the mantra:

*Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-māla-viśudhani gandha-vīri-padma-vare pratisamskara tathāgata-dhātu-dhare dhara dhara saṃdhara saṃdhara sarva-tathāgata-adhiṣṭhite svāhā*

"If any monks, nuns, laymen, or laywomen, whether building a stūpa themselves or instructing others to build one, whether repairing an old stūpa or making a small stūpa, whether of clay, brick, or stone, should first recite the mantra one thousand and eight times and then begin construction. The stūpa may be as small as a fingernail or as tall as one cubit, or even a yojana. By the power of the mantra and one's sincerity, from the clay stūpa a wondrous fragrance will emanate: *goṣīrṣa* sandalwood, red and white sandalwood, dragon-brain, musk, turmeric, and celestial fragrances.

"Whether done oneself or by instructing others, one accomplishes vast good roots and an accumulation of merit. If one's life is short, it will be extended. At the time of death, one will see ninety-nine billion hundred-thousand nayutas of buddhas. One will always be remembered by all the buddhas, who will bestow the prophecy of rebirth in the Land of Ultimate Bliss. One's lifespan there will be ninety-nine billion hundred-thousand nayutas of years. One will always possess the knowledge of past lives, the divine eye, a celestial body, divine hearing, and a divine nose. Celestial sandalwood fragrance will emanate from one's body, and the fragrance of blue lotus flowers will always issue from one's mouth. One will attain the five supernatural powers and non-retrogression from supreme perfect enlightenment.

"If one consecrates fragrant clay, even as little as a mustard seed, and applies it to the stūpa, that person will also obtain the great mass of merit as described above.

### Consecration and Assembly

"If monks, nuns, laymen, or laywomen write the dhāraṇī dharma in accordance with the dharma, with a pure mind making reverent offerings as to the Buddha himself, and also make excellent offerings to the scribe; and having written and sealed the mantras as described above, place them within the stūpa and within the repaired stūpa and within the wheel-finial post, accomplishing the dharma, that person will obtain vast good roots and an accumulation of merit."

When the Buddha taught this dhāraṇī seal dharma, all the tathāgatas of the ten directions spoke in unison and praised: "Excellent! Excellent! The Tathāgata Śākyamuni, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One, has well taught this great dhāraṇī seal dharma, causing all sentient beings to not pass by in vain, to obtain great benefit and gather great merit, up to and including non-retrogression from supreme perfect enlightenment."

Then from the assembly, the devas, nāgas, the eightfold assembly, all the bodhisattvas, Vajrapāṇi, the Four Kings, Śakra, Brahmā, Nārāyaṇa, Maheśvara, Maṇibhadra, Pūrṇabhadra, and the spirits of Bala, Yama, Varuṇa, Kubera, and Vāsava, and the assemblies of ṛṣis, having heard this dharma, generated the mind of renunciation, became tamed and gentle, and were filled with great joy. With great voices they said to one another: "Rare! Rare! The tathāgatas! Rare! Rare! The true and wondrous dharma! Rare! Rare! This dhāraṇī seal dharma! What the Tathāgata has taught is extremely difficult to encounter."

At that time the great brāhmaṇa Kapilacaṇḍa, hearing this great dhāraṇī dharma seal of supreme merit and benefit, immediately attained clear comprehension of the nature of dharma, became far removed from dust and defilement, severed all afflictions, eliminated all karmic obstructions, had his lifespan extended, and was filled with great, immeasurable, leaping joy. He caused all sentient beings likewise to attain purity of mind.

### Sarva-nīvaraṇa-viṣkambhin's Heart-Seal Dhāraṇī

Then Sarva-nīvaraṇa-viṣkambhin Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva, bearing a jeweled platform adorned with various precious gems in magnificent splendor, adorned with the adornments of the Buddha, out of love for the dharma, made offerings to the Tathāgata. He circumambulated three times, prostrated at the Buddha's feet, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! This great dhāraṇī altar dharma seal is extremely difficult to encounter. The World-Honored One has taught this treasury of wondrous dharma for all sentient beings, establishing it in Jambudvīpa, causing sentient beings to plant great good roots, granting them lifespan and eliminating afflictions. I too shall now, for the sake of causing sentient beings to plant good roots and to make offerings to all the tathāgatas, teach my own heart-seal dhāraṇī dharma before the Buddha."

Then he spoke the mantra:

*Namaḥ bhagavate nava-navatīnāṃ samyak-saṃbuddha-koṭi-nayuta-śata-sahasrāṇāṃ namaḥ sarva-nīvaraṇa-viṣkambhi-ni bodhisattvāya oṃ ṭuru ṭuru sarva-avaraṇa-viśuddhani sarva-tathāgata-māyūra-pāraṇi-vipulī-ṭi-māli sarva-siddhānāṃ maskṛte bhala-bhala sarva-sattvāvalokani hūṃ sarva-nīvaraṇa-viṣkambhi-ne sarva-pāpa-viśoddhani svāhā*

"World-Honored One! This dhāraṇī was spoken by ninety-nine billion buddhas. If anyone sincerely recites it even briefly, all sins will be eliminated. If one writes this mantra ninety-nine copies in accordance with the dharma and places them within a stūpa or around its four sides, and people prostrate before and praise, or offer fragrant flowers, anointing incense, lamps, and candles to this stūpa, those good men and women will in this very life have all sins eliminated, all obstructions removed, and all wishes fulfilled. This is equivalent to having made offerings to ninety-nine billion hundred-thousand nayutas of Ganges-sand tathāgatas, and equivalent to having made offerings to ninety-nine billion hundred-thousand nayutas of Ganges-sand relic stūpas. This constitutes the accomplishment of vast good roots and an accumulation of merit.

"If a monk, on the eighth, thirteenth, fourteenth, or fifteenth day of the month, bathes, maintains purity, wears clean garments, and for one day and one night does not eat, or at times eats only the three white foods, circumambulates a buddha-stūpa and recites this dhāraṇī one hundred and eight times, sins accumulated over a hundred thousand kalpas and the five heinous sins will all be eliminated. I, Sarva-nīvaraṇa-viṣkambhin, will then manifest in person and fulfill all their wishes, enabling them to see all the tathāgatas.

"If one recites two hundred and eight times, one attains all meditative absorptions. If one recites three hundred and eight times, one attains the samādhi of purifying all obstructions. If one recites four hundred and eight times, the Four Great Heavenly Kings will constantly come near and personally guard one's body, increasing great majestic virtue. If one recites five hundred and eight times, one gathers immeasurable, incalculable great good roots. If one recites six hundred and eight times, one accomplishes the root dharma of this mantra and becomes a celestial mantra-holding ṛṣi. If one recites seven hundred and eight times, one attains great majestic virtue endowed with radiance. If one recites eight hundred and eight times, one attains purity of mind. If one recites nine hundred and eight times, one attains purity of the five faculties. If one recites one thousand and eight times, one attains the fruit of stream-entry. If one recites two thousand times, one attains the fruit of once-returning. If one recites three thousand times, one attains the fruit of non-returning. If one recites four thousand times, one attains the fruit of arhatship. If one recites five thousand times, one attains the fruit of a pratyekabuddha. If one recites six thousand times, one attains the ground of Samantabhadra. If one recites seven thousand times, one attains the first ground. If one recites eight thousand times, one attains the fifth ground. If one recites nine thousand times, one attains the Universal Gateway Dhāraṇī. If one recites ten thousand times, one attains the Immovable Ground. If one recites eleven thousand times, one attains the ground of the Tathāgata, accomplishes the marks of a great being, and roars the great lion's roar.

"Furthermore, if a person wishes to accomplish merit and great benefit in this present life, they should repair an old stūpa, recite the mantra and circumambulate one hundred and eight times, and all the wishes in their heart will be fulfilled without exception."

Then the Buddha Śākyamuni praised Sarva-nīvaraṇa-viṣkambhin: "Excellent! Excellent! Good son! You are able to accord with the mantra dharma expounded by the Tathāgata and assist in proclaiming it."

Then the great yakṣa lord Vajrapāṇi addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! This great mantra-king dhāraṇī dharma is equal to the tathāgata-garbha and also equal to a buddha-stūpa. The World-Honored One, with this supreme dharma, has established Jambudvīpa, causing all sentient beings to attain liberation, and in the future it will accomplish great buddha-work."

The Buddha said: "Vajrapāṇi! When this great mantra dharma exists in the world, it is the same as if the Tathāgata were present, for it can accomplish the work that the Buddha accomplishes. Even a little effort yields a great mass of merit. How much more so for much effort! The good roots obtained, even if a hundred thousand billion nayutas of Ganges-sand buddhas were to speak of them, they could not exhaust them. As seen by the Buddha's eye, it is beyond analogy, beyond measure, beyond description."

Vajrapāṇi asked: "For what reason does little effort accomplish a great mass of merit?"

### Six Pāramitā Completion Dhāraṇī

The Buddha said: "Listen attentively; I shall explain for you. If monks, nuns, laymen, or laywomen wish to fully accomplish the great mass of merit, they should write ninety-nine copies each of the four great dhāraṇī mantra-dharma kings according to the methods described above. Then before a buddha-stūpa, construct a square altar, spread cow-dung and then cover it with clean earth, sprinkle it with fragrant water and polish it smooth. Place five offering bowls on the altar. Write the four dhāraṇīs ninety-nine copies each. Make ninety-nine small stūpas by hand, placing one copy within each. Place the wheel-finial mantra within the small stūpas' wheel-finial posts, arrange them in rows upon the altar, and offer them with various flowers and incense. Circumambulate seven times and recite this dhāraṇī:"

*Namaḥ nava-navatīnāṃ tathāgata-koṭīnāṃ gaṅgā-nadī-vālukā-samānāṃ oṃ vipulī vimālī pravarī śīnā-varī sara sara sarva-tathāgata-dhātu-garbhe sthiti-adhiṣṭhite svāhā anāṭu-ṭu-vani svāhā sarva-deva-nava-ārya-mibud-dhādhiṣṭhāna-samaya svāhā*

"If one practices in accordance with this dharma, the six pāramitās will all be fulfilled. This is equivalent to having constructed ninety-nine billion hundred-thousand nayutas of Ganges-sand seven-jeweled stūpas, and equivalent to having made offerings to ninety-nine billion hundred-thousand nayutas of tathāgatas, arhats, and perfectly enlightened ones, all with great celestial offering-clouds of various adornments, celestial palaces, and celestial implements. All those tathāgatas will remember these good men and women, causing them to attain vast good roots and an accumulation of merit.

"If anyone writes this mantra-king in accordance with the dharma, upholds, recites, makes offerings, honors, and wears it upon their body, by the power of the mantra that person will be protected. All enemies and enemy companions, all yakṣas, rākṣasas, pūtanas, and others will be unable to harm that person. Each will be filled with fear and flee in all directions.

"If anyone is able to speak with that person, even the five heinous sins will be eliminated. If anyone hears that person's voice, or is in their shadow, or touches their body, all their past heavy obstructions and sins will be eliminated. All poisons will be unable to harm them, fire cannot burn them, water cannot sweep them away, enchantments and evil spirits cannot trouble them, thunder and lightning cannot startle them. They will always be blessed by all the buddhas, all the tathāgatas will comfort and protect them, and all good devas and spirits will increase their power. No other mantra-art can overcome them.

"Therefore, one should seek this mantra dharma in every place, write it out and place it within stūpas on major roads, so that all sentient beings who pass by, even birds, beasts, moths, flies, and ants, will all be forever freed from all the hells and evil destinies and be reborn in the celestial palaces, always remembering past lives until attaining non-retrogression."

### Entrustment

Then the Buddha told Sarva-nīvaraṇa-viṣkambhin Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva, Vajrapāṇi, the Four Kings, Śakra, Brahmā, and their retinues, Nārāyaṇa, Maheśvara, and the others: "Good sons! I entrust this mantra-dharma king to you all. You should guard, maintain, and protect it. Bear it on your shoulders in a jeweled casket. In future times, do not let it be cut off. You should well uphold and well protect it, and bestow it upon all sentient beings of future generations, enabling them to hear and see it and be freed from the five heinous sins."

Then Sarva-nīvaraṇa-viṣkambhin Bodhisattva, Vajrapāṇi, the Four Kings, Śakra, Brahmā, Nārāyaṇa, Maheśvara, and the devas, nāgas, and eightfold assembly all prostrated at the Buddha's feet and spoke in unison: "We have already received the World-Honored One's blessing and have been entrusted with this mantra dharma and the dharma of constructing stūpas. We shall all guard, maintain, recite, write, and make offerings to it, for the protection of all sentient beings. In future times, we shall cause all sentient beings to hear and know it, so they will not fall into the hells or evil destinies. To repay the Tathāgata's great kindness, we shall all guard and protect it, causing it to circulate broadly, honoring and revering it as the Buddha himself, and we shall not allow this dharma to be destroyed."

The Buddha said: "Excellent! Excellent! You are able to firmly guard, maintain, and protect this dhāraṇī dharma." When the great assembly heard the Buddha's teaching, they joyfully accepted and put it into practice.
