# 入法界體性經
## Sūtra on Entering the Essential Nature of the Dharma-Realm

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0355

Translated from the Chinese. Sui dynasty, translated by Jñānagupta (闍那崛多)

---

At that time the Bhagavān was on Gṛdhrakūṭa Mountain in the city of Rājagṛha, together with a great assembly of five hundred bhikṣus. At that time, the youth Mañjuśrī, during the first watch of the night, came to where the Buddha was. Having arrived, he stood at a side door of the Buddha's quarters. At that time the Tathāgata was abiding in samādhi. Then the World-Honored One arose from samādhi, saw the youth Mañjuśrī standing outside the side door, and upon seeing him said: "Mañjuśrī! Come, come! Enter within and do not remain outside."

At that time the youth Mañjuśrī, having heard the Buddha's instruction, said to the Buddha: "Excellent, World-Honored One!" He then went to where the Buddha was. Having arrived, he bowed his head at the Buddha's feet and withdrew to stand to one side.

At that time the World-Honored One told the youth Mañjuśrī: "You may take a seat." Then the youth Mañjuśrī said: "Excellent, World-Honored One! I accept your instruction." He pressed his palms together toward the Buddha, withdrew, and sat to one side.

Then the youth Mañjuśrī said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One, in what samādhi was the World-Honored One abiding just now, from which he arose?"

The Buddha told Mañjuśrī: "There is a samādhi called Ratnakūṭa. I was practicing this samādhi at that time, and have now arisen from it."

Mañjuśrī again said to the Buddha: "For what cause and condition is this samādhi called Ratnakūṭa?"

The Buddha told Mañjuśrī: "It is like a great maṇi jewel that has been well polished and placed in a clean spot: wherever it is situated, it produces inexhaustible precious gems. In the same way, Mañjuśrī, when I abide in this samādhi, gazing toward the east, I see the presently existing buddhas, tathāgatas, arhats, samyaksaṃbuddhas in immeasurable asaṃkhyeya worlds. Likewise in the south, west, and north, in the four intermediate directions, above and below, in all ten directions of immeasurable asaṃkhyeya worlds, I see them all. These tathāgatas, abiding in this samādhi, teach the Dharma for the assembly. Mañjuśrī, when I abide in this samādhi, I do not see a single dharma that is not the dharma-realm. Mañjuśrī, furthermore, this samādhi is also called the Seal of Ultimate Reality. If any sincere man or woman practices this seal, their eloquence will be unceasing."

Mañjuśrī said: "World-Honored One, I have eloquence! Sugata, I know eloquence."

The Buddha said: "Mañjuśrī, how do you know eloquence?"

Mañjuśrī said: "World-Honored One, it is like that maṇi jewel, which does not rest upon anything else but rests upon its own jewel-ground. In the same way, World-Honored One, all dharmas have no other ground upon which they rest; they rest only upon ultimate reality."

The Buddha further told Mañjuśrī: "Do you know ultimate reality?"

Mañjuśrī said: "Yes, World-Honored One, I know ultimate reality."

The Buddha said: "Mañjuśrī, what is ultimate reality?"

Mañjuśrī said: "World-Honored One, the realm of self is precisely ultimate reality. The realm of ordinary beings is precisely ultimate reality. All karma and all fruition: all dharmas are entirely ultimate reality. World-Honored One, whoever has faith in this manner has true faith. World-Honored One, whoever has faith in what is inverted has right faith. What is practice and what is non-practice: these are both right practice. Why? 'Right' and 'not right' are merely verbal designations and cannot be grasped."

The Buddha said: "Mañjuśrī, what is the meaning of 'practice'?"

Mañjuśrī said: "World-Honored One, the meaning of practice is seeing ultimate reality."

The Buddha said: "Mañjuśrī, what is the meaning of 'cultivating the path'?"

Mañjuśrī said: "World-Honored One, cultivating the path means contemplating and realizing."

The Buddha said: "Mañjuśrī, how do you teach the Dharma to men and women who are beginning practitioners?"

Mañjuśrī said: "World-Honored One, for those good men and good women, I teach them to give rise to the view of self; this itself is teaching them the Dharma. World-Honored One, I do not extinguish desire and its afflictions in order to teach the Dharma. Why? Because these dharmas are by their original nature unborn and unperishing. World-Honored One, if one could extinguish ultimate reality, then one could extinguish the realm produced by the view of self. World-Honored One, I teach the Dharma to beginning good men and good women in this way: do not accept the Buddha's dharma, do not cling to the dharma of ordinary beings, and in regard to all dharmas neither take up nor discard. World-Honored One, for men and women who have first given rise to the aspiration, I should teach the Dharma in this manner."

Mañjuśrī said: "World-Honored One, when you yourself teach and transform sentient beings, how do you teach the Dharma?"

The Buddha said: "Mañjuśrī, I teach the Dharma without destroying form's arising, and also without destroying form's non-arising. Likewise with feeling, perception, volition, and consciousness: I teach the Dharma without destroying their non-arising. Mañjuśrī, I teach the Dharma without destroying desire, anger, and delusion. Mañjuśrī, for those I teach and transform, I enable them to know the inconceivable Dharma; this is how I teach the Dharma. Because of this, I have realized anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi. Mañjuśrī, without destroying any dharma, I have attained supreme awakening, and also without anything arising, I have attained supreme awakening. Mañjuśrī, what is called 'Buddha' is precisely the dharma-realm. The powers and fearlessnesses are also the dharma-realm. Mañjuśrī, I do not see any divisions within the dharma-realm. I do not see within the dharma-realm: 'This is the dharma of ordinary beings, this is the dharma of arhats, this is the dharma of pratyekabuddhas, this is the dharma of buddhas.' The dharma-realm has no superiority or difference, and no destruction or disorder. Mañjuśrī, it is like the Gaṅgā, the Yamunā, the Sarabū, and other such great rivers: when they enter the great ocean, their waters cannot be distinguished. In the same way, Mañjuśrī, when all the various dharmas with their many names enter the dharma-realm, there are no distinctions of name. Mañjuśrī, it is like a heap of various grains in which no individual grain can be singled out. In the dharma-realm there are likewise no distinct names of 'this' or 'that,' 'defiled' or 'pure.' 'Ordinary being,' 'sage,' and the dharma of buddhas: such names cannot be pointed to. The dharma-realm as I now teach it has no opposition and should be accepted with joy. Why? Mañjuśrī, because the realm of opposition and compliance and the dharma-realm are nondual, without coming or going, invisible, and without any place of arising."

When the Buddha had taught this Dharma, Mañjuśrī again said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One, I too do not see the dharma-realm heading toward the evil paths, nor do I see it heading toward the paths of humans and devas, nor heading toward nirvāṇa."

The Buddha further told Mañjuśrī: "If someone came and asked you, 'How is it that the six destinies presently exist?' What would you answer to such a question?"

Mañjuśrī said: "World-Honored One, to such a question I would offer this explanation. World-Honored One, it is like a person sleeping and dreaming, who might see the hell realm, or see the animal realm, or see the realm of Yama's people, or see an asura body, or see a heavenly place, or see human beings. World-Honored One, the dream experiences that person sees are various distinct destinies. If someone asks, one explains according to what was seen, but in reality there are no such sentient beings. In the same way, World-Honored One, although I speak of the various destinies as distinct, the dharma-realm truly has no such distinctions. World-Honored One, to such a questioner, I would explain truly, because neither this nor that exists. World-Honored One, for those who pursue the śrāvaka vehicle and grasp at nirvāṇa, one cannot teach them the true meaning. World-Honored One, even now in the present, one cannot make distinctions for them; one can only speak in terms of names. Why? Because they grasp at the boundaries of the dharma-realm. World-Honored One, it is like the great ocean in which there are seven kinds of treasures: shells, jade, coral, gold, silver, living creatures, and colored things, which can be distinguished from one another: 'This is a treasure.' But within the dharma-realm, one cannot discern any distinct characteristics. Why? World-Honored One, the dharma-realm is unborn and unperishing. The dharma-realm is undefiled and unpurified. The dharma-realm is without turbidity and without disorder. Within the dharma-realm there is nothing to be extinguished and nothing to arise."

At that time the World-Honored One, knowing the answer yet deliberately asking, said to Mañjuśrī: "Do you know the dharma-realm?"

"Yes, World-Honored One. I know the dharma-realm to be precisely my own realm."

The Buddha further asked Mañjuśrī: "Do you know the worldly realm?"

Mañjuśrī said: "World-Honored One, the place where an illusory person acts is the worldly place. World-Honored One, 'the world' is merely a name; there is no real thing to be seen. One speaks of 'worldly conduct.' World-Honored One, yet I do not see the world apart from the dharma-realm. Why? Because there is no world. If the World-Honored One asks, 'Where does the world operate?' the answer is: the nature of form is unborn and unperishing, and that operation is also unborn and unperishing. Likewise with feeling, perception, volition, and consciousness: the nature of consciousness is unborn and unperishing, and its operation is also without arising and without perishing. World-Honored One, in this way there is one characteristic, namely, the characteristic of no-characteristic."

The Buddha further asked: "Mañjuśrī, do you not think this thought: 'The presently existing Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha will enter parinirvāṇa'?"

Mañjuśrī answered: "World-Honored One, can the dharma-realm have been cultivated or not yet cultivated? Since the dharma-realm is without cultivation, how could there be cessation and non-appearing?"

The Buddha said: "Mañjuśrī, what do you think? The buddhas of the past, as numerous as the sands of the Gaṅgā, have entered parinirvāṇa. Do you not believe this?"

Mañjuśrī said: "World-Honored One, I do believe that all the tathāgatas have entered nirvāṇa, because I see the place from which they arose."

The Buddha said: "Mañjuśrī, what do you think? Do you say that ordinary beings die and are then reborn?"

Mañjuśrī said: "World-Honored One, I do not even see that ordinary beings exist; how could there be rebirth?"

The Buddha asked Mañjuśrī: "Do you delight in hearing the Dharma in the Buddha's presence?"

Mañjuśrī said: "World-Honored One, I do not see any characteristic of delight or non-delight."

The Buddha said: "Mañjuśrī, do you not delight in the dharma-realm?"

Mañjuśrī answered: "World-Honored One, I do not see a single dharma that is not the dharma-realm. What would there be to additionally delight in?"

The Buddha said: "Mañjuśrī, if the conceited were to hear your words, they would be greatly frightened."

Mañjuśrī said: "World-Honored One, if the conceited are frightened, then ultimate reality too would be frightened. Since ultimate reality has no fright, all dharmas are without fright, because there is nothing fabricated. This is a vajra statement."

The Buddha said: "Mañjuśrī, why is this called a vajra statement?"

Mañjuśrī said: "World-Honored One, the nature of all dharmas is indestructible; therefore it is called a vajra statement. World-Honored One, the inconceivable statement of the Tathāgata, which is the inconceivability of all dharmas, is a vajra statement."

The Buddha said: "Mañjuśrī, why again is this called a vajra statement?"

Mañjuśrī said: "World-Honored One, because all dharmas are without conceptual thought, this is a vajra statement. World-Honored One, all dharmas are bodhi; this is a vajra statement."

The Buddha said: "Mañjuśrī, why again is this called a vajra statement?"

Mañjuśrī said: "World-Honored One, all dharmas are without any substantial existence; there are only names and verbal designations. In all dharmas there is no 'this' or 'that'; all are without any substantial existence. That which is without this-or-that is suchness. What is suchness is truth. What is truth is bodhi. Therefore it is called a vajra statement."

Mañjuśrī said: "World-Honored One, all dharmas are the realm of the Tathāgata; this is a vajra statement."

The Buddha said: "Mañjuśrī, why is this called a vajra statement?"

Mañjuśrī said: "World-Honored One, the inherent nature of all dharmas is originally quiescent; therefore it is a vajra statement."

The Buddha told Mañjuśrī: "You should summon the bhikṣu Ānanda to come and receive and uphold this dharma text and its sentences."

Mañjuśrī said: "World-Honored One, within this I do not see a single dharma that can be spoken or heard. World-Honored One, I truly do not see a single syllable that has a place where it is spoken. How could there be many sentences to be upheld?"

The Buddha said: "Excellent, excellent! Mañjuśrī, you have spoken well. Mañjuśrī, I see that in the east, in immeasurable asaṃkhyeya worlds, the buddhas, tathāgatas, arhats, samyaksaṃbuddhas also teach this dharma text."

At that time the elder Śāriputra came out from his own dwelling and went to the place where the youth Mañjuśrī was staying. Having arrived, he did not see Mañjuśrī, so he went to where the Buddha was. Having arrived, he stood outside the Buddha's side door.

At that time the World-Honored One told the youth Mañjuśrī: "Mañjuśrī, the bhikṣu Śāriputra is now outside the door, wishing to hear the Dharma. Have him come in."

Mañjuśrī said: "World-Honored One, if the realm of Śāriputra and the realm of the dharma-realm: World-Honored One, do these two realms have an inside, an outside, or an in-between?"

The Buddha said: "No."

Mañjuśrī said: "World-Honored One, what is called 'ultimate reality' is also not ultimate reality. Such a realm and non-realm have no inside or outside, no coming or going. World-Honored One, the realm of the elder Śāriputra is precisely ultimate reality. The realm of Śāriputra is precisely the dharma-realm. World-Honored One, yet this dharma-realm has no going out or entering, no coming or going. From where has the elder Śāriputra come, and into what place should he enter?"

The Buddha said: "Mañjuśrī, if I were inside, conversing with the śrāvakas, and you were outside and not allowed to enter, would you not give rise to a thought of distress?"

Mañjuśrī said: "No, World-Honored One. Why? World-Honored One, whatever Dharma is taught does not depart from the dharma-realm. The Tathāgata's teaching of the Dharma is the dharma-realm. The dharma-realm is the Tathāgata. Teaching, the dharma-realm, and suchness: the dharma-realm and the realm of verbal expression are nondual and nonexistent. Those who name and those who speak: none of these depart from the dharma-realm. World-Honored One, for this reason, I have no distress. World-Honored One, even if for kalpas as numerous as the sands of the Gaṅgā I did not come to where the World-Honored One teaches the Dharma, at that time I would not give rise to longing, nor would I have distress. Why? If there were duality, then there would be distress. Since the dharma-realm is nondual, there is no distress."

At that time the World-Honored One told the elder Śāriputra: "Śāriputra, have you come to listen to the eloquence of Mañjuśrī?"

Śāriputra said: "Indeed, World-Honored One. I very much wish to listen. I am now outside the room, wishing to hear what the World-Honored One and the youth Mañjuśrī have been discussing."

At that time Mañjuśrī said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One, please allow the elder Śāriputra to enter and hear the Dharma."

At that time the World-Honored One told the elder Śāriputra: "Śāriputra, come forward and enter."

Śāriputra said: "Excellent, World-Honored One!" He then came forward, entered the room, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, withdrew, and sat to one side.

At that time Mañjuśrī said: "Elder Śāriputra, for what purpose have you come here?"

Śāriputra said: "Mañjuśrī, I have come here wishing to hear the Dharma. Here there should be the most supreme Dharma-meaning, because Mañjuśrī and the World-Honored One are together, each discussing and discoursing, and there must be something marvelous, something profoundly supreme in Dharma-meaning."

Then Mañjuśrī said: "So it is, so it is. Śāriputra, I teach the profound and supreme Dharma."

Śāriputra said: "Mañjuśrī, in what sense is this Dharma-teaching profound and supreme?"

Mañjuśrī said: "Śāriputra, this Dharma is difficult to know, because there is no vessel for it. Whatever is taught has no point of arising. This Dharma-teaching is not for the purpose of arousing the dharma of ordinary beings, nor for the purpose of arousing the dharma of arhats. It is the Tathāgata's Dharma that arouses this teaching. Because it has nothing to depend upon and nothing that depends upon it, this Dharma-teaching arises. Therefore the Dharma-teaching is equal and even, without any place of abiding. Because it teaches that all dharmas are ultimately quiescent, it has no abiding; therefore it is called supreme."

Śāriputra said: "Mañjuśrī, for what reason do you say that arhats whose outflows are exhausted are not vessels for this Dharma?"

Mañjuśrī said: "Elder Śāriputra, the arhat has only exhausted the coarse delusions of desire, anger, and delusion. How could such a one serve as a vessel? Śāriputra, for this reason I say that arhats whose outflows are exhausted are not vessels for this Dharma."

Śāriputra said: "Mañjuśrī, for this very reason I now seek you out, going from one dwelling place to another, from room to room, from cave to cave. I specifically seek you out because you are a place of Dharma-joy, and I wish to hear your eloquence. Mañjuśrī, I listen to the World-Honored One and to you teach the Dharma without ever being sated."

Then Mañjuśrī said: "Venerable Śāriputra, are you not content with hearing the Dharma?"

Śāriputra said: "Mañjuśrī, I am never weary of hearing the Dharma."

Mañjuśrī said: "Venerable Śāriputra, can the dharma-realm grasp the teaching of the Dharma?"

Śāriputra said: "No."

Mañjuśrī said: "Venerable Śāriputra, since you are never weary of hearing the Dharma, and since the dharma-realm and the venerable one's realm are nondual and without distinction, and the dharma-realm does not grasp the teaching of the Dharma: if it grasped, then there could be contentment. Since it does not grasp, there is no contentment."

Śāriputra said: "Mañjuśrī, apart from the tathāgatas, who hears the Dharma in such a way?"

Mañjuśrī said: "Venerable Śāriputra, do you say that the dharma of nirvāṇa is Śāriputra?"

Śāriputra said: "Mañjuśrī, I have faith."

Mañjuśrī said: "How do you have faith?"

Śāriputra said: "Because the original nature of all dharmas is already accomplished, I have no nirvāṇa."

Mañjuśrī further asked: "Śāriputra, do you believe in the dharma of no-death?"

Śāriputra said: "Mañjuśrī, I have faith."

Mañjuśrī said: "How do you have faith?"

Śāriputra said: "The dharma-realm neither dies nor is born. I have faith in this."

Mañjuśrī further asked: "Venerable Śāriputra, do you believe that without knowledge, one is fully an arhat whose outflows are exhausted?"

Śāriputra said: "I have faith."

Mañjuśrī said: "How do you have faith?"

Śāriputra said: "Because non-knowledge and knowledge are equal, one is fully an arhat whose outflows are exhausted. Why? It is not only knowledge that is free from non-knowledge; non-knowledge too is free from non-knowledge. Beyond the dharma of cessation there is no further knowledge. Without discrimination, free from knowledge: this is an arhat whose outflows are exhausted."

Mañjuśrī asked the Venerable Śāriputra: "Do you believe that the arhat whose outflows are exhausted has the dharma of liberation?"

Śāriputra said: "Mañjuśrī, I truly have faith."

Mañjuśrī said: "How do you have faith?"

Śāriputra said: "Those dharmas are separate from all dharmas, yet they do not grasp at any dharma. I have faith in this."

Mañjuśrī asked the Venerable Śāriputra: "Do you believe that the former tathāgatas, arhats, samyaksaṃbuddhas entered extinction yet did not attain nirvāṇa?"

Śāriputra said: "I have faith."

Mañjuśrī further asked: "How do you have faith?"

Śāriputra said: "Mañjuśrī, that inconceivable realm is without birth and without passing away. I have faith in this."

Mañjuśrī asked the Venerable Śāriputra: "Do you believe that all buddhas are one buddha?"

Śāriputra said: "I have faith."

Mañjuśrī said: "How do you have faith?"

Śāriputra said: "Mañjuśrī, the dharma-realm cannot be divided. I have faith in this."

Mañjuśrī asked the Venerable Śāriputra: "Do you believe that all buddha-lands are one buddha-land?"

Śāriputra said: "I have faith."

Mañjuśrī further asked: "How do you have faith?"

Śāriputra said: "Mañjuśrī, all buddha-lands rest upon suchness without end, and the lands too are without end. I have faith in this."

Mañjuśrī asked Śāriputra: "Do you believe that all dharmas cannot be realized, cannot be extinguished, cannot be thought upon, and cannot be fabricated?"

Śāriputra said: "Mañjuśrī, I have faith."

Mañjuśrī said: "How do you have faith?"

Śāriputra said: "Mañjuśrī, the self-nature does not know self-nature. The original nature does not abandon original nature. Self-nature also does not realize, nor does it have thought. It does not mutually contradict. It is unborn and unperishing. It neither grasps nor discards. It abides well upon that ground. I have faith in this."

Mañjuśrī asked: "Śāriputra, do you believe that in the conditioned realm, within the dharma-realm, no dharma arises, no dharma perishes, and no dharma accumulates?"

Śāriputra said: "I have faith."

Mañjuśrī further asked: "How do you have faith?"

Śāriputra said: "Mañjuśrī, the nature of those dharmas cannot be known as either arising or perishing or abiding in accumulation. I have faith in this."

Mañjuśrī said: "Venerable Śāriputra, do you believe that within the prajñā dharma-realm there is also the designation 'arhat'?"

Śāriputra said: "I have faith."

Mañjuśrī said: "How do you have faith?"

Śāriputra said: "Mañjuśrī, weary practice in the prajñā dharma-realm is the arhat's realm. Yet the essential nature of the dharma-realm is not the substance of desire, anger, and delusion. How could the arhat depart from the dharma-realm? I have faith in this."

Mañjuśrī said: "Venerable Śāriputra, do you believe that all dharmas are the realm of the Buddha's acceptance?"

Śāriputra said: "I truly have faith."

Mañjuśrī asked: "How do you have faith?"

Śāriputra said: "Mañjuśrī, the World-Honored One's original nature is awakened and his self-nature is free. I have faith in this."

At that time Mañjuśrī said: "Excellent, excellent! Venerable Śāriputra, according to your own realm of experience, you have explained for me. I asked in this way, and you answered in this way. Therefore I know that you possess this measure of practice."

At that time the World-Honored One told the elder Śāriputra: "Śāriputra, if any good men or good women receive, uphold, and keep this dharma text and its sentences, or explain it for others, or read or recite it, those people will swiftly attain eloquence."

Śāriputra said: "So it is, Bhagavān! So it is, Sugata! It is as the greatly virtuous World-Honored One has spoken. World-Honored One, those beings in former lives had already made offerings to the buddha world-honored ones. Those good men and good women were already established in this dharma-seal, and those beings shall attain great awakening."

At that time the elder Śāriputra said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One, what is the name of this dharma text? How should we receive and uphold it?"

The Buddha told the elder Śāriputra: "Śāriputra, this dharma text of mine is called 'The Questions of the Youth Mañjuśrī, as Explained by the Buddha.' Receive and uphold it thus. It is also called 'Entering the Dharma-Realm.' Receive and uphold it thus. It is also called 'Ultimate Reality.' Receive and uphold it thus. Śāriputra, those good men and good women should venerate it as a supreme treasure. If they receive, uphold, read, recite, and contemplate this dharma text and practice accordingly, they shall attain the acceptance of the non-arising of dharmas. If, for the purpose of producing good roots in others, they read even a small portion, they will be able to expound the Dharma-meaning extensively for others, and they shall attain unceasing eloquence."

When the Buddha taught this sūtra, the youth Mañjuśrī, the other bodhisattva-mahāsattvas, the senior Śāriputra, the other bhikṣus, and the assembly of devas, gandharvas, humans, asuras, and others, hearing what the Buddha had taught, all greatly rejoiced.
