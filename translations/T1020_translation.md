# 大方廣佛花嚴經入法界品頓證毘盧遮那法身字輪瑜伽儀軌
## Yoga Ritual for the Sudden Realization of Vairocana's Dharma-Body Letter-Wheel from the Chapter on Entering the Dharmadhātu of the Avataṃsaka Sūtra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1020

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空)

---

For those who wish to suddenly enter the one vehicle and cultivate the contemplation of the Dharma-body of Vairocana Tathāgata, one should first arouse the wondrous practices and vows of Bodhisattva Samantabhadra, and then empower body and mind with the three mysteries, whereby one will be able to awaken and enter the great ocean of wisdom of Mañjuśrī.

The practitioner should, first of all, in a quiet and secluded place, collect the thoughts and settle the mind, close the eyes, straighten the body, and sit in the full lotus posture. Extend the mind to universally embrace the boundless ocean of Buddha-fields, and carefully contemplate all the Tathāgatas of the three times, present before each and every Buddha and bodhisattva, reverently and earnestly performing prostrations and circumambulations; then offer cloud-oceans of various offerings to all these holy ones.

Having made these vast and great offerings, one should next contemplate one's own mind: the mind is fundamentally unborn; its own nature is accomplished; its radiance pervades everywhere, like empty space. One should then deeply arouse compassionate thought, taking pity on sentient beings who do not awaken to their own minds and who revolve through the various realms of saṃsāra, [resolving:] "I shall universally transform and rescue them, enabling all of them to awaken, leaving none behind."

One should further contemplate that one's own mind, the minds of all sentient beings, and the minds of all Buddhas are fundamentally without difference; equal and of one nature; forming the great mind of bodhi: luminously penetrating, pure and cool, vast and all-pervading, perfectly radiant and immaculate, forming a great moon-disk, its measure equal to empty space, without boundary or limit.

Next, within the moon-disk, one should arrange the forty-two Sanskrit letters in a clockwise direction, all of golden color, radiating great light, illuminating and penetrating the ten directions, clearly and distinctly manifesting. Within each ray of light, one sees boundless oceans of Buddha-fields with boundless Buddhas, surrounded before and behind by boundless assemblies, seated at the place of enlightenment, having attained supreme and perfect awakening, their wisdom penetrating the three times, their bodies pervading the ten directions, turning the great Dharma-wheel, liberating all classes of beings, enabling all to directly realize non-abiding nirvāṇa.

One should further awaken and enter the forty-two syllable-gates of the Perfection of Wisdom, understanding that in all phenomena there is nothing whatsoever to be attained, and that the correct wisdom that observes and the dharmadhātu that is observed are entirely equal, without difference or distinction. If the yoga practitioner is able to harmonize with the contemplative practice of this revolving dhāraṇī, they will directly realize the wisdom-body of Vairocana Tathāgata and attain unobstructed freedom throughout all phenomena.

## The Perfectly Radiant Letter-Wheel

The verse of the forty-two letters states:

> *a ra pa ca na la da ma ṇa ṣa va ta ya ṣṭha ka sa ma ga tha ja śva dha śa kha kṣa sa ta ña ra tha bha cha sma hva ta sa ga ṭha ṅa pha sa ka ya sa śca ṭha ḍha*
