# 佛說八正道經
## Sūtra on the Eightfold Right Path Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0112

Translated from the Chinese. Later Han dynasty, translated by An Shigao (安世高)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the country of Śrāvastī, in the Jetavana, the garden of Anāthapiṇḍada. The Buddha told his disciples: "Listen as I teach both the wrong path and the right path. What is the wrong path? Wrong view, wrong intention, wrong speech, wrong effort, wrong livelihood, wrong action, wrong mindfulness, wrong concentration. These are the eight wrong practices of the path.

"What are the eight right practices of the path?

"First, right view. What is right view? Having faith in giving, faith in worship, faith in offerings, faith that good and evil actions naturally bring their results, faith in parents, faith in the world's practitioners of the path, faith in seeking the path, faith in right practice, faith in right conduct. In this life and the next, one attains wisdom, gains realization, and achieves completion. Then one tells others: this is right view.

"Second, right intention. What is right intention? The mind's resolve to abandon desire, abandon the household life, not to be angry or wrathful, and not to harm others. This is right intention.

"Third, right speech. What is right speech? Not speaking divisively, not gossiping, not reviling, not speaking falsely. This is right speech.

"Fourth, right action. What is right action? Not killing, not stealing, not engaging in sexual misconduct. This is right action.

"Fifth, right livelihood. What is right livelihood? Here a disciple of the path who has heard the Dharma seeks what is proper and not improper: food, bedding, and medicine for illness are to be sought through proper means and not through improper means. This is right livelihood.

"Sixth, right effort. What is right effort? The mind of birth and death joins together with practice. One makes vigorous effort, exerts strength through causes and conditions, and strives with effort without weariness, the mind holding firm. This is right effort.

"Seventh, right mindfulness. What is right mindfulness? The mind of birth and death joins together with mindful contemplation, directed mindfulness, without delusion, and without seeking with a distracted mind. This is called right mindfulness.

"Eighth, right concentration. What is right concentration? The mind of birth and death joins together with mindful cessation, mutual cessation, guarded cessation, and gathered cessation. Not doing, not creating any offense, not falling into the middle court. This is called right concentration.

"Bhikṣus! All disciples of the path should receive and practice these eight right practices of the path. By practicing as taught, one can attain the awakening of the eight practices. Right view means having faith in giving: in the next life one obtains abundant merit. Faith in worship means seeing śramaṇas and practitioners of the path and gaining merit through paying respect. Faith in offerings means hanging silk banners, burning incense, scattering flowers, and lighting lamps. Faith that practicing the ten wholesome deeds naturally brings merit. Faith in parents means faith in filial piety. Faith in the world's practitioners of the path means joyfully receiving the sūtras. Faith in seeking the path means practicing the path. Faith in right practice means cutting off evil intention. Faith in right livelihood means not violating the precepts.

"In this life and the next, one's own wisdom leads to attaining wisdom and being able to teach others to gain realization. Having achieved completion oneself, one is able to bring others to completion. Being able to bring others to completion, one then tells them. This is called the knowledge of right view. Thus one liberates oneself and also liberates others.

"Second, right intention: what arises in the mind is loss of mindfulness. Wishing to abandon the household is contemplation of the path. Not being angry or wrathful is forbearance. Not harming others is right intention.

"Third, right speech: not reviling and not committing the four offenses of the mouth, but only speaking of sincerity and the essential of the factors of the path.

"Fourth, right action: not killing, not stealing, not engaging in sexual misconduct, but practicing sincerity and trustworthiness.

"Fifth, right livelihood and not falling into greed: seeking only one robe and one meal, being a humble physician.

"Sixth, right effort: directing oneself toward the thirty-seven factors of awakening.

"Seventh, right mindfulness: daily increasing the thirty-seven factors of awakening, the mind not departing from them.

"Eighth, right concentration means not forgetting causes and conditions. Cessation means constantly returning the mind to protection. Having ceased means not committing any offense whatsoever. Gathered cessation means attaining the merit of the path."

The Buddha spoke thus. All joyfully received it.
