# 頂輪王大曼荼羅灌頂儀軌
## Ritual of the Great Maṇḍala Consecration of the Uṣṇīṣa-cakravartin

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0959

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty

---

## The Great Maṇḍala Consecration Ritual of the Uṣṇīṣa-cakravartin

**Inner Court: the Samaya Boundary Path and the Central Platform Court.** In the center, paint the World-Honored Buddha seated upon a lion-throne adorned with various treasures. The Holy One sits upon a white lotus, his body golden-white in color, entering the samādhi of the Most Victorious King. He is seated in full cross-legged posture, in the aspect of teaching the Dharma. From his entire body there universally emanate twelve golden wheels of blazing radiance, surrounding him like halos. From his crown flow forth various rays of light: blue, yellow, red, and white. He possesses the marks of a great man and leans against the bodhi tree. The tree has various leaves, like cintāmaṇi jewels. From the top of the tree hang various silken banners, or vaiḍūrya jewels, celestial fruits, and exquisite garments, or clouds descending as rain. There are various sprouts, and auspicious birds all sit upon them. Among the branches, various jeweled leaves have formed a kalpa-tree. The World-Honored One's two shoulders lean back against this tree. To the Buddha's right, below the seat, paint the form of a cakravartin seated upon a white lotus, in the posture of contemplating the Buddha. His body is golden, surrounded by radiance on all sides, accomplished with the seven treasures. The wheel-treasure alone has a halo of light encircling it upon the lotus. The remaining six treasures are arranged in sequence.

Southeast corner: Destroyer Buddha-Uṣṇīṣa. South: Victorious Buddha-Uṣṇīṣa. Southwest corner: Boundless Sound Buddha-Uṣṇīṣa. West: White Parasol Buddha-Uṣṇīṣa. Northwest corner: Vast and Lofty Buddha-Uṣṇīṣa. North: Light-Gathering Buddha-Uṣṇīṣa. Northeast corner: Most Victorious Buddha-Uṣṇīṣa. Due east: Exalted Buddha-Uṣṇīṣa.

The above is the central court.

**Second Court: Single-Pronged Vajra Boundary Path.**

Southeast corner: Dharma-Pāramitā Bodhisattva. Then: Mahāsthāmaprāpta, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, Parṇaśabarī Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, Tārā Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, Bhṛkuṭī Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, Pāṇḍaravāsinī Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, Avalokiteśvara-rāja Bodhisattva. Southwest corner: Karma-Pāramitā Bodhisattva. Then: Dhyāna-Pāramitā Bodhisattva, Kṣānti-Pāramitā Bodhisattva, Dāna-Pāramitā Bodhisattva, Ākāśa-Vidyā Consort, Śīla-Pāramitā Bodhisattva, Vīrya-Pāramitā Bodhisattva, Prajñā-Pāramitā Bodhisattva. Northwest corner: Vajra-Pāramitā Bodhisattva. Then: Trailokyavijaya Vajra Bodhisattva, Vajra-General Consort, Vajra-General Youth, Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva, Vajra-Mother Bodhisattva, Subāhu Youth Bodhisattva, Carya-Mūrdhaja Youth Bodhisattva (same as the previous). Northeast corner: Ratna-Pāramitā Bodhisattva. Then: Prajñā-Pāramitā Bodhisattva, Maitreya Bodhisattva, Buddha-Compassion Bodhisattva, Buddha-Eye Bodhisattva, Buddha-Ūrṇā Bodhisattva, Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva, Cintāmaṇi Bodhisattva.

The above is the second court.

**Third Court: same boundary path as the second.**

Southeast corner: Song-Offering Bodhisattva. Then: Cintāmaṇi-cakra Bodhisattva, Newly-Resolved Dharma-Wheel Bodhisattva, Eight Holy Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, Lotus Auspicious Bodhisattva, Lotus Sundarī Bodhisattva, Nīlakaṇṭha Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, Lotus-Crest Bodhisattva, Lotus Yakṣa Bodhisattva, Eleven-Faced Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva. Southwest corner: Dance-Offering Bodhisattva. Then: Vārika Nāga King, Vārika Seven-Headed, Ānanda Nāga King, Nirmalā River Goddess, Pṛthivī Offering, Aparājitā Bodhisattva, Aparājitā Consort, Anavatapta Nāga King, Mucilinda Nāga King, Nāga Princess. Northwest corner: Joy-Offering Bodhisattva. Then: Ucchuṣma Vajra, Vajragarbha Bodhisattva, Amṛta-Kuṇḍalī Bodhisattva, Vajra-Sundarī Bodhisattva, Vajra-Love Bodhisattva, Vajra-Fist Bodhisattva, Abhayaṃdada Bodhisattva, Pāṇa-Śamarī Bodhisattva, Vajra-Needle Bodhisattva. Northeast corner: Garland-Offering Bodhisattva. Then: Sadāprarudita Bodhisattva, Dhāraṇī-Sovereign Bodhisattva, Māmakī Bodhisattva, Mahāmati Bodhisattva, Śānta Bodhisattva, Vimalamati Bodhisattva, Gagana-Vimala Bodhisattva, Sāgaramati Bodhisattva, Bhadrapāla Bodhisattva.

The above is the third court.

**Fourth Court outside the Vajra Boundary: Duty-Fulfilling Devas, etc., and outer offerings in sequence.** From the southeast corner: Lamp-Offering Bodhisattva. Then: Agni's Consort, Yama-rāja. At the gate: Vajra-Bell Bodhisattva. Then: Yama's Consort, Rākṣasa Lord. Southwest corner: Unguent Bodhisattva. Then: Rākṣasa Lord's Consort, Vaiśravaṇa. At the gate: Vajra-Hook Bodhisattva. Then: Vaiśravaṇa's Consort, Vāyu Lord. Northwest corner: Incense-Offering Bodhisattva. Then: Vāyu's Consort, Varuṇa-rāja. At the gate: Vajra-Lasso Bodhisattva. Then: Varuṇa's Consort, Īśāna. Northeast corner: Flower-Offering Bodhisattva. Then: Īśāna's Consort, Indra. At the gate: Vajra-Chain Bodhisattva. Then: Indra's Consort, Agni Lord.

The above is the fourth court.

**Fifth Court:** the food-offering path. At the four corners, paint five-pronged vajra implements, flaming garlands of awesome aspect.

The above maṇḍala of the Cakravartin Great Consecration Ritual, upper altar holy ones, totals one hundred and twenty-two deities.

When the ācārya confers consecration upon a disciple, beside the great maṇḍala, facing the holy assembly, a separate maṇḍala should be prepared with white powder, measured in cubits, and a lotus should be drawn throughout the center. Upon the central platform, paint a lion-throne and have the disciple who has completed the flower-casting sit upon it. Have one Dharma-skilled disciple hold a parasol and whisk, chanting auspicious praises slowly and without interruption. Take the water from the central vase, empowered with one hundred and eight recitations. Instruct the disciple to form the Buddha-Uṣṇīṣa Seal and place it upon the crown. Then blow the conch, beat the drum, and play various kinds of music.

> I now teach according to the sūtra the method for painting the Cakravartin image.
> Those versed in painting, possessing all the skills, may perform the rite accordingly.
> As the sūtra broadly describes, use white cotton cloth, properly squared,
> Five cubits or three cubits in size.

For painting the image of the Holy One: first, from the great ocean there rises Mount Sumeru, composed of four kinds of jewels. Upon the mountain, seated on a white lotus, the body is golden-white, entering the samādhi of the Most Victorious King in right concentration, in full cross-legged posture. From his entire body there universally flow forth twelve golden wheels of blazing radiance. Above, one should paint a mountain peak composed of various kinds of jewels. From the Holy One's crown flow forth various rays of light: blue, yellow, red, and white, revolving freely. To the Holy One's right, paint the recitation practitioner holding an incense burner, gazing upon the Tathāgata's face. Below, paint a lotus pool. Paint two great vidyārājas, their forms as described below. The first, Hayagrīva Great Vidyārāja: his body is the color of fire, wrathful in form, with a nose like an ape. He is adorned with serpents as necklaces, armlets, and shoulder-bands. His head is bound with a lotus garland, and he assumes a gazing posture.

Next, the Aparājita Wrathful King: his body is white, with four arms and a frowning, wrathful face. He wears a tiger-skin skirt. A python serves as his ear ornaments. Takṣaka Nāga King serves as his belt. Vāsuki Nāga King serves as his sacred cord, draped diagonally. His body is somewhat short. He is adorned with a crest-crown of poisonous serpents. His mouth bites the lower lip. His entire body blazes with fierce flames and a circular halo. His upper right hand holds a vajra. His lower right hand makes the threatening gesture. His upper left hand holds a three-pronged trident. His lower left hand holds a battle-axe. His front face assumes the posture of laughing fiercely with "aṭaṭa hasa." From his mouth blaze flames of various colors. His right face assumes a gazing posture. His left face looks downward. His top face assumes the posture of surveying the great assembly. He stands upon a jeweled lotus.

The above two holy ones are painted on either side outside the maṇḍala gate.

Next, contemplate the principal deity's five limbs: *a vi ra hūṃ khaṃ*. The syllable *a*, the syllable *vi*, the syllable *ra*, the syllable *hūṃ*, the syllable *khaṃ*.

Having first arranged the five limbs, next the yoga visualization is taught:

> The syllable *a*, true gold in color, universally manifests the vajra-wheel.
> Empowering the lower body, this is called the yoga-seat.
> The syllable *vi*, the light of a white moon, set amid a cluster of mist,
> Empowering from the navel upward, this is called the great ocean of compassion.
> The syllable *ra*, the glow of the rising sun, its form red within a triangle,
> Empowering the heart position, this is called the might of wisdom-fire.
> The syllable *hūṃ*, the flame of the kalpa-fire, dark as if smeared with the wind-wheel,
> Empowering the white ūrṇā point, this is called the power of sovereign freedom.
> The syllable *khaṃ*, arising from great emptiness, containing all forms,
> Empowering the crown, it is therefore called the Peerless One.

Next, the five limbs are correlated with earth, water, fire, wind, and space, and with the altar.

Visualize the syllable *a* beneath the seat: the vajra-seat is accomplished, its color like gold. This is the meaning of discipline. Its altar shape is square; the maṇḍala likewise. Visualize the syllable *vi* at the navel: its color is white, the water of compassion. This is the meaning of concentration. Its altar shape is round; the outer altar like a full moon. Visualize the syllable *ra* at the heart: its color is scarlet red. This is the meaning of wisdom. Its altar is triangular; the outer altar likewise. Visualize the syllable *hūṃ* between the eyebrows: its color is black, the unobstructed wind. This is the meaning of liberation. Its altar shape is like a half-moon in blue; the outer altar shape likewise. The syllable *khaṃ* is placed at the crown, accomplishing the great-emptiness dot. This is the meaning of liberation-knowledge.

The altar accords with the syllable-meaning. The emptiness-dot has no color or form but can accomplish all deeds. This is the meaning of dhāraṇī.

*Oṃ sukṣma vajra*

*Oṃ tiṣṭha vajra*

*Oṃ sphāraṇa vajra*

*Oṃ saṃhara vajra*

Next, the method for painting the Subāhu Youth, his form and retinue. His body is in the form of a youth, golden in color as his name suggests. The Honored One is depicted with awe-inspiring bearing, surrounded by the seven luminaries. Mīmako, Utpala-mukha, and Naṭakeśī are each painted in their own forms, standing upon flowers beside Hayagrīva Vidyārāja. Next, paint the Carya-Mūrdhaja Youth, his form and retinue. His body is golden, with topknot hair hanging down. His left hand holds a vajra, and his right hand is placed at his hip. Eight vināyakas surround him as his retinue: Vajra-Adornment, Vajra-Dust, Vajra-Lasso, Vajra-Axe, Vajra-Great-Laughter, Vajra-Accomplished-Adornment, Vajra-Crest, and Vajra-Vināyaka, each with weapons and implements as described in the text.

The above is the complete writing of the Uṣṇīṣa-Cakravartin altar method.

Colophon: Written on the first day of the fifth month of the first year of Tenki [1053 CE]. First collation completed by the monk Raishō. Collated against the text held by Master Jitsugon of Tendai. Tō-ji monk Dōkai. This ritual was perhaps compiled by Master Benkō? The characters *ben* and *ben* are used interchangeably. As a composition by a disciple of the Qinglong [Monastery] Master, it is most fitting to serve as a model.
