# Sūtra of the Great Vehicle, the Great and Vast Buddha-Crown, Spoken by the Buddha

## *Fó Shuō Dàchéng Dàfāngguǎng Fóguān Jīng* (佛說大乘大方廣佛冠經)

Taishō 438

Translated by Fǎhù (*Fǎhù*, 法護) and others of the Song dynasty.

*Note: This is a Mahāyāna sūtra in which the Buddha, dwelling at Vulture Peak in Rājagṛha, teaches Mahākāśyapa about the buddhas of the ten directions and their accompanying bodhisattvas, explaining the merits of hearing, reciting, and upholding their names. The sūtra follows a formulaic pattern: for each direction, the Buddha describes a distant buddha-field, names the presiding buddha and the bodhisattva who will succeed him, and then details the specific spiritual benefits gained by those who remember and uphold those names (such as recollection of past lives, birth by lotus transformation, never falling into evil destinies, attainment of various samādhis, mastery of dhāraṇīs, and non-retrogression from supreme enlightenment). The text belongs to the genre of buddha-name sūtras and is related to the Buddhāvataṃsaka tradition.*

---

## Fascicle One

Thus have I heard. At one time, the World-Honored One was in the city of Rājagṛha (*Wángshè chéng*, 王舍城), on Vulture Peak (*Jiùfēng shān*, 鷲峯山), together with a great assembly of 1,250 bhikṣus, all of whom were arhats with all outflows exhausted.

There were also a great assembly of bodhisattvas, with Maitreya (*Císhì*, 慈氏) Bodhisattva and Mañjuśrī (*Miàojíxiáng*, 妙吉祥) Bodhisattva as their leaders, together with innumerable devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, and hosts of vidyādhara celestial maidens.

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the Elder Mahākāśyapa (*Dà Jiāyè*, 大迦葉): "Kāśyapa! I now wish to teach for the purpose of bringing to maturity all the roots of merit of those bodhisattvas who have newly aroused the aspiration. Listen attentively and apply your minds well!"

Mahākāśyapa said to the Buddha: "Excellent, World-Honored One! We receive your teaching and listen."

### The Eastern Direction

The Buddha said: "Kāśyapa! To the east of this buddha-field, beyond worlds as numerous as the sands of one Ganges River, there is a world called Samādhi-Adorned (*Dìngyán*, 定嚴). In that land there is a buddha named Samādhi-Hand Supreme Auspiciousness Tathāgata (*Dìngshǒu Zuìshàng Jíxiáng Rúlái*, 定手最上吉祥如來), Worthy of Offerings, Perfectly and Fully Awakened, who now presides there, teaching and benefiting beings. In his buddha-field there is a bodhisattva-mahāsattva named离塵步 (*Líchén Bù*, Dust-Free Steps), who will succeed that buddha and attain supreme, perfect enlightenment, with the name Ever-Samādhi-Hand Supreme Auspiciousness Tathāgata."

"Kāśyapa! If any good men or good women who dwell in the bodhisattva vehicle hear the name of Samādhi-Hand Supreme Auspiciousness Tathāgata and the name of the bodhisattva-mahāsattva Dust-Free Steps, and are able to recite and uphold them, those persons in future lives will obtain the knowledge of recalling past lives. For a hundred kalpas they will turn away from birth-and-death, will be able to gather immeasurable accumulations of merit, and will approach the realization of supreme, perfect enlightenment."

"Furthermore, Kāśyapa! To the east, beyond worlds as numerous as the sands of two Ganges Rivers, there is a world called great enlightenment-Site Adorned with Wondrous Love. In that land there is a buddha named Lotus Supreme Auspiciousness Tathāgata, who now presides there. In his field there is a bodhisattva-mahāsattva named Lotus-Hand, who will succeed that buddha."

"Kāśyapa! Those who hear the name of Lotus Supreme Auspiciousness Tathāgata and the name of Lotus-Hand Bodhisattva, and are able to recite and uphold them, will in future lives obtain the knowledge of recalling past lives. Wherever they are born, they will always be born by lotus transformation, without attachment to any world, transcending all unwholesome dharmas, like a lotus unstained by water. They will attain non-retrogression from supreme, perfect enlightenment. For thirty thousand kalpas they will turn away from birth-and-death."

"Furthermore, Kāśyapa! To the east, beyond worlds as numerous as the sands of three Ganges Rivers, there is a world called Dust-Free Treasury. In that land there is a buddha named Sun-Wheel Radiance Supreme Victory Auspiciousness Tathāgata. In his field there is a bodhisattva-mahāsattva named Sun-Radiance, who will succeed that buddha."

"Those who hear and uphold these names will obtain the knowledge of recalling past lives. Riding the radiance of the sun-wheel, they will illuminate all wholesome dharmas and will never again fall into the three evil destinies, doubt, or fear. They will attain non-retrogression from supreme, perfect enlightenment. For a thousand kalpas they will turn away from birth-and-death."

"Furthermore, Kāśyapa! To the east, beyond worlds as numerous as the sands of four Ganges Rivers, there is a world called Attaining Sovereignty. In that land there is a buddha named Single Jewel-Canopy Supreme Tathāgata."

"Those who hear and uphold this name will obtain the knowledge of recalling past lives. They will not be born in low or impoverished families, but wherever they are born, it will always be in a royal palace. Wherever they are born, there will be no obstacle from the mother, no obstacle from the father, no difficulty from wealth and nobility, and no difficulty from illness. They will attain non-retrogression from supreme, perfect enlightenment. For thirty thousand kalpas they will turn away from birth-and-death."

"Furthermore, Kāśyapa! To the east, beyond worlds as numerous as the sands of five Ganges Rivers, there is a world called Pure Treasury. In that land there is a buddha named Samādhi Supreme Auspiciousness Tathāgata."

"Kāśyapa! That Samādhi Supreme Auspiciousness Tathāgata, when formerly practicing the bodhisattva path, made a great vow: 'When I attain buddhahood in the future, if any good men or good women who dwell in the bodhisattva vehicle hear my name and are able to recite and uphold it, those persons will in every life always go forth from the household. Having gone forth, they will each obtain a thousand samādhis, and within each samādhi they will further realize a hundred thousand koṭi nayuta samādhi-gates. The samādhis they have obtained will never be lost. They will be able to visit, pay reverence to, and hear the Dharma from ten thousand buddhas of the past who have already entered nirvāṇa. They will likewise see buddhas and hear the Dharma in the present and future. They will attain non-retrogression from supreme, perfect enlightenment. In every lifetime, for a thousand kalpas they will turn away from birth-and-death.'"

"Furthermore, Kāśyapa! To the east, beyond worlds as numerous as the sands of six Ganges Rivers, there is a world called Jewel-Adorned. In that land there is a buddha named Jewel-Wheel Radiance Lofty Victory Auspicious King Tathāgata."

"Those who hear and uphold this name will in future lives obtain the knowledge of recalling past lives, will be endowed with mindfulness and wisdom, their marks perfected, and will achieve trustworthy speech. They will obtain the eloquence of uninterrupted language. Among all the eloquences of the world of humans and gods, they will not be made timid, interrupted, or silenced by others, but will always possess uninterrupted verbal eloquence. Furthermore, when such good men or good women recite that buddha's name and listen to the Dharma, the Buddha's holy form will appear before them. Whatever dharmas they had not previously heard, as they receive and study them, they will immediately obtain immeasurable koṭi nayuta hundreds of thousands of revolving dhāraṇīs. Moreover, they will know whatever all sentient beings in this Jambudvīpa desire, and accordingly, in harmony with their faith and understanding, they will skillfully teach the Dharma for them. They will attain non-retrogression from supreme, perfect enlightenment. For innumerable koṭi nayuta hundreds of thousands of kalpas they will turn away from birth-and-death."

## Fascicle Two

### The Southern Direction

"Furthermore, Kāśyapa! To the south, beyond thirty thousand worlds, there is a world called Delightful Sound. In that land there is a buddha named Infinite Footprints Tathāgata."

"Those who hear and uphold this name will obtain the knowledge of recalling past lives and will attain the Moonwheel Pure Bodhisattva Samādhi. Abiding in this samādhi, they will be able to behold, in the eastern direction, all the buddha world-honored ones as numerous as the sands of the Ganges. Likewise in the south, west, north, the four intermediate directions, above and below: in each direction, they will behold all the buddha world-honored ones as numerous as the sands of the Ganges. From each of those buddhas they will hear and receive the Dharma and be able to understand it completely. They will always encounter the appearance of buddhas in the world, and at no time will they be separated from the buddha world-honored ones, until they reach the ultimate limit of attaining awakening. They will forever attain non-retrogression from supreme, perfect enlightenment. For thirty-five thousand kalpas they will turn away from birth-and-death."

"Furthermore, Kāśyapa! To the south, beyond thirty-five worlds, there is a world called Wondrous Fragrance Supreme Fragrance. In that land there is a buddha named Infinite Fragrance Supreme King Tathāgata."

"That Infinite Fragrance Supreme King Tathāgata, when formerly practicing the bodhisattva path, made a great vow: 'When I attain buddhahood, those who hear my name and uphold it will obtain many kinds of merit: wherever they are born, they will obtain the knowledge of recalling past lives; their pure conduct will be perfected; they will be adorned with the thirty-two marks of a great being. Furthermore, beings in other buddha-fields who are touched by my light will all come and gather, and I shall bring to fulfillment all their practices toward awakening. Moreover, if this great earth were to be entirely filled with great fire burning universally, and after the burning the earth were to produce various precious jewels for all who come seeking: even so, there would not be the slightest disturbance in their composure. They will attain non-retrogression from supreme, perfect enlightenment. For thirty thousand kalpas they will turn away from birth-and-death.'"

"Furthermore, Kāśyapa! To the south, beyond worlds as numerous as the sands of one Ganges River, there is a buddha named Jewel-Supreme Tathāgata."

"Those who hear and uphold this name will obtain the knowledge of recalling past lives. Furthermore, while still in their mother's womb, they will see the Buddha. That buddha will encourage and praise them, saying: 'Good son! You should bring to maturity supreme, perfect enlightenment. Quickly emerge from the mother's womb!' For a thousand kalpas they will turn away from birth-and-death."

### The Western Direction

"Furthermore, Kāśyapa! To the west, beyond worlds as numerous as the sands of three Ganges Rivers, there is a world called Not-Knowing Omniscience. In that land there is a buddha named Great Radiance Illumination Tathāgata."

"Those who hear and uphold this name will obtain the knowledge of recalling past lives and will attain the Sun-Wheel Radiance Supreme Samādhi. Abiding in this samādhi, they will see buddha world-honored ones as numerous as the sands of the Ganges, and great bodhisattvas such as Ajita and others will equally praise their merit."

"Kāśyapa! Furthermore, if a woman in her mother's womb hears the name of that tathāgata, that person in future lives will never again take on the form of a woman. After this life ends, she will become a man. Why? Because, having heard the name of such a tathāgata of great spiritual power and great awesome virtue, she is able to receive and uphold it."

Then the World-Honored One spoke this verse:

> "If there is a woman dwelling in her mother's womb
> Who hears the great sage buddha's supreme name,
> That person, having shed this female body,
> Shall become a wise and [virtuous] man.
> Realizing supreme, great enlightenment,
> She shall benefit all sentient beings,
> And among the gods above and humans below
> Universally serve as a guide for all."

### The Northern Direction

"Furthermore, Kāśyapa! To the north, beyond forty thousand worlds, there is a world called Having Jewels. In that land there is a buddha named Jewel-Blossom Universally Shining Auspiciousness Tathāgata."

"Those who hear and uphold this name will obtain the knowledge of recalling past lives and will attain the Jewel-Moon Bodhisattva Samādhi. Abiding in this samādhi, they will understand the languages and voices of all sentient beings. Receiving the Buddha's empowerment, they will achieve unobstructed verbal eloquence. If any sentient beings desire to see them standing in space and teaching the Dharma, they will immediately manifest as wished. Moreover, whatever sounds and utterances all sentient beings in this Jambudvīpa produce, they will each and every one fully know. And whatever sounds, utterances, and speech all sentient beings in this great chiliocosm produce, they will all know and, according to their faith and understanding, teach the Dharma for each. They will attain non-retrogression from supreme, perfect enlightenment. For sixty thousand kalpas they will turn away from birth-and-death."

### Further Eastern Directions

"Furthermore, Kāśyapa! To the east, beyond sixty thousand worlds, there is a world called Manifold Jewels. In that land there is a buddha named Jewel-Treasury Tathāgata."

"Those who hear and uphold this name will obtain the knowledge of recalling past lives and will accomplish the dharma of the seven factors of awakening."

"Furthermore, Kāśyapa! To the east, beyond incalculable worlds, there is a world called Jewel-Splendor. In that land there is a buddha named Jewel-Victory Tathāgata, who now presides there teaching the Dharma."

"Kāśyapa! Those who hear and uphold the name of Jewel-Victory Tathāgata will generate many kinds of merit-accumulations."

"Kāśyapa! Suppose good men or good women who dwell in the bodhisattva vehicle were to amass precious jewels in a quantity as great as Mount Sumeru, and with such jewels practice generosity, making vast offerings to the buddha world-honored ones, continuing such giving for koṭi nayuta hundreds of thousands of years without interruption. Yet if another person hears the name of Jewel-Victory Tathāgata and is able to recite and uphold it, the immeasurable merit of the former generosity would not equal one hundredth, one thousandth, one hundred-thousandth, one incalculable-fraction, one calculable fraction, or one analogical fraction of this merit."

---

Then the World-Honored One spoke these gāthās:

> "In all the buddha-fields of the east,
> The south, the west, and the north,
> The world-honored ones presently abiding in all directions
> Illuminate sentient beings and bring them benefit.

> "Whether the time is right or not, one should know:
> Day and night, constantly arousing reverent thoughts,
> Joining palms in sincerity and devotion,
> One should bow in faith to the supreme among humans.

> "Who can know these names and understand them?
> With mind and imagination, bow to the honored ones among humans.
> It is said that for a thousand kalpas
> It is extremely difficult to encounter a buddha's appearance in the world.

> "If one wishes to pay reverence to all the buddhas,
> Seeking supreme enlightenment,
> Hearing the buddha-names the Buddha has taught,
> One should constantly take refuge in them.

> "I now seek nothing else;
> I seek only what is born from buddha-wisdom.
> Whatever roots of merit are thereby obtained,
> I dedicate them to the inconceivable buddha-wisdom.

> "If one wishes to make offerings to all the buddhas,
> Always keeping the inconceivable Buddha in mind,
> Having heard those buddha-names,
> One should proclaim them widely for others.

> "Wishing to enable koṭis of sentient beings
> To escape the two kinds of existence and attain liberation,
> Having heard those buddha-names,
> Uphold them and never again be born in the human realm.

> "Wishing to enable koṭis of buddha-fields
> To be shaken by the touch of a toe,
> Having heard those buddha-names,
> Uphold them and proclaim them widely for many.

> "If one has formerly made offerings to the buddhas,
> One will hear buddha-names and be able to uphold them.
> It is not in one life, nor ten, nor twenty lives:
> This comes from planting roots of merit in many lives.

> "If one hears buddha-names and can uphold them,
> Compared with one who gives gold and jewels as numerous as a river's sands,
> The merit of the person who upholds buddha-names:
> The giving of wealth does not equal one hundredth part.

> "Those who hear buddha-names and uphold them
> Shall obtain the supreme human body,
> With wondrous form and dignified beauty in the world.
> Going forth, they will be worthy of the offerings of humans and gods."

When the Buddha had finished teaching this sūtra, the bhikṣus, the great bodhisattva-mahāsattvas, the Elder Mahākāśyapa, and all the devas, humans, asuras, gandharvas, and others in the world, having heard what the Buddha taught, were all greatly delighted, believed and accepted it, bowed to the Buddha, and withdrew.
