# 持齋念佛懺悔禮文
## Liturgy of Observing Purification Fasts, Buddha-Mindfulness, Repentance, and Prostration

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 2829

Author unknown.

---

Prostrating to the buddha-names, one is oppressed by dread, without any leisure. Transgressions reaching a hundred kalpas, a thousand kalpas, with no time of liberation, no leisure, no leisure at all. The merit of prostrating in reverence and making offerings to all buddhas and overcoming stinginess is therefore briefly set forth.

### Monthly Prostration Schedule

First month, first day, at the hour of dawn: face south and make four prostrations, eliminating the transgressions of one hundred kalpas.

Second month, ninth day, at the hour of cockcrow: face north and make four prostrations, eliminating the transgressions of one hundred kalpas.

Third month, seventh day, at the hour of settling in for the night: face west and make four prostrations, eliminating the transgressions of three hundred kalpas.

Fourth month, eighth day, at the hour of midnight: face north and make four prostrations, eliminating the transgressions of one hundred and thirty kalpas.

Fifth month, sixth day, at the hour of dusk: face south and make four prostrations, eliminating the transgressions of four hundred kalpas.

Sixth month, third day, at the hour of dusk: face east and make six prostrations, eliminating the transgressions of five hundred kalpas.

Seventh month, sixth day, at the hour of noon: face east and make nine prostrations, eliminating the transgressions of two thousand six hundred kalpas.

Eighth month, eighth day, at the hour of noon: face south and make nine prostrations, eliminating the transgressions of two thousand three hundred kalpas.

Ninth month, ninth day, at the hour of noon: face east and make nine prostrations, eliminating the transgressions of three thousand three hundred kalpas.

Tenth month, seventh day, at the hour of dusk: face south and make prostrations, eliminating the transgressions of four thousand kalpas.

Eleventh month, thirteenth day, at the hour of dusk: face south and make four prostrations, eliminating the transgressions of four thousand kalpas.

Twelfth month, thirteenth day, at the hour of dusk: face south and make thirty prostrations, eliminating the transgressions of four kalpas.

---

At that time, Dharma Master Xuanzang ordered this list of buddha-names to be copied and distributed to all the prefectures of the five turbid evil world. If good women are able to sincerely receive, uphold, and practice these prostrations according to the kalpas specified, their transgressions throughout incalculable Ganges-sand kalpas will be eliminated. After death, they will be reborn seated upon a jeweled lotus flower, receiving every kind of joy. When wishing for robes, silks and brocades of a thousand layers appear. When thinking of food, a hundred flavors all arrive. Furthermore, those who can teach others to receive and uphold these prostrations are assured of eliminating 82,500 transgressions without fail.

Also, in summary, the evil days of each month are also the ten purification-fast days:

### The Ten Purification-Fast Days

On this day, observe the purification fast and recite the Buddha's name, and you will not fall into the Hell of Sword-Trees.

On the eighth day, the great purification fast: recite the Buddha's name, and you will not fall into the Hell of Iron Plows.

On the fourteenth day, the Superintendent of Life descends. On this day, observe the fast and recite the Buddha's name, and you will not fall into the Hell of Boiling Cauldrons.

On the fifteenth day, the Great Spirit of the Five Paths descends. On this day, observe the fast and recite the Buddha's name, and you will not fall into the Hell of Excrement.

On the eighteenth day, King Yama descends. On this day, observe the fast and recite the Buddha's name, and you will not fall into the Hell of Saws.

On the twenty-third day, the Great Heavenly General descends. On this day, observe the fast and recite the Buddha's name, and you will not fall into the Hell of Copper Pillars.

On the twenty-fourth day, the Superintendent of Life descends. On this day, observe the fast and recite the Buddha's name, and you will not fall into the Hell of Iron Beds.

On the twenty-eighth day, the Lord of Mount Tai descends. On this day, observe the fast and recite the Buddha's name, and you will not fall into the Hell of Iron Forks.

On the twenty-ninth day, the Four Heavenly Kings descend. On this day, observe the fast and recite the Buddha's name, and you will not fall into the Hell of Sword-Mountains.

On the thirtieth day, Indra and Brahmā Kings descend. On this day, observe the fast and recite the Buddha's name, and you will not fall into the Hell of Iron Wheels.

The above ten purification-fast days are when the various great spirits, boys, and the Superintendent of Life descend. The schedule notes that they descend after the noon meal in the morning. We respectfully request the assembly: when the time comes, make a thousand, ten thousand efforts. Recite the Buddha's name and cultivate goodness. Be cautious and do not allow transgressions to be recorded. Who then could save you?

---

### Repentance and Offering Prayer

The True Teaching is vast and far-reaching; without the accumulation of goodness, one cannot reach it. The ocean of suffering is hard to cross; without a boat, one cannot traverse it. The mouth is like the thousand buddhas of the Fortunate Kalpa; their sacred transformations have taken form. The thirty-three heavens represent the wheel-turning king's meritorious deeds. Therefore Śākyamuni cultivated the Way through six years of hardship. The boy offered his body to seek the four phrases and eight characters. The ocean of the Buddha's merit is praised without end.

Now, the patron of the fast before the assembly, having respectfully initiated this, bows to the buddhas of the ten directions, the venerable Dharma of prajñā, and the holy saṃgha of arhats. May blessings descend upon those present. We request the assembly to constantly renew their vows throughout the year. May the household be at peace. We reverently set up the offering of a single midday repast. May it be that from now on, the fragrant mountain and fragrant water will cleanse body and mind. May the supreme amṛta flow through the body. May the form be like a jeweled mountain, unmoving and unshaken, and the life be enduring. May the bodhi advance daily and the meritorious work be lofty and bright. May merit, goodness, and vows be coterminous. May life equal the vajra and thus endure. May women be endowed with grace and beauty like the verdant trees unfolding, splendid and bright like clustered blossoms in their glory.

We further vow: may this merit ensure that the Emperor and Empress enjoy an endless sacred transformation. May they sit composedly on the throne of the one emperor, not clinging to the transformation of the thousand kings. May foreign lands bring tribute and, grasping the divine plan, eagerly arrive. May those who plow yield to those on the ridges, and the roads be shared in harmony. May the sacred reign eternally flourish and extend for ten thousand generations. May the three realms be transformed into a pure land. May the hells be transformed into a fragrant city. May all emerge from the gate of suffering and ascend together to the path of awakening.

The voice of Brahmā: "How does one attain long life and a vajra indestructible body? By what causes and conditions does the Buddha attain great and firm power? How does one, through this sūtra, ultimately reach the other shore? We pray the Buddha will open the subtle and esoteric and broadly explain for all sentient beings."

For the twenty-eight heavens, Indra, and Brahmā Kings, we reverently worship the ever-abiding Three Jewels. For the patron, to be adorned with merit and wisdom, we reverently worship the ever-abiding Three Jewels. For sentient beings of the six destinies, to depart from obstructions and attain liberation, we bow to all the worthies and sages.

### Verse for the Food Offering

The food now offered is presented above to all buddhas, in the middle to the worthies and sages, and below to sentient beings of the dharma-realm. May all be universally and fully satiated, equally receiving this food.

The fragrance of this food, not yet touched, is presented above to all buddhas, in the middle to the worthies and sages, and below to sentient beings of the dharma-realm. May all be universally and fully satiated.

### Verse of Inquiry for the Food Offering

Respectfully addressing the great assembly: the fast-food that this disciple has prepared was made with many hands together and much that is not in accord with the Dharma. We pray the assembly will broadly bestow their compassion and accept it with joy.

Again addressing the great assembly: the fast-food that this disciple has prepared, made with many hands together and much that is not in accord with the Dharma, was prepared for the midday fast-meal, but the time has now passed. Any remaining food is offered for the assembly to dispose of.

Universal recitation: "Abiding in the world like empty space, like a lotus unattached to water, the mind pure and transcending all, I bow in worship to the Unsurpassed Honored One."

### Verse for the Gift of Giving

All practices are preceded by giving. Through generosity, one departs from stinginess and greed. Therefore the present patron, having completed the fast, fears the fruit may not be complete, and further offers pure wealth as an act of giving. May the deceased be reborn in the Western Land. May those now living see their own nature and accomplish the buddha-way.

All respectfully: "I take refuge in the Buddha. May all sentient beings embody the correct awakening and arouse the bodhi-mind. I take refuge in the Dharma. May all sentient beings enter deeply into the treasury of sūtras, with wisdom like the ocean. I take refuge in the Saṃgha. May all sentient beings govern the great assembly, with all things unobstructed."

The assembly of men, hear:

> The Tathāgata's wondrous form-body is without equal in the world,
> Incomparable, inconceivable: therefore I now worship in reverence.
> The Tathāgata's form is inexhaustible; his wisdom is likewise.
> All dharmas are ever-abiding: therefore I take refuge.
> We reverently worship the ever-abiding Three Jewels.

### Eleven-Faced Avalokiteśvara Mantra

This is called the Great Heart Mantra.

*[Dhāraṇī]*

This mantra can subdue all ghost-illnesses with great efficacy. It also subdues ghosts and spirits who transform their bodies, or appear as barbarians, or in various forms, and enter into a person's body. In upholding it, one must maintain purity.

### Mantra for Advancing in Fruition and Attaining Realization through Cultivated Practice

*Taji-tā mūlura mūlura abhaje mūlura vīkṣa-yā svāhā.*

If a person receives, upholds, and recites this while also cultivating meritorious works, this person is an initial-ground bodhisattva.

### Pure Land Devotional Verses

Homage to Amitābha Buddha of the Western Land. The Buddha is in the Western Land of Ultimate Bliss. We universally exhort all of one mind to be reborn there. Without regard for noble or base, all are clearly guided. It is only ordinary beings whose karma is not yet accomplished. First, recite the Buddha's name and never forget. Second, keep mouth and mind level and fair. Third, diligently cultivate the six perfections' practices. Fourth, beyond the fruit, seek fame. If one can thus accord with the Buddha's intention, the lotus flowers of that land face eastward. At the moment of death, go directly there. Following the Buddha, rescue sentient beings in the six destinies. May we be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

Homage to Amitābha Buddha of the Western Land. Sentient beings in the six destinies cannot be saved. They see the Buddha's radiance yet do not arouse the mind. Amitābha himself calls, but they refuse to go. The ox-headed jailers of hell compete to welcome them. When karma is determined, even the Buddha's rescue cannot avail. The cauldrons of boiling broth and furnaces of charcoal deepen further. The Buddha hears and asks: "What transgressions has this person committed?" Drinking wine, eating meat, and engaging in sexual misconduct. We universally exhort the assembly of the place of the Way: may they relinquish the body of pus and blood in Jambudvīpa. May we together with all sentient beings be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

Homage to Amitābha Buddha of the Western Land. Once entering the true gate, one never turns back. With palms joined, one sits with devoted mind upon the jeweled platform. The waters of the eight meritorious qualities are always perfectly full. On four sides, lotus flowers surround the Buddha as he comes. The Buddha emits the light of the mark between his brows, illuminating those of the lower grades. The flowers spontaneously open. He wishes to go to other lands to rescue those in suffering. Upon his body he dares to wear the garment of two pearls. We universally exhort the assembly of the place of the Way: single-mindedly recite the Buddha's name and enter the true gate. May we together with all sentient beings be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

Homage to Amitābha Buddha of the Western Land. The golden lotus, alone seated, displays radiance. He thoroughly gathers the great Way for suffering sentient beings. If you wish to return together to one place, diligently cultivate and practice with the utmost precision. Bodhisattvas inwardly store the cultivation of the ten wholesome deeds. Ordinary beings outwardly practice and seek fame. The buddha-dharma is vast and broad, without duality. Foolish people discriminate and practice through multiple gates. Some say this body is identical with the nature. Some say the Buddha-nature must be sought elsewhere. Such people are not just one or two; they are certainly destined for the three evil paths and six destinies' pit. May they sever the two extreme views and cultivate equanimity. In the pure land of the West, rebirth will naturally occur. May we together with all sentient beings be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

Homage to Amitābha Buddha of the Western Land. All of you, exclusively cultivate the karma of the pure land. You should sit alone and quietly reflect. Do not say there are portions of time in Jambudvīpa. In an instant, without ceasing, impermanence arrives. Life is like a lamp-flame in the wind, also like a flash of lightning within the clouds. The sufferings of the three evil paths and eighteen kinds are innumerable. When impermanence suddenly arrives, where can one hide? Pausing to reflect deeply, how lamentable! How can one not be startled and alarmed day and night? Since ancient times, even sages and worthies have not escaped death. How much less can ordinary beings expect a long portion? Practitioners, strive with effort to cultivate the ten wholesome deeds, knowing this body has no fixed portion and impermanence is immediate. May we together with all sentient beings be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

Homage to Amitābha Buddha of the Western Land. This disciple's own nature dwells in the three realms, perpetually in the dark, abiding in murkiness. Revolving in the six destinies without rest, never having heard the sound of the pure land. Today, encountering a good teacher, may I relinquish this foul and impure body of an ordinary being. I humbly wish to be reborn in the Land of Ultimate Bliss, naturally born upon a lotus platform. May we together with all sentient beings be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

Homage to Amitābha Buddha. Now contemplating this body, it is truly loathsome. Various kinds of impurity comprise this falsely named body. Three hundred shattered bones mutually support it. This leaking body has never had a single part that is true. Rouge and vermillion lips are vainly used for adornment. In the end, one returns beneath the earth to become ash and dust. Afflictions blaze fiercely without rest: all of this is the cause of drifting through the three evil paths. We universally exhort the assembly of the place of the Way: strive with effort to diligently cultivate the cause of the pure land. May we together with all sentient beings be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

Homage to Amitābha Buddha of the Western Land. Once entering the three evil paths, it is hard to escape. Through successive kalpas one suffers in the body. When life's end approaches and there is no goodness, the mind is panicked and confused. All the transgressions and karma are all visible. The cauldrons of boiling broth and furnaces appear before the eyes. The ālaya-consciousness receives all the suffering. The four great elements are deeply buried in the earth's storehouse. Having now obtained a human body yet not reciting the Buddha's name, in the next life one becomes a fur-bearing creature in the borderlands. May all sentient beings be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

Homage to Amitābha Buddha of the Western Land. Wearing fur and bearing horns in the body of an animal: this is the bitter cause from my former life. Creating transgressions and killing living beings without rest, slighting teachers and monastics, and being heedless toward both parents. Drinking wine and eating meat without shame: today I suffer. To whom can I plead? My mouth is gagged and I cannot speak. Various kinds of floggings and beatings upon the body. In stinging pain, from both eyes tears fall. I universally exhort those in the next life to arouse the wholesome mind. May we together with all sentient beings be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

Homage to Amitābha Buddha of the Western Land. Through successive kalpas I have harbored jealousy and pride. Delusion gives rise to the three poisons. Hard to escape, constantly preyed upon by the six thieves. When drawing near, the year passes; upon returning, the year returns to the great earth. Before one is aware, the heart is naturally startled. Each of you, diligently cultivate wholesome deeds. Strive with effort to avoid the fire-pit ahead. May we together be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

Homage to Amitābha Buddha of the Western Land. Contemplate carefully: in the West there is a land. In that land there is a buddha named Amitābha. He abides in one place, teaching the Dharma for Ganges-sand kalpas, universally severing the māras for all worlds. In the city of peace and bliss, he ascends the holy seat. All the devas surround him and come from every side. Eighty-four thousand bodhisattvas in assembly offer flowers and fruits to purify the sahā world. May we together with all sentient beings be reborn in the Land of Peace and Bliss.

Homage to Amitābha Buddha of the Western Land. Heavenly palaces all appear in the sky. The seven-jeweled platforms and halls always bear banners, canopies, pennants, and flowers everywhere. Golden branches and jade trees are naturally present. Agate and crystal form the great earth. Coral forms the great ocean.
