# 天公經
## Sutra of the Celestial Sovereign

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 2876

Apocryphal text of Chinese composition. Translator unknown (失譯)

---

Pillars cast of bronze. Rafters made of elephant ivory. Screens fashioned of amber. Nets woven of pearls. Finials set with constellations. Latticed windows made of sun and moon. This may endure for one hundred million nine thousand years. Above is one minor kalpa; below is one yojana.

Though this sutra is small, great is its divine power. It is equal to the Lotus Sutra; it is equal to the Nirvāṇa Sutra; it encompasses the miscellaneous sutras. It is as the Eastern Sea; it is likened to Mount Tai. One who enters the sea to take treasure may return again however much one takes. One who enters the mountain to take timber may return again whatever the length of the timber.

Whoever copies this sutra will find the whorl-marks form upon their hands. Whoever reads this sutra will find doubled radiance arise in their eyes. Whoever recites this sutra will be greatly wise throughout kalpas.

Avalokiteśvara creates gold. Great sins will be eliminated; minor sins will be extinguished. If one is bound for the Mountain of Swords, the Mountain of Swords will crumble and break. If one is bound for the Tree of Swords, the Tree of Swords will break of itself. If one is bound for the furnace of burning coals, the furnace will extinguish of itself. If one is bound for the cauldron of boiling water, the cauldron will cool of itself.

Vast and resonant is the spiritual sound, clear and bright. At the center of the lecture hall the floor is of lapis lazuli; the walls are of white silver. In front, one ascends the golden throne; behind, immortals, kings, and commoners alike hold the golden sutra in hand.

Then the Buddha spoke, saying:

> Remember the Buddha as one remembers food;
> contemplate the Buddha as one contemplates life.
> Recite five times each day,
> and sins of fifteen million kalpas will be eliminated.
> All will be released and attain the Buddha-path.
