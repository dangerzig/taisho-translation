# 般若波羅蜜多心經
## Heart Sūtra of Prajñāpāramitā

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0255

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Facheng (法成)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Bhagavān was dwelling in the city of Rājagṛha, on Vulture Peak Mountain, together with a great assembly of bhikṣus and bodhisattva-mahāsattvas. At that time, the World-Honored One entered the samādhi known as the Dharma-gate called Profound Clarity. Furthermore, at that time, the Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Avalokiteśvara, when practicing the deep prajñāpāramitā, contemplated and illuminated the five aggregates and saw that their essential nature is entirely empty.

Then the venerable Śāriputra, empowered by the Buddha's might, addressed the holy Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Avalokiteśvara: "If a good man wishes to practice the profound prajñāpāramitā, how should he study it?" Having spoken thus,

the Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Avalokiteśvara answered the venerable Śāriputra: "If good men and good women wish to practice the profound prajñāpāramitā, they should contemplate thus: the essential nature of the five aggregates is entirely empty. Form is itself emptiness; emptiness is itself form. Form is not different from emptiness; emptiness is not different from form. Likewise, feeling, perception, volitional formations, and consciousness are also entirely empty. Therefore, Śāriputra! The emptiness-nature of all dharmas is without characteristics: not produced and not destroyed, not defiled and free from defilement, not decreased and not increased. Śāriputra! Therefore, at that time, within emptiness-nature there is no form, no feeling, no perception, no volitional formations, and no consciousness. There is no eye, no ear, no nose, no tongue, no body, and no mind. There is no form, no sound, no smell, no taste, no touch, and no mental object. There is no eye-element and so on up to no mind-consciousness element. There is no ignorance and no extinction of ignorance, and so on up to no old age and death and no extinction of old age and death. There is no suffering, origination, cessation, or path. There is no wisdom, no attainment, and also no non-attainment. Therefore, Śāriputra! Because there is nothing to attain, all the bodhisattvas, relying on prajñāpāramitā, have minds without obstruction. Because there is no fear, they transcend inversion and reach ultimate nirvāṇa. All Buddhas of the three periods of time also rely on prajñāpāramitā and thereby realize the supreme, perfect enlightenment. Śāriputra! Therefore know that prajñāpāramitā is the great secret mantra, the great luminous mantra, the unsurpassed mantra, the mantra equal to the unequaled, the mantra that can remove all suffering, true and without inversion. Therefore know that prajñāpāramitā is the secret mantra."

The mantra of prajñāpāramitā is then spoken:

*Gate gate pāragate pārasaṃgate bodhi svāhā*

"Śāriputra! Bodhisattva-mahāsattvas should study the profound prajñāpāramitā in this way."

At that time, the World-Honored One arose from that absorption and addressed the holy Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Avalokiteśvara: "Excellent, excellent! Good man! It is so, it is so, as you have spoken. One should study prajñāpāramitā in just this way. All Tathāgatas will also rejoice."

When the Bhagavān had spoken these words, the venerable Śāriputra, the holy Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Avalokiteśvara, and all the devas, humans, asuras, gandharvas, and others of the world, hearing what the Buddha taught, were all greatly joyful, and they accepted it with faith and practiced it.
