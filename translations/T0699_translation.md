# 佛說造塔功德經
## Sūtra of the Merit of Building Stūpas, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0699

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Divākara (地婆訶羅)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was in the Trāyastriṃśa heaven palace, upon a white jade seat, together with great bhikṣus, great bodhisattvas, and the lord of that heaven along with an innumerable assembly. At that time, Mahābrahmā, the Deva King, Nārāyaṇa, Maheśvara, and five gandharva kings, each with their retinues, came to the Buddha's place, wishing to ask the Tathāgata about the method of building stūpas and the measure of merit that stūpas produce.

In the assembly there was a bodhisattva named Avalokiteśvara, who knew their intention. He immediately rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, placed his right knee on the ground, joined his palms toward the Buddha, and said: "World-Honored One! These devas and gandharvas have come here to request the Tathāgata to teach the method of building stūpas and the measure of merit that stūpas produce. I humbly wish the World-Honored One to explain this for them, for the benefit of all innumerable sentient beings!"

At that time the World-Honored One told the Bodhisattva Avalokiteśvara: "Good man! If any of these devas here present, or any sentient beings of the future, in whatever region where there is no stūpa, are able to build one: its form may be lofty and wondrous, surpassing the three realms, or at the very smallest, like an āmalaka fruit. Its finial may reach to the Brahmā heaven, or at the very smallest, be like a needle. Its parasol-wheel may cover the great-thousand world, or at the very smallest, be like a jujube leaf. Within that stūpa, one should enshrine the Tathāgata's relics, hair, teeth, beard, or nails, down to even a single portion; or place therein the Tathāgata's dharma-treasury, the twelve divisions of scripture, down to even a single four-line verse. The merit of that person will be like that of Brahmā: at the end of their life they will be born in the Brahmā world. When their life there ends, they will be born in the five pure abodes, equal to the devas there, without any difference. Good man! What I have taught is the measure of merit from building stūpas, its causes and conditions. You devas should study and practice this!"

At that time the Bodhisattva Avalokiteśvara again said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! Regarding what you have said about placing relics and the dharma-treasury within the stūpa, I have already received and upheld this. But I am not certain of the meaning of the Tathāgata's four-line verse. I humbly wish you to explain it in detail for me!"

At that time the World-Honored One spoke this verse:

> All dharmas arise from conditions;
> I have taught those conditions.
> When the conditions are exhausted, there is cessation:
> This is what I teach.

"Good man! The meaning of this verse is called the Dharma-body of the Buddha. You should write it and place it within the stūpa. Why? Because all conditions and the dharmas that arise from them are empty and quiescent in nature. For this reason I call it the Dharma-body. If any sentient being understands the meaning of these conditions, you should know that such a person has seen the Buddha."

At that time, the Bodhisattva Avalokiteśvara, the devas, the entire great assembly, and the gandharvas, having heard what the Buddha said, were all filled with great joy, faithfully received it, and practiced it.
