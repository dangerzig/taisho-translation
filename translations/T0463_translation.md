# 佛說文殊師利般涅槃經
## Sūtra of Mañjuśrī's Parinirvāṇa Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0463

Translated from the Chinese. Western Jin dynasty, translated by Nie Daozhen (聶道真)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the country of Śrāvastī, in the Jetavana grove of Anāthapiṇḍada's park, together with eight thousand great bhikṣu monks. The Elder Śāriputra, Mahāmaudgalyāyana, Mahākāśyapa, and Mahākātyāyana were the leaders of the assembly. There were also sixteen bodhisattva-mahāsattvas, with Maitreya as their leader among the thousand bodhisattvas of the Fortunate Kalpa. There were also one thousand two hundred bodhisattvas from other lands, with Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva as their leader.

At that time, during the last watch of the night, the World-Honored One entered a samādhi called Universal Radiance. Having entered this samādhi, his entire body emitted a golden radiance, great and brilliant, illuminating the Jeta Grove so that it appeared entirely golden. The light circled and turned, illuminating Mañjuśrī's quarters, where it transformed into seven tiers of golden terraces. On each terrace, five hundred transformation-buddhas walked in meditation. Before Mañjuśrī's quarters, there spontaneously appeared five hundred seven-jeweled lotus flowers, round as chariot wheels, with silver stems, agate pedestals, and variously colored true pearls for their filaments. These flowers emitted light that illuminated the Buddha's monastery, and from the monastery the light returned to Mañjuśrī's quarters.

At that time, in the assembly there was a bodhisattva-mahāsattva named Bhadrapāla. When this auspicious sign appeared, Bhadrapāla came out of his quarters, paid homage at the Buddha's monastery, went to Ānanda's quarters, and told Ānanda: "You should know the time. Tonight the World-Honored One has displayed signs of supernatural power. In order to benefit sentient beings, he will teach the wondrous Dharma. You should sound the gandhāra bell."

At that time, Ānanda said: "Great One! The World-Honored One has now entered deep dhyāna concentration. Without a command from him, how can I assemble the community?"

As he was speaking, Śāriputra arrived at Ānanda's quarters and said: "Dharma-brother! It is time to assemble the community."

At that time, Ānanda entered the Buddha's monastery and bowed to the Buddha. Before he could raise his head, a voice from the sky told Ānanda: "Swiftly assemble the sangha."

Having heard this, Ānanda was greatly delighted. He struck the gandhāra bell to assemble the community, and the sound spread throughout the country of Śrāvastī and ascended to the Bhavāgra heaven. Śakra, Brahmā, the Guardian Kings of the World, and countless deva princes brought heavenly flowers and incense to the Jeta Grove.

At that time, the World-Honored One rose from samādhi and smiled. Five-colored light emerged from the Buddha's mouth. When this light appeared, the Jetavana monastery was transformed into lapis lazuli.

At that time, the Dharma-Prince Mañjuśrī entered the Buddha's monastery and bowed to the Buddha. Upon each of his knees five lotus flowers appeared. When Mañjuśrī joined his palms before the Buddha, from the tips of his ten fingers and the lines of his palms there emerged ten thousand golden lotus flowers, which he scattered upon the Buddha. They transformed into a great canopy of the seven treasures, from which hung all manner of banners and streamers, and all the Buddhas and bodhisattvas of the ten directions without number were reflected within the canopy. He circumambulated the Buddha three times and stood to one side.

At that time, Bhadrapāla rose from his seat, straightened his garments, bowed to the Buddha, knelt, joined his palms, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! This Dharma-Prince Mañjuśrī has already drawn near to hundreds of thousands of Buddhas. In this Sahā world he has performed the Buddha's work. Displaying transformations at will in the ten directions, after a long time in the future he will enter parinirvāṇa."

The Buddha told Bhadrapāla: "This Mañjuśrī possesses great compassion. He was born in this country, in the village of Tārā, in a brahmin family called Brahmadatta. At his birth, the house transformed to resemble a lotus flower. He emerged from his mother's right side. His body was the color of purple gold. Upon touching the ground, he could speak, like a heavenly child. A canopy of the seven treasures accompanied and covered him. He went to the seers and sought the method of renunciation. Among the ninety-five kinds of brahmins and the various debate-masters, none could match him. Only under my guidance did he leave home and study the path. He abides in the Śūraṅgama Samādhi. Through the power of this samādhi, in the ten directions he manifests birth, renunciation, death, entry into parinirvāṇa, and the distribution of relics, all for the benefit of sentient beings. This great being has long abided in the Śūraṅgama Samādhi. Four hundred and fifty years after the Buddha's nirvāṇa, he will go to the Snowy Mountains. For the sake of five hundred seers, he will proclaim, expound, and elaborate upon the twelve divisions of the sūtras. He will teach, mature, and transform the five hundred seers, causing them to attain non-retrogression. Together with the host of seers, he will take on the appearance of bhikṣus, fly through the sky, and return to his birthplace. In the empty wilderness, beneath a nyagrodha tree, he will sit cross-legged and enter the Śūraṅgama Samādhi. Through the power of this samādhi, from every pore of his body there will emerge golden light. This light will pervade and illuminate the worlds of the ten directions, liberating all those with whom he has a karmic connection. The five hundred seers will each see fire emerging from the pores of his body. At that time, Mañjuśrī's body will be like a mountain of purple gold, sixteen feet tall, with a circular halo of adornment, one fathom on each side. Within the circular halo there will be five hundred transformation-buddhas, each with five transformation-bodhisattvas as attendants. Mañjuśrī's crown will be adorned with a vaiḍūrya jewel of five hundred colors. Within each color, the sun, the moon, the stars, the palaces of devas and nāgas, and all the rare sights of the world will appear. The white hair between his brows, turning rightward, will pour forth transformation-buddhas that enter the net of light. His entire body's radiance will blaze in succession, and within each flame there will be five maṇi jewels. Each maṇi jewel will have its own different light and different color, clearly distinct. Within the various colors, the transformation-buddhas and bodhisattvas will be beyond description. In his left hand he will hold a bowl; in his right hand he will hold a Mahāyāna sūtra. Having manifested these marks, the light and fire will all cease, and he will transform into a lapis lazuli image. On his left arm there will be ten buddha-seals, and within each seal ten buddha-images, with the names of the Buddhas clearly visible. On his right arm there will be seven buddha-seals, and within each seal seven buddha-images, with the names of the seven Buddhas clearly visible. Within his heart there will be a true golden image, sitting cross-legged, six feet tall, upon a lotus flower, visible from all four directions."

The Buddha told Bhadrapāla: "This Mañjuśrī possesses immeasurable supernatural powers and immeasurable transformations, which cannot be fully recorded. I have now briefly spoken for the sake of the blind and deluded beings of the future. If there are sentient beings who merely hear the name Mañjuśrī, the sins of twelve billion kalpas of birth and death will be removed. If there are those who bow and make offerings to him, in every life wherever they are born they will always be born into the families of Buddhas, and will be protected by Mañjuśrī's awesome spiritual power. Therefore, sentient beings should diligently focus their thoughts and contemplate Mañjuśrī's image. The method of contemplating Mañjuśrī's image is to first contemplate the lapis lazuli image, visualizing it point by point as described above, making each detail clear. If one has not yet been able to see it, one should recite and uphold the Śūraṅgama Dhāraṇī and call upon the name of Mañjuśrī from one day to seven days. Mañjuśrī will certainly come to that person's place. If there are those with heavy karmic obstructions from past lives, they may see him in a dream. Those who see him in a dream, in their present body, if they seek the śrāvaka path, by having seen Mañjuśrī will attain the fruit of stream-entry up to the fruit of non-returning. If a renunciant sees Mañjuśrī, by virtue of having seen him, they will become an arhat within a single day and night. If there are those with deep faith in the vaipulya sūtras, this Dharma-Prince, while in dhyāna concentration, will teach them the profound Dharma. For those whose minds are scattered, in their dreams he will teach the true meaning, causing them to be firm and steadfast, and attain non-retrogression from the unsurpassed path."

The Buddha told Bhadrapāla: "This Dharma-Prince Mañjuśrī: if there are people who think of him, or wish to make offerings and cultivate merit, he will transform his own body and appear as one who is poor, orphaned, lonely, and suffering, and come before the practitioner. If there are people who think of Mañjuśrī, they should practice compassion. To practice compassion is itself to see Mañjuśrī. Therefore the wise should carefully contemplate Mañjuśrī's thirty-two marks and eighty minor characteristics. Those who contemplate in this way, through the power of the Śūraṅgama Samādhi, will swiftly see Mañjuśrī. Such contemplation is called correct contemplation. Any other contemplation is called wrong contemplation. After the Buddha's passing, all sentient beings who hear the name of Mañjuśrī, or see his image, will for a hundred thousand kalpas not fall into the evil paths. If there are those who receive, uphold, read, and recite the name of Mañjuśrī, even if they have heavy obstructions, they will not fall into the Avīci Hell of fierce fire. They will always be born in other pure lands, encounter the Buddhas, hear the Dharma, and attain the patience of non-arising."

When these words were spoken, five hundred bhikṣus were freed from dust and defilement and attained arhatship. Countless devas gave rise to the bodhi-mind and vowed to always follow Mañjuśrī.

At that time, Bhadrapāla addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! Who will build a seven-jeweled stūpa over the relics of Mañjuśrī?"

The Buddha told Bhadrapāla: "On the Fragrant Mountain there are eight great yakṣa spirits. They will carry the relics away and place them atop the Vajra Peak on the Fragrant Mountain. Countless devas, nāgas, spirits, and yakṣas will constantly come to make offerings. When the great assembly gathers, the image will constantly emit light, and that light will proclaim the Dharma of suffering, emptiness, impermanence, and non-self. Bhadrapāla! This Dharma-Prince has attained the indestructible body. I now tell you: receive and uphold this teaching well, and broadly proclaim it for all sentient beings."

When these words were spoken, Bhadrapāla and all the great bodhisattvas, Śāriputra and all the great śrāvakas, and the devas, nāgas, and the eightfold assembly heard what the Buddha had taught, all greatly rejoiced, bowed to the Buddha, and departed.
