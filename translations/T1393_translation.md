# 佛說摩尼羅亶經
## The Buddha's Teaching: Maṇiratan Sūtra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1393

Translated from the Chinese. Eastern Jin dynasty, translated by Dharmarakṣa/Tánwúlán (曇無蘭).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was in the country of Śrāvastī, in the Jeta Grove at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park. At that time, with the great bhikṣu-saṃgha, he was teaching the Maṇiratan Sūtra. The Buddha asked Ānanda: "The people of the world lack peace and security. For what reason? The multitudes of the world are afflicted by many diseases and pains. For what reason? Because of pain upon leaving the mother's womb, the pain of heartbreak, headaches, dizziness, inability to eat and drink: all these are caused by māras." At that time the bhikṣus were greatly terrified, and they came before the Buddha and said: "From where does this pain come, and where does it go? The people are filled with grief and unhappiness." The Buddha assembled the bhikṣus: Mahākāśyapa, Anuruddha, Revata, Mahāmaudgalyāyana, Śāriputra, Ānanda, and Indra.

The Buddha then taught the Maṇiratan Sūtra. The Buddha pronounced the names of the buddhas: the first, Vipaśyin Buddha; the second, Śikhin Buddha; the third, Viśvabhū Buddha; the fourth, Krakucchanda Buddha; the fifth, Kanakamuni Buddha; the sixth, Kāśyapa Buddha; the seventh, Śākyamuni Buddha. This sūtra has come forth from the mouths of all these buddhas.

The first, Śikṣarā; the second, Gandharā; the third, Gandhurā; the fourth, Mīkharā; the fifth, Gañjurā; the sixth, Mayūrā; the seventh, Asura; the eighth, Suiśramaṇa; the ninth, Suisun; the tenth, Ti.

For all the people, great and small, who suffer from illness, the Buddha now commands all bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, and upāsikās to recite this sūtra.

There are ghosts in the country, ghosts in the mountains, ghosts in the forests, ghosts in the grasses at dusk, ghosts among the tombs, ghosts within tombs, ghosts upon the earth, ghosts in the water, ghosts beside the water, ghosts in the fire, ghosts beside the fire, ghosts of the Northern Dipper, ghosts in the sky, ghosts of the marketplace, ghosts of the dead, ghosts of the living, hungry ghosts, ghosts outside the road, ghosts on the road, ghosts outside the hall, ghosts inside the hall, ghosts within the body, ghosts outside the body, ghosts at mealtime, ghosts at bedtime. The Buddha now says there are red-colored ghosts, black-colored ghosts, tall ghosts, short ghosts, great ghosts, small ghosts, medium ghosts, white-colored ghosts, yellow-colored ghosts, blue-colored ghosts, dark-colored ghosts, ghosts of nightmares, ghosts of the dawn, ghosts that walk, ghosts that fly, ghosts that question people's spirits, ghosts of the living, and ghosts of the dead.

The Buddha said: "When anger, swords, and staves arise, one should recite this Maṇiratan Sūtra, and all ghosts and spirits will be shattered."

The Buddha addressed the bhikṣus: "If anyone who has received this sūtra becomes sick, they should recite this sūtra. If there are headaches, dizziness, fevers and chills, or heartbreak, one should constantly recite this Maṇiratan Sūtra, and the heads of the ghosts and spirits will split into seven pieces.

"If there is trouble with officials, bandits, water, or fire, one should recite this Maṇiratan Sūtra, and the ghosts and spirits will no longer be able to harass and harm people. This sūtra has come forth from the mouths of the buddhas. If there are ghosts in the country, the first named Shenshe, the second named Fuqiu, these two ghosts are vigorous in seeking out people's faults. If there are headaches, dizziness, fevers and chills, or heartbreak, one should pronounce the names of these two ghosts and then recite the Maṇiratan Sūtra. Not one among these ghosts and spirits will not be shattered.

"If there are blue-colored ghosts, yellow-colored ghosts, black-colored ghosts, tall and great ghosts, low and small ghosts, broad and long ghosts, or any ghosts and spirits of any size who delight in harassing the people of the world, and whose names are: the gold-wreath ghost, the vetāḍa ghost, the hungry ghost, the miserly ghost, the toil ghost, the sickness ghost, the itching ghost, the thought ghost, the ghost within the body, the ghost outside the body, the disfigured ghost, the lame ghost, the madness ghost, the deaf and stupid ghost, the mute ghost, the moaning ghost, the weeping ghost, the idle-illness ghost, the wasting ghost, the jealousy ghost, the phantasm ghost, the will-o'-the-wisp ghost, the wandering-light ghost, the spell-binding ghost, the curse ghost, the hidden-corpse-haunting ghost, the leprosy-death-haunting ghost, the government-building-haunting ghost, the military-camp ghost, the post-station ghost, the prison-death ghost, the prisoner-death ghost, the water-death ghost, the drowning ghost, the fire-death ghost, the burning ghost, the traveler-death-unburied ghost, the market-death ghost, the road-death ghost, the thirst-death ghost, the starvation ghost, the choking ghost, the freezing ghost, the battle-death ghost, the blood-death ghost, the gore ghost, the fugitive-prayer ghost, the fighting ghost, the cudgeling ghost, the strangling ghost, the self-hanging ghost, the self-stabbing ghost, the vendetta ghost, the violent-death ghost, the rotting-skin ghost, the hair-cutting ghost, the blood-drinking ghost, the flying ghost, the riding ghost, the carriage ghost, the walking ghost, the encounter ghost, the mountain-spirit ghost, the stone-spirit ghost, the earth-spirit ghost, the sea-shore ghost, the sea ghost, the bridge ghost, the ditch ghost, the road ghost, the off-road ghost, the barbarian ghost, the foreign-captive ghost, the tree-spirit ghost, the insect-spirit ghost, the bird-and-beast-spirit ghost, the valley ghost, the door ghost, the outside-door ghost, the inner-door ghost, the outer-door ghost, the well-and-stove ghost, the dirty-pool ghost, the privy ghost, the regional-sorcery ghost, the gū-poison ghost, the insubordinate ghost, and the impersonator ghost: all ghosts and spirits, great and small, are not permitted to harass or harm their bodies. Any ghost or spirit that does not obey my words will have its head split into seven pieces.

"If a person becomes sick, one should pronounce the names of all these ghosts and spirits listed above, and recite the mantra over the sick person, and the illness will be cured and removed. This sūtra has come forth from the mouth of Śākyamuni Buddha. All ghosts and spirits who hear this sūtra shall from this time forward be entirely destroyed and the illness healed."

When the Buddha had spoken this sūtra, the bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, and upāsikās, the devas, nāgas, ghosts, spirits, and the people all received the Buddha's grace, came before the Buddha, bowed in reverence, and departed.
