# 文殊師利菩薩六字呪功能法經
## Sūtra of the Methods and Powers of Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva's Six-Syllable Mantra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1179

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown.

---

Homage to the Honored One Who Benefits All, Mañjuśrī, the Youth, Bodhisattva Mahāsattva! The mantra says:

*Aṃ vakeda nama*

Regarding the methods and powers of this six-syllable mantra of Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva, I shall now explain. If any person recites this mantra seven times daily, their sins will certainly be extinguished. If any person can recite this mantra one hundred and eight times daily, that person at the time of death will see Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva directly before them.

If any person wishes to receive and uphold this mantra and attain accomplishment, they should first eat vegetables, or milk porridge, or barley gruel, or barley flour, or go begging for food. Each day at dawn, noon, and sunset, they should bathe three times, each time putting on a fresh set of clean clothes, and then recite this mantra six hundred thousand times; the dharma will then be accomplished.

Thereafter, on uncut felt that has not been severed, the painter should take the eight precepts, and without using glue, paint an image of Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva seated on a lotus in the teaching posture, fully adorned, in the form of a youth, with heavenly garments draped over the left shoulder.

On the right side, paint the holy Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, seated on a lotus, hand holding a whisk. On the left side, paint the holy Samantabhadra Bodhisattva. Above the image on both sides in the sky, paint mantra-sages amid clouds, their hands holding garlands. Below the painting, paint the mantra-holder, hand grasping a censer, gazing up at Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva. On all four sides of the image, paint mountain peaks. Below the bodhisattvas' seats, paint a flower-pool filled with lotuses.

Then, before a śarīra stūpa, set up the painting facing west and make extensive offerings. Burn ghee lamps. Take one thousand and eight jāti flowers, empower each one with a single recitation, and scatter them before the image. A sound will emerge: one will hear a wondrous sound, or the painting will move. If one hears the wondrous sound, one's wishes will be fulfilled. If the painting moves, one will gain victory in all disputes, mastery of all worldly teachings, and the love of all beings. If one does not accomplish this method, all other rites will fail to succeed.

Another method: take aloeswood eight finger-widths in length, of any thickness, and anoint it with sumanā fragrant oil. On smokeless khadira wood charcoal, empower each piece with one recitation. Burn them from the first watch of the night until the dawn light appears. One will then see Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva coming in person before the mantra-master, and whatever one seeks will be fulfilled, with the sole exception of sexual desires.

Another method: before the painting, burn sandalwood day and night without ceasing while reciting this mantra. Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva will come in person, teach the profoundly deep dharma, one will attain supreme understanding, be forever free from all illness and gain mastery, and enter the bodhisattva stages.

Another method: take purple sandalwood six finger-widths in length and carve it into a lotus. Then grind more purple sandalwood into powder, mix it with water, and anoint the lotus. Recite one hundred thousand times. On the night of the fifteenth, when the moon is full, before the painting, take the flower-petals and, holding the flowers in both hands, recite the mantra until light emerges from the crown of the head. Then take that flower and it will cause one to become like a person of sixteen years, with a beautiful and dignified form, golden radiance surpassing a thousand suns, and the majestic light of a celestial youth. All accomplishments will be attained, all mantra-sages will pay homage, and one's life will span one kalpa. After the kalpa ends, one will be reborn in the Land of Ultimate Bliss.

Another method: during a lunar eclipse, take white calamus root and wash it in a mixture of the five products of the cow: dung, milk, yogurt, ghee, and urine. After washing, under a pipal tree leaf and fruit, recite until either smoke emerges, warmth appears, or flames arise. If warmth appears, one will gain the delight of all beings and victory in all debates. If smoke emerges, one will attain the power of invisibility and a lifespan of ten thousand years. If flames arise, one will attain the power of sky-walking and a lifespan of one great kalpa.

Another method: take ghee from a cow with a calf, place it in a wrought copper vessel, cover it with seven pipal leaves, and empower it with recitations. As above, three results may arise from smoke and the rest. Then consume the ghee, and one will attain either great learning, the power of invisibility, or the power of sky-walking.

Another method: during a lunar eclipse, place a budañkara seed in the sun and hold it in the mouth while reciting this mantra. When the seed moves, take three kinds of copper, fashion a bead with a hole, and place the seed inside. Hold it in the mouth and one becomes invisible; remove it and one becomes visible again.

Another method: take cloves and hold them in the mouth while reciting this mantra six hundred thousand times. Thereafter, whatever one requests from anyone will be obtained.

Another method: eating milk and barley, recite the mantra one million two hundred thousand times, and one will become a mantra-sage.

Another method: begging for food and maintaining silence, recite one hundred thousand times, and one will attain the power of invisibility. If one recites one koṭi times, one will see the holy Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva teaching the dharma, and will certainly attain entry among the great bodhisattvas of the ekajāti-pratibaddha rank.

If one constantly recites this mantra, all blessings will be attained and all endeavors will be accomplished.

Another method: take benzoin, pound it to powder, mix with ghee to form pills the size of small jujubes, making one hundred thousand pills. Empower each with one recitation and burn them in fire until all are consumed. One hundred thousand gold coins will appear.

Another method: in a great river flowing into the sea, take ten thousand lotuses, empower each with one recitation, and cast them into the river until all are gone. Treasure troves equal to the number of those lotuses will appear, and their riches may be used without end.

Another method: mix sesame with yogurt and ghee, and empower each handful with one recitation, placing it in the fire, up to one hundred thousand times. That person will attain great wealth and be able to give freely of all things.

Another method: take fresh cow dung, plaster the ground to make an altar, scatter various flowers upon it and sit down. Each day recite the mantra one thousand and eight times, then read the Mahāyāna sūtras. After one full month, one will attain great wisdom.

Another method: take gorocana, empower it one thousand and eight times, and apply it to the forehead. All beings who see that person will be filled with delight. Empower the hair on the crown of the head seven times and tie it in a knot; all evil persons will be unable to cause harm.

Another method: take ten thousand kicimara fruits, empower each with one recitation, and place them in the fire until all are consumed. All manner of illness will be entirely healed.

Colophon: Copied from the manuscript of Takao-san Jingo-ji at the hermitage of the Daiho-on-ji in the northern hills of Kyoto, at the gathering of the Transmitted Teachings. The Record of Eight Houses states: "Six-Syllable Divine Mantra Sūtra, one fascicle." A note says: "Also called 'Six-Syllable Mantra Sūtra.' The Zhenyuan and Bonshaku editions lack it." A further note says: "Also called 'Six-Syllable Mantra Method,' titled 'Methods and Powers of Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva's Six-Syllable [Mantra].'" The Usagusa-shi, fascicle two, cites this sūtra.
