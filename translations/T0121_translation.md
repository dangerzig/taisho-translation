# 佛說月喻經
## Sūtra of the Moon Simile Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0121

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dānapāla (施護)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the World-Honored One was in the city of Rājagṛha, in the Kalandaka Bamboo Grove Monastery, together with an assembly of bhikṣus. At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣus: "As is seen in the world, the bright moon, full and round, travels through the sky, pure and unobstructed. Bhikṣus who do not violate proper deportment, who are always like a newly ordained monk, who are fully endowed with a sense of shame and conscience, whose body and mind are never scattered or confused, who enter the homes of laypeople in accordance with the Dharma's manner, pure and undefiled: they are just like this. Bhikṣus! Further, it is like a person with clear eyes who enters deep and wide waters, or traverses the dangerous places of rivers, or walks over the heights and depths of mountain cliffs. Because of clear eyes, one can see everything and is free from doubt and fear. As I have described, a bhikṣu is likewise. Bhikṣus! What I now teach is like the moon traveling through the sky, pure and unobstructed. Like the person with clear eyes who traverses various dangers, free from doubt and fear: the bhikṣu Kāśyapa does not violate proper deportment, is always like a newly ordained monk, is fully endowed with a sense of shame and conscience, whose body and mind are never scattered or confused, who enters the homes of laypeople in accordance with the Dharma's manner, pure and undefiled, free from all timidity and fear, just like this."

At that time, the World-Honored One again addressed the bhikṣus: "Bhikṣus! When you enter a layperson's home, what mind should you arouse? With what characteristic should you enter?"

The bhikṣus addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! The Buddha is our refuge, the Buddha is the root of all dharmas, the Buddha is the pure eye. We do not know the meaning of this. We wish the World-Honored One to explain it well, so that the bhikṣus, having heard, may understand."

The Buddha said: "Bhikṣus! Listen carefully and attend well. I shall now teach you. When bhikṣus wish to enter a layperson's home, they should arouse a mind of non-attachment, non-bondage, and non-grasping. Relying on the characteristic of disciplined conduct, they enter the home. Although receiving offerings, they should wish only to create merit for those people, accepting only in proportion to what they themselves have received. Furthermore, attending well, they should not exalt themselves or look down upon others. Arousing such a mind, with such a characteristic, they should enter a layperson's home."

At that time, the World-Honored One raised his hand and touched the sky, and addressed the assembly of bhikṣus: "What do you think? Is space attached? Is it bound? Does it grasp?"

The bhikṣus said: "No, World-Honored One!"

The Buddha said: "If a bhikṣu enters a layperson's home with a mind of non-attachment, non-bondage, and non-grasping, it is also like this."

At that time, the World-Honored One again raised his hand and touched the sky, and addressed the assembly of bhikṣus: "Bhikṣus! What do you think? Is space attached? Is it bound? Does it grasp?"

The bhikṣus said: "No, World-Honored One!"

The Buddha said: "The bhikṣu Kāśyapa is also like this. With a mind of non-attachment, non-bondage, and non-grasping, he enters the homes of laypeople. Although receiving offerings, he wishes only to create merit for those people, accepting only in proportion to what he himself has received. Furthermore, attending well, he does not exalt himself or look down upon others. Bhikṣus! For this reason, one like the bhikṣu Kāśyapa is worthy of entering laypeople's homes and is worthy of receiving offerings."

At that time, the bhikṣus again addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! When bhikṣus teach the Dharma to laypeople, some do so purely and some impurely. How is this? We wish the World-Honored One to explain it well."

The Buddha said: "Bhikṣus! Listen carefully and attend well. I shall now teach you. If a bhikṣu, wishing to cause others to give rise to faith and to perform acts of faith in providing robes, food and drink, seats and bedding, and medicine for illness, teaches the Dharma to others for the sake of such gain, this is impure. But if a bhikṣu, regarding the Dharma taught by the Buddha, abides in right view, free from all defilement, like refining true gold and removing the ore's impurities, sees such Dharma, realizes such Dharma, as the Buddha himself aroused it, knowing that this Dharma can free one from birth, old age, illness, death, sorrow, grief, suffering, and affliction, and with this Dharma teaches others, causing them to hear such Dharma and practice accordingly, so that through the long night they attain great benefit and happiness: for this reason one generates a mind of loving-kindness, compassion, and equality. Because of this, the Buddha's true Dharma endures long in the world. Bhikṣus! If one arouses such a mind and teaches the Dharma to others, this is pure.

"Furthermore, bhikṣus! You should know that the bhikṣu Kāśyapa is able to arouse a pure mind and teach the Dharma to others. Because of this purity, the Buddha's true Dharma endures long in the world. Therefore, you bhikṣus should also do likewise, training properly in this way.

"Furthermore, bhikṣus! If there is one who can arouse such a mind and teach the Dharma to others, I declare this to be the supreme purity and truth, able to cause the Tathāgata's true Dharma to endure long in the world."

The Buddha, having spoken this sūtra, the assembly of bhikṣus joyfully and faithfully received it.
