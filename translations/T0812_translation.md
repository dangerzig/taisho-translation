# 菩薩行五十緣身經
## Sūtra of the Fifty Conditions of the Bodhisattva's Body

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0812

Translated from the Chinese. Western Jin dynasty, translated by Dharmarakṣa (竺法護)

---

The Buddha was in Rājagṛha, on Mount Gṛdhrakūṭa, together with an assembly of twelve hundred and fifty bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, upāsikās, and countless devas, nāgas, ghosts, and spirits, along with a hundred thousand bodhisattvas who had come from the ten directions, all seated naturally upon lion-thrones of seven-jeweled lotus flowers.

The bodhisattvas of the ten directions beheld the Buddha, upright and beautiful beyond compare, his body possessing the thirty-two marks and eighty secondary characteristics. Among the assembly there was a bodhisattva named Jñānaśrī, who addressed the Bodhisattva Mañjuśrī: "Venerable one! You who know the profound sūtras, can you yourself know by what causes and conditions the Buddha adorned his body and attained merit and virtue such as this?" The bodhisattvas of the ten directions, hearing these words, were all filled with joy.

Mañjuśrī then rose, came forward, knelt, and addressed the Buddha: "Among the bodhisattvas in this assembly, some have attained the stage of non-retrogression and some have not yet attained it, yet all behold the Buddha's body with its thirty-two marks and eighty secondary characteristics, adorning his person with beauty beyond compare. By what causes and conditions did you attain this? We pray the Buddha will explain the meritorious deeds of past lives for the bodhisattvas, so that hearing them they may further increase their merit and virtue."

The Buddha said: "Excellent, excellent! Mañjuśrī! That you have given rise to such an intention to ask this question!"

The Buddha said: "Listen as I explain the meritorious deeds of past lives.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva gave to others what he most dearly loved, his rarest and finest possessions, always giving with kind eyes and a good heart, for this reason the Buddha fully attained wisdom and knowledge of all the sūtras and dharmas.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva, with a good heart, gave courtesans, precious jewels, and adornments to others, for this reason the Buddha's voice issues forth in ten thousand kinds of sounds.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva always looked upon the people of the world with good wishes and a kind eye, for this reason people behold the Buddha and never tire of looking, just as the full moon on the fifteenth day is beautiful and lovely and one gazes at it without satiation.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva taught the sūtras and Dharma to others without any expectation of gain, wishing only that they find peace, for this reason when the Buddha teaches, those who hear are never satiated or weary.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva never spoke of the faults of others, and even when there were faults he did not tell other people, for this reason dust and grime do not cling to the Buddha's body.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva constantly endured humiliation, and though he had the strength to retaliate he always bore it patiently, for this reason when the Buddha walks, the high ground lowers and the low ground rises.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva, seeing people in distress, provided for and sustained them, and seeing people in ruin, settled them peacefully in their livelihood, for this reason when the Buddha walks upon the path, the earth displays spiritual power like a joyful countenance.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva never made bows, crossbows, swords, or weapons that caused people to harm one another, for this reason when the Buddha walks, thorns, broken tiles, stones, and mounds all clear themselves away.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva, having heard the Dharma from his teacher, never dared to transgress or be deficient, for this reason all the Buddha's endeavors are fully and completely accomplished.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva delighted in lighting lamps before the Buddhas, before his teachers, and before his parents, and when others had doubts he would resolve them, for this reason the Buddha's body radiates brilliant light, surpassingly beautiful and beyond compare, with majestic spiritual authority such as this.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva, when he saw a servant explaining the sūtras, did not scold or stop them but even listened himself, for this reason the Buddha speaks the sūtras without stumbling or error.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva never looked at anyone with hostile eyes, and even when anger arose he gradually restrained it, for this reason the Buddha's eyes are neither too large nor too small, drawn long and beautiful, and all who see the Buddha are filled with joy.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva, when he saw other men's beautiful wives, never directed lustful thoughts toward them, for this reason the Buddha's countenance and complexion are surpassingly beautiful and beyond compare, and all who see him are filled with joy.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva offered seasonal ripe fruits, fine incense, and flowers to the Buddhas, to the bhikṣu Saṃgha, to his teachers, and to his parents, for this reason fruit trees and flowering plants, upon seeing the Buddha, all bend and incline toward him.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva leveled rough terrain and bad roads, and where there were no bridges he built bridges, and never used money to coerce or oppress people, for this reason when the Buddha enters a place, low doorways become tall.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva, seeing empty, desolate places with no wells or trees, dug wells and planted trees, and provided various food and drink so that people could eat, for this reason wherever the Buddha walks, the earth sends forth springs, and the water issues forth with eight flavors.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva never spoke ill of people's conduct and behavior, for this reason the Buddha's entire body is beautiful and without blemish or flaw.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva anointed the Buddha's body with various mixed incenses and, with a good heart, offered them to the Buddha and applied them to the stūpa, for this reason the devas create a fragrant wind whose perfume makes offerings to the Buddha.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva offered various fragrant water to the Buddha and bodhisattvas for washing the face, along with willow-twigs for cleaning the teeth, for this reason the Buddha's face and mouth are all fragrant, like the seal of a treasure-house newly opened.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva, when he saw someone harboring anger toward a bodhisattva, always treated that person with kindness, for this reason when the Buddha walks upon the path, even insects and ants beneath his feet are brought to peace, such is the Buddha's majestic spiritual authority.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva offered his lands, fine incense, flowers, and robes to the Buddha, for this reason when someone scatters flowers upon the Buddha, they become a canopy of flowers.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva never destroyed people's dwellings and always delighted in building homes, for this reason the Buddha's body is of vajra-like strength, firm as a mountain on all four sides, and nothing can harm the Buddha's body.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva, seeing people who were hungry and thirsty, first fed them and only afterward taught them the sūtras, for this reason fragrant flowers and flowing water are always present before the Buddha's dwelling.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva, when people came seeking something, gave whatever he had without cherishing it, for this reason when the Buddha teaches the sūtras, all who hear are benefited without any futile suffering, and for this reason the Buddha subdues all māras.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva offered fine music to the Buddha and stūpas, for this reason when the Buddha teaches the sūtras for his disciples, all throughout the Buddha-realm can hear it fully.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva made trees of gold, silver, and various jewels and offered them to the Buddha, for this reason none among the devas, nāgas, ghosts, or spirits can see the crown of the Buddha's head.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva made stūpas for the Buddha and anointed them with various incenses, for this reason wherever the Buddha walks, precious jewels and fragrant flowers are scattered upon the Buddha from above.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva, seeing someone who had been poisoned, always went with a compassionate heart to heal them, for this reason wherever the Buddha walks, whether people or trees have been poisoned, all are cured.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva always followed the sūtras and Dharma without transgression, his mind always gentle and patient toward others, for this reason when anyone, even one who is mad and confused, comes before the Buddha, they are brought to peace.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva offered various kinds of incense to the Buddha's relics and anointed stūpas with them, for this reason the Buddha's body has no foul odor, no blemish or defilement, and calamity and misfortune cannot touch the Buddha's body, such is the Buddha's majestic spiritual authority.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva, when people were troubled by doubts or possessed by ghosts and spirits, always went to rescue and deliver them, for this reason wherever the Buddha goes, if there are insects and ants on the ground, they are all cleared away.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva served his teachers and parents, and if he saw them lying asleep he did not startle them awake repeatedly, but if he wished to wake them he would use music or kind words or sūtra-chanting to rouse them, for this reason when the Buddha is resting within in silence, the devas, Brahmā, and Śakra bring music and incense to rouse him.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva built fine monasteries for the Buddha and prepared good beds, bedding, and furnishings, for this reason mosquitoes, gnats, bees, snakes, fleas, lice, and such creatures do not dare approach the Buddha's body.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva, in the cold of winter, made fine and delicate garments for the Buddha and bodhisattvas, for this reason when the wind called Vairambhaka arises before the Buddha, it is neither cold nor hot, and does not even stir a single hair.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva, when he had rare and delicious food, never ate it alone, and when his teachers or parents had food he did not diminish or take their portions, for this reason even if the Buddha sets aside his spiritual powers and takes no food, the Buddha is still neither hungry nor thirsty, nor does his body become thin.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva never set fire to mountains or fields, and furthermore eliminated the three poisons in others, for this reason after the Buddha enters parinirvāṇa, though the fire blazes fiercely it cannot change the color of the Buddha's body.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva always offered to his teachers, parents, and persons of virtue fine dwellings, clothing, and food and drink, giving to any who came to beg, for this reason the devas, ghosts, spirits, and great elders bring all the precious jewels and treasures of the world as offerings to the Buddha.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva prepared the roads and paths for the Buddha, anointing the ground with fragrant oils, for this reason when the Buddha walks upon the path, beautiful and varied flowers are scattered in rows upon the ground before him.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva upheld the precepts without ever transgressing, and never taught others to transgress, for this reason when the wind called Vairambhaka arises from all four directions, it cannot stir a single hair of the Buddha.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva set up five-colored canopies for the Buddha along the path, and beneath them invited the Buddha and the bhikṣu Saṃgha for a meal, for this reason wherever the Buddha walks, the devas spread five-colored canopies and follow the Buddha, such is the Buddha's majestic spiritual authority.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva offered precious jeweled sandals to the Buddha with a good heart, for this reason when the Buddha walks, his feet are four inches above the ground and do not touch the earth, and the marks of his soles appear clearly.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva offered pennants, banners, flowers, and canopies of various five colors to the Buddha's stūpa, for this reason banners, pennants, and canopies of various colors arise naturally and follow the Buddha as he walks.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva prepared the roads for the Buddha, sprinkling the ground with various fragrant liquids, for this reason wherever the Buddha walks, the nāgas bring various fragrant liquids and sprinkle the ground.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva never spoke impure words or words that others would find offensive, and even if such words arose he controlled his mind and did not utter them, for this reason devas, nāgas, spirits, and flying birds do not pass above the Buddha.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva, when the Buddha or the bhikṣu Saṃgha approached, went out to support and welcome them and bowed in homage, for this reason wherever the Buddha walks, the devas, Brahmā, Śakra, asuras, ghosts, spirits, and the people of the world all come to welcome the Buddha, pressing their heads to the Buddha's feet.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva always guarded body, speech, and mind without committing any evil, not merely for himself but offering himself to the people of all the ten directions, for this reason when the bodhisattva is in his mother's womb, foul and impure secretions do not touch his body, and he is always at peace without fear, with nothing to disturb him.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva looked upon the Buddha with a good heart, and upon enemies and parents alike his mind was impartially the same without any difference, for this reason the Buddha's wisdom is fully complete, consisting entirely of wholesome qualities without any evil, and when anyone comes with doubts or difficult questions, the Buddha can resolve them all.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva offered various music, incense, and flowers to the Buddha and stūpas, for this reason when the Buddha enters a city, all the musical instruments in the city sound of themselves without being played.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva offered candles, lamps, gold, silver, and various jewels and ornaments to the Buddha and stūpas, to the bodhisattvas and bhikṣu Saṃgha, and to the people of the world, giving generously to all, always with a warm countenance and a joyful mind in conversation, for this reason when the Buddha goes on his alms-round, low doorways become tall.

"Because in life after life the bodhisattva erected open-sided pavilions for the Buddha, inviting the Buddha to dwell and walk beneath them, gazing upon the Buddha with a good heart and praising the Buddha's merit and virtue, for this reason the Buddha emits a radiance before which the sun, moon, and stars all grow dim, and nothing can withstand the Buddha's light, such is the Buddha's majestic spiritual authority."

The Buddha said: "When in past lives I was a bodhisattva, life after life my practices were like this, through numberless lifetimes. For the sake of people, I have roughly spoken of just a small portion of the merit and virtue. Through numberless lifetimes, knowing that all possessions are impermanent, I therefore gave them to others. The merit and virtue of what I accomplished life after life is such that neither Śakra, Brahmā, nor the devas could accomplish it; all of it was brought about by the meritorious deeds of past lives. Empty space may be traversed, Mount Sumeru may be weighed, the waters of the ocean may be measured, but the merit and virtue of the Buddha's deeds, accumulated over countless lifetimes, cannot be weighed or measured. If there is a person who single-mindedly contemplates the Buddha's merit and virtue, that person's blessings are immeasurable, to say nothing of one who bows in homage and offers praise! If there are those who wish to seek Buddhahood, they too should practice merit and virtue in this way."

When the Buddha had finished teaching the sūtra, the bodhisattvas and all the devas rejoiced, came forward, bowed their heads at the Buddha's feet, and departed.
