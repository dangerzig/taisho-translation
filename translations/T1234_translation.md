# 無能勝大明陀羅尼經
## Sūtra of the Aparājita Great Vidyā Dhāraṇī

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1234

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmadeva (法天).

---

> I bow in homage to the Perfectly Awakened One,
> The Dharma, and the assembly of worthy holy ones.
> The bodhisattva proclaimed this vidyā,
> And what was spoken destroys all obstacles.

The root spell-methods of vetāḍas and kaṭapūtanas, and all evil arts performed by yakṣas and rākṣasa hosts: this vidyā can destroy them all and constantly ensure right practice. If sentient beings with earnest sincerity and single mind receive and uphold this great vidyā, or write it on silk, or write it on birch bark, paper, or the like, then all evil ghosts and spirits everywhere will generate resolute compassion, eliminate disasters and obstacles, and accomplish the most supreme deeds. If one wears it upon the crown of the head and enters the battlefield where people are killing one another, it will provide protection: the mind will be free from confusion and fear, and not a single hair will be harmed. If one wears it upon the arm, swift victory will be obtained.

As the Buddha has said: "If good men or good women receive and uphold this vidyā, all their wishes will be accomplished. If imprisoned by royal law, if traveling through the wilderness and encountering dangers from lions, tigers, or wolves, if on the road encountering bandits who follow: by the power of this great vidyā they will be rescued and protected, freed from all fears, and will attain peace and happiness. If women wear it upon the crown of the head and uphold it, their family members will constantly be filled with love and respect, and they will always obtain wealth and treasures. If a woman suffers difficult childbirth, she will swiftly find peace. The great vidyā spell proclaimed by the bodhisattva provides protection day and night at all times. If wise persons single-mindedly uphold it, whether walking, standing, sitting, or lying down, ghost-spirits and all difficulties will be unable to cause harm, and even when intoxicated or asleep they will have no evil dreams. If this Aparājita vidyā is held and recited, all disputes among kings, villages, and people, and all obstacles, will be completely dispersed."

At that time, Aparājita Bodhisattva told the māras: "I now proclaim this vidyā. You bhūta hosts, listen to what I say. The bodhisattva, seeing those evil māra armies in various forms and colors standing before him, spoke to those māra armies thus: 'For koṭis of kalpas I have sought bodhi, giving up my own body and life, wife and children, wealth and treasures, doing what is hard to do, practicing great vigor, benefiting and bringing happiness to all sentient beings. With vows fulfilled, I abide at the bodhi tree, realizing the true dharma, earnestly cherishing it.' At that time a māra king led evil troops to create obstacles. By the power of the vidyā they were all subdued. What I contemplated were the māra companions of the past, who subdued them and rejoiced. Past enemies destroyed themselves. I now proclaim this vidyā to destroy the māra army." The great vidyā spell:

*Tadyathā huru-huru caṇḍa-rī-ma-daṃge svāhā*

When Aparājita Bodhisattva spoke this vidyā, she transformed her body into the form of a celestial woman, stood before the māras, and generated a mind of compassion. With palms joined above her head, she spoke these words:

> "The bodhisattva's heart is pure; as I proclaim the supreme meaning,
> Great wisdom and great radiance forever sever the darkness of ignorance.
> In the past, seeking bodhi, Māra's army is now subdued;
> Having subdued those māra hosts, great fearlessness was attained.
> Forever transcending the world, with certainty attaining perfect awakening,
> Becoming the teacher of devas and humans, swiftly liberating all beings.
> May all of you who see this generate great joy in your hearts.
> Māra King and all your retinue, do not tread the banks of the Nairañjanā.
> Now by the power of this vidyā, the covering of afflictions is destroyed."

At that time, Aparājita Bodhisattva, having subdued Māra King and his retinue, with all matters brought to completion and vows perfectly fulfilled, became the great teacher of compassion. Then she spoke this dhāraṇī:

*Tadyathā dhūpu-dhūpu-dhūru-dhūru-dhūmu-dhūmu iru-ci iru-ci iru-ci viru-ci viru-ci viru-ci iru-puru-śaṃkara piṅgale naṭa-naṭarī turu-turī hi-hi-hi-hi devā-varī devā-varī gūdu-carī gūdu-carī tanī-nī ahāmī-hāmī khaga-carī kerī-kecī kerī-kecī bhūtā-gṛma mama-maṣ-bhāmī dharmī-dharmī-dharmī na-se-na-se-na-se-na-se dhārī-dhārī dhūrī-dhūrī hanāmī-hanāmī vaṃ-varī varāya-pu-carā pu-carī carā-pu nandanīye kuru-kucī-kanī-kanī gana-varanī hū-hū-hū-hū kuru-kunnī kuru-sa-bhande kṣīrṇī gara-se mara-se gūrī-gandhārī caṇḍa-rle māda-ṃge gūgule-bumāṃga-se brahma-ṇī naṭanī triṇa-śayanī triṇa-pura-kṣīrṇī svāhā triṇa-vinādaranī siddhaye svāhā tadyathā hile-hile natiśya naṭa-vipu naja-raṃ-bhīte indrīyamī varuṇī sumāha-dhete svāhā*

"By the divine power of this great vidyā, the hosts of Great Brahmā and also śramaṇas, brāhmaṇas, and all beings of the world: devas, humans, and asura hosts will never again see the realm of the māras. Furthermore, this mantra can provide rescue and protection, benefit sentient beings, extinguish calamities, and neutralize all poisons, bringing peace and happiness. If there are humans and non-humans, celestial sons and celestial hosts and celestial women, asuras and asura hosts and asura women, gandharvas and gandharva hosts and gandharva women, nāgas and nāga hosts and nāga women, yakṣas and yakṣa hosts and yakṣa women, rākṣasas and rākṣasa hosts and rākṣasa women, piśācas and piśāca hosts and piśāca women, bhūtas and bhūta hosts and bhūta women, kaṭapūtanas and kaṭapūtana hosts and kaṭapūtana women, cāyas and cāya hosts and cāya women, unmānas and unmāna hosts and unmāna women, apasmāras and apasmāra hosts and apasmāra women, ostārakas and ostāraka hosts and ostāraka women, grahas and graha hosts and graha women, kākhordas and kākhorda hosts and kākhorda women, and further vetāḍa-ghosts, black ghosts, jūraṇa-yūgu-veṭānī-śva, poison-ghosts, boil-disease ghosts, ulcer-disease ghosts, leprosy-ghosts, choking-disease ghosts, vomiting-disease ghosts, overturning-ghosts, delusion-ghosts, the evil-minded, the wrathful: when possessed or bewitched by any of these ghosts, none can cause harm, and they will never gain advantage. The power of this great vidyā: fire cannot burn, water cannot carry away, poison cannot harm, swords, staffs, bows, and arrows cannot wound, fierce bandits and thieves cannot cause fear, untimely illness cannot intrude, life will not be cut short, and one's years will be long. All sentient beings who see the holder will be harmonious, all beings who see them will love and delight in them, their renown will spread far, and even enemies will become like brothers. All the various benefits described above will be obtained." He then spoke the vidyā:

*Namaḥ sarva-buddha-dharma-saṃghe-viyak siddha-yata-ma-para-ṣṭa vidyā mama-dharma-kara-senā-śya sa-parivāra-śya anā-ratha-prativari-yaṃ-dhaṃ arthī-śuca-sandarśa-yaṃ-dhaṃ svā-sthitya-stu mama-dharma-kara-senā-śya sa-parivāra-śya siddha-vidyā svāhā*
