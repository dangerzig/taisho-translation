# 入布薩堂說偈文等
## Verses Spoken upon Entering the Poṣadha Hall and Related Texts

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 2852

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown (失譯)

---

> Keeping the precepts in purity, like the full moon,
> body and speech bright and clean, without blemish or defilement;
> Pure and harmonious, without contention or violation,
> only then may one duly participate in the Poṣadha together.

## Verse for Receiving Water

> The water of eight meritorious qualities purifies all defilements;
> poured over the palms to remove impurity, the mind becomes unstained.
> Upholding and keeping the precepts without breach or violation,
> may all sentient beings equally be thus.

## Verse Spoken for the Bathing Tokens

> Arhat-saints and the sangha assemble,
> the multitude of ordinary beings gather in harmony;
> Fragrant water cleanses through bathing,
> the Poṣadha liberates sentient beings.

## Verse for Receiving the Fragrant Water

> Fragrant water bathes and cleanses away all impurities;
> the Dharmakāya is fully endowed, the five divisions complete.
> Prajñā illuminates in its fullness, liberation is perfected;
> all beings gather as one, merging into the Dharmadhātu.

## Verse for the Incense Procession

> The fragrance of precepts, the fragrance of dhyāna, the fragrance of liberation;
> a luminous cloud-pavilion pervades the world.
> We make offering to the immeasurable Buddhas of the ten directions;
> those who see and hear are universally perfumed and attain nirvāṇa.

## Verse for Receiving the Tokens

> The vajra-like, unobstructed token of liberation; a rare and precious fruit;
> I now joyfully receive it, bearing it upon my head.
> May all sentient beings equally be thus.

## Verse for Returning the Tokens

> With complete purity I received this token;
> with complete purity I return this token.
> Firmly upholding the precepts without breach or violation,
> may all sentient beings equally be thus.

## Wondrous Verse of Purity

> Pure as the full moon,
> purity befits the Poṣadha;
> The karma of body and speech are pure,
> only then may one observe the Poṣadha together.

## Verse Spoken at the Conclusion of Poṣadha

> The appearance of the Buddhas in the world is the foremost joy;
> hearing the Dharma and practicing it is joyful delight.
> The harmony of the assembly and the peace of nirvāṇa is joy;
> sentient beings' liberation from suffering into peace and bliss is joy.
