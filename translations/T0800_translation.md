# 佛說無上處經
## Sūtra on the Supreme Abodes, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0800

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown (失譯)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was dwelling at Anāthapiṇḍada's park in Jeta's Grove, in the land of Śrāvastī.

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣus: "There are three supreme abodes. Listen carefully, and I shall explain them to you."

The bhikṣus replied to the Buddha: "Yes, we are ready to receive your teaching."

The Buddha told the bhikṣus: "The three supreme abodes are: first, the supreme abode of the Buddha; second, the supreme abode of the Dharma; third, the supreme abode of the Sangha. Among all sentient beings; those with two feet, four feet, no feet, or many feet; those with form or without form; those with perception, without perception, or in neither-perception-nor-non-perception; the Tathāgata declares the supreme abode.

"Bhikṣus! If any sentient being can arouse a mind of faith and aspiration toward the supreme abode of the Buddha, among gods and humans they will obtain the supreme fruit. Bhikṣus, this is called the first supreme abode.

"Furthermore, bhikṣus, among all conditioned and unconditioned phenomena, among phenomena with form and without form, the Dharma of dispassion is the supreme abode. Bhikṣus! If any sentient being can arouse a mind of faith and aspiration toward this supreme abode of the Dharma, among gods and humans they will obtain the supreme fruit. Bhikṣus, this is called the second supreme abode.

"Furthermore, bhikṣus, as for the supreme abode of the Sangha: among all assemblies, groups, gatherings, and congregations, the Sangha of the Tathāgata's disciples is the supreme abode. Bhikṣus! If any sentient being can arouse a mind of faith and aspiration toward this supreme abode of the Sangha, among gods and humans they will obtain the supreme fruit. This is called the three supreme abodes."

At that time, the bhikṣus, having heard what the Buddha had said, rejoiced and put it into practice.
