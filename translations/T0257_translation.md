# 佛說聖佛母般若波羅蜜多經
## Sūtra of the Holy Mother of Buddhas Prajñāpāramitā Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0257

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dānapāla (施護)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the World-Honored One was in the city of Rājagṛha, on Vulture Peak Mountain, together with a great assembly of one thousand two hundred and fifty bhikṣus, and also surrounded by an assembly of bodhisattva-mahāsattvas.

At that time, the World-Honored One entered the samādhi called Profound Radiance Proclaiming the True Dharma. At that time, the Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Avalokiteśvara was in the Buddha's assembly. This bodhisattva-mahāsattva had already mastered the practice of the profound prajñāpāramitā and had seen that the self-nature of the five aggregates is entirely empty.

At that time, the venerable Śāriputra, empowered by the Buddha's might, addressed the Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Avalokiteśvara: "If good men and good women wish to study this profound Dharma-gate of prajñāpāramitā, how should they study it?"

Then the Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Avalokiteśvara addressed the venerable Śāriputra:

"Listen now with careful attention, and I shall explain it for you. If good men and good women wish to study this profound Dharma-gate of prajñāpāramitā, they should contemplate the self-nature of the five aggregates as entirely empty. What does it mean that the self-nature of the five aggregates is empty? It means: form itself is emptiness, emptiness itself is form. Form is not different from emptiness; emptiness is not different from form. Feeling, perception, volitional formations, and consciousness are likewise.

"Śāriputra! The empty characteristic of all these dharmas is such: nothing is produced and nothing is destroyed; there is no defilement and no purity; no increase and no decrease. Śāriputra! Therefore, in emptiness there is no form, no feeling, perception, volitional formations, or consciousness; no eye, ear, nose, tongue, body, or mind; no form, sound, smell, taste, touch, or mental object; no eye-element and no eye-consciousness element, and so on up to no mind-element and no mind-consciousness element; no ignorance and no extinction of ignorance, and so on up to no old age and death and no extinction of old age and death; no suffering, origination, cessation, or path; no wisdom, no attainment, and also no non-attainment.

"Śāriputra! Because there is no attainment, the bodhisattva-mahāsattva, through the practice corresponding to prajñāpāramitā, has a mind without attachment and without obstruction. Because there is no attachment and no obstruction, there is no fear. Far removed from all inverted false imaginings, one reaches ultimate and complete nirvāṇa. All Buddhas of the three periods of time, relying on this prajñāpāramitā, attain the supreme, perfect enlightenment.

"Therefore one should know that prajñāpāramitā is the vast and great radiance, the unsurpassed radiance, the radiance equal to the unequaled, which can still and remove all suffering and affliction. This is the true Dharma, real and without falsehood. All who study should study in this way. I now proclaim the great mantra of prajñāpāramitā:

*Tadyathā oṃ gate gate pāragate pārasaṃgate bodhi svāhā*

"Śāriputra! All bodhisattva-mahāsattvas who can recite this mantra-phrase of prajñāpāramitā are thereby studying the profound prajñāpāramitā."

At that time, the World-Honored One calmly arose from the samādhi and praised the Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Avalokiteśvara: "Excellent, excellent! Good man! As you have spoken, it is so, it is so! Prajñāpāramitā should be studied in this way. This is truly the most supreme and ultimate. All Tathāgatas also rejoice."

When the Buddha had spoken this sūtra, the Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Avalokiteśvara and all the bhikṣus, as well as the devas, humans, asuras, gandharvas, and the entire great assembly of the world, hearing what the Buddha taught, were all greatly joyful, and they accepted it with faith and practiced it.
