# The Sūtra of the Seven Buddhas
## 佛說七佛經

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 2

Translated from the Chinese by Fātian (法天), Song dynasty.

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was dwelling in the Jetavana monastery in the garden of Anāthapiṇḍada in the country of Śrāvastī. At that time, a great assembly of bhikṣus took up their bowls at mealtime and went to the Karīrī meeting ground, where they sat together in contemplation: "In past ages, what buddhas appeared in the world? What were their clans, lineages, and lifespans, and what are the meanings of these?" Having reflected thus, they questioned one another but could not find the answers.

At that time, the World-Honored One, knowing the thoughts of these bhikṣus, rose from his seat, went to the Karīrī meeting ground, and sat in full lotus posture. The bhikṣus bowed their heads to his feet, stood to one side, joined their palms in reverence, and gazed upon him with single-minded devotion.

The World-Honored One asked: "Bhikṣus, what are you thinking?" The bhikṣus replied: "We have been contemplating which buddhas appeared in past ages, but we cannot ascertain their clans, lineages, or lifespans." The Buddha told the bhikṣus: "Would you like to hear?" The bhikṣus replied: "Now is the right time. We wish you to expound this for us."

The Buddha said: "Listen well, and I shall explain. Ninety-one kalpas ago, the Buddha Vipaśyin, the Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened, appeared in the world. Thirty-one kalpas ago, the Buddha Śikhin and the Buddha Viśvabhū, Worthy Ones, Perfectly Enlightened, appeared in the world. In the sixth kalpa of this Fortunate Kalpa, the Buddha Krakucchanda, Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened, appeared in the world. In the seventh kalpa, the Buddha Kanakamuni, Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened, appeared in the world. In the eighth kalpa, the Buddha Kāśyapa, Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened, appeared in the world. In the ninth kalpa, I, Śākyamuni Buddha, appeared in the world, Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened. Furthermore, in the past kalpas, Vipaśyin, Śikhin, and Viśvabhū proclaimed the pure discipline of śīla and accomplished the supreme practice of wisdom.

"Furthermore, in this Fortunate Kalpa, Krakucchanda, Kanakamuni, and Kāśyapa also taught the pure precepts and monastic observances, as well as the dharma of meditation, concentration, and liberation. The dharma that I teach is likewise. Bhikṣus! In the past, the Buddha Vipaśyin was of the kṣatriya caste. He aroused pure faith, sought the homeless life, and attained the path of perfect enlightenment. The Buddhas Śikhin and Viśvabhū were also of the kṣatriya caste. The Buddhas Krakucchanda, Kanakamuni, and Kāśyapa were of the brahmin caste. I was born in the palace of King Śuddhodana, of the kṣatriya caste."

At that time, the World-Honored One, wishing to restate this meaning, spoke in verse:

> I speak of the past, ninety-one kalpas hence,
> When Vipaśyin appeared in the world.
> In thirty-one kalpas, Śikhin and Viśvabhū,
> These Perfectly Enlightened Ones were all of kṣatriya caste.
> Krakucchanda Tathāgata was of brahmin lineage,
> Kanakamuni and Kāśyapa were likewise.
> I dwell in Jambudvīpa as Śuddhodana's son;
> Though I attained bodhi, I too am of kṣatriya caste.

When the World-Honored One had spoken this verse, he told the assembly of bhikṣus: "Listen well! I shall now further describe the clan names of the seven Tathāgatas, Worthy Ones, Perfectly Enlightened. The Buddhas Vipaśyin, Śikhin, and Viśvabhū were of the Kauṇḍinya clan. The Buddhas Krakucchanda, Kanakamuni, and Kāśyapa were of the Kāśyapa clan. The Tathāgata Śākyamuni is of the Gautama clan."

Then the World-Honored One spoke again in verse:

> Vipaśyin Tathāgata, Śikhin, and Viśvabhū,
> These three buddhas were all of the Kauṇḍinya clan.
> Krakucchanda Tathāgata, Kanakamuni, and Kāśyapa,
> These three buddhas were all of the Kāśyapa clan.
> I dwell in the realm of Jambudvīpa,
> Born in the palace of Śuddhodana.
> Having gone forth and attained bodhi,
> I am of the Gautama clan.

When the World-Honored One had spoken this verse, he told the assembly of bhikṣus: "Listen well! I shall now further describe the lifespans of the seven Tathāgatas, Worthy Ones, Perfectly Enlightened. The Buddha Vipaśyin, Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened, appeared in the world with a lifespan of eighty thousand years. The Buddha Śikhin, Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened, appeared in the world with a lifespan of seventy thousand years. The Buddha Viśvabhū, Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened, appeared in the world with a lifespan of sixty thousand years. The Buddha Krakucchanda, Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened, appeared in the world with a lifespan of forty thousand years. The Buddha Kanakamuni, Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened, appeared in the world with a lifespan of thirty thousand years. The Buddha Kāśyapa, Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened, appeared in the world with a lifespan of twenty thousand years. As for me, I teach beings in the five turbidities, with a lifespan of one hundred years."

Then the World-Honored One spoke again in verse:

> Vipaśyin Tathāgata, Śikhin, Viśvabhū,
> Krakucchanda the World-Honored, Kanakamuni, Kāśyapa:
> When each appeared in the world,
> The human lifespan of their age was
> Eighty thousand, then seventy thousand,
> Sixty thousand and forty thousand,
> Thirty thousand down to twenty thousand.
> Thus Śākyamuni Buddha
> Appeared in the five turbidities
> When the human lifespan was one hundred years.

When the World-Honored One had spoken this verse, he told the assembly of bhikṣus: "Listen well! I shall now further describe the parents, capital cities, and various names associated with the seven Tathāgatas. The Buddha Vipaśyin's father was King Bandhumā, his mother was Bandhumatī, and the capital was also called Bandhumatī. The Tathāgata Śikhin's father was King Aruṇa, his mother was also called Aruṇā, and the city was named Aruṇavatī. The Tathāgata Viśvabhū's father was King Supratīta, his mother was Prabhāvatī, and the city was named Anupamā. The Buddha Krakucchanda's father was Yajñadatta, his mother was Viśākhā; the king was Kṣemadharma, and the city was Kṣema. The Buddha Kanakamuni's father was Yajñadattu, his mother was Uttarā; the king was Śubha, and the city was Śubhavatī. The Tathāgata Kāśyapa's father was Subrahmā, his mother was Brahmagupta; the king was Kṛkī, and the city was Vārāṇasī. As for me, the present Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened, my father was King Śuddhodana, my mother was Mahāmāyā, and the city was Kapilavastu."

Then the World-Honored One spoke again in verse:

> The Tathāgata Vipaśyin: his original birthplace,
> Bandhumā was his father, Bandhumatī his mother,
> And the great capital city was also called Bandhumatī;
> In that time the land was rich and prosperous,
> And the people always lived in happiness.
>
> The World-Honored Śikhin Buddha:
> King Aruṇa was his father,
> Aruṇavatī was his mother's name,
> And the city where he dwelt bore the same name, Aruṇavatī;
> The people were exceedingly flourishing,
> Greatly wealthy and always at peace.
>
> The Tathāgata Viśvabhū:
> His father the king and his mother were named
> Supratīta and Prabhāvatī;
> The capital city was called Anupamā,
> And that age too was peaceful,
> Entirely free from calamity.
>
> The World-Honored Krakucchanda:
> His father's given name was Yajñadatta,
> Viśākhā was his mother,
> King Kṣemadharma ruled from the city of Kṣema;
> At that time all beings
> Honored and revered what was virtuous.
>
> Kanakamuni: Yajñadattu
> Was his father's name, Uttarā his mother;
> The king was named Śubha,
> The city Śubhavatī,
> Broad and beautifully adorned,
> Where beings were free from all afflictions.
>
> Kāśyapa Buddha's father was Subrahmā,
> His mother was named Brahmā-Gupta,
> King Kṛkī ruled from the city of Vārāṇasī;
> Among all its beings,
> Day and night there was always peace.
>
> My own birthplace: Śuddhodana was my father,
> Mahāmāyā was my mother,
> And the city was called Kapilavastu.
> Thus the seven Tathāgatas, Perfectly Enlightened,
> Their parents and capital cities,
> Their distinct names are as described.

When the World-Honored One had spoken this verse, he told the assembly of bhikṣus: "Listen well! I shall now further describe the foremost śrāvaka disciples of the seven Tathāgatas, Worthy Ones, Perfectly Enlightened. The Tathāgata Vipaśyin, Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened, had a disciple of great wisdom named Khaṇḍatīkṣya, foremost among the śrāvakas. The Tathāgata Śikhin, Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened, had a disciple of great wisdom named Abhisambhava, foremost among the śrāvakas. The Tathāgata Viśvabhū, Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened, had a disciple of great wisdom named Yaśottara, foremost among the śrāvakas. The Tathāgata Krakucchanda, Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened, had a disciple of great wisdom named Saṃjīva, foremost among the śrāvakas. The Tathāgata Kanakamuni, Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened, had a disciple of great wisdom named Śroṇottara, foremost among the śrāvakas. The Tathāgata Kāśyapa, Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened, had a disciple of great wisdom named Bharadvāja, foremost among the śrāvakas. As for me, the present Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened, my disciple of great wisdom is named Śāriputra, foremost among the śrāvakas."

Then the World-Honored One spoke again in verse:

> The Tathāgata Vipaśyin had a son of great wisdom
> Named Khaṇḍatīkṣya. The World-Honored Śikhin Buddha
> Had a son of great wisdom named Abhisambhava.
> The Tathāgata Viśvabhū had a son of great wisdom
> Named Yaśottara. The Tathāgata Krakucchanda
> Had a son of great wisdom named Saṃjīva.
> Kanakamuni had a son of great wisdom,
> Śroṇottara. The World-Honored Kāśyapa Buddha
> Had a son of great wisdom, Bharadvāja.
> As for me, the Worthy, Perfectly Enlightened,
> I have a son of great wisdom named Śāriputra.
> Thus the sons of the seven buddhas
> Were each foremost among the śrāvakas.

When the World-Honored One had spoken this verse, he told the assembly of bhikṣus: "Listen well! I shall now further describe the attendant disciples of the seven Tathāgatas, Worthy Ones, Perfectly Enlightened. The Tathāgata Vipaśyin, Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened, had an attendant named Aśoka. The Tathāgata Śikhin, Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened, had an attendant named Kṣemakāru. The Buddha Viśvabhū, Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened, had an attendant named Upaśānta. The Buddha Krakucchanda, Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened, had an attendant named Buddhiyu. The Buddha Kanakamuni, Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened, had an attendant named Śrotriārya. The Tathāgata Kāśyapa, Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened, had an attendant named Sarvamitra. As for me, the present Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened, my attendant is named Ānanda." Then the World-Honored One spoke again in verse:

> Buddha's sons Aśoka, Kṣemakāru,
> Together with Upaśānta, and Buddhiyu,
> Śrotriārya, Sarvamitra,
> And Ānanda: all served as attendants to the buddhas.
> Always practicing hearts of compassion,
> Accomplished in samādhi,
> Penetrating the nature of all dharmas,
> Endowed with great wisdom,
> Learned and brilliant,
> They were great teachers of the dharma,
> Foremost in the assembly,
> Their fame known in the ten directions.
> Gods and humans all paid reverence;
> The power of their diligence was firm and steadfast.
> They severed all afflictions
> And realized the unborn and undying.
> Attending upon the World-Honored Buddhas,
> They constantly gained their own benefit.
> Such were the Tathāgatas'
> Accomplished, true buddha-sons.

When the World-Honored One had spoken this verse, he told the assembly of bhikṣus: "Listen well! I shall now further describe the assemblies of śrāvakas guided by the seven Tathāgatas. At the first teaching assembly of the Tathāgata Vipaśyin, sixty-two thousand bhikṣus attained the fruit of arhatship. At his second assembly, one hundred thousand bhikṣus attained arhatship. At his third assembly, eighty thousand bhikṣus attained arhatship. At the first assembly of the Tathāgata Śikhin, one hundred thousand bhikṣus attained arhatship. At his second assembly, eight billion bhikṣus attained arhatship. At his third assembly, seventy thousand bhikṣus attained arhatship. At the first assembly of the Tathāgata Viśvabhū, eighty thousand bhikṣus attained arhatship. At his second assembly, seventy thousand bhikṣus attained arhatship. At his third assembly, sixty thousand bhikṣus attained arhatship.

"At the one assembly of the Tathāgata Krakucchanda, forty thousand bhikṣus attained arhatship. At the one assembly of the Buddha Kanakamuni, thirty thousand bhikṣus attained arhatship. At the one assembly of the Tathāgata Kāśyapa, twenty thousand bhikṣus attained arhatship. At my one assembly, one thousand two hundred and fifty bhikṣus attained arhatship."

Then the World-Honored One spoke again in verse:

> Vipaśyin Tathāgata, Śikhin, Viśvabhū,
> Krakucchanda the World-Honored, Kanakamuni, Kāśyapa,
> And Śākyamuni: when each appeared in the world,
> The total of the śrāvaka assemblies they guided
> Numbered seven billion,
> Ninety thousand and three thousand more,
> And one thousand two hundred and fifty,
> All attaining arhatship, not subject to further existence.

When the World-Honored One had spoken this verse, he told the assembly of bhikṣus: "Listen well! Vipaśyin Buddha, Śikhin Buddha, Viśvabhū Buddha, up to my own appearance in the world: in upholding the teaching, proclaiming the dharma, taming sentient beings, maintaining the discipline and observances, carrying the robe and bowl, and seeking bodhi, there is not the slightest difference among us."

Then the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> Thus in kalpas past and present,
> Vipaśyin and the other buddhas
> Guided assemblies of bhikṣus
> Who accomplished great wisdom.
> They diligently cultivated the right path,
> The dharmas of the factors of bodhi:
> The five faculties and five powers,
> The four foundations of mindfulness, the four bases of spiritual power,
> The seven factors of awakening, the noble eightfold path,
> And samādhi.
> With peaceful faculties such as the eye,
> They penetrated the treasury of dharma,
> Awakened all beings,
> And increased the life of wisdom.
> Thus in this Fortunate Kalpa
> All praised what had never been before.
> The buddhas, with great compassion and wisdom,
> Self-awakened and awakening others,
> With majestic, great spiritual powers,
> Taught always in this manner.

When the World-Honored One had spoken this verse, together with the bhikṣus he rose from his seat and returned to the Jetavana monastery. After that night had passed, at the time of early dawn,

the assembly of bhikṣus departed from their dwelling places and went to the Karīrī meeting ground, where they questioned one another: "The Tathāgatas of the past entered the great nirvāṇa, free from all conceptual elaboration, having forever severed the cycle of rebirth, without any fault. Such great men possessed such wisdom, such morality, such samādhi, such liberation, such majestic power, such noble lineage, descending into the world to benefit beings. This is exceedingly rare and inconceivable."

At that time, the World-Honored One, knowing the thoughts of the bhikṣus, rose from his seat, went to the Karīrī meeting ground, sat in full lotus posture, and told the assembly of bhikṣus: "What are you thinking?" The bhikṣus addressed the World-Honored One: "We have heard the account of the Tathāgatas of the past, who entered the great nirvāṇa, free from all conceptual elaboration, having forever severed the cycle of rebirth, without any fault. Such great men possessed such wisdom, such morality, such samādhi, such liberation, such majestic power, such noble lineage, descending into the world to benefit beings. This is exceedingly rare and inconceivable." The Buddha said: "Bhikṣus, why do you say this?" The bhikṣus said: "The Buddha possesses the pure dharma-realm, the wisdom of true awakening, and knows all things without exception. We wish you to expound this for us."

The Buddha said: "Bhikṣus, listen well, and I shall explain. In the past, there was a great king named Bandhumā, and his queen was named Bandhumatī. At that time, the Bodhisattva Vipaśyin descended from the Tuṣita heaven to Jambudvīpa, entered his mother's womb, and while dwelling in the womb, emitted a great radiance that illuminated all the worlds, leaving no darkness. In all the evil destinies and hells, where even the light of the sun and moon cannot reach, the buddha's radiance suddenly brought great illumination. The beings there could see one another and exclaimed: 'How is it that there are other beings here?'"

Then the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> The Bodhisattva descended from Tuṣita,
> Coming down to Jambudvīpa;
> Like cloud, like wind,
> He arrived in his mother's womb in an instant.
> His body emitted a great radiance
> Illuminating the realms of gods and humans.
> The hells and the Iron-Encircling Mountains
> Were all entirely free from darkness.
> The buddha-fields and all their realms
> Abided with his mother's body.
> Thus the great assembly of sages
> Also came to gather together.

When the World-Honored One had spoken this verse, he told the assembly of bhikṣus: "Listen well! When the Bodhisattva Mahāsattva descended from Tuṣita heaven to Jambudvīpa and entered his mother's womb, the yakṣa Bhūma cried out in a loud voice: 'This great bodhisattva, of great majestic power, this great man, has relinquished his body among the gods and among the asuras, and now dwells in his mother's womb to take a human body.' In this way, the gods of the Four Kings' heaven, the Trāyastriṃśa heaven, the Yāma heaven, up to the Brahmā attendant gods, upon hearing this proclamation that the Bodhisattva had descended to dwell in his mother's womb, all came to gather."

Then the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> When the Bodhisattva descended from Tuṣita
> Into his mother's womb,
> Bhūma the great yakṣa proclaimed
> That the Bodhisattva
> Had relinquished his celestial body
> And the form of an asura,
> To dwell in this mother's womb
> And receive a human birth.
> The Four Kings, Trāyastriṃśa, Yāma, and Tuṣita,
> Up to the Brahmā world,
> All heard this news.
> The Bodhisattva descended to the human realm,
> Of subtle, true golden hue,
> And all the gods came to gather,
> Their hearts filled with great joy.

When the World-Honored One had spoken this verse, he told the assembly of bhikṣus: "Listen well! When the Bodhisattva Mahāsattva descended from Tuṣita heaven to Jambudvīpa, four great celestial princes of complete majesty, wearing armor and bearing bows and swords, guarded the Bodhisattva so that neither humans nor non-humans could cause him harm."

Then the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> When the Bodhisattva descended to birth,
> Śakra, Lord of Trāyastriṃśa,
> Dispatched the four heavenly kings,
> Each endowed with great power,
> Clad in golden armor,
> Bearing bows, swords, and spears,
> To guard him at all times.
> Rākṣasas and non-humans
> Dared not cause any harm.
> Resting in his mother's womb
> As in a great palace,
> He constantly enjoyed all pleasures.

When the World-Honored One had spoken this verse, he told the bhikṣus: "Listen well! When the Bodhisattva Mahāsattva descended from Tuṣita heaven to Jambudvīpa and dwelt in his mother's womb, his body was pure, its radiance shining like a *maṇi* jewel, and his mother's heart was at peace, free from all distress."

Then the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> When the Bodhisattva dwelt in the womb,
> He was pure and without blemish,
> Like a lapis lazuli jewel,
> Like a *maṇi* treasure.
> His radiance illuminated the world
> Like the sun emerging from clouds.
> He had accomplished the supreme truth
> And given rise to the highest wisdom.
> He kept his mother free from sorrow,
> And she constantly practiced virtuous deeds.
> All beings paid her reverence.
> She dwelt securely in the kṣatriya lineage.

When the World-Honored One had spoken this verse, he told the assembly of bhikṣus: "Listen well! When the Bodhisattva Mahāsattva descended from Tuṣita heaven to Jambudvīpa and dwelt in his mother's womb, it was never heard that the mother had any defiled desires; regarding form and the other five sense objects, she had no attachment." Then the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> When the Bodhisattva dwelt in the womb,
> His mother's mind was pure.
> No word of defilement was heard;
> She was far from the faults of the five desires.
> The root of craving was severed;
> She received no suffering or distress.
> Body and mind were always at peace,
> And she constantly experienced happiness.

When the World-Honored One had spoken this verse, he told the assembly of bhikṣus: "Listen well! When the Bodhisattva Mahāsattva descended from Tuṣita heaven to Jambudvīpa and dwelt in his mother's womb, the mother herself upheld the five precepts of a lay disciple: first, not killing; second, not stealing; third, not engaging in sexual misconduct; fourth, not speaking falsely; fifth, not drinking alcohol. The Bodhisattva was born from her right side, and after the mother's life ended, she was reborn in the celestial realm."

Then the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> When the Bodhisattva dwelt in the womb,
> His mother upheld the five precepts.
> The child was born from her right side
> Without any suffering or distress,
> Just as the Lord Śakra
> Enjoys the wondrous five pleasures.
> When afterward her life ended,
> She was swiftly reborn in heaven.

When the World-Honored One had spoken this verse, he told the assembly of bhikṣus: "Listen well! When the Bodhisattva Mahāsattva was born from the right side, the great earth trembled. His body was of true golden hue, free from all defilement, and emitted a great radiance that universally illuminated all the realms of the world. In all the evil destinies, the dark hells suddenly became bright, and the beings there could see one another, each wondering: 'How is it that there are other beings here?'"

Then the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> When the Bodhisattva descended to birth,
> The great earth trembled entirely.
> His body was like true gold,
> Unstained by any dust or filth.
> His majestic, great spiritual powers
> Cast light upon all things.
> Beings in dark realms of evil karma
> Could see one another.

When the World-Honored One had spoken this verse, he told the assembly of bhikṣus: "Listen well! When the Bodhisattva Mahāsattva was born from the right side, his mother felt no fatigue, neither sitting nor lying down. At that time, the Bodhisattva, the great man of majestic power, was not confused in mind, and his feet did not touch the ground, for four great celestial princes held the child's body."

Then the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> When the Bodhisattva descended to birth,
> His mother's mind was undisturbed.
> She neither sat nor lay down,
> But was at ease and comfortable.
> The great man of majestic power,
> His mind free from all darkness,
> Was held aloft by four celestial princes;
> His feet did not touch the ground.

When the World-Honored One had spoken this verse, he told the assembly of bhikṣus: "Listen well! When the Bodhisattva Mahāsattva was born from the right side, his body was pure like a lapis lazuli jewel, entirely free from all impurities such as pus, blood, mucus, spittle, and other unclean substances, just as a *maṇi* jewel wrapped in the cloth of Kauśika cannot be stained by dust or filth."

Then the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> When the Bodhisattva descended to birth,
> His bodily vessel was entirely pure,
> Far removed from all foulness:
> Pus, blood, mucus, spittle, and the like.
> Just as the cloth of Kauśika
> And the *maṇi* jewel
> Are lustrous and shining,
> So dust and filth could not adhere.

When the World-Honored One had spoken this verse, he told the assembly of bhikṣus: "Listen well! When the Bodhisattva Mahāsattva was born from the right side, two celestial princes descended from the sky with two kinds of water, one cool and one warm, to bathe the child."

Then the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> When the Bodhisattva descended to birth,
> Two celestials appeared in the sky,
> Bathing the child's body,
> Pouring down two kinds of water,
> Warm and cool, each as needed,
> Symbolizing the perfection of merit and wisdom.
> He accomplished the great fearlessness
> And regarded all beings with universal compassion.

When the World-Honored One had spoken this verse, he told the assembly of bhikṣus: "Listen well! When the Bodhisattva Mahāsattva was born from the right side, he was endowed with the thirty-two marks. His body was dignified and beautiful, and his eye faculty was so pure that he could see ten *yojanas*."

Then the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> When the Bodhisattva descended to birth,
> All his marks were complete.
> His eyes were pure and his form dignified;
> He could see ten *yojanas* distant.

When the World-Honored One had spoken this verse, he told the assembly of bhikṣus: "When the Bodhisattva Mahāsattva was born from the right side, he beheld the Trāyastriṃśa heaven. Śakra, Lord of the gods, seeing that this child was a true buddha body, held a white parasol and covered the child's body, so that cold, heat, wind, dust, and all harmful things could not affect him."

Then the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> When the child was first born,
> He gazed as far as Trāyastriṃśa.
> Śakra, beholding him,
> Took a white parasol in hand
> And came to cover his body.
> Cold, heat, wind, sun,
> And all poisons and evils
> Could in no way harm him.

When the World-Honored One had spoken this verse, he told the assembly of bhikṣus: "Listen well! When the Bodhisattva Mahāsattva was born from the right side, his birth mother, his wet nurse, his foster mother, and the palace women all surrounded and protected him, bathing him, anointing him with fragrant oils, and attending to him in every way."

Then the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> When the child was first born,
> Three mothers nurtured him,
> And the palace women
> Constantly surrounded him on all sides,
> Bathing him and anointing him with fragrance,
> Keeping him always safe and at ease.
> Thus, day and night,
> They were never apart from him for a moment.

When the World-Honored One had spoken this verse, he told the assembly of bhikṣus: "Listen well! When the Bodhisattva Mahāsattva was born from the right side, his body was dignified and beautiful, endowed with the thirty-two marks. King Bandhumā immediately summoned physiognomists to examine the child. The brahmins said: 'If this child remains a householder, he will receive the consecration as a wheel-turning king, ruling over the four continents, endowed with a thousand sons of majestic fearlessness. Without resort to arms, bows, or swords, he will be able to subdue all opposing armies. But if he goes forth from home and practices with firm determination, he will attain perfect, complete enlightenment.'"

Then the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> The brahmin physiognomists examined this celestial child
> And told his father the king:
> "He is endowed with the thirty-two marks,
> Like the moon appearing among the stars,
> Exceedingly rare in the world.
> If he remains always in the palace,
> He will surely inherit the wheel-turning throne,
> Ruling the four great continents,
> With a thousand princes born to him.
> But should he go forth from home,
> He will swiftly attain supreme awakening."

When the World-Honored One had spoken this verse, he told the assembly of bhikṣus: "Listen well! When the Bodhisattva Mahāsattva was born from the right side, his body was of true golden hue, with dignified marks, like a lotus born from water that dust and filth cannot stain. All beings gazed upon him without satiation. His voice was subtle, refined, clear, elegant, and melodious, like the *kalavinka* bird on the Himalayas that, intoxicated by eating flowers, produces a voice of elegant beauty and clear resonance. All beings who heard it could not help but delight in it. The child's voice was likewise."

Then the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> When the child was first born,
> His body was of true golden hue,
> Like a red lotus flower
> That dust and filth cannot stain.
> His voice was exceedingly subtle and wondrous,
> Like the song of the *kalavinka*.
> All who heard it
> Delighted in it without satiation.

When the World-Honored One had spoken this verse, he told the assembly of bhikṣus: "Listen well! When the Bodhisattva Mahāsattva was a child, he was far removed from crookedness and falsehood. His mind was pure and straightforward. Self-awakened, he awakened others, constantly practicing the right dharma. The people served and attended him as they would serve Śakra, Lord of the gods. He honored and respected his parents. For this reason he was named Vipaśyin."

Then the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> The Tathāgata Vipaśyin, when he was a child,
> Penetrated great wisdom and was far from crookedness and falsehood.
> Self-awakened and awakening others,
> He cultivated the practice of right dharma.
> The people always loved and revered him
> As they revere the Lord Śakra.
> He attended to his parents,
> And his fame filled the world.
> He was named Vipaśyin,
> Benefiting all sentient beings.
