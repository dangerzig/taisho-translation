# 佛說嗟韈曩法天子受三歸依獲免惡道經
## Sūtra of the Deva-Son Jvālanā-Dharma Who Received the Three Refuges and Was Freed from the Evil Paths, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0595

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Fatian (法天)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the World-Honored One was in the land of Śrāvastī, at the Jeta Grove of the Anāthapiṇḍada Garden, together with a great assembly of bhikṣus.

At that time there was a deva-son named Jvālanā-Dharma. His celestial life was about to end, with only seven days remaining, and the five signs of decay had already appeared: his body lost its majesty and splendor, filth and impurity began to arise, the flower garlands upon his head all withered, a foul odor issued from the various parts of his body, and sweat flowed from beneath both his arms. Because of this, Jvālanā-Dharma no longer delighted in his seat. He rolled upon the ground, weeping and crying in grief, and spoke these words: "How bitter! How bitter! The Mānasī Lake! How bitter! How bitter! The bathing pools! How bitter! How bitter! The jeweled chariots and the Kharā-Nandana mixed grove and all these gardens: I shall never play in them again! How bitter! How bitter! The pārijāta flowers: I shall never pick them again. The ground of soft and mixed jewels: I shall never walk upon it again! How bitter! How bitter! The celestial host of female musicians, supremely dignified and beautiful, who always attended me: now we must part."

At that time another deva-son, seeing these things, went to Indra's presence and said: "Lord of the Heavens! Jvālanā-Dharma's five signs of decay have appeared. His life has but seven days remaining. He rolls upon the ground, weeping and crying in grief. He speaks these words: 'How bitter! How bitter! The Mānasī Lake and bathing pools! How bitter! How bitter! The jeweled chariots and the Kharā-Nandana mixed grove and all these gardens: I shall never play in them again! How bitter! How bitter! The pārijāta flowers: I shall never pick them again. The ground of soft and mixed jewels: I shall never walk upon it again! How bitter! How bitter! The celestial host of female musicians, supremely dignified and beautiful, who always attended me: now we must part.' Lord of the Heavens! Having seen this, my heart is deeply pained, and so I have come to tell you."

Then Indra, the lord of the heavens, out of compassion in his heart, went to where Jvālanā-Dharma was and said to him: "Deva-son! How is it that you, worthy one, roll upon the ground, weeping and crying in grief, speaking of all these bitter things, moving all who see you?"

Then Jvālanā-Dharma, suddenly hearing these words, rose from the ground, adjusted his garments, composed himself, joined his palms and stood, and said to Indra: "Lord of the Heavens! My lifespan has only seven days remaining. After my life ends, I shall fall to Jambudvīpa, in the great city of Rājagṛha, where because of former karma I shall receive the body of a pig. Lord of the Heavens! Having received that body, for many years I shall eat and devour filth and excrement. I contemplate this suffering, and therefore I grieve."

Then Indra, having heard these words, was deeply moved with compassion. He said to the deva-son Jvālanā-Dharma: "Worthy one! You should with a sincere heart take refuge in the Three Treasures. You should speak thus: 'I take refuge in the Buddha, the most honored among beings of two feet. I take refuge in the Dharma, the most honored among that which is free from desire. I take refuge in the Saṃgha, the most honored among assemblies.'" Then the deva-son Jvālanā-Dharma, out of fear of death and dread of the animal realm, said to Indra: "Kauśika! I now take refuge in the Buddha, the most honored among beings of two feet. I take refuge in the Dharma, the most honored among that which is free from desire. I take refuge in the Saṃgha, the most honored among assemblies." When the deva-son had received the three refuges, his mind was unbroken, and so he remained until the end of his life.

It is the nature of the devas that those of lower wisdom can see downward but cannot observe upward. Then Indra, lord of the heavens, looked to see where that deva-son had been reborn. Had he been born in Jambudvīpa in the great city of Rājagṛha and received the body of a pig, or had he not? He exhausted his divine eye but could not see him. He also looked in the animal realm and the ghost realm, but again could not see him. He looked throughout the human world of this sahā world but again could not see him. He looked even through the Four Great Kings' Heaven and the Trāyastriṃśa Heaven, exhausting his power of observation, but could not see him anywhere.

Then Indra, unable to find him, became doubtful and anxious. He went to the Jeta Grove and came before the World-Honored One. He bowed at the Buddha's feet, sat to one side, and said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! The deva-son Jvālanā-Dharma's five signs of decay appeared, and his life had but seven days remaining. He rolled upon the ground, weeping and crying in grief, speaking of all these bitter things, moving all who saw him. At that time I went to him and, seeing this, asked him: 'How is it, worthy one, that you weep and grieve and are so distressed and haggard?' Then Jvālanā-Dharma told me: 'My lifespan has only seven days remaining. After my life ends, I shall fall to Jambudvīpa, be born in the great city of Rājagṛha, and receive the body of a pig. For many years I shall eat filth and excrement.' Hearing this, my heart was deeply moved with compassion, and I said to him: 'Now, worthy one, if you wish to escape this suffering, you should take refuge in the Three Treasures. Speak thus: "I take refuge in the Buddha, the most honored among beings of two feet. I take refuge in the Dharma, the most honored among that which is free from desire. I take refuge in the Saṃgha, the most honored among assemblies."' Then Jvālanā-Dharma, out of fear of death and dread of the animal realm, said to me: 'I now take refuge in the Buddha, the most honored among beings of two feet. I take refuge in the Dharma, the most honored among that which is free from desire. I take refuge in the Saṃgha, the most honored among assemblies.' After receiving the three refuges, he passed away. World-Honored One! I do not know where Jvālanā-Dharma has been reborn."

Then the World-Honored One, with his omniscient knowledge, said to Indra: "Kauśika! The deva-son Jvālanā-Dharma has now been born in Tuṣita Heaven, where he enjoys the five pleasures of desire."

Then Indra, hearing the Buddha's words, was overjoyed and delighted. His mind was content, his faculties were complete, and before the Buddha he spoke these gāthās:

> "If one takes refuge in the Buddha,
> That person does not fall into evil paths.
> After casting off the human body,
> One shall obtain a celestial body.
> If one takes refuge in the Dharma,
> That person does not fall into evil paths.
> After casting off the human body,
> One shall obtain a celestial body.
> If one takes refuge in the Saṃgha,
> That person does not fall into evil paths.
> After casting off the human body,
> One shall obtain a celestial body."

He spoke further gāthās:

> "If one sincerely takes refuge in the Buddha,
> That person shall obtain this:
> Whether by day or by night,
> The Buddha's mind always remembers them.
> If one sincerely takes refuge in the Dharma,
> That person shall obtain this:
> Whether by day or by night,
> The Dharma's power always sustains them.
> If one sincerely takes refuge in the Saṃgha,
> That person shall obtain this:
> Whether by day or by night,
> The Saṃgha's majesty always shields them."

When Indra had spoken these gāthās, the World-Honored One confirmed them: "Just so! Just so!

> "Taking refuge in the Buddha, Dharma, and Saṃgha,
> One assuredly does not fall into evil paths.
> After casting off the human body,
> One shall obtain a celestial body."

Then the World-Honored One spoke these gāthās:

> "If the two syllables 'Buddha'
> Come upon one's tongue,
> This is equal to taking refuge:
> One does not pass through life in vain.
> If the two syllables 'Dharma'
> Come upon one's tongue,
> This is equal to taking refuge:
> One does not pass through life in vain.
> If the two syllables 'Saṃgha'
> Come upon one's tongue,
> This is equal to taking refuge:
> One does not pass through life in vain."

He spoke further in verse:

> "Those who do not know the names of Buddha, Dharma, and Saṃgha
> Are the lowest and therefore do not obtain liberation.
> They revolve and turn in transmigration for a long time,
> Like a kāśī flower suspended in empty space."

When the Buddha had spoken this sūtra, the assembly of bhikṣus, Indra, and all the great assembly were filled with joy, faithfully received it, bowed, and departed.
