# 佛說不增不減經
## Sūtra of Neither Increase nor Decrease, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0668

Translated from the Chinese. Northern Wei dynasty, translated by Bodhiruci (菩提流支)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Bhagavān was dwelling in the city of Rājagṛha, on Mount Gṛdhrakūṭa, together with a great assembly of twelve hundred and fifty bhikṣus and innumerable, boundless bodhisattva mahāsattvas beyond reckoning.

At that time, the Venerable Śāriputra rose from his seat in the great assembly, went before the Buddha, bowed at the Buddha's feet, sat to one side, joined his palms, and said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! From beginningless time, all sentient beings have cycled through the six destinies, gone back and forth through the three realms, revolving in birth and death among the four modes of birth, enduring suffering without end. World-Honored One! Does this assembly of sentient beings, this ocean of sentient beings, increase or decrease? This meaning is deeply hidden; I am unable to understand it. If someone asks me, how should I answer?"

At that time the World-Honored One said to Śāriputra: "Excellent! Excellent, Śāriputra! For the sake of bringing safety and peace to all sentient beings, comfort and happiness to all sentient beings, compassion for all sentient beings, benefit to all sentient beings, and welfare, ease, and joy to all devas and humans, you are able to ask me about this profoundly deep meaning. Śāriputra! If you had not asked the Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha about this meaning, there would be great fault. Why? In the present age and in future ages, all devas, humans, and sentient beings would long suffer decline, vexation, harm, and injury, and would forever lose all benefit, peace, and happiness. Śāriputra! The great wrong view is the view that the realm of sentient beings increases or the view that the realm of sentient beings decreases. Śāriputra! Those sentient beings who hold this great wrong view, because of this view, are born blind and without eyes. Therefore, through the long night they wrongly walk the path of error. Because of these causes and conditions, in the present life they fall into the evil destinies. Śāriputra! The great peril is clinging firmly and with false attachment to the view that the realm of sentient beings increases, or clinging firmly and with false attachment to the view that it decreases. Śāriputra! Those sentient beings who cling firmly to these false attachments, through the long night wrongly walk the path of error. Because of these causes and conditions, in future lives they fall into the evil destinies.

"Śāriputra! It is because all foolish ordinary beings do not truly know the one dharma-realm and do not truly see the one dharma-realm that they give rise to wrong views, saying the realm of sentient beings increases or decreases. Śāriputra! While the Tathāgata is in the world, my disciples do not give rise to these views. But after my nirvāṇa, after five hundred years have passed, many sentient beings, foolish and without wisdom, though they have removed their hair and put on the three Dharma-robes, appearing outwardly as śramaṇas within the Buddha-Dharma, yet inwardly lacking the virtuous conduct of śramaṇas, such persons, who are truly not śramaṇas yet call themselves śramaṇas, who are not disciples of the Buddha yet call themselves disciples of the Buddha, they themselves say: 'I am a śramaṇa, a true disciple of the Buddha.' Such persons give rise to the views of increase and decrease. Why? These sentient beings, relying on sūtras of the Tathāgata that are not of definitive meaning, because they lack the wisdom-eye and are far from the true and real view of emptiness, do not truly know the Tathāgata's initial arising of the aspiration, do not truly know the cultivation of immeasurable merit and virtuous practices for bodhi, do not truly know the immeasurable dharmas attained by the Tathāgata, do not truly know the immeasurable powers of the Tathāgata, do not truly know the immeasurable realm of the Tathāgata, do not trust the immeasurable sphere of the Tathāgata's activity, do not truly know the inconceivable, immeasurable Dharma-sovereignty of the Tathāgata, do not truly know the inconceivable, immeasurable skillful means of the Tathāgata, cannot truly distinguish the immeasurable different realms of the Tathāgata, cannot skillfully enter the inconceivable great compassion of the Tathāgata, and do not truly know the Tathāgata's great nirvāṇa.

"Śāriputra! Foolish, ordinary beings without the wisdom of hearing, upon hearing of the Tathāgata's nirvāṇa, give rise to the view of annihilation and the view of extinction. Because they give rise to the thought of annihilation and the thought of extinction, they say the realm of sentient beings decreases; this constitutes a great wrong view, an extremely grave evil karma. Furthermore, Śāriputra, these sentient beings, based on the view of decrease, further give rise to three views. These three views are inseparable from that view of decrease, like a net. What are the three views? First, the annihilationist view, meaning ultimate cessation. Second, the extinctionist view, meaning nirvāṇa itself is extinction. Third, the view that there is no nirvāṇa, meaning nirvāṇa is ultimately empty and void. Śāriputra! These three views bind, grasp, and cling. Through the power and conditions of these three views, one further generates two more wrong views. These two views are inseparable from those three views, like a net. What are these two views? First, the view that there is no desire. Second, the view that there is ultimately no nirvāṇa.

"Śāriputra! Based on the view that there is no desire, one further gives rise to two views. These two views are inseparable from the view of no desire, like a net. What are these two views? First, the view of attachment to precepts and rules. Second, the view of purity in what is impure, an inverted view.

"Śāriputra! Based on the view that there is ultimately no nirvāṇa, one further gives rise to six views. These six views are inseparable from the view of no nirvāṇa, like a net. What are these six views? First, the view that the world has a beginning. Second, the view that the world has an end. Third, the view that sentient beings are created by illusion. Fourth, the view that there is neither suffering nor happiness. Fifth, the view that there are no matters pertaining to sentient beings. Sixth, the view that there are no noble truths.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra! Those sentient beings who hold the view of increase further give rise to two views. These two views are inseparable from that view of increase, like a net. What are these two views? First, the view that nirvāṇa is newly born. Second, the view that things arise without cause or condition, suddenly and from nothing. Śāriputra! These two views cause sentient beings to have no desire, aspiration, or diligent effort toward wholesome dharmas. Śāriputra, these sentient beings, having given rise to these two views: even if seven Buddhas, Tathāgatas, Arhats, Samyaksaṃbuddhas were to appear one after another and teach the Dharma for them, there is no possibility that they would give rise to desire or diligent effort toward wholesome dharmas.

"Śāriputra! These two views are the root of all ignorance and delusion: the view that nirvāṇa is newly born, and the view that things arise without cause or condition.

"Śāriputra! These two views are the fundamental root of the most extreme and evil affliction.

"Śāriputra! Based on these two views, all views arise. All these views are inseparable from those two views, like a net. What are all these views? They are all views, whether internal or external, whether coarse or subtle, whether middle or of various kinds.

"The views of increase and decrease, Śāriputra, these two views depend upon one realm, are united with one realm, and merge with one realm. It is because all foolish, ordinary beings do not truly know that one realm and do not truly see that one realm that they give rise to the most extreme, evil, great wrong views, saying the realm of sentient beings increases or the realm of sentient beings decreases."

At that time, the Venerable Śāriputra said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! What is this one realm? For it is said that all foolish, ordinary beings, because they do not truly know this one realm and do not truly see it, give rise to the most extreme, evil, great wrong views, saying the realm of sentient beings increases or decreases." Śāriputra said: "Excellent, World-Honored One! This meaning is extremely profound; I am unable to understand it. I humbly wish the Tathāgata to explain it for me, so that I may understand."

At that time the World-Honored One said to the Venerable Śāriputra: "This extremely profound meaning is the realm of the Tathāgata's wisdom and the sphere of the Tathāgata's mental activity. Śāriputra! This profound meaning is something that the wisdom of all śrāvakas and pratyekabuddhas cannot know, cannot see, and cannot observe. How much less can all foolish, ordinary beings fathom it! Only the wisdom of all the Tathāgatas can observe, know, and see this meaning. Śāriputra! Regarding this meaning, the wisdom of all śrāvakas and pratyekabuddhas can only have faith directed upward; they cannot truly know, see, or observe it. Śāriputra! The profound meaning is precisely the ultimate truth. The ultimate truth is precisely the realm of sentient beings. The realm of sentient beings is precisely the tathāgatagarbha. The tathāgatagarbha is precisely the Dharma-body. Śāriputra! The meaning of the Dharma-body as I teach it surpasses the sands of the Ganges: inseparable, indetachable, unseverable, undifferent, inconceivable buddha-dharmas and the merits and wisdom of the Tathāgata.

"Śāriputra! Just as a lamp in the world: its brightness, color, and warmth are inseparable and indetachable. And just as a maṇi gem: its brightness, color, and form are inseparable and indetachable. Śāriputra! The meaning of the Dharma-body as taught by the Tathāgata is likewise: surpassing the sands of the Ganges, inseparable, indetachable, unseverable, undifferent, inconceivable buddha-dharmas and the merits and wisdom of the Tathāgata.

"Śāriputra! This Dharma-body is the dharma of non-arising and non-ceasing. It is not of the past and not of the future, because it transcends both extremes. Śāriputra! That it is not of the past means it transcends the time of arising. That it is not of the future means it transcends the time of ceasing. Śāriputra! The Dharma-body of the Tathāgata is permanent, because it is the dharma of non-difference and the dharma of inexhaustibility. Śāriputra! The Dharma-body of the Tathāgata is eternal, because it is always a refuge and because it is equal to the furthest future. Śāriputra! The Dharma-body of the Tathāgata is cool, because it is the dharma of non-duality and the dharma of non-discrimination. Śāriputra! The Dharma-body of the Tathāgata is unchanging, because it is not a dharma of cessation and not a dharma of production.

"Śāriputra! This very Dharma-body, enveloped by afflictions surpassing the sands of the Ganges in number, from beginningless time has followed the worldly current, tossed by waves, going back and forth in birth and death: this is called 'sentient beings.' Śāriputra! This very Dharma-body, weary of and departing from the suffering of worldly birth and death, abandoning all desires of existence, practicing the ten perfections, gathering the eighty-four thousand dharma-gates, cultivating the practice of bodhi: this is called 'bodhisattva.'

"Furthermore, Śāriputra! This very Dharma-body, free from all worldly afflictions, latent tendencies, and bonds, having transcended all suffering, free from all defilement, attaining purity and cleanness, abiding in the pure dharmas of the other shore, having reached the land aspired to by all sentient beings, in all realms having attained ultimate penetration with nothing superior, free from all obstructions and all hindrances, in all dharmas having attained sovereign power: this is called Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha. Therefore, Śāriputra! The Dharma-body is not separate from the realm of sentient beings, and the realm of sentient beings is not separate from the Dharma-body. The realm of sentient beings is the Dharma-body; the Dharma-body is the realm of sentient beings. Śāriputra! These two dharmas have one meaning and different names.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra! As I have said above, within the realm of sentient beings there are also three kinds of dharma, all truly real, without difference or discrepancy. What are these three dharmas? First, the tathāgatagarbha's body of correspondence from the beginningless past, together with pure dharmas. Second, the tathāgatagarbha's body of non-correspondence from the beginningless past, together with the impure dharmas of affliction-entanglement. Third, the tathāgatagarbha's equality and permanence extending to the furthest future, together with dharmas of existence.

"Śāriputra, you should know! The tathāgatagarbha's body of correspondence from the beginningless past, together with pure dharmas: this dharma is truly real and not false, inseparable, indetachable, with the purity of wisdom. It is suchness, the dharma-realm, the inconceivable dharma. From the beginningless past there has been this pure dharma-body of correspondence. Śāriputra! Based on this pure suchness and dharma-realm, for the sake of sentient beings, I teach what is called the inconceivable dharma of the intrinsically pure mind.

"Śāriputra, you should know! The tathāgatagarbha's body of non-correspondence from the beginningless past, together with the impure dharmas of affliction-entanglement: from the beginningless past this has been detached, non-corresponding, the impure dharma of affliction-entanglement. Only the bodhi-wisdom of the Tathāgata can sever it. Śāriputra! Based on this affliction-entangled, non-corresponding, inconceivable dharma-realm, for the sake of sentient beings, I teach what is called the inconceivable dharma of the intrinsically pure mind defiled by adventitious afflictions.

"Śāriputra, you should know! The tathāgatagarbha's equality and permanence extending to the furthest future, together with dharmas of existence: this is precisely the root of all dharmas, replete with all dharmas, endowed with all dharmas. Within worldly dharmas, it is inseparable and indetachable, the truly real dharma of all things. It sustains all dharmas and gathers all dharmas. Śāriputra! Based on this unborn, unceasing, eternally constant, cool, unchanging, inconceivable, pure dharma-realm, I teach what is called 'sentient beings.' Why? Because what is called 'sentient beings' is precisely the unborn, unceasing, eternally constant, cool, unchanging, inconceivable, pure dharma-realm by another name. For this reason, based on that dharma, I teach what is called 'sentient beings.'

"Śāriputra! These three kinds of dharma are all truly real, without difference or discrepancy. Within this truly real dharma of non-difference and non-discrepancy, one ultimately does not give rise to the two most extreme, evil wrong views. Why? Because one sees as it truly is. The views of decrease and increase, Śāriputra, these two wrong views the Tathāgatas ultimately and forever abandon, and the Tathāgatas censure them.

"Śāriputra! If any bhikṣu, bhikṣuṇī, upāsaka, or upāsikā gives rise to one of these views or to both, the Tathāgatas are not that person's World-Honored One, and such persons are not my disciples. Śāriputra! Because such persons have given rise to these two views through causes and conditions, from darkness they enter darkness, from obscurity they enter obscurity. I say that such persons are called icchantika. Therefore, Śāriputra! You should now study this dharma and cause sentient beings to depart from the two views and abide in the right path. Śāriputra! You too should study such dharmas as these, departing from those two views and abiding in the right path."

When the Buddha had spoken this sūtra, the Venerable Śāriputra, the bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, upāsikās, bodhisattva mahāsattvas, and all the devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, humans and non-humans, and the entire great assembly were all filled with great joy, faithfully received it, and practiced it.
