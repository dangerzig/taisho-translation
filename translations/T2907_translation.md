# 普賢菩薩行願王經
## Sūtra of the King of Aspirational Prayers of Samantabhadra Bodhisattva

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 2907

Translated from the Chinese. Author unknown.

---

> To all the honored ones throughout the worlds of the ten directions,
> The lions among humans who traverse the three times:
> My body, speech, and mind are completely pure;
> Therefore I now bow down everywhere.
>
> Through the power of Samantabhadra's aspirational prayers,
> All the supreme ones appear before me.
> Bowing my body, as numerous as dust-motes of buddha-fields,
> With reverent mind I pay homage to the Great Sages.
>
> Upon a single dust-mote, buddhas numerous as dust-motes,
> Each surrounded by their buddha-sons:
> Throughout the dharma-realm, without boundary,
> The tathāgatas pervade all. One should have supreme understanding.
>
> These oceans of virtues are measurelessly inexhaustible;
> With branches and streams of words, limitlessly intertwined.
> To display the merit of all buddhas,
> I shall praise all wholesome aspirations.
>
> Supreme, wondrous flowers and garlands of flowers,
> Supreme, wondrous lamps and censers of incense,
> Sounds, unguent-incense, and jeweled canopies:
> Respectfully I offer these to all buddhas.
>
> Supreme, wondrous garments and aromatic incense,
> Powdered incense heaped as high as Mount Sumeru,
> Supreme, wondrous adornments of every kind:
> Respectfully I offer these to the Well-Departed Ones.
>
> With deep faith in the power of Samantabhadra's aspirational prayers,
> And such unsurpassed, vast offerings,
> All these I present throughout the ten directions,
> Respectfully making offerings to all buddhas.
>
> Whatever unwholesome deeds I have committed
> Through body, speech, and mind,
> Driven by greed, anger, and delusion:
> Before the tathāgatas, I now confess them all.
>
> All buddhas and bodhisattvas of the ten directions,
> Pratyekabuddhas, those in training and beyond training,
> And all the merits of sentient beings:
> I rejoice in all of these.
>
> Those in the ten directions who serve as lamps of the world,
> Who have realized buddhahood, the unattached and awakened:
> Before all these great guides,
> I request them to turn the unsurpassed wheel.
>
> Those buddhas who wish to enter nirvāṇa:
> For the benefit of all sentient beings,
> With palms joined, I kneel and request
> That they remain for kalpas as numerous as dust-motes of buddha-fields.
>
> Prostration, offering, and confession,
> Rejoicing, exhortation, and requesting:
> Whatever small roots of merit I have accumulated,
> All of these I dedicate to great bodhi.
>
> All sugatas of the past throughout the ten directions,
> And those now abiding in the world: I make offerings to all.
> All sugatas of the future:
> May they swiftly fulfill their aspirations and realize bodhi.
>
> May all buddha-fields of the ten directions
> Become vast and pure.
> The honored ones who attain equal awakening beneath the bodhi tree,
> And all the buddha-sons: may their aspirations be fulfilled.
>
> May all sentient beings of the ten directions
> Attain the undefiled and constant bliss.
> May all beings, in the meaning of the Dharma,
> Fulfill their hopes according to their wishes.
>
> The practices of bodhi I shall perform;
> May I, born in the six destinies, know my former lives.
> Whenever I receive a body in the world,
> May I always accomplish the fruit of renunciation.
>
> Following and studying all the supreme ones,
> May the practices of Samantabhadra be fulfilled.
> May my stainless, pure śīla and the rest
> Be always without deficiency or blemish.
>
> Devas, nāgas, yakṣas, kumbhāṇḍas,
> Humans and all other classes, producing their various sounds:
> In every one of their languages,
> I shall follow those sounds and teach the Dharma.
>
> Tamed, virtuous, diligently cultivating the ten perfections,
> May I never forget the mind of bodhi.
> Whatever evils have become obstructions:
> May all of these be utterly eliminated.
>
> From karma, afflictions, māras, and wrong paths,
> May I attain liberation while moving through the world,
> Unattached like a lotus upon water,
> Passing through space without obstruction like the sun and moon.
>
> Throughout all buddha-fields of the ten directions,
> May the suffering of the evil destinies be utterly eliminated.
> May sentient beings be established in places of peace and happiness,
> With beneficial practices bringing advantage to all.
>
> May I bring the practices of bodhi to fulfillment,
> Turning those practices in accordance with sentient beings.
> Revealing Samantabhadra Bodhisattva's practices,
> May I perform these practices to the end of future kalpas.
>
> Whoever can practice these practices together with me:
> May I always follow alongside them.
> In body-karma, speech-karma, and mind-karma,
> May my practices be one with theirs.
>
> All good friends who wish to benefit me,
> Who reveal and explain Samantabhadra's practices:
> May I always encounter them,
> And may I never go against their wishes.
>
> The great honored teachers, surrounded by their buddha-sons:
> May I always behold them directly.
> Among them, may I widely perform great offerings,
> Without weariness to the end of future kalpas.
>
> May I always uphold the most wondrous Dharma of all buddhas,
> Universally revealing the practices of the bodhisattvas.
> To purify Samantabhadra Bodhisattva's practices,
> In future kalpas I vow to practice.
>
> While revolving through the three realms of existence,
> May I attain inexhaustible merit and wisdom.
> Samādhi, wisdom, skillful means, and liberation:
> May I accomplish the inexhaustible treasury of all attainments.
>
> Upon one dust-mote, dust-mote numbers of buddha-fields;
> In one buddha-field, immeasurable buddhas,
> Surrounded by their buddha-sons, the honored ones in their midst:
> Practicing the bodhisattva practices, I gaze up at them.
>
> Thus, throughout boundless realms,
> Upon a single hair-tip, the three times are borne.
> Buddhas and buddha-fields, measureless as oceans;
> Practices like oceans, through many kalpas.
>
> One sound, with branches of speech as vast as the ocean:
> All of these are the pure sounds of the buddhas.
> Expounding in accord with the inclinations of all:
> May I always enter the teachings of the buddhas.
>
> When all the supreme ones traversing the three times
> Turn the unsurpassed Dharma-wheel,
> Into all the inexhaustible teachings of the buddhas,
> Through the power of wisdom, may I skillfully enter.
>
> All the many kalpas of the future,
> In a single instant I shall enter.
> All kalpas throughout the three times:
> Within the fraction of an instant I shall practice.
>
> The lions among humans traversing the three times:
> In every instant I gaze up at them all.
> Always in the supreme realms of the buddhas,
> Through the power of illusion-like liberation I enter.
>
> The adornments of buddha-fields throughout the three times,
> All established upon a single dust-mote:
> Throughout immeasurable realms,
> May all buddha-fields be universally adorned.
>
> All the future world-honored ones
> Who fully attain equal enlightenment and turn the Dharma-wheel,
> And display entering nirvāṇa and realizing the true limit:
> I vow to go before all buddhas.
>
> Through the power of supernatural abilities, swiftly and universally going;
> Through the power of the Great Vehicle, entering the universal gate;
> Through the power of practices, universally meritorious;
> Through the power of great compassion, universally pervading.
>
> Through the power of all good deeds and merit,
> Through the power of great wisdom free from attachment,
> Through the power of wisdom, skillful means, and samādhi:
> May the power of rightly cultivating bodhi be accomplished.
>
> Through the power of pure karma always,
> Forever eliminating the power of all afflictions,
> Rendering powerless the power of all māras:
> Fulfilling the power of Samantabhadra's practices.
>
> May oceans of buddha-fields be purified;
> May oceans of sentient beings be liberated.
> May oceans of dharmas be clearly seen;
> May oceans of wisdom be well realized.
>
> May oceans of practices be purified;
> May oceans of vows be fulfilled.
> May oceans of buddhas receive offerings;
> Through oceans of kalpas, may I practice without weariness.
>
> All the supreme honored ones traversing the three times,
> Whose practices and aspirations of bodhi are many:
> Through Samantabhadra's practices, having attained equal awakening,
> May I bring all of these to fulfillment.
>
> Most honored among all buddha-sons,
> Therefore named "Samantabhadra":
> That I may practice together with this great wisdom,
> I dedicate all these virtues.
>
> Body, speech, and mind completely pure,
> Practices pure, all buddha-fields also pure:
> However Samantabhadra skillfully dedicates merit,
> May I also follow in this practice.
>
> To practice the supremely good conduct of Samantabhadra,
> And also to fulfill the great vows of Mañjuśrī,
> Throughout all future kalpas without weariness:
> May all these bodhisattva practices be fulfilled.
>
> May such practices be without measure;
> May all merit also be without measure.
> While abiding in such immeasurable practices,
> May I know all transformations.
>
> If there were an end to the reaches of space,
> An end to sentient beings likewise,
> And an end to karma and afflictions:
> My vows would be equally boundless.
>
> All the immeasurable buddha-fields of the ten directions,
> Adorned with jewels and offered to the buddhas,
> And the supreme joys of humans and great beings,
> Offered for kalpas as numerous as dust-motes of buddha-fields:
>
> Yet if someone hears this sūtra of dedication
> And develops deep faith in accord with great bodhi,
> With merely one instant of faith in this,
> That person accomplishes the supreme accumulation of merit.
>
> If a person makes these vows of Samantabhadra,
> They will depart far from the evil destinies.
> Moreover, by forsaking all evil companions,
> That person will swiftly see Amitābha.
>
> All will attain the supreme lifespan,
> With a fortunate human birth in this life.
> Like Samantabhadra, the great bodhisattva,
> All will be able to swiftly accomplish this before long.
>
> Whoever, through the power of ignorance,
> Has committed the five heinous crimes and other grave transgressions:
> If that person recites these vows of Samantabhadra,
> All will swiftly be forever exhausted.
>
> In physical form, wisdom,
> Lineage, and appearance, all will be perfected.
> Neither māras nor non-Buddhists can overcome such a one;
> Throughout the three realms, all will make offerings.
>
> For the benefit of all sentient beings,
> Swiftly abiding beneath the bodhi tree,
> Attaining perfect, complete enlightenment, turning the Dharma-wheel,
> Vanquishing all the hosts of māras.
>
> Whoever upholds, recites, or explains
> These aspirational prayers of Samantabhadra's practices:
> The fruition thereof is known only to the buddhas.
> Do not be doubtful about unsurpassed bodhi.
>
> Just as Mañjuśrī has majestic virtue and wisdom,
> And Samantabhadra Bodhisattva likewise:
> I shall study and cultivate in accord with their practices
> And dedicate all these virtues.
>
> All the supreme ones traversing the three times,
> Their praises and dedications most excellent:
> All my roots of merit like these
> I dedicate for the practice of Samantabhadra's conduct.
>
> When I am about to reach the time of death,
> May all obscuring obstacles be removed.
> May I behold Amitābha face to face
> And travel to that land of Sukhāvatī.
>
> Having arrived there, may all these vows
> Be realized and manifest before me.
> May I bring all of these to perfect fulfillment,
> Benefiting beings throughout all worlds.
>
> In the beautiful bodhimaṇḍa of Sukhāvatī,
> Born upon a supreme, wondrous lotus,
> Before Amitābha, the World-Honored One:
> May I receive the prediction of awakening.
>
> Having received the supreme prediction,
> With transformation-bodies numbering many hundreds of koṭis,
> Through the power of wisdom throughout the ten directions,
> May I bring abundant benefit to all sentient beings.
