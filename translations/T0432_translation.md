# 佛說十吉祥經
## Sūtra of the Ten Auspicious Ones Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0432

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the city of Rājagṛha, on Mount Gṛdhrakūṭa, together with one thousand two hundred and fifty great bhikṣus and five thousand bodhisattvas.

At that time, the World-Honored One, with an immeasurable multitude of hundreds of thousands gathered around him, was teaching the Dharma.

In the great assembly there was a mahāsattva named Vimaladattaketu, who, empowered by the Buddha's awesome spiritual power, rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, placed his right knee on the ground, and with palms joined addressed the Buddha: "Are there presently Buddhas, World-Honored Ones, Tathāgatas, Arhats, Samyaksaṃbuddhas, such that if good men and good women who seek the Buddha's path hear their names and receive, uphold, read, and recite them, they will swiftly attain non-retrogression from the unsurpassed, true path?"

The Buddha told the mahāsattva Vimaladattaketu: "Excellent! Excellent! Son of good family, that you can ask such a question is of great benefit to many.

"There are, son of good family! In the east, beyond one Ganges-sand of buddha-worlds from here, there is a world called Ornament of the Directions. In that land there is a Buddha named Great Luminous Radiance Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, teaching the Dharma.

"Furthermore, son of good family! In the east, beyond two Ganges-sands of buddha-worlds, there is a world called Truth Victorious Over All Victories. In that land there is a Buddha named Wisdom Lamp Radiance Tathāgata. There is a world called Vajra; in that land there is a Buddha named Great Hero Tathāgata. There is a world called Pure Honored Abode; in that land there is a Buddha named Dustless Tathāgata. There is a world called Golden Radiance; in that land there is a Buddha named Supreme Image Banner, Tenfold Canopy King Tathāgata. There is a world called Great Awesome Spiritual Power; in that land there is a Buddha named Awesome Spiritual Power, Sovereign King Tathāgata. There is a world called Fragrant Incense; in that land there is a Buddha named Supreme Receiving, Supreme Reflection King Tathāgata. There is a world called Jewel Ornament; in that land there is a Buddha named Inner Jewel Tathāgata. There is a world called Ocean Lamp Radiance; in that land there is a Buddha named Great Ocean Tathāgata. There is a world called Ten Powers Lamp Radiance; in that land there is a Buddha named Ten Powers Manifest Tathāgata. You should know, Vimaladattaketu, if good men and good women who seek the Buddha's path were to fill Ganges-sand worlds with the seven treasures and use them as offerings to the Tathāgatas, the Perfectly Awakened Ones, and if other good men and good women were to hear these Buddhas' names, receive and recite them, embrace them in their hearts, and broadly explain them for others in the great assembly, the merit of the latter would surpass the former."

To further explain this meaning, the Buddha spoke in verse:

> As Ganges-sands of
> Buddha-worlds filled with the seven treasures,
> Used as offerings:
> If there are those who hear

> The Buddhas' names,
> Delight in them with faith without forgetting,
> Their merit surpasses the former.

> Those who hear the Buddhas' names,
> Reciting them without forgetting,
> Such persons should not doubt,
> Saying, "I shall not become a Buddha."
> The six supernatural powers without end,
> Through immeasurable kalpas,
> Their bodies shall be golden-colored,
> Adorned with auspicious marks.
> Those who uphold the Buddhas' names,
> Surpassing ten billion kalpas,
> Shall swiftly attain perfect enlightenment.

When the Buddha had finished teaching this sūtra, the mahāsattva Vimaladattaketu, all the bodhisattvas, devas, nāgas, ghosts, spirits, and worldly humans all rejoiced and reverently received the Buddha's teaching.
