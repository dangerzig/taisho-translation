# 佛說浴像功德經
## Sūtra of the Merit of Bathing the Image, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0697

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Ratnacinta (寶思惟)

---

Thus have I heard: At one time, the Bhagavān was in the city of Rājagṛha, on Vulture Peak, together with a great assembly of bhikṣus and innumerable bodhisattva mahāsattvas. At that time, there was a bodhisattva in the assembly named Viśuddhimati, who thought: "By what cause and condition does the Buddha Tathāgata obtain a pure body?" He further thought: "If one makes offerings in close attendance to the Buddha while he is in the world, and if after his parinirvāṇa one makes offerings to his relics: would the merit obtained by these two kinds of people be equal or not?" Having had this thought, sustained by the Buddha's awesome spiritual power, he rose from his seat, bowed at the Buddha's feet, and said: "World-Honored One! By what cause and condition does the Buddha Tathāgata obtain a pure body? If one makes offerings in close attendance to the Buddha while he is in the world, and if after his parinirvāṇa one makes offerings to his relics: would the merit obtained by these two kinds of people be equal or not?"

At that time, the World-Honored One told the Bodhisattva Viśuddhimati: "Excellent! Excellent! You are now able to ask such a question for the sake of the sentient beings of the future. Listen well! I shall explain it for you in detail."

At that time, the Bodhisattva Viśuddhimati said to the Buddha: "So be it, World-Honored One! I wish to hear with joy."

The Buddha told the Bodhisattva Viśuddhimati: "All the Buddha Tathāgatas, in seeking bodhi, in ages past cultivated samādhi, the precepts, concentration, patience, wisdom, loving-kindness, compassion, sympathetic joy, equanimity, liberation, the knowledge and vision of liberation, the powers, the fearlessnesses, all buddha-dharmas, and the knowledge of all modes, until all were purified. For this reason the Tathāgata obtained a pure body. Furthermore, by making offerings of flowers, incense, banners, and canopies; by bathing the Tathāgata's body with fragrant water; by covering it with a jeweled canopy above; and by making offerings of food, drink, drums, music, stringed instruments, songs, and praises to the Tathāgata: if one dedicates the merit of these deeds toward the knowledge of all modes, the merit obtained will be immeasurable and boundless, extending even to the attainment of unsurpassed bodhi. Why? Because the Tathāgata's wisdom is immeasurable, boundless, and inconceivable, and his merit is likewise!

"Viśuddhimati! After my parinirvāṇa, there are two kinds of relics: first, the Dharma-body; second, the transformation-body. If good men or good women make offerings to the relics, make a Buddha image even as small as a grain of barley, build a stūpa even as small as an āmalaka fruit, with a finial like a needle and a canopy like a floating leaf of duckweed, and place the Buddha's relics, even as small as a mustard seed, within it: the merit obtained is equal to that of my being present in the world, without any difference.

"Such a person obtains fifteen kinds of merit: first, one obtains a mind of pure recollection; second, one obtains a mind that accords with the Dharma; third, one obtains a mind of conscience and shame; fourth, one obtains the sight of the Tathāgata; fifth, one gives rise to a mind of pure faith; sixth, one is able to uphold the true Dharma; seventh, one practices as taught; eighth, one draws near to all the Buddhas; ninth, one is born at will in any buddha-land; tenth, if born among humans, one is born in a family of great lineage, with a gentle mind, respected by all; eleventh, as soon as one is born among humans, one obtains the mind of recollecting the Buddha; twelfth, the hosts of Māra are unable to disturb one; thirteenth, in the age of the declining Dharma, one is able to protect the true Dharma; fourteenth, one is always protected and sheltered by all the Buddhas of the ten directions; fifteenth, one swiftly attains the perfection of the five-fold Dharma-body."

At that time the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> If with a pure heart,
> After the Tathāgata's passing,
> One makes offerings to the relics,
> Or builds stūpas and temples,
> Or makes images of the Tathāgata,
> And before those stūpas and images,
> Sweeps and anoints the maṇḍala,
> And with various kinds of flowers and incense
> Scatters them upon it;
> And with the finest fragrant waters
> Bathes the Buddha image,
> And with the finest food and drink,
> Purely offered in devotion,
> Praises the Buddha's merit:
> Immeasurable, inconceivable,
> His wisdom and spiritual powers,
> And all his skillful means
> Have all reached the other shore.

At that time, the Bodhisattva Viśuddhimati, having heard the World-Honored One speak this verse, said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! While the Buddha is in the world and after his parinirvāṇa, in the future age, how should sentient beings bathe the image? I humbly wish the Tathāgata to reveal and explain this for the sake of sentient beings."

The Buddha said: "Viśuddhimati! As when the Buddha is in the world, sentient beings give rise to a pure mind; after the Buddha's parinirvāṇa, they should likewise do so. They should not cling to the notion of emptiness or existence. Toward all wholesome practices, their minds should be filled with longing and never grow weary. Why? In order to accomplish the Tathāgata's Dharma-body and reward-body. I have already taught you the Four Noble Truths, the twelve links of dependent origination, and the six perfections. Now I shall teach you the method of bathing the image, the most supreme among all kinds of offerings.

"Good man! If one wishes to bathe the image, one should use ox-head sandalwood, purple sandalwood, tamāla incense, gansung, ligusticum, white sandalwood, turmeric, camphor, aloeswood, musk, clove, and such various fine fragrances, using whatever one can obtain, to make a fragrant bath-water placed in a clean vessel. First prepare a square altar, spread a fine couch and seat, and place the Buddha upon it. With the various fragrant waters, bathe the image in order. After using the various fragrant waters to bathe the image all over, once more rinse it with clean water from above. Those who bathe the image should each take a small amount of the bathing water and place it upon their own head. They should burn various kinds of incense as offerings.

"When first pouring the water upon the image, one should recite this verse:

> 'I now bathe all the Tathāgatas,
> The assembly of pure wisdom, merit, and adornment.
> May the sentient beings of the five turbidities be freed from defilement;
> May they realize the Tathāgata's pure Dharma-body.'

"When burning incense, one should recite this verse:

> 'The fragrance of morality, concentration, wisdom, liberation, and the knowledge and vision of liberation
> Pervades the buddha-lands of the ten directions, always sweet.
> May this incense-smoke likewise
> Become the fivefold body of self and other.'"

When the World-Honored One had spoken this dharma, innumerable bodhisattva mahāsattvas in the assembly obtained the pure, immaculate samādhi and rose from their seats. Innumerable devas and humans attained the irreversibility of unsurpassed bodhi.

At that time Ānanda said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! What should this sūtra be called? How should we uphold it?"

The Buddha said: "This sūtra is called 'Bathing All the Buddhas and Obtaining Bodily Purity.' Uphold it in this way."

When this sūtra had been spoken, the entire assembly was filled with great joy, faithfully received it, and practiced it.
