# 長爪梵志請問經
## Sūtra of the Questions of the Long-Nailed Brahmin

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0584

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Yijing (義淨)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Bhagavān was in Rājagṛha on Mount Gṛdhrakūṭa, together with a great assembly of one thousand two hundred and fifty bhikṣus, as well as other bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, upāsikās, kings, great ministers, śramaṇas, brahmins, followers of other paths, devas, nāgas, yakṣas, humans, and non-humans, all gazing upon him reverently.

At that time the World-Honored One was teaching the wondrous Dharma realized by himself, which is good in the beginning, good in the middle, and good in the end, with skillful language and meaning, pure and complete, clean and bright, bearing the marks of the holy life.

At that time there was a long-nailed brahmin who came to the Buddha's presence, leaned on his staff, and stood. He asked: "Gautama! Have you truly made this declaration: 'The world arises from one's own karma; karma is what bestows; karma is the birthplace; karma is one's kin; karma is what one relies upon'?"

The Buddha said to the brahmin: "I have made this declaration: the world arises from one's own karma; karma is what bestows; karma is the birthplace; karma is one's kin; karma is what one relies upon."

The brahmin said: "If that is so, Śramaṇa Gautama, what previous karma did you perform that caused you to obtain an adamantine, indestructible, and firm body?"

The Buddha said to the brahmin: "In my previous lives I refrained from killing or harming the life-faculty of sentient beings. By the power of that karma, I have now obtained this fruit."

"Śramaṇa Gautama! What previous karma did you perform that caused you to obtain long, slender fingers with webbed markings?"

The Buddha said to the brahmin: "In my previous lives I refrained from stealing the property of others. By the power of that karma, I have now obtained this fruit."

"Śramaṇa Gautama! What previous karma did you perform that caused you to obtain a body fully endowed with form, strength, and complete faculties?"

The Buddha said to the brahmin: "In my previous lives I refrained from the defilement of desire with women. By the power of that karma, I have now obtained this fruit."

"Śramaṇa Gautama! What previous karma did you perform that caused you to obtain a broad, long tongue that extends to cover your own face?"

The Buddha said to the brahmin: "In my previous lives I refrained from false speech and deceiving others. By the power of that karma, I have now obtained this fruit."

"Śramaṇa Gautama! What previous karma did you perform that caused you to obtain a dignified gait like that of a lion?"

The Buddha said to the brahmin: "In my previous lives I refrained from all intoxicants and from places of heedlessness. By the power of that karma, I have now obtained this fruit."

"Śramaṇa Gautama! What previous karma did you perform that caused you to obtain a body adorned with wondrous marks of excellence?"

The Buddha said to the brahmin: "In my previous lives I refrained from singing, dancing, theatrical performances, and entertainments. By the power of that karma, I have now obtained this fruit."

"Śramaṇa Gautama! What previous karma did you perform that caused you to obtain a body suffused with a supremely fragrant scent?"

The Buddha said to the brahmin: "In my previous lives I refrained from perfumes, flower garlands, and ornamental adornments. By the power of that karma, I have now obtained this fruit."

"Śramaṇa Gautama! What previous karma did you perform that caused you to obtain the use of the adamantine, supreme, and wondrous seat?"

The Buddha said to the brahmin: "In my previous lives I refrained from high beds, large beds, and objects of arrogance and indulgence. By the power of that karma, I have now obtained this fruit."

"Śramaṇa Gautama! What previous karma did you perform that caused you to obtain forty teeth, brilliantly white and perfectly even?"

The Buddha said to the brahmin: "In my previous lives I refrained from eating food at improper times. By the power of that karma, I have now obtained this fruit."

"Śramaṇa Gautama! What previous karma did you perform that caused you to obtain the round and beautiful uṣṇīṣa atop your head?"

The Buddha said to the brahmin: "In my previous lives, before the Three Treasures, before my two teachers, before śramaṇas, brahmins, parents, and elders, in all places deserving of reverence, I placed the five points of my body on the ground and, with a mind free from arrogance, offered sincere prostrations. By the power of that karma, I have now obtained this fruit."

Then the brahmin, seeing the Buddha explain that causes and effects are not empty, said: "Gautama! What is this merit called? How should one receive and uphold it?"

The Buddha said: "This is called the eightfold purification precepts. If one can observe them for one day and one night, or for a longer period, receiving and upholding them from a teacher, one obtains such fruits as these."

At that time the long-nailed brahmin, having heard from the Buddha the teaching that by previously abstaining from base and evil deeds through the eight daily precepts one can obtain supreme and wondrous adornments, believed and accepted it deeply, and was filled with delight. Before the Buddha he let go of his arrogant mind, cast his staff to the ground, joined his palms in reverence, bowed at the Buddha's feet, and said: "World-Honored One! Today for the first time I know that the retribution of good and evil karma is not empty. From this day until the end of my life, I take refuge in the Buddha, the most honored among beings of two feet. Until the end of my life, I take refuge in the Dharma, the most honored among that which is free from desire. Until the end of my life, I take refuge in the Saṃgha, the most honored among assemblies. I accept the eight precepts of purification, from this time until tomorrow at sunrise. During this period:

> "I shall not harm any living being's life, not steal another's property,
> Not engage in sexual misconduct, not speak falsely, and from intoxicants and places of heedlessness,
> From flower garlands, singing and dancing, from high and large beds, and from eating at improper times,
> I now completely abstain, and uphold the pure eightfold precepts."

He said this a second and a third time.

The Buddha said to the brahmin: "Excellent! Excellent! So should you practice, and so should you uphold them."

When the World-Honored One had spoken this Dharma, the brahmin, the assembly of bhikṣus, the humans, devas, and all others were filled with great joy, and faithfully received and practiced it.
