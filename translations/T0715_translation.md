# 佛說舊城喻經
## Sūtra of the Parable of the Ancient City, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0715

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmabhadra (法賢)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was in the land of Śrāvastī, in the Jeta Grove of Anāthapiṇḍada, together with a great assembly.

At that time the Buddha told the bhikṣus: "Bhikṣus! In the past, before I had realized anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi, I was staying alone in a certain place when a doubt arose in my mind: 'For what reason do all sentient beings in the world suffer the pain of transmigration, namely birth, old age, and death, ceasing only to arise again? Because sentient beings do not truly know, they cannot escape the suffering of birth, old age, and death. I shall now reflect: from what cause does this suffering of old age and death arise, and from what condition does this old age and death come about?' Having thought thus, I let go of all mental grasping, settled my mind, and contemplated. Having carefully contemplated, I then truly knew: 'Old age and death exist because of birth; it is from the condition of birth that old age and death come about.'

"Having known this dharma, I further reflected: 'From what cause does birth arise, and from what condition does this dharma of birth come about?' Having thought thus, I let go of all mental grasping, settled my mind, and contemplated. Having carefully contemplated, I then truly knew: 'Birth arises because of becoming; it is from the condition of becoming that the dharma of birth arises.'

"Having known this dharma, I further reflected: 'From what cause does becoming arise, and from what condition does this dharma of becoming arise?' Having thought thus, I let go of all mental grasping, settled my mind, and contemplated. Having carefully contemplated, I then truly knew: 'Becoming arises because of grasping; it is from the condition of grasping that the dharma of becoming arises.'

"Having known this dharma, I further reflected: 'From what cause does grasping arise, and from what condition does this dharma of grasping come about?' Having thought thus, I let go of all mental grasping, settled my mind, and contemplated. Having carefully contemplated, I then truly knew: 'Grasping arises because of craving; it is from the condition of craving that the dharma of grasping comes about.'

"Having known this dharma, I further reflected: 'From what cause does craving arise, and from what condition does this dharma of craving come about?' Having thought thus, I let go of all mental grasping, settled my mind, and contemplated. Having carefully contemplated, I then truly knew: 'Craving arises because of feeling; it is from the condition of feeling that the dharma of craving comes about.'

"Having known this dharma, I further reflected: 'From what cause does feeling arise, and from what condition does this dharma of feeling come about?' Having thought thus, I let go of all mental grasping, settled my mind, and contemplated. Having carefully contemplated, I then truly knew: 'Feeling arises because of contact; it is from the condition of contact that the dharma of feeling comes about.'

"Having known this dharma, I further reflected: 'From what cause does contact arise, and from what condition does this dharma of contact come about?' Having thought thus, I let go of all mental grasping, settled my mind, and contemplated. Having carefully contemplated, I then truly knew: 'Contact arises because of the six sense bases; it is from the condition of the six sense bases that the dharma of contact comes about.'

"Having known this dharma, I further reflected: 'From what cause do the six sense bases arise, and from what condition does the dharma of the six sense bases come about?' Having thought thus, I let go of all mental grasping, settled my mind, and contemplated. Having carefully contemplated, I then truly knew: 'The six sense bases arise because of name-and-form; it is from the condition of name-and-form that the dharma of the six sense bases comes about.'

"Having known this dharma, I further reflected: 'From what cause does name-and-form arise, and from what condition does this dharma of name-and-form come about?' Having thought thus, I let go of all mental grasping, settled my mind, and contemplated. Having carefully contemplated, I then truly knew: 'Name-and-form arises because of consciousness; it is from the condition of consciousness that the dharma of name-and-form comes about.'

"Having known this dharma, I further reflected: 'From what cause does consciousness arise, and from what condition does this dharma of consciousness come about?' Having thought thus, I let go of all mental grasping, settled my mind, and contemplated. Having carefully contemplated, I then truly knew: 'This dharma of consciousness arises because of name-and-form; it is from the condition of name-and-form that the dharma of consciousness comes about. Only this condition of consciousness can give rise to all formations. Therefore: name-and-form is the condition for consciousness, and consciousness is the condition for name-and-form. Name-and-form is the condition for the six sense bases; the six sense bases are the condition for contact; contact is the condition for feeling; feeling is the condition for craving; craving is the condition for grasping; grasping is the condition for becoming; becoming is the condition for birth; birth is the condition for old age and death, grief, lamentation, suffering, and anguish. Therefore the great mass of suffering accumulates.'

"Having known this dharma, I further reflected: 'By what being absent is old age and death absent? By what ceasing is old age and death brought to cessation?' Having thought thus, I let go of all mental grasping, settled my mind, and contemplated. Having carefully contemplated, I then truly knew: 'If the dharma of birth is absent, then old age and death is absent. When the dharma of birth has ceased, old age and death also ceases.'

"Having known this dharma, I further reflected: 'By what being absent is the dharma of birth absent? By what ceasing is the dharma of birth brought to cessation?' Having thought thus, I let go of all mental grasping, settled my mind, and contemplated. Having carefully contemplated, I then truly knew: 'If the dharma of becoming is absent, then the dharma of birth is absent. When the dharma of becoming has ceased, the dharma of birth also ceases.'

"Having known this dharma, I further reflected: 'By what being absent is the dharma of becoming absent? By what ceasing is the dharma of becoming brought to cessation?' Having thought thus, I let go of all mental grasping, settled my mind, and contemplated. Having carefully contemplated, I then truly knew: 'If the dharma of grasping is absent, then the dharma of becoming is absent. When the dharma of grasping has ceased, the dharma of becoming also ceases.'

"Having known this dharma, I further reflected: 'By what being absent is the dharma of grasping absent? By what ceasing is the dharma of grasping brought to cessation?' Having thought thus, I let go of all mental grasping, settled my mind, and contemplated. Having carefully contemplated, I then truly knew: 'If the dharma of craving is absent, then the dharma of grasping is absent. When the dharma of craving has ceased, the dharma of grasping also ceases.'

"Having known this dharma, I further reflected: 'By what being absent is the dharma of craving absent? By what ceasing is the dharma of craving brought to cessation?' Having thought thus, I let go of all mental grasping, settled my mind, and contemplated. Having carefully contemplated, I then truly knew: 'If the dharma of feeling is absent, then the dharma of craving is absent. When the dharma of feeling has ceased, the dharma of craving also ceases.'

"Having known this dharma, I further reflected: 'By what being absent is the dharma of feeling absent? By what ceasing is the dharma of feeling brought to cessation?' Having thought thus, I let go of all mental grasping, settled my mind, and contemplated. Having carefully contemplated, I then truly knew: 'If the dharma of contact is absent, then the dharma of feeling is absent. When the dharma of contact has ceased, the dharma of feeling also ceases.'

"Having known this dharma, I further reflected: 'By what being absent is the dharma of contact absent? By what ceasing is the dharma of contact brought to cessation?' Having thought thus, I let go of all mental grasping, settled my mind, and contemplated. Having carefully contemplated, I then truly knew: 'If the six sense bases are absent, then the dharma of contact is absent. When the six sense bases have ceased, the dharma of contact also ceases.'

"Having known this dharma, I further reflected: 'By what being absent are the six sense bases absent? By what ceasing do the six sense bases also cease?' Having thought thus, I let go of all mental grasping, settled my mind, and contemplated. Having carefully contemplated, I then truly knew: 'If name-and-form is absent, then the six sense bases are absent. When name-and-form has ceased, the six sense bases also cease.'

"Having known this dharma, I further reflected: 'By what being absent is name-and-form absent? By what ceasing does name-and-form also cease?' Having thought thus, I let go of all mental grasping, settled my mind, and contemplated. Having carefully contemplated, I then truly knew: 'If the dharma of consciousness is absent, then name-and-form is absent. When the dharma of consciousness has ceased, name-and-form also ceases.'

"Having known this dharma, I further reflected: 'By what being absent is the dharma of consciousness absent? By what ceasing does the dharma of consciousness also cease?' Having thought thus, I let go of all mental grasping, settled my mind, and contemplated. Having carefully contemplated, I then truly knew: 'If the dharma of formations is absent, then the dharma of consciousness is absent. When the dharma of formations has ceased, the dharma of consciousness also ceases.'

"Having known this dharma, I further reflected: 'By what being absent is the dharma of formations absent? By what ceasing is the dharma of formations brought to cessation?' Having thought thus, I let go of all mental grasping, settled my mind, and contemplated. Having carefully contemplated, I then truly knew: 'If ignorance is absent, then the dharma of formations is absent. When ignorance has ceased, the dharma of formations also ceases. Therefore: when ignorance ceases, formations cease; when formations cease, consciousness ceases; when consciousness ceases, name-and-form ceases; when name-and-form ceases, the six sense bases cease; when the six sense bases cease, contact ceases; when contact ceases, feeling ceases; when feeling ceases, craving ceases; when craving ceases, grasping ceases; when grasping ceases, becoming ceases; when becoming ceases, birth ceases; when birth ceases, old age and death, grief, lamentation, suffering, and anguish cease. Therefore the great mass of suffering ceases.'

"Having clearly understood each of these dharmas, I further reflected: 'I have now traveled the path that the Buddha traveled. I have donned the armor that the ancients donned. I have arrived at the city of nirvāṇa where the ancients arrived.'"

The Buddha further said: "Bhikṣus! It is like a person who wishes to journey to a distant place. He follows the road that the ancients once traveled and dons the armor that the ancients once wore. Then, following the trail of the ancients, he reaches the old capital. He may pass through deep mountains or through vast wilderness. Traveling on without stopping, he arrives at that ancient city. The city is vast and grand; it was the capital of a king of old. This capital remains magnificent as before, with ponds, gardens, and pleasure groves all of exceptional beauty. Whoever sees it finds their heart without weariness or wish to depart. Seeing it, the person thinks: 'I shall now return to my own kingdom and report this matter to the king in full.' Having reached his own kingdom, he reports to the king: 'May the great king know! I donned the armor that the ancients once wore and followed the road that the ancients once traveled. Through deep mountains and vast wilderness, traveling without stopping, I arrived at an ancient city. The city is vast and grand; it was the capital of a king of old. That city with its ramparts remains magnificent as before, with ponds, gardens, and pleasure groves all of exceptional beauty. Whoever sees it finds their heart without weariness or wish to depart. The great king should go and make his capital there.' Having heard these words, the king approved the report and, together with his ministers, made his capital in that city. Because of the king's residence there, the capital became ever more magnificent, with the populace flourishing and prosperity and happiness doubling as before.

"Bhikṣus! I too am like this. I have traveled the ancient road that all the Buddhas traveled. I have donned the ancient armor that all the Buddhas wore. I have journeyed to the ancient city of nirvāṇa of all the Buddhas. Bhikṣus! What is the ancient road? What is the ancient armor? What is the ancient city? It is the noble Eightfold Path that the Buddhas of the past practiced: right view, right thought, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, and right concentration. Bhikṣus! This Eightfold Path is the ancient road, the ancient armor, the ancient city. What the former Buddhas practiced, I too have followed, and thereby I came to see the accumulation of old age and death. Therefore I realized the cessation of old age and death. So too I came to see the cessation of birth, becoming, grasping, craving, feeling, contact, the six sense bases, name-and-form, and consciousness. I also contemplated the accumulation of formations and brought about the cessation of formations. When the dharma of formations had ceased, ignorance also ceased. When ignorance had ceased, there was nothing left to contemplate. At that time, by my own spiritual power, I attained perfect and complete enlightenment.

"Bhikṣus! The right Dharma that I have proclaimed, you should diligently study it thus, practice it thus, remember and cultivate it, and accomplish the holy life. Proclaim the Dharma throughout the heavens and the human world, and broadly create great benefit for all sentient beings. The bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, upāsikās, brahmins, non-Buddhist Nirgrantha ascetics, and others should likewise cultivate and proclaim it, and broadly create great benefit for all sentient beings."

At that time, when the World-Honored One had spoken this sūtra, the entire great assembly, hearing what the Buddha had taught, faithfully received it and practiced it.
