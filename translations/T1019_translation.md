# 大方廣佛華嚴經入法界品四十二字觀門
## Gate of Contemplation on the Forty-Two Syllables from the Entering the Dharma-Realm Chapter of the Avataṃsaka Sūtra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1019

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空)

---

At that time, the youth Sudhana descended from the heavenly palace and proceeded toward the city of Kapilavastu. He arrived at the place of the youth Viśvāmitra, who was skilled in all arts, bowed with his head to the ground in reverence, and stood to one side. He addressed him: "Holy one! I have already generated the mind of anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi, but I do not yet know how a bodhisattva should study the bodhisattva's practice or how to cultivate the bodhisattva's path. I have heard that the holy one excels in instruction. I pray you will teach me." At that time, the youth told Sudhana: "Good man! I have obtained a bodhisattva liberation called 'Skilled in All Arts.' I constantly recite and uphold the root syllables that enter this liberation.

"When one pronounces the syllable *a* (the superior syllable), one enters the gate of Prajñāpāramitā of non-differentiated realm through the awesome virtue of the bodhisattva, awakening to the fact that all dharmas are originally unborn.

"When one pronounces the syllable *ra*, one enters the gate of Prajñāpāramitā of boundless differentiation, awakening to the fact that all dharmas are free from dust and defilement.

"When one pronounces the syllable *pa*, one enters the gate of Prajñāpāramitā of the edge of the dharma-realm, awakening to the fact that the supreme meaning of all dharmas is non-apprehensible.

"When one pronounces the syllable *ca* (lightly aspirated), one enters the gate of Prajñāpāramitā of universal wheel severing differentiation, awakening to the fact that all dharmas are without conditioned activity.

"When one pronounces the syllable *ṇa* (retroflexed), one enters the gate of Prajñāpāramitā of the non-ālaya realm, awakening to the fact that the nature and marks of all dharmas are non-apprehensible.

"When one pronounces the syllable *la*, one enters the gate of Prajñāpāramitā free from defilement, awakening to the fact that all dharmas transcend the world, because the causal condition of the link of craving never arises.

"When one pronounces the syllable *da*, one enters the gate of Prajñāpāramitā of non-retrogressive preparatory practice, awakening to the fact that all dharmas are tamed, quiescent, and of true-suchness equality without discrimination.

"When one pronounces the syllable *ma*, one enters the gate of Prajñāpāramitā of the vajra-ground, awakening to the fact that all dharmas are free from bondage and release.

"When one pronounces the syllable *ṇa* (the superior syllable), one enters the gate of Prajñāpāramitā of the universal wheel, awakening to the fact that all dharmas are free from the heat of deceptive defilement and attain coolness.

"When one pronounces the syllable *ṣa*, one enters the gate of Prajñāpāramitā of the ocean-treasury, awakening to the fact that all dharmas are without obstruction.

"When one pronounces the syllable *va*, one enters the gate of Prajñāpāramitā of universal arising and abiding, awakening to the fact that the path of speech is cut off for all dharmas.

"When one pronounces the syllable *ta* (the superior syllable), one enters the gate of Prajñāpāramitā illuminating dust and defilement, awakening to the fact that true suchness of all dharmas is unmoving.

"When one pronounces the syllable *ya*, one enters the gate of Prajñāpāramitā of differentiated accumulation, awakening to the fact that all dharmas are truly and really unborn.

"When one pronounces the syllable *ṣṭa* (combined, superior), one enters the gate of Prajñāpāramitā of universal radiance extinguishing heat and affliction, awakening to the fact that the mark of subjugation and sustaining of all dharmas is non-apprehensible.

"When one pronounces the syllable *ka* (the superior syllable), one enters the gate of Prajñāpāramitā of differentiated kinds, awakening to the fact that the agent of all dharmas is non-apprehensible.

"When one pronounces the syllable *sa* (the superior syllable), one enters the gate of Prajñāpāramitā of immediately descending great rain, awakening to the fact that the time-equality nature of all dharmas is non-apprehensible.

"When one pronounces the syllable *ma* (lightly aspirated), one enters the gate of Prajñāpāramitā of the great swift multitude of peaks, awakening to the fact that the self-grasping nature of all dharmas is non-apprehensible.

"When one pronounces the syllable *ga*, one enters the gate of Prajñāpāramitā of the universal wheel of nourishment, awakening to the fact that the grasping nature of activity of all dharmas is non-apprehensible.

"When one pronounces the syllable *tha* (the superior syllable), one enters the gate of Prajñāpāramitā of non-differentiated true suchness, awakening to the fact that the location of all dharmas is non-apprehensible.

"When one pronounces the syllable *ja*, one enters the gate of Prajñāpāramitā of the pure source of worldly transmigration, awakening to the fact that the producer and produced of all dharmas are non-apprehensible.

"When one pronounces the syllable *svā* (combined), one enters the gate of Prajñāpāramitā of recollecting the adornments of all the Buddhas, awakening to the fact that the nature of security of all dharmas is non-apprehensible.

"When one pronounces the syllable *dha*, one enters the gate of Prajñāpāramitā of contemplating the bodhimaṇḍa of the dharma-realm, awakening to the fact that the nature of sustaining the realms of all dharmas is non-apprehensible.

"When one pronounces the syllable *śa*, one enters the gate of Prajñāpāramitā of following all the Buddha's teachings, awakening to the fact that the nature of quiescence of all dharmas is non-apprehensible.

"When one pronounces the syllable *kha* (the superior syllable), one enters the gate of Prajñāpāramitā of the causal ground of present practice and the wisdom-treasury, awakening to the fact that the empty-space nature of all dharmas is non-apprehensible.

"When one pronounces the syllable *kṣa* (combined), one enters the gate of Prajñāpāramitā of the ocean-treasury of decisive cessation of all karmas, awakening to the fact that the nature of exhaustion of all dharmas is non-apprehensible.

"When one pronounces the syllable *sta* (combined, superior), one enters the gate of Prajñāpāramitā of crushing afflictions and the radiance of purity, awakening to the fact that the nature of sustaining the proper and improper and causing non-movement of all dharmas is non-apprehensible.

"When one pronounces the syllable *jña* (lightly aspirated, superior), one enters the gate of Prajñāpāramitā of understanding worldly arising, awakening to the fact that the nature of knower and known of all dharmas is non-apprehensible.

"When one pronounces the syllable *rtha* (combined, superior), one enters the gate of Prajñāpāramitā of the wisdom-bodhimaṇḍa of reversing the wheel of birth and death, awakening to the fact that the nature of clinging to meaning of all dharmas is non-apprehensible.

"When one pronounces the syllable *bha* (drawn out, departing tone), one enters the gate of Prajñāpāramitā of adorning the bodhimaṇḍa of all palaces, awakening to the fact that the destructible nature of all dharmas is non-apprehensible.

"When one pronounces the syllable *cha* (the superior syllable), one enters the gate of Prajñāpāramitā of the treasury-covering differentiated bodhimaṇḍa of preparatory practice, awakening to the fact that the nature of desire-covering of all dharmas is non-apprehensible.

"When one pronounces the syllable *sma* (combined), one enters the gate of Prajñāpāramitā of directly seeing and circumambulating all the Buddhas of the ten directions, awakening to the fact that the recollectable nature of all dharmas is non-apprehensible.

"When one pronounces the syllable *hva* (combined), one enters the gate of Prajñāpāramitā of the ocean-treasury of contemplating the readiness and capacity of all sentient beings and universally arising, awakening to the fact that the callable nature of all dharmas is non-apprehensible.

"When one pronounces the syllable *tsa* (combined), one enters the gate of Prajñāpāramitā of entering the source-foundation of the ocean of all merits and cultivated practice, awakening to the fact that the courageous nature of all dharmas is non-apprehensible.

"When one pronounces the syllable *gha* (departing tone), one enters the gate of Prajñāpāramitā of the adamantine ocean-treasury of upholding all Dharma-clouds, awakening to the fact that the nature of thick equality of all dharmas is non-apprehensible.

"When one pronounces the syllable *ṭha* (the superior syllable), one enters the gate of Prajñāpāramitā of vowing to visit the ten directions and directly seeing all the Buddhas, awakening to the fact that the nature of accumulation of all dharmas is non-apprehensible.

"When one pronounces the syllable *ṇa* (the superior syllable), one enters the gate of Prajñāpāramitā of the syllable-wheel accumulated koṭis of syllables, awakening to the fact that all dharmas are free from all clamor and contention, without going or coming, and that walking, standing, sitting, and lying down are non-apprehensible.

"When one pronounces the syllable *pha*, one enters the gate of Prajñāpāramitā of the bodhimaṇḍa visited in ripening all sentient beings, awakening to the fact that the universally pervading fruition of all dharmas is non-apprehensible.

"When one pronounces the syllable *ska* (combined, superior), one enters the gate of Prajñāpāramitā of the universal illumination of the radiance-wheel of the unattached and unobstructed liberation-ground treasury, awakening to the fact that the nature of the aggregated skandhas of all dharmas is non-apprehensible.

"When one pronounces the syllable *ysa* (combined, superior), one enters the gate of Prajñāpāramitā of proclaiming the realm of all the Buddha-Dharma, awakening to the fact that the marks of aging and decay of all dharmas are non-apprehensible.

"When one pronounces the syllable *śca* (combined, superior), one enters the gate of Prajñāpāramitā of universally illuminating all empty space with Dharma-cloud thunder-roars, awakening to the fact that the accumulated footprints of all dharmas are non-apprehensible.

"When one pronounces the syllable *ṭa* (the superior syllable), one enters the gate of Prajñāpāramitā of the selfless benefiting of sentient beings reaching the ultimate edge, awakening to the fact that the nature of compelling marks of all dharmas is non-apprehensible.

"When one pronounces the syllable *ḍha* (drawn out, departing tone), one enters the gate of Prajñāpāramitā of the non-differentiated treasury of the Dharma-wheel, awakening to the fact that the ultimate location of all dharmas is non-apprehensible.

"Good man! When I recite such root syllables entering the various liberations, these forty-two Prajñāpāramitā gates serve as the foremost. Through them, one enters immeasurable and innumerable Prajñāpāramitā gates.

"Furthermore, good man! Such syllable-gates are able to awaken one to the edge of the emptiness of dharmas. Apart from such syllables, the expression of the emptiness of all dharmas is non-apprehensible. Why? Because the meanings of such syllables cannot be proclaimed, cannot be displayed, cannot be grasped, cannot be written and upheld, and cannot be observed, for they are free from all marks. Good man! Just as empty space is the place to which all things return, so too these syllable-gates: the meaning of the emptiness of all dharmas enters these gates and thereby becomes clearly manifest. If a bodhisattva-mahāsattva obtains skillful wisdom regarding entry into these syllable-gates, then in all the meanings expressed and indicated by all sounds and words, there is no obstruction. One can fully realize and uphold the equal emptiness-nature of all dharmas, and obtains skillfulness in all forms of speech. If a bodhisattva-mahāsattva can hear such syllable-gates entering the liberations, seal them with the *a*-syllable seal, and having heard, receive, uphold, read, recite, and master them thoroughly, and explain them for others without craving fame or profit, then by this causal condition one obtains twenty kinds of supreme merit. What are the twenty? One obtains strong memory; one obtains supreme moral shame; one obtains adamantine strength; one obtains the purport of the Dharma; one obtains superior awakening; one obtains supreme wisdom; one obtains unobstructed eloquence; one obtains the gate of dhāraṇī; one obtains freedom from doubt; one obtains, upon hearing disagreeable or agreeable words, the non-arising of anger or attachment; one obtains dwelling in equality without high or low; one obtains skillfulness in the speech-sounds of sentient beings; one obtains skillfulness in the skandhas, āyatanas, and dhātus; one obtains skillfulness in dependent arising, causes, conditions, and dharmas; one obtains skillful knowledge of the superiority and inferiority of faculties, and skillful knowledge of others' minds; one obtains skillfulness in observing the stars and calendar; one obtains skillful knowledge of the divine ear, the recollection of past abodes, the mastery of miraculous powers, and the knowledge of the death and rebirth of beings; one obtains skillful knowledge of the exhaustion of the outflows; one obtains skillful knowledge of what is proper and improper; one obtains skillfulness in the deportment of coming, going, and other activities. These are the twenty kinds of supreme merit. Good man! I only know this wisdom of entering the root syllables of the various liberations. As for the bodhisattva-mahāsattvas who, in all the skillful arts of the world, penetrate to the other shore with wisdom: how could I know or express the practices of their merit?" At that time, the youth Sudhana bowed his head in reverence at the feet of Viśvāmitra, circumambulated innumerable times, and departed with longing and admiration.
