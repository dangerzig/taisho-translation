# 普賢金剛薩埵略瑜伽念誦儀軌
## Abbreviated Yoga Recitation Ritual of Samantabhadra Vajrasattva

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1124

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

I shall now explain the method of vajra recitation of body, speech, and mind of Samantabhadra Bodhisattva. By cultivating this method, one becomes equal to Vajrasattva. The practitioner who dwells on the stage of resolute practice must have previously entered the great maṇḍala of the Vajradhātu, received the bodhicitta precepts, and possess a mind of great compassion to rescue and bring comfort to all sentient beings, not sparing even one's own life. Moment by moment, one should constantly aspire to attain the body of Samantabhadra Bodhisattva and diligently practice in bodily action, contemplating all buddhas and bodhisattvas as if directly before one's eyes.

In one's dwelling, whether a mountain hermitage, a meditation chamber, a monastery, or a home, one establishes a practice site, facing east or west as convenient. Sitting upright in cross-legged posture, either full lotus, Samantabhadra lotus, or a comfortable position, one extends the mind to pervade the entire realm of sentient beings, purifying the three activities of body, speech, and mind of all beings. The mantra:

*Oṃ svabhāva-śuddhaḥ haṃ.*

Next, one should visualize the Tathāgata with all his marks and excellent features complete, and mentally make offerings: with celestial fine perfumes, flower-garlands, incense, lamps, candles, food and drink, and various praises. Then, following the four directional prostration mudrās and mantras, one prostrates to the tathāgatas of the four directions, offering one's body. This constitutes receiving the unobstructed wisdom-discipline precepts of the three times. One then rises, places the right knee on the ground, forms the Vajra-samaya mudrā at the heart, recites, and releases it above the crown. By forming this mudrā and reciting the mantra, one accomplishes universal prostration, offering, and service to all tathāgatas. The mantra:

*Oṃ vajra hūṃ.*

Next, one should confess one's transgressions, rejoice in merit, request the turning of the Dharma wheel, transfer merit, and make vows. Then, sitting in cross-legged posture, one reflects: "May all sentient beings attain the supreme supramundane accomplishment." The mantra:

*Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-abhiṣikta sarva-sattvānāṃ sarva-siddhayo saṃprāptaṃ tathāgata-sthādhiṣṭhitaṃ.*

Next, form the Vajra Palm mudrā and recite the mantra three times. The mantra:

*Oṃ vajrāñjali.*

By forming this mudrā and reciting the mantra, all accomplishments of the yoga-gates of correspondence are achieved. Form the Vajra Bond mudrā and recite the mantra:

*Vajra bandha.*

By forming this, all fetters are liberated, and the ten pāramitās are instantaneously perfected. Next, with the Bond mudrā, strike the chest three times while reciting the mantra:

*Oṃ vajra bandha trāṭ.*

By forming this mudrā and reciting the mantra, one enters the maṇḍala, is guided in by the ācārya, and the three-activity vajra of Vajrasattva enters the disciple's heart, causing the self-nature vajra-wisdom to be activated and manifested.

Next, form the Vajra Universal Entry mudrā: with the previous Vajra Bond, insert both thumbs into the palms and place them between the ring fingers. Recite the mantra:

*Oṃ vajra āveśa aḥ.*

By forming this mudrā, the three-activity vajra performs a great āveśa (entry) within the body, and one obtains great spiritual power and majestic virtue.

Next, based on the previous mudrā, bend both index fingers to press against the backs of the thumbs; this becomes the Vajra Fist mudrā. The mantra:

*Oṃ vajra muṣṭi aḥ.*

By forming this mudrā, the three-activity vajra abides firmly without scattering or loss.

Next, with the previous Vajra Bond mudrā, raise both middle fingers joined together; this is the Vajrasattva mudrā. The mantra:

*Oṃ vajra samaya sattvaṃ.*

By forming this mudrā, the practitioner should abide in the samādhi of Samantabhadra Bodhisattva, seated upon a moon disc, with the body's form and light complete and radiant.

Next, form the Surata Great Vow Reality mudrā: with the Bond mudrā, touch the tips of both thumbs and both little fingers together like a single-pronged vajra, with both middle fingers inserted into the palms, joined and upright, pressing against the heart. This completes it. One then reflects: "My body has now become Samantabhadra Bodhisattva." At the moment this thought arises, one achieves boundless liberation, contemplating all sentient beings as no different from self and other, with the great compassion of shared embodiment. Then recite the Great Vow Reality mantra:

*Oṃ vajra samaya hoḥ surata sattvaṃ.*

Next, one should abide in the samādhi of the Trailokyavijaya Wrathful Vajra. Establish the mudrā: with four faces and eight arms, majestic and radiant, blazing with light like the conflagration at the end of a kalpa, the left foot treading upon Maheśvara and the right foot upon Umā. With both hands in Vajra Fists, the little fingers hooking each other in reverse, the index fingers raised, rotate the mudrā to the left to ward off all māras of humans and devas and all who create obstacles, then rotate to the right to establish the directional boundaries. Even the buddhas and bodhisattvas do not transgress these boundaries; how much less those in the three realms who create obstacles! Then stamp the mudrā upon the heart, forehead, throat, and crown. The mantra:

*Oṃ sumbhani sumbhani hūṃ gṛhṇa gṛhṇa hūṃ gṛhṇapaya hūṃ ānaya hoḥ bhagavan vajra hūṃ phaṭ.*

By forming this mudrā and reciting the mantra with the three mysteries in correspondence, all the defiled seeds within the ālaya-vijñāna are burned to ashes by this vajra-wisdom fire, and no external obstacle can create hindrance.

Next, form the Lotus Family Samaya mudrā: with both hands in Vajra Bond, raise both thumbs and both little fingers joined together. Place the mudrā at the mouth and visualize one's own body as identical to Vajradharma Bodhisattva. The mantra:

*Oṃ vajra samaya sattvaṃ.*

By forming this mudrā, all the samādhi yogas being cultivated become directly manifest.

Next, form the Samādhi mudrā and contemplate the buddhas throughout space, as numerous as sesame seeds. Then recite the mantra:

*Oṃ sarva-yoga-citta-utpādayāmi.*

By forming this mudrā and reciting the mantra, all obstacles are eliminated, one obtains peace and joyful delight, transcends the realm of Māra, and becomes equal to all buddhas, receiving the offerings of all worldly beings and devas. Then recite the Penetrating Mind mantra:

*Oṃ citta-prativedhaṃ karomi.*

Next, enter the samādhi-wisdom of the nine kinds of conditioned arising, contemplating all dharmas as like an illusion, like a mirage, like a dream, like a reflection, like an echo, like a shadow, like the moon in water, like a transformation, and like empty space. Having contemplated thus, the mind penetrates both the defiled and the pure without obstruction, just like empty space. Next, one should enter the bodhicitta contemplation and recite the mantra. The Bodhicitta mantra:

*Oṃ bodhicitta-utpādayāmi.*

By forming this mudrā, one visualizes within the body, in the center of the chest, a perfectly full moon, pure, luminous, and cool. After reciting the Penetrating Bodhicitta mantra an unlimited number of times, the water of the mind becomes clear and settled, and the moon of bodhicitta appears reflected within it. Upon this full moon, one visualizes a five-pronged vajra, clearly and distinctly. Recite the mantra:

*Oṃ tiṣṭha vajra.*

By performing this visualization and reciting this mantra, the mind becomes like vajra.

Next, visualize the body as a five-pronged vajra. Recite the mantra:

*Vajrātmako 'haṃ.*

Next, visualize all the buddhas pervading space entering into the vajra and merging into one body. By performing this visualization, wisdom, and mantra recitation, the practitioner's three activities become like vajra. The practitioner should then visualize their own body as Samantabhadra Bodhisattva, wearing a crown of the five buddhas, the body like crystal of moon-color, the right hand holding a five-pronged vajra, the left hand holding a vajra bell, the body seated within a full moon disc, clearly and distinctly. Then recite the mantra:

*Oṃ vajrasattva samayo 'haṃ.*

Next, employ the Surata Vajra mudrā: form the Vajra Bond and insert the right thumb into the opening of the left hand; this completes it. With this mudrā, empower the heart, forehead, throat, and crown. The mantra:

*Oṃ surata sattvaṃ.*

Next, with both hands in Vajra Bond, raise both middle fingers with the upper joints bent like swords, bend both index fingers to press alongside the middle fingers, and bring both thumbs together as if in lotus posture; this completes it. This is called the Vajradhātu mudrā, also called the Five-Buddha Crown mudrā. The mantra:

*Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-ratna-abhiṣeka aḥ.*

Next, form the Consecration mudrā, placing it on the crown; use the same mudrā as before. The mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-māle abhiṣiñca maṃ.*

Next, with both hands in Vajra Fists at the forehead, wrap as if tying a garland band, then tie behind the head in the same manner. From the little fingers, let them drop down like trailing ribbons. The mudrā mantra:

*Oṃ dhaṃ.*

Next, form the Armor mudrā: with both hands in Vajra Fists, extend both index fingers, and at the fingertips visualize the syllables oṃ and dhaṃ. With both fingers, circle the heart three times, then the back reaching to the navel, both knees, again to the navel, then the waist returning to the heart, the left and right shoulders, the neck, then the crown, the forehead, and finally behind the head, close the fists as if fastening armor. The mantra:

*Oṃ abhaya vajra-kavaca-bandha-rakṣa maṃ hūṃ svāhā.*

Next, with both hands, rotate the fists in a dancing motion and give one exquisite clap to produce joy. The mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-tuṣya hoḥ.*

Next, form the Vajra Wheel Maṇḍala mudrā: both hands in Vajra Fists, with the index fingers and little fingers hooking each other; this completes it. Stamp the ground and the five places of the body. The mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-cakra hūṃ jaḥ hūṃ vaṃ hoḥ.*

Next, form the mudrā for Alerting All Holy Beings: cross both arms with the right pressing over the left, and snap the fingers. With each recitation, snap once, completing four times. The mantra:

*Oṃ vajra samāja jaḥ jaḥ.*

By performing this mudrā, Samantabhadra Bodhisattva and the entire retinue gather like clouds in the sky at that moment.

Next, form the mudrās and ritual for the four gate bodhisattvas. First, the eastern gate: Vajrāṅkuśa Bodhisattva (Vajra Hook), residing in the maṇḍala, blue in color. The southern gate: Vajrapāśa Bodhisattva (Vajra Lasso), yellow in color, holding a lasso as the mudrā. The western gate: Vajrasphota Bodhisattva (Vajra Chain), red in color, holding a chain as the mudrā. The northern gate: Vajraghaṇṭā Bodhisattva (Vajra Bell), green in color, holding a bell as the mudrā. Each of these four bodhisattvas wears a crown and garland of the five buddhas, adorned with various kinds of splendid ornaments.

Next, form the Hook mudrā: both hands in Vajra Fists, the little fingers hooking each other in reverse, the left index finger raised straight, the right index finger moving up and down like a hook. The mantra:

*Oṃ vajrāṅkuśe jaḥ.*

Next, without releasing the previous mudrā, change both index fingertips to touch each other like a ring; this is the Lasso mudrā. The mantra:

*Oṃ vajrapāśe hūṃ.*

Next, with the previous mudrā, cross both index fingers and thumbs with fingertips touching, and bend the arms; this is the Chain mudrā. The mantra:

*Oṃ vajrasphote vaṃ.*

Next, with the previous Chain mudrā, insert both thumbs into the palms and shake; this completes it. The mantra:

*Oṃ vajraghaṇṭe hoḥ.*

Next, recite the praise of the 108 names of the presiding bodhisattva. After reciting, form the Vajrasattva Great Vow Reality mudrā and recite the mantra to invite the holy assembly to descend into the maṇḍala. The mantra:

*He mahāsukha-vajrasattva ahehi-garimā mahāsukha-vajra-mukha-samaya-maṇa-pālaya pramoda muda surata-sattvaṃ anāraktu me bhava sutuṣya me bhava supoṣya me bhava anurañjaya me bhava. Bhagavan anadīna-dhānaka sarva-sattva-sarvāsiddhi me prayaccha. Iṣṭa-brahma-akṛśya-praveśa-samayairmā dhūrvaṃ vaśī karomi. Mai mudra-samantrapāne. Jaḥ hūṃ vaṃ hoḥ.*

By forming the mudrā and reciting the mantra, one alerts Samantabhadra Bodhisattva and all the holy retinue, delighting them so they come to the practice site without violating their original vows. This mudrā and mantra can summon, draw in, bind, and delight. When reciting the syllable *jaḥ*, the two middle fingers in the palms move back and forth like hooks. At *hūṃ*, they bend like a ring, forming the lasso. At *vaṃ*, they cross and interlock, forming the chain. At *hoḥ*, they shake like a bell, delighting all the holy ones. Recite three or four times.

Next, form the Arghya mudrā: with both hands in Vajra Palms, bring the tips of the middle fingers together, the index fingers behind the middle fingers in a hook shape, and each thumb pressing the base of the index finger; this completes it. With this mudrā, lift the arghya vessel and offer it at the forehead. If there is no arghya vessel in the practice site, simply cross both thumbs to form the Arghya mudrā. Visualize water of eight excellent qualities bathing the feet of all holy beings. The mantra:

*Parama-sukha-śayā-sāla-rito vilāsanā mi dhairanā me bhagavan daṃ jaḥ hūṃ vaṃ hoḥ hihi hihi praticcha kṛsu mañjari-ranātha.*

Through offering the arghya, one washes away the dust and defilements of beginningless afflictions and swiftly attains purity of body, speech, and mind.

Next, form the external offerings to all the deities. In the southeast, Vajralāsyā Bodhisattva (Play): with both Vajra Fists at the heart.

In the southwest, Vajrahāsyā Bodhisattva (Laughter): with both fists to the sides, opening outward.

In the northwest, Vajragītā Bodhisattva (Song): the left hand in a fist, arm raised, index finger pointing toward the body as if holding a harp, the right hand in a plucking motion.

In the northeast, Vajranṛtyā Bodhisattva (Dance): with both fists rotating in a dance mudrā. All four of these deities are golden in form. With the Play mudrā as the starting point, rotate both fists around the heart left and right, then recite the mantra:

*He rati vajra-vilāsinī trāṭ.*

Next, form the Laughter mudrā: with the previous mudrā placed beside the mouth, turn the palms outward, and gradually open from the little fingers, spreading outward. Maintain a smiling countenance. Recite the mantra:

*He rati vajra-hāse haha.*

Next, with the right hand in a fist, index finger raised, arm bent toward the body as if holding a harp, and the right hand in a fist with index finger raised in a plucking motion; this is the Song mudrā. The mantra:

*He rati vajra-gīte titi.*

Next, with both fists rotating from the heart upward, gradually rising to the crown, palms joining then opening; this is the Dance mudrā. The mantra:

*He rati vajra-nṛtye vaipa vaipa.*

Next, form the Cloud Vajra Bodhisattva Incense mudrā: with both fists placed together and cast downward; this completes it. Visualize this deity in the inner court's southeast corner, with form and garments entirely black, holding an incense burner as the mudrā. Having performed this visualization, recite the mantra:

*Oṃ megha-vajrī ghuru ghuru.*

Next, with both fists first moving as in the dance, then both fists placed together and raised upward as if scattering flowers; this is the Spring Vajra Bodhisattva mudrā. Visualize this deity in the inner court's southwest corner, with form and garments entirely white, holding flowers. The mantra:

*Oṃ madhu-vajrī kuṃ kuṃ.*

Next, form the Autumn Vajra Bodhisattva Lamp mudrā: both fists placed together with both thumbs raised upright. Visualize this deity in the inner court's northwest corner, with form and garments entirely red, holding a lamp as the mudrā. The mantra:

*Oṃ śārada-vajrī aṃ aṃ.*

Next, form the Cloud Vajra Bodhisattva mudrā: with both fists inverted, rub the chest and then spread outward to both sides. Visualize this deity in the inner court's northeast corner, with form and garments entirely green, holding a perfume vessel. The mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-śeṣire hūṃ hūṃ.*

Next, in the central position of the maṇḍala, visualize a full moon, and within the moon disc, Samantabhadra Bodhisattva abiding in the Vajra Pride mudrā.

Next, before the Great Being, visualize Vajrarāga (Desire Vajra), with form and garments red, wearing a crown, garland, and necklace of various adornments, eyes gazing upon the Great Being, abiding in the Vajra Bow and Arrow mudrā.

Next, to the right of the Great Being, visualize Kelikila, white in color, with both Vajra Fists crossing the arms, abiding in the Embrace mudrā.

Next, behind the Great Being, visualize Vajrasādhu (Love Vajra), with form and garments entirely blue, the left arm raised holding a makara banner, the right hand supporting the left elbow, both hands together holding the banner.

To the left of the Great Being, visualize Vajramāna (Pride Vajra), with form and garments entirely yellow, with both Vajra Fists placed on the hips, the head tilting slightly to the left. These five deities all abide in the Karma-samādhi mudrā.

Next recite the Vajra mantra:

*Jaḥ vajra-nṛṣṭi sāyake māṭha.*

Next recite the Kelikila mantra:

*Hūṃ vajra-kelikīle hūṃ.*

Next recite the Vajra Love mantra:

*Vaṃ vajra-niṣṭa smara rāṭa.*

Next recite the Pride Bodhisattva mantra:

*Hoḥ vajra-kāme śvarītaṃ.*

In forming the above seventeen holy beings with their forms, colors, garments, flower-seats, and moon discs, along with one's own body abiding in the great mudrā, all surrounded by their retinues, one must make everything clear and vivid.

Next, one should recite the Praise of Samantabhadra Bodhisattva:

*Sarvānurāga-sukha-sattva-nāna-sattvaṃ vajrasattva-parama-surata-bhavaṃ me mahā-sukha-nṛjūce rāya-japratipanīya siddha-cara gopa-praṇatāya.*

While reciting the first verse of the praise, form the Vajrarāga Karma mudrā.

While reciting the second verse, form the Kelikila Vajra Karma mudrā.

While reciting the third verse, form the Vajrasādhu Karma mudrā.

While reciting the fourth verse, form the Vajramāna Karma mudrā.

This completes the four kinds of hymns.

The four kinds of dance mudrās are employed when entering the great samādhi during the recitation of praise.

Next, recite the Mahāyāna Amoghā-samaya Reality mantra:

*Oṃ mahāsukha-vajrasattva jaḥ hūṃ vaṃ hoḥ surata sattvaṃ.*

Abide in the great mudrā, equal to Samantabhadra Bodhisattva. If one wishes to accomplish the presiding deity, one should perform recitation for one month, with unlimited recitation at four times daily. When fatigued, release the mudrā, join the palms in vajra añjali, and bow with the whole body. Use this as a rest, so that the mind does not become weary or averse.

On the night of the full moon, form the great mudrā and recite throughout the night until dawn. Samantabhadra Bodhisattva will come, with body-light like a moon disc, and embrace the practitioner, entering into every part of the body. The practitioner's body becomes identical to Samantabhadra's, with the five-buddha crown, wearing celestial garlands, jeweled necklaces, and flower-wreaths. Body, speech, and mind become like Vajrasattva's. All relatives who see that person manifesting such majestic virtue will be filled with astonishment and will bow in reverence and worship. That person will constantly perform great spiritual powers in their own home, manifest the body of a buddha and display great spiritual powers, and also manifest the body of the Trailokyavijaya Vajra of the three times, taming all who are difficult to tame. Flying through the sky at will, freely traveling to limitless worlds to make offerings to all buddhas, enjoying the sublime celestial five desires, with a lifespan lasting as long as space itself, benefiting and delighting boundless sentient beings to great profit, one ultimately attains the body of Vairocana Buddha.
