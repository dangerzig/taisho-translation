# 佛說八大菩薩經
## Sūtra of the Eight Great Bodhisattvas Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0490

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmabhadra (法賢)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the land of Śrāvastī, at the Jetavana Monastery of Anāthapiṇḍada, together with one thousand two hundred and fifty great bhikṣus, and eight great bodhisattva-mahāsattvas. Their names were:

Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva, Maitreya Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva, Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva, Samantabhadra Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva, Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva, Sarvanīvaraṇaviṣkambhin Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva, and Kṣitigarbha Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva, who served as leaders. There were also many great bodhisattva-mahāsattvas. Their names were:

Ajita Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva, Nāgalakṣaṇa Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva, Prītimanā Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva, Vimalagarbha Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva, Vimalakīrti Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva, Jñānarāja Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva, Anantasena Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva, Jñānaprabha Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva, Prajñāprabha Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva, Prajñāpradīpa Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva, Jñānapradīpa Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva, Brahmadatta Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva, and Devamukuṭa Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva. All these great bodhisattvas came and were seated in the assembly.

At that time, the World-Honored One told Śāriputra: "Listen well now. Past the eastern direction, beyond worlds as numerous as the sands of the Ganges, there is a buddha-land called Invincible. In that land there is a Buddha named Suśīlamaṅgala Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is presently teaching the sublime Dharma for sentient beings.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra! Again past the eastern direction, beyond ten Ganges-sands of worlds, there is a buddha-land called Selfless. In that land there is a Buddha named Samantāloka Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is presently teaching the sublime Dharma for sentient beings.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra! Again past the eastern direction, beyond three Ganges-sands of worlds, there is a buddha-land called Good Love. In that land there is a Buddha named Maṅgala Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is presently teaching the sublime Dharma for sentient beings.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra! Again past the eastern direction, beyond thirty-four Ganges-sands of worlds, there is a buddha-land called Tranquil Treasury. In that land there is a Buddha named Indraketudvajarāja Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is presently teaching the sublime Dharma for sentient beings.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra! Again past the eastern direction, beyond five Ganges-sands of worlds, there is a buddha-land called Free from Dust. In that land there is a Buddha named Puṇyatejaḥprabhāsvarendra Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is presently teaching the sublime Dharma for sentient beings."

The Buddha told Śāriputra: "If good men and good women hear the names of these Buddhas, Tathāgatas, in this sūtra, and listen single-mindedly, or recollect them themselves, or copy, read, and recite them, or explain them for others, then at the end of their lives such persons will not fall into the evil destinies. They will not be born in border regions, or among those of wrong views, evil conduct, or low-born families. Nor will they be born in the long-life heavens. Nor will they be born in the five turbidities of an evil age, during kalpas of famine, pestilence, or warfare. They will be free from the dangers of kings, water, fire, bandits, tigers, and wolves. They will always be born in buddha-lands or among devas and humans, with dignified bodily form, complete faculties, and fulfilled retinues. They will possess the knowledge of past lives, master the six pāramitās, practice the four immeasurables, penetrate all the profound Dharma-treasuries, and attain the supreme path."

When the Buddha had finished speaking this sūtra, the great bodhisattvas, Śāriputra, and the bhikṣus, together with the devas, humans, asuras, and others, hearing the Buddha's teaching, all greatly rejoiced, believed, accepted, and respectfully practiced it.
