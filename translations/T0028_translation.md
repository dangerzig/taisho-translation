# 園生樹經
## Sūtra of the Garden-Born Tree

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0028

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dānapāla

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was dwelling at Śrāvastī, in the Jetavana, the garden of Anāthapiṇḍada, together with an assembly of bhikṣus. At that time the Buddha addressed the bhikṣus, saying: "You should know that in the heaven of the Thirty-Three there is a great tree called the Garden-Born. Its spreading roots extend five yojanas, its height is one hundred yojanas, and its branches and leaves shade an area of fifty yojanas. When the assembly of devaputras comes at the proper time to wander and observe beneath that tree, the tree produces yellowish leaves. Upon seeing this, the devaputras are filled with joy, delight, and happiness. Not long thereafter the tree produces withered leaves. When the assembly of devaputras comes at the proper time to wander and observe, their delight and happiness increases further. Not long after that, a jeweled net appears, draped over the tree as adornment. When the assembly of devaputras comes at the proper time to wander and observe beneath the tree, their delight and happiness increases still further. Not long after that, buds appear. When the assembly of devaputras comes at the proper time to wander and observe beneath the tree and sees this, their delight and happiness increases yet further. Not long after that, seed pods appear. When the assembly of devaputras comes at the proper time to wander and observe beneath the tree, their delight and happiness increases further still. Not long after that, the outer sheaths appear. When the devaputras come at the proper time to wander and observe, their delight increases further. Not long after that, the Garden-Born tree opens into full blossom throughout all its branches. Its blossoms are pure and their fragrance rare and wondrous; when a gentle breeze stirs them, their fragrant perfume fills fifty yojanas; when a great wind blows, it fills a hundred yojanas; and a magnificent radiance illuminates a range of eighty yojanas. When the tree has thus blossomed, the assembly of devaputras, seeing it in full bloom, experience happiness doubly greater than before, and for the four months of summer they dwell beneath the tree in delight and enjoyment."

The Buddha addressed the bhikṣus: "You should know that this is the way of the Garden-Born tree: its wondrous blossoms and rare fragrance are loved and delighted in by all. So too it is with śrāvakas. When the tree first produces yellowish leaves, it is like when a śrāvaka first gives rise to faith, goes forth from household life, and turns toward the path. When the tree produces withered leaves, it is like when a śrāvaka shaves off beard and hair, dons the kāṣāya robe, and takes on the marks of a śrāvaka. When the tree produces the jeweled net, it is like when a śrāvaka grows weary of and detached from all desires, abandons unwholesome dharmas, and withdraws from various forms of discursive thinking and discrimination, attaining the first dhyāna, the samādhi of joy and pleasure born of seclusion.

"When the tree produces buds, it is like when a śrāvaka's outward bearing is composed and serene, while inwardly the mind rests in stillness, free from discursive thinking, with the concentrated mind unified in one-pointedness, attaining the second dhyāna, the samādhi of joy and pleasure born of concentration.

"When the tree produces seed pods, it is like when a śrāvaka is separated from all rapture and clinging, and the body attains lightness, ease, and wondrous pleasure, attaining the third dhyāna, the samādhi of wondrous pleasure with rapture transcended.

"When the tree produces the outer sheaths, it is like when a śrāvaka cuts off all sorrow, suffering, joy, and pleasure and abides in equanimity, attaining the fourth dhyāna, the samādhi of equanimity and mindfulness, purified.

"When the tree opens into full blossom, with its rare fragrance spreading everywhere and loved by all, it is like when a śrāvaka has utterly exhausted all āsravas with nothing of the āsravas remaining to increase, having realized the fruit of one beyond training, no longer subject to future existence, revered by humans and devas alike and receiving great offerings. Such is the correspondence with the blossoming of that tree."

The Buddha addressed the bhikṣus: "You should know that the devaputras of the Thirty-Three Heavens, each resplendent with a distinctive form and adorned with varied ornaments, gather together in the Sudharmā Hall, assembled and surrounding their lord, listening to and receiving the wondrous Dharma proclaimed by Śakra, lord of the devas. You bhikṣus, each having attained the purity and completeness of realized fruits, with the holy life fully accomplished, regarded with veneration by all, surround the World-Honored One, listening to and receiving his wondrous Dharma. It is just the same as that assembly of devas."

At that time the bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha expound the Sūtra of the Garden-Born Tree, each gave rise to joy and exaltation, and with faith received it and put it into practice.
