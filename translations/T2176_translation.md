# 諸阿闍梨真言密教部類總錄
## Comprehensive Catalog of Classifications of the Ācāryas' Mantra-Esoteric Teachings

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 2176

Translated from the Chinese. Japan, compiled by Annen (安然).

---

Based on eight catalogs, arranged into sixteen categories. The eight catalogs are: (1) the catalog of Reverend Chō of Mount Hiei; (2) the catalog of Reverend Kai of Kōya; (3) the catalog of Reverend Nin of Mount Hiei; (4) the catalog of Reverend Gyō of Reigan; (5) the catalog of Reverend Un of Anshō; (6) the catalog of Reverend Gyō of Oguri; (7) the catalog of Reverend Chin of Mount Hiei; (8) the catalog of Reverend Ei of Engaku.

The sixteen categories are: (1) the Abhiṣeka (Consecration) Dharma Catalog; (2) the Mahāvairocana Miscellaneous Catalog; (3) the Vajraśekhara Catalog; (4) the Susiddhikara Catalog; (5) the Various Tathāgatas Catalog; (6) the Various Buddhoṣṇīṣa Catalog; (7) the Various Buddha-Mother Catalog; (8) the Various Sūtra-Dharma Catalog; (9) the Avalokiteśvara Catalog; (10) the Various Bodhisattvas Catalog; (11) the Vajrapāṇi Catalog; (12) the Mundane Deities Catalog; (13) the Homa Offering Catalog; (14) the Worship and Confession Praise Catalog; (15) the Siddham Script Treatise Catalog; (16) the Stele Inscription and Biography Complete Catalog.

Within each of the sixteen categories, subdivisions are made by class. Within each class, the names of the sūtras and ritual procedures are listed. Under each name, the names of persons are further annotated. However, for fear of excessive length, only one character of the name is taken. The reader is asked for forbearance.

At the time of the ninth year of Gangyō [885 CE], the twenty-eighth day of the first month, at Gangyōji temple.

Preface by the imperially appointed abhiṣeka-transmitting Dharma-monk Annen.

---

I have privately examined the catalogs of the various ācāryas. In all cases, from within the Zhenyuan Catalog they extracted newly entered sūtras and ritual procedures to constitute the teaching-gate of the single school of mantra. The dhāraṇī-dharmas of the older translations were entirely excluded. As a result, students do not understand the reasoning for this selection and lack broad learning. Now, based on the secret catalogs of eight lineages, I have arranged them into twenty categories. Within each category, subcategories of dharma-classes are further divided. Within each class, I have considerably added older translations. Though they are not spirit-spells, I have also cited vow-texts. Moreover, among the various catalogs, some preserve only abbreviated titles based on the original catalog, while others list expanded names according to their preferences. This has made it difficult for later students to trace the originals. Now I record both the root and branch titles, and also show the differences between abbreviated and expanded entries. Furthermore, among the various catalogs, for those texts that the various ācāryas kept secret and did not record, if I have seen or heard of them, I have also added them.
