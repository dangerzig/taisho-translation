# 救諸眾生一切苦難經
## Sūtra on Saving All Sentient Beings from All Suffering and Difficulty

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 2915

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown (失譯)

---

In Mount Tiantai there was an old teacher, aged about ninety. In grief and weeping, with bloody tears streaming from his eyes, he cried aloud: "Suffering!"

His disciple Huitong joined his palms and made obeisance, tears falling from his eyes. "How can one escape these disasters and difficulties?"

The teacher replied to Huitong: "Sentient beings are perishing. Universally contemplate Maitreya. The calamities have already reached the regions of Xiang and Wei; right in the midst of this, in the third and fourth months, ghost soldiers will arise in turmoil without bound or limit. In the eighth and ninth months, at the final catastrophe, if sentient beings practice goodness, the ghost soldiers will destroy themselves. Heaven and earth will be in dark obscurity.

"Copy one copy of this sūtra and one household will be spared. Copy two copies and the six close relatives will be spared. Copy three copies and one village will be spared. Those who circulate this text are my true disciples. Those who slander this sūtra will enter Avīci Hell with no prospect of release. Those who see this sūtra and do not copy it will have their family line extinguished. Those who recite it with wholehearted sincerity will attain the Buddha-path."

> Black winds rise in the northwest;
> Ghost soldiers arise in the southeast.
> Forever heaven and earth remain dark —
> How can the mind find peace?
> First one must abstain from wine and meat;
> Let greed and anger no longer arise.
> Those who can be heedful of these matters
> Will attain the Buddha-path at once.

---

**Translator's note:** T2915 is a Chinese apocryphal text (疑偽經) rather than a translation from Sanskrit or another Indic language. It belongs to a genre of folk Buddhist prophetic literature predicting disasters and urging the copying and circulation of the text itself. The passage "已比相相魏之地" is likely corrupt; it appears to refer to the regions of Xiangzhou and Weizhou (in present-day Henan and Hebei). The character 愚 before "三月四月" is also of uncertain reading and may be a scribal corruption of 于 or a similar locative particle. The verse at the close follows a loose five-character couplet structure.
