# 無量壽如來觀行供養儀軌

## Ritual Manual for the Visualization Practice and Offerings to Amitāyus Tathāgata

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0930

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

At that time, Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva, within the great assembly of Vairocana Buddha, rose from his seat, joined his palms in reverence, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! For the sake of sentient beings burdened with evil karma in the defiled world of the final age to come, I shall teach the dhāraṇī of Amitāyus Buddha. By cultivating the three secret gates, one realizes the buddha-mindfulness samādhi, attains rebirth in the Pure Land, and enters the correct stage of a bodhisattva. It is not through small merit or unskillful wisdom that one attains birth in that land. Therefore, by relying on this teaching and practicing with right mindfulness, one will certainly be reborn in the Western Land of Ultimate Bliss at the highest grade of the highest birth and attain the first bhūmi.

"Whether lay or monastic, those who wish to be reborn in the Pure Land should first enter the maṇḍala and receive abhiṣeka, then receive the recitation ritual from a teacher. Whether in an excellent location or wherever one dwells, clean and purify the area and establish a square altar. Hang a celestial canopy above and drape banners all around. On the altar, arrange eight maṇḍalas and anoint the holy positions with ground white sandalwood. On the western face of the altar, place an image of Amitāyus. The practitioner sits on the eastern side of the altar, facing west toward the image, either on a kuśa grass mat or on a low-legged stool.

"Three times each day, scatter various flowers, burn various incense, and place two arghya vessels. Use bowls or cups of gold, silver, copper, stone, porcelain, or earthenware that have not been previously used, filled with perfumed water, and place them on the altar. At the four corners of the altar, place four precious vases. Incense, lamps, perfumed ointments, and food and drink should be prepared according to one's means. Empower each offering and make them sincerely.

"The practitioner should bathe daily and don fresh clean garments, or use a mantra empowerment as a substitute for bathing. Then reflect and contemplate: 'The original nature of all sentient beings is pure, but it is covered and obscured by adventitious defilements. Not awakening to true principle and having lost sight of awakening, they are submerged in birth and death and undergo immeasurable suffering. Therefore this teaching of the three secret empowerments is taught, to cause both self and others to become purified.' Then bring both hands together in the lotus-palm gesture and recite the mantra for purifying the three actions three times. The mantra reads:

*Oṃ svabhāva śuddha sarva-dharma svabhāva śuddho 'haṃ*

"Through the empowerment of this mantra, one achieves purification as an inner bathing of the mind.

"Each time one enters the practice hall, stand upright before the root deity, bring the palms together in the lotus gesture, close the eyes, and direct the mind. Visualize being in the Land of Ultimate Bliss, face to face with Amitāyus Tathāgata, together with all the bodhisattva retinue. Then prostrate with the five points of the body touching the ground, visualizing making obeisance before each and every buddha and bodhisattva. Recite the universal homage mantra:

*Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-pāda-vandanāṃ karomi*

"Then kneel on the right knee with palms joined at the heart, and with sincere devotion make a full confession of all karmic obstacles since beginningless time. Then rejoice in all the meritorious deeds cultivated by all buddhas, bodhisattvas, śrāvakas, pratyekabuddhas, and all sentient beings. Furthermore, request that all tathāgatas in the worlds of the ten directions who have attained perfect enlightenment turn the Dharma wheel. Request that all tathāgatas who are about to manifest nirvāṇa remain long in the world and not enter parinirvāṇa.

"Then make this vow: 'May all the immeasurable roots of goodness that I have accumulated through homage to the buddhas, confession, rejoicing, and exhortation, may I transfer this accumulated merit to all sentient beings. May all be reborn in the Land of Ultimate Bliss, see the Buddha, hear the Dharma, and swiftly realize unsurpassed perfect enlightenment.'

"Then sit in full cross-legged posture or half cross-legged posture, right leg pressing upon the left. Anoint the hands with perfume.

**Buddha Division Samaya**

"First form the Buddha Division Samaya Mudrā: bring both hands together in a hollow-palm gesture, open the two index fingers slightly bent and rest each alongside the upper joint of the middle finger, then open the two thumbs and each presses the lower first joint of the index finger. When the mudrā is formed, visualize Amitāyus Tathāgata with the thirty-two major marks and eighty minor marks clearly and distinctly. Then recite the Buddha Division Samaya Mantra:

*Oṃ tathāgatodbhavāya svāhā*

"Recite three or seven times, place the mudrā atop the crown, and release. By forming this mudrā and reciting this mantra, all buddhas of the Buddha Division are alerted and come to the assembly to empower and protect the mantra practitioner, swiftly enabling the purification of bodily karma, the destruction of karmic obstacles, and the increase of merit and wisdom.

**Lotus Division Samaya**

"Next form the Lotus Division Samaya Mudrā: bring both hands together in a hollow-palm gesture. The two thumbs and two little fingers each touch at their tips; the remaining six fingers are slightly bent, resembling an open lotus flower. When this mudrā is formed, visualize Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva with dignified and beautiful marks, surrounded by an immeasurable number of koṭis of holy beings of the Lotus family. Then recite the Lotus Division Samaya Mantra:

*Oṃ padmodbhavāya svāhā*

"Recite three or seven times with empowerment, place the mudrā at the crown, and release to the right. By forming this mudrā and reciting this mantra, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva and the holy assembly of the Lotus Division are alerted and come to empower the practitioner, enabling the purification of verbal karma, making one's voice dignified and authoritative so that people delight in hearing it, and gaining unobstructed eloquence and freedom in teaching the Dharma.

**Vajra Division Samaya**

"Next form the Vajra Division Samaya Mudrā: with the left hand face-down and the right hand face-up, let their backs touch. Cross the right thumb over the left little finger, and the left thumb over the right little finger. The six middle fingers are interlocked and pressed against the wrist, forming a three-pronged vajra shape. Form the mudrā at the heart and visualize Vajragarbha Bodhisattva with his marks and majestic radiance, surrounded by an immeasurable retinue of vajra-holders. Then recite the Vajra Division Samaya Mantra:

*Oṃ vajrodbhavāya svāhā*

"Recite three or seven times with empowerment, place the mudrā at the crown, and release to the left. By forming this mudrā and reciting this mantra, Vajragarbha Bodhisattva and the holy assembly of the Vajra Division are alerted and come to empower the practitioner, enabling the purification of mental karma, the realization of the mind of enlightenment, the manifestation of samādhi, and swift liberation.

**Armor Protection**

"Next form the Armor Protection Mudrā: the two little fingers and two ring fingers, right pressing left, are crossed inwardly. The two middle fingers stand upright with tips touching. The two index fingers are bent into hook shapes and placed against the back of the middle fingers without touching them. The two thumbs stand upright side by side and press against the ring fingers. When the mudrā is formed at the heart, recite the mantra and stamp five points of the body, one recitation per point: first the forehead, then the right shoulder, then the left shoulder, then the heart, then the throat. These are the five points.

"Then arouse the great mind of compassion, extending it to all sentient beings, wishing that all may be clad in the glorious armor of great compassion, swiftly freed from all obstacles and difficulties, and may they attain the supreme, extraordinary accomplishments of both the worldly and transcendent. Having contemplated thus, one becomes clad in vajra armor, and all māras dare not create obstacles. The armor protection mantra reads:

*Oṃ vajra-gini praṇapta-ya svāhā*

"Through forming this mudrā and reciting this mantra, by the power of a compassionate and caring mind, all heavenly māras and obstructors see the practitioner's majestic radiance blazing brilliantly like a solar disk. Each gives rise to a compassionate mind and is unable to create obstacles. Evil people likewise find no opportunity. Afflictions and karmic obstacles do not attach to the body. This also protects against the sufferings of the evil destinies in the future and hastens the realization of unsurpassed enlightenment.

**Vajra Stake for the Ground Boundary**

"Next form the Ground Boundary Vajra Stake Mudrā: first insert the right middle finger between the left index and middle fingers, and the right ring finger between the left ring and little fingers, both tips extending outward. Then wrap the left middle finger around the back of the right middle finger and insert it between the right index and middle fingers. Wrap the left ring finger around the back of the right ring finger and insert it between the right ring and little fingers. The two little fingers and two index fingers each touch at their tips. The two thumbs press together below. When this mudrā is formed, visualize the mudrā as a vajra shape. Touch the ground with the two thumbs, reciting the mantra once per touch. Do this three times, and an adamantine, indestructible seat is established. The lower-realm mantra reads:

*Oṃ kīli kīli vajra vajrī bhūr bandha bandha hūṃ phaṭ*

"Through forming this mudrā and the empowering power of the mantra, from below down to the vajra wheel boundary, an indestructible vajra realm is established. Even the most powerful māras cannot cause it to shake. With little effort, great accomplishment is attained. All foul and impure things in the ground are completely purified by the empowering power. The boundary follows the mind and is established in whatever size one intends.

**Vajra Wall**

"Next form the Vajra Wall Mudrā: following the previous ground boundary mudrā, open the palms and extend them upright with the two thumbs like a wall shape. Visualize from the mudrā a streaming of blazing flames. With the mudrā, revolve rightward around the body three times, corresponding to the previous ground boundary. An adamantine, indestructible city is established. The wall boundary mantra reads:

*Oṃ sara sara vajra-prakāra hūṃ phaṭ*

"Through forming this mudrā, reciting the mantra, and the power of contemplation, following the mind in whatever size, a vajra light-flame wall boundary is established in every corner. Māras, evil people, tigers, wolves, lions, and all poisonous creatures are unable to approach.

**Ākāśagarbha Offering**

"Next form the mudrā of Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva: bring both hands together in a gesture of reverence, with the two middle fingers right pressing left, crossed outwardly and pressed against the backs of the hands. The two index fingers are pressed together in a jewel shape. Visualize from the mudrā a streaming of immeasurable offering materials: garments, food and drink, palaces, pavilions, and towers, as extensively described in the yoga teachings. Then recite the mantra of Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva:

*Oṃ gagana sambhava vajra hoḥ*

"Even if the practitioner's contemplative power is feeble, by the empowering force of this mudrā and mantra, all the offerings become true and real, exactly as though one were making vast offerings within the Land of Ultimate Bliss itself.

**Transformation of the World**

"Next visualize within the altar the syllable *hrīḥ*, radiating great light the color of red beryl, illuminating the worlds of the ten directions. Sentient beings who encounter this light will all, without exception, have their karmic obstacles destroyed.

"Next form the Tathāgata Fist Mudrā: with the left hand, make a fist with the four fingers and extend the thumb upright. With the right hand, make a vajra fist and grasp the left thumb's nail. Stamp the ground with this fist mudrā and recite the mantra seven times to transform the world. The Tathāgata Fist Mantra reads:

*Oṃ bhūḥ khaṃ*

"Through the majestic power of forming this mudrā and reciting the mantra, this three-thousandfold great-thousandfold world is transformed into the Pure Land of Ultimate Bliss, with ground of the seven treasures. Water-birds, groves, and forests all proclaim the Dharma-sound, with immeasurable adornments as described in the sūtras. Then recite this verse:

> 'By the power of my own merit,
> By the Tathāgata's empowering force,
> And by the power of the dharmadhātu,
> May this become the Land of Bliss.'

"Through repeatedly practicing this concentration, the practitioner in this present life will see, each time within samādhi, the Land of Ultimate Bliss and Amitāyus Tathāgata amid the great bodhisattva assembly, and will hear the teaching of immeasurable sūtras. At the time of death, the mind will not be scattered. Samādhi will manifest, and in an instant one will be reborn in that land, born from a lotus flower, and realize the bodhisattva stage.

**Jeweled Chariot Invitation**

"Next form the Jeweled Chariot Mudrā: both hands face up, fingers interlaced with right pressing left. The two index fingers touch side by side. The two thumbs press the lower first joint of the index fingers. The sending-chariot mantra reads:

*Oṃ turu turu hūṃ*

"Through forming this mudrā, visualize a chariot adorned with the seven treasures, traveling to the Land of Ultimate Bliss. Invite Amitāyus Tathāgata together with all the bodhisattva retinue to board this chariot. Without releasing the mudrā, use the two thumbs to flick the tips of the two middle fingers toward yourself, and recite the chariot mantra:

*Namaḥ stri-ya divikānāṃ tathāgatānāṃ oṃ vajra-āñjya-ṃ-gha-ṃ-kara-ākṣaya svāhā*

"Then visualize the chariot arriving at the practice hall, hovering in space.

**Welcoming the Holy Assembly**

"Next form the Welcoming the Holy Assembly Mudrā: both hands, right pressing left, fingers interlaced inwardly into fists with palms touching. The left thumb is curled into the palm. The right thumb is bent into a hook shape, beckoning toward oneself. Then recite the welcoming mantra:

*Oṃ ālolikā ehy ehi svāhā*

"Through forming this mudrā and reciting the mantra to invite them, Amitāyus Tathāgata, not abandoning his compassionate vow, comes to the Pure Land practice hall created by this samādhi, together with an immeasurable number of koṭis of great bodhisattvas, to accept the practitioner's offerings and swiftly enable the highest accomplishment.

**Hayagrīva Boundary Protection**

"Next form the mudrā of Hayagrīva Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva for dispelling and sealing the boundary: bring both hands together in reverence. The two index fingers and two ring fingers are curled into the palms, each with backs facing. The two thumbs are slightly bent but do not touch the index fingers. Recite the mantra of Hayagrīva Vidyā-King:

*Oṃ amṛtodbhava hūṃ phaṭ svāhā*

"Recite three times, then with the mudrā, revolve leftward three times to dispel; all māras will retreat and scatter. Then revolve the mudrā rightward three times to establish a firm great boundary.

**Vajra Net**

"Next form the Vajra Net Mudrā: following the previous ground boundary mudrā, press the two thumbs against the lower first joint of the two index fingers. Recite the mantra three times, and as you recite, use the mudrā at the crown to revolve rightward and release. The net boundary mantra reads:

*Oṃ viṣphuradrakṣa vajra-pañjara hūṃ phaṭ*

"Through the empowering force of this mudrā and mantra, above is covered with an adamantine, indestructible net. Even the devas of the Paranirmitavaśavartin heaven cannot transgress or create obstacles. The practitioner's body and mind are at ease, and samādhi is easily accomplished.

**Vajra Fire Court Boundary**

"Next form the Vajra Fire Court Boundary Mudrā: cover the back of the right hand with the left palm so they touch, then extend the two thumbs upright. Visualize from the mudrā a streaming of immeasurable blazing flames. With the mudrā, revolve rightward three times. Outside the vajra wall, a ring of flames surrounds it, establishing an adamantine, pure great fire court boundary. The fire court mantra reads:

*Oṃ asamāṅgini hūṃ phaṭ*

**Arghya Offering**

"Next form the Offering Arghya Perfumed-Water Mudrā: hold the arghya vessel with both hands at the level of the forehead and offer it, reciting the mantra three times. Visualize bathing the feet of the holy assembly. The arghya mantra reads:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ gagana-samasama svāhā*

"Through this offering of arghya perfumed water, the practitioner's three actions are purified. All afflictions and karmic defilements are washed away. From the Stage of Resolute Understanding through the ten bhūmis and to the Tathāgata Stage, as one realizes each stage's pāramitā, one receives the amṛta Dharma-water of all tathāgatas in the form of abhiṣeka consecration.

**Lotus Throne**

"Next form the Lotus Throne Mudrā: following the previous Lotus Division Samaya Mudrā, slightly bend the fingers to make the form full and round. Having formed this mudrā, visualize from it a streaming of immeasurable vajra lotus flowers pervading the Land of Ultimate Bliss, with Amitāyus Tathāgata, all the great bodhisattvas, and the entire holy assembly each receiving a vajra lotus flower as their seat. The lotus throne mantra reads:

*Oṃ kamala svāhā*

"Through forming the lotus throne mudrā and reciting the mantra with empowerment, the practitioner attains the fulfillment of the ten bhūmis and will obtain the vajra seat. The three actions become as firm as vajra.

**Vast Unfailing Maṇi Offering**

"Next form the Vast Unfailing Maṇi Offering Mudrā: bring both hands together in a vajra gesture of reverence. The two index fingers are pressed together in a jewel shape. The two thumbs stand upright side by side. Then recite the Vast Unfailing Maṇi Offering Dhāraṇī:

*Oṃ amogha-pūja-maṇi-padma-vajre tathāgata-vilokite samanta-prasāra hūṃ*

"Merely reciting this Vast Unfailing Maṇi Offering Dhāraṇī three times, one immediately creates, within the assembly of Amitāyus Tathāgata and in the buddha-fields as numerous as dust motes, a rain of immeasurable vast offerings: oceans of clouds of various perfumed ointments, oceans of clouds of various flower garlands, oceans of clouds of various burned incense, oceans of clouds of various celestial exquisite food and drink, oceans of clouds of various celestial exquisite garments, oceans of clouds of various maṇi lamp and candle brilliance, oceans of clouds of various banners, pennants, jeweled curtains and canopies, oceans of clouds of various celestial exquisite music, all becoming true and vast offerings before the assemblies of all buddhas and bodhisattvas, solely through forming the mudrā and reciting this mantra for offerings. One thereby attains immeasurable accumulated merit, like space without boundary. Life after life, one will always be born within the great assembly of all tathāgatas, born from a lotus flower, attaining the five supernatural powers, able to divide the body into a hundred billion, and in defiled worlds rescue and deliver suffering sentient beings, bringing them all peace and benefit. In this present life, one receives immeasurable karmic fruits; in the future, one attains rebirth in the Pure Land.

**Samādhi Visualization**

"Next one should settle the mind, fix the intention, and focus single-pointedly. Visualize Amitāyus Tathāgata clearly and distinctly as if before one's very eyes, endowed with all marks and excellences, together with the immeasurable retinue and that buddha-field. With longing in every thought, one will in the present attain the accomplishment of samādhi. With sincere single-mindedness, vow to be reborn in that land. The mind does not deviate to other objects but continues without interruption from thought to thought. Then recite the Praise of Amitāyus Tathāgata three times:

*Namo amitābhāya, namo amitāyuṣe, namo nāma-cintya-guṇa-kara-dharma-ṇi namo amitābhāya jñānāya te munī sukhāvatī-ṃ-ya nidvā-anukampāya sukhāvatī-ṃ-ka naka-vicitra-kana-ṃ-nā manu-rathā sugatā-su-tairalaṅkṛtaṃ-tva-śraya-dha-pra-tighita-guṇa-śraya-dhimata-pra-yāmidam mahā-guṇa-ratna-sañcayam*

"The practitioner should recite this praise of the Buddha's merits three times daily. This alerts Amitāyus Tathāgata, who, not abandoning his compassionate vow, illuminates the practitioner with immeasurable radiance. All grave karmic obstacles are entirely eliminated. Body and mind are at ease, clear, tranquil, and joyful. Even sitting for long sessions of recitation, no fatigue arises. The mind becomes purified and samādhi is swiftly attained.

**Avalokiteśvara Visualization**

"Then enter the samādhi of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva. Close the eyes and settle the mind. Visualize within one's own body a pure, full, white circle, like a clear moon facing upward in the heart. Upon the pure moon, visualize the syllable *hrīḥ* radiating great light. The syllable transforms into an eight-petaled lotus. Upon the lotus is Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva with marks clearly manifest, the left hand holding a lotus, the right hand in the gesture of an opening petal.

"This bodhisattva contemplates: 'Within the bodies of all sentient beings, there exists this awakening lotus, the pure dharmadhātu, unstained by afflictions.'

"Upon each petal of the eight-petaled lotus, there is a tathāgata seated in meditation with legs crossed, facing Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva. Each has a circular halo behind the head, a body like the color of gold, with brilliant radiance. Then visualize this eight-petaled lotus gradually expanding and growing until it equals the extent of space. Then make this contemplation: 'With this awakening-flower, may I illuminate and touch the Tathāgata's ocean assembly, and may it become a vast offering.'

"If the mind does not move from this concentration, then from the depths of compassion one arouses great pity for all boundless sentient beings. Those upon whom this awakening-flower shines are liberated from suffering and afflictions and become equal to Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva.

"Then visualize the lotus gradually contracting until it equals the size of one's own body. Then form the Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva Mudrā and empower four points: the heart, forehead, throat, and crown. The mudrā: interlock the fingers of both hands outwardly, with the two index fingers touching like lotus petals, and the two thumbs standing upright side by side. Then recite the mantra of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva:

*Oṃ vajra-dharma hrīḥ*

"Through forming this mudrā and the empowerment of the mantra, one's own body becomes identical to Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, without any difference.

**Amitāyus Root Mudrā and Dhāraṇī**

"Next form the Amitāyus Tathāgata Root Mudrā: interlock the fingers of both hands outwardly into a fist, with the two middle fingers standing upright with tips touching, like the shape of a lotus petal. Having formed the mudrā, recite the Amitāyus Tathāgata Dhāraṇī seven times, then release the mudrā above the crown. The Amitāyus Tathāgata Root Dhāraṇī reads:

*Namo ratna-trayāya. Namaḥ ārya-amitābhāya tathāgatāya arhate samyaksaṃbuddhāya. Tadyathā: oṃ amṛte amṛtodbhave amṛta-sambhave amṛta-garbhe amṛta-siddhe amṛta-teje amṛta-vikrānte amṛta-vikrānta-gāminī amṛta-gagana-kīrtikare amṛta-duṇḍubhi-svare sarva-artha-sādhane sarva-karma-kleśa-kṣayaṃkari svāhā*

"Merely reciting this Amitāyus Tathāgata Dhāraṇī once destroys the ten evils, four grave offenses, and five sins of immediate retribution within the body. All karmic obstacles are entirely eliminated. If a bhikṣu or bhikṣuṇī has committed root offenses, upon reciting seven times, the purity of precepts is immediately restored. After reciting a full ten thousand times, one attains the samādhi of never forgetting the mind of enlightenment. The mind of enlightenment appears within the body, pure and luminous as a clear moon.

"At the time of death, one sees Amitāyus Tathāgata, surrounded by an immeasurable number of koṭis of bodhisattvas, coming to welcome the practitioner, bringing comfort to body and mind. One is immediately reborn in the Land of Ultimate Bliss at the highest grade of the highest birth and realizes the bodhisattva stage.

**Rosary Practice**

"Then take a lotus-seed rosary and hold it in both hands. Cup both hands around the beads in a gesture like an unopened lotus. Empower with the mantra for turning the rosary one thousand times, reciting seven times:

*Oṃ vajra-guhya-japa-samaye hūṃ*

"Having empowered them, hold the beads to the crown and make this vow: 'May all sentient beings swiftly accomplish their supreme wishes, both worldly and transcendent.' Then at the heart, gather the five fingers of each hand like an unopened lotus. The left hand holds the beads; the right hand turns the beads with the thumb and ring finger. Recite the dhāraṇī once per bead, moving one bead with each *svāhā*. The voice should be neither too slow nor too fast, neither too loud nor too soft. One should not merely call out the syllables of the mantra aloud but articulate each one clearly. The mind contemplates the Pure Land created by this samādhi, and the previously invited Amitāyus Buddha with all his marks, complete within the altar.

"Visualize the practice thus, clearly and distinctly. Focus on the recitation without interruption, far removed from distraction and agitation. In a single sitting, recite one hundred or one thousand times; if one does not complete at least one hundred and eight repetitions, the vow-count is not fulfilled.

"Through the empowerment of Amitāyus Tathāgata, body and mind become purified. With eyes open or closed, one constantly sees Amitāyus Tathāgata. Within samādhi one hears the teaching of the most profound and wondrous Dharma. In each and every syllable and phrase, one realizes immeasurable samādhi gates, immeasurable dhāraṇī gates, and immeasurable liberation gates. This body becomes equal to Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, and one can swiftly reach that land.

"When the recitation count is complete, hold the beads to the crown and make this vow: 'May all sentient beings be reborn in the Land of Ultimate Bliss, see the Buddha, hear the Dharma, and swiftly realize unsurpassed enlightenment.'

**Moon-Disk Bodhicitta Contemplation**

"Next form the concentration mudrā. Contemplate within the body the mind of enlightenment, pure, luminous, and full like the full moon. Then make this further contemplation: 'The essence of the mind of enlightenment is apart from all objects. It is without aggregates, elements, or sense-bases, and apart from grasper and grasped. Because all dharmas are without self, they are of one mark and equal. The mind is originally unborn and empty by nature. Being empty by nature...'

"Then upon the full, pure moon-disk, visualize the syllable *hrīḥ*. From the syllable streams immeasurable light. Within each ray of light, contemplate the formation of the Land of Ultimate Bliss, with the holy assembly surrounding Amitāyus Buddha.

**Conclusion of the Session**

"Having thus practiced recitation and cultivated samādhi, when wishing to leave the practice hall, form the root deity's mudrā, recite the root dhāraṇī seven times, release the mudrā above the crown, and recite the mantra of praise. After the praise, form the Universal Offering Mudrā and recite the Vast Unfailing Maṇi Offering Dhāraṇī. Again offer arghya. Whatever prayers are in the heart, address them to the holy assembly: 'May the holy ones, not departing from their original vows, fulfill my wishes.'

"Having thus completed the recitation, offerings, and vow-making, form the previous fire court mudrā and revolve leftward once to release the previously established boundary. Then form the jeweled chariot mudrā. Using the two thumbs to flick the middle finger tips outward, recite the root mantra to send off the holy assembly. Next form the three-division samaya mudrās, reciting each mantra three times. Then form the armor protection mudrā and stamp the five points of the body.

"Before the root deity, make a sincere vow and bow. Then leave the practice hall and walk about as one pleases. One should always read and recite the Amitāyus Sūtra. Do not harbor a mind of arrogant conceit. With earnest diligence, recite mantras, make buddha-seals, seal stūpas, delight in performing altar generosity, and cultivate the precepts, patience, diligence, meditation, and wisdom. Transfer all good practices, sharing them with all sentient beings for common rebirth in the Pure Land at the highest grade of the highest birth, realizing the Stage of Joy, and attaining the prediction of unsurpassed enlightenment.

**Amitāyus Tathāgata Heart Mantra**

*Oṃ lokeśvara-rāja hrīḥ*

"Reciting this mantra once is equivalent to reciting the Amitābha Sūtra once. Its merit is beyond reckoning because it is secret and supreme. It shatters grave obstacles.

*Oṃ amṛta-teje hara hūṃ*

"This method is common to all the recitation practices of Amitāyus Tathāgata of the Lotus Division. After completing one hundred thousand recitations, one will see Amitābha Tathāgata, and at the end of life one will certainly be reborn in the Land of Ultimate Bliss.
