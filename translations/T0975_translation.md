# 白傘蓋大佛頂王最勝無比大威德金剛無礙大道場陀羅尼念誦法要
## Essential Recitation Methods for the Dhāraṇī of the White Parasol Great Buddha-Uṣṇīṣa King of Supreme, Peerless, Great Awesome Virtue, Vajra Unobstructed Great Bodhimaṇḍa

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0975

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown

---

> Homage to the Buddhas and Tathāgatas
> pervading the dharma-realm and dust-mote lands.
> To the wondrously extraordinary Honored One
> of the supreme Buddha-uṣṇīṣa lineage,
> reverence to the Great Compassionate One
> and the assembly of lotus-bearing bodhisattvas,
> who purify the defilements of karmic obstruction
> and broadly benefit all sentient beings.
> I take refuge in the Lord of Secrets,
> Vajrapāṇi, King of the Treasury,
> who received adamantine strength from the Buddha
> and guards and protects all.
> The great ṛṣis of the vidyā-lords,
> the śrāvakas and pratyekabuddhas,
> the devas, nāgas, yakṣas, and others
> pay constant homage day and night.
> This great white parasol
> covers the entire great trichiliocosm.
> Its miraculous transformations are inconceivable,
> unobstructed like empty space.
> All the Buddhas praise it,
> calling it the Great Buddha-Uṣṇīṣa King.
> Through this one realizes bodhi
> and turns the unsurpassed Dharma-wheel.
> All the holy ones study and practice together;
> I too follow and cultivate accordingly.
> I briefly extract the essential secret rites;
> may all bestow their empowering protection.

First, with *hūṃ* one enters the bodhimaṇḍa, contemplates and gazes upon all the Buddhas. First, purify the three karmas and realize that the fundamental nature is without defilement. Body and vessel are entirely pure, identical in essence with all the Tathāgatas, pervading empty space. Direct the mind to pay universal reverence, confess boundless transgressions, and return equally to the emptiness of dharma-nature. Beseech all the Tathāgatas to abide permanently in the world without cessation. Rejoice in the merit of humans and devas, broadly benefiting all beings. Dedicate with single-minded concentration, together ascending to sarvajñā.

> May this life and lives to come
> always obtain the broad practice of the bodhisattva path.

The true words for purifying the vessel-realm:

"*Namaḥ sarva-buddha-bodhi-sattvānāṃ oṃ śodhana śuddhāya svāhā*"

This great purification vidyā is universally applicable to the Tathāgata division. First use it for sprinkling and cleansing, then establish the recitation chamber. By reciting seven times, body, speech, and mind, and even the entire dharma-realm, are all entirely purified.

Next, one should universally bow to all the Tathāgatas. The true words:

"*Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-pāda-vandanāṃ karomi*"

By reciting the true words three times, direct the mind to encompass the dharma-realm, pervading dust-mote lands, and the worship is completely fulfilled.

Next, the true words for confessing transgressions and obstructions from beginningless time:

"*Oṃ sarva-pāpa-dahana vajrāya svāhā*"

By reciting the true words three times, confess all faults. Transgressions as heavy as the five heinous crimes are burned and extinguished, leaving nothing remaining, like grass consumed by fire.

At that time, Śākyamuni Buddha addressed the entire assembly of bodhisattvas: "Good men! You should confer the great mudrā and true words of the samādhi of peerless power surpassing all Tathāgatas, born from all the Tathāgatas, abiding in the true-word body, the true and real great mudrā of the lineage of all Tathāgatas. With peerless awesome radiance and supernatural penetration, arising from the boundless and wondrously extraordinary great divine power, it can produce all bodhisattvas, can crush all hosts of kuṭi-māras, subdue all those difficult to tame, arouse compassionate minds, and accomplish all beneficial undertakings. Now I shall teach the great mudrā.

"Next, sit in full lotus posture facing the principal deity. Anoint the hands with scented unguent, and form the samaya mudrā of all Buddhas, bodhisattvas, and the vajra division. Interlace the fingers inward to make fists, with the two thumbs extended upright side by side. Following each phrase, recite the true words, and place the mudrā at the crown. This is the heart great mudrā of all the Tathāgatas.

"Next, bend the right index finger into the palm; this is the secret mudrā of the Lotus-Holder. Following each phrase, recite the true words and move to the right of the crown. Next, bend the left thumb into the palm, and extend the right index finger upright again. This is the secret mudrā held by all the Vajra lineage. Following the recitation of each true-word phrase, move the mudrā to the left of the crown. All share a single vidyā; the three divisions are fully complete.

"The true words:

"'*Namaḥ sarva-buddha-bodhi-sattvānāṃ a-vi-ra-hūṃ-khaṃ*'

"By forming this great mudrā equal to all the Tathāgatas, and through the empowerment of the great heroic true words and the true and real Dharma of all the Tathāgatas, one is fully able to liberate beings from hell, the animal realm, and the evil destinies of Yama, and enable all sentient beings to perform the works of the Tathāgata. It summons all assemblies of bodhisattva holy ones, and can summon all the devas, Brahma Kings, Indra, Yama, the Water Deity, Kubera, and others. Even tenth-stage bodhisattvas of great sovereign freedom can be summoned; how much more so the rest.

"Next, form the Vajra Armor protection-body mudrā.

"With both hands form hook-fists; the left is placed horizontally palm-up below the navel. The right hook strikes five places: heart, forehead, shoulders, throat, and crown. Following each, recite the true words:

"'*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ oṃ bhūr vajra-tejā hūṃ*'

"With this armor vidyā, practitioners should apply it, stamping the mudrā upon the entire body, immediately accomplishing great empowering protection. Among all the buddha-uṣṇīṣas, this mudrā has great awesome virtue. No māra can encroach upon one; donning this armor one is like a king. It can accomplish all undertakings, and swiftly one attains siddhi.

"Next, one should form the Great Wheel Mudrā, the Vajra Wheel.

"Next, form the great mudrā of Aparājita, the vidyā-rāja mudrā for dispelling māra-obstructions. One should kneel on the right knee with the left knee raised. The left hand reaches behind in a striking posture. The right fist with index finger raised is placed at the heart, the body leaning forward in a determined stance.

"The true words:

"'*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ oṃ huru huru caṇḍa-li-mātaṅgi svāhā*'

"The great vidyā of Aparājita was taught by the Buddha. It can crush all māras and dispel all obstructions. The powerful Desire-Lord, Māra the king of worldly demon armies, and even Maheśvara of great power, Nārāyaṇa, and all other classes of devas: whatever supernatural powers they possess are annihilated without remainder. Even the great vidyādhara bodhisattvas such as Hayagrīva Vidyā-rāja, upon hearing this true word recited, find their awesome radiance diminished to mere specks. Within the Great Buddha-Uṣṇīṣa division, it eliminates all calamities. Within a radius of twelve yojanas, it establishes a great protective boundary. Next, form the activity mudrā. Interlace both hands inward into fists; extend and join the ring fingers, bending the upper third joint. Open and bend the tips of the index fingers, keeping the thumbs extended evenly. Revolve leftward three times to accomplish it. The true words for dispelling:

"'*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ ṭaru hūṃ bandha bandha hūṃ phaṭ svāhā*'

"This activity true word is the heart of all the buddha-uṣṇīṣas. By merely recollecting it, all obstructing beings on water or land who wish to do harm will have their mouths sealed shut. Form the mudrā at the heart and empower the five places. Next, form the lotus mudrā, like an eight-petaled lotus opening, as the seat of all the Buddhas and Tathāgatas, and with utmost sincerity make the offering. Recite the true words seven times:

"'*Namaḥ sarva-buddha-bodhi-sattvānāṃ oṃ hūṃ kamalāya svāhā*'

"By making this offering, all the Buddhas accept their seats. In the future, one will assuredly obtain the supreme and wondrous vajra-seat. Next, form the offering of argha. With fragrant white flowers, offer with sincere mind. Make vajra-fists with both hands; extend and join the ring fingers with tips touching; bend the index fingers behind each other; the thumbs press the base of the index fingers.

"The true words:

"'*Namo bhagavate uṣṇīṣāya ehy ehi gaṇḍha puṣpa dhūpa dīpa naividya pañca praticcha hara hara sarva-buddhādhiṣṭhite dharma-rāja-pratihitāya svāhā*'

"By reciting this true word and making the offering to the Tathāgata, throughout dust-mote lands one accomplishes all manner of wondrous offerings. Next, form the Buddha-Eye mudrā. Among all the buddha-uṣṇīṣas, one should use this great mudrā, receiving and upholding it with purity; it is the supreme extinguisher of transgressions. Among all the buddha-uṣṇīṣa kings, one who constantly forms this contract will certainly attain siddhi and before long will surely accomplish it. In all the buddha-uṣṇīṣa Dharma practices, even if one accumulates merit for a hundred kalpas, the merit of obtaining this mudrā and vidyā equals that. This Buddha-Eye true word can accomplish all undertakings; it has been received and upheld by ten koṭis of Buddhas and Tathāgatas. By merely recollecting the vidyā, all holy devas appear, and all true-word teachings and Dharma practices are accomplished. Before those who are violent and wrathful, one should recite it to gain their delight. Difficult-to-tame evil spirits are subdued and become compliant. In places of battle and litigation, all is pacified. Recite seven times before and after, and swiftly one achieves verification.

"Next, form the One-Syllable King Golden Wheel Buddha-Uṣṇīṣa Mudrā.

"Interlace both hands inward into fists, extend and join the ring fingers, bending the upper third joint; open and bend the tips of the index fingers; the thumbs are extended evenly. Recite the true words seven times:

"'*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ oṃ rūṃ oṃ*'

"Buddhas as numerous as the sands of the Ganges have taught it. The present Buddhas now teach it; the future Buddhas will teach it. This mudrā is called the great mudrā, proclaimed as the Uṣṇīṣa-cakravartin King. This mudrā is itself the Buddha, benefiting all sentient beings. Through the great awesome virtue of this vidyā, it subdues all the worthy and holy ones. Within a radius of five yojanas, it prevents the descent of other honored ones. Even the bodhisattvas of the ten stages become bewildered and lose their composure; how much more so lesser beings, who cannot manifest supernatural powers. Where a wise practitioner forms this mudrā, no evil māra or obstacle dares remain. Even in a hundred koṭis of kalpas, or through a thousand times Ganges-sands of such sand-quantities, one could not fully express the merit, virtue, and awesome power. A wise practitioner who upholds it will never be undermined. The great teacher, the Tathāgata himself, has declared this, and yet cannot fully praise it. For one who cultivates the buddha-uṣṇīṣa practices, one swiftly attains accomplishment.

"Next, form the Universal All-Buddha-Uṣṇīṣa Accomplishment Mudrā.

"Interlace both hands inward into fists; extend the ring fingers upright; bend their upper joints. This is the universal mudrā for all applications. Recite the true words seven times, and release the mudrā above the crown:

"'*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ oṃ ḍharu oṃ bandha svāhā*'

"By this mudrā, it is as if one beholds and invites the Buddhas. Difficult-to-tame yakṣas, nāgas, asura hosts, all evil rākṣasas, and all the māras: through the awesome virtue of this vidyā, they are terrified and utterly dissolved. One who obtains this mudrā-contract attains all peace and happiness. Kings and worldly beings constantly receive benefit from it. Those who seek the benefit of the Dharma will assuredly obtain it, for this is the Universal True Word King, the fruit of accomplishment.

"Next, form the great mudrā of the White Parasol Buddha-Uṣṇīṣa King, whose body covers the great trichiliocosm with no edge in sight, or else form the mudrā of the shared heart of a hundred koṭis of Buddhas. Recite the great true words seven times.

"Next, form the root great mudrā of the White Parasol. Join both hands with hollow palms, bend the middle fingers into the palms, with the thumbs pressed upon the nails of the middle fingers. Bend the index fingers to form circles. This is the Buddha's Parasol Mudrā. Recite the true words seven times, then release above the crown. The true words:

"'*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ aparājita-śāsanānāṃ oṃ tathāgata-uṣṇīṣa anāvalokita-mūrdha-cakra-vartī oṃ mama hūṃ nīḥ*'

"This great Buddha-Uṣṇīṣa King is supremely peerless. It is the power of the Buddha's great compassion, flowing forth from the lion's roar. Empowered by all the Buddhas, the great compassion illumines the darkness of sorrow. Profoundly knowing the stainless, it brings about all auspiciousness. Neither bodhisattvas nor devas can undermine it. One obtains non-retrogression and all manner of peace and happiness. Poisons cannot harm one, and devas and nāgas dare not approach. Whether written or recited, one swiftly attains siddhi.

"Next, take up the rosary, empower it with seven recitations, raise it to the crown in reverence, and then perform the recitation, one hundred and eight times or one thousand and eight times. Set the rosary back in its place, form the principal deity's mudrā again, abide in samādhi, praise the Buddha's merit, and make offerings as before.

"Again form the samaya mudrā, protect the body, bow to the Buddha, and withdraw. The rosary mudrā: with both hands, use the thumbs to press the nails of the middle fingers, extend the ring fingers, and bend the index fingers behind the ring fingers; hold the rosary and recite. If one wishes to abide in the Buddha's deportment and adopt the heroic posture, sit upright in full lotus and form the Buddha's kṛti mudrā. The left palm faces up on the lap; the right hand is placed upright externally. The thumb presses the nail of the middle finger; the index finger bends behind the ring finger. Recite the true words seven times, then enter samādhi. The true words:

"'*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ vijaye mahā-śakti-uddhāri hūṃ phaṭ vijayini hūṃ phaṭ māgali hūṃ phaṭ svāhā*'

"One who forms this mudrā will not have their awesome power diminished. In this life and future lives, one attains the power of the Tathāgata. One who recites these true words receives the empowering protection of all the Buddhas. One who cultivates the Buddha-Uṣṇīṣa King and constantly recollects it at the three times will swiftly attain accomplishment and be unobstructed in the three realms." This great Buddha-Uṣṇīṣa King has immeasurable secret mudrā Dharma teachings. Now, for practitioners, only a brief collection of the essential secrets has been given. Those who delight in the great Dharma should seek consecration from a master, receive the samaya, and then broadly study and inquire into the Great White Parasol Great Buddha-Uṣṇīṣa Recitation Dharma.

This Dharma is secret; those who have not received consecration must not hear it casually. Practitioners who seek accomplishment, whether pursuing siddhi for themselves or acting on behalf of the principal deity, must not transmit or confer the secret mudrās upon others. The great samaya strict-secrecy true words:

"*Oṃ śaṅkali mahā-samayaṃ bandha bandha svāhā*"

The true words for empowering the rosary:

"*Namo bhagavate suddhyasiddhāya siddha-rathaye svāhā*"

The true words for purifying the rosary (join palms and raise the rosary):

"*Oṃ amṛta-bhūte vijaya-siddhi-sādharatī*"
