# T1333 虛空藏菩薩問七佛陀羅尼呪經

# Sūtra of Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva Asking the Seven Buddhas for Dhāraṇī Mantras

*Note: This sūtra is of unknown translator (shīyì 失譯). Set on Mount Kelāsa (Jīluóshā 雞羅莎), the narrative opens when the Buddha observes two monks in a nearby grove: one possessed by an evil spirit speaking foul words, another baring his body and raising his arms while crying out. Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva asks the Buddha to teach dhāraṇī for healing such afflictions. Through the Buddha's spiritual power, the six past buddhas (Vipaśyin, Śikhin, Viśvabhū, Krakucchanda, Kanakamuni, and Kāśyapa) appear in space, and each in turn, together with Śākyamuni, teaches a dhāraṇī with specific healing, protective, and ritual applications. The sūtra concludes with Ākāśagarbha's own protective dhāraṇī. The text reflects the genre of "seven buddhas' dhāraṇī" literature popular in Chinese Buddhism, combining Mahāyāna cosmology with practical ritual medicine.*

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was dwelling on Mount Kelāsa, at the summit of the mountain where the devas reside, not far from the assembly of worthies and sages, together with a great assembly surrounding him in front and behind. There were fully five hundred great bhikṣus. There were also five hundred great bodhisattvas, whose names were: Maitreya Bodhisattva, Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva, Samantabhadra Bodhisattva, Akṣayapuṣpa Bodhisattva, and Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva, together with five hundred bodhisattva-mahāsattvas, all at the stage of one more birth before buddhahood.

At that time the World-Honored One surveyed the four directions and, not far from where he was dwelling, observed in a grove a bhikṣu who was possessed by an evil ghost and whose mouth uttered various foul words. In the same grove there was another bhikṣu who had bared his body and was raising both hands, his mouth emitting various strange sounds, now calling out to heaven, now crying out to earth.

At that time Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, hearing these sounds, by the Buddha's spiritual power, straightened his garments, placed his right knee on the ground, joined his palms toward the Buddha, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! Why are these two bhikṣus making such sounds, raising both hands, and uttering such foul words?"

Thereupon the World-Honored One told Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva Mahāsattva: "One bhikṣu is afflicted by a serious illness and makes these sounds. The other bhikṣu is possessed by an evil ghost and runs about with his body bared."

At that time Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva Mahāsattva addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! For the sake of curing all illnesses of sentient beings and removing all obstructions caused by evil ghosts so that they do not arise, may the World-Honored One, for the sake of all sentient beings, teach the dhāraṇī mantras."

## The Appearance of the Seven Buddhas

At that time the World-Honored One manifested his spiritual power, and in the sky there appeared six buddhas. Then Śākyamuni Buddha himself rose into the air and sat in sequence with them. At this time the great assembly all beheld the seven buddha world-honored ones.

## Vipaśyin Buddha's Dhāraṇī

At that time Vipaśyin Buddha, for the sake of all sentient beings and to remove all obstructions caused by evil ghosts, spoke the great-majestic-virtue dhāraṇī mantra:

[Dhāraṇī]

Vipaśyin Buddha then told Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva Mahāsattva: "If there are good men or good women who receive and uphold this mantra, whether reading or reciting it, or if there are people who seek out this mantra, or who delight in hearing it, such persons will not be harmed by any weapons, will not drown in any waters, will not be afflicted by any suffering, and will not suffer any untimely death.

"Furthermore, if anyone gives poison to a person who upholds this mantra, the poison will transform into delicious food and will not harm the person. If anyone, whether eating or drinking, first recites the mantra seven times and then eats, after eating, all sorcery, curses, evil speech, and demonic spirits will all be eliminated and unable to cause harm. One who upholds this mantra will have a long life. Whatever one studies, one will understand upon a single hearing and never forget. Day and night one will constantly see buddha-images.

"Furthermore, if a person is struck by an evil ghost and cannot be cured, recite the mantra seven times at that person's ear and the affliction will immediately be healed. If a person suffers from ringworm, vitiligo, or madness, take sumanas flowers, water-fragrance lotus, fennel, and asarum in equal parts, fry them in sesame oil, and apply to the affected area. The condition will be cured. For all other illnesses, reciting this mantra over them will bring a cure without fail."

## Śikhin Buddha's Dhāraṇī

At that time Śikhin Buddha, in the sky, wishing to remove all illnesses and all obstructions caused by evil ghosts for the sake of all sentient beings, spoke the mantra:

[Dhāraṇī]

Śikhin Buddha then told Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva Mahāsattva: "This divine mantra has been spoken by hundreds of thousands of nayutas of countless billions of buddhas. I now speak it for the sake of all sentient beings, to remove all illnesses, all evil ghost obstructions, all nightmares, and all untimely deaths from illness."

He further told Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva Mahāsattva: "If good men or good women recite this mantra three times during the day and three times at night, or even simply recollect it in their minds, all sorcery, hexes, curses, evil speech, and demonic spirits will be unable to approach them. During sleep at night they will constantly see buddhas. All food that they eat will naturally be digested, nourishing their strength. When such persons pass away, the buddhas they saw during their dreams will all come to welcome their spirit, and will constantly accompany them.

"Furthermore, if a person wishes to use this mantra's power for specific purposes, one should make a knotted mantra-cord for all illnesses. If a person is critically ill, take sumanas wood, fry it in oil, and rub it on the person's body. The illness will be cured. If sentient beings are terrified day and night, take clean water, recite the mantra over it, and sprinkle it in the four directions. Make a mantra-cord and tie it to the person's body, and all demonic spirits will be unable to trouble them. If a young child has been struck by an evil ghost, use a blue thread to make a mantra-cord and tie it below the child's neck. The child will be cured. Furthermore, if a person has been struck by an evil ghost, recite the mantra seven times at the person's ear, and the person will also be cured. Whatever one's heart desires to accomplish, there is nothing that will not be achieved."

## Viśvabhū Buddha's Dhāraṇī

At that time Viśvabhū Buddha, in the sky, wishing to remove all illnesses and all evil ghost obstructions for the sake of all sentient beings, spoke the mantra:

[Dhāraṇī]

Viśvabhū Buddha, having spoken this mantra, told Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva Mahāsattva: "This divine mantra has been spoken by the past buddhas of the Bhadrakalpa, is now spoken by the present buddhas, and will be spoken by the future buddhas." He further told Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva: "You should receive and uphold this mantra. If in the future there are sentient beings who uphold this mantra, whether reading or reciting it, or if there are people who seek it out, they will have no fear from weapons. All sorcery, all fevers, all drowning, and all untimely deaths and serious illnesses will all be removed, except for retribution from past karma, which cannot be removed.

"If there are bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, or upāsikās who rise at dawn, bathe and wash their faces, and recite this mantra one hundred and eight times before a buddha-image, all evils will not affect their bodies, all illnesses will naturally be cured, their lives will be lengthened, all wealth will naturally be abundant, all bodily obstructions and unwholesome matters will be eliminated, and all enemies will naturally be reconciled. All hexes and curses will not affect their bodies. Such are the blessings from bathing and reciting the mantra.

"If a person is in a dangerous and frightening place and can recollect this mantra, one will escape from all terrors. If a person is involved in disputes or legal accusations by the authorities, make a mantra-cord with white thread, reciting the mantra one hundred and eight times, and tie it to the right arm.

"If a person has eye pain, make a mantra-cord with tangerine peel and tie it above the ear.

"If enemies from another region wish to invade, take ghee, curds, honey, and sesame in equal parts, mix them together, and before a buddha-image use a copper spoon to scoop one spoonful, recite the mantra once, and cast it into the fire. Do this up to one thousand and eight times. All enemies, whether far or near, will naturally retreat and scatter.

"Furthermore, if a person wishes to obtain a long life, take curds, honey, and roasted rice-flowers in three equal parts and mix them. Before a buddha-image, use a copper spoon to scoop one spoonful, recite the mantra once, and cast it into the fire, up to one thousand and eight times, and then stop.

"Furthermore, if a person wishes, for oneself or for others, to remove all terrors, to eliminate all sins, to seek wealth and treasure, or to purify bodily obstructions: if one wishes to recite and uphold this mantra, all terrors and obstructions will be eliminated, all undertakings will be accomplished, and one will also achieve success in all other mantra-practices, and no enemy will be able to gain advantage.

"If a person wishes to perform this practice, one must compound a medicine to anoint the body. The required ingredients are: fennel, rice, nārada-śirīṣa, agarwood incense, śirīya, saffron, cyperus root, indravāru, and bodhi-tree wood. Take these medicines in equal parts and mix them. Rise at dawn, wash and bathe, and anoint the body with the medicine.

"Furthermore, if a person wishes to meet a king or great minister, before a buddha-image, light a fire with tangerine wood, take five-finger pinches of sesame, recite the mantra once per pinch and cast it into the fire, one thousand and eight times, and then stop.

"If good men or good women wish all enemies to be naturally reconciled, they should wash and bathe cleanly, put on clean garments, and at the middle of the night, before a buddha-image, make offerings of incense and flowers. Take roasted rice-flowers and tangerine wood, light a fire, take three-finger pinches of the rice-flowers, recite the mantra once per pinch, and cast into the fire, one thousand and eight times, and then stop.

"If good men or good women have nightmares, or enemies are seeking an advantage, or all one's undertakings fail, on the fourteenth day, eat nothing all morning. In the evening, bathe the body with fragrant water and put on fresh clean garments. Before a buddha-image, recite the mantra according to one's capacity, without limit, with all one's strength, and do not leave the ritual space. Sleep before the image. On the morning of the fifteenth day, rise, wash a clean pot, fill it with clean water, and make a decoction. In the decoction, take various well-known herbs and plants according to what is available and place them in the water. Strain out the herbs, then add clove, purple sandalwood, and white sandalwood ground to powder, and add them to the decoction as available. Use copper vessels that hold five pecks each, two of them. Fill the vessels with the fragrant water and place them before the image. Recite the mantra one thousand and eight times. Then use this decoction to bathe one's body, and afterward eat.

"Furthermore, if a person has been poisoned, take purple ginger wood, cut it into one-inch segments the thickness of chopsticks, one thousand and eight pieces. Smear each with cow dung. Before the image, light a fire, take one piece per mantra recitation and cast it into the fire, one thousand and eight times, then stop. Call out the patient's name: 'I do this for that person to remove the poison.' The person will be cured. The efficacious powers of this mantra are inexhaustible. Whatever one's heart desires, if one follows this mantra-method, there is nothing that will not be accomplished."

## Krakucchanda Buddha's Dhāraṇī

At that time Krakucchanda Buddha, in the sky, wishing to remove all illnesses and all evil ghost obstructions for the sake of all sentient beings, spoke the mantra:

[Dhāraṇī]

Krakucchanda Buddha, having spoken this mantra, told Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva Mahāsattva: "This mantra has been spoken by as many buddhas of the same name, Krakucchanda, as there are grains of sand in the Ganges. I now speak it and also praise the inconceivable, immeasurable merit of this mantra. You should now receive and uphold it with utmost sincerity.

"If in the future there are people who uphold this mantra and have sincere faith in the Three Treasures, bathe with fragrant water, put on fresh clean garments, make offerings of incense and flowers before a buddha-image, kneel before the image with palms joined, and recite the mantra one hundred and eight times, they will know their past lives through seven hundred lifetimes. If they are reborn in heaven, they will be kings of the devas. If reborn among humans, they will be wheel-turning sage kings. If one upholds it daily, upon death one will be reborn in the land of Amitāyus and not return to the human realm.

"If a person recites this mantra day and night, all untimely illnesses will not affect that person's body. When about to eat, first recite the mantra seven times and then eat. All illnesses in the body will naturally be cured.

"If a person has been struck by evil ghosts or is possessed by evil ghosts, prepare good fragrant water, recite the mantra over it one thousand and eight times, then use this water to bathe the patient, and also recite the mantra directly over the patient one thousand and eight times. The illness will be cured.

"Furthermore, if a person's body has developed malignant sores, recite the mantra over the patient using a steel knife, and also recite the mantra over a copper needle, then needle the sore. The sore will be cured.

"Furthermore, if a person is bound in prison, recite the mantra over one's hands seven times and rub one's own face. All legal matters will naturally be resolved.

"If a person constantly upholds this mantra, all wealth and possessions will naturally be abundant with no lack, and all obstructions will be eliminated.

"If a person wishes to see a buddha, clean a room, anoint the floor with fragrant mud, set up a buddha-image, hang silk banners and canopies, make offerings of incense and flowers, bathe with fragrant water, put on clean garments, burn agarwood incense, and before the image, kneel facing east while the image faces west. Recite the mantra one thousand and eight times. Then lie down before the image with head to the east. In a dream one will see a buddha, and whatever one recollects in the heart, one will then see: whether concerning the length of one's life, whether one will have illness or not, or the outcome of disputes with enemies, victory or defeat. Whatever the heart recollects, one will know at that time. After experiencing this dream, all bodily obstructions and illnesses will be completely eliminated without remainder."

## Kanakamuni Buddha's Dhāraṇī

At that time Kanakamuni Buddha, in the sky, wishing to remove all illnesses and all evil ghost obstructions for the sake of all sentient beings, spoke the mantra:

[Dhāraṇī]

Kanakamuni Buddha told Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva Mahāsattva: "If good men or good women receive, uphold, copy, and circulate this mantra, they will be free from all enemies who will be unable to gain advantage over them. Fire will not burn them, water will not drown them, and lightning and thunderbolts will not come near them. If anyone gives poison to a person who upholds this mantra, that person will eat it as if consuming a fine meal, and it will be naturally digested with no poisonous effect. One will not suffer any untimely death. All food that one eats will be naturally digested, bringing radiance, strength, and long life. One will always be wealthy and possess abundant treasures, and all buddhas will recollect and protect one.

"If a person has committed grave karmic obstructions in the past, by reciting this mantra three times daily, they will be eliminated.

"Furthermore, if a person wishes to cure one's own illness or another's illness, clean a room, anoint the floor with fragrant mud, hang silk banners and canopies, set up a high seat with a buddha-image. The person should bathe with fragrant water, burn agarwood, frankincense, and other incense, and make various vegetarian food offerings before the buddha as one is able. Before the image, call out the patient's name and recite the mantra. The illness will be cured. All possessing ghosts, spirits, and demons will be eliminated by reciting this mantra over fragrant water and bathing the body.

"Furthermore, if a person's body has developed ringworm, vitiligo, or leprosy, take calamus root, pound it to powder, one pint, mix with white honey, and before the image recite the mantra one thousand and eight times. Each morning before eating, take a square-inch spoonful. The condition will be cured, as well as all other chronic ailments.

"If a person suffers from alternating chills and fever, whether recurring every four days, every three days, every two days, or daily, take mixed flowers to make a crown, recite the mantra one thousand and eight times before the image, and place the crown on the patient's head. The illness will be cured.

"Furthermore, if a person has been struck by an evil ghost and the mouth is locked shut, unable to speak, recite the mantra softly at the patient's ear one hundred and eight times. The illness will be cured.

"Furthermore, if a person has been struck by a ghost of madness, or a pūtana ghost, a kaṭapūtana ghost, a piśāca ghost, a kumbhāṇḍa ghost, or any other evil ghost, take flowers that have been offered on the buddha's body, recite the mantra over them one thousand and eight times, and burn them. Hold the smoke to the patient's nose. When the smoke enters the nose, the person will be cured.

"Whatever the heart desires will all be accomplished. The efficacious powers of this mantra are inexhaustible."

## Kāśyapa Buddha's Dhāraṇī

At that time Kāśyapa Buddha, in the sky, wishing to remove all evil illnesses and all evil ghost obstructions for the sake of sentient beings, spoke the mantra:

[Dhāraṇī]

Kāśyapa Buddha told Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva Mahāsattva: "This mantra has been spoken by as many buddhas as there are grains of sand in the Ganges. I have now spoken it. You should receive, uphold, read, and recite it, recollecting it day and night without letting it be forgotten. In the future, if good men or good women uphold this mantra, reciting it three times daily according to their capacity, in their dreams at night they will see buddhas. Upon having such a dream, all grave sins will naturally be eliminated. Even if a person has committed the five heinous crimes or does not believe in the Mahāyāna, such grave sins cannot be rescued by other means; but all undertakings, after reciting this mantra, will be accomplished.

"For all ringworm, vitiligo, leprosy, and other skin diseases, take flowers from the buddha's body, recite the mantra one thousand and eight times before the image, pound them to powder, mix with cold water, and apply to the affected area. The condition will be cured. Mixing the powder with cold water and drinking it is also effective.

"Furthermore, if a person has a headache, take flowers from the buddha's body, recite the mantra one thousand and eight times before the image, pound to powder, mix with cold water, divide into three portions, and apply to the head. When it dries, apply again. After three applications, the condition will be cured.

"If a woman has difficulty in childbirth, take flowers from the buddha's body, pound to powder, mix with cow's ghee, fry it, and place it before the image. Recite the mantra one thousand and eight times and apply to the birth canal. The child will be born easily and the mother will recover quickly.

"If it rains excessively and does not clear, take flowers from the buddha's body, recite the mantra one thousand and eight times before the image, go to an open area and burn them. The rain will stop.

"Furthermore, if a person has been struck by an evil ghost and the mouth is locked shut, unable to speak, or if struck by a piśāca ghost, or has chronic illness or damp-disease, take flowers from the buddha's body, pound to powder, and mix with wine, whether grape wine or rice wine. Place it in a silver bowl before the image, recite the mantra one thousand and eight times, and give it to the patient to drink. The condition will be cured.

"For all undertakings, recite the mantra one thousand and eight times before the image, and all will be accomplished. When wishing to perform any task, recite this mantra and do not give rise to doubt."

## Śākyamuni Buddha's Dhāraṇī

At that time Śākyamuni Buddha, in the sky, wishing to remove all illnesses and all evil ghost obstructions for the sake of sentient beings, spoke the mantra:

[Dhāraṇī]

Śākyamuni Buddha told Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva Mahāsattva: "If good men or good women receive, uphold, read, and recite this mantra, recollecting it day and night, such persons will not be harmed by any weapons, will not be afflicted by any evil illnesses, and will not suffer any untimely death. The efficacious powers are the same as those of the six buddhas' mantras described above, with no difference."

## Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva's Dhāraṇī

At that time Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva Mahāsattva addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I now wish to speak a mantra to safeguard and protect the divine mantras spoken by the seven buddhas. Therefore I shall speak it again. This mantra can destroy all karmic obstructions." He then spoke the mantra:

*Namo buddhāya. Namo dharmāya. Namo saṃghāya. Ākāśagarbhāya bodhisattvāya mahāsattvāya mahākāruṇikāya. Tadyathā: cili cili mahā-cili. Dhulu dhulu mahā-dhulu. Jvāli jvāli mahā-jvāli. Hili hili mahā-hili svāhā.*

"The efficacious powers of this mantra are as follows. If a person has been struck by an evil ghost, one must make a mantra-cord, reciting the mantra one thousand and eight times. Take sumanas wood, ghee, and mustard seeds in three equal parts and mix them. Before the patient, take three-finger pinches, reciting the mantra once per pinch, and cast into the fire, one thousand and eight times. The illness will be cured.

"Furthermore, if a person is frightened and wishes to set a boundary, take a knife, recite the mantra one thousand and eight times, and place it at the doorway. If a person wishes to set a boundary in open space for fear of evil ghosts seeking to do harm, take water, recite the mantra seven times, and sprinkle it in the four directions. For fear within one's own body, make a mantra-cord for personal protection.

"If a person has been poisoned, take ancient grass-thread and use it while reciting the mantra over the patient. The person will be cured.

"If a person has a wind-illness, recite the mantra over fragrant oil one thousand and eight times and apply it to the affected area. The condition will be cured.

"If a person has ear pain, take blue-wood incense fried in oil, and pour it into the ear. The condition will be cured.

"If a person has abdominal pain, take black salt, recite the mantra one hundred and eight times, and give it to the patient to take. The condition will be cured.

"If a person has a toothache, take sumanas water, recite the mantra one hundred and eight times, crush it, and use it to brush the teeth. The pain will stop.

"If a young child cries in fright day and night, take white thread and make a mantra-cord, reciting the mantra one hundred and eight times, and tie it below the child's neck. The fright will be removed.

"If a person has a headache, take a steel knife, or use one's hand, recite the mantra one hundred and eight times, and rub the top of the head. The condition will be cured.

"If a person has been hexed or cursed by another, take a steel knife and for seven days and seven nights recite the mantra over the patient. The condition will be cured.

"If thunder and lightning arise, break off a pomegranate branch with the hand without removing its thorns or leaves, hold it toward the direction of the thunder, and recite the mantra one hundred and eight times. The thunder will cease.

"For all undertakings one wishes to accomplish, recite this mantra. First make offerings of incense and flowers, then recite the mantra. There is nothing that will not be accomplished."

When this sūtra was spoken, Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, the devas, nāgas, and the eight classes of beings all rejoiced, praised the teaching, and with one mind faithfully practiced it.

This completes the Sūtra of Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva Asking the Seven Buddhas for Dhāraṇī Mantras.
