# 佛說六字呪王經
## Six-Syllable Spell-King Sūtra Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1044

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown.

---

Thus have I heard. At one time the Buddha was in the country of Śrāvastī, in the Jetavana grove. At that time there was a caṇḍālī woman of the non-Buddhist traditions who bewitched the venerable Ānanda with a love spell. The Tathāgata, seeing Ānanda in a daze, spoke the Six-Syllable Spell-King Sūtra for him: "As the former buddhas proclaimed this, so now do I proclaim it." He then spoke the spell:

*Andarī pandarī kārajñi khayurī tejāvatī bindhuvatī dādhurī dātirī dākumāti śumāti antarā pandarā daṇḍarā tidhūrā anāyandā mandavatī anā anā yāmadhūmavatī kāraṭa khayurā phuḍaṅgamiti tejāvatī bindhumati āravagavatī viṭavitinikhiṭavitini svāhā*

"If anyone has cast evil spells or curses against so-and-so, whether already done or about to be done, whether by devas, nāgas, yakṣas, rākṣasas, hungry ghosts, kumbhāṇḍas, pūtanas, piśācas, apasmāras, kṛtyās, vetālas, vidyādharas, half-vidyādharas, female half-vidyādharas, śramaṇas, brāhmaṇas, kṣatriyas, vaiśyas, śūdras, mātaṅgas, female mātaṅgas, caṇḍālas, female caṇḍālas, male servants, female servants, men, women, male or female non-Buddhist renunciants: all such kinds of spells, curses, and sorcery, I now, by this Six-Syllable Spell-King Sūtra, destroy them. If cast upward, upward they are destroyed; if cast downward, downward they are destroyed; if cast upon the ground, upon the ground they are destroyed; if cast upon a wall, upon the wall they are destroyed; if cast beside a pillar, beside the pillar they are destroyed; if cast within a city, within the city they are destroyed; if cast at a city gate, at the city gate they are destroyed; if cast within a palace, within the palace they are destroyed; if cast at a palace gate, at the palace gate they are destroyed; if cast upon a road, upon the road they are destroyed; if cast at a crossroads, at the crossroads they are destroyed; if cast by brāhmaṇas, all are destroyed; if cast in water, in the water they are destroyed; if cast beside water, beside the water they are destroyed; if cast by a river, by the river they are destroyed; if cast within a house, within the house they are destroyed; if cast beside a tree, beside the tree they are destroyed; if cast beneath a tree's roots, beneath the roots they are destroyed; if cast in fire, in the fire they are destroyed; if cast in a furnace, in the furnace they are destroyed; if cast beside a well or stove, beside the well or stove they are destroyed; if cast beside a mortar or millstone, beside the mortar or millstone they are destroyed; if cast beside a tomb, beside the tomb they are destroyed; if cast amid bedding, amid the bedding they are destroyed; if cast upon garments, ornaments, or clothing, all are destroyed; if cast at night, at night they are destroyed; if cast by day, by day they are destroyed; if cast perpetually by day and night, all are destroyed. This spell can sever the spell-craft of Śakra, can sever the spell-craft of Brahmā, can sever the spell-craft of the four heavenly kings, and can sever all sorcery and black arts of every manner of evil spirit. If anyone has cast curses, sorcery, or poisons against so-and-so, all are destroyed. Whatever has been done by vetālas, vidyādharas, kṛtyās, or pūtanas is entirely destroyed. All evils are exhausted, and all virtues fill their place. May this spell be ever auspicious. By these true and genuine words, may so-and-so have peace by day, peace by night, and constant peace day and night, attaining a lifespan of one hundred years and seeing one hundred autumns. Why? Because in this world there is none higher than the Buddha, revered by humans and devas and honored by all spirits. If anyone practices evil spell-craft, whether already done, about to be done, or done in between, whether accomplished or not yet accomplished, all is destroyed. And whatever poisons can harm a person, this Six-Syllable Spell-King Sūtra destroys them all." He then spoke the spell:

*Khajñi khaju khavijñi gaṃśu gaṃśu dajñi vājñi*

"If anyone has cast evil spells or curses against so-and-so, whether already done or about to be done, whether by devas, nāgas, yakṣas, rākṣasas, or any being capable of casting evil spells and curses, all are destroyed. Whatever has been done by vetālas, vidyādharas, or pūtanas is entirely destroyed. All evils are exhausted, and all virtues fill their place. May this spell be ever auspicious. By these true and genuine words, may so-and-so have peace by day, peace by night, and constant peace day and night, attaining a lifespan of one hundred years and seeing one hundred autumns. Why? Because in this world no dharma surpasses the Buddha-dharma, revered by humans and devas, honored by all spirits, free from desire, without attachment." He then spoke the spell:

*Khajñi khaju khavijñi gaṃśu gaṃśu dajñi vājñi*

"Why? Because the Saṅgha is the supreme field of merit in this world, revered by humans and devas, guarded by all spirits." He then spoke the spell:

*Khajñi khaju khavijñi gaṃśu gaṃśu dajñi vājñi hiṃtī aniti ājhūtī*

"If anyone has cast evil spells or curses against so-and-so, or has compounded poisons, all are destroyed." He then spoke the spell:

*Maśuva umati umadaśidanī khauda patiśidanī bindhumati jiti jitāpatiśidanī avacandavahaha cāri haha nami haha bhumati haha bhūdanī guśahaha nī hadā khauda nī viśa khauda inī minī dādā bhī svāhā*

"If any person reads and upholds this Six-Syllable Great Spell-King Sūtra, even if they were to cast this spell upon a withered tree, it could be made to sprout branches and leaves again. How much more so for a human body! May so-and-so attain a lifespan of one hundred years and see one hundred autumns. What the buddhas have proclaimed, Ānanda has transmitted. If a person reads and recites this fluently, they will be fully self-protected, and all evils will not touch their body. If they cast this spell for another, it can remove that person's afflictions. The reciter should abstain from the five pungent foods, be sincere and pure, and only then practice it. Homage to Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva Mahāsattva. Having paid respects to that great being, one then speaks this divine spell. May this divine spell be ever auspicious. May Avalokiteśvara illuminate my body, watch over my body, and have compassion for me." He then spoke the spell:

*Uhanī suhanī adanī hadajāvinī daṃvinī andarī pandarī svapiti pandarā pāsinī sāludhūsūṭṭha vāradhūsūṭṭha jitaṃjāvaya daṃhaya suhaya nivārayā yāvamūrā tinamūcati lūlūkhiṭṭha pandasī lūlūlūlūkhiṭṭha pandasī svāhā vahīri jhiri śūri māṭaṅgī caṇḍarī rākṣa rākṣa rāyā svāhā*

"This spell protects against all calamities. It is pure, immaculate, and liberating. With its bright illumination, it binds all thieves, confounds all evils, protects the body, and binds thieves. Tigers, wolves, lions, mad elephants, and jackals are all bound. May I, so-and-so, together with my traveling companions, in all places where this spell's power extends, find peace and auspiciousness. Homage to Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva. May this spell bring great auspiciousness. When practicing this spell, on the day of upholding it one should not eat the five pungent foods, should bathe in pure water, must not engage in sexual conduct, and must not drink alcohol or eat meat. Take a white cotton thread in hand and recite the spell seven times, tying one knot with each recitation. If one faces a legal case or accusation, a quarrel, a curse, or any evil, all can be destroyed. After the recitation, tie this thread to the person's garment-sash. When the matter is resolved, the person should untie it themselves."

"Homage to the buddhas of the past, future, and present, the three ages. Homage to Mañjuśrī, Samantabhadra, Maitreya, and all the great bodhisattvas. Having taken refuge in these bodhisattva mahāsattvas, one then speaks this great divine dhāraṇī spell." He then spoke the spell:

*Hīdhumati yaśavatī vārasādhanavatī uśuvatī uttaranī akṣayavatī anuśānti mūjāvarā mūktī vāravākīdhū svapati kāranī gati paviyaśa śūravāri dāti nuti sandāvati uttaranī śādamukha śādavārāviśānda kārī śāvasatnā vagati guśarāvasanī anu santi visanti jñiti ajñiti dhaṃnāgati adhaṃnāgati avasati haśavārānī vārānī mukhī mūrā mūrā vādanī śanī pāralusūrivatī śuti pāralusūvimārī ammārī*

"This spell removes all darkness. It is upheld by the tathāgatas, cherished by the devas, protected by all spirits, understood by Brahmā, honored by Śakra, guarded by the world-protectors, elucidated by the sages, and upheld by the ṛṣis. It harmonizes all dispositions and liberates from all bonds. All devas and humans sever afflictions and vanquish the army of Māra, subdue non-Buddhist practitioners, overcome all disputants and the arrogant, surpass all dharma teachers, never abandon the great assembly, delight those who study the dharma, protect the dharma treasury, benefit the Three Jewels, and compassionately care for all classes of sentient beings, adorning all meaning. Such are its benefits. It is called the Inexhaustible Treasury Dhāraṇī, protecting what cannot otherwise be protected. If anyone reads and recites it, they gain the thirty-three fearlessnesses. When practicing this spell, at dawn wash the hands, rinse the mouth, burn incense, bow, and read it three times. In all places one goes, in all speech and conversation, in all directions one faces, one will always be undefeated. All people will honor and respect one, those who see one will rejoice, and enemies will be subdued. Such are its benefits."
