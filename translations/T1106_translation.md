# 讚揚聖德多羅菩薩一百八名經
## Sūtra of Praise Extolling the One Hundred and Eight Names of the Holy and Virtuous Tārā Bodhisattva

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1106

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Devaśānti (天息災).

---

> With single-minded devotion I bow in homage,
> To the joyful and most auspicious one.
> On Mount Potalaka,
> That realm is adorned with all manner
> Of precious jewels.
> All manner of jeweled forests and trees,
> Their branches and vines densely drooping.
> There are all manner of accomplished
> Kusuma wondrous flowers,
> Their blossom-light shining universally.
> There are all manner of ponds and springs,
> Their streams producing all manner of sounds,
> And also all manner of colors.
> Fragrant elephants and deer kings,
> The wondrous songs of bee kings,
> The beautiful melodies of kiṃnarī maidens,
> The music of gandharvas.
> Holy devas and human folk,
> Munis and assemblies free from desire
> Are always gathered there,
> Together with other bodhisattva assemblies
> And those sovereign over the ten bhūmis.
> Holy Tārā Bodhisattva,
> Together with a thousand vidyā-consorts,
> Wrathful great vidyā-kings,
> Hayagrīva, and others surround her.
>
> At that time the Holy and Virtuous
> Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva,
> For the benefit of all sentient beings,
> Having completed the practice of the great conduct,
> He who abides in loving-kindness, compassion, joy, and equanimity,
> Dwelling in the womb of that treasury,
> Upon the auspicious lotus seat,
> Sits serene and composed,
> And for the great assembly of devas and humans
> Expounds the wondrous dharma.
>
> At that time Vajrapāṇi,
> The wrathful king of great power,
> Out of compassion for others,
> Questioned Avalokiteśvara:
> "Lions, elephants, tigers, serpents,
> Water, fire, bandits, fetters:
> From these eight difficulties,
> How can indolent and inferior beings
> Gain liberation?
> Long dwelling in the ocean of saṃsāra,
> Bound by the fetters of desire, anger, and delusion,
> Caught in the bonds of cyclic existence,
> If they are to be freed,
> I declare that one who can do so
> Is revered by the world."
>
> Then Avalokiteśvara
> Spoke in a beautiful and wondrous voice,
> Alerting Vajrapāṇi:
> "Lord of Secrets, listen well.
> From the vow of Amitāyus
> I was born, the mother of the world.
> Holding an utpala flower in hand,
> Radiating light to illumine this realm,
> Both the human world and the heavens,
> Startling this world.
> Yakṣas, rākṣasas, and others:
> Be told, do not be frightened.
> I am a transformation from the Buddha,
> To protect the world.
> All manner of perilous terrors,
> Blade-wars and famines,
> The various terrors of cyclic existence:
> I rescue sentient beings from them.
> The world calls me Tārā.
> I am called the Buddha's child.
> Excellent! She should be revered."
>
> Having spoken, she joined her palms,
> Then leapt into the sky and hovered.
> Her body blazed with radiant flames.
> She then declared these words:
> "This one hundred and eight names
> Were spoken by the buddhas of the past.
> Those sovereign over the ten bhūmis
> And innumerable bodhisattvas,
> Upon hearing them, gave rise to joy.
> If any person receives and upholds them,
> All offenses are extinguished.
> Merit increases, fame spreads wide,
> Wealth abounds, auspiciousness prevails.
> All diseases are dispersed,
> One dwells in peace upon the field of merit,
> Enjoying long life and happiness,
> Generating loving-kindness to ferry sentient beings.
> That one is called a great muni.
> Thus did the Virtuous One declare."
>
> Avalokiteśvara smiled,
> And gazed upon the ten directions.
> Transforming with a heart of compassion,
> He extended his right hand,
> His arm adorned with the marks of merit,
> And told the great wise one:
> "Excellent, Vajrapāṇi!
> Now hear these of great merit.
> These are the wondrously quiescent names.
> Speak them honestly for the people.
> Those who hear them gain peace and happiness,
> Abundant wealth and sovereign mastery.
> All diseases are liberated,
> All merits are complete.
> Untimely death is put to rest.
> At the end of life, one is born in Sukhāvatī.
> All devas and others should know:
> Listen well as I now proclaim.
> You all should deeply rejoice.
> The one hundred and eight names are:

*Oṃ śrī-kāryāṇi mahā-teja-loka-dhātrī mahā-yaśa-sāra-sarasvatī visāla-kṣipra-jñā. Śrī-buddhi-vṛddha-nirdhi-ṛtī-na puṣṭi-na svāhā. Oṃ kara-kamalā-rūpa-maṇi sarva-sattva-hitā-hunī yukta-saṃgraha-mudrā-ṇī jaya-prajñā pāramitā-devī-ye. Āryā-tārā manura-mānaṃ-nubhi-śaṅkhinī pūrṇa-vinīya-rāṅgī parivāṃ-vana-candrā-nāna. Mahā-gaurī ajita-bhitā-vasa-smahā-māyā mahā-śvetā mahā-bala-parākramā mahā-rudrā mahā-caṇḍa-duṣṭa-sattva-nisūdanī. Prasānta-śānta-rūpa-cavijayā jvālana-prabhā-vinī yura-mālī-dhvaja śaṅkha-ghaṇṭī-yadākṛta-nūra-dhara-śambhanī sādhambhanī. Kālikala-rātrī niśā-cārī rakṣā-himohānī śānti-kānta-rīndrā viṇī-śubhra-brahma-māṇī veda-mata-caughi-laghi-hāvā-sinī māṅgalya-śaṅkarī-saumyā jāta-veda-manu-java-kāpālinī mahā-devī-sandhyā. Sarva-trā jānuja sarva-rātha-sādhanī badra-ughī sitrī-dhātrī dhānanda āśaya-gaurā taṃ-bhī puṇyā śrī-dhā-lokeśvara-mā-jūtī.*

> "Having spoken these one hundred and eight
> Quiescent and secret names
> That can benefit all sentient beings,
> These are the rare and secret field of merit.
> In the heavens and the human world,
> They are most difficult to encounter.
> They cause all sentient beings
> To gain peace and happiness by gazing upon them.
> The wise should single-mindedly
> Bathe and purify their bodies,
> And at the three times concentrate in recitation.
> Before long, that person
> Will gain the king's love and respect,
> Be freed from suffering and gain happiness.
> The poor obtain wealth,
> The foolish gain great wisdom,
> The clever no longer doubt.
> Those in fetters gain liberation.
> The defeated regain victory.
> Enemies become good friends.
> Sharp fangs and keen horns,
> Disputes and dangers,
> All manner of terrors:
> Those who recollect these names
> Are rescued from all such fears.
> Untimely death is put to rest.
> One's fame spreads far and wide.
> One is always born in a good and noble clan.
> Whether alone,
> Or walking, sitting, or lying down,
> One will gain the praise of all.
> Lifespan is extended.
> In the human world one enjoys happiness.
> Devas, nāgas, yakṣas,
> Rākṣasas, gandharvas,
> Foul spirits and filth-eating spirits,
> Evil-hearted mātṛkās,
> Female spirits, ḍākinīs,
> Aupātrikas and others,
> Epilepsy demons and shoulder-riders,
> Vetāla great ghosts,
> Ḍākinīs and other spirits,
> And all others of evil mind:
> They dare not cross one's shadow,
> How much less a place of battle.
> Violent sentient beings,
> Binding spells and illusory arts:
> None can cause harm.
> Sovereign and majestic,
> Children, grandchildren, and wealth
> Increase without limit.
> One obtains the spiritual power of past-life knowledge.
> The noble clans all rejoice upon seeing one.
> One possesses the unobstructed great eloquence
> And thoroughly comprehends all treatises.
> One encounters good teachers,
> Adorns the mind of bodhi,
> And in every life, in every birth,
> Always draws near to the buddhas."
