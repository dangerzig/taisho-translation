# 佛說阿彌陀經
## Amitābha Sūtra Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0366

Translated from the Chinese. Yao Qin dynasty, translated by Kumārajīva (鳩摩羅什)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the country of Śrāvastī, in the Jetavana grove of Anāthapiṇḍada's park, together with a great assembly of one thousand two hundred and fifty bhikṣus, all of whom were great arhats, known to all. The Elder Śāriputra, Mahāmaudgalyāyana, Mahākāśyapa, Mahākātyāyana, Mahākauṣṭhila, Revata, Śuddhipanthaka, Nanda, Ānanda, Rāhula, Gavāṃpati, Piṇḍola-Bhāradvāja, Kālodayin, Mahākapphina, Vakula, and Aniruddha: such were all the great disciples. Also present were bodhisattva-mahāsattvas: the Dharma-Prince Mañjuśrī, the Bodhisattva Ajita, the Bodhisattva Gandhahastin, the Bodhisattva Nityodyukta, and such great bodhisattvas, together with Śakra Devānāmindra and an immeasurable multitude of devas.

At that time, the Buddha told the Elder Śāriputra:

"Passing from here to the west, beyond ten trillion buddha-lands, there is a world called Sukhāvatī. In that land there is a Buddha called Amitābha, who is there now, teaching the Dharma. Śāriputra! For what reason is that land called Sukhāvatī? The beings of that land are without any suffering and experience only every kind of joy; therefore it is called Sukhāvatī. Furthermore, Śāriputra! The land of Sukhāvatī has seven tiers of railings, seven layers of jeweled nets, and seven rows of trees, all formed of the four precious substances and encircling the land on every side. For this reason that land is called Sukhāvatī.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra! The land of Sukhāvatī has pools of the seven treasures, filled with water of eight meritorious qualities. The pool-bottoms are covered entirely with golden sand. On the four sides are stairways made of gold, silver, lapis lazuli, and crystal. Above are pavilions also adorned with gold, silver, lapis lazuli, crystal, giant clam-shell, red pearls, and agate. The lotus flowers in the pools are as large as cart-wheels: blue in color with blue radiance, yellow in color with yellow radiance, red in color with red radiance, white in color with white radiance, subtle, wondrous, fragrant, and pure. Śāriputra! The land of Sukhāvatī is accomplished with such merit and adornment.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra! In that buddha-land, heavenly music constantly plays. The ground is made of gold. Six times each day and night, heavenly mandārava flowers rain down. The beings of that land, at dawn each day, use the folds of their robes to gather all manner of wondrous flowers and make offerings to ten trillion Buddhas of other lands. At mealtime they return to their own land, eat, and practice walking meditation. Śāriputra! The land of Sukhāvatī is accomplished with such merit and adornment.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra! In that land there are always various kinds of rare and wondrous birds of many colors: white cranes, peacocks, parrots, śāri birds, kalaviṅkas, and jīvajīvakas. These birds, six times each day and night, produce harmonious and elegant sounds. Their songs proclaim the five roots, the five powers, the seven factors of awakening, the eightfold noble path, and such dharmas. When the beings of that land hear these sounds, they all become mindful of the Buddha, mindful of the Dharma, and mindful of the Sangha. Śāriputra! Do not think that these birds are actually born as a result of karmic retribution. Why? Because in that buddha-land there are no three evil destinies. Śāriputra! In that buddha-land there is not even the name of the three evil paths, let alone their reality. These birds are all produced by the transformative power of the Buddha Amitābha, who wishes the sounds of the Dharma to spread and flow. Śāriputra! In that buddha-land, when a gentle breeze stirs, the rows of jeweled trees and the jeweled nets produce subtle and wondrous sounds, as if a hundred thousand kinds of music were played simultaneously. All who hear these sounds naturally give rise to mindfulness of the Buddha, mindfulness of the Dharma, and mindfulness of the Sangha. Śāriputra! That buddha-land is accomplished with such merit and adornment.

"Śāriputra! What do you think? Why is that Buddha called Amitābha? Śāriputra! That Buddha's light is immeasurable, illuminating the lands of the ten directions without obstruction. Therefore he is called Amitābha. Furthermore, Śāriputra! That Buddha's lifespan and the lifespan of his people are immeasurable, boundless, and of incalculable asaṃkhyeya kalpas. Therefore he is named Amitābha. Śāriputra! Since Amitābha attained Buddhahood, ten kalpas have passed. Furthermore, Śāriputra! That Buddha has an immeasurable, boundless number of śrāvaka disciples, all of whom are arhats, their number beyond reckoning. The bodhisattvas are likewise. Śāriputra! That buddha-land is accomplished with such merit and adornment.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra! All beings born in the land of Sukhāvatī are in the stage of non-retrogression. Among them, many are of the rank of one-birth-supplement, and their number is exceedingly great, beyond reckoning, expressible only in terms of immeasurable, boundless asaṃkhyeya kalpas. Śāriputra! All beings who hear of this should aspire and vow to be reborn in that land. Why? Because they would be in the company of all such supremely virtuous persons.

"Śāriputra! One cannot be reborn in that land through few roots of merit, merit, or conditions. Śāriputra! If there are good men and good women who hear of the Buddha Amitābha and hold fast to his name, whether for one day, two days, three days, four days, five days, six days, or seven days, with an undivided mind: when that person's life draws to an end, the Buddha Amitābha and all the holy assembly will appear before them. At the moment of death, that person's mind will not be confused, and they will immediately be reborn in the land of Sukhāvatī of the Buddha Amitābha. Śāriputra! Because I see this benefit, I speak these words. If there are beings who hear this teaching, they should aspire and vow to be reborn in that land.

"Śāriputra! Just as I now praise the inconceivable merit of the Buddha Amitābha, so too in the east there are the Buddha Akṣobhya, the Buddha Sumeru-Mark, the Buddha Great Sumeru, the Buddha Sumeru-Radiance, and the Buddha Wondrous Sound, and Buddhas as numerous as the sands of the Ganges. Each in their own land extends their broad, long tongue to cover the entire great-thousand world, and speaks the truth: 'All you beings should have faith in this sūtra, the "Sūtra of Praise of Inconceivable Merit Protected and Remembered by All Buddhas."'

"Śāriputra! In the southern world there are the Buddha Sun-Moon-Lamp, the Buddha Renowned Radiance, the Buddha Great Blazing Shoulders, the Buddha Sumeru-Lamp, and the Buddha Immeasurable Diligence, and Buddhas as numerous as the sands of the Ganges. Each in their own land extends their broad, long tongue to cover the entire great-thousand world, and speaks the truth: 'All you beings should have faith in this sūtra, the "Sūtra of Praise of Inconceivable Merit Protected and Remembered by All Buddhas."'

"Śāriputra! In the western world there are the Buddha Immeasurable Life, the Buddha Immeasurable Marks, the Buddha Immeasurable Banners, the Buddha Great Radiance, the Buddha Great Brilliance, the Buddha Jewel Mark, and the Buddha Pure Radiance, and Buddhas as numerous as the sands of the Ganges. Each in their own land extends their broad, long tongue to cover the entire great-thousand world, and speaks the truth: 'All you beings should have faith in this sūtra, the "Sūtra of Praise of Inconceivable Merit Protected and Remembered by All Buddhas."'

"Śāriputra! In the northern world there are the Buddha Blazing Shoulders, the Buddha Supreme Sound, the Buddha Difficult to Deter, the Buddha Sun-Born, and the Buddha Net Brilliance, and Buddhas as numerous as the sands of the Ganges. Each in their own land extends their broad, long tongue to cover the entire great-thousand world, and speaks the truth: 'All you beings should have faith in this sūtra, the "Sūtra of Praise of Inconceivable Merit Protected and Remembered by All Buddhas."'

"Śāriputra! In the world below there are the Buddha Lion, the Buddha Renowned, the Buddha Renowned Radiance, the Buddha Dharma, the Buddha Dharma-Banner, and the Buddha Dharma-Upholder, and Buddhas as numerous as the sands of the Ganges. Each in their own land extends their broad, long tongue to cover the entire great-thousand world, and speaks the truth: 'All you beings should have faith in this sūtra, the "Sūtra of Praise of Inconceivable Merit Protected and Remembered by All Buddhas."'

"Śāriputra! In the world above there are the Buddha Brahmā-Voice, the Buddha Constellation King, the Buddha Supreme Fragrance, the Buddha Fragrant Radiance, the Buddha Great Blazing Shoulders, the Buddha Adorned with Variegated Jewel-Flowers, the Buddha Śāla-Tree King, the Buddha Jewel-Flower Merit, the Buddha Perceiving All Meanings, and the Buddha Like Mount Sumeru, and Buddhas as numerous as the sands of the Ganges. Each in their own land extends their broad, long tongue to cover the entire great-thousand world, and speaks the truth: 'All you beings should have faith in this sūtra, the "Sūtra of Praise of Inconceivable Merit Protected and Remembered by All Buddhas."'

"Śāriputra! What do you think? Why is this called the 'Sūtra Protected and Remembered by All Buddhas'? Śāriputra! If there are good men and good women who hear this sūtra, receive and uphold it, and hear the names of all these Buddhas, these good men and good women are all protected and remembered by all the Buddhas, and all will not retrogress from the supreme, perfect enlightenment. Therefore, Śāriputra! All of you should believe and accept my words and those of all the Buddhas.

"Śāriputra! If there are those who have already aspired, now aspire, or will in the future aspire and vow to be reborn in the land of the Buddha Amitābha, all such persons will not retrogress from the supreme, perfect enlightenment, and in that land they have already been born, are now being born, or will in the future be born. Therefore, Śāriputra! All good men and good women who have faith should aspire and vow to be reborn in that land.

"Śāriputra! Just as I now praise the inconceivable merit of all the Buddhas, those Buddhas also praise my inconceivable merit, saying: 'The Buddha Śākyamuni is able to accomplish an extremely difficult and rare thing. In the sahā land, in the evil age of the five turbidities, in the turbidity of the kalpa, the turbidity of views, the turbidity of afflictions, the turbidity of beings, and the turbidity of lifespan, he attains the supreme, perfect enlightenment and, for all sentient beings, teaches this Dharma that is difficult for the entire world to believe.' Śāriputra! Know that I, in this evil age of the five turbidities, accomplish this difficult thing: attaining the supreme, perfect enlightenment and, for all the world, teaching this Dharma that is difficult to believe. This is truly difficult indeed!"

When the Buddha had finished speaking this sūtra, Śāriputra and all the bhikṣus, and all the devas, humans, asuras, and other beings of the entire world, hearing what the Buddha taught, joyfully accepted it with faith, bowed in homage, and departed.

The dhāraṇī of rebirth in the Pure Land spoken by the Buddha Amitāyus:

*Namo amitābhāya tathāgatāya tadyathā amṛtabhave amṛtasaṃbhave amṛtavikrānte amṛtavikrānta-gāmini gagana-kīrtikare svāhā.*

Those who recite this dhāraṇī will have the Buddha Amitābha constantly dwelling upon the crown of their head. At the end of their lives, they will naturally be reborn in the Pure Land.

The Bodhisattva Nāgārjuna, aspiring to be reborn in the Pure Land, received this dhāraṇī in a dream.

The tripiṭaka master Yaśas recited this dhāraṇī. The Dharma Master Xiù of Tianping Temple received this dhāraṇī orally from the tripiṭaka master Yaśas. That person said: "The original text has not come from abroad. The method for receiving and upholding this dhāraṇī is: six times each day and night, recite it three times seven repetitions each time. At dawn and night, wash and rinse the mouth, chew a willow twig, light incense, and before an image, kneel with palms joined and recite three times seven repetitions. Do this daily without fail, and the four grave offenses, the five acts of immediate retribution, the ten unwholesome deeds, and the sin of slandering the vaipulya sūtras will all be eliminated. In the present life, one will not be troubled by any evil ghosts or spirits. At the end of life, one will naturally be reborn in the land of the Buddha Amitābha. How much more so for those who receive, uphold, read, and recite it day and night: their merit is inconceivable!"
