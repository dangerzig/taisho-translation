## PDFs WITH Glossaries

### 1. T0262 Lotus Sutra (largest glossary)

- **File**: `dBET_T0262_LotusSutra_2007.pdf`
- **Total pages**: 370
- **Glossary**: pp. 319-323 (~5 pages, ~50+ terms with full definitions)
- **Index**: pp. 327-334+ (detailed subject index)
- **Glossary format**: Term in bold, Sanskrit in parentheses, followed by colon and definition (1-3 sentences)
- **Sample entries**:
  - **anuttara samyaksambodhi**: Complete, perfect enlightenment
  - **arhat** ("one who is worthy"): A saint who has completely eradicated the passions and is no longer subject to rebirth. The highest stage of attainment in Hinayana Buddhism.
  - **bodhisattva** ("enlightenment being"): The spiritual ideal of the Mahayana. One who seeks enlightenment not only for oneself but for all sentient beings.
  - **dharani**: A spell or incantation, often in the form of a string of Sanskrit syllables
  - **kalpa**: An immeasurably long period of time; an eon

### 2. T0159 Previous Lives of the Buddha

- **File**: `dBET_T0159_Previous-Lives_2021.pdf`
- **Total pages**: 270
- **Glossary**: pp. 219-220 (~2 pages, Sanskrit terms with definitions, some with numbered sub-lists)
- **Index**: pp. 223-250 (~28 pages, the most extensive index found in the survey)
- **Bibliography**: p. 221
- **Glossary format**: Term followed by Sanskrit in parentheses, colon, and definition; some entries include numbered lists
- **Sample entries**:
  - **samadhi**: A state of focused concentration or mental composure
  - **six paths** (Skt. sadgati): Six states of transmigratory existence: (1) hell, (2) hungry ghosts (pretas), (3) animals, (4) demigods (asuras), (5) humans, and (6) gods (devas)
  - **six perfections** (Skt. satparamita): The perfection (paramita) of six qualities: (1) giving (dana), (2) morality (sila), (3) patience (ksanti), (4) effort (virya), (5) meditation (dhyana), and (6) wisdom (prajna)
  - **three poisons** (Skt. tridosa): Three basic mental afflictions: (1) desire or greed, (2) hatred or anger, (3) ignorance or delusion
  - **yaksa**: A class of demonic beings originally regarded as possessing a generally benevolent disposition

## Observations

1. **Glossary presence correlates loosely with translation era**: Three of the five glossary volumes are Alpha (older) editions (T0203, T0211, T0676), while the two newer glossary volumes (T0262, T0159) are notably larger texts where glossaries serve a clearer reader-assistance function.

2. **Glossary format is consistent**: All five glossaries use the same basic format: Sanskrit or technical Buddhist term, followed by a colon, followed by a brief English definition. The Lotus Sutra glossary is the most detailed, with multi-sentence definitions.

3. **All volumes have indices**: Every BDK volume examined includes a subject index, typically 5-15 pages. The largest index found is T0159 (Previous Lives) at ~28 pages.

4. **No separate Sanskrit-Chinese or Chinese-Sanskrit indices**: None of the 17 volumes examined contained bilingual terminology indices (Sanskrit-Chinese, Chinese-English, etc.). All indices are English-language subject indices with occasional Sanskrit terms included as index headings.

5. **Omnibus volumes**: Several PDFs contain two or more translated texts. Some have separate indices per text (e.g., AnalysisMiddleExtremes_Nagasena), while others have a single combined index (e.g., Master-of-Medicine_Candrottara, Srimala_Vimalakirti).

6. **Standard BDK back matter structure**: text body, notes/endnotes, [glossary if present], bibliography, index, BDK English Tripitaka catalog listing (~10-14 pages at end of every volume).
