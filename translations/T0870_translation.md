# 略述金剛頂瑜伽分別聖位修證法門

## A Brief Account of the Vajraśekhara Yoga Method for Distinguishing Sacred Positions and Cultivating Realization

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0870

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

### Preface

The tradition of mantra-dhāraṇī is the esoteric teaching of all tathāgatas, a gate of sudden realization through self-enlightened holy wisdom. It is also the bodhisattva's complete reception of pure precepts and immeasurable dignified conduct, entering the ocean-assembly altar of all tathāgatas to receive the bodhisattva's office, transcending the three realms, receiving from the Buddha the samādhi gate of empowered instruction, endowed with complete conditions, suddenly accumulating merit and vast wisdom, and becoming irreversible from unsurpassed enlightenment. Free from all celestial māras, all afflictions and karmic obstacles, which are dissolved moment by moment. One realizes the Buddha's four kinds of bodies, namely: the self-nature body (svabhāvikakāya), the enjoyment body (saṃbhogakāya), the transformation body (nirmāṇakāya), and the equal-flow body (niṣyandakāya), perfecting the five wisdoms, the thirty-seven and other unshared Buddha dharmas.

Now, the transformation body of the Tathāgata, in the Jambudvīpa kingdom of Magadha at the bodhimaṇḍa, achieved perfect enlightenment. For bodhisattvas before the bhūmis, for śrāvakas, pratyekabuddhas, and ordinary beings, he taught the three-vehicle Dharma, sometimes speaking according to the other's intent, sometimes from his own intent, using various methods for various root capacities, so that through proper practice they might attain the rewards of human and celestial realms, or the fruits of liberation of the three vehicles, either advancing or retreating from unsurpassed enlightenment, enduring three incalculable great kalpas of diligent and arduous practice before finally becoming a buddha. Born in the royal palace, passing away between the twin śāla trees, leaving relics for building stūpas and making offerings, bringing about the superior fruits of human and celestial rebirth and the causes of nirvāṇa.

This differs from the enjoyment-body Vairocana, who, at the summit of the form realm in the fourth dhyāna heaven, the Akaniṣṭha celestial palace, gathers all buddhas throughout empty space and the dharmadhātu, along with all great bodhisattvas who have fulfilled the ten bhūmis, to witness and awaken body and mind, suddenly realizing unsurpassed enlightenment.

The self-enjoyment buddha, from his mind, emanates immeasurable bodhisattvas, all of one nature, namely vajra nature. Facing the All-Illuminating Tathāgata, they receive the office of abhiṣeka. These bodhisattvas each proclaim the gate of the three mysteries and offer them to Vairocana and all tathāgatas, requesting empowerment and instruction. Vairocana Buddha says: "In the future, throughout immeasurable worlds, for those of the supreme vehicle, enable them to achieve worldly and supramundane siddhi in this very lifetime." Those bodhisattvas, having received the Tathāgata's instruction, prostrate at the Buddha's feet, circumambulate Vairocana Buddha, and each returns to their own direction and original position. They become the five wheels, their original-vow emblems. Whoever sees or hears them, or enters their maṇḍala, can sever the karmic obstacles of the five destinies' cycle of birth and death. Within the five liberation wheels, from one buddha to the next, offering and attending, all are enabled to attain unsurpassed enlightenment and become of determined nature. Like vajra, indestructible. This is Vairocana's assembly of the sacred, which then manifests as a stūpa of direct realization. Each bodhisattva and each vajra abides in their own samādhi, abides in their own liberation, all dwelling in the power of great compassion and vows to broadly benefit sentient beings. Whoever sees or hears them all realizes samādhi. Merit and wisdom are suddenly accumulated and accomplished.

At that time, Vajradhātu Vairocana Buddha, at the summit of the form realm in the Akaniṣṭha celestial palace, first achieved perfect enlightenment in his enjoyment body. Having realized the equal wisdom of all tathāgatas, he entered the samaya of the all-tathāgata vajra-equality-wisdom seal. He then realized the luminous wisdom treasury of the equal self-nature of all tathāgatas' Dharma. Having achieved perfect enlightenment, all tathāgatas produced from Sattvavajra the great maṇi jewel of the Ākāśagarbha, and with it consecrated his crown, causing the wisdom of Avalokiteśvara, the Dharma-King, to arise. He established the skillful wisdom of Viśvakarman of all tathāgatas and was sent to proceed to the summit of Mount Sumeru, the Vajra-Maṇi-Jewel-Peak pavilion.

Having assembled the sacred hosts, Vairocana Buddha then empowered all tathāgatas and arranged lion thrones in the four directions. Then Akṣobhya Tathāgata, Ratnasambhava Tathāgata, Avalokiteśvara-rāja Tathāgata, and Amoghasiddhi Tathāgata in turn empowered Vairocana Buddha.

Now, the enjoyment body is of two kinds: first, self-enjoyment; second, other-enjoyment. Vairocana Buddha, within his own mind, realized the four wisdoms of self-enjoyment: the great mirror wisdom (ādarśa-jñāna), the equality wisdom (samatā-jñāna), the investigative wisdom (pratyavekṣaṇā-jñāna), and the accomplishing wisdom (kṛtyānuṣṭhāna-jñāna). Externally, for the other-enjoyment of great bodhisattvas who have fulfilled the ten bhūmis, from the four wisdoms he emanated four buddhas, each abiding in their own direction upon their own throne.

Vairocana Buddha, within his own mind, realized the five-peaked vajra bodhicitta samādhi wisdom, and for self-enjoyment, from that samādhi wisdom emanated vajra light, illuminating all worlds of the ten directions, purifying the great bodhicitta of all sentient beings. Returning and gathering into one body, to enable all bodhisattvas to enjoy the samādhi wisdom, he became the form of Vajra-pāramitā, abiding in the moon-disk before Vairocana Tathāgata.

Vairocana Buddha, within his own mind, realized the ākāśa-jewel great-maṇi merit samādhi wisdom, and for self-enjoyment, from that samādhi wisdom emanated ākāśa-jewel light, illuminating all worlds of the ten directions, perfecting the merit of all sentient beings. Returning and gathering into one body, to enable all bodhisattvas to enjoy the samādhi wisdom, he became the form of Vajra-ratna-pāramitā, abiding in the moon-disk to Vairocana Tathāgata's right.

Vairocana Buddha, within his own mind, realized the karma-vajra great-vigor samādhi wisdom, and for self-enjoyment, from that samādhi wisdom emanated karma light, illuminating all worlds of the ten directions, eliminating all sloth from sentient beings and producing great vigor. Returning and gathering into one body, to enable all bodhisattvas to enjoy the samādhi wisdom, he became the form of Karma-pāramitā, abiding in the moon-disk to Vairocana Tathāgata's left.

If explained in sequential order, there are differences of before and after. But from the perspective of the enjoyment-body buddha, all three kinds of pure karma of body, speech, and mind are suddenly realized, pervading the dharmadhātu. In each Dharma gate, each principle, each pore and bodily mark, pervading empty space, without mutual obstruction, each abiding in their original position, forming the universally illuminating Vairocana self-enjoyment body and other-enjoyment body.

If explained according to the sequential method of the two vehicles, without fully cultivating the thirty-seven factors of awakening, it would be impossible to realize the fruits of the path. But to realize the self-enjoyment-body buddha, one must have the thirty-seven samādhi wisdoms to accomplish buddhahood. The Sanskrit Laṅkāvatāra, in its verse chapter, states: "Self-nature, enjoyment, transformation, and equal-flow: the Buddha's qualities number thirty-six, all identical with the self-nature body. Together with the dharmadhātu body, the total becomes thirty-seven."

At the very beginning, upon generating the bodhicitta for the supreme vehicle, through Akṣobhya Buddha's empowerment, one realizes the perfected bodhicitta. Through realizing awakening, one receives the abhiṣeka of Ratnasambhava Buddha descending from empty space, receiving the office of Dharma King of the three realms. Through Avalokiteśvara-rāja Buddha's empowerment, the speech-wheel can expound immeasurable sūtra Dharma gates. Through Amoghasiddhi Buddha's empowerment, in all Buddha activities and activities benefiting sentient beings, all that is done for benefit and joy is completely accomplished. Through Vajra-pāramitā's empowerment, one realizes the great mirror wisdom, perfected, pervading the dharmadhātu and empty space. Through Ratna-pāramitā's empowerment, toward boundless sentient-being worlds and material worlds, one realizes the equality wisdom. Through Dharma-pāramitā's empowerment, regarding immeasurable samādhi and dhāraṇī gates and all dharmas of liberation, one attains the investigative wisdom. Through Karma-pāramitā's empowerment, in the immeasurable establishment of defiled worlds and pure worlds, one realizes the accomplishing wisdom.

Through Vajrasattva Bodhisattva's empowerment, in a single fierce moment one suddenly realizes unsurpassed enlightenment. Through Vajrarāja Bodhisattva's empowerment, in the gates of benefit and joy for sentient beings, one is endowed with the four methods of attraction. Through Vajrarāga Bodhisattva's empowerment, toward boundless sentient beings, the unconditional great compassion never ceases. Through Vajrasādhu Bodhisattva's empowerment, one has unquenchable enthusiasm for all wholesome dharmas, and upon seeing even the slightest virtue, one offers praise. Through Vajraratna Bodhisattva's empowerment, one realizes unstained wisdom as vast and perfected as empty space. Through Vajrateja Bodhisattva's empowerment, one realizes the light of wisdom, like the sun's disk that illuminates everything. Through Vajraketu Bodhisattva's empowerment, one can fulfill the worldly and supramundane wishes of sentient beings, like the true cintāmaṇi jewel banner, without discrimination fulfilling all desires. Through Vajrahāsa Bodhisattva's empowerment, all sentient beings who see or hear are filled with joyful leaping, become determined in the Dharma, and receive the benefit and joy of the Dharma. Through Vajradharma Bodhisattva's empowerment, one realizes the fundamental purity of the dharma-nature, can expound the wondrous Dharma gates, and knows that all dharmas are like a raft metaphor. Through Vajratīkṣṇa Bodhisattva's empowerment, with the sword of prajñāpāramitā, one can sever the immeasurable mixed fetters and sufferings of self and others. Through Vajrahetu Bodhisattva's empowerment, in immeasurable buddha-worlds, one requests all tathāgatas to turn the wondrous Dharma wheel. Through Vajrabhāṣa Bodhisattva's empowerment, with the sixty-four kinds of dharma-voice pervading the ten directions, according to the type of sentient being, all receive the benefit of the Dharma. Through Vajrakarma Bodhisattva's empowerment, in boundless buddha-field ocean assemblies, one performs the great offering ceremonies. Through Vajrarakṣa Bodhisattva's empowerment, donning the armor of great vow adornments, one returns to saṃsāra to broadly perform bodhisattva deeds, guiding sentient beings and establishing them in the Buddha-Dharma. Through Vajrayakṣa Bodhisattva's empowerment, one can crush all celestial māras and non-Buddhist paths, and can defeat the enemies of beginningless afflictions. Through Vajramuṣṭi Bodhisattva's empowerment, in the gates of the three mysteries and immeasurable mantra samādhi mudrās, all are united into one body. Through Vajralāsyā Bodhisattva's empowerment, in the enjoyment of dharma-joy and perfected bliss, one attains autonomous enjoyment-wisdom. Through Vajramālā Bodhisattva's empowerment, one attains the flower garland of the factors of awakening as adornment. Through Vajragītā Bodhisattva's empowerment, one attains the Tathāgata's wondrous subtle voice, which listeners never tire of hearing, and regarding the sacred qualities and liberation, one fully comprehends that all dharmas are like an echo. Through Vajranṛtyā Bodhisattva's empowerment, one attains the ability to manifest division-bodies in an instant, suddenly reaching boundless worlds. Through Vajradhūpā Bodhisattva's empowerment, one attains the Tathāgata's pleasing unhindered wisdom-fragrance. Through Vajrapuṣpā Bodhisattva's empowerment, one can open the awakening-flower in the mud of sentient beings' afflictions. Through Vajrālokā Bodhisattva's empowerment, one attains the five purified eyes, benefiting self and others, illuminating dharmas with sovereign freedom. Through Vajragandha Bodhisattva's empowerment, one attains the Buddha's five kinds of untainted pure body. Through Vajrāṅkuśa Bodhisattva's empowerment, one attains the swift samādhi that summons all sacred assemblies. Through Vajrapāśa Bodhisattva's empowerment, one attains unhindered skillful wisdom like empty space. Through Vajrasphota Bodhisattva's empowerment, one attains the Buddha's firm, unstained contemplation of great compassion and liberation. Through Vajraghaṇṭā Bodhisattva's empowerment, one attains the voice of the Tathāgata's prajñāpāramitā, which for those who hear it can crush the evil seeds in the ālaya consciousness.

Through the empowering force of these thirty-seven internally-realized supreme Vajradhātu division-wisdoms, one suddenly realizes the body of Vairocana. From the invisible crown, immeasurable Buddha-crown dharmakāya emanate, gathering like clouds in empty space, forming the Dharma assembly. Light universally covers like the tiers of a stūpa. Those who have fulfilled the ten bhūmis cannot behold it. Yet it silently empowers sentient beings, causing their physical and mental karmic obstacles to be completely eliminated. None can know it; although they cannot consciously perceive it, it can quell their sufferings and cause them to be reborn in the good destinies. From the light emanate the sixteen bodhisattvas, the eight directional inner and outer great protectors, and so forth. Emanated light in succession illuminates and touches the evil destinies, forming the steps of the stūpa, guarding and protecting the dharmadhātu palace of all buddhas' stūpas, forming the tiers, causing the body to directly realize the body of Vajradhātu Tathāgata Vairocana, the All-Illuminating One.
