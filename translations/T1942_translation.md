# Supplementary Liturgy for the Visualization Practice of the Lotus Samādhi

## *Fǎhuá Sānmèi Xíngshì Yùnxiǎng Bǔzhù Yí* (法華三昧行事運想補助儀)

Taishō 1942

Composed by Zhànrán (*Zhànrán*, 湛然, 711-782) of the Tang dynasty.

In the practice of repentance liturgy, although the method is widely followed in the world, the ritual procedures and visualizations are often incomplete. Some merely read the repentance text roughly, hardly getting through half of it intelligibly. Others push or resist with sheer force, accomplishing nothing in their contemplative practice. The spirit of diligent cultivation lacks the initial thread of entry. As it is said, "Arduous practice and bitter austerities are not the cause of nirvāṇa." The liturgical guidelines for prostrations and recitations are not unclear in the texts, yet because of scattered minds and heavy dullness, the visualizations are difficult to accomplish. Drawing on Tiantai (*Tiāntái*, 天台), I have once again made detailed supplements, compiling the essential points so that with each word one gives rise to mindfulness, and with each thought one accomplishes the visualization. One gazes upon each item as though it were directly before one's eyes. Thus no moment is wasted and both the rite and the dharma are accomplished. Following the original text, the procedure covers ten dharmas in total, summarizing the essential matters of principle and practice.

First, purify the practice site. Second, purify the body. Third, the three modes of activity make offerings: next, offer incense; invite the buddhas; next, invite the buddhas and bodhisattvas. Fifth, praise the buddhas. Sixth, prostrate to the buddhas, including Prabhūtaratna (*Duōbǎo*, 多寶) Buddha, the emanation buddhas of the eastern direction, the buddhas of the three times, relics and images, and the rest. The Wonderful Dharma (*Miàofǎ*, 妙法). The various bodhisattvas, Śāriputra (*Shèlìfú*, 舍利弗) and others. Samantabhadra (*Pǔxián*, 普賢) Bodhisattva. Universal dedication. Seventh, repentance: one should employ the ten minds in forward and reverse order, as follows. Next, repent for the six sense-faculties. Exhortation. Sympathetic joy. Transfer of merit. Making vows. Walking meditation. Taking the three refuges.

The section on seated meditation is abbreviated. One should follow the ten dharmas for accomplishing the vehicle as described in the *Mohe Zhiguan* (*Zhǐguān*, 止觀, "Great Calming and Contemplation"). When practicing the Lotus Repentance at twilight and the fifth watch, if time is short, use this abbreviated text:

"I and all sentient beings of the dharma-realm, from beginningless time, have been shrouded by ignorance, confused and deluded. Through the six sense-faculties and three modes of activity, we have unwholesomely committed the ten evils, the four grave offenses, and the five heinous acts, as well as the ten major and forty-eight minor bodhisattva precepts, immeasurable and boundless beyond all description. The buddhas of the ten directions are always present in the world, their dharma-voice unceasing, their wonderful fragrance filling all, their dharma-flavor overflowing space, emitting pure radiance illuminating and touching everything. The eternally abiding wonderful principle pervades all of space. Yet from beginningless time, my six sense-faculties have been inwardly blind and my three modes of activity have been shrouded in darkness. I have not seen, not heard, not perceived, and not known. Because of these causes and conditions, I have long flowed through birth and death, passing through evil destinies for hundreds of thousands of myriads of kalpas, with never any prospect of escape. As the sūtra says, Śākyamuni Tathāgata is named Vairocana (*Pílúzhēnà*, 毘盧遮那, 'Pervading Everywhere'). Therefore one should know that all dharmas are none other than the buddha-dharma. Yet I have failed to understand this and have followed the current of ignorance. This means that within bodhi I have seen impurity, and within liberation I have given rise to bondage. Today I begin to awaken; today I begin to repent. I give rise to great shame and great fear. I recite and uphold the Great Vehicle, make offerings with the three modes of activity, take refuge in and turn toward Samantabhadra Bodhisattva and all the world-honored ones, burn incense, scatter flowers, and openly confess and repent. The actions of the six sense-faculties and three modes of activity are forever severed. I will continue without ever committing them again. Because of these causes and conditions, the actions of my three modes of activity and six sense-faculties, and those of all sentient beings of the dharma-realm, from beginningless time, whether presently being done or to be done in the future, whether done by oneself, taught to others, or witnessed and rejoiced in by others, whether remembered or not remembered, whether recognized or not recognized, whether doubted or not doubted, whether concealed or exposed, are all ultimately purified."
