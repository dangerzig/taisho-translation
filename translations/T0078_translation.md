# 兜調經
## The Sūtra of Doutiao

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0078

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown (失譯)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the country of Śrāvastī. In that country there was a brahmin named Doutiao who had a son named Gu. Doutiao was a sharp-tempered and wicked person who constantly reviled others with abuse. When he died, he was reborn as a dog in his own household, a dog named Luo. His son Gu was fond of this dog: he put a golden chain on it, had it sleep on a bed always spread with woolen rugs and fine carpets, and fed it delicacies on a golden plate. One day, when Gu had gone out to the marketplace, the Buddha passed by Gu's gate, and the white dog barked at the Buddha. The Buddha said: "In your former life you used to raise your hand and shout abuse; now you have become a dog and bark. Have you no sense of shame?" The dog, ashamed, ran away, pushed its head under the bed, and wept with tears streaming down. After the Buddha departed, the dog would no longer get up onto the bed where it used to sleep, but lay on the ground and would not eat when given food. When Gu returned home from outside, he saw the dog was not eating and asked the household: "What is the matter with the dog?" The household said: "A śramaṇa came by just now. We do not know what he said, but the dog ran under the bed and has lain on the ground ever since, refusing to eat." Gu said: "Which way did the śramaṇa go?" The household said: "He went east." Gu immediately followed after him and caught up.

The Buddha was beneath a tree, teaching the sūtras to the bhikṣus. The Buddha saw Gu coming from afar and told the bhikṣus: "Gu is coming, but he has not attained the Way. If he were to die now, he would fall into hell." The bhikṣus asked the Buddha: "Why would he fall into hell?" The Buddha said: "This man comes bearing an evil intention, wishing to harm another. Therefore he would fall into hell."

When Gu reached the Buddha, he asked: "Just now, which śramaṇa passed by my gate and scolded my dog, so that it will neither eat nor lie in its place?"

The Buddha replied: "I passed by your gate. The white dog barked at me, and I said to it: 'In your former life you used to raise your hand and shout abuse; now you have become a dog and bark. Have you no sense of shame!' The dog, ashamed, ran away, pushed its head under the bed, and wept with tears streaming down."

Gu asked the Buddha: "What was this dog to me?"

The Buddha said: "It is better not to ask. To hear it would displease you."

Gu said: "Please tell me."

The Buddha said: "To tell you would make you angry."

Gu said: "I will not be angry. I wish to hear it."

The Buddha said: "It is your father Doutiao."

Gu said: "My father Doutiao, when he was alive, was learned in the scriptures and the Way. He would not become a dog."

The Buddha said: "It was precisely because of his knowledge that he became proud and arrogant, and so he was reborn as a dog. If you wish to verify that this is truly your father, go home and say to the dog: 'If you are truly my father Doutiao, you should eat from the old vessel. If you are truly my father, you should sleep in the old place. If you are truly my father, you should show me where the treasures and valuables were hidden.'"

Gu returned home at once and called to the dog: "Luo! If you are truly my father Doutiao, eat this food." The dog immediately ate the food. Gu said again: "Luo! If you are truly my father, sleep in the old place." The dog immediately went and lay in the old place. Gu said again: "Luo! If you are truly my father, show me where the treasures and valuables were hidden." The dog used its mouth to point to the right leg of the bed and dug at the ground with its two front paws to show the spot. Gu dug where the dog had pawed and found a great abundance of precious treasures. Gu was overjoyed and went back to the Buddha.

The Buddha saw him coming from afar and told the bhikṣus: "Now Gu is coming. He has not attained the Way, but if he were to die now, he would be reborn in heaven." The bhikṣus asked the Buddha: "What is the cause for him to be reborn in heaven?" The Buddha said: "This man comes bearing a wholesome intention. Therefore he would be reborn in heaven."

When Gu arrived before the Buddha, he prostrated and addressed the Buddha: "It is truly as the Buddha has said." Gu then asked the Buddha: "Why is it that among people in this world, some are long-lived and some are short-lived? Why are some afflicted with much illness and others with little? Why are some handsome and others ugly? Why are some of high station and others low? Why are some attractive and others unattractive? Why are some wealthy and others poor? Why are some wise and others foolish?"

The Buddha told Gu: "A person in this world who delights in killing and lacks compassion, after death enters hell. When the punishment in hell is exhausted, they are reborn as a human but will be short-lived. A person in this world who does not kill and has compassion, after death goes to heaven. Coming down from heaven and reborn among humans, they will be long-lived. A person in this world who delights in strife and discord, who terrifies others with weapons and clubs, after death enters hell. When the punishment in hell is exhausted, they are reborn as a human but will have much illness. A person in this world who delights in harmony and does not terrify others with weapons and clubs, after death is reborn in heaven. Coming down from heaven and reborn among humans, they will have little illness. A person in this world who delights in anger and rage, who becomes angry upon hearing kind words, angry upon hearing harsh words, angry upon seeing a worthy person, angry upon seeing a foolish person, unable to distinguish good from evil and only wishing to be angry, after death enters hell. When the punishment in hell is exhausted, they are reborn as a human with a face that is sallow and withered. A person in this world who is free from anger and rage, who respects the worthy upon seeing them and is patient with the foolish, after death goes to heaven. Coming down from heaven and reborn among humans, they will always have a fine complexion, and will be harmonious, wise, and virtuous. A person in this world who is unattractive, who does not rise for the elderly, who is unfilial to parents, who does not show love and respect upon seeing parents, and who feels resentment toward those who are filial and respectful to their parents and elders, after death enters hell. When the punishment in hell is exhausted, they are reborn as a human but will be unattractive and detested by all. A person in this world who is filial to parents and respectful to elders, who teaches those who are unfilial and disrespectful of elders, and who delights in speaking kind words to others, after death goes to heaven. Coming down from heaven and reborn among humans, they will be loved and respected by all. A person in this world who is arrogant and disrespectful of superiors, who is domineering and overbearing, after death enters hell. When the punishment in hell is exhausted, they are reborn as a human of low station. A person in this world who is not arrogant and always respects superiors, who is not domineering or overbearing, after death goes to heaven. Coming down from heaven and reborn among humans, they will be of high station. A person in this world who is miserly and greedy, who though wealthy does not give to the poor, does not look after relatives, does not delight in generosity, who clings to food and drink, who does not give to śramaṇas and practitioners of the Way, and who does not even dare to eat their fill, after death enters hell. When the punishment in hell is exhausted, they are reborn as a human but will be destitute and reduced to begging. A person in this world whose mind is free from miserliness and greed, who regardless of poverty or wealth delights in giving to śramaṇas and practitioners of the Way, who gives to the poor and looks after relatives, and who always eats their fill, after death goes to heaven. Coming down from heaven and reborn in the world, they will be wealthy and happy, loved and respected by all. A person in this world who hears that there are learned masters of the scriptures, whether śramaṇas or ascetics, but does not go to ask about the Way that leads beyond the world, whose mind is jealous of the accomplished, after death enters hell. When the punishment in hell is exhausted, they are reborn as a human but will be foolish and ignorant, knowing nothing, no different from an animal. A person in this world who hears that there are learned masters of the scriptures, whether śramaṇas or ascetics, and delights in going to ask about matters of liberation, whose mind is without jealousy, who cherishes and admires the accomplished, after death goes to heaven. Coming down from heaven and reborn among humans, they will be learned in the scriptures and understand the Way, honored and relied upon by all."

The Buddha said: "Those who do good go to heaven; those who do evil descend into hell. Those who seek longevity obtain longevity; those who seek a short life obtain a short life. Those who seek illness obtain illness; those who seek freedom from illness obtain freedom from illness. Those who seek a fine complexion obtain a fine complexion; those who seek an ugly complexion obtain an ugly complexion. Those who seek high station obtain high station; those who seek low station obtain low station. Those who seek attractiveness obtain attractiveness; those who seek unattractiveness obtain unattractiveness. Those who seek wealth obtain wealth; those who seek poverty obtain poverty. Those who seek wisdom obtain wisdom; those who seek foolishness obtain foolishness. A person's good and evil deeds in this world are like planting grain and harvesting grain, planting wheat and harvesting wheat, planting rice and harvesting rice: do good and receive good; do evil and receive evil."

Gu then drew back and knelt upright, saying: "When I came earlier and saw the dog not eating, my heart was filled with anger and resentment. That was due to my own foolishness. Now, what the Buddha has said is like a blind person gaining sight, a deaf person gaining hearing, a person who has fallen into deep water being brought out, a person stricken with madness being cured, a person walking in darkness seeing the sun and moon. I beg the Buddha to have compassion and allow me to repent. With great kindness, I shall take up the five precepts and become an upāsaka."

The Buddha said: "In future ages, those who chant this sūtra and those who hear its words, whose hearts are moved, whose hairs stand on end, and whose tears flow: such persons shall become disciples of the Buddha Maitreya and shall attain liberation."
