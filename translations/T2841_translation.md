# 大佛略懺
## Great Buddha Abbreviated Confession

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 2841

Translated from the Chinese. Author unknown.

---

When one wishes to perform the confession liturgy, one must first pay reverence to the Three Jewels. Why is this? Because the Three Jewels are the excellent field of merit for all sentient beings. If one can turn toward them in refuge, one eliminates immeasurable transgressions and increases immeasurable merit. Those who can practice this leave birth and death and attain the joy of liberation.

Therefore, your disciple So-and-so takes refuge in all buddhas of the ten directions throughout the entirety of space. Takes refuge in all honored Dharma of the ten directions throughout the entirety of space. Takes refuge in all holy saṃgha of the ten directions throughout the entirety of space, and their disciples.

The reason your disciple today performs confession is precisely this: from beginningless time, in the stage of ordinary beings, regardless of noble or base station, transgressions are immeasurable. Some transgressions arise through the three karmas. Some faults arise through the six characteristics. Some arise from one's own wrong thinking. Some arise through the influence of external objects giving rise to attachment. In this way, the ten evils increase and the eighty-four thousand gates of defilement become firmly established.

However, though the characteristics of these transgressions are immeasurable, broadly speaking they do not go beyond three. What are these three? First, afflictions. Second, karma. Third, fruition. These three kinds of dharmas can obstruct the holy path as well as the excellent things of the human and heavenly realms. Therefore the sūtras call them "the three obstructions." This is why all buddhas and bodhisattvas teach the expedient means of confession to eliminate these three. When these three are eliminated, the six sense-faculties, the ten evils, and even the eighty-four thousand gates of defilement are all completely purified.

Therefore, your disciple today arouses this superior wholesome mind and wishes to confess and eliminate these three. To eliminate these three transgressions, what kind of mind should one employ so that they can be eliminated? One should first arouse seven kinds of mind as expedient means. Then these transgressions can indeed be eliminated. What are the seven? First, shame and remorse. Second, fear and dread. Third, disgust and renunciation. Fourth, generating the mind of bodhi. Fifth, equanimity toward enemies and friends. Sixth, mindfulness of repaying the Buddha's kindness. Seventh, contemplating the nature of transgressions as empty.

**The first, shame and remorse.** Consider that I and Śākyamuni Tathāgata were equally ordinary beings. Yet now the World-Honored One, since attaining the Way, has already passed through innumerable dust-mote kalpas. While we have together been intoxicated with the six objects of sense, drifting through birth and death, with never any escape from them. How shameful, how mortifying, how reprehensible, how disgraceful!

**The second, fear and dread.** Since we are ordinary beings, the karma of body, speech, and mind constantly corresponds with transgression. Because of these causes and conditions, after life ends, we should fall into the hells, animal realms, and hungry ghost realms, receiving immeasurable suffering. This is truly alarming, truly frightening, truly terrifying, truly dreadful.

**The third, disgust and renunciation.** One should contemplate that within birth and death there are only impermanence, suffering, emptiness, non-self, and impurity. Like bubbles on water, rapidly arising and rapidly perishing. Transmigration flows on like the turning of a wheel. Birth, old age, sickness, and death: the eight sufferings press upon one, with never a moment's rest. All of us should examine our own bodies from head to foot. Within there are only thirty-six substances: hair, bodily hair, nails, teeth, pus, mucus, saliva, the raw and cooked storehouses, the large intestine, small intestine, spleen, kidneys, heart, lungs, liver, gallbladder, fat, grease, membranes, brain, sinews, bones, marrow, urine and feces, the nine orifices constantly flowing. Therefore the sūtra says: "This body is an accumulation of many sufferings; all of it is impure. What wise person would delight in this body?" Since birth and death possess all these kinds of evil dharmas, they are truly to be dreaded and loathed.

**The fourth, generating the mind of bodhi.** The sūtra says: "One who always delights in the Buddha-body, which is the Dharma-body: it is born from immeasurable merit and wisdom, born from the six pāramitās, born from kindness, compassion, joy, and equanimity, born from the thirty-seven factors of awakening, born from such various kinds of merit and wisdom." If one wishes to attain the Buddha-body, one should generate the mind of bodhi, seeking the knowledge of all modes, the eternal, blissful, self, and pure fruit of sarvajña, purifying buddha-lands, accomplishing sentient beings, without any reluctance regarding body, life, or possessions.

**The fifth, equanimity toward enemies and friends.** One should give rise to kindness and compassion toward all sentient beings, without the notion of self and other. Why? If one perceives enemies and friends, this is discrimination. Because of discrimination, one gives rise to various attachments. Because of the conditions of attachment, afflictions arise. Because of affliction as a condition, one creates various evil karma. Because of evil karma as a condition, one obtains the fruit of suffering.

**The sixth, mindfulness of repaying the Buddha's kindness.** In past immeasurable kalpas, the Tathāgata gave up his head, eyes, marrow, brain, limbs, hands and feet, kingdoms, wife and children, elephants, horses, and the seven treasures, cultivating various austere practices for our sake. This kindness, this virtue, is truly difficult to repay. Therefore the sūtra says: "Even if one carries the Buddha on one's head and on both shoulders for kalpas as numerous as the sands of the Ganges River, one cannot repay this debt." If we wish to repay the Tathāgata's kindness, we should in this present life practice fierce vigor, endure toil and hardship, not sparing body or life, establish the Three Jewels, penetrate the Great Vehicle, broadly convert sentient beings, and together enter the right path.

**The seventh, contemplating the nature of transgressions as empty.** Transgressions have no real characteristics. They arise from causes and conditions; they exist through delusion. Since they arise from causes and conditions, they can also be eliminated through causes and conditions. "Arising from causes and conditions" means drawing near to evil companions and creating wrongdoing without reason. "Eliminated through causes and conditions" means now, because of deepened mind and confession. Therefore the sūtra says: "The characteristics of these transgressions are not within, not without, and not in between." Therefore these transgressions are originally empty in their arising.

Having thus aroused the seven kinds of mind as described above, before the buddhas, worthies, and sages of the ten directions, with fists raised and palms joined, we lay bare our utmost shame and remorse, reforming our former ways, unfurling our hearts and minds, cleansing our innermost being. Confessing in this way, what transgression will not be eliminated? What obstruction will not be dissolved?

If instead one merely goes through the motions, carelessly and casually, with empty feelings, one only toils the body in vain. What benefit is there in that? Moreover, human life is impermanent, like the flickering of a candle; one breath not returned, and one faces the ashes of the three paths of suffering and retribution. What should be received by this very body: quickly calculate and seek to escape! In the dark and murky depths, pardon has no scheduled time. Alone one endures this suffering, with no one to rely upon.

Do not say: "In this present life I have committed no such transgressions, and therefore I cannot confess with utmost sincerity." The sūtra says: "Every step and movement of an ordinary being is nothing but transgression." Furthermore, in all past lives, one has accomplished immeasurable evil karma that pursues the practitioner like a shadow following the body. If one is not ashamed, transgressions deepen daily. Concealing one's faults and defects is what the Buddha's teaching does not allow. It says to repent and confess former transgressions. This is what the Vimalakīrti esteems. Thus the wise are long sunk in the ocean of suffering because of concealment.

Therefore, your disciple today makes open confession. These obstructions mutually give rise to each other: because of afflictions, evil karma arises; because of evil karma, the fruit of suffering is obtained.

Therefore, your disciple today, with utmost mind: first, one should confess the obstruction of afflictions. Moreover, these afflictions are variously reproached by all buddhas, bodhisattvas, and sages who have entered the principle. They also call these afflictions "the enemy," because they can sever the root of wisdom-life of sentient beings. They also call these afflictions "a torrent," because they can sweep sentient beings into the great ocean of suffering of birth and death. They also call these afflictions "fetters and chains," because they can bind sentient beings in the prison of birth and death so they cannot escape. Thus throughout the six destinies, birth and death continue unbroken, evil karma is endless, and suffering does not cease. One should know that all of this is the fault of afflictions.

Therefore, your disciple today, with this superior wholesome mind, takes refuge in the Buddha, and likewise in all Three Jewels of the ten directions throughout the entirety of space.

Your disciple, from beginningless time up to today, whether receiving retribution in the human or heavenly realms, having this consciousness constantly harboring delusion, with afflictions filling the breast: through the three poisons one created all transgressions; through the three outflows one created all transgressions; through the three sensations one created all transgressions; through the three sufferings one created all transgressions; through the three provisional existences one created all transgressions; through craving for the three realms of existence one created all transgressions. Such transgressions, immeasurable and boundless, disturb all beings of the six destinies and four modes of birth. Today, with shame and remorse, I confess all of them.

Furthermore, your disciple, from beginningless time up to today: through the four abodes of consciousness one created all transgressions; through the four outflows one created all transgressions; through the four graspings one created all transgressions; through the four attachments one created all transgressions; through the four conditions one created all transgressions; through the four great elements one created all transgressions; through the four bonds one created all transgressions; through the four foods one created all transgressions; through the four modes of birth: all are confessed.

Furthermore, your disciple, from beginningless time up to today: through the five abodes of affliction one created all transgressions; through the five aspects of sensation one created all transgressions; through the five hindrances one created all transgressions; through the five kinds of miserliness one created all transgressions; through the five views one created all transgressions; through the five minds one created all transgressions.
