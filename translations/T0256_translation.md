## Preface to the Sanskrit Heart Sūtra of Prajñāpāramitā

The Sanskrit text of the Prajñāpāramitā Heart Sūtra is the translation of the Great Tang Tripiṭaka Master. When the Tripiṭaka Master set out on his journey to India, his route passed through Yizhou, where he lodged in the Dharma hall of the Konghui Temple. There he encountered a monk who was ill. He inquired about his circumstances and explained where he was going. The monk then sighed with admiration for the Dharma Master, saying: "To forget your own body for the sake of the Dharma is truly rare! Yet the five regions of India are distant, more than a hundred thousand li. The road crosses the Flowing Sands; the waves of the Weak Waters are deep. Where the barbarian winds rise, the frontier grasses stir and sadden the traveler. When the mountain spirits cry, the wilderness troops scatter like falling leaves. In the morning one walks upon snowy peaks; at evening one lodges on frozen cliffs. Monkeys hang from the trees, and the terrain is haunted by demons. Ranges upon ranges rise over the Onion Mountains, winding like white clouds laden with snow. Forests crowd upon Vulture Peak, towering as green pinnacles reaching to heaven. The road is full of hardship; how will you manage?"

"I have the essential Dharma-gate of the heart of all Buddhas of the three periods of time. If the Master receives and upholds it, it will protect you in your going and returning." He then orally transmitted it to the Dharma Master. By dawn, the monk had vanished.

The Tripiṭaka Master packed his bags and gradually left the borders of Tang. Whenever he encountered danger on the road, or when at times there was food to be had, he would remember and recite it forty-nine times. When he lost his way, a person would appear to guide him. When he craved food, delicacies would suddenly manifest. Whenever he prayed with sincerity, he always received auspicious protection. When he arrived at Nālandā Monastery in the country of Magadha in Central India, while circumambulating the sūtra repository, he suddenly saw the monk from before. They spoke to each other, and the monk said: "You have endured steep and perilous terrain, and I am glad you have reached this place. It was through the Heart Dharma-gate of All Buddhas of the Three Periods that I transmitted to you in the land of China that you were protected throughout your journey. Obtain the sūtras and return early, that your heart's wish may be fulfilled. I am the Bodhisattva Avalokiteśvara." Having spoken, he rose into the sky and vanished. Since such an extraordinary sign was manifested, this sūtra is the ultimate proof. Truly, prajñā is the sacred pivot. If one practices as it teaches, one shall surely transcend to the realm of awakening. To fathom the Tathāgata's intent, one passes through three incalculable kalpas. To recite the Tathāgata's sūtra can dissolve the three obstructions. If anyone receives and upholds it with sincere devotion, let them embody its principle and be diligent in it.

## Universal Praise of the Three Jewels by the Lotus Division

*Namo buddhāya guruve. Namo dharmāya dhaimine. Namo saṅghāya mahāte. Te vibhya viśāca kamarūkha kamara rucana kamara laśana kamara hastaka kamara bhāmuni kamara kamara saṃbhavāsa karamara kṣalana. Namo caitute.*
