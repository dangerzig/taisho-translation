# 十不善業道經
## Sūtra of the Ten Unwholesome Paths of Action

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0727

Compiled by Bodhisattva Aśvaghoṣa. Song dynasty, translated by Sūryakīrti (日稱) and others

---

These ten unwholesome paths of action are sinful in their essential nature. Those who joyfully seek the Buddha's path should depart far from these faults. They should be known as follows.

What are the ten? They are three kinds of bodily karma, four kinds of verbal karma, and three kinds of mental karma. The meaning of these will now be explained. The three bodily kinds are: killing, taking what is not given, and sexual misconduct. The four verbal kinds are: false speech, frivolous speech, divisive speech, and harsh speech. The three mental kinds are: covetousness, anger, and wrong views.

What is killing? When one first sees a sentient being, then ascertains their identity, decides with certainty to kill, sets the body in motion to carry out the act, and severs the life faculty: when these five conditions are sequentially complete, the sin of killing is accomplished and will certainly result in that retribution.

What is taking what is not given? When one first spies another's property, then deliberates upon it, decides with certainty to take it, sets the body in motion to carry out the act, and steals the property: when five conditions are complete, the sin of taking what is not given is accomplished.

What is sexual misconduct? Within this sin there are four categories: improper place, improper time, improper manner, and improper partner. Improper place means: near images of the Buddhas, bodhisattvas, and sūtras, near the residence of one's preceptor, ācārya, or parents, or in close proximity to any of these; all are improper. Improper time means: during the daytime, during menstruation, during pregnancy, just after giving birth, when the other party is unwilling, or when ill and afflicted, or while observing the eight precepts of the upoṣadha; all are inappropriate. Improper manner means: through the mouth, through an unnatural orifice, with virgin boys or girls, self-gratification, and so forth; all should not be done. Improper partner means: with another's wife, with a bhikṣuṇī, with a relative, with a being of another destiny, or with a prostitute; even with one's own lawful partner, engaging in unchaste conduct is not in accord with principle. These should be known as above.

What is false speech? When one has seen an object or something another has lost, fully knows what it is, decides to take it, and when the owner inquires, one speaks falsely: when five conditions are complete, the sin of false speech is accomplished.

What is frivolous speech? When, toward another person, with a defiled mind, one embellishes their faults and speaks of them face to face.

What is divisive speech? When one takes the secrets and private matters of others and, with unreasonable words, creates discord between them.

What is harsh speech? When, regarding the harmonious relations of desire, one speaks with defiled and harsh words in a severe voice.

What is covetousness? When one gives rise to a mind of love and delight toward others' wealth and their enjoyment of it, unreasonably hoping to obtain them.

What is anger? When one gives rise to a mind of fury and resentment toward sentient beings, causing harm, injury, and beating.

What is wrong views? Holding that there is no generosity, no afterlife, no making of offerings, no Buddha, no World-Honored One, no śrāvakas, no pratyekabuddhas, no sin, no merit, no karma performed, and no retribution received.

As stated in the Saddharmasmṛtyupasthāna Sūtra and other sūtras, these ten unwholesome paths of action are the cause of the hells. One should study and practice the ten wholesome paths of action, and then one will never fall into the evil destinies.
