# Sūtra on Extending Life

## *Yánshòumìng Jīng* (延壽命經)

Taishō 2888

Translator unknown. An apocryphal text.

At that time the World-Honored One was between the twin śāla trees (*suōluó shuāngshù*, 娑羅雙樹), about to enter parinirvāṇa (*bān nièpán*, 般涅槃). The fourfold assembly of bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, and upāsikās all came to gather together for the sake of extending life. They knelt with palms joined and said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One, we the fourfold assembly have all come to gather together. We reverently request the Tathāgata not to enter nirvāṇa. We pray that the Tathāgata will compassionately accept our exhortation and remain for one kalpa. Do not enter nirvāṇa."

At that time the World-Honored One said to Extending-Life (*Yánshòu*, 延壽) Bodhisattva: "All of you, listen carefully. I shall explain for you. Since I began teaching, it has been forty-nine years. I have taught and converted sentient beings, from flying birds above to ants below, all creatures that stir and possess sentience, with form and without form, four-footed, two-footed, many-footed, and footless, born from the womb, from eggs, from moisture, and by transformation. All such sentient beings I have caused to encounter good spiritual friends and ascend to right awakening.

"Now I am about to enter nirvāṇa, originally at the request of Pāpīyas (*Bōxún*, 波旬, Māra). Pāpīyas said to me: 'Gautama (*Qútán*, 瞿曇), all sentient beings in the three realms are subject to birth and death. Gautama, if today you remain for one kalpa, you would violate my original vow.' At that time I accepted Pāpīyas's request to enter nirvāṇa. If I were to go against Pāpīyas, how would this befit the buddhas?"

Extending-Life Bodhisattva said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One, Pāpīyas produces external life while the buddhas enter nirvāṇa. He schemes to become lord of the three realms. All sentient beings will be caught in Māra's net without rest. From birth and death to birth and death, from affliction to affliction, they will forever serve as Māra's retinue, never encountering the true dharma. After death they will enter the hells, and for a thousand kalpas, ten thousand kalpas, there will be no light. Sword-wheels, copper snakes, iron dogs, ox-headed hell-guards with hands like sharp blades, keen as frost and snow, from morning to evening seize and drag them. The terror is without limit. There is no place to hide. They do not hear the names of father, mother, or the Three Treasures. In a single day they die and are reborn a thousand, ten thousand times.

"Emerging from the hells, they receive all manner of evil bodies in rebirth. Some are born as camels. Some are born among donkeys, forever bearing heavy burdens, always afflicted by hunger and thirst. If born in the human realm, they are poor and lowly, used as servants by others. Their clothing does not cover their bodies, their food does not fill their mouths. Some are wealthy and distinguished but have no sons or daughters. Some are poor yet have many children. Some sentient beings lie bedridden with chronic illness, wasting away year after year, their flesh consumed. Evil sores, pus, and blood constantly flow over their entire bodies. People despise them. Some receive blindness, deafness, or muteness. Some live to a hundred without dying, enduring suffering. Some die young at thirty despite wealth and honor. Thus they are beset by calamities and snared in Māra's net.

"We pray the Tathāgata will remain for one kalpa. Do not accept Pāpīyas's request."

The Buddha said to Extending-Life Bodhisattva: "Good son, after my passing, if there are sentient beings who receive such various evil retributions, they need only produce one hundred copies of the Sūtra on Extending Life, or even one or two copies. It is like one person who has strength but cannot match the strength of ten, and the strength of ten cannot match one hundred. Producing the Sūtra on Extending Life is likewise. Simply produce this sūtra and distribute it to others to receive, uphold, read, and recite. I shall protect them. It is like a compassionate mother who has borne a child: if the child falls ill, the compassionate mother also falls ill; if the child recovers, the compassionate mother also recovers.

"Good son, all sentient beings are my children. Those who make wholesome vows: I shall extend my golden arm and use my hand to stroke their heads. Whatever their original vows, none shall fail to bear fruit."

At that time Extending-Life Bodhisattva praised in verse:

> The World-Honored One, complete with marks, of true golden color,
> Adorned with eighty excellent characteristics,
> For forty-nine years our great compassionate father.
> Pāpīyas, without cause, requested nirvāṇa.
>
> Quiescent extinction, like space, cannot be seen.
> It is certain that in the three realms there will be no more arising.
> When the buddha-dharma is extinguished, disasters will arise;
> The Māra King's poisonous mind will compete to advance.
>
> All the śrāvakas and bodhisattvas,
> All the people, the fourfold assembly:
> The river of dharma will dry up, the dharma-banner will break,
> The dharma-mountain will collapse, and the dharma-ship will sink.
>
> The kings of the eight countries will all beat their chests.
> Blood-sweat and tears will flow like violent rain.
> How unexpected that the Great Teacher enters nirvāṇa!
> We ordinary beings, how can we endure?
> Śrāvakas and pratyekabuddhas all exclaim "How excellent!"
> All sentient beings have lost their refuge.

At that time in the assembly there was a great bodhisattva named Universal Purity (*Pǔjìng*, 普淨) who asked Extending-Life Bodhisattva: "Now that the Tathāgata enters nirvāṇa today, to whom does he entrust the sentient beings of the evil destinies, so that their lives may be lengthened? Why does he not remain for even one kalpa, being foremost in the three realms as he is?"

Then Extending-Life said: "Universal Purity Bodhisattva, after the Buddha's passing, have one hundred copies of the Sūtra on Extending Life written and distributed to sentient beings, so that they may together read and recite it. Sentient beings of short life will thereby attain long life. The Tathāgata, with great compassion, will extend his golden arm to stroke their heads and give them a prediction. The Tathāgata himself will cover and protect them."

At that time Universal Purity praised: "Excellent, excellent! Receiving such words, they are surely not false or deceptive." He faithfully received them and put them into practice.
