# 佛說聖多羅菩薩經
## Sūtra of Holy Tārā Bodhisattva Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1104

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmabhadra (法賢).

---

Thus have I heard. At one time the Buddha was on Gandhamādana Mountain, in the palace of Pañcaśikha, King of the Gandharvas, where all manner of drums, string instruments, and songs produced a subtle and wondrous sound.

At that time the World-Honored One was in the great hall together with an assembly of bodhisattva mahāsattvas. Furthermore, many great saintly assemblies gathered, namely the assemblies of learners and non-learners among the śrāvakas, and immeasurable, boundless devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, humans, and non-humans. Furthermore, there were immeasurable, boundless accomplished vidyā-holders and vajra-holders, Vajrapāṇi, and wrathful kings and vidyā-kings numbering hundreds of thousands. Furthermore, there were hosts of various vidyā-holding Ratnakūṭa Buddha-Crown Cakravartin Kings, hosts of the lunar mansions, and mother-spirits and their chief, as well as Śakra, Brahmā, Maheśvara, Nārāyaṇa, Kāmeśvara, Mahākāla, Kumāra, Yama, Varuṇa, Vāyu, the four heavenly kings, and the five-power ṛṣis, all gathered at the assembly to make vast offerings, surrounding and attending, with palms joined in reverence.

At that time the World-Honored One sat in the assembly like Mount Sumeru towering above the great ocean. At that time Pañcaśikha, King of the Gandharvas, together with seventy-two hundred thousand gandharva maidens, playing all manner of music, came to where the Buddha was. Arriving, he bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, circumambulated the Buddha three times, withdrew to stand to one side, and with palms joined toward the Buddha spoke thus: "World-Honored One, I wish to hear a matter of benefit. I beseech the World-Honored One to proclaim it for me. The Holy Tārā Bodhisattva is the compassionate heart of all the buddha tathāgatas, the great vidyā-king who is supreme in vanquishing Māra, the great vidyā-holder, the great cakravartin, who manifests the profound and possesses great power. I beseech the World-Honored One, out of compassion for me and for devas, humans, asuras, and all others, that we may always gain benefit and obtain peace and happiness, to proclaim the One Hundred and Eight Names of Holy Tārā Bodhisattva."

At that time the World-Honored One received the request of Pañcaśikha, King of the Gandharvas, and praised him: "Excellent, excellent, King of the Gandharvas! You are able to ask the Tathāgata about such a matter, so that devas, humans, and all in the assembly may gain great benefit and peace and happiness. You should listen attentively and reflect well upon it. I shall now proclaim it for you in due order." The King of the Gandharvas, hearing the Buddha's words, received the teaching and listened.

At that time the World-Honored One, for the King of the Gandharvas and the entire assembly, first spoke the dhāraṇī of Holy Tārā Bodhisattva:

*Namo samanta-darśi-bhyaḥ sarva-tathāgatebhyo 'rhadbhyaḥ samyak-saṃbuddhebhyo namo bhagavatyai āryā-tārāyai oṃ sarva-tathāgata tathāta-bhūta kuṭi-pariśuddhi buddhi sarva-tathāgata sarva-dharmatā gagana amala-viśuddha dharmati mahā-naya parivāra svāhā*

At that time the World-Honored One, having spoken this dhāraṇī, further proclaimed the One Hundred and Eight Names of Tārā Bodhisattva for the King of the Gandharvas and the entire assembly, and spoke in verse:

> "This Tārā Bodhisattva
> Was originally born from the syllable A.
> She may manifest all manner of active marks,
> Yet is neither born nor destroyed.

> Her nature is like space,
> For she is born from the nature of space.
> Manifesting her original form according to need,
> One form and many, without obstruction.

> Her colored forms appear without limit,
> Supremely quiescent, her essence purely one.
> She constantly manifests illusory transformation-forms,
> Her secret words are true words.

> She encompasses the great true principle,
> True practice is constant practice.
> She bestows fearlessness,
> Ever quiescent, always dispelling anger.

> Free from all terrors and sufferings,
> She skillfully breaks the cage of afflictions,
> Can untie the bonds of the three existences,
> And the sea of suffering is forever left behind.

> In accomplishing dharmas she is able to act.
> Self and others both attain accomplishment.
> She bestows only the most supreme dharma,
> The Sovereign Queen of Total Retention.

> She is also born from total retention,
> Bestowing its great meaning.
> In correspondence she is greatly sovereign;
> Mind and intention are fully in correspondence.

> Not grasping the mark of correspondence,
> Yet the mark of correspondence is never apart.
> Her settled intention is always unmoving;
> Wherever there is motion, all is quiescent.

> She always dwells upon a lotus seat
> And is also born from the lotus.
> Her eyes are pure as blue lotuses;
> She is always the eye for sentient beings.

> Pure, supremely wondrous,
> With compassionate eyes she gazes upon all beings.
> The Buddha's wisdom-eye is without outflow,
> Endowed with pure, singular great compassion.

> She always gives rise to great compassion,
> Always practices the conduct of great compassion,
> Is also born from great compassion,
> And always possesses a heart of great compassion.

> Those hard to subdue she regards with tender pity,
> Subduing all universally with compassion.
> All those who suffer,
> She frees from suffering and brings them joy.

> She bestows the dharma-gift of the nectar-taste,
> Relieving suffering and granting peace.
> The sea of suffering becomes cool in mind.
> She is called the Sovereign Mother.

> Illuminating all the world,
> Her wondrous countenance blazes with splendid light.
> Crowned with a diadem of many precious jewels,
> Her pure radiance perfectly complete.

> Adorned with maṇi pearls,
> Her hair-topknot is dark blue.
> Her spiral curls turn to the right,
> Adorning a body of the unconditioned.

> She makes the unwholesome wholesome,
> And all wishes are perfectly fulfilled.
> Her body, beyond comparison, is adorned;
> Good in intent and good in departure.

> Quiescent, always at ease,
> With great power and wish-fulfilling spiritual penetration.
> The six recollections with the six penetrations complete,
> She attains all the pāramitās.

> She skillfully teaches the dharma of the Four Noble Truths,
> Dispelling delusion with great eloquence.
> In the sea of suffering's illusions,
> She manifests the active forms of illusion.

> Wielding a sword to shatter afflictions,
> With bow, arrows, and trident,
> Pāśa, staff, and spear,
> Ḍamaru, śakti,
> Musala, and vajra: all manner of weapons.

> Gazing upon the arrows she holds,
> She universally bestows fearlessness.
> Hurling the vajra from her hand,
> She manifests unobstructed spiritual penetration.

> She can subdue enemy armies,
> And upon her banner manifests the pennant-mark.
> Possessed of great wisdom-power,
> She skillfully shatters valiant armies.

> All the darkness of afflictions is consumed,
> Māra-enemies and thieves are all destroyed,
> Like the sun consuming the gloom.
> She is the supreme and most victorious.

> She skillfully crosses the ocean of saṃsāra.
> Supremely auspicious,
> She abides in the true, dwelling only in reality.
> Her fame and renown spread everywhere.

> She always bestows the wish for love and respect.
> Victorious over self and victorious over others,
> Ever practicing the giving of joy,
> Subtle, wondrous, supremely quiescent.
> She is none other than the wisdom of the tathāgata,
> Universally revered as the wish-fulfilling treasure.

> With the precept-mark fully upheld,
> She walks the true path through precepts.
> Her Brahma-voice is most subtle and wondrous,
> The supreme voice of the three ages.
> A single subtle resonance
> Is like the great treasury of space.

> She skillfully gives birth to all the world,
> A disputeless refuge.
> The three vehicles are a refuge,
> Practicing the three vehicles according to their ways.

> She liberates through the three vehicles' teaching,
> And all the buddhas rely on the three vehicles.
> Five faces and also five eyes,
> Born from the nature of the five wisdoms.
> True awakening accomplishes bodhi,
> Which is the great true path.

> All manner of various marks,
> A supremely wondrous pure body,
> All manifest as appropriate,
> Illuminating the world like celestial luminaries.

> She herself reaches the other shore
> And also skillfully ferries others across.
> Transforming as she wishes into hundreds of thousands,
> She skillfully gathers all wholesome meaning.

> With hundreds of thousands of merits complete,
> Purely and singly she transforms the world.
> She skillfully relinquishes all possessions,
> Manifesting all manner of illusory forms.

> Sovereign of the world,
> Auspicious, honored among all devas,
> She holds the wondrous blue lotus,
> With complete precepts, bestowing all wishes.

> Joyful in virtue, ever quiescent,
> Great quiescence, the flame of wisdom-light.
> All evil obstructions are forever removed.
> She is the sovereign over all māras.

> She grants deliverance and gives peace,
> With an unequaled, even mind.
> Quarrels and conflicts are forever ended.
> She skillfully removes the fear of death.

> Mother and sovereign of the bhūtas,
> She manifests as a yakṣa king,
> Appears in the form of a great nāga,
> With a hundred heads and a thousand eyes.

> A hundred tongues and a great fearsome visage,
> Yet a heart of truth and fearlessness.
> Of supreme seed, numbering hundreds of thousands,
> She, the earth-deva, skillfully sustains the world.

> She always delights in fearless speech.
> Epidemics and poisons are forever removed.
> With supremely wondrous great radiance,
> Immaculate and ever pure.

> Mother of all the world,
> She upholds the dharma to benefit the world.
> The supreme consecrated king,
> All tasks already accomplished.

> Endowed with the ten pāramitās,
> She always abides in the ten bhūmis.
> Revealing the ten dharma-vehicles,
> The ten great true principles.
> Abiding in the ten vajra-phrases,
> Her banner accomplishes the ten dharmas.
> The ten wisdoms dispel darkness.
> She, the eye of sovereign self-mastery in the world,
> Possesses great and wondrous colored forms,
> Equal to space, without limit.

> Or she appears as a five-power ṛṣi,
> With complete precepts and a sovereign form,
> Free from desire, her body worthy of offerings,
> The quiescent path, ever at peace.
> Praised by all the truly awakened,
> She is Tārā Bodhisattva."

When the World-Honored One had spoken this verse, he told the King of the Gandharvas and the entire assembly: "These are the One Hundred and Eight Names of Tārā Bodhisattva. I have now proclaimed them for you. You should all wholeheartedly receive, uphold, and practice these One Hundred and Eight Names and the dhāraṇī of Tārā Bodhisattva. The buddhas of the past and future have spoken and shall speak them, out of compassion for the benefit of all sentient beings.

"If any good man or good woman generates a sincere mind, at dawn, whether for self or for others, recites these One Hundred and Eight Names of Tārā Bodhisattva and the dhāraṇī, even silently; or paints the bodhisattva's image upon cloth as a banner or upon a clean wall, using the most superb colors according to the method; or carves the bodhisattva's form from sandalwood; preparing any of these with a pure mind as one wishes, and then before the bodhisattva's image sets out various offerings, three times each day joining palms and bowing, contemplating with single-minded devotion according to the method, and reciting these One Hundred and Eight Names and the dhāraṇī: this Tārā Bodhisattva possesses great power, is supremely auspicious, can extinguish all transgressions, and skillfully destroys the armies of Māra. In the beginning, middle, and end she is wholesome, and she can bestow the dharma of accomplishment.

"Furthermore, if any person generates a sincere mind and recites one time, seven times, or up to twenty-seven times, without missing any syllable during the recitation, that person shall obtain all wealth, auspiciousness, and accomplishment according to their wishes, without any obstruction forever. All the noble and worthy ones shall conceal their true forms and always act as protectors. Furthermore, the devas, nāgas, yakṣas, Śakra, Brahmā, the world-protectors, Nārāyaṇa, Maheśvara, and the mother-spirit chief, Mahākāla, Vināyaka, Kāmeśvara, and all others, down to all the buddhas and bodhisattvas, pratyekabuddhas, śrāvakas, all wrathful kings, and great vidyā-kings of majestic virtue, shall all say: 'Excellent, excellent, good man! You possess the conduct of the buddhas and bodhisattvas. You shall be reborn in the Sukhāvatī buddha-field.'"

At that time Pañcaśikha, King of the Gandharvas, was overjoyed and elated, and his mind was filled with faith and reverence. He rose from his seat and removed from his own body his priceless necklaces, gold, silver, pearls, maṇi gems, and all manner of precious body adornments, and offered them to the Buddha. With palms joined in reverence, gazing up at the World-Honored One, he spoke this praise:

> "Excellent, World-Honored One! How extraordinary, Sugata!
> You always with a compassionate heart
> Rescue and protect all beings.
> For me and for the entire assembly
> You have spoken this most supreme
> True and real meaning."

When the World-Honored One had finished speaking this sūtra, the bodhisattva mahāsattvas, the śrāvakas, the vidyā-holders, the great wrathful lords, all the vidyā-kings, the assemblies of devas and humans, and Pañcaśikha and the gandharvas, having heard what the Buddha taught, all greatly rejoiced, accepted it with faith, and put it into practice.
