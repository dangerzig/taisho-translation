# T1491 菩薩藏經

# Bodhisattva-piṭaka Sūtra

*Note: This sūtra was translated by Saṅghabhadra (Sēngqiépóluó 僧伽婆羅) during the Liáng dynasty. It presents a comprehensive liturgy for confession (chànhuǐ 懺悔), rejoicing (suíxǐ 隨喜), exhortation (quànqǐng 勸請), and merit-transference (huíxiàng 迴向), spoken by the Buddha at Jetavana in response to Śāriputra's question about how to confess sins and swiftly attain bodhi. The sūtra names the ten directional buddhas, provides the full confession formula covering all transgressions from beginningless time, and includes the narrative of the celestial woman Khegardhā (Jiégātuó 竭伽陀) who, through the merit of upholding this sūtra, was reborn as Śākyamuni. The text gives three alternative titles: "Elimination of Karmic Obstructions" (Miè yèzhàng 滅業障), "Bodhisattva-piṭaka" (Púsà zàng 菩薩藏), and "Severing All Doubts" (Duàn yīqiè yí 斷一切疑).*

---

Thus have I heard. At one time the Buddha was dwelling in the Jetavana (Qíshù jǐgūdú yuán 祇樹給孤獨園) at Śrāvastī, together with a great assembly of twelve hundred and fifty monks and seventy-two thousand bodhisattvas.

At that time the Elder Śāriputra (Shèlìfú 舍利弗), by the Buddha's spiritual power, rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, placed his right knee on the ground, joined his palms, and bowed to the Buddha, saying: "World-Honored One! How should good men and good women confess and eliminate sins and swiftly attain samyak-saṃbodhi?"

## The Confession Liturgy

The Buddha told Śāriputra: "Those who wish to study samyak-saṃbodhi, whether they are persons of the śrāvaka vehicle, the pratyekabuddha vehicle, the Mahāyāna, or other sentient beings, should recite the names of the ten buddhas of the ten directions and ten worlds, light ten thousand lamps of ghee or oil, along with the same number of incenses and ground incenses, and various kinds of flowers, fruits, and leaves, making great offerings and performing great generosity. They should fill ten piṅgala vessels and ten jars with water, bathe to purity, perfume the body with incense, don new clean garments, wash the hands and feet again, and hold ten lotus flowers in each hand. They should eat vegetarian food and ensure that all attendants and servants are clean. They should set up a buddha seat in each of the ten directions, and the person who confesses should arrange a seat as convenient in each direction. At their seat they should bow to the buddhas of the ten directions, and with their own mouths they should openly confess all the karmic actions they have done from the past, and also repent of all the evils committed since beginningless time through birth and death, reforming the past and cultivating the future, vowing never to commit them again."

The Buddha said: "Śāriputra! In the east is the world called Aśoka, where there is a buddha named Candrottama-śrī ('Moon-Victorious Auspicious'). In the south is the Nanda world, where there is a buddha named Candana-śrī ('Sandalwood Auspicious'). In the west is the Bhadra world, where there is a buddha named Anantaprabha ('Boundless Light'). In the north is the Raṃyacakṣus world, where there is a buddha named Dhvaja-śrī ('Banner Auspicious'). In the southeast is the Candraprabha world, where there is a buddha named Aśoka-śrī ('Griefless Auspicious'). In the southwest is the Dhvajavat world, where there is a buddha named Ratnakṣetra ('Jeweled Land'). In the northwest is the Ghoṣavat world, where there is a buddha named Puṣpaguṇa ('Flower Virtue'). In the northeast is the Kṣema world, where there is a buddha named Trivikrama ('Three Heroic Strides'). In the zenith is the Candravat world, where there is a buddha named Mahāpuṇya-śrī ('Great Merit Auspicious'). In the nadir is the Mahānāman world, where there is a buddha named Prabhāsa-śrī ('Radiant Auspicious').

"One should practice the path, bowing in worship, for six periods day and night, baring the right shoulder, placing the right knee on the ground, joining the palms toward the Buddha, and speaking these words:

"'I bow to all buddhas, the tathāgatas, those presently dwelling in the ten directions who have attained anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi, who presently turn the Dharma wheel, presently radiate the Dharma wheel, presently take up the Dharma wheel, presently rain the Dharma rain, presently beat the Dharma drum, presently blow the Dharma conch, presently raise the Dharma banner, presently light the Dharma torch, and presently give the Dharma as a gift to satisfy all beings, teaching according to the delights of all sentient beings, able to bring great benefit, to give peace and stability to sentient beings, to have compassion for the world, and to benefit gods and humans with meaning. I now bow my head to those tathāgatas. Those tathāgatas, those honored buddhas, should be worshiped. The buddhas are great wisdom; they are the eyes of the world; they can serve as witnesses for the world, as lords overseeing the world, presently knowing and presently seeing. With body, speech, and mind, I reverently bow to those buddhas.

## The Confession Formula

"'From beginningless births and deaths, whatever evil karma I have created that obstructs all sentient beings, whether arising from greed, from anger, or from delusion, not recognizing the Buddha, the Dharma, or the Saṅgha, not recognizing wholesome or unwholesome dharmas, whether with evil body, speech, or mind I have shed a buddha's blood, slandered the true Dharma, broken the harmony of the Saṅgha, killed a true arhat, killed a father or mother, or committed any of the ten unwholesome paths, whether already done, now being done, or to be done, whether seeing others do them and praising or rejoicing in them, or through the three of body, four of speech, and three of mind I have created evils, with harsh speech, curses, and slanders, or cheated others with false weights and measures, or in the six destinies brought distress to father and mother, or taken the property of stūpas and monasteries, or used the property of the Saṅgha, or used the property of the Saṅgha of the four directions, or broken the precepts established by the Buddha, or not followed the instructions of the upādhyāya and ācārya, or been angry and abusive, or slandered the śrāvaka, pratyekabuddha, and Mahāyāna, or out of stinginess and jealousy created various evil karma, or abused a tathāgata, or declared non-Dharma to be Dharma, or declared Dharma to be non-Dharma: all such evils I now confess and repent openly before the buddhas of the ten directions.

"'Those tathāgatas presently see, presently know, and presently bear witness. Before the buddhas I confess with a single mind, not daring to conceal. Having confessed openly, I vow henceforth never to do so again. The retribution of these sins should be birth in the hells, the realm of hungry ghosts, the realm of animals, or the realm of asuras, or enduring the eight difficulties. May all these sins be eliminated in the present and not arise in the future!

"'Today, in the presence of the buddhas, I confess openly, not daring to conceal. After this confession, I vow never to do so again. As the bodhisattvas of the past confessed for the sake of cultivating bodhi, and as they confessed, I likewise confess my karmic obstructions. After confessing openly, I vow never to do so again. As the bodhisattva-mahāsattvas of the future will confess, I likewise confess openly and vow never to do so again. As the bodhisattva-mahāsattvas of the present in the ten directions now confess for the sake of cultivating bodhi, I likewise confess openly and vow never to do so again. As the bodhisattva-mahāsattvas of the three times, past, future, and present, have confessed, will confess, and now confess for the sake of cultivating bodhi, I likewise confess and vow never to do so again.'

"Śāriputra! Good men and good women should confess in this way.

"Therefore, Śāriputra! If good men and good women wish to attain purity in all dharmas, free of all obstructions, they should confess their evil karmic obstructions in this way. Having confessed openly, they should vow never to do so again. If they wish to be born in a noble kṣatriya family of wealth and abundance, endowed with various provisions and fine appearance, and wish to attain the Mahāyāna, they should confess in this way. If they wish to attain the Heaven of the Four Kings, they should confess in this way." The same is said for each of the heavens up through the summit of the form realm and the four formless attainments, and for those who wish to attain the fruits of stream-entry, once-return, non-return, and arhatship, for those who wish the three insights, six superknowledges, and sovereign mastery of the śrāvaka, and for those who wish pratyekabuddha bodhi, and for those who wish all-knowledge, pure knowledge, inconceivable knowledge, peerless knowledge, and omniscience.

"Śāriputra! Why is this? All dharmas arise from causes and conditions. As the Tathāgata has taught, some dharmas arise from conditions and some dharmas cease from conditions. Through the successive turning of causes and conditions, regarding those past dharmas, they have ceased and turned; those karmic actions present no obstruction. Those dharmas not yet arisen also present no obstruction. Śāriputra! Why? All dharmas, as the Tathāgata has taught, are empty and quiescent, without sentient beings, without lifespan, without a person; they neither arise nor cease. Śāriputra! All dharmas are created by one's own body, and that 'own body' too is existent. Śāriputra! If good men and good women wish to enter this Dharma wisdom, namely, that there are no truly real sentient beings, this is called the elimination of all karmic obstructions." Thus ends the section on confession.

## Rejoicing

At that time Śāriputra addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! How should good men and good women who wish to attain anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi, the śrāvaka vehicle, the pratyekabuddha vehicle, the Mahāyāna, or others who cultivate merit, generate the wholesome root of rejoicing?"

The Buddha told Śāriputra: "If good men and good women wish to rejoice, they should say in the six periods day and night, baring the right shoulder, right knee on the ground, with joined palms in reverence:

"'Whatever meritorious deeds sentient beings throughout the ten directions have done, whether generosity, keeping precepts, or cultivation, I rejoice in all of them with the foremost rejoicing, the supreme rejoicing, the highest rejoicing, the unsurpassed rejoicing, the peerless rejoicing, the incomparably peerless rejoicing.'

"The same formula is repeated for merit that sentient beings will do in the future, and for merit that sentient beings are doing in the present. The same is said for the merit of bodhisattvas at each stage: first generating the bodhi-mind, cultivating for a hundred kalpas, attaining the forbearance of the non-arising of dharmas, attaining the stage of non-retrogression, progressing from the first through the tenth bhūmi, and practicing the six pāramitās in past, future, and present.

"The same is said for the merit accomplished by the tathāgatas of the past, future, and present in attaining anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi, turning the Dharma wheel, benefiting sentient beings, establishing peace, having compassion for the world, and benefiting gods and humans, together with all the merit accomplished by their śrāvakas, pratyekabuddhas, and bodhisattvas.

"Śāriputra! This is called the aggregate of merit from rejoicing. By this aggregate of merit from rejoicing, if good men and good women practice this merit, this merit-fruit is incalculable and immeasurable. Śāriputra! If all the sentient beings in a great trichiliocosm, or in as many worlds as sands of the Ganges, all became arhats free of defilements, and good men and good women were to supply them with the four requisites for their entire lifetimes, yet the merit of rejoicing by good men and good women as described above surpasses that merit immeasurably and boundlessly. Therefore, Śāriputra! If good men and good women wish to attain anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi, they should rejoice. If a woman wishes to become a man, she should rejoice."

## Exhortation

Śāriputra addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! You have already explained rejoicing as a light for present and future bodhisattvas. How should one exhort and entreat?"

The Buddha told Śāriputra: "Good men and good women who wish to attain anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi, the śrāvaka vehicle, the pratyekabuddha vehicle, the Mahāyāna, or other sentient beings, should say in the six periods day and night, baring the right shoulder, right knee on the ground, with joined palms in reverence:

"'I bow to all buddhas, the world-honored ones, those presently dwelling in the ten directions who have attained anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi and presently turn the Dharma wheel. Having bowed to those buddhas, I now exhort and entreat them to turn the Dharma wheel. May the buddhas, the world-honored ones, turn the Dharma wheel! May they light the Dharma lamp! May they open the Dharma eye! May they light the Dharma torch! May they raise the Dharma cloud! May they blow the Dharma conch! May they beat the Dharma drum! May they raise the Dharma banner! For the benefit of sentient beings, for the peace of sentient beings, for compassion toward the world, and for the benefit with meaning of all gods and humans!'

"And further: 'I bow to all buddhas, the world-honored ones. If any buddha in the ten directions is about to enter nirvāṇa, I exhort and entreat that buddha to remain long in the world, for the benefit of sentient beings, for the peace of sentient beings, for compassion toward the world, and for the benefit with meaning of all gods and humans. I perform this exhortation for the sake of anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi.'

"Śāriputra! This is called the aggregate of exhortation. The merit that good men and good women obtain through this aggregate of exhortation is incalculable. Śāriputra! Even if good men and good women were to fill a great trichiliocosm with the seven treasures and offer them to the Tathāgata, the merit of exhortation as described above surpasses that merit immeasurably and boundlessly. Even if worlds as numerous as the sands of the Ganges were filled with the seven treasures and offered to all buddhas, the merit of this exhortation still surpasses it immeasurably. By thus exhorting anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi, as I exhort: this, Śāriputra, is the aggregate of exhortation. The merit of good men and good women who now perform this exhortation is inconceivable. Why? Śāriputra! In my former practice of the bodhi path, I exhorted the buddhas in this way to turn the Dharma wheel, and by this merit I attained anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi. Indra, the devas, the lord of the sahā world, and Brahmā also exhorted me to turn the Dharma wheel, for the great benefit and peace of the world, for the benefit with meaning of all gods and humans. Śāriputra! Because I formerly exhorted the tathāgatas to let the Dharma long endure, by the root of this merit I attained the ten powers, the four fearlessnesses, the eighteen unique dharmas, the four unobstructed knowledges, great kindness and great compassion. Having entered nirvāṇa, my Dharma shall long endure."

## Merit-Transference

Śāriputra addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! How should good men and good women who wish to attain anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi, the śrāvaka vehicle, the pratyekabuddha vehicle, the Mahāyāna, or other sentient beings, practice the transference of wholesome roots toward sarvajñā?"

The Buddha told Śāriputra: "They should say in the six periods day and night, baring the right shoulder, right knee on the ground, with joined palms in reverence:

"'Whatever meritorious wholesome roots I have performed since beginningless births and deaths, whether toward the Buddha, the Dharma, or the Saṅgha, or even the gift of a single morsel of food to an animal, or confession, exhortation, or rejoicing, or taking refuge in the Three Jewels and receiving the precepts: I combine all of it and transfer it to all sentient beings. As all buddhas and world-honored ones, with present wisdom and unattached wisdom, transfer merit to all sentient beings, I likewise transfer it to all sentient beings. Like one who grasps a wish-fulfilling jewel and gives to all, like clouds raining down moisture: inexhaustible and undiminished, for the wealth of sentient beings without diminishing, for the merit without diminishing, for the Dharma without diminishing, for the wisdom without diminishing, for the eloquence without diminishing, for anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi, and for the attainment of sarvajñā. By this merit I give to sentient beings, combining all of it and transferring it toward anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi. By this wholesome root may all sentient beings also attain anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi and sarvajñā.

"'As the bodhisattvas of the past cultivated wholesome roots and transferred them toward sarvajñā, I likewise transfer toward sarvajñā. By this wholesome root, may I attain anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi and sarvajñā. As the bodhisattvas of the future will cultivate and transfer wholesome roots toward sarvajñā, I likewise transfer toward sarvajñā. As the bodhisattvas of the present cultivate wholesome roots toward sarvajñā, I likewise transfer toward sarvajñā. By this wholesome root, may all sentient beings attain anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi and sarvajñā.

"'As Śākyamuni Buddha formerly sat beneath the bodhi tree, abiding in the inconceivable stainless samādhi, vanquishing the evil māras, knowing, seeing, and perceiving all dharmas, in the last watch of the night when the morning star appeared, with a single thought of correspondence-wisdom he practiced the path of ending suffering and realized the supreme truth: I likewise desire that all sentient beings study anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi. As the Tathāgata Anantaprabha, the Tathāgata Prabhottama, the Tathāgata Viśuddhaprabha, the Tathāgata Guṇaprabha, the Tathāgata Siṃha, the Tathāgata Śataprabha, the Tathāgata Uccatejas, the Tathāgata Jālaprabha, the Tathāgata Maṇiprabha, the Tathāgata Agni-prabha, the Tathāgata Prabhārāja, the Tathāgata Vyūha, the Tathāgata Ratnaketu, the Tathāgata Dharmaketu, the Tathāgata Kāyottama, and the other buddhas, the worthy ones, the perfectly awakened ones, attained anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi and turned the Dharma wheel, for the benefit of the many, for the peace of sentient beings, for compassion toward the world, and for the benefit with meaning of all gods and humans: I likewise desire that all sentient beings attain anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi, turn the Dharma wheel, benefit the many, bring peace to sentient beings, have compassion for the world, and benefit all gods and humans with meaning.'

"Śāriputra! This is called the aggregate of merit-transference. This aggregate of merit surpasses the previous aggregate of generosity a hundredfold, a thousandfold, ten-thousand-fold, a hundred-million-fold, and beyond calculation and analogy.

## The Merit of Upholding This Sūtra

"Śāriputra! If good men and good women receive and uphold this sūtra and broadly expound it for others, the merit they obtain is innumerable and immeasurable. Śāriputra! If someone were able to cause all the miscellaneous sentient beings in a great trichiliocosm, in a single thought-moment, to all obtain human bodies, and were then able to cause them all to attain pratyekabuddha bodhi, and were to constantly supply each pratyekabuddha with the four requisites, giving to each one seven treasures like Mount Sumeru, doing so every day until they enter nirvāṇa, and after their nirvāṇa were to erect stūpas of the seven treasures and make offerings of flowers, incense, banners, and canopies of various kinds: Śāriputra, what do you think, would the merit of such a good man or good woman be great?"

Śāriputra replied: "Very great, World-Honored One! Very great, Sugata!"

"Therefore, Śāriputra! If good men and good women receive and uphold this sūtra, the merit they obtain is even greater than that. May this merit be directed toward anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi! This merit exceeds the former merit a hundredfold, a thousandfold, a hundred-million-fold, beyond calculation and analogy. Why? Śāriputra! Because good men and good women who have faith in this sūtra and exhort the buddhas of the ten directions to turn the Dharma wheel, it is as I have taught: the gift of Dharma surpasses the gift of wealth!"

## The Story of the Celestial Woman Khegardhā

At that time the four assemblies, ten thousand people together, rose from their seats, bared their right shoulders, placed their right knees on the ground, joined their palms toward the Buddha, and spoke these words: "World-Honored One! We shall receive and uphold this sūtra and broadly explain it for others, and we shall believe in it. Why? World-Honored One! We desire to attain anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi! We shall accomplish such wholesome roots and such wholesome dharmas!"

Then Śakra, lord of the gods, scattered celestial flowers to make offerings to the World-Honored One and to this sūtra-dharma, and said: "World-Honored One! This sūtra has great merit. It increases the wholesome roots of all bodhisattvas and eliminates karmic obstructions."

The Buddha then told Śakra: "Just so, just so, Kauśika! Why? O heavenly king! I recall that in the past, incalculable kalpas ago, there was a tathāgata named Mahāprabhā-saṃcaya ('Great Accumulation of Light'), an arhat, a perfectly awakened one, who appeared in the world. O heavenly king! The Tathāgata Mahāprabhā-saṃcaya had a lifespan of six billion eight hundred million years. At his first teaching, there were a hundred thousand koṭi of disciples, all of whom were arhats with defilements exhausted. At his second teaching, there were ninety-nine thousand koṭi of disciples, also all arhats with defilements exhausted. At his third teaching, there were ninety-eight billion hundred-thousand disciples, also all arhats with defilements exhausted. O heavenly king! The Tathāgata Mahāprabhā-saṃcaya dwelt for six billion eight hundred million years for the benefit of all beings of the world: devas, Brahmā kings, śramaṇas, and brāhmaṇas. At that time, Śakra the lord of the gods and the four assemblies received and upheld this sūtra from the Tathāgata Mahāprabhā-saṃcaya, for the great benefit of all the world and for the accomplishment of anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi.

"Furthermore, there was a celestial woman named Khegardhā (Jiégātuó 竭伽陀) who received and upheld this sūtra, generated the mind of anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi, became disgusted with her female body, and obtained a male body. She was always born among humans and devas, never passing through the evil destinies, and for eighty-four thousand lifetimes she was a wheel-turning monarch. Kauśika! What do you think? That celestial woman Khegardhā of old, was she someone else? She was none other than myself. In the past, throughout a hundred thousand koṭi of worlds, I encountered innumerable buddhas, all named Tathāgata Mahāprabhā-saṃcaya, and from each of those buddhas I heard this sūtra.

"If good men and good women hear the name of this tathāgata, they will certainly attain great parinirvāṇa. If a woman hears the name of the Tathāgata Mahāprabhā-saṃcaya, she shall transform her female body, and at the end of her life, free from confusion, she shall never again receive a female body. Kauśika! This sūtra has the great merit of grace. It can embrace the wholesome roots of all bodhisattva-mahāsattvas and can eliminate all karmic obstructions."

## The Names of This Sūtra

Śakra addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! What should this sūtra be called? How should we receive and uphold it?"

The Buddha told Śakra: "Kauśika! This sūtra is called 'Elimination of Karmic Obstructions.' You should receive and uphold it. It is also called 'Bodhisattva-piṭaka.' You should receive and uphold it. It is also called 'Severing All Doubts.' Receive and uphold it thus."

When the Buddha had finished speaking this sūtra, Śakra the heavenly king, the Elder Śāriputra, the assembly of monks, all the bodhisattvas, devas, humans, asuras, and gandharvas, and all the beings of the world, having heard what the Buddha taught, joyfully received and practiced it.
