# 過去世佛分衛經
## Sūtra of a Past Buddha's Alms Round

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0180

Translated from the Chinese. Western Jin dynasty, translated by Dharmarakṣa (竺法護)

---

The Buddha said: "In a past age, a Buddha entered a city to receive alms, accompanied by his honored disciples and bodhisattvas. The disciples and bodhisattvas were all of fine appearance and handsome form, and each had attained the path according to his practice.

"There was a woman who was several months pregnant. Seeing the Buddha and the sangha and being moved to devotion, she silently made a vow: 'When the child I carry is born, may he become a śramaṇa and a disciple of the Buddha.' When the months were full, she gave birth safely. The child was exceptionally beautiful, different from other people. The mother, bound by love and affection, had no thought of letting the child become a śramaṇa. But then she became aware of something within her and thought: 'I once made a vow that if I bore a son, I would have him enter the path. The child has now been born. He is extraordinary, and my delivery was peaceful, without the usual impurities. I must not, because of attachment and affection, go against my original intention.'

"When the child was seven years old, the family was poor and in straitened circumstances. She prepared food for two and three Dharma robes, and taking a water pitcher in hand, she personally brought her son to the Buddha's presence. She prostrated at the Buddha's feet and said: 'I beg you to have compassion on my son and allow him to become a śramaṇa. May he later attain the path, with a body like the Buddha's.' The Buddha immediately consented and allowed him to become a śramaṇa. The mother took the water pitcher and washed her son's hands. At that very moment, nine nāgas emerged from the mouth of the pitcher and poured water to bathe the child's hands. When the washing was complete, the remaining water was sprinkled upon the child's head. The drops of water on his head transformed into a jeweled canopy and a curtain interlaced with pearls. Within it was a lion throne, upon which sat a Buddha. The Buddha smiled, and five-colored light issued from his mouth, illuminating ten billion buddha-lands. The light then circled the Buddha's body and entered through the top of the child's head. The mother offered the food to the Buddha and also fed her son. The child then generated the aspiration for the supreme, impartial path. At that moment, the ten billion buddha-lands shook in six ways, and all the Buddhas of those lands spontaneously appeared. The Buddha was satisfied by the mother's food, and all those Buddhas, as well as their sanghas of bhikṣus, were likewise fully satisfied. Yet the food remained as before, neither diminished nor reduced. The mother was overjoyed, and she, together with countless devas and humans, all attained the stage of non-retrogression. At that time, the child's hair fell away and he became a śramaṇa, immediately attaining the ground of non-retrogression. The mother came before the Buddha and said: 'Today I have witnessed three marvels: when I washed my son's hands, nine nāgas poured water; this is the first marvel. When the remaining water was sprinkled on his head, it transformed into a jeweled canopy, a lion throne, and a seated Buddha; this is the second marvel. The Buddha smiled, and light from his mouth entered through the top of my son's head; this is the third marvel. I beseech the Buddha to explain these to me.' The Buddha said: 'In fourteen kalpas, this child shall become a Buddha. The nine nāgas bathing him, the lion throne, the jeweled canopy, and the light from the Buddha's smile entering through the top of his head are all signs of what is to come.' When the mother heard the Buddha's words, her joy doubled. In the future she shall become the mother of a wheel-turning sage king. After seven hundred successive lifetimes, when the kalpa's lifespan is exhausted, her female body shall be transformed, and she shall attain the stage of non-retrogression."

The Buddha said: "The child of that time is my present self. Such is my merit in the present world." All the devas, nāgas, spirits, and people, hearing what the Buddha taught, all attained the stage of non-retrogression.
