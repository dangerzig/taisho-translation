# 佛說聖最上燈明如來陀羅尼經
## The Buddha's Teaching: Dhāraṇī Sūtra of the Holy Supreme Lamp-Radiance Tathāgata

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1355

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dānapāla (施護).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was in the country of Śrāvastī, in the Jeta Grove, the garden of Anāthapiṇḍada, together with a great assembly of one thousand two hundred and fifty bhikṣus, and hundreds of thousands of innumerable human and celestial bodhisattvas, who reverently surrounded him as he taught the Dharma. At that time, beings from hundreds of thousands of koṭi buddha-fields all traveled to the Boundless-Flower World, where the Tathāgata Supreme Lamp-Radiance, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha, was presently manifesting and abiding, walking in meditation and teaching the Dharma. He had two bodhisattvas, one named Mahāprabhā and the other Amitābha.

At that time that buddha dispatched the two bodhisattvas to travel to the sahā world, to the Jeta Grove and the garden of Anāthapiṇḍada, to the place of Śākyamuni Tathāgata, Samyak-saṃbuddha. Upon arriving, they bowed their heads at the World-Honored One's feet and sat to one side. The two bodhisattvas addressed the World-Honored One: "Beyond this buddha-land, passing through hundreds of thousands of koṭi buddha-fields, there is a world named Boundless Flowers. In that land there is a buddha named Supreme Lamp-Radiance Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha, who presently abides there constantly teaching the wondrous Dharma. That World-Honored One, Supreme Lamp-Radiance Tathāgata, Samyak-saṃbuddha, has sent us to the sahā world to inquire after the World-Honored One's health, to ask whether you are free from illness and affliction, and whether your vitality and strength are light and at ease, abiding in happiness." He also said: "Here there are humans and non-humans, bhūtas, piśācas, yakṣas, rākṣasas, kumbhāṇḍas, kumbhāṇḍa generals, royal law and bandits, snakes, scorpions, malignant sores, ants, ka��a-bhava insects, and similar beings causing disturbance and affliction. Therefore the World-Honored One Supreme Lamp-Radiance Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha, spoke these dhāraṇī mantra phrases renowned throughout the ten directions, and sent us here for the benefit and happiness of all sentient beings, so that they may have joyful countenances, perfect physical characteristics, and peaceful bodies and minds. By the power of this secret merit, sentient beings are protected." He then spoke the dhāraṇī:

*Tadyathā: jvare jvala-gati huka muka samāti mahā-samāti mahā-jvare jvala-me sahi sāre mahā-jvare uke suke tame samāti mahā-samāti mahā-same svāhā.*

Then the World-Honored One addressed the Venerable Ānanda: "Listen carefully now. These dhāraṇī phrases are extremely rare and difficult to obtain. You should receive, uphold, read, recite them, and explain them for others. This amūni dhāraṇī is as rare as the appearance of a buddha in the world. If any noble man or noble woman hears, receives, and upholds it, and explains it for others, they will attain the knowledge of past lives, seeing the events of their past seven lifetimes. All their afflictions will be entirely eliminated, and they will not experience any past karma.

"Furthermore, Ānanda! These dhāraṇī mantra phrases were jointly proclaimed by seventy-seven koṭi buddhas. If any sentient being hears these dhāraṇī phrases and does not believe but slanders them, it is the same as slandering seventy-seven koṭi buddhas."

Then Aparājita Bodhisattva Mahāsattva rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, placed his right knee on the ground, joined his palms toward the Buddha, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I too wish to proclaim dhāraṇī mantra phrases for the benefit and happiness of all sentient beings, to protect sentient beings by this secret power, and to increase their supreme merit, strength, and joyful appearance." He then spoke the dhāraṇī:

*Tadyathā: aṭa vaṭa nāṭa mahā-ku-nāṭa akva-hi-kahi anā-kahi mānā-kahi aṇa-tā kha-cu mahā-kha-cu sānai-ke-tānai-ke-ta ru-me ṭuru-me here mire tire svāhā.*

Then the World-Honored One addressed the Venerable Ānanda: "Listen carefully now. These maṇi dhāraṇī phrases are extremely rare. You should receive, uphold, read, recite them, and explain them for others. The appearance of a buddha in the world is also extremely rare. Furthermore, Ānanda! If any noble man or noble woman hears these dhāraṇī phrases and receives, upholds, chants, reads, recites, and explains them, they will attain the knowledge of past lives, seeing the events of twelve past lifetimes. They will not experience any past karma.

"Furthermore, Ānanda! These dhāraṇī phrases were jointly proclaimed by eighty koṭi buddhas. If anyone slanders this dhāraṇī, it is exactly the same as slandering eighty koṭi buddhas."

Then Mañjuśrī Kumārabhūta rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, placed his right knee on the ground, joined his palms in reverence, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I too wish to speak these dhāraṇī phrases for the benefit and happiness of all sentient beings, to protect sentient beings by this supreme secret power, and to increase their physical beauty and bring joy to their bodies and minds." He then spoke the dhāraṇī:

*Tadyathā-tine: akai makai nā-ji-ni nā-gara-hi nā-here nā-here ja-hera can-ni caṇḍa-re caṇḍa-ra-vati svāhā.*

Then the World-Honored One addressed the Venerable Ānanda: "Listen carefully now. Ānanda! These dhāraṇī phrases are rare and precious. You should receive, uphold, read, recite them, and explain them for others. Ānanda! These dhāraṇī phrases are as rare as the appearance of a buddha in the world. Ānanda! If any noble man or noble woman hears, receives, upholds, reads, recites, chants, and explains these dhāraṇī phrases for others, they will attain the knowledge of past lives, seeing the events of thirteen past lifetimes. All their afflictions will be entirely eliminated, and they will not experience any past karma.

"Furthermore, Ānanda! These dhāraṇī phrases were jointly proclaimed by eighty-two koṭi bodhisattvas. If anyone slanders this dhāraṇī, it is exactly the same as slandering eighty-two koṭi bodhisattvas."

Then the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣus: "I too wish to speak these dhāraṇī phrases for the benefit and happiness of all sentient beings, so that all sentient beings' merit may increase, their bodies and minds may be joyful, their fame may be heard far and wide, they may have great power and authority, and their countenances may be dignified and beautiful, through the protection of this secret power." He then spoke the dhāraṇī:

*Tadyathā-tine: aṭa vaṭa nāṭa ku-nāṭa chaṭa-ke chaṭa-rake tāru-ve turu-ve rāṭa-ve laṃ-re ru-re ha-re mire tire śire śiri-haṃ-re apu-vapu nā-pu ku-nā-ci anā-pati vānā-pati kehi-kehi-kehi chaṭa-hi chaṭa-hi chaṭa-hi mahā-chaṭa-hi svāhā.*

Then the World-Honored One addressed the Venerable Ānanda: "Listen carefully now. Ānanda! Receive and uphold these dhāraṇī phrases, read, recite, and chant them, and explain them for others. Ānanda! These dhāraṇī phrases are extremely rare. Ānanda! These amūni dhāraṇī phrases are as rare as the appearance of a buddha in the world. Ānanda! If any noble man or noble woman hears, receives, upholds, and explains them for others, they will attain the knowledge of past lives, seeing the events of fourteen past lifetimes. All their afflictions will be entirely eliminated, and they will not experience any past karma.

"Furthermore, Ānanda! These dhāraṇī phrases were jointly proclaimed by eighty-four koṭi buddhas. If anyone slanders this dhāraṇī, it is exactly the same as slandering eighty-four koṭi buddhas.

"Ānanda! If any noble man or noble woman, beneath a withered and decayed tree, reads or recites these dhāraṇī phrases, secretly protecting and rescuing the tree so that fire cannot burn it, the withered tree will produce new branches, leaves, flowers, and fruit in lush bloom. How much more so for the person who upholds it!"

Then the World-Honored One further addressed the bhikṣus: "I now again speak dhāraṇī phrases for the benefit and happiness of all sentient beings, so that they may have joyful countenances, fame heard far and wide, increasing merit, dignified and beautiful appearance, through the protection of this secret power." He then spoke the dhāraṇī:

*Tadyathā-tine: aṭa vaṭa chaṭa-ke chaṭa-rake tāru-pati turu-pati turu-miti turu-mi-miti turu-ture sure here mure tire svāhā.*

Then the World-Honored One addressed the Venerable Ānanda: "Listen carefully now. These dhāraṇī phrases are extremely rare. You should all receive, uphold, read, recite them, and explain them for others. Ānanda! This amūni dhāraṇī is as rare as the appearance of a buddha in the world. Ānanda! If any noble man or noble woman hears these dhāraṇī phrases, receives, upholds, reads, recites, and explains them for others, they will attain the knowledge of past lives, seeing the events of twenty-one past lifetimes. All their afflictions will be entirely eliminated, and they will not experience any past karma.

"Furthermore, it protects against all bhūtas, all devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, humans and non-humans, none of whom will be able to cause harm. Even all malignant sores, snakes, insects, and kaṇa-bhava insects will also be unable to cause harm. Furthermore, all malarial fevers, whether of wind, bile, or phlegm, and all such diseases, whether momentarily arising, lasting one day, two days, three days, four days, or even seven days, half a month, one month, one year, or a long time, will be unable to cause harm. Furthermore, all sores, swellings, abscesses, boils, paralysis, scabies, leprosy, rashes, and ringworm, all illness and affliction, all karmic obstructions, all curses and sorcery, and all vetāḍas will be unable to cause harm.

"Furthermore, Ānanda! These dhāraṇī phrases were jointly proclaimed by ninety-nine koṭi buddhas. If anyone slanders this dhāraṇī, it is exactly the same as slandering ninety-nine koṭi buddhas.

"Furthermore, Ānanda! These dhāraṇī phrases should be remembered when entering the royal palace, when entering the midst of rākṣasas, when entering the midst of lions, tigers, and wolves, when entering the wilderness, when entering a violent and raging river, when facing the danger of water or fire, when poisoned, and at all times of fear. In all cases, no harm can be done.

"Furthermore, Ānanda! If one who upholds this dhāraṇī encounters an evil person bearing a blade or staff, approaching in anger and wishing to inflict harm, the blade and staff will naturally shatter and break, and the practitioner will be delivered.

"Ānanda! As I survey with my mind the realms of humans and devas, and the assemblies of Māra, Brahmā, śramaṇas, devas, humans, and asuras, when these dhāraṇī mantra phrases are used for protection, rescue, boundary-setting, guarding, and the pacification of calamities, there is nothing that surpasses this dhāraṇī. Why? Ānanda! Through the empowerment and mindful protection of this amūni dhāraṇī, all afflictions are entirely eliminated."

Then the four great heavenly kings addressed the World-Honored One: "When this true Dharma is perishing, we will assist it with our divine power, widely proclaiming and disseminating it so that the Dharma may long abide. We will also protect the Dharma-teachers who uphold it and eliminate all māras." The World-Honored One praised them: "Excellent! Four great heavenly kings! You have aroused brave and compassionate resolve to protect the true Dharma."

Then the four great heavenly kings, knowing the Buddha's intent, also rose from their seats and spoke the dhāraṇī:

*Tadyathā: ve-nan-ni ve-nan-ni mahā-nan-ni sa-dhaṃ-bha-ni ja��-bha-ni ṭha-ṭha-ni ṭha-ṭha-ni mahā-ṭha-ṭha-ni ku-ṭa ku-ṭa-ni mahā-ku-ṭa dharma-re cā-mahi svāhā.*

The World-Honored One said: "If any noble man or noble woman hears these dhāraṇī phrases, explains the meaning of the vidyā phrases, and receives, upholds, reads, and recites them, all their afflictions will be entirely eliminated. They will be unobstructed in all worldly matters, as if they upheld all the wondrous Dharma."

Then the World-Honored One addressed the four heavenly kings: "Excellent, excellent! As you have vowed, may the Dharma long abide." He further addressed Ānanda: "Listen carefully now to the true Dharma. Receive, uphold, read, recite it, and explain it for others, so that sentient beings may obtain great merit and their fame may be heard far and wide." Ānanda said: "So be it, World-Honored One! Whatever is to be heard, I shall commit it all to memory."

Then the World-Honored One, by means of the true meaning, skillfully revealed it and spoke in verse:

> This dhāraṇī sūtra
> Was spoken by all the buddhas of the past.
> I now also proclaim
> The meaning of these mantra phrases,
> So that those who hear, receive, and uphold them
> May benefit all sentient beings,
> And see the events of twenty-one past lives,
> All manner of past-life affairs.
>
> No human or non-human,
> No yakṣa or kumbhāṇḍa,
> No rākṣasa or pūtana
> Can create any obstacle,
> Through the proclamation of this dhāraṇī.
>
> Whatever is desired is freely attained.
> Māra, sinful karma, and the like,
> All obstacles naturally vanish,
> Through the speaking of this dhāraṇī.
>
> Parents, brothers and sisters,
> Friends, and relatives
> Cannot be harmed by evil,
> Through the upholding of this dhāraṇī.
>
> In the wilderness and on the roads,
> Lions, tigers, wolves, and the like
> Cannot cause fear,
> Through the recitation of this dhāraṇī.
>
> Throughout hundreds of thousands of koṭi kalpas,
> All sins committed in the past,
> In seven days are entirely eliminated,
> Through the reading of this dhāraṇī.
>
> In seven days one obtains
> The merit of all the bodhisattvas,
> Inexhaustible through koṭi kalpas,
> Through the explanation of this dhāraṇī.
>
> For this dhāraṇī,
> The four heavenly kings are always present:
> Dhṛtarāṣṭra and Vaiśravaṇa,
> Virūḍhaka and Virūpākṣa,
> Leading all their hosts,
> With beautiful forms and majestic divine power,
> Guarding and protecting the four directions
> And those who uphold the mantra.

When the World-Honored One had spoken these dhāraṇī phrases, Ānanda and the great assembly of śrāvakas, Maitreya Bodhisattva, Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, the four great heavenly kings, and the devas, humans, asuras, gandharvas, and the rest, having heard what the Buddha taught, received it with single-minded devotion, leapt for joy, bowed in reverence, and withdrew.
