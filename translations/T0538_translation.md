# 佛說呵雕阿那鋡經
## Sūtra of Hāṭhaka, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0538

Translated from the Chinese. Eastern Jin dynasty, translated by Zhu Tanwulan (竺曇無蘭)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the land of Śrāvastī, at the Jetavana. Hāṭhaka led five hundred laymen to the place of Śāriputra. After making obeisance, they sat to one side. Śāriputra taught them the Dharma, and they departed with great joy. They then went to the Buddha's dwelling, bowed with their heads to the ground, circumambulated the Buddha three times with palms joined, and sat to one side.

The Buddha asked Hāṭhaka: "What merit do you possess that enables you to teach and guide five hundred disciples to follow you?"

Hāṭhaka immediately knelt with palms joined and said: "I always practice four things that the Buddha has taught. What are the four? First, generosity toward others. Second, speaking good and wholesome words. Third, looking after one's fellow practitioners and providing for their needs. Fourth, sharing wealth with one's fellow practitioners without keeping account."

The Buddha said: "Excellent! Excellent! The Buddhas of the past never surpassed these four things. The Buddhas of the future shall also never surpass these four things. The Buddhas of the present also do not surpass these four things."

When the Buddha had finished teaching Hāṭhaka, all departed with great joy.

Hāṭhaka returned home and entered his house. He called together all his people, guests, servants, and maids, and seated them before him. He skillfully taught them the Dharma, explaining the paths of birth and death and of good and evil. He then went up to the upper hall and called the treasury keepers and the female musicians before him, and taught them the sūtra precepts. All were filled with great joy. He then went to the rear hall and taught the sūtra precepts to all the wives and women of the household. When this was finished, he returned to the main hall, sat upon a seat of purificatory discipline, sat upright in concentration, and attained equanimity of mind.

In the first heaven above, the Four Heavenly Kings invited all the devas to assemble. Together they praised Hāṭhaka's merit. The chief among the Four Heavenly Kings descended to Hāṭhaka's house and praised his merit to his face. At that time, Hāṭhaka had attained equanimity of mind and did not respond to the Heavenly King.

A bhikṣu from the Buddha's assembly came to Hāṭhaka's house. Hāṭhaka rose and welcomed the bhikṣu, who sat before him. The bhikṣu said: "The Buddha constantly praises your merit in the assembly."

Hāṭhaka asked the bhikṣu: "When the Buddha praised me, were there any laypeople present?"

The bhikṣu said: "There were no laypeople. But even if there had been laypeople, what would be the concern?"

Hāṭhaka said: "The Buddha's words are perfectly true. But I fear that if laypeople do not believe them, they will fall into the hells. And if they do believe the Buddha's words, they will come to attend upon me and make offerings to me. I do not wish to trouble and disturb others. That is why I ask." The bhikṣu then took his leave.

Hāṭhaka said: "I expect you have not yet eaten this morning. Please stay for a meal." He then served water for washing hands and set out the food.

When the meal was finished, the bhikṣu returned and told the Buddha: "I just went to Hāṭhaka's house and told him: 'The Buddha greatly praises your merit in the assembly.' Hāṭhaka then asked me: 'When the Buddha praised me, were there any laypeople present?' I said: 'There were no laypeople. But even if there had been laypeople, what would be the concern?' Hāṭhaka said: 'The Buddha praises me, and his words are truly sincere and not false. If laypeople do not believe the Buddha's words, they will fall into the hells. If they do believe, they will come to attend upon me. I do not wish to trouble and disturb others. That is why I asked.'"

The Buddha said: "Excellent! Excellent! Have I not always told you that Hāṭhaka has seven qualities? Now I add one more, making eight. What are the eight? First, non-seeking, not wanting others to know. Second, faith, not wanting others to know. Third, self-modesty, not wanting others to know. Fourth, self-shame, not wanting others to know. Fifth, diligence, not wanting others to know. Sixth, self-observation, not wanting others to know. Seventh, attainment of meditation, not wanting others to know. Eighth, keen wisdom, not wanting others to know. The reason he does not want others to know is that he does not wish to trouble and disturb others. Therefore he does not want others to know."

When the Buddha had finished speaking the sūtra, the bhikṣus were all filled with great joy. They rose and made obeisance to the Buddha.
