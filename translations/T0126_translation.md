# 佛說阿羅漢具德經
## Sūtra on the Qualities of Arhats Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0126

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Faxian (法賢)

---

At that time, the World-Honored One was in the country of Śrāvastī, in the Anāthapiṇḍada Monastery, where he had arranged his seat and was sitting. The bhikṣus, as well as devas, nāgas, the eight classes of beings, and human and non-human beings, surrounded the World-Honored One, silently joining their palms to hear what the Buddha would teach.

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣus: "I shall now praise the great śrāvakas who are able to practice with purity in the Buddha-Dharma, each possessing their own virtues in their respective attainments. Listen attentively, reflect well upon this, and I shall explain it for you in due order.

"Bhikṣus! Among my disciples there is a great śrāvaka who abandoned a royal throne and long ago went forth from the household life, the very first to realize the path, foremost in the holy life: the bhikṣu Kauṇḍinya.

"There is a śrāvaka of few desires who always delights in the ascetic practices: the bhikṣu Mahākāśyapa.

"There is a śrāvaka endowed with great eloquence, foremost in wisdom: the bhikṣu Śāriputra.

"There is a śrāvaka who cultivates diligent effort and possesses great supernatural powers: the bhikṣu Maudgalyāyana.

"There is a śrāvaka who, when observing, attains the great divine eye: the bhikṣu Aniruddha.

"There is a śrāvaka endowed with concentration and wisdom, foremost in learning: the bhikṣu Ānanda.

"There is a śrāvaka skilled in rules of conduct, able to uphold the Vinaya treasury: the bhikṣu Upāli.

"There is a śrāvaka who, in the great assembly, is able to teach the wonderful Dharma: the bhikṣu Pūrṇa Maitrāyaṇīputra.

"There is a śrāvaka whose requisites for sitting and lying down are all complete: the bhikṣu Nala Malla's son.

"There is a śrāvaka whose voice in teaching the Dharma is like a lion's roar: the bhikṣu Piṇḍola Bhāradvāja.

"There is a śrāvaka skilled in understanding the sūtras, the Vinaya, and the meaning of discourse: the bhikṣu Kātyāyana.

"There is a śrāvaka foremost in faith and understanding of the Buddha-Dharma: the bhikṣu Mākṛ.

"There is a śrāvaka who, through cultivating and attaining the fruit, has brought glory to his clan: the bhikṣu Kālodāyin.

"There is a śrāvaka who, when expounding, possesses a great and wonderful voice: the bhikṣu Bhaṇḍarika.

"There is a śrāvaka skilled in beautiful speech and able to engage in discourse: the bhikṣu Kumāra-Kāśyapa.

"There is a śrāvaka of great diligent effort in the four dignified deportments: the bhikṣu Śroṇa Koṭikarṇa.

"There is a śrāvaka able to attain swift supernatural penetration in all places: the bhikṣu Vāhika.

"There is a śrāvaka who skillfully advances and realizes the path and its fruit: the bhikṣu Cirīyaka.

"There is a śrāvaka able to exercise the supernatural power of the fire element: the bhikṣu Sugata.

"There is a śrāvaka who abandoned a noble family to delight in going forth: the bhikṣu Bhadra.

"There is a śrāvaka of the Śākya royal clan who renounced to go forth: the bhikṣu Uruvilvā Kāśyapa.

"There is a śrāvaka who, at sacred stūpa sites, was the first to receive an invitation: the bhikṣu Pūrṇa.

"There is a śrāvaka of great wisdom in whatever is expounded: the bhikṣu Mahākauṣṭhila.

"There is a śrāvaka of dignified and careful deportment, with a full and perfect form: the bhikṣu Upasena Vaṅgantaputta.

"There is a śrāvaka foremost in understanding the meaning of the Buddha-Dharma: the bhikṣu Panthaka.

"There is a śrāvaka able to cut through doubts regarding the Four Noble Truths: the bhikṣu Mahā-Panthaka.

"There is a śrāvaka whose speech is direct and without concealment, who admonishes the bhikṣus: the bhikṣu Kapphiṇa.

"There is a śrāvaka who constantly admonishes the bhikṣuṇīs: the bhikṣu Nandaka.

"There is a śrāvaka to whom many people turn and who constantly receives material support: the bhikṣu Śivala.

"There is a śrāvaka whose faculties are concealed and unfathomable: the bhikṣu Nanda.

"There is a śrāvaka who upholds the precepts and Vinaya with purity and without deficiency: the bhikṣu Rāhula.

"There is a śrāvaka who experiences little illness and little affliction in the body he has received: the bhikṣu Bākula.

"There is a śrāvaka who constantly practices generosity and is able to understand emptiness, foremost in this respect: the bhikṣu Subhūti.

"There is a śrāvaka who at all times is able to speak few words: the bhikṣu Aṅgatiriya.

"There is a śrāvaka endowed with the knowledge of past lives, who is able to explain all manner of things from previous existences: the bhikṣu Sobhita.

"There is a śrāvaka able to cultivate pure practice and dwell well in mountain caves: the bhikṣu护国.

"There is a śrāvaka whose requisites for sitting and lying down are all complete: the bhikṣu Gavāṃpati.

"There is a śrāvaka who, in his advancement, is able to attain concentration: the bhikṣu Revata.

"There is a śrāvaka who through cultivation is able to cut off afflictions that have already arisen: the bhikṣu Nārada.

"There is a śrāvaka who is able to halt afflictions that have not yet arisen: the bhikṣu Mitraka."

"There is a śrāvaka who constantly practices the conduct of loving-kindness: the bhikṣu Revata.

"There is a śrāvaka able to practice the conduct of compassion well amid suffering: the bhikṣu Pilindavatsa.

"There is a śrāvaka who possesses the great practice of equanimity: the bhikṣu Vanāri.

"There is a śrāvaka who possesses the great power of equanimity: the bhikṣu Jayata.

"There is a śrāvaka who fully understands both wholesome and unwholesome dharmas: the bhikṣu Kanakavatsa.

"There is a śrāvaka who is able to swiftly cut off all worldly greed: the bhikṣu Nanda.

"There is a śrāvaka who is able to swiftly remove anger and ill will: the bhikṣu Mighāra.

"There is a śrāvaka who cultivates the supreme fruit and swiftly cuts off conceit: the bhikṣu Mānava.

"There is a śrāvaka who is able to swiftly cut off the dharma of delusion: the bhikṣu Bhāradvāja Mānava.

"There is a śrāvaka who cultivates with purity and well understands cause and effect: the bhikṣu Mānava.

"There is a śrāvaka who cultivates the noble fruit and possesses a great sharp faculty: the bhikṣu Aṅgulimāla.

"There is a śrāvaka whose three karmas are harmonious and whose faculties are gentle: the bhikṣu Sarpaṇasa.

"There is a śrāvaka who skillfully asks about subtle and wonderful meanings: the bhikṣu Mahāki.

"There is a śrāvaka of great eloquence in all discourse: the bhikṣu Rādha.

"There is a śrāvaka who is able to ask his mother about the profound meaning: the bhikṣu Karapa.

"There is a śrāvaka skilled in the meaning of the Dharma and who compiled the gāthā verses: the bhikṣu Vaṅgīsa.

"There is a śrāvaka whose every word is entirely truthful: the bhikṣu Viśākha Pañcāli's son.

"There is a śrāvaka who, with a pure mind, constantly delights in seeking the Dharma: the bhikṣu Dharmika.

"There is a śrāvaka who cultivates the practice of joy and possesses the power of patience: the bhikṣu Pūrṇa.

"There is a śrāvaka who, through inner contemplation of dharmas, well penetrates the original mind: the bhikṣu Yaśoda.

"There is a śrāvaka who, in worldly matters, is skilled in interpreting signs: the bhikṣu Mṛgaśiras.

"There is a śrāvaka who, with a supreme mind, well understands the wonderful Dharma: the bhikṣu Dharmaka.

"There is a śrāvaka who is self-mastering regarding desire and well understands coming and going: the bhikṣu Pudgala.

"There is a śrāvaka who at all times is able to speak skillfully: the bhikṣu Pamāna.

"There is a śrāvaka who, through skillful means, is able to expound the wonderful Dharma: the bhikṣu Dharmila.

"There is a śrāvaka who, having attained the wonderful meaning of the Dharma, is able to teach it in proper order: the bhikṣu Gavadatta.

"There is a śrāvaka who at all times teaches the Dharma without weariness: the bhikṣu Kapidake.

"There is a śrāvaka who always maintains a dignified demeanor and is constantly joyful: the bhikṣu Ajita.

"There is a śrāvaka who constantly possesses great joy: the bhikṣu Sambuddha.

"There is a śrāvaka who attains liberation through wisdom alone: the bhikṣu Sujāta.

"There is a śrāvaka who is able to attain liberation through both concentration and wisdom: the bhikṣu Vaṣpa.

"There is a śrāvaka who is able to cut off greed: the bhikṣu Jambuka.

"There is a śrāvaka who possesses excellent liberation: the bhikṣu Vairāṭa Siṃha.

"There is a śrāvaka who cultivates with purity and, knowing this, gains faith and understanding: the bhikṣu Kṣamaka.

"There is a śrāvaka who has attained the most distinguished in the world: the bhikṣu Māhika.

"There is a śrāvaka of deep and lofty wisdom who is able to refute heterodox doctrines: the bhikṣu Jayanta.

"There is a śrāvaka who possesses right view and skillfully defeats Māra's army: the bhikṣu Nikṛtamaiṇḍa.

"There is a śrāvaka who, through wisdom, skillfully breaks through delusion: the bhikṣu Jaga Kāśyapa.

"There is a śrāvaka who constantly practices equanimity: the bhikṣu Bhūguka.

"There is a śrāvaka who cultivates pure wisdom and gradually becomes free from defilement: the bhikṣu Śroṇa Koṭikarṇa.

"There is a śrāvaka who, in his advancement, possesses great purity: the bhikṣu Uttara.

"There is a śrāvaka whose bodily form is without deficiency and whose faculties are fully complete: the bhikṣu Aṅgilu.

"There is a śrāvaka who, in his cultivation, well understands the dharma of emptiness: the bhikṣu Ṛṣidatta.

"There is a śrāvaka who, regarding the dharma of emptiness, has produced deep faith and understanding: the bhikṣu Cunda.

"There is a śrāvaka who, in his own attainment, possesses only supernatural power: the bhikṣu Mahāka.

"There is a śrāvaka who well contemplates the meaning of the eight liberations: the bhikṣu Śraddhājñeya.

"There is a śrāvaka who, in his dignified deportment, delights in fine-colored robes: the bhikṣu Moharāja.

"There is a śrāvaka who broadly discusses wonderful principles and possesses a great retinue: the bhikṣu Sañjaya.

"There is a śrāvaka endowed with concentration and wisdom who constantly receives offerings from humans and devas: the bhikṣu Subhadra.

"There is a śrāvaka who set his mind on going forth and delights in cultivating the noble practice: the bhikṣu Viśāla.

"There is a śrāvaka who, having encountered a cause of suffering, went forth: the bhikṣu Mucuchika Bharadvāja.

"There is a śrāvaka who, weary of saṃsāra, sought to go forth: the bhikṣu Śoṇārika Tiryakoṭa.

"There is a śrāvaka who, realizing the world's impermanence, generated deep weariness: the bhikṣu Śoṇārika Bhāradvāja.

"There is a śrāvaka of silent composure and careful deportment: the bhikṣu Sāhiṣṇuka.

"There is a śrāvaka who cultivates with purity and whose nature is simple and of few words: the bhikṣu Upananda.

"There is a śrāvaka who constantly advances in solitary cultivation and possesses the practice of stillness: the bhikṣu Nandika.

"There is a śrāvaka skilled in concentration and wisdom who has attained great liberation: the bhikṣu Kumbīra.

"There is a śrāvaka who is able to bring purity to those in delusion: the bhikṣu Nāgapalā.

"There is a śrāvaka who cultivates pure practice and was the last to go forth: the bhikṣu Subhadra."

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke further in verse:

> "Holy life and few desires, wisdom and supernatural powers,
> The divine eye and great learning, purity and upholding the Vinaya,
> Ease in sitting and lying down, possessing a lion's roaring voice,
> Faith and understanding, and the profound, skilled in distinguishing the sūtras and Vinaya,
> Bringing glory to one's clan, possessing a great and wonderful voice,
> Diligent effort beyond measure, skill and ability in discourse,
> Great swift supernatural power, first to receive invitation at stūpas,
> Direct speech without concealment, admonishing both monks and nuns,
> Able to conceal the faculties, constantly upholding pure precepts,
> Little illness in the body, constantly practicing generosity,
> Few words and constant silence, endowed with knowledge of past lives,
> Abundant requisites for sitting and lying, delighting in dwelling in mountain caves,
> Cutting off afflictions already arisen, halting those not yet arisen,
> Constantly entering samādhi, great loving-kindness and benefit,
> Able to remove all faults, compassionate heart stilling the wheel of suffering,
> Constantly practicing wholesome and unwholesome dharmas, swiftly removing the mark of conceit,
> Able to cut off greed, anger, and delusion, sharp faculties and purity,
> Well understanding cause and effect, able to ask about profound principles,
> Gentle in all faculties, endowed with great eloquence,
> Asking one's mother about profound meaning, able to proclaim truthful words,
> Skilled in compiling the gāthā verses, patience and endurance firm and strong,
> Constantly delighting in the profound Dharma, endowed with the power of patience,
> Well penetrating the original mind, able to know all signs of divination,
> Endowed with the joy of patience, self-mastering in all desires,
> Deep entry into the gate of wonderful Dharma, skillfully understanding the mind through wisdom,
> Broadly proclaiming the wonderful Dharma, teaching in proper order,
> Without weariness in broadly expounding, joyful and greatly joyful,
> Attaining liberation through wisdom, attaining liberation through both concentration and wisdom,
> Greed forever already removed, deeply attaining the gate of liberation,
> Understanding one's own faith and realization, attaining the most distinguished in the world,
> Able to refute heterodox teachers, skillfully dispelling delusion,
> Able to defeat Māra's armies, going forth and abandoning suffering and affliction,
> Pure wisdom and little defilement, fully complete in all faculties,
> Understanding emptiness as non-dual, possessing the power of supernatural penetration,
> Well contemplating the eight liberations, delighting in fine-colored robes,
> Having a great circle of relatives, receiving offerings from humans and devas,
> Going forth with a heart of faith, constantly practicing the conduct of equanimity,
> Weary of the world, delighting in going forth, deeply weary of the suffering of saṃsāra,
> Constantly practicing a mind of stillness, possessing a modest share of eloquence,
> Composed in silence and dwelling still, deep practice of liberation,
> Purifying those in delusion: endowed with such qualities,
> They are therefore called arhats."

When the World-Honored One had spoken these verses, he further told the bhikṣus: "In this assembly there are ten great śrāvakas, whom I shall now praise. You should listen well.

"They are: the bhikṣu Kauṇḍinya, the bhikṣu Kātyāyana, the bhikṣu Pūrṇa, the bhikṣu Bākula, the bhikṣu Revata, the bhikṣu Aṅgulimāla, the bhikṣu Yaśoda, the bhikṣu Sujāta, the bhikṣu Koṭikarṇa, and the bhikṣu Vaiśāla. These ten great śrāvakas are the foremost in this assembly."

At that time, the World-Honored One further told the bhikṣus: "I shall now praise the great śrāvaka bhikṣuṇīs, who also cultivate their own virtues in their respective attainments.

"Among my bhikṣuṇīs there is a great śrāvaka bhikṣuṇī who abandoned a royal family and long ago went forth, of pure deportment and constantly cultivating the holy life: the bhikṣuṇī Mahāprajāpatī.

"Of few desires, contented, and practicing the ascetic practices: the bhikṣuṇī Paṭācārā.

"Of deep and broad wisdom with great eloquence: the bhikṣuṇī Sulakṣaṇā.

"Able to practice good conduct, with surpassing power and virtue: the bhikṣuṇī Utpalavarṇā.

"Skilled in cultivation and attaining the divine eye: the bhikṣuṇī Somā.

"Cultivating the wisdom of hearing and thinking and attaining great learning: the bhikṣuṇī Śobhā Kṛśamāra's daughter.

"Skilled in upholding the Vinaya with faultless deportment: the bhikṣuṇī Kṛśā.

"Able to teach the wonderful Dharma with skill and eloquence: the bhikṣuṇī Dharmadā.

"Constantly proclaiming the wonderful Dharma with a heart of loving-kindness: the bhikṣuṇī Śākyā Dharmā.

"Diligently cultivating the noble cause and bringing glory to her clan: the bhikṣuṇī Mahāśvetā.

"Seeking the great fruit with a heart of faith and going forth: the bhikṣuṇī, the mother of the householder Śokālakā.

"Of deep prior good roots and endowed with great merit: the bhikṣuṇī Yaśodharā, the mother of Rāhula.

"Constantly devoted to cultivation with great diligent effort: the bhikṣuṇī Jaṭā.

"Able in her own attainment to achieve swift supernatural penetration: the bhikṣuṇī Bhadrā.

"Of deep wisdom, skilled in understanding the sūtras and Vinaya: the bhikṣuṇī Sugrīvā. These bhikṣuṇīs are the foremost in the great assembly."

At that time, the World-Honored One further told the bhikṣus: "Listen well! I shall now praise the upāsakas, who in their faith also cultivate their own practices.

"The first to generate a mind of faith and take refuge in the Three Jewels: the upāsaka Pusaka, and the upāsaka Bali, dwelling in Uruvilvā. Able to practice the pure precepts with full commitment: the upāsaka Nakula's father, dwelling in Bhagaṇḍa. Constantly making gifts to the Saṃgha: the householder Anāthapiṇḍada, dwelling in the city of Śrāvastī. Of deep prior good causes and endowed with great merit: the householder Supradata, dwelling in the city of Śrāvastī. Constantly offering food and drink to the Saṃgha: the chief householder, dwelling in the great city of Vaiśālī. Constantly making various offerings to the Buddha, Dharma, and Saṃgha: the householder Samajīva, dwelling in the city of Rājagṛha. Constantly giving medicine to those who are sick and suffering: the householder Mahānāma, dwelling in the city of Kapilavastu. Able to constantly practice the conduct of loving-kindness within one's faith: the householder Miṇḍaka, dwelling in the great city of Bhadra. Able to skillfully transform the assembly through the four means of attraction: the householder Citta, and Aṭṭavaka, dwelling together in the Great Wilderness. From the first arising of faith, able to possess great wisdom: the householder Upāli, dwelling in the city of Nālandā. Able to discourse like a lion's roar in the assembly: the householder Vīra, dwelling in the city of Rājagṛha. Of excellent great wisdom, able to refute heterodox teachers: the householder Dṛḍhamukha Licchavi, dwelling in the great city of Vaiśālī. Constantly expounding the wonderful Dharma broadly in the great assembly: the householder Citru, dwelling in the city of Supāraka. Of great eloquence in all discourse: King Prasenajit, with his capital in the city of Śrāvastī. Foremost in sharp faculties within one's faith: the younger brother of King Kālu, dwelling in the city of Śrāvastī. Able to possess great wisdom within one's faith: the upāsaka Ṛṣidatta, dwelling in the city of Śrāvastī. Able to uphold the holy life within one's faith: the upāsaka Pūrṇa, dwelling in the city of Śrāvastī. With treasures complete and storehouses abundant, broadly gathering the people and foremost in learning: King Bimbisāra of the country of Magadha, with his capital in the city of Rājagṛha. Constantly generating the mind of bodhi toward the Three Jewels, and with many children in the world: the householder Jīvaka, dwelling in the city of Rājagṛha. With faith and diligent effort, able to achieve swift supernatural penetration: Prince Abhaya, dwelling in the city of Rājagṛha. Having cut off the root, generating faith and understanding: King Ajātaśatru, the son of Vaidehī, of the country of Magadha, with his capital in the city of Rājagṛha. These upāsakas are the foremost in the great assembly."

At that time, the World-Honored One further told the bhikṣus: "Listen well! I shall now praise the upāsikās, who in their faith also cultivate their own practices.

"The first to generate a mind of faith and take refuge in the Buddha, Dharma, and Saṃgha: the upāsikā Nandā and the upāsikā Nandarī, dwelling in Uruvilvā. Able to generate an initial mind of faith and understanding of the precepts: Nakula's mother, dwelling in Bhagaṇḍa. Constantly making gifts to the Saṃgha: the upāsikā Viśākhā's mother, dwelling in the city of Śrāvastī. Of generous prior causes and endowed with great merit: Ṛddhi's mother, dwelling in the city of Śrāvastī. Preparing medicine for those who are sick and suffering: the wife of the householder Mahāsenā, dwelling in the city of Vārāṇasī. Able to attend to the sick: the wife of the householder Supremā, dwelling in the city of Vārāṇasī. Having long generated a mind of faith and constantly practicing the conduct of loving-kindness: the upāsikā Śyāmāvatī, dwelling in the country of Kauśāmbī. Foremost in great learning within the assembly: the upāsikā Kumudhuttarā, dwelling in the country of Kauśāmbī. Able to expound the wonderful Dharma with great eloquence: Princess Sumanas, dwelling in the city of Śrāvastī. Having long generated a mind of faith and possessing great wisdom: Princess Cundā, dwelling in the city of Rājagṛha. Foremost in beauty and dignified appearance among physical forms: Princess Sambuddha, dwelling in the city of Rājagṛha. Able to generate faith, understanding, and intention, and skillfully generating the mind of bodhi: the wife of the householder Mutrā, dwelling in the city of Rājagṛha. With many children in the world in which she lives: the wife of the householder Jīvaka, dwelling in the city of Rājagṛha. Constantly and skillfully cultivating with great diligent effort: the wife of the householder Śroṇā, dwelling in the city of Campā. Constantly providing lodging to the Saṃgha: the daughter of the householder Nandā, dwelling in the Bamboo Grove. Having long generated a mind of faith and deeply understanding meditation: the upāsikā Kālu, dwelling in the Bamboo Grove. These upāsikās are the foremost in the great assembly."

At that time, the great assembly of bhikṣus, as well as devas, humans, asuras, and all the rest, having heard what the Buddha taught, were all greatly delighted and faithfully received it and practiced in accordance.
