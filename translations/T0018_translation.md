# 信佛功德經
## Sūtra on the Merits of Faith in the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0018

Translated by Dharmabhadra of the Song dynasty

---

## Fascicle 1

### Sūtra on the Merits of Faith in the Buddha, Spoken by the Buddha

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was dwelling in the Āmravana of Aṇaka city, together with a great assembly. At that time, the Venerable Śāriputra, at the hour of the meal, put on his robe, took his bowl, and entered Aṇaka city. Having gone on alms-round in due order through the city, he returned to his place, put away his robe, washed his feet, spread out his seat, and ate. Having finished his meal, he went to where the Buddha was, bowed his head to the Buddha's feet, stood to one side, joined his palms toward the Buddha, and spoke these words:

"World-Honored One! I now arouse profound faith in the Buddha. Why is this? Because the Buddha's supernormal powers are supremely excellent and incomparable. Among all the śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas of the past, present, and future, there are none who can know the Buddha's supernormal powers, much less surpass them. How could any of them attain anuttara bodhi?"

The Buddha said: "Well said! Well said! Śāriputra! You are able to speak well of this profound and extensive meaning. You should uphold it and in the midst of the great assembly make the lion's roar, proclaiming it widely."

Śāriputra again addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! The faith I now arouse regarding the Buddha is such that throughout the past, future, and present there is no one capable of the same. Nor is there any śramaṇa or brāhmaṇa who knows the Buddha's supernormal powers or surpasses them. How could any of them attain anuttara bodhi?"

The Buddha addressed Śāriputra: "What do you think? All the Buddhas, Tathāgatas, Arhats, and Samyak-saṃbuddhas of the three times, who are endowed with pure moral conduct, wisdom, liberation, and wondrous supernormal practices: all these I fully know through my supernormal powers. And those Tathāgatas, Arhats, and Samyak-saṃbuddhas likewise know all my pure moral conduct, wisdom, liberation, and wondrous supernormal practices. Śāriputra! Do not suppose that only the present Śākyamuni Buddha alone possesses these supernormal powers."

Śāriputra said: "No, World-Honored One! I do not say: 'Only the Buddha possesses these supernormal powers.' I know that the Tathāgatas, Arhats, and Samyak-saṃbuddhas of the three times are all equally possessed of pure moral conduct, wisdom, liberation, and wondrous supernormal practices."

The Buddha said: "Śāriputra! So it is, so it is! All the Buddhas, Tathāgatas, and Samyak-saṃbuddhas of the three times are all endowed with these supernormal powers and qualities. You should simply proclaim these profound teachings for the sake of those sentient beings, uphold them with one-pointed mind, and in the midst of the great assembly make the lion's roar, proclaiming them widely."

Śāriputra addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! The vast, profound, and supremely wonderful Dharma proclaimed by my Buddha, from wholesome and unwholesome karma down to the various dependently arisen phenomena: all these I fully know one by one as they truly are. Having understood one dharma, I then cultivate one dharma; having cultivated one dharma, I then extinguish one dharma; having extinguished one dharma, I then realize one dharma. For this reason, I now arouse faith in the Buddha as the true Samyak-saṃbuddha."

The Buddha addressed Śāriputra: "Go now and ask others: 'In the past, were there any śramaṇas or brāhmaṇas who could fully know true supernormal powers equal to or surpassing those of the Buddha, and thereby attain the Buddha's bodhi?' Go and ask; what answer would they give?

"Moreover, Śāriputra! Go and ask others again: 'In the future, will there be any śramaṇas or brāhmaṇas equal to the Buddha, up to and including the attainment of the Buddha's bodhi?' Go and ask; what answer would they give?

"Moreover, Śāriputra! Go and ask others yet again: 'In the present, are there any śramaṇas or brāhmaṇas equal to the Buddha, up to and including the attainment of the Buddha's bodhi?'

"Moreover, Śāriputra! Go and ask others once more: 'Among all the śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas of the past, future, and present, in whom do they take refuge?' Go and ask; what answer would they give?"

At that time, Śāriputra addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! That could not be so. I have heard from the Buddha and retain it in mind: there are never two Buddhas appearing in the world simultaneously. Only the Buddha, the World-Honored One, is the true Samyak-saṃbuddha, the One of Right and Universal Knowledge, endowed with the highest supernormal powers. World-Honored One! I see no śramaṇa or brāhmaṇa who could know such supernormal powers, much less surpass the Buddha, up to and including the attainment of the Buddha's bodhi."

At that time, Śāriputra again addressed the Buddha: "I see that the World-Honored One possesses various supreme qualities. What are these supreme qualities? When the Buddha, the World-Honored One, teaches the Dharma, he fully knows all the good benefits thereby obtained. If there are śramaṇas or brāhmaṇas dwelling in mountain wilds, under trees, in charnel grounds, or in empty dwellings, who enter samādhi, sever the afflictions, bring their practice to perfection, increase wholesome qualities, and maintain right mindfulness; and if those śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas abandon unwholesome qualities and cultivate wholesome qualities, up to and including the realization of the fruit: all such things the Buddha fully knows. This is called the Buddha's supreme quality. There is no śramaṇa or brāhmaṇa who knows these supernormal powers equal to or surpassing the Buddha, up to and including the attainment of the Buddha's bodhi.

"Moreover, my Buddha the World-Honored One possesses the supreme quality of being able to discern the twelve āyatanas and widely expound them for others. There is no śramaṇa or brāhmaṇa who can understand these twelve āyatanas and discern them. The twelve āyatanas are: the eye-āyatana and form-āyatana, the ear-āyatana and sound-āyatana, the nose-āyatana and smell-āyatana, the tongue-āyatana and taste-āyatana, the body-āyatana and touch-āyatana, the mind-āyatana and dharma-āyatana. Only the World-Honored One fully knows all such qualities. This is called the Buddha's supreme quality. There is no śramaṇa or brāhmaṇa who surpasses the Buddha, up to and including the attainment of the Buddha's bodhi.

"Moreover, my Buddha the World-Honored One possesses the supreme quality of fully knowing the principles of pudgalas and expounding them for others. There is no śramaṇa or brāhmaṇa who knows such dharmas or can expound them for others. The pudgala-dharmas are of seven types: the śraddhānusārin, the dharmānusārin, the śraddhādhimukta, the dṛṣṭiprāpta, the kāyasākṣin, the prajñāvimukta, and the ubhayatobhāgavimukta. Only the World-Honored One fully knows these seven highest pudgala-dharmas. This is called the Buddha's supreme quality.

"Moreover, my Buddha the World-Honored One possesses the supreme quality of speaking truthfully without falsehood, without frivolous speech, and without divisive speech. All his utterances are truly of great benefit; this is the supreme quality. With fitting cause and condition, he is able in the midst of the great assembly to utter a wondrous voice expounding profound meaning. Only the World-Honored One fully knows this most excellent and true Dharma. This is called the Buddha's supreme quality.

"Moreover, my Buddha the World-Honored One possesses the supreme quality of contemplating through samāpatti the impurity and repulsiveness of the āsrava-laden body, namely, the various impure substances filling the body above and below: hair of the head, body-hair, nails, teeth, skin, flesh, sinews, and bones. The Buddha fully knows that this is not ultimate, that it is worthy of disenchantment. This is called the first samāpatti.

"Moreover, World-Honored One! If there are śramaṇas or brāhmaṇas who are able to contemplate with wisdom, as things truly are, the āsrava-laden and impure skin, flesh, bones, marrow, and various filths of the body above and below, this is the second samāpatti.

"Moreover, World-Honored One! If there are śramaṇas who contemplate the āsrava-laden body with wisdom, seeing that even within this one lifetime it is not ultimate, and are able to contemplate thus, this is the third samāpatti.

"Moreover, World-Honored One! If there are śramaṇas who are able to contemplate the āsrava-laden body with wisdom, seeing that it is not ultimate in this lifetime and is likewise not ultimate in the next lifetime, and are able to contemplate thus, this is the fourth samāpatti.

"Moreover, World-Honored One! If there are śramaṇas who are able to contemplate the āsrava-laden body with wisdom as described above, seeing that it is not ultimate in this lifetime, not ultimate in the next lifetime, and not ultimate even in lifetimes thereafter, and that it is impure and repulsive, and are able to contemplate thus, this is the fifth samāpatti.

"Moreover, World-Honored One! As for these āsrava-laden, impure, and non-ultimate phenomena, only the World-Honored One, with the purified divine eye surpassing the physical eye, fully sees the arising and ceasing of sentient beings, the fair and the ugly, the fortunate and unfortunate destinies, up to and including their birth in the heavenly realms, knowing all of this as it truly is. This is called the Buddha's supreme quality.

"Moreover, my Buddha the World-Honored One possesses the highest supreme quality: when the World-Honored One teaches the Dharma, if there are śramaṇas or brāhmaṇas who come to listen and receive it, seeking tranquility, they all rely upon the seven bodhyaṅgas. The seven bodhyaṅgas are: the dharmavicaya-bodhyaṅga, the vīrya-bodhyaṅga, the prīti-bodhyaṅga, the praśrabdhi-bodhyaṅga, the upekṣā-bodhyaṅga, the smṛti-bodhyaṅga, and the samādhi-bodhyaṅga. Only the World-Honored One fully knows these seven dharmas. This is called the Buddha's supreme quality.

"Moreover, my Buddha the World-Honored One possesses the supreme quality of skillfully discerning the four samyakpradhānas. The four samyakpradhānas are: causing evil already done to be abandoned, causing evil not yet done to be prevented, causing good already done to increase, and causing good not yet done to arise. Such dharmas are widely proclaimed in the heavenly and human realms for the benefit of all. This is called the Buddha's supreme quality.

"Moreover, my Buddha the World-Honored One possesses the supreme quality of manifesting great supernormal powers through right wisdom. These supernormal powers include: manifesting many from one and gathering many into one; appearing in emptiness with nothing there; passing through city walls, mountains, and rocks unhindered with the body; reaching from the earth with one's hand to touch the sky up to the Brahma realm; walking on water as on solid ground; sitting cross-legged in the sky; and moving like the sun and moon traversing empty space. If there are śramaṇas or brāhmaṇas who, upon witnessing these supernormal powers, give rise to disbelief, I declare that all such persons are deluded ordinary beings; they are not noble ones, they do not possess supernormal powers, they do not seek right enlightenment, nor do they delight in seeking the tranquility of nirvāṇa. These supernormal powers are called the Buddha's supreme quality.

"Moreover, World-Honored One! Regarding what beings in the world desire, pleasant forms and good forms, for those who seek them, the Tathāgata acts in accordance with the faculties of those sentient beings. This is called the Tathāgata's supernormal power.

"Moreover, World-Honored One! Regarding all pleasant and unpleasant forms, good and bad forms in the world, being free from both and not abiding in either, and fully knowing past lives: this is called the Tathāgata's supernormal power.

"Moreover, World-Honored One! Seeing form within form: this is called the Tathāgata's supernormal power.

"Moreover, World-Honored One! Internally free from the perception of form, yet seeing all external forms: this is called the Tathāgata's supernormal power.

"Moreover, World-Honored One! The body being fully liberated, having realized and established this attainment: this is called the Tathāgata's supernormal power.

"Moreover, World-Honored One! The sphere of infinite space, definitively realized: this is called the Tathāgata's supernormal power.

"Moreover, World-Honored One! The sphere of infinite consciousness, definitively realized: this is called the Tathāgata's supernormal power.

"Moreover, World-Honored One! The sphere of nothingness, definitively realized: this is called the Tathāgata's supernormal power.

"Moreover, World-Honored One! The sphere of neither-perception-nor-non-perception, definitively realized: this is called the Tathāgata's supernormal power.

"Moreover, World-Honored One! Having fully understood feeling and perception, and feeling and perception having ceased: this is called the Tathāgata's supernormal power. Only the World-Honored One fully knows all these most excellent supernormal realms. This is called the Buddha's supernormal power.

"Moreover, my Buddha the World-Honored One possesses the supreme quality whereby, concerning all the acts, conditions, karmic fruits, recollections, and lifespans of śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas through one life or many lives in the past, these I could not know even across koṭis of years. Only the World-Honored One knows the various places where those śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas abided throughout the past: whether in the form realm, the formless realm, the sphere of perception, the sphere of non-perception, or the sphere of neither-perception-nor-non-perception, fully knowing all their various acts, conditions, and karmic fruits. This is called the Buddha's supreme quality.

"Moreover, my Buddha the World-Honored One possesses the supreme quality that when teaching the Dharma, he always teaches as things truly are. But there are śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who, out of delusion, give rise to discriminating thoughts and doubting minds, thinking: 'The Buddha teaches the Dharma using only the characteristics of things; what he teaches is the same across the three times, whether near or far, including all mental phenomena; none of what he says is as things truly are.' The Buddha fully knows those who harbor such doubts. This is called the Buddha's supreme quality.

"Moreover, when my Buddha the World-Honored One teaches the Dharma, if there are śramaṇas or brāhmaṇas who originally have no doubts, but later, upon hearing someone say, 'The Buddha's teachings are all not as things truly are,' give rise to doubts and come to think that the World-Honored One teaches only through the characteristics of things, the Buddha also knows those who thus slander him. This is called the Buddha's supreme quality.

"Moreover, when my Buddha the World-Honored One teaches the Dharma, if there are śramaṇas or brāhmaṇas who originally have no doubts and do not regard the World-Honored One as teaching through the characteristics of things, but later, upon hearing someone speak, follow that person in giving rise to doubts, and then go on to tell others, causing them also to become doubtful, and from this doubt they give rise to discriminating thoughts, saying, 'This matter is as before, all not truly real; these are merely the various discordant minds of sentient beings,' the Buddha fully knows all such cases. This is called the Buddha's supreme quality.

"Moreover, my Buddha the World-Honored One, seeing śramaṇas dwelling in samādhi without doubt or speech, fully knows their practice and aspiration. And again, seeing śramaṇas emerging from concentration, the Buddha also fully knows all their affairs and the doubts on account of which they emerged from concentration. The Buddha resolves all such doubts. This is called the Buddha's supreme quality.

"Moreover, my Buddha the World-Honored One possesses the supreme quality of fully knowing all non-ultimate phenomena. If there are śramaṇas or brāhmaṇas dwelling in the mountains with mind established in samāhita, who through their own supernormal powers know the affairs of twenty kalpas of expansion and contraction, and think, 'I fully know all the affairs of kalpas of expansion and contraction in the past,' World-Honored One, those śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas cannot know the affairs of kalpas of expansion and contraction in the future or the present. Only the World-Honored One fully knows the affairs of kalpas of expansion and contraction throughout the three times. This is called the knowledge of the first non-ultimate phenomenon.

"Moreover, World-Honored One! If there are śramaṇas or brāhmaṇas dwelling in the deep mountains with mind established in samāhita, who through their own supernormal powers know the affairs of forty kalpas of expansion and contraction, and think, 'I already fully know all expansions and contractions in the future,' World-Honored One, those śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas cannot know the affairs of kalpas of expansion and contraction in the past and present. Only the World-Honored One fully knows all three times. This is called the knowledge of the second non-ultimate phenomenon.

"Moreover, World-Honored One! If there are śramaṇas or brāhmaṇas dwelling in the deep mountains with mind established in samāhita, who through their own supernormal powers know the affairs of eighty kalpas of expansion and contraction, and think, 'I fully know all the affairs of expansion and contraction in the past and future,' World-Honored One, those śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas cannot know the limits of the present. Only the World-Honored One knows one by one the limits of the three times. This is called the knowledge of the third non-ultimate phenomenon. Thus the World-Honored One, with the purified divine eye surpassing the physical eye, fully sees the arising and ceasing of sentient beings, up to and including their birth in the heavenly realms. This is called the Buddha's supreme quality.

"Moreover, my Buddha the World-Honored One possesses the supreme quality: through the Dharma of taming and subduing, the World-Honored One fully knows the preferred dharmas of all pudgalas and teaches accordingly. These pudgalas, having understood, practice in accordance with principle, sever the three afflictions, and before long realize the fruit of Srotāpanna, going against the stream of birth and death, traveling seven times to the heavens and seven times returning to the human realm, reaching the end of suffering. All this the World-Honored One fully knows. Furthermore, the World-Honored One knows the preferred dharmas of those pudgalas who practice in accordance with principle, severing the three afflictions as well as greed, hatred, and delusion, and before long realize the fruit of Sakṛdāgāmin, returning to the human realm once more, reaching the end of suffering. All this the World-Honored One fully knows. Furthermore, the World-Honored One fully knows the preferred dharmas of those pudgalas who practice in accordance with principle, severing the five afflictions together with their attendant afflictions, and before long realize the fruit of Anāgāmin. All this the World-Honored One fully knows. Furthermore, the World-Honored One fully knows those pudgalas who practice in accordance with principle and before long exhaust their āsravas, realizing the Dharma of liberation: 'My birth is exhausted, the holy life has been established, what had to be done has been done, there is no further becoming.' All such dharmas the World-Honored One knows one by one. This is called the Buddha's supreme quality."

"Moreover, my Buddha the World-Honored One possesses the supreme quality of fully knowing the four types of womb-awareness. The first type does not know entry into the womb, and likewise does not know abiding in or departing from it. The second type knows entry into the womb but does not know abiding in or departing from it. The third type knows entry and abiding but does not know departure from the womb. The fourth type fully knows entry, abiding, and departure. Among these four types, the differences in knowledge are understood one by one only by the World-Honored One. This is called the Buddha's supreme quality.

"Moreover, my Buddha the World-Honored One possesses the supreme quality of fully knowing all pudgalas and how they realize the noble fruits according to whatever obstructions they have severally abandoned. All such dharmas the Buddha fully knows. This is called the Buddha's supreme quality.

"Moreover, my Buddha the World-Honored One possesses the supreme quality: the World-Honored One knows those who have fully developed the faculty of faith, whose moral conduct is pure, whose wisdom is complete, who are truthful and without deception, selfless and without sloth, free from all illusion and distraction, without desire, who do not lead sentient beings astray through wrong paths, and who always practice right mindfulness. Only the World-Honored One fully knows all such qualities. This is called the Buddha's supreme quality."

At that time, Śāriputra again addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! All the deluded ordinary beings of the world grasp at pleasures of the senses, exhaust themselves, and seek what is of no benefit. The Buddhas and Tathāgatas are not like this; they delight only in benefiting others, not in seeking their own pleasure. They fully understand the dharmas of the mind, perceive the tranquility of the Dharma, abide in the state of bliss, free from desire and free from suffering, and have attained the four dhyānas. Therefore, World-Honored One, those men and women of superior faculties should see thus, hear thus, perceive thus, and know thus. These are truly those of superior faculties."

At that time, in the assembly there was a Venerable One named Nāgarakṣita, who stood in attendance at the Buddha's side holding a jeweled fly-whisk. The Venerable Nāgarakṣita addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I see the various heterodox paths and the Nirgrantha followers, who have never given rise to faith in the World-Honored One but compete only in proclaiming the superiority of their own wrong paths. Therefore, I now wish to erect banners and pillars to proclaim to the world, causing all to hear and know the Buddha's supreme merits, that my Buddha the World-Honored One is a great man, the most venerable and most excellent, without equal."

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the Venerable Nāgarakṣita: "Do not speak thus. Do not proclaim the Buddha's supreme merits to others; I do not now wish to be praised in this way." Thereupon the Venerable Nāgarakṣita praised the World-Honored One, saying: "Well said! Well said! Truly this is the Samyak-saṃbuddha."

At that time, the Buddha addressed the Venerable Śāriputra: "You should skillfully propagate and proclaim this right Dharma widely for bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, and upāsikās, as well as for all śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas, and even for māras, followers of heterodox paths, Nirgrantha followers, and all those of wrong views who have no faith in the Buddha, so that upon hearing this right Dharma they may give rise to profound faith, take refuge in the Buddha, give rise to right views, and come to understand the right Dharma." And further he said: "You, Śāriputra, should propagate and proclaim it thus."

At that time, the World-Honored One said to the Venerable Śāriputra, "I have finished speaking," and then fell silent and remained still. Thereupon the Venerable Śāriputra, empowered by the Buddha's might, having expounded this Dharma, bowed to the Buddha and withdrew.

At that time, all the members of the assembly, having heard the right Dharma, rejoiced, bowed in reverence, received it with faith, and undertook to practice it.
