# 大方廣佛華嚴經中卷卷大意略敘
## Brief Synopsis of the Main Meaning of Each Fascicle of the Avataṃsaka Sūtra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1740

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, composed by Li Tongxuan (李通玄).

---

### Fascicle One

The Buddha achieved the Way in the country of Magadha and taught the Dharma at the site of enlightenment. Those who in past kalpas had cultivated together with the Buddha were caused to gather at the assembly like ocean-clouds.

### Fascicle Two

The animal-born classes and the devas of the higher realms each, within the ocean of the Tathāgata's fruit, partially realize and attain liberation.

### Fascicle Three

The four kings, the eight classes, and the miscellaneous spirits all compose wondrous invocations and sing of the inconceivable Dharma.

### Fascicle Four

Forms and colors of ten thousand kinds, retinues of a thousand sorts: each explains the gate of liberation and praises in verse the Tathāgata's merit.

### Fascicle Five

Through the Tathāgata's inconceivable power, all the bodhisattvas rise from their seats. Heaven and earth stream forth auspicious signs, and the ocean-assembly makes offerings to boundless buddhas.

### Fascicle Six

The great assembly unanimously wishes and requests; the Tathāgata manifests his marks in response. The light-sound summons those with karmic connection, and new assemblies from the ten directions gather like clouds.

### Fascicle Seven

Samantabhadra enters the Treasury-Body Samādhi. The Tathāgata supports him with holy power. Rising from concentration, he teaches the Dharma of the ten oceans, broadly elucidating the establishment of world-systems.

### Fascicle Eight

A wind-wheel supports the great waters; from the great ocean, a lotus flower surges forth. Seed-realms and fragrant rivers are arranged, and world upon world is layered within the central ocean.

### Fascicle Nine

Spiraling rightward through ten oceans, each successively connected, interlacing layer upon layer, like a heavenly net of jewels.

### Fascicle Ten

The governed buddha-land oceans extend straight to the encircling rim. The dharma-realm equals the flower-treasury adornment: the fruit is presented to encourage all to give rise to faith and aspiration.

### Fascicle Eleven

Vairocana, in past kalpas, was once Prince Weiguang. He made offerings to buddhas as numerous as atoms in ten Mount Sumerus, thereby obtaining the fruit-ocean of the ten lotus-treasury worlds.

### Fascicle Twelve

The Tathāgata's names are of many kinds, adapted to the differing capacities of beings. The Four Noble Truths for the four births are named differently according to region, each enabling those who hear the Dharma to awaken and realize.

### Fascicle Thirteen

Mañjuśrī and nine leading bodhisattvas clarify ten kinds of profound meaning, all revealing the interfusion of nature and characteristics and destroying the doubts and attachments of all sentient beings.

### Fascicle Fourteen

Purifying practices give rise to boundless great vows. Mañjuśrī instructs in the skillful use of mind. Worthy Leader manifests virtuous conduct, singing and gathering to reveal the limitless great function of faith.

### Fascicle Fifteen

The hair-tip radiance that illumines and benefits beings; the samādhi that, through similes, reveals the depths of the profound meaning. The sūtra is praised as inconceivable and commended for upholding, while all buddhas touch the head in sympathetic joy.

### Fascicle Sixteen

Verse-praises on Sumeru reveal the Buddha's virtue: he does not depart yet ascends to heaven. The ten abodes and their gates of understanding elucidate the purpose and direction of the bodhisattva's entry into the stages.

### Fascicle Seventeen

Brahma-conduct causes one to understand that the object is empty and quiescent, not giving rise to dualistic understanding. The merit of the initial resolve is boundless: from the first moment of thought, one already achieves right awakening.

### Fascicle Eighteen

Clarifying the Dharma explains how the Three Jewels remain unbroken and how below they bring peace and joy to humans and devas. The ten practices and their ten Dharma-gates ascend as stages of advancement.

### Fascicle Nineteen

Verse-praises in the Yāma Heaven reveal the Buddha's inexhaustible virtue, while also displaying the Dharma-gates of the ten practices and broadly discussing the bodhisattva's skillful subtlety.

### Fascicle Twenty

The four practices of non-attachment and the equality of truth: elucidating the two gates of self-benefit and benefiting others. The attainments since realization are inconceivable; the concluding verses gather again the earlier virtues.

### Fascicle Twenty-One

The ten inexhaustible treasuries are followed by the buddhas of the three times in common. They do not belong solely to the Dharma-gate of the ten practices; their meaning also extends to the advancement beyond the dedications.

### Fascicle Twenty-Two

Ascending to the Tuṣita Heaven, the teaching host responds to conditions. Beholding the Buddha's divine radiance, the deva king receives benefit.

### Fascicle Twenty-Three

The deva of Contentment praises in verse the initial dedication among the ten. The bodhisattva's compassion and wisdom are boundless; through long kalpas, he rescues sentient beings from suffering.

### Fascicle Twenty-Four

With a steadfast mind that is indestructible, one equals the Dharma-gates of all buddhas. Vows and practices are all-encompassing, dedicating the good qualities of the Sugata to all times and places.

### Fascicle Twenty-Five

Wholesome causes contain the fruit, establishing the name of the inexhaustible treasury. Wholesome roots merge with the truth, becoming identical with the inexhaustible nature of suchness.

### Fascicle Twenty-Six

From giving of carriages and horses to giving of one's own body, the great causes span ten long sections of text, relinquishing everything both internal and external.

### Fascicle Twenty-Seven

The bodhisattva gives even his connected flesh, topknot, and crown, and goes so far as to rescue those of broken body. Adorning himself with ten thousand practices, he accomplishes the ten marks of a great person.

### Fascicle Twenty-Eight

From beginning to end, beholding the Buddha's manifestation, there are sixty great gates of giving. The three fascicles of long text, front and back, together gather the dedication of firm resolve.

### Fascicle Twenty-Nine

Conforming to sentient beings without marks, and following the mind's practice accordingly. The two kinds of giving are as vast as incalculable numbers, and one's vow is to be fully equipped in both material and Dharma giving.

### Fascicle Thirty

A hundred gates of virtue, all threaded by one suchness. Practicing virtue equal to empty space, it also equals the ever-expanding nature and characteristics.

### Fascicle Thirty-One

Because of markless delusion and bondage, this is named the dedication of liberation. Six kinds of subtle wisdom-gates: one vows to realize the virtue of Samantabhadra.

### Fascicle Thirty-Two

Containing the single body within the dharma-realm, gathering ten thousand phenomena upon a hair-tip. Dedicating wholesome roots equal to the dharma-realm, one accomplishes the great function of Samantabhadra throughout dust-mote lands.

### Fascicle Thirty-Three

Both the direct and environmental fruits are adorned with countless jewels. The concluding praise encourages the cultivation to fulfill the merit of the Samantabhadra stage.

### Fascicle Thirty-Four

In the Paranirmita-vaśavartin Heaven's maṇi-jewel palace, the ten-ground Dharma-gate first opens. Three empowerments and five requests precede the elucidation of the initial characteristics of the first ground.

### Fascicle Thirty-Five

The bodhisattva of the Ground of Freedom from Defilement cultivates the Dharma-gates of the three clusters of pure precepts and the ten wholesome deeds. The eight dhyānas and five superknowledges give rise to the boundless blazing characteristics of wondrous wisdom.

### Fascicle Thirty-Six

The blazing flame of wisdom that realizes: one advances in cultivating the Dharma-gates of the factors of awakening. Employing the śrāvaka's contemplation of the Four Noble Truths, one reveals the characteristics of the bodhisattva's superior ground.

### Fascicle Thirty-Seven

Prajñā is directly present; ten kinds of contemplation of dependent origination. Far-going over a long time: the seventh ground surpasses the merit of those before and after.

### Fascicle Thirty-Eight

If one does not realize the principle of non-arising, the Buddha provides seven exhortations as a bridge. With great compassion, one contemplates the ten kinds of dense forest of sentient beings. With great wisdom flowing, the bodhisattva's eloquence expounds the Dharma.

### Fascicle Thirty-Nine

Cloud-and-rain virtues of benefiting both self and others, yet even in small part not yet equal to the Tathāgata. Herein cause and fruit are interwoven; many similes compare what is beyond measure.

### Fascicle Forty

The ultimate fruit must proceed from the initial faith; therefore the assembly reconvenes at the Dharma-hall. Samantabhadra, receiving the command, proclaims the teaching, finally and formally presenting the surpassing function of the ten samādhis.

### Fascicle Forty-One

The samādhi of universal abiding in all lands, extending to ten kinds of adornment. The bodhisattva's sovereign supernatural powers are taught, revealing the inexhaustible merit of Samantabhadra.

### Fascicle Forty-Two

The sovereign function of entering and emerging from the samādhi of differentiation cannot be divided into other portions. The four mouths pour forth waves; the mind-stream flows with the four kinds of eloquent exposition.

### Fascicle Forty-Three

The samādhi of the great unimpeded wheel pervades the dharma-realm as its surpassing function, like the transformations of the heavenly sovereign king. It resembles virtual awakening in being the same as wondrous awakening.

### Fascicle Forty-Four

Rolling and unrolling with sovereign eloquence, elucidating the ten kinds of body. Supernatural powers are made clear through both Dharma and simile, presenting the characteristics of the ten patiences.

### Fascicle Forty-Five

Incalculable numbers and the Tathāgata's lifespan reveal the inexhaustible virtue of the Buddha. Response and manifestation adapt to each region, indicating the dwelling places of the bodhisattvas.

### Fascicle Forty-Six

The great assembly harbors doubts and wishes to inquire, not knowing what the characteristics of virtue are. The blue lotus opens in eloquent response: speaking of the inconceivable fruit-dharma of the Buddha.

### Fascicle Forty-Seven

From beginning to end, the vast buddha-works cultivate the gates of liberation. The banner of heroic and vigorous Dharma is raised high, proclaiming the far-reaching perfect sound.

### Fascicle Forty-Eight

The ocean of marks of the ten bodies and the radiance of the minor marks all reveal virtue and function that are inconceivable, with deep causes and far-reaching fruit.

### Fascicle Forty-Nine

The chapter on Samantabhadra's practices: a single delusion constitutes a million gates of obstruction. The concluding verses separately elucidate the ten practices, interwoven without confusion.

### Fascicle Fifty

The manifestation of the Tathāgata, through many gates of Dharma and simile. The ten bodies contain and interpenetrate. The single principle levels and pervades, illuminating all.

### Fascicle Fifty-One

The mouth's eloquence pours forth the characteristics of the perfect sound, responding with the hidden flow of wisdom-water. Breaking open a mote of dust, the great sūtra emerges; cleaving the ordinary mind, the holy wisdom appears.

### Fascicle Fifty-Two

Object, practice, and the attainment of right awakening; turning the Dharma-wheel and nirvāṇa. Approaching, seeing, and hearing are not in vain: one becomes an indestructible vajra-seed.

### Fascicle Fifty-Three

The bodhisattva dwells in the world without defilement; this is the chapter *Transcending the World*. Manifesting clouds of universal wisdom, raising the questions at the outset, Samantabhadra pours forth his responses as from a vase.

### Fascicle Fifty-Four

Beginning from ten kinds of gladness, extending to the gate of the unequaled abode: more than three hundred sections of long text, responding with the characteristics of the ten practices.

### Fascicle Fifty-Five

The meaning of ten kinds of impermanence; afterward, the gates of sovereign mastery and others are displayed. The purport therein is boundless, elaborating the stages of the preceding dedications.

### Fascicle Fifty-Six

Opening with ten kinds of non-obstruction, up to the display of the great lion's roar: numerous immeasurable Dharma-gates elaborating the virtue of the preceding ten grounds.

### Fascicle Fifty-Seven

Severing ten kinds of residual habits; thereafter discussing the mind of further advancement. More than thirty-three gates broadly elucidate the practices of freedom from defilement and the rest.

### Fascicle Fifty-Eight

Beginning from the bodhisattva's pure giving, down to the ten gates of descent and birth: the vows and practices therein are inexhaustible, responding with the characteristics of the cause being fulfilled and the fruit completed.

### Fascicle Fifty-Nine

The eight marks of descent, birth, and the womb; the verses exhort us to cultivate and uphold single-mindedly. Two thousand gates of liberation and practice: the portion of cultivating and completing the one division.

### Fascicle Sixty

The Tathāgata, without leaving the Jeta Grove, enters the Siṃhavijṛmbhita Samādhi. The bodhisattvas behold the divine transformations of the dharma-realm; the śrāvakas are all deaf and blind, unable to perceive.

### Fascicle Sixty-One

Samantabhadra, able to open the ten phrases; the Tathāgata extends a single ray of white ūrṇā-radiance. Mañjuśrī, with his retinue, journeys south: all manifesting the fruit-virtue of the samādhi-gate.

### Fascicle Sixty-Two

The Great Sage admonishes Sudhana to seek friends and travel south. He visits Meghaśrī and Sāgaramegha, and receives their Dharma-gates: the profundity is beyond measure.

### Fascicle Sixty-Three

Encountering Muktaka and the Elder Sagaramegha, one realizes the wondrous gate of the revolving dhāraṇī. Afterward, meeting the bhikṣu Supratisthita, one beholds the surpassing events manifested from every pore of his body.

### Fascicle Sixty-Four

Paying respects to the friend Śreṣṭhira Ghoṣa: merely grasping his hand, one tours the ten directions. He is urged to visit the mountain of the ascetic Jayoṣmāyatana: casting oneself into the fire, one obtains samādhi.

### Fascicle Sixty-Five

From the maiden Maitrāyaṇī onward, to the householder Sudarśana: these four good friends all transmit the Dharma-gates of the ten practices.

### Fascicle Sixty-Six

The Elder Ratnacūḍa and the Elder Samantanetra; King Anala and King Mahāprabha. Acala speaks of the past causes of venerating the Buddha; the Dharma-gates contain the profound meaning of both adverse and favorable conditions.

### Fascicle Sixty-Seven

Sarvasattvatrātṛ transforms the six destinies through various forms. Vasumitra's Dharma-gate has five divisions. The invincible general Jayottama ferries people across with four pressures. The bhikṣuṇī Siṃhavijṛmbhitā sits and teaches the Dharma.

### Fascicle Sixty-Eight

The Bodhisattvas Vasubandhu and Avalokiteśvara, the deva Mahādeva and the earth-goddess Sthāvarā as good friends, cultivating the Dharma-gates of the dedications. Veṣṭhila speaks of the holy stage of the first ground.

### Fascicle Sixty-Nine

The night-goddess Sarvajagatrakṣā-praṇidhāna-vīrya-prabhā teaches the gate of liberation through roaming-steps. Samantasattva-trāṇojaḥśrī, body like clouds of joy: she has made offerings to many buddhas, and her power extends ever further.

### Fascicle Seventy

Pramuditanayanajagadvirocana extends a single ray of ūrṇā-radiance. Sudhana obtains the three wheels of purity. The questions and answers reveal serving many honored ones over the ages; through distant kalpas, one encounters good friends and achieves understanding.

### Fascicle Seventy-One

Praśāntarutasāgaravatī and Samantāvarta-gāminī open the gate of liberation for Sudhana. Each is profoundly vast and inconceivable; layer upon layer, all are brought together with past and present.

### Fascicle Seventy-Two

The friend Open-Blooming Tree-Flower teaches the name of liberation. The Dharma-Radiance Wheel-Turning King establishes a place for the offering of the Dharma. The night-goddess gathers past events with Xiumu.

### Fascicle Seventy-Three

The great-vow Dharma-gate: Sudhana beholds the surpassing and wondrous city. The prince's father sees the Buddha; the prince is spared the offense of execution.

### Fascicle Seventy-Four

The wondrous-virtue spirit-goddess presides over the ten kinds of birth-treasury. In the womb of Queen Māyā, the divine transformations of the three-thousandfold world are manifested in their marvel.

### Fascicle Seventy-Five

Sudhana visits the spirit-goddess Gopā and receives the admonition to seek friends. He encounters the past event of the Buddha as a marriage partner and shares in the surpassing condition of cultivating vows and practices.

### Fascicle Seventy-Six

Māyā serves as the mother-exemplar of all buddhas, a model of vow, speech, and conduct. The friend Artisan-Master Prabhūtaratna: all are causes for encountering conditions and entering samādhi.

### Fascicle Seventy-Seven

The boy and girl admonish Sudhana a hundred layers on serving friends as the Dharma. Maitreya, compassionately mindful, praises in verse and urges him to return to the original teacher.

### Fascicle Seventy-Eight

Sudhana, moved by the encounter, speaks. Maitreya, replete in virtue, is able to accommodate. The sūtra has more than three hundred sections, revealing the surpassing and lofty merit of the aspiration for awakening.

### Fascicle Seventy-Nine

Sudhana enters through the pavilion gate and sees the events of immeasurable kalpas throughout dust-mote lands. Through Dharma and simile, question and answer, he withdraws from the assembly, having assured his future attainment of the fruit of the Way.

### Fascicle Eighty

Mañjuśrī extends his hand as if in support. Sudhana's merit reaches the ultimate fruit. Samantabhadra touches his head and urges in verse: do not harbor doubt regarding this sūtra.

---

*[Colophon:]* Completed on the eighteenth day of the second month of the seventh year of Daiei. The intention behind copying this is as follows: the *Avataṃsaka Sūtra* is the fundamental Dharma-wheel of the Buddha's entire teaching, directly revealing the Tathāgata's inner realization. Accordingly, whoever once hears it forever departs from the suffering of birth and death. Lay Scholar Tongxuan is a patriarch of the Avataṃsaka tradition; his sūtra-lectures accord with the Buddha's intention, and therefore golden light issued from his mouth. Whether one speaks of that or of this, one who does not believe is impossible. Yet the main meaning of eighty fascicles is barely squeezed into the paper and ink in one's palm: a subtle and wondrous essential art. I, Ken, daily receive it upon my head. Without regard for the scarcity of my writing paper, I have exerted myself with my poor brush to copy it. My prayer: may the Three Jewels and the invisible host receive it with compassion. Ken.
