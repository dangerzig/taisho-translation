# 佛說七俱胝佛母心大准提陀羅尼經
## Sūtra of the Great Cundī Dhāraṇī of the Heart of the Mother of Seven Koṭi Buddhas, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1077

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Divākara (地婆訶羅).

---

At that time the Buddha was in the country of Śrāvastī, in the Jetavana grove of Anāthapiṇḍada's park. At that time the World-Honored One, reflecting and contemplating, out of compassion for the sentient beings of the future, taught the dharma of the Cundī Dhāraṇī, Heart of the Mother of Seven Koṭi Buddhas. He then spoke the spell:

*Namaḥ saptānāṃ samyak-saṃbuddha-koṭīnāṃ tadyathā oṃ cale cule cundī svāhā*

"If any bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, or upāsikās receive, uphold, read, and recite this dhāraṇī to the full count of eight hundred thousand times, all the transgressions they have committed throughout immeasurable kalpas, including the five heinous crimes and all other offenses, shall be entirely extinguished. In every place of rebirth they shall encounter buddhas and bodhisattvas. All their material needs shall be fulfilled as desired. Life after life they shall always obtain renunciation, fully maintain the bodhisattva precepts of pure moral conduct, and always be reborn among devas and humans, never falling into evil destinies. They shall always be protected by the devas. If any lay men or women recite and uphold it, their households shall be free from calamities, misfortunes, illness, and suffering. All their undertakings shall succeed without exception, and all their words shall be trusted and accepted by others.

"If anyone recites this dhāraṇī spell to the full count of one hundred thousand times, in dreams they shall see buddhas, bodhisattvas, śrāvakas, and pratyekabuddhas, and shall see themselves vomiting black matter from their own mouths. If one bears grave transgressions, upon reciting to the full count of two hundred thousand times, one shall also see buddhas and bodhisattvas in dreams, and shall likewise see oneself vomiting black matter. If one who has committed the five heinous crimes does not obtain such auspicious dreams, one should recite to the full count of seven hundred thousand times, and at that time the same signs as before shall appear, even to the point of seeing oneself vomit white matter like curds or rice. One should know that this person has manifested the sign of sins extinguished and purity attained.

"Furthermore, I now teach the functions accomplished by this great dhāraṇī. Before a buddha image, or before a stūpa, or in a clean place, smear the ground with gomaya and construct a square altar of whatever size is suitable. Furthermore, with flowers, incense, banners, canopies, food and drink, lamps, and candles, offer whatever one can according to one's means. Furthermore, empower fragrant water with the spell and sprinkle it in the four directions and above and below to seal the boundary. At the four corners of the altar and at its center, each place a vase of fragrant water. The spell-holder, within the altar, should face east and kneel on one knee, and recite the spell one thousand and eight times. The fragrant water vases will then spontaneously rotate. Clasp the hands holding mixed flowers, empower them with the spell one thousand and eight times, and scatter them upon the surface of a mirror. Then gaze directly into the mirror and recite the spell another one thousand and eight times. One shall see images of buddhas and bodhisattvas. One should empower flowers with the spell one hundred and eight times and scatter them as offerings. One may then ask questions about the dharma, and all shall be clearly resolved.

"If there is a ghost-illness, empower kuśa grass with the spell and brush the sick person with it. They will immediately be cured. If a small child is possessed by a ghost, with five-colored threads have a young girl twist them into a cord. With each recitation of the spell, tie one knot, to a total of twenty-one. Fasten it around the child's neck. Empower mustard seeds with the spell seven times and scatter them upon the child's face. They will immediately recover.

"Furthermore, there is a method: before a sick person, draw their form in ink. Empower a willow branch with the spell and strike the drawing. They will also recover.

"Furthermore, there is a method: if a sick person is possessed by a ghost and their body is in a distant place, empower a willow branch with the spell a full seven times and send someone to carry it and strike the person. They will also recover.

"Furthermore, there is a method: if one is traveling on the road and recites this spell, one will have no fear of bandits, wild beasts, or other dangers.

"Furthermore, there is a method: if one always holds this spell, should any lawsuit or dispute arise, one will always prevail. If one wishes to cross a river, great river, or ocean, recite the spell and cross. One will have no trouble from water-dwelling evil creatures.

"Furthermore, there is a method: if one has been imprisoned, shackled, or bound in body, reciting this spell will bring immediate liberation.

"Furthermore, there is a method: if any country suffers from floods, droughts, unseasonal weather, or the spread of epidemics, one should mix ghee with sesame seeds and husked rice, take a three-finger pinch, empower it with one recitation of the spell, and place it in a fire to burn. Whether over seven days and seven nights, performing this six times each day in continuous succession without interruption, all calamities and epidemics shall be entirely eliminated.

"Furthermore, there is a method: on a sandbar amid a river, stamp the sand with a seal to form the shape of a stūpa, reciting the spell once for each stūpa stamped. When one has completed six hundred thousand times, one may see Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, or Tārā Bodhisattva, or Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva. Whatever one's heart desires shall be fulfilled. Or one may see the granting of celestial medicines, or see the conferring of a prediction of bodhi.

"Furthermore, there is a method: circumambulate a bodhi-tree image to the right while reciting the spell to the full count of ten million times. One shall then see a bodhisattva teaching the dharma, and if one wishes to follow the bodhisattva, one shall be able to do so.

"Furthermore, there is a method: if when begging for food one always holds this spell, one will not be troubled by evil people, vicious dogs, or other such beings. If before a stūpa, a buddha image, or a reliquary stūpa one recites and upholds this spell three hundred thousand times, and furthermore on the fifteenth day of the bright half of the month sets out a great offering, fasting for one day and one night with right mindfulness reciting the spell, one shall even see Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva, and that bodhisattva shall take the person to his own palace.

"Furthermore, there is a method: before a stūpa of the Turning of the Dharma Wheel, or before a stūpa at the Buddha's birthplace, or before the stūpa of the jeweled staircase descending from Trāyastriṃśa Heaven, or before a reliquary stūpa, before any such stūpas, if one recites the spell and circumambulates to the right, one shall see Aparājitā Bodhisattva and Hārītī Bodhisattva. Whatever one wishes shall all be fulfilled. If one needs celestial medicine, it shall be bestowed, and furthermore they shall teach the dharma and show the bodhisattva path.

"If any person recites this dhāraṇī, until the time one sits at the site of awakening, all the bodhisattvas shall always be one's good friends. Furthermore, this dharma of the great Cundī dhāraṇī, the great vidyā spell, was spoken by all the buddhas of the past, shall be spoken by all the buddhas of the future, and is spoken by all the buddhas of the present. I now likewise speak it, for the benefit of all sentient beings and to enable them to attain unsurpassed bodhi. If there are sentient beings of meager merit, those with scant good roots, those lacking the capacity, and those without the constituent dharmas of bodhi: if they hear this dhāraṇī teaching, they shall swiftly realize anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi. If any person can always recollect and recite and uphold this spell, immeasurable good roots shall all be accomplished."

When the Buddha taught this dharma of the great Cundī dhāraṇī, immeasurable sentient beings left the dust behind and departed from defilement, obtaining the merit of the great Cundī dhāraṇī, the great vidyā spell. Having seen the buddhas, bodhisattvas, and all the assemblies of sages of the ten directions, they paid homage and departed.
