# 佛說聖最勝陀羅尼經
## Sūtra on the Holy Supreme Victorious Dhāraṇī Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1409

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dānapāla (施護).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the kingdom of Pārapata, in the great city of Xingzuo, in the Guminā Monastery. After the summer retreat had ended, on the twentieth day of the ninth month, there was a bhikṣu named Vāyakhana. At that time, this bhikṣu left the great city of Xingzuo and was heading toward the city of Cīna, a distance of four yojanas. On the road, he saw a great person, three zhang in height, with a face four chi long. He recognized him as the Bodhisattva Mañjuśrī. He immediately prostrated at his feet, knelt with palms joined, and addressed the bodhisattva: "Why have you manifested in person and come to this place? There must be a cause." The bodhisattva answered: "Indeed, indeed. Bhikṣu! Within this Jambudvīpa, all sentient beings will experience illness and suffering. The asuras and garuḍas dwell on one face of Mount Sumeru and wage war against the devas and humans. For this reason, in the sky the sun and moon lose their light and the stars do not appear. When the devas are defeated and the asuras are victorious, the ghosts and spirits take advantage of the opportunity. Transforming into female forms, they disturb sentient beings and produce various diseases: headaches, stomachaches, eye pain, ear pain, nose pain, abscesses and boils, scrofula, hemorrhoids and fistulas, scabies, leprosy, sores, and ringworm; or malaria, recurring every one, two, three, or four days; and all evil diseases of wind, bile, and phlegm. Furthermore, within Jambudvīpa, great winds and rains descend, with cold and heat, floods and drought. The five grains do not ripen, and the people suffer famine and all die untimely deaths. Furthermore, tigers, wolves, lions, fierce beasts, and the like appear, harming sentient beings and stealing their vital energy. Such multitudes of calamities compete to press upon them."

At that time, the great person, having explained these matters, told the bhikṣu: "I have the Holy Supreme Victorious Dhāraṇī, which increases good roots and can eliminate all evils. If a person generates a pure mind and makes offerings to the Three Treasures and all worthy sages with incense, flowers, and the like, and for seven days and nights maintains pure fasting and recites this dhāraṇī, all such calamities will be eliminated." The dhāraṇī:

*namo bhagavate vimala-buddhi-gaṃbhīra-[garjita]-rāja-ya tathāgatāya. tadyathā vimala-[gṛhṇa]-te vimala-[yoni]-śi ala-gate arā-gate svāhā. namaḥ sarva-nīvaraṇa-viṣkaṃbhina-tathāgatāya. tadyathā hima hima mahā-mahī svāhā. namo guṇa-karāya tathāgatāya. tadyathā gagana-kari gagana-saṃbhave gagana-kīrti-te svāhā. namaḥ samanta-gandh-ottamāya tathāgatāya. tadyathā samaye svāhā. namo aparā[jita]-gāminaye tathāgatāya. tadyathā ama-ma-hī svāhā. namo mānasa-saṃbhavāya tathāgatāya. tadyathā mānasa-viśuddhe padma-[ī]śvari padma-saṃbhave kiṃ-kari hīṃ-[ta]-kariye svāhā. namaḥ sarva-buddha-bodhisattvānāṃ namo ārya-mañjuśriye. tadyathā jaye jaye rāma-[gā]hi hima hima mahā-mahī svāhā. namo ārya-avalokiteśvarāya bodhisattvāya. tadyathā gagana-dhāye gagana-saṃbhave gagana-madhye gagana-vi-klānde eha-ye hī svāhā. namo ārya-samanta-bhadrāya tathāgatāya. tadyathā hī bhadri mahā-bhadri amṛta-bhadri vigata-rāja-[siṃha] mahā-vigata-rāja-[siṃha] svāhā. namo ārya-vimala-kīrtaye tathāgatāya. ani-kīrtti-ta-sarva-[jñā]ni-[a]bhi-vajra-saṃbhave vajra-bhida-kari svāhā. namaḥ sarva-buddha-bodhisattvānāṃ. tadyathā citta-kara-[bhij-jvala]-niye svāhā. [ja]gaṃ-bhanī staṃbhanī mohanī puṇḍarīkāya svāhā. mohā-nandī nanda-bhanīye svāhā. [dhūra]-vi-[dhūrī]ye kāla-vi-kṛte svāhā. śānti-svi-nirvāpaṇīye svāhā. [dhūra]-vi-[dhūrī]ye svāhā. [lamba]-[ju]ṇāya svāhā. lokika-lok-uttarāya svāhā. agni-[yāna]-vi-[lindha]nīye svāhā. dhātu-karīye svāhā. megha-sphoṭanāya svāhā. brahma-[bhī]-grahāya svāhā. sarva-karma-[abhi]ṣiktāya svāhā. sarva-buddha-[abhi]saṃskṛtāya svāhā. eka-śṛṅgāya svāhā. bhūtāya svāhā. abhūtāya svāhā. sarva-duḥkha-praśamāya svāhā*

At that time, the Bodhisattva Mañjuśrī, having proclaimed this Holy Supreme Victorious Dhāraṇī, told the bhikṣu: "You should follow this true Dharma and its ritual procedures. Each day, with sincere devotion and a pure mind, make offerings to seven bhikṣus and to the Three Treasures and all worthy sages. During the first watch of the night, perform the homa fire ritual, and disasters and calamities will be swiftly dispelled. If good men or good women receive, uphold, read, recite, and broadly explain this true Dharma to others, all diseases will be cured."

Furthermore, the Bodhisattva Mañjuśrī told the bhikṣu: "Listen carefully. This Holy Supreme Victorious Dhāraṇī has the power of a great vidyā and broadly benefits sentient beings. It should circulate everywhere throughout Jambudvīpa, in all kingdoms, cities, and villages, causing all sentient beings to hear, receive, and uphold it. If a good man or good woman can read and recite this sūtra once, their own illnesses will be swiftly cured. If they read it twice, the illnesses of their spouse, sons, and daughters will all be cured. If they read it three times, the illnesses of all their relatives will be cured. If they read it four times, the illnesses of all the people of the nation will be cured. If they read it five times, all the people of every small kingdom within the king's domain will obtain peace and happiness, free from all disease."

Furthermore, the Bodhisattva Mañjuśrī said: "This true Dharma has vast benefits and is extremely rare. Cause it to be swiftly circulated so that sentient beings may obtain supreme merit. If someone generates a mind of contempt toward this sūtra and does not receive, uphold, read, or recite it, does not explain or circulate it for others, and the sentient beings within that kingdom do not get to hear it, that person incurs a transgression equal to the five grave offenses. Therefore, bhikṣu, receive and uphold this Holy Supreme Victorious Dhāraṇī with faith and reverence, and do not let it be forgotten. The beings that dwell on one face of Mount Sumeru, the devas and asuras and gandharvas, I shall myself subdue, causing the sentient beings of Jambudvīpa to obtain great peace and happiness." When the Bodhisattva Mañjuśrī had finished speaking this Dharma, he suddenly vanished. The bhikṣu Vāyakhana, having heard his teaching, was filled with great joy, and faithfully received and practiced it.

*Translator's note: The long dhāraṇī above contains invocations to numerous tathāgatas and bodhisattvas. The IAST reconstruction uses brackets for uncertain portions. Clearly identifiable names include: Vimala-buddhi-gaṃbhīra (Tathāgata of Pure Profound Wisdom), Sarva-nīvaraṇa-viṣkaṃbhin (Remover of All Obstructions), Guṇa-kara (Maker of Virtues), Samanta-gandh-ottama (Supreme Universally Fragrant), Mānasa-saṃbhava (Born from Mind), Ārya-Mañjuśrī, Ārya-Avalokiteśvara, Ārya-Samantabhadra, and Ārya-Vimalakīrti. Identifiable terms in the individual mantra phrases include: gagana (sky), padma (lotus), vajra (thunderbolt), amṛta (deathless), bhadra (auspicious), jaya (victory), citta-kara (mind-maker), jaṃbhanī (bewildering), staṃbhanī (paralyzing), mohanī (deluding), puṇḍarīka (white lotus), śānti (peace), nirvāpaṇī (extinguishing), lokika-lokuttara (worldly and transcendent), agni (fire), dhātu (element), megha-sphoṭana (cloud-bursting), sarva-karma-abhiṣikta (consecrated in all karma), sarva-buddha-abhisaṃskṛta (blessed by all buddhas), eka-śṛṅga (one-peaked), bhūta (spirit), abhūta (non-spirit), and sarva-duḥkha-praśama (quelling all suffering).*
