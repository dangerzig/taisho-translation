# 穢跡金剛說神通大滿陀羅尼法術靈要門
## Essential Gate of the Spiritual Art of the Great Complete Dhāraṇī Proclaimed by Ucchusma Vajra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1228

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Azhidaxian (阿質達霰).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was in the land of Kuśinagara, at the Mallas' birthplace, beside the Hiraṇyavatī River, between the twin śāla trees. At that time, as the Tathāgata was about to enter nirvāṇa, there were immeasurable hundreds of thousands of myriads of devas, nāgas, the eightfold assembly, humans and non-humans, weeping toward the Buddha, choking with grief on all four sides, dwelling in sorrow. At that time the various great assemblies of devas, Śakra Devānām Indra, and others all came to make offerings. Only the Spiral-Topknot Brahmā King, accompanied by celestial maidens, sat surrounded on all four sides, with thousands of millions of celestial maidens before and behind him, amusing themselves together. Though he heard that the Tathāgata was entering parinirvāṇa, he did not come to pay his respects.

At that time the great assembly said: "Today the Tathāgata is about to enter parinirvāṇa. Why has that Brahmā King not come? That king must have a mind of arrogance and therefore has not come here. Let us send our attendant minor mantra-ṛṣis there to fetch him." Having spoken thus, they dispatched hundreds of thousands of mantra-ṛṣis to that place, but they encountered all manner of filth and impurity forming a moat and rampart. The ṛṣis, upon seeing this, each violated their mantras and died.

At that time the great assembly found this unprecedented and strange. They further dispatched countless vajra-beings, also commanding them to hold mantras and go. After seven days, no one had succeeded in fetching him. The great assembly, seeing this, were doubly grieved.

At that time the great assembly spoke in unison this verse:

> "Alas, Great Holy Lord, how swiftly you enter the truth!
> The devas are still resolute; devas and humans call out and can reach you.
> Alas, Deva among Devas, entering truth like a flame extinguished."

When the great assembly had spoken this verse, they choked and wept and wailed all the more. At that time the Tathāgata, having compassion for the great assembly, by the power of his great omniscient supernatural force, from within his left heart manifested the Indestructible Vajra.

He then rose from his seat amid the assembly and addressed them: "I possess a great divine mantra that can fetch that Brahmā King." Having spoken thus, he displayed great supernatural powers amidst the assembly, causing this great trichiliocosm to quake six times. The celestial palaces, nāga palaces, and all ghost-spirit palaces all crumbled. He then soared to the Brahmā King's abode, and with a point of his finger, all the foul and filthy matter was transformed into solid ground.

At that time the Vajra arrived there and announced: "You are greatly foolish! Our Tathāgata is about to enter nirvāṇa. Why have you not gone?" Then, with the power of the indestructible vajra, he lightly pointed at the Brahmā King, who then generated the aspiration and came to the Tathāgata's presence.

At that time the great assembly praised him: "Great Powerful One! You possess such supernatural power as to fetch that Brahmā King and bring him here." The Vajra then replied: "If there are sentient beings in the world who are troubled by evil heavenly māras and all manner of non-Buddhist practitioners, let them but recite my mantra one hundred thousand times, and I shall personally manifest and cause all sentient beings to have their desires fulfilled, forever leave poverty, and always be at ease and happy."

"The mantra is as follows. First make this great vow: 'Homage to our original teacher Śākyamuni Buddha. After the Tathāgata's nirvāṇa, I shall uphold this mantra, vowing to deliver all beings and cause the Buddhadharma not to perish but to long abide in the world.'" Having spoken this vow, he proclaimed the Great Complete Dhāraṇī Divine Mantra, the Ucchusma True Word:

*Oṃ kuru-ṭha-ḍaṃ mahā-praṇadhi-viṃ-jiṃ-viṃ-vikī-vimānasi ucchusma-kuru-ṭha hūṃ hūṃ phaṭ phaṭ phaṭ svāhā*

When the Vajra had spoken this mantra, he further said: "After the Tathāgata's nirvāṇa, I shall constantly recite this mantra. If there are sentient beings who wish to receive and uphold this mantra, I shall always serve as their attendant and cause their wishes to be fulfilled."

"I now proclaim this divine mantra before the Tathāgata. I beseech the Tathāgata, from within the truth, to illuminate and acknowledge us. World-Honored One, if there are sentient beings greatly troubled by evil ghosts and spirits, this mantra will prevent all of them from causing harm, and they shall forever leave suffering and hardship."

"World-Honored One, if there are good men or good women who wish to rescue and cure all manner of illnesses, let them recite the above mantra four hundred thousand times. When they see a sick person and treat them, there will be results. Regardless of whether conditions are pure or impure, they may command at will, and I shall follow and fulfill all wishes."

"If one wishes to cause a withered tree to grow branches and leaves, take one large liang of white styrax resin and apply it to the tree's heartwood. With a willow branch, empower the tree with the mantra one hundred times, three sessions daily. After a full three days, it will produce flowers and fruit."

"If one wishes to cause a dried-up spring to produce water, surround it with pure ash. Then place three pecks of dawn-drawn well water into the spring water. At the hour of the Tiger, recite the mantra one hundred and eight times, and water will gush forth like a wheel."

"If one wishes to cause a barren mountain to grow grass and trees, take a steel sword. At the four directions surrounding the mountain, recite the mantra three thousand times. When seven days are complete, growth will appear."

"If one wishes to cause wild beasts to submit, take and burn agaru incense, and toward the place where the beasts dwell, recite the mantra one thousand times. At nightfall the beasts will all gather at the practitioner's gate, submitting like domestic animals, to be commanded at will, never to depart."

"If one wishes yakṣas to come and submit of their own accord, take ten peach branches, cut evenly. Take one dan of water and boil down to five pecks. Strain out the peach and willow branches. Add three large liang of cloves, three large liang of frankincense, and three large liang of white styrax resin. Mix with the willow water and boil down to five pecks. Place in a cracked basin. Take one peach branch three chi long, stir the water, and recite the mantra one hundred times. All yakṣas and rākṣasas will come and manifest, speak with the practitioner, and request to first serve as attendants."

"If one wishes to cause evil ghosts, spirits, venomous snakes, scorpions, fierce beasts, and other venomous creatures to be subdued, surround their dwelling-holes with pure ash, and they will all come out on their own. Then gently speak and recite the mantra one hundred times. The snakes and all poisonous beasts will each lose their venomous intent and not dare to harm people, and they will be swiftly liberated."

"If one wishes to prevent a vicious dog from harming people, take one ball of food, empower it with the mantra seven times, and give it to eat. It will never harm people again and will stop barking."

"If one wishes to cause an evil person to come and submit, write that person's name and place it beneath the practitioner's foot. Recite the mantra one hundred times while concentrating on that person. The person will immediately arrive, submit, and abandon their hostile mind."

"If one wishes to cause two people to hate each other, write the names of those two people and place them beneath one's own feet. Recite the mantra two hundred and eighteen times, and those people will separate and turn their backs on each other, no longer loving or respecting one another."

"If two people who hate each other should be caused to love and respect each other, write their names and place them beneath one's own feet. Recite the mantra one hundred and eight times, and those people will cherish each other and never part."

"If one wishes to bring peace and happiness to a person who is not at peace, write that person's name beneath one's foot. Recite the mantra three hundred times, and on that person's behalf make a great vow. At that time I shall personally deliver it, with unimpeded eloquence, and according to the practitioner's wishes, all that is needed shall be given."

"If the mantra-holder seeks various precious treasures, wish-fulfilling maṇi-gems, and the like, let them but recite the mantra with sincere mind, setting their own limit for the number, and I shall personally deliver them, fulfilling all wishes."

"If one wishes to treat a person's illness, form the illness-pressing seal. First, with the left hand's index and middle fingers press the binding-point. Empower it with the mantra one hundred times. Press the seal upon the patient seven times, and they will immediately recover."

"If the patient is on the verge of death, first perform the Five-Roads Prohibition Seal, then treat them. The seal prevents death. For the eye seal: as before, bend the ring finger into the palm, stand the little finger upright, and empower with the mantra one hundred times. The ailment will be swiftly removed."

"If treating a spirit-possession illness, simply burn agaru incense at the patient's bedside and recite the mantra. It will be immediately removed."

"If treating a worm-poison illness, write the patient's name on paper and recite the mantra over it, and they will recover. For treating specter-illness, proceed in the same manner."

"If treating a lingering transmitted illness, write the patient's name and also the name of the disease-ghost. Bury it beneath the patient's bed and recite the mantra. The ghost will present its name of its own accord and manifest in visible form. Then command that ghost to observe matters of the three times, and it will describe everything in detail. The illness will quickly be cured."

"If there is a patient with seasonal epidemic disease, the mantra-master need but look upon them, and they will recover."

"If one wishes to prevent the epidemic-disease ghost-king from entering the boundary, on the ten vegetarian days recite my mantra one thousand and eight times. This can eliminate illness within ten thousand li."
