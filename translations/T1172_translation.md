# 金剛頂超勝三界經說文殊五字真言勝相
## Victorious Characteristics of Mañjuśrī's Five-Syllable True Word, from the Vajraśekhara Trailokyavijaya Sūtra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1172

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

At that time Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva Mahāsattva and all the bodhisattvas, each before Vairocana Buddha, had each proclaimed their heart true words and mudrās. Then Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva Mahāsattva rose from his seat and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I now wish, for the sake of benefiting all sentient beings of the future, to enable them to swiftly accomplish mahāprajñāpāramitā. If a person recites this but once, it is as if they had recited the 84,000 sections of the Twelve-Division Scripture Treasury. If they recite it twice, Mañjuśrī and Samantabhadra will attend them, the fourfold assembly will surround and empower them, and the compassionate, fearless, dharma-protecting good spirits will stand before that person."

Ānanda addressed Mañjuśrī: "You should speak this true word."

At that time a hundred thousand koṭis of buddhas appeared. From each pore of these buddhas emerged a hundred thousand koṭis of bodhisattvas. From each pore of these bodhisattvas emerged a hundred thousand koṭis of nāga kings. From each pore of these nāga kings emerged a hundred thousand koṭis of nāga maidens. From each pore of these nāga maidens emerged a hundred thousand koṭis of blue elephants. From each pore of these blue elephants emerged a hundred thousand koṭis of white elephants. From each pore of these white elephants emerged a hundred thousand koṭis of fragrant elephants. From each pore of these fragrant elephants emerged a hundred thousand koṭis of mountain elephants. From each pore of these mountain elephants appeared a hundred thousand koṭis of jeweled courtyards. Within each jeweled courtyard appeared a hundred thousand koṭis of pools of water endowed with the eight virtues. These pools were composed of the four treasures. From each jeweled pool emerged a hundred thousand koṭis of rays of Jambudvīpa gold light. Within each ray of light appeared a hundred thousand koṭis of circles of radiance. From each circle of radiance manifested a hundred thousand koṭis of celestial maidens, bearing various offerings in splendor. Such an assembly, as numerous as the sands of the Ganges, all gathered at that great assembly at one time and with one voice spoke this true word, manifesting the samādhi of the three samādhis.

"I have now spoken only briefly. Know that its merit is immeasurable." He then spoke the five-syllable true word:

*A ra pa ca na*

"If good men or good women are able to uphold this true word, reciting it but once, they immediately enter the equality of all dharmas of the Tathāgata, and all syllables are likewise equal, and they swiftly accomplish mahāprajñā.

"When receiving this heart true word on behalf of disciples, have them form the secret mudrā: with both hands in vajra-bond, raise the patience and vow fingers with their upper joints bent like the shape of a sword. Place a flower upon the mudrā and scatter it upon the altar as an offering. Then one should declare: 'This heart dharma-gate is the most secret and supreme of all tathāgatas. Take care never to carelessly explain it to the foolish, lest you break your samaya precepts. Contemplate it well.'

"*A*: this is the meaning of non-arising.

"*Ra*: this is the meaning of being pure and undefiled, free from the dust of defilement.

"*Pa*: this is the meaning of no paramārtha, the equality of all dharmas.

"*Ca*: this is the meaning of all dharmas having no saṃskāras, no actions.

"*Na*: this is the meaning of all dharmas having no nature or characteristics; spoken words and syllables are all unobtainable.

"Because *Na* is without nature or characteristics, *Ca* is without actions. Because *Ca* is without actions, *Pa* is without paramārtha. Because *Pa* is without paramārtha, *Ra* is without defilement. Because *Ra* is without defilement, *A* means that all dharmas are fundamentally unarisen.

"Good man! One should contemplate that this mind is originally pure, without any defilement, free from the characteristics of self, mine, and discrimination. To enter this gate is called samādhi; this is true cultivation. Know that such a person is confirmed by the Tathāgata, and their surpassing merit is inconceivable.

"If one recites it once, it removes all suffering and difficulty for the practitioner. If one recites it twice, it extinguishes the grave sins of kalpas of birth and death. If one recites it three times, samādhi manifests. If one recites it four times, one attains dhāraṇī and never forgets. If one recites it five times, one swiftly accomplishes unsurpassed bodhi.

"If one can, with single-minded concentration, in a secluded and quiet place, write out the five-syllable wheel-altar in Sanskrit and recite in accordance with the dharma for a full month, Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva will appear in person, or from the sky will expound the essential dharma. At that time the practitioner will attain knowledge of past lives, unobstructed eloquence, mastery of supernatural powers, accomplishment of supreme aspirations, perfection of merit and wisdom, and will swiftly ascend to realization of the Tathāgata's dharma-body. Simply by receiving it with faith in one's heart, after sixteen lifetimes one will certainly attain perfect enlightenment.

"If one lacks the means to establish an altar, offer incense and flowers, or paint the honored image, one may use fragrant mud to anoint a śarīra stūpa, write out the five-syllable true word in Sanskrit, and circumambulate while reciting five hundred thousand times. Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva will appear before that person and teach the dharma. They will attain knowledge of past lives and eloquence, and all tathāgatas, bodhisattvas, vajra-holders, and holy beings as numerous as the sands of the Ganges will always protect and watch over them. Their aspirations will be swiftly fulfilled, and they will quickly realize bodhi. This is explained in detail in the Vajraśekhara Sūtra."
