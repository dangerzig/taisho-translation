# 大方廣佛華嚴經入法界品
## The Great Vaipulya Buddha Avataṃsaka Sūtra: Chapter on Entering the Dharma-Realm

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0295

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Divākara (地婆訶羅)

---

At that time, Lady Māyā further told the youth Sudhana: "Good son! In this world, in the Trāyastriṃśa Heaven, there is a king named Right Mindfulness. The king has a maiden named Devaprabha. Go to her and ask: 'How does a bodhisattva study the bodhisattva's practice and cultivate the bodhisattva's path?'"

Then the youth Sudhana, respectfully receiving her instruction, prostrated at her feet, circumambulated her countless times, gazed at her with longing, and withdrew, walking backward. He then went to the celestial palace, met the maiden, paid homage at her feet, circumambulated her, joined his palms, stood before her, and said: "Noble one! I have already generated the aspiration for anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi, but I do not yet know how a bodhisattva studies the bodhisattva's practice and cultivates the bodhisattva's path. I have heard that the noble one gives excellent instruction. I pray you will teach me."

The celestial maiden answered: "Good son! I have attained a bodhisattva liberation called Unobstructed Mindfulness of Purity and Adornment. Good son! I recall that in the past there was a supremely excellent kalpa named Blue Lotus. During that kalpa, I made offerings to Buddhas, Tathāgatas, as numerous as the sands of the Ganges. From the very first time each of those Tathāgatas left home, I attended, guarded, and made offerings to them all, building saṅghārāmas and preparing all requisites. Further, from the time those Buddhas, as bodhisattvas, dwelt in their mothers' wombs, at the time of their birth, when they took seven steps, when they gave the great lion's roar, while they dwelt as youths in the palace, when they went to the bodhi tree and attained perfect awakening, when they turned the wheel of the true Dharma, when they manifested the Buddha's divine transformations to teach and tame sentient beings, through all such deeds, from their initial generation of the aspiration until the Dharma was exhausted, I clearly remember all of it without omission, constantly present before me, mindful and unforgetting. I also recall that in the past, in a kalpa named Good Earth, I made offerings to Tathāgatas as numerous as ten times the sands of the Ganges. In another past kalpa named Wondrous Virtue, I made offerings to Tathāgatas as numerous as the atoms of one buddha-land. In a kalpa named Unattainable, I made offerings to eighty-four billion hundred-thousand nayutas of Tathāgatas. In a kalpa named Good Light, I made offerings to Tathāgatas as numerous as the atoms of Jambudvīpa. In a kalpa named Immeasurable Light, I made offerings to twenty times the sands of the Ganges of Tathāgatas. In a kalpa named Diligent Virtue, I made offerings to Tathāgatas equal in number to the sands of one Ganges. In a kalpa named Good Compassion, I made offerings to eighty times the sands of the Ganges of Tathāgatas. In a kalpa named Supreme Excursion, I made offerings to sixty times the sands of the Ganges of Tathāgatas. In a kalpa named Wondrous Moon, I made offerings to seventy times the sands of the Ganges of Tathāgatas. Good son! Thus recollecting kalpas as numerous as the sands of the Ganges, I have never abandoned any Buddha, Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha. From each and every one of those Tathāgatas, I heard this bodhisattva liberation of Unobstructed Mindfulness of Purity and Adornment. I received, upheld, cultivated, and practiced it without interruption, following and entering into it accordingly. All the Tathāgatas of those past kalpas, from when they first were bodhisattvas until the Dharma was exhausted, all their divine transformations, through the power of this liberation of Purity and Adornment, I follow in recollection: they are clearly present before me; I uphold and practice them in accord, without ever slackening. Good son! I only know this Unobstructed Mindfulness of Purity Liberation. As for the bodhisattva-mahāsattvas who have left the long night of birth and death, who are brilliantly clear and far from the darkness of delusion, never drowsy, whose minds are free of all coverings, whose bodies walk in ease, who clearly understand the nature of all dharmas, who have accomplished the ten powers and awaken all beings, how could I know or describe their meritorious practices? Good son! In the city of Kapilavastu, there is a boys' tutor named Sarvasattva-mitra. Go to him and ask: 'How does a bodhisattva study the bodhisattva's practice and cultivate the bodhisattva's path?'"

Then the youth Sudhana, his body and mind joyful from hearing the Dharma, with inconceivable good roots streaming forth and expanding, prostrated reverently at the feet of Devaprabha, circumambulated her countless times, gazed at her with longing, and departed. Descending from the celestial palace, he gradually approached that city. Arriving at Sarvasattva-mitra's place, he paid homage at his feet, circumambulated him, joined his palms respectfully, stood to one side, and said: "Noble one! I have already generated the aspiration for anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi, but I do not yet know how a bodhisattva studies the bodhisattva's practice and cultivates the bodhisattva's path. I have heard that the noble one gives excellent instruction. I pray you will teach me."

Sarvasattva-mitra answered: "Good son! Here there is a youth named Śilpa-abhijña, who studies the bodhisattva's letter-wisdom. You may ask him, and he will explain it for you."

Then Sudhana immediately went to him, prostrated reverently, stood to one side, and said: "Noble one! I have already generated the aspiration for anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi, but I do not yet know how a bodhisattva studies the bodhisattva's practice and cultivates the bodhisattva's path. I have heard that the noble one gives excellent instruction. I pray you will teach me."

Then the youth told Sudhana: "Good son! I have attained a bodhisattva liberation called Skillful Knowledge of All Arts. I constantly chant and uphold the root syllables that enter this liberation. When I chant the syllable *a*, I enter a prajñāpāramitā gate called the Distinct Realm of Each Bodhisattva's Awesome Virtue. When I chant the syllable *ra*, I enter a prajñāpāramitā gate called Equality of One Taste, Supreme and Boundless. When I chant the syllable *pa*, I enter a prajñāpāramitā gate called the Dharma-Realm Without Differentiated Marks. When I chant the syllable *ca*, I enter a prajñāpāramitā gate called the Universal Wheel Cutting Off Differentiation. When I chant the syllable *ta*, I enter a prajñāpāramitā gate called Attainment of the Unsupported Supreme. When I chant the syllable *la*, I enter a prajñāpāramitā gate called Freed from Support, Without Defilement. When I chant the syllable *ḍa*, I enter a prajñāpāramitā gate called the Non-Retrogressing Practice. When I chant the syllable *ba*, I enter a prajñāpāramitā gate called the Vajra Ground. When I chant the syllable *ḍa*, I enter a prajñāpāramitā gate called the Universal Wheel. When I chant the syllable *ṣa*, I enter a prajñāpāramitā gate called the Ocean Treasury. When I chant the syllable *ta*, I enter a prajñāpāramitā gate called Universally Arising and Abiding. When I chant the syllable *na*, I enter a prajñāpāramitā gate called the Fullness of Light. When I chant the syllable *ya*, I enter a prajñāpāramitā gate called Differentiated Accumulations. When I chant the syllable *ṣṭa*, I enter a prajñāpāramitā gate called Universal Light Extinguishing All Afflictions. When I chant the syllable *ka*, I enter a prajñāpāramitā gate called Differentiation of One Taste. When I chant the syllable *va*, I enter a prajñāpāramitā gate called Pouring Forth the Dharma Rain. When I chant the syllable *ma*, I enter a prajñāpāramitā gate called the Great Torrent Surging with Massed Peaks Standing Equal. When I chant the syllable *ga*, I enter a prajñāpāramitā gate called Universally Supreme and Established. When I chant the syllable *stha*, I enter a prajñāpāramitā gate called the Treasury of Tathatā, Universally Equal. When I chant the syllable *ja*, I enter a prajñāpāramitā gate called Entering the Ocean of the World in Purity. When I chant the syllable *śca*, I enter a prajñāpāramitā gate called the Right Mindfulness Adornment of All Buddhas. When I chant the syllable *da*, I enter a prajñāpāramitā gate called Observing the Fully Accomplished Dharma-Gathering. When I chant the syllable *śa*, I enter a prajñāpāramitā gate called the Instructional Wheel-Light of All Buddhas. When I chant the syllable *kha*, I enter a prajñāpāramitā gate called the Purified Causal Ground Manifesting the Wisdom Treasury. When I chant the syllable *kṣa*, I enter a prajñāpāramitā gate called Extinguishing All Karmic Oceans, Treasury of Aggregates. When I chant the syllable *sta*, I enter a prajñāpāramitā gate called Dispelling All Afflictive Obstructions, Opening Pure Light. When I chant the syllable *ña*, I enter a prajñāpāramitā gate called Becoming the Cause of the World's Awakening. When I chant the syllable *pha*, I enter a prajñāpāramitā gate called the Wisdom Wheel Cutting Off Birth and Death. When I chant the syllable *va*, I enter a prajñāpāramitā gate called All Palaces Fully Adorned. When I chant the syllable *ca*, I enter a prajñāpāramitā gate called Cultivating the Precept Treasury, Each Fully Accomplished. When I chant the syllable *sma*, I enter a prajñāpāramitā gate called Following the Ten Directions in Seeing All Buddhas Present. When I chant the syllable *hva*, I enter a prajñāpāramitā gate called Observing All Beings Without Affinity, Skillfully Gathering Them and Causing Them to Enter the Ocean Treasury. When I chant the syllable *ha*, I enter a prajñāpāramitā gate called Cultivating and Entering the Ocean of All Merits. When I chant the syllable *ga*, I enter a prajñāpāramitā gate called Upholding All Dharma-Clouds, the Firm Ocean Treasury. When I chant the syllable *ṭa*, I enter a prajñāpāramitā gate called the Buddhas of the Ten Directions Appearing According to Vows. When I chant the syllable *ṇa*, I enter a prajñāpāramitā gate called the Unmoving Syllable Wheel Gathering Billions of Syllables. When I chant the syllable *spha*, I enter a prajñāpāramitā gate called the Ultimate Destination of Transforming Beings. When I chant the syllable *ska*, I enter a prajñāpāramitā gate called All Grounds Fulfilled, Unattached, Unobstructed Wisdom-Light Wheel Pervading All. When I chant the syllable *ja*, I enter a prajñāpāramitā gate called Proclaiming the Realm of All Buddha-Dharmas. When I chant the syllable *tsa*, I enter a prajñāpāramitā gate called the Dharma-Thunder of All Empty Space Universally Roaring. When I chant the syllable *ṭha*, I enter a prajñāpāramitā gate called Awakening Those of Deluded Consciousness to the Lamp of Non-Self. When I chant the syllable *dha*, I enter a prajñāpāramitā gate called the Treasury of the Arising of All Dharma-Wheels. Good son! When I chant these root syllables that enter this liberation, these forty-two prajñāpāramitā gates are the forerunners, entering immeasurable, innumerable prajñāpāramitā gates. Good son! I only know this bodhisattva liberation of Skillful Knowledge of All Arts. As for the bodhisattva-mahāsattvas, who in all worldly and supramundane skillful dharmas penetrate to the other shore through wisdom, who comprehensively master all arts and specialized skills without remainder, who deeply understand writing, mathematics, and all forms of learning, who are skilled in medicine and spells and can cure all diseases, who can rescue all beings possessed by ghosts, cursed by enemies, afflicted by maleficent stars, chased by walking corpses, or suffering from epilepsy, wasting, and all manner of illness, and heal them completely; who also skillfully distinguish the places where gold, jade, pearls, shells, coral, lapis lazuli, maṇi, and various treasures are found, knowing their differing qualities and values, and who know the villages, townships, great and small cities, palaces, parks, cliffs, springs, and marshes, and all places where people dwell, and the bodhisattva can protect them all in every region; who also skillfully observe the patterns of heaven and earth, the auspicious and inauspicious signs of human physiognomy, the sounds of birds and beasts, the appearances of clouds and mists, whether the year's harvest will be abundant or lean, whether the country will be safe or in peril, and have mastered all such worldly arts and traced them to their source; who also can distinguish supramundane dharmas, correctly naming and defining them, observing their essence and character, following and practicing them accordingly, entering them through wisdom, without doubt, without obstruction, without darkness, without dullness, without worry, without sinking, with nothing they do not directly realize: how could I know or describe their meritorious practices? Good son! In this land of Magadha, there is a settlement. In it there is a city named Bhāṭanā. There is an upāsikā named Bhadrottamā. Go to her and ask: 'How does a bodhisattva study the bodhisattva's practice and cultivate the bodhisattva's path?'"

Then the youth Sudhana prostrated reverently at the feet of Śilpa-abhijña, circumambulated him countless times, gazed at him with longing, and departed. He went toward the settlement and the city, and arriving at Bhadrottamā's place, he paid homage at her feet, circumambulated her, joined his palms respectfully, stood to one side, and said: "Noble one! I have already generated the aspiration for anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi, but I do not yet know how a bodhisattva studies the bodhisattva's practice and cultivates the bodhisattva's path. I have heard that the noble one gives excellent instruction. I pray you will teach me."

Bhadrottamā answered: "Good son! I have attained a bodhisattva dharma-gate called the Bodhimaṇḍa Without Dependence. Having understood it myself, I also teach it to others. I have also attained the Inexhaustible Samādhi. It is not that the dharma of that samādhi is either exhaustible or inexhaustible. It is because it can produce the inexhaustible eye of all-knowing wisdom, because it can produce the inexhaustible ear of all-knowing wisdom, because it can produce the inexhaustible nose of all-knowing wisdom, because it can produce the inexhaustible tongue of all-knowing wisdom, because it can produce the inexhaustible body of all-knowing wisdom, because it can produce the inexhaustible mind of all-knowing wisdom, because it can produce the inexhaustible manifold wisdom-radiance of all-knowing wisdom, because it can produce the inexhaustible pervasive spiritual powers of all-knowing wisdom, because it can produce the inexhaustible immeasurable merits like the waves of the ocean of all-knowing wisdom, and because it can produce the inexhaustible light pervading the world of all-knowing wisdom. Good son! I only know this dharma-gate of the Bodhimaṇḍa Without Dependence. As for the bodhisattva-mahāsattvas' meritorious practices of complete non-attachment, how could I fully know or describe them? Good son! To the south there is a city named Fertile Field. There is an elder there named Dṛḍhādhyāśaya. You may go and ask him: 'How does a bodhisattva study the bodhisattva's practice and cultivate the bodhisattva's path?'"

Then Sudhana paid homage at Bhadrottamā's feet, circumambulated her countless times, gazed at her with longing, and departed southward. Arriving at that city, he went to the elder's place, paid homage at his feet, circumambulated him, joined his palms respectfully, stood to one side, and said: "Noble one! I have already generated the aspiration for anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi, but I do not yet know how a bodhisattva studies the bodhisattva's practice and cultivates the bodhisattva's path. I have heard that the noble one gives excellent instruction. I pray you will teach me."

The elder answered: "Good son! I have attained a bodhisattva liberation called Unattached Pure Mindfulness. Since I attained this liberation, my Dharma-vows have been fulfilled. Before the Buddhas of the ten directions, I have nothing further to seek. Good son! I only know this Pure Mindfulness Liberation. As for the bodhisattva-mahāsattvas who have attained the fearless great lion's roar and abide in the vast and lofty accumulation of merit and wisdom, how could I know or describe their meritorious practices? Good son! Right here in this city, there is an elder named Sūryaprabha, whose house is always filled with light. Go to him and ask: 'How does a bodhisattva study the bodhisattva's practice and cultivate the bodhisattva's path?'"

Then the youth Sudhana paid homage at Dṛḍhādhyāśaya's feet, circumambulated him countless times, and departed. He went to Sūryaprabha's place, paid homage at his feet, circumambulated him, joined his palms respectfully, stood to one side, and said: "Noble one! I have already generated the aspiration for anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi, but I do not yet know how a bodhisattva studies the bodhisattva's practice and cultivates the bodhisattva's path. I have heard that the noble one gives excellent instruction. I pray you will teach me."

Sūryaprabha answered: "Good son! I have attained a bodhisattva liberation called Pure Wisdom-Light. Good son! I only know this Wisdom-Light Liberation. As for the bodhisattva-mahāsattvas who have realized immeasurable liberation-gates, how could I know or describe their meritorious practices? Good son! To the south of here there is a city named Born of Emergence. There is an elder there named Ajitasena. Go to him and ask: 'How does a bodhisattva study the bodhisattva's practice and cultivate the bodhisattva's path?'"

Then Sudhana paid homage at Sūryaprabha's feet, circumambulated him countless times, gazed at him with longing, and departed. Gradually going toward that city, he arrived at the elder's place, paid homage at his feet, circumambulated him, joined his palms respectfully, stood to one side, and said: "Noble one! I have already generated the aspiration for anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi, but I do not yet know how a bodhisattva studies the bodhisattva's practice and cultivates the bodhisattva's path. I have heard that the noble one gives excellent instruction. I pray you will teach me."

The elder answered: "Good son! I have attained a bodhisattva liberation called the Inexhaustible Mark. Through realizing this bodhisattva liberation, I see immeasurable Buddhas and attain the inexhaustible treasury. Good son! I only know this Inexhaustible Mark Liberation. As for the bodhisattva-mahāsattvas who have attained limitless wisdom and unobstructed eloquence, how could I know or describe their meritorious practices? Good son! To the south of this city, there is a settlement named Dharma. In that settlement there is a brahmin named Śibi-uttama. Go to him and ask: 'How does a bodhisattva study the bodhisattva's practice and cultivate the bodhisattva's path?'"

Then the youth Sudhana paid homage at Ajitasena's feet, circumambulated him countless times, gazed at him with longing, and departed. Gradually journeying south, he arrived at that settlement, met Śibi-uttama, paid homage at his feet, circumambulated him, joined his palms respectfully, stood to one side, and said: "Noble one! I have already generated the aspiration for anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi, but I do not yet know how a bodhisattva studies the bodhisattva's practice and cultivates the bodhisattva's path. I have heard that the noble one gives excellent instruction. I pray you will teach me."

The brahmin answered: "Good son! I have attained a bodhisattva dharma-gate called the Words of Sincere Vows. The bodhisattvas of past, present, and future, through these words, up to the attainment of anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi, never retrogress, have never retrogressed, are not now retrogressing, and shall never retrogress. Good son! Because I abide in these words of sincere vows, whatever I wish to accomplish is all fulfilled. Good son! I only know this dharma-gate of Sincere Words. As for the bodhisattva-mahāsattvas, whose sincere vow-words are never at variance with their conduct, whose speech is always sincere and never false, and from whom immeasurable merits arise, how could I know or describe them? Good son! To the south of here there is a city named Wondrous Thought-Flower Gate. There are a youth named Sutejas and a maiden named Sutejā. Go to them and ask: 'How does a bodhisattva study the bodhisattva's practice and cultivate the bodhisattva's path?'"

Then the youth Sudhana, revering the Dharma, paid homage at the brahmin's feet, circumambulated him countless times, gazed at him with longing, and departed. Gradually journeying south, he arrived at that city, met the youth and the maiden, prostrated at their feet, and after circumambulating them, stood before them with palms joined and said: "Noble ones! I have already generated the aspiration for anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi, but I do not yet know how a bodhisattva studies the bodhisattva's practice and cultivates the bodhisattva's path. I humbly pray that you will compassionately teach me."

Then the youth and the maiden told Sudhana: "Good son! We have realized a bodhisattva liberation called Abiding in Illusion. Through this pure wisdom, we observe that all worlds abide in illusion, because they arise from causes and conditions. All sentient beings abide in illusion, because they arise from karmic afflictions. All dharmas abide in illusion, because they arise from the successive conditioning of ignorance, existence, and craving. All three realms abide in illusion, because they arise from inverted wisdom. The birth, aging, death, grief, and suffering of all sentient beings all abide in illusion, because they arise from false discrimination. All lands abide in illusion, because they are manifested by the inversion of perception, the inversion of mind, the inversion of views, and ignorance. All śrāvakas and pratyekabuddhas abide in illusion, because they are accomplished through the discrimination of wisdom and cessation. All bodhisattvas abide in illusion, because they are accomplished through the superior wisdom-mind of self-discipline and the teaching and transformation of sentient beings, together with their practices and vows. The assemblies, transformations, taming, and all undertakings of all bodhisattvas all abide in illusion, because they are gathered and accomplished through vows and wisdom. Good son! The intrinsic nature of the realm of illusion is inconceivable. Good son! We two only know this bodhisattva liberation. As for the bodhisattva-mahāsattvas who skillfully enter the boundless net of illusion of all affairs, their meritorious practices: how could we know or describe them?" When the youth and maiden had explained their own liberation, through the inconceivable power of their good roots, Sudhana's body became soft, supple, and radiant.
