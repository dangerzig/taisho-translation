# 佛說馬有三相經
## Sūtra Spoken by the Buddha on the Three Marks of a Horse

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 114

Translated from the Chinese. Eastern Han, translated by Zhī Yào (支曜)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was dwelling in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍada's Park, in the country of Śrāvastī. The Buddha then addressed the bhikṣus:

"A fine horse has three marks. When it enters royal service, it can serve as a mount and please the king's mind, and it earns the name of a royal horse. What are the three marks? First, a fine horse has good spirit and can run on its own; second, it has strength; third, it has an upright and handsome appearance. These are the three marks by which a fine horse enters royal service.

"A good person also has three marks. Having achieved a good mind, one's fame is illustrious; all people hold one in high esteem and find one admirable; one can receive the reverence of the people and bring blessings to the world. What are the three marks? A good person with an achieved mind: can run, has strength, and has an upright appearance.

"What is meant by a good person with an achieved mind being able to run? A good person with an achieved mind knows suffering as it truly is, knows its arising, knows its complete cessation, and knows the path. In this way, the good person with an achieved mind runs.

"What is meant by a good person with an achieved mind having strength? Whatever harmful and evil tendencies exist, one works with full effort to cut them off; striving diligently, one's mind abandons evil; harmful evil tendencies not yet arisen one prevents from arising; wholesome intentions not yet arisen one brings forth; wholesome intentions already arisen one sustains without diminishment, gradually increasing them; in conduct and mind alike, one pursues virtue with full effort; one controls the mind and abandons evil. In this way, the good person with an achieved mind attains strength.

"What is meant by a good person with an achieved mind having an upright appearance? A good person with an achieved mind avoids the five pleasures and avoids harmful evils, and is able to attain the four right strivings and practice them. In this way, the good person with an achieved mind has an upright appearance.

"These are the three causal conditions for genuine practice. The good person with an achieved mind: their fame spreads, and they become a leader whom all people hold in high regard and find admirable; they can receive the reverence of all under heaven. It is thus."

The Buddha spoke thus.
