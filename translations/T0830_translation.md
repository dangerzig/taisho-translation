# 大乘遍照光明藏無字法門經
## Mahāyāna Sūtra of the Universal Illumination Radiance Treasury, the Dharma-Gate Without Letters

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0830

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, retranslated by Divākara (地婆訶羅)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was in Rājagṛha, on Mount Gṛdhrakūṭa, together with great bodhisattvas and bhikṣu-monks numbering countless hundreds of thousands of billions of nayutas. All those bodhisattvas had attained great wisdom and skillful means. They could penetrate the Dharma-treasury without letters, possessed the eloquence of joyful explanation, and did not contravene conventional or ultimate truth. Courageous and diligent, they had forever left behind the hindrances and entanglements. Having tamed their faculties, they had no attachment. They cherished sentient beings as they would an only child. They treasured true wisdom as they would a great island of jewels. Conscience and modesty were their body, and concentration and wisdom were their leaders. They took great compassion as their fundamental nature. They knew wholesome and unwholesome dharmas, real and unreal dharmas. They illuminated the twofold emptiness and abided on the supreme, wondrous ground. Having attained great renown, they rested in eternal peace. They were resolute in cultivating the supreme Dharma. Having forever left behind the lowly body born of the womb, they manifested birth to guard and protect the lands. All their deeds were universally worthy and good. Transcending the three realms, they could rescue those in the three realms. Their practice was pure, and they well understood self and other. All had attained such merit as this. Their names were: the Bodhisattva Superior Thought, the Bodhisattva Superior Course of Practice, the Bodhisattva Wondrous Sound, the Bodhisattva Beautiful Sound, the Bodhisattva Eloquence-Possessor, the Bodhisattva Eloquence-Accumulator, the Bodhisattva Pearl-Topknot, the Bodhisattva Thousand-Spoked, the Bodhisattva Dharma-Felloe, the Bodhisattva Dharma Sound, the Bodhisattva Lotus Face, the Bodhisattva Lotus Eye, the Bodhisattva Earth-Holder, the Bodhisattva World-Holder, and the Bodhisattva Whose Voice Pervades the Great Earth. These and other bodhisattva mahāsattvas, all with the appearance of youths, were the foremost in this assembly, each together with their own retinue.

At that time the Bodhisattva Avalokiteśvara was surrounded by countless bodhisattvas who had been anointed for the position of crown prince. The Bodhisattva Mahāsthāmaprāpta was surrounded by countless billions of great Brahmā hosts. The Bodhisattva Superior Thought was surrounded by countless bodhisattvas and the lord Śakra. The Bodhisattva Ākāśagarbha was surrounded by countless hosts of the four heavenly kings. The Bodhisattva Known-to-All was surrounded by countless attendant women. The Bodhisattva Samantabhadra, the Bodhisattva Free-from-Doubt, the Bodhisattva Amoghadarśin, the Bodhisattva Who-Extinguishes-All-Hindrances, and the immeasurably skillful Bodhisattva Medicine-King and Bodhisattva Supreme-Medicine, each were surrounded by countless assemblies of bodhisattvas. The Elder Śāriputra, Mahāmaudgalyāyana, Mahākāśyapa, and the others were each surrounded by all the great arhats. From all the worlds of the ten directions, as numerous as the sands of the Ganges, the celestial sons of the sun and moon each came with their radiance to the Buddha's place. By the Buddha's spiritual power, their individual radiance could not shine, like a heap of ink beside Jambudvīpa gold. And countless Nārāyaṇa devas, the Water-Deva, the Nāga King Takṣaka, the Nāga King Anavatapta, and others were also surrounded by their retinues. The Gandharva King Beautiful Sound was also surrounded by countless gandharvas. The Garuḍa King Without-Turbidity was surrounded by seven billion garuḍa kings and their retinues. And from all the worlds of the ten directions, as numerous as the sands of the Ganges, all the bodhisattvas each made requests of their own Buddhas. Together with their retinues, they came to this Sahā world, bringing various supreme, excellent offerings surpassing anything in the world. Having made their offerings to the Buddha and the bodhisattvas, each bowed at the Buddha's feet, then stood to one side, and sat upon lotus-flower seats, gazing up at the World-Honored One.

At that time the Bodhisattva Mahāsattva Superior Thought rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, placed his right knee on the ground, joined his palms toward the Buddha, and said: "World-Honored One! For the sake of the fourfold assembly, I wish to ask the Tathāgata about the meaning of two words. We pray the Tathāgata will explain them for me, so that we and the rest may all gain benefit."

At that time the World-Honored One told the Bodhisattva Superior Thought: "Good man! Does the Tathāgata appear in the world for the sake of a single sentient being? No, it is for the benefit of countless sentient beings that he appears in the world. Good man! You are now able, for the sake of the fourfold assembly, to ask me about the meaning of two words. Ask as you please, and I shall explain for you."

Thereupon the Bodhisattva Superior Thought, receiving the Buddha's permission, addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! What dharma should the bodhisattva mahāsattva eliminate and what should he protect? And further, what dharma has the Tathāgata fully realized and awakened to? We pray you will explain these two meanings."

At that time the Buddha praised Superior Thought: "Excellent, excellent! Good man! You have already accomplished immeasurable merit and wisdom. Furthermore, empowered by the Tathāgata's spiritual authority, you are able to ask me about such a meaning. Listen carefully, listen carefully! Reflect well upon it! I shall analyze and explain it for you.

"Good man! There is one kind of dharma that the bodhisattva mahāsattva should eliminate. What is this one dharma? It is desire and craving. This one dharma is what the bodhisattva mahāsattva should eliminate. Good man! There is another dharma that the bodhisattva mahāsattva should eliminate. What is this one dharma? It is anger. This one dharma is what the bodhisattva mahāsattva should eliminate. Good man! There is another dharma that the bodhisattva mahāsattva should eliminate. What is this one dharma? It is delusion. This one dharma is what the bodhisattva mahāsattva should eliminate. Good man! There is another dharma that the bodhisattva mahāsattva should eliminate. What is this one dharma? It is grasping at a self. This one dharma is what the bodhisattva mahāsattva should eliminate. Good man! There is another dharma that the bodhisattva mahāsattva should eliminate. What is this one dharma? It is laziness. This one dharma is what the bodhisattva mahāsattva should eliminate. Good man! There is another dharma that the bodhisattva mahāsattva should eliminate. What is this one dharma? It is torpor and drowsiness. This one dharma is what the bodhisattva mahāsattva should eliminate. Good man! There is another dharma that the bodhisattva mahāsattva should eliminate. What is this one dharma? It is craving and attachment. This one dharma is what the bodhisattva mahāsattva should eliminate. Good man! There is another dharma that the bodhisattva mahāsattva should eliminate. What is this one dharma? It is doubt and confusion. This one dharma is what the bodhisattva mahāsattva should eliminate. Good man! There is another dharma that the bodhisattva mahāsattva should eliminate. What is this one dharma? It is ignorance. This one dharma is what the bodhisattva mahāsattva should eliminate. Good man! All such dharmas as listed above are what the bodhisattva mahāsattva should eliminate.

"Good man! You asked me: 'What further dharma should the bodhisattva mahāsattva protect?' Now I shall explain it for you. Good man! There is one kind of dharma that the bodhisattva mahāsattva should constantly protect. What is this one dharma? It is this: do not inflict upon others what you yourself do not desire. This one dharma is what the bodhisattva mahāsattva should constantly protect. Why? If the bodhisattva mahāsattva protects this dharma, he thereby protects all the precept-treasuries of all the Buddhas, the Tathāgatas. Just as all bodhisattvas, cherishing their own lives, should not kill; valuing their own wealth, should not steal; treasuring their own spouses, should not violate others; cherishing truthful speech, should not deceive others; cherishing harmony, should not sow discord; cherishing uprightness, should not speak deviously or frivolously; cherishing gentleness, should not speak harsh words; cherishing contentment, should not generate covetousness toward others; cherishing kindness and forbearance, should not generate anger toward others; cherishing right views, should not lead others to generate wrong views. Good man! Such bodhisattvas generate this intention and say: 'I now respectfully follow the Tathāgata's right teaching and should diligently guard and protect this dharma in my mind.' This is called the one dharma that the bodhisattva mahāsattva should protect. Good man! I see all such bodhisattvas who seek the unsurpassed great bodhi: all of them seek bodhi for the sake of happiness; there is none who seeks it for the sake of suffering. Good man! For this reason I teach: do not inflict upon others what you yourself do not desire. All such dharmas are what the bodhisattva mahāsattva should protect."

At that time the Bodhisattva Superior Thought again addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! What dharma has the Tathāgata fully realized and awakened to? We pray you will explain the meaning of this for us."

The Buddha said: "Good man! There is not a single dharma that the Tathāgata has awakened to or realized. Why? Among all dharmas, there is no awakening and no realization: this is the Tathāgata's realization and awakening. Good man! That all dharmas are originally without arising is what the Tathāgata has awakened to and realized. That all dharmas are originally without ceasing is what the Tathāgata has awakened to and realized. That the nature of all dharmas is apart from the two extremes is what the Tathāgata has awakened to and realized. That all dharmas are originally without reality is what the Tathāgata has awakened to and realized.

"Furthermore, good man! All dharmas arise from the power of the causes and conditions of one's own karma. Yet these causes and conditions abide not even for an instant, like a flash of lightning. Such are the karmic conditions that the Tathāgata has awakened to and realized. Therefore I teach: because of causes and conditions, dharmas arise; because of causes and conditions, dharmas cease. Apart from causes and conditions, there is no karmic retribution. Such matters as these are what the Tathāgata has awakened to. Good man! The nature of all dharmas thus awakened to is called the 'universal illumination radiance treasury.' Good man! Why is the dharma-nature called a 'treasury'? Because the worldly and world-transcending wisdom of all sentient beings arises in dependence upon this treasury. When one contemplates that dharma-nature with true wisdom, wisdom arises in dependence upon it: therefore it is called a 'treasury.'

"Furthermore, good man! I also teach that all dharmas are like an illusion, like a mirage, like the moon reflected in water: such matters as these are what the Tathāgata has awakened to and realized. Also, good man, the one-taste liberation that is the nature and marks of all dharmas: such matters as these are what the Tathāgata has awakened to and realized. Good man! Such is the one-taste liberation of the dharma-nature: this is what is called the 'universal illumination radiance treasury.'

"Furthermore, good man! There is yet another dharma that the Tathāgata has awakened to and realized. What is this one dharma? It is that all dharmas neither arise nor cease, neither increase nor decrease, neither come nor go, are neither grasped nor rejected, and are neither cause nor condition. Such dharmas as these are what the Tathāgata has awakened to and realized.

"Furthermore, good man! The Tathāgata knows and understands that all dharmas are without intrinsic nature, cannot be expressed through metaphor, and cannot be discussed through words and phrases. Such dharmas as these are what the Tathāgata has awakened to and realized. Good man! All such dharmas as listed above are what the Tathāgata has realized and awakened to."

When the Buddha taught this Dharma of the Universal Illumination Radiance Treasury, the Dharma-Gate Without Letters, bodhisattvas as numerous as dust-motes attained the tenth ground. Again, countless bodhisattvas abided on the various grounds. Again, countless bodhisattvas realized hundreds of thousands of great samādhis. Again, sentient beings as numerous as dust-motes gave rise to the aspiration for anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi. Again, countless sentient beings attained the fruit of the arhat. Again, countless sentient beings were freed from the various sufferings of the hells, hungry ghosts, and animals, and born among humans and devas to receive supreme, wondrous joy. All in the assembly gained benefit without exception; not a single person passed through in vain.

At that time the Buddha told Rāhula: "Good man! You should receive and uphold this essential Dharma of mine."

At that time ninety billion bodhisattva mahāsattvas in the assembly, hearing these words and empowered by the Buddha's spiritual authority, addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! We vow that in the Sahā world in the future age, when we see anyone capable of being a vessel of the Dharma, we shall teach this sūtra for them. May the World-Honored One have no concern."

At that time the four heavenly kings again addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! If in the future there are good men and good women able to receive and uphold this sūtra, we shall guard and protect them and cause all their wishes to be fulfilled. Why? Because such good men and good women who can uphold this sūtra are vessels of the Dharma."

At that time the World-Honored One, seeing that the ninety billion bodhisattvas and the four heavenly kings had made such requests, spoke thus: "Good men! This Dharma-gate of the Universal Illumination Radiance Treasury Without Letters that I have now taught, from the time I attained Buddhahood until now I had never taught. Today I teach it for all of you. Good men! Those sentient beings of the future who are able to hear this rare Dharma-gate: you should know that such persons have long since accomplished immeasurable merit and wisdom. You should know that such persons thereby serve and make offerings to me. You should know that such persons thereby carry the burden of the Buddha's great bodhi. You should know that such persons will certainly attain unobstructed eloquence. You should know that such persons will certainly attain a pure Buddha-land. You should know that at the time of death such persons will certainly see Amitābha Buddha, surrounded by a great assembly of bodhisattvas, manifesting before them. You should know that such persons constantly see my body on Mount Gṛdhrakūṭa and see this assembly of bodhisattvas. You should know that such persons have already obtained the inexhaustible Dharma-treasury. You should know that such persons have attained the knowledge of past lives. You should know that such persons will not fall into the evil destinies.

"Furthermore, good men! I now teach this unprecedented Dharma. If in the future there are good men and good women who, even if they have committed the five heinous crimes, upon hearing this Dharma-gate, are able to write it, uphold it, read and recite it, and explain it for others, or urge others to write it, uphold it, read and recite it, and explain it: I see that such persons will not fall into the evil destinies. All their affliction-obstructions, karmic obstructions, and retribution-obstructions will be entirely purified. In future lives such persons will possess the five eyes. Such persons will be anointed by all the Buddhas. Such persons will be guarded and remembered by all the Buddhas, World-Honored Ones, and by all the bodhisattvas. Wherever such persons are born in future lives, their faculties will be complete, without any deficiency."

When the Buddha had finished teaching this sūtra, the Bodhisattva Mahāsattva Superior Thought and the other bodhisattvas, the bhikṣus, the devas, nāgas, and the eightfold assembly, having heard what the Buddha taught, all rejoiced greatly, believed, received it, and practiced accordingly.
