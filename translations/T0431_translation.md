# 八佛名號經
## Sūtra of the Eight Buddhas' Names

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0431

Translated from the Chinese. Sui dynasty, translated by Jñānagupta (闍那崛多)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the country of Śrāvastī, in the Jetavana grove of Anāthapiṇḍada's park, together with one thousand two hundred and fifty great bhikṣu monks, and also ten thousand Great Vehicle bodhisattva-mahāsattvas.

At that time, the World-Honored One, surrounded by an immeasurable, countless multitude of hundreds of thousands of myriads, was teaching the Dharma.

At that time, the Venerable Śāriputra, in the midst of the great assembly, rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, placed his right knee on the ground, joined his palms toward the Buddha, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I have a question in my mind and now wish to ask it. May the Tathāgata, with compassion for all beings, explain it for me."

At that time, the World-Honored One told Śāriputra: "Good man! Ask whatever you wish, and if there is anything you doubt, I shall explain it for you in full."

Then Śāriputra, having received the Buddha's seal of approval and permission to resolve his doubts, joyful and elated, addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! Are there presently Buddhas, World-Honored Ones, in the worlds of the ten directions, who by the power of their past vows constantly teach the Dharma for the sake of beings? If good men and good women wish to recite and uphold these Buddhas' names, whether reading, hearing, or copying them, can the merit from this cause them to attain non-retrogression from anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi, without deficiency or diminishment, and enable them to swiftly attain anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi along the path of unsurpassed truth?"

At that time, the World-Honored One told Śāriputra: "Excellent! Excellent! Good man! The question you have raised is truly of wondrous eloquence, creating vast benefit for immeasurable beings of the future, with compassion and pity for all devas and humans, and able to ask the Tathāgata about such profound meaning. Therefore, listen carefully and receive it well, and reflect upon it with care! I shall explain it for you in full."

Śāriputra said: "Indeed, World-Honored One! I gladly wish to hear."

At that time, the World-Honored One told Śāriputra: "Good man! In the east, past one Ganges-sand of worlds from here, there is a buddha-land called Hard to Overcome. In that land there is a Buddha, World-Honored One, named Excellent Speech, Renowned Merit Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, teaching the wondrous Dharma for the great assembly.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra! In the east from here, past two Ganges-sands of worlds, there is a buddha-land called Unobstructed. In that land there is a Buddha, World-Honored One, named Indra-Marked Banner Star King Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, also teaching the wondrous Dharma for the great assembly.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra! In the east from here, past three Ganges-sands of worlds, there is a buddha-land called Beloved Delight. In that land there is a Buddha, World-Honored One, named Universal Radiance Merit Adornment Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, also teaching the wondrous Dharma for the great assembly.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra! In the east from here, past four Ganges-sands of worlds, there is a buddha-land called Universal Entry. In that land there is a Buddha, World-Honored One, named Excellent in Battle, Hard to Overcome, Transcending Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, also teaching the wondrous Dharma for the great assembly.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra! In the east from here, past five Ganges-sands of worlds, there is a buddha-land called Pure Assembly. In that land there is a Buddha, World-Honored One, named Universal Merit Radiance Adornment Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, also teaching the wondrous Dharma for the great assembly.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra! In the east from here, past six Ganges-sands of worlds, there is a buddha-land called Without Poison Lord. In that land there is a Buddha, World-Honored One, named Unobstructed Medicine Tree Merit Renown Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, also teaching the wondrous Dharma for the great assembly.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra! In the east from here, past seven Ganges-sands of worlds, there is a buddha-land called Brimming with Fragrance. In that land there is a Buddha, World-Honored One, named Treading upon Precious Lotuses Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, also teaching the wondrous Dharma for the great assembly.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra! In the east from here, past eight Ganges-sands of worlds, there is a buddha-land called Wondrous Sound Radiance. In that land there is a Buddha, World-Honored One, named Precious Lotus Excellently Abiding Śāla Tree King Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, also teaching the wondrous Dharma for the great assembly.

"Śāriputra! These Buddhas, Tathāgatas, Arhats, Samyaksaṃbuddhas: their buddha-lands are pure and without defilement, without the five turbidities, without the five desires. The beings therein have no minds of flattery, deception, or falsity, nor any conduct of desire, nor any women.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra! If good men and good women hear the names of these Buddhas, World-Honored Ones, and having heard them, can receive, uphold, read, recite, and practice them, and also proclaim and explain them for others, that such good men and good women should fall into the three evil paths is impossible, except for those who commit the five acts of immediate retribution, slander the correct Dharma, or slander sages.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra! If good men and good women hear the names of these Buddhas, World-Honored Ones, and having heard them, can receive, uphold, read, recite, and practice them, and also proclaim and explain them for others, such persons, from now until they attain bodhi, in every life where they are born, will always possess the five supernatural powers, and moreover will attain all dhāraṇī. Their six sense-faculties will be complete without any deficiency, and they will always experience joy, with their body-hair turning rightward.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra! If good men and good women hear the names of these Buddhas, World-Honored Ones, and having heard them, can receive, uphold, read, recite, and practice them, and also proclaim and explain them for others, such beings, wherever they are, will not be taken advantage of by magistrates or evil thieves. Fire cannot burn them, water cannot sweep them away, and evil nāgas and evil serpents cannot poison or harm them. Whether walking or standing, that lions, tigers, wolves, bears, jackals, leopards, yakṣas, rākṣasas, evil ghosts and spirits, kumbhāṇḍas, humans or non-humans could cause them fright is impossible, except as a consequence of past karma.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra! If there are women who can become weary of their bodies, focus their minds with single-pointed concentration, and receive, uphold, read, and recite the names of these World-Honored Buddhas, and also explain them in detail for others, that after their life ends they should again receive a female body is impossible.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra! If good men and good women, during the still of the night, can recite the names of these Buddhas, such beings, in their present life, will see all their endeavors swiftly accomplished and daily increasing, without any obstacles."

At that time, the World-Honored One, wishing to further clarify this meaning, spoke in verse:

> If there are beings
> Who can uphold these Buddhas' names,
> They will entirely abandon the evil paths
> And swiftly be born in good destinies.

> Always in the presence of the Buddhas,
> Constantly hearing the wondrous Dharma taught,
> Having beheld the Unsurpassed Honored Ones,
> They make offerings according to their hearts' desire.

> Through reciting and upholding the Buddhas' names,
> Surpassing billions of thousands of myriads of kalpas,
> All afflictions
> Are swiftly resolved, and they attain bodhi.

> If a person for seven full days
> Can recite these Buddhas' names,
> They will immediately attain the pure eye
> And be able to see the Buddhas.

> If there are those who hear the Buddhas' names
> And can immediately recite and uphold them,
> Wherever they are born,
> They will always be honored by others.

> With excellent marks and beautiful appearance,
> They are always born into blessed and joyful families.
> With joyful hearts they practice great generosity,
> Intelligent and without negligence.

> If there are women
> Who, through hearing these Buddhas' names,
> Recite them and teach them to others,
> Their merit is immeasurable.
> When this life is ended,
> They will certainly shed the female retribution.
> Attaining a male body,
> In every life they will always have sharp faculties.

> Through reciting and upholding the Buddhas' names,
> For many hundreds of billions of kalpas,
> Their breath will always be fragrant,
> Ever like the scent of candana.

> These great sages,
> Whose names are such:
> Those who can recite and uphold them
> Should be careful and not negligent.

> Through reciting and upholding the Buddhas' names,
> Parents and brothers
> And all other family members
> Will ultimately be free from unusual suffering.

> Through reciting and upholding the Buddhas' names,
> All the māra Pāpīyas
> And their dark retinues
> Will ultimately be unable to cause obstruction.

> Evil poisons cannot harm them;
> Swords, weapons, and fire,
> Magistrates and evil thieves:
> None can injure them.

> Through reciting and upholding the Buddhas' names,
> For thousands of billions of kalpas,
> They are always born upon precious lotus flowers,
> Complete in majestic marks and supernatural powers.

> Always in the sky above,
> Pervading inconceivable lands,
> They observe the various buddha-lands,
> Those among the lands that are pure.

> Having realized the unsurpassed path,
> They universally serve all the various beings,
> All devas and worldly humans,
> Able to be a refuge for them.

When the Buddha had finished teaching this sūtra, the Elder Śāriputra and all the devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, humans and non-humans, and the entire great assembly heard what the Buddha had taught, rejoiced, and faithfully practiced it.
