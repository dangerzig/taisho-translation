# 佛說勝幡瓔珞陀羅尼經
## Sūtra on the Dhāraṇī of the Victorious Banner and Garland Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1410

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dānapāla (施護).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was at the dwelling place of sages near the summit of Mount Prāmodya, not far from the heavenly palace, together with a great assembly of 1,250 bhikṣus. There were also bodhisattva-mahāsattvas: the youth Mañjuśrī, Bodhisattva Bhadrapāla, and the other sixteen great beings, all of whom came to the assembly.

At that time, the World-Honored One entered into proper concentration and observed the heavens above and the human world, wishing to proclaim the Dharma with equal generosity, so that sentient beings, having heard the Dharma, would praise its excellence and all be made to rejoice.

At that time, Mahābrahmā and the devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, humans, and non-humans also came to the assembly.

Then Mahābrahmā approached the place where the Buddha was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, joined his palms with reverence, and addressed the Buddha, saying: "World-Honored One! All sentient beings commit various kinds of evil karma. When their bodies perish and their lives end, they fall into the evil destinies: some fall into the realm of Yama, some into the realm of hungry ghosts, and some into the animal realm. Having fallen into these evil destinies, they suffer various kinds of pain, and their cycle through form and the rest is likewise. The World-Honored One's great compassion and immeasurable skillful means: may you provide expedient means to destroy the karmic fruits and retribution of all these sentient beings and enable them to attain liberation."

At that time, Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Avalokiteśvara, surrounded fore and aft by countless vidyādhara devas, also came to the assembly. Having reached the place where the Buddha was, he placed his five limbs on the ground, bowed at the Buddha's feet, joined his palms with reverence, and addressed the Buddha, saying: "World-Honored One, you of great majestic virtue, fully endowed with the ten powers: may you provide expedient and skillful means to rescue all sentient beings caught in the cycle of rebirth and enable them to escape."

The Buddha said: "Excellent! Good men! Listen carefully. I shall now proclaim for you and for all sentient beings of the future the Supreme Victorious Banner Vidyārāja. If anyone hears this name and is able to recite it, they shall obtain immeasurable and boundless merit and rewards." He then proclaimed the dhāraṇī:

*tadyathā kara kara kiri kiri kuru kuru sara sara śrī śrī suru suru sarva-buddhāvalokite vara vara dharma-mukhe cara cara saṃgha-tiṣṭhite bhara bhara aneka-buddha-koṭi-bhaṣite kṣaṇa kṣaṇa sarva-karmāvaraṇāni sarva-pāpāni sarva-duḥkhāni [ha]-panāya bhṛṣṭa bhṛṣṭa dhūrṣṭa dhūrṣṭa taṭa taṭa sphuṭaya sarva-karmāvaraṇa-[pāya]-durgatini sarva-sattvānāṃ ca svāhā*

"Good men! This Dhāraṇī of the Victorious Banner and Garland has been jointly proclaimed by buddhas as numerous as the sands of the Ganges, and I now proclaim it again for you. Good men! This dhāraṇī is rare and difficult to encounter. Just as it is difficult to encounter a tathāgata's appearance in the world, encountering this dhāraṇī is even more difficult by far. If good men or good women hear the name of this Dhāraṇī of the Victorious Banner and Garland, and with faithful hearts receive, uphold, and constantly recite it, all of that person's five grave transgressions will be entirely eradicated. They will obtain the visible reward of great wealth and prosperity, and in future lives will be born into noble families. Furthermore, if good men or good women, by the supreme merit of this dhāraṇī, broadly proclaim and disseminate it, explain it to others, or encourage others to copy, receive, uphold, and recite it, I shall know of it from afar. Vipaśyin Buddha, Śikhin Buddha, Viśvabhū Buddha, Krakucchanda Buddha, Kanakamuni Buddha, Kāśyapa Buddha, and all the tathāgatas, arhats, and perfectly awakened ones of the past, as numerous as the sands of the Ganges, shall all know of it. That person's merit is immeasurable and boundless."

At that time, Bodhisattva Avalokiteśvara, Bodhisattva Mañjuśrī, Bodhisattva Bhadrapāla, the great assembly of bhikṣus, and all the devas, humans, asuras, gandharvas, and non-humans, having heard what the Buddha proclaimed, were all greatly overjoyed and faithfully received and practiced it.
