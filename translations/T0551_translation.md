# 佛說摩鄧女經
## Sūtra of the Mātaṅgī Girl, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0551

Translated from the Chinese. Later Han dynasty, translated by An Shigao (安世高)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the land of Śrāvastī, at the Jetavana Monastery of Anāthapiṇḍada.

At that time, Ānanda took his bowl and went to beg for food. After eating, Ānanda was walking along the bank of a stream. He saw a young woman at the water's edge carrying water. Ānanda asked the woman for water, and she gave him some. The woman then followed Ānanda, observing where he was staying. She went home and told her mother, whose name was Mātaṅgī. At home the girl lay on the ground weeping. Her mother asked: "Why are you weeping?"

The girl said: "If mother wishes to arrange my marriage, do not give me to anyone else. At the water's edge I saw a śramaṇa who asked me for water. I asked his name; it is Ānanda. If I can have Ānanda, then I will marry. If mother cannot get him for me, I will not marry."

The mother went out to inquire about Ānanda. Ānanda served the Buddha. The mother learned this and returned to tell her daughter: "Ānanda follows the buddha-path and will not agree to be your husband."

The girl wept and refused to eat, saying: "Mother knows the arts of enchantment."

The mother went out and invited Ānanda to come for a meal. The girl was delighted. The mother said to Ānanda: "My daughter wishes to be your wife."

Ānanda said: "I keep the precepts. I may not take a wife."

She said: "If my daughter cannot have you as her husband, she will kill herself."

Ānanda said: "My teacher is the Buddha. I am not permitted to have relations with a woman."

The mother went inside and told the girl: "Ānanda will not agree to be your husband. He says that those who follow the path may not take a wife."

The girl wept before her mother and said: "Where is mother's power?"

The mother said: "Of all the powers in the world, none can surpass the buddha-path and the path of arhatship."

The Mātaṅgī girl said: "Just close the doors for me and do not let him leave. By evening he will become my husband."

The mother closed the doors and bound Ānanda with her enchantment.

At the hour of the late afternoon, the mother prepared a bed for the girl. The girl was delighted and adorned herself. Ānanda would not go to the bed. The mother caused fire to spring up from the ground in the courtyard, then seized Ānanda's robe and said: "If you will not be my daughter's husband, I shall throw you into the fire."

Ānanda was ashamed, thinking that he was a śramaṇa of the Buddha, yet today he was trapped and could not escape. The Buddha then used his supernatural power, knowing in his mind what had happened to Ānanda. Ānanda returned to the Buddha's dwelling and said: "Yesterday while begging for food, I saw a young woman at the water's edge and asked her for water. I returned to the Buddha's dwelling. The next day a woman named Mātaṅgī invited me to come for a meal. When I went, she seized me and tried to give me her daughter as a wife. I said: 'I keep the Buddha's precepts. I may not take a wife.'"

The girl, seeing that Ānanda had escaped, wept at home. The mother said: "Against those who serve the Buddha, my power cannot prevail. Did I not tell you so?"

The girl wept without ceasing, constantly thinking of Ānanda.

The next day the girl went out herself to look for Ānanda. She saw him again going on his alms-round. She followed behind him, gazing at his feet and gazing at his face. Ānanda was ashamed and avoided her, but the girl continued to follow without stopping. Ānanda returned to the Buddha's dwelling. The girl waited at the gate. Ānanda did not come out, and the girl went away weeping.

Ānanda addressed the Buddha: "The Mātaṅgī girl followed me again today."

The Buddha had her summoned. Seeing her, the Buddha asked: "You pursue Ānanda. What do you seek?"

The girl said: "I have heard that Ānanda has no wife, and I have no husband. I wish to be Ānanda's wife."

The Buddha said to the girl: "Ānanda is a śramaṇa and has no hair. You have hair. Are you willing to shave your head? I will have Ānanda be your husband."

The girl said: "I am willing to shave my head."

The Buddha said: "Go home and tell your mother. Shave your head and come back."

The girl returned to her mother and said: "Mother could not bring Ānanda to me. The Buddha said: 'Shave your head and come back. I will have Ānanda be your husband.'"

The mother said: "Child! I have raised and protected your hair since birth. Why would you become a śramaṇa's wife? There are great and wealthy families in this country. I myself can marry you to one of them."

The girl said: "In life and death I will be Ānanda's wife."

The mother said: "Why do you disgrace our family?"

The girl said: "If mother loves me, she should follow my heart's desire."

The mother wept and took up the blade, and shaved her daughter's hair.

The girl returned to the Buddha and said: "I have shaved my head."

The Buddha said: "What do you love about Ānanda?"

The girl said: "I love Ānanda's eyes. I love Ānanda's nose. I love Ānanda's mouth. I love Ānanda's ears. I love Ānanda's voice. I love Ānanda's way of walking."

The Buddha said: "The eyes have only tears. The nose has only mucus. The mouth has only saliva. The ears have only wax. The body has only excrement and urine, foul and impure places. When a man and woman become husband and wife, there are foul discharges. From foul discharges come children. When there are children, there is death. When there is death, there is weeping. In all of this, what benefit does the body bring?"

The girl then reflected on the foul impurities within the body. She straightened her mind, and at once she attained the path of arhatship.

The Buddha, knowing she had attained arhatship, said to the girl: "Rise and go to Ānanda."

The girl was immediately ashamed. She lowered her head and knelt before the Buddha, saying: "It was truly out of foolishness that I pursued Ānanda. Now my mind has been opened. It is like a lamp in the darkness. It is like a person in a boat when the boat breaks apart and they reach the shore. It is like a blind person gaining a guide. It is like an old person walking with a staff. Now the Buddha has given me the path, and my mind has been opened in just this way."

The bhikṣus all asked the Buddha: "This girl's mother practiced the arts of enchantment. How is it that the girl attained arhatship?"

The Buddha told the bhikṣus: "Do you wish to hear and know about this girl?"

The bhikṣus said: "We wish to receive the teaching."

The Buddha said: "In five hundred past lives, this Mātaṅgī girl was Ānanda's wife. Through five hundred lifetimes they always respected each other, cherished each other, desired each other, and loved each other. Now, through my sūtra precepts, they have both attained the path. From now on, when husband and wife meet, they are like brother and sister. What good is the buddha-path if not for this?"

When the Buddha had spoken this sūtra, the bhikṣus who heard it were all filled with joy.
