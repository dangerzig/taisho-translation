# 佛說如意摩尼陀羅尼經
## Sūtra on the Wish-Fulfilling Jewel Dhāraṇī Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1403

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dānapāla (施護).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the kingdom of Śrāvastī, in the city of Kālī. At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the Venerable Ānanda: "There is now a dhāraṇī that can eliminate all violent and evil thunder and lightning. You should receive and uphold it. This Wish-Fulfilling Jewel Sūtra was personally proclaimed by the tathāgatas, arhats, and perfectly awakened ones of the past, and I now also proclaim it, acting with great compassion in this world, to benefit and bring happiness to all sentient beings in the heavens and human realm." Ānanda said: "So be it, World-Honored One! I am delighted to hear it. I shall now receive and uphold it." The Buddha addressed Ānanda: "You should know that in the east there is a thunder-lightning named Agha. In the south there is a thunder-lightning named Śatiru. In the west there is a thunder-lightning named Tāprabhā. In the north there is a thunder-lightning named Saunamanī. Ānanda! If good men or good women know the names and directional locations of these thunder-lightnings, all thunder and lightning will not frighten those persons. If at any place one writes these thunder-lightning names, receives, upholds, and makes offerings to them, all thunder and lightning will be unable to cause harm." At that time, the World-Honored One proclaimed these protective mantra phrases:

*tadyathā nimi nimi nimiṃ-dhāri trailokya-lokinī śūla-pāṇinī rakṣaṃ sarva-viniyoga-bhaye bhiyāt svāhā*

At that time, the Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Avalokiteśvara, who was in the great assembly, rose from his seat, placed his palms together toward the Buddha, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I too shall proclaim protective mantra phrases for this true Dharma."

*tadyathā vināśa pratyādhikā pratyāmitrī śuddhi-mukte vimale prabhāsa-kari aṇḍāri paṇḍāri śvete paṇḍaravāsinī [māte] kārālā [ka]pī-la-piṅgalā kṛṣṇā-[ādi] [mu]kṣi rakṣi rakṣi[ṃ] sarva-kāla-mṛtyu-[abhi]yu-bhaye bhiyāt māṃ paśa-mantu sarva-kāla-mṛtyuvaṃ ārya-avalokiteśvara-tejasā svāhā*

At that time, the Lord of Secrets, Bodhisattva Vajrapāṇi, went before the Buddha, placed his palms together in prostration, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I too shall now proclaim dhāraṇī mantra phrases within this true Dharma."

*tadyathā muni muni muni mate muni-mate su-mate mahā-mate ha ha ha ha [mātṛ]-nāṃ-sthite [prapa]-vajra-pāṇi rahaṃ-[nir]dha svāhā*

"World-Honored One! This dhāraṇī of mine is called the Invincible Protection of Sentient Beings. If a person writes it and remembers it, all terrors and untimely deaths will be dispelled."

At that time, Mahābrahmā, Lord of the Sahā World, addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! For the benefit of all sentient beings, I too shall proclaim dhāraṇī mantra phrases for this true Dharma."

*tadyathā hili mili cili svāhā brahma-pūri mahā-brahmaṇī brahma-garbhi puṣpa-[śās]tāri svāhā*

"World-Honored One! This dhāraṇī of mine is called the Staff of Brahmā. It protects all sentient beings, keeps untimely death far away, dispels terror, and can also extinguish all karmic offenses."

At that time, Indra, Lord of the Devas, stood before the Buddha, placed his palms together in prostration, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I too shall now proclaim dhāraṇī mantra phrases for this true Dharma."

*tadyathā buddha-[maṇḍa]ni māninī [gau]rī gandhāri caṇḍāle [mā]ḍhaṃge puṣpākṣi [sāra]-brate hīnā-madhya-[uṃga] dhāraṇī maṇḍule [nī]ke cakara [vā]ki śavari śavari svāhā*

"World-Honored One! This dhāraṇī of mine is called the Vajra Seat. It liberates from all terrors and keeps all untimely deaths far away."

At that time, Dhṛtarāṣṭra, Virūḍhaka, Virūpākṣa, and Vaiśravaṇa, the Four Heavenly Kings, together went before the Buddha, placed their palms together in prostration, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! We too shall proclaim dhāraṇī mantra phrases for this true Dharma."

*tadyathā puṣpe su-puṣpe [durba]-parihāri ārya-praśaste śāntini [nir]-mukte maṅgale [ye] hiraṇya-garbhi stute [sthā]vite svāhā*

"World-Honored One! We four great heavenly kings have proclaimed this dhāraṇī called 'Fearlessness.' It grants fearlessness amid terrors, keeps untimely death far away, and extends the life-span."

At that time, there were also great ocean nāga kings, namely the Nāga King Mahāmati, the Nāga King Meghaprabhā, the Nāga King Anavatapta, the Nāga King Vidyujjihva, and the Nāga King Śataprabhā. They all went to where the Buddha was, placed their palms together in prostration, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! We have a dhāraṇī called the Wish-Fulfilling Jewel that protects sentient beings. If a person writes and recites it, it can eliminate all terror of thunder and lightning, all untimely death, all poisons, all evil diseases, and inauspicious ghosts and spirits will also be unable to take advantage. World-Honored One! If one upholds this mantra, all of our kind of nāgas will be filled with compassion and will not give rise to jealousy. Why is this? Among the nāga classes, jealousy is natural to their disposition. Yet upon hearing this Dharma, they become free from jealousy." The dhāraṇī states:

*tadyathā ajari māmari amṛte akṣaye avyaye puṇya-pariyāye sarva-pāpa-praśamanī svāhā ārya-puṇḍa-[rau]pakiye svāhā indra-[śa]nīye svāhā megha-[śa]nīye svāhā*

At that time, the World-Honored One, having heard Mahābrahmā, Indra Lord of the Devas, the Four Protective Heavenly Kings, and the great nāga kings proclaim these dhāraṇīs, praised them: "Excellent! Excellent! You enable many people to receive the great medicine of safety. You protect, uphold, and disseminate the true Dharma."

When the Buddha had finished proclaiming this sūtra, the entire great assembly of devas, nāgas, asuras, gandharvas, and others were all greatly overjoyed and faithfully received and practiced it.
