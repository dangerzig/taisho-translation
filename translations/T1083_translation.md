# 觀世音菩薩如意摩尼陀羅尼經
## Wish-Fulfilling Maṇi Dhāraṇī Sūtra of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1083

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Ratnacinta (寶思惟).

---

At that time Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One, I possess a vidyā-mantra dharma with a great altar called the Lotus Peak Vajra-Empowered Secret Unobstructed Avalokiteśvara Lotus Wish-Fulfilling Maṇi Cakravartin Heart Dhāraṇī, the most supreme accomplishment of the heart of Avalokiteśvara. World-Honored One, because it can fulfill the wishes of all sentient beings, in the World-Honored One's presence I shall now proclaim it. I beseech the World-Honored One to empower and protect me, so that for all vidyā-holders it may rain a great rain of treasures, like the great kalpa-tree, like the wish-fulfilling maṇi, and may cause all sentient beings to have their wishes fulfilled." The Buddha said: "I have already empowered you. You should now proclaim the secret wish-granting heart of Avalokiteśvara, by which all that is sought shall be fulfilled without any obstruction." At that time Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva Mahāsattva went to where the Buddha was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, circumambulated the Buddha three times, and before the World-Honored One, with a joyful smile and a heart of great compassion, spoke this spell-king. The spell says:

*Namo ratna-trayāya namaḥ ārya-avalokiteśvarāya bodhisattvāya mahāsattvāya mahā-kāruṇikāya tadyathā oṃ cakra-vartī cintā-maṇi mahā-padme ruru tiṣṭha jvala ākarṣāya hūṃ phaṭ svāhā*

"This is called the root spell."

Next he spoke the heart spell. The spell says:

*Oṃ padma cintā-maṇi jvala hūṃ*

Next he spoke the following-heart spell. The spell says:

*Oṃ varada padme hūṃ*

When he had spoken these spells, the great earth at that moment quaked in six ways. All the celestial palaces also shook. All the nāga palaces also trembled. All the yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, and mahoragas trembled greatly in every manner. All evil māras and vināyakas who create obstacles were all terrified. All the māra-palaces were without light. All vile and wicked sentient beings, evil nāgas, evil ghosts, female yakṣas, female rākṣasīs, and all the most wicked fell face-down upon the ground. All poisonous vermin hid in their holes. All the hells were shattered, and the beings in the hells were all liberated and reborn in the heavens. Before the World-Honored One, celestial offerings of flowers, incense, garments, and all manner of adornments rained down, and in the sky all manner of celestial music was played.

At that time the World-Honored One, in the beautiful and wondrous voice of a kalaviṅka bird, praised Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva: "Excellent, excellent! For the benefit of all sentient beings, you should proclaim the powers and functions of this spell." Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! If any good man or good woman, bhikṣu or bhikṣunī, who wishes to obtain various benefits in the present, the wish-fulfilling spell and mudrā should be diligently practiced. One need not perform any special ritual, need not consult astrological days, need not observe fasting, need not bathe, need not wear special garments. At the time of upholding, no special effort is required; simply by reading and reciting, all is accomplished. The reciter can accomplish all manner of undertakings and can perform hundreds of thousands of various tasks. There is no other spell equal to this one. First, it can extinguish offenses accumulated since the past. All undertakings are brought to accomplishment. I shall now proclaim: simply by reading and reciting, one can accomplish unsurpassed matters. Simply by reading, even those who have fallen into Avīci are purified. The five heinous crimes are extinguished without remainder. All the sick are healed. If there are any who are ill, simply by reading this spell all are cured. All manner of poison-spells, sorcery, and gu-curses cannot cause harm. All sores do not attach to one's body. All ghosts and spirits, vināyakas, all poisons, blades, cudgels, storms, evil disasters, māra-armies and bandits, foreign armies, royal calamities, enemies lying in wait, poisonous gu, and fierce beasts: none of these can cause harm. One will never die an untimely death and will not have evil dreams. In battles and disputes, one is always victorious. All such matters are accomplished simply by reading.

"If one silently recites one hundred times within the lips, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva will appear before that person on that very day and grant whatever is wished, bringing all to accomplishment. One will also see all the buddha tathāgatas, and see the Western Land of Amitāyus Buddha, the world of Sukhāvatī and the assembly of bodhisattvas, and the palace of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva on Mount Potalaka. One's body will be purified, one will be honored and served by the noble, all will delight in seeing one, and all obstructions of offenses and entanglements will be purified. Wherever one is born, one obtains the wisdom of past lives, is born from a lotus, and all wondrous provisions adorn one. Proceeding directly to bodhi, one forever leaves the evil destinies and ultimately attains buddhahood. Evil offenses are extinguished and the wholesome path is purified. Simply by reading and reciting, all is accomplished."

At that time Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva further spoke of the most supreme secret functions: "If for the benefit and compassionate aid of all sentient beings, to subdue and restrain evil persons and increase loving-kindness, one simply recites, all is accomplished. It can provide great benefit to sentient beings, cause the wise to obtain great peace and happiness, and increase goods, food, wealth, honor, and material provisions without limit, and physical strength flourishes. This spell is secret and must not be recklessly disclosed.

"If one wishes to truly accomplish the unsurpassed wish-fulfilling maṇi great mudrā, simply reciting brings accomplishment. Whether one has eaten or not eaten, whether pure or impure, by constantly reciting there is no transgression. At the time of recitation, one should think of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva and forever take him as one's refuge. Whether noble or humble, male or female, śramaṇa or non-Buddhist, one should fix the mind, concentrate, and recite this spell during the last watch of the night or at dawn. The most noble man: one thousand and eight times. The most noble woman: one thousand and ten times. The second-rank noble man: eight hundred times. The second-rank noble woman: seven hundred times. The third-rank noble woman: six hundred times. A brāhmaṇa: five hundred times. A kṣatriya: four hundred. A śūdra: three hundred. A vaiśya: two hundred. A bhikṣu: one hundred and eight times. A woman: one hundred and thirty times. A man: one hundred and fifty times. A boy: sixty. A girl: ninety.

"This method of recitation has none who do not take refuge in it; it gathers beings within a hundred yojanas, swift as the wind. Having recited eight hundred times, Avalokiteśvara in his true body appears before one and lets one see him, and all wishes are granted, and all vidyā-mantras are accomplished. Having recited ten thousand times, the Holy Vajradhara in his true body appears before the reciter and, like a beloved child, nurtures and embraces one. All vidyā-mantras are accomplished, and whatever one desires is granted and fulfilled. Having recited thirteen thousand times, one sees all the buddha tathāgatas. Having recited for a full seven days, all the vidyā-ṛṣi-kings appear in their true bodies before the reciter, each bestowing the accomplished vidyā-mantras, concealing their forms, following and protecting, and all peace and happiness manifests. During the seven days, each day during the last watch of the night, if one recites three thousand times, Śakra and the celestial maidens descend, take refuge, and grant the vidyā-wishes. All such matters are accomplished simply by reciting. The recitation method is complete."

At that time he further spoke of the supreme accomplishment of the dharma of subjugation by sight: "If one merely employs it, all submit.

"Gorocanā, white sandalwood, turmeric, camphor, musk, nutmeg, white cardamom, clove, red lotus flower, blue lotus flower, and gold-red earth.

"The above ingredients in equal parts, blended with white rock-candy. This is the cakravartin incense. Recite the spell one thousand and eight times while blending. Burn it to perfume garments, apply it to the forehead, eyelids, and body. Wherever one goes, one's majestic radiance is like the sun; all delight in seeing one. If held in the hand, all is accomplished. All sentient beings, whether noble or humble, together with their persons and possessions, all submit."

At that time Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, for the benefit of all sentient beings, further spoke the method of the eye medicine and its supreme accomplishment: "If any person uses it, accomplishment is certain beyond doubt. Manasśilā, orpiment, kakubha-tree sap, red lotus flower, blue lotus flower, sea-foam, gorocanā, turmeric root, small cypress root, pepper, long pepper, and dried ginger.

"Pound and grind all the above medicines into extremely fine powder. Blend with camphor and musk. Recite the heart spell one thousand and eight times, the following-heart spell one thousand and eight times, and the root great spell one thousand and eight times. With the hand, take the medicine and touch it to the feet of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, then apply it to the eyes. All eye diseases, including cataracts, blindness from birth, and pterygium, are all cured. With the second application, all fevers, headaches, migraines, and oral diseases are cured. With the third application, all fierce and evil ghosts, possessing spirits, and epilepsy are cured. With the fourth application, all evil vināyakas are extinguished. With the fifth application, one is victorious over all enemies and disputants. With the sixth application, all karmic obstructions, all poisons, and all who should fall into hell and suffer the offenses of the unremitting are extinguished; all evil dreams, evil thoughts, and inauspicious signs in the hundreds and thousands of assemblies are all extinguished and will never arise again. All this is accomplished through the eye medicine. On the seventh day of application, upon seeing any man or woman, noble or humble, ordained or lay, inner or outer relatives, all submit. After two weeks of application, one attains great sovereign mastery. After three weeks, one attains the rank of a regional king, and all submit. After four weeks, all yakṣas, female yakṣas, and their retinues all submit. After five weeks, rākṣasīs and their retinues all submit. After six weeks, accomplished illusionists who can manifest all manner of bodies are all subject to one's command, even to the point of bodhi, following and submitting. After seven weeks, Mahākāla and all the female ghosts and spirit-retinues all submit. After eight weeks, one sees invisible beings. After nine weeks, one sees all hidden treasures. After ten weeks, all doors of caves and dwellings open to one. After eleven weeks, all the divine gardens of immortal medicines are accomplished; all the plant spirits and tree spirits manifest their forms. After twelve weeks, all mountain gates open. After thirteen weeks, all doors without exception open, and all people without exception are seen. After fourteen weeks, all the celestial palaces of the desire realm open, and all are seen. After fifteen weeks, one sees at night as in the day. After sixteen weeks, one sees from afar all that lies beneath the earth. After seventeen weeks, all the hells in the world are seen, and those suffering there are all liberated. After eighteen weeks, one's body possesses majestic virtue like the light of the sun, and all caves and dwellings in the darkness are seen. After nineteen weeks, the Holy Vajradhara comes in person to serve as witness. After twenty weeks, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva personally appears to serve as witness, manifesting his own body and granting all wishes, fulfilling all that the heart desires. After twenty-one weeks, one attains flight through the sky without obstruction and sees all the buddha-lands, fully sees the bodies of the buddhas, and sees all the places where the bodhisattvas dwell. If the secret eye-medicine is applied for a full year, the five eyes are purified, shared equally with all sentient beings. All the secret accomplishments described above are fully obtained; do not give rise to doubt. Such accomplishments are all obtained simply by reading and reciting. All the tathāgatas turn the wheel. Such is the maṇi. The lotus-purity of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva: the eye-medicine accomplishment is complete."

He further spoke the method of the fire offering for the supreme accomplishment of subjugation: "Lifespan is increased, all offenses are extinguished, and all entanglements are left behind. All enemies are subjugated without exception, and all obstacles are spontaneously extinguished. All sentient beings naturally submit.

"White sandalwood, rice-flower, white mustard seed, blended with curds, honey, and ghee. Recite the spell one thousand and eight times and burn them, using fragrant wood for the fire. As soon as this is done, all karmic obstructions, including those of the unremitting, are spontaneously extinguished, and one's lifespan becomes one thousand years. After one week of burning, one obtains a lifespan of two thousand years, and the body is purified, forever free from entanglements. After two weeks of performing the rite, all men and women submit. After three weeks, the thirty-three devas and their retinues, including the sun deity, all come and submit. All wishes are fulfilled. The four heavenly kings and others all descend, in order to constantly nurture and protect the spell-reciter. The Holy Vajradhara grants the wish of accomplishment. Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, being the wish-granter, fulfills all that is sought. All delight in seeing one, and nothing is not within one's sovereign mastery."

"The method for bringing rain: blend ghee with mustard seed and burn for three nights. The nāgas will bring rain. If the rain is excessive, take the ashes and scatter them into the sky; the rain will stop of itself. If one takes the ashes and circles the mouth with them, then casts them into the fire one hundred and eight times, all evil winds, snow, and rain will cease. If one constantly recites, there will be no obstruction anywhere. One will obtain vigor, courage, and diligence. After death, one will be born in the Western Land of Sukhāvatī. In every place of birth, one will obtain the wisdom of past lives.

"Thus, World-Honored One! Whether bhikṣu, bhikṣunī, upāsaka, or upāsikā, all spell-holders who read and recite and accomplish this great wish-fulfilling maṇi cakravartin Avalokiteśvara heart-mudrā should receive, uphold, read, recite, and listen to it. They shall obtain such a great aggregate of merit, great roots of merit, and all men and women will submit. The spell-holder should always guard the mouth in all that is done, and one will never retrogress. One will receive great offerings, and all wealth and treasures will be obtained. The wishes of all sentient beings will be fulfilled."

At that time the World-Honored One told Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva: "For the benefit of all sentient beings, you should circulate this dharma in Jambudvīpa. This unsurpassed heart-spell brings accomplishment simply by reading. It should be thus practiced. Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva! You should protect and sustain all sentient beings, continually watching over them and repeatedly bringing them to accomplishment, so that the wishes of all sentient beings are fulfilled." Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! For the benefit and mindful protection of all sentient beings, I now receive and uphold this, so that all wishes may be accomplished. By reading and reciting the vidyā-mantra, all wishes are fulfilled. Therefore, World-Honored One, I now speak in all sincerity. I beseech the World-Honored One's compassionate empowerment and protection."
