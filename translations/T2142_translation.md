# 序聽迷詩所經
## The Sūtra of Hearing the Messiah's Teaching in Sequence

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 2142

Author unknown.

---

At that time, the Messiah spoke the Heavenly Honored One's preface to the Dharma of the community, saying: "The diverse views are many; who can speak of them all? The meanings of the scriptures are hard to settle; who can speak of these matters? Where is the Heavenly Honored One who will appear afterward? Where does he dwell? All the buddhas and non-human beings, the heavenly arhats: who has seen the Heavenly Honored One among living beings? No one can see the Heavenly Honored One. What person has the power to see the Heavenly Honored One? For this Heavenly Honored One's countenance is like the wind. What person can see the wind?"

The Heavenly Honored One does not remain even a short while, but travels throughout the world and dwells everywhere. For this reason, every person carries within them the breath of the Heavenly Honored One, and only thereby can they live. Only thereby can they dwell in peace at home. When the mind and intention are fully engaged, from sunrise to sunset, wherever one's thoughts and mind go, one arrives there entirely. The body dwells in luminous joy, tranquil passage, and peaceful abiding. In heaven there are all the buddhas. For this reason, the wind circulates throughout the world. The wind circulates and there is no place it does not reach. The Heavenly Honored One constantly dwells in a place of tranquil passage and joyful bliss. The fruits of retribution reach everywhere. Among the people of the world, who knows the movement of the wind? One only hears its sound in the treetops but never sees its form. No one can perceive his countenance and dignified appearance. He is neither yellow, nor white, nor blue-green. Nor does anyone know the place where the wind dwells in its power. The Heavenly Honored One possesses his own divine might and dwells in one place. Where he dwells, no one can grasp him. He has neither death nor birth. He has never encountered worldly desires. He created heaven and earth, yet has never been of this world. He possesses no mere supernatural power but eternally enjoys the bliss of immortal affinities.

When people are in dire need, they always call upon the Buddha's name. Many are the ignorant people who call upon spirits and compare them to the Heavenly Honored One and his kind, and they also fabricate notions of honored lords and honored pleasures. People of different lands speak in different tongues. The Heavenly Honored One whom I distinguish is always present. In every moment of faith and in every moment of dwelling, the Heavenly Honored One gives understanding to people in no small measure. Who can repay the Buddha's compassionate grace? One should properly reflect upon this.

Know clearly that evil and wrongdoing should not be cultivated. Heaven's power nurtures and sustains all through divine strength. When the human body reaches maturity, one should also reflect, as all living beings should, that the breath of life in every person's body is entirely caused by the Heavenly Honored One. All living beings are subject to the cycle of transmigration. The body abides on this earth. For this reason, he transformed and fashioned the motes of dust. All living beings should arouse good minds and reflect upon themselves. All who are born will die; all living beings should entrust this. The life of living beings depends on breath; without it there is no life. At the moment of death, the wind departs from the being. When mind and intention are without wind, it is by wind that one lives. When the wind departs from a being, at the time of going and staying, why do people not see the wind go, or the color of the wind's countenance? Whether it is crimson or green or another color, based on this one cannot see the wind.

How then can living beings say, "Where is the Heavenly Honored One?" Living beings foolishly ask, "Why can we not see the Heavenly Honored One?" Why can living beings, while immersed in sin, see the Heavenly Honored One themselves? The Heavenly Honored One is not the same as a human body; who then can see living beings? No one dares approach the Heavenly Honored One. Only those living beings of good fortune and good affinities can see the Heavenly Honored One. The world originally does not see the Heavenly Honored One. How can one know him? Living beings themselves cannot see heaven, so they should cultivate merit of their own, and then they will not fall into the evil paths or the hells. They will obtain the heavenly reward. If there are beings of evil karma, they fall into the evil paths. They do not see the bright fruit, nor do they attain the heavenly way. Living beings should reflect well upon this.

Heaven and earth are vast. The great lords who sustain the many evil beings: those who serve them with diligent minds for the sake of the nation receive official ranks and are bestowed with provisions without measure. If there are living beings who do not serve the heavenly lords and do not follow their commands, they receive no official rank, nor are they given any recompense. They are sentenced to exile or sentenced to death. This is not because the heavenly lords act of their own will, but because the prior causes and karmic seeds of previous lives have come to fruition. Every being must first contemplate the fruit of retribution of their own body.

The Heavenly Honored One undertook much suffering before establishing the principles governing living beings. The Buddha does not establish these from afar; the person himself bears responsibility. Good has its good fortune; evil has its evil affinities. Ignorant living beings then carve wooden idols of camels, elephants, oxen, donkeys, horses, and other animals, as well as river-deer. Though they fashion their forms, they cannot give them life. Living beings who have understanding should measure cause and effect for themselves. All that they possess and see, they also know of themselves. All of this is truly so.

For this reason, in this present world there are many living beings who of their own accord fashion various images and set them up. All such things are the Heavenly Honored One's domain, yet they cannot endow them with life. Living beings deceive themselves. They take gold and fashion images of elephants, silver spirit-images, copper images, spirit-images, and wooden spirit-images. They further make all manner of various domestic animals. They fashion people resembling people, horses resembling horses, oxen resembling oxen, donkeys resembling donkeys, yet these can neither move nor walk. They cannot speak. They cannot eat. They have no breath, no flesh, no skin, no vessels, no bones. To explain all these details in full would be immense; within the whole account, a brief description conveys much.

For the sake of all people, he preaches so that they may know good from evil. He takes food and drink, and from the many tastes a few, so that one may know which food has flavor and which has none. Those who serve the Heavenly Honored One are taught the meaning of the scriptures, and all such scriptures are composed. There are a great many matters worthy of praise, and many important points, though the account is brief. Those who serve the Heavenly Honored One are taught the meaning of heaven.

There are people who fear the Heavenly Honored One's law, who themselves practice goodness, who themselves do good, and who admonish others to do good. Such people have received the Heavenly Honored One's teaching and have received the Heavenly Honored One's precepts. People who constantly do evil and who teach others to do evil: such people have not received the Heavenly Honored One's teaching. They will fall into the evil paths, and their lives will belong to King Yama.

There are people who have received the Heavenly Honored One's teaching and who constantly say, "I have received the precepts" and who teach others to receive the precepts. People should fear the Heavenly Honored One. At all times they should admonish and correct others. All living beings should each fear the Heavenly Honored One and be governed by him. All living beings, in death and in life, are managed, guided, and governed in their very spirits. If living beings fear the Heavenly Honored One, they should also fear and revere the Sovereign. The Sovereign's merit in previous lives led the Heavenly Honored One to appoint him. It was not of his own accord. The Heavenly Honored One himself appointed him as Sovereign. All living beings follow the Sovereign's commands. If there are people who do not follow the Sovereign's orders and do not submit, such people among all beings are rebels and traitors. If there are people who follow the Sovereign's commands, they become persons of understanding among people, who submit to his commands and do good, who admonish others to do good, and who themselves do no evil. Such people are in a state of having received the precepts.

If there are people who have received the precepts yet do not fear the Heavenly Honored One, such people, though nominally following the Buddha's Dharma, have not truly attained the state of receiving the precepts. They are rebels and traitors.

Third, one must fear one's father and mother, honoring and serving them. Comparing them to the Heavenly Honored One and the Holy Sovereign: if a person first serves the Heavenly Honored One and the Sovereign, and serves father and mother without fail, this person obtains blessings from the Heavenly Honored One. These three duties, one should attend to in this order: first, serve the Heavenly Honored One; second, serve the Sovereign; third, serve father and mother.

For this reason, all under heaven and upon the earth follow the way of father and mother. Based on this, the Sovereign is divinely born. Though in this present world there are parents who are alive, living beings who have wisdom should fear the Heavenly Honored One and the Sovereign and also fear their parents. They should well receive the Heavenly Honored One's Dharma-teaching and not break the precepts.

What the Heavenly Honored One has received and what he teaches: first, he commands living beings to worship all the heavenly buddhas, because the Buddha endured suffering to establish heaven and earth, solely through the causes and conditions of pure and awesome power. The Sovereign should only diligently frequent the Sovereign's palace. From all the buddhas one obtains the Sovereign's body; all of this is free. The Heavenly Honored One says: "All living beings who rebel and commit various evils, who rebel against the Honored One, are also not filial."

The second commandment: one should be filial to one's parents and serve them respectfully. All living beings who filially support their parents and serve them without fail, at the end of their lives shall attain the heavenly way. One lives and makes a home in order to serve father and mother. If living beings had no father and mother, from whom would they be born?

The fourth commandment: if there is a person who has received the precepts, toward all living beings they should arouse a good mind. They must not harbor jealousy or evil.

The fifth commandment: living beings must not kill, nor should they encourage others to kill. For the lives of all beings are no different from human lives.

The sixth commandment: one must not commit adultery with another's wife or children, nor commit it oneself.

The seventh commandment: one must not steal.

The eighth: one must not, seeing others' wealth and possessions, their fields and homes, their servants, feel envy or jealousy.

The ninth commandment: if one has a fine wife and children and a fine golden house, one must not fabricate testimony to falsely accuse others.

The tenth commandment: one must not accept goods entrusted by others and then spend them.

The Heavenly Honored One further instructs on many matters. When encountering the weak, do not deceive others. If you see a poor child, do not truly turn your face away. When your own household is hungry, give generously of food and drink. Offer up your own possessions. If you see a man laboring, strive to give him drink. If you see a person without clothing, give them clothing. If you are entrusted with a child's property, do not keep it even for a single day, for the child's inheritance, however much or little, does not protect against cold and frost. When you see laborers, do not curse them. The spirits have awesome power; if you curse them, calamity and obstacles will surely come. If a poor child needs money, you should give it if you have it. If you have no money to give, send them away with kind words. Give alms from nothing. If you see another person with a chronic illness, do not truly laugh at them. This person is not in this condition of their own will. Such chronic illness, poverty, and lack of clothing, even if their garments are tattered, truly do not mock them. Do not deceive others and take their possessions. Do not falsely accuse others. If someone brings a complaint, deal with the matter truly and do not render an unjust verdict. If orphaned boys and girls or widowed women bring a complaint, do not create injustice. Do not cause people to bear resentment. Truly do not be arrogant. Do not boast. Do not spread gossip and set tongues wagging, causing people to fight each other. In one's entire life, do not pass through prefectures and counties making false accusations out of ignorance. Do not strike a person who has received the precepts even once. Do not harbor evil thoughts toward others. All living beings should constantly arouse good minds. If one has evil, do not wish evil upon others. From the many, select the few. Always do good toward all living beings.

If there are people who wish to know the person who received the precepts and who wrote this: whoever can follow this scripture is a person who has received the precepts. If there are living beings who cannot follow it, they have not attained the state of receiving the precepts. All instructions are from the Heavenly Honored One, directed to all elders and to the great and small. He bids them admonish one another to do good. This is the Heavenly Honored One's foremost instruction. Living beings should follow the Heavenly Honored One. He does not cause living beings to slaughter for sacrifices, nor does he command them to kill. Living beings who do not follow this teaching, who of their own accord kill and sacrifice, who eat meat and indulge in delicacies, who use false practices to deceive the spirits, who slaughter sheep and the like: such living beings do not follow this teaching.

Those who do good as instructed and others like them: such living beings turn their backs and do evil, thereby turning against the Heavenly Honored One. The Heavenly Honored One sees living beings in this state and feels compassion. Those who, despite much admonishment to do good, do not follow: for them the Heavenly Honored One caused a cool wind to visit a certain maiden named Mo-yan (Mary). The cool wind entered the belly of Mo-yan, according to the Heavenly Honored One's instruction. Thereupon Mo-yan conceived a child. Because the Heavenly Honored One sent the cool wind to the maiden's side, without a husband she conceived. This was to cause all living beings to see that without a husband she conceived, and to cause people of the world to see this and say: "The Heavenly Honored One has awesome power." This was to cause living beings to believe with pure minds and turn toward good affinities.

After Mo-yan conceived, she gave birth to a boy named Yi-shu (Jesus). His father was the aforementioned cool wind. There were ignorant living beings who said, "How can one conceive and give birth from the wind?" But when a worldly sovereign sends forth a single edict to wherever it goes, all living beings obey and submit. Based on this, the Heavenly Honored One, being in heaven above, universally governs heaven and earth.

When Yi-shu, the Messiah, was born, in whatever place in the world he dwelt, the bright fruit was seen in heaven and earth. The bright star made known its presence in heaven above. The star was as large as a cartwheel. Its bright purity was the Heavenly Honored One's doing. He was born in the city of Bethlehem in the Garden of the Roman Lands. When the Messiah was born, after five periods of one year, he spoke and preached the Dharma, directing living beings to do good.

When he was past twelve years of age, in a pure place called "the Difficult," he went to a man called John the Baptizer. At that time, this was the Messiah's first disciple, a sage dwelling in a gorge. From birth he had never eaten wine or meat, eating only raw vegetables and honey upon the ground. At that time, many living beings came to John to bow in reverence and to receive the precepts. Thereupon John caused the Messiah to enter the deep water and be washed. After the Messiah entered the water and came out, a cool wind descended from heaven. Its appearance was like a dove and it settled upon the Messiah. From the empty sky above a voice spoke, saying: "The Messiah is my son. All living beings in the world should follow the Messiah's commands. Whatever he instructs, do it well."

The Messiah then appeared among living beings as the heavenly way, for this was the Heavenly Honored One's decree, commanding the beings of the world below: "Cease serving false spirits." Thereupon there were beings who heard these words, ceased serving false spirits, ceased doing evil, and came to believe in good karma.

From when the Messiah was twelve until he was past thirty-two, he caused all beings of evil karma to turn toward good karma and the wholesome path. The Messiah had twelve disciples. Then, having undertaken suffering to go about: what was dead he brought to life; the blind received their sight; those of strange appearance were healed; the sick were cured; those harmed and possessed by demons had their demons driven out; the lame were made whole. All the sick who came before the Messiah and touched his robe were all healed.

All those who did evil and would not turn to the good path, who did not believe in the Heavenly Honored One's teaching, who were impure and greedy: all of them in this present world were not released. Those who indulged in wine and meat and served false spirits, these people remained attached to their ways and were deceived by the hostile ones. They wished to kill him. For this reason, many living beings came to believe in this teaching. Because of this, they were unable to kill him. Afterward, the Messiah was betrayed by evil-karma people using the hostile ones. People of pure faith made their own judgments, but they wished to kill the Messiah. Having no method or plan, they went before the great king and falsely spoke evil words, slandering him with evil matters.

The Messiah did good, and with even greater diligence he taught living beings. When he was past thirty-two years, those who practiced evil went before the great king Pilate (Piluodusi) and said, telling Pilate: "The Messiah deserves the death penalty."

The great king then summoned those of evil affinity as witnesses. The Messiah was brought before the great king Pilate. According to the Messiah's reckoning, he deserved the death penalty. The great king wished to pass sentence. "This person deserves the death penalty, but I truly have not heard nor seen it. This person should not deserve death. This matter should be decided by the people of evil affinity themselves."

The great king said: "I cannot kill him." He further said: "This person does not deserve death, upon my children's lives."

The great king Pilate called for water and washed his hands before the people of evil affinity, saying: "I truly cannot kill this person."

The people of evil affinity made their request again more urgently: "If you do not kill him, it will not do."

The Messiah offered his own body to the evil ones, so that all living beings might know that a person's life is like a flickering candle. For the living beings of this present world, he gave himself, substituting his life and accepting death.

The Messiah gave his own body, and thereupon he accepted death. The people of evil karma then took the Messiah to a separate place. On a wooden frame they bound him to a cross-beam, at a place called Golgotha. On the wood they bound him. They further took two robbers, one person on each side. On that day, when the Messiah was bound upon the wood at the fifth hour, it was the sixth day of fasting. They bound him at dawn, and by sunset, the four directions were dark, the earth shook, and the mountains collapsed. All the tombs in the world opened, and all the dead were brought back to life. Those people who saw such things and yet still did not believe in the scriptural teaching, both the dead and the living, all did so because of the Messiah. Among those people, there were many of great faith who spoke thus.
