# 諸佛心陀羅尼經
## Sūtra of the Heart-Dhāraṇī of All the Buddhas

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0918

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Xuanzang (玄奘)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Bhagavān was dwelling in the realm of the Tathāgata, the All-Jeweled Practice-Site, the capital of all the Buddhas, the delight of all the Buddhas, the wondrous palace of the bodhisattvas whose wisdom is free from hesitation. It was endowed with every kind of subtle and wondrous adornment, and its great hall of merit constantly resounded with the Dharma. Together with an incalculable number of great bodhisattvas, all true sons of the Tathāgata's dharma-body who had come from various buddha-lands to assemble there, and surrounded by an immeasurable host of devas, humans, asuras, and other worthy beings.

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the bodhisattva-mahāsattvas, saying: "Good men! There is a dhāraṇī called the Heart of All the Buddhas. Buddhas as numerous as the sands of the Ganges have together proclaimed it. It can universally benefit all those who are in terror and fear. If anyone with utmost sincerity receives, upholds, reads, and recites it, they will transcend the acute suffering of birth-and-death for a hundred thousand kalpas. They will assuredly be able to swiftly cultivate unsurpassed, perfect awakening and will forever be irreversible. Until they attain unsurpassed, perfect awakening, they will never be vainly born in a world without a Buddha. They will always skillfully comprehend all dhāraṇīs, constantly see the Tathāgata and draw near to make offerings, always recall their former lives, and deeply trust in cause and effect. In this present life, they can cause all harm from humans and non-humans to be removed; illness will not touch them, there will be no untimely death, and all evil demonic activities will be completely annihilated. All evil karma will be entirely extinguished, and all the armies of Māra will be frightened and will retreat in disarray. Good men! The syllables and phrases of this dhāraṇī have been praised by all the Buddhas together; they are the syllables and phrases of all the Buddhas. You should listen carefully."

The dhāraṇī:

*Buddhe subuddhe mahā-buddhe iti-buddhe tatra-buddhe buddhe buddhe sama-buddhe abhi-buddhe mṛdu-buddhe buddha-mati buddhe mahā-buddha-mati buddhe sarva-buddhā-numate sarva-buddhā-nujñāte buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha ananta-buddha-viṣaya ananta-dharma-deśaya eka-anta-manaskāra-saṃkṣipta-dharma-bhāvana etāni sarva-buddhe-vidhāraṇī saṃprakāśita-anukampaya sattvānāṃ sarva-dharmānāṃ mudrā-dhāraṇī tadyathā śuddhe suśuddhe śuddhane saṃśuddhane nirmale amala-aṃpagati gati-samati kālānti kālamī kālama-sāgarī sāgarī-mati śuke viśuke śuka-śamane śānte upaśānte praśānta-avabhāse sarva-puṇya-paripindite sarva-dharma-pratimandite hare hare malāṃ-hare care care saṃcare carā-carā saṃcarā trā trā saṃtrā samātrā niri niri suniri hṛdina-mati loka-tari loka-dhāraṇi tare tare thare thare huru-da huru-da mahā-vijaya-vāhinī hana hana sarva-buddhe sidhire sthita-sarva-jñā-paṭi sarva-jñā-parāmite mahā-pratibhāna-saṃpanne samanta-loke buddha-viśaye buddha-pratimandite bhagavatī sara sara prasara prasara visara visara sarva-duṣa-apagate svāhā*

When the Buddha had taught this Heart-Dhāraṇī of All the Buddhas, endowed with great awesome virtue, at that very moment the three-thousand great-thousand world system, the great earth and great oceans, the sovereign Mount Sumeru, all simultaneously experienced the eighteen kinds of trembling. All the palaces of the devas were shaken. The fierce and rebellious armies of Māra lost their radiant splendor; they gazed at one another in panic, trembling with fear and filled with dread. At that time, all the beings in the three-thousand world system who were destined for liberation, startled by these signs, all cried out three times in astonishment. Only the deva hosts who had faith in the Three Treasures were joyful and elated. Each of them gathered celestial flowers and scattered them upon the Buddha from afar, and they also exhorted the hosts of Māra to take refuge in the Buddha-Dharma.

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the bodhisattvas: "Out of compassion for all sentient beings, I have now taught this dhāraṇī to liberate them from suffering and hardship. You should rightly bear it in mind and proclaim it throughout the world, causing all to receive and uphold it and to obtain supreme benefit and happiness."

At that time, all the bodhisattvas and the rest of the assembly were all greatly delighted and faithfully received and practiced it.
