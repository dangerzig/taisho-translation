# 四諦經
## 四諦經

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0032

Translated from the Chinese. 後漢 安世高譯

---

Continuing from right livelihood:

— guarding, not committing — no guilt incurred, guilt already ceased — this is called right livelihood, this is called the noble truth of the path.

"What is, noble ones, right effort? Noble ones, disciples of the noble path are mindful, contemplating suffering as suffering, origination as origination, cessation as cessation, the path as the path — whatever energy one applies, whatever skillful means, whatever emerging from evil, whatever abiding in cessation, whatever capability, whatever spreading of virtue, whatever rejoicing — mindfully ceasing without destroying or extinguishing — this is called right effort. Also, maintaining observation of past karma, also being mindful through obtaining practice, seeing repentance through worldly practice, seeing cessation, transcending the world, unconditioned, quiescent, following cessation, without clinging, already obtaining the contemplation of the path and the mind of liberation — whatever energy, whatever skillful means, whatever emerging, whatever abiding in cessation, whatever spreading of virtue, whatever rejoicing — mindfully gathering and ceasing without destroying or extinguishing — this is called right effort, this is called the noble truth of the path.

"What is, noble ones, right mindfulness? Noble ones, when a disciple of the noble path contemplates suffering as suffering, origination as origination, cessation as cessation, the path as the path — being mindful and following mindfulness, mindfulness upon mindfulness not forgotten, speaking little, mindfulness not departing — this is called right mindfulness. Also, observing the maintenance of past karma, also practicing in accordance with the obtained path, mindfulness of worldly conduct beyond repentance, gathering, ceasing, transcending the world, unconditioned, quiescent, seeing cessation, unified virtue, without clinging, as if the mind of liberation — observing, being mindful, contemplating, being mindful — following mindfulness upon mindfulness, mindfulness not forgotten, speaking little, mindfulness not departing — this is called right mindfulness, this is called the noble truth of the path.

"What is, noble ones, right concentration? Noble ones, when a disciple of the noble path contemplates suffering as suffering, origination as origination, cessation as cessation, the path as the path — the mind resting and not moving, not running astray, having gathered and rested, mind and mindfulness dwelling in oneness — this is called right concentration. Also, observing the maintenance of past karma, also being mindful through the mind obtaining liberation, seeing repentance in worldly conduct, gathering, ceasing, transcending the world, unconditioned, seeing what is acceptable as it is, obtaining non-clinging, from the conditions of liberation the mind directed and contemplated, the mind at rest — rightly settled in oneness, not confused, not running astray, gathered and resting, mindfulness and concentration abiding in the second absorption — this is called right concentration, this is called the noble truth of the path.

"In past ages, noble ones, this was also the noble truth of cultivating and practicing the cessation of suffering; in future ages yet to come, this is also the noble truth of cultivating and practicing the path; now in the present age, this is also the noble truth of cultivating and practicing the cessation of suffering. Thus without difference — as it truly is, without loss, without confusion — true truth, as it was originally, having the virtue of the noble truth of the path. Noble ones, this truth — further seeing, obtaining, and according with liberation — it is therefore the cessation of suffering. Practicing in accordance with this is called the noble truth of the path. In conclusion, suffering, the origination of suffering, cessation — also, seeing the path — the practice proclaimed by the Buddha is immeasurable."

Thus Śāriputra taught, and the monks received and practiced it.

Maintaining the contemplation of past karma.
