# 北方毘沙門多聞寶藏天王神妙陀羅尼別行儀軌
## Separate Practice Ritual of the Marvelous Dhāraṇī of Vaiśravaṇa, the Treasure-Treasury Deva-King of the North, Who Hears Much

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1250

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

At that time, Vaiśravaṇa Deva-king addressed all sentient beings: "Good men! In this evil age of the five turbidities in Jambudvīpa, whether you are renunciants, bhikṣus or bhikṣuṇīs, or householders, upāsakas or upāsikās, all who are within the Dharma: I lead all the devas and benevolent spirits to guard and protect such persons. If anyone hears my name, or knows of me, I shall be at that person's side, guarding them as one guards the pupil of one's eye, as one guards one's own life, and I shall not allow evil ones to trouble them.

"If any man or woman serves me as a king, I shall serve them as a king serves. If they serve me with all manner of noble and supreme things, I shall serve them likewise. If they serve me with wealth, happiness, and music, I shall serve them with all manner of wealth, happiness, and music.

"If anyone can accept, uphold, and recite the dhāraṇī and practice the mudrā methods, and make offerings to my image according to the prescribed practices, then in all things I shall comply with whatever you seek, fulfilling your wishes to your satisfaction. If a renunciant upholds this, I shall assist them as a serving youth. If kings, ministers, and officials accept, uphold, and make offerings to me, I shall always fulfill their wishes for wealth, noble rank, military strength, and sovereignty, and I vow never to turn my back on them.

"Good men! This mantra method I have respectfully offered to the World-Honored One before the Buddha, to guard and protect the bhikṣu disciples of the Dharma in the Age of the Final Dharma, as well as all sentient beings. If anyone directs an evil mind against the disciples of my World-Honored One in the Age of the Final Dharma, I myself shall lead my many troops of ghost-spirits, yakṣas, gandharvas, and others to crush that evil person to dust.

"Good men! If anyone within my Dharma constantly makes offerings and upholds it according to the method, whatever you need and whatever you wish, I shall give to you.

"If anyone can accept and uphold my mantra method, one should first construct a four-cubit altar. In a clean room, dig out the foul earth to a depth of one *chi*, bring clean yellow earth from a distance, and pack it level. Mark out terraced steps one hand-span high; the upper tier is three *chi* square and one hand-span high. Open four gates. Use water-vases filled with fragrant water; four arrows, four knives, four mirrors. For offering food, use twelve trays: fried cakes, milk-curd, and ghee. Four lamps. Scatter flowers; in spring and summer, gather mixed fresh flowers; in autumn and winter, scatter colored silk flowers. On the lower tier of the altar, paint mountain rocks; on the upper tier, paint a great lotus flower, and at the four corners paint ocean waves. Install the image at the center upon the lotus. Above, hang sixteen banners, all made in the form of divine figures. The image faces south. The practitioner sits facing north, opposite the image, and recites.

"The altar mantra:

*Oṃ nanda nanda nāji nāji nanda-pari-ja svāhā.*

"If one is practicing the mantra method, when constructing the altar, first recite this mantra 108 times to empower the ground, earth, and stone of the site. All insects, ants, and creatures will move away and no longer dwell there, and the ground will become pure and fit for establishing the altar. When forming the altar, strictly empower and establish the boundary.

"The earth-boundary mudrā:

"The right middle finger protrudes outward from between the left index finger and thumb. The right ring finger does likewise from between the left little finger and ring finger. The left middle finger enters inward between the right index finger and thumb, protruding inward. The left ring finger does likewise between the right little finger and ring finger. Both little fingers and index fingers stand erect and joined; both thumbs also joined, pointing downward. Press the joined thumbs to the ground, with both forearms level. The mantra:

*Oṃ kiri kiri vajra-vajri bhūr bandha bandha hūṃ phaṭ.*

"This mudrā can remove all evil beings within the earth, down to the vajra boundary, eliminating all evil ghosts and spirits. Empower seven times, pressing against heaven, earth, above, and below; they cannot cause harm, and one obtains the merit of purification.

"Next, the four-directional boundary mudrā:

"Follow the previous earth-boundary mudrā, but change by opening both thumbs two *cun* apart, pointing toward the body and erect. Direct the mudrā with wrathful intent, turning to the right. The mantra:

*Oṃ sara sara vajra-prakāra hūṃ phaṭ.*

"This mudrā can remove māras, ghosts, and spirits in the four directions. At the site of the dharma-assembly altar, form the mudrā, recite the mantra seven times, and turn in the four directions following the sun. All yakṣas and ghosts will retreat and scatter, and the dharma-practice will succeed.

"Next, the space-boundary mudrā:

"Follow the previous earth-boundary mudrā, but change by placing both thumbs alongside the upper edge of the index fingers. Then direct the mudrā above the head, circling rightward three times. The mantra:

*Oṃ viṣphurad-rakṣa vajra-pañjara hūṃ phaṭ.*

"This mudrā can remove, from the sky above up to the summit of existence, all māras, ghosts, and spirits, who will not dare to harbor evil thoughts. Circle the mudrā three times above the head, empowering with the mantra; flying yakṣas will retreat and scatter.

"If one is able to establish a great practice hall for the lifelong accomplishment method, when selecting the ground, before marking the boundaries, recite the mantra once to establish the boundary, making the ground level, firm, and solid. Make offerings of incense and flowers, then recite the mantra once more to establish the boundary. When erecting the pillars, marking the perimeter, hanging streamers, bells, and ornaments, make a small flower offering, and recite the mantra once to establish the boundary. When arranging the colored seats and opening the various positions, recite the mantra once more to establish the boundary. When placing the lamps upon the altar, recite the mantra once to establish the boundary. When all the offering materials, including water-vessels, flowers, incense, cooked rice, food, ghee, honey, sesame, glutinous rice, and non-glutinous rice, have been brought into the practice hall and arranged, recite the mantra once more to establish the boundary. Before inviting the buddhas, Prajñāpāramitā, bodhisattvas, vajra-beings, and devas, recite the mantra once more to establish the boundary. At each establishment of the boundary, hold a vajra in the left hand to subjugate all, and hold an incense-burner in the right hand. With the mouth, invite all the holy assembly and the deva-king, circumambulating rightward three times to form the boundary. Visualize in the mind an extent of ten thousand *li*, a thousand *li*, a hundred *li*, or one yojana, all mentally marked, while speaking aloud: 'I establish the boundary within three *li* in each of the four nearby directions.' Then make offerings. All actions and deportment are complete in every dharma-characteristic, and all the noble ones, holy ones, and deva-kings rejoice. When performing the root mudrā and root mantra method, all involve invitation and boundary-establishment. Entering and exiting should be like the dignified deportment of a king, no different in bearing.

"Next, the incense-and-flower mudrā:

"The two ring fingers and two little fingers are crossed, right pressing left, within the palm, then bent toward the wrist. Extend the middle fingers with tips touching at an angle. Each index finger presses the third joint of its respective middle finger. Both thumbs are placed alongside the respective index fingers, with wrists joined. The mantra:

*Oṃ maṇa-artha paripūraka-āya svāhā.*

"Whether at a site with an established altar or without one, simply facing the image: if there is an altar, use the mantra mudrā method to empower the incense and flowers before entering the practice hall to make offerings. If there is no practice hall and one simply faces the deity's image and performs the mudrā offering, that also works. Whether there is an altar or not, incense and flowers are one and the same: all deva-kings, benevolent spirits, vajra-beings, and others will rejoice.

"Next, the food-offering mudrā:

"Follow the previous incense-and-flower mudrā, but change the two little fingers and two ring fingers to press against the center of the palm. The mantra:

*Namo jāgite bhāpa-sarāte tāṭa-rā-ṭaḍa-nālāyaṃ-yā-jabha svāhā.*

"With this mudrā and mantra, when preparing to offer food, empower each food tray one by one with the mantra. After empowering each, then enter the practice hall and arrange them on the altar as offerings. If there is no food, simply face the deva-king's image each morning and perform the empty mudrā and recite the mantra as an offering; one will still obtain merit, and the deva-king will rejoice.

"Next, the lamp mudrā:

"The right little finger and ring finger are bent into the palm. The middle finger stands erect. The index finger is also bent toward the palm. The thumb bends its upper joint to press the upper joint of the index finger. The thumb tip presses the side of the middle finger's middle joint. The mantra:

*Oṃ bhāpati-rai svāhā.*

"If there is a practice hall, one must empower the lamp with the mudrā and mantra before placing it within the altar as an offering. If there is no altar, simply place the deva-king's image in a clean room and still empower the lamp with the mudrā and mantra for the offering. The divine power of the light illuminates all places without obstruction, and the deva-king rejoices.

"The four deva-kings' boundary mudrā:

"Both little fingers are bent into the palm, right pressing left. The two middle fingers and ring fingers stand erect, one *cun* apart. Both index fingers press against both thumbs, which are bent into the palm. The mantra:

*Oṃ bhātiya-bandha-bandha haṃ hūṃ bi svāhā.*

"With this mudrā, establish the boundary again, sealing above and below three times each. All evil māras will be far scattered. Then raise the mudrā to the four directions, east, west, south, and north, in a complete circuit. Form the mudrā and recite the mantra to empower; all māra-obstacles will flee and not dare approach the practice hall. Establish the boundary three or four times according to the method; only then invite the deva-king. One obtains the power of accomplishment and becomes fearless.

"Next, the invitation mudrā:

"The right hand grasps the left wrist from the front. Above the crown, the four fingers of the left hand beckon back and forth. The mantra:

*Namo vaiśravaṇa-sāya-mādhi-iṣṭra-sāya-siddha-bhava-tubhata svāhā.*

"With this mudrā, recite the mantra seven times. The deva-king and his retinue will descend to the altar assembly. Say aloud: 'Deva-king, with compassion, please take your seat and accept this small offering. May all the retinue members summoned each take their seat and accept my offering.' Then kneel, holding an incense-burner, and before the deva-king, with utmost sincerity, respectfully invite all the spirit retinues, yakṣas, rākṣasas, all buddhas, bodhisattvas, vajra-warriors, and others to descend to the practice hall and bear witness for me. 'May Vaiśravaṇa, the Deva-king of the North, descend to the practice hall. May I attain the accomplishment of dharma-power and spiritual penetration, obtain great sovereign freedom, and have all my wishes fulfilled. May heaven and earth be moved, sovereign and ministers be in harmony, military forces support one another, and the people be loving and devoted. May all undertakings turn out as desired. If there is a divine sign and it proves true, may the deva-king manifest a transformed appearance at this place, letting me know the verification.' The deva-king's great-body dhāraṇī:

*Namo ratna-trayāya namaś caṇḍa-vajrapāṇaye mahā-yakṣa-senāpataye namo atha-kalu-bhūta-rāya-adhiśaya-vaiśravaṇa-vacana-sāya-mahā-rāja-sāya-yakṣa-adhipata-sāya-vāmata-sāya-satūrta-sāya-bhāśana-pravāha-ḍana-sāya-hima-dhāṭaṭani-praśayā mī tadyathā kuṣai mī kuṣai mī kuṣa-viśara-mada-sāya-mahā-rāja ai-bandha-kanīta-rā-macca-tu svāhā.*

"If anyone upholds this and purely recites it 300,000 times, the deva-king will empower them. In all undertakings and activities, whatever arises in thought, in subjugation, and in all matters of glory, rank, and official position, I shall comply with whatever your heart wishes. If anyone begins on the first or fifteenth day of the month and recites 300,000 times, and constructs an altar accompanied by music, then even with the slightest particle of a sincere mind following in devotion, I shall constantly guard and protect them, as one guards the pupil of the eye, as one guards one's own life. If one holds incense in the mouth while reciting and the mind is known to self and others, and if one constantly recites my mantra without ceasing, I shall follow that person whether sitting or lying down, I shall follow that person going out or coming in. Whatever that person's wishes are, I shall fulfill them. If one recites the mantra 108 times each day, one eliminates the transgressions of 108 kalpas from one's body. If one recites my mantra a full 10,000 times, one can extinguish the grave transgressions of the 84,000 forms of the ten evils and five heinous acts, including destroying stūpas, breaking precepts, and being unfilial; all will be entirely eliminated. If one recites the body-mantra 100,000 times facing the deva-king, one's affairs will also begin to be fulfilled in small ways. If one makes daily offerings to my image, and furthermore performs intentional music and milk-porridge offerings on my behalf, I shall manifest in a body of golden armor and fulfill all wishes.

"If one compounds various incenses, taking ox-bezoar and realgar in equal portions, empowers them, and places them beneath the feet of my image, I shall grant the accomplishment of the incense-method. According to your wish, anoint your own forehead with it. Wherever you go, all who see you, whether kings, princes, ministers, officials, nobles, or common people, will rejoice, and soldiers will feel affection.

"If one can, each day at midday, face north before the image and recite my heart-mantra a full 300,000 times, I shall manifest in the body of a great general, holding a halberd and sword, and appear before you. I shall appear in your dreams, or from atop a distant mountain peak, let you see me, and fulfill all your wishes. If you do not see my body, I shall still comply with your wishes and respond with empowerment as you contemplate.

"The heart-mantra of the Northern Deva-king:

*Namo ratna-trayāya namaś caṇḍa-vajrapāṇaye mahā-yakṣa-senāpataye namo atha-guru-yakṣa-sāya-mahā-rāja-mara-hūṃ-ṇa-lampra-viśami-sarva-satva-hitiṣṭhānāṃ oṃ maṇi-bhadrāya svāhā pūrṇa-bhadrāya svāhā maṇi-rtāya svāhā siddhi-kārāya svāhā vaiśravaṇāya svāhā nandanādhāya svāhā.*

"If anyone upholds and recites my heart-mantra and makes various offerings, my entire body, mind, and intention shall be upon you. I shall cherish you immeasurably. If anyone seeks wealth, seeks official rank, or seeks any other desire of the heart, I shall of myself cause your wishes to be fulfilled. I shall not allow evil persons to harm you. Those who uphold my Dharma, I shall cause to obtain wealth, happiness, peace, handsome sons and daughters, servants, carriages, the five grains and beans, wheat, fields and estates, wives and concubines. I shall certainly and definitely grant you all the accomplishment of every enjoyment, fulfilling your wishes. Even until the end of life, in whatever place of rebirth, I shall follow your wishes and never be contrary. If this is not true, then I am not worthy to be called the Dharma-protecting Deva-king, and I shall willingly fall into the Avīci Hell for your sake. But if those among you who uphold and recite should generate even the slightest doubt within my Dharma, or slander or revile it, know that this is because their hellish karmic conditions are not yet exhausted. Know that such persons have no hope of escape.

"You people who remember and uphold this: with incense, flowers, oil, ghee-lamps, and water-altars, install the image within and offer various grasses, fruits, food and drink, fried cakes, milk-curd, porridge, ghee, and honey on trays, making offerings according to the method, and perform music, hymns, and melodious praises. Thus each day, and on the first and fifteenth of each month, and the seventh day of the first month, face the image, recite, and practice circumambulation. I shall manifest my body, or within the altar bestow various treasures upon you to your satisfaction. Or following whatever direction your mind and actions go, in all places, east, west, south, north, the four intermediate directions, above, and below, and even in your night-dreams, I shall grant it all to you, making one fearless. In battle, even when swords and halberds are striking, none will be able to perceive you. Only keep my name, the mantra phrases, and the mudrā in your mind and hands, and whatever direction you are aimed at, all evil enemies, hostile soldiers, and bandits in that quarter will bind themselves, scatter of their own accord, fall of their own accord, destroy themselves, or go mad of their own accord. None can harm those who practice my Dharma.

"If anyone holding my heart-mantra compounds incense to hold in the mouth when meeting noble persons, take musk, turmeric, camphor, and cloves in whatever amounts, and compound them. Store in a gold or silver container. If no gold or silver container is available, use a white glass container. If no glass container is available, use a porcelain container for the compounded incense."
