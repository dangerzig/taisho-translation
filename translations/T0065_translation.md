# 伏婬經
## Sūtra on Subduing Desire

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0065

Translated from the Chinese. Western Jin, translated by Faju

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Bhagavat was in the city of Śrāvastī, in Jeta's Grove, the park of Anāthapiṇḍada. At that time, the householder Anāthapiṇḍada went to where the World-Honored One was. Having arrived, he prostrated at the World-Honored One's feet and stood to one side. Having stood to one side, the householder Anāthapiṇḍada addressed the World-Honored One: "Sir, World-Honored One! How many types of indulgence in desire are there in the world that can be known?"

"Householder! In the world there are ten types of indulgence in desire. What are these ten? Here, householder, a certain one who indulges in desire acquires wealth unlawfully, harming others, and acquires wealth unlawfully, harming himself. He does not provide for his own comfort, nor for his parents, wife and children, guests, servants and slaves, nor for śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas. He does not perform beneficial deeds, doing good to attain good and gain birth in heaven. Thus, householder, this is one type of indulgence in desire.

"Furthermore, householder, a certain one who indulges in desire acquires wealth unlawfully, harming others, and acquires wealth unlawfully, harming himself, yet he provides for his own comfort, for his parents, wife and children, guests, servants and slaves. However, he does not give to śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas, doing good to attain good and gain birth in a good destination. Thus, householder, this is one type of indulgence in desire.

"Furthermore, householder, a certain one who indulges in desire acquires wealth unlawfully, harming others, and acquires wealth unlawfully, harming himself, yet he provides for his own comfort, for his parents, wife and children, guests, servants and slaves, and is able to give to śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas, doing good to attain good and gain birth in a good destination. Thus, householder, this is one type of indulgence in desire.

"Furthermore, householder, a certain one who indulges in desire acquires wealth lawfully. Having acquired wealth lawfully, he does not provide for his own comfort, nor for his parents, wife and children, servants and slaves, nor does he give to śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas, doing good to attain good and gain birth in a good destination. Thus, householder, this is one type of indulgence in desire.

"Furthermore, householder, a certain one who indulges in desire acquires wealth lawfully. Having acquired wealth lawfully, he provides for his own comfort, and for his parents, wife and children, servants and slaves, but does not give to śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas, doing good to attain good and gain birth in a good destination. Thus, householder, this is one type of indulgence in desire.

"Furthermore, householder, a certain one who indulges in desire acquires wealth lawfully. Having acquired wealth lawfully, he provides for his own comfort, and for his parents, wife and children, servants and slaves, and gives to śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas, doing good to attain good and gain birth in a good destination. Thus, householder, this is one type of indulgence in desire.

"Furthermore, householder, a certain one who indulges in desire acquires wealth lawfully without harming others, and acquires wealth lawfully without harming himself, yet he does not provide for his own comfort, nor for his parents, wife and children, servants and slaves, nor does he give to śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas, doing good to attain good and gain birth in a good destination. Thus, householder, this is one type of indulgence in desire.

"Furthermore, householder, a certain one who indulges in desire acquires wealth lawfully without harming others, and acquires wealth lawfully without harming himself. He provides for his own comfort, and for his parents, wife and children, servants and slaves, but does not give to śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas, doing good to attain good and gain birth in a good destination. Thus, householder, this is one type of indulgence in desire.

"Furthermore, householder, a certain one who indulges in desire acquires wealth lawfully without harming others, and acquires wealth lawfully without harming himself. He provides for his own comfort, and for his parents, wife and children, servants and slaves, and gives to śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas, doing good to attain good and gain birth in a good destination. Yet having obtained his wealth, he becomes defiled by it, deeply defiled, does not see the danger in it, does not know how to relinquish it, and greedily consumes it. Thus, householder, this is one type of indulgence in desire.

"Furthermore, householder, a certain one who indulges in desire acquires wealth lawfully without harming others, and acquires wealth lawfully without harming himself. He provides for his own comfort, and for his parents, wife and children, servants and slaves, and gives to śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas, doing good to attain good and gain birth in a good destination. Having obtained his wealth, he is neither defiled nor attached, does not delight in it, does not dwell in it, knows the danger in it, and is able to relinquish and let go while consuming it. Thus, householder, this is one type of indulgence in desire.

"Here, householder, the one who indulges in desire and acquires wealth unlawfully, harming others, acquires wealth unlawfully, harming himself, and does not provide for his own comfort, nor for his parents, wife and children, servants and slaves, and does not give to śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas, doing good to attain good and gain birth in a good destination: this, householder, among the types of indulgence in desire, I declare to be the most base and evil.

"Here, householder, the one who indulges in desire and acquires wealth unlawfully, harming others, acquires wealth unlawfully, harming himself, yet provides for his own comfort, and for his parents, wife and children, servants and slaves, but does not give to śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas, doing good to attain good and gain birth in a good destination: this, householder, this type of indulgence is slightly superior, slightly superior.

"Here, householder, the one who indulges in desire and acquires wealth lawfully without harming others, acquires wealth lawfully without harming himself, provides for his own comfort, and for his parents, wife and children, servants and slaves, gives to śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas, doing good to attain good and gain birth in a good destination, and having obtained his wealth, is neither defiled nor attached, does not hold to it or delight in it, knows the danger in it, relinquishes, lets go, and then consumes it: this, householder, among the types of indulgence in desire, is the most superior, most wonderful, highest, best, supreme and unsurpassed. It is like, householder, from a cow comes milk, from milk comes curds, from curds comes ghee, from ghee comes butter, from butter comes clarified butter. This is declared the most superior, most wonderful, and unsurpassed. In the same way, householder, among all these types of indulgence in desire, this type of indulgence is the most superior, most wonderful, supremely wonderful, highest, unsurpassed, and unsurpassed." He spoke in verse:

> Whether unlawfully amassing wealth
> Or lawfully giving by the Dharma;
> Neither giving nor consuming,
> Nor giving for the sake of merit:
> Both are tainted with miserliness,
> Evil conduct consuming this desire.
> Lawfully seeking wealth,
> Wishing to give for the sake of merit,
> Both giving and consuming,
> And able to create merit:
> Both are free from miserly taint,
> And all possess this mastery of desire.
> Those who practice with wisdom,
> Mastering desire in all their conduct,
> Knowing change and knowing contentment,
> Content while consuming:
> Those who practice with wisdom
> Most wonderfully master desire.

Thus spoke the Buddha. The householder Anāthapiṇḍada, hearing what the World-Honored One had spoken, rejoiced and was delighted.
