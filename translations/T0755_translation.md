# 佛說淨意優婆塞所問經
## Sūtra of the Questions of the Upāsaka Śuddhāśaya, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0755

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dānapāla (施護)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was in the land of Śrāvastī, in the Jeta Grove of Anāthapiṇḍada, together with a great assembly. At that time in the city there was a clansman, a son of a dūṇiya family, named Śuddhāśaya ("Pure Intention"), who came to the Buddha's dwelling. Having arrived, he saw the Buddha's excellent marks and his heart was filled with joy. He offered various praises, joined his palms in reverence, bowed at the Buddha's feet, and withdrew to stand to one side.

Then the dūṇiya's son Śuddhāśaya addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I have a small question I wish to ask. I pray the Buddha, the World-Honored One, will grant me leave to speak."

The Buddha said: "Śuddhāśaya! Whatever doubts you have, you may now freely ask. The Buddha will resolve them for you one by one."

Then the dūṇiya's son Śuddhāśaya addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I see that among all the people of the world, their various characteristics and conduct each differ. I see some who obtain long life and some who are short-lived; some who have much illness and some who have little illness; some who are beautiful and some who are ugly; some for whom things go as they wish and some for whom things do not go as they wish; some who are born in low families and some who are born in high families; some who are wealthy and some who are poor; some who are foolish and some who are wise. Why are there such various differences? What causes and conditions account for such retribution? I pray the World-Honored One will explain this for me."

The Buddha said: "Śuddhāśaya! You should know that because the causal actions of sentient beings in the world are different, the fruits they obtain are each different." Then the dūṇiya's son Śuddhāśaya again addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! As the Buddha says that causes and fruits differ, I pray the World-Honored One will explain this briefly. If it is explained at length, I may not be able to comprehend the meaning." The Buddha said: "Śuddhāśaya! Listen carefully and reflect well. I shall now explain it for you." Then the dūṇiya's son Śuddhāśaya received the teaching and listened.

The Buddha said: "Śuddhāśaya! You should know that in the world there is a class of men and women who harbor malice in their hearts. Sometimes holding a knife, sometimes wielding a club, they seek opportunities to kill living beings. Without a compassionate mind, without any sense of shame, whether killing with their own hands or instructing others to kill: because of this, when the body perishes and life ends, they fall into the evil destinies and suffer the torment of the hells. When the retribution in the hells is exhausted, even if they are born as humans, wherever they are born their lifespans are short. Śuddhāśaya, you should know: from the cause of killing, one reaps the fruit of a short life.

"Furthermore, Śuddhāśaya, in the world there is a class of men and women who harbor no malice, who do not hold knives or clubs, who do not kill living beings, who possess a compassionate mind and have great shame. Because of this, when the body perishes and life ends, they are born in the wholesome destinies, receiving a body among devas and humans. When the retribution in heaven is exhausted and they are reborn among humans, wherever they are born their lifespans are long. Śuddhāśaya, you should know: from the cause of not killing, one reaps the fruit of a long life.

"Furthermore, Śuddhāśaya, in the world there is a class of men and women who sometimes take up clubs, wood, tiles, or stones and hurl them at sentient beings, or who with their own hands harass and injure others. Because of this, when the body perishes and life ends, they fall into the evil destinies and suffer the torment of the hells. When the retribution in the hells is exhausted, even if they are born as humans, wherever they are born they have much illness. Śuddhāśaya, you should know: from the cause of harming, one reaps the fruit of much illness.

"Furthermore, Śuddhāśaya, in the world there is a class of men and women who do not take up clubs, wood, tiles, or stones to hurl at sentient beings, and who do not with their own hands harass and injure others. Because of this, when the body perishes and life ends, they are born in the wholesome destinies, receiving a body among devas and humans. When the retribution in heaven is exhausted and they are reborn among humans, wherever they are born they have little illness. Śuddhāśaya, you should know: from the cause of not harming, one reaps the fruit of little illness.

"Furthermore, Śuddhāśaya, in the world there is a class of men and women who constantly give rise to resentment, hatred, and anger, generating various faults. Because of this, when the body perishes and life ends, they fall into the evil destinies and suffer the torment of the hells. When the retribution in the hells is exhausted, even if they are born as humans, wherever they are born their appearance is ugly. Śuddhāśaya, you should know: from the cause of anger, one reaps the fruit of ugliness.

"Furthermore, Śuddhāśaya, in the world there is a class of men and women who do not give rise to resentment, hatred, or anger, and who do not generate faults. Because of this, when the body perishes and life ends, they are born in the wholesome destinies, receiving a body among devas and humans. When the retribution in heaven is exhausted and they are reborn among humans, wherever they are born their appearance is beautiful. Śuddhāśaya, you should know: from the cause of not being angry, one reaps the fruit of beauty.

"Furthermore, Śuddhāśaya, in the world there is a class of men and women who, when they see others obtaining gain, reputation, and all manner of agreeable things, use expedient means to obstruct and prevent them from obtaining these. Because of this, when the body perishes and life ends, they fall into the evil destinies and suffer the torment of the hells. When the retribution in the hells is exhausted, even if they are born as humans, wherever they are born, whatever they undertake does not go as they wish. Śuddhāśaya, you should know: from the cause of obstructing others' agreeable things, one reaps the fruit of things not going as one wishes.

"Furthermore, Śuddhāśaya, in the world there is a class of men and women who, when they see others obtaining gain, reputation, and all manner of agreeable things, do not use expedient means to obstruct them but wish for others to obtain these. Because of this, when the body perishes and life ends, they are born in the wholesome destinies, receiving a body among devas and humans. When the retribution in heaven is exhausted and they are reborn among humans, wherever they are born, whatever they undertake goes as they wish. Śuddhāśaya, you should know: from the cause of not obstructing others' agreeable things, one reaps the fruit of things going as one wishes.

"Furthermore, Śuddhāśaya, in the world there is a class of men and women who, where they should show respect to others, do not show respect; where they should show reverence, do not show reverence; where they should make offerings, do not make offerings. They constantly give rise to arrogance and exalt themselves. Because of this, when the body perishes and life ends, they fall into the evil destinies and suffer the torment of the hells. When the retribution in the hells is exhausted, even if they are born as humans, wherever they are born, they are always born in low families. Śuddhāśaya, you should know: from the cause of arrogance and self-exaltation, one reaps the fruit of low birth.

"Furthermore, Śuddhāśaya, in the world there is a class of men and women who, where they should show respect to others, do show respect; where they should show reverence, do show reverence; where they should make offerings, do make offerings. They do not give rise to arrogance or exalt themselves. Because of this, when the body perishes and life ends, they are born in the wholesome destinies, receiving a body among devas and humans. When the retribution in heaven is exhausted and they are reborn among humans, wherever they are born, they are always born in high families. Śuddhāśaya, you should know: from the cause of not being arrogant or self-exalting, one reaps the fruit of high birth.

"Furthermore, Śuddhāśaya, in the world there is a class of men and women whose hearts are stingy. They do not give food, drink, clothing, bedding, medicine, lodging, seats, or even paste-incense or powdered incense to śramaṇas, brahmins, and others. Because of this, when the body perishes and life ends, they fall into the evil destinies and suffer the torment of the hells. When the retribution in the hells is exhausted, even if they are born as humans, wherever they are born they are poor and destitute. Śuddhāśaya, you should know: from the cause of stinginess, one reaps the fruit of poverty.

"Furthermore, Śuddhāśaya, in the world there is a class of men and women whose hearts are not stingy. They constantly give food, drink, clothing, bedding, medicine, lodging, seats, and even paste-incense and powdered incense to śramaṇas, brahmins, and others. Because of this, when the body perishes and life ends, they are born in the wholesome destinies, receiving a body among devas and humans. When the retribution in heaven is exhausted and they are reborn among humans, wherever they are born they are wealthy and free. Śuddhāśaya, you should know: from the cause of not being stingy, one reaps the fruit of wealth.

"Furthermore, Śuddhāśaya, in the world there is a class of men and women whose hearts do not delight in the sūtras of the right Dharma. Furthermore, they do not inquire of śramaṇas and brahmins about the meaning of things: What is good? What is not good? What should be undertaken? What should not be undertaken? What should be practiced? What should not be practiced? They are unable to make such inquiries. Because of this, when the body perishes and life ends, they fall into the evil destinies and suffer the torment of the hells. When the retribution in the hells is exhausted, even if they are born as humans, wherever they are born they are foolish and without wisdom. Śuddhāśaya, you should know: from the cause of not delighting in the Dharma and not inquiring about the Dharma, one reaps the fruit of foolishness.

"Furthermore, Śuddhāśaya, in the world there is a class of men and women whose hearts constantly delight in the sūtras of the right Dharma. Furthermore, they are able to inquire of śramaṇas and brahmins about the meaning of things: What is good? What is not good? What should be undertaken? What should not be undertaken? What should be practiced? What should not be practiced? They are able to make such inquiries. Because of this, when the body perishes and life ends, they are born in the wholesome destinies, receiving a body among devas and humans. When the retribution in heaven is exhausted and they are reborn among humans, wherever they are born they possess great wisdom. Śuddhāśaya, you should know: from the cause of delighting in the Dharma and inquiring about the Dharma, one reaps the fruit of wisdom.

"Furthermore, Śuddhāśaya, as I have explained above, these various differences of cause and fruit are such. Therefore you should know: the cause of short life yields the fruit of short life; the cause of long life yields the fruit of long life; the cause of much illness yields the fruit of much illness; the cause of little illness yields the fruit of little illness; the cause of ugliness yields the fruit of ugliness; the cause of beauty yields the fruit of beauty; the cause of things not going as one wishes yields the fruit of things not going as one wishes; the cause of things going as one wishes yields the fruit of things going as one wishes; the cause of low birth yields the fruit of low birth; the cause of high birth yields the fruit of high birth; the cause of poverty yields the fruit of poverty; the cause of wealth yields the fruit of wealth; the cause of foolishness yields the fruit of foolishness; the cause of wisdom yields the fruit of wisdom. Śuddhāśaya! Therefore you should truly understand: the causes and fruits of short life are spoken by the Buddha; the causes and fruits of long life are spoken by the Buddha; the causes and fruits of much illness are spoken by the Buddha; the causes and fruits of little illness are spoken by the Buddha; the causes and fruits of ugliness are spoken by the Buddha; the causes and fruits of beauty are spoken by the Buddha; the causes and fruits of things not going as one wishes are spoken by the Buddha; the causes and fruits of things going as one wishes are spoken by the Buddha; the causes and fruits of low birth are spoken by the Buddha; the causes and fruits of high birth are spoken by the Buddha; the causes and fruits of poverty are spoken by the Buddha; the causes and fruits of wealth are spoken by the Buddha; the causes and fruits of foolishness are spoken by the Buddha; the causes and fruits of wisdom are spoken by the Buddha. Therefore the wise should practice as taught.

"Śuddhāśaya, you should know: the Buddha says that long life is the supreme good reward; little illness is the supreme good reward; beauty is the supreme good reward; things going as one wishes is the supreme good reward; high birth is the supreme good reward; wealth is the supreme good reward; wisdom is the supreme good reward. Śuddhāśaya! These and other such dharmas are taught by the Buddha. You should study and practice them."

Then the dūṇiya's son Śuddhāśaya, having heard this Dharma, was filled with joy and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! It is as though in the dark places of the world, one cannot find the road ahead, and though one has eyes, one cannot see. If a wise person brings a torch, the road ahead can all be found. The World-Honored One is just like this: skillfully teaching the right Dharma, destroying all ignorance, and distinguishing each dharma one by one. This is good; this is not good. This should be undertaken; this should not be undertaken. This should be practiced; this should not be practiced. Such dharmas as these: only a Buddha, a Tathāgata, Worthy One, Perfectly Awakened One appearing in the world can skillfully teach them. Today I have quickly obtained a great benefit. I now take refuge in the Buddha, take refuge in the Dharma, and take refuge in the bhikṣu-Saṃgha. I uphold the precepts beginning with not killing as an upāsaka. I pray the Buddha, the World-Honored One, will accept me." The Buddha said: "Excellent! I now accept you."

Then the upāsaka Śuddhāśaya addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! Now if the clansmen of this city of Śrāvastī hear of this matter, they should all reflect: 'The son of the dūṇiya family, through the long night, has skillfully brought benefit and guidance to sentient beings.'" When the upāsaka Śuddhāśaya had spoken these words, he bowed to the Buddha and withdrew.
