# 佛說大七寶陀羅尼經
## Sūtra of the Great Seven-Jewel Dhāraṇī Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1368

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown (失譯)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was dwelling at Jetavana, Anāthapiṇḍada's park. The Buddha addressed Ānanda: "Receive and hold this dhāraṇī of the Great Seven Jewels." The World-Honored One thereupon proclaimed it:

> *śadyā dattaṃ tijalyatī pārajñā pāraśikṣā gṛṇabija*

"Ānanda! If there is one who receives, holds, reads, recites, and practices this dhāraṇī throughout the duration of their life, all enemies and adversaries will be made to rejoice; fire will be unable to burn them, blades unable to wound them, water unable to drown them; they will not be seized by ghosts or demons of any quarter, nor by devas, nāgas, asuras, gandharvas, or any beings human or non-human. Ānanda! This dhāraṇī of the seven jewels — whether one is in the midst of fire or water, in the midst of enemies and brigands, upon ingesting poison, or upon encountering elemental poison — one should mindfully recite this dhāraṇī. If one is seized by terror such that the hair stands on end and the like, all such fear will be entirely relieved. By the majestic virtue of Vipaśyin Buddha, the spiritual power of Śikhin, the wisdom of Viśvabhū, the strength of Krakucchanda Buddha, the precepts of Kanakamuni, the merit of Kāśyapa, and the vigor of Śākyamuni — may all sentient beings be completely freed from terror and attain peace and good fortune."

At that time Ānanda, having heard what the Buddha had taught, rejoiced and reverently undertook to practice it.
