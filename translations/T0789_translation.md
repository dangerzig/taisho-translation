# 金剛頂瑜伽念珠經
## Sūtra on the Rosary of the Vajraśekhara Yoga

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 789

Translated from the Chinese. Tang, translated by Amoghavajra (不空)

---

At that time, the World-Honored One Vairocana addressed Vajrapāṇi, saying: "Excellent, excellent! For the sake of all bodhisattvas who practice the mantra path, expound the various ritual procedures. Out of compassion for all sentient beings of the future, expound the merits and excellent benefits of the rosary. Through hearing such wondrous principles, they will swiftly attain siddhi."

At that time, the bodhisattva Vajrasattva said to the Buddha: "Yes, World-Honored One! I shall now expound this."

At that time, the bodhisattva Vajrasattva recited these verses:

> The beads represent the supreme fruit of the bodhisattva;
> The gap between them represents the severing of the āśravas;
> The threading cord represents Avalokiteśvara;
> The mother bead represents Amitāyus.
> Take care not to skip past it, lest one commit a transgression of the Dharma;
> All merit is accumulated through the rosary.
>
> A rosary of giant clam shell: one-fold merit;
> A rosary of soapberry seeds: two-fold merit;
> A rosary of iron beads: three-fold merit;
> A rosary of refined copper beads: four-fold merit;
> Crystal, pearl, and various precious gems —
> These rosaries yield a hundredfold merit;
> Śakra-seed beads: a thousandfold merit;
> Vajra-seed beads: koṭi-fold merit;
> A lotus-seed rosary: a thousand koṭi;
> Bodhi-seed beads: immeasurable merit.
>
> For the Buddha family, recite with bodhi seeds;
> For the Vajra family practice, use vajra seeds;
> For the Ratna family, recite with various gems;
> For the Padma family, use lotus seeds;
> For the Karma family rosary,
> Various beads intermingled should be strung together.
>
> Rosaries are distinguished into four types:
> The highest, most excellent, medium, and lesser.
> One thousand and eighty beads constitute the highest;
> One hundred and eight beads are the most excellent;
> Fifty-four beads constitute the medium;
> Twenty-seven beads are of the lesser class.
>
> Hold the beads with both hands at the level of the heart;
> In meditative stillness, free from thoughts, with focused mind;
> In one-pointed yoga with one's tutelary deity —
> One attains accomplishment in both principle and practice.
>
> Whether placed upon the crown topknot or worn upon the body,
> Or placed on the neck or upon the arm,
> Whatever words are spoken become acts of recitation;
> Through this recitation, the three karmas are purified.
>
> By placing upon the crown topknot, one is purified of ānantarya crimes;
> By wearing on the neck, one is purified of the four grave offenses;
> Held in the hand or worn upon the arm, all sins are eliminated —
> This enables the practitioner to swiftly attain purification.
>
> If one practices mantra and dhāraṇī
> And recites the names of the Tathāgatas and bodhisattvas,
> One shall obtain immeasurable, supreme merit,
> And all excellent aspirations sought shall be accomplished.

The method for empowering and stringing the rosary is precisely as described in the *Susiddhikara Sūtra*. This Yoga Sūtra speaks only of the rosary's functions and principles, not of its physical characteristics. This should be understood.
