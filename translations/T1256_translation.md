# 佛說摩利支天陀羅尼呪經
## Sūtra of the Dhāraṇī Spell of Mārīcī Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1256

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown.

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Bhagavān was in Jetavana, Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in Śrāvastī, together with a great assembly of twelve hundred and fifty bhikṣus. At that time the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus: "There is a deva named Mārīcī who constantly travels ahead of the sun and moon. This Mārīcī: no one can see her, no one can catch her, she cannot be deceived by anyone, she cannot be bound by anyone, no one can take her wealth, and no enemy can gain advantage over her."

The Buddha told the bhikṣus: "If anyone knows the name of Mārīcī, that person likewise cannot be seen, cannot be caught, cannot be deceived by anyone, cannot have their wealth taken, and no enemy can gain advantage over them." The Buddha told the bhikṣus: "If good men or good women who know the name of Mārīcī should speak these words: 'I, disciple so-and-so, because I know the name of Mārīcī, no one can see me, no one can catch me, no one can deceive me, no one can bind me, no one can take my wealth, and no enemy can gain advantage over me.'" At that time the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus: "This Mārīcī has a dhāraṇī spell that can guard and protect people." He then spoke the spell:

*Tadyathā arka-māsī makha-māsī jīvara-māsī jīvara-māsī mahā-jīvara-māsī andhari-dhana-māsī*

"On the road, protect me. Off the road, protect me. In the daytime, protect me. In the night, protect me. Among enemies, protect me. From danger by kings, protect me. From danger by bandits, protect me. From danger by water, protect me. From danger by fire, protect me. From danger by disease, protect me. *Ākuli-ākuli-mārīcī-te kili-kili anti-anti*. In all places and at all times, protect me, disciple so-and-so. *Svāhā*."

The Buddha told the bhikṣus: "If good men or good women, bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, upāsikās, kings, great ministers, or any people hear this dhāraṇī spell of Mārīcī and single-mindedly receive and uphold it, they will not be harmed by any of the aforementioned evils."

The Buddha told the bhikṣus: "If good men or good women who copy this sūtra, receive, uphold, read, and recite it, single-mindedly observing the precepts and purifying a room, plastering the ground with fragrant mud, for seven days and seven nights recite this dhāraṇī spell of Mārīcī to a full one hundred and eight times, then in whatever battle or confrontation with enemies and bandits, all will lay down their weapons. When traveling, if one copies this dhāraṇī and places it in one's topknot or carries it within one's garments upon one's person, no evil whatsoever can cause harm, and all will retreat and scatter, with none daring to oppose.

"If one encounters illness, one should invite a bhikṣu or bhikṣuṇī, upāsaka or upāsikā who upholds the pure precepts, and as before purify a room, plaster the ground with fragrant mud, burn various fine incenses, set out seven platters of fruit and cakes, spread out five-colored hangings, set out five-colored rice, and invite Mārīcī. Light lamps and keep them burning for seven days and seven nights, and recite this Mārīcī dhāraṇī spell sūtra to a full two hundred times. All disease-causing ghosts will generate minds of compassion and release the sick person, who will immediately be cured.

"If one has been arrested and detained by the authorities, likewise purify a room as before, set up offerings according to the method and arrange seats, light lamps and keep them burning for seven days and seven nights, and recite this Mārīcī dhāraṇī spell sūtra five hundred times. When one's wish has been fulfilled, hold a feast and dismiss the assembly. All calamities and difficulties will be completely eliminated."

At that time the bhikṣus, having heard what the Buddha proclaimed, were all greatly delighted, faithfully received the teaching, and upheld it.
