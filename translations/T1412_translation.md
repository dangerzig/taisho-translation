# 佛說寶生陀羅尼經
## Sūtra of the Dhāraṇī of Jewel-Birth Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1412

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dānapāla (施護)

---

*[Dhāraṇī]*

> namaḥ ratna-raśmi-candra-pratimaṇite-vinigate jagatīśvarā-rājāya tathāgatāya arhate samyaksaṃbuddhāya | tadyathā | ratne ratne ratna-kiraṇi ratna-pratimaṇḍite ratna-saṃbhave ratna-prabhā ratnodgate svāhā

If there are sentient beings who receive, uphold, and make offerings to the dhāraṇī name of this Tathāgata, those persons, life after life, will attain the station of a wheel-turning king, accomplish brahmacarya, possess great supernatural powers, and obtain ten kinds of dhāraṇī. Moreover, they will encounter Buddhas and Tathāgatas as numerous as the sands of the Ganges without any falsity, and for koṭis of kalpas they will not enter the path of saṃsāra, will not sever the seed of bodhi, will not lose the mind of bodhi, will permanently extinguish all transgressions, and will attain the sambhogakāya Tathāgata.

If a person recites this dhāraṇī for a full seven days, that person will attain purity of the divine eye. If a person hears it with the ear and continuously calls it to mind, that person will certainly attain bodhi, and the roots of merit planted in past lives will also manifest before them.

If one transmits it to even a single person, all ānantarya karmic offenses will be entirely eliminated and saṃsāra permanently severed. That person will not be harmed by water, fire, or thieves; their sense faculties will not be deficient; the manifold diseases will not arise; ghosts and demons will not afflict them; and they will be beloved and respected by all. In future lives they will receive and uphold the subtle and wondrous Dharma of the Tathāgata and make offerings to all Buddhas.

If a person, having heard this, is joyful at heart and pays homage and offers praise, that person's merit will be immeasurable and boundless. In each successive life, a wondrous fragrance will emerge from their mouth, spreading one yojana in breadth; from the pores of their body there will constantly be radiance that always illuminates around them; and they will perpetually perform the victorious deeds of the Tathāgata, like Ānanda. Such are these inconceivable merits.
