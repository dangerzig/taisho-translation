# 釋迦文尼佛金剛一乘修行儀軌法品
## Chapter on the Ritual Methods of the Vajra One-Vehicle Practice of Śākyamuni Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0938

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown

---

Homage to Vairocana Tathāgata, the great ocean of wisdom, and to the Secret Lord Vajrapāṇi, the great bodhisattva! I, Śubhakarasiṃha, having received the Buddha's holy command and consulted the Sanskrit text, have obtained the secret ritual procedures for cultivating the gate of the three mysteries. Therefore I now teach the yoga practice of Śākyamuni World-Honored One.

Those who wish to practice this method should first generate the mind of unsurpassed, true, and right awakening. The body should wear the holy garment of patience. The mouth should constantly read and recite. When the three mysteries thus correspond without transgression, one should then go to a yoga ācārya and receive the secret rites. By maintaining recitation and practice, one will swiftly attain accomplishment.

Vairocana said: "That ācārya, if he sees a sentient being who is a worthy vessel of the Dharma, free from all defilements, possessed of great faith and understanding, diligent and courageous, deeply believing, and constantly mindful of benefiting others: if a disciple possesses such characteristics, the ācārya should himself go and encourage that person. Furthermore, there are two kinds of disciples: first, one who has already generated the bodhicitta, to whom the master goes and offers encouragement; second, one who has not yet generated the bodhicitta, who goes to the master's place for encouragement. Among these, the one with wisdom is the most supreme. Having thus discerned, only then may transmission be given."

Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva addressed Vairocana Buddha: "I see the Buddha's form-body wearing a great jeweled crown. Within the crown, the emblem of the five Buddhas appears, neither cause nor effect. He abides in a great moon-disc, dwelling in empty space at the place of eternal quiescent light, constantly teaching the vajra secret doctrine, which śrāvakas and pratyekabuddhas cannot know. Or, seated upon a lotus platform, he manifests the reward-body and further teaches the vajra one-vehicle Dharma-realm, the mind-only unsurpassed secret Dharma. We, relying on the merit of inner realization, constantly see and constantly hear. Only those who have newly generated the aspiration are confused and do not understand. May the World-Honored One, in accordance with the minds of sentient beings, act for their benefit. If the Buddha permits, I wish to teach the method by which the Tathāgata manifests as Śākyamuni, so that foolish beings may obtain the Buddha's wisdom. May the Tathāgata, in accordance with each being's capacity, teach the Dharma. Having heard his skillful wisdom, may all sentient beings be shown the true and real aspect and awaken to the vajra vehicle, entering the gate of the syllable *a*."

Having spoken thus, Vairocana said: "Excellent, Samantabhadra! You know my mind and cause me to rejoice. In the past, I cultivated the gate of the three mysteries and realized the holding of the vajra. I sat upon the vajra wondrous bodhi-seat, subdued all the māras, and attained perfect enlightenment. Since then, I have passed through many ages. Sentient beings, by the power of their past merit, though they come to my assembly, see according to their capacity and hear according to their faculties. As you see me upon the summit of the form-realm in the fourth dhyāna attaining perfect enlightenment, I then descended to the summit of Mount Sumeru and, upon the Vajra Great Indra Altar, all the Tathāgatas invited me to turn the four kinds of Dharma-wheels. Within each wheel, there are thirty-seven holy ones, abiding in the samādhi that is beyond pity, subduing all the celestial māras and causing them to enter right view. Now is the proper time. I wish to further teach the unsurpassed secret Dharma of the Mahāyāna, and afterwards return to the path of perfect quiescence. The Tathāgata's secret spiritual powers must not be recklessly proclaimed among those without wisdom. I now grant permission. You should quickly teach the method of my manifested form in accordance with each category. Relying on the true and real Dharma ritual, you should teach what should be taught and keep secret what should be secret." Having spoken thus, he sat in silence.

At that time Vajrapāṇi, his heart surging with joy, appeared before the assembly and taught this Dharma:

If one wishes to repay the immeasurable grace of the supreme World-Honored One, first construct a maṇḍala. In the center, paint the image of Śākyamuni: his body golden in color, possessing the thirty-two major and eighty minor marks, wearing the kāṣāya robe in the aspect of the response-body teaching the Dharma. The wisdom hand forms the auspicious seal; the principle hand faces upward, placed before the wisdom hand. He sits in full cross-legged posture on a white lotus platform. Samantabhadra, Mañjuśrī, Avalokiteśvara, and Maitreya reside at the four corners as taught in the Garbhadhātu, each seated on a lotus in half cross-legged posture. Before the Buddha is the Tathāgata's bowl. To the right of the Buddha is a worthy vase containing flowers. Behind the Buddha is a monk's staff. To the left of the Buddha is a jeweled conch. Each is placed upon a lotus leaf, encircled by rays of light. Having painted the maṇḍala, in a quiet and secluded place where sages have attained the Way, or in a clean room, or before a reliquary stūpa, or beneath a tree on a mountaintop, choose a place and set up the maṇḍala.

Next, arrange the altar with adornments and offerings: unguent, seasonal flowers, burned incense, food and drink, lamplight, and argha fragrant water, making six kinds. Or one of wisdom may make offerings through principle alone. Bathe the body three times daily and nightly, or contemplate the principle and sprinkle the crown with the true and real mantra as water.

Each time one arrives at the practice-hall, bow. There are three kinds of bowing: first, voice-bowing; second, mind-bowing; third, body-bowing. These are the three-karma prostrations. Whether walking, standing, sitting, or lying down, if one recites "namaḥ," this is voice-bowing. Furthermore, at all times, though one does not produce sound, if within the mind alone one contemplates the World-Honored One's virtues, rejoices, and takes refuge, this is mind-bowing. Furthermore, placing the five limbs upon the ground, or joining the palms and lowering the head, this is body-bowing. Bowing does not necessarily require prostrating on the ground; taking refuge with reverence constitutes bowing. Therefore, one touches one's own head to the World-Honored One's lower feet, and this is called "bowing the head."

Next, confess karmic obstructions through both phenomenal and principle confession. Next, rejoice in the merits of both the holy and the ordinary. Next, request the Buddha to remain in the world. Next, dedicate merit and make vows. Then broadly arrange offerings, perform recitation, and chant Mahāyāna sūtras, doing each as one wishes.

If one abides in this Dharma, all that is needed will be accomplished. Without transmigrating from this body, one will see the Buddha's form-body. In life after life, one will be separated from all evil destinies and will constantly possess the merit of propagating the Dharma and benefiting beings, and will swiftly realize unsurpassed perfect enlightenment. Each attains accomplishment according to their faculties. If one seeks the fruit of a ṛṣi, the Buddha manifests as the ascetic Gautama and teaches the Dharma. If one seeks the Mahāyāna, Samantabhadra manifests in person and teaches the Dharma, gradually guiding that person to enter the Buddha-Way.

Next, the hand-seals. The Tathāgata's Bowl Seal:

First, with the right hand at the heart, palm facing up. Next, place the left hand palm-down upon the right hand. The tip of the left little finger touches the tip of the right thumb, and the tip of the left thumb touches the tip of the right little finger. The seal and mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ oṃ loka-pāla-dṛṣṭi-dhara-dhāra-dhāra mahā-nava-buddha-pātra svāhā*

This Dharma-seal and mantra possesses great vigor and is constantly empowered and protected by the spiritual power of all the Tathāgatas. If one forms this seal and recites this mantra once, then recollects the hell and hungry-ghost beings for one hundred and eight repetitions, all the hungry ghosts in hell will be fully satisfied with food. If one is traveling in a wilderness, also form this seal and recite this mantra: all the ghosts and spirits of the wilderness will not cause harm. If one wishes to make the bowl fly, also use this seal and mantra.

Next, the Tathāgata's Amṛta Seal:

With the right hand, the thumb presses across the nails of the index, middle, ring, and little fingers. The mantra:

*Oṃ iṃ gini bhūta-ni svāhā*

This seal and mantra enables the practitioner to realize the great liberation gate of the amṛta Dharma.

Next, the Tathāgata's Staff Seal:

First, with the right hand, bend the thumb into the palm. With the index, ring, and little fingers, grip tightly to make a fist. Bend the elbow with the palm level and forward. With the left hand, hold the corner of the kāṣāya robe, extending four inches beyond the tip, also bending the elbow and holding it level in front. The mantra:

*Oṃ dhūna-jīta dhāraṇa hūṃ*

This Dharma-mantra brings roots of merit to accomplishment and compassionate vows to perfection. If one encounters any evil or hostile being, form this seal to protect the body.

Next, the Great Dharma-Conch True-Word Seal:

The concentration and wisdom hands form open palms joined. Bend the wind and space fingers to cross. The mantra:

*Aṃ*

Next, the Root Seal of Vairocana's Transformation-Body:

The concentration hand: the water and space fingers pinch together. The wisdom hand likewise. The water and space fingers of both hands touch each other. The remaining earth, fire, and wind fingers stand upright and spread.

The true word of Śākyamuni Buddha:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ bha sarva-kleśa-nirṣū-dana sarva-dharma-vaśita-prāpta gagana-sama-sama svāhā*

With this mantra, concentrate single-mindedly in recitation. The praise for the response-body:

*Vame-kacīra-satā-piṇa-ni sūtana-sattvaṃ-paśeka-hasta-bhiri-kuṭi-mukha-ke-kāla-kṣa-na jisthiri-ya-divi-ke-jina-dvāri-prati-saṃstūta-sattvaṃ arje-kana-maṇi-ya ācala-ceṭa-na-mi bidhi-yaṃ*

The offering rites, invitations, and dismissals should all follow those of the Tathāgata Division, as taught in the *Susiddhikara* and other texts. Take Buddha-Eye as the division-mother, and the Wrathful Aparājita as the boundary-protecting Vidyārāja. Having thus practiced, exit the practice-hall and chant the Mahāyāna sūtras. The mind should constantly be fixed upon the one true realm without interruption. This Dharma is easily accomplished.

The teaching of the wondrous vajra-vehicle Dharma of Śākyamuni is thus concluded.
