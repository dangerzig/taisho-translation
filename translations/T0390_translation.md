# 佛臨涅槃記法住經
## Sūtra of the Buddha's Prediction on the Eve of Nirvāṇa Concerning the Duration of the Dharma

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0390

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Xuanzang (玄奘)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Bhagavān was in the city of Kuśinagara, in the Malla clan's birthplace, in the twin śāla grove, together with immeasurable, countless śrāvakas and bodhisattva-mahāsattvas, as well as devas, humans, asuras, and the rest, all in the great assembly gathered around him. At that time, the Bhagavān, on the eve of his parinirvāṇa, out of compassion for sentient beings, spoke to Ānanda in a gentle and kindly voice:

"I shall soon enter parinirvāṇa. All conditioned things are to be entirely relinquished; all buddha-works have been fully accomplished. I have proclaimed the Dharma that leaves behind the cave-dwelling, the wondrous Dharma of sweet dew, the Dharma of supreme sovereignty, the Dharma of utmost peace and happiness. This Dharma is deep and subtle, difficult to understand and difficult to know, beyond the reach of discursive thought, transcending the domain of discursive thought. It is what all the great sages have internally realized. I have also turned the unsurpassed wheel of the Dharma three times. This wheel is awesome and powerful, complete with twelve aspects, and no other śramaṇa, brahmin, deva, māra, or Brahmā is able to truly turn it. I have blown the great Dharma conch for all devas and humans, struck the great Dharma drum, awakening those who slept in the long night of ignorance. I have raised the great Dharma banner for all devas and humans, lit the great Dharma torch, universally illuminating all and dispelling darkness. I have built the great Dharma bridge for all sentient beings, fashioned the great Dharma ship, ferrying across all who are drowning in the torrent. I have poured forth the great Dharma stream for all sentient beings, sent down the great Dharma rain, so that all that was withered and parched has been moistened and refreshed. I have opened and revealed the correct path of liberation, guiding those in the world who had lost their way. Those sentient beings whom I should liberate have all been liberated. Those not yet liberated have all been given the causes and conditions for their future liberation. I have vanquished all non-Buddhist teachers. I have destroyed all wrong doctrines. I have overthrown the palaces of all the māras. I have defeated all the māra armies. With the correct lion's roar I have accomplished the great buddha-work, fulfilled the original vow of a great person, and guarded the Dharma-eye so that it is without damage or deficiency. I have transformed the śrāvakas and bestowed predictions upon the bodhisattvas. As the unsurpassed buddha-eye for the future, it shall illuminate the world without interruption. Ānanda! You and the others should diligently guard and protect this unsurpassed correct Dharma so that it does not perish. Ānanda! I now have nothing more to do. Only the great nirvāṇa is my destination."

At that time, Ānanda, hearing the Buddha's words, was overcome with grief. After a long while, he said: "I do not know: the unsurpassed correct Dharma that the Tathāgata obtained for sentient beings through three incalculable kalpas of arduous effort, after the Buddha's passing, how long will it remain in the world to benefit devas, humans, asuras, and the rest, before it gradually disappears?"

At that time, the World-Honored One again spoke to Ānanda in a compassionate voice: "In all the Buddhas' transformative manifestations, the Dharma is always thus. Do not be sorrowful. The unsurpassed correct Dharma, after my passing, will remain in the world for one thousand years, benefiting devas, humans, asuras, and the rest. After that, it will gradually disappear.

"Ānanda! You should know: during the first century after my nirvāṇa, the holy Dharma within my holy teaching will be firmly established. My disciples will be wise and learned, with fearless eloquence able to refute wrong doctrines, possessed of great spiritual powers, greatly benefiting sentient beings. For this reason, the devas and nāgas will joyfully and diligently guard and protect them. Kings, great ministers, elders, and householders will likewise recognize the field of merit. Toward the Buddha, the Dharma, and the Saṅgha they will have deep and pure faith, making offerings with respect, honor, and praise. At the end of the first century, a great king named Aśoka will appear in the world, possessed of great power, ruling over Jambudvīpa. He will build stūpas, tall, vast, and magnificently adorned, numbering eighty-four thousand in all, to make offerings to the relics I have left behind. He will cause immeasurable multitudes who see and hear of them to joyfully plant the karma of rebirth in heaven and liberation.

"During the second century after my nirvāṇa, tranquility will be firmly established within my holy teaching. My disciples will be wise and learned, like the Teacher of Devas and Humans, possessed of great awesome virtue, greatly benefiting beings. For this reason, the devas and nāgas will joyfully and constantly guard and protect them. Kings, great ministers, elders, and householders will likewise recognize the field of merit, with deep and pure faith toward the Buddha, the Dharma, and the Saṅgha, making offerings with respect, honor, and praise.

"During the third century after my nirvāṇa, correct practice will be firmly established within my holy teaching. My disciples who attain liberation through wisdom, liberation of both parts, direct witness, attainment through seeing: there will be immeasurable hundreds of thousands of them. Because so many attain the holy fruits, the devas and nāgas will joyfully and constantly guard and protect them. Kings, great ministers, elders, and householders will likewise recognize the field of merit, with deep and pure faith toward the Buddha, the Dharma, and the Saṅgha, making offerings with respect, honor, and praise.

"During the fourth century after my nirvāṇa, seclusion will be firmly established within my holy teaching. My disciples will delight in dwelling in empty and solitary places, diligently cultivating tranquil concentration. For this reason, the devas and nāgas will joyfully and constantly guard and protect them. Kings, great ministers, elders, and householders will likewise recognize the field of merit, with deep and pure faith toward the Buddha, the Dharma, and the Saṅgha, making offerings with respect, honor, and praise.

"During the fifth century after my nirvāṇa, the meaning of the Dharma will be firmly established within my holy teaching. My disciples will love the correct Dharma, diligently cultivating, studying, debating, and making distinctions. For this reason, the devas and nāgas will joyfully and constantly guard and protect them. Kings, great ministers, elders, and householders will likewise recognize the field of merit, with deep and pure faith toward the Buddha, the Dharma, and the Saṅgha, making offerings with respect, honor, and praise.

"During the sixth century after my nirvāṇa, the Dharma teachings will be firmly established within my holy teaching. My disciples will mostly be devoted to diligently reciting and studying the doctrinal teachings, without weariness of mind, and will be able to greatly benefit immeasurable sentient beings. For this reason, the devas and nāgas will joyfully and diligently guard and protect them. Kings, great ministers, elders, and householders will likewise recognize the field of merit, with deep and pure faith toward the Buddha, the Dharma, and the Saṅgha, making offerings with respect, honor, and praise. However, they will have many doubts concerning the deep meaning.

"During the seventh century after my nirvāṇa, material gain will be firmly established within my holy teaching. Devas, nāgas, yakṣas, asuras, and others will make offerings to the Buddha, Dharma, and Saṅgha with respect, honor, and praise, but my disciples will mostly be attached to material gain, reverence, and fame, and will not diligently cultivate the higher trainings of precepts, concentration, and wisdom.

"During the eighth century after my nirvāṇa, discord will be firmly established within my holy teaching. My disciples will mostly be jealous of one another, forming alliances with wicked people, spreading slander and defamation, scorning those who uphold the precepts, looking down upon the learned, not practicing the six kinds of harmony, but devoted solely to discord. Their views will be unskillful; they will not respect their teachers, will not abide in right knowledge, and will be deceitful and crooked. Their speech will be coarse and rough, like that of outcastes. They will depend upon kings, great ministers, and elders, and through various schemes deplete the resources of the Three Treasures, forming wicked alliances and suppressing good people.

"During the ninth century after my nirvāṇa, worldly occupations will be firmly established within my holy teaching. My disciples will mostly be engaged in secular occupations: plowing, planting, trade, conveying messages, and making a living thereby. Regarding the rules of training established by all the Tathāgatas, they will be negligent and in violation.

"During the tenth century after my nirvāṇa, idle discussion will be firmly established within my holy teaching. My disciples will mostly be devoted to studying and practicing all kinds of idle discussion. They will abandon the correct teachings of the Buddhas that transcend the world, namely sūtras, geyas, vyākaraṇas, gāthās, udānas, nidānas, avadānas, itivṛttakas, jātakas, vaipulyas, adbhutadharmas, and upadeśas. They will diligently recite worldly idle discussions, namely discussions of kings, discussions of thieves, discussions of warfare, discussions of food, discussions of drink, discussions of clothing, discussions of vehicles, discussions of self, discussions of lust, discussions of men, discussions of women, discussions of various countries, discussions of rivers and seas, and discussions of non-Buddhist doctrines. Because they delight in these various kinds of idle discussion, they will cause śramaṇas, brahmins, and others to scorn and reject my holy teaching. Within my correct Dharma and Vinaya, there will be such wicked bhikṣus and bhikṣuṇīs who do not properly cultivate body, precepts, mind, and wisdom. They will be angry and contentious with one another, scheming, slandering, and defaming. They will be attached to fine robes, bowls, lodgings, and bedding. Because they associate with evil companions, although they may guard the pure precepts for many years, in a single moment they will violate them all. Although they may accumulate wholesome roots for many years, through much anger and resentment they will lose them all. For these reasons, the devas, nāgas, and other multitudes will be sorrowful and distressed, abandoning their protection. Kings, great ministers, elders, and householders will lose their pure faith in the Three Treasures, slandering and scorning them. For this reason, the correct Dharma will be extinguished.

"After that, the bhikṣus and others will commit ever deeper evil, and kings, great ministers, elders, and householders will increasingly withhold their respect. Yet the residual power of the Three Treasures will not yet have been completely extinguished. Therefore at that time, there will still be bhikṣus and bhikṣuṇīs of few desires and contentment, who guard the precepts, cultivate quiet meditation, and love learning. They will receive and uphold the Tathāgata's Tripiṭaka teachings, broadly explaining them for the four assemblies, benefiting and bringing happiness to immeasurable sentient beings. There will also be kings, great ministers, elders, and householders who cherish the correct Dharma, making offerings to the Three Treasures with respect, honor, and praise, protecting and establishing them without reservation. You should know that all of these are inconceivable bodhisattvas who, through the power of their original vows, are born at that time to guard and protect the Tathāgata's unsurpassed correct Dharma and to bring great benefit to all sentient beings."

At that time, Ānanda, the śrāvakas, the bodhisattvas, the devas, nāgas, yakṣas, humans, non-humans, and the entire great assembly, hearing the Bhagavān's prediction concerning the future duration of the Dharma and the differences in the conduct of the bhikṣus, all were moved to deeper grief. They believed, accepted, and faithfully practiced it.
