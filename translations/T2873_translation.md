# Sūtra of the Bhikṣu Śūra

## *Shǒuluó Bǐqiū Jīng* (首羅比丘經)

Taishō 2873

Translator unknown.

*Note: This is an apocryphal Chinese Buddhist text of a millenarian and apocalyptic character, placed in the "suspected apocrypha" section of the Taishō canon. The text takes the form of a dialogue between the bhikṣu Śūra (Shǒuluó) and a "Great Immortal" (dàxiān), and later between Śūra and a figure called "Moonlight Child" (Yuèguāng Tóngzǐ), concerning the coming age of calamities and the eventual appearance of Moonlight as a messianic savior-king. The text describes three great disasters (water, plague, and demonic invasion), the appearance of thirty-six great demons riding dragon-horses, seven days of darkness with flesh-eating yakṣas, and the establishment of a golden city. It names specific "worthies" and "sages" with Chinese surnames who are said to be secretly present in the world, identifies specific Chinese geographic locations (Penglai Mountain, Hailing Mountain, etc.), and includes a dhāraṇī for protection. The text explicitly threatens severe karmic punishment for anyone who conceals or fails to circulate it. Its Chinese geographic and political references, use of Chinese personal names, and millenarian tone clearly mark it as a Chinese composition. Multiple Dunhuang manuscripts survive.*

---

[The text opens mid-dialogue, with Śūra questioning the Great Immortal:]

... "Is there yet another method to escape this?" The Great Immortal replied to Śūra: "Reverently believe in the Three [Treasures], observe the fast, perform prostrations, with devoted and unremitting reverence, and you shall be saved from the disaster of great flood."

Śūra again asked the Great Immortal about the disaster of illness. The Great Immortal said: "Bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas [and upāsikās], from now on, uphold the precepts and observe the fast, keeping all pure and clean. Act with knowledge, speak with propriety, receive the precepts as I command. Do not act according to your ordinary inclinations, and you shall be able to cross over."

Śūra asked: "What further method is there to escape the disaster of demons and evil spirits?" The Great Immortal replied: "Demons and evil spirits are of many kinds. Those who receive my command should be careful not to believe in them."

### The Coming of Moonlight and the Golden City

"When Moonlight (*Yuèguāng*, 月光) appears in the world, only those who are good will see him. Those of the five heinous sins and great evil will never see him."

Śūra asked about the cities and their quarters. The Great Immortal answered: "The city is over seven hundred leagues in breadth, a thousand feet high, with a base of a thousand feet and battlements of over five hundred feet. It has seventy-two gates and is made of purple-polished gold. Within it is the Tuṣita City, also a thousand feet high with a thousand-foot base and five-hundred-foot battlements, also of purple-polished gold. Within it are eight cities, each over thirty leagues wide, also of purple-polished gold, each with a thousand lanes, lane aligned with lane, gate facing gate, all looking out upon the Dharma-King."

### The Eight Worthies and the Sixteen Sages

The Great Immortal said: "I shall speak their names." He then named: Shí Xián, Dé Yán, Xián Míng, Sūn Xián, Qí Huā Xián, Dé Wú Xián, Shǐ Zhèng Xián, Dāng Guān Xián, and Bǎo Zhào Xián. "These are the names of the eight worthies. You should go and seek them out. They are your true guides, able to carry you across birth-and-death."

"Besides these eight, there are more worthies: Qín Chāoshì, Pān Dàochéng, Lú Huìyuàn, Bǎn Guóxīng, Fú Nányáng, Liú Dàoguì, Wáng Yánshòu, Zhào Xiǎnzōng, Zhāng Dàobǎn, Gù Shì'ān, and Lǐ Luóchà. All such worthies wander throughout the world. With your fleshly eyes you cannot distinguish them."

"Receiving my command, you should practice equanimity toward all. Why? Because the great worthies are difficult to distinguish. They may appear mad, foolish, dull, poor, lowly, or humble. Some may drink wine and eat meat, or eat at night and break the fast. Manifesting in such ways, who can recognize them? They appear to possess the three poisons and the marks of [worldly] views, conforming to worldly ways, difficult to know. Only through equanimity can one encounter the worthies and sages."

### The Three Great Disasters

Moonlight Child said: "In coming years there will surely be a flood disaster. The water will rise over forty leagues above flat ground. When the water comes, it will surge from the northwest and flow to the southeast, with great waves roaring louder than thunder and lightning, beyond any comparison."

"Only those who uphold the precepts with purity and diligently seek to cross over the world [shall be saved]. The Moonlight Child will command the Great Dragon King to lead people broadly to the floating mountains."

"Furthermore, the three disasters of plague will spread. Of ten, nine will perish, with all manner of strange illnesses bringing untimely death. The king should believe in this. Command everyone in the land, from one year old and above, who can walk, speak, and understand, to receive the three refuges and five precepts."

"Whether old or young, all should be encouraged to receive the three refuges and five precepts and practice the wholesome Dharma. Such persons will all cross over the evil age."

### The Dhāraṇī

"If one reads this dhāraṇī, lions, tigers, wolves, evil spirits, and all demons will naturally depart."

*Yōuqiūní yōuqiūní dànpó dànpó yùlí yùlí wūhū wūhū nísàhū sàhū dànchā dànchā chā āyóuchí chíhūní yàotā yàotā suǒyóu fùnúní fùnúní.*

"When reading this dhāraṇī, wash your hands clean, rinse your mouth, and read it. It will cause people to be peaceful and at ease day and night, and they will immediately see the Bodhisattva. Reading this dhāraṇī one hundred times, one will see the Bodhisattva emitting great radiance, manifesting before the person."

### Exhortation to Circulate the Sūtra

Śūra told the four assemblies: "The great disaster is approaching! Do not act according to your ordinary inclinations. If you have a root teacher, parents, sovereign, patrons, friends, acquaintances, or relatives, give this sūtra to all of them. Whether near or far, all who obtain this sūtra should diligently circulate and distribute it so that everyone may hear it. Anyone who conceals and hides this sūtra will in future lives surely fall into the evil paths."

"Suppose a person were to give away the elephants, horses, seven treasures, people, grain, silk, and wealth of the three-thousand great-thousand world-system as an act of generosity. It would not equal the merit of a person who circulates this sūtra. The merit is a hundredfold greater."

"If one obtains this sūtra, diligently distribute it throughout cities, towns, and villages so that all men and women, great and small, may hear it. Anyone who conceals this sūtra will in the present life be unfortunate and in future lives will contract illness."

"The wise will believe it; the foolish will discard it."

[The text continues with an account of the king and great ministers of the "Land of the Noble" traveling to Penglai Mountain to meet the Moonlight Child, who teaches them about the coming disasters and the protective power of reciting the *Avalokiteśvara Sūtra* and this sūtra. The text includes prophetic references to specific Chinese geographic locations and Chinese-named sages, descriptions of Vimalakīrti's activities among the people, and concluding exhortations to diligent practice and sūtra-circulation.]
