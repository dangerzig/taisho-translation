# Summary Verses of the Mūlasarvāstivāda Vinaya Nidāna-Mātṛkā

## *Gēnběn Shuō Yīqiè Yǒubù PínàiyéNítuónà Mùdéjiā Shè Sòng* (根本說一切有部毘奈耶尼陀那目得迦攝頌)

Taishō 1456

Translated by Yìjìng (*Yìjìng*, 義淨) of the Tang dynasty.

*Note: This is a mnemonic verse index to two subsections of the Mūlasarvāstivāda Vinaya: the Nidāna ("Origins" or "Occasions") with fifty-two verses, and the Mātṛkā ("Matrix" or "Systematic Topics") with forty-eight verses. Together they provide a comprehensive table of contents for the vinaya's practical regulations, covering ordination, distribution of deceased monks' property, boundary-demarcation, monastery construction, stūpa protocols, dietary rules, clothing, medicine, and community governance. Like T1457, this text was translated in 710 CE by Yìjìng with the same team of Indian and Central Asian collaborators.*

---

The Nidāna has fifty-two verses.

The Mātṛkā has forty-eight verses.

## Nidāna: Major Division Summary Verse

> First, explaining the reception of full ordination;
> Next, the distribution of a deceased person's property;
> The ordination-platform and the door-hook;
> The bodhisattva image: five divisions.

---

### Division One: Summary Verse

> Full ordination, knowing the day-count;
> Boundary distinctions, not entering the ground;
> The boundary's edge, the five assemblies' dwelling;
> Not cutting skin or raw flesh.

[Ten sub-section verses follow, covering:]

(1) Full ordination: the characteristics of men and women; those not fully ordained should not serve as preceptors; the ten kinds without a preceptor, including those with difficulties; "Do not ordain me at seven years old."

(2) The day-count should always be known; when announcing, reduce the nights; on the sixth day and eighteenth day, the poṣadha should not be held too frequently.

(3) Boundary distinctions: one does not announce purity or perform karmic acts [across boundaries]; when riding through the sky, one does not carry the consent [vote]; "Release the former before binding the latter."

(4) Not entering the boundary, relinquishing the boundary; the tree-boundary, and the World-Honored One [established boundaries]; what is not to be crossed and what may be crossed; the karmic-act officiator dies [the procedure continues].

(5) Earth, walls, and such matters for conducting business; establishing a boundary without giving consent; sitting only in one place, one can perform the four karmic acts.

(6) The great boundary is two-and-a-half post-stations [in extent]; below the water, above the mountain peak; those with divergent views whose dawn has passed; the five assemblies receive the seven-day [leave].

(7) The five assemblies sit for the rains-retreat; relatives and such request permission for days of absence; if there is doubt about a sūtra passage, one may travel to seek explanation.

(8) Even if the cloth is not cut, there are occasions when all [cloths] may be worn; the cloth may be sized to fit the body; if short, one may make a cylindrical garment.

(9) Five kinds of skin should not be kept, because they involve faults; what is permitted for use is [leather] that accommodates sitting and lying.

(10) Raw flesh and various vinegar-pickles have five kinds of distinctions; for hemorrhoid illness, nails are not to be injured; when redirecting offerings, one should understand the [donor's] wishes.

---

### Division Two: Summary Verse

> Distribution of the deceased's [property], and chanting guidance;
> Spreading cloth, and the probationary student;
> Re-performing, collecting, and expelling;
> The novice-seeker, the shared wall.

[Ten sub-section verses follow, covering distribution of deceased bhikṣus' belongings; chanting and eulogizing; the kaṭhina cloth-spreading ceremony; probationary students and their procedures; re-performing invalidated karmic acts; collecting and gathering within the boundary; expulsion of those who break precepts; giving novice-seekers ordination; distinctions of identical and non-identical offenses; and regulations for conducting affairs on walls.]

---

### Division Three: Summary Verse

> Ordination-platform, novice's fall;
> Single robe, smoke, medicine vessel;
> Iron hammer, hair, and doors;
> One should not follow: iron construction.

[Ten sub-section verses follow, covering ordination-platform regulations; novice-seekers who fall from their vows; single-robe regulations; smoke-tubes and nasal tubes; medicine vessels and felt mats; iron hammers and pestles; regulations regarding hair, nails, and stūpas; doors, eaves, and foundations; the prohibition on driving pegs into stūpas; and iron stūpas and precious-metal offerings.]

---

### Division Four: Summary Verse

> Door-rings used as needed;
> Wetting cloth, latrine-matters;
> Dyeing cloth, stretching, identifying cloth;
> Purchased cloth, fruit, and the purifier.

[Ten sub-section verses follow, covering door-rings, straps, and nets; monastery property and its proper use; monks' property getting wet in rain; latrine and walking regulations; dyeing vats, water vessels, and bowls; the kaṭhina stretching-frame and sewing regulations; non-Buddhist practices of concealing and identifying robes; purchasing cloth on credit; fruit-garden maintenance and four kinds of non-distributable property; and regulations for self-collection of food when no purifier is available.]

---

### Division Five: Summary Verse

> Bodhisattva image, offerings;
> Auspicious occasions, communal meals;
> Great assemblies, grass bundles for seating;
> Assembling the saṃgha, sounding the great drum.

[Ten sub-section verses follow, covering the making of bodhisattva images and five kinds of banners; offerings to bodhisattva images including ornaments, incense, and parasols; auspicious occasions and flower garlands; communal meals during the Vaiśākha month; seating arrangements for great assemblies using grass-bundles; assembling the saṃgha by sounding the great drum and distributing wealth.]

---

## Mātṛkā: Major Division Summary Verse

> First, the confession and apology;
> Second, the designation of property;
> Third, the requisites and robes;
> Mātṛkā summary verse.

---

### Mātṛkā Division One: Summary Verse

> Confession, grass, field-middle;
> Release, shadow-conqueror [King Bimbisāra];
> Sugar, ghee, roots, and such are permitted;
> Dog-meat, cups, sugarcane.

[Ten sub-section verses follow, covering confession procedures; grass and field regulations; releasing trapped animals; King Bimbisāra's bed-offerings; prohibited meats including crows, magpies, cranes, vultures, and eagles; dog-meat and carrion-eating animals; small cups and cloth-wrapping with their faults; sugarcane, curd, meat, and sesame as medicines; sugar-water as a permitted beverage for seven-day storage; and root-bark, stem, leaf, flower, and fruit medicines soaked in mild spirits.]

---

### Mātṛkā Division Two: Summary Verse

> Designated property, owner's location;
> One must inquire; Kośala;
> From the image, prior appointment;
> Great reduction, assemblies, nuns' assembly.

[Ten sub-section verses follow, covering designated property not to be moved; property in deity-temples; asking donors about their intentions; the story of Kośala white cotton; the bodhisattva image entering the city; prior announcement for five assemblies' city-processions; appointing a property-manager; reducing monastery levels when too large; great assemblies and drum-calling; and nuns' assembly seating with an empty seat reserved.]

---

### Mātṛkā Division Three: Summary Verse

> Requisites, robes, the foolish;
> If appointed, not using the layperson;
> Correctly making, the elder's gift;
> Razor, stūpa;
> Rice-cakes, curd, leaves and water;
> And washing bowls and such matters:
> These twelve verses in summary
> Should be known as essentials.

[Ten sub-section verses follow, covering thirteen requisite items to be listed by name and guarded; the foolish who do not understand the Tripiṭaka; procedures when twelve persons are appointed; not using the five kinds of fat; laypersons, novices, and probationers not sharing seats; ongoing work not requiring one to stand [for seniors]; an elder's donated property and inquiry procedures; razors, tweezers, and latrines; stūpa circumambulation and holy sites; and rice-cakes, curd, foot-washing basins, and bowl-washing mindfulness.]

---

### Mātṛkā Division Four: Summary Verse

> Given fields, shares should not [be changed];
> Bare-bodied, designated property, offerings;
> Saṃgha robes, lettering, traveling;
> Sugarcane, fruit, guest's skirt.

[Ten sub-section verses follow, covering fields given and mutual assistance; not allowing thieves, eunuchs, or probationers to distribute voting-sticks; not going bare-bodied under robes; designated property remaining at its designated location; saṃgha robes inscribed with donor names; traveling-robes sent and received; equal distribution of sugarcane; fruit offered by yakṣas to be purified before eating; guest and resident monks' procedures; and skirts, monk's-foot shoes, incense-paste stained robes, and the ten kinds of dust.]
