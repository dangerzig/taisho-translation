# 佛說常瞿利毒女陀羅尼呪經
## Sūtra of the Dhāraṇī Spell of Cāṅgulī the Poison-Maiden Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1265

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Gupta (瞿多).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was in Jetavana, Anāthapiṇḍada's Park in Śrāvastī, together with a great assembly of twelve hundred and fifty bhikṣus, as well as devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, humans, and non-humans, all assembled to hear the dharma. He universally opened for sentient beings the gate of dhāraṇī. Observing that all sentient beings of the Tathāgata, in the future evil age when the three calamities would arise together, would be harmed by evil venomous snakes and poison-nāgas spewing toxic vapors, by flying and crawling venomous insects killing sentient beings, by vipers, scorpions, centipedes, and their kind, by pythons, venomous serpents, and all manner of poisonous creatures dwelling in mountain ravines or in pits and underwater, all capable of releasing poison to invade sentient beings.

At that time the World-Honored One addressed the great assembly: "Once, I was on the north side of the Himalayas, in the Fragrant Mountains. Within those mountains I saw a maiden, her body adorned with marks of a hundred blessings. She wore deer-skin garments, and had adorned herself with poisonous snakes as necklaces. Pythons, scorpions, and all venomous insects served as her playmates. When hungry she ate poisonous fruits; when thirsty she drank poisonous liquids. That maiden, seeing me, addressed me: 'Benevolent one! You should listen to my Cāṅgulī dharma-gate, which can extinguish all worldly poisons.'" She then spoke the spell:

*Tadyathā irī-midī-tidī-rī-midī-irī-tidī-rī-midī-ruṇme-ruṇma-rīye-nudci-nudcā-rīye ṭakkarāṇa-vakkarāṇa kaśmīri-kaśmīra mukgidī-aggini-agganī-agghanāganī irīye-aggaye-appaye śphetī-śpheta-duṇḍa anā-nurakṣa svāhā*

"At that time, after Cāṅgulī spoke this spell, five thousand evil venomous nāga-serpents within the Himalayas, hearing the sound of this spell, all fainted. At once their heads split open, their scales scattered in all directions, their venomous fangs fell out, their scalps burst apart, their bodies broke and bled, and all were self-bound and unable to move."

"At that time the World-Honored One, observing these sentient beings harmed by the poisonous spell, spoke the heart-spell to release the previous poison." The spell:

*Tadyathā ilī-milī-gara-milī-cakuru-cakuru-ṃgara-mīmi-kuṭaye-kuṭaye-ti-supuṭaye-ti-supuṭaye-ti nā-garī nāga-cakka-rā-ndī-rī ica-viṣa-ke taṭṭha-taṭṭha-sittaṭṭha-sittaṭṭha-sittaṭṭha svāhā*

"At that time, when the World-Honored One spoke this spell, all the poisonous nāgas, scorpions, and the rest were revived, and scattering in all directions they fled, with their poison unable to act. Therefore I now tell you: if you uphold this spell of Cāṅgulī the Poison-Maiden, do not recite it loudly before nāgas and serpents, for if they hear it they will surely die." The Buddha told the four assemblies: "Good men! If one wishes to rescue the sentient beings of the world from poison, the divine spell of the youth Cāṅgulī can treat all such cases. Although this Cāṅgulī appears in female form, she is in truth not a woman. Good men! The wisdom and supernatural powers of the buddhas and bodhisattvas can manifest in various forms for sentient beings, controlling all poisons and not allowing evil venomous nāgas and serpents to invade sentient beings. If a person hears the great vidyā body-spell of Cāṅgulī, for seven years afterward they will not be harmed by poison, and it can also crush and eliminate the poisons within the body."

At that time, the World-Honored One further told the Vanquishing-Enemy Bird King: "You and your powerful kinfolk, your mighty flock! After my nirvāṇa, in the age of the final dharma, you must likewise gather in all the ninety thousand poisonous bird kings, who must all be summoned. Ten thousand must all be corralled. Hearing my Cāṅgulī great vidyā spell, all must comply and assist, serving as skillful means for rescue and protection, and must not disobey."

At that time the Buddha told the four assemblies: "Good men! This spell of Cāṅgulī the Poison-Maiden's body can treat all manner of worldly poisons numbering in the tens of thousands: snakebite, scorpion sting, evil sores, pustulant sores, abscesses, boils, mountain-spring toxins, raw-metal poisons, and all manner of *gū*-poisons. Simply recite the spell over water and drink it, or gargle and hold the spelled water. If one recites the spell over the poison three times, intoning seven times per recitation, all poisons will come out from the root of themselves, and the poison will be unable to cause harm. If one suffers from mountain-spring poison, recite the spell over water, wash the body with it, and also drink the spelled water; all mountain-spring poisons will be completely eliminated. If one can with devoted mind make offerings, honor, and uphold this divine spell, one will obtain immeasurable merit."

At that time the World-Honored One addressed the four assemblies: "If good men wish to practice rescue and protection, they should paint the form of Cāṅgulī as a maiden, adorned with marks of a hundred blessings, wearing skin garments and *kauśeya* silk, with a great divine wheel-shaped topknot. Her right hand holds a poison-subduing sword, and her left hand holds a poison-controlling wood-seal. Her entire body is adorned with necklaces of poisonous snakes wound around as bracelets. Before Cāṅgulī, paint a youth dressed in blue, holding a water bowl and incense table, with a hundred kinds of poison-fruits and various poisonous liquids, all placed before Cāṅgulī. On all four sides paint mountains, with tigers, wolves, and lions painted on both sides of the mountains, along with vipers, poison-nāgas, scorpions, and all manner of evil venomous serpents surrounding all four sides. For making the altar, use one cubit or two cubits as desired. Place the image upon the altar, recite the spell, and make offerings. Plaster the altar with cow dung mixed with fragrant mud. Below the mud, place five platters of food with bird-head broth, and after the offerings are complete, dispose of them in a clean place. Burn *mandārava* incense as offerings and recite the spell seven thousand times. For superior accomplishment, or middling accomplishment, recite thirty thousand times. For lower accomplishment, seventy thousand times. One will be able to rescue the world and treat all poisons. Simply recite the spell over water and drink it to cure all poisons."

A brief summary of the essential dharma.

At that time, when the World-Honored One had spoken this spell, the immeasurable mantra-ṛṣis in the assembly, as well as the yakṣas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, humans, and non-humans, having heard the Buddha speak this divine poison-treating spell, were all greatly delighted. They all approved and accepted, bowed in homage with the teaching held above their heads, and departed.

The protection-against-defilement dhāraṇī, for whenever a practitioner goes out or enters and encounters impurity:

*Oṃ vajra-kurudhana hūṃ jah*
