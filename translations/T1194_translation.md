# 妙吉祥平等觀門大教王經略出護摩儀
## Abbreviated Homa Ritual Extracted from the Sūtra of the Great Teaching King of Mañjuśrī's Equality Contemplation-Gate

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1194

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Cixian (慈賢).

---

> I pay homage to all buddhas and the great bodhisattva assemblies!
> I shall now explain the homa and its various ritual forms.
> First recite the mantra to empower the shovel and mattock,
> And afterward one should make the detailed distinctions. The empowerment mantra:

*Oṃ nikhana-vasudhe svāhā.*

Recite this mantra to empower the shovel and mattock, then dig the homa hearth. Before digging the hearth, first take river water in a clean vessel, recite the mantra of the presiding buddha of one's tradition to empower it, and sprinkle the water on the ground, on the purified shovel and mattock, and on the person who will dig the hearth. The purification mantra:

*Oṃ amṛtodbhavāya hūṃ phaṭ svāhā.*

When performing homa according to the method, one night before digging the hearth, empower cooked rice with the Upper Vidyā-king mantra and offer it to the local earth-spirits and the eight classes of devas and nāgas; only then may one dig the hearth. The soil from inside the hearth should be collected in a clean place. Then take sand from the river and first empower it with the mantra:

*Oṃ vasumatī vasurīye oṃ.*

After reciting this mantra to empower it, fill the hearth with the sand. Next, sprinkle the top with five grains, seven jewels, and four metals in powdered form, and then one may use clean bricks. Build the homa hearth; do not use fired tiles. First recite the mantra of the Vajra-Karma Bodhisattva to empower the clean bricks. The mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-karma kaṃ.*

After empowering thus, use the clean bricks to build it according to the method.

The homa method speaks of four kinds. The four are: first, *śānti* (pacification); second, *puṣṭi* (increase); third, *vaśa* (subjugation through attraction); fourth, *abhicāra* (fierce subjugation). These four homas have four corresponding hearth shapes. For *śānti*, make a round hearth. For *puṣṭi*, make the hearth in a half-moon or octagonal shape. For *vaśa*, make the hearth square. For *abhicāra*, make the hearth triangular. These four are made according to what is required.

Next, the hearth measurements, briefly of three kinds. The first offering hearth is eight cubits (an octagonal hearth), with a depth of two cubits. The second offering hearth is one and a half cubits, with a depth of one and a half cubits, totaling six cubits on all four sides. The third offering hearth is one cubit, totaling four cubits on all four sides, with a depth also of one cubit.

According to the hearth's size, make a triple rim. On the western side of the third rim at the front of the hearth, measuring proportionally to the main half, also use clean bricks to build up nine tiers, becoming progressively smaller toward the top. On the right side of the main half, build steps; on the left side of the main half, build straight up and down. The triple rim of the hearth represents the three realms; the nine tiers represent the nine grounds.

When this is complete, on the sand inside the hearth beneath the bricks, place the seven jewels, four metals in powdered form, and five grains. Visualize them as the five seeds and the seven jewels and four metals. The seven jewels, the four metals, and the five grains: empower the five grains and four metal powders by reciting the Vairocana Buddha mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-dhātu vaṃ.*

After reciting this mantra, next recite the mantras of the four Pāramitā Bodhisattvas to empower the seven jewel powders:

Vajra-Pāramitā Bodhisattva:

*Oṃ sattva-vajrī hūṃ.*

Ratna-Pāramitā Bodhisattva:

*Oṃ ratna-vajrī trāṭ.*

Dharma-Pāramitā Bodhisattva:

*Oṃ dharma-vajrī hrīḥ.*

Karma-Pāramitā Bodhisattva:

*Oṃ karma-vajrī aḥ.*

After reciting the four mantras to empower them, place them inside the hearth. One should mix all fragrant powders with the clay. The mantra for empowering the clay:

*Oṃ vasumatī-śrīye-śrī-vasudhe svāhā.*

After reciting this mantra, use the fragrant clay to coat the hearth.

When all the above has been arranged in sequence, one should within the hearth attentively visualize the altar, mentally installing it. For the *śānti* method, visualize the Buddha's body as white in color, and further contemplate one's own body as likewise. One should count the rosary upon the second joint of the power finger of the left hand, burning offerings. Use a crystal rosary of 108 beads, seated facing east. For the *puṣṭi* method, visualize the Buddha as golden, and oneself likewise. Count upon the middle joint of the vow finger, using a rosary of 100 bodhi-seeds, seated facing south. For the *vaśa* method, visualize the Buddha as reddish-yellow, and oneself likewise. Count upon the middle joint of the skillful-means finger, using a rosary of 90 lotus-seeds, seated facing west. For the *abhicāra* method, visualize the Buddha as green, and oneself likewise. Count upon the first joint of the power finger, using a rosary of 70 soap-nut seeds, seated facing north.

When performing the homa method, according to the four kinds of aims sought at the time, one should visualize a separate altar within the hearth and install the presiding buddha of the corresponding tradition in the central position. For the four kinds of homa, beyond the specific aims, if it is a regular ongoing offering, visualize Akṣobhya Buddha in the center. The rosary count may be 108 or as needed. The method of visualizing the altar above is described extensively in the Great Teaching.

When this mental installation is complete, take kuśa grass. Use eight knots to place in the eight directions of the hearth, representing the purification of the eight positions. Also use three knots of grass to place within the hearth, representing the purification of the Three Jewel positions. Also, tie three knots of kuśa grass into one bundle; three bundles making nine knots in total. These are burned first in the homa, representing the purification of the hearth. Also use two additional knots of grass: one placed inside the abhiṣeka vase, and one placed upon the precept finger of the right hand, visualized as a homa pestle, also representing the purification of the hands, representing Vajrapāṇi. The grass knots should be shaped like a vajra. Empower them with the four wisdom mantras:

*Oṃ siddha-locanī sarva-rathā-sādhanī svāhā.*

*Oṃ kelikeli māmake sarva-rathā-sādhanī svāhā.*

*Oṃ śvete paṇḍara-vāsinī jāti-nī sarva-rathā-sādhanī svāhā.*

*Oṃ tāri tuttāri tutrī hūṃ svāhā.*

After empowering, take the firewood for burning, properly dried and suitable, of a length equal to one cubit of the patron's hand. Sprinkle it with fragrant water and recite the Vairocana mantra to empower it:

*Oṃ vajra-dhātu vaṃ.*

After the method's empowerment, place the wood in the hearth with root and tip in proper alignment. Thorny wood may not be used. When the above arrangement is complete, one should empower the homa wood, also reciting the Vairocana mantra. In Sanskrit, *pañca-sāyera-sthī* represents the five tathāgatas' proper offering of food. The five milk-sap woods to use are the nyagrodha tree and similar. Since these are unavailable in this region, one may take any evergreen wood without fruit, with fine and slender branches. Dip the tips and roots in ghee, and cast three sticks, or five branches, or twenty-five branches into the fire, visualizing the offering to the Buddha. One should use the five grains to perform the burning offering. Recite the Vajra-Pāramitā Bodhisattva mantra, first empowering the five grains:

*Oṃ sattva-vajrī hūṃ.*

After empowering the five grains, make the offerings in sequence; these represent the root of food. Ghee, honey, milk, yogurt, and so forth are also empowered by reciting the Vajra-Pāramitā mantra first. Next, with five kinds of porridge, perform the homa burning offering. The five porridges are: milk-curd porridge, yogurt porridge, ghee porridge, honey porridge, and sugar porridge. Recite the Dharma-Pāramitā Bodhisattva mantra to empower them:

*Oṃ dharma-vajrī hrīḥ.*

After empowering, these represent the pure offering. Then empower the five kinds of cooked rice with the Root Pāramitā mantra:

*Oṃ śaṃkarī śāntikārī ghuṭanī ghaṭāya sarva-rathā-sādhanī svāhā.*

After empowering the five kinds of rice, the representation is the same as before. When all the above empowerments according to the method are complete, one should visualize atop the hearth a vidyā-king, whose body is like a śarīra stūpa. Then visualize all buddhas within the stūpa. The homa being performed is a mental offering; only then may one take the fire. The fire is visualized as the seed-syllable of the presiding buddha of whichever tradition one is working in, serving as the fire. Or one may empower with the root mantra, or inwardly visualize the wisdom-fire blazing with great radiance, or visualize the vidyā-king with flames covering the entire body, transforming the burning materials into offerings.

Having lit the fire, one may sprinkle pure water and paste-incense on the fire to purify it. Then add water, representing purification water. Then use ghee, honey, milk, yogurt, and the five kinds of wood, five grains, porridge, and rice, each cast five times into the fire, making separate offerings to the five tathāgatas. For each tathāgata, recite the respective root mantra and burn the offering materials. The mantras for offering to the five tathāgatas:

Vairocana Tathāgata:

*Oṃ vajra-dhātu vaṃ.*

Akṣobhya Tathāgata:

*Oṃ akṣobhya hūṃ.*

Ratnasambhava Tathāgata:

*Oṃ ratna-saṃbhava trāṭ.*

Amitābha Tathāgata:

*Oṃ amitābha hrīḥ.*

Amoghasiddhi Tathāgata:

*Oṃ amogha-siddhi aḥ.*

Having visualized the offering to the five buddhas, next add water as an offering, visualizing it as water for the buddhas to rinse their mouths. The water-offering mantra:

*Oṃ kṛrū-nādhi hūṃ kṛrū-daka oṃ.*

Next offer flowers, casting them into the hearth, visualizing flowers placed atop the buddhas' crowns. The flower-offering mantra:

*Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-puṣpa-pūja.*

Next offer paste-incense, sprinkling it into the fire, visualizing anointing all the buddhas. The paste-incense offering mantra:

*Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-gandha-pūja.*

Next offer incense, visualizing it entering the buddhas' noses, making offerings to all tathāgatas. The incense-burning offering mantra:

*Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-dhūpa-pūja.*

Next ring the bell and recite praises or gāthās, all as appropriate to the occasion.

Next burn the five grains, reciting the mantra of the presiding buddha of whichever tradition one seeks, empowering the burning materials. If there is no specific aim, simply recite the Dharma-Śarīra mantra for the burning.

The offering mantra:

*Oṃ dharma-dhātu-garābhe svāhā.*

After burning the five grains, cast ghee, honey, and milk-curd each three times into the fire. Next add water, mentally making offerings, all as in the previous method. Next offer porridge: first burn milk porridge, then other porridges as desired. When burning porridge, take eight clean vessels or eight leaves, sprinkle them with paste-incense, and place the porridge and rice in the clean vessels. Empower them with the mantras of the ten directional devas, and at the eight directions of the hearth, offer to the ten directional devas above and below. Mentally visualize offering to the ten directional devas. The mantras in sequence:

Eastern direction, Indra Deva:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ indrāya svāhā.*

Southeastern direction, Agni Deva:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ agnaye svāhā.*

Southern direction, Yama Deva:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ yamāya svāhā.*

Southwestern direction, Lord of Rākṣasas:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ megha-śanāya svāhā.*

Western direction, Varuṇa Deva:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ varuṇāya svāhā.*

Northwestern direction, Vāyu Deva:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ vāyave svāhā.*

Northern direction, Vaiśravaṇa Deva:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ vaiśravaṇāya svāhā.*

Northeastern direction, Īśāna Deva:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ īśānāya svāhā.*

Upper direction, Brahmā Deva:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ brahmāya svāhā.*

Lower direction, Pṛthivī Deva:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ pṛthivīye svāhā.*

For these ten directional devas, first arrange eight seats at the eight directions of the hearth. Brahmā and Pṛthivī Devas are visualized above and below the hearth. Then pour water from the vase into the offering food. Next use flowers, incense, and paste-incense according to each position for offerings, reciting each root mantra to empower the offering materials. Recite the root mantra three times and place at the corresponding direction; only then may one burn the porridge offering.

After burning the porridge, cast ghee, honey, and milk-curd each three times into the fire. Add water in sequence, all as in the previous method. Next offer the cooked rice: first burn milk-rice, then ghee-rice, then yogurt-rice. After burning the rice, cast ghee, honey, and milk-curd each three times into the fire. Add water in sequence, all as in the previous method. Then take homa wood, dip the tips and roots in ghee, and cast them into the fire. Again use ghee, honey, and milk-curd, each cast three times into the fire.

When all these steps are complete, then stand, holding the ghee vessel and the homa ladle in hand, ring the bell and recite, pouring ghee into the fire. All those who are participating with sympathetic joy should each anoint their hands with paste and hold flowers. When the recitation of praise and pouring of ghee is complete, each person casts their flowers into the hearth, goes to the fire altar and bows, representing the completion of the offering.

Next, use a clean cloth to wipe the ghee and cover the fire for burning, visualizing it as the Buddha's garments. When all the above is finished, use a clean vase to pour water around the hearth, again visualizing rinsing the mouth for purification.

When performing the homa, the mental visualization should be wholehearted. Make offerings without haste. The amount of each offering material should be about the size of an āmalaka fruit.

Regarding the homa altar, know that the tongues of the seven buddhas and the mouths of koṭis of devas, among all offerings, declare the homa to be supreme.

Whatever remains unburnt of the five grains, porridge, rice, and so forth, together with additional food and drink, flowers, fruit, and so forth, should be placed together in a single vessel, sprinkled with paste-incense, and fumigated with burning incense. First form the Hook-Summoning mudrā, the Samaya-Lotus mudrā, and the Vajra-Bond mudrā. Then add water, ring the bell half way, and recite the mantra empowering the food, visualizing offerings to the eight classes of devas, nāgas, and other beings, and to all devas outside the fire altar, mentally offering to them all.

When the homa offering is thus complete, then bid farewell to all the holy assembly.

> May the supreme merit of this gathered secret
> Universally moisten all sentient beings.
