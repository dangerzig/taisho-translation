# 千轉大明陀羅尼經
## Thousand-Turn Great Vidyā Dhāraṇī Sūtra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1036

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dānapāla (施護).

---

Thus have I heard. At one time the World-Honored One was in the Trāyastriṃśa Heaven beneath the Pārijāta tree. At that time, Śakra, Lord of the Devas, together with all the hosts of celestial warriors, surrounded him and came to the Buddha's abode. Each with sincere devotion joined palms in homage, gazed reverently upon the Tathāgata, and took their places in due order.

Then Śakra, Lord of the Devas, addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One, I possess a dhāraṇī called the Thousand-Turn Great Vidyā. This dhāraṇī brings great benefit to all beings in the world. All devas, nāgas, yakṣas, hungry ghosts, kumbhāṇḍas, and all beings including humans and non-humans who hear this dhāraṇī are entirely subdued. Whatever is harmful to sentient beings is entirely eliminated. I humbly beseech the World-Honored One, in his great compassion, to permit me to proclaim it."

The Buddha said: "Lord of the Devas, excellent, excellent! As you have said, this great Thousand-Turn Great Vidyā Dhāraṇī can bring great benefit to the world. If you wish to proclaim it, do so as you please."

Then Śakra, Lord of the Devas, spoke the Thousand-Turn Great Vidyā Dhāraṇī:

*Tadyathā nahi tasya-bhate bhagavatu samya-sthinanya gatu*

"All bhūtas who hear this proclamation are startled, terrified, bewildered, confused, and scatter in retreat. The yakṣa lord Ratnavīra and the Wheel-Accomplishment host manifest in various forms and cause all bhūtas, ghosts, spirits, roaming directional beings, sky-walkers, vetālas, malaria-demons, and all malevolent spirits, including all who employ poisons, root sorcery, and heterodox practices to torment sentient beings with harm, to be entirely liberated.

*Anāpati upānāpati aṅga-śaṃkari giri-ghaṇḍari svāhā. Namo buddhāya namo dharmāya namo saṅghāya. Namaḥ ārya-avalokiteśvarāya bodhisattvāya mahāsattvāya mahākāruṇikāya. Namaḥ ārya-mahā-sthāma-prāptāya. Tadyathā: mukte vimukte bhedani vibhedani nirmale vimale sumukhe vimukhe muṅgale sumuṅgale.*

"Liberation from all fear of kings. Liberation from all fear of baleful star-omens and prodigies. Liberation from all fear of one's own and enemy armies warring. Liberation from all fear of weapons. Liberation from all fear of flood and fire. Liberation from all fear of terrible diseases and afflictions. Liberation from all fear of poisons and harmful injuries. Liberation from fear of lions, elephants, bears, tigers, and all fierce beasts. Liberation from fear of drowning in the great ocean, fear of solitude in deep mountains, and fear of perilous rapids. From all such fears, one is entirely liberated.

*Namaḥ sakṛc-chaṅkati-chaṅkati karuṇati karuṇati niye svāhā*

"When entering the royal palace, when meeting great ministers, when marching with armies, when encountering enemies, when seeing venomous snakes or poisonous nāgas, when beset by yakṣas, piśācas, rākṣasas, or upasmaras, and when amid perilous mountains and seas, from all such fears one is entirely liberated.

*Namo trarāṃ bhava-śīlāya-rāṃ hile hile cile cile kapāle madaṃgi svāhā*

"Furthermore, if one is seized by royal law, or one's body is confined in stocks, fetters, manacles, and chains, one is entirely liberated from all unjust suffering and anguish.

*Namo tāri tāri uttāri turi svāhā*

"Furthermore, if one is oppressed by baleful stars, or evil dreams and prodigies bring disease, suffering, premature death, or even disputes, litigation, and mutual intimidation, from all such fears one is entirely liberated.

*Namo cara cara sara sara visara visara buddhaya buddhaya bandhaya miti svāhā. Rakṣa rakṣa vaṃ sarva-sattvānāṃ ca svāhā. Ārya-śrī-maṇi-dharaṃ puṣṭi-kuru svāhā. Mama pūrva-lamaṇḍhānāṃ śānti-aṃkura svāhā. Namo hati-ni hati sarva-anuṣṭhāṃ-pratiyantu jani moha-ni-mokṣaṇi svāhā. Namo kati niti kaṭati svāhā. Namo āraṇi-vāraṇi sarva-nirvāraṇi svāhā.*

"Furthermore, all evil persons performing unwholesome deeds, maintaining heterodox vows and spells, conducting sorcerous rituals meant to cause sentient beings to become deluded, confused, and maddened: from all such things one is entirely liberated.

*Ruru keri ruke-ti tiṣṭha bandha yāmi yāvā-gavaca-vi muñcanti hile cile kapāle kaṣṭhaṅga sarva-tā sarva-bhaye-bhyaḥ svāhā*

"World-Honored One, this Thousand-Turn Great Vidyā of mine is like the wish-fulfilling jewel called Cintāmaṇi possessed by a wheel-turning holy king: whatever one desires naturally appears before one. My Thousand-Turn Great Vidyā is just like this: whoever obtains it is entirely liberated from all suffering and affliction.

"World-Honored One, in the final age, sentient beings will have meager merit. Not knowing to take refuge in the Three Jewels, they will indulge body, speech, and mind in constant wrongdoing. Therefore, the world's evil ghosts and spirits will seize their opportunity, concealing their true forms and assuming false manifestations, tormenting beings day and night and causing them various sufferings and afflictions.

"World-Honored One, if any sentient being, when beset by evil ghosts' various torments and afflictions, or whenever any undesirable thing occurs in the world, goes to a clean piece of ground and takes seven handfuls of earth, recites this great vidyā over each handful seven times to empower it, then forms them into seven balls and ties them to a corner of their garment, this will cause all harmful evil ghosts and spirits to dare not approach. They will scatter in terror, and that person will be at peace day and night. All inauspiciousness will be entirely eliminated."

Then Śakra, Lord of the Devas, having spoken this Thousand-Turn Great Vidyā Dhāraṇī, leapt with joy, and together with all the celestial hosts bowed to the Buddha and withdrew.
