# 佛說犢子經
## Sūtra of the Calf, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0808

Translated from the Chinese. Wu dynasty, translated by Zhi Qian (支謙)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was in the land of Śrāvastī, in the Jeta Grove, in the fine garden of Anāthapiṇḍada.

At that time the Buddha had come down with a wind-ailment and needed cow's milk. There was a brahmin of great wealth who lived not far from the city. The Buddha sent Ānanda, saying: "Go to the brahmin's home and ask for cow's milk."

Ānanda received the instruction and went to the brahmin's home. The brahmin asked Ānanda: "What have you come seeking?"

Ānanda said: "The Tathāgata has come down with a slight wind-ailment, and so he sent me to ask for cow's milk."

The brahmin said: "The cows are over there. Go and milk one yourself."

Ānanda went to where the herd was. There was one cow that was habitually fierce and ill-tempered, and no one could come near her. Ānanda then thought to himself: "It is not proper for me in the Dharma to milk the cow myself."

At that time Śakra knew Ānanda's thoughts and came, taking the form of a brahmin, and stood beside the cow. Ānanda went to him and asked: "Brahmin! Please milk the cow for me."

He spoke to the cow: "The Tathāgata has come down with a slight wind-ailment. Give your milk so that the Tathāgata may drink it. If he is cured, the merit you gain will be immeasurable and beyond reckoning. The Tathāgata is the great teacher above and below the heavens. With a mind of compassion he cares for all creatures that crawl and creep, wishing to deliver all from suffering and affliction."

The cow said: "This hand that touches my udder, how pleasant it feels! Take from the two front teats, but leave the two rear teats for my calf. My calf has had nothing to eat since this morning."

At that time the calf was standing nearby. Hearing the Buddha's name, it addressed its mother: "Take my share of milk too, all of it, and give it to the Buddha. The Buddha is the great teacher above and below the heavens. He is exceedingly difficult to encounter. I can eat grass and drink water; that is enough to keep me alive. Why do I say this? In my past lives I always drank milk, and now, born as a cow, I again drink milk. The foolish and deluded in this world are exceedingly numerous and without measure. In my former life, because I followed the teaching of evil companions and did not believe in the Buddha's sūtras, I have been a cow or a horse for sixteen kalpas. Only now have I been able to hear the Buddha's name. Take my share of food, all of it, and give it to the Buddha. Fill the vessel and go, so that in my future life I may have wisdom and intelligence and attain the path as the Buddha has."

Ānanda took the milk and returned to the Buddha's dwelling.

The Buddha asked Ānanda: "What did the cow mother and calf say?"

Ānanda said: "It was truly remarkable. The cow had been extremely fierce and ill-tempered, impossible to approach. A brahmin milked her for me, and the cow immediately became gentle. The mother and calf spoke to each other."

The Buddha said: "This cow mother and calf in their past lives did not believe in the Buddha's sūtras, and so they fell among cattle and horses for sixteen kalpas. Only now have they awakened. Hearing the Buddha's name, a mind of compassion arose in them, and they gave milk as an offering to the Buddha. After the calf's death, it will offer silks, banners, and canopies to me, scatter flowers, burn incense, and receive and uphold the sūtras and precepts. After twenty kalpas it will become a Buddha named Milk-Light Tathāgata and will deliver all beings."

The Buddha said: "Because the cow, with a good and virtuous mind, gave milk to the Buddha, it will cross over all sufferings and difficulties and afterward receive immeasurable blessings and rewards. For this reason, the Buddha must not be disbelieved, the sūtras must not go unread, and the path must not go unstudied. Proclaim this to all above the heavens and below, that all may know."
