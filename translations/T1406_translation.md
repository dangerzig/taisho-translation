# 佛說辟除賊害呪經
## Sūtra Spoken by the Buddha: The Incantation for Warding Off Thieves and Harm

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1406

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown (失譯)

---

Homage to the Buddha. Homage to the Dharma. Homage to the Saṃgha of bhikṣus. Homage to the Seven Buddhas of the Past. Homage to all Buddhas. Homage to all disciples of the Buddhas. Homage to all teachers. Homage to all disciples of the teachers. Homage to Murlibheli, King of Spirits.

Having paid these homages, then recite this incantation, so that all upon whom I pronounce this incantation may come to pass as wished.

In the north there is a mountain called Gandha-Mahāṣu. There dwells a King of Spirits named Murliāmri, who abides there.

There are four sisters. What are the four?

*Andāni Yamāni Yemāni Muhāni* ...

*Andāni*, causing the thief to become blind; *Muhāni*, causing the thief to halt; *Yemāni*, causing the thief to sit down; *Muhāni*, causing the thief to become utterly foolish and dull.

Thus: *Aurali Murali Daṇḍīja Padmajā Kāli*; causing the thief's mouth and teeth to clench shut. Until the spell-cord is untied, the thief cannot be freed.

Having recited this incantation, then declare: "On behalf of so-and-so and such-and-such persons, I perform protection and warding off of evil harm, granting them all peace and security."

When performing the recitation of this scripture, it should be done on the twenty-ninth day of the lunar month. Before a Buddha image, light seven lamps, burn resin incense, and scatter flowers. Recite this incantation seven times. With a prayer of dedication, invoke the Spirit King Murlirā to receive merit and virtue, and likewise light lamps, burn incense, and scatter flowers for him. Also light seven lamps for Hārītī, burn incense and scatter flowers, and likewise recite this scripture seven times. After the concluding incantation, make the declaration as instructed above, and all will come to pass as wished.
