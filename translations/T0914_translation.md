# 火吽軌別錄
## Supplementary Record of the Hūṃ Fire Ritual

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0914

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ oṃ varuṇāya svāhā*

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ oṃ vāyave svāhā*

The mantra of the Vaiśravaṇa Deva King of the north:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ oṃ yakṣa-vidhyā-dharī svāhā*

The mantra of the Mahā-brahmā Deva King above:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ oṃ prajāpataye svāhā*

The mantra of the Earth-Dwelling Deva King below:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ oṃ pṛthivīye svāhā*

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ oṃ ādityāya svāhā*

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ oṃ candrāya svāhā*

The mantra of the Star Celestials:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ oṃ nakṣatra-devatāya svāhā*

The mantra of the Dragon Kings:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ oṃ megha-śanaye svāhā*

The mantra of all the messengers and sundry spirits and ghosts:

*Piśi piśi viśāci-nāṃ svāhā. Ghu ghu bhūta-nāṃ svāhā*

In general, when practicing the recitation of Acala's mantra, one must always construct an altar. The altar method is as shown below. Use blue or black flowers, burn aloeswood and benzoin incense, and offer seasonal fruits, food and drink, lamplight, and fragrant water. When making offerings, the practitioner should bathe and don fresh garments. First recite the principal deity's mantra and form the principal deity's seal. With a ladle, take the fragrant water and sprinkle it over all the offering items. Then make the offerings. This is called "purification and protection," whereby all the māras cannot defile them. Additionally, use the Ākāśagarbha heart-offering method, which is the most inconceivable.

When the arrangement of offerings is complete, recite the samaya mantra to expel obstacles and seal the boundary.

Vairocana's samaya mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ oṃ a-samaye tri-samaye samaye svāhā*

Form the samaya seal and seal the forehead, left and right shoulders, heart, and throat, reciting the above mantra once at each place.

Then form Acala's seal and recite Acala's mantra seven times, rotating to the left for expulsion. Then recite three more times, rotating to the right to seal the boundary. When the boundary is sealed, form the hook seal to invite all the Buddhas, bodhisattvas, vajra-holders, and others to descend and assemble at the practice-site. The hook seal and mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ āḥ sarva-trā pratyeha-ta tathāgatāṃ kūśa-bodhi-caryā pariśo-dhaka svāhā*

Recite once for each hook-gesture, three hooks in total. Then visualize all the Buddhas, bodhisattvas, and immeasurable holy hosts all descending and assembling. Afterwards, sit, bow, confess, make vows, and state what one seeks.

Then form Vairocana's heart seal and recite the five-syllable mantra as follows.

Vairocana's mantra for subduing the four māras, liberating the six destinies, and fulfilling sarvajña-jñāna:

*Ā vīra hūṃ khaṃ*

Facing the deity's image, form the above seal and recite this mantra seven times. Then take up the prayer beads and recite one hundred or up to one thousand times; the more the better. Then form Acala's seal and recite Acala's mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-vajrāṇāṃ caṇḍa-mahāroṣaṇa sphaṭaya hūṃ trāṭ hāṃ māṃ*

Facing the deity's image, form the above seal and recite this mantra seven times. Then take up the prayer beads and recite one hundred or up to one thousand times; the more the better.

When recitation is complete, again form the principal deity's seal and recite the mantra seven more times. Make earnest vows and bow. Then form the samaya seal again and recite the samaya mantra three times. Form the sword seal and rotate once to the left to release the boundary. Visualize all the holy hosts each returning to their own lands. Then exit the practice-hall. In walking, standing, sitting, and lying down, maintain the principal deity's mantra in the mind without interruption.

Acala's food-offering mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-vajrāṇāṃ trāṭ amogha-caṇḍa-mahā-roṣaṇa sphaṭaya hūṃ tramaya tramaya hūṃ trāṭ hāṃ māṃ*

At every meal, always set aside a portion of food as an offering and recite this mantra once. In all places one will constantly receive protection.

Bathing mantra:

*Oṃ jala svāhā*

Take a handful of water, recite the above mantra seven times, and empower each handful of water to sprinkle upon the body; this constitutes bathing. As before, make the food offering in the eight directions. Take beans, fruits, cakes, rice, sesame, glutinous rice, powdered incense, and various flowers, mix with water and place in a vase or basin. With a joyful mind, scatter in the four directions as offerings to all the devas and spirits. Powdered and scattered incense for the retinue: offer each with its own mantra.

*Oṃ īśānāya svāhā*

The mantra for the Indra Deva of the east:

*Oṃ indrāya svāhā*

The mantra for the Agni Deva of the southeast:

The mantra for the Yama King of the east:

*Oṃ yamāya svāhā*

The mantra for the Rākṣasa of the southwest:

*Oṃ rākṣasa-adhipataye svāhā*

The mantra for the Nāgas and Water Spirits of the west:

*Oṃ varuṇāya svāhā*

The mantra for the Wind Spirits of the northwest:

*Oṃ vāyave svāhā*

The mantra for the Yakṣas of the north:

*Oṃ yakṣa-vidhyā-dharī-pataye svāhā*

The mantra for offering to the various spirits above:

*Oṃ ghi ghi mānasani bhūtānāṃ svāhā*

The mantra for below:

*Oṃ piśi piśi viśāca-nāṃ svāhā*

Having made offerings according to the above method, wash the hands and rinse the mouth, then re-enter the altar. Bow to the Buddhas and bodhisattvas and proceed with the regular recitation:

> "You devas, you protecting ones!
> Toward all the guiding Buddha-teachers,
> Practice the most supreme conduct:
> Purify the earth pāramitā.
> As he who defeated the armies of Māra,
> The Lion of the Śākyas, Savior of the World,
> I too shall subdue the māras:
> I now draw the maṇḍala."

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ oṃ pṛthivīye svāhā*

## Homa Fire Ritual, One Fascicle

## Homa Method

Contemplate the bodhicitta with the syllable *raṃ*.

First abide.

Then invite the fire deity, saying:

> "I now respectfully invite the chief of fire devas, the sage among fires.
> First paying homage with reverence, descend to this seat and accept the homa."

The mantra for inviting the fire deity:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ oṃ agnaye svāhā*

Furthermore, the fire deity's seal and mantra: based on the root seal, only bend the two middle fingers into a half-ring shape, with their tips not touching. This is the single Dharma-seal for inviting the fire deity and making offerings to him. When the offerings are complete and the fire deity is to be dismissed, then straighten the two middle-finger tips. The seal and mantra:

*Namo bhagavate uṣṇīṣāya ehyehi cala-mari-ṇi sūrya-svāhā*

This single seal and mantra is for feeding the fire deity. Do it three times at the proper hour. First invite the fire deity and burn food as an offering; afterwards, burn food as offerings to all the Buddhas, bodhisattvas, vajra-holders, and mantra-deities. The fire deity's mantra:

*Oṃ agni-dāya hūṃ svāhā vākā-nāya svāhā*

When the rite is complete, dismiss the fire deity using the above fire deity mantra and seal.

The fire-hearth faces east, or according to the original diagram.

There are three kinds of fire altars: first, for pacification; second, for increase; third, for subduing enemies.

First, the pacification altar. On open ground, prepare an altar. In the center, draw the Buddha seated on a lotus throne. On the four sides of the altar, draw Prajñā Bodhisattva, the śrāvakas, and the Buddha's Compassion Bodhisattva. Draw the Buddha with the auspicious marks. On the first of the month, additionally draw Aparājita, the Pure-Abode Devas, and the Dragon King. In the center of the altar, make offerings of incense and flowers. At the southwest corner of the altar, construct a fire hearth four inches high. In the center, dig a fire pit one cubit square, and plaster the center of the hearth with white clay. The platform is four inches high and one inch from the wheel. Burn mulberry wood, or fig wood, or persimmon wood; the wood should be one handspan long. Take branches from the treetops, the fattest parts. Smear both ends with ghee, honey, and curd. With each mantra recitation, burn one stick. Take barley, wheat, ghee, honey, curd, and milk; with each mantra recitation, burn one portion. Complete one thousand and eight recitations. Three sessions daily for seven days, or half a month, or a full month, and pacification will be achieved. Wear fresh white garments. In the center of the altar, recite the mantras, then proceed to the fire hearth to perform the fire-burning rite. Begin on the first day of the month. Perform the rite at dusk. At that time, the Pure-Abode Devas will assemble, and the pacification method will be accomplished. Sit facing north on a kuśa grass mat in full cross-legged posture. Burn the fire at the hearth. Three times daily, plaster the altar and make offerings of incense, flowers, and other items. The incense should be without thorns. Make offerings, and one will accomplish the elimination of all disasters and calamities, attain longevity, cure all illness, and remedy all strange and ominous occurrences. This method applies to all such cases.

Next, the increase fire-altar method.

If one wishes to perform the fire-increase method, first prepare an altar. In the center, draw the Buddha; on the four sides, draw Avalokiteśvara, Mahāsthāmaprāpta, and all the great bodhisattvas, Brahmā-deva, Mahāśrī-devī, all the great yakṣas, the sages, Yamāntaka, and Hārītī Mother, and make offerings before them. For this method, begin on the fifteenth. At dusk, face east and sit cross-legged on a round kuśa grass mat, wearing clean garments with a joyful mind. At this time the devas assemble. When that person performs the rite, the devas rejoice, and accomplishment is obtained. Outside this altar, on the southern side, construct a large altar two cubits square, two inches wide at the rim, dug one cubit deep. The central platform is one handspan wide and four inches high. Use red clay for the altar. On the platform, make offerings with lotus-and-other incense. Burn fig wood, milk-porridge, ghee, curd, and the like; or sesame mixed with rice is also acceptable. Three sessions daily, one thousand and eight recitations per session. The wood should be one handspan long; take branch-ends about finger-width thickness. Smear both ends with ghee, honey, milk, and curd. With each mantra recitation, burn one stick, completing one thousand and eight recitations, or one hundred and eight. For seven days or half a month. Performing the rite thus, the method will be accomplished.

Third, the subjugation fire-altar method.

If one wishes to perform the subjugation fire method, use the eighth or fifteenth of the dark fortnight, or from midnight. At this time, all evil ghosts, yakṣas, and rākṣasas assemble. Construct a triangular altar facing south. Use black clay to plaster the altar, one cubit square, three inches high and two inches wide. In the center, place a trident. Take donkey dung, camel dung, and ashes; use these three substances to plaster the altar. If one has an enemy causing vexation and anger, then perform this method. Wear red garments or blue garments. For an even more powerful method, take blood to dye the clothing. Squat down with the feet pressing together, face west, furrow the brows, glare, clench the teeth, sit upright with body straight, and with the mind generate the speech directed at the altar. One may also take chaff mixed with mustard seeds. Three times daily, plaster the altar. Upon the altar, take oleander flowers and thorny wood, neem, and smear the altar with them. One may also take chaff mixed with mustard seeds, mixed with one's own blood. With each mantra recitation, burn one item, completing one thousand and eight recitations. Three times daily, burning likewise human bones, head-hair, and such items. Make vows of anger, one mantra, one burning, calling the target by name. This is the subjugation method: it causes the target to suffer prolonged illness, lose wealth and possessions, die with blood flowing, lose bodily joints, and become shunned by all people.

Furthermore, the universal fire-altar method. The pit is sixteen inches deep and thirty-two inches wide. On the east side, place a wheel. On the south side, a three-pronged trident. On the west side, a dragon. On the north side, a vajra. In the center, a lotus. Other offerings follow the same method as before. Begin on a day ruled by the fire-star.

Next, the methods for the seals of the various fire deities.

Next, the seals of the various fire spirits. Next, the proper times for accomplishment. If one performs the method in the fifth month, rain will certainly come. If one performs in the third month, various kinds of obstructions will certainly manifest. If one performs in the first month, various obstacles will certainly occur. If one performs in the ninth month, sudden thunder and lightning will certainly come. In all such months, one should use the fifteenth. When such signs appear, accomplishment is certain.

For the pacification method, use the fifteenth, seventh, third, or thirteenth of each month; all of these are days of the ghost-star. Furthermore, solar and lunar eclipses are the most auspicious of all. For the increase method, perform in the second, third, tenth, or eleventh months. For the subjugation method, perform in the fourth or fifth months.
