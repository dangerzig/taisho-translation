# 穢跡金剛禁百變法經
## Sūtra of Ucchusma Vajra's Prohibitions and Hundred Transformations Method

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1229

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Azhidaxian (阿質達霰).

---

At that time the Vajra again addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! If there are good men or good women who uphold my mantra and it is without efficacy, such a thing cannot be."

"If one wishes to cause a mountain to crumble, take three sheng of white mustard seeds and the finest agaru incense. At a mountain where treasure is suspected, take a steel sword and draw a square boundary. Use one clean cloth and one incense burner, and burn agaru incense. First recite the mantra one thousand and eight times, then scatter white mustard seeds in all four directions, up to seven recitations. Perform this method, and the mountain will crumble of its own accord. If there is a place with treasure, the treasury spirit will abandon the treasure and depart, and one may use it at will."

"If one wishes to cause the sea to dry up, first recite the mantra one thousand and eight times. Fashion a bronze dragon-form and cast it into the sea; the sea will immediately dry up."

"If one wishes to cause a river to flow in reverse, take agaru incense and fashion an elephant-form of any size, cast it into the water, and recite the mantra one hundred and eight times. The water will immediately flow in reverse. To restore it to normal, empower a clean stone with the mantra and cast it into the water, and the water will return to normal."

"If there are thunder, lightning, thunderbolts, venomous dragons, sudden gales, or evil rains, then form the thunder-stopping seal: with the left hand, bend the middle finger, ring finger, and little finger together into the palm. Place the thumb upon the middle joint of the index finger. Recite the mantra over it. Point the seal from afar toward the place of thunder and hail, and they will stop of their own accord."

"If one wishes to cause all ghosts and spirits to come and submit as servants, take three pecks of water and fill a copper vessel. Surround it with pure ash. Then form the command-and-capture seal: bend both ring fingers together into the palms with their backs leaning against each other. Join the tips of both middle fingers. Both index fingers and little fingers are each spread open like blossoming flowers. Place the thumbs upon the middle joints. Silently recite the mantra one hundred and eight times. All evil ghosts within that world will come gathering like clouds and manifest their forms, abandoning their venomous and evil minds, submitting to the practitioner's command."

"If one wishes to seal a mountain: at the mountain you arrive at, recite the mantra one hundred times and shout loudly three times. Then form the mountain-sealing seal: with the right hand, bend the ring finger into the palm and stand it upright. The middle finger, index finger, and thumb stand upright pointing upward. Apply the seal seven times. Then walk backward seven steps, and afterward seal the mountain seven times with the seal. All birds and beasts within the mountain will move out."

"If one forms this seal and recites the mantra seven times, then applies the seal toward empty space three times seven, not even a mote of dust will pass through that space."

"If one wishes to cause a person to be unable to speak, write that person's name and hold it in your mouth. That person's mouth will be unable to speak. Spit it out, and they will speak again."

"When reciting all other mantras, one must first construct an altar. But when reciting my mantra, no altar is needed. Simply carve a single-pronged vajra pestle. In a buddha's stūpa or in a quiet room, smear the ground with fragrant mud to whatever size, arrange various fragrant flowers as offerings, and place the pestle in the center of the altar. Recite the mantra one hundred and eight times, and the pestle will move of its own accord. It may transform into various strange objects; do not be alarmed. Recite the mantra one hundred and eight more times, and the pestle will rise three chi above the ground, or five, six, seven chi, up to one zhang. At that moment the dharma-holder must take refuge, repent, and make vows. I shall then manifest my true body there. Whatever the practitioner wishes and desires will all be swiftly obtained as wished. I shall then bestow upon them the prediction of bodhi, and they will attain liberation of body and mind. One must first complete the full one hundred thousand recitations, and only then perform the method. If the required number is not fulfilled, there will be no efficacy."

### Section Two: Seal Methods

"This seal is one inch and eight parts square, carved [into wood]. Empower it with the mantra one thousand times, using white styrax resin to consecrate it daily. When carving the seal, do not let anyone see. Use the seal to stamp the heart, and one attains heart-wisdom, spontaneous wisdom, and knowledge of past lives. After maintaining the seal for one hundred days, one attains abiding in various great dharma-gates."

"This seal is one inch and two parts square. Empower with the mantra six hundred times, using agaru incense to consecrate it. Carry it on one's person, and all people will feel love and delight. One attains great sovereign freedom and forever leaves all suffering."

"This seal is one inch and five parts square, carved [into wood]. Empower with the mantra six hundred times, using white styrax resin to consecrate it. Use the seal to stamp the feet, and one will be able to fly through empty space, going wherever one wishes."

"This seal is one inch and eight parts square, carved [into wood]. Use white styrax resin to consecrate it, empowered with the mantra seven thousand times. Use the seal to stamp the feet. One can then travel three million li in a single day, unseen by anyone."

### Supernatural Transformation and Life-Extension Methods

"For lingering transmitted illness: write [the talisman] upon the heart, and one will immediately recover. Great auspiciousness. Urgently, urgently, in accordance with the statutes and ordinances."

"For heart pain: write it and it will be immediately removed and cured. Great auspiciousness and benefit. Urgently, urgently, in accordance with the statutes and ordinances. First recite the mantra seven times."

"For ghost-illness: write in vermilion and swallow it." "For a person with specter-ghost illness: write in vermilion, swallow seven large written sheets, and they will immediately recover. Divinely efficacious."

"If one follows this dharma, take two zhang, one chi, and seven cun of white sandalwood silk. Wrap it in white silk and place it upon the earth-wheel of the world. This will cause a person's lifespan to extend to seventy years. If there is no one to deliver it, place it in the courtyard of one's own home and bury it seven chi deep; this also works. One will also gain clarity of mind, great wisdom, and unimpeded eloquence."

"These seven methods can also cure ten thousand illnesses. Swallowing them also causes longevity and increased wisdom. Greatly efficacious."

"The above seven methods: write in vermilion on paper and swallow one thousand sheets. This causes lifespan to be extended, and one will attain a life equal to that of heaven and earth. Do not let anyone see them."

"The above seven methods: if a person suffers from any illness, write these talismans and all will be cured. If a person writes the talismans and swallows them, their lifespan will be extended and their wisdom increased. Greatly efficacious."

"The above seven methods: if a person seeks various precious treasures, write these talismans in vermilion and swallow them. After a full seven days, various wondrous treasures will arrive of their own accord. If one seeks another person's possessions, write that person's name beneath the talisman, and that person will immediately deliver the goods."

"The above three talismans: write in vermilion upon the four legs of the bed. The eight great vajra-beings will constantly guard and protect, never departing even for a moment. One must maintain strict purity and never allow defiled objects to enter the room. This must be carefully observed."

"If a great fire disaster arises, write a talisman, cast one sheet into the fire while reciting the mantra one hundred and eight times, and in a moment the disaster will be extinguished of its own accord."

"If an evil wind arises, write this talisman and recite the mantra one hundred and eight times. Cast it toward the wind, and it will immediately stop."

"If a great flood arises, write this talisman and cast it into the water. The flow will immediately be cut off, and the water will not drown people."

"If there is heavy rain, write this talisman and empower it with the mantra one hundred and eight times. Cast it toward the rain, and the rain will immediately cease."

"Write this talisman in vermilion and swallow three sheets. Afterward one may also write talismans for others, and they will be efficacious. If one does not do this first, using the various talismans will have no effect."

At that time, when Ucchusma Vajra had proclaimed these talismans, the great assembly praised in unison: "Excellent, Great Powerful One! You have proclaimed this great and wondrous dharma, causing all sentient beings to attain liberation."

At that time the Vajra addressed the great assembly: "Know that wherever this dharma circulates among you, I and the great devas shall constantly protect the one who practices this dharma and assist them to accomplishment."

At that time the Vajra further said: "If there are sentient beings who practice this dharma, I shall go to that place and manifest before that person. Whatever they wish and desire, I shall also bestow. I shall cause them to obtain various transformations, various supernatural powers, and to act without obstruction. They must constantly be mindful of our original teacher Śākyamuni Buddha, and I shall constantly follow them, assisting all dharmas to accomplishment."

When the Vajra had proclaimed this dharma, the great assembly was doubly moved with both grief and joy. The devas, nāgas, great ghost-spirits, and others all received the holy words, bowed at his feet, and departed.
