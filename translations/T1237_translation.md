# 阿吒婆拘鬼神大將上佛陀羅尼神呪經
## Sūtra of the Dhāraṇī Spell Presented to the Buddha by Āṭavaka, the Ghost-Spirit Great General

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1237

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown.

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was in the Kalandaka Bamboo Grove in Rājagṛha. At that time, within the city of Rājagṛha there was a bhikṣu who had been robbed by bandits, bitten by a snake, and tormented by ghosts, suffering great affliction. At that time, Āṭavaka, the ghost-spirit great general, saw this bhikṣu enduring such suffering and, with a mind of compassion, went to the Buddha's presence. Having arrived, he bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, stood to one side, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! Having subdued all extremely evil ghosts and spirits, I now, out of compassion for all sentient beings and for the sake of subduing all evil ghosts, spirits, evil persons, and venomous creatures, present to the Buddha, the World-Honored One, an extremely severe and fierce spell to subdue the ghosts and spirits. If anyone reads and recites this spell, that person's awesome power can even subdue Great Brahmā, let alone lesser evils." At that time the Buddha told Āṭavaka, the ghost-spirit great general: "I do not need this extremely severe and fierce spell, lest it cause harm to sentient beings." At that time Āṭavaka again addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! In the future evil age, evil ghosts will grow in power, evil persons will be numerous, and venomous insects and beasts will harm sentient beings. Some will encounter various difficulties: dangers from kings, bandits, flood, fire, weapons, fear, enmity, evil ghosts, and other hardships. For the Buddha's disciples, whether monastics or laypersons, whether dwelling in seclusion, mendicant practitioners, those in charnel grounds or under trees, the four assemblies, whether traveling through wilderness, mountain forests, or paths, or dwelling in cities, villages, lanes, or alleys, I shall rescue and protect them and not allow them to encounter evil. World-Honored One, with your compassion, I beseech you to accept this. Well-Gone World-Honored One, I beseech you to take notice." At that time the World-Honored One, having heard these words, silently accepted. At that time Āṭavaka, seeing the Buddha's silent acceptance, was filled with joy and delight, and before the Buddha spoke the spell:

*Dulume dulume dame dame dulume dulume dulume dulume duru-mileni-li-nili-nili nāra-nāra-nāra nili-nili-nili-nili nāra-ṇu-phunili duru-ṭhani duru-ṭhani mahā-duru-ṭhani duru-ṭhani kuṭṭhani-kuṭṭhani mahā-kuṭṭhani taṭṭhani-taṭṭhani mahā-taṭṭhani taṭṭhani ṭha-ṭha-ṭha-ṭha-ṭha-ṭha-ṭha-ṭha mahā-ṭha-ṭha-ṭha-ṭha abhi-abhi mahā-abhi-abhi abhili-abhili mahā-abhili-abhili ava-abhi ava-abhi ava-abhi ava-abhi luśi-luśi mahā-luśi-luśi līṇi-līṇi mahā-līṇi-līṇi śūlu-śūlu mahā-śūlu-śūlu gulu-gulu mahā-gulu-gulu lu-gumu lu-gumu lu-gumu lu-gumu guma-guma-guma-guma hili-hili-hili-hili idhi-idhi-idhi-idhi bidhi-bidhi-bidhi-bidhi hara-hara-hara-hara hini-hini-hini-hini śuni-śuni-śuni heni-heni hanā-hanā-hanā-hanā muni-muni-muni-muni mahā-muni-muni sāra-sāra-sāra-sāra śrī-gharugha-cāri-jhe ṭhinā-ṭhinā-ṭhinā-ṭhinā musāvanā-mujhe-śukhadutthamuni kārama-kārama-kārama jāgati-dajhe śama-dharma śama-dharma śama-dharma yama-dharma yama-dharma śama-mukta-midina-vārā-jhanā me-phuru-ṣadama-muni-nā-vijhānā-mi-śukhadutthamuni-nā-vijhānā-me svāhā*

"World-Honored One! These dhāraṇī phrases serve as protection and rescue for all sentient beings. They protect and guard this person, causing all to attain safety and tranquility, to be freed from decline and affliction, to extinguish all evil poisons, and to be released from all sufferings, dangers from kings and bandits, and enmity. If devas, nāgas, ghosts, spirits, rākṣasas, yakṣas, kumbhāṇḍas, pūtanas, apasmāras, and others touch or torment them, or if they are invaded and damaged by such beings, all will be eliminated. Furthermore, all worldly poisons, whether from fragrant grasses, wood, roots, flowers, or fruits, clothing, food and drink, or worldly things, and insects, birds, animals, and beasts that are capable of venomous evil and harming people, will all be neutralized and unable to cause evil. Furthermore, if from the sky the sun, moon, stars, and planets, whirlwinds, or ghosts and spirits raise winds intending to harm people, if ghosts and spirits come seeking food, absorbing people's vital essence or consuming their flesh and blood, causing epidemic diseases or fevers, whether lasting one day, two days, three days, four days, up to seven days, or causing cold diseases, wind diseases, dampness diseases, and cold ailments, whether internal or external, whether lasting seven days or sixteen days, all will be completely eliminated and unable to cause harm. All such ghosts and spirits, whether they use hands, mouths, feet, tongues, or minds to torment people, and also evil persons who wish to cause affliction and harm: should one first recite this spell, its power can silence and hold them, causing those evil persons and evil ghosts to be silenced, crushed, and made to lose their faculties, unable to do evil. World-Honored One! I shall now speak another divine spell for protection."

*Acha-acha muni-muni mahā-muni-muni ṭhani śu-śu mahā-nāga śu-śu durgha-naji ahu agha-naji ata-naji aṭa-aṭa naṭa-naṭa lu-dulu-dulu-dulu śu-śu duru hini-hini-hini-hini ugghu-maghu-maghu-maghu-maghu hili-hili-hili-hili nili-nili mahā-nili svāhā*

"This dhāraṇī serves as protection for those who receive, uphold, and recite it. If there are ghosts that consume people's vital essence, that damage people's possessions and deplete their wealth, all such fears and enmities are warded off through this boundary-establishment. Now for a certain country, for such-and-such a person and all their household, I provide vast protection. I shall now further speak a spell to ward off all evil." He then spoke the spell:

*Lumu-lumu-lumu-lumu lu-malu-malu-malu-ma hili-hili-hili-hili-hili-hili gunā-gunā-gunā-gunā-gunā guṇu-guṇu-guṇu-guṇu gulu-gulu-gulu-gulu śūlu-śūlu-śūlu-śūlu hili-muśu-muśu-muśu-muśu mu-hili-mu-hili-mu-hili-mu-hili śumu-śumu-śumu-śuma śume-dhimā-me-sma adhi-kāra-me-ḍu svāhā*

At that time the great general again addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! This spell has extremely great divine power, as described above. Do not allow the upholder of this spell to encounter fear from kings, fear from bandits, fear from fire, water, venomous wind, or weapons, fear from the sun, moon, stars, planets, ghosts, or spirits. Or if other evil acquaintances with wrathful and envious minds harbor harmful intentions wishing to invade and torment them, one should first recite this spell to establish a boundary, and those evil ghosts, evil persons, and enemies who harbor thoughts of invasion will become foolish, deluded, silenced, and crushed, encountering various evils upon themselves and unable to cross this boundary or to harm the reciter of this spell. World-Honored One! If good men or good women recite this spell, all devas, nāgas, asuras, evil ghosts and spirits, humans, and non-humans will all attend and protect them, not allowing them to encounter evil. World-Honored One! I am the great general of the ghost-spirits, with the power to subdue all evil ghosts. If anyone recites this spell, I shall lead the ghosts and spirits to remain at their side day and night, protecting that person and not allowing them to encounter evil, not allowing evil ghosts or evil persons to take advantage of them. If anyone invades, damages, or torments the reciter of this spell, I shall crush their heads with my thousand-spoked wheel and cause the ghosts and spirits to bring calamity upon them. World-Honored One! This spell has extremely great divine power and awesome virtue. I beseech you to circulate it widely, bestowing peace and happiness upon the multitudes. World-Honored One! The virtuous power of the reciter of this spell is known only to the Buddha. World-Honored One! This great divine spell should be entrusted to virtuous, wise, and good persons. If one cannot recite it, one should write it on fine paper, place it in a silk pouch with various incenses, and always carry it on one's person. If there is worry, fear, or danger, one should always recollect this spell, and all will be eliminated. World-Honored One! If there are troubles, worries, fears, evil ghosts and spirits, or evil dreams that one wishes to eliminate, one should first establish a boundary so that all evils cannot arise, causing those evil persons, evil ghosts, and evil bandits to suffer their own calamity, their bodies withered and their minds deranged. At the time of establishing the boundary, one should bathe in clean water, don clean garments, and plaster the ground clean. Set out seven vessels of liquid offerings and two vessels containing a small amount of blood-drink, and two vessels containing various liquid offerings. Light eight lamps, burn frankincense, and with concentrated mind make offerings. I shall lead the ghosts and spirits to come to their side and grant their wishes. That person should recite this spell and tie a red cord, then carry it forth, and it will be able to eliminate all difficulties."

At that time the Buddha told Ānanda: "This spell has extremely great divine power, able to eliminate all difficulties and all untimely evils, protecting sentient beings and bringing them great benefit. You should well receive and uphold it and cause it to circulate widely. If there are cities, villages, or settlements where this spell is recited, none will fail to benefit. If kings or great ministers recite this spell, their territories will be free from evil bandits, fearful difficulties, calamities, epidemics, drought, floods, wind, or frost. If one encounters evil bandits, one should recite this spell. If it is tied to the top of a high banner, when the bandits see this banner, they will immediately retreat and be subdued. Ānanda! This spell has extremely great divine power and awesome virtue. It should be caused to be well recited and upheld by the four assemblies." At that time the assembly, having heard what the Buddha proclaimed, joyfully put it into practice.
