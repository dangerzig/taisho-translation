# 佛說聖觀自在菩薩梵讚
## Brahmā-Hymn to Holy Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1055

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmabhadra (法賢).

---

## Imperial Hymn to Avalokiteśvara by Emperor Tàizōng of the Great Ming

> The Great Sage, Sovereign Avalokiteśvara,
> In hundreds of thousands of millions of manifestations, without end,
> Spiritual powers unobstructed, abiding nowhere,
> With great compassion having pity on all beings.

> The six sense-faculties interchangeable, wisdom profound,
> Through hearing, thinking, and practice entering samādhi,
> Saving and liberating according to conditions, manifesting freely,
> Like one moon reflected in a thousand rivers.

> Each river has its moon, each river is bright,
> Yet the bright illumination is but one, fundamentally not two.
> Worldly people merely see form with their eyes,
> But the bodhisattva perceives sound with the eye.

> Those who only have the ordinary are blocked in their perception,
> And therefore cannot attain wondrous function.
> The Great Being, having eyes, does not see in darkness;
> Ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind are likewise.

> One who can perceive with this kind of perception
> Will never be upside-down in delusion.
> When the habit of seeing and hearing is stilled and ceased,
> There is also no wondrous form-body.

> One who can penetrate this tenfold space
> Together realizes the ocean of perfect interpenetration samādhi.
> Body and mind, immeasurable, pervade the sand-grain worlds,
> Not moving from the fundamental ground, responding to all who seek.

> Like the myriad flowers that meet the spring wind,
> Each kind of sprout and bud naturally shoots forth,
> Blossoming, flourishing, and thriving in its own way,
> Some becoming branches, leaves, and fruit.

> Sour, salty, sweet, bitter, green, and red:
> All are fulfilled exactly as wished.
> This present dharma is inconceivable;
> Not grasping at any mark is what makes it true.

> A single hair-tip contains the space of the dharma-realm,
> Washing clean the five turbidities and all fevers,
> Shattering the mountains of obstruction, all dissolved,
> With boundless evil destinies entirely escaped.

> The sahā world becomes a city of joy,
> And wondrous marks like Ganges sands all manifest.
> Taking great space as the body's form,
> Jeweled topknots cluster like five Mount Sumerus.

> The four great oceans serve as the gateway of his mouth,
> The shining sun and moon are his two eyes.
> The forest of myriad phenomena serves as his necklace,
> And he has made the supreme vow with perfect eloquence.

> He bestows the merit of the fourteen fearlessnesses,
> And wondrously manifests in thirty-two response-bodies,
> A universal object of reverence for all beings.
> Broadly he employs the compassionate mind of all the buddhas.

> His body is one body with all beings,
> Responding to their invocations, stirring at every point of contact.
> Thus with right view one can truly see me,
> And I, with no-seeing, perceive my own sovereign freedom.

> Like mirror reflecting mirror, like valley answering sound,
> All-embracing, radiant, and clear, entirely natural.
> To realize this samādhi is at once to transcend the ordinary.
> One dharma fully contains all the buddhas.

> He opens the treasury of compassion and merit,
> And universally shows the gate of virtuous skillful means.
> May the sentient beings of the Fortunate Kalpa yet to come
> All realize bodhi and ascend to true awakening.

---

> I bow to the Buddha, the Dharma, and the Sangha,
> And pay homage to Avalokiteśvara.
> I now praise
> The great compassion's forest of merit.

## Brahmā-Hymn, First

*Bhuvana-traya-vandita-loka-guru amara-dhipati iṃ stutiḥ brahma-vālaṃ muni-rājaṃ dhūru vasidhi-kālaṃ praṇamāmi vālokitanātha-vālaṃ*

## Brahmā-Hymn, Second

*Sugata amṛta-rūpa su-rūpa dhava-hulakṣaṇa bhūṣita-dehavālaṃ kaṇa-kañja vibhūṣita-vāma-kālaṃ kuṭi-ramara-labhi iṃgarā-mautaja-jhikāṃ*

## Brahmā-Hymn, Third

*Śaśi-viṃma-mi mau-jvala pūrṇa-mukhaṃ karmāra-yata-lu-cāna-cā-ru-vālaṃ hima-khaṇḍa-dhi-paṇḍara-gaṇḍa-pucī-jhaṃ mṛga-rāja-vi-maudhita-vāsa-tra-yughan*

## Brahmā-Hymn, Fourth

*Prathama-ruṇa-laṃ chita-rāja saṃdhya avālaṃ gita-paṃka chī nā-anu-saṃdhya svarā-na-mu-nā-ga chī-ta mīgha-lu-da-nva hu-lakṣaṇa bhūṣita svāya-kālaṃ*

## Brahmā-Hymn, Fifth

*Taru-kumāra-sa-dhāra paṇita laṃ mṛga-cāri-ma-ni viṣṭhita-vāsa-kālaṃ śubhaṃ kuṇḍara-maṇi tā-lu-rā-dhālaṃ vimalaṃ kāma-lu-nā-rānā bhī-ta-laṃ*

## Brahmā-Hymn, Sixth

*Maṇi-maṇita mi-khara hima-vālaṃ kati viṣketa citta su-vāsitrā-vālaṃ chī-nāga-ya-ṇa mahū-nā-dhi parāga-da-nva hū puṇḍaya mupa aricchita ra-ma-dhā-pa-naṃ*

## Brahmā-Hymn, Seventh

*Jvara-vāya-dihā-laṃ vahu-saukhaya kālaṃ tri-bhava artha-kālaṃ svālaṃ vividha-kula nīrcchita māra-valaṃ vara-nu-pura-śedhita-padana-vālaṃ*

## Brahmā-Hymn, Eighth

*Gaja-maṃta vi-rālaṃ mī-ta haṃsa-gatī iṃ paripūrṇa mahā-mṛta rā buddha-gatī iṃ varakṣa ramahū-nā-dhi varaṇa-dhālaṃ śrī-putra-rā-kadhi-nī-vāsa-kālaṃ*
