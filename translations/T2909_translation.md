# 佛說地藏菩薩經
## Sūtra on Kṣitigarbha Bodhisattva, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 2909

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown (失譯)

---

At that time, Kṣitigarbha Bodhisattva was dwelling in the Lapis Lazuli World (Vaiḍūrya-jagat) of the south. With the pure divine eye he observed sentient beings suffering torments in the hells: being pounded by iron pestles, ground by iron millstones, plowed by iron plows, and sawn apart by iron saws. Cauldrons of boiling water surged and bubbled; fierce fires blazed day and night. When hungry, the beings swallowed red-hot iron pellets; when thirsty, they drank molten copper. They endured all manner of torments without any respite.

Kṣitigarbha Bodhisattva could not bear to witness this. He immediately journeyed from the south and descended into the hells, where he sat together with Yāmarāja in the same place, though on separate seats. There were four reasons for this: first, lest Yāmarāja's judgment of offenses be arbitrary; second, lest the records and case files be in error; third, to intercede for those who should not yet have died; fourth, to receive at the edge of the hell pond those who had completed their punishment and were departing.

If there are good men or good women who create images of Kṣitigarbha Bodhisattva, copy the Sūtra of Kṣitigarbha Bodhisattva, and recite the name of Kṣitigarbha Bodhisattva, such persons will certainly attain rebirth in the Western Land of Ultimate Bliss (Sukhāvatī). They shall pass from one buddha-land to another, from one heavenly realm to another.

If there are persons who create images of Kṣitigarbha Bodhisattva, copy the Sūtra of Kṣitigarbha Bodhisattva, and recite the name of Kṣitigarbha Bodhisattva, such persons will certainly attain rebirth in the Western Land of Ultimate Bliss. On the day such persons depart this life, Kṣitigarbha Bodhisattva will personally come to receive them, and they shall always dwell together with Kṣitigarbha Bodhisattva.

Having heard what the Buddha had proclaimed, all rejoiced greatly, accepted it with faith, and reverently put it into practice.
