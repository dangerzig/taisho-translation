# 大花嚴長者問佛那羅延力經
## Sūtra on the Elder Mahāpuṣpavyūha's Question to the Buddha about the Power of Nārāyaṇa

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0547

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Prajñā (般若) and Lìyán (利言)

---

Thus have I heard.

At one time, the Bhagavān was dwelling in the Jetavana, Anāthapiṇḍada's park, in the land of Śrāvastī. At that time, the Elder Mahāpuṣpavyūha rose from his seat, arranged his robes, knelt reverently with palms joined, and addressed the Buddha: "Venerable World-Honored One! For those who give food and drink to all sentient beings, what reward shall they obtain?"

At that time, the Bhagavān said to the elder: "Those who give food obtain the power of the Tathāgata."

The great elder again addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! Regarding the power of the Tathāgata, is there a simile by which it may be explained, so that I might hear it?"

The Buddha told the elder: "There is, good man! Yet it cannot be conveyed with few causes and conditions." He further told the elder: "The power of ten ordinary oxen equals the power of one hump-backed bull. The power of ten hump-backed bulls equals the power of one rhinoceros. The power of ten rhinoceroses equals the power of one ordinary elephant. The power of ten ordinary elephants equals the power of one Śarabha. The power of ten Śarabhas equals the power of one battle elephant. The power of ten battle elephants equals the power of one elephant in musth. The power of ten elephants in musth equals the power of one wild elephant. The power of ten wild elephants equals the power of one Kareṇuka elephant. The power of ten Kareṇuka elephants equals the power of one Kumuda-flower elephant. The power of ten Kumuda-flower elephants equals the power of one Utpala-flower elephant. The power of ten Utpala-flower elephants equals the power of one Nīlotpala elephant. The power of ten Nīlotpala elephants equals the power of one great fragrant elephant. The power of ten great fragrant elephants equals the power of one five-colored lion king. The power of ten lion kings equals the power of one mighty man among humans. The power of ten mighty men equals the power of one Nakula. The power of ten Nakulas equals the power of one Arjuna. The power of ten Arjunas equals the power of one Bhīmasena. The power of ten Bhīmasenas equals the power of one Nārāyaṇa. The power of six hundred and sixty-three Nārāyaṇas equals the power of one pore of the Buddha, the World-Honored One. Among the eighty-four thousand pores, each one possesses such power of Nārāyaṇa. The power of the eighty-four thousand pores equals the power of one joint of the Bhagavān."

The Buddha told the elder: "Know therefore that in each and every joint of all the Buddhas and Tathāgatas, there are eighty-four thousand six hundred and sixty-three kinds of Nārāyaṇa power."

When the Buddha had finished speaking this sūtra, the Elder Mahāpuṣpavyūha, having heard what the Buddha had said, rejoiced and reverently put it into practice.
