# 佛說賢者五福德經
## Sūtra Spoken by the Buddha on the Five Meritorious Virtues of the Virtuous Person

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 777

Translated from the Chinese. Western Jin, translated by Bái Fǎzǔ

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was dwelling in the Jetavana of Anāthapiṇḍada's park in the land of Śrāvastī. The Buddha addressed the bhikṣus: "When a virtuous person teaches the Dharma, there are five meritorious virtues. What are the five? Wherever that person is reborn, they will obtain long life; this is the first meritorious virtue. Wherever that person is reborn, they will obtain great wealth, an abundance of riches and treasures; this is the second meritorious virtue. Wherever that person is reborn, their form will be supremely beautiful; this is the third meritorious virtue. Wherever that person is reborn, their renown will spread far and wide; this is the fourth meritorious virtue. Wherever that person is reborn, they will possess keen intelligence and great wisdom; this is the fifth meritorious virtue.

"Why does a virtuous person who teaches the Dharma obtain long life? Because in a previous life, when teaching the Dharma, the beginning was good, the middle was good, and the end was good; the meaning was complete and sufficient, leading to quiescent stillness and the unconditioned. Those who delighted in killing, upon hearing the Dharma, immediately ceased and killed no more. Because of this, such a person obtains long life.

"Why does a person who teaches the Dharma obtain great wealth, an abundance of riches and treasures? Because in a previous life, when teaching the Dharma, the beginning, middle, and end were complete in meaning, leading to quiescent stillness and the unconditioned. Those who engaged in theft, upon hearing the sūtra, immediately ceased stealing and became capable of generosity. Because of this, such a person obtains great wealth.

"Why does a person who teaches the Dharma obtain supremely beautiful form? Because in a previous life, when teaching the Dharma, the beginning, middle, and end were complete in meaning, leading to quiescent stillness and the unconditioned, causing those who heard the Dharma to be harmonious and at ease, their countenances brightening with joy and naturally radiating a luminous glow. Because of this, such a person obtains beauty of form.

"Why does a person who teaches the Dharma obtain a renown that spreads far and wide? Because in a previous life, when teaching the Dharma, the beginning, middle, and end were complete in meaning, leading to quiescent stillness and the unconditioned, causing those who heard the Dharma to revere the Buddha, revere the Dharma, and revere the Saṃgha of bhikṣus. Because of this, such a person obtains widespread renown.

"Why does a person who teaches the Dharma obtain keen intelligence and great wisdom? Because in a previous life, when teaching the Dharma, the beginning, middle, and end were complete in meaning, leading to quiescent stillness and the unconditioned, causing those who heard the Dharma to clearly comprehend wondrous wisdom. Because of this, such a person obtains keen intelligence and great wisdom. These are the five dharmas by which one who teaches the sūtras obtains merit."

The bhikṣus, having heard the sūtra, rejoiced and came forward to pay obeisance to the Buddha.
