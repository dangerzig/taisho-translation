# 轉法輪菩薩摧魔怨敵法
## Method of Dharmacakrapravartana Bodhisattva for Crushing Māra and Enemies

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1150

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

At that time the Bodhisattva Who Crushes Māra-Enemies addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! The various bodhisattvas have each already proclaimed their secret true word teachings. World-Honored One! I now wish, for the sake of protecting kings and protecting the national boundaries and all sentient beings in the future age of the declining dharma, to proclaim the secret, supremely secret, most secret of all secrets: the Method for Crushing Māra and Enemies. I beseech the World-Honored One to compassionately grant permission."

The Buddha said: "Proclaim it."

"World-Honored One! If neighboring countries invade and disturb the national boundaries, or if the country's own armies are few and weak, or if there are disloyal subjects who rise in evil rebellion, one should take bitter neem-wood and fashion a banner-post, twelve finger-widths long and eight finger-widths in circumference, whittled perfectly round. In accordance with what the World-Honored One has commanded all devas, nāgas, and the eight classes to protect all countries, protect all kings, and protect all sentient beings of the national boundaries, removing calamities and granting happiness: when difficulty arises, one should paint upon the banner-post the images of the guardian spirits who protect the land.

"For this Great Tang realm, the land-protecting spirits are: Viśvakarman Yakṣa, Kapila Yakṣa, Dharmapāla Yakṣa, Skandha-netra Yakṣa, Virūpākṣa Yakṣa, Senāpati Yakṣa, Maṇibhadra Yakṣa, Pūrṇabhadra Yakṣa, Vidyādhara Yakṣa, and Aṭavaka Yakṣa; Vāsuki Nāga King, Sumanā Nāga King, and Puṣpavimāna Great Nāga King; Hāritī Great Celestial Queen, Airāvaṇī Great Celestial Queen, and Dvinetra Great Celestial Queen. Each has five thousand spirit generals as their retinue.

"All those who should be painted are painted in their original forms, holding their emblems, depicted upon the banner-post. Furthermore, upon the top of the banner-post, write the Anāvila-rāja Daśākṣara Buddhoṣṇīṣa True Word. The final syllable *Ṭa* is placed in the hub of the wheel. The names of the leaders of all enemies and rebels should be written between the spokes of the wheel. At the bottom of the banner-post, paint one wheel with eight complete spokes; as above, place the syllable *Ṭa* in the hub of the wheel.

"When calamity arises, one should invite a person who understands the dharma and who has received instruction in this teaching. In the middle of the night, draw a square altar. Upon the square altar, additionally draw a triangular altar. Erect this banner-post in the center of the altar. On the four sides of the altar, place various foods, drinks, and two argha vessels. Burn benzoin incense and light ghee lamps. The dharma master sits facing south.

"Then form the mudrā of the Hook of All Tathāgatas. With both hands interlocked inwardly as fists, use the right index finger as the hook. Recite the true word while calling the names, inviting the sixteen great protectors and their retinues, and the great spirit generals of awesome virtue, to come to the altar for offerings."

The Tathāgata Hook True Word:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ aḥ sarvatra-pratihata tathāgataṃ kuśa-bodhi-carya-paripūraka bhava svāhā*

"The practitioner should next enter the Trailokyavijaya Vajra Wrathful Samādhi, visualizing the awesome, fiercely blazing one with four faces and eight arms, the left foot treading upon Umā and the right foot treading upon Maheśvara.

"Then form the Trailokyavijaya Mudrā. With both hands in vajra fists, the little fingers and thumbs hooking each other, and the index fingers raised upright. When formed, recite the true word three, five, or seven times. The true word:

*Oṃ sumbhani sumbha hūṃ phaṭ gṛhṇa gṛhṇa hūṃ phaṭ gṛhṇa-paya hūṃ phaṭ ānaya hoḥ bhagavan vajra hūṃ phaṭ*

"Next, form the mudrā for commanding the devas of the upper realm. With both hands each forming a vajra fist, the two index fingers spiraling around each other. When the mudrā is formed, issue the command: 'You sixteen great protectors, great yakṣa generals and others! You have received the Buddha's entrustment to protect kings, protect the national boundaries, and protect all sentient beings, granting them happiness and removing calamities. Now our country faces such-and-such a difficulty. You great protectors, assist our army and crush the enemy. You shall cause their army to be bound and bewildered, to fall into chaos, and cause our troops to be strong and victorious.' Having issued this command, recite the true word three times:

*Hūṃ paridina dvāri ṣṭa vajra-kṣa haṃ jaḥ*

"Next, form the mudrā for commanding the devas who roam through space. With both hands in vajra-bond, bend both index fingers so the nails face each other, and raise both thumbs pressing upon both wind-fingers. When the mudrā is formed, issue the same command as in the preceding text, then recite the true word three times:

*Oṃ vajra-gra-paṇāya vajra hūṃ*

"Next, form the mudrā for commanding the devas who abide in space. It is the same as the Trailokyavijaya Mudrā, with the two index fingers spiraling around each other, and the mudrā placed atop the crown. When formed, issue the same command as in the preceding text, then recite the true word three times:

*Oṃ vajra-māra-kra vaṃ*

"Next, form the mudrā for commanding the devas who dwell on earth. With both hands in vajra-bond, extend both index fingers with tips touching, which is the same as the Vajra-guardian Bodhisattva Mudrā. When formed, issue the same command as in the preceding text, then recite the true word three times:

*Oṃ vajra-bandha haṃ*

"Next, form the mudrā for commanding the devas beneath the earth. With both hands extended, palms facing outward, both thumbs touching at the tips, both index fingers hooking each other, both middle fingers touching at the tips. Place the mudrā between the brows. Issue the same command as in the preceding text, then recite the true word three times:

*Oṃ heruka vajra-samaya svānuṣṭha-samāya mudrā-pra haṃ jaka hūṃ phaṭ*

"Having proclaimed this, form the wisdom hand in a vajra fist and press it upon the banner-post. Recite the Daśākṣara Buddhoṣṇīṣa True Word one hundred and eight times.

"Before reciting this Daśākṣara Buddhoṣṇīṣa True Word, one should first form the mudrā and recite it seven times. The mudrā: with both hands interlocked inwardly as fists, both thumbs entering the palms, both index fingers each bent to press against the backs of the thumbs. The true word:

*Oṃ vajra-satva-uṣṇīṣa hūṃ phaṭ*

"When the recitation is complete, burn more incense and offer flowers. Then hold up the argha and, on behalf of the sovereign and all the people, make vast aspirations for benefit, happiness, and supreme blessings. Then form the vajra-bond, with both middle fingers raised upright. Recite the Vajra Liberation True Word three times and send the holy assembly each back to their original palaces. The true word:

*Oṃ vajra muḥ*

"When the dharma ceremony is complete, store the banner-post in a clean box or chest. The offering food and drink and so forth should be sent and placed in a flowing river in accordance with the dharma."

Colophon: The timing for dispatching troops should be determined according to the lunar mansions and astrological calculations.

Collated on the nineteenth day of the ninth month of the third year of Jōkyō [1686 CE]. The Kawanami monk of longevity, Jōgon.
