# 建立曼荼羅護摩儀軌

## Manual for Establishing Maṇḍalas and Homa Rituals

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0912

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, compiled by Fǎquán (法全).

---

On the first day, empower one's own body with the Tathāgata's nature, make offerings, alert the earth deity, select the ground by removing all foul objects, and tamp it firmly. On the second day, within the altar dig one cubit deep, empower the five treasures with the Acala mantra one hundred and eight times, establish the positions of the holy ones, and mark the positions of the five buddhas and four bodhisattvas with white sandalwood dots. On the third day, place the vases and empower them with the Acala mantra one hundred and eight times. On the fourth day at dusk, next sprinkle the perfumed water mantra one hundred and eight times, then purify by sprinkling. On the fifth day, protect the body with offerings, reciting Acala or Trailokyavijaya one hundred and eight times, then recite the earth-holding mantra. On the sixth night, the master and disciple bathe and don clean garments, proceed to the altar and make offerings according to the rite. With mudrās in correspondence, press the hand upon the central court, reciting the mantra once per press up to seven times; the positions of all the holy ones are likewise established. On the seventh day, next teach the disciple the three refuges, confession, and arousing the mind of enlightenment.

> I bow my head in homage
> To all the Bhagavāns,
> To the Dharma and all the bodhisattvas,
> To the great wrathful Vajrapāṇi!
> For the benefit of those who practice yoga,
> To swiftly accomplish great siddhi,
> Far removed from all faults,
> I establish the maṇḍala
> And set forth the homa method.
> One who abides well in yoga
> First uses the pure dharmadhātu
> To establish the vessel world,
> Contemplating the wondrous bodhicitta.
> The body becomes identical to the being,
> Its color like blue beryl,
> Abiding upon the karma wheel,
> With the great mantra-king
> Pervading all the limbs.
> Empowerment performed according to the rite,
> Everywhere becoming a garland of flames.
> When an auspicious day and time arrive,
> With stars and constellations all in accord,
> Before the morning meal, at an auspicious sign,
> One should select the ground
> And remove all faults and evils.
> First pay homage to all the Tathāgatas,
> Alert the earth deity,
> And speak this verse:
>
> "You who are the heavenly guardian of this place,
> Toward the Buddha, the guiding teacher,
> Practice the supreme practice,
> The perfection of purifying the ground.
> Just as the armies of Māra were vanquished,
> The Lion of the Śākyas saved the world.
> I too shall subjugate Māra.
> I now draw the maṇḍala."
>
> Kneeling with the right hand extended,
> Press the ground and repeatedly recite the verse.
> With perfume, flowers, and the like,
> Make offerings to the buddhas and bodhisattvas
> And to the earth deity and her retinue.
> Sprinkle with pure perfumed water,
> Then prepare the ground,
> With the earth deity maintaining the sequence.

The mantra reads:

*Oṃ bhūḥ khaṃ*

> With vajra-bond, open the palms,
> Press face-up three times seven,
> Press face-down likewise,
> And the ground becomes adamantine.
> Sweep, sprinkle, and wipe clean.
> At dusk, holding flowers and incense,
> Enter the altar site alone,
> Face east, hold the incense burner,
> And recite the verse requesting the earth:
>
> "All buddhas who compassionately care for beings,
> We humbly ask you to keep us in your thoughts.
> I now address the noble sages,
> Dṛḍhā, the steadfast earth goddess, and her retinue,
> All the Tathāgatas and sons of the Buddha!
> Without abandoning your compassionate vows, descend,
> I request this ground to seek accomplishment.
> Be my witnesses and protectors."
>
> Having earnestly invited them,
> The holy assembly gathers like clouds.
> Mahāvairocana's pure body
> Pervades the entire dharmadhātu;
> The buddhas of the ten directions likewise.
> With body, speech, and mind, bow sincerely.
> Kneeling on both knees in the long-kneeling posture,
> With the concentration hand hold the vajra,
> Keeping it upright at the heart;
> With the wisdom hand, extend the five fingers,
> Palm flat pressing upon the ground.
> As before, alert the earth deity,
> Pressing once for each recitation,
> Practicing with the root mantra.
> One should sincerely announce:
>
> "I respectfully address all the Tathāgatas,
> The great bodhisattva assembly, śrāvakas and pratyekabuddhas,
> Devas, nāgas, and the eight classes of beings,
> And the spirits dwelling in this place!
> I, [name], relying on the vidyādhara-piṭaka teaching,
> Hold the mantra of [deity],
> Seeking the unsurpassed Way.
> In this ground I wish
> To establish a maṇḍala
> And diligently practice recitation.
> May the honored ones recall their original vows
> And grant me permission for this establishment.
> Protect and assist me; do not allow
> Heavenly māras or evil spirits
> To disturb or create obstacles.
> When Śākyamuni sat beneath the Bodhi Tree,
> He vanquished the hosts of Māra.
> I now do likewise.
> If there are obstacles,
> May all the buddhas and bodhisattvas
> Show me a prior sign.
> Do not let ghosts and spirits
> Manifest deceptions and delusions.
> If there are no obstacles,
> May I see an auspicious vision."
>
> Sitting silently in peace,
> Recite the root mantra
> One thousand one hundred times,
> Leaving behind all discursive thought.
> When the count is complete, lie down peacefully,
> Concentrating on the mind of enlightenment:
> A moon disk like a wish-fulfilling jewel,
> Brilliantly luminous within and without.
> Accomplishment or non-accomplishment
> Will all appear in the mirror of the mind.
> If inauspicious, do not force the matter,
> Lest one invite harm upon oneself.

After the prior signs are auspicious, establish the site. At the center of the altar, dig a square of one cubit on each side, removing tiles and stones, then refill and tamp it level. Test whether it is solid or hollow. If it is suitable for establishment, empower the ground with the Acala mantra one hundred and eight times, then proceed to the full excavation.

The shovel mudrā: vajra-bond, with the dhyāna, prajñā, vīrya, and bala fingers extended upward.

The mantra reads:

*Oṃ nikhana vasudhe svāhā*

> Select and remove all impurities;
> Replace unfit soil with clean earth,
> Sprinkle repeatedly with perfumed water,
> Level and firm like a pure mirror.
> In the center altar, place five treasures:
> Gold, silver, pearl,
> Sapphire, and crystal.
> The efficacious medicines are: gastrodia,
> Ginseng, poria,
> Sweet flag, and asparagus root.
> Rice, wheat both great and small,
> Mung beans, sesame, and the like.
> Agarwood, sandalwood, cloves,
> Turmeric, and dragon-brain camphor,
> Placed in gold or silver vessels.
> Seal and protect with the earth-goddess mantra,
> Mix perfumed water with cow dung,
> And empower with the Aparājitā mantra.

The mantra for anointing the ground reads:

*Oṃ karāli mahākarāli svāhā*

The mantra for sweeping the ground reads:

*Oṃ hara hara cakra haṇa ya svāhā*

> Anoint the positions of the holy ones with white sandalwood;
> Recite the activity mantra one thousand times.
> Next with the eyes of compassion
> Contemplate all sentient beings
> And practice the amṛta mantra.
> Visualize the position of one's own heart:
> The dwelling place of the guiding teacher,
> An eight-petaled lotus arising from the mind,
> Supremely magnificent.
> Within a full moon disk,
> Stainless as a pure mirror,
> Forever abiding there,
> The true-word savior of the world,
> Golden, endowed with brilliant flames,
> Poison-destroying, abiding in samādhi,
> Difficult to gaze upon like the sun.
> All the noble ones likewise,
> Within their circular mirrors,
> See their own bodily form
> Equal to the body of a buddha.
> The practitioner dwells in the Buddha's chamber,
> The uṣṇīṣa emitting a hundred rays of light,
> Illuminating boundless buddha-fields.
> Skillfully regulate the breath and vital energy,
> Continuously recite the syllable *a*,
> From one breath up to three,
> Exerting full effort in recitation.
> When sensations arise and the mind corresponds,
> Leave behind all discriminations.
> From the pure dharmadhātu essence,
> Establish the maṇḍala.
> Pay homage to the World-Honored One
> And to Prajñāpāramitā, Mother of Buddhas!
> Next prepare the colored pigments,
> Protect them with Aparājitā,
> Arrange and distribute the holy ones,
> And paint their adorned bodies.
> Next set forth the homa,
> The secret method of accomplishment.
> In a newly purified room,
> Establish the ritual arena,
> Set out fine incense and flowers,
> And make offerings with sincere devotion,
> Sealing and purifying according to the rite.

> Pacification and enrichment,
> Attraction and subjugation:
> Their divine power is unfathomable.
> They can turn the hearts of heaven and earth.
> Their function is without limit,
> Greatly expanding the blessings of all beings.
> The various homa altars,
> Their presiding deities being numerous,
> Each category seeking different wishes,
> Each with its own distinctions.
> Homa is broadly of two kinds:
> Inner and outer.
> What is called inner homa:
> All sentient beings
> Are born from karma.
> To purify and remove karma
> Is liberation itself.
> What can burn away past karma
> Is the mind of enlightenment;
> This is called inner homa.
> Just as worldly fire
> Burns things to ashes,
> Here it is not so.
> Using one's own fierce wisdom,
> One burns all afflictions
> Like the fire at the end of a kalpa,
> Leaving no residue.
> Three places become one body:
> The great altar is the homa;
> The homa is one's own body;
> One's own body is Agni;
> Agni is Mahāvairocana.
> Body, speech, and mind united,
> The three equalities without difference,
> Endowed with the three bodies,
> Extending throughout the dharmadhātu,
> Neither arising nor ceasing,
> Beyond words, beyond the marks of words.
> Both the born and the unborn
> Are none other than the Great Sun Lord!
> Contemplate the gate of the syllable *a*:
> Covered and obscured by delusion,
> Enter into fierce wisdom,
> Surrounded on all sides by flames,
> Blazing like the fire at a kalpa's end,
> Holding a sword and a lasso,
> Burn away karmic afflictions,
> The stumps and roots of ignorance,
> Until nothing remains.
> Next contemplate the mind of enlightenment,
> Bathing it with the water of great compassion.
> Visualize throughout the entire body
> The flowing of white amṛta,
> Sprinkling upon the ten direction realms
> Upon all heat-tormented sentient beings,
> Nourishing the sprout of awakening.
> In sequence the various offspring arise:
> This itself is called the Dharmakāya,
> The secret inner homa.

> Furthermore, the World-Honored One said:
> After I achieved awakening,
> I taught twelve kinds of fire.
> The fire of wisdom is foremost,
> Named the Great Indra fire,
> The light of bodhicitta wisdom,
> Square in form and golden in color.
> That lord is supremely dignified,
> Surrounded by a garland of flames,
> Abiding in the samādhi of tranquility.
> Contemplate and recite the secret words;
> It can accomplish all things.
> Following worldly convention, the altar and hearth
> And all offerings and garments are yellow.
>
> The second is the Fire of Fulfillment,
> Its form like the autumn moon,
> Its radiance pervading everywhere,
> Body and garments of pure white,
> A moon-disk maṇḍala,
> Holding a rosary and water vessel.
> It removes water, fire, insects, and frost,
> Diseases of self and others,
> Various evil calamities,
> Hesitation and unsettled minds:
> This fire can purify them all.
>
> The third is named Māru,
> Dark and parched in form and color,
> With a wind-wheel half-moon altar,
> Holding a cloth in a wind-blown posture.
> For those practitioners
> Whose doubtful minds vacillate,
> Whose beginningless delusions
> And unremoved roots of affliction
> Constantly wind around and bind them,
> Adding dark obscuration:
> Repeatedly rely on this fire to burn and remove them,
> Scattering and destroying the heavy clouds.
>
> The fourth is Rohita,
> Its body dignified and full,
> A triangular garland of flames,
> Red as the color of the rising sun,
> Holding a sword with a slightly wrathful expression.
>
> The fifth is Mṛtyu,
> Its form pale yellow in color,
> With hair, mustache, and whiskers upon the body,
> And great majestic light atop the crown.
> The left eye wrathful, the right smiling,
> The left color red, the right yellow,
> The left seat triangular.
> The concentration hand holds a sword, the wisdom hand a vajra.
> It can accomplish two kinds of rites:
> Summoning and pacification together.
>
> The sixth is the Wrathful Fire,
> Its form flame-colored,
> One eye closed, roaring greatly,
> Fangs protruding above and below,
> Disheveled hair standing upright,
> Accomplishing two rites: fire and wind together.
>
> The seventh is named Ūṣman,
> The Great Parinirvāṇa fire,
> Swift and extremely wrathful,
> Its syllable endowed with five colors.
>
> The eighth is named Kṣaya,
> Its color like gathered lightning,
> Extremely difficult to gaze upon,
> Seated within a vajra wheel,
> Able to remove and dispel heavy obstacles
> And their chief retinues.
>
> The ninth is the Mind-Born Fire:
> This lord accomplishes according to the mind,
> Manifesting universal gate bodies of every kind,
> With great power, sovereignty, and wisdom.
>
> The tenth is named Kalāvi,
> That lord's right hand held sideways,
> The pure practice *oṃ* seal and sound.

The eleventh.

> The twelfth is Mohana,
> Its form like the fire at the end of a kalpa,
> The fire that subdued Māra at the Bodhi site.
> If sentient beings
> Create evil that cannot be stopped,
> And admonition only increases their misdeeds,
> This fire can purify and remove them,
> Skillfully restraining and subduing,
> Confusing and rendering them unaware,
> Gradually guiding them into the path.

> Next I set forth the four secret
> Ritual manuals to be practiced.
> The root deity's mantra and mudrā,
> Body, speech, and mind in correspondence,
> The three positions perfectly united,
> Performing all ritual activities.
> From outer, guide inward:
> The principle is without distinction.

**Pacification (Śāntika)**

> Pacification begins at the first watch of night.
> Enrichment begins at the first part of day.
> The middle period is for fierce subjugation.
> Hook-summoning is performed at all times.
> Attraction rites are done at night.
>
> Śāntika pacifies calamities:
> Intelligence and long life,
> Removal of obstacles and difficulties,
> Affliction by the five planets,
> Various calamities of every kind,
> Quarrels and disputes,
> Oppression by the royal court,
> Inner and outer disharmony,
> The seven luminaries departing from their normal courses,
> Wind and rain coming at the wrong times,
> Epidemics and famine,
> Ghosts, spirits, and all manner of inauspiciousness.
> When such calamities arise,
> Face north with legs crossed,
> Knees raised, right thigh first,
> All garments should be pure white,
> Food, drink, incense, flowers, ground,
> Lamps and candles likewise.
> Arrange the mantras upon the moon disk;
> The syllables should also be white.
> Abide in the contemplation of the three liberations:
> Enter the samādhi of emptiness,
> Directing the mind throughout the dharmadhātu,
> Suddenly there is not a single dharma.
> For a moment, settle the mind,
> Extinguishing all views;
> To remove attachment to emptiness,
> Enter the samādhi of signlessness,
> Leaving behind all marks;
> Next, the samādhi of wishlessness:
> In that true suchness wisdom,
> Beyond marks and originally empty and tranquil,
> There is also nothing wished for.
> Arising from samādhi,
> Carefully visualize at the chest:
> A luminous circle of about one cubit,
> Like the light of the autumn moon,
> Bright and clear, facing upward at the heart.
> Next contemplate the root deity's mouth
> And the secret phrases it holds.
> The syllables clearly form a path of letters,
> Entering the practitioner's mouth.
> The letters revolve rightward,
> Arrayed upon the moon disk in the heart.
> Each letter is golden in color,
> Streaming forth great radiance.
> Body and mind unscattered and unmoved,
> Enter the gate of the syllable *a*.
> Then upon each and every letter,
> Contemplate the meaning of true reality.
> This is called samādhi recitation.
> First recite the homage formula
> Three times seven, then remove it.
> Begin from *oṃ*,
> "For [name], remove calamities,"
> And end with *svāhā*.
> Additions not in the original are made as the occasion requires.
> Recite in a soft voice,
> The mind must be tranquil.
> The protective deity takes a wrathful form.

If one constructs a fire altar, dig a round hearth, one cubit in diameter and half a cubit deep. At the bottom of the hearth, fashion a wheel from clay with eight spokes, one span in diameter. Mark the four quarters as the perimeter. Anoint the altar entirely in white. Upon the step, lay kuśa grass, arranged clockwise following the sun, with roots at the base and tips outward. Or spread out various colored fabrics, resplendent in their beauty. When all the decorations are complete, burn agarwood incense and light ghee lamps. Offer fresh fine flowers, fruits, food, and drink.

From the first of the month to the eighth day, perform homa three times at the appropriate hours. With a compassionate heart, generate joy and look upon all beings. This, in brief, is the homa site. One should hold the activity mantra, using the Kīlī mantra-king to seal and protect all substances.

The wisdom finger presses the ground while extending the remaining pāramitā fingers. Sprinkle everywhere with perfumed water.

The mantra reads:

*Oṃ kīli kīli vajra hūṃ phaṭ*

> When wishing to perform ritual activities,
> To accomplish the rite of sealing the altar,
> Protect the body and the disciple,
> And dispel all obstacles.
> Through this mudrā and mantra,
> The devas of the pure abodes
> Will always come to this place,
> Watching over and hastening the results.
> The hearth faces the maṇḍala;
> Externally they appear separate.
> The offerings are as for the great altar.
> Fill half the hearth with burning charcoal,
> Branches from the inner wood of trees,
> Milk-wood and dried kindling,
> Cast into the kuṇḍa hearth.
> Strike fire fresh, never use old fire.
> Fan the flame; do not blow with the mouth.
> Then sprinkle pure perfumed water
> And light the hearth while reciting the following mantra:

*Oṃ jvala hūṃ*

> When the fire sends forth its flames,
> The wrathful king removes all impurities.
> Empower all substances with the seal and mantra;
> Touch each one while reciting the mantra.
> Then within the fire's flames,
> Visualize the syllable *raṃ*.
> It transforms into the Fire Light Lord,
> Body red, hair yellow,
> Three eyes and four arms,
> Flames of light covering the entire body.
> The wise one extends the right hand,
> Curling the space finger into the palm,
> Pressing with the earth, water, and fire fingers,
> While the wind finger hooks in summoning:
>
> "I now bow my head and invite
> Agni, foremost among the devas,
> Great ṛṣi among the gods,
> Revered by those of pure conduct.
> May you kindly descend here
> And accept this homa offering."

The invocation mantra reads:

*Oṃ ehy ehi mahābhūtāni vāriśikhī vijasatma gṛhītvā hutima mahā-ṛṣima asmin sannidhito bhava agni aye havya kavya vāhanāya svāhā*

> When Agni comes and takes his seat,
> Purify the fire with the seal and sprinkled water.

The mantra reads:

*Oṃ amṛte hana hana hūṃ phaṭ*

> Scatter flowers upon the kuṇḍa,
> Offer arghya perfumed water.
> With vajra fist, extend the wind finger
> And stir the arghya water.
> Turning rightward, sprinkle into the hearth,
> Visualizing a rinsing of Agni's mouth.

The auspicious mantra reads:

*Oṃ varada vajra dhāṃ*

> The various accessories for homa
> Are arranged on the right side:
> Two ladles, empty vessels, and so forth.
> Ghee, honey, oil, firewood, and the like
> Are placed on the left.
> Following the homa procedure,
> Both hands rest upon the knees,
> Turning rightward in the proper direction.
> The concentration hand holds rosary and vajra.
> Extend the wind finger with the large ladle.
> The small ladle is held in the wisdom hand.

> Pouring it to fill the large ladle.
> The wisdom hand releases the small ladle and takes up the large.

> Oil, honey, milk, yogurt,
> Milk porridge, five-grain porridge,
> Polished rice, rice-paddy flowers:

The mantra reads:

*Agnaye havya havya vāhanāya dīpya dīpya kuru svāhā*

> Draw out the sound of *hā*;
> The pressing sound matches the fruit of the offering.
> The practitioner stands to the left.
> The master, holding a vajra in the wisdom hand,
> Hooks with the earth finger upon the space finger,
> Commanding the offering of food to Agni.

The pacification homa mantra reads:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ amahā-śānti-gata śāntikara-praśama-dharma-nirjata-abhāva-svabhāva dharma-samata-prāpta svāhā*

> Request Agni to leave the hearth
> And take his position in the southeast.
> Next, holding the incense burner,
> Invite the ruling planet,
> Together with the life-star, career-star, and natal constellation.
> With the fist mudrā, hook with wind and fire fingers.
> Make offerings and send them back to their original positions.
> Next invite the root deity.
> With both hands hold seasonal flowers,
> Purify and brighten them,
> Then toss them across the hearth.
> Visualize the flowers covering all the seats.
> As before, purify the fire again,
> Invite the deity to enter the hearth,
> And make offerings with sincere devotion.
> Concentrate on the holy assembly
> And emanate vast clouds:
> Food, banners, pennants, and canopies
> Pervading as many realms as there are dust motes.
> Offer them to the holy celestial assembly.
> The radiance of the light that touches them
> Relieves the suffering of beings in the three evil destinies,
> Bringing peace and joy to body and mind.
> Including the world-protecting devas,
> Perform three rounds of homa.
> All the offerings of incense, flowers, and food
> Are mixed together as one.
> For the *rāja* homa,
> Dedicate it to one's retinue, the hundred officials,
> All beings in the dharmadhātu,
> And oneself, each with three rounds of homa.
> Finally, on the four sides of the hearth,
> Place food, drink, and various fruits,
> Empower them with the homa.
> The master goes out, washes his hands,
> Then at a pure location
> Makes offerings to the eight directional devas.
> Returning, he again makes offerings to the fire
> And joyfully bids farewell to the devas:
>
> "Great devas with sovereign divine power,
> Who can fulfill the countless wishes of sentient beings,
> May all that is sought be accomplished.
> I bow to the devas as they return to their palaces.
> When in the future there is sincere supplication,
> May you come again to accept our offerings."

The mantra reads:

*Kīli kīli vajra mukha*

**Enrichment (Puṣṭika)**

> For enrichment rites,
> Named puṣṭika,
> With a joyful mind in correspondence,
> From the ninth to the fifteenth of the month:
> The five supernatural powers and treasure troves,
> Swords, wheels, vajras, wealth,
> Medicine pills, eye-medicines,
> Promotion and increase of lifespan,
> Merit, wisdom, and renown,
> Official posts and royal appointments,
> Auspiciousness, victory, and peace.
> Face east in full cross-legged posture,
> All garments yellow.
> The additions and subtractions to the mantra phrases
> Are as before, with no difference.
> Concentrate on the clouds of offerings
> Pervading the ten direction realms,
> Illuminating all sentient beings,
> Who all obtain prosperity,
> Wealth, honor, and long life.
> These very clouds of light,
> At the place where one dwells,
> Rain down the seven precious resources,
> And rain celestial amṛta
> That anoints and pours into the practitioner's heart.
> The hearth is square, two cubits in diameter,
> Round in circumference, half a cubit deep.
> At the bottom, place a three-pronged vajra.
> Anoint with sandalwood and turmeric.
> The star Ābhāsvara and Jupiter,
> The Prosperity-Friend and the Mind constellation,
> And so forth: all offerings follow the previous procedure.

**Attraction (Vaśīkaraṇa)**

> For the rite of attraction,
> Named vaśīkaraṇa,
> With a mind blending joy and wrath,
> On the sixteenth and twenty-third of the month:
> Summoning humans, devas, nāgas,
> Ghosts, spirits, and non-human beings,
> Winning the respect and love of all,
> Harmonizing family, nation, and retinue,
> Enemies and friends alike,
> Making them peaceful and joyful,
> And obtaining wondrous eloquence.
> The eight classes of devas and nāgas,
> All buddhas and bodhisattvas,
> Śrāvakas and pratyekabuddhas:
> All everywhere protect and remember.
> For one whose wishes are fulfilled,
> Face west in half cross-legged posture.
> The upper procedures are as for enrichment.
> "For [name], attract [target]."
> The mantra-holder has a joyful mind.
> The protective deity takes a tranquil-wrathful form.
> The vajra hook summons,
> And hook-clouds extend throughout the dharmadhātu.
> Next summon those in the three evil destinies
> And establish them in the human and heavenly realms.
> Joyful flower-arrow clouds
> Pierce those of the two vehicles who have weariness and aversion,
> Enmity, and the three heavy obstacles,
> And remove their harmful, hateful minds,
> Bringing them to the Stage of Supreme Joy.
> The hearth is shaped like an eight-petaled lotus,
> Blooming with stem and stamens,
> One cubit in diameter, half a cubit deep.
> At the bottom, place a red lotus.
> Two rims, each four finger-widths.
> The garments are red, white, and yellow.
> Press the space finger and raise the wind finger,
> Making one dot in each direction,
> Thereby completing the great boundary seal.
> Protect everywhere with the syllable *hūṃ*.
> Burn cloves and storax.
> Light lamps with fruit oil.
> Euphorbia and other thorny woods
> And red flowers for offerings.
> Within the flames, visualize *ai-ra*,
> Red with dark blue hair.
> The Brahmā-born one and Venus,
> The Embryo constellation and related stars:
> Invite them all and request their joyful aid.

**Subjugation (Abhicāruka)**

> For the rite of subjugation,
> Named abhicāruka,
> On the twenty-fourth and at month's end:
> Controlling ghosts, spirits, and evil people
> Who destroy the eye of the true Dharma,
> And evil poisonous nāgas
> Who send untimely violent rains,
> Frost and hail that damage crops,
> Floods and droughts at the wrong times,
> Ghosts and spirits roaming everywhere,
> Strange apparitions manifesting in every place,
> People unable to rest in peace,
> Harassment and disturbance wherever they dwell,
> And those who act with disloyalty and impiety:
> This method can restrain and remove them,
> For the peace and happiness of the world.
> Therefore this great skillful means is established,
> Which also benefits those subdued.
> Inwardly arouse the mind of compassion;
> Outwardly display great wrath.
> Left foot pressing upon the right,
> Face south, sitting in a crouch.
> All colors tend to dark blue-black.
> Within the heart, contemplate a luminous circle
> That transforms into a great solar disk,
> Blazing without equal,
> Radiating like the fire at a kalpa's end.
> Recite the mantra with fierce vigor.
> Others nearby may listen.
> The protective deity's mind is tranquil,
> Yet contemplates the wrathful lord.
> From all the pores of the body
> Stream forth clouds of weapons,
> Offered throughout all of space
> To all the wrathful lords.
> Weapons rain down
> Upon the dwelling place of the enemy above.
> The fire hearth is made triangular,
> With a single-pronged vajra placed in the center.
> The dimensions are the same as for vaśīkaraṇa.
> Anoint the altar ground with dark red-black.
> Use thorny-tree wood and red flowers,
> Black flowers without fragrance,
> Scattered as offerings.
> Iron liquid, black vitriol water,
> Black flowers, mustard seeds, and cypress:
> Place a small amount of each in the arghya.
> For incense, burn asa foetida.
> For lamp wicks, use mustard-seed oil.
> Beef tallow and *rudhira*
> As offerings to the holy ones.
> Wrathful empowerment and protection throughout.

The mantra reads:

*Hūṃ kīli kīli vajra hūṃ hūṃ hūṃ phaṭ phaṭ phaṭ*

> Proceeding around the kuṇḍa,
> Spread foul-smelling thorny grass.
> Place bitter sophora root as kindling in the hearth.
> When the fire is lit, sprinkle purifying water.
> The syllable *raṃ* becomes Agni,
> Dark red with pervasive smoky flames.
> Beef tallow and *rudhira*,
> Ghee, milk, honey, sesame oil,
> And viśa, all mixed together,
> Cast three times seven into the kuṇḍa.
> Bones, hair, evil-smelling wood,
> Thorns, bitter sophora root, and viśa,
> Dipped in *rudhira* oil,
> Poured over all while reciting the mantra.
> Point toward the target's direction,
> Invoke the ruling Mars,
> The Shen constellation and the Willow constellation,
> And make vows, singing hymns of praise:
>
> "Great devas with wrathful divine power,
> Who can fulfill sentient beings' wish to destroy enemies,
> I now seek to vanquish foes and extinguish enmity.
> I bow to the devas as they return to their palaces.
> When in the future there is sincere supplication,
> May you come again to accept our offerings."

The farewell mantra reads:

*Pūjito dūmāya bhaktiya gaccha agnaye svabhāvanaṃ punarāgamanāya svāhā*

> Release the boundary according to the proper procedure.
> Directly before the deity,
> Stretching and moving the limbs,
> Yawning, spitting, and making sounds:
> Such things as these,
> Restrain them, and do not be arrogant.
> A great teaching ācārya,
> A practitioner of deep practice and skillful wisdom,
> Who wishes to seek great siddhi,
> Should frequently perform homa.
> For every three hundred thousand recitations completed,
> Perform one fire offering.

**Signs of Accomplishment**

> Furthermore, the signs of homa:
> Red flames or golden color,
> Moist and lustrous like cow's ghee,
> Fragrant, with silent flames,
> Flames arising upon flames,
> Spreading in the shape of parasols and canopies.
> The empowered substances
> Emit warmth, smoke, and light.
> From within arise great sounds,
> The lion's roar of Brahmā's voice,
> Resounding like thunder,
> Subtle and wondrous tones,
> The sound of bells, drums, and elegant music:
> Know these as signs of accomplishment.
> If the fire's energy is cold,
> The color without brightness or luster,
> With great sparks shooting outward,
> Or a foul smell and smoky flames,
> And the embers arise and sink into the ground:
> Know these as inner and outer obstacles.

**Dream Signs**

> When first practicing the pacification method,
> One may dream of buddhas, bodhisattvas,
> Vajra-holders, or kings,
> Maṇḍala lords, mantra lords,
> Vidyā-kings, or the Three Treasures,
> Brahmins or laypeople.
> One sees oneself reciting mantras,
> Wearing fine new white garments,
> The body adorned with jeweled ornaments.
> Beautiful women in splendid attire,
> Pregnant or with children,
> Holding incense, flowers, vases, and canopies;
> Or receiving respectful offerings,
> Circumambulating in the practice of the Way.
> Eating polished rice, yogurt-rice,
> Amṛta, milk, fruits, and flowers.
> Lush forests, ascending mountains,
> Ascending stūpas and tall pavilions;
> Or riding carriages, horses, elephants,
> Lions, oxen, deer, and such,
> White cranes, the king of peacocks,
> Garuḍas, or boats.
> Sailing across pure waters of the sea,
> Or soaring through the sky at will,
> The body surrounded by a halo of light,
> Seated in a Dharma assembly listening to the teaching,
> Or in an assembly, expounding the Dharma.
> Obtaining pearls and precious jewels,
> Conch shells, wheels, swords, and vajras.
> Kind words of comfort and praise,
> Auspicious clouds and lightning,
> Gentle breezes and rains of celestial flowers,
> Fragrant incense and bathing:
> All these are signs of accomplishment.
> Upon awakening, do not sleep again.
>
> If one dreams of butchers,
> Pigs, donkeys, camels, or dogs,
> Either touching one or being nearby,
> Corpses likewise,
> Evil ghosts and frightening hordes,
> One's body clad in tattered, torn garments,
> Women of ugly and hideous form,
> And various inauspicious things:
> These are obstacles, not signs of accomplishment.
> Or if deluded thoughts arise,
> Or one has violated the samaya:
> Earth-water-inner forms the bond,
> Fire wheel encircled by two winds.
> Bring the front together and practice the great wheel.
> Three times seven times, all obstacles are consumed.
> For sleep, the division-mother protects;
> The division-lord protects the dwelling place.
> For inauspicious evil dreams,
> Recite the division-mother mantra one hundred times.
> Whenever about to eat,
> Offer the food to the root deity with the mantra.
> Recite the division-lord's true mantra
> To empower both the food and the eating.
> Homa is like a rite of reverence.
> Its subtle details are explained by the mantra.
> If even a small part is not in correspondence,
> All manner of evils will gather.
> When three million recitations are completed,
> One will always see the root deity.
> The mind penetrates the gate of liberation.
> Wisdom becomes immeasurable.
> All wishes, worldly and transcendent,
> Will all be fulfilled.
