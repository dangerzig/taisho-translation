# 佛說苾芻五法經
## Sūtra on the Five Dharmas for Bhikṣus, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1479

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmadeva (法天).

---

The Tathāgata, the Worthy One, the Perfectly Awakened One, was in the kingdom of Śrāvastī, dwelling there for the sake of observing and helping future generations. At that time, innumerable devas and humans, knowing the Buddha, the World-Honored One, to be the teacher of gods and humans, reverently made offerings with devotion, veneration, and praise. His beneficial reputation was the most excellent, and each offered fine garments, exquisite food, bedding, and medicines. The World-Honored One, for their benefit and joy, accepted and used them all without attachment, like a lotus in water.

He caused the hosts of devas, humans, and sentient beings to obtain excellent merit-fruit and wondrous adornment. He sent down sweet dew for the devas and humans, causing them to abide in it for a long, long time. He further caused immeasurable koṭi-nayuta hundreds of thousands of sentient beings to obtain the sweet dew. He would even deliver them from the suffering of birth, old age, sickness, death, and the cycle of saṃsāra, and even deliver them from the great peril of the hells, enabling them to attain peace, tranquility, equanimity, and fearlessness, and to attain nirvāṇa.

Moreover, throughout the kingdoms of Magadha, Vāmanī, Kāśi, Kośala, Kuru-Pañcāla, and others, the kings such as the Vatsa king, Matsya king, Śūrasena king, Śivi king, and Naśaraṇa king: all these kings, upon seeing him, were subdued by the Buddha's wisdom-power. Moreover, he was able to walk the celestial walking-path, the Brahmā walking-path, the holy walking-path, the emptiness walking-path, the quiescence walking-path, the buddha-of-buddhas walking-path, the teacher-of-teachers walking-path, the knower's walking-path, and the omniscient walking-path, attaining the mind's liberation and the supreme pāramitā. Furthermore, wherever there were walking-paths, and whatever walking-paths were sought, the World-Honored One could walk them all.

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣu assembly: "There are five kinds of dharmas that should be possessed for one's conduct. These are dharmas that bhikṣus of the future should always practice.

"What are the five? A bhikṣu who does not know āpatti, does not know non-āpatti, does not know light āpatti, does not know grave āpatti, and is newly ordained or has less than five years. A bhikṣu who possesses these five dharmas is not qualified to serve in the role of admonisher.

"A bhikṣu who possesses the knowledge of five dharmas is qualified to serve as admonisher. What are the five? A bhikṣu who knows what constitutes āpatti for a bhikṣu, who knows non-āpatti, who knows light āpatti, who knows grave āpatti, and who has completed five years or has more than five years. A bhikṣu who possesses these five dharmas is qualified to serve as admonisher.

"Furthermore, a bhikṣu who possesses five dharmas is not permitted to live without dependence. What are the five? A bhikṣu who does not know the prātimokṣa, does not know how to recite the prātimokṣa, does not know the boundary-demarcation, does not know the procedures of boundary-demarcation, and is newly ordained or has less than five years. A bhikṣu who possesses these five dharmas is not permitted to live without dependence.

"A bhikṣu who possesses these five dharmas is permitted to live without dependence. What are the five? A bhikṣu who knows the bhikṣu prātimokṣa, knows how to recite the prātimokṣa, knows the boundary-demarcation, knows the procedures of boundary-demarcation, and has completed five years or has more than five years. A bhikṣu who possesses these five dharmas is permitted to live without dependence.

"Bhikṣus, there is another set of five dharmas that, if possessed, means a bhikṣu is not permitted to live without dependence. What are the five? A bhikṣu who does not know the poṣadha, does not know the procedures of the poṣadha, does not know the boundary-demarcation, does not know the procedures of boundary-demarcation, and is newly ordained or has less than five years. A bhikṣu who possesses these five dharmas is not permitted to live without dependence.

"A bhikṣu who possesses these five dharmas is permitted to live without dependence. What are the five? A bhikṣu who knows the bhikṣu poṣadha, knows the procedures of the poṣadha, knows the boundary-demarcation, knows the procedures of boundary-demarcation, and has completed five years or has more than five years. A bhikṣu who possesses these five dharmas is permitted to live without dependence.

"Furthermore, bhikṣus have five right mindfulnesses. What are the five right mindfulnesses? Conduct, retribution, appropriate action, livelihood, and death: one does not engage in the worldly for the sake of material gain. Bhikṣus, these are the five right mindfulnesses.

"Furthermore, bhikṣus, there are separately five dependence-related right mindfulnesses. What are the five? Food and drink, ritual duties, not transgressing the teacher's boundary, dwelling together with the teacher, and being clear about the five-year period. These are the five dependence-related right mindfulnesses.

"Bhikṣus, regarding these five dependences, right mindfulnesses, retributions, and the like, one should know āpatti. What kinds of āpatti should one know? One should know the five āpatti categories. What are the five? Pārājika, saṃghāvaśeṣa, and pāyattika precepts: one should know that each has four modes of description. These are the five known categories of āpatti. Thus one knows āpatti.

"Furthermore, one should know non-āpatti. How does one know the characteristics of non-āpatti? One constantly practices non-āpatti. Thus one knows non-āpatti.

"Furthermore, one should know light āpatti. How does one know light āpatti? One practices with deep apprehension of light āpatti. What practice? Practicing the four pārājika dharmas is light āpatti; practicing the thirteen saṃghāvaśeṣa dharmas is light āpatti; practicing the thirty niḥsargika-pāyattika dharmas is light āpatti; practicing the ninety-two pāyattika dharmas is light āpatti; practicing the ninety-two pāyattika dharmas in purity, with each having four descriptions, is light āpatti; the remaining fifty precept-dharmas are light āpatti. Such is the practice of obtaining light āpatti. Thus one knows light āpatti.

"Next, one should know grave āpatti. How does one know grave āpatti? The practice one practices that yields grave āpatti: the remaining fifty precept-dharmas are grave āpatti with four descriptions each; the ninety-two pāyattika dharmas are grave āpatti through practice of purity. The ninety-two pāyattika dharmas being pure, the thirty niḥsargika-pāyattika dharmas are grave āpatti through practice of purity. The thirty niḥsargika-pāyattika dharmas being pure, the thirteen saṃghāvaśeṣa dharmas are grave āpatti through practice of purity. The thirteen saṃghāvaśeṣa dharmas being pure, the four pārājika dharmas are grave āpatti. Such is the practice of obtaining grave āpatti. Thus one knows grave āpatti. Whether five years, completing five years, or more than five years: thus one obtains the dependence of faith.

"Furthermore, one should know the prātimokṣa. How does one know the prātimokṣa? This is the prātimokṣa: reflecting, seeing before one the conduct of walking, standing, sitting, and lying down, the past and present actions of body, speech, and mind, and observing excellent conduct. Thus one knows the prātimokṣa.

"Next, one should know the recitation of the prātimokṣa. How does one know the recitation of the prātimokṣa? There are seven prātimokṣa recitations. What are the seven? How should one recite?

"Recite the four pārājika dharmas; the rest is as heard. This should be recited. This is the first recitation of the prātimokṣa.

"Next, recite the four pārājika dharmas and the thirteen saṃghāvaśeṣa dharmas; the rest is as heard. This should be recited. This is the second recitation of the prātimokṣa.

"Next, recite the four pārājika dharmas, the thirteen saṃghāvaśeṣa dharmas, and the thirty niḥsargika-pāyattika dharmas; the rest is as heard. This should be recited. This is the third recitation of the prātimokṣa.

"Next, recite the four pārājika dharmas, the thirteen saṃghāvaśeṣa dharmas, the thirty niḥsargika-pāyattika dharmas, and the ninety-two pāyattika dharmas in purity; the rest is as heard. This should be recited. This is the fourth recitation of the prātimokṣa.

"Next, recite the four pārājika dharmas, the thirteen saṃghāvaśeṣa dharmas, the thirty niḥsargika-pāyattika dharmas, the ninety-two pāyattika dharmas in purity, each with four explanations; the rest is as heard. This should be recited. This is the fifth recitation of the prātimokṣa.

"Next, recite the four pārājika dharmas, the thirteen saṃghāvaśeṣa dharmas, the thirty niḥsargika-pāyattika dharmas, the ninety-two pāyattika dharmas in purity, each with four descriptions, and the fifty precept-dharmas; the rest is as heard. This should be recited. This is the sixth recitation of the prātimokṣa.

"Next, recite each one in full detail. This is the seventh recitation of the prātimokṣa. Thus one recites the prātimokṣa and thus one knows the recitation of the prātimokṣa and knows the boundary-demarcation.

"How does one know the boundary-demarcation? The present assembly demarcates the boundary on the fourteenth or fifteenth day. Thus one knows the boundary-demarcation. Next, one should know the procedures of boundary-demarcation. How does one know the procedures? First, receiving and upholding; second, requesting the name; third, the invitation; fourth, inviting the five assemblies for boundary-demarcation. Thus one knows the procedures of boundary-demarcation. Whether five years, completing five years, or more than five years: thus one knows and obtains the dependence of mindfulness.

"Next, one should know the poṣadha. How does one know the poṣadha? The present assembly holds the poṣadha on the fourteenth or fifteenth day. Thus one knows the poṣadha. Next, one should know the procedures of the poṣadha. How does one know the procedures? First, receiving and upholding; second, requesting the name; third, the invitation; fourth, inviting the five assemblies for the poṣadha. Thus one knows the poṣadha. Next, one should know the boundary-demarcation. How does one know it? There are five kinds. Five kinds of boundary-assembly. Thus one knows the procedures of boundary-demarcation. How does one know the procedures? Niḥsargika items may not be used within the boundary. One should use a low, small seat. Food and drink should be prepared; after eating and drinking, what needs to be done should be done. Thus one knows the procedures of boundary-demarcation. Whether five years, completing five years, or more than five years: thus one obtains the dependence of mindfulness.

"Furthermore, one should know the practice of the Way. How does one practice the Way? This means the ācārya's practice of the Way, and one's own practice of the Way. Thus it is the dependence of mindfulness. Next, one should distinguish the practice of the Way. How does one distinguish? One knows the ācārya's recitation of the precepts and knows one's own recitation of the precepts. Thus one obtains the dependence of mindfulness.

"Furthermore, one should distinguish the passing of time. How does one know the passing of time? The ācārya's passing of time and one's own passing of time. Thus one obtains the dependence of mindfulness. Thus the passing of time is not for material gain. How is it not for material gain? The ācārya dwelling in the saṃghārāma is not there for material gain. Thus one obtains the dependence of mindfulness. Thus, not being for material gain, one practices the half-monthly observance. How does one practice the half-monthly observance? The ācārya, every half-month, joins in confession. Thus one obtains the dependence of mindfulness. Thus, practicing the half-monthly observance, one is without āpatti. How is one without āpatti? The ācārya directs one: 'Go to such-and-such a place and receive dependence.' Thus one obtains the dependence of mindfulness.

"Furthermore, the verbal practice. How does one practice verbally? According to the ācārya's words: 'I do not require your dependence-practice.' Thus one obtains the dependence of mindfulness. Thus, the dependence-practice does not transgress the teacher's boundary. How does one not transgress the teacher's boundary? One does not transgress the boundary of the teacher's village. Thus one obtains the dependence of mindfulness, and thus one does not transgress the boundary of the teacher's village.

"One confesses together with the teacher. How does one confess together with the teacher? Every half-month, one confesses together with the teacher. Thus one obtains the dependence of mindfulness. Thus, confessing together with the teacher, with the five-year period being clear, one obtains the dependence of mindfulness.

"Bhikṣus, one should fully possess these five kinds of dharmas. This is the dharma that bhikṣus should practice according to dependence. In that dharma there is no āpatti. With regard to all such precept-dharmas, one should daily obtain niṣkṛta."

At that time, when the World-Honored One had spoken these five kinds of dharmas, the assembly of bhikṣus and all devas and humans gazed up in reverence, circumambulated him, made obeisance, and withdrew.
