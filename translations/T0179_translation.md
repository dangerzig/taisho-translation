# 銀色女經
## Sūtra of the Silver-Colored Woman

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0179

Translated from the Chinese. Northern Wei dynasty, translated by Buddhaśānta (佛陀扇多)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Bhagavān was dwelling in the country of Śrāvastī, in the Jeta Grove, the Anāthapiṇḍada Monastery, together with a great assembly of one thousand two hundred and fifty bhikṣus.

At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus: "Bhikṣus! If there were sentient beings who could know the merit of generosity and the fruit and reward of giving as I know them, then at mealtimes, whether it be the first morsel or the last morsel, if they did not give in charity, they should not eat it themselves."

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke this verse:

> "If there are sentient beings,
> As the Buddha has taught,
> Who reduce their own portion and give it in charity,
> They shall achieve a great reward.
> Whether the first morsel or the last morsel,
> If it is not used for generosity,
> Then one should not eat it oneself."

At that time, the World-Honored One, having spoken this verse, told the bhikṣus: "Bhikṣus! Long ago in the past, through immeasurable kalpas, there was a royal capital whose king was named Padma. In that city there was a woman named Silver Color, who was exquisitely beautiful and of wondrous appearance, with all the marks complete, possessing the most supreme and excellent form.

"This Silver-Colored Woman, having business to attend to, left her own home and went to another's house. Upon entering, she saw the woman of that household, who had just given birth to a boy of fine and wondrous appearance, with excellent complexion. The new mother was holding the child in her hand and was about to eat him. Silver Color immediately asked: 'Sister, what are you doing?'

"She answered: 'I am extremely hungry and have no strength. I do not know what to eat, so I intend to eat my child.'

"Silver Color said: 'Sister, please stop! This must not be done. Sister! Is there truly nothing in this house that a person can eat?'

"She answered: 'Sister! I have long accumulated the stains of stinginess and miserliness. Therefore I now have nothing to eat.'

"Silver Color said: 'Sister, please stop for now. Wait while I go home and bring you food.'

"She replied: 'Sister! Both my sides are about to break open, my back is about to split, my heart trembles with unease, and everything around me is dark. The moment you leave this house, my life will end.'

"Silver Color then thought: 'If I take the child away, the woman will die. If I do not take the child, she will surely eat him. What means can I use to save both their lives?'

"She then said: 'Sister! Is there a sharp knife in this room? I need one.'

"She answered: 'There is.' She then took the knife and handed it to Silver Color.

"Silver Color took the knife and cut off both her own breasts. She gave them to the woman and said: 'Eat my breasts, and let your body be freed from the suffering of hunger and thirst.'

"The woman ate them. Silver Color then asked: 'Sister, are you full?'

"She answered: 'I am full.'

"Silver Color said: 'Sister, you should know that this child has been redeemed with the flesh of my own body. I entrust him to you for now. I must go home and bring food and drink.'

"Having said this, with blood streaming over her body and trailing on the ground, she departed.

"When she returned home, Silver Color's relatives and kin saw her and all asked: 'Who did this to you?'

"Silver Color answered: 'I did it to myself.'

"They asked: 'Why?'

"Silver Color answered: 'I have already set my mind upon the great compassion that I shall not abandon. I did this in order to attain the supreme, perfect enlightenment.'

"Her relatives all said: 'If one practices generosity but then regrets it, that may be called dāna, but it is not the pāramitā.'

"Having said this, they asked her: 'When you cut them off, were you joyful? Do not let the pain lead you to regret and sorrow.'

"Silver Color then made this solemn vow: 'In cutting off my two breasts, I did not generate a mind of regret, nor did my mind harbor any other thought. By the truth of this vow, may my two breasts be restored as before.' Having made this vow, her two breasts were instantly restored as before.

"At that time, the yakṣas throughout the city of Padma raised a great cry: 'Silver Color has given her own breasts!' The earth deities heard and proclaimed it again. The devas in the sky heard and passed it along. In this way, the proclamation was transmitted all the way up to the Brahma heaven.

"At that time, the Lord Śakra thought: 'This is an extraordinary event! This Silver-Colored Woman, out of compassion for sentient beings, has given her own breasts. I should now go and test her.' Having thought this, he transformed his body into that of a brāhmaṇa. In his left hand he held a golden water pitcher and a golden bowl. In his right hand he held a golden staff. He went to the royal city of Padma and gradually approached the dwelling of Silver Color. Standing outside the gate, he called out for alms.

"Silver Color, hearing the call for alms outside the gate, immediately prepared food in a vessel and brought it out. The brāhmaṇa said to her: 'Sister, please stop for now. I do not need food.'

"She asked: 'Why not?'

"The brāhmaṇa said: 'I am Śakra. I have come here because I have great doubt about you. Let me ask you, and you must answer me.'

"She said: 'Great brāhmaṇa! Please ask. Ask whatever you wish, and I shall answer. I shall surely satisfy the brāhmaṇa's mind.'

"The brāhmaṇa asked: 'Sister, did you truly cut off both your breasts and give them to another?'

"She answered: 'Truly so, great brāhmaṇa!'

"The brāhmaṇa said: 'Why did you do this?'

"Silver Color said: 'It was with the mind of great compassion, in order to attain the supreme, perfect enlightenment.'

"The brāhmaṇa said: 'This is an extremely difficult thing. What is most difficult is the supreme, perfect enlightenment. If after giving one generates a mind of regret, that may be called dāna, but it is not the pāramitā. When you gave, were you joyful? When you cut, did the pain cause you to think otherwise?'

"Silver Color answered: 'Kauśika! I now make this vow. With the mind that seeks all-knowledge, the mind that seeks to be supreme in all the world, and the mind that seeks to save all sentient beings, I cut off these breasts and truly did not generate regret. If I did not regret, may my woman's body be transformed into that of a man.' When Silver Color made this vow, she was instantly transformed into a man. Seeing that her female body had become male, her heart was filled with joy and leaping delight beyond measure. She went to another place, beneath a tree, and fell asleep.

"At that time, King Padma suddenly died. The king had no son. At that time, it was very hot. The ministers went from tree to tree, from village to village, from city to city, from capital to capital, searching everywhere for a person with the proper marks to be king. The ministers all said: 'How can we find a ruler who will govern according to the Dharma?'

"At that time, one of the chief ministers, overcome by the heat, entered a flower pond.

"There the minister saw a person of surpassing beauty beneath a tree, complete with all the marks, sleeping and unaware. Although the sun had moved, the shade of the tree did not leave that person. The minister snapped his fingers to wake him. Once awakened, he was brought to the royal palace, where his head was shaved, he was dressed in royal garments, and a jeweled crown was placed upon his head. They said: 'You must govern the affairs of the kingdom.'

"He answered: 'I truly cannot govern the affairs of the kingdom.'

"They said: 'Now you must govern the affairs of the kingdom.'

"He answered: 'If I am to be king, I shall govern the country according to the Dharma. If all of you accept and practice the ten wholesome deeds, then I shall be king.'

"They all answered: 'Your ministers shall comply.' They all immediately accepted the ten wholesome deeds.

"Having thus guided the people with the ten wholesome deeds, he governed the affairs of the kingdom. He was called King Silver Color. The lifespan of the people in that land was seventy thousand nayutas of years. That king governed for immeasurable hundreds and thousands of years, and then his life came to an end.

"At the moment of death, he spoke these words:

> "'All things are impermanent;
> They must be destroyed and perish.
> All who meet must part;
> All who live must die.
> According to the deeds one has done,
> Whether good or unwholesome,
> All who are born
> Shall not dwell long in life.'

"When the king died, he was reborn in that same place, the capital of King Padma. He took birth in the womb of an elder's wife. After eight or nine months, a boy was born, exquisitely beautiful, of every fine quality. After the boy was past eight years of age, five hundred boys accompanied him to the schoolhouse. At the schoolhouse, there were already five hundred boys studying.

"The boy asked those already there: 'What are you doing here?'

"They answered: 'We are studying writing.'

"He said: 'What benefit does studying writing bring? Why do you need to study this? You should instead generate the aspiration for the supreme, perfect enlightenment.'

"The other boys said: 'What does generating the aspiration for the supreme, perfect enlightenment involve?'

"The boy answered: 'One must practice the six pāramitās. What are the six? They are: the pāramitā of generosity, the pāramitā of morality, the pāramitā of patience, the pāramitā of diligence, the pāramitā of meditation, and the pāramitā of wisdom.'

"When they heard this, they all said: 'We generate the aspiration for the supreme, perfect enlightenment.'

"Having caused those people to generate the aspiration for the supreme, perfect enlightenment, the boy thought: 'I now wish to give even something small in charity. I shall now practice giving for the benefit of two-legged and four-legged creatures, birds, beasts, and deer.' Having thought this, he went to the charnel ground. With a sharp knife he pierced his body, letting blood flow, and smeared it all over himself. He then anointed himself with oil and lay down in the charnel ground, calling out: 'All two-legged and four-legged creatures, birds, beasts, and deer, near and far, who need food, come here and eat my body's flesh.'

"At that time, among the flocks of birds, one bird named Has-Hands came and sat upon his forehead. It plucked out his right eye, then put it back.

"He asked the bird: 'Why did you pluck out my right eye and then put it back?'

"The bird answered: 'Of all the flesh on the human body, there is nothing more delicious than the eyes.'

"He said to the bird: 'Even if you pluck out my right eye a thousand times and put it back each time, I shall not generate a mind of hatred.'

"The bird then ate both his eyes. Countless birds gathered in the charnel ground, and they all ate his flesh until only white bones remained.

"Having given up his body, he was immediately reborn in the capital of King Padma. He took birth in the womb of a brāhmaṇa's wife. After a full ten months, a boy was born, exquisitely beautiful, of the most supreme quality, with excellent form complete.

"After the age of twenty, his parents said to him: 'Young brāhmaṇa! You should build a house.'

"The boy said to his parents: 'What good would building a house do for me? My mind has no interest in houses. I beg you to let me enter the deep mountains.' His parents consented.

"He left his home and went to the mountain forest. When he arrived, he found that two brāhmaṇa sages had been dwelling there for a long time. The young brāhmaṇa went to the two sages and asked: 'What are you doing in this mountain forest, O brāhmaṇa sages?'

"The two sages answered: 'Young brāhmaṇa! We are here for the benefit of all sentient beings, practicing austerities and doing various deeds.'

"He said: 'I too have come here for the benefit of all sentient beings, wishing to practice austerities.' The young brāhmaṇa then went to another place in the forest and measured the ground to build a shelter.

"Through the power of his merit from cultivating wholesome deeds, he suddenly gained the divine eye. He then saw, not far from where he dwelt, a mother tigress about to give birth. The young brāhmaṇa saw this and thought: 'This tigress will give birth before long. After she gives birth, she may starve to death, or in extreme hunger suffer great anguish, or eat her own cubs.' Having thought this, he asked the two brāhmaṇa sages: 'Who is able to cut his own body and give it to this tigress?'

"They answered: 'We are not able to cut our own bodies and give them.' Having spoken thus, another seven days passed, and the tigress gave birth.

"After giving birth, the tigress held her cubs in her mouth, then placed them on the ground, then picked them up again. When the young brāhmaṇa saw this, he went to the two sages and said: 'Great sages! The tigress has given birth. If we are to practice austerities for the benefit of sentient beings, now is the time. You should cut your flesh and give it to this tigress.'

"The two brāhmaṇa sages then went to the tigress. They reflected: 'Who is able to endure such suffering and practice such great giving? Who is able to cut the flesh of his own beloved body and give it to this hungry tigress?' As they reflected, the mother tigress charged at them. The two sages, cherishing their lives, flew away into the sky.

"The young brāhmaṇa called out from afar to the two brāhmaṇa sages: 'Is this your vow?' Having spoken thus, he made this solemn vow: 'I now give up my body to save this starving tigress. May the cause and condition of this act surely bring me the supreme, perfect enlightenment.' Having made this vow, he found a sharp knife on the ground. He cut open his own body and gave it to the starving tigress.

"Bhikṣus! I say this lest doubt should arise in your minds. Bhikṣus! Do not harbor any other doubt or any other view. Why? You should know: the Silver-Colored Woman of that time in the capital of King Padma, who cut off her two breasts, is none other than my present self.

"Bhikṣus! Do not harbor any other doubt or any other view. Why? You should know: I am the Silver-Colored Woman of that time in the capital of King Padma.

"Bhikṣus! Do not harbor any other doubt or any other view. Why? You should know: I am the one named Silver Color, who gave her two breasts to save that child.

"Bhikṣus! Do not harbor any other doubt or any other view. Why? You should know: Rāhula is none other than the child of that time.

"Bhikṣus! Do not harbor any other doubt or any other view. Why? You should know: the one who in the charnel ground of the capital of King Padma gave his body to the flocks of birds is none other than my present self.

"Bhikṣus! Do not harbor any other doubt or any other view. Why? You should know: the two brāhmaṇa sages of that time are none other than you bhikṣus.

"Bhikṣus! Do not harbor any other doubt or any other view. Why? You should know: I am the young brāhmaṇa of that time.

"Bhikṣus! Therefore I now say to you: if you bhikṣus know the merit of generosity and the fruit of giving, you should give the first morsel or the last morsel, and in this way eat."

When the Buddha spoke this, all the bhikṣus were greatly joyful.
