# 七俱胝獨部法
## Independent Division Method of the Seven Koṭi [Buddhas]

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1079

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Śubhakarasiṃha (善無畏).

---

The mudrā that encompasses the great maṇḍala seals of all twenty-five divisions: cross the ring fingers and little fingers of both hands inward, extend both middle fingers upright touching at the tips, press both index fingers against the first joint of the middle fingers, and press both thumbs against the middle joints of the ring fingers of the left and right hands. If one wishes to summon, move the index fingers back and forth.

The Buddha said: "This spell and mudrā can extinguish the ten evil deeds, the five heinous crimes, and all grave offenses, and accomplish all white dharmas and merits. This practice does not distinguish between lay and ordained. Even if a lay person drinks wine, eats meat, and has a spouse, without concern for purity or impurity, as long as they practice according to my method, there will be nothing that is not accomplished."

First: the altar method. Second: the recitation method. Third: the method for verifying accomplishment. Fourth: the extensive explanation of sovereign mastery. Fifth: obtaining the great divine powers of a celestial being.

## First: The Altar Method

The Buddha said: "If one seeks accomplishment, first perform the altar method. Unlike the elaborate offerings of other divisions, dig the ground and construct an altar, smearing it with fragrant clay. Set up nothing more than a single new, clean mirror that has never been used. Before a buddha image, on the fifteenth night of the month, make offerings according to one's capacity. Burn olibanum incense and set out pure water. First quiet the mind, free from all thought. Then form the mudrā and recite the spell, empowering the mirror one hundred and eight times. Store the mirror in a pouch or case and always carry it with oneself. When one wishes to recite, simply place this mirror before oneself, form the mudrā, and recite the spell. Using the mirror as the altar, one attains accomplishment."

## Second: The Recitation Method

The Buddha said: "If one wishes to uphold this dharma, on the fifteenth night, bathe cleanly, put on fresh clean garments, face east, sit in half-lotus posture, place the mirror in front, and set out whatever incense, flowers, clean water, and other items one can provide. First quiet the mind and cease all thought. Then form the mudrā, press it upon the heart, and recite this spell one hundred and eight times. When upholding this spell, it can cause those of short lifespan to gain longevity. Even kāmala disease can be cured; how much more so other diseases. If they are not cured, that is impossible.

"On the first, eighth, fourteenth, fifteenth, eighteenth, twenty-third, twenty-fourth, twenty-eighth, twenty-ninth, and thirtieth of each month, these ten days, each morning at dawn, rinse the mouth with clean water, face east, and recite this spell one hundred and eight times, then eat. Even if one has a spouse and does not abstain from pungent foods or meat, one shall still attain verified results."

## Third: The Method for Verifying Accomplishment

"On the eighteenth of each month, use the mirror, form the mudrā, and practice recitation. On the ten observance days, there is no need to face the mirror or form the mudrā. Simply each morning, before having eaten pungent foods or meat, recite and uphold this spell one hundred and eight times. If this is continued without interruption for forty-nine days, whenever there are auspicious or inauspicious signs, changes of fortune, or calamities, Cundī Bodhisattva will cause two holy ones to follow that person. Whatever good or evil matters are in the mind, recite the spell seven times, wash the face, and sprinkle the body. In the mind, generate a thought of joy. This can cause kings, great ministers, and elders to give rise to a heart of respect. They will then be delighted, like a minister respecting a lord, like a father loving a child, and will comply with whatever one desires, even to the point of not begrudging life itself. All will be accomplished.

"If any being of short lifespan or chronic illness, on the fifteenth night of the month at dawn, burns olibanum incense and recites this mantra one thousand times: demons, ghosts, those who have lost their minds and run wildly, fox-spirits, and evil ghosts will all reveal their forms in the mirror. They may be killed or released as one wishes, and will never return. One's lifespan will be increased immeasurably.

"Furthermore, if a person has no merit or auspicious marks, seeks promotion without success, and is oppressed by poverty and suffering, by always reciting this spell on the ten observance days, one can obtain in this present life the position of a cakravartin king. Whatever official position one seeks shall be attained as desired.

"If a person is about to travel or undertake something, first contemplate the Holy One Cundī, fix the mind, and recite this spell a full seven times. If the matter will succeed, sit upright, and after a brief moment the body will naturally sway. From this one knows it will be successfully accomplished and auspicious. If the body remains stiff and moves forward or backward, one knows it will not succeed and there will certainly be a calamity.

"If one wishes to summon the four heavenly kings, Brahmā, Śakra, the twenty-eight celestial divisions, Pāpīyas, or others, face the mirror, form the mudrā, and move the index fingers back and forth. In a clean place, recite loudly twenty-one times. The noble, worthy, Yama-king, and ghost assemblies will assuredly come and not dare to hold back. Whatever one commands, all will be done as wished.

"If one wishes to know whether this practice has succeeded or not, recite the spell a full seven times according to the method. In dreams one will see buddhas and bodhisattvas, flowers and fruits, will vomit black matter and then eat white matter. From this one knows that accomplishment has been attained."

## Fourth: Extensive Explanation of Sovereign Mastery

The Buddha said: "This dhāraṇī has great power. It can move Mount Sumeru and the great ocean waters. If the spell is recited upon withered trees, they can produce flowers and fruit. If one always recites and upholds it, water cannot drown, fire cannot burn, and poisons, weapons, enemies, and diseases cannot cause harm.

"If one knows there are buried treasures underground, form the mudrā and recite the spell a full seven times. The underground treasures will naturally surge forth, and one will be satisfied in all one's desires. In battle formations and against bandit armies, form the mudrā and point at them, and wherever one points they will all retreat and scatter.

"If someone has been suddenly killed by ghosts or spirits, form the mudrā and recite the spell seven times, pressing the mudrā-seal upon the heart. The suddenly dead or gravely ill person will immediately be restored to life. If a dwelling is beset by misfortune from ghosts and spirits, empower four blocks of earth with the spell and place them as anchors. The spirits will depart.

"Furthermore, if a person's six kinds of relatives are in discord and people do not love or care for one another, recite the spell according to the method. Wherever one goes, harmony will prevail. Upon hearing one's name or seeing one's person, all will give rise to delight and will not wish to be apart.

"In the midst of a raging fire, point with the mudrā, and the fire will be extinguished. Point with the mudrā at water, and the waves will all become still.

"Whatever one wishes, there is nothing that will not be fulfilled. How much more so for one who also forms the mudrā, observes the ritual fast, maintains complete precepts, and practices recitation according to the method: without leaving this fleshly body, one shall proceed to the pure lands of the four directions."

## Fifth: Obtaining Great Divine Powers

"If one seeks longevity and wishes to command the immortals and obtain their medicines, in an ancient stūpa, in the deep mountains, or in a pure room, using the mirror as an altar, practice for a full seven days or twenty-one days. Burn blue lotus flowers mixed with olibanum incense. During the first watch of the night, recite the spell one hundred and eight times, then sleep. In one's dreams one may eat celestial medicines or receive formulas and methods. Or before the mirror five-colored light will appear, and within the light there will be medicine. Eat it as one wishes, and one shall attain the way of the immortals. The remaining powers are hundreds of thousands of millions of billions, and cannot be fully described."

## Separate Cundī Methods

First, with the left hand, fold the index finger, ring finger, and little finger into the palm, and press the thumb upon the nails of the index, ring, and little fingers. Do the same with the right hand. Join the tips of the two middle fingers. This is the mudrā for recitation.

For the boundary-sealing mudrā: cross the middle fingers and ring fingers of both hands inward, extend both little fingers upright, touch both index fingers together, and press both thumbs against the middle joints of the index fingers. Recite the spell.

For the body-protection mudrā: join both palms, cross both index fingers outward, press both thumbs together upon the middle joints of the index fingers, and keep the middle finger and the two fingers below it extended and joined. If one encounters bandits or thieves, raise the mudrā to the crown of the head, then press it upon the left shoulder, then the right, then before the heart, then below the head, then between the eyebrows. They will not dare to intrude.

For the healing mudrā: cross both index fingers and both middle fingers inward, and join both ring fingers and both little fingers touching outward in a palm-joining position, with both thumbs pressing the middle joints of the index fingers.

For the encompassing mudrā: cross the little fingers and ring fingers of both hands into the palms, extend both middle fingers upright with tips touching, spread both index fingers slightly bent, and press both thumbs upon the ring fingers. When summoning, move the index fingers back and forth. It resembles a lotus-platform seat. Recite the spell seven times, then use the mudrā to press upon both shoulders, below the throat, between the eyebrows, and at the hairline. Then hold this mudrā at the heart and recite the spell one hundred and eight times.

For the māra-destroying mudrā: with both hands, fold all four fingers over the thumbs into the palms and clench the fists tightly, then thrust them forward. Wherever there is a frightening place, or where one suspects demons, poisonous nāgas, or the like, form this mudrā and recite the spell in a wrathful voice.

For the ghost-and-spirit-summoning mudrā: with the right hand, press the middle finger and thumb together against the altar. Extend all the remaining fingers like fangs, and move the index and little fingers back and forth. Recite the spell seven times. By the third recitation, all ghosts and spirits will come.
