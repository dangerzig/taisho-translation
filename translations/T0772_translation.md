# 大乘四法經
## Mahāyāna Sūtra on the Four Dharmas

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 772

Translated from the Chinese. Tang Dynasty, translated by Divākara

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Bhagavān was dwelling at Śrāvastī in the Jetavana, the garden of Anāthapiṇḍada, together with a great assembly of bhikṣus numbering one thousand two hundred and fifty, and also an immeasurable assembly of bodhisattva-mahāsattvas.

At that time the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣus: "There are four dharmas which bodhisattva-mahāsattvas should constantly practice throughout their entire lives, and which must not be abandoned even should circumstances arise that would cost them their lives. What are these four? Bhikṣus, bodhisattvas must never, throughout their lives and even should they encounter circumstances that would cost them their lives, abandon the mind of enlightenment (*bodhicitta*). Bhikṣus, bodhisattvas must never, throughout their lives and even should they encounter circumstances that would cost them their lives, abandon a good spiritual friend (*kalyāṇamitra*). Bhikṣus, bodhisattvas must never, throughout their lives and even should they encounter circumstances that would cost them their lives, abandon forbearance and joyful acceptance (*kṣānti*). Bhikṣus, bodhisattvas must never, throughout their lives and even should they encounter circumstances that would cost them their lives, abandon the forest hermitage (*araṇya*). Bhikṣus, these four dharmas are what bodhisattvas should firmly uphold throughout their entire lives; they would sooner lose their bodies and lives than abandon them."

At that time the World-Honored One, restating this meaning, spoke a *gāthā*:

> Those who are wise and discerning in the world
> should arouse the mind of enlightenment,
> constantly contemplate omniscience,
> and always keep close to good spiritual friends.
> Dwell in forbearance and joyful acceptance,
> rely upon the forest hermitage,
> and, like the lion king,
> be far removed from all fear and terror.

"Those who are wise and discerning and who practice these dharmas will swiftly transcend the nets of all the māras, and quickly realize unsurpassed, perfect, complete enlightenment (*anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi*)."

When the Bhagavān had finished speaking this sūtra, the bhikṣus and the rest of the assembly rejoiced and faithfully upheld it.
