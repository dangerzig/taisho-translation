# 佛說壞相金剛陀羅尼經
## Sūtra on the Appearance-Destroying Vajra Dhāraṇī Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1417

Translated from the Chinese. Yuan dynasty, translated by Śārabha (沙囉巴).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Bhagavat was at the vajra-site. At that time, through the Tathāgata's spiritual power, Vajrapāṇi's body was made into vajra, and that very day he entered vajra-samādhi. Then Vajrapāṇi, empowered by the Buddha's spiritual force and the blessings of all tathāgatas and bodhisattvas, proclaimed in vajra-wrath this most wondrous vajra-heart dhāraṇī, which is unbroken and indestructible, true and adamantine, without any obstruction and without timidity. It causes all sentient beings to tremble in awe. It can crush the afflictions of sentient beings, sever all spells and suppress all spells, crush all practices and defeat others' practices, crush all māras and release those seized by māras, and gather in all classes of bhūtas. Furthermore, it enables all who practice vidyā-mantras: those who have not yet achieved accomplishment to achieve it, and those who have already achieved it to not lose their attainment; whatever they wish will be fulfilled. It can also protect all sentient beings, pacify disasters, and bring increase. It can also restrain all deluded and confused evil beings. All this is through the spiritual power of this dhāraṇī mantra. Vajrapāṇi, empowered by the Buddha, then proclaimed the mantra:

*namo ratna-trayāya namaś caṇḍa-vajra-pāṇaye mahā-yakṣa-senāpataye tadyathā oṃ traṭa traṭa [tu]rāṭaya [tu]rāṭaya sphuṭa sphuṭa sphaṭaya sphaṭaya gṛhṇa gṛhṇa gṛhṇāpaya gṛhṇāpaya sarva-sattvāni bandha bandha saṃbandhaya saṃbandhaya pramatha pramatha saṃpramāthaya saṃpramāthaya sarva-bhūtāni kuṭa kuṭa saṃkuṭaya saṃkuṭaya sarva-śatrūṇāṃ kaṭa kaṭa saṃkaṭaya saṃkaṭaya sarva-vidyā vajra vajra sphuṭa vajra vajra kaṭa vajra vajra maṭa vajra vajra matha vajra vajra aṭṭahāsa-nira vajra su-vajrāya svāhā he bhuru niru bhuru kṛṇa kuru [mīr]curu kuru kuru vajra-vijayāya svāhā kīlikīlāya svāhā kaṭa kaṭa maṭa maṭa raṭa raṭa maṭhana-pramaṭhanāya svāhā caraṇi cara hara hara sara sara māraya vajra-vidhāraṇāya svāhā cinta cinta pinta pinta mahā-kīlikīlāya svāhā bandha bandha [kara]ṭa [kara]ṭa kīlikīlāya svāhā curu curu caṇḍa-kīlikīlāya svāhā trāsaya trāsaya vajra-kīlikīlāya svāhā hara hara vajra-tarāya svāhā prahara prahara vajra-prabañjanāya svāhā mati-sthira-vajra su-sthira-vajra prati-sthira-vajra mahā-vajra apratihata-vajra amogha-vajra ihi-vajra [śa]kalā-vajrāya svāhā tara tara tiri tiri ṭuru ṭuru sarva-vajra-kulam āvartaya svāhā amogha māraya phaṭ namaḥ samanta-vajrāṇāṃ sarva-balāvartaya mahā-vajra-mahā-[bale]-vikaraṇa-[acite] cara cara tiri tiri [diṃ]-kare daha daha [ti]capati tiri tiri bandha bandha mahā-[bale] vajrāṅga-carāya svāhā namo ratna-trayāya namaś caṇḍa-vajra-pāṇaye mahā-yakṣa-senāpataye tadyathā oṃ hara hara vajra matha matha vajra ṭuṇa ṭuṇa vajra daha daha vajra paca paca vajra tara tara vajra tāraya tāraya vajra tāruṇa tāruṇa vajra cinta cinta vajra pinta pinta vajra hūṃ phaṭ namaś caṇḍa-vajra-krodhāya huru huru tiṣṭha tiṣṭha bandha bandha hana hana amṛte hūṃ phaṭ*

Translator's note: This dhāraṇī is proclaimed by Vajrapāṇi as great general of the yakṣas. Identifiable Sanskrit terms include: homage to the Three Jewels (ratna-traya), Caṇḍa Vajrapāṇi, ritual commands such as sphuṭa/sphaṭaya (shatter), gṛhṇa (seize), bandha (bind), pramatha (crush), māraya (destroy), hara (seize), trāsaya (terrify), prahara (strike), daha (burn), paca (cook), hana (slay), tāraya (save/deliver), descriptions of vajra qualities such as mati-sthira (firm-minded), su-sthira (well-established), apratihata (unobstructed), amogha (infallible), and the closing syllables hūṃ phaṭ with amṛte (immortal).

> All karmic transgressions are purified,
> And all suffering entirely removed.
> It is the root of all sūtras,
> Adorned with wondrous virtuous marks.
> Beings whose faculties are impaired,
> Whose lives end or who die untimely,
> Who encounter scarcity,
> Or who are forsaken by the devas,
> Friends and kin who turn to hatred,
> Those who offend brahmans,
> Disturbance and disharmony,
> Poverty and vexation,
> Grief, sorrow, suffering, and affliction,
> Encountering hardship, want, and distress,
> Evil constellations and curses,
> Or being harmed by māras,
> Anxiety from pregnancy,
> Or seeing inauspicious dreams:
> By bathing, all is made pure;
> One should hear this most excellent sūtra.
> If one has a pure and virtuous mind,
> Wearing clean and spotless garments,
> Following the Buddha's profound practice,
> And receives and upholds this sūtra,
> By this majestic spiritual power,
> For all those sentient beings,
> Whatever illness and suffering they have
> Will be entirely eliminated.
> Blessings and longevity will all increase,
> Far removed from all transgression and defilement.
> With pearls, mustard seeds, fragrant vines,
> Flawless gems and sandalwood,
> Jade, vajra pestles, and flowers,
> Pure water in golden vessels,
> Or vessels made of silver,
> Tied with pure silk and filled to the brim,
> Reciting twenty-one times,
> Or a full one hundred and eight times,
> The Appearance-Destroying Vajra Mantra:
> The king should regularly perform ablutions.
