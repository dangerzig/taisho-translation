# 何耶揭唎婆觀世音菩薩受法壇
## Altar for Receiving the Dharma of Hayagrīva Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1074

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown.

---

If any śramaṇas or brāhmaṇas, good men or good women, wish to receive and uphold the dharma of this bodhisattva, they should construct an altar of four cubits. One should seek a superior and pure location, sweep and sprinkle it clean, smear the ground with fragrant water and cow dung, and hang various multicolored banners, canopies, jeweled bells, pendant mirrors, and gold and silver ornaments of every variety to adorn the sanctuary. In the center of the sanctuary, erect a five-colored altar, four cubits square. First lay down white, then yellow, then red, then black, and make four gates. At the center of the altar, make a lotus seat and install an image of Hayagrīva Avalokiteśvara. At the eastern gate, make a flower-seat and install an image of the Eleven-Faced Bodhisattva. At the northern gate, make a lotus seat and install the Eight-Armed Avalokiteśvara. At the southern side, install the eight nāga kings: Nanda Nāga King, Upananda Nāga King, Takṣaka Nāga King, Karkoṭaka Nāga King, Padma Nāga King, Mahāpadma Nāga King, Śaṅkhapāla Nāga King, and Kulika Nāga King. Light forty-five lamps. First invoke the eight nāga kings:

*Oṃ jayā vijayā aṣṭa-nāga-rājayā āra-caṃdu svāhā*

At the western gate of the altar, near the south, install a fire pit. Next invite the Hayagrīva Bodhisattva at the center, then invite the Eight-Armed Avalokiteśvara:

*Oṃ yaśe yā cchetāṃ svāhā*

Next use the flower-seat mudrā for coming and going. Having invited the Eleven-Faced Bodhisattva, scatter various flowers, burn all manner of fine incenses, including aloeswood, frankincense, and sandalwood, and make offerings. The spell-master should fast for one day. If one cannot endure the hunger, one may eat only ghee. Bathe in fragrant water, put on fresh clean garments, enter the sanctuary, and make offerings. One will always be kept in the thoughts of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva. One may also combine this with the Kuṇḍalī boundary-sealing method for offerings. Use the mantra-mudrā to protect the body and seal the boundary. Empower a piece of wood seven times, or empower water seven times. Also use the mudrā to empower water, or empower white mustard seed and ash seven times. Plant the wood upright at the four corners to form the boundary, and scatter the mustard seed, ash, and water in the ten directions to complete the boundary-sealing. Clasp both hands in the outer bond, extend both index fingers upright with about five fen between them, join both thumbs touching each other, each bent at one joint without touching the index finger. The mantra says:

*Oṃ praveśa-siddha vajra samprāraṇa svāhā*

The sword-unbinding mudrā of Hayagrīva:

*Oṃ āsmaṃgīni svāhā*

The great dharma-body mudrā of Hayagrīva:

*Oṃ dhuna dhuna mātha mātha khadha khadha hayagrīva hūṃ phaṭ svāhā*

Next, the heart mudrā of Hayagrīva:

*Oṃ amṛtodbhava hūṃ phaṭ svāhā*

Next, the head mudrā:

*Tadyathā cakra-bhū cakra-kṣa-bhū cakra-padmākṣarī-bhū amogha-śaśi-rā-kīrā-pra-śa-vaṅga-ca-tu svāhā*

Next, the crown mudrā:

*Oṃ śrī rāri ji hūṃ phaṭ svāhā*

Next, the mouth mudrā:

*Oṃ huru-rū kuru dhāna hūṃ phaṭ svāhā*

Next, the tusk mudrā:

*Namo ratna-trayāya namaḥ ārya-avalokiteśvarāya bodhisattvāya mahāsattvāya mahā-kāruṇikāya tadyathā tarāṭa tarāṭa maṭa maṭa chinda chinda bhinda bhinda hūṃ phaṭ phaṭ svāhā*

The great spell of Hayagrīva Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva and the separate great spell of Hayagrīva: these two spells are pratiṣṭhā-siddha-pāpānti, meaning that even without receiving the altar offerings according to the full method, reciting them will achieve efficacy. If one wishes to dismiss Hayagrīva Avalokiteśvara, hold a white flower and empower it with one recitation of the spell, then strike the image once. Do this seven times and the deity will depart. The mantra says:

*Tadyathā sarva-pra-sara jayā vijayā vajra-kula saṃbhāva-saupadana-kaca-muktu-ki svāhā*
