# 佛說呪目經
## Sūtra Spoken by the Buddha: The Spell for the Eyes

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1328

Translated from the Chinese. Eastern Jin dynasty, translated by Tan Wulan (曇無蘭)

---

*Dhāraṇī:*

tuī-phu pan-phu aṭa-pan-phu kūrī-phu kūrī-bī-phu kūra-kū-bī-phu śarī svā-bō-dī-phu irī-phu irā-yī-phu irabya-phu

If the eyes are painful, recite [this incantation] seven times and they will immediately be healed.

---

**Translator's note:** The dhāraṇī represents a Chinese phonetic transcription of what was likely a Prakrit or Sanskrit incantation; the syllables above are romanized from their Middle Chinese pronunciations and cannot be fully reconstructed to an original Indic form. The character 𧁢 (appearing twice) is a rare sinograph used in phonetic transcription whose precise value remains uncertain. The text's extreme brevity (55 characters total) is consistent with apotropaic dhāraṇī collections of the Eastern Jin period.
