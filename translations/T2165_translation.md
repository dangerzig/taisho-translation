Regarding the aforementioned Dharma-gates: I, Ennin, arrived at the Yangzhou Metropolitan Prefecture of Great Tang in the eighth month of the fifth year of Jōwa. I traveled through the various monasteries within the city and copied [the texts] as recorded above.

There I encountered the Venerable Zongru of Zhongnan Mountain, a man of learning that surpassed previous masters, whose awakening had penetrated into the profound subtleties [of the Dharma], who was able to understand both Sanskrit and Chinese, and who was exquisitely versed in the sounds of the Siddham script. As he was on the verge of setting out for the Western Regions [India] and taking leave of [China], I was fortunate to encounter him by chance. I received his instruction in Sanskrit Siddham and also studied both Sanskrit and Chinese.

I further met Quanya Ācārya, a dharma-transmission disciple of Bianhong Ācārya, Imperial Chaplain of Great Tang. I sought and received the secret Dharma from him. The Venerable [Bianhong] was moved by my sincere determination from afar and entrusted me with the secret essentials, thereupon transmitting and conferring the methods of recitation and practice, together with the altar designs for the maṇḍalas of the Garbhadhātu and Vajradhātu divisions, and so forth.

Afterward, I intended to inquire into the Tiantai teachings, but the roads were long and distant, and the journey there and back would have exceeded the allotted time. Moreover, an imperial edict did not permit me to set out and proceed. My grief and sorrow were beyond expression. Although the Dharma-gates I sought have not been fully assembled, I have for now recorded the scrolls and their cases as verified and determined above.

The twentieth day of the fourth month, in the year *jǐwèi*, the fourth year of Kaicheng of Great Tang [839 CE].

Recorded by Ennin, holding the rank of Dharma-lamp Transmission Priest Seeking Instruction of the Tiantai School.

**Translator's note:** The character 漆 in "一百三十漆部" (137 fascicles) is a scribal variant for 七 (seven). The group subtotals account for 135 fascicles; the discrepancy likely reflects that individual text titles within each group are not reproduced in this edition of the catalog. The one entry using 帖 (booklet-folder) rather than 帙 (scroll-case) reflects a distinct physical format.
