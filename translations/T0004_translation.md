# 七佛父母姓字經
## Sūtra on the Clans and Names of the Seven Buddhas' Parents

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0004

Translator unknown

---

## Fascicle 1

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the country of Śrāvastī. In that country there was a woman whose son was named Wuyan, and she was accordingly called the Mother of Wuyan. The Buddha, accompanied by five hundred bhikṣus, went to the home of the Mother of Wuyan, where they sat in the hall and took their meal. When the meal was finished, several tens of bhikṣus gathered in a secluded place and spoke among themselves: "The Buddha is the divine one we revere most, of boundless spiritual power. The Buddha freely transforms according to his will; what can he not accomplish? He knows the past Buddhas and the Buddhas yet to come: their ages and lifespans, their parents' clan and personal names, the number of their disciples, and the teachings and intentions they promulgated."

The Buddha heard the bhikṣus speaking of these matters with his divine ear. He then went to where the bhikṣus were and asked them: "Just now, what were you all discussing together?" The bhikṣus replied: "We were reflecting that the Buddha's divine path and virtue are wondrous and far-reaching in what he knows, and none can surpass the Buddha. The Buddha knows the past Buddhas and the Buddhas yet to come: their ages and lifespans, their parents' clan and personal names, the number of their disciples, and the teachings and intentions they promulgated." The Buddha said: "Excellent! Excellent! It is so. You who practice the śramaṇa way should simply keep these wholesome reflections in mind." The Buddha said: "Do you wish to hear the clan and personal names of the past Buddhas, their fathers and mothers, and their various disciples?" The bhikṣus replied: "We wish to hear them."

The Buddha said: "All of you, listen! The first Buddha was named Vipaśyin; ninety-one kalpas have passed since his parinirvāṇa. The second Buddha was named Śikhin; thirty-one kalpas have passed since his parinirvāṇa. The third Buddha was named Viśvabhū; likewise thirty-one kalpas have passed since his parinirvāṇa. In this Bhadra kalpa, there will be one thousand Buddhas. The first is Krakucchanda Buddha, the second is Kanakamuni Buddha, the third is Kāśyapa Buddha, and the fourth is I myself, Śākyamuni Buddha.

"Vipaśyin Buddha's clan was Kauṇḍinya; Śikhin Buddha's clan was likewise Kauṇḍinya; Viśvabhū Buddha's clan was likewise Kauṇḍinya. Krakucchanda Buddha's clan was Kāśyapa; Kanakamuni Buddha's clan was likewise Kāśyapa; Kāśyapa Buddha's clan was likewise Kāśyapa. I, who have become Śākyamuni Buddha, am of the Gautama clan.

"Vipaśyin Buddha was of the kṣatriya lineage; Śikhin Buddha was likewise of the kṣatriya lineage; Viśvabhū Buddha was likewise of the kṣatriya lineage. Krakucchanda Buddha was of the brāhmaṇa lineage; Kanakamuni Buddha was likewise of the brāhmaṇa lineage; Kāśyapa Buddha was likewise of the brāhmaṇa lineage. I, who have become Śākyamuni Buddha, am of the kṣatriya lineage.

"Vipaśyin Buddha's father was named Bandhumat, a kṣatriya king; his mother was named Bandhumatī; the country he ruled was named Kṣemāvatī. Śikhin Buddha's father was named Aruṇa, a kṣatriya king; his mother was named Prabhāvatī; the country he ruled was named Aruṇavatī. Viśvabhū Buddha's father was named Supratīta, a kṣatriya king; his mother was named Yaśavatī; the country he ruled was named Anupamā. Krakucchanda Buddha's father was named Agnidatta, of the brāhmaṇa lineage; his mother was named Viśākhā; the country in which he dwelt was named Kṣemāvatī, whose king was named Kṣema. Kanakamuni Buddha's father was named Yajñadatta, of the brāhmaṇa lineage; his mother was named Uttarā; the country in which he dwelt was named Śobhavatī, whose king was named Soma. Kāśyapa Buddha's father was named Brahmadatta, of the brāhmaṇa lineage; his mother was named Dhanavatī; the country in which he dwelt was named Vārāṇasī, whose king was named Kṛkin. I, who have become Śākyamuni Buddha, have a father named Śuddhodana, a kṣatriya king; my mother is named Mahāmāyā; the country I rule is named Kapilavastu, whose former great king was named Bhadra.

"Vipaśyin Buddha lived in the world for eighty thousand years; Śikhin Buddha lived in the world for seventy thousand years; Viśvabhū Buddha lived in the world for sixty thousand years; Krakucchanda Buddha lived in the world for forty thousand years; Kanakamuni Buddha lived in the world for thirty thousand years; Kāśyapa Buddha lived in the world for twenty thousand years. I, who have become Śākyamuni Buddha, have a lifespan of barely one hundred years, whether somewhat more or somewhat less.

"Vipaśyin Buddha's son was named Samavattakkhandha; Śikhin Buddha's son was named Atula; Viśvabhū Buddha's son was named Suprabhā; Krakucchanda Buddha's son was named Uttara; Kanakamuni Buddha's son was named Uttarasena; Kāśyapa Buddha's son was named Sārthavāha. I, who have become Śākyamuni Buddha, have a son named Rāhula.

"When Vipaśyin Buddha attained enlightenment and became a Buddha, it was beneath the Pāṭalī tree. When Śikhin Buddha attained enlightenment and became a Buddha, it was beneath the Puṇḍarīka tree. When Viśvabhū Buddha attained enlightenment and became a Buddha, it was beneath the Śāla tree. When Krakucchanda Buddha attained enlightenment and became a Buddha, it was beneath the Śirīṣa tree. When Kanakamuni Buddha attained enlightenment and became a Buddha, it was beneath the Udumbara tree. When Kāśyapa Buddha attained enlightenment and became a Buddha, it was beneath the Nyagrodha tree. When I became Śākyamuni Buddha, it was beneath the Aśvattha tree.

"The attendant of Vipaśyin Buddha was named Aśoka; the attendant of Śikhin Buddha was named Kṣemaṅkara; the attendant of Viśvabhū Buddha was named Upaśānta; the attendant of Krakucchanda Buddha was named Buddhija; the attendant of Kanakamuni Buddha was named Sāgata; the attendant of Kāśyapa Buddha was named Sarvamitta. I, who have become Śākyamuni Buddha, have as my attendant Ānanda.

"Vipaśyin Buddha's first disciple was named Khaṇḍa, and his second disciple was named Tissa. Śikhin Buddha's first disciple was named Abhibhū, and his second disciple was named Sambhava. Viśvabhū Buddha's first disciple was named Soṇa, and his second disciple was named Uttara. Krakucchanda Buddha's first disciple was named Sañjīva, and his second disciple was named Vidhura. Kanakamuni Buddha's first disciple was named Bhadravarman, and his second disciple was named Uttara. Kāśyapa Buddha's first disciple was named Tissabrahmā, and his second disciple was named Bharadvāja. I, who have become Śākyamuni Buddha, have as my first disciple Śāriputra, and my second disciple Mahāmaudgalyāyana.

"Vipaśyin Buddha held three assemblies, expounding sūtras for the bhikṣus. At the first assembly, one hundred thousand bhikṣus all attained arhatship; at the second assembly, ninety thousand bhikṣus all attained arhatship; at the third assembly, eighty thousand bhikṣus all attained arhatship. Śikhin Buddha also held three assemblies, expounding sūtras. At the first assembly, ninety thousand bhikṣus all attained arhatship; at the second assembly, eighty thousand bhikṣus all attained arhatship; at the third assembly, seventy thousand bhikṣus all attained arhatship. Viśvabhū Buddha held two assemblies, expounding sūtras. At the first assembly, seventy thousand bhikṣus all attained arhatship; at the second assembly, sixty thousand bhikṣus all attained arhatship. Krakucchanda Buddha held one assembly, expounding sūtras, and forty thousand bhikṣus all attained arhatship. Kanakamuni Buddha held one assembly, expounding sūtras, and thirty thousand bhikṣus all attained arhatship. Kāśyapa Buddha held one assembly, expounding sūtras, and twenty thousand bhikṣus all attained arhatship. I, who have become Śākyamuni Buddha, hold one assembly, expounding sūtras, and one thousand two hundred and fifty bhikṣus all attain arhatship."

The Buddha addressed the bhikṣus: "The wisdom of the Buddha is beyond all reckoning and beyond all measure. He is able to know the seven Buddhas' ancestral origins, their fathers and mothers, the kings of their lands, and the teachings they promulgated."

The Buddha addressed the bhikṣus: "The sūtras must not go unstudied; the path must not go unpracticed. The Buddha may be likened to the helmsman of a great ship upon the ocean: many thousands and tens of thousands of people all rely upon him and are able to cross the sea. The Buddha's teaching causes all under heaven to practice goodness and attain the path to transcend the world; it is just so."

The bhikṣus, having heard the sūtra, rejoiced, made obeisance, and withdrew.
