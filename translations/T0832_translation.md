# 佛語經
## Sūtra of the Buddha's Words

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0832

Translated from the Chinese. Northern Wei dynasty, translated by Bodhiruci (菩提流支)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Bhagavān was in the city of Vaiśālī, in the great forest, in the upper story of the peaked hall, together with a great assembly of eight thousand bhikṣus, eighty-four thousand great bodhisattvas, and further surrounded by a countless multitude of learners and non-learners, to whom he was teaching the Dharma.

At that time in the assembly there was a bodhisattva named Noble Dragon-Majesty King, who rose from his seat, adjusted his robe over his right shoulder, placed his right knee on the ground, joined his palms toward the Buddha, and said: "World-Honored One! The Tathāgata has previously taught, among the sūtras concerning the Buddha's words, that there are also words that are not the Buddha's words. World-Honored One! What is the meaning of this? How should we receive and uphold it?"

At that time the Buddha told the Bodhisattva Noble Dragon-Majesty King: "Good man! As you have asked, regarding the statement in the sūtras that there are the Buddha's words and what is not the Buddha's words: good man, that which is non-speech is itself the Buddha's words. Good man, reflect well upon this! I shall now explain it for you."

The Bodhisattva Noble Dragon-Majesty King then addressed the Buddha: "Excellent, World-Honored One! I am glad to hear it."

The Buddha said: "Good man! What is called 'non-speech' is itself the Buddha's words. Good man! What is called 'the Buddha's words' is then called the most weighty bodily karma. What I teach is entirely without any bodily karma, verbal karma, or mental karma that is not beneficial. Furthermore, there is no speech, no one who can speak, and no one who utters words.

"Good man! Whatever speech pertains to form is not the Buddha's words. Noble Dragon-Majesty King! If form, which is non-speech, is not the Buddha's words, then feeling, perception, formations, and consciousness, which are also non-speech, are likewise not the Buddha's words. Good man! If there is no speech of form, no speech of feeling, perception, formations, or consciousness, this is called the Buddha's words.

"Good man! If there is speech of bodily, verbal, and mental karma, it is not the Buddha's words. Good man! If there is no speech of bodily, verbal, and mental karma, this is the Buddha's words. Good man! If there is speech of earth, water, fire, wind, and space, this is not the Buddha's words. Good man! If there is no speaking of earth, water, fire, wind, and space, this is the Buddha's words. Good man! If there is speech of greed, anger, and delusion, it is not the Buddha's words. Good man! If there is no speech of greed, anger, and delusion, this is the Buddha's words. Good man! If there is speech of the tainted and the untainted, it is not the Buddha's words. Good man! If it is neither tainted speech nor untainted speech, this is the Buddha's words. Good man! If there is wishful speech, such speech is not the Buddha's words, because the Buddha's words do not seek or wish for anything. Good man! If there is neither high nor low, such speech is the Buddha's words. Good man! If there is speech of things and speech of non-things, it is not the Buddha's words. Good man! If there is speech that is neither of things nor of non-things, this is the Buddha's words. Good man! If regarding the dharma of intrinsic purity one speaks of attaining realization, that is not the Buddha's words. Good man! If it is speech that is neither of intrinsic nature nor of other-nature, this is the Buddha's words. Good man! If there is true speech and untrue speech, it is not the Buddha's words. Good man! If there is neither true speech nor untrue speech, this is the Buddha's words. Good man! If one says 'this speech is spoken by ordinary people' and 'this speech is spoken by the noble,' it is not the Buddha's words. Good man! If there is neither ordinary speech nor noble speech, this is the Buddha's words. Good man! If there is inner speech, outer speech, and inner-outer speech, it is not the Buddha's words. Good man! If there is no inner speech, no outer speech, and no inner-outer speech, this is the Buddha's words. Good man! If regarding dharmas there is speech that depends on form, or speech that depends on feeling, perception, formations, and consciousness, it is not the Buddha's words. Good man! If regarding dharmas there is no form upon which to depend, and likewise no feeling, perception, formations, or consciousness upon which to depend, such speech is the Buddha's words. Good man! If there is speech of any place, it is the speech of the king of māras and the speech of māra's subjects, and is not the Buddha's words. Good man! If there is no speech of any place at all, this is the Buddha's words. Good man! If there is speech that arises from the discernment and discrimination of form, or speech that arises from the discernment and discrimination of feeling, perception, formations, and consciousness, it is not the Buddha's words. Good man! If there is speech without the discernment and discrimination of form, and without the discernment and discrimination of feeling, perception, formations, and consciousness, this is the Buddha's words. For this reason, Māra and his subjects cannot find any opportunity.

"Furthermore, good man! As for the term 'bodhisattva': if form is without self and one does not discriminate that it is not 'mine,' and if likewise feeling, perception, formations, and consciousness are without self and one does not discriminate that they are not 'mine,' such a one is called a bodhisattva."

The Bodhisattva Noble Dragon-Majesty King then addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! For what reason is there speech at all? What is speech?"

The Buddha said: "Good man! Māra Pāpīyān... Furthermore, good man! If a bodhisattva does not give rise to the thought 'I shall be thus' regarding form, and likewise does not give rise to the thought 'I shall be thus' regarding feeling, perception, formations, and consciousness, then such a bodhisattva has no speech at any place. Noble Dragon-Majesty King! Those good men who are of the highest excellence have cut off all speech, cut off all obstructions, extinguished all arrogance, cut through all nets, left behind all dualistic views, and departed from all conceptualization. Since there is no speech, how could there be words? There is also nothing that can be spoken. Therefore, non-speech is called the Buddha's words. Good man! For this reason, you should know that this is the Buddha's words.

"Good man! If there is no body and no bodily action, no speech and no verbal action, no mind and no mental action; if it is neither action nor non-action, neither slander nor non-slander; if it does not arise and does not occur; if it is without concept and without place; if it does not abide and does not perish; if it is neither quiescent nor active; if it is true speech that does not move, and yet it is not non-moving and yet does not abide; if it is spontaneous and without conditions, and yet not without conditions: good man, this is the Buddha's words. Because there is nothing that can be spoken, this is called the Buddha's words.

"Good man! When a bodhisattva can study in this way, this is called studying the supreme, supreme wisdom, the radiant Buddha's words, the cooling Buddha's words, that which gladdens the bodies of all sentient beings, that which opens and inspires the minds of all sentient beings, causing them to proceed toward the Buddha's wisdom and to receive and uphold the meaning of the Dharma. It gladdens all the assemblies of bodhisattvas, awakens those who are asleep, skillfully enters the Dharma-realm, and with right determination proceeds toward the Dharma-wheel. It turns the Dharma-wheel, strikes the great Dharma-drum, subdues the hosts of māras, defeats hostile adversaries, overcomes all assemblies of non-Buddhist teachers, rescues and protects those heading toward the evil paths, adorns the Buddha-worlds, and is praised and extolled by all the Buddhas. Such a one will certainly sit upon the seat of enlightenment. Such a bodhisattva has already sat upon the seat of enlightenment. Such a bodhisattva has already obtained all the dhāraṇīs of a bodhisattva."

When this Dharma-gate of the Buddha's words was taught, the Holy Bodhisattva Noble Dragon-Majesty King's factors of awakening were all fully perfected, and he immediately obtained the patient acceptance of the non-arising of dharmas. Twenty-six thousand bodhisattvas obtained dhāraṇīs and various samādhis. Eight thousand bhikṣus attained the dharma without outflows. Furthermore, eighty-four thousand sentient beings all gave rise to the aspiration for anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi and, with supernatural power, rained down various flowers as offerings to the World-Honored One.

When the Tathāgata taught this Dharma-gate, the Holy Bodhisattva Noble Dragon-Majesty King, along with the entire great assembly of devas, humans, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, and all the rest, having heard what the Buddha taught, all rejoiced greatly, believed, received it, and practiced accordingly.
