# 播般曩結使波金剛念誦儀
## Recitation Ritual of the Padanakeśipa Vajra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1232

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown.

---

> I take refuge in the Vajra-Handed One,
> Upon whom all buddhas rely.
> Endowed with the practices of Samantabhadra,
> He abides in this originally existent body.
> Beginningless and endless, tranquil,
> With wondrous function granting all wishes.
> The māra king conforms to the three poisons;
> Across kalpas he tames and softens them,
> Rescuing all sentient beings,
> From ordinary beings to the buddha-ground,
> Accomplishing all in the present life.
> I now expound the meaning of recitation.

Next, if there are bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, or upāsikās who wish to practice the heart mantra of Padakṣepa Vidyārāja, they should prepare the ground and establish the practice-hall according to the dharma. Performing the preliminary practices, sit facing east. Arrange whatever offerings are needed: food and drink, incense, and flowers, according to one's ability, ensuring all is clean and pure. Having entered the practice-hall, bow to all buddhas, take single-minded refuge, and aspire to attain perfect enlightenment. Then sit in full lotus or half-lotus, apply fragrant mud to the hands, and prepare all the mudrās. Next, perform the three-division protection and the body-protection; know the various ritual procedures. These are here briefly omitted.

Next, sit with body upright and mind still, ceasing all discursive thoughts. Contemplate that one's own body was formed by causes from past lives into the four great elements and five aggregates. Seeking reality in each one, it cannot be found. They are impermanent and subject to decay, swiftly arising and swiftly perishing: form itself is emptiness. Having thus clearly and decisively understood with firm conviction, that which observes cannot be extinguished: it becomes true wisdom, vast and pervading the dharma-realm, equal in measure to space. Out of great compassion, arising from truth to function, then visualize one's own body in the form of the principal deity, equal in bearing, neither one nor different.

Then form the preceding samādhi vajra-pestle mudrā and seal the heart. Recite the heart mantra:

*Oṃ hrīḥ jūṃ hūṃ*

By this mudrā and mantra empowerment, the practitioner's mind becomes like the principal deity.

Then raise the mudrā and seal the head. Recite the mantra:

*Oṃ bhūṃ*

By this mudrā and mantra empowerment, one becomes identical to the principal deity.

Also seal the forehead, right shoulder, left shoulder, heart, and throat with this mudrā, each time reciting the armor mantra:

*Oṃ jūṃ*

By this mudrā and mantra empowerment, one is clad in vajra armor that nothing can harm. Through the preceding visualization practice and mantra empowerment, the practitioner's body becomes no different from the principal deity. The buddhas bestow their prediction, devas and nāgas pay homage, and one will certainly attain supreme perfect enlightenment.

**Vajra Stake Mudrā**

The mudrā: insert the right ring finger between the left ring finger and little finger, insert the middle finger between the left middle finger and index finger, and do likewise with the left hand. The remaining fingers and thumbs press together. Visualize it becoming a flaming vajra-pestle shape. Touch the thumb to the ground, plant it firmly, and pull upward. Pull once to three times while reciting, then stop. Mark the boundary in one's mind, large or small as desired; it then becomes the vajra-firm ground-realm. The mantra:

*Oṃ kili-kili vajra-vajrī bhūr-bandha-bandha hūṃ phaṭ*

By this mudrā and mantra empowerment, down to the vajra boundary all becomes like a vajra seat. Heavenly māras and all obstructors cannot cause harm.

**Vajra Wall Boundary for the Cardinal and Intermediate Directions**

The mudrā: same as the preceding ground-realm mudrā, but open both thumbs and stand them upright, held sideways like a wall shape. Circle the body to the right three times. Mark the boundary in one's mind, large or small; it then becomes a vajra-firm citadel. Even buddhas and bodhisattvas do not transgress it, let alone others. Those difficult to tame cannot approach. The mantra:

*Oṃ sara-sara vajra-prakāra hūṃ phaṭ*

The practitioner should then visualize within the altar an eight-petaled lotus with a lion seat above it. Upon the seat is a seven-jewel pavilion draped with necklaces, silk ribbons, pennants, and canopies. Jeweled pillars stand in rows hung with wondrous celestial garments. Fragrant clouds fill the air, raining down various flowers. All manner of music plays. Jeweled vases, arghya, celestial wondrous food and drink, and maṇi-gem lamps are present. Having thus visualized:

**Great Ākāśagarbha Mudrā**

The mudrā: palms joined at the heart, pinch both index fingers into a jewel shape, extend both thumbs to press the base of the index fingers, and cross both middle fingers right over left on the outside, with all fingers interlocking to complete the mudrā. Recite the mantra:

*Oṃ gaganā-saṃbhava vajra hoḥ*

By forming this mudrā and reciting the mantra with empowerment, the visualized offerings become truly real and are enjoyed by the holy assembly.

**Seven-Jewel Chariot Mudrā**

The mudrā: interlock both hands internally with palms facing up. Both index fingers touch horizontally. Both thumbs each press the base of the index fingers below. Visualize a seven-jewel chariot with a vajra charioteer driving it through space to the palace of the principal deity. Recite the mantra three times:

*Oṃ turu-turu hūṃ*

By this mudrā and mantra empowerment, the seven-jewel chariot arrives at the deity's palace. Visualize the great holy principal deity and all the retinue mounting the chariot.

**Invitation Chariot Mudrā**

The mudrā: same as the preceding, but with both thumbs hook the middle fingers toward the body. Recite the mantra three times:

*Namas-triyā-dhvikānāṃ sarva-tathāgatānāṃ oṃ vajrī-jñāna-yakara-ṣāya ehy-ehi bhagavan svāhā*

By this mudrā and mantra empowerment, the holy assembly comes from their original palace to the practice-hall and abides in space.

**Principal Deity Samaya Descent-to-Practice-Hall Mudrā**

The mudrā: interlock both hands internally in fists. The right thumb enters the palm. The left thumb hooks toward the body in invitation. The mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-dhṛka ehy-ehi bhagavan vajra-dhṛka*

By this mantra and mudrā empowerment, the principal deity does not violate the original vow and assembles at the practice-hall.

**Padakṣepa Mudrā**

The physical mudrā: first stand upright. Raise the right foot and step across to the left through space. Turning clockwise, stamp it on the ground. Then move the left foot to meet the right as in the original standing position. Then raise the left foot and step across to the right through space, then stamp it to the left on the ground while bending the knee. Then raise the left hand with the index finger and remaining fingers clenched in a fist, the thumb pressing the side of the index finger. Visualize holding a three-pronged vajra-pestle, extending the arm to the left with full force in a poised stance. The right hand: the thumb enters the palm, gripped by the middle and ring fingers, with the index and little fingers both extended. With full force, lower the arm with the back of the hand facing outward, displaying a wrathful face with angry eyes, gazing to the left. Visualize the remaining arms all holding implements as the principal deity does, like a bodhisattva. For each visualized implement in hand, recite the implement mantra:

*Oṃ jah*

Also visualize the entire body blazing with triple flames like the fire at the end of a kalpa, reaching throughout the ten directions and all worlds, broadly performing buddha-deeds and illuminating, touching, speaking to, and awakening beings. For all tathāgatas who have newly attained the path, pray that they swiftly turn the dharma wheel. For those who are liberating beings, shatter their limited vessels. For those approaching nirvāṇa, beseech them to remain long. Next, encourage and inspire the assemblies of bodhisattvas: may all swiftly realize supreme bodhi, and in the interim never retreat or falter. Further, awaken the practitioners of the two vehicles: remove their limited understanding and cause them to be endowed with the profound emptiness-wisdom of the Mahāyāna. For the realm of ordinary beings reached by one's awesome light, within the ālaya-consciousness the defiled seeds are forever removed from the three evil destinies, and they are endowed with the practices of Samantabhadra. For animals and hungry ghosts, at the end of this life they will not receive such bodies again. Also visualize all hells being destroyed and annihilated without remainder, sinners being liberated, and the mind of bodhi being generated. All evil māras who encounter this are subdued, repelled, and scattered. When this extensive benefit is complete, visualize collecting the awesome flames back into the body, then emerge from concentration. Then again sit according to the dharma as at the beginning.

**Upper Vajra Net Mudrā**

The mudrā: same as the preceding wall mudrā, with both thumbs pressing the lower joint of the index fingers. Recite the mantra three times, turning right above the head three times. The mantra:

*Oṃ viṣphuradakṣa vajra-pañjara hūṃ phaṭ*

By this net mudrā and mantra empowerment, a vajra-firm net is established.

**Fire-Wall Sealed Enclosure Mudrā**

The mudrā: the left hand covers the back of the right hand, both thumbs standing upright. Recite the mantra three times, circling the body to the right three times. Visualize flames encircling the outside of the vajra wall. The mantra:

*Oṃ asamāginī hūṃ phaṭ*

Next, hold the arghya vessel near the forehead and visualize bathing the feet of the holy assembly.

**Universal Offering Mudrā**

The mudrā: join both palms with the right pressing over the left, fingers interlocked and slightly separated. Recite the mantra three times. Contemplate with focused mind immeasurable and boundless cloud-seas of unguent, cloud-seas of powdered incense, cloud-seas of burning incense, cloud-seas of flower garlands, cloud-seas of lamp-light, cloud-seas of food and drink, cloud-seas of garments, and clouds of all other offering substances, all becoming pure and abundantly vast offerings. The universal offering mantra:

*Namas-sarva-tathāgatebhyo viśva-mukhebhyaḥ sarvathā-khaṃ udgate spharaṇa-imaṃ gagana-kaṃ svāhā*

The principal deity's heart mantra:

*Oṃ hrīḥ vīṃ bhūṃ sūṃ jūṃ jah*

Next, contemplate that one's own body is no different from the principal deity. Holding the vajra rosary, recite the preceding heart mantra. During recitation, visualize the principal deity's form, or visualize the letters of the mantra, or visualize the mantra syllables upon the principal deity's heart, or recite in correspondence with a tranquil mind, neither too fast nor too slow, neither too quiet. When the allotted count is complete and one wishes to conclude, one should still remain for a long while abiding in the visualization of the principal deity. Then offer arghya again, form the universal offering mudrā, offer with directed mind, and recite the root mantra. Next, form the fire-wall sealed enclosure mudrā and turn it once to the left above the head: this constitutes the dissolution of the boundary. Then form the descent-to-practice-hall mudrā and push it outward, visualizing the holy assembly returning to their palace. Then form the body-protection mudrā, sealing the five places as before, each time reciting the root mantra.

Next, recite the Mahācakra Vajra mantra twenty-one times. By reciting this mantra, all violations and transgressions of the ritual rules are completely eliminated without remainder, and no merit is wasted.

The three types of practice (pacifying, increasing, and subjugating) are extensively described in the *Susiddhikara Sūtra*; one may perform them to attain accomplishment.

One should know the meritorious method of painting the image. Use cloth of elbow-measure, clean and pure according to the ritual. Paint the principal deity with body entirely blue, gazing to the right. The right foot is extended and the left knee is bent. The upper right hand hangs down with the thumb entering the palm, the middle and ring fingers gripping it, and the other two fingers extended, with the back of the hand facing outward. The next hand holds a peach, hanging down. The next lower hand has the palm facing down with flames in the palm. The next hand holds an iron axe. Next, a jade ring. Next, an upright pointing finger with a wheel upon it. Next, a fist with the thumb pressing the side of the index finger. Next, a lasso. Next, all five fingers extended with long, sharp nails and the palm facing up. The last hand holds a bow.

The upper left hand closest to the body holds a three-pronged vajra-pestle with arm extended upward. Next, a sword. Next, gripping a round stone. Next, a short-handled mace shaped like a heavy wheel. Next, two arrows. Next, a staff. Next, a single-pronged vajra-pestle. Next, an extended palm facing up with thumb and index finger pinching together. Next, a jeweled club, thick at the top and tapering near the grip. The palm faces down with a flame garland, all scattered and standing, also colored.

A dragon serves as the crown, with garlands, necklaces, hand and foot bracelets, and waist sash. The brows are furrowed in a fierce gaze with sharp fangs bared. A red square skirt and red parasol.

Māra King Pāpīyān lies prostrate on the ground, four-armed, his entire body blue, his face turned upward, begging for mercy and pleading for his life. His right hand with five fingers extended is held near his forehead in a gesture of submission. His left hand clutches the ground below. In both hands there is a nāga, which binds him from behind the elbows. His hair is red and scattered, as if in great terror, his head near the great holy one's right foot.

To the left and right of the principal deity there is one vajra youth each, both with bodies entirely red, with radiant and robust forms. The one on the right holds a peach in his right hand and a sword in his left, poised to cut down at Māra King, with hair hanging behind in red. The one on the left draws a bow with his left hand in a stance of shooting at Māra King's head, with hair tied in a knot of reddish-gauze color.

[Note: The right described above is actually left, and left is actually right, reversed for the sake of secrecy.]
