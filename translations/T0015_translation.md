# The Sūtra of Śakra's Questions

## Śakraparipṛcchā Sūtra

Taishō No. 0015, 1 fascicle

Translator: Dharmabhadra (法賢), Song dynasty

---

Thus have I heard. At one time, the Buddha was in the kingdom of Magadha, east of the city of Rājagṛha in the Āmra Garden, north of the great brāhmaṇa village, in the Indasāla Cave on Mount Vediyaka, together with a great assembly.

At that time, Śakra, lord of the devas, heard that the Buddha was in the kingdom of Magadha, in the Indasāla Cave on Mount Vediyaka. He then addressed Pañcaśikha, prince of the gandharvas, saying: "Do you know? I have heard that the Buddha is in the kingdom of Magadha, in the Indasāla Cave on Mount Vediyaka. I wish to go with you to the Buddha's place to approach and make offerings." At that time, Pañcaśikha, prince of the gandharvas, hearing these words, said to Śakra: "Excellent, lord of the devas!" Having said this, he took up his lapis lazuli-adorned harp and followed Śakra. The heavenly host, hearing that Śakra, lord of the devas, and Pañcaśikha, prince of the gandharvas, had set their minds on going to the Buddha's place to approach and make offerings, each also set their minds to follow along and go to the Buddha's place to approach and make offerings.

At that time, Śakra, lord of the devas, together with Pañcaśikha, prince of the gandharvas, and that heavenly host, vanished from that heaven. In the time it takes a strong man to flex and extend his arm, they arrived beside Mount Vediyaka in the kingdom of Magadha. At that time, a great light suddenly appeared on that mountain, illuminating all around. The people on all four sides of the mountain, seeing that light, said to one another: "Why is there a great fire blazing on this mountain, concealing its original appearance so that it looks like a mountain of jewels?"

At that time, Śakra, lord of the devas, said to Pañcaśikha, prince of the gandharvas: "Do you see this mountain with such wondrous and extraordinary appearance? Because the Buddha, the World-Honored One, abides therein, the four things are pure. Furthermore, all the halls and palaces on this mountain are entirely made of jewels; those who dwell here have exhausted all afflictions and fully realized the holy fruit, and even devas of great power constantly abide here." He further said: "Therefore, this is difficult for us to encounter. As I said before about approaching and making offerings, now is the right time. You, Pañcaśikha, prince of the gandharvas, should make an offering with the music you carry. Why? After this, such an encounter will truly be hard to find."

At that time, the gandharva prince, hearing these words, said to Śakra: "Excellent! Excellent!" Having said this, he immediately thought: "The Buddhas, the Tathāgatas, possess the penetration of the divine ear; whether far or near, they can hear everything." Having thought this, he struck up the lapis lazuli-adorned harp he carried, and from its sound he produced gāthās, and in those gāthās he spoke of what he desired. The gāthās said:

> O you, Sūryakāntā, virtuous maiden,
> Please petition your father the king
> To give me your hand as kin;
> Thus I know your goodness.
> My longing for you
> Is like one tormented by heat
> Thinking of coolness,
> Like a thirsty person craving water;
> Like the sick thinking of medicine,
> Like the hungry longing for food,
> Like a great elephant goaded by a hook
> Unable to go forward.
> And like an arhat
> Delighting in the dharma of cessation,
> What I now seek and wish for
> Is likewise in meaning.
> Craving increases affliction;
> In this there is no truth.
> Not attaining what one seeks,
> One endures various kinds of suffering.
> The merit I have created
> In making offerings to arhats,
> The fruit I have obtained,
> I shall share with you.
> I seek the maiden Sūryakāntā
> With a mind most resolute;
> Śakra, lord of all the devas,
> Grant me my wish.

At that time, the World-Honored One, in the Indasāla Cave, with the penetration of the divine ear heard that sound from afar. Through his spiritual power he addressed Pañcaśikha, prince of the gandharvas, from afar, saying: "Excellent! Excellent! Prince of the gandharvas, you are skilled in music! When you strike the strings, there comes forth a wondrous sound like a beautiful singing voice; when you sing, it sounds again like the tone of the strings. For what reason have you long been producing music, and within those strings produced gāthās? And within those gāthās spoken of three kinds of sound, namely the sound of love and delight, the sound of the nāga, and the sound of the arhat?"

At that time, Pañcaśikha, prince of the gandharvas, empowered by the Buddha's spiritual force, heard the Buddha's words from afar, and said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I recall that once there was a gandharva king named Timbarū. That king had a daughter named Sūryakāntā. My heart desired her and I sought her as my consort. At that time, though I employed various means, my wish was not fulfilled. So before the maiden I played such music, and within the strings of that music I produced gāthās, and within those gāthās I spoke of three kinds of sound. World-Honored One! When I was playing this music, at that assembly of the good Dharma there were devas who said to one another: 'This Pañcaśikha, prince of the gandharvas, has neither seen nor heard our Buddha, the World-Honored One, complete in the ten epithets: Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, Vidyācaraṇasaṃpanna, Sugata, Lokavidū, Anuttara, Puruṣadamyasārathi, Śāstā Devamanuṣyāṇām, Buddha, Bhagavat.' Thereupon I said to those devas: 'You devas have well praised the virtues of the Buddha.' The devas replied: 'Pañcaśikha, prince of the gandharvas! The merit of praising the Buddha that we possess, we share with you.' Pañcaśikha, prince of the gandharvas, hearing the devas' words, suddenly had an enlightenment and said: 'Noble ones! I now take refuge in the Buddha, the World-Honored One.' It is for this reason that I have played such music before the Buddha."

At that time, Śakra, lord of the devas, thought: "Now this Pañcaśikha, prince of the gandharvas, has ripened in his roots and conditions. Even before arriving in the Buddha's presence, he has already extended his offerings." Having thought this, he said to Pañcaśikha, prince of the gandharvas: "Take my words and go to the Buddha's place. Prostrate with your head at his feet, and speak in my name thus: 'Śakra, lord of the devas, bows his head at your feet and inquires of the World-Honored One: Are you free from illness and affliction? Are your daily activities light and easy? Is your strength well? Is your coming and going free from distress? I now, together with the Trāyastriṃśa heavenly host, wish to come to the Buddha to approach and make offerings. We await the Buddha's assent.'"

At that time, Pañcaśikha, prince of the gandharvas, hearing these words, said to Śakra: "Excellent, lord of the devas!" Having said this, he went to the Buddha's place, prostrated with his head at the Buddha's feet, stood to one side, and said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! Śakra, lord of the devas, together with the Trāyastriṃśa heavenly host, has sent me here to bow at the Buddha's feet and to inquire of the World-Honored One: Are you free from illness and affliction? Are your daily activities light and easy? Is your strength well? Is your coming and going free from distress? We wish today to come to the Buddha's place to approach and make offerings, and so he has sent me to await the Buddha's assent."

The Buddha replied: "You may return and tell Śakra and that heavenly host: now is the right time." Pañcaśikha, prince of the gandharvas, having received the Buddha's holy assent, returned to Śakra's place and conveyed the World-Honored One's words that now was the right time.

At that time, Śakra and the Trāyastriṃśa heavenly host went to the Buddha's place. Having arrived, they bowed at the Buddha's feet and stood to one side. At that time, the lord of the devas thought: "This Indasāla Cave is narrow and confined; how shall the countless heavenly beings be seated?" The Buddha, knowing his thoughts, through his spiritual power made the cave spacious and wide, so that all the heavenly beings could be accommodated without crowding one another. Śakra, lord of the devas, and that heavenly host each bowed to the Buddha and sat down in order. When all were settled, Śakra, lord of the devas, joined his palms and said: "World-Honored One! Throughout the long night I have desired to see the Buddha and to hear the true Dharma. World-Honored One! I recall that once the Buddha was in the kingdom of Śrāvastī, at the Jeta Grove of Anāthapiṇḍada, and entered the fire-element samādhi. At that time, I was in the palace of Vaiśravaṇa, and I saw in that palace a lady named Subāhu, who, seeing the Buddha enter this fire-element samādhi, joined her palms reverently and single-mindedly contemplated the Buddha. Seeing that the World-Honored One had not yet emerged from samādhi, I said to Subāhu: 'Wait until the Buddha, the World-Honored One, emerges from samādhi, and convey my sincere regards to the Buddha: Is he free from illness and affliction? Are his daily activities light and easy? Is his strength well? Is his coming and going free from distress?' I further said: 'Wait until the Buddha emerges from concentration and convey my sincere regards; do not let them be forgotten.' World-Honored One! Is this matter true?"

The Buddha said: "Śakra! This matter is indeed true. That lady did on your behalf convey respectful regards." The Buddha further said: "Lord of the Devas! While in samādhi I also heard your words, and not long afterward I emerged from samādhi."

At that time, Śakra said: "World-Honored One! In former times I heard that when a Buddha, a Tathāgata, a Samyaksaṃbuddha, appears in the world, he brings great benefit and through great skillful means leads beings according to their kind, sometimes concealing the human form, sometimes manifesting the heavenly body. I now know for myself that the Buddha's appearance in the world is of great benefit, and that through skillful means he leads beings according to their kind, sometimes concealing, sometimes manifesting. World-Honored One! There are śrāvakas who have gone forth from home under the Buddha and practiced the holy life; upon death they were reborn in the Trāyastriṃśa heaven, where those heavenly beings delight in three things: lifespan, physical form, and renown. World-Honored One! In former times there was a Śākya woman named Gopikā who had gone forth from home under the Buddha and maintained the holy life. She constantly loathed her female body and sought the form of a man. Upon death she was reborn in the Trāyastriṃśa heaven, became my son, was named Gopaka, and possessed great power, a great man. World-Honored One! There were also three bhikṣus who practiced the śrāvaka path but had not been able to cut off the mind of craving and desire; upon death they were reborn in the heavenly realm as sons of the gandharva Vīṇā, and they constantly came to serve as attendants to the devaputra Gopaka. At that time, the devaputra Gopaka spoke a gāthā to the sons of the gandharva Vīṇā:

> "'I was formerly a woman,
> Wise, named Gopikā;
> Loathing the female, I sought the male form,
> Constantly making offerings to the Buddha, Dharma, and Sangha.
> At that time I saw you three
> Practicing the śrāvaka path;
> Now reborn in a lower lineage,
> You serve as my attendants.
> You should now know this:
> I speak the truth to you.
> In former times when you were human,
> The four requisites were abundantly supplied.
> You did not rely on the Buddha's precepts;
> Now you may well feel shame.
> To understand the mind is itself the true Dharma;
> Only the wise can understand.
> Formerly we practiced the same path,
> Drew near the Buddha, heard the true Dharma,
> Aroused faith, upheld the Buddha's precepts,
> And made offerings to the holy community.
> Because I practiced right conduct,
> I became a son of Śakra,
> Possessed of great heavenly power,
> Knowing my own name as Gopaka,
> Dwelling in a supremely excellent palace,
> Transformed from female to male form.
> You gandharva sons, who under the Buddha
> Maintained the holy life
> And heard the Buddha's supreme Dharma,
> Have instead become attendants.
> In the heavenly realm I had not seen this before;
> Now I see it:
> Those who practiced the śrāvaka path,
> Yet reborn in a lower lineage.
> You gandharva sons, receive my, Gopaka's, instruction;
> The rebirth you have received
> Is not that of sons of the Buddha.'
> The gandharva sons said: 'What the deva says is true.
> We, on account of craving and desire,
> Have fallen into the gandharva realm.
> We now arouse diligence
> And contemplate only the Buddha's true Dharma,
> Knowing that craving produces faults,
> We cut off the mind of craving.
> Craving is the bond of affliction;
> Its power surpasses Māra's host.
> Abandoning the Buddha's true Dharma,
> We were not reborn among the superior devas.
> Śakra and Brahmā
> Sit in the assembly of the good Dharma,
> Observing the excellent conduct of the devas
> Who wander through the heavenly realm.
> They see us reborn in a lower lineage,
> Wandering through the heavenly realm;
> Because our conduct was not upright,
> We have not obtained the superior fruit.'
> Then the devaputra Gopaka
> Said to his father Śakra:
> 'Royal father, now know this:
> Our Buddha, the most supreme,
> Has appeared in the world,
> Skillfully subduing the host of Māra,
> Named Śākyamuni.
> These three gandharvas
> Are sons of that Buddha
> Who forgot right mindfulness
> And fell into the gandharva realm.
> Among those three,
> Only one lacks right understanding;
> The other two have returned to the right path,
> Constantly turning toward the Buddha's bodhi
> And walking in the true Dharma.
> Among all the śrāvakas observed,
> None can compare with them.
> They have distanced themselves from desire,
> Are able to sever affliction,
> Think only of the Buddha, the World-Honored One,
> And no longer give rise to other thoughts.
> All the Dharma not yet understood,
> Those two fully and rightly know;
> They shall obtain the superior fruit
> And be reborn in the Brahmā heaven.'

"World-Honored One! At that time, having heard the devaputra Gopaka speak these verses, I had some unresolved questions about this matter, so I have come to the Buddha's presence wishing to respectfully inquire. May the Buddha in his compassion expound this for me."

At that time, the World-Honored One thought: "Throughout the long night, Śakra, lord of the devas, has been without slackening, without neglect, without dust, without defilement. If he has questions, he truly does not know the answers; it is not a demonic undertaking. What he asks, I should expound." Having thought thus, he spoke a gāthā, saying to Śakra:

> Śakra, now know this:
> What your heart desires
> And the meaning you wish to ask about,
> Ask me, and I shall expound it for you.

At that time, Śakra, lord of the devas, spoke a gāthā, saying to the World-Honored One:

> Now receiving the Buddha's permission,
> As my mind desires,
> I now raise my request:
> May the Buddha expound it for me.

Having spoken this gāthā, Śakra said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! By what are all devas, humans, asuras, gandharvas, and all ordinary beings afflicted?"

The Buddha said: "They are afflicted by enmity and affection. Śakra, lord of the devas! All devas, humans, asuras, gandharvas, and all ordinary beings think to themselves: 'Alas! I first did no harm to others, harbored no resentment, engaged in no fighting, no disputing, no complaint, no litigation, and held nothing against anyone. Why then have they treated me thus in return?' Lord of the Devas! Such matters arise from enmity and affection; because enmity and affection arise, affliction ensues."

Śakra said: "World-Honored One! So it is, so it is, as the Buddha has said. I now understand this meaning from the Buddha: that enmity and affection are the affliction. My doubt is severed, and my heart's desire is fulfilled."

At that time, Śakra, lord of the devas, rejoiced at hearing the Buddha's teaching, received it with faith, and again said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! This affliction of enmity and affection: what is its cause, what its origin? What is its arising, what its condition? By what cause does it exist? By what cause does it not exist?"

The Buddha said: "Lord of the Devas! This affliction of enmity and affection has enemies and loved ones as its cause, originates from enemies and loved ones, arises from enemies and loved ones, has enemies and loved ones as its condition. Because of enemies and loved ones, the affliction of enmity and affection exists; if there are no enemies and loved ones, enmity and affection do not exist."

Śakra said to the Buddha: "So it is, so it is, as the Buddha has said. I now understand this meaning from the Buddha: that the affliction of enmity and affection has enemies and loved ones as its cause; if there are no enemies and loved ones, there is no enmity and affection." He further said: "World-Honored One! By what does the existence of enemies and loved ones arise? From what does it originate? From what does it arise? On what condition does it depend? By what cause does it exist? By what cause does it not exist?"

The Buddha told Śakra: "Desire is its cause; from desire it originates; from desire it arises; on desire it depends as its condition. Because of desire there are enemies and loved ones; if there is no desire, there are no enemies and loved ones."

Śakra said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! So it is, so it is, as the Buddha has said: enemies and loved ones exist because of desire." He further said: "World-Honored One! This desire: what is its cause? From what does it originate? From what does it arise? On what does it depend as its condition? By what cause does it exist? By what cause does it not exist?"

The Buddha said: "Śakra! Desire has doubt and uncertainty as its cause; from doubt and uncertainty it originates; from doubt and uncertainty it arises; on doubt and uncertainty it depends as its condition. Because of doubt and uncertainty there is desire; if there is no doubt and uncertainty, there is no desire."

Śakra said: "World-Honored One! So it is, so it is, as the Buddha has said: desire exists because of doubt and uncertainty." He further said: "World-Honored One! Doubt and uncertainty: what is their cause? What their origin? What their arising? What their condition? By what cause do they exist? By what cause do they not exist?"

The Buddha told Śakra: "False construction is their cause; from false construction they originate; from false construction they arise; on false construction they depend as their condition. Because of false construction there is doubt and uncertainty; because of doubt and uncertainty there is desire; because of desire there are enemies and loved ones; because of enemies and loved ones there arises enmity and affection; because of enmity and affection there is taking up of weapons, litigation, fighting, and disputing; duplicity of feeling arises, and speech becomes untrue. Such various kinds of unwholesome actions and unskillful dharmas come to be, and thereby a great mass of suffering is gathered. Lord of the Devas! If there is no false construction, there is no doubt and uncertainty; if there is no doubt and uncertainty, there is no desire; if desire does not exist, what enemies and loved ones can there be? If enemies and loved ones are not established, enmity and affection are naturally removed; because enmity and affection are absent, the taking up of weapons, litigation, fighting, and disputing, duplicity of feeling and untruthful speech, such various kinds of unwholesome actions and unskillful dharmas, all cease; thus a great mass of suffering is extinguished."

Śakra said to the Buddha: "So it is, so it is, as the Buddha has said: because of doubt and uncertainty there is false construction." He further said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! By what dharma is false construction extinguished? And how should bhikṣus practice?"

The Buddha told the lord of the devas: "What extinguishes false construction is the noble Eightfold Path. The noble Eightfold Path comprises: right view, right intention, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, and right concentration. Through these eight dharmas, false construction is extinguished. If bhikṣus practice these dharmas, this is what is called the practice that extinguishes false construction."

Śakra said to the Buddha: "So it is, so it is. World-Honored One! What extinguishes false construction is the noble Eightfold Path." Śakra further said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! Regarding the dharma of extinguishing false construction that bhikṣus practice, how many kinds of dharma are there within the dharma of individual liberation?"

The Buddha said: "Lord of the Devas! The dharma of false construction, within the dharma of individual liberation, comprises six kinds. What are the six? They are: the eye perceiving form, the ear hearing sound, the nose smelling fragrance, the tongue discerning flavor, the body sensing contact, and the mind discriminating dharmas. Lord of the Devas! The eye perceiving form has two aspects: what may be perceived and what may not be perceived. What may not be perceived refers to the domain of all defiled dharmas, which should not be perceived; what may be perceived refers to the domain of all wholesome dharmas, which may be observed. Thus with the eye perceiving form, and likewise up to the mind discriminating dharmas, it is also so."

"World-Honored One! I now understand this meaning from the Buddha. What may not be perceived: all the unwholesome dharmas within the domain of the eye, if one perceives them, this increases defiled dharmas and diminishes wholesome dharmas. What may be perceived: all the wholesome dharmas within the domain of the eye, if one perceives them, this increases wholesome dharmas and diminishes defiled dharmas. Likewise up to the mind discriminating dharmas, it is also so. World-Honored One! Having heard this Dharma from the Buddha, my aspiration is fulfilled and my doubt is severed." He further said: "World-Honored One! If a bhikṣu wishes to extinguish false construction, how many dharmas should he abandon, and how many dharmas should he practice?"

The Buddha said: "Lord of the Devas! If a bhikṣu wishes to extinguish the dharma of false construction, he should abandon three dharmas and practice three dharmas: first, doubt and uncertainty; second, hankering; third, meaningless speech. These three kinds of dharmas likewise have what is to be practiced and what is not to be practiced. What is not to be practiced: regarding these three kinds of unwholesome dharmas, one should abandon and not practice them; if one practices them, unwholesome dharmas increase and wholesome dharmas diminish. What is to be practiced: regarding these three kinds of unwholesome dharmas, one diligently practices their elimination, and thus unwholesome dharmas diminish and wholesome dharmas increase."

Śakra said: "World-Honored One! So it is, so it is. I now understand this meaning from the Buddha. Doubt and uncertainty, hankering, and meaningless speech: these three kinds of dharmas, if practiced, diminish wholesome dharmas and increase the unwholesome. If a bhikṣu diligently practices the elimination of these three dharmas, unwholesome dharmas diminish and wholesome dharmas increase." He further said: "World-Honored One! If a bhikṣu practices the dharma that extinguishes false construction, how many kinds of body are there?"

The Buddha said: "Lord of the Devas! If a bhikṣu practices the dharma that extinguishes false construction, there are three kinds of body. The three kinds of body are: the body of ease and satisfaction, the body of suffering and distress, and the body of equanimity. The body of ease and satisfaction has two aspects: what is to be practiced and what is not to be practiced. What is to be practiced refers to all wholesome dharmas; what is not to be practiced refers to all unwholesome dharmas. So too with the body of suffering and distress and the body of equanimity."

Śakra said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! So it is, so it is. I now understand this meaning from the Buddha. If a bhikṣu practices the dharma that extinguishes false construction, with regard to the body of ease and satisfaction, the body of suffering and distress, and the body of equanimity: among these three bodies, all wholesome dharmas are to be practiced, and all unwholesome dharmas are not to be practiced."

At that time, Śakra further said: "World-Honored One! Are the desires, recollections, and physical forms of all sentient beings identical?"

The Buddha said: "No. Lord of the Devas, all sentient beings do not share one desire, nor one recollection, nor one physical form. Lord of the Devas! All sentient beings, though dwelling each in their own realm and destiny, do not know the distinctions among realms and destinies. Because they do not know these distinctions among realms and destinies, they walk the dark path and in return grasp foolish dharmas as being true and real. These sentient beings, not knowing the various distinctions of realms and destinies, understand only the realm of darkness, and though they understand even this, they constantly walk the dark path and firmly grasp foolish dharmas as being true and real."

Śakra said: "World-Honored One! So it is, so it is, as the Buddha has said. I now understand this meaning from the Buddha: all sentient beings do not share one desire, nor one recollection, nor one physical form; because those sentient beings do not know distinctions, they grasp the foolish and the dark as being true and real."

At that time, Śakra further said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! Have all śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas attained the ultimate purity of the holy life?"

The Buddha said: "No. There are two aspects to this. Lord of the Devas! If those śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas are unable to exhaust craving, they will decidedly not attain the ultimate purity of the holy life. If those śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas are able to exhaust craving, they attain the unsurpassed liberation, the right liberation of mind: this is called attaining the ultimate purity of the holy life."

Śakra said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! So it is, so it is, as the Buddha has said. I now understand this meaning from the Buddha. If śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas do not exhaust craving, they will decidedly not attain the ultimate purity of the holy life. If śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas exhaust craving, they will decidedly attain the unsurpassed liberation, the right liberation of mind: this is called attaining the ultimate purity of the holy life."

At that time, Śakra further said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! How may I now permanently depart from the illness of all views, so that it no longer arises? This illness of views arises from mind-consciousness; what is to become of this mind-consciousness of mine? Though I have asked the Buddha about various meanings, why can I not obtain the holy fruit of the Buddha, the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Samyaksaṃbuddha? I beseech the World-Honored One: please sever for me the root of doubt and the illness of all views."

The Buddha said: "Lord of the Devas! Do you know? In former times there were śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who also asked about this matter."

Śakra said: "World-Honored One! I now recall that at one time, devas of great majesty and power assembled at the assembly of the good Dharma in the Trāyastriṃśa heaven. At that time, some devas in the assembly, not knowing the Dharma, hastily wished to become Buddhas, and with this intention addressed the World-Honored One. The Buddha, discerning their foolishness and ignorance, did not grant them the prediction. At that time, those devas, their wish unfulfilled and their minds in discord, rose from their seats and each returned to their own realm. Their own realm did not appear, and consequently they fell. Those devas, because they had fallen, were greatly alarmed and terrified, and their minds gave rise to doubt and uncertainty. Each thought: 'Our own realm has not appeared; we surely know we have fallen. If we can find any śramaṇa or brāhmaṇa, we will go and ask: Are you the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Samyaksaṃbuddha?' At that time, those devas, some of whom saw me walking alone, came to my presence and asked me: 'Noble one! Who are you?' I replied: 'I am Śakra, lord of the devas.' Those devas, their minds in distress, said: 'Lord of the Devas! Do you not see our suffering? Because we should have inquired about the Dharma from the Buddha but did not inquire, should have taken refuge but did not take refuge, with discordant minds we returned to our own realm, our own realm did not appear, and we knew we had fallen. Therefore we suffer. We beg your protection and aid. Lord of the Devas! From this day forward we vow to take refuge in the Buddha as śrāvaka disciples.' At that time I spoke a gāthā in reply to those devas:

> "'You all gave rise to wrong thoughts;
> Your words too were not right.
> Seeking the Buddha with discordant minds,
> Because of this you long endure suffering.
> Perhaps you saw some śramaṇa
> Or some brāhmaṇa
> Walking in meditation, and asked:
> Are you the Perfectly Enlightened One?
> If you are the Perfectly Enlightened One,
> I take refuge and make offerings.
> I then asked them:
> How should one make offerings?
> What was asked, they could not answer;
> Only the Buddha knows the true and correct path.
> At that time those devas,
> In what their hearts desired,
> With mind and mental factors,
> Wavered in doubt and discrimination.
> I knew their mental dharmas,
> As the World-Honored One has taught.
> I then spoke to them:
> In those three realms,
> Only the Buddha, the World-Honored One,
> Is the great teacher of the world,
> Skillfully subduing Māra's great host,
> Able to ferry all sentient beings
> To the further shore of nirvāṇa.
> The Tathāgata, the great and awakened lord,
> Among devas and among humans,
> Has no equal;
> The fearless great man
> Has skillfully severed the illness of craving and affection.
> O Tathāgata, great solar lord,
> Bow your heads now and pay homage.'"

At that time, after Śakra, lord of the devas, had related this matter, the Buddha further told him: "Lord of the Devas! Do you know that past matter, namely the benefit of discrimination and the benefit of gratification?"

Śakra said: "World-Honored One! I now recall that at one time devas and asuras engaged in battle; the devas were victorious and the asuras were defeated and retreated. I thought: 'All the pleasure of the devas and all the pleasure of the asuras, I alone receive and obtain gratification.' Through obtaining such a benefit of gratification, throughout this life there would be fighting and disputing and mutual harm by weapons; this is called the consequence of the benefit of gratification. The benefit of discrimination means that throughout this one life there is no fighting and no disputing, and even weapons do not harm one another; this is called the benefit of discrimination." Śakra further said: "World-Honored One! Having now heard this true Dharma from the Buddha, my faith is deepened even further, and I give rise to this aspiration and vow: may I at the end of my life, if reborn among humans, be born into a noble family of great wealth and abundant grain, with many precious gems accumulated, with carriages and vehicles and fine possessions, with a flourishing retinue fully equipped, always without deficiency. May I be reborn into such a superior family and enter the womb of one wise, with my body and limbs complete, my form and appearance outstanding, eating the finest flavors, honorable and sovereign, with a long lifespan. May I arouse right faith and go forth from home under the Buddha, shave off hair and beard, don the dharma robes, and become a bhikṣu; may I constantly maintain the holy life without any breach or transgression, and realize the fruits of srotāpanna and sakṛdāgāmin, up to attaining the very end of suffering. World-Honored One! I have further heard that there is the Akaniṣṭha heaven; may I, when I die among humans, be reborn in that heaven."

The Buddha said: "Lord of the Devas! Excellent, excellent. Lord of the Devas! It shall be as you aspire. By what cause and condition has this distinguished attainment come about?"

Śakra said: "World-Honored One! I have no other cause; it is precisely from having heard the true Dharma from the Buddha that I have aroused deep faith, and through the power of aspiration I realize such a fruit. World-Honored One! Today, in this assembly, having heard the true Dharma, through the power of the Dharma my wisdom has increased and my lifespan too has increased."

At that time, having made this aspiration, Śakra attained the pure Dharma eye, far from dust, apart from defilement. Moreover, eighty thousand heavenly beings also obtained the purity of the Dharma eye. At that time, Śakra, lord of the devas, having heard the Dharma and seen the Dharma and being able to understand it, was established in the Dharma and firm, with all doubts severed. Having thus realized, he rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, joined his palms, bowed his head, and said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I am liberated! I am liberated! From this day onward, for the rest of my life, I take refuge in the Buddha, the Dharma, and the Sangha, and uphold the precepts of an upāsaka."

At that time, Śakra, lord of the devas, turned before the Buddha and spoke to Pañcaśikha, prince of the gandharvas, saying: "You have now brought about great good benefit for me and for the welfare of many. Because you came earlier and offered that wondrous music to the Buddha, I and the others were thereby enabled to hear the Dharma and attain the fruit. When I return to my palace, I shall fulfill your wish."

At that time, Śakra, lord of the devas, further addressed the Trāyastriṃśa heavenly host, saying: "Noble ones, you should now intone with brahmic voice the three refuges in the Buddha. What do you think? The Buddha, the World-Honored One, has already attained the brahmic abode of tranquil nirvāṇa." At that time, the heavenly host followed Śakra in circumambulating the Buddha three times, then prostrated with their heads at the Buddha's feet, stood before the Buddha, and with one voice in unison intoned with brahmic voice the three refuges in the Buddha:

*Namo namaḥ sattvasamaye bhagavate tathāgatāya arhate samyaksaṃbuddhāya*

Śakra, lord of the devas, together with that heavenly host, having three times taken refuge in the Buddha, and together with Pañcaśikha, prince of the gandharvas, and the others, vanished from the assembly and returned to the heavenly realm.

At that time, the Great Brahmā King, lord of the Sahā world, after that day had passed, in the watches of the night, came to the Buddha's place, his bodily radiance blazing brilliantly, illuminating the Indasāla Cave. Having arrived before the Buddha, he prostrated at the Buddha's feet, then sat to one side, joined his palms in homage, and spoke a gāthā:

> Śakra, for the welfare of many,
> Came to the Buddha to ask about the true Dharma;
> The Buddha, with his wondrous voice,
> Caused all doubt and confusion to be severed.

At that time, having spoken the gāthā, the Brahmā King said: "World-Honored One! When the Buddha expounded the true Dharma, Śakra, lord of the devas, attained the pure Dharma eye, far from dust and free from defilement; and eighty thousand heavenly beings also attained the pure Dharma eye." The Buddha said: "It is so, it is so." At that time, the Great Brahmā King, lord of the Sahā world, having heard the Buddha's words, rejoiced and received them in faith, prostrated at the Buddha's feet, vanished from sight, and returned to the heavenly realm.

At that time, the World-Honored One, during the watches of the night, went to where the assembly of bhikṣus sat surrounding him, and addressed the bhikṣus, saying: "After that day had passed, in the watches of the night, the Great Brahmā King, lord of the Sahā world, came to my place. Having prostrated at my feet, with palms joined in reverence, he spoke a gāthā:

> "'Śakra, for the welfare of many,
> Came to the Buddha to ask about the true Dharma;
> The Buddha, with his wondrous voice,
> Caused all doubt and confusion to be severed.'

"He then said to me: 'When Śakra, lord of the devas, heard the true Dharma, he attained the pure Dharma eye; and eighty thousand heavenly beings also attained the pure Dharma eye.' I then replied: 'It is so, it is so.' At that time, having heard my words, that Brahmā King rejoiced and received them in faith, prostrated at my feet, vanished from sight, and returned to the heavenly realm."

At that time, the entire assembly of bhikṣus, having heard the Buddha expound this Dharma, all rejoiced greatly, prostrated before the Buddha, and withdrew.
