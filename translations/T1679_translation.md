# 佛一百八名讚
## Praise of the Buddha's One Hundred and Eight Names

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1679

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmadeva (法天).

---

> Homage to the Omniscient One,
> Teacher of all worlds,
> The Muni, great King of the Dharma,
> Whose names number one hundred and eight.
> A boundless ocean of merit,
> Fully endowed with all auspiciousness,
> Able to extinguish in all sentient beings
> Their transgressions and afflictions.
>
> I now bow in homage
> To the one of all-encompassing great auspiciousness,
> Who rescues and liberates all living beings
> And brings them great peace and happiness.
>
> I now bow in homage
> To the Compassionate One, honored among bipeds,
> Who brings to all sentient beings
> The fulfillment of all auspicious deeds.
> I now bow in homage
> To the Signless, Unsurpassed Honored One,
> Who has accomplished, as Deva of devas,
> The esoteric and great luminous meaning.
> The Tathāgata, Perfectly Awakened One,
> Benefits and delights the world.
> The supreme one hundred and eight names,
> I now gather and proclaim them here.

Namo to Sarvārthasiddha, All-Purposes-Accomplished. Namo to the Perfectly Awakened One. Namo to the Omniscient One. Namo to the Great Son of the Śākyas. Namo to the One Free and Fearless in All Dharmas. Namo to the Great Golden Sage. Namo to the Ocean of Merit. Namo to the Tathāgata. Namo to the Arhat. Namo to the Sugata. Namo to the Bhagavat. Namo to the Knower of the World. Namo to the One Free from All Defilements. Namo to the Supreme Lamp of the Dharma. Namo to the Fearless Prince of King Śuddhodana. Namo to the One of the Ikṣvāku Lineage. Namo to Gautama. Namo to the One of the Solar Lineage. Namo to the Auspicious Great Muni. Namo to the One of the Āṅgīrasa Lineage. Namo to the Teacher of Gods and Humans. Namo to the One with Ten Powers Who Subdues the Hosts of Māra. Namo to the One Free from Faults Who Removes Poison. Namo to the Tamer of Persons. Namo to the Wondrously Liberated One. Namo to the One Who Removes the Darkness of Obscurations. Namo to the One Who Stills and Subdues the Sense Faculties. Namo to the One of Pure Morality. Namo to the Stainless King. Namo to the One Who Extinguishes Transgressions, Without the Mark of Self. Namo to the One Without Fear or Terror. Namo to the One Complete in the Supreme Dharma. Namo to the Boundless One Beyond Comparison. Namo to the Great Master of Discourse. Namo to the One of Rare and Inconceivable Vigor. Namo to the Compassionate Father of the Three Realms. Namo to the One with Three Knowledges, Knower of the Three Times. Namo to the Extinguisher of the Three Poisons. Namo to the One Endowed with the Three Transformative Powers. Namo to the Proclaimer of the Bodhi of the Three Vehicles. Namo to the One Without Marks and Without Aging. Namo to the One Without Self and Without Dual Grasping. Namo to the One Without Enmity and Without Idle Discourse. Namo to the One Without Karma and Without Fear. Namo to the Granter of Wishes and Fearlessness. Namo to the Lion of the Dharma, Honored Among Bipeds. Namo to the Tamer Who Removes Afflictions. Namo to the One of Supremely Pure Mind. Namo to the One Perfect in Knowledge and Conduct. Namo to the Sovereign King of Transformation. Namo to the One of Pure and Tamed Mind. Namo to the Unsurpassed One Free from Dust. Namo to the One Who Stills All Transgressions. Namo to the One Who Has Attained Coolness. Namo to the One Who Has Attained Quiescence. Namo to the Savior and Teacher of the World. Namo to the One of Heroic, Great, Pure Wisdom. Namo to the One of Perfect Auspicious Marks. Namo to the Conqueror of Enemies. Namo to the Moon of the Renunciants. Namo to the Lion of the Śākyas. Namo to the Performer of Good and Pure Deeds. Namo to the One Adorned with the Six Buddha-Dharmas. Namo to the One with Pure Eyes of the Six Faculties. Namo to the One Supreme in the Six Supernatural Powers. Namo to the One Who Has Crossed the Ocean of the Six Destinies to the Far Shore. Namo to the Self-Awakened One Without a Teacher. Namo to the One Accomplished in the Virtues of the Sugata. Namo to the One Beloved of All Worlds. Namo to the One of Unequalled Great Wisdom. Namo to the One Constantly Entering Samādhi. Namo to the Lord of the Benefit of All Sentient Beings. Namo to the One Who Truly Subdues the Faculties. Namo to the One Honored by All Worlds. Namo to the Diligent One Pervading All Sentient Beings. Namo to the One Who Has Forever Transcended the Suffering of Saṃsāra. Namo to the One Who Fulfills All Wishes. Namo to the Conqueror Who Supremely Proclaims the Four Noble Truths. Namo to the Great Nāga King Who Reaches the Far Shore and Ferries Others. Namo to the One Who Attains Supreme Nirvāṇa. Namo to the Honored Teacher of the Great Brahma-Conduct. Namo to the One of Supreme Quiescent Bliss. Namo to the Secret and Supreme Great Being. Namo to the Tamer of the Śrāvakas. Namo to the Benefactor of All Sentient Beings. Namo to the One Offered by the World, of Transcendent Wisdom. Namo to the One Whose Sacred Wisdom Illuminates the World. Namo to the One Free from Worldly Gain. Namo to the One Who Transcends the World as the Tathāgata. Namo to the Great Lord of the Dharma. Namo to the Great King of the Dharma. Namo to the Light of Taming and Guiding. Namo to the Supreme Savior, Honored Among Bipeds. Namo to the Practitioner of Patience. Namo to the One of Good Intention and Dignified Appearance. Namo to the One Who Well Upholds the Good Precepts. Namo to the Sugata of Golden Light. Namo to the Sugata Surpassing All in Love and Reverence. Namo to the Auspicious Cloud of the Lion Among Men. Namo to the Buddha. Namo to the Fearless One Who Alone Removes Darkness. Namo to the One of Boundless Benefit to the World. Namo to the Unequalled Teacher of the Three Realms of Existence. Namo to the One Who Can Sever All Fetters. Namo to the Foremost of the Selfless. Namo to the All-Illuminating Universal Eye. Namo to the One Whose Pure Wisdom-Eye Realizes Truth. Namo to the True Eye of the Three Wisdoms.

Such are the one hundred and eight names. If a person, in the early morning, gives rise to a sincere mind and either reads and recites them, or bows and contemplates them, or remembers and upholds them, or listens to them, that person will obtain the most supreme auspicious merit. All afflictions and transgressions will be swiftly purified. One will not be subject to the cycle of rebirth and shall attain liberation, even up to the attainment of buddhahood.

> These meritorious names of the Great Muni,
> I now read, recite, bow to, and contemplate.
> I universally dedicate this to all living beings,
> That together we may realize the fruit of bodhi.
