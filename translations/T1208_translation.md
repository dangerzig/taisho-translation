# 俱力迦羅龍王儀軌
## Ritual Manual for Kurikara Nāga King

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1208

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Vajrabodhi (金剛智).

---

This form manifests for those of the supreme, most excellent faculties, and it necessarily appears to practitioners who are bound to its constant contemplation. It is the practice-aspect of the three poisons and afflictions from the causal stage of Mahāvairocana Tathāgata.

At that time, Mahāvairocana Tathāgata, the Vajra Wrathful Lords, the devas, and others manifested the wrathful nāga king body for our practitioners, to frighten the three poisons and afflictions and cause them to forever leave the suffering karma of the three evil destinies and saṃsāra. Therefore this secret mantra shall be spoken.

Next, the mudrā and mantra for the manifestation of Kurikara Nāga King. Both hands form the wrathful gesture, raised and offered at both shoulders.

Next, the mudrā and mantra of Kurikara Nāga King as Acala Vidyārāja. Both hands in vajra añjali, the fire-fingers and space-fingers spread open, the two little fingers tucked into the palms.

The heart-within-the-heart seal: internal bond, the water-fingers and fire-fingers spread open.

The root true word for the manifestation of the Acala Tathāgata, Mahāvairocana, and Nāga King:

Reciting this true word, one will certainly manifest in person and be reborn in the pure lands of the ten directions. If one even beholds the honored countenance, until the attainment of anuttara-bodhi one will never fall into the three evil destinies. This nāga king possesses the supernatural power of the fire-light samādhi. The Fire Deva speaks through the four wisdoms. The holy Fire Deva's blazing radiance of the fire-light samādhi is entirely the capability of this fire-deva body. One should contemplate the inconceivable supernatural power of this nāga king.

The method of contemplating the original-birth dharma is as follows: transform Mahāvairocana into Acala, transform Acala into the syllable on the sword, which in turn transforms into the Nāga King. The mudrā: with the left and right ring fingers and little fingers interlocked within the palms, the two middle fingers stand upright touching at the tips. The two index fingers press against the backs of the upper joints of the middle fingers. The two thumbs, slightly bent, each press the middle joints of the index fingers, moving back and forth.

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ kurikāya-karaja-myōga-śaniye svāhā*

Next, the mudrā and mantra for the secret practice of Kurikara Nāga King: internal bond with both middle fingers, both thumbs spread open.

Next, the mudrā and mantra for Kurikara's transformation-body seal.

Both hands in añjali, the wind-fingers and space-fingers touching at the tips, the water-fingers standing apart.

Next, the vajra embodiment mantra and seal:

*Oṃ haṃ hūṃ vajra-maye vajra*

Next, the ocean samādhi seal and mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-kṣa*

Next, the ever-abiding fire-light samādhi seal and mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-kṣa haṃ*

If a practitioner constantly maintains contemplation of this Kurikara Nāga King, the fire-light samādhi of the homa will naturally be accomplished. The four-armed vidyārāja employs the power of this nāga king entirely. One should always distribute the syllable *A* upon one's body; within it is a sword that pervades the five syllables, and the entire body becomes the vajra body of Acalanātha. Having thus contemplated, recite the one-syllable true word:

*Namaḥ samanta-vajra-kṣa haṃ*

Next, the seal and mantra for Kiṃkara.

The seal: both hands in vajra añjali, the two middle fingers and two thumbs tucked into the palms. The mantra:

*Oṃ kiṃkara-na vajra*

Next, the seal and mantra for Ceṭaka.

Both hands in internal bond, the earth-fingers and wind-fingers spread open and slightly bent.

*Oṃ vajra-kṣa hūṃ ceṭakāya svāhā*

Next, the seal and mantra for Lotus Auspiciousness.

Both hands in añjali, the water-fingers and wind-fingers interlocked within.

*Namaḥ samanta vajra-kṣa hūṃ hūṃ haṃ ye-dhārāya svāhā*

These four attendants, together with Acala, make five attendants. This represents the five wisdoms of Mahāvairocana Tathāgata embodied in form.
