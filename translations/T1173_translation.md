# 金剛頂經曼殊室利菩薩五字心陀羅尼品
## Chapter on the Five-Syllable Heart Dhāraṇī of Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva from the Vajraśekhara Sūtra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1173

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Vajrabodhi (金剛智).

---

At that time, the Vajradhara Bodhisattva Mahāsattva and all the other bodhisattvas, each before Vairocana Buddha, proclaimed their own heart-dhāraṇī mudrās. Thereupon Mañjuśrī, Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, rose from his seat and addressed the Buddha, saying: "World-Honored One! I too, wishing to benefit all sentient beings of the future and enable them to swiftly accomplish mahāprajñāpāramitā, shall also teach a heart-dhāraṇī."

At that time the Buddha said to Mañjuśrī, Bodhisattva Mahāsattva: "Excellent, excellent! Good man! Now is the proper time. You should proclaim it."

At that time, Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva, receiving the Buddha's command, spoke the dhāraṇī:

*A ra pa ca na.*

"If any good man or good woman can uphold this dhāraṇī, they will enter the equality of all dharmas of the Tathāgata; all written characters will also become equal; and they will swiftly accomplish mahāprajñāpāramitā. A single recitation is equivalent to upholding all 84,000 sūtra-piṭaka collections.

"Those who wish to uphold it should first request entry into the abhiṣeka maṇḍala. The ācārya, on the fifteenth day of the bright fortnight, in a pure room, should spread a round altar and coat the ground with a paste of sandalwood and camphor incense. In the center of the altar, paint Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva in the form of a youth, with the right hand holding a vajra-jewel sword and the left hand holding a Sanskrit text of Mahāprajñā. Around the perimeter of the altar, write in Sanskrit the syllables *a ra pa ca na*. One should make offerings with one's whole heart, using various fine incenses and wonderful flowers. The ācārya should form the Vajra mudrā and recite according to the method. After performing the abhiṣeka for the disciple, one then transmits the Heart Dhāraṇī and instructs them to form the secret mudrā: with the Vajra Bond, raise both patience-vow fingers with the upper joints bent, place flowers atop the mudrā, then scatter them as an offering.

"One should then tell them: 'This heart dharma-gate is the essential secret of all tathāgatas. You must never carelessly explain it to others, lest you break your samaya. I shall now explain its meaning to you. Listen well and reflect carefully upon it.

"'*A* means "unborn": it is the meaning of non-arising. *Ra* means "pure and without defilement": it is the meaning of being free from dust and impurity. *Pa* means "also no ultimate truth": all dharmas are equal. *Ca* means "all dharmas are without activities." *Na* means "all dharmas are without nature or characteristics": words, letters, and language are all unattainable.

"'Because *na* is without nature or characteristics, *ca* is without activities. Because *ca* is without activities, *pa* is without ultimate truth. Because *pa* is without ultimate truth, *ra* is without dust or impurity. Because *ra* is without dust or impurity, *a* is the dharma that is fundamentally unborn. Because *a* is the dharma that is fundamentally unborn, *na* is without nature or characteristics.

"'If you understand this essential principle, you should contemplate this mind as originally pure, without any defilement, and free from the marks of discriminating self and what belongs to self. One who enters this gate is said to be in samādhi; this is true cultivation. Know that such a person is sealed with the Tathāgata's approval and possesses supreme merit.'

"Having received this dharma, one should recite at the altar four times daily, making offerings as described above, contemplating the heart-mudrā and entering samādhi. If one recites once, it eliminates all sufferings and hardships of the practitioner. If one recites twice, it eliminates the grave sins of birth and death accumulated over billions of kalpas. If one recites three times, samādhi manifests directly. If one recites four times, one attains total retention and never forgets. If one recites five times, one swiftly accomplishes unsurpassed bodhi.

"After single-mindedly reciting for a full month, Mañjuśrī will manifest his body, or within the meditation chamber will expound the essential Dharma. At that time the practitioner will attain the knowledge of former lives, unobstructed eloquence, sovereign spiritual powers, and the fulfillment of all superior vows, and will swiftly realize the vajra dharma-body of the Tathāgata.

"Alternatively, one may paint the image as described above on silk and complete 500,000 recitations; this also achieves accomplishment. Or one may coat a śarīra stūpa with fragrant paste, write the five syllables in Sanskrit, circumambulate while reciting 500,000 times, and Mañjuśrī will appear before that person and teach the Dharma. One will constantly receive the protection and mindfulness of all buddhas and Vajradhara bodhisattvas, and all superior vows will be completely fulfilled."

"The essential practice method for the Heart Dhāraṇī of Mañjuśrī: whenever a practitioner enters a temple, first bow to the bodhisattva at the eastern gate, then bow at the southern gate, and so on through the northern gate in the same manner. Having entered the temple, face west toward the bodhisattva, prostrate with the five limbs touching the ground, and take refuge with a single mind. Then, holding an incense burner in hand or offering fine flowers, extend the mind to make offerings to all buddhas. Gazing up at the bodhisattva with a mind of joyful delight, confess all one's own sins and offenses, and earnestly repent. Next, praise the merits of the Tathāgata, circumambulate seven times while reciting twenty times, then kneel and make great vows: 'May I, from this day forward, having heard the dharma-gate of the heart-ground, vow never to regress from unsurpassed bodhi, to broadly liberate all sentient beings, and to be the same as Mañjuśrī's great compassionate vows of practice.' Having made this aspiration, one sits in half-lotus, letting body and mind be relaxed and peaceful in the joy of meditation. Then, with paste-incense, purify both hands, invite the three families, and above and below in the eight directions, establish the Vajra Boundary."

"Vajra Flame Ground Boundary Dhāraṇī Mudrā:

"Insert the patience finger into the space between the power and vow fingers, the precept finger into the space between the wisdom and power fingers, and the vow finger from behind into the space between the progress and patience fingers. Insert the skillful-means finger between the charity and precept fingers. The tips of the charity-wisdom, progress-power, and concentration-knowledge fingers each touch, turned downward, with the concentration-knowledge fingers pressing the ground like stakes. Recite the dhāraṇī three times. Visualize a single-pronged vajra with blazing flames, the vajra-stake piercing down to the vajra boundary. The dhāraṇī:

*Oṃ kiri kiri vajra vajrī bhūḥ bandha bandha hūṃ phaṭ.*

"Vajra Flame Enclosure Boundary Dhāraṇī Mudrā:

"Based on the previous ground mudrā, open the concentration-knowledge fingers and rotate to the right through the eight directions. Recite the dhāraṇī three times. The distance may be near or far as wished. Visualize a vajra-fire fortress with flying flames spiraling like lightning. The dhāraṇī:

*Oṃ sara sara vajra-prakara hūṃ phaṭ.*

"Vajra Flame Net Boundary Dhāraṇī Mudrā:

"Also based on the previous mudrā: press the concentration-knowledge fingers against the lower sides of the progress-power fingers, rotate above the crown to the right, and recite the dhāraṇī three times. Visualize a vajra flame net extending upward to the Akaniṣṭha heaven. The dhāraṇī:

*Oṃ viṣphurad-rakṣa vajra-pañjara hūṃ phaṭ.*

"One who establishes these boundaries will cause the māras of the six desire realms and all vināyakas to flee in terror with no place to hide."

"Next, the Yoga Samaya Dhāraṇī Mudrā:

"Merit and wisdom complete, the ten pāramitās in harmonious union and firm. Raise the patience-vow fingers and place them upon the heart. The dhāraṇī:

*Oṃ samaya sattvaṃ.*

"Having performed this method, all buddhas, remembering their former vows, observe and give their protective mindfulness."

"Opening the Heart-Ground Gate Dhāraṇī Mudrā:

"After forming the firm bond, visualize the syllable *trāṭ* on the right breast and the syllable *ṭa* on the left breast. With heart and mouth in correspondence, recite the dhāraṇī, then simultaneously extend all ten fingers and flick them upon the heart, splitting open the two syllables like opening the two wings of a door, thereby opening the heart. The dhāraṇī:

*Oṃ vajra bandha trāṭ.*

"One who performs this method will be able to awaken to the dharma-gate of the heart-ground and will soon realize all samādhis."

"Entry of Wisdom Syllable Dhāraṇī Mudrā:

"Furthermore, before oneself visualize a lotus of ruby-red color. Within it is the syllable *a*, its light brilliant and blazing like a white maṇi jewel. When one can see it clearly, form the firm bond with the concentration-knowledge fingers inserted in the center and the progress-power fingers touching like a ring. Visualize grasping that syllable and placing it within the heart. The dhāraṇī:

*Oṃ vajra āveśa aḥ.*

"Why is this so? This syllable *aḥ* is the meaning of the serene wisdom of all tathāgatas. It also exists within the mental activities of all sentient beings, but has not yet been manifested. Now, through the skillful empowerment of the Tathāgata's wisdom, it is illuminated within. Therefore the practitioner should earnestly generate the thought that this is rare and difficult to encounter, and cultivate according to the method."

"Closing the Wisdom Syllable Dhāraṇī Mudrā:

"Based on the previous entry mudrā, only bend the progress-power fingers to press against the backs of the concentration-knowledge fingers. Recite the dhāraṇī and, with the mudrā at the heart, visualize closing a door. The dhāraṇī:

*Oṃ vajra muṣṭi vaṃ.*

"One who performs this method, having obtained the serene wisdom of the Tathāgata, earnestly guards it in secrecy. Know that such a practitioner will swiftly realize the path of serene bodhi."

"Samādhi Gate Dhāraṇī Mudrā:

"Both hands interlaced outwardly, placed facing up below the navel. Sit upright with correct intention, ceasing all mental grasping. Each in-breath and out-breath should be distinctly clear. Contemplate the immeasurable buddhas throughout space, complete with marks and excellent features, each as small as a sesame seed, as numerous as motes of dust, pervading the entire dharma-realm. One should, before each and every buddha, prostrate with the five limbs and take refuge with a single mind. The dhāraṇī:

*Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-pāda-vandanāṃ karomi.*

"At that time, the Buddha, before the practitioner, simultaneously snaps his fingers to alert and awaken the practitioner, telling them: 'Good man! You who have generated the bodhicitta, you should now contemplate your own mind.' And he speaks the dhāraṇī:

*Oṃ citta-prativedhaṃ karomi.*

"At that time, having received this teaching, the practitioner leaps with joy and prostrates before all buddhas. Reciting the secret mantra, one contemplates the *aḥ* syllable placed within the heart: it is like a full moon, not yet fully manifested, as if veiled by a light mist. Having performed this contemplation in a single moment of thought, one addresses all the buddhas, saying: 'I have seen the mind; it is like the moon, but not yet clear. May the World-Honored Ones compassionately grant skillful means.'

"At that time all the buddhas praise in unison: 'Excellent, excellent! Good man! Just so, just so! We shall now empower you with this dhāraṇī to make it clearly manifest.'

*Oṃ bodhicitta-utpādayāmi.*

"Having recited this secret mantra and again contemplated until the heart-moon is extremely bright and pure, within that moon one visualizes Mañjuśrī's one-syllable dhāraṇī.

"The syllable *dhaṃ*, golden in color, transforms into the Fierce Vajra-Jewel Sword, its radiance illuminating the ten directions. Entering this samādhi, one then recites the dhāraṇī:

*Oṃ tiṣṭha vajra-tīkṣṇa dhaṃ.*

"When the moon and sword are extremely clear and distinct, gradually expand them to pervade the entire dharma-realm, their measure equal to space, purely one without mixture, without any marks of self, other, or anything else. This sword is one's own body; the contemplating mind is within the sword, also forming one body. When entering this samādhi, the dhāraṇī:

*Oṃ sphara vajra-tīkṣṇa.*

"Abide as long as one's strength allows. Then again contemplate the sword gradually contracting, and the buddhas throughout space follow it inward. It returns to the size of one's own body and stops, shining brightly. When entering this samādhi, the dhāraṇī:

*Oṃ saṃhara vajra-tīkṣṇa.*

"Because all tathāgatas have entered the body-sword, through the power of empowerment, one's own body immediately transforms into Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva: body of purple-gold color, five topknots upon the head, circular halo behind head and back, left hand holding a blue lotus, right hand holding the Vajragarbha Sanskrit text. The practitioner, having become the bodhisattva, fearing that the mind might scatter and regress, further empowers with the dhāraṇī mudrā.

"Bodhisattva Three-Activity Dhāraṇī Mudrā:

"After forming the firm bond, raise the patience-vow fingers straight with the upper joints bent. The dhāraṇī:

*Oṃ mukhā-cinna dhaṃ.*

"Stamp the mudrā upon the heart, then the forehead, then the throat, then place it on the crown, reciting once at each. After this empowerment, even if the mind scatters, the original form does not change. All non-human beings who see the practitioner see them as identical to Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva, without any difference."

"Five-Topknot Dhāraṇī Mudrā:

"All ten fingers interlaced: the precept-wisdom and charity fingers in their proper positions, the patience-power and vow-progress fingers each touching at the tips, and the concentration-knowledge fingers standing upright together. Recite the dhāraṇī and stamp upon the heart, then right, then left, then shoulder, then throat, and place upon the crown, reciting once at each. Having performed this, the five directional tathāgatas are all present atop the five topknots. The dhāraṇī:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ apratihatā-śāsanānāṃ. Tadyathā: oṃ rara smara apratihata-śāsana kumāra-rūpa-dhāriṇī hūṃ hūṃ saphaṭ svāhā.*

"Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva Abhiṣeka Dhāraṇī Mudrā:

"Merit and wisdom complete, with the concentration-knowledge fingers inserted in the center and the progress-power fingers pressed together like a maṇi jewel, place upon the forehead. The dhāraṇī:

*Oṃ ratna-kuśa āghriya hūṃ.*

"Jewel-Garland Dhāraṇī Mudrā:

"After the abhiṣeka, open the mudrā into two parts. The dhāraṇī:

*Oṃ ratna-kuśa āghriya māle.*

"Circle the forehead three times like tying a jeweled garland, then separate the hands and circle behind the head three times as well, then bring forward and down, releasing from the charity-wisdom fingers like trailing ribbons."

"Compassion Vajra Armor Dhāraṇī Mudrā:

"With both wisdom fingers firm, cross the progress-power fingers sideways. On the face of the progress finger, visualize the syllable *oṃ*; on the face of the power finger, visualize a middle syllable. They emit green light, unbroken like drawing lotus fibers. Circle the heart three times, then the back three times, then above the navel to behind the waist, then above the crossed legs to behind the seat, then return to the chest and again to the back, then to the throat and behind the neck, then to the forehead and behind the crown. After three circlings at each point, bring forward and down, releasing from the charity-wisdom fingers like trailing celestial garments."

"First, at the heart of the painted image within the altar, visualize the syllable *dhaṃ* as a vajra sword, which transforms into the bodhisattva's true body, and then reinvite it to enter within the image."

"Inviting the Bodhisattva, Vajra Hook Dhāraṇī Mudrā:

"With both wisdom fingers firm, place the contemplation hand upon the calming hand, hook the charity-wisdom fingers together, raise the power finger straight, and bend the progress finger like a hook. The dhāraṇī:

*Oṃ vajrāṅkuśa jaḥ.*

"Recite three times with the progress finger beckoning three times; the bodhisattva's true body arrives in response to one's thought."

"Vajra Lasso Dhāraṇī Mudrā:

"Based on the previous inviting mudrā, only bend the progress-power fingers to touch like a ring. The dhāraṇī:

*Oṃ vajrapāśa hūṃ.*

"Form at the heart, recite three times, and visualize the bodhisattva's dharma-body entering the painted image."

"Vajra Chain Dhāraṇī Mudrā:

"With both wisdom fingers firm, hook the progress-power fingers with the right pressing over the left, pressing against the backs of the concentration-knowledge fingers at the middle joint. The dhāraṇī:

*Oṃ vajrasphota vaṃ.*

"One who performs this method secures the holy being's own body with empowerment so it does not scatter."

"Vajra Bell Dhāraṇī Mudrā:

"Based on the previous chain mudrā, reverse-hook the progress-power and charity-wisdom fingers. The dhāraṇī:

*Oṃ vajraghaṇṭā hoḥ.*

"One who performs this method causes all buddhas, bodhisattvas, and the presiding holy being to be filled with delight."

"Arghya Water Offering Dhāraṇī Mudrā:

"Fill an arghya vessel with water perfumed with saffron, camphor, and white sandalwood. Open the Buddha-family mudrā and lift it in offering. The dhāraṇī:

*Oṃ vajra-nāda-gātha.*

"One who makes this offering is as if using the vajra ambrosia of all tathāgatas to anoint the crowns of all sentient beings, eliminating immeasurable karmic obstacles. Those who drink this water are freed from all calamities."

"Hundred-Syllable Dhāraṇī Mudrā:

"Form the previous Sword mudrā. The dhāraṇī:

*Oṃ ghaghā-sattva-samaya-manu-pālaya ghaghā-sattva-tvenopatiṣṭha dṛḍho me bhava sutoṣyo me bhava anurakto me bhava supoṣyo me bhava sarva-siddhiṃ me prayaccha sarva-karmasu ca me citta-śreyaḥ kuru hūṃ ha ha ha ha hoḥ bhagavān sarva-tathāgata-ghagha mā me muñca ghaghī me bhava mahā-samaya-sattva aḥ.*

"Reciting this dhāraṇī causes the holy being to rejoice, strengthens bodhi, and swiftly accomplishes all superior vows."

"Vajra Play Inner Offering Dhāraṇī Mudrā:

"After forming the firm bond, raise the concentration-knowledge fingers straight and place the mudrā at the heart. The dhāraṇī:

*Oṃ mahārati.*

"One who performs this method offers the wisdom of all tathāgatas to all buddhas as play."

"Vajra Garland Inner Offering Dhāraṇī Mudrā:

"With the previous mudrā, extend the arms forward as if lifting and offering a garland. The dhāraṇī:

*Oṃ rūpa-śubhe.*

"One who performs this method offers as if with bodhi flower-garlands."

"Vajra Song Inner Offering Dhāraṇī Mudrā:

"Based on the previous mudrā, raise from the navel upward to the mouth and then scatter, as if song were issuing forth. The dhāraṇī:

*Oṃ śurutra-sāṃgīye.*

"One who performs this method offers with the secret words of all tathāgatas as song."

"Vajra Dance Inner Offering Dhāraṇī Mudrā:

"Based on the previous mudrā, visualize as if singing, and at the mouth scatter. Rotate to the right with palms joined, and scatter above the crown. The dhāraṇī:

*Oṃ sarva-pūjye.*

"One who performs this method offers with the eloquence of all tathāgatas."

"Vajra Incense Dhāraṇī Mudrā:

"With the firm bond, scatter downward toward the ground, visualizing as if burning incense. The dhāraṇī:

*Oṃ vajra-dhūpe.*

"One who performs this method offers as if burning all the sublime fragrances of the world. This enables all sentient beings to attain the fruit of coolness."

"Vajra Flower Dhāraṇī Mudrā:

"With the firm bond, scatter upward as if scattering flowers. The dhāraṇī:

*Oṃ vajra-puṣpe.*

"One who performs this method offers as if with all the sublime flowers of the world. This enables all sentient beings to swiftly attain the complete thirty-two marks."

"Vajra Lamp Dhāraṇī Mudrā:

"As with the play mudrā, quickly press the concentration-knowledge fingers. The dhāraṇī:

*Oṃ vajra-ruke.*

"One who performs this method offers with the wisdom-lamp of all tathāgatas. This enables sentient beings to swiftly accomplish the wisdom of the Tathāgata."

"Vajra Perfume Dhāraṇī Mudrā:

"With the firm bond, scatter toward the heart. The dhāraṇī:

*Oṃ vajra-gandhe.*

"One who performs this method offers with the fragrance of śīla-wisdom. This enables all sentient beings to swiftly attain a pure precept-body."

"Having completed the eight offerings, place both hands interlaced face-up below the navel. Attentively contemplate the bodhisattva: the five-syllable dhāraṇī, in five-colored radiance, issues from his mouth and enters the heart-moon of the practitioner. The syllable *a* is placed in front, and the remaining four syllables are arranged clockwise in sequence. One by one, contemplate the meaning of the five syllables: this is called samādhi recitation.

"For vajra recitation, visualize the syllables as before, firmly close the mouth and teeth, and let the tongue move slightly.

"For spoken recitation, also visualize each syllable's form within the heart, turning according to the syllables, neither too slowly nor too quickly, so that only oneself can hear.

"Form the previous Sword mudrā, recite seven times, then lift the bodhi-rosary to the heart and recite. Every day during the four periods, do not allow any interval to be missed. Each period, recite one thousand times, or two thousand, or five hundred, or three hundred, or at minimum one hundred and eight; do not allow fewer than this. If the body becomes exhausted, think of the beings in the evil destinies and redouble one's diligence, practicing loving-kindness, compassion, sympathetic joy, and equanimity.

"Cultivating in this way, know that the practitioner fulfills the six pāramitās, realizes all the samādhis of the tathāgatas, and constantly has Mañjuśrī and all bodhisattvas as companions. The supreme cautionary instructions for the higher levels are difficult to state in advance; all practitioners should realize and awaken to them themselves.

"To state it briefly: through diligent cultivation and practice, in this very life one will realize the first bhūmi, and after sixteen subsequent births one will accomplish anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi. Therefore practitioners should respectfully honor this teaching.

"When one wishes to stop, there are two methods: first, dismissal; second, summoning the bodhisattva to enter one's own body. For dismissal, perform each of the eight offerings as before, then with the Sword mudrā recite the dhāraṇī:

*Oṃ vajra-tīkṣṇa muḥ.*

"This constitutes the dismissal.

"For summoning the bodhisattva, after performing the four attractions to draw the bodhisattva into one's own body, again make offerings with the eight mudrās, don the vajra armor, and again recite the Samaya Dhāraṇī. Then, abiding in the four dignified postures, going wherever one pleases, all sentient beings, human and non-human alike, who approach the practitioner, hearing the sound and seeing the form, will gain the same merit as if they had personally attended Mañjuśrī. The benefits are beyond calculation; the finest things of the world will come unsought.

"When one sees anyone to whom one should pay respect, one should visualize a tathāgata form atop that person's head, and only then bow or kneel. If one does not do so, one harms that sentient being and furthermore breaks one's own samaya precept.

"If one enters an impure place and wishes to release the body visualization, one should first visualize the bodhisattva entering into candana."
