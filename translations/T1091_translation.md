# 七星如意輪祕密要經
## Secret Essential Sūtra of the Seven Stars and the Wish-Fulfilling Wheel

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1091

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

Thus have I heard. At one time the Buddha was at the great Snow Mountain, in the Kāya Nālandā, together with a great assembly of twelve hundred and fifty bhikṣus. Their names included: Mahāmaudgalyāyana, Śāriputra, Mahākātyāyana Bhadra, Ānanda, Mahākāśyapa, Pūrṇa Maitrāyaṇīputra. There were also eighteen thousand bodhisattvas, all of whom had attained the dhāraṇī eloquence of non-retrogression. Among such assemblies, as numerous as the sands of the Ganges, the great dharma-wheel was being turned.

At that time a messenger from within the palace of the city of Kāśī came to where the Buddha was, bowed at the Buddha's feet, and addressed the Buddha: "King Prasenajit has sent me to make inquiry. The great nation of Kuśīla has advanced with an army of innumerable millions to surround Kāśī. The king and ministers are in turmoil, and the people are in terror. We beseech the World-Honored One, through his great wisdom-power, to rescue and protect us."

At that time the World-Honored One told Devariddhi-rāja Bodhisattva: "Go to the palace within the city of Kāśī and swiftly establish the Wish-Fulfilling Jewel Wheel Pantara Sanctuary and the Seven-Star Fire-Altar Secret Gate. The so-called Pantara Sanctuary is constructed in a clean room; the Seven-Star Fire-Altar is set up in the open air. If performed for one day and one night according to the method, the enemy forces will naturally retreat and scatter.

"As for the ritual: one must first seal the boundary, then perform the Pantara Dharma-Gate: 'Homage to the Wish-Fulfilling Wheel-King, Great Dharma-Master! I now reverently construct the Pantara. May those with celestial ears and eyes, those with spiritual powers, witness and verify this rite.' Then recite the Wish-Fulfilling Wheel mantra seven times, empower water with the spell seven times, and sprinkle it on the ground, taking the boundary in all directions to complete the seal.

"The so-called Pantara is a supremely clean room with ground as level as a mirror. In the center, construct a five-colored wheel-image like a cartwheel, with ten or twelve spokes. Between the spokes, paint vajra patterns, and upon the spokes likewise. At the center, install the Wish-Fulfilling Wheel-King Bodhisattva. In each space between the spokes, install images of the seven stars and Hārītī. Before each image, set out lamps, ghee, incense, and various offerings. Write the name of the person for whom the rite is performed upon their natal star. The ācārya sits outside the wheel and initiates the ritual procedures one by one for accomplishment. For the empowered recitation, one hundred and eight times per sitting. If contemplation does not succeed, do not count that session.

"As for the fire-altar: in the open air, construct a fire-pit, one or two cubits in size. Empower one person, either an upāsaka or an upāsikā, who should wear fresh clean garments. Prepare the natal-star food offerings, cakes, ghee, honey, incense, and medicines. Then face the seven stars, bowing once, burning one offering, and concentrating the mind upon the natal star. If movement, changes, or light appear, accomplishment is attained. If a pure ācārya performs this, whether for a king, a minister, or even the common people, performing their own fire-altar rites and making requests, all their wishes shall be accomplished as desired."

At that time Devariddhi-rāja Bodhisattva received the command and went to the palace within the great city of Kāśī, performing every part of the ritual. Thunderbolts and lightning crashed, and the enemy forces scattered and were destroyed. The king and ministers rejoiced, and the people were at peace and happy.

Then Devariddhi-rāja Bodhisattva returned to where the Buddha was, bowed at the Buddha's feet, and addressed the Buddha: "How extraordinary, World-Honored One! What an inconceivable secret dharma-gate! Where now is the Wish-Fulfilling Wheel-King Bodhisattva? We and our assembly, sustained by the Tathāgata's power, wish to go to that world and pay homage to the Wish-Fulfilling Wheel-King Bodhisattva."

The Buddha told Devariddhi-rāja Bodhisattva: "Excellent, excellent! In the true south there is a world called the Seven-Treasure Cakravartin King. Upon a seven-jeweled wheel, the dharma-protecting seven stars and all the celestial assembly surround him, turning the dharma-wheel to liberate sentient beings."

Then Devariddhi-rāja Bodhisattva addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One, for what reason does one not simply contemplate the Wish-Fulfilling Wheel-King Bodhisattva, but also make homage to the natal stars?"

The Buddha said: "Long ago, in the presence of Dīpaṃkara Buddha, I received this dharma-gate. The star-spirits descended from heaven, and the great deity Hārītī emerged from the earth, saying: 'May we protect this great dharma. If any person reverently performs this rite, we shall arrive first to accomplish the ritual.' For this reason, the construction of the rite is also preserved."

Then Devariddhi-rāja Bodhisattva, sustained by the Buddha's great spiritual power, went to that world and paid homage to the Wish-Fulfilling Wheel-King Bodhisattva, speaking these words:

> "We dwell in the sahā world,
> Studying the causal gate of the Tathāgata.
> Having rescued King Prasenajit of Kāśī,
> By this connection we pay homage to the Benevolent One.

> Adorned with seven jewels, the Jewel-Wheel King,
> The bodhisattva's marks and excellences are inconceivable.
> Both the dependent and direct rewards are supremely wondrous.
> I now reverently bow to the Wish-Fulfilling Wheel."

Then the Wish-Fulfilling Wheel Bodhisattva spoke in verse:

> "The bodhisattva path I now cultivate
> Is to liberate beings from the suffering of the long night.
> Now the adornment of the Wish-Fulfilling Wheel seat
> Causes all sentient beings to be likewise.

> If there are sentient beings in the world with wishes,
> I, the Wish-Fulfilling Jewel-Wheel King,
> According to the various joys they seek,
> Fulfill all from the heart, as wished.

> If there are sentient beings in the world
> Who wish to penetrate the unconditioned path,
> Whose karmic obstructions are deep and thick, preventing accomplishment,
> I, with the Jewel Wheel, cause them to awaken.

> If in any world the three calamities arise,
> And the lands and peoples have no peace,
> If anyone among them performs my rite,
> The Wish-Fulfilling Holy Wheel grants great bliss.

> If in any world enemies from other lands
> Press from four sides, disturbing the peace,
> If anyone in that land performs my rite,
> The Wish-Fulfilling Holy Wheel grants peace and happiness.

> If in any land there is famine and hardship
> And all the people have no joy,
> If anyone performs my Pantara rite,
> The Wish-Fulfilling Holy Wheel can bring relief.

> If any sentient being in the world
> Is punished by royal law and imprisoned,
> By performing my great Pantara rite,
> The Wish-Fulfilling Holy Wheel can bring relief.

> If any sentient being is in peril,
> The Wish-Fulfilling Holy Wheel can bring relief.

> If any sentient being in the world
> Is blind, deaf, lame, or afflicted with various diseases,
> By recollecting my great Pantara rite,
> The Wish-Fulfilling Holy Wheel can bring relief.

> If any sentient being in the world
> Wishes to enrich their household and seek prosperity,
> By performing my great Pantara rite,
> The Wish-Fulfilling Holy Wheel fulfills their wish.

> If there is such a Wish-Fulfilling Wheel,
> There is no karmic obstruction it cannot remove,
> No auspiciousness it cannot bring about,
> No place it cannot reach to relieve suffering.

> Among all devas, nāga-spirits, and humankind,
> All sentient beings have a refuge.
> He does not go against their hearts to speak falsely.
> This dharma can remove all transgressions.

> It increases all meritorious virtues,
> With sovereign mastery illuminating the five destinies,
> And fulfills the wishes of all sentient beings."

Then Devariddhi-rāja Bodhisattva paid homage to the Holy Wheel, returned to his original place, and with single-minded devotion received and upheld this inconceivable Mahā-Pantara Secret Dharma-Gate Mantra:

*Namo ārajinā ā ānāṃ ārya-avalokiteśvarāya bodhisattvāya mahā-kāruṇikāya tadyathā oṃ cakara-vatī cintā-maṇi mahā-padme ruru tī cakara āsa hūṃ rī svāhā oṃ padme cintā-maṇi mahā cakara ā hūṃ oṃ varada padme hūṃ*

The Seven-Star Root-Division mudrā is the Wish-Fulfilling Wheel root mudrā for sustained recitation. If a person has a wish in their heart, or has illness and difficulty, and seeks to extend life or gain prosperity, down to whatever the heart desires, one should appeal to one's natal star, which will assuredly respond to one's heart.

The Tathāgata, while dwelling in the world, made a great vow that for this division the fire offering should be performed. For this reason the seven stars are used, as they have proven spiritual efficacy. This is most secret and must not be transmitted externally. Whether for a king or a great minister, one may perform this rite, but it must not be performed by or for common people or those of shallow understanding.

[Correspondences of zodiacal directions to wood-types and mantras follow for Horse (south), Goat-Snake (south-southwest), Monkey-Dragon (west-southeast), Rooster-Hare (west-east), Dog-Tiger (northwest-northeast), Pig-Ox (north-northeast), and Rat (north).]
