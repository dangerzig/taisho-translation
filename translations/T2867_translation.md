# 慈仁問八十種好經
## Sūtra on Ciren's Question about the Eighty Excellent Marks

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 2867

Translated from the Chinese. Author unknown.

---

Ciren asked the Buddha: "Through what causes and conditions were the eighty excellent marks acquired?"

The Buddha said:

(1) Because in life after life he treated people with an impartial mind, his feet tread the ground with flat soles, and marks appear.

(2) Because whenever people wished for something, he gave it to them and brought them peace, his feet have fine and beautiful distinguishing marks.

(3) Because he harmonized with people and caused all who saw him to rejoice, the patterns beneath his feet were exquisite, and those who received his sūtras all gained clear understanding.

(4) Because he cleared and leveled mounds, walls, and uneven ground and removed thorns, the soles of his feet are moist and lustrous, and he always walks in peace and stability.

(5) Because he never harbored resentment toward others and never caused others to quarrel, the muscles of his feet are fine and soft, and he walks well without limit.

(6) Because whenever he saw deep pits and muddy water, he always had bridges built, his steps are flat and secure.

(7) Because he held thousands of tens of thousands of kinds of articles, arrayed them along the path, and gave them in charity, the wheel-spokes beneath his feet form rows when he teaches the sūtras.

(8) Because wherever he went he always benefited the lifespans of all people under heaven, the wheel-spokes are properly aligned, and he naturally attains great honor.

(9) Because wherever he went he gave profound sūtras, teaching both himself and others, the feet-wheels are all deeply marked.

(10) Because wherever he went he was gentle and benevolent, treating people as old friends, the patterns beneath his feet do not collect dust and are soft.

(11) Because he did not enumerate others' faults or cause others to be resentful, the soles of his feet are entirely straight, with wholesome intent directed to the ten directions.

(12) Because wherever he went he taught people in proper sequence without laziness, all his toe-joints are straight and upright.

(13) Because he praised and recited the merit of the buddhas, his toes are transparent and beautiful.

(14) Because along the public roads he planted trees, established wells, and built bathing pools, his fame became widespread.

(15) Because he resolved people's doubts and difficulties without holding anything back, his nails are the color of red copper.

(16) Because in all his undertakings he always saw them through to completion, and because he did not separate from those who loved and valued him, his toes are well-formed, and he is born into a family of good lineage, attaining buddhahood that none can surpass.

(17) Because he upheld the precepts without wavering, the spaces between his toes are smooth, and he knows all that people do.

(18) Because he upheld the precepts without wishing to be born in heaven, his toes are even.

(19) Because in his clan he reformed people who did evil, his heels are level, and he knows people's thoughts.

(20) Because he was not covetous and immediately explained whatever he was asked, he attained unsurpassed wisdom.

(21) Because he guarded himself and was diligent, his heels are moist, lustrous, and beautiful, and he knows whenever people harbor evil intent.

(22) Because he was utterly sincere, his heels are unshackled, and he knows what people think in their hearts.

(23) Because he wished that people would come forward to learn the Buddha's sūtras and all delighted in asking about them, his bodily strength is such that none can break it.

(24) Because he gave sūtras to people, his body is complete, and when he teaches the sūtras, they are accurate and without error.

(25) Because when people were teaching sūtras he never stopped them, and when he transmitted sūtras to people they were always complete, his body is broad and full. When he teaches the sūtras, all creatures that move are able to hear and know.

(26) Because when people had doubts, he used the three piṭakas of the Buddha to explain for them, he is surpassingly correct, noble, and excellent.

(27) Because he did not injure or damage people's bodies or minds and did not mock them in appearance, he obtained the eighty excellent marks, and all who see him are filled with joy.

(28) Because when he saw women who were ill, he treated them with the mind of wisdom, his body is upright, dignified, and without compare.

(29) Because he bathed and washed people's bodies and eliminated the three defilements, his body is pure and clean, its light illuminating the ten directions.

(30) Because when he saw the arrogant and disrespectful, he always respectfully corrected them, his body is soft and moist.

(31) Because he did not act as a matchmaker or scheme against people, his face is free of wrinkles and he has attained fearlessness.

(32) Because when he saw the sick, he gave them medicine and restored their health, he is free from worry.

(33) Because when he saw people of poor appearance, he did not mock them but only praised their good qualities, upon becoming a buddha, his body is moist, pure, and clean.

(34) Because he encouraged and delighted people in performing merit and doing good, his body delivers beings from evil places.

(35) Because when he saw the weary, hungry, and thirsty, he inquired after them and massaged them, his back is broad and fine, and he also heals the illness of people's craving.

(36) Because when he saw people doing good, he rejoiced and exalted them, his two shoulder-blades are level above and below, and he knows all people's thoughts.

(37) Because he followed what people wished, giving them precepts and causing them to be patient and control their minds, his two shoulder-blades are transparent and fine, and he teaches the sūtras in proper sequence.

(38) Because when he saw women in difficult childbirth, he assisted them with a compassionate heart and caused them to give birth safely, his navel is deep and fine, and he delivers people from the root of love and attachment.

(39) Because he was never angry, never erred in his actions, and taught those who were disrespectful, his navel is round and prominent.

(40) Because he used the precepts of his teacher to teach himself and also to teach others, the patterns of his navel are orderly and in accord, and he makes offerings.

(41) Because he himself did not commit sexual misconduct and also kept others from it, from below the navel downward there is no foul discharge within the abdomen.

(42) Because when people's minds were joyful and at ease, he did not disturb their peace and did not involve himself in mundane affairs, the marks on his body are like divine adamant that none can destroy.

(43) Because with a compassionate mind he thought of all people under heaven equally, his two shoulder-bones are full and rounded.

(44) Because his thoughts were vast and broad, he taught people to do good and be full and complete, his two shoulders are broad and great, and his knowledge is immeasurably deep.

(45) Because his wisdom was courageous and firm and he held his mind without wavering, his two arms are transparently long, with ten-thousand-character marks at his fingertips.

(46) Because he did not speak of deceptive, contemptuous, or lewd matters, his lips and mouth are like true cinnabar, without any foul odor or discharge.

(47) Because he did not obstruct people's understanding and had no anger, flattery, or guile in his mind, his mouth is neither too large nor too small, perfectly proportioned, as if containing a smile.

(48) Because he gave people food of five flavors and the five medicines, his tongue is without blemish.

(49) Because he did not mock or curse people, all who hear his words are delighted and gain understanding.

(50) Because he upheld the precepts and practiced contemplation and did not speak about women, his teeth are round, fine, and firm, and with the power of concentration he destroys desire.

(51) Because he analyzed and resolved people's doubts and difficulties, his teeth are firm and sharp, and desire is scattered and destroyed.

(52) Because when people asked about one thing, he explained many matters for them, his teeth are even, square, and sharp, and he subdues the non-Buddhists in sequence.

(53) Because he mixed fragrant unguents and placed them in people's nostrils, his nose is extraordinarily fine, and he never smells foul odors but only fragrance.

(54) Because when he saw heaps of refuse, he cleared and disposed of them, his light is pure and clean, and he regards people with beautiful eyes.

(55) Because in life after life he made buddha-images, his eyes are long and beautiful, and those who study his sūtras all gain clear understanding.

(56) Because in life after life he gazed upon people with a pure mind, his eyes are as if containing a smile, and whatever he looks upon, he sees.

(57) Because in life after life when he saw people of wealth and status, his mind did not covet or desire, his eyes are neither too large nor too small, perfectly clear and proportioned, never squinting.

(58) Because in life after life he put people at ease with a wholesome and tranquil mind, his eyes are free from rheum and film, and he beholds the heavens above and below.

(59) Because in life after life he did not falsely recognize, and because his bright eyes did not mock or ogle women, his brows and eyes are beautiful and bright, and wherever he goes, he is a teacher among people.

(60) Because in life after life he was mindful of his future lives and also mindful of others' good, his brows are fine, like half-moons, and he sees all things in the ten directions.

(61) Because in life after life he was never angry or discolored, his brows are lustrous.

(62) Because in life after life he treated enemies and kin with an equal mind, his brows are soft and moist.

(63) Because in life after life he delighted in serving wise teachers and wished to understand the profound sūtras of the later, wondrous teaching, his brows are of a deep blue-black color.

(64) Because in life after life when he saw people teaching the sūtras, he did not seek out faults and tell others, his ears are open and penetrating, hearing even the heavens above.

(65) Because in life after life he did not pull people's ears or seize their ears, hands, or feet, his ears are neither too large nor too small, perfectly proportioned, moist and fine, and do not cling to sounds.

(66) Because in life after life when he saw those practicing the Way and guarding themselves, he did not dare to startle or frighten them, he remembers all things without forgetting.

(67) Because in life after life the sūtras and dharmas he heard were never increased or diminished in their meaning, his forehead is six inches long, and his spiritual authority is manifest.

(68) Because in life after life his teaching and instruction followed proper sequence, his hair follows orderly lines, moist and fine, and does not collect dust or impurity.

(69) Because in life after life when speaking with people, he always showed a harmonious countenance and pleasing face, never cursing or speaking harshly, his hair is fine and beautiful beyond compare.

(70) Because in life after life he reverently served his teachers and wise ones and those who uphold the precepts, giving them ghee, sesame oil, and clothing, his hair is moist, lustrous, and beautiful.

(71) Because in life after life he taught and instructed people in various merits, his hair is evenly distributed without gaps, and all who see it are delighted.

(72) Because in life after life when he saw people's rights and wrongs, he did not spread gossip about their good or evil, filth and dust do not cling to him, and he does not see their faults.

(73) Because in life after life he did not extinguish the lamps before the buddhas or before people, dust and impurity do not cling to his hair, and it radiates light.

(74) Because in life after life with a good mind he gave people caps, sable ornaments, hair-coverings, and mats, his hair-whorls generate spiritual authority, and those who see them never tire.

(75) Because he spread his hair for his parents and teachers to tread upon, his hair-whorls are visible, and he knows the thoughts of people in the ten directions.

(76) Because in life after life when people wished for precious treasures, he always kindly gave to them, his hair is evenly distributed, and all his wishes are fulfilled.

(77) Because in life after life he wished to cause people to keep the precepts and never demanded things from them, constantly wishing to serve good teachers and those learned in the sūtras, his hair-whorls arise in patterns between them like a vase, and his majestic voice is heard in the ten directions.

(78) Because in life after life he taught his fellow students and those learned in the sūtras, his hair is even and his mind is settled.

(79) Because in life after life whatever he undertook to study or accomplish, he always saw it through to completion, his meritorious qualities are fully accomplished.

(80) Because in life after life he constantly brought benefit to people and made them at ease, his compassionate teaching is vast, moistening, and gentle, without any limit.

In life after life, the bodhisattva's practices are thus.
