# 諸菩薩求佛本業經
## Sūtra on the Fundamental Practices Sought by All Bodhisattvas for Buddhahood

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0282

Translated from the Chinese. Western Jin dynasty, translated by Niè Dàozhēn (聶道真)

---

The bodhisattva Jñānaśrī asked the bodhisattva Mañjuśrī: "What cause enables a bodhisattva to act with body, speech, and mind without allowing others to find fault? What conditions lead to the body not scrutinizing others' faults, the mouth not speaking of others' faults, and the mind not contemplating others' faults?

"Through such bodily action, speech, and mental thought, all arhats, pratyekabuddhas, and all devas and worldly people cannot comprehend them.

"No one can match their bodily action, speech, and mental thought; no one can move or shake them. No one can harm their bodily action, speech, and mental thought. Their bodily action, speech, and mental thought all succeed and accord with their intentions.

"Their bodily actions are pure, their speech is pure, their mental thoughts are pure. Their bodily actions are without blemish, their speech is without blemish, their mental thoughts are without blemish. Wisdom is at the forefront of their bodily actions, wisdom is at the forefront of their speech, wisdom is at the forefront of their mental thoughts.

"When born, they are beautiful. When born, they are wise. When born, they are generous. When born, they are of good lineage. When born, they are in noble families. When born, their complexion is fine. When born, they have excellent marks distinguishing them from the multitude.

"All their thoughts are powerful. They protect and maintain much without forgetting. They have no attachment or stinginess. Their talents are lofty and bold: heroic among the brave, noble among the brave, courageous among the brave, fearless among the brave, without equal among the brave.

"What they know cannot be measured. What they discuss is without limit, incalculable, without boundary, without anyone who can surpass them. They cherish the Buddha's sūtras; all this is achieved through the exertion of past lives.

"They go out and speak often, and people trust and follow them. No one fails to revere and be drawn to them. Their bodily actions are never impure. Whatever sūtras they study, they completely understand them all.

"With single-mindedness they subdue their thoughts, contemplating with clear understanding. Turning their contemplation, they enter dhyāna, going in and out of the five tones, entering the four things, entering the three things, entering the twelve things, entering the meritorious virtues of past, future, and present arising, entering the seven factors of awakening, entering the emptiness of space, impermanence, and non-obstruction, and entering the sūtras of the six pāramitās.

"They fully possess mindfulness, goodness, compassion, pity, and equanimity; their minds are entirely without hatred or attachment. They enter the ten powers of wisdom. They are worshipped by all devas, Brahmā, Śakra, asuras, ghosts, spirits, and dragons.

"They cherish and protect the people of the ten directions. All who are frightened take refuge and are freed. All who are afraid and in desperate circumstances find safety and peace. They illuminate the ten directions like a lamp, like a torch, like a great fire, like the sun and moon. They ferry people across the world like a ship's pilot, like those aboard a ship.

"They are the honored teachers among all devas and worldly people, among insects and creeping creatures, supremely good, supremely great, foremost of all in the great assembly, uniquely honored, heroic among heroes, of the very highest, with none their equal.

"What practice leads to all of this?"

The bodhisattva Mañjuśrī said to the bodhisattva Jñānaśrī: "Excellent, excellent! Child of the Buddha, that you should ask such a question shows extremely great compassion, delivering many and liberating them. That you should generate this aspiration and ask! What you have asked about: there are bodily actions, speech, and mental thoughts of the bodhisattva, a child of the Buddha. There is a Way of mental intention, and through the meritorious deeds one practices, all can be attained, never increasing or decreasing.

"The Buddha's sūtras of past, future, and present are all received and attained through this. All arise and are accomplished from this. All bring safety and peace to worldly people. All teachings are truthfully received. All that one possesses is happy. All that one possesses is good. There is no one equal to this.

"Thus, Child of the Buddha, one who walks the great Way attains all of this.

"When a bodhisattva is living as a householder, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions not be bound by sensual desire, and may all enter the Dharma of empty space.'

"When a bodhisattva is filially serving and making offerings to their parents, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions quickly attain the Buddha Way, so as to deliver all people of the ten directions.'

"When a bodhisattva is dwelling with wife and children, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions have all sensual desires completely vanish.'

"When a bodhisattva at home has something on their mind, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions be freed from the midst of sensual desire and attain the Way of supreme deliverance.'

"When a bodhisattva at home is enjoying music and entertainment, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions hear and receive all the sūtras, hearing them all as when music is played.'

"When a bodhisattva is putting on the seven jewels, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions be freed from their heavy burdens and all find rest.'

"When a bodhisattva is among attendant women, enjoying amusement, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions enter the Buddha's sūtras and cast off licentiousness.'

"When a bodhisattva is in an upper chamber, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions ascend to the Buddha's sūtra lecture hall and receive all the sūtras, with no one their equal.'

"When a bodhisattva is giving generously, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions only wish to give to others, with no one being greedy or attached.'

"When a bodhisattva is with wife and children in loving affection, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions quickly be freed from the foulness of licentiousness, casting it all away, and awaken to entry into empty space.'

"When a bodhisattva is weary and dissatisfied at home, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions quickly be freed, with nothing binding them any longer.'

"When a bodhisattva leaves home to study the Way, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions be able to leave and never again enter the midst of sensual desire, craving nothing more.'

"When a bodhisattva arrives at a Buddhist monastery, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions only be mindful of the Buddha and enter all the sūtras, with no more obstruction.'

"When a bodhisattva goes to their teacher or preceptor, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions have all good thoughts fulfilled, and all enter the true sūtras.'

"When a bodhisattva seeks to become a śramaṇa, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions accomplish whatever they set out to do, and never have regret or stop midway.'

"When a bodhisattva removes their white garments, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions shine brightly in merit, and never be lax.'

"When a bodhisattva receives the kāṣāya, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions be unstained, and hold their minds like the Buddha's.'

"When a bodhisattva shaves their head, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions have all defilement removed and never gather with it again.'

"When a bodhisattva becomes a full śramaṇa, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions return to upāya-kauśalya-pāramitā and attain all the sūtras.'

"When a bodhisattva serves as a śramaṇa, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions, upon becoming śramaṇas, become like the Buddha and deliver all people of the ten directions.'

"When a bodhisattva upholds the precepts, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions guard and uphold the prohibitory precepts and never transgress the Dharma.'

"When a bodhisattva receives their preceptor, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions be mindful of all they know through dhyāna, crossing to the supreme shore, with no obstruction.'

"When a bodhisattva receives their teacher, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions succeed in all they do, becoming thoroughly practiced, upholding the teaching without loss.'

"When a bodhisattva takes refuge in the Buddha, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions find no lack of joy in the Buddha's Dharma, and all be born in supremely good places.'

"When a bodhisattva takes refuge in the Dharma, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions attain the treasury of profound sūtras, and may their wisdom be vast as the great ocean.'

"When a bodhisattva takes refuge in the Sangha, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions find refuge and salvation, as the bhikṣu-sangha finds its refuge, and delight in the Buddha's Way and virtue.'

"When a bodhisattva opens a door, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions quickly open the gate of heaven, enter the gate of the Buddha's sūtras, and never weary or turn back, reaching the Buddha's path of nirvāṇa.'

"When a bodhisattva enters a room, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions attain deliverance, with their abode quickly becoming like the Buddha's. May they quickly attain the profound sūtras that all arhats and pratyekabuddhas cannot reach.'

"When a bodhisattva closes a door, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions quickly close the gate of the evil destinies, and may all their past evil karma be burned away.'

"When a bodhisattva spreads their bedding, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions quickly enter the profound sūtras and see all people of the ten directions as empty space.'

"When a bodhisattva sits down, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions sit in peace like the Buddha upon the lion's throne, with no attachment in their minds.'

"When a bodhisattva sits upright, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions enter the practice of true merit, without doubt or wavering in the Buddha's sūtras.'

"When a bodhisattva breathes, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions have their breathing settled, achieving contentment.'

"When a bodhisattva turns their contemplation to observe, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions make this contemplation: seeing the Dharma of impermanence.'

"When a bodhisattva rises from sitting, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions perceive the Dharma of empty space, with nothing they do not thoroughly understand.'

"When a bodhisattva's foot treads the ground, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions abide in safety, never again shaken.'

"When a bodhisattva puts on their lower robe, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions gather and maintain merit, be modest before the world, and quickly attain the Buddha Way.'

"When a bodhisattva ties their belt, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions bind their merits firmly.'

"When a bodhisattva puts on their saṃghāṭī, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions increasingly gain merit, and all enter the sūtras, crossing to the supreme shore.'

"When a bodhisattva puts on their uttarāsaṅga, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions always delight in the Buddha's sūtras, never parting from them.'

"When a bodhisattva takes a tooth-cleaning stick, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions study all the sūtras and attain purity.'

"When a bodhisattva rinses their mouth, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions have all defilement purified and abide in purity.'

"When a bodhisattva goes to relieve themselves, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions cast off all evil, cutting off licentiousness, anger, and delusion.'

"When a bodhisattva goes to the water, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions ascend to the Buddha's sūtras in purity.'

"When a bodhisattva carries water, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions be meticulous and mindful, with gentle, good, and pure minds.'

"When a bodhisattva washes their hands, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions, with gentle and good hands, take hold of the sūtras and the Way of Dharma.'

"When a bodhisattva washes their face, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions enter the Buddha's sūtra Way, with the gate of their faces free of blemish.'

"When a bodhisattva goes out through the gate, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions attain everything they seek swiftly, with no more obstruction.'

"When a bodhisattva faces the road, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions quickly attain Buddhahood and never turn back.'

"When a bodhisattva walks along the road, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions enter the bottomless sūtras, deeply entering the sūtras, with bodies purified and no obstruction.'

"When a bodhisattva walks up a hill, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions delight in the Buddha's sūtras without any limit.'

"When a bodhisattva walks down a hill, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions enter the Buddha's great Way, threading through all forms of wisdom.'

"When a bodhisattva walks along a winding road, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions have no crooked thoughts and no harsh speech.'

"When a bodhisattva walks along a straight road, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions have minds set on the right Way, without flattery or deceit.'

"When a bodhisattva sees dry dust filling the road, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions have their desires depart and always attain the bright sūtras.'

"When a bodhisattva sees settled dust filling the road, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions always have gentle and soft minds and attain all forms of compassion.'

"When a bodhisattva sees a shady tree, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions have all evil dharmas removed, and enter the Buddha's sūtras with thorough understanding.'

"When a bodhisattva sees a lecture hall or monastery, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions hear and receive all the sūtras and enter therein.'

"When a bodhisattva sees a great tree in a grove, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions take refuge in it and make offerings, whether in heaven or in the world.'

"When a bodhisattva sees a mountain, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions have minds set on lofty talent, illuminating all dharmas of merit, with no one able to surpass them.'

"When a bodhisattva sees a thorny tree, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions swiftly distance themselves from licentiousness, anger, and delusion.'

"When a bodhisattva sees a leafy tree, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions be covered by the Way, attaining dhyāna, and turning to enter samādhi.'

"When a bodhisattva sees a flowering tree, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions adorn their bodies and attain the thirty-two marks.'

"When a bodhisattva sees a fruit-bearing tree, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions attain flowers and fruit, fully accomplished in the Buddha's sūtras.'

"When a bodhisattva sees flowing water, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions enter the flowing depths of the Buddha's sūtras and attain the Buddha's wisdom.'

"When a bodhisattva sees a well, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions quickly open the gate of the sūtras, of one taste without difference.'

"When a bodhisattva sees someone drawing water, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions have all the wisdom of the Way fully accomplished, entering the dharma of merit.'

"When a bodhisattva sees a spring, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions have their questions about wisdom yield much understanding, all meeting in the Way of the Buddha's sūtras.'

"When a bodhisattva sees a great body of water, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions hold all dharmas of merit without exhaustion, with no one surpassing them.'

"When a bodhisattva sees a bridge, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions attain the sūtras that ferry people to the supreme shore, as a bridge ferries people, without limit or end.'

"When a bodhisattva sees a dwelling, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions be far from sensual desire, and know the thoughts of all people of the ten directions.'

"When a bodhisattva sees a garden, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions have minds free of attachment, not delighting in the five kinds of music or the five objects of thought.'

"When a bodhisattva sees an orchard, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions have no worry or grief in their hearts, and attain the deep root of wisdom.'

"When a bodhisattva sees a pleasure garden, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions be diligent and never depart from the Buddha's sūtras.'

"When a bodhisattva sees a splendidly adorned assembly going out, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions be adorned and attain all of the thirty-two marks.'

"When a bodhisattva sees someone grieving, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions grieve no more.'

"When a bodhisattva sees someone happy, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions find joy in the profound sūtras.'

"When a bodhisattva sees someone unhappy, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions have no attachment in their affections.'

"When a bodhisattva sees someone at peace, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions be at peace, attaining the peace of the Buddha.'

"When a bodhisattva sees someone suffering, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions have all suffering extinguished and see the true, right Way.'

"When a bodhisattva sees someone strong and healthy, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions be strong and healthy, as the Buddha was in his time.'

"When a bodhisattva sees someone ill, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions contemplate impermanence, enter empty space, and fully fathom the Buddha's sūtras, never turning back.'

"When a bodhisattva sees a beautiful person, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions love and delight in the Buddha's sūtras.'

"When a bodhisattva sees an ugly person, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions never fall into ugliness.'

"When a bodhisattva sees someone repaying kindness, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions repay the kindness of all bodhisattvas.'

"When a bodhisattva sees someone ungrateful, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions have no stinginess or greed, and show all people the right Way.'

"When a bodhisattva sees a śramaṇa, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions receive all the sūtras and fully fathom them.'

"When a bodhisattva sees a follower of another path, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions have all evil roots entirely consumed, and fully fathom all the sūtras.'

"When a bodhisattva sees a ṛṣi, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions have all their wishes fulfilled and all their undertakings accomplished.'

"When a bodhisattva sees someone wearing armor, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions don the armor of the Buddha's sūtras and be fully equipped.'

"When a bodhisattva sees someone foolish, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions be wise and capable, and may their undertakings never fall into evil.'

"When a bodhisattva sees a sūtra lecture, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions understand with wisdom all that they hear.'

"When a bodhisattva sees a sovereign, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions become kings among the sūtras, naturally turning the sūtra-wheel, teaching the Way without ceasing.'

"When a bodhisattva sees a crown prince, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions become children of the Buddha, always born by transformation within the sūtras.'

"When a bodhisattva sees ministers, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions be illuminated in the profound sūtras, so that whatever questions of wisdom are asked, all are resolved and employed.'

"When a bodhisattva sees subordinate officials, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions have right thoughts, without evil, and never be far from all the bodhisattvas.'

"When a bodhisattva sees a city, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions have bodies with no equal, and may all people be supremely good, with no one surpassing them.'

"When a bodhisattva sees a palace, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions have bright and joyful minds, always encountering good merit.'

"When a bodhisattva sees someone carrying a staff, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions always do good, be revered by all, always wish to give to others, and teach people to practice the good Dharma.'

"When a bodhisattva holds their bowl, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions give generously, receive all offerings, and enter the bottomless merit.'

"When a bodhisattva goes on alms-round, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions enter the Buddha's Dharma-place, never forgetting or erring.'

"When a bodhisattva arrives at someone's gate, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions arrive at the gate of the Buddha's sūtras.'

"When a bodhisattva enters through the gate, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions enter the inner wisdom of the Buddha.'

"When a bodhisattva has not yet received food, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions have no adversity, and all enter the Prajñāpāramitā Sūtra.'

"When a bodhisattva has not yet obtained food, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions never again fall into the hells, the animal realm, the hungry ghosts, the demon prisons, or the evil destinies.'

"When a bodhisattva sees an empty bowl, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions be emptied of sensual desire.'

"When a bodhisattva sees a full bowl, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions be filled with merit.'

"When a bodhisattva sees their bowl received with food, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions practice the Way of the Buddha.'

"When a bodhisattva sees someone ashamed, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions feel shame before sensual desire.'

"When a bodhisattva sees someone without shame, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions have all evil thoughts cast away, with no lack of compassion.'

"When a bodhisattva receives fine food, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions have all their wishes fulfilled, with minds unstained.'

"When a bodhisattva receives coarse food, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions have gentle, soft minds, with no lack of pity.'

"When a bodhisattva eats, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions eat as in dhyāna, satisfied and always filled with the sūtras.'

"When a bodhisattva tastes food, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions be sated with flavor, as when the Buddha's throat transforms flavor, and may all attain the sweet-dew sūtra.'

"When a bodhisattva finishes eating, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions accomplish all their undertakings, entering the Buddha's sūtras and crossing to the supreme shore.'

"When a bodhisattva teaches the sūtra and offers benedictions, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions speak the Way without end, and enter all the profound sūtras of the Buddha.'

"When a bodhisattva finishes the benediction and goes out, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions go beyond the three states of form, impermanence, and emptiness, and receive the Buddha's wisdom.'

"When a bodhisattva enters the water, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions enter the Buddha's wisdom, in which past, future, and present are all equal.'

"When a bodhisattva bathes, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions wash away the stains of the mind, and may all depart and shine with supreme illumination reaching the farthest extent.'

"When a bodhisattva experiences heat, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions be far from heat, crossing to the supreme shore.'

"When a bodhisattva experiences cold, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions become leaders among humanity, attaining the supremely bright, cool, and good place.'

"When a bodhisattva sees someone reciting a sūtra, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions understand the meaning of all the sūtras, seeking all wisdom and grasping all forms of discernment.'

"When a bodhisattva sees the Buddha, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions meet with all the Buddhas, with no obstruction in their minds.'

"When a bodhisattva looks up at the Buddha, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions see without obstruction, perceiving the limitless realm.'

"When a bodhisattva prostrates to the Buddha with their head touching the ground, they think: 'May no one in the worlds of the ten directions, whether in heaven above or the world below, be able to see the top of the Buddha's head.'

"When a bodhisattva rises from prostration and looks at the Buddha, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions practice the sūtras with no one their equal.'

"When a bodhisattva circumambulates the Buddha once, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions complete all their supremely good undertakings and be rewarded with the sūtra's illumination.'

"When a bodhisattva circumambulates the Buddha three times, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions always have courageous minds in all their undertakings, and never be far from the Buddha Way.'

"When a bodhisattva praises the Buddha's merit and divine power, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions have incalculable merit and incalculable divine power in all their undertakings, crossing to the supreme merit.'

"When a bodhisattva washes their feet, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions attain the Buddha's divine-foot contemplation and flight, with no more obstruction, attaining all fully.'

"When a bodhisattva praises the Buddha's marks, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions have bodies fully accomplished, like the body described in the Buddha's sūtras.'

"When a bodhisattva lies down to sleep, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions never again be bound by sensual desire, and may all become pure amid suffering.'

"When a bodhisattva wakes up, they think: 'May all people in the worlds of the ten directions attain the Buddha's wisdom and the ten powers.'

"These are the practices that a bodhisattva always follows."

In the buddha-land of Śākyamuni, there were altogether one hundred billion Śakra-devānam-indras, all in Trāyastriṃśa Heaven, each thinking of how to invite the Buddha. All the Śakra-devānam-indras prepared for the Buddha, upon their indigo-blue principal palaces, seven-jeweled lion thrones, with the finest celestial kapok fabric of manifold colors, surpassingly beautiful, spread upon the thrones. They each set up magnificently fine jeweled canopies to their own satisfaction.

The Buddha immediately knew all of this. The Buddha then divided his body through divine power, appearing at the outer gate of each of the one hundred billion Śakra-devānam-indras in Trāyastriṃśa Heaven. For each Śakra-devānam-indra there was one Buddha, altogether one hundred billion Buddhas, each accompanied by bodhisattvas.

All the Śakra-devānam-indras were greatly joyful. They all came out to welcome the Buddha, paid homage, and invited the Buddha to enter.

The Buddha then entered with the bodhisattvas, going up to the indigo-blue principal palace and sitting within the jeweled canopy. All the bodhisattvas each sat on a seven-jeweled lotus lion throne within their own jeweled canopy.

The Buddha continued to sit in these one hundred billion minor lands together with the bodhisattvas, his divine power unmoving. Bodhisattvas from the ten directions came in great numbers to the assembly: the bodhisattva Dharma-ratna-mati, another Dharma-ratna-mati Bodhisattva, Śrīmati Bodhisattva, Guṇamati Bodhisattva, Dayāmati Bodhisattva, Śāntimati Bodhisattva, Jñānamati Bodhisattva, Satyamati Bodhisattva, Akṣayamati Bodhisattva, Śālamati Bodhisattva, and Sarvamati Bodhisattva.
