# 說矩里迦龍王像法
## Method for the Image of Kurikā Nāga King

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1207

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown.

---

His form is like a serpent, in the posture of lightning and thunder, body golden in color, wearing a wish-fulfilling jewel, samādhi flames rising. He has the form of four feet trampling. On his back stand erect seven vajra sharp needles. From his forehead grows a single jade horn. He coils around a sword upon which is inscribed the syllable *A*. One should also use contemplative observation of the mind, clearly distinguishing each detail.

If depicted in human form, the face displays joy and wrath, the entire body is clad in armor and helmet, like the form of King Virūpākṣa. The left hand supports the hip while grasping a lasso; the right arm is bent at the elbow, raised upward, holding a sword. Upon the crown is coiled the nāga king, standing upon a vajra mountain.

An alternate text says: Kurikā Nāga King has the joyful and serene appearance of a celestial being. Above the head are painted seven dragon heads emerging. He kneels, gazing upward at the Tathāgata, his palms joined together offering a jeweled lotus.

The syllable *A* transforms into this nāga attendant. This syllable is the supreme seed of bodhi. All who recite and uphold it possess this mind, because they aspire to unsurpassed bodhi. One contemplates this syllable upon the sword; from it all dharmas are born; it is the bodhicitta itself.

The practitioner who applies the dot-mark attains the accomplishment of merit and wisdom. The great space means the accomplishment of bodhi. The nirvāṇa dot-mark means the sense of removal: all defilements enter nirvāṇa. The compound syllable is like a great nāga king of awesome virtue, who raises clouds and brings rain with complete sovereign mastery. The syllable *A* is likewise: all the deeds of the Tathāgata are born from this syllable.

Next, the dharma-body seal mudrā and true word of the Nāga King. With the left and right ring fingers and little fingers interlocked within the palms, the two middle fingers stand upright touching at the tips. The two index fingers press against the backs of the upper joints of the middle fingers. The two thumbs, slightly bent, each press against the middle joints of the index fingers, moving back and forth. The true word:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ oṃ bhuki-buhe-bhuga-pati hūṃ svāhā*

The dharma-body seal of all nāga kings: place the right hand atop the left, with the right four fingers slightly bent, the thumb pressing the tip of the index finger with slight bending. The left hand is reversed and curved over the right hand in the same manner. Both hands face each other in the form of a serpent's mouth, with the four fingers of both hands opening and moving. [True word follows.]

Next, the lotus-seat seal: the eight fingers of both hands are slightly bent yet upright, all pointing upward. The two little fingers are two inches apart, the ring and middle fingers each two inches apart, and the two index fingers four inches apart. The thumbs stand upright, touching each other.

Next, the tusk seal: bend the right elbow upward, bend the ring finger and little finger into the palm, the middle finger stands upright, bend the index finger and press it against the side of the middle finger joint, and press the thumb against the side of the index finger. When making an altar offering, form this seal while reciting the true word, using the foregoing [invitation] to summon [the deity] into the altar. All will be delighted.

If one wishes to pray for rain, one should prepare a complete altar with offerings, and rain will fall, measuring from three, five, seven, eight, or nine to one inch and so on. The full ritual is set forth in a separate text.

Next, the root true word:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ kurikāya-karaja-myōga-śaniye svāhā*

Using the foregoing seal, if there is a patient, one should form the nāga king body-seal and empower it. Then take up the sword, with supreme concentrated mind, visualize the syllable *A* transforming into a golden dragon of wrathful, blazing aspect. Direct the visualization toward the patient, and the disease will be removed and cured.

If there are afflictions by grahā spirits, or epidemic-disease ghosts who dwell in the southern quarter, or malaria, or various other ailments: construct a one-cubit water altar. At the four corners place vases with their mouths filled with fresh flowers; colored flowers may also be used. Also place four vessels at the four sides, filled with fragrant water. Arrange all offering articles in splendid array. Then invite the Nāga King to abide upon the altar. In the center of the altar, place the sword. State the purpose of the ritual, bow in worship, make offerings, and recite the true word with empowerment one hundred and eight times. Wield the sword to empower the patient, and the disease will be extinguished. Then send off [the deity] and recite the altar-dissolution mantra.

Next, visualize the wind-wheel dissolving the altar. Having completed this, do not allow [the deity] to linger. This dharma is the most secret of secrets. Do not rashly transmit it.

Kurikā Nāga is the king among all nāgas. The full account is found in the Mañjuśrī Dharma texts and others.
