# 聖虛空藏菩薩陀羅尼經
## Dhāraṇī Sūtra of Holy Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1147

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmadeva (法天).

---

Thus have I heard. At one time the World-Honored One was on the summit of Nandana Mountain, at a celestial palace not far from the dwelling of the ṛṣis, together with a great assembly of five hundred bhikṣus, and also bodhisattva mahāsattvas. All these bodhisattvas were those who in one more life would attain unsurpassed samyak-saṃbodhi. They were:

Maitreya Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, Samantabhadra Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, Anantakusuma Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, Samantakusuma Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, and Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva Mahāsattva. There were five hundred such bodhisattva mahāsattvas.

At that time the World-Honored One and the bodhisattvas saw beneath that mountain, in a forest by a river, two bhikṣus, naked, with arms outstretched, shouting and weeping. Then Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva Mahāsattva rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, placed his right knee on the ground, joined his palms in reverence, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! Those two bhikṣus beside the Ganges River, what cause and condition makes them naked, with arms outstretched, shouting and weeping?"

The World-Honored One told Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva Mahāsattva: "Those two bhikṣus are entangled in disease and seized by bhūtas. Therefore they are naked, shouting, and weeping."

Then Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva Mahāsattva addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! How can such diseases be removed? How can such bhūtas be expelled?"

At that time the World-Honored One, through his supernatural power, summoned the buddhas. In the sky six tathāgatas appeared, in order to bear witness to the dharma of the seventh world-honored one, Śākyamuni Tathāgata.

Then Vipaśyin Tathāgata, wishing to benefit all sentient beings, remove disease, and expel bhūtas, joyfully proclaimed the dhāraṇī:

*Namo buddhāya namo dharmāya namaḥ saṃghāya oṃ cara cara hire hire hirāya namaḥ śamocana ya namaḥ namaḥ svāhā*

Then Vipaśyin Tathāgata, having spoken this vidyā, told Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva Mahāsattva: "If any person receives, upholds, reads, recites, and makes offerings to this dhāraṇī, know that this person will be free from illness and suffering, will not be harmed by weapons, will not be swept away by water, will not be harmed by poison, will not die prematurely, will have no trouble from food and drink, will attain retentive memory, will have a long lifespan, and will at all times be protected by all buddhas. Furthermore, if one is deaf, empower oil with the mantra seven times and drip it into the ear, and the deafness will naturally dissipate."

Then Śikhin Tathāgata, wishing to benefit all sentient beings, remove disease, and expel bhūtas, joyfully proclaimed the dhāraṇī:

*Namo buddhāya namo dharmāya namaḥ saṃghāya oṃ paca paca paca pacāya sarva-bhūtānāṃ ghṛṇi kileya parāviṇīya nāṃ svāhā*

Then Śikhin Tathāgata, having spoken this vidyā, told Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva Mahāsattva: "My heart-seal great dhāraṇī has been spoken in common by a hundred thousand koṭis of innumerable buddhas. If any sentient being, six times day and night, remembers and upholds my heart dhāraṇī, disease is removed, ghosts and spirits are kept far away, evil dreams are dispelled, one is forever free from untimely death, calamities do not invade, poisons cannot harm, one sees the Tathāgata in dreams, and one enters the realm of the buddhas.

"Furthermore, for wind-related illness or child-ghost sickness, tie a blue thread cord, empower it with the mantra over sumanā flowers, wear it on the body and recite the mantra beside the ear. At all times, may all such afflictions be eliminated."

Then Viśvabhū Tathāgata, wishing to benefit all sentient beings, remove disease, and expel bhūtas, joyfully proclaimed the dhāraṇī:

*Namo buddhāya namo dharmāya namaḥ saṃghāya oṃ kara kara kara kara kukurusana na kurusarva-graha-kṣa svāhā*

Then Viśvabhū Tathāgata, having spoken this vidyā, told Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva Mahāsattva: "Listen attentively now. This dhāraṇī has been spoken in common by the tathāgatas of the Fortunate Kalpa and the buddhas of the three times. If any person receives, upholds, reads, recites, and makes offerings to this dhāraṇī, know that that person will not be harmed by weapons, poisons cannot harm, there will be no disease, water cannot sweep one away, there will be no untimely death, and past karma is extinguished.

"If any bhikṣu, bhikṣuṇī, upāsaka, or upāsikā first bathes and stands before the World-Honored One, reciting and upholding this vidyā for a full eight hundred recitations, all disease and all disturbance cannot invade or harm. One attains long life, exhausts all afflictions, and obtains abundant storehouses of wealth and grain.

"If any evil being causes fear, contemplate and recite this dhāraṇī, and the fear is removed and one attains delight.

"If there is any quarrel or dispute, tie a white thread cord and recite the dhāraṇī: all will be reconciled.

"If one suffers from eye pain, make a thread from milky tree bark, recite the dhāraṇī, and tie it around the neck: the pain will be cured.

"Furthermore, if armies face each other, take honey, curds, ghee, and apamārga; with each recitation cast one portion into the fire as a homa, for a full eight thousand recitations. All the armies will withdraw from each other, and one will have a long lifespan.

"If a king or minister seeks peace and security, perform a homa of curds, honey, and rice-flowers in the fire for a full eight thousand recitations. In all places, body and mind will be at ease. If for oneself or all sentient beings, to remove accumulated evil, calamity, karmic obstacles, and inauspicious signs, perform a homa with white milky-tree wood and sesame seeds empowered by the mantra in the fire for a full eight thousand recitations, and all calamities will be removed. Furthermore, if a person wishes to accomplish all vidyās for themselves, take one hundred flowers: nāraṇa, priyaṃgu, ox-head sandalwood, śrīmati-kuṅkuma-musk-staka, indra-varuṇa-coral-jāti. Place all these medicines together in a bhadra vase, with bodhi-tree branches and sumanā leaves inserted in the mouth of the vase. Place it before the World-Honored One. On the fifteenth day of the bright half of the month, for one day and one night, recite the dhāraṇī before the World-Honored One for a full eight hundred recitations, and the siddhi will be accomplished."

Then Krakucchanda Tathāgata, wishing to benefit all sentient beings, remove disease, and expel bhūtas, joyfully proclaimed the dhāraṇī:

*Namo buddhāya namo dharmāya namaḥ saṃghāya namaḥ krakucchanda ya tathāgatāya arhate samyak-saṃbuddhāya oṃ kaṭa kaṭa kaṭa kaṭa kici kici kici kici namaḥ sarva-tathāgate vihyūrahate viyak-saṃbuddhi vihyū svāhā*

Then Krakucchanda Tathāgata, having spoken this vidyā, told Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva Mahāsattva: "Listen attentively now. My heart-seal great dhāraṇī has been spoken in common by buddha world-honored ones as numerous as the sands of the Ganges. If any person receives, upholds, makes offerings to, hears, and rejoices in this dhāraṇī, know that this person attains retentive memory, is freed from disease, has no trouble from food and drink, and is freed from bondage. In the future, if any bhikṣu, bhikṣuṇī, upāsaka, or upāsikā offers flowers, incense, and perfume to the Three Treasures, bathes the World-Honored One, generates the aspiration, and recites this dhāraṇī for a full eight hundred recitations, they attain the knowledge of past lives. For seven lifetimes they will be reborn in heaven as sons of devas, and when born below among humans, they will be cakravartin kings. If one does not follow the prescribed method but simply recites as conditions allow, one will always be born among humans, and upon the end of one's human life be reborn in the Land of Peace and Happiness. If one empowers various food and drink with seven recitations, all diseases will be kept far away.

"If one wishes to quell all ghosts and spirits and the eight thousand obstacles, one should first bathe, then recite this vidyā for a full seven recitations. If one has an evil sore, empower the sore with seven recitations of the mantra. If one has a carbuncle or tumor, empower a copper needle with the mantra, recite and insert the needle, and it will be cured. For all quarrels, recite this vidyā for a full seven recitations, and they will be dispersed. For all bondage, recite this vidyā for a full seven recitations, and one will be freed. Wealth and treasure will increase. Recite the dhāraṇī constantly without interruption. For Vināyaka obstacles and all difficulties, recite the dhāraṇī for a full seven recitations, and they will be kept far away.

"If a person wishes to know the desires of the heart, at a pure place or before a buddha stūpa, prepare and adorn the altar, burn aloeswood incense, and recite this vidyā for a full eight thousand recitations. Lie down with the head to the east, and in a dream one will see the Tathāgata, and matters of lifespan, good and bad fortune, and superior and inferior outcomes will all be seen."

Then Kanakamuni Tathāgata, wishing to benefit all sentient beings, remove disease, and expel bhūtas, joyfully proclaimed the dhāraṇī:

*Namo buddhāya namo dharmāya namaḥ saṃghāya namaḥ kanakamunaye tathāgatāya arhate samyak-saṃbuddhāya oṃ sara sara sara sara siri siri siri siri sirāya dhama dhama dhama dhama dumu dumu dumu dumu dumāya namaḥ namaḥ kanakamunaye tathāgatāya arhate samyak-saṃbuddhāya svāhā*

Then Kanakamuni Tathāgata, having spoken this vidyā, told Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva Mahāsattva: "If any person receives, upholds, reads, recites, and constantly makes offerings to this dhāraṇī, know that this person will not be harmed by blades, weapons, water, or fire, nor by fear, sorcery, spells, or poisons. There will be no untimely death. All karmic obstacles from previous lives are extinguished by reciting three times daily. Furthermore, for scabies, skin disease, abdominal lumps, and evil tumors, take calamus, grind it to powder, mix with honey to make pills, and perform a homa in the fire for a full eight thousand recitations. Furthermore, for malaria occurring every day, every two days, every three days, or every four days, empower jāti-sumanā flowers with the mantra and wear them on the body. Furthermore, for deafness, recite the mantra beside the ear. Furthermore, for wind-epilepsy or kaṭa-ghost illness, empower buddha-flowers with the mantra and wear them on the body. May all such diseases be eliminated. One will enjoy wealth, honor, and long life, will always see the Tathāgata, and at all times will be protected by all the buddhas."

Then Kāśyapa Tathāgata, wishing to benefit all sentient beings, remove disease, and expel bhūtas, joyfully proclaimed the dhāraṇī:

*Namo buddhāya namo dharmāya namaḥ saṃghāya oṃ hara hara hara hara hahā namaḥ kāśyapāya tathāgatāya arhate samyak-saṃbuddhāya siddhantu mantra-padāni svāhā*

Then Kāśyapa Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha, having spoken this vidyā, told Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva Mahāsattva: "If any person receives, upholds, reads, recites, and makes offerings to this dhāraṇī, know that this person's karmic obstacles are all eliminated. At all times, all buddhas will gather them in, and they will be far from evil dharmas and abide securely in wholesome dharmas.

"Furthermore, if a person suffers from white leprosy, empower buddha-flowers for a full eight thousand recitations. For chronic fever, empower buddha-flowers for a full eight thousand recitations. For illness in its initial stages, empower buddha-flowers for a full eight thousand recitations, wear them on the body, and at all times may all such afflictions be eliminated.

"Furthermore, if a woman has difficulty in childbirth, empower buddha-flowers, fry them in oil, and apply. For wind-related skin sores, empower buddha-flowers, cook them in ghee, and apply. Furthermore, for carbuncles, tumors, and all hemorrhoids and fistulas, empower buddha-flowers, mix with good honey, and apply to the sore.

"Furthermore, for malaria occurring every day, every two, three, or four days, consume empowered incense and flowers. For all ghost-sickness or epilepsy, empower buddha-flowers and wear them on the body. May all such diseases, through the power of the vidyā empowerment, at all times be eliminated."

Then Śākyamuni Tathāgata, wishing to benefit all sentient beings, remove disease, and expel bhūtas, spoke this heart-seal dhāraṇī:

*Namo buddhāya namo dharmāya namaḥ saṃghāya tadyathā oṃ kumi kumi mahākumi tiri tiri mahātiri cari cari mahācari dhara dhara mahādhara bhiri bhiri mahābhiri kiri kiri mahākiri ruru mahārumī rī mīri mīri mahāmīri tiri tiri mahātiri dumi dumi mahādumi cari cari mahācari kiri kiri mahākiri ciri ciri mahāciri svāhā*

Then the World-Honored One, Śākyamuni Tathāgata, having spoken this vidyā, told Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva Mahāsattva: "Ākāśagarbha! If any person receives, upholds, reads, recites, and makes offerings to the dhāraṇī, know that that person will be free from bodily disease. Blades and weapons cannot harm, water cannot drown, one will not suffer anguish, and there will be no untimely death. Ākāśagarbha! You should now know that this dhāraṇī has great awesome power. It can remove disease, remove all poisons, remove inauspiciousness and all fears. Those bhūtas and their assemblies cannot invade or harm; those pūtanas and their assemblies cannot invade or harm. May all such beings generate hearts of loving-kindness, and at all times grant them peace and security."

Then Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva Mahāsattva addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! The Tathāgata has already spoken the great dhāraṇī that benefits sentient beings and accomplishes peace and security. I now also wish to speak a dhāraṇī that benefits sentient beings, purifies the dharmadhātu, and accomplishes peace and security:

*Tadyathā cari cari mahācari turu turu mahāturu jvari jvari mahājvari hiri hiri mahāhiri svāhā*

Then Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, having spoken this vidyā, further said: "If one wishes to accomplish supramundane supreme merit, one should receive and uphold this dhāraṇī. All worldly blades, weapons, imprisonment, disease and affliction, dark clouds of obstruction, and all viṣa-vāmana poisons cannot invade or harm. Furthermore, for karuṇa-cetana-ati-bṛṣṭira disease, may the power of the vidyā protect and eliminate it."

At that time, when the World-Honored One had spoken this dharma, Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva Mahāsattva and all the great assembly, hearing what the Buddha taught, single-mindedly received and upheld it, and joyfully put it into practice.
