# 佛說八部佛名經
## Sūtra of the Eight Divisions of Buddha Names Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0429

Translated from the Chinese. Northern Wei dynasty, translated by Gautama Prajñāruci (瞿曇般若流支)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was traveling in Vaiśālī, in the nata-tree garden, together with a great assembly of bhikṣus, one thousand two hundred and fifty bhikṣus, and bodhisattvas beyond number.

At that time, there was a householder's son named Sukṛta. He came out from the city and went to the nata-tree garden. Arriving at the World-Honored One's place, he bowed his head to the Buddha's feet, circumambulated him three times to the right, stood to one side, and with palms joined addressed the Buddha: "I wish to ask a question. If the Great Sage will permit it, I will venture to speak."

The Buddha told Sukṛta: "Ask whatever you wish. The Tathāgata will explain it in full."

Sukṛta, having received permission, addressed the Buddha: "O Heaven among Heavens, are there Buddhas who have cultivated their original vows and thereby attained perfect awakening, who are now present in the world teaching the Dharma? May the Great Sage proclaim these Buddhas' names. Those who hear them and uphold them, bearing them atop their heads, receiving and venerating the teaching, bowing and taking refuge, and proclaiming these merits far and wide, shall they be free from all calamities, never fall into the three evil paths, and through hearing these Buddha names, shall neither humans nor non-humans be able to take advantage of them? If before the magistrates, shall reciting these Buddhas' names prevent anyone from wrongly seizing their possessions? Shall their nature be firm and strong, free from cowardice and fear, and shall they gain peace and victory? If they enter battle, shall swords not wound their bodies and arrows not pierce them? Shall yakṣas, ghosts, devas, nāgas, and spirits dare not trouble them? Shall lions, tigers, wolves, wild beasts, and venomous creatures be unable to harm them?"

The Buddha told Sukṛta: "Listen carefully! Reflect well upon this! I shall teach you protections against calamity, freedom from fear, and the attainment of peace." Thereupon Sukṛta received the teaching and listened.

The Buddha said: "In the east, from here, there is a Buddha named Devoted to Sincerity Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, teaching the Dharma. His world is called Renowned Footprints.

"Furthermore, in the east there is a Buddha named Firmly Advancing, Ferrying Thoughts, Auspicious Meaning Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, teaching the Dharma. His world is called None Can Match.

"Furthermore, in the east from this buddha-land, there is a Buddha named Contemplating Radiant Merit Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, teaching the Dharma. His world is called Auspicious Peace.

"Furthermore, in the east from this buddha-land, there is a Buddha named Compassion-Glory Tranquil Chief Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, teaching the Dharma. His world is called Without Anger or Resentment.

"Furthermore, in the east from this buddha-land, there is a Buddha named True Nature Supreme Chief Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, teaching the Dharma. His world is called Abandoning Weapons.

"Furthermore, in the east from this buddha-land, there is a Buddha named Mindful of Beings, Renowned Supreme Chief Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, teaching the Dharma. His world is called Blazing Splendor Chief.

"Furthermore, in the east there is a Buddha named Leaping Chief, Lofty Beyond Sumeru Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, teaching the Dharma. His world is called Luminous Blazing Heat Chief.

"Furthermore, in the east from this buddha-land, there is a Buddha named Victorious Renown Supreme Chief Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, teaching the Dharma. His world is called Beloved Delight.

"If there are persons who hear from afar the names of these Tathāgatas of the east, and receive these Buddhas' World-Honored names, faithfully venerating and reflecting well, embracing them in their hearts, upholding, reciting, and reading them: then the precepts, virtue, wisdom, and path-foundations of these Buddhas are all equal. Moreover, the adornments and purity of these buddha-lands are extraordinarily virtuous, preeminent and without peer. There is no dust or defilement, no women or the affliction of impurity, no calamity of the five turbidities or bitter toil, no misery of the three evil paths, and no sand, gravel, stones, thorns, ditches, or pits."

The Buddha said: "Sukṛta! If there are those who can uphold and venerate the names of these Buddhas, World-Honored Ones, and proclaim them far and near, rising in the first watch of the night to practice walking meditation, praising and reciting these Buddhas' names, and rising in the middle and last watches of the night to abide with single-pointed mind, abandoning what is without benefit and reflecting on what is to be cultivated, then those who practice in this way will see their virtue increase daily and will never suffer loss."

The Buddha told Sukṛta: "You should delight in this Sūtra of the Eight Buddha Names. The Buddha teaches this Dharma to sons of good family: embrace it in your heart and never forget or abandon it. If you immediately engage in diligent practice, you will then see eight thousand Buddhas."

Thereupon Sukṛta, hearing the sage's proclamation, took jeweled flowers worth eight thousand gold pieces and scattered them as offerings before the World-Honored One. He bowed his head to the Buddha's feet, circumambulated him three times to the right, and then sat down at a distance.

At that time, Śakra, Lord of the Devas, was present at the assembly. He came forward and addressed the Buddha: "Indeed, Great Sage! I have already received these eight Buddhas' names, recited and read them, and embraced them in my heart. O Heaven among Heavens, I shall diligently practice and uphold these names of the Buddhas of the eight divisions, reflecting and concentrating upon them without ceasing even during meals, treasuring and revering them as the unsurpassed jewel."

The Buddha told the Lord of the Devas: "For this very reason, when the devas and asuras wage battle together, if there is fear, Kauśika, you should be mindful and there will be nothing to fear. Why is this? If there are persons who praise and extol the names of these Tathāgatas and the sūtra of the eight divisions, they thereby proclaim the work of fearlessness. Those who proclaim the names of these Tathāgatas and their sūtras will not encounter the calamity of afflictions and many troubles. Those who can transmit the names of these Tathāgatas and the essentials of their sūtras thereby proclaim an age of great peace and abundant harvest. Those who can transmit the names of these Buddhas and their sūtras thereby proclaim safety and the removal of great affliction. Those who can transmit the names of these Buddhas and their sūtras thereby proclaim tranquility and peaceful meditation. Those who transmit the names of these Buddhas and their sūtras will be freed from all immeasurable fears. Those who can transmit this sūtra of the Buddhas' names will have peaceful dreams and joyful waking, will not fear magistrates, and water, fire, thieves, enemies, and creditors will naturally withdraw. Ghosts, spirits, rākṣasas, yaksas, sprites, goblins, and oppressive demons will all be unable to withstand them. If they enter mountain ridges, valley streams, open wilderness roads, bandits and robbers will naturally not appear, and lions, tigers, wolves, bears, and serpents will all hide away. Why? The virtue of these Buddhas, most honored, surpasses Mount Sumeru; their wisdom surpasses rivers and seas; their insight surpasses empty space. They walk alone in the three realms, and none can match them. All in the ten directions are ferried across by them."

When the Buddha had spoken thus, the householder's son Sukṛta, Śakra Lord of the Devas, all the bhikṣus, and the entire assembly of devas, nāgas, spirits, asuras, and worldly humans heard the sūtra and rejoiced. They bowed in reverence and departed.
