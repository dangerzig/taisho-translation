# 釋迦牟尼如來像法滅盡之記
## Record of the Extinction of the Semblance Dharma of Śākyamuni Tathāgata

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 2090

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Facheng (法成).

---

There came a man from the kingdom of Khotan. Six generations of kings had passed, and the seventh king was named Vijayakīrti. During his reign, in that kingdom there was a monastery called Sākya-prāhaṇa. Not far from this monastery was a mountain valley called Śākya-gīna. In that valley dwelt an arhat, who had a bhikṣu disciple studying the Vinaya under him. After reading the *Sūtra of the Questions of Candragarbha Bodhisattva* and other holy teachings, the disciple asked his arhat teacher: "After the Buddha's passing, how long will the semblance Dharma, stūpas, and the like remain in the world in Khotan, Kashgar, and Parthia? Who will destroy them? And where will everything ultimately come to rest? Please explain this for us."

The arhat then said: "After the passing of Śākyamuni Tathāgata, the images and semblance of his Dharma, along with his stūpas and temples, will remain in the world for two thousand years and then be extinguished. In these three kingdoms of Khotan and the others, the Chinese, the Red-Faced People, the Suvī, the Turks, the Uyghurs, and other raiders will raise arms and come to attack and destroy them. Because of this, the buddha-dharma will gradually decline. They will demolish stūpas and monasteries, and the provisions of the saṃgha will also be entirely cut off.

"Among these three kingdoms, in Parthia and Kashgar, the Dharma will not be practiced, and invading armies will disturb the stūpas and monasteries. Most will be burned and demolished, and all will be empty and abandoned. Most of the monks will migrate to the kingdom of Khotan. The stūpas and monasteries of Khotan are constantly protected by five hundred bodhisattvas: two hundred fifty in the guise of monastics and two hundred fifty born among the laity to protect them. The monastery at Ox-Head Mountain is a place that the one thousand five buddhas of the Fortunate Kalpa will always tread upon and use as their palace. Because of the majestic virtue and compassion of the worthies and sages who empower the stūpas, monasteries, and wondrous Dharma of Khotan, those who practice the Dharma are more numerous than in other kingdoms, and the Dharma will long remain in the world.

"At that time, the various kings will contend and quarrel, each wishing to seize the kingdom of Khotan. Whichever king obtains it will serve as a great patron to Khotan and will not destroy or demolish it but will constantly make offerings. At that time, the king of the Red-Faced People will have great power and will invade many other kingdoms, making them his own territories. At that time, a bodhisattva will take birth in the kingdom of the Red-Faced People and become king. Within his own kingdom he will broadly practice the wondrous Dharma. He will invite Dharma-teachers and sūtras from other kingdoms. In the Red-Faced Kingdom he will establish vihāras, construct stūpas, and ordain many into the saṃgha. The king, his ministers, and all the people of the kingdom will broadly practice the correct Dharma.

"At that time, Khotan will be subject to the Red-Faced King. The correct Dharma will be broadly practiced. Stūpas and monasteries will be established. The Three Treasures will be provided with households, fields, and gardens, and great offerings will be made. For seven generations, the kings of the Red-Faced Kingdom will practice the wondrous Dharma. These seven kings will not harbor evil intentions toward the Three Treasures and stūpas in other kingdoms, nor will they cause harm.

"At that time, the monks of Khotan, as prophesied in the *Sūtra of the Prophecy of Candragarbha Bodhisattva*, will for the most part gradually lose their faith. They will disregard the precepts and the Vinaya, seeking worldly gain and fame. They will involve themselves in the confidential affairs of kings and ministers, causing the correct Dharma to gradually decline.

"At that time, the kings, ministers, and their descendants will lose their pure faith. The property belonging to the permanent endowments will be consumed by the bhikṣus for their own use. Thus the permanent endowments belonging to the stūpas, monasteries, and the correct Dharma of Khotan will not be as they were in former days and will gradually deteriorate. The kings and ministers will no longer delight in the monastic community.

"Later, at another time, a bodhisattva will become the seventh king of the Red-Faced Kingdom. That king will take a Chinese bodhisattva princess as his consort. She will bring six hundred attendants to the Red-Faced Kingdom. This princess will have deep faith in the buddha-dharma and will be greatly endowed with merit. The Red-Faced King will also have profound pure faith, surpassing that of his predecessors, and will broadly promote the correct Dharma.

"At such a time, the king of Khotan will be young and will not practice the Buddha's correct Dharma. He will address the bhikṣus, saying: 'Either return to lay life, or go wherever you wish.' Under this coercion, all the monks will gather at Jatsa Monastery to deliberate the matter. Before the assembly in that monastery, seven portions of golden food will suddenly appear. The five thousand bhikṣus whose food the king has cut off, pressed by hunger, will exchange the golden food for provisions to last three months.

"At that time, some will return to lay life, and some will not. Those who do not return to lay life will depart for other lands. Separated from fathers, mothers, relatives, and their native country, they will be overcome with grief. Weeping and wailing, the assembly of monks will set out on their journey and arrive at the monastery of Mūhun. At that place there is a great stūpa. A pure-faith spirit-king will open that stūpa and take out a golden bowl filled with pearls, which he will present to the monks. Yet other faithful patrons will also prepare provisions and present them to the monks.

"In this way, the monks will gradually proceed to the Nuru Valley. With those pearls, they will have provisions for half a month. When the monks reach the Nuru Valley, they will stay at the Songduohe Gongniang Monastery. At that time, Vaiśravaṇa and Śrīdevī will transform themselves into husband and wife and dwell in that place. They will respectfully address the monks: 'We wish to perform merit. Please accept our offering.' For half a month, they will hold a great feast and provide for the monks. Śrīdevī will take from her sleeve golden coins and give them to the monks.

"The monks will then all journey from the territory of the Great Red-Faced Kingdom until they arrive at the Mountain Pass. There they will encounter border guards who will detain them and refuse to let them pass, saying: 'There are other roads elsewhere; you may go wherever you wish.'

"At that time, Vaiśravaṇa will transform himself into a white yak, with a sore on its back and a halter-rope. The monks, seeing it, will say to one another: 'This yak with the sore on its back is someone's animal. Where is it going? Let us follow it.' The yak will then lead them along a direct road, and within four or five days all the monks will reach the territory of Satpi in the Red-Faced Kingdom. The military commander of the Satpi garrison, hearing that a great number of monks had come from the west, will hurry to report to the Red-Faced King.

"At that time, the queen will hear that the great assembly of monks has been driven from their land and displaced. She will address the king: 'I shall prepare pack animals and provisions. I wish to invite the monks to come to the Red-Faced Kingdom.' The king will also consent and prepare pack animals to welcome the monks to the Red-Faced Kingdom. The king and the princess's attendants will make offerings and pay reverence. They will ask the great elders among the monks who are well versed in the Tripiṭaka: 'Besides you, are there other displaced monks?' The Tripiṭaka masters will respectfully reply: 'In Parthia, Kashgar, Balūr, and Kashmir, there are many displaced monks who have scattered and now dwell in those places.' The king will immediately dispatch envoys to invite and bring all these monks to the Red-Faced Kingdom. At that time, seven monasteries will be established in the Red-Faced Kingdom, with provisions and permanent endowments surpassing those of former days, and the monks will be settled in the seven monasteries.

"After three or four years, a malignant sore will appear over the princess's heart. During her illness, the princess will address the king: 'Due to this illness, I shall certainly not recover. All my servants and possessions I wish to donate to the Three Treasures.' The king will consent. The six hundred attendants will all be released to leave home and become monastics. Thereafter, the princess's life will come to an end.

"After the princess's death, throughout the territories of the Red-Faced King, the smallpox disease will arise. Ministers, officials, and many of the royal descendants will die. The ministers will then assemble and address the king: 'Within your majesty's borders, there was never such a disease of painful sores. Now these displaced foreign monks have come to this place. The princess has passed away. Many ministers and officials have died. Therefore, these monastics should not remain within the king's borders. They should properly be expelled. On a previous day, they should have been expelled but were not. Let us deliberate carefully.' The ministers, of one mind, will favor expelling the monks and will so inform the king, intending to drive the monks from the kingdom.

"At that time, the native bhikṣus dwelling within the Red-Faced King's borders will say in anger: 'If these monks are expelled, we too shall not remain here.' The ministers will angrily reply: 'You may also leave as you wish.'

"After the princess came to the Red-Faced Kingdom, the Chinese king promoted the Daoist religion, and therefore all Chinese monks came to the borders of the Red-Faced Kingdom. At such a time, all the monks in that territory, together with all the śarīra relics, holy scriptures, treatises, offering implements, and permanent endowments within the Red-Faced Kingdom, will be gathered up and carried to the great kingdom of Gandhāra in the west.

"At that time, from China to the Red-Faced Kingdom, Brahmā-walking Kingdom, and Khotan, all the way to the Ganges River, the semblance Dharma will be extinguished with nothing remaining. On the other side of the Ganges, in the kingdom of Kuśumī, the semblance Dharma will remain for three months and then finally be extinguished.

"All the monks will arrive at the shore of the ocean where the Nāga King Elāpattra dwells in the territory of Gandhāra. Through the majestic power of the Three Treasures, the sea will surge with waves. The nāga king will think: 'Why are the waves surging where I dwell?' With his celestial eye he will observe and know that the holy teaching and semblance Dharma of Śākyamuni Tathāgata is about to be extinguished, and that this is the final occasion for seeing the saṃgha. He will transform himself into an old man and emerge from the sea. Bowing to the monks, he will ask: 'From where has this assembly of monks come? Where are they going?'

"The monks will answer: 'We originally dwelt in the territories of the Red-Faced Kingdom. The patrons lost their faith and destroyed the permanent endowments and monasteries. Therefore we are going to the great kingdom of Gandhāra.'

"The nāga king will ask: 'The assembly of monks is very large. What provisions do you have? The great kingdom of Gandhāra is more than forty days' journey around the sea to reach. The monks presently have only twenty days' provisions. How can they get there?'

"Hearing this, all the monks will weep greatly. Seeing this, the nāga king will be overcome with grief and will address the monks: 'There is a direct route here. There is a serpent bridge. Those who can climb and cross it will reach the other side quickly.' Having spoken these words, the old man will vanish.

"The nāga king will then reveal a mountain path and transform his own body into a great serpent, forming a bridge over the sea. He will think: 'If I now ferry these monks across, perhaps the evil karma by which I received this animal body may also be redeemed.' Making this vow, he will maintain the serpent bridge for more than fifteen days. Many people and pack animals will cross over it. As people and animals cross the serpent bridge, some will fall into the water and perish. Because of the people and animals passing back and forth, the serpent's back will crack and break apart, blood flowing like rain, and the sea will turn to blood.

"The great assembly of monks will cross over the serpent bridge. At the very end, the remaining Red-Faced People will obstruct them and not allow them to pass. Afterward, the great serpent will fall into the sea and die. Upon death, it will be reborn in Tuṣita Heaven. The sea will also dry up.

"The assembly of monks will then arrive in the kingdom of Gandhāra. The king of that kingdom will prepare offerings and support them for two years, bringing peace and ease to all. After two years, the king will die. He has two sons: one who believes in the buddha-dharma and one who follows non-Buddhist practices. They will contend for the throne. The monks will assist the son who has faith in the Dharma, and he will prevail and assume the kingship. During his reign, he will increase the monks' stipends and provisions.

"After half a year, a bhikṣu will kill the king and seize the throne himself. All the people of Gandhāra will then rise up at once and kill the bhikṣu-king. All the remaining monks will be entirely expelled beyond the kingdom's borders. Thus the semblance Dharma in the kingdom of Gandhāra will also be extinguished.

"All the monks will be displaced and scattered, fleeing to other kingdoms. At such a time, the king of the western lands, the king of the northern lands, and the Yavana king will have merit and fortune surpassing their predecessors. These three kings will form an alliance. The three kings will then govern the western and northern lands. Each of the three kings will lead one hundred thousand soldiers to attack the kingdom of Kuśumī. The king of Kuśumī will destroy the three hundred thousand soldiers and their kings, leaving not a single one.

"The king of Kuśumī, wishing to repent of the sin of killing the armies, will summon all the monks within Jambudvīpa to the kingdom of Kuśumī. Afterward, however, all the assembled monks will quarrel among themselves and mutually kill one another until none remain. Thus, throughout Jambudvīpa, the semblance Dharma of the Buddha will from that point be entirely extinguished.

"Each of these events is explained in detail in the *Sūtra of the Prophecy of Candragarbha Bodhisattva*. After the parinirvāṇa of Śākyamuni Tathāgata, after fifty-seven koṭi and sixty million years, the World-Honored Maitreya will appear in the sahā world to guide and transform sentient beings."
