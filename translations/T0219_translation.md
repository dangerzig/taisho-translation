# 醫喻經
## Sūtra of the Medical Simile

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 219

Translated from the Chinese. Song, translated by Dānapāla

---

Thus have I heard.

At one time the World-Honored One was dwelling in the land of Śrāvastī together with an assembly of bhikṣus. At that time the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣus, saying: "You should know that an excellent physician of the world, who knows diseases and recognizes medicines, possesses four qualities. If he fully embodies them, he may be called a King of Physicians. What are these four? First, he recognizes which disease requires which medicine. Second, he knows the origin of the disease and applies medicine according to that origin. Third, for diseases that have already arisen, he treats them so as to expel the disease. Fourth, he cuts off the source of the disease so that it does not arise again. These are the four.

"What is meant by 'recognizing which disease requires which medicine'? It means first recognizing the characteristics of a given disease, and knowing that such-and-such medicine is appropriate for treating it, so that the patient may obtain ease and well-being.

"What is meant by 'knowing the origin of the disease and applying medicine accordingly'? It means knowing that a disease arises from wind, or from bile, or from phlegm, or from retained humors, or from the joints, or from accumulation and excess. Knowing the source of such diseases, medicine is applied accordingly, so that the patient may obtain ease and well-being.

"What is meant by 'for diseases that have already arisen, treating them so as to expel the disease'? It means knowing that the disease should be expelled through the eyes, or treated through the nose by various means, or expelled by fumigation or nasal irrigation, or by drawing breath out through the nostrils, or by vomiting and purging, or by inducing perspiration throughout the body, and so on through the upper and lower parts of the body as appropriate. Knowing the pathways through which such diseases may be expelled, medicine is skillfully applied so that the patient may obtain ease and well-being.

"What is meant by 'cutting off the source of the disease so that it does not arise again'? It means recognizing the characteristics of the disease's source and knowing how it should be removed. One must diligently bring forth energetic effort and accomplish this task, skillfully severing and eliminating the source, so that the disease will never arise again afterward and the patient may obtain ease and well-being. Such are what are called the four kinds of knowing diseases and recognizing medicines.

"The Tathāgata, Worthy One, Perfectly and Fully Enlightened One, is likewise: appearing in the world, he proclaims four kinds of supreme Dharma-medicines. What are these four? They are the Noble Truth of Suffering, the Noble Truth of Origination, the Noble Truth of Cessation, and the Noble Truth of the Path. The Buddha knows these Four Noble Truths as they truly are, and teaches them to sentient beings, enabling them to sever the process of birth. Because the process of birth, which is the very root of suffering, is severed, old age, sickness, death, sorrow, lamentation, pain, and distress, all suffering, is permanently extinguished. For this benefit, the Tathāgata, Worthy One, Perfectly and Fully Enlightened One proclaims these supreme Dharma-medicines, enabling all sentient beings to be liberated from all suffering. Bhikṣus, furthermore, just as a Wheel-Turning Sage King, being fully equipped with his four armies, thereby obtains sovereignty as he wishes, so too is it with the Tathāgata, Worthy One, Perfectly and Fully Enlightened One."

When the Buddha had finished teaching this sūtra, the assembly of bhikṣus rejoiced and accepted it with faith.
