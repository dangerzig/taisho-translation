# 佛頂尊勝陀羅尼經
## Sūtra of the Uṣṇīṣavijaya Dhāraṇī

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0968

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Du Xíngyǐ (杜行顗)

---

Homage to the All-Knowing One.

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the kingdom of Śrāvastī, in the Jeta Grove, the park of Anāthapiṇḍada, together with a great assembly of eight thousand bhikṣus, and thirty-two thousand bodhisattvas who had attained right wisdom, illuminated all dharmas, and in knowing what is to be known were free from all obstruction. Their names were: Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, Mahānaya Bodhisattva, Maitreya Bodhisattva, Mañjuśrī Kumāra-bhūta Bodhisattva, Padmottama-garbha Bodhisattva, Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva, Dharaṇīṃdhara Bodhisattva, Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva, Sarva-nīvaraṇa-viṣkambhin Bodhisattva, and Samantabhadra Bodhisattva as the foremost. Such were the thirty-two thousand bodhisattva-mahāsattvas. There were also ten thousand Brahma-devas, with Subrahmā as their foremost, who had come from other realms to the Buddha's place and were gathered in the assembly.

There were also twelve thousand hosts of the Śakra-devas, together with immeasurable devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, humans, and non-humans, all gathered in the assembly.

At that time, the Holy Honored One, surrounded by the four assemblies, revered and honored with offerings, was teaching the Dharma. In the hall of the Good Dharma in the Trāyastriṃśa Heaven, there was a deva named Supratiṣṭhita, who dwelt in a great jeweled palace, fully enjoying pleasures. Beautiful attendants waited upon him, and he sported, galloped, and toured about, amusing himself and fulfilling all his desires. Suddenly, in the night, a voice called out: "Deva Supratiṣṭhita! Your time has come. In seven days you will die. Through seven lives in Jambudvīpa, you will enter hell. After that, you may be born as a human, poor and blind from birth, suffering all manner of affliction." Supratiṣṭhita, hearing this, was terrified and panic-stricken. His hair stood on end with grief. He rushed to the place of Śakra-devānām-indra, bowed at Indra's feet, and in frantic fear addressed him: "I beg you, Lord, to have mercy and save me from this suffering! Save me from this suffering! I was enjoying celestial pleasures, gratifying my heart at will, when suddenly a voice said: 'In seven days your life will end. Through seven lives in Jambudvīpa, you will enter hell. After that, you may be born as a human, poor and blind from birth, suffering all manner of affliction.' I am now frantic, my mind confused and my consciousness disordered. I know no way out. May the Lord, in compassion, rescue me from this poisonous suffering." Śakra, hearing these words, was deeply grieved and astonished: "How can it be, seven lives?" After a moment of silent contemplation, he saw that upon Supratiṣṭhita's death, he would assume the body of a pig. After the pig, the body of a dog. After the dog, the body of a fox. After the fox, the body of a monkey. After the monkey, the body of a poisonous snake. After the snake, the body of a vulture. After the vulture, the body of a crow. Through these seven lives, he would constantly eat filth and refuse. Śakra, seeing this, deeply pitied Supratiṣṭhita, who was to undergo such great suffering. What plan, what recourse? Who could save him? He thought: "Only the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Samyak-saṃbuddha can save him."

Thereupon Indra, in the latter part of that night, took various flower-garlands, various incenses, powdered incense, burned incense, celestial garments, necklaces, and all manner of adornments, and proceeded to the Jeta Grove, to the Holy Honored One's place. He bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, circumambulated seven times to the right, and having made great offerings, sat to one side before the Buddha and reported the matter of Supratiṣṭhita in full to the Holy One. At that time, from the Tathāgata's crown there emanated a great radiance of various colors, flowing and illuminating all the realms of beings in the ten directions. It returned to the Buddha's place, circumambulated three times to the right, and entered through the Buddha's mouth. The Buddha then smiled gently and told Śakra: "There is a Buddha-consecration, purifying-all-destinies Uṣṇīṣavijaya Dhāraṇī that entirely purifies all karmic obstructions, the suffering of hell, the animal realm, and the realm of Yama, birth and death. It shatters the path to hell and elevates one to the path of the Buddhas. Lord of the Devas! This purifying-all-destinies Uṣṇīṣavijaya Dhāraṇī: merely for one who hears it, all karmic obstructions of the continuity of birth and death, all manner of suffering and affliction, are entirely extinguished. One will obtain wholesome fruits and the knowledge of past lives. From one Buddha-land one will be born in another Buddha-land, from one heaven one will be born in another heaven, up to the Trāyastriṃśa Heaven palace, always knowing one's past lives. One who can practice and uphold it will, within one's present hundred-year span, have one's life further extended. Body, speech, and mind become purified, the mind peaceful and content, all bodily suffering removed. One obtains all excellent sensations. All the Buddhas gaze upon one, all the devas guard and protect one, and all the bodhisattvas hold one in compassionate affection. For one who reads and recites it, all the hells, the animal realm, the realm of Yama, and all the hungry ghosts will cease, disperse, and their domains become empty. The gates of merit in all the Buddha-lands, the bodhisattvas' celestial palaces will all be opened, guiding one to enter."

Thereupon Śakra addressed the Buddha: "Holy Honored One! In pity and protection of all sentient beings, I pray you will teach the purifying-all-destinies Uṣṇīṣavijaya Dhāraṇī."

At that time, the Holy Honored One, accepting Indra's request, spoke the dhāraṇī:

"*Namo bhagavate trailokya-prativiśiṣṭāya buddhāya tadyathā oṃ viśuddhe viśuddhe mama samanta-avabhāsa-spharaṇa-gati-gahana svabhāva-viśuddhe abhiṣiñcatu sugata-varacana amṛta-vibhūṣite āhara āhara āyuḥ-sandhāraṇi śuddhe śuddhe gagana-viśuddhe uṣṇīṣa-vijaya-viśuddhe sahasra-raśmi-saṃcodite sarva-tathāgata-adhiṣṭhāna-adhiṣṭhite abhiri-vajra-kāya-saṃhata-na-viśuddhe sarva-āvaraṇa-viśuddhe pratyaya-niyata-bhaya āyuḥ-viśuddhe samaya-adhiṣṭhite maṇi maṇi mama maṇi-tathata bhūta-koṭi-pariśuddhe visphuṭa-buddhi-śuddhe jaya jaya vijaya vijaya smara smara buddha-adhiṣṭhita-śuddhe vajri vajra-garbhe vajrāṃ-bhavatu mama mahā-yāna-prabhāgata-viśuddhe sarva-gati-pariśuddhe sarva-tathāgata samāśvāsa-adhiṣṭhite buddhaya buddhaya vibudhaya samanta-pariśuddhe sarva-tathāgata-adhiṣṭhāna-adhiṣṭhite svāhā*"

"Lord of the Devas! This purifying-all-destinies Uṣṇīṣavijaya Dhāraṇī purifies all transgressive obstructions and removes all evil destinies. Eighty-eight koṭis of hundreds of thousands of Ganges-sands of Buddhas have together proclaimed it, guarded it, and praised it with joy. All the Tathāgatas have sealed it with the wisdom-seal, for the sake of removing all the evil destinies; for the sake of liberating all those in hell, the animal realm, and the realm of Yama, all transgressive and evil beings submerged in the ocean of suffering and enduring all manner of painful poison; for the sake of all beings of short life-span, scant merit, lowly station, base birth, and evil karma; for the sake of all beings heading toward hell, all manner of life-forms that die prematurely and lose their minds, and all beings who have strayed from the right path, this dhāraṇī was taught. Throughout Jambudvīpa, it purifies transgressive karma and leads beings to liberation. Lord of the Devas! You should uphold this Uṣṇīṣavijaya Dhāraṇī and teach it to Supratiṣṭhita. You should proclaim it to the beings of Jambudvīpa, the devas and celestial princes, and all sentient beings. Read, recite, contemplate, practice, revere, make offerings, receive, uphold, and cultivate it. I now, with the dhāraṇī-seal, entrust and commit it to you, Lord of the Devas. You should uphold it well. One who hears this dhāraṇī will have all karmic obstructions accumulated over hundreds of thousands of kalpas entirely purified. They will not fall into hell, will not be born among animals, in the realm of Yama, among hungry ghosts, or in any evil destiny. They will not receive the body of an asura, yakṣa, rākṣasa, pūtana, kaṭapūtana, epileptic or other ghost-spirit, nor even the body of a dog, turtle, bird, beast, mosquito, gadfly, poisonous snake, or creeping creature, nor any small insect; they will never again receive such bodies. They will constantly be with the Buddha. They will be born into a victorious bodhisattva lineage, or into a brahmin great family, a kṣatriya great family, or a great-family householder. By the power of this dhāraṇī, one obtains a purified birth, and upon sitting at the seat of bodhi, one attains perfect, universal awakening. Lord of the Devas! This purifying-all-destinies Uṣṇīṣavijaya Dhāraṇī has vast and great spiritual power, great meritorious fruits, great efficacy, and great auspiciousness. Like a sun-treasury jewel, brilliant and without stain, pure as empty space, wherever it abides there is the shining of radiance. Wherever this dhāraṇī is placed, its spiritual power pervades and covers, just as described. One who upholds this dhāraṇī cannot be stained by any transgression. It is gentle, moist, pure, and without stain, like Jāmbūnada gold. One who writes, studies, reads, recites, makes offerings to, receives, upholds, and cultivates this dhāraṇī should know that in that place the karma of hell is removed and all destinies are purified. If one writes this dhāraṇī and places it upon a banner-pole, erecting it on a high mountain, a high rooftop, or any other elevated place, or within a stūpa: if any bhikṣu, bhikṣuṇī, upāsaka, upāsikā, good man, or good woman sees this dhāraṇī-banner, or comes near the banner, or if the shadow of the banner touches their body, or if the wind from the banner blows upon their body, or if dust carried by the banner's wind touches their body, their transgressive karma will be eliminated. They will not be born in hell, the animal realm, the realm of Yama, among hungry ghosts, or among asuras. They will not fall into any evil destiny. All the Tathāgatas and Buddhas will bestow upon them the prophecy of attaining non-retrogression and ascending to right awakening. With various flowers and incense, various garlands, various banners and parasols, pennants and canopies, necklaces, all manner of adornments and embellishments, one should make great offerings. At a crossroads, build a stūpa and enshrine this dhāraṇī within. Those who practice the path and bow in reverence should know that such persons are mahāsattvas, children of the Buddha's Dharma, pillars of the Dharma, and stūpas of the relics."

At that time, Yama Dharma-rāja, as the night was ending toward dawn, came to the Buddha's place. With various celestial flowers, various incenses, mixed incenses, burned incenses, celestial garments, and necklaces, he made great offerings, circumambulated seven times to the right, bowed at the Buddha's feet, and addressed the Buddha: "Holy Honored One! I know the Buddha's grace, and am not ungrateful. Now, to repay the Buddha's grace, I will protect all beings and constantly serve this dhāraṇī of great spiritual power and great meritorious fruit with diligence. I will close the gates of hell and purify all the evil destinies."

At that time, the Four Heavenly Kings circumambulated the Buddha three times and addressed the Buddha: "May the Holy Honored One teach the method of practice for this dhāraṇī." The Buddha told the Four Heavenly Kings: "Listen carefully! Listen carefully! I will now teach it. If anyone has a short life-span and seeks longevity, on the fifteenth day of the bright half of the month, one should bathe clean, put on pure garments, observe the eight precepts and fast, and recite this dhāraṇī one thousand times. All obstructions will be removed, illness will be healed, life-span extended, and one will obtain great peace and happiness. The sufferings of hell, the animal realm, and all the evil destinies will all be liberated. Even animals and evil-destiny beings whose ears hear this dhāraṇī will never again receive an evil-destiny body. Those entangled in grave illness will find all illness dispersed, all destinies purified, and after death they will be born in the realm of present happiness, transformed from a lotus, not received in a womb, and will always know their past lives. If any sentient being, dragged by transgressive karma, is born after death in an evil destiny, one should empower earth with this dhāraṇī mantra twenty-one times and scatter it upon the bones of the deceased. Wherever they have been born, whether in hell, the animal realm, the realm of Yama, or the hungry-ghost destiny, even in the Avīci Hell or among birds, beasts, and insects, they will shed their body of suffering and be born in heaven to enjoy happiness. If one recites this dhāraṇī twenty-one times daily, transgressions will be extinguished and merit increased. All people will love and respect one. After death, one will be born in the land of Sukhāvatī. If one constantly recites and upholds this dhāraṇī, after death one will be born in the various pure lands, proceeding from one Buddha-land to another, creating great radiance in all the Buddha-lands, constantly being with the Buddha, nurtured and protected by all the Buddhas, receiving the prophecy of Buddhahood, and ultimately attaining the bliss of great nirvāṇa. One who practices this dhāraṇī should prepare a square altar with sides of equal length, scatter various pure flowers, burn various incenses, place the right knee on the ground, kneel upright with single-minded attention, and recollect all the Buddhas of the ten directions universally. Form the ten-fingernail-join seal and place it at the heart. The two index fingers and the two thumbs each bend and press against each other. Say 'sādhu' and recite this dhāraṇī one hundred and eight times. This constitutes making offerings and serving eighty-eight koṭis of Ganges-sands of nayutas of hundreds of thousands of Buddhas, making offerings as the Buddhas make offerings. It also constitutes making offerings to the Four Great Heavenly Kings. All will praise and commend one as a child of the Buddha. With great bodhi one adorns one's mind and obtains unobstructed wisdom. Whenever one wishes to recite this dhāraṇī, one should with utmost sincerity form the ten-fingernail-join seal according to the Dharma. This is the method for cultivating and practicing the dhāraṇī."

The Buddha told Śakra: "Lord of the Devas! You should use this Dharma to rescue beings from the suffering and affliction of hell and all the evil destinies, purify the karma of all destinies, and obtain the fruit of longevity. Lord of the Devas, you should return and bestow this dhāraṇī upon the deva-prince Supratiṣṭhita. After seven full days, you and Supratiṣṭhita should come together to my place."

Śakra, having received the Buddha's teaching, returned to the celestial palace and bestowed this Uṣṇīṣavijaya Dhāraṇī upon the deva-prince Supratiṣṭhita. Supratiṣṭhita, having received it, practiced and made offerings for six days and six nights. Upon the seventh day, all the karmic obstructions of the evil destinies were entirely liberated. He dwelt in the celestial realm of merit, obtained great longevity, and with joy and exultation cried out in a loud voice: "Aho buddhāya! Aho dharmāya! Aho saṃghāya! Aho bidyā, which rescues and delivers sentient beings from all suffering and calamity, has caused me to be freed from the suffering of all the evil destinies!"

At that time, Śakra-devānām-indra, surrounded by his retinue and attendants, together with the deva-prince Supratiṣṭhita, brought various flowers, incense, burned incense, unguent, various garlands, parasols, pennants, banners, garments, and necklaces. Riding a celestial jeweled chariot, fully adorned with celestial embellishments, they came to the Buddha's place and made great offerings. They circumambulated ten thousand times to the right, then stood to one side before the Buddha. Having praised the Buddha in verse, they withdrew and sat to hear the Dharma. The Buddha extended his golden right hand to comfort and protect Supratiṣṭhita, and bestowed upon him the prophecy of Buddhahood.
