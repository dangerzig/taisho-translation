# 佛說苾芻迦尸迦十法經
## Sūtra on the Ten Kāśika Dharmas for Bhikṣus Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1480

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmadeva (法天).

---

The Tathāgata, Arhat, Perfectly Awakened One, was in the kingdom of Śrāvastī together with the assembly of bhikṣus. At that time, the Tathāgata broadly explained the code of a teacher for those gathered, both devas and humans, as well as for those of the future.

The Tathāgata then addressed the assembly of bhikṣus: "A bhikṣu who is complete in ten dharmas may ordain others, allowing them to go forth from the household life and receive the precepts as bhikṣus. He may dwell independently without reliance for one lifetime and may serve as a support for others. What are these ten? First, possessing a sense of shame and delight in the precepts. Second, having extensive learning of the Dharma. Third, having extensive learning of the Vinaya. Fourth, having the capacity for correct practice, so that when offenses against the precepts arise, he acts in accordance with the Dharma and the Vinaya. Fifth, having the capacity for correct practice, so that when offenses, wrongful conduct, or wrong views arise, he acts in accordance with the Dharma and the Vinaya. Sixth, having the capacity for correct practice in caring for the sick and providing them stable support. Seventh, having the capacity for correct practice, delighting in the established Dharma and Vinaya, explaining them himself and causing others to explain them. Eighth, having the capacity for correct practice in explaining the precepts of bodily conduct. Ninth, having the capacity for correct practice in explaining the precepts of the pure conduct of one who has gone forth. Tenth, having ten years' standing, or having completed ten full years, or more than ten years. Bhikṣus, you should abide complete in these ten dharmas.

"How does a bhikṣu possess a sense of shame and delight in the precepts? This bhikṣu gains the following understanding: 'How can I, not yet having committed an āpatti, swiftly avoid committing an āpatti, and act as a bhikṣu in accordance with the Dharma and the Vinaya?' This is possessing a sense of shame and delight in the precepts.

"How does a bhikṣu have extensive learning and knowledge of the Dharma? This bhikṣu gains what the Dharma treasury teaches and gains what extensive learning teaches, so as to gain knowledge of the power of the Dharma, and can explain the Four Noble Truths both broadly and concisely. Bhikṣus, this is having extensive learning and knowledge of the Dharma.

"How does a bhikṣu have extensive learning and knowledge of the Vinaya? This bhikṣu knows the explanations of the two Vinayas. He knows the two Prātimokṣas broadly and recites them. In walking, standing, sitting, and lying down, he recites them by mouth and reflects on them in mind, observing with subtle attention. Bhikṣus, this is having extensive learning and knowledge of the Vinaya.

"How does a bhikṣu have the capacity for correct practice, so that when offenses against the precepts arise, he acts in accordance with the Dharma and the Vinaya? This bhikṣu knows what constitutes an āpatti and what does not constitute an āpatti. He knows a light āpatti and a grave āpatti. He knows an āpatti arising from karma and one not arising from karma. He knows a middling āpatti, a prior āpatti, and a subsequent āpatti. He knows an āpatti already arisen and one not yet arisen. When an āpatti arises, he can identify each one. Bhikṣus, this is having the capacity for correct practice, acting in accordance with the Dharma and the Vinaya.

"How does a bhikṣu have the capacity for correct practice, so that when offenses, wrongful conduct, or wrong views arise, he acts in accordance with the Dharma and the Vinaya? Bhikṣus, this capacity for correct practice means he can explain dependent origination broadly and concisely, namely: ignorance conditions formations, formations condition consciousness, consciousness conditions name-and-form, name-and-form conditions the six sense-bases, the six sense-bases condition contact, contact conditions feeling, feeling conditions craving, craving conditions grasping, grasping conditions becoming, becoming conditions birth, and birth conditions aging, death, grief, sorrow, suffering, and distress. Thus the entire great mass of suffering arises. When ignorance ceases, formations cease; when formations cease, consciousness ceases; when consciousness ceases, name-and-form ceases; when name-and-form ceases, the six sense-bases cease; when the six sense-bases cease, contact ceases; when contact ceases, feeling ceases; when feeling ceases, craving ceases; when craving ceases, grasping ceases; when grasping ceases, becoming ceases; when becoming ceases, birth ceases; when birth ceases, aging, death, grief, sorrow, suffering, and distress all cease. With such cessation, the entire great mass of suffering ceases. Bhikṣus, this is having the capacity for correct practice regarding offenses, wrongful conduct, and wrong views, acting in accordance with the Dharma and the Vinaya.

"How does a bhikṣu have the capacity for correct practice in caring for the sick and providing stable support? Bhikṣus, this capacity for correct practice in caring for the sick means that according to the patient's illness one provides medicine, fills one's own dwelling, and does not harbor aversion. Bhikṣus, this is having the capacity for correct practice in caring for the sick and providing stable support.

"How does a bhikṣu have the capacity for correct practice, delighting in the established Dharma and Vinaya, explaining them himself and causing others to explain them? Bhikṣus, this capacity for correct practice means delighting in the Dharma of marks and signlessness, explaining it oneself and causing others to explain it, and being fully delighted and willing to teach. Bhikṣus, this is having the capacity for correct practice, delighting in the established Dharma and Vinaya, explaining them oneself and causing others to explain them.

"How does a bhikṣu have the capacity for correct practice in explaining the precepts of bodily conduct? Bhikṣus, this capacity for correct practice means observing offenses, and having observed the offenses, inspecting them: observing the manner of opening the saṃghāṭī robe, receiving and using the bowl, receiving food and drink, and so forth including speech and love. Bhikṣus, this is having the capacity for correct practice in explaining the precepts of bodily conduct.

"How does a bhikṣu have the capacity for correct practice in explaining the precepts and āpattis of the pure conduct? Bhikṣus, this capacity for correct practice means the four foundations of mindfulness, the four right efforts, the four bases of spiritual power, the four immeasurables, the five faculties, the five powers, the seven factors of awakening, the eightfold noble path, śamatha, vipaśyanā, mindfulness, the precepts, freedom from afflictions, and selflessness. Thus, bhikṣus, this is having the capacity for correct practice in explaining the precepts and āpattis of the pure conduct.

"How does a bhikṣu have ten years, or ten full years, or more than ten years? Bhikṣus, when the years are complete, this is called being fully qualified.

"Bhikṣus, when a bhikṣu is complete in these ten dharmas, he may ordain others, allowing them to go forth from the household life and receive the precepts. He may dwell independently without reliance for one lifetime and may serve as a support for others. If a bhikṣu is deficient in these ten dharmas and yet ordains others, grants them precepts, dwells without reliance, or serves as a support for others, then day by day his precepts incur a duṣkṛta offense. For each of his dharmas in which he is deficient, he incurs an āpatti. For each of his dharmas in which he is deficient, day by day his precepts incur a duṣkṛta offense. Bhikṣus, with regard to these ten years of precepts, you should faithfully uphold the three dharmas with a single mind. What are the three? Ordaining others, serving as support, and dwelling without reliance."

Thus the World-Honored One, Arhat, Perfectly Awakened One, having explained these ten dharmas, the precepts, offenses, and their consequences, the great assembly of bhikṣus and others were filled with joy, faithfully received and practiced them, bowed to the Buddha, and withdrew.
