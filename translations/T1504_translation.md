# 菩薩五法懺悔文
## Text of the Bodhisattva's Fivefold Confession

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1504

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown.

---

### Confession

"Buddhas of the ten directions and three times, with the five eyes you illuminate the world. With the three kinds of great [knowledge], there is nothing you do not know; you clearly see the characteristics of transgression and merit. Your disciple so-and-so, from countless kalpas past, not having encountered a good spiritual friend, has committed every kind of transgression. I have broken the precepts, violating the four grave offenses, the six grave offenses, and the eight grave offenses. I have slandered the Dharma and severed wholesome roots, fully becoming an icchantika. Fortunately, I have now encountered all the tathāgatas, the scriptural Dharma, and the assembly of worthies and sages, who are able to remove all transgressions. Your disciple bows with head to the ground. May the evil clouds disperse and may the unsurpassed wisdom be awakened."

When the confession is complete, prostrate with the five limbs touching the ground.

### Requesting the Buddhas

"All buddhas of the ten directions, when you first ascended to the place of awakening, contemplated the tree, practiced walking meditation, and had not yet turned the Dharma wheel, ignorance, old age, and death brought long decline and grief. May you provide the medicine of the Dharma to cure all affliction and suffering. May the rain of the Dharma flow and spread to all withered sentient beings. Having attained the path with clear understanding, all buddhas presently in the ten directions, having already liberated those with affinity: sentient beings are mostly indolent, and through skillful means you manifest nirvāṇa. Your disciple sincerely bows and requests the buddhas to remain long in the world. All bodhisattvas who have already generated the unsurpassed aspiration, may you diligently apply vigor. In worlds without a buddha, may you manifest the attainment of supreme, perfect awakening and universally liberate all living beings. None surpass the Buddha in compassion; therefore, with utmost sincerity, I make this request."

When the request to the buddhas is complete, bow with head to the ground.

### Rejoicing

"Throughout past lives, harboring jealousy, pride, hatred, and delusion, when seeing others gain benefit it was like an arrow piercing the heart; when hearing of others' happiness it was like a nail driven into the eye. Due to these transgressions and obstructions, I fell into the three evil destinies and never encountered the buddhas. Today, with single-minded awakening, I generate the great mind of sympathetic joy. The buddhas of the ten directions and three times, together with their assemblies of disciples, whose numbers are beyond measure, from the first arising of a single thought up to sitting at the place of awakening, their great giving through the four immeasurables, their pure upholding of the precepts, concentration, wisdom, and liberation, and their immeasurable knowledge and insight: your disciple rejoices in all of it. With a mind of wisdom bright and clear, the darkness of ignorant obstruction is extinguished. In a single thought of sympathetic joy, the merit fills the ten directions, and wisdom equals that of all buddhas."

When the rejoicing is complete, prostrate with the five limbs touching the ground.

### Dedication

"Going back and forth in birth-and-death, from birth one proceeds to death, from nobility one returns to lowliness, for one has not yet attained nirvāṇa. The Dharma body is eternally pure, and prajñā is wondrous liberation. I should now seek this benefit. All meritorious deeds that I may have, I gather together and dedicate to sentient beings, that we may jointly accomplish the unsurpassed path. Vast as empty space, formless as true wisdom, ultimately exhausting the dharma-realm, may the vajra wisdom of emptiness be ever present before me. Without practice, supernatural powers respond to every need."

When the dedication is complete, bow with head to the ground.

### Aspiration

"With a sincere mind I generate a great aspiration, to practice the path in accordance with my vow. May my mind of wisdom be like a fierce wind, and my power of concentration like vajra. After this dedication, thought after thought, may compassion increase. Casting off attachment and clinging, may I joyfully liberate all beings. When the time comes to relinquish body and life, may the Buddha emit light to extinguish all difficulties and obstructions. May I be born by transformation in Tuṣita Heaven and behold the Honored Maitreya face to face. Cultivating the marks of excellence to full perfection, with all six sense faculties completely penetrating, hearing the Buddha expound the wondrous Dharma, I shall immediately realize the patient acceptance of the unborn, and all shall abide in the stage of non-retrogression. Riding the power of great supernatural abilities, I shall travel throughout the lands of the ten directions, making offerings to all buddhas with immeasurable wondrous sounds, praising the merits of the buddhas. In the twenty-five states of existence, there shall be no time when I do not manifest a body. Like the sun illuminating the world, with radiance brightening the ten directions, in every dark and hidden place, I shall serve as a lamp. Though I attain the path of buddhahood, turn the Dharma wheel, and manifest nirvāṇa, as long as sentient beings have not all become buddhas, I shall not abandon the vows of Samantabhadra and Mañjuśrī."

When the aspiration is complete, purify the mind and bow.
