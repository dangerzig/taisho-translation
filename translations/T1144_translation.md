# 佛說彌勒菩薩發願王偈
## Verses of the King of Aspirations of Maitreya Bodhisattva Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1144

Translated from the Chinese. Qing dynasty, translated by Gongbu Chabu (工布查布).

---

Sanskrit: *Ārya-praṇidhāna-rāja*

Chinese: The Holy Maitreya's King of Aspirations

Homage to all buddhas, bodhisattvas, and mahāsattvas.

The Buddha told Ānanda: "Maitreya Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, in the past when he vowed to practice the path of bodhi, six times day and night, bared his right shoulder, placed his right knee on the ground, joined his palms in reverence, and uttered these aspiration verses:

> "Homage to all the buddhas, and to the ṛṣis who possess the divine eye,
> To the bodhisattva mahāsattvas, and to all the śrāvaka assemblies.
> They cause beings to avoid the pits of evil destinies, and reveal the path to nirvāṇa,
> Leading them into the realm free of old age and death: homage to the bodhicitta.
> Whatever offenses I have committed through my ignorant mind,
> I now, before all the buddhas, repent of them without remainder.
> The accumulated merit and provisions from the three gates of my body, speech, and mind:
> These are the seeds of my universal wisdom; may my path be without end.
> In all the lands of the ten directions, whatever offerings have been made to the buddhas,
> And whatever the buddhas have rejoiced in: I now rejoice in all of them.
> Repenting of all offenses, rejoicing in all wholesome roots,
> Paying homage to all the buddhas: may I attain great wisdom.
> In all places of the ten directions, wherever there are great bodhisattvas
> Who abide on the ten bhūmis: I beseech them to swiftly attain perfect awakening.
> Having realized the heart of perfect enlightenment and vanquished all the hosts of Māra,
> For the benefit of all sentient beings: I beseech them to turn the dharma wheel.
> With the sound of the great dharma drum, may they ferry sentient beings beyond the shore of suffering;
> May they, for immeasurable kalpas, ever abide in the world teaching the dharma.
> I pray that they set in motion great compassionate wisdom, mercifully aiding all sentient beings,
> Those who have sunk in the mire of desire and are bound by the fetters of attachment.
> Sentient beings are obstructed, yet the buddhas do not despise or abandon them;
> With compassion for all the conscious, they pull them from the ocean of the three realms of existence.
> Whatever path-dharmas practiced by all buddhas of the three times,
> I now follow and learn from all of them: may I realize the path of bodhi.
> Together ascending to the six further shores, liberating sentient beings of the six destinies,
> Realizing the six supernatural powers: may I accomplish the unsurpassed path.
> The unborn and the original source, without location, without self-nature:
> Awakening to this principle of true emptiness, there is no wisdom and no attainment.
> No life, no sentient being, no turning away, no approaching:
> May I comprehend the dharma of no-self, and so equal the great ṛṣi buddhas.
> May I not abide in the two attachments to any possession whatsoever,
> But for the benefit of sentient beings, unstintingly practice generosity.
> Though goods and resources be lavish and abundant, I should use them abundantly;
> The nature of things is equal to empty quiescence: perfecting the pāramitā of giving.
> May the precepts I receive and observe be flawless and most pure,
> Free from delusion, pride, and arrogance: perfecting the pāramitā of moral discipline.
> Like earth, water, fire, and wind, the four great elements neither resisting nor rejecting,
> Bearing all transgressions without anger or hatred: perfecting the pāramitā of patience.
> Generating the mind of diligence from beginning to end, ever joyful and never slack,
> With courageous strength of body and mind: perfecting the pāramitā of vigor.
> Subduing and gathering in right concentration, the illusory samādhi,
> Vajra-dhyāna and the rest: perfecting the pāramitā of meditation.
> The three gates of liberation, the dharma of equality in the three times,
> Clearly understanding through hearing, thinking, and cultivating: perfecting the pāramitā of wisdom.
> Praised by all the buddhas, with blazing, brilliant, and radiant light,
> Diligently cultivating the bodhisattva path, fulfilling the aspirations for self and others.
> Practicing this path of bodhi, renowned as a child of Maitreya,
> Perfecting the practice of the six pāramitās, transcending to the stage of the ten bhūmis."

> "By these wholesome aspirations, may sentient beings, at the end of their lives, swiftly go to the Tuṣita Palace,
> Born within the Dharma-Viewing Pavilion, becoming the eldest child of Maitreya.
> Descending to Jambudvīpa with sovereignty over the ten bhūmis,
> When I realize the great stage of the ten powers,
> May I receive the amṛta taste of the wondrous dharma,
> Perfectly fulfilling all the practices of a buddha without remainder.
> From this life's end, by the power of aspirations,
> May I be immediately reborn in the Tuṣita Heaven,
> Joyfully serving the Honored Maitreya,
> And swiftly receive the prediction of bodhi."

"Ānanda, you should know that Maitreya Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, by thus truly and skillfully cultivating pure practices, with a smooth and level path attained unsurpassed bodhi, perfect awakening, and accomplished the gate of true and great skillful means."

Translator's note: This sūtra has not been found in the Chinese or Tibetan canons, and no Tibetan edition exists either. However, the sages of India have composed considerable commentary upon it, and it is universally practiced as a regular devotional recitation, together with the "Chapter on Conduct and Vows" from the Avataṃsaka Sūtra and the "Chapter on the Thirty-Five Buddhas' Conduct and Vows," the three being called the Three Great Repentance-Aspirations. I have briefly summarized it here. It appears that when it was first translated from Sanskrit, it was perhaps excerpted from the essential portions of the Maitreya Ascent and Maitreya Descent sūtras. Now that peace prevails within the realm and the three teachings flourish together, with resources in no shortage and many people inclined toward goodness, I have recently heard of those who organize Dragon-Flower assemblies. Thinking that there may be those who wish to recite this, I have specially translated it into Chinese for the faithful to recite and pray.

May the holy Maitreya's compassionate protection spread broadly and circulate widely: this is my aspiration.
