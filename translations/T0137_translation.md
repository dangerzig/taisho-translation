# 舍利弗摩訶目連遊四衢經
## Sūtra on Śāriputra and Mahāmaudgalyāyana Traveling at the Crossroads

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0137

Translated from the Chinese. Later Han dynasty, translated by Kang Mengxiang (康孟詳)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Śākyan was in the Āmalaka Medicine-Tree Garden. At that time, the worthy Śāriputra and Mahāmaudgalyāyana, together with a great assembly of five hundred bhikṣus, had been traveling through various countries for a full year. They returned to the Medicine-Tree Garden, wishing to see the World-Honored One. As they arrived, the many bhikṣus were speaking to one another, each putting on their robes and taking up their bowls, and their voices were loud and their sounds carried far.

The Buddha, knowing this in advance, asked the worthy Ānanda: "Who are these bhikṣus raising such a great clamor, their sounds ringing out, like fishermen shouting as they haul their catch?"

Ānanda said to the Buddha: "Indeed, World-Honored One! Śāriputra and Maudgalyāyana have been traveling through various countries for a full year. Together with a great assembly of five hundred bhikṣus, they have arrived at the Medicine-Tree Garden. Seeing the bhikṣus each talking to one another, putting on their robes and taking up their bowls, their voices are loud and their sounds carry far."

The Buddha told Ānanda: "Do not let those bhikṣus come to me."

Ānanda said to the Buddha: "So be it." He received the command, rose from his seat, bowed at the Buddha's feet, circumambulated the Buddha three times, and withdrew. He went to where the bhikṣus Śāriputra and Maudgalyāyana were. Amid the noise of their conversation, he stood to one side and addressed the worthy Śāriputra and Maudgalyāyana: "The rest of the bhikṣus should not go to where the Buddha is. The World-Honored One has given this instruction."

When Śāriputra and Maudgalyāyana heard Ānanda's words, they immediately rose from their seats and went to where the Buddha was. They bowed at his feet, circumambulated the Buddha three times, then quickly gathered their robes and bowls and went out to the Medicine-Tree Garden together with the assembly of bhikṣus.

At that time, the Śākya lay-followers were all assembled, discussing a certain matter. From afar they saw Śāriputra and Mahāmaudgalyāyana coming with the assembly of bhikṣus, wearing their robes and carrying their bowls, going to the Medicine-Tree Garden in the morning daylight. "Five hundred bhikṣus are with them. Perhaps we should go and inquire about their well-being."

The assembly of Śākya lay-followers then quickly rose and went to where Śāriputra and Maudgalyāyana were. They bowed at their feet and stood to one side.

The faithful laypeople asked Śāriputra and Maudgalyāyana: "Why are you wearing your robes and carrying your bowls, going to the Medicine-Tree Garden in the daytime?"

Śāriputra and Maudgalyāyana answered the Śākya faithful laypeople: "We have been traveling through various countries and have returned to the assembly of bhikṣus. We are all weary and must lodge here in the open."

The faithful laypeople replied: "Worthy ones! We have here all that is needed: we shall provide seats and light lamps for illumination. We beseech you and the assembly of bhikṣus to condescend to stay. If the Buddha himself should reject you, then of course you would withdraw." The worthy Śāriputra and Mahāmaudgalyāyana silently agreed. They went to the provided quarters, sat upon the couches, and together with the Saṃgha entered the room and sat down.

At that time, the Śākya faithful laypeople went to where the Buddha was, bowed at his feet, and with palms joined said to the Buddha: "We beseech the World-Honored One to have compassion and allow them to stay. Great Sage, please have trust in the assembly of bhikṣus! Why? Among those bhikṣus whose defilements are exhausted, who have already attained arhatship and whose tasks are accomplished, we have no doubt. Regarding these bhikṣus we have no hesitation. But as for those bhikṣus who are young, newly studying, and who have just gone forth and entered this Dharma and Vinaya not long ago: their minds are still susceptible to change and may alter. It is like a sudden flood that comes with nothing to check it. Thus, World-Honored One, newly studying bhikṣus who have just gone forth and entered this Dharma and Vinaya not long ago, whose minds are still susceptible to change: if they do not see the Great Sage, we fear they may change their resolve."

At that moment, Brahmā suddenly descended and stood before the Buddha, and with palms joined said: "We beseech the World-Honored One to have compassion and allow them to stay. Great Sage, please have trust in the assembly of bhikṣus! Why? Among those bhikṣus whose defilements are exhausted, who have already attained arhatship and whose tasks are accomplished, we have no doubt. Regarding these bhikṣus we have no hesitation. But as for those bhikṣus who are young, newly studying, and who have just gone forth and entered this Dharma and Vinaya not long ago: their minds are still susceptible to change."

The Buddha then assented to Brahmā's request. The worthy Mahāmaudgalyāyana, seeing clearly with the divine eye from afar that the Buddha's mind had assented, and having been vouchsafed a vision of the Great Sage's virtue, saw it as clearly as a great lecture hall, clean and freshly coated, with all its windows open: when the sun first rises in the east and enters through the windows, its light illuminates the western wall. Just so, the worthy Maudgalyāyana, seeing clearly with the divine eye, saw from afar the World-Honored One's magnificent marks and characteristics. Maudgalyāyana then said to the assembly of bhikṣus: "Worthy ones! Rise, put on your robes and carry your bowls. Brahmā has made the request, and the young ones should all go."

The bhikṣus said: "We shall follow this instruction." They quickly adjusted their robes and, following Śāriputra and Mahāmaudgalyāyana, went to where the Buddha was. They bowed at his feet and withdrew to sit to one side.

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed Śāriputra: "In the past I also provided for the assembly of bhikṣus. What do you think about this?"

Śāriputra thought to himself: "The World-Honored One in his former lives provided for the assembly of bhikṣus. In the Great Sage's presence, the bhikṣus are simple and sincere, moderate in their wishes, knowing the proper measure, their conduct peaceful, their resolve always diligent. The Buddha, the Deva among Devas, is the King of Dharma. He tames those who are untamed, and they receive his teaching. The assembly of bhikṣus are light and impetuous in their movements, yet today the Great Sage has compassion for the Saṃgha."

The Buddha said: "Excellent! Excellent! Śāriputra! You should maintain precisely this thought and remove evil thoughts. Why? Who can bear the heavy burden for the assembly of bhikṣus? Only the Tathāgata, who dwells in all, together with Śāriputra and Mahāmaudgalyāyana."

Then the Buddha addressed Mahāmaudgalyāyana: "What do you think? Who has reverence for the assembly of bhikṣus? Who governs the assembly of bhikṣus?"

"I think: now the Buddha, the World-Honored One, has reverence for and governs the assembly of bhikṣus. Indeed, Great Sage! Among these bhikṣus, some are simple and sincere, of few desires and contented. Others may not be so, yet they consider their conduct peaceful and their diligence unwearying. The Tathāgata, the King of Dharma, should naturally be so. I also am thus."

The Buddha said: "Stop! Do not have such a thought! You should think differently. Why? Maudgalyāyana, who can bear the task of removing the heavy burden? Only the Tathāgata, together with Śāriputra and Mahāmaudgalyāyana.

> "By faith one crosses the flowing stream; heedlessness serves not as a vessel.
> The noble truths bring one across the suffering; through wisdom one reaches the final crossing."

When the Buddha analyzed and spoke these words, sixty bhikṣus' defilements were exhausted and their minds were liberated. Countless bhikṣus became free from the dust of defilement, and the Dharma-eye arose in them.

The Buddha spoke thus. The bhikṣus, faithful laypeople, devas, nāgas, and spirits all rejoiced.
