## Fascicle 1

### Sūtra on the Perception of Delight, Spoken by the Buddha

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Bhagavān was dwelling at Śrāvastī in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍada's park. At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣus: "Among those śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who have the perception of earth in regard to earth, who take delight in earth, who reckon earth as self, who declare, 'Earth is my self'—I say they do not know water, fire, or wind. The devas, Brahmā, the Ābhā devas, the Abhibhū devas, who have the perception of purity in regard to the pure, who take delight in purity, who reckon purity as self, who declare, 'Purity is my self'—I say they do not know the sphere of infinite space, the sphere of infinite consciousness, the sphere of nothingness, or the sphere of non-perception. Whether one or many, whether individually seeing, hearing, knowing, cognizing, attaining, observing, perceiving, or acting, from this world to the next world, from the next world to this world—those who have pleasurable perception about all this, who take delight in it, who reckon all of it as self—I say they do not know.

"Among those śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas who take earth as a matter for supernormal knowledge, who do not take delight in earth, who do not take delight in earth, who do not reckon earth as self, who do not declare, 'Earth is my self'—I say they already know water, fire, and wind. The devas, Brahmā, the Ābhā devas, the Abhibhū devas, who take purity as a matter for supernormal knowledge, who do not take delight in purity, who do not take delight in purity, who do not take purity as self—I say they already know the sphere of infinite space, the sphere of infinite consciousness, the sphere of nothingness, and the sphere of non-perception. Whether one or many, whether individually seeing, hearing, cognizing, knowing, attaining, observing, perceiving, or acting, from this world to the next world, from the next world to this world—those who, through supernormal knowledge, demonstrate non-delight and likewise non-delight, who do not reckon self, who do not declare, 'This is my self'—I say they already know.

"Furthermore, I myself take earth as a matter for supernormal knowledge; I do not take delight in earth, I do not take delight in earth, I do not take earth as self, I do not reckon earth as self; I already know water, fire, and wind. The devas, Brahmā, the Ābhā devas, the Abhibhū devas—through supernormal knowledge I do not take them as pure, do not take them as pure, do not reckon purity as self; I already know the sphere of infinite space, the sphere of infinite consciousness, the sphere of nothingness, and the sphere of non-perception. Whether one or many, whether individually seeing, hearing, cognizing, knowing, attaining, observing, perceiving, or acting, from this world to the next world, from the next world to this world—all of this, through supernormal knowledge, is non-delight, non-delight, not reckoned as self; I already know this."

Thus spoke the Buddha. Having heard what the World-Honored One taught, the bhikṣus rejoiced and were gladdened, and all āsavas arising therefrom were exhausted.
