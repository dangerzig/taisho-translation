# 佛說一切如來安像三昧儀軌經
## Sūtra of the Samādhi Ritual Manual for Installing Images of All Tathāgatas, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1418

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dānapāla (施護).

---

At that time, the World-Honored One entered the samādhi called the Vajra Image-Installation Samādhi of All Tathāgatas. Rising from that samādhi, he taught the ritual methods for the consecration and celebration of installing images of all buddhas and the assembly of worthy sages, whether molded, painted, carved, or adorned.

When one wishes to create a buddha image, one must first understand ten kinds of rules. The ācārya must further be well versed in all the perfect marks of the Buddha's body, as well as the proportions and measurements, large and small. Regarding the mantras, mudrās, Dharma-procedures, and ritual matters for pacification and increase, all must be thoroughly understood. Only then may one create the honored images within monasteries, halls, and stūpas.

If the buddha image that is created has deficient or lacking marks, the image-installation celebration must not be performed. If the marks are not complete, it will cause those sentient beings to experience great suffering and terror in the present and future. Therefore, one should single-mindedly strive to create a complete image.

Once the image is complete, one should have the ācārya who knows the Dharma-procedures invite the Buddha to install the image, make offerings, and celebrate according to the mantra ritual. Then the tathāgatas and worthy sages will descend and rejoice, and the meritorious qualities will be accomplished.

If, after creating an image, a long time passes without performing the image-installation celebration, then afterward one will instead reap inauspiciousness. Even if someone makes offerings and prostrations, there will ultimately be no merit or benefit. It is like an unintelligent person who lacks a proper human appearance, causing the world to look upon them with contempt. If the ācārya invites the installation of a buddha image without knowing the proper methods or with deficient ritual procedures, it is like pouring ghee into ashes: if one attempts to perform the fire-offering, there will certainly be no result. Therefore, in creating images one must have complete and perfect marks, and the ritual of image-installation and celebration must be fully carried out, so that all sentient beings may obtain great merit.

At that time, a certain bodhisattva addressed the Buddha, saying: "World-Honored One! The Buddha's body is without marks, like empty space pervading all places. Why do you now teach the installation of buddha images?"

The Buddha said: "Listen well now. For those who have long cultivated, I teach that the Dharma-body is without marks, unconditioned, pervading all places like empty space, originally tranquil, neither arising nor ceasing. How could one install an image? Now, for the sake of sentient beings who have newly aroused the aspiration, so that they may obtain merit, I teach these methods of image-installation, celebration, and consecration."

"When one wishes to establish a maṇḍala, first select an auspicious star and auspicious day. At a consecrated and superior site in a pure place, arrange parasols, banners, streamers, incense, flowers, lamps, and fruit. Then have the ācārya, according to the Dharma-procedures, single-mindedly perform the invitation. First, on the ground of the consecrated site, using five-colored powder, stretch lines and make a maṇḍala. Invite the tathāgatas of the five families and their bodhisattva retinue; one must follow this root ritual and not use a different procedure for the invitation.

"Furthermore, when the ācārya enters the buddha-hall to install the image, he must wear bracelets and rings on his wrists and fingers as adornments for his body. Both the ācārya and the disciples should single-mindedly visualize all the perfect marks of the Tathāgata. Then, joining palms and bowing in reverence, gazing upon the worthy sages, with pure incense, flowers, fragrant water, lamps, and fruit, according to the Dharma-procedures, they offer the arghya, incense, and lamps. After offering, one enters samādhi with mind free from doubt and thought, and recites this mantra three times:

"*[Dhāraṇī]* 'Samaya satvāṃ.'

"*[Dhāraṇī]* 'Samaya satvāṃ.'

"Having recited this mantra, one makes the following invitation: 'Just as all buddhas abide in Tuṣita Heaven, and just as the Buddha dwelt in the womb of Queen Māyā, may the Buddha in his compassion abide here likewise. May the Buddha abide here always. The disciple named so-and-so, having aroused the mind of bodhi, receives this incense and flower offering.' After inviting the buddha image, one further confers the precepts upon the patron and disciples. All the offering procedures and such must follow the ācārya's instructions without negligence, so that the patron obtains great merit.

"Having invited the Tathāgata to be seated in his place, with incense, flowers, lamps, fruit, and food, recite mantras and offer the arghya as offerings. The buddha image should be placed facing east and covered with a yellow cloth. The ācārya performs the visualization, imagining the Buddha as a mass of fire, and recites this mantra seven times while casting white mustard seeds:

"*[Dhāraṇī]* 'Oṃ sarva-pāpa-sphoṭana-hana vajrāya svāhā.'

"Having recited this mantra, one further visualizes the Tathāgata as a true body with all marks perfectly complete. Then, using the three syllables oṃ, aḥ, hūṃ, one installs them on three places of the image's body: the syllable oṃ is placed on the crown, the syllable aḥ is placed on the mouth, and the syllable hūṃ is placed on the heart. If one has attained the root mantra of one's personal deity, one places it only on the heart. Whether using the invitation mudrā, the vajra mudrā, or the hook mudrā, one performs them according to the method. If a person lacks the means, one should do what one can.

"For the vessels used, whether of gold, silver, precious jade, copper, stone, ceramic, wood, conch, or tree-leaves, all may serve as arghya offerings. Among the methods, using a copper vessel for the arghya is most supreme, like gold and stone. If the buddha image has grown dark, apply fragrant oil to it and use a grass brush to scrub and clean it. Then use songs of praise and musical instruments, and have maidens sing and dance. Next, recite the fragrant-oil mantra:

"*[Dhāraṇī]* 'Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-kāya-viśodhanī svāhā.'

"For the auspicious vases, use five or eight, and smear them with white sandalwood paste. Fill the vases with various kinds of fragrant water and empower them. Then have maidens twist thread, dyed in five new and fresh colors, and wind it around the vases. Further place the five treasures, five medicines, flowers, and fruit into the auspicious vases. Recite the Most Victorious Uṣṇīṣa mantra or the White Parasol mantra, and cast white mustard seeds as empowerment. One may also recite a pacification mantra.

"When bathing the buddha image, first apply fragrant oil, then use powder made from leaves of the nyagrodha tree and the udumbara tree, together with five grains and five kinds of pure water in a copper vessel, and bathe the buddha image. Then have the disciples and all those present recite verse-praises together with various music, as an auspicious sound to please the honored image, like the consecration ritual. Then recite the Buddha-Eye Bodhisattva mantra, which can remove all defilements:

"*[Dhāraṇī]* 'Oṃ sarva-rajo-paharaṇa svāhā.'

"Having recited this mantra, the ācārya says: 'Just as at the time of the Buddha's descent and birth, they bathed the Buddha's body, and all tathāgatas are likewise, I now with the most pure and supreme water bathe this buddha image.' Next, recite the consecration mantra:

"*[Dhāraṇī]* 'Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-abhiṣeka-samaya-śrīye hūṃ.'

"For the merit of a painted image, use a mirror to reflect the painted merit. With the aforementioned five kinds of pure water, write upon the mirror and bathe the reflection in the mirror. After bathing the gold, silver, and other images and the painted banner-images, recite the clothing mantra:

"*[Dhāraṇī]* 'Oṃ vajra-vāsase svāhā.'

"Next, recite the ear-installation mantra:

"*[Dhāraṇī]* 'Oṃ karaṇa-mārudhāraṇa hūṃ.'

"Next, recite the hair-knot installation mantra:

"*[Dhāraṇī]* 'Oṃ sarva-keśa-avatāraṇa hūṃ svāhā.'

"Next, recite the fingernail installation mantra:

"*[Dhāraṇī]* 'Oṃ sarva-śūnya-nakha-chedana-rāja svāhā.'

"Next, recite the beard and whisker installation mantra:

"*[Dhāraṇī]* 'Oṃ sarva-smaśru-dhāraṇa hūṃ hūṃ hrīṃ.'

"Next, recite the anointing-paste offering mantra:

"*[Dhāraṇī]* 'Oṃ vajra-gandhe svāhā.'

"Next, recite the adornment installation mantra:

"*[Dhāraṇī]* 'Oṃ vajra-bhāraṇa-vibhūṣaṇi svāhā.'

"Having recited this mantra, according to the consecration ritual one enters the maṇḍala and recites for the buddha image the personal deity's samādhi mantra:

"*[Dhāraṇī]* 'Oṃ hūṃ trāṃ hrīḥ aḥ.'

"Having recited this mantra, thus crown the image. Place the buddha image facing east, and the patron will obtain great merit.

"Next, make offerings with incense, flowers, and the like. First recite the flower-offering mantra:

"*[Dhāraṇī]* 'Oṃ vajra-puṣpe hūṃ.'

"Having recited this mantra, say: 'This flower is most supreme, whether water-born or land-born. I now with sincere mind offer it. May the Buddha accept it.'

"Next, recite the incense-offering mantra:

"*[Dhāraṇī]* 'Oṃ vajra-dhūpe.'

"Having recited this mantra, say: 'This incense is most supreme, whether natural incense or blended incense. I now with sincere mind offer it. May the Buddha accept it.'

"Next, recite the anointing-paste mantra:

"*[Dhāraṇī]* 'Oṃ vajra-gandhe svāhā.'

"Having recited this mantra, say: 'This anointing paste is most supreme and excellent. May the Buddha accept it.'

"Next, recite the lamp mantra:

"*[Dhāraṇī]* 'Oṃ vajra-dīpe svāhā.'

"Having recited this mantra, say: 'This is a lamp offering, whether a ghee lamp or an oil lamp, whose light dispels darkness. I now with sincere mind offer it. May the Buddha accept it and dispel all ignorant darkness.'

"Next, recite the food mantra:

"*[Dhāraṇī]* 'Oṃ bimba-upasthāna-pratigṛhṇāna svāhā.'

"Having recited this mantra, say: 'This is a food offering of the most supreme food and drink, complete in color, fragrance, and fine taste. I now with sincere mind offer it. May the Buddha accept it.'

"When all these offering procedures have been fully carried out, one further performs the opening of the eyes of the buddha image, shining their light, like the act of dotting the eyes. Recite the two eye-opening light mantras:

"*[Dhāraṇī]* 'Oṃ cakṣu cakṣu samanta-cakṣu-viśodhanī svāhā.'

"*[Dhāraṇī]* 'Oṃ netra-sphaṭa-rūpa-haraṇa hrīṃ.'

"Next, recite the Buddha-entering-samādhi mantra:

"*[Dhāraṇī]* 'Oṃ dhyāna-samādhi-na-pūrṇanī svāhā.'

"Having recited this mantra, further have the patron and disciples enter the maṇḍala. The ācārya says: 'You have now invited me to install and celebrate the buddha image. The ritual I have performed for you is now entirely complete. You should now receive consecration.' Recite this mantra:

"*[Dhāraṇī]* 'Samaya satvāṃ.'

"Next, perform the fire-offering (homa), either one thousand, five hundred, or two hundred and fifty repetitions. The homa furnace should be made square. Use wood with white sap, wet, and place it in the furnace. Further use ghee, curds, honey, and the five grains, blended together, to perform the homa, which can bring pacification and increase to oneself and all disciples. Recite the homa mantra:

"*[Dhāraṇī]* 'Oṃ vajra-jyose svāhā.'

"This is the homa mantra.

"Next, recite the life-increasing mantra:

"*[Dhāraṇī]* 'Agaccha-hari-piṅgala-dipta-svāhā-vahā-lohita-kṣadi-hīna-nāpaya-indra-jā-mikāriyam-kuru svāhā.'

"This is the life-increasing mantra.

"Next, recite the pacification-and-increase mantra:

"*[Dhāraṇī]* 'Oṃ vajra-puṣṭaye svāhā.'

"This is the pacification-and-increase mantra.

"Next, for the fire-deity, establish a seat within the homa furnace and perform the visualization. Visualize the homa furnace divided into three parts, leaving one part empty as the fire-deity's seat.

"*[Dhāraṇī]* 'Oṃ supratiṣṭhita-vajra svāhā.'

"This is the mantra for installing the fire-deity.

"Next, with incense, flowers, and the like, make offerings to the world-protectors and Īśāna Deva. Recite the world-protector mantra:

"*[Dhāraṇī]* 'Indrāya svāhā.'

"Having recited this mantra, perform music, song, and dance for the sending-off, and say: 'In the above, we have broadly performed buddha-work. The offerings may have been in many ways not in accord with the proper Dharma. To Brahmā, the devaputras, and all the host of devas who have come: for the patron, according to this ritual, in all places perform great pacification and increase, and proceed to the buddha-fields. Should there be another invitation, may you graciously descend once more. May the spirits of earth, water, fire, and wind return to their own realms. Arouse good minds and cherish the installed buddha images. Throughout hundreds of thousands of kalpas, protect the patron and their children and kin, as well as the ācārya and all disciples, and may all obtain auspiciousness.'

"Regarding the auspicious times for performing the altar rites: the first, second, third, fourth, fifth, eleventh, and twelfth months. The days to be used are: the second, third, seventh, tenth, thirteenth, and fifteenth days of the bright fortnight; the dark fortnight may also be used. One should further select an auspicious asterism and day for performing the rites. If the Wing (Hasta), Hyades (Rohiṇī), Dipper (Mūla), Beak (Mṛgaśīrṣa), Tail (Jyeṣṭhā), Heart (Anurādhā), Ghost (Puṣya), Winnowing-Basket (Uttarāṣāḍhā), or Chamber (Pūrvabhādrapadā) asterisms are in office, these are the most auspicious stars, and all endeavors will be accomplished.

"If the Well (Punarvasu) asterism: the rites will succeed, all requests will be fulfilled, and one will further gain kin from afar. If the Tail, Dipper, or Beak asterisms: one will gain people's love and respect and obtain wealth. If Orion (Ārdrā): one obtains pacification. If the Winnowing-Basket: one obtains long life for descendants. If the Chamber: one constantly accomplishes fine matters. If the Ghost: one obtains the most supreme of all. If the Heart or Room (Svāti) asterisms: one obtains happiness and satisfaction.

"If Jupiter, Mercury, or Venus are in office, these are the foremost in auspiciousness. If these three planets are conjoined on the same day, performing the image-installation celebration ritual obtains the most supreme merit.

"If the third, seventh, sixth, tenth, or eleventh houses: these five, if conjoined with the Moon, are greatly auspicious. If the second, sixth, tenth, or eleventh houses are conjoined with Mercury, they are greatly auspicious. If the second, seventh, ninth, or eleventh houses are conjoined with Jupiter, performing invitations and consecrations is most excellent and greatly auspicious. If the sixth, seventh, or tenth houses are conjoined with Venus, they are greatly auspicious. If the first, third, or fifth houses, they can destroy wealth. The ācārya should thus well understand the power of the stars and constellations.

"On the day of performing the rites, if the stars and planets are harmonious and favorable, the endeavor will be accomplished in a single instant. If the image-installation celebration obtains the most supreme merit, one will attain the position of a wheel-turning king or a great national king, without any doubt. Furthermore, if the stars and planets are mutually favorable, without any obstruction or difficulty, the installed buddha image and stūpa will, from life to life, bring accomplishment and benefit to people.

"The artisans who created the image and the various workers should all be given wealth and kind words of comfort, and should not be left without support, for this accords with the ritual. Whatever remains of the food offered to the Tathāgata, if people eat it, all will have their offenses eliminated.

"The ācārya then exhorts the patron to form connections and increase merit through giving. Whatever gold, silver, wealth, elephants, horses, and carriages there may be, one should give generously according to one's ability, offering with sincere mind, causing the ācārya to rejoice, and the worthy sages will also rejoice. Further, to bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, and upāsikās, with wealth and food, one should make offerings according to one's ability, so that merit may be fully realized.

"During the first watch of the night, perform great musical entertainment with song, dance, banners, flowers, conch-shells, and cymbals, with the patron's kin surrounding the proceedings in reverence. All the offering materials within the maṇḍala should be given entirely to the ācārya and must not be diverted elsewhere, nor should they be given to other bhikṣus, as this would not accord with the ritual.

"When the altar rites are completed, collect the altar materials. The five-colored powder should be sent into a river. Use kuṣṭha fragrant water to wash and wipe the altar ground, and the consecration merit will be accomplished according to the Dharma."
