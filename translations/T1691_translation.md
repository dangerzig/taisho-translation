# 迦葉仙人說醫女人經
## Sūtra on Kāśyapa the Seer's Medical Care for Women

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1691

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmabhadra (法賢).

---

At that time, the Seer Jīvaka suddenly had this thought: "All sentient beings in the world are born from women. From the time a woman first conceives until the full ten months, or sometimes as long as twelve months when the pregnancy is prolonged, the child is finally born. During that time, there may be illness, and at the time of illness the suffering is extreme. I shall now seek a skillful method, asking my teacher, to receive prescriptions and provide a cure." Having thought this, he went to his teacher, the Seer Kāśyapa, performed the rites proper to a student, and asked: "Great Teacher Kāśyapa, you who are greatly wise: I now wish to ask a question. May you grant me leave to ask." The Seer Kāśyapa said: "Ask whatever you wish." The Seer Jīvaka then said: "When a woman conceives, the expected term is ten months, or twelve months, and only when the days are complete is the child born. How is it that during this time various illnesses occur, causing the womb to shift and become unsettled, or causing damage with immeasurable suffering? My teacher, in your great wisdom, please explain the prescriptions for curing such illness and suffering." Having asked this, he listened and waited.

At that time, the Seer Kāśyapa addressed the Seer Jīvaka: "When a woman is pregnant and does not know how to take care of herself, the womb becomes unsettled. I shall now briefly explain for you the month-by-month protective medicines. If a pregnant woman's womb is unsettled during the first month, one should use sandalwood, lotus, and utpala flower, grind them together with water, then add milk and milk-sugar and decoct them. Taking this medicine warm can prevent damage and suffering for the newly pregnant woman and bring her comfort."

He further addressed the Seer Jīvaka: "If a pregnant woman's womb is unsettled during the second month, one should use blue utpala flower, kumuda flower root, water caltrop kernel, and kajjala-like medicines. Take equal parts of these medicines, pound and sift them into powder, decoct with milk, and take when cool. This medicine can prevent damage to the womb, stop pain, and bring comfort day and night.

"If a pregnant woman's womb is unsettled during the third month, one should use kākoli medicine, kṣīra-kākoli medicine, and castor root in equal parts. Grind them together with water until extremely fine, then add milk and decoct until done. Afterward add milk-sugar and honey, mix, and take cool. This medicine can settle the womb and stop pain. Those with illness will find comfort from taking it.

"If a pregnant woman's womb is unsettled during the fourth month, one should use tribulus root with its branches and leaves, and utpala flower with its stems, in equal parts. Grind together with water until extremely fine, then decoct with milk until done, and take when cool. This medicine can settle the womb and stop pain. Those with illness will find comfort from taking it.

"If a pregnant woman's womb is unsettled during the fifth month, one should use gourd root and utpala flower in equal parts, pound and sift until fine, then add grape juice, milk, and milk-sugar, decoct together, and take when cool. This medicine can settle the womb and stop pain. Those with illness will find comfort from taking it.

"If a pregnant woman's womb is unsettled during the sixth month, one should use bidāla medicine, jimadhikāla-rasa medicine, and sakṛta-va medicine in equal parts. Grind with water until extremely fine, then add milk and decoct. Afterward add milk-sugar and honey, and take when cool. This medicine can settle the womb and stop pain. Those with illness will find comfort from taking it.

"If a pregnant woman's womb is unsettled during the seventh month, one should use tribulus branches, leaves, and root, pound and sift into powder, form into pills with milk-sugar and honey, and take with meat broth. Also take rice with meat broth as a meal, or eat mung bean porridge and rice. This medicine and food can settle the womb. Those with illness will find comfort from taking and eating them.

"If a pregnant woman's womb is unsettled during the eighth month, one should use trikaṭu medicine, lotus, blue utpala flower, and tribulus in equal parts. Grind with cold water until extremely fine, then add milk, sugar, and honey and decoct together. Take when cool. This medicine can settle the womb and stop pain. Those with illness will find comfort from taking it.

"If a pregnant woman's womb is unsettled during the ninth month, one should use castor root, kākoli medicine, śarapārṇī medicine, and bṛhatī medicine in equal parts. Grind with cold water until extremely fine, add milk and decoct, and take when cool. This medicine can settle the womb and stop pain. Those with illness will find comfort from taking it.

"If a pregnant woman's womb is unsettled during the tenth month, one should use mung beans and utpala flower in equal parts. Grind with water until extremely fine, then add milk-sugar, honey, and milk, decoct together, and take when cool. This medicine can settle the womb and stop pain. Those with illness will find comfort from taking it.

"If a pregnant woman's pregnancy is prolonged to the eleventh month and the womb is unsettled, one should use blue utpala flower, sālūka medicine, and lotus with its stems in equal parts. Grind with cold water until extremely fine, then add milk and milk-sugar, decoct together, and take when cool. This medicine can settle the womb and stop pain. Those with illness will find comfort from taking it.

"If a pregnant woman's pregnancy is prolonged to the twelfth month and the womb is unsettled, one should use kākoli medicine, kṣīra-kākoli medicine, licorice, and utpala flower in equal parts. Pound and sift until fine, grind together with water, then add milk, mix, and decoct until done. Take when cool. This medicine can settle the womb and stop pain. Those with illness will find comfort from taking it."

At that time, the Seer Jīvaka, having heard his teacher explain these methods for the care and nourishment of pregnant women, joyfully accepted them with faith, paid his respects, and withdrew.
