# 菩薩十住行道品
## Chapter on the Practice of the Path through the Bodhisattva's Ten Abodes

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0283

Translated from the Chinese. Western Jin dynasty, translated by Dharmarakṣa (竺法護)

---

Dharmaprabhāsa Bodhisattva, bearing the awesome spiritual power of the Buddha, entered the samādhi called Anāvaraṇa-avabhāsa before the Buddha, and beheld the Buddhas of the ten directions. The Buddhas of the ten directions were as numerous as the dust-motes of a thousand Buddha-lands; each dust-mote represented one Buddha-land. Thus in the four directions and ten directions, he beheld the Buddhas of the ten directions, and they all said: "Excellent, excellent! Dharmaprabhāsa Bodhisattva!" The Buddhas of the ten directions all bestowed upon him wisdom and addressed Dharmaprabhāsa Bodhisattva: "It is through the power of Śākyamuni Buddha's original vows in past lives and the merit and awesome spiritual power he has accomplished that you are benefited. The benefit of all the sūtras is the Buddha's awesome spiritual power. The places where one enters deeply into the sūtras reveal the dharmas of empty space in all the ten directions, and the mind is without attachment. One enters entirely into unobstructed freedom, enters entirely into the great path, and swiftly approaches the attainment of Buddhahood. One knows all the sūtras, knows all the thoughts of beings in the ten directions, and studies and knows all the sūtras taught in the ten directions and how to apply them all. Therefore, for all the bodhisattvas, teach the ten Dharma-abodes of the bodhisattva. Entirely bearing the awesome spiritual power of the Buddha, one is able to teach."

At that time, what Dharmaprabhāsa Bodhisattva taught was without obstruction, without difficulty; without end, without any measure that could gauge it; without a point of culmination; without anyone who could overtake it, without anyone who could find its length or brevity; never a moment of forgetfulness, without anyone who could not gain clarity. All was equal without difference; never a moment of laxity, beyond what the multitude could attain. Bearing the power of this samādhi, the Buddhas of the ten directions each extended their right hands and placed them upon Dharmaprabhāsa Bodhisattva's head. With Dharmaprabhāsa Bodhisattva awakened within the samādhi, they addressed the bodhisattva: "All sons of the Buddha, listen! The abode of the bodhisattva is exceedingly vast, all like empty space. In the place of the sūtras, for what reason is the abode of the bodhisattva so vast? All the Buddhas of the past were born therein; all the Buddhas of the future will be born therein; all the Buddhas of the present are born therein. For what reason do the bodhisattvas enter the great path? From what causes and conditions do they enter this great path? Why is it thus?

"The bodhisattva has ten Dharma-abodes, as classified and taught by the Buddhas of the past, future, and present. What are the ten Dharma-abodes of the bodhisattvas?

"The first is the Pāramitā Bodhisattva Dharma-abode.

"The second is the Ajāta Bodhisattva Dharma-abode.

"The third is the Yuajā Bodhisattva Dharma-abode.

"The fourth is the Jāmakī Bodhisattva Dharma-abode.

"The fifth is the Pāyusaṃpad Bodhisattva Dharma-abode.

"The sixth is the Agrī-saṃpad Bodhisattva Dharma-abode.

"The seventh is the Avaivartika Bodhisattva Dharma-abode.

"The eighth is the Kumāra-bhūta (Youth) Bodhisattva Dharma-abode.

"The ninth is the Yurāja Bodhisattva Dharma-abode.

"The tenth is the Abhiṣeka Bodhisattva Dharma-abode.

"First: What is the Pāramitā Bodhisattva Dharma-abode? At the outset, seeing the Buddha, supremely handsome beyond compare, gazing upon his countenance and color without tiring, with none who can match him, noble and honored with none who can surpass him, able to fly with none who can surpass him, teaching with none who can surpass him. This bodhisattva, seeing the awesome spiritual power and deportment of the Buddha, gradually enters the Buddha's path, being progressively guided and opened, all taught and ferried according to their dispositions. Seeing those in suffering, the bodhisattva pities and grieves for them, gradually approaching and coming to understand the Buddha's words, generating faith and devotion. Having newly aroused the aspiration to study the Buddha's path, one wishes to fully comprehend the Buddha's wisdom and the ten difficult points, and wishes to attain knowledge of them all. What are the ten difficult points? The ten powers.

"The Pāramitā Bodhisattva teaching has ten aspects. What are the ten? First, one should make offerings to the Buddhas and bodhisattvas. Second, one should teach and speak according to beings' inclinations. Third, wherever born, one is always of noble station. Fourth, in heaven and on earth, there is none who can equal one. Fifth, one will certainly attain all the wisdom the Buddha possesses. Sixth, wherever born, one will always see immeasurable Buddhas. Seventh, one will attain all the profound samādhi-sūtras. Eighth, the path of death and rebirth has come from a place without limit. Ninth, one's life is not long. Tenth, one will ferry all beings of the ten directions to liberation. Why? Because one has deeply entered the Buddha-Dharma.

"Second: What is the Ajāta Bodhisattva Dharma-abode? One has ten kinds of intention toward beings of the ten directions. What are the ten kinds of intention? First, mindfulness of the goodness of all worldly beings. Second, a pure and clean mind. Third, a mind of universal peace. Fourth, a gentle and soft mind. Fifth, universal love and equality. Sixth, a mind intent solely on giving to others. Seventh, a mind that protects all. Eighth, a mind that thinks of others as no different from oneself. Ninth, a mind that regards all beings of the ten directions as one's teachers. Tenth, a mind that regards all beings of the ten directions as Buddhas. The Ajāta Bodhisattva should study many sūtras. Having studied many sūtras, one should dwell in solitude. Having dwelt in solitude, one should attend upon a good teacher. Having attended a good teacher, one should remain at the teacher's side. Having remained at the teacher's side, one should be easy to instruct. Being easy to instruct, one should act according to the time. Acting according to the time, one should be courageous in what one undertakes. Being courageous, one should study and enter into wisdom. Having entered into wisdom, one should uphold all the Dharma one has received. Having upheld the Dharma, one should not forget. Not forgetting, one should dwell in a place of peace. Why? Because this benefits one's compassion for all beings in the ten directions.

"Third: What is the Yuajā Bodhisattva Dharma-abode? Entering into all dharmas through ten aspects. What are the ten? First, all that exists is impermanent. Second, all that exists is suffering. Third, all that exists is empty. Fourth, all that exists is not mine. Fifth, all that exists has no abiding place. Sixth, all that exists has no benefit. Seventh, all that exists has no resting point. Eighth, all that exists has no location. Ninth, all that exists is without attachment. Tenth, all that exists is non-existent. All dharmas enter therein and do not return.

"The Yuajā Bodhisattva's teaching is that one should be mindful of all beings in the ten directions. Having been mindful of all beings, one should be mindful of the place of the Dharma. Having been mindful of the place of the Dharma, one should be mindful of all the Buddha-lands. Having been mindful of all the Buddha-lands, one should be mindful of the dharma of earth. Having been mindful of the dharma of earth, one should be mindful of the dharma of water. Having been mindful of the dharma of water, one should be mindful of the dharma of fire. Having been mindful of the dharma of fire, one should be mindful of the dharma of wind. Having been mindful of the dharma of wind, one should be mindful of the dharma of the desire-realm. Having been mindful of the dharma of the desire-realm, one should be mindful of the dharma of the form-realm. Having been mindful of the dharma of the form-realm, one should be mindful of the place where there is neither desire nor form. Having been mindful of the place of neither desire nor form, the mind is without craving. Why? Because through this one attains illumination of the Dharma.

"Fourth: What is the Jāmakī Bodhisattva Dharma-abode? One is constantly mindful of being born in the Buddha's presence, through ten aspects. What are the ten? First, there is no returning. Second, one has deep affection for the Buddha. Third, one deeply contemplates the Dharma. Fourth, one regards all beings of the ten directions with a compassionate mind. Fifth, one contemplates that the ten directions are ultimately non-existent. Sixth, all the Buddha-lands of the ten directions are empty. Seventh, past actions are ultimately non-existent. Eighth, all that exists is empty, like the work of an illusionist. Ninth, all suffering is non-existent. Tenth, nirvāṇa is empty and non-existent. Because of this one is born in the Buddha-Dharma. This is the Jāmakī Bodhisattva's teaching.

"The Buddhas of the past are empty and non-existent; the Buddhas of the future are empty and non-existent; the Buddhas of the present are empty and non-existent. The Dharma of the Buddhas of the past is non-existent; the Dharma of the Buddhas of the future is non-existent; the Dharma of the Buddhas of the present is non-existent. If one searches for the origin from which the Dharma-mindfulness of the Buddhas of the past arose, there is ultimately nothing to find. If one searches for the origin from which the Dharma-mindfulness of the Buddhas of the future will arise, there is ultimately nothing to find. If one searches for the origin from which the Dharma-mindfulness of the Buddhas of the present arises, there is ultimately nothing to find. The Dharma of all the Buddhas is entirely non-existent. Why? The Dharma of the three times is ultimately non-existent, all one.

"Fifth: What is the Pāyusaṃpad Bodhisattva Dharma-abode? All merit accomplished is for the deliverance of beings in the ten directions, through ten aspects. What are the ten? First, protecting all beings in the ten directions. Second, causing all beings in the ten directions to become good. Third, being mindful of the safety of all beings in the ten directions. Fourth, loving all beings in the ten directions. Fifth, having compassion for all beings in the ten directions. Sixth, teaching all beings in the ten directions not to commit evil. Seventh, guiding all beings in the ten directions onto the bodhisattva's path. Eighth, purifying all beings in the ten directions. Ninth, ferrying all beings in the ten directions across. Tenth, liberating all beings in the ten directions and causing them to enter nirvāṇa.

"Beings in the ten directions are beyond reckoning, beyond counting, beyond conceiving, beyond measuring, beyond calculating; they are entirely beyond speech. What are beings of the ten directions? All beings of the ten directions are empty beings, not-my-self beings, non-existent beings; all that exists, all is without anything extraordinary. Why? Because the mind is without attachment.

"Sixth: What is the Agrī-saṃpad Bodhisattva Dharma-abode? One has ten dharmas of deep affection and compassion. What are the ten? First, when people speak well or ill of the Buddha, the mind is without difference. Second, when the Dharma is spoken of well or ill, the mind is without difference. Third, when bodhisattvas are spoken of well or ill, the mind is without difference. Fourth, when those who seek the bodhisattva's path and other people speak well or ill of each other, the mind is without difference. Fifth, when some say beings in the ten directions are many or few, the mind is without difference. Sixth, when all beings speak well or ill of each other, the mind is without difference. Seventh, when some say beings in the ten directions are easy or hard to liberate, the mind is without difference. Eighth, when the Dharma is spoken of as much or little, the mind is without difference. Ninth, when the Dharma is spoken of as destroyed or not destroyed, the mind is without difference. Tenth, whether there is a place of Dharma or no place of Dharma, the mind is without difference. This bodhisattva should study these dharmas that have no fixed place.

"There are further ten aspects. What are the ten? First, all dharmas have no fixed place. Second, all dharmas cannot be seen in any place. Third, studying all dharmas is like a magical creation. Fourth, all dharmas are empty. Fifth, all dharmas are of manifold emptiness. Sixth, all dharmas are unobstructed, utterly empty. Seventh, all dharmas are like an illusionist's transformations. Eighth, all dharmas are like what is seen in a dream. Ninth, all dharmas are beyond reckoning. Tenth, all dharmas are non-existent and cannot be perceived. Why? Because one deeply enters the Buddha-Dharma and none can surpass one.

"Seventh: What is the Avaivartika Bodhisattva Dharma-abode? The bodhisattva hears ten aspects and stands firm. What are the ten? First, whether there is a Buddha or no Buddha, one does not waver. Second, whether there is Dharma or no Dharma, one does not waver. Third, whether there are bodhisattvas or no bodhisattvas, one does not waver. Fourth, whether there are those who seek the bodhisattva's path or none who seek it, one does not waver. Fifth, whether by upholding this Dharma one attains or does not attain, one does not waver. Sixth, whether there are Buddhas of the past or no Buddhas of the past, one does not waver. Seventh, whether there are Buddhas of the future or no Buddhas of the future, one does not waver. Eighth, whether there are Buddhas of the present or no Buddhas of the present, one does not waver. Ninth, whether the Buddha's wisdom is exhaustible or inexhaustible, one does not waver. Tenth, whether the affairs of past, future, and present are of many kinds or one kind, the mind ultimately does not waver.

"This bodhisattva should be taught to study ten aspects. What are the ten? First, from one wisdom enter into manifold wisdom. Second, take manifold wisdom and enter into one wisdom. Third, from one wisdom enter into manifold affairs. Fourth, from manifold affairs enter into one wisdom. Fifth, hold that the place of beings in the ten directions is empty space. Sixth, hold that empty space enters into all beings in the ten directions. Seventh, hold thought and enter into unmoving stillness. Eighth, hold unmoving stillness and enter into thought. Ninth, hold empty space and enter into thought. Tenth, hold thought and enter into empty space. Why? Because all the dharmas of merit enter therein; therefore one no longer wavers.

"Eighth: What is the Kumāra-bhūta (Youth) Bodhisattva Dharma-abode? The bodhisattva abides in ten aspects. What are the ten? First, all actions of body, speech, and mind are entirely pure. Second, no one can find any fault. Third, with a single turn of thought, one can be born wherever one wishes. Fourth, one knows who among beings of the ten directions has a compassionate mind. Fifth, one knows everything that all beings of the ten directions trust and rely upon. Sixth, one knows all the manifold kinds of beings in the ten directions. Seventh, one knows all the undertakings of beings in the ten directions. Eighth, one knows the formation and destruction of all lands. Ninth, by supernatural power and thought, one can fly wherever one wishes. Tenth, one studies the purity of all dharmas.

"This bodhisattva should further study ten aspects. What are the ten? First, one should study and know all Buddha-lands. Second, one should study the ability to shake all Buddha-lands. Third, one should study the awesome spiritual power of sovereign freedom in action. Fourth, one should study the contemplation of all Buddha-lands. Fifth, one should study going from one Buddha-land to another. Sixth, one should study visiting immeasurable Buddha-lands. Seventh, one should study knowing the immeasurable dharmas and answering all questions. Eighth, one should study transformation like an illusionist's creation, with sovereign freedom. Ninth, one should study the Buddha's voice and its resonance. Tenth, one should study, in a single turn of thought, making offerings to immeasurable Buddhas, reaching all of them instantly. Why? Because entering into one dharma, much is accomplished.

"Ninth: What is the Yurāja Bodhisattva Dharma-abode? The bodhisattva attains it through ten aspects. What are the ten? First, one knows all the births of beings in the ten directions. Second, one knows all the attachments and affections of beings in the ten directions. Third, one knows the thoughts of beings in the ten directions, their origins and where they come from. Fourth, one knows all the past good and evil deeds and destinations of beings in the ten directions. Fifth, one knows the manifold dharmas of all kinds. Sixth, one knows all the manifold thoughts and transformations of beings in the ten directions. Seventh, one knows the destruction, decline, good, and evil of all the Buddha-lands. Eighth, one knows the affairs of the past, future, and present through immeasurable ages. Ninth, one knows the equality and inequality of all beings in the ten directions. Tenth, one knows how to teach beings of the ten directions and to expound the dharma of emptiness.

"The bodhisattva has ten further areas of study. What are the ten? First, one should study the palace of the Buddha-Dharma. Second, one should study the construction of the palace of the Buddha-Dharma. Third, one should study all that is within the Buddha's palace. Fourth, one should study the instructions issued within the Buddha's palace. Fifth, one should study the Buddha's coming and going from the palace. Sixth, one should study the Dharma-palace. Seventh, one should study the palace of the Dharma-King. Eighth, one should study the instructions of the Dharma. Ninth, one should study the peaceful conduct of the Dharma-King. Tenth, one should study anew the construction and the instructions of the King within the Dharma, putting them into practice. Why? Because the mind gradually enters the Buddha's great path, and whatever Dharma one hears, one applies in one's own teaching.

"Tenth: What is the Abhiṣeka Bodhisattva Dharma-abode? The bodhisattva enters into ten kinds of wisdom and fully discriminates and knows them all. What are the ten? First, how should one shake immeasurable Buddha-lands? Second, one should illuminate the affairs within immeasurable Buddha-lands. Third, daily one should establish bodhisattvas within immeasurable Buddha-lands. Fourth, daily one should ferry beings across in immeasurable Buddha-lands. Fifth, one should bring peace to beings in immeasurable Buddha-lands. Sixth, immeasurable beings in the ten directions should all hear my voice. Seventh, one should know all the people of the ten directions. Eighth, all the good and evil thoughts of beings in the ten directions: I should know them all. Ninth, all beings in the ten directions: I should guide them all into the Buddha's path. Tenth, all beings in the ten directions: I should liberate them all.

"The Yurāja Bodhisattva cannot know or attain the Abhiṣeka Bodhisattva's actions of body, speech, and mind, or undertakings; one cannot know or attain the affairs of the Abhiṣeka Bodhisattva. One cannot know or attain the supernatural powers or flights; one cannot know or attain the past, future, and present affairs of the Abhiṣeka Bodhisattva; one cannot know or attain the Buddha-lands one contemplates; one cannot know or attain the mind's thoughts, practices, and wisdom through the ten aspects. The Yurāja Bodhisattva cannot know or attain the affairs practiced by the Abhiṣeka Bodhisattva. The Abhiṣeka Bodhisattva has entered into the Buddha's ten wisdoms.

"What are the ten wisdoms? First, studying from the Buddha through past, future, and present, through beginningless and frequent encounters. Second, studying from the Buddha with all the Buddha's dharmas fully complete. Third, studying from the Buddha the unobstructed place of all dharmas. Fourth, studying from the Buddha the boundless and fathomless place of all dharmas. Fifth, studying from the Buddha the merit and awesome spiritual power of guarding and upholding all lands. Sixth, studying from the Buddha the clear knowledge of the boundless and limitless places of incalculable lands. Seventh, studying from the Buddha the bringing of peace to the immeasurable Buddha-lands of the ten directions. Eighth, studying from the Buddha all the practices of beings in the ten directions. Ninth, studying from the Buddha all the wisdom that enters into all dharmas. Tenth, studying from the Buddha all the wisdom that the Buddha possesses. Why? Because one fully knows and thoroughly comprehends the Buddha's wisdom. There is nothing more to learn from anyone. This is the practice of the path by the Abhiṣeka Bodhisattva through the ten Dharma-abodes."
