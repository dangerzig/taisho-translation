# 佛說大方廣未曾有經善巧方便品
## Chapter on Skillful Means, from the Great Vaipulya Sūtra of the Unprecedented, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0844

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dānapāla (施護)

---

At that time there was a bodhisattva mahāsattva named Great Intention, who rose from his seat in the assembly, properly arranged his robes, joined his palms in reverence, bowed at the Buddha's feet, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! For those who cultivate the bodhisattva's practice, what skillful means should they employ regarding the five objects of desire, so that they take them up without attachment and practice constantly without obstruction?"

At that time the Buddha told the Bodhisattva Great Intention: "Excellent, excellent! Great Intention! Your name is 'supreme seeing of supreme meaning,' and you are able to ask such a question in this assembly. Those who cultivate the bodhisattva's practice, in future ages, will deeply plant good roots at the place of all the Buddhas. This is called skillful means, and thereby one is established by all the Buddhas together."

Then the Bodhisattva Great Intention again addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! How do those who cultivate the bodhisattva's practice deeply plant good roots at the place of all the Buddhas in future ages? We pray the World-Honored One, for the benefit and joy of all sentient beings, will broadly explain this."

The Buddha said: "Good man! Listen carefully, and I shall explain it for you. If a person practices even a small act of giving and is able to generate something superior, broadly dedicated for all sentient beings, the merit obtained from the most excellent wholesome mind is transferred to all sentient beings. Such wholesome benefit is inexhaustible. It is like the rain from heaven falling into the great ocean: each drop is innumerable, flowing continuously without end. For one who practices giving, skillfully transferring merit to all sentient beings, the merit is just like this, nourishing wholesome dharmas and progressively increasing, likewise without end, until Buddhahood is attained and all wholesome qualities are perfected.

"Furthermore, Great Intention! If someone sees a place where a maṇḍala has been set up and offers a single stick of incense, at the time of making this offering one should give rise to this thought: 'May all sentient beings attain the supreme fragrance of precepts, be complete with all enjoyments, and obtain everything they wish.'

"If one offers a single flower, may all sentient beings, in the heavens and among humans, equally receive great offerings.

"If one offers a single lamp, may all sentient beings have their blind eyes opened, may darkness be dispelled, and may they attain great illumination like the light of the sun and moon.

"If one offers perfumed ointment for adornment, may all sentient beings attain the mark of a true golden complexion, adorned with celestial fragrance.

"If one offers garments, may all sentient beings be fully possessed of a sense of shame and conscience, free from all faults.

"If one offers a ball of food, may all sentient beings attain the celestial amṛta samādhi, and further attain the supreme taste of the amṛta of Dharma, fully possessed of happiness.

"If one offers a bell, may all sentient beings attain the supreme, pure, and wondrous sound of Brahmā, bringing delight to all who hear it.

"If one offers a parasol, may all sentient beings be freed from the hardship of wind and rain, attain great coolness, and be sheltered universally.

"If one offers a banner, may all sentient beings have bodies like the bright moon, pure, clean, white, and luminous, illuminating universally, beloved by all people in the world.

"If one offers a precious fly-whisk, may all sentient beings be free from all dust and defilement, with bodies pure, possessing great renown, and fully endowed with supreme auspiciousness.

"If one offers various adornments, may all sentient beings attain all the jewels of the Buddha's merits and the adornments of the holy.

"If one offers song and music, may all sentient beings attain supreme faculties of hearing and always hear the wondrous and excellent sounds of all the Buddhas.

"If one offers boats, vessels, or vehicles for riding, may all sentient beings attain the supreme vehicle.

"Furthermore, following whatever sentient beings delight in, one gives it all to them, so that they attain a youthful appearance, soft and wonderful, receive celestial happiness, and in all their giving find delight and ease.

"If one offers sandalwood incense, may all sentient beings attain the thirty-two marks of a great man, fully adorned even to the invisible crown of the head, which always emits a wondrous fragrance.

"If one offers a seat, may all sentient beings receive celestial wealth and happiness, and everywhere be seated upon the supreme, the most excellent great vajra seat.

"If one offers bedding, may all sentient beings attain supreme joy and delight.

"If one offers a monastic dwelling, may all sentient beings dwell in celestial palaces of wondrous jewels that delight the mind, with the four bases of supernatural power fully perfected.

"If one offers an empty plot of land, may all sentient beings peacefully abide on the supreme and wondrous ten grounds and swiftly arrive at the most supreme and perfect ground of Buddhahood.

"If one offers one's retinue and attendants, may all sentient beings be constantly attended and protected by the devas, humans, and asuras of the world, firm and indestructible, supreme and most excellent, without any comparison in all their acts of giving.

"If one offers medicine, may all sentient beings be free from all illness and suffering, and like the great medicinal tree, attain an indestructible vajra body, fully adorned, and always enjoy the supreme delight and happiness.

"If one offers a mirror, may all sentient beings shine like the light of the moon, universally illuminating all.

"If one offers a vase, may all sentient beings attain the great worthy vase, pouring forth the water of Dharma to moisten all.

"If one offers a garden, grove, or trees, may all sentient beings have bodies like the wish-fulfilling tree of a kalpa.

"If one offers butter and curds, may all sentient beings attain the supreme taste of joy.

"If one offers sweet and delicious things, may all sentient beings attain the supreme, delightful taste of serene ease.

"Furthermore, when those who cultivate the bodhisattva's practice see any man, woman, or servant, they should give rise to this thought: 'May all sentient beings be entirely freed from the bonds of attachment and love, from sorrow, affliction, and the like, and may each and every one attain self-mastery and happiness.'

"If one sees a scene of suffering, one should give rise to this thought: 'May all sentient beings be freed from all suffering, attaining liberation and self-mastery equal to the Buddhas.'

"If toward all sentient beings one gives rise to the thought of regarding them as one's parents, teachers, and preceptors, treating them with reverence and respect, then in one's current life and in future births among devas and humans, whether as a śramaṇa, brahmin, or in any other birth, one's faculties will be tamed and one will be free from all suffering.

"When enjoying the five pleasures of the senses, one should give rise to this thought: 'May all sentient beings obtain whatever they delight in and wish for, and may all be endowed with the supreme practice of the bodhisattva.'

"When bathing, one should give rise to this thought: 'May all sentient beings be free from all dust and defilement, pure and unstained, equal to all the Buddhas.'

"When entering a garden, grove, temple, dwelling, or any place, one should give rise to this thought: 'May all sentient beings enter the supreme gate of the Dharma of liberation.'

"When leaving any place, one should give rise to this thought: 'May all sentient beings escape the realm of cyclic existence and abide in the Dharma of peace and joy.'

"When eating or drinking, one should give rise to this thought: 'May all sentient beings be freed from the evil destinies and sever the thought of food and drink.'

"When among one's household, one should give rise to this thought: 'May all sentient beings be far from quarreling, mutually loving and respectful as wholesome friends, harmonious and equanimous.'

"When opening a door, one should give rise to this thought: 'May all sentient beings open the gate of liberation and enter therein.'

"When closing a door, may all sentient beings close the gate to the evil destinies and never again enter them.

"When walking upon a road, one should give rise to this thought: 'May all sentient beings practice according to principle and proceed upon the right path.'

"When riding in a boat or vehicle, one should give rise to this thought: 'May all sentient beings ride the supreme vehicle and ascend the path of perfect awakening.'

"Whenever one crosses over any place, one should give rise to this thought: 'May all sentient beings pass beyond the sufferings of the hells and never again enter them.'

"Whenever one is in ordinary conversation, one should give rise to this thought: 'May all sentient beings have right mindfulness present before them, with all dharmas perfectly fulfilled.'

"If one sees someone giving rise to anger, one should generate joy and think thus: 'May all sentient beings extinguish all harmful anger and be far from the disturbance of māras.'

"Whenever someone speaks words of wholesome Dharma, one should give rise to this thought: 'May all sentient beings attain the wisdom of all the Buddhas and unobstructed eloquence.'

"When engaged in play, laughter, song, music, or singing of praises, one should give rise to this thought: 'May all sentient beings be constantly filled with great joy, receiving all wealth and happiness, at ease and delighted.'

"When going to sleep, one should give rise to this thought: 'May all sentient beings give rise to the supreme mind, escape the mire of ignorance and dark defilement, and in all their endeavors be established by the Buddhas, abiding in perfect wisdom.'

"When walking in meditation, one should give rise to this thought: 'May all sentient beings constantly tread upon all the holy paths.'"

At that time the Bodhisattva Great Intention, hearing this Dharma, was filled with great joy, and again addressed the Buddha: "Excellent, World-Honored One! You have taught this Dharma well. It enables all who cultivate the bodhisattva's practice, in all their bodily, verbal, and mental actions, to act in accord with the good. They will always be embraced and received by all the Buddhas, and all sentient beings in the evil destinies will be rescued and delivered by the Buddha.

"World-Honored One! In the latter age to come, those who cultivate the bodhisattva's practice should practice the skillful means taught by the Buddha in this way. This is what it means to cultivate the bodhisattva's practice."

The Buddha said: "Great Intention! Just so, just so. When those who cultivate the bodhisattva's practice study in this way, their faith becomes firm and they do not retreat, their seeds are complete and manifest in their conduct. Great Intention, you should know: this is called the supreme mark of the bodhisattva, and it is the most excellent skillful means. All of you should diligently cultivate and practice this right Dharma, and explain it for others."

At that time the Bodhisattva Mahāsattva Great Intention, having heard the Buddha teach this Dharma, joyfully praised it, bowed at the World-Honored One's feet, withdrew, and stood to one side.
