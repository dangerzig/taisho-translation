# 長者施報經
## Sūtra on the Householder's Rewards of Giving

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0074

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmadeva (法天)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the country of Śrāvastī, in Jeta's Grove, in the park of Anāthapiṇḍada. At that time, there was a householder named Anāthapiṇḍada who came to the Buddha's presence, bowed his head to the Buddha's feet, and sat to one side.

The Buddha told the householder: "If a person offers fine food and drink as a proper gift, giving with their own hand, or giving regularly, but cannot obtain vast and great merit, what do you think the reason is? It is because that person's mind seeks wealth and pleasure. If, however, a person gives fine food and drink as a proper gift not for the sake of clothing, food, bedding, wealth, or pleasure, then they shall obtain great riches, as well as a wife, sons and daughters, servants, and devoted and obedient family members. What do you think the reason is? It is because that person gives for the sake of all sentient beings."

The Buddha said: "Householder, in the past there was a brahmin householder named Velāma who held a great festival of giving. He gave eighty thousand golden platters filled with gold dust as gifts. He further gave eighty thousand silver platters filled with silver dust as gifts. He further gave eighty thousand golden platters filled with silver dust as gifts. He further gave eighty thousand silver platters filled with gold dust as gifts. He further gave eighty thousand copper platters filled with every variety of exquisite food and drink as gifts. He further gave eighty thousand milch cows as gifts. He further gave eighty thousand maidens adorned with the finest garments, pearl necklaces, and every manner of ornament as gifts. He further gave eighty thousand beds of gold, beds of silver, beds of ivory, and beds of wood, furnished with every variety of fine cushions and coverlets, as gifts. He further gave eighty thousand palanquins, chariots, and carriages, spread with white silk and Kauśika cloth and adorned in every manner, as gifts."

The Buddha said: "Householder, though Velāma gave in this manner, if a person were to give food and drink to a single person of right view, the merit obtained would surpass all the foregoing. What is the reason? Because such a person does not fall into wrong views."

The Buddha said: "Householder, though Velāma gave in this manner, giving to a single person of right view does not compare to giving to a hundred persons of right view. The merit obtained surpasses the foregoing."

The Buddha said: "Householder, though Velāma gave in this manner, giving to a hundred persons of right view does not compare to giving to a single stream-enterer. The merit obtained surpasses the foregoing."

The Buddha said: "Householder, though Velāma gave in this manner, giving to a single stream-enterer does not compare to giving to a hundred stream-enterers. The merit obtained surpasses the foregoing."

The Buddha said: "Householder, though Velāma gave in this manner, giving to a hundred stream-enterers does not compare to giving to a single non-returner. The merit obtained surpasses the foregoing."

The Buddha said: "Householder, though Velāma gave in this manner, giving to a single non-returner does not compare to giving to a hundred non-returners. The merit obtained surpasses the foregoing."

The Buddha said: "Householder, though Velāma gave in this manner, giving to a hundred non-returners does not compare to giving to a single arhat. The merit obtained surpasses the foregoing."

The Buddha said: "Householder, though Velāma gave in this manner, giving to a single arhat does not compare to giving to a hundred arhats. The merit obtained surpasses the foregoing."

The Buddha said: "Householder, though Velāma gave in this manner, giving to a hundred arhats does not compare to giving to a single pratyekabuddha. The merit obtained surpasses the foregoing."

The Buddha said: "Householder, though Velāma gave in this manner, giving to a single pratyekabuddha does not compare to giving to a hundred pratyekabuddhas. The merit obtained surpasses the foregoing."

The Buddha said: "Householder, though Velāma gave in this manner, giving to a hundred pratyekabuddhas does not compare to giving to a single Tathāgata, Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened One. The merit obtained surpasses the foregoing."

The Buddha said: "Householder, though Velāma gave in this manner, giving to a Tathāgata, Worthy One, Perfectly Enlightened One does not compare to giving to the Buddha together with the great assembly of bhikṣus who follow him. The merit obtained surpasses the foregoing."

The Buddha said: "Householder, though Velāma gave in this manner, giving to the Buddha and the great assembly of bhikṣus who follow him does not compare to providing food for all bowl-carrying monks of the four directions. The merit obtained surpasses the foregoing."

The Buddha said: "Householder, though Velāma gave in this manner, providing food for all bowl-carrying monks of the four directions does not compare to offering parks and groves to the Saṅgha of the four directions. The merit obtained surpasses the foregoing."

The Buddha said: "Householder, though Velāma gave in this manner, offering parks and groves to the Saṅgha of the four directions does not compare to offering monasteries to the Saṅgha of the four directions. The merit obtained surpasses the foregoing."

The Buddha said: "Householder, though Velāma gave in this manner, offering monasteries to the Saṅgha of the four directions does not compare to wholeheartedly taking refuge in the Buddha, Dharma, and Saṅgha for the rest of one's life. The merit obtained surpasses the foregoing."

The Buddha said: "Householder, though Velāma gave in this manner, wholeheartedly taking refuge in the Buddha, Dharma, and Saṅgha for the rest of one's life does not compare to upholding for the rest of one's life the precepts of not killing, not stealing, not engaging in sexual misconduct, not speaking falsely, and not drinking intoxicants. The merit obtained surpasses the foregoing."

The Buddha said: "Householder, though Velāma gave in this manner, upholding these five precepts for the rest of one's life does not compare to a person who, throughout the ten directions and in all places, practices great loving-kindness to benefit all beings, free from all discrimination, with a mind without characteristics. The merit obtained surpasses the foregoing."

At that time, the World-Honored One, having spoken these words, told the householder: "The brahmin Velāma who in the past held the great festival of giving was no other śramaṇa or brahmin. It was I myself."

When the householder Anāthapiṇḍada heard the Buddha's teaching, he was free from the view of self, the view of person, the view of sentient beings, and the view of a lifespan. Departing from deluded thinking, his mind attained purity, and he awakened to the patience of cessation.
