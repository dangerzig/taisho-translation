# 餓鬼報應經
## Sūtra of Hungry Ghost Retribution

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0746

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown

---

The Venerable Mahāmaudgalyāyana was with the Buddha on Vulture Peak Mountain. Wandering along the banks of the Ganges River, he saw many hungry ghosts, each suffering different punishments. When they saw the Venerable Maudgalyāyana, all gave rise to a mind of respect and came to ask about their karmic causes.

One ghost asked: "I constantly suffer from headaches. What sin has caused this?"

Maudgalyāyana answered: "When you were a human, you could not cultivate patience. You struck sentient beings on the head with a staff. Now you are receiving the flower of this retribution; its fruit will be in the hells."

One ghost asked: "I constantly suffer from sores. What sin has caused this?"

He answered: "When you were a human, you lacked a compassionate mind. You set fire to mountains and wilds, harming sentient beings. Now you are receiving the flower of this retribution; its fruit will be in the hells."

One ghost asked: "My entire body is covered with rotting sores, unbearable to endure. What sin has caused this?"

He answered: "When you were a human, you delighted in singeing pigs and sheep. Now you are receiving the flower of this retribution; its fruit will be in the hells."

One ghost asked: "I cannot eat enough to be satisfied and have never been full. What sin has caused this?"

He answered: "When you were a human, though you fed sentient beings, you always left them unsatisfied. Now you are receiving the flower of this retribution; its fruit will be in the hells."

One ghost asked: "I constantly suffer from headaches that no treatment can cure. What sin has caused this?"

He answered: "When you were a human, you did not respect those of virtue, and you heaped abuse and insult upon them. Now you are receiving the flower of this retribution; its fruit will be in the hells."

One ghost asked: "I have borne sons and daughters, all beautiful and lovable, yet they all died young. The grief is unending. What sin has caused this?"

He answered: "When you were a human, you saw your children killing living creatures and approved with delight, eating their flesh. Because of killing, their lives are short; because of your delight, you suffer the poison of pain. Now you are receiving the flower of this retribution; its fruit will be in the hells."

One ghost asked: "I have a husband who keeps many other wives. When it is my turn to share his bed, he will not receive me. What sin has caused this?"

He answered: "When you were a human, you did not respect your husband and engaged in adulterous and improper conduct. Now you are receiving the flower of this retribution; its fruit will be in the hells."

One ghost asked: "I constantly suffer from headaches and my private parts are covered with rotting sores. What sin has caused this?"

He answered: "When you were a human, in the pure precincts of a Buddhist stūpa and monastery, you engaged in sexual misconduct. Now you are receiving the flower of this retribution; its fruit will be in the hells."

One ghost asked: "I have obtained this body, which is rough, coarse, and impure. What sin has caused this?"

He answered: "When you were a human, you did not honor those of virtue. You looked down upon good people and threw sand and dirt at śramaṇas. Now you are receiving the flower of this retribution; its fruit will be in the hells."

One ghost asked: "I cannot eat even a whole bushel yet am never satisfied. What sin has caused this?"

He answered: "When you were a human, you were originally a bhikṣu. When seeking goods for the Saṃgha, you kept them for yourself to eat. Now you are receiving the flower of this retribution; its fruit will be in the hells."

One ghost asked: "I have received this form with swollen feet and a goiter on my neck. What sin has caused this?"

He answered: "When you were a human, you forced people and beasts of burden to carry heavy loads without mercy. Now you are receiving the flower of this retribution; its fruit will be in the hells."

One ghost asked: "I have received this body and constantly suffer from feverish thirst. What sin has caused this?"

He answered: "When you were a human, you delighted in fishing and hunting. You threw the fish you caught upon the sand to die in agony. Now you are receiving the flower of this retribution; its fruit will be in the hells."

One ghost asked: "I have received this body and am mad, deluded, and without wisdom. What sin has caused this?"

He answered: "When you were a human, you gave wine to others. Now you are receiving the flower of this retribution; its fruit will be in the hells."

One ghost asked: "The children I bear all turn and devour me. What sin has caused this?"

He answered: "When you were a human, you did not cultivate filial care. Now you are receiving the flower of this retribution; its fruit will be in the hells."

One ghost asked: "I constantly vomit up whatever I eat. What sin has caused this?"

He answered: "When you were a human, there was someone who came in the afternoon seeking food. You scolded and abused them while giving it. Now you are receiving the flower of this retribution; its fruit will be in the hells."

One ghost asked: "In this life my wealth and resources are never lacking, yet I delight in wearing tattered clothes. What sin has caused this?"

He answered: "When you were a human, though you enjoyed practicing generosity, after giving you would immediately regret it. Now you are receiving the flower of this retribution; its fruit will be in the hells."

One ghost asked: "I have received this form and must constantly lean against alleyways and roadsides, with no fixed dwelling place. What sin has caused this?"

He answered: "When you were a human, when travelers came seeking lodging, you would not let them rest in peace. Seeing others hosting travelers, you would scold and abuse them. Now you are receiving the flower of this retribution; its fruit will be in the hells."

One ghost asked: "I have obtained this form, being neither male nor female. What sin has caused this?"

He answered: "When you were a human, you lacked a compassionate mind and enjoyed castrating livestock. Now you are receiving the flower of this retribution; its fruit will be in the hells."

One ghost asked: "I have obtained this body but am crippled and cannot walk. What sin has caused this?"

He answered: "When you were a human, you enjoyed acting without principle, binding people and beasts and preventing them from moving. Now you are receiving the flower of this retribution; its fruit will be in the hells."

One ghost asked: "I have this body and constantly suffer from feverish thirst. Walking along, I see the Ganges River, cool and beautiful. I enter it, hoping to bathe and obtain cool relief, to relieve the heat and suffering. But as I enter, my entire body rots and decays. When I try to drink, a single mouthful entering my throat scorches and destroys my five organs, and the flesh separates from the bones. What sin has caused this?"

He answered: "When you were a human, you enjoyed being a physiognomist, reading people's fortunes. You were rarely truthful and mostly false, sometimes slandering and sometimes praising, claiming to have virtue in order to move people's hearts and seek gain. Furthermore, toward your parents, brothers, and relatives you were deceitful and dishonest. Now you are receiving the flower of this retribution. Its fruit will be in the hells, where the suffering will be a hundred million times greater, beyond description."

One ghost asked: "Ever since I received this body, there has been a vicious dog with a great body, sharp teeth, and blazing red eyes that constantly comes day and night to devour my body. Before my life force has expired, the flesh grows back, and I suffer again. The pain is unbearable. What sin has caused this?"

He answered: "When you were a human, you were the priest of a temple to the heavenly spirits. In that temple you took the blood of cattle and sheep to sacrifice to the heavens, eating their flesh yourself. You told the people: 'If you sacrifice to the heavens, you will obtain great good fortune!' Through such evil and deviant words you deluded the common people. Now you are receiving the flower of this retribution; its fruit will be in the hells."

One ghost asked: "Ever since I received this body, I have constantly dwelt in impurity, my entire body smeared with filth. Whatever I eat is all impure. I constantly suffer this and cannot escape it; the stench and torment envelop my body, and the sorrow is beyond reckoning. What sin has caused this?"

He answered: "When you were a human, you were a brahmin who did not believe in the Buddha-Dharma and had no wish to make offerings to śramaṇas and practitioners. Whenever they came to beg, you never wished to see them. Once there was a practitioner who came to beg from you. You devised a scheme: 'I will make it so he never comes again.' You took his bowl, placed excrement at the bottom, and covered it with rice on top. When the practitioner received it, he returned to his dwelling and set it to one side. After he had washed his hands and sat down, he took the bowl and was about to eat, but the bowl was so foul with stench that he could not approach it. Because of this, you received this stench and torment. Now you are receiving the flower of this retribution. Its fruit will be in the hells, where you will constantly swallow hot iron pellets, your body rotting and decaying, with suffering beyond words."

One ghost asked: "Ever since I received this body, my tongue constantly protrudes, and there is a spontaneous axe that chops and severs it, countless times. What sin has caused this?"

He answered: "When you were a human, you were a young temple attendant. Assigned by the Saṃgha, you drew cold clean water and prepared a rock-sugar drink to distribute to the assembly. The rock sugar was large and hard. You broke off a small piece and stole a mouthful before the Saṃgha had eaten. Because of stealing from the Saṃgha, now you are receiving the flower of this retribution. Its fruit will be in the hells, where you will have molten copper poured down your throat, with suffering beyond words."

One ghost asked: "Ever since I received this body, I have constantly suffered from hunger and thirst. I go to the latrine hoping to eat the excrement there, but at the latrine there is a powerful ghost who beats me with a staff, and I can never get near. What sin has caused this?"

He answered: "When you were a human, you were a practitioner serving as the head of a Buddhist temple. You were stingy with the Saṃgha's goods and did not provide good food to the assembly of monks, always giving them coarse and poor fare. Sometimes when you were about to prepare good food and visiting bhikṣus arrived, you would stop and not prepare it, waiting until they left before serving. With such a mean-spirited and stingy mind, now you are receiving the flower of this retribution; its fruit will be in the hells."

One ghost asked: "I have received this body with a belly as large as a jug, while the rest of my body is small. My throat is as thin as the eye of a needle, so food cannot pass through. What sin has caused this?"

He answered: "When you were a human, you were a village headman. Relying on your power, you oppressed and cheated the common people, constantly using unjust means to demand food and drink, causing the people to suffer. Now you are receiving the flower of this retribution; its fruit will be in the hells."

One ghost asked: "I have received this body with a copper vessel on my shoulder, filled with molten copper. With one hand I hold a ladle, scoop from it, and pour it over my own head. My entire body is scorched and burned, countless times. The suffering and pain are immeasurable. What sin has caused this?"

He answered: "When you were a human, you went forth and practiced the path. As the one in charge of the Saṃgha's food and drink, there was a jar of ghee that you hid in another place. When visiting practitioners came, you did not give them any. Only after the visitors had left did you take out the ghee and distribute it to the resident monks. This ghee was the property of the universally shared Saṃgha, in which all have a share. Because you concealed the Saṃgha's property and distributed it unequally, you received this suffering. Now you are receiving the flower of this retribution; its fruit will be in the hells, with suffering beyond reckoning."

One ghost asked: "Ever since I received this body, there has constantly been a person holding various knives and saws who flays and cuts my body, opens my belly, and takes out my five organs. When the flesh is gone and sinews severed, the pain is unbearable. In an instant the flesh grows back, and the person returns to cut again. What sin has caused this?"

He answered: "When you were a human, you were a brahmin who did not believe in the right Dharma, constantly holding wrong views. You served the heavenly spirits, always using cattle and sheep for sacrificial offerings. Because of this sin, now you are receiving the flower of this retribution; its fruit will be in the hells."

One ghost asked: "Ever since I received this body, there has constantly been a person holding various knives and saws who flays and cuts my body, opens my belly, and takes out my five organs. When the flesh is gone and sinews severed, the excruciating pain is unbearable. In an instant the flesh grows back, and the person returns to cut again. What sin has caused this?"

He answered: "When you were a human, you were an executioner, assigned to carry out killings. You had no compassionate mind and performed the task with delight. Because of this sin, you received this evil. Now you are receiving the flower of this retribution; its fruit will be in the hells."

One ghost asked: "I have received this body, and beneath both armpits there are constantly hot iron wheels turning. The great heat scorches my body, and both armpits are burned through. What sin has caused this?"

He answered: "When you were a human, you went forth to study the path. When making various cakes for the Saṃgha, before the monks had sat down to eat, with a greedy mind you stole some of the Saṃgha's cakes and hid them under your armpits, eating them in a hidden place. Because of this, you received this suffering. Now you are receiving the flower of this retribution; its fruit will be in the hells, with suffering beyond measure."

One ghost asked: "I have received this body, and there are constantly spontaneous red-hot iron pellets that fall from the air and enter my mouth down to my belly. Sometimes they enter from the left and exit from the right, sometimes enter from the right and exit from the left. My entire body is scorched and burned. The excruciating pain is immeasurable. What sin has caused this?"

He answered: "When you were a human, you went forth and became a novice. When cooking āmra fruit for the Saṃgha and distributing it, when you reached your preceptor, you would always give him extra, seven pieces more. Because of this, you received this evil. Now you are receiving the flower of this retribution; its fruit will be in the hells."

One ghost asked: "I have received this body, and whenever I wish to walk forward, I spin and turn back around like a whirlwind, never able to advance. The anguish and distress are beyond reckoning. What sin has caused this?"

He answered: "When you were a human, you were a diviner. You deceived and deluded others, causing their minds to be confused, sometimes joyful and sometimes frightened, none of it true. Because of this, you received this evil. Now you are receiving the flower of this retribution; its fruit will be in the hells."

One ghost asked: "I have received this body, and there is constantly a heat within, as though embracing boiling water and fire, which in turn burns and cooks me. The suffering is manifold. What sin has caused this?"

He answered: "When you were a human, you were the chief consort of a king. At that time the king favored and respected a lesser consort. You constantly harbored jealousy and devised an evil scheme to kill her. You watched for when she was sleeping, boiled ghee until it was hot, and poured it on her belly. She suffered immeasurable pain and died as a result. Because of this, you received this suffering. Now you are receiving the flower of this retribution; its fruit will be in the hells."

One ghost asked: "I have received this body and am by nature constantly fearful. I always dread that someone will come to arrest me, confine and bind me, shackle and torture me. I have never had a moment of happiness. What sin has caused this?"

He answered: "When you were a human, you enjoyed committing adultery and violating others' wives. You constantly feared being discovered and your mind was never at ease. Now you are receiving the flower of this retribution. Its fruit will be in the hells, where you will lie upon iron beds or embrace copper pillars. The suffering is beyond reckoning."

One ghost asked: "I have received this body, and spontaneous hot iron forms a cage and net that wraps around my body, burning and scorching until I am charred. The poisonous pain is beyond words. What sin has caused this?"

He answered: "When you were a human, you constantly used traps and nets to kill fish and beasts, and cages to capture birds. Because of this, you received this excruciating suffering. Now you are receiving the flower of this retribution; its fruit will be in the hells."

One ghost asked: "I have received this body without hands or feet, like a lump of flesh, lying in the wilderness. Foxes, wolves, tigers, leopards, vultures, and various birds compete to seize and devour me. The suffering is beyond words. What sin has caused this?"

He answered: "When you were a human, you devised evil means, whether for yourself or for others, and when someone was pregnant you would give them drugs to cause the fetus to dissolve and be destroyed. Because of this, you received this suffering. Now you are receiving the flower of this retribution; its fruit will be in the hells."

Three more ghosts came at the same time and asked: "We have received these bodies, and there are constantly iron nails that fall from the air and nail our bodies. They pierce through flesh and shatter bone; the poisonous pain reaches the marrow. What sin has caused this?"

He answered: "When you were humans, one of you was a horse trainer, one was a cattle trainer, and one was an elephant trainer. Greedily seeking others' wealth, you jabbed them with needles without propriety, causing them to suffer pain, yet you did not relieve their suffering. Because of this, you received this suffering. Now you are receiving the flower of this retribution; its fruit will be in the hells."

One ghost asked: "I have received this body without a head. My eyes, ears, nose, and mouth are all on my chest. What sin has caused this?"

He answered: "When you were a human, whenever you saw someone being executed, you would hold their head, pulling it with delight and without compassion. Because of this, you received this body. Now you are receiving the flower of this retribution; its fruit will be in the hells."

When Maudgalyāyana had answered all the ghosts, they all gave rise to a mind of respect and came forward to repent. Maudgalyāyana, seeing their joy, further taught the Dharma for them. When the ghosts had heard it, they all greatly rejoiced.
