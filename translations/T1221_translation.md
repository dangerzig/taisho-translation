# 青色大金剛藥叉辟鬼魔法
## Method of the Blue Great Vajra Yakṣa for Repelling Ghost-Demons

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1221

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, compiled by Kōngqí (空蜝).

---

The root seal. Join both hands in añjali. Bend both ring fingers into the palms with their second joints back to back. Place the right middle finger against the tip of the left index finger, and the left middle finger against the tip of the right index finger, forming a slight wheel shape. Wherever this seal is applied, it crushes and destroys all demon-ghosts. The true word for destroying obstacles:

*Phaṭ-ṭha nāga-vināyaka phaṭ vāsara-ṇa phaṭ hūṃ mahā-nidānaya phaṭ kumbhāṇḍa-sādhānaya phaṭ valita-viśācānaya phaṭ yakṣa-viśācānaya phaṭ duṣṭa-yama-sphadhara-ṭhāya hūṃ hūṃ phaṭ-ṭha-ṭha apratihata-varati-triloka-bhayaṃ-kaṭhayira-ciñña-pāyati hūṃ phaṭ-ṭha-ṭha svāhā*

If one wishes to repel and remove the ghost-illness of transmitted corpse-disease, one should understand the symptoms. The symptoms are as follows: the person's body and mind are feverish and tormented, they gradually waste away, they sometimes eat or sometimes sleep excessively, and sometimes suffer greatly. The symptoms cannot all be described. Furthermore, this ghost-illness gradually spreads from person to person, spreading and circulating everywhere, transmitting to husband, wife, children, grandchildren, brothers, and sisters. For this reason people call it "transmitted corpse-ghost." The finest physicians in the world cannot treat it.

When the Buddhadharma has grown feeble, if kings, great ministers, empresses, consorts, palace ladies, or monks and nuns in the land are afflicted by this, the superior grade becomes leprosy, the middle grade becomes transmitted death, and the lower grade becomes madness. Therefore parents avoid their own kin, and husbands and wives sever their bonds of duty.

This transmitted corpse-disease ghost is also called the "Heavenly Māra Rāna-śithila Ghost." The Dhāraṇī Collection Sūtra states: the symptoms of this ghost-illness resemble wind-disease, and also resemble the symptoms of madness, one by one exactly so. If not treated within forty-nine days, death is certain.

When treating this illness: recite the preceding great-body mantra three times seven. With the right hand take white mustard seeds, recite the preceding body-mantra three times seven in reverse, and scatter them over the patient's head and face. The ghost's body will shatter and split apart, as if burned by fire. Or grasp the person, recite the preceding body-mantra three times seven in reverse, strike with a branch while reciting the preceding body-mantra three times seven, and they will be healed.

If moxibustion should be applied to the patient, there are nine points: first, the center of the cross-mark at the crown; second, the Wind Gate point; third, the mirror of the gallbladder; fourth, the heart organ; fifth, the cinnabar field; sixth, the left Wind Market; seventh, the right Wind Market; eighth, the left Pēngjiǎo point; ninth, the right Pēngjiǎo point.

When applying moxibustion at the great crown cross-mark, recite *Hūṃ Phaṭ* five times seven, endowed with the merit-accumulation marks of the Hundred-Light Universal Illumination King. Next, apply moxibustion at the Wind Gate while reciting *Hūṃ Phaṭ* five times seven. Next, apply moxibustion at the gallbladder mirror while reciting *Hūṃ Phaṭ* five times seven. Next, apply moxibustion at the heart organ while reciting *Hūṃ Phaṭ* five times seven. Next, apply moxibustion at the cinnabar field while reciting *Hūṃ Phaṭ* five times seven. Next, apply moxibustion at the left and right Wind Markets while reciting *Hūṃ Phaṭ*. Next, apply moxibustion at the left and right Pēngjiǎo points while reciting *Hūṃ Phaṭ* five times seven. All the above are empowered with the preceding root seal. The moxibustion points are the locations where ghosts, binding-ghosts, and obstacles reside.

When applying moxibustion, recite the body-mantra the appropriate number of times to demonstrate its efficacy. Add aloeswood incense, white sandalwood incense, and the like into a copper vessel. Recite the preceding body-mantra three times seven to empower the incense. Add it to a decoction of iris, willow, and pomegranate, and bathe and wash the patient's moxibustion sites. The transmitted corpse-disease will cease.

The method for receiving and maintaining the seal-dharma: the practitioner should wear fresh clean garments, dwell in a quiet room, discipline the emotions, aspire above to seek bodhi and below to transform sentient beings. Vow to recite the preceding body-mantra three times daily. After three times seven days, circumambulate the altar walking the path while reciting the mantra to empower the moxibustion decoction for the patient. The practitioner should always use the Mahāmayūrī Vidyārāja root seal and true word for protection, removing all ghost-spirit obstacles.

The seal for summoning the Blue Great Vajra Yakṣa: join both hands in añjali, with both ring fingers inserted into the palms, backs together, atop the nails of both middle fingers. Place both index fingers on the backs of the two ring fingers and open the right index finger. Beckon the honored deity five times; this also forms the five-yakṣa invitation seal. Recite the preceding body-mantra five times, and they will certainly arise and come to assemble. The true word:

*Ha saṃ hūṃ u-hu u-hu*

Construct internally. In the center, place two honored ones. The two honored ones are: first, the six-eyed, two-wheeled Holy Acala Vidyārāja; second, the Blue Great Vajra Yakṣa. Before these two honored ones, place a blazing sun-disc. Within the sun-disc, paint images of three honored ones. The three honored ones are: first, the Universal Illumination King Śākyamuni Golden-Wheel Buddha; second, the Medicine-Light Buddha; third, the Holy Cintāmaṇicakra Bodhisattva. The Buddha family, Vajra family, and Lotus family positions are as in the diagram below.

Next, make three ghost-images and place them in a copper cymbal-vessel filled with oil. Recite the Holy Acala Boundary True Word one hundred and eight times. Boil the three ghost-images, and perform homa twenty times in the furnace at night. If there is still no effect, practice for three times seven days. At that time the three ghosts will take form. In the ashes there will be syllables, and the syllables transform into the Serene Holy Acala Lord. This is called "the three ghosts return to abide in the awakened immovable true suchness."

If kings diligently practice this dharma, they will be free from illness, enjoy long life, and their lands will be peaceful, prosperous, and flourishing. For its sublime efficacy in healing illness, if one recites this vidyārāja's mantra one thousand eight hundred times, the deity will immediately manifest in visible form and empower the practitioner. If one wishes to remove ghost-specter illness, recite toward this blue vajra seven hundred times, and the deity will also manifest the ghost-specter's form for that person and grant protection. If one wishes to eliminate seasonal epidemic diseases, strike the patient with a branch, and the disease will be immediately eliminated.

This is a brief explanation of small wholesome wishes; the complete account is found in the Dhāraṇī Collection Sūtra. This method for repelling ghost-demons is the most secret of secrets. One should always use the Fire Realm fifteen seals for empowered protection.

The Blue Vajra possesses three eyes and four arms. His countenance is of great wrathful aspect with dog-fangs protruding upward, three eyes of great terrifying appearance. He wears a crown of skulls, his hair grows upward in reverse, and a great serpent is wound about his crown. The four arms: the first left hand holds a wish-fulfilling jewel; the first right hand grasps a lasso; the second left hand holds a great sword; the second right hand grasps a golden wheel. On both shoulders are skull-necklaces, and he wears a tiger-skin skirt. His left and right feet each tread upon a ghost; the ghosts beneath the left and right feet are in the form of corpses. This Great Vajra Yakṣa is extremely wrathful.

He has four great yakṣa attendants: the yellow and red yakṣas both hold great swords; the green and black yakṣas hold three-pronged blades. These four great yakṣas all have long claws, sharp as swords. Each leads eighty thousand yakṣa attendants to guard and protect all sentient beings in Jambudvīpa.

After this maṇḍala is complete, at the time of a solar or lunar eclipse, quickly construct a small altar. Adorn this maṇḍala, and according to one's ability prepare food offerings. When the sun or moon returns to fullness, consume the food. If one practices the six methods in this way, there is nothing further to clarify. The thirteen items for the altar are: food offerings, four pure mirrors, four great swords, four arrows, and five-colored thread. These are the royal palace offering implements. Next, protect the offering implements according to the standard offering rites.

Pacification, love-and-respect, official rank, blessings and fortune, subjugation, and all other methods are each accomplished and swiftly realized. Foolish beings of the final age who take refuge in this dharma: the Tripiṭaka translator considered it profoundly secret and did not transmit it. Only after the great Vairocana vajra-abhiṣeka may one transmit and receive this text.

The four-line praise:

*Ha vajra-yakṣa-rāja namas-tute apratihata-ya namo sarva-māra-bhaya vimukti-tute vajra-jvāla-pūra haṃ namas-tute*

This secret praise must not be recklessly transmitted.

The Great Vajra is bestowed at a separate time.

Since all treasuries contain various precious treasures, this is called the siddhi. The ghost-demon sun acts as the draining. The seal-form is the same wheel-seal as before. But open the ring fingers. This is secret within secrets; it must never be transmitted to even two persons.

Regarding the forms of the three ghosts: first, the human ghost, in the form of a dead corpse. Second, the heavenly ghost, in the form of an owl-bird, also called a heavenly fox. Third, the earth ghost, in the form of a ground fox, also called an epilepsy ghost. Abiding in great courageous mind, recite the Acala Fire Realm Mantra one hundred times and boil the three ghost-images; then enter the Acala Lord's fire-born samādhi and constantly abide therein. For the meaning, see the ritual manual text.

Regarding observation of ghost-demon and dual illnesses: this requires deep contemplative power and mantra power to achieve a cure. For karmic illness, inwardly one requires effort and outwardly one must perform repentance to achieve a cure.

If all treatments fail to obtain the desired effect, one must not grasp the blade and handle the edge while seeking longevity on one's own. The Classic of Longevity states: "A drum-poison in the eyes causes darkness and foul breath and falling teeth. A drum-substance in the belly attacks the organs, diminishes vitality, and makes one sensitive to others' good and ill, daring to consume sentient beings. A drum in the feet disturbs the five essences, causing restless agitation of the spirit, and then harms Longevity." At one point, the Heavenly Emperor records people's transgressions, severs their register of life, and desires them to die quickly. The po-soul enters the Three Springs: at that time they are called ghosts. They cause harm to humans: heart pain, spasms, and palpitations. Ghost-illness torments and transmits to children, grandchildren, brothers, and sisters as transmitted corpse-disease, and further to connected bone-disease. Sometimes an entire clan dies out without anyone knowing the cause. In more serious recent cases, death comes within months. For this reason people greatly fear it, and fathers and sons sever their bonds, husbands and wives abandon their duty. This is karmic illness, exactly as stated in the original sūtra text.

"Corpse" means "exhaustion"; one should study this.

Regarding correct contemplation: it also arises when the practitioner entertains wrong thoughts during seated meditation, and the benefit-māra manifests various garments, food, seven treasures, and miscellaneous objects. If one accepts them, the contemplation enters the heart and becomes illness. This illness is difficult to treat.
