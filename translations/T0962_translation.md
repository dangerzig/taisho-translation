# 寶悉地成佛陀羅尼經
## Sūtra of the Dhāraṇī of the Jewel-Siddhi for Attaining Buddhahood

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0962

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Bhagavān was in the land of Magadha, beside the white lotus pool of the pure garden, beneath the seven-jeweled wish-fulfilling tree king, together with Vajrapāṇi and other great bodhisattvas, and with immeasurable great śrāvakas, and with countless Ganges-sands of devas, nāga kings, yakṣas, rākṣasas, and various other hosts, all surrounding him. Each one bowed at the Buddha's feet and withdrew to sit to one side.

At that time, the Tathāgata abided in the great dhāraṇī-king wish-fulfilling jewel cakravartin secret samādhi. From the uṣṇīṣa protuberance atop his head, he emitted a great radiance called "Jewel-Raining, Wish-Fulfilling, Pure, Removing-the-Karmic-Obstructions-of-Sentient-Beings Radiance." Emitting this radiance, it illuminated and touched the ten directions, startling and awakening all the Buddha-lands of the ten directions and the great palaces of the eight divisions, causing them to shake in six ways. It universally rained fragrant flowers and immeasurable precious jewels, broadly performing the deeds of the Buddha. Within the radiance, there appeared relics and wish-fulfilling jewels, performing vast and great acts of benefit. At that time, Vajra-Secret-Hand Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva, together with immeasurable hundreds of thousands of vajra-clan retinue, rose from his seat, bowed at the Buddha's feet, joined his palms in reverence, and addressed the Buddha: "May the World-Honored One, in great compassion and pity for sentient beings and for us, resolve our doubts. For what cause and condition does the Tathāgata today emit this great radiance and manifest these extraordinary auspicious signs? We wish to know the cause. May the World-Honored One expound the essential Dharma." At that time, the Buddha told Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva: "Excellent, excellent! You, Vajrapāṇi, for the benefit of all sentient beings, freely question the Tathāgata about the profoundly essential Dharma. I will now expound it. All of you, listen carefully." Vajrapāṇi again addressed the Buddha: "May the Tathāgata expound the secret essentials. We rejoice to hear them." Having thus requested three times, he fell silent and waited.

The Buddha told Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva: "With my Buddha-eye I observe all sentient beings of every kind. Though they dwell amid the defilements and impurities of the various destinies, the jewel of their mind-nature is without stain. Their virtues and benefits are fully complete, no different from mine. Yet sentient beings do not know the cause of suffering and do not realize the fruit of happiness. They are forever submerged in the ocean of suffering with no time of emergence. Therefore the Tathāgata emerges from the vajra pure realm and enters the defiled and evil world of the womb, expounding the essential Dharma to benefit sentient beings, extinguishing transgressions and generating merit, subduing Māra, accomplishing the Buddha-body, and returning to abide in nirvāṇa. Ultimately equal, there is nowhere he does not reach. Therefore the Tathāgata today abides in the wish-fulfilling jewel-king samādhi and teaches this pure, equal, principle-reality, profoundly meaningful, essential Dharma, the ultimate jewel-seal."

At that time, when the Tathāgata taught this Dharma, the entire great assembly realized the patience of the birthlessness of the profoundly deep Dharma, and also realized the samādhi of the wish-fulfilling jewel radiance, equal and without any difference in stages, like milk and water. At that time, Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva praised in gāthā verse:

> "Excellent, excellent, Great Guide! Emitting radiance to benefit all beings.
> Excellent, excellent, Great Tamer! Raining jewels to benefit all beings.
> Excellent, excellent, Great Man! Expounding secret teachings to benefit the multitudes.
> Excellent, World-Honored One, Great Lion! Devouring all the evil māras.
> Excellent, World-Honored One, Great Nāga King! Raising great cloud-rains to moisten the world.
> May the Great Sun, Universally Illuminating Honored One,
> Reveal the prajñā that enters the syllable *a*."

At that time, Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva, having spoken these verses, cast the five-pronged vajra from his right hand into the air, where it returned to his hand. He returned to his seat and gazed upon the Buddha's intent, his eyes never turning away for a moment. At that time, the Buddha told Vajrapāṇi, Ākāśagarbha, and the other great bodhisattvas: "All sentient beings and all the Tathāgatas share one and the same equal dharma-body. There is no separate essence and no separate mind. Phenomena and principle are equal, like empty space, constantly abiding in one characteristic without any difference. Yet sentient beings are deeply attached to wrong views. Because of conditioned dharmas, they do not relinquish deluded thoughts and give rise to discriminating marks. They do not know that the Tathāgata's ten-thousand-virtued radiance, the indestructible relic, constantly abides within the single thought-moment of sentient beings' minds, a pure white lotus, like the full moon. Therefore they are constantly born in the six destinies, poor and of low station. They do not befriend the good, do not approach the wise. Creating evil karma, they drift through birth and death without obtaining the siddhi and without any time of emergence. Therefore, I now, for the sake of delivering those sentient beings, teach the great jewel-dhāraṇī, called the Dharma-Body Dhātu Wish-Fulfilling Jewel Amṛta Medicine-King Vajra Vigorous Ever-Abiding True-Suchness Jewel-King Great Seal. In the age of the final Dharma, in southern Jambudvīpa, if there are good men or good women who obtain a Buddha-relic, even one grain or a single fraction, and faithfully receive and uphold it, one should know that such a person, being a true child of the Buddha through the Buddha-relic, is the ever-abiding body of the Dharma-body Śākyamuni Tathāgata. This person is then called Mahāvairocana, and also called the World-Saving Great Ācārya. One who holds a Buddha-relic and recites this true word thereby obtains such a title, and is called a Great Wisdom Skillful-Means Sattva. If any humans, devas, or the entire great assembly uphold this mantra, contemplate the Buddha-nature, and pay reverence to the relics, all the heavy transgressions of the four pārājikas, the five heinous crimes, and slandering the Dharma will be extinguished at once. All auspicious matters and siddhis will be accomplished. If there are good men or good women with courageous and unwearying faith who contemplate the Buddha-nature and observe the principle-reality, extinguishing karma and generating goodness; who constantly recollect, chant, uphold, and recite the mantra: short life-span and enmity will be cut off at once, like the fire at the end of a kalpa burning dry grass without any remainder. If there are sentient beings who, due to the karma of miserliness and greed, are born into a family of poverty, who are sickly and emaciated, who create transgressions and turn their backs on goodness, and who should fall into hell and other difficult places: if such a person confesses the evil karma of past lives, takes refuge in the Three Treasures, reveres and trusts the dhātu-relic, takes refuge in the original-awakening jewel-relic, and then skillfully adorns the practice-hall; if they invite all the Buddhas and Tathāgatas of the ten directions and their vajra-treasury bodies, complete bodies, fragments, and transformation-body relics, and prepare incense and flowers to extinguish transgressions and generate merit: how much more so for one who, according to the Dharma, forms the seal, recites the mantra, and contemplates their own nature, in whose heart the Tathāgata's great awesome-virtue relic constantly abides! All siddhis will be accomplished at once. Though one has not entered the great maṇḍala consecration-altar, one enters the samaya of the equality of merit and wisdom, receives the permission-gate of all the Buddhas, the profoundly secret great consecration Dharma, and immediately realizes the great nāga-king seal of the Buddha-nature wisdom-ocean."

He further told Vajrapāṇi: "In the future world, in southern Jambudvīpa, if there are persons of extremely heavy evil karma, or even evil animals, and at the time of death they encounter a greatly magnanimous, deeply wise, supremely discerning, greatly compassionate world-saving great-wisdom bodhisattva or world-saving great-wisdom elder, that person should quickly take earth from beneath the hall, tower, or stūpa where relics are enshrined, draw clean water, wash this pure earth, and pour it into the mouth and sprinkle it upon the crown of the evil person or animal. At the moment of death, all their torment will cease, they will abide rightly in correct mindfulness, gradually generate shame and remorse, abandon birth and death, generate the aspiration for rebirth, and will be reborn. They will swiftly realize the stage of the dharma-body. If there are bhikṣus or the four kinds of disciples, in the final age when the Dharma is about to be extinguished, and all sentient beings wish to accomplish the great siddhi-dharma, following the previous essential method, they should construct stūpas. The size may be large or small, according to one's ability: of gold, silver, crystal, earth, or wood, and enshrine Buddha-bones within. If those of scant merit cannot obtain relics, then with gold, silver, copper, iron, maṇi, sea-treasures, ox-king or deer-king horn, pearls, agate, wondrous stones, or rare woods, even the size of a mustard seed, called 'dhātu,' enshrine them in a stūpa or jeweled casket-box. The number should be six grains or up to seven grains as the supreme number, or as few as one grain as the minimum number. Bow to and make offerings to them, and the seven treasures will rain down in a torrential shower. The four great dharmas and the three kinds of siddhis will be fulfilled at once. Good men! You should know: the Tathāgata's relics are unsurpassed. They are rare in the world. Even the bodhisattvas find them difficult to encounter. How much more the ordinary beings of the world! These Buddha-relics are the formless maṇi, the true-nature wish-fulfilling jewel, the secret great vigorous effort. The adornment-treasures of all the Buddha-lands of the ten directions, the wish-fulfilling banners, maṇi necklaces, pearl curtains, jade couches, pearl celestial canopies, seven-jeweled screens, and various-colored bedding: such wondrous things, being the most supreme of precious jewels, cannot be valued, nor is there any vessel that can contain them. Therefore this sūtra teaches: use a round object to contain the relic. Like the form of your heart, like the form of the Buddha's heart. Because your heart and the Buddha's heart are mutually placed, because your body and the Buddha's body mutually interpenetrate. Since they mutually interpenetrate, the nature is non-dual. The Buddha-body relic should be upheld in this way. It may also be enshrined in a five-ring stūpa, a Many-Treasures stūpa, a three-pronged stūpa, a five-pronged stūpa, or a single-pronged stūpa. The same objects are placed therein: mutually interpenetrating and non-different, equal in stage. If bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, upāsikās, and the four assemblies receive and uphold a Buddha-relic, even one grain or a single fraction, and constantly practice reverence and always carry it upon their person, wherever such a person goes becomes a great maṇḍala of the Vajradhātu. All things they come near become the essence of the four kinds of dharma-body. Whether near a man or a woman, near a cow or a horse, near a dog or a cat, near any bird, beast, insect, spider, or any sentient being of the animal realm, all will in that very birth enter the first gate of bodhi and attain the initial stage of a bodhisattva." At that time, the World-Honored One spoke the root dhāraṇī of the dhātu:

"*Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-uṣṇīṣa-dhātu mudrāṇi sarva-tathāgatāṃ sa-dhātu vibhūṣita-adhiṣṭhite hūṃ vaṃ hoḥ hūṃ svāhā*"

The Buddha told Vajra-Yakṣa Bodhisattva: "If any sentient beings wish to see the Buddhas of the ten directions and the great bodhisattvas, or wish to seek the most supreme siddhi, they should face the relic-stūpa, form the Tathāgata Great Bowl Seal, and recite the true word twenty-three thousand times. They will certainly see my constantly abiding complete body, and all siddhis will definitely be accomplished without the slightest doubt. The seal is as follows: the two thumbs are stacked, with the right hand placed upon the left palm, and the tips of the two space-fingers touching each other. This is called the Dharma-Body Great Bowl Seal, also called the Jewel-Casket Vajra Seal.

"Furthermore, there is a divine mantra called the Wish-Fulfilling Jewel Fulfilling-Prayer Heart Dhāraṇī, which can accomplish all deeds. Whatever one thinks of in the mind will be accomplished without exception, except for those who lack faith, who are not single-minded, or who take the essential Dharma lightly." Then he spoke the mantra:

"*Oṃ cintāmaṇi ratna-kiṃ jeyā-dāna namo śāntika ra huṃ huṃ svāhā*"

"If anyone recites this true word two times, three times, or up to seven times, all transgressions will be extinguished and all merits will be generated. If one recites twenty-one times daily, one will certainly obtain a true-body relic of the Buddhas, the miraculous jewel-relic of the seven Buddhas' uṣṇīṣa-cakravartin treasured by the great ocean nāga king, and the unsurpassed siddhi will all be accomplished.

"Furthermore, there is a divine mantra called the Secret True-Nature Wish-Fulfilling Jewel Seal. If one can uphold and recite it, beginningless karmic obstructions will be swiftly extinguished, immeasurable merit and wisdom will be obtained without seeking, māras and enemies will be subdued, one will immediately be freed from birth and death, and all siddhis will be accomplished without exception. The seal is as follows: the two hands form a jewel-lotus joined-palm mudrā, the two fingertips in the jewel shape, the two thumbs standing side by side. This is called the True-Nature Maṇi Jewel Seal, also called the Great Vigorous Peak Tathāgata Crown-Jewel Seal." Then he spoke the divine mantra:

"*Oṃ cintāmaṇi dhātu hūṃ svāhā*"

"Recite one hundred and eight times daily, and the five-division siddhis and the three-division wish-fulfilling jewels will all be perfectly fulfilled. Furthermore, there is a divine mantra called the Ability-to-Fulfill-Wishes Great Full Ākāśagarbha Jewel. If one can uphold it, all one's wishes will be swiftly fulfilled. The seal is as follows: the two thumbs form an outward bond, the two fingertips in the jewel shape, the two thumbs standing side by side." Then he spoke the mantra:

"*Oṃ moḍa-śarīra-vajra-ratna oṃ hūṃ vaṃ hoḥ*"

"If one obtains a Buddha-relic, regardless of pure or impure conditions, one should make offerings with it always upon one's person. Never separating it from the chest, the *hṛdaya* wondrous lotus space, one should constantly recollect the Buddha's virtues day and night without ceasing. In this very body, one will realize the Buddha's pure body and abide upon the vajra-peak. Such a person is called one who upholds the Buddha's body, one who upholds the vajra, one who upholds the jewel-dharma. This is a great bodhisattva-mahāsattva."

The Buddha further said: "Good men! Suppose there are in this world foolish children, foolish ordinary persons, foolish and confused beings, aged women, ignorant monks and nuns. If such beings obtain a Buddha-relic, even one grain or a single fraction, or objects in which relics have been enshrined, and carry it upon their body, whether day or night, whether pure or impure, whether in contact with defilement or not, never separating it from their body but always carrying it, the merit obtained is beyond all calculation and comparison. Whether upon the crown, the neck, before the heart, behind the back, at either side, or at the waist, one who carries a relic is like the horn of a rhinoceros in this world: when a person carries it, water recedes six feet. One who carries a relic is likewise: separated from all transgressive karma, evil karma and evil destinies cannot stain them. Roots of merit gradually grow, evil obstructions gradually depart. Good people naturally come, and evil people naturally leave. All deeds performed are the same as the Buddha's deeds, because one is the same as Śākyamuni Tathāgata. That body is a response-manifestation in this world, and is the pure dharma-body Mahāvairocana Buddha. That person will certainly enter the great nirvāṇa stage. In this very body, they will realize the formless dharma-body great vigorous peak. Therefore, one should know: the twelve great celestial deities, the vajra-devas, and all the spiritual guardians will constantly guard and protect them. Their life-span will be extended, and having seen a hundred autumns, at the end of life they will definitely be reborn in the Tuṣita Heaven palace, hearing the Dharma with joy. Following their vows, they may also be reborn in the pure lands of the ten directions, see the Buddha, hear the Dharma, and realize the stage of non-retrogression. If any sentient being wishes to attain Buddhahood in this very body and to deliver sentient beings, they should form the great seal of the wondrous wisdom that makes sentient beings equal with all the Buddhas in one characteristic: the left and right fists touching, with the left wind-finger placed at the heart. This seal is also called the Seal that Extinguishes the Darkness of Ignorance. By the empowerment of this seal, sentient beings and all the Buddhas become equal in one characteristic. This is the dharma-body relic wondrous-wisdom great seal, also called the Wish-Fulfilling Jewel-King Accomplishing-All-Great-Siddhis Seal. There is also a great dhāraṇī called the Golden-Wheel-King One-Syllable Dhātu Vidyā." Then he spoke the mantra:

"*Bhruṃ oṃ*" (combined, with a sharp exhalation)

"Good men! This mantra is the secret divine mantra of the relic-jewel. One who upholds and recites it should keep this mantra hidden. Do not carelessly proclaim it among those without wisdom. If one sees a worthy vessel of the Dharma, teach this secret, most-supreme-vehicle Dharma without allowing it to be cut off, benefiting the world. All will accomplish the vajra indestructible dhātu dharma-body, and all siddhis will be accomplished. If there is a great ācārya who promotes the Buddha-vehicle, a bodhisattva of great capacity who promotes the Buddha's legacy, or one of great world-saving wisdom, they should take this Buddha-relic and, day and night, bow in reverence, generate the unsurpassed vow, rain all precious jewels and wondrous wealth, and broadly benefit all sentient beings of the dharma-realm. From my abode, precious wealth issues forth and springs from the Buddha-relics, filling and gradually pervading the dharma-realm. With this precious wealth, promote and flourish the Buddha-Dharma, establish the Buddha's legacy, and then benefit those sentient beings with whom one has karmic connections, gradually extending to those without connections, binding them with the lasso of compassion, leading them to the vajra jewel-field of the dharma-realm."

At that time, when the World-Honored One taught this Dharma, all the Tathāgatas' true-body and transformation-body relics and bodhisattva-mahāsattvas of the ten directions, the Vajrasattva Bodhisattva-Mahāsattvas of the Buddha-lands of the ten directions, Vajra-King, Vajra-Love Bodhisattva, and others, all the vajra-clan retinue, the yakṣa generals, all the wrathful hosts, Mañjuśrī, Avalokiteśvara, Ākāśagarbha, Ratnākara, Ratnaketu, and the other great bodhisattvas, Lotus-Eye, the Lotus-Clan and other great beings, Brahmā, Śakra, the World-Protectors, the twelve great devas, the vidyādhara-cakravartin ṛṣis, the māra-kings, the human kings, the great ocean-lord Sāgara Nāga King, the Anavatapta Pool Nāga King, Suśrī Nāga Princess, and others, the entire great assembly, having heard and upheld this secret Dharma, were all greatly delighted, faithfully received it, and practiced accordingly.
