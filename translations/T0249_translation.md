# 佛說帝釋般若波羅蜜多心經
## Heart Sūtra of Prajñāpāramitā Spoken by the Buddha to Śakra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0249

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dānapāla (施護)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the city of Rājagṛha, on Vulture Peak Mountain, with a countless great assembly of bhikṣus, and ten koṭis of bodhisattva-mahāsattvas of youthful appearance.

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed Śakra, Lord of the Devas: "Kauśika! This prajñāpāramitā, its meaning is extremely profound. It is neither one nor different, neither with characteristics nor without characteristics, neither grasped nor abandoned, neither increased nor decreased, neither with afflictions nor without afflictions, neither abandoned nor not abandoned, neither abiding nor not abiding, neither corresponding nor not corresponding, neither affliction nor non-affliction, neither conditioned nor unconditioned, neither real nor unreal, neither Dharma nor non-Dharma, neither with a destination nor without a destination, neither the limit of reality nor not the limit of reality.

"Kauśika! Thus all dharmas are equal, and prajñāpāramitā is also equal. All dharmas are quiescent, and prajñāpāramitā is also quiescent. All dharmas are unmoving, and prajñāpāramitā is also unmoving. All dharmas are discriminated, and prajñāpāramitā is also discriminated. All dharmas are fearful, and prajñāpāramitā is also fearful. All dharmas are fully known, and prajñāpāramitā is also fully known. All dharmas are of one taste, and prajñāpāramitā is also of one taste. All dharmas do not arise, and prajñāpāramitā also does not arise. All dharmas do not cease, and prajñāpāramitā also does not cease. All dharmas are empty-space false conceptions, and prajñāpāramitā is also an empty-space false conception. Form is boundless, and prajñāpāramitā is also boundless. Likewise, feeling, perception, volitional formations, and consciousness are boundless, and prajñāpāramitā is also boundless. The earth element is boundless, and prajñāpāramitā is also boundless. Likewise, the water element, fire element, wind element, space element, and consciousness element are boundless, and prajñāpāramitā is also boundless. The diamond is equal, and prajñāpāramitā is also equal. All dharmas are indestructible, and prajñāpāramitā is also indestructible. The nature of all dharmas is unobtainable, and prajñāpāramitā is also unobtainable. The nature of all dharmas is equal, and prajñāpāramitā is also equal. All dharmas are without nature, and prajñāpāramitā is also without nature. All dharmas are inconceivable, and prajñāpāramitā is also inconceivable. Likewise, the pāramitā of generosity, the pāramitā of morality, the pāramitā of patience, the pāramitā of diligence, the pāramitā of meditation, the pāramitā of skillful means, the pāramitā of vows, the pāramitā of powers, and the pāramitā of knowledge are also inconceivable. The three karmas are pure, and prajñāpāramitā is also pure. Thus, prajñāpāramitā: its meaning is boundless.

"Furthermore, Kauśika! There are the eighteen emptinesses. What are the eighteen emptinesses? Internal emptiness, external emptiness, internal-and-external emptiness, emptiness of emptiness, great emptiness, ultimate emptiness, conditioned emptiness, unconditioned emptiness, boundless emptiness, unchanging emptiness, beginningless emptiness, original-nature emptiness, own-characteristic emptiness, signless emptiness, no-nature emptiness, self-nature emptiness, the emptiness of the self-nature of no-nature, and the emptiness of all dharmas. In verse:

> "Like a star, a lamp, a visual aberration,
> A dream, an illusion, a bubble, a dewdrop,
> Like lightning, and likewise like a cloud:
> One should contemplate in this way.
> I now briefly speak this
> Prajñāpāramitā:
> Not arising and not ceasing,
> Not annihilated and not eternal,
> Neither one meaning nor many,
> Neither coming nor going.
> Thus the twelve links of dependent origination
> Are stilled and made quiescent,
> As the Perfectly Awakened One has taught.
> With reverent faith in the supreme teacher,
> I take refuge in the Buddhas of the ten directions,
> Past, present, and future,
> And the pāramitā of the Three Treasures.
> The ocean of immeasurable merit:
> Making offerings to all the Tathāgatas,
> The great and luminous true secret."

The mantra is spoken:

*Tadyathā prajñe prajñe mahāprajñe prajñe vāvaśī prajñe rūka kari aniyā nā vidhamani siddhi susiddhi siddhāntu me bhagavatī sarvāṅga sundarī bhakti vātsalī prasartā hā sati samasvāsa kāri sthiṭa sthiṭa buddhi buddhi kampa kampa cala cala rava rava agaccha agaccha bhagavatī mā vilamba soha svāhā*

*Namo dharmaudgatāya bodhisattvāya mahāsattvāya mahākāruṇikāya. Namo sadāparudita bodhisattvāya mahāsattvāya mahākāruṇikāya. Namo prajñāpāramitāyai. Tadyathā muni dharmi saṃgraha dharmi anugraha dharmi vimukta dharmi sattvānugraha dharmi vaiśravaṇa dharmi samantānuparivartana dharmi guṇagraha saṃgraha dharmi sarvatra anugata dharmi sarvakalpa pariprapaṇa dharmi svāhā. Prajñāpāramitāyai tadyathā ākhanī nākhanī ākhanānī khanī āvaravaṃdhanī bandhanī bandhanī panadri svāhā.*

*Namo prajñāpāramitāyai. Tadyathā aṃga aṃga nātira aṃga nāti nāvabhāsa aṃga svāhā.*

*Namo prajñāpāramitāyai. Tadyathā śrīye śrīye muniśrīye muniśrīyase svāhā. Prajñāpāramitāyai tadyathā oṃ vamale svāhā.*

*Namo prajñāpāramitāyai. Tadyathā oṃ hliṃ śliṃ dhliṃ śrūti dhṛti smṛgati vijaye svāhā.*

*Namo prajñāpāramitāyai. Tadyathā vaṃ vari vaṃ vari mahā vaṃ vari dhuru dhuru mahā dhuru svāhā.*

*Namo prajñāpāramitāyai. Tadyathā hūte hūte hūtaśani sarvakarma vāraṇani svāhā.*

*Namo prajñāpāramitāyai. Tadyathā oṃ arūlikā svāhā.*

*Namo prajñāpāramitāyai. Tadyathā oṃ sarvavidu svāhā.*

*Namo prajñāpāramitāyai. Tadyathā gate gate pāragate pārasaṃgate bodhi svāhā.*

At that time, when the World-Honored One had spoken this sūtra, Śakra, Lord of the Devas, and all the bodhisattva-mahāsattvas, devas, humans, gandharvas, asuras, and the entire great assembly, hearing what the Buddha taught, were all greatly joyful, and they accepted it with faith and practiced it.
