# 佛說樓閣正法甘露鼓經
## Sūtra of the Pavilion of the True Dharma, the Drum of Sweet Dew, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0704

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Devaśāntika (天息災)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the World-Honored One was in the land of Śrāvastī, at the Jeta Grove of Anāthapiṇḍada. At that time, the Venerable Ānanda, with a sincere and truthful heart, went to the World-Honored One's place. Arriving, he bowed his head at the World-Honored One's feet. Having paid his respects, he stood to one side. Then the Venerable Ānanda said to the World-Honored One: "How does one plant pure roots of merit? How does one make a maṇḍala? How does one take refuge and uphold the rules of training? What wholesome karma is accomplished when one joins one's ten fingers together in reverence toward the Tathāgata? World-Honored One! How can the roots of merit of cyclic existence not be destroyed? How does one exhaust karma and attain nirvāṇa? What is the merit of making a Tathāgata image?"

The World-Honored One said: "Ānanda! You should purely cultivate and uphold the five virtues. I shall further explain what words one should speak to arouse one's own mind and the minds of others, and further cause the noble and wise to rejoice, so that wholesome karma becomes towering and all wishes are fulfilled, and at the end of one's life one is born in the Sugata heaven.

"Ānanda! If someone, with a devoted heart, for the sake of the Buddha makes offerings and creates a square maṇḍala, I say that in future lives such a person will become a lord of wealth in the northern continent of Uttarakuru, and after death will be born in the Trāyastriṃśa heaven.

"Ānanda! If someone, with a devoted heart, for the sake of the Buddha makes offerings and creates a half-moon-shaped maṇḍala, I say that in future lives such a person will become a lord of wealth in the eastern continent of Pūrvavideha, and after death will be born in the Yāma heaven.

"Ānanda! If someone, with a devoted heart, for the sake of the Buddha makes offerings and creates a circular maṇḍala, I say that in future lives such a person will become a lord of wealth in the western continent of Godānīya, and after death will be born in the Tuṣita heaven.

"Ānanda! If someone, with a devoted heart, for the sake of the Buddha makes offerings and creates a carriage-shaped maṇḍala, I say that in future lives such a person will become a lord of wealth in the southern continent of Jambudvīpa, and after death will be born in the Nirmāṇarati heaven.

"Ānanda! If someone takes refuge in the Buddha, the Dharma, and the Saṃgha, and protects and upholds the pure precepts, I say that the merit of such roots of merit is immeasurable and boundless. All śrāvakas and pratyekabuddhas, even unto the limit of their nirvāṇa, are unable to compare with it."

At that time, the World-Honored One told the Venerable Ānanda: "As you know, the red lotus flower is soft, stainless; the aśoka leaf is copper-colored and subtle. My tongue is like these: it extends and covers the face up to the hairline, as you have seen. Ānanda! Who, by false speech, frivolous speech, harsh speech, or divisive speech, could possess such a tongue? The tongue of the Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha speaks only truth; thus his tongue is as it is.

"Ānanda! If someone takes refuge, joins their palms, and bows to that World-Honored One, the Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, and performs this act of taking refuge: such a being is rescued by me. Why? Ānanda! Because the dharma-realm of the Tathāgata is definitive. If someone, with a sincere and definitive heart, joins their palms, bows, and also practices generosity:

"Ānanda! Suppose there is a person who, in the short time it takes to wash one's hands and clean one's vessels, gives rise to a mind of benefiting living beings, wishing that all sentient beings may be at peace and happy. Ānanda! I say that such a person opens the gate of merit, closes the doors of the evil destinies, and gains freedom from the three kalpas of calamity. What are the three kalpas? They are the kalpa of war, the kalpa of plague, and the kalpa of famine.

"Ānanda! Furthermore, if a person observes for one day the precept of abstaining from killing, that person will not be born in the kalpa of war. If one gives even a single harītakī fruit to the Saṃgha, that person will not be born in the kalpa of plague. If one gives even a single bowl of food to the Saṃgha, that person will not be born in the kalpa of famine.

"Ānanda! There are three roots of merit that are inexhaustible and boundless: even while in cyclic existence they are not destroyed, and they will lead to nirvāṇa. Ānanda! What are the three? The roots of merit planted in relation to the Tathāgata are inexhaustible and boundless; even in cyclic existence they are not destroyed, and in the end they lead to nirvāṇa. The roots of merit planted in relation to the Dharma and the Saṃgha are likewise inexhaustible and boundless; even in cyclic existence they are not destroyed, and in the future they will certainly lead to nirvāṇa."

At that time, the World-Honored One, by the merit of the Tathāgata, said to Ānanda: "Do you see the southern continent of Jambudvīpa?"

Ānanda said to the World-Honored One: "Yes, I have seen it!"

"Ānanda! If a man or woman of good family were to make a Jambudvīpa of the seven treasures, carriage-shaped, seven thousand yojanas in length and breadth, and give it in offering to the Saṃgha of the four directions, including stream-enterers, once-returners, non-returners, arhats, and pratyekabuddhas; and if, after the parinirvāṇa of the Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, someone were to make a stūpa of clay the size of an āmalaka fruit, mount upon it a parasol-wheel the size of a needle, cover it with a canopy the size of a jujube leaf, place a Buddha image within it the size of a grain of barley, and inter relics below it the size of a white mustard seed: I say this merit is vast and surpasses the former.

"Ānanda! Set aside Jambudvīpa. If a man or woman of good family were to make the eastern continent of Pūrvavideha of the seven treasures, half-moon-shaped, eight thousand yojanas around, and give it in offering to the Saṃgha and the four fruits and pratyekabuddhas; and if someone were to make a stūpa of clay the size of an āmalaka fruit, with a parasol-wheel the size of a needle, a canopy the size of a jujube leaf, a Buddha image the size of a grain of barley, and relics the size of a white mustard seed: I say this merit is vast and surpasses the former.

"Ānanda! Set aside Jambudvīpa and Pūrvavideha. If a faithful man or woman of good family were to make the western continent of Godānīya of the seven treasures, round like the full moon, nine thousand yojanas around, and give it in offering to the Saṃgha and the four fruits and pratyekabuddhas; and if someone were to make a stūpa of clay the size of an āmalaka fruit, with a parasol-wheel the size of a needle, a canopy the size of a leaf, a Buddha image the size of a grain of barley, and relics the size of a white mustard seed: I say this merit surpasses the former.

"Ānanda! Set aside the three continents. If a man or woman of good family were to make the northern continent of Uttarakuru of the seven treasures, square, ten thousand yojanas on each side, and give it in offering to the Saṃgha and the four fruits and pratyekabuddhas; and if someone were to make a stūpa of clay the size of an āmalaka fruit, with a parasol-wheel the size of a needle, a canopy the size of a leaf, a Buddha image the size of a grain of barley, and relics the size of a white mustard seed: I say this merit is vast and surpasses the former.

"Ānanda! Set aside the four great continents. If a man or woman of good family were to make of the seven treasures the Hall of the Good Dharma of Śakra, Lord of the Devas, and give it in offering to the Saṃgha and the four fruits and pratyekabuddhas; and if someone, after the parinirvāṇa of the Tathāgata, were to make a stūpa of clay the size of an āmalaka fruit, with all the adornments, parasol-wheel, canopy, Buddha image, and relics as before: I say this merit is vast and surpasses the former.

"Ānanda! Set aside the four continents and the Hall of the Good Dharma. If a faithful man or woman of good family were to make of the seven treasures the entire three-thousand great-thousand world system and give it in offering to the Saṃgha and the four fruits and pratyekabuddhas; and if someone, after the parinirvāṇa of the Tathāgata, were to make a stūpa of clay the size of an āmalaka fruit, with all the adornments, parasol-wheel, canopy, Buddha image, and relics as before: I say this merit is vast and surpasses the former. Why? Ānanda! Because the Tathāgata's generosity, morality, patience, vigor, meditative concentration, and omniscience are immeasurable and boundless, as are the ten powers, the four wisdoms, the three distinctive qualities, and the four foundations of mindfulness, extending to the great compassion, which is also immeasurable and boundless. This is because the Tathāgata possesses such merit!

"Ānanda! When the Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha spoke this dharma, the three-thousand great-thousand world system trembled and shook throughout."

At that time, the Venerable Ānanda said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! What should this true Dharma be called? How should it be upheld?"

The Buddha told Ānanda: "This sūtra is called 'The Drum of Sweet Dew of the True Dharma,' also called 'The Unprecedented True Dharma.' Uphold it in this way!"

At that time, the Venerable Ānanda, with a heart of wonder, received the Buddha's holy instruction, faithfully upheld it, bowed, and withdrew.
