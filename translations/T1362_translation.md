# 佛說善夜經
## The Buddha's Teaching: Sūtra of the Auspicious Night

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1362

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Yijing (義淨).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Bhagavat was in Rājagṛha, at the Bamboo Grove. Not far away there was a bhikṣu dwelling beside the hot springs. A certain deva of handsome countenance and extraordinary radiance appeared after the first watch of the night and came to where the bhikṣu was. The deva's majestic light shone brilliantly all around, illuminating the entire area of the hot springs. Joining his palms in reverence, he sat to one side and said to the bhikṣu: "Venerable one! Have you previously heard the Sūtra of the Auspicious Night?" The bhikṣu answered: "I have never heard such a sūtra." He asked the deva in return: "Have you yourself heard it?" The deva said: "I too do not know it." The bhikṣu said: "Who would know it?" The deva said: "The unsurpassed compassionate father is at the Bamboo Grove. You may go there now and request instruction. Practice as the Buddha teaches." Having said this, he suddenly vanished.

When dawn came, the bhikṣu went to where the World-Honored One was, bowed at his feet, stood to one side, and said: "World-Honored One! Last night, after the first watch, a deva came to where I was. His radiance illuminated the entire area of the hot springs. He asked me: 'Have you previously heard the Sūtra of the Auspicious Night?' I said: 'I have not heard it.' I asked the deva: 'Have you heard it?' He answered: 'I do not know it.' I further asked: 'Who would know it?' He said: 'The unsurpassed compassionate father is at the Bamboo Grove. You may go and ask. Practice as the Buddha teaches.' Having said this, he suddenly vanished. Because of this matter I have come here to request instruction from the World-Honored One."

The Buddha asked the bhikṣu: "Do you know who that deva was?" He answered: "I do not know." "You should know: that was a sublime celestial prince of the Trāyastriṃśa Heaven, the mighty general named Candana. Wishing to benefit sentient beings, he came to awaken you and to ask the name of this sūtra."

Then the bhikṣu further addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I now wish to hear the Sūtra of the Auspicious Night. May the World-Honored One compassionately teach it."

At that time the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣu: "This Sūtra of the Auspicious Night possesses great merit. If anyone hears it, they can sever afflictions and swiftly realize awakening. You should listen carefully and give your utmost attention. I shall now teach it. The dharmas of the past should not be pursued with longing. The dharmas of the future should not be hoped for. The dharmas of the present should not give rise to attachment. One who practices thus is called truly liberated."

At that time the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> The past should not be pursued in thought;
> The future should not be hoped for.
> In the time of the present,
> Observe all things in accordance with Dharma.
>
> Deluded thoughts are hard to dismiss;
> The wise should observe them well.
> One should diligently practice with haste:
> How can one know if tomorrow will come?
>
> The hosts of the Lord of Death
> Are constantly close behind you.
> Therefore I, the Sage,
> Now teach the Sūtra of the Auspicious Night.
>
> May all sentient beings always
> Be freed from suffering and obtain peace and joy,
> Not create any evil karma,
> And constantly cultivate all wholesome deeds.

At that time the World-Honored One, wishing to benefit all sentient beings and enable them to obtain peace and joy throughout the long night, to be free from all obstructions and afflictions, and in every life to increase their wholesome roots, constantly encounter the Three Jewels, and not fall into the evil destinies, further spoke this dhāraṇī:

*Tadyathā: vi-ni-bha-rā-ṇi bha-tha-ma-ta-ṇe-mā-ṇi-ni ṭā ṭā ṭā ṭā gu-ri ghandha-ri caṇḍa-ri ma-daṃ-gi sa-rā-laṃ-ti mo-hu-rā-ṇi śe-pa-ri ca-kra bha-ki śe-va-ri mo-hā śe-va-ri pu-jiṃ-ga-ni ni-mi-ni miṃ-ga-ni kṛ-ta-ni svāhā. Saṃ-ye-ti daṃ a-hāṃ-ga-ti na-rā-ga-ti bhaṃ-su-ka pa-yī ka-pu te-ka pa-yī da-pu da-ni svāhā.*

"If any bhikṣu, bhikṣuṇī, upāsaka, upāsikā, or other good man or good woman, from within this Sūtra of the Auspicious Night, whether a single verse or a single mantra-phrase, reads, recites, receives, upholds, makes offerings to, or honors it, and clearly understands its meaning and expounds it for others: know that this person at all times will be free from calamity and adversity, free from unjust wrongdoing and all manner of misfortune and affliction. They will know the events of seven past lives and will never lose the mind of great awakening. They will certainly reach the true path of nirvāṇa. If any good man or good woman receives, upholds, reads, and recites this Sūtra of the Auspicious Night, in future lives wherever they are born, they will certainly obtain the knowledge of their past abodes and will constantly enjoy honor, security, and happiness."

He further spoke in verse:

> This person at all times
> Will be free from unjust wrongdoing,
> For having created karma in accord with the times,
> They are forever freed from untimely death.
>
> Guarding and protecting all sentient beings,
> Freeing them from illness, grief, and terror,
> From inauspicious omens and evil dreams:
> On perilous paths they are always secure.
>
> Whether man or woman,
> Those who carry and uphold this sūtra
> Will be endowed with the marks, honored by all,
> And all their wishes will be perfectly fulfilled.
>
> Whatever unwholesomeness
> Of body, speech, or mind,
> Through the majestic power of this sūtra,
> Will never bring an evil retribution.
>
> Whether water, fire, kings, bandits,
> Thunder, lightning, poisons, and harms,
> Or enmity and conflict,
> By reciting this sūtra all will be freed.
>
> There is furthermore a vidyā-mantra;
> If one is able to read and recite it,
> At all times
> It increases goodness and extinguishes all evils.

He then spoke the mantra:

*Tadyathā: ni-mi ni-miṃ da-ri ti-ri lu-ka lu-ki ni-ti-ri śu-rā dha-ri ni a-ku bi-ji-ri ti-nu-rī ku-tu-kuṃ-ti ku-tu-ku-ci ke-gṛ-ti ku-bi-ni. Protect and guard me, so-and-so, in all places of terror, in all places of illness and painful suffering, in all places of grief and affliction, in all places of venomous insects and poisons, in all places of ghosts and sorcery, in all places of kings, bandits, water, and fire, in all places of wild beasts and terrors, in all places of slander and litigation, in all places of enmity and conflict, in all places of evil karma of body and mind, and in all four kinds of verbal transgression, in all places of crisis and mortal peril. And may the Vajra-Spirits constantly guard me, so-and-so, together with all my kin. Svāhā.*

He further spoke the mantra:

*Tadyathā: hiri hiri miri miri pi-śā-ji pa-ṇa śe-va-ri ji-ri svāhā.*

When the World-Honored One had spoken this sūtra, the bhikṣu and the entire great assembly, humans, devas, the eight classes of beings, and all ghosts and spirits, all greatly rejoiced, believed, received it with faith, and put it into practice.
