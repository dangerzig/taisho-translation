# 大乘方廣總持經
## The Mahāyāna Vaipulya Dhāraṇī Sūtra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0275

Translated from the Chinese. Sui dynasty, translated by Viniścaya (毘尼多流支)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was at Rājagṛha on Mount Gṛdhrakūṭa, together with a great assembly of sixty-two thousand bhikṣus and eighty billion bodhisattva-mahāsattvas, as well as sixty billion hundreds of thousands of upāsakas of the kingdom of Magadha.

At that time, the World-Honored One, having completed the summer retreat, was approaching the time of nirvāṇa. He entered the Thus-Is-the-Dharma Samādhi. Having entered samādhi, the entire trichiliocosm was universally adorned with hanging silk banners and canopied standards, jeweled incense vessels placed throughout, adorned with various fragrances, and everywhere were scattered thousand-petaled lotus flowers.

At that time, the billions of hundreds of thousands of beings of this trichiliocosm, all the Brahmā kings, together with billions of hundreds of thousands of retainers, came to the Buddha's abode. Having arrived, they prostrated at his feet, joined their palms toward the Buddha, and withdrew to stand to one side. Additionally, there were billions of hundreds of thousands of Pure Abode devaputras, the Īśvara Heaven King, the Maheśvara Heaven King, dragon kings, yakṣa kings, asura kings, garuḍa kings, kiṃnara kings, and mahoraga kings, each with billions of hundreds of thousands of retainers, who came to the Buddha's abode. Having arrived, they prostrated at his feet, joined their palms toward the Buddha, and withdrew to stand to one side. At that time, from the ten directions, bodhisattva-mahāsattvas as numerous as the sands of the Ganges, of great majestic virtue, came to the Buddha's abode. Having arrived, they prostrated at his feet, joined their palms toward the Buddha, and withdrew to stand to one side. At that time, this trichiliocosm up to the Summit of Existence was entirely assembled, and the great multitudes filled every space with no gap remaining. Additionally, there were other devas, dragons, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, and mahoragas of great spiritual power who all came to the assembly.

At that time, the World-Honored One, with right mindfulness present, arose from samādhi and surveyed the great assembly. He yawned and stretched like a lion king, doing so three times. At that time, the World-Honored One extended from his mouth the broad, long tongue, which covered the trichiliocosm everywhere. When the Tathāgata had displayed this spiritual transformation, he again surveyed the great assembly. At that time, the entire great assembly immediately rose from their seats, joined their palms in reverence, and stood silently.

At that time, the Buddha addressed the Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Maitreya: "Ajita! The Tathāgata will not be long before entering nirvāṇa. If you have doubts about any dharma, I am now present. If you wish to ask, now is the time. After the Buddha's nirvāṇa, do not generate sorrow and regret."

At that time, the Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Maitreya addressed the Buddha: "Yes, World-Honored One! You yourself know the proper time. All buddhas and tathāgatas have exhaustively penetrated all dharmas. May you proclaim them, so that this Dharma-eye may long abide in the world."

At that time, in the assembly, the Maheśvara Devaputra, together with eighty billion Pure Abode celestial beings in his retinue, prostrated at the Buddha's feet, joined his palms in reverence, and addressed the Buddha, saying: "World-Honored One! This Mahāyāna Vaipulya Dhāraṇī Dharma-gate has already been proclaimed by past immeasurable buddhas, tathāgatas, worthy ones of right and universal knowledge. May the World-Honored One now again expound it for the benefit, peace, and joy of immeasurable humans and devas, enabling the Buddha-Dharma to long abide in the world." At that time, the World-Honored One silently consented. Then the Maheśvara Devaputra, knowing the Buddha had consented, was filled with joy and delight, joined his palms in reverence, and withdrew to stand to one side.

At that time, the Buddha addressed the Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Maitreya: "Ajita! This Mahāyāna Vaipulya Dhāraṇī Dharma-gate is not taught by me alone. Past, future, and presently existing buddhas of the ten directions, immeasurable in number, also constantly proclaim it. If there are sentient beings who, regarding what the Buddha has taught, say it is not the Buddha's teaching, and who slander the Dharma and the Sangha, such slanderers will fall into evil destinies and suffer the torments of hell."

At that time, the Buddha addressed the Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Maitreya: "If there are good men and good women who generate the aspiration for bodhi, and who regarding this Mahāyāna Vaipulya Dhāraṇī sūtra receive, uphold, read, and recite it, and further explain it for others, one should know that such persons will not fall into evil destinies."

At that time, the World-Honored One further addressed the Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Maitreya: "Ajita! From the night I became a buddha to the time I shall enter nirvāṇa without remainder, during this entire interval, has there been any forgetfulness or arousal of evil karma in the Buddha's body, speech, or mind, in his actions, speech, thoughts, or contemplations?"

The Bodhisattva Maitreya said: "No, World-Honored One!"

The Buddha said: "Maitreya! As you have said. From the time I attained the path until nirvāṇa, everything I have spoken and taught has been entirely true, without any falsehood. If there are foolish people who do not understand what the Tathāgata has taught through skillful means, and who say, 'This dharma is thus; this dharma is not thus,' slandering the true Dharma and the buddhas and bodhisattvas, I say that such persons are heading toward hell."

The Buddha said: "Ajita! After my nirvāṇa, in the world of five turbidities, if there are bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, and upāsikās who are not truly bodhisattvas but call themselves bodhisattvas, they are externalists. Because of vow-power from having made offerings to buddhas in past lives, they leave the household life within the Buddha's Dharma and Vinaya. Wherever they go, they seek many friends and companions, fame, profit, and offerings. They indulge in defilement, abandon faith, accomplish evil deeds, do not restrain themselves, do not subdue themselves, and are greedy for various offerings. They are entirely distant from all dharma-gates and the firm samādhi of arising, truly knowing nothing. For the sake of relatives, they falsely claim understanding, dwelling in deceit, speaking one thing with their mouths while acting differently with their bodies.

"Ajita! My bodhisattva-path treats all sentient beings equally, abiding in great compassion, with skillful means and right mindfulness never forgotten. The Tathāgata abides in the power of unequaled equality, without obstruction or hindrance, and teaches the Dharma accordingly. If there are sentient beings who say, 'The sūtras the Buddha taught for śrāvakas should not be studied or listened to by bodhisattvas; this is not the true Dharma, not the true path. The dharma of pratyekabuddhas should likewise not be studied,' and who further say, 'The dharmas practiced by bodhisattvas should likewise not be studied or listened to by śrāvakas. The dharma of pratyekabuddhas is also thus,' and who further say, 'The words and teachings of bodhisattvas should not be listened to by śrāvakas and pratyekabuddhas. Their mutual words and deeds contradict each other and do not accord with the sūtras.' Such persons cannot faithfully accept the Dharma of true liberation as it really is. Those who rely on such teachings will not even be reborn in heaven, let alone attain liberation!

"Ajita! I now teach the Dharma to convert beings according to their faith, as numerous as the sands of the Ganges.

"Ajita! I now wish to go to the worlds of the ten directions and teach the Dharma according to conditions, benefiting sentient beings. This is not for those who are not truly bodhisattvas yet assume the appearance of bodhisattvas, nor for those who are venomous, deceitful, and of little learning.

"Those in my Dharma who make two divisions, such persons who make two divisions say, 'This is what bodhisattvas should study; this is what they should not study.' They slander the Buddha, Dharma, and Sangha. When such persons' bodies perish and their lives end, they fall into hell for many hundreds of thousands of kalpas, unable to escape. Even if they manage to escape, they are reborn in impoverished families. At a later time, although they receive a prediction of buddhahood, they achieve perfect enlightenment in a world of five turbidities, just as I today, in this world of birth, death, and five turbidities, have become a buddha. Because of these causes and conditions, you should listen carefully and should know: following evil companions, their conduct is like this.

"Ajita! I recall that in the incalculably distant past, there was a buddha named Stainless-Flame-Achievement-of-Merits-Arising-King Tathāgata, Worthy One, of Right and Universal Knowledge, Perfected in Wisdom and Conduct, Well-Gone, Knower of the World, Unsurpassed Noble One, Tamer of Beings, Teacher of Gods and Humans, Buddha, World-Honored One, who appeared in the world. That buddha's lifespan was eighty-thousand nayuta years, and he taught the Dharma for all beings.

"At that time, in the Dharma of the Stainless-Flame-Achievement-of-Merits-Arising-King Tathāgata, there was a bhikṣu named Pure Life. He was a dhāraṇī-master of fourteen billion sūtras and six million Mahāyāna sūtras, serving as a great Dharma-teacher. His words were pure and beautiful, his eloquence was unimpeded, and he benefited immeasurable and boundless sentient beings, instructing, teaching, benefiting, and gladdening them.

"At that time, when the Stainless-Flame-Achievement-of-Merits-Arising-King Tathāgata was approaching nirvāṇa, he addressed the bhikṣu Pure Life, saying: 'In the future age, you should protect and maintain my true Dharma-eye.'

"At that time, Pure Life, having received the Buddha's instruction, for tens of millions of years after the Buddha's nirvāṇa protected and transmitted the secret treasury of all buddhas. Regarding this Vaipulya Dhāraṇī Dharma-gate, he received, upheld, read, and recited it, deeply comprehending its meaning and purport. In the eighty thousand cities of that world, he broadly proclaimed it for all sentient beings according to their aspirations and desires.

"At that time, there was a great city named Bhadra. Going to that city, he taught the Dharma for the eighty billion households according to their desires. At that time, the eighty billion people of the city gained pure faith. One hundred million people abided in the path of bodhi, and seventy-nine billion people, abiding in the śrāvaka vehicle, were tamed and subdued. At that time, the Dharma-teacher Pure Life, together with an assembly of ten thousand bhikṣus accompanying him, went to cultivate the practice of bodhi.

"At that time, in the city of Bhadra there was another bhikṣu named Dharma. He received and upheld a thousand volumes of the Mahāyāna Vaipulya orthodox sūtras and had attained the four dhyānas. He converted the sentient beings of that city solely through the Vaipulya emptiness teaching, unable to teach through skillful means according to their desires. He would say: 'All dharmas are entirely empty and tranquil. What I teach is truly the Buddha's teaching. What the bhikṣu Pure Life teaches is mixed and impure. This bhikṣu is truly not "Pure Life" while being called Pure Life. Why? Because this bhikṣu accepts various flowers but does not use them for offerings, using them for his own enjoyment instead. Aromatic incense and powdered incense are likewise. The bhikṣu Pure Life is foolish and lacks wisdom. He cannot understand that I have long cultivated the pure practice. He is young, has not been ordained long, is arrogant, faithless, and given to much negligence. These people know nothing but consider him a precept-holding bhikṣu called Pure Life.'

"At that time, because Dharma, with his evil mind, slandered one who upheld the Dharma, when his body perished and his life ended, he fell into hell for seventy kalpas and fully experienced all manner of suffering. After seventy kalpas were complete, he fell among animals. After sixty kalpas had passed, he encountered the Buddha Fragrant-Jewel-Radiance, and in that buddha's Dharma he generated the aspiration for bodhi. For ninety thousand lifetimes he was still born among animals. After those ninety thousand lifetimes had passed, he was born among humans. For sixty thousand lifetimes he was born in poverty and lowliness, constantly without a tongue.

"The bhikṣu Pure Life, who gained pure faith in all dharmas and taught the Dharma for others, thereafter encountered sixty-three nayutas of buddhas, always serving as a Dharma-teacher, endowed with the five superknowledges, and requesting those buddhas to turn the wondrous Dharma-wheel.

"Ajita! You should now know: the bhikṣu Pure Life of the past, was he some other person? Do not think otherwise. He is the present Amitābha Buddha. Ajita! You should now know: the bhikṣu Dharma of the past, was he some other person? Do not think otherwise. He is none other than myself. Because I was foolish and ignorant in the past, slandering others, I suffered like this. Because of this karmic cause, I achieved perfect enlightenment in a world of five turbidities.

"Therefore, Ajita, if there are bodhisattvas who make two divisions in the dharmas, because of these causes and conditions, they achieve buddhahood in a world of five turbidities. In their buddha-lands there are various māras who constantly create obstacles when the Dharma is being taught."

At that time, the great assembly, hearing the Buddha's teaching, all wept sorrowfully, tears streaming down their faces, and together declared: "May we never make two divisions in the Buddha-Dharma, as did the bhikṣu Dharma!"

At that time, in the assembly, one hundred bodhisattvas immediately rose from their seats, placed their right knees on the ground, and wept aloud with tears.

At that time, the World-Honored One, knowing the reason, deliberately asked those bodhisattvas: "Good men! Why do you weep aloud like this?"

At that time, the bodhisattvas, with a single voice in unison, addressed the Buddha, saying: "World-Honored One! We each examine ourselves and see that we too should have such evil karmic obstructions."

At that time, the World-Honored One said: "It is so, it is so! In the past, in the time of Dīpaṃkara Buddha, you also left the household life in his Dharma to cultivate the path. After Dīpaṃkara Buddha's nirvāṇa, there was a bhikṣu named Accumulated Wisdom. At that time, you all slandered this bhikṣu. Since then, you have not been able to see the Buddha, have not been able to generate the aspiration for bodhi, have not obtained dhāraṇī or any samādhi. Later, together with you all, I simultaneously abided in the path of bodhi. Good men! In the final era of this Bhadra-kalpa, at the time of the last buddha, you will attain the patient acceptance of the non-arising of dharmas. Thereafter, after three incalculable kalpas of practicing the bodhisattva-path, you will attain anuttara-samyak-saṃbodhi.

"Therefore, good men, when a bodhisattva sees another bodhisattva, one should not generate thoughts of discrimination. One should regard them as a stūpa, as seeing the Buddha. Therefore, when a bodhisattva sees another bodhisattva, do not generate different thoughts or think of them as non-buddha. If one generates different thoughts, this is self-deception. One should uphold this and not generate different thoughts, harmonizing with each other: 'If I now look at an initial-aspiration bodhisattva and do not regard them as a buddha, then I am deceiving all the buddhas presently existing in immeasurable, countless worlds throughout the ten directions.'

"Therefore, good men, in the future, in the world of five turbidities, those bodhisattvas who attain dhāraṇī and samādhi do so entirely through the Buddha's spiritual power. Therefore, good men, if one slanders a Dharma-teacher, this is the same as slandering the Buddha, without any difference.

"Good men! After the Buddha's nirvāṇa, if there is a Dharma-teacher who skillfully follows beings' desires and teaches the Dharma for others, enabling bodhisattvas studying the Mahāyāna and the great assembly to generate even a hair's breadth of joyful mind, or to shed even a single drop of tears, one should know that this is entirely through the Buddha's spiritual power.

"If there are foolish people who are not truly bodhisattvas but falsely call themselves bodhisattvas, who slander true bodhisattvas and their practices, and who further say, 'What does that person know? What does that person understand?' Maitreya! I recall that in the past, when I was in Jambudvīpa practicing the bodhisattva-path, because I cherished the Dharma, for the sake of a single sentence or a single verse, I abandoned my beloved head, eyes, wife, and children, and even renounced my throne. Why? Because I sought the Dharma. Yet those foolish people, solely for the sake of fame and attachment to profit and offerings, relying on their own small abilities, do not go to the Tathāgata's Dharma-transmitter to listen to and receive the true Dharma.

"Maitreya! If both sides harmonize, then they can maintain and transmit my Dharma. If both sides contend, then the true Dharma will not spread. Ajita! You should observe how these slanderers of the Dharma accomplish such extremely grave offenses, falling into the three evil destinies and finding it difficult to escape.

"Furthermore, Maitreya, when I first attained buddhahood, with wondrous wisdom I broadly proclaimed the true Dharma for sentient beings.

"If there are foolish people who do not believe and accept what the Buddha has taught, like the bhikṣu Dharma who, although he read and recited a thousand volumes of Mahāyāna sūtras and explained them for others and attained the four dhyānas, because he slandered others, suffered great anguish for seventy kalpas in hell. How much worse for those who are foolish, ignorant, and inferior, who truly know nothing, yet say, 'I am a Dharma-teacher who clearly understands the Mahāyāna and can broadly propagate it,' slandering the true Dharma-teacher, saying he lacks understanding, and also slandering the Buddha-Dharma while being self-aggrandizing.

"If those foolish people slander even a single four-line verse of the Buddha's Mahāyāna, one should know that this karma certainly leads to hell. Why? Because they defame the Buddha-Dharma and the Dharma-teacher. Because of these causes and conditions, they constantly dwell in evil destinies, never seeing the Buddha. Because they have slandered the Buddha, Dharma, and Sangha, they are also able to create obstacles for those who have initially generated the aspiration for bodhi, causing them to retreat from the true path. One should know that such persons adorn themselves with great offenses. For immeasurable kalpas, their bodies fall into hell to receive great suffering as retribution. Because they look upon those who generate the aspiration for bodhi with evil eyes, they receive the retribution of having no eyes. Because they slander those who generate the aspiration for bodhi with evil mouths, they receive the retribution of having no tongue.

"Ajita! I see no other single evil dharma that can exceed the gravity of the offense of destroying the aspiration for bodhi. Because of this offense, one falls into evil destinies. How much worse is slandering other bodhisattvas!

"If there are bodhisattvas who can teach sentient beings as things truly are, who do not give rise to nihilism or eternalism, who do not say that sentient beings definitely exist or definitely do not exist, and who also do not stubbornly cling to the existence or non-existence of dharmas: Ajita, those studying the bodhisattva-path should abide in this way. Those who abide in this way possess the pure wholesome karma of all bodhisattvas. Whatever they cultivate and accumulate is entirely without attachment. If there are sentient beings who give rise to attachment, one should know that such persons are born in a world of five turbidities.

"Furthermore, there are bodhisattvas who skillfully follow beings' faculties and desires, able to teach the Dharma in various ways for sentient beings. Ajita! A bodhisattva who thus completely cultivates the six pāramitās can accomplish unsurpassed bodhi.

"Those foolish people trust their own convictions and say, 'Bodhisattvas should study only the Prajñāpāramitā; do not study the other pāramitās, for the Prajñāpāramitā is the most supreme.' Such speech does not accord with principle. Why? Ajita! In the past, when King Kāśika was practicing the bodhisattva-path, he abandoned his beloved body, head, eyes, marrow, and brain. Did that king at that time lack wisdom?"

Maitreya addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! Truly as the Sage says: he truly possessed wisdom."

The Buddha addressed Ajita: "From the past through immeasurable time, I have completely cultivated the six pāramitās. Without completely cultivating the six pāramitās, one can never attain unsurpassed bodhi."

"It is so, World-Honored One!"

The Buddha addressed Ajita: "As you have said. In the past, for sixty kalpas I practiced the Dāna-pāramitā, the Śīla-pāramitā, the Kṣānti-pāramitā, the Vīrya-pāramitā, the Dhyāna-pāramitā, and the Prajñā-pāramitā, each for sixty kalpas.

"Those foolish people falsely say, 'One can attain bodhi by cultivating only the single Prajñā-pāramitā.' This is impossible. They harbor the view of emptiness and therefore make such impure teachings. Those who speak thus have body, speech, and mind karma that contradicts the Dharma. Although they understand the emptiness teaching and proclaim it for others, they do not practice as they teach regarding emptiness. Because they are without practice, they are far from the meaning of emptiness. Their minds harbor jealousy and they are deeply attached to profit and offerings, exceeding even relatives.

"Ajita! In former lives I served as a wheel-turning king, renouncing precious jewels, head, eyes, hands, and feet. Still I did not attain unsurpassed bodhi. How much less those foolish people who, for the sake of food and drink, go about to others' homes, proclaiming only the emptiness teaching, saying that what they teach is the path to bodhi, is the bodhisattva practice, that only this Dharma is correct and all else is wrong! They further say, 'What I understand, immeasurable Dharma-teachers all corroborate.' They seek fame and praise their own abilities while being jealous and resentful of those with clear understanding.

"Ajita! I see their minds seeking profit and offerings to sustain their livelihoods. Although they may have some good practices, even after a hundred kalpas they cannot attain even a small mind of dharma-patience. How then could they accomplish unsurpassed bodhi?

"Ajita! I do not teach bodhi to those whose minds and mouths are contradictory and who deceive others. I do not teach bodhi to those who are jealous. I do not teach bodhi to the arrogant and disrespectful. I do not teach bodhi to the faithless. I do not teach bodhi to the untamed. I do not teach bodhi to the sexually immoral. I do not teach bodhi to those who affirm themselves and negate others.

"Ajita! Because of their arrogance, those foolish people consider themselves superior to the Buddha, slandering the Mahāyāna sūtras taught by the Buddha, saying these are the teachings of the Hīnayāna śrāvakas."

At that time, the Buddha addressed the Elder Subhūti: "One should not teach the Prajñāpāramitā to people of dualistic views."

Subhūti addressed the Buddha: "Yes, World-Honored One! As the Buddha has taught."

The Buddha said: "It is so, Subhūti! Giving with a mind of non-attachment: this is called bodhi."

Subhūti said: "It is so, World-Honored One!"

The Buddha said: "Subhūti! Giving without self-praise or disparagement of others: this is called bodhi."

Subhūti said: "It is so, World-Honored One!"

The Buddha said: "Subhūti! Observe how foolish people give rise to the view of self and ego, without shame or remorse, loving their relatives, greedily seeking to sustain their lives, delighting in receiving others' offerings. One should know that such persons create only evil karma.

"Furthermore, Ajita! A bodhisattva should not generate fear regarding any dharma, any bodhisattva dharma, any pratyekabuddha dharma, any śrāvaka dharma, any ordinary being's dharma, any affliction dharma, any dharma of exhaustion, difficult diligence, what is and what is not, action and non-action, fear and non-fear, existence and non-existence, mind and non-mind, enlightenment and non-awakening, karma and non-karma, good and not-good, peace and non-peace, liberation and non-liberation, cultivation and non-cultivation, dharma and non-dharma, stillness and disturbance, provisional and real, faith and non-faith, wholesome thought and unwholesome thought, abiding and non-abiding. Thus, a bodhisattva should generate no fear regarding any dharma whatsoever.

"Ajita! Because I cultivated such fearless dharmas in the past, I attained right enlightenment, able to know the mental realms of all sentient beings. Yet regarding what I know, I do not give rise to the mark of knowing. Based on what I have realized, I expound according to the capacity of beings, enabling all bodhisattvas who hear the Dharma to obtain the Radiance Dhāraṇī Seal. Because they obtain the Dharma-seal, they are forever non-retrogressing. If one does not truly know this Dharma and says there is no skillful mastery, one can never accomplish unsurpassed bodhi.

"Ajita! When I teach this Dharma for the sentient beings of the four continents, these sentient beings, through the Buddha's spiritual power, each see Śākyamuni Tathāgata teaching the Dharma for them. In this manner, up to the Akaniṣṭha heaven, those sentient beings also think: 'The Tathāgata solely teaches for me.' As in one set of four continents, so throughout the trichiliocosm it is likewise: all these sentient beings think, 'Śākyamuni Tathāgata was born solely in our land and turns the great Dharma-wheel only for us.'

"Ajita! Through such great skillful means, in immeasurable, boundless worlds, constantly each morning I universally observe the sentient beings who should be converted and teach the Dharma for them. At noon and evening, I constantly with the Dharma-eye equally observe sentient beings and teach all dharmas for them in those worlds. Such immeasurable buddha-realms and all sentient beings: those studying the bodhisattva-path should cultivate in this way.

"If those foolish people slander the true Dharma taught by the Buddha and stubbornly cling to their own understanding as true: those who slander the Dharma do not believe the Buddha. Through this evil karma, they fall into hell, fully experiencing all manner of suffering, and never hear the Dharma.

"Furthermore, Ajita! You should receive and uphold the Tathāgata's esoteric teaching, and through skillful means broadly explain it for others."

At that time, the youth Mañjuśrī, the Bodhisattva Merit-Light-Equality, the Bodhisattva Without-Doubt-or-Confusion, the Bodhisattva Determined-Aspiration, the Bodhisattva Wondrous-Mind-Opening-Intention, the Bodhisattva Radiance, the Bodhisattva Joy-King, the Bodhisattva Fearless, the Bodhisattva Mind-Reaching-Boundless-Buddha-Lands, the Bodhisattva Avalokiteśvara, the Bodhisattva Fragrant-Elephant, the Bodhisattva Extinguishing-All-Evil-Karma, the Bodhisattva Abiding-in-Concentration, the Bodhisattva Hundred-Thousand-Merit-Adornment, the Bodhisattva Wondrous-Sound-Heard-Afar, the Bodhisattva All-Wisdom-Unforgetting, the Bodhisattva Great-Fame-Distant-Trembling-Jewel-Banner-Adornment, the Bodhisattva Seeking-All-Dharmas, the Bodhisattva Abiding-in-Buddha-Realm, the Bodhisattva Moonlight-Adornment, and the Bodhisattva All-Worlds-Great-Assembly-Adornment, these bodhisattva-mahāsattvas addressed the Buddha, saying: "World-Honored One! It is so, it is so! Truly as the Sage teaches. We traveled eastward past sixty Ganges-sands of buddha-lands, respectfully prostrating to all the buddhas. In each buddha-land, we saw only Śākyamuni Tathāgata appearing in the world. Over seven days, we traveled throughout the ten directions and also saw Śākyamuni Tathāgata appearing in the world, and beheld no other buddha. Having traveled everywhere, we returned to our original land to listen to and receive the true Dharma."

At that time, the Buddha addressed the youth Mañjuśrī: "You should now carefully observe: the Tathāgata's wisdom is inconceivable; the Tathāgata's realm is also inconceivable. Such unequaled equality is the Tathāgata's Dharma. Yet those foolish people say, 'Only the single Prajñāpāramitā is the Tathāgata's practice, the bodhisattva's practice, the practice of ambrosia.'"

The Buddha addressed Mañjuśrī: "Those who speak thus contradict the Dharma. Why? The bodhisattva's practice-dharma is extremely difficult to accomplish fully. The practice of non-attachment is the bodhisattva's practice. The practice of no-self and no-ego is the bodhisattva's practice. The practice of emptiness is the bodhisattva's practice. The practice of signlessness is the bodhisattva's practice.

"Mañjuśrī! Such practices are the bodhisattva's practices. Those studying the bodhisattva-path should receive and uphold them thus. If those foolish people harbor deviant views, one should know that such persons do not understand my Dharma.

"Mañjuśrī! All you bodhisattvas should guard your body and speech. Do not allow negligence regarding unwholesome dharmas. Strengthen your minds so they do not retrogress. Teach the Dharma completely for all sentient beings, and you yourselves should also abide in the Dharma.

"Since the distant past of incalculable kalpas, I have fully accomplished unsurpassed bodhi, and through skillful means broadly teach for others, enabling sentient beings to leave behind evil destinies.

"Mañjuśrī! If there are foolish people who slander the wondrous Dharma, this is slandering the Buddha and also slandering the Sangha.

"Again saying, 'This dharma is right; that dharma is wrong': such speech also constitutes slandering the Dharma.

"Saying, 'This dharma was taught for bodhisattvas; this dharma was taught for śrāvakas': such speech also constitutes slandering the Dharma.

"Saying, 'This is what bodhisattvas should study; this is not what bodhisattvas should study': such speech also constitutes slandering the Dharma.

"Again saying, 'Past buddhas have already passed away, future buddhas have not yet come, the present buddha has no fixed abode, and only I have obtained the dhāraṇī Dharma': such speech also constitutes slandering the Dharma. Because of slandering the Dharma, to claim one has obtained dhāraṇī is an impure dharma.

"Defaming a true Dharma-teacher's cultivation; slandering a Dharma-teacher's understanding as not matching their practice; slandering a Dharma-teacher's conduct as deviating from the path; slandering a Dharma-teacher's body as not upholding precepts; slandering a Dharma-teacher's mind as lacking wisdom; slandering a Dharma-teacher's intention as lacking clear understanding; slandering a Dharma-teacher's words as lacking eloquent clarity; having no faith in the Tathāgata's written teachings; again saying, 'This sūtra is right, this sūtra is wrong; this verse-text is right, this verse-text is wrong; this dharma is believable, this dharma is not believable'; seeing someone teaching correctly and making contrary arguments; creating hindrances for those listening to the true Dharma; saying 'this is practice, this is not practice; this is accomplished, this is not accomplished; this is the right time, this is not the right time': all such speech constitutes slandering the Dharma.

"Furthermore, Mañjuśrī, whether śrāvakas teach the Dharma or bodhisattvas teach the Dharma, one should know that all of it is through the Tathāgata's spiritual power of protection and mindfulness, which causes the bodhisattvas and others to speak in this way.

"Mañjuśrī! Those foolish people generate slander even while the Buddha is present in the world. How much more will the Dharma-teachers who receive and uphold my Dharma after my nirvāṇa be slandered! Why? Because they are Māra's retinue. One should know that such persons fall into evil destinies.

"Like those foolish people who greedily seek profit and offerings to sustain their relatives, having no faith in the Tathāgata's Dharma, they further destroy the Tathāgata's teaching. Such people's relatives, with partisan minds, go to the homes of brahmins and elders, speaking thus in praise of that foolish person: 'In the Dharma and its meaning, he can know and understand, clearly perceiving beings' faculties and desires, and skillfully teaching for others.' They accept others' faithful offerings without any shame. Because they slander the Dharma, both they and their retinues fall into hell.

"Mañjuśrī! I never teach the bodhisattva practice for the faithless. Nor do I teach the pure Dharma for those attached to household life. Nor do I teach the Dharma of liberation for those of dualistic views. Nor do I teach the Dharma of escaping suffering for those of one-sided views. Nor do I teach the truly pure Dharma for those who delight in worldly things.

"Mañjuśrī! I teach with a mind of non-attachment through Ganges-sands of dharma-gates for others. And I also teach with a mind of attachment through Ganges-sands of dharma-gates for sentient beings.

"For beings who delight in emptiness, I teach the emptiness dharma. For beings who delight in wisdom, I teach the wisdom dharma. For beings who delight in signlessness, I teach the signless dharma. For beings who delight in signs, I teach the dharma of signs. For beings who delight in compassion, I teach the compassion dharma. For beings who delight in causes and conditions, I teach the dharma of causes and conditions. For beings who delight in the absence of causes and conditions, I teach the dharma of no causes and conditions.

"'This is the dharma of proper deportment; this is the dharma of improper deportment. This is the emptiness dharma; this is the existence dharma. This is the conditioned dharma; this is the unconditioned dharma. This is the dharma of gathering; this is the dharma of covering. This is the dharma of ordinary persons; this is the dharma of noble ones. This is the form dharma; this is the unwholesome dharma. This is the dharma of the foolish; this is the dharma of concentration.'"

The Buddha addressed Mañjuśrī: "All such dharmas belong to the path of the Prajñāpāramitā. Those foolish people, in all they say, do not rely on the Tathāgata's true and pure teaching, and slander the Buddha's true Dharma."

At that time, the youth Mañjuśrī addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! As the Buddha has taught, such foolish people, because they are close to evil companions, generate slander in this present life. World-Honored One, by what causes and conditions can they be freed from this fault?"

The Buddha addressed Mañjuśrī: "In former lives, for seven years, six times daily, day and night, I confessed the grave offenses of body, speech, and mind. After this, I finally became pure. After ten kalpas had passed, I attained the patient acceptance of dharmas.

"Mañjuśrī! You should know that this sūtra is the bodhisattva vehicle. Those who have not yet awakened, it can cause to awaken. If upon hearing this sūtra they do not believe and accept it, because of this slander they fall into evil destinies. These bodhisattvas should clearly receive my Dharma, and only afterward may they proclaim it for others. Receiving and upholding it in this way enables one to stay far from evil destinies."

The Buddha addressed Mañjuśrī: "There are four dharmas of equality that bodhisattvas should study. What are the four? First, a bodhisattva is equal toward all sentient beings. Second, equal toward all dharmas. Third, equal toward bodhi. Fourth, equal in teaching the Dharma. Such are the four.

"Bodhisattvas should know these four dharmas. Having known them, they teach them for sentient beings. Those who have faith leave behind evil destinies. Those who lack faith fall into evil destinies. If good men and good women abide in these four dharmas, one should know that such persons do not fall into evil destinies.

"There are four more dharmas. What are the four? First, toward all sentient beings, one's mind does not retrogress. Second, one does not look down upon Dharma-teachers. Third, toward those of wisdom, one's mind does not generate slander. Fourth, toward all that the tathāgatas have taught, one constantly generates reverence. These are the four dharmas. If good men and good women can skillfully study them, they will never fall into evil destinies.

"Furthermore, Mañjuśrī! If a bodhisattva fills Ganges-sands of buddha-lands with the seven treasures and for Ganges-sands of kalpas daily offers them to Ganges-sands of buddhas and world-honored ones, and if there is a good man or good woman who, regarding this Mahāyāna Vaipulya wondrous sūtra, reads and recites even a single sentence or a single verse three times, the merit obtained surpasses the merit of the former offering. If there is someone who recites and upholds this entire sūtra, the merit obtained is far greater still. Even if there were a person who cultivated the six pāramitās of generosity, moral discipline, patience, diligence, meditative concentration, and wisdom, the merit obtained would still not equal it.

"Mañjuśrī! This sūtra's name and meaning are vast and great, without equal. All you bodhisattva-mahāsattvas should skillfully study, receive, uphold, read, and recite it, and broadly analyze and explain it for sentient beings."

At that time, the entire great assembly, including all the bodhisattva-mahāsattvas who had come from the ten directions, together addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! It is so, it is so! As the Buddha has taught, we receive and uphold it."

When this Dharma was taught, thirty Ganges-sands of bodhisattvas attained the patient acceptance of the non-arising of dharmas. Seventy Ganges-sands of bodhisattvas attained non-retrogression from anuttara-samyak-saṃbodhi.

Furthermore, sixty-three billion hundreds of thousands of nayutas of the entire great assembly of trichiliocosms, hearing what the Buddha taught, generated joyful minds. Over eighty kalpas they crossed the stream of birth and death, and further attained non-retrogression from anuttara-samyak-saṃbodhi. After sixty-three kalpas, they fully accomplished unsurpassed bodhi.

The bodhisattvas, the entire great assembly of devas, dragons, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, humans and non-humans, hearing what the Buddha taught, were all filled with great joy, made obeisance, and put it into practice.
