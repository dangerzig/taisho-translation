# 無字寶篋經
## Sūtra of the Precious Casket Without Letters

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0828

Translated from the Chinese. Northern Wei dynasty, translated by Bodhiruci (菩提流支)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Bhagavān was in Rājagṛha, on Mount Gṛdhrakūṭa. At that time, countless hundreds of thousands of billions of great bodhisattvas surrounded the Tathāgata. All were of great wisdom, skilled in skillful means, courageous and diligent. They had well penetrated the Dharma-gate without letters and well purified what is the case and what is not. Covered by the net of conscience, modesty, and liberation, they had tamed all their faculties. With the fangs of compassion and the fangs of conscience and modesty, they cherished sentient beings. Having attained great samādhi, with wisdom as their leader, they reverently served the mother of wisdom. Like a great island of jewels, a treasury of great treasures, they fully knew wholesome and unwholesome dharmas and awakened to the affairs of the three times, all perfectly accomplished. Possessing eloquence without letters, they well penetrated the twofold emptiness and attained the supreme, wondrous ground. Well-learned in all truths, they penetrated the ultimate reality. Of boundless heroic strength, with no attachment, they well penetrated the dharmas of the supreme intrinsic nature. They could skillfully contemplate the two Dharma-gates of what is real and what is not real. Manifesting birth in the womb, they had forever left birth-and-death behind. Well awakened to the secret and well knowing all marks, they were fit to guard all lands and had attained great renown. All had further attained the supreme name and the supreme treasury. Having attained the treasury without words, they rested in peace, forever at ease. All their deeds were entirely wholesome and joyful. Their names were heard everywhere, transcending the three realms. They could rescue sentient beings dwelling in the three realms. Well awakened to suchness, they universally manifested Samantabhadra's practice. Manifesting their bodies everywhere, their faculties were pure. Well able to awaken to their own bodies and others' bodies, they had all accomplished keen and sharp wisdom. Their names were: the Bodhisattva Superior Sound, the Bodhisattva Dharma Sound, the Bodhisattva Surpassing All Divisions, the Bodhisattva Dharma Eye, the Bodhisattva Thousand Marks, the Bodhisattva Accumulation of Eloquence, the Bodhisattva Superior Thought, the Bodhisattva Earth-Tamer, the Bodhisattva Tamer of Earth's Border, the Bodhisattva Sound of Deep Entry into Earth's Border, the Bodhisattva Earth Sound, the Bodhisattva Possessed of All Eloquence, the Bodhisattva Supreme Accumulation, the Bodhisattva Lotus Eye, the Bodhisattva Utpala Eye, the Bodhisattva Crown-Topknot, and the Bodhisattva Mañjuśrī Sound. These and other bodhisattva mahāsattvas, innumerable, all with the appearance of youths, had come from worlds in other directions, as numerous as the sands of an unobservable number of Ganges rivers. All abided in the position of crown prince, ready to receive the office of Dharma-king.

The Bodhisattva Superior Thought was surrounded by countless hosts of Śakra and Brahmā. The Bodhisattva Samantabhadra, the Bodhisattva Ākāśagarbha, and others were surrounded by the four great divine kings and countless wheel-turning kings. The Bodhisattva Mahāsthāmaprāpta and the Bodhisattva Avalokiteśvara were surrounded by countless hosts of Brahmā. The Bodhisattva Amoghadarśin was surrounded by countless Vaiśravaṇa kings. The Bodhisattva Star-King was surrounded by countless stars and other world-guardians. There were also the Bodhisattva Doubt-Destroyer and the Bodhisattva Remover-of-All-Obstacles, whose own bodies manifested as the bodies of Tathāgatas, surrounded by countless Buddhas. The Elders Śāriputra, Mahāmaudgalyāyana, and Mahākāśyapa, and others, all great arhats, were present. The Bodhisattva Refined-Training and the Bodhisattva Superior Thought were surrounded by countless celestial women. The Bodhisattva Medicine-King and the Bodhisattva Supreme-Medicine were each surrounded by countless retinues.

From all the worlds of the ten directions, as numerous as the sands of the Ganges, all the devas of the sun and moon, who prided themselves on their majestic virtue and harbored conceit, all came to the Buddha's place. Having arrived, they stood to one side before the Tathāgata, and then they saw that their own radiance could no longer be seen, as if a heap of ink were set beside Jambudvīpa gold. These devas of the sun and moon, standing before the Tathāgata, could not display themselves; it was likewise with all their radiance. They had no wish to remain, nor any wish to speak; their majestic virtue could not be displayed.

Nārāyaṇa and others were surrounded by countless hosts of devas. The great divine Nāga Kings Takṣaka and Anavatapta and other great nāga kings were surrounded by countless nāgas. The Gandharva King Beautiful Sound was surrounded by countless billions of gandharvas. The Garuḍa King Insatiable was also surrounded by seven billion garuḍas, all coming to the Buddha's place.

At that time, throughout this great trichiliocosm, from worlds as numerous as the sands of the Ganges, all the bodhisattvas in each place made requests to their Buddhas. Having made their requests, they came to the Sahā world, surrounded by the fourfold assembly, bringing various offerings surpassing anything in the world. They came to the Buddha's place and, having made their offerings, each sat upon a lotus-flower seat.

At that time there was a bodhisattva mahāsattva named Superior Thought, who came to the Buddha's place and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! If the World-Honored One will permit me, I dare to ask the Tathāgata about the meaning of two words."

At that time the Buddha told the Bodhisattva Superior Thought: "You may ask as you please. The Tathāgata did not attain the path in this place for the sake of a single sentient being, but appeared here for you and all such great dragon-elephants."

Thereupon the Bodhisattva Superior Thought addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! What is the one dharma that bodhisattvas should eliminate? What is the one dharma that the Tathāgata has awakened to?"

At that time the World-Honored One praised the Bodhisattva Mahāsattva Superior Thought: "Excellent, excellent! Brahmin! You have thoroughly ripened immeasurable good roots, and all the Buddhas empower you to ask about the meaning of this phrase. Good man! Listen carefully, reflect well upon it, and I shall explain it for you."

At that time the Bodhisattva Superior Thought bowed at the Tathāgata's feet and reverently received the teaching.

The Buddha said: "Good man! There is one kind of dharma that bodhisattvas should eliminate, namely desire. Good man! This is the one dharma that should be forever eliminated. Good man! There is another dharma that bodhisattvas should eliminate, namely anger. Good man! This is the one dharma that should be forever eliminated. Good man! There is another dharma that bodhisattvas should eliminate, namely delusion. Good man! This is the one dharma that should be forever eliminated. Good man! There is another dharma that bodhisattvas should eliminate, namely the view of a self. Good man! This is the one dharma that should be forever eliminated. Good man! There is another dharma that bodhisattvas should eliminate, namely laziness. Good man! This is the one dharma that should be forever eliminated. Good man! There is another dharma that bodhisattvas should eliminate, namely torpor and drowsiness. Good man! This is the one dharma that should be forever eliminated. Good man! There is another dharma that bodhisattvas should eliminate, namely craving and attachment. Good man! This is the one dharma that should be forever eliminated. Good man! There is yet another dharma that bodhisattvas should eliminate, namely ignorance. Good man! This is the one dharma that should be forever eliminated."

At that time the Brahmin Superior Thought addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! What is the one dharma that all bodhisattvas should protect and guard day and night?"

At that time the World-Honored One told the Bodhisattva Superior Thought: "Good man! It is this: as a bodhisattva, what you yourself do not desire, do not urge upon others. Good man! If there are good men and good women who protect and uphold this dharma, those good men and good women thereby uphold all the precept-treasuries of all the Tathāgatas. Why? Because one who cherishes one's own life will not kill living beings. One who values one's own wealth will not steal from others. One who treasures one's own spouse will not violate another's. Good man! These sentient beings generate this intention and say: 'I respectfully follow the Tathāgata's right teaching.' That good man should always diligently guard and protect this dharma in his mind. For what reason? Good man! If there are good men and good women who seek the unsurpassed, right, and perfect great bodhi, those persons all seek it for the sake of happiness, and there is no one who seeks suffering for themselves. In the world, there are only those who delight in receiving happiness. For this reason I teach: you should know that what you yourself do not desire, you should not urge upon others. Good man! This is the one dharma that bodhisattvas should constantly protect day and night.

"Good man! As you asked: 'What is the one dharma that the Tathāgata has awakened to?' Good man! There is not a single dharma that the Tathāgata has awakened to. Good man! Having no dharma as an object of awakening is the Tathāgata's awakening. Good man! The non-arising of all dharmas is the Tathāgata's awakening. The non-ceasing of all dharmas is the Tathāgata's awakening.

"Furthermore, good man! The departure of the dharma-nature from the two extremes is the Tathāgata's awakening. The non-reality of all dharmas is the Tathāgata's awakening. Good man! The Tathāgata well teaches karmic causes and conditions. The Tathāgata well awakens to karmic causes and conditions. That all dharmas are bound by causes and conditions is what the Tathāgata has well awakened to. Good man! Those causes and conditions are like a flash of lightning, and the Tathāgata's awakening is apart from causes and apart from conditions. The Tathāgata has said that there is no karmic retribution that, after having attained right awakening, one must undergo. The vast adornment treasury of all dharmas is what the Tathāgata has awakened to. Good man! For what reason is it called the 'vast adornment treasury'? Good man! All worldly and world-transcending wisdom: from what does it arise? When that wisdom, through true observation, rightly contemplates, prajñā-pāramitā becomes exceedingly profound. At that time, that dharma comes to be called a 'treasury.'

"Good man! I also teach that all dharmas are like an illusion, like a mirage: this is the Buddha's awakening. The one-taste liberation that is the nature of marks is the Buddha's awakening. All that pertains to the one-taste liberation of the nature of marks is the vast adornment treasury of all dharmas.

"Good man! If good men and good women ask further, there is one dharma that the Buddha has awakened to. Good man! It is this: all dharmas neither go nor come, are neither cause nor condition, neither arise nor cease, are neither thought nor non-thought, and neither increase nor decrease. Good man! The intrinsic nature of all dharmas, which is the ultimate dharma-nature, is not an intrinsic nature. It cannot be expressed through metaphor, nor can words and phrases discuss it. This is the one dharma that all the Tathāgatas have awakened to."

When this Dharma-gate of the Adorned-King Vast Adornment Treasury, the Precious Casket Without Letters, was taught, sentient beings in number equal to the dust-motes seen by bodhisattvas of the tenth ground, whose eyes had not beheld it, all gave rise to the aspiration for anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi. Again, sentient beings equal in number attained the fruit of the arhat. Furthermore, beyond this number, sentient beings were freed from the suffering of the hells and born among devas and humans. Countless bodhisattvas manifested hundreds of thousands of myriads of samādhi-gates. How much more could be said that would be of further benefit?

At that time the Buddha told Rāhula: "Good man! Are you able to receive and uphold this essential right Dharma that I have taught?"

When he spoke these words, by the Buddha's spiritual power, from worlds as numerous as the sands of the Ganges, ninety billion bodhisattva mahāsattvas rose from their seats and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! We are all able to uphold this Dharma-gate. In the future age in this Sahā world, when we see any who will circulate and teach it, we shall do so for those whom we know to be vessels of wisdom."

At that time the four great divine kings addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! We too are able to receive and uphold the essential Dharma taught by the Tathāgata, and we shall cause the wishes of those bodhisattvas to be fulfilled, provided they are vessels of wisdom."

At that time the World-Honored One surveyed the entire assembly and said: "Good men! It was not through cultivating meager good roots that I attained right awakening. Those sentient beings who are able to hear this right Dharma have likewise not cultivated meager good roots. If they can receive and uphold this Dharma-gate of the Adorned-King Vast Adornment Treasury, the Precious Casket Without Letters, or if they can hear it, those persons have thereby honored me and revered me, respected and praised me. Good men! Such good men and good women carry bodhi upon their two shoulders. Those persons will attain unobstructed eloquence and will purify all the Buddha-lands. At the time of death, they will see Amitābha Buddha, surrounded by a great assembly of śrāvakas and bodhisattvas, standing before them. They will also see my body here on the peak of Mount Gṛdhrakūṭa and see this assembly of bodhisattvas. Good men! Those good men and good women have thereby obtained the great, inexhaustible Dharma-treasury. They have attained the knowledge of past lives. They will not be born in the evil destinies.

"Good men! I now teach this Dharma-gate that all the world finds difficult to believe. Good men! If those good men and good women, even if they have committed the crimes of immediate retribution, through their ability to read, recite, receive, and uphold this supreme Dharma-gate, or if they themselves write it, or urge others to write it, or themselves read and recite it, or urge others to read and recite it, or themselves uphold it, or urge others to uphold it: good men! I declare that those persons will not see the evil destinies, and they are recorded by all the Buddhas. Those bodhisattvas will all attain the five eyes, their faculties will be complete, they will be guarded and remembered by all the Buddhas, and they will be embraced and received by all the bodhisattvas. Immeasurable afflictions and karmic obstructions will be eliminated, and they will attain purity.

"Good men! This that I have spoken is my final utterance. From the time I attained the path and right awakening until now, I have not spoken this word."

When the Buddha had finished teaching this sūtra, Superior Thought and the other great bodhisattvas, Śakra and the other devas, the four great kings, humans, devas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, gandharvas, and all the world, having heard what the Buddha taught, all rejoiced greatly.
