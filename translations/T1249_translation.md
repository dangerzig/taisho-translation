# 毘沙門儀軌
## Vaiśravaṇa Ritual Manual

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1249

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

**Method for Constructing the Altar**

Plaster the ground with cow dung, then apply fragrant mud on top. The outer border measures two cubits square, and the inner border measures one cubit square. The image is placed on the north side facing south. Within the inner precinct, place clean water, fragrant flowers, and fruits. Within the outer precinct, place milk porridge, cakes, and similar offerings.

The mantra for incense offerings:

*Maṇḍa-artha-paripūrakāya svāhā*

Blend frankincense, sandalwood, camphor, deodar incense, and olibanum, pound them, and mix with honey. Before the image, recite this spell one hundred and eight times. Hold the incense in the right hand with utmost sincerity and offer it before the image. When the count is complete, present the offering. If incense is unavailable, reciting this mantra at the proper time also constitutes making offerings.

**Historical Account of the Northern Vaiśravaṇa, Great Heavenly King**

In the first year of the Tianbao era of the Tang dynasty, the *renwu* year [742 CE], the great Shi and five Kang kingdoms besieged the city of Anxi. On the eleventh day of the second month of that year, a memorial arrived requesting military reinforcement. The Sage Emperor told the Chan Master Yixing: "Reverend! Anxi has been besieged by the Kang kingdoms, and a memorial has arrived requesting troops. Anxi is twelve thousand *li* from the capital, and the march takes eight months. By the time they arrive, Anxi will no longer be under my dominion." Yixing said: "Why does Your Majesty not request the divine soldiers of the Northern Vaiśravaṇa, Heavenly King, for reinforcement?" The Emperor said: "How can I make such a request?" Yixing said: "Summon the foreign monk Mahāprajñā [Amoghavajra], and he can make the request." By imperial decree, Mahāprajñā was summoned to the inner court. He said: "Has the Sage summoned this monastic minister because Anxi has been besieged by the five kingdoms?" The Emperor said: "Indeed." Mahāprajñā said: "Let Your Majesty take up the incense burner and enter the ritual hall, and I shall request the divine soldiers of the Northern Heavenly King to rescue Anxi."

The Emperor urgently entered the ritual hall and made the request. Before the mantra had been recited twice-seven times, the Emperor suddenly saw two or three hundred divine figures in armor standing before the ritual hall. The Emperor asked the monk: "Who are these?" Mahāprajñā said: "This is Dujian, the second son of the Northern Vaiśravaṇa, Heavenly King, leading celestial soldiers to rescue Anxi. He has come to take his leave." The Emperor provided food and dispatched them.

In the fourth month of that year, a memorial arrived from Anxi stating: "After the hour of *si* and before the hour of *wu* on the eleventh day of the second month, thirty *li* to the northeast of the city, clouds and mist darkened the sky. Within the mist there appeared figures, each about one *zhang* tall, approximately three to five hundred of them, all wearing golden armor. After the hour of *you*, drums and horns sounded mightily, their sound shaking three hundred *li*, the earth trembling and mountains collapsing, and this lasted three days. The five kingdoms were greatly terrified and all withdrew their armies and pulled back their troops. Among the enemy camps, golden rats were found to have gnawed through all bowstrings and damaged weapons and equipment beyond use. Those old and weak who could not flee, your subject's troops wished to kill them, but a voice from the sky said: 'Let them go. Do not kill them.' Following the sound and looking back, above the north gate tower of the city there was a great radiance, and Vaiśravaṇa, Heavenly King, manifested his form upon the tower. The appearance of the Heavenly King is respectfully included with this memorial."

In the fourteenth year of Tianbao [755], at the residence of the Inner-Court Offering Monk Dabei, the sūtra and image were copied. In the fifth year of Dali [770], in Jizhou, the Inner-Court Offering Monk, Dharma Master Liangben, was found to have relocated to the Kaiyuan Temple in Jizhou. The sūtra and image were compared and found to match Dabei's original. Formerly, for the defense of the realm's borders, the Buddha's teaching and imperial decree commanded the third son Naṭa to carry the tower and accompany the Heavenly King. The Tripiṭaka Master Mahāprajñā said: "On the first day of each month, the Heavenly King and all devas and spirits hold an assembly. On the eleventh day, the second son Dujian takes leave of his father the king to patrol the boundaries. On the fifteenth day, there is an assembly with the four heavenly kings. On the twenty-first day, Naṭa and his father the king exchange the tower. On those days, milk porridge should be offered. If milk is unavailable, use ghee-and-honey porridge to make offerings to the Heavenly King, for he possesses heavenly spiritual power." By imperial decree, this was transmitted to the military governors of the ten circuits, who in their respective commands were ordered to install images and make offerings with prayers. On the twenty-third day of the fourth month of the first year of Tianbao, the Palace Attendant Supervisor Gao Huiming proclaimed that the Heavenly King's second son Dujian constantly leads celestial soldiers to protect the realm's borders. The Heavenly King's third son, Prince Naṭa, carries the tower and always accompanies the Heavenly King. Śrī Mahādevī, also named Guṇadevatā, has her own mantra. The Ṛṣi Vasu, according to Mahāprajñā, is a transformation body of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva.

**Invitation Procedure**

When inviting the Heavenly King, form the root mudrā and recite the invitation mantra seven times, then release the mudrā above the crown. The mantra:

*Tadyathā namo vaiśravaṇāya namo dhana-dāya dhaneśvarāya agaccha aparimita-dhaneśvara-parama-kāruṇika sarva-sattvā-hita-citta mama dhana-manu-prayaccha svayam-āgaccha svāhā*

Private note:

*Oṃ yakṣa-bhūtana hūṃ indrā-bhe svāhā vicā-jaye svāhā*

Vaiśravaṇa, Heavenly King's heart mantra:

*Namo ratna-trayāya namo vaiśravaṇāya mahā-rājāya sarva-sattvānāṃ-śa-paripūraṇāya siddhi-kārāya sukhandanāya tasmai-namaḥ-kṛtvā idaṃ vaiśravaṇāya hṛdayam-āvattāya iṣāmi sarva-sattva-sukhā-vahaṃ tadyathā oṃ siddhi-siddhi-siddhi-siddhi sumu-sumu ca-ca-ca-ca-cā raca-raca kara-kara kiri-kiri kuru-kuru juru-juru sādhaya adān-mama nityam-arthā-nu-bhava svāhā vaiśravaṇāya svāhā dhana-dāya svāhā maṇḍala-ratha-paripūrakāya svāhā*

Translated by the Central Indian Tripiṭaka Brāhmaṇa Dharmadeva:

*Namo mahā-śrīyāya namo vaiśravaṇāya mahā-yakṣa-senā-pataye tadyathā juve-juve svā-svā śāntyam-matī svāhā śivā-tatrī svāhā jayā-patrī svāhā*

"I now proclaim the root mudrā. With both hands, cross the right over the left with fingers interlocked inward. Raise both ring fingers with tips touching. Bend both index fingers like hooks. When inviting, release above the crown. Then take up the rosary and recite with focused attention. Next, form the body-mudrā of Śrī Mahādevī: join both palms with hollow center, open both index fingers, both middle fingers, and both ring fingers, bending them like a lotus shape. Both thumbs and both little fingers stand upright and touching. When reciting, form the mudrā at the heart, recite the mantra seven times, and release above the crown."

**Additional Methods**

"Another method: on the fifteenth day of the bright or dark fortnight, in a pure room or in the open air, sit facing north before the image. Offer milk porridge and burn frankincense. Recite this dhāraṇī for three nights, one thousand and eight times.

"Another method: if seeking an official position, before the image for one day and one night, recite the name of the desired patron once per repetition, until completing one thousand and eighty times. The desired position will then be obtained.

"If at a place of combat or litigation and one wishes to prevail, use white cotton to make a cord, tying one knot per recitation for one thousand and eighty times. Bind it to the left arm and victory will follow.

"Another method: place ox-yellow in a copper vessel and recite one hundred and eight times before the image. Then apply it to the body, and all people will love and respect you.

"Another method: if you wish the king to think of you, recite one thousand and eighty times each day and night, and the king will think of you, and whatever you need will be granted.

The same Tripiṭaka's additional translation:

*Atha gabhūtra-yā-dhiśaya vaiśravaṇa-śya mahā-rājā-śya yakṣa-adhipatyā-śya bhāma-taśya sādu-taśya bhāśāya-prabahanā-śya himā-daṣṭa-ṣṭanī-praśāya-me tadyathā kuśāmi-kuśāmi-kuśā vaiśravaṇa-śya mahā-rājā-śya kībandha-ga-śya tārama-ca-du svāhā*

"The practitioner's upper and lower garments must all be clean. One should bathe each time after using the latrine. Beginning on the fifteenth day of the bright fortnight, before the image recite the spell to a full count of one hundred thousand. Afterward, apply fragrant paste to the altar and set out various food and drink. The practitioner should take one hundred and eight pieces of frankincense, recite the spell once per piece and cast it into the fire. The Heavenly King will then appear.

"Another method: if the practitioner wishes to gain the love and respect of a king, take red beans, recite the spell once per bean, and cast it into the fire. Complete one hundred and eight times, and the king will urgently send someone to summon you.

"Another method: if you wish to gain the love and respect of a high official, take white mustard seeds, recite the spell once per seed, and cast it into the fire. Complete one hundred and eight times, and the official will come of their own accord with great delight and respect.

"Another method: if you wish to travel far, recite the spell over a wooden staff, and all difficulties and obstacles will be removed.

"Another method: if you wish people to love and respect you, take one hundred and eight neem seeds, recite the spell once per seed, and cast it into the fire. All people will love and respect the spell-master as their own parents, and all wishes will be fulfilled.

"Another method: if you wish an enemy to depart far away, take one hundred and eight neem seeds, recite the spell once per seed, and cast it into the fire. That person will immediately depart.

"Another method: if you wish to cause someone to submit, speak the person's name and recite the spell over yogurt one hundred and eight times, then burn it.

"Another method: if you wish to possess sovereign awesome radiance, recite the spell over ink and apply it to the forehead. All who see you will love and admire you.

"Another method: if one has a ghost-illness or heart pain, recite the spell over pomegranate flower juice and drink it for immediate cure.

"Another method: if one suffers from fox-spirit possession, recite the spell over a willow switch and strike the patient for immediate cure.

"Another method: if one suffers from any wind ailment, recite the spell over ghee twenty-one times and consume it for immediate cure.

"Another method: if one suffers from malaria, recite the spell over a willow branch twenty-one times and have the patient struck with it for immediate cure.

"Another method: if one speaks madly with ghost-voices, recite the spell over water and have the patient drink it for immediate cure.

"Another method: if one suffers from heart pain, recite the spell over yellow earth and apply it.

"If one suffers from any ghost-illness, recite the spell over a pomegranate-wood staff one hundred and eight times and strike the patient for immediate cure."

**Mudrā Method**

Hook both little fingers together. Both ring fingers stand upright facing outward. Both middle fingers and both index fingers cross in reverse. Both thumbs stand upright facing outward, with palms facing away from each other. When the mantra count is complete, recite the messenger's mantra seven times, then recite the eight heavenly kings' mantras three times each.

Vaiśravaṇa, Heavenly King's messenger spell:

*Namo vaiśravaṇa-patāya mahā-yakṣa-senā-patāya oṃ sarva-karaṇa-viśodhānī svāhā*

*Namo samanta-buddhānāṃ aparatihata-śasanānāṃ oṃ khakha-khahi-khahi hūṃ-hūṃ jvāla-jvāla prajvāla-prajvāla tiṣṭha-tiṣṭha-sthirī sphaṭa-sphaṭa svāhā viśaya svāhā oṃ yakṣa-bhūtana hūṃ indrā-bhe svāhā viśācana svāhā*

The mudrā for reciting this mantra: both hands make fists with both index fingers standing upright. Then open both thumbs. Next, the right index finger crosses over the back of the left index finger and enters the palm. The left index finger enters the right palm. Then both thumbs grip the right index finger on the left side, with both tips extending outward.

The eight great heavenly kings: Maṇibhadra, Pūrṇabhadra, Pāñcika, Sātāgiri, Hemavata, Viśākha, Āṭavaka, and Pāṃśurāga.
