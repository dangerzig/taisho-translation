# 聖觀自在菩薩心真言瑜伽觀行儀軌
## Ritual Manual for the Yoga Contemplative Practice of the Heart Mantra of Holy Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1031

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

One who cultivates yoga should first, in a quiet place, establish a maṇḍala, sprinkle it with fragrant water, and scatter various seasonal flowers upon the altar. The practitioner must first bathe and put on fresh, clean garments, then enter the practice hall. Before the honored image, touch the five limbs to the ground with earnest mind and pay homage to all the tathāgatas and bodhisattvas. Then sit in the cross-legged posture and visualize all the buddhas as if present before one's eyes. Afterward, with utmost sincerity, confess all sins, speaking as follows:

"I, [name], from beginningless time until this very day, revolving in the cycle of birth and death, have committed immeasurable, boundless sins without knowing it, whether done by myself, by instructing others, or by witnessing and rejoicing. I now confess and will commit them no more. May all the buddhas compassionately receive me and cause my karmic obstructions to be swiftly eliminated."

One should further take a personal vow and receive the three refuges, speaking as follows:

"May the buddhas and bodhisattvas have compassion and protect me. I, [name], from this day forward until I sit upon the seat of enlightenment, take refuge in the tathāgatas' unsurpassed three bodies. I take refuge in the vast Mahāyāna dharma treasury. I take refuge in the sangha assembly of bodhisattvas."

"I, [name], have taken refuge in the Buddha. I have taken refuge in the Dharma. I have taken refuge in the Sangha. From now until I attain buddhahood, I will not take refuge in any other, whether of the two vehicles or of non-Buddhist paths. May all the buddhas compassionately receive me."

Next, one should offer one's body, speaking as follows:

"May the buddhas and bodhisattvas compassionately receive me. From now until I attain buddhahood, I shall always offer my body in service to all the tathāgatas and bodhisattvas. May they compassionately protect me."

### Elemental Visualization

Next, in the lowest empty space, visualize the syllable *ha*, black in color. That syllable transforms into the wind wheel, whose form is a half-moon.

Upon the wind wheel, visualize the syllable *va*, white in color. That syllable transforms into the water wheel, whose form is perfectly circular.

Upon the water wheel, visualize the syllable *ra*, golden in color. That syllable transforms into a fierce vajra mace, from which flow vajra flames. Its form is triangular. The flames rise upward from below, reaching the earth wheel and one's own body. The flames burn everything until only ashes remain.

Those ashes then transform into a vajra wheel, white in color, solid and dense, of whatever size is appropriate, perfectly square.

### Visualization of Avalokiteśvara

Next, upon the vajra wheel, visualize an eight-petaled great lotus with jeweled stamens and pistils. Upon the lotus platform, visualize the syllable *sa*, golden in color, endowed with immeasurable radiance.

Transform this syllable *sa* into Holy Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, seated cross-legged. His body is golden, his circular halo blazing. He is draped in light gauze and silk garments and wears a red skirt. His left hand rests at the navel, holding an unopened lotus flower. His right hand is at his chest, in the gesture of opening a flower petal. He is adorned with a crown and necklaces, and upon his head sits Amitāyus Buddha. He abides in the aspect of samādhi.

### Samaya Mudrā

Having completed this visualization, form the samaya mudrā: with both hands at the heart, press the palms secretly together and raise both thumbs upright. Recite the mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ asamimtri-saṃmimsamaye svāhā*

Recite the mantra three times. With the mudrā, seal the five places of the body: forehead, right shoulder, left shoulder, heart, and throat. Release the mudrā above the crown.

By forming this mudrā, one can swiftly fulfill the practices and vows of the ten grounds and the ten pāramitās, can see the stage of all tathāgatas, and can transcend the realm of the dharma path, namely, transcending the ground of resolute understanding, the ground of pure mind, and the ground of the tathāgata.

### Dharmadhātu-udbhava Mudrā

Next, form the dharmadhātu-arising mudrā. With both hands, each form a vajra fist and place them side by side. Raise both index fingers with their tips touching, and place the mudrā upon the crown.

At the crown, visualize the syllable *raṃ*. From that syllable streams white radiance that pervades one's entire body, inside and out. Then contemplate one's body as identical with the dharmadhātu. Recite the mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ dharmadhātu-svabhāvaku-haṃ*

Recite the mantra three times. With the mudrā at the crown, separate the two fists and slowly lower and release them on both sides.

### Turning the Dharma Wheel Mudrā

Next, form the turning the dharma wheel mudrā. With both hands at the heart, backs facing each other, with the right pressing upon the left, interlock the four fingers. The left thumb is concealed within the right palm, and the tips of the right thumb and left thumb touch.

Visualize oneself as Vajrasattva Bodhisattva: left hand holding a vajra bell resting upon the left hip, right hand holding a five-pronged vajra at the heart in a leaping posture. The body is white as moonlight. Upon the head sits a five-buddha crown. Seated within a moon disk. Recite the mantra:

*Namo samanta-vajrāṇāṃ vajrātmaku-haṃ*

Recite the mantra three times, then release the mudrā above the crown.

### Mahāvairocana Sword Mudrā

Next, form the Mahāvairocana Tathāgata sword mudrā. With both hands at the heart in añjali, bend both index fingers so the middle knuckles press against each other horizontally. Press both thumbs upon the upper joints of the index fingers, forming a sword shape.

Having formed this mudrā, visualize within one's heart an eight-petaled lotus. Within the lotus, visualize the syllable *a*, radiating golden light in resonance with the mudrā. Contemplating that syllable *a*, realize that all dharmas are originally unborn. Then recite the mantra:

*Namo samanta-buddhānāṃ a-vira-hūṃ-khaṃ*

Recite the mantra eight times. With the mudrā, seal the five places of one's body as before, then release the mudrā above the crown.

### Universal Offering Mudrā

Next, form the universal offering mudrā. With both hands in añjali, cross the upper finger joints with the right pressing upon the left. This completes the mudrā. Recite the mantra:

*Namo samanta-buddhānāṃ sarvathaṃ udgate spharaṇa-himaṃ gaganakhaṃ svāhā*

Hold the mudrā at the heart and recite the mantra five times. Visualize that from the mudrā flow forth immeasurable, boundless fragrant flowers, food and drink, as offerings to all the worthies and sages throughout space and the dharmadhātu. Release the mudrā above the crown.

### Syllable Placement

Next is the practice of contemplative syllable placement.

The yoga practitioner should visualize and place the syllable *hūṃ* between the eyebrows, in reddish-gold color. It transforms into the white ūrṇā mark. Within the cranial suture, place the syllable *aṃ*, white in color, its light filling the interior of the brain. Upon the crown, place the syllable *raṃ*, with red light and flaming rays shooting upward. Above the buddha-crown, visualize the syllable *oṃ*, with white light illuminating the dharmadhātu. Upon the sole of the right foot, place the syllables *svā*. Upon the sole of the left foot, place the syllable *hā*.

### Contemplating Bodhicitta

Then contemplate one's own mind as the mind of enlightenment, free from all self, free from the aggregates, sense-bases, and elements, free from subject and object. In dharmas, there is equality. Realize that one's own mind is originally unborn, empty and without self-nature.

Therefore one should observe: one's own mind is not of the nature of self, person, being, or life-span. Why? Because the nature of self, person, and so forth is without fabrication and without attainment. If self and person are fundamentally non-existent, how could there be self-nature? Thus one becomes far removed from all views of self.

This mind is also not of the nature of the aggregates, sense-bases, or elements. Why? Because the nature of these aggregates and so forth is truly unobtainable in the ultimate sense. The discriminated self-nature of the aggregates, sense-bases, and elements is not that mind.

This mind is also not subject or object: not the mind that grasps through false conception, and not the grasped appearances of blue, yellow, and so forth.

The World-Honored One said: "The mind does not dwell inside, nor outside, nor in between. Why? Because it is originally pure and free from discrimination."

Observing thus, one knows that one's own mind is selfless and equal, that all dharmas are originally unborn, free from false discrimination, all without self-nature, like empty space.

Then, turning one's thoughts to all sentient beings, contemplate with compassion: from beginningless time they have not known that their own minds are originally pure. Generating false discriminations and inversions, they are devoured by demons and ghosts, suffering various miseries in the cycle of birth and death. "How shall I now rouse great diligence so that all sentient beings may awaken to their own minds, realize the pure dharma, and become far removed from false discrimination?" Such great compassion constitutes the mind of enlightenment.

### Transformation into Vairocana

Having generated this mind, upon the syllable *ra* add the circular bindu, making it *raṃ*, the seed of the dharmadhātu. Visualize two *ra* syllables placed within both eyes, like brimming lamplight illuminating everything.

With these eyes of radiant wisdom, contemplate the syllable *a* placed within one's heart. Realize that all dharmas are originally unborn. Then from the syllable *a* streams forth white radiance, illuminating the boundless dust-mote worlds, removing the darkness of ignorance within the bodies of all sentient beings.

Then visualize one's own body transforming into Vairocana Tathāgata, adorned with crown and necklaces, seated upon a white lotus, body golden in color, radiance shining brilliantly, abiding in samādhi. One should contemplate in this way.

### Avalokiteśvara Heart Mudrā

Next, form the heart mudrā of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva. Interlock both hands internally, and raise both thumbs upright. This completes the mudrā. Hold the mudrā at the heart and recite the heart mantra of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva seven times, sealing the heart, forehead, throat, and crown with the mudrā. Recite the mantra:

*Oṃ ārolik svāhā*

While reciting the mantra, beckon toward the body with the right thumb. This constitutes the invocation.

Then visualize within the heart of the honored deity a perfectly full, tranquil moon disk. Within the moon disk, arrange the dhāraṇī syllables clockwise. All the syllables emit white radiance pervading the dharmadhātu. That light returns and enters the practitioner's crown. Within the moon disk in the yoga practitioner's heart, arrange the syllables clockwise as before, clearly and distinctly. Those syllables again emit radiance; contemplate as before.

Having completed this contemplation, the yoga practitioner's own body and the honored deity Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva's body are equal and without difference, like a mirror reflection: neither one nor other.

### Syllable Meaning Contemplation

Next, one should contemplate the meanings of the syllables. The gate of the syllable *a*: because all dharmas are originally unborn. The gate of the syllable *ra*: because all dharmas are far removed from dust. The gate of the syllable *la*: because the characteristics of all dharmas are unobtainable. This syllable *la*, when given the augmented vowel, becomes *li*. From this syllable *li* flows forth the syllable *ka*. The gate of the syllable *ka*: because all dharmas are without fabrication. One should contemplate in this way.

Fix the mind upon the syllables of the mantra, then contemplate the meanings indicated beneath the syllables: "originally unborn" and the other four meanings as above. Contemplating in this way, cycling from end to beginning, is called samādhi recitation.

If one wishes to recite aloud, within the mantra one should add the following phrase. Recite the mantra:

*Oṃ ārolik kirāti-kṣami-viputi-mina-da svāhā*

### Conclusion

Next, one should visualize the four syllables *a*, *ro*, *li*, *ka* in golden color and recite the mantra as before. One then attains increase, and all that is sought within the dharma will be obtained.

The syllable *a* placed in the heart and the syllable *aṃ* in the brain are one in essence. The syllable *ra* in the eyes and the syllable *raṃ* at the crown. The meaning of these four syllables constitutes the statement of self-realization of equal enlightenment.

Again contemplate the mind of enlightenment. Then form the samaya mudrā as before, and also form the dharmadhātu-arising, turning the dharma wheel, and sword mudrās, sealing the five places of one's body, reciting each one's own mantra. Then pay homage to the Buddha, make vows, dedicate the merit, and exit the practice hall.

One should constantly study the great Mahāyāna teachings such as the *Avataṃsaka* and *Prajñāpāramitā*, and also stamp buddha seals, stamp stūpa seals, practice walking meditation, and circumambulate stūpas, so as to bring about swift accomplishment.
