# 仁王般若念誦法
## Recitation Method for the Humane King Prajñāpāramitā

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0995

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空)

---

## Preface to the Ritual of Dhāraṇī Recitation from the Newly Translated Humane King Prajñāpāramitā Sūtra

Our Emperor, whose sage virtue extends far and wide, who benevolently nurtures all beings, has succeeded to the throne and sounded the great Dharma-conch, correcting the weight of the Sanskrit texts and alerting the ears and eyes of those who have gone astray. How magnificent! Thus he opened the Xingshan Monastery and commissioned the Great Broad Wisdom Tripiṭaka Master Amoghavajra, together with the doctrinal monk Liángbēn and thirteen others, the Commissioner Yú Cháo'ēn, the Hanlin Academician Cháng Gùn, and others, who in the summer of the fourth month of the previous year, at the Southern Peach Garden, retranslated this sūtra. In the autumn, in the ninth month, an imperial decree ordered a hundred-seat exposition at the Zīshèng and Xīmíng monasteries. It descended from the Purple Tenuity Palace escorted by five-colored clouds, and as it proceeded along the great thoroughfare, ten thousand people paid reverence. The streets were thronged and packed like walls. Examining the records of the monastic community and reviewing the annals, from the time Mātaṅga entered the Han and Saṃghavarman traveled to Wu, no auspicious Dharma arrival has equaled this. The sūtra says: "If in future ages there are kings who establish the right Dharma and protect the Three Treasures, I shall command the bodhisattvas of the five directions to go and protect their kingdoms, causing them to be free from disaster." It also says: "The five bodhisattvas, before the Buddha, each made a great vow: 'I have dhāraṇīs that can empower and protect. This is the swift gate originally practiced by all the Buddhas. If a person hears it even once pass through the ear, all karmic obstructions will be extinguished, how much more so if one studies and recites it until fluent.' The Buddha then praised them: 'If one recites and upholds these dhāraṇīs, I and all the Buddhas of the ten directions will constantly protect them. All evil spirits and demons will revere them as they would a Buddha, and before long they will attain anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi.'" Thus one knows that these dhāraṇīs are the foundation of the syllable-mothers and the wellspring of all yogas. Like water and wood returning to their source, the bodhisattvas proclaimed them, kings established them, the common people recite them, and the Buddhas praised them, causing prosperity and flourishing, causing merit and benefit. Herein one can see the mind of the sage. The benefit being so vast and the ritual being so wondrous, if sincerity does not prevail, how can blessings descend? Therefore the Tripiṭaka Master translated the text with its manifold meanings, and Dharma Master Liángbēn received the concise essentials, beginning with the place and preparatory skillful means, ending with the contemplation-practice ritual procedures, organized into ten gates arranged in order of rank, each clearly displayed so that none should be confused. May all monastics and laypersons who wish to protect their lives and fulfill their aspirations tread the path of bodhi and ascend to the realm of humane longevity; what means could be better than this?

At that time, the Buddha addressed King Prasenajit: "Now, for the king's sake, I shall teach the method of upholding the vidyā of the Humane King's Protection of the Nation Prajñāpāramitā. May the king listen attentively and receive it with single-minded concentration. First, I shall explain the ritual for entering the bodhimaṇḍa.

"If any humane king seeks to avert disasters, one should first bathe clean and put on new, pure garments. If a Dharma-practicing king, one should take the upavāsa precepts. One should generate a weighty Mahāyāna mind, seeking accomplishment without sparing one's body or life, and toward boundless sentient beings arouse the mind of compassionate vows to ferry them across. One who can practice thus will swiftly attain accomplishment. Having entered the bodhimaṇḍa, prostrate with the five limbs touching the ground and bow universally to all the Three Treasures of the dharma-realm. Kneel on the right knee, confess all transgressions and obstructions of the three karmas, beseech the Buddhas of the ten directions to turn the right Dharma-wheel, and request all the Tathāgatas to abide long in the world. Rejoice in the merit and wisdom cultivated by the three vehicles. May the merit cultivated by me, so-and-so, be entirely dedicated to unsurpassed bodhi. May I, together with all sentient beings of the dharma-realm, swiftly fulfill the siddhi we seek. Next, sit in full lotus.

"If circumstances prevent bathing, anoint both hands with scented unguent, generate a weighty mind, and form the purity mudrā: both hands at the heart in a hollow-palmed añjali like an unopened lotus. Recite the true words:

"'*Oṃ svabhāva-śuddhaḥ sarva-dharma svabhāva-śuddho 'haṃ*'

"Recite this true word three times. While reciting, direct the mind to broadcast universally that all dharmas are originally pure, and therefore my body is also entirely pure. Then close the eyes and visualize, filling all of empty space, the bodhimaṇḍa assemblies of all the Buddhas and bodhisattvas, holding all manner of supreme and wondrous incense and flowers. With the three karmas in utmost sincerity, bow with the head touching the ground."

## First: Form the Buddha-Division Samaya Mudrā

"Both hands at the heart, interlaced inward into fists, with both thumbs extended upright together. Recite the true words:

"'*Oṃ jina-jika svāhā*'

"Recite this true word silently three times (the same applies below). Release at the crown. By forming this mudrā-contract and reciting these Buddha-division samaya true words, all the Buddhas of the ten directions and the dharma-realm will all gather like clouds, filling empty space, and empower the practitioner to be free from all obstructing afflictions, with the three karmas purified, and all practices and vows swiftly accomplished."

## Second: Form the Bodhisattva-Division Samaya Mudrā

"Both hands at the heart as before, forming fists. The left thumb bends into the palm. Recite the true words:

"'*Oṃ ālolik svāhā*'

"Recite three times as above, and release at the crown. By forming this mudrā-contract and reciting these bodhisattva-division samaya true words, one immediately obtains Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva and all the bodhisattvas of the ten directions and the dharma-realm, who all gather like clouds, filling empty space, and empower the Dharma-practicing practitioner so that the three karmas become pure and free from all disasters. Because the bodhisattvas, carrying their original compassionate vows, cause whatever is sought to be entirely fulfilled."

## Third: Form the Vajra-Division Samaya Mudrā

"As with the previous mudrā, extend the left thumb and bend the right thumb into the palm. Recite the true words:

"'*Oṃ vajra-dhṛk svāhā*'

"Recite three times as above, and release at the crown. By forming this mudrā-contract and reciting the vajra-division samaya true words, one immediately obtains all the vajra-holders of the ten directions and the dharma-realm, who manifest wrathful forms and gather like clouds, filling the realm of empty space, and empower the Dharma-practicing practitioner so that the three karmas become firm as vajra. Because those holy ones, bearing the Buddha's awesome power and their own vow-power, on the great scale protect the boundaries of the nation and cause it to be free from disaster, and on the small scale even protect a single person and cause them to be free from all afflictions."

## Fourth: Form the Body-Protection Mudrā

"Again, use the three mudrā-contracts already formed and recite the true words. Empower the five places: forehead, left shoulder, right shoulder, heart, and throat. Release at the crown. This constitutes donning the vajra adamantine armor. Through this empowerment, the practitioner's body blazes with awesome radiance. All māras who attempt to obstruct and afflict cannot bear to look upon them and flee in haste."

## Fifth: Form the Dispelling Mudrā and Vajra Directional Jewel-Boundary Mudrā

"Using the previous vajra-division mudrā-contract, recite the true words while circling the altar leftward three times. This dispels all the great powerful māras. All hidden beings, following the Buddhas and bodhisattvas, depart far to other realms. Following one's intention, whether large or small, circle rightward three times. This establishes the vajra directional jewel-boundary, which even the Buddhas and bodhisattvas do not transgress, let alone any obstructing being finding an opening. Release at the crown."

## Sixth: Form the Mudrā for Inviting the Holy Assembly to Descend to the Altar

"Using the previous three-division mudrā-contracts and reciting the true words, with the thumb beckon toward oneself, three recitations and three beckonings. Immediately the three-division holy assemblies filling the sky each take their proper positions without obstruction and abide in stillness. Release at the crown."

## Seventh: Offering the Argha Scented Water Mudrā

"With both hands, hold up a maṇi-jewel vessel filled with scented water. Place it at the level of the brow and recite the true words:

"'*Oṃ vajra-udaka hūṃ*'

"Recite three times as above. Direct the mind to broadcast universally, bathing all the holy assembly in sequence. Release at the crown. By offering the argha, from the stage of resolute practice up to the cloud-of-dharma stage, at every stage the Buddhas and bodhisattvas of the ten directions and the dharma-realm all empower and protect one, and the Buddhas bestow the consecration."

## Eighth: Offering the Jeweled Seat Mudrā

"With both hands at the heart in a hollow-palmed añjali, the two thumbs and two little fingers touching and slightly curved, the remaining six fingers each spread and gently curved, like an opening lotus. The true words:

"'*Oṃ kamalā svāhā*'

"By forming this mudrā-contract and reciting the true words, the offered jeweled seat causes all the holy assembly to truly receive and enjoy it, and will cause the Dharma-practicing practitioner, at the stage of fruition, to obtain the vajra adamantine jeweled seat."

## Ninth: Form the Universal Offering Mudrā

"With both hands in añjali, the five fingers interlaced with right over left, placed upon the heart. Recite the true words:

"'*Namo samanta-buddhānāṃ sarvathā-kham udgate spharahi-māṃ gagana-kaṃ svāhā*'

"By forming this mudrā and reciting the true words, the lotus-mind broadcasts universally throughout the dharma-realm, to the ocean-assemblies of all the Buddhas and bodhisattvas, universally raining all manner of offering-implements. At the first recitation, jeweled vessels as numerous as dust-motes and sands, filled with unguent, universally anoint the holy assembly. At the second recitation, various garlands universally and splendidly adorn. At the third recitation, various burned incense universally makes offerings. At the fourth recitation, supreme celestial food and drink descend, placed in jeweled vessels as universal offerings. At the fifth recitation, all manner of maṇi-jewels descend as lamp-light, universally making offerings to all the Buddhas and bodhisattvas. Through the empowering power of reciting the true words, the incense and other offerings presented to the ocean-assemblies all become real. The holy assembly accepts and enjoys them, and the Dharma-practicing practitioner will always obtain such reward in future lives."

## Tenth: Form the Root Mudrā of Prajñāpāramitā

"With both hands back-to-back, draw in the two index fingers. Bend the two little fingers into the palms, with the thumbs each pressing the tips of both fingers. Place at the heart. Recite the dhāraṇī from the sūtra seven times. By forming this mudrā and reciting the dhāraṇī, the Dharma-practicing practitioner's own body immediately transforms into the Prajñāpāramitā Bodhisattva, the mother of all the Buddhas. The bodhisattva's form: sitting in full lotus upon a white lotus, body of golden color, adorned with jeweled necklaces covering the body, wearing a jeweled crown with white silk ribbons hanging down on both sides. The left hand at the heart holds a Prajñā Sanskrit text. The right hand at the breast makes the teaching mudrā, with the thumb pressing the tip of the ring finger. Then visualize the bodhisattva, from crown to feet, with light of various colors streaming from every pore, filling the dharma-realm. Within each ray of light, transform into immeasurable Buddhas, pervading the realm of empty space and all the worlds, universally preaching the profound Dharma of Prajñāpāramitā to sentient beings according to their capacities, causing all to awaken and understand. Abide in samādhi, and having completed this contemplation as the Dharma-practicing king, release the mudrā at the crown. Hold the rosary in the palms of the hands, join the palms at the heart, and recite the true words:

"'*Oṃ vairocana-mālā svāhā*'

"Recite three times to empower the rosary, raise it to the crown, then hold it at the heart. The left hand supports the rosary; the right hand moves the beads. Thought after thought in correspondence, abiding in the samādhi of the Buddha-mother, contemplating the mind without interruption. Recite one hundred and eight times, or twenty-one times. Recite the true words:

"'*Namo ratna-trayāya namaḥ āryāvalokiteśvarāya tathāgatāya arhate samyak-saṃbuddhāya namaḥ ārya-samantabhadra-bodhisattvāya mahāsattvāya mahā-kāruṇikāya tadyathā jñāna-praṇidhe akṣaya-kuśe pratibhāna-vati sarva-buddhāvalokite yoga-pariṇiṣpannī gambhīra-nura-vāga-hetirya-dhva-pariṇiṣpannī bodhi-citta-saṃjanani sarva-viśeka-viṣkṛṣṭe dharma-sāgara-saṃbuddhe amogha-śrāvaṇī mahā-samanta-bhadra-bhūmi-niryāte viyā-karaṇa-pariprapani sarva-siddha-namaskarite sarva-bodhi-sattva-saṃjanani bhagavatī buddha-māte arāṇī-karāṇī araṇā-karāṇī mahā-prajñā-pāramite svāhā*'

"Having thus recited with utmost sincerity the complete number of times, raise the rosary to the crown and return it to its place. Form the samādhi mudrā: extend both hands horizontally with the right over the left, placed below the navel. Sit upright with eyes closed, the head tilted very slightly forward. Focus the mind upon the heart, and contemplate a circular bright mirror of wisdom, one cubit in width and length, gradually pervading the dharma-realm. The syllables are arranged in rows turning rightward. Contemplate each syllable one by one, with light shining through clearly, from the outside inward to the earth-syllable, then from the inside outward, gradually contemplating each syllable, circling back to the beginning. By the third cycle, the mind becomes well settled, clearly and distinctly contemplating the meanings they express: unborn and unceasing, each and all equal, pervading the dharma-realm, neither moving nor still, concentration and wisdom operating together, forever free from all marks. This is the samādhi contemplation of Prajñāpāramitā. From this, form the Prajñāpāramitā mudrā again, recite the dhāraṇī seven times, and release at the crown. Next, form the universal offering mudrā and, as before, direct the mind to make offerings in sequence. Before the holy assembly, take all the merit generated by the practice just performed and dedicate it entirely to fulfilling all prayers, for the nation, for the family, and for the benefit of others. Then transfer the merit to all sentient beings, transfer it to adorn the pure land, transfer it toward ultimate reality, and transfer it seeking unsurpassed bodhi. May I, together with all sentient beings, swiftly reach the other shore. Next, form the previous boundary mudrā, recite the previous true words three times while turning leftward; this dissolves the boundary. Next, form the previous three-division mudrās, recite the previous true words three times, and with the thumbs flick outward; this dispatches the holy assembly, each returning to their own lands. The practitioner bows and departs, engaging in walking meditation as usual, receiving, upholding, reading, and reciting the Mahāyāna, and should not be scattered or agitated. Thus, from the fifth watch to dawn is the first session. From after noon to the hour of the sheep is the second session. From after dusk to midnight is the third session. From after midnight to the fifth watch is the fourth session. In each session, practice diligently according to the prescribed number without laziness. All enemies will naturally be subdued, and all disasters will never again arise."

At that time, King Prasenajit, having heard the Buddha's teaching, rose from his seat, bowed, circumambulated, and with great joy and exultation, faithfully received it and practiced accordingly.
