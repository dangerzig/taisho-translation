# 佛說救拔焰口餓鬼陀羅尼經
## The Buddha's Teaching: Dhāraṇī Sūtra for Rescuing the Flaming-Mouth Hungry Ghosts

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1313

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

At that time, the World-Honored One was at the Nyagrodha Saṃghārāma in the city of Kapilavastu, together with many bhikṣus, bodhisattvas, and an innumerable assembly surrounding him on all sides, as he taught the Dharma.

At that time, Ānanda was dwelling alone in a quiet place, reflecting on the Dharma he had received. During that very night, after the third watch, he saw a hungry ghost named Flaming-Mouth. Its form was hideous, its body withered and emaciated. Fire blazed from its mouth. Its throat was as narrow as the point of a needle. Its hair was tangled and disheveled, its claws and fangs long and sharp. It was extremely terrifying. Standing before Ānanda, it addressed him: "Three days from now your life will end, and you will be born among the hungry ghosts."

When Ānanda heard these words, his heart was filled with terror. He asked the hungry ghost: "If after my death I am to be born as a hungry ghost, what skillful means can I employ to be freed from this suffering?"

The hungry ghost addressed Ānanda: "Tomorrow, if you are able to give food to hungry ghosts as numerous as the sands of hundreds of thousands of nayutas of Ganges Rivers, and also to hundreds of thousands of brahmin sages, giving to each one bushel of food and drink by the measure used in Magadha, and furthermore make offerings to the Three Treasures on my behalf, then your lifespan will be increased, and I shall be freed from the suffering of the hungry ghosts and be born in the heavens."

When Ānanda saw this Flaming-Mouth hungry ghost, emaciated, withered, scorched, extremely ugly, with fire blazing from its mouth, throat like a needle-point, hair tangled and disheveled, body-hair and claws long and sharp, and heard these inauspicious words, he was greatly terrified. Every hair on his body stood on end. He immediately rose from his seat and went swiftly to the Buddha. He prostrated with all five limbs touching the ground, bowed at the Buddha's feet, his body trembling, and addressed the Buddha: "I pray you will save me from this suffering! For what reason? While dwelling in a quiet place, reflecting on the Dharma I had received, I saw the Flaming-Mouth hungry ghost, who said to me: 'In three days your life will certainly end, and you will be born among the hungry ghosts.' I asked: 'How can I be freed from this suffering?' The hungry ghost replied: 'If you can now give various food and drink to hungry ghosts as numerous as the sands of hundreds of thousands of nayutas of Ganges Rivers, and to hundreds of thousands of brahmin sages, your lifespan will be increased.' World-Honored One! How can I possibly provide food for so many hungry ghosts and sages?"

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed Ānanda: "Do not be afraid! I have a skillful means by which you can give food to so many hundreds of thousands of Ganges-sand-numbered hungry ghosts and brahmin sages. Do not be troubled."

The Buddha addressed Ānanda: "There is a dhāraṇī called the Immeasurable Majestic Sovereign Radiant Supreme Wondrous Power. If anyone recites this dhāraṇī, they can fully satisfy koṭi-nayuta hundreds of thousands of Ganges-sand-numbered hungry ghosts and brahmin sages with supremely wonderful food and drink. And for each and every one among them, there will be food amounting to seven times seven bushels by the measure used in Magadha.

"Ānanda! In a former life, when I was a brahmin, I received this dhāraṇī from Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva and from the Tathāgata of Majestic Sovereign Radiance in the World. Because of this, I was able to give and scatter food to innumerable hungry ghosts and sages of various kinds, enabling the hungry ghosts to be liberated from their suffering bodies and born in the heavens.

"Ānanda! Receive and uphold this now, and your merit, lifespan, and all else will increase and grow."

At that time, the World-Honored One then taught Ānanda the dhāraṇī:

*Namaḥ sarva-tathāgatāvalokite oṃ saṃbhara saṃbhara hūṃ.*

The Buddha addressed Ānanda: "If any good man or good woman wishes to seek long life, increased merit, and flourishing prosperity, and wishes to swiftly fulfill the pāramitā of giving, then every morning and at all other times, without obstruction, take a clean vessel, fill it with clean water, and place in it a small amount of rice, parched grain, cakes, and other food. With the right hand placed upon the vessel, recite the preceding dhāraṇī a full seven times. Then call the names of the four Tathāgatas:

*Namo bhagavate prabhūta-ratnāya tathāgatāya.*

"By the empowerment of calling the name of Prabhūtaratna Tathāgata, one can destroy the miserly evil karma accumulated over many lifetimes by all the ghosts, and immediately their merit becomes perfected.

*Namo bhagavate surūpāya tathāgatāya.*

"By the empowerment of calling the name of Surūpa Tathāgata, one can destroy the hideous evil forms of the ghosts, and immediately they obtain perfect physical beauty.

*Namo bhagavate vipula-gatrāya tathāgatāya.*

"By the empowerment of calling the name of Vipula-Gātra Tathāgata, one can cause the throats of all the ghosts to become wide, and the food that is given satisfies them to their hearts' content.

*Namo bhagavate abhayaṃ-karāya tathāgatāya.*

"By the empowerment of calling the name of Abhayaṃkara Tathāgata, one can cause all the ghosts' fears to be entirely eliminated, and they are freed from the hungry-ghost destiny."

The Buddha addressed Ānanda: "If a person of good family, having called the names of the four Tathāgatas and made the empowerment, snaps their fingers seven times, takes the food vessel, and upon clean ground extends their arm and pours it out, then by this act of giving, in the four directions there will be hungry ghosts as numerous as hundreds of thousands of nayutas of Ganges sands, and before each one there will be food amounting to seven times seven bushels by the measure of Magadha. Having received this food, all will be fully satisfied. All these ghosts will abandon their ghost bodies and be born in the heavens.

"Ānanda! If any bhikṣu, bhikṣuṇī, upāsaka, or upāsikā constantly uses this secret mantra and the names of the four Tathāgatas to empower food and give it to the ghosts, they will be endowed with immeasurable merit. It is equal to the merit of making offerings to hundreds of thousands of koṭis of tathāgatas, without any difference. Their lifespan will be extended, their physical power and color increased, their roots of merit perfected. All non-human beings, yakṣas, rākṣasas, and evil ghosts and spirits will not dare to harm them. They will also accomplish immeasurable merit and lifespan.

"If one wishes to give to the brahmin sages, fill a clean vessel with clean food and drink, empower it with the preceding secret mantra fourteen times, and cast it into a clean flowing stream. Having done so, one has thereby offered celestial and immortal exquisite food to brahmin sages as numerous as hundreds of thousands of koṭis of Ganges sands. Because those sages receive empowered food, through the power of the secret mantra, each will accomplish their fundamental wishes and various wholesome merits. Each will simultaneously make a vow, saying: 'May the person who gave this food have their lifespan extended, their physical power secure and blissful. May that person's mind and what they see and hear be pure in right understanding, perfectly accomplishing the majestic virtue of Brahmā and the conduct of Brahmā. May the merit also equal making offerings to hundreds of thousands of Ganges-sand-numbered tathāgatas. May all enemies be unable to cause harm.'

"If any bhikṣu, bhikṣuṇī, upāsaka, or upāsikā wishes to make offerings to the Buddha, Dharma, and Saṃgha, they should take incense, flowers, and clean food and drink, empower them with the preceding secret mantra twenty-one times, and present them to the Three Treasures. That good man or good woman will then have offered celestial delicacies of supreme flavor to the Buddha, Dharma, and Saṃgha throughout the ten directions of the Dharma-realm. They will also have accomplished the merit of praising, requesting, and rejoicing. They will constantly be remembered and praised by the buddhas. The devas and benevolent spirits will constantly come to protect them. They will thereby fulfill the pāramitā of giving.

"Ānanda! Follow my words, practice according to the Dharma, and broadly propagate and distribute this, so that all sentient beings may universally see, hear, and obtain immeasurable merit. This is called the 'Dhāraṇī Sūtra for Rescuing the Flaming-Mouth Hungry Ghosts and Suffering Sentient Beings.' By this name you should receive and uphold it."

The entire great assembly, together with Ānanda, having heard what the Buddha taught, wholeheartedly believed, received it with joy, and put it into practice.
