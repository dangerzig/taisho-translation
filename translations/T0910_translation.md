# 梵天擇地法
## Brahmā's Method for Selecting a Site

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0910

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown

---

According to the Mahābrahmā-rāja Inner Secret Sūtra: when the Tathāgata was on the causal ground, for five million lifetimes he was a mantra-holding sage. Whenever he traveled through the mountains and dwelt in secluded, pure chambers, he always attracted the various Brahmā devas who came to attend and guard him. Devas, nāgas, and the eight classes of beings also came and manifested their forms. Yet even though auspicious signs appeared, he was still plagued by Māra; the Dharma-rites did not succeed. This was either because the mantras were recited without following the proper sequence, or because the rites of subjugation were improperly performed, or because the maṇḍala site was ill-chosen. For these reasons, the practice often did not succeed. Now, according to the sūtra, there are forty-two methods for selecting a site suitable for constructing a maṇḍala, enabling the mantra-holder to accomplish the rites as wished. Without this method, one wastes one's days in vain.

**First**: Enter a deep mountain stone cave. When all four sides are stone and the center has soil, as if someone had swept the ground, this is a place where a holy person attained the Way. If unusual flowers grow from the center of such ground, the site is of the first rank. Constructing a platform here and reciting mantras will certainly bring a response from all the Tathāgatas. Only a precept-holding bhikṣu may use this site according to the method; without holding the precepts, one must not use this site. Be cautious.

**Second**: In the deep mountains, where great beasts such as tigers and lions sit, and where no grass grows, this is certainly a place where a holy person sat. These beasts are all stūpa-guardian spirits. Constructing a platform on this ground, the Dharma-rites will immediately succeed. When this poor monk entered the deep mountains of Guangzhou in the fifth year of Kaihuang [585 CE], he found such a site. Following the above method, he practiced there. A being appeared whose body was golden and who wore white garments, a great spirit about three zhang in height. This poor monk was startled and afraid. The being said: "I am a spirit of the Great Buddha's Uṣṇīṣa. Seeing you seated upon this blessed ground, I was moved to manifest my form and have come to receive your Dharma."

**Third**: Ascending to the summit of a mountain, if one sees a great stone that is as polished as a mirror and reflects one's image, this is a holy person's auspicious stone. Take white cogon grass for a mat, sit at the northwest corner, and recite mantras and perform the rites: accomplishment will certainly follow. Only a precept-holding bhikṣu may sit there.

**Fourth**: When a mountain site has grass, trees, and forest on all four sides, but the center is bare and clean as if swept by a person, constructing a platform there will certainly succeed.

**Fifth**: In the mountains, if one sees five-colored stones, blue, yellow, red, white, and black, each in its proper direction, this is a place where a holy person practiced the Way. This site is certain to be supreme. Constructing a platform here, the Four Heavenly Kings will come of their own accord.

**Sixth**: In the mountains, if one sees a serpent with horns upon its head coiled upon a stone, and the ground where the serpent rests has no grass, and it rises and departs when people approach, constructing a platform here is most excellent.

**Seventh**: In the mountains, if one sees a white deer lying on the ground, and within a range of one zhang on all sides there is no grass or tree, this ground is also suitable for constructing a platform.

**Eighth**: In the mountains, where one sees a white deer's resting place with no grass or trees on all sides, seeing such a sign indicates ground suitable for constructing a platform.

**Ninth**: In the mountains, if there is a being one zhang or more in height, wearing black garments, with four eyes on its face, seated upon a stone that is so polished and bright that one can see one's face reflected in it, a bhikṣu who ascends there will certainly succeed.

**Tenth**: In the mountains, if there is a white-haired elder whose brows and temples are all white, if one follows his gaze and traces where he walks, and finds that no grass or trees grow upon that ground, the site is suitable for constructing a platform. If there is grass and trees, it is unsuitable.

**Eleventh**: In the mountains, if one sees five-colored clouds rising from stones or earth, visible even when it is not overcast or rainy, this place is certainly where a holy person sat. It is excellent for constructing a platform.

**Twelfth**: In the mountains, if there is a stone chamber where lions and fierce beasts dwell, and no grass or trees grow there, the ground is suitable for constructing a platform.

**Thirteenth**: In the mountains, if there is level ground with no grass, and the ground bears human footprints of about one foot in size, it is suitable for constructing a platform.

**Fourteenth**: Entering a mountain valley, if one sees grass growing flat on the ground as though someone has sat upon it, with the grass-tips bent low, the site is suitable.

**Fifteenth**: On a mountaintop or on level ground below, if unusual flowers appear on all four sides with an open space of about eight feet in the center, the site is excellent.

**Sixteenth**: In the mountains, wherever flowers grow, they mark a place where a holy person sat. Regardless of spring, summer, autumn, or winter, unusual flowers always appear there. Wherever flowers appear that differ from ordinary flowers, the site is excellent.

**Seventeenth**: In the mountains, while reciting mantras, if one sees white light emerging from the ground and there is no grass on all sides, the site is excellent.

**Eighteenth**: In the mountains, if at an unusual time one senses a fragrance, and the ground also seems to emit a haze, the site is excellent.

**Nineteenth**: In the mountains, if five-colored light emerges from the ground or from stones, examine the spot: where the light emerges is excellent.

**Twentieth**: In the mountains, if one sees peacocks or other birds carrying grass and assorted flowers and dropping them at a particular spot, the ground is suitable. It is a place where a holy person appeared. Excellent.

**Twenty-first**: In the mountains, where there is five-colored soil, at dawn the next morning if one sees five-colored light emerging, the site is suitable.

**Twenty-second**: In the mountains, if one sees four white cranes facing one another where no grass grows, this is ground for establishing a platform.

**Twenty-third**: In the mountains, if one sees twin trees standing opposite each other, and between them a shape resembling a human form, the site is suitable.

**Twenty-fourth**: In the mountains, if one sees a yellow or white ox lying upon ground where no grass grows, as if swept by a person, the site is suitable.

**Twenty-fifth**: In the mountains, if there is a stone that, when struck, rings like a bell, the site is suitable.

**Twenty-sixth**: In the mountains, if one sees a cave whose interior, when one calls out, produces no echo, it is excellent. If there is an echo, it is not suitable.

**Twenty-seventh**: In the mountains, if there is a temple, and when one examines the ground at dawn before sunrise one sees five-colored light, it is suitable.

**Twenty-eighth**: In the mountains, if one sees a divine being wearing celestial garments, the ground at the place of its appearance is suitable.

**Twenty-ninth**: In the mountains, if there is an earthen mound like an ant-hill, about five feet high, the site is suitable.

**Thirtieth**: In the mountains, if one sees a stone or earthen formation in the shape of a dragon, or markings on the ground that resemble drawings, it is suitable.

**Thirty-first**: In the mountains, if one sees an immortal or a horned being or beast, examine the ground at the place of its appearance: it is suitable.

**Thirty-second**: At an unusual time and place, if light suddenly appears, or there is a flash of fire, or a fragrant haze, this is ground where an arhat attained the Way. It is suitable.

**Thirty-third**: If one constructs a platform within a temple or in a layperson's home, it is a hundred or a thousand times inferior to a mountain site. If one wishes to save sentient beings, one must ultimately select the proper ground. When digging down two feet within a temple's saṃghārāma and discovering bones, the site is certainly unsuitable. If one finds tiles or stones, one should bury the seven treasures within the ground; only then may the site be used.

**Thirty-fourth**: If one digs three feet deep and discovers a small unusual object or a treasure, this is called superior ground. It is suitable.

**Thirty-fifth**: If one digs two feet deep and the earth contains nothing, one may construct a platform. The soil extracted from the ground should be carefully sorted, mixed with powdered incense, and firmly packed.

**Thirty-sixth**: Ground where no one has ever dwelt, slept, or lain is suitable.

**Thirty-seventh**: Ground where no pits, cellars, ovens, or resting places for chickens and dogs have ever been is suitable.

**Thirty-eighth**: At the fifth watch, examine the vapors of the ground. If there is a dark light, it is unsuitable. If the light that emerges is of any other kind, it is suitable.

**Thirty-ninth**: If one constructs a platform in a layperson's home, it mostly fails to accord with the proper method and results in harm. Neither the mantra-master nor the patron derives any benefit. One should simply establish the boundary without constructing a platform. This poor monk has tested this and has suffered losses.

**Fortieth**: If a platform must be established, have the ill person purify themselves, bathing daily and wearing fresh, clean clothing, and sit separately in a room. The mantra-master should select the ground as before and construct the platform. Concentrate single-mindedly, invoke the name, and recite the Dharma. The ill person must not be allowed to see the inside of the platform. If the ill person is brought within the platform, it will harm the mantra-master.

**Forty-first**: If a precept-holding bhikṣu of the highest order recites mantras and constructs a platform, the result is a hundred thousand times superior to that of a layperson. Once the proper ground has been selected and a platform constructed, even when the cosmic conflagration arises at the end of the kalpa, that ground will not be destroyed.

**Forty-second**: If constructing a water-platform, there is no need for elaborate preparations, provided one has selected a supremely excellent site. If one does not obtain the right ground, there will ultimately be no beneficial result.
