# 寶藏天女陀羅尼法
## Dhāraṇī Method of the Treasure-Treasury Celestial Maiden

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1282

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown.

---

At that time, the Lord of the Thirty-Three Heavens, Maheśvara Deva-king, together with the celestial maiden assembly, praised the Treasure-Treasury Deva-king named Ṭārākha, who possesses great majestic power and incomparable beauty and strength. He can perform supremely excellent deeds in the world: turning the heavens, shaking the earth, toppling mountains, and overturning seas. Victory and defeat in battle are accomplished as he wills. Wealth, silk, gold, and jewels are heaped like mountains for the world's use. He then spoke the mantra:

*Oṃ ṭārākha ṭārākha vitāra-nī svāhā.*

If one wishes to receive and uphold this method, first paint the form of a celestial maiden. The paste-sandalwood method: for seven days before the fifth day of the fifth month, eat thin porridge as a strict fast, and the place where one intends to go must be clean and pure. Whenever practicing this method, the celestial maiden comes of her own accord without being summoned; however, one must concentrate and contemplate to demonstrate utmost sincerity. This method must be undertaken on the night of the fifth day of the fifth month, and the fruit of accomplishment will certainly be achieved.

On that night, concentrate in meditative contemplation, reciting the mantra 1,080 times without haste. At the third watch, the mind will become hazy as if drowsy. The celestial maiden will send various maidens to test and play with the practitioner; one must not speak with them under any circumstances. At the second time, the celestial maiden Ṭārākha herself will come and appear. The practitioner must not rush forward, but should kneel, join the palms, bow at the crown, and prostrate twice. She will ask: "What do you need?" The practitioner should respond freely, requesting excellent medicines, immortality and eternal life, spiritual communion, knowledge of all worldly affairs, and lifelong devotion. The celestial maiden may desire to become husband and wife. Hearing this, she will be greatly delighted, and that very night will consummate the union. All wishes will be fulfilled as desired. If one wishes instead to become siblings, the matter must be clearly stated, and one must not harbor doubt.

The altar method: two and a half cubits in measurement. Coat the ground with cow dung, then apply fragrant paste, and decorate with five-colored drawings. Use five-colored silk, each piece two *chi* and one *cun*. Within the altar, install a seat for the celestial maiden. One tray each of ghee, honey, milk, and fruits. Five each of the five-colored flower-cakes. Burn mixed grain and wheat as flowers, with offerings as desired.

The method for painting the image: the celestial maiden is two *chi* and five *cun* tall, with a flower crown on the head. The flowers adorning her are supremely beautiful. She wears a purple robe, golden belt, and black boots. The right hand holds a lotus flower; the left hand holds a wish-fulfilling jewel. The celestial maiden is of such surpassing beauty and radiance that no painter in the world can adequately depict her. One must employ a skilled painter, and the price must not be haggled over.

If accomplished in a timely manner, one will gain spiritual communion and sovereign freedom of mind, overflowing wealth, advancement to high official rank, thorough understanding of all fortune and misfortune, and the joyful delight of both noble and humble, male and female. Whenever any action is undertaken, all ghosts and spirits will come to attend and greet one as if reverently receiving a celestial official, with awe and admiration, and one will be unimpeded. Even the finest arts of the world will be accomplished as one desires. If an evil person comes to do harm, simply call out, and the enemies will each bind themselves and beat themselves. When one says "Release," they are released. The world's lost things, deaths, and events will be verified in real time. All diseases before one's eyes will be cured by simply directing one's intention.

Furthermore, for the eight mantras, one must in each case bathe purely and put on fresh clean garments. Before a buddha image, recite each mantra twenty-one times, and only then perform the method.

The first mantra:

*Oṃ hiṃ-bhārāti-ṭa jī jī svāhā.*

The second mantra:

*Oṃ barā-ti-ṭa-rī-mana-rā svāhā.*

The third mantra:

*Oṃ rakṣa-na-ti-ṭa-sa-ṭa svāhā.*

The fourth mantra:

*Oṃ bhabhā-rai-ja-ti-ṭa svāhā.*

The fifth mantra:

*Oṃ bhujjara-ti-ṭa svāhā.*

The sixth mantra:

*Oṃ yakṣa yakṣa dīnabha svāhā.*

The seventh mantra:

*Oṃ cāti-vija-ye hūṃ phaṭ svāhā.*

The eighth mantra:

*Oṃ jara-na-yā-ri-bha-ti-ṭa svāhā.*

The mudrā: bend the middle finger and ring finger, press both nails with the thumb. Extend the index finger and little finger and place them against the heart. The right hand holds the rosary for reciting the mantra. This is called the Jade-Ring Mudrā.
