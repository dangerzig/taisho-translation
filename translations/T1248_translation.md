# 北方毘沙門天王隨軍護法真言
## Mantra of the Northern Vaiśravaṇa Heavenly King's Dharma-Protection Following the Army

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1248

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

The root mantra:

*Namo ratna-trayāya atha garu-bhūta-rāya diśaya vaiśravaṇāya mahā-rājāya yakṣa-adhipataye bhāsa-duṭha-sa-śra-para-sahana-ya himā-daṣṭa-ṣṭa-viparaśā-yame-pa-da-yathā pau-ṣṇi pau-ṣṇi kuśā-vaiśravaṇāya mahā-rāja-ya kī-badha-ganī-tarā-ma-cidu mama-da-sa-sādhaya-sa namo bhagavate sidhentu-ma-tara-padanī svāhā*

If a practitioner wishes to uphold this mantra, one must first paint an image. The pigments used must not contain any glue. Upon white cotton cloth, paint Vaiśravaṇa, adorned with seven-jewel garments and armor. The left hand holds a halberd, and the right hand rests on the hip. Beneath the deity's feet, paint two yakṣa ghosts, their bodies entirely black. Vaiśravaṇa's face should be painted in a most fearsome form, with wrathful eyes gazing upon all ghosts and spirits in a commanding posture.

His tower is offered to Śākyamuni Buddha. "I command you: when leading celestial soldiers to guard the boundaries and protect the realm, how shall you protect my dharma?" He then dispatched the third son Naṭa to carry it and follow, never leaving his side. "Your eyes are venomous and wrathful; I fear they may harm sentient beings."

At that time, when the practitioner recites this mantra, they should select good ground free from defilement, making it extremely clean and pure. Plaster it with cow dung, then apply fragrant mud. Scatter various flowers and burn incense as offerings. The practitioner's upper and lower garments must all be clean and pure. One should bathe each time after using the latrine. Beginning on the fifteenth night of the bright fortnight, recite the mantra before the image to a full one hundred thousand times. Afterward, apply more fragrant mud to the altar, scatter excellent flowers within it, and set out various food and drink. Take one hundred and eight pieces of frankincense, recite the mantra once per piece and scatter them into the fire. At that time Vaiśravaṇa will manifest in person.

If the practitioner sees the deity, offer the finest food and drink. By Vaiśravaṇa's supernatural power, whatever the practitioner wishes in their heart, the deity sees it all. If the practitioner does not see the deity, a voice will come from space saying: "Simply maintain your mind in recitation, and all things you desire will accord with your wishes." If one neither sees the deity nor hears the voice, but there is a great wind or clouds that spread and fill the altar, the method has been accomplished. If none of the above signs appear, the practitioner should recite the mantra seven times. If they are able to bind someone who enters, the method is accomplished. Have no further doubts.

**Additional Methods**

"If the practitioner wishes to gain a king's love and respect, take red beans one at a time, recite the spell once per bean and cast it into the fire. Complete one hundred and eight times, and the king will urgently send someone to summon you.

"If one wishes to gain a high official's love and respect, take white mustard seeds one at a time, recite the spell once per seed and cast it into the fire, completing one hundred and eight times. The official will come of their own accord with great delight and respect.

"On the fifteenth day of the bright fortnight, make an altar eight cubits square before the image. Plaster the ground with cow dung, then apply fragrant mud again. Paint a triple-bordered precinct in five colors. Within the center of the altar, paint one great wheel. At the four corners, place crossed vajras in a cross pattern. In the second precinct: on the east side paint a fire-jewel with flaming radiance; on the south side paint a three-pronged vajra; on the west side paint a nāga with lotus flowers emerging from its mouth; on the north side paint a great chain. In the third precinct: on the east side paint a lion; on the south side paint a nāga king; on the west side paint a peacock king bird; on the north side paint a rākṣasa standing with a staff. At the four corners of the altar, install fragrant water vases containing mixed fruits and willow branches. The spell-master should bathe, don new clean garments, and before the image set out various food and drink as offerings. Then recite the mantra. Take one *harītakī* fruit, recite the spell once per fruit and cast it into the fire, completing one hundred and eight times. From that day forward, one gold piece will be delivered to the spell-master daily, and there will never be any shortage. If one obtains this wealth, it must be spent in full and not hoarded.

"Recite the spell over ghee, honey, and fruit one hundred and eight times and burn them in the fire. All devas will be greatly delighted and will guard and protect the spell-master.

"If one wishes to travel far, recite the spell over a wooden staff, and all dangerous places will be traversed without fear.

"If an evil person causes trouble, visualize the form of this spell-deity in one's mind, then raise the mind and recite the spell. Before completing even one recitation, that person will be unable to move. If one wishes them well, release them in one's mind and they will be freed.

"If one wishes an enemy to be unable to remain in the same place, take one hundred and eight neem seeds, recite the spell once per seed and cast it into the fire. That person will immediately depart far away.

"If one recites the spell over sesame one hundred and eight times and scatters it into the fire, all people will come to make offerings to the spell-master.

"If one recites the spell over white mustard seeds one hundred and eight times and scatters them in the mountains, all fierce beasts will be unable to harm people. Any wild animal that sees a person will immediately prostrate itself and dare not cause harm. After the person has passed, it will rise again.

"If one wishes to pray for rain, take one hundred and eight apricot pits and recite the spell twenty-one times over all of them, then cast them into water where a nāga dwells. Rain will certainly fall. If one wishes to stop the rain, take paulownia seeds and burn them in the fire, and the rain will stop.

"If the practitioner wishes to beg for food, first recite the spell over the alms bowl one hundred and eight times. Wherever one goes, food and drink will naturally be obtained. Any remaining food will disappear on its own. If one needs food and drink, face the bowl with utmost sincerity and recite the spell once, and the bowl will be as before.

"If one wishes to see Guṇadevatā, fast for one day and one night. Before a buddha image, burn storax incense. Recite the spell over white flowers one hundred and eight times and scatter them before the deity. Guṇadevatā will manifest in person. Whatever is needed, all wishes will be fulfilled, and whatever wealth and treasures are sought will be obtained as desired.

"If one wishes to repel all ghosts and spirits, recite the spell over white mustard seeds and ghee, one per recitation, and cast them into the fire.

"If there are officials who harbor unwholesome minds toward the Three Jewels or torment the Buddha's disciples, and one wishes to cause them to generate compassion, one must perform subjugation. Recite the spell over one's hand thirty-one times and strike from a distance. That person will immediately fall ill. If one wishes them well, recite once with a compassionate mind and they will be cured.

"Recite the spell over earth twenty-one times and scatter it upon one's own body. All people who see you will revere and admire you. If one wishes one's own body to possess awesome radiance, recite the spell over ink and apply it to the forehead. All who see you will love and admire you. Recite the spell over ash twenty-one times and apply it to the body. In the midst of a great assembly, all who see you will perceive the appearance of merit and virtue, and the assembly will make offerings.

"If one wishes to travel a long distance without being harmed by evil people or fierce beasts, with the speed of a galloping horse, recite the spell over the left thumb, form the mudrā, and depart.

"If one wishes to use a mirror to see events, recite the spell over a boy or girl and ask about fortune and misfortune. The person will speak of their own accord, telling the name of the ghost causing the illness, and one will know what kind of disease it is.

"If one has been poisoned by *gū*-magic, recite the spell over the consumed food twenty-one times, and the poison will be vomited out.

"If one suffers from ghost-illness, recite the spell over a five-colored thread, tying one knot per recitation for one hundred and eight times. Tie it to the head, crown, or arm, and all illnesses will be eliminated.

"If one suffers from heart pain, recite the spell over pomegranate flower juice and drink it for immediate cure.

"If one suffers from fox-spirit possession, recite the spell over a five-colored thread. Have a child twist it into a cord, reciting once per knot for one hundred and eight times. Tie it below the neck, then recite the spell over a willow switch and strike the patient for immediate cure.

"If one suffers from bone and joint pain, recite the spell over a knife and seal the affliction; it will be cured.

"For body protection, recite the spell over white mustard seeds twenty-one times. Ash or water may also be used. Scatter them in the four directions to establish a boundary.

"If one suffers from headache or body pain, recite the spell over ghee or sesame oil twenty-one times and massage it onto the body for immediate cure. If one suffers from ear-wind, recite the spell over ghee twenty-one times and consume it for cure. If one suffers from eye ailments, recite the spell over apricot-kernel oil and apply it for immediate cure. If one suffers from swelling, recite the spell over pomegranate rind one hundred and eight times and apply it for immediate cure; white sandalwood may also be used. If one suffers from headache, recite the spell over rhubarb twenty-one times and apply it to the forehead for immediate cure. If one suffers from seasonal illness, recite the spell over powdered costus root twenty-one times, mix with water, and drink for immediate cure. If one suffers from evil sores, scabies, or ringworm, take veratrum powder mixed with oil, apply it, and it will be immediately cured. If one suffers from malaria, recite the spell over a willow branch twenty-one times and have the patient struck with it for immediate cure. If stung by a scorpion, recite the spell over dried meat, burn it in the fire, and while hot press it to the sting for immediate cure. If bitten by a snake, recite the spell over long pepper twenty-one times and apply for immediate cure. If a woman has swollen breasts, recite the spell over sesame, chew it, and apply to the affected area for cure. If one suffers from vaginal discharge, recite the spell over clove water and drink for immediate cure. If a person is suddenly seized by a ghost and speaks madly, recite the spell over water and have them drink it. If one suffers from heart pain, recite the spell over yellow earth and apply it. If one suffers from any ghost-spirit possession, recite the spell over a pomegranate-wood staff one hundred and eight times and strike the patient for immediate cure.

"If one wishes to accomplish the three types of attainment, before the image make an altar. Within the altar, scatter various fine flowers and set out various food and drink. Take one red copper vessel and three *liang* of realgar, finely ground, mixed with honey-water to form pills. Place them before the image, cover with a lotus leaf, and press one's hand upon it while reciting without ceasing. When signs appear: if smoke rises, apply it to the body and one will attain the philosopher's stone. If fire emerges, take the fire-flame with the hand and apply it to the face. All sentient beings who see this person will be greatly delighted and admiring, possessing great awesome radiance. Whatever instructions one gives, all people will faithfully receive them with respect and compliance. If warmth arises, take it and apply it to the body, and one will obtain inexhaustible wealth.

"Before a śarīra stūpa, recite the spell over ox-yellow. Apply it according to the preceding medicine method to the body. All merit and virtue will gather upon one's body, and all sentient beings in every place will see one as no different from a king.

"If one knows of a mountain cave that resembles an asura cave, recite the spell over white mustard seeds mixed with one's own blood, reciting once per cast against the mountain opening, until a full one thousand and eight times. The cave will open of itself. The asura women, their bodies like blazing fire, will come bearing various incense and flowers to welcome the spell-master. When the practitioner enters this cave, their lifespan will last one great kalpa. Their bodily strength will be like that of an asura, and like vajra, nothing can harm them. If one wishes to depart, one may take all the asura women as one's retinue. If one does not return, one will also survive the final conflagration without harm.

"Take this image before a śarīra stūpa. Plaster the ground with fragrant paste, scatter incense and flowers within the altar, and make excellent offerings. Take a pear-wood box and grind *añjana* stone into powder, use *qin*-glue water to form pills, place them in the box, and install it within the altar. The practitioner should don new clean garments. Before the image, fast for one day, and recite this mantra one thousand and eight times. Without the need for the three signs to appear, upon completing one thousand and eight recitations, apply the pills to both eyes, then to the soles of the feet, the center of the palms, the chest, both shoulders, and the crown. With utmost sincerity, join the palms and contemplate the body as identical to space, without any bodily form. While making this visualization, the practitioner will become invisible, able to travel a thousand *li* in a day, and all affairs and undertakings will be fully perceived.

"If one wishes to subdue non-Buddhist practitioners by entering fire, take one's own blood and fish blood. With a wrathful mind, recite this mantra twenty-one times and cast it into the fire. Then enter the fire and for the duration of one meal the body will not be burned.

"If one wishes to subdue non-Buddhist practitioners, form the mudrā in one's hand. With a wrathful mind, recite the mantra one hundred and eight times, and all methods practiced by the non-Buddhists will be destroyed and fail.

"If one wishes to subdue the soldiers and bandits of various kingdoms, paint one image wearing purple-gold armor. In a pure room, burn various fine incenses, olibanum, and frankincense, and make offerings of various colored incense, flowers, food, and drink. With a sincere mind, recite the Heavenly King mantra one hundred thousand times. The Heavenly King will lead celestial soldiers to provide aid, and the enemy forces of other kingdoms will retreat and scatter of their own accord. If one can recite day and night without ceasing, the Heavenly King will dispatch Prince Dujian to lead one thousand celestial soldiers to guard and protect without ever leaving one's side. All wishes will accord with one's will, and whatever one contemplates will be accomplished."

"If one wishes to subdue the enemy forces before one, in a pure room observe a fast and paint one image of the Heavenly King wearing purple-gold armor. Hang it on a pole two *zhang* tall. Fifty paces before the army, point it at the enemy, and the enemy will be unable to cause harm."

**Mudrā**

Hook both little fingers together. Both ring fingers stand upright facing outward. Both middle fingers and both index fingers cross in reverse. Both thumbs stand upright facing outward, with palms back to back: this is the mudrā. All the various methods described above should use this mudrā for accomplishment. I now proclaim the root mudrā. With both hands, cross the right over the left with fingers interlocked inward. Raise both ring fingers with tips touching. Bend both index fingers like hooks. When inviting, hook toward the body. When dispatching, push outward. When reciting, form the mudrā at the heart, recite seven times, then release above the crown. Then take up the rosary and recite with focused attention.

Next, the mudrā of Śrī Mahādevī: join both palms with hollow center. Open both index fingers, both middle fingers, and both ring fingers, bending them like a lotus shape. Both thumbs and both little fingers stand upright and touching. When reciting, form the mudrā at the heart, recite the mantra seven times, and release above the crown.

The *Sūtra of the Four Heavenly Kings* states: "If anyone recites the mantra of the Northern Vaiśravaṇa, Heavenly King, and makes offerings of melons, fruits, food, and drink, on the fifteenth and twenty-first of each month, with music played on single and double strings, gongs, and drums, with dancing, and with ghee, honey, and milk porridge, reciting with a sincere mind, the Heavenly King will manifest in person and fulfill whatever one's heart desires."

The defilement-purification dhāraṇī:

*Oṃ kurudhana oṃ*

Praise for the Heavenly King:

> "Benefiting the myriad beings: that is the Northern Quarter.
> Protecting and upholding the realm: that is our Heavenly King.
> His noble generals, entrusted with command, firmly guard the borders.
> With devoted hearts fulfilling their duties, they bestow blessings and auspiciousness.
> His stellar palace surpasses all, towering above the lofty ridges.
> His sacred image stands in majesty, adorned with myriad treasures.
> His awesome spirit blazes and shines, subjugating the untamed wilds.
> Loyal and steadfast, he supports and aids the holy Tang."
