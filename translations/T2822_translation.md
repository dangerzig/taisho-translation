# Essential Words of the Great Vehicle

## *Dàchéng Yàoyǔ* (大乘要語)

Taishō 2822

Author unknown. A Dunhuang Chan anthology of essential sayings.

*Note: This is a compilation of Chan/Zen essential phrases and verses, likely assembled from the sayings of various masters. The text blends doctrinal passages with paradoxical Chan dialogues and verse. Much of the language draws on Yogācāra and Prajñāpāramitā thought.*

Water and oil share the same form, but unless one is a farmer, one cannot distinguish them. When conditions come, things appear; when conditions go, things are not manifest. Clearly know coming and going; do not discriminate according to appearances.

The lion chases the person, not the clod of dirt. The dog chases the clod of dirt, not the person.

The mind of bodhi is as small as the tip of a hair. Buddha-nature is not existent, like empty space; nor non-existent, like the horns of a hare.

It is like two elephants whose strength is balanced. If one is wounded, it retreats and does not fight again. The lion walks alone, driving out the six thieves. The Chan master shoots and kills the five rebel kings.

Another question: "Does the stone woman's child have a child?" Answer: "The stone woman's child does not know how to give birth to the way. Does it not exist?" "Since she has a child, where does it go?" Answer: "It becomes a bee." "Where does it become a bee?" "In the gandharva city." "What weapons and provisions does it get?" Answer: "It takes arrows made of turtle fur, a bow made of rabbit horns, and foam gathered from water as provisions." "In the gandharva city, in which corner (east, west, south, or north) does it become a bee?" Answer: "It sits in none of the four corners. Does it sit in the center?" "If it sits in none of the four corners or the center, who sits there?"

The bodhisattva is a merchant. The place of trade is the bodhi-mind. The marketplace is the return home.

If the outflow of desires is not first eliminated, accumulating writings becomes a mistaken excess. Using writings to cure the Buddha: why does the Buddha not cure himself?

Hearing has no reliance. One luminous essence divides into the six harmonious unions. If one faculty stops and rests, the functions of all six do not arise.

In delusion, the awakened mind and the deluded mind are not different. Only because there is a mind does the mind seem different. Using the mind to seek the Buddha requires passing through countless kalpas. Receiving an empty hand, carrying a hoe and hook, walking on foot while riding a water buffalo. Riding the buffalo, crossing the water: the bridge flows but the water does not flow.

Green, green, the emerald willows pervade true suchness. Luxuriant, luxuriant, the yellow flowers are none other than prajñā.

Thought and awareness do not contradict each other. When the illness is exhausted, the medicine does not arise. Still flying without awareness.

If you see this thing, you can see my seeing. If you see the same as I see, this is called seeing me. If I am not seen at this time, why do you not see my seeing? If seeing is not-seeing, it is naturally beyond comparison.

This is the sun.

When hearing subdues the root of obscuring film, and the dust and details are all perceived as pure and clean, when purity reaches its ultimate and light penetrates, quiescent illumination merges with empty space.

Then come back and observe the world: it is like an event in a dream. Only what awakening illuminates is called "awakened illumination." If you awaken but there is no illumination, this is called "illuminated awakening." If awakening is not named and what is called "awakening" is without ignorance, then having something present is not awakening. Ignorance is not named "ignorance." Having non-awakening is actually the bright nature.

If you know it is only consciousness manifesting, departing from what the mind obtains, discrimination does not appear before you, and one does not abide in its nature.

The five aggregates are a thicket of honey. True suchness is level ground. One cloud drops dew and rain. The three grasses compete in clarity.

Turbid yet always not turbid. Clear yet not clear either. Clear and turbid, joined or not? Hence it is called "arising of non-arising."

One awakening is original awakening. The principle of not yet knowing original awakening is like someone who obtains a gold coin: what is obtained is also not obtained.

Space gives rise to the great enlightenment within. Like seaweed growing in the ocean, the lands of outflowing motes of dust all arise from empty space. When the bubbles vanish, space is originally without anything. How much more so the three realms of existence?

If dharmas arise, then make the view of cessation disappear. If dharmas cease, then make the view of cessation and existence disappear.

The mind's function follows the nature of conditions; the principle is the true pure nature. The function of the mind cannot be destroyed. Illuminating the true gold: habitual tendencies do not leave the mind. The mind also does not resemble the mind. Both are but momentary habits of what came before. Their substance has no difference.

Tying and untying share the same cause. Sages and ordinary beings have no two paths. It is like observing the nature of a tent pole: both emptiness and existence are negated. What is not firm becomes firm when awakening is well established in inversion.

From the extremity of afflictions and distress, one accomplishes the unsurpassed way.

> Dharmas arising from causes and conditions:
> This meaning is cessation, not arising.
> Cessation of all arising and ceasing:
> This meaning is arising, not ceasing.

What you all see and hear: conditions produce no different arising. The six sense-objects are all said to have characteristics that differ in arising. In the nature, characteristics are known. In function, characteristics are all the same. The two aspects of error are equal. Take the knowledge of false sounds.

If you know the previous thought is an illness, do not give rise to the subsequent thought. If you give rise to a subsequent thought, it is still the same as not being awake.

> The arousing of the aspiration and the ultimate:
> These two are not different.
> Of these two minds, the first mind is difficult.
> The first arousing of aspiration makes one a teacher of humans and devas,
> Surpassing śrāvakas and pratyekabuddhas.

> The bodhisattva is a cool moon,
> Roaming in the sky of ultimate emptiness.
> When the water of sentient beings' minds is pure,
> The reflection of bodhi appears within.

> The body is the bodhi tree;
> The mind is the bright mirror's stand.
> From time to time wipe it clean,
> And do not let dust and grime collect.

Without a tree, yet there is a reflection; the tracks of a bird in the wind-road. These sights are all difficult to discern. What can be dispelled and what is created.

> Color is also not the dharma of color.
> To see the Tathāgata in that:
> That difficulty is also like this.

> A thousand kinds of leaves upon the tree;
> The root grows from an empty place.
> The wind blows but the leaves do not move;
> The sun shines and they all gleam clearly.

If you wish to see with perfect clarity, you need only see clearly into the principle. It is like a person who, upon recognizing a thief, cannot be deceived.

> Taming what is difficult to tame,
> Liberating what Māra has bound.
> Transcendently seated upon the ground of the stages,
> Recognizing the aggregates is parinirvāṇa.

> The nature of the ocean of awakening is clear and round.
> Clear and round, awakening is without anything wonderful.
> Ignorance illuminates birth and death;
> Then it establishes the view and what is hoped for.
