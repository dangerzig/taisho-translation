# 佛說舍利弗悔過經
## Sūtra on Śāriputra's Repentance, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1492

Translated from the Chinese. Later Han dynasty, translated by An Shigao (安世高).

---

The Buddha was at Rājagṛha, on Mount Gṛdhrakūṭa, together with one thousand two hundred fifty bhikṣus and one thousand bodhisattvas, all seated in assembly.

The foremost disciple Śāriputra rose, came forward, knelt, and with palms joined asked the Buddha: "If good men and good women wish to seek the Way of the Buddha, yet have committed evil in former lives, how should they repent of it?"

The Buddha said: "Excellent, excellent, Śāriputra! That you are concerned for the devas and the people is truly fine."

The Buddha said: "If good men or good women wish to seek the path of the arhat, or the path of the pratyekabuddha, or the Way of the Buddha, or wish to know the affairs of past and future, they should always at dawn, midday, sunset, the first watch, midnight, and cockcrow, bathe and rinse, arrange their clothing, and with palms joined, bow in reverence to the ten directions. Facing whatever direction they choose, they should speak words of repentance:

'We have, from innumerable kalpas of past lives until the present, committed transgressions and evils. We repent of the lust we have committed, the anger we have committed, and the delusion we have committed. When we did not know the Buddha, when we did not know the Dharma, when we did not know the bhikṣu-saṃgha, when we did not know good from evil: if the body committed transgressions, if speech committed transgressions, if the mind committed transgressions, if thought committed transgressions. If in thought we wished to harm the Buddha, or envied and detested the sūtras and the Way, or caused schism in the bhikṣu-saṃgha, or killed an arhat, or ourselves killed our father or mother.

'If we committed the three of body, four of speech, and three of mind: ourselves taking life, teaching others to take life, or seeing others take life and rejoicing on their behalf. Ourselves stealing, teaching others to steal, or seeing others steal and rejoicing on their behalf. Ourselves deceiving others, teaching others to deceive, or seeing others deceive and rejoicing on their behalf. Ourselves engaging in divisive speech, teaching others divisive speech, or seeing others' divisive speech and rejoicing on their behalf. Ourselves uttering abuse, teaching others to abuse, or seeing others' abuse and rejoicing on their behalf. Ourselves speaking falsehood, teaching others falsehood, or seeing others' falsehood and rejoicing on their behalf. Ourselves being jealous, teaching others to be jealous, or seeing others' jealousy and rejoicing on their behalf. Ourselves being greedy, teaching others to be greedy, or seeing others' greed and rejoicing on their behalf. Ourselves being without faith, teaching others to be without faith, or seeing others' faithlessness and rejoicing on their behalf. Ourselves not believing that doing good brings good results and doing evil brings evil results, or seeing others do evil and rejoicing on their behalf. Ourselves stealing sacred property from a Buddhist temple or the possessions of the bhikṣu-saṃgha, teaching others to steal, or seeing others steal and rejoicing on their behalf. Ourselves using false weights, short measures, and crooked rulers to cheat people, or using heavy weights, large measures, and long rulers to exploit people, or seeing others exploit people and rejoicing on their behalf. Ourselves deliberately committing crimes, teaching others to commit crimes, or seeing others commit crimes and rejoicing on their behalf. Ourselves being wickedly rebellious, teaching others to be rebellious, or seeing others be rebellious and rejoicing on their behalf.

'Throughout all the rebirths our bodies have experienced in the five destinies: when in the hells, when among the animals, when among the hungry ghosts, when among the humans, when among the heavenly beings. In whichever of these five destinies we were born, we repent of all transgressions and evils committed: not being filial to parents, not being filial to teachers, not being respectful to good friends, not being respectful to good śramaṇas and practitioners of the Way, not being respectful to elders. Treating parents with contempt, treating the teacher-father with contempt, treating those who seek the arhat path with contempt, treating those who seek the pratyekabuddha path with contempt, or slandering and envying them.

'Seeing the Way of the Buddha and calling it wrong; seeing the evil path and calling it right; seeing what is correct and calling it incorrect; seeing what is incorrect and calling it correct. We pray to the buddhas of the ten directions for compassion and repentance of all our transgressions and evils, that in this present life we may not suffer the consequences of these transgressions, and that in future lives also we may not suffer these consequences.

'The reason we pray to the buddhas of the ten directions for compassion is this: the buddhas can see through all things and hear all things. We dare not deceive before the buddhas. We have transgressions and evils and dare not conceal them. From now on, we will never dare to commit them again.'"

The Buddha said to Śāriputra: "If good men or good women do not wish to fall into the hells, the animal realm, or the hungry ghost realm, they should repent of all transgressions they have committed and not conceal them. After receiving the precepts, they should not commit evil again. Those who do not wish to be born in the borderlands where there is no Buddha, no sūtras, no bhikṣu-saṃgha, no righteous principle, and no distinction of good and evil, should all repent and not conceal. Those who do not wish to be foolish, deaf, blind, or mute; those who do not wish to be born in a household of butchers, fishermen, hunters, or prison wardens; those who do not wish to be reborn in a poor family: all should repent and not conceal. Women who wish to be reborn as men should all repent. Those who wish to attain the path of the stream-enterer and no longer fall into the hells or hungry ghost realm should all repent. Those who wish to attain the path of the once-returner and ascend to the heavens and become human, those who wish to attain the path of the non-returner and ascend to the twenty-fourth heaven, those who wish to attain the arhat's nirvāṇa, those who wish to attain the arhat's path within this world, those who wish to attain the pratyekabuddha's path, those who wish to know the affairs of past and future: all should repent and not conceal."

The Buddha said to Śāriputra: "Good men and good women should each, three times by day and three times by night, bow and prostrate, performing obeisance to the buddhas of the ten directions, and say:

'May the buddhas of the ten directions hear our words. The buddhas of the ten directions have, with upright and balanced teaching, turned the people of all under heaven, wherever the sun and moon shine, to do good. The Buddha rains the sūtras and the Way upon all under heaven, just as the rain of heaven makes the hundred grains and grasses and trees all flourish. Because the Buddha rains the sūtras and the Way upon all under heaven, there arise kings and lords, the Four Heavenly Kings, and beings up to the thirty-three heavens who are noble, wealthy, and happy. The Buddha produces stream-enterers, once-returners, non-returners, and arhats.

'We pray that the buddhas of the ten directions hear our words: Whatever good or evil is done by the people of all under heaven and by creatures that fly and creep, if there are those who give in charity, who uphold the Way with diligence and do not transgress the sūtra-precepts, who with a compassionate mind think of the people, who do immeasurable good, who give to bodhisattvas and the bhikṣu-saṃgha, who give to ordinary people and the destitute, down to those who show compassion to animals: we encourage them in doing good and share in their joy.

'For all the people whom the buddhas of the past have delivered to nirvāṇa, we all share in their joy. For all whom the buddhas of the future will teach to do good, to leave behind the five evils and the path of birth and death, enabling them to attain the arhat's and pratyekabuddha's path, we all share in our encouragement and joy, wishing them to do good and become like the Buddha.

'For all whom the buddhas of the ten directions now present are delivering, teaching people to give in charity and not transgress the sūtra-precepts, to have compassion for people and for creatures that fly and creep, enabling them to be liberated from the hells, the animal realm, the hungry ghost realm, and foolish poverty, enabling them to attain the paths of stream-enterer, once-returner, non-returner, arhat, and the pratyekabuddha's nirvāṇa: we all encourage them in doing good and share in their joy.

'For all the bodhisattvas of the past who had not yet attained buddhahood, who practiced the six pāramitās and did good, who practiced dāna-pāramitā in giving, who practiced śīla-pāramitā in not transgressing the precepts, who practiced kṣānti-pāramitā in patience, who practiced vīrya-pāramitā in diligence, who practiced dhyāna-pāramitā in one-pointed concentration, who practiced prajñā-pāramitā in wisdom, completing the six pāramitās: for all the bodhisattvas of the past who practiced the six pāramitās, we encourage them in joy and share in their joy. For all the bodhisattvas of the future who will practice the six pāramitās, we encourage them in joy and share in their joy. For all the bodhisattvas now present who practice the six pāramitās, we encourage them in joy and share in their joy.

'All the merit we have obtained, we distribute to the people of all under heaven in the ten directions, to our fathers and mothers, to creatures that fly and creep, to those with two feet, to those with four feet, to those with many feet: may all attain buddhahood.'

The merit thereby gained surpasses that of a person who takes the gold, silver, and treasures from the four great cities and gives them in charity, by a hundredfold, a thousandfold, ten thousandfold, a hundred millionfold."

The Buddha said to Śāriputra: "Good men and good women, three times by day and three times by night, should prostrate and bow to the buddhas of the ten directions, saying:

'May the buddhas of the ten directions hear our words. If there are buddhas of the ten directions who, having attained buddhahood, do not yet teach the sūtras, we now encourage and exhort them to teach the sūtras for the devas and the people and for creatures that fly and creep, enabling them to be liberated from the hells, the animal realm, the hungry ghost realm, and foolish poverty, enabling them to attain the path of nirvāṇa. For all in the ten directions who are about to enter parinirvāṇa, we pray for compassion: please do not yet enter parinirvāṇa. Let the devas and the people and creatures that fly and creep obtain their blessings, and let all be liberated from the hells and the hungry ghost realm.'"

The Buddha said to Śāriputra: "In our past lives, when we were bodhisattvas, we encouraged and urged the buddhas to teach the sūtras and not yet enter parinirvāṇa. Because of this, we became a buddha. The first was the Four Heavenly Kings; the second was Śakra, lord of the heavens, who descended, joined his palms, made obeisance, and asked for compassion, requesting that we teach the sūtras and guard the devas and the people. Innumerable devas urged us not yet to enter parinirvāṇa."

The Buddha said to Śāriputra: "Thus the people, in all their varieties, each obtain according to their kind. Doing good, one obtains one's own blessings; doing evil, one obtains one's own calamity."

Śāriputra addressed the Buddha: "If good men or good women wish to seek the Way of the Buddha, what vow should they make to attain it?"

The Buddha said: "Good men and good women should, three times by day and three times by night, prostrate and bow to the buddhas of the ten directions, saying:

'May the buddhas of the ten directions hear. All the merit we have obtained from innumerable kalpas of past lives until now: whether through giving, upholding the sūtras and the Way, maintaining wholesome thoughts, doing good for the Buddha's sake, doing good for the Dharma's sake, doing good for the bhikṣu-saṃgha's sake, doing good for ordinary people's sake, or doing good for animals' sake. Doing evil, one obtains one's own calamity; doing good, one obtains one's own blessings; for evils, one repents. Upholding the sūtra-precepts without transgression; accepting the precepts and not associating with women; encouraging the buddhas, bodhisattvas, and all people to do good; exhorting the buddhas not yet to enter parinirvāṇa.

'All the merit we have obtained since we began studying the Way, we gather, collect, and unite together, and with wholesome mind distribute it to the people of all under heaven in the ten directions, to our fathers and mothers, to creatures that fly and creep: may all obtain their blessings. Whatever small remainder there may be, may we obtain it. May we practice the Way of the Buddha and carry out the Buddha's sūtras. Those who have not yet been delivered, we shall deliver them. Those who have not yet been liberated, we shall liberate them. Those who have not yet attained nirvāṇa, we shall enable them to attain nirvāṇa.'"

The Buddha said to Śāriputra: "Suppose all the men and women under heaven were to become arhats and pratyekabuddhas. If a person were to make offerings to all the arhats and pratyekabuddhas under heaven for a thousand years, would that merit be great?"

Śāriputra said: "To make offerings to even one arhat or pratyekabuddha for a single day, the merit would be immeasurable. How much more so for all the arhats and pratyekabuddhas under heaven for a thousand years?"

The Buddha said: "To make offerings to all the arhats and pratyekabuddhas under heaven for a thousand years does not equal upholding this Sūtra of Repentance and reciting it three times by day and three times by night for a single day. The merit thus obtained surpasses that of making offerings to all the arhats and pratyekabuddhas under heaven, by a hundredfold, a thousandfold, ten thousandfold, a hundred millionfold."
