# 佛說迴向輪經
## Sūtra of the Dedication Wheel, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0998

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Śīladharma (尸羅達摩)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was on the Great Maṇi Vajra Jewel Mountain Peak, together with a great assembly of bodhisattvas. At that time, the World-Honored One addressed Vajra-Maṇi Bodhisattva: "Son of the Buddha! If there is a bodhisattva who seeks great bodhi, who wishes to save and deliver all sentient beings, who has already received the bodhisattva-prātimokṣa precepts, who has studied and has previously generated the great bodhicitta, who has great compassion and always wishes to serve all the Tathāgatas, who wishes to broadly accumulate the merit-provisions of the great bodhisattvas, who wishes to realize the Buddha's wisdom, who wishes through unobstructed samādhi, dhāraṇī, and the gates of liberation to play with supernatural powers, who wishes to make empty space into a great treasury, who wishes to make their own hands like a wish-fulfilling jewel and thus make offerings, such a one should say: 'May all the Buddhas and World-Honored Ones, and all the great bodhisattvas who have attained the ten stages, and all the vajra-holders, be mindful of me! Your disciple so-and-so has generated the bodhicitta but has not yet become established on the right path. I now offer body and mind, at all times and in all ways, to the Buddhas and bodhisattvas of the three times. May the greatly sovereign Buddhas and Tathāgatas, and all the bodhisattvas who have realized the great stages, accept me. May my great merit, wisdom, and provisions, my fierce vigor, vast capacity of mind, my peaceful composure, tamed deportment, and sovereign supernatural powers, and the pāramitās be swiftly perfected.'

"Furthermore, one should say: 'May the Greatly Compassionate One be mindful of me! Your disciple so-and-so has fallen into saṃsāra, bound with great fetters, imprisoned in a jail, separated from the right Dharma, oppressed by the powerful enemies of afflictions, without protector, without savior, without eyes, without support, without general, without guide, wandering on a deviant path, heading toward birth and death with one's back to great nirvāṇa, following the evil destinies and about to fall into a perilous path, on the verge of tumbling into evil ways, constantly encountering evil companions, separated from good spiritual friends, confused about right and wrong, benefit and harm, meaning and non-meaning, far from all goodness and accomplished in non-goodness, constantly turning one's back on the holy ones, all the Buddhas and bodhisattvas, oppressed by birth, old age, sickness, death, grief, and affliction, without peace, without fearlessness, the mind constantly scattered, lacking cultivation of peaceful composure, tamed deportment, generosity, discipline, and non-negligence, far from the two gates of samādhi and dhāraṇī, the Dharma-patience of the ten stages, unable to abide in the profound prajñā-pāramitā and the nature of equality, not cultivating the immeasurable kindness, compassion, sympathetic joy, and equanimity, lacking the bodhisattva's three collections of pure precepts, far from the unobstructed great wisdom, unable to abide in the causes of quiescent cessation and the well-established Dharma, far from supernatural powers, the powers, and fearlessness, not abiding in right mindfulness, unable to accomplish the practices and entries, lacking the Dharma of non-forgetfulness, deficient in all merits, view-attachment and doubt obstructing penetration of the seven factors of awakening, lacking cultivation of the true and real eight aspects of the noble path, oppressed by immeasurable hundreds of thousands of sufferings and afflictions, constantly blazing with the press of manifold sufferings, the mind constantly downcast, resentful, and scattered, constantly following afflictions. May all the Buddhas and bodhisattvas, in accordance with great compassion, rescue and protect me, be my refuge, be my guide. May you cause me to swiftly perfect the great path of right awakening, the provisions of the bodhi-path of great bodhi. Furthermore, may you cause me to swiftly perfect the Tathāgata's ten powers, four fearlessnesses, eighteen uncommon qualities of the Tathāgata, unobstructed eloquence, samādhi, liberation, dhāraṇī-gates, merits, supernatural powers, and the pāramitās. Furthermore, may you grant me all accomplishments and all merits.'

"Next, to turn the vast offering, one should say: 'Furthermore, may all the Buddhas and World-Honored Ones, and all the great bodhisattva-assemblies who have attained great power, be mindful of me! Your disciple so-and-so now turns great reverence, service, and offerings to all the Buddhas and bodhisattvas, offering my body in dedication. In all the worlds of the ten directions, there are great adornments: various jeweled palaces, jewel-adorned jewel-trees, jewel-mountains, jewel-seats, jewel-altars, jewel-arrays, jewel-parasols, jewel-banners, jeweled necklaces, pearl-nets, jewel-vessels, jewel-heaps, accumulated jewel-radiance. In the jeweled continents are great maṇi jewels, pure lamp-trees, jewel-bells ringing harmoniously, maṇi radiance, fine-gold pendants and whisks, draped and connected with wondrous golden lotuses, with pistils of Jāmbūnada gold. Golden tree-rows and various jeweled palaces, jeweled clouds floating densely and raining jewel-drops. In the jewel-treasury halls, wondrous maṇi gems intersperse the walls, with pearl balustrades. The delightful gardens of the great vidyādhara, with houses, jeweled palaces, halls, and groves, all lovely. With all such things, without attachment and without possession, I dedicate them to all the Buddhas and bodhisattvas. In Jambudvīpa, all the wondrous medicines and longevity medicines, all the maṇi jewels that can give beings all the medicines they seek, and the naturally beautiful supreme food and drink. And all the fragrant trees of various kinds: sandalwood trees, agaru trees, aloeswood, red sandalwood, great-leaf everbloom flowers, intoxicating-fragrance and other kinds of trees, with their delightful wondrous fragrances, without attachment and without possession, and the wish-fulfilling trees. In the great ocean, various kinds of maṇi jewels and wish-fulfilling pearls, and Mount Sumeru, Mount Kiṃśara, Fragrant Mountain, Mount Maṇi, the great Mount Indra, Mount Mandāra, the Vajra Mountains, and other great mountains with their various jeweled peaks, jeweled terraces, jeweled stones, jeweled caves, jeweled palaces, jeweled ornaments, jeweled stairs. The maṇi pillars, the jeweled lamp-wheels, the vaiḍūrya and other maṇi varieties. All the ownerless celestial palaces in the heavens, the celestial flowers of delight, the celestial pleasures, the gentle breezes that blow, the celestial harmonies and beautiful sounds from the empty palaces, the celestial music that sounds forth spontaneously without being struck, the celestial birds dwelling in the empty palaces singing wondrous melodies, and the jeweled clouds resounding with great beautiful sounds, the celestial maṇi songs and music. And all the wondrous fragrant-flower trees, garland-trees, unguent-incense and burned-incense trees, lamp-trees, banner-trees, pennant-trees, and jewel-trees. All these, without attachment and without possession, I offer to all the Buddhas and bodhisattvas. All the great offering-clouds beheld by the Buddha-eye in all the Buddha-lands: flower-clouds, incense-clouds, garland-clouds, burned-incense clouds, unguent-clouds, jewel-parasol clouds, jewel-banner clouds, jewel-pennant clouds, jewel-garment clouds, jewel-heap clouds, jewel-vessel clouds, great maṇi-heap clouds, and various wondrous great jewel-pearl clouds. Furthermore, with fragrant oil filling great lamp vessels equal in size to a thousand worlds, and wicks equal to a hundred Mount Sumerus, I offer them to all the Buddhas and World-Honored Ones and all the great bodhisattvas. By the supreme merit arising from these offerings, I will dedicate all toward unsurpassed bodhi. As the Buddhas of the past cultivated all their wholesome deeds, with non-apprehension as the skillful means, dedicating them toward bodhi, so I likewise, holding this merit, dedicate it toward unsurpassed, right, and equal bodhi. I request all the Buddhas: may you proceed to the seat of bodhi, subdue the māra-enemies, realize great bodhi, and turn the great Dharma-wheel. For those about to enter final cessation, for the sake of all sentient beings I request: may you remain for a full great kalpa. I rejoice in all the merits cultivated by all the Buddhas, bodhisattvas, and all sentient beings. I disclose and confess all my transgressions. Just as the Buddhas and bodhisattvas of the three times confess their karmic obstructions, so I likewise pray that my transgressions may be extinguished.' When the offering and confession are complete, with a purified mind, recollect all the Buddhas and bodhisattvas. Before a Buddha image, recite this secret mantra eight times in full. The secret mantra:

"*Oṃ sphara sphara vimana sara mahā-java hūṃ*"

"Having recited the secret mantra, whatever celestial wondrous great offering-clouds one wishes will be accomplished and will appear before all the Buddhas. If good men or good women keep the Buddha in mind, and whether by day or by night they turn and recite this great dedication-wheel, such good men or good women will have their transgressions extinguished, their afflictions will gradually become thin, and they will obtain all the merits described above. If one turns it even once, one obtains the merit of a hundred Brahmā-heavens. One will constantly see the Buddha in dreams, and after death one will be born in a pure land."

When the Buddha had finished teaching this sūtra, the assembly of bodhisattvas joyfully received it and practiced accordingly.
