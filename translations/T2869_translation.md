# 妙好寶車經
## Sūtra of the Wondrous Jeweled Vehicle

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 2869

Translated from the Chinese. Author unknown.

---

At that time there was a bodhisattva who said: "Do not all beings imprisoned in every kind of suffering long to be freed? Do not all the sick long to be cured? Do not those in darkness long to see the light? Is it not like a loving mother longing to see her child? Is it not like a traveler on a distant journey longing for an early return? Is it not like a widow longing for a husband? Is it not like a cripple longing to walk? Is it not like a poor person longing for clothing? Is it not like one who is cold longing for fire? Is it not like a farmer longing for grain? Is it not like a blind person longing to see things? Is it not like a deaf person longing to hear sounds? Is it not like a mute longing to speak? With such various longings and yearnings as these, the way of the Buddha is supremely honored and will never deceive you."

The Buddha said to Jeweled Vehicle Bodhisattva: "I now tell you. South of Mount Sumeru is the land called Jambudvīpa. To the west is Godānīya. To the north is Uttarakuru. To the east is Pūrvavideha. Sentient beings in all four directions shall all hear and know. If they wish to hear, it will be like citizens finding their king, like prisoners freed from jail, like the sick being cured, like those in darkness seeing the light, like a loving mother finding her child, like a distant traveler returning home, like a widow finding a husband, like a cripple being able to walk, like a poor person finding clothing, like one who is cold finding fire, like a farmer harvesting grain, like a blind person gaining sight, like a deaf person being able to hear, like a mute being able to speak. With such various longings and yearnings for the way of the Buddha, the World-Honored One will never deceive you."

Jeweled Vehicle Bodhisattva addressed the Buddha, saying: "World-Honored One, how many years is the Tathāgata's lifespan?"

The Buddha said to Jeweled Vehicle Bodhisattva: "My present lifespan is inconceivable. Towering and majestic, it is coterminous with the sun and moon. All day and all night they circle Mount Sumeru, ending and beginning again. It has neither beginning nor original source. If you wish to hear, the matter is indeed difficult. All dharmas are empty, without anything. There is nothing that permanently abides, nor is there any vessel that is extinguished. It is like east, west, south, and north: neither blue nor red, neither white nor black. It is like an echo: you cannot grasp it."

The Buddha then said to Jeweled Vehicle Bodhisattva: "You should now teach all sentient beings so that all may hear and know. You should teach them to receive and uphold the three refuges and five precepts, the ten wholesome deeds and eight purification observances. Through rebirth they shall all attain the path of stream-entry, the path of once-returning, the path of non-returning, and the path of arhatship, exhausting all outflows with the four paths fully accomplished."

Jeweled Vehicle Bodhisattva again addressed the Buddha, saying: "World-Honored One, even while the Tathāgata is in the world there are many who harbor enmity and jealousy. How much more so after his parinirvāṇa! Those who slander this scripture will after death enter hell, revolving therein with no prospect of escape. How painful! How painful! The pain is beyond words. I now, out of compassion for all of you, have composed this scripture. It is like a blind person who cannot see the sun and moon: this is not the fault of the sun and moon."

> In times of love, one does not see; when love is not seen, love still brings sorrow.
> Therefore do not create love. Enmity arises from evil causes.
> From delight arises love; love can give rise to sorrow.
> If one can sever emotional love, sorrow and grief will not arise.
> Love and delight give rise to enmity; love and delight give rise to fear.
> If one is freed from craving and desire, what sorrow and what fear?
> The heavenly palaces are blissful, boundless, and inexhaustible.
> One must be reborn in those places: there is a beginning but no end.
> Take the wholesome as the wholesome Dharma; cultivating the wholesome is the place of the Way.
> Wholesome deeds serve as the cart-oxen, carrying people into the hall of merit.
> Take the evil as the evil dharma: evil deeds bind one in fetters.
> If in life you do not receive the precepts, in death you enter hell.
> The vehicle of the three refuges and five precepts is not made of mountain wood.
> If you hear the Sūtra of the Jeweled Vehicle, in death you will not enter hell.
> Not in the sky, not in the sea, not within mountains or rocks:
> When it arrives, it cannot be evaded; it will surely come and seize you.
> A red cord binds my shoulders; a black rope ties my throat.
> Where will they take me? They send me to the Eastern Mountain.
> The high mountain rises ten thousand fathoms, precipitous and sheer on every side.
> How many thousands and tens of thousands of years before returning to the Yellow Springs?
> Human life is like morning dew; one's life will not last long.
> If you do not believe my words, just look at the sparks from chiseled stone.
> How is being born different from dying? Departing, one does not cross a bridge.
> In panic at the crossroads of the five destinies, one is stripped of one's garments.
> They hold a seven-foot staff in hand; iron hooks are sharp as barbs.
> The fire-chariot arrives in a flash; there are copper pillars and iron beds.
> Freezing ice severs human bones; at the prison gate, a cauldron of boiling broth.
> The officials sit facing each other; attendants flank from both sides.
> Together they examine the documents, asking about the transgressions of my lifetime.
> Event by event, they determine which path: the pain is unbearable.
> Alas, O God among gods! Is there some method to avoid death?
> Enmity and attachment are like emotional love; they call out that the body is permanent.
> The foolish and ignorant people of this world may not believe this scripture.
> They are like an untamed ox that drags a person east and west.
> The three refuges are like the cart-tracks; the five precepts are like the cart-shafts.
> The ten wholesome deeds form the cart-box; the eight purification observances serve as the cart-rail.
> A straight mind is like the axle; the three knowledges serve as axle-pins.
> Upholding the precepts is like the linchpin; breaking the precepts is like the cart's screen.
> The cart's keen hearing is like a pair of ears; a focused mind is like sitting in dhyāna.
> Expedient means are like the cart's ribs; the covering is like skillful means.
> The buddha-way serves as the cart-hub; the spokes all converge at the front.
> The worthy ones are like the cart-net; the Dharma-teacher is at the māra's gate.
> Backsliding is like a loosened pin; repentance drives the pin firm.
> Diligent effort serves as the cart-oxen, pulling the cart to Brahmā Heaven.
> In Brahmā Heaven's various palaces, radiance is entirely natural.
> Like spring flowers in bloom, their wondrous colors delight all hearts.
> Such meritorious virtues as these are heard through the Sūtra of the Jeweled Vehicle.
> The buddha-way is higher than heaven; merit is weightier than earth.
> The great Bodhisattva Jeweled Vehicle teaches me to practice repentance.
> The foolish and ignorant people of this world do not believe the Sūtra of the Jeweled Vehicle.
> In death they fall into the hells, and hungry ghosts come before them.
> They seize their heads toward the Mountain of Swords; their faces are white as their lips turn blue.
> They do not see the light of sun and moon, but only hear the sound of ghosts being struck.
> Such various kinds of suffering: how dark, how dim!
> In life they did not receive the precepts; when the time comes, what can they plead?
> There are iron-beaked birds that fly down and perch upon their shoulders.
> They lower their heads and peck out the tongue; they raise their heads and pluck out the eyes.
> These people in their former lives, on observance days, spoke evil curses.
> They refused to accept the teacher's instruction or make repentance before the Buddha.
> Today they suffer these punishments: what could they have done to deserve them?

The Buddha said: "If good men and good women, on observance days, before the Three Honored Ones, openly confess and take delight in acknowledging their faults, their transgressions will be extinguished and their merit will be born. They will not undergo the Mountain of Swords, the Forest of Blades, the cauldron of boiling broth, or the furnace of charcoal. They will not pass through the three evil paths. If sentient beings do not encounter the eight difficulties, they shall join hands and clasp wrists with all buddhas and bodhisattvas, entering the various heavenly palaces to observe them all with joy and delight, broadly fulfilling their vast vows."

The Buddha said to Jeweled Vehicle Bodhisattva: "Those who can receive and uphold the Sūtra of Jeweled Vehicle Bodhisattva, even if they enter the great ocean, will be like geese, ducks, mandarin ducks, and phoenixes in the ocean waters, wonderfully startled and wonderfully soaring. Like a person in a hall with lutes, zithers, flutes, and pipes, singing and dancing with performers, joyful and happy. Their merit is immeasurable. Like fish and turtles in the ocean waters, emerging and submerging freely without fear of tigers or wolves. Like facing shadowy demons and goblins: who dares, who can prevail?"

The Buddha said to Jeweled Vehicle Bodhisattva: "You should now teach all sentient beings so that all may hear and know."

The Buddha said: "What is your method for liberating people? What is this matter?"

Jeweled Vehicle answered: "I am able to take in hand the torch of prajñā-wisdom and enter the cave-dwelling of sentient beings' ignorance. I illuminate the blossoming of wholesome roots and reveal the fruit of wisdom and liberation. I use patience as armor and staff, I hold in hand the sharp lance of prajñā, and I use skillful expedient means as a siege-chariot to smash the great citadel of sentient beings' ignorance. I wage war against the five-aggregate māra, I strike the army of birth and death, and I slay the thieves of affliction. Emerging from the realms of aggregates, sense-fields, and elements, I reach the dharma-body's place of the Way. All of this is through the awesome and numinous sword of Jeweled Vehicle Bodhisattva. Such is his towering majesty."

The Buddha said to Jeweled Vehicle Bodhisattva: "You should now attain the mind of anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi. Good men and good women who can receive and uphold the Sūtra of Jeweled Vehicle Bodhisattva and then fall into hell: there is no such possibility. Why? Because one rides the Tathāgata's power and drives the great ship of prajñā to ferry sentient beings across the great river of birth and death, across the shore of the ocean of afflictions, out of the deep abyss of love and desire, over the pits and gullies of ignorance, past the evil paths of perverse views. Using patience as tools, one hews away the stumps and blocks of the six sense-entrances, uproots the cross-roots of perverse views, and trims the leaves and shoots of afflictions. One harnesses the great ox of vast vows, uses wisdom as a sharp plow, and breaks open the wasteland of sentient beings' ignorance. Using sympathetic joy as a harrow, one breaks up the hardened clods of sentient beings' ignorance. Using the six perfections as seeds, one scatters them upon the boundless field of merit. Using the ten wholesome deeds as irrigation channels, one grows the seedlings and harvest of reality. One sharpens the keen sickle of prajñā and reaps the true fruit of nirvāṇa. One harnesses the great ox of vast vows, drives the jeweled vehicle of the three vehicles, loads it upon the headboard of the six supernatural powers, takes the correct road of the Eightfold Path, gathers the sheaves of comprehensive retention, and piles them at the threshing floor of the place of the Way. Such is Jeweled Vehicle's method of liberating people."

> When a mother's child falls ill, she kills living beings to supplicate demons and spirits.
> Not even a single word of merit is gained; the transgression is like Mount Sumeru.
> Think of the time when one grows old: a hundred illnesses arise at once.
> Like water drying up, like fire going out, the knife-wind severs the form.
> Bones separate, sinews and veins are severed; the great life must inevitably end.
> I dread this, and therefore I seek the Way, wishing that merit may be born.
> I think only of old age, illness, and death: the three realms are a great affliction.
> When merit is exhausted, life ends, and one is cast into the Yellow Springs.
> The body rots and returns to earth; the soul follows causes and conditions.
> I dread this, and therefore I seek the Way, which surpasses nirvāṇa.
> When the eyes grow old, they cannot see forms; reading the words of scriptures becomes difficult.
> When the ears grow old, they cannot hear sounds; listening to and receiving the Dharma becomes difficult.
> When the nose grows old, it cannot smell fragrance; distinguishing scents also becomes difficult.
> When the mouth grows old, it cannot speak; expounding the meaning of scriptures also becomes difficult.
> When the mind grows old, much is forgotten; explaining the Dharma and interpreting meaning becomes difficult.
> When the body grows old, it cannot walk; going forth to accumulate merit becomes difficult.
> If in this life one does not receive the precepts, being born again as a human becomes difficult.
> Killing brings the retribution of a short life; wishing for long life becomes difficult.
> "I killed, and now they come for me": fleeing and escaping is also difficult.
> The foolish and ignorant people of this world do not believe the Sūtra of the Jeweled Vehicle.
> They indulge freely in the five desires, dragged along by their karma.
> Like a bridled horse, they raise their heads and gaze at the blue sky, but their deeds lead only to hell.
> They do not think of being reborn in heaven. The wise delight in merit;
> The foolish only nourish the body, like a horse craving water and grain.
> Though spoken to with good words, they will not listen. Tossed about in the cauldron of boiling broth,
> The pain is beyond description. "Tell my family: if you wish to save me,
> Burn incense and invite a person of the Way, and you will not undergo the suffering of the eight difficulties."
> All because of the Sūtra of the Jeweled Vehicle:
> If the body is straight, the shadow is also straight; if the body is crooked, making the shadow straight is difficult.
> If the mind is straight, all things are straight; if the mind is crooked, making things straight is difficult.
> Fish and turtles hide in the sea; sweeping the ground to find a livelihood is difficult.
> Monkeys cling desperately to trees; without trees, escaping is also difficult.
> People in desperation rely on the Buddha; without precepts, seeking the Buddha is difficult.
> Indulging the body and giving free rein, the hosts of māras all come before you.

A bhikṣu said to Jeweled Vehicle: "Why do you not ask the Buddha from where the Four Noble Truths and the two types of dependent origination first emerged?"

The Buddha then said to Jeweled Vehicle: "I arose in the kingdom of Vārāṇasī. Sometimes the fruit is born from the cause; sometimes the cause emerges from the fruit. The wise can settle this; the foolish then dig out the treasure."

Jeweled Vehicle skillfully asked how to break down the three into one. The bhikṣu said to Jeweled Vehicle: "Raise your intention to be like a great one! You who are called people of the three vehicles do not recognize nirvāṇa and revolve in the six destinies. Escaping the various difficulties is also hard. I vow, through the power of expedient means, to lead them into nirvāṇa."

The bhikṣu said to Jeweled Vehicle: "The matter is indeed unknowable. A grain of millet becomes heaven and earth. A mustard seed becomes Mount Sumeru. Such spiritual power is like this. Therefore it is called 'inconceivable.'"

Jeweled Vehicle Bodhisattva and his retinue of ten million billion people, hearing what the Buddha taught, joyfully received and practiced it, single-mindedly taking refuge without any confusion.

---

When the Buddha was formerly in the world, he transformed himself into a wandering practitioner and went to the house of a great brahmin to beg for alms. The brahmin asked the practitioner: "Why do you not farm but only go around begging for food? What is the practitioner's surname? What is his given name? Where is his home? Under which district and prefecture?"

The practitioner said: "My home is east of the Vast Waters and west of the Drawn Waters. It falls under Prajñā District, Original Limit Prefecture. My surname is Emptiness, and my given name is Signlessness. I dwell beneath a bodhi tree and have transcended into the city, beyond pursuit of profit."

The brahmin said: "The practitioner has such a vocation. Why do you not farm but only beg for food?"

The practitioner answered: "My farming is different from yours. I use the four dhyānas as my plow and the six perfections as my seeds. I scatter them upon the boundless wilderness. I moisten them with the spring water of the eight liberations. I grow and nurture the four fruits as my harvest. I hoe with the ten wholesome deeds as my irrigation channels. I use the keen sickle of prajñā. I bind the sheaves with skillful means as binding. I pile them upon the great vehicle of the three vehicles. I transport them to the boundless great city. I distribute them to all the worthy. Such is the poor practitioner's farming, which is inexhaustible."

As the practitioner finished speaking these words, he suddenly vanished. The brahmin immediately attained the fruition of the Way.

---

*[Colophon:]* In the thirteenth year of the Daye era, the Buddhist disciple Zhang Foguo, for the benefit of his good friend and companion Liu Shizhang, reverently produced one fascicle of the Sūtra of the Jeweled Vehicle for circulation, recitation, exposition, and practice. He vowed that through this great karmic undertaking of Great Vehicle propagation, they would together journey to supreme realms, tread upon wondrous traces, turn their backs on the eight wrong paths and return to the eightfold correct path, possess the power of the śūraṅgama-samādhi, and attain the merit of the four foundations of mindfulness. He vowed that in the immeasurable kalpas of the future, life after life, generation after generation, he would together with his disciple form deep and wholesome causes as companions in bodhi, arouse the Great Vehicle mind, and seek the Mahāyāna. Fully endowed with wisdom, supernatural powers, awesome virtue, faculties, powers, factors of awakening, and the path, all would be accomplished. Together they would cultivate the pure practice and together ascend to the seed of awakening.
