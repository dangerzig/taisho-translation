# 天台智者大師齋忌禮讚文
## Liturgy of Praise and Worship for the Memorial Fast of the Great Master Zhiyi of Tiantai

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1948

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, composed by Zunshi (遵式).

---

### Preface

I have observed that in various places where the teaching-lineage of our school is transmitted, on the occasion of the Great Master's distant memorial, when monks gather to cultivate a memorial fast in longing remembrance, they all lack a proper liturgical form for the rites. I have now composed several verses to briefly express our veneration. Inwardly, the accomplishment of quiescent illumination brings entry into the stages of realization; outwardly, supernatural powers and Dharma-teaching respond to all conditions. Therefore, from the beginning to the end of his life of transformation, there are twelve verses in all, which in truth encompass the functions of wisdom and concentration. Although they occasionally depart from the strict sequence of the separate biography, this is because a distinct liturgical occasion has its own principle of ordering. The first two verses concern the samādhi and the subduing of māras. Next comes the teaching of the *Lotus Sūtra*. Then the teaching of the *Mohe Zhiguan*, the *Vimalakīrti Sūtra*, the *Nirvāṇa Sūtra*, the release of living beings, and the bestowing of the precepts, all in due order. The verse on miraculous responses after his passing can hardly exhaust his splendid legacy. The verse on his body in other realms requests the protection of the transmitted teaching. Together with the five repentances, there are seventeen verses in all. The reader should not object that the words are plain and the principle is thin. This is merely a provision for the sincere feelings of remembrance appropriate to the season. It may also be abbreviated for practical use. When it is chanted, it should be done with gravity and solemnity. May it surely bring a response, and may it forever shelter the gate of the teaching. It is entitled "Liturgy of Praise and Worship for the Memorial Fast of Zhiyi."

### Liturgy

I bow my head to the ground before the Master of the Tiantai Teaching, the Chan Master of Folong Peak: his discernment of prajñā is boundless; the radiance of his liberation defies exhaustion. His voice rings through the empty valley in response. Why doubt that when one's capacities strike the true compassion, the response will come without fail?

I humbly consider that true suchness is pure; originally there was no semblance of form or appearance. Yet the dharmadhātu contains all, and so there comes to be the aspect of coming and going. Manifesting his form among the myriad beings, his traces mingled in every quarter. Who knew that his realization of the wondrous stage was so exalted? We can only look up and see: the deeper the pond, the greater the lotus. He harbored the extraordinary marks of two rulers, Yao and Shun, and served as honored teacher of two dynasties, Chen and Sui. His teaching of the Dharma was perfectly complete; his subduing of māras was sovereign and free. How majestic was his wisdom and concentration! How immeasurable was his compassion! The sūtra says: "Wearing the Tathāgata's robe, entering the Tathāgata's room; sitting where the Tathāgata sits; walking where the Tathāgata walks; receiving the Tathāgata's hand upon one's head; being borne upon the Tathāgata's shoulders. One should make offerings to him as one makes offerings to the Tathāgata." His merit is beyond reckoning; his praise could hardly be exhausted.

Now we humbly observe the distant departure at the Crane Grove. Dare we claim these humble offerings of grain and millet are fragrant? We modestly present our sincere devotion, and humbly pray for his luminous regard.

With single mind, I reverently invite the Master of the Tiantai Teaching, the Great Master Zhiyi.

We humbly pray: descend to this place of practice and accept our offerings.

With single mind, I reverently invite the High Patriarch of the Tiantai, Fearless Treatise-Master, the Venerable Nāgārjuna.

With single mind, I reverently invite the Second Patriarch of the Tiantai School's Teaching, the Venerable Huiwen of Northern Qi.

With single mind, I reverently invite the Third Patriarch, direct heir of the Tiantai, the Venerable Huisi of Nanyue.

With single mind, I reverently invite the Fifth Patriarch of Tiantai, compiler of the Teaching Treasury, the Venerable Zhang'an.

With single mind, I reverently invite the Sixth Patriarch of the Tiantai Teaching and Contemplation, the Venerable Fahua.

With single mind, I reverently invite the Seventh Patriarch of the Tiantai Teaching and Contemplation, the Venerable Tiangong.

With single mind, I reverently invite the Eighth Patriarch of the Tiantai Teaching and Contemplation, the Venerable Zuoxi.

With single mind, I reverently invite the Ninth Patriarch of the Tiantai, Master of Records, the Venerable Jingxi.

The dharmadhātu, perfectly interpenetrating: forms are formed without any fixed form. True suchness, pure: transformation transforms without any fixed transformation. Though forms are formed without any fixed form, there is nowhere it does not manifest. Though transformation transforms without any fixed transformation, there is nowhere it does not transform. Resplendently, he manifested the marks of two kings. Heroically, he served as teacher of two dynasties. His karmic ties of transformation fulfilled the words of Nanyue. His fame and virtue matched the stature of Donglin.

### Chapter One: Cultivating the Lotus Samādhi on Mount Dasu

With single mind, I bow my head to the Tiantai Zhiyi, in the body he manifested when cultivating the Lotus Samādhi on Mount Dasu.

> His mind roaming the dharmadhātu, all other minds were stilled.
> Like the sun resting in space, not abiding in space.
> The three thousand reality-characteristics, suddenly and perfectly bright;
> The eighty thousand afflictions, equally as truly pure.
> At Vulture Peak, the wondrous assembly appeared in intimate vision;
> The Jeweled Stūpa's entire body was clearly contemplated.
> Were it not for his personal encounter with the Master of Nanyue,
> Who could have recognized this profound dhyāna-mark?

Therefore, with single mind, I take refuge and bow my head.

### Chapter Two: Subduing the Celestial Māras on Huading Peak

With single mind, I bow my head to the Tiantai Zhiyi, in the body he manifested when subduing the host of celestial māras on Huading Peak.

> In solitary meditation on a bare rock, his mind ever purer,
> Wrapped in deep clouds in the cold, the night not yet past.
> Suddenly a thousand thunderbolts struck the earth;
> Stealthily a hundred terrors burst upon him from the sky.
> First he perceived the ghosts and spirits barely dispersing,
> Then they became his father and mother, tenderly entwining him.
> Through the skill of quiescent illumination, both hard and soft attacks dissolved.
> A holy monk appeared and personally praised him.

### Chapter Three: Opening the Wondrous Meaning of the Lotus at Waguan Monastery

With single mind, I bow my head to the Tiantai Zhiyi, in the body he manifested when opening the wondrous meaning of the *Lotus Sūtra* at Waguan Monastery.

> The supreme teaching proclaimed at Vulture Peak: the sūtra is called "Wondrous."
> For more than forty years, the secret was not spoken.
> The family legacy must await the legitimate heir for its transmission;
> The topknot-pearl is not obtained through ordinary merit.
> The provisional and the real, majestic and lofty: only the Buddha comprehends them.
> Source and trace, deep and vast: even the successor has doubts.
> Zhiyi in his time personally heard and received it;
> At Waguan he once more expounded it with clarity.

### Chapter Four: Teaching the Mohe Zhiguan at the Jade Spring Pavilion

With single mind, I bow my head to the Tiantai Zhiyi, in the body he manifested when teaching the *Mohe Zhiguan* at the Jade Spring Jeweled Pavilion.

> Ninety days of compassionate rain opened the perfect practice;
> The ten dharmas, discussed in their interpenetration, established the wondrous vehicle.
> The jeweled cart, tall and vast, fully adorned;
> The white ox, plump and strong, walking straight and true.
> Peacefully it bears the four conditions, traversing the direct path;
> It passes through the ten realms of contemplation, arriving at the cool pond.
> The broken donkey-carts that fill the world:
> I bow my head to Tiantai and pledge to rely on him.

### Chapter Five: Lecturing on the Renwang Prajñā at the Taiji Hall

With single mind, I bow my head to the Tiantai Zhiyi, in the body he manifested when lecturing on the *Renwang Prajñā* at the Taiji Hall.

> A hundred seats arrayed like stars around the Pole Star;
> The one who embodies the four reliances is like the moon rising over the eastern sea.
> His eightfold eloquence flowing abundantly, the myriad states are at peace;
> The twofold emptiness sweeping clean, a thousand calamities are dispersed.
> Jade fingers tap and ring on the golden hall;
> Ox-head incense rises again from the beast-shaped censer.
> I bow my head to his majestic body of the five patiences;
> Worthy of receiving the earnest rites of the emperor of ten thousand chariots.

### Chapter Six: Conferring the Sudden Precepts in Both Courts of the Chen and Sui

With single mind, I bow my head to the Tiantai Zhiyi, in the body he manifested when conferring the sudden precepts in both courts of the Chen and Sui dynasties.

> Jade pendants chiming, they ringed the Dharma-seat;
> Golden censers, clouding with fragrance, faced the Teacher of Men.
> Bowing, they all sought to don the sash of the precepts;
> Gazing intently, they could not bear to turn from his compassionate countenance.
> His five virtues, clear and pure, like the full moon;
> His twofold adornment, deep and vast, like the blue sea.
> A lion among men, a dragon among the Dharma:
> The Sui Emperor from then on called him "Zhiyi."

### Chapter Seven: Explaining the Vimalakīrti in Meditation and Lecture at Folong

With single mind, I bow my head to the Tiantai Zhiyi, in the body he manifested when explaining the *Vimalakīrti Sūtra* in meditation and lecture at the Dharma-tree of Folong.

> Walking meditation in the frosty grove, the fragrant path swept clean;
> Stilling the mind beneath the moonlit trees, the stone chamber cold.
> The merit of bright and pure concentration is known only to oneself;
> The deep and secluded realm: what person could reach it?
> Taking up the brush, sometimes at the Sui Emperor's command;
> Opening the sūtra, on occasion a visiting Indian monk arrived.
> We of this later age regret we were not there.
> I take refuge in the sea of dhyāna and wisdom of Tiantai.

### Chapter Eight: Opening the Ten Thousand Phrases of the Nirvāṇa at Lingshi Shore

With single mind, I bow my head to the Tiantai Zhiyi, in the body he manifested when opening the ten thousand phrases of the *Nirvāṇa Sūtra* at Lingshi Shore.

> A sea-mountain, a single dot within the autumn light;
> A golden pavilion, perilously tiered, amid the verdant cliffs.
> Ten thousand phrases perfectly opened the gate of liberation;
> A thousand students rivaled one another to fill the hall of compassion.
> On the sūtra-desk at midnight, the moon over the river village;
> The lecture bell's cold tones reaching the waterside homes.
> The three virtues ever nourishing, the three contemplations bright:
> No one else has shared in the full flavor of the ghee.

### Chapter Nine: Lecturing on the Golden Light's Flowing-Water at the Pond of Released Lives

With single mind, I bow my head to the Tiantai Zhiyi, in the body he manifested when lecturing on the Golden Light's Flowing-Water at the Pond of Released Lives.

> Square-bottomed boats, bobbing on the clear river;
> Dharma-words, murmuring, in the heart of the green waves.
> On both banks, the scent drifted as rustic elders followed;
> In mid-stream, the chime-stone sounded and golden-scaled fish leaped.
> Ji Xu once answered his call of distress in prison;
> At the meditation hall, he understood the repayment of that kindness.
> Even now, as of old, his compassionate eyes are many.
> In other realms, he should look upon us with pity.

### Chapter Ten: Manifesting the Good Extinction at the Golden Monastery of Shicheng

With single mind, I bow my head to the Tiantai Zhiyi, in the body he manifested when manifesting the good extinction at the Golden Monastery of Shicheng, like the twin trees.

> When the conditions of transformation were exhausted, like fire dying as fuel is spent,
> He sat upright in the dimness before the stone image.
> His children wailed, yet he would not return;
> The multitudes of beings, blinded, who would guide them?
> The Milky Way grew dim; the golden orb waned;
> The wind moaned through the clouded valley; the crane-tree bent.
> The hundred pieces of gold and the wondrous medicine were entrusted to Zhang'an.
> Still he regretted there was no one to whom he could transmit the dhyāna-wisdom.

### Chapter Eleven: The Emperor's Enshrinement of the Numinous Stūpa at Folong

With single mind, I bow my head to the Tiantai Zhiyi, in his full body enshrined within the numinous stūpa sealed by imperial decree beneath the trees at Folong.

> In lotus posture, just as when he sat in dhyāna,
> The sealed stūpa was like a closed meditation chamber.
> In the early night, streaming light reached the distant grove;
> From time to time, a finger-snap was heard from within the deep courtyard.
> Year after year, a purple decree departed the golden palace;
> Season after season, imperial envoys opened the stone door.
> When the seal was broken for the last time, the former body was gone.
> No one knows to what realm he has departed.

### Chapter Twelve: Universally Born in Buddha-Lands of Other Realms

With single mind, I bow my head to the Tiantai Zhiyi, in his body universally born in the buddha-lands of the ten directions.

> With compassionate mind and celestial eye, he must see from afar:
> More than sixty provinces where he once forged karmic ties.
> Having sown the seeds in the fertile field,
> He must come as sweet rain for the sprouts.
> The father has gone, leaving behind his medicine;
> The parent has departed, leaving the jewel sewn in the drunkard's robe.
> To the divine land and Folong, may he return again,
> Swiftly causing all beings to open the buddha-wisdom.

With single mind, I bow my head to the High Patriarch of the Tiantai, Fearless Treatise-Master, the Venerable Nāgārjuna.

With single mind, I bow my head to the Second Patriarch of the Tiantai School's Teaching, the Venerable Huiwen of Northern Qi.

With single mind, I bow my head to the Third Patriarch, direct heir of the Tiantai, the Venerable Huisi of Nanyue.

With single mind, I bow my head to the Fifth Patriarch of Tiantai, compiler of the Teaching Treasury, the Venerable Zhang'an.

With single mind, I bow my head to the Sixth Patriarch of the Tiantai Teaching and Contemplation, the Venerable Fahua.

With single mind, I bow my head to the Seventh Patriarch of the Tiantai Teaching and Contemplation, the Venerable Tiangong.

With single mind, I bow my head to the Eighth Patriarch of the Tiantai Teaching and Contemplation, the Venerable Zuoxi.

With single mind, I bow my head to the Ninth Patriarch of the Tiantai, Master of Records, the Venerable Jingxi.

### Repentance

With utmost mind, I repent. I and all sentient beings of the dharmadhātu of the ten directions: since the beginningless time of ignorance, following the flow of existence and consciousness, the bonds and latent defilements have moved body, speech, and mind. Following greed, anger, and delusion, we committed the transgressions of the seven impediments and the five heinous acts, and violated the precepts of the ten grave offenses and the four pārājikas. We slandered the correct teachings of the three vehicles, and belittled the five kinds of Dharma-teachers. We practiced the four kinds of wrong livelihood and the five kinds of wrong livelihood, sitting without shame or contrition, treating the three evil destinies as pleasure parks and adorning ourselves with the eight sufferings. Life after life, we were born into households of wrong views. World after world, we emerged into worlds devoid of a buddha. By what conditions were we able to obtain a human body today, to emulate the Snow Mountain Master in leaving home, and to encounter the transmitted teaching of Tiantai? Yet we regret that dark clouds have long covered us and the net of views has entangled us. The Dharma-gates of the thousand suchnesses, provisional and real, we falsely claim to have tasted. The wondrous contemplation of the ten dharmas forming a single vehicle: we have never let it enter our minds. Vainly we stand by the pond and envy the fish, not knowing enough to return home and weave a net. The deluded ape remains unbound, flying endlessly through the forest of the five dusts. The thirsty deer chases mirages, galloping forever in the marsh of the six desires. Thus our bodies surround the Dharma-seat while our minds circle among worldly conditions. The eightfold eloquence thunders in vain; the four unobstructed explanations merely display the color of milk. Moreover, we jested with the sacred scriptures and made light of the texts of the teaching. Never did we think that the Dharma-buddha is the complete body. Never did we reflect upon the Snow Mountain's half-verse. In the present we invite ignorance and darkness; in the future we invite the retribution of wrong delusion. The transgressions of slandering people and slighting the Dharma are obstructions as vast as ten thousand kalpas and a thousand mountains. Such sins and evils are immeasurable and boundless. Before the Three Treasures we lay them all out. Before the four reliances we openly confess. We cut off the continuing thought. We abandon the concealing mind. May we universally receive the radiance of repentance, and may all sins become as pure as empty space.

Having repented, with single mind I take refuge and bow to all.

### Exhortation

With utmost mind, I reverently exhort:

> In a single thought, at a single time, I universally exhort
> All tathāgatas of the worlds and kalpas of the ten directions:
> For sentient beings, turn the Dharma-wheel;
> Do not enter nirvāṇa and return to quiescent extinction.
> All worthies, sages, and good teachers of the three vehicles,
> With all their compassion, abide in the world.
> So long as they serve as conditions of benefit and joy for sentient beings,
> I exhort them all, bowing in body and mind.

Having exhorted, with single mind I take refuge and bow to all.

### Sympathetic Joy

With utmost mind, I share in joy:

> Throughout empty space and the dharmadhātu, dust-mote numbers
> Of buddhas, from the time they first aroused the Way-mind,
> Cultivating through an ocean of kalpas and attaining bodhi,
> And even distributing their śarīra upon entering nirvāṇa:
> The birthless attainments of the worthies and sages of the three vehicles,
> The conditioned causes of the ten wholesome deeds of ordinary beings,
> And even the smallest roots of goodness, such as a mere bow of the head:
> I place them all upon my crown and generate joyful delight.

Having shared in joy, with single mind I take refuge and bow to all.

### Dedication

With utmost mind, I dedicate:

> From the beginning up through sympathetic joy,
> All roots of goodness cultivated through the three activities:
> I turn them back into the true reality of empty space,
> Equally benefiting and gladdening all sentient beings.
> May all cultivated merit and wisdom be joined in harmony;
> May all accomplished bodies and lands be fully adorned.
> At the final, dust-mote-number of places of the Way,
> May we together with all beings accomplish right awakening.

Having dedicated, with single mind I take refuge and bow to all.

### Vow

With utmost mind, I make this vow:

> May I always follow all buddhas in their study,
> Cultivating through dust-mote worlds and kalpas of seas.
> Splitting bone to inscribe the sūtras, rivaling Mount Sumeru;
> Relinquishing the body for the Dharma throughout sand-grain realms.
> Proclaiming the buddhas' perfect, eternal teaching,
> Opening for sentient beings the gate of liberation.
> Even when I have ultimately attained the buddha's bodhi,
> I shall not abandon the practice of Samantabhadra.

> May I, for all my remaining lives, have no other thought:
> Only the wondrous teaching of Tiantai shall accompany me.
> Let the vast sounds and fragrances cease to sway my emotions;
> Let the broad expanse of fame and gain cease to steal my resolve.
> Unrolling scrolls, studying at my desk in the clouded chamber at night;
> Stilling the mind, exploring the hidden in the snowy room:
> The patriarchs' ocean of vows in transmitting and promoting the teaching is deep.
> May they always empower me in hidden ways and bring me to awakening.

Having vowed, with single mind I take refuge and bow to all.
