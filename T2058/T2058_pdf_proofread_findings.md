## Method

The PDF was rendered page by page as images and inspected visually, with rendered-page OCR and embedded PDF text used as cross-checks. The pass focused on visible text, punctuation, glyph rendering, page furniture, headings, margin references, page numbering, and likely copy-edit problems.

No missing pages, broken page sequence, obvious blank-page errors, major glyph-rendering failures, or margin-reference collisions were found. Blank pages appear intentional.

## Confirmed Findings

### 1. Chinese Leaks Into Running English Text

Location: printed p. 64, PDF image page 82

Current text:

> there was a king in that country named 端嚴 (Dignified Appearance).

Issue: Chinese appears in running English prose after "named." This looks like an unresolved translation/romanization note.

Suggested correction:

> there was a king in that country named Dignified Appearance.

Alternative:

> there was a king in that country named Duanyan, "Dignified Appearance."

### 2. Unidiomatic Phrase

Location: printed p. 41, source line 673

Current text:

> In reason, I cannot give him to you.

Issue: "In reason" is not idiomatic English.

Suggested correction:

> I cannot reasonably give him to you.

### 3. Likely Translation/Word-Choice Artifact

Location: printed p. 113, source line 1646

Current text:

> planting unroots of merit.

Issue: "unroots" is not idiomatic and reads like a generation or translation artifact.

Suggested correction:

> planting roots of demerit.

Alternative:

> planting unwholesome roots.

### 4. Redundant Wording

Location: printed p. 55, source line 907

Current text:

> installed him upon a jeweled seat, and personally assisted him onto it.

Issue: The two clauses duplicate the same action.

Suggested correction:

> installed him upon a jeweled seat.

Alternative:

> personally assisted him onto a jeweled seat.

### 5. Awkward Register / Redundancy

Location: printed p. 93, source line 1346

Current text:

> with an ugly and homely appearance

Issue: "ugly" and "homely" are redundant, and "homely" may sound colloquial in this context.

Suggested correction:

> with an unattractive appearance

Alternative:

> with a rough and haggard appearance

### 6. Awkward Metaphor

Location: printed p. 112, source line 1636

Current text:

> His contained poison raged intensely

Issue: The phrase is grammatical but awkward in English.

Suggested correction:

> The poison he harbored raged intensely

Alternative:

> His pent-up malice raged intensely
