# 佛說聖曜母陀羅尼經
## The Buddha's Teaching: Dhāraṇī Sūtra of the Holy Mother of the Luminaries

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1303

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmadeva (法天).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was in the great city of Aṇakāvatī. At that time there were innumerable devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, humans and non-humans, as well as Jupiter, Mars, Venus, Mercury, Saturn, the Moon, the Sun, Rāhu, Ketu, and the twenty-seven luminaries, who reverently surrounded this great vajra-samādhi-adorned ritual site. Furthermore, there were innumerable thousands of bodhisattva mahāsattvas: Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva, Vajra-Krodha Bodhisattva, Vajra-Sena Bodhisattva, Vajra-Pāṇi Bodhisattva, Vajra-Pati Bodhisattva, Vajrālaṃkāra Bodhisattva, Vajra-Prabhā Bodhisattva, Vajra-Puṣpa Bodhisattva, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, Sarva-Loka-Darśin Bodhisattva, Maṅgala Bodhisattva, Padma-Dhvaja Bodhisattva, Padma-Garbha Bodhisattva, Padma-Netra Bodhisattva, Maitreya Bodhisattva, and Mañjuśrī Dharma-Prince Bodhisattva Mahāsattva. All these great bodhisattvas reverently surrounded the Buddha as he taught the Dharma, excellent in the beginning, middle, and end, profound in meaning, skillful in expression, purely unmixed, complete in purity and purity of conduct, adorned and wish-fulfilling.

At that time, Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva Mahāsattva rose from his seat, and by his supernatural power circumambulated the World-Honored One innumerable hundreds of thousands of times. He bowed his head to the ground in reverence, then sat in padmāsana. With pure eyes he observed the great assembly, formed vajra-añjali and placed it at his heart, and addressed the World-Honored One: "There are certain constellations and luminaries whose forms are coarse and malign and whose minds are given to much wrath. They harm sentient beings, severing their life-roots, depleting their wealth and treasures, diminishing their vitality, or shortening their years. Thus they harm all sentient beings. I humbly pray that the World-Honored One will teach the true secret Dharma to provide protection."

The Buddha said: "Excellent! Excellent! You are able to compassionately benefit all sentient beings and inquire of the Tathāgata about the supreme secret Dharma. Listen carefully now, consider well, and I shall teach you.

"Vajrapāṇi! For these evil constellations and luminaries, and for the devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, humans and non-humans: one should present the supreme arghya water, music, and the like, empowering each according to the method and making offerings, causing them to rejoice and extinguishing all evils."

Then Śākyamuni Buddha emitted from his own heart a great radiance called the Light of Compassion, which entered the crowns of all the constellations and luminaries. In an instant, all the constellations and luminaries, the sun and moon and the rest, rose from their seats, bowed their heads at the Buddha's feet, knelt with right knee on the ground, joined their palms in reverence, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! Tathāgata, Worthy of Offerings, Perfectly and Truly Awakened One! May you graciously accept us and teach the True Dharma. If we hear it, we shall protect sentient beings and ensure they are free from calamity and harm. All bindings, fetters, swords, weapons, and venomous creatures shall not harm them. We shall bind the ground as a boundary for their protection."

At that time, Śākyamuni Tathāgata then taught the mantras for making offerings to the constellations and luminaries:

*Oṃ miṅghurā-kāya svāhā. Oṃ śitāṃśu-vi svāhā. Oṃ laktāṅga-kumārāya svāhā. Oṃ bodhāya bodhāya svāhā. Oṃ bhuga-sapanāya svāhā. Oṃ asura-sattamāya svāhā. Oṃ kṛṣṇa-varaṇāya svāhā. Oṃ amṛta-priyāya svāhā. Oṃ jyoti-keta-ve svāhā.*

At that time, the World-Honored One, having spoken these mantras, addressed Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva: "These mantras of the nine luminaries: those who recite them will all achieve accomplishment. First, according to the method, coat a maṇḍala with fragrant water, twelve finger-spans across. In vessels of gold, silver, copper, or clay, present arghya offerings to the constellations and luminaries. Using the preceding mantras, recite each 108 times. All that is sought will be accomplished."

The Buddha addressed Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva: "I now further teach a dhāraṇī called the Holy Mother of the Luminaries. It possesses great vidyā-power and can provide protection. All constellations and luminaries, upon hearing it, rejoice. If any bhikṣu, bhikṣuṇī, upāsaka, or upāsikā hears this sūtra and presents arghya offerings at a maṇḍala while reciting this mantra seven times, they will obtain wealth, nobility, and long life. If they recite it daily, all the constellations and luminaries will be able to fulfill every wish of sentient beings." The Dhāraṇī of the Mother of the Luminaries:

*Namo ratna-trayāya. Namo buddhāya. Namo vajra-dharāya. Namo padma-dharāya. Namaḥ sarva-grahāṇāṃ sarva-śāṃ-paripūrakāṇāṃ. Namo nakṣatrāṇāṃ. Namo dvādaśa-rāśīnāṃ. Tadyathā oṃ buddhe vajri vajri padme sara sara prasara prasara sammara sammara dhāraṇāya kṛṇāya mardanāya gatāya gatāya sarva-vighnān kuru kuru chinda chinda bhinda bhinda kṣapaya kṣapaya śānti śānti nānti nānti namāya namāya dhruta-nāri-daśāya-tad-manāṃ mama rakṣa rakṣa sarva-sattvāṃś-ca sarva-nakṣatra-graha-benāṃ maṇi-rvara-ye bhagavatī mahā-māye prasādhaya sarva-pāpāni-mi sarva-sattvānāṃ cānṇi cānṇi turu turu bhagavate cānṇi cānṇi sumu sumu jumu jumu bhava bhave ugri ugra-tame purāya mama sarva-sattvānāṃ ca manuradha sarva-tathāgatādhiṣṭhita-samāye svāhā. Oṃ svāhā. Hūṃ svāhā. Hrīḥ svāhā. Dhuṃ svāhā. Dhiṃ svāhā. Vajra-dharāya svāhā. Padma-dharāya svāhā. Ādityāya svāhā. Somāya svāhā. Dhāraṇī-sutāya svāhā. Budhāya svāhā. Bṛhas-pataye svāhā. Śukrāya svāhā. Kṛṣṇa-varaṇāya svāhā. Rāhave svāhā. Ketave svāhā. Buddhāya svāhā. Vajra-dharāya svāhā. Padma-dharāya svāhā. Kumārāya svāhā. Nakṣatrāṇāṃ svāhā. Sarvopadravāṇāṃ svāhā. Oṃ sarva-vidye hūṃ phaṭ svāhā.*

At that time, the World-Honored One, having spoken this dhāraṇī, addressed Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva: "This mantra is the most supreme and secret. It can fulfill all the wishes of sentient beings. If a person seeks long life and the like, beginning from the seventh day of the eighth month, they should receive and uphold the precepts and fasting. On the fourteenth night, make offerings to the constellations and luminaries according to the method. On the fifteenth day, throughout one full day and night, recite this dhāraṇī. That person will obtain a long life reaching ninety-nine years. All thunder, lightning, nāga-ghosts, and evil constellations and luminaries will be unable to cause them fear. They will further obtain the knowledge of past lives, and all their wishes will be fulfilled."

At that time, all the constellations and luminaries, hearing what the Buddha had taught, praised him: "Excellent! Excellent! How exceedingly rare! We receive and uphold this." They then bowed their heads at the World-Honored One's feet and suddenly vanished.
