# 緣起經
## Sūtra on Dependent Origination

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0124

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Xuanzang (玄奘)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Bhagavān was in Śrāvastī, dwelling in the Jeta Grove, the garden of Anāthapiṇḍada, together with immeasurable and countless śrāvakas, bodhisattvas, devas, humans, and others. At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the assembly of bhikṣus: "I shall now expound for you the meaning of the initial formulation and the differentiation of dependent origination. You should listen attentively and reflect well upon it. I shall now analyze and explain it for you."

The assembly of bhikṣus said: "Indeed, we wish you to speak. We delight in hearing it."

The Buddha said: "What is the initial formulation of dependent origination? It means: because this exists, that exists; because this arises, that arises. That is to say: conditioned by ignorance, there are formations. Conditioned by formations, there is consciousness. Conditioned by consciousness, there is name-and-form. Conditioned by name-and-form, there are the six sense bases. Conditioned by the six sense bases, there is contact. Conditioned by contact, there is feeling. Conditioned by feeling, there is craving. Conditioned by craving, there is clinging. Conditioned by clinging, there is becoming. Conditioned by becoming, there is birth. Conditioned by birth, there arise old age and death, grief, lamentation, suffering, sorrow, and vexation. This is called the arising of the entire great mass of suffering. This is the meaning of the initial formulation of dependent origination.

"What is the differentiation of dependent origination? 'Conditioned by ignorance, there are formations': what is ignorance? It is not knowing the past, not knowing the future, not knowing both the past and the future. Not knowing the internal, not knowing the external, not knowing both the internal and the external. Not knowing karma, not knowing the ripening of karma, not knowing both karma and its ripening. Not knowing the Buddha, not knowing the Dharma, not knowing the Saṃgha. Not knowing suffering, not knowing its arising, not knowing its cessation, not knowing the path. Not knowing causes, not knowing effects, not knowing dharmas that have arisen from causes. Not knowing what is wholesome, not knowing what is unwholesome. Not knowing what is blameworthy, not knowing what is blameless. Not knowing what should be cultivated, not knowing what should not be cultivated. Not knowing what is inferior, not knowing what is superior. Not knowing what is dark, not knowing what is bright. Not knowing what has distinct aspects. Not knowing, as they truly are, the six sense bases conditioned by contact. Such not knowing, not seeing, not directly realizing regarding all these matters: this is foolishness, ignorance, and darkness. This is called ignorance.

"What are formations? There are three kinds of formations: bodily formations, verbal formations, and mental formations. These are called formations.

"'Conditioned by formations, there is consciousness': what is consciousness? There are six types of consciousness: first, eye consciousness; second, ear consciousness; third, nose consciousness; fourth, tongue consciousness; fifth, body consciousness; sixth, mind consciousness. These are called consciousness.

"'Conditioned by consciousness, there is name-and-form': what is name? It refers to the four formless aggregates: first, the aggregate of feeling; second, the aggregate of perception; third, the aggregate of formations; fourth, the aggregate of consciousness. What is form? It refers to all form whatsoever: all four great elements and what is derived from the four great elements. This form and the aforementioned name are summarized together and collectively called name-and-form. This is name-and-form.

"'Conditioned by name-and-form, there are the six sense bases': what are the six sense bases? They are the six internal sense bases: first, the internal sense base of the eye; second, the internal sense base of the ear; third, the internal sense base of the nose; fourth, the internal sense base of the tongue; fifth, the internal sense base of the body; sixth, the internal sense base of the mind. These are the six sense bases.

"'Conditioned by the six sense bases, there is contact': what is contact? There are six types of contact: first, eye-contact; second, ear-contact; third, nose-contact; fourth, tongue-contact; fifth, body-contact; sixth, mind-contact. These are called contact.

"'Conditioned by contact, there is feeling': what is feeling? There are three kinds of feeling: pleasant feeling, painful feeling, and feeling that is neither painful nor pleasant. These are called feeling.

"'Conditioned by feeling, there is craving': what is craving? There are three kinds of craving: craving for sensual desire, craving for form, and craving for the formless. These are called craving.

"'Conditioned by craving, there is clinging': what is clinging? There are four kinds of clinging: first, clinging to sensual desire; second, clinging to views; third, clinging to precepts and vows; fourth, clinging to a doctrine of self. These are called clinging.

"'Conditioned by clinging, there is becoming': what is becoming? There are three kinds of becoming: becoming in the desire realm, becoming in the form realm, and becoming in the formless realm. These are called becoming.

"'Conditioned by becoming, there is birth': what is birth? For each and every sentient being, among each and every class of sentient beings, the various kinds of birth, equal birth, entering a destiny, arising, coming forth, appearing, obtaining aggregates, obtaining elements, obtaining sense bases, the arising of aggregates, and the appearing of the life faculty: this is called birth.

"'Conditioned by birth, there are old age and death': what is old age? It is the greying of the hair, the wrinkling and sagging of the skin, the failing and deterioration of the body, the hunching and curvature of the spine, dark spots appearing on the body, panting and gasping for breath, the stooping of the posture, reliance on a walking staff, dullness and feebleness, declining and waning, the deterioration and decrepitude of the sense faculties, the breakdown of their functions, the wearing out of the formations, and the rotting and decay of the body. This is called old age.

"What is death? For each and every sentient being, departing from each and every class of sentient beings: the ending, destruction, and perishing; the giving up of the lifespan and the giving up of warmth; the ceasing and extinguishing of the life faculty; the abandoning of the aggregates; the final running out of the time of death. This is called death. This death and the aforementioned old age are summarized together and collectively called old age and death. This is the meaning of the differentiation of dependent origination.

"Bhikṣus! I have now expounded for you the meaning of the initial formulation and the differentiation of dependent origination as I set forth."

When the Bhagavān had spoken this sūtra, the śrāvakas, bodhisattvas, devas, humans, and all the rest, having heard what the Buddha taught, were all greatly delighted. Having gained what they had never had before, they faithfully received it and practiced in accordance.
