# 佛說觀彌勒菩薩上生兜率天經
## Sūtra Spoken by the Buddha on Contemplating the Bodhisattva Maitreya's Ascent to the Tuṣita Heaven

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0452

Translated from the Chinese. Liu Song dynasty, translated by Jùqú Jīngshēng (沮渠京聲)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was in the land of Śrāvastī, in the Jetavana of Anāthapiṇḍada's park. At that time, the World-Honored One, during the first watch of the night, emitted light from his entire body. The light was golden in color, circling the Jeta Grove seven times and illuminating the house of Sudatta, also turning it golden. A golden radiance like a mass of clouds pervaded the land of Śrāvastī, raining golden lotuses everywhere. Within that radiance were immeasurable hundreds of thousands of great transformation-buddhas, all proclaiming in unison: "Here within this assembly are a thousand bodhisattvas. The first to attain buddhahood is named Krakucchanda, and the last to attain buddhahood is named Rucika."

When these words were spoken, the Venerable Ājñāta Kauṇḍinya arose from meditation together with his retinue of two hundred and fifty. The Venerable Mahākāśyapa, together with his retinue of two hundred and fifty. The Venerable Mahāmaudgalyāyana, together with his retinue of two hundred and fifty. The Venerable Śāriputra, together with his retinue of two hundred and fifty. The bhikṣuṇī Mahāprajāpatī, together with her retinue of one thousand bhikṣuṇīs. The elder Sudatta, together with three thousand upāsakas. The mother Viśākhā, together with two thousand upāsikās. There was also a bodhisattva-mahāsattva named Bhadrapāla, together with his retinue of sixteen bodhisattvas. The Dharma-prince Mañjuśrī, together with his retinue of five hundred bodhisattvas. Devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, and all the rest of the great assembly, beholding the Buddha's radiance, all gathered like clouds.

At that time the World-Honored One extended his broad long tongue and emitted a thousand rays of light. Each ray of light contained a thousand colors, and within each color were immeasurable transformation-buddhas. These transformation-buddhas, speaking with different mouths but in unison, all taught the pure and profound, inconceivable dhāraṇī dharmas of the great bodhisattvas, namely: the Ānanda-mukhī Dhāraṇī, the Śūnyatā-prajñā Dhāraṇī, the Anāvaraṇa-svabhāva Dhāraṇī, and the Mahā-vimokṣa-animitta Dhāraṇī. At that time the World-Honored One, with a single voice, taught one hundred billion dhāraṇī gates. When these dhāraṇīs had been taught, a bodhisattva in the assembly named Maitreya, hearing what the Buddha had spoken, immediately attained one million billion dhāraṇī gates. He then rose from his seat, arranged his robes, joined his palms, and stood before the Buddha.

At that time Upāli also rose from his seat, bowed his head to the ground, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! In past times the World-Honored One, in the Vinaya and in the sūtra collection, declared that Ajita would next become a buddha. Yet this Ajita possesses an ordinary person's body and has not yet cut off all defilements. When this person's life ends, where will he be reborn? Though this person has left the household life, he does not cultivate meditation concentration and has not severed afflictions. Yet the Buddha has predicted that this person will without doubt attain buddhahood. When this person's life ends, in what land will he be born?"

The Buddha told Upāli: "Listen carefully, listen carefully, and reflect well upon it. The Tathāgata, Worthy of Offerings, Perfectly Awakened One, now before this assembly declares the prediction of supreme, right, and perfect enlightenment (anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi) for the bodhisattva-mahāsattva Maitreya. This person, twelve years from now, will reach the end of his life and will certainly be reborn in the Tuṣita Heaven above.

"At that time in the Tuṣita Heaven above, there will be five million billion deva-sons, each cultivating the profound pāramitā of giving, in order to make offerings to the bodhisattva who is one birth away from buddhahood. Through the power of heavenly merit, they create palaces. Each removes the sandalwood and maṇi-jeweled crown from his own body, kneels with palms joined, and makes this vow: 'I now offer this priceless jeweled pearl and heavenly crown to honor this being of great aspiration. This person in a future life will soon attain supreme, right, and perfect enlightenment. If I may receive a prediction in the adorned land of that buddha, may my jeweled crown transform into offerings.' In this way all these deva-sons, kneeling one by one, make their vast vows in like manner.

"When these deva-sons have made their vows, all their jeweled crowns transform into five million billion jeweled palaces. Each jeweled palace has seven rings of walls, each wall made of the seven treasures. Each treasure emits five hundred billion rays of light. Within each ray of light are five hundred billion lotuses. Each lotus transforms into five hundred billion rows of trees made of the seven treasures. Each tree-leaf has five hundred billion jeweled colors. Each jeweled color emits five hundred billion rays of Jambu-river gold. Within each ray of Jambu-river gold appear five hundred billion heavenly jeweled maidens. Each jeweled maiden stands beneath a tree, holding a hundred billion jeweled ornaments and countless garlands, producing exquisite music. Within that music they expound the Dharma-wheel practice of the stage of non-retrogression. The trees bear fruit the color of crystal, and all colors enter the crystal color. These rays of light spiral and revolve, flowing forth as sounds, and the sounds expound the Dharma of great loving-kindness and great compassion. Each ring of wall is sixty-two yojanas in height and fourteen yojanas in thickness. Five hundred billion nāga kings surround these walls, and each nāga king rains down five hundred billion rows of trees of the seven treasures to adorn the wall-tops. A natural wind arises and stirs these trees, and as the trees strike against one another, they expound the teaching of suffering, emptiness, impermanence, non-self, and the pāramitās.

"At that time within this palace there is a great spirit named Lāmbudhi. He rises from his seat, bows to the buddhas of the ten directions, and makes a vast vow: 'If my merit warrants the construction of a Hall of Good Dharma for the Bodhisattva Maitreya, may a jewel spontaneously appear on my forehead.' Having made this vow, five hundred billion jeweled pearls spontaneously appear on his forehead, including lapis lazuli, crystal, and every color complete, like a dark blue maṇi translucent within and without. The light of this maṇi spirals through the sky and transforms into forty-nine tiers of exquisite jeweled palaces. Each balustrade is made from the combined substance of myriads of billions of Brahma-maṇi jewels. Between the balustrades, nine hundred million deva-sons and five hundred billion deva-maidens spontaneously appear. In the hand of each deva-son are immeasurably many lotus flowers of the seven treasures. Upon each lotus flower are immeasurable billions of lights, and within those lights are all manner of musical instruments. These heavenly instruments play without being struck, and when these sounds arise, the maidens spontaneously take up various instruments and compete in song and dance. The songs they sing expound the ten wholesome deeds and the four great vows, and all the devas who hear them generate the aspiration for the supreme path.

"At that time within the gardens there are channels of eight-colored lapis lazuli, each channel composed of five hundred billion jeweled pearls. Within each channel are waters of eight flavors, replete with eight colors. These waters surge upward and flow among the beams and ridges, and at the four gates they produce four kinds of flowers. The water emerges from within the flowers and flows like jeweled streams. Upon each flower are twenty-four deva-maidens whose bodies are exquisitely beautiful, like the adorned forms of bodhisattvas. In their hands they spontaneously produce five hundred billion jeweled vessels, each vessel spontaneously filled to the brim with divine ambrosia. On their left shoulders they bear countless garlands, and on their right shoulders they carry countless instruments. Like clouds hovering in the sky, they emerge from the water and praise the six pāramitās of the bodhisattva. If anyone is reborn in the Tuṣita Heaven, they naturally receive the attendance of these deva-maidens.

"There is also a great lion-throne of the seven treasures, four yojanas in height, adorned with Jambu-river gold and immeasurable jewels. At the four corners of the throne grow four lotuses, each composed of a hundred treasures, each treasure emitting a hundred billion rays of light. These lights, exquisitely subtle, transform into five hundred billion multicolored jeweled flowers to adorn a jeweled canopy.

"At that time, a hundred thousand Brahmā kings from the ten directions each hold an exquisite Brahmā-treasure and fashion it into a jeweled bell, which they hang upon the jeweled canopy. Young Brahmā kings hold heavenly treasures and fashion them into a net to drape over the canopy. Thereupon, hundreds of thousands of immeasurable deva-sons and deva-maidens with their retinues each hold jeweled flowers and scatter them upon the throne. These lotuses spontaneously produce five hundred billion jeweled maidens, holding white whisks and standing within the canopy in attendance.

"At the four corners of the palace are four jeweled pillars. Each jeweled pillar has a hundred thousand chambers, adorned with Brahma-maṇi pearls as interlacing ornaments. Between the chambers are a hundred thousand deva-maidens of incomparable beauty, holding instruments. Within that music they expound the teaching of suffering, emptiness, impermanence, non-self, and the pāramitās. In this heavenly palace there are a hundred billion myriads of immeasurable jeweled colors, and each of the maidens shares in that jeweled radiance.

"At that time, immeasurable devas of the ten directions, at the end of their lifespans, all aspire to be reborn in the Tuṣita heavenly palace.

"At that time there are five great spirits in the Tuṣita heavenly palace:

"The first great spirit is named Jeweled Banner. From his body rain the seven treasures, which scatter within the palace walls. Each jeweled pearl transforms into immeasurable instruments that hang in the air and play without being struck. They produce immeasurable sounds that delight all beings.

"The second great spirit is named Flower-Virtue. From his body rain multitudes of flowers that cover the palace walls and transform into flower-canopies. Each flower-canopy has a hundred thousand banners and pennants to serve as guides.

"The third great spirit is named Incense-Sound. From the pores of his body rains an exquisite sandalwood incense from the near shore of the sea. The fragrance rises like clouds of a hundred jeweled colors and encircles the palace seven times.

"The fourth great spirit is named Joyful-Delight. He rains wish-fulfilling jewels, and each jeweled pearl naturally rests atop the banners and pennants, proclaiming the manifold teachings of taking refuge in the Buddha, taking refuge in the Dharma, taking refuge in the Sangha, and also the five precepts, immeasurable wholesome dharmas, and the pāramitās, benefiting and encouraging those who have generated the aspiration for enlightenment.

"The fifth great spirit is named Right-Sound-Voice. From the pores of his body flow forth streams of water, and upon each stream are five hundred billion flowers. Upon each flower are twenty-five jade maidens, and from the pores of each jade maiden arise all manner of sounds that surpass all the music of the consorts of Māra, lord of the devas."

The Buddha told Upāli: "This is called the Tuṣita Heaven, the supreme and marvelous abode of merit that is the reward of the ten wholesome deeds. If I were to remain in the world for a full minor kalpa and speak at length of the rewards and fruits of the ten wholesome deeds for the one-birth bodhisattva, I could not exhaust them. Now for all of you I shall explain them in brief."

The Buddha told Upāli: "If there are monks and all the great assembly who do not weary of birth-and-death but delight in being reborn in the heavens, who love and revere the unsurpassed aspiration for enlightenment, who wish to become disciples of Maitreya, they should practice this contemplation. Those who practice this contemplation should uphold the five precepts, the eight precepts, and the full precepts; should be diligent in body and mind without seeking to sever the fetters; should cultivate the ten wholesome deeds; and should contemplate one by one the supremely wonderful pleasures of the Tuṣita Heaven above. Those who contemplate in this way are called practitioners of right contemplation. If one contemplates otherwise, it is called wrong contemplation."

At that time Upāli rose from his seat, arranged his robes, bowed his head to the ground, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! The Tuṣita Heaven above possesses such supremely wonderful and joyful things. When will this great being depart from Jambudvīpa and be born in that heaven?"

The Buddha told Upāli: "Maitreya was previously born in the Kapparī village of the country of Vārāṇasī, in the family of the great brahmin Pāpārī. Twelve years hence, on the fifteenth day of the second month, he will return to the place of his birth, sit in the cross-legged posture as if entering the concentration of cessation, his body of purple-gold color and brilliant radiance like a hundred thousand suns, and ascend to the Tuṣita Heaven. His bodily relics will be like a cast golden image, unmoving and unshaking. Within the circular radiance of his body, the characters and meaning of the Śūraṅgama Samādhi and Prajñāpāramitā will be clearly visible. At that time, humans and devas will immediately erect a stūpa of many treasures to venerate the relics.

"Then within the seven-treasure terrace of the Tuṣita Heaven, upon the maṇi hall, on the lion-throne, he will suddenly be manifested. Seated in the cross-legged posture upon a lotus, his body will be the color of Jambu-river gold, sixteen yojanas in height, complete with the thirty-two marks and eighty excellent characteristics. The topknot on his crown will be the color of dark blue lapis lazuli, adorned with Śakra-abhilagna-maṇi pearls and a hundred thousand billion kiṃśuka jewels as his heavenly crown. His heavenly jeweled crown will have a hundred million colors, and within each color will be immeasurable hundreds of thousands of transformation-buddhas, with transformation-bodhisattvas as attendants. There will also be great bodhisattvas from other regions who perform the eighteen transformations and abide in his heavenly crown at will.

"Between Maitreya's eyebrows there will be a white ūrṇā mark whose light flows forth in a multitude of rays of a hundred jeweled colors. Each of the thirty-two marks will have five hundred billion jeweled colors, and each excellent characteristic will also have five hundred billion jeweled colors. Each mark and characteristic will emit eighty-four thousand clouds of radiance. Together with the deva-sons, each seated on a flower-throne, six times day and night he will constantly teach the Dharma-wheel practice of the stage of non-retrogression. In a single session he will bring five hundred billion deva-sons to non-retrogression from supreme, right, and perfect enlightenment. Abiding thus in the Tuṣita Heaven, day and night he will constantly teach this Dharma and liberate the deva-sons. After five billion six hundred million years of Jambudvīpa time, he will descend and be born in Jambudvīpa, as taught in the Sūtra of Maitreya's Descent."

The Buddha told Upāli: "This is the account of how the Bodhisattva Maitreya departs from Jambudvīpa and is born in the Tuṣita Heaven. After the Buddha's nirvāṇa, my disciples, if any diligently cultivate various merits with deportment free from defect, sweep the stūpa-grounds, spread incense, and make offerings of various famous incenses and exquisite flowers, practicing the many samādhis and entering deeply into right absorption, reading and reciting the sūtras: such people should do so with wholehearted sincerity. Though they have not severed the fetters, they will be as if they had attained the six supernatural penetrations. They should concentrate their thoughts, be mindful of the Buddha's form, and recite the name of Maitreya. If such people, even for a single moment of thought, receive the eight precepts and cultivate various pure deeds, generating vast vows, then at the end of their lives, as swiftly as a strong person bends and extends their arm, they will be reborn in the Tuṣita Heaven. Seated in the cross-legged posture upon a lotus, a hundred thousand deva-sons will play heavenly music, holding heavenly māndārava and mahā-māndārava flowers and scattering them upon them, saying in praise: 'Excellent, excellent! Good man, in Jambudvīpa you broadly cultivated meritorious deeds and have come to be born here. This place is called the Tuṣita Heaven, and the lord of this heaven is now named Maitreya. You should take refuge in him.' Upon hearing this, they immediately bow in homage. Having bowed, they gaze upon the white ūrṇā mark between his eyebrows, and they immediately transcend the sins of birth and death accumulated over ninety billion kalpas. At that time the bodhisattva, according to their past conditions, teaches them the wonderful Dharma, causing them to be firmly established in non-retrogression from the unsurpassed aspiration for the path.

"If such beings purify their deeds and practice the six things, they will without doubt be reborn in the Tuṣita Heaven and there encounter Maitreya. They will also follow Maitreya when he descends to Jambudvīpa, be among the first to hear the Dharma, and in a future age encounter all the buddhas of the Bhadrakalpa. In the Nakṣatra Kalpa they will also encounter all the buddha-world-honored-ones and receive the prediction of awakening in the presence of those buddhas."

The Buddha told Upāli: "After the Buddha's nirvāṇa, if monks, nuns, laymen, laywomen, devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, or mahoragas, any among this great assembly, hear the name of the bodhisattva-mahāsattva Maitreya and upon hearing it rejoice, pay respects, and bow, at the end of their lives, in the snap of a finger, they will be reborn there just as described above, without any difference. Merely hearing the name of Maitreya ensures that at the end of one's life one will not fall into places of darkness, border regions, wrong views, or unwholesome codes of conduct. One will always be born with right views, with family connections complete, and will not slander the Three Treasures."

The Buddha told Upāli: "If good men or good women have violated the precepts and committed various evil deeds, yet hear the name of this bodhisattva of great compassion and prostrate with all five limbs on the ground in sincere repentance, all those evil deeds will swiftly be purified. If beings in the future hear the name of this bodhisattva of great compassion, create images of him, and make offerings of incense, flowers, clothing, silk canopies, banners, and pennants, bowing and concentrating their thoughts, then at the end of their lives the Bodhisattva Maitreya will emit from between his eyebrows the great white ūrṇā light of the marks of a great person, and together with the deva-sons, raining māndārava flowers, he will come to welcome them. In an instant they will be reborn, encounter Maitreya, bow their heads in reverence, and before they even raise their heads, they will hear the Dharma and immediately attain non-retrogression from the unsurpassed path. In future lives they will encounter buddha-tathāgatas as numerous as the sands of the Ganges."

The Buddha told Upāli: "Listen well now. This Bodhisattva Maitreya will in the future serve as a great refuge for all beings. If anyone takes refuge in the Bodhisattva Maitreya, know that this person has attained non-retrogression from the unsurpassed path. When the Bodhisattva Maitreya attains the status of Tathāgata, Worthy of Offerings, Perfectly Awakened One, such practitioners, seeing the Buddha's radiance, will immediately receive the prediction of awakening."

The Buddha told Upāli: "After the Buddha's nirvāṇa, the fourfold assembly, devas, nāgas, and spirits, if any wish to be reborn in the Tuṣita heavenly palace, should practice this contemplation, concentrating their thoughts and being mindful of the Tuṣita Heaven. They should uphold the Buddha's precepts, from one day to seven days, reflecting on the ten wholesome deeds and practicing the ten wholesome paths. With this merit, they should transfer the aspiration to be reborn before Maitreya. Those who practice this contemplation, if they see even one heavenly being or one lotus, or for even a single thought recite the name of Maitreya, will thereby eliminate twelve hundred kalpas of the sins of birth and death. Merely hearing the name of Maitreya and joining one's palms in reverence eliminates fifty kalpas of the sins of birth and death. If anyone bows in homage to Maitreya, it eliminates one hundred billion kalpas of the sins of birth and death. Even if one is not reborn in that heaven, in a future age beneath the Dragon-Flower Bodhi Tree, one will also encounter him and generate the unsurpassed aspiration."

When these words were spoken, the immeasurable great assembly rose from their seats, bowed at the Buddha's feet, and bowed at Maitreya's feet, circumambulating the Buddha and the Bodhisattva Maitreya a hundred thousand times. Those who had not yet attained the path each made a vow: "We, the assembly of devas, humans, and the eight groups, now before the Buddha make a sincere and true vow: in the future, to encounter Maitreya. Having relinquished this body, may we all be reborn above in the Tuṣita Heaven."

The World-Honored One declared: "All of you, and those in future ages who cultivate merit and uphold the precepts, shall all be reborn before the Bodhisattva Maitreya and be gathered in by the Bodhisattva Maitreya."

The Buddha told Upāli: "Those who practice this contemplation are called practitioners of right contemplation. If one contemplates otherwise, it is called wrong contemplation."

At that time the Venerable Ānanda rose from his seat, joined his palms, knelt, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! How excellent, World-Honored One, to have joyfully taught all the merits of Maitreya, and to have predicted the fruits that beings who cultivate merit in future ages will obtain. I now rejoice in this. But, World-Honored One, what is the essential point of this Dharma? How should it be upheld? And what should this sūtra be called?"

The Buddha told Ānanda: "Uphold the Buddha's words and take care not to forget them. For the sake of beings in future ages, open the road to rebirth in the heavens, reveal the marks of awakening, and do not sever the Buddha-seed. This sūtra is called 'The Bodhisattva Maitreya's Parinirvāṇa,' and it is also called 'Contemplating the Bodhisattva Maitreya's Birth in the Tuṣita Heaven and Exhorting the Generation of the aspiration for enlightenment.' Uphold it in this way."

When the Buddha spoke these words, ten thousand bodhisattvas who had come from other regions attained the Śūraṅgama Samādhi. Eighty thousand billion devas generated the aspiration for enlightenment and all vowed to follow Maitreya when he descends to be born.

When the Buddha spoke these words, the fourfold assembly, devas, nāgas, and the eight groups, hearing what the Buddha had taught, all rejoiced greatly. They bowed to the Buddha and departed.
