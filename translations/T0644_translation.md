# 佛說金剛三昧本性清淨不壞不滅經
## Sūtra of the Vajra Samādhi of Intrinsic Purity, Indestructible and Imperishable, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0644

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the land of Vaiśālī, at the Great Grove Monastery, in the upper-story lecture hall, together with a great assembly of five thousand bhikṣus: the Venerable Mahākāśyapa, the Venerable Śāriputra, the Venerable Mahāmaudgalyāyana, the Venerable Mahākātyāyana, and others well known to the assembly; and eighteen thousand bodhisattva mahāsattvas: the Bodhisattva Mañjuśrī, the Bodhisattva Brahmaguṇa, the Bodhisattva Prabhāguṇa, the Bodhisattva Nakṣatraguṇa, the Bodhisattva Siṃharāja, the Bodhisattva Siṃhagarbha, the Bodhisattva Mañjughoṣa, the Bodhisattva Śvetagandhahastin, the Bodhisattva Vajradhvaja, the Bodhisattva Vimuktimati, the Bodhisattva Sumerulakṣaṇa, and the Bodhisattva Maitreya Mahāsattva, all such as these being the foremost. From other lands there were also the Bodhisattva Prajñāguṇa, the Bodhisattva Nakṣatraguṇa, the Bodhisattva Sadālaṃkāra, the Bodhisattva Samantaprabha, the Bodhisattva Samantabhadra, the Bodhisattva Pūrṇacandra, the Bodhisattva Avalokiteśvara, the Bodhisattva Mahāsthāmaprāpta, the Bodhisattva Mañjughoṣa, the Bodhisattva Ākāśagarbha, and the Bodhisattva Viśuddhanāda, all such bodhisattva mahāsattvas, eighteen thousand in all. Together with Brahmā, Śakra, the world-protecting heavenly kings, and innumerable deva-sons; the Nāga King Nanda, the Nāga King Upananda, and the four great nāga kings with their retinues of hundreds of thousands of nāgas, each holding a wish-fulfilling gem-king as an offering to the Buddha; the king of the gandharvas, the king of the asuras, the king of the garuḍas, the king of the mahoragas, and the great yakṣa kings, each with their retinues innumerable in number, holding hard black aloeswood and sandalwood from the coast of the sea, and various mixed incenses as offerings to the Buddha. From another land, a Brahmā king named Viśālalocana, together with ten thousand brahmās including Sumatibhāsa and Jālaprabhā, held heavenly mandārava flowers and great mandārava flowers and scattered them upon the Buddha and the great assembly. The subtle celestial flowers scattered by the brahmās were soft, fresh, and exceedingly lovely. Above the Buddha they transformed into a canopy of flowers, brilliantly adorning the upper-story lecture hall, which was like a pure land adorned with the seven treasures.

At that time the World-Honored One came forth from the monastery and went to the Dharma-seat. He spread his sitting-cloth himself, sat cross-legged, and entered the samādhi of cessation of mental activity, body and mind unmoving. Arising from the samādhi of cessation of mental activity, he entered the lion's roar samādhi. Arising from that, he entered the lion's vigorous power king samādhi. Arising from that, he entered the great radiance king samādhi. Arising from that, he entered the great compassion king samādhi. Arising from that, he entered the unconditioned loving-kindness contemplation samādhi. Arising from that, he entered the supreme loving-kindness samādhi. Arising from that, he entered the great emptiness samādhi. Arising from that, he entered the suchness samādhi. Arising from that, he entered the liberation-characteristic samādhi. Arising from that, he entered the indestructible and imperishable king samādhi. Arising from that, he entered the vajra samādhi. Arising from that, he entered the great emptiness nirvāṇa-characteristic samādhi.

At that time, the World-Honored One arose from all these samādhis and emitted light from his entire body. The light was like a cloud, entering the Buddha's face-gate and emerging from the crown of his head, like a vajra banner abiding in space, universally illuminating the great assembly, the city of Vaiśālī, and the upper-story lecture hall, all in the color of white jewels. When the entire great assembly beheld this sight, Maitreya immediately rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, circumambulated the Buddha seven times, bowed at the Buddha's feet, placed his right knee on the ground, and said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! O great ṛṣi of the Tathāgatas! For what reason today have you entered this supreme samādhi, with radiance more brilliant than ever before? Surely you are about to teach the dharma-princes the dharma of the Dharma-King's position and the Dharma-King's ground of practice. How does a bodhisattva mahāsattva abide in the Śūraṅgama Samādhi? By what adornments, what skillful means, and what cultivation of wisdom does one abide in the vajra samādhi and thereby accomplish anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi?" At that time the great assembly, hearing the Bodhisattva Maitreya ask the Buddha this question, were all filled with great joy. With one voice they praised the Bodhisattva Maitreya: "Excellent! Excellent! O Dharma-prince! You are able to ask the Buddha such a great question."

At that time the World-Honored One said to the Bodhisattva Maitreya: "Listen carefully, reflect well upon it! I shall now explain for you in detail the dharma of the grounds of merit practiced by the bodhisattva. The bodhisattva of the first ground is like the new moon: its radiance has not yet fully appeared, yet all its bright characteristics are already complete. The bodhisattva of the second ground is like the moon on the fifth day. The third ground is like the eighth-day moon. The fourth ground is like the ninth-day moon. The fifth ground is like the tenth-day moon. The sixth ground is like the eleventh-day moon. The seventh ground is like the twelfth-day moon. The eighth ground is like the thirteenth-day moon. The ninth ground is like the fourteenth-day moon. The tenth-ground bodhisattva is like the fifteenth-day moon: full and beautiful to behold, its brightness complete. The mind is tranquil and settled, abiding without moving, neither sinking nor retreating, abiding in the Śūraṅgama Samādhi. When the bodhisattva abides in the Śūraṅgama Samādhi, it is like the palace of the Moon Deva, made of ten treasures, from which grow ten treasure-trees whose fruit is the moon-essence maṇi gem. Through the power of this gem, the palace of the Moon Deva traverses Jambudvīpa, universally bestowing coolness. The bodhisattva mahāsattva abiding in the Śūraṅgama Samādhi is likewise.

"Maitreya, you should know! After the bodhisattva mahāsattva has abided in the Śūraṅgama Samādhi and cultivated the hundred samādhi-gates, only then does one enter the vajra samādhi. What are the hundred? First, the nature-emptiness king samādhi. Second, the empty-ocean samādhi. Third, the empty-realm samādhi. Fourth, the cessation-of-emptiness samādhi. Fifth, the great emptiness samādhi. Sixth, the non-abiding-in-emptiness samādhi. Seventh, the non-seeing-of-mind samādhi. Eighth, the wisdom-seal-of-emptiness samādhi. Ninth, the space-non-abiding samādhi. Tenth, the empty-king-indestructible samādhi. Eleventh, the great-vigorous-power king samādhi. Twelfth, the flower-adornment samādhi. Thirteenth, the universal-manifestation radiance king samādhi. Fourteenth, the sunlight samādhi. Fifteenth, the sun-treasury samādhi. Sixteenth, the blazing-sunlight samādhi. Seventeenth, the universal-sun samādhi. Eighteenth, the gathering-of-sounds samādhi. Nineteenth, the silent-radiance samādhi. Twentieth, the cessation-of-sense-objects samādhi. Twenty-first, the trembling samādhi. Twenty-second, the great-trembling samādhi. Twenty-third, the universal-trembling samādhi. Twenty-fourth, the all-pervading-trembling samādhi. Twenty-fifth, the universal-surging samādhi. Twenty-sixth, the universal-roaring samādhi. Twenty-seventh, the universal-adornment samādhi. Twenty-eighth, the lion-characteristic samādhi. Twenty-ninth, the lion-power king samādhi. Thirtieth, the lion's-roar-power king samādhi. Thirty-first, the sun-blaze samādhi. Thirty-second, the wisdom-torch samādhi. Thirty-third, the universal-gate samādhi. Thirty-fourth, the lotus-treasury samādhi. Thirty-fifth, the indestructible-purity samādhi. Thirty-sixth, the cessation-of-thought samādhi. Thirty-seventh, the jewel-seal samādhi. Thirty-eighth, the māra-shaking samādhi. Thirty-ninth, the firm-abiding-in-emptiness samādhi. Fortieth, the universal-cessation samādhi. Forty-first, the arising-stillness samādhi. Forty-second, the adornment-of-marks samādhi. Forty-third, the Dharma-King's-position radiance samādhi. Forty-fourth, the Dharma-wheel-manifestation samādhi. Forty-fifth, the vajra-treasury samādhi. Forty-sixth, the vajra-banner samādhi. Forty-seventh, the vajra-seal samādhi. Forty-eighth, the vajra-assembly samādhi. Forty-ninth, the great-compassion king samādhi. Fiftieth, the non-action-compassion samādhi. Fifty-first, the great-compassion-superior-thought samādhi. Fifty-second, the non-abiding-compassion samādhi. Fifty-third, the solar-disk-radiance samādhi. Fifty-fourth, the cessation-of-all-marks-and-subjugation-of-māras samādhi. Fifty-fifth, the superior-thought-compassion samādhi. Fifty-sixth, the lapis-lazuli-radiance samādhi. Fifty-seventh, the seven-treasure-fruit-radiance samādhi. Fifty-eighth, the Buddha-assembly-treasury samādhi. Fifty-ninth, the merit-perfection-supreme samādhi. Sixtieth, the skillful-wisdom samādhi. Sixty-first, the non-wisdom-characteristic samādhi. Sixty-second, the great-ocean-radiance samādhi. Sixty-third, the Buddha-ocean-fullness samādhi. Sixty-fourth, the universal-ocean samādhi. Sixty-fifth, the ocean-wisdom samādhi. Sixty-sixth, the unmoved-wisdom samādhi. Sixty-seventh, the past-Buddhas'-seal samādhi. Sixty-eighth, the dhāraṇī-assembly samādhi. Sixty-ninth, the dhāraṇī-seal-and-cord samādhi. Seventieth, the eight-eloquences samādhi. Seventy-first, the complete-Brahmā-voice samādhi. Seventy-second, the white-ūrṇā-ocean samādhi. Seventy-third, the wisdom-radiance samādhi. Seventy-fourth, the penetrating-wisdom samādhi. Seventy-fifth, the all-Buddhas'-seal-and-script samādhi. Seventy-sixth, the white-light-surging-radiance king samādhi. Seventy-seventh, the skillful-wisdom-pure Śūraṅgama samādhi. Seventy-eighth, the Sumeru-peak samādhi. Seventy-ninth, the Brahmā-peak samādhi. Eightieth, the universal-penetration-radiance samādhi. Eighty-first, the penetrating-wisdom-radiance samādhi. Eighty-second, the sweet-dew-supreme samādhi. Eighty-third, the pure-five-eyes samādhi. Eighty-fourth, the divine-eye-seal samādhi. Eighty-fifth, the wisdom-eye-seal samādhi. Eighty-sixth, the Dharma-thought-gem samādhi. Eighty-seventh, the space-color samādhi. Eighty-eighth, the mind-non-attachment samādhi. Eighty-ninth, the cessation-of-speech samādhi. Ninetieth, the no-mind-and-thought samādhi. Ninety-first, the precept-wisdom samādhi. Ninety-second, the crown-supreme-one samādhi. Ninety-third, the mind-taming samādhi. Ninety-fourth, the non-seeing-wisdom samādhi. Ninety-fifth, the severing-twelve-links samādhi. Ninety-sixth, the vajra-radiance-wisdom samādhi. Ninety-seventh, the maṇi-blaze samādhi. Ninety-eighth, the vajra-seat-manifestation samādhi. Ninety-ninth, the Dharma-wheel-king's-roar-power samādhi. One hundredth, the receiving-the-Dharma-King's-seal samādhi.

"Maitreya, you should know! These hundred samādhis are like maṇi gems whose lights illuminate one another, following one into the ocean of the Śūraṅgama Samādhi. After the bodhisattva mahāsattva has abided in these hundred samādhis, his wisdom is like the sun in the sky, and the ocean of afflictions is like a wisp of smoke.

"Maitreya, you should know! Just as from the great Anavatapta pool flow four great rivers, and these four rivers divide into eight rivers, and all the streams of Jambudvīpa flow into the great ocean, and because of the Vaḍavāgni fire the ocean does not increase, and because of the vajra wheel the ocean does not decrease, and because this vajra wheel turns according to the season, the water of the great ocean is of one salty taste; so too are these hundred samādhis. Maitreya, you should know! Just as a wheel-turning king, through the power of the ten wholesome deeds, has the seven treasures come to him, and the golden wheel-treasure is of supreme authority, universally subduing all, and the spirit-gem treasure fulfills the wishes of sentient beings without obstruction, and through his thousand sons he is adorned with might and valor. When this wheel-turning king wishes to travel, hairs grow beneath his feet and he treads upon the void, and ten treasure-flowers support his feet.

"Maitreya, you should know! These hundred samādhis are born from the wisdom of the seed of the Way and the ten perfections. They abide in peace without departing, and also do not dwell in the unconditioned stillness; they abide on the Blazing Ground. On this Blazing Ground, without perfuming and without cultivation, one naturally attains eighty-four thousand samādhi-gates. These samādhis are like a vajra mountain, indestructible. They ultimately abide at the edge of great emptiness and also enter the signless dharma-realm. Among all dharmas, one sees no coming, going, abiding, or ceasing. The mind is utterly still, and one then transcends and enters the vajra samādhi. This vajra samādhi is like the Indra gem upon the crown of the Brahmā King: its color and form are not seen, yet it has radiance. The vajra samādhi does not see the ocean of the latent tendencies or the shores of those tendencies.

"Maitreya, you should know! Just as the fire-gem of the Sovereign Deva is without form or characteristic, yet has radiance, soft and lovely, and can rain fragrant flowers to delight the devas, and further emits golden radiance that outshines the bodily light of all the devas; Maitreya, you should know, this fire-gem's radiance is without mind or consciousness, yet when it would dispel darkness, through the power of the gem the darkness naturally ceases, and the bodily radiance of the devas becomes many times brighter than usual. The vajra samādhi is likewise: without extinguishing the latent tendencies, the ocean of tendencies dries up of itself; without severing birth and death, the three poisons are extinguished of themselves.

"Maitreya, you should know! Just as a strong man has a bright gem upon his forehead and a gem behind his elbow, and through mantras he always conceals these gems, not letting others see them; so the great radiance of the vajra samādhi is hidden in stillness. Without seeing the latent tendencies, the mountain of tendencies collapses of itself. Without observing the afflictions, the four great elements are extinguished, the rivers of craving dry up, and the wind of impermanence is cut off. Maitreya, you should know! Just as the lion-king shakes his awesome power and roars greatly, and all beasts naturally submit: the vajra samādhi, emerging from vipaśyanā, enters śamatha, like a vajra sword entering a vajra mountain, leaving no trace. This vajra samādhi does not abide and does not arise, does not cease and is not destroyed, does not sever and does not differ, does not liberate and does not change. Entering the nature of wisdom-radiance, it is raised up from the profoundly deep, and with the wisdom of one-unified-characteristic, seeing neither body, mind, nor dharma, one then accomplishes anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi. This bodhi-wisdom neither separates nor arises; it has no manifold characteristics and cannot be destroyed. Like a vajra mountain, nothing can cause it to tremble. The vajra samādhi neither retreats nor sinks. It enters the place of ultimate great cessation and roams freely in the ocean of samādhis. It is through the power of this king of samādhis that all the Tathāgatas universally reach all the emptiness of the dharma-realm and are able to roam freely in the holy realm of liberation."

When the Buddha had spoken these words, the Bodhisattva Maitreya immediately attained the hundred dharma-gates of illumination. The great assembly of bodhisattvas, with joy in body and mind, attained either the Śūraṅgama Samādhi or the hundred dharma-gates of illumination, their number immeasurable. Brahmā, Śakra, the world-protecting heavenly kings, and the deva-sons rained celestial flowers and played all manner of music as offerings to the Buddha. The great assembly with one voice praised the Bodhisattva Maitreya: "Excellent! Excellent! Good man! You are able to ask the Tathāgata about such supreme and great wisdom. Through you we have drunk the supreme nectar of the Dharma and obtained great benefit. May the Venerable One inquire on our behalf: what merit will sentient beings of the future obtain upon hearing this dharma?"

The Buddha said to the great assembly: "Listen carefully and reflect well upon it! In the past, ninety-one kalpas ago, there was a Blessed Buddha named Ratnakusuma, complete in the ten titles. At that time, the Buddha Ratnakusuma broadly taught the bodhisattvas the hundred samādhi-gates just as I have described. In that assembly there were a thousand bhikṣus who, hearing the Buddha teach this samādhi, rejoiced in body and mind. Through the power of the causes and conditions of that wholesome root of sympathetic joy, they transcended five hundred billion asaṃkhyeya kalpas of the wrongdoing of birth and death. Who were those thousand bhikṣus? They are none other than the thousand Buddhas of the present Fortunate Kalpa."

The Buddha said to the great assembly: "After the Buddha has entered nirvāṇa, if bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, upāsikās, devas, nāgas, the eightfold assembly, and all the rest, even briefly hear the Buddha's supreme wisdom and deeply rejoice without giving rise to slander, for a hundred thousand kalpas they shall never fall into the three evil destinies. In every life they shall constantly encounter Buddhas and bodhisattvas as their companions. If anyone hears this dharma without doubt or slander, after their life ends they shall certainly be born in the Tuṣita heaven, where they shall encounter Maitreya and hear the teaching of the profound and irreversible ground of the turning of the Dharma-wheel. If there are those who receive, uphold, read, recite, explain, and copy this sūtra, making offerings with incense, flowers, music, and all manner of things, at the end of their lives, if they can singlemindedly contemplate the Buddha's Dharma-body, at that moment ninety billion Buddhas shall appear together to extend their hands to them. They shall be born as they wish in the pure lands of any Buddha, roaming freely in the ocean of samādhis."

The Buddha told Maitreya and instructed Ānanda: "You should receive and uphold this well. Do not forget it. Up to the time of the extinction of the Dharma, it should be broadly proclaimed."

Ānanda said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! What shall this sūtra be called? What is the essential teaching of this dharma, and how should it be upheld?"

The Buddha said to Ānanda: "This sūtra is called the 'Ocean of the Hundred Samādhis, Indestructible and Imperishable.' It is also called 'The Vajra Characteristic: Serene Cessation, Unmoving.' It is also called 'The Sūtra of the Vajra Samādhi of Intrinsic Purity, Indestructible and Imperishable.' It should be reverently upheld."

When the Buddha had spoken these words, Śāriputra and the other great śrāvakas, Maitreya and the other great bodhisattvas, the devas, nāgas, the eightfold assembly, and the entire great assembly were all filled with great joy. They bowed to the Buddha and withdrew.
