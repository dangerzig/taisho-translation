# 佛說戒德香經
## Sūtra on the Fragrance of Moral Virtue Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0116

Translated from the Chinese. Eastern Jin dynasty, translated by Zhu Tanwulan (竺曇無蘭)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was traveling in the country of Śrāvastī, in the Jetavana, the garden of Anāthapiṇḍada. At that time, the Venerable Ānanda, dwelling alone in quiet contemplation, thought: "In the world there are three kinds of fragrance: first, the fragrance of roots; second, the fragrance of branches; third, the fragrance of flowers. These three kinds of fragrance only travel with the wind, not against it. Is there a fine fragrance that travels both with and against the wind?"

The Venerable Ānanda, contemplating alone the destination of this question and not knowing where it led, rose from his seat and went to where the Buddha was. He bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, knelt with joined palms, and addressed the Buddha: "Dwelling alone in thought, I considered that the world has three kinds of fragrance: first, the fragrance of roots; second, the fragrance of branches; third, the fragrance of flowers. These three kinds of fragrance can only travel with the wind, not against it. Is there a fine fragrance that travels both with and against the wind?"

The Buddha told Ānanda: "Excellent! Excellent! Just as you ask, there is a true and right fragrance that travels both with and against the wind."

Ānanda addressed the Buddha: "I wish to hear of this fragrance."

The Buddha said: "If in a district, province, county, town, or village there are good men and good women who cultivate the ten wholesome actions, whose bodies do not kill, steal, or engage in sexual misconduct, whose mouths do not speak falsely, divisively, harshly, or frivolously, whose minds are free from jealousy, anger, and delusion, who are filial to their parents, serve the Three Treasures, and possess benevolence, compassion, moral virtue, dignified deportment, and proper etiquette, then śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas in the numberless lands of the east sing the praises of their virtue. Śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas of the south, west, north, the four intermediate directions, above and below, all sing the praises of their virtue: 'In such-and-such a district, land, county, town, or village, there are good men and good women who practice the ten wholesome actions, respectfully serve the Three Treasures, are filial, benevolent, compassionate, virtuous, gracious, and upright, never failing in propriety.' This fragrance is called 'traveling with the wind and against the wind,' pervading and illuminating everywhere, proclaimed in the ten directions, benefiting all."

The Buddha then spoke in verse:

> Though there are beautiful fragrant flowers, they cannot perfume against the wind.
> Not to mention candana and all the fragrances of the many rains.
> But a disposition that is harmonious and refined: that fragrance indeed goes against the wind.
> The righteous one, called a true person, perfumes universally in the ten directions.
> Tagara, candana, blue lotus, and the many rain-fragrances:
> Among all these fragrances, the fragrance of moral conduct is supreme.
> Those who are thus pure, whose conduct is without heedlessness,
> Do not know Māra's pathways, do not see the destinations of return.
> This path leads to eternal peace; this path is the most supreme.
> What is gained cuts off the source of defilement, conquering and severing Māra's net.
> Using the hall of the supreme Buddha-path, one ascends to inexhaustible wisdom.
> With this, proclaim the meaning of the sūtras and remove all faults.

The Buddha told Ānanda: "This fragrance spreads without being obstructed by Mount Sumeru, rivers, heaven, or earth; without being obstructed by the four elements of earth, water, fire, and wind. It penetrates the eight directions, above and below likewise, through boundless realms where all sing of its virtue.

"One who does not kill in this body will have long life in every lifetime, without an untimely death. One who does not steal will be wealthy and abundant in every lifetime, without mistakenly losing possessions; treasures will always be preserved; generosity is the root of the path. One who does not engage in sexual misconduct will have a spouse whom no one violates, and wherever one is reborn, it will be within a lotus. One who does not speak falsely will have a fragrant mouth; whatever one says will be trusted. One who does not speak divisively will always have harmony in the household, without any separation. One who does not speak harshly will always have a fine tongue, with eloquent and fluent speech. One who does not speak frivolously will have words that all who hear them receive with respect and treasure. One who is free from jealousy will be respected by all people wherever one is born. One who is free from anger will be handsome in every lifetime, and people will rejoice upon seeing them. One who is free from delusion will have wisdom wherever one is born, and all will come to ask counsel. Abandoning wrong views, one constantly abides in the right path. From one's actions, each result naturally arises. Therefore one should abandon what is wrong and follow what is true and wonderful."

The Buddha spoke thus. At that time, the bhikṣus, having heard, rejoiced, paid their respects, and departed.
