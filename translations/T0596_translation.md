# 佛說天王太子辟羅經
## Sūtra of Piluo, Prince of the Heavenly King, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0596

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was in Śrāvastī, at the Jetavana Monastery of Anāthapiṇḍada. A prince of the heavenly king, named Piluo, flew down from heaven and arrived at the Buddha's presence. He prostrated with the five points of his body on the ground, bowed at the Buddha's feet, then stood to one side with his palms joined and asked the Buddha: "All the people of the world seek clothing and food, the seven treasures and every pleasure, official rank and territory. But is there a precious practice that seeks out a person?"

The World-Honored One sighed and said: "A great question indeed! There are indeed lands, precious treasures, and every delight that go seeking a person."

Piluo said: "As for this wish-fulfilling practice that goes seeking a person: what does it mean?"

The World-Honored One replied: "There are two kinds of conduct. Good conduct brings merit; evil conduct brings calamity. Calamity and merit follow a person, just as a shadow follows the body."

Piluo said: "Excellent! Excellent! It is truly as the Buddha teaches. In my past life, I lived in the world as a king. Mindful that life is impermanent, I wished to give generously. I assembled all my ministers and said to them: 'I wish to have a great drum made, whose sound when struck can be heard for a hundred miles. Is there anyone who can make it?' All the ministers said: 'None of us are able to do so.' There was one minister named Kuangshang, who was always loyal to his lord and compassionate in aiding the people. He came forward and said: 'I am able to do it, but I shall need resources.' The king said: 'Excellent!' He immediately opened the treasury and entrusted the resources to him. The minister had the treasures carted to the palace gate and had the drum struck with this proclamation: 'The benevolent king's generosity is boundless in its compassion. He wishes to aid the people in their poverty and destitution, and to provide clothing and food for those who practice the Way. If there are any in need, let them all come to the palace gate.' The destitute from all four quarters came carrying their children on their backs, supporting one another, filling the land and blocking the roads. They looked up to heaven and sighed: 'We who are impoverished among the people have today been given life!'

"After one year, the king issued a decree: 'Is the drum finished?' The minister replied: 'It is finished.' The king said: 'Then why have I not heard its sound?' The minister said: 'May the wise king trouble his noble person to come forth and inspect the land within his domain, and listen to the Dharma-drum of the Buddha, whose sound shakes the ten directions.' The king then ordered his carriage prepared and went forth through his land. His people were shoulder to shoulder. The king said: 'How numerous the people are!' The minister replied: 'Previously Your Majesty commanded me to make a great drum whose sound could be heard for a hundred miles, in order to spread Your Majesty's renown to the four corners. But I thought that dry wood and dead hide cannot spread Your Majesty's merit and fame. The treasure I received I used to provide clothing and food for śramaṇas and brahmins, and to aid the people in their destitution. When word was spread, the neighboring peoples came seeking sustenance, as hungry children flock to a loving mother.' The king asked the people: 'From where have you come?' The people bowed and said: 'Some have come a hundred miles, some two hundred miles, some even ten thousand miles.' All said: 'The wise king's great grace refreshes the four quarters. That is why we have left the lands of our birth and come seeking this sustenance to save ourselves.' The king said: 'Excellent! I understand now. When a country is not at peace, it is like the body being ill. I cure it with medicine, and my minister feeds them with rice-gruel.' The king said: 'Whatever the people seek, provide it for them. There is no need to report to me.'

"Afterward, when the king's life ended, his spirit ascended to be born in heaven, becoming a wondrous heavenly king. When his heavenly lifespan ended, he descended and was reborn in the world as a flying wheel-turning emperor, at ease wherever he went, with the seven treasures flying before and behind in his retinue. Now he has ascended to heaven again and become a prince of the heavenly king. The reason for all this is that by personally keeping the precepts and providing for all beings, this is what he obtained. If one upholds the Buddha's precepts and keeps one's body and mind in right conduct, there is none who does not obtain such merit."

The Buddha said to Piluo: "The conduct of every person is like a shadow following the body, like an echo answering a voice: there is nothing that does not receive its recompense."

Piluo was filled with joy, bowed, and departed.
