# 十一面神呪心經
## Heart Sūtra of the Eleven-Faced Divine Spell

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1071

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Xuanzang (玄奘).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Bhagavān was dwelling in the Bamboo Shoot Practice-Site in Śrāvastī, together with a great assembly of twelve hundred and fifty bhikṣus, as well as bodhisattva-mahāsattvas beyond number, with Maitreya Bodhisattva at their head. Also present were countless bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, upāsikās, as well as devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, kumbhāṇḍas, piśācas, humans, and non-humans, all assembled in a great congregation surrounding him, making offerings, paying respects, honoring, and praising him as he expounded the dharma.

At that time Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, together with countless koṭi-nayuta-hundred-thousand spell-holding divine sages, surrounded on all sides, came to the place of the Buddha. Having arrived, he prostrated at the World-Honored One's feet, circumambulated him three times to the right, and took a seat to one side. He addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I have a divine spell-heart called 'Eleven-Faced,' possessing great awesome power, spoken by one hundred and ten koṭis of buddhas. I now speak it, wishing to benefit and bring ease to all sentient beings: to eliminate all illness, to extinguish all evil, to stop all ill omens, to ward off all evil dreams, to prevent all untimely death, to cause those with evil minds to become pure, to give those with worry and suffering ease, to bring reconciliation to those with enmity, to cause all māra-ghost obstructions to be eliminated, and to cause all wishes of the heart to be fulfilled. World-Honored One! I do not see anyone in the world, whether deva, māra, brahmā, śramaṇa, brāhmaṇa, or any other, who, having used this divine spell to protect their body, received, upheld, read, recited, copied, or circulated it, could be harmed by any calamity, māra-obstacle, weapon, poison, sorcery, or curse. Nor do I see anyone who, having established a boundary with this divine spell in any place, near or far, could be violated by any being coming to harass or harm them, except for those whose determined evil karma must ripen. World-Honored One! You should know that this matter is certainly so. One should accept it with faith and not entertain doubts, for thus all calamities will be kept far away and unable to approach. This divine spell-heart is praised by all buddhas alike, approved with joy by all buddhas alike, and held in memory and guarded by all tathāgatas. World-Honored One! I recall that in the past, beyond the sands of the Ganges in kalpas gone by, there was a buddha who appeared in the world named Hundred-Lotus-Eyed Unobstructed Supreme Blazing-Merit Light King Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha. At that time I was a great sage, and from that World-Honored One I received this spell. When I received this spell, I saw the buddhas of the ten directions, and at once I attained the patient endurance of the birthless. Know therefore that this spell possesses great awesome power. Therefore, if pure-faithed good men, good women, or others wish to receive, uphold, read, and recite this divine spell, they should do so with reverence and single-minded attention. Each morning, after purifying themselves according to the method, they should recite this spell one hundred and eight times. If one can do this, one will obtain ten kinds of supreme benefit in this present body. What are the ten? First, the body will always be free from illness. Second, one will always be received and embraced by the buddhas of the ten directions. Third, wealth, clothing, food, and provisions will be inexhaustible. Fourth, one can subdue enemies and be free from fear. Fifth, one will cause the honored and noble to address one first with respect. Sixth, sorcery, poisonous ghosts, and malevolent spirits will be unable to cause harm. Seventh, all weapons will be unable to cause injury. Eighth, water will be unable to drown one. Ninth, fire will be unable to burn one. Tenth, one will never die an untimely death. One will further obtain four kinds of meritorious benefit: first, at the time of death, one will see all the buddhas. Second, one will never fall into the evil destinies. Third, one will not die through dangerous adversity. Fourth, one will be reborn in the Land of Ultimate Bliss. World-Honored One! I recall that in the past, beyond ten times the sands of the Ganges in kalpas, and still beyond that, there was a buddha who appeared in the world named Beautiful-Sound Fragrance Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha. At that time I was in the body of a great householder, and from that buddha I received this spell. Having received this spell, I transcended the realm of birth and death for forty thousand kalpas. Through reciting and upholding this spell, I further attained the great compassion wisdom-treasury and the liberation dharma-gate of all bodhisattvas of all buddhas. By this awesome power, I can rescue all those in prison, bound in fetters, stocks, and chains, facing execution, and those endangered by water, fire, wind, bandits, sorcery, curses, and humans and non-humans, from all manner of suffering and difficulty. Through this, I can serve as refuge, savior, comforter, haven, and shelter for all sentient beings. By the power of this spell, I gather in all fierce and evil yakṣas and rākṣasas, first causing them to generate minds of loving-kindness and compassion, and then establishing them in *anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi*. World-Honored One! My divine spell has great awesome power. If one recites it even once, it can eliminate the four root offenses and the five heinous crimes without remainder; how much more so for one who can practice as taught. If anyone has planted roots of merit before hundreds of thousands of koṭi-nayuta buddhas, only then can they hear this spell in the present age; how much more so for one who can receive, uphold, and practice as taught. If one can read, recite, receive, and uphold this divine spell day and night, I will cause all that person's wishes to be fulfilled. If there are those who, on the half-month days of the fourteenth or fifteenth, observe the precepts, purify themselves according to the method, fix their minds upon me, and recite this divine spell, they will transcend the realm of birth and death for forty thousand kalpas. World-Honored One! Through this spell, my name and title are honored and difficult to hear. If one calls upon the names of hundreds of thousands of koṭi-nayuta buddhas, and another person briefly calls upon my name with utmost sincerity, the merit of those two is equal. All who call upon my name will attain the stage of non-retrogression, be freed from all illness, liberated from all obstacles and fears, and will eliminate all evil of body, speech, and mind. How much more so for one who can receive, uphold, read, recite, and practice according to the divine spell I have spoken; know that such a person holds supreme enlightenment in the palm of their hand."

At that time the World-Honored One praised Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva: "Excellent, excellent! Good man! You are able to give rise to such great compassion for all sentient beings and wish to reveal this great divine spell. Good man! By this skillful means you can rescue and liberate all sentient beings from all illness, suffering, obstacles, difficulties, and fears, from all evil of body, speech, and mind, and can even establish all sentient beings in *anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi*. Good man! I too rejoice in and accept your divine spell. You may now speak it." Then Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva Mahāsattva rose from his seat, bared one shoulder, placed his right knee on the ground, and addressed the Buddha: "One who recites this spell should speak thus:

"Homage to the Three Treasures. Homage to Holy Wisdom-Ocean Universally-Illuminating Adornment King Tathāgata. Homage to all tathāgatas, arhats, samyak-saṃbuddhas. Homage to Holy Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, the Great Compassionate One.

*Tadyathā oṃ dhara dhara dhiri dhiri dhuru dhuru itti vitti cale cale pracale pracale kusume kusuma-vare ili mili citi jala-mapanāya bodhi-sattva mahā-kāruṇika svāhā*

"World-Honored One! This is the root divine spell. If one recites it, one will obtain all the meritorious benefits described above."

"Homage to the Three Treasures. Homage to Holy Wisdom-Ocean Universally-Illuminating Adornment King Tathāgata. Homage to all tathāgatas, arhats, samyak-saṃbuddhas. Homage to Holy Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, the Great Compassionate One.

*Tadyathā ha ha ha ha ile mile cile vile kile hile svāhā*

"World-Honored One! This is the spell for empowering water and clothing. When wishing to enter the practice hall, one should first bathe. Then empower water with this spell seven times and sprinkle it on the body for purification. Further empower the clothing with this spell seven times, and then put it on."

"Homage to the Three Treasures. Homage to Holy Wisdom-Ocean Universally-Illuminating Adornment King Tathāgata. Homage to all tathāgatas, arhats, samyak-saṃbuddhas. Homage to Holy Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, the Great Compassionate One.

*Tadyathā juru juru ha ha ha ha svāhā*

"World-Honored One! This is the spell for empowering incense and lamps. When entering the practice hall and wishing to burn incense as an offering, first empower the incense with this spell seven times, and then burn it. When wishing to light a lamp, first empower the oil with this spell seven times, and then light the lamp."

"Homage to the Three Treasures. Homage to Holy Wisdom-Ocean Universally-Illuminating Adornment King Tathāgata. Homage to all tathāgatas, arhats, samyak-saṃbuddhas. Homage to Holy Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, the Great Compassionate One.

*Tadyathā śrī śrī dhiri dhiri dhiri śrī svāhā*

"World-Honored One! This is the spell for empowering flowers, incense, and garlands. When entering the practice hall and wishing to offer flowers, incense, and garlands, first empower the flowers seven times with this spell and scatter them before the image. Further empower the incense seven times and apply it to the image. Further empower the garland seven times and adorn the image with it."

"Homage to the Three Treasures. Homage to Holy Wisdom-Ocean Universally-Illuminating Adornment King Tathāgata. Homage to all tathāgatas, arhats, samyak-saṃbuddhas. Homage to Holy Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, the Great Compassionate One.

*Tadyathā sādhe sādhe sādhi sādhi saudhā saudhā svāhā*

"World-Honored One! This is the spell for empowering offerings to the Buddha. When wishing to offer food and drink, flowers, fruits, and other items, first recite this spell twenty-one times to empower them, and then present the offering."

"Homage to the Three Treasures. Homage to Holy Wisdom-Ocean Universally-Illuminating Adornment King Tathāgata. Homage to all tathāgatas, arhats, samyak-saṃbuddhas. Homage to Holy Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, the Great Compassionate One.

*Tadyathā maśi-da-si jala jala huru huru juru juru suru suru muru muru svāhā*

"World-Honored One! This is the spell for empowering firewood. When wishing to perform an activity using the above root divine spell, first empower *jātī* flower-wood once with this spell, to be used for kindling the fire. Then separately take *jātī* flower-wood, cut into inch-long pieces totaling thirty-one segments, and soak them overnight in a mixture of *dadhi*, ghee, and honey. Take one piece at a time, empower it with one recitation, and cast it into the fire until all are finished. Then proceed with whatever activity is to be performed."

"Homage to the Three Treasures. Homage to Holy Wisdom-Ocean Universally-Illuminating Adornment King Tathāgata. Homage to all tathāgatas, arhats, samyak-saṃbuddhas. Homage to Holy Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, the Great Compassionate One.

*Tadyathā ili mili pili dhili cili hili svāhā*

"World-Honored One! This is the boundary-establishing spell. When wishing to establish a boundary, first empower water with this spell seven times and scatter it in the four directions. Alternatively, empower mustard seeds or clean ash, each seven times, and scatter them in the four directions. The boundary will be established as far as the mind intends, forming a perimeter of protection."

"Homage to the Three Treasures. Homage to Holy Wisdom-Ocean Universally-Illuminating Adornment King Tathāgata. Homage to all tathāgatas, arhats, samyak-saṃbuddhas. Homage to Holy Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, the Great Compassionate One.

*Tadyathā piṭi piṭi dhiṭi dhiṭi ciṭi ciṭi phiṭi phiṭi gaccha gaccha bhagavān ārya-avalokiteśvara-svabhāvana svāhā*

"World-Honored One! This is the spell for requesting me to return to my own palace. When the activity has been completed and one wishes to request me to return to my palace, empower water with this spell seven times, scatter it in the four directions, and I shall depart."

"World-Honored One! Even without formal establishment, these divine spells can still accomplish various activities. If one recites them with utmost sincerity, no wish will go unfulfilled. If one suffers from malarial fever, whether occurring daily, every other day, every third day, or every fourth day; if one suffers from ghost illness, whether caused by bhūta ghosts, ḍākinīs, piśācas, *kaṭa-pūtanas*, epilepsy ghosts, seizure ghosts, or any other kind of evil ghost, one should empower the afflicted person with this spell one hundred and eight times, and they will be cured. If the karmic obstruction is severe, use a five-colored cord, reciting the spell once per knot for a total of one hundred and eight knots, and tie it around the sick person's neck or arm; the karmic obstruction will be eliminated and the illness cured. If one suffers from boils, abscesses, fistulae, pustules, carbuncles, ulcers, ringworm, or any other kind of evil illness; if one has been wounded by a sword, arrow, lance, or spear; or stung by a snake, scorpion, centipede, or venomous bee, one should empower the afflicted area with this spell seven times for immediate cure. If the obstruction is severe, empower yellow earth made into mud with up to seven recitations and apply it to the afflicted area, and the suffering will be cured. If one suffers from paralysis, hemiplegia, wind-illness, deafness, nasal congestion, or stupor, one should recite this spell with utmost sincerity, empowering the afflicted person one hundred and eight times, and the illness will be cured. If the obstruction is severe, fry birch bark and blue wood-incense in oil or ghee, empower it seven times with the spell, and apply it to the body, or drip it into the ears or nose, or have the patient take it internally, and the affliction will be cured. For all other various illnesses, one should recite this spell over them with utmost sincerity, or recite it oneself, and recovery will follow.

"World-Honored One! If one wishes to formally establish this divine spell, one should carve an image of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva from fine, solid, unblemished white sandalwood, one and a half hand-spans tall. The left hand holds a red lotus and a kuṇḍikā. The right arm is extended, with a rosary, forming the fearlessness-bestowing mudrā. The image should have eleven faces. The front three faces have compassionate expressions. The left three faces have wrathful expressions. The right three faces resemble bodhisattva faces with white fangs protruding upward. The rear face has a fiercely laughing expression. The top face is a buddha face. All the head-crowns contain a buddha image. The body of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva should be adorned with jeweled necklaces and various ornaments. When the image is complete, one who wishes to make a petition should don new clean garments and observe the precepts. From the first of the bright fortnight to the eighth day, recite this spell one hundred and eight times or countless times, three times daily. From then on, in a quiet place, spread a clean seat and install the Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva image facing west. According to one's means, offer various food and drink, and burn only aloeswood and styrax incense. The practitioner should eat only barley and milk. Continue reciting as before until the thirteenth day. From then on, the offerings must be far superior to before. The practitioner should eat only the three whites: milk, yogurt, and rice. Before the image, kindle a fire of bodhi-tree wood. Further take that wood, cut into inch-long pieces totaling one thousand and eight segments. Soak them in *turūṣka* aromatic oil. Take one piece at a time, recite the spell once, and cast it into the fire until all are finished. At that time the great earth will shake and tremble, and the image's body will also move. From the mouth of the topmost face, a voice will emerge praising the practitioner: 'Excellent, excellent! Good man! You have been able to practice so diligently in seeking your vow. I shall cause all your vows to be fulfilled. I shall grant you flight through space from this very place, or grant you unobstructed travel everywhere, or make you king among spell-holding sages, or make you free and unobstructed like me.'

"Furthermore, the practitioner may, on the fifteenth of the bright fortnight, place the Eleven-Faced Avalokiteśvara image in a caitya containing buddha relics, don new clean garments, observe the precepts, and for one day and one night abstain from all food and drink. Take one thousand and eight *sumanā* flowers. Take one flower at a time, empower it with one recitation, and cast it upon the image until all are finished. At that time, from the mouth of the front center face of the image, a voice will emerge like a thunderclap, and the great earth will shake. The practitioner should at that time calm the mind and not be frightened; simply continue reciting the spell. When the thunder sounds, one should immediately make one's petition, speaking thus: 'Homage to Holy Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, the Great Compassionate One! When will I be able to serve as a great refuge for all sentient beings? When will I be able to fulfill the wishes of all sentient beings?' Then Avalokiteśvara will grant the vow. When the vow is granted, the devas, nāgas, and all others will be unable to create any obstacles. Furthermore, the practitioner, at the time of a lunar eclipse, should take one *liang* of ghee in a silver vessel and place it before the image. Recite the spell until the moon returns to its full brightness, then take the ghee and eat it. All illnesses within the body will be completely cured.

"Furthermore, the practitioner should take equal parts of realgar and ox-yellow, place them before the image, and recite the spell one thousand and eight times. Mix with water and apply between the eyebrows: the three kinds of attainment will be accomplished as previously described. If mixed with warm water for bathing the body, all obstacles, evil dreams, and epidemic illnesses will be completely cured.

"Furthermore, if enemies from another region are about to invade one's territory, take one piece of rouge, empower it with this spell one hundred and eight times, and apply it to the wrathful face on the image's left side, facing directly toward the enemy's direction. The enemy forces will be unable to advance.

"Furthermore, if an epidemic breaks out among people or livestock in a kingdom, before the image kindle a fire of *juhva* wood. Further take that wood, cut into inch-long pieces totaling one thousand and eight segments. Take one piece at a time, smear it with mustard seed oil, empower it with one recitation, and cast it into the fire until all are finished. Further take a scarlet cord and make seven spell-knots, one recitation per knot, and tie it to the topknot of the uppermost buddha face. This can cause all epidemic illnesses to be completely eliminated. When the epidemic is cured, remove the spell-cord.

"Furthermore, if someone has suddenly been afflicted by a ḍākinī, bhūta ghost, or other spirit causing illness, take white cord and make twenty-one spell-knots, one recitation per knot. Tie it to the topknot of the front compassionate face for one night, then take it and tie it around the sick person's neck; the illness will be cured. If the karmic obstruction is severe and recovery does not follow, take the cord and empower it with an additional one hundred and eight recitations, tie it to the image's topknot as before for one night, then take it and tie it around the sick person's neck. Recovery will certainly follow.

"Furthermore, if someone has a prolonged illness that will not resolve, or if evil spirits and ghosts enter a dwelling, take one hundred and eight pieces of frankincense. Before the image, empower each piece with one recitation and cast it into the fire until all are finished. Further take white cord, make twenty-one spell-knots, one recitation per knot, and tie it to the topknot of the front compassionate face for one night. Take it and tie it around the sick person's neck; the affliction will be cured and the evil ghosts will retreat and scatter.

"Furthermore, if an enemy is seeking an opportunity to cause harm through conflict, sorcery, or ill-wishing, make offerings of various incense, flowers, and other items to the image. Before the image kindle a fire of *bhārṣaka* wood. Take one hundred and eight rapeseed grains, empower each with one recitation, and cast them into the fire. Further take white cord and tie one hundred and eight knots, one recitation per knot. Tie it to the topknot of the wrathful face on the image's left side for one night. Take the cord and, calling out the enemy's name, cut one knot at a time, each in a separate place, one call per cut until all are finished. The enemy's endeavors will fail, and they will naturally submit.

"Furthermore, if a person wishes to seek good fortune, take five-colored cord and tie a spell-cord of one hundred and eight knots, one recitation per knot. Further, before the image, empower it seven times and tie it to the topknot of the front compassionate face for one night. Take it and tie it to one's own body, and whatever one seeks will be fulfilled.

"Furthermore, if one knows that obstacles exist within one's body, and that one's pursuit of good things often goes unfulfilled, and misfortune frequently arrives without cause, one should bathe the image with fragrant water. Then take this water, empower it with one hundred and eight recitations, and use it to bathe a Vināyaka image. Then empower it with an additional one hundred and eight recitations and sprinkle it over one's own body. All obstacles will naturally be eliminated, and whatever one seeks will be fulfilled."

At that time, when Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva Mahāsattva had finished speaking this sūtra, the entire great assembly praised in unison: "Excellent, excellent! Great One! You are able to speak this divine spell for the benefit and ease of all sentient beings. We, the assembly, rejoice and also wish to receive and uphold it." At that time the great assembly rejoiced greatly and leapt for joy. They circumambulated the Buddha three times, made obeisance, and departed.
