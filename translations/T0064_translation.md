# 瞻婆比丘經
## Sūtra of the Bhikṣu of Campā

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0064

Translated from the Chinese. Western Jin, translated by Faju

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Bhagavat was in Campā, on the bank of the Gaṅgā, beside the Dharma-pool. At that time, on the fifteenth day, the World-Honored One was reciting the prātimokṣa, seated before the assembly of bhikṣus. After the World-Honored One had taken his seat, he observed the thoughts in the minds of the bhikṣus. Having observed the thoughts in the minds of the bhikṣus, during the first watch of the night he sat in silence. Then a certain bhikṣu rose from his seat, arranged his robe to one side, joined his palms toward the World-Honored One, and addressed the World-Honored One: "Sir, World-Honored One! The first watch of the night has already passed. The World-Honored One and the assembly of bhikṣus have been sitting for a long time. May the World-Honored One please recite the prātimokṣa." At that time, the World-Honored One remained in silence. The World-Honored One sat in silence until midnight. The bhikṣu again joined his palms toward the World-Honored One and addressed him: "Sir, World-Honored One! The first watch of the night has passed, and midnight has passed. World-Honored One! The assembly of bhikṣus has been sitting for a long time. May the World-Honored One please recite the prātimokṣa." At that time, the World-Honored One also remained in silence. At that time, the World-Honored One sat in silence past midnight. The bhikṣu a third time joined his palms toward the World-Honored One and addressed him: "May the World-Honored One know that the first watch has passed, midnight has passed, and the time past midnight has come; the morning star is about to appear, and before long it will rise. World-Honored One! The assembly of bhikṣus has been sitting for a long time. May the World-Honored One please recite the prātimokṣa." At that time, the World-Honored One told this bhikṣu: "Among my assembly of bhikṣus, there is one who is impure."

At that time, the Venerable Mahāmaudgalyāyana was also in the assembly. Being present in the assembly, the Venerable Mahāmaudgalyāyana thought: "Of which bhikṣu does the World-Honored One speak when he says there is an impure one in the assembly? I should enter the appropriate samādhi and with my concentrated mind observe the thoughts in the minds of the bhikṣus." Then the Venerable Mahāmaudgalyāyana entered the appropriate samādhi and with his concentrated mind observed the thoughts in the minds of the bhikṣus. The Venerable Mahāmaudgalyāyana immediately knew the bhikṣu of whom the World-Honored One spoke. Then the Venerable Mahāmaudgalyāyana arose from samādhi, went to where that bhikṣu was, seized that bhikṣu by the arm, and led him out through the gate: "You fool, go! You should not stay here. No bhikṣu will dwell with you. You are now outside the bhikṣu community." Then the Venerable Mahāmaudgalyāyana, having seized that bhikṣu by the arm and led him out the gate, shut the door behind him. Having shut the door, he went to where the World-Honored One was, prostrated at the World-Honored One's feet, and sat to one side. Having sat to one side, the Venerable Mahāmaudgalyāyana addressed the World-Honored One: "World-Honored One! The bhikṣu of whom you spoke, saying there is an impure one in the assembly, I have seized him by the arm and led him out the gate, saying, 'You fool, go! You should not stay here. No bhikṣu will dwell with you. You are now outside the bhikṣu community.' Sir, World-Honored One! The first watch of the night has passed, midnight has passed, the time past midnight has come, and dawn is approaching, the morning star about to rise. World-Honored One! The assembly of bhikṣus has been sitting for a long time. May the World-Honored One please recite the prātimokṣa."

"You, Maudgalyāyana! That foolish person has incurred much blame, for he has disturbed the World-Honored One and the assembly of bhikṣus. Maudgalyāyana! If, when there is an impure bhikṣu in the assembly, the Tathāgata were to recite the prātimokṣa, that fool's head would split into seven pieces. Therefore, Maudgalyāyana, from this day forward, you all should recite the prātimokṣa among yourselves. The Tathāgata will no longer come to recite it. Why is this? Maudgalyāyana! This one fool, having committed such transgressions, observes and surveys, bending and stretching, rolling and unfolding, carrying his saṅghāṭī robe and bowl, and among those of the holy life, claims himself to be one of the holy life. This, Maudgalyāyana, if people say he is of the holy life, if people think, 'This śramaṇa is corrupt, this śramaṇa is coarse, this śramaṇa is a thorn, this śramaṇa is not what he claims.' Knowing thus, they cast him out. Why is this? For fear of corrupting the other pure bhikṣus. It is like, Maudgalyāyana, a well-established rice field or wheat field in which harmful weeds grow. Those weeds have roots like wheat roots, and their stalks, joints, leaves, and fruit are likewise. Before they bear seed, they should be discarded. Maudgalyāyana! When the seeds have ripened, the farmer of that field thinks, 'This weed will ruin the wheat, it is a thorn to the wheat, it is coarse among the wheat.' He then pulls it up and casts it outside. Why is this? For fear of ruining the other clean wheat. In the same way, Maudgalyāyana! If a foolish person, having committed such transgressions, observes and surveys, bending and stretching, carrying his saṅghāṭī robe and bowl, and claims to be of the holy life among the other practitioners. Maudgalyāyana! If others call him one of the holy life, and they think, 'This śramaṇa is corrupt, this śramaṇa is a thorn, this śramaṇa is coarse, this śramaṇa is not what he claims.' Knowing thus, they cast him outside. Why is this? For fear of corrupting the other pure bhikṣus. It is like, Maudgalyāyana, that farmer who, in the winter months, works at a great heap of grain. Whatever is solid and remains in place, it all separates to one side, namely whatever chaff or leaves there are. He winnows them into the wind and they blow away in one direction. The farmer stands in the middle holding a broom. Maudgalyāyana! If a foolish person's conduct has transgressed in this way, it is by observing the grain that one sees. In the same way, Maudgalyāyana, if a foolish person, having committed such transgressions, observes and surveys, bending and stretching, carrying his saṅghāṭī robe and bowl, and claims to be of the holy life among the other practitioners. Maudgalyāyana! If others call him one of the holy life, and they think, 'This śramaṇa is corrupt, this śramaṇa is coarse, this śramaṇa is a thorn.' Knowing thus, they cast him outside. Why is this? For fear of corrupting the other bhikṣus. It is like, Maudgalyāyana, that farmer who, wanting water and wishing to channel it, takes a very sharp axe and enters the forest. Striking trees with his axe, whatever trees are solid and firm the axe cannot penetrate. Whatever trees are dry and dead, when struck, the axe sinks in. The farmer cuts at the root, and having cut the root, hollows out the middle. Having hollowed out the middle, he makes a wooden channel to convey the water. In the same way, Maudgalyāyana, if a foolish person's conduct has transgressed in this way, observing and surveying, bending and stretching, carrying his saṅghāṭī robe and bowl, and claiming to be of the holy life among the other practitioners. Maudgalyāyana! If others call him one of the holy life, and they think, 'This śramaṇa is corrupt, this śramaṇa is coarse, this śramaṇa is a thorn, this śramaṇa is not what he claims.' Knowing thus, they cast him outside. Why is this? For fear of corrupting the other pure bhikṣus." He spoke in verse:

> Dwell together and know this well:
> Evil desires and anger,
> Resentment, the refusal to abandon greed,
> The refusal to forsake deception and flattery.
> Such a person, falsely claiming peace,
> Calls himself a śramaṇa,
> Yet commits all manner of evil deeds;
> Evil views bring no happiness.
> He utters many false words.
> Having known him thus,
> None shall associate with him;
> Expel him and do not dwell together.
> Knowing the time, endowed with pure conduct,
> Having discerned who speaks truth,
> He who is not at peace yet claims to be a śramaṇa,
> After such evil conduct is expelled,
> Do not dwell with the wicked.
> Again and again, day after day,
> All shall assemble together
> And bring an end to this mass of suffering.

Thus spoke the Buddha. The bhikṣus, hearing what the World-Honored One had spoken, rejoiced and were delighted.
