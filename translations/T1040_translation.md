# 金剛頂降三世大儀軌法王教中觀自在菩薩心真言一切如來蓮華大曼荼羅品
## Chapter on Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva's Heart Mantra and All Tathāgatas' Great Lotus Maṇḍala, from the Vajraśekhara Trailokyavijaya Great Ritual Dharma-King Teaching

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1040

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

At that time, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva Mahāsattva addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One, I wish to speak my own heart mantra within this Trailokyavijaya Ritual Dharma-King assembly. World-Honored One, if it is merely uttered, the samādhi of all tathāgatas will manifest, and all fears, calamities, obstructions, diseases, and afflictions will be extinguished."

Then the Bhagavān told Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva: "Excellent, excellent! You should well proclaim it." Then Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, receiving the Buddha's holy command, spoke his own heart mantra:

*Ārolik svāhā*

The moment this heart mantra was spoken, all tathāgatas gathered from their own buddha-lands like clouds at the summit of Sumeru in the great assembly of Vairocana, surrounding him. Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva Mahāsattva then further spoke the great maṇḍala of his heart mantra:

> The courtyard is set out as a square with four inner corners and four gates.
> In the very center, a well-shaped cross of four paths
> Reveals eight positions, forming an eight-petaled flower.
> This should be colored in vermilion, with Avalokiteśvara at the central platform.
> On each of those petals, paint a form of the Great Awakened One.
> The inner court has no gate, with eight paths inside and out.
> When this wondrous construction is established, the ācārya should enter,
> Face the maṇḍala and form the great lotus seal,
> Straighten the body, steady the limbs, and abide in samādhi.
> Next, form the hook mudrā to invite the holy ones.
> If any sentient being enters this maṇḍala,
> Though dwelling in an ordinary station, the stains of desire and anger are extinguished,
> Like a lotus flower unsoiled by water.
> The superior-born are likewise unstained by desires.

"The ācārya then uses the great lotus mudrā, bending the index finger like a hook, to invite Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva. The lotus-hook mantra says:

*Oṃ mahā-padma hūṃ jaḥ*

"At that moment, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva will come to the ritual space, manifesting a wondrous body or emitting radiance. The ācārya should then duly lead the disciples in. The disciples should wear white garments, covered further with white silk, and wrap their heads in clean cloth. Once they have been led in, the following verse is spoken:

> 'Like a lotus flower unsoiled by water,
> The superior-born are likewise unstained by desires.'

"Then with the left hand one gives a lotus flower to the disciple, and with the right hand gives a rosary. Another verse is spoken:

> 'The great lotus of the buddhas,
> Among all maṇḍalas
> Is the most supreme.
> One should always contemplate and distinguish this,
> Devoting body and mind to Avalokiteśvara.'

"Afterward, teach the mudrās and the gates of samādhi."

## Heart-Mantra Lotus Maṇḍala Chapter

"Furthermore, having established the maṇḍala as before, the practitioner should sit upon the central platform and enter the all-tathāgatas-blossoming-lotus samādhi. After reciting one hundred thousand times, one attains the body of Avalokiteśvara."

## Heart-Mantra Samaya Maṇḍala Chapter

"Again, having established the maṇḍala as before, one should take one thousand lotus flowers, reciting once per flower and placing each on the central platform as an offering. Then lead the disciples in and speak the samaya words."

## Karma Maṇḍala Chapter

"Furthermore, having established the maṇḍala as before, make the four kinds of offerings. Next, the method for accomplishing the image: on clean silk or cotton, paint Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva seated atop Sumeru, surrounded by eight buddhas. Starting from the first of the bright half-month, before this image, enter samādhi and recite without interruption at all times, until the painting emits light. Within the light, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva and all the tathāgatas emerge and enter the practitioner between the eyebrows at the place of consecration, remaining firmly there. At that moment, the practitioner's body becomes identical to Avalokiteśvara. In an instant one sees the Land of Ultimate Bliss, and all sentient beings can also see it. At the time of death, one's lifespan will be immeasurable."

"I shall now further explain the methods of accomplishment. After one hundred thousand recitations, all obscurations are entirely purified. After two hundred thousand recitations, all sins including the five heinous crimes are extinguished without remainder. After three hundred thousand recitations, all merit gathers together, all tathāgatas are fully present, and all-accomplishing wisdom manifests.

"Furthermore, if one forms the great mudrā and recites one hundred thousand times, and recites without limit through the night until dawn, all tathāgatas enter the practitioner's heart, and one's body becomes identical to a tathāgata. If they enter through the mouth, one's body becomes identical to Avalokiteśvara.

"Furthermore, for accomplishing specific activities: one recitation protects oneself; two recitations protect others, including cities and villages; three recitations accomplish all tasks. For the āveśa rite, burn sandalwood. To dispel ghosts and spirits, add the syllable HRĪḤ to the recitation. To make all beings compliant, empower a vajra-flower and give it to them. To make hostile minds unmoving, subdue them with white clay. To win affection and respect, empower sumanā flowers and give them, and the recipient will submit. To immobilize all birds and beasts wherever they are, empower white mustard seeds and cast them from a distance. To produce visions, empower a peacock tail-feather and wave it from a distance. To know all things in dreams, recite three times, and that night one will see them. To dispel nightmares, perform homa with sesame. To stop gossip and slander, perform homa with chicken wing-feathers. For a maiden, use bhaga-pistils; for a courtesan, use menstrual cloth in homa: this accomplishes vaśīkaraṇa.

"If one seeks all things, one should make extensive offerings to Avalokiteśvara. On the eighth day, fast for one day and night, and recite to fulfill all aspirations. Furthermore, before an image of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, recite three times at each of the three daily periods for a full three months, and the bodhisattva will fulfill one's wishes. Also, before a buddha image, recite three times at each of the three daily periods for a full three months, and the buddha will fulfill one's wishes."

## Mudrā Chapter

"Join the palms. Cross the water fingers and wind fingers over the earth and space fingers, which each spread apart and stand upright. The fire fingers touch together. This is the root mudrā. Lotus-bud palms joined together is the great lotus mudrā. Without changing the great lotus mudrā, spread the earth and space fingers upright: this is the seizing-and-purifying-all-sins mudrā. Also, with the outer-crossing vajra-bond, join both wind fingers like lotus petals with both space fingers standing close together: this is the samādhi mudrā. Also, separate both hands into lotus fists, then with the wisdom fist rotate around the samādhi fist in a gesture of opening eight petals: this is the karma mudrā. The syllable HRĪḤ contemplated on the heart-moon-lotus: this is the dharma mudrā. Contemplating all dharmas as like a lotus unsoiled by water, and all dharmas likewise unstained by desires: this is the wisdom mudrā.

"When the lotus ācārya wishes to confer the ācārya rank upon a disciple, place a lotus flower in the disciple's two palms, and further empower a lotus garland with one thousand eight recitations and bind it on the disciple's head. The empowerment mantra says:

*Oṃ padma-māla-abhiṣiñca lokeśvara susoḥ*

"Thereby one becomes an ācārya of the Lotus Division.

"I shall now explain the precepts and rules of conduct for Lotus Division practice. First, make offerings to Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, then consume one cupped-handful of cow urine or a small amount of māyī, and thereupon all becomes purified. Then contemplate one's own body as Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva. Recite one thousand times daily for up to seven days, and the precept-observance is accomplished. From then on, all food and all places and all actions are free from impurity.

"I shall now explain the universal offering ritual for all occasions. With both hands form vajra fists, place them at each side of the waist, and tilt the head slightly to the left: this is the reverent bow. Then with both hands starting from the forehead, move behind the head in a garland-tying gesture, and let them descend past the ears to hang like garland-streamers: this is offering a flower garland. Then with both hands starting from the navel, gradually rise to the mouth and pour forth: this is offering a song. Then with both hands from the mouth, extend the wisdom and offering fingers, rotate them spiraling up to the crown, and join palms in empty space: this is offering a vajra dance.

"Through these offerings, even the rank of tathāgata is not difficult to attain, let alone other siddhis. Why? Because no bliss compares to this Vajrasattva."
