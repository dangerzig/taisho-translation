# 佛說俱利伽羅大龍勝外道伏陀羅尼經
## Dhāraṇī Sūtra Spoken by the Buddha on the Great Dragon Kurikara's Subjugation of Heterodox Teachers

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1206

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown (失譯)

---

Thus have I heard.

At one time, the Buddha was in the great city of Rājagṛha. At that time, Ratnaketu Dhāraṇī Bodhisattva addressed the Buddha, saying: "By what causes and conditions does the Great Dragon Kurikara swallow a sharp sword and have four limbs coiled around it?"

The Buddha told Ratnaketu Dhāraṇī Bodhisattva: "Long ago, in the Akaniṣṭha Heaven, at the city of Jñānavijaya belonging to Maheśvara, Acalanātha engaged in debate with heterodox teachers, both sides manifesting various miraculous transformations through wisdom. At that time, Acalanātha transformed into a sword of wisdom-fire. At that time, among the ninety-five kinds of heterodox teachers, their leader, named Jñānadhara, likewise transformed into a sword of wisdom-fire. Then Acalanātha's great sword of wisdom-fire transformed into the Great Dragon Kurikara, which had four limbs: Trailokyavijaya, Kuṇḍali, Yamāntaka, and Vajrayakṣa; these four great Vidyārājas. The neck of this king bore a lotus named Wisdom-Fire, which contained the syllable Kurikara; it rose one hundred thousand yojanas in height, and from its mouth issued breath like twenty trillion thunderclaps sounding at once. Upon hearing this, the demon kings of the heterodox teachers abandoned their evil doubts and wrong attachments."

The Buddha then proclaimed the dhāraṇī:

*Namaḥ siddhi siddhi susiddhi siddhi garārāya kuyam samama śrī ajama siddhi svāhā*

"The power of this mantra removes all inauspiciousness and subjugates all demon kings. If a person is afflicted by malevolent spirits, write down their name and recite this mantra twenty-one times; the spirit or ghost will suddenly be burned away. Abstain from the five pungent vegetables, alcohol, and meat; do not defile yourself with impure attachment to women; recite this mantra with one-pointed mind, and all that you seek will certainly be fulfilled. This mantra's power, this mantra's merit, can cause trees to blossom out of season, transform the four oceans into mountains, and turn Sumeru, the king of mountains, into an ocean. It burns ice as though it were oil; it makes hollow-hearted trees flow like water. All things follow the mind, just as with the Bhagavān."

Therefore, by way of summary, a verse is proclaimed:

> One who practices in devoted service,
> Just as the Bhagavān himself,
> Attains the supreme samādhi
> And dwells in the same rank as bodhisattvas.
> As for the Dragon Kurikara:
> Calling upon and reciting that name,
> In the present life, fears and māra-obstacles are removed;
> In the next life, one is born in Sukhāvatī.

When the Buddha had spoken this sūtra, all the evil demon kings and the ninety-five kinds of great dragon kings rejoiced greatly, faithfully received it, and vowed to practice accordingly.

---

**Translator's notes:**

- *Kurikara* (俱利伽羅): The great black dragon coiled around a blazing sword, an esoteric manifestation of Acala (Acalanātha, 無動明王). The text explains the iconography: the sword of Acala's wisdom-fire becomes the dragon's body, whose four limbs are identified with the four directional Vidyārājas.
- *Jñānadhara* (智達): Conjectural Sanskrit reconstruction of the name of the chief heterodox teacher; literally "accomplished in wisdom."
- *Jñānavijaya* (知勝城): Conjectural Sanskrit reconstruction of the city name; literally "the city of knowing-victory."
- The dhāraṇī is rendered phonetically from the Chinese transliteration characters; the underlying Sanskrit cannot be reconstructed with certainty.
- *Sukhāvatī* (安樂國): The Pure Land of Amitābha Buddha, "the Land of Peace and Bliss."
