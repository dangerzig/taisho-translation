# 大乘離文字普光明藏經
## Mahāyāna Sūtra of the Universal Radiance Treasury Apart from Letters

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0829

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Divākara (地婆訶羅)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was in Rājagṛha, on Mount Gṛdhrakūṭa, together with great bodhisattvas numbering countless hundreds of thousands of billions of nayutas. All were of great wisdom and diligent skill, had realized the Dharma beyond words and obtained wondrous eloquence. In the matter of what is the case and what is not, they were without contradiction. They had well tamed body and mind, were complete in all liberations, and constantly roamed in samādhi without abandoning great compassion. Conscience and modesty were their body; wisdom was their leader. They brought benefit to many, like a great island of jewels. They fully knew the marks of wholesome and unwholesome dharmas, were not attached to words yet possessed speech. Regarding the gates of conventional and ultimate truth, they penetrated without obstruction. They clearly understood the true nature of reality yet did not abide in it. They skillfully distinguished yet had nothing they grasped. Though weary of birth-and-death, they always guarded the world. Pervading the ten directions with great renown, they rested in silence in the true and wondrous treasury. Though manifesting bodies, they had forever transcended the three realms. They practiced in all existences to rescue sentient beings, teaching with impartial instruction, their aspirations always worthy and good. With impartial compassion, their minds were free from attachment, enabling both themselves and others to be entirely pure. Having accomplished such immeasurable merit, their names were: the Bodhisattva Superior Thought, the Bodhisattva Dharma-Thunder-Sound, the Bodhisattva Wondrous Body, the Bodhisattva Dharma-Felloe, the Bodhisattva Accumulation of Eloquence, the Bodhisattva Earth-Holder, the Bodhisattva World-Holder, the Bodhisattva Great Renown, the Bodhisattva Possessed of All Eloquence, the Bodhisattva Thousand-Faceted, the Bodhisattva Mountain of Merit, the Bodhisattva Lotus-Eye, the Bodhisattva Lotus-Face, the Bodhisattva Pearl-Topknot, and the Bodhisattva Wondrous Sound. These and other bodhisattva mahāsattvas, all with the appearance of youths, handsome and dignified, were the foremost in this assembly.

At that time the Bodhisattva Avalokiteśvara was there with bodhisattvas who would succeed to the honored position, equal in number to the sands of the Ganges; the Bodhisattva Supreme Vision was there with countless lords of the devas; the Bodhisattva Ākāśagarbha was there with countless bodhisattvas and countless hosts of the four heavenly kings; the Bodhisattva Mahāsthāmaprāpta was there with countless billions of Brahmā hosts; the Bodhisattva Universal Auspiciousness was there with countless attendant women; the Bodhisattva Samantabhadra, the Bodhisattva Amoghadarśin, the Bodhisattva Star-King, the Bodhisattva Free-from-Doubt, the Bodhisattva Who-Extinguishes-All-Hindrances, the Bodhisattva Medicine-King, and the Bodhisattva Supreme-Medicine were each there with countless assemblies of bodhisattvas. Among them were also countless Buddhas who had transformed their own bodies into the appearance of bodhisattvas. The Elders Śāriputra, Mahāmaudgalyāyana, and Mahākāśyapa, and other such great arhats, were each there with countless assemblies of śrāvakas. Nārāyaṇa and countless other celestial hosts, and even the devas of the sun and moon from all the lands as numerous as the sands of the Ganges, their majestic light shining, all came to the Buddha's place. Having arrived, the radiance of those devas could no longer be seen, as if a heap of ink were set beside Jambudvīpa gold. The Nāga King Varuṇa, the Nāga King Takṣaka, the Nāga King Anavatapta, the Gandharva King Beautiful-Sound, and the Garuḍa King Without-Turbidity were each there with countless retinues, all entering the assembly. From all the worlds of the ten directions, as numerous as the sands of the Ganges, bodhisattvas had each requested their Tathāgata in their own land, and together with the fourfold assembly had come at the same time. Each brought various offerings surpassing anything in the world, supremely excellent, and presented them to the Buddha and the bodhisattvas. Then, within the assembly, they sat upon lotus-flower seats.

At that time the Bodhisattva Mahāsattva Superior Thought rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, placed his right knee on the ground, joined his palms toward the Buddha, and said: "World-Honored One! I now wish to ask about the meaning of two words. I pray the Tathāgata will graciously consent to answer."

The Buddha told the Bodhisattva Superior Thought: "Good man! If you wish to ask, ask as you please. The Tathāgata does not appear in the world for the sake of a single sentient being, but appears for the benefit of countless sentient beings!"

Thereupon the Bodhisattva Superior Thought addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! What is the one dharma that bodhisattvas should forever leave behind? What is the one dharma that bodhisattvas should constantly protect and uphold? What is the one dharma that the Tathāgata has presently awakened to?"

The Buddha said: "Excellent, excellent! Good man! By the spiritual authority of the Tathāgata, you are able to ask me such a profound question. Listen carefully, listen carefully! Reflect well upon it! I shall explain it for you.

"Good man! There is one dharma that bodhisattvas should leave behind, namely desire and craving. Good man! This one dharma is what all bodhisattvas should forever leave behind. Good man! There is another dharma that bodhisattvas should leave behind, namely anger. This one dharma is what all bodhisattvas should forever leave behind. Good man! There is another dharma that bodhisattvas should leave behind, namely delusion. This one dharma is what all bodhisattvas should forever leave behind. Good man! There is another dharma that bodhisattvas should leave behind, namely grasping at a self. Good man! There is another dharma that bodhisattvas should leave behind, namely doubt and confusion. Good man! There is another dharma that bodhisattvas should leave behind, namely arrogance. Good man! There is another dharma that bodhisattvas should leave behind, namely laziness. Good man! There is another dharma that bodhisattvas should leave behind, namely torpor and drowsiness. Good man! There is another dharma that bodhisattvas should leave behind, namely craving and attachment. Good man! This one dharma is what all bodhisattvas should forever leave behind.

"Good man! You further asked me: 'What is the one dharma that all bodhisattvas should constantly protect and uphold?' Good man! It is this: bodhisattvas should not inflict upon others what they themselves do not find agreeable. If bodhisattvas guard this dharma, they are thereby upholding all the prohibitions and precepts of all the Tathāgatas. Why? Cherishing one's own body and life, one should not kill living beings. Valuing one's own wealth, one should not steal from others. Protecting one's own spouse, one should not violate another's. All such practices are called the one dharma. Good man! If there are those who respectfully follow the Tathāgata's words, they should always be mindful of this one dharma. Why? There is no sentient being who delights in suffering. Whatever they do, all beings seek happiness, and even the bodhisattva who seeks anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi does so because both self and others may thereby obtain happiness. Good man! For this reason I teach: do not inflict upon others what you yourself do not find agreeable. This one dharma is what all bodhisattvas should constantly protect and uphold.

"Good man! As you asked: 'What is the one dharma that the Tathāgata has presently awakened to?' Good man! There is not the slightest dharma that the Tathāgata has awakened to. Why? What the Tathāgata awakens to is without any object of awakening. Good man! The non-arising of all dharmas is the Tathāgata's awakening. The non-ceasing of all dharmas is the Tathāgata's awakening. The departure of all dharmas from the two extremes is the Tathāgata's awakening. The non-reality of all dharmas is the Tathāgata's awakening. Good man! The intrinsic nature of all karma is the Tathāgata's awakening. That all dharmas arise from causes and conditions is the Tathāgata's awakening. That dharmas of cause and condition are like a flash of lightning is the Tathāgata's awakening. That because of causes and conditions there is karma is the Tathāgata's awakening.

"Good man! The universal radiance treasury that is the nature of all dharmas is the Tathāgata's awakening. Good man! Why is the nature of dharmas called the 'universal radiance treasury'? Good man! Because both worldly and world-transcending wisdom arise in dependence upon it, as a mother carries a child within her womb, it is called a 'treasury.' When wisdom arises and reflects back upon its source, this dharma-nature is then embraced by prajñā-pāramitā. For this reason it is called the 'universal radiance treasury.'

"Good man! That all dharmas are like an illusion, like a mirage, is the Tathāgata's awakening. Good man! The one-taste liberation that is the true nature of dharmas is the Tathāgata's awakening. One-taste liberation is precisely what is called the 'universal radiance treasury.' Good man! The one-mark dharma is the Tathāgata's awakening. What is one mark? It is that all dharmas neither come nor go, are neither cause nor condition, neither arise nor cease, are neither grasped nor rejected, and neither increase nor decrease.

"Good man! The intrinsic nature of all dharmas has originally been without any existence and cannot be expressed through metaphor. It is not something that words and phrases can discuss. This one dharma is what all the Tathāgatas have presently awakened to."

When the Buddha taught this Dharma-gate of the Adorned-King Universal Radiance Treasury Apart from Letters, sentient beings in number equal to the dust-motes seen by bodhisattvas of the tenth ground all gave rise to the aspiration for anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi. Again, sentient beings equal in number to those dust-motes all gave rise to the aspiration for the śrāvaka and pratyekabuddha path. Again, sentient beings equal in number to those dust-motes who were in the hells were all freed from suffering and born among devas and humans. Countless bodhisattvas entered the first ground. Countless bodhisattvas attained hundreds of thousands of samādhis. Countless sentient beings all received benefit, and none were without gain.

At that time the Buddha told Rāhula: "Good man! You should receive and uphold this essential Dharma of mine."

When he spoke these words, ninety billion bodhisattva mahāsattvas in the assembly, empowered by the Buddha's spiritual authority, immediately rose from their seats and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! We vow to receive and uphold the essential Dharma taught by the Tathāgata. In the final age in this Sahā world, when we see anyone who will circulate and teach it, we shall do so."

At that time the four heavenly kings addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! If there are those who can uphold this sūtra, we shall guard and protect them and cause all their aspirations to be fulfilled. Why? Because one who can uphold this sūtra is a vessel of the Dharma."

At that time the World-Honored One surveyed the entire assembly and said: "All of you! This profound, vast, and rare Dharma-gate that I have taught is not something that sentient beings of meager good roots can hear and receive. Those who can hear and receive it thereby serve and make offerings to me, and also shoulder the burden of unsurpassed bodhi. This person will attain unobstructed eloquence and will certainly be born in a pure Buddha-land. This person, at the time of death, will certainly see Amitābha Buddha and the great assembly of bodhisattvas manifesting before them. Just as I am now on Mount Gṛdhrakūṭa, surrounded by the assembly of bodhisattvas, so too will that person see the same at the time of death. You should know that this person has already obtained the inexhaustible treasury of Dharma. You should know that this person has attained the knowledge of past lives. You should know that this person will not fall into the evil destinies.

"Good man! I now teach this Dharma that is difficult for all the world to believe. Even if there are sentient beings who have committed the five heinous crimes, upon hearing this sūtra, if they write it, uphold it, read and recite it, and explain it for others, all their karmic obstructions will be entirely eliminated. They will never receive the suffering of the evil destinies. This person is then guarded and protected by all the Buddhas and bodhisattvas. Wherever they are born, their faculties will be complete, and they will receive the Buddha's anointing, with the five eyes purified. Good man! In short, I see that this person has already accomplished the path of the Buddha."

When the Buddha had finished teaching this sūtra, Superior Thought and all the bodhisattvas, along with the śrāvakas, devas, nāgas, and the eightfold assembly, all rejoiced greatly, believed, received it, and practiced accordingly.
