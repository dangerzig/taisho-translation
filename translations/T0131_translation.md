# 佛說婆羅門避死經
## Sūtra of the Brahmins Who Sought to Avoid Death

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0131

Translated from the Chinese. Later Han dynasty, translated by An Shigao (安世高)

---

Thus have I heard.

At one time, the Blessed One was dwelling at Śrāvastī in Jetavana, Anāthapiṇḍada's park. At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣus:

"Long ago there were four brahmin sages who diligently cultivated wholesome dharmas and possessed the five supernormal powers, yet were constantly gripped by fear of death. These four brahmin sages, diligently cultivating wholesome dharmas and possessing the five supernormal powers, reflected among themselves: 'In what place shall we dwell so as to remain in the world forever?'

"At that time, one of the brahmins, who diligently cultivated wholesome dharmas and possessed great spiritual power and the five supernormal powers, entered into the sky, thinking: 'Here death cannot reach me.' Yet the one who entered the sky passed away there. The second brahmin, diligently cultivating wholesome dharmas and possessing the five supernormal powers, fearing death entered the great ocean, thinking: 'In the ocean death cannot reach me.' Yet he too passed away within the ocean. The third brahmin, diligently cultivating wholesome dharmas and possessing great majesty and the five supernormal powers, fearing death entered the interior of a mountain, and there too he died. That fourth brahmin, diligently cultivating wholesome dharmas and possessing the five supernormal powers and great majesty, fearing death entered into the earth, thinking: 'There I shall escape death.' Yet he passed away within the earth.

"At that time, the World-Honored One, with his divine eye, pure and without blemish, observed those four brahmins who diligently cultivated wholesome dharmas, possessed great majesty and the five supernormal powers, and feared death: one perished in the sky, one entered the ocean and perished there as well, one entered the interior of a mountain and perished there, and one entered the earth and perished there. Having thus seen those four brahmins who diligently cultivated wholesome dharmas and possessed the five supernormal powers and great majesty, the World-Honored One spoke this verse:

> Not in the sky, nor in the midst of the sea,
> Nor by entering amid the mountain crags,
> There is no place anywhere in the world
> Where one escapes and is not claimed by death.

At that time, the bhikṣus, having heard what the Buddha taught, rejoiced and undertook to practice it.
