# 梁京寺記
## Record of Temples in the Liang Capital

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 2094

Compiled in Chinese. Liang dynasty (502–557 CE); compiler unknown.

---

## The Small Adornment Monastery

The Small Adornment Monastery of the Liang is located in the Dingyin Ward of Jianye. It originally stood on the site of the shrine to the Prince of Lingling of the Jin dynasty. In the sixth year of the Tianjian era, Dhyāna Master Du established and constructed it.

At that time there was a man named Shao Wenli, whose family had for generations made their livelihood through the butchering and slaughter of animals. Once, when he was about to kill a deer, the deer knelt down and wept. He took this as an ill omen. The deer was carrying a fawn soon to be born; its anguish as it approached the slaughter was acute, and both mother and fawn were cut open together. Because of this, Shao contracted an illness: his eyebrows and beard fell out entirely, and his body became covered with sores and wasted away. Thereafter he deeply repented and reproached himself, and sought the Way.

Dhyāna Master Du made a great vow, exhausted his entire household resources, and used the money to purchase this land, upon which he established a saṃghārāma.

## Tongtai Monastery

Emperor Wu of the Liang changed the reign era name to Datong and established Tongtai Monastery within the Taicheng palace precincts, exhausting the state treasury to construct a great seven-story Buddhist pavilion. It was destroyed by fire. Emperor Wu offered his own body and gave wealth to pray for the Buddha's blessing. From the Datong era onward, there was not a year without calamity.

## Xingguo Dhyāna Monastery

In the thirteenth year of the Tianjian era of Emperor Wu of the Liang, for two hundred thousand cash, he acquired the Solitary Dragon Mound on the ridge before Dingling Monastery, as a burial place for the Venerable Baozhi. Princess Yongding used her personal stipend to construct a five-story stūpa upon it. In the fourteenth year, Kaishan Monastery was established directly before the stūpa.

## Shengyuan Monastery

Shengyuan Monastery is none other than Waguan Monastery. It stands in the western corner of the city, looking out over the river, with its rear resting against a high ridge. It is the most ancient of all historical sites. Having repeatedly suffered destruction by the fires of warfare, scarcely a trace of it remains. During the time of the Li kings, the Shengyuan Pavilion was still standing; a surviving artifact from the Liang dynasty.

## Great Reverent Love Monastery

Built in the first year of the Putong era of Emperor Wu of the Liang. It stands on the high peak to the north of Jiang Mountain.

## Dharma Treasure Monastery

This occupies half the original site of Tongtai Monastery of the Liang. According to the *Record of Monasteries of Jiankang*: in the first year of the Datong era, Emperor Wu of the Liang founded Tongtai Monastery. The monastery was situated behind the palace, and a separate gate was opened, called the Datong Gate. The Emperor would hold Dharma discourses morning and evening, and frequently passed through this gate.

## Dharma Light Monastery

This is the Xiao Emperor's Monastery of the Liang. According to old tradition, it was built in the thirteenth year of the Tianjian era. The *Monastery Record* of Yuan Jiang states that the original ancient name is unknown, and so later people designated it by the emperor's surname.

## Baolin Monastery

During the Tianjian era of the Liang, Emperor Wu toured this mountain together with the Venerable Bao. Seeing that its forests and peaks were supremely excellent, he ordered the construction of a fine saṃghārāma.

## Changyu Monastery

Five *li* to the south of Jiankang there is a mountain ridge, with flat land in between where common people live interspersed. There are places known as Great Changyu, Small Changyu, and East Changyu; all are place names. Small Changyu lies to the south of Waguan Monastery; at the western end of the lane one reaches the great river. In the early Liang dynasty, Changyu Monastery was established there.
