# 佛說報恩奉盆經
## Sūtra Spoken by the Buddha on Repaying Kindness by Presenting Bowls

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 686

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown (失譯)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was dwelling in the Jetavana Grove of Anāthapiṇḍada, in the kingdom of Śrāvastī. Mahāmaudgalyāyana had just attained the six supernatural powers. Wishing to deliver his parents and repay the kindness of being nursed and nourished, he used his Dharma-eye to observe the world, and saw that his deceased mother had been reborn among the hungry ghosts. She could find no food or drink, and her skin and bones clung together like a post. Maudgalyāyana was overcome with grief and sorrow. He immediately filled a bowl with rice and went to offer it to his mother. When his mother received the bowl of rice, she used her left hand to shield the food and her right hand to gather it into a ball, but before the food could enter her mouth it transformed into burning coals, and she was unable to eat.

Maudgalyāyana hastened back and reported everything to the Buddha, explaining all that had happened in full. The Buddha told Maudgalyāyana: "Your mother's roots of sin are deeply entangled. This lies beyond what your own power alone can accomplish. The spiritual power of the entire Saṅgha is required for her to attain liberation. I shall now expound the method of rescue and deliverance, so that all beings in difficulty may be freed from sorrow and suffering."

The Buddha told Maudgalyāyana: "On the fifteenth day of the seventh month, for the sake of parents of seven generations who are in difficulty and hardship, one should prepare roasted grain and rice, the five fruits, vessels for drawing and pouring water, fragrant oil, candles for the courtyard, beds and couches, and bedding, together with all the finest delicacies in the world, to make offerings to the Saṅgha. On that day, all the holy assembly—some engaged in meditative absorption in the mountains, some having attained the four fruits of the path, some walking in meditation beneath trees, some possessed of the six supernatural powers and flying through the air, teaching and transforming śrāvakas and pratyekabuddhas—and great bodhisattvas who skillfully manifest as bhikṣus within the great assembly, all together with one mind receive the bowl-offerings. The way of this holy assembly, fully endowed with pure precepts, is vast and boundless in virtue. Whoever makes offerings to such an assembly, their parents of seven generations and five classes of relatives will emerge from the three evil realms, and at that very moment attain liberation, with food and clothing arising of themselves."

The Buddha commanded the Saṅgha: "For the sake of the parents of seven generations of the donor's family, you should first cultivate meditative concentration of mind, and only then partake of this offering."

Maudgalyāyana, the bhikṣus, and all the assembly rejoiced and received these teachings in practice.
