# 應法經
## Sūtra on Concordant Dharmas

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0083

Translated from the Chinese. Western Jin dynasty, translated by Dharmarakṣa (竺法護)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Bhagavān was in the land of Kuru, at a place governed by Dharma. At that time, the Buddha addressed the bhikṣus: "In this present world, there is such sensual desire, such craving, such love, such pleasure, such delight, yet unloved and unpleasant dharmas increase and decay, while loved dharmas grow. With such sensual desire, such craving, such love, such pleasure, such delight, unwholesome dharmas continue to increase while love of wholesome dharmas continues to decrease. My Dharma is profound, difficult to see, difficult to perceive, difficult to comprehend, difficult to know. And yet, my Dharma being profound, difficult to see, difficult to perceive, difficult to comprehend, difficult to know, now the unloved wholesome dharmas decrease while loved wholesome dharmas increase. There are four dharmas concordant with this that exist in the world. What are the four? There is a dharma concordant with present pleasure that brings future suffering. There is a dharma concordant with present suffering that brings future pleasure. There is a dharma concordant with present suffering that brings future suffering. There is a dharma concordant with present pleasure that brings future pleasure.

"How is a dharma concordant with present pleasure and future suffering? There are those who find pleasure and delight in killing, who through killing find pleasure and delight; who with delight and pleasure engage in stealing, sexual misconduct, and false speech, even to wrong views, who through wrong views find delight and pleasure. Such bodily pleasure and mental pleasure, being unwholesome, does not lead to supernatural powers, does not reach the true path, and is not concordant with nirvāṇa. This dharma is concordant with present pleasure and future suffering.

"How is a dharma concordant with present suffering and future pleasure? There are those who practice austerity and take no pleasure in abandoning killing, who through abandoning killing find suffering and no delight; who with self-imposed suffering and no delight abandon stealing, sexual misconduct, and false speech, even to wrong views, who through abandoning wrong views for right views find suffering and no delight. Such bodily suffering and mental suffering, being wholesome, leads to supernatural powers, reaches the true path, and is concordant with nirvāṇa. This dharma is concordant with present suffering and future pleasure.

"How is a dharma concordant with present suffering and future suffering? There are those who practice suffering and find no delight in killing, who through killing find suffering and no delight; who with self-imposed suffering and no delight engage in stealing, sexual misconduct, and false speech, even to wrong views, who through wrong views find no pleasure and no delight. Such bodily suffering and mental suffering, being unwholesome, does not lead to supernatural powers, does not reach the true path, and is not concordant with nirvāṇa. This dharma is concordant with present suffering and future suffering.

"How is a dharma concordant with present pleasure and future pleasure? There are those who practice with pleasure and find delight in abandoning killing, who through abandoning killing find pleasure and delight; who with delight and pleasure abandon stealing, sexual misconduct, and false speech, even to wrong views, separating from and abandoning them, who through separating from and abandoning wrong views find pleasure and delight. Such bodily pleasure and mental pleasure, being wholesome, leads to supernatural powers, reaches the true path, and is concordant with nirvāṇa. This dharma is concordant with present pleasure and future pleasure.

"Now, regarding the dharma concordant with present pleasure and future suffering: the unwise person says, not knowing it as it truly is, that this dharma is concordant with present pleasure and future suffering. Not knowing it as it truly is, they do not abandon such practice. Because they do not abandon it, unloved and unpleasant dharmas continue to increase while loved dharmas continue to decrease. It is like āmalaka medicine, perfect in color, perfect in fragrance, perfect in taste, yet mixed with poison. A sick person drinks it: it is pleasant to the mouth and goes down the throat smoothly, but once consumed it becomes harmful rather than curative. So too is the dharma concordant with present pleasure and future suffering: without wisdom, one does not know it as it truly is. Not knowing it as it truly is, one does not abandon such practice. Because one does not abandon it, unloved and unpleasant dharmas continue to increase while loved and pleasant dharmas continue to decrease. This dharma is not wisdom.

"Now, regarding the dharma concordant with present suffering and future pleasure: the unwise person, not knowing it as it truly is, does not practice it and abandons it. Because one does not practice and abandons it, unloved and unpleasant dharmas continue to increase while loved and pleasant dharmas continue to decrease. This dharma is not wisdom.

"Now, regarding the dharma concordant with present suffering and future suffering: the unwise person, not knowing it as it truly is, does not abandon such practice. Because one does not abandon it, unloved and unpleasant dharmas continue to increase while loved and pleasant dharmas continue to decrease. It is like excrement mixed with poison. A sick person takes and drinks it: it is bitter to the throat and does not go down, it is foul and tasteless, and once consumed it becomes harmful rather than curative. So too is the dharma concordant with present suffering and future suffering: not knowing it as it truly is, one does not abandon such practice, and unloved and unpleasant dharmas continue to increase while loved and pleasant dharmas continue to decrease. This dharma is not wisdom.

"Now, regarding the dharma concordant with present pleasure and future pleasure: the unwise person, not knowing it as it truly is, does not practice it and abandons it. Because one does not practice and abandons it, unloved and unpleasant dharmas continue to increase while loved and pleasant dharmas continue to decrease. This dharma is not wisdom.

"The person who knows practiced dharma as it truly is and unpracticed dharma as it truly is, who knows practiced dharma as it truly is and unpracticed dharma as it truly is: what has not been practiced should be practiced, and what should be practiced should not be abandoned. If what has not been practiced should be practiced and what should be practiced should not be abandoned, yet unwholesome dharmas continue to increase and wholesome dharmas continue to decrease: this dharma is not wisdom.

"Now, regarding the dharma concordant with present pleasure and future suffering: the wise person knows it as it truly is. Knowing it as it truly is, one does not practice it and abandons and separates from it. Having abandoned and separated from it, unwholesome dharmas decrease and wholesome dharmas continue to increase. This dharma is wisdom.

"Now, regarding the dharma concordant with present suffering and future pleasure: the wise person knows it as it truly is. Knowing it as it truly is, one practices it and does not abandon it. Having practiced and not abandoned it, unloved and unpleasant dharmas continue to decrease while loved and pleasant dharmas continue to increase. This dharma is wisdom. It is like excrement mixed with various medicinal herbs. A sick person takes and drinks it: it does not go down the throat smoothly and is harsh to the throat, but once consumed it acts as proper medicine. So too is the dharma concordant with present suffering and future pleasure: the wise person knows it as it truly is. Knowing it as it truly is, one practices and does not abandon it. Having practiced and not abandoned it, unloved and unpleasant dharmas continue to decrease while loved and pleasant dharmas continue to increase. This dharma is wisdom.

"Now, regarding the dharma concordant with present suffering and future suffering: the wise person knows it as it truly is. Knowing it as it truly is, one does not practice it and abandons and separates from it. Having abandoned and separated from it, unloved and unpleasant dharmas continue to decrease while loved and pleasant dharmas continue to increase. This dharma is wisdom.

"Now, regarding the dharma concordant with present pleasure and future pleasure: the wise person knows it as it truly is. Knowing it as it truly is, one practices it and does not separate from it. Having practiced and not separated from it, unloved and unpleasant dharmas continue to decrease while loved and pleasant dharmas continue to increase. This dharma is wisdom. It is like ghee and honey mixed with various medicines. A sick person takes and drinks it: it is smooth to the throat and goes down easily, and once consumed it acts as proper medicine. So too is the dharma concordant with present pleasure and future pleasure: the wise person knows it as it truly is. Knowing it as it truly is, one practices it and does not separate from it. Having practiced and not separated from it, unloved and unpleasant dharmas continue to decrease while loved and pleasant dharmas continue to increase. This dharma is the wisdom of the wise.

"Knowing practiced dharma as it truly is, knowing unpracticed dharma as it truly is: what should not be practiced one does not practice, and what should be practiced one does practice. When what should not be practiced one does not practice, and what should be practiced one does practice, unwholesome dharmas continue to decrease and wholesome dharmas continue to increase. This dharma is the wisdom of the wise. These four dharmas concordant with the world exist: this is what has been taught and the reason for this teaching."

The Buddha spoke thus. The bhikṣus, having heard what the World-Honored One taught, were delighted and joyful.
