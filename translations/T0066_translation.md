"At that time, the Tathāgata Krakucchanda, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One, surrounded by an immeasurable assembly of hundreds of thousands, was expounding the Dharma. The Tathāgata Krakucchanda, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One, saw from afar his disciple, his head broken, his robes and bowl destroyed, coming from the distance. Seeing this, he told the bhikṣus: 'You bhikṣus! See this bhikṣu. The evil Māra has told the brāhmaṇas and householders, "Take those diligent śramaṇas and abuse them, strike them, be angry with them, somewhat strike and anger them, and their minds may become unsettled." And this evil Māra has found an opening, sought an opportunity, and obtained an opportunity. You bhikṣus! You should abide, pervading one direction with a mind of loving-kindness, then the second, the third, the fourth, above, below, and in all directions, with a mind of loving-kindness, without enmity, without duality, without anger, exceedingly vast, exceedingly great, immeasurable, well-developed, pervading all directions, and abide in this attainment. Likewise with compassion, joy, and equanimity, pervading all directions and abiding in this attainment. Then the evil Māra, seeking an opening, seeking an opportunity, will not find an opening, will not find an opportunity.' Then Pāpīyas, addressing the disciples of the Tathāgata Krakucchanda, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One, spoke thus: 'They abide pervading one direction with loving-kindness, likewise the second, third, fourth, above, below, and in all directions, with a mind of loving-kindness, without enmity, without duality, without anger, exceedingly vast, great, immeasurable, well-developed, pervading all directions and abiding. Likewise with compassion, joy, and equanimity, pervading all directions and abiding. The evil Māra, seeking an opening, seeking an opportunity, cannot find an opening, cannot find an opportunity.' Then the evil Māra Pāpīyas thought: 'By this method I cannot find an opening against these śramaṇas, cannot find an opportunity. Let me rather go to the brāhmaṇas and householders and say, "Take those diligent śramaṇas and revere them, serve them, honor them, make offerings to them." If they somewhat make offerings, serve and honor them, and their minds become unsettled, then the evil Māra will find an opening, seek an opportunity, and obtain an opening, obtain an opportunity.' This evil Māra Pāpīyas then told the householders and brāhmaṇas: 'Those diligent śramaṇas! Make offerings to them, serve and honor them.' He caused the brāhmaṇas and householders to remove their garments and spread them on the ground, saying: 'Let these diligent śramaṇas tread upon them and walk over them. These diligent śramaṇas practice such extreme austerities; let them bring us benefit and gain through the long night.' He caused the brāhmaṇas and householders to wash their own hair and spread it on the ground, saying: 'Diligent śramaṇas! Tread upon it and walk over it. These diligent śramaṇas practice such extreme austerities; let them bring us benefit and gain through the long night.' He caused the brāhmaṇas and householders to take pouches filled with various goods, saying: 'Noble sirs! Please take these and use them as you wish. May this bring us benefit and gain through the long night.' He caused the brāhmaṇas and householders, with faith and delight, to take those diligent śramaṇas by the hand and lead them into their own houses, offering whatever they wished: 'Noble sirs! Please take these gifts and use them as you wish. May this bring us benefit and gain through the long night.' Those brāhmaṇas and householders of that time, upon their death, because of those causes and conditions, when their bodies perished, were reborn in a good destination, in heaven. Having been born there, they thought: 'Our happiness here surpasses everything. It is because we held right view toward the diligent śramaṇas.' Then Pāpīyas, having arranged for the disciples of the Tathāgata Krakucchanda, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One, to receive offerings, reverence, service, and honor, went to where the disciples of the Tathāgata Krakucchanda, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One, were.

"At that time, the Tathāgata Krakucchanda, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One, surrounded by an immeasurable assembly of hundreds of thousands, was expounding the Dharma. The Tathāgata Krakucchanda, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One, saw from afar his disciple, honored with offerings, served and revered by others, coming from the distance. Seeing this, he told the bhikṣus: 'You bhikṣus, do you see? This evil Māra Pāpīyas has told the brāhmaṇas and householders, "Make offerings to, revere, serve, and honor those diligent śramaṇas." If they somewhat revere, serve, honor, and make offerings, and the mind becomes unsettled, then the evil Māra Pāpīyas will find an opening, seek an opportunity, and obtain an opening, obtain an opportunity. You bhikṣus! You should see impermanence in all conditioned things, see their end, see dispassion, see cessation, see stopping, see the place of stopping. Then the evil Māra Pāpīyas, seeking an opening, seeking an opportunity, will not find an opening, will not find an opportunity.' Then Pāpīyas addressed the disciples of the Tathāgata Krakucchanda, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One, with these words: 'In all conditioned things, see impermanence, see their end, see dispassion, see cessation, see the place of stopping. The evil Māra Pāpīyas, seeking an opening, seeking an opportunity, cannot find an opening, cannot find an opportunity.'

"Then the evil Māra Pāpīyas thought: 'By this method I cannot find an opening against the diligent śramaṇas, cannot find an opportunity. Let me transform into the appearance of a young boy and stand beside the road. Holding a large piece of wood, I shall strike the Venerable Vidhūra on the head and make his blood flow.' At that time, the Tathāgata Krakucchanda, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One, was dwelling near a village or town. Rising early, putting on his robes, taking his robe and bowl, he was about to go to the village or town to beg for food, and the Venerable Vidhūra was among the bhikṣus following him. Then the evil Māra, at another place, transformed into the appearance of a young boy and stood by the roadside. Holding a large piece of wood, he struck the Venerable Vidhūra on the head, making his blood flow. Then the Venerable Vidhūra, having been struck, his head broken and bleeding, followed behind the Tathāgata Krakucchanda, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One. Then the Tathāgata Krakucchanda, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One, having reached the village, with the full power of his body turned to the right, looking and observing, without fear, without terror, without alarm, without dread, surveying all directions. The Tathāgata Krakucchanda, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One, seeing the Venerable Vidhūra with his head struck and broken, blood flowing, coming from behind, spoke these words: 'This evil Māra has done wrong; he is insatiable.'

"Furthermore, Pāpīyas! Before the Tathāgata Krakucchanda, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One, had finished speaking, at that moment the evil Māra, in that very body, fell into the great hell. In that great hell, Pāpīyas had four features and was without any pleasant contact among the six. His body experienced pain directly, and he was bound with hook-chains. The wardens of that hell came to the evil Māra and said: 'When these chains loosen from you, know that you will have been in this hell for a full hundred years.' At that time, Māra Pāpīyas was terrified, and the hair on his body stood on end."

Then the Venerable Mahāmaudgalyāyana spoke in verse:

> How did he come to dwell in hell,
> Causing evil to abide there?
> He who offended the Buddha, a brāhmaṇa,
> And also offended this bhikṣu.
> It is named the Avīci Hell,
> Causing evil to dwell within.
> He who offended the Buddha, a brāhmaṇa,
> And also offended this bhikṣu.
> When the chain loosens, a hundred years have passed,
> Suffering pain within.
> In the Avīci Hell,
> Causing evil to dwell therein.
> If one does not know
> That the bhikṣu is a disciple of the Buddha,
> One receives such suffering,
> And shall receive the dark retribution.
> In the parks and groves
> And among the beings of this land,
> Rice is eaten without sowing;
> One shall be born in Uttarakuru.
> Near the great Mount Sumeru,
> Close to liberation,
> One who can discern for oneself
> Practices mindfulness of body.
> In that mountain's springs he dwells,
> Abiding constantly through this kalpa,
> His form like the color of gold,
> His radiance illuminating everywhere.
> Making various kinds of music,
> This is where Śakra delights in play.
> There too are both together,
> Standing before him with reverence.
> When Śakra walks ahead
> And ascends this lofty hall,
> He sees where Śakra comes from,
> Each delighting in their own pleasure.
> If he sees a bhikṣu come,
> He turns and looks with a sense of shame.
> If one ascends the hall,
> One can then inquire of the bhikṣu.
> Know that there is this Māra;
> With craving exhausted, liberation is attained.
> A prediction shall be given to the bhikṣu;
> Having heard, one should practice thus.
> Kauśika, I know you:
> With craving exhausted, liberation is attained.
> Having heard the prediction of wisdom spoken,
> Śakra attains joy and happiness.
> The bhikṣu, having practiced much,
> Shall further explain this.
> If one ascends this hall,
> Śakra is able to inquire.
> What is this hall called,
> Upon which you, Śakra, sit?
> Śakra, I shall give a prediction:
> This is called the Place of Retribution.
> Thus in a thousand world-systems,
> There are a thousand such world-systems.
> None surpasses this hall:
> Such is the Place of Retribution.
> Śakra freely travels
> Within it, most pure and bright,
> Transforming one into a hundred,
> Upon this Hall of Retribution.
> Śakra freely travels,
> Ascending upon this hall.
> With his toe he can cause it to move,
> Seen by the divine eye.
> Śakra freely travels,
> Ascending upon the Deer Hall.
> With supernatural power he can move and turn it,
> Profoundly deep and well concealed.
> Difficult to move and difficult to turn,
> There is a ground of vaiḍūrya,
> The dwelling place of sages,
> Smooth, lustrous, and extremely soft.
> The bedding spread is supremely soft,
> And speech is also gentle.
> The supreme lord of devas
> Can produce fine music.
> Of various and manifold kinds,
> The devas come to gather.
> Those heading toward stream-entry,
> Immeasurable thousands of kinds,
> And hundreds of nayutas,
> Reaching the Trāyastriṃśa heaven.
> Expounding the Dharma, serving as the eye:
> Having heard this Dharma,
> With faith and delight one accepts it.
> I know there is this Dharma,
> And this one is called a sage,
> Who has reached the Brahmā heaven.
> He can cause the Brahmās to inquire.
> Those Brahmās hold this view;
> Their view is as before:
> The view of permanence and eternal abiding.
> I shall give the Brahmā a prediction:
> Sage, my view is this,
> Not as it was before.
> I am permanent and eternally abiding.
> I see retribution corresponding;
> The Brahmā body stands before me.
> What shall I now say?
> I have always believed in permanence.
> Know that in this world
> What the Perfectly Enlightened One has declared:
> If one has habitual tendencies,
> One is reborn and receives retribution accordingly.
> Fire has no such thought,
> "I shall burn the fool."
> After fire burns the fool,
> He is burned according to his deeds.
> So too you, Pāpīyas,
> Having approached this Tathāgata,
> Long having committed such evil deeds,
> The retribution too shall be long.
> You, Māra, do not despise the Buddha,
> Nor harass his bhikṣus.
> This bhikṣu has now spoken;
> Māra is in the land of Bhesakaḷā.
> The spirit bore a mind of sorrow,
> Moved by Maudgalyāyana's exhortation.
> In extreme terror and dread,
> He suddenly vanished from sight.
