# 佛說罪業應報教化地獄經
## Sūtra of the Retribution of Sinful Karma and the Teaching on the Hells, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0724

Translated from the Chinese. Later Han dynasty, translated by An Shigao (安世高)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was dwelling in Rājagṛha on Mount Gṛdhrakūṭa, together with great bodhisattva mahāsattvas and a retinue of śrāvakas, as well as bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, upāsikās, and devas, nāgas, ghosts, spirits, and others, all gathered in assembly. At that time the Bodhisattva Xinxiang said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! There now exist the hells, hungry ghosts, animals, servants and slaves, the poor and the rich, the noble and the base, in many varieties. I humbly wish the World-Honored One to fully expound the Dharma! If sentient beings can hear the Buddha's teaching, it is like an infant finding its mother, like a sick person finding a physician, like the weakened finding food, like one in darkness finding a lamp. The World-Honored One's teaching of the Dharma to benefit sentient beings is just like this."

At that time the World-Honored One, seeing that the time was right and that this bodhisattva was earnest in his entreaty, emitted from between his brows the light of the white ūrṇā, which illuminated the world and brought respite to the hells and peace to their suffering. Then all the beings undergoing punishment, following the Buddha's light, came to the Buddha's place, circumambulated the Buddha seven times, bowed to the Buddha, and entreated the World-Honored One to expound and teach, so that these sentient beings might gain liberation.

At that time the Bodhisattva Xinxiang, making inquiry on behalf of all sentient beings, said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! There are now beings undergoing punishment whose bodies are chopped and pounded and cut by the hell-wardens, from head to foot and back to the crown; when the cutting is finished, a clever wind blows them back to life, and they are cut again. What sin has caused this?"

The Buddha said: "In a past life, because of not believing in the Three Treasures, not being filial to parents, and being butchers and slaughterers who cut apart sentient beings, they incur this punishment."

"Second, there are sentient beings whose bodies are numb and insensate, whose eyebrows and beard fall out, whose entire bodies are covered in rotting sores. Birds roost and deer lodge nearby, but human footprints are forever absent. They contaminate their own kinfolk, and people do not wish to see them. This is called leprosy. What sin has caused this?"

The Buddha said: "In a past life, because of not believing in the Three Treasures, not being filial to parents, destroying stūpas and monasteries, robbing renunciants of their robes, shooting and injuring sages and saints, harming teachers and elders, constantly being ungrateful and faithless, betraying kindness and forgetting duty, constantly acting shamelessly, engaging in sexual misconduct with persons of higher or lower rank without any scruples, they incur this punishment."

"Third, there are sentient beings with long bodies who are deaf and foolish, without feet, writhing on their bellies, eating only mud to sustain their lives, constantly eaten by small insects, enduring this suffering beyond what can be borne. What sin has caused this?"

The Buddha said: "In a past life, because of being self-willed and not believing good words and wholesome speech, not being filial to parents, and rebelling against the ruler of the time; or being an emperor, a great minister, a commander guarding the four quarters, a prefect or magistrate, an official or prison warden, who relied on their power and authority to plunder the people's property without any principle, causing the people to suffer and groan as they went about, they incur this punishment."

"Fourth, there are sentient beings who are blind in both eyes and cannot see anything, who bump into trees or fall into ditches. When they die and receive a new body, it is the same. What sin has caused this?"

The Buddha said: "In a past life, because of not believing in the retribution of virtue and sin, obstructing the Buddha's light, sewing shut the eyes of hawks, caging and confining sentient beings, and covering heads with leather bags so they could not see, they incur this punishment."

"Fifth, there are sentient beings who stutter and are mute, whose mouths cannot speak; when they have something to say, they close their eyes and wave their hands but still cannot make themselves understood. What sin has caused this?"

The Buddha said: "In a past life, because of slandering the Three Treasures, belittling the holy path, discussing others' good and bad points, seeking out others' faults, falsely accusing the virtuous and good, and being jealous of the worthy, they incur this punishment."

"Sixth, there are sentient beings with large bellies and thin necks who cannot swallow food; whatever they eat turns into pus and blood. What sin has caused this?"

The Buddha said: "In a past life, because of stealing the Saṃgha's food, or at a great assembly where food was offered as merit, stealing and eating it in secret; being stingy with one's own possessions yet coveting others' wealth; constantly harboring evil intent and giving people poison so they could not breathe, they incur this punishment."

"Seventh, there are sentient beings whose every joint is pierced by the hell-wardens with red-hot iron nails, and when the nailing is done, fire spontaneously arises and burns their bodies until all is scorched and ruined. What sin has caused this?"

The Buddha said: "In a past life, because of being a physician who practiced acupuncture and moxibustion, piercing people's bodies without curing their illnesses, deceiving them to take their money while they endured suffering for nothing, causing them distress, they incur this punishment."

"Eighth, there are sentient beings constantly in a cauldron of boiling water. The ox-headed hell-wardens use three-pronged iron forks to pierce them and throw them into the boiling cauldron, cooking them until they dissolve, then blowing them back to life and cooking them again. What sin has caused this?"

The Buddha said: "In a past life, because of believing in perverse and inverted views, making sacrifices to ghosts and spirits, slaughtering sentient beings, scalding them to remove their hair, and boiling them in cauldrons, in incalculable numbers, they incur this punishment."

"Ninth, there are sentient beings constantly in a city of fire, with hot ashes reaching to their chests. Four gates are all open, but whenever they try to approach, the gates close. They run east and west but cannot escape, and are consumed by fire. What sin has caused this?"

The Buddha said: "In a past life, because of setting fire to mountains and marshes, roasting eggs in hot ashes, burning others' villages and paths, and burning and scalding sentient beings until their skin peeled and their bodies were ruined, they incur this punishment."

"Tenth, there are sentient beings constantly in a snowy mountain, where cold winds blow, their skin and flesh cracking open, seeking death but unable to die. What sin has caused this?"

The Buddha said: "In a past life, because of being highway bandits who stripped people of their clothing, causing them to freeze to death in the winter months, and skinning cattle and sheep alive in unbearable agony, they incur this punishment."

"Eleventh, there are sentient beings constantly on mountains of knives and trees of swords; wherever they land, they are immediately cut and their limbs are severed. What sin has caused this?"

The Buddha said: "In a past life, because of making a livelihood as butchers, slaughtering sentient beings, cutting and slicing, separating bone from flesh, the heads and feet scattered about, hanging them on high racks, weighing and selling them; or hanging sentient beings alive in unbearable suffering, they incur this punishment."

"Twelfth, there are sentient beings born without their five sense faculties complete. What sin has caused this?"

The Buddha said: "In a past life, because of hunting with hawks and hounds, using slings and arrows against birds and animals, cutting off their heads or severing their feet, and plucking the wings of living birds, they incur this punishment."

"Thirteenth, there are sentient beings who are contracted and lame, hunchbacked and bent, unable to move their waists or hips, with crooked feet and clenched hands, unable to grasp or walk. What sin has caused this?"

The Buddha said: "In a past life, because of being cruel and merciless, placing spears along roads, or setting hidden bows, laying out traps and pitfalls so that sentient beings fell in, breaking their heads and legs, causing injuries beyond count, they incur this punishment."

"Fourteenth, there are sentient beings who are constantly shackled and fettered by the hell-wardens and cannot gain release. What sin has caused this?"

The Buddha said: "In a past life, because of netting and catching sentient beings, caging and confining them, causing them hunger and destitution; or being officials and magistrates who greedily seized money, wrongfully imprisoning the virtuous and good, who cried to heaven in their anguish, unable to move freely, they incur this punishment."

"Fifteenth, there are sentient beings who are epileptic, or mad, or deluded, or foolish, unable to distinguish good from bad. What sin has caused this?"

The Buddha said: "In a past life, because of drinking wine and becoming intoxicated and disorderly, committing the thirty-six faults, they later obtain a deluded body, like a drunken person who cannot recognize high from low or distinguish good from bad. For eighty thousand kalpas they fall into the hell of boiling excrement, where the hell-wardens chop and grind them. They cannot achieve death, and they cannot achieve life. Through the five kinds of destitution and the six extremes, they suffer through the long night, all because of the fault of craving wine. When their punishment is served, they first obtain a human body, but one that is crippled, lame, and deformed, hated by others. Wherever they go, they encounter others' misfortunes and never have a time of happiness. Because of the fault of wine, they incur this punishment."

"Sixteenth, there are sentient beings whose forms are very small but whose private parts are very large. They drag them along until their bodies are exhausted, and they must keep pulling them forward. Whether walking, standing, sitting, or lying down, they are an impediment. What sin has caused this?"

The Buddha said: "In a past life, because of engaging in trade and commerce, praising their own goods while disparaging others' merchandise, tampering with measures and scales, stomping the balance forward and back, and cheating people, they incur this punishment."

"Seventeenth, there are sentient beings born without male organs, as eunuchs, unable to take a wife. What sin has caused this?"

The Buddha said: "In a past life, because of delighting in castrating elephants, horses, cattle, sheep, pigs, and dogs in countless numbers, causing these sentient beings unbearable agony, dying and reviving repeatedly, they incur this punishment."

"Eighteenth, there are sentient beings who from birth to old age have no children, standing alone and solitary. What sin has caused this?"

The Buddha said: "In a past life, because of being violent and wicked, not believing in the retribution of virtue and sin; during the season when flocks of birds are hatching and nursing, they went with bottles and vessels along the great sandbanks to collect the eggs of swans, geese, parrots, and other birds, carrying them home to boil and eat. The parent birds, having lost their young, cried out in grief, blood flowing from their eyes, they incur this punishment."

"Nineteenth, there are sentient beings who are orphans from youth, without parents or siblings, serving as menials for others and earning their livelihood through hardship. When they grow to adulthood, they suffer undeserved calamity, are seized by government officials, and imprisoned. With no one to bring them food, they suffer hunger and destitution with no one to appeal to. What sin has caused this?"

The Buddha said: "In a past life, because of delighting in capturing eagles, vultures, hawks, falcons, bears, and tigers, chaining and confining them, separating these sentient beings from their parents and siblings who constantly worried and grieved for them, crying out until it broke one's heart; unable to feed themselves, they were constantly starved, with bones protruding and skin sagging, seeking death but unable to die, they incur this punishment."

"Twentieth, there are sentient beings who are extremely ugly in form, with bodies as black as lacquer, eyes that are blue, swollen heads and cheeks, with blistered faces and flat noses, yellow-red eyes, sparse and broken teeth, foul-smelling breath, dwarfed and hunchbacked, with large bellies and protruding hips, twisted feet, curved spines and misshapen ribs, consuming much clothing and food, covered with foul sores running with pus and blood, swollen or withered, with scabies, boils, and abscesses, and every kind of affliction gathered upon their bodies. Though they seek to draw close to others, no one pays them any mind. If others commit wrongs, the blame falls on them. They never see the Buddha, never hear the Dharma, and never encounter the Saṃgha. What sin has caused this?"

The Buddha said: "In a past life, because of being unfilial children to parents, disloyal subjects to rulers, rulers who did not respect their subordinates, friends who were not trustworthy, neighbors who did not show proper courtesy, courtiers who did not observe proper rank; because of acting heedlessly, with inverted minds and no sense of measure; because of not believing in the Three Treasures, killing rulers and harming teachers, attacking nations and plundering people, besieging cities and destroying strongholds, sneaking through fortifications to rob and steal, with countless evil deeds; praising themselves and maligning others, bullying the orphaned and the elderly, slandering sages and saints, showing contempt for elders, and cheating the humble and lowly. Having committed all these crimes, the retribution of all evils gathered together causes this punishment."

At that time all the beings undergoing punishment, hearing the Buddha speak thus, wept in sorrow that shook the earth. Their tears fell like rain, and they said to the Buddha: "We humbly wish the World-Honored One to abide long and teach the Dharma, so that beings such as we may gain liberation!"

The Buddha said: "If I were to abide long, those of meager virtue would not plant roots of merit, thinking that I will always be here and not reflecting on impermanence. Good men! It is like an infant: when the mother is always at its side, no thought of how rare this is arises. But when the mother is away, the child gives rise to a heart of longing and yearning. When the mother returns, joy arises. Good men! I too am like this. I know the karmic conditions of all sentient beings, their wholesome and unwholesome deeds, and the beauty or ugliness of their retribution. Therefore I enter parinirvāṇa."

At that time the World-Honored One spoke these verses for all the beings undergoing punishment:

> Water flows but does not stay full;
> Fire blazes but does not burn long.
> The sun rises but soon sets;
> The moon waxes full but wanes again.
> Even the honored, the noble, and the powerful
> Are subject to impermanence more than all else.
> Reflect on this and diligently cultivate,
> And bow in reverence to the Unsurpassed One.

When the World-Honored One had spoken these verses, the beings undergoing punishment said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! What wholesome practices should we cultivate to escape this suffering?"

The Buddha said: "You should diligently be filial to your parents, respectfully serve your teachers and elders, take refuge in and honor the Three Treasures; diligently practice generosity, morality, patience, vigor, meditative concentration, and wisdom; cultivate loving-kindness, compassion, sympathetic joy, and equanimity; treat enemies and loved ones equally, regarding them the same as yourself without distinction; do not cheat the orphaned and elderly, do not look down upon the humble and lowly, but protect them as you would yourself. If you can practice in this way, you will have already repaid the kindness of the Buddha and will be forever free from all suffering."

When this sūtra had been spoken, the bodhisattva mahāsattvas attained anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi. The śrāvakas and pratyekabuddhas attained the six supernatural powers, the three forms of clear knowledge, and the full eight liberations. Some attained the purity of the Dharma eye. If any sentient beings hear this sūtra, they will not fall into the three evil paths or the eight difficulties, the hells will find respite, and suffering will be at peace.

At that time the Bodhisattva Xinxiang said to the Buddha: "What should this sūtra be called? How should it be upheld?"

The Buddha told the Bodhisattva Xinxiang: "Good man! This sūtra is called 'The Retribution of Sinful Karma and the Teaching on the Hells.' You should uphold it and cause it to circulate widely. Its merit is immeasurable!"

The devas and the great assembly, hearing this sūtra, were filled with joy. They prostrated with their five limbs on the ground, bowed, and practiced the teaching.
