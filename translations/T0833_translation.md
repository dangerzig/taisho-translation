# 第一義法勝經

## The Sūtra on the Supreme Dharma of Ultimate Truth

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0833

Translated from the Chinese. Northern Wei dynasty, translated by Gautama Prajñāruci (瞿曇般若流支).

---

### Record of the Translation of the Sūtra on the Supreme Dharma of Ultimate Truth

Those who love the Dharma surely have deep good roots. The Nirvāṇa Sūtra says: "Serving the Buddha for two Ganges-fulls of kalpas." The Grand Secretary and Commander-in-Chief, the Honorable Lord Gao of the Wei court, had a devoted heart for the Dharma, conceived the thought of supreme treasure, broadly collected Sanskrit texts, and greatly promoted translation. As for the Sūtra on the Supreme Dharma of Ultimate Truth, among all Dharma-gates this is the very marrow. The Lord's intent was earnest and sincere, and he was moved to inscribe its title. The śramaṇas Tánlín and Gautama Prajñāruci, in the fourth year of the Xīnghé era, the rénxū year, on the first day of the ninth month, a jiǎzǐ day, exchanged the text. From beginning to end, through four revisions, the substance and meaning were settled: five thousand five hundred and seventy-six characters.

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Bhagavān was dwelling at the city of Gayā, not long after attaining the path. He was accompanied by monks who were the most excellent among the excellent, ninety-nine koṭis of bodhisattvas, twenty-eight koṭis of devas, eighty-six thousand monks, nuns, laymen, and laywomen, and sixty thousand strongmen. There were also twelve koṭis of Nirgrantha followers and eighty-four thousand five-powered seers who scorched their bodies with the five fires, emaciated until only skin and bones remained, their belly-skin pressed against their spines, their hair matted into felt, their bodies hunched and bent. They wore deerskin garments or bark clothing, carried ablution jars in their hands, and had all come to the Buddha's presence, intending to debate.

At that time, the World-Honored One's radiance surpassed all others, and he was extraordinarily dignified and beautiful, surpassing all the seers. He was like Mount Sumeru amid black mountains, like the king of six-tusked elephants amid a flock of sheep, like the light of fireflies beside the sun and moon, like a mandārava flower amid fragrant blossoms, like the king of garuḍas amid a flock of birds; the Buddha amid the seers was also like this, doubly dignified.

At that time, the World-Honored One entered the Treasure-Relinquishing Samādhi and manifested immeasurable supernatural transformations. From his left and right he emitted immeasurable light. He projected from his body immeasurable koṭis of tathāgata forms, immeasurable koṭis of bodhisattva forms, immeasurable koṭis of Śakra and Brahmā kings, world-rulers, and again immeasurable hundreds of thousands of arhats, immeasurable thousands of monks, nuns, laymen, and laywomen, immeasurable wheel-turning kings, lesser wheel-turning kings, and petty kings, as well as people of the Eastern Continent's borderlands, people of the southern lands of India, accomplished sorcerers, frontier-dwellers, kṣatriyas of great families, brāhmaṇas, elders, householders, humans and non-humans, of every variety and appearance and adornment. All such beings with names, up to the heavenly assemblies, all emerged from the Tathāgata's body.

At that time, the great assembly all harbored doubts, looking at one another. The bodhisattvas in the assembly all felt great joy and rained down various treasures, and indeed rained down every manner of adornment.

Then the World-Honored One arose from the Treasure-Relinquishing Samādhi and, like a lion shaking its mane, surveyed the ten directions. As he surveyed them, the worlds of all the buddhas in all ten directions and their world-honored ones observed the sahā world as clearly as if looking at the palm of their hand. All those buddhas manifested supernatural transformations just as this World-Honored One Śākyamuni had done, and the beings projected from those tathāgatas' bodies all came to the assembly of the World-Honored One Śākyamuni. Having arrived, they all entered the World-Honored One's great assembly.

At that time, immeasurable bodhisattvas in this assembly, together with Ganges-sands of monks, nuns, laymen, and laywomen, offered the World-Honored One with unspeakable varieties of offerings. Having made their offerings, they stood near the Tathāgata.

Likewise, devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, humans and non-humans, having witnessed the World-Honored One's supernatural display, all came to the Buddha's presence.

At that time, the bodhisattvas who had come from the ten directions made unsurpassed offerings to the World-Honored One. Having done so, they sat upon seats accomplished through the six perfections. Likewise, humans and non-humans each sat upon seats befitting their kind.

The World-Honored One's projections ascended as far as the Akaniṣṭha heavenly palaces and descended as far as the Avīci hell. Moreover, having reached the Avīci hell, all the forms projected by the buddhas of the ten directions entered the pores of Śākyamuni, while all the forms projected by this World-Honored One Śākyamuni entered the bodies of the buddhas of the ten directions.

At that time, there was in the assembly a bodhisattva mahāsattva named Victorious Aggregate, who rose from his seat, arranged his robes to one side, placed his right knee on the ground, joined his palms toward the Buddha, and praised him in verse:

> "Lord of Humanity! Most rare,
> Swiftly revealed for the world to see,
> Never before has such a thing been.
> The māra armies are hidden from view;
> They look at one another and say:
> 'This is most extraordinary!
> Why have we come here?
> It was to cause destruction.
> We seers are now no longer seers;
> Our bodies are wasted, only skin remains,
> Dried and old, without any joy;
> We have not gained such supernatural powers.
> He displays supernatural powers and speaks not;
> He removes the thorns of the Buddha's Dharma.'
> The Buddha's disciples rejoice in the morning;
> The Honored One is lord of the Buddha's Dharma.
> Beings feel rare wonder,
> Their hearts purified and joyful.
> The heavenly hosts all declare:
> 'We wish to attain buddhahood. Lord of Humanity!'
> Dwelling in this assembly,
> Mañjuśrī faces forward;
> Immeasurable Buddha-disciples
> Attend together in this assembly.
> This Mañjuśrī
> Has made offerings to many buddhas.
> The Buddha in this great assembly
> Displays various supernatural powers.
> What is the aspect of this dharma?
> What does the Muni intend to do?
> This assembly harbors doubt;
> May you have compassion and speak for these beings."

At that time, by the Tathāgata's awesome spiritual power, a great seer in the assembly named Blazing Torch spoke to the Bodhisattva Victorious Aggregate: "Young man, be silent. Be silent. I shall now pose a difficult question. If this śramaṇa can resolve my doubts and purify himself, then he may rightly be called one who accords with omniscience. If Nārāyaṇa's, Maheśvara's, or any other sorcerers' magical illusions could produce such things, then this would not be remarkable; such illusions can be accomplished by ordinary people, and it would not necessarily be the work of a buddha."

At that time, the World-Honored One smiled serenely. Having surveyed the assembly of seers, he addressed the Great Seer Blazing Torch: "Venerable Great Seer, you may question as you wish, according to your ability, and I shall answer clearly."

Then the Great Seer Blazing Torch said: "I ask Gautama: from what place are all sentient beings born? What are sentient beings? For what reason does the kalpa end in conflagration? In what place did sentient beings gather in the past before being born in the human realm? By what characteristic can one know the subtle inner self within a sentient being's body? Is it one cubit in measure? Two cubits? Two finger-breadths? One finger-breadth? The size of a grain of barley? A grain of wheat? A bean? A sesame seed? A mustard seed?"

At that time, the World-Honored One praised the Great Seer Blazing Torch: "Excellent, Great Seer! Excellent, excellent! You, Great Seer, who have a lifespan of sixty kalpas, have always cultivated practice, and today you pose such a question."

Among the seers, there was another great seer who thought: "I have always dwelled in the forest, yet I never knew the measure of Great Seer Blazing Torch's lifespan. No one has ever spoken of it. How can the śramaṇa Gautama know this?"

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the Great Seer Blazing Torch: "Great Seer, listen carefully and consider well. I shall now explain for you. As for your question about where all sentient beings are born: such a matter has no words, no explanation. The arising of sentient beings proceeds through the sequence of ignorance and conditions, up to birth, old age, death, and so forth. Furthermore, Great Seer, sentient beings arise from conditions, namely the conditions of father and mother. Furthermore, Great Seer, the union of father and mother is the cause of sentient beings; at the time of kalpa-formation, the wind of karma blows consciousness into the female womb, and this is the condition. Furthermore, Great Seer, the noble truth of suffering, the truths of the origin of suffering, its cessation, and the path to its cessation are what constitute sentient beings. Furthermore, the five appropriated aggregates and the eighteen elements constitute sentient beings. Great Seer, you should know: those truths, aggregates, and elements are sentient beings, not different from karma; and karma is not different from sentient beings. Great Seer, you should know: the realm of sentient beings neither decreases nor increases."

Then the great seer asked: "Gautama! If sentient beings neither decrease nor increase, why do sentient beings possess autonomous bodies in human and heavenly realms, later become dogs, and then again attain autonomous human and heavenly bodies?"

The Buddha said: "Great Seer! As you say, this reasoning does not hold. If there were autonomous self-mastery, it would not be subject to another. Thus, Great Seer, if the body were autonomous, how could it later lose its autonomy? Great Seer, you should know: just as a firefly conceives the thought, 'My light can illuminate all of Jambudvīpa,' yet the firefly's light has no power whatsoever to illuminate all of Jambudvīpa, so too all those with untamed minds have no real autonomy. Furthermore, Great Seer, if self-mastery were truly autonomous, afflictions would diminish; but dogs have more afflictions, so dogs would be autonomous; then non-autonomy would also be autonomy, and autonomy would also be non-autonomy. If dogs were autonomous with afflictions being equal, then sentient beings would neither decrease nor increase."

The great seer said: "Gautama, do you not sever afflictions?"

The Buddha said: "Great Seer! Afflictions are not something I understand as fixed entities; I understand afflictions, but I do not sever them."

The great seer said: "If that is so, then you are autonomous."

The Buddha said: "Great Seer! Just so, just so. Because there is no real substance, I am autonomous."

The great seer said: "Gautama! Let us set that aside. Gautama, you said earlier that sentient beings are born from the union of father and mother. Yet many beings engage in union, but few sentient beings are born. How is this?"

The Buddha said: "Great Seer! I shall now question you with an analogy. One seed produces one tree, and from one tree come immeasurable fruits, and those immeasurable fruits contain seeds that could generate new trees, yet some can serve as seeds and some cannot. How is this?"

He answered: "Gautama! It is because wind disperses the seeds from the tree."

The Buddha said: "Great Seer! The fruits of sentient beings are also like this, scattered by the wind of karma. Great Seer, you should know: some seeds in their stored places are eaten by worms, and some are scattered and destroyed by the wind of karma. Great Seer, you should know: the loss of trees through such calamities is small and hardly worth mentioning, but the sentient beings lost through such calamities are beyond telling. Furthermore, Great Seer, the realm of sentient beings arises from discrimination. Great Seer, you should know: the realm of sentient beings, through the mental factors of mind, operates in various places, all with objects of grasping. This meaning has been explained. Thus, Great Seer, the realm of sentient beings arises from discrimination."

When the great seer heard this, he spoke thus: "Just so, just so. Gautama has purified this first difficult question. Furthermore, Gautama, you should further explain: for what reason does the kalpa burn?"

The Buddha said: "Great Seer! Because of impermanence, I teach that the dharma-realm undergoes kalpa-burning. Great Seer, you should know: if the earth were not burned when the kalpa ends, then the dharma-realm would have two aspects, one permanent and one impermanent. If that were so, all tathāgatas would be speakers of untruth. Furthermore, Great Seer, if all dharmas are impermanent, changing and not abiding, then the Tathāgata rightly receives the name of omniscient one."

When the great seer heard this, he told the young ascetics: "This one is truly the omniscient one, as the name accords with reality."

The Buddha said: "Great Seer! If the Tathāgata did not send forth the kalpa-burning, all sentient beings would not know the seasons, would not recognize the name of kalpas, would not recognize the time of conflict, would not recognize the time of goodness. Great Seer, you should know: if the Tathāgata did not send forth the kalpa-burning, there would be no one who could know the different fruits of good and bad karma. Furthermore, Great Seer, you should know that the conflagration at the kalpa's end is the skillful means of the tathāgatas. Great Seer, you should know: those sentient beings who hear and believe that the kalpa will end in burning, out of fear of the coming fire, all gather merit and have faith in the Tathāgata.

"Furthermore, Great Seer, it is like a great serpent called 'Saliva-Summoner,' which summons with its eyes, summons with its ears, summons with its nose, and summons with its mouth. Likewise, Great Seer, the Tathāgata is just so: he gathers beings through generosity, kind speech, beneficial action, and shared endeavor.

"Furthermore, Great Seer, it is like a person placing gold in fire, not placing it in fire out of anger toward the gold, but because it is unrefined and he wishes to refine it, to make it accord with a true treasure, so that when it accords with treasure it becomes precious. Great Seer, you should know: for this reason it is hammered, refined, and purified. Likewise, Great Seer, the buddhas and tathāgatas do not send forth the kalpa-ending conflagration without reason, and no sentient being is burned by the kalpa-fire."

Then the Great Seer Blazing Torch said: "How extraordinary, World-Honored One! The kalpa-ending fire is sent forth to burn, yet not a single sentient being is burned by the kalpa-fire."

The Buddha said: "Great Seer! Not a single sentient being is burned by the Tathāgata. Furthermore, Great Seer, it is like fine rain falling in the ten directions. The number of tathāgatas is even greater than those raindrops. Tenth-stage bodhisattvas are likewise, dwelling above. Having so dwelt, they each rescue sentient beings with their own hands and bring them to liberation. Great Seer, you should know: when those sentient beings see the tathāgatas and bodhisattvas with their dignified and beautiful forms, having seen them and having also seen the great fire of the kalpa's end, and having seen themselves delivered, they feel great joy, and a purified mind arises. They make this vow: 'May I too deliver sentient beings like this! May I too have such dignified form! May I too have a body of golden color!' After sentient beings give rise to such thoughts, some attain liberation of mind and immediately realize arhatship; some, seeing the kalpa-fire and feeling revulsion, attain stream-entry, once-returning, non-returning, or arhatship; some attain the path of the pratyekabuddha; some attain the patient acceptance of the unborn; some attain the stage of non-retrogression; some are born in the heaven of the Four Kings, the heaven of the Thirty-Three, Yāma heaven, Tuṣita heaven, the heaven of Transformation of Joy, or the heaven of Free Enjoyment of Others' Transformations; some are born in the Brahma heaven, the Brahma Retinue, the Brahma Assembly, and so on up to Akaniṣṭha; some attain the rank of wheel-turning king, mighty wheel-turning king, or minor king of India; and so on, Great Seer, down to kṣatriyas, brāhmaṇas, and elders. Great Seer, you should know: through this skillful means, having seen the Tathāgata's physical form and then seen their own bodies, they feel great terror. Having attained liberation, they know the Tathāgata's kindness and repay it, draw near to the Tathāgata, and hear the true Dharma. Having heard the Dharma, they practice in accord with the Dharma without negligence, and through this skillful means sentient beings are kept from entering the evil paths.

"Furthermore, Great Seer, the fields of perception of the tenth-stage bodhisattvas, the earth-elements therein, and the realms of sentient beings that extend even beyond these: all those sentient beings, seeing the fault of saṃsāra, enter the nirvāṇa realm without remainder. Great Seer, you should know: it is for this reason that the kalpa-ending conflagration is sent forth."

At that time, the Great Seer Blazing Torch thought to himself: "This son of the Śākyas, this great man accomplished in the six perfections, this foremost and supreme person, does not address me by name but calls me 'Great Seer.' Let me test whether this is truly an omniscient one or not. I now truly know that this is an omniscient one. I should address him by his true name."

Then the Great Seer Blazing Torch, having reflected, said to the Buddha: "O great accumulation of merit! O being of immeasurable wisdom! O omniscient one! Please explain further for me: in what place do sentient beings gather?"

The Buddha said: "Great Seer! You should know that sentient beings gather in no particular place. You should know that sentient beings gather in equality; this is called gathering. You should know that sentient beings gather in the one vehicle; this is called gathering. All gather as bodhisattvas, in the realm of nirvāṇa without remainder. Great Seer, you should know: sentient beings gather in no particular place.

"Great Seer, you should know: just as various small and great rivers enter the great ocean and all become one flavor, so too, Great Seer, when the outflows of all sentient beings are exhausted, they all share the flavor of liberation, equally gathered as bodhisattvas.

"Furthermore, Great Seer, if sentient beings gather together within the ocean of saṃsāra, I say that such gathering is not true gathering. Furthermore, Great Seer, it is like moths blown together by the wind; when the wind stops, they scatter. Likewise, Great Seer, the realm of sentient beings is mutually bound by karma. Those who perform the actions of hell are born in hell and gather in hell. Great Seer, you should know: sentient beings thus bound by mutual karma, those who perform the actions of hungry ghosts are born among hungry ghosts and gather as hungry ghosts; those who perform the actions of animals are born among animals and gather as animals; those who perform the actions of humans and devas are born among humans and devas and gather as humans and devas."

The great seer further said: "O autonomous one, omniscient one, worthy of offerings from the world! Please explain further: how can one know that a certain sentient being born in the human realm, having gathered in a certain place, was born here? How can one know?"

The Buddha said: "Great Seer! If sentient beings who previously gathered in hell are born in the human realm, then upon seeing one another they give rise to evil thoughts, with mutual dislike and hatred; when they see each other, some get headaches, some lose control of their bowels, some lose control of their bladders. Great Seer, you should know: these are the signs of beings who gathered in hell and were born in the human realm. If one sees such signs among humans, one should know that they gathered in hell and came from there.

The great seer further said: "O light of the world, omniscient one! Please explain further: how can one know beings who gathered among animals and then were born in the human realm? What signs do they have?"

The Buddha said: "Great Seer! If one's previous life was among animals, and one gathered there and was born in the human realm, then upon seeing one another they give rise to anger, seek out each other's faults, and constantly look for opportunities to cause harm. Great Seer, you should know: these are the signs of beings who gathered among animals and were born in the human realm. If one sees such signs among humans, one should know they gathered among animals and came from there.

"Furthermore, Great Seer, if one's previous life was among hungry ghosts, and one gathered there and was born in the human realm, such people love foul smells, are greedy for food and drink, miserly and unwilling to share. Those who came from the same place among hungry ghosts, upon seeing others prosperous and happy, give rise to jealousy and covet others' possessions. Great Seer, you should know: these are the signs of beings who gathered among hungry ghosts and were born in the human realm. If one sees such signs among humans, one should know they gathered among hungry ghosts and came from there.

"Furthermore, Great Seer, if one's previous life was in the human realm, and one gathered in a different place and was again born human, then upon seeing each other they give rise to affection. If one sees such signs among humans, one should know they gathered in the human realm and came from there."

The great seer asked: "World-Honored One! If beings gathered in the heavens and then fell to be born in the human realm, what signs do they have? How can one know?"

The Buddha said: "Great Seer! If one's previous life was among the heavens and one gathered there and was born in the human realm, then upon seeing each other, they delight in gazing and cannot bear to part. If one sees such signs among humans, one should know they gathered in the heavens and came from there.

"Great Seer, you should know: these are the causal signs of beings' gatherings."

At that time, the Great Seer Blazing Torch, having heard the Buddha's teaching, felt great joy and said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! If there are beings who do not seek omniscience, everything they do is empty and yields nothing."

Then the World-Honored One addressed the Great Seer Blazing Torch: "As for your question about the subtle self within the body of sentient beings, Great Seer, listen: if one could discern and find sentient beings, then one could discern the subtle self of sentient beings. Great Seer, you should know: it is like a person born blind, and someone asks them, 'What does the color white look like?' What do you think? That person born blind has never seen color; could they speak of such a color?"

The great seer answered: "They could not."

The Buddha said: "Just so, just so. Great Seer, the person born blind has never seen with their eyes, and therefore cannot speak. I am likewise: I do not see a subtle inner self within sentient beings, and therefore I do not speak of one.

"Furthermore, Great Seer, the eye is not a sentient being; likewise, neither the ear, nose, tongue, body, nor mind can be called a sentient being. Furthermore, Great Seer, the five appropriated aggregates cannot be called a sentient being; the eighteen elements, the twelve divisions, and the twelve links of dependent arising cannot be called a sentient being. Furthermore, Great Seer, inner emptiness cannot be called a sentient being, outer emptiness cannot, and the emptiness of both inner and outer cannot be called a sentient being.

"Great Seer, you should know: the eye is momentary, not abiding, changing and not stopping; likewise, the ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind are all momentary, not abiding, changing and not stopping. Likewise, Great Seer, the five aggregates are also momentary, not abiding, changing and not stopping. Likewise, Great Seer, the thirty-six kinds of impurities are all momentary, not abiding, changing and not stopping. In all of these there is no name of 'sentient being.'

"Furthermore, Great Seer, it is the combination of material elements that is given the designation 'sentient being.' If one reflects upon and examines part by part, one finds no life, no nourisher, no person, no young man, and so forth.

"Great Seer, you should know: if there were sentient beings, the Tathāgata would not teach the four noble truths. Because there are no sentient beings, there is the Tathāgata. If one does not understand the Dharma, it is like grasping at a collection, like grasping at what the mind apprehends."

At that time, the Great Seer Blazing Torch said: "World-Honored One! I, Blazing Torch, from this day forward seek omniscience. World-Honored One! If one great kalpa were reckoned as one day and night, and thirty such days were counted as one month, and twelve such months were counted as one year, and such years were counted to complete one kalpa, World-Honored One, I would rather dwell in a fire-pit for such immeasurable kalpas. Though Mount Sumeru is lofty and great, reaching as high as Akaniṣṭha, I would rather for such immeasurable kalpas throw myself from the top of that mountain, plunging to the ground. Though the kalpa-fire burns fiercely in five places, I would rather for such immeasurable kalpas constantly scorch myself with such five fires. World-Honored One, I would rather endure such sufferings, but I will never abandon my effort to seek omniscience, and will never rest from the causes and conditions of seeking omniscience."

At that time, the five-powered seers all rose from their seats, drew near to the World-Honored One, and said: "From this day forward, we seek anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi, exerting ourselves with all our strength."

Then the World-Honored One, after the seers had spoken, emitted light from between his eyebrows called Vinīpati. When this light appeared, it illuminated the ten directions. The buddhas of the ten directions also emitted such light from between their eyebrows. Through the power of the Vinīpati light, this great earth shook in six ways: trembling, evenly trembling, quaking, evenly quaking, rising, and evenly rising, with the west high and east low, the south high and north low. All the buddhas of the ten directions in the heavens above rained flowers upon the Buddha's assembly, heavenly drums produced a most delightful sound, the gandharva king played music of five divisions to make offerings to the Tathāgata, and wind carried the heavenly fragrance to perfume the Tathāgata. The bodhisattvas rejoiced and cast their necklaces into the air above the Tathāgata, with flower-incense, burnt incense, fine garlands, unguents, scattered fragrances, fine garments, banners, and canopies to make offerings to the Tathāgata. The devas rejoiced and in the sky rained mandārava flowers and great mandārava flowers. The entire great assembly, hearts filled with joy, offered their own fine garments to the Tathāgata.

At that time, the Vinīpati light of Śākyamuni Tathāgata ascended as far as the Akaniṣṭha heavenly palaces, descended as far as the Avīci hell, and having so illuminated, circled the buddhas of the ten directions, then returned and entered the crown of the World-Honored One's head.

At that time, the venerable Subhūti, in fine verse, requested the Tathāgata:

> "O morning sun, son of the Śākyas,
> Emitting light to illuminate the ten directions,
> This is not without cause;
> We pray you explain for us.
> Seeing the Lord of Humanity's display,
> This assembly all harbors doubt,
> Yet with joyful hearts
> And purified minds of aspiration.
> Some join their palms,
> Some say 'Excellent!'
> We pray the Tathāgata will speak
> To remove the doubts of beings.
> In the sky, the Lord of the gods,
> Brahmā, and the world-rulers
> All feel great joy
> And praise his true merits.
> The devas in the sky
> Rain down various wondrous flowers;
> Many kinds of music
> Sound without being struck."

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the venerable Subhūti: "Subhūti, do you see the Great Seer Blazing Torch?"

"Subhūti! This person, in the future, in the world called Moonlight, shall attain buddhahood with the name Vipaśyin Tathāgata, Worthy of Offerings, Perfectly Enlightened. He shall appear in the world at the same time as the final tathāgata among the thousand tathāgatas of this Fortunate Kalpa. Subhūti! If any sentient being hears the name of Vipaśyin Tathāgata, they shall receive his power like a wish-fulfilling jewel, and all who desire shall obtain. Subhūti! The eighty-four thousand great seers, having heard this Dharma-gate, shall attain the stage of non-retrogression. Subhūti! All of them, in the Dharma of the World-Honored Maitreya, shall attain the tenth stage, and after three hundred kalpas shall be born in the world of the Tathāgata Self-Illuminating Light, Worthy of Offerings, Perfectly Enlightened.

"Subhūti! Immeasurable bodhisattvas who hear this Dharma-gate shall immediately attain the śūraṅgama samādhi, the samādhi of sound-wisdom, the samādhi of receiving the supreme stage, the samādhi of illusion, the samādhi of realm-victory, the samādhi of the wisdom-king, the samādhi of ocean-treasury, the samādhi of earth-treasury, the samādhi of space-treasury, and the samādhi of obtaining illumination. Subhūti! Ganges-sands of koṭis of devas shall all attain the patient acceptance of the unborn. Immeasurable monks and nuns, laymen and laywomen, shall realize arhatship. Subhūti! Ganges-sands of devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, humans and non-humans, shall arouse the aspiration for anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi.

"Subhūti! Seeing these causes and conditions, the Tathāgata emitted this Vinīpati light to illuminate all."

At that time, the World-Honored One smiled serenely and extended his tongue to cover his own face. From the tongue emerged immeasurable colors, various colors including blue, yellow, red, white, purple, and beryl, pervading immeasurable, boundless worlds, then returning and entering the World-Honored One's feet.

At that time, the Bodhisattva Inexhaustible Intent rose from his seat, arranged his robes to one side, placed his right knee on the ground, and with palms joined said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! If there were no cause, the Tathāgata would not smile. For what cause does the World-Honored One now smile?"

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the Bodhisattva Inexhaustible Intent: "Good man! I extended my tongue and smiled for the benefit of beings who lack faith; for one who does not speak falsely has such a tongue."

At that time, the Bodhisattva Inexhaustible Intent said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! If a good man or good woman can explain even one verse of this Dharma-gate for others, how much merit do they obtain?"

The Buddha said: "Good man! In all the worlds of the ten directions, as far as the buddha-eye can see, if one were to take all the offerings and all the pleasant things of all the buddhas and offer them all, and even after all those buddhas enter nirvāṇa build jeweled stūpas for them, all that merit; yet if someone can explain even one verse of this Dharma-gate of speaking truth for another's sake, the merit obtained would be far greater, surpassing the former merit.

"Good man! If anyone, upon hearing someone explain this supreme Dharma-gate, gives rise to a purified mind and praises them saying 'Excellent!', such a person praises all buddhas. And if anyone can make offerings to the speaker, it is no different from making offerings to me."

At that time, the World-Honored One universally surveyed the entire assembly. Having done so, he spoke thus: "I now speak the truth. Good men! Wherever this Dharma-gate abides, in that place it shall be regarded by all buddhas. Good men! This Dharma-gate, in the future, shall be medicine for sentient beings in Jambudvīpa. If anyone can practice this Dharma-gate in three ways: reading it, chanting it, or explaining it for others, that person is no different from one who requests the turning of the Dharma-wheel when the Buddha is in the world.

"Good men! If anyone can copy this Dharma-gate, that person will easily attain anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi, and it will not be difficult. They will be able to uphold all the treasury of the buddhas.

"Good men! Those destined for the evil paths will never hear this Dharma-gate. Good men! If any sentient being hears this Dharma-gate even once, after shedding their human body they will be born in a pure buddha-world. Good men! Those who have made offerings to a thousand buddhas and planted good roots, for such a one will this Dharma-gate reach their ears.

"Good men! If a good man or good woman hears this Dharma-gate, and having heard gives rise to faith, upholds it, reads and chants it, and can explain it for others, I declare that person holds bodhi in their hand. I declare that person will certainly attain the five eyes, their faculties will not be deficient from this point forward, and at the time of death they will not lose right mindfulness. That person shall attain the samādhi of the collection of all buddhas, the Vairocana lion-display samādhi, the dhāraṇī-treasury samādhi, the jewel-seal-crest samādhi, the prophecy samādhi, the world-contemplation-seal samādhi, and the wordless-casket samādhi; they shall attain the supreme dhāraṇī of all dharmas, the doubt-severing dhāraṇī, and the first-meaning definitive dhāraṇī, and immeasurable hundreds of thousands of such dhāraṇī-gates. They shall attain the five supernatural powers, and in accordance with their mindful recollection, they shall freely choose their rebirth."

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed Mañjuśrī, son of the Dharma-king: "Mañjuśrī! You have already made offerings to many buddhas and world-honored ones, and can guard and bear in mind this Dharma-gate, and can widely explain it for others in various places. Mañjuśrī! The offerings and reverential service you have made to however many buddhas, the good roots from your honoring, praising, and attending them, Mañjuśrī, what do you think? Do these good roots have a limit? Can they be measured?"

Mañjuśrī answered: "World-Honored One! They cannot be measured."

The Buddha said: "Mañjuśrī! If you can widely explain this Dharma-gate for others in this sahā world during the time of the five defilements, the merit will surpass that. Mañjuśrī! If you only offered robes, food, bedding, and medicine to all those buddhas, but did not explain this Dharma-gate for others, then with regard to those buddhas you would be at fault. Mañjuśrī! If you had never once made offerings to a single buddha, but widely explained this Dharma-gate for others, you would thereby be making offerings to all tathāgatas."

When the World-Honored One had finished speaking, Mañjuśrī, son of the Dharma-king, the Great Seer Blazing Torch, the entire great assembly, and all devas, humans, and asuras, gandharvas and others, having heard what the Buddha taught, rejoiced and praised it.
