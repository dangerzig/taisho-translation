# 佛說無畏陀羅尼經
## The Buddha's Teaching: Dhāraṇī Sūtra of Fearlessness

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1388

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmadeva (法賢).

---

At that time, the Buddha addressed the bhikṣus: "All of you, listen carefully. I shall now proclaim the Dhāraṇī of Fearlessness. This dhāraṇī can bring sentient beings the cessation of all difficulties. Bhikṣus! If a person hears it and receives and upholds it, the merit that person obtains will be immeasurable and boundless." He then spoke the dhāraṇī:

*Oṃ namo ārya-valokite-śvarāya bodhi-sattvāya mahā-sattvāya mahā-kāruṇikāya. Tadyathā: oṃ mukti su-mukti kṣe-nāni vi-kṣe-nāni nirmāli maṅgali su-maṅgali su-mukhe vi-mukhe sarva-bhaya-vimokṣaṇi sarva-bhayopadrava-vibhaya-vimokṣaṇi rāja-bhayaṃ cora-bhayaṃ maraṇa-bhayaṃ abhirya-bhayaṃ śastra-bhayaṃ agni-bhayaṃ udaka-bhayaṃ viṣa-bhayaṃ para-cakra-bhayaṃ sarva-bhayaṃ śatru-bhayaṃ aśani-bhayaṃ sarva-bhayaṃ senā-mārga-tu-va cora-mārga-tu-va siṃha-mārga-tu-va megha-rā-mārga-tu-va yakṣa-mārga-tu-va davi-pa-mārga-tu-va caṇḍa-mārga-tu-va kṛṣṇa-sarpa-mārga-tu-va aja-kara-mārga-tu-va hasti-mārga-tu-va samudra-mārga-tu-va kāla-pāśa-mārga-tu-va nigaḍa-bandha-tu-va kaṣṭa-bandha-tu-va carma-bandha-tu-va viṣa-kṛmi-tu-va durabhikṣa-tu-va nigaḍa-bandha-tu-va para-śastra-tu-va sarvādaiva-bhaye-śu sarvopadrava-śu buddhe sarva-bhaya-buddhe rakṣa rakṣa mama sarva-sattvānāṃ āyur-rūpa-śrīyaṃ vardhati ārya-valokite-śvarā-kṣati vihati hale vihale sarvī-pratihārī-thi kamu-canī mokṣaṇī vīryaṇī oṃ namaḥ svāhā.*

At that time the World-Honored One, having spoken this dhāraṇī, further addressed the bhikṣus: "If a good man or good woman hears this dhāraṇī, arouses a mind of sincere devotion, reverently believes in it, makes offerings to it, reads, recites, receives, and upholds it, copies it by hand or has others copy it, explains it to others, and broadly proclaims and disseminates it, that person will not be harmed by danger from kings, danger from water or fire, danger from swords and weapons, or danger from enemies and bandits. Furthermore, they will not be harmed by danger from yakṣas, danger from bonds and imprisonment, danger from thunder and hail, danger from lightning, danger from drowning in the great ocean, danger from pythons and venomous stings, danger from lions and maddened elephants, or danger from fierce tigers, wild beasts, famine, or epidemic. All such dangers will cause them no harm, and even all terrors will be entirely dispelled."

At that time the assembly of bhikṣus, having heard what the Buddha taught, joyfully believed and received it, bowed in reverence, and departed.
