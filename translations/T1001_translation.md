# 法華曼荼羅威儀形色法經

## The Lotus Sūtra Maṇḍala: Sūtra on the Ritual Forms and Colors of Dignified Appearance

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1001

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

The maṇḍala of the Wondrous Dharma Lotus Sūtra King, confirmed and accomplished: upon the eight-petaled lotus, the jeweled stūpa surges up from below; within it, the two-footed Bhagavān; the eight great bodhisattvas and the three-layered square altar's samaya retinue; the wondrous dignified forms and colors; the wish-fulfilling assembly's directional positions and emblems; the maṇḍala of the eight equalities; the great assembly for accomplishing the Lotus Samādhi; the sūtra on the dharma of entering the first bhūmi in this present life and assuredly realizing bodhi.

At that time, Vairocana Bhagavān addressed Vajrapāṇi, the Secret Hand Bodhisattva: "Son of the Buddha! Listen with focused mind as I explain the confirmation and accomplishment of the great maṇḍala of the Wondrous Dharma, the positions of the wish-fulfilling assembly for all the sacred beings, and the dharma of their dignified forms and colors.

"What is called the inner-mind maṇḍala: the ground of the body is the self-nature of the dharmadhātu, empowered with the mantra's secret mudrā empowerment. Originally pure in nature, because it is protected by the four great vajra guardians, it is purified of all dust and defilement, and of the faults of self, person, sentient being, life-span, mind-born, youth, and insentient beings.

"The ground is of indigo lapis lazuli; the field is bounded by gold. Exquisite lotuses are scattered everywhere upon it. The square altar faces west with open passage, and boundary paths encircle it. Within, the mind-born eight-petaled flower king appears, with citron-colored stem, its petals spread in intricate and wondrous beauty, fresh and white as snow, radiating pure white light that surpasses all worldly things, with three stamens between each petal.

"Upon the flower's calyx, a great stūpa of the seven jewels surges forth, five hundred yojanas tall, three hundred and fifty yojanas in both breadth and width, perfectly equal. Upon the pavilion rests a wish-fulfilling jewel, encircled by a garland of flames. Vajra chains are strung, and jeweled bells are hung everywhere. It is adorned with every kind of precious treasure. Five thousand balustrades, ten million niches and chambers, and countless banners are suspended. Seven-jeweled necklaces and five-jeweled bell-pendants hang and blaze on all four sides, all emitting the fragrance of tamāla-pattra and sandalwood. Throughout the great trichiliocosm, heavenly jeweled flowers rain down in profusion as offerings.

"At that time, upon the lion throne within the stūpa, Śākyamuni and Prabhūtaratna, the two-footed sacred lords, each sit in half-lotus upon the eight-petaled flower.

"The eight great bodhisattvas, the most honored and special bodies in all the world, transcending body, speech, and mind, reach the realm of the mind-ground where they attain supreme and extraordinary realization. Upon the eight petals, beginning from the northeast corner and proceeding rightward, the eight great bodhisattvas are arranged: first, Maitreya Bodhisattva; then Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva, Bhaiṣajyarāja Bodhisattva, Gadgadasvara Bodhisattva, Nityodyukta Bodhisattva, Akṣayamati Bodhisattva, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, and Samantabhadra Bodhisattva.

"In the four corners within this inner court: in the northeast corner, Mahākāśyapa; in the southeast, Subhūti; in the southwest, Śāriputra; in the northwest, Mahāmaudgalyāyana.

"In the second court: at the east gate, place Vajra-Chain Bodhisattva; at the south gate, Vajra-Bell Bodhisattva; before the stūpa, Vajra-Hook Bodhisattva; at the north gate, Vajra-Lasso Bodhisattva. North of the east gate, place Mahāsthāmaprāpta Bodhisattva; south of the gate, Ratnapāṇi Bodhisattva. East of the south gate, place Ratnadhvaja Bodhisattva; west of the gate, Nakṣatra-rāja Bodhisattva. South of the west gate, place Ratnacandra Bodhisattva; north, Pūrṇacandra Bodhisattva. West of the north gate, place Pradānaśūra Bodhisattva; east, Sarvākāra-saṃpad Bodhisattva. In the northeast corner, place Flower-Offering Bodhisattva; in the southeast, Lamp-Offering Bodhisattva; in the southwest, Perfume Bodhisattva; in the northwest, Incense-Offering Bodhisattva.

"In the third court: at the east gate, place Dhṛtarāṣṭra, Heavenly King; at the south gate, Virūḍhaka, Heavenly King; at the west gate, Virūpākṣa, Heavenly King; at the north gate, Great Brahmā, Heavenly King. South of the north gate, place Śakra Devānām Indra. East of the south gate, place Maheśvara; west of the gate, Nāga King Nanda. South of the west gate, place Wonderful Dharma, Kiṃnara King; north of the gate, Music-Sound, Gandharva King. West of the north gate, place Rāhula, Asura King; east of the gate, As-You-Wish, Garuḍa King. In the northeast corner, Sacred Ucchuṣma Vajra; in the southeast corner, Sacred Kuṇḍalī Vajra; in the southwest corner, Sacred Acala Vajra; in the northwest corner, Sacred Trailokyavijaya Vajra.

"On the four sides of the altar, paint the food-offering boundary paths. Also paint four gates. Above the altar, hang a celestial canopy, with twenty-four banners suspended on all four sides. At each of the four corners, erect banner-pennons and four wisdom-vases with dark bases filled with fragrant water, with fresh and beautiful flowers inserted in the mouths. At both sides of each of the altar's four gates, place two argha vessels filled with fragrant water, containing saffron and floating seasonal flowers, extremely fragrant and pure. At the four gates, place four incense burners and burn five-flavored incense as offerings. At the four corners, place copper lamp-stands with ghee oil for illumination. Outside the four corners, drive khadira-wood pegs; if this wood is unavailable, cast copper pegs as substitutes. This presents the accomplished yoga visualization wisdom ritual of the Wondrous Lotus Sūtra King: the single dharma explaining dignified forms and colors, and the single mudrā and directional positions."

"Having explained the rites within the maṇḍala and the adornments of the single-story jeweled stūpa, I now explain the dignified forms and colors of the two sacred lords within the stūpa.

"Prabhūtaratna Tathāgata:

> "Uṣṇīṣa crown with dark-blue hair,
> Between the brows, a thread of ūrṇā-light
> Illuminating all universally;
> Body of golden color.
> Concentration-wisdom, the wisdom-fist mudrā,
> Full lotus with right over left,
> The left wheel hanging down;
> Body clad in kāṣāya robes.
> Dwelling upon a great lotus,
> Ever abiding in the full moon disk,
> Radiance pervading everywhere.
> Surging forth within the jeweled stūpa,
> Confirming the wheel of the Wondrous Dharma,
> Causing it never to cease,
> Broadly saving all sentient beings."

"Śākyamuni Tathāgata:

> "Uṣṇīṣa with dark blue topknot,
> Between the brows, a white ūrṇā light
> Illuminating eighty thousand leagues to the east;
> Body of golden color.
> Left hand in concentration forming a fist mudrā,
> Right wisdom-hand open facing outward,
> Ring and little fingers bent,
> Middle, index, and thumb extended.
> Full lotus with left over right,
> The right wheel hanging down;
> Clad in kāṣāya robes.
> Dwelling upon a white lotus,
> Ever abiding within the moon disk,
> Radiance blazing and brilliant.
> To cause the teaching to spread abroad,
> He dwells there and expounds the Dharma,
> Surrounded by the samādhi assembly,
> Each one a holder of the sūtras."

"Having explained the forms and marks of the two tathāgatas, I now explain the marks and excellences of the eight great bodhisattvas. Upon the eight-petaled flower king, proceeding rightward from the northeast petal, Maitreya is first.

"First, Maitreya Bodhisattva:

> "Upon the crown, a wondrous jeweled diadem,
> Dark-blue hair falling to both shoulders;
> Body also of flesh-color.
> Left concentration-hand holds a purple lotus
> With a vajra bell upon it;
> Right wisdom-hand pressed upon the knee.
> Full lotus, right over left;
> Countenance of great compassion and great mercy.
> Body clad in kāṣāya robes,
> Pearl garlands and jeweled necklaces,
> Bangles and bracelets as arm ornaments.
> A thousand-colored wondrous celestial robe,
> Soft and fine about the waist;
> Wondrous lower garment of vermilion red.
> Body adorned with marks of excellence,
> Complete with the thirty-two marks;
> Moonlight and conch-shell color.
> His radiance pervades everywhere;
> The finest flower forms his seat,
> Dwelling within the great moon disk."

"Second, Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva:

> "Upon the crown, a five-tuft diadem,
> Dark-blue hair falling to the brows;
> Body of golden color.
> Left concentration-hand holds a blue lotus
> With three swords upon it.
> Full lotus, right over left;
> Countenance of compassionate welcome.
> Body clad in kāṣāya robes,
> Flower garlands and wondrous necklaces,
> Bangles and bracelets as arm ornaments.
> Exquisite jeweled celestial robes
> With colored silk about the waist;
> Garment of reddish-light color,
> Adorning the supreme wondrous body.
> Body's radiance like an autumn rainbow;
> Jeweled flower forms his seat,
> Dwelling within the moon disk."

"Third, Bhaiṣajyarāja Bodhisattva:

> "Upon the crown, a wondrous jeweled diadem,
> Dark-blue hair falling beside the ears;
> Body the color of the morning sun.
> Left concentration-fist pressed upon the knee;
> Right wisdom-hand: a sun above clouds.
> Full lotus, right over left;
> Countenance of great compassion saving the world.
> Body adorned with wondrous flower garlands,
> Celestial robes and necklaces,
> Bangles and bracelets as arm ornaments.
> Fine brocade about the waist,
> Red silk as wondrous lower garment,
> Adorning the body of excellent marks.
> Body's radiance shining brilliantly everywhere;
> Jeweled lotus forms his seat,
> Dwelling within the moon-disk ocean."

"Fourth, Gadgadasvara Bodhisattva:

> "Upon the crown, a great jeweled diadem,
> Dark-blue hair falling below the ears;
> Body of reddish-yellow color.
> Left concentration-hand holds an unopened lotus;
> Right wisdom-fist pressed upon the knee.
> Full lotus, right over left;
> Countenance of sorrowful compassion saving the world.
> Flower-hair and necklaces,
> Kāṣāya and wondrous celestial robes,
> Jade rings as arm ornaments.
> Myriad-colored cloth about the waist;
> Garment of bimba-fruit color,
> Adorning the body of myriad virtues.
> Body's radiance pervading all;
> Lotus forms his seat,
> Dwelling within the full moon disk."

"Fifth, Nityodyukta Bodhisattva:

> "Upon the crown, a wondrous jeweled diadem,
> Dark-blue hair falling to both ears;
> Body of white flesh-color.
> Left concentration-hand touching the knee;
> Right wisdom-hand holds a maṇi jewel.
> Full lotus, right over left;
> Countenance of compassion for all.
> Pearl garlands and celestial robes,
> Kāṣāya and wondrous necklaces,
> Arm ornaments exceedingly rare.
> Silk gauze as waist-cloth;
> Garment of deep red color,
> Adorning the body of a hundred merits.
> Circular halo like blazing fire;
> Jeweled lotus forms his seat,
> Dwelling within the moon-disk palace."

"Sixth, Akṣayamati Bodhisattva:

> "Wondrous crown adorning five tufts of hair,
> Dark-blue hair falling to both shoulders;
> Body of reddish flesh-color.
> Left concentration-hand holds a lotus garland;
> Right wisdom-fist pressed upon the knee.
> Full lotus, right over left;
> Countenance of great compassion and great mercy.
> Jeweled garlands and celestial robes,
> Kāṣāya and wondrous necklaces,
> Bangles and bracelets as arm ornaments.
> Hundred-merit cloth as waist-cloth;
> Wondrous garment of vermilion-light color.
> Circular halo encircling the body;
> Jeweled lotus forms his seat,
> Dwelling within the moon disk."

"Seventh, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva:

> "Jeweled crown adorning the head,
> Within it appears Amitāyus;
> Dark-blue hair falling to both shoulders.
> Ten fingers in the gesture of an open lotus.
> Bangles and bracelets as arm ornaments,
> Fine silk as waist-cloth;
> Lower garment of deep red color,
> Adorning the body of compassion.
> Circular halo pervading the world;
> Jeweled flowers supporting both feet,
> Dwelling within the moon-disk palace."

"Eighth, Samantabhadra Bodhisattva:

> "Wondrous crown adorning the topknot,
> Dark-blue hair falling beside the ears;
> Body of light purple color.
> Left concentration-hand: a sword upon a lotus;
> Right wisdom-fist pressed upon the knee;
> Countenance of great compassion and tender mercy.
> Pearl garlands and kāṣāya,
> Celestial robes and wondrous necklaces,
> Bangles and clasps as arm ornaments.
> Thousand-colored cloth as waist-cloth;
> Wondrous garment of reddish-light color.
> Body's radiance like the colors of a rainbow;
> Jeweled lotus forms his seat,
> Dwelling within the moon-disk ocean."

"Having explained the forms and marks of the eight great bodhisattvas, I now explain the images of the four great śrāvakas. They are in the four corners outside the eight petals, within the inner court.

"First, Mahākāśyapa; second, in the southeast, Subhūti; third, in the southwest, Śāriputra; fourth, in the northwest, Mahāmaudgalyāyana.

"Having explained the forms and marks of the four great śrāvakas, I now explain the dignified forms and colors of the sixteen bodhisattvas in the second court.

"At the east gate of the second court, Vajra-Chain Bodhisattva:

> "Upon the crown, a wondrous jeweled diadem;
> Body of white flesh-color.
> Left concentration-hand in fist mudrā;
> Right wisdom-hand holds the vajra chain.
> Body adorned with garlands and necklaces;
> Full lotus, right over left."

"At the south gate, Vajra-Bell Bodhisattva:

> "Upon the crown, a wondrous jeweled diadem;
> Body of deep blue color.
> Left concentration-hand in fist mudrā;
> Right wisdom-hand holds the vajra bell."

"At the west gate, Vajra-Hook Bodhisattva:

> "Upon the crown, a wondrous jeweled diadem;
> Body of deep black color.
> Left concentration-hand in fist mudrā;
> Right wisdom-hand holds the vajra hook."

"At the north gate, Vajra-Lasso Bodhisattva:

> "Upon the crown, a wondrous jeweled diadem;
> Body of white-yellow color.
> Left concentration-hand holds the vajra lasso;
> Right wisdom-fist pressed upon the knee."

"In the northeast corner, Flower-Offering Bodhisattva:

> "Upon the crown, a wondrous jeweled diadem;
> Body of white-yellow color.
> Empty wisdom-hand holds fresh flowers;
> Full lotus, right over left."

"In the southwest, Perfume-Offering Bodhisattva:

> "Upon the crown, a wondrous jeweled diadem;
> Body of deep blue color.
> Concentration and wisdom hands hold perfume vessels;
> Body and adornments as described above."

"In the southeast, Lamp-Offering Bodhisattva:

> "Upon the crown, a wondrous jeweled diadem;
> Body of deep black color.
> Concentration and wisdom hands hold incense vessels;
> Body adorned as Maitreya,
> And as described above."

"In the northwest, Incense-Offering Bodhisattva:

> "Upon the crown, a wondrous jeweled diadem;
> Body of deep black color.
> Concentration and wisdom hands hold incense vessels;
> And as described above."

"North of the east gate, Mahāsthāmaprāpta Bodhisattva:

> "Upon the crown, a five-tuft diadem,
> With a vajra bell within the crown;
> Left concentration-hand holds a white lotus,
> Flourishing but not yet opened,
> Surrounded by a circular halo.
> Right wisdom-hand in the preaching mudrā;
> Body and adornments as Samantabhadra."

"To the south, Ratnapāṇi Bodhisattva:

> "Upon the crown, a five-tuft diadem;
> Body of white flesh-color.
> Left concentration-hand in the preaching mudrā;
> Right wisdom-hand holds a great lotus
> With a vajra upon it,
> And a jewel with a garland of flames."

"East of the south gate, Ratnadhvaja Bodhisattva:

> "Upon the crown, a five-topknot diadem;
> Body of white flesh-color.
> Left concentration-hand holds a great lotus
> With banner-pennons upon it;
> Right wisdom-hand holds a jewel
> With a garland of flames encircling it.
> Hundreds of thousands of necklaces;
> Body and adornments as Samantabhadra."

"To the west, Nakṣatra-rāja Bodhisattva:

> "Upon the crown, a five-topknot diadem;
> Body of reddish-yellow color.
> Left concentration-fist pressed upon the knee;
> Right wisdom-hand: a bowl upon a lotus."

"South of the west gate, Ratnacandra Bodhisattva:

> "Upon the crown, a five-topknot diadem;
> Body of luxuriant yellow color.
> Left concentration-hand holds a vajra;
> Right wisdom-fist pressed upon the knee."

"To the north, Pūrṇacandra Bodhisattva:

> "Upon the crown, a five-topknot diadem;
> Body of deep yellow color.
> Left hand: a blue-cloud moon above;
> Right wisdom-hand in the fearlessness mudrā."

"East of the north gate, Sarvākāra-saṃpad Bodhisattva:

> "Upon the crown, a five-topknot diadem;
> Body of golden color.
> Left concentration-hand holds a golden wheel;
> Right wisdom-hand holds a lotus."

"To the west, Pradānaśūra Bodhisattva:

> "Upon the crown, a five-topknot diadem;
> Body of white flesh-color.
> Left concentration-hand: a sūtra upon a lotus;
> Right wisdom-fist pressed upon the knee."

"The above sixteen bodhisattvas are adorned as Maitreya."

"Having explained the dignified adornments of each of the sixteen great bodhisattvas, I now explain the rites of the third court.

"At the east gate of the third court, Dhṛtarāṣṭra, Heavenly King:

> "Jeweled crown adorning the topknot;
> Face of blue flesh-color.
> Left concentration-hand holds a sharp spear;
> Body adorned with scattered necklaces.
> Kāṣāya and celestial robes,
> Vajra jeweled armor covering all.
> Right wisdom-hand at the waist;
> Countenance of wrathful authority.
> Full lotus, right over left;
> Lotus leaves as adornment."

"At the south gate, Virūḍhaka, Heavenly King:

> "Crown adorning the topknot;
> Face of red flesh-color.
> Left concentration-fist pressed upon the knee;
> Right wisdom-hand holds a vajra sword."

"At the west gate, Virūpākṣa, Heavenly King:

> "Jeweled crown adorning the topknot;
> Face of white-yellow color.
> Left concentration-hand holds a jeweled stūpa;
> Right wisdom-hand holds a jeweled sword."

"The above four great heavenly kings are adorned as Dhṛtarāṣṭra.

"South of the east gate, Śakra Devānām Indra:

> "Jeweled crown adorning the head,
> Bearing a three-pronged vajra;
> Face of white-yellow color.
> Left concentration-hand in fist mudrā;
> Right wisdom-hand holds a single-pronged vajra.
> Hundreds of thousands of necklaces,
> Kāṣāya and celestial flowing robes;
> Dwelling in concentration upon the wondrous high seat."

"To the north, Great Brahmā, Heavenly King:

> "Four-faced crown with topknots;
> Face of white flesh-color.
> Each face with three eyes.
> Four hands: the wisdom-hand holds flowers;
> The next wisdom-hand holds a rosary;
> The upper concentration-hand holds a vajra bell;
> The lower concentration-hand sideways,
> Bending the index finger with the rest upright.
> Body adorned with wondrous necklaces,
> Kāṣāya and celestial robes;
> Circular halo encircling the jeweled body.
> His realm holds the seven-wheeled chariot."

"East of the south gate, Maheśvara:

> "Upon the crown, a wondrous celestial diadem;
> Face like a purple lotus.
> Concentration and wisdom hands grasp a sharp trident;
> Mounted upon a great black bull.
> Supreme jeweled necklaces,
> Celestial robes and flowing garments,
> Adorning the body in wondrous color;
> Dwelling within the moon-disk ocean."

"To the west, Nanda, Nāga King:

> "Upon the head, a nine-headed nāga;
> Body of white flesh-color.
> Left concentration-hand in fist mudrā;
> Right wisdom-hand holds a lasso.
> Countenance of wrathful subjugation;
> Lotus forms his seat."

"South of the west gate, Wonderful Dharma, Kiṃnara King:

> "Face of deer-head and horse-head;
> Body of red flesh-color.
> Holding musical instruments;
> Body in unclothed form."

"To the north, Music-Sound, Gandharva King:

> "Upon the crown, a horn-diadem;
> Body of red flesh-color.
> Body like a great bull-king.
> Left concentration-hand holds a flute;
> Right wisdom-hand holds a jeweled sword;
> Bearing great power and might."

"East of the north gate, As-You-Wish, Garuḍa King:

> "Upon the head, a wish-fulfilling jewel;
> Bird-headed with beak and two wings.
> Body of blue-black color;
> Mouth blowing a conch-shell flute."

"To the west, Rāhula, Asura King:

> "Upon the head, a topknot-crown;
> Body of blue-black color.
> Right hand in the asura-wisdom mudrā;
> Face with wrathful countenance.
> Left concentration-hand holds a vajra water-vessel;
> The wind-wheel wound upon the space-wheel above."

"In the northeast, Ucchuṣma:

> "Topknot sweeping the ground;
> Body of deep blue color.
> Vajra jeweled necklaces;
> Exceedingly wrathful countenance.
> Left concentration-hand holds a lasso;
> Right inner hand holds a jeweled rosary.
> Right wisdom-hand holds a trident;
> Right knowledge-hand in the wish-granting mudrā,
> The wisdom and direction fingers bent,
> The knowledge and power fingers straight and outstretched.
> Clad in animal skin.
> On the right, two red serpents
> Coil and hang down over the chest,
> Facing the principal deity.
> Also four arms; two upper arms are white
> With one serpent coiled around them,
> Its color very blue-white;
> Dwelling upon a lotus in a jeweled pool."

"In the southeast, Sacred Kuṇḍalī:

> "Topknot with skull-crown;
> Color of thunder, lightning, and dark clouds.
> Three eyes, terrifying countenance;
> Eight arms grasping implements.
> Left concentration-hand holds a golden wheel;
> Left inner hand holds a battle-axe;
> Left concentration-hand holds a vajra hook.
> Right wisdom-hand holds a trident-sword;
> Right knowledge-fist pressed at the side,
> First forming the vajra fist
> Then raising the precept-wind-wheel straight
> Beneath the right side.
> Right wisdom-hand in the fearlessness mudrā;
> Concentration-wisdom in the great-vow mudrā:
> First the right wisdom-wrist
> Presses upon the left concentration-wrist,
> Each forming a vajra fist
> With concentration-wisdom precept-wind-wheels
> Each raised straight before the chest.
> Vajra jeweled necklaces.
> On the eight left arms, eight serpents
> Coil around the eight arms as ornaments;
> Two serpents coil around both upper arms;
> Two serpents coil around both ears.
> Clad in the skin of the king of beasts;
> White lotuses support both feet;
> Wrathful ears with flame-garlands."

"In the southwest, Sacred Acala:

> "Upon the topknot, an eight-petaled lotus;
> Topknot hanging down over the left shoulder.
> One eye gazing intently;
> Face with water-wave markings.
> Greatly wrathful with white fangs;
> Left concentration-hand holds a lasso.
> Vajra jeweled necklaces,
> Adorned with every kind of treasure.
> Wrathful body with fire-garland;
> Dwelling upon the seven-jeweled throne."

"In the northwest, Sacred Trailokyavijaya:

> "Great topknot with skull-crown;
> Color of summer rain-clouds.
> Three faces, each with three eyes;
> Laughing 'ha-ṭa-ṭa' with a slight smile,
> Exceedingly great wrathful countenance.
> Left concentration-hand holds a battle-axe;
> Left inner hand grasps a jeweled bow;
> Left concentration-hand holds a vajra lasso.
> Right wisdom-hand holds a vajra;
> Right knowledge-hand holds jeweled arrows;
> Inner-knowledge in the world-saving mudrā:
> First the left concentration-wrist
> Presses upon the right wisdom-wrist,
> With the right thumb-earth-wheel
> Hooking the left thumb-earth-wheel
> Like suspended interlocking hooks.
> The left concentration index-water-wheel
> And middle-fire-wheel
> Bend into the palm,
> While the ring-wind-wheel
> And the little-wisdom-wheel stand straight,
> Each touching at the tips.
> The arms like a bird's two beaks;
> The right wisdom-hand likewise.
> Vajra jeweled necklaces
> Adorning the supreme wondrous body.
> Body's radiance like thunder and lightning;
> Flame-garlands pervading everywhere.
> Maheśvara and his consort,
> Both their bodies forming the seat."

"Having explained the directional positions and dignified forms and colors of all sacred beings in the three-layered square altar, I now describe the altar's coloring: the inner court's ground is of indigo lapis lazuli color; the second court's ground is of red pearl color; the third court's ground is of pale red color. From the inner court to the second court, the ground is of vaiḍūrya, with five-pronged vajra patterns. From the second court to the third gate, the ground is of purple-golden lotus gold, with three-pronged vajra patterns. From the third court to the lotus gates, the ground is of reddish-yellow gold, with single-pronged vajra patterns.

"The four directional gates are lotus gates. At the four corners, khadira-wood vajra pegs, which are crossed vajras. Also called gandha-wood pegs. Blue lotuses encircle all four sides and four corners."

When the Bhagavān had finished speaking this sūtra, the wish-fulfilling assembly of the equality great gathering's one-vehicle maṇḍala, all the bodhisattvas, Vajrapāṇi and the rest, having heard what the Buddha had spoken, were greatly joyful in their hearts and faithfully received and practiced it.
