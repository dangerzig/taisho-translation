# 十不二門
## The Ten Gates of Non-Duality

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1927

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, composed by Zhanran (湛然).

---

Now, in the trace gate, we discuss cause and fruit, as well as self and other, in order to make all the teachings of the entire lifetime interpenetrate and enter into the wondrous. Therefore, in all doctrinal commentaries, we rely on the four teachings and the five flavors, with the intent of opening the teachings so that all enter ghee. Contemplation of the mind is the pivot of teaching and practice, yet for the present we merely touch upon it briefly, entrusting it to the various expositions, where it may appear or not appear, since it is not the main intent of the work. Even if there are provisional arrangements, entrusting matters to Dharma principles or elucidating the ten contemplations and listing their names, what is elucidated is principle as object, wisdom, practice, stage, Dharma, the transforming and the transformed: the intent lies in the capacity to express, and within the expression all is wondrous. In order to elucidate the beginning and end, self and other within the expression, the ten wondrous teachings are fully expounded, thoroughly encompassing the great meaning of the single lifetime of teaching, that of transcending the world, exhaustively and without remainder. Therefore, one cannot fail to understand the great outline of the ten wondrous teachings. Hence, we gather the ten wondrous teachings as the great body of the contemplation method. If one understands the wondrous of the trace gate, the wondrous of the origin gate is not far away. One should know that it is merely a difference of separation and combination. The meaning of cause and fruit is one; how could self and other differ? Therefore, the text below says: "Though the origin and trace differ, the inconceivable is one." How much more so when essence, tenet, and function are merely the Dharma of self and other, cause and fruit! And how much more when the classification of the teaching is merely the analysis of the preceding four sections, making those four sections permanently distinct from all other texts. If one understands this purport, then the teaching has a destination. The vertical and horizontal of one lifetime do not go beyond one mind. The three thousand worlds are precisely emptiness, the provisional, and the middle. From the principle-object all the way to benefit, it is the same. Then the ten vehicles of śamatha-vipaśyanā constitute the cause and fruit of one's own practice in the present; the single section on initiating the teaching constitutes the present capacity and object of transforming others. Then both sides are clearly manifest, the practice of the *Lotus Sūtra* is accomplished, effort is not wasted, and what is expressed can be recognized. Therefore, we further use ten gates to gather and subsume the ten wondrous teachings. How so? To bestow the provisional for the sake of the real is to make the non-dual into the dual. To open the provisional and reveal the real is to make the dual into the non-dual. Since the Dharma and the teaching-division are both opened to become wondrous, these ten gates take "non-duality" as their heading. Under each gate, one examines with the six identities. The origin gate has already broadly cited authentic testimony. Below, we merely set forth the single principle directly, making the purport of the entire sūtra crystal-clear before our eyes.

The ten gates are: first, the gate of the non-duality of form and mind; second, the gate of the non-duality of inner and outer; third, the gate of the non-duality of cultivation and nature; fourth, the gate of the non-duality of cause and fruit; fifth, the gate of the non-duality of defilement and purity; sixth, the gate of the non-duality of the dependent and the direct; seventh, the gate of the non-duality of self and other; eighth, the gate of the non-duality of the three kinds of karma; ninth, the gate of the non-duality of the provisional and the real; tenth, the gate of the non-duality of receiving moisture. Among these, the first takes its name from the wondrous object; the second and third take their names from wisdom and practice; the fourth takes its name from stage and Dharma; the fifth, sixth, and seventh take their names from sympathetic response and supernatural powers; the eighth and ninth take their names from teaching the Dharma; the tenth takes its name from retinue and benefit.

---

**First, the gate of the non-duality of form and mind.** The ten-suchness object, up to and including the absence of truth: each and every one can be taken in both a comprehensive and a differentiated sense. Comprehensively, all are in one thought-moment; differentiated, they are divided into form and mind. How so? Among the initial ten suchnesses: characteristics pertain only to form; nature pertains only to mind; essence, power, activity, and conditions partake of both form and mind; cause and fruit pertain only to mind; retribution pertains only to form. Among the twelve links of dependent origination, suffering and karma partake of both, while delusion pertains only to mind. Among the four truths, three partake of both form and mind, while cessation pertains only to mind. In the two truths and the three truths, the conventional has both form and mind, while the ultimate and the middle pertain only to mind. The single reality and the absence of truth can be understood by analogy. Having understood the differentiated, we gather the differentiated into the comprehensive. All dharmas are nothing but the mind-nature. One nature is without nature, and the three thousand are resplendent. One should know that the form and mind of the mind are precisely what is called "transformation" by the name of mind. Transformation is called "creation"; creation refers to essence and function. Thus, it is neither form nor mind yet is form and mind; it is solely form and solely mind precisely because of this. Therefore, know that by recognizing a single thought-moment, one universally sees self and other, sentient beings and buddhas. Other beings and other buddhas are still identical with the mind; how much less could one's own mind-born beings and buddhas diverge from a single thought? Therefore, the various objects and dharmas are differentiated yet not differentiated.

**Second, the gate of the non-duality of inner and outer.** Whatever object is contemplated does not go beyond inner and outer. "Outer" means relying on the dependent and direct retribution, form and mind, which are precisely emptiness, the provisional, and the middle. Being precisely emptiness, the provisional, and the middle is wondrous; therefore the essence of form and mind is cut off. Only the single true nature: without emptiness, the provisional, or the middle, form and mind are resplendent, broadly identical with true purity. There are no longer the seven expedient differences among sentient beings. One does not see distinctions of pure and defiled among lands. Yet the Indra's-net of dependent and direct retributions is brilliantly clear of itself. "Inner" means: first comprehending that external form and mind are one thought of no-thought. Only the inner essence: the three thousand are precisely emptiness, the provisional, and the middle. Thus, external dharmas are entirely the mind-nature. The mind-nature has no outside; its encompassing is all-inclusive. The buddhas of the ten directions and all sentient beings of the dharma-realm: their nature-essence is without difference, and all is pervaded. Who speaks of inner and outer, form and mind, self and other? This is accomplished using the preceding gate of the non-duality of form and mind.

**Third, the gate of the non-duality of cultivation and nature.** The virtue of nature is precisely the realm-suchness of one thought-moment. This inner realm-suchness is complete with three dharmas. Although the nature is originally so, it depends on wisdom to initiate cultivation. Through cultivation one illuminates the nature; through the nature one activates cultivation. Preserving the nature, then the entirety of cultivation becomes the nature. Initiating cultivation, then the entirety of the nature becomes cultivation. The nature does not shift; cultivation is always resplendent. Cultivation is further of two kinds: concordant cultivation and contrary cultivation. Concordant means understanding the nature as practice. Contrary means turning against the nature and becoming deluded. The two minds of delusion and understanding: though the mind is not dual, the two natures of the contrary and the concordant, in terms of phenomena, are always distinct. It is possible that through phenomena, without shifting the mind, one can cause deluded cultivation to become understanding. Therefore, one must, throughout one lifetime of delusion and understanding, illuminate the nature and accomplish cultivation, see the nature and cultivate the mind, so that both minds are simultaneously extinguished. Furthermore, understanding concordant cultivation, compared to the nature, has separation and combination. Separation means that cultivation and nature each have three. Combination means that cultivation has two and nature has one. The two of cultivation, each having three, jointly manifest the three of the nature. Thus, although cultivation is fully equipped with nine, the nine are precisely three. Because cultivation is elucidated in contrast with nature, we combine cultivation as two; the two and the one nature are like water becoming waves. The two are also not two, like waves and water. One should know that the nature refers to the three obstructions; hence it is equipped with three. Cultivation is accomplished from the nature, accomplishing three dharmas as such. Penetrating to where there is no cultivation and no nature, only the single wondrous vehicle: without any discrimination, the dharma-realm is luminously clear. This is accomplished using the preceding gate of the non-duality of inner and outer.

**Fourth, the gate of the non-duality of cause and fruit.** The cause-mind of sentient beings is already equipped with the three tracks. When this cause accomplishes the fruit, it is called the three nirvāṇas. Cause and fruit are without difference; beginning and end are one in principle. If so, since the virtue of the cause is already complete, why not abide in the cause? It is merely because one is deluded about the cause and each regards it as real. If one understands the reality of the nature of delusion, one merely abides in the cause. Through long investigation of this cause, when the cause is manifest, it is called the fruit. Precisely because cause and fruit are one in principle, one uses this single principle as the cause; when the principle is manifest, there is no longer the name "fruit." How could one still retain the label "cause"? When cause and fruit are both extinguished, the principle-nature itself vanishes. It is only because the extinction of wisdom varies in proximity and distance that delusion varies in thickness and thinness. Because delusion is thick or thin, we forcibly distinguish three kinds of confusion, and the meaning opens up the six identities, naming the shallowness and depth of wisdom. Therefore, like striving diligently in a dream, the empty name of delusion is extinguished. The illusory cause being fulfilled, the mirror-image fruit is perfected. Although the empty and the image are immediately the same in meaning, yet the empty is unreal and the image is real. Because the image is real, it is called "the principle is originally inherent." Because the empty is unreal, delusion turns and becomes the nature. Thus, the non-dual becomes dual: cause and fruit are established as distinct. The dual becomes non-dual: beginning and end are one in essence. If one says the cause differs from the fruit, then the cause is also not a cause. Understanding that the fruit comes from the cause, the cause can then achieve the fruit. Therefore, the three thousand, at the level of principle, are collectively called ignorance; the three thousand, when the fruit is accomplished, are collectively called permanence and bliss. The three thousand are without change: ignorance is precisely illumination. The three thousand are all permanent, with both essence and function complete. This is accomplished using the preceding gate of the non-duality of cultivation and nature.

**Fifth, the gate of the non-duality of defilement and purity.** If one recognizes the beginningless, then the dharma-nature has become ignorance. Therefore one can understand that now, ignorance is precisely the dharma-nature. The dharma-nature together with ignorance universally creates all dharmas: this is called defilement. Ignorance together with the dharma-nature universally responds to all conditions: this is called purity. Turbid water and clear water: as waves, the moisture is the same. Although clarity and turbidity are due to conditions, the turbid is originally inherent. Although the turbid is originally inherent, its entire essence is clear. Because the principle of the two waves interpenetrates, raising the essence is precisely function. Therefore, the three thousand of cause and fruit are all called dependent origination. Deluded and awakened dependent origination does not depart from the single moment. The nature of the single moment is permanent; the principle of dependent origination is one. Within one principle, purity and defilement are distinguished. Separately, there are six defiled and four pure. Comprehensively, all ten pervade both purity and defilement. Therefore, know that the defiled essence of the single moment is entirely pure. The three thousand not yet manifest verifies that the essence is still deluded. Therefore, at the stage of resemblance, the six faculties universally illuminate. The illumination extends to the ten realms, each vividly clear. How could a person of pure six faculties regard ten as definitely ten? When partial truth manifests traces, the ten realms are likewise. All the way to the accomplishment of the fruit, one equals the hundred realms. Therefore, from the very first mind, one must both block and illuminate. Because of illumination, the three thousand are always complete. Because of blocking, dharmas are naturally empty and middle. Throughout the entire day, both are extinguished; throughout the entire day, both illuminate. Without stirring from this thought, one universally responds in every direction. According to the stimulus, one acts: purity and defilement are both extinguished. Because purity and defilement are extinguished, through emptiness and the middle, one further transforms defilement into purity through emptiness and the middle. Because one understands that defilement and purity, emptiness and the middle, are themselves extinguished. This is accomplished using the preceding gate of the non-duality of cause and fruit.

**Sixth, the gate of the non-duality of the dependent and the direct.** Having realized that Vairocana is one essence, not dual, it is precisely because from the beginningless, one thought-moment contains three thousand. Among the three thousand, the two thousand of sentient beings and their aggregates constitute the direct retribution; the one thousand of lands constitutes the dependent retribution. Since the dependent and the direct both reside in one mind, how can one mind be divided into subject and object? Although there is no subject and object, the dependent and the direct are resplendent. Thus, at the stages of principle-nature, naming, and contemplative practice, there is already the characteristic of non-duality of the dependent and the direct. Therefore, the cause and fruit of self and other are mutually encompassing. But sentient beings, at the level of principle, though the fruit is not yet achieved, everything is nothing other than the wondrous object of Vairocana. Yet one should further understand: the Dharma-essence of all buddhas is not pervading yet pervades; the principle-nature of sentient beings is not confined yet is confined. Beginning and end do not change; the great and the small do not obstruct. The principle of cause and fruit is the same; how could the dependent and the direct differ? Therefore, the pure and impure lands, the superior and inferior bodies: the dust-body and the Dharma-body are equal in measure; the dust-land and the Land of Eternally Quiescent Light are without difference. Thus, each and every dust-mote land is all lands; each and every dust-mote body is all bodies. Broad and narrow, superior and inferior, are inconceivable. Pure and defiled, directions and places, are without end. Were it not for the three thousand being precisely emptiness, the provisional, and the middle, how could such sovereign function be accomplished? Only thus does one know that sentient beings and buddhas are equal: the phenomena and principles of self and other mutually encompass each other. This is accomplished using the preceding gate of the non-duality of defilement and purity.

**Seventh, the gate of the non-duality of self and other.** Benefiting others according to their capacities relies in its workings on the origin. "Origin" means the single nature completely equipped with self and other. Upon reaching the stage of the fruit, the self precisely benefits others. Like the three virtues, three truths, and three thousand of the principle-nature: one's own practice resides solely in emptiness and the middle, while benefiting others means the three thousand respond to beings. The capacities of beings are immeasurable, yet do not go beyond three thousand. The responses, though many, do not go beyond the ten realms. Realm after realm is mutually manifested, yet does not go beyond one thought. Land after land is mutually produced, yet does not go beyond the Eternally Quiescent Light. Because sentient beings are equipped in principle with three thousand, they can receive the stimulus. Because all buddhas have fulfilled the principle of three thousand, they can respond. The response pervades and the stimulus pervades; the joyful going-to-meet is without discrepancy. Were it not so, how could it be like a mirror reflecting images? The mirror has the principle of reflecting images; the form has the nature of generating an image. If one form is presented yet cannot produce an image, then the mirror's principle has a limit, and the form's function does not pass through. If one is separated from the mirror, then such reasoning may be possible; but there has never been a form presented that does not produce an image. If the mirror has not yet reflected an image, it is because dust obscures it. Removing the dust depends on a person's polishing; the image that appears has nothing to do with the polisher. Understanding the Dharma through this analogy, the general purport can be known. One should know that although in principle self and other are fully equipped, one must rely on the conditioning and illuminating functions for the work of benefiting others. Furthermore, because the conditioning and illuminating functions unite with the nature, one can then act in accord with the nature, provisionally establishing ten thousand expedients, and without departing from the self-nature, transform beings in every direction. This is accomplished using the preceding gate of the non-duality of the dependent and the direct.

**Eighth, the gate of the non-duality of the three kinds of karma.** In the gate of transforming others, the workings are divided into the three secret activities. Following the principle of beings, the names differ accordingly. The mind-wheel discerns capacities; the two wheels of body and speech carry out the transformation. Manifesting the body and teaching the Dharma are never off by a hair's breadth. With respect to the body, they are divided into the true and the response bodies. With respect to the Dharma, they are divided into the provisional and the real. If the two bodies were different, why would it say "precisely this is the Dharma-body"? If the two teachings diverged, why would it say "all accomplish the buddha-path"? If it were solely the Dharma-body, there should be no descent into the world. If it were solely the buddha-path, who would bestow the three vehicles? The body is still without body; speech is necessarily not speech. Body and speech are equal, equal to the mind-wheel. Mind and form are one suchness, transforming without deliberation. Constantly in silent accord with the ultimate, acting in accord with beings: is this not the one mind of the hundred realms? Realm after realm is nothing other than the three kinds of karma. Since the realms are a single thought, how could the three kinds of karma differ? The function of the fruit is without deficiency; the cause must correspond to the fruit. If one has faith in cause and fruit, only then does one know that the three secret activities have their origin. The three kinds of karma of the hundred realms are all emptiness, the provisional, and the middle. Therefore, responding appropriately and universally meeting beings constitutes the fruit. Each and every response-form, each and every speech-sound, is without exception fully equipped with the three kinds of karma of the hundred realms. Transformation further producing transformation: this is what is meant. Therefore, in a single thought of the ordinary mind, there already exists the ocean of marks of the three secret activities of the principle-nature. A single mote of retribution-form is simultaneously in the original principle of Vairocana. Only then is this called "the three without difference." This is accomplished using the preceding gate of the non-duality of self and other.

**Ninth, the gate of the non-duality of the provisional and the real.** The great wisdom of equality constantly illuminates the dharma-realm. It is also because in the principle-nature, nine are provisional and one is real. The real further encompasses nine realms; the provisional is likewise. The provisional and the real are silently fused; the hundred realms are one thought, indistinguishable, functioning spontaneously and always so. Upon reaching the fruit, it is through according with the original single principle. Neither provisional nor real, yet provisional and real: this is precisely the sovereign mind-wheel described before, causing body and speech to respond to the capacities of the provisional and the real. The three kinds of karma, in a single thought, do not diverge from the provisional and the real. Acting without stirring: how could there be separation or difference? In relation to teaching, names are established through the provisional and the real. In relation to the body, names are given as the true and the response. The principle of the three kinds of karma is the same; the provisional and the real are silently fused. This is accomplished using the preceding gate of the non-duality of the three kinds of karma.

**Tenth, the gate of the non-duality of receiving moisture.** The principle of beings is originally equipped in its nature with the provisional and the real. From beginningless habituation, some are habituated toward the provisional and some toward the real. Though the provisional and the real come from habituation, the principle is always equal. Encountering the time, habituation is accomplished and practice and vows are supported. Without the original cause, habituation would be set up in vain. Encountering habituation, the differences naturally arise, not because the nature is different. Although the nature is without difference, it must rely on the illusory to be activated. The illusory capacity, the illusory stimulus, the illusory response, the illusory meeting: the capacity to respond and what is transformed are both neither provisional nor real. Yet because sentient beings are equipped with the neither-provisional-nor-real and become the capacity for the provisional and the real, and the Buddha is also equipped in the fruit with the neither-provisional-nor-real and becomes the response of the provisional and the real, when the being's capacity and the Buddha's response meet, body and land are without partiality, identical with the Eternally Quiescent Light, nothing other than the dharma-realm. Therefore, know that the three thousand are simultaneously in the mind-ground, not different from the three thousand in the Buddha's mind-ground. The four subtle elements share the same essence; the benefit of the provisional and the real is equal. This is accomplished using the preceding gate of the non-duality of the provisional and the real.

---

Therefore, these ten gates interpenetrate gate after gate: from form and mind all the way to receiving moisture, it is all so. Hence, the ten wondrous teachings are one in principle from beginning to end. Like the object that is originally equipped with three, based on the principle one generates understanding, hence it is called wisdom. Wisdom-understanding guides practice; practice-understanding accords with principle. The three dharmas correspond to each other: not different yet different. Provisionally establishing shallow and deep, setting up stages to prevent confusion, the three dharmas are precisely the realization of those three principles. The lower five sections are the three dharmas giving rise to function. Since it is the three thousand of one thought, precisely emptiness, the provisional, and the middle, being accomplished, there is function. If one understands one thought, then the dharmas of the buddhas of the ten directions and the three times, origin and trace, are not far away. Therefore, the ten gates are restated, so that the contemplative practice can be recognized. Since the main title is as described, comprehending the particular to form the general, one can know by matching it against the text.
