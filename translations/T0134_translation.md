# 佛說長者子六過出家經
## Sūtra on the Householder's Son Who Went Forth Six Times, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0134

Translated from the Chinese. Liu Song dynasty, translated by Huijian (慧簡)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Bhagavān was in the city of Śrāvastī, in the Jetavana, the garden of Anāthapiṇḍada. At that time, Saṃgharāma, the householder's son, had gone forth six times in order to practice the path. He went to where the World-Honored One was, bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, and stood to one side. Saṃgharāma, the householder's son, then said to the World-Honored One: "I beseech the World-Honored One to allow me to go forth and study the path." Saṃgharāma was permitted to go forth and study the path.

The World-Honored One then addressed the bhikṣu Saṃgharāma: "You should practice two dharmas. What are the two? Calming and insight."

The bhikṣu Saṃgharāma said to the Buddha: "I understand deeply, World-Honored One! I understand deeply, Tathāgata!"

The World-Honored One said: "I spoke concisely. How can you say you understand deeply?"

Saṃgharāma said to the Buddha: "Calming means that all fetters are permanently stilled. Insight, World-Honored One, means insight into all dharmas."

The World-Honored One said: "Excellent! Excellent! Saṃgharāma! I spoke the Dharma concisely, and you are able to analyze it broadly." Then Saṃgharāma received this teaching from the Buddha. In a secluded place he contemplated the meaning. Having contemplated the meaning in a secluded place, that son of good family shaved off his hair and beard, put on the three Dharma robes, and with firm faith went forth from the household life to study the path. He cultivated the unsurpassed holy life, exhausted the source of birth and death. The holy life was established, what was to be done had been done, and he would not again be received into a mother's womb. The Venerable Saṃgharāma attained the fruit of arhatship.

Now, before Saṃgharāma went forth, he had a wife, and her mother was still alive. The mother-in-law heard that her daughter's husband had gone forth: the householder's son Saṃgharāma had gone to the Tathāgata's place as many as seven times to go forth and study the path, no longer practicing desire, and had abandoned his household property and attendants. Saṃgharāma's mother-in-law then brought her daughter to where the bhikṣu Saṃgharāma was. She stood before him in silence, sighed repeatedly, and then spoke: "You, Saṃgharāma! Without propriety or courtesy, without proper reflection, you have abandoned my daughter and gone forth at the Tathāgata's place to study the path! Among all the kings, great ministers, householders, brāhmaṇas, and kṣatriyas of the city of Śrāvastī, whoever sees my daughter is completely captivated and beside themselves. They constantly cherish respectful thoughts and wish to live with her. Yet you abandon my daughter without a thought!"

Then the Venerable Saṃgharāma spoke this verse:

> "Beyond this there is nothing more essential; beyond this there is nothing comparable.
> Beyond this there is no other contemplation; careful reflection finds nothing that surpasses this."

At that time, the Venerable Saṃgharāma's mother-in-law said to Saṃgharāma: "What fault or offense does my daughter have that you should discard her and go forth at the Tathāgata's place to study the path?"

The Venerable Saṃgharāma then spoke this verse:

> "Foul speech and constant slander, jealousy and a deceitful heart,
> Anger and delight in falsehood: the Tathāgata calls these great evils."

At that time, the wife and mother-in-law of the Venerable Saṃgharāma looked at this bhikṣu from head to foot, and again from foot to head. Then, looking at the Venerable Saṃgharāma's feet, she said: "Venerable one, please forgive our foolishness, for we did not discern the truth."

Saṃgharāma said: "I wish you well, sister. May you be safe and live long."

Then Saṃgharāma's wife said to her mother: "Saṃgharāma calls me 'sister.' He surely no longer has any desire." She then said to Saṃgharāma: "Venerable Saṃgharāma, please forgive our foolishness, for we did not discern the truth."

Saṃgharāma said: "I wish you well, sister. May you be safe and live long."

Then Saṃgharāma's wife and mother-in-law bowed their heads at his feet, circumambulated three times, sighed repeatedly, and departed.

At that time, the Venerable Ānanda, from within the city of Śrāvastī, saw from afar the wife and mother-in-law of the Venerable Saṃgharāma. Seeing them, he said: "Sisters, have you seen Saṃgharāma?"

Saṃgharāma's wife replied: "I saw Saṃgharāma and also spoke with him, but my wish was not fulfilled."

The Venerable Ānanda then walked on in silence. The Venerable Ānanda returned to his own quarters, put away his robes and bowl, washed his hands and feet, placed his sitting cloth over his shoulder, and went to where the World-Honored One was. He bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, sat to one side, and after a moment withdrew from his seat and reported this matter in full to the World-Honored One.

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke this verse:

> "To seek a sprout from a rotten tree, to seek a drop of water in a fire,
> To seek fire in the water, to seek desire in one who is desireless."

At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus: "Among my śrāvaka disciples, the foremost bhikṣu in subduing Māra is the bhikṣu Saṃgharāma."

At that time, the bhikṣus, having heard what the Buddha taught, joyfully practiced in accordance.
