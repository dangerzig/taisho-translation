# 何耶揭唎婆像法
## Method for the Image of Hayagrīva

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1073

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown.

---

Furthermore, take a clean cloth that has not been cut or trimmed. Commission a painter of the highest skill, and do not bargain over his fee. Have him bathe in fragrant water, put on fresh clean garments, and receive the eight precepts, doing so each day. In a clean place, construct a water-altar, four cubits square. After the spell-master has protected his body and sealed the boundary, within the altar burn various incenses and scatter various flowers. When the offerings are complete, within this altar paint the bodhisattva. His body is one buddha-span in height, with four faces in total. The central bodhisattva face should be made supremely beautiful, with a compassionate expression, reddish-white in complexion, with pure dark hair. The face on the left is greatly wrathful and black in color, with canine fangs protruding upward and hair slightly raised in the color of flames. The face on the right has a greatly laughing expression, reddish-white and beautiful like the bodhisattva face, with pure dark hair. On the head of each of the three faces is a celestial crown with earrings. Upon each crown sits a transformation-buddha in full lotus posture. On top of the central face is a blue-green horse head with its mouth closed. Below the bodhisattva's neck, jeweled necklaces are worn, with multiple rings of circular light behind the body in various colors. The left arm is bent with the hand before the chest holding a red lotus. The lotus is level with the bodhisattva's head, directly above the left shoulder. On the lotus platform sits a transformation-buddha wearing a crimson kāṣāya in full lotus posture, with light behind the body. The right hand is palm-up with five fingers extended, the arm bent at a level elbow. The palm supports a cintāmaṇi jewel, round and white, with red flames surrounding it. Directly below the jewel from the right hand, various treasures rain down. On the left shoulder is worn a deer-skin garment, worn like a saṅghāṭī, fastened with a strap under the right arm, with tiger skin wrapping the hips. Arm-bands, celestial garments, skirts, and other adornments all follow the standard method for painting bodhisattvas. The bodhisattva stands upright on a red lotus flower, with a jeweled parasol suspended in the air above the bodhisattva's head. In the sky above, paint various celestial musical instruments, and on both sides in the sky, Tuṣita heaven devas dance in offering.

When painting this image, use fragrant juice; do not use animal-skin glue. Take sappanwood and sea-shell, pound them to an extremely fine powder, and mix with whey, as one would mix parched flour. Empower it with the Hayagrīva heart-spell twenty-one times. Have the sick person take it on an empty stomach. All poisonous parasites within the abdomen will be entirely vomited out.

Another method for painting the image: take a new clay or ceramic vase that has never been used, and dye it blue. On the clay vase, paint an image of Hayagrīva Avalokiteśvara. The image should be one tathāgata-span in height, with four joyful faces. The face on the left is painted black, with green eyes, and canine fangs protruding upward. The face on the right is painted red, called the "spell-eating face." The front central face is a bodhisattva face, made supremely beautiful, painted white. Separated from the crown of the image and suspended in the sky above, paint a blue face with a mouth open, spitting out a jewel. Upon each of the four heads is a jeweled crown with a transformation-buddha seated upon each. The bodhisattva's left hand holds a lotus with the elbow bent upward before the shoulder. The right arm hangs down with all five fingers extended, forming the fearlessness-granting hand. On both wrists are jeweled bracelets. Below the image's neck is a jeweled necklace. The body's adornments follow the standard method for adorning the body of Avalokiteśvara as described elsewhere. The image stands upon a jeweled lotus.

When this image is completed, beginning from the tenth day of the bright half of the month, eat barley and milk porridge. Then for the thirteenth, fourteenth, and fifteenth days, fast for three days and three nights. Take sāgara-vījā incense and gandha-kurī flowers, empower these two substances, and toss one with each recitation toward the front face, up to eight thousand times. From the face's mouth a great radiance will emerge, encircle the spell-master's body, and return into the image's mouth. From within the mouth a wish-fulfilling jewel will emerge. Immediately take and secure the jewel. One shall then gain a lifespan of fourteen thousand years, become a cakravartin king of the seven treasures, and after death be reborn in the Land of Bliss, seated at the feet of Avalokiteśvara.

Another method: one should eat only alms-food and barley-milk porridge, and recite seventy thousand times. At ten thousand times, the vināyakas will be bound. At the second ten thousand, the spell-practice is accomplished. At the third ten thousand, the medicines añjana and manāśilā are accomplished. Fast for three days and three nights while performing the practice. Hold the medicine in one's hand, and while empowering it, if three signs appear in the medicine, one knows the medicine is accomplished. The three signs are: warmth, smoke, and flame. One then accomplishes the antardhāna, ascending into the sky, becoming a spell-wielding ṛṣi with sovereign mastery, able to enter the asura palaces at will. At the fourth ten thousand, one walks four finger-widths above the ground at will. At the fifth ten thousand, one ascends into the sky and travels freely as desired. At the sixth ten thousand, one attains a lifespan of one thousand years. The spell-master becomes an accomplished spell-wielding ṛṣi; whatever one undertakes is effortlessly accomplished, and all whom one summons will come. At the seventh ten thousand, the spell-master's hair transforms into spiral curls in accomplishment. Whatever one needs comes at the thought. One personally attends upon Avalokiteśvara and knows the past, present, and future of all sentient beings, their dying here and being reborn there. This is accomplishment.

Another method: if one wishes to subdue heretics, before the Hayagrīva image, fast for three days and three nights. On the fragrant altar scatter various incenses and flowers, and with pure food and drink, milk-porridge, fruits, and other items make various offerings. Pound sajjara-sa incense, mix it with white honey to make eight thousand pills. Empower one pill with each recitation and cast it into the fire. When all the incense pills are consumed, the heretical assembly will first see a horse head appear, and after a short while it will transform into the head of Hayagrīva. When the spell-master mounts and advances toward the assembly, all the heretics will scatter and vanish.

Another method: first give barley to a cow to eat, then collect the barley from the dung, wash it thoroughly, pound it, and cook it into milk-porridge. From the first day of the bright half of the month, eat this porridge until the thirteenth day, then fast. Before the Hayagrīva image, smear the ground with fragrant paste, scatter various flowers, burn fine incenses, and make offerings of various food and drink. Take kāpitha incense, pound it to a fine powder, mix with water to form pills numbering eight thousand. Take khadira wood as fuel to light the fire, and cast one incense pill into the fire with each recitation. When all eight thousand pills are consumed, the charcoal will transform into suvarṇa gold, weighing hundreds of thousands of measures.

Another method: if one wishes to consume another's spell, take a small amount of one's own blood and mix it with white mustard seed to make eight thousand pills. On the fourteenth day of the dark half, fast for one day, take khadira wood and light a fire in the furnace. Take the mustard seed pills and cast one into the fire with each recitation. When all the pills are consumed, all hostile spells are entirely consumed.

Another method: simply recite the spell while traveling in the wilderness or across sandy wastes, and food and drink will naturally appear.

Another method: at the time of a solar or lunar eclipse, take one measure of pure cow ghee in a new clay bowl. Hold the bowl and gaze up at the sun or moon while empowering the ghee with the spell, until the ghee becomes warm, or smoke appears, or flame emerges. At that moment, drink the ghee. Having drunk it, wherever one wishes to go, one arrives at the mere thought.

If one always recites this spell, all terrors become fearless, and all obstructions and difficulties are entirely liberated.

Another method: if one suffers from rudra-sores, or has been bitten by venomous snakes, or suffers from kuṣṭha sores, empower yellow earth-clay with the spell one thousand times and apply it to the sores. They will immediately be cured. All terrors are eliminated merely by thinking of the spell.

Another method: if a person suddenly encounters any water peril, by recollecting the spell in the mind one will not be drowned or submerged. Such methods as these cannot be described in full; all are accomplished according to one's wish. If one constructs the great maṇḍala, one must not engage in sexual activity.

The healing mudrā of Hayagrīva:

*Oṃ huru-rū kuru dhāna hūṃ phaṭ svāhā*

The alms-seeking mudrā of Hayagrīva:

*Oṃ śi-nā-ya*

The above two mudrās should follow in sequence after the sword-unbinding mudrā. When performing this practice, one should first bind and restrain the vināyakas. Facing the Hayagrīva image, take a piece of wood eight finger-widths long, set it burning until flames emerge, and make eight hundred pills of olibanum incense. Cast one pill into the fire with each recitation. When all the incense is consumed, the vināyakas will be bound of their own accord. The spell says:

*Namo tadyathā juru juru vijuru juru gatāya sā mahā-vāra sarva-vicina vināyaka-gaṃ pāṭa-bha mokucī bhūtāya phaṭ*

If a person experiences nocturnal emissions, take a white thread, empower it with the spell eight hundred times, then make it into a spell-cord, tying one knot per recitation to make twenty-one knots. Fasten it around the waist, and there will be no further losses. The spell says:

*Namo tadyathā buddhi buddhi su-buddhi indra-buddhi māme śukā-ṃ bharā-maucatu svāhā*

Another method: empower a kāṣāya with the spell seven times and tie one knot. This constitutes the body-protecting accomplishment. The spell says:

*Tadyathā hari hari muhā-indra kuhā-indra sarva-duṣṭāṃ jambhāya miyā bagala dhinā muñjāmi svāhā*
