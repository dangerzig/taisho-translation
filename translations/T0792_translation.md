# 佛說法受塵經
## Sūtra Spoken by the Buddha on Receiving the Dust of Dharmas

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0792

Translated from the Chinese. Later Han, translated by An Shigao (安世高)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was sojourning at Śrāvastī, in the Jetavana, the garden of Anāthapiṇḍada.

The Buddha addressed them: "O bhikṣus!" The bhikṣus replied: "We receive your teaching," and they listened to the Buddha.

The Buddha said: "Bhikṣus! Ordinary people, on account of one dharma, receive defilements and defile themselves, become confused and sorrowful, and sink down without beginning or end. I see that they are unable to attain the unsurpassable and auspicious path. Consider the man who desires to behold the form of a woman: a man fond of form thereby becomes stained, becomes intoxicated, becomes greedy, becomes defiled, becomes confused, becomes attached, dwells in it, and receives it. On account of the words of a lustful woman, he runs about for a long time, coming and going, receiving toil and suffering. His ears constantly wish to hear her voice; his nose wishes to smell her fragrance; his tongue wishes to taste her flavor; his body wishes to experience her smooth and supple touch. Therefore he runs about for a long time, coming and going, receiving suffering. For this reason, one should not be stained and confused by a woman's form, sound, fragrance, flavor, and touch. One should be aware of this.

"Furthermore, bhikṣus! Ordinary people, on account of dharmas, receive defilements and defile themselves, become confused and sorrowful, and sink down without limit. I see that they are unable to attain the unsurpassable and auspicious path. Consider the lustful woman who desires to behold the form of a man: a woman fond of form thereby becomes stained, becomes intoxicated, becomes greedy, becomes defiled, becomes confused, becomes attached, dwells in it, and receives it. On account of a man's form, she runs about for a long time, coming and going, receiving suffering. Her ears constantly wish to hear his voice; her nose wishes to smell his fragrance; her tongue wishes to taste his flavor; her body wishes to experience his smooth and supple touch. Therefore she runs about for a long time, coming and going, receiving suffering. For this reason, one should not be stained and confused by a man's form, sound, fragrance, flavor, and touch. One should be aware of this."

When the Buddha had finished speaking, they all rejoiced and accepted it to practice.
