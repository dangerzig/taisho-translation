# 聖八千頌般若波羅蜜多一百八名真實圓義陀羅尼經
## Dhāraṇī Sūtra of the One Hundred and Eight Names of the True and Complete Meaning of the Holy Prajñāpāramitā in Eight Thousand Lines

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0230

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dānapāla (施護) and others

---

> Homage to the supreme mother of all Buddhas,
> The Dharma of prajñāpāramitā.
> Past, future, and present:
> All Buddhas are born from this.
> She who gives good birth to all Buddhas is the mother of Buddhas.
> No-nature, self-nature, I am pure.
> The Buddha broadly taught this to Subhūti;
> As he taught it, I now briefly compile it.

Prajñāpāramitā has one hundred and eight names: (1) Supreme Prajñāpāramitā, (2) All-Knowledge, (3) Knowledge of All Aspects, (4) The Limit of Reality, (5) Suchness, (6) Indestructible Suchness, (7) Undifferentiated Suchness, (8) True Nature, (9) Arising from Reality, (10) Non-Inversion, (11) Emptiness, Signlessness, and Wishlessness, (12) No-Nature, (13) Self-Nature, (14) The Self-Nature of No-Nature, (15) Dharma-Nature, (16) Dharma-Realm, (17) Dharma-Certainty, (18) Dharma-Abiding, (19) Dharma-Selflessness, (20) Dharma-Characteristic, (21) Non-Being, (22) Non-Life, (23) Non-Nourishment, (24) Non-Person, (25) Non-Individual, (26) Non-Speech, (27) Beyond the Path of Speech, (28) Apart from Mind, Mentation, and Consciousness, (29) Without Equal, (30) Equal to the Unequaled, (31) Without Pride, (32) Without Self, (33) Without Elaboration, (34) Apart from Elaboration, (35) Beyond All Elaboration, (36) Mother of All Buddhas, (37) Giving Birth to All Bodhisattvas, (38) Giving Birth to All Śrāvakas and Pratyekabuddhas, (39) Nourishing and Sustaining All Worlds, (40) Inexhaustible Meritorious Practice Complete, (41) Wielding Wisdom, (42) Activating Supernatural Powers, (43) Producing the Pure Divine Eye, (44) Producing the Pure Divine Ear, (45) Producing the Knowledge of Others' Minds, (46) Producing the Knowledge of Past Lives, (47) Producing the Knowledge of the Exhaustion of Defilements, (48) Noble Purity, (49) Auspiciousness, (50) Abiding in the Four Foundations of Mindfulness, (51) Possessing the Four Right Efforts, (52) Wielding the Four Bases of Supernatural Power, (53) Purity of the Faculties, (54) Completeness of the Powers, (55) Adorned with the Seven Factors of Awakening, (56) Showing the Eightfold Noble Path, (57) Bestowing the Seven Noble Treasures, (58) Perfecting the Nine Sequential Absorptions, (59) Possessing the Ten Sovereignties, (60) Abiding on the Ten Grounds, (61) Perfecting the Ten Powers, (62) Adorned with the Ten Totalities, (63) Wielding the Ten Knowledges, (64) Skillfully Taming the Ten Kinds of Supreme Enemies, (65) Giving Birth to All Meditative Absorptions, (66) Transcending the Three Realms, (67) Wonderfully Abiding in All Omniscient Awareness, (68) Possessing the Knowledge of All-Knowledge, (69) Internal Emptiness, (70) External Emptiness, (71) Internal-and-External Emptiness, (72) Emptiness of Emptiness, (73) Great Emptiness, (74) Ultimate Emptiness, (75) Conditioned Emptiness, (76) Unconditioned Emptiness, (77) Absolute Emptiness, (78) Boundless Emptiness, (79) Dispersal Emptiness, (80) Unchanging Emptiness, (81) Shared-Characteristic Emptiness, (82) Own-Characteristic Emptiness, (83) Unobtainable Emptiness, (84) No-Nature Emptiness, (85) Self-Nature Emptiness, (86) The Emptiness of the Self-Nature of No-Nature, (87) Non-Production, (88) Non-Arising, (89) Non-Ceasing, (90) Non-Annihilation, (91) Non-Permanence, (92) Not the Meaning of One, (93) Not the Meaning of Many, (94) Non-Coming, (95) Non-Going, (96) Right Contemplation of Dependent Origination, (97) Beyond Discursive Thought, (98) Without Grasping, (99) Without Possessions, (100) Originally Without Any Activity, (101) Non-Duality, (102) Not Non-Duality, (103) Quiescent Wisdom Without Destination, (104) Unbound, Unstained, Equal to Space, (105) Apart from the Ten Kinds of Verbal Expression, (106) The Self-Nature of All Dharmas Is Like Illusion and Dreams, (107) Like a Potter's Wheel, (108) All Dharmas Are of One Taste.

These are the one hundred and eight names of prajñāpāramitā. Whoever constantly recites them shall extinguish all offenses, and shall be praised by all Buddhas. All bodhisattvas and noble sages shall guard and protect such a person for a long time. The dhāraṇī of the true and complete meaning of prajñāpāramitā is then spoken:

*Tadyathā oṃ prajñe prajñe mahāprajñe yā vaśī prajñe yā rūka kari aniyā na vidhamani siddhi susiddhi siddhāntu me bhagavatī sarva sampatya sarvāṅga sundarī bhakti vātsalī prasartā hā sati maṣa saṣṇa kārti sthiṭa sthiṭa kampa kampa cala cala gava gava garija garija agaccha agaccha bhagavatī mā vilamba soha tihliṃ śiliṃ śrūti smṛti vijaye svāhā*

These secret phrases of the dhāraṇī, if one constantly remembers, upholds, reads, and recites them: the merit obtained is beyond reckoning.
