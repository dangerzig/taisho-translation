# 浴佛功德經
## Sūtra of the Merit of Bathing the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0698

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Yijing (義淨)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Bhagavān was on the summit of Vulture Peak in the city of Rājagṛha, together with a great assembly of twelve hundred and fifty bhikṣus, and innumerable, boundless bodhisattva mahāsattvas, the eight classes of devas and nāgas, all gathered together.

At that time, the Bodhisattva Viśuddhimati was seated in the assembly. Out of compassion for all sentient beings, he thought: "By what cause and condition do the Buddha Tathāgatas obtain a pure body, complete with all marks and features?" He further thought: "Those sentient beings who encounter the Tathāgata, draw near, and make offerings obtain immeasurable and boundless merit. But after the Tathāgata's parinirvāṇa, what offerings should sentient beings make and what merit should they cultivate, so that their roots of merit may swiftly bring them to unsurpassed bodhi?" Having had this thought, he rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, bowed at the Buddha's feet, knelt for a long while with palms joined, and said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I wish to ask a question. May you please grant your permission."

The Buddha said: "Good man! Ask whatever you wish. I shall explain it for you."

At that time the Bodhisattva Viśuddhimati said to the Buddha: "By what cause and condition do the Buddha Tathāgatas, Arhats, Samyaksaṃbuddhas obtain a pure body, complete with all marks and features? Furthermore, those sentient beings who encounter the Tathāgata, draw near, and make offerings obtain immeasurable and boundless merit. But after the Tathāgata's parinirvāṇa, what offerings should sentient beings make and what merit should they cultivate, so that their roots of merit may swiftly bring them to unsurpassed bodhi?"

At that time the World-Honored One told the Bodhisattva Viśuddhimati: "Excellent! Excellent! You are able to ask such a question for the sake of the sentient beings of the future. Listen carefully, reflect well upon it, and practice as taught! I shall explain it for you in detail."

The Bodhisattva Viśuddhimati said: "So be it, World-Honored One! I wish to hear with joy."

The Buddha told the Bodhisattva Viśuddhimati: "Good man! You should know that because generosity, morality, patience, vigor, meditative concentration, wisdom, loving-kindness, compassion, sympathetic joy, equanimity, liberation, the knowledge and vision of liberation, the powers, the fearlessnesses, all buddha-dharmas, and the knowledge of all modes are well-purified, the Tathāgata is pure. If toward such Buddha Tathāgatas one makes various offerings with a pure heart, with fragrant flowers, garlands, banners, canopies, and mats spread before the Buddha, with various adornments, and bathes the honored image with the finest fragrant water, and burns incense to pervade all, directing the mind toward the dharma-realm; and further makes offerings of food, drink, drums, music, stringed instruments, songs, and praises of the Tathāgata's distinctive merits, and generates supreme aspirations, dedicating all toward the ocean of the knowledge of all modes: the merit thus produced is immeasurable and boundless, continuing always until bodhi is attained. Why? Because the Tathāgata's merit and wisdom are inconceivable, innumerable, and without equal. Good man! All the Buddha World-Honored Ones possess three bodies: the Dharma-body, the enjoyment-body, and the transformation-body. After my nirvāṇa, if one wishes to make offerings to these three bodies, one should make offerings to the relics. There are two kinds of relics: first, bodily relics of bone; second, dharma-verse relics." Then he spoke in verse:

> All dharmas arise from conditions;
> The Tathāgata has taught their cause.
> The cessation of those conditions:
> This the great śramaṇa has taught.

"If men or women, bhikṣus, or the fivefold assembly wish to make a Buddha image, or if one lacks the means, then make one as small as a grain of barley; and build a stūpa as small as a jujube, with a finial like a needle, a canopy like a flake of chaff, and relics as small as a mustard seed, or write the dharma-verse and place it within. With precious offerings as described, according to one's ability, with utmost sincerity and earnestness: it is as though I were present in person, with no difference at all.

"Good man! If any sentient being is able to make such a supreme offering, they achieve fifteen distinguished merits with which they adorn themselves: first, one always has a sense of conscience and shame; second, one gives rise to a mind of pure faith; third, one's mind is honest and straightforward; fourth, one draws near to good friends; fifth, one enters the wisdom free from outflows; sixth, one always sees all the Buddhas; seventh, one always upholds the true Dharma; eighth, one is able to practice as taught; ninth, one is born at will in a pure buddha-land; tenth, if born among humans, one is of great lineage and noble station, respected and served by all, and people rejoice upon seeing one; eleventh, when born among humans, one naturally recollects the Buddha; twelfth, the hosts of Māra are unable to harm or disturb one; thirteenth, in the age of the declining Dharma, one is able to protect the true Dharma; fourteenth, one is blessed and protected by all the Buddhas of the ten directions; fifteenth, one swiftly attains the perfection of the five-fold Dharma-body."

At that time the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> After my parinirvāṇa,
> One who can make offerings to relics,
> Or build stūpas
> And images of the Tathāgata;
> At those stūpas and images,
> Sweeping and anointing the maṇḍala,
> With various kinds of fragrant flowers
> Scattered and spread upon them;
> With pure and fine fragrant water
> Bathing the image's body,
> With the finest food and drink,
> All presented as offerings,
> Praising the Tathāgata's virtue,
> Immeasurable, inconceivable,
> With skillful means, wisdom, and spiritual powers,
> One swiftly reaches the other shore.
> One obtains the vajra body,
> Complete with the thirty-two marks
> And eighty minor features,
> And saves all living beings.

At that time the Bodhisattva Viśuddhimati, having heard this verse, said to the Buddha: "How should the sentient beings of the future bathe the image?"

The Buddha told the Bodhisattva Viśuddhimati: "You should give rise to a mind of right recollection toward the Tathāgata. Do not cling to either extreme or be confused by emptiness and existence. Toward all wholesome practices, be filled with longing and never grow weary. Skillfully cultivate wisdom through the three gates of liberation. Always seek escape; do not remain in birth and death. Toward all sentient beings, give rise to great loving-kindness and compassion. Aspire to swiftly accomplish the three kinds of body.

"Good man! I have already taught you the Four Noble Truths, the twelve links of dependent origination, and the six perfections. Now I shall further teach you, and all kings, princes, great ministers, queens and consorts, devas, nāgas, humans, and ghosts, the method of bathing the image, which is the foremost among all kinds of offerings, surpassing the giving of seven treasures equal to the sands of the Ganges. When bathing the image, one should use ox-head sandalwood, white sandalwood, purple sandalwood, aloeswood, frankincense, turmeric, camphor, lavendar, and agastache, grinding them on a clean stone to make a fragrant paste for the fragrant water, and place it in a clean vessel. In a clean place, prepare an altar of fine earth, either square or round, of a size appropriate to the occasion. Upon it place a bathing platform, and in the center place the Buddha image. Pour the fragrant water over it and wash it clean. Then rinse again with pure water. All water used must be strained to avoid harming insects. Of the bathing water, take two fingers' worth and place it upon one's own head: this is called 'auspicious water.' Pour it out on clean ground; do not let it be stepped upon. With a fine, soft cloth wipe the image clean. Burn various famous incenses to pervade the surroundings with fragrance. Then return the image to its original place.

"Good man! Because of performing such bathing of the Buddha image, you and all the great assembly of humans and devas will in the present life receive wealth and happiness, freedom from illness and long life. Whatever you wish for, nothing will fail to accord with your intentions. Relatives and kin will all be safe and secure. You will forever leave the eight difficulties and permanently escape the source of suffering. You will not receive a female body, and will swiftly attain true enlightenment." After placing the image back, one should again burn incense and, face to face before the image, join one's palms with utmost sincerity and speak this praise:

> I now bathe all the Tathāgatas,
> The assembly of pure wisdom, merit, and adornment.
> May the sentient beings of the five turbidities
> Swiftly realize the Tathāgata's pure Dharma-body.
> The fragrance of morality, concentration, wisdom, liberation,
> And the knowledge and vision of liberation
> Pervades the buddha-lands of the ten directions, always sweet.
> May this incense-smoke likewise
> Accomplish immeasurable buddha-deeds.
> May the suffering of the three evil paths be stilled,
> And all beings be freed from heat and obtain coolness.
> May all give rise to the aspiration for unsurpassed bodhi,
> Forever leaving the river of craving and reaching the other shore.

When the Buddha had spoken this sūtra, innumerable, boundless bodhisattvas in the assembly obtained the immaculate samādhi. Innumerable devas obtained the wisdom of non-retrogression. The assembly of śrāvakas all aspired to seek the fruit of buddhahood. Eighty-four thousand sentient beings all gave rise to the aspiration for anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi.

At that time the Bodhisattva Viśuddhimati said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I am fortunate that the great teacher has had compassion upon us and taught us the method of bathing the image. I shall now encourage kings, great ministers, and all who have faith and delight in merit to bathe the honored image every day, obtaining great benefit. I shall always reverently receive this teaching and joyfully practice it!"
