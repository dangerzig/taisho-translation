# 北方毘沙門天王隨軍護法儀軌
## Ritual Manual of the Northern Vaiśravaṇa Heavenly King's Dharma-Protection Following the Army

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1247

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

At that time, Prince Naṭa, holding a halberd in his hands, gazed upon the four directions with wrathful eyes and addressed the Buddha: "I am the third prince and second grandson of Northern Heavenly King Vaiśravaṇa-rāja. My grandfather the heavenly king and I, Naṭa, together three times daily address the Buddha: 'We protect and uphold the buddha-dharma, and wish to seize and bind evil persons or those who give rise to unwholesome minds. Day and night we guard the king, great ministers, and all officials. As for those who commit killing and assault against one another, we, Naṭa, shall pierce their eyes and hearts with the vajra staff. If anyone harbors unwholesome or murderous intent toward bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, or upāsikās, we shall also strike their heads with the vajra club.'"

At that time, Vaiśravaṇa's grandson Naṭa addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! For the sake of future unwholesome sentient beings, to subdue, seize, bind, and completely destroy and scatter them, and also to protect the boundaries of the realm, I shall proclaim my own heart's fierce mantra. May the World-Honored One grant me permission to speak." The Buddha said: "Excellent, excellent! Naṭa, Heavenly King! For the sake of subduing all those kings, great ministers, and officials who commit killing and assault, and also those who oppose the Buddha's dharma, speak as you wish." The mantra:

*Oṃ diśa-naṭa vaiśravaṇāya mahā-rājāya yakṣa-adhipataye namo bhagavate mātar-bhadranī svāhā*

"If a practitioner wishes to uphold this mantra, one must first commission a painting. The pigments used must not contain any glue. Upon white cotton cloth, paint Vaiśravaṇa and his grandson Naṭa, adorned with the seven treasures. His left hand holds teeth at his mouth, his right hand rests on his hip holding a triple-pointed halberd. Beneath the deity's feet, paint a yakṣa woman in padmāsana, colored dark blue-black with slight red.

"When reciting this mantra, select good ground free from defilement, and offer various flowers and burning incense. The practitioner's upper and lower garments must all be clean, and when using the latrine one must maintain bodily protection. Begin on the fifteenth night of the dark fortnight, and before the image recite the mantra to a full count of three hundred thousand. Afterward, offer the sacred image with fragrant mud.

"If rebellious ministers or officials who oppose the king harbor unwholesome minds, perform a homa with pine needles, and within seven days they will be subdued by the vajra.

"If any bhikṣu or bhikṣuṇī commits an offense, perform a homa with neem wood, and within seven days they will be annihilated.

"Another method: if there is a person who has offended against the king, write their name on a piece of khadira wood as an effigy, repeatedly pass it in and out of a triangular fire while orally reciting the above mantra and mentally visualizing the effigy, and they will be subdued.

"Another method: if pūtanas or any ghost kings come seeking an opportunity, perform a homa with vajra wood, and within seven days they will be annihilated. If evil enemies come to assault and kill, form the heart-mudrā and recite the above mantra seven times, and they will be spontaneously annihilated and dare not approach.

"Another method: to subdue enemies and violent persons, perform a homa with neem wood. Also, boil neem wood to extract its juice, mix it with yellow earth to form an effigy, write the person's name on its chest, make seven effigies, and over seven days cast them into the fire, repeatedly reciting the mantra and forming the mudrā; without doubt they will be annihilated.

"Another method: if monastics of the four groups harbor unwholesome minds toward the seven treasures, or are undutiful toward parents, brothers, sisters, and the six types of relatives, perform a homa with cypress wood according to the method, and all precious wealth will come of its own accord.

"Another method: for wife, children, servants, carriages, and the six kinds of domestic animals, perform a homa with cypress wood according to the method, and all livestock will come of their own accord.

"Another method: to overcome all persons and evil enemies, make a triple-pointed halberd from pine wood and repeatedly strike the altar floor. Place three effigies beneath the altar and repeatedly strike and sever them.

"Another method: for the entrance to an asura cave, strike it with the triple-pointed halberd, and the entrance will open of itself.

"Another method: perform a homa with white powder mixed with salt, and command a heavenly army of forty thousand who will surely come. The enemy forces of one's own and other lands will all be annihilated at once.

"The heart-mudrā: interlock both hands inward, extending both little fingers and both thumbs outward."

In former times, the five kingdoms were in great turmoil for eight months. Month after month many methods were practiced but none proved effective. When this method was practiced, the five kingdoms were subdued, and an army of fifty thousand found peace of its own accord. Therefore it is named "Dharma-Protection Following the Army."
