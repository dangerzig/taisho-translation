# 毘沙門天王經
## Sūtra of Vaiśravaṇa, Heavenly King

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1244

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

At that time, Vaiśravaṇa, Heavenly King, stood before the Buddha, joined his palms, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! For the benefit, happiness, abundant wealth and treasures, and protection of the realm of all future sentient beings, I shall proclaim my own mantra. This mantra of mine is like the cintāmaṇi jewel king, able to fulfill all wishes. World-Honored One, please grant me permission to speak." The Buddha said: "Excellent, excellent! Heavenly King! You are able to have compassion and speak for the sake of sentient beings. Speak as you wish."

At that time, Vaiśravaṇa, Heavenly King, was filled with boundless joy, and before the Buddha spoke the heart mantra:

*Namo ratna-trayāya namo vaiśravaṇāya mahā-rājāya sarva-sattvānāṃ-śa-paripūraṇāya siddhi-kārāya sukhandanāya tasmainamaḥ-kṛtvā idam vaiśravaṇa hṛdayam-āvat-tayā iṣami sarva-sattva-sukhā-vahaṃ tadyathā oṃ siddhi-siddhi sumu-sumu ca-ca-ca-ca-cā raca-raca sara-sara kara-kara giri-giri kuru-kuru muru-muru curu-curu sādhaya adān-mama nityam-arthā-nu-bhava svāhā vaiśravaṇāya svāhā dhana-dāya svāhā maṇḍala-ratha-paripūrakāya svāhā*

At that time, Vaiśravaṇa, Heavenly King, having spoken this mantra, addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I now proclaim the method of upholding this mantra. First obtain frankincense, white sandalwood, camphor, tagara incense, olibanum, and storax, and blend these incenses as offerings to me, Vaiśravaṇa, Heavenly King. When making the invitation, form the root mudrā, hook both index fingers toward the body three times, then recite the mantra seven times and release the mudrā above the crown."

*Tadyathā namo vaiśravaṇāya namo dhana-dāya dhaneśvarāya agaccha-agaccha aparimita-dhaneśvara-parama-kāruṇika sarva-sattvā-hita-citta mama dhana-maṇḍa-prayaccha svayam-āgaccha svāhā*

"The practitioner should recite without interruption until Vaiśravaṇa, Heavenly King's son Jaṇiṣa appears in the form of a youth and tells the practitioner: 'What business have you summoned my father for?' The practitioner should say: 'For the sake of making offerings to the Three Jewels, grant me wealth and treasures.' The youth Jaṇiṣa will immediately return to Vaiśravaṇa, Heavenly King, and tell his father: 'The practitioner seeks various treasures for the sake of making offerings and benefiting sentient beings.' Vaiśravaṇa, Heavenly King, will tell the youth Jaṇiṣa: 'Give him one hundred gold coins daily, until the end of his life.' The youth Jaṇiṣa will daily deliver one hundred gold coins to the practitioner, placing them beside his head, and the gold coins will bear an extraordinary fragrance. Whatever one first vows to obtain should, aside from personal use, be given away in charity and should not be hoarded with a stingy mind. One should always generate a mind of great compassion toward all sentient beings and not give rise to anger. With excellent incense, flowers, food and drink, and lamps, in a quiet place make offerings to the Buddha, Dharma, and Saṃgha according to the dharma, and further contemplate without interruption. Out of gratitude toward Vaiśravaṇa, Heavenly King, and all his retinue, one should constantly recite the auspicious praise so that the heavenly king may obtain all auspicious blessings. May Vaiśravaṇa, Heavenly King, his sons, daughters, retinue, inner and outer relatives, ministers, attendants, messengers, and followers, and all sentient beings within the realm, all obtain the ten kinds of merit praised by the Buddha, namely: first, pure faith; second, precepts; third, learning; fourth, generosity; fifth, receiving; sixth, wisdom; seventh, physical beauty; eighth, strength; ninth, eloquence; tenth, mastery over form, sound, smell, taste, touch, wealth, and honor. May they open the dharma-eye within the buddha-dharma, realize the holy fruit, obtain the sweet dew of wondrous dharma, and also obtain the thirty-seven factors of the path to awakening. The practitioner should make such aspirations daily, and Vaiśravaṇa, Heavenly King, will be delighted and tell his own attendant retinue: 'Look at that practitioner who deeply reveres me.'

"He will further tell his son Jaṇiṣa: 'Whatever the practitioner hopes for, whether to see me, Vaiśravaṇa, Yakṣa King, or to close the gates to the evil destinies, may all their supreme wishes be fulfilled: a lifespan of immeasurable hundreds of thousands of years, obtaining the wish-fulfilling jewel, flying through space, obtaining the philosopher's stone and hidden treasures. Whether male or female, and even kings, all will be caused to love and respect them. They shall also understand the languages of all birds and beasts, and shall obtain abundant wealth and be forever free from poverty.'"

"That practitioner should, on the eighth and fifteenth days of the bright fortnight, have a painter receive the eight precepts, bathe, don new clean garments, and on uncut white cotton cloth paint the image, using no animal-based glue in the pigments. In the center, paint Śākyamuni Buddha in the teaching posture. To the Buddha's right, paint the form of Śrī Mahādevī, with broad, long eyes and a serene countenance, wearing a celestial crown, her body adorned with necklaces and armlets. Her right hand forms the wish-granting gesture, and her left hand holds an open lotus flower. When the painting is complete, install the image in a pure place and make offerings with unguents, flower garlands, incense, food, drink, and lamps to both the Buddha and Śrī Mahādevī. The upholder should not harbor an inferior mind or generate fear, but with a resolute mind recite in accordance with the dharma the mantra of Śrī Mahādevī:"

*Namaḥ śrī-gaṇāya namo vaiśravaṇāya mahā-yakṣa-rāja-adhirājāya namaḥ śrīyai mahā-devyai tadyathā oṃ tara-tara turu-turu suṣṭhu-suṣṭhu maṇi-kānaka-vajra-vaidūrya-mukta-nāma-laṃkṛta-pūjā sarva-sattvā-hita-kāma vaiśravaṇa-śrī-devī-valambhya ihi guraṇa-guraṇa ma-sama-sama nadarśaya-siddhi dāda-hime nadarśana-kāma-śya nadarśa-naṃ prahlāda-hlāda dayā-manāḥ svāhā*

"At that time, Vaiśravaṇa, Heavenly King, seeing the practitioner recite this mantra and make offerings to the Tathāgata, out of compassion for the practitioner, will manifest in person in the form of a youth or householder, holding a wish-fulfilling jewel in his right hand and a golden casket in his left, with a serene countenance, coming before the image. Having bowed to the Buddha's image, he will tell the practitioner: 'What wish do you now seek from me? Do you wish to enter an asura cave? Do you seek hidden treasure? Do you seek the mastery of mercury and fire? Do you seek the philosopher's stone or the love and respect of kings? The accomplishment of yellow ochre? The accomplishment of eye-medicine? The accomplishment of mantra-holding? Flying through space? A lifespan of one great kalpa? All such wishes can be accomplished.' The practitioner should address Vaiśravaṇa, Heavenly King: 'May I be unobstructed everywhere, obtaining gold and silver, inexhaustible fame and merit, a lifespan of immeasurable kalpas, flying through space, and various transformations and yogic mastery.' Vaiśravaṇa will say: 'As you wish.'"

At that time, Vaiśravaṇa, Heavenly King, wishing to further clarify the meaning, spoke this verse:

> "Even if the sun and moon
> Were to fall from space to earth,
> Or the great earth were to overturn,
> Such things might indeed occur,
> But one should not have the slightest doubt:
> This dharma is easy to accomplish.
> Without requiring fasting or precepts,
> It benefits the impoverished.
> All people will revere them,
> Even until their lifespan ends.
> Vaiśravaṇa's empowerment
> Keeps all calamities far away.
> Yakṣa generals will guard and protect
> And always follow the upholder.
> If one can uphold this teaching,
> All wishes will be accomplished,
> Swift as the shooting of an arrow.
> Kings will revere that person,
> Who obtains inexhaustible treasures.
> A thousand koṭis of yakṣas
> Guard and protect the practitioner,
> Able to fulfill all supreme wishes
> And liberate from all evil destinies.
> If one sees Vaiśravaṇa,
> Kubera bestows wealth.
> One obtains great wisdom,
> Even the divine eye,
> And a lifespan of koṭis of years.
> If a person with earnest mind
> Loves and reveres this teaching,
> They should seek accomplishment,
> And it will come without doubt.
> This dharma of bodily protection,
> Proclaimed by the Heavenly King of Much Learning,
> By the power of its empowerment,
> Is the supreme mantra siddhi."

He then recited the body-protection vidyā:

*Namo ratna-trayāya namo vaiśravaṇāya mahā-rājāya tadyathā oṃ hī gha-gha jaṇa-jaṇa guṇa-gunu mahā-rāja-yakṣa-yakṣa idaṃ sat-pada vaiśravaṇa-yakṣa svāhā*

"I now proclaim the root mudrā. With both hands, cross the right over the left with fingers interlocked inward. Raise both ring fingers with tips touching. Bend both index fingers like hooks. When making the invitation, hook toward the body. When dispatching, push outward. During recitation, form the mudrā at the heart, recite seven times, then release above the crown. Then take up the rosary and recite with focused attention.

"Next, the body-mudrā of Śrī Mahādevī: join both palms with hollow center. Open both index fingers, both middle fingers, and both ring fingers, bending them like a lotus shape. Both thumbs and both little fingers stand upright and touching. When reciting, form the mudrā at the heart, recite the mantra seven times, and release above the crown."

Vaiśravaṇa, Heavenly King's spell:

*Namo vaiśravaṇāya mahā-rājāya śibhī svāhā śiva-bhadrī svāhā*

"If one recites this spell over pure oil seven times and uses it to anoint one's sleeping place, whatever wealth and possessions one seeks will be obtained as wished."
