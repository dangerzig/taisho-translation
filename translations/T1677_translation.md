# 三身梵讚
## Brahmic Praise of the Three Bodies

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1677

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmabhadra (法賢)

---

*[Translator's note: The entire body of this text consists of phonetically transcribed Sanskrit (梵讚, "Brahmic verse"). The Chinese characters serve exclusively as phonograms, bearing no semantic content in Chinese. The following is a reconstructed IAST romanization of the underlying Sanskrit hymn praising the Three Bodies (trikāya) of the Buddha. Clearly identifiable Sanskrit terms include: svabhāva, nirvikāraṃ, dharmakāya, sukṛta, pratiṣṭha, sattvānāṃ, buddhānāṃ, saddharma, nirvāṇakāya, pāramitā, mahāyāna, sugatānāṃ, trayāṇaṃ, praṇāmaṃ, kuśala, bodhimārge, and samāpti. Portions of uncertain reconstruction are enclosed in brackets.]*

[Dhāraṇī]

> yunaikuṇa bhediga sāparihita mahāsampanna dhārabodhu nevabhāmuna
> bhavakhamīva samara sunir-vibhava svabhāva-nirlīnaṃ nirvikāraṃ śivaṃ
> samāsamāye pūrvaṃ pañcamāṇḍe pradyotamāvidah tat-karmānupāma
> dharmakāya-jñānāṃ loketa-mañjide sukṛta samāpalāma tumanuyu
> vibhūtiṃ pariśuddha mati-vicitram satpāyati mahāti-dhimatā daṃbhīlati
> hetu buddhānāṃ sarvaloke praśrita māviraṭu nara-saddharma [k/j]uśammāṇe
> sambhogakāya-[jyo]tatmahādharma-rājayaṃ pratiṣṭha sattvānāṃ pākahetu
> kecinnāṃ raivabhati yuṇe-vimāna sambodhi-dharma kṛkeci-nabica
> punardarśeti praśāntanika karaprabṛhmate saṃbhava-bhayaharaṃ
> viśrūpirūpamāṇe nirvāṇakāya darśane anugadantaṃ mahārdham munina
> sattvarītaika kṛpāṇam pāramitā mahāyāna puṇyadāyānaṃ kāyanāṃ
> sugatānāṃ prativigata manuvakupathānaṃ trayāṇaṃ kṛtvā-bhaktye
> praṇāmaṃ kuśala-mupacidāṇa māyā-bodhivijayate trikāya-siddhena
> labdha-jñāgane namaḥ-kīrtaṃ bodhimārge yujate trikāya-sattva samāpti
