# 不空羂索呪經
## Sūtra of the Amoghapāśa Mantra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1093

Translated from the Chinese. Sui dynasty, translated by Jñānagupta (闍那崛多).

---

Thus have I heard. At one time the Bhagavān was at the summit of Mount Potalaka, in the place where Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva's palace is situated. On that mountain there were many śāla trees, tamāla trees, campaka flower trees, atimuktaka flower trees, and so forth, as well as various boundless jewel-trees adorning all around. He was together with a great assembly of eight thousand bhikṣus. Also there were countless Śuddhāvāsa devas, hundreds of thousands surrounding on all sides, including the host of Īśvara devas and the host of Maheśvara devas. Furthermore there were various Great Brahmā Kings and deva-princes, requesting the Buddha to teach the Dharma.

At that time Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, in the midst of that great ocean-assembly, arose from his seat, arranged his robes baring his right shoulder, placed his right knee on the ground, and joined his palms toward the Buddha. With a serene and joyful face, smiling, he addressed the Buddha:

"Bhagavān! There is a heart-mantra called King Amoghapāśa. In the past, since I first generated the aspiration for awakening over ninety-one kalpas ago, in that kalpa there was a world called Avalokana, and in that land there was a buddha named Lokādhirāja Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha. That Tathāgata, out of compassion for me, spoke this heart-mantra. At that time, from that Buddha World-Honored One, I received and upheld it without forgetting. By this power, from that time until now, I have constantly taught and transformed countless hundreds of thousands of Maheśvara great deva hosts, Śuddhāvāsa Deva Kings and deva-princes beyond measure, directing them toward anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi.

"World-Honored One! After I obtained this mantra from that buddha, I immediately attained ten thousand samādhis, including the samādhi of Unforgetting Wisdom Victory Adornment.

"World-Honored One! Wherever this heart-mantra exists, know that in that place there are great hosts of devas, namely Īśvara, Maheśvara, Great Brahmā, Brahmā, and twelve thousand others who guard and protect it. World-Honored One! Wherever this mantra exists, one should regard that place as a stūpa. World-Honored One! In whatever direction this heart-mantra exists, one should know that those people have already made offerings to hundreds of thousands of millions of billions of nayutas of buddhas and planted good roots.

"World-Honored One! If any person hears this Amoghapāśa heart-mantra, know that if such a person in the past committed foul evil deeds toward others, performed unlawful acts, slandered their teachers, or slandered the true Dharma, and would certainly fall into the Avīci hell in a future life, abandoned by all buddhas, bodhisattvas, śrāvakas, and pratyekabuddhas: if that person afterward gives rise to a mind of remorse and no longer commits evil, and can fast for one day and one night and recite this heart-mantra, then that person's grave offenses will be lightened and received in this present life. Perhaps for one day they will suffer a fever, or for two days, or three days, or four days, or seven days they will suffer fever. Or perhaps they will suffer eye pain, or ear pain, or pain of the lips or teeth, or pain of the tongue or palate, or heart pain, or abdominal pain, or knee pain, or pain of the ribs, flanks, waist, spine, or between the ribs, or pain of the joints, or hemorrhoids, or difficulty with urination or defecation, or dysentery, or affliction of the hands or feet, or headache, or sores and ringworm, or white leprosy, great leprosy, swelling sores, boils, inverted-flower sores, malignant ulcers, lunar-eclipse sores, or epilepsy and various ghost-illnesses. Or they may encounter curses, wild-path spells, and various incantations: either worked on behalf of another but rebounding upon oneself, or worked by oneself yet still afflicting oneself. Or they may be shackled and imprisoned, beaten by others, killed by others, threatened, reviled, insulted, or slandered.

"World-Honored One! I now speak briefly: or they may encounter oppression through deeds of body, speech, and mind, or have evil dreams at night. Because these are received in the present, all such evil karma is entirely eliminated. How much more so for pure beings with right faith and right practice: that their sins would not be eliminated is impossible.

"World-Honored One! If any of the four assemblies, even with a deceitful mind, hear this mantra, or read and recite it, or receive and uphold it, or constantly recite it day and night, or explain it for others and cause them to hear it, or even speak this heart-mantra before animals so that their ears may hear it, and furthermore can speak these vajra-sentences, thinking in their own minds: 'What are these vajra-sentences? They are: not abandoning anything; not discriminating anything; the unconditioned in all things; not being delayed in anything; not fabricating anything; not being defiled in anything; equanimity of mind toward all; nothing arrived at in all things; not discarding anything; not departing from the five aggregates.' Through such skillful means one should be mindful of the Buddha. For those people, buddhas numbering one thousand throughout the ten directions will appear before them, and those buddhas will teach them to repent and eliminate their sins.

"World-Honored One! I now speak briefly: if any person writes this heart-mantra on bamboo or silk and receives, upholds, venerates, and pays homage to it, there are such immeasurable, boundless accumulations of merit. World-Honored One! I now speak briefly: those who hear this mantra should not give rise to contention. World-Honored One! If a person in the midst of fighting or quarreling hears this mantra, or hears it while frightened by a great household, or while guarding another's feelings, or through jest and play: World-Honored One! Those people should reflect: 'That my ear-faculty hears this mantra is through the awesome spiritual power of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, not through my own power.'

"World-Honored One! It is like someone who takes sandalwood incense, or camphor, or musk, and various other fragrances, yet slanders, insults, reviles, and speaks ill of them. After the slandering, insulting, reviling, and speaking ill, they still apply those fragrances to their body. Yet that sandalwood, and even the musk, do not think: 'That person slandered me, reviled me, insulted me.' Could they have the thought: 'I give my fragrance to this one but not to that one'? That is impossible. World-Honored One! They simply possess their own fragrant nature and do not abandon their original nature. Just so, just so.

"World-Honored One! If any person slanders or insults my heart-mantra, or speaks ill and defames it, or even, as described above, venerates and honors it with a deceitful mind: World-Honored One! Those deceitful beings, in future lives, will be able to make those good roots serve as a cause for buddhahood. In every birth and every place, they will never be separated from the fragrance of morality, concentration, superior wisdom, and the accumulation of merit. In future lives they will be able to uphold the fragrance of the precepts.

"World-Honored One! If good men and good women, or bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, or upāsikās, or other people, for the sake of this heart-mantra, on the eighth, fourteenth, and fifteenth days of the month fast for one day and one night without eating, maintain the precepts in body, practice diligence in mind, and recite this heart-mantra seven times during the day and seven times at night without thinking of other matters or speaking with others: World-Honored One! Such people will obtain twenty kinds of merit in this present life.

"What are these twenty kinds of merit? First, no illness or pain afflicts their body; even if illness occurs, through the power of merit it is quickly healed. Second, their body becomes subtle, soft, and lustrous, and many people love and respect them. Third, their sense faculties become tamed. Fourth, they obtain great wealth, their desires are fulfilled, and they are not robbed by others. Fifth, fire cannot burn them. Sixth, water cannot sweep them away. Seventh, kings cannot seize them, and all their undertakings are always auspicious. Eighth, harmful hail does not injure them, and evil nāgas' poisons are absorbed. Ninth, they do not suffer drought, and have no fear of evil winds or torrential rain. Tenth, if insects eat their crops, they should take sand, ash, or water and enchant it seven times as they wish, then bind the eight directions, above and below, universally sealing them, and all terrors and insect-poisons will be eliminated. Eleventh, evil ghosts that absorb people's vital essence, or appear as a spouse in dreams seeking to bewitch, also cannot harm them. Twelfth, among all evil beings they are always at ease, and their mind constantly remains respectful without momentary abandonment. Thirteenth, if enemies arise with malicious intent seeking vengeance, these are naturally destroyed. Fourteenth, anyone with evil intentions seeking to harm them cannot succeed, and naturally departs. Fifteenth, all curses and sorcery are naturally controlled and cannot cause harm. Sixteenth, they are the strongest in any assembly. Seventeenth, afflictions cannot entangle them. Eighteenth, when on a battlefield facing weapons, if they single-mindedly recite the mantra, no swords, staffs, or arrows can touch their body. Nineteenth, all good spirits constantly follow and protect them. Twentieth, in every birth and every place, they never depart from lovingkindness, compassion, sympathetic joy, and equanimity.

"World-Honored One! If a person can receive and uphold this heart-mantra, they will obtain these twenty kinds of merit.

"World-Honored One! Furthermore they will obtain eight kinds of qualities. First, on the day they die, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva will appear before them in the form of a bhikṣu. Second, at the time of death, their mind will not be scattered, the four elements will be peaceful, and no suffering or affliction will trouble their body. Third, even when severely ill, there will be no discharge or defilement of feces or urine. Fourth, on the day of death, they will attain right mindfulness, and their mind will not be confused. Fifth, on the day of death, they will not die face-down. Sixth, on the day of death, they will attain boundless eloquence. Seventh, on the day of death, if they wish to be reborn in any buddha-land, they may go there as they desire. Eighth, they will always have good spiritual friends who never abandon them. These eight kinds of auspicious signs will appear before such a person.

"If any person can recite and be mindful of this mantra three times daily, reciting it three times each session, and upholds it while abstaining from alcohol, meat, and the five pungent foods, then the merit they obtain will increase day and night. A bodhisattva does not act for themselves alone; knowing the mental nature and capacity of sentient beings, they should teach accordingly. Do not be miserly or stingy, and do not be arrogant or jealous, thinking: 'I can do it for you, but you cannot.' Why? Because a bodhisattva abandons jealousy and envy, creating great benefit for all sentient beings and thereby attaining bodhi and entering the ranks of bodhisattvas. Bodhi means great wisdom; 'sentient beings' means skillful means. These two principles exist for the sake of sentient beings and thereby bear fruit.

"World-Honored One! May the Buddha permit me to speak this heart-mantra. I wish to proclaim it before the Tathāgata, and also for the sake of the four assemblies of sentient beings, that they may obtain peace, happiness, and benefit, and further for sinful beings, that their sins may be eliminated."

At that time the World-Honored One addressed Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva: "You who purify sentient beings, speak this heart-mantra. You should know the right time; the Tathāgata rejoices in accord. This heart-mantra, for good men and good women who practice the bodhisattva path in future ages, should be regarded as father and mother."

At that time Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, gazing at the Honored One's face without taking his eyes away for even a moment, addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! The Buddha has permitted me to speak this heart-mantra. All bodhisattvas should venerate, pay homage to, and respectfully honor this gate of complete liberation, for the benefit and happiness of many people, out of compassion for many people, and for devas and humans in the world to attain worldly happiness.

"I now bow my head to all buddhas of the three times, and to the saṃgha of bodhisattvas, and to the śrāvakas and pratyekabuddhas of past, future, and present.

"I now bow my head to all the sages who practice rightly and are rightly directed.

"I now bow my head to the greatly wise Śāriputra.

"I now bow my head to the Lord Maitreya and the assembly of bodhisattvas.

"I now bow my head to our original teacher, Amitābha Tathāgata.

"I now bow my head to the Eternally Abiding Three Jewels.

"Homage to Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, the greatly compassionate one."

Then he spoke the mantra:

"This mantra is effective as soon as it is recited. Three times daily, reciting one hundred and eight times each session, one can eliminate the five heinous sins and all karmic obstructions. Burn agarwood incense when wishing to establish a boundary. Enchant water, or ash, or mustard seeds, or four pieces of purple sandalwood each seven times and drive them into the four directions. For all malarial fevers and chills, make an enchanted cord, recite twenty-one times, and tie it below the throat, and all such illnesses will be healed. Or enchant ghee, or oil, or water each twenty-one times, and after enchanting, have them drink it. If one wishes to break a curse worked by another, make a figure of that person from flour, clay, or wax, and cut it into pieces with a steel knife. If a person is frightened and wishes to protect their body, make an enchanted cord and wear it on the body. If a person suffers abdominal pain, enchant salt water and have them drink it. If a person has been poisoned by insects or bitten by a snake, enchant a salve and apply it. If a person suffers eye pain, take a white thread, make it an enchanted cord, and tie it to their ear. If a person suffers toothache, take a karañja wood, enchant it twenty-one times, then chew it and rub it on the teeth.

"To establish a great boundary, take five-colored thread, enchant it twenty-one times, take four pieces of purple sandalwood for stakes, wind the cord around the stakes, and drive them into the four corners to complete the great boundary. For all frightening and terrifying places where one wishes to protect the body, make an enchanted cord and wear it while traveling; or enchant water and sprinkle it; or enchant ash and scatter it. If someone is possessed by a ghost, take five-colored thread, make it an enchanted cord, and wear it or tie it to the body. For all fevers and chills, take white thread, enchant it twenty-one times, make it a cord, and wear it while traveling. For all kinds of malignant sores, take long pepper, pound it to powder, mix with honey, enchant twenty-one times, and apply it to the sores. For eye afflictions, take fragrant water or licorice water, enchant twenty-one times, and wash both eyes. For ear afflictions, boil oil, enchant twenty-one times, and put it in the ear. For all quarrels, all legal proceedings, and all frightening situations, take water, enchant twenty-one times, and wash the face.

"If within a country there is a great epidemic, or in a city, town, village, or even within a household, one should establish a ritual arena. Smear the ground with cow dung and sprinkle it with fragrant water. At the four corners place new, clean water vessels. Put various flowers in the vessels, and scatter various flowers throughout the ritual arena. Prepare various foods and beverages, all fragrant and delicious. Also spread various clean grasses to furnish the ritual arena, and place the food on top along with various fruits. At the four corners, make lamp dishes from flour, use clean cloth for wicks, and burn excellent incense, making offerings of the most superior kind. The mantra-master should bathe in fragrant water, wear fresh, clean garments, spread new, clean cushions on the seat and sit upon them, and recite this mantra. All calamities and catastrophes will then be eliminated. If anyone is gravely ill, take the vessel water and enchant it, then sprinkle it on that person. All who are sprinkled will have their epidemic illness eliminated. For all ghosts causing haunting, and all terrifying places, sprinkle water and all will be healed. If a person has been bound by another's curse, grind sandalwood, enchant it twenty-one times, and apply it over the heart, and they will be healed. To eliminate all karmic obstructions and the five heinous sins and four grave offenses, recite constantly without ceasing.

"If within a household there is an epidemic, or ghosts disturb the household, take one hundred and eight lotus flowers, enchant each lotus flower once, and cast them into a fire, and the affliction will be healed. If one wishes to win the hearts and minds of all beings, take one hundred and eight pieces of sandalwood, each two inches long, enchant each piece once, and cast them into a fire. To cure all ghost-illnesses and frightening situations, take jayā grass, vijayā grass, nakulī, gandha-nakulī grass, jālinī grass, abhaya-paṇi grass, indra-paṇi grass, tagara, cakra, mahā-cakra, viṣughna, sumara, and sunanda. Pound all these grasses to powder, sift finely, mix with water, and form into pills the size of large dates. Enchant one hundred and eight times. Wear them on the head or tie them to the hand while traveling, and all ghost-illnesses and epidemic diseases will be unable to cause harm. If a small child has ghost-illness or is frightened by an epidemic, tie one pill below their throat. If a woman has inauspicious features making marriage difficult, or has various illnesses, take one pill of this medicine, mix with water, and bathe with it. All inauspicious features will be removed, most wonderfully, most excellently, and without difficulty as one desires. If a woman does not bear sons, have her wear one pill tied at the neck, and she will bear a son. In all frightening situations, this serves as great protection. If a person has been poisoned by insects, this medicine can also cure it. Those who wear this medicine while traveling will not be harmed by fire or other calamities. For malignant sores, applying this medicine will bring healing. If a person is oppressed by various evil circumstances, wearing this medicine will remove the difficulty. If one wishes to stop evil winds and evil rain, enchant water twenty-one times and scatter it in the four directions, and they will cease. If there is great hail, take a tree branch, recite the mantra, and wave it from afar.

"This heart-mantra of Avalokiteśvara, even without formal reception and maintenance, by merely reciting it, can destroy all karmic obstructions and accomplish all things, and all will be achieved.

"If a person upholds this mantra, that person should weave a single white cotton cloth, five feet wide and one zhang long, without cutting it. When setting up the loom threads for this cloth, one should paint a buddha upon it. The pigments must not contain glue; one should mix the colors with incense and milk for painting. On the right side, paint an image of Avalokiteśvara in the form of Maheśvara, with the hair on his head all in conch-shaped topknots, wearing a flower crown. On the shoulders, paint a black deerskin covering the left shoulder, with the rest of the body adorned with various necklaces and ornaments. The painter should maintain the eight precepts, avoid mixed foods, bathe daily in fragrant water, and wear clean garments. The mantra-upholder should install the image in a pure place. Before the image, smear the ground with cow dung in a uniform square, sixteen feet on each side. Throughout the arena, scatter only pure white flowers. Place eight water vessels, each holding one dou, all new and clean, filled with fragrant water, and put various flowers in the vessels. Set out clean grasses and establish eight grass seats at the eight directions. Prepare eight portions of food and place them on the grass seats. Then prepare an additional sixty-four portions of food, each of various fine delicacies. All other miscellaneous foods should be prepared lavishly and arranged in abundance. Only the five pungent foods, alcohol, and meat are excluded; everything else that can be obtained should all be provided.

"When one wishes to recite the mantra, one should fast for three days and three nights, burning agarwood incense. The mantra-upholder should bathe in excellent fragrant water three times daily. After urination or defecation, one must also bathe. If three days and three nights of fasting is not possible, then fast for just one day and one night. One should eat only three kinds of white food: milk, yogurt, and polished rice. Before the image, kneeling for a long time and having set out the food and drink, one should recite the mantra one thousand and eight times. At that time, before the image, the practitioner will see their own body emit great radiance like flames of fire. Having seen this, they give rise to great joy. Then Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva himself will come and appear, granting whatever accords with their heart's wish.

"If one wishes to become invisible, take orpiment or indigo stone, pound to fine powder, and sift carefully. Before the image, enchant one thousand and eight times, and apply to the eyes. One will then become invisible, and even fly through the sky. Furthermore one will attain the samādhi called 'Amoghapāśa Wisdom-Vision Adorned.' After performing this method, all subsequent undertakings will be accomplished. This is the method for upholding the mantra, which I have now finished explaining."

At that time, when the Buddha had spoken this sūtra, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, the Śuddhāvāsa deva-princes, Brahmā the Lord of the Sahā world, and all the bodhisattvas, śrāvakas, and the entire great assembly of people, having heard Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva speak this sūtra before the Buddha, all rejoiced greatly and put it into practice.
