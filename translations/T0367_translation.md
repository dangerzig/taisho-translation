# 稱讚淨土佛攝受經
## Sūtra in Praise of the Pure Land and the Buddha's Gathering In

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0367

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Xuanzang (玄奘)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Bhagavān was dwelling in the city of Śrāvastī, in the Jeta Grove, in Anāthapiṇḍada's park, together with a great assembly of twelve hundred and fifty bhikṣus, all of whom were senior śrāvakas known and recognized by all, great arhats. Their names were: the Venerable Śāriputra, Mahāmaudgalyāyana, Mahākāśyapa, Aniruddha, and other such great śrāvakas as their chief leaders. There were also immeasurable bodhisattva-mahāsattvas, all abiding in the stage of non-retrogression, adorned with immeasurable merits and virtues. Their names were: Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva, Invincible Bodhisattva, Ever-Vigorous Bodhisattva, Never-Resting Bodhisattva, and other such great bodhisattvas as their chief leaders. There were also Śakra, the great Brahmā King, the lord of the Sahā world, the four world-protecting kings, and other such chief ones, with hundreds of thousands of koṭis of nayutas of devaputras, together with other immeasurable devas, humans, asuras, and others of the world, who had come to sit in the assembly for the sake of hearing the Dharma.

At that time the World-Honored One told Śāriputra:

"Do you know this? To the west of this world, passing beyond a hundred thousand koṭis of nayutas of buddha-lands, there is a buddha world called Sukhāvatī. The World-Honored One there is named Amitāyus and Amitābha, a Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha complete in the ten epithets, who now presently abides there in safety and ease, expounding the profound and subtle Dharma for all sentient beings, enabling them to obtain supreme benefit and happiness.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra, for what cause and what reason is that buddha world called Sukhāvatī? Śāriputra, because among the sentient beings in that realm there is not the slightest suffering of body or mind, and there is only immeasurable pure joy and happiness, it is therefore called the world of Sukhāvatī.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra, in the pure buddha land of the world of Sukhāvatī, everywhere there are seven rows of exquisite jeweled balustrades, seven rows of jeweled tāla trees, and seven layers of exquisite jeweled nets, surrounding it on all sides, adorned with four treasures: gold, silver, vaiḍūrya, and sphaṭika, wonderfully decorated and interlaced. Śāriputra, in that buddha land there are such marvelous ornaments, meritorious adornments most lovely and delightful; therefore it is called the world of Sukhāvatī.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra, in the pure buddha land of the world of Sukhāvatī, everywhere there are seven jeweled pools filled to the brim with water possessing eight qualities of merit. What are the eight qualities of meritorious water? First, it is clear and pure. Second, it is cool and refreshing. Third, it is sweet and delicious. Fourth, it is light and soft. Fifth, it is moistening and nourishing. Sixth, it is peaceful and harmonious. Seventh, when drunk it removes hunger, thirst, and immeasurable other afflictions. Eighth, after drinking it, it surely nourishes all the faculties and the four great elements, increasing and benefiting all manner of supreme good roots. It is constantly enjoyed by beings of abundant merit. The bottoms of all these jeweled pools are spread with golden sand. On all four sides there are four flights of steps, adorned with four treasures, most lovely and delightful. Surrounding all the pools are exquisite jeweled trees, arranged in ornamental rows, fragrant and sweet-scented, adorned with seven treasures, most lovely and delightful. The seven treasures are: first, gold; second, silver; third, vaiḍūrya; fourth, sphaṭika; fifth, red pearls; sixth, aśmagarbha gems; seventh, musāragalva gems. In all these pools there are constantly various multicolored lotuses, as large as carriage wheels: blue in form, blue in hue, blue in radiance, blue in shadow; yellow in form, yellow in hue, yellow in radiance, yellow in shadow; red in form, red in hue, red in radiance, red in shadow; white in form, white in hue, white in radiance, white in shadow; of four forms, four hues, four radiances, and four shadows. Śāriputra, in that buddha land there are such marvelous ornaments, meritorious adornments most lovely and delightful; therefore it is called the world of Sukhāvatī.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra, in the pure buddha land of the world of Sukhāvatī, there naturally and constantly resounds immeasurable and boundless exquisite music, its tones and melodies harmonious and elegant, most lovely and delightful. When sentient beings hear these wonderful sounds, all their evil afflictions are completely extinguished, immeasurable wholesome dharmas gradually increase and grow, and they swiftly realize unsurpassed, perfect, and complete awakening. Śāriputra, in that buddha land there are such marvelous ornaments, meritorious adornments most lovely and delightful; therefore it is called the world of Sukhāvatī.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra, in the pure buddha land of the world of Sukhāvatī, the ground throughout is composed entirely of true gold, soft to the touch, fragrant, pure, and luminous, adorned with immeasurable and boundless exquisite jewels. Śāriputra, in that buddha land there are such marvelous ornaments, meritorious adornments most lovely and delightful; therefore it is called the world of Sukhāvatī.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra, in the pure buddha land of the world of Sukhāvatī, during the six periods of day and night there constantly rain down various supremely wonderful celestial flowers, radiant, fragrant, pure, fine, soft, and multicolored. Although they delight the body and mind of those who see them, they do not give rise to attachment. They increase immeasurable, countless, and inconceivable supreme merits for sentient beings. During the six periods of day and night, those sentient beings constantly make offerings to Amitāyus Buddha. Each morning they take these celestial flowers and, within the time of a single meal, fly to other innumerable worlds, making offerings to a hundred thousand koṭis of buddhas. At each buddha's place they scatter a hundred thousand koṭis of tree-flowers as offerings, then return to their own land to enjoy celestial pleasures. Śāriputra, in that buddha land there are such marvelous ornaments, meritorious adornments most lovely and delightful; therefore it is called the world of Sukhāvatī.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra, in the pure buddha land of the world of Sukhāvatī, there are constantly various wondrously lovely multicolored birds, namely: geese, egrets, herons, cranes, peacocks, parrots, kalaviṅkas, and jīvañjīvaka birds. All these birds, during the six periods of day and night, constantly gather together and produce harmonious and elegant sounds, each according to its kind proclaiming the subtle Dharma, namely: the profound foundations of mindfulness, right efforts, bases of spiritual power, faculties, powers, factors of awakening, path factors, and other immeasurable subtle dharmas. When the beings of that land hear these sounds, they each attain immeasurable merits of mindfulness of the Buddha, mindfulness of the Dharma, and mindfulness of the Saṃgha, which perfume and cultivate their persons. Śāriputra, what do you think? Are the birds of that land actually animals belonging to the lower realms? Do not hold such a view. Why? In that pure buddha land there are no three evil paths; one does not even hear the names of the three evil destinies, let alone their actual existence from the retribution of evil karma. You should know that all these birds are transformations created by Amitāyus Buddha, causing them to proclaim immeasurable Dharma sounds for the benefit and happiness of all sentient beings. Śāriputra, in that buddha land there are such marvelous ornaments, meritorious adornments most lovely and delightful; therefore it is called the world of Sukhāvatī.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra, in the pure buddha land of the world of Sukhāvatī, there are constantly wonderful breezes blowing through the jeweled trees and jeweled nets, producing subtle and exquisite sounds. It is as if a hundred thousand koṭis of celestial instruments were playing together simultaneously, producing subtle and exquisite sounds most lovely and pleasing. Thus in that land there are constantly wonderful breezes blowing through the multitude of jeweled trees and jeweled nets, striking forth all manner of subtle and exquisite sounds that proclaim all manner of Dharma. When the beings of that land hear these sounds, they give rise to mindfulness of the Buddha, the Dharma, and the Saṃgha, and to other immeasurable merits. Śāriputra, in that buddha land there are such marvelous ornaments, meritorious adornments most lovely and delightful; therefore it is called the world of Sukhāvatī.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra, in the pure buddha land of the world of Sukhāvatī, there are such immeasurable, boundless, inconceivable, and exceedingly rare things. Even if one were to spend a hundred thousand koṭis of nayutas of kalpas, using immeasurable hundreds of thousands of koṭis of nayutas of tongues, with each tongue producing immeasurable voices to praise its merits, one could never exhaust them; therefore it is called the world of Sukhāvatī.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra, in the pure buddha land of the world of Sukhāvatī, for what reason is the Buddha named Amitāyus? Śāriputra, because that Tathāgata and all the sentient beings there have lifespans of immeasurable and countless great kalpas; for this reason, the Tathāgata of that land is named Amitāyus. Śāriputra, since Amitāyus Buddha realized anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi, ten great kalpas have already passed. Śāriputra, for what reason is that Buddha named Amitābha? Śāriputra, because that Tathāgata constantly emits immeasurable and boundless wonderful light, illuminating all the buddha lands of the ten directions, performing buddha-works without any obstruction; for this reason, the Tathāgata of that land is named Amitābha. Śāriputra, that pure buddha land is accomplished with such meritorious adornments, most lovely and delightful; therefore it is called the world of Sukhāvatī.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra, in the pure buddha land of the world of Sukhāvatī, Amitāyus Buddha constantly has immeasurable śrāvaka disciples, all of whom are great arhats endowed with all manner of subtle merits and virtues, their number boundless and beyond reckoning. Śāriputra, that pure buddha land is accomplished with such meritorious adornments, most lovely and delightful; therefore it is called the world of Sukhāvatī.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra, in the pure buddha land of the world of Sukhāvatī, Amitāyus Buddha constantly has immeasurable bodhisattva disciples, all of whom are in their final birth, endowed with all manner of subtle merits and virtues, their number boundless and beyond reckoning. Even if one were to spend countless kalpas praising their merits, one could never exhaust them. Śāriputra, in that buddha land there are such meritorious adornments, most lovely and delightful; therefore it is called the world of Sukhāvatī.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra, all sentient beings born in that land are non-retrogressive. They will assuredly never again fall into the perilous evil destinies, into frontier regions, into lowly stations, or among barbarous peoples. They will constantly travel through the pure lands of all buddhas, with their supreme practices and vows increasing and advancing thought after thought, and they will assuredly realize anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi. Śāriputra, in that buddha land there are such meritorious adornments, most lovely and delightful; therefore it is called the world of Sukhāvatī.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra, if any sentient beings hear of the immeasurable meritorious adornments of the pure buddha land of the western Amitāyus Buddha, they should all make the aspiration to be born in that buddha land. Why? If born in that land, they will share in the same assembly with such great beings adorned with immeasurable merits, enjoy the pure buddha land adorned with such immeasurable merits, and delight in the Mahāyāna Dharma without ever retrogressing, with immeasurable practices and vows increasing and advancing thought after thought, and they will swiftly realize unsurpassed, perfect, and complete awakening. Therefore, Śāriputra, sentient beings born in that buddha land accomplish immeasurable and boundless merits. It is not with few good roots that sentient beings can be reborn in the pure buddha land of the world of Sukhāvatī of Amitāyus Buddha.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra, if there are good men or good women of pure faith who hear the immeasurable, boundless, inconceivable name of meritorious Amitāyus Buddha and the meritorious adornments of the world of Sukhāvatī, and having heard, contemplate them, whether for one day and night, or two, or three, or four, or five, or six, or seven, maintaining mindfulness without confusion, then when those good men or good women approach the time of death, Amitāyus Buddha, together with his immeasurable śrāvaka disciples and bodhisattva assembly, will surround them and come to stand before them, bestowing compassionate protection and ensuring their minds remain undisturbed. Having relinquished their lives, they will follow the Buddha's assembly and be born in the world of Sukhāvatī of Amitāyus, the pure buddha land.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra, observing such a great matter of benefit and happiness as this cause and condition, I speak these truthful words: if there are good men or good women of pure faith who hear the inconceivable name of meritorious Amitāyus Buddha and the pure buddha land of the world of Sukhāvatī, they should all have faith, accept it, and make the aspiration to cultivate practice as taught and be born in that buddha land.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra, just as I now extol and praise the immeasurable, boundless, inconceivable merits of the buddha land of Amitāyus Buddha, so too in the east there are presently Akṣobhya Tathāgata, Merudhvaja Tathāgata, Mahāmeru Tathāgata, Meruprabhāsa Tathāgata, and Mañjudhvaja Tathāgata, and other such buddhas as numerous as the sands of the Gaṅgā, abiding in the east. From their own pure buddha lands, each manifests a broad, long tongue that covers and encompasses the entire great trichiliocosm, and they speak these truthful words: 'All you sentient beings should have faith in and accept this Dharma-gate of Praising Inconceivable Buddha-Land Merits, by which All Buddhas Gather In.'

"Furthermore, Śāriputra, in the south likewise there are presently Candrasūryaprabha Tathāgata, Yaśaḥprabha Tathāgata, Mahārciskandha Tathāgata, Meruprabha Tathāgata, and Anantavīrya Tathāgata, and other such buddhas as numerous as the sands of the Gaṅgā, abiding in the south. From their own pure buddha lands, each manifests a broad, long tongue that covers and encompasses the entire great trichiliocosm, and they speak these truthful words: 'All you sentient beings should have faith in and accept this Dharma-gate of Praising Inconceivable Buddha-Land Merits, by which All Buddhas Gather In.'

"Furthermore, Śāriputra, in the west likewise there are presently Amitāyus Tathāgata, Amitāskandha Tathāgata, Amitābha Tathāgata, Amitādhvaja Tathāgata, Maheśvara Tathāgata, Mahāprabha Tathāgata, Prabhārcis Tathāgata, Mahāratnaketu Tathāgata, and Raśmipramukta Tathāgata, and other such buddhas as numerous as the sands of the Gaṅgā, abiding in the west. From their own pure buddha lands, each manifests a broad, long tongue that covers and encompasses the entire great trichiliocosm, and they speak these truthful words: 'All you sentient beings should have faith in and accept this Dharma-gate of Praising Inconceivable Buddha-Land Merits, by which All Buddhas Gather In.'

"Furthermore, Śāriputra, in the north likewise there are presently Amitābha-vyūha-samyak-prativedha-jñāna Tathāgata, Amitadundubhi-svara-mahānirghosa Tathāgata, Mahāskandha Tathāgata, Prabhājāla Tathāgata, and Śālarāja Tathāgata, and other such buddhas as numerous as the sands of the Gaṅgā, abiding in the north. From their own pure buddha lands, each manifests a broad, long tongue that covers and encompasses the entire great trichiliocosm, and they speak these truthful words: 'All you sentient beings should have faith in and accept this Dharma-gate of Praising Inconceivable Buddha-Land Merits, by which All Buddhas Gather In.'

"Furthermore, Śāriputra, in the nadir likewise there are presently Darśana-sarva-saddharma-nyāya-nityapradīpta-agni-rāja-śreṣṭhaguṇa-prabhāsa Tathāgata, Siṃha Tathāgata, Yaśas Tathāgata, Yaśaḥprabha Tathāgata, Saddharma Tathāgata, Saddharmapratibhāsa Tathāgata, Dhvajarāja Tathāgata, Puṇyamitra Tathāgata, and Puṇyanāman Tathāgata, and other such buddhas as numerous as the sands of the Gaṅgā, abiding in the nadir. From their own pure buddha lands, each manifests a broad, long tongue that covers and encompasses the entire great trichiliocosm, and they speak these truthful words: 'All you sentient beings should have faith in and accept this Dharma-gate of Praising Inconceivable Buddha-Land Merits, by which All Buddhas Gather In.'

"Furthermore, Śāriputra, in the zenith likewise there are presently Brahmasvara Tathāgata, Nakṣatrarāja Tathāgata, Gandhaprabha Tathāgata, Padmaśrī-uttamaguṇa Tathāgata, and Darśana-sarvārtha Tathāgata, and other such buddhas as numerous as the sands of the Gaṅgā, abiding in the zenith. From their own pure buddha lands, each manifests a broad, long tongue that covers and encompasses the entire great trichiliocosm, and they speak these truthful words: 'All you sentient beings should have faith in and accept this Dharma-gate of Praising Inconceivable Buddha-Land Merits, by which All Buddhas Gather In.'

"Furthermore, Śāriputra, in the southeast likewise there is presently Paramavistīrṇa-meghagarjitasvararāja Tathāgata, and other such buddhas as numerous as the sands of the Gaṅgā, abiding in the southeast. From their own pure buddha lands, each manifests a broad, long tongue that covers and encompasses the entire great trichiliocosm, and they speak these truthful words: 'All you sentient beings should have faith in and accept this Dharma-gate of Praising Inconceivable Buddha-Land Merits, by which All Buddhas Gather In.'

"Furthermore, Śāriputra, in the southwest likewise there is presently Parama-sūryaprabha-yaśaḥ-puṇyaguṇa Tathāgata, and other such buddhas as numerous as the sands of the Gaṅgā, abiding in the southwest. From their own pure buddha lands, each manifests a broad, long tongue that covers and encompasses the entire great trichiliocosm, and they speak these truthful words: 'All you sentient beings should have faith in and accept this Dharma-gate of Praising Inconceivable Buddha-Land Merits, by which All Buddhas Gather In.'

"Furthermore, Śāriputra, in the northwest likewise there is presently Amitapuṇya-agni-rāja-prabhāsa Tathāgata, and other such buddhas as numerous as the sands of the Gaṅgā, abiding in the northwest. From their own pure buddha lands, each manifests a broad, long tongue that covers and encompasses the entire great trichiliocosm, and they speak these truthful words: 'All you sentient beings should have faith in and accept this Dharma-gate of Praising Inconceivable Buddha-Land Merits, by which All Buddhas Gather In.'

"Furthermore, Śāriputra, in the northeast likewise there is presently Asaṃkhyeya-koṭi-sahasra-vipulajñāna Tathāgata, and other such buddhas as numerous as the sands of the Gaṅgā, abiding in the northeast. From their own pure buddha lands, each manifests a broad, long tongue that covers and encompasses the entire great trichiliocosm, and they speak these truthful words: 'All you sentient beings should have faith in and accept this Dharma-gate of Praising Inconceivable Buddha-Land Merits, by which All Buddhas Gather In.'

"Furthermore, Śāriputra, for what reason is this sūtra called 'Praising Inconceivable Buddha-Land Merits: the Dharma-Gate by which All Buddhas Gather In'? Śāriputra, because in this sūtra there is extolling and praising the inconceivable buddha-land merits of the world of Sukhāvatī of Amitāyus Buddha, and because the buddha world-honored ones of the ten directions, wishing to skillfully benefit and bring happiness to sentient beings, each abide in their own lands, manifest great spiritual transformations, and speak truthful words exhorting sentient beings to have faith in and accept this Dharma; therefore this sūtra is called 'Praising Inconceivable Buddha-Land Merits: the Dharma-Gate by which All Buddhas Gather In.'

"Furthermore, Śāriputra, if good men or good women have already heard, will hear, or now hear this sūtra, and having heard it, give rise to deep faith and understanding, then having given rise to faith and understanding, they will assuredly be gathered in by the buddha world-honored ones, as numerous as the sands of ten Gaṅgā rivers, abiding in the ten directions. Those who practice as taught will all assuredly attain non-retrogression from anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi and will all assuredly be born in the pure buddha land of the world of Sukhāvatī of Amitāyus Buddha. Therefore, Śāriputra, all of you sentient beings should have faith in, accept, and understand the words of myself and the buddha world-honored ones of the ten directions. You should diligently make effort and practice as taught, without giving rise to doubt.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra, if good men or good women have already made the aspiration, will make the aspiration, or now make the aspiration regarding the meritorious adornments of the pure buddha land of the world of Sukhāvatī of Amitāyus, they will assuredly be gathered in by the buddha world-honored ones, as numerous as the sands of ten Gaṅgā rivers, abiding in the ten directions. Those who practice as taught will all assuredly attain non-retrogression from anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi and will all assuredly be born in the pure buddha land of the world of Sukhāvatī of Amitāyus Buddha. Therefore, Śāriputra, if there are good men or good women of pure faith, they should all deeply have faith in and understand the pure buddha land of the world of Sukhāvatī of Amitāyus, make the aspiration to be born there, and not practice negligently.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra, just as I now extol and praise the inconceivable buddha-land merits of the world of Sukhāvatī of Amitāyus Buddha, so too the buddha world-honored ones of those ten directions also extol and praise my inconceivable and boundless merits, all speaking thus: 'How rare and extraordinary! Śākyamuni the Peaceful, Śākyamuni the Dharma King, a Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, one perfect in knowledge and conduct, Well-Gone One, Knower of the World, Unsurpassed One, Tamer of Persons, Teacher of Devas and Humans, Buddha, World-Honored One, has been able in this Sahā world, in an evil age of five turbidities, namely: the turbidity of the kalpa, turbidity of sentient beings, turbidity of afflictions, turbidity of views, and turbidity of lifespan, to realize anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi, and for the sake of skillfully benefiting and bringing happiness to sentient beings, to teach this Dharma that is extremely difficult for the world to believe.' Therefore, Śāriputra, you should know that I have now, in this defiled Sahā world, in an evil age of five turbidities, realized anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi, and for the sake of skillfully benefiting and bringing happiness to sentient beings, teach this Dharma that is extremely difficult for the world to believe. This is exceedingly rare, inconceivable.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra, in this defiled Sahā world, in an evil age of five turbidities, if there are good men or good women of pure faith who hear taught this Dharma that is extremely difficult for all the world to believe, and are able to give rise to faith and understanding, to receive, uphold, expound, and practice as taught, you should know that these people are exceedingly rare, who have planted good roots in the presence of immeasurable buddhas. When these people's lives end, they will assuredly be born in the world of Sukhāvatī in the west, where they will enjoy the Mahāyāna Dharma-joy of the pure buddha land adorned with all manner of meritorious adornments, during the six periods of day and night drawing near to and making offerings to Amitāyus Buddha, traveling throughout the ten directions making offerings to all buddhas, hearing the Dharma and receiving predictions at each buddha's place, with their provisions of merit and wisdom swiftly becoming complete, and swiftly realizing unsurpassed, perfect, and complete awakening."

When the Bhagavān had finished teaching this sūtra, the Venerable Śāriputra and all the great śrāvakas, the bodhisattva-mahāsattvas, and the immeasurable devas, humans, asuras, and the rest of the great assembly, having heard what the Buddha taught, all greatly rejoiced, had faith, accepted it, and put it into practice.
