# 佛說末羅王經
## Sūtra of King Malla, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0517

Translated from the Chinese. Liu Song dynasty, translated by Juqu Jingsheng (沮渠京聲)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the land of Śrāvastī, at the Jetavana Monastery of Anāthapiṇḍada, together with one thousand two hundred and fifty bhikṣus.

At that time, there was a king named Malla, whose land was fertile and whose people were strong and brave. In his kingdom there was a boulder, stretching tens of miles in circumference, right in the middle of the royal road. The ministers consulted together and petitioned the king to have the stone removed. The king then selected from the entire country a total of nine hundred million people and ordered them to dig up and remove the stone. Yet after months and years, the people were utterly exhausted and still could not move the stone.

The Buddha, reflecting on the foolishness of the people who were laboring in vain while the stone remained unmoved, called Ānanda: "Come with me." In the snap of a finger, they arrived in that country.

At that time, the Buddha had taken the form of a śramaṇa, wearing his robes and standing beside the stone. He said to the people: "Why are you digging to remove this stone?" At first no one answered. He asked three times. The people replied angrily: "We have been laboring to dig up this stone for years. Who are you, that you come and question us?" Each of them set down their tools and walked away.

The Buddha then smiled. With the tip of his toe he flipped the stone up. He caught it with his hand, tossed it into the sky, caught it again with his hand, and set it upon the ground. The Buddha then emitted radiance and manifested his excellent marks. When the nine hundred million people saw the Buddha's supernatural power, all were shaken with awe. They all bowed their heads to the ground and said: "We were foolish and could not distinguish the true from the false. What kind of god or deva are you?"

The Buddha said: "I am the Buddha."

The people asked: "By what power is the Buddha able to lift this stone?" He answered: "I have four powers. What are the four? The first is the power of diligence. The second is the power of patience. The third is the power of generosity. The fourth is the power of honoring one's parents.

"What is the power of diligence? It means not killing, not stealing, not committing sexual misconduct, and not deceiving. It means broadly proclaiming the sūtra Dharma, opening the way and guiding all beings, never slackening. This is the power of diligence.

"What is the power of patience? If any should cruelly harm, injure, defame, or insult me, or inflict evil upon me, my mind is like the earth, which receives all things without exception. This is the power of patience.

"What is the power of generosity? It means giving one's own country, treasures, wife, children, head, and eyes, all to others, without a thought of resentment or regret. This is the power of generosity.

"What is the power of honoring one's parents? It means receiving from one's parents the gift of one's body, their nursing and nurturing care. Even if one were to pile up treasures from the ground to the summit of the twenty-eight heavens and give them all to others, it would not equal the merit of caring for one's parents. This is the power of honoring one's parents."

The people further asked: "Are there yet other powers?"

The Buddha said: "There are four more powers. What are the four? They are birth, old age, sickness, and death. These are the four powers."

They further asked: "Will the Buddha always remain in the world?"

The Buddha said: "I too shall enter parinirvāṇa."

The people said: "The Buddha is so divinely holy, with excellent marks and a golden complexion, rare in the world, and yet he too will enter parinirvāṇa. How much more so for the likes of us?" The king, ministers, and all nine hundred million people, with one mind, understood. They requested to receive the five precepts and the ten virtues, and took refuge in the Three Honored Ones. Their bonds were loosened and their defilements removed, and they immediately attained the fruit of stream-entry.

Ānanda straightened his robes, made obeisance to the Buddha, and said: "What merit did this king and the nine hundred million people possess, that upon hearing the sūtra they attained understanding so quickly?"

The Buddha said: "Long ago, in the time of Krakucchanda Buddha, the king and the nine hundred million people of this country all made vows together. Some received the five precepts and the ten virtues, some observed the purificatory fasts, some lit lamps, some burned incense and scattered flowers, some recited the sūtras, and some listened to the sūtras. Therefore they have now come together, and upon hearing the sūtra, they immediately understood."

The bhikṣus were filled with joy and came forward to make obeisance to the Buddha.
