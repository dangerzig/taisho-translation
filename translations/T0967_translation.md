# 佛頂尊勝陀羅尼經

## The Buddhōṣṇīṣa Vijaya Dhāraṇī Sūtra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0967

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Buddhapāla (佛陀波利).

---

### Imperial Preface to the Buddhōṣṇīṣa Vijaya Dhāraṇī Sūtra

We consider that when the Tathāgata taught the Mahāyāna, he opened the gate of skillful means. The thousands of sūtras and myriad Dharma teachings are all for the purpose of ferrying sentient beings across so they do not sink into karmic retribution. The Buddhōṣṇīṣa Vijaya Dhāraṇī Sūtra is the wisdom seal of all tathāgatas, of vast compassion, profoundly rare, universally benefiting those in confusion. It is truly a bridge across the great ocean, a sun and moon in the darkness, food and drink for the hungry and thirsty. If good men and good women of this world and all sentient beings can arouse the mind of enlightenment and recite, uphold, and wear this dhāraṇī, their merit and blessings will be of every kind and inexhaustible. They will be forever freed from all evil paths of suffering. All the transgressions accumulated since beginningless time through hundreds of thousands of koṭis of kalpas will be entirely eliminated. If one practices diligently day and night without slackening, one will receive the abhiṣeka of all buddhas, the protection of celestial spirits, and immeasurable merit and longevity. Such supreme fruits can be witnessed with one's own eyes. They are sincere and not false.

We preside over all under heaven, and we take pity on the people who are caught in confusion: their actions lead them into the evil destinies, yet they do not realize such errors. This is truly pitiful. Therefore, with this sūtra and dhāraṇī, we cause all sentient beings throughout the Ganges-sand buddha-fields to share equally in the benefits, with life extended and merit increased, all reaching the Buddha Way together. Moreover, the Buddha has a solemn vow to broadly deliver sentient beings, and he always first delivers the loyal and filial. All loyal ministers and filial children who are born in the central land and live in an era of good governance enjoy various kinds of happiness. This is all because in serving their lord and serving their parents they fully exhaust the Way. Moreover, they revere the Three Treasures and accumulate good causes. In the time it takes to lift a foot, they have already ascended the path of awakening. As for evildoers who are unaware of repentance, whose actions grow worse day by day, whose karmic burdens are deep and heavy, who willingly fall into degradation, their bodies are as if lacquered, and seeking purity is truly difficult. Fallen into the dark prison, by what means can they escape the world? If they can turn their hearts toward goodness, this very path can transcend the dust of kalpas.

Ninth year of the Yǒnglè era [1411], sixth month.

### Preface to the Buddhōṣṇīṣa Vijaya Dhāraṇī Sūtra

The Buddhōṣṇīṣa Vijaya Dhāraṇī Sūtra: the brahmin monk Buddhapāla came from the western lands to China in the first year of the Yífèng era [676] and arrived at Mount Wutai. He then prostrated with the five points of his body touching the ground and bowed toward the mountain peak, saying: "After the Tathāgata's passing, all the sages have hidden their spiritual presence. Only the great being Mañjuśrī, in this mountain, draws forth sentient beings and instructs all the bodhisattvas. I, Pāla, lament that I was born in the eight difficult circumstances and have not beheld the holy visage. I have crossed the vast desert to come and pay my respects. I humbly beg, in your great compassion and great mercy, that you may universally grant me a vision of your venerable form." Having spoken, he wept with tears streaming, and bowed toward the mountain peak.

After bowing, as he raised his head, he suddenly saw an old man emerge from within the mountain, who then spoke in the Brahmin language and said to the monk: "Dharma Master, your heart is devoted to the Way, and you have pursued the holy traces. Without fearing hardship, you have traveled far to seek the sacred sites. However, the sentient beings of the Chinese lands create much evil karma, and even among those who have left the household life, many violate the precepts and rules. Only the Buddhōṣṇīṣa Vijaya Dhāraṇī Sūtra can destroy all the evil karma of sentient beings. Does the Dharma Master happen to have brought this sūtra with him?"

The monk replied: "This poor monk came solely to pay respects. I did not bring the sūtra."

The old man said: "Since you did not bring the sūtra, what good does it do to come empty-handed? Even if you were to see Mañjuśrī, how would you recognize him? Master, you should go back to the western lands and fetch this sūtra, then bring it here to circulate in the Chinese lands. This would be a universal offering to all the sages, a great benefit to all sentient beings, a rescue for those in the dark realms, and a repayment of the kindness of all buddhas. When you bring the sūtra here, this disciple will then show you where Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva resides."

The monk, hearing these words, was overjoyed beyond measure. Restraining his tears of emotion, he bowed with utmost sincerity. In the time it took to raise his head, the old man had suddenly vanished. The monk was astonished and redoubled his devotion with concentrated sincerity. He returned to the western lands and retrieved the Buddhōṣṇīṣa Vijaya Dhāraṇī Sūtra.

In the second year of the Yǒngchún era [683], he returned to the western capital and reported the above events to the Great Emperor. The Emperor took the manuscript into the palace and invited Tripiṭaka Master Rizhào, and by imperial command the Director of the Guest Reception Bureau of the Court of Diplomatic Relations, Dù Xíngyǐ, and others jointly translated this sūtra. The Emperor bestowed thirty bolts of silk upon the monk. The sūtra manuscript was kept in the palace and not released.

The monk wept and addressed the throne: "This poor monk risked his life and traveled far to bring back this sūtra. My sincere wish is to universally deliver all sentient beings and rescue them from suffering and difficulty. I did not do this for material reward or with any concern for fame and profit. I beg that the sūtra manuscript be returned for circulation, so that all sentient beings may equally benefit."

The Emperor then retained the translated version and returned the Sanskrit original to the monk. The monk took the Sanskrit text to Xīmíng Monastery, where he found a Chinese monk named Shùnzhēn who was skilled in Sanskrit. He memorialized the throne to jointly translate the text, and the Emperor granted the request. The monk then translated the text with various senior monks and Shùnzhēn. When the translation was complete, the monk took the Sanskrit original and went to Mount Wutai, entered the mountain, and has not come out to this day.

Both the earlier and later translations now circulate in the world. Where there are small differences in wording between them, one should not be surprised.

In the third year of the Chuígǒng era [687], the monastery head of Dìngjué Monastery, the monk Zhìjìng, while staying at the Wèiguó East Monastery in the divine capital [Luoyang], personally met with Tripiṭaka Master Rizhào. When the Dharma Master was asked about his travels, the account was exactly as stated above. Zhìjìng then received instruction in the sacred dhāraṇī from the Tripiṭaka Master. The Dharma Master orally transmitted the Sanskrit pronunciation over fourteen days, line by line, with meticulous care, complete in every Sanskrit syllable without a single error. He further collated the old Sanskrit translation to verify it, and all omissions and mistakes were corrected and finalized. The dhāraṇī annotated at the end as "final separate translation" is this version. Where the dhāraṇī phrases differ slightly from the version translated under Director Dù, this new dhāraṇī has been corrected and its pronunciation annotated without error. Future students should examine this carefully.

In the eighth month of the first year of the Yǒngchāng era [689], at the Great Jìng'ài Monastery, he met Senior Seat Dharma Master Chéng of Xīmíng Monastery and asked about his travels. The account was the same as before. The translation monk Shùnzhēn was still living at Xīmíng Monastery at that time.

This sūtra's power to rescue and deliver beings both seen and unseen is most inconceivable. Fearing that future students might not know this, I have recorded these details in full to transmit them to those not yet awakened.

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Bhagavān was in Śrāvastī, dwelling in the Jetavana, the garden of Anāthapiṇḍada, together with a great assembly of twelve hundred and fifty bhikṣus, and also with twelve thousand great bodhisattva-monks.

At that time, in the Heaven of the Thirty-Three, in the Hall of Good Dharma, there was a deva named Supratiṣṭhita. Together with the great devas, he toured the celestial gardens, and together with the great devas, he enjoyed supreme honor and nobility. Surrounded in front and behind by celestial maidens, with various kinds of music, they entertained one another and experienced every kind of happiness.

At that time, the deva Supratiṣṭhita, during the night watch, heard a voice saying: "Deva Supratiṣṭhita, after seven days your life will come to an end. After death, you will be born in Jambudvīpa and take the body of an animal seven times in succession. Then you will undergo the sufferings of hell. After escaping from hell, you will rarely obtain a human body, and you will be born into a poor and lowly family. While still in the mother's womb, you will have no eyes."

At that time, the deva Supratiṣṭhita, hearing this voice, was greatly alarmed and terrified. His hair stood on end. Overcome with grief and without joy, he rushed to the presence of Śakra Devānām Indra. Weeping and crying out in fear and distress, having no recourse, he bowed at Indra's feet and addressed him: "Hear what I say. I and the celestial maidens were gathered together experiencing every happiness when I heard a voice saying: 'Deva Supratiṣṭhita, after seven days your life will come to an end. After death, you will be born in Jambudvīpa and take the body of an animal seven times in succession. After those seven lives, you will fall into the various hells. After escaping from hell, you will rarely obtain a human body, and you will be born into a poor and lowly family without eyes.' Lord of the Devas, how can I be freed from this suffering?"

At that time, Indra, hearing the words of the deva Supratiṣṭhita, was greatly astonished. He thought to himself: "What seven successive bodies in the evil destinies will this deva Supratiṣṭhita undergo?" Then Indra sat still for a moment, entered concentration, and with discerning contemplation he saw that Supratiṣṭhita would undergo seven successive bodies in the evil destinies: a pig, a dog, a jackal, a monkey, a python, a crow, and a vulture, all eating foul and impure filth. When Indra saw that the deva Supratiṣṭhita was to fall into seven successive bodies in the evil destinies, he was deeply distressed, his heart as if pierced. He reflected: "There is no recourse. To whom can I turn for refuge? Only the Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Samyaksaṃbuddha can free Supratiṣṭhita from this suffering."

At that time, Indra, at the first watch of that same night, took various flower garlands, perfumed ointments, powdered incense, and fine celestial garments, adorned and held them, and went to the Jetavana grove. He arrived before the World-Honored One, bowed at the Buddha's feet, circumambulated seven times to the right, and made vast offerings before the Buddha. He then knelt before the Buddha and addressed him: "World-Honored One, how is it that the deva Supratiṣṭhita will undergo seven successive animal bodies in the evil destinies?" He recounted the matter in full as described above.

At that time, from the crown of the Tathāgata there radiated various kinds of light, pervading all the worlds of the ten directions. The light then returned, circled the Buddha three times, and entered through the Buddha's mouth. The Buddha smiled and addressed Indra: "Lord of the Devas, there is a dhāraṇī called the Tathāgata Buddhōṣṇīṣa Vijaya. It can purify all evil destinies, can purify and remove all the suffering of birth and death, and can purify and remove the sufferings of all hells, the realm of King Yama, and the animal realm. It can also destroy all hells and turn beings toward the good path.

"Lord of the Devas, if anyone hears this Buddhōṣṇīṣa Vijaya Dhāraṇī even once, all the evil karma of the hells created in previous lives will be entirely eliminated. That person will attain a purified body, and wherever they are born, they will remember and never forget. From one buddha-field to another, from one heavenly realm to another, passing through all the thirty-three heavens, wherever they are born, they will remember and never forget.

"Lord of the Devas, if at the moment when a person's life is about to end, they bring this dhāraṇī to mind even for an instant, their lifespan will be restored and increased. They will attain purity of body, speech, and mind. The body will be free from pain and suffering. According to their merit and blessings, wherever they dwell they will be at peace. They will be observed by all tathāgatas, constantly attended by all celestial spirits, respected by all people, with evil obstacles eliminated, and protected by all bodhisattvas with one heart.

"Lord of the Devas, if anyone can even briefly read and recite this dhāraṇī, all the sufferings of the hells, animal realm, the realm of King Yama, and the hungry ghost realm that belong to that person will be shattered and eliminated without remainder. All the buddha-fields, all the celestial palaces, and all the gates where bodhisattvas dwell will be without obstruction, and one may enter them at will."

At that time, Indra addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One, we humbly ask the Tathāgata to teach sentient beings the method for increasing lifespan."

At that time, the World-Honored One, knowing what was in Indra's mind and his desire to hear the Buddha teach the dhāraṇī Dharma, then spoke the dhāraṇī:

*Namo bhagavate trailokya-prativiśiṣṭāya buddhāya bhagavate. Tadyathā: oṃ viśodhaya sama-samantāvabhāsa-spharaṇa-gati-gahana-svabhāva-viśuddhe abhiṣiñcatu-sugata-varacana amṛta-abhiṣeke mahā-mantra-pade āhara āhara āyuḥ-sandhāraṇi śodhaya śodhaya gagana-viśuddhe uṣṇīṣa-vijaya-viśuddhe sahasra-raśmi-sañcodite sarva-tathāgata-avalokani ṣaṭ-pāramitā-paripūraṇi sarva-tathāgata-hṛdaya-adhiṣṭhāna-adhiṣṭhite mahā-mudre vajra-kāya-saṃhatana-viśuddhe sarva-āvaraṇa-viśuddhe prati-nivartaya āyuḥ-śuddhe samaya-adhiṣṭhite maṇi maṇi mahā-maṇi tathatā-bhūta-koṭi-pariśuddhe visphuṭa-buddhi-śuddhe jaya jaya vijaya vijaya smara sarva-buddha-adhiṣṭhita-śuddhe vajri vajra-garbhe vajraṃ bhavatu mama śarīraṃ sarva-sattvānāṃ ca kāya-viśuddhe sarva-gati-pariśuddhe sarva-tathāgata-samāśvāsa-yantu sarva-tathāgata-samāśvāsa-adhiṣṭhite buddhi buddhi vibuddhi bodhaya bodhaya vibodhaya vibodhaya samanta-pariśuddhe sarva-tathāgata-hṛdaya-adhiṣṭhāna-adhiṣṭhite mahā-mudre svāhā*

The Buddha told Indra: "This dhāraṇī is called 'Purifying All Evil Destinies, the Buddhōṣṇīṣa Vijaya Dhāraṇī.' It can remove all transgressions, karmic obstacles, and the like. It can destroy all the sufferings of the foul evil destinies.

"Lord of the Devas, this great dhāraṇī was proclaimed together by eighty-eight Ganges-sand koṭis of hundreds of thousands of buddhas, who rejoiced in, received, and upheld it. Mahāvairocana Tathāgata sealed it with his wisdom-seal. It is for the purpose of destroying the sufferings of the foul evil destinies of all sentient beings, for the liberation of all sentient beings in the hells, animal realm, and the realm of King Yama, for the liberation of sentient beings in dire distress who have fallen into the ocean of birth and death, and for the benefit of sentient beings who are short-lived, lacking in merit, without protector, and who delight in creating various defiled evil karma.

"Furthermore, this dhāraṇī, through its sustaining power in Jambudvīpa, can liberate sentient beings in the hells and evil destinies, beings of slight merit who undergo various kinds of cyclic birth and death, and beings who do not believe in good and evil karma and have lost the right path."

The Buddha told the Lord of the Devas: "I have taught this dhāraṇī and entrust it to you. You should transmit it to the deva Supratiṣṭhita. You should further receive, uphold, read, recite, contemplate, cherish, remember, and make offerings to it. In Jambudvīpa, broadly proclaim this dhāraṇī-seal to all sentient beings. Also for the sake of all devas, I have taught this dhāraṇī-seal and entrust it to you. Lord of the Devas, you should guard and protect it well. Do not let it be forgotten or lost.

"Lord of the Devas, if anyone hears this dhāraṇī even briefly, all the evil karma of grave obstacles accumulated over a thousand kalpas, which should cause them to undergo various kinds of cyclic birth and death in the hells, as hungry ghosts, as animals, in the realm of King Yama, with an asura body, as yakṣas, rākṣasas, ghosts, spirits, pūtanas, kaṭapūtanas, apasmāras, mosquitoes, gadflies, turtles, dogs, pythons, various birds, ferocious beasts, and all manner of crawling and creeping creatures down to the body of an ant, they will never again receive such bodies. They will immediately attain a transfer of birth, born in the same assembly as the tathāgatas of all buddhas, or with bodhisattvas of the stage of one more birth. Or they will be born into a great brahmin family, or into a great kṣatriya family, or into the most eminent and noble family.

"Lord of the Devas, the reason that such a person is born in these exalted places is entirely because of hearing this dhāraṇī. The transfer of their birth-places will all be purified. Lord of the Devas, even reaching the most supreme place of the Bodhi site is all due to praising the merits of this dhāraṇī.

"Thus, Lord of the Devas, this dhāraṇī is called 'Auspicious, Purifying All Evil Destinies.' This Buddhōṣṇīṣa Vijaya Dhāraṇī is like a sun-treasury maṇi jewel, pure and without blemish, pure as space, with brilliant radiance illuminating everywhere without exception. If sentient beings uphold this dhāraṇī, it is just like this: like Jambudvīpa gold, bright, pure, and soft, delightful to see, unstained by foul impurities. Lord of the Devas, if sentient beings uphold this dhāraṇī, it is just like this. Borne by this wholesome purity, they are born in the good destinies.

"Lord of the Devas, wherever this dhāraṇī abides, if one is able to write it out, circulate it, receive, uphold, read, recite, listen to, and make offerings to it, then all evil destinies are purified and all the sufferings of the hells are entirely eliminated."

The Buddha told the Lord of the Devas: "If anyone is able to write out this dhāraṇī and place it atop a high banner, or on a high mountain, or atop a tower, or even within a stūpa, Lord of the Devas, then if any bhikṣu, bhikṣuṇī, upāsaka, upāsikā, man, or woman of good family sees the banner or comes near it, or if its shadow falls upon their body, or if the wind blows the dust from the dhāraṇī banner onto their body, Lord of the Devas, all the evil karma of those sentient beings that should have led to the evil destinies, the hells, the animal realm, the realm of King Yama, the hungry ghost realm, or the sufferings of an asura body in the evil destinies, they will not undergo any of these, nor will they be stained by the filth of transgression. Lord of the Devas, these sentient beings will receive the prediction of all buddhas and will all attain non-retrogression from anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi.

"Lord of the Devas, how much more so if, with abundant offerings of flower garlands, perfumed ointments, powdered incense, banners, pennants, canopies, garments, and necklaces, one adorns a stūpa at a crossroads where four roads meet, places the dhāraṇī within it, and with palms joined in reverence circumambulates it in practice, taking refuge and bowing in homage. Lord of the Devas, one who can make such offerings is called a mahāsattva, a true son of the Buddha, a pillar of the Dharma, and this is indeed a stūpa containing the complete relics of the Tathāgata."

At that time, King Yama the Dharma-King came to the Buddha's presence during the night. Upon arriving, with various celestial garments, wondrous flowers, perfumed ointments, and adornments, he made offerings to the Buddha and circumambulated seven times, bowed at the Buddha's feet, and said: "I have heard that the Tathāgata has proclaimed and praised this great and powerful dhāraṇī, and therefore I have come to study it. If anyone receives, upholds, reads, and recites this dhāraṇī, I shall always follow and protect that person, never allowing the upholder to fall into the hells, for I guard and keep them in mind because they follow the Tathāgata's teaching."

At that time, the Four Great Heavenly Kings who guard the world circumambulated the Buddha three times and addressed him: "World-Honored One, we humbly ask the Tathāgata to broadly teach us the method of upholding this dhāraṇī."

At that time, the Buddha addressed the Four Heavenly Kings: "Listen well now! I shall teach you the method of receiving and upholding this dhāraṇī. I also teach it for the sake of sentient beings with short lives.

"One should first bathe and don fresh clean garments. On the full moon day, the fifteenth of the bright half of the month, observe the precepts and recite this dhāraṇī a full one thousand times. Sentient beings with short lives will thereby have their lifespan restored and increased, forever freed from the suffering of illness. All karmic obstacles will be entirely eliminated, and all the sufferings of the hells will likewise be resolved. If any bird, animal, or creature with consciousness hears this dhāraṇī even once, for the remainder of this life it will never again receive such a body."

The Buddha said: "If a person encounters a great and severe illness and hears this dhāraṇī, they will be forever freed from it. All diseases will also be eliminated. If they were destined for the evil paths, those too will be severed. They will be reborn in the tranquil world. After this body, they will never again take birth from a womb. Wherever they are born, they will be born from a lotus, and in every birth they will remember and never forget, always recalling their past lives."

The Buddha said: "If a person has previously committed all manner of extremely grave evil deeds and then comes to the end of life, borne by this evil karma and destined for the hells, the animal realm, the realm of King Yama, the hungry ghost realm, or even the Great Avīci Hell, or birth in the water, or birth as a bird or beast of another species, then take a bone from the body of the deceased and a handful of earth, recite this dhāraṇī twenty-one times, and scatter it upon the bones of the deceased. That person will immediately be reborn in heaven."

The Buddha said: "If a person is able to recite this dhāraṇī twenty-one times daily, all the vast offerings of the world will be repaid, and at death one will be reborn in the Land of Ultimate Bliss. If one constantly recites and maintains it, one attains great nirvāṇa, further increases one's lifespan, and receives supreme happiness. After leaving this body, one will be reborn in various wondrous and exquisite buddha-fields, always in the same assembly as all buddhas. All world-honored ones will constantly teach them the subtle and wondrous meaning. All world-honored ones will bestow predictions upon them, and their bodily radiance will illuminate all buddha-fields."

The Buddha said: "When reciting this dhāraṇī Dharma, before the Buddha, first take pure earth and make an altar of whatever size, square with four corners. Scatter various grasses and flowers upon the altar and burn various renowned incenses. With the right knee on the ground in the kneeling posture, the mind always mindful of the Buddha, form the dhāraṇī mudrā: curl the index fingers and press them with the thumbs, palms joined at the heart. Recite this dhāraṇī one hundred and eight times. Upon the altar, like a rain king pouring down flowers, one can universally make offerings to eighty-eight koṭis of Ganges-sand nayutas of hundreds of thousands of buddhas. Those world-honored buddhas will all praise with one voice: 'Excellent! How rare! A true son of the Buddha!' One then attains the unobstructed wisdom samādhi and the great bodhicitta adornment samādhi. This is how one should uphold this dhāraṇī Dharma."

The Buddha told the Lord of the Devas: "By this skillful means, all sentient beings who should fall into the path of the hells are enabled to gain liberation. All evil destinies are likewise purified. Furthermore, the upholder's lifespan is increased. Lord of the Devas, go now and take my dhāraṇī and transmit it to the deva Supratiṣṭhita. After seven full days, come with Supratiṣṭhita together to see me."

At that time, the Lord of the Devas received this dhāraṇī Dharma from the World-Honored One, upheld it, and returned to his own heaven, where he transmitted it to the deva Supratiṣṭhita. When the deva Supratiṣṭhita had received this dhāraṇī, after six full days and nights, upholding it according to the Dharma, all his wishes were fulfilled. All the sufferings of the evil destinies that he should have undergone were immediately resolved. He abided in the path of awakening, his lifespan increased immeasurably. Overjoyed, he exclaimed in a loud voice: "How rare the Tathāgata! How rare the wondrous Dharma! How rare the clear proof! Exceedingly difficult to obtain, it has liberated me!"

At that time, on the seventh day, Indra and the deva Supratiṣṭhita led the celestial assembly, solemnly bearing flower garlands, perfumed ointments, powdered incense, jeweled banners, pennants, canopies, celestial garments, and necklaces, wondrously adorned. They went to the Buddha's presence and made a great offering. They offered fine celestial garments and various necklaces to the World-Honored One, circumambulated a hundred thousand times, and stood before the Buddha. Leaping with joy, they sat and listened to the Dharma.

At that time, the World-Honored One extended his golden-colored arm and touched the crown of the deva Supratiṣṭhita, and taught him the Dharma, and bestowed upon him the prediction of awakening. The Buddha said: "This sūtra is called 'Purifying All Evil Destinies, the Buddhōṣṇīṣa Vijaya Dhāraṇī.' You should receive and uphold it."

At that time, the great assembly, having heard the Dharma, rejoiced, accepted it with faith, and put it into practice.
