# 佛說鉢蘭那賒嚩哩大陀羅尼經
## The Buddha's Teaching: Great Dhāraṇī Sūtra of Praṇaśvari

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1384

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmadeva (法賢).

---

At that time the World-Honored One addressed Ānanda: "I observe that in the world, innumerable sentient beings create much evil karma and fall into the evil destinies, where they pass immeasurable time before they can escape. Even if they are born as humans, their limbs and bodies are incomplete. And even if they are born complete, they have meager merit and little wisdom, and evil māras and evil ghosts and spirits throughout day and night watch for opportunities to afflict and harm them in various ways, so that they find no peace. I have compassion for them, and therefore teach the Great Dhāraṇī of Praṇaśvari to ward off evil māras and evil ghosts and spirits. If any sentient being hears this dhāraṇī, arouses a mind of utmost sincerity, receives, upholds, reads, recites, makes offerings to it, and reverently honors it, then all evil māras and evil ghosts and spirits will depart far away. Calamities and harms will be eliminated. Epidemics will not arise. The place where they dwell will be peaceful, secure, and joyful." Then the World-Honored One spoke the dhāraṇī:

*Namo ratna-trayāya. Namo ārya-amitābhāya tathāgatāya arhate samyak-saṃbuddhāya. Namo ārya-avalokiteśvarāya bodhisattvāya mahāsattvāya mahā-kāruṇikāya. Namo mahā-sthāma-prāpta-patāya bodhisattvāya mahāsattvāya. Vāma-nītu namaḥ śamitān namaḥ śamī vāma-nī piśāci praṇaśvari praṇaśvari bhagavatī pāśa-praśuddhāriṇi. Yāni-kāni cad-ubhayāni yutpattenti yākāci-nit yakāci-tumā ye-ke-ci-duppat-rāva ye-ke-ci-dupāya-sā ye-ke-ci-dabi-saṃbhaya ye-ke-ci-dupasarga upasarga saṃvādhava utpattenti. Sarva-nitāni sarva-satā sarvī-tīvara-tā imu utpattenti. Na-pa-nī-ta tad-de-nā saste-nā saste-vā-ke-nā. I-vi-pa-nī-ta diṣṭa-nai mahā-mantra-padāṃ. Jaja jaja mama saparivāra-śa. Rauṃ kṣa huru yuk-pa ṭiṃ paripālanā śānti. Su-siddhe-ya-nā huru. Tadyathā: oṃ amṛte māśe. Sadaṅgi-ma māra māra śama praśama upaśama. Dhanu vidhanu dhure dhumūre svāhā. Oṃ praṇaśvari svāhā. Namaḥ śavarāṇāṃ mahā-śavarāṇāṃ. Piśāci praṇaśvari piśāci. Aśane māśane svāhā. Oṃ piśāci svāhā. Aṃ kūr mā-kūr praṇaśvari svāhā. Namaḥ śavarāṇāṃ piśāci praṇaśvari piśāci svāhā.*

At that time Ānanda, having heard the World-Honored One speak this dhāraṇī, addressed him: "World-Honored One! May you have no concern. I shall remember and uphold this and propagate it in the world, enabling all sentient beings to obtain great benefit." Having spoken these words, he bowed his head to the World-Honored One's feet, rejoiced, and withdrew.
