# 佛說大金色孔雀王呪經

## The Great Golden Peacock King Dhāraṇī Sūtra Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0987

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown.

---

Homage to the Buddha. Homage to the Dharma. Homage to the Bhikṣu Saṅgha. Homage to the seven buddhas of right and perfect awakening. Homage to the pratyekabuddhas. Homage to all arhats. Homage to Maitreya and all bodhisattvas. Homage to all anāgāmins, homage to sakṛdāgāmins, homage to srotāpannas. Homage to those in the world who have right faith and aspiration.

Having paid reverence to that assembly, I wish to expound the Great Peacock King Dhāraṇī Sūtra. May all wishes be fulfilled auspiciously!

All ghosts and spirits, listen clearly! Whether dwelling in the sky, on the earth, or in the water: devas, nāgas, asuras, marutas, garuḍas, gandharvas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, yakṣas, rākṣasas, hungry ghosts, piśācas, bhūtas, kumbhāṇḍas, pūtanas, kaṭapūtanas, gandhas, unmaḍas, cāyas, apasmāras, and uttarakas, listen to what I declare!

Ghosts that feed on various things: ghosts that consume breath, ghosts that consume sugar, ghosts that consume blood, ghosts that consume marrow, ghosts that consume flesh, ghosts that consume fat, ghosts that consume fetuses, ghosts that consume life-force, ghosts that consume strength, ghosts that consume hair, ghosts that consume flowers, ghosts that consume sounds, ghosts that consume scent, ghosts that consume ill will, ghosts that consume secret plotting, ghosts of harmful intent, and all ghosts and spirits who love to take the lives of others: listen! I wish to expound this Great Peacock King Dhāraṇī Sūtra. I now present incense, flowers, food, and drink for you all to receive. May your harmful intentions, your secret plotting, your intent to come and harm, your consumption of incense and expulsion of foul breath all be set aside. With compassionate minds, wholesome minds, and minds of faith in the Buddha, the Dharma, and the Bhikṣu Saṅgha, listen to what I declare!

The fourteen rākṣasī are: first, named Darkness; second, named Maker-of-Darkness; third, named Kumbhāṇḍī; fourth, named White-Visage; fifth, named Flower-Eye; sixth, named Child-Taker; seventh, named Hair-Taker; eighth, named Maker-of-Yellow; ninth, named Drooping; tenth, named Extremely-Drooping; eleventh, named Watching-for-Opportunity; twelfth, named Yama's-Messenger; thirteenth, named Yama's-Rākṣasī; fourteenth, named Flesh-Eater. You all, receive my flowers, incense, food, and drink! Protect such-and-such a person and their family. May my dhāraṇī words be fulfilled auspiciously!

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was dwelling in the state of Śrāvastī, in the Jeta Grove, in Anāthapiṇḍada's park. At that time in the park there was a young bhikṣu named Maṅgala, who had not long left the household life. He was chopping firewood for the saṅgha's bath-water. There was an unusual piece of wood beneath which a black serpent emerged and bit the bhikṣu on the toe. He collapsed unconscious to the ground, his eyes rolling back and foam coming from his mouth.

At that time the Elder Ānanda saw that Bhikṣu Maṅgala's condition was extremely grave. Alarmed, he went to the Buddha, touched his forehead to the ground in prostration, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! Here in Śrāvastī, in the Jeta Grove, in Anāthapiṇḍada's park, there is a newly ordained bhikṣu named Maṅgala. While chopping firewood for the saṅgha's bath-water, a black serpent emerged from beneath an unusual piece of wood and bit the bhikṣu on his right toe. He has collapsed unconscious, his eyes rolling back and foam coming from his mouth. How can he be treated?"

The Buddha then told Ānanda: "You should take the Tathāgata's Great Peacock King Dhāraṇī Sūtra and use it to protect Bhikṣu Maṅgala. Bind the boundary and bind the dhāraṇī for him, so that poison cannot harm him, weapons cannot touch him, and all afflictions are completely removed. Whether caused by devas, nāgas, marutas, garuḍas, gandharvas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, yakṣas, rākṣasas, hungry ghosts, piśācas, bhūtas, kumbhāṇḍas, pūtanas, black pūtanas, gandhas, unmaḍas, cāyas, apasmāras, uttarakas, kīṭas, karmaṇas, khakkhudas, kīlaṇas, viṭaḍas, jīcas, or vaiśākas; whether from improper eating or improper vomiting, improper looking or improper lifting, improper transgressing or improper touching; whether from fevers of one day, two days, three days, four days, five days, six days, or seven days, of half a month or one month, occurring once a day, once at a given hour, or at intervals; whether from ghostly fever, from disease caused by wind, fire, or water, from cholera fever, from half-body pain, headache, eye pain, face pain, jaw pain, throat pain, neck pain, ear pain, toothache, mouth pain, heart pain, back pain, abdominal pain, waist pain, genital pain, thigh pain, arm and hand pain, foot pain, or joint pain: may all pains be completely removed and healed. Protect such-and-such a person."

He spoke this gāthā-dhāraṇī:

"Safe by day, safe by night, safe always in between. Safe and secure day and night, protected by all buddhas."

Then he spoke the dhāraṇī:

[Main Peacock King Dhāraṇī]

*Iṭi biṭi kiṭi ghiṭi bhāṭi niṭi aṭi kaṭi ṭukkaṭi aṭi pāṅkuṭi bhāsu piśāci-nī alohani bālohani ūlohani ile mile tile tile mile tile ṭūle ṭuṭūle ici mici vilede vimāli hulū hulū aśe-bhake kaṭi mahā-kaṭi bhāki nāki jiku-lū kulū-da pha-lū kūlū kūlū dūsā-ḍambha dū-ḍambha ḍū-ma-ḍambha guṇāya pirāya hesū hesū heli heli mili mili tili tili cūlū cūlū mauhū mauhū mauhū mauhū maulū maulū maulū maulū maulū hū hū hū hū hū hū hū hū hū bhāhū bhā bhā bhā bhā bhā bhā bhā bhā bhā jābhā jārā-jā jārā-jā jārā-mādha mādha nidā-bhā dā-bhā ni-sā-bhā sā-bhā rāra-ni cen-ḍū-bhi ka-jā-ni baṣa-ni bhūṭa-ni bhāca-ni kaṃbha-ni mādha-ni mānci-ci ka-mā ka-li śāli-ka-kali śā-ka sāli bhāra-ni ṭumā ṭumā-li bhāji mi-ku-rā yā bhāli vi-rā ya baṣa-ṭū ti-bhā-sa maṇ-ṭū ti-bhā-sa maṇ-ṭi-nā iki-li sī svāhā*

"This Great Peacock King Dhāraṇī Sūtra, if recited in the morning for self-protection, brings safety throughout the day. If recited in the evening for self-protection, brings safety throughout the night."

Then he spoke the dhāraṇī:

[Night-protection Dhāraṇī]

*Hū hū hū hū hū hū nāgāli-lī-ḍambhā rīrī-lī vi-mārī rīlī hvīya hvīya vijā vijā ṭūbi kūbi ilā milā igārā-mī ili milā ili mi-tili ci-li mili ici-li heli tili mi-tili sūli mi-tili ṭusu ṭu-kūrā ci-rā cā-bhārā vi-rā-cā ici-li vici-li cili namaḥ arhate arhadara baṣa-ṭū ti-bha samantinaḥ*

"Ānanda! I now speak for you the heart dhāraṇī of the Great Peacock King."

Then he spoke the dhāraṇī:

[Heart Dhāraṇī of the Great Peacock King]

*Ici mici tili pili mici mili timi sūḍambhā ṭūbhā sūbhā ciliki vīya vinā mici namo buddhā nā ciliki sī bhāda mūli ici hārā-lū heda mūrā ṭūbhā ambā kūci kūku nāci kūnāci ci-rā kūjā nāci aṭa baṭṭa yānā bhāmā sā-dha śāmā sidi ili mili kili mili khilā milā khiṭū mūle ṭuṭumā sa-ṭū mili dāli mi-sa-phū bhādi phū-sadi phū-sadi ilā-bhā siṭā-bhā khinā kārā nārā-mā kili milā giḍrā-mā kele i-di sa-jāle ṭūbi ṭūbi adi bhārā nādi anā nanā-ṭi baṣa-ṭū ti-bhāya śandā khinā nārā ghiṭi bhārā ghiṭi hāli-dāli kū-dāli ili mili ki-tili mi-di ili mi-siṭaṃ ṭū-dhārā vi-dha-sā svāhā. Dhāraṇī words fulfilled auspiciously.*

"Ānanda! This is called the heart of the Great Peacock King Dhāraṇī."

"Ānanda! This Great Peacock King Dhāraṇī was also taught in accordance by Maitreya Bodhisattva."

Then he spoke the dhāraṇī:

[Maitreya's Dhāraṇī]

*Śilī rī-śili-śili bhādi diśu-śu ci bhādile hāle hāle rīli arī-nidi śā-bhāle śibi-ṭārā-ṭārā bhaṇi bhaṇi bodhi bodhi bodhi bodhi saṭū-bha bodhi bhāli cāra śiye svāhā*

"Ānanda! This Great Peacock King Dhāraṇī was also taught in accordance by Brahmā, the Lord of the Sahā World."

Then he spoke the dhāraṇī:

[Brahmā's Dhāraṇī]

*Hele hele hāle rīlī mi-māli-nī bhā-kuli kili kili kili kili kili kili di brahmā māye kūlaṇḍa khivi ṭuhā hūlū hūlū hūlū hūlū svāhā*

"Ānanda! This Great Peacock King Dhāraṇī was also taught in accordance by Śakra, Lord of the Devas."

Then he spoke the dhāraṇī:

[Śakra's Dhāraṇī]

*Dhāra-jandūle māra-jandūle cāci-jandūle mahā-nīga bhāni-ga-dā-nī heli-śili-jūdi-śili da-liū da-liū nāsī he ha ha ha ha sīhe cidi vi-lyādi kūlū kūlū bhārā-śe ṭuṭa ṭuṭa sī-bhāṭa bhāṭa sī sili sili kabīle kabīrā mūle hāhe. Hūṃ! May all misfortune and evil be completely subdued! Bind their hands, feet, and joints so they cannot move! Up to the Thirty-Three Heavens! Uccāri-śurā bhadī bhāśi-rā bhāśi-rā bhāśi-rā bhāśi-rā bhadī svāhā*

"Ānanda! Receive also the names and titles of the ancient ṛṣis who came forth in the past, who achieved perfect accomplishment, accomplished aspiration, and accomplished mantra-craft. They constantly practiced austerities and dwelled in mountain forests. They had sovereign power and swift magical transformation, the five supernatural powers, could travel through the air at will, and moved through space as they wished. I now declare their names and titles.

"The ṛṣi named Aṣṭa-vimukta, the ṛṣi named Jya, the ṛṣi named Jya-deva, the ṛṣi named Pāli, the ṛṣi named Śukladāna, the ṛṣi named Sunanda, the ṛṣi named Prāvṛta-vāsin, the ṛṣi named Kaśyapa, the ṛṣi named Vṛddha-Kaśyapa, the ṛṣi named Bhrūkuṭi, the ṛṣi named Giri-dhara, the ṛṣi named Ānanda, the ṛṣi named Nāma, the ṛṣi named Pūrva, the ṛṣi named Mṛga-śīrṣa, the ṛṣi named Jālā, the ṛṣi named Dvipakva, the ṛṣi named Kṛṣṇa-dvipakva, the ṛṣi named Cayanā, the ṛṣi named Kṣānti, the ṛṣi named Yaśas, the ṛṣi named Nau, the ṛṣi named Kapota, the ṛṣi named Aśva, the ṛṣi named Hima, the ṛṣi named Raktākṣa, the ṛṣi named Duṣkara, the ṛṣi named Vijayaśaya, the ṛṣi named Kavaca, the ṛṣi named Śarat, the ṛṣi named Śukra, the ṛṣi named Saṃvatsara, the ṛṣi named Avivāda, the ṛṣi named Rātri-cara, the ṛṣi named Prabuddha, the ṛṣi named Bandhu-aṅguli, the ṛṣi named Gandhahasta, the ṛṣi named Ekaśṛṅga, the ṛṣi named Ṛṣyaśṛṅga, the ṛṣi named Gālgulya, the ṛṣi named Daṇḍāyana, the ṛṣi named Śīrṣa, the ṛṣi named Vibhājya, the ṛṣi named Kapila, the ṛṣi named Gautama, the ṛṣi named Lohitāśva, the ṛṣi named Romakūṭa, the ṛṣi named Nirānanda, and the ṛṣi named Kinīru.

"Ānanda! These are the ancient ṛṣis who came forth in the past. They composed the four Vedas, accomplished all that they intended, were upright in practice, strict and swift, and possessed great power. All their undertakings were brought to completion. They too used this Great Peacock King Dhāraṇī Sūtra to protect such-and-such a person, granting a lifespan of a hundred years, to see a hundred autumns."

Then he spoke the dhāraṇī:

[Ṛṣi Dhāraṇī]

*Hāli hāli hāli ṭhili mili sūli sūli heli heli mili mili dā-phū dā-ṭā-phū ka-bhāni mādha-ni dā-hāni ka-dāni bhācani bha-dāni hānani rāca rāca rā-ni bhaṭa-ni mahā-ni ḍambha-ni jābha-ni svāhā*

"The Four Heavenly Kings and all the great spirit-kings also use the Peacock King Dhāraṇī to protect such-and-such a person, granting a lifespan of a hundred years, to see a hundred autumns."

Then he spoke the dhāraṇī:

[Four Heavenly Kings' Dhāraṇī]

*Akādi akādi vanrāni vanrāni arani hū-khī hū-khī bhūkhī bhūkhī haṭa haṭa haṭa haṭa haṭa haṭa haṭa haṭa haṭa haṭa haṭa. Subdue all enemies!*

*Dāha dāha dāha dāha dāha dāha dāha dāha dāha dāha. Whoever harbors ill thoughts toward such-and-such a person, punish them all!*

*Bhāṭa bhāṭa bhāṭa bhāṭa bhāṭa bhāṭa bhāṭa bhāṭa bhāṭa bhāṭa. Whoever seeks to take advantage, punish them all!*

*Ḍū ḍū ḍū ḍū ḍū ḍū ḍū ḍū ḍū ḍū. Extinguish all enmity and evil!*

*Ha ha ha ha ha ha ha ha ha ha. Jādi jādi jādi jādi jādi jādi jādi jādi jādi jādi. Extinguish all enemies!*

*Cūlū cūlū cūlū cūlū cūlū cūlū cūlū cūlū cūlū cūlū. Heli heli heli heli heli heli heli heli heli heli. Mili mili mili mili mili mili mili mili mili mili. Hūlū hūlū hūlū hūlū hūlū hūlū hūlū hūlū hūlū hūlū. Cici cici cici cici cici cici cici cici cici ci-cī he-khī mi-khī ci-khī mā-khī.*

"Virtuous and auspicious, supremely virtuous, accomplishing all things. Stainless, pure, wondrous. Candragupta. Sūryakānta. Ḍāmbiśā. Ḍāmbi-vīṃ. Kāle. Protect such-and-such a person, granting a lifespan of a hundred years, to see a hundred autumns."

Then he spoke the dhāraṇī:

Concluding dhāraṇī following the sūtra's exposition:

*Akārā arunan-ridu ani-nandalū yathā sa-ḍaṃ kā-śa-ni ci-lū rā ani-ha ani-ha jūlu soṣu idi mūsī ci ḍoṃ bha-ṭū ḍoṃ-bha adi hī-vā ci bi-bi na-mū mūci namo sa-ḍan-samya-buddhānaṃ hīci jāci ṭhī-kiṃ-mā da mūrā uṭu-mā-śa mā ṭumā ali-kūlī di-dā kū-kinā bhāli*

*Whoever breaks the precepts and harasses others, may the ten devas raise a great flood so they find no place to dwell. Such-and-such a person. Hāli-kā. ## The Sūtra for Repelling Thieves Spoken by the Buddha

Homage to the Buddha. Homage to the Dharma. Homage to the Bhikṣu Saṅgha. Homage to all seven buddhas of the past. Homage to all disciples of the Buddha. Homage to the spirit-king Lipeili.

Having paid reverence thus, I speak this dhāraṇī sūtra, which is fulfilled according to one's wishes.

In the north there is a mountain called Gandhara Mahāyāna. Upon it dwells a spirit named Lipeili. He has four spirit-disciples: Andani, who causes forgetfulness in thieves; Bimani, who causes thieves to sit in stupor; Candamani, who causes thieves to be hastily reborn; and Lipracchani, who renders thieves dull-witted. Thus Ūlāri-dā-ṭi-mā, Bī-kāli: strike the mouths and teeth of thieves!

This is not released until the cord is untied. Protect such-and-such a person and ward off all misfortune and evil. May those who would harm or steal all be extinguished and made invisible!

When first reciting this Sūtra for Repelling Thieves, on the twenty-ninth day of the month, light a lamp and burn glue-incense for Hāritī-yā. Light seven lamps and scatter loose flowers for Ībhū. Light seven lamps for the Mother of Demons. Recite seven times before the Mother of Demons, and it will be auspicious. When the title of Hāli-lī, the King of Demon Children, brings merit, light seven lamps and burn incense and scatter flowers.

## The Lotus Sūtra Divine Dhāraṇī Sūtra

*Anji manji māṇi māṇi jile jile cāli-di śāme śāli da-vī śāṃ-di mukdi mu-kda mu-kda-lī śāli avī-sāli saṅ-li sāli cā-ye a-ā jā-ni śān-di a-cā-ye śāli dhāraṇī a-lū-gā bhā-phā cā-vi-cā-ni ni vi-dī a-bin hūlā-ni li-dī a-lū da-bhāle śu-dā-ū-kūle a-rā-le bhā-rā-lī śū-kā-cā a-san-mā-san-li buddhā kivi-li kūdi da-mā bhā-li cā-di saṅ-gā ni-gū-śā-ni bhāśa bhāśa śu-dā man-dā-rā kṣa man-dā-rā kṣā ye-dā nā-lū-dā nā-lū-dā kyauśa lyā-kū akṣa-rā akṣa-ye da-ye a-bhā-lū a-mā-nā da ye*

"World-Honored One! This dhāraṇī was spoken by sixty-two Ganges-sands of buddhas. If anyone harms this Dharma teacher, it is as if they have harmed all those buddhas."

The dhāraṇī spoken by Yǒngshī:

*Jāle mahā-jāle ukki mukki arāle arā-bhadi nile nile dā-rā-di ici-ni vīci-ni nile ghaṃ nile aṃ-bhādi*

"World-Honored One! This dhāraṇī was spoken by Ganges-sands of buddhas."

The dhāraṇī spoken by Vaiśravaṇa, Heavenly King:

*Ari ṇari ṇū-ṇāri a-nā-lū-nā-li kūnāli*

The dhāraṇī spoken by Dhṛtarāṣṭra, Heavenly King:

*Akāni gāni gūli ghandāli caṇḍāli māḍaṃ-gī jāṅ-kūli phūlū-sā-ṇi aṃ-di*

"World-Honored One! This dhāraṇī was spoken by forty-two hundred million buddhas."

The dhāraṇī spoken by the rākṣasī:

*Iṭi-lī iṭi-min iṭi-lī aṭi-lī iṭi-lī min-lī min-lī min-lī min-lī min-lī lū-he lū-he lū-he lū-he da-he da-he ṭū-he ṇū-he*

"Rather let our heads be split into seven pieces, like the branches of an arjaka tree, than allow this Dharma teacher to be harassed!"

The dhāraṇī from the *Mahāparinirvāṇa Sūtra*, spoken by Māra Pāpīyas, King of the Sixth Heaven:

*Ṭuki ṭaṭṭā-rā ṭuki-lū hā-le mahā-lū hāli a-rā-cā rā da-rā sāhā*

At that time the World-Honored One accepted his divine dhāraṇī but did not accept his offerings.
