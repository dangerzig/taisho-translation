# 佛說四未曾有法經
## Sūtra on the Four Unprecedented Qualities Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0136

Translated from the Chinese. Western Jin dynasty, translated by Dharmarakṣa (竺法護)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Bhagavān was in the city of Śrāvastī, in the Jetavana, the garden of Anāthapiṇḍada. At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣus:

"A wheel-turning sage-king has four unprecedented qualities. What are the four?

"First, a wheel-turning sage-king is cherished and loved by all the people, never causing them harm. He is like a father to a child. The wheel-turning sage-king likewise cherishes and loves the people and never has anger toward them. He is like a father with his only child. This is the wheel-turning sage-king's first unprecedented quality.

"Second, when a wheel-turning sage-king travels among the people, all who see him are delighted, just as a child rejoices upon seeing its father. This is the wheel-turning sage-king's second unprecedented quality.

"Third, when a wheel-turning sage-king remains in one place and does not travel, those among the people who see him are all delighted. When the wheel-turning sage-king teaches the Dharma to the people, those who hear it are all delighted. The people, upon hearing the wheel-turning sage-king teach the Dharma, are never sated. This is the wheel-turning sage-king's third unprecedented quality.

"Fourth, when a wheel-turning sage-king is seated and does not travel, those among the people who see him are all delighted. The wheel-turning sage-king instructs the people: 'This should be done; this should not be done. This should be associated with; this should not be associated with. If you do this, through the long night you will gain merit without limit. If you do that, through the long night you will endure suffering without respite.' When the people hear such instructions from the wheel-turning sage-king, they are delighted without satiety. This is the wheel-turning sage-king's fourth unprecedented quality.

"Just so, the bhikṣu Ānanda also has four unprecedented qualities. What are the four?

"First, when the bhikṣu Ānanda enters the assembly of bhikṣus, all the bhikṣus who see him are delighted. When the bhikṣu Ānanda teaches the Dharma, all who hear it are delighted. The bhikṣus who hear what Ānanda teaches are never sated. This is the bhikṣu Ānanda's first unprecedented quality.

"Second, when the bhikṣu Ānanda silently enters the assembly of bhikṣuṇīs, all who see him are delighted. When the bhikṣu Ānanda teaches the Dharma, all who hear it are delighted. The assembly of bhikṣuṇīs, upon hearing Ānanda teach the Dharma, are never sated. This is the bhikṣu Ānanda's second unprecedented quality.

"Third, when the bhikṣu Ānanda silently goes to the upāsakas, the upāsakas who see him are all delighted. When the bhikṣu Ānanda teaches the Dharma, the assembly of upāsakas who hear what Ānanda teaches are never sated. This is the bhikṣu Ānanda's third unprecedented quality.

"Fourth, when the bhikṣu Ānanda silently enters the assembly of upāsikās, those who see him are all delighted. When the bhikṣu Ānanda teaches the Dharma, the upāsikās who hear it are never sated. This is the bhikṣu Ānanda's fourth unprecedented quality."

At that time, the bhikṣus, having heard what the Buddha taught, joyfully practiced in accordance.
