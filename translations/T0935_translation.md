# 極樂願文
## Prayer for Rebirth in Sukhāvatī

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0935

Translated from the Chinese. Qing dynasty, translated by Dalai Lama Gabchu Samdandarji (達喇嘛嘎卜楚薩木丹達爾吉)

---

> Rare One who, for the sake of beings, performs inexhaustible deeds of generosity,
> Whose mere recollection can cause the lord of death to retreat afar,
> Who constantly regards sentient beings as a parent regards an only child:
> Before you, World-Honored Amitāyus Buddha, I bow in reverence.

> In the successive lives of Śākyamuni,
> The supreme realm he repeatedly praised with virtuous words:
> The prayer for rebirth in Sukhāvatī,
> Out of compassion, I now speak with all my strength.
> Beings, burdened by the heavy darkness of ignorance of good and evil,
> Whose life as devas and humans is ravaged by the weapons of anger,
> Bound by craving in the prison of saṃsāra,
> Drifting upon the waters of karma into the ocean of the three existences,
> Tossed by the waves of sickness, old age, and suffering,
> Crushed into the jaws of the sea-monster, the lord of death,
> Constantly pressed by the burden of unwanted suffering,
> Crying out in lament without refuge or recourse.
> May he who witnesses the mind as companion
> Guide these wretched ones: none other than Amitābha,
> Avalokiteśvara, Mahāsthāmaprāpta,
> And their retinue! Before them I earnestly pray.
> For our sake, throughout many kalpas,
> You once made supreme vows; do not forget them!
> Like the wondrous Garuḍa lord traversing empty space,
> Descend by the power of your compassion and spiritual might!

I humbly pray: may the ocean of the two accumulations, the causes and conditions of self and others across the three times, be universally merged as a single force upon which to rely. May we, at the moment of death, directly see Amitābha coming to receive us, with the two great bodhisattvas and all the retinue appearing before us. At that time, may we generate the most devoted and sincere faith toward the Buddha and his retinue, without the agony of dying, and not lose our faith and sincerity, nor our mindfulness of the vision before us. At the moment of death, may the eight great bodhisattva-sons all come by spiritual transformation to point the way to Sukhāvatī, and following their guidance, may we proceed at once to the supreme realm of Sukhāvatī and be reborn from within a jeweled lotus as beings of the Mahāyāna lineage with the sharpest faculties.

At the moment of rebirth, may we obtain all the dhāraṇīs, all the meditations, the unconditioned mind of the Way, inexhaustible eloquence, and all the immeasurable supreme merits, fully and completely. May we then receive the teaching of the unsurpassed guide Amitābha Buddha, and may all the Buddhas and bodhisattvas of the ten directions be entirely delighted. May we receive the instructions of the Mahāyāna Dharma, and in an instant completely penetrate the meaning of all those teachings. Then, riding upon unobstructed spiritual transformations, may we go to the vast and expansive buddha-lands, extensively practice the wondrous deeds of the bodhisattva, and fulfill them all.

Having been reborn in the pure land, may we generate courageous and resolute compassion, and by unobstructed spiritual transformation, go especially to the impure lands. For all sentient beings, may we teach each one according to their own conditions and in accord with the Dharma. May we establish them all upon the pure path praised by all the Buddhas, universally practice the most rare and extraordinary deeds, swiftly bring all to completion, and benefit boundless sentient beings, so that all may easily attain the buddha-fruit.

> May I, at the moment of death, see Amitābha
> Surrounded by the ocean-assembly,
> Clearly and brightly manifest before my eyes,
> And may faith and compassion fill my heart.
> When the signs of the intermediate state appear,
> May the buddha-sons immediately guide and instruct me.
> Having been reborn in the pure land,
> May I return to transform the beings of the impure world.

If I have not yet been reborn in that supreme realm, may I, in life after life and world after world, constantly encounter the Buddha's teaching. Among all the sūtras and treatises, may I achieve realization through hearing, reflection, and cultivation, and obtain a pure body.

Having obtained that body, may I be adorned with the seven virtues of high birth and never be separated from them.

Thus, at all times, may I recall my previous lives and obtain the knowledge of past existences.

In life after life and world after world, whenever I see the three realms of existence, may my mind find no benefit in them. May I immediately generate the aspiration to seize the merits of liberation. Among all the Buddha's vinaya teachings, may I obtain ordination.

Having gone forth, may I be entirely undefiled by any evil transgression. May I perfectly uphold the precepts and practice to the gate of the ultimate. May I attain great bodhi and become like the great bhikṣu Akṣobhya Buddha.

Furthermore, in world after world and life after life, among all defiled and pure dharmas, may I be endowed with dhāraṇī that retains without loss the path of realization and completion as things truly are. May I obtain all of these in abundance.

When demonstrating to others what I have upheld, may I obtain pure and unobstructed eloquence.

Furthermore, in life after life and world after world, may I attain all the concentrations such as the *śūraṅgama*, the five eyes including the physical eye, and the six supernatural powers including miraculous travel, without ever being separated from any of them.

Furthermore, in life after life and world after world, by my own innate power, may I distinguish all paths of good and evil, and obtain vast and great wisdom.

Among all defiled and pure dharmas, may I distinguish their subtle and minute differences as though they were clearly visible, without any confusion, and obtain sharp and keen wisdom.

May all thoughts of non-understanding, misunderstanding, and doubt, the moment they arise, be entirely extinguished without remainder. May I obtain swift-seeing wisdom.

May the subtle meanings of the wondrous Dharma that others cannot fathom be attained without laborious investigation. May I obtain profound and secret wisdom.

In sum, may I distinguish the errors of letting wisdom leak away and the meanings that transcend wisdom, and from skillful means and right wisdom, may I practice the deeds of bodhi and reach the other shore. May I become like the most venerable Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva.

With such vast, sharp, swift-seeing, and profound and secret wisdoms, all easily obtained, may I tame wrong views by upholding what accords with conditions, and bring joy to the wise. Among all the teachings the Buddha has proclaimed, may I skillfully distinguish, discuss, and compose, and attain the mastery of the wise.

Furthermore, in life after life and world after world, may I completely sever any tendency to prioritize self-benefit or to retreat from the vast deeds of the bodhisattva. With a courageous mind, may I diligently pursue the benefit of others, reaching the other shore in all. From skillful means and bodhi, may I practice the bodhisattva deeds to the ultimate. May I become like the supreme Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva.

Furthermore, in life after life and world after world, when practicing the mutual benefit of self and others and crushing all māras, heretics, and adversaries, from skillful means and courageous power, may I practice the bodhisattva deeds to their final consummation. May I become like the Secret Lord, Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva.

In life after life, with vigor that abandons indolence, may I complete the wondrous deeds of the bodhisattva. From the first generation of the mind, without a single moment of laziness, and through great vigor, may I attain supreme bodhi. May I become like the peerless Śākyamuni King Buddha.

In life after life, may I pacify all interruptions on the path of practice and all illnesses of body and mind. By merely reciting his honored name, may I extinguish the afflictions and transgressions of body, speech, and mind. May I become like Bhaiṣajyaguru Vaiḍūrya-prabhā-rāja Tathāgata.

Furthermore, in life after life, whenever I wish for a life-span without limit, by merely reciting his honored name, may I crush all untimely and premature death. May I become like Amitāyus Buddha.

When obstacles to life-span approach, may I immediately see Amitāyus as my savior. From among the four kinds of activity, may he appear in whatever form is needed to discipline me. Having seen him, may all obstacles to life-span be entirely removed without remainder.

When he appears in that form to deliver me, may I immediately recognize him as Amitāyus, generate uncontrived and firm sincerity, and by the power of this, in whatever place I am born, may I never be separated from Amitāyus Buddha as my personal spiritual guide.

Furthermore, in life after life, may I encounter a good spiritual teacher endowed with worldly and supramundane merits, the foundation of all virtues, who is perfect in the characteristics of the Mahāyāna. May I joyfully attend and serve this teacher.

While attending this teacher, may I generate irreversible and firm faith, and may I always bring this teacher joy.

Not for a single instant may I cause displeasure.

May I fully receive all the instructions given by my good spiritual teacher, and may I comprehend the meaning of those teachings as things truly are, practice and cultivate them, and reach their ultimate consummation.

Not for a single instant may I be led astray by evil companions.

In life after life, may I achieve complete understanding of reverent trust in cause and effect, renunciation, the wondrous bodhicitta, and the pure view. May my practice be free from mere hesitant experimentation, and may it never be interrupted.

In every place of birth, may all the wholesome roots cultivated by body, speech, and mind be entirely dedicated to the benefit of others. May they all become causes of pure bodhi.

> O World-Honored One, if that wondrous supreme fruit
> I have not yet realized at present,
> May I obtain a pure body for the path of practice
> And in every life recall my past and always go forth.
> May I obtain eloquence, concentration, supernatural powers, and dhāraṇī,
> Upholding the inexhaustible treasury of all merits.
> May I find the peerless companion of compassion and wisdom
> And swiftly practice bodhi to its ultimate consummation.
> When the signs of untimely death appear,
> May the body of Amitāyus Buddha brightly manifest,
> Severing the might of the lord of death.
> May I swiftly attain the deathless lineage.
> My wish, life after life: may Amitāyus
> Personally serve as my Mahāyāna teacher and protector.
> Upon the path praised by the Buddhas,
> Not for a single instant may I regress or grow idle.
> May the thought of abandoning beings for my own benefit,
> The mind of discrimination, never even arise.
> May the principle of benefiting others never be obscured,
> And may skillful wisdom generously benefit all.
> By my merely reciting the honored name,
> May all invasive karmic fruits be eliminated,
> Transformed into causes of abundant well-being and happiness,
> And may I ascend the stairway of the supreme vehicle.
> When the buddha-sons give the slightest sign of liberation,
> May all hindrances to the bodhisattva's deeds be extinguished.
> May all needs be abundantly met,
> And every thought of the mind be perfectly fulfilled.
> Śākyamuni, Amitābha, Maitreya,
> Mañjuśrī, Vajrapāṇi, Avalokiteśvara:
> By the true causal connections with the Sugatas and their retinues,
> May these prayers, not in vain, be swiftly fulfilled.
