# 佛說幻化網大瑜伽教十忿怒明王大明觀想儀軌經

## The Sūtra of the Ritual Manual for Visualizing the Ten Wrathful Vidyā-Kings with Great Mantras, from the Great Yoga Teaching of the Māyājāla, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0891

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Fǎxián (法賢).

---

At that time, the Bhagavān was in the pure great pavilion in the Heaven of Pure Light. That pavilion was adorned with vajra pillars and the most supreme precious jewels in manifold decorations. All such adornments and offerings were manifestations of the Tathāgata's supernatural powers of transformation. In that assembly were the Tathāgatas Akṣobhya, Ratnasambhava, Amitāyus, Amoghasiddhi, and others.

At that time, from the womb of Mahāvairocana Tathāgata, through the four directional gates, there were born the great assembly of bodhisattvas, the great vidyā-holders, and countless noble sages. There were also wrathful vidyā-kings with their retinues: Ciṭaciṭī, Duṭaduṭī, Kiṅkara-kiṅkarī, and others. There were also devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, and countless other beings, all gathered in the assembly surrounding the Bhagavān. At that time, Mahāvairocana Tathāgata was always abiding in the samādhi of eternal peace and bliss, never departing from great wisdom, great practice, great compassion, and great mercy, rescuing and delivering sentient beings.

At that time, Mahāvairocana Tathāgata, in the great assembly, observed Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva and emitted great radiance illuminating the assembly. Then Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, gazed up at the Tathāgata, cast his vajra with his right hand, knelt on his right knee, joined his palms in reverence, and addressed the Buddha: "For what cause and what reason does the Tathāgata emit great radiance? Without cause or reason, the Buddha would not emit light. Seeing this light, my mind gives rise to doubt. The great assembly likewise harbors doubt. We humbly ask the Bhagavān to make the lion's roar, to skillfully teach the wondrous Dharma and sever our net of doubt."

At that time, the Bhagavān Vairocana Tathāgata, having received Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva's request, said: "I now emit this light because I wish to teach the Great Teaching King of the Māyājāla Yoga of Great Wisdom and Great Sovereignty. Vajrapāṇi! This vajra practice of great wisdom and skillful means is supreme among all that is accomplished in the ocean of all meanings. Among all teachings, it is the uppermost of the upper, able to bring peace and joy to all sentient beings." Having spoken thus, he entered the samādhi of the great bliss vajra of wisdom, skillful means, great sovereignty, and vajra deliverance. Emerging from samādhi, he taught the ritual method for visualizing the ten wrathful vidyā-kings within the samādhi-maṇḍala of the great Māyājāla yoga teaching.

---

**1. Yamāntaka (焰鬘得迦), the Wrathful Vidyā-King**

"Next, the vidyā-holder should take the syllable *Hūṃ* as the great wisdom-visualization. This *Hūṃ* syllable transforms into Yamāntaka, the wrathful great vidyā-king. His radiance is like the fire at the end of an eon. His body is the color of a great blue-black cloud, with six faces, six arms, and six feet. His body is short and his belly large, with a great wrathful appearance. His fangs are like vajras. Each face has three eyes. He is adorned with the eight great nāga-kings. He wears a tiger-skin garment and a skull-crown. He rides upon a water-buffalo and his feet trample lotuses. His beard is reddish-yellow and he possesses great eloquence. He is crowned with Akṣobhya Buddha, seated in sovereign ease, with a greatly fierce gaze. His front face has a smiling expression. His right face is yellow with the tongue extending outward. His left face is white with lips pressed together. He is the transformation-body of Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva. His first right hand holds a sword; his second right hand holds a vajra; his third right hand holds an arrow. His first left hand holds a lasso with the index finger raised; his second left hand holds the Prajñāpāramitā Sūtra; his third left hand holds a bow. Beneath this vidyā-king, visualize the celestial māras in terror paying homage. His body has a solar-disk halo. He transforms into Buddhas as numerous as clouds. If a vidyā-holder thus visualizes and recites the great mantra according to the method, that person, in all activities of walking, standing, sitting, and lying, will always enjoy the five kinds of pleasure without transgression, just as nothing can cling to empty space. This is called the samādhi of the great wisdom-vajra that accomplishes creation and destruction."

Then the Bhagavān Mahāvairocana Tathāgata, as the foremost of all Tathāgatas, entered the great wisdom Mahāvairocana Tathāgata vajra samādhi. Emerging from samādhi, through the three vajra gates he spoke this great mantra of the wrathful great vidyā-king Yamāntaka:

[Long dhāraṇī omitted]

When Mahāvairocana Tathāgata had spoken this great mantra, all Buddhas and bodhisattvas of great awesome power praised it as unprecedented, with minds fixed on the great vajra Vairocana Buddha. "Seeing this great wisdom, the great transformation vajra, the great wrathful Yamāntaka of awesome power appearing in the world: the vidyā-holder who establishes a boundary according to the method can eliminate all evils and is the most supreme."

"Next, for the accomplishment practice, the vidyā-holder should use the skull of a naturally-deceased person. Take only a single piece that is complete and whole, and one through which both ears are connected: this is suitable for the practice. If the skull is in two pieces, or the ears are not connected through, it is not suitable. Take the soil from beneath the feet of an enemy (*śatru*), make clay, shape it into the enemy's form, and place it inside the skull. The vidyā-holder tramples it with the foot, reciting this great mantra to summon swiftly. Relying on this visualization, it will be subdued of its own accord."

---

**2. Aparājita (無能勝), the Wrathful Vidyā-King**

Then the Bhagavān Mahāvairocana Vajra Tathāgata further taught the visualization method for Aparājita, the wrathful great vidyā-king: "The syllable *Praṃ* is the great wisdom. Visualize the syllable *Praṃ* transforming into Aparājita, the wrathful great vidyā-king, with three faces, each with three eyes, and six arms. His body is yellow, with a solar-disk halo, vast and radiant. He abides in sovereignty, adorned with the eight great nāga-kings. His front face has a smiling expression. His right face is deep blue with a slightly wrathful appearance. His left face is white, with lips pressed together, showing a greatly fierce aspect. His first right hand holds a vajra; his second right hand holds a jeweled staff; his third right hand holds an arrow. His first left hand holds a lasso with the index finger raised; his second left hand holds the Prajñāpāramitā Sūtra; his third left hand holds a bow. Visualize the appearance of creation and destruction. He is crowned with Akṣobhya Buddha. If one recites according to the method, one swiftly attains perfect awakening. This is called the samādhi of the great wisdom-vajra Aparājita."

Then Mahāvairocana Tathāgata, having taught this visualization, entered the great Amoghasiddhi vajra foremost ambrosia wrathful vajra samādhi. Emerging from samādhi, through the three vajra gates he spoke the great mantra of Aparājita, the wrathful great vidyā-king:

[Dhāraṇī omitted]

When Mahāvairocana Tathāgata had spoken this great mantra, all Buddhas and bodhisattvas of great awesome power all praised it. All evil māras and non-Buddhist practitioners were greatly terrified, their bodies and minds trembling, with minds fixed only on Mahāvairocana Buddha.

---

**3. Padmāntaka (鉢訥鬘得迦), the Wrathful Vidyā-King**

"The syllable *Hūṃ* is the great wisdom. Visualize it transforming into Padmāntaka, the wrathful great vidyā-king, with three faces, each with three eyes, and eight arms. His front face has a smiling expression. His right face is deep blue, with the tongue extending outward like a vajra. His left face is yellow, with fangs pressing the lips. He wears a tiger-skin garment. His first right hand holds a vajra; his second right hand holds a jeweled staff; his third right hand holds a *kanāya* (ritual implement); his fourth right hand holds an arrow. His first left hand holds a lasso with the index finger raised; his second left hand holds the Prajñāpāramitā Sūtra; his third left hand holds a lotus; his fourth left hand holds a bow. Visualize the appearance of creation and destruction. He can transform into countless Buddhas. He is crowned with Akṣobhya Buddha. If one visualizes according to the method, one will certainly attain accomplishment. This is called the supreme Horse-Head Vajra Samādhi."

Then Ratnasambhava, the Great Vajra Tathāgata, entered the jewel-transformation great wisdom vajra samādhi. Emerging from samādhi, he spoke the great mantra of Padmāntaka:

*Namo samantabuddhānāṃ oṃ hrīḥ ṣṭiṃ hūṃ hūṃ phaṭ svāhā*

---

**4. Vighnāntaka (尾覲難得迦), the Wrathful Vidyā-King**

"The syllable *Hūṃ* is the great wisdom. Visualize it transforming into Vighnāntaka, the wrathful great vidyā-king. His body is the color of a great blue-black cloud, with three faces, each with three eyes, and six arms. His front face has a smiling expression. His right face is white. His left face is yellow, with a wrathful appearance and pressed lips. His right foot tramples the māras; his left foot tramples a lotus. His first right hand holds a sharp sword; his second right hand holds a battle-axe; his third right hand holds an arrow. His first left hand holds a lasso with the index finger raised; his second left hand holds the Prajñāpāramitā Sūtra; his third left hand holds a bow. He is crowned with Akṣobhya Buddha. He possesses great supernatural powers and can subdue all māras, raining down great compassion. Visualizing thus according to the method, one eliminates all māras. This is called the samādhi of the most victorious great vajra."

Then the Bhagavān Amitāyus, the Great Vajra Tathāgata, entered the great vajra samādhi of the immeasurable treasury of merit. Emerging from samādhi, he spoke the great mantra of Vighnāntaka:

[Long dhāraṇī omitted]

When Amitāyus Tathāgata had spoken this great mantra, the great assembly was all greatly terrified, with minds fixed only on the great wisdom, great vajra, great compassion, Vairocana Buddha. "If the vidyā-holder visualizes a lotus in the palm, and within the lotus visualizes the syllable *Hūṃ* blazing with great flames pervading all, thus relying on the wrathful vidyā-king samādhi, one can eliminate all poisons of the three realms."

---

**5. Acala (不動尊), the Wrathful Vidyā-King**

"The syllable *Haṃ* is the great wisdom. Visualize it transforming into Acala, the wrathful great vidyā-king. He appears as a one-eyed youth, with six arms, three faces each with three eyes, adorned as a youth. His front face has a smiling expression. His right face is yellow, with the tongue extended and blood-colored above. His left face is white, wrathful, uttering the sound *Hūṃ*. His body is the color of kingfisher-jade. His feet trample a lotus and a jeweled mountain. He stands in a dancing posture, able to eliminate all māras. Blazing flames pervade his body, with a solar-disk halo. His first right hand holds a sword; his second right hand holds a vajra; his third right hand holds an arrow. His first left hand holds a lasso with the index finger raised; his second left hand holds the Prajñāpāramitā Sūtra; his third left hand holds a bow. He is crowned with a Buddha-diadem and is a transformation of Akṣobhya Tathāgata. He possesses great supernatural powers and transforms into Buddhas as numerous as clouds. The vidyā-holder who thus visualizes will attain unsurpassed bodhi before long. This is called the samādhi of the great sovereign vajra of great wisdom that produces all Buddha-Tathāgatas, Acala."

Then Mahāvairocana Tathāgata entered the vajra-lamp blazing great radiance vajra samādhi. Emerging from samādhi, he spoke the great mantra of Acala, the wrathful great vidyā-king:

[Long dhāraṇī omitted]

When Mahāvairocana Tathāgata had spoken this great mantra, all devas, nāgas, yakṣas, rākṣasas, kiṃnaras, māras and the rest were all greatly terrified, as though burned by a great fire, confused and falling to the ground, with minds fixed only on Mahāvairocana Buddha. "This Acala, the wrathful great vidyā-king, can summon all celestial maidens, causing them to be terrified, and swiftly performs all manner of desired deeds. He is a supernatural transformation of Vairocana Tathāgata."

---

**6. Ṭakki (吒枳), the Wrathful Vidyā-King**

"The syllable *Hūṃ* is the great wisdom. Visualize it transforming into Ṭakki, the wrathful great vidyā-king, with three faces, each with three eyes, and six arms. He is crowned with a jeweled diadem with a Buddha above it. The vidyā-king's hair hangs down. His front face has a smiling expression. His right face is yellow with furrowed brows. His left face is white, wrathful, with pressed lips. His body is the color of a blue-black cloud, with a solar-disk halo. His left and right hands form the root-mudrā. His second right hand holds a vajra; his third right hand holds an arrow. His second left hand holds the Prajñāpāramitā Sūtra; his third left hand holds a bow. His feet trample lotuses in a dancing posture. He transforms into all Buddhas. Below him, the māras appear terrified. The vidyā-holder who visualizes according to the method swiftly realizes bodhi. This is called the samādhi of summoning all, the most victorious vajra."

Then Vairocana Tathāgata entered the universally blazing treasury vajra samādhi. Emerging from samādhi, he spoke the great mantra of Ṭakki:

*Namo samantabuddhānāṃ oṃ ṭakki hūṃ jaḥ*

When Mahāvairocana Tathāgata had spoken this great mantra, all Buddhas and bodhisattvas praised it. All māras were confused, lost consciousness, and fell to the ground.

---

**7. Nīladaṇḍa (儞羅難拏), the Wrathful Vidyā-King**

Then the Bhagavān Amoghasiddhi, the Great Vajra Tathāgata, taught the visualization method for Nīladaṇḍa, the wrathful great vidyā-king: "The syllable *Hūṃ* is the great wisdom. Visualize it transforming into Nīladaṇḍa, the wrathful great vidyā-king, with three faces, each with three eyes, and six arms. His front face is blue with a smiling expression. His left face is yellow. His right face is white with pressed lips. His body is the color of a blue-black cloud, adorned with the eight great nāga-kings. His hair-knot is sleek and dark. He is crowned with a Buddha. His feet trample lotuses in a dancing posture. His first right hand holds a vajra; his second right hand holds a jeweled staff; his third right hand holds an arrow. His first left hand holds a lasso with the index finger raised; his second left hand holds the Prajñāpāramitā Sūtra; his third left hand holds a bow. Solar-disk halo. He transforms into Buddhas as numerous as clouds. This is called the samādhi of the great wisdom-vajra that subdues māra-enemies."

Then Amoghasiddhi, the Great Vajra Tathāgata, entered the unerring, supremely born, great jewel-banner, great maṇi, great vajra samādhi. Emerging from samādhi, he spoke the great mantra of Nīladaṇḍa:

[Long dhāraṇī omitted]

When Amoghasiddhi Tathāgata had spoken this great mantra, the māra-palaces in all the lands of the ten directions all greatly trembled, with minds fixed only on the three-vajra Mahāvairocana Buddha. "The vidyā-holder who performs the practice according to all the mudrās and this great mantra will accomplish all. If one recites this great mantra 800 times, one can vanquish all māra-enemies."

---

**8. Mahābala (大力), the Wrathful Vidyā-King**

"The syllable *Hūṃ* is the great wisdom. Visualize it transforming into Mahābala, the wrathful great vidyā-king, with three faces, each with three eyes, and eight arms. His body is the color of a blue-black cloud, adorned with the eight great nāga-kings. Blazing flames pervade his body; his hair stands on end. His eyes are greatly reddened. He is crowned with Akṣobhya Buddha. His front face has a smiling expression. His right face is gold. His left face is white with pressed lips. His feet trample lotuses in a great wrathful posture. The devas scatter in terror to all directions. Solar-disk halo. His first right hand holds a vajra; his second right hand holds a jeweled staff; his third right hand holds a sword; his fourth right hand holds an arrow. His first left hand holds a lasso with the index finger raised; his second left hand holds the Prajñāpāramitā Sūtra; his third left hand holds a mace; his fourth left hand holds a bow. He transforms into Buddhas as numerous as clouds. This visualization is called the samādhi of the great-power, great-wisdom, most victorious great vajra."

Then Akṣobhya, the Great Vajra Tathāgata, entered the universally transforming, adorning, great vajra samādhi. Emerging from samādhi, he spoke the great mantra of Mahābala:

[Dhāraṇī omitted]

When Akṣobhya Tathāgata had spoken this great mantra, all celestial māras were terrified, with minds fixed only on Mahāvairocana Vajra Tathāgata. "The vidyā-holder who recites this great mantra can grant all wishes, including stopping rain or bringing rain, all according to one's desire."

---

**9. Śumbha (送婆), the Wrathful Vidyā-King**

"The syllable *Hūṃ* is the great wisdom. Visualize it transforming into Śumbha, the wrathful great vidyā-king. His body is deep blue. With his left and right hands he forms the root-mudrā. His second right hand holds a sword; his third right hand holds an arrow. His second left hand holds the Prajñāpāramitā Sūtra; his third left hand holds a bow. This great vidyā-king's awesome power is the most supreme within the three realms."

Then Mahāvairocana Tathāgata spoke the great mantra of Śumbha:

*Namo samantabuddhānāṃ oṃ śumbha niśumbha hūṃ gṛhṇa gṛhṇa hūṃ gṛhṇapaya gṛhṇapaya hūṃ ānaya ho bhagavan vidyārāja hūṃ phaṭ*

When Mahāvairocana Tathāgata had spoken this great mantra, all celestial maidens were greatly terrified and fell at the vidyā-king's feet, with minds fixed only on the great wisdom Mahāvairocana Buddha. "This great mantra has great awesome power. It can serve as the vajra-lasso and vajra-hook, hooking and dragging all celestial maidens and others, who come without delay. Among all summoning methods, this mantra is the most supreme."

---

**10. Vajrapātāla (嚩日囉播多羅), the Wrathful Vidyā-King**

Then Mahāvairocana Tathāgata further taught the visualization method for Vajrapātāla, the wrathful great vidyā-king: "The syllable *Tu* is the great wisdom. Visualize it transforming into Vajrapātāla, the wrathful great vidyā-king. His body is the color of white milk, with six arms. His first right hand holds a vajra; his second right hand holds a vajra-hook; his third right hand holds an arrow. His first left hand holds a lasso with the index finger raised; his second left hand holds the Prajñāpāramitā Sūtra; his third left hand holds a bow. He appears in the aspect of subduing all asuras."

Then Mahāvairocana Tathāgata spoke the great mantra of Vajrapātāla:

*Oṃ pañca sarvapātāla naityagaṇa hūṃ hūṃ hūṃ phaṭ*

---

Then the Bhagavān Mahāvairocana Vajra Tathāgata said: "These ten great wrathful vidyā-kings each have three faces, each face with three eyes, greatly reddened. They all display wrathful gazes. The black nāga-king Nanda and the nāga-king Kulika bind their hair-knots. The golden nāga-king Takṣaka serves as their ear-rings. The great nāga-king Mahāpadma, colored like red gold, serves as their arm-bracelets. The white nāga-king Karkoṭaka serves as their shoulder-chain. The nāga-king Vāsuki, colored like a red lotus, serves as their belt. The white, conch-colored nāga-king Padma serves as their ankle-bells. Thus the eight great nāga-kings serve as their adornments.

"All ten great vidyā-kings display greatly fierce aspects. They wear tiger-skin garments and skull-crowns. Their hair-knots stand upright, reddish-yellow in color. They are all crowned with Akṣobhya Tathāgata. Each has a solar-disk halo and seat. Their front face has a smiling expression. Their right face shows a slightly wrathful appearance. Their left face shows a greatly fierce aspect, with fangs pressing the lips, brows furrowed, standing in a dancing posture. The vidyā-holder who visualizes thus according to the method will accomplish all wishes without exception."
