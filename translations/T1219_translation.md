# 曼殊室利焰曼德迦萬愛祕術如意法
## Mañjuśrī Yamāntaka Secret Art of Universal Love and Wish-Fulfillment

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1219

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, compiled by Yixing (一行).

---

The source of the true-word teaching: the homa rite is supreme. Within it, the most secret teaching is the love-and-respect dharma. For the sake of gaining the reverence of sentient beings in the final age, one seal and one true word are taught, able to benefit sentient beings.

First, the image is described. His body has six faces, six arms, and six feet, and he rides a water buffalo. Regarding the six faces: three faces are on the crown. The central face is in the bodhisattva's gentle and soft aspect, and upon its crown is Amitābha Buddha. The six arms: the left hands hold a trident, a lasso-wheel, and a bow. The right hands hold a sword, a jeweled staff, and an arrow, drawn with the bow. The six feet: the left three feet stand upon a wheel, and the right three feet stand atop the wheel below which is a wooden ox, the ox's four feet standing upon a lotus pedestal. Behind the image are flames like a rainbow and like vaiḍūrya. In the center of the flames, beneath the image's right foot, is the Tiger Spirit, followed in sequence by the twelve spirits, with the Tiger and Monkey facing each other.

Next, the altar is a circular water-altar. Within the altar, place a wheel for practice. Whether for increasing benefit, love-and-respect, pacification, or subjugation, all may be placed therein. The oral transmission specifies the details, and the body-practice is also orally transmitted.

Next, the one-seal oral transmission. The mantra:

*Oṃ stri-kāra-rūpa hūṃ khaṃ svāhā*

With this seal-mantra, face the Buddha to perform universal homage, then sequentially contemplate and recite.

Next, the maṇḍala method. In the inner court is the vidyārāja; behind the vidyārāja is the dharma-form of Mañjuśrī. In the outer court: to the east, the Summoning Youth, in the form of a youth with topknot, standing, holding a bow and arrow. To the southeast, Keśinī Youth, in similar form, the left fist closed, the right holding a triangular trident, the left fist placed at the hip. To the south, Wisdom-Rescue Youth, in similar form, the left hand throwing a wheel, the right in the mudrā of fearlessness with the right foot raised. To the southwest, Upakeśinī Youth, in similar form, both hands holding a flower-bowl. To the west, Light-Net Youth, the left fist at the hip, the right holding a peacock-tail fan. To the northwest, Earth-Wisdom Youth, the right hand holding a trident, like Vajragarbha King. To the north, Immaculate-Light Youth, in similar form, the left fist at the hip, the right holding a lasso upward. To the northeast, Inconceivable Youth, the left fist at the hip, the right holding a sword in the posture of striking downward at something on the ground. Each body stands upon a five-colored wheel, and beneath each wheel is a lion. Such is the dharma described.

The method for attaining accomplishment: on a high mountaintop, construct a pure room. For forty-nine days maintain silence, extinguish all fires, and in accordance with the dharma complete one million recitations. Afterward, the dharma is accomplished and attained.

Next, the method of accomplishment through talismans.

The earth talisman is transmitted orally.

The heaven talisman is transmitted orally.

Having thus explained, sentient beings of the final age faithfully received and practiced accordingly.

Colophon: On the twenty-third day of the seventh month of the second year of Hōgen [1157 CE], collated once at the Western Bright Hall of Kanjuji by the monk Chikai.
