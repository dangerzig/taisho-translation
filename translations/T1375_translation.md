# 佛說莊嚴王陀羅尼呪經
## The Buddha's Teaching: Adorning King Dhāraṇī Mantra Sūtra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1375

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Yijing (義淨).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Bhagavat was on Mount Potalaka, teaching the essential Dharma for the assembly of humans, devas, and the entire great multitude. Surveying the ten directions, he addressed Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva and Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva in a wondrous voice: "Good men! There is a sūtra called the Adorning King Dhāraṇī of All Tathāgatas' Protection and Observation of Sentient Beings Manifesting Buddha-Fields. When I first aroused the mind in the past, at the place of the Tathāgata Puṣpa-Prabhā-Abhyudgata, together with ninety koṭi sentient beings, upon hearing this Dharma we all attained a share of the tathāgata's wisdom. All received predictions that they would attain buddhahood in various buddha-fields, and I too received a prediction among them. Good men! With the buddha-eye I see that in the past thirty thousand tathāgatas taught this essential Dharma for sentient beings. You, Avalokiteśvara and Mañjuśrī, at the time of each teaching served as the leading guides."

When the World-Honored One had spoken these words, the great assembly present, bodhisattvas, śrāvakas, and all who had come to listen, all aroused minds of faith and made offerings to that Buddha. At that time immeasurable hundreds of thousands of koṭi-nayuta sentient beings all received predictions and also attained the bodhisattva's supreme samādhi. Their illnesses were all cured, their hindrances and entanglements destroyed, their aspirations fulfilled, and their roots of merit perfected. Their countenances became dignified and beautiful, their wealth and treasures abundant. Kings, great ministers, and all manner of beings rejoiced upon seeing them. Their mindfulness, wisdom, and supernatural powers were all fully accomplished. They attained unshakable faith in the Buddha, Dharma, and Saṃgha. Their lifespans, physical beauty, and strength possessed great majestic power. They were freed from the suffering of separation from loved ones and from association with the hateful.

"If any good man or good woman is able, upon encountering this sūtra, to prostrate with all five limbs touching the ground, arousing a mind of wonder, and with flowers, incense, garlands, banners, and canopies reverently makes offerings, takes refuge, praises, reads, recites, receives, and upholds it;

"If one copies it oneself or has others copy it, immeasurable merit will all be accomplished. Those who see one who receives and upholds it and arouse a mind of faith and reverence will also, in this very body, be endowed with all virtues and attain the bodhisattva's supreme samādhi. From their mouths will constantly come forth the fragrance of sandalwood. Their eyes will be as bright and pure as blue lotus petals. During the day and night they will see the forms of buddhas and great bodhisattvas. If they have committed sins such as the five acts of immediate retribution or other karmic obstructions, all will be eliminated. The devas will guard and protect them. At the time of death they will see the buddhas and bodhisattvas, their minds will not be confused, and they will certainly be reborn in the Land of Ultimate Bliss. I give such a person a prediction of attaining buddhahood. That person will see my body, reverently make offerings, and will not doubt the wondrous awakening.

"Furthermore, if a person reads, recites, receives, upholds, makes offerings to, and reveres this Dharma-gate, or copies it themselves or has others copy it, and if there is anyone who hears the name of this sūtra, know that this person is a bodhisattva, and offerings should be made to them.

"Furthermore, if such a person, due to evil karma from past lives, finds that their lifespan, wealth, physical strength, and both inner and outer resources are all deficient and impoverished, that their wishes are unfulfilled, that they are separated from loved ones, that their land is ravaged and in ruin, and that they face calamities from kings and bandits, then by the power of this sūtra their retributions in this present body will become light. They may briefly experience a headache, receive poor clothing or food, or encounter slander, abuse, or other humiliations, but all their past karmic obstructions will be spontaneously eliminated. Good men! That person who upholds the sūtra should give rise to this thought: 'In the past, as I wandered through the sea of birth and death, I created various evils against sentient beings, and my unwholesome karma grew day and night. Now I have become aware and dare not conceal it. I lay bare the source of my transgressions and sincerely repent. I arouse unshakable faith in the Buddha, Dharma, and Saṃgha.'

"Furthermore, if that person had committed evil deeds against the jewels of the Buddha, Dharma, and Saṃgha, or against pratyekabuddhas, śrāvakas, parents, or teachers, the karmic fruits of clothing and food would all be exhausted. But by the power of this sūtra, such karma will be entirely eliminated, and clothing and food will be sufficient.

"Furthermore, if that person has karma that would cause them to suffer in the hells, the suffering of separation from loved ones, the suffering of being born blind, or the maturation of karma resulting in missing or ambiguous sense faculties, all of these will also be eliminated.

"Furthermore, if through the force of evil karma from past jealousy, anger, and hatred they were bound to fall into the realm of King Yama, the animal realm, the hungry ghost realm, or other evil destinies, by the power of this sūtra all evil karma will be entirely eliminated and all merit will increase and grow.

"Good men! In whatever place this Dharma-gate exists, the people there will constantly be protected by this sūtra. Their merit will be complete, their wishes fulfilled, they will constantly enjoy wealth and happiness, their evil karma will be eliminated, and they will be free from untimely death, evil dreams, illness and suffering. In battle they will always be victorious, their lifespans will be extended, their physical beauty and strength will be complete, and they will possess great majestic power. All beings in the world, humans, devas, nāgas, and all ghosts and spirits will follow their wishes. They will not encounter evil poisons, or calamities from water, fire, or other dangers. Moreover, by the power of upholding this divine mantra, they will constantly receive predictions from all the buddhas.

"If any good man or good woman hears this divine mantra and receives, upholds, reads, and recites it, copies it themselves or has others copy it, and reverently makes offerings, that good man or good woman will obtain all the merit previously described."

When the World-Honored One had spoken these words, the great earth suddenly trembled with six kinds of quaking. The great assembly offered wondrous flowers, incense, scented paste, garlands, canopies, and supreme robes to the World-Honored One, and with one voice proclaimed: "Excellent, excellent! How is it that we have been able to hear the divine mantra taught by the Tathāgata!" Then the World-Honored One spoke the mantra:

*Namaḥ sarva-tathāgatānāṃ. Tadyathā: buddhi su-buddhi loka-viloke loka-vati kānti sattvāvalokani sarva-tathāgatādhiṣṭhite sarva-āśā-paripūraṇi juḍiṃ-da-le nara-deva-pūjite tathāgata-jñāna-dhāte tathāgatādhiṣṭhānena sarva-loka-sukhi-bhavatu sarva-karma-paricchedāya mama amukasya sarva-sattvānāṃ ca rakṣa-bhavatu tathāgatādhiṣṭhānena svāhā.*

"Good men! This divine mantra was proclaimed and jointly empowered by thirty thousand tathāgatas. I now also speak this divine mantra, wishing to benefit all sentient beings and enable them to attain peace and happiness. It constantly provides protection, removes their illness and suffering, and enables them to be born in the buddha-lands.

"Furthermore, if a person thinks: 'The merit of the divine mantra taught by the Buddha is as described, but how can I accomplish it?', they should rise early in the morning, purify themselves, arouse great compassion and a mind of sympathy for all sentient beings, eliminate all jealousy, arrogance, and anger, and with single-minded reverence before a buddha image, make extensive offerings of incense, flowers, food, and drink. They should bow to the buddhas of the ten directions, mentally express their aspirations, and recite this divine mantra one hundred and eight times. With each recitation they should empower one flower, and with each flower make an offering to the Buddha. This person will then have all their wishes fulfilled as they desire. In their dreams they will see the buddhas. At the end of their life they will behold the buddhas and bodhisattvas. After leaving this body they will certainly be reborn in the World of Ultimate Bliss. Their lifespan, physical beauty, and strength will all be complete, and all enemies will become joyful."

When this mantra was spoken, sixty thousand sentient beings attained the patient acceptance of the non-arising of dharmas, forever eliminated all obstructions, and had all their aspirations fulfilled.

When the World-Honored One had spoken this sūtra, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva, and the entire great assembly, having heard what the Buddha taught, joyfully put it into practice.
