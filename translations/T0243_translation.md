# 大樂金剛不空真實三麼耶經
## Sūtra of the Great Bliss Vajra, the Non-Empty True Samaya

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0243

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空)

---

### Chapter on the Principle and Purport of the Prajñāpāramitā

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Bhagavān had accomplished the supreme vajra-empowerment samaya-wisdom of all Tathāgatas, had attained the jeweled crown of consecration of all Tathāgatas, had become Lord of the Three Realms, had realized the omniscience of all Tathāgatas, was sovereign in yoga, and was able to perform the various activities of the equality of all mudrās of all Tathāgatas. For all sentient beings without remainder and without end, he perfectly fulfilled all their aspirations and activities. Eternally throughout the three times, in all eras, with the vajra body, speech, and mind activities, he was the great Tathāgata Vairocana. He was in the Paranirmitavaśavartin heaven's palace in the desire realm, the place where all Tathāgatas constantly sport, in the Great Maṇi Hall of Auspicious Praise, with various interspersed bells, tassels, and silk streamers stirred by gentle breezes, adorned with strings of pearls, garlands, and half-moon and full-moon ornaments. Together with eighty koṭi bodhisattvas, namely: Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva, Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva, Vajramuṣṭi Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva, Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva, the Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Who Just Resolves and Turns the Dharma-Wheel, Gaganakośa Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva, and the Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Who Crushes All Māras. Together with these great bodhisattvas, reverently surrounding him, he taught the Dharma for them. Good in the beginning, middle, and end, with skillful meaning and language, purely complete, clean, and pure, he proclaimed the gate of pure sentences of all dharmas, namely: "The pure sentence of exquisite bliss is the bodhisattva's station. The pure sentence of the arrow of desire is the bodhisattva's station. The pure sentence of touch is the bodhisattva's station. The pure sentence of the bonds of love is the bodhisattva's station. The pure sentence of complete sovereignty is the bodhisattva's station. The pure sentence of seeing is the bodhisattva's station. The pure sentence of delight is the bodhisattva's station. The pure sentence of love is the bodhisattva's station. The pure sentence of pride is the bodhisattva's station. The pure sentence of adornment is the bodhisattva's station. The pure sentence of the nourishing moisture of intention is the bodhisattva's station. The pure sentence of radiance is the bodhisattva's station. The pure sentence of bodily bliss is the bodhisattva's station. The pure sentence of form is the bodhisattva's station. The pure sentence of sound is the bodhisattva's station. The pure sentence of scent is the bodhisattva's station. The pure sentence of taste is the bodhisattva's station. Why? Because the self-nature of all dharmas is pure, the prajñāpāramitā is pure. Vajrapāṇi! If any person hears these pure sentences on the principle and purport born from the prajñāpāramitā, then even unto the seat of bodhi, all veils and obstructions, affliction obstructions, dharma-obstructions, and karma-obstructions, even if widely accumulated, will certainly not cause that person to fall into the hells and other evil destinies. Even if they commit grave sins, it will not be difficult to eliminate them. If one can receive, uphold, and daily read, recite, and contemplate it, in this very life one will realize the samādhi of the equality of all dharmas, the vajra-samādhi. In all dharmas, one will attain sovereignty and receive immeasurable delight and joy. Through the births of the sixteen great bodhisattvas, one will attain the station of the Tathāgata and of the vajra-holder."

At that time, the Bhagavān, the supreme being Vajrasattva who holds the vajra of the samaya of all maṇḍalas of the Mahāyāna direct realization of all Tathāgatas, who tames all in the three realms without remainder and accomplishes all purposes, Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva, wishing to clarify this meaning, smiled with delight. With his left hand he formed the vajra-pride mudrā, and with his right hand he grasped and brandished the primordial great vajra in a posture of courageous advance, and spoke the heart of the great bliss vajra non-empty samaya:

*Hūṃ*

At that time, the Bhagavān, the Tathāgata Vairocana, further taught the principle and purport of the prajñāpāramitā born from the quiescent dharma-nature of all Tathāgatas, the direct and perfect enlightenment. Namely: "The direct and perfect enlightenment of vajra-equality, because the great bodhi is firm as the vajra. The direct and perfect enlightenment of the equality of meaning, because the great bodhi has a single purpose of benefit. The direct and perfect enlightenment of the equality of Dharma, because the self-nature of the great bodhi is inherently pure. The direct and perfect enlightenment of the equality of all karmic activities, because the great bodhi is of the nature of non-discrimination in all discriminations. Vajrapāṇi! If any person hears these four dharmas of arising, reads, recites, and upholds them, even if presently committing immeasurable grave sins, they will certainly transcend all evil destinies and even come to sit upon the seat of bodhi and swiftly realize unsurpassed perfect enlightenment."

At that time, the Bhagavān, having spoken thus, wishing to further clarify this meaning, smiled with delight, held the wisdom-fist mudrā, and spoke the heart of the equality of the self-nature of all dharmas:

*Āḥ*

At that time, the Tathāgata Śākyamuni, who tames the difficult to tame, further taught the principle and purport of the prajñāpāramitā, the most supreme arising from the equality of all dharmas. Namely: "Because desire is by nature free from proliferation, anger is also by nature free from proliferation. Because anger is by nature free from proliferation, delusion is also by nature free from proliferation. Because delusion is by nature free from proliferation, all dharmas are by nature free from proliferation. Because all dharmas are by nature free from proliferation, one should know that the prajñāpāramitā is by nature free from proliferation. Vajrapāṇi! If any person hears this principle and purport, receives, upholds, reads, and recites it, even if they were to kill all sentient beings of the three realms, they will not fall into evil destinies, for they are taming beings, and they will swiftly realize unsurpassed perfect bodhi."

At that time, the great bodhisattva Vajrapāṇi, wishing to further clarify this meaning, held the Trailokyavijaya mudrā, and with a lotus-face, smiling yet wrathful, with furrowed brows and fierce gaze, sharp fangs appearing, standing in the subjugation posture, spoke the heart of the vajra *hūṃkāra*:

*Hūṃ*

At that time, the Bhagavān, the Tathāgata of Attained Inherent Purity of the Dharma-Nature, further taught the principle and purport of the prajñāpāramitā, the wisdom-seal of Avalokiteśvara born from the equality of all dharmas. Namely: "Because all worldly desire is pure, all anger is pure. Because all worldly defilement is pure, all sin is pure. Because all worldly dharmas are pure, all sentient beings are pure. Because all worldly omniscience is pure, the prajñāpāramitā is pure. Vajrapāṇi! If any person hears this principle and purport, receives, upholds, reads, recites, and contemplates it, even if dwelling amidst all desires, like a lotus flower, they will not be defiled by adventitious dusts and stains. They will swiftly realize unsurpassed perfect bodhi."

At that time, the Bhagavān, the great bodhisattva Avalokiteśvara, wishing to further clarify this meaning, smiled with delight, forming the gesture of the open lotus flower, contemplating desire without defilement, and spoke the heart of the various kinds of form and mind of all beings:

*Hrīḥ*

At that time, the Bhagavān, the Tathāgata who is Lord of All Three Realms, further taught the principle and purport of the prajñāpāramitā, the consecration wisdom-treasury of all Tathāgatas. Namely: "Through the gift of consecration, one attains the position of Dharma-king in the three realms. Through the gift of beneficial action, one attains the complete fulfillment of all wishes. Through the gift of Dharma, one attains the perfection of all dharmas. Through the gift of material sustenance, one attains complete happiness of body, speech, and mind."

At that time, the great bodhisattva Ākāśagarbha, wishing to further clarify this meaning, smiled with delight, bound his head with a vajra-jewel garland, and spoke the heart of the samaya jewel of all consecrations:

*Trāṃ*

At that time, the Bhagavān, the Tathāgata who Attains the Wisdom-Seal of All Tathāgatas, further taught the principle and purport of the prajñāpāramitā, the vajra-wisdom-seal empowerment of all Tathāgatas. Namely: "If one holds the body-seal of all Tathāgatas, one becomes the body of all Tathāgatas. If one holds the speech-seal of all Tathāgatas, one attains the Dharma of all Tathāgatas. If one holds the mind-seal of all Tathāgatas, one realizes the samādhi of all Tathāgatas. If one holds the vajra-seal of all Tathāgatas, one accomplishes the most supreme vajra siddhi of the body, speech, and mind activities of all Tathāgatas. Vajrapāṇi! If any person hears this principle and purport, receives, upholds, reads, recites, and contemplates it, that person will attain all sovereignty, omniscience, all activities, and all accomplishments. They will attain the vajra nature of all body, speech, and mind, and all siddhis, and will swiftly realize unsurpassed perfect bodhi."

At that time, the Bhagavān, wishing to clarify this meaning, smiled with delight, held the vajra-fist great samaya-mudrā, and spoke the heart of the samaya of the siddhi of all firm vajra-seals:

*Āḥ*

At that time, the Bhagavān, the Tathāgata of All Non-Proliferation, further taught the principle and purport of the prajñāpāramitā of the revolving syllable-wheel. Namely: "Because all dharmas are empty, they correspond to the nature of marklessness. Because all dharmas are markless, they correspond to the nature of non-activity. Because they are non-active, they correspond to the nature of wishlessness. Because they are wishless, all dharmas are originally pure, and the prajñāpāramitā is pure."

At that time, the youth Mañjuśrī, wishing to further clarify this meaning, smiled with delight, and with his own sword slashed all the Tathāgatas, then spoke the most supreme heart of the prajñāpāramitā:

*Āṃ*

At that time, the Bhagavān, the Tathāgata Who Enters the Great Wheel of All Tathāgatas, further taught the principle and purport of the prajñāpāramitā of entering the great wheel. Namely: "By entering vajra-equality, one enters the Dharma-wheel of all Tathāgatas. By entering the equality of meaning, one enters the wheel of the great bodhisattvas. By entering the equality of all dharmas, one enters the wheel of the wondrous Dharma. By entering the equality of all activities, one enters the wheel of all activities."

At that time, the great bodhisattva who Just Resolves and Turns the Dharma-Wheel, wishing to further clarify this meaning, smiled with delight, turned the vajra-wheel, and spoke the heart of the samaya of all vajras:

*Hūṃ*

At that time, the Bhagavān, the Tathāgata of the Treasury of Various Offerings of All Tathāgatas, Vast Rites Arisen, further taught the principle and purport of the prajñāpāramitā, the most supreme arising from all offerings. Namely: "Giving rise to the bodhi-mind is a vast offering to all Tathāgatas. Rescuing all sentient beings is a vast offering to all Tathāgatas. Receiving and upholding the wondrous scriptures is a vast offering to all Tathāgatas. Receiving, upholding, reading, reciting the prajñāpāramitā, copying it oneself and having others copy it, contemplating and cultivating it, and making various offerings: this is a vast offering to all Tathāgatas."

At that time, the great bodhisattva Gaganakośa, wishing to further clarify this meaning, smiled with delight, and spoke the heart of the non-empty samaya of all activities, the vajra of all:

*Oṃ*

At that time, the Bhagavān, the Tathāgata Who Tames with the Held Wisdom-Fist, further taught the principle and purport of the prajñāpāramitā, the treasury of all-taming wisdom. Namely: "Because all sentient beings are equal, wrath is also equal. Because all sentient beings are tamed, wrath is also tamed. Because all sentient beings are of the nature of Dharma, wrath is also of the nature of Dharma. Because all sentient beings are of vajra-nature, wrath is also of vajra-nature. Why? Because the taming of all sentient beings is bodhi itself."

At that time, the great bodhisattva who Crushes All Māras, wishing to further clarify this meaning, smiled with delight, and in the form of a vajra-yakṣa, holding a vajra-fang, having terrified all the Tathāgatas, spoke the heart of the vajra's great wrathful laughter:

*Haḥ*

At that time, the Bhagavān, the Tathāgata Who Establishes the Equality of All, further taught the principle and purport of the prajñāpāramitā, the most supreme arising from the samaya of all dharmas. Namely: "Because all is of the nature of equality, the prajñāpāramitā is of the nature of equality. Because all is of the nature of benefit, the prajñāpāramitā is of the nature of benefit. Because all is of the nature of Dharma, the prajñāpāramitā is of the nature of Dharma. Because all is of the nature of karma, the prajñāpāramitā is of the nature of karma. This should be known."

At that time, Vajrapāṇi, having entered the empowerment samādhi of the samaya of the bodhisattvas of all Tathāgatas, spoke the heart of the non-empty samaya of all:

*Hūṃ*

At that time, the Bhagavān Tathāgata further taught the principle and purport of the prajñāpāramitā of the empowerment of all sentient beings. Namely: "The tathāgatagarbha of all sentient beings is the treasury of the bodhisattva Samantabhadra, because of the universal self of all. It is the vajra-treasury of all sentient beings, because of the vajra-treasury consecration. It is the wondrous Dharma-treasury of all sentient beings, because of turning all speech. It is the karma-treasury of all sentient beings, because of corresponding to the nature of performing all that can be performed."

At that time, the outer vajra division, wishing to further clarify this meaning, made sounds of joy and spoke the heart of the vajra-sovereign true reality:

*Tāḥ*

At that time, all the mother-goddesses prostrated at the Buddha's feet and offered the samaya heart of truth that hooks, summons, enters, can kill, and can accomplish:

*Vyūḥ*

At that time, the three Mādhukara brothers and their retinue prostrated at the Buddha's feet and offered their own heart:

*Svā*

At that time, the four sister-goddesses offered their own heart:

*Hāṃ*

At that time, the Bhagavān, the Tathāgata of the Immeasurable, Boundless, and Ultimate, wishing to empower this teaching and cause it to be ultimately and perfectly fulfilled, further taught the principle and purport of the prajñāpāramitā of the arising from the equality of the vajra. Namely: "Because the prajñāpāramitā is immeasurable, all Tathāgatas are also immeasurable. Because the prajñāpāramitā is boundless, all Tathāgatas are also boundless. Because all dharmas are of one nature, the prajñāpāramitā is of one nature. Because all dharmas are ultimate, the prajñāpāramitā is also ultimate. Vajrapāṇi! If any person hears this principle and purport, receives, upholds, reads, recites, and contemplates its meaning, that person will attain the ultimate perfection of the Buddha-bodhisattva practices."

At that time, the Bhagavān, the Tathāgata Vairocana, having attained the secret dharma-nature of all Tathāgatas and awakened to all dharmas of proliferation, further taught the principle and purport of the prajñāpāramitā of the dharma-nature of the great bliss vajra non-empty samādhi of equality, the most supreme, without beginning, middle, or end. Namely: "Because the bodhisattva-mahāsattva supremely accomplishes great desire, they attain the supreme accomplishment of great bliss. Because the bodhisattva-mahāsattva supremely accomplishes great bliss, they attain the supreme accomplishment of the great bodhi of all Tathāgatas. Because the bodhisattva-mahāsattva supremely accomplishes the great bodhi of all Tathāgatas, they attain the supreme accomplishment of crushing the great-powered Māra of all Tathāgatas. Because the bodhisattva-mahāsattva supremely accomplishes the crushing of the great-powered Māra of all Tathāgatas, they attain the accomplishment of being sovereign lord throughout the three realms. Because the bodhisattva-mahāsattva attains the accomplishment of being sovereign lord throughout the three realms, they accomplish the ultimate purification and removal of the clinging and transmigration of all sentient beings without remainder. With great diligence, they constantly dwell in birth and death, rescuing all beings, benefiting and bringing bliss, supremely and ultimately accomplishing all. Why?

> "The bodhisattva of supreme wisdom,
> Even to the very end of birth and death,
> Constantly acts for the benefit of sentient beings
> And does not proceed toward nirvāṇa.
> Prajñā and skillful means,
> Empowered by the pāramitā of wisdom:
> All dharmas and all existences
> Are all entirely pure.
> Desire and the rest tame the world,
> Causing it to be purified.
> From the summit of existence to the evil destinies,
> Taming beings and exhausting all existences.
> As the lotus is inherently pure
> And not stained by filth,
> The nature of desires is also thus:
> Unstained, it benefits beings.
> Great desire attains purity,
> Great ease and bliss, abundance.
> Sovereignty in the three realms,
> Able to render firm and lasting benefit.

"Vajrapāṇi! If any person hears this primordial principle and purport of the prajñāpāramitā, and every morning either recites or listens to it, that person will attain the ultimate siddhi of the non-empty great bliss vajra-samādhi of all ease and delight. In this present life, they will attain sovereignty and joyful bliss in all dharmas. Through the births of the sixteen great bodhisattvas, they will attain the station of the Tathāgata and of the vajra-holder.

*Hūṃ*

At that time, all the Tathāgatas and the vajra-holding bodhisattva-mahāsattvas all came together in assembly. Wishing this Dharma to be non-empty, unobstructed, and swiftly accomplished, they together praised Vajrapāṇi, saying:

> "Well done, well done, great being!
> Well done, well done, great bliss!
> Well done, well done, Mahāyāna!
> Well done, well done, great wisdom!
> You have well expounded this Dharma-teaching,
> Empowered by the vajra-sūtra.
> One who upholds this most supreme king of teachings
> Cannot be harmed by any māra.
> They attain the most supreme station of Buddhas and bodhisattvas
> And before long obtain all siddhis."
> All the Tathāgatas and bodhisattvas,
> Having together made this supreme statement,
> To cause the upholder to swiftly accomplish,
> All greatly rejoiced, believed, accepted, and practiced it.
