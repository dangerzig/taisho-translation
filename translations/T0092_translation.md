# 十支居士八城人經
## Sūtra of the Householder Dasama of Aṭṭhaka

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0092

Translated from the Chinese. Later Han dynasty, translated by An Shigao (安世高)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the senior venerable bhikṣus were in the city of Pāṭaliputra, in the Kukkuṭārāma. It was not long after the World-Honored One's parinirvāṇa. At that time, the householder Dasama of Aṭṭhaka, a man of great wealth, went to Pāṭaliputra to conduct business. Having bought everything with his money and goods, he was exceedingly delighted, having obtained great profit, exceedingly joyful and pleased. He departed from Pāṭaliputra and went to the Kukkuṭārāma. Having arrived, he prostrated at the feet of the senior venerable bhikṣus and sat to one side. The senior venerable bhikṣus then taught him the Dharma, encouraged and further encouraged him, instructed and further instructed him, and by immeasurable skillful means taught him the Dharma, encouraged and further instructed him, and made him rejoice. Having done so, they sat in silence.

Then the householder Dasama of Aṭṭhaka, having heard the Dharma taught by the senior venerable bhikṣus and having been encouraged to the point of rejoicing, addressed the senior venerable bhikṣus: "Venerable ones! Where is the Venerable Ānanda now residing? I wish to see him."

"Householder! The Venerable Ānanda is in Vaiśālī, on the bank of the Monkey Pond. If you wish to see him, you should go there." Then the householder Dasama of Aṭṭhaka rose from his seat, prostrated at the feet of the senior venerable bhikṣus, circumambulated them, and departed, making his way to where the Venerable Ānanda was. Having arrived, he prostrated at the Venerable Ānanda's feet and sat to one side. Having sat to one side, the householder Dasama of Aṭṭhaka addressed the Venerable Ānanda: "Ānanda! I wish to ask something. Please hear my question."

"Ask, householder! Having heard, I shall answer."

"Venerable Ānanda! The World-Honored One who had wisdom and vision, the Tathāgata, the Worthy One, the Perfectly Awakened One, seeing with his eye the supreme meaning, taught one dharma through which a noble disciple, while dwelling in the remainder, exhausts the outflows and achieves liberation of mind. Is this so?"

"Householder! The World-Honored One who had wisdom and vision, the Tathāgata, the Worthy One, the Perfectly Awakened One, seeing with his eye the supreme meaning, taught one dharma through which a noble disciple, while dwelling in the remainder, exhausts the outflows and achieves liberation of mind."

"How, Venerable Ānanda, did the Tathāgata, the Worthy One, the Perfectly Awakened One, seeing with his eye the supreme meaning, teach one dharma through which a noble disciple, while dwelling in the remainder, exhausts the outflows and achieves liberation of mind?"

"Householder! A noble disciple, as instructed, being liberated from sensual desire, enters and dwells in the four dhyānas in correct attainment. That disciple, depending on entry into the Dharma, contemplates and examines the characteristics of dharmas and abides. That disciple, depending on entry into the Dharma, contemplating and examining the characteristics of dharmas and abiding, may in that very state exhaust the outflows. If not abiding in that state, one may still exhaust the outflows. If one delights in the Dharma, loves the Dharma, practices the Dharma, and reverences the Dharma, obtaining the joy of the Dharma, then the five lower fetters are exhausted and one obtains a transformation birth, attaining parinirvāṇa as a non-returner, not returning to this world. This, householder, is what the World-Honored One who had wisdom and vision, the Tathāgata, the Worthy One, the Perfectly Awakened One, seeing with his eye the supreme meaning, taught as one dharma through which a noble disciple, while dwelling in the remainder, exhausts the outflows and achieves liberation of mind.

"Furthermore, householder! A noble disciple, as instructed, with a mind pervaded by loving-kindness, fills one direction and abides in correct attainment. Likewise with the second, third, and fourth directions, above, below, and all around, the mind pervaded by loving-kindness: without enmity, without duality, without anger, extremely vast, extremely great, immeasurable, and thoroughly developed, fills all directions and abides in correct attainment. Likewise with a mind pervaded by compassion, sympathetic joy, and equanimity, filling all directions and abiding in correct attainment. That disciple, depending on entry into the Dharma, contemplates and examines the characteristics of dharmas and abides. Abiding in that state, one may exhaust the outflows. If not abiding in that state, one may still exhaust the outflows. If one delights in the Dharma, loves the Dharma, practices and reverences the Dharma, and obtains the joy of the Dharma, then the five lower fetters are exhausted and one obtains a transformation birth, attaining parinirvāṇa as a non-returner, not returning to this world. This, householder, is what the World-Honored One who had wisdom and vision, the Tathāgata, the Worthy One, the Perfectly Awakened One, seeing with his eye the supreme meaning, taught as the foremost dharma through which a noble disciple, while dwelling in the remainder, exhausts the outflows and achieves liberation of mind.

"Furthermore, householder! A noble disciple, as instructed, transcending all perceptions of form, up to entering and dwelling in the sphere of neither-perception-nor-non-perception in correct attainment. That disciple, depending on entry into the Dharma, contemplates and examines the characteristics of dharmas, up to exhausting the outflows and achieving liberation of mind."

Then the householder Dasama of Aṭṭhaka addressed the Venerable Ānanda: "How wonderful! Venerable Ānanda! I asked the Venerable Ānanda about one door to the deathless, and you have taught me twelve doors to the deathless. How abundant, Venerable Ānanda! Among these twelve doors to the deathless, depending on each respective door, one may safely guide oneself. It is like, Venerable Ānanda, a multi-storied building not far from a city or village, with twelve doors on each side of each room. If a person enters and has some urgent matter, or if a fire breaks out to burn the building, and the person's life is imperiled, without benefit, without safety, without happiness: that person can safely escape through any of the twelve doors as they wish. Just so, how wonderful, Venerable Ānanda! I asked the Venerable Ānanda about one door to the deathless, and you have taught me twelve doors to the deathless. Venerable Ānanda! This is most abundant. Among the twelve doors to the deathless, depending on each respective door, one may each find escape.

"Venerable Ānanda! If even those brāhmaṇas who follow evil dharmas still give offerings to their teachers, how much more should I, now in the presence of the Great Teacher, not make offerings of material things?" Then the householder Dasama of Aṭṭhaka, that very night, prepared fine and pure food and drink. Having prepared the fine and pure food and drink, at the proper time he set out seats and invited the bhikṣu saṃgha of the Kukkuṭārāma and the bhikṣu saṃgha of Vaiśālī, gathering them all together. After the bhikṣu saṃghas had gathered, he served the fine and pure food and drink with his own hands. When they had finished eating and put away their bowls, and the washing water had been passed, he purchased a dwelling with five hundred types of goods and gave it to the Venerable Ānanda as a personal possession. The Venerable Ānanda then gave it to the resident saṃgha.

Ānanda spoke thus. The householder Dasama of Aṭṭhaka, having heard what the Venerable Ānanda taught, was delighted and joyful.
