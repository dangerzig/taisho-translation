# 佛說十號經
## Sūtra of the Ten Titles, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0782

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Devaśāntika (天息災)

---

Tathāgata, Worthy of Offerings, Perfectly Awakened One, Complete in Knowledge and Conduct, Well-Gone One, Knower of the World and Unsurpassed One, Tamer of Men, Teacher of Devas and Humans, Buddha, World-Honored One.

Ānanda asked: "What is meant by Tathāgata?"

The Buddha said: "Bhikṣu! In the past, when I was on the causal ground as a bodhisattva, I cultivated numerous practices through many lives, seeking the unsurpassed, perfect awakening. Now I have attained bodhi, nirvāṇa, and all that is true and real. Through the right view of the noble eightfold path I have realized this, and therefore I am called Tathāgata. Just as the Perfectly Awakened Ones of the past tamed and stilled the mind and attained nirvāṇa, so too am I called Tathāgata."

"What is meant by Worthy of Offerings?"

The Buddha said: "In the past, on the causal ground, the wholesome dharmas I practiced, the deportment and precepts, the ten wholesome roots and powers, I cultivated and caused to grow. Having cultivated and fulfilled them to their ultimate completion, at the time of realizing nirvāṇa I severed all afflictions entirely, causing body, speech, and mind to be pure and undefiled, forever destroying the afflictions like cutting off the head of a palm tree so that it never sprouts again. Furthermore, because the afflictions of greed, anger, delusion, and the like are exhausted, in all the destinies one is never again bound to rebirth. One transcends the four difficult sufferings of birth, old age, illness, and death. The two kinds, affliction and suffering, are forever unarisen. Therefore the title Worthy of Offerings is established.

"Furthermore, if the people of the world offer clothing, bedding, food and drink, medicine, banners, jeweled canopies, incense, flowers, lamps, and fruit, as well as the most excellent things of devas and humans, as offerings to the Buddha, they obtain the supreme good fortune of wealth, honor, and auspiciousness. This is the meaning of the title Worthy of Offerings."

"What is meant by Perfectly Awakened One?"

The Buddha said: "The Tathāgata possesses omniscience and in all places there is nothing he does not know. Through the four foundations of mindfulness, the four right efforts, the four bases of spiritual power, the five faculties, the five powers, the seven factors of awakening, the noble eightfold path, the twelve links of dependent arising, the four noble truths, and such dharmas, he equally awakens all sentient beings, causing them to give rise to wisdom, sever delusion, and realize the fruit of srotāpanna, sakṛdāgāmin, anāgāmin, and arhat, complete with the three insights and six supernatural powers. Furthermore, for those who set their minds on the Great Vehicle and cultivate through the various grounds, severing all habitual bonds and accomplishing the unsurpassed awakening: this is called Perfectly Awakened One."

"What is meant by Complete in Knowledge and Conduct?"

The Buddha said: "Knowledge refers to the three knowledges: the knowledge of the divine eye, the knowledge of past lives, and the knowledge of the exhaustion of outflows. Conduct being complete means that the Tathāgata's karma of body, speech, and mind is well-cultivated and fulfilled, right, true, and pure. Having a great robe, bowl, and such things, he freely contemplates them without attachment. Through the power of his own vows, all practices are cultivated to fulfillment. This is the title Complete in Knowledge and Conduct."

"What is meant by Well-Gone One?"

The Buddha said: "This means wondrously departed. Just as greed, anger, delusion, and the like lead sentient beings to the evil destinies, which is not a good departure, the Tathāgata's right wisdom can sever all delusions and wondrously transcend the world, arriving at the fruit of Buddhahood. Therefore he is called the Well-Gone One."

"What is meant by Knower of the World and Unsurpassed One?"

The Buddha said: "The world refers to the desire realm, the form realm, and the formless realm. Hell-dwellers, hungry ghosts, animals, and such, each possess the form-aggregate, feeling-aggregate, perception-aggregate, formations-aggregate, and consciousness-aggregate; the eye-faculty, ear-faculty, nose-faculty, tongue-faculty, body-faculty, and mind-faculty; and the objects cognized by the six kinds of consciousness. All such dharmas are called 'the world.' To have perfect awakening and perfect knowledge of these is called Knower of the World. Furthermore, among all beings in the world, whether two-footed, four-footed, many-footed, or without feet, among the devas of desire and form, whether with perception, without perception, or neither with perception nor without perception, whether ordinary or noble, among all sentient beings, only the Buddha is first, supreme, and without equal. This is the title Unsurpassed One."

"What is meant by Tamer of Men?"

The Buddha said: "The Buddha is the great being who is able to tame both the good and the evil. The evil, through unwholesome karma of the three kinds, commit various evils and fall into the hells, the realm of hungry ghosts, and the realm of animals, receiving evil retribution. The good, through body, speech, and mind, cultivate various wholesome deeds and obtain the fruit of happiness among devas and humans. Both good and evil are all created by the mind. The Buddha uses the Dharma of ultimate-meaning nirvāṇa to reveal and tame them, causing them to be separated from defilement and to obtain the supreme, quiescent nirvāṇa. Therefore he is called the Tamer of Men."

"What is meant by Teacher of Devas and Humans?"

The Buddha said: "The Buddha is not merely a teacher for Ānanda alone, one bhikṣu. All bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, upāsikās, and the devas and humans, śramaṇas, brahmins, māra kings, non-Buddhist practitioners, Śakra, Brahmā, nāgas, and devas all take refuge in him, follow his teaching, and practice it. All become children of the Buddha. Therefore he is called Teacher of Devas and Humans."

"What is meant by Buddha?"

"Wisdom is complete, and the three awakenings are perfectly bright. Therefore he is called Buddha."

The Buddha told Ānanda: "Once, as I was walking, a brahmin came and asked me: 'Why did your father and mother give you the name Buddha?' The Buddha answered: 'What the world knows, I can know. What the world perceives, I can also perceive. What can be attained through cessation, I have also attained through cessation. I possess omniscience and know all things. Through innumerable kalpas of various practices, I have left the dust far behind and parted from defilement. Now I have attained the unsurpassed bodhi, and therefore the title Buddha is established.'"

"What is meant by World-Honored One?"

The Buddha said: "On the causal ground, I carefully examined and observed all wholesome dharmas, the dharmas of the precepts, the dharmas of the mind, and the dharmas of wisdom. I also observed how the unwholesome dharmas of greed and the like can bring about the suffering of existence, birth, cessation, and the like. With undefiled wisdom I destroyed those afflictions and attained the unsurpassed awakening. Therefore devas and humans, ordinary beings and noble ones, the worldly and the world-transcending all honor and revere him. Hence the title World-Honored One."
