# 佛說栴檀香身陀羅尼經
## Sūtra of the Candana Fragrant Body Dhāraṇī Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1387

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmabhadra (法賢)

---

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed Ānanda, saying: "There is a dhāraṇī called the Candana Fragrant Body. This dhāraṇī possesses great power and is able to bestow upon sentient beings a vast accumulation of merit. If there is a person who obtains this dhāraṇī, arouses a most sincere mind, and reads, recites, receives, and upholds it firmly without regression, all the extremely weighty past karma of such a person will be entirely extinguished, and in lives to come they will attain a supremely excellent karmic result.

"Furthermore, if there is a person who wishes to behold Bodhisattva Avalokiteśvara, they should first recite and master [the dhāraṇī] in a pure place. Then, selecting an auspicious day, at the time of sunrise they should use white sandalwood paste to prepare a maṇḍala, burn candana incense within it, and offer exquisite flowers, then begin reciting the dhāraṇī eight thousand times. When the count is complete, they should spread kuśa grass before the maṇḍala and lie down there with a devout heart. Having done so for seven days, the bodhisattva will appear in his very own form and cause whatever the reciter seeks to be fulfilled.

"Furthermore, if there is a person who, due to karmic retribution, suffers from leprosy and malignant sores and skin ailments, they should recite the dhāraṇī three times before the Buddha, empowering gomaya and scented clay to form a maṇḍala. Having made offerings to the Buddha, they should then take the scented clay of the maṇḍala and smear it upon their own body; the sores and leprosy from which they suffer will quickly be healed and cured, and even their past karma will likewise be extinguished."

He then spoke the dhāraṇī:

*Namo candanāgāya tathāgatāya arhate samyaksaṃbuddhāya, namo candraprabhāvāya kumārabhūtāya, namo ākāśagarbhāya bodhisattvāya mahāsattvāya, namo āryāvalokiteśvarāya bodhisattvāya mahāsattvāya mahākāruṇikāya, āryāvalokiteśvarāya adhiṣṭhāni nāni śāye śāmi tathāvigati māricike nṛdhāmāmi durānupraveṣṭhi candanagandha samantapariśodhanī śuddhaśrīcaritrāvatī mahāpratibhe sarvabuddhādhiṣṭhite bhagavati pāpavināraṇī svāhā*

At that time, Ānanda, having heard the Buddha speak this great dhāraṇī, rejoiced and received it with faith, paid homage to the Buddha, and withdrew.
