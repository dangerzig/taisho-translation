# 摩醯首羅天法要
## Essential Methods of Maheśvara Deva

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1279

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown.

---

At that time, Maheśvara Deva was in the heavens, sporting with various celestial maidens, enjoying transformative pleasures and performing divine music, when suddenly from his hair there appeared a celestial maiden, surpassing in beauty and foremost in the arts, whom none among the heavenly host could equal. He then spoke this mantra:

*Namo ujjhiśa-mahā-viṣa-śikhā-dhi-biyā prapratīya śikara ja saṃlim saṃlim tadyathā śvanoti-kaṃ-bhara-mā-nok-ṛ-nī hūṃ phaṭ.*

"One who recites this mantra need not distinguish between pure and impure, married or unmarried, whether one eats the five pungent foods and meat with wine or abstains. One who constantly upholds and recites it will have all virtuous wishes fulfilled. How much less doubt can there be that one of pure three karmas will quickly attain siddhi? If a person recites this mantra 108 times at sunset, efficacious results will immediately appear.

"Next, the method for painting the image. First paint an image of Maheśvara Deva, of exceptional and surpassingly beautiful countenance. From the hairline emerges a celestial maiden, the most beautiful, wearing wondrous jeweled necklaces, with bangles and bracelets on both arms. Her left hand holds celestial flowers. She gazes at the Deva-king with a joyful and charming expression. Her right hand reaches down as if grasping her skirt. Seated upon the left knee is Mātaṅga Deva.

"Next, the mudrā. Both hands externally crossed, left pressing right, forming a bond. Thumbs, little fingers, offering and power fingers erect. Dhyāni and wisdom fingers extended and joined; the dhyāni finger is used for invitation and summoning. The mantra:

*Oṃ vitāra-sanī paramā-ri-dha-nī cinda-nī cinda-nī bhinda-ni bhinda-nī svāhā.*

"If a person daily upholds this mudrā and mantra and makes offerings to Maheśvara Deva, various verifications will be obtained. Also, by always performing this mudrā and mantra as an offering to all buddhas, the buddhas will generate joy, and it also proves effective in curing all diseases.

"If one recites it a full 1,000 times, women will become joyful. If one recites 2,000 times, all people will become joyful. If one recites 3,000 times, non-human beings will delight. Recite 4,000 times, and all vināṭara ghosts will rejoice. Recite 5,000 times, and all nāga-maidens will generate a joyful heart. If one recites up to 10,000 times, all devas, nāgas, yakṣas, and five-supernatural-power sages will all rejoice.

"If one wishes to see one's original body, take charred firewood remaining from cremation, recite the mantra 108 times while summoning, and one will see one's original body.

"Another method: if one wishes to subjugate an enemy, take red earth and draw the person's form, write their name, and tread upon it with the left foot. Recite the mantra 108 times. Regardless of distance, that person will naturally come and submit.

"Another method: if one wishes to cause husband and wife to love each other, draw their forms, write their names, and tread upon it as before. They will immediately love and respect each other.

"Another method: if one wishes to gain the love and respect of kings, great ministers, or all people, perform it as before.

"To cure ghost-affliction: take *agāra* wood, cut it into twenty-one pieces, recite the mantra twenty-one times, burning one piece per recitation. The disease will be cured.

"For spirit-affliction: take asafoetida, benzoin, realgar, costus root, and chinaberry leaves in equal parts. Pound and sieve, mix with water into pills. Recite the mantra and burn them. The disease will be cured.

"For fox-enchantment affliction: take realgar, benzoin, ligusticum, single-headed garlic, rhinoceros horn, ram's urine, white horse's hoof-scrapings, and donkey dung in equal portions. These eight ingredients are prepared as a vermilion preparation and burned. Fumigate into the nostrils and anoint the body.

"If one wishes to establish a boundary: empower water with the mantra seven times, then sprinkle it in the four directions. The boundary may extend as near or far as desired, and will be properly established."

---

*Note*: The Maheśvara in this text is the Īśāna Deva of the northeast.
