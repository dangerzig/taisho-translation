# 聖無能勝金剛火陀羅尼經
## Sūtra of the Holy Aparājita Vajra-Fire Dhāraṇī

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1236

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmadeva (法天).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was in a jeweled-peak pavilion on Mount Sumeru, adorned with various gold and treasures, emitting countless rays of light illuminating all around the mountain peaks. There were also sandalwood, aloeswood, unguent, powdered incense, various fine flowers, and jeweled canopies and bells adorning the scene.

At that time, devas, nāgas, yakṣas, asuras, gandharvas, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, vidyādhara-ṛṣis, bhūtas, vināyakas, piśācas, and visible and invisible mātṛ-hosts came and assembled, filling an area of five hundred yojanas.

There were also seven thousand five hundred bodhisattva mahāsattvas and twelve thousand five hundred bhikṣus, who all came and assembled to hear the dharma.

At that time, the devas, nāgas, yakṣas, asuras, gandharvas, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, vidyādhara-ṛṣi hosts, bhūtas, vināyakas, piśācas, and visible and invisible mātṛ-hosts in the assembly cried out loudly: "Alas, alas!" At that time, Śākyamuni Tathāgata, Arhat, Perfectly Awakened One asked them: "Why do you cry out in fear and suffering?" They addressed the World-Honored One: "We have been terrified and subdued by a great powerful holy one. We have now come here. We beseech your compassion to grant us rescue and protection."

The World-Honored One said: "In this most supreme and vast maṇḍala, there is a bodhisattva mahāsattva who has attained the great stage of succession, who shall inherit the position of the Abhiṣeka Dharma King, and who can awaken beings to wholesome and unwholesome karma. He shall comfort your minds."

Having spoken thus, in an instant there appeared a bodhisattva mahāsattva named Vajrapāṇi, like a vajra mountain emitting golden light illuminating the entire world. He wore a jeweled crown, his face like a full moon. In his hands he held a vajra mace, a vajra stake, and a vajra lasso, endowed with great merit-power able to destroy all evil. He led five thousand members of the vajra family as his retinue. Their names were: Vajra-Naṇḍa General, Vajra-Vajragarabha General, Vajra-Vajraketu General, Vajra-Vajravabhāsa General, Vajra-Nīlavajra General, Vajra-Ratnavajra General, Vajra-Guhyavajra General, Vajra-Cakravajra General, Vajra-Mahāvajra-Naṇḍa Lord, Vajra Guardian of the World Vidyārāja, and also Vajra-Māra, Vajra-Vināyaka, Vajra-Planet Youth, Vajra-Ostāraka, Vajra Great Evil, and others. All came and assembled in the great maṇḍala, emitting pure light illuminating everything, causing the minds of sentient beings to be filled with joy.

At that time, Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva bared his right shoulder, bowed at the Buddha's feet, circumambulated to the right three times, joined his palms, and stood.

At that time the World-Honored One commanded Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva: "In this assembly there are devas, nāgas, yakṣas, asuras, and various classes of bhūtas who cry out in fear and suffering. Comfort them and bring them happiness." Thereupon Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva rose from his seat and, before the hosts of devas, nāgas, asuras, and bhūtas, entered a samādhi called "Most Supreme Vajra Implement Radiant Lamp." He displayed great supernatural powers, causing the great trichiliocosm to resound with thunder, hail, lightning, and flashes like great flames of fire.

When the sentient beings saw this, their suffering and fear were removed. Furthermore, sentient beings as numerous as the sands of the Ganges, upon seeing this, generated minds of pure brahma-conduct, precept-observance, and patience. They offered all their treasures to Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva and all wished to follow and receive the wondrous dharma.

At that time, Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva proclaimed for the hosts of devas, nāgas, and yakṣas the four noble truths, dependent origination, the samādhi gate, the ten pāramitās, and the method of the root vajra family's great maṇḍala ritual. All those of the Brahmā Heaven, Maheśvara Heaven, Varuṇa, Kubera, Maruts, Garuḍa, Nārāyaṇa, Yama Heaven, and Indra Heaven who attained accomplishment; asuras and gandharvas who attained accomplishment; and even evil bhūtas and great rākṣasas who were difficult to tame were tamed. Those who harbored evil minds generated wholesome minds. Those who held wrong views attained right views. Those who harbored anger arose compassion. Those who were enveloped in ignorance became clear and understanding. Those who previously possessed great strength lost their strength. Those who previously could become invisible could no longer hide. Others' mantras could be cut off and terminated; one's own mantras could be brought to perfection. Abiding in the maṇḍala, all were awakened and subdued. It should be thus.

At that time the Buddha commanded Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva: "Proclaim the Aparājita Vajra-Fire Great Dhāraṇī, and cause them to receive and uphold it."

Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva, by the power of this dhāraṇī's true awesome virtue, emitted great light illuminating the entire assembly of devas, humans, asuras, and all the worthies and holy ones. Furthermore, by the power of the dhāraṇī, the light of the sun, moon, and stars was eclipsed, the great ocean surged and boiled, and Mount Sumeru, king of mountains, trembled throughout.

At that time, from within the great blazing-light treasury jeweled peak, the Vajrapāṇi maiden transformed her own body into a great blazing flame and went through space to the presence of the Mahāsthāmaprāpta Bodhisattva Mahāsattva. She circumambulated to the right three times, bowed in homage, and standing to one side, addressed the assembly: "Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva has proclaimed the Tathāgata's samādhi-heart. You should each lay down your vajra implements and seek this great dhāraṇī dharma."

At that time the World-Ruler Great Brahmā King, devas, humans, asuras, and others all spoke in unison: "As the bodhisattva commands, we now obey." Thereupon Maheśvara laid down his trident, Nārāyaṇa laid down his wheel, Yama laid down his staff, Kubera laid down his mace, Indra laid down his vajra pestle, Varuṇa laid down his lasso, the heavenly māra laid down his obstacles, the asuras laid down their might, the Maruts laid down their delusions, the mother-ghost hosts abandoned their distraction, the nāga-spirit hosts abandoned their venomous anger, and the yakṣas, rākṣasas, kiṃnaras, piśācas, bhūtas, brahmas, māras, kṣasas, kumbhāṇḍas, and others all relinquished their powers. Before the bodhisattva, with single-minded reverence, Vajrapāṇi proclaimed the dhāraṇī:

*Namo ratna-trayāya namaś-caṇḍa-vajra-pāṇaye mahā-yakṣa-senā-pataye tadyathā tathāgata-samādhi-dhāraṇī-māvārita iṣyamī ehi vajrī kuṭila-kuṭi pāvaka kula-śikhe kāpī-rā-keśi tacci-caci-juci moṭṭhani-kāri-kaci muci-vaci vajriṇi dhiyurule muku-muku sumu-sumu bhara-sara-sara sphuṭa-sphuṭa-sphuṭa-sphuṭa uniyute piṅgale caci-vicaci jvāla-piṅgale sarva-deva-gaṇa-praharaṇa māya-siddhi-ṇa ike suluṃru vajra-guṇi nīrdhā-sara-praharaṇi ratna-traya-ṇi-dikāra-bhūte vajra-dhara-viśva-sanika-prahata pramaridhanī svāhā*

"If there are people who hear this dhāraṇī and with single mind read, recite, recollect, and uphold it, they will enter the samādhi of all worldly and supramundane maṇḍalas. If a person rejoices in it and reveres it, that person will not be troubled by binding mantras, poisons, or any manner of terrors."

At that time, when the Buddha commanded Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva to proclaim the great dhāraṇī, all the celestial sons of the desire and form heavens rained down various supremely wondrous fragrant flowers from the sky as offerings, and further praised it with sublime brahma-toned songs. At that time, all the worthies, holy ones, and bhikṣu assemblies of the great maṇḍala gathering, together with the great bodhisattva mahāsattvas, were all greatly delighted, faithfully received the teaching, and put it into practice.
