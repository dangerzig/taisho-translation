# Text of Merit-Transfer

## *Huíxiàng Wén* (迴向文)

Taishō 2848

Author unknown. A Dunhuang liturgical text of merit-transfer dedications for various officials and clergy.

*Note: This is a formulaic Dunhuang merit-transfer text (*huíxiàng wén*, 迴��文), dedicating the merit of a Buddhist ceremony to a succession of secular and religious dignitaries. It dates from the period of Tang or Five Dynasties governance in the Dunhuang/Hexi region.*

Furthermore, I uphold this supreme goodness and next dedicate it to adorn the present Sage Ruler of the Great Tang. I humbly pray: may the sage's longevity be flourishing and grand. May the imperial golden wheel turn together with the cosmic wheel. May the light of Shùn (*Shùn*, 舜) and the light of the Buddha shine on high together. May a hundred kings be perpetually renewed through ten thousand kalpas. May the Imperial Crown Prince, the hidden star above the lesser sea, be clear and radiant. May he rise like a great rock, firm as a bulwark, securing the realm.

Furthermore, I uphold this supreme goodness and dedicate it to adorn the six preceptors (*héshàng*, 和尚). I humbly pray: may they long receive imperial favor, serve as consecration masters (*guàndǐng*, 灌頂) and national teachers, forever stabilize the terrace steps, and assist the enlightened king's governance and transformation.

Next, I uphold this supreme goodness and dedicate it to adorn the Chief Monastic Administrator (*Dūsēngtǒng*, 都僧統). I humbly pray: may the peak of your wisdom-mountain tower loftily. May the ocean of your virtue be vast and deep. May your lifespan equal the pine and bamboo. May your blessings and good fortune extend far into the distance. May you long receive the nation's favor, forever spreading the wind of compassion. May your excellent reputation overflow in all directions, and may your refined example flow through the age of the semblance dharma.

Next, I uphold this supreme goodness and dedicate it to adorn the honored person of the Minister (*Shàngshū*, 尚書). I humbly pray: may the mountain of your blessings be forever firm. May your divine longevity be without limit. May your achievements tower above the clouded mountains, and may the depth of your virtue surpass the great sea. Going forth, you bear the banner and tally to pacify all regions. Entering, you sit in the court hall, and all the hundred officials take you as their model. May you forever preserve the springtime of auspicious days. May your lifespan equal the pine and bamboo, your longevity the kalpa-stone.

Furthermore, I uphold this supreme merit and dedicate it to adorn the Grand Master. I humbly pray: may your form endure like the great earth through a thousand ages, ever at peace. May your blessings be compared to mountains and rivers, spanning ten thousand ages in perpetual firmness. Then may your fragrant reputation resound in the imperial capital, soaring on wings through the clouded avenues, your refined honor flowing at the luan-phoenix pond, your scales washing in the waters of the Han.

Furthermore, I uphold this supreme goodness and dedicate it to adorn the two Attending Censors, Ān (*Ān*, 安) and Yáo (*Yáo*, 姚). I humbly pray: may you raise jade terraces beyond the heavens, may you fly purple canopies through the clouded avenues. May your lifespans be as long as heaven, your blessings as far as the earth. May you preserve the border regions with movement and rest in safety. May the eight directions face your shining with coming and going in pure good fortune.

Next, I uphold this supreme goodness and dedicate it to adorn the Minister's sons: may they inherit the favor of a thousand autumns, forever residing in the glory of ten thousand generations. May loyalty and filial piety be fully combined, and may the vermilion gates be inherited in succession.

Next, I uphold this supreme goodness and dedicate it to adorn the Buddhist Teaching Instructors. I humbly pray: may your glory and honor be ever renewed, your fragrant fame spread like rosy clouds. Entering, you support the imperial seat; going forth, you govern the monastic assembly. May groups of living beings, following their kind, successively ascend the heights. May the treasure-raft be constantly bestowed upon the ocean of suffering.

Next, I uphold this supreme goodness and dedicate it to adorn the Buddhist Vinaya Masters. I sincerely pray: may your physical strength grow firm, and may your lifespans extend like clouds. May you always serve as the ford and bridge across the ocean of suffering, forever standing as the walls and moat of the dharma-gate.

Next, I uphold this supreme merit and dedicate it to adorn the Military Governors and lords. May longevity stretch far into the distance. As days pass and months come, may blessings and good fortune mutually shine. As long as heaven and earth endure.

Next, I uphold this supreme merit and dedicate it to adorn the Tribal Commissioners and others. I sincerely pray: may blessings and emoluments bring unceasing joy. May honor and fame be daily renewed and flourishing. May fine reputation be as fully equipped as rosy clouds. May the city walls be splendidly adorned. May all be wholly loyal. May the tribal officials be purely upright.

Next, I uphold this supreme merit and dedicate it to adorn the Elder Monks and Worthies of Great Virtue. May they long bestow the teachings of the semblance age, protect and guard the city of the dharma, forever illuminate the lamp of compassion, and maintain the order of the brahma-abode.

Next, I uphold this supreme goodness and dedicate it to adorn the Dharma-Generals and Monks of Great Virtue. I sincerely pray: may the mountain of wisdom stand towering, may the waters of virtue be clear and pure. May the branches of meditation flourish and the flowers of awakening bloom. May the incense of the precepts perfume and the jewel of wisdom shine.

Next, I uphold this supreme merit and dedicate it to adorn the Nuns of Great Virtue. I sincerely pray: may the four reliances be constantly fulfilled and the six pāramitās practiced with undivided attention. May the tree of aspiration be forever spring and the lamp of the mind shine eternally. May the water of the eight merits wash away defilement and eliminate disaster. May the seven pure wonderful flowers adorn the dharma-body.

Next, I uphold this supreme merit and dedicate it to adorn the Officials and Elders. I sincerely pray: may your longevity be like the pine and cypress, your blessings like the rivers and lakes. Through ten thousand years and a thousand autumns, may there be no storm and no change.

Next, I uphold this supreme merit and dedicate it to adorn the Lay Elders. I sincerely pray: may they hear once and awaken a thousandfold, attaining the great dhāraṇī. May the defilement of sin be eliminated without delay. May the wholesome sprouts of merit grow and increase. May spring and autumn bring good fortune, may heat and cold be in season. May wealth and honor be preserved for a thousand ages, and may auspicious joy extend through accumulated kalpas.

Next, I uphold this supreme merit and dedicate it to adorn the Venerable Lǐ (*Lǐ*, 李) and others. Since I have long received their instruction and teaching, and in vain received their great hall, I regret that during their lifetime I could not fully follow their guidance, and after their passing I am ashamed to ascend the dharma seat. Reflecting upon this, I increase the offerings of praise and reverently present this merit-transfer. I sincerely pray: may their spirits roam in the Garden of Nirvāṇa (*Nàiyuàn*, 奈苑), reverently seeking awakening and pursuing truth. In the evening may they draw near to the flower terrace, steeped in the fragrance of the precepts, offering to the sacred golden wheel in endless turning, ever coursing through the garden of joy, room after room of flourishing repose, floating far upon the waters of merit.

Next, I uphold this supreme merit and next dedicate it to adorn the departed parents of a certain person, for the sake of their spirits on the road ahead. I humbly pray: may their feet step upon the golden realm, may their spirits roam the pure world. May they cross on the boat of compassion over the waters of meditation, forever drying up the river of delusion. May they wield the sword of wisdom through the forest of characteristics, may they forever receive this among their relatives, may their purity extend to distant branches, and may all receive the moistening benefit.
