# 六字大陀羅尼呪經
## Sūtra of the Great Dhāraṇī of Six Syllables

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1046

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown (失譯)

---

Thus have I heard.

At one time the Bhagavān was dwelling on Gṛdhrakūṭa Mountain near Rājagṛha, together with a great assembly of five hundred bhikṣus. At that time the elder Ānanda had been seized by the spell-craft of a Caṇḍālī woman. At that time the elder Ānanda addressed the Buddha, saying: "World-Honored One! I am now being forcibly taken away by another. Bhagavān! I am now being forcibly taken away by another. Sugata!"

At that time the Bhagavān said to the elder Ānanda: "Come, Ānanda! Do not be frightened. Ānanda! You should receive and maintain the Great Dhāraṇī of Six Syllables, for the sake of bringing benefit, security, happiness, and good fortune to the fourfold assembly." And he recited the dhāraṇī:

*Siddhi-tedhāṃ. Aṇḍāli. Paṇḍāli. Kalāci. Keyūre. Sāti-pati. Ye-pati. Diśā-pati. Bindu-mati.*

"Ānanda! This dhāraṇī can cause food that has not been digested to be immediately digested; it can cure diseases such as vomiting and diarrhea; it can eliminate wind ailments, fever ailments, cold ailments, and miscellaneous ailments; it can destroy all manner of harmful spells and sorcery; it can destroy reanimated corpses; it can destroy all enmity-rites performed through effigies and venom-curses. Ānanda! If any person knows the name of this divine dhāraṇī, that person will have no fear of the danger of royal persecution, no fear of the danger of enemies, no fear of the danger of bandits, no fear of the danger of fire, no fear of the danger of water. Whether in cities, towns, villages, or in the open wilderness, one will be entirely without fear. Nor will others lie in wait to seek out one's faults; there will be no faults to speak of. If one ingests poisonous substances, the poison will be unable to cause harm; it will be transformed into benefit.

"Ānanda! This Great Dhāraṇī of Six Syllables was spoken by the seven samyaksaṃbuddhas; it was also spoken by Brahmarāja, lord of the Sahā world; Śakra Devānām Indra and the Four Great Heavenly Kings likewise spoke it and all rejoiced in it; destroying all harmful spells, suppressing and subduing reanimated corpses, and utterly shattering and extirpating all enmity-rites performed through effigies and venom-curses."

The elder Ānanda, having heard what the Buddha had taught, rejoiced and respectfully carried it out.
