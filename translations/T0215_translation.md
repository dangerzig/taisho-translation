# 群牛譬經
## Sūtra of the Parable of the Herd of Oxen

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0215

Translated from the Chinese. Western Jin dynasty, translated by Fajù (法炬)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Bhagavān was in the city of Śrāvastī, in the Jeta Grove, the Anāthapiṇḍada Monastery.

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the bhikṣus: "Suppose there is a herd of oxen, gentle and well-trained in disposition. Wherever they go, they select tender grass to eat and drink cool, clear water. Then a donkey thinks to himself: 'These oxen are gentle and well-trained in disposition. Wherever they go, they select tender grass to eat and drink cool, clear water. I too can do as they do: select tender grass to eat and drink cool, clear water.' The donkey then enters the herd of oxen, pawing the earth with his front hooves, jostling and annoying the oxen, and imitating the oxen's bellowing, though he cannot change his own voice: 'I too am an ox! I too am an ox!' But the oxen gore him with their horns and kill him, and then leave him behind.

"It is the same in this case. If there is a bhikṣu who is not diligent and practices unwholesome dharmas; who is not a śramaṇa yet claims to be a śramaṇa; who does not cultivate the brahma-conduct yet claims to cultivate the brahma-conduct; who is not learned and holds wrong views; whose deportment is not complete, whose walking, coming, going, bending, stretching, looking up, and looking down are improper, who does not understand how to wear the robe or hold the bowl; who cannot obtain robes, food and drink, bedding, or medicines for illness: if such a one sees bhikṣus who are diligent and practice wholesome dharmas, who among the śramaṇas accomplish the śramaṇa's conduct, who cultivate the brahma-conduct, who are learned and broadly studied and practice wholesome dharmas, whose deportment is entirely good, whose walking, coming, going, bending, stretching, looking up, looking down, wearing the robe, and holding the bowl all accord with proper etiquette, who obtain robes, food and drink, bedding, and medicines for illness; then the bhikṣu of unwholesome conduct thinks to himself: 'These many bhikṣus are diligent and practice wholesome dharmas. Among the śramaṇas they accomplish the śramaṇa's conduct, among the brahma-conduct they accomplish the brahma-conduct. Their deportment is complete; their walking, coming, going, bending, stretching, looking up, looking down, wearing the robe, and holding the bowl are all fully equipped. They obtain robes, food and drink, bedding, and medicines for illness, all fully provided. I should now enter their assembly, and I too shall obtain robes, food and drink, bedding, and medicines for illness.'

"Then this unwholesome bhikṣu who practices unwholesome dharmas, who has no śramaṇa conduct yet claims to be a śramaṇa, who has no brahma-conduct yet claims to cultivate the brahma-conduct, who has little learning and holds various wrong views, enters the assembly of those many diligent bhikṣus, wishing to imitate their deportment, their etiquette, their walking, coming, going, bending, stretching, looking up, looking down, wearing the robe, and holding the bowl, just like those excellent bhikṣus who are diligent, practice wholesome dharmas, walk, come, go, bend, stretch, look up, look down, wear the robe, and hold the bowl. He then says: 'I am a śramaṇa! I am a śramaṇa!' But the excellent bhikṣus all perceive and know that this bhikṣu is not diligent yet claims to be diligent, is not a śramaṇa yet claims to be a śramaṇa, does not cultivate the brahma-conduct yet claims to cultivate the brahma-conduct, is not learned and holds various wrong views. Then the excellent bhikṣus expel him beyond the boundary: 'Leave at once! Do not remain in our assembly!' This is like the herd of gentle, well-trained oxen that drive out the donkey.

"Therefore, bhikṣus! That which is not the conduct of a śramaṇa, not the conduct of a brāhmaṇa, should be abandoned and forsaken. The wholesome conduct of the śramaṇa and the wholesome conduct of the brāhmaṇa should be well recited and upheld. Thus, bhikṣus, should you train."

At that time, the bhikṣus, hearing what the Buddha taught, joyfully accepted and practiced it.
