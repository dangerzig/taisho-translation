# 佛為黃竹園老婆羅門說學經
## The Buddha Teaches the Old Brahmin of the Yellow Bamboo Garden about Learning

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0075

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown (失譯)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Bhagavān was in the Yellow Bamboo Garden of Verañjā. At that time, the brahmin of Verañjā, who was old and aged and approaching the end of his life, one hundred and twenty years of age, holding a staff in his hand, went walking after the midday meal, wandering along until he reached the World-Honored One. Having arrived, he exchanged greetings face to face with the World-Honored One. After these greetings, the Verañjā brahmin stood leaning on his staff before the World-Honored One. Having stood to one side, the Verañjā brahmin addressed the World-Honored One: "Gautama, I have heard that the śramaṇa Gautama is young in years and a beginner in training. When great elder śramaṇas and brahmins come to visit, he neither shows them proper respect nor rises from his seat, nor does he invite them to sit. Gautama, I cannot approve of this."

"Brahmin, I do not see anyone in this world, among gods, Māra, Brahmā, śramaṇas, brahmins, the host of gods, or among humans, who could make the Tathāgata show respect by rising from his seat to greet them. Brahmin, if the Tathāgata were to show such deference and rise from his seat, that person's head would split into seven pieces."

"This śramaṇa Gautama is simply lazy and arrogant!"

"Brahmin, there is a reason one might say I have arrogance, but it is not as you suppose. Brahmin, as for the relishing of forms, the relishing of sounds, the relishing of smells, and the relishing of tactile sensations, the Tathāgata has exhausted them, has fully understood them, has cut them off at the root, so that they will not arise again in the future with their attendant fears. Brahmin, there is a reason in this regard, but it is not as you suppose."

"The śramaṇa Gautama is without fear."

"Brahmin, there is again a reason I am without fear, but it is not as you suppose. Brahmin, as for the fear of forms, the fear of sounds, the fear of smells, the fear of tastes, and the fear of tactile sensations, the Tathāgata has exhausted them, has fully understood them, has cut them off at the root, so that they will not arise again in the future. Brahmin, there is a reason in this regard, but it is not as you suppose."

"This śramaṇa Gautama will not enter a womb again."

"Brahmin, there is a reason I will not enter a womb again, but it is not as you suppose. Brahmin, whatever śramaṇas and brahmins must return to existence and enter a womb, I have exhausted this, have fully understood it, have cut it off at the root, so that it will not arise again in the future. I declare that I will not enter a womb. Brahmin, the Tathāgata, the Worthy One, the Perfectly Enlightened One will not return to existence or enter a womb. I have exhausted this, have fully understood it, have cut it off at the root, so that it will not arise again in the future. I will not enter a womb. Brahmin, there is a reason in this regard, but it is not as you suppose.

"Furthermore, brahmin, all beings in this world were caught in ignorance, delighting in ignorance, wrapped in ignorance. I was the first to distinguish the Dharma; I was foremost among beings in proclaiming it. Just as, brahmin, a hen produces ten or twenty eggs and sits upon them at the proper time, turns them at the proper time, and whatever activity that hen performs, among those eggs there is a chick that breaks the shell with its feet and emerges safely. That chick's first action is this. In the same way, brahmin, beings were wrapped in ignorance, covered by ignorance as a shroud. I was the first to distinguish the Dharma; I am declared foremost among beings.

"Brahmin, I carried a handful of grass to the foot of the Bodhi tree. Having arrived, I spread the grass beneath the Bodhi tree, arranged my sitting cloth upon it, and sat cross-legged, vowing not to break my posture until I had accomplished the exhaustion of the outflows. Brahmin, I did not break my posture until I exhausted the outflows.

"Brahmin, liberated from sensuality and liberated from unwholesome states, through self-investigation and self-practice I attained the joy and happiness of the first jhāna and abided therein. Brahmin, at that time I attained the first contemplation, saw the Dharma, and abided in happiness and ease, dwelling without losing the peaceful abode that leads to nirvāṇa.

"Brahmin, with the stilling of initial and sustained thought, with inward confidence and one-pointedness of mind, without initial or sustained thought, attaining the joy born of concentration, I entered and abided in the second jhāna. Brahmin, at that time I attained the second contemplation, saw the Dharma, and abided in happiness and ease, dwelling without losing the peaceful abode that leads to nirvāṇa.

"Brahmin, with the fading of joy, remaining free from attachment, mindful and clearly comprehending, experiencing bodily ease, which the noble ones describe as dwelling in equanimity with mindfulness and ease, I entered and abided in the third jhāna. Brahmin, at that time I attained the third contemplation, saw the Dharma, and abided in happiness and ease, dwelling without losing the peaceful abode that leads to nirvāṇa.

"Brahmin, with the abandoning of pleasure and the abandoning of pain, with the earlier disappearance of joy and sorrow, without pain or pleasure, with equanimity and purified mindfulness, I entered and abided in the fourth jhāna. Brahmin, at that time I attained the fourth contemplation, saw the Dharma, and abided in happiness and ease, dwelling without losing the peaceful abode that leads to nirvāṇa.

"Brahmin, with this concentration my mind became purified, bright, without defilements, free from the secondary defilements, pliant in action, steady, immovable, and I directed it to the knowledge of recollecting past lives, taking it as my witness, guiding my mind accordingly. Brahmin, through these deeds and these teachings, I recollected immeasurable past lives: one birth, two births, a hundred births, a thousand births, one eon, half an eon, immeasurable eons. I recalled the name and clan of this and that being, their character, what they ate, their experience of pleasure and pain, the length of their life. Having died here, they were reborn there; having died there, they were reborn here. Here they had such a name and clan, ate such food, had such a lifespan. Brahmin, at that time, at midnight, I attained the first sacred knowledge. Having formerly practiced without heedlessness, I now attained the practice of concentration. Ignorance was destroyed and knowledge arose; darkness was dispelled and light appeared; the absence of knowledge ceased and knowledge was attained, namely the knowledge of recollecting past lives, taken as witness.

"Brahmin, with this concentration my mind became purified, bright, without defilements, free from the secondary defilements, pliant in action, steady, immovable, and I attained the knowledge of the divine eye, taking it as my witness, guiding my mind accordingly. Brahmin, with the divine eye, purified and surpassing that of humans, I saw beings dying and being reborn, of good appearance and bad appearance, beautiful and ugly, born into good destinies and bad destinies, according to the actions they had performed. I knew this as it truly is. Beings who engage in bodily misconduct, verbal misconduct, and mental misconduct, who are displeasing to the noble ones, who hold wrong views and act in accordance with wrong views: because of these causes and conditions, when their body perishes at death, they are reborn in the evil destinies, in the hells. Beings who engage in good bodily conduct, good verbal conduct, and good mental conduct, who have faith in meritorious action, who hold right views and act in accordance with right views: because of these causes and conditions, when their body perishes at death, they are reborn in the good destinies, in the heavens. Brahmin, at that time, past midnight, I attained the second knowledge. Having formerly practiced without heedlessness, I now attained the practice of concentration. I abandoned ignorance and attained knowledge; darkness was dispelled and light was attained; the absence of knowledge ceased and knowledge arose, namely the knowledge of the divine eye, taken as witness.

"Brahmin, with this concentration my mind became purified, bright, without defilements, free from the secondary defilements, pliant in action, steady, immovable, and I attained the knowledge of the exhaustion of the outflows, taking it as my witness, guiding my mind accordingly. Brahmin, I knew as it truly is: 'This is suffering.' I knew as it truly is: the origin of suffering, the cessation of suffering, and the path leading to the cessation of suffering. I knew as it truly is: 'These are the outflows, the origin of the outflows, the cessation of the outflows, and the path leading to the cessation of the outflows.' Knowing this and seeing this, my mind was liberated from the outflow of sensuality, the outflow of existence, and the outflow of ignorance. Being liberated, I knew that I was liberated. Birth is ended, the holy life has been established, what was to be done has been done, and I knew as it truly is that there is no further existence.

"Brahmin, at that time, toward dawn, I attained the third knowledge. Having formerly practiced without heedlessness, I now attained the practice of concentration. Ignorance was exhausted and knowledge arose; the absence of knowledge ceased and knowledge was attained, namely the knowledge of the exhaustion of outflows, taken as witness.

"Brahmin, there is a correct way to speak of this: a person born in this world among beings has, through practice, become free from both suffering and pleasure. Brahmin, this is the correct way to speak of me. Why is this? Because, brahmin, I am not a person born into this world in ignorance. I have emerged into this world as the most sublime, without suffering or pleasure."

Thereupon, the brahmin of Verañjā set down his staff on the ground, prostrated with his head at the World-Honored One's feet, and praised the World-Honored One: "The World-Honored One is supreme! The World-Honored One is sublime! The World-Honored One is most supreme and sublime! None is equal to the World-Honored One; none can compare with the World-Honored One! The World-Honored One is without affliction, and the World-Honored One bears no anger toward anyone. World-Honored One, I now take refuge in the Dharma and in the Bhikṣu Saṅgha. World-Honored One, I now declare myself an upāsaka. From this day forward, for the rest of my life, I shall abstain from killing. I now take refuge."

Thus spoke the Buddha. The brahmin of Verañjā, having heard what the World-Honored One said, rejoiced and was delighted.
