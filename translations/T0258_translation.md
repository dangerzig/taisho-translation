# 佛說聖佛母小字般若波羅蜜多經
## Sūtra of the Holy Mother of Buddhas Small-Letter Prajñāpāramitā Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0258

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Devaśāntika (天息災)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the World-Honored One was in the city of Rājagṛha, on Vulture Peak Mountain, together with a great assembly of one thousand two hundred and fifty bhikṣus, as well as a hundred thousand koṭi-nayuta bodhisattvas, and also a hundred thousand koṭi-nayuta Brahma kings, Śakra, the world-protectors, and all the great assemblies, reverently surrounding him.

At that time, the World-Honored One was seated in the lotus position upon the Auspicious Treasury Lion Throne. Then the Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Holy Avalokiteśvara rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, placed his right knee on the ground, gazed up at the Honored One without looking away for even a moment, joined his palms in reverence, joyful and exultant, bowed his head to the Buddha's feet, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! May the World-Honored One please teach this Small-Letter Prajñāpāramitā Sūtra for me. Let all sentient beings who hear this Dharma obtain great merit and have all their karmic obstructions definitively eliminated, and swiftly attain the supreme enlightenment in the future. If there are sentient beings who with utmost sincerity receive, uphold, read, and recite this dhāraṇī, whatever they wish for will definitively be accomplished, and they will be free from all māra-obstacles."

At that time, the World-Honored One told the Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Holy Avalokiteśvara: "Excellent, well spoken! Excellent, excellent! Good man! You are able to thus, with utmost sincerity and for the sake of all sentient beings, bring them peace, joy, and long life. Good man! Listen carefully, and with utmost attention hear me teach this Small-Letter Prajñāpāramitā Sūtra. If sentient beings hear this Dharma taught, they will obtain great merit and all their karmic obstructions will be entirely eliminated, and they will definitively and swiftly realize the supreme, perfect enlightenment. If sentient beings resolve to receive and uphold this dhāraṇī, they will be free from all māra-afflictions, and all will be accomplished."

At that time, the Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Holy Avalokiteśvara again addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! May the Sugata now teach, for the sake of all sentient beings, that they may attain peace and joy."

Then the World-Honored One at that time entered the samādhi called Liberation of All Sentient Beings. Having arisen from this concentration, from the white hair-mark between his brows he emitted a hundred thousand koṭi-nayuta rays of light. This great radiance universally illuminated all buddha-lands. All the immeasurable sentient beings who were bathed in this light would definitively and swiftly realize the supreme, perfect enlightenment. All sentient beings in the hells obtained peace and joy. The buddha-lands experienced six kinds of trembling, and above all the Buddhas there rained exquisite candana, agaru, and fine powder incense as offerings.

At that time, the World-Honored One taught this Prajñāpāramitā Sūtra. Then all the bodhisattva-mahāsattvas each gave rise to minds of equanimity, minds of compassion, minds mindful of benefiting others, minds of renouncing all karmic obstructions, minds of various benefits, and minds of prajñāpāramitā. Then the World-Honored One told the Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Holy Avalokiteśvara: "All of you, listen carefully. I now teach you the dhāraṇī of the Holy Mother of Buddhas Small-Letter Prajñāpāramitā:

*Namaḥ Śākyamunaye tathāgatāya arhate samyaksaṃbuddhāya. Tadyathā: mune mune mahāmunaye svāhā.*

The Buddha told the Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Holy Avalokiteśvara: "This dhāraṇī of the Holy Mother of Buddhas Small-Letter Prajñāpāramitā is the means by which all Buddhas attained the supreme, perfect enlightenment. I too, by means of this Small-Letter Prajñāpāramitā dhāraṇī, attained the unsurpassed, perfect enlightenment. In the past there was a Buddha also named Śākyamuni Tathāgata; from that Buddha I heard this Dharma taught, and that Buddha said: 'Thus all Buddhas of the three periods of time, by means of this Dharma, attained Buddhahood.'"

The Buddha further told the Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Holy Avalokiteśvara: "I now bestow upon you a prediction. In the human world, in a future age, you will accomplish the path of Buddhahood, and your title will be Universal Radiance Auspicious Jewel Peak King Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha. Having heard this wondrous Dharma, you should receive, uphold, read, and recite it. Whether you copy it yourself or cause others to copy it, contemplate and understand it, and broadly explain its meaning to all sentient beings, causing them to copy this sūtra, to receive, uphold, and recite it in their own homes, then in future lives they will swiftly accomplish the supreme, perfect enlightenment. At that time, all Tathāgatas will together bear witness for you. I now further teach you the Prajñāpāramitā Dhāraṇī:

*Tadyathā: oṃ jaya jaya padmāvabhāsamini sara-saraṇi viri viri vira-viri kiri kiri nivāta-nupara-nibuddha-rāṇi pūraṇi pūraya bhagavati sarva-śamama-pūraya sarva-sattvānāṃ ca sarva-karma-āvaraṇāni viśodhaya viśodhaya buddhi-adhiṣṭhānāni svāhā.*

The Buddha told the Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Holy Avalokiteśvara: "This supremely wondrous Prajñāpāramitā Dhāraṇī is the mother that gives birth to all Buddhas and bodhisattvas. If there are sentient beings who even briefly hear this Dharma, all the karmic obstructions they have created will be entirely eliminated. This Dharma: even if all Buddhas and all bodhisattvas were to spend a hundred koṭi kalpas proclaiming its merit, they could not exhaust it. Those who can receive, uphold, read, and recite this dhāraṇī are thereby equivalent to having entered all maṇḍalas and received abhiṣeka. It is also as if they had upheld all mantras; all will be accomplished."

At that time, the Bodhisattva Holy Avalokiteśvara addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! For what reason do you further teach this Prajñāpāramitā Dhāraṇī?"

The World-Honored One replied: "I do so out of compassion for all sentient beings of few roots of merit, few skillful means, and much indolence. Therefore I teach this Prajñāpāramitā Dhāraṇī, so that they may receive, uphold, read, and recite it, whether copying it themselves or causing others to copy it. All such sentient beings will swiftly realize the supreme enlightenment."

"So it is, so it is! The World-Honored One has well taught this Prajñāpāramitā Dhāraṇī."

At that time, the Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Holy Avalokiteśvara again addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! This Dharma is truly unprecedented. World-Honored One! This Dharma is truly unprecedented. The Sugata World-Honored One, out of great compassion, wishing to save and liberate all sentient beings of few roots of merit, few skillful means, and much indolence, and to bring them benefit and joy, has taught this wondrous Dharma."

At that time, when the World-Honored One had finished teaching this sūtra, all the great śrāvakas, all the bodhisattva-mahāsattvas, and all the devas, humans, asuras, gandharvas, and the entire world, hearing what the Buddha taught, were all greatly joyful, and they accepted it with faith and practiced it, bowed, and withdrew.
