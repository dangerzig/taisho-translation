# Sūtra on Protecting the Body and Life

## *Hùshēnmìng Jīng* (護身命經)

Taishō 2866

Translator unknown. An apocryphal text.

*Note: This apocryphal sūtra emphasizes devotion to the text itself as a means of protection and wish-fulfillment, and includes a scene where five hundred women led by "Good Faith" receive the five precepts from the Buddha. A colophon records that the layman Zhāng Āyí copied this sūtra on the fifteenth day of the twelfth month of the second year of Zhèngguāng (521 CE). The text survives only in fragmentary form; the opening portion is lost.*

[Opening lost] ...faith in the wondrous dharma of the Buddha, receiving and studying it without forgetting. Sixth: not coveting worldly glory, severing mundane causes and conditions. Seventh: vowing to study holy wisdom as protection for all beings. Eighth: vowing to gather sentient beings and serve as a leader on the Way. Ninth: in whatever place of rebirth, always vowing to encounter the Buddha. Tenth: upholding the prohibitive precepts and broadly delivering all beings. Eleventh: broadly studying all forms of knowledge and proclaiming it to the fourfold assembly. Twelfth: always vowing to meet the Buddha life after life, broadly expounding the essentials of the Way and delivering immeasurable beings. These are the twelve kinds of holy wisdom.

The Buddha addressed Ānanda (*Ānán*, 阿難): "If there are bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, upāsikās, princes, and attendant women who are able to practice these twelve dharmas, in whatever place of rebirth they will always encounter the Buddha. Circulating and teaching, they will forever leave behind all suffering and enter the three dharma-gates, and their merit will be accomplished. They will swiftly reach buddhahood, striding through the three realms without any obstruction, and none can sever this. Through the merit of cultivating these practices in their own previous five lives, they themselves attain buddhahood.

"If there are kings, great ministers, elders, the ladies of the inner palace and their retinue, and all good men and good women of the fourfold assembly who wish to seek this unsurpassed Way and broadly deliver all beings, they should read this sūtra. Those who wish to seek the dharma of the three vehicles should also read this sūtra. Those who wish to swiftly transcend the world and attain the unconditioned should also read this sūtra. Those who wish to be born in the wondrous heavenly joys above should also read this sūtra. Those who wish to become a wheel-turning holy king should also read this sūtra. Those who wish for honored status and wealth should also read this sūtra. If there is a woman who seeks to be reborn in a man's body, she should also read this sūtra. Those who wish life after life always to follow a clear teacher for mutual instruction and transformation should also read this sūtra. For all such wishes and desires, one should read this sūtra.

"This sūtra is supremely powerful. Good men and good women who are able to practice and uphold this sūtra will always be protected by the awesome divine power of all the presently abiding buddhas of the ten directions. They will also be protected by the seven buddhas of the past, and by all the honored bodhisattvas and great disciples. Furthermore, the devas, nāgas, ghosts, spirits, the sun, moon, the five planets, the twenty-eight lunar mansions, and all the honored heavenly beings will constantly follow and protect those who read this sūtra, never allowing them to die an untimely death. All evil and deviant forces will be unable to disturb or harm them. Because these good men and good women practice the bodhisattva path, the holy ones of the ten directions all jointly protect and watch over them. The merit of reading this sūtra is immeasurable. The Buddha's words are never false. Those who hear this sūtra, who receive and uphold it, who copy it: all their wishes will be fulfilled.

"Ānanda, listen carefully, receive and uphold this sūtra. Diligently teach all people to receive, study, chant, and recite it. Do not be forgetful or negligent."

Ānanda replied: "I receive the Heavenly Honored One's teaching."

---

When the Buddha was teaching this sūtra, there was among the assembly an elder's daughter named Good Faith (*Shànxìn*, 善信), who together with five hundred companions all arose at once. They bowed their heads at the Buddha's feet and prostrated with all five limbs on the ground. Kneeling for a long time, she addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One, we women are defiled in our sinful bodies. Yet we have received the Buddha's compassionate grace and been permitted to hear the holy dharma. Though we are lowly women, we wish to become disciples."

The Buddha said: "Welcome, women. You have merit from past lives and should encounter the Buddha. Those who delight in hearing the dharma will truly attain the crossing beyond suffering. In life after life, you will leave behind the female body."

The Buddha conferred the five precepts and made them *upāsikās*. All in the assembly rejoiced. Each came forward, placed their foreheads upon the ground, and all received the five precepts.

The Buddha addressed Ānanda: "Good Faith and her companions have long lived in the world. Before they heard the Buddha, before they heard the dharma, they were led astray by false teachers. Following worldly conditions, they sometimes destroyed the true dharma, sometimes demolished stūpas and monasteries, sometimes killed true practitioners, sometimes misappropriated the wealth of the Three Treasures, sometimes killed their parents, sometimes committed many offenses, all without self-awareness. The sins they committed are beyond reckoning. Now, having seen the Buddha, their minds have opened and their understanding has cleared. They recognize that their actions in past lives were wrong. Good Faith and her companions, from now on, will reform and cultivate, revering the Buddha's honored precepts and also upholding this sūtra, never transgressing life after life. We pray the Buddha will compassionately grant us all the five precepts."

The Buddha addressed the women: "Listen carefully, receive, and uphold them. First: uphold benevolence and do not kill; extend kindness to all living beings. Second: with righteousness protect and do not steal; diminish yourself to aid the multitude. Third: be chaste and do not commit sexual misconduct; conduct yourselves without stain. Fourth: let your words be without falsehood; do not commit offenses of the mouth. Fifth: keep far from liquor and do not drink; do not commit offenses against the community.

"Cultivate and observe the precepts of fasting and always practice great compassion. Invoke blessings upon all beings and reverently practice the Buddha's words. Never regret in the midst. To the extent of your ability, it is better to uphold the good and die than to commit offenses and live. Having come now to hold the Buddha's prohibitive precepts, the devas praise you. Do not follow the customs of the age, bowing to ghosts and spirits in error. Uphold the precepts as if your head were on fire. Sit and contemplate your own body: form does not long endure, appearance does not long remain, life is like a wind encountering a slight illness. Do not indulge in appearance or allow yourselves to dwell in harmful conduct. All things are impermanent. You should establish diligent effort, abandon and diminish worldly thoughts, and act as in the bodhisattva path. Whatever aspirations you arouse: in this life and the next, whatever you seek, as you wish, all shall be fulfilled. This is never false."

The Buddha addressed Ānanda: "You and the others, by protective practice, can reach buddhahood. The buddha-way must be studied. The sūtras and dharma must be read. What I have now attained as a buddha is merely the result of accumulated study. The great-vehicle wondrous dharma: there is no male, no female, no noble, no lowly. Those who hear this sūtra are protected by all buddhas. All their wishes are fulfilled."

When the Buddha was speaking this sūtra, the devas, nāgas, ghosts, spirits, the emperor, the people, and the fourfold assembly, three hundred million in number, saw the Buddha speak this sūtra. All greatly rejoiced and exclaimed that this was unprecedented. Each gave rise to the mind of the unsurpassed, true, and right Way. They came forward, placed their foreheads upon the ground, bowed, and put it into practice.

The Buddha addressed Ānanda: "Listen carefully, receive and uphold, broadly proclaim and circulate it. Cause all sentient beings with form to receive, uphold, read, recite, copy, and make offerings. The essential dharma of the Buddha can eliminate the extremely grave sins of immeasurable kalpas. It can deliver immeasurable, boundless, incalculable sentient beings, all attaining liberation. This sūtra has issued from the Buddha's mouth. If one reads even a single phrase or verse and praises it, all evil and deviant forces and all hostile ghosts will be unable to approach. One's lifespan will be truly long, and one will never die an untimely death. Whatever is sought will be fulfilled as wished."

When this sūtra was spoken, the entire great assembly and all the nāgas, ghosts, spirits, and fourfold assembly bowed at the Buddha's feet, received the Heavenly Honored One's teaching, and wholeheartedly put it into practice.

---

On the fifteenth day of the twelfth month of the second year of Zhèngguāng (*Zhèngguāng*, 正光) [521 CE], the layman Zhāng Āyí (*Zhāng Āyí*, 張阿宜) copied this Sūtra on Protecting the Body and Life, to receive, uphold, read, recite, and make offerings. Above, for the sake of his parents for seven generations; below, for his own body: may all sincerely follow the path of wholesome happiness.
