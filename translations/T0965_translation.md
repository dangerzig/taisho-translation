# 大妙金剛大甘露軍拏利焰鬘熾盛佛頂經

## The Sūtra of the Great Wondrous Vajra, Great Amṛta Kuṇḍalī, Flame-Garland Blazing Buddhōṣṇīṣa

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0965

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Dharmacintā (達磨栖那).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Bhagavān Vairocana Tathāgata was on Kukkuṭapāda Peak, ninety yojanas above the ground, together with buddhas from the ten directions numbering ninety times the sand-grains of the Ganges times koṭis of nayutas, all gathered in this place. Then the Tathāgata, empowered by the accumulated merit and wisdom of myriad practices cultivated through an incalculable number of koṭis of Ganges-sand kalpas, abiding in the great vajra samādhi within the dharmadhātu of space, manifested a great vajra jewel-peak pavilion. It was immeasurably tall, without center or boundary, adorned with great wondrous jewels and wondrous jewel-clouds, interspersed with garlands of turning flowers and interlocking lattice, blazing with immeasurable hundreds of thousands of rays of light coiling and reflecting upon one another. Immeasurable jeweled banners stood arrayed on four sides, and immeasurable hundreds of thousands of variegated wondrous flowers were scattered in adornment.

Then the Tathāgata entered the Wondrous Vajra Great Amṛta Kuṇḍalī Flame-Garland Blazing Great Samādhi, and the entire dharmadhātu of space, without boundary, became a single amṛta flame-garland. Moreover, in all the buddha-fields as numerous as the dust motes of hundreds of thousands of koṭis of worlds, all became universally pervading flame-garlands. The buddhas of the ten directions each entered the Immeasurable Hundred-Thousand Revolving Great Wondrous Vajra Great-Bliss Amṛta Kuṇḍalī Flame-Garland Blazing Great Samādhi and abided in tranquility.

At that time, the Bhagavān Vairocana Tathāgata further entered the Amṛta Hundred-Thousand Light-Assembly Supernatural-Display Wondrous Crown Wheel-Turning Great Vajra Amṛta Kuṇḍalī Blazing Great Samādhi. Throughout the three-thousandfold great-thousandfold dharmadhātu of space, there appeared a great eight-spoked vajra flame-wheel. At that time, the World-Honored One, without moving from this place, abided at the wheel's hub. Moreover, within each spoke there appeared a great eight-colored vajra flame-wheel, illuminating the ten directions and revolving rightward, extending to the utmost upper realm, middle realm, lower realm, and four boundless horizons, producing amṛta flame-garlands, each revolving like lasso-wheel garlands turning freely.

Then Mahāvairocana Tathāgata, having entered this samādhi, slightly opened both eyes from within concentration.

At that time, in the buddha-fields as numerous as the dust motes of the Ganges-sand worlds of the ten directions, all the buddhas, stationed in space, each with supernatural displays of wondrous lion's roars, simultaneously praised Mahāvairocana Tathāgata with one voice, speaking four verses of wondrous gāthā:

*Namo buddha subuddho vairocana subuddhaṃ daśa-niśi-subuddhi mahāyoga-īśvara-laṃbhi-siddhi*

Having spoken these verses, the buddhas of the ten directions said: "World-Honored One! What Dharma do you wish to teach today that you display such signs? In all the buddha-fields of past buddhas, we have never seen this samādhi."

At that time, Mahāvairocana Tathāgata, with his vajra body, speech, and mind, extended his wondrous golden arms, brought both palms together, curled the two index fingers into the palms, and pressed them with the two thumbs. With this great mudrā placed before his heart, from the mudrā there arose immeasurable hundreds of thousands of koṭis of variegated great jewel-lights, variegated jewel-clouds, and variously crafted flowers, revolving in space like a great golden wheel.

Further, from the mudrā there appeared ten Ganges-sand koṭis of buddha-fields, each radiating great light illuminating the ten directions, raining jeweled wheels, wondrous jeweled flowers, great parasol-canopies, hooks, vajras, wheels, swords, jeweled conch shells.

Further, supremely wondrous sounds emerged, reaching hundreds of thousands of koṭis of buddha-fields in the distance.

Furthermore, from each jeweled wheel, wondrous flower, great parasol-canopy, hook, vajra, wheel, sword, and conch shell, there each appeared hundreds of thousands of koṭis of nayutas of buddha-field dust motes, each producing immeasurable wondrous sounds, their voices shaking the worlds of the ten directions in their hundreds of billions.

At that time, the World-Honored One Mahāvairocana Tathāgata, with his fierce supernatural power, using his flame-garland radiant hand, made nine rightward revolutions above his crown in space, like the gesture of a lasso ring, gathering all the buddhas in space into the light. At that time, the nine revolutions of light were unbroken, and there appeared nine Ganges-sand koṭis of buddha-fields, each seated upon white lotus flowers, surrounded by their own assemblies, radiating immeasurable jeweled light.

At that time, the World-Honored One manifested in his body the form of the All-Gathering Buddhōṣṇīṣa Wheel-King, holding an eight-spoked golden wheel and seated on a seven-lion throne, his body radiating immeasurable hundreds of thousands of rays of light. From the fire-flames, atop the spinning parasol-canopy, there appeared one koṭi of buddha-bodies radiating great light, all revolving and seated within the parasol-canopy.

Thus the World-Honored One, with the mudrā, further produced an eight-spoked golden wheel. In the eight directions, eight colored wheels appeared, and within each wheel appeared a flower-throne bearing its respective emblematic implement, each surrounded by eight Ganges-sand koṭis of buddha-bodies, each radiating immeasurable variegated jewel-light flames. Atop the parasol-canopy once again appeared one koṭi of buddhas radiating great light, each revolving.

In the eastern direction, before the Tathāgata, within a red wheel, there appeared the Light-Assembly Buddhōṣṇīṣa Wheel-King, holding the Tathāgata Crown-Seal, radiating great clusters of light, seated on a red lotus.

In the front-right corner of the Tathāgata, within a yellow wheel, appeared the All-Generating Buddhōṣṇīṣa Wheel-King, holding a yellow lotus, radiating yellow light, seated on a yellow lotus.

To the right of the Tathāgata, within a white wheel, appeared the White Parasol Buddhōṣṇīṣa Wheel-King, holding a white parasol, radiating white light, seated on a great white lotus.

In the rear-right corner, within a variegated wheel, appeared the Victorious Crown Wheel-King, holding a sharp sword, radiating variegated light, seated on a variegated lotus.

Behind the Tathāgata, within a crimson wheel, appeared the All-Obstruction-Removing Buddhōṣṇīṣa Wheel-King, holding a crimson lotus with a hook upon it, radiating crimson light, seated on a crimson lotus.

In the rear-left corner, within a blue wheel, appeared the Golden Buddhōṣṇīṣa Wheel-King, holding a three-pronged vajra, radiating blue light, seated on a blue lotus.

To the left of the Tathāgata, within a green wheel, appeared the One-Syllable Supreme Crown Wheel-King, holding an eight-spoked vajra wheel, radiating green light-flames, seated on a green lotus.

In the front-left corner of the Tathāgata, within a purple wheel, appeared the Boundless-Sound Buddhōṣṇīṣa Wheel-King, holding a white conch, radiating purple light-flames, seated on a purple lotus.

Each of the eight directional buddhōṣṇīṣa kings radiated light of their respective directional colors, each surrounded by eight Ganges-sand koṭis of buddhas, each seated on a lotus of their directional color, each with one koṭi of buddha-fields coiling atop their crowns forming canopy-shapes.

At that time, when all the buddhōṣṇīṣa wheel-kings had been assigned their eight directional seats and each had manifested the form of eight types of buddhōṣṇīṣa wheel-kings, the World-Honored One further manifested the seven-jeweled retinue: the jewel-woman, the jewel-horse, the jewel-treasury-guardian spirit, the jewel-wheel, the jewel-elephant, the wish-fulfilling jewel, and the jewel-army, surrounding the great wheel on all four sides.

At that time, the World-Honored One further entered the Hook-Summoning Samādhi, summoning throughout the entire dharmadhātu of space, in order to establish all sentient beings and swiftly lead them to the path of unsurpassed enlightenment, to enable those dwelling in birth and death to directly realize the buddha-body. Then the koṭis of buddhas of the ten directions, hearing the hook-summoning of the All-Gathering Buddhōṣṇīṣa Wheel-King, all from within samādhi manifested supernatural powers, raining jeweled rains, jeweled flowers, jeweled garlands, jeweled incense, jeweled lamps, jeweled silk, and other offerings, all arriving at the Great Vajra Jewel-Peak Pavilion in the dharmadhātu of space. Gathered in space, they all said: "World-Honored One Wheel-King, why do you not speak the ten-syllable Dharma, to enable all sentient beings in this present life to swiftly attain the buddha-body?"

At that time, the All-Gathering Buddhōṣṇīṣa King, having heard these words, wishing to display great supernatural power and might, wishing to teach the root practice realized by all the buddhas, the practice of the buddhas of the past, the practice of the buddhas of the present, and the practice of the buddhas of the future, in order to enable good men and good women in this very life, without transforming their physical bodies, to accomplish great buddha-deeds, and for the benefit of all sentient beings to attain the buddha-body, he spoke the Vajra Blazing-Light Buddhōṣṇīṣa Wheel-King Sovereign Ten-Syllable True Words:

*Namo Vairocana-ya. Namo Bhagavat-Uṣṇīṣa-ya. A buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha. Tru hūṃ. Buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha. Ṭru hūṃ. Buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha. Dhru hūṃ. Buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha. Nru hūṃ. Buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha. Hru hūṃ. Buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha. Śru hūṃ. Buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha. Sru hūṃ. Buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha. Bru hūṃ. Buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha. Hūṃ. Buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha buddha.*

After speaking this true words, throughout the three-thousandfold great-thousandfold worlds and in all the buddha-fields as numerous as their dust motes, there were six kinds of earthquakes, and there rained jeweled flowers, jeweled clouds, celestial wish-fulfilling jewels, white whisks, silk canopies, fragrant flowers, lamps and lights, musical sounds and the playing of hundreds of thousands of kinds of music as offerings to the Tathāgata. Furthermore, all the koṭis of buddhas of the ten directions, having heard this teaching, all bowed their heads to the ground at his feet and praised the All-Gathering Buddhōṣṇīṣa Wheel-King Tathāgata, speaking these secret words:

> "Blazingly radiant, without equal,
> Supremely wondrous, auspicious, sovereign in wisdom,
> Able to tame and turn the wheel in birth and death,
> Swiftly enabling sentient beings to attain perfect enlightenment."

At that time, the World-Honored One Wheel-King further spoke these secret words in reply to the koṭis of tathāgatas of the ten directions:

> "I now speak these words of truth
> To benefit sentient beings and bring them to perfect enlightenment.
> May the buddhas of the ten directions swiftly grant empowerment
> And cause those beings to fully realize me."

After speaking these verses, at that time the great bodhisattvas of the worlds of the ten directions, namely Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva, Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva, Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva, Maitreya Bodhisattva, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, Kṣitigarbha Bodhisattva, Sarvanīvaraṇaviṣkambhin Bodhisattva, and Samantabhadra Bodhisattva, all simultaneously gathered at the Jewel-Peak Pavilion in the dharmadhātu of space before the World-Honored One Wheel-King, bowed their heads to his feet, and all together spoke this verse of praise:

*Namaḥ sarva-jñāna-ya manuśini sarva-jagad-anuśini-ya śaśi-ninī prabhu-va-anu-ka-vandha sama-satani-niyadi-pacakrama-riṇī namo siddhu-te tatra-tāri-cakra-vārti-nī namo siddhu-te*

Having spoken these words, at that time each of the eight great bodhisattvas manifested wheels of light, each transforming into the eight great vajra vidyā-kings, in accordance with the Tathāgata's wheel.

At that time, Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva manifested as Trailokyavijaya Vajra Vidyā-King, radiating blue light, displaying two fangs at the mouth, laughing the *aṭaṭa* laugh, and hurling a five-pronged vajra with the right hand. He spoke the eight-syllable heart mantra:

*Oṃ niṣumbha vajra hūṃ phaṭ*

At that time, Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva manifested as the Six-Armed, Six-Headed, Six-Footed Vajra Vidyā-King, radiating blue-black light, biting the lower lip, with both eyes and eyebrows raised, holding a sharp sword in hand. He spoke the three-syllable heart mantra:

*Hūṃ a hūṃ*

At that time, Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva manifested as the Great-Laughing Vajra Vidyā-King, radiating ash-black light, the mouth displaying a great laughing expression with two fangs protruding upward. The left hand leaned on a blue staff and the right hand held a lasso. He spoke the ten-syllable heart mantra:

*Oṃ vajra ṭahāsya hūṃ phaṭ*

At that time, Maitreya Bodhisattva manifested as the Great-Wheel Vajra Vidyā-King, with a body entirely yellow, radiating great fire. The right hand held an eight-spoked vajra wheel and the left hand leaned on a single-pronged vajra. He spoke the six-syllable heart mantra:

*Oṃ vajra cakra hūṃ*

At that time, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva manifested atop his crown as Hayagrīva Vajra Vidyā-King, with a blue-green body radiating red light. The right hand raised high above the crown horizontally held a lotus in a striking posture, and the left hand held a kuṇḍikā vessel-mudrā. He spoke the ten-syllable heart mantra:

*Oṃ haya grīva hūṃ phaṭ*

At that time, Kṣitigarbha Bodhisattva manifested as Aparājita Vajra Vidyā-King, with a body entirely yellow, radiating flames of fire-light. The right hand hurled a vajra and the left hand made a gesture toward the mouth. He spoke the thirteen-syllable heart mantra:

*Oṃ huru huru caṇḍāli mātaṅgi svāhā*

At that time, Sarvanīvaraṇaviṣkambhin Bodhisattva manifested as Acalanātha Vajra Vidyā-King, with a body entirely blue, radiating flames of fire-light. The right hand held a sword and the left hand held a lasso. A single topknot hung to the left. He spoke the fourteen-syllable heart mantra:

*Oṃ acala-kaṇa-caṇḍa sādhiya hūṃ yaṃ ṭa*

At that time, Samantabhadra Bodhisattva manifested as Padanakṣipa Vajra Vidyā-King. The right hand held a revolving canopy and the left hand held a vajra, with a body the color of space, radiating flames of fire-light. He spoke the seven-syllable heart mantra:

*Oṃ hrīḥ hūṃ kuru hūṃ bru hūṃ sru hūṃ ja ru hūṃ nyak*

At that time, the eight vajra vidyā-king buddhas appeared before the Tathāgata to receive his command, saying: "We shall carry out your instructions."

At that time, the World-Honored One Wheel-King addressed the vajra vidyā-king buddhas: "If there are good men or good women who hold this ten-syllable heart mantra, you must constantly, day and night, never leave those people, and never allow them to fail in accomplishment. All transgressions and karmic obstacles of the three times shall be swiftly eliminated. Each of you shall use your supernatural powers and weapons to shatter the impurities of their three actions and render them pure. In the Dharma of contemplative samādhi, cause them in this present life to become no different from me. I shall now further speak for you a secret one-syllable heart mantra. Listen well:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ a*

Having spoken this heart mantra, Vajrapāṇi and all the great vidyā-kings bowed in reverence and said: "We have heard this Dharma today, and we shall always bear the buddha-wheel upon our bodies. May the Buddha, in compassion, further teach us the method of cultivating samādhi, to swiftly enable all sentient beings to attain the buddha-body."

At that time, the koṭis of buddhas of the ten directions in their Ganges-sand numbers all said: "Please further teach us the one-syllable heart secret mudrā and the siddhi methods. How can sentient beings swiftly attain the unsurpassed Dharma in this present life? How does one attain upper, middle, and lower accomplishment of the secret Dharma? How does one attain supernatural powers? How does one cultivate the samādhi method? May the World-Honored One, in compassion, broadly benefit all sentient beings."

At that time, the World-Honored One Wheel-King addressed all the buddhas and Vajrapāṇi Buddha and the others: "Listen well. I shall further broadly teach you the method of cultivating samādhi, to swiftly enable realization of the unsurpassed fruit of the Way in this present life, to dwell in birth and death without entering nirvāṇa, and to attain constant sovereign freedom of the body.

"Good sons! I shall now first teach you the root secret mudrā method. Bring both hands together in a hollow-palm gesture, curl the two index fingers into the palms, and press them with the two thumbs. This is called the heart-mudrā of the one-syllable method. When forming this mudrā, the buddhas of the ten directions in their koṭi numbers swiftly come to that place and empower that person's body, speech, and mind, and swiftly grant them purity. As one abides in the midst without perceiving body or mind, one realizes the station of the great being. Together with all buddhas, one shares the same station. One becomes the teacher of future buddhas, the teacher of present buddhas, and the transmitting teacher of past buddhas.

"If a good man cultivates this method, he should first visualize within the heart the syllable *a*, white in color, becoming a great moon disk. Its extent pervades the dharmadhātu, without upper, middle, or lower boundary, becoming a great eight-spoked golden wheel. Moreover, within each spoke, revolving rightward, arrange eight-colored vajra wheels. Following the previous contemplation, visualize the body as the great self. At the wheel's hub, take the form of the crown wheel-king. Then form the Accomplishing-All-Activities Buddhōṣṇīṣa All-Buddhas Heart-Root Samaya Mudrā: open both hands and spread the ten fingers, joining the two palm-heels. Then upon the fingers of this mudrā, scatter and arrange the ten-syllable true words. Upon each and every syllable, scatter immeasurable hundreds of thousands of variegated jewel-lights, radiating and shooting throughout the buddha-fields of the ten directions with blazing brilliance unequaled in the three times. Then, with the mudrā-hands, revolve rightward in the gesture of a lasso ring: one revolution, one buddha, one wheel; nine buddhas, nine wheels; eighty-one hundred-million koṭi-numbered buddhas. The colors and forms, the flower-thrones, all are likewise. When performing this original initial wheel-mudrā, do not allow the mudrā's light to be broken or the flame to cease.

"This mudrā is called the All-Gathering Buddhōṣṇīṣa Wheel-King Root Samaya Mudrā that Generates the Boundless Karmic Actions of All Buddhas. Without needing the methods of boundary-sealing or offering, it swiftly accomplishes the great samādhi ground of all buddhas and produces the buddha-body. Even tenth-stage bodhisattvas cannot perceive it with their eyes or hear it with their ears, and even buddhas of the same class cannot see their own bodies. How much less other bodhisattvas! If one performs this method through one hundred and eight repetitions, one will realize the buddha-body in this present existence. For those of superior capacity, accomplishment is attained. If one goes through one thousand repetitions, those of middling capacity will certainly attain accomplishment. If one goes through eighteen thousand repetitions, those of lower capacity will certainly attain the great accomplishment."

At that time, the World-Honored One Wheel-King further addressed the buddhas of the ten directions and Vajrapāṇi Buddha and the others: "I shall now further teach the wooden seal method.

"Upon sandalwood, carve an eight-spoked vajra wheel. In the wheel's hub, write the syllable *bru hūṃ*. On the four sides of the wheel, carve radiating fire-flame marks. On the back, carve an eight-petaled lotus. Upon the flower, place a crescent-moon form. Having made this seal, recite the *a* one-syllable heart mantra twenty-one times. In a pure place, anoint it with cinnabar and musk. Using the seal, stamp the heart, forehead, throat, crown, both shoulders, below the navel, and both knees. Recite the one-syllable mantra, the syllable *a*, once per stamp. In this way, after ten thousand repetitions, one attains supernatural powers and can go to the Ganges-sand worlds of the ten directions, to the lands of wondrous bliss, the Flower Treasury Buddha-Field, and the nāga palaces, and to the heavens of non-perception. Everywhere within the three times, one moves freely without any obstruction, and all bodhisattvas, devas, nāgas, and others cannot perceive or see one. This is called the supernatural-power siddhi.

"Furthermore, regarding the method of cultivating samādhi: constantly visualize within one's own heart the syllable *a*, which becomes the buddha-form, extending throughout all of space without boundary, brilliantly pure. One realizes the buddha-body, without birth or death. Without transforming the physical body, one attains the fruit of no outflows. One constantly teaches in the worlds of the ten directions, instructing the two-vehicle buddhas and all sentient beings, freely and at will teaching all yogas according to the capacities of beings. This is called the siddhi of cultivating the samādhi method.

"Furthermore, Vajrapāṇi Buddha! When that good man is about to achieve accomplishment, the buddhas of the ten directions will all hurl their eight-spoked great wheels upon his crown. He should not be afraid, for this is to destroy the extremely heavy karmic obstacles of the three actions and the obstructions of dharma-attachment, the known, the seen, and discrimination. This wheel emerges from his heart and passes beneath his feet, carrying his buddha-body. He then attains my state. One should always single-mindedly perform this accomplishment without letting it dissipate."

At that time, the Ganges-sand buddhas, having heard the Dharma, each praised the Great Sun World-Honored One. They scattered various flowers and incense upon the Buddha, praised him with wondrous gāthās, and each returned to their own buddha-fields. Then Vajrapāṇi Buddha and the others, having heard the teaching, further asked: "Noble one! To what kind of people should this method be given for accomplishment?" At that time, the World-Honored One Mahāvairocana Tathāgata said: "This method should be given to people of straightforward mind, people without discriminating mind, and people without obstructions of knowledge and views. They will certainly attain accomplishment identical to my body."

Then Vajrapāṇi and the eight great bodhisattvas performed this method before the Buddha, and in a single moment became equal to the Buddha. The eight great bodhisattvas, now buddhas, returned by singing praises to the Buddha with buddha-flowers, wishing to return to their own lands.

At that time, the World-Honored One said: "Although you have attained the buddha-body, you have not yet repaid the Buddha's kindness." The eight great bodhisattvas addressed the Buddha: "What constitutes repaying the Buddha's kindness?" The Buddha said: "Having all attained buddhahood, you must broadly liberate sentient beings. You must not abide in tranquility and enjoy quiescence for yourselves alone."

At that time, the buddha-bodhisattvas, having heard this teaching, immediately reversed their transformation and returned to being bodhisattvas, bowing at the Buddha's feet. The Buddha addressed Vajrapāṇi: "You should use this Dharma to broadly liberate sentient beings and swiftly enable them to attain buddhahood." Vajrapāṇi and the others said: "If I do not use this Dharma to liberate sentient beings and cause them all to attain buddhahood, I vow not to attain perfect enlightenment."

At that time, the World-Honored One Wheel-King further addressed Vajrapāṇi: "Good son! I shall now further teach you the heart within the heart, the secret within the secret: the one-syllable gate of the syllable *a*. If a good man constantly abides in this one-syllable gate, within birth and death there is neither arising nor ceasing, and without transforming the physical body, one realizes this Dharma. This one-syllable gate is that in which the buddhas of the past three times, the present three times, and the future three times all constantly abide. If accomplishment is sought apart from this Dharma, that is impossible."

At that time, when Vajrapāṇi heard the Buddha's teaching, the Buddhōṣṇīṣa Golden Wheel gradually expanded, vast without center or boundary. Form and marks both vanished. It became the tranquil dharmadhātu. Vajrapāṇi's eyes could not reach it, and he spoke this verse of praise:

> "The nature of all buddhas is boundless.
> The wheel-form embodies the dharmadhātu.
> We who have realized this Dharma
> Shall broadly liberate all sentient beings.
> How has the World-Honored One entered
> And abides in the great tranquil samādhi?
> May he appear before us
> And not violate his original vow."

At that time, the World-Honored One Wheel-King, having heard the request of Vajrapāṇi and the others, did not manifest a visible body. From the position within the dharmadhātu of space, a voice addressed Vajrapāṇi:

> "The nature of all buddhas is boundless.
> Form and marks are both sovereign.
> Sound and form are both true reality.
> Those who are deluded make distinctions.
> Manifesting form yet without form,
> Originally empty and tranquil.
> You should forever uphold this Dharma
> And broadly liberate all sentient beings."

At that time, Vajrapāṇi and the eight great bodhisattvas, having heard the Buddha's teaching, joyfully bowed in reverence, accepted it with faith, and put it into practice.
