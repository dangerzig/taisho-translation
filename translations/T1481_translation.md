# 佛說五恐怖世經
## Scripture on the Five Fearful Ages of the World, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1481

Translated from the Chinese. Liu Song dynasty, translated by Juqu Jingsheng (沮渠京聲)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was dwelling in the land of Śrāvastī, in the Jeta Grove, at Anāthapiṇḍika's Park. The Buddha addressed the bhikṣus: "There are five future fears that have not yet arrived and not yet come to pass, yet they are not far off and will come before long. They are to be dreaded. One should reflect well upon them and through skillful means keep far from them. What are the five?

"First: In days to come there will be bhikṣus who are without proper conduct, without precepts, with undisciplined minds, and without wisdom arising from practice. Themselves inwardly without proper conduct, without precepts, with undisciplined minds, and without wisdom, such persons will nonetheless be received into training and given the full precepts, and students will follow and take them as teachers—students who themselves can maintain precepts, discipline their own minds, and illuminate wisdom. Those who originally learned from teachers of higher quality: in the beginning the later students learn the Dharma as those before them did, but subsequently they undermine the Vinaya, slander the sutras of the Dharma, and depart far from true wisdom. Bhikṣus! This is the first future fear to be dreaded, which will come before long. One should reflect well upon it and through skillful means avoid it.

"Second: In days to come there will be bhikṣus who are without proper conduct, without precepts, with undisciplined minds, and without wisdom. Being praised by others as possessing hidden treasures, they proceed out of proper sequence, inwardly without precepts, violating the instructions of venerable masters, and causing others to study under them—claiming that through this one can reach the other shore, yet in truth leading them ever further away. This is the second future thing to be dreaded, which will come before long. One should reflect well upon it and through skillful means avoid it.

"Third: In days to come there will be bhikṣus who are without proper conduct, without precepts, with undisciplined minds, and without wisdom. Originally having no qualified teacher, they receive incorrect Dharma. When other bhikṣus receive Dharma from them and practice it, the meaning of the Dharma and Vinaya is lost. This is the third future thing to be dreaded, which will come before long. One should reflect well upon it and through skillful means avoid it.

"Fourth: In days to come there will be bhikṣus who are without proper conduct, without precepts, with undisciplined minds, and without wisdom. Following those who are likewise without proper conduct, without precepts, with undisciplined minds, and without wisdom, bhikṣus will gather together to expound the Vinaya and explain its meaning. They will present later material as though it belongs in the middle, and middle material as though it belongs at the end. In this way, clarity is lost and darkness descends; they cannot discern these matters for themselves, and they depart far from the true Dharma. This is the fourth future thing to be dreaded, which will come before long. One should reflect well upon it and through skillful means avoid it.

"Fifth: In days to come there will be bhikṣus who are without proper conduct, without precepts, with undisciplined minds, and without wisdom. Following those who are likewise without proper conduct, without precepts, with undisciplined minds, and without wisdom, they will receive with delight all the clever rhetoric and licentious speech that the world produces, and when they see such things recited their minds will be pleased and joyful. But when the venerable one speaks of the twelve nidānas, of duḥkha, śūnyatā, and anitya, their minds are displeased; they reject everything and receive nothing. Hearing such teachings, doubt arises in their minds and they do not believe it to be true, nor do they take any joy or delight in it. This is the fifth future thing to be dreaded, which will come before long. One should reflect well upon it and through skillful means avoid it."

This is what the Buddha taught.
