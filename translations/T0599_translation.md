# 佛為海龍王說法印經
## Sūtra of the Dharma Seal Spoken by the Buddha for the Sea Dragon King

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 599

Translated from the Chinese. Tang Dynasty, translated by Yijing (義淨)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Bhagavān was dwelling in the palace of the Sea Dragon King, together with a great assembly of bhikṣus, twelve hundred and fifty in number, and also together with many bodhisattvas-mahāsattvas.

At that time, Sāgara, the Dragon King, rose from his seat, advanced, and paid homage at the feet of the Buddha, saying: "World-Honored One! Is it possible to uphold a small amount of Dharma and thereby obtain great merit?"

The Buddha addressed the Sea Dragon King: "There are four supreme Dharmas. If one upholds, recites, and understands their meaning, though the effort expended is small, the merit obtained is very great — equal in every way to the merit of reciting the eighty-four thousand Dharma collections. What are the four? They are the recitation of:

> All conditioned phenomena are impermanent;
> all things are suffering;
> all dharmas are without self;
> nirvāṇa is bliss.

Dragon King, know this! These are called the four supreme Dharmas. Through them, bodhisattvas-mahāsattvas, with their inexhaustible Dharma-wisdom, swiftly realize non-arising and quickly reach complete nirvāṇa. Therefore, you all should constantly recite them."

At that time, when the World-Honored One had finished speaking this Sūtra of the Four-Phrase Dharma Seal, the śrāvakas and the great assembly of bodhisattvas, together with the eight classes of heavenly beings and dragons, asuras, gandharvas, and others — having heard what the Buddha had spoken, all rejoiced greatly, received it with faith, and undertook to practice it.
