# 十二品生死經
## Sūtra on the Twelve Classes of Death

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 753

Translated from the Chinese. Liu Song dynasty, translated by Guṇabhadra

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was staying at Jetavana, Anāthapiṇḍada's park, in Śrāvastī. At that time, the Buddha addressed the bhikṣus: "I shall expound a sūtra for you." The bhikṣus responded: "Yes, World-Honored One! We wish to receive your instruction."

The Buddha said: "When persons die, there are twelve classes of death. What are these twelve? The first is called 'death without remainder'; this refers to the arhat, who has no attachments. The second is called 'death having transcended'; this refers to the anāgāmin, who does not return again. The third is called 'death with remainder'; this refers to the sakṛdāgāmin, who goes and returns. The fourth is called 'death of one learning to transcend'; this refers to the śrotāpanna, who perceives the tracks of the path. The fifth is called 'death without deception'; this refers to a person of the eighth rank. The sixth is called 'death with joy'; this refers to those who practice one-pointedness of mind. The seventh is called 'repeated death'; this refers to those of evil conduct. The eighth is called 'death with regret'; this refers to ordinary persons. The ninth is called 'untimely death'; this refers to the suffering of those who are alone and helpless. The tenth is called 'death bound in attachment'; this refers to animals. The eleventh is called 'death by burning and putrefaction'; this refers to the hells. The twelfth is called 'death by hunger and thirst'; this refers to the hungry ghosts.

"Bhikṣus! You should clearly understand this and practice accordingly. Do not be negligent; do not give rise to lustful desire; remain far from all misfortune; and with a pure mind, constantly bring to fulfillment what has not yet been attained. Why is this? Repeated death is exceedingly painful; death with regret is also painful; untimely death is extremely severe; death bound in attachment is also severe; death by burning and putrefaction is extremely agonizing; death by hunger and thirst is also agonizing. Thus, bhikṣus, you should practice accordingly: dwell in seclusion, or beneath a tree; cultivate dhyāna and one-pointedness of mind; do not engage in frivolous activities; do not come to regret later. This is the teaching of the Buddha; this is the rule of the Buddha-Dharma."

When the Buddha had spoken thus, the bhikṣus rejoiced, bowed their heads to the ground, and withdrew.
