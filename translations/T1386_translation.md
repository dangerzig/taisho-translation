# 佛說妙色陀羅尼經
## Sūtra on the Dhāraṇī of Wondrous Form, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1386

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmabhadra (法賢)

---

At that time, the Buddha addressed Ānanda, saying: "There is a dhāraṇī called 'Wondrous Form,' which has been proclaimed together by all Buddhas of the three times and is capable of bringing great benefit to sentient beings. If a person hears this dhāraṇī, gives rise to the thought that it is difficult to encounter, arouses a courageous mind, recites and upholds it, makes offerings, and shows reverence, that person will throughout this life accumulate a great store of merit and be at peace both day and night. Furthermore, if a person, with a mind of great compassion, in a quiet place prepares various foods and drinks as an offering, and recites this dhāraṇī seven times as an empowerment, then speaks these words: 'I now present this offering to all the beings of the evil destinies and hungry ghosts throughout the world; may those who partake of this offering swiftly depart from the evil destinies'—at the moment of speaking these words, one snaps one's fingers three times. Visualize that all those hungry ghosts who receive this food are each fully satisfied, transform into bodies of wondrous form, give rise to the mind of awakening, and will gradually, in the time to come, attain the fruit of Buddhahood."

He then recited the dhāraṇī:

*namaḥ bhagavate surūpāya tathāgatāya arhate samyaksaṃbuddhāya | tad yathā oṃ surū surū prasurū prasurū smarā smarā vara vara saṃvara saṃvara sarva-preta-piśācānāṃ āhāraṃ dadāmi svāhā*

At that time, upon hearing the Buddha's words, Ānanda was filled with joy, received them with faith, made obeisance, and withdrew.
