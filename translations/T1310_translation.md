# 北斗七星護摩法
## Homa Ritual of the Seven Stars of the Northern Dipper

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1310

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, composed by Yixing (一行).

---

The mantra of Auspicious Accomplishment:

*Namaḥ ratna-trayāya. Namaḥ sumā sarva-nakṣatra-rājāya caturdiśa-ālokārāya. Tadyathā: numati panumati sabhini khaśi svāhā.*

The Mudrā of the Seven Stars of the Northern Dipper: Interlock the two ring fingers and two thumbs, pressing them together. The two middle fingers are joined and point forward. The two little fingers and two index fingers stand upright and spread apart. This completes the mudrā.

The mantra:

*Oṃ saptāna-jñānāya paṃca-mijaya yaṃ-cu-tha-ma-svāmi-nara-kṣaṃ-bhavatu svāhā.*

The Mudrā of the Sun Deva: First join the palms. The four fingers from the index downward press their tips together, with the front greatly opened. The two thumbs are each placed beside the index fingers. The mantra:

*Namaḥ ratna-trayāya. Namaḥ sūrya-sarva-nakṣatra-rājāya. Oṃ amogha-śāya-śānti svāhā.*

The Mudrā of the Moon Deva: With the concentration hand, press the tips of the ring finger and thumb together. The remaining fingers stand upright, as if holding a lotus. The mantra:

*Oṃ candra-nakṣatra-rājāya-śānti svāhā.*

The Mudrā of the Fire Star of the South [Mars]: The left hand forms a fist and is placed at the waist. The five fingers of the right hand stand upright and together. The thumb is bent into the palm. The index finger is bent at the middle joint in a beckoning gesture at the svāhā. The mantra:

*Oṃ aṅgāraka-ālugya svāhā.*

The Mudrā of the Water Deva of the North [Mercury]: The right hand forms a fist and is placed at the waist. The left hand forms a bent fist. The thumb does not enter the palm. The index finger stands upright, slightly bent at the middle joint. The mantra:

*Oṃ budha-nakṣatra-svāna-kenu-ma svāhā.*

The Mudrā of the Wood Star of the East [Jupiter]: The two hands form the vajra-joined-palms mudrā. The two thumbs stand upright. The mantra:

*Oṃ bṛhaspati-nāma-bhitavānaya-māla-vara-dhani svāhā.*

The Mudrā of the Metal Star of the West [Venus]: The two hands are joined in añjali. The two thumbs stand upright. The mantra:

*Oṃ śukra-gandha-vara-vara-rājāya-śrīkari svāhā.*

The Mudrā of the Earth Star of the Central Palace [Saturn]: First join the palms. The four fingers from the index downward press their tips together, with the front greatly opened. The two thumbs are each placed beside the index fingers. The mantra:

*Oṃ śanaiścara-nakṣatra-brahmā-nā-rūbhaya-puṣṭikari svāhā.*

The Mudrā of Rāhu: The two hands form the vajra-joined-palms mudrā. The two thumbs stand upright. The mantra:

*Oṃ rāhu-nā-asura-rājāya-sma-śatunāya-śāntikari svāhā.*

The Mudrā of Ketu: The two hands are joined in añjali. The two thumbs stand upright. The mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-ketu-nā-nakṣatra-rājāya hūṃ svāhā.*

"Ketu" translates as "banner," meaning a comet. Rāhu is the eclipse deity at the intersection of the lunar nodes. These two hidden stars, together with Jupiter, Venus, and Saturn, making five in total, all use the same mudrā of two hands joined in añjali with two thumbs standing upright.

The General Mudrā of the Nine Grahas: The true-reality joined-palms, with the two thumbs together standing upright. The two index fingers are extended, each standing at its original position, moving back and forth. The general mantra:

*Oṃ graha-aśrīrya-prapta-ti-māya svāhā.*

The General Mudrā of the Twenty-Eight Lunar Mansions: Firmly joined palms, with the two thumbs together standing upright at the heart. Beckon with the two thumbs. The general mantra:

*Oṃ nakṣatra-ni-sudāniye svāhā.*

The Mudrā of All the Deities: The right hand forms a fist. The tip of the middle finger extends slightly and bends in a beckoning gesture. The mantra:

*Oṃ sarva-nārāyaṇāya-iheye svāhā.*

The Life-Extending Mantra: First recite one thousand times.

*Oṃ vajrāyuṣe svāhā.*

The Scented-Paste Mudrā: The wisdom-hand faces outward. The concentration-hand, turned upward, grasps the wrist of the wisdom-hand at the heart. The mantra:

*Oṃ viśuddha-gandha-udbhava svāhā.*

The Flower-Offering Mudrā: The two hands are interlaced inward, then opened and turned upward. The two index fingers touch at their side tips. The thumbs are placed beside the index fingers, at the heart. The mantra:

*Oṃ mahā-maitrīya-bhyudgate svāhā.*

The Incense-Burning Mudrā: The six fingers from the middle down on both hands stand with their backs touching. The two index fingers extend at their side tips, not quite touching. The two thumbs are placed beside the index fingers, at the heart. The mantra:

*Oṃ dharma-dhātv-anugate svāhā.*

The Food-Offering Mudrā: The two hands are cupped together with the palms open and turned upward, as if scooping water, at the heart. The mantra:

*Oṃ arara karara malin-danda-mi malin-dade mahā-bali svāhā.*

The Lamp Mudrā: The wisdom-hand forms a fist. The tip of the thumb presses the face of the ring finger, pointing upward. Offer to the seven stars of the Northern Dipper, one's natal star, the grahas and mansions, and the original celestial cycle. The mantra:

*Oṃ tathāgata-arci-spharaṇa-vabhāsana-gagana-udaya svāhā.*

The Universal Offering Mudrā: Vajra-joined-palms. The two index fingers form a jewel shape. The two thumbs stand upright alongside. The mantra:

*Oṃ amogha-pūja-maṇi-padma-vajre tathāgata-vilokite samanta-prasara hūṃ.*

The Praise Mudrā: Vajra-joined-palms.

*Ayandu-ne-vā-ca-ga sura-kiṃnara-nara-kṣa-karanāya-pravara dharma-gṛta-dhigara vidharma-ca praśama-caukiyā-ni-meṃ-ta-bhūta-meṃ-ta-prakāśa-ya tani-ha śramaṇāya dhā hūṃ.*

Visualize seven "ru" syllables within the fire-hearth, transforming into the seven stars of the Northern Dipper. Place one's natal star at the center, with the six stars as companions. Then kneel and join the palms, and facing the hearth, make this invocation:

> "With utmost sincerity I respectfully invoke the seven stars of the north pole:
> Greedy Wolf, Great Gate, Salary Preservation, Literary Composition,
> Purity and Uprightness, Martial Composition, and Army Destroyer, honored stars.
> For the liberation of calamities and adversity, for the extension of lifespan,
> May I live to see a hundred autumns. I now perform this homa.
> May the honored stars descend to this place and accept this homa offering.
> Strike out the records of execution and death,
> Record my name on the tablets of longevity.
> I cast flowers to serve as your seats."

The Mudrā for Summoning the Northern Dipper: Hollow-hearted joined palms. The two thumbs press the nails of the two ring fingers. The middle fingers form a lotus-leaf shape. The two index fingers are slightly spread and bend back and forth. The mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-nārāṇa. Ehe-ki-pha-i-ha-i-na-i-ka-i-ra-mū-ra-ta-ra-ga-ga-haṃ svāhā.*

Visualize the seven stars of the Northern Dipper arriving to attend. Next, complete the offering. Then the Mudrā for Casting Flowers and Dismissal: Hollow-hearted joined palms. The two thumbs press the nails of the two ring fingers. The middle and little fingers form a lotus shape. The two index fingers are slightly spread and bend outward in a flicking motion. The mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-nārāṇa. Gaccha-pha-i-ha-i-ka-i-ra-mū-ra-ta-ra-ga-ha-haṃ svāhā.*

The Supplementary Mantra for Deficiencies:

*Oṃ huru-huru jaya-boke-i svāhā.*

Those who practice this Homa Ritual of the Seven Stars of the Northern Dipper will have their names struck from the registers of death and inscribed on the tablets of longevity. Its divine efficacy is most manifestly clear. For extending life and increasing one's allotted years, eliminating calamities and inviting wealth, there is no method for extending life that surpasses this one.

## The Northern Dipper Method

## Essential Method of Blazing Radiance

At that time Śākyamuni Buddha was in the Pure Abode Heavenly Palace and addressed the devas of the constellations, the grahas, and the twelve zodiacal spirits: "I shall now teach the mantra of the Tathāgata King of the Sāla of the past. If in any king's realm the five planets encroach upon the twenty-eight lunar mansions or the imperial throne, one should each morning in the place of practice form the mudrā and recite this mantra seven times. The five planets and all the grahas that encroach upon the various thrones will be unable to cause calamity. If the fire-planet [Mars] is about to enter the Southern Dipper, one should, beneath the image of a wrathful honored one, draw the shape of the Southern Dipper and mark the fire-planet. Within this, draw the form of that enemy and write their name upon the heart. Then form the mudrā, place the mudrā upon the body of the wrathful image, and recite the mantra seven times. At the svāhā line, add the name of that enemy. Recite the mantra in a fierce, wrathful voice, and all calamity will be moved away.

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ aparatihata-śāsanānāṃ. Oṃ kha kha khāhi khāhi hūṃ hūṃ jvala jvala prajvala prajvala tiṣṭha tiṣṭha sthiri sphaṭa sphaṭa svāhā.*

"The mudrā is as follows: the two hands are interlaced inward to form fists. The two middle fingers stand upright and are joined. Each index finger bends behind the middle finger, forming a vajra shape. The two thumbs each press the second joint of the middle fingers. This mantra has been empowered by all the tathāgatas and is called the Blazing Majestic Virtue Buddhauṣṇīṣa Mantra. It can accomplish eighty thousand kinds of auspicious affairs and can eliminate eighty thousand kinds of calamities, inauspicious events, and evil dreams. If one sees or hears any inauspicious matter, by reciting it once with a pure mind, it will be unable to cause harm. When the five planets reach any throne-position and cause encroachment, whether entering, passing through, or retreating and impinging, one should add the name of that planet at the svāhā line and recite the mantra seven times, commanding that planet to be benign and unable to cause harm. When Mars, Venus, or others reach the divisions and fields of various mansions or the imperial throne, within the division-field mansion, as before, draw the form of the evil person beneath the image, and this will also weaken that person and prevent them from giving rise to evil intentions.

"Or if Rāhu eclipses the sun or moon. Or if a comet appears. Or if there is drought or flood. Then in a hidden place within the place of practice, wearing black robes and sitting facing south, draw a triangular altar with cremation ash. In the center, write the forms of Rāhu, the comet, or similar bodies. In times of drought or flood, draw the form of a dragon. Upon the heart of that person's image, write that person's name. At midnight and at noon, burn asa-foetida incense. Recite the mantra, adding that name within the lines. With a pomegranate branch, whip that image. Recite the mantra twenty-one times. With each recitation, strike once, and the calamity will be removed. If the five planets' encroachment is powerful and one's fate is declining, also perform this method."

The Great Sage's Calamity-Pacifying Mantra:

*Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-śriye svāhā.*

The Mantra for Destroying Calamity from Mansions and Grahas:

*Oṃ sarva-tara-śriye samaye svāhā.*

The Mantra of the Seven Stars of the Northern Dipper:

*Kārike-jvārya-bara-paṭa-śuci-rāmāya svāhā.*

"For incense, use frankincense and white sandalwood. These may be placed on the altar."
