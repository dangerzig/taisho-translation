# 龍王兄弟經
## Sūtra of the Nāga King Brothers

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0597

Translated from the Chinese. Wu dynasty, translated by Zhi Qian (支謙)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the land of Śrāvastī, at the Jetavana Monastery of Anāthapiṇḍada. At that time there were countless bhikṣus, all of whom were arhats.

Anāthapiṇḍada came to the Buddha's presence, bowed in greeting, and sat to one side.

The Buddha said: "One should practice generosity, keep the precepts, practice patience, be diligent, cultivate meditation, and cultivate wisdom."

Anāthapiṇḍada heard this with joy. He immediately rose and said to the Buddha: "Tomorrow morning I invite the Buddha and the bhikṣu-saṃgha to graciously descend to my home, where I have prepared a simple meal." The Buddha was silent in assent. Anāthapiṇḍada circumambulated the Buddha three times and departed.

The Buddha told the bhikṣu-saṃgha: "Tomorrow we shall first ascend to the heavens, then descend at noon to gather at Anāthapiṇḍada's home."

As though in the time it takes to stretch out one's arm, the Buddha was already in the sky. An arhat named Sudāna adjusted his robes and, in the sky, knelt and said to the Buddha: "I constantly travel up and down, but I have never seen it as dark as it is today."

The Buddha said: "There are two nāga kings who, in their anger, have created this transformation. They have exhaled their breath as clouds, and that is why."

Another arhat named Aipo said to the Buddha: "I wish to go and stop them."

The Buddha said: "These nāgas have great supernatural power. If you go, they will surely give rise to evil intent and release water to flood and drown the people of the world."

Mahāmaudgalyāyana then adjusted his robes and knelt in the sky, saying to the Buddha: "Today it is so dark that Mount Sumeru, Indra's palace, and the pārijāta tree below can no longer be seen."

The Buddha said: "There are two nāga kings: the first named Nanda, the second named Upananda. In great fury they said: 'What śramaṇa dares fly over and brush the top of our heads?' The nāga bodies have coiled around Mount Sumeru seven times, and with their heads they cover the summit, exhaling mist and fog, and that is why it is dark."

Maudgalyāyana said to the Buddha: "I wish to go and rebuke them and make them stop."

The Buddha said: "Very good!" Maudgalyāyana circumambulated the Buddha three times and set out.

Śakra, lord of the Trāyastriṃśa heaven, was in his rear garden with eighty-eight thousand celestial maidens, enjoying themselves together. Maudgalyāyana passed by his place first. The lord of the heavens came to welcome him, bowed, and made obeisance. After they had greeted each other, Maudgalyāyana went on to the nāgas' place. The two nāgas saw him and were greatly enraged. They transformed and emitted smoke, and in a moment emitted fire. Maudgalyāyana, by the Buddha's power, also transformed and emitted smoke, which encircled the two nāgas three times. Then he gradually divided his body and entered the bodies of the two nāgas: entering the right eye and emerging from the left eye, entering the left eye and emerging from the right eye; entering the right ear and emerging from the left ear, entering the left ear and emerging from the right ear; entering the right nostril and emerging from the left nostril, entering the left nostril and emerging from the right nostril; then he flew into their mouths. The two nāgas thought Maudgalyāyana was inside their bellies. But Maudgalyāyana also assumed the form of a nāga and coiled around the two nāgas fourteen times, covering Mount Sumeru and the two nāgas with his head. The two nāgas, trapped below, trembled and thrashed. They shook Mount Sumeru and lashed the ocean waters with their tails, and all the creatures were terrified.

The Buddha called out to Maudgalyāyana from afar: "These nāgas are now able to release water and flood the world into destruction. You should stop for the moment!"

Maudgalyāyana said: "I have heard this Dharma from the Buddha and I know it. I have the four bases of supernatural power, and I shall faithfully uphold and practice them. I am able to take these two nāgas together with Mount Sumeru, place them in the palm of my hand, and leap across to another world. I am also able to grind Mount Sumeru to dust. I can also grind Mount Sumeru and the ground below it so that the people of the world would not even notice."

The two nāgas, terrified, bowed their heads. Maudgalyāyana resumed his śramaṇa form. The two nāgas took human form, bowed to Maudgalyāyana, and repented. Maudgalyāyana brought them to the Buddha's presence.

The two nāgas said: "We were confused, deluded, and mad. We did not know the honored sage. Our offense was like thunder. We beg that our crime be pardoned!" They then received the five precepts and departed.

Anāthapiṇḍada went to the monastery to look for the Buddha and the bhikṣu-saṃgha, but did not see a single person. He knelt and said to the Buddha: "The meal is fully prepared. May the Buddha graciously come."

The Buddha then descended and went to his home. After the meal, Anāthapiṇḍada said: "I looked for the Buddha but could not find you. Where has the Buddha come from?"

The Buddha said: "Maudgalyāyana was contending with two nāga kings. We have just come down from the heavens."

He asked: "Who was the victor?"

The Buddha said: "Maudgalyāyana."

Anāthapiṇḍada said: "Excellent! Excellent! These nāgas rode upon the precepts with mighty strength; their slightest transgression caused them to become nāgas, yet their supernatural power remains formidable. And Maudgalyāyana was able to subdue them! From this day forward, when I invite the Buddha and the bhikṣu-saṃgha, I shall proclaim the merit of Maudgalyāyana."

The Buddha pronounced a blessing on the householder Anāthapiṇḍada: "In your many past offerings of food to those who have attained the Way, the good spirits shall protect your household and keep all within it in peace."

Anāthapiṇḍada bowed and departed.
