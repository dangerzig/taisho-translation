# Short Sūtra on the Extinction of the Dharma

## *Xiǎo Fǎ Mièjìn Jīng* (小法滅盡經)

Taishō 2874

Translator unknown. An apocryphal prophetic text.

Thus have I heard. At one time the Buddha was in the kingdom of Kuśinagara (*Jūyínàjié*, 拘夷那竭). The Tathāgata would enter parinirvāṇa (*bān nìhuán*, 般泥洹) in three months. Together with the bhikṣus, the honored bodhisattvas, and a boundless assembly, they came to the Buddha's presence, prostrated with their heads to the ground, and gathered around him in a circle, thirsting to hear the dharma. The World-Honored One was silent and said nothing, and his radiance did not shine. The Venerable Ānanda (*Ānán*, 阿難) knelt at length with palms joined and said to the Buddha: "In all your previous teachings, your light shone forth in solitary brilliance. Now, before this great assembly, your radiance does not appear. What is the cause of this? There must be something extraordinary. We wish to hear its meaning." The Buddha remained silent and did not respond. Three times it was asked.

The Buddha said to the Venerable Ānanda: "After my parinirvāṇa, in the corrupt age of the five turbidities, the way of Māra will flourish. Māra will become monks and corrupt my way. They will wear lay clothing yet delight in fine kaṣāya robes of five colors. They will drink wine, eat roasted meat, kill living beings, and crave flavors. They will lack compassionate hearts and will hate and envy one another.

"At that time there will be bodhisattvas, pratyekabuddhas, arhats, and bhikṣus who diligently cultivate virtue. All will treat them with respect, and people will honor them. Their teaching and conversion will be equal, and they will take pity on the poor and remember the old, rescue and relieve the destitute and distressed, and constantly have people venerate sūtras and images. They will perform all kinds of meritorious deeds. Their dispositions will be kind and good, never harming others, sacrificing themselves to help all beings, never sparing themselves. But if such people exist, the Māra-bhikṣus will all despise and hate them, slander them and spread evil about them, expel and drive them away, not permitting them to remain.

"After this, they will themselves fail to cultivate the way and its virtues. Monasteries and temples will be empty and desolate, with no one to repair them, and they will gradually fall into ruin. The Māra-bhikṣus will only covet money and possessions, accumulating and not distributing them, never performing meritorious deeds. They will buy and sell slaves, plow fields and cultivate land, burn mountains and forests, and harm sentient beings without compassion. Male slaves will become bhikṣus; female slaves will become bhikṣuṇīs. They will be without the way or virtue, licentious, filthy, and disordered, with no distinction between men and women. It is on account of such people that the way will become cheap and base.

"Some, fleeing the magistrate's authority, will rely on my way and seek to become śramaṇas, yet they will not cultivate the precepts or discipline. At the half-month and full-moon recitation of the precepts, they will hang up the name but be weary and slack, unwilling to listen. They will abbreviate the beginning and end, unwilling to recite it in full. They will not read or lecture on the sūtras. And if there are those who do read, they will not recognize the characters and phrases, yet will insist they are right and refuse to consult those who are learned. Arrogant and seeking fame, they will strut about puffing their chests to heaven, considering this a worthy accomplishment, hoping people will make offerings to them. Such Māra-bhikṣus, after their lives end, will have their spirits fall into the Avīci hell. Not one of the five heinous offenses will they have failed to undergo. For kalpas as numerous as the sands of the Ganges, after their punishments are complete, they will be reborn in borderland countries where the Three Jewels are absent.

"When the dharma is about to be extinguished, women will be diligent, constantly performing meritorious deeds. Men will be negligent and will not heed the words of the dharma. They will look upon śramaṇas as they would upon dirt and dung, without any faith. As the kalpa nears its end, all the devas will weep. Rains and droughts will be out of season. The five grains will not ripen. Epidemics will spread. The dead will be many. The people will toil in suffering. Magistrates will be harsh and unjust, not following the principles of the dharma. All will delight in chaos. Evil people will multiply like sand in the sea.

"Because the kalpa is nearing its end, the sun and moon will be shortened. Good people will be extremely few, one or two at most. Human lifespans will be brief. At forty, hair turns white. Men, through licentiousness and exhaustion of their vital essence, will die young. Their lifespan will be sixty. Women will live to seventy, eighty, ninety, or a hundred. Great floods will suddenly arise, coming without warning. The people of the world will not believe it. Regardless of rank, all will be engulfed and drowned, floating on the waters, devoured by fish and turtles.

"The bodhisattvas, pratyekabuddhas, arhats, and bhikṣus will be driven away by the Māra-hosts and will not be allowed to join the assemblies. The practitioners of the three vehicles will enter the mountains, places of merit, where they will dwell in simplicity and contentment, taking this as their joy. Their lifespans will be extended, and the devas will protect them.

"When Candraprabha (*Yuèguāng*, 月光, 'Moonlight') appears in the world, those who encounter him will together revive my way." The five sūtras that have not yet been transformed will depart in text.
