# 佛說聖觀自在菩薩不空王祕密心陀羅尼經
## Sūtra of the Secret Heart Dhāraṇī of Noble Avalokiteśvara's Amoghapāśa-rāja, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1099

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dānapāla (施護) and others.

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the World-Honored One was on Mount Potalaka, in the palace of the Noble Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva. There were countless great śāla trees, tamāla trees, campaka trees, aśoka trees, and atimuktaka trees, adorned with all manner of precious trees such as these. He was attended by a great assembly of 1,800,000 bhikṣus, ninety koṭi-nayuta-hundred-thousand bodhisattvas, and furthermore Īśvara, Maheśvara, and other Brahmā-devaputras, together with countless hundreds of thousands of Pure-Light devaputras. This entire assembly surrounded him in reverence, listening and receiving the Dharma.

At that time, the Noble Avalokiteśvara, Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, placed his right knee on the ground, joined his palms toward the Buddha in reverence, prostrated, and with a gentle smile addressed the Buddha, saying:

"World-Honored One! I possess a dhāraṇī called Amoghapāśa-rāja, which is the Secret Heart. In the distant past, ninety-one kalpas ago, in the Universal Contemplation World, I personally heard and received it from the Avalokiteśvara-rāja Tathāgata. At that time, that Tathāgata taught the dharma of anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi to countless hundreds of thousands of Brahmā-devaputras and Pure-Light devaputras. At that time, I attained the Amoghajñāna and other hundred-thousand samādhi-gates.

"World-Honored One! In whatever place this Secret Heart Dhāraṇī is proclaimed and circulated, know that in that place twelve thousand Maheśvara-devaputras secretly provide protection. That place is the same as if Buddha-stūpas were present; one should honor, revere, and make offerings to it. Furthermore, if any person widely circulates this Amoghapāśa-rāja Secret Heart Dhāraṇī, know that this person has already deeply planted good roots before countless koṭi-nayuta-hundred-thousand buddhas. Furthermore, if any person briefly hears this dhāraṇī, even if that person has slandered the Buddha, slandered the Dharma, and slandered the bodhisattvas, śrāvakas, pratyekabuddhas, and other noble sages, having widely committed such extremely grave offenses deserving of falling into the Avīci hell, if they can with sincere mind fast purely for one day and recite this dhāraṇī according to the method, all such sins will be eliminated.

"Furthermore, if any person suffers from malarial fevers, whether of one day, two days, or up to seven days' duration, or from eye pain, ear pain, nose pain, or headache, or from scabies, leprosy, sores, ringworm, abscesses, tumors, or blisters, or is seized, bound, struck, or thrown about by any non-human being, evil spirit, or demon, or is afflicted by spells, curses, and deluding sorcery: to speak in brief, for all illnesses of body and mind collectively, and even for seeing inauspicious things in dreams, by the empowering force of this dhāraṇī, all are eliminated and none can cause harm.

"World-Honored One! Furthermore, if among kṣatriyas, brāhmaṇas, vaiśyas, śūdras, and other such castes there are those with deceitful and dishonest hearts, they too are permitted to hear, receive, copy, read, and recite this dhāraṇī. And even for all animals and other creatures, one should recite this dhāraṇī on their behalf so they may hear it, and having heard it, each will obtain benefit according to their capacity. How much more so for bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, and upāsikās who have generated pure and resolute faith and sincerely hear and receive this dhāraṇī!

"Furthermore, if any person contemplates with focused mind the secret chapters of this dhāraṇī, they should be free from all faults: not slandering, not praising, not generating doubt, developing a mind free from self-attachment, without mind of near or far, without mind of contrivance, without defiled mind, without mind of high or low. Dwelling in such a mind and practicing contemplation of the Buddha, that person will immediately see one thousand buddhas in each of the ten directions appearing before them.

"World-Honored One! If in any lay household where this dhāraṇī sūtra is present, whether master or servant, upon seeing this sūtra, even if they treat it lightly or laugh at it, by my spiritual power they will naturally come to hear the sound of this dhāraṇī.

"World-Honored One! It is as if someone were to take sandalwood incense, or camphor, or musk, and beat it, chop it, or saw it, and furthermore curse it with abusive words, and then grind it upon a coarse, rough stone and apply it to the body. Yet even if someone were to say, 'Do not beat, chop, curse, or grind it,' once the sandalwood and other fragrances have been applied to the body, the fragrance remains. World-Honored One! My Amoghapāśa-rāja Secret Heart Dhāraṇī is just like this. Even if someone treats it lightly or laughs at it, or even lacks a sincere heart, if they merely make offerings to this dhāraṇī, by the power of the good roots from those offerings, in life after life they will always possess the perfume of the aggregates of śīla, samādhi, prajñā, and other virtues.

"Furthermore, when any bhikṣu, bhikṣuṇī, upāsaka, or upāsikā wishes to uphold this Amoghapāśa-rāja Secret Heart Dhāraṇī, they should arrange an altar with offerings according to the method on the eighth day of the bright fortnight. The practitioner must fast in purity and, with an unconditioned mind, recite this Secret Heart Dhāraṇī seven times. That person will obtain twenty kinds of merit:

1. They will be born amid wealth and honor.
2. Having been born, all past-life karmic illnesses will quickly be eliminated.
3. They will always have a body that is smooth, fine, and beautiful, with faculties well-formed and hidden, beloved by all.
4. They will obtain great wealth.
5. Their possessions will not be taken by thieves.
6. Their possessions will not be lost to fire or flood.
7. They will always have expanding fields.
8. Their crops will not be damaged by thunder or hail.
9. Their fields will not be harmed by excessive rain.
10. Their vigor and spirit will suffer no diminishment.
11. They will always be honored and loved by all beings.
12. They will not be frightened by any enemy.
13. If enemies arise, they will quickly be reconciled.
14. They will not be frightened by non-human beings.
15. They will not be bound by evil illness.
16. They will not be frightened by ḍākinīs.
17. They will be far from all afflictions.
18. They will not die by water, fire, weapons, or poison.
19. Wherever they are, they will always be secretly guarded by noble sages.
20. In every life they are born, they will always remain inseparable from loving-kindness, compassion, sympathetic joy, and equanimity.

These are called the twenty kinds of merit.

"Furthermore, World-Honored One! Those who recite this dhāraṇī will additionally obtain eight kinds of auspicious signs:

1. At the time of death, I will appear before them in the form of a bhikṣu.
2. At the time of death, their eyes will be undisturbed, and their body and mind will be peaceful and at ease.
3. At the time of death, their hands will not grasp at the air, their feet will not kick the ground, and there will be no defiling discharge of excrement or urine.
4. They will not die face-down.
5. At the time of death, they will dwell in right mindfulness.
6. At the time of death, they will not be separated from spiritual friends.
7. After death, they will be reborn in whatever buddha-land they wish.
8. In their next life, they will attain inexhaustible eloquence.

These are called the eight kinds of auspicious signs.

"Furthermore, World-Honored One! If any person fasts purely, avoids the five pungent vegetables and all meat and impure foods, and recites this dhāraṇī three times during each of the three periods of the day, all their wishes will be fulfilled. Furthermore, if any person has the good fortune to hear this Amoghapāśa-rāja Secret Heart Dhāraṇī of the true Dharma, and is able to cut off all stinginess and jealousy, remove all defilements, generate a pure mind, and respectfully take refuge in guarding and upholding this secret, that person will be counted among the bodhisattvas, will well benefit beings greatly, and will ultimately attain the fruit of buddha-bodhi.

"World-Honored One! Here, 'bodhi' means 'right wisdom,' and 'sattva' means 'skillful means.' If one obtains both wisdom and skillful means in full measure, one can then perform the work of benefiting beings."

At that time, the Noble Avalokiteśvara, Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, again addressed the Buddha, saying: "World-Honored One! I now wish, in the presence of the Tathāgata, to proclaim all the Secret Heart Dhāraṇīs, so that the fourfold assembly in this gathering may hear them for their benefit and happiness, and so that all others who have committed sinful karma may also obtain good benefit."

The Buddha said to the Noble Avalokiteśvara, Bodhisattva Mahāsattva: "Pure Great Being! You should well proclaim them. Now is the proper time, and the Tathāgata today will also rejoice. And even in the final age to come, they will serve as a good refuge for those who cultivate the bodhisattva vehicle."

At this, the Noble Avalokiteśvara joyfully raised his eyes, gazed upon the World-Honored One, and addressed the Buddha, saying: "May the bodhisattvas in this assembly listen well and attentively, and furthermore take refuge! This wheel of liberation can give benefit and happiness to many people, compassionately caring for the world and performing all works of great benefit and joy. I shall now proclaim this dhāraṇī. First I pay homage to all the buddhas and bodhisattvas well-abiding in the three times, past, future, and present, and to all the śrāvakas and pratyekabuddhas of those three times. Furthermore I pay homage to the Tathāgata Amitābha, the Arhat, the Perfectly Enlightened One. I pay homage to the Bodhisattva Mahāsattva Maitreya and other bodhisattvas. I pay homage to the Arhat Śāriputra of Great Wisdom and other arhats."

He then spoke the dhāraṇī:

*Namo ratna-trayāya. Nama āryāvalokiteśvarāya bodhisattvāya mahāsattvāya mahākāruṇikāya. Idaṃ namaḥ-kṛtvā āryāvalokiteśvara-mukhodyānaṃ amogha-pāśana-mahṛdayaṃ tathāgata-sammukhā-bhāṣitaṃ mahā-tāpa-pariśodhana-mati ahimāna-nivartaya iṣṭāya asiddhāmi sarva-kāryāṇi sarva-bhayeṣu jokṣmi rakṣa-bhavatu. Tadyathā: oṃ cara cara ciri ciri curu curu mahākāruṇika śrī śrī ciri ciri viri viri mahā-padma-sattvāya kara kara kiri kiri kuru kuru mahā-śuddha-sattvāya buddha buddha dhava dhava kini kini parama-śuddha-sattvāya kara kara kiri kiri kuru kuru mahā-sthāma-prāptāya cara cara saṃcara vicara iṭa iṭa bhara bhara viri viri bhuru bhuru ihi ihi mahākāruṇika mahā-paśupati-viśa-dhara sara sara vara vara para para vara vara hara hara haha hihi huhu. Oṃ kara-brahma-viśa-dhara dhara dhara dhiri dhiri dhuru dhuru tara tara sara sara para para vara vara rāśmi-śata-sahasra-pratimānita śarīra jvala jvala tapa tapa. Bhagavān soma agni-deva yama varuṇa kubera brahma indra ṛṣi deva-gaṇa-vyāricita caraṇa suru suru curu curu muru muru sanatkumāra-rudra-vāsava viṣṇu-danadṛśi-vidyā-karma-vihuvihi-viśa-dhara dhara dhara dhiri dhiri dhuru dhuru ghara ghara para para rara rara rahu rahu hara hara mara mara vara vara varanāyaka. Samanta-valokita-vilokita-lokeśvara-mahīśvara mahu mahu muru muru muya muya mañca mañca rakṣa rakṣa mama sarva-sattvāṃśca sarva-bhayebhyaḥ sarvopadrava-vibhyaḥ sarvopasargaḥ sarva-graha-vibhyaḥ mādhya-mandhana-tana-tanātra-jara-rājata-skara-ghnāt-yuddha-kavi-śaśastra-parimocaka gaṇa gaṇa gini gini kunu kunu cara cara indriya-mala-mogha-caturya-sādhe saṃprakāśaka. Tatma tatma dama dama dhama dhama sama sama mātha mātha sama sama mahā-tamo-dhakāra-vidhamana ṣaṭ-pāramitā-paripūraka miri miri ṭa ṭa ṭa ṭa ṭhī ṭhī ṭhī ṭhī cha cha cha cha chi chi chi chi inīya-carma-kṛta-parikara. Ihi ihi mahākāruṇika īśvara-mahā-bhūta-gaṇa-pañjaka kuru kuru para para kara kara kaṭa kaṭa maṭa maṭa viśuddha-viśaya-vāsina mahākāruṇika satya-nigama-upavitra-ratna-mukuṭa-māla-dhara sarva-nīya-śirasi-kṛta-mukuṭa mahādbhuta-kamāra-kṛta-kara-tārāṅgana-samādhi-vimokṣya-prakāśya-bahusattva-śānti-paripācaka mahākāruṇika sarva-karma-āvaraṇa-viśodhaka sarva-maitri-pramocaka ākāra-mṛtyu-upraśamana sarva-sattva-samaśvāsa-kāra sarva-śamānurtha-paripūrakana. Namaḥ stute svāhā.*

The Great Heart Dhāraṇī:

*Amoghāya svāhā.*

The Small Heart Dhāraṇī:

*Ajitāya svāhā.*

The Heart-within-Heart Dhāraṇī:

*Varapradāya svāhā.*

The Weapon Dhāraṇī:

*Aparājitāya svāhā.*

The Armor Dhāraṇī:

*Idaṃ karma kuru namastute svāhā.*

The Crown Dhāraṇī:

*Oṃ hūṃ jayaḥ svāhā.*

The Topknot Dhāraṇī:

*Oṃ hrīḥ strailokya-vijaya amoghapāśāya pratihata hrīḥ haḥ hūṃ phaṭ.*

"World-Honored One! This dhāraṇī of mine can accomplish all undertakings. If one constantly recites it, all endeavors will be achieved. Furthermore, if any person has committed the five heinous crimes, if that person can recite this dhāraṇī three times during each of the three periods of the day, all such karmic obstructions will be completely purified.

"If one wishes to perform a boundary-establishment method, one should empower aloeswood incense with this dhāraṇī and use it according to the method. If one wishes to stop malarial fevers, one should empower ash-water, or make stakes from khadira wood, and use them according to the method. If one wishes to eliminate all illnesses, one should empower ghee, oil, or water and use them for anointing. If one wishes to stop illness caused by kākhordha demons, one should empower a knife and use it according to the method. If one wishes to perform various protection methods, one should empower thread and use it according to the method. If one wishes to stop stomach pain, one should empower salt-water and drink it according to the method. If one wishes to eliminate evil poisons, one should empower earth or water and use them according to the method. If one wishes to cure eye disease, one should empower white thread and tie it to the ear on the corresponding side. If one wishes to cure toothache, one should empower a piece of karavīra wood and use it as a tooth-stick.

"Furthermore, if one wishes to perform a boundary-establishment method, one should take five-colored thread, empower it twenty-one times, make stakes from khadira wood and drive them into the four directions of the boundary, then stretch the thread to demarcate the boundary. This completes the boundary establishment. Furthermore, if one wishes to perform a protection method, one should empower water, cremation-ground ash, or pure thread and use them as appropriate. If one wishes to release those seized by demons, one should empower five-colored thread and use it according to the method. If one wishes to cure malarial fevers, one should empower white thread and use it according to the method. If one wishes to cure scrofula, tumors, or swellings, one should empower long pepper, mix it with honey, and apply it to the affected area. If one wishes to cure eye disease, one should empower fragrant water, licorice water, or water infused with prāśa leaves. If one wishes to stop quarrels, disputes, and litigation, one should empower pure water and wash the person's face with it. If one wishes to protect a king's territory from all incursions and disturbances and to achieve victory, one should select a fine vessel, place it in a clean location, fill it with water, and arrange offerings. The practitioner should wear fresh, clean garments, recite this dhāraṇī to empower the water, and then take the water from the vessel and sprinkle it throughout the territory. The realm will then be peaceful and secure, calamities will not arise, and both humans and animals will be fully protected. If one wishes to dispel an evil seal, one should empower sandalwood powder twenty-one times and apply it to one's own heart center, and the seal will be removed. If one wishes to perform protection for one's own dwelling, one should perform a lotus-flower homa offering. If one wishes to subdue those who are difficult to tame, one should perform a sandalwood homa offering.

"Furthermore, the accomplishment method: one should take jayagandha incense, paramajayagandha incense, abhayahasta incense, indrahasta incense, vāruṇī incense, nākulī incense, another variety of nākulī incense, priyaṅgu incense, and tagara incense, together with candrarāja medicine, prīti medicine, cakra medicine, mahācakra medicine, and viṣṇukrānta medicine.

"Take all the above fragrances and medicines, combine and form them into a single pill, and empower it with this dhāraṇī one hundred and eight times. If any person is seized by ghosts, spirits, or demons and is frightened, they may wear it on the crown of the head or on the upper arm, and it will provide protection. If a woman is about to give birth, take the aforementioned fragrant medicine, grind it into powder, empower it according to the method, and dissolve it in water for bathing. The woman will then give birth to a child of superior merit, safely and without difficulty. All inauspicious occurrences will be eliminated, and furthermore, after birth, the child will be well protected, free from all sufferings, and evil poisons will not arise; should any arise, they will quickly be dispelled. If one wishes to protect a newborn child, take the aforementioned fragrant medicine pill, empower it according to the method, and place it on the child's crown for protection. If one wishes to stop wind, clouds, thunder, and hail, one should take a branch of the vāruṇī tree, place it in pure water, empower it according to the method, and then, holding the branch, sprinkle the water about.

"World-Honored One! This Secret Heart Dhāraṇī of mine can accomplish the supreme attainment. All the methods described above will without exception be accomplished. Even if the practitioner has not yet become proficient in recitation, they will still achieve results.

"Furthermore, I shall explain the method for accomplishing the vision of the presiding deity. If a person wishes to see my form and seek accomplishment, they should, on the fifteenth day of the bright fortnight, prepare a painting-banner according to the method, using the finest unmixed colors to paint my image in the form of Maheśvara, with a jeweled crown on the head, an antelope skin as a chest-sash, and adorned with all manner of precious jewels. When the painting is complete, it should be installed according to the method. The practitioner should then, before that painted image, make a maṇḍala of cow-dung, scatter various fine flowers, arrange eight kinds of incense as offerings, and furthermore set out sixty-four varieties of prepared food and drink, all pure and free from blood, meat, and rank or impure substances. Burn aloeswood incense. The practitioner should fast day and night, bathe the body three times daily, wear fresh clean garments, and with focused mind make offerings. Then, before the painted image, recite this dhāraṇī one thousand and eighty times. Afterward, visualize the form of the presiding deity, and one will see one's own body blazing with great flames. When the practitioner sees this, their heart should be filled with joy; in a moment they will see my form come and appear before them, bestowing all that is sought and fulfilling every wish. At that time, the practitioner should apply orpiment or añjana medicine to the eyes, and they will be able to become invisible, fly through the sky, and freely enter the Amoghajñāna-vyūha-samādhi-gate. Whatever they desire and whatever they undertake will all be perfectly fulfilled."

When the Buddha had finished speaking this sūtra, the Noble Avalokiteśvara, Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, together with the remaining Īśvara, Maheśvara, and other Brahmā hosts and Pure-Light devaputras, and all the bodhisattvas, śrāvakas, and the entire great assembly, having heard what the Buddha proclaimed, all greatly rejoiced, faithfully received it, and put it into practice.
