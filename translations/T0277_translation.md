# 佛說觀普賢菩薩行法經
## Sūtra Spoken by the Buddha on the Contemplation of the Practice of Samantabhadra Bodhisattva

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0277

Translated from the Chinese. Liu Song dynasty, translated by Dharmamitra (曇無蜜多)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the land of Vaiśālī, in the Great Forest Monastery, in the storied lecture hall. He addressed the bhikṣus: "Three months from now, I shall enter parinirvāṇa."

The Elder Ānanda immediately rose from his seat, adjusted his robes, joined his palms together, circumambulated the Buddha three times, prostrated to the Buddha, and knelt with palms joined, gazing intently upon the Tathāgata without taking his eyes away for even a moment. The Elder Mahākāśyapa and the Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Maitreya also rose from their seats, joined their palms in reverence, and gazed up at the World-Honored One's countenance.

At that time, these three great beings spoke with different mouths but a single voice, addressing the Buddha: "World-Honored One! After the Tathāgata's nirvāṇa, how can sentient beings give rise to the bodhisattva-mind, cultivate the Mahāyāna Vaipulya sūtras, and with right mindfulness contemplate the realm of One Reality? How can they avoid losing the mind of unsurpassed bodhi? How can they, without severing afflictions and without departing from the five desires, purify the sense-faculties, eliminate all transgressions, and with the pure ordinary eyes born of their parents, without severing the five desires, see things beyond all obstructions?"

The Buddha addressed Ānanda: "Listen carefully, listen carefully, and reflect well upon it. In the past, the Tathāgata at Mount Gṛdhrakūṭa and at other places of residence already broadly analyzed the path of One Reality. Now in this place, for all sentient beings of the future who wish to practice the unsurpassed Mahāyāna Dharma and who wish to study the practice of Samantabhadra, I shall now teach the method of contemplation. If one sees Samantabhadra or does not see him, the method for removing transgressions is the same. I shall now broadly analyze it for you.

"Ānanda! The Bodhisattva Samantabhadra was born in the Pure and Wondrous Land in the east. The characteristics of that land have already been broadly analyzed in the *Lotus Sūtra*. I shall now briefly explain it here.

"Ānanda! If bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, upāsikās, the eight classes of celestial beings and dragons, or any sentient being who recites the Mahāyāna sūtras, who cultivates the Mahāyāna, who has generated the Mahāyāna aspiration, who delights in seeing the form-body of the Bodhisattva Samantabhadra, who delights in seeing the stūpa of the Buddha Many-Treasures, who delights in seeing Śākyamuni Buddha and the buddhas of his emanation-bodies, who delights in obtaining purity of the six sense-faculties: such a person should learn this contemplation. The merit of this contemplation removes all obstructions and enables one to see supreme and wondrous forms. Without entering samādhi, merely through recitation and upholding, concentrating the mind and cultivating practice, with thought following thought without departing from the Mahāyāna, for one day to three times seven days, one will see Samantabhadra. Those with heavy obstructions will see him after seven times seven days. Those with still heavier obstructions will see him in one lifetime; those with yet heavier obstructions in two lifetimes; those with the heaviest in three lifetimes. Thus the karmic retributions are various and different; therefore the teaching varies accordingly.

"The Bodhisattva Samantabhadra's body is immeasurable, his voice is immeasurable, and his form is immeasurable. Wishing to come to this world, he enters the samādhi of sovereign spiritual transformation and contracts his body to a small size. Because the people of Jambudvīpa have heavy triple obstructions, through the power of wisdom he transforms himself to ride a white elephant. The elephant has six tusks, and its seven limbs touch the ground. Beneath its seven limbs grow seven lotus flowers. The elephant's color is a brilliant white surpassing even crystal and snowy mountains, which cannot compare. Its body is four hundred and fifty yojanas long and four hundred yojanas tall. At the tip of each of the six tusks is a bathing pool, and in each bathing pool grow fourteen lotus flowers, equal in size to the pool. When the flowers open, they are like celestial tree-kings. Upon each flower sits a jade maiden whose complexion is rosy, surpassing even that of celestial maidens. In their hands naturally appear five kinds of harps, and each harp has five hundred musical instruments as its retinue. There are five hundred flying birds: mandarin ducks, geese, and swans, all of jeweled colors, born among the flower petals. The elephant's trunk bears a flower whose stem is the color of a red pearl. The flower itself is golden, yet still in bud and not yet open.

"Having seen these things, one should further confess and, with a sincere mind, carefully contemplate, thinking upon the Mahāyāna with a mind that does not cease. When the flower opens, it is golden in color with golden light. The lotus pedestal is of the kiṃśuka jewel, with wondrous brahma-maṇi forming the flower garlands and vajra jewels forming the stamens. One sees transformation-buddhas seated upon the lotus pedestals, and many bodhisattvas seated upon the lotus stamens. From the brows of the transformation-buddhas also emanate golden light, which enters the elephant's trunk. From the elephant's trunk it emerges and enters the elephant's eyes. From the elephant's eyes it emerges and enters the elephant's ears. From the elephant's ears it emerges and illuminates the top of the elephant's head, transforming into a golden platform.

"Upon the elephant's head are three transformation-beings: one holds a golden wheel, one holds a maṇi pearl, and one wields a vajra pestle. When the pestle is raised toward the elephant, the elephant is able to walk. Its feet do not touch the ground; it treads upon the void, hovering seven feet above the earth. Yet marks appear on the ground: in the imprints, wheel-spokes and rims are all fully formed. In each space between the rims grows a great lotus flower, and upon each lotus flower a transformation-elephant appears, also with seven limbs, following the great elephant's steps. Each time it raises or lowers a foot, seven thousand elephants are born as its retinue, following the great elephant.

"On the elephant's trunk, which is the color of a red lotus, above sits a transformation-buddha who emits light between the brows. This golden light, as before, enters the elephant's trunk. From the trunk it emerges and enters the elephant's eyes. From the elephant's eyes it emerges and enters the elephant's ears. From the elephant's ears it emerges and reaches the elephant's neck, gradually ascending to the elephant's back, where it transforms into a golden saddle, adorned with seven-jeweled ornaments. On the four sides of the saddle are seven-jeweled pillars, adorned with many jewels forming a jeweled platform.

"Within the platform is a seven-jeweled lotus flower. The stamens of the lotus are made of a hundred jewels. The lotus pedestal is a great maṇi jewel. Upon it sits a bodhisattva in the cross-legged position, named Samantabhadra. His body is the color of white jade, with fifty kinds of radiance. These fifty kinds of radiance and color form his halo. From all the pores of his body flow golden light, and at the tip of each golden ray are immeasurable transformation-buddhas. The transformation-bodhisattvas form his retinue. He walks calmly and steadily, raining down great jeweled flowers as he arrives before the practitioner. The elephant opens its mouth, and upon the elephant's tusks, the jade maidens in the pools play music and sing, their voices subtle and wondrous, praising the path of the One Reality of the Mahāyāna.

"Having seen this, the practitioner is filled with joy and reverence. He further recites and reads the profound sūtras, prostrates universally to the immeasurable buddhas of the ten directions, prostrates to the stūpa of Many-Treasures and to Śākyamuni, and prostrates to Samantabhadra and all the great bodhisattvas. He makes this vow: 'If my accumulated merit should allow me to see Samantabhadra, may the honored Universally Auspicious One show me his form-body.' Having made this vow, day and night six times he prostrates to the buddhas of the ten directions, practices the method of confession, recites the Mahāyāna sūtras, reads the Mahāyāna sūtras, contemplates the Mahāyāna meaning, keeps the Mahāyāna in mind, respectfully makes offerings to those who uphold the Mahāyāna, regards all people as if they were the Buddha, and regards all sentient beings as if they were his parents.

"Having formed these thoughts, the Bodhisattva Samantabhadra then emits between his brows the great-being mark of white-tuft radiance. When this light appears, the Bodhisattva Samantabhadra's body appears dignified and splendid like a mountain of purple gold, upright, beautiful, and with all thirty-two marks fully present. From all the pores of his body radiate great light, illuminating his great elephant and making it golden. All the transformation-elephants also become golden. All the transformation-bodhisattvas also become golden. The golden light illuminates the eastern direction without limit, making all the worlds the same golden color. The south, west, north, and the four intermediate directions, above and below, are likewise.

"At that time, in each of the ten directions there is one bodhisattva riding a six-tusked white elephant king, identical to Samantabhadra, without any difference. Thus in the ten directions, immeasurably and boundlessly, the transformation-elephants fill every space. Through the spiritual power of the Bodhisattva Samantabhadra, those who uphold the sūtras all see them.

"At this time, the practitioner sees all the bodhisattvas and, with body and mind filled with joy, prostrates to them and says: 'O greatly compassionate and greatly merciful ones! In pity for me, please teach me the Dharma.' As he speaks these words, the bodhisattvas, with different mouths but a single voice, each teach the pure Mahāyāna Dharma, composing various verses praising the practitioner. This is called the initial contemplation of the Bodhisattva Samantabhadra, the first realm of practice.

"At that time, having seen these things, the practitioner keeps the Mahāyāna in mind day and night without ceasing. In his dreams, he sees Samantabhadra teaching the Dharma for him, the same as when awake, comforting his mind and saying: 'You recited and upheld a certain sūtra, and you have forgotten this phrase and this verse.' At that time, the practitioner, hearing what the Bodhisattva Samantabhadra teaches, deeply understands its meaning and purport, remembers and upholds it without forgetting. Day after day it continues thus, and his mind gradually becomes sharp.

"The Bodhisattva Samantabhadra teaches him to recollect the buddhas of the ten directions. Following Samantabhadra's teaching, with a right mind and right intention, he gradually, with the mind's eye, sees the buddhas of the eastern direction, their bodies of golden color, dignified and wondrous. Having seen one buddha, he then sees another. Gradually, in this way, he sees all the buddhas of the eastern direction. Because his mind's perception has become sharp, he sees all the buddhas of the ten directions everywhere.

"Having seen all the buddhas, he generates joy in his mind and says: 'Because of the Mahāyāna, I have been able to see these great beings. Because of the great beings' power, I have been able to see all the buddhas. Although I see the buddhas, I still do not see them clearly. When I close my eyes I can see them; when I open my eyes I cannot.' Having said this, he prostrates with his entire body on the ground, making universal obeisance to the buddhas of the ten directions. Having prostrated to all the buddhas, he kneels with palms joined and says: 'All world-honored buddhas, with the ten powers, fearlessness, and eighteen special qualities, with great compassion and the three mindful abodes, always present in the world, supreme among all forms! What transgression do I have that I cannot see them?' Having said this, he further confesses.

"After confessing and becoming pure, the Bodhisattva Samantabhadra again appears before him, whether walking, standing, sitting, or lying down, never departing from his side. Even in his dreams, he constantly teaches the Dharma. When the person awakens, he attains the joy of the Dharma. In this way, day and night, after three times seven days, he then obtains the revolving dhāraṇī. Because he obtains dhāraṇī, the wondrous Dharma taught by all buddhas and bodhisattvas is remembered and never lost. He also constantly dreams of the past seven buddhas. Only Śākyamuni Buddha teaches the Dharma for him, and each of these world-honored ones praises the Mahāyāna sūtras.

"At that time, the practitioner further confesses and universally prostrates to the buddhas of the ten directions. Having prostrated to the buddhas of the ten directions, the Bodhisattva Samantabhadra stands before him, teaching him all the karmic causes of past lives, instructing him to reveal and expose all his black evil transgressions. Before all the world-honored ones, he confesses with his own mouth. Having confessed, he immediately attains the samādhi of the buddhas appearing before him.

"Having attained this samādhi, he sees the Buddha Akṣobhya and the Abhirati world in the east, clearly and distinctly. Likewise, in each of the ten directions he sees all the buddhas and their supreme and wondrous lands, clearly and distinctly.

"Having seen the buddhas of the ten directions, in a dream he sees atop the elephant's head a vajra being who, with a vajra pestle, touches each of his six sense-faculties in turn. Having touched the six sense-faculties, the Bodhisattva Samantabhadra teaches the practitioner the Dharma of confessing and purifying the six sense-faculties. Confessing in this way, from one day to seven days, through the power of the samādhi of the buddhas appearing before him, and through the majesty of the Bodhisattva Samantabhadra's Dharma-teaching, his ears gradually hear sounds beyond obstructions, his eyes gradually see things beyond obstructions, and his nose gradually smells fragrances beyond obstructions. As broadly explained in the *Lotus Sūtra*, having obtained the purity of the six sense-faculties, his body and mind are filled with joy, free from all evil marks. His mind is pure Dharma, and he accords with the Dharma.

"He further obtains hundreds of thousands of millions of billions of revolving dhāraṇīs, and further broadly sees hundreds of thousands of millions of billions of immeasurable buddhas. All these world-honored ones each extend their right hands to touch the practitioner's head, saying: 'Excellent, excellent! Practitioner of the Mahāyāna! One who has generated the mind of great adornment! One who keeps the Mahāyāna in mind! When we in the past generated the aspiration for bodhi, we were all just like you, diligently persevering without flagging. Because we practiced the Mahāyāna in past lives, we have now become the pure bodies of right and universal knowledge. You should now also diligently cultivate without negligence. This great Mahāyāna canon is the treasure of all buddhas, the eye-organ of the buddhas of the three times in the ten directions, and produces the seed of the tathāgatas of the three times. One who upholds this sūtra upholds the Buddha-body, practices the Buddha's work, and one should know that such a person is an emissary of all buddhas, covered by the robes of all buddha world-honored ones, a true Dharma-child of all tathāgatas. You practice the Mahāyāna and do not cut off the Dharma-seed. You should now carefully observe the buddhas of the eastern direction.'

"As these words are spoken, the practitioner immediately sees that in all the immeasurable worlds of the eastern direction, the ground is as flat as the palm of one's hand, without any mounds, hillocks, or thorns. The ground is made of lapis lazuli, bordered with gold. In the ten directions, the worlds are likewise. Having seen this ground, he then sees jeweled trees. The jeweled trees are five thousand yojanas tall, and from the trees constantly emerge gold, silver, and seven-jeweled adornments. Beneath the trees there naturally appear jeweled lion-thrones. The lion-thrones are twenty yojanas tall, and from the thrones also emanate hundred-jeweled radiance. All such trees and jeweled thrones, each and every one, naturally bear five hundred white elephants upon them, and upon each elephant sits a Bodhisattva Samantabhadra.

"At that time, the practitioner prostrates to all the Samantabhadras and says: 'What transgression do I have that I see only jeweled ground, jeweled thrones, and jeweled trees, but do not see the buddhas?' As he speaks these words, upon each throne there is a world-honored one, dignified and wondrous, seated upon the jeweled throne.

"Having seen all the buddhas, his mind is filled with great joy, and he further recites and studies the Mahāyāna sūtras. Through the power of the Mahāyāna, a voice in the sky praises him, saying: 'Excellent, excellent, good man! Through your practice of the Mahāyāna, and through the merit of that practice, you are able to see all the buddhas. Although you have now seen the buddha world-honored ones, you are still unable to see Śākyamuni Buddha, his emanation-body buddhas, or the stūpa of the Buddha Many-Treasures.' Having heard the voice from the sky, he further diligently recites and practices the Mahāyāna sūtras. Because he recites the Mahāyāna Vaipulya sūtras, immediately in a dream he sees Śākyamuni Buddha with all the great assemblies at Mount Gṛdhrakūṭa, teaching the *Lotus Sūtra* and expounding the meaning of the One Reality.

"Having been taught to confess, he yearns with great longing to see, and with palms joined and kneeling, he faces Mount Gṛdhrakūṭa and says: 'The Tathāgata, the World-Hero, is always present in the world. In pity for me, please manifest your body for me.' Having said this, he sees Mount Gṛdhrakūṭa adorned with seven treasures, and countless bhikṣus and the great śrāvaka assemblies, and rows of jeweled trees, and the jeweled ground, flat and even, further spread with wondrous jeweled lion-thrones. Śākyamuni Buddha emits radiance between his brows, and his light pervades the worlds of the ten directions and further extends beyond immeasurable worlds of the ten directions.

"To these light-illuminated places, the emanation-body Śākyamuni Buddhas of the ten directions all gather like clouds, as broadly explained in the *Lotus Sūtra*. Each emanation-body buddha has a body of purple-gold color, a body of immeasurable size, seated on a lion-throne, with hundreds of billions of immeasurable bodhisattva-mahāsattvas as their retinues. Each bodhisattva's practice is the same as Samantabhadra's. The immeasurable buddhas of the ten directions and their bodhisattva-retinues are likewise.

"When the great assembly has gathered, he sees Śākyamuni Buddha, from every pore of his entire body, emitting golden light. Within each ray of light are hundreds of billions of transformation-buddhas. All the emanation-body buddhas emit between their brows the great-being mark of white-tuft radiance, and that light streams into the crown of Śākyamuni Buddha's head. Having seen this mark, the emanation-body buddhas each emit golden light from every pore. Within each ray of light there are further Ganges-sands of numberless transformation-buddhas.

"At that time, the Bodhisattva Samantabhadra further emits between his brows the great-being mark of radiance, which enters the practitioner's mind. Having entered his mind, the practitioner naturally remembers the past innumerable hundreds of thousands of buddhas in whose presence he received, upheld, read, and recited the Mahāyāna sūtras. He sees his former bodies clearly, just as if through the superknowledge of knowing past lives, without any difference. He is suddenly and greatly awakened, attaining revolving dhāraṇī and hundreds of thousands of millions of billions of dhāraṇī-gates.

"Arising from samādhi, face to face he sees all the emanation-body buddhas beneath the many jeweled trees, seated upon lion-thrones. He further sees the lapis lazuli ground, which is like a gathering of lotus flowers. From beneath, from the space below, they spring forth. Between each flower, bodhisattvas as numerous as atoms sit in the cross-legged position. He also sees the emanation-body Bodhisattva Samantabhadras in that assembly, praising the Mahāyāna.

"At that time, the bodhisattvas, with different mouths but a single voice, teach the practitioner to purify the six sense-faculties. Some say: 'You should be mindful of the Buddha.' Some say: 'You should be mindful of the Dharma.' Some say: 'You should be mindful of the Sangha.' Some say: 'You should be mindful of the precepts.' Some say: 'You should be mindful of generosity.' Some say: 'You should be mindful of heaven.' 'These six dharmas constitute the mind of bodhi and give rise to the bodhisattva-dharma. You should now, before all the buddhas, reveal and expose your prior transgressions and sincerely confess. Through the causes and conditions of immeasurable lifetimes, the eye-faculty has been attached to various forms. Because of attachment to forms, it has been greedy and loving toward dust. Because of loving dust, it has received a woman's body. Life after life, in every place of birth, it has been confusedly attached to various forms. Form destroys your eyes and makes you a slave to passion. The emissary of form causes you to travel through the three realms. Because of this wretched emissary, you are blind and without sight. Now you recite the Mahāyāna Vaipulya sūtras. This sūtra says that the form-bodies of the buddhas of the ten directions are indestructible. Do you now truly see this? Your eye-faculty has not been wholesome and has caused you much harm. Follow my words and take refuge in the buddhas. Śākyamuni Buddha will proclaim for you the transgressions of your eye-faculty. All buddhas and bodhisattvas, with the Dharma-water of wisdom-brightness, will wash them away and make you pure.'

"Having said this, he universally prostrates to the buddhas of the ten directions, faces Śākyamuni Buddha and the Mahāyāna sūtras, and further says: 'The grave transgressions I now confess, the heavy sins that obstruct and defile my eye-faculty, which have made me blind and without sight: may the Buddha's great compassion bestow pity and protection. May the Bodhisattva Samantabhadra, riding the great Dharma-vessel, universally ferry all beings. May the immeasurable bodhisattvas of the ten directions serve as companions. May you all have compassion and hear my confession of the unwholesome evil karmic-obstruction dharmas of the eye-faculty.' Thus he speaks three times, prostrates with his entire body on the ground, maintains right mindfulness of the Mahāyāna, and does not forget it in his mind. This is called confessing the transgressions of the eye-faculty.

"Those who invoke the names of all buddhas, burn incense, scatter flowers, generate the Mahāyāna aspiration, hang silk banners and canopies, speak of the eye's faults, and confess transgressions: such persons in this present life will see Śākyamuni Buddha and the emanation-body immeasurable buddhas. For incalculable kalpas they will not fall into evil destinies. Through the power of the Mahāyāna and the vow of the Mahāyāna, they will constantly be companions of all dhāraṇī-bodhisattvas. Those who form such thoughts are called persons of right mindfulness. Those who form other thoughts are called persons of wrong mindfulness. This is called the initial realm of practice for the eye-faculty.

"Having purified the eye-faculty, one further recites and reads the Mahāyāna sūtras, prostrating six times daily, day and night, kneeling and confessing, saying: 'Why is it that I can only see Śākyamuni Buddha and his emanation-body buddhas, but cannot see the stūpa of the Buddha Many-Treasures? The stūpa of Many-Treasures is eternally present and indestructible. Because my eyes are turbid and defiled, I therefore cannot see it.' Having said this, he further confesses. After seven days have passed, the stūpa of the Buddha Many-Treasures springs forth from the earth. Śākyamuni Buddha then opens the stūpa door with his right hand. Within, one sees the Buddha Many-Treasures, who has entered the samādhi of universally manifesting form-bodies. From every pore stream forth rays of light as numerous as the Ganges sands, and within each ray of light are hundreds of thousands of millions of billions of transformation-buddhas.

"When this mark appears, the practitioner is filled with joy and composes verses of praise as he circumambulates the stūpa. After completing seven circuits, the Tathāgata Many-Treasures speaks with a great voice, praising: 'Dharma-child! You now truly practice the Mahāyāna. Following Samantabhadra's method of confessing the eye-faculty, because of these causes and conditions, I have come to your place to serve as your witness.' Having said this, he praises: 'Excellent, excellent! Śākyamuni Buddha can teach the great Dharma, rain the great Dharma-rain, and bring to accomplishment the turbid and defiled sentient beings.'

"At this time, having seen the stūpa of the Buddha Many-Treasures, the practitioner goes again to the Bodhisattva Samantabhadra's presence, joins his palms in reverence, and says: 'Great teacher! Please teach me to confess my faults.'

"Samantabhadra further says: 'Throughout many kalpas, the causes and conditions of your ear-faculty have been chasing after external sounds. When you hear wondrous sounds, your mind becomes confusedly attached. When you hear evil sounds, eight hundred kinds of afflictions arise as thieves to cause harm. Such evil ears receive evil retribution: you constantly hear evil sounds and generate all kinds of grasping and clinging. Because of inverted hearing, you will fall into evil destinies, into places of wrong views on the frontier where the Dharma is not heard. You should now, today, recite the Mahāyāna sūtras, and through the merit of this practice you have seen the buddhas of the ten directions and the stūpa of the Buddha Many-Treasures, who appears now as your witness. You should on your own speak of your faults and confess your transgressions.'

"At this time, the practitioner, hearing these words, again joins his palms, prostrates with his entire body on the ground, and says: 'O world-honored ones of right and universal knowledge, you now appear as my witnesses. This Vaipulya sūtra is the master of compassion. May you observe me and hear what I say. From many kalpas until now, the causes and conditions of my ear-faculty, hearing sounds, have led me to confused attachment like glue sticking to grass. Hearing various evil sounds has caused afflictions and poisons to arise. Everywhere I am confusedly attached, without a moment's rest. Because of these openings through which sound enters, my consciousness has been wearied and has fallen into the three evil destinies. Now I first awaken and know this. Before all the world-honored ones, I reveal and expose my dark evils and confess.'

"Having confessed, he sees the Buddha Many-Treasures emitting great radiance. This golden light universally illuminates the eastern direction and the worlds of the ten directions, where immeasurable buddhas, their bodies of true gold, appear. In the sky of the eastern direction, a voice proclaims: 'In this place there is a buddha named Virtue-of-Sandalwood.' He also has countless emanation-body buddhas, seated on jeweled thrones beneath jeweled trees, all in the cross-legged position. All these world-honored ones have entered the samādhi of universally manifesting form-bodies. They all praise, saying: 'Excellent, excellent, good man! You now recite the Mahāyāna sūtras. What you recite is the realm of the Buddha.'

"Having said this, the Bodhisattva Samantabhadra further teaches the method of confession: 'Throughout many kalpas in past lives, because of attachment to fragrance, you have discriminated among the various consciousnesses. Everywhere you have been greedily attached, falling into birth and death. You should now contemplate the cause of the Mahāyāna. The cause of the Mahāyāna is the true reality of all dharmas.' Having heard this, he prostrates with his entire body on the ground and further confesses.

"Having confessed, he should say these words: 'Homage to Śākyamuni Buddha! Homage to the stūpa of the Buddha Many-Treasures! Homage to the emanation-body buddhas of Śākyamuni Buddha throughout the ten directions!' Having said this, he universally prostrates to the buddhas of the ten directions: 'Homage to the Buddha Virtue-of-Sandalwood in the east and his emanation-body buddhas.' As his eyes can see, he prostrates one by one in his mind, making offerings of incense and flowers. When the offerings are completed, he kneels with palms joined and praises all the buddhas with various verses. Having praised them, he describes the ten evil deeds and confesses all transgressions. Having confessed, he says: 'In former lives, through immeasurable kalpas, because of attachment to fragrance, taste, and touch, I have committed many evils. Because of these causes and conditions, for immeasurable lifetimes I have constantly undergone hell, hungry ghost, and animal rebirth, frontier regions, wrong views, and all manner of unwholesome bodies. These evil karmic retributions I now reveal and expose. I take refuge in the buddhas, the true Dharma-kings, and confess my transgressions.'

"Having confessed, with body and mind unwavering, he further recites and reads the Mahāyāna sūtras. Through the power of the Mahāyāna, a voice in the sky announces: 'Dharma-child! You should now, toward the buddhas of the ten directions, praise and proclaim the Mahāyāna. Before all the buddhas, speak of your own faults. All the buddha tathāgatas are your compassionate fathers. You should on your own speak of the unwholesome evil deeds committed by your tongue-faculty. This tongue-faculty sets harmful karmic marks in motion: false speech, frivolous speech, harsh speech, divisive speech, slander, and lying, praising wrong views, speaking useless words. Such various mixed evil deeds: creating discord, disruption, and the teaching of what is not Dharma as if it were Dharma, such diverse transgressions. Now confess them all before the world-heroes.'

"Having said this, he prostrates with his entire body on the ground, universally prostrating to the buddhas of the ten directions. With palms joined, he kneels and says: 'The faults of this tongue are immeasurable and boundless. All the thorns of evil karma spring from the tongue-faculty. It is from this tongue that the wheel of true Dharma is cut off; it is from this tongue that the seeds of merit are destroyed. In what is not meaningful, it argues at great length. It praises wrong views like adding fuel to fire, like a blazing fire that harms sentient beings, like one who drinks poison and dies without a wound. Such karmic retribution, evil and unwholesome, will send one to evil destinies for a hundred kalpas, a thousand kalpas. Because of false speech one falls into the great hells. I now take refuge in the buddhas of the southern direction and reveal and expose my dark evils.'

"When he forms these thoughts, a voice in the sky says: 'In the southern direction there is a buddha named Virtue-of-Sandalwood. That buddha also has immeasurable emanation-body buddhas, all of whom teach the Mahāyāna to remove evil.'

"'All such transgressions I now reveal and expose before the immeasurable buddhas of the ten directions, the greatly compassionate world-honored ones, and sincerely confess.' Having said this, he prostrates with his entire body on the ground, and again prostrates to all the buddhas. At this time, all the buddhas further emit radiance to illuminate the practitioner's body, causing his body and mind to be naturally filled with joy, generating great compassion with universal thoughts for all.

"At that time, all the buddhas broadly teach the practitioner the Dharma of great compassion, joy, and equanimity. They also teach loving words and the cultivation of the six harmonies. At that time, the practitioner, hearing this instruction, is filled with great joy and further recites and practices without ever ceasing.

"From the sky again comes a subtle and wondrous voice, saying: 'You should now confess with body and mind. The body's transgressions are killing, stealing, and sexual misconduct. The mind's transgressions are thinking of all that is unwholesome and committing the ten evil deeds and the five heinous acts. Like a monkey, like birdlime, it grasps and clings everywhere, extending to all six sense-faculties. The karma of these six faculties, with their branches, flowers, and leaves, fills the three realms and the twenty-five states of existence, every place of birth. It also augments ignorance, old age, and death, the twelve painful matters. The eight deviations and the eight adversities: there is nowhere one does not pass through. You should now confess such evil and unwholesome karma.'

"At that time, the practitioner, hearing these words, asks the voice in the sky: 'Where should I now practice the method of confession?'

"Then the voice in the sky speaks these words: 'Śākyamuni's name is Vairocana, "Pervading All Places." This buddha's dwelling place is called the Land of Eternally Tranquil Light, the place established by the perfection of permanence, the place established by the perfection of self, the place where the perfection of purity extinguishes the marks of existence, and the place where the perfection of bliss does not abide in the marks of body and mind. Not seeing the marks of existence and non-existence of all dharmas, like tranquil liberation: thus, up to the Prajñāpāramitā, this is the dharma of permanence of form. Thus should one contemplate the buddhas of the ten directions.'

"At that time, the buddhas of the ten directions each extend their right hands to touch the practitioner's head, saying: 'Excellent, excellent, good man! Because you recite the Mahāyāna sūtras, the buddhas of the ten directions teach the method of confession. What bodhisattvas practice does not sever afflictions and does not abide in the ocean of the afflictions. Contemplate the mind: there is no mind. From inverted thoughts it arises. Such a thought-mind arises from delusive thinking. Like wind in space, without any place of reliance. Such is the nature of dharmas: neither arising nor ceasing. What is transgression? What is merit? The mind itself is empty, and transgressions and merits have no master. All dharmas are thus: without abiding, without destruction. Confessing in this way, contemplating the mind as no-mind, dharmas not abiding in dharmas, all dharmas are liberated, the truth of cessation is tranquil and still. Such contemplation is called the great confession, the adorned confession, the confession without the mark of transgression, the confession that destroys mind-consciousness. Those who practice this confession have body and mind purified, not abiding in dharmas, like flowing water, in each moment of thought able to see the Bodhisattva Samantabhadra and the buddhas of the ten directions.'

"At that time, the world-honored ones, with the radiance of great compassion, teach the practitioner the formless Dharma. The practitioner, hearing this teaching of the supreme meaning of emptiness, is not frightened. At that moment he enters the correct position of a bodhisattva."

The Buddha addressed Ānanda: "Such a practitioner is called one who confesses. This confession is the method of confession practiced by all buddhas of the ten directions and all great bodhisattvas."

The Buddha addressed Ānanda: "After the Buddha's nirvāṇa, the Buddha's disciples who confess evil and unwholesome karma should simply recite the Mahāyāna sūtras. These Vaipulya sūtras are the eye of all buddhas. Because of them, the buddhas fully possess the five eyes. The three kinds of Buddha-body are born from the Vaipulya. This is the great Dharma-seal that seals the ocean of nirvāṇa. From this ocean can be born the three kinds of pure Buddha-body. These three kinds of body are the field of merit for humans and devas, the foremost among those worthy of offerings. Those who recite and read the great Vaipulya canon, one should know, possess the merits of the Buddha. All evil is forever extinguished; they are born from the Buddha's wisdom."

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke in verse:

> "If there are eyes with evil roots, karma that obstructs and makes impure,
> Simply recite the Mahāyāna, contemplate the supreme meaning:
> This is called confessing the eyes, exhausting all unwholesome karma.
> The ear-faculty hears disordered sounds, destroying the meaning of harmony;
> From this arises wild disturbance, like a deluded monkey.
> Simply recite the Mahāyāna, contemplate dharmas as empty and signless:
> Forever exhaust all evil; celestial ears hear throughout the ten directions.
> The nose-faculty is attached to various fragrances; following the taint, all touch arises;
> Such a wildly deluded nose, following the taint, gives rise to all dust.
> If one recites the Mahāyāna sūtras, contemplating dharmas as the true reality,
> One forever leaves all evil karma; in future lives it will not arise again.
> The tongue-faculty generates five kinds of evil-mouth unwholesome karma.
> If one wishes to tame oneself, one should diligently cultivate the compassionate mind,
> Contemplating the Dharma's true and tranquil meaning, without any discriminating mark.
> The mind-faculty is like a monkey, without even a moment of rest.
> If one wishes to subdue it, one should diligently recite the Mahāyāna,
> Keeping in mind the Buddha's great awakened body, accomplished by the powers and fearlessnesses.
> The body is the master of the mechanism, turning like dust following the wind,
> With the six thieves sporting within, free and unobstructed.
> If one wishes to extinguish this evil, to forever leave all dust and toil,
> To constantly dwell in the city of nirvāṇa, with a mind at ease and tranquil,
> One should recite the Mahāyāna sūtras, keeping in mind the mother of all bodhisattvas.
> Immeasurable supreme skillful means are obtained from contemplating true reality.
> Such six dharmas are called the six sense-faculties.
> The entire ocean of karmic obstructions is born from delusive thinking.
> If one wishes to confess, one should sit upright and contemplate true reality.
> All transgressions are like frost and dew; the sun of wisdom can melt them away.
> Therefore, with a sincere mind, confess the six sense-faculties."

Having spoken this verse, the Buddha addressed Ānanda: "You should now uphold this 'Method of Confessing the Six Faculties and Contemplating the Bodhisattva Samantabhadra' and broadly explain it for the devas and people of the ten directions. After the Buddha's nirvāṇa, the Buddha's disciples who receive, uphold, read, recite, and explain the Vaipulya sūtras should, in a quiet place, whether in a charnel ground, in a forest beneath trees, or in a secluded dwelling, recite the Vaipulya, contemplate the Mahāyāna meaning. Through the power of mindfulness, one will see my body, the stūpa of the Buddha Many-Treasures, the emanation-body buddhas of the ten directions without limit, the Bodhisattva Samantabhadra, the Bodhisattva Mañjuśrī, the Bodhisattva Medicine-King, and the Bodhisattva Supreme-Medicine, who out of respect for the Dharma, hold various wondrous flowers, abiding in the sky, praising and revering those who uphold the Dharma. Merely through reciting the Mahāyāna Vaipulya sūtras, all the buddhas and bodhisattvas day and night make offerings to such Dharma-holders."

The Buddha addressed Ānanda: "Together with the bodhisattvas of this Bhadra-kalpa and the buddhas of the ten directions, by contemplating the true meaning of the Mahāyāna, I eliminated the transgressions of hundreds of millions of billions of kalpas, incalculable in number. Through this supreme and wondrous method of confession, I now in the ten directions have each become a buddha. If one wishes swiftly to attain anuttara-samyak-saṃbodhi, or wishes in this present life to see the buddhas of the ten directions and the Bodhisattva Samantabhadra, one should bathe in clean water, put on clean garments, burn various renowned incenses, and in an open and quiet place recite the Mahāyāna sūtras and contemplate the Mahāyāna meaning."

The Buddha addressed Ānanda: "If there are sentient beings who wish to contemplate the Bodhisattva Samantabhadra, they should make this contemplation. Those who make this contemplation are called persons of right contemplation. Those who make other contemplations are called persons of wrong contemplation. After the Buddha's nirvāṇa, the Buddha's disciples who follow the Buddha's words and practice confession should know that such persons practice the practice of Samantabhadra. Those who practice the practice of Samantabhadra do not see evil marks or evil karmic retribution. If there are sentient beings who, six times daily, day and night, prostrate to the buddhas of the ten directions, recite the Mahāyāna sūtras, and contemplate the supreme meaning of the most profound emptiness, in the snap of a finger they eliminate the transgressions of hundreds of millions of billions of incalculable kalpas of birth and death. Those who practice this practice are truly children of the Buddha, born from all the buddhas. The buddhas of the ten directions and all the bodhisattvas serve as their preceptors. Such persons are called those who fully possess the bodhisattva precepts. They do not require a karman ceremony; they naturally accomplish it. They should receive the offerings of all humans and devas.

"At that time, if the practitioner wishes to fully possess the bodhisattva precepts, he should join his palms in an open and quiet place, universally prostrate to the buddhas of the ten directions, confess transgressions, and speak of his own faults. Then in a quiet place, he addresses the buddhas of the ten directions, saying: 'All world-honored buddhas, always present in the world! Because of my karmic obstructions, although I believe in the Vaipulya, I cannot see the Buddha clearly. Now I take refuge in the Buddha. May Śākyamuni, of Right and Universal Knowledge, the World-Honored One, serve as my preceptor. May Mañjuśrī, who possesses great wisdom, use his wisdom to bestow upon me the pure bodhisattva-dharma. May the Bodhisattva Maitreya, the supreme sun of great compassion, in pity for me, also permit me to receive the bodhisattva-dharma. May the buddhas of the ten directions appear as my witnesses. May all the great bodhisattvas, each called by name, these supreme great beings, cover and protect sentient beings, and assist and protect us. Today I receive and uphold the Vaipulya sūtras. Even at the cost of my life, and even if I fall into hell and endure immeasurable suffering, I will never slander the true Dharma of all buddhas. Through the power of this merit and these causes and conditions, now Śākyamuni Buddha is my preceptor, Mañjuśrī is my ācārya, and the future Maitreya will bestow the Dharma upon me. May the buddhas of the ten directions serve as my witnesses. May the great-virtue bodhisattvas serve as my companions. Today, relying on the Mahāyāna sūtras' profound and wondrous meaning, I take refuge in the Buddha, take refuge in the Dharma, take refuge in the Sangha.' Thus he says three times.

"Having taken refuge in the Three Treasures, he should next make his own vow to receive the six grave dharmas. Having received the six grave dharmas, he should next diligently cultivate the unobstructed pure practice, generate the mind of broad deliverance, and receive the eight grave dharmas. Having established these vows, in an open and quiet place he burns various renowned incenses, scatters flowers in offering to all buddhas and all bodhisattvas and the Mahāyāna Vaipulya, and says: 'Today I generate the aspiration for bodhi. Through this merit, may all beings be universally ferried across.' Having said this, he again prostrates universally to all buddhas and all bodhisattvas, contemplating the Vaipulya meaning. For one day to three times seven days, whether one has left the household life or remains at home, one does not require a preceptor, does not need a teacher, does not announce a karman ceremony. Through the power of receiving, upholding, and reciting the Mahāyāna sūtras, and through the encouragement of the Bodhisattva Samantabhadra, because this is the eye of true Dharma of the buddhas of the ten directions, through the agency of this Dharma one naturally accomplishes the five aspects of the Dharma-body: precepts, concentration, wisdom, liberation, and liberation-knowledge-and-vision. All the buddha tathāgatas are born from this Dharma, and through the Mahāyāna sūtras they receive the prediction of supreme enlightenment.

"Therefore, O wise ones! If śrāvakas have transgressed and broken the three refuges, the five precepts, the eight precepts, the bhikṣu precepts, the bhikṣuṇī precepts, the śrāmaṇera precepts, the śrāmaṇerī precepts, the śikṣamāṇā precepts, or any rules of deportment, and if because of foolish, unwholesome, and deviant minds they have committed many violations of the precepts and deportment, and if they wish to eliminate these faults and return to being bhikṣus who fully possess the śramaṇa-dharma, they should diligently recite the Vaipulya sūtras, contemplate the supreme meaning of the most profound emptiness, and enable this wisdom of emptiness to accord with their minds. One should know that such persons, in each moment of thought, have all transgressions forever and completely exhausted without remainder. This is called one who fully possesses the śramaṇa-dharma and all rules of deportment, and who is worthy of receiving the offerings of all humans and devas.

"If upāsakas have violated the rules of deportment and committed unwholesome deeds, meaning proclaiming faults and evils of the Buddha-Dharma, discussing the transgressions committed by the four groups, theft, sexual misconduct, shamelessness: if they wish to confess and eliminate all such transgressions, they should diligently recite the Vaipulya sūtras and contemplate the supreme meaning.

"If kings, great ministers, brahmins, householders, elders, or officials, these persons among them, are insatiable in greed, commit the five heinous acts, slander the Vaipulya sūtras, and are complete in the ten evil deeds: the retribution for such great evil should send them to evil destinies, surpassing even a torrential downpour, and they are certain to fall into the Avīci hell. If they wish to eliminate these karmic obstructions, they should generate shame and remorse and reform their transgressions.

"What is called the method of confession for kṣatriyas and householders? The method of confession requires only that one correct one's mind, not slander the Three Treasures, not obstruct those who leave the household life, not create evil difficulties for those who practice the pure conduct. One should be mindful and cultivate the six recollections, and one should also make offerings to and support those who uphold the Mahāyāna. One need not necessarily prostrate, but should keep in mind the profound sūtra-Dharma of the supreme meaning of emptiness. Contemplating this Dharma is called the first confession for kṣatriyas and householders.

"The second confession is filial devotion to parents and reverence for teachers. This is called the second method of confession.

"The third confession is governing the country with right Dharma, not oppressing the people unjustly. This is called the third confession.

"The fourth confession is, on the six poṣadha days, commanding all within one's domain, to the extent of one's power, to practice non-killing. Cultivating this method is called the fourth confession.

"The fifth confession is merely having deep faith in cause and effect, faith in the One Reality path, and knowing that the Buddha does not perish. This is called the fifth confession."

The Buddha addressed Ānanda: "In the future age, if there are those who cultivate this method of confession, one should know that such persons wear the garment of shame and remorse, are protected and assisted by all buddhas, and will not be long before accomplishing anuttara-samyak-saṃbodhi."

When these words were spoken, ten thousand devaputras attained the pure Dharma-eye. The Bodhisattva Maitreya and all the great bodhisattvas, as well as Ānanda, hearing what the Buddha taught, were filled with joy and put it into practice.
