# 佛說無量壽大智陀羅尼
## The Dhāraṇī of Immeasurable Life and Great Wisdom Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1389

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmabhadra (法賢)

---

*[The following is a dhāraṇī rendered in Chinese phonetic transcription. The Sanskrit is reconstructed below from the Chinese phonetic equivalents.]*

namaḥ bhagavate aparimitāyurjñānasuviśiṣṭatejorājāya tathāgatāya arhate samyaksaṃbuddhāya | tathā | oṃ sarvasaṃskārapariśuddhadharmate gaganasamudgate svabhāvaviśuddhe mahānayaparivāre svāhā ||

---

*Translator's note: This brief text consists entirely of the Aparimitāyurjñāna dhāraṇī, a well-known formula associated with Amitāyus, the Buddha of Immeasurable Life. The Chinese characters serve purely as phonetic transcription of the Sanskrit. The dhāraṇī invokes Amitāyus by his epithet "the Tathāgata, the Worthy One, the Perfectly Enlightened One, whose resplendent sovereignty is renowned for immeasurable life and wisdom," followed by a consecratory formula evoking the purity of all conditioned things, the vast expanse of sky, and the purity of intrinsic nature, concluding with the dedication svāhā.*
