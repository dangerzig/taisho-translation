# 佛說法集名數經
## Sūtra of the Collected Dharma Names and Numbers, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0764

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dānapāla (施護)

---

Homage and prostration to all the Buddhas, to the wisdom of all wisdoms, to the Teacher of Devas and Humans. The boundless and innumerable dharmas taught by the Buddha are here briefly collected as a list of the names of the right Dharma. First, homage to the Three Treasures: the Buddha, the Dharma, and the Saṃgha.

What are the three vehicles? The Great Vehicle, the Pratyekabuddha Vehicle, and the Śrāvaka Vehicle.

What are the seven kinds of supreme offering? Prostration, offering, repentance, sympathetic joy, exhortation, making vows, and dedication of merit.

What are the three roots? Generating the aspiration for bodhi, the pure mind, and the emptiness of inherent nature that severs the view of self.

What are the ten pāramitās? Giving, morality, patience, vigor, dhyāna meditation, wisdom, skillful means, vows, power, and knowledge.

What are the eighteen emptinesses? Internal emptiness, external emptiness, internal-and-external emptiness, emptiness of emptiness, great emptiness, emptiness of ultimate meaning, emptiness of the conditioned, emptiness of the unconditioned, ultimate emptiness, emptiness of the beginningless, emptiness of dispersal, emptiness of all dharmas, emptiness of inherent nature, emptiness of particular characteristics, emptiness of signlessness, emptiness of non-existence, emptiness of self-nature, and emptiness of the non-existence of self-nature.

What are the four immeasurables? Immeasurable kindness, immeasurable compassion, immeasurable sympathetic joy, and immeasurable equanimity.

What are the four means of attraction? Giving, kind words, beneficial action, and working together.

What are the five supernatural powers? The divine eye, the divine ear, knowledge of others' minds, knowledge of past lives, and the power of miraculous transformation.

What are the Four Noble Truths? The truth of suffering, the truth of its origin, the truth of its cessation, and the truth of the path.

What are the five aggregates? The aggregate of form, the aggregate of feeling, the aggregate of perception, the aggregate of formations, and the aggregate of consciousness.

What are the five supramundane aggregates? The aggregate of morality, the aggregate of concentration, the aggregate of wisdom, the aggregate of liberation, and the aggregate of the knowledge and vision of liberation.

What are the twelve links of dependent arising? Ignorance, formations, consciousness, name-and-form, the six sense-entries, contact, feeling, craving, grasping, becoming, birth, and old age and death.

What are the thirty-seven factors of awakening? The four foundations of mindfulness, the four right efforts, the four bases of spiritual power, the five faculties, the five powers, the seven factors of awakening, and the noble Eightfold Path.

What are the four foundations of mindfulness? Contemplation of the body as the body-foundation of mindfulness; contemplation of feelings as the feeling-foundation of mindfulness; contemplation of the mind as the mind-foundation of mindfulness; contemplation of dharmas as the dharma-foundation of mindfulness.

What are the four right efforts? Not allowing unwholesome dharmas that have not yet arisen to arise; rightly cutting off unwholesome dharmas that have already arisen; causing wholesome dharmas that have not yet arisen to arise; causing wholesome dharmas that have already arisen to increase. Truly and without error, one gives rise to a mind of vigor and brings it to fulfillment.

What are the four bases of spiritual power? The base of spiritual power endowed with the concentration of intention and the exertion of effort; the base of spiritual power endowed with the concentration of mind and the exertion of effort; the base of spiritual power endowed with the concentration of vigor and the exertion of effort; the base of spiritual power endowed with the concentration of investigation and the exertion of effort.

What are the five faculties? The faculty of faith, the faculty of vigor, the faculty of mindfulness, the faculty of concentration, and the faculty of wisdom.

What are the five powers? The power of faith, the power of vigor, the power of mindfulness, the power of concentration, and the power of wisdom.

What are the seven factors of awakening? The awakening factor of mindfulness, the awakening factor of investigation of dharmas, the awakening factor of vigor, the awakening factor of joy, the awakening factor of tranquility, the awakening factor of concentration, and the awakening factor of equanimity.

What is the noble Eightfold Path? Right view, right thought, right speech, right action, right livelihood, right effort, right mindfulness, and right concentration. These are the thirty-seven factors of awakening.

What are the four dharma-seeds? Right benefit, right phrasing, right knowledge, and right discernment.

What are the six recollections? Recollection of the Buddha, recollection of the Dharma, recollection of the Saṃgha, recollection of morality, recollection of giving, and recollection of the devas.

What are the four dharma seals? All conditioned phenomena are impermanent; all conditioned phenomena are suffering; all dharmas are without self; nirvāṇa is quiescence.

What are the ten wholesome deeds? Not killing, not stealing, not engaging in sexual misconduct, not speaking falsely, not speaking divisively, not speaking harshly, not speaking frivolously, not being covetous, not being angry, and not holding wrong views.

What are the four root afflictions? Greed, anger, delusion, and pride.

What are the five views? The view of a self in the body, the view of extremes, wrong views, attachment to views, and attachment to rules and rituals.

What are the four outflows? The outflow of desire, the outflow of existence, the outflow of ignorance, and the outflow of views.

What are the three liberations? The liberation of emptiness, the liberation of signlessness, and the liberation of wishlessness.

What are the eight form-dharmas? Earth, water, fire, wind, scent, taste, contact, and dharmas.

What are the two formless dharmas? Space and the nature of consciousness.

What are the eight liberations of meditative attainment? Having form internally, contemplating external form: this is the first liberation. Without form internally, contemplating external form: this is the second liberation. Contemplation of purity, fully abiding: this is the third liberation. Contemplation of the sphere of boundless space: this is the fourth liberation. Contemplation of the sphere of boundless consciousness: this is the fifth liberation. Contemplation of the sphere of nothingness: this is the sixth liberation. Contemplation of the sphere of neither-perception-nor-non-perception: this is the seventh liberation. Contemplation of the cessation of perception and feeling: this is the eighth liberation.

What are the nine divisions of the teaching? Sūtras, geyas, vyākaraṇas, gāthās, udānas, nidānas, itivṛttakas, jātakas, and vaipulyas.

What are the twelve dhūta practices? Always begging for food, begging in order of houses, eating at one sitting, finishing before rising, wearing only three robes, wearing woolen garments, wearing robes of discarded rags, sitting on grass and not lying down, dwelling in open places, dwelling beneath trees, dwelling among tombs, and dwelling in empty and secluded places.

What are the ten grounds? The Ground of Joy, the Ground of Freedom from Defilement, the Ground of Radiance, the Ground of Blazing Wisdom, the Ground of Difficult to Conquer, the Ground of Manifest Presence, the Ground of Far-Reaching, the Ground of Immovability, the Ground of Good Wisdom, and the Ground of the Dharma Cloud.

What are the ten subjugations of the bodhisattva? Subjugation of life, subjugation of mind, subjugation of enjoyment, subjugation of dharmas, subjugation of birth, subjugation of supernatural power, subjugation of liberation, subjugation of vows, subjugation of karma, and subjugation of knowledge.

What are the ten powers of the bodhisattva? The power of liberation, the power of removing suffering, the power of contemplation, the power of patience, the power of knowledge, the power of severance, the power of learning, the power of vows, the power of fulfillment, and the power of love.

What are the ten powers of the Tathāgata? Knowledge of what is possible and impossible; knowledge of the ripening of one's own karma; knowledge of the dispositions of sentient beings; knowledge of the superiority or inferiority of faculties; knowledge of the various realms; knowledge of the various inclinations; knowledge of the paths leading to all destinations; knowledge of dhyānas, liberations, samādhis, and attainments; knowledge of the recollection of former abodes; and knowledge of the exhaustion of the outflows.

What are the four knowledges? Knowledge of all dharma-seeds; knowledge of all Dharma-teachings; knowledge of the right path to nirvāṇa; and knowledge of the severance through the wisdom of exhaustion of outflows.

What are the five jealousies? Jealousy of the Dharma, jealousy of gain, jealousy of dwelling, jealousy of goodness, and jealousy of fame.

What are the eighteen uncommon dharmas? No error of body, no error of speech, no error of mind, no unsettled mind, no mind of differing perception, no unknowing equanimity, no diminishment of aspiration, no diminishment of mindfulness, no diminishment of vigor, no diminishment of wisdom, no diminishment of liberation, no diminishment of the knowledge and vision of liberation, bodily action following wisdom, verbal action following wisdom, mental action following wisdom, unobstructed knowledge of the past, unobstructed knowledge of the future, and unobstructed knowledge of the present.

What are the thirty-two marks? The mark of level and full soles; the mark of thousand-spoked wheel patterns on the soles; the mark of soft and supple hands and feet; the mark of golden webbing between the fingers and toes; the mark of fingers and toes that are slender, long, and rounded; the mark of heels that are broad and long in proportion to the instep; the mark of an instep that is high, broad, soft, and full; the mark of calves that are slender and rounded like a deer king's; the mark of arms that are straight and rounded, reaching past the knees when standing; the mark of the private parts being concealed like an elephant king's; the mark of each hair-pore producing a single hair, curling to the right in dark blue; the mark of body-hair growing upward, dark blue and soft; the mark of skin that is thin, smooth, and on which dust and water do not remain; the mark of a body of true golden color, lustrous and adorned; the mark of fullness at the seven places of hands, feet, palms, neck, and both shoulders; the mark of both shoulders and the crown of the head being round and full; the mark of both armpits being fully fleshed; the mark of dignified bearing; the mark of a body that is broad and tall with form and proportions in harmony; the mark of the upper body being like a lion king's; the mark of the body constantly radiating light, one fathom in each direction; the mark of teeth that are white as snow, forty in number, even and close-set; the mark of four canine teeth that are sharp, bright, and gleaming white; the mark of always obtaining the supreme taste among all tastes; the mark of a tongue that is broad and thin, able to cover the face and reach the hairline; the mark of a Brahmā-voice, resonant and elegant, heard equally by all; the mark of eyelashes that are regular and even like those of a bull king; the mark of pupils adorned above by a crimson ring; the mark of a face like a full moon with eyebrows like the new moon; the mark of a white hair-tuft between the brows, curling to the right and soft; and the mark of an uṣṇīṣa on the crown like a heavenly parasol.

What are the eighty minor marks? The marks of nails that are narrow, long, lustrous, thin, and clean; fingers and toes that are slender, rounded, straight, with joints not visible; hands and feet that are evenly matched with spaces between the fingers filled; hands and feet that are soft at will; sinews and veins that are firm and deeply hidden; both ankles not visible; walking with a straight stride like a dragon or elephant king; walking evenly and solemnly like a lion king; walking with stability like a bull king; advancing and pausing gracefully like a goose king; whenever turning, the whole body turns together; limbs and joints rounded, wondrously and well-proportioned; joints without gaps like a coiled dragon; knees that are firm and finely adorned; private parts are marked, rounded, and pure; skin that is soft, smooth, bright, and free from impurity; bearing that is respectful and dignified, without any fearfulness; limbs that are closely-set and well-arranged; limbs that are steady without fidgeting; physical form that is bright and pure, perfectly adorned all around; body-light constantly shining all around; belly that is square, upright, soft, and not prominent; navel that is deep, round, and wondrous; navel that is thick and fine; no protrusion; skin that is pure without any stain; hands and feet that are full; hand-lines that are unbroken; lips like a bimba fruit; face that is well-proportioned and dignified; tongue that is broad and thin; Brahmā-voice that is deep and far-reaching; Brahmā-voice that is beautiful and fully formed; nose that is high, straight, and well-shaped; teeth that are square and even; canines that are round and white; eyes that are clear with blue and white clearly distinguished; eyes like petals of a blue lotus; eyelashes that are dense and not pale; eyebrows that are long and soft; eyebrows the color of dark lapis lazuli; eyebrows that are raised, prominent, and lustrous; ears that are thick and proportionate with lobes that are round and full; both ears level and even; bearing that is broad and inspiring respect and love; forehead that is broad, level, and even; upper body that is without comparison; head-hair that is long, dense, and dark blue; head-hair that is fragrant and clean; head-hair without tangles; head-hair that does not fall out; head-hair that is smooth with dust not adhering; body that is full and solid like Nārāyaṇa; body that is broad, great, upright, and straight; orifices that are clean and pure; limbs without equal; an appearance that one never tires of beholding; face like a full moon; always facing others and not turning away; facial expression that is joyful; limbs without impurity; face that always has a fragrance; hair-pores that always have a fragrance; head like a mandārava flower; body-hair that is lustrous like the neck-feathers of a peacock; Brahmā-voice measured and in accord with truth; crown-bone that no one can see; hands, feet, and fingers tinged like red copper; when walking, four finger-widths above the ground, able to leave imprints; self-possessed and not requiring others' protection; those with evil minds seeing him find joy, the fearful find peace; voice that is harmonious and pleasing, according with all beings' wishes; speech conforming to the language and intent of each kind of being; teaching the Dharma with one voice so each kind understands according to type; teaching the Dharma in order, always with the right conditions; observing all assemblies without partiality or aversion; first observing and then acting, deportment fully complete; marks and signs that cannot be seen to their end; crown-bone that is firm and solid; countenance that does not age; and auspicious whorls on hands, feet, and chest.

What are the seven treasures of a wheel-turning king? The golden wheel treasure, the elephant treasure, the horse treasure, the maṇi-jewel treasure, the queen treasure, the general treasure, and the treasurer treasure.

What are the eight worldly dharmas? Gain, loss, censure, slander, praise, honor, suffering, and happiness.

What are the three times? Past, future, and present.

What are the four kinds of kalpas? The great kalpa, the muṇḍa-kalpa, the saṃvarta-kalpa, and the bhadra-kalpa.

What are the four ages? The holy age, the right age, the semblance age, and the final age.

What are the two dharmas? The dharma of arising and the dharma of ceasing.

What are the four kinds of birth? Birth from a womb, birth from an egg, birth from moisture, and spontaneous birth.

What are the five defilements? The defilement of the kalpa, the defilement of views, the defilement of afflictions, the defilement of sentient beings, and the defilement of the lifespan.

What are the four māras? The māra of afflictions, the heavenly māra, the māra of the aggregates, and the māra of death.

What are the six destinies? The destiny of devas, the destiny of humans, the destiny of asuras, the destiny of animals, the destiny of hungry ghosts, and the destiny of the hells.

What are the eight cold hells? The Arbuda hell, the Nirarbuda hell, the Aṭaṭa hell, the Hahava hell, the Aṭaṭa hell, the Blue Lotus hell, the Red Lotus hell, and the Great Red Lotus hell.

What are the eight hot hells? The Sañjīva hell, the Kālasūtra hell, the Saṃghāta hell, the Raurava hell, the Mahāraurava hell, the Tapana hell, the Pratāpana hell, and the Avīci hell.

What are the four great continents? The southern Jambudvīpa, the western Godānīya, the northern Uttarakuru, and the eastern Pūrvavideha. Each has five hundred small islands as attendants. There are also two iron enclosing mountains: the lesser iron enclosing mountain and the great iron enclosing mountain.

What are the seven golden mountains? Mount Yugaṃdhara, Mount Īṣādhara, Mount Khadiraka, Mount Sudarśana, Mount Aśvakarṇa, Mount Vinataka, and Mount Nimindhara.

What are the seven seas? The salt-water sea, the milk sea, the curd sea, the butter sea, the honey-water sea, the auspicious-grass sea, and the wine sea.

What are the six heavens of the desire realm? The heaven of the Four Great Kings, the Trāyastriṃśa heaven, the Yāma heaven, the Tuṣita heaven, the Nirmāṇarati heaven, and the Paranirmitavaśavartin heaven.

What are the seventeen heavens of the form realm? The Brahmakāyika heaven, the Brahmapurohita heaven, the Mahābrahmā heaven, the Parīttābha heaven, the Apramāṇābha heaven, the Ābhāsvara heaven, the Parīttaśubha heaven, the Śubhakṛtsna heaven, the Apramāṇaśubha heaven, the Anabhraka heaven, the Puṇyaprasava heaven, the Bṛhatphala heaven, the Avṛha heaven, the Atapa heaven, the Sudarśana heaven, the Sudṛśa heaven, and the Akaniṣṭha heaven.
