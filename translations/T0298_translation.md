# 大方廣普賢所說經
## Sūtra Spoken by Samantabhadra of the Great Vaipulya

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0298

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Śikṣānanda (實叉難陀)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the place sustained by the Tathāgata's supernatural power, together with bodhisattva-mahāsattvas as numerous as the atoms in ten ineffable, ineffable hundred-thousand koṭi-nayuta buddha-lands, surrounding him in front and behind. He was teaching them the Dharma, and all had perfected the practice of Samantabhadra. The Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Samantabhadra was the foremost among them.

At that time, within the assembly there were ten bodhisattva-mahāsattvas, each accompanied by retinues of bodhisattvas as numerous as the atoms in ten ineffable, ineffable hundred-thousand koṭi-nayuta buddha-lands. They suddenly appeared from the ten directions, all seated upon unobstructed, adorned lion thrones. Their names were: the Bodhisattva Universal Light Treasury, the Bodhisattva Profound Treasury, the Bodhisattva Majestic Radiance Treasury, the Bodhisattva Cloud-Sound Treasury, the Bodhisattva Vajra Treasury, the Bodhisattva Universal Sound Unmoving Majestic Radiance Treasury, the Bodhisattva Universally Renowned Majestic Radiance Treasury, the Bodhisattva Mountain King Unmoving Majestic Radiance Treasury, the Bodhisattva Universally Manifesting All Forms Majestic Radiance Treasury, and the Bodhisattva Ten Powers Pure Majestic Radiance Treasury.

When those bodhisattvas appeared, within this assembly, with the sole exception of Samantabhadra, all the other bodhisattva-multitudes could not help but tremble, and all their majestic radiance disappeared. Each of the bodhisattvas rained down ten ineffable, ineffable hundred-thousand koṭi-nayuta incense-clouds, paste-incense-clouds, garland-clouds, robe-clouds, jeweled canopy-and-banner-clouds, pure world-clouds, jeweled pavilion-clouds, bodhisattva-assembly and enlightenment-site-clouds, great light-net universally illuminating-clouds, enlightenment-site adornment-clouds, and Tathāgata form and kāṣāya-clouds. Each raised such inconceivable offering-clouds, filling the dharma-realm, as offerings to the Tathāgata.

The thrones upon which those bodhisattvas sat were adorned with many jewels, subtle and pure. Within each and every one of those adornments were universally manifested all immeasurable worlds, immeasurable sentient beings, immeasurable Buddhas, and immeasurable bodhisattvas. There also appeared the ineffable, ineffable immeasurable worlds of the past and future, and their Buddhas, seated at the sites of enlightenment, transforming sentient beings, turning the wondrous Dharma-wheel, with assemblies of bodhisattvas making offerings to the Tathāgatas, cultivating and purifying all the pāramitā practices, ceaselessly and without interruption.

At that time, the entire assembly thought: "From what worlds and from which Buddhas have these bodhisattvas come?" Together they asked the Bodhisattva Samantabhadra.

Then the Bodhisattva Samantabhadra universally addressed all the bodhisattva-multitudes: "Sons of the Buddha! Each of you should investigate for yourselves where they have come from."

Then the Bodhisattva Unobstructed Eye entered the Universally Swift Samādhi, the Reaching Everywhere Samādhi, the Brightly Illuminating the Dharma-Realm Samādhi, the Possessing All Supernatural Powers Samādhi, the Comprehending All Realms Samādhi, the Manifesting the Bodies of All Sentient Beings Supernatural Power Samādhi, and the Knowing All Buddha-Lands Samādhi. He entered ten asaṃkhyeya hundred-thousand koṭi-nayuta bodhisattva samādhis such as these, and by the power of those samādhis, he saw his own body traveling to all worlds of the ten directions, even into every atom, yet he could not see the lands from which those bodhisattvas had come, nor the place where they had cultivated the brahma-conduct under their Tathāgata. All the other bodhisattva-multitudes likewise each entered their own bodhisattva samādhis and were equally unable to see. All arose from concentration and addressed the Bodhisattva Samantabhadra: "We each entered ten asaṃkhyeya hundred-thousand koṭi-nayuta bodhisattva samādhis, yet we were utterly unable to see the lands from which those bodhisattvas came."

The Bodhisattva Samantabhadra further told them: "The buddha-lands from which they came are profoundly vast and extremely difficult to perceive. You should now investigate further together."

Then the bodhisattvas each further entered ten buddha-land-atom-number bodhisattva samādhis to search, yet still could not see. Each reported this matter again to Samantabhadra.

At that time, the Bodhisattva Samantabhadra rose from his seat, ascended into the sky, circumambulated the World-Honored One innumerable times to the right, and then from the sky universally surveyed the assembly and spoke thus: "Sons of the Buddha! Contemplate the Buddha's body: unobstructed in adornment, equal throughout the three times, entering universally into all the lands of the dharma-realm. All the worlds of the ten directions, all Tathāgatas, all bodhisattvas, all sentient beings, and all destinies are without exception reflected and manifested within the Tathāgata's body, and according to the wishes of sentient beings, all are caused to awaken. You should abide in the eye of universal vision, the pure wisdom-eye pervading the reaches of space, and the vast wisdom-eye that comprehends all realms. You should also universally request all the Buddhas of the ten directions for their protective mindfulness. All should be single-minded: leave behind all places, all supports, all attachments, and all existences, and contemplate the Tathāgata's body. You should enter the subtle realm of the ten powers, and within one realm comprehend all inexhaustible realms, and contemplate the Tathāgata's body."

Then the bodhisattvas respectfully followed his instruction. All turned toward the Tathāgata, bowed their heads to the ground, and with single-minded attention gazed upward. Suddenly they saw within the wheel-marks on the soles of the World-Honored Vairocana's feet a world named Dharma-Realm Wheel. Its Buddha was named Dharma-Realm Adornment King, abiding in the world and teaching the Dharma. The Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Universal Light Treasury, together with bodhisattva-mahāsattvas as numerous as the atoms in ten ineffable, ineffable hundred-thousand koṭi-nayuta buddha-lands, had come from that buddha-land to sit in this assembly.

Within the ankles was a world named Unobstructed Treasury, with a Buddha named Unobstructed Pure Light, abiding and teaching the Dharma. The Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Profound Treasury and retinue had come from that buddha-land.

Within the knees was a world named True Gold Treasury, with a Buddha named Gold Treasury King, abiding and teaching the Dharma. The Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Majestic Radiance Treasury and retinue had come from that buddha-land.

Within the thighs was a world named All-Jewel Adornment Treasury, with a Buddha named Multitudinous Wondrous Light, abiding and teaching the Dharma. The Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Cloud-Sound Treasury and retinue had come from that buddha-land.

Within the navel was a world named Vairocana Treasury, with a Buddha named Vairocana Majestic Adornment King, abiding and teaching the Dharma. The Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Vajra Treasury and retinue had come from that buddha-land.

Within the heart was a world named Victorious Light Treasury, with a Buddha named Wondrous Mark Adornment Treasury, abiding and teaching the Dharma. The Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Universal Sound Unmoving Majestic Radiance Treasury and retinue had come from that buddha-land.

Within the two shoulders was a world named Gold Color, with a Buddha named Gold Color King, abiding and teaching the Dharma. The Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Universally Renowned Majestic Radiance Treasury and retinue had come from that buddha-land.

Within the mouth was a world named Wondrous Jewel Adornment, with a Buddha named Immeasurable Light Adornment King, abiding and teaching the Dharma. The Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Mountain King Unmoving Majestic Radiance Treasury and retinue had come from that buddha-land.

Within the space between the brows was a world named Dharma-Realm Inexhaustible Treasury, with a Buddha named Inexhaustible Wisdom of the Three Times, abiding and teaching the Dharma. The Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Universally Manifesting All Forms Majestic Radiance Treasury and retinue had come from that buddha-land.

Within the crown of the head was a world named Encompassing and Sustaining Without Dispersal, with a Buddha named Jewel Flower Accumulation, abiding and teaching the Dharma. The Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Ten Powers Pure Majestic Radiance Treasury and retinue had come from that buddha-land.

When the bodhisattvas had seen these inexhaustible worlds, the Tathāgata's sites of enlightenment, the bodhisattva assemblies, and the Buddha's supernatural transformations, each one attained the Dharma-Realm Treasury Samādhi and ten other great samādhis as numerous as the atoms in ten buddha-lands; the All Dharma-Ground Dhāraṇī and ten other dhāraṇīs as numerous as the atoms in ten buddha-lands; the Stainless Treasury Prajñāpāramitā and ten other pāramitās as numerous as the atoms in ten buddha-lands; and the Power Lightning Light and ten other all-knowledge lightning lights as numerous as the atoms in ten buddha-lands.

Then the Bodhisattva Samantabhadra again told the great assembly: "Sons of the Buddha! This Dharma is only for those who practice Samantabhadra's conduct and are embraced by good teachers; only they can hear and see it. Therefore, regarding this Dharma-gate, generate the vajra-mind, with supreme resolve, and guard, uphold, read, and recite it. Do not let it be forgotten or lost."

When this Dharma was taught, all those bodhisattva-mahāsattvas joyfully accepted it with faith.
