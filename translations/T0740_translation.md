# 佛說頞多和多耆經
## Sūtra of the Arthavargīya, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0740

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was in the land of Vaiśālī, at a place with many trees, together with an assembly of bhikṣus, five hundred bhikṣus in all. On the fifteenth day of the month, at the full moon, at midnight when all was silent and still, the bhikṣus were questioning one another. At that time the deva Candana came, bearing a heavenly form with majestic spiritual radiance. He went straight forward to the Buddha. Having come before the Buddha, he removed all the jewels from his body and set them to one side, wearing only a single garment. He touched his forehead to the Buddha's feet. When his salutation was complete, he addressed the Buddha: "The disciples of all the Buddhas of the past, the Perfectly Awakened Ones, possessed a sūtra called the Arthavargīya. The Buddha's present disciples also recite this Arthavargīya Sūtra. I pray the Buddha will teach this sūtra for us, so that the disciples may uphold it." The Buddha remained silent and did not respond. Candana bowed his head and withdrew to sit.

The Buddha then taught the assembly of bhikṣus: "I shall teach you the Arthavargīya Sūtra. All of you listen and keep it in your hearts; be mindful of it and do not forget."

The bhikṣus said: "We pray the Buddha will teach. We shall all receive the sūtra and precepts."

The Buddha said: "There are eight aspects to the practice of giving. What are the eight? A foolish person gives, yet merely gives, without knowing what is truly good or where the benefit lies. Having given, the fool does not comprehend that nothing in the world is permanent; the fool treats it as permanent. The suffering of the world is without limit; the fool treats it as happiness. All that is in the world, the fool says, 'This is mine and can be kept forever.' All worldly people are upside-down: the body is impure, foul, and full of discharges, yet the fool regards it as fine. The fool does not know that doing good brings good and doing evil brings evil. The fool gives to others without knowing the depth or shallowness of the recipient's virtue. To give with a wholesome mind to one who has attained the path brings merit beyond measure."

The Buddha told the bhikṣus: "How can one know that a fool does not understand giving? There are ten causes and conditions. What are the ten? A fool gives without knowing reverence, thinking that what one gives oneself brings good. A fool does not give wholeheartedly to others. A fool, having given to others, shows no respect at the time of giving. The fool does not give with one's own hand, but instructs another to give. Having given, the fool does not wish for the merit of it. Not immediately receiving the merit, the fool forgets it. A fool gives to a Buddha, a pratyekabuddha, or an arhat without being able to know how great the merit is. A fool gives without believing in the Buddha, the pratyekabuddha, the arhat, the anāgāmin, the sakṛdāgāmin, or the srotāpanna, regarding it all as a waste and a loss, with no future consequence. A fool gives only wishing to gain a reputation, wanting others to praise them. This is the giving of fools."

When the Buddha had spoken this sūtra, Candana and all the bhikṣus rejoiced and came forward to bow to the Buddha.
