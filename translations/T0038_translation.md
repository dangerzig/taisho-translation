# 輪王七寶經
## The Sūtra on the Seven Treasures of the Wheel-Turning King

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0038

Translated by Dānapāla (Song dynasty)

---

## Fascicle 1

### The Sūtra on the Seven Treasures of the Wheel-Turning King, Spoken by the Buddha

Thus have I heard.

At one time, the Buddha was dwelling in the land of Śrāvastī, together with a great assembly of bhikṣus. At that time the Buddha addressed the bhikṣus, saying: "You should know that when a kṣatriya great anointed king has received the royal consecration and attained the position of cakravartin, majestic and sovereign, revered by all people, and appears in the world, at the time of that king's appearance seven treasures manifest. What are the seven? They are: the wheel-treasure, the elephant-treasure, the horse-treasure, the treasury-minister treasure, the army-minister treasure, the maṇi-treasure, and the woman-treasure. These seven treasures appear together with the king.

"What is called the wheel-treasure? It is a supremely marvelous golden wheel with a thousand spokes, perfect in all its marks and possessed of great power. This golden wheel-treasure descends from the sky and comes to rest at the gate of the royal palace. At that time the kṣatriya great anointed cakravartin, upon seeing the wheel-treasure appear, is filled with great joy and immediately says to his attending ministers: 'Quickly array the fourfold army; we shall go forth on a royal progress.' The attending ministers, having received the king's command, array the fourfold army; and when the array is complete, they go to the king's presence and report: 'The fourfold army is arrayed; now is the right time for the king to go forth on a royal progress.' Then the kṣatriya great anointed cakravartin rises from his seat, arranges his garments, and departs through the palace gate. The thousand-spoked wheel proceeds before the king, turning to the king's right in due order. At that time the king travels throughout the four seas and returns to the royal palace in a brief moment, by virtue of the superior power of the wheel-treasure. Bhikṣus, this is called the first treasure to appear when the kṣatriya great anointed cakravartin appears: the wheel-treasure.

"Furthermore, bhikṣus, when the kṣatriya great anointed cakravartin appears, there also appears an elephant-treasure. Its form is supremely marvelous, pure white and unmixed, like a great nāga, perfect and complete in its seven aspects, dwelling in fullness. From the north it comes riding through the sky and takes up position at the gate of the royal palace. When the court ministers witness this, they proceed to the king's presence and report the matter in full to the king. The great anointed cakravartin, upon seeing the elephant-treasure appear, is filled with great joy and thinks to himself: 'Truly excellent and supremely outstanding; I shall have use for it and must certainly take possession of it.' Then the king says to his ministers: 'The elephant-treasure has appeared; you should all diligently guard and protect it for my use.' The attending ministers, having received the king's command, for a long time diligently guard and protect it, skillfully training and conditioning it without the slightest loss or damage, keeping it in readiness for the king's use."

The Buddha said to the bhikṣus: "In former times there was a kṣatriya great anointed cakravartin who appeared in the world, and at that time there also appeared an elephant-treasure. At dawn the king mounted that elephant-treasure, traveled throughout the four seas, and returned immediately to his palace. You should know that the elephant-treasure possesses such superior and wondrous power. Bhikṣus, this is called the second treasure to appear when the great anointed cakravartin appears: the elephant-treasure.

"Furthermore, bhikṣus, when the great anointed cakravartin appears, there also appears a horse-treasure, four in number, each perfect in all its parts and each possessing its own supremely beautiful color and form: blue, yellow, red, and white. Their necks are beautifully formed like the mora; their gait is swift and their disposition gentle and tractable. These four horse-treasures appear at the palace gate. When the court ministers see these horse-treasures, so rare and wondrously fine, they proceed to the king's presence. The great anointed cakravartin, upon seeing these four horse-treasures, is filled with great joy and thinks to himself: 'The horse-treasures have appeared, truly excellent; I shall certainly have use for them as I wish.' The great anointed cakravartin then declares: 'All you ministers, for a long time diligently guard and protect them, skillfully training and conditioning them; when I have use for them I shall certainly take them.' The court ministers, having received the king's command, guard them for a long time in readiness for the king's use. Bhikṣus, in former times there was a great anointed cakravartin who appeared in the world, and at that time there also appeared a horse-treasure. At dawn the king mounted that horse-treasure, traveled throughout the four seas, and returned immediately to his palace. You should know that the horse-treasure possesses such superior power. Bhikṣus, this is called the third treasure to appear when the great anointed cakravartin appears: the horse-treasure.

"Furthermore, bhikṣus, when the cakravartin appears, there also appears a treasury-minister treasure. At that time there is a great treasury, solid and firm, fully equipped with enormous wealth and riches, which that minister oversees. The treasury-minister goes to the king's presence and speaks thus: 'There is a great treasury fully stocked with all manner of supremely marvelous precious things, gold and every sort of treasure. For whatever use the king requires, I shall provide the king with all that he desires, without the slightest deficiency.' The cakravartin, upon seeing the treasury-minister treasure appear, is filled with great joy, and hearing his words that gold and all precious things are fully stocked, obtains great satisfaction. The king then says to the treasury-minister treasure: 'You now possess such form, spiritual power, and majestic virtue, capable of overseeing the vast hidden treasures within the earth, fully stocked with gold and all precious things. To contemplate this matter is truly extraordinary and supremely excellent. Keep careful stewardship; when I have desires you shall supply them, and what lies beyond my desires you shall likewise keep in good stewardship.' Bhikṣus, you should know that these hidden treasures within the earth are unseen by ordinary people but are seen by non-human beings. When the cakravartin appears, the treasury-minister presents himself of his own accord, guarding and providing all supplies for the king. This is called the fourth treasure to appear when the cakravartin appears: the treasury-minister treasure.

"Furthermore, bhikṣus, when the cakravartin appears, there also appears an army-minister treasure. At that time that minister possesses great wisdom and strategic acumen, courageous and majestic in virtue, endowed in every respect with great strength and noble form, skilled in commanding troops, protecting the royal domain, and preventing encroachment and disturbance. The army-minister goes to the king's presence and speaks thus: 'Great King, please know that I am skilled in overseeing the army and protecting the royal territory; whether in season or out of season, in all undertakings I will act in accordance with the king's wishes, without the slightest deficiency.' The king, upon seeing the army-minister treasure appear, is filled with great joy and says to him: 'You now possess great wisdom and strategic acumen, courageous and majestic in virtue, with great strength and noble form. Whether in season or out of season you can accomplish all things, skillfully commanding troops and protecting the national domain; truly excellent. Remain by my side for a long time as a close counselor; in all affairs employ skillful means. You are now the great protector of the king.' Bhikṣus, this is called the fifth treasure to appear when the cakravartin appears: the army-minister treasure.

"Furthermore, bhikṣus, when the cakravartin appears, there also appears a great maṇi-treasure. This maṇi-treasure is of supreme form and appearance, marvelous and outstanding, possessed of great luminosity, perfect and complete; its radiance is vast, illuminating all things, endowed with great power. In the royal palace, when this treasure is present, even in the darkness of night where no lamplight shines, the treasure emits its radiance, naturally shining and glowing like the light of the sun. Bhikṣus, in former times there was a great cakravartin who appeared in the world, and at that time there also appeared a great maṇi-treasure possessing great luminosity. The king at that time wished to test its power and commanded his ministers to quickly array the fourfold army for a nighttime excursion to the garden grove. The court ministers, having received the king's command, arrayed the fourfold army and quickly went to the king's presence, reporting: 'The fourfold army is assembled; now is the right time for the king to go forth on a royal progress.' Then the king placed the great maṇi-treasure atop the royal standard to lead before him, and in the middle of the night went forth to the garden grove. The treasure's radiance illuminated a full yojana; the king's fourfold army was entirely bathed in light, all luminously reflecting upon one another, no different from the light of the heavens. Bhikṣus, this is called the sixth treasure to appear when the cakravartin appears: the great maṇi-treasure.

"Furthermore, bhikṣus, when the cakravartin appears, there also appears a woman-treasure, of supreme form and appearance, perfect in all aspects, marvelous and foremost among all; none in the world can compare with her. Delicately subtle and soft like the kadalī plant, from the pores of her body there emanate all manner of wondrous fragrances, like a precious vessel filled with fragrance whose fragrant air is ever present at all times. Moreover, the breaths she exhales and inhales are each like the fragrance of the blue lotus, beloved and delightful to all. As the king proceeds, the woman-treasure follows behind; in all she does she brings ease and contentment. Her nature and conduct are chaste and upright, untainted by defilement; she ever speaks words of love, delightful for all to hear; her face is radiant, delightful for all to behold. Bhikṣus, this is called the seventh treasure to appear when the cakravartin appears: the woman-treasure."

The Buddha said to the bhikṣus: "These are called the seven treasures that appear when the cakravartin appears. You should know that when the Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha appears, he proclaims the Dharma of the seven factors of awakening. What are the seven? They are: the mindfulness factor of awakening, the investigation-of-dharmas factor of awakening, the vigor factor of awakening, the joy factor of awakening, the tranquility factor of awakening, the concentration factor of awakening, and the equanimity factor of awakening. These are called the Dharma of the seven factors of awakening. These are proclaimed only when the Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha appears in the world; just as the aforementioned seven treasures likewise appear only when the great anointed cakravartin appears. You should know that the Dharma of the seven factors of awakening proclaimed by the Tathāgata, when sentient beings cultivate and practice them in accordance with principle, enables all to attain happiness and benefit. You should all diligently cultivate and study them in this way."
