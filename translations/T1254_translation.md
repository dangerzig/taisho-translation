# T1254 末利支提婆華鬘經

# Mārīcī Deva Flower Garland Sūtra

*Note: This sūtra was translated by Amoghavajra (Bukong 不空) during the Tang dynasty. It presents the cult of Mārīcī (Molizhi 末利支), a celestial goddess who "always travels before the sun," invisible to all yet able to see the sun herself. The text, set at Jetavana with Śāriputra as principal questioner, provides an extensive collection of dhāraṇī methods for protection from kings, thieves, fire, water, poison, and evil spirits. It includes detailed instructions for making Mārīcī images, mudrā sequences, altar (maṇḍala) construction, homa fire-offering procedures for various purposes (travel safety, debate victory, healing, intelligence, binding demons), and appended mantras from Mañjuśrī and Vaiśravaṇa. The text reflects the mature Tang-dynasty esoteric Buddhist integration of Indian vidyā-dhāraṇī practice with elaborate ritual technology.*

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was dwelling at Jetavana, in the garden of Anāthapiṇḍada, in the country of Śrāvastī, together with a great assembly of two hundred and fifty arhats. There were also innumerable great bodhisattvas: Maitreya Bodhisattva, Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva, and Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva as foremost, along with Mārīcī and the various devas, nāgas, and the eight classes of beings surrounding them in front and behind.

At that time Śāriputra rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, placed his right knee on the ground, joined his palms, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! In the future age of the final dharma, what practice can sentient beings perform to escape various difficulties?" The Buddha told Śāriputra: "Listen carefully, listen carefully. I shall now explain this matter to you." The assembly then rejoiced and leapt with joy, and again earnestly requested the teaching. The World-Honored Buddha then spoke these words: "There is a celestial being named Mārīcī who always travels before the sun. The sun cannot see her, yet she can see the sun." He then spoke the mantra:

> "Namo buddhāya, namo dharmāya, namo saṃghāya."

[Dhāraṇī]

"In the midst of danger from kings, protect me. In danger from thieves, danger on the road, danger in the wilderness of losing one's way, in daytime and nighttime, in danger from water and fire, danger from rākṣasas, danger from ḍākinī ghosts, and danger from poisoning. The words of the Buddha are true, the words of the Dharma are true, the words of the Saṃgha are true, the words of the devas are true, the words of the ṛṣis are true: protect me." The mantra states:

[Dhāraṇī]

## Image-Making and Worship

The Buddha said: "If a person wishes to practice this method, among all dharma-practices this one is supreme. If a person wishes to make offerings to Mārīcī Deva, one should use gold, silver, red copper, white sandalwood, or purple sandalwood to make an image of Mārīcī Deva. The method for making the image is as follows: it should resemble a celestial maiden in form. The height may be one inch, two inches, three inches, up to one cubit; among these, one or two inches is best. The artisan who makes it must be the most skillful, and should receive the eight precepts and fast, bathing daily and wearing clean white garments while working. Whatever price the artisan requests, one must not haggle.

"Once the image is made, if a bhikṣu wishes to travel far, he should wrap the image in a piece of his kāṣāya. If a lay upāsaka who holds the five precepts wishes to carry it, he should place the image in his topknot. When relieving oneself, one should set the image aside and not have it on one's person while urinating or defecating.

## Mudrā and Altar Methods

"Next I shall explain the mudrā and altar methods.

"Cross the two little fingers and two ring fingers inside the palms, right pressing left. Extend both index fingers straight up with their tips touching. Hook both middle fingers behind the index fingers with their tips pressing together. Place both thumbs side by side, bound to the sides of the index fingers, and move the thumbs back and forth. This is the body-mātṛkā-dhāraṇī mudrā. Following the body mudrā, bend the upper joints of both middle fingers so that their tips point toward the thumbs with the nails facing each other, and also bend the upper joints of both thumbs so their tips point toward the palms. This is the crown mudrā.

"If a bhikṣu or bhikṣuṇī wraps the image in their kāṣāya, or if a layperson places the image in their topknot, one should form this crown mudrā and press it upon the image while reciting the mantra twenty-one times. When traveling on the road, form the body mudrā as before but open the tips of the two index fingers about two divisions apart: this is the protection mudrā, used to protect one's body.

"With the left hand, press the tip of the thumb on the first joint-line of the ring finger, and close the remaining four fingers into a fist. This is the joy mudrā. If one forms this mudrā and recites the mantra while approaching kings and ministers, those persons will feel joy.

"With the left hand, bend the arm forward. With the index finger and the three fingers below it, form a fist. Press the thumb on top of the index fingernail. Then open a hole inside the palm. With the right hand, extend the palm flat and slide it from the left wrist along the palm to the hole, then cover the hole with the right palm. In one's mind, visualize: the left palm is Mārīcī's heart, the right palm is Mārīcī's body, and within the left palm my own body resides. Mārīcī Deva conceals my body. Mārīcī is above my crown, protecting my body. This is the Mārīcī mudrā. If one repeatedly recites the mantra, one obtains great efficacy.

"The practitioner must not eat rice meals, but should eat only barley, milk, curds, ghee, and vegetables. If one cannot endure this, one may beg for rice to eat, but must not eat the food of the monastic community. When one has completed one hundred thousand recitations, one obtains efficacy.

"In a clean and pure ritual space, having installed the image of Mārīcī, the practitioner should bathe daily. If unable to bathe, one should wash one's hands and rinse one's mouth before entering the ritual space. Form the body mudrā, invoke Mārīcī, install her, and make various offerings. Recite the mantra daily one hundred and eight times or one thousand and eight times, continuing until a full one hundred thousand recitations are completed.

## Altar Construction

"Afterward, at a good location, prepare the ground: remove harmful objects, tree roots, tiles, gravel, hair, and bones. Tamp the earth firmly and make it level. The day for constructing the altar is the fifteenth of the twelfth month. Make it with five colors, and place Mārīcī's seat in the center, with a lotus seat and image painted on it. On the east side, install an attendant named Bhadraśrīyā. On the north side, install an attendant named Keśinī. On the south side, install an attendant named Mālinī. Then the mantra-master invokes them and installs them. After offering incense, flowers, food in eight plates, and sixteen lamps as various offerings, the mantra-master sits at the western gate facing east and recites the mantra one thousand and eight times, makes various offerings, and then dismisses them. There is also a special method: if one performs this altar ritual on a day of a solar or lunar eclipse, the efficacy is greatly enhanced.

## Homa Fire Offerings

"If a person wishes to travel east or west on a long journey, one should first construct a water-altar, invoke and install Mārīcī, take unhusked rice mixed with ghee, burn one offering per mantra recitation for one thousand and eight times while reciting the mantra. Whichever direction one wishes to go, one obtains great efficacy.

"Another method: for seven days, daily construct a water-altar, invoke and install Mārīcī, and also set up a fire hearth burning grain-wood. In the fire, burn unhusked rice and black sesame one hundred and eight times with the mantra, three times daily, one hundred and eight times per session, for seven days. After completing this, when one approaches kings and ministers, those persons will scatter and flee.

"Another method: if one wishes to win debates, following the previous method, burn pear-wood branches in one hundred and eight pieces, each one foot long, with the mantra for seven days. One who performs this method becomes a great debater.

"Another method: following the previous method, burn ghee in the fire one hundred and eight times with the mantra. All birds, beasts, and poisonous creatures will be unable to harm one.

"Another method: if a person wishes to see Mārīcī in person, following the previous method, light a fire with grain-wood, take twenty-one pieces of divine wood, smear them with ghee, honey, and curds, and burn them in the fire with the mantra. Daily eat only unhusked rice, milk, and ghee, and eat nothing else. For seven days, daily increase the offerings abundantly. On the seventh day, while performing this practice, Mārīcī herself will appear and enter the ritual space, and ask the practitioner: 'What dharma do you seek?' The practitioner may answer as he wishes. When Mārīcī grants permission and departs, one knows the efficacy has been obtained, and all the devas also rejoice.

"Another method: for seven days, three times daily, burn fennel grass, white calamus, and white mustard seeds, three substances, with the mantra. After completing this, when one approaches the king, that person will feel joy.

"Another method: if one wishes to approach officials, following the previous method, burn white mustard seeds in the fire, three times daily, one hundred and eight times per session with the mantra. For seven days eat only unhusked rice, milk, and curds, and eat nothing else. After completing seven days, when one approaches officials, those persons will feel joy.

"Another method: following the previous method, burn āmalaka-pear leaves in the fire one hundred and eight times with the mantra for seven days. This cures all ghost-caused illnesses and obtains efficacy.

"Another method: following the previous method, take well-pounded barley and mix it with honey to make balls the size of plums, one hundred and eight of them. During the seven days, the practitioner should eat nothing at all on the first day; for the remaining six days, eat as one wishes. Daily burn the barley balls in the fire with the mantra. After burning all one hundred and eight balls, extinguish the fire with water, then hold both palms over the smoke to fumigate them, recite the mantra twenty-one times, and make this vow: 'May my hands accomplish all practices and obtain all efficacies.' One then obtains efficacy in all things, and those before one feel joy.

## Healing and Protection Methods

"Another method: if a person has a fever, take fresh green grass, hold it near the mouth, recite the mantra twenty-one times, and rub the sick person fifty-four times with the mantra. The illness will be cured.

"Another method: recite the mantra once daily, make the great body-protection three times, make the great boundary-setting three times, and recite the mantra five times: whatever beloved person one desires, one will obtain. Recite the mantra six times with boundary-setting, and at night one may enter cemeteries without any fear.

"Another method: if one wishes to travel far, first in one's private room burn frankincense seven times in the fire with the mantra. When setting out on the road, repeatedly recite the mantra. Dangers from thieves and ghosts on the road will be unable to approach the traveler.

"Another method: following the previous method, on the eighth day of the bright fortnight, in a clean room, take fine incense and flowers along with a small amount of unhusked rice, and burn them in the fire one thousand and eight times. The practitioner should fast for one day and wear clean garments. One who performs this method will be respected, honored, and given offerings by others.

## Method for Obtaining Intelligence

"Another method: take cow dung, catching it in a vessel before it falls to the ground, and do not put it elsewhere. Use it mixed with water to make a water-altar. In the center of the altar, place a Buddha image or Buddha relic. Then take the milk of a mother cow with a calf and a yellow cow and make ghee, placing it in a golden bowl. With the right hand ring finger, stir the ghee while reciting the mantra. If fire appears above the ghee, one knows one has obtained great intelligence and can memorize a thousand verses at a single recitation. If fire does not appear but only smoke and warmth, one knows one has obtained moderate intelligence. If there is neither smoke nor warmth, one should prostrate on the ground facing the ghee vessel, and with the right hand ring finger scoop and eat the ghee: one will obtain a small degree of intelligence.

## Elaborate Ritual for Obtaining Efficacy

"Another method: outside the eastern gate of a city, prepare the ground well and make a four-cubit altar. Take five unfired bricks, placing one in the center and one on each of the four sides. Also take four water-jars filled with water, plug their mouths with willow branches, and place them on the bricks of the four sides. Take purple sandalwood ground in water, and sprinkle the water on the altar. Offer red flowers on the altar. Also place four sesame-oil lamps, one beside each water-jar. Encircle the altar on all four sides with five-colored thread. Outside the four sides of the altar, offer various flowers, incense, food, and drink to the ghosts. At the four gates of the altar, spread seats of green grass. The mantra-master, wearing fine clean garments, should sit cross-legged on the green grass, invoke Mārīcī and the attendants, install them, and make various offerings. The mantra-master holds green grass and recites the mantra, rubbing the grass from his head to his feet one hundred and eight times. Then the mantra-master forms the Mārīcī body mudrā, holding the green grass inside the mudrā, and places it on his own crown. He instructs a disciple to take the four water-jars from the altar and pour each one over the mantra-master's crown. After this, the mantra-master puts on fine clean garments, forms the protection mudrā, recites the Buddha's name, pays homage to the Buddha and to Mārīcī, and takes nāgapuṣpa flowers, borneol camphor, and ghee, three fragrant substances. For seven days the mantra-master eats only unhusked rice and cow's milk porridge, nothing else, and daily recites the mantra over the three substances one hundred and eight times. On the seventh day, he places the substances on his crown, right shoulder, left shoulder, heart, throat, and forehead, and obtains efficacy. This instruction applies regardless of any other special method in use.

## Healing with Knotted Thread

"Another method: following the previous method, if a person suffers from hemorrhoids, take a black thread and make it into a mantra-cord. The patient should lie on a bed with head to the east and feet to the west. Tie the cord around the patient's waist, and also tie a cord to the bed-leg, reciting the mantra twenty-one times. The hemorrhoids will be cured. For a neck ailment, follow the same method but tie the cord around the neck instead of the waist.

## Binding the Demon King Vināyaka

"Another method: take clay from both banks of a river and make one hundred ghost figures. Among them, the ghost king is named Vināyaka. Make the ghost king's head in the form of an elephant head. Make the other ghosts' heads in the forms of various birds and beasts, but their bodies, hands, and feet should all be in human form, four to eight finger-breadths in height. Take purple sandalwood ground in water and use the water mixed with clay on the ground to make an altar. With five-colored earth, make seats on the altar: one seat in the center, two on the north, two on the south, two on the east. On the central seat place the ghost king figure, and on the remaining six seats distribute the ninety-nine ghost figures. Offer various incense and flowers, light seven ghee lamps, and make food and drink offerings. Also take benzoin mixed with ghee and burn it as an offering. The mantra-master sits at the western gate facing east and recites the Mārīcī mantra seven times. He then recites the mantra over various colored threads twenty-one times. He takes the ghost figures from the three sides of the altar and gathers them all near the ghost king in the center, then binds all the ghost figures together with the seven-colored thread. He takes one hundred and eight balls of calf dung, burning each one in the fire with one recitation of the mantra. Before each recitation, he first says 'Bind all ghosts,' and then says 'svāhā.' After completing all one hundred and eight balls, he digs a hole elsewhere, deep to a person's waist, and places all the bound ghost figures in the hole. He offers various incense, flowers, food, and drink to the ghosts, then seals the top of the hole, tamps the earth firmly and levels it. The ghosts can never escape. When the mantra-master is on his deathbed due to karmic illness, he should mentally resolve to release the ghosts, and they will be freed. One who performs this method obtains the great efficacy of Mārīcī and efficacy in all mantras.

## Additional Methods

"Another method: throughout one's entire life, daily eat only unhusked rice and milk porridge, and repeatedly recite the mantra. One obtains great intelligence. Among the four castes, one obtains the great love of brāhmaṇas. If one burns ghee, curds, and milk in the fire, one obtains the love of kṣatriyas. If one burns barley, milk, and curds, one obtains the love of vaiśyas. If one burns sesame dregs, one obtains the love of śūdras.

"Another method: if a person has a ghost-caused illness and cannot speak at all, recite the mantra over water twenty-one times and give it to the patient to drink; the person will speak.

"Another method: for a ghost-caused illness where the mouth is locked shut and the person cannot speak, recite the mantra over the corner of a kāṣāya twenty-one times and strike the person with it; the person will speak.

"Another method: take a handful of ash, recite the mantra seven times, and scatter it in the four directions to set a boundary. Also, make ten clay pellets, throw each one in one of the ten directions, and set a great boundary.

"Another method: if a woman has difficulty in childbirth, recite the mantra over black sesame oil seven times and rub it on her navel. She will give birth easily.

"Another method: if after winning a debate the opponent becomes hateful and brings accusations against you, or if there is any quarrel or legal dispute, take white calamus, recite the mantra twenty-one times, and tie it to your right arm. Also form the joy mudrā with the left hand and recite the mantra. One will obtain complete vindication. If one recites the mantra repeatedly, one obtains efficacy in all things.

"Another method: if a person has been stung by a venomous creature, the mantra-master takes five-colored thread and makes a mantra-cord with twenty-one knots. He ties it to his own right arm, then goes to the stung person, takes a willow branch, recites the mantra over it, and repeatedly rubs the person with his hand. The person is cured.

"If a person has been bitten by a deadly venomous snake and is near death, the mantra-master cups water in his hands, rinses his mouth seven times while reciting the mantra, and sprinkles the water on the patient twenty-one times. The person is cured.

"If a person's body has developed malignant sores, mix clay and apply it to the sores twenty-one times with the mantra. The person is cured.

"If livestock have contracted an epidemic, light a fire of grain-wood in the center of the city, burn cow's milk in the fire with the mantra, and the illness is cured. This should be done at night. The next day at noon, again light a fire of grain-wood, take white mustard seed oil mixed with white mustard seeds, and burn them one thousand and eight times with the mantra. The illness is cured.

"Another method: take one thousand and eight pieces of udumbara wood, burn each one in the fire with one recitation of the mantra. All kumbhāṇḍas, yakṣas, and other ghost-spirits will all be pleased.

"If one burns small pieces of winter melon one thousand and eight times in the fire, all the wangliang spirits will be pleased.

"If one takes wood from a tree growing on a tomb, one thousand and eight pieces, mixed with black sesame, and burns them in the fire with one thousand and eight recitations of the mantra, all the great evil ghosts and spirits will be pleased.

"Another method: if one takes one thousand and eight pieces of bodhi tree branches, smears each with ghee, and burns them in the fire with one thousand and eight mantra recitations, the Four Great Heavenly Kings will be pleased and feel affection.

"Another method: if a person has an illness caused by madness, the mantra-master takes all five grains mixed together, scoops them in his hands, recites the mantra, and burns them one thousand and eight times. The ghost-spirits are pleased and the illness is cured.

"If one takes benzoin, pounds it into pills, smears them with ghee, and burns them one thousand and eight times with the mantra, Maheśvara and the surrounding devas will all be pleased.

"If following the previous method one constructs an altar with various offerings, places a Buddha image or Buddha relic in the center, takes three thousand and eight pieces of harītakī-śamī wood, smears them with ghee, curds, and honey, and burns each piece one at a time with one recitation of the mantra until all three thousand and eight are completed, one who performs this method can purify even the four grave offenses and the five heinous crimes, and obtains efficacy.

"If the practitioner, following the previous method, constructs a water-altar, and from the eighth to the fifteenth of the bright fortnight, daily takes one thousand and eight pieces of purple ginger wood, smears them with ghee, and burns them with the mantra, Mārīcī will come and enter the ritual space, and grant the practitioner's wishes. The practitioner will see Mārīcī in person and obtain great efficacy.

"If one fasts for one day and performs this method with various offerings, one obtains the great efficacy of Mārīcī.

"Another method: if one wishes to obtain wealth, on the fourteenth and fifteenth days of the dark fortnight, three times each day, burn black sesame, unhusked rice, and rice flowers, three substances, with the mantra. One will obtain wealth.

"Another method: if one wishes to bind demons, for seven days daily take one thousand and eight branches of the chinaberry tree (also called the bodhi tree), smear each with white mustard seed oil, and burn them in the fire. One achieves binding. If a mantra-master or layperson practicing this mantra is caught by government authorities and placed in shackles and bonds, repeatedly reciting this mantra will cause the bonds to never hold.

"If people are angry with one another, take sesame oil dregs mixed with rice chaff and burn them one thousand and eight times with the mantra. The angry person will immediately become joyful. If one burns black sesame one thousand and eight times, the person will feel love and joy.

"Another method: if one wishes to obtain wealth, for seven days daily take one thousand and eight segments of pomegranate grass stalks, six finger-breadths long, and burn each one with the mantra. One will obtain wealth.

"Another method: if one wishes to go to another person's place to request a beloved object, take white calamus, recite the mantra one thousand and eight times, and tie it to one's arm. Whatever one asks for, one will obtain.

"Another method: for twenty-one days, three times daily, take benzoin pounded into one thousand and eight pills, smear them with ghee, curds, and honey, and burn each one in the fire with the mantra. When one goes before kings and officials, those persons will feel joy and affection. If one wishes to become an administrator, for seven days, three times daily, take various famous incenses pounded into one thousand and eight pills, smear each with ghee, burn in the fire with the mantra, and one will become an administrator.

"Another method: if the practitioner bathes, enters the ritual space, constructs a water-altar with various offerings, invokes and installs Mārīcī, and completes one hundred thousand recitations, then afterward declares a boundary, whatever the practitioner wishes will be accomplished. All difficult matters will easily be achieved, and one can also break the ritual workings of others."

## Mārīcī's Own Teaching

At that time Mārīcī addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I have a separate method that I now wish to explain. Take fine purple sandalwood, three finger-breadths wide and three inches long. On one face of the wood, carve the form of Mārīcī as a celestial maiden. On either side of the image, carve two attendants of Mārīcī, also in feminine form. Then with a separate piece of purple sandalwood, make a cover for it. Once this image is made, if one wishes to travel far, carry this image without separating it from one's body, keeping it concealed. Do not let others know, and daily recite the mantra repeatedly.

"If one has a wish and wants to construct a water-altar, place the image in the center of the altar, invoke and install Mārīcī, make various offerings, and also take one hundred and eight lotus flowers to offer. The offering method is: take each lotus flower in hand, recite the mantra over it, and offer it. Then burn black sesame and unhusked rice one thousand and eight times in the fire with the mantra. After this, holding the image, one obtains efficacy in all things. All the above methods require constructing a water-altar with various offerings before obtaining efficacy." When Mārīcī finished speaking this dharma, together with the various devas, nāgas, and the eight classes of beings, she paid homage to the Buddha and withdrew.

[Dhāraṇī]

One version states: the method for making the celestial image is as follows. The image has two hands. The left hand is bent at the elbow, raised horizontally level before the left breast, with a fist holding a whisk, shaped like the whisk held by a dharma-preaching master on a high seat. On the whisk, make an Indic svastika character, like the character on a Buddha image's chest. In each of the four curves of the character, place a sun-disc form, four sun-discs in all. Above the whisk, make a flame form. The right hand extends the arm and fingers, hanging straight down. For making the image, whether painted or sculpted it is the same with no difference. The image body should be one or two inches up to one cubit in height.

[Dhāraṇī]

The following separate mantra is for binding thieves.

Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva spoke a mantra:

Homage, as in the thousand-turning head.

[Dhāraṇī]

Also, if while traveling one encounters thieves, recite the mantra while firmly pressing the thumb of one hand, and one will encounter no danger from thieves. Alternatively, recite the mantra over one's sleeve-hem or garment-lapel, twisting it leftward seven times, grasping it firmly while reciting the mantra as one passes.

### Anti-Sleep Mantra

[Dhāraṇī]

If a person frequently falls asleep during seated practice, recite this mantra seven times with utmost sincerity before a Buddha image, and drowsiness will diminish and vanish.

### Vaiśravaṇa's Mantra

[Dhāraṇī]

If one recites the mantra over clean oil seven or fourteen times and uses it to anoint one's sleeping place, one will obtain wealth and goods as wished.

### Mantra for Binding All Thieves

[Dhāraṇī]

### Another Mantra for Binding Thieves

[Dhāraṇī]

Also, if one has been robbed, burn incense and recite the mantra. If there is a suspect, call out the name. If the thief is unknown, simply recite the mantra face-to-face. The thief will bind himself and confess the stolen goods. Once the goods are recovered, then release the thief. This obtains great efficacy.

This completes the Mārīcī Deva Flower Garland Sūtra.
