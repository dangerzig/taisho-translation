# 貝多樹下思惟十二因緣經
## Sūtra of Contemplating the Twelve Links of Dependent Arising Beneath the Bodhi Tree

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0713

Translated from the Chinese. Wu dynasty, translated by Zhi Qian (支謙)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was in the land of Śrāvastī, dwelling in the Jeta Grove of Anāthapiṇḍada.

At that time the Buddha said: "Bhikṣus!" The bhikṣus answered: "Yes, we are here!" The bhikṣus listened to the Buddha. The Buddha then spoke: "Bhikṣus! Before I had attained the Buddha's path, when I was still a bodhisattva, I had this thought: 'Alas! This world is exceedingly harsh, with birth, old age, and death. Beings go forth and receive only suffering. When will there be an escape from old age and death?'

"Bhikṣus, I then reflected and attained this understanding: 'Why is there old age and death? And what is the condition for old age and death?' Bhikṣus, I then contemplated its origin and attained this appropriate understanding: 'Because there is birth, there is old age and death; and birth is the condition for old age and death.'

"Bhikṣus, I then reflected: 'Why is there birth? And what is the condition for birth?' Bhikṣus, I then reflected and contemplated its origin, and attained this appropriate understanding: 'Because there is becoming, there is birth; and becoming is the condition for birth.'

"Bhikṣus, I then reflected: 'Why is there becoming? And what is the condition for becoming?' Bhikṣus, I then reflected and contemplated its origin, and there arose this appropriate understanding: 'Because there is grasping, there is becoming; and grasping is the condition for becoming.'

"Bhikṣus, I then reflected: 'Why is there grasping? And what is the condition for grasping?' Bhikṣus, I then contemplated its origin and attained this appropriate understanding: 'Because there is craving, there is grasping; and craving is the condition for grasping.'

"Bhikṣus, I then reflected: 'Why is there craving? And what is the condition for craving?' Bhikṣus, I then reflected and contemplated its origin, and attained the understanding: 'Because there is feeling, there is craving; and feeling is the condition for craving.'

"Bhikṣus, I then reflected: 'Why is there feeling? And what is the condition for feeling?' Bhikṣus, I then reflected and contemplated its origin, and attained this appropriate understanding: 'Because there is contact, there is feeling; and contact is the condition for feeling.'

"Bhikṣus, I then reflected: 'Why is there contact? And what is the condition for contact?' Bhikṣus, I then reflected and contemplated its origin, and there arose this appropriate understanding: 'Because there are the six sense bases, there is contact; and the six sense bases are the condition for contact.'

"Bhikṣus, I then reflected: 'Why are there the six sense bases? And what is the condition for the six sense bases?' Bhikṣus, I then reflected and contemplated its origin, and attained this appropriate understanding: 'Because there is name-and-form, there are the six sense bases; and name-and-form is the condition for the six sense bases.'

"Bhikṣus, I then reflected: 'Why is there name-and-form? And what is the condition for name-and-form?' Bhikṣus, I then reflected and contemplated its origin, and attained this appropriate understanding: 'Because there is consciousness, there is name-and-form; and consciousness is the condition for name-and-form.'

"Bhikṣus, I then reflected: 'Why is there consciousness? And what is the condition for consciousness?' Bhikṣus, I then reflected and contemplated its origin, and there was this appropriate understanding: 'Because there is name-and-form, there is consciousness; and name-and-form is the condition for consciousness.'

"Bhikṣus, I then reflected and this thought arose: 'This consciousness turns back and does not proceed further. Name-and-form is the condition for consciousness, and consciousness is the condition for name-and-form. Name-and-form is the condition for the six sense bases; the six sense bases are the condition for contact; contact is the condition for feeling; feeling is the condition for craving; craving is the condition for grasping; grasping is the condition for becoming; becoming is the condition for birth; birth is the condition for old age and death, grief, lamentation, suffering, displeasure, and anguish. From this the complete great mass of suffering arises.'

"Bhikṣus, I then reflected: 'When what is absent is old age and death absent? And by the cessation of what does old age and death cease?' Bhikṣus, I then reflected and contemplated its origin, and attained this appropriate understanding: 'When there is no birth, there is also no old age and death. When birth ceases, old age and death also cease.'

"Bhikṣus, I then reflected: 'When what is absent is birth absent? And by the cessation of what does birth cease?' Bhikṣus, I then reflected and contemplated its origin, and there arose this appropriate understanding: 'When there is no becoming, there is no birth. When becoming ceases, birth also ceases.'

"Bhikṣus, I then reflected: 'When what is absent is becoming absent? And by the cessation of what does becoming cease?' Bhikṣus, I then reflected and contemplated its origin, and there arose this appropriate understanding: 'When there is no grasping, there is no becoming. When grasping ceases, becoming also ceases.'

"Bhikṣus, I then reflected: 'When what is absent is grasping absent? And by the cessation of what does grasping cease?' Bhikṣus, I then reflected and contemplated its origin, and attained this understanding: 'When there is no craving, there is no grasping. When craving ceases, grasping also ceases.'

"Bhikṣus, I then reflected: 'When what is absent is craving absent? And by the cessation of what does craving cease?' Bhikṣus, I then reflected and contemplated its origin, and the understanding arose as appropriate: 'When there is no feeling, there is no craving. When feeling ceases, craving also ceases.'

"Bhikṣus, I then reflected: 'When what is absent is feeling absent? And by the cessation of what does feeling cease?' Bhikṣus, I then reflected and contemplated its origin, and attained this understanding: 'When there is no contact, there is no feeling. When contact ceases, feeling also ceases.'

"Bhikṣus, I then reflected: 'When what is absent is contact absent? And by the cessation of what does contact cease?' Bhikṣus, I then reflected and contemplated its origin, and attained this appropriate understanding: 'When the six sense bases are absent, contact is absent. When the six sense bases cease, contact ceases.'

"I then reflected: 'When what is absent are the six sense bases absent? And by the cessation of what do the six sense bases cease?' I then reflected and contemplated its origin, and there was this appropriate understanding: 'When name-and-form is absent, the six sense bases are absent. When name-and-form ceases, the six sense bases also cease.'

"I then reflected: 'When what is absent is name-and-form absent? And by the cessation of what does name-and-form cease?' I then reflected and contemplated its origin, and there was this appropriate understanding: 'When consciousness is absent, name-and-form is also absent. When consciousness ceases, name-and-form also ceases.'

"I then reflected: 'When what is absent is consciousness absent? And by the cessation of what does consciousness cease?' I then reflected and contemplated its origin, and attained this appropriate understanding: 'When formations are absent, consciousness is also absent. When formations cease, consciousness also ceases.'

"I then reflected: 'When what is absent are formations absent? And by the cessation of what do formations cease?' I then reflected and contemplated its origin, and attained this understanding: 'When ignorance is absent, formations are also absent. When ignorance ceases, formations also cease. When formations cease, consciousness ceases. When consciousness ceases, name-and-form ceases. When name-and-form ceases, the six sense bases cease. When the six sense bases cease, contact ceases. When contact ceases, feeling ceases. When feeling ceases, craving ceases. When craving ceases, grasping ceases. When grasping ceases, becoming ceases. When becoming ceases, birth ceases. When birth ceases, old age and death cease; grief, sorrow, suffering, displeasure, and extreme anguish all cease. Thus the immeasurably great mass of suffering completely ceases.'"

The Buddha told the bhikṣus: "At that time I reflected in this way, and by my own effort discovered the ancient path. The Buddha followed it from this point onward; having followed it, I knew old age and death, knew the origin of old age and death, knew the cessation of old age and death, and knew the practice leading to the cessation of old age and death. So too with birth, becoming, grasping, craving, feeling, contact, the six sense bases, name-and-form, consciousness, and formations. I knew ignorance, knew the origin of ignorance, knew how ignorance ceases, and knew the practice that leads to the cessation of ignorance. It is like, bhikṣus, a person traveling through an empty wilderness in a remote place who discovers an ancient path, an ancient trail where people once walked. That person follows the trail. Having followed it, he sees an ancient city, with fine gardens, fine groves and trees, fine ponds and pools, fine rivers, fine ramparts and moats, and fine, thriving, delightful parks. He then thinks: 'If I now go and report to the king, saying, "As I was traveling through the empty wilderness in a remote place, I discovered an ancient path, an ancient trail where people once walked. I followed it, and having followed, I found an ancient city with fine gardens, fine groves and trees, fine ponds and pools, fine rivers, fine ramparts and moats, and a fine, thriving, delightful park. The king may wish to dwell there."' The king then takes up residence there. Afterward, the city gradually becomes magnificent, gradually grows and thrives, with many people flourishing."

The Buddha told the bhikṣus: "I too am like this. I discovered the ancient path, the path that the Buddhas of the past once walked, and I followed it. Having followed it, I knew old age and death and where it comes from, knew the accumulation of old age and death, knew how old age and death ceases, and knew what practice leads beyond it. So too with birth, becoming, grasping, craving, feeling, contact, the six sense bases, name-and-form, consciousness, and formations. I knew ignorance, knew from what ignorance arises, knew from what ignorance ceases, and knew what practice eliminates ignorance and leads beyond the world."

The Buddha then told the bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, and upāsikās: "If a bhikṣu, as a bhikṣu, practices appropriately and receives the teaching, he attains the path, does not lose the path, and is skilled in the path. So too with bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, and upāsikās. If they truly receive and rightly practice, they follow the path and attain the path. When one can practice in accordance with the Dharma, the practitioners of the unconditioned increase, multiply, and spread far and wide."

The devas and humans, having seen the Buddha teach thus, and the bhikṣus having taken it to heart, all who practiced the Buddha's teaching received it.
