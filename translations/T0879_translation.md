Universally on behalf of Brahmā, Śakra, the Four Kings, the eight classes of devas, nāgas, and others, sovereign rulers and human kings, preceptors, teachers, parents, and spiritual friends, the assembly of the practice-hall, and all sentient beings throughout the Dharma-realm: may all sever their various obstructions. Taking refuge, I confess and repent.

With utmost sincerity, I confess! We, your disciples and assembly, from beginningless, vast kalpas until the present day, have been deluded and without awakening to non-self, falsely reckoning a self and a person. Once the notion of self has arisen, we have always grasped at "mine." Sense-faculties and sense-objects have overwhelmed us; the aggregates of consciousness have rushed about. Stirring body and mind like the turning of lightning, our originally pure eyes, ears, nose, tongue, body, and mind, in a single moment of non-awakening, have turned into six masters, stealing the Dharma-king's treasure and supplying the bandit of extreme views. With the bandit grown powerful, they have destroyed the city of nirvāṇa, ravaged the Dharma-body, and set ablaze the life of wisdom. Such transgressions as these exceed in number the grains of sand, and from delusion to delusion, one cannot fathom their beginning or end. Now, for the first time, we have become aware and deeply repent, ashamed of ourselves. Day and night we are alarmed and fearful, body and mind trembling. We forever sever the delusion of awakening, the craving and attachment to self and person, and take refuge in the city of nirvāṇa, returning to the land of peace and bliss. With the awakening of non-self, we subdue the six masters, recover the Dharma-king's treasure, and store it in the three firm treasuries, to sustain the life of wisdom and increase the Dharma-body. We light the lamp of the Dharma-nature, which constantly illumines without end. In vow and practice, in principle and actuality, we do not depart from the Dharma-realm. The Three Treasures and the Three Vehicles we vow to forever promote and protect. May the transgressions of delusion and awakening, following this confession, be extinguished. Having confessed and transferred the merit, with utmost sincerity we take refuge and bow to the Three Treasures.

With utmost sincerity, we make this vow! We, your disciples and assembly, together with all sentient beings throughout the Dharma-realm, from this day forward until the attainment of unsurpassed bodhi: may we be firm in every thought, advancing in every thought, with body and mind at ease, with eloquence and exposition unobstructed. In a single moment of mind, may we possess all-knowledge. One should know that all dharmas are ultimately empty and quiescent, yet one constantly liberates all sentient beings. May all alike realize nirvāṇa, without taking nirvāṇa as a realization. Having made this vow, with utmost sincerity we take refuge and bow to the Three Treasures.
