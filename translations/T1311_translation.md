# 梵天火羅九曜
## Brahmā's Horā: The Nine Grahas

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1311

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, composed by Yixing (一行).

---

Compiled by the Chan Master Yixing. The twenty-eight lunar mansions rotate leftward in the sky, and through calculation one may know which positions a person has offended. From the first year of Wude of the Great Tang, the year wuyin, to the fourteenth year of Xiantong, the year jiawu, is a total of two hundred and fifty-seven years. The diagram of Brahmā's Horā of the Nine Grahas, together with the two hidden stars Rāhu and Ketu, is presented here. By examining the overall positions of these stars, one may determine with certainty a person's lifetime fortune, good and ill, with unfailing accuracy in ten thousand cases. Truly marvelous indeed.

### 1. Rāhu, the Eclipse Deity Star

The mantra:

*Oṃ rāhu-na asura-rājāya sma-śatunāya śānti-kari svāhā.*

Oral instruction for making offerings to Rāhu with coins: make offerings in the direction of the ox and tiger [north-northeast]. Ages 1, 10, 19, 28, 37, 46, 55, 64, 73, 82, 91 are all greatly inauspicious.

When one's current year reaches this mansion, it is inauspicious. The star is hidden and invisible. It has several names: Rāhu, Rāśi, Yellow Banner, and Fire Yang. When it reaches one's natal destiny, there will be worry over official matters and loss of position, serious illness that entangles, loss and scattering of wealth, mourning garments, and anxiety over quarrels and disputes. For the king, if the deity is not properly worshipped, when reaching this mansion one should cultivate merit to avert it. Offer spirit-money and paint the form of the offended deity to worship. This will bring great fortune.

### 2. Saturn, the Earth Star of the Central Palace (Kaikhwān)

The mantra:

*Namaḥ. Oṃ śanaiścarāya svāhā.*

Another mantra:

*Oṃ śanaiścarāya nakṣatra-parāhaṇa-ruṣāya puṣṭi-kari svāhā.*

Another mantra:

*Oṃ śuśu-karāgandha-vara-vara-rāja-śrī svāhā.*

Spring: southeast. Summer: southwest. Autumn: northwest. Winter: northeast. In the intercalary summer month, worship with one platter of fruit. Oral instruction: the direction is not fixed; in spring, face southeast, and for the rest one may know by analogy. Ages 2, 11, 20, 29, 38, 47, 56, 65, 74, 83, 92 are all greatly inauspicious.

When one's current year reaches this mansion, it is the Earth Star of the Central Palace. The star's circumference is ninety li; it governs the territory of Chu. This mansion is the most inauspicious, especially afflicting the palace and obstructing people. This mansion brings caution, illness, and imprisonment. For the noble person, it is a year of severe adversity. It is called the Earth Star, and one should not offend it. Its form is like a brāhmaṇa, with an ox-crown on its head, holding a monk's staff, bringing calamity to people. The king should worship with one platter of fruit in the intercalary summer month. One should send spirit-money for one's natal deity, paint the form of the offended deity to worship, and avert it with yellow garments. This will certainly eliminate calamity and increase blessings and longevity. One's natal destiny belongs to the Red Void Heaven. All matters will not go as wished. Men should avoid official affairs; women should avoid marriage. Avoid the gengxin days of the third and ninth months.

### 3. Ketu, the North Pole Star (Dīpa)

The mantra:

*Oṃ kuśi-dha-tathā-dhiṭhā-ri-da gai-gai ci-ci nīti svāhā.*

Another mantra:

*Oṃ budhāya nakṣatra-svāmi nakhe-nu-ma svāhā.*

The heart-mantra:

*Oṃ su-tiriṣṭha svāhā.*

In the month of mid-summer, worship with oil, making offerings in the northern direction. Ages 3, 12, 21, 30, 39, 48, 57, 66, 75, 84, 93 are slightly auspicious.

When one's current year reaches this mansion, it is named the North Pole. It is also called the Cunning Star and the Drop Star. Its circumference is one hundred li; it governs the territory of Yan and Zhao. When it reaches one's natal destiny, it brings yin-type anxiety, quarrels and disputes, and trouble with thieves. Its deity has the form of a woman, wearing an ape-crown on her head and holding paper and brush. The king should worship with oil in the month of mid-summer, and it is advisable to make offerings to the Northern Dipper to avert it. Count through the twelve palaces, starting from one's birth month with the first mansion counting as one day, counting leftward to one's birthday to determine it. One's natal destiny belongs to the Wandering Salty Heaven. All matters will not succeed. Men should avoid official affairs; women should avoid marriage. Avoid the bingxu day of the fourth month.

### 4. Venus, the Great White Star of the West

The mantra:

*Namaḥ. Oṃ śukra-vībhi svāhā.*

Another mantra:

*Oṃ śukra-gandha-vara-dhava-vara-rāja-śrī svāhā.*

Always in the month of mid-autumn, worship with raw coins, making offerings in the western direction. Ages 4, 13, 22, 31, 40, 49, 58, 67, 76, 85, 94 are slightly inauspicious.

When one's current year reaches Nāhiji, this is Venus, the Metal Essence of the West. The star has several names: Great White, Long Glow, and Nāhiji. Its circumference is one hundred li; it governs the territory of Qin. When it reaches one's natal year and destiny, there will be weeping, lamentation, and the danger of weapons. Its form is like a woman wearing a head-crown, clothed in white silk, playing a stringed instrument, bringing affliction to people. The king should always worship with raw coins in the month of mid-autumn. When one's current year reaches this star, one should wear white garments and make offerings to one's natal spirit to cultivate merit. This brings great fortune. One's natal destiny belongs to the Flying Thrust Heaven. All matters are auspicious; worries are resolved and afflictions cease. Men will have wealth and success in office; women and children will be connected and harmonious. Livelihood will increase ten-thousandfold.

### 5. The Sun, the Secret Star of Great Yang (Mitra)

The mantra:

*Namo ratna-trayāya. Namaḥ sūrya-sarva-nakṣatra-rājāya. Oṃ amogha-śāya-śānti svāhā.*

On the day of the winter solstice, worship with various jewels, making offerings in the direction of the hare and dragon [east-southeast]. Ages 5, 14, 23, 32, 41, 50, 59, 68, 77, 86, 95 are greatly auspicious.

When one's current year reaches this mansion, it belongs to the Great Yang, the Sun. The star's circumference is fifteen hundred li; it completes one revolution of heaven per day. When it reaches one's natal destiny, one will gain promotion and increase in salary, have joyful events, constantly receive the guidance of noble persons, and succeed in all undertakings. The king should worship with various jewels on the day of the winter solstice. This is greatly auspicious. The mantra of this star is inconceivable. If one wears it on the body and makes offerings for one year with a sincere mind, one will not die an untimely death. One's natal destiny belongs to the Pervading Light Heaven. All matters go as wished; the heavenly spirits protect one, and there will be no real calamity. All paths open up, and blessings and fortune come of themselves.

### 6. Mars, the Fire Star of the South (Aṅgāraka)

The mantra of Mars:

*Namaḥ. Oṃ mahā-ari-da svāhā.*

Another mantra:

*Oṃ aṅgāraka-ārugya svāhā.*

In the month of mid-summer, worship with fire, making offerings in the southern direction. Ages 6, 15, 24, 33, 42, 51, 60, 69, 78, 87, 96 are all inauspicious years.

When one's current year reaches this star, it is also called the Southern Yinghuo Star, the Sili Star, and the Void Han. Its circumference is seventy li; it governs the territory of Wei. When it reaches one's natal destiny, it will certainly produce quarrels and disputes, with illness entangling. Its deity has the form of a non-Buddhist practitioner, wearing a donkey-crown on its head, with four arms holding weapons and blades. The king should worship with fire in the month of mid-summer. When one's current year reaches this star: for the noble person, fortune comes, and their office and affairs advance and increase; for the common person, fortune comes, and calamities and harm do not arise. If one reveres it, there will be no misfortune or blame. One's natal destiny belongs to the Cloud Heaven. The heavenly spirits protect one and dare not cause harm; money and wealth overflow, and blessings and fortune arrive abundantly. Although there may be minor worries, they resolve themselves without suffering.

### 7. Ketu, the Eclipse Deity Star

The mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-ketu-nā nakṣatra-rājāya hūṃ.*

Paint this form and worship it in a secluded room to avert calamity and turn misfortune into fortune. Make offerings in the direction of the ram and monkey [south-southwest]. Rāhu wears jewels, as do the Sun and Moon; Ketu wears brocade and embroidered garments. Ages 7, 16, 25, 34, 43, 52, 61, 79, 88, 97 are all inauspicious.

When one's current year reaches this, Ketu is also a hidden star. It is also called Leopard Tail and Great Hidden. Its head is hidden and invisible; it moves unseen and has no fixed form. When it afflicts a person, official entanglements are most numerous and oppressive, one's pursuit of office will not succeed, affairs are subject to transfer and relocation, official summonses entangle, and there is much worry and illness. This star is inauspicious. The king does not worship it.

When one's current year reaches this, one should send offerings to the Commander of the Five Paths. Paint the form of this deity and worship it in a secluded room to avert calamity and turn misfortune into fortune. One's natal destiny belongs to the Warm Solitary Heaven. All directions are unfavorable, travel brings no profit, husband and wife weep or the husband sheds tears. Avoid the jiayǐ days of the fifth and sixth months.

### 8. The Moon, the Great Yin (Candra)

The mantra:

*Oṃ candrāya nakṣatra-rājāya śānti svāhā.*

Always on the day of the summer solstice, worship with various jewels, jade, and water, making offerings in the direction of the monkey and rooster [west-southwest]. Ages 8, 17, 26, 35, 44, 53, 62, 71, 80, 89, 98.

When one's current year reaches this mansion, it is the Great Yin, belonging to the Moon. The Moon's circumference is fifteen hundred li; it completes one revolution beneath heaven per day. When it reaches one's natal destiny, it is greatly auspicious: one will gain promotion and increase in salary, succeed in all undertakings, receive the guidance of noble persons, and constantly have joyful and celebratory events. The king should worship on the day of the summer solstice with various precious jewels and water. When one's current year reaches this, all affairs are harmonious, one's pursuit of office will succeed, and all undertakings are greatly auspicious. One's natal destiny belongs to the Subtle Offering Heaven. All manner of things are inauspicious; one will encounter illness and fear death, fields and silkworms will yield no harvest, and there will be worry over official matters. Avoid the bingding days of the first and ninth months.

### 9. Jupiter, the Wood Star of the East (Bṛhaspati)

The mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ. Oṃ indrāya svāhā.*

Another mantra:

*Oṃ bṛhaspati-nāma-dhipita-vānaya-māla-vara-dhanī svāhā.*

Always in the month of mid-spring, worship with various jewels, making offerings in the eastern direction. Ages 9, 18, 27, 36, 45, 54, 63, 72, 81, 90, 99 are all greatly auspicious.

When one's current year reaches Umosi, this is Jupiter, the Wood Essence of the East, also called Sheti. Its circumference is one hundred li; it governs the territory of Lu and Wei. When it reaches one's natal destiny, one will gain promotion and increase in salary, and all matters will be auspicious. Its deity has the form of a minister or chancellor, wearing blue garments, with a pig-crown, holding flowers and fruit. The king should always worship with various jewels in the month of mid-spring.

When one's current year reaches this mansion, it is advisable to associate with noble persons, and marriages will be harmonious. This year is one of great auspiciousness. One's natal destiny belongs to the High Granary Heaven. All matters are harmonious, all directions are favorable, coming and going brings profit, one's dwelling is peaceful, and descendants will flourish.

### Mantra of the Seven Stars of the Northern Dipper

*Namaḥ samanta-nārāṇa nārāṇa-paca-rā hūṃ.*

### General Mantra of the Nine Grahas

*Namaḥ. Oṃ graha-aśrīrya-prapta-ti-rāmāya svāhā.*

The eclipse deity's head: from the first month to the end of the year, it always resides in two mansions, the Wing Mansion and the Extended-Net Mansion, and the Tail Mansion and the Low Mansion. The eclipse deity's tail: from the first month to the end of the year, it always resides in these two mansions. According to the Yusi scripture: ordinary people only know of the seven luminaries and are not aware of the hidden stars called Rāhu and Ketu. These stars are in hidden positions and are invisible; when they encounter the sun or moon, an eclipse occurs, and they are called eclipse deities. Ketu is the tail of the eclipse deity, called Leopard Tail. If one's current year reaches this mansion, one must paint the form of the offended deity, worship it in a secluded room, and burn spirit-money to avert it; then calamity and harm will not arise. When one encounters an evil star, one must avert it. If one does not go against the offended deity, misfortune will be transformed into fortune. If one does not believe, fortune will be transformed into misfortune. When encountering an auspicious star, joyful celebrations come in layers, and blessings and virtue are self-abiding. When encountering an evil star, calamities and harms arise in competition; even princes and lords who offend it will be banished from office and demoted. One should face the Northern Dipper at the hour of the pig [9-11 PM] and worship one's natal star with utmost sincerity. One must never urinate facing north, for this shortens one's lifespan. One should think truthful thoughts and contemplate the good, and one will obtain blessings, good fortune, and wealth. If one does not avert it, calamities and harms will arise in competition.

## Immortal Ge's Method of Worshipping the Northern Dipper

Stabilizing the upper mysterious nine stars of the north pole and Northern Dipper: from kings and lords down to scholars and common people, all belong to the seven stars of the Northern Dipper. One should always revere them, and then one will not encounter untimely disasters and evil events. This universally rescues the afflictions of people in the world, enabling them to extend their years and increase their allotted lifespan, free from all calamities and difficulties. If one makes wholehearted offerings to one's natal spirit, all will have their destined fortune fulfilled. Calamity, harm, misfortune, blame, confusion, and obstruction all arise from not revering the star images and not knowing that one has offended the stars. Walking blindly in darkness, calamity and difficulty naturally arrive. Averting them brings great fortune. For the days of worshipping one's natal spirit, there are six days in a year. With utmost sincerity, on one's natal day, use good paper to cut spirit-money according to one's age; use three platters of tea and fruit; set up one clean mat; burn incense with a devoted mind facing the Northern Dipper; bow twice; and announce: "Living in seclusion with few companions, I love the Way and seek the numinous, always wishing to see the honored countenance. On my natal day, I respectfully offer silver coins and immortal fruits to worship the Northern Dipper, the planet-stars, and my natal spirit form. May my life be long, my years extended, free from all untimely disasters. May my spirit-soul be at peace, my primal spirit at ease, and may the afflictions of the year be driven far away." Bow twice, burn the spirit-money, join palms, and make offerings.

The star Army Destroyer holds the Great Pointer.

The star Martial Composition: Guest of the Great East.

The star Purity and Uprightness: Master of Non-Irrigation.

The star Literary Composition: Master of Subtle Wisdom.

The star Salary Preservation: Meeting of Fortune.

The star Great Gate: Master of Upright Literature.

The star Greedy Wolf: Master of Seeking Rarity.

Regarding the Book of Fortune and Destiny:

Those belonging to the star Army Destroyer eat one sheng per day and have a remaining lifespan of eighty years. Men and women born in the year of the horse, their name-character is Dajingzi.

Those belonging to the star Martial Composition eat one shi per day and have a remaining lifespan of eighty-five years. Men and women born in the years of the snake and ram, their name-character is Dadongzi.

Those belonging to the star Purity and Uprightness eat one sheng per day and have a remaining lifespan of eighty years. Men and women born in the years of the dragon and monkey, their name-character is Shubulianzi.

Those belonging to the star Literary Composition eat four sheng per day and have a remaining lifespan of ninety years. Men and women born in the years of the hare and rooster, their name-character is Weihuizi.

Those belonging to the star Salary Preservation eat five sheng per day and have a remaining lifespan of eighty years. Men and women born in the years of the tiger and dog, their name-character is Lucunhuizi.

Those belonging to the star Great Gate eat eight sheng per day and have a remaining lifespan of eighty years. Men and women born in the years of the ox and pig, their name-character is Zhenwenzi.

Those belonging to the star Greedy Wolf eat two sheng per day and have a remaining lifespan of sixty-five years. Men and women born in the year of the rat, their name-character is Sixishenzi.

### Mantra Spoken by All Tathāgatas for Destroying All Obstructions of the Constellations and Bringing Auspiciousness

*Oṃ sarva-nakṣatra-samaye śrīye śānti-kā kuru svāhā.*

Men should beware of the Heavenly Net; women should beware of the Earthly Snare. If one wishes to know decline and misfortune, it lies only in the current year: whatever one has offended, nothing will go unknown. Throughout a person's life-years, auspicious times are few and inauspicious times are many. It is better to know and avoid than to sit and receive calamity. So it is said regarding the inauspicious days.

## Brahmā's Horā Diagram, One Sheet

I respectfully address the Great Brahmā Heavenly King, Śakra the Heavenly King, Yama the Heavenly Prince, the Great Spirit of the Five Paths, the Lord Governor of Mount Tai, the Directors of Destiny and Fortune, the twelve zodiacal spirits, the seven luminaries and nine grahas, the twenty-eight lunar mansions, the yakṣas and yakṣa-women, the piśācas and piśācīs, the bhūtas, and the nāga-devas, as well as all the protective dharma-spirits who guard the local territories, the great and small spirits that guard the dwelling, and all beings of the six destinies and four modes of birth in the three-thousand great-thousand worlds, dust-realms beyond speech and description. I, so-and-so, on this auspicious day and favorable hour of this month, establish a spirit offering and an unrestricted feast. Those of great aspiration may know the heart of this.

Next, face east with palms joined for the invitation. The above is complete.

One, two, three, four, five, six, seven, eight, nine.

This is called the Method of All Lesser Deva Mantras.

General mantra for all devas:

*Oṃ loka-kālu-yaye svāhā.*
