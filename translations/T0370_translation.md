# 阿彌陀鼓音聲王陀羅尼經
## Amitābha Drum-Sound King Dhāraṇī Sūtra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0370

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the great city of Campā, at the Gagā Spirit Pool, together with a great assembly of five hundred bhikṣus.

At that time, the World-Honored One told the bhikṣus: "I shall now teach you. In the west, in the world of Sukhāvatī, there is now a Buddha called Amitābha. If any of the four assemblies can rightly receive and uphold that Buddha's name, by this merit, at the time when their life is about to end, Amitābha will come with a great assembly to that person and cause them to see him. Having seen him, they will immediately give rise to joy and celebration, and their merit will be doubled. By this cause, wherever they are reborn, they will forever leave behind the foul form of birth from the womb and will dwell only in fresh and wondrous jeweled lotus flowers, born by spontaneous transformation, endowed with great supernatural powers and blazing radiance.

"At that time, the Buddhas of the ten directions, as numerous as the sands of the Ganges, will all together praise the world of Sukhāvatī: 'All the buddha-dharmas therein are inconceivable; the supernatural manifestations and various skillful means are inconceivable. If there are those who can believe such things, know that those persons are inconceivable, and the karmic recompense they obtain is also inconceivable.'

"The Buddha Amitābha, together with his śrāvakas, is a Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha. His land is called Qingtai. It is the abode of a holy king, and its city extends ten thousand yojanas in length and breadth, filled with the kṣatriya lineage. The Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha Amitābha: his father was named Moon-Superior, a cakravartin king; his mother was named Supremely Wondrous Countenance; his son was named Moon-Bright. His attendant disciple was named Vimalakīrti. His wisdom disciple was named Worthy Radiance. His disciple foremost in supernatural diligence was named Great Transformation. At that time, the Māra-king was named Invincible. There was also a Devadatta, named Tranquil. The Buddha Amitābha was together with a great assembly of sixty thousand bhikṣus.

"If there are those who receive and uphold that Buddha's name, with a firm and resolute mind, remembering without forgetting, for ten days and ten nights abandoning distraction, diligently cultivating the buddha-mindfulness samādhi, knowing that the Tathāgata constantly abides in the world of Sukhāvatī, with continuous mindfulness never letting it be interrupted, receiving, upholding, reading, and reciting this Drum-Sound King Great Dhāraṇī for ten days and ten nights, six times each day and night with single-pointed focus, prostrating with the five limbs touching the ground in reverence to that Buddha, with firm and right mindfulness eliminating all distraction: if they can make their mindfulness unbroken thought after thought, within ten days they will certainly see the Buddha Amitābha, and will also see the Tathāgatas of all the worlds of the ten directions and the places where they dwell. Only those with heavy obstructions and dull faculties will be unable to perceive this in such a short time. Dedicating all wholesome deeds, aspiring to be reborn in the world of Sukhāvatī: at the day of their death, the Buddha Amitābha and the great assembly will appear before that person, comfort them, and praise them. That person will then be filled with joy and celebration. By this cause, in accordance with their vow, they will immediately be reborn."

The Buddha told the bhikṣus: "What is the Drum-Sound King Great Dhāraṇī? I shall now teach it. Listen well."

"We accept the teaching."

Then the World-Honored One spoke the dhāraṇī:

*Tadyathā: pari apari samāpara-ṇi diśa-niñja-dani nirmoci nirmoci-jala-bhara-carya-dhani sukha-pati-niḍi-śa amita-yubhari amita-ja-gavani hari amita-ja-para-sādani nepati akāśa-nipata akāśa-niḍiśa akāśa-nijāti akāśa-kuśali akāśa-daśani akāśa-tiratna-ni rūpani-diśa-rūpa-tandhi ceśa-caturi dharma-para-sādani ceśa-caturi ari-ja-sati-ja-para-sādani ceśa-caturi mārga-vana-para-sādani vīra-vīrya-para-sādani dharma-saṃthāni kuśali kuśala-ni-diśa kuśala-prati-tathāni buddha-kuśali vibuddha-para-paṃsita-dharma-kāla-ni-nijānti-nibhūti vimali viraja rāja rāja-śirasa gara-sāgara-bhari rāja-sāgara-atitāni kuśali prati-kuśali vikuśali tati-śuddha-taciti śu-para-śata-taciti śu-para-ticiti-śu-riti śu-muktita-dhame tata-tati-riti vaca-vari anuśa-vari buddha-kāśani-jumi buddha-kāśa-jumi svāhā.*

"This is the Amitābha Drum-Sound King Great Dhāraṇī. If any bhikṣu, bhikṣuṇī, upāsaka, or upāsikā, with constant sincerity, receives, upholds, reads, and recites it, and practices in accordance with this teaching: they should dwell in a quiet and secluded place, bathe the body, wear new and clean garments, eat pure and white food, abstain from wine, meat, and the five pungent vegetables, and always cultivate the brahma-conduct. With fine incense and flowers, they should make offerings to the Tathāgata Amitābha and the great bodhisattvas of the Buddha's assembly. One should always, with single-pointed focus, concentrate the mind, make vows and aspire to be reborn in the world of Sukhāvatī, and practice with diligence and without slackening. In accordance with one's vow, one will certainly be reborn in that buddha-world.

"At that time, the Buddha Amitābha and the great assembly sit upon jeweled lotus flowers. The groves and gardens of that land have fresh flowers and fruits in abundant display, interspersed and adorned. There are also king-trees, fragrant breezes fanning forth harmonious and elegant sounds, all proclaiming the unsurpassed, inconceivable Dharma. There are also wondrous fragrances called Radiance, and various kinds of paste-incenses, all of which are jeweled fragrances.

"The Buddha Amitābha sits in the lotus position upon a great jeweled flower. There are two bodhisattvas: one named Avalokiteśvara and the other named Mahāsthāmaprāpta. These two bodhisattvas stand in attendance at the left and right, and innumerable bodhisattvas surround him on every side. In this assembly, if anyone can have deep faith without doubt, they will certainly be reborn in the land of Amitābha. Its ground is made of true gold, and seven-jeweled lotus flowers naturally spring forth.

"If any of the four assemblies receive, uphold, read, and recite that Buddha's name, they will be free from the terrors of water, fire, poison, weapons, and swords; and also free from the terrors of yakṣas and such beings. Except for those with past heavy sins and karmic obstructions, within at most seven lifetimes, their wishes will certainly be fulfilled."

When the Buddha taught this Amitābha Drum-Sound King Dhāraṇī, immeasurable sentient beings all made vows aspiring to be reborn in the world of Sukhāvatī. Then the World-Honored One praised them: "Excellent, excellent! As you have vowed, you will certainly be reborn there."

Having heard what the Buddha taught, the devas, nāgas, and the eightfold assembly joyfully leaped with delight, bowed in reverence, and practiced accordingly.
