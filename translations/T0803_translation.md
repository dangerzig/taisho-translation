# 佛說清淨心經
## Sūtra Spoken by the Buddha on the Pure Mind

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 803

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dānapāla (施護) et al.

---

At one time, the Buddha, the World-Honored One, was dwelling in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍada's Park, in the land of Śrāvastī, together with an assembly of bhikṣus.

The Buddha addressed the bhikṣus, saying: "Listen carefully! If any śrāvakas who are practicing the correct path wish to attain a pure mind, they should abandon five dharmas and cultivate seven dharmas, bringing them to complete fulfillment.

"What are the five dharmas? First, sensual desire; second, ill-will; third, sloth and torpor; fourth, restlessness and remorse; fifth, doubt. These five hindrances should be cut off and eliminated.

"What are the seven dharmas? First, the awakening factor of investigation of phenomena; second, the awakening factor of mindfulness; third, the awakening factor of energy; fourth, the awakening factor of joy; fifth, the awakening factor of tranquility; sixth, the awakening factor of concentration; seventh, the awakening factor of equanimity. These seven dharmas should be cultivated.

"O bhikṣus! As for what is called the pure mind, know that it is another name for liberation of mind and liberation through wisdom. Due to defilement by craving, the mind is not pure; due to defilement by ignorance, wisdom is not pure.

"If bhikṣus cut off and eliminate the defilement of craving, they attain liberation of mind. If they cut off and eliminate ignorance, they attain liberation through wisdom.

"Furthermore, O bhikṣus, one who is free from the defilement of craving and has attained liberation of mind; this is called direct realization in the body. One who has cut off and eliminated ignorance and attained liberation through wisdom; this is called a non-learner. Having forever abandoned craving and attachment, thoroughly understanding what is true, with right knowledge present before them, they directly realize their own fruit and reach the very end of suffering.

"O bhikṣus! As has been taught, you should train accordingly."
