# 如來示教勝軍王經
## Sūtra of the Tathāgata's Teaching for King Prasenajit

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0515

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Xuanzang (玄奘)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Bhagavān was in the city of Śrāvastī, dwelling in the Jeta Grove of Anāthapiṇḍada, together with an innumerable assembly of great bhikṣus, devas, and humans.

At that time, King Prasenajit, the lord of Kośala, having fully attained to his honored position and majestic virtue, wished to pay reverence and ask questions of the Buddha. He arrayed his carriage, departed the city, and went to the Tathāgata's dwelling. He dismounted at the stopping-place and walked into the garden. From afar he saw the World-Honored One seated beneath a tree, with extraordinary dignity and beauty, his faculties composed and serene, his mind in tranquil repose. He had well attained the ultimate of the supreme regulation and cessation. He had well reached the far shore of the foremost regulation and cessation. He could skillfully guard, conceal, and subdue the faculties. He was a dragon among men, a bull-king among men, a thoroughbred among men. He was pure and untroubled, like a clear spring pool. His majestic virtue blazed and his radiance shone, like a great golden mountain. The thirty-two marks of a great person adorned him in fullness, and the eighty minor marks embellished his limbs.

King Prasenajit, seeing the Buddha from afar, was so overjoyed that he could not contain himself. He then removed the five regal insignia that attend a kṣatriya consecrated king: first, the jeweled crown upon his head; second, his divine sword; third, his canopy adorned with many treasures; fourth, his maṇi flywhisk; fifth, his woven jeweled shoes. Having removed these, he went to the Tathāgata's dwelling. Arriving, he bowed his head to the World-Honored One's feet, circumambulated him three times to the right, and sat to one side.

At that time, King Prasenajit, having sat to one side, said to the Buddha: "Greatly Compassionate World-Honored One! May the Tathāgata mercifully instruct me. May the Sugata mercifully admonish me, so that through the long night I may realize great benefit, welfare, and happiness."

At that time, the World-Honored One said to the great king: "Excellent! Excellent, great king! You are now able to ask the Tathāgata about such a great matter. Great King! You are now the sovereign of a great country. You should govern with right Dharma and not with wrong Dharma. You should act in accordance with Dharma and not in accordance with non-Dharma. Why?

"Great King, you should know! If there are kings or their subordinates who abandon right Dharma and use wrong Dharma to rule the country, they will be condemned and reproached by all sages and saints in the present life, and afterward will have cause for regret. When their bodies perish and their lives end, they will fall into the evil destinies and be born in the hells, where they will suffer intense pain. Furthermore, if kings or their subordinates abandon wrong Dharma and use right Dharma to rule the country, they will be praised by all sages and saints in the present life, and afterward will have no regret. When their bodies perish and their lives end, they will ascend to the good destinies and be born among the devas, where they will enjoy wondrous happiness.

"Great King! It is like parents who love and pity their children, always wishing them to be free from suffering and to obtain happiness. A king should be likewise. Toward all the beings in your lands and cities, your servants, attendants, ministers, and officials, you should employ the four methods of attraction taught by all Buddhas to guide and govern them. What are the four? First, generosity. Second, kind words. Third, beneficial actions. Fourth, cooperation in common endeavor. By skillfully governing the beings of your lands and cities in this way, along with your servants, attendants, ministers, and officials, you embrace two kinds of benefit leading to the same goal. What are the two kinds? First, the present life. Second, future lives.

"Great King, you should know! It is like a man or woman who, in a dream, sees lovely gardens, lovely mountain valleys, lovely lands and cities, and various other things. When they awaken, everything they saw is gone. In the same way, great king, the sovereignty of the realm and the life of the body are false and impermanent. All is just as what is seen in a dream.

"Great King, you should know! The happiness of a king's wealth and honor, the happiness of a king's sovereignty, the happiness of a king's sensual pleasures, and all other enjoyments of sensual pleasure, such as elephant, horse, chariot, and infantry forces, palaces, queens, crown princes, subordinate kings, ministers, officials, guards, soldiers, father, mother, brothers, sisters, wives, concubines, male and female attendants, great and small servants, the people of the cities and lands, gold, silver, precious treasures, maṇi gems, pearls, clothing, grain, and the contents of the storehouses: all these enjoyments of sensual pleasure, at the time of death, must all be abandoned. One goes alone to the next world; not a single one of them accompanies you.

"Great King, you should know! All the enjoyments described above are not permanent, not eternal, and not to be trusted. They are subject to change, swift and unceasing. They flow, shift, and move, finally ending in destruction, changing from moment to moment, in the end becoming ashes. They all proceed toward the gate of complete cessation. They are subject to loss and decay, attended by fears and disasters, full of grief and distress. They are all things that fall, crumble, break, and scatter.

"Great King, you should know! It is like seeing a forest of trees first blossom, then bear fruit, then lose its fruit. First the leaves are seen, green and luxuriant; soon they wither and yellow, and in the end they all fall. In the same way, great king, the sovereignty of the realm, the body, and life, and the happiness of a king's wealth, sovereignty, and sensual pleasures, and all other enjoyments of sensual pleasure, such as elephant, horse, chariot, and infantry forces, and so forth, in full detail down to all being things that fall, crumble, break, and scatter, are likewise just so.

"Great King, you should know! It is like a great mass of fire. First it blazes, then it blazes more fiercely, then it spreads in conflagration, and then it burns through completely. Though it blazes long and intensely, in the end it is extinguished. In the same way, great king, the sovereignty of the realm, the body, and life, and all enjoyments of sensual pleasure, and so forth, in full detail down to all being things that fall, crumble, break, and scatter, are likewise just so.

"Great King, you should know! The orbs of sun and moon possess great supernatural function and great power. They emit great radiance to adorn themselves and illuminate the whole world, yet in the end they must set. In the same way, great king, all enjoyments of sensual pleasure, and so forth, are likewise just so.

"Great King, you should know! It is like a great cloud covering the sky, with violent wind, sudden thunder, flashing lightning, and pouring rain, shaking heaven and earth, yet in a moment it has scattered and vanished. In the same way, great king, all enjoyments of sensual pleasure, and so forth, are likewise just so. Therefore, great king, you should diligently cultivate contemplation of impermanence and contemplation of cessation and extinction. You should always be alarmed and fearful of the end of your own life. As a great king, you should govern with right Dharma and not with non-Dharma. You should act in accordance with Dharma and not in accordance with non-Dharma.

"Great King, you should know! It is like four great mountains approaching from the four directions, solid and dense, without any gap or fissure, filling and covering everywhere, merging into one mass, reaching from the sky above to the foundation of the earth below. All the grasses, trees, branches, flowers, and leaves within, and all sentient and crawling beings, would all be ground to dust. It would be difficult to escape by courage, difficult to resist by force, and impossible to stop by means of spells, wealth, or medicine. In the same way, great king, there are four kinds of great terror in the world, each coming to grind down all beings. They are difficult to escape by courage, difficult to resist by force, and impossible to stop by means of spells, wealth, or medicine.

"What are the four kinds of great terror? Great King, you should know! First, old age approaches and crushes the youth and vigor of beings. Second, sickness approaches and crushes the well-being of beings. Third, death approaches and crushes the lifespan of beings. Fourth, decline approaches and crushes the prosperity of beings.

"Great King, you should know! It is like a lion, the king of beasts, who enters a herd of deer and seizes one deer. Whether it has been eaten or not, the lion acts freely and without difficulty. That deer, once in the lion's jaws, whatever power it had before is of no use. In the same way, great king, once all beings have entered the jaws of the lion-king of impermanence, whatever power they possessed is of no use.

"Great King, you should know! It is like a person of great courage and strength who is struck by a poisoned arrow, and all their fierce might is completely destroyed. In the same way, great king, all beings, however stubborn and headstrong, once struck by the arrow of death, have no more power, no more rescue, no more refuge, and nowhere to flee. When they are about to give up their lives and their joints come apart, their blood and flesh are dried up, their hearts and chests burn with agony, scorched with thirst, gasping with mouths open, hands and feet flailing in disorder, unable to do anything, without any power, mucus and tears streaming, soiled by their own waste. The six sense-organs close up, the throat chokes, breathing grows ever more labored. The skilled physician puts down his hands and abandons all excellent medicines. Whatever delicious foods and fine provisions they may have, all are given up. They lie upon their beds, about to depart for another destiny, sinking endlessly into the raging torrent of birth, old age, sickness, and death. At the point of death with only moments remaining, because the power of karma is strong, the next existence appears before them. Terrified by the messengers of Yama the king of death, swallowed by the vast darkness of night, the outgoing and incoming breaths are about to cease for the last time. Utterly alone, with no companion or second person, they suddenly depart this life and go to the next world. This is a great transfer into a great thicket, entering a great darkness, wandering in a great wilderness, floating upon a great ocean, blown by the wind of karma, heading toward a place with no landmarks, a desolate and silent direction. At that time, there is no other rescue, no other refuge, nowhere else to flee, except right Dharma. Great King! At that time, only right Dharma can serve as rescue, as a dwelling, as a refuge, as the place to run to, as the place to flee to. It can deliver beings from the suffering of birth and death.

"Great King, you should know! It is like a person oppressed by cold who can find relief only in a warm fire, sunlight, or clothing. One oppressed by heat finds relief only in forests and springs. One traveling a long road finds relief only in cool shade. One oppressed by thirst finds relief only in cool water. One oppressed by hunger finds relief only in ample fine food. One oppressed by illness finds relief only in a good physician, medicine, and attendants. One oppressed by fear finds relief only in strong companions. In the same way, great king, all beings, struck by the arrow of death, without power, without rescue, without refuge, without anywhere to flee, at the point of giving up their lives and their joints coming apart, their blood and flesh drying up, their hearts burning with agony, scorched with thirst, gasping with mouths open, hands and feet in disorder, unable to do anything, in full detail, at that time only right Dharma can serve as rescue, dwelling, and refuge, as the place to run to and the place to flee to. It can deliver beings from the suffering of birth and death. Why?

"Great King, you should know! This body, though guarded and protected, though washed and adorned for a long time, though filled to satisfaction with the finest foods and drinks, at the time of death, inevitably suffers the oppression of hunger and thirst and then gives up its life.

"Great King, you should know! This body, though constantly bathed, anointed with scented paste, powdered incense, burning incense, and flower garlands, adorned at will, because this body is by its nature foul and leaking, at the time of giving up its life, inevitably returns to impurity and stench.

"Great King, you should know! This body, though covered and wrapped with the finest garments, because this body is composed of various impure substances, at the time of death, various impurities inevitably flow forth.

"Great King, you should know! This body, though dwelling in a palace surrounded by queens, consorts, palace ladies, courtesans, and attendants, with many kinds of music, singing, and entertainment, enjoying pleasure and delight, at the time of death one sees terrible portents and inevitably experiences terror, grief, and anguish.

"Great King, you should know! This body, though dwelling in palaces with colorful paintings, multi-storied towers, doors and windows with elaborate latticework, heavily barred and sealed, adorned with various fragrant flowers, lit by various lamps and torches to increase brightness, with rare screens and curtains, burning famous incenses and scattering wondrous flowers, with fragrant flower vases placed everywhere, furnished with various beds and seats of gold, silver, lapis lazuli, and many treasures, spread with the finest carpets, brocades, embroidered coverlets, and flower-patterned felts, with crimson pillows placed at both ends of the bed, covered with the finest quilts of gauze and brocade, sleeping in comfort and amusement and attachment: after death, there is no more awareness. The corpse may be sent to the burial ground, where crows, magpies, vultures, eagles, foxes, jackals, hungry dogs, owls, and various evil birds and beasts vie to eat the flesh. Bones, flesh, pus, blood, hair, and filth flow upon the ground, stinking and repulsive.

"Great King, you should know! This body, which formerly rode upon fragrant elephants, fine horses, and jeweled palanquins, with drums beaten and conches blown, making great music, with jeweled canopies and parasols following behind, with attendants holding flywhisks that sway and shimmer, with innumerable brave elephant troops, horse troops, chariot troops, and infantry troops leading and following, guards on left and right, hundreds of thousands of ministers serving with utmost devotion, while the people of cities and towns joined their palms in awe: though one received such supreme and wondrous rewards, such sovereignty and glory, before long one must close the eyes and let go the hands, motionless, the corpse lying face-up on the funeral carriage, borne by the multitude out through the great city gates. Father, mother, wife, children, brothers, sisters, servants, attendants, ministers, officials, relatives, and household members follow on either side, their hearts bound in grief, their hair disheveled, raising their hands to beat their heads, pounding their chests and choking with sobs, wailing with shattered grief, all crying 'How painful!' The people of the cities and lands weep and mourn as they follow the funeral procession to the burial ground. Some expose the corpse for the birds and beasts: crows, magpies, hungry dogs, vultures, eagles, owls, foxes, jackals, and various evil beasts snatch and tear at the flesh, bones and flesh scattered about, dried by wind and sun, soaked by rain, encrusted with frost, joints coming apart and scattered in different places. Some pile up firewood and cremate the body. Sinews and bones scorch and crumble, blood and flesh dissolve, the stench of smoke billows and fills the air on every side. The fire dies, the bones dissolve, the ashes scatter in the dust. Some dig the earth and bury the body in a tomb. After a long time, the flesh decays and the bones rot.

"Great King, you should know! This body changes and decays in just this way, and is impermanent. All sentient beings and all conditioned things are also like this: not permanent, not eternal, not to be trusted. They are subject to change, swift and unceasing. They flow, shift, and move, finally ending in destruction, changing from moment to moment, in the end becoming ashes. They all proceed toward the gate of complete cessation. They are subject to loss and decay, attended by fears and disasters, full of grief and distress. They are all things that fall, crumble, break, and scatter.

"Therefore, great king, you should diligently cultivate contemplation of impermanence and contemplation of cessation and extinction. Do not be defiled by greed and desire. Do not be stirred by anger. Do not be obscured by delusion. Do not cling to the happiness of a king's wealth and honor. Do not cling to the happiness of a king's sovereignty. Do not cling to the happiness of a king's sensual pleasures. Do not cling to other enjoyments of sensual pleasure. You should extinguish the pride of possessions and life. You should be alarmed and fearful of the demon of impermanent death, who arrives suddenly without appointment. As a great sovereign, you should govern with right Dharma and not with non-Dharma. You should act in accordance with Dharma and not in accordance with non-Dharma. You should have compassion for all beings as for an only child. You should sincerely uphold and protect the Buddha-Dharma and perpetuate the Three Treasures without distraction. Why? Because in the ocean of birth and death, transmigration has no beginning. Only the Buddha's right Dharma is the great ford and bridge, and even in a thousand million kalpas it is truly difficult to encounter.

"Great King, you should know! I never say that one who obtains worldly enjoyments of sensual pleasure, accumulating and enjoying them, is truly wealthy and honored. I say that one who obtains the holy wisdom-treasure of the right Dharma of all Buddhas, accumulating and enjoying it, is the one who is called perfectly and truly wealthy and honored. Therefore, great king, you should become weary of and turn away from all worldly enjoyments of sensual pleasure. You should aspire to and seek the holy wisdom-treasure of the right Dharma of all Buddhas."

When the Bhagavān had finished teaching this sūtra, King Prasenajit, lord of Kośala, and all the devas, humans, and others in the world, having heard what the Buddha taught, were all greatly overjoyed. They received it with faith and practiced accordingly.
