# 佛說陀隣尼鉢經
## The Buddha's Teaching: Sūtra of the Dhāraṇī-Bowl

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1352

Translated from the Chinese. Eastern Jin dynasty, translated by Dharmarakṣa/Tánwúlán (曇無蘭).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was in the country of Śrāvastī, in the Jeta Grove at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park, together with a great assembly of twelve hundred and fifty bhikṣus and ten thousand bodhisattvas. At that time, beyond a hundred thousand koṭi-niyutas of buddha-lands from this buddha-realm, there is a world called Ānandakūrma. Its buddha is named Ekapratikhyā Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha, who is presently in the world. He sent two bodhisattvas, one named Amitā-Dharma and the other Mahā-Dharma. At that time the two bodhisattvas came to the Buddha, bowed their heads at the Buddha's feet, knelt with hands joined, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! Beyond a hundred thousand koṭi-niyutas of buddha-lands from here, there is a world called Flower-Heap. Its buddha, named Supreme Deva-King Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha, is presently in the world. He sent us to inquire of the World-Honored One: 'Is the teaching of the Dharma peaceful and secure? Are those who receive it progressing, all without trouble? Are they not being disturbed by devas, nāgas, yakṣas, ghosts and spirits, vetāḍas, kumbhāṇḍa spirits, rākṣasa spirits, tigers, wolves, or humans and non-humans?' That Tathāgata has sent us bearing the Dhāraṇī-Bowl, for the sake of all, wishing to bestow peace and security, renowned fame heard far and wide, beautiful appearance, strength and vigor. It is as follows:

*Jāri mahā-jāri jā-rā-ṇi ukhi mukhe saṃpati mahā-saṃpati.*

Then the Buddha addressed Ānanda: "Receive this Dhāraṇī-Bowl. Uphold, chant, recite, and study it. It is exceedingly difficult to encounter a buddha, the world-honored one, and likewise difficult to hear the Dhāraṇī-Bowl. If good men or good women receive and uphold it, chanting, reciting, and studying, they will know the past lives of seven lifetimes. If good men or good women receive and uphold it, chanting, reciting, and studying, no ghost, spirit, human or non-human, snake, viper, or scorpion will be able to harm them. Poisons will not strike, sorcery-paths will not function. They will not be injured by swords and weapons. Emperors will find no way to oppress them, and Brahmā will not be angered by them. Ānanda! This Dhāraṇī-Bowl has been spoken by seventy-seven koṭis of buddhas. If anyone is harmed in spite of it, then the words of all those buddhas are untrue."

Ajita Bodhisattva, whose name is Maitreya, addressed the Venerable Ānanda: "I too shall speak a Dhāraṇī-Bowl. Why? Likewise to bestow peace and security upon all, renowned fame heard far and wide, beautiful appearance, and abundant strength and vigor. It is as follows:

*Aṭ-ṭhi baṭ-ṭhi me-ji chā-rī re-ji rā-laṃ mi-lu-laṃ mi-he-rī mi-rī ti-rī.*

Then the Buddha addressed Ānanda: "Receive this Dhāraṇī-Bowl. Uphold, chant, recite, and study it. It is exceedingly difficult to encounter a buddha, the world-honored one, and likewise difficult to hear this Dhāraṇī-Bowl. If good men or good women receive this Dhāraṇī-Bowl, reverently uphold, chant, recite, and study it, they will know the past lives of fourteen lifetimes. If good men or good women reverently uphold the Dhāraṇī-Bowl, chanting, reciting, studying, and teaching it, they will never be touched or disturbed by any ghosts or spirits, humans or non-humans. Snakes, vipers, scorpions, and all venomous creatures will be unable to harm them. Poisons will not strike, sorcery-paths will not function. Swords and weapons will be unable to wound them. Emperors will find no way to oppress them. Brahmā, Śakra, and the Four Heavenly Kings will all together guard and protect them. Ānanda! This Dhāraṇī-Bowl has been spoken by eighty-four thousand koṭis of buddhas."

The Buddha addressed Ānanda: "I too wish to speak a Dhāraṇī-Bowl, to bestow peace and security upon all, renowned fame heard far and wide, beautiful and upright appearance, and abundant strength and vigor. It is as follows:

*Aṭ-ṭhi baṭ-ṭhi neṭ-ṭhi ku-neṭ-ṭhi te-rī chā-rā pa-ti an-nā-pa-ti pā-nā-pa-ti ka-je-nī mahā-je-nī.*

Then the Buddha addressed the Venerable Ānanda: "Receive this Dhāraṇī-Bowl. Uphold, chant, recite, and study it. Broadly teach it for all. If good men or good women receive this Dhāraṇī-Bowl, uphold, chant, recite, and study it, they will know the past lives of incalculable lifetimes. This Dhāraṇī-Bowl, Ānanda, has been spoken by an uncountable number of koṭis of buddhas. Ānanda! This Dhāraṇī-Bowl: whether one is traveling on a road, or is among bandits, or encounters tigers and wolves, or is upon the water, or faces trouble with emperors and officials, one should recite this Dhāraṇī-Bowl, chanting, reciting, studying, and upholding it. This Dhāraṇī-Bowl, Ānanda, can cause a withered tree tied with it to immediately sprout leaves, flowers, and fruit. How much more so for one who recites it for a sick person, whose illness will not be cured? One should use it as a mantra for all the sick."

When the Buddha had spoken this sūtra, the Venerable Ānanda and all in the assembly joyfully received it and put it into practice.
