# 佛說十二佛名神呪校量功德除障滅罪經
## The Buddha's Teaching: Sūtra on the Twelve Buddha-Names and Divine Mantra, Comparing Their Merit in Removing Obstacles and Eliminating Sins

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1348

Translated from the Chinese. Sui dynasty, translated by Jñānagupta (闍那崛多).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Bhagavat was dwelling in the city of Rājagṛha on Mount Gṛdhrakūṭa, together with a great assembly of one thousand two hundred and fifty bhikṣus, and also a great assembly of twelve thousand bodhisattvas, with Ajita Bodhisattva as the foremost.

Then the World-Honored One addressed Maitreya Bodhisattva: "Maitreya! To the east of this buddha-field, passing beyond buddha-fields equal in number to the atoms in ten ineffably many hundreds of thousands of koṭis of buddha-fields, there is a buddha-land called Liberation-Lord World. Within that world there is a buddha named Ākāśa-guṇa-viśuddhi-paramāṇu-sama-netra-sundaraguṇa-lakṣaṇa-prabhā-puṣpa-padma-vaiḍūrya-prabhā-ratnakāya-gandha-uttama-gandha-pūjā-kṛta-nānā-vyūha-cūḍā-ananta-aparimāṇa-sūryacandra-prabhā-praṇidhāna-vyūha-vikurvita-vyūha-dharmadhātu-udbhava-anāvaraṇa-rāja Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha. He is presently abiding there, wandering freely as he pleases, and teaching the Dharma wherever he goes.

"If any good man or good woman has committed the four grave offenses, the five acts of immediate retribution, slander of the Three Jewels, or the four pārājikas, the sins of such a person are heavy. Suppose the earth of Jambudvīpa were reduced to fine atoms, and each atom became one kalpa: that person would have sins equal to that many kalpas. Yet if they call upon the name of this single buddha and make one prostration, all these sins will be eliminated. How much more so for one who day and night receives, upholds, reads, recites, remembers, and does not forget! The merit of such a person is inconceivable.

"Within that buddha-world there is a bodhisattva named Anupama. The Tathāgata Anāvaraṇa-rāja has given that bodhisattva a prediction that they will attain buddhahood, with the name Keśa-lakṣaṇa-sūryacandra-prabhā-puṣpa-ratna-padma-dṛḍha-vajrakāya-vairocana-anāvaraṇa-netra-paripūrṇa-daśadiś-raśmi-prabhāsa-sarva-buddhakṣetra-lakṣaṇa-rāja Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha, Sugata, Knower of the World, Unsurpassed One, Tamer of Humans, Teacher of Devas and Humans, Buddha, World-Honored One.

"In the east there is also a buddha named Sarva-vyūha-vimala-prabhā Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha. One should also call upon that buddha's name, cultivate reverence, recite his name, and be mindful of his name in one's heart.

"In the south there is a buddha named Pratibhāna-keyūra-smṛti Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha. One should also call upon that buddha's name, cultivate reverence, recite his name, and be mindful of his name in one's heart.

"In the west there is a buddha named Vimala-candra-lakṣaṇa-rāja-yaśas Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha. One should also call upon that buddha's name, cultivate reverence, recite his name, and be mindful of his name in one's heart.

"In the north there is a buddha named Puṣpa-vyūha-kṛta-prabhā Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha. One should also call upon that buddha's name, cultivate reverence, recite his name, and be mindful of his name in one's heart.

"In the southeast there is a buddha named Dīpaṃkara Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha. One should also call upon that buddha's name, cultivate reverence, recite his name, and be mindful of his name in one's heart.

"In the southwest there is a buddha named Ratnottama-lakṣaṇa-yaśas Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha. One should also call upon that buddha's name, cultivate reverence, recite his name, and be mindful of his name in one's heart.

"In the northwest there is a buddha named Abhaya-darśin Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha. One should also call upon that buddha's name, cultivate reverence, recite his name, and be mindful of his name in one's heart.

"In the northeast there is a buddha named Abhaya-atrasya-romakūpa-notsaṅgin-yaśas Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha. One should also call upon that buddha's name, cultivate reverence, recite his name, and be mindful of his name in one's heart.

"In the nadir there is a buddha named Siṃha-vikrānta-gāmin Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha. One should also call upon that buddha's name, cultivate reverence, recite his name, and be mindful of his name in one's heart.

"In the zenith there is a buddha named Suvarṇa-prabhā-tejarāja-sadṛśa Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha. One should also call upon that buddha's name, cultivate reverence, recite his name, and be mindful of his name in one's heart."

Then the Buddha addressed Maitreya: "If any good man or good woman of right faith, while calling upon the names of these twelve buddhas, practices for ten days repenting of all sins, rejoicing in the merit of all sentient beings, requesting all buddhas to long abide in the world, and transferring all wholesome roots to the Dharma-realm, at that time all their sins will be eliminated and all their karmic obstructions purified. They will accomplish and adorn all buddha-fields, attain complete fearlessness, accomplish the adornment of bodily characteristics, accomplish a retinue of bodhisattvas surrounding them, accomplish immeasurable dhāraṇīs, accomplish immeasurable samādhis, accomplish the adornment of buddha-fields at will, and accomplish immeasurable good friends. They will swiftly accomplish all that has been described above, neither more nor less, practicing anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi within the midst of afflictions, and will attain the karmic reward of being handsome and delightful. They will also have abundant wealth and treasures, will always be born into families of great lineage and noble clan, will have perfect physical characteristics, and will be surrounded by harmonious relatives."

Then the World-Honored One, restating this meaning, spoke in verse:

> If any good man,
> Or good woman,
> Receives and upholds these buddha-names,
> In life after life,
> They will be loved and respected by others,
> With great radiant majestic power.
> In every place of birth they will be honored among people,
> And in the end they will attain buddhahood.

The Buddha further addressed Maitreya: "If any good man or good woman receives and upholds these buddha-names, they should also recite this dhāraṇī mantra:

*Tadyathā: akime-kime samanta-mukhe saute yukate nirukate nirukate bhabhi samāyu-gi citta-vibhati akime-kime mādhani vibhati samanta-kuni satya-rāmi yukite ba-yukite hiri-miri māsire anāme-cāme acci-ci macci-ci buci arā-he bha-śu-he cā-yugara-he-he mābhati śute mate dharma-cinti cūta-bhaṃki abhi-ccā-bhi arāda-bhakṣami saṃ-gandha-vibhati. Namaḥ sarva-buddha-bodhisattva-bhir-vyā. Siddhantu mantra-padā buddha-adhiṣṭhita.*

When the World-Honored One had spoken this mantra, he again spoke in verse:

> Those who receive and uphold this mantra
> Will encounter sixty koṭi
> Buddhas presently in the ten directions,
> Who constantly remember them.
>
> In every place of rebirth
> They will always encounter good friends.
> Whatever the heart wishes and aspires to,
> All will be auspicious.
>
> Those who recite this dhāraṇī
> Will always be born before the buddhas,
> On wondrous lotus blossoms,
> Transformed-born, not entering the womb.
>
> With right mindfulness, right conduct, and right intention,
> Their wisdom will increase and grow.
> Upon hearing once they will retain all,
> And what they hear they will never forget.
>
> Those who receive and uphold this mantra
> Will obtain merit such as this.
> Those who recite these buddha-names,
> In every place of rebirth,
> Will be far removed from the evil paths
> And swiftly born in wholesome realms.
>
> They will attain dhāraṇī-samādhi,
> The most wondrous and supreme state,
> Receiving and upholding without forgetting,
> All the way to awakening,
> All through the power of the buddha-names
> And this dhāraṇī.
>
> If a person were to give the seven treasures
> Filling a thousand million koṭi buddha-fields,
> Receiving and upholding these buddha-names
> Would surpass the merit of that gift.
>
> Practicing the conduct of awakening,
> Constantly knowing their past lives,
> Far removed from all calamities,
> They will attain a state of freedom.
> Always born in lands where buddhas dwell,
> They will encounter buddhas and hear the true Dharma,
> And attain the mind of faithful practice.
>
> Having attained this mind of faith,
> They will constantly make offerings to the buddhas.
> Having encountered buddhas and heard the Dharma,
> Their hearts will give rise to great joy.
> Having seen all the buddhas,
> Bearing wondrous offering-goods,
> They will make offerings to all the buddhas.
>
> Those who uphold the buddha-names,
> For thousands of millions of koṭi kalpas,
> Will abandon the sins of birth and death
> And swiftly accomplish the path of buddhahood.
>
> If a person can, with utmost sincerity,
> Recite the buddha-names for seven days,
> They will attain the pure eye
> And be able to see immeasurable buddhas.
>
> Those who can uphold these buddha-names
> Will attain immeasurable merit.
> In every place such a person is born,
> Devas and humans will constantly revere them.
>
> If a person upholds the buddha-names,
> For thousands of millions of koṭi kalpas,
> They will not be insulted or humiliated by others.
> In all the worlds
> Their fame will be spread far and wide.
>
> If a person upholds the buddha-names,
> For thousands of millions of koṭi kalpas,
> Devas and humans will constantly make offerings to them.
> No one will be able to slander them,
> And they will swiftly attain the path of buddhahood.
>
> If a person upholds the buddha-names,
> They will not give rise to timidity or weakness.
> Their wisdom will be free from flattery and crookedness,
> And they will always be in the presence of all the buddhas.
>
> If a person upholds the buddha-names,
> In every place of rebirth,
> Devas, nāgas, yakṣas,
> Gandharvas, kiṃnaras,
> Asuras, garuḍas,
> And mahoragas,
> Humans and non-humans,
> Will constantly make offerings to them.
>
> If a person upholds the buddha-names,
> In every place of rebirth, life after life,
> They will always be born in a wealthy and noble family,
> With the marks of a great person fully complete,
> Without miserly or jealous thoughts,
> Brave and strong and loving generosity.
> From all the pores of their body
> Wondrous fragrances will constantly emanate.
> From between their teeth
> Sweet fragrance will also emerge,
> The scent of heavenly red sandalwood
> And various other fragrances.
> For thousands of millions of koṭi kalpas,
> The fragrance will be constant and unceasing.
> In every place of rebirth, life after life,
> Their voice will be like Brahmā's voice,
> And like the kalaviṅka
> And the jīvañjīvaka and other birds.
> Those who uphold the names of the Dharma-King:
> Their merit will also be like this.
>
> If a person upholds the buddha-names,
> They will be born within seven-jeweled flowers,
> With flowers of a thousand koṭi petals,
> Their majestic radiance and characteristics fully complete.
>
> If a person upholds the buddha-names,
> Their parents and all their relatives
> Will be harmonious and free from quarrel and dispute,
> And they will never be separated.
>
> If any woman
> Receives and upholds this sūtra,
> She will abandon the female form
> And be reborn with the body of a wise man.
> Having attained a male body,
> She will accomplish awakening,
> Turn the unsurpassed Dharma-wheel,
> And enter nirvāṇa at will.
>
> If a person upholds the buddha-names,
> Blades and weapons cannot harm them,
> Water and fire cannot burn or sweep them away,
> And officials cannot put them to death.
>
> If a person upholds the buddha-names,
> Māras and Pāpīyas,
> Whether they are walking, standing, sitting, or lying down,
> Cannot gain any advantage over them.
>
> If a person upholds the buddha-names,
> In every place of rebirth, life after life,
> Their body will travel freely through space,
> Able to reach boundless buddha-fields,
> See the buddhas face to face,
> And ask them about the profound meaning.
> Those immeasurable buddhas
> Will know their heart's intent
> And teach them the subtle and wondrous Dharma,
> Giving them predictions of awakening.
>
> Having heard the predictions,
> Their hearts will give rise to great joy,
> And in the teachings of all the buddhas
> They will be decisive and without doubt.
>
> The profound Dharma
> Taught by those buddha-world-honored-ones:
> When that person has heard the Dharma,
> They will receive, uphold, and never lose it.
>
> In the future they will accomplish
> The six pāramitās and the bhūmis,
> The fearlessnesses and the powers,
> The marks and the minor characteristics,
> The buddha-fields and sentient beings,
> Chosen at will as they please.
> Born in those buddha-fields,
> They will swiftly attain perfect awakening,
> Turn the unsurpassed Dharma-wheel,
> And enter nirvāṇa at will.
>
> If any good man
> Or good woman
> Receives and upholds this sūtra,
> They will gain the merit described above.
>
> Those who uphold this sūtra
> Should be careful and not be heedless.
> Having heard of the meritorious karma described above,
> Whether for a full kalpa
> Or for less than a kalpa,
> They should constantly and diligently read and recite,
> Never giving rise to a lazy mind.
>
> I command that it be thus:
> These buddhas
> Are the permanent treasury of merit,
> Kings among the clouds of space,
> Who constantly abide in their places.
>
> If a person hears the buddha-names
> For a kalpa, or less than a kalpa,
> And upon hearing gives rise to reverence,
> That is the most vigorous of persons,
> One of great wisdom,
> The foremost among humans,
> A valiant man,
> Of great fame in the world.
>
> Therefore I now say:
> Maitreya, listen to me!
> A person of great wisdom,
> Skillful in understanding expedient means,
> Constantly diligent and not heedless,
> Always drawing near to good friends,
> Will hear this sūtra
> And then proceed upon the path of awakening.

When the Buddha had spoken this sūtra, Maitreya Bodhisattva Mahāsattva and the assembly of bodhisattvas, the great assembly of bhikṣus, the devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, humans and non-humans, having heard what the Buddha taught, joyfully put it into practice.
