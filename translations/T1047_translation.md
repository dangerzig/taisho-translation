# 佛說聖六字大明王陀羅尼經
## Holy Six-Syllable Great Vidyā-King Dhāraṇī Sūtra Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1047

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dānapāla (施護).

---

Thus have I heard. At one time the World-Honored One was in the country of Śrāvastī, in the Jetavana grove of Anāthapiṇḍada's park. Then the World-Honored One told the venerable Ānanda: "Ānanda, listen carefully. I possess the Six-Syllable Great Vidyā-King Dhāraṇī, which was proclaimed in the past by immeasurable buddhas for the great beings. Ānanda, I now proclaim this dhāraṇī for you and for sentient beings of the final age. It shall enable those sentient beings of the future who obtain this dhāraṇī to gain great benefit."

Then Ānanda, together with Mahābrahmā, sovereign of this sahā world, Śakra, the four great deva kings, and others addressed the World-Honored One: "World-Honored One of great compassion, we beseech you to proclaim it, so that we and the sentient beings of the final age may gain benefit and happiness through the long night."

Then the World-Honored One spoke the Six-Syllable Great Vidyā-King Dhāraṇī:

*Tadyathā nandire madu-mati maṇḍarike pūrṇanike svāhā*

Then the Buddha told Ānanda: "If any sentient being hears the words of this Great Vidyā-King Dhāraṇī, trusts and upholds it, recollects and recites it, and makes offerings of incense, flowers, and various items, that person will be freed from all fears: fear of kings, fear of water and fire, fear of bandits, and fear of enemies. Furthermore, poisons and even weapons cannot cause harm. Furthermore, devas, humans, non-humans, yakṣas, rākṣasas, kumbhāṇḍas, and even kaṭa-pūtanas and their possessions and enchantments cannot cause harm. Furthermore, malarial fevers of one day, two days, three days, four days, or many days, or sudden fevers, as well as headaches, earaches, back pain, stomach pain, and even edema and all malignant diseases cannot cause harm. Day and night one will always be at peace, and all wishes will be fulfilled as desired."

Then the World-Honored One spoke another dhāraṇī:

*Tadyathā aṇari paṇari nāmi namani vākase kusuke kukaccheke bhūta-garaccheti jīvati yaśavati svāhā*

The Buddha told Ānanda: "If any sentient being hears this dhāraṇī, I do not see any deva, nāga, yakṣa, rākṣasa, preta, piśāca, bhūta, kumbhāṇḍa, pūtana, kaṭa-pūtana, or any human or non-human acting with harmful intent who would dare to transgress. Ānanda, if any such harmful being transgresses against my dhāraṇī, their head shall split into seven parts, like the branch of an arjaka tree."

Then the World-Honored One further told Ānanda: "The power and might of this dhāraṇī's merit is immeasurable and boundless, benefiting sentient beings without end. You should uphold it and spread it widely, so that in future ages it shall not be cut off." When the Buddha had spoken this sūtra, Ānanda, Mahābrahmā, Śakra, the four great deva kings, and all who heard the Buddha's teaching rejoiced, accepted it with faith, bowed to the Buddha, and withdrew.
