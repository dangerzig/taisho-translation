# 堅牢地天儀軌
## Ritual of Dṛḍhā, the Earth Deva

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1286

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Śubhākarasiṃha (善無畏).

---

At that time, Dṛḍhā the Earth Deva and the Great Merit Deva both rose from their seats and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! If any bhikṣu, bhikṣuṇī, upāsaka, upāsikā, boy, or girl constantly restrains the five sense-faculties and with focused mindfulness and utmost sincerity contemplates me, the Earth Deva, and the Merit Deva, bowing, reverencing, making offerings, and reciting my mantra, I shall constantly cause the earth's essence to emerge and nourish that person, increasing longevity within their body. This vital energy of the earth shall fill the practitioner's body. They shall obtain beauty and strength, mindfulness, joy, diligence, great wisdom, eloquence, the three insights and six supernatural powers, love and respect from humans and devas, and incomparable and inexhaustible great merit.

"It is like the merit of a wheel-turning sage-king. The practitioner shall dwell wherever they go as if in the pleasure-gardens of Trāyastriṃśa Heaven. In this life, the next life, and through a thousand or ten thousand lives without limit, they shall obtain vast and immeasurable merit. Day after day, they shall be attended and nourished by tens of millions of retinue members. Moreover, immeasurable and innumerable sentient beings in hardship shall be sustained; storehouses shall overflow with the seven treasures without limit: the elephant-jewel, horse-jewel, wheel-jewel, and the wish-fulfilling-woman-jewel, who comes of her own accord without being summoned, transcending birth and death.

"Such a dharma-practicing bhikṣu shall be supplied with various gold, silver, jeweled pearls, various necessities of life, and various grains and rice in full abundance. With such focused mindfulness, from the first watch of the night through the middle and last watches, whether sitting, lying, walking, or standing, contemplating the Dharma with a mind free from all external distractions, the practitioner should: build monasteries, or construct a single building, or plant a grove of trees, or provide food, clothing, bedding, and seats, and for the sick, medicines and various necessities of life, always supplying them. Having obtained these things without lack, the body is at ease and free from anxiety. The mind is constantly joyful, one obtains a fine field of merit and becomes a vessel of the Dharma. Having given thus, in future lives one obtains great fruition, deeply delights in the Dharma, is not born in evil destinies, and does not create evil dharmas. I shall thus protect that person, who will cultivate the mind of compassion, and in this way all manner of supremely wonderful good roots shall increase without diminishing."

The Buddha said: "Excellent! Excellent! This is the mind of your diligent effort, able to swiftly increase the power of merit within the Mahāyāna."

At that time, the earth spirit Dṛḍhā and the Merit Deva together addressed the Buddha: "For the benefit and happiness of beings, we wish to teach the heart-mantra." Then the Buddha addressed the earth spirit and Merit Deva: "Excellent! Since you wish to speak a divine spell for the benefit of all sentient beings, speak it as you wish." They then spoke the mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ oṃ prabhāva-ratni-śiri samanta ādha ādha ātu ātu pṛthivī pṛthivī ye svāhā.*

"If a person recites this mantra a full 100,000 times as the initial practice, reciting the mantra while making offerings to the Earth Deva, they shall obtain limitless wealth and nobility, inexhaustible seven treasures, and swiftly accomplish all siddhi.

"Next, the root body-mudrā:

"Join the palms. Cross both index fingers and little fingers within the palm. Both middle fingers and ring fingers stand erect with tips touching. Both thumbs stand side by side pressing alongside the index fingers. The thumbs move back and forth. This accomplishes all undertakings.

"Next, the various homa methods for accomplishment. Perform homa with rice-grain flowers to obtain abundant harvests of the five grains.

"Another method: perform homa with jātī flowers to obtain the accomplishment of raising a thousand catties of silkworms.

"Another method: perform homa with non-glutinous rice to obtain inexhaustible merit and to plant fields of merit life after life.

"Another method: at a place where a deity resides, gather people at that location and perform homa to obtain great wealth.

"Another method: perform homa with barley and wheat for wealth and nobility.

"Another method: perform homa with kiṃkula grass to obtain innumerable livestock and wealth.

"Another method: before an ancient tomb where a great yakṣa resides, perform homa with jātī flowers to obtain great wealth.

"Another method: perform homa with lotus-bud flowers to obtain people's love.

"Another method: when one wishes to summon a fortune-spirit dwelling nearby, take blue horse-tail hairs, cut each into one-*cun* lengths, twenty-one pieces. At each session, perform homa and recite. The fortune-spirit will certainly come and attach to my body.

"Another method: take blue horse dung, empower it twenty-one times with the preceding Earth Deva mantra, then smear it on the altar and on the stove-front of the person from whom one seeks assistance. This yields wealth as one desires.

"Another method: take blue horse forelock hair, make twenty-one offerings to the fortune-spirit. The fortune-spirit will certainly be pleased and bestow fortune.

"Another method: take earth from the stove-front of a fortunate person's home. On the eighth day of the twelfth month, bring it to my place and install it. One will certainly obtain wealth. When taking the earth, recite the preceding deva mantra twenty-one times. The upāsaka Sudatta, the upāsaka Dānapati, and the upāsikā Ratnaprabhā practiced this method.

"Another method: when the earth's energy is overly strong, causing punishment to befall the fourfold assembly, empower as before to obtain removal and relief.

"Another method: take earth from beneath the mortar of a wealthy person's home and perform homa. One will certainly become wealthy.

"Another method: take earth from inside the entrance of a wealthy person's tomb and perform homa. One will also become wealthy.

"The method for painting the image of Dṛḍhā, the Earth Spirit:

"The male deva is flesh-colored. The left hand holds a bowl filled with flowers; the right palm faces outward. The female deva is white flesh-colored. The right hand is held against the heart; the left hand is held against the thigh."
