# 毘俱胝菩薩一百八名經
## Sūtra of the One Hundred and Eight Names of Bhṛkuṭī Bodhisattva

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1114

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmadeva (法天).

---

Homage to all tathāgatas, worthy of offerings, omniscient and awakened. I shall now proclaim this heart mantra of all the tathāgatas. If there are devas, humans, vidyā-holding immortals, and assemblies who bow in homage to and make offerings to all the buddhas, who receive, uphold, read, recite, and praise the mantra, and who penetrate the characteristics of the dharma: if one pronounces the syllable OṂ, this is the meaning of perfect fulfillment; if one pronounces the syllable NA, this is the meaning of freedom from fear, also called the meaning of destroying Māra; if one pronounces the syllable YA, this is the meaning of breaking bonds; if one pronounces the syllable SA, this is the meaning of subjugating enemies and obstacles; if one pronounces the syllable NI, this is the meaning of destroying foes. Those who are fearful, by the power of the mantra, are freed from fear. He then spoke the dhāraṇī:

[Dhāraṇī]

These are the one hundred and eight secret mantra-names. If any person single-mindedly receives, upholds, and recites them, or writes them out, or explains them for others, that person's lifespan will increase, auspiciousness will prevail, their form will be handsome and their marks of merit complete, and all people will love and respect them. They will be far from the realm of Māra and freed from the peril of birth and death. The devas, humans, and asuras will reverently make offerings, and furthermore the devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, garuṇas, vidyā-holding immortals, and other assemblies will honor and praise them. All fetters and imprisonments will be spontaneously released. Lions, tigers, wolves, all evil hungry ghosts, enemies, and bandits cannot press upon or harm one. Violent winds, fierce fires, thunder, hail, flooding rivers and seas, poisons, and grave diseases cannot damage or injure one. If any person recollects these names of Bhṛkuṭī, the bodhisattva will constantly rescue and protect them. If any evil māras or enemies come to press upon and torment one, at that time the bodhisattva secretly radiates bodily light, universally illuminating space like a hundred thousand suns. That blazing radiance destroys the māra-enemies, and even heaven and earth are all purified. Furthermore, that person's wisdom increases and grows. For seven lifetimes, one obtains the spiritual power of past-life knowledge, is born in a kṣatriya clan and receives the body of a king. After the end of that life, one is reborn in the Western Land of Sukhāvatī.
