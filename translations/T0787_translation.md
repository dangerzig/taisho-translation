# 曼殊室利呪藏中校量數珠功德經
## Sūtra on Comparing the Merit of Prayer Beads, from Mañjuśrī's Dhāraṇī Treasury

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0787

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Yijing (義淨)

---

At that time the Bodhisattva Mahāsattva Mañjuśrī, Dharma-Prince, rose from his seat amid the great assembly, arranged his robes, bared his right shoulder, joined his palms reverently, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! For the benefit of all sentient beings, I wish to explain the merit of receiving and holding prayer beads, comparing the differences in blessings and benefits. I pray the World-Honored One will compassionately grant me permission."

The Buddha told Mañjuśrī: "Excellent, excellent! You may speak."

The Bodhisattva Mahāsattva Mañjuśrī said: "If good men or good women are able to recite the various dhāraṇīs and the names of the Buddhas, and for the sake of benefiting themselves and protecting others wish to swiftly accomplish the various dharmas and obtain their efficacy, the method of prayer beads should be as follows: one should receive and hold them with this intention. However, the materials of the beads are of various kinds. If iron is used for the prayer beads, reciting and turning them one round yields merit multiplied fivefold. If red copper is used, reciting and turning them one round yields merit multiplied tenfold. If pearls, coral, or other such jewels are used, reciting and turning them one round yields merit multiplied a hundredfold. If rudrākṣa seeds are used, reciting and turning them one round yields merit multiplied a thousandfold. If lotus seeds are used, reciting and turning them one round yields merit multiplied ten-thousandfold. If indra-rudrākṣa seeds are used, reciting and turning them one round yields merit multiplied a million-fold. If ulūdra-rudrākṣa seeds are used, reciting and turning them one round yields merit multiplied a hundred-million-fold. If crystal is used, reciting and turning them one round yields merit multiplied a billion-fold. If bodhi seeds are used, whether one turns and recites or merely holds them in the hand, reciting one round yields merit that is immeasurable, incalculable, and beyond comparison. If one wishes to be born in the pure lands of the Buddhas, one should receive and hold these beads in accordance with the Dharma."

The Bodhisattva Mañjuśrī said: "As for bodhi seeds: if a person holds these bodhi-seed beads in hand but is unable to recite the names of the Buddhas or dhāraṇīs according to the proper method, yet is able simply to hold them in hand and carry them on one's person, then whether walking, standing, sitting, or lying down, whatever words one speaks, whether good or evil, because this person holds the bodhi seeds, the merit obtained is no different from reciting the names of all the Buddhas and chanting the dhāraṇīs. The blessings obtained are immeasurable. As for the prayer beads themselves, they should have a full complement of one hundred and eight. If these are difficult to obtain, then fifty-four, or twenty-seven, or merely fourteen may also be used. These are the differences in the merit of prayer beads.

"For what reason do I now particularly praise the use of bodhi seeds as yielding the supreme benefit?"

The Bodhisattva Mañjuśrī said: "Long ago in the past, a Buddha appeared in the world and attained perfect awakening beneath this tree. At that time, a non-Buddhist of wrong views and a corrupted mind slandered the Three Treasures. That non-Buddhist had a son who was suddenly struck and killed by non-human spirits. The non-Buddhist thought: 'My views have been mistaken. I wonder what spiritual power the Buddhas truly possess. Since the Tathāgata attained perfect awakening beneath this tree, if it is truly holy, the tree should have the power to respond.' He then laid his dead son beneath the bodhi tree and said: 'If the Buddha's tree is truly holy, my son should be restored to life.' After seven days of chanting the Buddha's name, the son was indeed restored to life. The non-Buddhist was overjoyed and praised: 'The spiritual power of the Buddhas is something I have never seen! The tree where the Buddha attained the path has manifested this wonder. Its great spiritual power is difficult to conceive.' When the other non-Buddhists heard of this, they all abandoned their wrong views, turned to what is right, generated the mind of bodhi, and came to trust in the inconceivable spiritual power of the Buddha. Because of this, the people of the world all called it the Life-Extending Tree. Thus the bodhi tree came to have two names: the first is the Bodhi Tree, and the second is the Life-Extending Tree."

When the Bodhisattva Mahāsattva Mañjuśrī had spoken these words, the Buddha said: "Excellent, excellent, Mañjuśrī! It is as you have said."

At that time the great assembly, having heard the sūtra on the merit of holding prayer beads, were all greatly filled with joy. They received it with faith and practiced accordingly.
