# 佛說滅除五逆罪大陀羅尼經
## Sūtra of the Great Dhāraṇī Spoken by the Buddha for Eliminating the Sins of the Five Heinous Acts

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1399

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmabhadra (法賢)

---

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed Ānanda, saying: "There is a great dhāraṇī possessed of tremendous power and immeasurable merit, capable of eliminating the grave sins of the five heinous acts committed by sentient beings. If there is a person who hears this dhāraṇī, gives rise to a mind of utmost sincerity, and throughout the duration of this life is constantly able to carry it upon the crown of their head, receive and uphold it, and recite it, the merit obtained by such a person is no different from upholding [the teachings of] a thousand Buddhas." He then spoke the dhāraṇī:

> *Namo ratnatrayāya, namas āryāvalokiteśvarāya bodhisattvāya mahāsattvāya mahākāruṇikāya, tadyathā oṃ śuddhe viśuddhe suviśuddhe, cini paricini, puṣpe supuṣpe, jaloharaṇi, hara hara, sarvāvaraṇani, paca paca, sarvapāyasthānani, padme padmaci, padmaviśāle, vare vare vare vare, ha ha ha ha ha, agniyāle agniyāle, siddhijāle, svāhā.*

At that time, having heard the Tathāgata proclaim the dhāraṇī for eliminating the karmic sins of the five heinous acts, Ānanda was filled with joy and leaped up in delight, bowed in reverence to the Buddha, and withdrew.
