# 出生無邊門陀羅尼經
## Sūtra on Producing Infinite Gateways Dhāraṇī

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1009

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Bhagavān was staying at the Great Forest, in the Storied Pavilion, in Vaiśālī, together with a great assembly of eight thousand monks and many bodhisattva-mahāsattvas.

At that time, the World-Honored One, knowing through mindful wisdom that he would relinquish his life-force, reflected that after three months he would enter parinirvāṇa. Then the World-Honored One commanded the Venerable Mahāmaudgalyāyana to go throughout the great-thousandfold world system and announce to all the monks that they should all assemble at the Great Forest Storied Pavilion. Mahāmaudgalyāyana addressed the Buddha: "I respectfully receive this instruction." In an instant, by his own supernatural power, he reached the summit of Mount Sumeru and proclaimed in a great voice throughout the great-thousandfold world system:

> Listen, all in this world system!
> Sentient beings who are disciples of the Buddha,
> The Great Teacher will now rain down the dharma.
> All who delight in hearing, come and assemble!

Then forty thousand monks all came and assembled at the Great Forest Storied Pavilion. When those monks had seen the World-Honored One, they prostrated at the Buddha's feet and withdrew to sit to one side.

Then Śāriputra, empowered by the Buddha's might, gave rise to this thought: "I should perform such a display of supernatural power. By performing this display, I shall cause all those dwelling throughout the three-thousandfold great-thousandfold world system, whether of the śrāvaka vehicle, the pratyekabuddha vehicle, or the Mahāyāna, to assemble at the Great Forest Storied Pavilion." Then Śāriputra performed such a display of supernatural power. By this display, all those dwelling throughout the three-thousandfold great-thousandfold world system, whether of the śrāvaka vehicle, the pratyekabuddha vehicle, or the Mahāyāna, all came and assembled at the Great Forest Storied Pavilion. Having come and seen the World-Honored One, they prostrated at his feet, circumambulated him three times, and withdrew to sit to one side.

Then the World-Honored One addressed the great bodhisattva-mahāsattvas, namely: Amoghadarśin Bodhisattva, Mañjuśrī-kumārabhūta Bodhisattva, Durgati-pariśodhana Bodhisattva, Daurmanasya-tamomardana Bodhisattva, Sarva-nīvaraṇa-viṣkambhin Bodhisattva, Jālinīprabha Bodhisattva, Sarva-viṣaya-nirodha-prajñā Bodhisattva, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, Akṣayamati Bodhisattva, Gandhahastin Bodhisattva, Śūraṅgama Bodhisattva, Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva, Amitaprabhā Bodhisattva, Candraprabha Bodhisattva, Jñānadhvaja Bodhisattva, Bhadrapāla Bodhisattva, Sāgaramati Bodhisattva, Akṣayamati Bodhisattva, Vajragarbha Bodhisattva, Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva, Samantabhadra Bodhisattva, Pratibhānakūṭa Bodhisattva, and Maitreya Bodhisattva: "You should go to the buddha-fields of the ten directions, as numerous as the sands of the Ganges, and summon the final-body bodhisattvas, one-lifetime successor bodhisattvas, non-retrogression bodhisattvas, bodhisattvas who have attained the patience of non-arising dharmas, and faith-understanding bodhisattvas. Cause them all to assemble at the Great Forest Storied Pavilion."

Then those bodhisattvas, receiving the Buddha's holy command, said: "So it is, World-Honored One!" In an instant, they performed such a display of supernatural power. By this display, at the Great Forest Storied Pavilion, an inexpressible number of hundred-thousand koṭi nayutas of final-body bodhisattvas came and assembled. Furthermore, ninety koṭi hundred-thousand nayutas of one-lifetime successor bodhisattvas came and assembled. Furthermore, three nayuta hundred-thousand non-retrogression bodhisattvas came and assembled. Furthermore, thirty koṭi hundred-thousand nayutas of bodhisattvas who had attained the patience of non-arising dharmas came and assembled. Furthermore, eight koṭi nayuta hundred-thousand faith-understanding bodhisattvas came and assembled. All these bodhisattvas came. Having seen the World-Honored One, they prostrated at his feet, circumambulated him three times, and withdrew to sit to one side.

Then Śāriputra, seeing the great bodhisattvas assembled, gave rise to this thought: "I should question the Tathāgata, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One about such principles. By hearing such appropriate and fitting instructions, the bodhisattva-mahāsattvas will have all doubts severed, will swiftly attain the wisdom of unobstructed eloquence, and in buddha-fields as numerous as the sands of the Ganges will hear the essential dharma from the tathāgatas. Having heard it, they will retain it all. Until they attain supreme enlightenment, in the meantime they will remember and never forget the essential dharma they have heard.

"Bodhisattvas have four practices of purification. What are the four? Purity of sentient beings, purity of dharma, purity of aspiration, and purity of the merit of adorning buddha-fields.

"Having attained those, there are four kinds of joyful dharma: joy of body, joy of speech, joy of mind, and joy of birth.

"Having attained those, one can enter four dhāraṇī gates. What are the four? Entering the dhāraṇī gate of producing the inexhaustible, entering the dhāraṇī gate of skill in beings' faculties, entering the dhāraṇī gate of skill in karmic fruition that is unconditioned, and entering the dhāraṇī gate of profound dharma patience."

Then Śāriputra, having reflected definitively on these principles as described above, broadly spoke them to the World-Honored One: "May the World-Honored One expound the essential principles so that the bodhisattvas' practice may become pure. May the World-Honored One deign to teach them."

Having thus spoken, he told Śāriputra: "Excellent! Excellent! Śāriputra! You are able to compassionately think of the many, and for the peace, welfare, and benefit of the many, of devas and humans. You are able to ask about such principles. Listen well, listen most attentively, think about them and keep them in mind. I shall explain for you."

Hearing the Buddha, Śāriputra said: "So it is, World-Honored One! Please expound this."

Then the World-Honored One told Śāriputra: "If a bodhisattva-mahāsattva does not grasp and is not attached to any dharma whatsoever, they should uphold this true-word dhāraṇī phrase:

*Tadyathā aṇi ake make mukhe samantamukhe sumevādiyarame saundi yukteni niruktenī nirukti parābhi-hilī-hili-kabhī-bhi-ka-pasi-salī sarvavatī hilī hilī hilī hilī hilī hilī hili hili-lī mahā-hihī-jāṇḍhī cavane jaracara-ne ajalī mājalī ānantī ānanta-gatī araṇa-śmane-śvapane-śvattaṇḍa-śdante dharmadhārī nihale nihale vimale śīla-viśudhani prakṛtinipanī-bhāva vibhāvani asaṅgī asaṅga vihale dāme vimalī vimala-prabhī saṅkariṣaṇī dhile didhilī mahā-didhilī yaśe-yaśuvatī jale ajale mājale sammājale nirdhā-sandhi suṣṭhile asaṅgī asaṅga vihale asaṅga nirhale nihāra-vimale nihāra-śudhane nirdhā-sume methilī sthame sthama-vati mahā-parābhe samanta-parābhe vipula-parābhe vipula-raśme samantamukhe sarvatra-nugatī anajñenī dhāraṇī dharma-nidhāna udāle samanta-bhadrī sarva-tathāgata-adhiṣṭhāna-adhiṣṭhite svāhā*

Having spoken this dhāraṇī, he told Śāriputra: "When upholding this dhāraṇī, the bodhisattva-mahāsattva does not think of conditioned or unconditioned dharmas; there is nothing to attain; one does not slander, does not abandon, does not grasp, does not develop. In what is attained, one does not generate increase in cutting off or cultivation. One neither acts nor refrains from acting, does not manifest. One does not see dharmas coming together or scattering, does not see dharmas arising. One does not see past dharmas ceasing, does not see future or present dharmas increasing or decreasing. One does not posit that dharmas are beneficial or not beneficial. One should only be mindful of the Buddha-samādhi.

"In the time of cultivation: no form and no non-form; no marks and no non-marks; no minor marks and no non-minor marks; no consciousness and no non-consciousness; no afflictions and no non-afflictions; no precepts and no non-precepts; no samādhi and no non-samādhi; no wisdom and no non-wisdom; no liberation and no non-liberation; no knowledge-and-vision of liberation and no non-knowledge-and-vision of liberation; neither born nor unborn; neither of noble birth nor not of noble birth; neither with retinue nor without retinue; neither abiding nor not abiding; neither attaining nor not attaining; neither realizing nor not realizing; neither exhaustion of afflictions nor non-exhaustion of afflictions; neither aggregates, elements, and sense-bases nor non-aggregates, elements, and sense-bases; neither knowledge nor non-knowledge; neither teaching dharma nor non-teaching dharma; neither self-purity nor other-purity; neither purity of beings nor non-purity of beings; neither self-benefit nor other-benefit; neither dharma nor discipline; neither bodily purity nor verbal purity; neither mental purity nor practice-purity; neither purity of past conduct nor purity of future conduct nor purity of present conduct; neither one's own form nor another's form.

"Śāriputra! This bodhisattva's mindfulness of the Buddha-samādhi that enters all dharmas without words is the equality of all dharmas. It is named the fearless dhāraṇī that abides in ultimate truth. It is named the samādhi that definitively fulfills all aspirations. It is the accumulation of all good roots and wisdom, the dharma-treasury lineage that does not depend on another's power, the minor marks, named the dhāraṇī of not being humiliated by others, the most excellent skill in seeking out māras. It is also named the dhāraṇī of transcending all māra-karma.

"Śāriputra! If a bodhisattva attains the meaning of this 'Producing Infinite Gateways Dhāraṇī,' they attain non-retrogression and will swiftly realize supreme perfect enlightenment. Why? Because it definitively contains the treasury of all buddhas' dharma-merit, and all the differentiated practices of bodhisattvas are obtained through this signless dhāraṇī."

Then the World-Honored One spoke these verses:

> Do not seek the empty dharma;
> Do not engage in idle speculation about enlightenment.
> Without wavering from the dharmadhātu,
> One attains the dhāraṇī.
>
> You should listen to this sūtra,
> The inexhaustible dhāraṇī.
> Through this, wisdom is accomplished,
> And from this, enlightenment is realized.
>
> Holding this dhāraṇī,
> The bodhisattva attains fearlessness.
> From the buddhas of the ten directions
> They hear the supreme dharma.
>
> They can know the superb, wondrous dharma,
> Where meaning and text correspond.
> Like the radiance of the sun,
> They attain words and meanings likewise.
>
> Attaining the supreme dharma,
> The vast, great dhāraṇī,
> All things manifest before them
> Through upholding this sūtra.
>
> If sentient beings for an entire kalpa
> Were to raise difficult questions,
> All their doubts would be severed,
> And wisdom would be inexhaustible.
>
> The eldest son of the dharma king
> Draws near supreme enlightenment,
> Entrusted with the treasury of the buddha-dharma,
> Through love of this sūtra.
>
> All beings delight in them,
> And all buddhas show compassion.
> Their fame spreads throughout the world
> Through holding the dhāraṇī.
>
> Eighty koṭi buddhas
> Appear at the time of death,
> Extending their hands to receive that person,
> Through holding the dhāraṇī.
>
> The sins committed
> Over a thousand koṭi kalpas
> Are all purified in a single month
> Through holding the dhāraṇī.
>
> The accumulation of merit
> A bodhisattva gathers over koṭi kalpas
> Is surpassed in a single month
> Through holding the dhāraṇī.
>
> All the sentient beings of the three realms,
> Even if they all became māras,
> Could not create obstacles
> Through upholding this sūtra.
>
> Mindful practice and wisdom,
> Supreme hearing and retention,
> Always turning on the tip of the tongue,
> Until enlightenment is realized.
>
> As spoken in this sūtra,
> Definitively attaining dhāraṇī,
> The Tathāgata has spoken this:
> Within it, enlightenment is attained.
>
> Through hearing this dhāraṇī,
> Dīpaṃkara gave me my prophecy.
> In an instant I saw buddhas
> As numerous as the sands of the Ganges.
>
> If you wish to know all the dharma
> That the buddhas have taught,
> You should study this sūtra
> And swiftly obtain it all.
>
> For purifying buddha-fields,
> For accomplishing the śrāvaka path,
> For radiant purity of marks,
> This sūtra can accomplish all.
>
> For seven days one should be diligent,
> Contemplating without negligence.
> Eighty koṭi buddhas
> Will bestow the dhāraṇī.
>
> What is thought should not be thought;
> What is not thought, carefully do not think.
> What is thought, do not think it.
> Then one attains the dhāraṇī.
>
> Like entering the great ocean
> Without seeking material treasures,
> Attaining this dhāraṇī,
> One does not seek other comforts.
>
> Drawing near to perfect enlightenment,
> Therefore you should study this.
> Attaining the phrase of quiescence,
> One attains the three types of enlightenment.

Then the World-Honored One further told Śāriputra: "A bodhisattva who accomplishes four things attains this dhāraṇī. What are the four? Not being attached to desire; not generating jealousy toward any sentient being; giving away all one's own possessions without regret; and day and night delighting in the dharma, amusing oneself with the dharma. Śāriputra! A bodhisattva-mahāsattva who accomplishes these four things attains the dhāraṇī."

Then the World-Honored One spoke these verses:

> One should abandon foul, impure desire,
> The wretched realm of Māra.
> Through this one falls to the hells,
> And it is the cause of evil destinies.
>
> Do not be jealous of others
> For the sake of kinship, fame, or gain.
> View beings with eyes of compassion
> And attain a great, dignified, and wondrous form.
>
> Contention among beings
> Has accumulation as its root.
> Therefore one should abandon craving;
> Abandoning craving, one attains the dhāraṇī.
>
> Day and night, single-mindedly seek the dharma,
> With one mind seeking enlightenment.
> The dhāraṇī will appear before you
> Through studying this sūtra.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra! A bodhisattva-mahāsattva who accomplishes four things attains the dhāraṇī. What are the four? Dwelling in forest hermitages of utmost non-contention; abiding in profound dharma patience and forbearance; not being attached to gain, reverence, or fame; and giving away all cherished things without clinging, even body and life. Śāriputra! By accomplishing these four things one attains the dhāraṇī."

Then the World-Honored One spoke these verses:

> Dwell in the forest, praised by the Buddha.
> Dwelling there, do not slight others.
> Delight in profound dharma patience,
> Diligent as if rescuing one's burning head.
>
> Do not generate attachment to gain;
> Through such causes, one's practice becomes crooked.
> Whether coarse or fine, be content like a bird;
> Having obtained a human body, make it bear fruit.
>
> How wonderful to obtain the tathāgata's dharma!
> Abandon the household, the source of much suffering.
> Purify body, speech, and mind,
> And deeply honor the buddha-dharma.
>
> One who craves gain has no mindful wisdom,
> No faith, no precepts, no contemplation of dharma.
> Enlightenment is as distant as an empty field;
> Therefore distance yourself from the mind of craving and attachment.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra! A bodhisattva-mahāsattva who accomplishes four things attains this dhāraṇī. What are the four? Entering the meaning of eight syllables. What are the eight?

"The syllable *pa*: in the ultimate sense, it enters the non-self of all dharmas.

"The syllable *la*: because of marks and minor marks and the absence of marks and minor marks, it enters the dharmakāya of all tathāgatas.

"The syllable *va*: the dharma of the foolish and the dharma of the noble enter non-duality without distinction.

"The syllable *ja*: birth, aging, and death, and non-birth, non-aging, and non-death; going and not going enter non-arising and non-ceasing.

"The syllable *ka*: karmic ripening enters non-karmic-ripening.

"The syllable *dha*: the essential dharma of dhāraṇī, emptiness, signlessness, and wishlessness enter the dharmadhātu.

"The syllable *śa*: *śamatha* and *vipaśyanā*, non-*śamatha* and non-*vipaśyanā*, all dharmas enter true suchness.

"The combined syllable *kṣa*: all dharmas are momentary, inexhaustible, indestructible, and without inherent substance, because they are originally quiescent; they enter the nirvāṇa of all dharmas.

"One should enter the meaning of these eight syllables; this is the first way of entering. In the essential dharma of this dhāraṇī, one should carefully write it out and uphold it; this is the second way of entering. In the essential dharma of this dhāraṇī, every half-month one should read, diligently study, and keep it in mind; this is the third way of entering. The bodhisattva-mahāsattva who practices this dhāraṇī dharma should encourage, comfort, and praise all sentient beings, urging them to study this dhāraṇī; this is the fourth way of entering.

"Śāriputra! A bodhisattva-mahāsattva who accomplishes these four things attains this dhāraṇī."

Then the World-Honored One spoke these verses:

> Contemplate the meaning of the eight syllables;
> Write out and uphold this sūtra.
> Every half-month, study and recite it,
> And also encourage other sentient beings.
>
> Drawing near enlightenment's vast wisdom,
> You will see all the buddhas in person,
> In the ten directions where they dwell,
> And from them learn and generate faith.

"Śāriputra! A bodhisattva-mahāsattva who cultivates this dhāraṇī attains four kinds of merit. What are the four? Being embraced and accepted by all the tathāgatas of the ten directions; being free from all māra-obstacles; swiftly becoming far removed from karmic obstructions; and attaining unobstructed eloquence. Śāriputra! A bodhisattva-mahāsattva who cultivates this dhāraṇī attains these four kinds of merit."

Then the World-Honored One spoke these verses:

> All buddhas embrace and accept them;
> The māra host cannot find an opening.
> Karmic obstructions are swiftly removed;
> They attain unobstructed eloquence.

"Śāriputra! Long ago in the past, through numberless, surpassingly numberless, vast, lofty, distant, immeasurable kalpas, at that time there was a buddha named Ratnamaṅgala-tejorāja-kalpa Tathāgata, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One, who appeared in the world, complete with the ten epithets.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra! When that Ratnamaṅgala-tejorāja-kalpa Tathāgata entered parinirvāṇa, there was a human king named Dhāraṇaprabhā, a cakravartin endowed with the seven treasures. That king had a son named Acintyaguṇa-ratnamaṅgala, aged sixteen, who heard from that buddha this essential dharma of the 'Producing Infinite Gateways' dhāraṇī.

"Having just heard this dhāraṇī, he abided in diligent exertion. For seventy thousand years he never slept. He did not covet the throne, body, life, or wealth. For seventy thousand years he sat in unbroken stillness, his sides never touching the ground. From ninety thousand koṭi buddhas he heard the true dharma. Having heard it, he retained it all. He then served that Ratnamaṅgala-tejorāja-kalpa Tathāgata, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One, and went forth to ordination in that buddha's presence. After ninety thousand years, he accomplished this 'Producing Infinite Gateways' dhāraṇī. Having accomplished it, he broadly expounded it for all sentient beings. In one lifetime, he established eighty thousand koṭi nayutas of beings in the supreme perfect enlightenment at the stage of non-retrogression.

"Śāriputra! At that time in the assembly there was an elder's son named Candrasūryadhvaja, who heard from the dharma-teacher monk this 'Producing Infinite Gateways' dhāraṇī. Having heard it, he deeply rejoiced. By the good root of this rejoicing, from ninety thousand koṭi buddhas he heard the true dharma. Having heard it, he retained it all. He thereby became a supreme holder of dhāraṇī, a supreme speaker of dignified words, a supreme possessor of unbroken eloquence. Through three kalpas he reverently served those many buddhas. After three kalpas he realized supreme perfect enlightenment.

"Śāriputra! If any should have doubts, thinking differently about who that elder's son Candrasūryadhvaja of that time might be, they should not think so. Why? Dīpaṃkara Buddha was at that time the elder's son Candrasūryadhvaja.

"Śāriputra! If any should have doubts, thinking differently about who that dharma teacher Acintyaguṇa-ratnamaṅgala might be, they should not think so. Why? Amitāyus Tathāgata was at that time the dharma teacher Acintyaguṇa-ratnamaṅgala.

"Śāriputra! We bodhisattva-mahāsattvas of this Bhadrakalpa, having heard this sūtra, deeply rejoiced. By the good root of that rejoicing, we turned away from forty thousand koṭi kalpas of the cycle of birth and death. From ninety thousand koṭi buddhas we heard the true dharma and all became supreme holders of dhāraṇī, supreme speakers of dignified words, supreme possessors of unbroken eloquence.

"Therefore, Śāriputra! Those who wish to swiftly attain supreme perfect enlightenment, the bodhisattva-mahāsattvas, should at least practice rejoicing in this dharma. Why? Such a bodhisattva attains the stage of non-retrogression. Serving the dharma teacher is a cause of supreme enlightenment. How much more so for those who write, uphold, recite, rightly contemplate, and explain it to others! The mass of merit from this, except by the tathāgatas, cannot be known by any sentient being. It is unknowable and inconceivable."

Then the World-Honored One spoke these verses:

> Having heard this sūtra and generated rejoicing,
> Having written, upheld, and recited it,
> No sentient being can fathom
> The inexhaustible stream of merit born from it.
>
> In every life they see the buddhas
> And attain inconceivable pure faith.
> They comprehend the profound sūtras and their principles
> And swiftly awaken to supreme enlightenment.
>
> They do not lose samādhi,
> Do not lose supernatural powers or dhāraṇī,
> Do not lose form, wealth, or seeing buddhas,
> Until they realize the unsurpassed enlightenment.
>
> I recall in ages past, in a former life,
> As an elder's son I heard the dhāraṇī.
> I personally saw buddhas as numerous as the sands of the Ganges
> And through rejoicing awakened to great enlightenment.
>
> Dīpaṃkara was formerly the elder's son;
> Infinite Radiance in a former life.
> The Buddha Amitāyus was the dharma teacher;
> We of this Bhadrakalpa all rejoiced.
>
> Those who wish to swiftly realize enlightenment,
> Who wish to swiftly crush all māras,
> Who wish for the adornment of a hundred-merit form,
> Through this practice, it is not difficult.
>
> If worlds as numerous as the sands of the Ganges
> Were entirely filled with seven treasures and given away,
> The merit born from writing and upholding this,
> Even by analogy, that giving cannot match.
>
> Therefore, having heard, one should be single-mindedly diligent.
> The wise bodhisattva upholds this.
> One who writes the dhāraṇī and contemplates it:
> I declare that enlightenment is not difficult for them.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra! For the bodhisattva-mahāsattva who practices this 'Producing Infinite Gateways' dhāraṇī, there are eight great yakṣas dwelling in the Snow Mountains who will come to increase the practitioner's bodily power and guard and protect them day and night. What are the eight? First, the yakṣa named Śūra. Second, the yakṣa named Nirdha. Third, the yakṣa named Prabhū. Fourth, the yakṣa named Nārāyaṇa-malla. Fifth, the yakṣa named Cāritramati. Sixth, the yakṣa named Durdaśa. Seventh, the yakṣa named Kaṇara. Eighth, the yakṣa named Sumukha.

"When all of them come, the practitioner should bathe and put on fresh, clean garments. One should practice walking meditation without clinging to body or life. One should generate great compassion pervading all sentient beings. One should recite this dhāraṇī. Then those eight great yakṣas will swiftly reveal the gateways to the practitioner.

"There are eight great bodhisattvas born in the desire heavens who will also come to bless and embrace the practitioner. What are the eight? Samantāloka Bodhisattva, Prabhākara Bodhisattva, Prajñāprabhā Bodhisattva, Sūryaprabhā Bodhisattva, Prabodhana Bodhisattva, Sarvāśāparipūrṇa Bodhisattva, Nakṣatrarāja Bodhisattva, and Caryāmati Bodhisattva.

"These eight bodhisattva-mahāsattvas have attained the dhāraṇī and abide in the practice of cultivating the dhāraṇī. The practicing bodhisattva should abide in true commitment and faith, knowing and repaying kindness, delighting in the buddha-dharma, and abiding in profound dharma patience.

"The bodhisattva-mahāsattva who cultivates the dhāraṇī and practices walking meditation should cultivate equality with respect to both material wealth and dharma. If one cultivates equality even in giving small things, how much more so with great things."

When the World-Honored One taught this dhāraṇī, thirty Ganges-sand nayutas of hundred-thousand koṭi bodhisattvas, by attaining this 'Producing Infinite Gateways' dhāraṇī, achieved non-retrogression from supreme perfect enlightenment. One hundred sixty *vimbara* humans and devas, who had not previously generated the aspiration for supreme perfect enlightenment, generated the aspiration for supreme enlightenment.

Then Śāriputra addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! What is this sūtra called? How should I uphold it?"

The Buddha told Śāriputra: "This sūtra is named 'Producing Infinite Gateways'; you should uphold it thus. It is also named 'Definitively Attaining Omniscient Wisdom'; you should uphold it thus. It is also named 'Definitively Producing Bodhisattvas'; you should uphold it thus. It is also named 'Crushing the Māra Host'; you should uphold it thus."

When the Buddha had taught this sūtra, the Venerable Śāriputra together with the great bodhisattvas, devas, humans, asuras, gandharvas, and others all greatly rejoiced, faithfully accepted, and put it into practice.
