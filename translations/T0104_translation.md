# 佛說法印經
## Sūtra on the Dharma Seal Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0104

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dānapāla (施護)

---

At that time, the Buddha was in the country of Śrāvastī, together with an assembly of bhikṣus. The Buddha then addressed the assembly of bhikṣus: "You should know: there is a sacred Dharma seal that I shall now explain and distinguish for you. You should give rise to pure knowledge and vision, listen carefully, receive it well, attend to it skillfully, and remember and contemplate it." The bhikṣus then addressed the Buddha: "Excellent, World-Honored One! We wish to hear your teaching. We delight in listening."

The Buddha said: "Bhikṣus! The nature of emptiness is without anything, without delusive thought, without arising, without cessation, and transcends all knowledge and views. Why? Because the nature of emptiness has no location, no form or characteristic, is not a perception, was never produced, and is beyond what knowledge and views can reach, transcending all attachment. Because it transcends attachment, it embraces all dharmas and abides in the view of equality. This is the true and authentic view. Bhikṣus, you should know: the nature of emptiness is like this, and all dharmas are likewise. This is called the Dharma seal.

"Furthermore, bhikṣus! This Dharma seal is the three gates of liberation. It is the root dharma of all Buddhas, the eye of all Buddhas, and the destination to which all Buddhas return. Therefore, listen carefully, receive it well, remember and contemplate it, and observe it as it truly is.

"Furthermore, bhikṣus! If there is a practitioner, one should go to the forest, or dwell under a tree or in other quiet places, and observe as it truly is: form is suffering, is empty, is impermanent. One should give rise to disenchantment and abide in the view of equality. Likewise, observe feeling, perception, formations, and consciousness: they are suffering, empty, and impermanent. One should give rise to disenchantment and abide in the view of equality. Bhikṣus! The aggregates are fundamentally empty, produced by the mind. When the dharma of the mind ceases, the aggregates have no function. Understanding thus is correct liberation. Having been correctly liberated, one transcends all knowledge and views. This is called the gate of liberation through emptiness.

"Furthermore, abiding in samādhi, observe that all objects of form are entirely extinguished, transcending all perceptions. Likewise, sound, smell, taste, touch, and mental objects are also entirely extinguished, transcending all perceptions. Observing in this way is called the gate of liberation through signlessness. Having entered this gate of liberation, one attains purity of knowledge and vision. From this purity, craving, aversion, and delusion are all entirely extinguished. When they are extinguished, one abides in the view of equality. Abiding in this view, one is free from the view of self and the view of what belongs to self, and one fully understands all views: nothing arises, nothing is relied upon.

"Furthermore, having left behind the view of self, there is no seeing, no hearing, no perceiving, no knowing. Why? Because consciousness arises from causes and conditions, and those very causes and conditions, together with the consciousness they produce, are all impermanent. Because they are impermanent, consciousness cannot be grasped. When the aggregate of consciousness is empty and has no function, this is called the gate of liberation through wishlessness. Having entered this gate of liberation, one knows the dharma ultimately. Without attachment to dharmas, one realizes the quiescence of dharmas."

The Buddha addressed the bhikṣus: "This is called the sacred Dharma seal, which is the three gates of liberation. Bhikṣus, if you who are practitioners cultivate this, you will attain purity of knowledge and vision."

At that time, the bhikṣus, having heard this dharma, all greatly rejoiced, bowed their heads in respect, and faithfully received it.
