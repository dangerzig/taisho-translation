# 觀世音菩薩授記經
## Sūtra on the Prediction for the Bodhisattva Avalokiteśvara

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0371

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmakṣema (曇無竭)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was in the Deer Park of the sages at Vārāṇasī, together with a great assembly of twenty thousand bhikṣus and twelve thousand bodhisattvas. Their names were: Lion Bodhisattva, Lion-Mind Bodhisattva, Peaceful-Mind Bodhisattva, Incomparable-Mind Bodhisattva, Dharaṇīdhara Bodhisattva, Bāla-datta Bodhisattva, Divine-Deva Bodhisattva, True-Affairs Bodhisattva, Gahuta Bodhisattva, Worthy-Power Bodhisattva, Bright-Deva Bodhisattva, Delight-in-Joy Bodhisattva, Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva, Wisdom-Practice Bodhisattva, Concentrated-Practice Bodhisattva, Manifesting-Unobstructed Bodhisattva, and Maitreya Bodhisattva. Together with such chief leaders, there were twelve thousand bodhisattva-mahāsattvas. There were also twenty thousand devaputras, with Good-Realm Devaputra and Good-Abiding Devaputra at their head, all abiding in the Mahāyāna.

At that time the World-Honored One, surrounded by immeasurable hundreds of thousands of attendants, was teaching the Dharma.

At that time, in the assembly, a bodhisattva named Flower-Virtue Treasury rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, placed his right knee on the ground, pressed his palms together toward the Buddha, and said: "I pray the World-Honored One will grant me a moment; I have something to ask."

The Buddha told the Bodhisattva Flower-Virtue Treasury: "Ask as you wish. Whatever doubts you have, I already know them and shall explain them for you, bringing you delight."

At that time Flower-Virtue Treasury said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One, how does a bodhisattva-mahāsattva become non-retrogressive from anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi, attain the five spiritual powers, gain the Illusion-Like Samādhi, and, using skillful means, transform his body and, in accordance with the various forms of beings and the good roots to be accomplished, teach the Dharma so that they attain anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi?"

The Buddha told the Bodhisattva Mahāsattva Flower-Virtue Treasury: "Excellent! Excellent! That you can ask such a question before the Tathāgata, the Equally and Perfectly Enlightened One! Flower-Virtue Treasury, you have already planted good roots in the presence of past buddhas, made offerings to countless hundreds of thousands of millions of hundreds of millions of buddha world-honored ones, and given rise to great compassion for sentient beings. Excellent, Flower-Virtue Treasury! Listen carefully, listen carefully! Ponder well. I shall explain for you."

He replied: "Indeed! I delight in wishing to hear."

The Buddha told the Bodhisattva Mahāsattva Flower-Virtue Treasury: "Accomplishing one dharma, one attains the Illusion-Like Samādhi. Having attained this samādhi, one uses skillful means to transform one's body and, in accordance with the various forms of beings and the good roots to be accomplished, teaches the Dharma so that they attain anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi. What is this one dharma? It is non-dependence. One does not depend on the three realms, nor on the inner, nor on the outer. In non-dependence one attains right contemplation. Having rightly contemplated, one attains right cessation, and in that awakened knowing there is no diminishment. With a mind of non-diminishment, one fully crosses over into right wisdom: namely, that all dharmas arise from conditions and exist as provisional designations. All dharmas are born from causes and conditions; without causes and conditions, no dharma arises. Although all dharmas arise from causes and conditions, nothing truly arises. One who thus penetrates the dharma of non-arising enters the bodhisattva's true path, which is also called entering the great mind of loving-kindness and compassion that has pity for and liberates all sentient beings. Having well and deeply understood this meaning, one knows that all dharmas are like illusions, being merely mental projections, verbal constructions, and created dharmas. Yet these mental projections, verbal constructions, and created dharmas are all ultimately empty. Having well penetrated the emptiness of all dharmas, this is called attaining the Illusion-Like Samādhi. Having attained this samādhi, one uses skillful means to transform one's body and, in accordance with the various forms of beings, accomplishes good roots and teaches the Dharma so that they attain anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi."

At that time the Bodhisattva Mahāsattva Flower-Virtue Treasury said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One, is there a bodhisattva in this assembly who has attained this samādhi?"

The Buddha said: "Yes. In this present assembly, Maitreya Bodhisattva, Mañjuśrī, and sixty other great beings adorned with inconceivable great vows have attained this samādhi."

He further said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One, is it only bodhisattvas in this world who have attained this samādhi? Are there also bodhisattvas in other worlds who have accomplished this Illusion-Like Samādhi?"

The Buddha told Flower-Virtue Treasury: "To the west, past a hundred thousand koṭis of lands from here, there is a world called Sukhāvatī. In that land there is a buddha named Amitābha Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who now presently teaches the Dharma. There are bodhisattvas there: the first is named Avalokiteśvara, the second is named Mahāsthāmaprāpta. They have attained this samādhi. Furthermore, Flower-Virtue Treasury, if a bodhisattva hears the Dharma from those great beings for seven days and seven nights, they will attain the Illusion-Like Samādhi."

The Bodhisattva Flower-Virtue Treasury said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One, that land should have immeasurable bodhisattvas who have attained this samādhi. Why? Because the other bodhisattvas born in that land will all go to where those great beings are and hear this Dharma."

The Buddha said: "So it is! So it is! As you say. Immeasurable asaṃkhyeyas of bodhisattva-mahāsattvas have obtained this samādhi from those great beings."

The Bodhisattva Flower-Virtue Treasury said to the Buddha: "Excellent! World-Honored One, Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha! I pray that by your spiritual power you will cause those great beings to come to this world, and cause both sides to see each other. Why? When those great beings come to this land, good men and good women who have accomplished good roots, upon hearing their teaching, will attain this samādhi. I also pray that they may see Amitābha Buddha in the world of Sukhāvatī, causing these good men and good women to generate the aspiration for anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi and aspire to be born in that land. Having been born there, they will never retrogress from anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi."

At that time the World-Honored One, having accepted this request, emitted from between his brows white ūrṇā radiance that universally illuminated the great trichiliocosm. The grasses, trees, earth, and stones of this world, Mount Sumeru, the Mucilinda Mountains, the Great Mucilinda Mountains, the Cakravāḍa and Great Cakravāḍa Mountains, and even the dark spaces between worlds all became golden in color, shining with great brightness. The radiance of the sun and moon and all their mighty splendor entirely vanished. The light illuminated the western lands for a hundred thousand koṭis of realms, reaching the world of Sukhāvatī, making everything golden. The great light circled to the right around that buddha seven times and then vanished before the Tathāgata.

The sentient beings, bodhisattvas, and śrāvakas of that land all saw this world and Śākyamuni with his great assembly, surrounded as he taught the Dharma, as clearly as one observes an āmalaka fruit in the palm of one's hand. They all gave rise to minds of love, delight, and joy, and proclaimed: "Homage to Śākyamuni Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha!"

In this assembly, the bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, upāsikās, devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, humans and non-humans, Śakra, Brahmā, the four heavenly kings, and the bodhisattvas and śrāvakas all saw Amitābha Buddha in the world of Sukhāvatī, surrounded by his bodhisattva and śrāvaka retinue. Radiant like a jeweled mountain, lofty and supremely distinctive, his majestic light blazed forth, universally illuminating all lands. It was as clear as a person with clean eyes seeing a person's face within arm's reach, without any obstruction. Having seen this, they were joyful and exultant, and proclaimed: "Homage to Amitābha Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha!"

At that time, eighty-four thousand beings in this assembly all generated the aspiration for anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi and, planting good roots, aspired to be born in that land.

At that time the bodhisattvas and śrāvakas in the world of Sukhāvatī, having seen this land, were astonished at this unprecedented event and joyfully pressed their palms together, bowing to Śākyamuni Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, saying: "Homage to Śākyamuni Buddha, who is able to teach such Dharma for bodhisattvas and śrāvakas!"

At that time the world of Sukhāvatī quaked in six ways: trembling, trembling everywhere, trembling equally everywhere; shaking, shaking everywhere, shaking equally everywhere; quaking, quaking everywhere, quaking equally everywhere.

At that time the Bodhisattvas Mahāsattvas Avalokiteśvara and Mahāsthāmaprāpta said to that buddha: "How extraordinary, World-Honored One! Śākyamuni Tathāgata has manifested rare events. Why? Śākyamuni Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha has merely manifested his name and title, yet the great earth of Non-Conception has quaked in six ways."

At that time Amitābha Buddha told those bodhisattvas: "Śākyamuni manifests his name and title not only in this land but also in all other immeasurable buddha-worlds. The great light universally illuminating and the six kinds of quaking are likewise. In those worlds, immeasurable asaṃkhyeyas of sentient beings, upon hearing Śākyamuni's praised name and title, accomplish their good roots and all become non-retrogressive from anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi."

At that time, forty billion bodhisattvas in that assembly, upon hearing the name and title of Śākyamuni Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, together made vows and dedicated their good roots toward anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi. The Buddha then gave them the prediction that they would attain anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi.

At that time the Bodhisattvas Mahāsattvas Avalokiteśvara and Mahāsthāmaprāpta went to where that buddha was, bowed their heads at his feet, reverently pressed their palms together, stood to one side, and said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One, for what cause and reason has Śākyamuni emitted this radiance?"

At that time that buddha told Avalokiteśvara: "The Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha has not emitted this radiance without cause or reason. Why? Today Śākyamuni Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha is about to teach the Bodhisattva's Jewel-Treasury Samādhi Sūtra, and therefore has first manifested this auspicious sign."

At that time the Bodhisattvas Mahāsattvas Avalokiteśvara and Mahāsthāmaprāpta said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One, we wish to go to the Sahā world to bow, make offerings to, and worship Śākyamuni Buddha, and to hear his teaching."

The Buddha said: "Good men, this is the right time."

Then the two bodhisattvas said to each other: "Today we shall surely hear the wondrous Dharma taught by that buddha."

Then the two bodhisattvas, having received the Buddha's instruction, told their retinue of forty billion bodhisattvas: "Good men, let us all go together to the Sahā world to bow, make offerings to, and worship Śākyamuni Buddha, and to hear and receive the true Dharma. Why? Śākyamuni Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha is able to do what is difficult: he abandoned a pure and wonderful land, and by the power of his original vows, with a mind of great compassion, in a turbid, evil world of beings with little virtue and few blessings, with increasing greed, anger, and delusion, he realized anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi and teaches the Dharma."

When these words were spoken, the bodhisattvas and śrāvakas all together exclaimed: "Those beings who hear the name of Śākyamuni Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha have truly gained excellent benefit, let alone those who, having seen him, give rise to joyful minds! World-Honored One, we should all go together to that world to bow, make offerings to, and worship Śākyamuni Buddha."

The Buddha said: "Good men, this is the right time."

At that time the Bodhisattvas Mahāsattvas Avalokiteśvara and Mahāsthāmaprāpta, together with the forty billion bodhisattvas, surrounded by their retinue, in that world used their spiritual powers, each transforming for their retinue forty billion adorned jeweled platforms. These jeweled platforms were twelve yojanas in length and breadth, dignified and subtly wonderful. On these jeweled platforms, some parts were gold, some silver, some vaiḍūrya, some crystal, some red pearls, some cornelian, some agate. Some had two treasures: gold and silver. Some had three: gold, silver, and vaiḍūrya. Some had four: gold, silver, vaiḍūrya, and crystal. Some had five: gold, silver, vaiḍūrya, crystal, and red pearls. Some had six: gold, silver, vaiḍūrya, crystal, cornelian, and red pearls. Some had seven treasures, including agate. They were further adorned with red pearls, sandalwood, utpala flowers, padma flowers, kumuda flowers, and puṇḍarīka flowers.

There rained down sumanā flowers, campaka flowers, pāṭala flowers, atimukta flowers, varuṇa flowers, kuraṇṭaka flowers, mandārava flowers, great mandārava flowers, pāruṣya flowers, great pāruṣya flowers, mañjūṣaka flowers, great mañjūṣaka flowers, rucana flowers, great rucana flowers, jāka flowers, great jāka flowers, sūrya-jāka flowers, candana flowers, great candana flowers, sūryacandana flowers, canottara flowers, tāla flowers, and great tāla flowers. The jeweled platforms displayed various colors, brilliant and radiant, pure and shining.

On the jeweled platforms there were eighty-four thousand transformed jade maidens, some holding harps, lutes, zithers, flutes, pípas, drums, and conch shells, and immeasurable such jeweled instruments, playing subtle and wonderful music, standing in dignity. Some jade maidens held red sandalwood incense, agarwood-sandalwood incense, or dark agarwood-sandalwood incense, standing in dignity. Some jade maidens held utpala, padma, kumuda, and puṇḍarīka flowers, standing in dignity. Some jade maidens held mandārava, great mandārava, pāruṣya, great pāruṣya, rucana, great rucana, candana, great candana, sūrya-candana, jāka, great jāka, sūrya-jāka, tāla, great tāla, and sūrya-tāla flowers, standing adorned. Some jade maidens held all manner of flowers and fruits, standing in dignity.

On the jeweled platforms were jewel-adorned lion-thrones, and on each throne sat a transformed buddha, adorned with the thirty-two marks and eighty minor characteristics. Above each platform hung eighty-four thousand strands of blue, yellow, red, white, and multicolored true pearls. On each jeweled platform were eighty-four thousand exquisite jeweled vases, filled with powdered incense, arrayed upon them.

On each jeweled platform were eighty-four thousand jeweled canopies spread above.

On each jeweled platform were eighty-four thousand jeweled trees planted.

On each jeweled platform were eighty-four thousand jeweled bells arrayed above. Between the jeweled trees were seven-treasure pools, filled with eight-merit-quality water. Blue, yellow, red, white, and multicolored jeweled lotuses shone with fresh, radiant colors. A gentle breeze stirred the rows of jeweled trees, producing subtle and wonderful sounds, harmonious and elegant, surpassing celestial music.

On each jeweled platform were eighty-four thousand exquisite jeweled ropes strung between the trees. The radiance of each jeweled platform illuminated eighty-four thousand yojanas with great brightness.

At that time the Bodhisattvas Mahāsattvas Avalokiteśvara and Mahāsthāmaprāpta, together with their retinue of eighty billion bodhisattvas, with adorned jeweled platforms all equal, as quickly as a strong man bends and extends his arm, vanished from that land and arrived in this world.

Then those bodhisattvas, by their spiritual powers, made the ground of this world level and smooth as water. With eighty billion bodhisattvas surrounding them, they were accomplished in great meritorious adornments, dignified and supremely distinctive beyond all comparison, their radiance universally illuminating the Sahā world.

These bodhisattvas went to where Śākyamuni Buddha was, bowed their heads at his feet, circumambulated him seven times to the right, withdrew to stand to one side, and said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One, Amitābha Buddha inquires after the World-Honored One: Are you free from illness, free from distress? Are your daily activities at ease and comfortable? Are you practicing in peace and happiness?"

When they further displayed the wondrous adornments of that land, the bodhisattvas and śrāvakas of this assembly, seeing these jeweled platforms with their wonderful adornments, exclaimed that this was unprecedented, and each had this thought: "These jeweled platforms, so wonderfully adorned, have come from the land of Sukhāvatī to this world. Is this the Buddha's power? Or the bodhisattvas' power?"

At that time the Bodhisattva Flower-Virtue Treasury, empowered by the Buddha's spiritual power, said to the Buddha: "How extraordinary! World-Honored One! Unprecedented! Now this Sahā world has jeweled platforms so wonderfully adorned. Whose power is this?"

The Buddha said: "It is the spiritual power of Avalokiteśvara and Mahāsthāmaprāpta that manifests these great adornments in this world."

"How extraordinary, World-Honored One! Inconceivable! These good men, whose vows and practices are pure, are able by their spiritual power to adorn jeweled platforms and manifest them in this world."

The Buddha said: "So it is! So it is! As you say. These good men, throughout countless koṭis of nayutas of hundreds of thousands of kalpas, have purified their good roots and attained the Illusion-Like Samādhi. Abiding in this samādhi, they are able by their spiritual transformations to manifest such things. Furthermore, Flower-Virtue Treasury, now look to the east and tell me what you see."

Then Flower-Virtue Treasury, with the various celestial eyes of a bodhisattva, gazed toward the east throughout Gaṅgā-sands' worth of buddha-worlds. He saw that before each buddha, Avalokiteśvara and Mahāsthāmaprāpta were present, adorned as before, making reverent offerings, each saying: "Amitābha Buddha inquires after the World-Honored One: Are you free from illness, free from distress? Are your daily activities at ease and comfortable? Are you practicing in peace and happiness?" In the south, west, and north, in the four intermediate directions, above and below, it was likewise.

At that time the Bodhisattva Flower-Virtue Treasury, having seen these things, was joyful and exultant at this unprecedented event, and said to the Buddha: "How extraordinary, World-Honored One! These great beings have accomplished such a samādhi. Why? These great beings are able to manifest such adornments in all these buddha-lands."

At that time the World-Honored One, by his spiritual power, caused this assembly to see these things, and thirty-two thousand people generated the aspiration for anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi.

The Bodhisattva Flower-Virtue Treasury said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One, how long ago did these two great beings generate the aspiration for anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi, and in the presence of which buddha? I pray you explain this, so that the bodhisattvas may cultivate these vows and practices to full accomplishment."

The Buddha said: "Listen carefully and ponder well. I shall explain for you."

"Excellent, World-Honored One! I delight in wishing to hear."

The Buddha said: "Long ago, in a vast, distant, immeasurable, inconceivable asaṃkhyeya kalpa, I was at that time a king through hundreds and thousands of reigns. At the time when the first great king-kalpa was about to end, there was a world called Immeasurable Virtue Accumulation Joyful Manifestation. In that land there was a buddha named Golden Light Lion Sport Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, Endowed with Knowledge and Conduct, Well-Gone One, Knower of the World, Unsurpassed One, Tamer of Persons, Teacher of Devas and Humans, Buddha, World-Honored One. The pure adornments of that buddha-land I shall now describe for you. What do you think? The pure adornments of the land of Amitābha Buddha in the world of Sukhāvatī: are they many or not?"

He replied: "Very many! Inconceivable, difficult to fully describe."

The Buddha told Flower-Virtue Treasury: "Suppose someone were to split a single hair into a hundred hairs, and with one such strand of hair, draw up a drop of the great ocean. What do you think? Between the water on the tip of that hair and the water of the great ocean, which is greater?"

He replied: "The ocean water is vastly greater, beyond comparison."

"Just so, Flower-Virtue Treasury. You should know that the adornments of Amitābha's land are like the water on the tip of that hair, while the land of Golden Light Lion Sport Buddha is like the great ocean. The difference in śrāvakas and bodhisattvas is likewise. That Golden Light Lion Sport Tathāgata also taught the Dharma of the three vehicles for sentient beings. Even if for Gaṅgā-sands' worth of kalpas I were to describe the meritorious adornments of that buddha-land and the joys of its bodhisattvas and śrāvakas, I could never exhaust them. At that time, in the Dharma of Golden Light Lion Sport Tathāgata, there was a king named Mighty Virtue King. He ruled with the true Dharma over a thousand worlds and was called the Dharma King. This Mighty Virtue King had many sons, all endowed with the twenty-eight marks of a great person. All these princes were established on the unsurpassed path. The king had seventy-six thousand park-gardens, in which his sons sported."

Flower-Virtue Treasury said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One, were there women in that buddha-land?"

The Buddha said: "Good man, in that buddha-land there was not even the name 'woman,' let alone actual women. The beings of that land cultivated the brahma-practice in purity, all were born by transformation, and fed on the joy of meditation. That Mighty Virtue King, for eighty-four thousand hundred million years, served the Tathāgata and did not study any other dharma. The Buddha, knowing his sincerity, then taught him the Immeasurable Dharma-Seal. What is the Immeasurable Dharma-Seal? Flower-Virtue Treasury Bodhisattva, in all one cultivates, one should generate immeasurable vows. Why? The bodhisattva-mahāsattva's generosity is immeasurable, discipline is immeasurable, patience is immeasurable, vigor is immeasurable, meditation is immeasurable, and wisdom is immeasurable. The six pāramitās practiced to encompass birth and death are immeasurable. Compassion for sentient beings is immeasurable. The adornments of the pure land are immeasurable. Sound is immeasurable. Eloquence is immeasurable. Flower-Virtue Treasury, even a single thought of wholesome dedication is immeasurable. Why is the dedication immeasurable? Because one dedicates to all sentient beings, causing all sentient beings to attain the realization of non-arising, to enter nirvāṇa through the Buddha's nirvāṇa. This is called immeasurable dedication. Boundless space is immeasurable. Signlessness is immeasurable. Wishlessness is immeasurable. Non-action is thus, without desire. Ultimate reality, the dharma-nature, non-arising, non-attachment, liberation, and nirvāṇa are immeasurable. Good man, I have only briefly spoken of the immeasurability of dharmas. Why? Because all dharmas are without limit.

"Furthermore, Flower-Virtue Treasury, that Mighty Virtue King, in one of his park-gardens, entered samādhi. To the king's left and right, two lotus flowers sprang forth from the earth, adorned with various colors, their fragrance sweet, like celestial sandalwood. Two boys were born by transformation within them, sitting cross-legged. The first was named Jewel Intent, the second Jewel Superior. Then Mighty Virtue King arose from meditation, saw the two boys sitting in the lotus treasury, and asked in verse:

> "'Are you deva, nāga king, yakṣa, or kumbhāṇḍa?
> Are you human or non-human? Please declare your names.'

"Then the boy on the king's right answered in verse:

> "'All dharmas are empty; why ask for names?
> Past dharmas have already perished; future dharmas have not yet arisen.
> Present dharmas do not abide. Of whom do you ask the name?
> Empty dharmas are not persons, not nāgas, not rākṣasas.
> Humans and non-humans and all the rest: none can be apprehended.'

"The boy on the left spoke in verse:

> "'Names and namers are all empty. Names and namers are ungraspable.
> All dharmas are without names, yet you wish to ask for a name.
> If you seek a true name, it has never been seen or heard.
> What is born by nature perishes: how can you ask its name?
> Spoken names and words are all provisional designations.
> My name is Jewel Intent; his name is Jewel Superior.'

"Flower-Virtue Treasury, when these two boys had spoken these verses, together with Mighty Virtue King they went to where the Buddha was, bowed their heads at his feet, circumambulated him seven times to the right, and with joined palms and reverence, stood to one side.

"Then the two boys together, with one voice, asked the Buddha in verse:

> "'What constitutes an offering to the Unsurpassed Honored One of Two Feet?
> We pray you explain its meaning; those who hear shall put it into practice.
> Flowers, incense, and various music, clothing, food, medicine, and bedding:
> Among such offerings, what is supreme?'

"At that time that buddha spoke in verse for the boys:

> "'Generate the aspiration for bodhi and broadly save all beings:
> This is the offering to the Perfectly Enlightened One of thirty-two radiant marks.
> Even if one filled Gaṅgā-sands' worth of lands with precious and wonderful adornments
> And offered them to all tathāgatas, who joyfully received them atop their heads,
> It would not compare to a compassionate mind dedicated toward bodhi.
> This merit is supreme, immeasurable and without limit.
> No other offering surpasses it; it transcends all calculation.
> Such an aspiration for bodhi will assuredly achieve perfect awakening.'

"Then the two boys spoke further in verse:

> "'All devas, nāgas, ghosts, and spirits, hear my lion's roar!
> Today before the Tathāgata, I make vast vows and generate the aspiration for bodhi.
> Through immeasurable kalpas of birth and death, the original beginning is unknowable.
> For the sake of even one sentient being, through that many kalpas I would practice the path.
> How much more so throughout these kalpas, liberating immeasurable beings,
> Cultivating the path of bodhi, would I give rise to a mind of weariness?
> If from this day forward I give rise to a mind of desire,
> Then I deceive and delude all the buddhas of the ten directions.
> Anger, delusion, and defilement, stinginess and jealousy likewise.
> Now I speak truthful words, far from all falsehood.
> If from this day forward I give rise to the mind of a śrāvaka,
> Not delighting in cultivating bodhi, then I deceive the World-Honored One.
> Nor will I seek the pratyekabuddha path, saving only myself alone.
> Throughout ten thousand koṭis of kalpas, with great compassion I will save sentient beings.
> As with the buddha-land of today, pure and wonderfully adorned,
> When I attain the path, may it surpass this by a hundred thousand koṭis.
> May my land have no śrāvaka assemblies, nor the pratyekabuddha vehicle,
> But only bodhisattvas, their number without limit.
> May sentient beings be pure and stainless, endowed with supreme and wonderful joy,
> Born from perfect awakening, maintaining the treasury of all dharmas.
> If this vow is sincere and true, may it shake the great trichiliocosm.'
> When they had spoken these verses, at that moment there was universal quaking.
> Hundreds and thousands of musical instruments played harmonious and elegant sounds.
> Radiant and subtly wonderful garments spiraled down from above.
> The devas in the sky rained down various powdered incense,
> Whose fragrance spread and perfumed all, delighting the minds of sentient beings."

The Buddha told Flower-Virtue Treasury: "What do you think? At that time, was Mighty Virtue King some other person? He was myself. The two boys at that time are the present Bodhisattvas Mahāsattvas Avalokiteśvara and Mahāsthāmaprāpta. Good man, these two bodhisattvas, in the presence of that buddha, first generated the aspiration for anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi."

At that time Flower-Virtue Treasury said to the Buddha: "How extraordinary, World-Honored One! These good men, who had not yet generated the aspiration, had already accomplished such profound wisdom and penetrated the ungraspability of names. World-Honored One, had these two great beings already made offerings and performed meritorious deeds in the presence of former buddhas?"

"Good man, all the sands of the Gaṅgā can be counted, but the prior offerings these great beings have made to buddhas and the good roots they have planted are beyond reckoning. Although they had not yet generated the aspiration for bodhi, they had already adorned themselves with the inconceivable and, among all sentient beings, were the most heroic and valiant."

At that time the Bodhisattva Flower-Virtue Treasury said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One, in what direction is the land called Immeasurable Virtue Accumulation Joyful Manifestation?"

The Buddha said: "Good man, the present world of Sukhāvatī in the west was, at that time, called Immeasurable Virtue Accumulation Joyful Manifestation."

The Bodhisattva Flower-Virtue Treasury said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One, I pray you explain this so that immeasurable sentient beings may gain great benefit. In what land will Avalokiteśvara realize perfect awakening? What will the world's adornments, radiance, and name be? What of the śrāvakas, bodhisattvas, and lifespans, through to the accomplishment of buddhahood? If the World-Honored One describes this bodhisattva's former vows and practices, the other bodhisattvas, upon hearing these vows, will surely cultivate them to fulfillment."

The Buddha said: "Excellent! Listen carefully, I shall explain for you."

He replied: "Indeed! I delight in wishing to hear."

The Buddha said: "Good man, the lifespan of Amitābha Buddha is immeasurable hundreds of thousands of hundreds of millions of kalpas, but there will be an end. Good man, in the vastly distant, incalculable future, Amitābha Buddha will enter parinirvāṇa. After his parinirvāṇa, the true Dharma will remain in the world for a period equal to that Buddha's lifespan. The sentient beings liberated during his lifetime and after his passing will be equal in number. After the Buddha's nirvāṇa, there may be sentient beings who do not see the Buddha, but there will be bodhisattvas who, having attained the Samādhi of Mindfulness of the Buddha, will constantly see Amitābha Buddha. Furthermore, good man, after that Buddha's passing, all the jeweled objects, bathing pools, lotuses, rows of jeweled trees will constantly proclaim the Dharma-sound, no different from when the Buddha was present. Good man, after the true Dharma of Amitābha Buddha has been extinguished, past the middle of the night, when the dawn-sign appears, the Bodhisattva Avalokiteśvara, beneath the seven-jeweled bodhi tree, will sit in the cross-legged posture and realize perfect awakening. He will be called Universal Radiance Merit Mountain King Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, Endowed with Knowledge and Conduct, Well-Gone One, Knower of the World, Unsurpassed One, Tamer of Persons, Teacher of Devas and Humans, Buddha, World-Honored One. That buddha-land will be naturally composed of the seven treasures, with wondrous adornments. The buddha world-honored ones could not exhaust the description even in Gaṅgā-sands' worth of kalpas. Good man, I now offer you an analogy: the adornments of the land of Golden Light Lion Sport Tathāgata, compared to the land of Universal Radiance Merit Mountain King Tathāgata, would be a hundred-thousandfold, a hundred-millionfold, a hundred-trillion-fold less, and even beyond all calculation. That buddha-land will have no names of śrāvakas or pratyekabuddhas; only bodhisattvas will fill that land."

The Bodhisattva Flower-Virtue Treasury said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One, will that buddha-land be called Sukhāvatī?"

The Buddha said: "Good man, that buddha-land will be called All Treasures Universally Gathered Adornment. Good man, Universal Radiance Merit Mountain King Tathāgata will live out his lifespan. The Bodhisattva Mahāsthāmaprāpta will personally attend and make offerings until his nirvāṇa. After his parinirvāṇa, he will uphold the true Dharma until its complete extinction. After the Dharma is completely extinguished, in that very land he will realize anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi and will be called Good Abiding Merit Jewel King Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, Endowed with Knowledge and Conduct, Well-Gone One, Knower of the World, Unsurpassed One, Tamer of Persons, Teacher of Devas and Humans, Buddha, World-Honored One. His buddha-land's radiance, lifespan, and bodhisattva assembly, through to the duration of the Dharma, will be no different from Universal Radiance Merit Mountain King Tathāgata's. If any good man or good woman hears the name of Good Abiding Merit Jewel King Tathāgata, they will all become non-retrogressive from anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi. Furthermore, good man, if any woman hears the names of the past Golden Light Lion Sport Tathāgata and Good Abiding Merit Jewel King Tathāgata, she will be freed from the female body, will eliminate the transgressions of forty billion kalpas of birth and death, and will become non-retrogressive from anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi. She will constantly see buddhas, hear and receive the true Dharma, and make offerings to the saṃgha. Having relinquished this body, she will go forth and attain unobstructed eloquence and swiftly gain the power of retention."

At that time, sixty billion beings in the assembly exclaimed with one voice: "Homage to the buddhas of the ten directions who have entered parinirvāṇa!" With one mind they resolved together and generated the aspiration for anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi. The Buddha then gave them the prediction that they would attain anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi.

There were also eighty-four thousand nayutas of sentient beings who, becoming distant from defilements, obtained the pure Dharma-eye regarding all dharmas. Seven thousand bhikṣus had their outflows exhausted and their minds liberated.

At that time the Bodhisattvas Avalokiteśvara and Mahāsthāmaprāpta, by their spiritual power, caused this entire assembly to see the innumerable buddha world-honored ones of the ten directions, all giving these two great beings the prediction of anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi. Having seen this, they exclaimed: "How extraordinary, World-Honored One! These tathāgatas give such predictions for these great beings!"

At that time the Bodhisattva Flower-Virtue Treasury said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One, if any good men or good women receive, uphold, read, recite, explain, copy, and widely circulate this profound sūtra of the Tathāgata, how much merit will they gain? I pray the Tathāgata explain in detail. Why? In the evil age to come, beings of little virtue will not have faith in or accept this profound sūtra of the Tathāgata, and because of this, they will suffer through the long night and find liberation difficult. World-Honored One, I pray you explain this, out of compassion, for the benefit of all sentient beings. World-Honored One, in this present assembly there are many good men and good women of sharp faculties who will become great lights in the age to come."

The Buddha said: "Flower-Virtue Treasury, excellent! Listen carefully, I shall explain for you."

He replied: "I accept the teaching! I delight in wishing to hear."

The Buddha said: "If a good man were to place all the sentient beings of the great trichiliocosm on his two shoulders and, for the entirety of their lives, provide them with whatever they needed, whether clothing, food, bedding, medicine, and make offerings, would the merit gained be great or not?"

"Very great, World-Honored One! If with a compassionate mind one provides even one sentient being with what they need, the merit is immeasurable, let alone providing for all."

The Buddha said: "If a good man or good woman, regarding this sūtra, receives, upholds, reads, recites, explains, copies, makes various offerings, and widely circulates it, and generates the aspiration for bodhi, the merit gained surpasses the former by hundreds of thousands of ten-thousands of times, beyond all comparison."

The Bodhisattva Flower-Virtue Treasury said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One, from this day forward, regarding this sūtra taught by the Tathāgata, and the names of the three buddhas of past and future, I shall constantly receive, uphold, read, recite, explain, copy, and widely circulate it. I will be far from the minds of desire, anger, and delusion, and will generate the aspiration for anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi, never speaking falsely. World-Honored One, when I attain buddhahood, if any woman hears this Dharma, she will be transformed out of the female body in this present life. Having been transformed, I shall give her the prediction of anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi. She shall be called Free-from-Defilement Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha."

When this sūtra was taught, the Bodhisattva Mahāsattva Flower-Virtue Treasury, the bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, bodhisattvas, śrāvakas, devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, humans and non-humans, having heard what the Buddha taught, all greatly rejoiced.
