# 佛說造立形像福報經
## Sūtra of the Reward of Merit from Making Images, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0693

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown

---

The Buddha came to the land of Kauśāmbī. There was a garden owner named Ghosita.

At that time the king of the land was named Udayana, aged fourteen. Hearing that the Buddha was coming, the king immediately ordered his ministers and attendants to prepare a grand procession. The king set out to welcome the Buddha. Seeing the World-Honored One from afar, his heart leaped and he was exceedingly joyful. He halted his chariot and descended to walk, dismissing his ministers, attendants, retinue, and the parasol-bearers. He went forward to meet the Buddha, bowed with his head to the ground, rose and went forward again, and did this three times.

The Buddha said: "Come forward."

The king went further forward, placed his head at the Buddha's feet, rose, circumambulated three times, knelt with palms joined, and said to the Buddha: "Among all the devas, humans, and people above and below, none equals the Buddha! Now I see the Buddha's face, body, and bearing; his radiant splendor is majestic and wonderful. I gaze upon the Buddha and never grow weary. The Buddha is the great teacher of all the devas, humans, and people above and below. The Buddha's compassion and the multitude he has rescued and saved are very great." The Buddha was silent and did not respond.

The king further said to the Buddha: "When a person does good, what reward of blessing will they receive? After the Buddha departs, I fear I will no longer see the Buddha. I now wish to make an image of the Buddha and serve it with reverence. What reward will I obtain? I wish the Buddha to have compassion and explain this for me. I wish to hear and understand."

At that time the Buddha told the young king: "Your question is very good! Listen carefully to what I say, and keep it in your heart."

The king bowed his head and said: "I accept your teaching."

The Buddha told the king: "If anyone makes an image of the Buddha, the reward of blessing they obtain, I shall now explain it all for you."

The king said: "Yes! I am grateful."

The Buddha said: "Anyone in the world who is able to make an image of the Buddha: in every future life wherever they are born, their eyes will be pure and clear, their face and features upright and handsome, and their body, hands, and feet will always be fine and soft. Born in heaven, they will also be pure and clear, surpassing all the other devas, with a face and features of incomparable beauty. Making a Buddha image, the reward is such as this.

"One who makes a Buddha image, wherever born, will be free from all evils and their body will be perfect and complete. After death, they will be born in the seventh Brahmā heaven, surpassing all other devas, upright and handsome beyond compare, revered by all the devas. Making a Buddha image, the reward is such as this.

"One who makes a Buddha image, in later lives will always be born into a distinguished and noble family, receiving its strength and standing, surpassing the world. Wherever born, they will not fall into a poor family. Making a Buddha image, the reward is such as this.

"One who makes a Buddha image, in later lives wherever born, their form will be exceptional, the color of purple-golden jāmbūnada, upright and handsome beyond compare, always respected and loved by all. Making a Buddha image, the reward is such as this.

"One who makes a Buddha image, in later lives will always be born in a wealthy and noble family, with wealth, possessions, and treasures beyond counting, always cherished by parents, siblings, and kinfolk. Making a Buddha image, the reward is such as this.

"One who makes a Buddha image, in later lives, if born in Jambudvīpa, will always be born in the family of an emperor, a lord, or a worthy and good household. Making a Buddha image, the reward is such as this.

"One who makes a Buddha image, in later lives will become an emperor, supremely honored among all the kings of the nations, and all the kings will submit to them. Making a Buddha image, the reward is such as this.

"One who makes a Buddha image, in later lives will become a wheel-turning king, and the kings of all four continents will be his subjects. Making a Buddha image, the reward is such as this.

"One who makes a Buddha image, in later lives will become a wheel-turning king, flying to heaven and descending again at will, accomplishing everything without limit. Making a Buddha image, the reward is such as this.

"One who makes a Buddha image, in later lives will be born in the seventh Brahmā heaven, with a lifespan of more than one kalpa, with wisdom honored and supreme, and none will equal them. Making a Buddha image, the reward is such as this. When their lifespan in the Brahmā heaven ends, they will again descend and be born into a filial and virtuous family that practices the Way. Making a Buddha image, the reward is such as this.

"One who makes a Buddha image, in later lives wherever born, will always be cherished by their parents. At the end of their life, they will again be born in heaven. Making a Buddha image, the reward is such as this.

"One who makes a Buddha image, after death will never again fall into the three evil paths. One will keep one's own mind and always aspire to seek the Buddha.

"One who makes a Buddha image, in later lives wherever born, will revere the sūtras and always wish to offer fine incense and flowers, fine silks, lamps and light, and the most precious and rare things of the world to the Buddha's stūpas. After innumerable kalpas, one will certainly attain the Buddha's path of nirvāṇa. Those who, with a devoted mind, offer precious and beloved things to the Buddha are no ordinary persons. They are all those who in past lives made Buddha images and practiced the bodhisattva path. Making a Buddha image, the reward is such as this.

"The reward of making a Buddha image is without limit, without end, beyond all reckoning. Even the waters of all the rivers and oceans of the four continents can be measured by the cupful, but the reward of making a Buddha image is many times greater, surpassing the rivers and oceans of the four continents tenfold. In later lives, one will always protect the Buddha-path.

"Making a Buddha image is like having a good shelter when it rains from the sky: one has nothing to worry about or fear.

"One who makes a Buddha image, after death will never again enter the evil paths of hell, animals, or hungry ghosts. Anyone among sentient beings who sees a Buddha image and, with a reverent heart, joins their palms and takes refuge in the Buddha's stūpa and relics: for a hundred kalpas after death they will not enter the evil paths of hell, animals, or hungry ghosts. Upon death they will be born in heaven. When their heavenly lifespan ends, they will be born again in the human world in a distinguished and wealthy family, with riches and treasures beyond counting. Thus the reward of blessing is incalculable, and they will certainly attain the Buddha's path of nirvāṇa."

The Buddha told King Udayana: "When a person does good and makes a Buddha image, the reward of blessing is such as this; it is never in vain."

At that time, King Udayana praised the Buddha in verse:

> The Buddha, the great sage,
> Teaches the Dharma for sentient beings.
> At the garden of Ghosita in Kauśāmbī,
> Udayana asked with palms joined.
> Hearing the Brahmā voice, true and precise,
> Unmoved, a hundred merits complete:
> "One who makes a Buddha image,
> What reward of merit will they obtain?"

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke in verse in reply:

> King, listen well as I explain:
> The blessed ground surpasses the highest earth.
> No merit exceeds
> The reward of making a Buddha image.
> Always born in a great and wealthy family,
> Noble and precious beyond measure;
> Kinfolk always revere:
> The reward of making a Buddha image.
> In every life, the body is free from illness;
> One always obtains the divine eye's reward,
> Of an incomparable deep-blue hue:
> The reward of making a Buddha image.
> Parents see and rejoice;
> One's form is dignified and commanding.
> They delight and never grow weary:
> The reward of making a Buddha image.
> A golden body blazing with radiance,
> Like a wondrous lion image;
> Sentient beings see and are delighted:
> The reward of making a Buddha image.
> In Jambudvīpa's great families,
> Kṣatriyas and brahmins:
> The blessed are born among them:
> The reward of making a Buddha image.
> Not born in a border land,
> Not blind, not ugly or deformed;
> The six faculties always complete:
> The reward of making a Buddha image.
> At life's end, remembering past lives,
> One sees the Buddha before them;
> One does not feel the pain of death:
> The reward of making a Buddha image.
> Becoming a king of great renown,
> A golden-wheeled flying emperor,
> Ruling the four continents:
> The reward of making a Buddha image.
> Becoming Śakra, named Indra,
> With divine feet ruling the second heaven,
> Honored by the Thirty-Three:
> The reward of making a Buddha image.
> Transcending the desire realm,
> Becoming a Brahmā, a Brahmā King;
> All of Kāyika Brahmā revere:
> The reward of making a Buddha image.
> The blessings one receives are exactly so,
> Whether carved, painted, or fashioned.
> Heaven and earth may be weighed and measured,
> But this merit is beyond all reckoning.
> Therefore make offerings to the Buddha:
> Flowers, incense, fragrant ointment.
> One who makes offerings to the great sage
> Attains the exhaustion of outflows, the unconditioned.

When the Buddha had finished speaking the sūtra, the king was greatly delighted. He rose, went forward, and placed his head at the Buddha's feet. The bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, and upāsikās, together with his five hundred ministers and attendants, all rose with joy, bowed to the Buddha, and afterward were all born in the land of Amitābha Buddha, becoming great bodhisattvas of the highest and foremost rank. King Udayana, having heard the teaching, joyfully received it with understanding and immediately attained the fruit of stream-entry.
