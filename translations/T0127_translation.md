# 佛說四人出現世間經
## Sūtra on Four Kinds of People Appearing in the World Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0127

Translated from the Chinese. Liu Song dynasty, translated by Guṇabhadra (求那跋陀羅)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Bhagavān was in the city of Śrāvastī, in the Jetavana, the garden of Anāthapiṇḍada. At that time, King Prasenajit mounted his feathered carriage, surrounded by ministers, and went out from the city of Śrāvastī to the garden where the World-Honored One was. He bowed his head at the Buddha's feet and sat to one side, wishing to hear the teaching of the Dharma.

The World-Honored One then addressed King Prasenajit: "There are four kinds of people who appear in the world. What are the four? There are those who are first low and afterward lofty. There are those who are first lofty and afterward low. There are those who are first low and afterward low. There are those who are first lofty and afterward lofty.

"How is a person first low and afterward lofty? There may be a person born into a lowly family: a caṇḍāla family, a butcher family, a craftsman family, a barber family, or a poor family where there is no grain and food is insufficient. Though food may sometimes be obtained, it is foul and wretched. Born into such a family, with an unpleasant appearance that people do not like to see, despised and looked down upon, yet such a person practices good with the body, practices good with speech, and practices good with the mind. Having cultivated good conduct of body, speech, and mind, when this person sees śramaṇas and brāhmaṇas, a humble intention arises, and they attend to them with respect without missing the proper occasions. When they see people making offerings, they rejoice and leap with delight. At the time of death, they are born in a good destination in heaven. It is like a person who goes from the ground to a small bed, from a small bed to a large bed, from a large bed to a horse, from a horse to an elephant, and from an elephant to a great lecture hall. For this reason I say this person is first low and afterward lofty. Thus is such a person first low and afterward lofty.

"How is a person first lofty and afterward low? There may be a person born into a noble family: a great kṣatriya family, a great brāhmaṇa family, a great householder family, a royal family, a crown prince's family, a minister's family, or other great families. Their complexion is beautiful beyond compare, like a peach blossom. Yet such a person practices evil with the body, practices evil with speech, and practices evil with the mind. When they see śramaṇas, brāhmaṇas, and respected elders, they have no mind of respect, do not pay homage, and do not converse with them. When they see people making offerings, they give rise to a mind of jealousy. These are comprised of wrong views and doubting views: holding the wrong view that there is no gift-giving, no merit, no receiver of offerings, no fruit of good or evil actions, no this world or next world, no father or mother, and no arhats or practitioners of the excellent way in the world who swiftly attain realization and dwell in bliss. Because of being established in such evil wrong views, at the time of death they are born in the three evil destinies and in hell. It is like a person who descends from a lecture hall to the back of an elephant, from an elephant to a horse, from a horse to a large bed, from a large bed to a small bed, from a small bed head-first to the ground. For this reason I say this person is first lofty and afterward low. Thus is such a person first lofty and afterward low.

"How is a person first low and afterward low? There may be a person born into a lowly family: a caṇḍāla family, a butcher family, a craftsman family, a barber family, or other poor and lowly families where there is no food or drink, or though food may sometimes be obtained, it is foul and difficult to taste. Born into such a family, such a person practices evil with the body, practices evil with speech, and practices evil with the mind. Having practiced evil conduct of body, speech, and mind, when they see śramaṇas, brāhmaṇas, and respected elders, they are not respectful, do not pay homage, and do not converse with them. They hold wrong views concordant with doubting views, holding the view that there is no gift-giving, no merit, no receiver of offerings, no fruit of good or evil actions, no this world or next world, no father or mother, and no śramaṇas, brāhmaṇas, or practitioners in the world, no arhats who in this world or the next swiftly attain realization and dwell in bliss. Concordant with wrong views, at the time of death they are born in the three destinies and enter hell. It is like a person who goes from darkness to darkness, from obscurity to obscurity. For this reason I say this person is first low and afterward low. Thus is such a person first low and afterward low.

"How is a person first lofty and afterward lofty? There may be a person born into a noble family: a great kṣatriya family, a great brāhmaṇa family, a great householder family, a royal family, a crown prince's family, a minister's family, extremely wealthy with abundant treasures. Their complexion is beautiful beyond compare, like a peach blossom. Such a person cultivates good conduct of body, cultivates good conduct of speech, and cultivates good conduct of mind. Having cultivated good conduct of body, speech, and mind, when they see śramaṇas, brāhmaṇas, and respected elders, they are respectful, pay homage, make offerings, and provide for them. When they see people making offerings, attending to them with respect, they give rise to joy. They hold right views concordant with non-inverted views, holding the view that there is gift-giving, there is merit, there is a receiver of offerings, there is fruit of good and evil actions, there is this world and the next, there are father and mother, and there are śramaṇas, brāhmaṇas, and practitioners of the holy life in the world, and arhats who in this world and the next swiftly attain realization and dwell in bliss. Because of being established in good views, at the time of death they are born in heaven. It is like a person who goes from one lecture hall to another, from one high terrace to another, traveling from one palace to the next. For this reason I say this person is first lofty and afterward lofty. Thus is such a person first lofty and afterward lofty. Thus, great king, there are four kinds of people who appear in the world."

Then the World-Honored One spoke these verses:

> "Great king! A person who is poor and lowly, yet who has faith and delights in giving,
> Who sees śramaṇas, brāhmaṇas, and all who come begging,
> Who attends to them with respectful homage, equally cultivating all good karma,
> Who sees giving and is always joyful, and also gives to those who beg:
> Such giving is a wonderful karma, and one no longer receives defilement.
> Thus, great king, such a person, at the time of death,
> Is born in the Heaven of the Thirty-Three: first low and afterward lofty.
>
> "Great king! A person who has wealth, yet lacks faith and harbors jealousy,
> Who always wishes to practice misconduct, who holds wrong views without a teacher,
> Who sees śramaṇas, brāhmaṇas, and all who come begging,
> Who slanders and constantly reviles them, who is miserly and greedy as if having nothing,
> Who sees giving and blocks it, and does not give to those who beg:
> Such a life is not wonderful karma, and such a person receives defilement.
> Thus, great king, such a person, approaching the time of death,
> Will surely be born in hell: first lofty and afterward low.
>
> "Great king! A person who is poor and lowly, lacking faith, with a miserly and greedy mind,
> Who always wishes to practice misconduct, who holds wrong views without a teacher,
> Who sees śramaṇas, brāhmaṇas, and all who come begging,
> Who slanders and constantly reviles them, who is miserly and greedy, saying he has no wealth,
> Who sees giving and blocks it, and does not give to those who beg:
> Such a life is not wonderful karma, and such a person receives defilement.
> Thus, great king, such a person, approaching the time of death,
> Will surely be born in hell: first low and afterward low.
>
> "Great king! A person who has abundant wealth, with good faith and who constantly gives,
> Who sees śramaṇas, brāhmaṇas, and all who come begging,
> Who attends to them with respectful homage, equally cultivating all good karma,
> Who sees giving and is always joyful, and also gives to those who beg:
> This is the world's wonderful karma, and one no longer receives defilement.
> Thus, great king, such a person, approaching the time of death,
> Is born in the Heaven of the Thirty-Three: first lofty and afterward lofty.

"Therefore, great king, you should train in this way. Thus, great king, you should train to be first lofty and afterward lofty, and not learn to be first low and afterward low. Thus, great king, you should train."

At that time, King Prasenajit, having heard what the Buddha taught, joyfully practiced in accordance.
