# 禮懺文
## Text for Worship and Confession

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 2855

Translated from the Chinese. Author unknown.

---

*[Opening fragmentary:]* ...press to the ground, with both hands embracing... be reverent. Do not use a scattered and casual mind. Single-mindedly concentrate, for this is a weighty matter. If one is not earnest and solemn in one's sincerity, the buddhas will descend but not take one in. The petition and request will also be nullified, and one will instead incur the transgression of disrespect. One must diligently contemplate. Like cool water for one who is ill, like good medicine that heals and cures.

Your disciple So-and-so and others, with one mind, take refuge in the lives of all buddhas and world-honored ones throughout the ten directions, three times, and the entirety of space, great compassionate fathers who have pity on sentient beings, who do not forsake their original vows, who do not forsake their compassion.

All such buddhas, throughout immeasurable, countless great kalpas, have broadly practiced every difficult deed, benefiting self and others, performing austere practices difficult to perform. For the sake of beings like us, they gave up head, eyes, marrow, brain, blood, flesh, and bodily parts, kingdoms, wife and children, elephants, horses, carriages, gold, silver, the seven treasures, clothing, food and drink, bedding, and medicine. Whatever anyone came seeking, they gave it all, without any reluctance, with hearts of joy. With courageous vigor, they cultivated the six perfections: generosity, precept-keeping, patience, vigor, dhyāna, and wisdom, skillful means, vows, and all the pāramitās. The twelve divisions of the sūtra-piṭaka, the exceedingly profound right Dharma, good at the beginning, good in the middle, good at the end, its meaning deep and far-reaching, its words skillful and wondrous, purely unmixed, fully endowed with the mark of the pure brahma-conduct. Relying on the pure, realized wisdom of all buddhas, established through the single awakened understanding: if any sentient being upholds even a single four-line verse, this surpasses generosity of bodies as numerous as the sands of the Ganges. The ten powers, fearlessness, great compassion, the three mindfulnesses, the four fearlessnesses, the four unobstructed knowledges, great kindness, great compassion, great joy, great equanimity, all supernatural powers, all excellent marks, all merits, all wisdoms, all myriad practices were fully studied. The three vehicles, the three piṭakas, the unconditioned and uncompounded, the five pure eyes, the six supernatural powers, merit and wisdom having been perfected, they thereby accomplished unsurpassed, right, equal bodhi, the thirty-two major marks and eighty minor marks, the indestructible, adamantine, firm body, the Dharma-body, the eternal body, and the transformation-body. Throughout the three realms and six destinies, they rescue sentient beings. Kings among sages, above the heavens and below the heavens, the most honored, the most noble, none can surpass them, none can equal them. They are called "Teacher of Gods and Humans," "Great Compassionate Father." They constantly use skillful means and immeasurable kindness, compassion, joy, and equanimity to liberate all suffering sentient beings, enabling them to attain the enduring realm of peace and happiness of nirvāṇa.

If anyone takes refuge in, reverently makes offerings to, praises and extols them, or hears their names, or beholds their forms, burns incense, bows in worship, or circumambulates in walking practice, the merit generated is immeasurable and boundless, surpassing Mount Sumeru, deeper than the great ocean. They regard all sentient beings equally as their only child, universally equal, without love or hatred. They serve as the world-transcending cause for all sentient beings. For beings like us, they have great kindness and bestow great benefit. They are our true refuge, our true good spiritual friends, our true field of merit, difficult to encounter in a hundred thousand ten-thousand kalpas, impossible to fully praise in a hundred thousand ten-thousand kalpas.

We today give rise to deep shame, deep yearning, deep joy, deep love and admiration, deep cherishing and reverence, deep congratulation, deep respect. May your disciples' accumulated transgressions and obstructions be eliminated. With a single prostration to the mother of merit, we bow in petition and praise.

The merit within the bodies of all buddhas has already been briefly described above. May all accumulated transgressions and obstructions be eliminated. The petition being complete, we now sincerely confess.

Now, before all buddhas, worthies, and sages, we deeply reproach ourselves, repenting our faults, reforming the past and cultivating the future. With utmost sincerity, reaching the depths, we lay open our hearts and expose our innermost being. With painful anguish and sorrowful weeping, with flowing tears, with utmost sincerity we openly confess. All light and heavy transgressions are utterly eliminated.

It is like clothing covered in much grease and dirt. If one wishes to wash it, one uses lye. With one wash, the grease and dirt are all removed. Then, using clear water to rinse again, one obtains fresh, clean garments. Confession is likewise. Having committed transgressions and obtained their burden, through confession the transgressions are eliminated, and what remains is the pure body of merit.

Straighten the body, kneel, with the right knee touching the ground (not the left knee), palms joined at the heart, giving rise to deep yearning.

---

Your disciple So-and-so and others, together with all those in the practice-hall, with one mind take refuge in all buddhas, all great bodhisattva-mahāsattvas throughout the ten directions, three times, and the entirety of space. Great compassion, great kindness: have pity on your disciples, take us in, do not forsake us, protect us, rescue us, liberate us, save us, support us, bring us benefit, guide us, deliver us, have compassion on us, and receive your disciples.

We confess before the buddhas. From beginningless time until today, our bodies have dwelt in the stage of ordinary beings, fully endowed with afflictions. Covered by greed, anger, delusion, and immeasurable afflictions, the three karmas are deluded, the three karmas are unwholesome. Greed, anger, jealousy, self-conceit, arrogance, lacking benevolence, lacking righteousness, lacking reverence, lacking esteem. Toward our parents, we have not practiced filial kindness. In speech and response, with crooked eyes and angry glares, we have caused resentment, caused suffering. We have disturbed and vexed our parents without knowing it.

Or toward brothers, sisters, and the six classes of kin, our minds have lacked respect and obedience, we have not practiced filial duty. Or toward neighbors, friends, associates, and those in the surrounding community, we have constantly encroached upon them, constantly spoken against them, constantly slandered and criticized them.

Or we have stolen and used the property of the Three Jewels. Having borrowed, we did not repay. We have accumulated debts. The ten evils, the five heinous crimes, the grave transgressions of uninterrupted retribution. Giving rise to great wrong views, denying cause and effect. Destroying stūpas and ruining temples, burning sūtras and images. Slandering the Dharma of the Three Vehicles, saying it is not the Buddha's teaching. Obstructing the Dharma and blocking the difficulties, bullying superiors and inferiors. Beating and cursing great and small, using public authority for private ends. Creating all manner of transgressions and obstructions, breaking fasting rules and breaking precepts. Insulting and reviling teachers and monks, slandering and criticizing worthies and sages. Disrespecting monastics and laypersons.

The three of body, four of speech, and three of mind: the unwholesome actions of body are killing, stealing, and sexual misconduct. The unwholesome actions of speech are false speech, frivolous speech, divisive speech, and harsh speech. The unwholesome actions of mind are greed, anger, and wrong views. Constantly, day and night, practicing the ten unwholesome karmas. All transgressions and obstructions are immeasurable and boundless.

Or in estates and gardens, irrigating and channeling water for fields, killing and harming insects and ants, swatting and destroying mosquitoes and flies, killing lice. In material generosity and Dharma generosity, we have constantly been miserly, not providing what was needed. Seeing others in suffering, we could not rescue them. We did not give rise to compassion or kindness. Instead, we took delight in it. Seeing others' well-being, seeing those who surpassed us, we did not rejoice for them, but rather gave rise to jealousy, with a mind that could not bear it. Without kindness, without compassion, without shame, without remorse. Our hearts harbored cruelty, like tigers and wolves, like rākṣasas. Going in and out of temple buildings, treading upon filth, drinking wine and eating meat without limit or satiation. Eating the five pungent plants: chives, garlic, and other foul-smelling things that offend both ordinary beings and sages.

Or in the marketplace, with greedy minds seeking profit, using light scales and short measures, with various contrivances cheating and robbing sentient beings. Harming innocent people. With evil craving, seeking much, taking others' wealth without knowing it. Killing without measure, severing great compassion. Stealing without measure, discarding others' wealth and treasure. Sexual misconduct without measure, defiling the pure brahma-conduct. False speech without measure, deceiving all. Craving without measure, the mind without satiation. Divisive speech without measure, disrupting harmony. Harsh speech without measure, insulting ordinary beings and sages. Frivolous speech without measure, making sport of the right Dharma. Anger without measure, beating and cursing sentient beings. Widely creating transgressions without measure or limit.

Seeing those who cultivate practice and perform wholesome deeds, who go to temple buildings to bow to the Three Jewels, who maintain fasting rules and precepts, who read and recite the sūtras, who generate the mind of bodhi and practice the bodhisattva practices, who sit in meditation and cultivate the Way and perform meritorious deeds: we have not been able to assist them, not been able to rejoice for them, not been able to encourage them, not been able to praise them. Instead, we have created obstacles, slandered and criticized them, mocked and ridiculed them, destroying sentient beings' cultivation of the cause of world-transcendence. Causing those who cultivate practice to harm sentient beings' eyes of gods and humans, causing them to regress from the mind of bodhi. Making ourselves great enemies, evil spiritual friends. On the path, we have dragged others down, causing them to fall into the three evil destinies, passing through immeasurable kalpas receiving the suffering of the hells. Emerging from the hells, even if we obtain a human body, we are blind, deaf, mute, with faculties incomplete. Or born in border regions where there is no buddha-dharma. Not knowing propriety, not distinguishing good from evil. Living like animals, born into families of wrong views. Widely creating transgressions, we fall back into the hells. Through immeasurable great kalpas, liberation is difficult to attain.

Therefore, today, before all buddhas of the ten directions, the twelve divisions of the sūtra-piṭaka, all great bodhisattva-mahāsattvas, the pratyekabuddhas, śrāvakas, arhats, and holy saṃgha, Brahmā, Śakra, the Four Kings, nāgas, the eight classes of beings, the officials of the unseen world, the karmic judges, and all spiritual powers, those with the divine eye who see from afar, those with the divine ear who hear from afar, those who know others' minds from afar, the supernaturally powerful worthies and sages: bear witness that your disciple, today, at this very time, gives rise to deep fear and dread, deep disgust and renunciation, deep self-awareness, deep self-reform, deep self-reproach, deep shame and remorse, not daring to conceal anything.

From this day forward, reforming the past and cultivating the future, never again daring to commit such acts. All transgressions and obstructions, whether many or few, whether light or heavy, today, at this very time, in a single thought, may all transgressions and obstructions be utterly eliminated.

---

We humbly pray that all the great compassionate honored ones of the ten directions bear witness and hold us in their mindfulness. I now confess and will never again commit these deeds. May I and all sentient beings swiftly eliminate the immeasurable kalpas of the ten evils, the five heinous crimes, the four grave offenses, the transgressions of delusion, slander of the Three Jewels, and icchantika.

Further, one should contemplate: the nature of all such transgressions arises merely from the deluded and inverted mind. There is nothing real to be obtained; originally, all is empty and quiescent. May I and all sentient beings swiftly reach the source of the mind and forever extinguish the root of transgressions.

The confession being complete, we take refuge in and bow to the Three Jewels.
