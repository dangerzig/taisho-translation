# 佛說安宅陀羅尼呪經
## Sūtra of the Dhāraṇī Spell for Pacifying the Dwelling, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1029

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the kingdom of Śrāvastī, in the Jeta Grove, in the park of Anāthapiṇḍada, together with a great assembly of one thousand two hundred and fifty bhikṣus and forty thousand great bodhisattvas. The lord of the Sahā world, Brahma King, Śakra-devānām-indra, the Four Great Heavenly Kings (Dhṛtarāṣṭra, Virūḍhaka, Virūpākṣa, and Vaiśravaṇa), leading the twenty-eight divisions of ghost-master generals: such were the eighty-four thousand devas. Aridda, together with his sons and retinue, all came to the assembly.

At that time, beyond this Buddha-world, past ten trillion Buddha-lands, there was a world named Multitude of Flowers, whose Buddha was named Supreme Lamp King Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha, presently teaching the Dharma. He dispatched two bodhisattvas, the first named Great Radiance and the second named Immeasurable Radiance, and told them: "Good men! Take this dhāraṇī spell and deliver it to Śākyamuni Buddha in the Sahā world. This spell brings great benefit. It can cause sentient beings to enjoy peace and security through the long night, and to obtain great wholesome benefit, beauty, strength, and renown." He then spoke the spell:

"*Tadyathā parā-śurī śurī śurī-rī śama-dī mahā-samādī samanī mahā-samanī salī salālī svāhā*"

At that time, the two bodhisattvas received and upheld the dhāraṇī spell from the Buddha. In the time it takes a strong man to bend and extend his arm, they vanished from the Multitude of Flowers world and arrived in the kingdom of Śrāvastī, in the Jeta Grove, in the park of Anāthapiṇḍada. The two bodhisattvas approached the Buddha, bowed their heads at his feet, withdrew and stood to one side, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! Beyond this world, past ten trillion Buddha-lands, there is a world named Multitude of Flowers, whose Buddha is named Supreme Lamp King Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha, presently teaching the Dharma. He has sent us to convey greetings to the World-Honored One: 'Are you free from illness and affliction? Is your strength at ease?' And to ask whether the disciples are troubled by any māra, any deva, any ghost, any rākṣasa, any bhūta, any kumbhāṇḍa, any pūtana, any piśāca, any hungry ghost, any asura, any garuḍa, any human ghost, any gandha-ghost, any pestilence-ghost, any madness-causing ghost, any mind-disturbing ghost, any crossroads ghost, any vetāla ghost, any one-day fever ghost, any two-day, three-day, four-day, or up to seven-day fever ghost, or any constantly recurring fever ghost, or any such calamities. Supreme Lamp King Tathāgata has sent this dhāraṇī spell to give to the World-Honored One, also wishing to cause all the sentient beings of the Sahā world to enjoy peace and security through the long night, and to obtain great wholesome benefit, beauty, strength, and renown. The spell is as stated above."

At that time, the Buddha told Ānanda: "You should receive and uphold this, read, recite, and master it thoroughly. Explain it for others. Write and make offerings to this dhāraṇī spell. Why? Because the appearance of a Buddha in the world is rare, and upholding this spell is likewise extremely rare. Ānanda! If one receives, upholds, reads, recites, and thoroughly masters it, one obtains great merit and benefit."
