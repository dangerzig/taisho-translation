# 佛說彌勒下生經
## Sūtra Spoken by the Buddha on Maitreya's Descent and Birth

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0453

Translated from the Chinese. Western Jin dynasty, translated by Dharmarakṣa (竺法護)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was in the land of Śrāvastī, in the Jeta Grove of Anāthapiṇḍada's park, together with a great assembly of five hundred monks.

At that time Ānanda bared his right shoulder, placed his right knee on the ground, and addressed the Buddha: "The Tathāgata's profound insight leaves nothing unexamined. Past, future, and present, all three times, are entirely clear to you. The names and surnames, the disciples and bodhisattvas, and the size of the retinues of all past buddhas are fully known to you. Whether for one kalpa, a hundred kalpas, or immeasurable kalpas, you observe them all in the same way, as well as the kings, ministers, and people's names, distinguishing them all. The present states and their extent are also fully clear to you. In the long distant future, Maitreya will appear as a Tathāgata, the Truly Perfected One, Perfectly Awakened. I wish to hear about that transformation, about his disciples and retinue, about the prosperity and joy of the Buddha's realm, and how long it will last."

The Buddha told Ānanda: "Return to your seat and listen to what I shall teach about Maitreya's appearance, the prosperity of the land, and the number of his disciples. Reflect well upon it and hold it in your heart."

At that time Ānanda received the Buddha's instruction and returned to his seat.

The World-Honored One then told Ānanda: "In the long distant future, in this land there will be a city called Ketumatī, twelve yojanas from east to west and seven yojanas from north to south. The land will be rich and fertile, the people thriving, the streets and lanes well-ordered. At that time within the city of Ketumatī there will be a nāga king named Water-Light, who will rain fragrant moisture at night while the days remain clear and mild. At that time within the city of Ketumatī there will be a rākṣasa spirit named Leaf-Flower, who acts in accordance with the Dharma and does not violate the right teaching. Each night after the people have gone to sleep, he removes all filth and impurities, always sprinkling the ground with fragrant water so that it is supremely fragrant and clean.

"Ānanda, you should know that at that time Jambudvīpa will be ten thousand yojanas from east to west and north to south. All mountains, rivers, and stone cliffs will have naturally disappeared, and each of the four great seas will have receded by ten thousand. At that time Jambudvīpa will be perfectly level, clear as a mirror. Throughout Jambudvīpa, grain and food will be abundant and inexpensive, the people thriving, with many precious treasures. The villages will be so close together that the crowing of roosters can be heard from one to the next. At that time, harmful flowers and fruit trees will have withered, and filth and foulness will have naturally disappeared. All the remaining trees, with their sweet fruits and excellent fragrance, will grow upon the earth.

"At that time, the climate will be mild and harmonious, the four seasons regular. In the human body there will be none of the one hundred and eight afflictions. Greed, anger, and delusion will not be intense. People's minds will be even and equal, all of one accord. When they meet they will be joyful, and they will speak kind words to each other. Their speech will be of one type, without any differences, just like the people of Uttarakuru, without any distinction.

"At that time the people of Jambudvīpa, whether men or women, when they wish to relieve themselves, the earth will naturally open, and when they are finished, the earth will close again.

"At that time Jambudvīpa will naturally produce wild rice, without husks, supremely fragrant and delicious, eaten without any harm or suffering.

"What are called gold, silver, precious treasures, conch shells, agate, true pearls, and amber will be scattered upon the ground with no one taking notice. At that time the people will pick up these treasures in their hands and say to one another: 'In former times, because of these treasures people injured one another, imprisoned one another, and suffered immeasurable miseries. Now these treasures are no different from tiles and stones, and no one guards them.'

"At that time a dharma-king will appear, named Śaṅkha. He will rule by the true Dharma, accomplished in the seven treasures. The seven treasures are: the wheel-treasure, the elephant-treasure, the horse-treasure, the jewel-treasure, the consort-treasure, the treasurer-treasure, and the minister-treasure. He will govern Jambudvīpa, subduing all without weapons or force, by natural submission alone.

"And now, Ānanda, four great treasure-vaults: the Irāpatra treasure-vault in the kingdom of Gandhāra, filled with immeasurable rare and wondrous objects; the second, the Jūlā great treasure-vault in the kingdom of Mithilā, also filled with many precious treasures; the third, in the great kingdom of Surāṣṭra, a great treasure-vault, also filled with many precious treasures; the fourth, in the land of Vārāṇasī, the Śaṅkha great treasure-vault, filled with immeasurable precious treasures. These four great treasure-vaults will naturally appear, and the guardians of the vaults will each come and address the king: 'May the great king use the contents of these treasure-vaults to bestow gifts upon the poor.' At that time King Śaṅkha, having received these treasures, will give them no further thought, his mind free from any conception of wealth. At that time in Jambudvīpa, on the natural trees, garments will grow, supremely fine and soft. People will take and wear them, just as the people of Uttarakuru now wear clothes grown naturally on trees, without any difference.

"At that time the king will have a great minister named Subrahmā, the king's companion from youth, whom the king loves and esteems. His appearance will be handsome, neither tall nor short, neither fat nor thin, neither pale nor dark, neither old nor young. At that time Subrahmā will have a wife named Brahmāvatī, the foremost beauty among the king's women, supremely lovely, like the consort of Lord Śakra. Her mouth will have the fragrance of the utpala lotus, her body the fragrance of sandalwood. The eighty-four feminine wiles will be entirely absent in her, and she will be free from illness and disordered thoughts.

"At that time the Bodhisattva Maitreya, from the Tuṣita Heaven, will observe his father and mother as being neither old nor young, and will descend and enter the womb, being born from the right side, just as I today was born from the right side, without any difference. The Bodhisattva Maitreya will be likewise. The devas of Tuṣita will each proclaim: 'The Bodhisattva Maitreya has descended and been born!'

"At that time Subrahmā will give his son the name Maitreya. The Bodhisattva Maitreya will have the thirty-two marks and eighty excellent characteristics adorning his body, his body the color of gold. At that time people will have extremely long lives, free from all afflictions, each living eighty-four thousand years. Women will marry at the age of five hundred.

"At that time, not long after Maitreya dwells as a householder, he will leave home to study the path. At that time, not far from the city of Ketumatī, there will be a bodhi tree called Dragon-Flower, one yojana in height and five hundred paces in breadth. The Bodhisattva Maitreya will sit beneath that tree and attain the supreme fruit of the path.

"On that very night that Maitreya leaves home, on that very night he will attain the supreme path. At that time the great trichiliocosm will tremble in six ways. The earth spirits will report to one another: 'Maitreya has now attained buddhahood!' The report will reach the palace of the Four Heavenly Kings: 'Maitreya has attained the buddha-path.' The report will spread in succession through the heavens of the Thirty-Three, the Yāma Heaven, the Tuṣita Heaven, the Nirmāṇarati Heaven, and the Paranirmitavaśavartin Heaven. The sound will spread in turn until it reaches the Brahmā Heaven: 'Maitreya has attained the buddha-path.'

"At that time Māra the king, named Great General, will rule by the Dharma. Hearing the resonant voice of the Tathāgata's name, he will rejoice so greatly that he cannot contain himself and will neither sleep nor rest for seven days and seven nights. At that time Māra the king will lead the immeasurable beings of the desire realm to the presence of the Buddha Maitreya to pay respects and bow.

"The holy lord Maitreya will then gradually teach the Dharma and subtle discourse for the devas and humans. The discourse is: discourse on giving, discourse on precepts, discourse on rebirth in the heavens, the impurity of desire, and the excellence of renunciation. At that time, when Maitreya sees that the assembly has generated joyful minds, he will teach what all the buddha-world-honored-ones always teach: suffering, its arising, its cessation, and the path. He will broadly distinguish the meaning of these for all the devas and humans. At that time, eighty-four thousand deva-sons on the seats will have all their defilements exhausted and attain the pure Dharma-eye.

"At that time Māra the king, the Great General, will tell the people of his realm: 'You should all quickly go forth from the household life! Why? Because Maitreya has today already crossed to the other shore, and he will also help you cross to the other shore.'

"At that time in the city of Ketumatī there will be an elder named Sudhana. Hearing Māra's exhortation and also hearing the Buddha's resonant voice, he will lead eighty-four thousand people to the presence of the Buddha Maitreya. They will bow their heads at his feet and sit to one side.

"At that time Maitreya will gradually teach them the Dharma and subtle discourse. The discourse is: discourse on giving, discourse on precepts, discourse on rebirth in the heavens, the impurity of desire, and the excellence of renunciation. At that time, when Maitreya sees that the assembly's minds are open and understanding, he will teach what all the buddha-world-honored-ones always teach: suffering, its arising, its cessation, and the path. He will broadly distinguish the meaning for the assembly. At that time eighty-four thousand people on the seats will have all their defilements exhausted and attain the pure Dharma-eye. Then Sudhana and the eighty-four thousand will come forward, address the Buddha, and request to leave the household life. They will cultivate the holy life well and all attain arhatship.

"At that time, in the first assembly of Maitreya, eighty-four thousand people will attain arhatship.

"At that time King Śaṅkha, hearing that Maitreya has attained the buddha-path, will go to the Buddha to hear the Dharma. The Buddha Maitreya will teach the king the Dharma: good in the beginning, good in the middle, good in the end, with profound meaning. At that time the great king will subsequently install the crown prince as king, reward the barber with precious treasures, and also give various treasures to the brahmins. He will lead eighty-four thousand people to the Buddha's presence and request to become śramaṇas. They will all attain the fruit of the path and become arhats.

"At that time the great elder Subrahmā, hearing that Maitreya has attained the buddha-path, will lead eighty-four thousand brahmin followers to the Buddha's presence and request to become śramaṇas. They will attain arhatship, except for Subrahmā himself, who alone will sever the three fetters and be certain to reach the end of suffering.

"At that time the Buddha's mother Brahmāvatī will lead eighty-four thousand attendant women to the Buddha's presence and request to become śramaṇīs. At that time all the women will attain arhatship, except for Brahmāvatī herself, who alone will sever the three fetters and attain stream-entry (sotāpatti).

"At that time the kṣatriya women, hearing that the Tathāgata Maitreya has appeared in the world and attained perfect enlightenment, will come by the thousands and ten-thousands to the Buddha's presence. They will bow their heads at his feet, sit to one side, and each generate the aspiration to become śramaṇīs, to leave the household life and study the path. Some will attain realization in sequence, and some will not yet attain realization.

"At that time, Ānanda, those who do not attain realization in sequence are all people who uphold the Dharma, who are weary and disenchanted with all worldly things, and who cultivate the contemplation of the undesirable.

"At that time Maitreya will teach the three vehicles, just as I do today. Among my disciples, the great Kāśyapa, who practices the twelve dhūtaṅga austerities and has well cultivated the holy life in the presence of past buddhas: this person shall assist Maitreya in exhorting and transforming the people."

At that time Kāśyapa was seated not far from the Tathāgata in the cross-legged posture, his body upright, his mind focused, his mindfulness directed to what was before him.

The World-Honored One then told Kāśyapa: "I have now grown old and frail, past the age of eighty. Yet the Tathāgata has four great śrāvaka disciples capable of traveling and teaching, with inexhaustible wisdom and replete with all virtues. Who are the four? The monk Mahākāśyapa, the monk Piṇḍola-Bhāradvāja, the monk Rāhula, and the monk Khadiravaṇika. You four great śrāvaka disciples must not enter parinirvāṇa until my Dharma has completely disappeared; only then may you enter parinirvāṇa. As for Mahākāśyapa, he especially must not enter parinirvāṇa, but must wait until Maitreya appears in the world. Why? Because all the disciples whom Maitreya will teach are in fact disciples of Śākyamuni, who through my transmitted teaching will exhaust all their defilements.

"In the Magadha region, in the village of Vidiśā, Mahākāśyapa will dwell within that mountain. When the Tathāgata Maitreya, surrounded by immeasurable thousands of people, goes to that mountain, through the Buddha's grace the ghosts and spirits will open the door so that they may see Kāśyapa's meditation cave. At that time Maitreya will extend his right hand and point to Kāśyapa, telling the assembly: 'Long ago, in the distant past, the disciple of the Buddha Śākyamuni named Kāśyapa is still present today. He was foremost in the practice of dhūtaṅga austerities.' At that time the assembly, seeing this, will exclaim that it is unprecedented. Immeasurable hundreds of thousands of beings will have all their defilements exhausted and attain the pure Dharma-eye. Some among the assembly, upon seeing Kāśyapa's body, will attend the first great assembly of ninety-six hundred million, all attaining arhatship. These people are all my disciples. Why? Because it was all accomplished through receiving my instruction, and also through the four means: generosity, kind speech, beneficial action, and impartial cooperation.

"At that time, Ānanda, the Tathāgata Maitreya will take Kāśyapa's saṃghāṭī robe and put it on. At that time Kāśyapa's body will suddenly disperse like falling stars. Then Maitreya will gather various flowers and incense to make offerings to Kāśyapa. Why? Because all the buddha-world-honored-ones have a mind of reverence toward the true Dharma. Maitreya also received the true Dharma from me, through which he attained the unsurpassed, right, and true path.

"Ānanda, you should know that in the second assembly of the Buddha Maitreya, ninety-four hundred million people will all be arhats, and they too will be my legacy-disciples, accomplished through the practice of the four kinds of offerings.

"In the third assembly of Maitreya, ninety-two hundred million people will all be arhats, and they too will be my legacy-disciples.

"At that time the monks will all bear the surname and be called disciples of Maitreya, just as today my śrāvaka disciples are all called disciples of Śākyamuni.

"At that time Maitreya will teach the Dharma to his disciples: 'You monks! You should contemplate the thought of impermanence, the thought that what seems pleasant contains suffering, the thought that what seems to be a self is non-self, the thought that what seems real is empty, the thought of the body's decay, the thought of the blue-black discoloration of a corpse, the thought of bloating, the thought of undigested food, the thought of pus and blood, and the thought that all worldly things are undesirable. Why? Monks, you should know that these ten contemplations were all taught to you by the Buddha Śākyamuni in the past, enabling you to exhaust your defilements and liberate your minds.

"'Among this assembly, those who were disciples of the Buddha Śākyamuni in the past, who cultivated the holy life, have come to be with me now. Those who in the time of the Buddha Śākyamuni made offerings to the Three Treasures have come to be with me now. Those who in the time of the Buddha Śākyamuni, for even the snap of a finger, cultivated a root of goodness have come to this place. Those who in the time of the Buddha Śākyamuni practiced the four immeasurable minds have come here. Those who in the time of the Buddha Śākyamuni received and upheld the five precepts and the three refuges have come to be with me. Those who in the time of the Buddha Śākyamuni built spirit temples have come to be with me. Those who in the time of the Buddha Śākyamuni repaired old temples have come to be with me. Those who in the time of the Buddha Śākyamuni received the dharma of the eight precepts have come to be with me. Those who in the time of the Buddha Śākyamuni made offerings of incense and flowers have come here. Those who upon hearing the Dharma wept with grief and shed tears have come to be with me. Those who in the time of the Buddha Śākyamuni focused their minds on receiving the Dharma have come to be with me. Those who throughout their lives cultivated the holy life well have come to be with me. Those who copied and recited the sūtras have come to be with me. Those who served and made offerings have come to be with me now.'"

At that time Maitreya spoke this verse:

> "Those who increased in precepts, learning, and virtue,
> in meditation and the work of contemplation,
> who well cultivated the holy life,
> have come to be with me now.
> Those who encouraged giving with joyful minds,
> who cultivated the original source of the mind's practice,
> whose thoughts were without diversity,
> have all come to be with me now.
> Those who generated the mind of equanimity,
> who served all the buddhas
> and fed the holy assembly,
> have all come to be with me now.
> Those who recited the precepts and sūtras,
> who skillfully studied and taught others,
> who blazed forth the root of the Dharma,
> have now come to be with me.
> Those of the Śākya clan who skillfully taught,
> who made offerings to the relics
> and served and honored the Dharma,
> have now come to be with me.
> Those who copied the sūtras,
> who spread them upon fine silk,
> who made offerings to the sūtras,
> have all come to be with me now.
> Those who with silk, colored cloth, and various objects
> made offerings to the spirit temples,
> who proclaimed 'Homage to the Buddha,'
> have all come to be with me now.
> Making offerings to the presently abiding
> and to the buddhas of the past:
> meditation and concentration are equal and balanced,
> neither increasing nor decreasing.
> Therefore, in the Buddha's Dharma,
> serving the holy assembly,
> single-mindedly devoted to the Three Treasures:
> one will certainly reach the unconditioned state."

"Ānanda, you should know that the Tathāgata Maitreya will teach this verse among that assembly. At that time the assembly of devas and humans, contemplating these ten thoughts, eleven billion people will have all their defilements exhausted and attain the pure Dharma-eye.

"For a thousand years of the Tathāgata Maitreya's time, the sangha will be without blemish or stain. At that time there will always be a single verse as the precept:

> "Do not commit evil with mouth, mind, or body.
> Let the body also be free from transgression.
> Remove these three courses of action,
> and swiftly escape the barrier of birth and death."

"After a thousand years, there will be those who violate the precepts, and further precepts will then be established.

"The Tathāgata Maitreya will live for eighty-four thousand years. After his parinirvāṇa, the Dharma he leaves behind will endure for eighty-four thousand years. Why? Because the people of that time will all have sharp faculties.

"Any good men or good women who wish to see the Buddha Maitreya, his three assemblies of śrāvaka disciples, the city of Ketumatī, King Śaṅkha, the four great treasure-vaults with their precious treasures, who wish to eat the naturally growing rice, to wear the naturally growing garments, and at the end of their lives to be reborn in the heavens: such good men and good women should diligently increase their efforts without becoming lax. They should also make offerings and serve the Dharma-teachers, with famous flowers, rubbed incense, and all manner of offerings, not letting any be wanting. Thus, Ānanda, you should study in this way."

At that time Ānanda and all the great assembly, hearing what the Buddha had taught, joyfully put it into practice.
