# Sūtra of the Eight Yang Divine Spells of Heaven and Earth, Spoken by the Buddha

## *Fó Shuō Tiāndì Bāyáng Shénzhòu Jīng* (佛說天地八陽神呪經)

Taishō 2897

Attributed to Yìjìng (*Yìjìng*, 義淨) of the Tang dynasty.

*Note: This is a well-known apocryphal sūtra (one of the most widely circulated in medieval China, with many Dunhuang manuscripts surviving) that blends Buddhist and Chinese cosmological concepts. Despite its attribution to Yìjìng, the text is universally recognized by modern scholars as a Chinese composition, placed in the "suspected apocrypha" section of the Taishō canon. The sūtra addresses the Bodhisattva Wúài ("Unobstructed") and teaches that reciting this text three or seven times can cure illness, ward off evil spirits, protect homes during construction, ensure safe burial, facilitate marriage, and generate boundless merit. Notably, it incorporates the Heart Sūtra's emptiness formulations (form is emptiness, emptiness is form) into a schema mapping the six sense faculties to six tathāgatas and six heavens, and further correlates the eight consciousnesses (including the ālayavijñāna) with specific sūtras. The text explicitly polemicizes against Chinese geomantic and calendrical superstitions, arguing that all days are equally auspicious and that true merit comes from reciting the sūtra rather than consulting fortune-tellers.*

---

Thus have I heard.

At one time, the Buddha was in the city of Vaiśālī (*Píyēdámó chéng*, 毘耶達摩城), in the Vast and Spacious Abode, surrounded by followers from the ten directions and the four assemblies.

At that time, the Bodhisattva Unobstructed (*Wúài*, 無礙), from within the great assembly, rose from his seat and said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! The sentient beings of this Jambudvīpa succeed one another in birth, generation after generation, continuing without interruption from beginningless time. Those with understanding are few; those without understanding are many. Those who are mindful of the Buddha are few; those who are not are many. Those with spiritual powers are few; those without are many. Those who keep the precepts are few; those who break them are many. Those who are vigorous are few; those who are indolent are many. Those with wisdom are few; those who are deluded are many. Those who are long-lived are few; those who die young are many. Those who practice meditation are few; those whose minds are scattered are many. Those who are wealthy and noble are few; those who are poor and lowly are many. Those who are gentle are few; those who are harsh are many. Those who practice generosity are few; those who are miserly are many. Those who are honest are few; those who are deceitful are many. The ways of the world are shallow and base; government laws are harsh and oppressive; corvée labor is heavy and burdensome. The common people are impoverished and find it difficult to obtain what they need. All of this is because they believe in deviant and inverted views, and so they suffer such afflictions. We humbly pray the World-Honored One will teach the dharma of right view for the sake of all these beings of wrong views, so that they may awaken and be freed from their many sufferings."

The Buddha said: "Excellent, excellent! Bodhisattva Unobstructed! With great compassion, you ask the Tathāgata about the dharma of right view for the sake of beings with wrong views. This is inconceivable. All of you, listen well! Attend carefully! I shall now analyze and expound for you the Sūtra of the Eight Yang of Heaven and Earth. This sūtra was taught by all buddhas of the past, will be taught by all buddhas of the future, and is now taught by all buddhas of the present."

"Among all that exists between heaven and earth, human beings are the most excellent and supreme, more precious than all things. A 'human' (*rén*) means one who is 'true' (*zhēn*) and 'upright' (*zhèng*): the mind is free from falsehood and the body acts in truth and uprightness. The left [side of the character] signifies 'true'; the right signifies 'upright.' Because one constantly acts in truth and uprightness, one is called a 'human.' Therefore, know that it is humans who can broaden the Way and thereby nourish the self. Relying on the Way and relying on humanity, all can accomplish the holy Way."

---

### On the Merit of Receiving This Sūtra

"Furthermore, Bodhisattva Unobstructed! All sentient beings who have obtained a human body but do not cultivate merit, who turn their backs on truth and face falsehood, who create all kinds of evil karma: when their lives are about to end, they will surely sink into the sea of suffering and receive all kinds of punishment. If they hear this sūtra and receive it in faith without resistance, they will immediately be liberated from all sinful difficulties and escape the sea of suffering. Good spirits will protect them, removing all obstacles. Their years will be extended and their lifespan increased, without untimely death. Through the power of faith they obtain such blessings. How much more for those who can fully copy, receive, uphold, read, and recite it, and practice in accordance with the Dharma! The merit of such practice is beyond description, beyond measure, without limit. After their life ends, they will all attain buddhahood."

The Buddha told the Bodhisattva Unobstructed: "If sentient beings believe in deviant and inverted views, they will be troubled by demonic heretics, ghosts, goblins, evil omens of bird-calls, and all manner of evil ghosts and spirits competing to vex them, inflicting sudden illness, evil afflictions, and evil possession upon them, causing suffering without respite. If they encounter a good spiritual friend who reads this sūtra three times for them, all those evil ghosts will be entirely annihilated, the illness will be cured, and their bodies will be strong and vigorous. Such is the merit of reading this sūtra."

"If there are sentient beings greatly afflicted by lust, anger, delusion, miserliness, or jealousy, and they see this sūtra, reverently make offerings to it, and read it three times, all such evils as delusion will be entirely eliminated, and they will obtain compassion, joy, equanimity, and a share in the Buddha-dharma."

---

### On Construction, Travel, and Daily Life

"Furthermore, Bodhisattva Unobstructed! If good men or good women who engage in worldly affairs first read this sūtra three times before building walls, breaking ground, establishing a household, constructing the south hall or north hall, east wing or west wing, kitchen, storeroom, doors, wells, stoves, mills, granaries, or livestock pens, then all the spirits of the daily and monthly cycles, the Grand General, Grand Year, the Yellow Banner and Leopard Tail, the Five Earth Deities, the Azure Dragon, White Tiger, Vermilion Bird, and Dark Warrior, the Six Jia prohibitions, the Twelve Spirits, the Earth Office and Hidden Dragon, and all ghosts and goblins will all hide themselves and retreat far away in the four directions. Their shadows will vanish and they will not dare cause harm; there will be great auspiciousness and immeasurable merit."

"Good man! After construction is complete, the halls will be forever peaceful and the buildings secure. Wealth, nobility, and auspiciousness will come of their own accord. For those who travel afar or go to war, take office, or engage in commerce, everything will be greatly beneficial. The household will prosper, with a hundred sons and a thousand grandsons. Father will be loving and son filial; husband loyal and wife chaste; elder brother respectful and younger brother obedient; husband and wife harmonious. All wishes will be fulfilled."

"If sentient beings are suddenly seized by county officials or dragged away by bandits, reading this sūtra three times will immediately bring release. If good men or good women receive, uphold, read, recite, or copy this Eight Yang Sūtra for others, even if they enter water or fire, they will not be burned or swept away. In mountains or marshes, tigers, wolves, and fierce beasts will flee and not dare approach. Good spirits will guard and protect them, accomplishing the unsurpassed Way."

---

### On Funerals, Burial, and Marriage

The Bodhisattva Unobstructed said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! For people in this world, birth and death are the most important matters. For birth, one does not choose a day; when the time comes, one is born. For death, one does not choose a day; when the time comes, one dies. Why then, for burial, do people consult for auspicious days before interring the dead? After burial, there are still calamities: many become poor, and not a few see their families destroyed. We pray the World-Honored One will explain the causes for beings of wrong views and no wisdom, so they may attain the right Way and be rid of their inverted thinking."

The Buddha said: "Excellent! Good man! You have truly asked well about the matters of birth, death, and burial for sentient beings. Listen carefully! I shall explain the principle of wisdom and the dharma of the great Way. Heaven and earth are vast and pure; the sun and moon are broad and bright. The seasons are good and beautiful, with truly no difference among them."

"Good man! Foolish people without wisdom trust deviant teachers, consult diviners hoping for good fortune, yet do not cultivate goodness, creating all kinds of evil karma. After death, those who obtain a human body again are as few as the dirt under a fingernail; those who fall into hell, the animal realm, or the hungry ghost realm are as numerous as the dirt of the great earth."

"Good man! Every day is a perfectly good day. Every month is a perfectly good month. Every year is a perfectly good year. There truly are no inauspicious intervals. When one must conduct a burial, simply proceed. On the day of burial, read this sūtra seven times, and there will be great auspiciousness and immeasurable merit. The household will prosper, people will be honored, years will be extended and lifespan increased, and upon death all will attain the holy Way."

"Good man! For the burial ground, do not inquire about east, west, south, or north. A place of peace where people are pleased and ghosts and spirits are pleased: simply read this sūtra three times and then proceed with the interment. Place it within the grave, and there will be no calamities forever. The family will be wealthy, the household will prosper, and there will be great auspiciousness."

Then the World-Honored One, wishing to restate this meaning, spoke in verse:

> "For undertakings, every day is a good day.
> For rest and burial, every hour is a good hour.
> Read and recite this sūtra at birth and death,
> And you will gain great benefit.
> Every month is a bright and good month;
> Every year is a great and good year.
> Read this sūtra and proceed with burial;
> Prosperity and glory for ten thousand generations."

At that time, 77,000 people in the assembly, hearing what the Buddha taught, had their minds opened and their understanding cleared. They abandoned the deviant and returned to the correct, gained a share of the Buddha-dharma, forever severed their doubts, and all aroused the aspiration for supreme, perfect awakening.

[The sūtra then addresses the topic of marriage in similar fashion, concluding that one should not worry about the five-element incompatibilities or zodiac conflicts, but should simply read this sūtra three times at the wedding ceremony.]

---

### The Eight Bodhisattvas and Their Dhāraṇī

At that time, eight bodhisattvas, empowered by the Buddha's awesome spiritual force and having attained the great dhāraṇī, constantly dwell in the human world, blending their light with the dust, destroying the deviant and establishing the correct. Their names are:

Bhadra Bodhisattva, Ratnaketu Bodhisattva, Gauḍika Bodhisattva, Nārada Bodhisattva, Sumerugambhīra Bodhisattva, Indradatta Bodhisattva, Haricakra Bodhisattva, and Apramāṇadṛṣṭi Bodhisattva.

These eight bodhisattvas together said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! We have received from all the buddhas the dhāraṇī divine spells, and we now speak them to guard and protect those who receive, uphold, read, and recite the Eight Yang Sūtra, so that they may forever be free from fear, and all unwholesome beings will be unable to harm the dharma-master who reads this sūtra."

They then spoke the dhāraṇī before the Buddha:

*Akhani nikhani abhira maṃle maṃtale svāhā.*

"World-Honored One! If any unwholesome being wishes to trouble the dharma-master, upon hearing this spell, its head will split into seven pieces, like the branches of an arjaka tree."

---

### The Meaning of "Eight Yang"

At that time, the Bodhisattva Infinite-Body (*Wúbiānshēn*, 無邊身) rose from his seat and said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! Why is this called the Eight Yang Sūtra? We pray the World-Honored One will explain its meaning for the assembly, so that they may awaken and swiftly realize their original mind, enter the Buddha's knowledge and vision, and forever sever doubt and regret."

The Buddha said: "Listen carefully! 'Eight' (*bā*) means 'to distinguish'; 'yang' (*yáng*) means 'to illuminate and understand.' It illuminates the Mahāyāna principle of emptiness and non-existence, and is able to distinguish the causes and conditions of the eight consciousnesses, which are empty and without obtainment."

"What are the eight consciousnesses? The eye is the form-consciousness; the ear is the sound-consciousness; the nose is the fragrance-consciousness; the tongue is the taste-consciousness; the body is the tangible-consciousness; the mind (*manas*) is the discriminating consciousness. The six sense faculties constitute the six consciousnesses. [In addition], there is the storehouse consciousness (*ālayavijñāna*). These are called the eight consciousnesses."

"To illuminate and distinguish the root-source of the eight consciousnesses as empty and without obtainment is to know that the two eyes are the Heaven of Radiance, and within the Heaven of Radiance there appears the World-Honored One of Sun-Moon Radiance. The two ears are the Heaven of Those Who Hear the Voice, and within it appears the Tathāgata of Immeasurable Sound. The two nostrils are the Heaven of Buddha-Fragrance, and within it appears the Tathāgata of Accumulated Fragrance. The mouth is the Heaven of Dharma-Taste, and within it appears the Tathāgata of Dharma-Joy. The body is the Heaven of Vairocana, and within it appear the Accomplished Vairocana Buddha, the Mirror-Image Vairocana Buddha, and the Radiance Vairocana Buddha. The mind is the Heaven of Non-Discrimination, and within it appear the Immovable Tathāgata and the Great Radiance Buddha. The heart-mind is the Heaven of the Dharma-Realm, and within it appears the Tathāgata King of Emptiness. The storehouse consciousness heaven brings forth the *Āgama Sūtras* and the *Mahāparinirvāṇa Sūtra*. The ālayavijñāna heaven brings forth the *Mahāprajñāpāramitāśāstra* and the *Yogācārabhūmiśāstra*."

"Good man! Buddha is Dharma and Dharma is Buddha. United as one characteristic, there appears the Tathāgata of Great Penetrating Wisdom and Victory."

---

When the Buddha taught this sūtra, the great earth quaked in six ways. Light illuminated heaven and earth without limit, vast and boundless beyond naming. All the dark realms became bright and clear. All the hells were entirely annihilated. All sinners were freed from suffering and all aroused the aspiration for supreme awakening.

At that time, 88,000 bodhisattvas in the assembly simultaneously attained buddhahood, with the title Tathāgata King of Emptiness, in the kalpa named Departure from Suffering, in the land named Boundless. All the people practiced the bodhisattva's six pāramitās and attained the dharma of non-obtainment. 66,000 bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, and upāsikās obtained the great dhāraṇī and entered the gate of non-duality. Innumerable devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, humans, and non-humans obtained the pure dharma-eye and practiced the bodhisattva path.

---

### Closing Teachings

"Furthermore, good man! If a person reads this sūtra three times on the day of taking office or entering a new home, there will be great auspiciousness; good spirits will protect them, their years will be extended, their lifespan increased, and their merit perfected."

"Good man! To read this sūtra once is like reading the entire canon once. To copy one fascicle is like copying the entire canon once. The merit of this is beyond description, beyond measure, equal to space, without limit. Such persons will accomplish the holy Way."

"Furthermore, if sentient beings do not believe in the true Dharma and constantly give rise to wrong views, and upon hearing this sūtra immediately slander it, saying it is not the Buddha's teaching, such persons in this present life will contract leprosy, with evil sores and pus flowing over their entire bodies. After death they will fall into the Avīci hell of uninterrupted suffering."

The Buddha spoke this verse for sinners:

> "The body is a natural body, the five limbs naturally complete.
> Growth is natural growth; aging is natural aging.
> Birth is natural birth; death is natural death.
> Seeking to be tall, one cannot be tall; seeking to be short, one cannot be short.
> Suffering and joy you yourself must bear; the deviant and the correct depend on you alone.
> If you wish to accomplish meritorious deeds, read the sūtras without consulting a master.
> For a thousand thousand, ten thousand ten thousand lifetimes,
> You shall attain the Way and turn the Dharma wheel."

When the Buddha had finished teaching this sūtra, the entire great assembly, having obtained what they had never had before, with minds illumined and thoughts purified, joyfully leapt with delight. They all saw that all marks are non-marks, entered the Buddha's knowledge and vision, awakened to the Buddha's knowledge and vision: no entering and no awakening, no knowing and no seeing, not obtaining a single dharma, and therefore the bliss of nirvāṇa. All the bodhisattvas, holy assemblies, celestial spirits, and earth deities joyfully received and practiced accordingly.
