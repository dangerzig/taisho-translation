# 瑜伽金剛頂經釋字母品
## Chapter on the Explanation of the Syllables from the Vajraśekhara Yoga Sūtra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0880

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空)

---

The syllable-gate *a*: because all dharmas are originally unborn.

The syllable-gate *ā*: because all dharmas are quiescent.

The syllable-gate *i*: because the root of all dharmas is unobtainable.

The syllable-gate *ī*: because the calamities and misfortunes of all dharmas are unobtainable.

The syllable-gate *u*: because metaphors for all dharmas are unobtainable.

The syllable-gate *ū*: because the diminishment of all dharmas is unobtainable.

The syllable-gate *ṛ*: because the supernatural powers of all dharmas are unobtainable.

The syllable-gate *ṝ*: because analogies for all dharmas are unobtainable.

The syllable-gate *ḷ*: because the defilement of all dharmas is unobtainable.

The syllable-gate *ḹ*: because the submersion of all dharmas is unobtainable.

The syllable-gate *e*: because the seeking of all dharmas is unobtainable.

The syllable-gate *ai*: because the mastery of all dharmas is unobtainable.

The syllable-gate *o*: because the torrents of all dharmas are unobtainable.

The syllable-gate *au*: because the transformative birth of all dharmas is unobtainable.

The syllable-gate *aṃ*: because the limits of all dharmas are unobtainable.

The syllable-gate *aḥ*: because the distancing of all dharmas is unobtainable.

The syllable-gate *ka*: because all dharmas are apart from karmic action.

The syllable-gate *kha*: because all dharmas are equal to empty space and unobtainable.

The syllable-gate *ga*: because the movement of all dharmas is unobtainable.

The syllable-gate *gha*: because the unification of all dharmas is unobtainable.

The syllable-gate *ṅa*: because the components of all dharmas are unobtainable.

The syllable-gate *ca*: because all dharmas are apart from all change and transformation.

The syllable-gate *cha*: because the reflected images of all dharmas are unobtainable.

The syllable-gate *ja*: because the arising of all dharmas is unobtainable.

The syllable-gate *jha*: because the warring adversaries of all dharmas are unobtainable.

The syllable-gate *ña*: because the cognition of all dharmas is unobtainable.

The syllable-gate *ṭa*: because the conceit of all dharmas is unobtainable.

The syllable-gate *ṭha*: because the nurturing of all dharmas is unobtainable.

The syllable-gate *ḍa*: because the adversaries of all dharmas are unobtainable.

The syllable-gate *ḍha*: because the grasping of all dharmas is unobtainable.

The syllable-gate *ṇa*: because the contention of all dharmas is unobtainable.

The syllable-gate *ta*: because the suchness of all dharmas is unobtainable.

The syllable-gate *tha*: because the abiding-place of all dharmas is unobtainable.

The syllable-gate *da*: because the giving of all dharmas is unobtainable.

The syllable-gate *dha*: because the realm of all dharmas is unobtainable.

The syllable-gate *na*: because the name of all dharmas is unobtainable.

The syllable-gate *pa*: because the ultimate truth of all dharmas is unobtainable.

The syllable-gate *pha*: because all dharmas are insubstantial, like a mass of foam.

The syllable-gate *ba*: because the bondage of all dharmas is unobtainable.

The syllable-gate *bha*: because the existence of all dharmas is unobtainable.

The syllable-gate *ma*: because the self of all dharmas is unobtainable.

The syllable-gate *ya*: because the vehicle of all dharmas is unobtainable.

The syllable-gate *ra*: because all dharmas are apart from all dust and defilement.

The syllable-gate *la*: because the marks of all dharmas are unobtainable.

The syllable-gate *va*: because the path of speech of all dharmas is cut off.

The syllable-gate *śa*: because the original nature of all dharmas is quiescent.

The syllable-gate *ṣa*: because the nature of all dharmas is dull.

The syllable-gate *sa*: because all truths of all dharmas are unobtainable.

The syllable-gate *ha*: because the cause of all dharmas is unobtainable.

The syllable-gate *kṣa*: because the exhaustion of all dharmas is unobtainable.
