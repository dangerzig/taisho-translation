# 大聖歡喜雙身大自在天毘那夜迦王歸依念誦供養法
## Method of Taking Refuge, Recitation, and Offerings for the Great Holy Joyous Dual-Bodied Maheśvara Vināyaka King

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1270

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Śubhākarasiṃha (善無畏).

---

The Great Holy Maheśvara Deva is Maheśvara, the Great Sovereign Deva-king. His wife is Umā, and they begot three thousand sons. His left-hand group of fifteen hundred is headed by Vināyaka King, who carries out all manner of evil deeds and commands 107,000 vināyaka beings. His right-hand group of fifteen hundred is headed by Senāyaka, the Deva of Virtuous Goodness, who cultivates all virtuous benefits and commands 178,000 beings of merit, skill, and goodness. This Senāyaka King is a transformation body of Avalokiteśvara. In order to harmonize and tame the evil conduct of Vināyaka King, he was born into the same class, becoming his brother and spouse, manifesting the form of mutual embrace in a single body. The original causes and conditions are found in detail in the *Sūtra of the Great Vidyā Thief*. I now briefly explain the altar method, recitation, offerings, and their sequence for practitioners. If one does not know this method, it will be difficult to achieve success in the methods of other deities, and moreover many obstacles will arise. Therefore one should first cultivate this path.

If any good man or good woman wishes to make offerings to this deva and seek blessings, take fragrant wood and carve the paired image, husband and wife in standing embrace. The body is five *cun* tall, elephant-headed with a human body, wearing celestial garments and a waist-cloth. The husband's trunk curves downward; the wife's trunk curves upward. They stand upon a four-petaled seat. Once the image is made, it must not be traded or sold. Place it in a private chamber, not in the buddha-hall.

The great-body mantra:

*Namo vināyaka-śahā-asti-mukha-śa tadyathā oṃ dāye ha dāye ha vināyu ha vināyu ha tārāyu ha paritārāyu ha śaṃkha-kāśti śaṃkha-kāśta śānti-kara svāhā.*

This great-body mantra is used for the methods of pacification and attraction through affection.

The heart mantra:

*Oṃ kiri ñak.*

This mantra is used for the method of increasing merit.

The heart-within-heart mantra:

*Oṃ ñak ñak hūṃ phaṭ.*

This mantra is used for the method of subduing enemies.

**The body-mantra mudrā**: Both little fingers and ring fingers internally crossed. Both middle fingers and index fingers layered and crossed, standing erect. Both thumbs standing straight side by side. This is called the root mudrā. Seal the heart, forehead, throat, and crown with this mudrā to accomplish body protection.

**The heart-mantra mudrā**: The right little finger and ring finger grasp the middle and index fingers, which are layered horizontally and erect. The thumb stands erect alongside. This is simply the half-mudrā of the previous body-mudrā. Use this mudrā to purify and protect the offering implements and the bathing oil.

**The heart-within-heart mudrā**: Both hands form the vajra-bond and place them together at the forehead. Recite the mantra and cast it forth; evil persons and hostile enemies will certainly be subdued.

**The first altar method**: Construct a round altar two cubits across. Mix cow dung with yellow earth to make the clay coating. Prepare two sets of offerings, namely: arghya water, paste-incense, seasonal flowers, burning incense, radish dumplings, pure ghee and honey, seasonal fruits, and milk-porridge dishes. Store two sets of each offering item and place them on the left and right sides of the altar. Separately, in a copper bowl, place one *shēng* of pure sesame oil. Recite the great-body mantra 108 times to purify it, then warm the oil and bathe the deity's image.

The water-empowerment mantra:

*Oṃ anabha-cāri bhāli nāha.*

**The second method: recitation and offerings**. The Buddha-mother heart mudrā: both hands in internal bond at the forehead, both thumbs placed alongside the index fingers. The mantra:

*Oṃ jina-ji-ga svāhā.*

Next, the Lotus-mother heart mudrā: as the previous Buddha-mother, but extend the right index finger, moving it back and forth. The mantra:

*Oṃ āruri-ga svāhā.*

Next, the Vajra-mother heart mudrā: the mudrā is the same as before, but the left index finger moves back and forth. The mantra:

*Oṃ vajra-dhṛk-ka svāhā.*

Next, the armor mudrā: the left hand in a fist pressing the hip; the right hand likewise in a fist, sealing five places: forehead, left shoulder, right shoulder, heart, and throat. The mantra:

*Oṃ bhūr vajra hūṃ.*

Next, the purification-water mudrā: use the vajra mudrā. The mantra:

*Oṃ kiri kiri vajra hūṃ phaṭ.*

Next, use the half root mudrā to purify the offering implements upon the altar.

Next, face the deity's image, bow in repentance, rejoice in others' merits, and beseech the teaching.

Next, arouse the contemplation that this Holy Lord Maheśvara Deva is none other than Mahāvairocana Tathāgata, who, for the sake of enriching sentient beings lacking merit, provisionally manifests in this form. One who arouses this contemplation and takes refuge, making offerings, will certainly obtain immeasurable accumulations of merit.

Next, establish the earth boundary: touch the ground with the root mudrā and recite the heart mantra.

Next, establish the four-directional boundary: use the previous mudrā and mantra, circling in all four directions as desired to complete it.

Next, use the pure-earth transformation mudrā and mantra to purify the earth at seven places.

Next, the chariot-sending mudrā: both hands internally crossed, then open the palms. Both index fingers extend straight with tips touching. Use both thumbs to flick the tips of the middle fingers. The mantra:

*Oṃ turu turu hūṃ.*

Next, the invitation mudrā: as the previous root mudrā, but move the thumbs back and forth. The mantra:

*Oṃ bhagara-junadai-bha-patāya.*

Next, form the four-petaled seat: join both palms with both index fingers side by side, both ring fingers and little fingers touching alongside each other, left and right middle fingers and ring fingers each touching and standing apart. This forms the four-petaled seat. Recite the heart mantra and visualize the holy one coming to sit upon this seat.

Next, establish the space boundary: the mudrā and mantra are as before; rotate the mudrā upward three times to complete it.

Next, the fire-flame mudrā: the left hand secretly covers the back of the right hand, stacked together. Both thumbs stand erect, about two *cun* apart. The mantra:

*Oṃ asama-agni hūṃ.*

Next, still the mind, settle the thoughts, and perform the offering method. Vajra joined palms, reciting the mantra:

*Oṃ aḥ svāhā.*

Next, place the deity's image into the warm oil bowl. Beginning with arghya water, take each offering in sequence, form the hand mudrā, lift it to the crown in homage, and present it as an offering. The hand mudrā: both hands in fists placed together, with the offering vessel placed on top. With a mind of sincere reverence, mentally make the offering of arghya. The mantra:

*Oṃ huṃ-ḍa-ri-ñāya-ya svāhā.*

The mudrā for paste-incense, flowers, burning incense, and all other offering implements is the same. The mantra:

*Oṃ hūṃ-ḍa-ri-ñāya-ya svāhā.*

For the wine offering, use the mudrā and mantra for presenting arghya water.

Next, form the maṇi offering mudrā: both hands in vajra joined palms, both index fingers forming a jewel shape, both thumbs standing erect. The mantra:

*Namo amogha-pūjā-maṇi-padma-vaśri tathāgata-vilokite samanta-prasāra hūṃ.*

This accomplishes offerings vast enough to fill the entire dharma-realm.

Next, chant the Four Wisdom Praises. Vajra joined palms at the forehead, then circle the mudrā left and right.

Next, chant the holy one's praise three times; the mudrā is the same as before:

*Oṃ gaṇa-pati śānti-svā-siddhi mahā-gaṇa-pati svāhā.*

Next, form the root mudrā, recite the great-body mantra seven times, seal the five places of the body, and bow to Mahāvairocana Tathāgata.

Next, bow to Gaṇapati-devī-prajñā.

Next, the recitation: using a spoon, ladle oil and pour it over the image. Seven hundred pourings in a single day: four hundred in the early morning and three hundred at midday. While pouring the oil, concentrate the mind with solemn reverence and do not give rise to other thoughts. At the end of every 108 recitations, offer praises and make offerings, repeatedly making vows and stating the matters you contemplate. Within three to seven days, all virtuous wishes will be fulfilled as desired, and all evil affairs will be entirely extinguished. This Maheśvara Deva dwells in the Paranirmita-vaśavartin Heaven. Having descended to roam the lower world, the wind-wheel binds and sinks his body. Therefore one uses wine, medicines, radishes, and oil as offerings. Because he always maintains a fast, he does not accept offerings in the evening. The food that has been offered the mantra-master should eat himself; his complexion, strength, merit, and longevity will all increase.

**The third method: binding the trunks together**. If after three to seven days of performing the method and making offerings to the holy lord there is no response to one's prayers, burn incense again, make vows, and state the reasons for one's petition. Take a white thread, recite the great-body mantra 108 times over it, twist the thread, and tie 108 knots. Cut it into two pieces and bind the trunks of the two devas together. The mudrā is the conch mudrā: both hands in vajra joined palms, left and right index fingers and thumbs each pinching together. After binding the trunks, for three days and nights one must not go elsewhere or speak with other people.

**The fourth method: the harmonizing method**. The elephant-headed, human-bodied image in its fierce form has six arms, broken tusks, and a curling trunk. The left hands hold a knife, a fruit tray, and a wheel; the right hands hold a staff, a lasso, and a tusk. When there is a time of wrathful displeasure, before this image make a small round altar. Set out delicacies, food and drink, wine, medicines, radish joy-balls, and so forth, and perform the invitation. Both palms joined, bend both index fingers to pinch the thumbs. The mantra:

*Hūṃ hūṃ cāṭuru pharā-mātha-nī ye svāhā.*

With each recitation, move the index fingers back and forth. Then with utmost sincerity, bow and repent, and with each offering item, present it with praises. Recite the Maheśvara mantra 1,080 times. The mudrā: both little fingers and ring fingers crossed within the palm; both middle fingers erect with tips touching; both index fingers each placed against the back of the first joint of the middle finger. The mantra:

*Oṃ vitāra-sani-bhara-mārtha-ni cita-ni cita-ni vita-ni vita-ni svāhā.*

When the offerings are complete, state the matters you contemplate. Remove the offering implements from the altar. During the first watch of the night, outside the altar make a water-maṇḍala. Mix the offerings with clean water and scatter them over the water-maṇḍala and cast them in all directions. The mantra:

*Aaiśa-gaśa-bhaccha.*

**The fifth method: accomplishing all undertakings**. If one seeks to gain the affection of a king or similar personage, burn kṛṣṇāgaru incense. Over three days and nights, establish the altar and offerings, and recite the Bhāgara mantra three times daily, 1,000 recitations per session. The mudrā: left and right little fingers and ring fingers crossed within the palm. Both middle fingers erect with tips nearly touching but not joined. Both index fingers and thumbs press against the ring fingers. Seal seven places: both shoulders, below the throat, between the eyebrows, the hairline, the crown, and the back of the head. The mantra:

*Oṃ vināyaka senāyaka-deva-bhāgara-ṇāya svāhā.*

If one wishes to subjugate an evil person, write that person's name beneath one's own feet. For one day and night, abstain from food and drink. Form the binding mudrā and recite the abhicāra mantra, 1,000 recitations per session. The mudrā: both hands joined face-down. Both middle fingers extend straight with tips touching. Both ring fingers are bent. Both middle fingers and little fingers extend with tips touching. Both thumbs press atop the two middle fingers. Both index fingers are each bent over the thumb. The thumb and index finger tips each move. Use: *Oṃ gaṇapati abhicāru-śa svāhā.*

If one wishes to stop quarrels and slander, take well-water at dawn, recite the great-body mantra 100 times over the water, and scatter it toward the direction of the offender. The quarrels will certainly cease. For all matters one contemplates and seeks, use the three mantras of the body, heart, and heart-within-heart, practicing the recitation as one wishes. There is no worldly undertaking that cannot be accomplished. This is the essential path of worldly methods. In the secret arts of human relations, what instantaneous method requiring no effort could surpass this? I have now searched out the hidden treasury of the secret teaching and transmit it for posterity. I pray that those with aspiring minds and wisdom may test it, but be cautious: do not transmit it recklessly.
