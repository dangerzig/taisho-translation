# 佛說俱枳羅陀羅尼經
## Sūtra of the Kokila Dhāraṇī Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1385

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmabhadra (法賢)

---

Thus have I heard. At one time the Buddha was dwelling in the Jetavana, the garden of Anāthapiṇḍada, in the land of Śrāvastī, together with a great assembly of bhikṣus. Also present were devas, humans, asuras, gandharvas, and others who had all come together and were seated.

At that time, the Buddha said to the Venerable Ānanda: "Listen carefully. I have a dhāraṇī called the Kokila. It possesses great power: it is capable of bringing good fortune; capable of destroying the hold exerted by bhūtas and malevolent astral influences; and capable of curing all diseases, namely: digestive illness, fever and chills, headache and migraine, coughing, wheezing, and shortness of breath, as well as hemorrhoids and leucorrhea, and various wounds and sores. Ānanda, even for living beings whose lives are about to be cut short, it can extend them. Therefore I now proclaim it for you. Ānanda, if there are people who hear this dhāraṇī, who arouse a firm resolve to read, recite, and uphold it, the wholesome benefit such people will obtain is immeasurable and boundless." The World-Honored One then spoke the dhāraṇī:

> *tathā oṃ ili miri ciri bhavara kukile śrīmati kuṇḍale nunde nunde indra vajra giri mūriṇī sarva vajra giri mūriṇī brahma svāhā yathā guru buddha sattvanāṃ virāga śreṣṭha sarva dharmanāṃ gaṇa pravara saṃgha sattvakāyena agnina sattvakāyena mama sarva sattvanāṃ asitaṃ betaṃ kāṇinaṃ aśānitaṃ samyak kuskhena pariṇāmagato svāhā*

When the World-Honored One had finished speaking this dhāraṇī, he said to Ānanda: "Wherever this dhāraṇī is present, I see no one throughout heaven or among humans, whether Brahmā, Māra, śramaṇa, or brahmin, who would dare transgress it. You should uphold it and proclaim it to others, so that many people may obtain great benefit."

Ānanda and the devas, humans, asuras, gandharvas, and others in the assembly, having heard what the Buddha said, rejoiced, had faith, and accepted it; they made obeisance and withdrew.
