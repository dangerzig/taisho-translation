# 觀自在菩薩心真言一印念誦法
## Method of Recitation with One Mudrā for Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva's Heart Mantra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1041

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

When the practitioner wishes to recite, one should first recite the purification mantra seven times. The mantra says:

*Oṃ śuddhi-udbhāvanāya svāhā*

By reciting this mantra, one obtains purification of the three karmas, as if bathing body and mind with the waters of meritorious dharma. Then form the mudrā of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva.

Cross both hands and make fists, with the thumbs extending outward and standing upright: this completes the mudrā. Recite the heart mantra seven times. The mantra says:

*Oṃ ārolik svāhā*

By forming this mudrā and reciting the mantra, the samaya is established. Then, forming the same mudrā and reciting the mantra three times, rotate the mudrā leftward three circles: this dispels all māras and obstructors. Then, forming the same mudrā and reciting three times, rotate rightward three circles: this establishes the boundary.

Visualize before oneself an eight-petaled lotus. Then, forming the same mudrā and reciting seven times, beckon with the thumb toward oneself.

Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva and all the holy assembly of the Lotus Division then gather together. Take up the arghya vessel of scented water in both hands, raise it to the forehead as an offering, recite seven times, and visualize bathing the holy assembly.

Then, forming the same mudrā and reciting seven times, this becomes the offering of a lotus seat.

Then, forming the same mudrā and reciting three times, this becomes the offering of perfume.

Then, forming the same mudrā and reciting three times, this becomes the offering of flower garlands.

Then, forming the same mudrā and reciting three times, this becomes the offering of various celestial foods and drink.

Then, forming the same mudrā and reciting three times, this becomes the offering of lamps.

Then, forming the same mudrā at the heart and reciting seven times, visualize oneself seated upon the platform of an eight-petaled lotus, with marks and majestic radiance complete and perfect, identical to Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva. On each of the eight petals, a tathāgata sits in meditation, facing Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva. The practitioner should make this visualization extremely vivid and clear, then raise the mudrā above the crown and release it.

Take up the rosary and coil it in the palm. Join the palms at the heart and recite the rosary-empowerment mantra seven times. The mantra says:

*Oṃ vairocana-mālā svāhā*

Then, with both hands at the heart, gather all five fingers of each hand like an unopened lotus. Recite the mantra at the heart, neither hurriedly nor sluggishly, clearly articulating each syllable without voicing it aloud. In silent recitation, move one bead in time with the sound of the final syllable of each mantra. During the heart mantra phrase, omit the first and last syllables. While reciting, do not allow distraction; keep body and mind still and focused, attending to no other object but the principal deity, concentrating entirely on recitation.

Visualize before oneself, upon an eight-petaled lotus, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva with marks and excellences complete, seated within a moon-disc. Then contemplate one's own body as identical to Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, without any difference.

When the prescribed number of recitations is complete, raise the rosary to the crown of the head, generate a mind of compassion, and make a great vow: "May all sentient beings' hopes and supreme worldly aspirations be swiftly fulfilled." Then return the rosary to its place.

Then, forming the same mudrā at the heart and reciting seven times, the samaya is established.

Then, using the mudrā, empower one's own body at five points: the forehead, right shoulder, left shoulder, heart, and throat. These are called the five points. Recite the mantra once for each empowerment, then release the mudrā above the crown.

Then, forming the same mudrā as before, offer the five kinds of offerings, reciting the mantra once for each, and these become vast, extensive offerings. Then take the arghya water in both hands, raise it to the forehead as an offering, and recite once.

Then, forming the same mudrā and rotating leftward once, the boundary is dissolved.

Then, forming the same mudrā and flicking the thumb outward, recite three times and raise the mudrā to the crown: this dismisses all the holy assembly to return.

Then, forming the same mudrā, empower one's own body at the five points, reciting once for each: this becomes the armor. Release the mudrā above the crown.

If the practitioner follows this essential secret method with sincerity, reciting and practicing with wholehearted intention, all that one undertakes will be swiftly accomplished. Worldly and supramundane aspirations are all fulfilled, karmic obstructions are eliminated, samādhi manifests, and without leaving this very body, one ascends freely through space at will, visiting the pure lands of the ten directions, attending upon all the buddhas, and swiftly attaining supreme awakening.
