# 難儞計濕嚩囉天說支輪經
## The Sūtra on the Zodiacal Wheel Spoken by Nandīkeśvara Deva

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1312

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmabhadra (法賢).

---

At that time Nandīkeśvara Deva surveyed all the men and women of the world, and how, according to the lunar mansions under which they were born, they experience good and evil from the time of birth until the end of their lives. During that span, some are noble and some base, some rich and some poor, some filial and some loyal, some love literature and some love martial arts. As for beauty and ugliness, stupidity and intelligence, illness and untimely death, widowhood and orphanhood, fierceness and rigidity, gentleness and compliance, honest speech and upright conduct, awareness of shame and conscience, mastery of celestial lore, keen perception of worldly affairs; or devotion to the Buddha-Dharma and delight in cultivating wholesome deeds; or admiration for demonic ways, belief in heterodoxy, and commission of evil; or love of wandering in mountains and waters, following one's nature to preserve one's true self; or delight in trade and commerce in precious goods; or fondness for generosity or else miserliness and greed: all of these are manifested in one's physical features, in one's appearance and limbs, as banner-marks, conch-marks, birthmarks, and moles. All beauty and ugliness are borne by a single body, and this is also because of causes and conditions from past lives that one encounters these stars and luminaries in the present. Lest people not understand, he briefly proclaimed the following:

"The celestial sign of Aries is under the jurisdiction of the fire-planet [Mars], ruling over the entire portions of the Lousu and Weisu mansions and one portion of the Maosu mansion. Those men and women born on these days will have handsome countenances and delicate bodies. They will be filial toward their parents, respectful toward elders and superiors, have many relatives, plentiful servants, possess bravery and vigor, and have great merit. They will not tolerate hunger and cold. They will delight in the Dharma, know shame and conscience, love generosity, and be firm of mind. Their words and deeds will be true and genuine, their understanding clear and perceptive. They are destined to be loved and valued by the sovereign and admired by all. Their eyes will be lustful and fond of beauty, and they will love to travel. Throughout their lives they will have many teeth ailments and bile disorders, and their lifespan may end prematurely. In the fourth year after birth there will be a danger of fire. At age eighteen there will again be a risk of premature death. At age twenty-five there will be a danger from tigers and wolves, and also a risk of death at sea. When these mortal dangers arise, if an auspicious luminary happens to shine upon them, their lifespan will be extended to one hundred years. They will die in the eighth month, on a day when the fire-planet and the Maosu mansion are in conjunction, at midnight.

"The celestial sign of Taurus is under the jurisdiction of the metal-planet [Venus], ruling over three portions of the Maosu mansion and two portions each of the Bisu and Sensu mansions. Those men and women born on these days will have handsome countenances, tall and slender bodies, and a star-shaped mole on their face. They are destined for great wealth and will constantly enjoy happiness. They will have many relatives and plentiful servants. Their temperament will be strong and rigid, and they will not be well-suited to wives and concubines. In youth they will face hardship, but in later years they will obtain their parents' wealth and then enjoy happiness. They will have vitality, skill in crafts, clear perception and understanding, and firmness in all they undertake. They will love generosity but be fond of the taste of food and frequently suffer from throat ailments. At age twenty-five, their life may be cut short. If at that time an auspicious luminary shines upon them, evil will be transformed to good and their lifespan will be extended to one hundred years. They will later suffer from wind-madness disease and will die in the ninth month, on a day when the water-planet [Mercury] and the Bisu mansion are in conjunction, at a holy site.

"The celestial sign of Gemini is under the jurisdiction of the water-planet [Mercury], ruling over two portions of the Sensu mansion and three portions each of the Zuisu and Jingsu mansions. Those men and women born on these days will be gentle and good by nature, with dignified physical appearance. Their speech will be truthful and their conversation and debate quick and nimble. In their friendships they will be loyal and enduring. They will be especially fond of incense and flowers and enjoy the taste of food. By nature they will be witty, and they will be excessively fond of women. Upon seeing anything beautiful, their minds will become entranced. They will love all manner of magical arts, and if they practice siddhi methods, they will certainly succeed. If they study dragon-binding or water-empowering spells, they will achieve the greatest mastery in these two methods. In youth they will face hardship, but from middle age onward they will gain reputation and will be loved and esteemed by those who meet them. From birth until the fifth year there will be a risk of premature death. If at that time an auspicious luminary shines upon them, evil will be transformed to good and their lifespan will be extended to eighty years. They will die in the tenth month, on a day when the water-planet and the Sensu mansion are in conjunction, at midday, from illness.

"The celestial sign of Cancer is under the jurisdiction of the moon [the lunar luminary], ruling over the entire portions of the Jingsu, Guisu, and Liusu mansions. Those men and women born on these days will have tall and slender bodies and be gentle and good by nature. They will have wisdom and the ability to be patient, and will be filial and obedient to their parents, earning their parents' special love and affection. On their palms and the soles of their feet there will be auspicious marks in the form of lotuses, conches, wheels, and auspicious-fruit patterns. They will have many wives, concubines, and relatives, as well as many servants. In youth they will be of humble station, but in later years they will become wealthy. They will delight in the Dharma with bravery and love generosity, making offerings to sages and teachers. By nature they will love traveling far. From birth to age twenty, there will be a danger of falling from a tree, and this may cause an untimely death. If at that time an auspicious luminary shines upon them, evil will be transformed to good and their lifespan will be extended to eighty years. They will die in the eleventh month, on a day when the Sensu mansion and the metal-planet are in conjunction, at midnight.

"The celestial sign of Leo is under the jurisdiction of the sun [the solar luminary], ruling over one portion each of the Xingsu, Zhangsu, and Yisu mansions. Those men and women born on these days will be impatient and hasty by nature, often acting in anger. They will have two or three children whom they will love more than their own lives. Though wealthy in treasures, their hearts will always be content. They will love making offerings to all sages. They will be unconstrained in manner, carefree and self-satisfied. They will be especially fond of eating meat and also of eating fish. Even when traveling to other lands, they will not suffer hunger or cold. They will constantly fear illness but will not fear great floods. At age fifteen there will be a risk of premature death. If an auspicious luminary shines upon them, evil will be transformed to good and their lifespan will be extended to one hundred years. They will die in the twelfth month, on a day when the earth-planet [Saturn] and the Xingsu mansion are in conjunction, on the north bank of the Ganges River.

"The celestial sign of Virgo is under the jurisdiction of the water-planet [Mercury], ruling over three portions of the Yisu mansion and two portions each of the Zhensu and Jiaosu mansions. Those men and women born on these days will have faces like monkeys with wide eyes and a mole below the ribs. People who see them will feel respect and affection. They will delight in the Dharma and love incense and flowers. In youth they will be of humble station, but in later years they will be wealthy. However, they will suffer many illnesses, especially eye ailments. They will prefer living in other lands and will not be well-suited to wives and concubines. Whenever they see something beautiful, they will become entranced. At age thirty there will be a risk of premature death. If at that time an auspicious luminary shines upon them, evil will be transformed to good and their lifespan will be extended to eighty years. They will die in the first month from a sore.

"The celestial sign of Libra is under the jurisdiction of the metal-planet [Venus], ruling over two portions of the Jiaosu mansion and three portions each of the Kangsu and Disu mansions. Those men and women born on these days will be filial and obedient, with natural merit and fortune. They will be impatient and hasty by nature, often becoming angry. However, their perception and understanding will be clear and penetrating, their observation meticulous and subtle. They will also have a sense of honor and the ability to be firm and enduring. They will be generous toward relatives and friends. They will be witty and fond of beauty, traveling frequently to foreign lands, and may sometimes obtain unexpected pleasures. They are destined to have banner and canopy marks on their palms. At age twenty-two there will be a risk of premature death. If at that time an auspicious luminary shines upon them, evil will be transformed to good and their lifespan will be extended to ninety-seven. They will die in the second month, on a day when the fire-planet and the Fangsu mansion are in conjunction.

"The celestial sign of Scorpio is under the jurisdiction of the fire-planet [Mars], ruling over one portion each of the Disu, Fangsu, and Xinsu mansions. Those men and women born on these days are destined to have sharp faculties, and whatever they study they will easily master. They will have a sense of honor and, though wealthy in treasures, their wealth will alternately accumulate and disperse. At age eighteen there will be a danger from snakes, scorpions, tigers, and wolves. At age twenty there will again be a risk of premature death. If at that time an auspicious luminary shines upon them, evil will be transformed to good and their lifespan will be extended to seventy-seven years. They will die in the third month, on a day when the fire-planet and the Fangsu mansion are in conjunction.

"The celestial sign of Sagittarius is under the jurisdiction of the wood-planet [Jupiter], ruling over one portion each of the Weisu, Jisu, and Dousu mansions. Those men and women born on these days are destined to have dignified and handsome bodies and auspicious moles. They will be gentle and good by nature, firm in upholding the precepts, and their every word will be truthful. They will possess abundant wisdom and love discourse and debate. Throughout their lives they will dwell in great wealth, and in youth they will also discover hidden treasures. At age eighteen there will be a risk of premature death. If at that time an auspicious luminary shines upon them, evil will be transformed to good and their lifespan will be extended to seventy-seven years. They will die in the fourth month, on a day when the metal-planet and the Niusu mansion are in conjunction, by fasting and entering the water.

"The celestial sign of Capricorn is under the jurisdiction of the earth-planet [Saturn], ruling over three portions of the Dousu mansion and two portions each of the Niusu and Nüsu mansions. Those men and women born on these days will have straight and upright bodies with a mole in a hidden place. They will be narrow-minded and impetuous, often angry, with shifty and cunning eyes. However, they will have a sense of honor and uncommonly great wisdom. They will be thoroughly loyal and filial, have many friends, possess a brave heart, love warfare and combat, love song and dance, and be fond of arts and skills. They will also love incense and flowers and have many wives and concubines. At age thirty there will be a risk of premature death. If at that time an auspicious luminary shines upon them, evil will be transformed to good and their lifespan will be extended to seventy-five. They will die in the fifth month, on a day when the earth-planet and the Weisu mansion are in conjunction.

"The celestial sign of Aquarius is under the jurisdiction of the earth-planet [Saturn], ruling over two portions of the Nüsu mansion and three portions each of the Weisu and Shisu mansions. Those men and women born on these days are destined for great wealth and will constantly enjoy happiness. They will have many relatives and plentiful servants. They will be devoted to their parents with heartfelt filial care. Though they have spouses, they will not guard their wealth well, and among the children they bear, some may die young. Their eyes will be shifty, but they will have a sense of honor. In all matters they will be keenly observant and act with lasting purpose. They will not be miserly but will love generosity, though the wealth they accumulate will also suffer losses. They will fear tigers and wolves. At age eighteen there will be a danger from tigers and wolves. If at that time an auspicious luminary shines upon them, evil will be transformed to good and their lifespan will be extended to eighty-four. They will die in the sixth month, on a day when the sun and the Kuisu mansion are in conjunction, from a sore.

"The celestial sign of Pisces is under the jurisdiction of the wood-planet [Jupiter], ruling over one portion each of the Shisu, Bisu, and Kuisu mansions. Those men and women born on these days will have dignified and beautiful bodies with shifty eyes. They will have many wives, concubines, and relatives, as well as plentiful servants. They will be impatient and hasty by nature, often becoming angry. They will have muscular strength, be fond of meat and also of fish. However, they will love generosity and making offerings to sages. They will love incense and flowers, be fond of arts and skills, be attached to beauty, and may suffer from female jealousy. Their speech will be mostly truthful, but they may also be destined to slander their teachers. They will be prone to ear ailments and are also destined for long life. At age eighteen there will be a danger of falling from a tree or wall. If an auspicious luminary shines upon them, evil will be transformed to good and their lifespan will be extended to ninety-five. They will die in the seventh month, on a day when the earth-planet and the Bisu mansion are in conjunction, from a sore."

"Furthermore, the mansions are grouped into three destinies:

"The nine mansions of Kuisu, Lousu, Sensu, Jingsu, Guisu, Zhensu, Kangsu, Fangsu, and Niusu belong to the deva-destiny. The nine mansions of Weisu, Maosu, Zuisu, Liusu, Xingsu, Jiaosu, Disu, Xinsu, and Weisu belong to the rākṣasa-destiny. The nine mansions of Jisu, Shisu, Zhangsu, Bisu, Nüsu, Weisu, Dousu, Bisu, and Yisu belong to the human-destiny. If one is born under the shining of the mansions belonging to the deva-destiny, both men and women will dwell in wealth and nobility. Their descendants will flourish, and all will be auspicious. This is the foremost."

At that time Nandīkeśvara Deva, having spoken the zodiacal wheel for all the men and women of the world, departed with joy.
