# 佛說施一切無畏陀羅尼經
## The Buddha's Teaching: Dhāraṇī Sūtra of Bestowing Fearlessness upon All

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1373

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dānapāla (施護) and others.

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the World-Honored One was traveling in the land of Magadha, in the Āmra Grove. He was dwelling in a cave called Śakra's Rock on Mount Vaidehī.

At that time Śakra Devendra came to where the Buddha was and addressed him: "World-Honored One! I am beset by fear. The asuras, throughout the long night, come to disturb and harass me. The asuras are my enemies. We humbly pray that the World-Honored One will teach us a Dharma-gate of protection."

At that time the World-Honored One addressed Śakra Devendra: "Lord of the Devas! I have a dhāraṇī called Bestowing Fearlessness upon All. This Dharma can skillfully accomplish all tasks, can eliminate all diseases, and can restrain all animals. It can undo all bonds of enmity. If there are asuras, rākṣasas, bhūtas, or any evil anomalies, or if one is seized by any evil ghosts and spirits, namely seizure by evil forces, seizure by devas, seizure by nāgas, seizure by yakṣas, seizure by gandharvas, seizure by kiṃnaras, seizure by vināyakas, seizure by mother-ghosts, and the like, all will be eliminated. Furthermore, those who suck vital essences, those who eat flower garlands, those who obstruct childbirth, and those who eat impurities will all be expelled. Furthermore, all malarial fevers and diseases caused by wind, jaundice, phlegm, and congestion will all be dispersed. All battles, lawsuits, and hostile armies will all be defeated. I shall now teach you this dhāraṇī as your protection." The dhāraṇī states:

*Tathā iṇi miṇi pra-miṇi pra-miṇi rigu rī-muri muṣṭhi-nati vati kha-rati kha-ra-ṇi ga-ṇa-ni ga-ṇa pra-ga-ni gaṇa-gani muni prati-muni kāri pra-kāri caṇi prati-caṇi vige prati-vige ati-vige su-nā-guṇā mahā-pra-mahā maṇa-pra-maṇa nāśani pra-nāśani dhavani pra-dhavani vālokani pra-vālokani nirmitani pra-nirmitani kru-dhani ati-kru-dhani hana hana vihana vihana sarva-duṣṭa-pra-duṣṭa-nāśaya sarva-bhaya-pra-ṇā rakṣa rakṣa māṃ sarva-medhi-bhaya-duṣṭa-śatru-taskara-nirmita-rūpa-ṇa-dra-vibhaya-sa-parivāra laṃ kṣe-ṇi kṣe-ṇi sarva-sattvā-hite-rā-ti-nāma sarva-buddhānāṃ svāhā.*

"If one who recites and upholds this Dhāraṇī of Bestowing Fearlessness upon All empowers a clean thread forty-one times and ties it upon the crown of the head, or empowers white mustard seeds, or empowers ox-bezoar, then whatever application is made will be accomplished.

"Furthermore, if one empowers fragrant ointment once and applies it to one's body, it will be like wearing armor: one will be brave and fearless. One will not be wounded by blades, not harmed by poison, and will not be afflicted by malarial fevers of one day, two days, and so on up to seven days. One will not be burned by fire, not drowned by water, not harmed by spells, not troubled by ghosts and goblins, not afflicted by illness, and will not die an untimely death from any cause.

"Furthermore, this dhāraṇī can skillfully accomplish all corresponding activities. All methods of accomplishment will harmonize together. Whatever acts of accomplishment are performed, none will fail. It can halt the workings of others and cannot be bound by others. It can destroy evil vidyā. One's own Dharma will turn as one wills. All māra-obstacles can be expelled, and all possessing spirits can be dissolved. If any possession cannot be removed thereby, the possessor's head will split into seven pieces like the branch of an arjaka tree."

At that time Śakra Devendra, having heard what the Buddha taught, leaped for joy, bowed in reverence, and departed.
