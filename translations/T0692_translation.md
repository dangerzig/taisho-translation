# 佛說作佛形像經
## Sūtra of Making Buddha Images, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0692

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown

---

The Buddha came to the land of Kauśāmbī. There was a garden owner named Ghosita. At that time the king of the land was named Udayana, aged fourteen. Hearing that the Buddha was coming, the king immediately ordered his ministers and attendants to prepare a grand procession. The king then set out to welcome the Buddha. Seeing the Buddha from afar, his heart leaped with joy. The king dismounted from his chariot and walked, dismissing his ministers and the attendants bearing the parasol. He hastened forward to welcome the Buddha, bowed with his head at the Buddha's feet, circumambulated the Buddha three times, knelt with palms joined, and said to the Buddha: "Among all devas and humans above and below, none can equal the Buddha! Now I see the Buddha's face, body, and bearing; his radiant splendor is majestic. How wonderful it is! I gaze upon the Buddha and never grow weary. The Buddha is the teacher of all devas and humans above and below. The Buddha's compassion extends to so many."

The Buddha was silent and did not respond.

The king further said to the Buddha: "When a person does good, what reward of blessing will they receive, and where will it lead? After the Buddha departs, I fear I will no longer see the Buddha. I wish to make an image of the Buddha and serve it with reverence. What merit will I afterwards obtain? I wish the Buddha to have compassion and explain this for me. I wish to hear and understand."

The Buddha said: "Young king! Your question is very good! Listen to my words. After hearing them, keep them in your heart!"

The king said: "Yes! I accept your teaching."

The Buddha told the king: "If one makes an image of the Buddha, the reward of blessing one obtains, I shall explain it all for you."

The king said: "I am grateful!"

The Buddha said: "Anyone in the world who makes an image of the Buddha: in their future lives, wherever they are born, their eyes will be pure and clear, their face and features will be upright and handsome, and their body, hands, and feet will always be fine. Born in heaven, they will also be pure and clear, surpassing all the other devas, with a face and features of incomparable beauty. Making a Buddha image, the reward is such as this.

"One who makes a Buddha image, wherever born, will have no defects of body; their form will be wholly perfect. After death, they will be born in the seventh Brahmā heaven, superior to all other devas, upright and handsome beyond compare, revered by all the devas. Making a Buddha image, the reward is such as this.

"One who makes a Buddha image, in future lives will always be born into a noble and distinguished family, truly surpassing all worldly people. Wherever born, they will not be a child of a poor family. Making a Buddha image, the reward is such as this.

"One who makes a Buddha image, in future lives their body will always be the color of purple-golden jāmbūnada, upright and handsome beyond compare.

"One who makes a Buddha image, in future lives wherever born will be in a wealthy family, with riches and treasures beyond counting, always cherished by parents, siblings, and kinfolk. Making a Buddha image, the reward is such as this.

"One who makes a Buddha image, in future lives born in Jambudvīpa, will always be born in the family of an emperor, a king, or a prince, a child of a worthy and good household. Making a Buddha image, the reward is such as this.

"One who makes a Buddha image, in future lives will become the most honored and supreme among emperors and kings, and all the kings of the nations will submit to them. Making a Buddha image, the reward is such as this.

"One who makes a Buddha image, in future lives will become a wheel-turning king, ascending to heaven and descending again at will, accomplishing everything without limit. Making a Buddha image, the reward is such as this.

"One who makes a Buddha image, in future lives will be born in the seventh Brahmā heaven, with a lifespan of one kalpa, and wisdom that none can equal.

"One who makes a Buddha image, after death will never again be born in the evil paths. Wherever born, one will always keep self-discipline; one's mind will always aspire to seek the Buddha-path. Making a Buddha image, the reward is such as this.

"One who makes a Buddha image, in future lives will always revere the Buddha, with compassion toward the sūtras, always offering fine silks, beautiful flowers, fine incense, burning lamps, and all the precious and rare things of the world to the Buddha's relics. After innumerable kalpas, one will certainly attain the path of nirvāṇa. Those who, with a devoted mind, offer precious things to the Buddha are no ordinary persons; in past lives they practiced the Buddha-path. Making a Buddha image, the reward is such as this.

"One who makes a Buddha image: the reward in future lives is without end or limit, beyond all reckoning. Even the waters of all the rivers and oceans of the four continents can be measured cup by cup until exhausted, but the reward of making a Buddha image surpasses the waters of the four continents' rivers and oceans tenfold. In future lives, one will be respected and protected by all.

"Making a Buddha image is like having a good shelter when it rains from the sky: one has nothing to fear.

"One who makes a Buddha image, after death will never again be born in the evil paths of hell, animals, or hungry ghosts. Anyone who sees a Buddha image and, with a compassionate heart, joins their palms and takes refuge in the Buddha's stūpa and relics: for a hundred kalpas after death they will not enter the evil paths of hell, animals, or hungry ghosts. Upon death they will be born in heaven. When their heavenly lifespan ends, they will be born again in the human world as a child of a wealthy family, with precious treasures beyond counting. Afterwards they will certainly attain the Buddha's path of nirvāṇa."

The Buddha told the king: "O doer of good! Making a Buddha image, the reward of blessing is such as this; it is not in vain."

The king was delighted. He went before the Buddha, bowed in reverence, and placed his head at the Buddha's feet. The king and all his ministers bowed to the Buddha and departed. At the end of their lives, they were all born in the land of Amitābha Buddha.
