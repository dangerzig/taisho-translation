# 大毘盧遮那佛眼修行儀軌

## The Mahāvairocana Buddhanetra Practice Ritual Manual

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0981

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, compiled by Yīxíng (一行).

---

At that time, Acala Vidyārāja, wielding the wisdom-sword in his fierce and wrathful aspect, paints cloud-dragons in the direction he faces, and with the great wisdom-sword mudrā severs and destroys them, whereupon those obstructions immediately scatter and vanish. The reason is this: when people in the world paint a maṇḍala on open ground outdoors, if an evil nāga sends rain it immediately destroys it. When a practitioner paints the great compassion maṇḍala on the ground of the mind, it is just the same. If the three-poison evil nāgas send violent rain and rushing waters that cause the inconceivable colored pigments to be submerged and lost, then one uses the stainless wisdom-body to become Acala Vidyārāja in fierce and mighty form, and with the wisdom-sword mudrā severs the dark clouds. Whichever direction one faces is then completely clear and open.

The *khaḍga* sword is the vajra peg. Its head is shaped like a single-pronged vajra, while the lower body is sharp and pointed like a peg. If still smaller, it is called a vajra needle; their forms differ. When an ācārya establishes a maṇḍala, or when one courageously arouses bodhicitta and uses the vajra-peg method to calm wind and rain, one should use khadira wood. If that is unavailable, use bitter neem wood or even wrought iron. Use Vajrasattva's empowerment for accomplishing all tasks. Contemplate that one's own body is identical to all vajra beings, and by driving pegs into the directions from which obstructions arise, cause the entire ground of the practice-hall to become wholly vajra. For this reason, no obstruction can cause harm.

Then form the mudrā: join both palms in vajra-bond, bend the two index fingers with tips pointing downward, and press each thumb against the side of the index finger. Place the mudrā at the heart, recite the mantra three times, and scatter at the crown. The mantra is:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ kaṃ*

Next, contemplate Acala Vidyārāja in his fierce and mighty form, dwelling in the position of his own maṇḍala. This is a triangular maṇḍala with blazing flames all around, its interior slightly dark. There are two approaches: either contemplate the Sacred Acala dwelling in his position, with samādhi-feet treading upon the head of the obstructor; or contemplate that one's own body is Acala himself, dwelling in that position, with samādhi-feet pressing upon the obstructor's head, confronting it with wrathful authority. Whatever beings cause obstruction will immediately retreat and scatter. If they stubbornly refuse to obey the command and will not depart quickly, they will certainly meet their own end. The mantra-holder should generate compassion and not allow their life-root to be severed.

The reason for this is as follows: when the pure-bodhicitta Acala Vidyārāja kills the life-span of sentient beings' karmic afflictions, he simultaneously gives birth to the true and constant life of their dharma-nature. If the buddhas were to cause practitioners to fall certainly into the ash-annihilation nirvāṇa, that would constitute the sin of killing sentient beings. One should not rigidly adhere to the literal text and lose the Tathāgata's secret intention.

Next, the practitioner should enter the contemplation of Trailokyavijaya Vidyārāja in wrathful authority, to purify and eliminate obstructions born from the self. Cross both arms in vajra fists, hook the little fingers and thumbs together, and raise the index fingers. This is the mudrā method. The practitioner then visualizes the body emitting fierce flames, with eight arms and four wrathful faces, sharp fangs upright, a tiger's roar, the syllable *hūṃ* resounding like thunder, rotating rightward above the crown to establish the boundary. The mantra is:

*Oṃ sumbha-ni sumbha hūṃ gṛhṇa gṛhṇa hūṃ gṛhṇa-paya hūṃ ānaya hoḥ bhagavan vajra hūṃ phaṭ*

Then enter the samādhi of the vajra-wrathful, brilliant, blazing Trailokyavijaya. The bodhisattva has four wrathful faces and eight arms, each grasping a weapon. The left foot treads upon Maheśvara and the right foot upon Umā, standing in a T-shaped stance. The entire body is ablaze with fire, burning like the conflagration at the end of a kalpa. This is the samādhi contemplation of the Sacred Trailokyavijaya.

The practitioner should abide in the arousal of bodhicitta and deeply give rise to compassion, extinguishing inner, outer, human, and divine obstructions. With the mudrā, rotate leftward three times to dispel obstructions, then rightward three times to establish the boundary at whatever size you wish. Stamp the mudrā on heart, forehead, throat, and crown, reciting once at each point, then scatter the mudrā at the crown. Through forming this mudrā and reciting this mantra, abiding in this wrathful samādhi, all the karmic obstructions of body and mind's afflictions are burned completely by the vajra's fierce and sharp wisdom-fire.

Next, form the body-protection mudrā of Kuṇḍalī Vidyārāja. Cross both hands with the little fingers within the palms. Bend the two ring fingers doubled into the palms, pressing them upon the crossed little fingers. Join the wrists and raise the middle fingers with tips touching. The two index fingers are slightly bent, positioned behind the upper joint of the middle fingers without touching them. The mantra is:

*Oṃ huru huru tiṣṭha tiṣṭha bandha bandha hana hana amṛte hūṃ phaṭ*

After reciting the mantra seven times: for anyone who wishes to constantly uphold the vajra-dharma, each morning after washing hands and face, scoop clean water with the right hand, recite the mantra seven times over it, and scatter it three times toward the east. Then enter the room and perform the body-protection. The body-protection method: without releasing the mudrā while reciting the mantra, stamp the crown, then the right shoulder, then the left shoulder, then the front of the heart, then below the throat, then between the eyebrows, then the hairline, then the top of the crown. After stamping the crown, this completes the mudrā method.

Next, the mudrā of Ṣaḍpāda Vidyārāja. Interlace both hands inward in vajra-bond. Raise the two middle fingers straight with tips joined. Extend the two index fingers slightly bent at the first joint like a three-pronged form. Empower the five places. This is the mudrā. The five places are: forehead, both shoulders, heart, and throat. Each thumb presses the other's nail, right over left, entering the center. The mantra is:

*Oṃ kiliṣṭri vikilitana hūṃ sarva-caturdasāya sadambhaya sadambhaya subhaṭa subhaṭa hūṃ phaṭ svāhā*

Through forming this mudrā and reciting this mantra: beginningless ignorance that has held sovereignty throughout all births and deaths can be subdued only by this bodhicitta vidyārāja. He even kills the impermanent body and grants it the constant life. The rest may be understood by analogy.

Next, form the mudrā of Vajrayakṣa Vidyārāja. Interlace both hands inward in vajra-bond with the two index fingertips touching, the space between them like an eye. Raise both thumbs alongside the index fingers without touching them, bending their first joints like fangs. Extend both little fingers with their first joints slightly bent, also like two fangs facing each other. Recite the mantra while empowering forehead, both shoulders, heart, and throat. Additionally, open the five eyes: with the mudrā, wipe right eyebrow, left eyebrow, right eye, left eye, and the point between the brows. Then scatter the mudrā at the crown. The mantra is:

*Oṃ mahā-yakṣa vajra-sattva-vaja hūṃ vaṃ hoḥ bala-vecaye hūṃ phaṭ*

Through forming this mudrā and reciting this mantra: when poisonous substances would cause obstruction, apply this toward the image. Through such treatment, the six sense-faculties of the obstructor burn as if by fire, and it immediately retreats and scatters.

Next, form the mudrā of Hayagrīva Vidyārāja. Join both palms together. Bend the two index fingers and two ring fingers into the palms, each back to back. Place the two thumbs together, slightly bent without touching the index fingers. Recite the mantra. Rotate leftward three times to dispel obstructions. Rotate rightward three times to establish the boundary. The mantra is:

*Oṃ amṛtodbhava hūṃ phaṭ svāhā*

Through forming this mudrā and reciting this mantra: the pungent and bitter medicine is the sharp remedy of impermanence, suffering, emptiness, and non-self. Just as when a disease penetrates deep into the heart, causing confusion and delirium, only then can the root source be fully reached. Even Śakra, Brahmā, and other such sovereign and blissful lords of the world, when the Tathāgata applies this dharma to them, immediately awaken to realize that body and mind blaze like a fire's harm, develop aversion to and fear of saṃsāra, and rightly accord with nirvāṇa. How much more so for other sentient beings!

Next, form the root secret mudrā of Aparājita Vidyārāja. Join both palms in a hollow, with all ten fingertips touching and sealed together. This is the mudrā.

The mantra: [Due to this mudrā and mantra, all obstructions are thoroughly stilled, causing the Tathāgata's perfect samaya and its various commands to be beyond the ability of anything to conceal.]

Furthermore, place the left palm face up over the back of the right palm. Press the right thumb upon the nail of the left thumb. Snap the right index finger three times. Then bend and grasp the index finger and the four fingers below it on both hands, raising the thumbs upright. With the right hand, rotate the mudrā leftward three times for dispelling and rightward three times for establishing the boundary. Then, with the same mudrā, point toward the four directions, four corners, and upward and downward, and stamp the mudrā on the body's five places, reciting *hūṃ* at each point for empowerment.

Next, form the mudrā of Ekākṣara Buddhōṣṇīṣa Vidyārāja. Interlace both hands inward in fists. Raise the two middle fingers, bending the upper joints like a sword shape. Place both thumbs side by side upright. Bend the two index fingers to press on the upper joint of the thumbs. This is the mudrā. Empower the five places with this mudrā. The mantra is:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ bhruṃ oṃ*

Through forming this mudrā and reciting this mantra, all evil spirits dare not approach. This mantra-master uses it to protect his own body, to protect others, to summon spirits, to dispatch spirits, and to seek the accomplishment of all undertakings. If one has been reciting mantras without spiritual verification, recite this mantra one hundred and eight times and the desired result will be accomplished. If there is still no effect, the obstructing spirit is thereby annihilated.

Next, form the Buddhanetra Vidyā-rājñī mudrā. Join both palms. Bend the two index fingers to press against the backs of the middle fingers. Bend the two thumbs into the palms. Empower the five places with this mudrā and mantra. The mantra is:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ oṃ ruru sphuru jvala-tiṣṭha siddha-locanī sarva-artha-sādhanī svāhā*

Through forming this Buddhanetra mudrā and reciting this mantra, the Secret Lord can touch upon any category and extend its meaning, pervading all dharmas. This is why it is called "knowing the three mysteries." The Buddha is then cited as witness.

Why is this? The World-Honored One is himself the samaya of all mantras, because he abides in his own lineage-nature. Just as a cakravartin king, when he abides in his own lineage-nature and follows the ancient cakravartin methods of governing the world without increase or decrease, then within his four domains all naturally submit to his command. The World-Honored One is the same: because he constantly abides within the Tathāgata's lineage-nature without transgression, all mantra-kings without exception practice in accordance with the dharma, taking equality of nature as their precept and not daring to violate it.

Furthermore, just as kṣatriyas, brāhmaṇas, and the four castes, when they abide in their own lineage-nature and follow its dharma, are praised and honored by the people of the world and achieve success in their undertakings, so too with this original maṇḍala. Equality and immobility are the vajra-ground nature, the unborn nature. Quiescence and perfect fullness are the great-compassion water nature, the nature beyond language. Sharpness and illumination are the wisdom-fire nature, the nature free from dust and defilement. Expansion and contraction, formation and destruction are the intrinsic wind nature, the nature free from causes and conditions. Formless images are the inconceivable space, the great-emptiness original nature.

These original natures, even the three-world buddhas cannot create them. How much less can any obstructor conceal them? For this reason, bodhisattvas who cultivate the bodhisattva-path of the mantra-gate should also inherit the Tathāgata's lineage-nature, emulating the sacred beings who dwell in the positions of the original maṇḍala to perform all activities.

If one wishes to perform the rite of pacification, one should manifest as the principal deity's body and dwell in a circular maṇḍala. If performing the rite of increase, dwell in a square maṇḍala. If performing the rite of subjugation, dwell in a triangular maṇḍala. If performing the rite of summoning, dwell in a half-moon maṇḍala. If one wishes to produce various adornments, dwell in a multicolored maṇḍala. This enables the practitioner to achieve verification identical to the principal deity, with no obstruction able to overshadow or overpower.

Next, contemplate the maṇḍala. Upon the platform of an eight-petaled lotus, visualize the variously adorned maṇḍala. Above the maṇḍala, visualize the ocean-assembly of the three families. Having performed this contemplation, form the vajra añjali and recite this verse:

> "Through the power of my merit,
> The power of the Tathāgata's empowerment,
> And the power of the dharmadhātu,
> I make universal offerings to all sacred beings.
> May I be born in the Land of Bliss,
> Making universal offerings and there abiding."

Next, form the Mahācakra altar mudrā. Place the left hand face down horizontally across the chest. Place the right hand palm up upon the left. Cross all eight fingers with one another. The mantra is:

*Oṃ vajra-cakra hūṃ jaḥ hūṃ vaṃ hoḥ*

Form this mudrā and recite the mantra. Place it before the heart and recite three times. Then face downward and press upon the ground, then stamp the maṇḍala, then stamp upward, then stamp the forehead, then stamp the throat. When placing at the mouth, press the mudrā to the lips once as if entering the mouth, then scatter.

Next, contemplate the boundary paths. First form the five-pronged mudrā: join both palms, with the ring and index fingers each crossed externally, the little fingers and thumbs each upright and not touching, and the middle fingers straight and upright like needles. This is the mudrā. Place it at the heart and contemplate the five-colored boundary paths within the inner court of the maṇḍala, adorning it all around.

Having performed this contemplation, visualize the syllable *ra* in white. Recite *ra* once and trace one circuit with the mudrā from the northeast corner rightward. Next, recite *raṃ* in red, one recitation and one circuit as before. Next, contemplate *ka* in yellow, one recitation and one circuit as above. Next, contemplate *ma* in blue, one recitation and one circuit as above. Next, contemplate *ha* in black, one recitation and one circuit as above.

Then recite the five-syllable mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ ra raṃ ka ma ha*

Next, form the invitation mudrā. Interlace both hands inward. Extend the right index finger, slightly bent like a hook. Recite the mantra seven times:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ vaṃ*

Next, offer argha water. Join both palms with the ring fingers and thumbs touching at the tips, the remaining fingers spread open and slightly bent. The mantra is:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ gagana-sama-sama svāhā*

Next, form the flower-seat mudrā, the same as the argha mudrā above. Recite the mantra three times:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ aḥ*

Through forming this mudrā and reciting this mantra, one attains the fulfillment of the ten bhūmis.

Next, form the perfume mudrā. Raise the five fingers of the right hand straight upward with the palm facing outward. Grasp the right wrist with the left hand. Recite the mantra three times:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ daṅgi*

Through forming this mudrā and reciting this mantra, one attains the complete fivefold unstained dharma-body of the Tathāgata.

Next, form the flower mudrā. Interlace all eight fingers of both hands inward, open the palms facing up, with the two index fingers touching at the sides. Recite the mantra three times:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ nī nī*

Through forming this mudrā and reciting this mantra, one attains the complete unobstructed wisdom of the Tathāgata.

Next, form the burnt-incense mudrā. With both palms facing up, extend the little, ring, and middle fingers upright with their backs touching. The index fingertips touch each other. Place each thumb alongside the index finger. Recite the mantra three times:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ gami*

Through forming this mudrā and reciting this mantra, one swiftly attains the Tathāgata's body with the thirty-two major marks and eighty minor characteristics.

Next, form the food-and-drink mudrā. Extend both palms open, with the right palm placed face up over the left palm. Recite the mantra three times:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ puraye*

Through forming this mudrā and reciting this mantra, one attains the food of dharma-joy and dhyāna-bliss.

Next, form the lamp mudrā. Make a fist with the left hand and place it at the waist. Also make a fist with the right hand, extending the middle finger straight upward. Recite the mantra three times:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ gṛ*

Through forming this mudrā and reciting this mantra, one swiftly attains the Tathāgata's pure five eyes.

Next, form the vajra añjali and recite the praise of Samantabhadra's vows, lauding and extolling the principal deity. Then form the earlier Buddhanetra mudrā and recite the vidyā to empower the five places.

Next, form the rosary-empowerment mudrā. Place the rosary within the joined palms and empower it seven times. The mantra is:

*Oṃ vairocana-mālā svāhā*

Next, grasp the rosary with the right hand and place it in the left palm, then place it within the joined palms. Recite the thousand-revolution mantra for empowerment seven times:

*Oṃ vajra-guhya-japa-samaye hūṃ*

The practitioner then enters the samādhi contemplation: close the eyes, direct the mind with single-pointed focus on one object, and contemplate the principal deity at neither too great nor too small a distance. With suspended thought and utmost sincerity, recite the root vidyā, neither hurried nor slow, neither loud nor soft, making each syllable of the mantra clear and distinct. Whether three sessions or four sessions daily, whether one hundred and eight or one thousand and eighty recitations as the limit. When the count is complete, perform the offering rite, and then escort the principal deity and all sacred beings back to their original palace. This is the supreme and profoundly secret dharma; do not carelessly transmit it. The mantra is:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ muḥ svāhā*

The above is what Vajrapāṇi spoke. What follows is the World-Honored One's seal of approval.

"Just so, Secret Lord! It is as you have declared. Furthermore, Secret Lord! When one speaks of the maṇḍala positions of the various sacred beings and the forms of the deities, know that it is likewise." This was first spoken by the former buddhas, and a later buddha further expanded its meaning.

As previously stated: when sacred beings are described as yellow in color, this is the vajra-body color, and they should be placed in the square maṇḍala of Indra's direction. When white, this is the color of pure-nature compassion, and they should be placed in the circular maṇḍala. When red, this is the color of fierce destruction of obstacles, and they should be placed in the triangular maṇḍala. When black, this is the color of the great wind of kalpa-destruction, and they should be placed in the half-moon maṇḍala. When blue, this is the color of indestructible, unconquerable space, and these should be wrathful vidyā-holding kings, placed either in the triangle or the half-moon.

As with color and maṇḍala, know that forms and expressions follow the same principle. When yellow, create a countenance of joyful aspiration and dharma-delight. When white, create a countenance of compassionate quiescence and meditation. When red, create a countenance of fierce vigor. When black, create a countenance of great power and bold dynamism. When blue, create a countenance of indestructible resilience. When yellow-white, it combines the countenances of increase and fierce authority. The rest may be inferred by analogy.

As for various mixed and variegated colors, one should simply examine and clarify each case according to the situation and understand its essential nature. Even what is not explicitly stated within the secret treasuries of the buddhas of the ten directions and three times may all employ this principle to generate corresponding activities. Thus the former buddhas are cited as witness that the dharma is naturally universal in application: "It is not that I alone, having appeared in the world, speak in this way."

Those who cultivate yoga should understand this intention and practice the path accordingly. After completing recitation, perform body-protection and leave the hall. Stamp the buddha-mudrā on buddha images and stūpas, and also practice the yoga of the principal deity. Then, move about freely as you wish.
