# 觀世音菩薩如意摩尼輪陀羅尼念誦法
## Method of Recitation for the Wish-Fulfilling Maṇi Wheel Dhāraṇī of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1084

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Ratnacinta (寶思惟).

---

The root spell says:

*Namo ratna-trayāya namaḥ ārya-avalokiteśvarāya bodhisattvāya mahāsattvāya mahā-kāruṇikāya tadyathā oṃ cakra-vartī cintā-maṇi mahā-padme ruru tiṣṭha jvala ākarṣāya hūṃ phaṭ svāhā*

If any good man or good woman recites and upholds this spell, chewing a willow twig at dawn each day before commencing recitation, and if one recites this spell to the full count of one hundred thousand times, the Holy Wish-Fulfilling Wheel Bodhisattva shall appear and grant whatever one wishes.

In general, this spell does not distinguish between lay and ordained, whether one drinks wine, eats meat, or has a spouse. Simply by reciting this spell, one will certainly attain accomplishment. The person who recites this spell need not perform any special ritual, need not consult astrological days, need not observe fasting, need not bathe, and need not wear special garments. By simply reading and reciting, all is accomplished, as extensively described in the sūtras.

The root mudrā: first join the palms, bend the two middle fingers slightly, touch the tips of the two index fingers together, and extend both thumbs upright side by side at the heart. Recite seven times, then release above the crown. If one wishes to summon, move the two thumbs back and forth. By the power of this spell and mudrā-seal, one's own body becomes like the principal deity in majestic spiritual power, and even the great and powerful māras cannot prevail.

Next, the heart-spell says:

*Oṃ padma cintā-maṇi jvala hūṃ*

Next, the following-heart spell says:

*Oṃ varada padme hūṃ*

Next, place the right hand, palm down, over the left hand. Press the tip of the right little finger upon the tip of the left thumb, and press the tip of the right thumb upon the tip of the left little finger. The spell says:

*Oṃ ārya-avalokiteśvarāya bodhisattvāya mahāsattvāya mahā-kāruṇikāya tadyathā oṃ cakari cakari mahā-cakari cakari thākari cakari dhāra svāhā*

With the mudrā, recite this spell three times, then release above the crown.

The Hayagrīva Avalokiteśvara body-protection and boundary-sealing method, mudrā and spell: from the middle fingers of both hands downward, cross the three fingers outward, each pressing against the back of the other hand in a palm-joining position. Extend both index fingers upright about five fen apart, and join both thumbs touching, each bent at one joint without touching the index finger. Move the tips back and forth. The spell says:

*Oṃ praveśa-siddha vajra-śara-ji svāhā*

The Hayagrīva great dharma-body mudrā: cross the three fingers from the index fingers down outward, with fingertips each turned against the back of the other hand in a palm-joining position. Extend both little fingers upright joined together, and join both thumbs touching. Bend the wrathful thumbs and move them back and forth. The spell says:

*Oṃ dhuna dhuna mātha mātha khadha khadha khadha hayagrīva hūṃ svāhā*

The Hayagrīva dharma-body mudrā: cross the four fingers from the index fingers down outward, with tips against the back of the other hand in a palm-joining position. Join both thumbs touching, each bent at one joint without touching the index finger. Move the thumbs back and forth. The spell says:

*Oṃ amṛtodbhava hūṃ svāhā*

Next, the boundary-sealing Hayagrīva Avalokiteśvara mudrā for avoidance and boundary-sealing: join both palms. Bend both index fingers and both ring fingers into the palms, each back to back. Join both thumbs slightly bent without touching the index fingers. Then recite the Hayagrīva Vidyā-King spell:

*Oṃ amṛtodbhava hūṃ phaṭ svāhā*

Recite three times. Then rotate the mudrā leftward three circles: this dispels all māras, who retreat and scatter of their own accord. Rotate the mudrā rightward three circles: this establishes the firm great boundary.

The above methods of body-protection also use the Bhṛkuṭī mudrā for the earth-binding.

## Method for the Bhṛkuṭī Earth-Binding of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva

Cross the middle fingers and lower fingers of both hands, reversed in the palms, right over left. The finger-backs press against the palms. Extend both thumbs upright side by side, touching at the tips. Bend both index fingers with their nails back to back. Press both thumbs against the ground. The binding spell:

*Oṃ bhayita jñi hūṃ*

For those of superior, middling, and inferior capacity, the recitation method is listed in full in the sūtras. This method does not go beyond the method of recitation as described.

By reciting this spell, the wise attain great peace and happiness. Wealth and food will certainly be accomplished, and riches, honors, and material goods will be abundantly fulfilled. All the buddhas of the three ages, when appearing in the world, have spoken this spell. This spell is secret and must not be recklessly disclosed; if revealed at the wrong time, the verified results will all scatter and disappear. Therefore it must not be carelessly transmitted; only an ācārya may receive it. If transmitted to an improper person, both the giver and receiver will fall into the three evil paths. Without concern for purity or impurity, the recitation is as listed above.

If one recites while in the embrace of a woman, by simply reading and reciting one will certainly achieve great verified results. At the time of recitation, one should always contemplate the form of the Holy Wish-Fulfilling Wheel Bodhisattva and forever take it as one's refuge. Whether male or female, by fixing the mind and reciting this spell, one should recite during the last watch of the night or at dawn before eating, whether a thousand times or one hundred and eight times, as many or as few as one wishes, neither increasing nor decreasing.

When one has recited thirteen thousand times, one will see all the buddhas and bodhisattvas and the Great Compassionate One, the principal deity. This spell is the most honored and supreme among all spells. Reciting it even once produces immeasurable merit; how much more so reciting it daily, whose merit is beyond conception. If one completes one hundred thousand times, all worldly and supramundane goals are accomplished. In this present life, whatever wealth, treasures, grain, rice, and fruits one seeks shall be accomplished as desired.

If one wishes all people to love and cherish one, this too can be accomplished. By reciting one thousand times each day, in this present life, in this fleshly body, one enters the first bhūmi and achieves penetrating understanding. One swiftly encounters the tathāgatas and quickly obtains the prediction of samyak-saṃbodhi.

## Method for Painting the Image

If any beings recite this spell, they should paint an image. In the pigments, one must not use animal-skin glue; use only frankincense juice as the binder. The painter should observe the eight fasting precepts. The bodhisattva's form should be depicted in the contemplative posture, with six arms. The upper left hand holds a golden wheel, the middle hand holds a lotus, and the lower hand presses upon a mountain. The right hand forms the contemplative gesture, the middle hand holds a wish-fulfilling jewel, and the lower hand holds a rosary. The right foot rests upon a lotus of thirty-two petals. Upon the crown is a transformation-buddha, with marks and excellences perfectly complete, riding upon a moon-disc, with majestic radiance shining like the light within the moon. On the right side, paint the image of the Hayagrīva Wrathful Great Vidyā-King, with blazing majestic radiance and perfect marks, with upper fangs hanging down. The image described above may be painted at any size one wishes.

## Ritual for Recitation of the Wish-Fulfilling Maṇi Wheel Dhāraṇī of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva

The dharmas of the various deities follow both the practice of the two realms. The practitioner's mind alone is central. In the Vajra-realm, the five aspects complete the body. The five places where "I become the Lotus Body" is the Wish-Fulfilling Wheel. Although this selects one class among the five divisions, the phrase "Lotus Body" has its specific meaning. In the ritual manual of Ācārya Vajrabodhi for the Wish-Fulfilling Wheel, one follows the Vajra-realm, and therefore the practitioner should face west. Even when following the Vajra-realm, one should not perform the great ritual. For such a separate practice, one should cultivate the samādhi method. When following the Womb-realm, one cultivates the method of following practice, but alone in a separate place. For such a practitioner, one uses the Vajra-realm invocation, the five repentances, and follows the Eighteen-Path method. The Eighteen Paths constitute the manual of wondrous accomplishment. Those who do not know the inner details and worldly people who disparage it are most laughable. The ritual manual of Ācārya Amoghavajra follows the Eighteen Paths. At the place of the principal deity's mudrā, for all dharma-practices one first forms the mudrā of Mahāvairocana and recites the five-syllable mantra. Then one forms the mudrā of the particular principal deity. This means one first abides in Mahāvairocana's dharma-realm palace, and according to the patron's desire, gives rise to the great compassion vow. Upon leaving the hall, the principal deity's transformation body accomplishes the patron's heart-wish. This is the great secret oral transmission. During the fire offering, one uses the heart-spell:

*Oṃ namaḥ cintā-maṇi jvala hūṃ caṃ kamala*

The face of the king's principal deity: for all buddhas, bodhisattvas, and vajra-beings, one may follow the method of pacification and similar rites. But for this particular practice, one should accept it and face west for practice. Previous masters have mostly practiced following this direction.
