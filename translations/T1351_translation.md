# 佛說持句神呪經
## The Buddha's Teaching: Sūtra of the Sentence-Holding Divine Mantra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1351

Translated from the Chinese. Wu dynasty, translated by Zhi Qian (支謙).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was in the country of Śrāvastī, in the Jeta Grove at Anāthapiṇḍada's Park, together with a great assembly of twelve hundred and fifty bhikṣus and ten thousand bodhisattvas. At that time, beyond hundreds of thousands of koṭis of buddha-lands from this buddha-realm, there is a world called Innumerable Flowers. Its buddha is called Lamp-Exalted-King Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyak-saṃbuddha, who is presently teaching the Dharma. That buddha sent two bodhisattvas, one named Immeasurable Light and the other Great Light. The two bodhisattvas came to where Śākyamuni Buddha was, bowed their heads at the Buddha's feet, stood to one side, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! Beyond hundreds of thousands of koṭis of buddha-lands from this buddha-realm, there is a world called Innumerable Flowers. Its buddha, Lamp-Exalted-King Tathāgata, has sent us here. Is the World-Honored One in good health as always? Is your assembly at ease, without troubles? Are there devas, nāgas, ghosts, spirits, hungry ghosts, female ghosts, kumbhāṇḍas, humans and non-humans, tigers, wolves, or venomous beasts causing disturbance to the people? For this reason the Buddha has sent this sentence-holding mantra, out of compassion for sentient beings, to bring them peace and good fortune. Its renown, majestic spiritual power, and efficacy are as follows:

*Jāri mahā-jāri rāṇi ukha mukha sa-pa-ti mahā-sa-pa-ti.*

The Buddha addressed Ānanda: "Receive this sentence-holding mantra. Uphold, recite, and proclaim it. It is difficult to encounter a buddha in the world, and the sentence-holding mantra is likewise difficult to encounter. If good men and good women receive this sentence-holding mantra, chant, recite, and study it, they will know the past lives of seven lifetimes. Those who uphold it will find that all bhūta-ghosts and spirits, whether human or non-human, cannot approach to cause harm. Venomous snakes will not dare to bite, poisons will naturally be neutralized, blades will be unable to wound, kings will be unable to cause injury, and even Brahmā will not be angered by them. This sentence-holding mantra has been spoken by seventy-seven koṭis of buddhas. Those who transgress against this mantra will incur grave sin."

Furthermore, eighty bodhisattvas including Maitreya addressed the Venerable Ānanda: "We too shall teach a sentence-holding mantra, out of compassion for sentient beings, to bring them peace and good fortune. Its renown, majestic spiritual power, and efficacy are as follows:

*A-ji ha-ji ṭa khā rā rā rī mi-hi-ri mi-ri ti-ru-ru Maitreya.*

The Buddha addressed Ānanda: "Receive this sentence-holding mantra. Uphold, recite, and proclaim it. It is difficult to encounter a buddha in the world. If one chants and recites this sentence-holding mantra, one will know the past lives of fourteen lifetimes. Those who uphold it will find that all bhūta-ghosts and spirits, whether human or non-human, cannot approach to cause harm. Venomous snakes will not dare to bite, poisons will naturally be neutralized, blades will be unable to wound, kings will not dare to cause injury, and even Brahmā will not be angered by them. This sentence-holding mantra has been spoken by eighty-four koṭis of buddhas. Those who slander this mantra will incur grave sin."

The Buddha addressed Ānanda: "I too shall teach a sentence-holding mantra, out of compassion for sentient beings, to bring them peace and good fortune. Its renown, majestic spiritual power, and efficacy are as follows:

*A-ji ha-ji nā-ji ku-nā-ji ti-rī śā-rā pa-ti a-nā-pa-ti pa-nā-ti pa-nā-ka-ha-ni mahā-ka-ha-ni.*

The Buddha addressed Ānanda: "Receive this sentence-holding mantra. Uphold, recite, and proclaim it. It is difficult to encounter a buddha in the world, and the sentence-holding mantra is likewise difficult to encounter. If good men and good women receive this sentence-holding mantra, chant, recite, and study it, they will know the past lives of innumerable lifetimes. Those who uphold it will find that all bhūta-ghosts and spirits, whether human or non-human, will not dare to approach to cause harm. Venomous snakes will not dare to bite, poisons will naturally be neutralized, blades will not dare to wound, kings will not dare to cause injury, and even Brahmā will not be angered by them. This sentence-holding mantra has been spoken by innumerable koṭis of buddhas. Those who transgress against this mantra will incur grave sin."

The Buddha addressed Ānanda: "This sentence-holding mantra should be recited when traveling on the road. Whether one encounters officials, or finds oneself among bandits, or is threatened by sorcery, poison, bladed weapons, humans or non-humans, one should recite it. This sentence-holding mantra can cause a withered tree to sprout leaves, flowers, and fruit. How much more so when one ties the cord for a person's sake! It shall bring auspiciousness. All the hundred diseases will be eliminated. There will naturally be peace and security, and all calamities and harm will be averted. Homage to the Buddha! May this mantra grant the fulfillment of all wishes."

When the Buddha had spoken this sūtra, all rejoiced greatly.
