# 佛說信解智力經
## Sūtra on the Power of Faithful Understanding, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0802

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmabhadra (法賢)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was in the land of Śrāvastī, in the Jeta Grove of Anāthapiṇḍada, together with a great assembly of bhikṣus.

At that time the Buddha addressed the bhikṣus: "Bhikṣus! You should know that the dharma of the power of faithful understanding can realize the truth of reality. It is the Tathāgata's dharma of fearlessness, which only a Buddha can know. Bhikṣus, there may be śrāvakas who say: 'I truly understand this dharma of the power of faithful understanding. I know it as it truly is. My diligence is not in vain, and I have left behind all defilements.' They may further say: 'I can skillfully teach. I skillfully tame and subdue. I should teach the Dharma; now is the right time. This is the most supreme. Let others rely upon it. Such is the truth; one should study it and one should diligently practice it.' They may further say: 'One should know it thus: this dharma is the most honored and supreme, without equal. One should see it thus, hear it thus, perceive it thus, and know it thus.'"

The Buddha told the bhikṣus: "You should know that any śrāvakas who speak thus speak falsely. They have not seen it thus, not heard it thus, not perceived it thus, not known it thus. Why? This dharma is the true principle of all the Buddhas. Only a Buddha can understand it; it is not a dharma shared with the śrāvakas."

The Buddha told the bhikṣus: "The dharma of faithful understanding consists of the Tathāgata's five powers and ten powers. What are the five? First, the power of faith; second, the power of diligence; third, the power of mindfulness; fourth, the power of concentration; fifth, the power of wisdom. The dharma of faithful understanding is complete with these five powers. Furthermore, the Tathāgata, Worthy of Offerings, Perfectly Awakened One, is complete with the Tathāgata's ten powers. By these he is able to know the unsurpassed state of the Buddha, and in the great assembly he makes the lion's roar and skillfully turns the wondrous Dharma-wheel for sentient beings. What are the Tathāgata's ten powers?

"The Tathāgata truly knows what is possible and what is impossible. Because the Tathāgata truly knows what is possible and what is impossible, this is the Tathāgata's first power of wisdom. With this power of wisdom, the Tathāgata, Worthy of Offerings, Perfectly Awakened One, being complete in this power, is able to know the unsurpassed state of the Buddha, makes the lion's roar in the great assembly, and skillfully turns the wondrous Dharma-wheel for sentient beings.

"Furthermore, the Tathāgata's power of wisdom truly knows the karmic actions, causes, conditions, and retributions of all dharmas of the past, present, and future. Having thus truly known the karmic actions, causes, conditions, and retributions of all dharmas of the past, present, and future, this is the Tathāgata's second power of wisdom. With this power of wisdom, the Tathāgata, Worthy of Offerings, Perfectly Awakened One, being complete in this power, is able to know the unsurpassed state of the Buddha, makes the lion's roar in the great assembly, and skillfully turns the wondrous Dharma-wheel for sentient beings.

"Furthermore, the Tathāgata truly knows the dhyāna concentrations, liberations, samādhis, and samāpattis, and even the afflictions, karmas, and sufferings, all of which are entirely purified. Having thus truly known the dhyāna concentrations, liberations, samādhis, and samāpattis, and even the afflictions, karmas, and sufferings, all entirely purified, this is the Tathāgata's third power of wisdom. With this power of wisdom, the Tathāgata, Worthy of Offerings, Perfectly Awakened One, being complete in this power, is able to know the unsurpassed state of the Buddha, makes the lion's roar in the great assembly, and skillfully turns the wondrous Dharma-wheel for sentient beings.

"Furthermore, the Tathāgata truly knows the various faculties and dispositions of all sentient beings. Having thus truly known the various faculties and dispositions of all sentient beings, this is the Tathāgata's fourth power of wisdom. With this power of wisdom, the Tathāgata, Worthy of Offerings, Perfectly Awakened One, being complete in this power, is able to know the unsurpassed state of the Buddha, makes the lion's roar in the great assembly, and skillfully turns the wondrous Dharma-wheel for sentient beings.

"Furthermore, the Tathāgata truly knows the various kinds of faithful understanding of all sentient beings. Having thus truly known the various kinds of faithful understanding of all sentient beings, this is the Tathāgata's fifth power of wisdom. With this power of wisdom, the Tathāgata, Worthy of Offerings, Perfectly Awakened One, being complete in this power, is able to know the unsurpassed state of the Buddha, makes the lion's roar in the great assembly, and skillfully turns the wondrous Dharma-wheel for sentient beings.

"Furthermore, the Tathāgata truly knows the various realms and destinies. Having thus truly known the various realms and destinies, this is the Tathāgata's sixth power of wisdom. With this power of wisdom, the Tathāgata, Worthy of Offerings, Perfectly Awakened One, being complete in this power, is able to know the unsurpassed state of the Buddha, makes the lion's roar in the great assembly, and skillfully turns the wondrous Dharma-wheel for sentient beings.

"Furthermore, the Tathāgata truly knows the nature of the desires and inclinations of all sentient beings. Having thus truly known the nature of the desires and inclinations of all sentient beings, this is the Tathāgata's seventh power of wisdom. With this power of wisdom, the Tathāgata, Worthy of Offerings, Perfectly Awakened One, being complete in this power, is able to know the unsurpassed state of the Buddha, makes the lion's roar in the great assembly, and skillfully turns the wondrous Dharma-wheel for sentient beings.

"Furthermore, the Tathāgata can recall the dharmas of boundless past actions, namely one life, ten lives, a hundred lives, a thousand lives, a hundred thousand lives, and even countless, boundless increasing and decreasing kalpas. Through such numbers of lives and kalpas, the names, clans, food, sufferings, joys, lifespans, and their lengths of all sentient beings are known, whether they dwelt for long or short times, whether they died here and were born there, or died there and were born here, with all their characteristics and functions. The boundless past events, countless and limitless, can all be recalled. Having thus truly known the dharmas of the boundless past actions, this is the Tathāgata's eighth power of wisdom. With this power of wisdom, the Tathāgata, Worthy of Offerings, Perfectly Awakened One, being complete in this power, is able to know the unsurpassed state of the Buddha, makes the lion's roar in the great assembly, and skillfully turns the wondrous Dharma-wheel for sentient beings.

"Furthermore, the Tathāgata, with the pure heavenly eye surpassing the fleshly eye, observes all sentient beings in the world: their births and deaths, their beauty and ugliness, their nobility and lowliness, their destinies in good and evil paths, and the karma they have created. Even the sentient beings who, with body, speech, and mind, commit unwholesome deeds, slander the worthy and holy, and hold wrong views, performing unwholesome deeds and practicing unwholesome dharmas: all of this arises from wrong causes. Because of these wrong causes and conditions, after death they fall into the evil destinies and suffer the torments of hell. Furthermore, sentient beings who, with body, speech, and mind, practice the ten wholesome deeds, who do not slander the worthy and holy, who give rise to right views, perform wholesome deeds, and practice wholesome dharmas: all of this arises from right causes. Because of these right-view causes and conditions, after death they are born in the heavenly realms as devas. All such dharmas are truly known. Having thus truly known with the pure heavenly eye surpassing the fleshly eye, observing all sentient beings in the world, their births and deaths, their beauty and ugliness, their nobility and lowliness, their destinies in good and evil paths, and the karma they have created: this is the Tathāgata's ninth power of wisdom. With this power of wisdom, the Tathāgata, Worthy of Offerings, Perfectly Awakened One, being complete in this power, is able to know the unsurpassed state of the Buddha, makes the lion's roar in the great assembly, and skillfully turns the wondrous Dharma-wheel for sentient beings.

"Furthermore, the Tathāgata has exhausted all outflows. Without outflows, liberated; through wisdom, liberated. By his own supernatural powers he comprehends all dharmas: my births are exhausted, the holy life has been established, what was to be done has been done, and I will not receive any further existence. All such things are truly known. Having thus truly known the exhaustion of all outflows, this is the Tathāgata's tenth power of wisdom. With this power of wisdom, the Tathāgata, Worthy of Offerings, Perfectly Awakened One, being complete in this power, is able to know the unsurpassed state of the Buddha, makes the lion's roar in the great assembly, and skillfully turns the wondrous Dharma-wheel for sentient beings."

At that time the Buddha told the bhikṣus: "Regarding the Tathāgata's power of knowing what is possible and what is impossible, if someone asks: 'How does the Tathāgata's power of wisdom see, hear, perceive, and know regarding what is possible and what is impossible, even unto attaining perfect enlightenment?' When asked thus, one should answer truthfully.

"Furthermore: 'How does the Tathāgata's power of knowing all sentient beings' own karma see, hear, perceive, and know by means of his power of karmic wisdom, even unto attaining perfect enlightenment?' When asked, one should answer truthfully.

"Furthermore: 'How does the Tathāgata's power of knowing the dhyāna concentrations, liberations, samādhis, and samāpattis see, hear, perceive, and know by means of his power of concentration-wisdom, even unto attaining perfect enlightenment?' When asked, one should answer truthfully.

"Furthermore: 'How does the Tathāgata's power of knowing all sentient beings' various faculties see, hear, perceive, and know by means of his power of faculty-wisdom, even unto attaining perfect enlightenment?' When asked, one should answer truthfully.

"Furthermore: 'How does the Tathāgata's power of knowing all sentient beings' various kinds of faithful understanding see, hear, perceive, and know by means of his power of faithful-understanding-wisdom, even unto attaining perfect enlightenment?' When asked, one should answer truthfully.

"Furthermore: 'How does the Tathāgata's power of knowing all sentient beings' various realms see, hear, perceive, and know by means of his power of realm-wisdom, even unto attaining perfect enlightenment?' When asked, one should answer truthfully.

"Furthermore: 'How does the Tathāgata's power of knowing all sentient beings' desires and inclinations see, hear, perceive, and know by means of his power of desire-wisdom, even unto attaining perfect enlightenment?' When asked, one should answer truthfully.

"Furthermore: 'How does the Tathāgata's power of recollecting past lives see, hear, perceive, and know by means of his power of past-recollection-wisdom, even unto attaining perfect enlightenment?' When asked, one should answer truthfully.

"Furthermore: 'How does the Tathāgata's power of knowing the births and deaths of all sentient beings see, hear, perceive, and know by means of his heavenly-eye-wisdom, even unto attaining perfect enlightenment?' When asked, one should answer truthfully.

"Furthermore: 'How does the Tathāgata's power of knowing the exhaustion of outflows see, hear, perceive, and know by means of his outflow-exhaustion-wisdom, even unto attaining perfect enlightenment?' When asked, one should answer truthfully."

He further told the bhikṣus: "This power of knowing what is possible and what is impossible I teach only to those who are in right concentration, not to those who are not in right concentration. This power of karmic wisdom I teach only to those who are in right concentration, not to those who are not in right concentration. This power of knowing the dhyāna concentrations, liberations, samādhis, and samāpattis I teach only to those who are in right concentration, not to those who are not in right concentration. This power of knowing all sentient beings' various faculties I teach only to those who are in right concentration, not to those who are not in right concentration. This power of knowing all sentient beings' various kinds of faithful understanding I teach only to those who are in right concentration, not to those who are not in right concentration. This power of knowing all sentient beings' various realms I teach only to those who are in right concentration, not to those who are not in right concentration. This power of knowing all sentient beings' desires and inclinations I teach only to those who are in right concentration, not to those who are not in right concentration. This power of recollecting past lives I teach only to those who are in right concentration, not to those who are not in right concentration. This power of the heavenly eye I teach only to those who are in right concentration, not to those who are not in right concentration. This power of knowing the exhaustion of outflows I teach only to those who are in right concentration, not to those who are not in right concentration."

The Buddha told the bhikṣus: "Right concentration is the correct path; not being in right concentration is the wrong path. Therefore, all of you should know this."

When the Buddha had finished teaching this sūtra, the bhikṣus, having heard what the Buddha taught, believed, received it, and practiced accordingly.
