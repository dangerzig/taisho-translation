# 金剛頂瑜伽三十七尊出生義
## The Meaning of the Production of the Thirty-Seven Deities in the Vajraśekhara Yoga

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0872

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空)

---

Our Capable-Sage Tathāgata, out of compassion for the delusion of the three realms and six destinies, saw that beings, always burdened by the aggregates, elements, and sense-fields, undergo the false clinging of birth-and-death. Flowers in the sky are without substance yet vainly conceived; the jewel in the robe exists yet remains unknown. Thereupon he withdrew his traces to the Tuṣita heavenly palace, descended to be born in central India, erected a conjured city to receive them, and enticed them through the removal of dung. When great-family persons had ripened their Dharma-affinity and the time arrived for the teaching of the three secrets, he then withdrew to the self-enjoyment body and, taking his seat in the Akaniṣṭha heavenly palace, entered the samādhi of the Non-Empty King. He universally gathered all the holy and worthy ones, removed the graduated stages of the grounds, and opened the sudden teaching of the equal and wondrous. From the Samantabhadra vajra-nature-ocean, he brought forth dust-mote numbers of empowered form-bodies. Then he expounded the secret mantra of Samantabhadra's vajra speech-karma, revealed the secret mudrā of Samantabhadra's vajra body-karma, disclosed the intent-wisdom of Samantabhadra's vajra mind-karma, and perfected the gateway of deliverance through the vajra three karmas of sentient beings. Those who do not understand take the movements of the limbs for mere play, and the recitation of syllable-bodies for yet more attachment. How could they know that entering here and emerging there, employing the shallow to accomplish the deep, is also like Vajrapāṇi, who has barely mounted the lion and suddenly stamps his royal foot: pressing upon the hills, all is already leveled? This is the source-stream of the inconceivable, which is not even within the domain of the Three Worthies or Four Fruits. How much less can ordinary sentiments fathom it? Therefore, one who attains this enters right concentration through the five sense-faculties; illuminates great emptiness amid the ten thousand phenomena; leads forth the Buddha-realm to universally purify all sentient beings; and gathers all beings to converge upon the One Wisdom.

Thus, the practitioner first abides in semblance, and then receives the power of empowerment. One whose defilements are thin gradually perceives the Dharma-light and attains a share of samādhi. One who enters deeply comprehends both emptiness and form together, and then possesses the universally-pure body. When habitual tendencies and covering obstructions are utterly and completely removed, the source of quiescent illumination and the function of all activity are both accomplished. The Dharma-King is sovereign, and benefit is equally bestowed. Then one knows that all the honored ones of right awakening have been eternally present from the beginning, and the great assembly of bodhi has always been united. Non-Buddhists are barred by the obstruction of self-clinging; the Two Vehicles are stuck in the realization of emptiness; those of near-sighted disposition err through grasping and rejecting; those of shallow wisdom are confused by existence and non-existence. For this reason they break their own boats and bridges and cannot arrive. As for directly realizing the Samantabhadra-mind and deeply entering the perfectly bright wisdom, this is the great method by which the mantra-practicing bodhisattva undertakes the yoga. Only then can one solidify the bodhi of the mind, adorn the marks of the mind, open and reveal the Dharma-treasury of the mind, accomplish the supernatural powers of the mind, and bring to quiescent cessation the conceptual elaboration of the mind. From this, one activates clear knowing and seeing, accomplishes sentient beings, abides at the gate of correspondence, and performs all the Buddha's works.

Therefore, from the Great Perfect Mirror Wisdom there arises the vajra-equality perfectly-awakened body, which is in the east of the stūpa: the Tathāgata Akṣobhya. From the Equality-Nature Wisdom there arises the meaning-equality perfectly-awakened body, which is in the south of the stūpa: the Tathāgata Ratnasambhava. From the Wondrous Contemplation Wisdom there arises the Dharma-equality perfectly-awakened body, which is in the west of the stūpa: the Tathāgata Amitābha. From the Accomplishment-of-Action Wisdom there arises the karma-equality perfectly-awakened body, which is in the north of the stūpa: the Tathāgata Amoghasiddhi. From the wisdoms of the four Tathāgatas, the four pāramitā bodhisattvas are produced: they are the mothers who give birth to, nurture, and rear all the holy and worthy ones of the three times. Thus, the stamp of the Dharmadhātu-Nature Wisdom self-enjoyment body is in the exact center of the stūpa: the Tathāgata Vairocana.

The four close-attendant bodhisattvas are the seals of those four pāramitās. Immeasurable great compassion is born from this; immeasurable skillful means of protection emerge from this. From the firm vajra-body of all the Tathāgatas' bodhi, Vajrasattva is born. From the fourfold-gathering body of all the Tathāgatas' bodhi, Vajrarāja is born. From the unstained, pure body of all the Tathāgatas' bodhi, Vajrarāga is born. From the body of praise uttered by all the Tathāgatas, Vajrasādhu is born. These are the four close-attendant bodhisattvas of the Tathāgata Akṣobhya in the eastern Vajra-Majesty Adorned Realm. They are accomplished through the great precept and patience pāramitā of all the Tathāgatas.

From the great adornment-meaning of all the Tathāgatas, Vajraratna is born. From the great radiant-glory meaning of all the Tathāgatas, Vajrasūrya is born. From the great wish-fulfilling meaning of all the Tathāgatas, Vajraketu is born. From the great joyful meaning of all the Tathāgatas, Vajrahāsa is born. These are the four close-attendant bodhisattvas of the Tathāgata Ratnasambhava in the southern Jewel-Radiance Merit Realm. They are accomplished through the non-abiding dāna pāramitā of all the Tathāgatas.

From the sovereign, unstained wisdom of all the Tathāgatas, Vajradharma is born. From the wisdom of all the Tathāgatas that forever severs habitual tendencies, Vajratīkṣṇa is born. From the wisdom of all the Tathāgatas that turns the great Dharma-wheel, Vajrahetu is born. From the wisdom of all the Tathāgatas that transcends speech and conceptual elaboration, Vajrabhāṣa is born. These are the four close-attendant bodhisattvas of the Tathāgata Amitāyus in the western Great Lotus Dharma-Treasury Realm. They are accomplished through the samādhi and great prajñā pāramitā of all the Tathāgatas.

From the gate of skillful arts and crafts of all the Tathāgatas, Vajrakarma is born. From the gate of the great compassion-armor of all the Tathāgatas, Vajrarakṣa is born. From the gate of fearless subjugation of all the Tathāgatas, Vajrayakṣa is born. From the gate of abiding and sustaining accomplishment of all the Tathāgatas, Vajramuṣṭi is born. These are the four close-attendant bodhisattvas of the Tathāgata Amoghasiddhi in the northern Transformation-Wheel Action Realm. They are accomplished through the great vigor pāramitā of all the Tathāgatas, who never abandon sentient beings.

What these sixteen great beings hold in their hands are all the banners of their root samādhis. Seeing the objects and seeking their meaning, how far can it be?

As for entering the gate of the six pāramitās in conformity with the realm of sentient beings: from the four wisdoms within the nature-ocean of all the Tathāgatas' essential body, the four gathering bodhisattvas are born, namely Vajrāṅkuśa, Vajrapāśa, Vajrasphoṭa, and Vajraghaṇṭā. They can summon, draw in, firmly retain, and bring joy. At all places of practice they serve all teachings and commands. Humans and devas obtain them and assemble the multitude of liberation; holy and worthy ones employ them and receive the deluded and wayward. They occupy the positions outside the four gates of the stūpa, wielding their functions. They arise from the wisdoms of the four bodhisattvas. These holy ones cannot rest idle in their own palatial abodes; as swiftly as overturning the palm, they respond to the requests of all quarters. If the mantra-practitioner can enter this samādhi, then one can produce this cloud-ocean of offerings and accomplish the practice of benefiting both self and others. This is the great meaning of the thirty-seven deities of the central assembly. Furthermore, one abides in the crown-born samādhi and manifests the crown-born body.

Now, the reason the upper level of the stūpa has only the assembly of the Five Wheel Kings is that all the crown-born bodies are gathered into this unsurpassed fivefold crown-wisdom. As for that which no direction can ultimately reach: it is the crown-mark of the Buddha. So too this supremely victorious Dharma cannot be ultimately fathomed; therefore it is called "crown." The five crown-kings are also the sovereign rulers over all the mantra-deities; therefore they are called "kings." Among the five crown-wheels, the golden wheel is supreme. Were it not so, who would know that the supremely transcendent is but one Dharma? Therefore, from the Bodhisattva Avalokiteśvara on down, all take frightened refuge.

Furthermore, the lower level has sixteen vajra-guardian deities, who are all transformations born from the courageous bodhicitta of all the Tathāgatas. This also reveals that when the Tathāgata was practicing, there were dust-mote numbers of mental obstructions and afflictions, which were shattered by this vajra-wisdom. After the great awakening, they became dust-mote varieties of wisdom-gates, employed through this vajra-wisdom. Therefore they again manifest wrathful, terrifying bodies, wielding the wisdom of great might, to subjugate those difficult to tame. Their shouts cause the great trichiliocosm to tremble and shake; their glances cause the hosts of māra to cower and flee. The demon-mother hides in fear and withdraws her traces; the elephant-headed one dreads their authority and retreats far away. Even the great lord of delusion, Maheśvara, also receives their affliction and thereby achieves right awakening. From this we know that the anger of that time was precisely great compassion. These vajra-guardians are as numerous as river-sands and dust-motes; now sixteen are selected to represent all, and the meaning of dust-mote numbers does not exceed these.

As for the remaining great beings, devas, and humans, they are all bodies manifested according to kind, serving as steps and boats for the mountains of heterodoxy and seas of suffering. They also emerge from the gate of Mahāvairocana Tathāgata's skillful karma-function. Therefore this stūpa may be called the comprehensive leader of the secret purport of the One Vehicle. How much more so that the paths of the provisional and the real are fully present within it! As for universally manifesting form-bodies and the hundreds of thousands of samādhis, the four immeasurable minds, the beneficial skillful means of the six pāramitās and their progressive sequence, and even sitting without rising while traveling through all the Buddha-lands, making offerings, serving, and bringing joy and benefit to sentient beings, imperceptibly perfuming and secretly transforming the realm of sentient beings through inconceivable means: regarding such principles and functions, other sūtras may have only the names but lack the actual methods. As for the practices, procedures, and ritual guides, all are fully provided in this teaching. Since it surpasses all the Great Vehicle, the transmission and reception are difficult. Even when the transmitting ācārya has selected a suitable vessel, one must first confer the bodhisattva-nature precepts, lead the candidate into the great assembly Dharma-altar, gather the holy and worthy ones of the Vajradhātu, hold fast the amṛta abhiṣeka of the vajra-vehicle, and only then reveal the threshold of entering the Buddha's mind. If this is not done, then the one who receives and practices gains no benefit, and the one who transmits incurs transgression.

Therefore, from the Buddha onward, each has in succession entrusted the next: "Śākya-siṃha received it from the Tathāgata Vairocana, and with a solemn vow transmitted it to Vajrasattva. Vajrasattva received it, and after several hundred years transmitted it to the Bodhisattva Nāgārjuna. The Bodhisattva Nāgārjuna received it, and after several hundred years transmitted it to the Ācārya Nāgabodhi, who also sustained it for several hundred years and transmitted it to the Ācārya Vajrabodhi. The Ācārya Vajrabodhi, by the power of his compassionate vows, wished to spread and expound it in China. Carrying his flask and staff, in the seventh year of the Kaiyuan era he arrived in the supreme capital. In the fourteenth year he found a suitable person and, with a solemn vow, transmitted it to the Ācārya Amoghavajra. After that, the branches and successors of the transmission are rather numerous, like cairns in succession. The lineage follows this pattern."

According to the original teaching, whoever receives this abhiṣeka has Vajrasattva perpetually abiding in their body and mind, guarding the mind-king as a bulwark and leading them to the Path. Though one has not yet realized entry, one is born from the Dharma, worthy of bearing the vajra-name, and already counted among the bodhisattvas. Those who come into contact with or behold such a person thereby plant the cause of beholding bodhi.
