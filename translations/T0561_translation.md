# 佛說老母經
## Sūtra of the Old Mother, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0561

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the land of Vaiśālī, at a place called Joyful Sound, together with eight hundred bhikṣus and ten thousand bodhisattvas.

At that time a poor old mother came to the Buddha's dwelling, placed her head on the ground, paid respects to the Buddha, and said: "I wish to ask a question."

The Buddha said: "Excellent! Excellent! You may ask."

The old mother said: "Where do birth, old age, sickness, and death come from, and where do they go? Where do form, sensation, perception, formations, and consciousness come from, and where do they go? Where do the eyes, ears, nose, tongue, body, and mind come from, and where do they go? Where do earth, water, fire, wind, and space come from, and where do they go?"

The Buddha said: "Birth, old age, sickness, and death come from nowhere and go nowhere. Form, sensation, perception, formations, and consciousness come from nowhere and go nowhere. The eyes, ears, nose, tongue, body, and mind come from nowhere and go nowhere. Earth, water, fire, wind, and space come from nowhere and go nowhere."

The Buddha said: "All dharmas are likewise. It is like two sticks rubbed together: fire comes forth and burns the sticks. When the sticks are consumed, the fire goes out."

The Buddha asked the old mother: "Where does this fire originally come from, and where does it go when it is extinguished?"

The old mother replied: "When conditions come together, fire arises. When conditions disperse, the fire goes out."

The Buddha said: "All dharmas are likewise. When conditions come together, they are formed. When conditions disperse, they cease. All dharmas come from nowhere and go nowhere. When the eye sees a pleasant form, that is the mind; the mind is form. Both are empty, arising from nothing, and their cessation is the same.

"All dharmas are like a drum: it is not made of one thing alone. There is a person who takes a mallet and strikes the drum, and the drum produces sound. The drum's sound is empty. The future sound is empty. The past sound is empty. The sound does not come from the wood, the hide, the mallet, or the person's hand. It is the combination of all these things that produces the drum's sound, and the sound arises from emptiness and returns to emptiness.

"All the ten thousand things are likewise. Self, person, and lifespan are all likewise. Their original ground is pure, with nothing whatsoever from which they arise and nothing whatsoever from which they are caused. Through conditions, dharmas are made, and dharmas too are nothing whatsoever.

"It is like clouds arising: when they darken and gather, rain falls. The rain does not come from the dragon's body, nor from the dragon's mind. It is all produced through the dragon's conditions, and thus rain comes about. All dharmas come from nowhere and go nowhere.

"It is like a painter who first prepares a board and canvas, then mixes and blends the various colors, and paints as he pleases. The painting does not come from the board, canvas, or colors. As the mind directs, whatever is intended is accomplished. Birth and death are the same: each of the various destinies is different. Naraka, the animal realm, the hungry ghost realm, the heavens, and the human world are all like this. If there is one who understands this wisdom, they do not cling. But if one clings, then it continues to exist."

The old mother, hearing the Buddha's words, was filled with great joy and said: "Through the grace of the God of Gods, I have obtained the Dharma-eye. Though my body is old and frail, today I have found peace."

Ānanda straightened his robes, came forward, and knelt. He said to the Buddha: "This old mother heard the Buddha's words and was immediately liberated. For what reason is her wisdom so great?"

The Buddha said: "Her virtue is vast and majestic; that is why she was immediately liberated. This old mother was my mother in a past life, when I first generated the bodhisattva resolve."

Ānanda said to the Buddha: "The Buddha's mother in a past life: what is the reason for her being so destitute and poor?"

The Buddha said: "Long ago, in the time of Krakucchanda Buddha, when I was on the bodhisattva path, I wished to become a śramaṇa. My mother, out of loving attachment, would not allow me to become a śramaṇa. I was sorrowful and did not eat for one day. Because of this, for five hundred lifetimes before and after, she has been born into the world and suffered hardship like this."

The Buddha said to Ānanda: "When this old mother's life ends, she shall be born in the land of Amitābha Buddha and make offerings to the Buddhas. After sixty-eight billion kalpas, she shall become a Buddha named Fúbōjiàn, in a land named Transformation. The clothing and food of the people shall be like those of the Trāyastriṃśa heaven. The people of that land shall all live for one kalpa."

When the Buddha had finished speaking the sūtra, the old mother and Ānanda, the bodhisattvas, bhikṣus, devas, nāgas, spirits, and asuras were all filled with great joy. They came forward, placed their heads on the ground, paid respects to the Buddha, and departed.
