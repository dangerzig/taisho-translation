# 大雲無想經卷第九
## Great Cloud Sūtra of Non-Conception, Fascicle Nine

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0388

Translated from the Chinese. Yao Qin dynasty, translated by Zhu Fonian (竺佛念)

---

...with a mind of pity, accepted us and the sins we previously committed. Our weighty transgressions were extinguished, and wholesome dharmas gradually arose. Immeasurable sentient beings attained rootless faith. Immeasurable sentient beings generated the aspiration for anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi.

At that time, in the assembly there was a brahmin named Straight Path, who rose from his seat, raised his hands in alarm, and declared: "Let the great assembly all know: the śramaṇa Gautama speaks great falsehoods! He previously always said: 'Those who commit the five heinous transgressions, slander the true Dharma, revile sages, misappropriate the property of the permanent saṃgha or the property of the Three Treasures, violate the four grave prohibitions, defile bhikṣus or bhikṣuṇīs, or hold wrong views: these ten kinds of people are seeds of hell. In the present they cannot sever the bonds of the desire realm, they cannot realize the fruit of the śramaṇa path, and they cannot advance the supreme true Dharma.' But now he says: 'Those with a mind of right faith who repent of their former deeds will have their transgressions extinguished, and they will again be able to advance the Tathāgata's true Dharma. Those without faith are called people of hell; those with right faith are called abiders in the Dharma.' How can Gautama make two kinds of statements? 'Those who believe my Dharma are named holders of right views; those who do not believe my Dharma are named holders of wrong views.' The śramaṇa Gautama does not consider his former words and yet speaks these words. How can one who says all such things be called Omniscient? The śramaṇa Gautama is not omniscient, is not all-seeing, yet claims to be all-seeing. The various non-Buddhist practitioners also say: 'I am omniscient! I am all-seeing! Those who believe my path have right views; those who believe in Gautama have wrong views.' Between these two statements, what is the difference? If there is no difference, how can one distinguish which is right and which is wrong?"

At that time the World-Honored One told the Bodhisattva Mahāsattva Great Cloud Secret Treasury: "Good man, listen carefully, listen carefully! All sentient beings are surrounded by the four inversions, and there are further four things they lack, namely: discipline, views, comportment, and right livelihood. Because of these eight dharmas, sentient beings walk in darkness and cannot distinguish the wrong from the right, the Dharma from the non-Dharma.

"Good man, this brahmin Straight Path stands in the great assembly, raises his hands in alarm, and says: 'The śramaṇa Gautama speaks false words.' These words of his are themselves false. Good man, all dharmas are false, and the Tathāgata is able to teach this. Therefore the Tathāgata does not speak falsely. All dharmas are without nature, without fixity, without substance, and without conditions. They cannot be definitively spoken of. They are empty, without arising or perishing. They are not called 'things.' They are neither pure nor impure. They are like a dream, like an illusion, like the moon in water, like a mirage, like an echo, like a city of gandharvas, like turtle hair, like rabbit horns. The substance of desire, anger, and delusion has no true reality. It arises from unwholesome thoughts and perceptions. What originally did not exist now exists; what exists returns to non-existence. Good man, all sentient beings do not know this characteristic. Therefore they proclaim: 'The śramaṇa Gautama speaks in two ways, falsely and deceitfully.'

"Although the Tathāgata speaks of ten kinds of beings, those who have faith can extinguish their many transgressions; those without faith will enter hell. Good man, if a person who has committed transgressions can contemplate the Dharma-characteristic in this way, that person is called one with faith and can extinguish many transgressions. If one is unable to contemplate in this way, one is called one without faith and is a person of hell.

"Good man, there is a dhāraṇī called Lion's Roar Supreme Stairway of Wisdom, Turning Back to the Acceptance of Non-Arising, Dharma-Gate of Boundless Spiritual Powers. If a bodhisattva-mahāsattva perfects this dhāraṇī, and in a great assembly recites a single verse, it can crush and extinguish all wrong views, destroy the inverted mind and its mental factors, dispel all doubts, and shatter the defilements of stinginess, greed, anger, and delusion.

"Good man, if there is a person who has committed immeasurable transgressions and, upon hearing this dhāraṇī, for seven days single-mindedly recollects the Buddha and does not think of any worldly affairs or afflictive bonds, that person will at once see all of the three realms, the three times, the aggregates, sense-bases, and elements. Just as a great wind, that person's mind at that time becomes like the wind, contemplating all dharmas, the three realms, the three times, the aggregates, sense-bases, and elements, without any attachment whatsoever, beyond expression. This person is called one who is not attached to the aggregates, sense-bases, and elements of the three realms. At the time of such contemplation, within the three realms, nothing is named pure or impure, nothing is named liberated or bound, nothing is named 'this' or 'that,' nothing is named ordinary being or sage, nothing is named going or staying. This person will never be deluded by the dharmas of ordinary beings. Having severed all conceptualizations and inverted views and inverted mind, one sees the five sensual pleasures as like wind, like space. Although in terms of worldly convention one speaks of five desires, inwardly the mind has no attachment whatsoever. Without arising and without perishing, one is able to sever and remove all evil dharmas, as previously described regarding the ten kinds of beings.

"Good man, this brahmin, because he does not understand, proclaims: 'The śramaṇa Gautama speaks in two ways, falsely and deceitfully.' Good man, if a person can contemplate the Dharma-characteristic in this way, that person attains the acceptance of the non-arising of dharmas. If one attains this acceptance, one should know that this person will assuredly attain the supreme fruit of the path. If any good man or good woman obtains this dhāraṇī-gate, joyfully praises and recites it, and mindfully contemplates the Three Treasures, making offerings with a sincere mind, that person will be revered by all humans and devas, and will also be protected by the devas, the humans, and the four great heavenly kings. Although not yet liberated, one can extinguish all weighty transgressions, karmic obstructions, affliction-obstructions, retribution-obstructions, and dharma-obstructions. Even in dreams, one will never lose the aspiration for bodhi. One will fully attain the four unobstructed wisdoms. One will not be defiled by worldly dharmas, like a lotus flower. Freed from all fears, the four māras and great enemies cannot do harm. One's wholesome dharmas will increase. Whatever one speaks, the assembly will delight in hearing and accepting. All who see one will feel compassion, and feeling compassion, they will come to one's aid. One will be far from wrong texts and unwholesome evil companions. One's body will be free from the four hundred and four diseases, and one will be able to relieve sentient beings of all their sufferings. One will constantly give joy and happiness to sentient beings. Wherever one is born, all faculties will be complete. The four immeasurable minds will be unshakable. Seeing those who are frightened, one will give rise to tender regard for them like one's own parents, constantly acting for sentient beings. One will be delightful to see and beloved by all. Although one does not seek gain, one will be made offerings by everyone. One will be far from the five hindrances and will delight in following wholesome dharmas.

"If, due to incidental conditions, one briefly sleeps, in dreams one will see the tathāgatas and bodhisattvas of the ten directions. Whatever Dharma this bodhisattva has not yet heard, all will be heard. One will discern the ten wholesome and ten unwholesome dharmas, demonstrate the sufferings of saṃsāra, open up great skillful means, and teach the bodhisattva precepts. Having heard the Dharma, one will give rise to joyful faith. By this cause and condition, at the time of relinquishing one's life, the mind will be non-retrogressive and without fear. One will see the buddhas of the ten directions. One's words will be without error. Having relinquished one's body, one will be born in a pure and wonderful land, will see buddhas and hear the Dharma, and will constantly draw near to the buddhas, cultivate the five spiritual powers, and practice the naturally arising, not depending on others to attain. One will constantly obtain rebirth by transformation, not through the three evil paths. One will never lose the opportunity to see buddhas, will delight in hearing the true Dharma, and will make offerings to the saṃgha.

"Why? Good man, this dhāraṇī dharma-gate was taught by all the buddhas of the past, for the sake of extinguishing the transgressions of beings in the evil age. The buddhas of the past clearly and joyfully saw that sentient beings in the future evil age would be unable to draw near to good teachers, would be far from the right path, would walk in darkness, would cultivate wrong views, and would constantly dwell amid the heavy illness of afflictions, far from the path of bodhi. For this cause and condition, the buddhas of the past all together taught this dhāraṇī. Good man, all the presently existing buddhas of the ten directions also together teach this dhāraṇī. All the future buddhas of the ten directions will also together teach this dhāraṇī, all for the sake of extinguishing the heinous transgressions of Straight Path the Brahmin and others like him."

At that time the World-Honored One told the brahmin Straight Path: "Good man, you speak of 'the five great elements of a sentient being's body': this is what is called the five aggregates, the twelve sense-bases, and the eighteen elements. Within these five aggregates, through the eighteen elements, sentient beings in their inversion give rise to the thought of 'self.' Because of the thought of self, they revolve in birth and death like a wheel. Because of these four inversions, sentient beings are born blind and die blind, receiving immeasurable suffering, separated from the joys of humans and devas and from the supreme joy. Their minds lack shame, right faith, and wisdom. Therefore they say: 'The four great elements, the five aggregates, the twelve sense-bases, and the eighteen elements are self and what belongs to self.' Good man, I now ask you; answer as you wish. According to your doctrine, is the earth element itself the self and what belongs to self?"

"No, World-Honored One. Because of the earth element, there is a self."

"Good man, if the self arises from the earth element, then the self is earth and earth is the self. Therefore, earth is also called earth, also called self, also called sentient being, also called lifespan, also called desire-vessel, also called growth, also called man, also called youth, also called doer, also called causer, also called ariser, also called one who causes to arise, also called speaker, also called one who causes to speak, also called receiver, also called one who causes to receive, also called knower, also called one who causes to know. The remaining great elements and the dharma sense-base and elements are likewise. Good man, none of the great elements, aggregates, sense-bases, or elements is the self, nor through to 'one who causes to know' is any of them the self. Good man, all the great elements, aggregates, sense-bases, and elements, whether past, future, or present, are not self and not what belongs to self. Therefore, the great elements, aggregates, sense-bases, and elements cannot be grasped or held. They are unborn, unperishing, without activity, and cannot be seen or heard. Good man, the eye is empty, form is empty, eye-consciousness is empty. The contact that arises from the conditions of these three is also empty. The feeling that arises from the conditions of these four is also empty. Therefore, all dharmas are without a master, do not attain autonomy, and have no 'this' or 'that.' Good man, all the great elements, aggregates, sense-bases, and elements are neither male nor female. The mind and its mental factors are not called male or female. For this reason, all dharmas are like empty space, arising and perishing moment by moment, without self-nature, like a flash of lightning, and inexpressible.

"If a person kills and incurs the transgression of killing, does this person incur the transgression through the present body and mind? Through the past body and mind? Through the future body and mind?"

The brahmin said: "World-Honored One, the past has already perished. The future has not yet arisen. The present has no abiding."

The Buddha said: "For this reason, there should be no transgression of killing. If one says there is killing, what is called killing? Earth does not kill earth. All the remaining great elements, aggregates, sense-bases, and elements are likewise."

The brahmin said: "World-Honored One, because of an evil mind as a condition, it is called killing."

The Buddha said: "Good man, you say that because of an evil mind one incurs the name of killing. Where does this evil mind abide? Is it in the desire realm? In the form realm? In the formless realm? In empty space? In the mind-element? In the conditioned element? In the non-perception element? In the element of outflows? In the element without outflows? In the element of existence? In the element of non-existence? Good man, if a person gives rise to an evil mind and commits the five heinous transgressions, who is the one who gives rise? What is the nature of this mind? Who turns this mind toward good or not-good? Where does it abide while making this turning? If there is no doer and no receiver, how can you say there is a self and what belongs to self?"

At that time the brahmin was silent and did not speak.

"Good man, for what reason are you silent and do not answer?"

The brahmin said: "World-Honored One, I have now entered this dharma-gate. I see that all dharmas are without action, without grasping, without abiding, without nature, without substance, and are inexpressible; like empty space, like a mirage, like a dream, an illusion, the moon in water. World-Honored One, all dharmas have no place of abiding, no 'that' or 'this.' Therefore I am now silent and without words."

The Buddha said: "Excellent, excellent! Good man, this is called the Lion's Roar Supreme Stairway of Wisdom, Turning Back to the Acceptance of Non-Arising, the Dharma-Gate of Boundless Spiritual Powers and Dharma-Practice. It severs all suffering, enters the gate of great wisdom, and is the initial practice of the path to bodhi. It severs the two kinds of birth and is far removed from the dust of afflictions. This dharma-gate is not shared with any śrāvakas or pratyekabuddhas.

"Good man, although you have killed your father and mother, you have only incurred weighty karma, not the heinous transgressions. Now that you have also entered this dharma-gate and given rise to great faith, gradually all will be extinguished without remainder."

The brahmin, having heard this, was greatly joyful in his mind. He immediately went before the Buddha, bowed in reverence, knelt with joined palms, and said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One, I was foolish and benighted. I slandered what the Buddha taught. Now I sincerely repent before the Tathāgata of both my heinous transgressions and my offense of slandering the Buddha."

"Good man, do you wish to retain any remainder of these transgressions?"

"World-Honored One, what is meant by 'remainder'?"

"Good man, although you have felt shame, confessed openly, and repented, if you do not generate the aspiration for anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi, this is called 'remainder.' If you can have compassion for all sentient beings and generate the aspiration for anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi, this is called 'without remainder.' Such a person can also sever the four inversions, also attain the wisdom of the sages, draw near to the brahma-abodes, increase wholesome dharmas, and make their good roots firm. Although there may be adverse conditions, these cannot cause them to retrogress. They will attain unobstructed wisdom."

At that time the brahmin Straight Path and immeasurable nayutas of sentient beings said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One, we now, with sincere minds and compassion for all sentient beings, generate the aspiration for anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi and repent of our former deeds. We only pray that the Tathāgata, out of great compassion, accept our profound repentance. We further pray that the Tathāgata, for our sake, teach the Lion's Roar Supreme Stairway of Wisdom, Turning Back to the Acceptance of Non-Arising, the Dharma-Gate of Boundless Spiritual Powers Dhāraṇī. This dhāraṇī is not shared with śrāvakas or pratyekabuddhas. Even if there are shared aspects, these are not what we seek. What we now seek is the Tathāgata's supreme-meaning, unsurpassed-wisdom dhāraṇī, which can adorn all wholesome dharmas, all worldly dharmas, and the Buddha's unsurpassed wisdom, fearlessness, and generosity: the dhāraṇī. For the great assembly, to increase wholesome dharmas, so that they are not forgotten or lost, that they may be sincerely received and upheld, able to shatter the four inversions, accomplish the path of bodhi, and destroy all bonds and evil karma. This dhāraṇī is the great treasure-heap of the bodhisattva-mahāsattvas, the supreme samādhi. For this reason, it can ensure that bodhisattvas, even in dreams, have no retrogression of mind. Wherever they go, whether in assemblies of śramaṇas, brāhmaṇas, or kṣatriyas, their minds are without fear. They constantly delight in generosity and practice vigor diligently. They are far from all evil, unwholesome actions. They can cause enemies to give rise to joyful minds. They are far from all negligence, wrong texts, wrong views, and evil companions, and can also subdue such people. They can constantly teach the dharma-gate of unobstructed eloquence and cause the Dharma-teacher to be free from illness, suffering, and evil matters, and not be troubled by māras. They always know their former lives. They obtain bodies of transformation-birth, far from all evil existences. They constantly draw near to the buddhas of the ten directions, hear and receive the true Dharma, and make offerings to the saṃgha. World-Honored One, this dhāraṇī is called the secret treasury of all bodhisattva-mahāsattvas. We pray that the Tathāgata, out of compassion, explain it in detail."

The Buddha said: "Excellent, excellent! Good man, this dhāraṇī can accomplish immeasurable wholesome dharmas, as the Dharma teaches. Listen carefully, listen carefully! I shall now teach it for you."

At that time the World-Honored One taught it:

[Dhāraṇī] "Pari kari paka kari taka kari dharā viṣṭi parā pañcana mukha parā juna nidi parā nidi viṇḍa viṣṭi śāmpara ṇa indirī. Far from form, fragrance, and taste; far from inversion; able to burn all poisons; able to be far from all evil ghosts, evil paths, and illness. Unobstructed dharma-gate, complete dharma-gate, true-wisdom dharma-gate, unobstructed-mind great dharma-gift, complete dharma-gift, able to shatter the five hindrances. Svāhā."

"Good man, if any disciple of the Buddha, whether bhikṣu, bhikṣuṇī, upāsaka, or upāsikā, receives, upholds, reads, and recites this dhāraṇī-spell, or copies it, and on the eighth day of the month, cleanses and bathes their body, puts on fresh garments, purifies their inner mind, and in the presence of the Buddha, a buddha image, a stūpa, or relics, recites it a thousand times, and ties one hundred and eight knots in a cotton thread and binds it to their head, then even if this person is traveling among evil assemblies, all will be unable to see their form. When sleeping, there will be no evil dreams. This is called one sentence of the dhāraṇī.

"Good man, there is a further sentence, namely:

[Dhāraṇī] "Suīnī umunī palāṃ munī upa jhāpi kuśī ḍha jhā. Not losing the mind of intention; firm, stable, lightning-like mind; abiding in the Dharma; equal abiding. Able to give rise to the Tathāgata's wisdom-light. Vī dhu matsu patti. Unobstructed quiescence, mindful mind, increasing. Svāhā."

"If any good man or good woman receives, upholds, reads, and recites this dhāraṇī sentence, or copies it, and before the Buddha, a buddha image, a stūpa, or relics recites it a thousand times, and drinks a potion of black-honey lotus-flower garland juice, in a single day one will be able to recite a thousand verses and attain the knowledge of others' minds. Good man, if any good man or good woman, or bhikṣu, bhikṣuṇī, upāsaka, or upāsikā, in a quiet place, sincerely and in accordance with the Dharma, practices this dhāraṇī, that person within seven days will attain the four dhyānas, destroy the bonds of the desire realm, see the buddhas of the ten directions, and attain the samādhi of Realization-Mind. One will be able to transform sentient beings, extinguish all obstructions, namely karmic obstructions, retribution-obstructions, and affliction-obstructions; purify sentient beings' faculties; and burn up all sentient beings' afflictions, inversions, and bonds. One will draw near to and abide upon the path of the worthy and the sagely. Nothing can cause one's bodhi to retrogress. One will draw near to omniscience. Without depending on any other cause, wisdom will arise. One will attain the dharma-gate of all buddhas' unobstructed and fearless three-time wisdom. By the power of this samādhi's causes and conditions, one will attain the power of aspiration, be able to purify all evil sentient beings, and swiftly attain anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi."

When this dhāraṇī was taught, in the great assembly eighty-four thousand nayutas of sentient beings attained this dhāraṇī. Eight nayutas of sentient beings, through the removal of weighty karmic conditions, attained acceptance in accordance with the Dharma. Immeasurable sentient beings who had not yet generated the aspiration for the śrāvaka path were caused to generate it. Those who had not yet generated the aspiration for the pratyekabuddha path were caused to generate it. Immeasurable sentient beings who had not yet generated the aspiration for anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi were caused to generate it. Immeasurable sentient beings attained non-retrogression from the aspiration for bodhi.

At that time the Bodhisattva Mahāsattva Great Cloud Secret Treasury said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One, in the future age, if any bhikṣu, bhikṣuṇī, upāsaka, or upāsikā receives, upholds, reads, and recites this Great Cloud Sūtra, what merits will they gain? What afflictions can they further extinguish? What fruition-retributions can they further be freed from? What power of wisdom will they further attain? When will they attain the great-vehicle wisdom and be able to cross the boundless ocean of birth and death? When will they turn the unsurpassed Dharma-wheel and bestow upon sentient beings the pure Dharma-eye?"

The Buddha said: "Good man, regarding such a question, one should not answer at all." The Bodhisattva Great Cloud asked three times, and the Buddha also did not answer.

At that time Mañjuśrī said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One, to whom should this sūtra be entrusted, to subdue the evil sentient beings of the future? World-Honored One, in the future age, there will be kṣatriya caṇḍālas, brāhmaṇa, vaiśya, and śūdra caṇḍālas. There may be some who have not yet heard this sūtra. Or some may inflict evil upon the four groups. Because of such karma, they will receive retribution in the three evil paths for immeasurable years. We pray that the Tathāgata, for the sake of extinguishing the evil karmic fruition of such caṇḍālas, entrust this sūtra to an attendant."

The Buddha said: "Mañjuśrī, after my nirvāṇa, in this buddha-world the lineage of true kings will be cut off without remainder. Evil people will become rulers. Such evil people will cut off the Dharma of true kings; they will be proud, jealous, shameless, and negligent; they will take as refuge what is not a refuge. All the great ministers, elders, śramaṇas, and brāhmaṇas of such lands will likewise be violators of precepts, stingy, greedy, and shameless, endowed with the ten unwholesome dharmas. They will not believe in the Three Treasures, will have no mind of offering, and will not seek to request teachings, constantly delighting in proclaiming that there is no cause and no effect. When the bodhisattva-mahāsattvas see the sentient beings of such lands habitually practicing evil in this way, they will move to pure lands in other regions. After the bodhisattvas have moved away, in this world the evil age and evil times will intensify. Sentient beings will have many illnesses, grain and rice will be extremely expensive, the four armies will rise in competition, and people will rob each other, all because sentient beings do not know contentment!

"Good man, to whom should I entrust this sūtra? Who can expound and explain it among such evil sentient beings? Good man, if there is someone who can endure hunger, thirst, and all manner of suffering, verbal abuse, and physical beating, without sparing body or life, then that person can circulate this sūtra in the future age. If such a person exists, I shall entrust this supreme sūtra to them!"

At that time, in the assembly there was a bodhisattva named Fearless Merit Swift-Traveler. He rose from his seat, prostrated, knelt, and said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One, I am able, throughout immeasurable ages, to endure immeasurable sufferings, whether hands and feet cut off, head cracked, eyes gouged out, hunger, thirst, cold, heat, beating, and verbal abuse, and even the sufferings of the three evil paths, in order to circulate the Tathāgata's sūtra. I am able to travel throughout the great cities, villages, and hamlets of the Tathāgata's world, among nāgas, among ghosts, circulating this sūtra, for the sake of severing sentient beings' four inversions, causing them to uphold the precepts, practice vigor diligently, have right views fully accomplished, and accomplish the six pāramitās, so that they may attain unsurpassed bodhi."

The Buddha said: "Excellent, excellent! Good man, as you have vowed, you are fully able to accomplish it, causing immeasurable sentient beings to perform the Buddha's work. Good man, by your vow, you will be able to fully accomplish the six pāramitās and swiftly attain anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi. Good man, although there are immeasurable great assemblies, those like you are truly rare to find. In the future age, those who accept your words will also be rare. Even among those who accept them, those who give rise to deep faith, uphold, read, recite, expound, and explain them with detail and instruction will also be rare. If in the future age there are those who can receive, uphold, read, recite, explain, copy, and delight in teaching this sūtra, they will gain ten merits: their bodies will always be free from illness; without seeking, they will naturally receive offerings of robes, food, bedding, and medicine; without seeking good companions, they will draw near to them; the buddhas of the ten directions will love and care for them; whatever Dharma they teach, people will delight in hearing and accepting; they will attain śamatha and vipaśyanā; they will be complete in both worldly and transcendent meaning; body and mind will be quiescent; the Three Treasures will increase; and they will attain the supreme dhāraṇī. Good man, although I speak of these ten merits, if one can further teach people to repent and extinguish immeasurable evil transgressions, one will also attain immeasurable merit. Furthermore, good man, if such a person sincerely, in the future age, receives, upholds, reads, recites, and widely explains this for others, they will attain immeasurable merit. When this person relinquishes their life, they will clearly see the buddhas of the ten directions. Each buddha will say: 'Good man, be born in my world, hear my true Dharma, sever the four inversions, extinguish all evil dharmas, accomplish the wisdom of the sages, and abide in the brahma-abodes. Having received my Dharma, you will constantly obtain transformation-birth, be freed from the three evil paths, and swiftly attain anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi, and even enter the great parinirvāṇa.' Having heard this, that person will give rise to a joyful mind and will be born in those pure buddha-lands.

"Good man, such a person, having received this Dharma, wherever they are born will have all faculties complete. They will obtain supremely wonderful form and will be delightful for all sentient beings to see. They will attain faith in the Three Treasures and be able to make offerings. If they hear the true Dharma, they will at once attain liberation and destroy all karma. The true Dharma they receive they will firmly uphold without losing it, and they will attain all dhāraṇī-gates. The samādhis of śrāvakas, pratyekabuddhas, and bodhisattvas will enable them to surpass the paths of śrāvakas and pratyekabuddhas. They will further be able to teach and transform immeasurable sentient beings, establishing them in the brahma-abodes. If in the future there are those who uphold these words, they will accomplish such merits."

Mañjuśrī said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One, this bodhisattva who has given rise to such a rare and difficult aspiration is the right one to whom this sūtra should be entrusted."

At that time the World-Honored One told the Bodhisattva Fearless Merit Swift-Traveler: "I now entrust this sūtra to you!"

At that time the Bodhisattva Fearless Merit Swift-Traveler, together with immeasurable bodhisattvas, reverently accepting the Buddha's instruction, received this sūtra. Brahmā-abiding and immeasurable brahmā devas, Blue-eyed and immeasurable Śakras, the four heavenly kings and immeasurable spirits, and the nāga kings Nanda, Upananda, and others also together received and upheld it. Immeasurable sentient beings generated the aspiration for anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi.

*Offered by the laywoman of pure faith, Zhāng Xuān'ài.*

*Completed on the eleventh day of the first month of a water-hare year.*
