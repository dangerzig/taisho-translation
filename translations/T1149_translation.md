# 五大虛空藏菩薩速疾大神驗祕密式經
## Secret Ritual Sūtra of the Swift Great Divine Efficacy of the Five Great Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattvas

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1149

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Vajrabodhi (金剛智).

---

> I rely upon what the yoga sūtra teaches, for bhikṣus of scant merit in the age of semblance and final dharma,
> Who long uphold the secret words of all the buddhas, yet whose merit and wisdom do not manifest,
> And for those without office or rank, of noble family but scant karmic fortune:
> To convert and liberate all sentient beings, here I reveal this most secret dharma.
> The divinely efficacious path of the buddhas of the three times, a swift and profoundly ultimate method.
> The bhikṣus who inherit Śākyamuni's transmitted dharma should, according to their means, give alms of wealth,
> As an offering named for the fruit of bodhi. Transmit, receive, carefully remember, and practice.
> If one gives without utmost reverence, one will receive the path but practice without response.
> To reveal the great efficacious power of this dharma, one should first establish the secret divination board.

Take white sandalwood, cypress, cassia, or wood that is over a hundred years old and spiritually potent. Use it to make a round celestial board, two and a half or three inches in diameter, and a square terrestrial board, six or seven inches on a side. The celestial board should have a blue ground throughout: the inner court yellow, the middle court blue, the outer court red. The lower terrestrial board has four sides, with blue-green below and yellow underneath. The celestial board is one inch and three-tenths thick; the terrestrial board is one inch or eight-tenths. Paint them accordingly.

On the eastern face of the celestial board, paint the image of Puṇyajñāna Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva, seated on a white jeweled lotus, wearing a five-buddha jeweled crown, adorned with various garlands. The right hand forms the abhaya mudrā, and the left hand holds a jeweled lotus. Upon the lotus is a karma-vajra. The form is yellow in color.

In the south, there is the syllable *Trāṃ*, which transforms into Pūraṇa Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva (Wish-Fulfilling Ākāśagarbha), seated on a red lotus, wearing a five-buddha jeweled crown, adorned as before. The form is flesh-red in color. The right hand holds a jeweled sword, and the left holds a blue lotus. Upon the lotus is a wish-fulfilling jewel surrounded by flames.

In the west, there is the syllable *Hrīḥ*, which transforms into Praṇidhāna Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva (Wish-Granting Ākāśagarbha), seated on a purple lotus, with jeweled crown and adornments as before. The palms are joined at the chest. The form is reddish-pink in color.

In the north, there is the syllable *Aḥ*, which transforms into Vimala Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva (Stainless Ākāśagarbha), seated on a white lotus, with jeweled crown and adornments as before. The left hand forms the abhaya mudrā, and the right hand holds a jeweled lotus. Upon the lotus is a moon-disc. The form is water-white in color.

In the center, there is the syllable *Vaṃ*, which transforms into Vimokṣa Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva (Liberation Ākāśagarbha), with jeweled crown and adornments as before, seated on a yellow-white lotus. The right hand forms the abhaya mudrā, and the left hand holds a jeweled lotus. Upon the lotus is a single-pronged vajra. The color is yellow.

Next, on the top of the celestial board, paint the images of the Seven Star Kings. Next, in the inner court of the terrestrial board, paint the images of the eight devas in the eight directions, with the Four Heavenly Kings at the four corners, as customary. Śakra Devānām Indra in the east, Yama Deva in the south, Brahmā Deva in the west, Vaiśravaṇa Deva in the north, each in their customary form.

Next, in the middle court, paint the twenty-eight lunar mansions: in the east, seven male figures in blue clothes of blue color, holding implements; in the south, seven male figures in red clothes of red color, holding implements; in the west, seven male figures in white clothes of white color, holding implements; in the north, seven male figures in black clothes of black color, holding implements.

Next, in the outer court, paint the thirty-six animals, beginning from the jia-yi [cycle]. These belong to the yakṣa division; consult the reference. Their forms and images should be painted with names, characters, and colors matching their respective directional corners. The inner court ground is yellow, the middle court ground is blue, the outer court ground is red. Know this and paint in color accordingly.

### Chapter Two: Accomplishment of Siddhi through the Five Great Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattvas

The root supreme heart dhāraṇī:

*Namo ākāśagarbhāya oṃ ari kamāri rī svāhā*

The eastern bodhisattva's mantra:

*Oṃ vajra hūṃ*

The southern bodhisattva's mantra:

*Oṃ vajra na trāṃ*

The western bodhisattva's mantra:

*Oṃ vajra hrīḥ*

The northern bodhisattva's mantra:

*Oṃ vajra aḥ*

The central bodhisattva's mantra:

*Oṃ vajra vaṃ*

The mudrās are as described in the yoga sūtra. The practitioner should first establish an appropriate altar site wherever one desires, selecting a quiet place, far from noise, filth, and impurity. Do not associate with boys and girls, faithless novices, or those who are poor and in soiled clothing. At the site for seeking accomplishment, prepare the offerings carefully and reverently. Place the divination board of the main deity, and facing the auspicious direction, recite to completion as desired: six times day and night, the root vidyā one million times. One will then attain great wisdom and obtain the supreme great happiness among humans.

If one wishes for the greatest sovereign power in all things, prepare offerings and homa in the appropriate direction, and all wishes will be accomplished. The substances appropriate for the homa and other details are as described in the Chapter on the Accomplishment of All Merits and Siddhis.

If a person wishes to attain great wisdom, petition the southern bodhisattva combined with Vaiśravaṇa Deva; it will certainly be attained.

If a person wishes to be greatly beloved by the king, petition the western bodhisattva combined with Vaiśravaṇa Deva; it will certainly be attained.

If a person wishes to be deeply loved by the queen, consorts, and all noble women, petition the same bodhisattva combined with the same deva; it will certainly be attained.

If a person wishes to be beloved by great ministers and lords, petition the same bodhisattva with the same deva; it will certainly be attained.

If a person wishes to swiftly become a greatly fortunate elder, petition the southern bodhisattva combined with the same deva; it will certainly be attained.

If a person wishes to attain a great official position with reverence and esteem, petition the eastern bodhisattva combined with Śakra Deva; it will certainly be attained.

If a person wishes to surpass all others and be sovereign under heaven, petition the northern bodhisattva combined with Brahmā Deva; it will certainly be attained.

If a person wishes to fulfill all hopes for supramundane matters, petition the central bodhisattva combined with the eastern deva; it will certainly be attained.

If a person wishes to manifest great divine efficacy for others to see, petition the southern bodhisattva combined with the northwestern deva; it will certainly be so.

If a person wishes to benefit all sentient beings through great efficacy, petition the eastern bodhisattva combined with the southwestern deva; it will certainly be so.

If a person wishes to obtain official rank and emolument, petition the eastern bodhisattva combined with Vaiśravaṇa Deva; it will be attained.

If a person wishes to be beloved by all people, petition the western bodhisattva combined with the same deva; it will certainly be attained.

If a person wishes to be victorious in debate, petition the southern bodhisattva combined with Brahmā Deva; it will certainly be attained.

If a person wishes to subdue evil persons, petition the northern bodhisattva combined with the southwestern deva; it will certainly be so.

If a person wishes to stop febrile illness, petition the central bodhisattva combined with the northwestern deva; it will certainly be removed.

If a person wishes to stop tumors, swelling, gan-sores, and carbuncles, petition the northern bodhisattva combined with the southwestern deva; it will certainly be removed.

If a person wishes to remove obstacles to their official position, petition the eastern bodhisattva combined with the northwestern deva; it will certainly be attained.

If a person wishes to become the king of all dharma masters, petition the southern bodhisattva combined with the southeastern deva; it will certainly be attained.

If a person wishes to be protected by all buddhas and bodhisattvas, petition the central bodhisattva combined with the same deva; it will certainly be attained.

If a person wishes to be beloved by all the wealthy, petition the southern bodhisattva combined with Vaiśravaṇa Deva; it will certainly be attained.

If a person wishes to bind celestial and terrestrial fox-spirits, petition the eastern bodhisattva combined with the southwestern deva.

If a person wishes to eliminate all ghost and spirit possessions, petition the northern bodhisattva combined with Yama Deva.

If a person wishes to eliminate the determined karma of others, petition the eastern bodhisattva combined with the same deva.

If a person wishes to eliminate untimely death and short lifespan in children, petition the eastern bodhisattva combined with the same deva.

If a person wishes to help women in difficult childbirth to deliver swiftly, petition the northern bodhisattva combined with the same deva.

If a person wishes to stop all evil ghosts who cause evil poisoning and confusion, petition the eastern bodhisattva combined with the same deva.

If a person wishes to reverse curses and spells cast by evil persons, petition the northern bodhisattva combined with the southwestern deva; it will certainly be attained.

If a person wishes to stop the disaster of fire, petition the central bodhisattva combined with the northwestern deva.

If a person wishes to end the disaster of drought, petition the central bodhisattva combined with Śakra Deva.

If a person wishes to end the disaster of epidemic disease, petition the northern bodhisattva combined with Yama Deva.

If a person wishes to attain the highest grade of rebirth in Sukhāvatī, petition the western bodhisattva combined with Yama Deva.

If a person wishes to rescue seven generations of parents, six kinds of relatives, and all sentient beings from the suffering of the hells and grant them rebirth in Sukhāvatī, petition the western bodhisattva combined with Brahmā Deva.

If a person wishes for husband and wife to love each other, petition the western bodhisattva combined with Vaiśravaṇa Deva, write the names of the man and woman, seal them between heaven and earth, and petition.

If a person wishes to cause an ill-matched husband and wife to separate, petition the northern bodhisattva combined with the southwestern deva.

If a person wishes a separated husband and wife to love each other as before, petition the western bodhisattva combined with Vaiśravaṇa Deva.

If a person wishes to recover stolen or lost property, petition the northern bodhisattva combined with Yama Deva; it will certainly be attained.

If a person wishes to recover runaway servants, cattle, or horses, petition the southern bodhisattva combined with Brahmā Deva; it will be attained.

The empowered ghee-honey method and other detailed procedures for seeking retentive memory follow the ritual manual as described; consult and understand them.

This dharma was transmitted from Bodhgayā in India. Its preparations are not overly elaborate; in its time, all people called it the Efficacious Method.

The mantra for empowering offering items:

*Oṃ amṛtodbhava hūṃ phaṭ svāhā*

The Great Wheel Altar Mudrā: with both hands each form a fist, the earth and wind fingers each hooking together at the chest. Recite the true word three times and rotate. Next empower the altar. Next place the mudrā in one's own mouth. The true word:

*Oṃ vajra-cakra jaḥ hūṃ vaṃ hoḥ*

The mantra for empowering the prayer beads:

*Oṃ vairocana-mālā svāhā*

Next, the mantra for rotating the prayer beads:

*Oṃ vajra-guhya-japa-samaye hūṃ*

The mantra of Śrī Mahādevī:

*Oṃ mahāśriye svāhā*

The Brahmic mantra of the Deva King:

*Oṃ vijñā-sūta-kaścā svāhā*

The shared mantra of the Four Heavenly Kings:

*Oṃ jambhala śakutarāya svāhā*

The messenger's mantra:

*Oṃ turu turu dhiri dhiri duru duru kathāya svāhā*

The Great Samaya Mudrā and True Word: with both hands form vajra fists, backs touching, and extend both index fingers with tips meeting. With the mudrā, touch from one's own heart to the crown three times, raising it, then scatter it at the crown.

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ dharmadhātu-svabhāvaku haṃ*

The Vast Amogha-maṇi Universal Offering Mudrā: with both hands in vajra añjali, both index fingers pressed together like a jewel shape, both thumbs raised upright. Recite the Amogha-maṇi Offering True Word:

*Oṃ amogha-pūjā-maṇi-padma-vajre tathāgata-vilokite samanta-prasāra hūṃ*

Next take the single-pronged vajra. With the left hand in a fist, place it at the left hip. Recite the vajra true word three times, and with the vajra rotate left and right three times each, toss upward three times, empower the five places, and rest it upon the right breast. Next hold the bell in the left hand, recite the true word three times, ring five times upward, ring twice beside the left ear, then pause, and ring twice more upward.

Next with the vajra, rotate left and right three times each, empower the five places, and rest both vajra and bell in their original positions.

The vajra true word:

*Oṃ vajra-pāṇi hūṃ*

The bell true word:

*Oṃ vajra-ghaṇṭā tuśya hoḥ*

Next offer the argha with mudrā and vidyā. Next form the Ākāśagarbha four-directional seal. Next the vajra wall. Next the purification sprinkling true word.
