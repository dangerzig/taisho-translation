# 大虛空藏菩薩念誦法
## Recitation Method for Mahā-Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1146

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

I now rely upon the Vajraśekhara Yoga Sūtra to proclaim the true word teaching of Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva of the Jewel Division, out of compassion for householders and mountain-dwellers of scant merit and little virtue who lack resources, and whose prayers for worldly and supramundane supreme wishes are often unfulfilled. If one cultivates in accordance with this teaching, karmic retribution and other obstacles are all eliminated, merit and virtue increase, the mind and spirit are at ease, one's pure faith in the Mahāyāna benefits sentient beings, and the mind does not retrogress. All the wealth and treasures of the worldly and supramundane realms will all be obtained, and one will be able to bring benefit to all sentient beings. The merit accumulated from a single invocation or a single recollection is like space itself; how much more so for one who intentionally cultivates in accordance with the dharma: whatever is wished will certainly attain supreme accomplishment.

The practitioner should first enter the abhiṣeka ritual site and receive the ritual procedures face-to-face from a master. Whether in the mountains at a quiet place or in a temple, at whatever location one desires, establish a practice room. Make a square altar of whatever size, smear the ground with gomaya, and arrange eight maṇḍalas. Hang banners all around and place a celestial canopy above. On the western side of the altar, place an image of Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva. The reciter sits east of the altar facing the image to recite. Scatter various seasonal flowers upon the altar, and with incense, lamplight, food, drink, and fruits, according to one's means, make offerings.

Each time one enters the ritual site, before the honored image, prostrate with the five limbs on the ground, paying homage to all tathāgatas and all the holy assembly. Then repent, rejoice, exhort, and make vows. Afterward, sit in full or half lotus as one wishes, with the body upright and mind correct. Apply perfume to both hands, then join the palms like an unopened lotus. Recite the purification true word three or seven times, then scatter the mudrā above the crown: the three karmas are then purified, accomplishing the supreme-meaning ablution.

The Purification of the Three Karmas True Word:

*Oṃ svabhāva-śuddhāḥ sarvadharmāḥ svabhāva-śuddho 'haṃ*

Next, form the Buddha Division Heart Samaya Mudrā.

With all ten fingers of both hands interlocked inwardly in fists, raise the thumbs together upright. When the mudrā is formed, visualize all the buddhas pervading space, then recite the Buddha Division Heart True Word three times and scatter the mudrā above the crown. The true word:

*Oṃ jina-jik svāhā*

Next, form the Lotus Division Heart Samaya Mudrā.

Following the same form as the Buddha Division Heart Mudrā, bend the ring finger into the palm, and raise only the thumb upright. Having formed this mudrā, visualize Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva and his retinue on the right side of all the tathāgatas. With the mudrā, recite the Lotus Division Heart True Word three times, and scatter the mudrā above the right side of the crown. The true word:

*Oṃ ārolik svāhā*

Next, form the Vajra Division Heart Samaya Mudrā.

Following the same form as the Buddha Division Heart Mudrā, bend the thumb into the palm, and raise only the ring finger upright. Visualize Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva and his retinue on the left side of all the tathāgatas, then recite the Vajra Division Heart True Word three times and scatter the mudrā above the left side of the crown. The true word:

*Oṃ vajradhṛk svāhā*

Next, form the Armor Body-Protection Mudrā.

With the left ring finger placed horizontally across the palm, grip it with the index, middle, ring, and little fingers in a fist. When this mudrā is formed, seal the five places of the body: the forehead, right shoulder, left shoulder, heart, and throat. This is called the five-place body protection. The true word:

*Oṃ bhur bhuvaḥ hūṃ*

By forming this mudrā and empowering the five places, one is as if clad in vajra-light blazing armor of adamantine firmness. All māras cannot cause obstruction, and the true words one upholds will swiftly be accomplished.

Next, form the Mudrā for Inviting Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva.

With both hands in vajra-bond, raise the middle fingers upright, bending their tips together like a jewel shape, with the index fingers each bent like hooks. Visualize within the altar a jeweled pavilion, and within the pavilion an eight-petaled open lotus. Recite the true word four times, and with the index fingers beckon toward oneself: the main deity and his retinue all come and gather. The invocation true word:

*Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-viṣaya-vajraratna-sarva-śa-paripūraka jaḥ hūṃ vaṃ hoḥ trāṃ*

Next, form the Kuṇḍalī Body Mudrā.

With the little fingers of both hands, right pressing over left, interlocked within the palms; the ring fingers pressed together atop them; the thumbs pressed together atop the ring fingers; the middle fingers raised upright with tips touching; the index fingers bent like hooks, forming a three-pronged vajra shape. Then recite the Kuṇḍalī True Word: while reciting, rotate the mudrā counterclockwise three times to dispel all māras, then clockwise three times to seal the boundary. The true word:

*Namo ratna-trayāya namaś caṇḍa-mahā-vajra-krodhāya oṃ huru huru tiṣṭha tiṣṭha bandha bandha hana hana amṛte hūṃ phaṭ svāhā*

Next, offer argha water.

The practitioner should always place two clean vessels near the knees upon the altar, filled with fragrant water as argha. The one on the right is offered at the time of initial invitation; the one on the left is offered at the time of the final sending-off. Each time an offering is made, hold the argha vessel with both hands at forehead level and offer it while reciting the true word, visualizing bathing the main deity and all the holy assembly. The true word:

*Oṃ vajrodaka hūṃ*

Next, form the Mudrā for Offering the Lotus Seat.

With both palms joined hollow, the little fingers and thumbs each touching tip to tip, and the six fingers in between slightly curved with tips separated, like the petals of an open lotus. The true word:

*Oṃ kamala svāhā*

By forming this mudrā and empowering with the true word, all the holy assembly and the main deity each receive a seven-jeweled lotus as their seat.

Next, form the Universal Offering Mudrā of Mahā-Ākāśagarbha.

Join both palms, with the ring fingers interlocked on the outside, and the index fingers bending back together like a jewel shape. When the mudrā is formed, recite the true word four times. The universal offering true word:

*Oṃ gagana-sambhava vajra hoḥ*

Visualize that from the mudrā there arise immeasurable varieties of offering items: fragrant flowers, lamp-candles, perfume, food and drink, jeweled banners, pennants, and canopies. Before the main deity and all the holy assembly, true and vast offerings are thereby accomplished.

Next, form the Karma Mudrā.

Place the left hand at the heart, palm up, with the ring finger and thumb touching. Bend the ring finger back like a jewel shape. Extend the right hand palm-up, facing forward in the wish-granting gesture. Having formed this mudrā, contemplate: "My body is identical to Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva." Then recite the karma true word:

*Oṃ vajraratna haṃ*

Through this visualization and empowerment, the practitioner's own body becomes identical to the main deity, Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva.

Next, form the Samaya Mudrā.

With both hands in vajra-bond, the index fingers bending back together like a jewel shape, the thumbs raised upright together, placed at the heart. Then recite the samaya true word seven times. The true word:

*Oṃ vajraratna hūṃ*

Then take a crystal prayer bead, place it in the palm, join palms at the heart, and recite the bead-empowerment true word three times. The true word:

*Oṃ virūcana-mālā svāhā*

Then hold the beads atop the crown and make this vow: "Throughout the worlds of the ten directions, may all practitioners of true words, whatever true words they uphold, swiftly accomplish them." Then hold the beads in the left hand, place the right hand at the heart, and move the beads neither too fast nor too slow, with the mind free from distraction. Whether one thousand or one hundred, when the appointed count is finished, hold the beads atop the crown and make this vow: "May all sentient beings' hopes for worldly and supramundane supreme rewards, through the merit of my recitation, swiftly be accomplished." Then place the beads in their original spot. Again form the main deity's samaya mudrā, recite the samaya true word seven times, and scatter the mudrā above the crown. Again form the universal offering mudrā, recite the universal offering true word seven times, and scatter the mudrā above the crown. Then recite the praise of Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva:

*Vajraratna su vajraratha vajrākāśa mahāmaṇi ākāśagarbha vajraḍa vajragarbha namastute*

Having recited the praise, take the argha on the left and offer it at forehead level. Then form the Kuṇḍalī Mudrā as before, rotate counterclockwise once to release the boundary. Next form the samaya mudrā and send off all the holy assembly. The sending-off true word:

*Oṃ vajraratna muḥ*

Raise the mudrā atop the crown, recite the true word seven times, and all the holy assembly is thereby sent off. Again form the three division heart samayas, reciting each three times. Next form the body-protection mudrā, sealing the five places as before. Then bow to the Buddha, make vows, and freely leave the ritual site. Turn and read the Mahāyāna, print buddha images and stūpas, widely practice generosity, and always bring benefit to all sentient beings.
