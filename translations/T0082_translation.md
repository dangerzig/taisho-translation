# 意經
## Sūtra on the Mind

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0082

Translated from the Chinese. Western Jin, translated by Dharmarakṣa (竺法護)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Bhagavat was dwelling at Śrāvastī in the Jeta's Grove, Anāthapiṇḍada's park. At that time, a certain bhikṣu was sitting alone in his cell, and his mind gave rise to this thought: "By what is the world led? By what does one come to experience suffering? Having arisen, having arisen, into what does one enter as a follower?"

Then that bhikṣu, rising in the late afternoon, went to where the World-Honored One was. Upon arriving, he paid homage at the feet of the World-Honored One and sat to one side. Having sat to one side, that bhikṣu said to the World-Honored One: "Venerable Sir! Today, while sitting alone in my cell, my mind gave rise to this thought: 'By what is the world led? By what does one come to experience suffering? Having arisen, having arisen, into what does one enter as a follower?'"

"Excellent! Excellent! Bhikṣu! You have a noble path, noble vision, and good discernment. What you have contemplated is good: 'By what is the world led? By what does one come to experience suffering? Having arisen, having arisen, into what does one enter as a follower?' Bhikṣu, is this indeed the question you have asked?"

"Yes, indeed, World-Honored One!"

"Bhikṣu! It is because of the mind that the world is led; it is because of the mind that one experiences suffering; arising through the mind, having arisen through the mind, one enters as a follower. Bhikṣu! It is the mind that causes the world to be led, that causes one to experience suffering, and that causes one, having arisen, having arisen, to enter as a follower. Bhikṣu! The noble disciple is without attachment. By that one is led, by that one goes, and having arisen through that, having arisen, one then follows accordingly. This, bhikṣu, is the noble disciple who is an Arahant: one who is able to govern his own mind and does not follow his own mind."

"Excellent, World-Honored One!" That bhikṣu, having heard what the World-Honored One had said, was delighted and gave his approval. Then he asked the World-Honored One again: "Venerable Sir! As for what is said of a bhikṣu of great learning, a bhikṣu of great learning: Venerable Sir, by how much is a bhikṣu called a bhikṣu of great learning? How many does the Tathāgata say make a bhikṣu one of great learning?"

"Excellent! Excellent! Bhikṣu! You have a noble path, noble vision, and good discernment. What you have said is good: 'World-Honored One, as for great learning, as for a bhikṣu of great learning: Venerable Sir, by how much is a bhikṣu called a bhikṣu of great learning? How many does the Tathāgata say make a bhikṣu one of great learning?' Bhikṣu, is this indeed the question you have asked?"

"Yes, indeed, World-Honored One!"

"I extensively teach bhikṣus concerning sūtra, geya, vyākaraṇa, gāthā, nidāna, udāna, avadāna, itivṛttaka, jātaka, vaipulya, adbhutadharma, and upadeśa. Bhikṣu, if a son of good family, having recited even a single four-line verse to me, then understands its meaning and understands the Dharma, practices in conformity with the Dharma, conducts himself in accord with the Dharma, and teaches in accordance with the Dharma: such a bhikṣu is truly one of great learning; such a bhikṣu is called one of great learning; thus does the Tathāgata speak of a bhikṣu as one of great learning."

"Excellent, World-Honored One!" That bhikṣu, having heard what the World-Honored One had said, reflected well upon it, held it in mind, was delighted, and gave his approval. Then he again asked the World-Honored One: "Venerable Sir! A bhikṣu who, having heard, is swift in wisdom; a bhikṣu who, having heard, is swift in wisdom: Venerable Sir, by how much is such a bhikṣu called one who, having heard, is swift in wisdom? How many does the Tathāgata say make a bhikṣu one who, having heard, is swift in wisdom?"

"Excellent! Excellent! Bhikṣu! You have a noble path, noble vision, and good discernment. What you have said is good: 'Venerable Sir, a bhikṣu who, having heard, is swift in wisdom; a bhikṣu who, having heard, is swift in wisdom: World-Honored One, how many does the Tathāgata say make a bhikṣu one who, having heard, is swift in wisdom?' Bhikṣu, is this indeed the question you have asked?"

"Yes, indeed, World-Honored One!"

"Bhikṣu! When that bhikṣu has heard of duḥkha and, through wisdom, knows it as it truly is; the arising of duḥkha, the cessation of duḥkha, and the path leading to the cessation of duḥkha: this is what is called having heard. Through wisdom he knows as it truly is the arising of duḥkha, the cessation of duḥkha, and the path leading to the cessation of duḥkha: this is what is called having heard and knowing as it truly is through wisdom. In this way, bhikṣu, that bhikṣu who has heard is swift in wisdom. Thus does the Tathāgata speak of a bhikṣu as one who, having heard, is swift in wisdom."

"Excellent, World-Honored One!" That bhikṣu, having heard what the World-Honored One had said, was delighted and gave his approval. Then he again asked the World-Honored One: "Venerable Sir! A bhikṣu who is discerning, wise, and swift in eloquence; a bhikṣu who is discerning and swift in wisdom: Venerable Sir, by how much is such a bhikṣu called one who is discerning and swift in wisdom and eloquence? How many does the Tathāgata say make a bhikṣu one who is discerning and swift in wisdom and eloquence?"

"Excellent! Excellent! Bhikṣu! You have a noble path, noble vision, and good discernment. What you have said is good: 'Venerable Sir, a bhikṣu who is discerning and swift in wisdom; a bhikṣu who is discerning and swift in eloquence: World-Honored One, by how much is such a bhikṣu called one who is discerning and swift in wisdom and eloquence? How many does the Tathāgata say make a bhikṣu one who is discerning and swift in wisdom and eloquence?' Bhikṣu, is this indeed the question you have asked?"

"Yes, indeed, World-Honored One!"

"Bhikṣu! This refers to a bhikṣu who does not contemplate self, does not contemplate others, and does not contemplate both. That bhikṣu, having attended only to the mind itself, benefits himself, benefits others, and benefits many people. Out of compassion for the world, he benefits gods and humans through the meaning of the Dharma. Such a bhikṣu is one who is discerning and swift in wisdom. Thus does the Tathāgata speak of a bhikṣu as one who is discerning and swift in wisdom and eloquence."

"Excellent, World-Honored One!" That bhikṣu, having heard what the World-Honored One had said, reflected well upon it and held it in mind. Having received, upheld, recited, and studied it, he rose from his seat, paid homage at the feet of the World-Honored One, circumambulated the World-Honored One, and departed.

The World-Honored One having taught him with this illustration, that bhikṣu dwelt alone in a quiet place with an undistracted and tranquil mind. Having so dwelt alone in a quiet place with an undistracted and tranquil mind, that son of good family, for whose sake he had shaved his beard and hair, donned the kāṣāya robes, and with faith and joy gone forth from the household life to practice the Way, walked the supreme path of the holy life. He directly realized the Dharma, achieved supernormal knowledge, and dwelt in direct realization. Birth is exhausted, the holy life is fulfilled, what had to be done has been done; name-and-form is known as it truly is. That Venerable One, having come to know the Dharma, attained Arahantship.

Thus did the Buddha speak. That bhikṣu, having heard what the World-Honored One had said, was filled with joy and delight.
