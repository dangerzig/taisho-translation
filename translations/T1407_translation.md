# 佛說辟除諸惡陀羅尼經
## Sūtra Spoken by the Buddha on the Dhāraṇī for Warding Off All Evils

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1407

Translated from the Chinese. Song Dynasty, translated by Dharmabhadra (法賢)

---

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed Ānanda, saying: "I observe that when calamities and pestilences arise in the world, flying locusts and poisonous insects appear, and mosquitoes, horseflies, tigers, and wolves multiply in every region, damaging seedlings and crops or injuring vegetables and fruits, so that the land gradually falls into famine and sentient beings are afflicted without finding peace. I shall now proclaim to you the Dhāraṇī for Warding Off All Evils.

"If sentient beings uphold and recite it with single-minded intention, it will benefit not only themselves but others as well. If one who upholds and recites it wishes to perform the accomplishment ritual, one must first purify oneself and observe the precepts, arouse a mind of sincere truthfulness, and having recited the dhāraṇī one hundred thousand times until fully proficient, one may then perform the accomplishment ritual wherever one wishes. When performing the ritual, having again purified oneself, recite the names of the various buddhas and bodhisattvas, and entreat them for their empowerment and blessing. Then take a small amount of sand, place it in a clean vessel, recite the dhāraṇī eight hundred times to empower the sand, and scatter it at places where there are poisonous insects. All poisonous insects will thereby be entirely dispelled."

He then proclaimed the dhāraṇī:

*namaḥ sarva-buddha-bodhisattvā nādīṣāṃ | namaḥ kṛtvā | idaṃ viprayojayāmi | idaṃ visamṛddhatumi | tathā hā hā hā | hrī hrī hrī | dhvā dhvā dhvā dhvā | svā ya hi hi | kṛ kṛ | mṛ mṛ | praṇakānāṃ duṣṭān vandha-karomi svāhā | sarveṣāṃ maśakānāṃ | sarveṣāṃ maṅkuṇānāṃ | sarveṣāṃ paśukānāṃ | sarveṣāṃ aśvānāṃ | sarveṣāṃ mūṣakānāṃ | sarveṣāṃ śuka-śārikānāṃ | sarveṣāṃ sūkarānāṃ | sarveṣāṃ markaṭānāṃ | sarveṣāṃ maraṇānāṃ | sarveṣāṃ sarva-gātakānāṃ duṣṭān bandha-karomi svāhā*

At that time, Ānanda, having heard the World-Honored One proclaim this Dhāraṇī for Warding Off All Evils, rejoiced, received it with faith, bowed to the Buddha, and withdrew.
