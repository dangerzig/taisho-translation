# 三廚經
## Sūtra of the Three Celestial Kitchens

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 2894

Translated from the Chinese. Western Indian brāhmaṇa Dattara and Jñānagupta and others translated by imperial decree.

---

The first is the Kitchen of Compassionate Self-Awakening and Spontaneity. The second is the Kitchen of Non-Thought of Pratyekabuddhas and the Four Fruits of Śrāvakas. The third is the Kitchen of Neither Existence nor Non-Existence, the Divine Spontaneity. If one can recite these three kitchen methods, they cause a person not to hunger, and to abide in clarity and long life.

One must first have faith in and reverence for the Three Jewels, constantly worship the Three Jewels, and be diligent morning and evening in unbroken mindfulness. One should care for one's teachers, monastics, parents, and relatives, and be able to practice the six pāramitās without sparing one's body or life. Only then may one receive and transmit this method of the three kitchens. This is the spontaneous kitchen of all buddhas and bodhisattvas of the three times, the ever-abiding, serene, unmoving, compassionate kitchen of countless kalpas.

This method of the three kitchens was spoken by the Buddha as he approached nirvāṇa, before all buddhas and bodhisattvas of the ten directions, amid the great assembly of the eight classes. He spoke this Dharma of the ever-abiding, serene, unmoving, compassionate celestial kitchen of all buddhas of the past, present, and future. He then spoke verses:

**Verse of the East, Wood:**

> One vital breath harmonizes with the Great Harmony; attaining the One, the Way is all Great Harmony, which is the primordial One Harmony.
> The mysterious principle merges with the mysterious boundary.

This is the verse of long-life wood. Recite it ninety times.

**Verse of the South, Fire:**

> Do not think with intentional thought; also do not seek non-thought; with intention yet without further thought:
> Hold this matter just as it is.

This is the verse of non-heat fire. Recite it thirty times.

**Verse of the Center, Earth:**

> All food-breath congeals the breath; it is not that the various long-settled congealed breaths return to their original breath:
> Following accumulation, one should follow release.

This is the verse of non-hunger earth of the center. Recite it one hundred twenty times.

**Verse of the West, Metal:**

> Cultivate and order the will, order the will; accumulate cultivation without relying on burial; the will yet without cultivating the will:
> Completed karma has no completed wisdom.

This is the verse of non-cold metal. Recite it seventy times.

**Verse of the North, Water:**

> Do not lead the mind to conditions of mind; also do not abide in severing conditions; when the mind is present, do not preserve the mind:
> In truth, guard the true abyss.

This is the verse of non-thirst water. Recite it fifty times.

This compassionate kitchen was spoken by the Buddha as he approached nirvāṇa, before all buddhas and the great assembly of the eight classes of the ten directions. He spoke of this spontaneous three-kitchen of all buddhas and bodhisattvas of the three times, ever-abiding, neither hungering nor thirsting, neither cold nor hot, neither aging nor young: the serene, unmoving, spontaneous three kitchens of all the holy assembly.

If one can receive and uphold the name of even one celestial kitchen, or recite it daily without thinking of food and drink, one may go a hundred days without eating, or ten years, a hundred years, a thousand years, ten thousand years, or through kalpas as numerous as dust-motes, mindful of it, ever-abiding and unmoving, without thinking of food and drink, just as in the land of Amitābha Buddha's immeasurable life, with no difference whatsoever.

The Buddha told the great assembly: "Those who practice this method need only preserve in their hearts faith and reverence for the Three Jewels. Those who can enter the deep mountains to study the Way may then practice and circulate this method. If anyone generates doubt, disbelief, or slander, it must not be rashly transmitted, lest those who slander fall into the evil paths. Therefore it must not be recklessly transmitted in the world."

Recite the Eastern verse ninety times through to the Northern verse fifty times; this constitutes one complete recitation. If one is not yet satiated, recite again as above. It will not exceed two recitations before one is satiated, neither hungry nor thirsty. When reciting the verses, always begin at the hour of the tiger and continue until the period of settling, dividing into four sessions. The number of recitations is as stated above.

Also, when first beginning to recite, one should move the lips and turn the tongue, severing all else and reciting silently. When saliva descends, one may swallow it. From the first day through the third, the urine may be reddish in color; do not be alarmed. From the beginning of recitation through the third day, bodily strength becomes extremely weak. After passing these three days, one reaches the seventh day. After the warmth of seven days, bodily strength gradually increases. When the method is accomplished, one need not recite further. Within a hundred days, one will constantly dream of obtaining the finest flavors of the world and being satiated, and will also smell celestial fragrances. After three hundred days, the merit-power is fully complete. One may eat if one wishes to eat; if one does not eat, one will not hunger.

---

*The Sūtra of the Three Kitchens Spoken by the Buddha, in one fascicle.*

The names of the celestial kitchens are given in full in certain editions. The five-line verses in this edition are themselves the names of the celestial kitchens. This method was received orally from Master Gupta of the South. One utterance obtains earth; three utterances obtain fire; five utterances obtain water; seven utterances obtain metal; nine utterances obtain wood.

Eastern wood: ninety recitations. Southern fire: thirty recitations. Central earth: one hundred twenty recitations. Western metal: seventy recitations. Northern water: fifty recitations. From ninety through to fifty of the North constitutes one complete recitation. If not yet satiated, recite again as above. It will not exceed two recitations before one is certainly satiated.

All recitations should begin on a ren-day. Begin at the hour of the tiger and continue until the period of settling; this may be divided into four sessions. Recite as described above. Also, when first wishing to recite, one should clench and move the tongue, severing all else and reciting silently. When there is saliva, one should swallow it. Afterward, for intervals of two, three, or four days, one will urinate once. Do not be alarmed. This is simply because all sentient beings are constituted by the breath of yin and yang to form their bodies. Since one is now taking in the breath of the celestial kitchen, it can benefit the entire body, and thus it is so.

From the beginning of recitation through the third day, strength gradually weakens most. After passing that day, one reaches the seventh day. From then onward, accomplishment is certain beyond doubt. Within a hundred days, one constantly dreams of obtaining the finest flavors and food and drink of the world. After a hundred days, one dreams of obtaining the food and drink of the heavens. The nose constantly smells the fragrance of celestial food. After three hundred days are complete, merit and virtue are fully perfected, and one attains the fruit of the Way.

May you only be diligent, sever the three poisons, have compassion for sentient beings, take refuge in and revere the Three Jewels, and constantly be mindful of Amitābha Buddha. Within one year, you will certainly see the Buddha. This is determined beyond doubt.

---

The Tripiṭaka master orally said: "Those who seek unsurpassed bodhi with single-minded concentration in meditation are only troubled by excessive eating, which obstructs the cultivation of dhyāna and prevents attainment of the fruit of the Way."

First, according to the *Divination of Karmic Retribution Sūtra*, after repenting one's transgressions, one should be in mountain forests, quiet valleys, or a secluded room. First sit in full lotus, place the left hand upon the right, close both eyes, bring the upper and lower teeth together, and sit upright, harmonizing the body. Initially, follow the four sessions of the hours of the hare, rooster, rat, and horse, facing the corresponding direction. Concentrate the mind and recite silently, as if in the navel region. Do not let the mind chase external objects.

From ninety recitations through to fifty, if not yet satiated, it will not exceed two recitations before one is satiated. During recitation, from time to time use the tongue to break the seal of the external saliva and swallow it.

For non-hunger and non-thirst, recite each verse one by one, during the intervals of the hours of the rooster, rat, and horse, reciting constantly. After eating this three-kitchen food for seven days, one will know whether the method has been accomplished, whether one has been sincere, whether one has merit, whether one is free of transgressions, whether one has attained dhyāna concentration, and whether one has reached the fruit of the Way.

If one recites these verses three hundred sixty times, one will not hunger.

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke the dhāraṇī:

*Nazhinu nazhinu suohē wunazhì móhē wunazhì suōhē*

For the above method, when receiving and upholding it, one must wear clean and pure clothing. In a quiet room, burn various fine incenses as offerings to all worthies and sages. Then recite the above method twenty-one times. To cross over one lifetime requires earnest devotion. For seven days, one must not speak with others. One must be extremely cautious. One must not see women or domestic animals. If parched, one may drink cow's milk along with dates and beverages.

If one wishes to know the names of the kitchen spirits, I shall now speak them:

*Namo shíjié mòsì pó namo mòjiā mózhìnà namo zhībōluó tìsǐ*

At dawn, facing the chen direction, the earth will resound like heavenly drums. On the fourteenth day, one will be fed with jade nectar.

All good men and good women who wish to practice this method of the three kitchens should first observe the purificatory precepts with sincere mind, burn priceless fine incenses with sincere mind, and silently recite the above names of the kitchen spirits. Perform obeisance and repentance, begging for their aid and protection. Perform forty-nine prostrations for the kitchen spirits. When this is done, one will clearly discern their forms and names. Then one may recite the verses.

If one simply recites the verses for one hundred days, one will not hunger for one hundred days. If one seeks the conditions for transcending the world and swift realization of the four fruits, one must receive and uphold the names of the kitchen spirits. Worldly people do not know this; having obtained the spirit-names by mouth, their minds become disrespectful. If the mind treats the supreme method with contempt, the method will never be accomplished.

> The four wu and four ji make the intestines firm and sealed; the four ren and four gui eliminate the need for food.
> The six jia and six ding fill the great storehouse; the five geng and five xin make the five viscera steadfast.

> Immortals and jade maidens serve my spirit; celestial officials prepare kitchens to nourish my body.
> They cause my complexion to be always full and radiant, extending my years and increasing my lifespan by tens of thousands of years.

The dhāraṇī spoken by the Buddha for summoning the kitchen and the three foods:

*Namo hēluódáná dáluōyè nǎ dòuliú dòuliú móhē dòuliú ǎn jísù hēní shè ǎn jísù hēní shè*

The separate practice for this dhāraṇī: proceed to a secluded, uninhabited place. At dawn, wash the bowl with clean water. Use a willow branch to brush the inside of the bowl. Take a handful of clean grass and spread a clean cloth upon it. Place the bowl face-down upon it, facing east. Sit in full lotus, close the eyes, press the tongue against the upper palate, and silently recite the above dhāraṇī one hundred eight times. Open the eyes and look: the food will have arrived, and one may eat freely. If there is food remaining, recite the previous dhāraṇī seven times, also with eyes closed, mentally thinking: "May the remaining food return to its original place." It will then depart.

*The Sūtra of the Three Kitchens Spoken by the Buddha, in one fascicle. Supplementary method for the three kitchens, the grain-family method. The following method was separately obtained from a brāhmaṇa, who brought the essential supplement for the three kitchens.*

> Avalokiteśvara bestows upon me the Dharma; the immortals grant me sustenance. The matter accords with the colors of the five directions:
> Blue, black, red, white, and yellow. Harmoniously blending, one obtains nourishment; all dust is thereby warded off.

Then he spoke the dhāraṇī:

*Namo fo jìn namo fǎ jìn namo sēng jìn namo suōlì suō jìn suōhē*

*Rú jiàn rú rú bù rú rú jiàn yǒu fēi kōng yǒu fēi zì yǒu fēi tā yǒu suōhē*

This dhāraṇī is for when evil māras come to trouble a person and create obstacles and difficulties.
