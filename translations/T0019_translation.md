Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0019

Translated from the Chinese. 宋 法天譯

## Fascicle 1

### Sūtra of the Great Assembly, Spoken by the Buddha

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was dwelling in the Kapila Grove, together with a great assembly of bhikṣus, all of them arhants in whom all outflows had been extinguished, their deeds accomplished, their own benefit attained, all fetters of existence severed, their minds free — five thousand five hundred in all.

At that time, the Śakras and Brahmās of the ten directions, together with gods of great majesty and virtue, surrounded with reverence by their retinues, their bodies radiant and majestic, blazing with luminous splendor, came to the Kapila Grove, approached the World-Honored One, and with forehead touching his feet paid homage, then stood to one side. At that time, four great Brahmā kings each proclaimed the Buddha's virtues in verse. The first Brahmā king spoke this verse:

> "At this great assembly, the wondrous sound of Dharma is proclaimed;
> My Buddha, Invincible, is attended by gods and men gathered everywhere."

The second Brahmā king spoke this verse:

> "Through vast kalpas of asaṃkhyeya, he cultivated deep and unwavering faith,
> Guarding the sense faculties — the eye and the rest — dwelling in none of the sense objects."

The third Brahmā king spoke this verse:

> "Precepts, meditation, wisdom — genuine and true, pure and free from defilement;
> Like the vajra of Indra, firm and indestructible."

The fourth Brahmā king spoke this verse:

> "Whoever takes refuge in the Buddha will never fall into evil destinies;
> When that person's life comes to its end, swiftly they will be reborn in the heavens."

At that time, the World-Honored One, with his pure divine eye, universally surveyed the great assembly — the multitude of humans and gods, immeasurable and innumerable — and addressed the assembly of bhikṣus: "The Tathāgatas of the past, the Worthy Ones, the Perfectly Enlightened, assembled the multitudes of humans and gods and expounded the Dharma for them, even as I do now. Today I gather together humans and gods universally, wishing to expound the Dharma. You should receive and uphold it. Those who are courageous, resolute and fearless, like a lion, firm in deep faith without attachment — throughout the great earth, the mountains, even unto the Brahmā world — all shall attain nirvāṇa." When this Dharma was proclaimed, another seventeen hundred gods and humans in the stage of learning, and innumerable further gods, blazing with luminous splendor, came to where the Buddha was. The Buddha told the bhikṣus: "Listen well. I observe those gods who have arrived: they should be delivered by the Dharma delightful to śrāvakas."

At that time, seven thousand great yakṣas, endowed with great supernormal powers and majestic virtue, blazing with luminous splendor, surrounded with reverence by their retinues, came to the Kapila Grove.

Also, six thousand great yakṣas dwelling on the Golden Mountain, endowed with great supernormal powers and majestic virtue, blazing with luminous splendor, surrounded with reverence by their retinues, came to the Kapila Grove.

Also, three thousand great yakṣas dwelling on Mount Sāda, endowed with great supernormal powers and majestic virtue, blazing with luminous splendor, surrounded with reverence by their retinues, came to the Kapila Grove.

Also, Kumbhīra and a hundred thousand yakṣas dwelling on Vipula Mountain at Rājagṛha, endowed with great supernormal powers and majestic virtue, blazing with luminous splendor, surrounded with reverence by their retinues, came to the Kapila Grove.

Also, the yakṣa generals Śvāmimitra, Pañcaśikha, Viśvameva, and others, endowed with great supernormal powers and majestic virtue, blazing with luminous splendor, together with their retinues, came to the Kapila Grove.

Also, the Guardian King of the East, lord of the gandharvas, named Dhṛtarāṣṭra, endowed with great supernormal powers and immeasurable majestic virtue, his body of wondrous appearance, blazing with radiant splendor, surrounded with reverence by his retinue, came to the Kapila Grove to assemble and hear the Dharma. The Guardian King of the South, lord of the kumbhāṇḍas, named Virūḍhaka, endowed with great supernormal powers and immeasurable majestic virtue, his body of wondrous appearance, blazing with radiant splendor, surrounded with reverence by his retinue, came to the Kapila Grove to assemble and hear the Dharma. The Guardian King of the West, great lord of the nāgas, named Virūpākṣa, endowed with great supernormal powers and immeasurable majestic virtue, his body of wondrous appearance, blazing with radiant splendor, surrounded with reverence by his retinue, came to the Kapila Grove to assemble and hear the Dharma. The Guardian King of the North, great lord of the yakṣas, named Kubera, endowed with great supernormal powers and immeasurable majestic virtue, his body of wondrous appearance, blazing with radiant splendor, surrounded with reverence by his retinue, came to the Kapila Grove to assemble and hear the Dharma. Furthermore, the spirits attending the four great heavenly kings — namely Māyā, Kāci, Vikṛti, Baligu, Balikuci, and others — all possessing supernormal powers and might, their forms coarse and fearsome with pride and ignorance, undergoing various transformations, surrounded with reverence by their retinues, came to the Kapila Grove to assemble and hear the Dharma.

Also, yakṣa spirit generals — namely Indra, Movaruṇa, Prajāpati, Bharadvāja, Īśāna, Candana, Kāma, Śrīṣṭha, Kuṇikaṇṭha, Nikaṇṭha, Maṇimaṇimaṇicara, Prāṇana, Upapañcaka, Sātagiri, Hemavata-Pūrṇakhenira-Kuviṭagupara-yakṣa, Aṭavikuṇāra-rāja, Nāriṣabhacittrasena, Gandharvā, Dhirghaśatimātali, Pañcaragandasumomanamirgu — together with their retinues, Triphalā, Trikaṇṭakā, and all those who walk in the world, all possessed of supernormal powers and majestic virtue, blazing with luminous splendor, surrounding with reverence, came to the Kapila Grove to assemble and hear the Dharma.

Also, the great venomous nāgas — namely Takṣaka, Karmaraśvatairu, Prāpta, Prajāgu, Sāgunasahasūna-saku, Dhṛtarāṣṭra, Kucura, Airāvaṇī, and other nāgas — fierce and violently malevolent, endowed with great supernormal powers and majestic virtue, blazing with radiant splendor, surrounded with reverence by their retinues, came to the Kapila Grove to assemble and hear the Dharma.

Also, the garuḍas named Abhaya, together with the birds with pure eyes and other flying creatures and their retinues, likewise endowed with supernormal powers and majestic virtue, blazing with luminous splendor, surrounding with reverence, came to the Kapila Grove to assemble and hear the Dharma.

Also, the Invincible Vajrapāṇi, those dwelling within the sea, all the fearsome yakṣas, together with their retinues, all endowed with supernormal powers and majestic virtue, blazing with luminous splendor, surrounding with reverence, came to the Kapila Grove to assemble and hear the Dharma.

Also, the assembly of asuras — namely Vimacitra, Sucitra, Prahlāda, Namuci, together with a hundred Māli asura sons, and all their retinues — endowed with great supernormal powers and immeasurable majestic virtue, blazing with luminous splendor, surrounding with reverence, came to the Kapila Grove to assemble and hear the Dharma.

Also, the gods of the Four Great Heavenly Kings, the gods of Trāyastriṃśa, the gods of Yāma, the gods of Tuṣita, the gods of Nirmāṇarati, the gods of Paranirmitavaśavartin, and the various assemblies of gods, all endowed with supernormal powers and majestic virtue, blazing with luminous splendor, surrounding with reverence, came to the Kapila Grove to assemble and hear the Dharma.

Also, the named gods — namely the god Varuṇa, the god Vāruṇī, the god Soma, the god Pṛṣṭha, the god Āgama, the god Mitravāruṇī, the Earth God, the Water God, the Fire God, the Wind God — these ten gods, endowed with great majestic virtue, manifesting supernormal transformations, together with their retinues, blazing with luminous splendor, surrounding with reverence, came to the Kapila Grove to assemble and hear the Dharma.

Also, the god Nārāyaṇa, the god Sahārikara, the Sun God, the Moon God, the Constellation God, the god Indra, the god Maghavā, the supremely honored god of all sages and worthies, together with the gods of two retinues — these ten gods, endowed with great supernormal powers and immeasurable majestic virtue, blazing with luminous splendor, surrounding with reverence, came to the Kapila Grove to assemble and hear the Dharma.

Also, various goddesses — namely the goddess Sāhikā, the goddess Jvalā, the goddess Ariṣṭā, the goddess Sumā, the goddess Uttamā, the goddess Puṣpavasinī, the goddess Cāraka, the goddess Subhadrā, the goddess Kakṣācarāyuta, the goddess Prādyumna, Sānikā, and others — innumerable goddesses, together with their retinues, endowed with great supernormal powers and immeasurable majestic virtue, their forms majestic and dignified, blazing with luminous splendor, surrounding with reverence, came to the Kapila Grove to assemble and hear the Dharma.

Also, great yakṣa women — namely the yakṣiṇī Śāmā, the yakṣiṇī Mahāśāmā, the yakṣiṇī Mānuṣā, the yakṣiṇī Mānuṣottamā, the yakṣiṇī Kṛṣṇā, the yakṣiṇī Pramuṣā, the yakṣiṇī Mānapradūṣikā, the yakṣiṇī Mārāhā, the yakṣiṇī Mahāmārā, the yakṣiṇī Bhikṣumuṇikā — these ten great yakṣa women, likewise endowed with supernormal powers and majestic virtue, blazing with luminous splendor, surrounding with reverence by their retinues, came to the Kapila Grove to assemble and hear the Dharma.

Also, ten more great yakṣa women — namely the yakṣiṇī Śuklā, the yakṣiṇī Karṇamālā, the yakṣiṇī Kāruṇā, the yakṣiṇī Nīlakavasinī, the yakṣiṇī Āvanatakeśā, the yakṣiṇī Pramukhakṣiptakavasinī, the yakṣiṇī Sānamātā, the yakṣiṇī Hārītī, the yakṣiṇī Rucikā — these and such yakṣa women, likewise endowed with supernormal powers and majestic virtue, blazing with luminous splendor, surrounding with reverence by their retinues, came to the Kapila Grove to assemble and hear the Dharma.

Also, Hārītī together with her retinue of boy and girl attendants, likewise endowed with majestic virtue and supernormal powers, blazing with luminous splendor, surrounding with reverence by their retinues, came to the Kapila Grove to assemble and hear the Dharma.

Thus the Brahmā kings and Indras of the ten directions, the gods, humans, and the eight classes of beings, the great bhikṣus — immeasurable and innumerable — all came and gathered in assembly.

At that time, within the assembly there was a great dark spirit named Cudūna, endowed with great supernormal powers, fierce and violently malevolent, tormenting and harming humans and gods, obstructing the cultivation of wholesome deeds. He struck the ground with his hand, sending forth a great terrible sound, and in the sky above conjured great clouds of wind, hail, thunder, and lightning — every manner of evil omen — so that the multitude was seized with terror. The Buddha at once contemplated the situation and proclaimed: "Hear the Dharma." When the māra heard this, he took refuge and ceased his malice, and together with the bhikṣus dwelt within the śrāvaka vehicle. At this moment, the assembled multitude, witnessing the Buddha subdue the māra, leapt for joy, received the teaching in faith, and reverently practiced it.
