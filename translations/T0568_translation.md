# 有德女所問大乘經
## Mahāyāna Sūtra on the Questions of the Virtuous Girl

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0568

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Bodhiruci (菩提流志)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the land of Vārāṇasī, in the Deer Park where the sages dwell, together with a great assembly of five hundred bhikṣus and countless bodhisattva mahāsattvas.

At that time the World-Honored One, at the time for eating, put on his robe, took his bowl, and together with the Bodhisattva Mahāsattva Ajita entered the great city of Vārāṇasī to beg for food. In the city, begging in order from house to house, they came to the home of the Virtuous Brahmin Girl.

The Virtuous Girl, seeing from afar the Tathāgata's dignified form and excellent features, his faculties perfectly still, his mind serene and supremely unmatched, his bearing and gaze measured and composed, she saw he was like a dragon king of great majestic virtue, like a pillar of pure gold standing firm and unmoving, like a clear pure pool radiant and free of all impurity, unmoved by joy or sorrow like one in the fourth dhyāna, his body radiating light that penetrated within and without.

The Virtuous Girl, seeing these qualities, gave rise to pure faith. She came to the Buddha's presence, bowed at the feet of the Tathāgata and the Bodhisattva Mahāsattva Ajita, and with respectful body and joined palms facing the Buddha, she said: "Welcome, World-Honored One! Welcome, Well-Gone One! I now wish to ask about my doubts. I humbly beg your compassion; please stay here a moment."

The World-Honored One silently accepted the invitation, and a seat was spread for him. He sat down, and the Bodhisattva Mahāsattva Ajita also sat with the Buddha.

Then the Virtuous Brahmin Girl's heart was filled with joy. With even greater reverence, she said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I have heard that the Tathāgata, in the Deer Park where the sages dwell in Vārāṇasī, has turned the wondrous Dharma wheel. I do not yet know what Dharma the World-Honored One has taught in turning the Dharma wheel."

The Buddha told her: "Virtuous Girl! In turning the Dharma wheel, I taught: ignorance conditions formations; formations condition consciousness; consciousness conditions name-and-form; name-and-form conditions the six sense-bases; the six sense-bases condition contact; contact conditions feeling; feeling conditions craving; craving conditions grasping; grasping conditions becoming; becoming conditions birth; and birth conditions old age, death, sorrow, lamentation, suffering, and distress. When ignorance ceases, formations cease. When formations cease, consciousness ceases. When consciousness ceases, name-and-form ceases. When name-and-form ceases, the six sense-bases cease. When the six sense-bases cease, contact ceases. When contact ceases, feeling ceases. When feeling ceases, craving ceases. When craving ceases, grasping ceases. When grasping ceases, becoming ceases. When becoming ceases, birth ceases. When birth ceases, old age, death, sorrow, lamentation, suffering, and distress cease. Virtuous Girl! This is what the Tathāgata taught in the Deer Park where the sages dwell in Vārāṇasī when turning the Dharma wheel. In all the world, whether among śramaṇas, brahmins, devas, māras, or Brahmā, there is none who can turn it as the Dharma prescribes."

Then the Virtuous Brahmin Girl said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! You speak of ignorance. Is it inside? Or is it outside?"

The Buddha said: "No."

The Virtuous Girl said: "World-Honored One! If there is no ignorance either inside or outside, how can there be formations conditioned by ignorance? Furthermore, World-Honored One, does a dharma from another life come to this present life or not?"

The Buddha said: "No."

The Virtuous Girl said again to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! Is the active characteristic of ignorance something that truly exists?"

The Buddha said: "No. The self-nature of ignorance arises from deluded discrimination. It does not arise from what is real. It arises from inversion. It does not arise from correct reasoning."

The Virtuous Girl said again to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! If that is so, then there is no ignorance. How then can formations arise and give rise to suffering in the cycle of birth and death? World-Honored One! If a tree has no roots, then there are no branches, leaves, flowers, or fruits. Likewise, since ignorance has no self-nature, the arising of formations and so forth is assuredly impossible."

The Buddha said: "Virtuous Girl! All dharmas are ultimately empty. Foolish ordinary beings are confused and inverted. They do not hear the meaning of emptiness, or even if they hear it, they lack the wisdom to understand it. Because of this, they create all manner of karmic deeds. Once there are karmic deeds, the various states of existence arise. Within those states of existence, they endure all manner of suffering. In the ultimate truth, there are no karmic deeds, nor are there any states of existence arising from karma, nor the various kinds of suffering.

"Virtuous Girl! The Tathāgata, the Arhat, the Samyaksaṃbuddha, accords with the world and broadly teaches the Dharma to all beings, wishing them to awaken to the ultimate truth. Virtuous Girl! The ultimate truth too has its name established in accordance with the world. Why? Because in the realm of true meaning, the one who awakens, what is awakened, all of it without exception cannot be grasped.

"Virtuous Girl! It is like the Buddhas creating an illusory person. This illusory person then further creates various illusory things. The illusory person is false and unreal, and the illusory things too have no true substance. This is likewise so: all karmic deeds that are created are false and unreal, and the existence that arises from karma is also without true substance."

Then the Virtuous Girl said again to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! As I understand the meaning of the Buddha's teaching, the Dharma wheel that the Tathāgata has now turned is the Dharma wheel of empty space, the Dharma wheel of the nature of emptiness, the Dharma wheel of liberation, the Dharma wheel of penetration, the inconceivable Dharma wheel, the Dharma wheel that none can turn, the unequaled Dharma wheel, the Dharma wheel of reality, the unborn Dharma wheel, the Dharma wheel without self-nature, the markless Dharma wheel. World-Honored One! The Tathāgata has already turned this Dharma wheel." Having spoken thus, she took sandalwood powder in both hands and scattered it upon the Buddha's feet, saying: "World-Honored One! I vow that by the power of this root of goodness, in a future life I may turn these very kinds of Dharma wheels."

At that time the World-Honored One gently smiled, and from his mouth he emitted various kinds of light. The light was brilliant and contained many colors. It pervaded the ten directions and countless worlds, filling each one completely. Then it returned to the Buddha, circled him three times on the right, and entered through the crown of his head.

At that time the Bodhisattva Mahāsattva Ajita said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! What causes and conditions bring about this sudden gentle smile from the Tathāgata? As I understand it, it is not without cause."

The Buddha told him: "Ajita! Did you see this brahmin girl take sandalwood powder in her hands and scatter it upon my feet?"

He replied: "I have seen it."

The Buddha said: "Because of the root of goodness this girl has now planted, for eighty-four thousand billion kalpas she shall not fall into the evil destinies. In the presence of sixty-four thousand Buddhas, with a mind of reverence she shall serve and make offerings and hear the true Dharma, guarding, receiving, and upholding it. While those Buddhas are in the world and after their nirvāṇa, throughout that time, in unbroken succession, she shall also cause immeasurable incalculable numbers of beings to turn toward awakening. Thereafter, in this great trichiliocosm, in the kalpa called Radiance, she shall become a Buddha named Dharmaprabhāsa Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha. That Buddha's lifespan shall be a full kalpa, and she shall teach and transform immeasurable incalculable numbers of beings, leading them to nirvāṇa."

At that time the Bodhisattva Mahāsattva Ajita said again to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! In what past life did this Virtuous Brahmin Girl plant these roots of goodness?"

The Buddha said: "Ajita! You should know that in a past life, when the Buddha Vipaśyin appeared in the world, this Virtuous Girl was already in a female body. Seeking anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi, she removed the necklace from her body and offered it to that Buddha.

"When the Tathāgata Śikhin appeared in the world, in his presence she asked about the profound meaning. She offered fine garments and, in his Dharma, left the household to study the Way, cultivating pure conduct for a full thousand years.

"When the Buddha Viśvabhū appeared in the world, she prepared all manner of excellent food and drink and for half a month made offerings to that Buddha and the śrāvaka assembly, providing whatever was needed without lack.

"When the Buddha Krakucchanda appeared in the world, she scattered atimuktaka flowers upon that Buddha as an offering, received the five precepts, and upheld them without flaw.

"When the Buddha Kanakamuni appeared in the world, she vowed to offer various food and drink, garments, bedding, medicine, and other necessities for the body throughout the Buddha's entire lifespan. At that time, that Buddha for two months accepted her food, drink, leather sandals, and other bodily provisions.

"When the Tathāgata Kāśyapa appeared in the world, she again scattered golden flowers upon the Buddha as an offering. All that she did was for the sake of attaining anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi. She also said: 'I vow that by these roots of goodness from making offerings, I may swiftly receive the prediction of anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi. Until I receive the prediction, I shall never wish to abandon the body of a woman.' Ajita! The roots of goodness this Virtuous Brahmin Girl planted in past lives are as I have described. This is the very last time she shall receive the retribution of being born as a woman."

When the Buddha had finished speaking this sūtra, the Bodhisattva Mahāsattva Ajita, the Virtuous Brahmin Girl, and all in the world, including the devas, humans, and asuras, were all filled with great joy. They received the teaching with faith and put it into practice.
