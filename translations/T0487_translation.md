# 離垢慧菩薩所問禮佛法經
## Sūtra on the Method of Worshipping the Buddha as Asked by Vimalamati Bodhisattva

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0487

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Puṇyodaya (那提)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the city of Śrāvastī, in the Jetavana of the Grove of Supreme Virtue, at the garden of Anāthapiṇḍada, together with five hundred great bhikṣus, and innumerable bodhisattvas. There were also immeasurable brāhmaṇas, vaiśyas, śūdras, and elders, each the leader of their assembly, who came with their followers to the Buddha's presence. There were also devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, and mahoragas, at the great Dharma assembly, surrounding him on all sides.

At that time, among the assembly there was a bodhisattva-mahāsattva named Vimalamati, who rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, placed his right knee on the ground, joined his palms with reverence, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! I wish to ask a small question. May you kindly grant me permission."

The Buddha said: "Ask freely whatever you wish; I shall answer as you desire."

Vimalamati Bodhisattva, having heard the Buddha's permission, rejoiced immeasurably and addressed the Buddha: "If good men and good women, before the Tathāgata, how should they pay reverence, prostrate, and make offerings?"

The Buddha said: "Vimalamati! Excellent! Excellent! You have great compassion, benefiting and bringing peace and happiness to all devas and humans. Listen well, and I shall explain it for you.

"If good men and good women wish to perform acts of reverent worship before the Buddha, they should first make vows, speaking these words: 'I now sincerely prostrate to all the Buddhas of the ten directions, universally entering into all supreme dharmas. I now perform the five-limb prostration before the Buddha, in order to sever the five paths of rebirth, to be free from the five coverings. May all sentient beings always abide in the unbreakable five supernatural powers and be complete in the five eyes. May the moment my right knee touches the ground cause all sentient beings to attain the path of true enlightenment. May the moment my left knee touches the ground cause all sentient beings, regarding the dharmas of non-Buddhist paths, to not give rise to wrong views, and all to be established in the path of true enlightenment. May the moment my right hand touches the ground be like the World-Honored One sitting upon the vajra seat, his right hand touching the earth, shaking and manifesting auspicious signs, realizing great bodhi. So too may I now, together with all sentient beings, equally realize the path of awakening. May the moment my left hand touches the ground cause all non-Buddhists who are difficult to subdue to be gathered in by the four means of attraction, causing them to enter the correct Dharma. May the moment the crown of my head touches the ground cause all sentient beings to abandon the mind of arrogance, and all to accomplish the uṣṇīṣa mark that is invisible from above.' Vimalamati! These are the characteristics of the five-limb prostration.

"Next, in paying homage to all the Buddhas presently existing in the ten directions, one should speak these words:

"'Namo to Akṣobhya Tathāgata in the east. And to all the Tathāgatas of the immeasurable worlds of that direction, to all the great Dharma-treasuries, and to all the bodhisattvas, śrāvakas, pratyekabuddhas, and all the noble ones.

"'Namo to Ratnaketu Tathāgata in the south. And to all the Tathāgatas of the immeasurable worlds of that direction, to all the great Dharma-treasuries, and to all the bodhisattvas, śrāvakas, pratyekabuddhas, and all the noble ones.

"'Namo to Amitāyus Tathāgata in the west. And to all the Tathāgatas of the immeasurable worlds of that direction, to all the great Dharma-treasuries, and to all the bodhisattvas, śrāvakas, pratyekabuddhas, and all the noble ones.

"'Namo to Dundubhisvara Tathāgata in the north. And to all the Tathāgatas of the immeasurable worlds of that direction, to all the great Dharma-treasuries, and to all the bodhisattvas, śrāvakas, pratyekabuddhas, and all the noble ones.

"'Namo to Indraketudvajarāja Tathāgata in the southeast. And to all the Tathāgatas of the immeasurable worlds of that direction, to all the great Dharma-treasuries, and to all the bodhisattvas, śrāvakas, pratyekabuddhas, and all the noble ones.

"'Namo to Ratnapadavikrāmin Tathāgata in the southwest. And to all the Tathāgatas of the immeasurable worlds of that direction, to all the great Dharma-treasuries, and to all the bodhisattvas, śrāvakas, pratyekabuddhas, and all the noble ones.

"'Namo to Śālendrarāja Tathāgata in the northwest. And to all the Tathāgatas of the immeasurable worlds of that direction, to all the great Dharma-treasuries, and to all the bodhisattvas, śrāvakas, pratyekabuddhas, and all the noble ones.

"'Namo to Amitadhvajarāja Tathāgata in the northeast. And to all the Tathāgatas of the immeasurable worlds of that direction, to all the great Dharma-treasuries, and to all the bodhisattvas, śrāvakas, pratyekabuddhas, and all the noble ones.

"'Namo to Jñānaprabha Tathāgata above. And to all the Tathāgatas of the immeasurable worlds of that direction, to all the great Dharma-treasuries, and to all the bodhisattvas, śrāvakas, pratyekabuddhas, and all the noble ones.

"'Namo to Vairocana Tathāgata below. And to all the Tathāgatas of the immeasurable worlds of that direction, to all the great Dharma-treasuries, and to all the bodhisattvas, śrāvakas, pratyekabuddhas, and all the noble ones.

"'Next, I also take refuge in Śākyamuni Tathāgata, the original teacher of the sahā world. And to all the Tathāgatas of the immeasurable worlds of the ten directions, to all the great Dharma-treasuries, and to the bodhisattva-mahāsattvas who have entered the bhūmis, the śrāvakas, pratyekabuddhas, and all the noble ones.'

"One should also say: 'I now single-mindedly prostrate to all the above Buddhas, the Dharma, and all the noble assemblies. May they bear witness and acknowledge me. From this day until I attain bodhi, I constantly practice taking refuge in the Buddha, the World-Honored One, the one of great compassion, the one of omniscience, the one of complete vision and knowledge, the one who has abandoned all fears, the great lion among humans, the great nāga king, the great seer among humans, the great man, the one of universal knowledge, the one of inconceivable body, the unsurpassed body, the unequaled body, the body not shared with the two vehicles, the pure dharma-body, the most honored and supreme among all assemblies. I now sincerely, with single mind, take refuge.' Taking refuge in this way a hundred times, a thousand times, a hundred thousand times, and even immeasurable and innumerable times, to the end of the future, with utter sincerity to the bone and marrow, I take refuge in all the Buddhas, World-Honored Ones, as described above.

"One should also say: 'I now, with the roots of merit of my three karmas of body, speech, and mind, together with all sentient beings, take refuge in the Buddha and am never separated from the Buddha. Thus I take refuge in the one who sits at the seat of enlightenment, the one who is eternally abiding, constant, and unchanging, the one without aging, death, or deterioration, the one without abiding and without conditions, whose nature is quiescent, the one who abides in the Dharma-house and serves as a great protector and refuge, as an island and haven, as a reliance and support, the one who has realized nirvāṇa, the one who abides in the most supreme among all dharmas. I now sincerely, earnestly, and respectfully take refuge in the correct Dharma of all such Buddhas.' One should again, as above, sincerely take refuge in the bodhisattvas and others who abide in the stages, from the Joyful Ground up to the Cloud of Dharma, in the Sangha of bodhisattvas abiding in the ten bhūmis.

"Next one should confess, speaking these words: 'May all the Buddhas, World-Honored Ones, of the ten directions, bear witness, be mindful, and compassionately receive my confession. The three kinds of bodily karma: killing, stealing, and sexual misconduct. The four kinds of verbal karma: false speech, harsh speech, divisive speech, and idle chatter. The three kinds of mental karma: craving, aversion, and delusion. Whether done by myself, instructed others to do, or seen done and rejoiced in: all these ten evils I now confess entirely.' One should further reflect: 'Since beginningless time, transmigrating through the six paths of rebirth, I have deceived and harmed sentient beings in various ways: with large measures and heavy scales taking others' goods for my own use, and with light scales and small measures giving to others; falsely adorning gold garments and secretly administering poison, all for the purpose of harming. All this I now confess. If I have slandered the three vehicles, falsely taught the Dharma and precepts, looked down upon the Three Treasures, deceived and deluded my parents, or before elders, preceptors, teachers, and the aged failed to show reverence: all such past wrongdoings I now confess entirely. Present evil karma I sincerely expose and reveal. What has not yet been done, I dare not do again. Now, before all the Buddhas, bodhisattvas, the greatest assembly leaders, the incomparable, the unsurpassed, the unequaled, I expose and confess, not daring to conceal. Having confessed once, I will never again commit these deeds.' Thus confessing, a second and third time, as described above.

"Next one should exhort, speaking these words: 'All the Buddhas of the ten directions, those who have not yet turned the Dharma-wheel and those who wish to enter nirvāṇa: I exhort and invite them all. May they please abide for a long time, for immeasurable kalpas, out of compassion for sentient beings, raining the great rain of Dharma, turning the correct Dharma-wheel, and not entering parinirvāṇa.'

"Next one should rejoice, speaking these words: 'As numerous as the sands of the Ganges, all the noble ones of the three vehicles in the ten directions, and all sentient beings who practice the six pāramitās and the dharmas assisting bodhi: I rejoice in them all.'

"Next one should dedicate, speaking these words: 'All the deeds performed by all the Buddhas of the three times in the ten directions, and the six pāramitās practiced by all the bodhisattvas, śrāvakas, and pratyekabuddhas, all of which they have dedicated toward supreme bodhi: I too thus dedicate toward the Buddha's path.'

"Next one should make vows: 'Looking up to all the Buddhas and bodhisattvas of the three times in the ten directions, all of whom have made great vows, to the ends of space, pervading the dharma-realm, flowing their transformations everywhere for sentient beings, with nothing in the three realms left unattended. May all sentient beings be benefited, matured in happiness, complete in good conduct, and abide in great nirvāṇa, all now manifest before them. I too make such great vows of adornment. May I, upon the unsurpassed path, have a mind that is never scattered, always see the Buddhas, always hear the correct Dharma, and attend to and practice it without any time passing in vain. May the wholesome dharmas I perform and the bodhi-mind never be lost. Wherever I am born, may I make offerings to the noble assembly, teach and transform sentient beings, attain the unsurpassed path, and turn the correct Dharma-wheel, complete in great spiritual powers. May all sentient beings likewise cultivate and study, reaching the stage of non-retrogression.

"'Furthermore, may all sentient beings swiftly sever all suffering, quickly realize nirvāṇa, and abide in the Tathāgata's wisdom. Having myself transcended birth and death and awakened to all, may I also cause sentient beings to transcend birth and death, be liberated from afflictions, and awaken to all. May all the Buddhas of the ten directions bear witness that I practice the bodhisattva path and have raised this ocean of vows.'"

Then the World-Honored One spoke again in verse:

> May I, wherever I am born,
> Receiving form according to karma,
> Attain a great man's body,
> With faith and all faculties complete.

> Mastering skills, arts, and abilities,
> Broadly understanding all teachings,
> Completely abandoning worldly affairs,
> Far removed from all desires.

> With right speech abiding in Dharma-mindfulness,
> Adorning the bodhi-mind,
> Respectfully serving good spiritual friends,
> Honored and esteemed by all beings.

> With precepts and deportment fully complete,
> Receiving joyful rewards through pure mindfulness,
> Always fearing evil karma,
> Treading the path of wholesome dharmas.

> Always relying on the practice of the ten perfections,
> Thus attaining bodhi,
> And even to the very last body,
> Always bringing happiness to sentient beings.

> Among jewels, the maṇi is supreme,
> Able to accomplish the benefit of others.
> May I, to the end of the future,
> Benefit without cease.

The Buddha told Vimalamati Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva: "If sentient beings practice the bodhisattva path, they should cultivate and study according to what I have taught."

When this sūtra had been spoken, Vimalamati and the great assembly, the devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, humans, non-humans, and others, hearing the Buddha's teaching, joyfully accepted and practiced it.
