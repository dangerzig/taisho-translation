# 閻羅王五天使者經
## 閻羅王五天使者經

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0043

Translated from the Chinese. 劉宋 慧簡譯

---

## Fascicle 1: Sūtra Spoken by the Buddha on the Five Celestial Messengers of King Yāma

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was dwelling in the Jeta Grove, Anāthapiṇḍada's Park, in Śrāvastī. The Buddha addressed the bhikṣus: "Listen well and take this to heart. With my divine eye I see clearly all beings, their births and deaths, the wholesome and unwholesome paths they travel — some ugly and wicked, some bold and strong, some timid and weak; some born into good paths, some into evil paths. Whatever ordinary beings do, I know and distinguish it all.

"When a person's body acts wickedly, their mouth speaks wickedly, their mind thinks wickedly, they slander and revile the noble ones, and holding wrong views they follow wrong conduct — when that person's life ends, they fall into an evil path and enter the hells. When a person's body acts virtuously, their mouth speaks virtuously, their mind thinks virtuously, they praise and honor the noble ones, and holding right views they follow right conduct — when that person's life ends, they are born in the heavens or among humans. Thus, bhikṣus, with my divine eye I see people at death being born in the heavens or among humans.

"Just so, bhikṣus, with my divine eye I observe rainwater falling upon water, seeing one bubble arise as another bubble bursts. Thus do I see people dying and their consciousness-spirits departing for rebirth — some with fine complexions, some with poor complexions; some bold and strong, some timid and weak; some born in realms of ease, some in realms of suffering. Their arising and ceasing is no different from water bubbles.

"It is further like a person threading five-colored silk through a crystal bead: because the bead is clear, all the colors of the thread — blue, yellow, red, white, and black — appear distinctly. Thus too do I see the spirits of the dead departing for rebirth.

"It is also like placing a bright moonstone at a palace gate on a dark night: a person standing to one side can see, one by one, all who enter and exit through the gate. It is likewise like standing atop a tall tower and looking down at people below — those going and those coming, those running and those walking, those seated and those standing. Thus do I see the spirits of the dying departing for rebirth — handsome or ugly, bold or timid — and according to what they have done, I distinguish and know it all."

The Buddha addressed the bhikṣus: "When a person lives in the world without filial piety toward father and mother, without reverence for śramaṇas and religious practitioners, without practicing benevolence and righteousness, applying their mind to nothing of worth, without studying the sūtras and precepts, without fear of the next life — when that person's body dies, their spirit-soul will fall before King Yāma. The master of hell immediately brings them forward and reports their faults and evil deeds to the king: 'This person has acted unlawfully: without filial piety toward father and mother, without reverence for śramaṇas and religious practitioners, not following benevolence and righteousness, applying their mind to nothing of worth, possessing no merit or virtue, without fear or dread of death. They must be made to see. May the Great King determine their punishment.'

"In response, King Yāma, ever measured and deliberate, speaks with sincere and upright words, presenting the five celestial messengers, and asks: 'Have you not seen, in the world, a person from the very time they were an infant — lying helpless in their own filth and urine, unable to protect themselves, their mouth unable to speak, knowing neither good nor ill?'

"The person says: 'Yes, I have seen it — all beings have this.'

"The king says: 'Do you suppose you alone were exempt from this? The spirit of a person follows their actions, and at death there is certainly rebirth. Even before fully understanding this, one should always practice good, rectifying one's body, rectifying one's speech, rectifying one's mind. Why did you give free rein to your heart and indulge your desires?'

"The person says: 'In truth, it was only because of my stupidity and ignorance.'

"The king says: 'You yourself, through your own foolishness and delusion, committed evil deeds. This is not the fault of your father and mother, your teachers, the sovereign, the devas, śramaṇas, or religious practitioners. The offense belongs to you alone. Can it be stopped merely because you are unwilling? You must now receive it.'

"This is King Yāma presenting the first celestial messenger.

"King Yāma further asks: 'When you were a human being, the celestial messengers came to you in succession. Were you able to perceive them?'

"The person says: 'In truth, I did not perceive them.'

"The king says: 'Have you not seen men and women of the world in their old age — their hair whitened, their teeth fallen out, their bodies thin and stooped, walking and rising only with the aid of a staff?'

"The person says: 'Yes, I have seen this.'

"The king says: 'Did you suppose you alone could escape old age? All beings who are born must by nature grow old. While still in the fullness of strength, one should always practice good, rectifying body, speech, and mind, and upholding the sūtras and precepts. Why did you give yourself over to license?'

"The person says: 'It was only because of stupidity and ignorance.'

"The king says: 'You yourself, through foolishness and delusion, committed evil deeds. This is not the fault of your father and mother, the sovereign, the devas, śramaṇas, or religious practitioners. The offense belongs to you alone. Can it be stopped merely because you are unwilling? You must now receive it.'

"This is King Yāma's right teaching presenting the second celestial messenger.

"King Yāma further asks: 'When you were a human being, did you not see men and women in the world struck by illness — their bodies racked with pain, unable to sit or rise in comfort, their lives drawing near an end amid anxiety and distress, beyond the cure of any physician?'

"The person says: 'Yes, I have seen this.'

"The king says: 'Did you suppose you could escape illness? When a person grows old, they must by nature fall ill. While the body is still robust and healthy, one should diligently practice good, uphold the sūtras and precepts, and rectify body, speech, and mind. Why did you give yourself over to license?'

"The person says: 'It was only because of stupidity and ignorance.'

"The king says: 'You yourself, through foolishness and delusion, committed evil deeds. This is not the fault of your father and mother, the sovereign, the devas, śramaṇas, or religious practitioners. The offense belongs to you alone. Can it be stopped merely because you are unwilling? You must now receive it.'

"This is King Yāma's right teaching presenting the third celestial messenger.

"King Yāma further asks: 'When you were a human being, did you not see the many dead of the world — some buried, some abandoned and left to decay, their flesh and sinew rotting and putrefying from one day to seven days, foxes and all manner of birds coming to feed upon them, the bodies of ordinary people dead and foul with decomposition?'

"The person says: 'Yes, I have seen this.'

"The king says: 'Did you suppose you alone could escape death? All beings who are born must by nature die. While still living in the world, one should always practice good, discipline body, speech, and mind, and uphold the sūtras and precepts. Why did you give yourself over to license?'

"The person says: 'It was only because of stupidity and ignorance.'

"The king says: 'You yourself committed evil deeds. This is not the fault of your father and mother, the sovereign, the devas, śramaṇas, or religious practitioners. The offense belongs to you alone. It cannot be stopped merely because you are unwilling. You must now receive it.'

"This is King Yāma's right teaching presenting the fourth celestial messenger.

"King Yāma further asks: 'When you were a human being, did you not see in the world wicked persons and evil offenders seized by officials and subjected to punishments befitting their crimes — hands and feet severed, noses and ears cut away, the skin lanced and scraped and branded, burning sand and boiling fat poured over their bodies, wrapped in kindling and set ablaze, hung upside down and scorched under the sun, their limbs hacked asunder and the agony of poison mingled with all the rest?'

"The person says: 'Yes, I have seen this.'

"The king says: 'Did you suppose that you alone, having done evil, could go free? Before your very eyes the retribution and blessing of the world are made plain. Why did you not hold fast to goodness, discipline body, speech, and mind, and uphold the sūtras and the Dharma? How did you allow yourself to indulge your heart?'

"The person says: 'It was only because of stupidity and ignorance.'

"The king says: 'You yourself, by the application of your own mind, acted without loyalty or uprightness. This is not the fault of your father and mother, the sovereign, the devas, śramaṇas, or religious practitioners. This calamity and offense must now be received by yourself alone. Can it be stopped merely because you are unwilling?'

"This is King Yāma's sincere and upright teaching presenting the fifth celestial messenger."

When the Buddha had finished speaking, all the disciples received his admonishments and came forward to pay reverence.
