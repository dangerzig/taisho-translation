# 說妙法決定業障經
## Sūtra on Teaching the Wondrous Dharma to Determine Karmic Obstacles

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0841

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Zhìyán (智嚴)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was in the Dharma-Realm Treasury Palace, in the assembly-hall of all the Buddhas, in the boundless bodhimaṇḍa, together with a great assembly of bhikṣus and bodhisattva mahāsattvas. At that time in the bodhimaṇḍa there was a woman named Merit-Adornment Blossoming Flower, who joined her palms toward the Buddha, withdrew, and sat to one side.

At that time the woman addressed the Buddha: "If there are beginning bodhisattvas, what kind of people are unwholesome companions with whom they should not dwell?"

The Buddha told the woman: "Among the beings of the three realms, whether Brahmā, Śakra, the four heavenly kings, śramaṇas, or brahmins, all are wholesome companions for bodhisattvas who cultivate the path, with the sole exception of the śrāvakas, who are unwholesome companions (for fear that the śrāvakas will cause the cultivating bodhisattva to retreat from the Mahāyāna path). Why? Because śrāvakas and pratyekabuddhas, acting for their own benefit, urge and lead beginning bodhisattvas to turn back and enter the lesser vehicle. For this reason, those of the śrāvaka vehicle are unwholesome companions.

"Woman, you should know: beginning bodhisattvas should not share quarters with śrāvaka bhikṣus, should not sit on the same seat, and should not walk the same path. If a beginning bodhisattva's wisdom has grown broad, and without dualistic discrimination the bodhisattva has awakened to the Mahāyāna Dharma, then as a skillful means to urge and lead the śrāvakas into the Great Vehicle, sharing a dwelling is permitted. If śrāvaka bhikṣus have meager merit and narrow wisdom, the cultivating bodhisattva should not teach them the profound Great Vehicle, lest they slander it. Furthermore, cultivating bodhisattvas should not frequently read the treatises and sūtras of the lesser vehicle. Why? Because they obstruct the path to Buddhahood.

"Woman, you should know: a cultivating bodhisattva would rather relinquish body and life than abandon bodhi and enter the śrāvaka path to seek arhatship. When a bodhisattva has already exhorted all sentient beings, if at that point he abandons the aspiration for bodhi and takes up a different path, entering the śrāvaka way and the fruit of arhatship, then because he has caused confusion and disturbance, the bodhisattva retreats from bodhi, and both persons fall into the Avīci hell."

The Buddha told the woman: "A cultivating bodhisattva would rather commit the five great offenses of killing and the like than study the fruit of the srotāpanna and retreat from bodhi. A cultivating bodhisattva would rather endure hell's torments for one kalpa, a hundred kalpas, or even a thousand kalpas than study the fruit of the sakṛdāgāmin and retreat from bodhi. A cultivating bodhisattva would rather fall among the animals than study the fruit of the anāgāmin and retreat from bodhi. A cultivating bodhisattva would rather kill living beings and fall into hell than cultivate the fruit of the arhat and retreat from bodhi. The arhat realizes for himself alone and privately enters nirvāṇa, like a petty thief secretly entering another's house. The cultivating bodhisattva, because of the aspiration for bodhi, embraces all sentient beings. He would rather enter a pit of fire than abide in the quiescent nirvāṇa of the śrāvakas, and will not retreat from bodhi. For this reason, in order to embrace sentient beings and cause them to enter the Buddha's path, such a cultivating bodhisattva is honored and revered by all the devas, humans, and asuras in the world, is worthy of offerings and surpasses the śrāvakas, and the evil hosts of Māra cannot disturb him."

At that time the woman addressed the Buddha: "What are the hosts of Māra?"

The Buddha told the woman: "Wherever the Mahāyāna sūtras are expounded, if there are sentient beings who, hearing the Great Vehicle taught, do not delight in listening, who mock and slander it, you should know that these are the hosts of Māra. Because of their mind of slandering the Mahāyāna sūtras, at death they fall into the Avīci hell and suffer immeasurably. Then they are reborn as hungry ghosts, eating fire, excrement, and urine. After immeasurable kalpas of suffering, they are later born among humans, but blind, deaf, mute, leprous, and with incomplete faculties. After these beings die, they pass through immeasurable lifetimes before they are able to encounter the Tathāgata, serve and make offerings to him, and at the place of the Buddhas hear the Mahāyāna sūtras once more, purely and without admixture."

At that time the Tathāgata emitted sounds from every pore. From each pore came countless billions of rays of Dharma-light, and further arose immeasurable verses of Dharma in praise. At that time, whoever in the assembly was a śrāvaka heard the Dharma of the śrāvaka vehicle; whoever was of the pratyekabuddha vehicle heard the Dharma of the pratyekabuddha vehicle; whoever was a practitioner of the Great Vehicle heard the wondrous Dharma of the Great Vehicle; and birds and beasts each heard the Buddha-Dharma in their own language. Those sentient beings present in the assembly who had never in the past heard the Buddha-Dharma saw the Tathāgata sitting in silence, not speaking. The remaining sentient beings who had slandered the Great Vehicle in past lives, although they had fallen into hell and the hungry ghost realm for many kalpas of suffering, because the Great Vehicle had entered their ears at the time of their slander, they now, in the Buddha's presence, heard the Great Vehicle and gave rise to joy, and they generated the aspiration for unsurpassed bodhi, which will ultimately result in the accomplishment of anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi.

At that time the woman addressed the Buddha: "Regarding the Great Vehicle that has been taught, why is it called the Great Vehicle? Why is it spoken of as the Great Vehicle?"

The Buddha said: "Woman! Excellent, excellent! Your deep delight in the Great Vehicle is the reason. Reflect well upon it, and I shall explain the names of the Great Vehicle for you. First, it causes people to delight deeply: this is called the Great Vehicle. Second, it is unmoving: this is called the Great Vehicle. Third, it is without fault: this is called the Great Vehicle. Fourth, it is immeasurable: this is called the Great Vehicle. Fifth, it is like the four great oceans: this is called the Great Vehicle. Sixth, it is revered by garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, and diverse beings: this is called the Great Vehicle. Seventh, it is praised by the gandharvas: this is called the Great Vehicle. Eighth, it is reverenced by the devas: this is called the Great Vehicle. Ninth, Brahmā takes refuge in it: this is called the Great Vehicle. Tenth, the lord of the devas honors it: this is called the Great Vehicle. Eleventh, the four kings embrace it: this is called the Great Vehicle. Twelfth, the nāga kings make offerings to it: this is called the Great Vehicle. Thirteenth, the bodhisattvas uphold it: this is called the Great Vehicle. Fourteenth, it accomplishes the Buddha-nature: this is called the Great Vehicle. Fifteenth, the worthy and the noble take refuge in it: this is called the Great Vehicle. Sixteenth, it is universally fit to be received by all: this is called the Great Vehicle. Seventeenth, it is like the king of medicinal trees: this is called the Great Vehicle. Eighteenth, it severs all afflictions: this is called the Great Vehicle. Nineteenth, it can turn the Dharma-wheel: this is called the Great Vehicle. Twentieth, it is without words and without speech: this is called the Great Vehicle. Twenty-first, it is like the mark of empty space: this is called the Great Vehicle. Twenty-second, the lineage of the Three Jewels is unbroken: this is called the Great Vehicle. Twenty-third, beings of dull faculties do not believe in it: this is called the Great Vehicle. Twenty-fourth, it surpasses all: this is called the Great Vehicle."

When the Buddha spoke these names and mighty power of the Great Vehicle, this great trichiliocosm quaked in six ways. Hundreds of thousands of musical instruments sounded without being played. In the sky the devas rained down flowers. Countless hundreds of thousands of devas all gave rise to the aspiration for unsurpassed bodhi. Countless hundreds of thousands of śrāvakas all gave rise to the aspiration for anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi. Furthermore, beginning bodhisattvas who had not yet awakened to the Dharma all attained awakening.

At that time Ānanda addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! What is this Dharma called? How should we receive and uphold it?"

The Buddha said: "This sūtra is called 'The Great Vehicle Guṇa-Jaya.' Receive and uphold it. It is also called 'Teaching the Wondrous Dharma to Determine Karmic Obstacles.' Receive and uphold it."

When the Tathāgata had finished teaching this sūtra, Ānanda, the woman Merit-Adornment Blossoming Flower, and all the devas, nāgas, and the eightfold assembly all rejoiced greatly and received and practiced it.
