# 葉衣觀自在菩薩經
## Sūtra of Parṇaśavarī Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1100

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

At that time the Bhagavān was abiding in the world of Sukhāvatī, expounding the wondrous dharma for the great assemblies. Then Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, and with both knees on the ground bowed at the feet of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva Mahāsattva. He addressed Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva: "Holy One, you abide in the samādhi of great compassion, liberation, and illusion, able to remove the suffering and anguish of all sentient beings and grant the benefit and happiness of both worldly and supramundane matters. Suppose all the sentient beings of the great trichiliocosm simultaneously have all manner of suffering, anguish, and the afflictions of the eight difficulties, or hope for worldly or supramundane rewards: if they can single-mindedly call the name of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, you immediately, without forsaking the great compassion vow, manifest all manner of bodies appropriate to their kind. You can fulfill all the supreme wishes of sentient beings, and can also protect the national boundaries and rescue those in suffering and peril. You can also gather, nurture, and increase auspiciousness. You can also halt imprisonment, suffering, and bodily punishment. You can also extinguish gu-poisons, ghost-possessions, and all manner of evil diseases. You can also restrain blades and cudgels in battle. You can also extinguish the disasters of water and fire. You can also sever inauspicious prayers, spells, and curses. You can also seal and protect the directions and the boundaries of the earth.

"I beseech the Holy One, out of compassion for all future sentient beings, kings, men, and women, who have pure faith in the Three Treasures and protect and uphold the buddha-dharma, and who transmit the royal succession without interruption: for their sake, please proclaim the ritual dhāraṇī and the skillful means of empowerment."

At that time Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva Mahāsattva rose from his seat, bowed at the Buddha's feet, circumambulated three times, returned to his seat, and with palms joined toward the Buddha, addressed the Buddha: "I beseech the World-Honored One's compassionate empowerment. I possess a dhāraṇī of Parṇaśavarī Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva Mahāsattva that can remove all the disasters and calamities of sentient beings: epidemics, famines, banditry, warfare, droughts, floods, imbalances of climate, and disorderly movements of the lunar mansions. It can also increase merit and virtue, bring prosperity to the national boundaries, and give peace and happiness to the people. I now wish to proclaim it. I beseech your permission." The Buddha said: "Excellent, excellent! Proclaim it as you wish." At that time Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, sustained by the Buddha's spiritual power, spoke the dhāraṇī:

[Dhāraṇī]

The heart mantra says:

*Oṃ praṇa-śavarī hūṃ phaṭ*

When Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva had spoken this dhāraṇī, he addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! If any good man or good woman recites this dhāraṇī once, they protect their own body. If they recite it twice, they protect their companions. If they recite it three times, they can protect an entire household. If they recite it four times, they protect an entire village. If they recite it five times, they protect an entire kingdom.

"If an epidemic is spreading within the kingdom, one should take a white cloth one and a half cubits wide and two cubits long. First instruct the painter to maintain purity and fasting precepts, and using gomaya juice mixed with a little verdigris, blended with fragrant glue (not skin glue), on the day of the Puṣya lunar mansion, paint an image of Parṇaśavarī Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva. The image should be in the form of a celestial maiden, wearing a jeweled crown with Amitāyus Buddha within the crown, adorned with necklaces, rings, and armlets, with a circular halo and flames surrounding the body. The image has four arms: the first right hand holds an auspicious fruit at the heart; the second right hand forms the wish-granting gesture. The first left hand holds a battle-axe; the second left hand holds a lasso. She sits upon a lotus. When the painting is complete, hang it upon a pole and have one person hold it. The pole-bearer should recite the Parṇaśavarī Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva dhāraṇī without interruption, with drums and bells sounding. The stick used for striking should be empowered with the Stambha-king mantra twenty-one times before striking. The mantra says:

*Oṃ stambhi hūṃ jaḥ*

"Furthermore, have two people recite praises: one recites the praise of auspicious celebration and one recites the praise of good fortune. Have three to five dharma-knowing disciples: one holds an incense burner burning benzoin incense (the incense should be empowered with the Parṇaśavarī Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva heart mantra one hundred and eight times before burning; the incense smoke must not be interrupted). One holds a jeweled vase filled with fragrant water with flower and fruit branches inserted. The vase-holder goes in front, leading the image. Two people blow conches, leading the image into the royal palace, circling once. Exit through the south gate, enter again from the east, circle through the wards and markets within the city once, then exit through the south gate of the city. Outside the south city gate, place a large water vase. Within it, place all manner of food, drink, mixed fruits, and parched grain. The ācārya recites the Tathāgata of Wondrous Form mantra seven times to empower it, then recites the Parṇaśavarī Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva dhāraṇī seven times, and within the mantra-phrases calls the king's name and title in empowerment, wishing that the king's realm be free from all disasters and calamities. Then at the roadside in the open fields, throw the water vase and break it, making this proclamation: 'Epidemic-spreading ghosts of the realm of Yama and others: you should accept this food and drink and return the way you came. Generate the heart of great loving-kindness for all sentient beings, and let this kingdom be free from all disasters and calamities.'

"Another method: if one seeks longevity and freedom from illness, paint an image of Parṇaśavarī Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva on cloth of whatever size one wishes, and beneath the wish-granting hand paint the figure of the man or woman. Place the image in the bodhimaṇḍa, and each day make offerings of flowers, incense, food, and drink, circumambulate, and make vows. One will always receive empowerment and have all wishes fulfilled.

"Another method: if royal sons or daughters are difficult to raise, or are short-lived, bedridden with chronic illness, or unable to eat and sleep in peace, all due to past karmic causes producing inauspicious natal stars, or due to the five planets repeatedly pressing upon the natal star causing bodily discomfort: at one's dwelling, using gorocanā, paper, or white silk, write the mantras of the twenty-eight great yakṣa generals and affix them to the four walls.

"First, on the eastern wall, affix the mantras of four great yakṣa generals, beginning from the northeast corner:

"First yakṣa general: *Oṃ Nīragaṭa-ḍhī hūṃ jaḥ svāhā*

"Second yakṣa general: *Oṃ Sunītra-ḍhī hūṃ jaḥ svāhā*

"Third yakṣa general: *Oṃ Pūrṇaka-ḍhī hūṃ jaḥ svāhā*

"Fourth yakṣa general: *Oṃ Kapila-ḍhī hūṃ jaḥ svāhā*

"Next, on the southern wall, affix four great yakṣa general mantras:

"First: *Oṃ Saṃgha-ḍhī hūṃ jaḥ svāhā*

"Second: *Oṃ Upasaṃgha-ḍhī hūṃ jaḥ svāhā*

"Third: *Oṃ Śaṅkhira-ḍhī hūṃ jaḥ svāhā*

"Fourth: *Oṃ Nanda-ḍhī hūṃ jaḥ svāhā*

"Next, on the western wall, affix four great yakṣa general mantras:

"First: *Oṃ Hari-ḍhī hūṃ jaḥ svāhā*

"Second: *Oṃ Harikeśa-ḍhī hūṃ jaḥ svāhā*

"Third: *Oṃ Prabhū-ḍhī hūṃ jaḥ svāhā*

"Fourth: *Oṃ Kapila-ḍhī hūṃ jaḥ svāhā*

"Next, on the northern wall, affix four great yakṣa general mantras:

"First: *Oṃ Dhāraṇa-ḍhī hūṃ jaḥ svāhā*

"Second: *Oṃ Dhārananda-ḍhī hūṃ jaḥ svāhā*

"Third: *Oṃ Unīyuga-para-ḍhī hūṃ jaḥ svāhā*

"Fourth: *Oṃ Viṣṇu-ḍhī hūṃ jaḥ svāhā*

"Next, at the northeast corner, affix one great yakṣa general mantra: *Oṃ Pañcika-ḍhī hūṃ jaḥ svāhā*

"Next, at the southeast corner: *Oṃ Pañcara-gaṇḍa-ḍhī hūṃ jaḥ svāhā*

"Next, at the southwest corner: *Oṃ Satāgiri-ḍhī hūṃ jaḥ svāhā*

"Next, at the northwest corner: *Oṃ Haimavata-ḍhī hūṃ jaḥ svāhā*

"Next, on stones at the lower level where feet do not tread, engrave the mantras of four great yakṣa generals and place them underground in the four directions:

"Eastern underground: *Oṃ Bhūma-ḍhī hūṃ jaḥ svāhā*

"Southern underground: *Oṃ Subhūma-ḍhī hūṃ jaḥ svāhā*

"Western underground: *Oṃ Kāla-ḍhī hūṃ jaḥ svāhā*

"Northern underground: *Oṃ Upakāla-ḍhī hūṃ jaḥ svāhā*

"Next, at the upper level on the four corners of the dwelling, each affix one great yakṣa general mantra:

"Northeast upper: *Oṃ Sūrya-ḍhī hūṃ jaḥ svāhā*

"Southeast upper: *Oṃ Agni-ḍhī hūṃ jaḥ svāhā*

"Southwest upper: *Oṃ Soma-ḍhī hūṃ jaḥ svāhā*

"Northwest upper: *Oṃ Vāyu-ḍhī hūṃ jaḥ svāhā*

"Having affixed the mantras, at each of the twenty-eight great yakṣa general positions, anoint a small altar with perfume. Upon each altar, burn incense, and offer mixed flowers, food and drink, lamp-candles, and argha. Sincerely announce: 'May the twenty-eight great yakṣa generals and all their retinues each abide in their own direction, protecting and guarding So-and-so, removing all disasters, inauspiciousness, illness, and untimely death. May they obtain physical vigor, increased intelligence, majestic and handsome appearance, and be easy to nurture and raise, with a long lifespan.'

"Having made this empowerment, the twenty-eight great yakṣa generals, not daring to transgress the buddhas, and following the command of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva and Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva, will guard and protect day and night. Sleep will be peaceful, waking will be peaceful, and great majestic virtue will be obtained. If any king performs this method, all epidemics and diseases within the royal domain will be extinguished, the land will be at peace, and the people will rejoice.

"Another method: one should paint one's natal star and make monthly offerings. If one performs this method, inauspicious natal influences are transformed into auspiciousness. Carve an image of Parṇaśavarī Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva from white sandalwood, and write this mantra on birch bark to carry together. If performing this method, receive the abhiṣeka on the day of the Puṣya lunar mansion. The abhiṣeka vase should have silk tied at its neck, filled with fragrant water. In the water, place the seven treasures and five kinds of medicine, namely sahacara, sahadevī, kaṇṭakārikā, vṛhatī, and girikarṇa, as well as five kinds of seeds and various incenses. Empower with the Parṇaśavarī Avalokiteśvara dhāraṇī one hundred and eight times and use it for the abhiṣeka, washing away all obstacles and difficulties. After the abhiṣeka, take an earthen bowl filled with all manner of food and drink, and circle it three times above the head of the man or woman. Have a dharma-knowing person carry it far away and throw and break it. Then tie a thread-cord, empower it with the Parṇaśavarī mantra, and tie it upon the person's head. If one performs this method, all bodily diseases, ghost-possessions, inauspicious prayers, and the oppression of natal stars are all extinguished.

"Another method: if there is an epidemic among the people, take śamī-wood to light a fire, then perform a ghee-homa. Cast human hair and human bone into the fire one hundred and eight times. For seven days, make daily offerings to Parṇaśavarī Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva. At the time of the homa, call the name of the city, village, and ward. All disasters and calamities will be extinguished. For epidemics among elephants, horses, cattle, or water buffalo, take the bones and fur of each respective animal and perform a homa for seven days and nights; the epidemics will likewise be extinguished.

"Another method: take a vase filled with fragrant water, empower it with the mantra one hundred and eight times, and pour the water upon the livestock. All epidemic diseases will be extinguished.

"Another method: if a person has a headache, take a fragrant flower, empower it one hundred and eight times, and have them smell it. The headache will be cured.

"Another method: if a person is possessed by ghosts, take rice flour and make a figure of that ghost. With a steel knife, recite the mantra while cutting it piece by piece. Perform a homa for seven days, and the possession will be cured.

"Another method: if a person suffers from malaria, whether it comes every day, every two days, every three days, or up to every seven days, or is a chronic malaria, write this mantra in gorocanā and wear it. The malaria will be cured.

"Another method: if an infant is possessed by ghosts, write this mantra and have the infant wear it, and the possession will be cured.

"Another method: if bandits and thieves invade the wards, markets, and villages, or if one is about to travel far and fears highway robbers, take khadira wood-powder and perform a homa, reciting the mantra one hundred and eight times. Wherever one goes, there will be no obstacles or difficulties.

"Another method: if insects eat the crops, take sand and empower it with the mantra one hundred and eight times, then scatter it in the fields. The insects will depart of their own accord, and the five grains will ripen abundantly."
