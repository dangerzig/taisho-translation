# 大威燈光仙人問疑經

## Sūtra on the Questions of the Great Seer Blazing Light

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0834

Translated from the Chinese. Sui dynasty, translated by Jñānagupta (闍那崛多) and others.

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Bhagavān was at the city of Gayā, not long after attaining the path, together with a complete assembly of monks. Among them were those who had attained the first fruit, the second, third, and fourth fruits, and in accordance with their attainments their merits were all luminous and pure. There were also ninety-nine koṭis of bodhisattvas, twenty-eight koṭis of devas, and immeasurable numbers of monks, nuns, laymen, and laywomen, as well as sixty thousand strongmen, twelve koṭis of Nirgrantha followers, and eighty-four thousand five-powered seers. There were also five hundred heterodox practitioners, all with ashes smeared on their bodies, their chests bared, their flesh consumed and fat wasted, with only skin and bones remaining. They were hunched and bent, their matted hair wrapped about their heads, wearing bark clothing, carrying jars in their hands, searching everywhere for debate and discourse.

At that time, the World-Honored One was like Mount Sumeru standing within black mountains, radiant and luminous, of unsurpassed virtue and majesty. The Tathāgata amid the seers was also like this, foremost among them. He was also like a pure white six-tusked elephant alone among white sheep, like the moon at night outshining the multitude of fireflies, like a mandārava flower growing amid a field of reeds, like a garuḍa bird in a flock of crows. The World-Honored One amid those seers shone with even greater eminence.

At that time, the World-Honored One entered the Jewel-Relinquishing Samādhi and manifested immeasurable supernatural transformations, emitting pure light everywhere, his entire body brilliantly illuminated, with rays intertwining on his left and right. From his own body he projected immeasurable koṭis of emanation-buddhas, and from each emanation-body there emerged yet more immeasurable koṭis of emanation-buddhas. From his body he further projected immeasurable koṭis of bodhisattva forms, immeasurable Śakra forms, immeasurable Brahmā king forms, immeasurable Four Heavenly King forms, immeasurable hundreds of thousands of arhat forms, immeasurable hundreds of thousands of monks, nuns, laymen, and laywomen, immeasurable great wheel-turning king forms, immeasurable lesser wheel-turning king forms, immeasurable petty kings, immeasurable people of the Eastern Continent's borderlands, immeasurable people of all the lands of southern India, immeasurable kṣatriyas of great families, brāhmaṇas, great wealthy elders, and all varieties of different forms, different dress, and different speech. All beings belonging to every heavenly domain all emerged from the Tathāgata's body.

At that time, the entire great assembly each harbored doubt, looking at one another. The bodhisattvas all felt great joy and rained down precious jewels and offerings, including necklaces, to make offerings to the Tathāgata.

At that time, the World-Honored One, having displayed this portent, returned from the Jewel-Relinquishing Samādhi. Having risen from samādhi, like a lion stretching and looking about, he universally surveyed the ten directions. Having done so, he immediately saw all the buddha-lands in all the worlds of the ten directions and this sahā great-thousand world, and with the buddha-eye he saw them all clearly, as though in the palm of his hand.

Just as this Śākyamuni Tathāgata had displayed great supernatural transformations of every kind, the buddhas of all ten directions likewise manifested emanation-buddha forms, and from those emanation-buddha forms displayed further emanation-buddha forms. All the emanation-buddhas of those tathāgatas came to gather at the World-Honored One's great assembly. Moreover, immeasurable Ganges-sands of bodhisattvas, monks, nuns, laymen, and laywomen, beyond all comparison, each carrying various offerings and supplies appropriate for making offerings, came to the Buddha's presence. Further, devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, humans and non-humans, the entire great assembly, each from their dwelling places, having seen the Tathāgata's display of supernatural power, came from their abodes to attend this ocean-assembly.

At that time, the bodhisattvas who had come from the ten directions each made unsurpassed offerings to the Tathāgata. Having done so, they sat upon lion-thrones accomplished through the six perfections, each fitting their stature. Likewise, humans and non-humans each sat upon seats befitting their forms.

All those taught by Śākyamuni Tathāgata, from as high as the Akaniṣṭha heaven down to the Avīci hell, all returned. Having returned, at that time, through the Buddha's power, all could see the worlds of the buddhas of the ten directions as though they were a single assembly. All those taught by the buddhas of the ten directions entered the pores of Śākyamuni Tathāgata, and all those taught by Śākyamuni Tathāgata entered the bodies and pores of those buddhas.

Having displayed this, at that time in the assembly there was a bodhisattva named Victorious Part, who rose from his seat, proceeding with measured and dignified bearing, handsome in appearance. He bared his right shoulder, placed his right knee on the ground, and with palms joined toward the Buddha spoke in verse:

> "The Buddha's time is most rare,
> Revealed for the masses to see;
> This has never been before,
> Covering over all the māras.
> They look at each other's faces
> And declare: 'This is most extraordinary!
> Why have we come?'
> They say: 'I am destroyed.
> Our kind is pitiable;
> Only skulls and bones remain.
> We are emaciated, withered, and old,
> Having lost all joy;
> Without words or supernatural powers,
> Our path's thorns are covered over.'
> The great supernatural Buddha's sons
> Now reveal the Buddha's Dharma;
> These beings have doubting hearts,
> Yet give rise to great joy.
> This assembly all declares:
> 'We wish to become buddhas.'
> Mañjuśrī dwells in the assembly,
> The Buddha's sons surrounding him.
> Mañjuśrī has served many buddhas
> And comes to display supernatural powers.
> For what dharma does this sign appear?
> What does the Buddha now teach?
> All harbor this doubt;
> We pray you will speak for our assembly."

At that time, through the power of Māra, a seer in the assembly named Blazing Light addressed the Bodhisattva Victorious Part: "Young man! Be silent for now. I shall now pose a question. If this śramaṇa can resolve my doubts, then he may be called one who knows all; if he cannot resolve my doubts, how can he be called omniscient? Such supernatural transformations, whether performed by illusionists, by the spells of Maheśvara or Nārāyaṇa, ordinary worldly people using such methods can also accomplish such immeasurable feats. Is this really so remarkable?"

When these words were spoken, the Tathāgata smiled serenely. Having smiled, he universally surveyed the entire assembly of seers. Having observed them, he addressed the Great Seer Blazing Light: "O Blazing Light! Now is the right time. Ask whatever you wish, and according to my wisdom-power I shall explain for you."

Then the Great Seer Blazing Light asked the Buddha: "Śramaṇa Gautama! First tell me: from what place is the body of sentient beings born? How gross? How subtle? As for the inner nature of sentient beings' bodies: is it one arm-span? One foot? One finger? Down to the size of a grain of barley, wheat, large bean, small bean? Down to the size of a mustard seed: is there an inner body-nature of sentient beings?"

Having asked this, the World-Honored One praised the Great Seer Blazing Light: "Excellent, excellent! O Blazing Light, well asked. You are one whose lifespan is sixty thousand kalpas."

When the World-Honored One spoke these words, the seers were all greatly astonished and thought: "We have long dwelled together with this great seer, yet we still did not know the measure of Great Seer Blazing Light's lifespan. No one has ever spoken of it. How can this Gautama swiftly know such things?"

Then the World-Honored One further addressed the Great Seer Blazing Light: "O Great Seer! Listen carefully, receive it well, and consider it wisely. I shall now explain for you fully. You asked me: 'From what place is the body of sentient beings born?' Great Seer, you should know: there truly are no words, no phrases, with which to say sentient beings come from some place. It is only through the conditions of ignorance, formations, and so forth, up to birth, old age, illness, and death, that sentient beings arise. Great Seer, there are further conditions that give rise to sentient beings: namely, with the mother as cause and the father as condition, sentient beings are born. Furthermore, the union of father and mother serves as the cause; deluded thoughts give rise to the wind of karma, which blows the seed of consciousness into the womb, and this is the condition. Furthermore, the noble truth of suffering, and the noble truths of the origin of suffering, its cessation, and the path, constitute sentient beings. Furthermore, the five aggregates and the eighteen elements in their combination constitute sentient beings. Furthermore, Great Seer, there are no sentient beings apart from karma, and no karma apart from sentient beings; sentient beings are karma, and karma is sentient beings. You should know: the realm of sentient beings neither increases nor decreases."

The great seer said: "Gautama! If the realm of sentient beings neither increases nor decreases, why do sentient beings, after abandoning a defiled body, attain an autonomous body?"

The Buddha said: "O Great Seer! What you say is greatly untenable. Why? If an autonomous being attains autonomy, it should never fall; it should always remain autonomous. If an autonomous body does not attain autonomy, how can it be called autonomous? Great Seer! It is like a firefly that conceives the thought: 'My light can illuminate all of Jambudvīpa.' Yet even if the firefly could actually emit light to illuminate all of Jambudvīpa, it could never make one whose mind is not subdued truly autonomous. Furthermore, Great Seer, if autonomy were truly autonomous, all afflictions should be exhausted. Since one is not autonomous, afflictions increase. If afflictions and autonomy coexist equally, then the realm of sentient beings has no increase or decrease that can be perceived."

Then the great seer said: "Gautama! Do you not bring afflictions to an end?"

The Buddha said: "O Great Seer! I also do not bring afflictions to an end, nor do I increase them."

The great seer said: "If that is so, Gautama, you also should not say: 'I have attained autonomy.'"

The Buddha said: "Great Seer! Just so, just so. Great Seer, you should know: I also do not say 'I have attained autonomy.' Why? Because there is nothing real in me, I am also not autonomous."

The great seer said: "Gautama, let us set that aside. Gautama! As you said before, sentient beings are born from the union of father and mother. Yet many beings come together in union, but few sentient beings are born. How is this?"

The Buddha said: "Great Seer! I shall now give you an analogy; explain it for me according to your ability. Great Seer! Just as in a grove, one seed may produce many trees, and from one tree come boundless branches, and from each branch come immeasurable flowers, and each flower should bear fruit, yet why do some bear fruit and some not? And those that have borne fruit should all ripen and produce seeds; why then are some ripe and some not? How is this?"

The great seer said: "Gautama! Because the wind blows, some bear fruit and some do not. Those that do bear fruit fall and do not ripen, and are unfit to be seeds."

The Buddha told the great seer: "Because the wind of karma blows and turns, the karmic fruits of sentient beings fall, and therefore few sentient beings are born. Great Seer, you should know: some in the womb are eaten by worms, and some are broken and scattered by the wind of karma. You should know: the loss of trees through such calamity is small and not worth mentioning, but the sentient beings lost through such calamity are beyond telling. Furthermore, Great Seer, it is through deluded thoughts that the realm of sentient beings arises. If sentient beings can have any amount of mental transformation, exactly that number will receive subsequent birth. Therefore I say it is through deluded thoughts that the realm of sentient beings arises."

Then the great seer said: "Gautama! Just so, just so. As I asked, you have answered me, and this meaning is established. Gautama! Please further explain for me: for what reason does the kalpa end in conflagration?"

The Buddha said: "Great Seer! You should know: it is because nothing is created that it is called the dharma-realm. If the great earth were not burned at the kalpa's end, then the dharma-realm would have two kinds: some part impermanent and some part permanent. If that were so, the tathāgatas would not be speakers of truth. If all is impermanent, and in the unconditioned dharma there is nothing that can be conceptualized, then the Tathāgata deserves the name of omniscient one."

Then the great seer, having heard these words, turned his head and told his own disciples: "Do you know? This Gautama is truly the omniscient one."

Then the World-Honored One further addressed the great seer: "If at the kalpa's end the great earth were not burned, one could not distinguish between initial and final times, nor know the difference in fruits of good and bad karma, of wholesome and unwholesome retribution. You should know: this burning that consumes all at the kalpa's end is brought about by the great skillful-means power of the tathāgatas. Those sentient beings who can hear and believe that the kalpa will end in conflagration, burning all to ashes, exactly those sentient beings shall receive the tathāgatas' caring embrace. You should know: it is like a great python-serpent whose eyes, ears, mouth, and nose can each, through the power of its venom, attract and capture all manner of flying and crawling creatures. You should know that the Tathāgata is likewise: through the 'venom-power' of generosity, kind speech, beneficial action, and shared endeavor, he can attract, embrace, subdue, and train all sentient beings. Great Seer! It is also like a person who places a gold ingot in fire, not placing it there out of anger toward the gold, but because it is unrefined and he wishes to refine it, to make it a true treasure, to increase its value and yield great wealth. So he places the gold in fire, hammers it repeatedly, and makes it soft, pure, and bright. Likewise, all sentient beings are subdued through the tathāgatas' sending forth the kalpa-ending conflagration. When the great earth burns at the kalpa's end, there truly is no sentient being who suffers."

The great seer said: "World-Honored One! How extraordinary! When the kalpa-ending fire burns and the great earth is destroyed, not a single sentient being suffers?"

The Buddha said: "No, indeed. Great Seer! The buddhas and tathāgatas do not cause a single sentient being to be oppressed or afflicted. Why? Great Seer, it is like fine raindrops falling in the ten directions. Are those raindrops numerous?"

The great seer said: "Exceedingly numerous, World-Honored One!"

The Buddha said: "Great Seer! The buddhas and tathāgatas and tenth-stage bodhisattvas are far more numerous than those. When the great earth burns at the kalpa's end, they dwell above in the sky and with their bodies of compassion, wisdom, and hands rescue sentient beings, not allowing any suffering to touch their bodies. Why? Because those buddhas, tathāgatas, and all bodhisattvas have wondrous, vast bodies with dignified and beautiful marks. Sentient beings who see them are all filled with joy and give rise to right faith, declaring: 'May we in future lives all accomplish such rescue! May we attain such forms and such dignified, beautiful bodies!' At that time, some whose minds are already liberated attain the arhat fruit. Some, with revulsion arising in their hearts, attain the fruit of stream-entry, once-returning, or non-returning. Some attain the patient acceptance of the unborn; some attain the stage of non-retrogression. Some are born in the heaven of the Four Kings, some in the heaven of the Thirty-Three, Yāma heaven, Tuṣita heaven, the heaven of Transformation of Joy, or the heaven of Free Enjoyment of Others' Transformations. In brief, some are born as far as the Akaniṣṭha heaven.

"At that time, all the great wheel-turning kings, lesser wheel-turning kings, and petty regional kings, and the great seers, down to the great kṣatriya families, great brāhmaṇas, and great wealthy elders, in this order, having seen the Tathāgata's wondrous form, and further having seen that they themselves have been delivered from the great terror of the ocean of birth and death, give rise to boundless joy, and toward the Tathāgata they feel gratitude and wish to repay his kindness. Having received the Dharma from the Tathāgata, each practices diligently within the ten courses of wholesome action. Through the power of this skillful-means condition, they swiftly abandon the ten courses of unwholesome action.

"At that time, those who have attained the tenth stage of great bodhisattvahood, with the scope of their eyes' illumination extending across the atoms of the great earth, and even those in the realm of sentient beings that extends beyond this, all come to know the nature of afflictions as defiled and impure, and from the unconditioned path of nirvāṇa enter the realm of nirvāṇa without remainder, far more numerous than those atoms. You should now know: it is for such great benefit that the tathāgatas display by skillful means the conflagration at the kalpa's end."

At that time, all the great seers, having heard these words, felt wonder and astonishment: "How extraordinary! Most rare! Earlier, the venerable son of the Śākyas addressed me as 'Great Seer' and revealed my lifespan. Though I heard these things, I still thought he was not truly the omniscient one. Now that he has bestowed what is rarest in the world, difficult among the difficult, I know that this son of the Śākyas is truly the omniscient one. From now on I shall address him by his true name."

Then all the great seers declared: "O great accumulation of merit! O being of boundless great wisdom! O knower of all! We see sentient beings, like stars streaming on the wind of karma, each different and separate. Where can they achieve true gathering? We pray the World-Honored One will explain for us, so that we may be awakened."

Then the World-Honored One addressed the great seer: "O Great Seer! You should know: there is no time, no direction, and no place where sentient beings can truly be gathered. Great Seer! Only in equality can sentient beings be gathered; in the path of the one vehicle can sentient beings be gathered; on the bodhisattva-ground can sentient beings be gathered; in the realm of nirvāṇa without remainder can sentient beings be gathered. You should now know: just as there are streams, rivers, springs, channels, and water-sources that all flow into the great ocean, and having entered the great ocean they dwell in one flavor, which is the single salty flavor, without distinction, so too, Great Seer, you should now know: when all beings in the realm of sentient beings have their outflows exhausted, all dwell in one flavor within the flavor of liberation. You should now know: although I say sentient beings are gathered in the equality of afflictions, this is also not truly gathering. Why? It is like a great wind that rises and blows together all manner of mosquitoes and insects into one gathering; when the wind subsides, they each scatter and disperse. Likewise, all classes of sentient beings are each bound by the wind of karma. Some, falling into hell, are gathered there by those of their kind; bound by the wind of karma, sometimes they are gathered among hungry ghosts by those of their kind; or gathered among animals by those of their kind; and so on."

The seer further said: "O you who know all, omniscient one! We pray you will explain: if such-and-such beings were previously born together in the human realm, gathered in the same place, how can one know today, in the present? And for all those who were once in the animal realm or the hungry ghost realm, who previously gathered, how can one know? We pray you will explain."

The Buddha said: "Great Seer! Those sentient beings who previously gathered in hell, if born in the present world as humans, upon seeing one another feel no joy and give rise to anger and resentment. Sometimes they get headaches, or sometimes they lose control of their bowels or bladders. You should know these are the signs of beings who previously gathered in hell. If such signs appear, one should know: 'That person and I have certainly once dwelled together in hell.'"

Then the great seer said to the Buddha: "O you who can bring all to great peace, omniscient one! Please further explain: if in a previous life one dwelled among animals, gathered with thousands of other bodies in one place, and was then born in the human realm, how can one know?"

The Buddha told the great seer: "If such beings are born in the human realm, upon seeing one another they form enmity and anger, constantly seeking each other's faults: 'Where can I find an opportunity against them?' This is the sign of one who dwelled among animals, gathered in one place with many bodies. One should know: 'I have certainly dwelled together with that one among animals.'

"If one previously dwelled together in the hungry ghost realm, one constantly loves foul smells, is excessively greedy for food, and if about to give to another, the heart cannot let go, becoming miserly and attached. Or, seeing another's wealth and power, one gives rise to jealousy and constantly covets their possessions. When one sees such signs, one should know with certainty that this person and oneself dwelled together in the hungry ghost realm.

"If in a previous life one dwelled together in the human realm in the same place, upon seeing each other in this present life, desire arises."

At that time, the Great Seer Blazing Light said to the Buddha: "If in a previous life one dwelled together in the heavens in the same place, and now in the human realm one encounters the other, how can one know?"

The Buddha said: "Great Seer! If in a previous life one was born among the heavens together, and in the present human realm they see each other, they draw each other in with their gaze from afar, sharing mutual affection. If such signs appear, they certainly came from gathering in the heavens. If one observes with such signs, one can know the signs of sentient beings' gatherings."

At that time, the great seer, having heard these words, was joyful and exultant, filled with a sense of wonder, and said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! Only now do I know that those sentient beings who are deluded and lost, not seeking to study omniscience, may truly be called greatly deceived."

Then the World-Honored One further addressed the great seer: "As for your earlier question about how many subtle inner body-natures sentient beings have: Great Seer, if there were a sentient being's body-nature that could be found, then one could determine how subtle, long, or short it is. You should now know: it is like a person born blind from birth, and another person asks them, 'Person! What does the color white look like?' What do you think? That person has never seen; could they say, 'This color is such-and-such'?"

The seer answered: "Since that person has never clearly seen color, how could they dare to make such a definitive judgment?"

The Buddha said: "Just so, just so. Great Seer! Ordinary people are like the one born blind: they do not see the body-nature of sentient beings, and one cannot say, 'The subtle inner nature of sentient beings is of such-and-such length or shortness, coarseness or roughness.'

"Furthermore, Great Seer, the eye is not a sentient being; the ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind are also not sentient beings. The conditioned aggregate-components are also not sentient beings; the eighteen elements and the twelve links of dependent arising are also not sentient beings. The name of 'sentient being' cannot be found; it is not through inner emptiness, outer emptiness, or the emptiness of both inner and outer that one obtains the name 'sentient being.' Why? Great Seer, you should know: the eye is a provisional designation, momentarily not coinciding; the ear, nose, tongue, body, and mind are provisional designations, momentarily not coinciding; the five aggregates are a provisional designation, momentarily not coinciding; the thirty-six kinds of impurities are all provisional designations, momentarily not coinciding. In all of these there is no sentient being that can be found, nor is it through the combination of material elements and their various particles that one obtains a sentient being. The material elements and particles are each separate and scattered; those dharmas are also not sentient beings. There is no life, no nourisher, no master, no person, and no self; none of these can be found.

"Furthermore, Great Seer, if there were sentient beings, the tathāgatas would not teach the four kinds of Four Noble Truths. Because there truly is no nature of sentient beings, all the buddhas and tathāgatas attain these dharmas, practice them accordingly, and realize the Tathāgata's body."

At that time, the Great Seer Blazing Light, wishing to attain omniscience, made a great vow, speaking thus: "World-Honored One! Suppose there were a great fire-pit, and I should dwell in it for the entirety of a kalpa. And suppose there were a great mountain like Mount Sumeru, its cliffs steep and towering, reaching as high as the Akaniṣṭha heaven. During that time, I would throw myself from the top and plummet down. And suppose there were a great fire, its mass like the fire at the kalpa's end, blazing fiercely, scorching my body with the five fires. The long days, with one day's duration equal to one kalpa, thirty such days making one month, twelve such months making one year, and such years completing the entire kalpa. I would practice such austerities joyfully and willingly, and would never on account of this even briefly abandon my vigor or cease to seek omniscience."

When the Great Seer Blazing Light spoke these words, all the five-powered seers in the great assembly rose from their seats with reverence and, with palms joined toward the Buddha, spoke thus: "World-Honored One! We seers, from this day forward, shall each courageously and diligently strive to seek anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi."

After the seers had spoken, the World-Honored One emitted various rays of light from between his eyebrows. This light was called Invincible. The buddhas of all ten directions likewise emitted such light from the white tuft between their eyebrows. At that time, through the power of the Buddha's radiance, the great earth shook in six ways and in eighteen aspects: shaking, pervading-shaking, equal-pervading-shaking; surging, pervading-surging, equal-pervading-surging; rousing, pervading-rousing, equal-pervading-rousing; rising, pervading-rising, equal-pervading-rising; trembling, pervading-trembling, equal-pervading-trembling; roaring, pervading-roaring, equal-pervading-roaring; with the east surging and the west subsiding, the west surging and the east subsiding, the south surging and the north subsiding, the north surging and the south subsiding, the center surging and the edges subsiding, the edges surging and the center subsiding, and likewise surging above and subsiding below.

At that time, the buddhas of the ten directions in the sky above Śākyamuni Tathāgata rained down various flowers, various wondrous fragrances, and various heavenly music, to be seen and heard as the mind delights.

Furthermore, the gandharva king and immeasurable hosts of devas all played music of five kinds to delight the Tathāgata. From all the music came forth various voices of praise, singing the praises of the Tathāgata. The heavenly fragrance was carried by a gentle breeze, dense and billowing, pervading the sky before the Tathāgata.

Furthermore, the bodhisattva mahāsattvas and the entire great assembly who had come from the ten directions, leaping with joy, each rained upon the Buddha various flowers, necklaces, precious jewels, mixed fragrances, flower garlands, unguents, powdered incenses, fine garments, banners, and canopies, and such immeasurable, boundless offerings to make offerings to the Tathāgata.

Furthermore, immeasurable, boundless hosts of devas from other regions all felt great joy and in the sky rained heavenly mandārava flowers and great mandārava flowers and such offerings to make offerings to the Tathāgata.

At that time, the great assembly, filled with a sense of wonder, each took the various fine garments they wore and scattered them upon the Buddha as offerings to the Tathāgata.

At that time, the great radiant flame Invincible ascended to the Akaniṣṭha heaven, descended to the Avīci hell, illuminated the great assemblies of all the buddhas of the ten directions, and circled all those tathāgatas. Having done so, the great radiance returned and entered the crown of the World-Honored One's head.

At that time, the Elder Subhūti rose from his seat, went before the Buddha, prostrated at the Buddha's feet, placed his right knee on the ground, knelt with palms joined, and spoke in verse:

> "It is not without cause that Śākyamuni today
> Emits wondrous radiance throughout the lands;
> May the Buddha have compassion for us
> And speak to the assembly to remove their doubts.
> Seeing the World-Honored One display his awesome power,
> Some deepen their doubts, some their joy;
> Among them, some raise a single hand,
> Leaping to praise the Buddha, the World-Honored One.
> Śakra, the Brahma-host, and the Four Kings,
> Filling the sky, extol the Buddha's virtue;
> Rain heavenly fragrance, flowers, and necklaces;
> Instruments unsounded produce wondrous tones."

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the Elder Subhūti: "Do you now see this Great Seer Blazing Light?"

Subhūti said: "Yes, World-Honored One! I have seen him. A true practitioner, I have seen."

Then the World-Honored One further addressed Subhūti: "Subhūti! You should now know: this Great Seer Blazing Light, in the future, after the thousand buddhas of this Fortunate Kalpa have passed, there shall be another kalpa also called Fortunate, in a land called Moon-Lord. In that world he shall attain buddhahood with the name Vipaśyin Tathāgata, Worthy of Offerings, Perfectly Enlightened, complete with the ten epithets.

"Subhūti! You should know: when Vipaśyin Tathāgata appears in the world, any being who hears his name shall benefit without exception, like a wish-fulfilling jewel that satisfies all desires.

"Furthermore, Subhūti, you should know: the eighty-four thousand seers in this present assembly, having heard this Dharma-source, have all attained the stage of non-retrogression. When Maitreya descends into the world, they shall all fulfill the vow-practice of the tenth stage. After three thousand kalpas have passed, they shall attain buddhahood with the name Blazing Light Tathāgata, Truly Thus, Perfectly Enlightened. In this great assembly there are further immeasurable koṭis of bodhisattvas who, having heard this Dharma-source, shall all attain the śūraṅgama samādhi, the supreme-wisdom-awesome samādhi, the tathāgata-received-stage samādhi, the illusion-like samādhi, the four-element-invincible samādhi, the mind-king samādhi, the ocean-treasury samādhi, the training-adornment samādhi, the true-mind-treasury samādhi, and the purity samādhi, among others. Furthermore, koṭis of Ganges-sands of heavenly hosts shall all abide in the patient acceptance of the unborn. Immeasurable hundreds of thousands of monks, nuns, laymen, and laywomen shall all attain the arhat fruit. Ganges-sands of devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, humans and non-humans, those who have not yet aroused the aspiration, shall all arouse the aspiration for anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi.

"Subhūti! You should now know: seeing such great benefits, I emit this radiance."

At that time, the World-Honored One again extended his tongue-mark to cover his face. From that tongue-mark there emerged various colors and various lights, including blue, yellow, red, white, purple, lapis lazuli, vermillion, gold, crystal, and other colors. These lights shone forth to reach immeasurable, boundless worlds, then returned and entered the soles of the Tathāgata's feet.

At that time, the Bodhisattva Inexhaustible Intent rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, placed his right knee on the ground, knelt with palms joined, and said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! The Tathāgata would never without cause display the tongue-mark. We pray the World-Honored One will explain for us: for what cause and condition do you display the tongue-mark and emit this radiance?"

The Buddha told the Bodhisattva Inexhaustible Intent: "Good man! I display this tongue-mark for sentient beings who lack faith. The Tathāgata would never use this tongue to speak falsely."

At that time, the Bodhisattva Inexhaustible Intent said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! If in the future a good man or good woman, within this sūtra, explains even one phrase or one verse for another, how great is the merit? We pray you will explain."

The Buddha said: "Good man! In all the buddha-lands of the ten directions that the buddhas and world-honored ones' eyes can see, if one were to take all the resources and delightful things and offer them all in worship to the buddhas of all ten directions, and even after they enter great parinirvāṇa, to build stūpas of every kind of precious material for their relics: yet if someone within this Dharma-source of true suchness, even one phrase or one verse, discriminates and explains it for another, the merit obtained would be far greater than the former. Furthermore, good man! If when this Dharma-source is being explained, one can offer praise within it, saying 'Excellent! How wonderful!', one should know that such a person is praised by all buddhas. If anyone makes offerings to this sūtra, one should know that person is making offerings to my body."

At that time, the World-Honored One universally surveyed the great assembly. Having done so, he addressed them: "Good men! If this sūtra exists in any place, that ground shall be remembered by all buddhas. Good men! You should know that this sūtra, in the future, shall be great medicine for sentient beings in Jambudvīpa. If anyone within this sūtra reads it themselves, causes others to read it, one time, two times, or three times, one should know that person has personally requested the Tathāgata to turn the wondrous Dharma-wheel. If a good man copies this sūtra himself or causes another to copy it, one should know that person upholds all the profound Dharma-treasury of all buddhas, and shall always obtain joy and swiftly attain peace. In the future they shall attain buddhahood. If a good man or good woman is destined to fall into the evil paths, they shall never hear this wondrous sūtra. Good men and good women who are able to hear this wondrous sūtra, after shedding this body, shall certainly be born in a pure land.

"Furthermore, good men and good women who hear this sūtra, and having heard it feel joy, faith, and delight, uphold it, and widely read, chant, and explain it for others: one should know that such a person shall swiftly attain bodhi, and certainly before long their six faculties shall be complete and their five eyes purified. At the time of death they shall not forget right mindfulness. They shall further attain immeasurable, boundless hundreds of thousands of samādhi and dhāraṇī gates, namely: entry into the samādhi of all buddhas, the samādhi of universal illumination and display, the total-retention treasury samādhi, the crest-jewel-seal samādhi, the consecration-stage samādhi, the contemplation-seal samādhi; and further attain the wordless-satisfaction dhāraṇī, the all-dharmas-invincible dhāraṇī, the doubt-severing dhāraṇī, and the true-suchness definitive-meaning dhāraṇī, and such immeasurable, boundless hundreds of thousands of dhāraṇī gates. They shall further attain the five supernatural powers, and in the place of birth and death their right mindfulness shall not be confused."

At that time, the World-Honored One addressed the bodhisattva mahāsattva Mañjuśrī: "Good man! Because you have already made offerings to immeasurable, boundless hundreds of thousands of buddhas, I entrust this Dharma to you. You should in the future widely explain this Dharma-source for others. Mañjuśrī! The various offerings, various acts of reverence, and various attendances you have made to all those past buddhas, what do you think? The merit from your honoring, praising, and attending them, Mañjuśrī, can it have a limit? Can it be measured?"

Mañjuśrī said: "No, World-Honored One!"

The Buddha said: "Mañjuśrī! If you, in the future, in this sahā world during the time of the five defilements, widely proclaim and circulate this Dharma-source, the merit obtained will be many times greater than that. Mañjuśrī! If in your past, at the presence of all those world-honored ones, you made various offerings of robes and the four requisites, always keeping them well supplied, yet you never explained this Dharma-source on behalf of others, by such reason you would have much fault with regard to those buddhas. If you had never made offerings to even one buddha in the past, but were able to widely explain this profound and wondrous Dharma-source for others, one should know that this constitutes complete offerings to all buddhas and world-honored ones, without fault."

When the Buddha had finished speaking this sūtra, Mañjuśrī and all the bodhisattvas, the Great Seer Blazing Light and all the seers with their retinues, the eight classes of devas and nāgas, spirits and others, the entire great assembly, having heard what the Buddha taught, rejoiced and practiced accordingly.
