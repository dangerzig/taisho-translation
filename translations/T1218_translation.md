# Manjusri Yamantaka Spell Methods (文殊師利耶曼德迦呪法)

## Taisho T1218

**Brought by:** Zongrui (宗叡 *Zōngruì*), Tang
**Fascicles:** 1

*Note: This is an esoteric ritual manual for the wrathful deity Yamantaka (耶曼德迦 yēmàndéjiā, "Death's Destroyer"), a fierce emanation of Manjusri Bodhisattva. The text was brought from China to Japan by the Japanese monk Zongrui, who traveled to Tang China in the mid-ninth century. It is set at Rajagrha (王舍城 Wángshèchéng), where the Great Vajra Yaksa General speaks of Yamantaka's origins. The Buddha, in a former life as the bodhisattva Sumedha (善惠), practiced wrathful king spells through many kalpas before receiving Dipankara Buddha's prediction of future buddhahood. The text is explicitly intended for punishing those who destroy the Buddha-dharma in the degenerate age, NOT for personal vendettas: using it for minor personal affairs is said to bring immediate karmic retribution and future hell. The ritual methods are extremely aggressive, including charnel-ground practices, image painting with blood and corpse materials, homa fire-offerings, ash-consecration, yaksini summoning, and elaborate subjugation procedures. Multiple mantras are given (root mantra, heart mantra, small heart mantra, one-syllable heart mantra "hum") along with over ten mudra seals. Notably, the text contains an explicit reversibility clause: all afflictions caused can be healed by the original spell-caster using lotus, sandalwood, grass-hemp wash, and the Manjusri root mantra.*

---

At that time, the World-Honored One was dwelling in the Bamboo Grove (竹林 *Zhúlín*) within the city of Rajagrha (王舍城 *Wángshèchéng*), discussing with Manjusri (文殊師利 *Wénshūshīlì*) the circumstances of his past encounter with Dipankara Buddha (燃燈佛 *Rándēng Fó*) and receiving the prediction of future buddhahood.

At that time, there was a Great Vajra Yaksa General (大金剛藥叉將 *dà jīngāng yàochā jiàng*) who, from amid the assembly, rose from his seat, bared his right shoulder, placed his right knee on the ground, joined his palms facing the Buddha, and addressed the Buddha: "The dharma that the World-Honored One now discusses with Manjusri concerns past events from former causes. While the World-Honored One was on the causal ground through many kalpas, he cultivated the wrathful king spell methods. In brief: at the very end he attained buddhahood with the name Sumedha (善惠 *Shànhuì*), encountered Dipankara Tathagata (定光如來 *Dìngguāng Rúlái*) and received the prediction that he would be called Śākyamuni Buddha (釋迦牟尼佛 *Shìjiāmóuní Fó*). Later, when he would appear in the saha world (娑婆世界 *suōpó shìjiè*) and achieve buddhahood, I vowed that in this world-realm I would manifest great awesome power to subdue all the obstinate and wicked.

"Dipankara Buddha immediately understood my intention. Now, for the sake of sentient beings in the future age, after the Buddha's final nirvāṇa, when the Buddha-dharma is about to be extinguished, in the time of great terror, in the evil age, when all sravakas and pratyekabuddhas have hidden away and are nowhere to be seen: for the sake of protecting the Buddha-dharma, for the stability of the world, for stopping all evil people, for punishing those who destroy the Buddha-dharma, for the skillful means of accomplishing sentient beings, for fulfilling the inconceivable bodhisattva practices, and for the removal of inconceivable karmic obstructions of sentient beings. For sentient beings of the future age, when the Buddha-dharma is being extinguished, if one can receive and hold these Yamantaka wrathful king spell methods in accordance with the Dharma, that person will definitely attain accomplishment.

"At that time, this is for punishing evil acaryas (阿闍羅 *āshéluo*) and for punishing the uncompassionate and wicked. It is fundamentally not for use against good people; it is only for punishing those who destroy the Dharma. It is not for those who harbor thoughts of personal enmity. It is spoken for the sake of protecting the Buddha-dharma."

## The Earth Shakes

At the time when the Vajra [General] was speaking this Dharma, the Buddha silently entered the Buddha's Transformation Samādhi (佛變化三昧 *Fó biànhuà sānmèi*). Manjusri, the youthful one, also became silently speechless. The great earth shook in six ways. The entire great assembly and all the devas were terrified. The naga kings (龍王 *lóngwáng*), the danava ghosts, the divine mothers, the seizing-ghosts, and all evil people, putanas (布單那 *bùdānnà*), and acaryas were all filled with terror and took refuge in the Dharma King:

"We beseech the World-Honored One and Manjusri, the youthful one, to protect and rescue us, out of compassion. This spell is extremely great and fearsome. It burns and boils us, causing us misery, confusion, and collapse; we fall to the ground not knowing east from west."

At that time Manjusri, the youthful one, announced in a great voice: "All of you, devas, nagas, the eight classes of beings, humans and non-humans, do not be afraid. I now wish here to establish a great teaching. If each and every one follows my teaching and universally practices it, with the great mind of compassion, calling to mind Śākyamuni Tathagata, the honored one endowed with the two feet, and his emanation-body Yamantaka, the wrathful king: when he enters the samādhi of fierce exertion (奮迅三昧 *fènxùn sānmèi*), the spiritual powers and awesome radiance of all the sages of the ten stages of bodhisattvahood are completely extinguished. How much more so for all of you, devas, nagas, eight classes, humans and non-humans, and all auspicious beings! If you hear my great wrathful king proclaim the great divine spell, you will be terrified, confused, and not know east from west.

## Protective Mantras

"All the spells spoken by the Buddha include the Usnisa mantra (佛頂呪 *Fódǐng zhòu*), the Vairocana Cintamani One-Syllable Wheel mantra (毘盧遮那帝珠羅一字輪呪), the Jayabuddha and Jayosnisa mantras, the Avalokitesvara (觀音菩薩 *Guānyīn púsà*) mantra, the Bhrkuti, Tara, Pandara, Vasini, Mahasveta, Yasodhara, and Hayagriva mantra kings. These heart-name mantras are the foremost. If one can recite the One-Syllable Cakravartin mantra, which among the above mantras is the supreme, then any being among the devas, nagas, eight classes, humans or non-humans who recites it even once will be protected from Yamantaka's disturbance.

"The Mamaki, Sankara, Sikhara, Vajra, Krodha, Musthini, and Rosana mantras: if one calls to mind the Vajra retinue, one will also obtain protection. The mantra of Ever-Fragrant King Bodhisattva (常香王菩薩 *Chángxiāngwáng Púsà*) and the mantra of Samantabhadra Bodhisattva (普賢菩薩 *Pǔxián Púsà*), if one calls them to mind, one will also obtain protection and freedom from fear. The Manibhadra mantra, and [the mantras of] the sravakas and pratyekabuddhas, if one calls them to mind and makes offerings, one will also be free from fear. The Sriharapara-deva and the king of the formless heavens and others who have departed from desire: if one calls them to mind, one will also be free from fear.

"If one constantly calls to mind and makes offerings to the Three Jewels and constantly trusts the Buddha-dharma, such a person will be free from illness throughout their life, and Yamantaka will be unable to harm any of these people. Those who can receive and hold [this teaching] should not give rise to a mind of retreat.

## Image-Painting Method

"If there is an evil person who is destroying the Buddha-dharma, without regard for auspicious or inauspicious days and without attending to the positions of the constellations, on the fourteenth or eighth day of the dark half of the month, take the clothing of a dead person from a charnel ground (屍陀林 *shítuólín*). If one can obtain a brahmin's garment, that is even better. Dye it with blood, then wash and sun-dry it. Invite an artist to paint the image. If one paints it oneself, that is the very best. The countenance should be fierce and inspiring of fear.

"Paint it in the charnel ground, and it must be completed within three days. On the fifteenth of the dark half, take the fat of a dead person for a lamp. The spell-master faces south; the image faces north. Recite the spell to establish the boundary.

"The spell-master sits on a skull (劫波 *jiébō*) and performs the rite. In the first watch, the enemy and evil person become feverish and confused. In the second watch, their torment intensifies until they are confused and do not know east from west. In the third watch, they collapse to the ground. If the spell-master is joyful and turns their mind, the affliction can be removed and they recover. If the spell-master does not turn to compassion toward the evil person's afflicted state, that person will only have others discuss them in vain, as their body withers and dries until death, and their household and dwelling are all ruined and scattered.

"The method of the rite is as follows: the image has six faces, six arms, and six legs. It is black, with a slender waist and a kilaka-necklace. The faces are wrathful and fierce. The hair stands straight up like flames of fire shooting upward. The eyes are red as blood. It wears a tiger-skin loincloth. The hands hold a club and various implements. It has three eyes on each face. The body is smoke-colored or black-cloud-colored, making a fearsome appearance. It rides a water buffalo (水牛 *shuǐniú*) and can cause all beings to be terrified.

"When painting this image, use blood mixed as the red pigment, with ox fat and human fat placed inside a skull-cap to mix the colors. Take a dead person's hair for the brush. When the painting is complete, make extensive offerings. Use sandalwood (紫檀 *zǐtán*) to polish the altar and use red flowers as offerings. Take the fat of a dead person for the lamp, dead person's flesh for the incense, and make offerings three times daily. When the image-painting is complete, pay the artist generously. Do not let the painter harbor resentment; one must give them ample wealth. Afterward, the spell-master holds the spell and swiftly attains accomplishment and perfect fulfillment.

"If one does not properly perform the boundary-protection and body-protection rites before holding the spell, one will certainly be killed by the [spirit's] harm, and one's six kinds of relatives will all perish. If one performs the rite properly, one will obtain protection.

## Fire-Offering Methods

"If the practitioner holds properly, then all subsequent rites one performs will be accomplished. If the one performing the rite does so only against those who destroy the Buddha-dharma, they will be utterly annihilated without remainder. If done for one's own minor affairs, it must not be performed. In this life one will swiftly meet with misfortune, and in the afterlife one will reap the fruit of hell.

"For the fire-offering method (燒火法 *shāohuǒ fǎ*): place the image in a clean location, with the image facing north. Before the image, make a fire-pit on the ground, of whatever size one wishes. Burn dry thorny kindling, and recite the Yamantaka mantra, invoking the Fire God (火天 *huǒtiān*) using the seal of Yama (閻羅王 *Yánluó wáng*). First burn one thousand and eight offerings in this way, always with an angry mind calling the evil person's name. This achieves the rite. In the first watch, the man or woman dies. In the second watch, the wife dies, and relatives and retinue are all annihilated. In the third watch, that evil person dies.

"If one further recites the spell oneself wishing to destroy enemies, all enemies' entire clans will be annihilated. If one recites the spell at midnight, the entire country can be annihilated: either fire descends from heaven and burns the land, or incessant rain falls, or foreign nations punish that land, or a famine arises, or thunderbolts strike and kill people, or epidemics arise in the country, or the acarya's body withers and dries until death, or ghosts and spirits disturb dwellings day and night making sleep impossible, and raksasas, yaksas, wolves, tigers, and leopards ceaselessly cause constant terror and disturbance beyond normal measure. Mahesvara (大自在天 *Dàzìzài tiān*), Brahma (梵王 *Fànwáng*), Indra (帝釋 *Dìshì*), and the worldly powers cannot provide protection or rescue. If the spell-master's angry mind does not cease, no one can save the person targeted.

"If the spell-master recites the spell day and night, constantly chanting without forgetting, they can destroy the people of an entire Jambudvipa (閻浮提 *Yánfútí*).

"On the fourteenth of the dark half, place the image in a charnel ground, in a secluded place, or beneath a solitary tree, or in a stone cave, or in an empty house, or in a great temple, or on a seashore, or in a place where one dwells alone without anyone. After placing the image, perform the rite as one wishes. The accomplishments of this Dharma are inconceivable. These Yamantaka wrathful king spell methods are also inconceivable. The accomplishment of the spell-reciter is also inconceivable. Not even all the bodhisattvas can protect those whom Yamantaka punishes as evil people. How much less can Mahesvara and the like provide protection? Still less can sravakas and pratyekabuddhas override Yamantaka's spell-punishment of evil people.

"If the spell-master is angry and does not relent, there is no escape. If escape is needed, the original spell-master must turn their mind to compassion toward the afflicted person, and then they can be freed from suffering.

## Medicinal Substances Method

"Another method: take chinaberry leaves (苦練葉 *kǔliànyè*), white mustard seed, oil, poison, human blood, human flesh, salt, pepper, long pepper (畢鉢 *bìbō*), dried ginger, mustard seed, and cantharides (蟊盃), and grind them into powder. Mix with sour jujube juice, wine, sea salt, naduta root, kustha root, castor root, yavaksara, barley ash, indigo flower, madhana root, garlic, and parasa. Combine all the above medicines in equal parts. Before the image, recite one spell per offering and burn to a total of one thousand and eight. This can destroy enemies and their retinue.

"This medicine method should be performed in the first watch. If it reaches the targeted country, famine and epidemic will arise, yaksas and raksasas will fill the world, fires will break out, hail will fall and kill, and the villages and towns of that country will all be ruined. The home country will experience various misfortunes.

"Another method: take naduta root; recite one spell per offering and burn to a total of one thousand and eight. The evil person will become deranged and deluded. Or take the three pungents and perform the same spell-and-burn one thousand and eight times; this will cause the evil person's body and mind to be feverishly tormented beyond all reckoning. Or take sour tamarind and perform the same spell one thousand and eight times; this will cause the targeted person's body to tremble with cold. After two days or seven days and seven nights, that person will wither and die. Before performing the fire method, first form the seal.

"Another method: take the patron-deity or natal star of the targeted person. Using charnel-ground charcoal, draw the form of that deity or star-spirit. Write the targeted person's name beneath the feet. Place it before the painted image. The spell-master treads on its head with his foot, reciting the spell with wrathful anger. The targeted person will then contract various diseases or die. If they do not die, their illness will be severe, or evil ghosts and spirits will possess them. Their retinue will likewise [suffer].

## The Yaksini Method

At that time, the Yamantaka Vajra, having spoken in the great assembly, bowed at the Buddha's feet and stood silently. Then, for the further benefit of sentient beings, he again spoke of the methods and accomplishment procedures for the various yaksini (夜叉女 *yèchā nǚ*) spirits. This was spoken for those who are lustful, for those seeking wealth and treasure, for those unable to follow the Buddha's teaching, for those who do not understand contemplative practice, for those unable to produce dhyana, and for those who from beginningless time have revolved in birth and death unable to feel weariness. If one perceives the dharma-nature and comprehends the true principle, one should not desire this method. He then spoke the mantra:

"Om iristi mahanagani hum phat svaha."

"This dharma-mantra: every night after midnight, one should rise and constantly recite it, without a fixed number of recitations. After one full month, without sleeping from [the final night] until dawn, after the month is complete, the yaksini-raksasi women will come, become one's wife, and grant whatever one seeks, fully fulfilled. They will deliver twenty-five gold coins daily and also provide the elixir of longevity.

"At that time, immeasurable hundreds of thousands of yaksa, raksasa, and yaksini dharma-methods will all be accomplished. Simply recite the Yamantaka mantra before the image and perform the rite; all the spirits and ghosts one wishes to see will gather, and whatever one seeks will be swiftly and fully obtained."

## The Root Mantra

At that time, the Vajra Bodhisattva bowed at the Buddha's feet and beside the Buddha spoke the Yamantaka mantra, for the sake of punishing evil people and subduing sentient beings. He then spoke the mantra:

"Namo samanta-buddhanam, apratihata-sasananam. Namo pratigabuddharya-sravakam. Namo sarva-bodhisattvanam, dasabhumi-prapustaye, sarva-bodhisattvanam, mahabodhisattvanam. Tadyatha: om khakha khakha, hehi, dusta-sattva-damaka, asmusa-raprasubha-saha, satocaturu-bhujacaturu-mukha-satcarana, gagaga, caca, mahavikrta-naga, kahakad-vikrta-nadana, sarvabhutabhayankaratatahasa, nadinivyakarama-manima-sana, bhutakurukuru, sarvakarma hum, chinda chinda, sarvamantra hum, bhinda bhinda, bhinda-pramudhara hum, akarsaya akarsaya, sarvabuddha hum, nirmada nirmada, sarvadusta-sattvam, prabhesaya-mandala-madhe, baivaca-hata-vicitraka, rakurukuru, mama karicchinda, hadadaha, dava, vacavaca, mavilambha mavilambha, samaya-manusma hum hum, sasa, phat, sphotaya sphotaya, sarvasatpari-puraka, ehyehi, bhagavan, kincara-yasi, mama-samsthanasadhaya svaha."

## Yamantaka's Declaration

At that time, the Great Vajra addressed the devas: "This wrathful king Yamantaka's mantra-king can control Yama (閻羅王 *Yánluó wáng*) and perform various punishments and subjugations. It can also control all mantras, all divine-immortal mantras, and mantras that punish evil people who act without reason. It can also control the mantras of bodhisattvas of the ten stages and worldly mantras, with limitless power and function. This Yamantaka wrathful king: all the accomplishments proclaimed in all spell methods [are achieved by] holding this wrathful king's [mantra]. Simply by reciting this mantra, one can also command all other mantra-spirits. This Yamantaka wrathful king mantra need only be recited with single-minded focus to achieve accomplishment."

## The Heart Mantra

He then spoke the heart mantra:

"Namo sarva-buddha-bodhisattvanam, apratihata-sasananam. Om karakara, kurukuru, mama-dhriyam-svabhasvabha, sarva-vighanan, nahanaha, sarva-vajra-vinayaka hum, mauradhataka-dvivittakala-mahavikrta-rupina, vajravajra, sarvadusta hum, mahaganapati-sivicitrakarabandhabandha, sarva-grahamsa, mukhasabhucasa, cana-ruranama-nayasnumanaya-marahamandayan-divana-naya, mavilambha mavilambha, rahurahu, mandala-madhi-prabhijaya, samaya-manusma hum hum hum hum hum hum, svaphat svaphat svaha."

## Seals and Mudras

At that time, the great wrathful Vajra looked around at the great assembly in all four directions, and wishing to further expound the wrathful-dharma methods, he announced: "All of you devas and all ghosts and spirits dwelling in the dharma-realm: when performing the rite, first form the seals (印 *yìn*) for body-protection, and accomplishment will come swiftly. Then perform the fire method, causing all ghosts, spirits, and maras to be completely subdued and unable to cause harm.

"When performing the rite, first take the wrathful painted image and install it in a temple of Mahesvara. Take poison, red mustard seed, fat, and blood, mix them, and use chinaberry leaves as offerings for the painted image. Take a dead person's entrails and lay them across. The spell-master takes a skull for their own seat. When the spell-master recites the spell, with the left hand they hold the aforementioned skull, and with the right hand they hold a knife and strike at [the image of] Mahesvara. The spell-master visualizes with wrathful intention the aforementioned evil person who bullies them or others, or those who are wealthy and arrogant, with evil companions, who are fearsome, who have great wealth, or who are ministers and nobles. One then performs the aforementioned method with the doors closed, sitting unclothed. If a lay spell-master, with hair unbound and left foot treading on [the image of] Mahesvara, recites the spell, Mahesvara's body will spontaneously split in two. In the sky there will be a mighty roaring sound. The spell-master should not be afraid but should steady their own mind. On that very day, the lords and the like will be possessed by tiger-ghosts or by evil ghosts and raksasas. If one continues reciting the spell, all the evil people will lose their lives. If one recites the spell at night, the household members and retinue will all [perish].

"Another method: face a temple of Mahesvara for an entire day, fasting for one night. On the fourteenth of the dark half, take wood from a charnel ground to make a fire. Take red mustard seed mixed with blood and poison; recite one spell per offering. In the sky there will be a great sound, and at that time all the hungry ghosts will assemble and appear before the spell-master. One must not be afraid. The spell-master tells the aforementioned hungry ghosts: 'Destroy my enemies and evil people for me.' The ghosts reply to the spell-master: 'We will certainly fulfill your wish.' They then vanish. Whether the evil person is a hundred or a thousand li away, near or far, the ghosts will destroy all enemies completely. In the time of a single thought, all will be eliminated, and their lineage will be entirely scattered. Such methods and limitless others can all be accomplished.

## The Ash-Consecration Method

"Another method: in an empty and clean dwelling, wearing clean garments, take white cotton cloth. Recite one spell per offering and burn to a total of one thousand and eight. With both hands, scoop up the ash from the aforementioned cotton, and wrap it in clean cloth. Take two clay bowls, placing a ball of ash in each. Then recite the spell to perform the body-protection rite. Take the two packets of ash to the charnel ground.

"On the eighth or fourteenth of the dark half, the spell-master faces south. Place the two clay bowls of ash before you. The spell-master, bare-bodied with hair unbound, fully exposed and wrathful, recites the spell ten thousand times. This achieves accomplishment. Among the ghosts and spirits, any who try to seize the ash before you must not be allowed to have it. If the ghosts and spirits try to take the ash by force, immediately call to mind the Yamantaka one-syllable mantra:

"Hum.

"Making this sound, all ghosts and spirits will be completely scattered and destroyed, and all obstacles will be entirely purified. While holding the ash, carefully note which hand holds which portion; you must not become confused or forget. Mark left and right. The spell-master must not be careless; the spell must always be recited with body-protection. When dawn arrives and the sun rises, bathe and put on clean clothing, then return home.

"After returning home, the ash held in the right hand is used for body-protection. Against all ghosts, humans, four-footed creatures, all sentient beings, devas, nagas, and yaksas: scatter this ash upon devas, nagas, ghosts, spirits, and humans, and they will be subdued. The ash held in the left hand: if scattered upon a man or woman, they will be subdued.

"The ash held in the right hand, if applied to the aforementioned person, will cause the man's organ to become impotent, like a eunuch's (黃門 *huángmén*). If scattered on a man's groin, he will be unable to perform the act of desire. If scattered on a woman's private parts, she will be unable to engage in the act of desire with a man; her opening will close. Only her rightful husband can engage with her; her opening will open for him. Others cannot gain access. For a man's functioning, this is the same.

"If this ash is scattered upon any man or woman, regardless of status, all will be subdued and will act according to one's wishes. [Further details of the ash's effects on the human body are described.]

"Furthermore, this ash: the spell-master's mind may generate attachment. If the spell-master has any evil person in any direction, or any place where they wish something accomplished, they take this ash and scatter it following the direction of the wind, and it will be as they wish. Whether done personally or through an agent, both work.

"For places where one sits or lies, beds, benches, all adornments, clothing, blankets, felts, and all such things, as well as screens, walls, and the four walls of houses: if the spell-master scatters ash on these, all enemies will contract evil illness. Some will have lice that emerge and bite terribly, or bedbugs that emerge and bite so that the person's body becomes swollen and putrid, unable to find rest. After several days of suffering, they will reach the point of death. No physician in the world and no medicine can cure this person's suffering. The mantra-power of all the devas also cannot remove it. Only my spell-master can rescue them; all others are powerless.

"If the spell-master turns their mind and wishes to heal them, take grass-hemp, blue lotus, and white sandalwood, grind them into a fine powder, mix with cold water, and apply to the sick person from head to foot, using the Manjusri root mantra seal. The person will then be healed.

## Further Methods

"Another method: if one thinks of a worthless woman, scatter the aforementioned ash following the wind, and she will contract evil illness, and her private parts and breasts will all become swollen, putrid, and fall away. For a man likewise: the organ, hair, beard, and breasts will rot away entirely. Such methods can produce limitless harmful effects. Whether the spell-master acts personally or through an agent, both work. For those whom one loves and cherishes, one should not perform this method. For those whom one does not love, one may perform this method, but do not let anyone know.

"Whatever illness the spell-master thinks of, the targeted person will contract that illness. If touched on the head, they get a headache. If touched on the mouth, sores immediately grow on the mouth. If touched on the heart, the heart aches. If touched on the feet, the feet ache. Pain, illness, wasting, and ruin [can all be caused]. One can summon people from afar and bring them before you to subdue them. If traveling through dangerous and frightening places, scatter the ash following the wind.

"With both hands, scatter ash upon city walls and gate-towers: these and people's walls will all collapse, or fire will arise and burn them. Soldiers and generals who encounter this ash will also be scattered and destroyed, or severe epidemics will arise, or the people in the city will open the gates and flee in scattered flight. Or if one is oppressed by another's offense, scatter the ash following the wind; that army will contract great and powerful diseases, or malaria and the like.

"This method can produce various harmful effects. Whatever one's mind thinks and acts upon, all will be as one wishes. One's own household will obtain protection, and one's companions will also obtain protection.

## Reversal Method

"As for reversing the above-described methods and removing them: take the Yamantaka wrathful painted image and install it on the altar. Take a bowl of milk-porridge, recite one spell per offering and burn to a total of one thousand and eight. All enemies will then be restored to their former state without pain."

After this come [methods concerning] the yaksini, raksasi, and spirit-women and the practices of those who deal with them.

## Further Heart Mantras

The Yamantaka Vajra, the great wrathful king, then spoke the heart-mantra method:

"Om grstri krta-nada, samasatru-nasaya, satambhaya, phat phat phat svaha."

"This heart-mantra should be constantly recited without interruption. Each day, make a vow saying: 'May all the wicked who cause various harms be broken, destroyed, scattered, and annihilated without remainder.'

"The small heart-mantra:

"Om trikala-rupa hum kham svaha.

"This small heart-mantra also uses the same seal as above. One should further recite it one thousand and eighty times at the appropriate time to accomplish the Dharma.

"The supreme one-syllable heart-mantra:

"Hum.

"This one-syllable heart-mantra also uses the aforementioned trident seal. This mantra can win the praise of all the Buddhas. It can also eliminate all evil and wrong-viewed people. It can also subdue all ghosts and spirits and accomplish all wholesome deeds. Each day, constantly recite this mantra one thousand and eighty times for swift accomplishment.

"These Yamantaka mantras are not limited by time. Whenever there is a matter of difficulty, perform them immediately. Generally all rites should be performed on the dark half of the month, or at noon or midnight, and all will accord with one's wishes.

"A further heart-mantra:

"Om trilokya-rupa hum kham svaha."

## The Seal Methods

The Yamantaka Vajra then spoke the seal-division (印部 *yìnbù*), the various seals and the binding-all body-protection seal.

**Binding-All Seal:** First, with the left hand, the ring finger crosses toward the tiger-mouth [the space between thumb and index finger] and extends outward. The right hand likewise. The two little fingers intertwine and enter through the ring-finger opening, extending out past the tiger-mouth. The two middle fingers stand upright with tips touching. The two index fingers bend and rest on the upper joint of the middle fingers. The two thumbs stand straight, touching the middle joint of the middle fingers. This is the Binding-All Seal.

**Summoning Seal:** Based on the previous seal, the two index fingers point downward. This is the Summoning Seal.

**Great Trident Seal:** Based on the previous seal, the two index fingers open and stand straight, separated from the middle fingers by half a cun (寸). This is the Great Trident Seal.

**Incense-Burner Seal (offering seal):** With the left hand, the middle finger wraps behind the ring finger. The thumb presses on the upper joint of the ring finger. The index finger and little finger bend slightly. The right hand's index finger hooks the left index finger. The right middle finger hooks the left little finger. The right ring finger and [right] index finger bend into the palm, with the thumb pressing on the nail of the little finger. This is the Incense-Burner Seal.

**Mutual-Subjugation Seal:** The two hands intertwine. The five fingers bend into the palms. The right thumb stands upright. The left thumb presses on the ring-finger joint. This is the Mutual-Subjugation Seal.

**Trident Seal:** Based on the previous Mutual-Subjugation Seal, only the two middle fingers stand upright touching each other. This is the Trident Seal.

**Subduing-All-Evil Seal:** Based on the previous seal, the right thumb bends into the palm. This is the Subduing-All-Evil Seal.

**Mace Seal:** Based on the previous seal, the two thumbs are changed to stand straight upright. This is the Mace Seal.

**Iron-Arrow Seal:** For punishing all enemies. Based on the previous Mace Seal, the two thumbs open slightly apart. This is the Iron-Arrow Seal.

**Fang Seal:** The two hands are placed together, five fingers standing straight upright. The two ring fingers bend into the palms, touching the second joint of the thumbs. The middle fingertips each approach close to the two little fingers and remain thus.

**Throwing Seal:** The two palms are placed together flat, five fingers standing straight upright. While held before the chest, rotate this seal to the right side with a throwing gesture, then pull to the left.
