# 佛說出生無邊門陀羅尼儀軌
## Ritual Manual of the Dhāraṇī of the Infinite Gate of Arising, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1010

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空)

---

The mantra is as given in the sūtra.

> Practicing this dhāraṇī of the infinite gate of arising,
> Directing the three kinds of karma toward [transformation], relying on the three gates of mystery —
> Namely the three vajras: the body-mudrā, speech-mantra,
> And the mind abiding in samādhi, by entering the three equalities —
> Through skillful abiding in yoga, one's own body becomes identical with the honored deity.
> While yet an ordinary being, one attains perfect enlightenment; this Dharma is the most profound secret.
>
> The king of scriptures, the Mahāvairocana Sūtra, declares that even those of the rank of eka-jāti-pratibaddha
> Are not within its sphere; how much more so those of inferior wisdom.
> Like obtaining the jewel of the wheel-turning king, guard it secretly and do not speak of it carelessly.
>
> This honored one is Karma-pāramitā Bodhisattva;
> By abiding in [the practice of] arising, she manifests the form of a young woman,
> Clearly revealing the Great Compassionate Mother; all buddhas dwell in this wisdom.
> Able to universally manifest form-bodies, she dwells in the great mind of bodhi.
> In full-lotus posture upon a lotus throne, with the mahāmudrā and dignified bearing,
> Her appearance is the same as that of Amoghasiddhi Tathāgata.
>
> The samādhi-hand forms the vajra fist; at the heart she holds a lotus flower
> Bearing the prajñā-brahma scripture. The wisdom-hand displays the mudrā of expounding the Dharma,
> Raising the palm and extending the five finger-wheels, with the peak of the forbearance-finger manifesting the karma-vajra.
> The crossed vajra wheel adorns the crown; the head is ornamented with the five Tathāgatas.
> The entire body is grass-green in color; moreover, upon the limbs of the body,
> The eight syllabic gates are arranged:
>
> The syllable PA dwells at the heart; the syllable RA forms the ūrṇā mark.
> The syllable VA is placed at the tip of the tongue; the syllable JA is placed at the head,
> In blue color forming the uṣṇīṣa mark. The syllable KA is placed in the wisdom-palm,
> In green color forming the karma-[wheel], the wheel of the twelve [nidānas].
> The syllable DHA in yellow is placed in the samādhi-palm, forming the floral prajñā scripture.
> The syllable ŚA is settled at the vipaśyanā foot; the syllable KṢA is placed at the śamatha foot.
> Five syllables are all pure white, like snow, milk, a goose, and the moon;
> These syllables form the cakra-mark.
>
> The samaya-mudrā:
> This is the empowerment crown-mudrā.
> The method of sequence from beginning to end
> Is the same as the ritual manuals of all [other] sections.
>
> Eighty koṭi buddhas
> Surround and dwell around this honored one;
> Moreover there are eight bodhisattvas
> Abiding in the eight directions,
> Together with eight yakṣas,
> The four means of attraction and eight offerings,
> Arranged in their proper sequence,
> Completing the secret maṇḍala.
>
> Reciting and upholding the mantra scripture,
> What is accomplished accords with the original teaching.
> The ritual procedures of practice,
> Their collection and discernment is now complete.

*Colophon: On the twenty-ninth day of the first month of the sixteenth year of the Genroku era [1703], this was re-collated against the text of the Venerable Jōgon. [Signed] Sonkyō.*
