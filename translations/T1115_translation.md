# 觀自在菩薩阿麼𪘨法
## Method of the Amogha-rajñī of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1115

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown.

---

Long ago in the kingdom of Khotan there was a devout layman who earnestly recited the mantra of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, wishing to see the Great Saint. Year after year passed without fulfilling his heart's desire, yet he held firm to his resolve and redoubled his diligence. One evening he heard a voice in the sky saying: "Go to the neighboring kingdom and seek audience with its king." The practitioner followed these words and went to that kingdom. He heard that the king's laws were extremely severe and harsh, and that all who transgressed were punished. The practitioner thereupon sought audience with the king, who received him kindly and asked what he desired. He stated his original wish, and was then led deep into the inner palace, where everything was like a buddha-land. The king was in fact Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva. The practitioner addressed the bodhisattva: "Great Saint, why does your governance employ execution and punishment? Where is the great compassion?" The bodhisattva replied: "The sentient beings of this land are stubborn and hard to govern. Out of compassion, I have been guiding them, manifesting a division-body as their king. Those who are punished and executed are all transformation-beings, to make these fierce customs fear authority and follow the wind of reform. When the governance is accomplished, I shall withdraw."

The practitioner was detained by the king for several months, during which he gained thorough understanding of the Mahāyāna. When he later asked to return home, the king silently granted permission. The practitioner addressed the bodhisattva: "Your disciple has been fortunate to meet the Great Saint's compassion, to see and hear the dharma, and to personally receive your compassionate transformation. For twelve years I labored before obtaining this verification. In future ages, those who seek to see the bodhisattva, if their heart's wish does not change, will endure long suffering in vain with no result, and the moment doubt arises they will slander the dharma and incur offense. I beseech your compassion to show the essential dharma for the future." The bodhisattva said: "I possess the Amogha-rajñī mantra-phrases. I shall now bestow them upon you. The offerings are simple and the time required is not long. If one recites with a devoted mind, I shall appear in person and fulfill their wishes." The practitioner, having received this instruction, returned to his home country and transmitted it widely. Devout laypeople in the five regions of India obtained verification, and their number was not small. All who practiced diligently according to the teaching saw the bodhisattva. This is the ferry across the five turbidities, the raft upon the ocean of suffering. The Amogha-rajñī mantra of Avalokiteśvara says:

*Namo ratna-trayāya namaḥ ārya-avalokiteśvarāya bodhisattvāya mahāsattvāya mahā-kāruṇikāya tadyathā amogha-rajñī prama-rajñī vimala-rajñī tara-rajñī saṃpra-priya-rajñī irini girini kirini āruhani nīruhani dhārani dhāraṇī dhapani avatārani sarva-tārani lepani alepani naphana naphana naphani naḍana naḍana naḍani juju-ni smṛdantu me āśamana para-pūrdhani yaṃ namaḥ ārya-avalokiteśvarāya svāhā*

"Furthermore, if one who recites and upholds this mantra goes to a quiet, clean place, or to an araṇya, or before a śarīra-stūpa, places an image of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, makes offerings according to one's means, bathes with clean water, eats pure food, and alone in the bodhimaṇḍa recites throughout one day and one night until dawn, the Great Saint will appear. All wishes will be fulfilled, all people will love and respect one, one will obtain the wisdom of past lives, and one can realize the samādhi of the ten bhūmis manifesting before one. If one has committed the five heinous crimes, after two days and two nights one will see the bodhisattva."

At that time the bodhisattva-king, having spoken this mantra, told the practitioner: "This mantra has great spiritual power. It can extinguish all the karmic obstructions of sentient beings, swiftly bring spiritual verification, and fulfill all wishes. If one wishes to recite this mantra and seek to see Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, the rituals of boundary-sealing, empowerment, and offering should all follow the procedures described in the great teachings of the *Susiddhikara Sūtra* and the *Vajraśekhara Yoga*. First one must devotedly recite the mantra a full thousand times.

"In a clean place, or an araṇya, or a mountain forest, or before the śarīra-stūpa of a clean monastery, establish a bodhimaṇḍa for Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva according to the method. Place an image of Avalokiteśvara and make offerings with utmost sincerity according to one's means. Bathe cleanly, wear fresh clean garments, carefully guard the three karmas without forgetting, refraining from both delight and anger. Alone for one day and one night, cutting off all speech, with the mind not straying to other objects, single-mindedly contemplating Avalokiteśvara without interruption, recite this mantra. Burn aloeswood incense or five-flavor blended incense, and do not let the incense smoke be interrupted. Thought after thought in succession, single-mindedly seek to see the bodhisattva.

"At the four corners of the altar, place a burning censer at each. Also at each corner place a cup of fragrant water: one of rock-candy water, one of pure cow's milk, one of turmeric-fragrant water, and one of the usual white sandalwood-fragrant water. For the offerings: cakes, fruits, porridge, rice, and the like. The food offerings made at dawn should be collected at the regular mealtime of each day, taken to a clean place and given to the birds. Then set out newly prepared food and drink as offerings. At evening, remove the offerings as before and again set out freshly prepared ones. Continue until dawn. Throughout the day and night, one must not allow any drowsiness or laziness, lest the vision be difficult to obtain.

"Also visualize that from the ūrṇā between the bodhisattva's brows, two beams of light radiate: one beam goes straight into all the hells, rescuing suffering beings and liberating them; one beam enters the crown of the practitioner. Visualize it pervading one's own body, so that all evil karmic deeds are completely extinguished and one becomes inwardly and outwardly pure. Through such visualization and recitation, with a devoted mind one will certainly see me. I shall appear in person for that one and fulfill all their wishes. You should receive and uphold this.

"Even one who has committed the five heinous crimes of unremitting retribution, by diligently reciting for two days and two nights, will certainly see the bodhisattva. How much more so one who is originally pure? The only exception is one who lacks a devoted mind.

"The method for painting the image of Abhaya Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, also called the Amogha-rajñī Avalokiteśvara image: the form is like that of a Pāramitā Bodhisattva, with three eyes and four arms, riding a white lion whose head turns toward the left knee. On the head is a jeweled crown adorned with white lotus flowers. The front two hands play a phoenix-headed harp. The left hand holds a makara fish. The right hand holds a white auspicious bird. The left foot is bent upon the lion's neck, and the right foot hangs down. The body is adorned with celestial garments and jeweled necklaces, the entire body blazing with radiant light. The face is compassionate, gazing attentively to the left.

"The mudrā: join both hands in a lotus position. The two index fingers form hooks at the heart. At each recitation of the word *svāhā*, move the hooks. Sincerely form the mudrā and recite one hundred and eight times without releasing the mudrā; this constitutes one session of practice. One should have an assistant hold the beads to keep count. Thus, three times each day, all according to this ritual."
