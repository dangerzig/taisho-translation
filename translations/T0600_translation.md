# 十善業道經
## Sūtra of the Ten Wholesome Courses of Action

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0600

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Śikṣānanda (實叉難陀)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was in the palace of the Sāgara Nāga King, together with a great assembly of eight thousand bhikṣus and thirty-two thousand bodhisattva mahāsattvas.

At that time the World-Honored One said to the Nāga King: "Because the mental conceptions of all sentient beings differ, the karma they create also differs, and for this reason there is transmigration through the various destinies. Nāga King! Do you see in this assembly and in the great ocean that the forms and colors of the various kinds are each different? All of this, without exception, is brought about by the mind creating wholesome or unwholesome karma of body, speech, and mind. Yet the mind has no color and cannot be apprehended; it is merely the arising of false dharmas through aggregation, ultimately without a master, without a self or anything belonging to a self. Although each follows their karma and manifests differently, in truth there is no agent among them. Therefore all dharmas are inconceivable; their self-nature is like an illusion. When the wise understand this, they should cultivate wholesome karma, because the aggregates, sense-fields, and elements produced thereby will all be upright and beautiful, and those who see them will not be wearied.

"Nāga King! Observe the body of the Buddha, born from hundreds of billions of merits, adorned with the marks, radiant and resplendent, outshining the entire great assembly. Even if immeasurable billions of sovereign Brahmā kings were present, they would all be invisible beside him. All who gaze upon the body of the Tathāgata are dazzled. Observe also these great bodhisattvas, of wondrous color, pure and dignified; all of them have been born from the cultivation and accumulation of wholesome karma and merit. And the devas, nāgas, eight classes of beings, and other beings of great power have also been born from wholesome karma and merit. Now, the various sentient beings in the great ocean, whose forms are coarse and ugly, whether great or small, have all created unwholesome karma of body, speech, and mind through the various conceptions of their own minds, and therefore each receives their retribution according to their karma. You should now train accordingly and also cause sentient beings to understand cause and effect and cultivate wholesome karma. You should abide in right view without wavering and not fall again into the views of annihilation or eternalism. Rejoice in and reverently serve the various fields of merit! In this way, you too will receive the reverence and offerings of humans and devas.

"Nāga King! You should know that bodhisattvas have one dharma that can sever the suffering of all the evil destinies. What is this one dharma? It is to constantly recollect, contemplate, and observe wholesome dharmas day and night, causing wholesome dharmas to increase moment by moment, not allowing even the slightest unwholesomeness to intrude. This is what can cause all evil to be permanently severed and wholesome dharmas to be perfected, and one will constantly be close to the Buddhas, bodhisattvas, and the rest of the noble assembly. What is meant by 'wholesome dharmas'? They are the human and heavenly states, the awakening of the śrāvaka, the awakening of the pratyekabuddha, and the supreme enlightenment. All of these depend upon this dharma as their root and are thereby accomplished; therefore it is called 'wholesome dharma.' This dharma is precisely the ten wholesome courses of action. What are the ten? They are the permanent abandonment of killing, stealing, sexual misconduct, false speech, divisive speech, harsh speech, idle talk, covetousness, anger, and wrong views.

"Nāga King! If one abandons killing, one immediately accomplishes ten freedoms from vexation. What are the ten? First, one universally grants fearlessness to all sentient beings. Second, one always gives rise to great compassion toward sentient beings. Third, one permanently severs the habitual tendencies of anger. Fourth, one's body is always free from illness. Fifth, one's lifespan is long. Sixth, one is always protected by non-human beings. Seventh, one never has bad dreams and sleeps and wakes in happiness. Eighth, enmity is eliminated and all grudges dissolve of themselves. Ninth, one has no fear of the evil destinies. Tenth, at the end of one's life one is born in heaven. These are the ten. If one can dedicate them toward anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi, upon becoming a Buddha in the future, one attains the Buddha's sovereignty over one's own lifespan.

"Furthermore, Nāga King! If one abandons stealing, one immediately attains ten kinds of trustworthy dharmas. What are the ten? First, one's wealth accumulates in abundance and cannot be scattered or destroyed by kings, thieves, water, fire, or unworthy heirs. Second, one is loved by many people. Third, one is not deceived or wronged by others. Fourth, one is praised in the ten directions. Fifth, one has no anxiety about harm. Sixth, one's good reputation spreads. Seventh, one is fearless among assemblies. Eighth, one is complete and without deficiency in wealth, life, beauty, strength, ease, and eloquence. Ninth, one always harbors the intention to give. Tenth, at the end of one's life one is born in heaven. These are the ten. If one can dedicate them toward anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi, upon becoming a Buddha in the future, one attains the pure wisdom of great bodhi.

"Furthermore, Nāga King! If one abandons sexual misconduct, one immediately attains four kinds of dharmas praised by the wise. What are the four? First, one's faculties are tamed and compliant. Second, one is permanently free from agitation. Third, one is praised by the world. Fourth, no one can violate one's spouse. These are the four. If one can dedicate them toward anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi, upon becoming a Buddha in the future, one attains the mark of the Tathāgata's concealed organ.

"Furthermore, Nāga King! If one abandons false speech, one immediately attains eight kinds of dharmas praised by the devas. What are the eight? First, one's mouth is always pure, with the fragrance of the utpala flower. Second, one is trusted and accepted by all the world. Third, one's words serve as testimony, revered and loved by humans and devas. Fourth, one always comforts sentient beings with kind speech. Fifth, one attains superior mental happiness, and one's three karmas are pure. Sixth, one's words are without error, and one's mind is always joyful. Seventh, one's words are held in esteem, and humans and devas carry them out. Eighth, one's wisdom is supreme and cannot be overcome. These are the eight. If one can dedicate them toward anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi, upon becoming a Buddha in the future, one attains the Tathāgata's truthful speech.

"Furthermore, Nāga King! If one abandons divisive speech, one immediately attains five kinds of indestructible dharmas. What are the five? First, one attains an indestructible body, because nothing can harm it. Second, one attains an indestructible retinue, because nothing can break it apart. Third, one attains indestructible faith, because it accords with one's fundamental karma. Fourth, one attains indestructible practice of the Dharma, because one's cultivation is firm. Fifth, one attains indestructible good teachers, because one is not deceived or deluded. These are the five. If one can dedicate them toward anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi, upon becoming a Buddha in the future, one attains a proper retinue that no māras or non-Buddhists can undermine.

"Furthermore, Nāga King! If one abandons harsh speech, one immediately accomplishes eight kinds of pure karma. What are the eight? First, one's words never exceed propriety. Second, one's words are all beneficial. Third, one's words invariably accord with principle. Fourth, one's speech is beautiful and refined. Fifth, one's words can be received and followed. Sixth, one's words are trusted. Seventh, one's words are beyond reproach. Eighth, one's words are entirely lovable and pleasing. These are the eight. If one can dedicate them toward anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi, upon becoming a Buddha in the future, one is endowed with the Tathāgata's voice of Brahmā.

"Furthermore, Nāga King! If one abandons idle talk, one immediately accomplishes three kinds of certainties. What are the three? First, one is certainly loved by the wise. Second, one can certainly use wisdom to answer questions truthfully. Third, one certainly possesses the highest authority among humans and devas, without any falsehood. These are the three. If one can dedicate them toward anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi, upon becoming a Buddha in the future, one attains the Tathāgata's predictions, none of which are in vain.

"Furthermore, Nāga King! If one abandons covetousness, one immediately accomplishes five kinds of sovereignty. What are the five? First, sovereignty over the three karmas, because one's faculties are complete. Second, sovereignty over wealth, because no enemy or thief can seize it. Third, sovereignty over merit, because whatever one's heart desires, all things are provided. Fourth, sovereignty over royal position, because rare and wondrous treasures are all offered to one. Fifth, what one obtains surpasses what one originally sought a hundredfold, because in former times one was free from miserliness and jealousy. These are the five. If one can dedicate them toward anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi, upon becoming a Buddha in the future, one is the most honored in the three realms, revered and served by all.

"Furthermore, Nāga King! If one abandons anger, one immediately attains eight kinds of joyful dharmas. What are the eight? First, a mind free from harm and vexation. Second, a mind free from anger. Third, a mind free from contention. Fourth, a gentle and straightforward mind. Fifth, the compassionate mind of the noble ones. Sixth, a mind that always acts for the benefit and ease of sentient beings. Seventh, a dignified and beautiful physical form, revered by all. Eighth, because of one's harmony and patience, one is swiftly born in the Brahmā world. These are the eight. If one can dedicate them toward anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi, upon becoming a Buddha in the future, one attains an unobstructed mind, and those who behold one are never wearied.

"Furthermore, Nāga King! If one abandons wrong views, one immediately accomplishes ten kinds of meritorious dharmas. What are the ten? First, one attains truly wholesome intentions and truly wholesome companions. Second, one deeply believes in cause and effect and would sooner lose one's life than do evil. Third, one takes refuge only in the Buddha, not in other devas. Fourth, one has a straightforward mind and right view, forever free from all nets of doubt about fortune and misfortune. Fifth, one is always born among humans and devas and does not fall into the evil destinies. Sixth, one's immeasurable merit and wisdom progressively increase. Seventh, one permanently leaves wrong paths and walks the noble path. Eighth, one does not give rise to the view of self, and one abandons all evil karma. Ninth, one abides in unobstructed views. Tenth, one does not fall into difficult circumstances. These are the ten. If one can dedicate them toward anuttarā samyaksaṃbodhi, upon becoming a Buddha in the future, one swiftly realizes all buddha-dharmas and accomplishes sovereign supernatural powers."

At that time the World-Honored One further said to the Nāga King: "If there is a bodhisattva who, relying upon these wholesome actions, practices generosity while abandoning killing, that person will always be rich in wealth that none can seize; will have a long life without untimely death; and will not be harmed by any enemy or thief. If one practices generosity while abandoning stealing, one will always be rich in wealth that none can seize; will be supreme and without compare; and will be able to gather all the Dharma-treasures of the Buddhas. If one practices generosity while abandoning unchaste conduct, one will always be rich in wealth that none can seize; one's household will be upright and harmonious; and no one will be able to look upon one's mother, wife, or children with desire. If one practices generosity while abandoning false speech, one will always be rich in wealth that none can seize; one will be free from all slander; one will uphold the true Dharma; and whatever one vows to do will surely be accomplished. If one practices generosity while abandoning divisive speech, one will always be rich in wealth that none can seize; one's retinue will be harmonious, of one purpose and one joy, and never at odds. If one practices generosity while abandoning harsh speech, one will always be rich in wealth that none can seize; all assemblies will joyfully take refuge in one; one's words will all be trusted and received, and none will refuse or reject them. If one practices generosity while abandoning idle talk, one will always be rich in wealth that none can seize; one's words will never be spoken in vain; people will all respectfully accept them; and one will be able to skillfully resolve all doubts. If one practices generosity while abandoning the covetous mind, one will always be rich in wealth that none can seize; all that one possesses will be relinquished through wisdom; one's faith and understanding will be firm; and one will possess great authority. If one practices generosity while abandoning the angry mind, one will always be rich in wealth that none can seize; one will swiftly accomplish unobstructed mental wisdom; one's faculties will be dignified and beautiful; and all who see one will respectfully love one. If one practices generosity while abandoning the inverted mind, one will always be rich in wealth that none can seize; one will always be born into families of right view and reverent faith; one will see the Buddha, hear the Dharma, and make offerings to the Saṃgha; and one will never forget the mind of great bodhi. These are the great benefits obtained by the great being who, while cultivating the bodhisattva path, practices the ten wholesome actions adorned with generosity.

"Nāga King! To speak of the essentials: by practicing the ten wholesome courses of action adorned with the precepts, one can produce all the benefits of the buddha-dharmas and fulfill the great vows. Adorned with patience, one attains the Buddha's perfect voice and is endowed with all the marks. Adorned with vigor, one can defeat all māras and enemies and enter the Dharma-treasury of the Buddhas. Adorned with concentration, one can give rise to mindfulness, wisdom, shame, conscience, and ease. Adorned with wisdom, one can sever all discriminative false views. Adorned with loving-kindness, one does not give rise to harm toward sentient beings. Adorned with compassion, one pities sentient beings and never wearily abandons them. Adorned with sympathetic joy, one sees those who cultivate goodness without jealousy. Adorned with equanimity, toward favorable or adverse conditions one has no mind of love or hatred. Adorned with the four methods of attraction, one constantly and diligently guides and transforms all sentient beings. Adorned with the foundations of mindfulness, one skillfully cultivates the contemplation of the four foundations of mindfulness. Adorned with right effort, one is able to sever all unwholesome dharmas and accomplish all wholesome dharmas. Adorned with the bases of supernatural power, one constantly keeps body and mind at ease and in happiness. Adorned with the five faculties, one's faith is deep and firm, one's vigor is untiring, one is never confused or forgetful, one is serene and tamed, and one severs all afflictions. Adorned with the powers, all enemies are annihilated and none can cause harm. Adorned with the factors of awakening, one always clearly awakens to all dharmas. Adorned with the noble path, one attains right wisdom that is always present. Adorned with calm, one can thoroughly cleanse all bonds and tendencies. Adorned with insight, one can know as it truly is the self-nature of all dharmas. Adorned with skillful means, one swiftly accomplishes the happiness of the conditioned and the unconditioned.

"Nāga King! You should know that these ten wholesome actions can even bring to perfection the ten powers, the fearlessnesses, the eighteen exclusive dharmas, and all the dharmas of a Buddha. Therefore you should diligently cultivate and train in them! Nāga King! Just as all cities, towns, and villages depend upon the great earth to abide securely, and all medicinal herbs, flowering plants, and forests also depend upon the earth to grow, so too are these ten wholesome courses of action: all humans and devas are established upon them; the awakening of all śrāvakas and pratyekabuddhas, all the practices of the bodhisattvas, and all the dharmas of the Buddhas all depend upon the great earth of these ten wholesome courses of action and are thereby accomplished."

When the Buddha had spoken this sūtra, the Sāgara Nāga King and the great assembly, and all the devas, humans, asuras, and others of the world, were all filled with great joy, faithfully received it, and practiced it.
