# 金剛頂經多羅菩薩念誦法
## Method of Recitation for Tārā Bodhisattva from the Vajraśekhara Sūtra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1102

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

> I bow in homage to the Sovereign King of Yoga,
> Who skillfully abides in the samādhi of illusion,
> And throughout all the pure and defiled ocean-realms
> Can manifest all manner of bodies appropriate to each.
> I rely on the gate of Lotus correspondence
> To reveal the dharma of Tārā's great compassion,
> So that those who cultivate samādhi
> May depart from the compassionless concentration of the two vehicles,
> Swiftly perfect the pāramitā of spiritual powers,
> And suddenly realize the stage of the tathāgata.
>
> The practitioner should generate the mind of Samantabhadra,
> And from the master fully receive the vajra precepts.
> Without regard for life, generate the heart of loving-kindness,
> And only then can one enter the wheel of liberation.
> One should receive the samaya from the master,
> With mudrā-seals and secret phrases as stated in the sūtra.
> Revere the ācārya as one would the Buddha,
> And toward fellow students maintain an earnest mind.
>
> Whether in the mountains, in an araṇya,
> By flowing springs, in bathing pools, in delightful places,
> On mountain peaks, in stone caves, or beside remote trees,
> Establish the bodhimaṇḍa according to the proper method.
> Adorn the meditation chamber and install the principal deity.
> Make offerings according to one's means and abide single-mindedly.
> Contemplate the ocean of buddhas in the ten directions.
> Confess and make vows, all as instructed.
>
> To accomplish the vajra of the three karmas,
> In both hands, on the tongue, and in the mind,
> Visualize the syllable HŪṂ becoming a five-wisdom vajra.
> This empowerment alone can bring siddhi.
>
> Next, one should form the mudrā called the Arousing.
> Both hands each form a vajra fist.
> The little fingers and thumbs hook together; the index fingers extend upright.
> The two index fingers touch at the tips: this is the Arising Mudrā.

The mantra says:

*Oṃ vajro-tiṣṭha*

> Next, one should reverently bow to Akṣobhya,
> Offering the body in prayer for non-retrogression.
> With vajra-palms joined, extend them above the crown.
> The whole body touches the ground in heartfelt homage.

The mantra says:

*Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-pūjā-upasthānāya ātmānaṃ niryātayāmi sarva-tathāgata-vajra-sattva-adhiṣṭhān sarva-vaṃ*

> Next, bow to Ratnasaṃbhava in the south,
> Offering the body in prayer for the abhiṣeka stage.
> With vajra-palms joined at the heart,
> Touch the forehead to the ground in sincere homage.

The mantra says:

*Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-pūjā-abhiṣekāya ātmānaṃ niryātayāmi sarva-tathāgata-vajra-ratna-abhiṣiñca vaṃ*

> Next, bow to the Sovereign Avalokiteśvara,
> Offering the body in prayer for samādhi.
> With vajra-palms joined, place them at the crown.
> Touch the mouth to the ground with a sincere heart in prayer.

The mantra says:

*Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-pūjā-pravartanāya ātmānaṃ niryātayāmi sarva-tathāgata-vajra-dharma-pravartāya vaṃ*

> Next, bow to Amoghasiddhi,
> Offering the body in prayer for skillful wisdom.
> With vajra-palms joined at the heart,
> Touch the crown to the ground, bowing in prayer.

The mantra says:

*Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-pūjā-karmāṇi ātmānaṃ niryātayāmi sarva-tathāgata-vajra-karma kuru vaṃ*

> Next, contemplate all buddhas pervading space.
> Form the holding-mudrā and universally bow at their feet.
> Cross the meditation and wisdom hands behind,
> Kneel on the right knee and place the mudrā at the crown.

The mantra says:

*Oṃ vajra-bhī*

> Next, with the accomplishment mantra,
> Universally wish that all beings share in the siddhi.
> By the dharma praised by all tathāgatas,
> May empowerment bring swift accomplishment.

The mantra says:

*Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-siddhi sarva-sattvānāṃ sarva-siddhayo saṃprayacchantu tathāgata-śāsana-adhiṣṭhāntu*

> Next, one should sit upright with joined mudrā,
> Purifying the three karmas to make them clean.
> Because the original nature of all dharmas is pure,
> May self and others alike be without stain.

The mantra says:

*Oṃ svabhāva-śuddhaḥ sarva-dharma svabhāva-śuddho 'haṃ*

> Next, form the Lotus-samaya:
> Interlace all ten fingers in a firm bind.
> The ring fingers stand upright joined like a lotus petal.
> Visualize one's body as identical to the Tārā deity.

The mantra says:

*Oṃ vajra-padma-samana-sattvaṃ*

> Next, form the Extreme Joy samaya mudrā.
> The meditation and wisdom hands in firm bind.
> The ring and middle fingers cross and join within.
> The little fingers, thumbs, and index fingers stand touching.

The mantra says:

*Oṃ samaya hoḥ su-rata-sattvaṃ*

> Next, open the heart and enter the buddha's wisdom.
> Empower both breasts with the syllables TRAṬ.
> Form the vajra-bind at the heart.
> Pull it open three times like opening a fan.

The mantra says:

*Oṃ vajra-bandha traṭ*

> Next, contemplate the syllable A on the lotus-platform,
> With two dots adorning it, becoming quiescent wisdom.
> The meditation and wisdom fingers bend into the vajra-bind.
> The invocation-syllable flows into the heart.

The mantra says:

*Oṃ vajra-āveśa aḥ*

> Next, form the sealed vajra fist.
> This empowerment makes it firm and immovable.
> The entering mudrā, with index fingers resting on meditation and wisdom:
> This firmly holds without retrogression.

The mantra says:

*Oṃ vajra-muṣṭi vaṃ*

> Next, form the Lotus Māra-Crushing Mudrā
> To purify and remove all obstacles.
> Using the vajra-palms joined posture,
> The index fingers stand like fangs, thumbs upright.
> Inwardly abide in loving-kindness while outwardly manifesting wrath.
> Rotate rightward three times to establish the boundary.

The mantra says:

*Oṃ mahā-caṇḍa-viśva-rūpa-vikaṭa-padma-naṣṭara-karara-bhiṣaṇa-vaṭa trāṭ trāṭ sāya svāhā padma-yakṣa-khadādhi*

> Next, sit upright and abide in samādhi.
> Cross both hands as the meditation mudrā.
> The ocean of buddhas in the dust-bodies of space:
> Arouse them to observe the true heart.

The mantra says:

*Oṃ citta-prativeda-dhaṅ karomi*

> Then contemplate the syllable A as a moon-disc.
> Further with the mantra make it clearly manifest.

The mantra says:

*Oṃ bodhi-citta-mutpādayāmi*

> Because the self-mind is originally pure in nature,
> One should skillfully contemplate the vajra-lotus.

The mantra says:

*Oṃ tiṣṭha padma*

> To accomplish the single characteristic of purity,
> Gradually open it to become equal to the dharma-realm.
> One attains the samādhi of great compassion,
> Able to universally purify the realm of sentient beings.

The mantra says:

*Oṃ sphara padma*

> To bring the samādhi to maturity,
> Expanding and contracting with sovereign mastery,
> Gradually gather the wisdom-lotus to the measure of one's own body.
> Universally radiate pure light, illuminating the samādhi.

The mantra says:

*Oṃ saṃhara padma*

> Next, with the firm and wondrous mantra,
> Empower it so that it cannot be shaken.

The mantra says:

*Oṃ nir-dhātiṣṭha padma*

> The tathāgatas appearing in space
> All enter the awakening-flower as one body.
> One should know oneself as equal to all buddhas,
> The firm bodhi-vow body.

The mantra says:

*Oṃ padma-ātma-kuhaṃ samayohaṃ mahā-samayohaṃ sarva-tathāgata-visaṃbodhi padma-ātma-kuhaṃ*

> Then contemplate the wondrous lotus as the principal deity.
> The body is pure and full, the color of green-gold.
> Adorned with maṇi jewel necklaces.
> The jeweled crown bears Amitāyus.
> The right hand displays the supreme wish-granting gesture.
> The left, in the meditation hand, holds a blue lotus.
> Abiding in samādhi upon a moon-disc,
> Universally radiating the light of compassion, illuminating the three realms.
>
> Next, with the root blue-lotus mudrā,
> Empower the heart, forehead, throat, and crown.

The mantra says:

*Oṃ tāre tuttāre hūṃ*

> Next, form the jewel-mudrā for self-abhiṣeka.
> Both hands in firm vajra-bind.
> The index fingers and thumbs form a jewel shape.
> Empower at the forehead, then tie behind the crown.

The mantra says:

*Oṃ vajra-ratna-abhiṣiñca vaṃ sarva-mudrā-me nirjikuru vara-kavaceda vaṃ*

> With both hands hanging down like garland-streamers,
> One should then don the firm armor.
> Form vajra fists with index fingers extended.
> Visualize the syllables OṂ CHAṂ at the fingertips.
> Heart, back, navel, waist, and both knees,
> Throat, forehead, and behind the crown, each circled three times.
> The little fingers and thumbs scatter forward, hanging like celestial garments.
> This makes one firm and immovable.
> With both hands, clap three times:
> This is called the Lotus Joy Mudrā Rite.

The mantra says:

*Oṃ padma-tuṣya hoḥ*

> Next, one should adorn and purify the buddha-land.
> In order to serve all the tathāgatas,
> Carefully contemplate the boundless ocean of fragrant water.
> Upon a wondrous lotus, hold the Lotus Treasury realm.
> Adorned with maṇi jewel palaces,
> Surpassing the celestial offering implements,
> The celestial beings of space form the fifth.
> All that is desired arises from space.
> The heart delights in offering to all the holy assemblies.
> May all wishes be fulfilled universally.
> Having made this true empowerment,
> One should form the vajra-palms joined posture.

The mantra says:

*Oṃ gagana-saṃbhava vajra hoḥ*

> Upon the jeweled ground, adorned with flower-seats,
> From the seed-syllable TĀṂ the principal deity arises,
> Radiating pure light surpassing the sun and moon.
> The lotus retinue surrounds on all sides.
> Next, with the secret skillful means of invocation,
> Summon the deity's body to enter the wisdom-body.
> The meditation and wisdom hands in firm bind.
> Both index fingers curve outward as hooks.

The mantra says:

*Oṃ padma-aṅkuśa-kārṣāya mahā-padma-kulān-hāya gṛhva samaye hūṃ jaḥ*

> Next, form the Lotus Lasso Great Mudrā.
> The lotus-palm, with wisdom entering the index and thumb.
> With this secret mudrā and mantra,
> Invoke the principal deity and draw them in.

The mantra says:

*Oṃ amogha-pāśa-kuru-dha samaye praveśa praveśāya sarva-samayān hūṃ*

> Next, form the Flower-Hand Locking Mudrā.
> The index fingers, thumbs, and middle fingers hook together.
> With this Lotus Restraining Mudrā,
> The principal deity is made to abide firmly.

The mantra says:

*Oṃ padma-śaṅkhalī vaṃ*

> Next, form the Lotus Bell Secret Mudrā.
> The thumbs bend into the lotus-palm.
> With this secret mudrā and mantra,
> The principal deity is made wondrously joyful.

The mantra says:

*Oṃ padma-ghaṇṭā-dhāri-śikara-māve-śāya samaya-samu-khāḥ*

> Next, with a delightful and wondrous gāthā,
> Hold and present the argha, offering fragrant water.
> The wondrous sound reaches the boundless realms.
> By this empowerment, accomplishment is swift.

The mantra says:

*Namo yeda-sate-na bhagavatī bodhi-mude-ranuttara-vajra-dharma-prayogina-tena-sate-na siddhaṃ oṃ tāri tuttāri ture svāhā*

> Next, one should broadly make the four inner offerings.
> The flower-palm with the thumbs standing upright.
> By this lotus sport,
> The pāramitā of giving is fulfilled.

The mantra says:

*Oṃ padma-rati-pūjaye hoḥ*

> Next, form the Lotus Garland Secret Mudrā.
> With the lotus-palm, extend the arms forward.
> By offering the flower-garland,
> The pāramitā of morality is fulfilled.

The mantra says:

*Oṃ padma-viśeka-pūjaye raṭa*

> Next, form the Lotus Song Mudrā.
> The flower-palm moves from the navel to the mouth and releases.
> By offering this wondrous dharma-voice of the tathāgata,
> The pāramitā of patience is fulfilled.

The mantra says:

*Oṃ padma-gīta-pūjaye gī*

> Next, form the Lotus Dance Offering.
> The flower-hand dances in rotation, placed at the crown.
> By this secret mudrā and mantra,
> The pāramitā of diligence is swiftly perfected.

The mantra says:

*Oṃ padma-nṛtya-pūjaye kṛṭa*

> Next, form the Lotus Incense-Burning Mudrā.
> The flower-palm scatters downward like burning incense.
> By the power of this brahmā-incense mudrā,
> The pāramitā of meditation is realized.

The mantra says:

*Oṃ dhūpa-padmāṃ-nī hūṃ*

> Next, form the Lotus Flower Offering.
> The lotus-palm scatters upward like offering flowers.
> By this offering of wondrous flowers and adornment,
> The pāramitā of wisdom is swiftly realized.

The mantra says:

*Oṃ padma-muṣṭi hūṃ*

> Next, form the Lotus Lamp Mudrā.
> The thumbs press forward against the lotus-hand.
> By this lamp-light offering,
> The pāramitā of skillful means is fulfilled.

The mantra says:

*Oṃ padma-kura-sundarī dharma-ruke-pūjaye pūjāya hūṃ*

> Next, form the Lotus Anointing-Perfume Mudrā.
> Scatter from the open palms at the heart as if anointing.
> By this mantra and secret mudrā rite,
> The pāramitā of vows is fulfilled.

The mantra says:

*Oṃ padma-gandhi hūṃ*

> Next, form the principal deity's root mudrā.
> Empower one's own heart with the mudrā.
> Both wisdom-fists face each other at the knuckles.
> The index fingers and thumbs stand upright joined.

The mantra says:

*Oṃ padma-tāre hūṃ*

> Next, form the Amogha Tārā Heart.
> Empower the forehead with the mudrā.
> Following the previous root secret mudrā,
> Change the little fingers and thumbs to stand upright touching.

The mantra says:

*Oṃ vipura-tāre hūṃ*

> Next, form the principal deity's jeweled crown mudrā.
> Place this great mudrā at the crown.
> Following the previous heart mudrā, the ring fingers stand upright.
> The index fingers curve far back-to-back.

The mantra says:

*Oṃ prabhāvara-tāre hūṃ*

> Next, form the True Empowerment Mudrā.
> This can summon all.
> Following the previous abhiṣeka crown mudrā,
> Only the index fingers move back and forth.

The mantra says:

*Oṃ amogha-tāre hūṃ*

> Next, form the Māra-Crushing Mudrā.
> This can subdue the hard to tame.
> Following the previous abhiṣeka crown mudrā,
> Extend the middle fingers straight, rotating rightward.

The mantra says:

*Oṃ samaya-tāre hūṃ*

> Next, distribute the seed-syllables upon one's body:
> OṂ at the crown, TĀ at the forehead,
> RE at both eyes, TU at both shoulders,
> TTĀ at the heart, RE at the navel,
> TU at both knees, RE at both shins,
> SVĀ at the left foot, HĀ at the right foot.
>
> The four vidyās draw the deity into one's own body.
> By this empowerment, the two become one.
> One should form the blue-lotus root mudrā
> And recite the Lotus Hundred-Syllable Vidyā.
> Cross the meditation and wisdom hands inward.
> The index fingers and thumbs stand upright touching.

The mantra says:

*Oṃ padma-sattva-samaya-manu-pālaya padma-sattva-tvenopa-tiṣṭha nir-ṇa-hurme-bhava su-tuṣyo-me-bhava anu-raktu-me-bhava su-puṣyo-me-bhava sarva-siddhiṃ-me pra-yaccha sarva-karma su-ca-me citta-śreyaḥ kuru hūṃ ha ha ha ha hoḥ bhagavan sarva-tathāgata padma mame muñca padme-bhava mahā-samaya-sattva hrīḥ*

> Having recited the Hundred-Syllable Mantra,
> Without releasing the mudrā, recite the root vidyā.

The mantra says:

*Namo ratna-trayāya namaḥ ārya-avalokiteśvarāya bodhisattvāya mahāsattvāya mahā-kāruṇikāya tadyathā oṃ tāre tuttāre ture svāhā*

> Then empower the bead-garland with the mantra.
> Raise them to the crown, then recite at the heart.

The mantra says:

*Oṃ vajra-pavitra-samaya hūṃ*

> When the fixed count of recitation is complete,
> Once more offer the argha and recite the wondrous praise.
> Having again made the eight offerings and vows,
> Release the boundary, and visualize the deity returning to the original palace.
> Form the previous Lotus Samaya,
> Release flowers above the crown, and bow at the feet.

The mantra says:

*Oṃ kṛtva sarva-sattvārtha-siddhi-datāya tha nu ga-gadbhī buddha-viṣaya-punar-gamanāya tu oṃ padma-sattva muḥ*

> Having sent off all the principal deities,
> Empower with the abhiṣeka and don the armor.
> Firmly abide in the samādhi of the principal deity.
> Freely remain, rest, or walk about.
> One should also read and recite the Mahāyāna,
> Always keeping the pure karma uninterrupted.
> One shall obtain Tārā's personal appearance,
> And all supreme wishes will be perfectly fulfilled.
> In this present life, one enters the Bhūmi of Joy.
> After sixteen lifetimes, one attains bodhi.
