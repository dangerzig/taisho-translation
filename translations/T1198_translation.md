# 聖者文殊師利發菩提心願文
## Aspiration Text of the Noble Mañjuśrī for Generating Bodhicitta

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1198

Translated from the Chinese. Yuan dynasty, translated by Prajñā (智慧)

---

I pay homage to all Buddhas and bodhisattvas!

> Standing as savior and protector before all [beings],
> I ultimately arouse the mind of bodhi;
> For the benefit of all sentient beings,
> May those wandering in saṃsāra reach the other shore.
> The mind of delusion, the mind of anger; these arise from one's own fundamental nature;
> Miserliness, greed, and arrogance; these too arise from one's own fundamental nature.
> From this very day until I attain bodhi,
> In the interval before realization, I will create [them] no more.
> All evil karma, greed, and anger I abandon entirely;
> In maintaining precepts, study, and practice I constantly rejoice;
> I rejoice in the liberating conduct of perfect enlightenment.
> The merit I constantly cultivate myself...
> I will not take [it] solely for the direct path to bodhi;
> As long as even one sentient being has not been liberated,
> I will dwell through dark kalpas, constantly working to transform and benefit [beings].
> Through immeasurable and inconceivable kalpas,
> May I constantly and forever journey through the pure lands of the Buddhas.
> May the sins committed by [name], the one bearing this name,
> Be universally heard throughout all the realms of the ten directions.
> The evil karma of my own body and speech...
> May it be forever purified in all places;
> The evil karma within my mind, too, may it be purified;
> Boundless evil karma I will not commit again.

---

**Translator's note:** The phrase 某甲執名 (line 18) is a liturgical formula in which the reciter substitutes their own name for 某甲 ("so-and-so"), rendering it a personal public confession. The phrase 本自性 (lines 5–6) is ambiguous; it may reflect a Tibetan philosophical notion that afflictions arise from one's own mind-stream (rang bzhin), or may simply acknowledge that such mental states are inherent features of conditioned existence; the vow that follows commits to not perpetuating them regardless.
