# 妙臂印幢陀羅尼經
## Dhāraṇī Sūtra of the Wondrous-Arm Seal Banner

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1364

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Śikṣānanda (實叉難陀).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Buddha was on Mount Gṛdhrakūṭa, in the wondrous palace where all the beasts dwell, together with a great assembly of twelve hundred and fifty bhikṣus and also with innumerable bodhisattva mahāsattvas, with Mañjuśrī Dharma-Prince at their head. All were in the form of youths, together with Bhadrapāla and the other sixteen men of stature.

At that time the World-Honored One, with the wisdom of right mindfulness, expounded the essentials of the Dharma for the devas and humans, causing them all to rejoice. Then the Great Brahmā King, together with the devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, asuras, garuḍas, kiṃnaras, mahoragas, humans and non-humans, each joined their palms and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! There are sentient beings who, because they have created various evil karma, are bound to fall into the hells, the realm of hungry ghosts, the animal realm, and the domain of Yama, where they will undergo all manner of suffering. We humbly pray that the World-Honored One, out of great compassion, will teach a skillful means to eliminate such suffering and enable those sentient beings to be liberated from the difficulties of the evil destinies." Then Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva Mahāsattva, together with innumerable great mantra-holding sages surrounding him, bowed at the Buddha's feet and said: "World-Honored One! We humbly pray that you will take compassionate pity and, through skillful means, rescue the world." The Buddha said: "Good man! There is a dhāraṇī called the Wondrous-Arm Seal Banner. If one can constantly recite, study, and contemplate it, one will swiftly eliminate the five sins of immediate retribution, and will be born into a family of wealth, ease, and great nobility." Then the World-Honored One spoke the mantra:

*Tadyathā: kara kara kiri kiri kuru kuru sara sara siri siri suru suru sarva-buddha avalokite dhara dhara dharma avalokite jara jara saṃgha adhiṣṭhite bhara bhara anika-buddha-koṭi-bhasthitā-nā sarva aparāṇa-nirmita sarva-duḥkha apanāya bhrīta bhrīta diśeta diśeta taṭa taṭa sasphuṭāya sasphuṭāya sarva aparāṇa apāya duggate niḥ svāhā.*

"Good man! This dhāraṇī has been spoken by buddhas as numerous as the sands of the Ganges. Ānanda! It is difficult to encounter a buddha in the world, and difficult to obtain a human body. To receive and uphold this mantra is yet more difficult still. Good man! I recall that in the past, from Vipaśyin Buddha onward, in succession up to Kāśyapa Tathāgata, each taught me this dhāraṇī, wishing universally to bring ease and joy to all sentient beings. From each of those buddhas I personally heard and received it." When the World-Honored One had spoken this mantra, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva Mahāsattva and the rest, together with the devas, nāgas, the eight classes of beings, and the entire great assembly, having heard what the Buddha taught, all greatly rejoiced, believed, received it with faith, and put it into practice.
