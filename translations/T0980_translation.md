# 大勝金剛佛頂念誦儀軌
## Recitation Ritual of the Great Victorious Vajra Buddha-Uṣṇīṣa

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0980

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Vajrabodhi (金剛智)

---

At that time, the World-Honored One, for the sake of all sentient beings of the final age, manifested five kinds of radiance. From the crown of his head he emitted a fire-light that illuminated the three realms. All the evil māras, ghosts, and spirits who did not believe in the Buddha's teaching were simultaneously consumed by the flames. Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva, seeing this sign, addressed the World-Honored One: "The Tathāgata's wisdom-fire has burned all the celestial māras, ghosts, and spirits who cause harm to sentient beings, and they are all terrified and without peace. What Dharma do you wish to teach? May the Tathāgata please expound it." At that time, Mahāvairocana Buddha told Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva: "There is a vidyārāja named Great Victorious Buddha-Uṣṇīṣa Heart Mantra. Reciting it even once fulfills all desires. Among all the Buddhas of the ten directions, this is the most supreme vidyārāja. If any vajra-practitioner recites it fifty thousand times in full, all the Buddhas, great bodhisattvas, devas, nāgas, celestial women, and their hosts will guard and protect them day and night, causing all their wishes to be fulfilled." At that time, the Tathāgata spoke the vidyā:

"*Oṃ mahā-vajra-uṣṇīṣa huṃ trūṃ hrīḥ aḥ huṃ*"

"The two hands interlace inwardly, with the patience and vow fingers bent like a peak. This is called the Root Great Seal. If, when the final Dharma is turning, there are sentient beings who constantly form this seal and recite the true word, no military force can harm them, water and fire cannot drown or burn them, and no evil māra or evil ghost can afflict them. The holy assembly of both the Vajradhātu and Garbhadhātu will come, whether the practitioner is sitting or lying down, to surround and protect them.

"Furthermore, I will teach the method of painting the image. On the dark half of the month, on the day of the Puṣya asterism, take a piece of clean white silk and paint the Great Victorious Vidyārāja. He wears a crown of the five Buddhas and manifests twelve arms, abiding in the wisdom-fist mudrā. He holds a five-pronged vajra, a blue lotus, a wish-fulfilling jewel, a karma-vajra, a hook, a lasso, a chain, a bell, a sharp sword, a wheel, and other implements. He rests upon a thousand-petaled white lotus. His body is the color of white light, and his entire body blazes with fire-light. At the four corners, paint four vidyārājas: at the southeast corner, paint Acala Vajra; at the southwest corner, Hayagrīva Vajra; at the northwest corner, Ṣaḍ-pada Vajra; at the northeast corner, Yakṣa Vajra. Paint these four wrathful kings, each holding weapons, displaying an extremely fierce and powerful aspect. Next, paint the eight directional guardian kings, each leading their retinue, surrounding with reverence. If any true-word practitioner wishes to make offerings, in a clean chamber or in a quiet mountain forest, burn incense and scatter seasonal flowers, and recite the true word five hundred thousand times. I and the eight buddha-uṣṇīṣas, immeasurable celestial bodhisattvas, vajra-devas, and others will manifest in person and constantly attend to the practitioner's wishes. If any sentient being recites and upholds this true word daily, in this present life they will surpass all devas and humans, and in a future life their body will be like that of Vairocana. One who upholds this seal and true word can accomplish one billion kinds of wishes. Furthermore, if any practitioner performs various māra-subjugation rites or any āveśa practices, I will create ten thousand vajra-youths who will constantly abide with the one who recites according to their wishes. With a compassionate mind, without hatred toward others, contemplate the syllable *hūṃ* at the heart. Whether walking, standing, sitting, or lying down, abide in the samādhi of the principal deity. All wishes will be accomplished, and all sentient beings who see this person will be delighted and filled with affection. This person is like Vairocana: immeasurable Buddhas will constantly come to shelter and protect them without abandoning them. The true-word practitioner should not give rise to greed, anger, delusion, or other afflictions, but should deeply take refuge in the Three Treasures. If one dedicates roots of merit to the six destinies, one will swiftly realize unsurpassed bodhi. When the practitioner is in impure places, use the Ucchuṣma Vajra mantra and seal to empower the five places. With the right hand, form a fist with four fingers and snap them with the thumb standing upright." The secret word:

"*Oṃ krodha-na huṃ jaḥ*"

Colophon: First collation completed. Twenty-ninth day of the second month of the second year of Kōgen [1257 CE]. Written at the Kakudō-in. Vajra disciple Shinzon.
