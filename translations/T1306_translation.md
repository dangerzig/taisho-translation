# 北斗七星護摩祕要儀軌
## Secret Essential Ritual of Homa to the Seven Stars of the Northern Dipper

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1306

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, narrated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

I now teach, for the sake of sentient beings of the latter age who have meager merit, this sequential ritual procedure for offerings and homa to the seven stars of the Northern Pole. In a quiet room, construct a water-altar, either square or round, each side measuring one cubit. Mix five types of fragrance (kuśa-grass incense, nardostachys, frankincense, camphor, and white sandalwood) and coat the altar with the paste. Within the altar, install a round furnace. Arrange food and fruit separately. The food for burning consists of cooked rice, fruit-cakes, ghee and honey, and the like. Use the five grains together with lactuca wood, selecting them according to the person's natal star.

First, recite the invocation: "With utmost sincerity I reverently invoke the seven luminous nāla of the Northern Pole: Greedy Wolf, Great Gate, Emolument Preserver, Literary Curve, Honest and Correct, Military Curve, and Army Breaker. The honored star serves as the presiding lord. May so-and-so be liberated from calamity and misfortune, may their lifespan be extended, and may they see a hundred autumns. I now construct this maṇḍala. I humbly pray that you will graciously descend to this place, accept this homa, and protect the patron so-and-so, that calamities be resolved, lifespan increased, and all wishes fulfilled from the heart."

Next, form the mudrā: a hollow-palmed añjali. Press both thumbs upon both ring fingernails. Both middle fingers and both little fingers open like lotus petals. Both index fingers slightly separate and curl, moving back and forth.

Next, recite the one-syllable Cakravartin mantra and the mantra for summoning the seven stars of the Northern Dipper.

The Cakravartin mantra:

*Namaḥ samanta-nāra-nāra-pañcara hūṃ.*

The mantra for summoning the Northern Dipper:

*Namaḥ samanta-nāra-nā ehi hi pha i ha i na i ka i ra i morā-tara-gara haṃ svāhā.*

With the hand, take wood, grains, ghee, honey, oil, and the like, and cast them into the furnace to be burned by fire. Focus one's gaze upon one's natal star. If it trembles, changes color, blazes with penetrating radiance, or appears yellow-white, the wish is being answered. Following the previous method, perform this for seven days and nights, and it will certainly respond as desired.

If the star hides and disappears from sight, note that the seven stars of the Northern Dipper are the essences of the sun, moon, and five planets. They encompass the seven luminaries and shine upon the eight directions. Above they illumine the celestial spirits; below they preside over the human world. They adjudicate good and evil and distribute fortune and misfortune. All the stars pay court to them; all the spirits look up to them. If a person is able to bow in reverence and make offerings, they will enjoy long life, merit, and nobility. Those who lack faith and reverence will not long retain their allotted destiny.

Therefore the books on destiny and fortune say: "In the world there is a Spirit of Life-Administration. On every *gengshen* day, this spirit ascends to the Heavenly Emperor to report the sins and evils of all people. For grave sins, an entire reckoning is removed from the lifespan; for minor sins, a lesser record is deleted." When the register of reckonings and records has been exhausted, the lord of destiny has spoken. It is for this reason that the Tathāgata, for the sake of sentient beings in the latter age who have meager merit, short lives, and die untimely, taught this ritual procedure for the one-syllable Cakravartin summoning of the seven stars of the Northern Dipper for offerings and homa. Those who make offerings will have their natal star's count of years restored; they will be struck from the death register and returned to the register of life.

If kings construct a maṇḍala within their own palaces and perform homa, bow, and make offerings according to the method, the Northern Dipper will rejoice and provide protection. They will long occupy their supreme position, constantly enjoying security and stability. The queens, consorts, ladies of the rear palace, princes, ministers, the three dukes, and the hundred officials will all be harmonious above and below, not practicing injustice. The people will flourish, the harvests will be abundant, the land will be at peace, free from calamity and catastrophe. No strange omens will appear, and epidemics and death will not arise. Within the borders, enemies and bandits will naturally disperse and retreat. Therefore this method is supremely secret and essential.
