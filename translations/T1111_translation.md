# 青頸觀自在菩薩心陀羅尼經
## Heart Dhāraṇī Sūtra of Blue-Necked Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1111

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, annotated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

At that time the World-Honored One, in the palace of Vaiśravaṇa, spoke of the past causes and conditions of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva:

"In the immeasurable, boundless, incalculable kalpas of the past, there was a buddha named Avalokana-vidarśana Tathāgata. When he had attained the way of buddhahood, he dwelt for twenty-seven days expounding the dharma. At the time of his parinirvāṇa, there was a heavenly prince named Viṣṇu-nārāyaṇa-bala. At that time the tathāgata spoke for him this Blue-Necked Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva Heart Mantra. When that heavenly prince heard it, he attained the samādhi of great compassion and made this vow: 'If any sentient being who is in fear or calamity hears my name, may they all be freed from suffering and liberated, and may they swiftly realize unsurpassed samyak-saṃbodhi. Better to call the name of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva once than to call the names of a hundred thousand times the Ganges-sand number of tathāgatas.'"

At that time the World-Honored One further told Vaiśravaṇa: "You should know that this heavenly prince, after my parinirvāṇa, will circulate this heart mantra and broadly perform the buddha's work, benefiting boundless sentient beings and establishing them upon the path of unsurpassed bodhi. The dhāraṇī says:

"Next, the method for painting the image of this Blue-Necked Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva. The image has three faces. The front face has a compassionate and joyful expression. The right side has a lion face. The left side has a boar face. On the head is a jeweled crown, and within the crown is a transformation Amitāyus Buddha. There are also four arms: the first right arm holds a staff, the second right arm holds a lotus. The first left arm holds a wheel, and the second left arm holds a conch. He wears a tiger skin as a skirt, and a black deer skin is draped diagonally from the left shoulder. A black serpent serves as his sacred thread. He stands upon an eight-petaled lotus, and his body is adorned with necklaces, armlets, rings, pendants, and a blazing radiance. The sacred thread drapes diagonally from the left shoulder.

"The Blue-Necked mudrā:

"Cross both hands outward. The left thumb is inserted into the palm. The right thumb extends upright. Both middle fingers form a lotus-petal shape. Both ring fingers extend upright. The left little finger presses over the right, both extended upright and crossed."
