# 佛說無量功德陀羅尼經
## Sūtra of the Dhāraṇī of Immeasurable Merit Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0934

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmabhadra (法賢)

---

At that time the World-Honored One addressed Ānanda, saying: "Listen carefully. I shall now expound for you and for sentient beings of the age of the Declining Dharma the Dhāraṇī of Immeasurable Merit. You must memorize it and not allow it to be forgotten, so that in the degenerate and evil age to come it may bring great benefit to all sentient beings.

"Ānanda! If any sentient being hears this dhāraṇī and recites it twenty-one times each morning, all the evil karma that person has accumulated over a thousand kalpas will be entirely extinguished, and in their present body they will obtain peace and happiness. If a person wholeheartedly recites it one lakṣa (100,000) times, in a future life that person will behold Bodhisattva Maitreya. If one recites it two lakṣas of times, in a future life one will behold Bodhisattva Avalokiteśvara. If one recites it three lakṣas of times, in a future life one will behold the Buddha Amitāyus."

He then proclaimed the dhāraṇī:

> namo ratnatrayāya | namas āryāmitābhāya tathāgatāya arhate samyaksaṃbuddhāya | tadyathā | amite amitoddbhave amitasaṃbhave amitavikrānte amitavikrāntagāmine gaganakīrti kare sarvaklīśakṣayaṃkari svāhā

At that time Ānanda, having heard the Tathāgata expound this Dhāraṇī of Immeasurable Merit, received it with joy and faith, bowed to the Buddha, and withdrew.
