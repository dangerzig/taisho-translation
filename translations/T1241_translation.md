# 伽馱金剛真言
## The Gāthā-Vajra Dhāraṇī

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1241

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown (失譯)

---

*Note: This text consists entirely of Chinese phonetic transcriptions of a Sanskrit dhāraṇī formula. The following represents a scholarly reconstruction of the underlying Sanskrit from those transcriptions.*

[Dhāraṇī]

namo ratna-trayāya | namaś caṇḍa-vajra-pāṇaye mahā-yakṣa-senāpataye | namo bhagavate ārya-vajra-gāthā-dhāriṇe aneka-rāśmi śata-sahasra-prajvālita-dīpatī-rājacase | ugra-vimabhaye nirjaye yogaye viṣama-bhāgiṇiye samukhe dhava-daśane trāya | vikelāna-kaśiye | aneka-vividha-rūpa-veṣa-dhāraṇiye | ihy ehi bhagavati mahā-vajra-gāthā-dhāriṇi | trāya-ratna satyena | tadyathā | oṃ | agaḍha agaḍha | varade | bandha | maheśa | valaṃgurda | viṣṇu | kumāra | brahma | indra ca | adhitiṣṭha rakṣa | saṃbhūte | viśargu-bandha | agniye | saṃvara | kuveraṃ | dhṛtarāṣṭraṃ | virūḍhaṃ | virūpākṣaṃ | saṃgo-kariṇaṃ | tri-kariṇaṃ | sthūra-kariṇaṃ | go-kariṇaṃ | mahā-kariṇaṃ | mahā-giriṃ nadi-keśava | vīragrīva-vinayaṃ | vinayaṃ | dharmaṃ | pañcaṃ | lohaṃ | ṣaṣṭhī-devakoṭy-agriṃ | durgatī-āyana-guṇaṃ | mahāgati-āyana-guṇaṃ | iṣṭa-guṇaṃ | nairṛtye | siddhi-guṇaṃ | giriṃ | mahā-giriṃ | vajra-giriṃ | cūra-giriṃ | agniṃ | vāyuṃ | vijayaśrī-gavatī | śatākṣaṃ | nyagrodha-bandha | raṇī-varahamaṇi | kumāra | maheśvara | vaiṣṇava | viśva | candīśvara | vākyaye | kiṃye | samaye tiṣṭha | didaṃ āvartaya eṣāmi śikharaṃ | gṛhāṇa gṛhāṇa | oṃ | guru guru | huru huru | curu curu | muru muru | dhama dhama | raga raga | pura pura | purāya purāya | āveśa āveśa | bhagavati mahā-vajra-gāthā-dhāriṇi | siddhyasva | mahā-vajra-pāṇi rājāpāyate | oṃ | kirihi | ha ha ha | hūṃ phaṭ svāhā ||

---

**Translator's remarks:** This dhāraṇī invokes Caṇḍa-Vajrapāṇi ("Fierce Holder of the Vajra") in his form as Mahāyakṣa-senāpati ("Great Commander of the Yakṣas"), one of the principal protector deities of the Mahāyāna tradition. The formula opens with homage to the Three Jewels and proceeds to invoke Vajrapāṇi as the Ārya-vajra-gāthā-dhāriṇī, "the Noble Holder of the Vajra Verse," blazing with the light of a hundred thousand flames. The central section calls upon a broad assembly of deities; Maheśa (Śiva), Viṣṇu, Kumāra (Skanda), Brahmā, and Indra; together with the four Lokapālas: Dhṛtarāṣṭra (East), Virūḍhaka (South), Virūpākṣa (West), and Kubera (North). The dhāraṇī concludes with the classic repetitive invocation sequence (*guru guru, huru huru*, etc.) calling the deity to enter (*āveśa*), seize (*gṛhāṇa*), and fulfill (*purāya*) the practitioner's aims, closing with the standard sealing syllables *hūṃ phaṭ svāhā*. Several syllable clusters in the middle section remain phonetically ambiguous and the reconstructions offered above are tentative.
