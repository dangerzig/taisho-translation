# 佛說大乘聖無量壽決定光明王如來陀羅尼經
## Sūtra of the Dhāraṇī of the Holy Mahāyāna Aparimitāyur-niścita-tejas-rāja Tathāgata, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0937

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dharmadeva (法天)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the World-Honored One was in the kingdom of Śrāvastī, in the Jeta Grove, the park of Anāthapiṇḍada, together with a great assembly of twelve hundred and fifty bhikṣus. All had extinguished their outflows, their minds were liberated, and they were free from afflictions. They had attained self-mastery, their minds were well-liberated, and they were known to all as great arhats. There were also bodhisattva-mahāsattvas of vast wisdom, great merit, and adorned deportment, who had all come to the assembly to hear the Dharma. Among the assembly, Mahāmati Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva was the foremost.

At that time, Śākyamuni Buddha, out of compassion for all short-lived sentient beings of the future world, in order to extend their life-spans and bring great benefit, taught a secret, profoundly subtle, and supremely wondrous Dharma that is beyond conception.

The World-Honored One addressed Mahāmati Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva: "Listen well. To the west of this southern Jambudvīpa, beyond immeasurable buddha-lands, there is a world called Immeasurable Merit Treasury. That land is splendid and beautiful, adorned with many jewels, pure and supreme, peaceful and blissful, surpassing the ten directions, subtle and wondrous, the foremost. Within that world of Immeasurable Merit Treasury, there is a Buddha named Aparimitāyur-niścita-tejas-rāja Tathāgata, of unsurpassed perfect awakening. He presently dwells in that world, generating great compassion, expounding the wondrous Dharma for all sentient beings, enabling them to obtain supreme benefit and happiness."

The Buddha further told Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva: "In this present Jambudvīpa world, the human life-span is a hundred years, yet many therein create evil karma and die prematurely. Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva! If any sentient being sees the merit of this Sūtra of the Dhāraṇī of Aparimitāyur-niścita-tejas-rāja Tathāgata and hears his name; if they write it themselves or cause another person to write the entire sūtra; if they place it in their own dwelling, upon a high tower, or in a vihāra or palace hall; if they receive, uphold, read, and recite it, venerate and bow to it, and make offerings to the Sūtra of the Dhāraṇī of Aparimitāyur-niścita-tejas-rāja Tathāgata with various fine flowers, burned incense, powdered incense, unguent, and flower-garlands: such a short-lived person, if with a devoted mind they write, receive, uphold, read, recite, make offerings to, and bow to it, will have their life-span increased to a full one hundred years.

"Furthermore, Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva! If any sentient being hears the name of Aparimitāyur-niścita-tejas-rāja Tathāgata and is able to recite it with a devoted mind one hundred and eight times, such a short-lived being will have their life-span extended. Or if they merely hear his name and with a devoted mind faithfully receive and venerate it, such a person will also have their life-span increased.

"Furthermore, Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva! If there are good men and good women who constantly, with minds never relaxing for a moment, sincerely seek the wondrous Dharma, listen well! I will now teach the One-Hundred-and-Eight-Name Dhāraṇī of Aparimitāyur-niścita-tejas-rāja Tathāgata."

The dhāraṇī:

*Namo bhagavate aparimitāyur-jñāna-suviniścita-teja-rājāya tathāgatāya arhate samyak-saṃbuddhāya tadyathā oṃ sarva-saṃskāra-pariśuddha-dharmate gagana-samudgate svabhāva-viśuddhe mahā-naya-parivāre svāhā*

"Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva! This One-Hundred-and-Eight-Name Dhāraṇī of Aparimitāyur-niścita-tejas-rāja Tathāgata: if anyone writes it personally or causes another to write it, and places it atop a high tower or in a clean place within a palace hall, adorns it properly with various offerings, a short-lived person will regain a long life, fulfilled to one hundred years. Such a person, when their life in that place comes to an end, will be reborn in the world of Immeasurable Merit Treasury, the buddha-land of Aparimitāyur-niścita-tejas-rāja Tathāgata."

When Śākyamuni Buddha taught this Sūtra of the Dhāraṇī of Aparimitāyur-niścita-tejas-rāja Tathāgata, ninety-nine koṭis of Buddhas, with one mind but different mouths, in unison also taught this same sūtra. Then eighty-four koṭis of Buddhas, with one mind but different mouths, in unison also taught this same sūtra. Then seventy-seven koṭis of Buddhas, with one mind but different mouths, in unison also taught this same sūtra. Then sixty-six koṭis of Buddhas, with one mind but different mouths, in unison also taught this same sūtra. Then fifty-five koṭis of Buddhas, with one mind but different mouths, in unison also taught this same sūtra. Then forty-four koṭis of Buddhas, with one mind but different mouths, in unison also taught this same sūtra. Then thirty-six koṭis of Buddhas, with one mind but different mouths, in unison also taught this same sūtra. Then twenty-five koṭis of Buddhas, with one mind but different mouths, in unison also taught this same sūtra. Then ten Ganges-sands of koṭis of Buddhas, each without any difference of mind and with different mouths in unison, all also taught this same sūtra.

"This dhāraṇī sūtra: if anyone writes it themselves or causes another to write it, such a person will not fall into the hells, will not become a hungry ghost, will not become an animal, and will not be subject to the karmic judges of Yama's realm. They will never receive evil retribution in any of these evil destinies. By the power of the merit of writing this Sūtra of the Dhāraṇī of Aparimitāyur-niścita-tejas-rāja Tathāgata, in all future places of birth, life after life, they will possess the knowledge of previous existences. This sūtra: if anyone writes it or causes another to write it, the merit obtained is no different from that of writing all eighty-four thousand divisions of the Dharma-treasury. This sūtra: if anyone writes it or causes another to write it, the merit obtained is no different from building eighty-four thousand jeweled stūpas. This sūtra: if anyone writes it or causes another to write it, and such a person bears the karma of the five heinous crimes leading to the uninterrupted hell, by the power of this merit, that karma will be entirely extinguished. This sūtra: if anyone writes it or causes another to write it, such a person will not fall among the hosts of Māra or Māra's retinue, will not fall into the realm of yakṣas or rākṣasas, and will not die an untimely or violent death. They will never receive such evil retributions. This sūtra: if anyone writes it or causes another to write it, at the moment of death, ninety-nine koṭis of Buddhas will appear before them, coming to receive such a person and conduct them to rebirth in that buddha-land. Have no doubts about this. This sūtra: if anyone writes it or causes another to write it, such a person will in the future never receive a female body. This sūtra: if anyone writes it or causes another to write it, such a person will constantly receive the hidden protection and escort of Dhṛtarāṣṭra, Lord of the Gandharvas, the King of the East; Virūḍhaka, Lord of the Kumbhāṇḍas, the King of the South; Virūpākṣa, Lord of the Great Nāgas, the King of the West; and Vaiśravaṇa, Lord of the Great Yakṣas, the King of the North.

"If anyone is able to give even a small amount of wealth for this sūtra, that person's merit is equivalent to filling the entire three-thousand great-thousand world with the seven treasures of gold, silver, lapis lazuli, giant clam, agate, coral, and amber, and giving them all in charity. If anyone makes offerings to this sūtra, it is equivalent to making offerings to all the true Dharma-treasury. If anyone were to make offerings of the most supreme seven treasures to Vipaśyin, Śikhin, Viśvabhū, Krakucchanda, Kanakamuni, Kāśyapa, and Śākyamuni, the Tathāgatas, Arhats, Samyak-saṃbuddhas, the merit obtained would be beyond measurement or reckoning. And if anyone makes offerings to this Sūtra of the Dhāraṇī of Aparimitāyur-niścita-tejas-rāja Tathāgata, the merit obtained is also beyond measurement or reckoning. Furthermore, as the waters of the four great oceans fill them completely, and one cannot know the number of each individual drop: if anyone writes, makes offerings to, receives, upholds, reads, and recites this sūtra, the merit obtained is likewise beyond measurement or reckoning. If anyone writes this sūtra, the place where it rests becomes the equivalent of a stūpa containing the true relics of all the Buddhas and should be venerated and worshipped. If any sentient being hears this dhāraṇī, that being will never receive the body of a bird, a four-footed animal, a many-footed creature, or any other non-human form. In the future, they will swiftly accomplish the non-retrogression of unsurpassed, perfect awakening. If anyone were to accumulate the seven treasures to the height of the wondrous Mount Sumeru and give them all in charity, the merit would be beyond measurement or reckoning. And if anyone makes charitable gifts for the sake of this sūtra, the merit obtained is also beyond measurement or reckoning. If anyone writes this sūtra and bows to it and makes offerings, such a person's merit is no different from bowing to and making offerings to all the Tathāgatas of all the buddha-lands of the ten directions."

At that time, the World-Honored One Śākyamuni Buddha spoke these verses:

> "Through the power of practicing generosity, one attains accomplishment.
> Through the power of generosity, one attains Buddhahood.
> If one enters the chamber of great compassion
> And but briefly hears this dhāraṇī,
> Even if generosity is not yet perfected,
> That person swiftly realizes the Teacher of Devas and Humans.
> Through the power of practicing discipline, one attains accomplishment.
> Through the power of discipline, one attains Buddhahood.
> If one enters the chamber of great compassion
> And but briefly hears this dhāraṇī,
> Even if discipline is not yet perfected,
> That person swiftly realizes the Teacher of Devas and Humans.
> Through the power of practicing patience, one attains accomplishment.
> Through the power of patience, one attains Buddhahood.
> If one enters the chamber of great compassion
> And but briefly hears this dhāraṇī,
> Even if patience is not yet perfected,
> That person swiftly realizes the Teacher of Devas and Humans.
> Through the power of practicing vigor, one attains accomplishment.
> Through the power of vigor, one attains Buddhahood.
> If one enters the chamber of great compassion
> And but briefly hears this dhāraṇī,
> Even if vigor is not yet perfected,
> That person swiftly realizes the Teacher of Devas and Humans.
> Through the power of practicing meditation, one attains accomplishment.
> Through the power of meditation, one attains Buddhahood.
> If one enters the chamber of great compassion
> And but briefly hears this dhāraṇī,
> Even if meditation is not yet perfected,
> That person swiftly realizes the Teacher of Devas and Humans.
> Through the power of practicing wisdom, one attains accomplishment.
> Through the power of wisdom, one attains Buddhahood.
> If one enters the chamber of great compassion
> And but briefly hears this dhāraṇī,
> Even if wisdom is not yet perfected,
> That person swiftly realizes the Teacher of Devas and Humans."

When the Buddha had finished teaching this sūtra, all the great bhikṣus, all the bodhisattvas, and all the devas, humans, asuras, gandharvas, and the rest of the world, having heard what the Buddha taught, were all greatly delighted, faithfully received it, and practiced accordingly.
