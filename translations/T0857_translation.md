# 大日經略攝念誦隨行法
## Abbreviated Compendium of the Recitation Practice According to the Mahāvairocana Sūtra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0857

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空)

---

> I bow to the Unobstructed Wisdom,
> The mind-born son of the esoteric teaching.
> Relying on that sūtra,
> I compile this practice manual.

> The bodhisattva of mantra practice
> First abides in the equal vow.
> Speech-secret and body-secret together,
> Afterward performs the yoga practice.

The samaya dhāraṇī:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ oṃ asame trisame samaye svāhā*

> The mudrā: bring together the finger-wheels and join,
> And raise the two thumbs together.
> At the five places: crown, shoulders, heart,
> Finally add the throat position.

> Next, with the Holy Immovable One,
> Repel obstacles and remove defilements,
> Able to purify all matters.
> Form the protective seal in accordance with the practice.

The Acalanātha dhāraṇī:

*Namaḥ samanta-vajrāṇāṃ caṇḍa-mahāroṣaṇa sphaṭaya hūṃ trāṭ hāṃ māṃ*

> Samādhi thumb on earth and water,
> Wind and fire erect at the heart.
> The wisdom-sword is likewise,
> Drawn from the sheath, able to accomplish all.

> Next is described the Tathāgata's hook,
> Used to invite the principal deity.
> All the holy lords
> Come by the power of their original vow.

The Tathāgata Hook dhāraṇī:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ aḥ sarvatra-apratihata tathāgatāṃ-kuśa-bodhicaryā-paripūraka svāhā*

> The śamatha and vipaśyanā hands interlock inward,
> Firmly join the wisdom index finger upright.
> Slightly bend at the first joint;
> The remaining wheels form a ring shape.
> By the compassionate vow-power of the holy devas,
> They all come and descend as invited.
> Present the samaya offering;
> Mantra and seal as previously explained.
> Having presented the original vow,
> Joy arises without error.

> Next, according to one's ability,
> Make offerings to express sincere devotion:
> Argha water, incense, food, and lamps,
> Down to a single flower and water,
> Or merely visualize mentally;
> The supreme merit is immeasurable.
> One should use the universal seal,
> Together with the secret mantra.
> Both manifest and non-manifest,
> All are accomplished at once.

The Universal dhāraṇī:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ sarvathā-udgate spharahimaṃ gaganakam svāhā*

> Dhyāna and wisdom mutually intertwined,
> The finger-wheels join together above the crown.
> Direct the mind universally throughout;
> All that is contemplated appears before one.

> Having thus made the offerings,
> Practice the regular recitation.
> First don the vajra armor;
> Form the protective seal in accordance with the practice.

The Vajra Armor dhāraṇī:

*Namaḥ samanta-vajrāṇāṃ vajra-kavaca hūṃ*

> First make a hollow-heart joining;
> The wind wheel twists around fire,
> Great space rests at the base of fire,
> Touching everywhere, then resting at the heart.

> Next form the directional boundaries,
> As with the previous Acala mantra.
> Turning left accomplishes the banishing,
> Turning right, and up and down,
> Touching all parts of the body:
> The protective seal is completely firm.
> The mantra and root seal
> Are as previously distinguished.

> Having completed these preparations,
> One should display the root seal,
> Again empowering the five places,
> Rotating seven times or two or three.
> Release the seal, opening above the crown.

> In half-lotus, straighten body and mind,
> Or assume the yoga posture,
> According to the direction as the teaching instructs.

> Directly before one, in front of the body,
> Behold a perfectly bright image,
> Pure and without flaw,
> Like a full moon disc.
> Within it is the form of the principal deity,
> Wondrous in color, transcending the three realms,
> Adorned with fine silk garments,
> Jeweled crown, dark hair flowing down,
> In serene samādhi,
> Radiant flames surpassing all lightning.
> As within a pure mirror,
> Deep and profound, the true countenance appears.
> Joy or wrath displayed in countenance and color,
> Holding implements that correspond to the vows.
> In right reception, the body of correspondence,
> Clear and lucid, the mind without disturbance.
> The markless, pure Dharma-body,
> In accordance with vows, brings relief to all beings.

> With focused attention, recite and uphold;
> When the set number is complete,
> Rest only when fully exhausted.

> Again form the universal seal.
> With devotion, make aspirations;
> With earnest reverence, bow to the holy honored one.
> Turn left with the power of the Immovable One,
> Releasing the previously formed protections.
> Again present the principal deity's seal,
> Releasing it above the crown.

> One session, two, or three,
> Or four, all performed likewise.
> In remaining time, circumambulate stūpas,
> Bathe images, praise the vaipulya,
> Adorn and decorate the maṇḍala,
> Scatter flowers and praise the Buddha's virtues,
> Or, free of distracted thoughts,
> Focus in meditative equipoise.
> Through this, purifying the three karmas,
> Siddhis swiftly manifest,
> Empowered by the holy power,
> Because practice and vows are in accord.

> All who delight in practice
> Should follow the teacher and receive instruction.
> The vidyādhara transmits the original teaching
> Without transgressing the samaya.
> Diligently exhorting uninterrupted practice,
> Free from the hindrances and intoxication,
> Practicing in accord with the training rules,
> Siddhis are accomplished according to one's capacity.

> I rely on the Mahāvairocana teaching
> To briefly demonstrate the yoga practice.
> May the supreme merit of practice and realization
> Universally moisten all sentient beings.
