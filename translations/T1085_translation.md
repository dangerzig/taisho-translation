# 觀自在菩薩如意輪念誦儀軌
## Ritual Manual for the Recitation of the Wish-Fulfilling Wheel of Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1085

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

According to the *Abhiṣeka-Bodhimaṇḍa Sūtra*, those who practice the dhāraṇī dharma-gate and seek to swiftly leave the great ocean of birth and death should first enter the abhiṣeka-bodhimaṇḍa, the oceanic assembly of all buddha tathāgatas. Having received the abhiṣeka, one generates a joyful mind and personally receives from the master the rules of recitation practice. Afterward, in a clean room, a mountain forest, or by flowing water, which are the most superior settings, one establishes the bodhimaṇḍa and installs the principal deity. The mantra practitioner faces east. One should smear the ground with gomaya and grind white sandalwood into fragrant paste to apply to the altar, either square or round, of whatever size one wishes. Upon the altar, scatter all manner of fine flowers, and make offerings of burnt incense and lamps. Take two clean vessels filled with fragrant water and place them upon the altar as offerings. Whether the practitioner bathes or does not bathe, there is no obstruction. One should simply direct the mind to contemplate and observe: the original nature of all sentient beings is pure; it is obscured by adventitious defilements, so that the pure dharma-nature of true suchness is not seen. In order to purify them, one should wholeheartedly form the lotus-palms joined and recite this secret phrase. The mantra says:

*Oṃ svabhāva-śuddho 'haṃ*

By the empowering force of this mantra, the karmas of body, speech, and mind are all purified. Then, touching the five limbs to the ground, one bows in homage to all the buddhas of the ten directions, all the great bodhisattvas, and the vast Mahāyāna. Kneeling on the right knee, one confesses, rejoices, exhorts, and makes vows. Wholeheartedly, one recites this verse each time:

> "I bow in homage to the truly awakened of the ten directions,
> To the most supreme and wondrous dharma, and the assembly of bodhisattvas.
> With the pure karma of body, speech, and mind,
> Earnestly, with palms joined, I reverently bow.
> Throughout beginningless saṃsāra in all the realms of existence,
> The offenses born of karma of body, speech, and mind:
> As the buddhas and bodhisattvas have confessed,
> So now do I confess likewise.
> In the practices and vows of the buddhas and bodhisattvas,
> The merit born of the vajra three karmas,
> That of pratyekabuddhas, śrāvakas, and sentient beings:
> In all the roots of merit they have gathered, I fully rejoice.
> All the lamps of the world who sit at the bodhimaṇḍa,
> Whose awakened eye opens and shines upon the three realms of existence:
> I now kneel and first exhort them
> To turn the unsurpassed and wondrous dharma-wheel.
> All the tathāgatas, lords of the three realms,
> Who are about to enter nirvāṇa without remainder:
> I exhort them all to abide long,
> Not forsaking their compassionate vow to save the world.
> May the merit of confession, rejoicing, and exhortation
> Cause me never to lose the mind of bodhi.
> Among the wondrous assemblies of buddhas and bodhisattvas,
> May I always be a good friend, never rejected.
> Free from the eight difficulties, born where there is no difficulty,
> With the wisdom of past lives, adorned in bodily marks,
> Far from delusion, endowed with compassion and wisdom,
> May I fully perfect the pāramitās.
> Wealthy, prosperous, and born in a noble clan,
> With an extensive retinue, ever flourishing,
> The four unobstructed eloquences, the ten sovereign masteries,
> The six penetrations, and all dhyānas perfectly complete.
> Like Vajradhvaja and Samantabhadra,
> May my vows of praise and dedication be likewise."

Next, before the principal deity, one sits in full or half lotus posture and generates the heart of great loving-kindness: "I practice this dharma for all sentient beings. May they swiftly realize unsurpassed samyak-saṃbodhi." First, one grinds various incenses and applies them to the hands. Then one forms the Buddha-division samaya dhāraṇī mudrā. With both hands in a hollow-palm joined position, open both index fingers and bend them against the upper joints of the middle fingers. Bend both thumbs against the lower joints of the index fingers; the mudrā is formed. Place the mudrā at the heart. Visualize the tathāgata's thirty-two marks and eighty minor excellences, clear and distinct as if directly before the eyes. Wholeheartedly recite this mantra seven times. The mantra says:

*Oṃ tathāgato 'dbhavāya svāhā*

By forming this mudrā and reciting the mantra, all the tathāgatas are aroused and will all protect, remember, and empower the practitioner. Their radiance shines and touches one; all karmic obstructions are extinguished, lifespan is extended, and merit and wisdom increase. The holy assembly of the Buddha division protects and rejoices. In every life, one is freed from the evil destinies, born from a lotus, and swiftly realizes unsurpassed samyak-saṃbodhi.

Next, one forms the Lotus-division samaya mudrā. With both hands in a hollow-palm joined position, spread both index fingers, middle fingers, and ring fingers, bending them slightly into an open-lotus shape. Place the mudrā at the heart, visualize Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva with complete marks holding a wondrous lotus, and recite the mantra seven times. Release the mudrā at the crown to the right. The mantra says:

*Oṃ padmo 'dbhavāya svāhā*

By forming this mudrā and reciting the mantra, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva and all lotus-holding bodhisattvas of the Lotus division are aroused. The entire holy assembly rejoices, empowers, and keeps one in mind. The radiance of all bodhisattvas shines upon one; all karmic obstructions are extinguished. All bodhisattvas always serve as good friends.

Next, one forms the Vajra-division samaya mudrā. Turn the left hand palm outward. Place the back of the right palm upon the back of the left hand. Hook the thumbs and little fingers of both hands together in a vajra shape. Place the mudrā at the heart, visualize Vajragarbha Bodhisattva holding a wondrous vajra with majestic radiance and complete marks, and recite the mantra seven times. Release at the crown to the left. The mantra says:

*Oṃ vajro 'dbhavāya svāhā*

By forming this mudrā and reciting the mantra, all the vajra holy assembly is aroused to empower and protect. All karmic obstructions are extinguished, all pain and suffering never attaches to the body, one attains the indestructible vajra body, and no māra can intrude.

Next, one forms the body-protection vajra-armor mudrā. Cross the fingers inward, right over left. Extend both middle fingers upright with tips touching. Bend both index fingers in a hook shape behind the middle fingers without touching them. Place both thumbs upon the ring fingers; the mudrā is formed. Empower the five places of the body with the mudrā: forehead, then right shoulder, then left shoulder, then heart, then throat. Release the mudrā above the crown. Recite the mantra once at each point. The mantra says:

*Oṃ vajrāgni-praṇidhaptāya svāhā*

By forming this mudrā and reciting the mantra, one is as if clad in vajra armor. All vināyakas and celestial māras who create obstacles retreat and flee. They see the practitioner's body radiant with majestic virtue and sovereign power. Whether in mountain forests or amidst dangers, one is entirely without fear. Disasters of water, fire, and all calamities; tigers, wolves, lions, blades, cudgels, fetters, and shackles: all such things are extinguished, and all who see one are delighted. After death, one does not fall into the evil destinies but is born in the pure and wondrous buddha-lands.

Next, one forms the earth-boundary vajra-stake mudrā. Insert the right ring finger into the left ring and little fingers; insert the right middle finger into the left middle and index fingers. The left hand does likewise. All fingertips emerge outward. The remaining fingers are joined tip to tip. Visualize the mudrā becoming a blazing vajra shape. Press the thumbs against the ground and pull; with each pull recite once, up to three times, then stop. Fix the mind on whatever size one wishes, and the firm earth-boundary is established. The mantra says:

*Oṃ kiri kiri vajra-vajri bhūḥ bandha bandha hūṃ phaṭ*

By forming this mudrā and reciting the mantra to empower the earth-boundary, below to the water-element it becomes like a vajra-throne. Celestial māras and all obstacles cannot cause harm, and with little additional effort accomplishment is swiftly attained.

Next, one forms the directional vajra-wall mudrā. Following the previous earth-boundary mudrā, open both thumbs upright, tilted like a wall. Visualize the mudrā in vajra shape and circle it rightward around the body three times. Fix the mind on whatever size one wishes, and the firm vajra city is established. Even the buddhas and bodhisattvas do not transgress it; how much less all other hard-to-subdue beings, vināyakas, poisonous creatures with sharp fangs and claws, who cannot even approach. The mantra says:

*Oṃ sara sara vajra-prakāra hūṃ phaṭ*

The practitioner should next visualize within the altar a great lotus of eight petals. Upon the lotus is a wondrous lion-throne. Above the throne is a seven-jeweled pavilion, hung with all manner of necklaces, silk streamers, banners, and canopies, with jeweled pillars in rows draped with celestial garments. Fragrant clouds pervade, raining down various flowers. All manner of music is played. Jeweled vases, argha, and celestial food and drink are set out, with maṇi gems as lamps. Having made this visualization, one recites this verse:

> "By the power of my merit,
> By the empowering power of the tathāgata,
> And by the power of the dharma-realm,
> May universal offerings be made and abide."

Having spoken this verse, next one forms the great Ākāśagarbha universal offering mudrā. Join both palms. Cross both middle fingers, right over left, outward, pressing against the back of the hand. Touch both index fingers together, curved back in a jewel shape. Having formed the mudrā, recite the mantra four times. The universal offering mantra says:

*Oṃ gagana-saṃbhava vajra hoḥ*

By forming this mudrā and reciting the mantra, the visualized offerings are truly real, and all the holy assembly receives and enjoys them.

Next, one should form the jeweled chariot mudrā. Cross both hands inward, palms upward. Join both index fingers horizontally touching. With both thumbs, each press against the base of the index finger. Visualize the mudrā becoming a seven-jeweled chariot, driven by a vajra-being. The jeweled chariot rides through the sky to the world of Sukhāvatī to invite all the holy assembly. Recite the mantra three times. The mantra says:

*Oṃ turu turu hūṃ*

By forming this mudrā and reciting the mantra, the seven-jeweled chariot arrives at the Land of Sukhāvatī. Then visualize the Wish-Fulfilling Wheel Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva and all the holy assembly and retinue, riding the jeweled chariot, arriving at the bodhimaṇḍa and hovering in the sky.

Next, one forms the chariot-inviting mudrā. Then recite the chariot-inviting mantra. With both thumbs, beckon toward oneself by flicking the tips of the middle fingers. Recite three times. The mantra says:

*Namaḥ strīya-dhvikānāṃ tathāgatānāṃ oṃ vajrī-nikarṣāya svāhā*

By the empowerment of this mantra and mudrā, the holy assembly comes from its home land and arrives at this bodhimaṇḍa, hovering in the sky.

Next, one forms the mudrā for invoking the principal deity's samaya to descend to the bodhimaṇḍa. Cross both hands inward to make fists. The left thumb bends into the palm. With the right thumb, beckon toward oneself. Recite the mantra seven times. The mantra says:

*Oṃ ārolik āgaccha āgaccha svāhā*

By the empowerment of this mantra and mudrā, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, not transgressing the original vow, comes to gather at the bodhimaṇḍa and accepts the offerings.

Next, one should dispel all obstacle-makers by forming the Lotus-division Vidyā-King Hayagrīva Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva mantra and mudrā. Join both palms. Bend both index fingers and ring fingers into the palms with nail-backs facing. Extend both thumbs upright, slightly bent away from the index fingers. Both little fingers and middle fingers each join upright; the mudrā is formed. While reciting the mantra, rotate the mudrā above the crown leftward three times, visualizing the dispelling of all obstacle-makers. All māras, seeing this mudrā, retreat and flee. Then rotate rightward three times; this establishes the boundary. The Vidyā-King mantra says:

*Oṃ amṛtodbhava hūṃ phaṭ svāhā*

Next, one forms the upper vajra-net mudrā. Following the previous wall mudrā, press both thumbs upon the lower joints of the index fingers. Recite the mantra three times and rotate the mudrā above the crown rightward three times. The mantra says:

*Oṃ visphura-drakṣa vajra-pañjara hūṃ phaṭ*

By forming this mudrā and reciting the mantra, an indestructible vajra net is established.

Next, one forms the vajra fire-court sealed-seam mudrā. Place the left hand over the back of the right hand. Extend both thumbs upright. Recite the mantra three times and circle rightward around the body three times. Visualize three layers of fire-flames surrounding the vajra wall. The mantra says:

*Oṃ asamāṅgini hūṃ phaṭ*

Next, the argha fragrant-water offering mantra and mudrā. With both hands hold the vessel at the level of the forehead and present it, visualizing washing the feet of the holy assembly. Recite the mantra three times. The mantra says:

*Namaḥ samanta-buddhānāṃ gagana-sama-samaḥ svāhā*

By offering the argha fragrant-water, the practitioner's three karmas are purified and the defilements of affliction are washed away.

Next, one should form the lotus-seat offering mudrā. With both hands in a hollow-palm joined position, spread and open both ring fingers, middle fingers, and index fingers, bending them into a slightly opening lotus shape. Within the jeweled pavilion, all the holy ones and the principal deity each sit in their original positions, surrounded by their retinues, clearly and distinctly visualized. Recite the mantra three times. The mantra says:

*Oṃ kamala svāhā*

By the empowerment of forming the lotus-seat mudrā, the practitioner shall attain the fulfillment of the ten bhūmis and the vajra-throne.

Next, one forms the universal offering mudrā. Join both palms. Interlace the ten fingers, right over left, each crossing at the upper joints; the mudrā is formed. Recite the mantra three times. Visualize from the mudrā streaming forth immeasurable, boundless oceans of clouds of anointing perfume, flower garlands, burnt incense, food, drink, and lamps, all becoming pure and vast offerings. Recite the universal offering mantra three times. The mantra says:

*Namaḥ sarva-tathāgatebhyo viśva-mukhebhyaḥ sarvathā-kham udgāti spharaṇa imaṃ gagana-khaṃ svāhā*

Next, one should recite the verse of praise:

*Kamala-mukha kamala-locana kamala-saṃna kamala-hasta kamala-smṛni kamala kamala-saṃsvāsa-kara-mālā kṣa-rana namo 'stu te*

Next, one should contemplate and visualize: within one's own body, directly at the center of the chest, a full-moon shape, brilliantly shining. Upon the moon is an eight-petaled lotus. In the center of the lotus is a wish-fulfilling jewel, the color of red crystal, blazing with radiance illuminating immeasurable worlds. Within the radiance there emerges the principal deity, the Wish-Fulfilling Wheel Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, complete with six arms, with marks and excellences perfectly complete, abiding in the contemplative posture. Having made this visualization, one generates the heart of great compassion and forms the Wish-Fulfilling Wheel root mudrā. Join both palms. Bend both index fingers into a jewel shape. Bend both middle fingers touching in a lotus-petal shape. Join both thumbs upright; the mudrā is formed. Visualize the Wish-Fulfilling Wheel Bodhisattva as if directly before the eyes, clear and distinct. Recite the root dhāraṇī seven times and release the mudrā above the crown. The mantra says:

*Namo ratna-trayāya namaḥ ārya-avalokiteśvarāya bodhisattvāya mahāsattvāya mahā-kāruṇikāya tadyathā oṃ cakra-vartī cintā-maṇi mahā-padme ruru tiṣṭha jvala ākarṣāya hūṃ phaṭ svāhā*

Next, one forms the Wish-Fulfilling Wheel heart mudrā. Following the previous root mudrā, cross both ring fingers and little fingers, right over left, outward; the mudrā is formed. Recite the heart mantra seven times and release above the crown. The mantra says:

*Oṃ padma cintā-maṇi jvala hūṃ*

Next, one forms the heart-within-the-heart mudrā. Following the previous heart mudrā, cross the middle fingers outward, and extend the little fingers horizontally; the mudrā is formed. Recite the heart-within-the-heart mantra seven times and release above the crown. The mantra says:

*Oṃ varada padme hūṃ*

Then take up the prayer beads, coil them in the palm, and empower them with the heart-within-the-heart mantra seven times. Afterward, hold the beads at the heart and count the recitations in order, completing one hundred and eight or one thousand and eighty times. The mind abides in the samādhi of the principal deity without straying to other objects, clear and distinct. When the count is complete, place the beads in the palm, hold them above the crown, and return them to their place. Then form the three mudrās of the root, heart, and heart-within-the-heart, and enter the samādhi contemplation. Contemplate the mind as a round brightness gradually expanding to pervade the dharma-realm; one no longer sees body or mind, becoming the pure dharma-realm. After about the time of a meal, emerge from samādhi. Next, form the universal offering mudrā, offer flowers, incense, and argha water, recite praise and make vows. Then form the fire-court boundary mudrā and rotate it above the crown leftward once to release the boundary. Next, form the chariot mudrā and push outward. Next, form the invocation mudrā and push outward. Next, form the body-protection mudrā and seal the five places. Next, form the three-division samaya mudrā. Then one leaves the bodhimaṇḍa and freely walks about, reads and recites Mahāyāna scriptures, and dedicates the merit toward the upper, middle, and lower accomplishments sought in the heart.

If the practitioner can perform this recitation three times daily according to this method, karmic obstructions are extinguished, great wisdom is obtained, samādhi is accomplished, the principal deity appears before one, and the merits obtained are as described in the sūtras.
