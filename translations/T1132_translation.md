# T1132 金剛王菩薩祕密念誦儀軌

# Secret Recitation Ritual Manual of Vajrarāja Bodhisattva

*Note: This is an esoteric ritual manual (yìguǐ 儀軌) translated by Amoghavajra (Bùkōng 不空) during the Tang dynasty. It presents the complete sādhana for Vajrarāja Bodhisattva (Jīngāng wáng púsà 金剛王菩薩), who emanates from Vairocana Tathāgata's self-nature dharma-body and the great mirror wisdom (dàyuánjìngzhì 大圓鏡智) of the Vajradhātu. The text includes: selecting a qualified ācārya, prostrations to the four directional buddhas, four immeasurables with mantras, the five-stage bodhicitta visualization (moon disc, vajra, expansion, contraction, self-identification), transformation into the four-armed principal deity, a seventeen-deity maṇḍala, the hook-lasso-chain-bell mudrā sequence, the hundred-syllable mantra, and the complete offering and dismissal procedures.*

---

Out of compassion for all those who seek equal awakening, and because some do not know this secret yoga method for swiftly becoming a buddha and instead endure arduous practices throughout three great incalculable kalpas without reaching supreme bodhi, I have pitied them and therefore, from the hundred thousand verses of the *Vajraśekhara* (Jīngāngdǐng 金剛頂), I have briefly explained the recitation ritual manual of Vajrarāja Bodhisattva (Jīngāng wáng púsà 金剛王菩薩), who is another name for the other-enjoyment body flowing forth from the self-nature dharma-body Vajradhātu great mirror wisdom of Vairocana Tathāgata. Through the three secrets of practice, the great mudrā, and the rest, one can enable mantra-practicing bodhisattvas to swiftly realize the Tathāgata's stage of equal awakening, obtain sarvajñā wisdom, abide in the great Samantabhadra ground, and throughout the boundless realm of birth and death, tame all sentient beings and establish them in supreme bodhi without fatigue.

## Selecting an Ācārya

First, one should carefully select an ācārya who has thoroughly mastered the Vajraśekhara Yoga and seek to receive the abhiṣeka of the five divisions or the vidyādhara abhiṣeka. If one fails to select carefully, one falls into ruin. Having encountered a true ācārya, one should regard the ācārya as a manifestation of the Tathāgata and offer all one's finest worldly possessions. Why? Because this supreme vehicle Dharma is honored by all buddhas of the three times. One should inquire into every detail of this Dharma and gain thorough understanding of the maṇḍala method, the painted-image method, the self-abhiṣeka method, the five kinds of secret four mudrās for pacification and other rites, the great mudrā as one mudrā, the five wisdoms constituting the body, the three-secret empowerment, and the secret offerings.

If a mantra-practicing bodhisattva abides in the great bodhi-mind and dedicates all merit toward the fruit of equal awakening, then through great compassion and the benefit of beings, one swiftly attains buddhahood. If one departs from this, not only will one fail to attain siddhi, but one slanders all buddhas and will certainly fall into the three evil destinies. If all one's actions are directed toward bodhi and the benefit of sentient beings, then all wishes of the mind will be accomplished without exception.

## Entering the Practice Hall

Having received the Dharma, the mantra practitioner should establish a ritual arena, install the principal image, don new clean garments, and perform recitation at the four periods according to the yoga method. At the very least, two periods must not be omitted. One should always abide in harmony with the blissful samādhi.

Upon first entering the ritual arena, one bows to the buddha and kneels, with both hands forming an open lotus. This is the pure realm-boundary mantra-mudrā. Mantra: "Oṃ rajopagata-sarva-dharma."

Without changing the previous mudrā, one recites the purification of the three karmas mantra, empowering the four places. Mantra: "Oṃ svabhāva-śuddha-sarva-dharma-svabhāva-śuddho'haṃ."

## Vajra Arising and Prostrations to the Four Buddhas

Next, one forms the vajra-arising mudrā: with both hands in vajra fists, the little fingers hook each other, and the index fingers touch head to head at the sides. Before forming this mudrā, one visualizes a five-wisdom vajra on the tongue and on both palms. Raising the mudrā three times, one recites this mantra to rouse all tathāgatas throughout space: "Oṃ vajrotiṣṭha."

With each raising, one recites once. Then one visualizes buddhas as numerous as sands of the Ganges filling all of space.

Then, extending both arms above the crown in vajra añjali, stretching both legs, one prostrates fully to the ground before Akṣobhya Tathāgata (Bùdòng rúlái 不動如來) in the east, offering one's body. Mantra: "Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-pūja-upasthānāya ātmānaṃ niryātayāmi, sarva-tathāgata-vajra-sattva adhiṣṭha svāhā hūṃ."

One contemplates: "In order to serve and make offerings to all tathāgatas, I now offer my body. May all tathāgatas, out of compassion, accept me."

Drawing the legs together, with vajra añjali placed on the heart and forehead to the ground, one bows to Ratnasaṃbhava Tathāgata (Bǎoshēng rúlái 寶生如來) in the south, offering one's body. Mantra: "Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-pūja-abhiṣekāya ātmānaṃ niryātayāmi, sarva-tathāgata-vajra-ratna-abhiṣiñca svāhā trāḥ."

One contemplates: "In order to make offerings to all tathāgatas and request abhiṣeka, I now offer my body. May all tathāgatas bestow the vajra jewel abhiṣeka upon me."

With añjali placed on the crown and mouth to the ground, one bows to Amitāyus Tathāgata (Wúliàngshòu rúlái 無量壽如來) in the west, offering one's body. Mantra: "Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-pūja-pravartanāya ātmānaṃ niryātayāmi, sarva-tathāgata-vajra-dharma-pravartaya svāhā hrīḥ."

One contemplates: "In order to make offerings by turning the Dharma wheel for all tathāgatas, I now offer my body. May all tathāgatas turn the vajra Dharma wheel for me."

With vajra añjali on the heart and the crown of the head to the ground, one bows to Amoghasiddhi Tathāgata (Bùkōng chéngjiù rúlái 不空成就如來) in the north. Mantra: "Oṃ sarva-tathāgata-pūja-karmaṇi aḥ ātmānaṃ niryātayāmi, sarva-tathāgata-vajra-karma kuru svāhā aḥ."

One contemplates: "In order to make offerings to all tathāgatas by performing activities, I now offer my body. May all tathāgatas perform the vajra activities for me."

## The Vajra Holding Mudrā and Visualization

Next, with right knee on the ground, one forms the vajra-holding mudrā, places it on the crown, and visualizes bowing universally to the feet of all tathāgatas and bodhisattvas. The mudrā is: left hand face-down and right face-up, the little fingers mutually hooking. Mantra: "Oṃ vajra-dhuḥ."

> Next, rejoicing, entreating,
> Dedicating, and making vows.
> Then, in half-lotus posture,
> Both hands in vajra fists
> Placed upon both knees.
> On the heart, tongue, and both hands,
> The syllable hūṃ blazes with golden light,
> Like the Bhagavat
> Abiding in the teaching posture.
> The body dwells in a pure moon disc,
> As if seated on a bright mirror,
> Radiance pervading the dharmadhātu,
> Universally purifying the realm of sentient beings.
> Then with the māṭa eye
> One gazes at the buddhas in space,
> Revolving one's gaze in the eight directions,
> Scattering vajra flames,
> Creating the boundary and expelling hindrances;
> The place becomes a vajra citadel.

## The Four Immeasurables

Next, one abides in the samādhi of the four immeasurable minds. Within the heart's moon disc, one visualizes the karma-vajra and with great compassion severs the suffering of all sentient beings, visualizing the karma wheel pervading the dharmadhātu.

Great compassion mantra: "Oṃ mahā-karuṇāya-sphara."

Next, generating loving-kindness, one extends the karma wheel throughout the realm of beings, bestowing immeasurable joy.

Great loving-kindness mantra: "Oṃ mahā-maitrīya-sphara."

Next, with sympathetic joy, one extends the karma wheel throughout the realm of beings.

Great joy mantra: "Oṃ sarva-śuddha-pramoda-sphara."

Next, extending equanimity, the karma wheel pervades the realm of beings, accomplishing great equanimity.

Great equanimity mantra: "Oṃ mahopekṣā-sphara."

## The Binding, Opening, and Seed-Syllable Visualization

Next, the vajra añjali mudrā: ten fingers interlaced right pressing left. Mantra: "Oṃ vajrāñjali."

This same mudrā becomes the vajra-binding. Mantra: "Oṃ vajra-bandha."

Next, the heart-opening mudrā: first, on the right breast one visualizes the syllable "trāḥ," and on the left breast the syllable "ṭa." One imagines these two syllables as door-leaves. With the previous binding mudrā, one strikes the heart and pulls open three times. Mantra: "Oṃ vajra-bandha trāṭ."

Next, one cubit before oneself, one visualizes an eight-petaled lotus. Upon the lotus one places the syllable "aḥ" emitting great radiance, white like crystal. With the vajra-binding, one extends the two wind-fingers as if picking up the syllable and places it within the heart palace. Mantra: "Oṃ vajra-āveśa aḥ."

Having placed the syllable, it remains vividly in the heart.

Next, with the vajra-binding, bend both thumbs into the palms and let each wind-finger bend to touch the thumb-backs, then touch the mudrā to the chest. Mantra: "Oṃ vajra-muṣṭi vaṃ."

Having sealed the heart-gate, one visualizes the syllable dwelling clearly.

## The Samantabhadra Samaya and Bodhicitta Visualization

Next, the Samantabhadra samaya mudrā: vajra-binding with the two middle fingers extended and joined. Mantra (one recitation): "Oṃ samaya-sattvaṃ."

Next, the joyful samaya mudrā: as before in binding, the ring fingers enter the palms and interlock; the little fingers and thumbs each stand upright and joined. With this great desire arrow, one shoots at the seeds of the three vehicles. Mantra: "Oṃ samaya hoḥ sūrata-sattvaṃ."

Next, the Trailokyavijaya mudrā: both hands in vajra fists, the little fingers hooking back to back, both index fingers straight. Mantra: "Oṃ sumbhani sumbhani hūṃ, gṛhṇa gṛhṇa hūṃ, gṛhṇapāya hūṃ, ānaya hoḥ bhagavan vajra hūṃ phaṭ."

Rotating this mudrā leftward creates the expulsion; rotating rightward creates the boundary.

Next, the concentration mudrā: both hands outwardly interlaced, palms up, placed below the navel; the index fingers press the thumbs. Mantra: "Oṃ samādhi padme hrīḥ."

Sitting upright and straight, one contemplates: "All dharmas arise from one's own mind. From the very beginning, they have never existed." Having entered the cessation samādhi, one then visualizes in space innumerable buddhas, as countless as sesame seeds covering the whole earth. Those buddhas each extend their right hand and snap their fingers to rouse the practitioner, saying: "Good man! What you have realized is the one path of purity, but you have not yet realized the ocean of sarvajñā. You should recollect the mind of bodhi and accomplish all the practices of Samantabhadra."

Having heard the rousing, the practitioner beholds oneself bowing before each buddha, and addresses them: "What is the bodhi-mind?" The buddhas reply: "Observe the syllable-gate in your heart. Its original nature is pure, like the full pure moon." They confer the mantra: "Oṃ citta-prativedikaṃ karomi."

The practitioner silently recites once and then visualizes one's own heart as the pure full moon. Then the buddhas further say: "Good man! The bodhi-mind's essence and characteristics are like this." They confer another mantra: "Oṃ bodhi-citta-utpādayāmi."

Having silently recited once, the practitioner contemplates: "The bodhi-mind's essence-nature is firm." Immediately upon the moon disc one visualizes a five-wisdom vajra. Mantra: "Oṃ tiṣṭha-vajra."

One visualizes the vajra as golden, emitting pure radiance within the moon disc, like crystal, luminous within and without. Then one visualizes this vajra expanding to pervade the dharmadhātu. Mantra: "Oṃ sphara-vajra."

Then one visualizes the vajra gradually contracting, and all the tathāgatas in space merge into one body, equal in measure to oneself, and stopping. Mantra: "Oṃ saṃhara-vajra."

One then further contemplates: "This body of mine has now become the vajra body." Mantra: "Oṃ vajrātma-kuhaṃ."

Knowing oneself to be the five-wisdom vajra, one then transforms into the body of the principal deity. The body has four arms: the upper two are in the posture of drawing a bow and arrow, the lower right hand holds a vajra face-up at the heart, the lower left hand in vajra fist holds a vajra bell at the left hip. The face is frowning yet slightly smiling, white in color, wearing the five-buddha crown, a crimson skirt and celestial robes, seated in half-lotus on a lotus atop a moon disc.

## The Root Mudrā and Maṇḍala

One forms the root mudrā: both hands in vajra fists, the little fingers and index fingers hooking in reverse. Mantra: "Ṭa-ki hūṃ jaḥ."

One empowers the four places of heart, forehead, throat, and crown with this mudrā. Then one forms the Vajradhātu sovereign mudrā: firm binding with two middle fingers extended, their first joints touching, and two index fingers attached to their backs. Mantra: "Oṃ bhuḥ khaṃ."

One places the mudrā on the crown while reciting the preceding mantra. Then on the forehead: "Oṃ vajrasattva." Then on the right of the crown: "Oṃ vajraratna." Then on the back of the crown: "Oṃ vajradharma." Then on the left of the crown: "Oṃ vajrakarma."

Next, the garland mudrā and armor are applied. Then the joy mudrā with three claps: "Oṃ vajra-tuṣya hoḥ."

One visualizes in the pure moon disc the syllable "hoḥ" transforming into the principal deity. Then one forms the vajra-entering mudrā: with the binding, both thumbs enter the palms. Mantra: "Oṃ vajrasattva aḥ."

And: "Oṃ vajrasattva dṛśya."

Next, with the four mudrās and four vidyās, one summons and draws the wisdom body into one's body. With the previous joyful samaya mudrā, the two middle fingers serve as the four attractors. Mantra: "Jaḥ hūṃ vaṃ hoḥ."

What was previously visualized is the Dharma body; what is now visualized is the wisdom body. Their merging signifies that they are one body.

## The Maṇḍala Visualization

Next, one should adorn the world through this heart-offering gate:

> Within the altar, visualize a white lotus
> With a wonderful golden vajra stem,
> Eight petals complete with stamens,
> Adorned with jewels, constantly emitting immeasurable light,
> Surrounded by hundreds of thousands of lotuses.
> Above it, further visualize
> The lion throne of the Great Awakened One,
> Adorned with the king of jewels,
> In a great palace with rows of jeweled pillars,
> Everywhere banners and canopies,
> Pearl garlands interlacing,
> Draped with wonderful jeweled cloths.
> All around, clouds of incense and flowers
> And clouds of various jewels
> Rain down mixed blossoms to adorn the ground.
> Harmonious celestial sounds
> Play various music.
> Within the palace, visualize pure and wonderful
> Worthy vessels and argha water,
> Jewel-trees blooming in splendor,
> Illuminated by maṇi lamps.
> In the ground of samādhi and dhāraṇī,
> The sovereign attendant maidens,
> The buddha-pāramitās and others,
> The wonderful ornamental flowers of bodhi,
> Perform various entertainments through skillful means,
> Singing the sound of the wonderful Dharma.
> By the power of my merit,
> The power of the Tathāgata's empowerment,
> And the power of the dharmadhātu,
> May universal offerings be established.

Then one recites the great Ākāśagarbha vidyā mantra: "Oṃ gaganasaṃbhava vajra hoḥ."

Reciting three times, all wholesome wishes will be accomplished.

Next, within the altar on the lion throne's moon disc, one visualizes the syllable "oṃ" becoming the principal deity. Before the deity one places the syllable "ma" for the Mind-Born Vajra. To the right, "ha" for the Kelikila Vajra. Behind, "su" for the Love Vajra. To the left, "kha" for the Spirit Vajra. At the northwest corner, "va" for the Mind-Born Vajra Woman. At the northeast, "jra" for the Kelikila Vajra Woman. At the southeast, "sa" for the Love Vajra Woman. At the southwest, "tva" for the Spirit Vajra Woman. In the east gate, "jaḥ" for the Form Bodhisattva. In the south gate, "hūṃ" for the Sound Bodhisattva. In the west gate, "vaṃ" for the Scent Bodhisattva. In the north gate, "hoḥ" for the Taste Bodhisattva. In the outer court northwest corner, "su" for the Spring Season Bodhisattva. Northeast, "ra" for the Rain Season Bodhisattva. Southeast, "ta" for the Autumn Season Bodhisattva. Southwest, "sattvaṃ" for the Winter Season Bodhisattva.

Next, on the heart of the painted image one places the syllable "oṃ," with the syllable "jaḥ" on either side, to form the principal deity. One then forms the hook, lasso, chain, and bell mudrās to invite and welcome:

> Both hands in vajra fists,
> The earth-fingers hooking in reverse,
> Both wind-fingers straight,
> The right wind-finger bent like a hook.
> Having formed this, recite the mantra
> And beckon three times with the right wind-finger:
> This is the vajra hook.

Mantra: "Oṃ vajrāṅkuśa jaḥ."

> Without changing this mudrā,
> The two wind-fingers face to face,
> Pressed together like a ring:
> This is the vajra lasso.

Mantra: "Oṃ vajra-pāśa hūṃ."

> Without changing the lasso mudrā,
> The two wind-fingers hook in reverse:
> This is the vajra chain.

Mantra: "Oṃ vajra-sphoṭa vaṃ."

> Without changing this mudrā,
> The earth-fingers and wind-fingers
> All face to face and join:
> This is the vajra bell.

Mantra: "Oṃ vajra-ghaṇṭā hoḥ."

> By forming the vajra hook,
> One brings down the principal deity.
> By the vajra lasso mudrā,
> One draws in the holy one.
> By the vajra chain mudrā,
> One causes the holy one to remain.
> By forming the vajra bell,
> One delights the holy ones.

## Offerings and Attendant Mudrās

Next, one offers argha water with the vajra añjali mudrā turned sideways to the left, empowering the vessel together with the mantra, then presenting it.

Then, with left vajra fist at the hip and right vajra fist face-up at the heart, one recites: "Hoḥ vajrasattvaṃ sūrata-sattvaṃ."

The Vajrarāja mudrā: left fist as if holding a bow, right as if drawing an arrow. This is the Mind-Born Vajra mudrā. Mantra: "Oṃ vajra-sattve hūṃ."

Both vajra fists with right pressing left, arms crossed embracing the chest: this is the Kelikila Vajra mudrā. Mantra: "Oṃ vajra-kelikili."

Left vajra fist supporting the right elbow, right fist raised like a banner: this is the Love Vajra mudrā. Mantra: "Oṃ vajra-giri hoḥ."

Both fists placed on the hips: this is the Spirit Vajra mudrā. Mantra: "Oṃ vajra-game hini."

The bow-drawing gesture performed more softly and directed slightly downward: this is the Mind-Born Vajra Woman mudrā. Mantra: "Jaḥ vajra-dṛṣṭi-sya-kema-ṭa."

The embracing gesture performed softly: this is the Kelikila Vajra Woman mudrā. Mantra: "Hūṃ vajra-kelikile hūṃ."

The banner mudrā as before: this is the Love Vajra Woman mudrā. Mantra: "Vaṃ vajra-ni-smara-rāṭ."

Both fists at the hips as before: this is the Spirit Vajra Woman mudrā. Mantra: "Hoḥ vajra-kāme-śvarī-traṃ."

Scattering upward: this is the Spring mudrā. Mantra: "Oṃ vajra-puṣpe."

Scattering downward: this is the Rain mudrā. Mantra: "Oṃ vajra-dhūpe."

With the binding, touching both thumbs' tips together and placing at the eyes: this is the Autumn Vajra mudrā. Mantra: "Oṃ vajra-āloke."

Anointing the chest with both hands: this is the Winter Vajra mudrā. Mantra: "Oṃ vajra-gandhe."

The hook is the Form mudrā; the lasso is the Sound mudrā; the chain is the Scent mudrā; the bell is the Taste mudrā (with corresponding mantras as given above).

## The Root Mantra Activation and Hundred-Syllable Mantra

Next, with the Vajrarāja mudrā, rotating the right fist inward toward the body three or four times, one loudly recites the mantra, causing the ten directions of worlds to quake, and all buddhas and bodhisattvas empower the practitioner, swiftly bestowing siddhi. Mantra: "Ṭa-ki hūṃ jaḥ."

Then one places the fist that was dancing on the heart, which pacifies the worlds in the ten directions. Mantra: "Hūṃ ṭa-ki hoḥ."

Next, one forms the root mudrā and recites the hundred-syllable mantra seven, three, or one time. Without releasing the mudrā, one recites the root mantra seven times and then disperses the mudrā above the crown.

The hundred-syllable mantra: "Oṃ vajrasattva samayam anupālaya, vajrasattva tvenopatiṣṭha, dṛḍho me bhava, sutoṣyo me bhava, anurakto me bhava, supoṣyo me bhava, sarva-siddhiṃ me prayaccha, sarva-karmasu ca me cittaṃ śreyaḥ kuru hūṃ, ha ha ha ha hoḥ, bhagavan sarva-tathāgata vajra mā me muñca, vajrī-bhava, mahā-samaya-sattva aḥ."

## Rosary Recitation and Conclusion

Next, one holds the rosary with both hands, lifts it to the crown, then returns it to the heart for recitation, empowering the rosary with one thousand turns and the mantra seven times. Mantra: "Oṃ vajra-guhya japa-samaye hūṃ."

Among the four kinds of recitation described in the yoga, vajra recitation (jīngāng niànsòng 金剛念誦) is the most fitting. One should recite ten thousand, one thousand, or at minimum one hundred and eight times, or more than ten thousand. With the mind settled on a fixed count, one should maintain that initial count for all subsequent sessions.

When the set count is completed, one again presents the inner and outer offerings, offers argha water, and makes one's personal aspirations. One forms the Trailokyavijaya mudrā again and recites the root mantra once, rotating the mudrā leftward once to release the boundary. One forms the initial samaya mudrā, places it on the crown, and recites the vajra liberation mantra to send off the noble deities and their retinue. Mantra: "Oṃ vajrasattva muḥ."

Having sent them off, one again forms the samaya mudrā, recites the mantra empowering the four places, performs the crown abhiṣeka, dons the armor, and makes the joy mudrā.

After leaving the ritual arena, at all times one should either abide in the great bodhi-mind or constantly maintain the great mudrā. In this very life one will attain equal awakening; how much more so will all other fruits be accomplished! The sole exceptions are the mind that does not benefit sentient beings and the mind that abandons bodhi. All other wholesome aspirations will be unfailingly obtained.
