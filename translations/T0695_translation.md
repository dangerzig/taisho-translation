# 佛說灌洗佛形像經
## Sūtra of Bathing the Buddha Image, Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0695

Translated from the Chinese. Western Jin dynasty, translated by Faju (法炬)

---

At that time the Buddha told the great kṣatriyas, the devas, and the people: "All listen with one mind." The Buddha said: "A human body is difficult to obtain, and the unconditioned Way is likewise. A Buddha's appearance in the world is rare. From the time of asaṃkhyeya kalpas past, my body was that of a layperson, and through cumulative kalpas I accumulated merit. In every life I disciplined myself, revolving through the five destinies, not coveting wealth or treasures, giving up my body to others without reluctance. I thereby became a crown prince. On the eighth day of the fourth month, at midnight when the bright star appeared, I was born. Descending to the ground, I walked seven steps, raised my right hand, and said: 'Above the heavens and below, I alone am honored. I shall become the unsurpassed teacher of devas and humans.' When the prince was born, the earth shook greatly. The first of the Four Heavenly Kings, up to the Brahmā heaven and Śakra, Lord of the Trāyastriṃśa Heaven, and all the devas among them, each brought twelve kinds of fragrant water mixed with various famous flowers and bathed the prince. The prince attained the Buddha-path, revealed the holy Dharma, and rescued all beings."

The Buddha told the devas and people: "All the Buddhas of the ten directions were born at midnight on the eighth day of the fourth month. All the Buddhas of the ten directions left home and entered the mountains to study the Way at midnight on the eighth day of the fourth month. All the Buddhas of the ten directions attained buddhahood at midnight on the eighth day of the fourth month. All the Buddhas of the ten directions entered parinirvāṇa at midnight on the eighth day of the fourth month."

The Buddha said: "The reason for the eighth day of the fourth month is that at the juncture of spring and summer, all wrongdoing and calamity are completed, all things are born, poisonous vapors have not yet stirred, it is neither cold nor hot, and the weather is mild and suitable. This is precisely the day of the Buddha's birth. Good men and good women! After the Buddha's passing, you should wholeheartedly contemplate the Buddha's immeasurable power of merit and bathe the Buddha image as though the Buddha were present. The merit obtained will be immeasurable and beyond all reckoning."

The Buddha said: "When I was formerly practicing the bodhisattva path, I was thirty-six times Śakra, king of the devas, thirty-six times a wheel-turning holy king, and thirty-six times a flying emperor. All disciples of the Buddha who have faith and a good heart should contemplate the merit and virtue of the Buddhas of the ten directions. If with fragrant flowers and various offerings they bathe the Buddha image, all their wishes will be fulfilled, and the devas, nāgas, and spirits will always follow and protect them, and will bear witness."

The Buddha told his disciples: "A human body is difficult to obtain, and the sūtras are difficult to hear. If one is able to reduce from the share meant for one's wife and children, and from the possessions that belong to the five claimants, and use it to bathe the Buddha image: as though the Buddha were present, all one's wishes will be fulfilled. If one wishes to transcend the world and attain the unconditioned Way, to be born life after life without encountering death, this can be obtained. If one wishes to be as diligent and courageous as Śākyamuni Buddha, this can be obtained. If one wishes to become like Mañjuśrī, an avaivartika bodhisattva, this can be obtained. If one wishes to become a wheel-turning holy king who flies and teaches, this can be obtained. If one wishes to become a pratyekabuddha or an arhat, this can be obtained. If one wishes to be forever free from the three evil paths, this can be obtained. If one wishes to be born in heaven or among humans with wealth and happiness, this can be obtained. If one wishes for a hundred sons and a thousand grandsons, this can be obtained. If one wishes for long life without illness, this can be obtained. The people of the world are greedy like the ocean. They would rather cut a piece of flesh from their own body than part with a single coin to give to another. When a person is born, they bring not a single coin; when they die, they take not a single coin. Wealth and possessions remain in the world while the person dies and goes alone. Think upon this suffering! One who bathes the Buddha image: the merit from this deed accompanies one through birth and death, without end."

The Buddha said: "If a person has even a single thought of goodness and performs this meritorious deed, all the good devas and spirits, the eight classes of devas and nāgas, and the Four Heavenly Kings will all join together to protect them. Whoever bathes the Buddha image: the reward of blessing in every life will always be purity. From this cause and condition, one attains the Buddha-path."

The Buddha said: "One who bathes the Buddha image with fine incense will obtain one's own reward: wisdom, purity, merit, and renown. One who scatters beautiful flowers upon the Buddha will obtain one's own reward: an upright and beautiful complexion with none to compare. One who offers silken banners to the Buddha will obtain one's own reward: in every life born with fine garments naturally provided, without limit."

The Buddha said: "Through cumulative merit, accumulated virtue, practicing goodness with utmost sincerity, keeping the precepts, practicing patience, vigor, single-mindedness, and wisdom, I thereby attained buddhahood. Today, the worthy one, so-and-so, with a compassionate and good heart, believing in the Buddha-path and wishing to attain liberation, bathes the Buddha image with various fragrant flowers. This is done for the sake of one's parents of seven generations and five kinds of relatives, siblings, wife, and children who are in difficulty; for the sake of all in the five destinies of the ten directions who toil in suffering; and for the sake of the Buddha's people who in their ignorance do not believe in the Buddha-path. May they in future lives be born as humans, upright, fine, pure and white, respected by all, unsoiled by dust. Wherever they are born, may they always meet the Buddha. Wherever they are born, may they always meet the Dharma. Wherever they are born, may they always meet the Saṃgha of bhikṣus. May so-and-so master the sūtras, with wisdom and clear understanding of the Buddha's twelve divisions of scripture, the four Āgamas, the ānāpānasmṛti, the thirty-seven factors of the path, the four foundations of mindfulness, the four right efforts, the four bases of spiritual power, the five faculties, the five powers, the seven factors of awakening, and the noble eightfold path. If one is able to wholeheartedly seek the Buddha-path, one will swiftly attain the stage of non-retrogression.

"May one attain the Buddha's thirty-two marks, eighty minor marks, the color of purple-golden jāmbūnada, the ten powers, the four fearlessnesses, and the eighteen distinctive qualities. May one's mouth produce the eight kinds of voice, with the ability to fly and see through all things, reaching everywhere. May the devas, nāgas, ghosts, spirits, and Hārītī and her retinue protect one's body, so that in going and coming one is always safe and secure. When traveling in the mountains, may one not encounter tigers or wolves. When entering an army, may one not be struck by weapons. When traveling on rivers or lakes, may one not encounter storms. May there be no illness upon one's bed. May there be no summons from the authorities. If one's wife, daughters, or children are in the difficulty of childbirth, may they have an easy delivery. In trade and commerce, may profit be a hundredfold. May evil spirits and noxious influences have no power to intrude. May flood, fire, thieves, enemies, and creditors have no power to harm. May slander and gossip be extinguished and suppressed. May one be diligent and not fall into remorse midway. May one practice like a bodhisattva and attain the Way like a Buddha."
