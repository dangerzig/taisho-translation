# 那羅延天共阿修羅王鬪戰法
## The Battle Method of Nārāyaṇa Deva and the Asura King

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1281

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Ratnacinta (寶思惟)

---

At that time, Nārāyaṇa Deva was atop Mount Sumeru, gathering together a great assembly of the devas: Brahmarāja, King of the Brahma Heaven; the Great Lord Maheśvara; Śakra, Lord of the Devas; and also immeasurable devas, nāgas, yakṣas, gandharvas, kinnaras, mahoragas, humans and non-humans; and also countless ghosts and spirits; all had assembled to hear the Dharma. The amṛta, the wondrous medicine, had been blended together and placed within a vessel. At that time the Asura King transformed his form and entered into the vessel, consuming the wondrous amṛta medicine. Thereupon Nārāyaṇa Deva saw the Asura King enter the vessel, and with his golden wheel struck the side of the vessel. The Asura King's body was thus severed in two.

For the asura who causes sickness: take white mustard seeds, benzoin, sesame, ghee, and honey; blend them together. Take khadira wood of finger-length, one hundred and eight pieces. With one recitation and one burning, calling that person's name, one will immediately obtain healing and recovery.

Next, the explanation of the mudrā methods.

**The Protective Body Mudrā.** Cross the two little fingers inside the palm; press the two ring fingers on top of the little fingers; then hold the two middle fingers straight upright; use the two index fingers to pinch atop the two middle fingers; press the two thumbs against the middle joints of the middle fingers. Rotate rightward three times; this is called the Protective Body [mudrā]. Thereafter, all Dharma activities may be performed without obstruction.

**The Sword Mudrā.** On the upper joints of the left hand's little finger and ring finger, press the thumb on top of the joints of the little finger and ring finger; the middle finger and index finger stand straight upright pointing up.

**The Wheel Mudrā.** With the samādhi hand, the wind [index] finger and the space [thumb] finger interlock and pinch, forming a circle in the shape of a wheel. Cast it upward three times; strike toward the place of obstruction; all asuras will be completely severed.

**Next, the Fire Mudrā.** The samādhi hand forms the vajra fist; the right hand extends its five fingers with a slight trembling motion; use the left index finger as a prop against the right palm. This can burn and destroy the minds of all śatrus. Direct this great blazing seal-impression toward them and visualize fire burning them. Performing this together with the homa will assuredly accomplish this mudrā.
