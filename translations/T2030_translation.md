# 大阿羅漢難提蜜多羅所說法住記
## Record of the Abiding of the Dharma as Spoken by the Great Arhat Nandimitra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 2030

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Xuanzang (玄奘).

---

Thus has it been transmitted and heard. In the eight hundredth year after the parinirvāṇa of the Buddha Bhagavān, in the royal capital of King Jayasena of the country of Siṃhala, there was an arhat named Nandimitra. He was endowed with the eight liberations, three knowledges, six supernatural powers, non-contention, vow-knowledge, marginal concentration, and other such qualities. His immeasurable merits were all fully complete. He possessed great supernatural might, and his fame and renown spread far and wide. Through the power of his vow-knowledge, he could know the various mental dispositions of all sentient beings in this world, and could further accord with them to bring various benefits.

When his conditions for teaching were complete, being about to enter parinirvāṇa, he assembled the bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, and others. He spoke of the various marvelous merits he had realized, and that all the superior deeds that should be accomplished for the benefit and happiness of sentient beings had been fully completed. He addressed the assembly, saying: "From this time forward, there is nothing more for me to do. Only nirvāṇa without remainder is my destination. You should know that if you have doubts, you may ask."

At that time, the great assembly, hearing these words, raised their voices and wailed, unable to restrain themselves, writhing on the ground. Some rose and exclaimed: "The Buddha Bhagavān entered nirvāṇa long ago. The noble disciples have also followed into cessation. The world has long been empty, without a true tamer. Now only the Venerable One serves as the eye of gods and humans. How can you wish to abandon us? We pray you will take pity on us and retain your lifespan a while longer."

The Venerable Qingyou consoled and admonished the assembly: "Do not weep. You should know that it is the nature of worldly things that whatever is born must perish. The buddha tathāgatas, having conquered the four māras, are autonomous over their lifespans. Yet in accord with worldly convention, they still manifest nirvāṇa. How much less should I presume to remain forever! Even if I were to accede to your request, there would be no benefit. You should embody this meaning and not give rise to worry or distress. If you have doubts, please ask quickly."

The bhikṣus and others, though they had received this admonition, only wept all the more. After a long while, they asked: "We do not know how long the unsurpassed right Dharma of the World-Honored One Śākyamuni will abide."

The Venerable told them: "Listen carefully. The Tathāgata already spoke the Sūtra on the Abiding of the Dharma in the past. I shall now explain it to you again in outline. At the time of the parinirvāṇa of the Buddha Bhagavān, he entrusted the unsurpassed Dharma to sixteen great arhats and their retinues, commanding them to protect and uphold it so that it would not perish. He further commanded them to serve in their own persons as true fields of merit for donors, so that those who give offerings might obtain great rewards."

At that time, the great assembly, hearing these words, found their grief somewhat relieved. They further asked: "As for the sixteen great arhats you have spoken of, we do not know what their names are."

Qingyou replied: "The first venerable one is named Piṇḍolabharadvāja. The second venerable one is named Kanakavatsa. The third venerable one is named Kanakabharadvāja. The fourth venerable one is named Subinda. The fifth venerable one is named Nakula. The sixth venerable one is named Bhadra. The seventh venerable one is named Kālika. The eighth venerable one is named Vajraputra. The ninth venerable one is named Gobaka. The tenth venerable one is named Panthaka. The eleventh venerable one is named Rāhula. The twelfth venerable one is named Nāgasena. The thirteenth venerable one is named Iṅgada. The fourteenth venerable one is named Vanavāsin. The fifteenth venerable one is named Ajita. The sixteenth venerable one is named Cūḍapanthaka.

"These sixteen great arhats all possess the three knowledges, six supernatural powers, eight liberations, and other immeasurable merits. Free from the defilements of the three realms, they recite and uphold the Tripiṭaka and are broadly learned in the outer texts as well. In accordance with the Buddha's command, through the power of supernatural penetrations they have extended their own lifespans. As long as the right Dharma of the World-Honored One should abide, they constantly follow and protect it. They also serve as true fields of merit for donors, so that those who give offerings may obtain great rewards."

At that time, the bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, and others again asked: "We do not know where the sixteen venerable ones mostly dwell, protecting the right Dharma and benefiting sentient beings."

Qingyou replied: "The first venerable one, together with his retinue of one thousand arhats, mostly dwells in Aparagodānīya. The second venerable one, together with his retinue of five hundred arhats, mostly dwells in the northern land of Kaśmīra. The third venerable one, together with his retinue of six hundred arhats, mostly dwells in Pūrvavideha. The fourth venerable one, together with his retinue of seven hundred arhats, mostly dwells in Uttarakuru. The fifth venerable one, together with his retinue of eight hundred arhats, mostly dwells in Jambudvīpa. The sixth venerable one, together with his retinue of nine hundred arhats, mostly dwells on the island of Tāmradvīpa. The seventh venerable one, together with his retinue of one thousand arhats, mostly dwells on the island of Saṃghaṭa. The eighth venerable one, together with his retinue of eleven hundred arhats, mostly dwells on the island of Prāṇava. The ninth venerable one, together with his retinue of nine hundred arhats, mostly dwells on Mount Gandhamādana. The tenth venerable one, together with his retinue of thirteen hundred arhats, mostly dwells in the Heaven of the Thirty-Three. The eleventh venerable one, together with his retinue of eleven hundred arhats, mostly dwells on the island of Priyaṅgu. The twelfth venerable one, together with his retinue of twelve hundred arhats, mostly dwells on Mount Pāṇḍava. The thirteenth venerable one, together with his retinue of thirteen hundred arhats, mostly dwells on Mount Vipulapārśva. The fourteenth venerable one, together with his retinue of fourteen hundred arhats, mostly dwells on Mount Vāsana. The fifteenth venerable one, together with his retinue of fifteen hundred arhats, mostly dwells on Vulture Peak. The sixteenth venerable one, together with his retinue of sixteen hundred arhats, mostly dwells on Mount Iṣādhara.

"Virtuous ones! If in this world-system, all the kings, ministers, high officials, elders, and householders, whether men or women, arouse sincere and pure minds and establish great assemblies of offerings for the sangha of the four directions; or establish unrestricted assemblies of giving every five years; or establish great assemblies to celebrate monasteries, images, sūtras, banners, and the like; or invite the sangha to come to their dwellings and establish great assemblies of merit; or go to monasteries and places for walking meditation and arrange excellent bedding, robes, medicines, food, and drink as offerings to the sangha; at such times, these sixteen great arhats and their retinues, according to what is appropriate, will disperse and attend. Manifesting various forms and concealing their holy deportment, they will mingle with ordinary people and secretly receive the offerings, causing the donors to obtain superior rewards.

"In this way, the sixteen great arhats protect the right Dharma and benefit sentient beings. When the human lifespan in this southern continent of Jambudvīpa has declined to its shortest, reaching only ten years, the kalpa of swords will arise, and people will slaughter one another. At that time, the Buddha-Dharma will temporarily disappear. After the kalpa of swords, the human lifespan will gradually increase until it reaches one hundred years. The people of this continent, weary of the killing and suffering of the previous kalpa of swords, will again delight in cultivating wholesome deeds. At that time, the sixteen great arhats and their retinues will return among humans, proclaiming and teaching the unsurpassed right Dharma, liberating immeasurable multitudes and enabling them to leave home. They will perform deeds of benefit for sentient beings. In this manner, until the human lifespan in this continent reaches sixty thousand years, the unsurpassed right Dharma will circulate in the world, blazing and unceasing.

"Afterward, when the human lifespan reaches seventy thousand years, the unsurpassed right Dharma will finally disappear forever. At that time, the sixteen great arhats and their retinues will all gather together on this continent. Through the power of supernatural penetrations, using the seven treasures, they will construct a stūpa, magnificent and lofty. All the remaining relics of Śākyamuni Tathāgata, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One, will be gathered within it. At that time, the sixteen great arhats and their retinues will circumambulate the stūpa, using various incense and flowers to make offerings, honoring and praising it. After circumambulating hundreds of thousands of times and gazing up in reverence, they will all rise into the sky, and facing the stūpa, speak these words: 'Homage to the World-Honored One, Śākyamuni Tathāgata, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One! We received your command to protect the right Dharma and to bring benefits to gods and humans. The treasury of the Dharma has now disappeared, and our conditions are fulfilled. We now take leave and enter cessation.'

"Having spoken these words, they will all at once enter nirvāṇa without remainder. By the power of their prior vows, fire will arise and consume their bodies. Like a lamp flame going out, no bones or remains will be left. At that time, the stūpa will sink into the earth, descending to the golden wheel at its base before coming to rest.

"At that time, the unsurpassed right Dharma of the World-Honored One Śākyamuni will disappear forever from this three-thousand great-thousand world-system, never to be heard of again. In this buddha-land, seventy thousand koṭis of pratyekabuddhas will appear all at once. When the human lifespan reaches eighty thousand years, the pratyekabuddha assembly will also all enter cessation.

"After that, the Maitreya Tathāgata, Arhat, Perfectly Enlightened One, will appear in the world. At that time, Jambudvīpa will be vast, pure, and magnificent, free of thorns and brambles, ravines, valleys, or mounds. The ground will be level, smooth, and moist, covered with golden sand. Everywhere there will be clear pools, flourishing groves, famous flowers, auspicious grasses, and heaps of various treasures, all radiating splendor upon one another, exceedingly delightful. The people will all have minds of lovingkindness and will practice the ten wholesome deeds. Because of their cultivation of goodness, their lifespans will be long, and they will live in abundance, happiness, and peace. Men and women will be plentiful, and cities and towns will be so close together that the flight of a rooster could span from one to the next. In their farming, a single sowing will produce seven harvests, naturally maturing without need for weeding or hoeing. Virtuous ones! At that time, the splendor of the lands and the rewards of sentient beings are difficult to describe in full. The details are as told in the Sūtra on Maitreya's Attainment of Buddhahood.

"After Maitreya Tathāgata attains perfect enlightenment, he will teach the Dharma for the śrāvaka assembly in three great assemblies, enabling them to escape birth and death and attain the realization of nirvāṇa. In the first assembly, he will liberate ninety-six koṭis of the śrāvaka assembly. In the second assembly, he will liberate ninety-four koṭis of the śrāvaka assembly. In the third assembly, he will liberate ninety-two koṭis of the śrāvaka assembly.

"If any kings, great ministers, elders, householders, men, women, or any other donors, within the right Dharma of the present Śākyamuni Buddha, are able to perform deeds for the Buddha and plant roots of goodness themselves, or teach others to plant them, using the seven treasures, gold, silver, true pearls, jade, fragrant wood, brass-stone, copper, iron, wood, stone, clay, or lacquer, or using silk threads, or colored paintings, to make buddha images and stūpas, whether large or small, down to the very smallest, the size of a finger-joint, or using incense, flowers, and various excellent offerings, whether many or few, to make offerings: those people, by the power of such roots of goodness, when Maitreya Tathāgata attains perfect enlightenment, will obtain good human birth. In the first assembly of that Buddha, with pure faith they will renounce the household life, shave their heads and beards, don the Dharma robes, join the noble assembly, and, according to their past vows, attain nirvāṇa. This is the first type of reward obtained by those who plant roots of goodness for the sake of the Buddha.

"If any kings, ministers, or common people, any donors whatever, within the right Dharma of the present Śākyamuni Buddha, are able to perform deeds for the Dharma and plant roots of goodness themselves, or teach others to plant them: that is, within the Mahāyāna Sūtra-piṭaka, regarding all those profound Mahāyāna sūtras that accord with emptiness, namely: the Prajñāpāramitā Sūtra, the Saddharma-puṇḍarīka Sūtra, the Suvarṇaprabhāsa Sūtra, the Vajrapāṇigarbha Sūtra, the Śūraṅgama-samādhi Sūtra, the Māyopama-samādhi Sūtra, the Mahāprātihārya-samādhi Sūtra, the Sūtra of the Samādhi that Gathers All Merits, the Sūtra of the Samādhi of the Tathāgata's Wisdom-Seal, the Sūtra of the Samādhi Endowed with All Majestic Light, the Jewel Platform Sūtra, the Sūtra of the Samādhi that Gathers All Bodhisattvas, the Sūtra of the Buddhas' Embrace, the Sūtra of Collected Inquiries, the Questions of Brahmā Sūtra, the Questions of Subhūti Sūtra, the Questions of Vīra Sūtra, the Questions of Pūrṇa Sūtra, the Questions of the Nāga King of the Ocean Sūtra, the Questions of the Nāga King Anavatapta Sūtra, the Questions of the Nāga King Drumadhvaja Sūtra, the Questions of Ratnapāṇi Sūtra, the Questions of Ratnacūḍa Sūtra, the Questions of Gaganagañja Sūtra, the Questions of Ākāśaghoṣa Sūtra, the Questions of Māyājāla Sūtra, the Questions of the Jewel Maiden Sūtra, the Questions of the Wondrous Maiden Sūtra, the Questions of Subāhu Sūtra, the Questions of Siṃha Sūtra, the Questions of Vīradatta Sūtra, the Questions of the Golden Light Maiden Sūtra, the Sūtra Teaching Inexhaustible Wisdom, the Sūtra Teaching Vimalakīrti, the Sūtra of King Ajātaśatru, the Sūtra of Truth, the Nārāyaṇa Sūtra, the Buddhāvataṃsaka Sūtra, the Padmapāṇi Sūtra, the Sūtra of the Names of Ten Buddhas, the Sūtra of the Assembly of Immeasurable Light, the Sūtra of the Sukhāvatī Assembly, the Sūtra Gathering Pure Flowers, the Mahāsannipāta Sūtra, the Sūtra of Entering All Paths, the Jewel Banner Sūtra, the Jewel Heap Sūtra, the Jewel Casket Sūtra, the Painted Colors Sūtra, the Supreme Crown King Sūtra, and such Mahāyāna sūtras as these, which number one hundred koṭis of divisions and further divisions; and moreover the Mahāyāna Vinaya-piṭaka and Abhidharma-piṭaka with their many divisions, all of which belong to the Bodhisattva-piṭaka; and further the Śrāvaka Tripiṭaka, namely the Sūtra-piṭaka, Vinaya-piṭaka, and Abhidharma-piṭaka: the Sūtra-piṭaka has the Five Āgamas, namely the Dīrgha-āgama, the Madhyama-āgama, the Ekottara-āgama, the Saṃyukta-āgama, and the Kṣudraka-āgama; within the Vinaya-piṭaka there are the Bhikṣu Prātimokṣa Sūtra, the Bhikṣuṇī Prātimokṣa Sūtra, the Vibhaṅga, the various Skandhaka divisions, and the Ekottara-vinaya; within the Abhidharma-piṭaka there are the Saṃgraha, the Ṣaṭpāda, the Prakaraṇa, the Saṃyukta, and the Jñānaprasthāna, among numerous other divisions; and there are also the Jātakamālā and the Pratyekabuddhamālā.

"Within such treasuries of the right Dharma, whether spoken by the Buddha, spoken by bodhisattvas, spoken by śrāvakas, spoken by ṛṣis, spoken by gods, or spoken by the wise, whatever can lead to benefit: if anyone, even regarding a verse of four lines, whether reciting it oneself or teaching others to recite it, whether reading it oneself or teaching others to read it, whether upholding it oneself or teaching others to uphold it, whether explaining it oneself or teaching others to explain it; or who honors and makes offerings to the Dharma-teacher; or who honors and makes offerings to the sūtra scrolls, using various incense, flowers, banners, canopies, music, and lamps as offerings; or who adorns the sūtra scrolls with various colored silks, bags, wrappings, threads, and ribbons: by the power of such roots of goodness, when Maitreya Tathāgata attains perfect enlightenment, they will obtain good human birth. In the second assembly of that Buddha, with pure faith they will leave the household life and enter the homeless state, shave their heads and beards, don the Dharma robes, join the noble assembly, and, according to their past vows, attain nirvāṇa. This is the second type of reward obtained by those who plant roots of goodness for the sake of the Dharma."

If any kings, ministers, common people, or any other donors, within the right Dharma of the present Śākyamuni Buddha, are able to perform deeds for the Sangha and plant roots of goodness themselves, or teach others to plant them: that is, by inviting bhikṣus and bhikṣuṇīs, whether in sequence or as circumstances allow, on the first, eighth, or fifteenth day of the month, to establish vegetarian offerings; or by going to a monastery to make offerings to the Buddha, to make offerings to the assembly, or to serve as attendants; or by making offerings to those who practice meditation; or by making offerings to Dharma-teachers; or by seeing someone who wishes to study and propagate the right Dharma and is learning from a teacher, not creating obstructions, providing what is needed so they do not become timid or turn back; or by establishing unrestricted assemblies of giving every five years; or by giving to the sangha of the four directions; or by giving monastery buildings, bedding, bells, chimes, gardens, or groves; and other such offerings to the sangha assembly: by the power of such roots of goodness, when Maitreya Tathāgata attains perfect enlightenment, they will obtain good human birth. In the third assembly of that Buddha, with pure faith they will leave the household life and enter the homeless state, shave their heads and beards, don the Dharma robes, join the noble assembly, and, according to their past vows, attain nirvāṇa. This is the third type of reward obtained by those who plant roots of goodness for the sake of the Sangha.

At that time, the great arhat Qingyou, having broadly spoken of the above matters for the great assembly, through the power of supernatural penetrations, rose bodily into the sky before the great assembly, ascending to the height of seven tāla trees. He manifested various inconceivable paired miraculous transformations, causing those who watched to advance on the superior path. After the Venerable One had manifested these miraculous transformations, he sat cross-legged in the sky, abandoned all vital functions and life-forces, and entered the realm of nirvāṇa without remainder. By the power of his prior vows, fire arose and consumed his body. In the sky, his bodily relics rained down. At that time, the great assembly lamented in wonder and competed to collect the relics. They built stūpas and, with various incense, flowers, jeweled banners, canopies, music, and lamps, constantly made offerings.

This Record of the Abiding of the Dharma has been transmitted from teacher to teacher since ancient times, recited and upheld without being forgotten. It was composed so that all kings, great ministers, elders, householders, and various donors might understand cause and effect, grow weary of the bodies of birth, old age, sickness, and death, which are like plantain trees, illusions, flames, bubbles, and foam, and cultivate superior deeds, so that in the future they might encounter and serve Maitreya, be liberated from afflictions, attain the great nirvāṇa, and give rise to delight. Thus may they protect, uphold, and establish the right Dharma of the Buddha, causing it to endure for a long time and not perish.
