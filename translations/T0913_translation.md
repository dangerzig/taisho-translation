# 火𤙖供養儀軌
## Ritual Manual for Homa Fire Offerings

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0913

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown

---

When one wishes to practice the dhāraṇī method, one must first adorn the practice-hall, keeping the interior and exterior clean, with fresh and pure clothing, free from clamor and idle discourse. Follow the method of recitation without missing any of the four daily sessions. If one misses even a single session or violates the procedure, one must start over from the point of the violation. Even if one were about to complete the full count, lacking only one session, the loss of that session renders the entire count void. One must maintain an unbroken practice from beginning to end before one may speak of having completed the count. The number of recitations follows the sequence proper to one's own principal deity's division. When the count is completed, signs will naturally appear. However, if during recitation one violates the samaya, if the mind is not concentrated and focused, or if extraneous thoughts arise, one cannot achieve correspondence, and the pure signs will not manifest before one. The dedicated practitioner must make offerings each month according to one's division, with whatever one can provide. If offerings are neglected, this too prevents correspondence.

The method of offering: all use the time of the first sunrise. Prepare the altar-food: sweet, fragrant, and crisp cakes; foods with fillings, steamed with oil; milk-porridge and curd-gruel with added ghee and honey; and various exquisite delicacies according to one's means. Arrange all items in separate dishes with various arrangements of beans. When preparing the food and when about to enter the practice-hall, always use the Kuṇḍalī heart-vidyā and its seal. Seal all the offering articles. The vidyā:

*Oṃ aṃ amṛta hūṃ phaṭ*

Seal everything with three or seven recitations. This prevents airborne rākṣasas from stealing the offerings. If one does not attend to this, the offerings are often contaminated by impurities, and the messenger-spirits will not be pleased, thereby obstructing accomplishment.

When establishing the boundary and protecting the body, one must act swiftly without hesitation or delay, lest quick-footed yakṣas come to cause disturbance. Therefore, when establishing the boundary-protection and making offerings, one must be thoroughly prepared and act with urgency.

When making offerings, first bathe and cleanse oneself. Outside the practice-hall, sprinkle the body with fragrant water. Then call out the syllable *hūṃ* three times. With the right hand, make a vajra fist. Visualize the holy assembly filling the practice-hall like reflections layered upon each other without mutual obstruction. Then bow three times.

Next, recite the Mahācakra-vajra mantra.

Make the vajra añjali and place both knees on the ground. Next, visualize the syllable *raṃ* burning and destroying the world until it becomes like ash and dust. Next, visualize the syllable *haṃ* as a black wind that sweeps and scatters everything until it is pure and free of all dust. Next, visualize the syllable-gate *vaṃ*, envisioning a milky water, clear and brilliantly reflective. Then upon it, visualize the syllable-gate *su* becoming Mount Sumeru. Below the mountain is a milky ocean pervading the Dharma-realm. The mountain's summit is level, with groves of fruit trees, flowers, and fragrant blossoms. The water and trees are clear and radiant, sparkling and gleaming.

Next, take the censer and burn incense while directing the visualization. Upon Mount Sumeru, visualize a vast palace of a hundred jewels, with boundless jeweled pillars and jeweled reliquary towers with hanging banners. Atop jeweled pennants are half-moon wheels, and upon the wheels blaze maṇi-jewels. In orderly rows, kiṃkara nets are strung between them. Then visualize the syllable *a* becoming a stūpa. Then visualize the stūpa transforming into Mahāvairocana, seated upon a jeweled lotus in full cross-legged posture at the center. On all four sides, row upon row of a hundred million lotuses, and upon each lotus sits a holy being: the Buddhas of the ten directions come as witnesses, with bodhisattvas and vajra-bearers attending before and behind. The assembly of the Two Vehicles is immeasurable and boundless; devas and nāgas fill every space beyond reckoning. Above in the sky, refined music and wondrous sounds, clear and delightful, broadly perform the Buddha's work: these are the Pure Abode devas creating their wondrous music. On all sides of the assembly are worthy vases, argha water, clouds of fragrance, and precious flowers raining down in profusion. Fragrant foods and rare delicacies overflow from jeweled dishes. Wondrous fruits, extraordinary nectars, and exquisite flavors of supreme richness abound. Jeweled garments with their trailing radiance illuminate the central hall. Jewel-torches and luminous brilliance, colors of every kind, shine and intermingle. Having completed this visualization, next follow the method of recitation, briefly reciting more or fewer repetitions of the principal deity's mantra.

Next, empower the food and drink with the Ākāśa-vidyā-devī and the Vaipulya mantra: this is called the dharma-offering. When the offering is complete, follow the method of recitation and send off the deities. The homa fire method follows entirely the same protective and recitation procedures as above.

The fire method has, in brief, four kinds:

**First, the Śāntika method.** One should face north. The hearth is made round. All the offering articles should be white. This is for pacifying disasters and extinguishing transgressions. The clothing and painted images should all be white.

**Second, the Pauṣṭika method.** One should face east. The hearth is made square. All the offering articles should be yellow. This is for accomplishment, the fulfillment of wishes, and all manner of superior undertakings.

**Third, the Ābhicāruka method.** One should face south. The hearth is made triangular. All the offering articles should be red.

**Fourth, the Vaśīkaraṇa method.** One should face west. The hearth is made octagonal, like lotus petals. All the offerings should be the color of purple sandalwood. This is for seeking audience with great personages and for the method of attraction and love.

All four hearths above should be one cubit square and half as deep. At the bottom of the hearth, mold a single-pronged vajra from clay, eight finger-breadths long. For the round hearth and the octagonal hearth, make a karma-vajra instead. The hearth lip should have three tiers, progressively lower from inside to outside, each tier four finger-breadths wide. Upon the lip's edge, paint flowers at will; if unable to paint, visualize them instead. The firewood, incense, flowers, and other materials should all be placed to the right.

Each day, perform three sessions of one hundred and eight offerings each. The firewood should be mulberry, silk-tree, mulberry, cypress, pine, and the like. Each piece should be split and trimmed to the thickness of a middle finger and twelve finger-breadths in length, free of knots and straight. Select medium-sized young trees; do not use old, withered trees or those with insects. When gathering the wood, one must first see auspicious signs before collecting it. If one sees an unfilial child, a person with a disability, a woman, or an evil beast or bird, one should not collect the wood there but seek it elsewhere. One hundred and eight pieces are needed for each session, stacked neatly in a rack to the right of the hearth.

**Method for unguent:** Take a block of white sandalwood of about half a pound and grind it on a stone with pure water. Add a small amount of aloeswood and grind it in. Add a small amount of saffron and grind it fine with the aloeswood. Also add a small amount of camphor.

**Method for argha:** Take pure, filtered water. Add a small amount of unguent to the pure water. Place fine, fresh flowers in the water as well. Use a new, clean porcelain bowl to hold it. Also, place a bottle of fragrant water before one's seat, facing the hearth.

**Method for ball-incense:** Aloeswood two liang, light sandalwood eight liang, storax three liang, nutgrass one liang, benzoin two liang, frankincense two liang, costus-root one liang, clove two liang, white sandalwood two liang. Camphor one part, saffron one part, musk one part: these three should be ground together separately. Opercula two liang. Combine all ingredients with refined honey in proper proportion. Add the camphor and the other two ingredients last.

**Method for powder-incense:** Nardostachys two liang, Lysimachia two liang, ligusticum two liang, clove one liang, Eupatorium two liang, aloeswood one liang, fine light sandalwood three liang. Storax two liang, camphor, saffron, musk, and white mustard seed (to be kept separately).

**The three flavors:** Ghee, honey, and curd.

**The five grains:** Unhusked rice, sesame, mung beans, red beans, and wheat and polished rice.

**Method for regular incense:** Use camphor; if unavailable, one or two grains of white cardamom will also serve. For the offering fire-food and hearth-food, also use dried dates, chestnuts, brown sugar, and rock sugar, arranged according to one's means.

For the initial offering, invitation, boundary-establishment, and body-protection, follow the procedures listed above. When establishing the boundary, add the earth-peg, vajra wall, vajra net, vajra flame, and the Acala sword-seal vidyā, and so forth. When issuing invitations, invite the eight-direction guardian deva-kings individually, reciting seven times each with the vajra invitation seal. First make offerings to the fire-deva with the three flavors, three ladles of the five grains, ball-incense, benzoin, and the like. Then direct the mind to universally offer to the holy assembly of the five divisions, reciting the Vaipulya mantra and the Ākāśa-vidyā-devī mantra. Next, recite the principal deity's mantra. Dip each end of the firewood into the three flavors; with each recitation, cast one piece into the hearth. One must maintain a steady and composed mind, without haste or violence. After all the firewood has been cast in, burn the five grains. Before each *svāhā*, add the specific prayer for one's aim. Frequently sprinkle fragrant water over all the materials and the fire. While sprinkling, recite the Kuṇḍalī heart-within-the-heart vidyā.

The mantra for initially kindling the fire:

*Oṃ bhūḥ jvala*

Fan the fire with a fan; do not blow with the mouth. If the fire is within a temple on pure ground, do not use fire that has been used to cook meat or impure substances in a lay household. Otherwise, one must obtain fire by drilling. Again, do not blow with the mouth. Use two censers: to the right of the kuṇḍa, burn ball-incense and aloeswood; to the left of the kuṇḍa, burn benzoin, or frankincense, or white gum resin. The quantity of offerings follows what the season allows: in times of abundance, do not burn excessively; in times of scarcity, do not allow the offerings to run out. If one cannot prepare offerings, then during the last watch of the night and at dawn, direct the mind to make expansive offerings, and recite the Vaipulya and Ākāśa-vidyā-devī mantras extensively: this will also suffice.

When casting wood and burning the fire, one should observe the fire's signs. The fire's color should be white, yellow, or red; black or blue are bad signs. Also listen to the fire's sounds: if the sounds are like pleasing music, this is auspicious; if they resemble the sounds of pigs, dogs, donkeys, mules, or owls, this is inauspicious. If the flames stream and flow smoothly, this is auspicious; if the fire smolders with billowing smoke that assaults the practitioner, this is inauspicious. Crackling and scattering sparks are also bad signs. When the signs are auspicious, the fire does not feel hot to the practitioner. The coals should moreover be straight and evenly arranged. One should wash them with fragrant water, all about twelve finger-breadths long. The mantras for the eight directional devas and the fire-deva are separately recorded in full. If the fire-signs are auspicious, one may then burn fruits in the hearth as offerings. If the fire-signs are inauspicious, do not burn them. The fire-offering practice is limited to seven days. If circumstances require it, twenty-one days is also acceptable. This fire-offering method supplements the recitation practice and causes swift accomplishment.

The Ābhicāruka and similar methods should not be performed, as seeking such accomplishments would obstruct the greater purpose. As for the visions encountered during visualization or in dreams, these are extensively described in the sūtras and will not be fully detailed here.

If the fire method does not quickly succeed, one should add the division-mother's mantra and recite it in tandem. After another twenty-one days of adding the division-mother's mantra, if one's original aim is still not fulfilled, stamp stūpa seals one thousand times daily. After yet another twenty-one days, one's aim will certainly be accomplished. The stūpa seal should be one cubit in length. One should also carefully assess one's own strength and the refinement of one's practice. Although there may be a set number of recitations, if one's conduct and protection are coarse and careless, correspondence will not occur. The three karmas must be gentle and the ritual methods thoroughly mastered. One must examine whether one's practice accords with the principle. When one naturally responds to the holy ones, it is like a polished mirror naturally reflecting one's own image.
