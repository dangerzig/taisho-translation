# 顯無邊佛土功德經
## Sūtra Revealing the Merit of Boundless Buddha-Lands

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0289

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Xuanzang (玄奘)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Bhagavān was in the country of Magadha, in the Tranquil Dharma Forest, seated upon a lion throne of wondrous enlightenment, of vajra firmness, adorned with immeasurable precious jewels and red lotus blossoms, together with bodhisattvas as numerous as the atoms in ten ineffable koṭi-nayuta-hundred-thousand buddha-lands, as well as devas, humans, asuras, and an immeasurable great assembly surrounding him in front and behind.

At that time, within the assembly there was a bodhisattva-mahāsattva named Inconceivable Light King. Empowered by the Buddha's might, he rose from his seat, bowed his head to the Buddha's feet, joined his palms in reverence, and said to the Buddha: "World-Honored One! Among the buddha-lands of all the Buddhas, are there differences, superior and inferior, in their time-divisions and adornments?"

The Buddha said: "Good man! One kalpa of this sahā world, the buddha-land of Śākyamuni Buddha, is one day and night in the buddha-land of the Buddha Amitābha in the world of Sukhāvatī. One kalpa of the world of Sukhāvatī is one day and night in the buddha-land of the Buddha Vajra-Firm-Joyful in the world of the Kāṣāya Banner. One kalpa of the world of the Kāṣāya Banner is one day and night in the buddha-land of the Buddha Supremely Wondrous Full Red-Lotus-Manifest-Body in the world of the Non-Retrogressive Wheel Sound. One kalpa of the world of the Non-Retrogressive Wheel Sound is one day and night in the buddha-land of the Buddha Dharma Banner in the world of Absolute Purity. One kalpa of the world of Absolute Purity is one day and night in the buddha-land of the Buddha Lion in the world of Bright Lamp. One kalpa of the world of Bright Lamp is one day and night in the buddha-land of the Buddha Universal Illumination in the world of Wondrous Light. One kalpa of the world of Wondrous Light is one day and night in the buddha-land of the Buddha Body-Radiating-Dharma-Light in the world of Difficult to Transcend. One kalpa of the world of Difficult to Transcend is one day and night in the buddha-land of the Buddha All-Supernatural-Powers-Wisdom-Light-King in the world of Adornment. One kalpa of the world of Adornment is one day and night in the buddha-land of the Buddha Moon Awakening in the world of Mirror Wheel.

"Good man! In this way, worlds progressively increase, filling up to ten ineffable koṭi-nayuta-hundred-thousand buddha-lands of atoms in number. One kalpa of the last buddha-land of the last world is one day and night in the buddha-land of the Buddha Wise Virtue in the world of Lotus Virtue. In that world, the assembly of bodhisattvas cultivates and perfects the supreme ground of the practices of Samantabhadra.

"Good man! As the days and nights of these worlds progressively increase, so too do the lifespans and bodily characteristics of the Buddhas, and the adornments of the bodhisattvas' worlds, because the merit of the sentient beings in those lands progressively increases.

"If there are good men or good women who hear this 'Dharma-Gate Revealing the Merit of Boundless Buddha-Lands' and rejoice in it with faith and reverence, receive, uphold, read, and recite it, reflect on it in accordance with principle, and broadly explain it to others, then at the time of death, immeasurable Buddhas from the buddha-lands of the ten directions will all appear before them, comfort and praise them, and cause them to increase immeasurable roots of merit. According to their vows, they shall be reborn in the pure lands of the Buddhas. Up until the supreme, perfect enlightenment, in every life they shall always remember their past lives, practice the bodhisattva conduct, and swiftly perfect it."

When the Bhagavān had spoken this sūtra, the Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva Inconceivable Light King and all the devas, humans, asuras, and the entire assembly, hearing what the Buddha taught, were all greatly joyful, and they accepted it with faith and practiced it.
