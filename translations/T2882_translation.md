# 呪魅經
## Sūtra of Spells against Sorcery

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 2882

Translated from the Chinese. Author unknown.

---

At that time, the Buddha was in the kingdom of Śrāvastī, with the Wondrous Nāga and all sentient beings. "The transgressions one commits are beyond reckoning. I shall teach the Way so that you may attain enlightenment. I shall now explain for you the visions of past and future.

"In the past, at the time of the Void-King Buddha, in the Jetavana of the Solitary-Giver Garden, there was an old person who, before a fox-hole, burned fat-fires. At midnight, the sun, moon, and five planets descended, and he created sorcery and enchantments. Above, he summoned the heavenly spirits. Below, he summoned the five emperors. He further summoned the mountain spirits. He further summoned the river-lord generals. He further summoned the great spirits of the five paths. He called upon a hundred ghosts to create sorcery and enchantments.

"Some made rush-figures, some made human effigies, some made talismanic writings and imprecatory spells. Some took people's personal names, some took thorn-needles, some took yellow earth, or muttered alone facing the sun and moon without being aware of it. Some took clothing from people's households, or took people's sashes, or took five-colored threads, or took steamer-bands from people's homes, or took crockery and earthen vessels, or took hair from people's donkeys, horses, oxen, sheep, pigs, and dogs, or took the tools of the five trades, or took door-wedges, or took spoons and chopsticks to create sorcery. Some rode enchanted hemp-horses; some pierced people's bodies; some pierced people's hearts and wombs; some bound people's hands and feet; some needled people's eyes; some needled people's waists and backs. Some took people's head-hair, yellow earth, five-colored silk, or upper and lower garment-sashes. Some enchanted people's field-crops. Some, grasping a group, chanted facing a wall and muttering alone. Some made beast-figures, some made human-head figures, some made talismanic writings and imprecatory spells, or caused human figures to speak irrationally. Some enchanted people's oxen, horses, sheep, dogs, chickens, pigs, and dogs. Some made living persons' mouths and tongues, or made horse-forms, or ghost-spirit forms, or forms of oxen, sheep, pigs, and dogs.

"Those who create sorcery are without the Way in life and death. They forcefully kill the virtuous. If they teach others to do it, a thousand calamities and ten thousand transgressions follow. The creators themselves shall receive the three beams and six pillars, and it shall return to the original master. Ten thousand transgressions and calamities shall return, and they shall perish of themselves. Urgently depart three thousand six hundred ten leagues, and do not tarry long."

"Now I invite the Blue Emperor Spirit-King of the East to come and devour the sorcerer's belly. Now I invite the White Emperor Spirit-King of the West to come and devour the sorcerer's head. Now I invite the Red Emperor Spirit-King of the South to come and devour the sorcerer's eyes. Now I invite the Black Emperor Spirit-King of the North to come and devour the sorcerer's feet. Now I invite the Yellow Emperor Spirit-King of the Center to come and devour the sorcerer's hands."

At that time, within the assembly there was a bodhisattva named Great Power, who adjusted his robes, bowed to the Buddha, and said: "World-Honored One, your disciple does not understand merit and transgression. I see that sentient beings in the world commit many of the five heinous crimes, do not believe in or follow the Way, devour one another, and constantly harbor evil thoughts. They do not know that being born in the heavenly palace brings happiness, and do not know that there are hells of grief and suffering. They torment one another and bewitch the virtuous with sorcery. We humbly pray the World-Honored One will separately explain this for your disciples, so that we may attain the traces of the Way and deliver all beings."

At that time, the Buddha told Bodhisattva Great Power: "You do not yet understand. I shall separately explain it for you all. Listen carefully now. I dispatch the Four Heavenly Kings and the others to descend into the world to remedy the hundred evils. They must not tarry. Urgently depart a thousand leagues, and you may attain liberation."

"The spell says: The sorcery-father is named Panyongdu. The sorcery-mother is named Panshinu. Now I know your family-name and your personal name. If you create sorcery, the calamity shall return upon you. If you teach others to do it, you yourself shall perish. I now invite the Four Heavenly Spirit-Kings to come and record the sorcerer's name. I now spell the sorcerer: may their head split into seven pieces, like the branches of an arjaka tree. Now I know your name. Urgently depart to another place and do not tarry."

"Now I invite Namo Buddhāya to come and spell the sorcerer: may their head split into seven pieces, like the branches of an arjaka tree."

"Now I invite the Four Heavenly Nāga-Kings to come and spell the sorcerer: may their head split into seven pieces, like the branches of an arjaka tree."

"Now I invite Abhimukha to come and spell the sorcerer: may their head split into seven pieces, like the branches of an arjaka tree. Do not tarry."

"The Buddha has such a great divine spell: may this sorcerer's head split into seven pieces, like the branches of an arjaka tree."

"Now I invite Bodhisattva Samantabhadra to come and spell the sorcerer: may their head split into seven pieces, like the branches of an arjaka tree."

"Now I invite Bodhisattva Great Wisdom to come and spell the sorcerer: may their head split into seven pieces, like the branches of an arjaka tree."

"Now I invite Bodhisattva Further Radiance to come and spell the sorcerer: may their head split into seven pieces, like the branches of an arjaka tree."

"Now I invite Bodhisattva Moonlight to come and spell the sorcerer: may their head split into seven pieces, like the branches of an arjaka tree."

"Now I invite Bodhisattva Covered Light to come and spell the sorcerer: may their head split into seven pieces, like the branches of an arjaka tree."

"Now I invite Bodhisattva Firelight to come and spell the sorcerer: may their head split into seven pieces, like the branches of an arjaka tree."

"Now I invite Bodhisattva Flower-Light to come and spell the sorcerer: may their head split into seven pieces, like the branches of an arjaka tree."

"Now I invite Bodhisattva Moon-Radiance to come and spell the sorcerer: may their head split into seven pieces, like the branches of an arjaka tree."

"Now I invite Bodhisattva Pattern-Light to come and spell the sorcerer: may their head split into seven pieces, like the branches of an arjaka tree."

"Now I invite Bodhisattva Dragon-Light to come and spell the sorcerer: may their head split into seven pieces, like the branches of an arjaka tree."

"Now I invite Bodhisattva Bright Star to come and spell the sorcerer: may their head split into seven pieces, like the branches of an arjaka tree."

"Now I invite Bodhisattva Thousand Suns to come and spell the sorcerer: may their head split into seven pieces, like the branches of an arjaka tree."

"Now I invite Bodhisattva Universal Radiance to come and spell the sorcerer: may their head split into seven pieces, like the branches of an arjaka tree."

"Now I invite Bodhisattva Great Radiance to come and spell the sorcerer: may their head split into seven pieces, like the branches of an arjaka tree."

"Now I invite Bodhisattva Same Light to come and spell the sorcerer: may their head split into seven pieces, like the branches of an arjaka tree."

"Now I invite Bodhisattva Midday Sun to come and spell the sorcerer: may their head split into seven pieces, like the branches of an arjaka tree."

"Now I invite Bodhisattva Establishment to come and spell the sorcerer: may their head split into seven pieces, like the branches of an arjaka tree."

"Now I invite Bodhisattva Tree-King to come and spell the sorcerer: may their head split into seven pieces, like the branches of an arjaka tree."

"Now I invite Bodhisattva Dragon-Deva to come and spell the sorcerer: may their head split into seven pieces, like the branches of an arjaka tree."

"Now I invite Bodhisattva Earth-Shaker to come and spell the sorcerer: may their head split into seven pieces, like the branches of an arjaka tree."

"Now I invite the Blue Emperor Spirit-King of the East to come. The sorcerer must not tarry. Now I invite the Red Emperor Spirit-King of the South to come. The sorcerer must not tarry. Now I invite the White Emperor Spirit-King of the West to come. The sorcerer must not tarry. Now I invite the Black Emperor Spirit-King of the North to come. The sorcerer must not tarry. Now I summon the sun, moon, five planets, and twenty-eight lunar mansions to come and seize the sorcerer. They must not tarry. Urgently depart a thousand leagues."

"The great beast of the East shall come and devour the sorcerer's body. The centipede of the South shall come and devour the sorcerer's eyes. The white elephant of the West shall come and devour the sorcerer's head. The black elephant of the North shall come and peck out the sorcerer's heart-organ. The Yellow Emperor Spirit Dragon-King of the Center shall come and devour the sorcerer's spirit."

"I see the sorcerer: eyes bulging, teeth bared, or acting wildly, not resembling a human form. Or at people's gates, or inside the house, or upon the mortar, or leaping incessantly in people's fields. Or on New Year's Day, or on the fifteenth of the first month, or on festival days, burning fat-fires and chanting without ceasing. I know your name. Do not tarry. Urgently depart to another place. Whether sitting, you perish; whether standing, you are destroyed. May your head split into seven pieces, like the branches of an arjaka tree. If red mouth and red tongue appear, the bodhisattvas' spell shall cause their destruction. Urgently depart. Do not linger."

At that time, the World-Honored One said: "I now tell all sentient beings of the ten directions. I have now, for all sentient beings, good men and good women, spoken this Sūtra of Spells against Sorcery."

At that time, all the buddhas of the ten directions, the seven buddhas of the past, the sixteen prince-buddhas, the bodhisattvas of the heavens, devas, humans, arhats, the Four Great Heavenly Kings, nāgas, the eight classes of beings, all good spirits, and those who have attained the four fruits were all assembled at the summit of Mount Sumeru beneath the Nāgapuṣpa tree, assessing how sentient beings might be saved.

"If any bhikṣu, bhikṣuṇī, upāsaka, upāsikā, man, or woman can burn incense, bow to the Buddha, receive and uphold this sūtra and make offerings without ceasing, they will cause others to obtain merit. If any person in grief or suffering can give rise to wholesome thoughts, all will attain the happiness of the heavenly palace. If any person circulates this sūtra, they should first purify and cleanse themselves, practice the Way, burn incense, and receive and uphold it, and they may attain liberation. If one can recite this sūtra seven times, the sorcerer's head shall split into seven pieces like the branches of an arjaka tree, and all manner of suffering shall be entirely liberated."

At that time, the entire assembly of all sentient beings, having heard the sūtra, were joyful and reverent. They bowed to the Buddha, received and upheld it, and put it into practice.
