# 求欲經
## 求欲經

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0049

Translated from the Chinese. 西晉 法炬譯

## Fascicle 1

### The Sūtra on Seeking and Desiring, Spoken by the Buddha

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Bhagavān was dwelling at Vāgī in the Crocodile Forest at Bhesakalā, in the Deer Park. At that time the Venerable Śāriputra addressed the bhikṣus: "Friends! There are four kinds of persons in the world who may be known here and now. What are the four?

"Friends! There is a person who has craving within, and he has craving within, yet does not know it as it truly is. Friends! There is a person who has craving within, and he has craving within, and knows it as it truly is. Friends! There is a person who has no craving within, and he has no craving within, yet does not know it as it truly is. Friends! There is a person who has no craving within, and he has no craving within, and knows it as it truly is.

"Friends! Regarding that first person: he who has craving within, and has craving within yet does not know it as it truly is — I declare that this person is the most wretched and corrupt. Friends! Regarding that person who has craving within, and has craving within and knows it as it truly is — I declare that this person is the most excellent. Friends! Regarding that person who has no craving within, and has no craving within yet does not know it as it truly is — I declare that this person is the most wretched and corrupt. Friends! Regarding that person who has no craving within, and has no craving within and knows it as it truly is — I declare that this person is the most excellent."

At that time, a certain bhikṣu rose from his seat, arranged his robe on one shoulder, and with joined palms turned toward the Venerable Śāriputra, and said to him: "How is this, Venerable Śāriputra? For what reason and on what account are these first two persons — both having craving, both having attachment of mind — declared, one to be wretched and one to be most excellent? And again, for what reason and on what account are these latter two persons — both having no craving, both having no attachment — declared, one to be wretched and one to be most excellent?"

"Friends! That person who has craving within, and has craving within yet does not know it as it truly is — know that he does not engage in joyful practice, does not engage in striving, does not engage in diligent effort. He abides in craving; his mind, attached in craving, comes to the end of life. When his mind, attached in craving, comes to the end of life, his dying is not good, nor is he reborn in a good destination. Why is this? Because he dies with his mind attached in craving. It is like, friends, a person who comes from a marketplace or a hired worker's house carrying a bronze bowl that is foul and impure. Having brought it, he neither washes it at the proper time, nor wipes it at the proper time, nor polishes it at the proper time, but leaves it in the dust. In this way the bronze bowl only accumulates further defilement. So too, friends, that person who has craving within, and has craving within yet does not know it as it truly is — know that he does not engage in joyful practice, does not strive, does not exert diligent effort. He abides merely in craving, and his mind, attached in inner craving, comes to the end of life. When his mind, attached in inner craving, comes to the end of life, his dying is not good, nor is he reborn in a good destination. Why is this? Because he dies with his mind attached in inner craving and seeking.

"Friends! That person who has craving within, and has craving within and knows it as it truly is — know that he engages in joyful practice and diligent effort. His craving ceases; his mind, without craving and unattached, comes to the end of life. When his mind, without craving and unattached, comes to the end of life, his dying is good and his place of rebirth is also good. Why is this? Because he dies with a mind free of craving and attachment. It is like, friends, a person who comes from a marketplace or a hired worker's house carrying a bronze bowl that is foul and impure. Having brought it, he washes it at the proper time, wipes it at the proper time, polishes it at the proper time, and does not leave it in the dust. In this way, in time, the bronze bowl becomes pure and bright. So too, friends, that person who has craving within, and has inner craving and knows it as it truly is — know that he engages in joyful practice, striving, and diligent effort. His craving is cut off; his mind, without craving and unattached, comes to the end of life. When his mind, without craving and unattached, comes to the end of life, his dying is good and he is reborn in a good destination. Why is this? Because he dies free of craving, with a mind unattached.

"Friends! That person who has no craving within, and has no craving within yet does not know it as it truly is — know that he is unable to guard the faculties of eye, ear, and mind with regard to phenomena. Abiding thus unable to guard them, lust arises in his mind; with mingled desire and mingled seeking, his mind attached, he comes to the end of life. When, with mingled desire and mingled seeking, his mind attached, he comes to the end of life, his dying is not good, nor is his rebirth good. Why is this? Because he dies with mingled desire and mingled seeking, his mind attached. It is like, friends, a person who comes from a marketplace or a hired worker's house carrying a pure, clean bronze bowl. Having brought it, he neither washes it at the proper time, nor wipes it at the proper time, nor polishes it at the proper time, but leaves it in the dust. In this way the bronze bowl in time becomes foul and impure. So too, friends, that person who has no craving within, and has no craving within yet does not know it as it truly is — know that he is unable to guard the faculties of eye, ear, and mind with regard to phenomena. Being unable to guard them, a mind of lust arises, a mind of mingled desire; with mingled desire and mingled seeking, his mind attached, he comes to the end of life. When, with mingled desire and mingled seeking, his mind attached, he comes to the end of life, his dying is not good, nor is his rebirth good. Why is this? Because he dies with mingled desire and mingled seeking, his mind attached.

"Friends! That person who has no craving within, and has no craving within and knows it as it truly is — know that he is able to guard the faculties of eye, ear, and mind with regard to phenomena. Having guarded them, he has no mind of desire; without desire, without seeking, his mind unattached, he comes to the end of life. When, without desire, without seeking, his mind unattached, he comes to the end of life, his dying is good and his rebirth is good. Why is this? Because he dies free of seeking and desire, with a mind unattached. It is like, friends, a person who comes from a marketplace or a hired worker's house carrying a pure, clean bronze bowl. Having brought it, he washes it at the proper time, wipes it at the proper time, polishes it at the proper time, and does not leave it in the dust. In this way the bowl becomes increasingly pure and bright. So too, friends, that person is likewise: he has no craving within, and has no craving within and knows it as it truly is — know that he is able to guard the faculties of eye, ear, and mind with regard to phenomena. Having guarded them, he is free from lust; without mingled desire and seeking, his mind unattached, he comes to the end of life. When, without mingled desire and mingled seeking, his mind unattached, he comes to the end of life, his dying is good and his rebirth is good. Why is this? Because he dies free of mingled desire and mingled seeking, with a mind unattached. This, friends, is the reason and the account by which, of these first two persons who have craving and attachment of mind, one is called wretched and one is called most excellent; this is the reason and the account by which, of these latter two persons who have no inner craving and whose mind is unattached, one is called wretched and one is called most excellent."

At that time a certain bhikṣu rose from his seat, arranged his robe on one shoulder, and with joined palms turned toward the Venerable Śāriputra, and said to him: "How is this, Venerable Śāriputra? What is meant by craving? Craving — why is it called craving?"

"Friends! Because craving gives rise to immeasurable evil phenomena, it is called craving.

"Friends! There is a person in whom a little craving arises; he has committed some offense and does not wish others to know of it, yet he has committed it. Friends! It can be known from this: when that person's offense becomes known to others, his mind becomes indignant. Friends! When that mind is indignant and he wishes to act, there is only unwholesomeness.

"Friends! There is a person in whom a little craving arises; he has committed some offense, and he speaks of it privately to another rather than in the assembly — that is his offense. Friends! It can be known from this: that person's offense is spoken of in the assembly, not privately but in the assembly, and his mind becomes indignant. Friends! When that mind is indignant and he wishes to act, there is only unwholesomeness.

"Friends! There is a person who has committed an offense and wishes to tell those of equal standing, not those of unequal standing. Friends! It can be known from this: his offense is spoken of to those of unequal standing — the offense he would tell to those of unequal standing is thus made known — and his mind becomes indignant. Friends! When that mind is indignant and he wishes to act, there is only unwholesomeness.

"Friends! There is a person in whom craving arises: 'I shall sit before the World-Honored One and question him; he shall expound the Dharma for the bhikṣus before me — let no other bhikṣu sit before the World-Honored One and question him and expound the Dharma for the bhikṣus.' Friends! It can be known from this: a certain other bhikṣu sits before the World-Honored One, questions him, and is able to expound the Dharma for the bhikṣus — and his mind becomes indignant. Friends! When that mind is indignant and he wishes to act, there is only unwholesomeness.

"Friends! There is a person in whom craving arises: 'When bhikṣus go somewhere, let me walk in front — let no other bhikṣu walk in front when bhikṣus go somewhere.' Friends! It can be known from this: when bhikṣus go somewhere, a different bhikṣu walks in front; when bhikṣus go somewhere, a different bhikṣu walks in front, and yet another bhikṣu walks before that bhikṣu — and his mind becomes indignant. Friends! When that mind is indignant and he wishes to act, there is only unwholesomeness.

"Friends! There is a person in whom craving arises: 'When bhikṣus enter a household, let me be seated first, receive water first, and receive alms-food first — let no other bhikṣu, when bhikṣus enter a household, be seated first, receive water first, and receive alms-food first.' Friends! It can be known from this: a certain other bhikṣu, when bhikṣus have entered, is seated first, receives water first, and receives alms-food first — and his mind becomes indignant. When that mind is indignant and he wishes to act, there is only unwholesomeness.

"Friends! There is a person in whom craving arises: 'After the bhikṣus have eaten and put away their bowls and vessels, let me expound the Dharma to the householders, encourage, instruct, and gladden them — let no other bhikṣu, after the bhikṣus have eaten and put away their bowls and vessels, expound the Dharma to the householders, encourage, instruct, and gladden them.' Friends! It can be known from this: another bhikṣu, after the bhikṣus have eaten and put away their bowls and vessels, expounds the Dharma to the end of gladdening them — and when that other bhikṣu has done so, his mind becomes indignant. Friends! When that mind is indignant and he wishes to act, there is only unwholesomeness.

"Friends! There is a person in whom craving arises: 'When householders enter the garden, let me converse and discourse with them — let no other bhikṣu, when householders enter the garden, converse and discourse with them.' Friends! It can be known from this: a certain other bhikṣu, when householders have entered the garden, converses and discourses with them — and his mind becomes indignant. Friends! When that mind is indignant and he wishes to act, there is only unwholesomeness.

"Friends! There is a person in whom craving arises: 'Let kings, ministers, brahmins, and householders — not just one person — all come to know me — let no other bhikṣu be known to kings, ministers, brahmins, and householders of many sorts.' Friends! It can be known from this: that other bhikṣu comes to be known to kings and ministers, and to brahmins and householders of many sorts — and his mind becomes indignant. Friends! When that mind is indignant and he wishes to act, there is only unwholesomeness.

"Friends! There is a person in whom craving arises: 'Let me receive offerings from the fourfold assembly — bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, and upāsikās — let no other bhikṣu receive offerings from the fourfold assembly — bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, and upāsikās.' Friends! It can be known from this: another bhikṣu receives offerings from the fourfold assembly — bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, and upāsikās — and his mind becomes indignant. Friends! When that mind is indignant and he wishes to act, there is only unwholesomeness.

"Friends! There is a person in whom craving arises: 'Let me obtain robes, bedding, lodging, and medicine for illness — let no other bhikṣu obtain robes, bedding, lodging, and medicine for illness.' Friends! It can be known from this: another bhikṣu obtains robes, bedding, lodging, and medicine for illness — and his mind becomes indignant. Friends! When that mind is indignant and he wishes to act, there is only unwholesomeness.

"Friends! There is a person who is likewise a person of wisdom and holy life; he should abandon these immeasurable evil and unwholesome practices of craving and should not engage in them. If he dwells among non-śramaṇas yet conducts himself as a śramaṇa; if he dwells among unwise śramaṇas yet conducts himself as a wise śramaṇa; seeks the seat of a senior though he ought not; claims to have meditative attainment when he has none; claims to have purity while dwelling in impurity — friends! Regarding such a person, those of wisdom and holy life, perceiving these immeasurable evil and unwholesome practices: knowing that one who dwells with non-śramaṇas is not a śramaṇa; knowing that one who dwells with unwise śramaṇas is not a wise śramaṇa; knowing that seeking the seat of a senior when one ought not is improper; knowing that claiming attainment when there is none is false; knowing that claiming purity while dwelling in impurity is impure — it is like, friends, a person who comes from a marketplace or a hired worker's house carrying a bronze bowl filled with filth, with another bowl inverted over it, and brings it into a crowd of people. Those many people, seeing it, wish to eat and wish to obtain it, not knowing the filth within. When that person carries it into the crowd and opens the vessel, those who wished to eat no longer wish to eat — how much less would the one who carried it wish to eat? So too, friends, a person of wisdom and holy life who commits these immeasurable evil and unwholesome acts — dwelling among non-śramaṇas yet acting as a śramaṇa, dwelling among unwise śramaṇas yet acting as a wise śramaṇa, seeking the seat of a senior when he ought not, claiming attainment when he has none, claiming purity while dwelling in impurity — friends! Such a person, having these immeasurable evil and unwholesome qualities, is known thus: one who dwells with non-śramaṇas is a non-śramaṇa; one who dwells with unwise śramaṇas is not a wise śramaṇa; one who seeks the seat of a senior improperly is improper; one who dwells without attainment is without attainment; one who dwells without purity is without purity. Friends! Such a person should not be associated with, should not be respected, should not be served. A bhikṣu who, not having shown respect to one deserving of respect, nevertheless shows respect; who, not having served one deserving of service, nevertheless serves him — for that person, throughout the long night, there is only loss and no benefit, suffering and association with an evil destination. Therefore, friends, such a person should not be associated with, should not be respected, should not be served.

"Friends! There is a person in whom no craving arises: 'I have committed an offense and do not wish others to know of it.' Yet he has committed an offense. Friends! It can be known from this: if others come to know of that person's offense, the mind of that person has no indignation. Friends! When that mind has no indignation and he wishes to act, there is only wholesomeness.

"Friends! There is a person in whom no craving arises; he has committed an offense, and speaks of it to others rather than to the assembly alone. Friends! It can be known from this: that person's offense is spoken of in the assembly, not privately to one person alone — the offense is spoken of in the assembly — and his mind has no indignation. Friends! When that mind has no indignation and he wishes to act, there is only wholesomeness.

"Friends! There is a person in whom no craving arises: 'Having committed an offense, I will tell those of equal standing, not those of unequal standing.' He has committed an offense. Friends! It can be known from this: his offense is told to those of unequal standing, not to those of equal standing; the offense is told to those of unequal standing — and his mind has no indignation. Friends! When that mind has no indignation and he wishes to act, there is only wholesomeness.

"Friends! There is a person in whom no craving arises: 'I have sat before the World-Honored One and questioned him, and the World-Honored One has expounded the Dharma for the bhikṣus — I do not wish that another bhikṣu sit before the World-Honored One, question him, and have the World-Honored One expound the Dharma for the bhikṣus.' Friends! It can be known from this: a certain other bhikṣu is before the World-Honored One, and the World-Honored One expounds the Dharma for the bhikṣus. Another bhikṣu sits before the World-Honored One and questions him, and the World-Honored One expounds the Dharma for the bhikṣus — and his mind has no indignation. Friends! When that mind has no indignation and he wishes to act, there is only wholesomeness.

"Friends! There is a person in whom no craving arises: 'When bhikṣus go somewhere, let me walk in front; let other bhikṣus also walk in front. Let no other bhikṣu be prevented from walking in front when bhikṣus go somewhere.' Friends! It can be known from this: a certain other bhikṣu, when bhikṣus go somewhere, walks in front. That other bhikṣu, when bhikṣus go somewhere, walks in front — and his mind has no indignation. Friends! When that mind has no indignation and he wishes to act, there is only wholesomeness.

"Friends! There is a person in whom no craving arises: 'When bhikṣus have entered a household, let me be seated first, receive water first, and receive alms-food first — let no other bhikṣu, when bhikṣus have entered a household, be seated first, receive water first, and receive alms-food first.' Friends! It can be known from this: a certain other bhikṣu, when bhikṣus have entered a household, is seated first, receives water first, and receives alms-food first — and his mind has no indignation. Friends! When that mind has no indignation and he wishes to act, there is only wholesomeness.

"Friends! There is a person in whom no craving arises: 'After the bhikṣus have eaten and put away their bowls and vessels, let me expound the Dharma to the householders, instruct, encourage, and gladden them — let no other bhikṣu, after the bhikṣus have eaten and put away their bowls and vessels, expound the Dharma to the householders, instruct, encourage, and gladden them.' Friends! It can be known from this: a certain other bhikṣu, after the bhikṣus have eaten and put away their bowls and vessels, expounds the Dharma to the householders, encourages, instructs, and gladdens them — and his mind has no indignation. Friends! When that mind, free of indignation, wishes to act, there is only wholesomeness.

"Friends! There is a person in whom no craving arises: 'When householders enter the garden, let me converse with them — let no other bhikṣu, when householders enter the garden, converse with them.' Friends! It can be known from this: a certain other bhikṣu, when householders have entered the garden, converses with them; another bhikṣu, when householders have entered the garden, converses with them — and his mind has no indignation. Friends! When that mind, free of indignation, wishes to act, there is only wholesomeness.

"Friends! There is a person in whom no craving arises: 'Let kings, ministers, brahmins, and householders of many sorts come to know me — let no other bhikṣu be known to kings, ministers, brahmins, and householders of many sorts.' Friends! It can be known from this: a certain other bhikṣu is known to kings, ministers, brahmins, and householders of many sorts. That other bhikṣu is known to kings, ministers, brahmins, and householders of many sorts — and his mind has no indignation. Friends! When that mind, free of indignation, wishes to act, there is only wholesomeness.

"Friends! There is a person in whom no craving arises: 'Let me receive offerings from the fourfold assembly — bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, and upāsikās — let no other bhikṣu receive offerings from the fourfold assembly — bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, and upāsikās.' Friends! It can be known from this: a certain other bhikṣu receives offerings from the fourfold assembly — bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, and upāsikās — and his mind has no indignation. Friends! When that mind, free of indignation, wishes to act, there is only wholesomeness.

"Friends! There is a person in whom no craving arises: 'Let me obtain robes, bedding, lodging, and medicine for illness — let no other bhikṣu obtain robes, bedding, lodging, and medicine for illness.' Friends! It can be known from this: another bhikṣu obtains robes, bedding, lodging, and medicine for illness. That other bhikṣu obtains robes, bedding, lodging, and medicine for illness — and his mind has no indignation. Friends! When that mind has no indignation and he wishes to act, there is only wholesomeness.

"Friends! That person who is a person of wisdom and holy life, who cultivates these immeasurable wholesome practices: if, not knowing that one dwells among śramaṇas, one says they are not a śramaṇa; not knowing that one dwells among wise śramaṇas, one says they are not a wise śramaṇa; not knowing that one dwells with those of senior rank, one says they are not of senior rank; not knowing that one dwells in attainment, one says they have no attainment; not knowing that one dwells in purity, one says they are not pure — friends! Regarding a person of wisdom and holy life who has these immeasurable wholesome practices: all should be known as they are. One who dwells with śramaṇas should be known to dwell with śramaṇas; one who dwells with wise śramaṇas should be known to dwell with wise śramaṇas; one who dwells in attainment should be known to dwell in attainment; one who dwells in purity should be known to dwell in purity. It is like, friends, a person who comes from a marketplace or a hired worker's house carrying a bronze bowl filled with food and drink of various fine flavors, with another bowl inverted over it, and brings it into a crowd of people. Those many people, seeing it, do not want to eat and do not wish to obtain it, thinking poorly of it, saying: 'It is the same vessel as before, it is the same vessel as before.' But when he carries it into the crowd and opens the vessel, those who did not wish to eat now wish to eat — how much more those who had seen it before and did not wish to eat? So too, friends, a person of wisdom and holy life who has these immeasurable wholesome practices, yet cannot be recognized at first: one who dwells with śramaṇas is said not to be a śramaṇa; one who dwells with wise śramaṇas is said not to be a wise śramaṇa; one who dwells with those of senior rank is said not to be of senior rank; one who dwells in attainment is said to have no attainment; one who dwells in purity is said to have no purity. But then, friends, regarding such a person of wisdom and holy life who has immeasurable wholesome practices: afterwards it is known that one who dwells with śramaṇas is a śramaṇa; one who dwells with wise śramaṇas is a wise śramaṇa; one who dwells with those of senior rank is known to be of senior rank; one who dwells in attainment is known to have attainment; one who dwells in purity is known to have purity. Friends! Such a person should be respected, served, and revered. Bhikṣus should associate with him, should respect him if he is one worthy of respect, should serve him if he is one worthy of service, should ever follow the proper course of conduct. For that person, throughout the long night, there is only benefit and happiness. Therefore, friends, such a person should be associated with, respected, and served."

At that time the Venerable Mahāmaudgalyāyana was also present in the assembly. Then Mahāmaudgalyāyana said to the Venerable Śāriputra: "Venerable Śāriputra! I wish to offer a simile — shall I speak it?"

"Speak it, friend Maudgalyāyana!"

"Venerable Śāriputra! Once at Rājagṛha on Gṛdhrakūṭa Mountain, I arose early in the morning, put on my robes, and with bowl and robe went to Rājagṛha for alms. While walking through Rājagṛha for alms, I came to the house of a skilled craftsman, and there I saw Nirmāṇarata Pūrṇa at the skilled craftsman's house, watching him shape a wheel-axle. Nirmāṇarata Pūrṇa, while at the craftsman's house, had this thought arise: 'Whatever place the craftsman trims and works on this axle — if that is what obstructs the axle, then it will be removed.' Nirmāṇarata Pūrṇa, while at the craftsman's house, had this thought arise, and the craftsman, just as he had thought, took the axle and shaped it with his adze. Then Nirmāṇarata Pūrṇa spoke thus: 'This craftsman knows my mind — he has taken this axle and shaped it just so; thus this axle will be enhanced and without obstruction.'

"In this way, Venerable Śāriputra — that person who is given to flattery and deception, faithless and without trust, lazy and without diligent effort, of scattered mind and without attainment, of corrupt wisdom and confused mind, whose faculties are unsettled, who is lax in conduct, who does not discern the śramaṇa's practice — the Venerable Śāriputra, knowing his thought, expounded the teaching in response. Venerable Śāriputra! That person who is free of flattery and deception, not a deceiver, who has faith and confidence, who practices diligent effort, whose mind is ever settled, who is learned in wisdom, who is reverent in conduct, who greatly discerns the śramaṇa's practice — having heard the Dharma expounded by the Venerable Śāriputra, it was like drinking deeply and eating well; with mouth and mind he received and held it entirely. It is like, Venerable Śāriputra, a kṣatriya woman, a brahmin woman, a householder's wife, or a craftsman's wife who, having bathed and anointed herself with fragrance and clothed herself in clean white garments — if a person, moved by compassion, wishing to benefit her and bring her ease, were to offer her a garland of utpala flowers, a garland of campaka flowers, a garland of vārsika flowers, or a garland of atimukta flowers, she would receive them with both hands and crown her own head with them. So too, Venerable Śāriputra, that person who is free of flattery and deception, who is not faithless but has faith, who is able to practice diligent effort, whose mind is ever settled, who is respectful and learned in wisdom and conduct, who is greatly discerning in the śramaṇa's practice — having heard the Dharma from the Venerable Śāriputra, it was like drinking deeply and eating well; with mouth and mind it was the same. How remarkable, Venerable Śāriputra, that the Venerable Śāriputra, for those of holy life, removes the unwholesome and establishes them in the wholesome! Well done, friend! This is the mark of a true person."

Each having thus spoken, rejoicing in one another's words, they rose from their seats and returned each to their own place.
