## Miscellaneous Items

The aforementioned box contains the personal correspondence of Dharma Master Ennin, who sought the teachings: one fascicle of the *Prajñāpāramitānaya-sūtra-ṭīkā*; the Vajracchedikā-sūtra in Sanskrit script; the Prajñāpāramitā-hṛdaya-sūtra in Sanskrit; the Vajracchedikā-sūtra-śāstra verses in Sanskrit script; Sanskrit miscellaneous vocabulary; seventeen altar diagrams; homa altar diagrams; Garbhadhātu mudrā diagrams; the *Pañcaguhya* ritual manuals; and other items. These are held in one sealed box. The box is fully sealed and must not be opened or removed. There is a particular reason for this. It is not that the Dharma teachings are being withheld.

The above represents what was obtained by Dharma Master Ennin, holder of the rank of Dharma-Lamp Transmitter, through his request for teachings: newly translated and compiled Dharma texts and the two maṇḍalas, now being sent to Enryaku-ji. Regarding the dhāraṇī ritual manuals and related items, the Tang masters strictly enjoined that they must not be carelessly dispersed. The catalog was first attached to the second ship, entrusted to the Recorder Awata. Accordingly, the record is set down here as above.

Jōwa 7th year, 1st month, 19th day \[840 CE\].

Superintendent, Dharma-Lamp Resident rank, bhikṣu Jinquan.

Temple Master, Dharma-Lamp Resident rank, bhikṣu Zhizhe.
