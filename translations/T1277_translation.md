# 速疾立驗魔醯首羅天說阿尾奢法
## The Āveśa Method of Swift and Immediate Verification Taught by Maheśvara Deva

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1277

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空).

---

At that time, Nārāyaṇa Deva was at the summit of Mount Gandhamādana. He invited Maheśvara to his Sovereign Palace, made offerings, bowed with his head touching the feet of Maheśvara, and addressed him: "The Garuḍa messenger whom I employ can accomplish whatever worldly matters are required, yet it cannot do so swiftly. May the Great Deva, for the sake of sentient beings in the future, teach the method of āveśa (spirit-possession) for swift and immediate verification."

Then Maheśvara addressed Nārāyaṇa: "Listen carefully. I shall teach you the method of swift accomplishment using messengers, which can perform pacification, increase, subjugation, and attraction. It can also dispatch messengers to travel back and forth in the realm of Yama. It can reveal future good and evil, auspicious and inauspicious outcomes, success and failure, untimely droughts and floods, invasions by neighboring states, and rebellions by evil persons, along with various other omens of calamity and fortune.

"If one wishes to know future events, one should select four or five boys or girls, aged about seven or eight, whose bodies bear no scars or marks, and who are intelligent and perceptive. First have them observe a vegetarian diet for one week, or three days. Whenever performing this method, one must select an auspicious day: either the Ghost Mansion day, the Year Mansion direct day, or the Amṛta direct day, which is supreme. Bathe the entire body with fragrant water, put on clean garments, and place camphor and cardamom in the mouth.

"The mantra-reciter sits facing east. Before him, coat a small altar with white sandalwood paste, about one cubit in size. Have the boy or girl stand upon the altar. Scatter flowers before the child. Place an arghya brazier and take benzoin incense, empower it with the great-mudrā mantra seven times, and burn it so the child's hands are fumigated. Then take red flowers, empower them seven times, and place them in the child's palms. Then have the child cover their face with their hands.

"The mantra-reciter then forms the great mudrā: both palms joined with fingers externally crossed, left pressing right, with the palms hollow. Empower one's own body at five places with this mudrā: forehead, right shoulder, left shoulder, heart, and throat. Scatter the mudrā above the crown. Then recite the mantra:

*Namo bhagavatī mahā-mudrā-kaṭa-la-śikhi-dṛśi mudrā-dṛśi ruca-nī īśa-nī paśu-pati svāhā.*

"Then press this mudrā upon the child's crown and visualize above the head a triangular red, blazing fire-wheel of radiance. Recite the mantra seven times. The fire-wheel mantra:

*Oṃ agni-śikhi svāhā.*

"Then press this mudrā upon the child's mouth and visualize a water-wheel within the mouth, white, in the shape of a half-moon. Recite the mantra seven times:

*Oṃ jala-cūrṇa-maṇi svāhā.*

"Next, move the mudrā and press it upon the child's heart. Visualize an earth-wheel, square and yellow in form. Recite the mantra seven times:

*Oṃ mahā-māla-parakrama svāhā.*

"Next, move the mudrā and press it upon the child's navel. Visualize a wind-wheel, round and black in form. Recite the mantra seven times:

*Oṃ vināta-kula-nanda svāhā.*

"Next, empower the child's two feet with the great mudrā and visualize a garuḍa. Recite the mantra:

*Oṃ pakṣirāja-pada svāhā.*

"Next, with the great mudrā, recite the armor mantra and empower the child, rotating around the entire body:

*Oṃ kavaca-mahā-bhūtādhipati svāhā.*

"The practitioner should next visualize himself as Maheśvara Deva: three eyes, adorned with a crown and jeweled necklaces. Above the crown is a buddha and half-moon. The throat is blue. Eighteen arms, holding various weapons. A nāga serves as the sacred thread, wound diagonally across the body. Furthermore, writing on it anointed with blood. After contemplating one's own body for a moment, one should then empower the child's 108 vital joints with the great mudrā. The mantra:

*Oṃ mṛtyu-viyāṭa-tajva-yūra-ka-śam.*

"Form the great mudrā, recite the mantra, and rotate it around the entire body to empower and protect all 108 vital joints.

"Next, empower the flowers, incense, and arghya water with the great mudrā and mantra.

"Next, establish boundaries in the ten directions with the great mudrā and mantra. Then, facing the child, recite the messenger-mantra of Maheśvara:

*Rī-cī-ṭaka-matana-ga-viṣa-da-paṇa-samana-gara-vikrama-vīra-sa-gatī hī-jīṭaka pūra-ri-pa-dhaṃ la-cala-cala cāri-cāri paṇa-paṇa paṇi-paṇi bhañji-bhañji kacchi-kacchi āviśa āviśa hī-jīṭaka-rū-nu-suṃ-ki-ñā-bhaya-tī svāhā.*

"This mantra should be recited seven times. The child will then begin to tremble and shake. Know that the holy being has entered the body. Then snap the fingers and recite the mantra again. If there is no manifest response, next recite the mantra for pressing the messenger:

*Hī hī yama-ruhita-sūra-sūra-pūjita-hana-hana-para-haṃ-ma-da-ni-da-turuni-turuni-muni-muni bhañji-bhañji kacchi-kacchi āviśa āviśa hī-jīṭaka-rū-nu-suṃ-ki-ñā-bhaya-te svāhā.*

"Recite this mantra and a swift response will certainly come. Ask about future good and evil and all matters of fortune and calamity. If the spirit does not speak, or speaks too slowly, then form the rod mudrā.

"Both palms joined. Both ring fingers braced externally. Both middle fingers standing side by side. Both index fingers each hook the tips of the ring fingers. Both thumbs each press the cross of the middle fingers. Recite the mantra:

*Oṃ munaga-ra turu turu svāhā.*

"Upon forming this rod mudrā, the spirit will speak. After asking about various matters, empower the arghya water with the great mudrā and mantra, sprinkle the child's face three times, and then release. For this messenger-mantra, one should first recite it 10,000 times for the method to be accomplished. Then the messenger will appear in person. One must then offer arghya water and make a petition: 'May the holy being, at all places and all times, accomplish whatever service is required.' The messenger then becomes invisible. Afterward, whenever one wishes to employ the messenger, coat a small altar and arrange incense, flowers, food, and drink, and recite the mantra 108 times. The messenger will then appear. One may then command: 'Fetch the longevity medicine from the nāga palace,' or 'Fetch the wish-fulfilling jewel,' or 'Go to King Yama's realm to extend life and increase longevity,' or 'Go above to fetch the wondrous amṛta,' or 'Go to another country and inquire about its good and evil.' The messenger can also assist in military formations, crushing hostile enemies. Whatever service is required, all can be accomplished.

"This method is the most supreme among all garuḍa methods, secret and difficult to obtain. You should select those who are suitable vessels worthy of transmission, and transmit it to them. If given to an unfit person, it will harm them, and afterward this method will no longer work. Therefore it should be kept utterly secret; do not transmit it recklessly."

The Maheśvara Garuḍa Dhāraṇī:

*Namo bhagavate rudra-rāya cinda-kalpara-rāya sarva-vināyaka-rāya sarva-karma-sādhana-rāya sarva-vaśīkaraṇa-rāya sarva-śatru-vināśana-rāya oṃ kapāla-citta-cinda-kapāla-bhūta-rudru-ki-ñā-bhaya-tī svāhā.*

"With this dhāraṇī, to subjugate a person: take a red plantain leaf, draw the person's image, and write their name upon the heart. Recite the dhāraṇī 108 times, then bury it in cow dung. The person will be subdued.

"If one wishes to cause a couple to hate each other: on a palmyra leaf, draw the figures of the man and woman, write their names facing away from each other, bind with thread, and take mouse-wolf hair, mountain-pheasant feathers, and shed snakeskin; burn them as fumigation. Recite the dhāraṇī 108 times, place inside a skull, and bury it in a charnel ground. They will come to hate each other. Remove it to reverse the effect.

"If one wishes to cause them to love each other: on a palmyra leaf, draw the husband's form, write the names facing together, bind with white thread. Take sparrow feathers and snakeskin, burn them as fumigation. Recite the dhāraṇī 108 times. They will come to love each other.

"Another method: if one wishes to cause two persons to fight each other, on tiger skin or cow hide draw two persons grasping each other's topknots. Write their names, bind with thread, heat over fire, and bury beneath a mortar and pestle. They will fight each day. Remove to stop.

"Another method: if one wishes to subjugate a person, on a palm leaf draw a human form and write their name. Recite the dhāraṇī 108 times and bury it beneath the bed. The person will be subdued.

"Another method: to cause someone to urinate blood, on a palmyra leaf draw that person and write their name. Take a nail, recite the dhāraṇī 108 times, and drive the nail in. After forty-nine days, remove it.

"Next, the eye-medicine method. If one wishes to subjugate a youth: take a snake head, *kṣāra-añjana*, costus root, and two wasps. On the fourteenth day of the dark fortnight, grind them to a powder, mix with flesh, and apply to the corner of the eye. All youths will follow and obey; even those in the heavens will come, not only those in the human realm."
