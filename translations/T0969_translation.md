# 佛頂最勝陀羅尼經
## Sūtra of the Supreme Victorious Buddha-Uṣṇīṣa Dhāraṇī

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0969

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Divākara (地婆訶羅)

---

Preface:

The principles of karma are subtle and fine: the two vehicles are insufficient to fathom their ultimate. The spiritual ruler is dim and elusive: even the ten stages cannot easily reach their depth. Thus we know that the assignment of fates and their interweaving is something the Master Kong rarely spoke of; that the intricacies of retribution and response remained obscure even to the Grand Historian. Therefore, the pattern of first laughing then weeping, the omens of hawks and sparrows, cannot be used as a standard; the sequence of initial misfortune ending in good fortune, the portent of a chariot over grain, cannot be verified. The doctrine of "leaning or lying hidden," which the Master Beneath the Pillar nearly illuminated in its subtlety; the discourse on "becoming calamity or fortune," which the Old Man at the Border could scarcely approach: if one were to investigate conditions and penetrate karma, to trace the fruit and illuminate the cause, and compare these in greatness to the hundred schools, what could rival the Ten Powers? Therefore, this teaching can extinguish the myriad evils of Supratiṣṭhita, shut the round gates of Yama, transform the ordinary into the holy, and extend the short-lived into the long-lived. Compared to Duke Jǐng of Song's method of causing Mars to retreat, it still clings to three lodges; compared to Lord Lǔyáng's waving back the setting sun, it is awkward in making noon twice. Not only does it offer shelter and extend blessings; seeing and hearing it attract great benefit, putting to shame the divine incense of foreign lands and the spirit-herb of the jeweled fields. Beyond this: light dust attaches and the nine afflictions are expelled; a clear breeze hunts and the three obstructions are annihilated. A bright mirror matches the radiance of a sun-jewel; its brilliance compares in splendor to celestial gold. Though the matter seems as easy as turning over one's palm, the seizing of the right moment to manifest the wondrous cannot be approximated through the ears and eyes of ordinary people to the rare and subtle of the Great Sage.

This sūtra was translated and presented at court on the fifth day of the first month of the fourth year of Yifeng [679 CE] by Du Xíngyǐ, Cavalier Attendant-in-Ordinary and Chief of the Protocol Office of the Court of State Ceremonial, together with General Ningyuan Dubà and others, by imperial command. At that time, because of the imperial and dynastic name-taboos, certain words were concealed and avoided: "World-Honored One" became "Holy Honored One," "world" became "living-realm," "Great Power" became "Great Approach," "rescue and heal" became "rescue and remove," and so forth. When the Emperor had finished reading it, he said to Xíngyǐ: "Since this is sacred speech, there is no need to avoid taboos." But Du, having received the edict and taking the taboos as established, set the matter aside. Not long after, by imperial decree, the Central Indian Dharma Master Divākara was commissioned to translate Dharma-treasures at the Hongfu Monasteries in the two capitals of east and west, and at Taiyuan. Du was consistently selected to assist, and I also participated at the end of the assembly. Du once said to me: "This disciple is of mediocre talent and unfamiliar with literary style. I beg you, Master, by imperial decree, to revise and correct, and I also wish you to edit according to the text." I declined, citing my own lack of skill. Seasons passed, and in the course of time, this gentleman passed away. Sighing with grief and streaming with tears, I thought of what kind of person he was. Fearing also that his great kindness would be set aside and that the palm-leaf text would be departed from, I requested the śramaṇa Daocheng and nine others to ask the Indian Dharma Master to again examine the profound intent, so that the text would not be marred by taboo-avoidance, honoring the imperial concern above while fully conveying the local idiom below for fluent circulation. Therefore, I composed a detailed memorial setting forth the entire story from beginning to end, so that readers who browse it will harbor no suspicion about this teaching. The date was the twenty-third day of the fifth month of the first year of Yongchun [682 CE].

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the kingdom of Śrāvastī, in the Jeta Grove, the park of Anāthapiṇḍada, together with a great assembly of eight thousand bhikṣus. There were also bodhisattva-mahāsattvas who had all attained the torch of right wisdom, illuminating all dharmas without any obstruction. Their names were: Mañjuśrī Bodhisattva, Padmottama-garbha Bodhisattva, Sarva-nīvaraṇa-viṣkambhin Bodhisattva, Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva, Mahāsthāmaprāpta Bodhisattva, Vajrapāṇi Bodhisattva, Ākāśagarbha Bodhisattva, Samantabhadra Bodhisattva, Maitreya Bodhisattva, and Dharaṇīṃdhara Bodhisattva-Mahāsattva. Such were the foremost, thirty-two thousand persons in all.

There were also ten thousand Brahma-kings, with Subrahmā and others, each coming from other worlds to the Buddha's place.

There were also twelve thousand hosts of devas, together with immeasurable devas, nāgas, and the eight divisions, humans and non-humans, all coming to the Buddha's place.

At that time, the World-Honored One, surrounded by the four assemblies, honored and revered with offerings, was teaching the Dharma. At that time, among the hosts of Śakra-devas, there was a deva named Supratiṣṭhita who, together with celestial women in a great jeweled palace, was heedlessly sporting and gratifying his desires in pleasure. In the night, a voice called to Supratiṣṭhita: "After seven days, you will certainly die. In Jambudvīpa you will undergo birth and death seven times. After that, you will again enter hell. Upon emerging from hell, you will obtain a human body, but you will be blind from birth, poor, and fully subject to all manner of suffering." Supratiṣṭhita, hearing this, was startled and terrified. His hair stood on end, and he trembled with unease. He rushed to the place of Śakra, reported what he had heard, and begged to be saved. Śakra, hearing Supratiṣṭhita's words, was startled and grieved. Silently he thought: "Seven lives? Born as what?" Having thus reflected, he immediately saw the signs of Supratiṣṭhita's death manifest before him: he would receive a pig's body; after the pig, a dog's body; then in succession a fox, a monkey, a poisonous snake, a crow, and a vulture. Through these seven lives he would constantly eat filth and refuse. Śakra, seeing this, deeply pitied Supratiṣṭhita, who must undergo such suffering. He thought: "I myself have no power to help. Only the Buddha, the World-Honored One, can save him."

Thereupon Śakra, in the latter part of the night, brought various flower-garlands, garments, necklaces, and various kinds of incense to the kingdom of Śrāvastī, made offerings to the World-Honored One, bowed at the Buddha's feet, stood to one side, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! In the palace where I dwell, there is a celestial prince named Supratiṣṭhita who has been indulging in heedless pleasures and roaming at will. Suddenly, in the night, a voice called out and said to him: 'In seven days you will certainly die. In Jambudvīpa you will undergo seven evil rebirths, and in each place of birth you will always eat filth and refuse. After that, you will again enter hell. Upon emerging from hell, you will be born as a human, blind, poor, and fully subject to all manner of suffering.' He came to me begging for rescue, but I have no power to help. I thought: only the World-Honored One can save him."

At that time, the World-Honored One, having heard Śakra's petition in full, emitted from his crown various multicolored rays of radiance that streamed forth and illuminated all the worlds of the ten directions. The radiance returned to the World-Honored One's place, circumambulated three times to the right, and entered through the Buddha's mouth. The Buddha then smiled gently and told Śakra: "I have a purifying-all-destinies consecration supreme great dhāraṇī that can fulfill your wish, save him from that suffering, and also greatly benefit all sentient beings. Lord of the Devas! The power of my divine mantra can extinguish all karmic obstructions, the various sufferings of hell, the animal realm, and the realm of Yama, and can moreover shatter the path to hell and other evil ways, causing sentient beings to set forth on the path of right awakening. Lord of the Devas! If anyone can even briefly hear it, all karmic obstructions, the sufferings of birth and death, will be entirely extinguished. One will obtain wholesome benefit and the knowledge of past lives. From one Buddha-land one will be born in another Buddha-land; from one heaven one will be born in another heaven. Wherever one is born, one will always know one's past lives. If anyone studies and practices this sūtra-mantra, within one's present hundred-year span, one's life will be further extended. Body, speech, and mind will become purified, and one will be at peace and bliss. All the Buddhas will gaze upon one, all the devas will guard and protect one, and all the bodhisattvas will hold one in compassionate affection. If one constantly reads and recites this sūtra-mantra, the sufferings of the three evil paths will cease and no longer operate. The gates of merit of all the Buddha-lands and all the celestial realms will be opened, guiding one to enter."

At that time, Śakra, hearing the Buddha praise this dhāraṇī, addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! May the Tathāgata, in pity and protection of all sentient beings, please teach the purifying-all-destinies supreme great Buddha-Uṣṇīṣa Dhāraṇī."

At that time, the World-Honored One, accepting Indra's request, spoke the dhāraṇī:

"*Namo namas-tasmad bhagavate trailokya-prativiśiṣṭāya saṃbuddhāya mahā-munaye tadyathā oṃ hūṃ viśudhaye samā-samanta-avabhāsa-saspharaṇa gatigahana sarvāvabhāva-śuddhe abhiṣiñca-ma sugata-vara-vacanā amṛta-viśeke āhara āhara āyuḥ-sandhāraṇi viśudhaye viśudhaye sarva-karma-āvaraṇa-nirmūla-vara gagana-viśuddhe uṣṇīṣa-vara-haratnā-vijaya dharma-dhātu-suviśuddhe sahasra-raśmi saṃjudite maṇi mahā-maṇi suc-inta maṇi vara sarva-tathāgata-adhiṣṭhāna-adhiṣṭhita mahā-mudrī vajra-kāya-saṃhata-na-suśuddhe sarva-āvaraṇa-viśuddhe pratibhānibhatāya āyu-śuddhe samaya-adhiṣṭhite maṇi maṇi tathata-bhūta-koṭi-pariśuddhe visphuta-buddhi-śuddhe jaya jaya vijaya vijaya vijaya smara smara sarva-buddha-adhiṣṭhita-śuddhe vajrī vajra-garbhe vajraṃ bhavatu mama sarva-sattvānāṃ kāya-viśuddhe sarva-gati-pariśuddhe sarva-tathāgata-samāśvāsa-adhiṣṭhite buddhaya buddhaya bodhaye bodhaye samanta-māra-dharma-dhātu-pariśuddhe sarva-tathāgata-adhiṣṭhāna-adhiṣṭhite svāhā*"

The Buddha told Indra: "This dhāraṇī was proclaimed together by eighty-eight koṭis of hundreds of thousands of Ganges-sands of Buddhas and World-Honored Ones. They guard, protect, and praise it, and have sealed it with the wisdom-seal. All these Buddhas, for the sake of liberating those submerged in the ocean of suffering in hell, the animal realm, and the realm of Yama, and their various poisonous sufferings; and for the sake of benefiting all beings of short life-span, scant merit, lowly station, base birth, and evil karma; and for the sake of bringing comfort to all beings heading toward the evil paths who die prematurely and whose minds are confused and for all their suffering and affliction: for these reasons, the Buddhas taught this supreme great dhāraṇī. Lord of the Devas! You should bestow this great dhāraṇī upon the deva Supratiṣṭhita and cause him to practice it. You should also proclaim and teach it for all the celestial princes, the devas, and all sentient beings of Jambudvīpa, causing those beings to receive, uphold, read, recite, make offerings, revere, honor, and praise it. Lord of the Devas! I now entrust this Dharma dhāraṇī-seal to you. You should uphold it respectfully. One who upholds this mantra can entirely extinguish all transgressive karma of hundreds of thousands of kalpas. Wherever one is born, one will constantly encounter the Buddhas, and ultimately attain unsurpassed bodhi. Lord of the Devas! This Dharma-seal great dhāraṇī of mine possesses great auspiciousness. Like a sun-treasury jewel, wherever it abides there is the shining of radiance. It is also compared to Jāmbūnada gold, without defilement or flaw, unstained by dust. If anyone can read, recite, write, uphold, recollect, practice, and make offerings to it, they will obtain all the merits described above. Lord of the Devas! If anyone writes this great dhāraṇī, places it upon a high banner-pole, sets it on a high mountain, a high rooftop, a high place, or in a high stūpa, and causes the four assemblies far and near who see it to also obtain all the merits described above; or if the shadow of the banner covers their body, or the wind from the banner blows upon them, or dust from the waving banner settles upon their body, they also obtain all the merits described above. Lord of the Devas! If any of the four assemblies, at a crossroads, build a stūpa-shrine and enshrine this great dhāraṇī, and daily make offerings with various flower-garlands, silken pennants, banners, parasols, necklaces, and incense, and are able to circumambulate, revere, and bow: one should know that such persons are great bodhisattvas, true children of the Buddha, bridges of the Dharma, and stūpas of the relics of all the Buddhas."

At that time, Yama Dharma-rāja, having heard of this matter, as the night was ending toward dawn, brought various multicolored jeweled flowers, various incenses, jeweled garments, and necklaces to the Buddha's place. He bowed at the Buddha's feet, circumambulated seven times to the right, stood to one side, and with these offerings honored the Tathāgata. Having made the offerings, he addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! Now, to repay the Buddha's grace, I will constantly and diligently serve this great dhāraṇī of great spiritual power and great meritorious fruit, which protects all, closes the gates of hell, and purifies all destinies."

At that time, the Four Heavenly Kings rose from their seats, circumambulated the Buddha three times, and addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! We pray you will teach us the method of practicing this dhāraṇī."

The Buddha told the Four Heavenly Kings: "Listen carefully! Listen carefully! Reflect upon it well! I will now teach you and all sentient beings the method of practicing this dhāraṇī. If anyone has a short life-span and seeks longevity, or has illness and suffering and seeks to be cured, or has evil karma and should fall into hell, the hungry-ghost realm, the animal realm, or other unwholesome destinies: all such persons should, on the fifteenth day of the month, bathe clean, put on fresh, pure garments, and also observe the eight precepts and fast. Then recite this great dhāraṇī one thousand times in full. One will obtain peace and happiness, one's life-span will be extended, all illness and suffering will be cured, and all evil karmic retributions will be entirely extinguished without remainder. If anyone merely hears this mantra with the ear, at the end of their life-span they will proceed to the land of Sukhāvatī, be transformed from a lotus, and always know their past lives. If anyone, dragged by evil karma, has already died, one should also recite this dhāraṇī mantra twenty-one times, empower earth with the mantra, and scatter it upon the corpse. Wherever that person has been reborn, they will shed their body of suffering and be born in heaven to enjoy happiness. If one can recite this supreme great dhāraṇī sūtra twenty-one times daily, one will also obtain all the merits described above. In life after life, one will constantly abide in the pure Buddha-lands, creating great radiance in all the Buddha-lands, constantly being with the Buddha, receiving the Buddha's prophecy, and ultimately attaining great parinirvāṇa. If anyone, within this Dharma-gate, seeks any of the above heart-wishes, one should prepare an altar, with sides of equal length, scatter various flowers within, burn various famous incenses, and with single-minded attention universally recollect the Buddhas of the ten directions. Place the right knee on the ground, kneel upright with palms joined, bend the two index fingers and press them against the two thumbs, hold them at the heart, say 'excellent!' and then recite this dhāraṇī mantra. If anyone can recite and uphold this mantra, completing one hundred and eight times daily in full, this constitutes making offerings to the Ganges-sands of Buddhas and World-Honored Ones as described above, and also constitutes making offerings to you, the four kings. One should know that such a person is praised as excellent by all. With great bodhi they adorn their mind and obtain unobstructed wisdom. They are a true child of the Buddha."

At that time, the World-Honored One, having spoken these words, told Indra: "Lord of the Devas! You may bestow this dhāraṇī Dharma upon Supratiṣṭhita. After seven full days, you may bring him to my place."

Thereupon Śakra, having received the Buddha's teaching and command, returned to his celestial palace, summoned Supratiṣṭhita, and bestowed upon him the divine mantra. Supratiṣṭhita, having heard it, practiced with reverence and made offerings, carrying out the teaching as instructed. By the seventh day, all his evil karma was entirely extinguished. In his own celestial realm, his life-span was extended. With joy and exultation, he cried out in a loud voice: "Wondrous, the Buddha! Wondrous, the Dharma! Wondrous, the Saṃgha! Wondrous, the power of the supreme dhāraṇī-seal! It has extinguished all suffering and calamity, and freed me from all this evil karma." When the deva Supratiṣṭhita had witnessed these events, he went to Śakra and said: "By the grace of the Lord of Heaven, you petitioned the Buddha on my behalf. The Buddha taught me the great dhāraṇī, causing all my evil karma to be entirely extinguished. I wish to go and thank the World-Honored One for his grace. May the Heavenly King accompany me."

Thereupon Śakra, leading his retinue and together with Supratiṣṭhita, brought various incenses, flowers, and wondrous necklaces. Riding a celestial jeweled chariot, they came to the Buddha's place. With these offerings they honored the Tathāgata, circumambulated hundreds of thousands of times, and stood to one side, singing the Buddha's virtues, which words could not fully express. At that time, the World-Honored One extended his golden hand to comfort Supratiṣṭhita, and with a gentle and elegant voice, bestowed upon him the prophecy of Buddhahood. The deva Supratiṣṭhita, filled with joy, sat back to hear the essential Dharma taught by the Tathāgata. Having heard the teaching, his heart surged with exultation. Together with Śakra, he bowed to the Buddha and withdrew.

## Dhāraṇī of the Great Wheel Vajra

*Namaḥ sattriya-divikānāṃ tathāgatānāṃ oṃ virāji virāji mahā-cakra-vajri sata sata sārate sārate trayi trayi vidhamani saṃbhañjani tramāti siddha agriyataṃ svāhā*

Reciting this dhāraṇī twenty-one times, one may enter any maṇḍala, and all undertakings will be accomplished.

## Mantra of Sūryaprabha Bodhisattva

*Namo buddha-guṇa-me namo dharma-mahā-te namo saṃgha-tāyanī dhī-rī-bhū-pī satva-taṃ namaḥ*

*Oṃ mārīciye sarva-satva-ādhāri-śālī svāhā*

Reciting this mantra provides extreme protection for one's person. One should recite it one hundred and eight times at each of the three periods of the day.
