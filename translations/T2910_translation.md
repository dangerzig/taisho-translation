# Gold-Possessing Dhāraṇī Sūtra

## *Jīnyǒu Tuóluóní Jīng* (金有陀羅尼經)

Taishō 2910

Translator unknown. A dhāraṇī text.

*Note: This is a protective dhāraṇī sūtra in which the Buddha teaches Śakra (Indra), lord of the gods, a powerful spell to counter the illusion-magic, secret spells, and poisons of the asuras in battle. The text uses the unusual translation "Hundred-Sacrifice" (Bǎishī, 百施) for Śakra's epithet Śatakratu. It survives as a Dunhuang manuscript and includes detailed instructions for practical applications of the spell: tying knotted threads, sprinkling consecrated water, scattering graveyard earth, and chewing certain herbs.*

Thus have I heard. At one time the Bhagavān was dwelling at a bamboo enclosure, together with the yakṣa Vajrapāṇi (*Jīngāng Shǒu*, 金剛手).

At that time the heavenly lord Śatakratu (*Bǎishī*, 百施) went to where the World-Honored One was. Having arrived, he bowed his head at the Buddha's feet, withdrew, and sat to one side. The heavenly lord Śatakratu addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One, when I enter the battlefield and do battle, through the illusion-magic, secret spells, and medicinal powers of the asuras, I have been brought to defeat. I humbly pray that the World-Honored One will compassionately have pity on me and, in order to crush and subdue the illusion-magic, secret spells, and medicinal powers of the asura hosts, graciously speak the supreme great secret spell."

Then the Bhagavān addressed the heavenly lord Śatakratu: "Kauśika, it is so, it is so. When you do battle with the asuras, you truly are brought to defeat by their vidyā spells, secret spells, and medicinal powers. Kauśika, out of compassion I shall now speak the vidyā spell that causes illusion-magic spells to disperse and retreat, battles and disputes all to be extinguished, and all secret spells and medicines to be severed and eliminated."

Then the Bhagavān spoke the great Gold-Possessing vidyā spell: "I now speak of the three incalculable kalpas. All other external-path practitioners, wandering ascetics with exposed bodies, who harbor evil thoughts and create various obstacles: from among all of these, whatever illusion-magic and vidyā spells exist, I can subdue them all. The six pāramitās are complete. I sever and eliminate the afflictions caused by all other external-path practitioners and wandering ascetics. Through vidyā spells, secret spells, medicines, and the great vidyā of all the assembled māras: Kauśika, you should, for the sake of gathering all sentient beings, receive and uphold this supreme great secret spell."

The heavenly lord said: "So it is, World-Honored One. I humbly receive your teaching."

Then the World-Honored One spoke the Gold-Possessing great vidyā spell:

*Tadyathā. Oṃ. Hini hini. Hiri hiri. Miṅgiri miṅgiri. Himiṅgiri. Nihi nihi nihirā. Bini. Kañcana paṭi. Bhuṭi. Dhamantra adhikṛti. Bheṭi bheṭi bheṭi. Dhamantra adhikaraṭi. Hana hana. Hava hava. Chinda chinda. Bhinda bhinda. Bhagavatī. Caye vicaye. Saṃbhani. Siddhambhani. Bandhani. Muhani. Amogha śakti dhāraṇī. Kṛṇa kṛṇa. Kaṭana. Manasaṃbhavaya. Saṃbhavaya. Bandhaya bandhaya. Siddhambhavaya siddhambhavaya. Bandhaya bandhaya. Muhaya muhaya.*

Whatever illusion-magic exists: whether of devas, whether of nāgas, whether of yakṣas, whether of rākṣasas, whether of kiṃnaras, whether of gandharvas, whether of asuras, whether of mahoragas, whether of great serpents, whether of vidyā-holders, whether of vidyā-adept kings, whether of ṛṣis, whether of all vidyā-holders, whether of any beings, whether any illusion-magic whatsoever:

*Rara rara. Racaya racaya. Dhuma dhuma. Dhudhuma. Rabha rabha. Rabhanacakra. Laṃ ḍaṃkara. Laṃ dhanī. Hana hana. Sarva vaitāśa. Dhurunān siddhambhavaya. Śūyān siddhambhavaya. Sunān siddhambhavaya. Vārunān siddhambhavaya. Agniān siddhambhavaya. Utkaṃtarī. Dharī dharī. Dhāraṭī. Vidhāranī. Paśa. Hastibhe. Śa dhāyā. Vaśena.* If anyone is able to be my enemy, a hostile thief, wrathful, harboring an evil mind, engaged in conflict and extreme dispute, wishing to do anything harmful: *Hana hana. Daha daha. Paca paca. Bañcaya bañcaya. Saṃbhavaya saṃbhavaya. Siddhambhavaya siddhambhavaya. Bandhaya bandhaya. Muhaya muhaya. Mahāmuhanī. Bhagavatī svāhā.* For all terrors, afflictions, and epidemics, may you protect me! *Gaṃdhā svāhā.*

"Kauśika, if good men or good women, if kings or royal ministers, are able to recollect this Gold-Possessing vidyā spell, they shall have no fear from others. The armies of their adversaries shall be unable to afflict them. Neither devas, nor nāgas, nor yakṣas, nor gandharvas, nor asuras, nor kiṃnaras, nor mahoragas, nor vidyā-holders, nor sky-flying mothers shall be able to cause untimely death. Vidyā spells, secret spells, and all medicines shall be unable to cause harm. The armies of adversaries shall be unable to surround them. The armies of adversaries shall not take their lives. Blades cannot harm them. Water, fire, poison, vidyā spells, secret spells, and all medicines shall be unable to assail them. These shall revert to the perpetrators themselves, to those who committed, instructed, or rejoiced in the offenses, returning to their own place.

"Kauśika, therefore, faithful bhikṣus, bhikṣuṇīs, upāsakas, upāsikās, good men, and good women who consecrate water by reciting this vidyā spell seven times and wash their own bodies with it shall be protected in body. If anyone wishes to transcend all terrors, all afflictions, all epidemics, all vidyā spells, all secret spells, all medicines, and all sorcery, they should recollect this Gold-Possessing vidyā spell.

"If kings or royal ministers wish to crush the armies of adversaries and subdue the armies of adversaries, they too should recollect this Gold-Possessing vidyā spell.

"If one consecrates a thread seven times and ties seven knots, then fastens it upon the body; if one consecrates water seven times and thereby protects oneself; if one writes out the dhāraṇī of fearlessness in all terrors, or can receive and uphold it, or ties it below the knee, or places it upon a high banner and enters an army encampment: one shall safely escape. By the awesome spiritual power of this gold spell, one's inner clan and family shall safely pass through.

"What has not been accomplished shall be accomplished. If one wishes to crush and subdue vidyā-holders, one consecrates a white thread seven times and ties seven knots: this can bind and subdue them. If one wishes to crush illusion-magic, one takes graveyard earth, consecrates it seven times, and scatters it: this can crush illusion-magic. In a debate, if one wishes to silence the opponent's mouth, one takes thorny bramble, consecrates it seven times, and chews it: one shall be able to answer all arguments.

"Those who receive, uphold, read, recite, and praise this spell: all their sins shall be entirely extinguished, reverting to the perpetrators and to the place where they conceived the deed. If one ties a cord or uses consecrated water for self-protection, upon that person's body all vidyā spells, secret spells, and medicines shall be unable to cause harm. What has not been accomplished shall all be accomplished. Whatever is sought shall all be granted."

When the Bhagavān had spoken this sūtra, the heavenly lord Śatakratu, having heard what the Buddha spoke, faithfully received it and put it into practice.
