# 地藏菩薩十齋日
## The Ten Fasting Days of Kṣitigarbha Bodhisattva

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 2850

Translated from the Chinese. Translator unknown (失譯)

---

On the first day, the Divine Child descends. Contemplate Dīpaṃkara Tathāgata. One will not enter the Hell of Knives and Spears. Observing the fast removes sins of forty kalpas.

On the eighth day, the Divine Prince descends. Contemplate Bhaiṣajyaguru Vaiḍūryaprabhā Buddha. One will not enter the Hell of Filth and Excrement. Observing the fast removes sins of thirty kalpas.

On the fourteenth day, the Life-Inspector descends. Contemplate the Thousand Buddhas of the Bhadrakalpa. One will not enter the Hell of Boiling Cauldrons. Observing the fast removes sins of one thousand kalpas.

On the fifteenth day, the Great General of the Five Paths descends. Contemplate Amitābha Buddha. One will not fall into the Hell of Cold Ice. Observing the fast removes sins of two hundred kalpas.

On the eighteenth day, Yama Rāja descends. Contemplate Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva. One will not fall into the Hell of Sword-Trees. Observing the fast removes sins of ninety kalpas.

On the twenty-third day, the Great General descends. Contemplate Vairocana Buddha. One will not fall into the Hell of Hungry Ghosts. Observing the fast removes sins of one thousand kalpas.

On the twenty-fourth day, the Lord of Mount Tai descends. Contemplate Kṣitigarbha, the Bodhisattva of Hell. One will not fall into the Hell of Chopping and Hacking. Observing the fast removes sins of one thousand kalpas.

On the twenty-eighth day, Śakra descends. Contemplate Amitābha Buddha. One will not fall into the Hell of Iron Saws. Observing the fast removes sins of ninety kalpas.

On the twenty-ninth day, the Four Heavenly Kings descend. Contemplate Bhaiṣajyaguru, the Supreme Bodhisattva. One will not fall into the Hell of the Grinding Millstone. Observing the fast removes sins of seven thousand kalpas.

On the thirtieth day, King Brahmā descends. Contemplate Śākyamuni Buddha. One will not fall into the Hell of Ashes. Observing the fast removes sins of eight thousand kalpas.

---

**Translator's notes:**

- The first three entries use 不塗 ("will not be immersed in") while the remaining seven use 不墮 ("will not fall into"); both are rendered uniformly as "will not fall into/enter" for readability.
- Day 24's 地獄菩薩 ("Bodhisattva of Hell") is rendered as Kṣitigarbha, consistent with the text's title and Kṣitigarbha's canonical role as protector of beings in the hell realms.
- Day 29's 藥師上菩薩 may be read as "Bhaiṣajyaguru, the Supreme Bodhisattva," or alternatively as Bhaiṣajyasamudgata Bodhisattva (藥上菩薩), a distinct figure paired with Bhaiṣajyarāja in the *Guānyào-wáng guānyào-shàng* sūtra tradition.
- Day 30's 灰何地獄 likely represents a scribal variant of 灰河地獄, the "Hell of the Ash River," a standard torment realm in Chinese Buddhist hell taxonomies.
