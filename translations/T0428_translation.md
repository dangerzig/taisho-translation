# 佛說八陽神呪經
## Sūtra of the Eight Radiances' Divine Dhāraṇī Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0428

Translated from the Chinese. Western Jin dynasty, translated by Dharmarakṣa (竺法護)

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time, the Buddha was in the city of Rājagṛha, on Vulture Peak Mountain, together with one thousand two hundred and fifty great bhikṣus and one thousand bodhisattvas, all of the rank of Maitreya Bodhisattva and his peers.

The Buddha told Śāriputra: "In the east, beyond a distance of one Ganges-sand of buddha-lands, there is a Buddha named Joyful Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, teaching the Dharma. His land is called Unsurpassable.

"Śāriputra! In the east, beyond a distance of two Ganges-sands, there is a Buddha named Moon-Glory Banner King Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, teaching the Dharma. His land is called Joyful Happiness.

"Śāriputra! In the east, beyond a distance of three Ganges-sands, there is a Buddha named Equal Pervasive Radiance Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, teaching the Dharma. His land is called Beloved Delight.

"Śāriputra! In the east, beyond a distance of four Ganges-sands, there is a Buddha named Distinguishing Surpassing Purity Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, teaching the Dharma. His land is called Inner Delight.

"Śāriputra! In the east, beyond a distance of five Ganges-sands, there is a Buddha named Equal Merit Radiance Chief Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, teaching the Dharma. His land is called Without Doubt.

"Śāriputra! In the east, beyond a distance of six Ganges-sands, there is a Buddha named Fundamental Medicine Tree Chief Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, teaching the Dharma. His land is called Without Venom.

"Śāriputra! In the east, beyond a distance of seven Ganges-sands, there is a Buddha named Surpassing Precious Lotus Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, teaching the Dharma. His land is called Lotus Fragrance.

"Śāriputra! In the east, beyond a distance of eight Ganges-sands, there is a Buddha named Precious Joyful Lotus Happily Abiding Tree King Tathāgata, Arhat, Samyaksaṃbuddha, who is now present, teaching the Dharma. His land is called Sweet Sound Proclaimed.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra! The lands of these Buddhas, Tathāgatas, are pure. In those lands there are no five turbidities, no craving and desire, no women, no mental defilements, no sorcery, and no discrimination.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra! These Buddhas, Tathāgatas: if there are good men and good women who hear the names of these eight Buddhas, receive them, uphold them, recite them, and practice accordingly, they will never fall into the three evil paths, and the five sins without interval will be removed.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra! If such good men and good women uphold the names of these eight Buddhas and their lands, receive them, recite them, and practice accordingly, then by this merit, if they have given rise to the bodhisattva aspiration, wherever they are born they will always encounter dhāraṇī, always encounter auspicious marks, always encounter auspicious sounds, and always encounter the blessing of rightward turning. Such good men and good women who uphold the teachings of these eight Buddhas and practice accordingly: women, wherever they are born, will be transformed into men.

"Furthermore, Śāriputra! These Buddhas, Tathāgatas: if such good men and good women, at dawn, bathe and rinse their mouths with pure water, straighten their garments, and read and recite this sūtra three times during the day and three times at night, they will attain immeasurable merit, and foremost the four heavenly kings will always protect them. If such good men and good women are before the magistrates, they should recite this sūtra. If among enemies, they should recite this sūtra. If among thieves, they should recite this sūtra. If amid water or fire, they should recite this sūtra. If on the sea encountering wind and waves and terror, they should recite this sūtra. If in the midst of armies and battle, they should recite this sūtra. If struck by sorcery or poison, they should recite this sūtra. If they hear evil bird-omens or have nightmares, they should recite this sūtra. If afflicted by nāgas or spirits, they should recite this sūtra. If troubled by māras with terror and the hair standing on end, they should recite this sūtra. If there is urgent distress, epidemic, or painful illness, they should recite this Sūtra of the Eight Radiances' Dhāraṇī, and they will immediately be healed."

At that time, the Buddha spoke in summary verse:

> In these lands, there are no Tathāgatas like these;
> Upholding these lands' names,
> All evils are eliminated,
> And one swiftly ascends to the correct path.

> Wherever born, one always meets the Buddhas;
> Seeing them, one awakens with great joy.
> Illuminating the World-Honored Ones above,
> With an even mind one serves and makes offerings.

> Through a hundred kalpas beyond reckoning,
> Attachments should always be abandoned.
> Swiftly attaining the path of nirvāṇa,
> One upholds these Buddhas' names.

> Now present, all these Tathāgatas:
> Practice and honor their bright teachings' names.
> Being plain, straightforward, and gentle,
> Wherever one is born,

> With complete and beautiful marks,
> One is born into families of immense wealth.
> Courageous and delighting in generosity,
> Without miserliness or greed.

> Women who hear this essential teaching,
> Leaping with great joy,
> Will shed the female form
> And wherever born become men.

> Soldiers dare not harm them;
> Sorcery and poison cannot affect them.
> Magistrates and thieves
> Will never harm such persons.

> The five māras cannot trouble them;
> Generals and officials
> Who uphold this sūtra:
> None can take advantage of them.

At that time, foremost the four heavenly kings, Maitreya Bodhisattva, and the others addressed the Buddha: "We shall together protect all who study and uphold this Sūtra of the Eight Radiances' Dhāraṇī, and with our combined power, cause all illnesses to be healed."

The Buddha said: "So it shall be."

Foremost the four heavenly kings, Maitreya Bodhisattva, all the bhikṣus, and all the devas, nāgas, ghosts, spirits, humans, and asura kings heard the sūtra and rejoiced. They bowed to the Buddha and departed.

The eight bodhisattvas' names:

The first is the Bodhisattva Bhadrapāla, the second is the Bodhisattva Ratnaketu, the third is the Bodhisattva Kiaujiḍu, the fourth is the Bodhisattva Nārada, the fifth is the Bodhisattva Suśīma, the sixth is the Bodhisattva Indradatta, the seventh is the Bodhisattva Mahāsusaṃvāsa, and the eighth is the Bodhisattva Harmonious Wheel. These eight persons have been seeking the path for immeasurable kalpas and have not yet attained Buddhahood.

These eight persons, when they were originally studying the path together, made a vow: "May all the people in the eight directions, above and below, all attain Buddhahood. If anyone is in urgent distress, they should call upon the names of us eight persons, and they will immediately be delivered. When their life is about to end, we eight shall fly to them and welcome them." May the merit of upholding these single names cause all people to be as joyful as the eight bodhisattvas.
