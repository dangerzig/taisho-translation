# 佛說稱讚如來功德神呪經
## The Buddha's Teaching: Sūtra of the Divine Mantra for Praising the Merits of the Tathāgatas

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1349

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Yijing (義淨).

---

Thus have I heard:

At one time the Bhagavat was in Rājagṛha, on Vulture Peak, together with a great assembly of twelve hundred and fifty bhikṣus and twelve thousand bodhisattva mahāsattvas, all great bodhisattvas of the Fortunate Kalpa. At that time the World-Honored One, within this assembly, addressed Maitreya Bodhisattva Mahāsattva: "The supremely wondrous and auspicious names of all the buddhas and tathāgatas presently in the ten directions: if any good man or good woman remembers and upholds these tathāgata-names, single-mindedly and reverently, all their karmic obstacles, retributive obstacles, and grave sins of breaking the precepts will be entirely eliminated. The supremely wondrous wholesome roots, true merits, and all the merits of exhortation, rejoicing, dedication, and vow-making will ensure that they never regress from the mind of awakening. I shall now teach this; you should listen carefully.

"Homage to Vimalaprabha Tathāgata of the East.

"Homage to Samantavyūha-Pratibhāna Tathāgata of the Southeast.

"Homage to Nirmala-Candra-Dhvaja-Ketu-Rāja Tathāgata of the South.

"Homage to Raśmi-Vyūha Tathāgata of the Southwest.

"Homage to Ratnaśreṣṭha Tathāgata of the West.

"Homage to Kumāra-Prabha Tathāgata of the Northwest.

"Homage to Abhaya-Vimala-Yaśas Tathāgata of the North.

"Homage to Vigata-Bhaya-Trāsa-Mahāyaśas Tathāgata of the Northeast.

"Homage to Siṃhavikrāntamati Tathāgata of the Zenith.

"Homage to Suvarṇa-Puṣpa-Prabha Tathāgata of the Nadir."

At that time the World-Honored One further addressed Maitreya Bodhisattva: "East of here, beyond ten inexpressible hundreds of thousands of koṭis of dust-mote buddha-lands, there is a buddha-world called Wondrous True Pearl. In that land there is a buddha whose name is Ākāśa-Guṇa-Viśuddha-Nayana-Vimalaprabha-Guṇa-Lakṣaṇa-Padmajvāla-Vaiḍūrya-Varṇa-Ratna-Kāya-Gandha-Agra-Pūjā-Nānā-Citrālaṃkāra-Uṣṇīṣa-Sūkṣma-Lakṣaṇa-Ananta-Candrāditya-Prabha-Praṇidhāna-Vyūha-Vikurvāṇa-Vyūha-Vipula-Vyūha-Dharmadhātu-Uttama-Asaṃkliṣṭa-Ratnarāja Samyak-saṃbuddha. He has given the prediction of awakening to a peerless bodhisattva: after that buddha's parinirvāṇa, that bodhisattva will next become a buddha named Ūrṇā-Lakṣaṇa-Viśiṣṭa-Prathamāditya-Dīpaprabha-Candrajvāla-Padma-Kāya-Suvarṇavarṇa-Ākāśadhātu-Pūrṇa-Prabha-Vipula-Anāvaraṇa-Vyūha-Maṇḍala-Daśadig-Samantāvabhāsa-Sarvajña-Dhvaja-Ketu-Rāja Samyak-saṃbuddha."

The Buddha said: "Maitreya! You should know that if any good man or good woman is able to receive and uphold these buddha-names, to bow in worship, and to single-mindedly revere them; and for ten days confesses their sins, furthermore making exhortation, rejoicing, dedication, and vows with the supremely wondrous wholesome roots: all their karmic obstacles, retributive obstacles, and grave sins of breaking the precepts will be entirely eliminated. Although they remain in saṃsāra and have not yet escaped transmigration, they will not regress from the mind seeking awakening. In every place of birth, their lineage will be noble and honored. They will be far from poverty, borderlands, and lowliness. Their six faculties will be complete, and they will be loved and respected by all, incomparably handsome. They will constantly encounter good friends and never meet evil persons. Whatever wishes they may have will all be granted according to their will. Wealth, treasure, honor, and rank will all accord with their hearts. After death they will be born in the buddha-lands." Having first performed these rites of worship and confession, one should then single-mindedly recite this dhāraṇī. He then spoke the mantra:

*Tadyathā: akhe makhe samanta-mukhe saubhe yukti-dhāni samā-yogi citta vibhakta akhe mukhe sata-dhāni vivede samanta-guṇe sata-hāra-me mukte pari-mukti hiri miri mu-sa-rī a-samī daṃ-mī a-jī-rī ma-jī-rī bodhi-hara-ṇi bhāhu-ce māyu-ga-rā hi-sindhi-bhaṃ-ke hima-vatī śu-ti-va-tī dharma-jiṃ-ti a-vi-kī-pī hāra di-kī-pī skaṃdha vi-bhak-tī svāhā.*

At that time the World-Honored One further spoke in verse:

> Sixty koṭis of great sages
> All receive and uphold this mantra.
> They constantly recite without distraction,
> Presently dwelling in the ten directions.
>
> In every life they are born,
> They constantly meet good spiritual friends.
> Whatever they hope and wish for
> Is granted according to their will.
>
> They see the buddhas face to face,
> Seated upon lotus thrones.
> Those who constantly uphold this mantra
> Are born by transformation endowed with the marks.
>
> Mindfulness and concentration are all secure;
> Wisdom grows brighter each day.
> All that they hear they can retain;
> The meaning of each phrase is not forgotten.
>
> If one upholds the names of the buddhas,
> This is the world's refuge.
> They will not fall into the evil destinies
> But will constantly be born in wholesome realms.
>
> Through the buddhas' power such persons
> Constantly obtain supreme stations.
> Until they realize awakening,
> They constantly cultivate the wondrous practices.
>
> Buddha-lands of many thousands of koṭis
> Filled with gold offered in giving:
> If a person upholds the buddhas' names,
> Their store of merit surpasses even that.
>
> Such persons deserve offerings.
> In every place of birth
> They are always born in wealthy, noble families,
> Brave, strong, generous and unstinting.
>
> All manner of fragrant scents,
> The celestial red sandalwood fragrance,
> For many hundreds of koṭis of kalpas
> Constantly issue from their mouths.
>
> If in a person's home
> The buddha-names are written and offerings made,
> Their family will always be at peace,
> Free from all disturbance and trouble.
>
> If a person constantly recites and upholds them,
> They will have long life and be free from illness.
> They will always meet good friends,
> And at death their mind will not be confused.
>
> The accumulated merits of the Tathāgatas,
> Their measure of blessings is inconceivable.
> The wise should recite and uphold them;
> Be diligent and do not be negligent.

At that time, when the Bhagavat had spoken this sūtra for Maitreya Bodhisattva and the entire great assembly, Maitreya Bodhisattva and all the rest, as well as the bhikṣus, devas, humans, and the great assembly, having heard what the Buddha taught, all greatly rejoiced, reverently received it, and put it into practice.
