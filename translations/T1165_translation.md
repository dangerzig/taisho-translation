# 聖持世陀羅尼經
## Holy Vasudhārā Dhāraṇī Sūtra

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 1165

Translated from the Chinese. Song dynasty, translated by Dānapāla (施護).

---

At that time, Vasudhārā Bodhisattva addressed the Buddha: "World-Honored One! If there are people who receive, uphold, read, and recite this dhāraṇī, how should they uphold it? We beseech the Buddha to explain."

At that time, the World-Honored One told Vasudhārā: "If there are people who, on the first day of the bright half of the first or second month, go to a river that flows directly into the sea and enter the water. When the water reaches the chest, face east toward the sun, stand with palms joined, and from sunrise until sunset, recite and uphold this mantra. Having come out of the water, abstain from food for seven days, reciting day and night, not speaking with anyone. Then paint the original form of Vasudhārā. Before applying colors, with a reverent mind, apply white sandalwood fragrant water to the silk and leave it overnight as an expression of heartfelt offering.

"Commission a painter and instruct them to observe the precepts and purification, to bathe and wear fresh garments, to arise reverent mind, and to color it in accordance with the dharma. The image should be ten cubits high and three cubits wide. The color is deep green. She is seated upon a lotus, adorned without measure, brilliantly colored and pure, radiant as the sun. Her right hand holds a fruit; her left hand is in the gesture of reassurance. Her countenance is joyful and serene.

"Before the seat, a nāga king holds a jewel-casket, pearls, jeweled vase, coral, agate, gold, silver, lapis lazuli, and various treasures, making offerings with a pure mind. Above, place the devas. The Great Auspicious Goddess of Jewel-Cloud Rain-Net holds a lotus, reassuring with the right hand. Auspicious Jewel-Palm stands attendant on the left, form and color full and perfect, adorned with necklaces, holding a lotus in the gesture of reassurance.

"At the base of Vasudhārā's image, where the relics are, place arghya fragrant water, various fine foods, flower garlands, and ghee-oil lamps as offerings. Also use fine flowers, ghee-oil lamps, fragrant incense, food and drink, and various offerings for the Piṇḍa and Yaga, that there may be no māra-disturbances. Having made the offerings, also use sandalwood, aloeswood, and various fine incenses, ghee, fragrant oil lamps, and limitless offerings.

"Facing the bodhisattva's image, with focused mind and single-pointed concentration, sit upright and recite. Beginning at the hour of the Tiger, enter the altar. For seven days, fourteen days, or even one day, at the time of sunrise, the aspired wish will certainly be answered and all will be accomplished."

At that time the World-Honored One told Vasudhārā: "If there are people who, at midnight, enter the water and recite this mantra a full eight hundred times, within that month they will obtain the fulfillment of their wishes."

The Buddha told Vasudhārā: "If there are people who, at midnight, recite and uphold Vasudhārā Bodhisattva's name in accordance with the dharma and with purity, within six months they will certainly obtain their wishes."

The Buddha told Vasudhārā: "If there are people who have hidden away possessions and later forgotten where, if they recite and uphold in accordance with the dharma, the possessions will certainly be recovered."

The Buddha told Vasudhārā: "If there are people who wish to attain a position of honor, let them enter the water by day and recite this mantra until midnight. Also use sesame seeds for homa, one grain per recitation, one grain per burning, for eight hundred recitations. They will certainly obtain great joy, and wealth and honor will be fulfilled."

The Buddha told Vasudhārā: "If there are people who use white mustard seeds mixed with ghee, one grain per recitation, one grain per burning, performing homa as before, the king will obtain joyful celebration, and the land will be free from calamity."

The Buddha told Vasudhārā: "If there are people who use fine frankincense mixed with ghee for homa, performing as before, this can pacify all calamities of the great ministers."

The Buddha told Vasudhārā: "If there are people who, at midnight, recite the bodhisattva's name with focused mind, maintaining the dharma and purity, within seven days they will obtain great wealth and joy, accomplished and complete."

The Buddha told Vasudhārā: "If there are people who, on the first day of the bright half of the month, begin the practice, observe the precepts and purification, place the bodhisattva's image at a reliquary, make offerings in accordance with the dharma, recollect, and recite the bodhisattva's name with focused mind and aspiration, the bodhisattva will certainly manifest a transformed body before them for their comfort, and they will attain non-retrogression and arise the bodhisattva's practice."

The Buddha told Vasudhārā: "If there are people who eat only vegetarian food, practice celibacy, abstain from alcohol and meat, and recite day and night, they will obtain great wealth and honor."

At that time, the World-Honored One, proclaiming this ritual manual called the root mantra, spoke the mantra before the assembly:

*Namo ratna-trayāya oṃ vasudhāri svāhā oṃ rakṣa-mi bhūtara-ṇi vāsinīye svāhā*

At that time, having spoken this mantra, the World-Honored One told Vasudhārā: "Regarding the seal method, it represents the mind-dharma and is also called the outer expression. When forming this seal, use both hands in añjali with the center hollow. Place the index fingers in fists between the palms. The ring fingers stand upright. The little fingers cross each other alternately. The middle fingers stand straight like needles. This seal form abides in the eight directions, each separate. This is the seal of Vasudhārā's ritual. Form this seal in accordance with the dharma and recite the vidyā-mantra. All wishes will be accomplished, broadly benefiting sentient beings."

At that time, the World-Honored One spoke the mantra:

*Oṃ śrīye śrīkāri-dhāna-kāri-dhānī-ya-kāri svāhā*

At that time, having spoken this mantra, the World-Honored One told Vasudhārā: "For this samādhi seal, one should first join the palms and bend the thumbs into the palms. This is called the Vasudhārā samādhi seal."

At that time the World-Honored One spoke the mantra:

*Oṃ samaye saumīye samaya-kāri mahā-samaye svāhā*

At that time the World-Honored One told Vasudhārā: "This samādhi seal and divine mantra are thus.

"Furthermore, Vasudhārā! One should bend the index and middle fingers at the third joint, with the thumb standing upright. This seal is named the Vasudhārā Bodhisattva's root seal. For summoning holy ones and sending off holy ones, this seal is always used."

The Buddha told Vasudhārā: "One should extend the index finger outward horizontally. This is called the flower seal." At that time the World-Honored One spoke the mantra:

*Oṃ vasudhi svāhā*

The Buddha told Vasudhārā: "This flower seal and mantra should be received and upheld thus."

At that time the World-Honored One further told Vasudhārā: "If there are people who extend both middle fingers horizontally, this is called the incense seal."

At that time the World-Honored One spoke the mantra:

*Oṃ vasudhāri svāhā*

At that time, having spoken this mantra, the World-Honored One told Vasudhārā: "If there are people who extend the ring finger outward, this is called the unguent seal." At that time the World-Honored One spoke the mantra:

*Oṃ vasumati-śrīye svāhā*

At that time, having spoken this mantra, the World-Honored One told Vasudhārā: "If there are people who press the little finger and thumb together, with the remaining three fingers extended like a three-pronged vajra, this is called the lamp seal."

At that time the World-Honored One spoke the mantra:

*Oṃ vasudhāraṇi svāhā*

At that time, having spoken this mantra, the World-Honored One told Vasudhārā: "If there are people who use both hands as if scooping water, with the thumbs crossing over past the index fingers, this is called the food seal."

At that time the World-Honored One spoke the mantra:

*Oṃ dhāraṇi dhāraṇi svāhā*

At that time, having spoken this mantra, the World-Honored One told Vasudhārā: "For all other offerings, use the root seal and empower with the mantra. For regular daily practice, empower with the regular mantra and seal.

"If there are people who paint an image of Vasudhārā Bodhisattva, they should use new silk, fine and flawless. According to the buddha-cubit measurement, it should be two chi long and one chi wide. Above the image, place a buddha. On the right, place an image of Avalokiteśvara. On the left, establish an image of Vajrapāṇi. From the buddha's center, place the Vasudhārā image, golden in color. The bodhisattva's right hand is in the wish-granting gesture, adorned with various pearls, lapis lazuli, and agate, all used as ornaments. Below on the right, place the reciter, right hand in prostration, left hand holding a crown.

"During the bright half of the month, paint the bodhisattva. Recite this mantra one hundred thousand times. Make offerings according to one's ability."

At that time, the World-Honored One told Vasudhārā: "If there are people about to plant fields, the reciter should bathe and wear fresh garments, observe the precepts and purification, and place the bodhisattva's image in the field. Also use unguent, flower garlands, necklaces, and fine food as reverent offerings. Recite the bodhisattva's name eight hundred times. Also, before the image, empower fragrant water with eight hundred recitations, fill a merit-vase, and place it before the image. Have the farmers and oxen with plows stand in rows. Sprinkle them with the water from the merit-vase, and likewise sprinkle the reciter. Have the reciter circumambulate the field and the farmers with oxen seven times, and then sow the seeds."

At that time, the World-Honored One told Vasudhārā: "If there are people about to harvest the fields, at the four corners of the land, install monks and have them observe the vegetarian precepts, with food provided properly. The reciter should bathe in clean garments, observe the precepts and purification. Also place the honored image in the field, and use flowers, incense, lamps, food, and various offerings. Recite the mantra with empowerment a full eight hundred times. Also, before the image, fill a vase with fragrant water and empower it with eight hundred recitations. Place the bodhisattva's honored image facing east. Offer unguent, ghee-oil, flower garlands, and food with a pure mind. The grain harvest will multiply, and calamities will be avoided."

At that time, the World-Honored One told Vasudhārā: "If there are people who, at the four corners of the grain pile, station four young girls wearing fresh clean garments, eating vegetarian food and observing the precepts, and recite this mantra with empowerment, the grain will also increase and calamities will not intrude.

"Furthermore, Vasudhārā! When measuring the grain, that person should recollect the bodhisattva's honored name. If the reciter does not speak aloud but silently concentrates, by the time the measuring is complete, the grain will also have doubled."

The Buddha told Vasudhārā: "If there are people whose fields have just ripened, they should first make offerings to the Buddha and the holy assembly. The increase in merit is immeasurable and cannot be fully praised."

At that time, the World-Honored One told Vasudhārā: "If there are people who, at the time of storing the harvest, first draw two short figures inside the granary, naked, big-bellied, holding grain-baskets in the posture of pouring grain, the grain golden in color. Set out various items inside the granary. Then burn fine incenses inside the granary, along with flowers, arghya, fragrant water, parasols, banners, pennants, and various offerings. Also place the bodhisattva inside the granary. Also sprinkle cow's milk inside the granary. Recite the true word. Remove all the items, and then bring in the wealth and grain. It will be auspicious and free from trouble.

"Furthermore, Vasudhārā! In this storehouse, set out fresh food, offer fragrant flowers, and receive, uphold, read, and recite this dhāraṇī. Calamities will be avoided. Furthermore, Vasudhārā! If there are people who write the Vasudhārā Dhāraṇī Sūtra on colored silk, perfume it with incense, read, recite, and place it upon the crown of the head, their merit will increase without measure, and calamities will be extinguished."

The Buddha told Vasudhārā: "If a person wishes to recite, they should first purify the mind and recite this dhāraṇī eight hundred times, three sessions daily. Their wishes will certainly be answered, and the bodhisattva will grant protection.

"Furthermore, Vasudhārā! If there are people who seek various wishes, in their own dwelling, toward the northeast corner, they should set up an image of the Holy Vasudhārā Bodhisattva, painted on fine colored silk in accordance with the dharma. When the work is complete, place it in a clean location and with focused mind make offerings of arghya, fragrant water, unguent, incense, sandalwood, aloeswood, and various incenses. Also use five treasures, five medicines, and five grains. Cover the arghya vase with silk and do not open it. Recite this dhāraṇī in accordance with the dharma. All wishes will be completely fulfilled, possessions will increase, and merit will be obtained without measure."

At that time, the World-Honored One further told Vasudhārā: "If there are sentient beings who wish to build a storehouse, of whatever size, when they wish to withdraw from it, they should use ghee-food to make offerings to the honored image and recite this dhāraṇī one hundred and twenty times in accordance with the dharma. When this number is fulfilled, then they may withdraw. Calamities will be extinguished."

At that time, the World-Honored One told Vasudhārā: "If there are people who send possessions on a distant journey, and their family is worried, take the juice pressed from white sedge grass, mix with ox-bezoar, turmeric, camphor, sandalwood, and musk together with arghya water, and paint upon the ship. Recite this dhāraṇī with a pure mind. The possessions will arrive swiftly, the distant road will be smooth and safe, and prosperity will increase and be fulfilled."

At that time the World-Honored One spoke the mantra:

*Tadyathā cili cili agaccha agaccha bhagavatī*

At that time, having spoken this mantra, the World-Honored One further told Vasudhārā: "This dhāraṇī, if received and upheld, can fulfill the wishes of all sentient beings. It is like the great waters pervading the earth, like sunlight that can dispel all darkness, and like moonlight that brings coolness to all things."

At that time the World-Honored One spoke the mantra:

*Tadyathā dhāna-nu-varuṇa-śrīṃva indra-śrīṃva sute jā-sma-nu-nu-gacchī citā-ya-tu śaṭhā-yāṃ sāna-pra-ya-tamya-thā kalaṃ-si-dentu mantra-padani-ha tadyathā khaṭa-khaṭa khe-ti khe-ti kuṭaku-ṭaku suru-suru muru-muru pañca-pañca namari-namari dehi-dehi dapaya-dapaya pātra-pratiṣṭhā-te-hiraṇya suvarṇa-pra-dapaya svāhā vasudhi svāhā anutpannāya svāhā vāhudhi svāhā vasudhā-dhi-pataye svāhā yukyo svāhā surābhi svāhā indrāya svāhā varuṇāya svāhā vaiśra-maṇāya svāhā vaśe-bhiyu vaśe-bhiyak svāhā*

*Utpada-yantu-mi kaṃ-kṣā-ṇa-ha avira-hāṇa-ṇa mo-da-yantu oṃ ta-suhi-me vaśe-hi-da-da-paya svāhā*

At that time, having spoken this mantra, the World-Honored One told Vasudhārā: "This mantra is called the 'Vasudhārā Bodhisattva Heart Dhāraṇī.' If there are people who, with a pure mind, receive and uphold this dhāraṇī, remembering it without forgetting, it can extinguish grave offenses, obtain immeasurable merit, leave the three evil destinies, and fulfill perfect wealth and honor. In all their wishes, nothing will be left unfulfilled, up to and including the ultimate attainment of the supreme fruit."

At that time, Vasudhārā Bodhisattva and all the holy assembly, having heard what the Buddha proclaimed, were filled with immeasurable joy. They bowed their heads to the ground at his feet, faithfully received the teaching, and put it into practice.
