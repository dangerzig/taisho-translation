# 佛說三十五佛名禮懺文
## Confession and Repentance Text of the Thirty-Five Buddha Names Spoken by the Buddha

Taishō Tripiṭaka No. 0326

Translated from the Chinese. Tang dynasty, translated by Amoghavajra (不空)

---

"Namo Śākyamuni Buddha. Namo Vajradṛḍha-Saṃnipātana Buddha. Namo Ratnārci Buddha. Namo Nāgeśvara-rāja Buddha. Namo Vīrasena Buddha. Namo Vīranandin Buddha. Namo Ratnāgni Buddha. Namo Ratnacandraprabha Buddha. Namo Amoghadarśin Buddha. Namo Ratnacandra Buddha. Namo Vimala Buddha. Namo Vigatakalmāṣa Buddha. Namo Śūradatta Buddha. Namo Brahmacarya Buddha. Namo Brahmadatta Buddha. Namo Varuṇa Buddha. Namo Varuṇadeva Buddha. Namo Bhadraśrī Buddha. Namo Amitatejas Buddha. Namo Candanaśrī Buddha. Namo Prabhāśrī Buddha. Namo Aśokaśrī Buddha. Namo Nārāyaṇaśrī Buddha. Namo Kusumaśrī Buddha. Namo Padmajyotir-vikrīḍitābhijña Buddha. Namo Dhanaśrī Buddha. Namo Smṛtiśrī Buddha. Namo Suparikīrtita-nāmadheyaśrī Buddha. Namo Indraketudhvaja-rāja Buddha. Namo Vijitasaṃgrāma Buddha. Namo Vikrāntaśrī Buddha. Namo Vikrāntagāmin Buddha. Namo Samantāvabhāsa-vyūhaśrī Buddha. Namo Ratnapadma-vikrāmin Buddha. Namo Ratnapadma-supratiṣṭhita-giri-rāja Buddha.

"All Buddhas, World-Honored Ones, in all the worlds of the ten directions, appearing in the world, abiding and moving freely: may all of you observe and have compassion for me. Whether in this life or in other lives, from beginningless time, I have extensively committed many offenses: whether done by myself, or rejoiced in when done by others, or caused others to do. Whether I have stolen the possessions of the Buddha, or the possessions of the saṅgha of the four directions: whether done by myself, or rejoiced in when done by others, or caused others to do. Whether I have committed the five acts of immediate retribution, or the ten unwholesome courses of action: whether done by myself, or rejoiced in when done by others, or caused others to do. Due to these karmic obstructions that cover and conceal my body and mind, I may be born in the eight difficult conditions, or fall into the hells, among animals, or among hungry ghosts, or be born in a border land or among barbarians, or be born in the long-life heavens. Even if I attain a human body, my faculties may be incomplete, or I may give rise to wrong views and deny cause and effect, or I may be averse to the appearance of Buddhas in the world.

"All such karmic obstructions, I now, before all the Buddhas, World-Honored Ones, who possess all-knowledge, who possess the five eyes, who have realized the limit of reality, who weigh and measure, who know and see, I now sincerely confess and repent them all. I dare not conceal them. May my morality and precept-conduct be restored as before. Furthermore, may all Buddhas, World-Honored Ones, embrace, protect, be mindful of, and bear witness for me. Whether in this life or in other lives, from beginningless time throughout the cycle of birth and death, if I have ever given even a single morsel of food to an animal, or ever upheld a single pure precept, or ever cultivated a wholesome root of the brahma-conduct, or ever cultivated even the slightest wholesome root of the supreme wisdom: I gather, calculate, and reckon all of them together. As all Buddhas of the three periods of time, in the supreme and unsurpassed dedication of merit, may all be dedicated toward the supreme, perfect enlightenment.

> "All offenses I confess and repent;
> In all merit I rejoice.
> I also urge and invite all the Buddhas:
> May they realize the supreme wisdom.
> Buddhas of the past and future,
> And the honored ones among humans of the present,
> Ocean of immeasurable merit:
> I now bow my head in homage."
